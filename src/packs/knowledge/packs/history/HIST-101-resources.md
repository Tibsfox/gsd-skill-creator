# HIST-101: History & Civilization -- Curated Resources

Resources are organized by audience and depth. All links verified as of February 2026.

## Foundational Texts

Essential reading for understanding historical thinking pedagogy and practice.

- [Thinking Historically by Sam Wineburg](https://www.temple.edu/tempress/titles/1506_reg.html) -- The foundational work on how historical thinking differs from natural thinking. Explains why history is cognitively difficult and why it matters for democracy. Essential reading for anyone teaching or facilitating historical inquiry.
- [The Historian's Craft by Marc Bloch](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/296304/the-historians-craft-by-marc-bloch/) -- Written by a French historian executed in 1944. A beautiful, accessible meditation on what historians do, how they evaluate evidence, and why the discipline matters. Shows history as a living human endeavor.
- [History Lessons by Dana Lindaman and Kyle Ward](https://thenewpress.com/books/history-lessons) -- How the same historical events are taught in different countries. Concrete demonstration of why multiple perspectives matter and how national narratives shape historical understanding.
- [Silencing the Past by Michel-Rolph Trouillot](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/174846/silencing-the-past-20th-anniversary-edition-by-michel-rolph-trouillot/) -- How power determines which histories are told and which are suppressed. Uses the Haitian Revolution as a case study. Essential for understanding the politics of historical knowledge.

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, primary source collections, and engaging resources for students of all ages.

- [Stanford History Education Group (SHEG)](https://sheg.stanford.edu) -- Free, research-based materials for teaching historical thinking. Includes Reading Like a Historian lessons with curated primary source sets for multiple topics and grade levels.
- [Library of Congress Primary Source Sets](https://www.loc.gov/programs/teachers/classroom-materials/primary-source-sets/) -- Curated collections of primary sources organized by theme, with teacher guides and analysis tools. Free and authoritative. The gold standard for primary source access.
- [National Archives DocsTeach](https://www.docsteach.org) -- Interactive tools for analyzing primary source documents from the U.S. National Archives. Structured activities guide learners through document analysis.
- [Crash Course World History](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLBDA2E52FB1EF80C9) -- Engaging, fast-paced video overviews of world history topics. Good for building narrative context, though learners should practice analyzing the perspective and selectivity of the series itself.
- [Facing History and Ourselves](https://www.facinghistory.org) -- Free curriculum and resources connecting historical events to contemporary issues. Strong focus on perspective-taking, ethical reasoning, and civic engagement.
- [British Library Discovering History](https://www.bl.uk/discovering-history) -- Primary sources from one of the world's greatest libraries, with contextual essays and analysis guides. Strong on global history.

## For Parents and Mentors

Resources for adults supporting historical thinking, even without history backgrounds.

- [Why Won't You Just Tell Us the Answer? by Bruce Lesh](https://www.stenhouse.com/content/why-wont-you-just-tell-us-answer) -- Practical guide to teaching historical thinking through inquiry rather than lecture. Written for teachers but accessible and useful for any adult facilitating history learning.
- [National Council for History Education](https://www.nche.net) -- Professional organization for history educators with resources, webinars, and community connections. Useful for parents seeking guidance on supporting historical inquiry.
- [Smithsonian Learning Lab](https://learninglab.si.edu) -- Create and share learning experiences using millions of Smithsonian resources. Parents and mentors can build curated collections of primary sources for specific topics.
- [Historical Thinking Matters](http://historicalthinkingmatters.org) -- Interactive investigations of historical questions using primary sources. Designed for classroom use but works well for independent learners with adult support.

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for motivated high school students, college learners, and lifelong historians.

- [The Landscape of History by John Lewis Gaddis](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-landscape-of-history-9780195171570) -- How historians think about the past. An accessible introduction to the philosophy and methodology of history for advanced learners.
- [The Oxford History of Historical Writing](https://global.oup.com/academic/content/series/o/oxford-history-of-historical-writing-ohhw/) -- Five-volume series covering how history has been written across cultures and centuries. For learners interested in historiography as a subject in its own right.
- [JSTOR Daily](https://daily.jstor.org) -- Free articles connecting current events to scholarly historical research. Each article links to the underlying academic papers, providing a bridge between popular and scholarly history.
- [Internet History Sourcebooks Project](https://sourcebooks.fordham.edu) -- Massive collection of public domain historical texts organized by region and period. Free access to thousands of primary sources for research projects.
- [MIT OpenCourseWare: History](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/history/) -- Free college-level history courses from MIT. Includes syllabi, readings, and assignments for self-directed advanced study.

## Standards and Frameworks

Educational standards documents that inform this pack's structure.

- [C3 Framework for Social Studies State Standards](https://www.socialstudies.org/standards/c3) -- The College, Career, and Civic Life framework defining inquiry-based social studies education. The primary standards framework for this pack.
- [National Council for the Social Studies Standards](https://www.socialstudies.org/standards) -- Comprehensive standards for social studies education including history, geography, civics, and economics.
- [National History Standards](https://phi.history.ucla.edu/nchs/history-standards/) -- Standards for historical thinking and content knowledge developed by the National Center for History in the Schools at UCLA.
