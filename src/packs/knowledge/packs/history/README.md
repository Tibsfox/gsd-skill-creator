# HIST-101: History & Civilization

**Classification:** Core Academic
**Status:** Alpha
**Version:** 1.0.0

History as a way of thinking: analyzing sources, understanding causation, recognizing multiple perspectives, and constructing evidence-based arguments about the past. Not memorizing dates and names, but learning to think historically.

## Modules

| ID | Module | Focus |
|----|--------|-------|
| M1 | Sources & Evidence | Identifying, evaluating, and interpreting primary and secondary sources |
| M2 | Causation & Consequence | Why events happen, chains of cause and effect, contingency |
| M3 | Continuity & Change | Patterns across time, periodization, turning points |
| M4 | Perspectives & Empathy | Understanding historical actors in context, multiple viewpoints |
| M5 | Historical Argumentation | Constructing and defending evidence-based historical claims |

## Grade Levels

- **Foundation (PreK-2):** 30-50 hours -- Family history, timelines, stories about the past
- **Elementary (3-5):** 50-80 hours -- Community history, simple source analysis, cause and effect
- **Middle School (6-8):** 80-120 hours -- Document analysis, multi-causal reasoning, perspective comparison
- **High School (9-12):** 120-180 hours -- Historiography, research projects, advanced argumentation
- **College+ (13+):** 180-240 hours -- Archival research, original contribution, philosophy of history

## Core Philosophy

History is not about memorizing facts -- it is about learning to think. Every module in this pack emphasizes the skills of historical inquiry: examining evidence, tracing causation, considering multiple perspectives, and building arguments from sources. The content changes across grade levels, but the thinking skills are the same from kindergarten to college. The best historians are not the ones who know the most dates, but the ones who ask the best questions.
