# MATH-101: Assessment Framework

This document defines the assessment approach for the Mathematics Foundational Knowledge Pack. Assessment emphasizes understanding, reasoning, and communication over speed or rote recall.

---

## Rubric Levels

### Beginning

At the Beginning level, learners are building initial familiarity with mathematical ideas and starting to develop basic skills.

**Number & Operations (M1):**
- Counts reliably with one-to-one correspondence
- Recognizes small quantities (1-5) without counting
- Solves simple addition and subtraction problems within familiar ranges
- Understands that numbers represent quantities

**Patterns & Algebra (M2):**
- Extends simple visual patterns (AB, ABB)
- Identifies the repeating unit in a pattern
- Tries to find rules but may describe only specific examples

**Geometry & Spatial Thinking (M3):**
- Identifies basic shapes (circle, square, triangle, rectangle)
- Understands some spatial vocabulary (above, below, next to)
- Recognizes obvious symmetry in simple shapes

**Data & Statistics (M4):**
- Sorts objects by one attribute (color, size, shape)
- Reads simple bar graphs and pictographs
- Organizes data into basic categories

---

### Developing

At the Developing level, learners are applying concepts with increasing flexibility and starting to see connections between ideas.

**Number & Operations (M1):**
- Applies all four operations to solve problems
- Understands number composition and decomposition (e.g., 47 = 40 + 7)
- Uses tools (number lines, manipulatives) accurately
- Begins developing computational fluency

**Patterns & Algebra (M2):**
- Describes patterns in multiple ways (words, numbers, diagrams)
- Starts to generalize rules beyond specific examples
- Uses variables to represent unknowns in simple equations
- Translates between representations (table to words)

**Geometry & Spatial Thinking (M3):**
- Works with area and perimeter of common shapes
- Performs basic transformations (reflection, rotation)
- Begins thinking about 3D shapes and their properties
- Uses measurement tools with reasonable accuracy

**Data & Statistics (M4):**
- Chooses appropriate graph types for different data
- Calculates mean, median, and mode
- Interprets basic statistical measures
- Begins to understand probability as likelihood

---

### Proficient

At the Proficient level, learners reason mathematically, solve multi-step problems, and communicate their thinking clearly.

**Number & Operations (M1):**
- Operates with efficiency on multi-digit problems
- Solves multi-step word problems with appropriate strategies
- Estimates reasonably and checks answers for plausibility
- Understands fractions, decimals, and percents as numbers

**Patterns & Algebra (M2):**
- Uses algebraic language (expressions, equations) accurately
- Solves linear equations and explains solution steps
- Connects multiple representations (table, graph, equation, narrative)
- Understands functions as input-output relationships

**Geometry & Spatial Thinking (M3):**
- Proves simple geometric relationships with logical reasoning
- Works in coordinate systems with confidence
- Applies geometric formulas for area, volume, and surface area
- Understands congruence and similarity

**Data & Statistics (M4):**
- Designs data collection studies with clear methodology
- Interprets probability using both experimental and theoretical approaches
- Recognizes bias in sampling and representation
- Draws reasonable conclusions from data analysis

---

### Advanced

At the Advanced level, learners create, generalize, and teach. They see mathematics as a connected system and apply it to novel situations.

**Number & Operations (M1):**
- Invents efficient computational strategies
- Works with number systems beyond whole numbers (integers, rationals, irrationals)
- Understands the structure of number systems (closure, commutativity, associativity)
- Creates problems that illustrate mathematical concepts

**Patterns & Algebra (M2):**
- Creates and analyzes functions (linear, quadratic, exponential)
- Builds abstract structures and proves properties
- Solves systems of equations using multiple methods
- Connects algebraic ideas to other mathematical domains

**Geometry & Spatial Thinking (M3):**
- Uses transformational geometry to prove relationships
- Applies geometric reasoning to novel and non-standard problems
- Works with trigonometry and coordinate geometry fluently
- Connects geometry to algebra and analysis

**Data & Statistics (M4):**
- Conducts statistical inference with appropriate methodology
- Uses mathematical models to describe real-world phenomena
- Evaluates the validity of statistical claims
- Teaches statistical concepts to others effectively

## Formative Assessment

Formative assessment happens during learning and helps both the learner and mentor understand what is working and what needs attention.

### Mathematical Discourse Observations

Observe learners during discussions and collaborative problem-solving. Listen for:
- **Vocabulary use:** Are they using mathematical terms correctly and precisely?
- **Explanation quality:** Can they explain not just what they did, but why it works?
- **Listening and responding:** Do they engage with others' strategies and build on them?
- **Question quality:** Are they asking questions that deepen understanding?

### Error Analysis

When learners make mistakes, the response reveals understanding:
- **Self-correction:** Can they identify where their reasoning went wrong?
- **Misconception awareness:** Do they recognize the misconception behind the error?
- **Strategy adjustment:** Can they try a different approach after identifying an error?

### Flexibility Checks

Present the same problem and ask for multiple solution paths:
- Can they solve it at least two different ways?
- Can they explain why both methods give the same answer?
- Can they identify which method is more efficient and why?

### Application Recognition

Present a real-world scenario and ask whether mathematics can help:
- Do they recognize when a mathematical approach is relevant?
- Can they formulate the problem mathematically?
- Do they check whether the mathematical answer makes sense in context?

## Summative Assessment

Summative assessment provides evidence of mastery and readiness to progress.

### Portfolio Assessment

A learner's portfolio should demonstrate growth and depth across modules. Suggested portfolio contents:

**Problem-Solving Investigations:**
- At least 3 write-ups showing complete problem-solving process
- Evidence of multiple approaches attempted
- Reflection on what was learned from each investigation

**Mathematical Communication:**
- Written explanations of concepts for a peer or younger student
- Annotated solutions showing reasoning at each step
- Vocabulary log with definitions in the learner's own words

**Pattern Discovery and Proof:**
- Documentation of a pattern found independently
- Justification for why the pattern works
- Extension of the pattern to new cases

**Data or Geometry Project:**
- A complete data analysis project (collection, organization, analysis, conclusions)
- OR a geometric construction or design project with mathematical justification

### Projects That Demonstrate Mastery

**Probability Game Design:**
Design a game using dice, cards, or spinners. Calculate the theoretical probability of each outcome. Play the game 50+ times and compare experimental to theoretical results. Explain whether the game is fair and why.

**Pattern Art Creation:**
Create an artwork that uses mathematical patterns (tessellations, fractals, symmetry, the golden ratio). Write a mathematical description of the rules governing the artwork. Explain which mathematical concepts are demonstrated.

**Real-World Data Investigation:**
Choose a question about something that matters to the learner. Collect data (at least 30 data points), analyze it using appropriate statistical measures, create visualizations, and present findings. Address potential sources of bias.

**Mathematical Teaching Project:**
Teach a mathematical concept to someone younger or less experienced. Plan the lesson, create materials, deliver the teaching, and reflect on what worked and what could be improved.

## Portfolio Suggestions

### Foundation Level (Pre-K to Grade 2)

- Collection of number representations (drawings, manipulative photos)
- Pattern creations with descriptions
- Simple data displays (favorite color chart, pet survey)
- Photos or drawings of shapes found in the real world

### Elementary Level (Grade 3-5)

- Problem-solving journal with 5+ entries
- Fraction and decimal explorations
- Geometry constructions with measurements
- Data collection mini-project

### Middle School Level (Grade 6-8)

- Algebraic investigations with multiple representations
- Statistical analysis of real data
- Geometric proof or construction project
- Mathematical modeling of a real-world situation

### High School Level (Grade 9-12)

- Function analysis portfolio (linear, quadratic, exponential)
- Statistical inference project with proper methodology
- Geometric or trigonometric application project
- Mathematical proof portfolio
