# MFAB-101: Materials & Manufacturing -- Curated Resources

Resources are organized by audience and depth. All links verified as of February 2026.

## Foundational Texts

Essential reading for understanding materials science education and material literacy.

- [Stuff Matters by Mark Miodownik](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/220697/stuff-matters-by-mark-miodownik/) -- A materials scientist explores the stories behind 10 everyday materials (steel, paper, chocolate, concrete, plastic, glass, graphite, porcelain, foam, implant). Engaging, accessible, and scientifically accurate. The best introduction to material thinking for any age.
- [Cradle to Cradle by William McDonough and Michael Braungart](https://mcdonough.com/cradle-to-cradle/) -- The manifesto for circular economy thinking. Reimagines manufacturing so that every material has a next life. Essential context for sustainability in materials.
- [Materials and Design by Mike Ashby and Kara Johnson](https://www.elsevier.com/books/materials-and-design/ashby/978-0-08-098205-2) -- The definitive guide to materials selection for designers. Introduces the Ashby chart methodology for comparing materials across multiple properties. Professional reference that is accessible to motivated high school students.
- [Made to Break by Giles Slade](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674025721) -- The history of planned obsolescence and disposable culture. Essential context for understanding why sustainability in manufacturing matters.

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, videos, and hands-on resources for students of all ages.

- [How It's Made (Discovery/Science Channel)](https://www.sciencechannel.com/show/how-its-made) -- Television series showing manufacturing processes for thousands of products. The gold standard for seeing how materials become objects. Available on streaming platforms.
- [Tinkercad by Autodesk](https://www.tinkercad.com) -- Free browser-based 3D design tool. Great for designing objects and thinking about how they would be manufactured from different materials.
- [Instructables: Workshop Projects](https://www.instructables.com/workshop/) -- Community-contributed fabrication projects organized by material and process. Hundreds of free tutorials covering wood, metal, plastic, fabric, and mixed materials.
- [Engineering Toolbox](https://www.engineeringtoolbox.com) -- Free reference for material properties, engineering data, and design tools. Searchable database of material properties for project-based material selection.
- [Khan Academy: Chemistry of Materials](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/chemistry) -- Free video lessons covering atomic structure, bonding, and material properties. Self-paced with practice exercises. Explains the science behind why materials behave as they do.
- [Precious Plastic](https://preciousplastic.com) -- Open-source plastic recycling machines and community. Learn how plastic recycling actually works, build your own recycling tools, and understand the practical challenges of materials recycling.

## For Parents and Mentors

Resources for adults supporting material learning, even without science or engineering backgrounds.

- [ASM International Education Resources](https://www.asminternational.org/education) -- Educational materials from the world's largest materials engineering society. Teacher resources, classroom kits, materials camp information, and career guidance. Many resources are free.
- [Maker Ed](https://makered.org) -- Resources for educators supporting maker-based learning. Practical guidance for setting up material exploration activities at home or school. Free facilitator guides and community forums.
- [iFixit Repair Guides](https://www.ifixit.com) -- Free repair guides for thousands of products. Taking things apart and fixing them is the best way to understand material and manufacturing choices firsthand. Also teaches sustainability through repair culture.
- [Teach Engineering: Materials Lessons](https://www.teachengineering.org) -- Free K-12 engineering curriculum aligned to national standards. Materials-related lesson plans, activities, and background information for non-specialist educators.
- [The Art of Tinkering by Karen Wilkinson](https://www.exploratorium.edu/tinkering/art-of-tinkering) -- From the Exploratorium's Tinkering Studio. Shows how open-ended material exploration develops scientific and engineering thinking naturally.

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for motivated high school students, college learners, and lifelong materials enthusiasts.

- [MIT OpenCourseWare: Materials Science and Engineering](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/materials-science-and-engineering/) -- Free college-level materials science courses from MIT. Covers fundamentals through advanced topics. Rigorous but accessible for motivated high school students.
- [Granta EduPack (Ansys)](https://www.ansys.com/products/materials/granta-edupack) -- The professional materials selection tool used in university engineering programs worldwide. Interactive Ashby charts, material property databases, and design exercises. Free educational licenses available.
- [MatWeb Material Property Data](https://www.matweb.com) -- Free database of material property data for over 100,000 materials. Essential reference for engineering projects requiring specific material data.
- [Coursera: Materials Science Specializations](https://www.coursera.org/browse/physical-science-and-engineering/materials-science) -- Structured multi-course programs in materials science from top universities including Georgia Tech, UC Davis, and the University of Manchester.
- [NIST Materials Data Repository](https://materialsdata.nist.gov/) -- US government materials data from the National Institute of Standards and Technology. High-quality reference data for research and advanced engineering.

## Standards and Frameworks

Educational standards documents that inform this pack's structure.

- [Next Generation Science Standards: Matter and Its Interactions](https://www.nextgenscience.org/topic-arrangement/matter-and-its-interactions) -- The NGSS standards for understanding matter and material properties across K-12. Defines what students should know about materials at each grade band.
- [ITEEA Standards for Technological and Engineering Literacy](https://www.iteea.org/stel) -- Comprehensive standards for technology and engineering education including manufacturing technologies and materials processing.
- [ASTM International Educational Resources](https://www.astm.org/studentmember/) -- Standards organization that defines how materials are tested worldwide. Student memberships and educational resources available.

## Sustainability Resources

Resources specifically focused on material sustainability and lifecycle thinking.

- [Ellen MacArthur Foundation: Circular Economy](https://www.ellenmacarthurfoundation.org) -- The leading organization for circular economy thinking. Free educational resources, case studies, and tools for understanding material lifecycles and designing for reuse.
- [Material Connexion](https://materialconnexion.com) -- The world's largest materials library with a focus on innovative and sustainable materials. Online database for designers and students exploring material alternatives.
- [EPA Sustainable Materials Management](https://www.epa.gov/smm) -- US Environmental Protection Agency resources on sustainable materials management. Data on waste generation, recycling rates, and lifecycle impact of common materials.
