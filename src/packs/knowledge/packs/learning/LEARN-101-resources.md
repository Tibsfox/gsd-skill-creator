# LEARN-101: Learning to Learn — Resources

Evidence-based resources to deepen your understanding of learning science, metacognition, and effective study strategies.

---

## Foundational Texts

These books contain the research and ideas that form the basis of LEARN-101.

- [Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/18770267-make-it-stick) — Brown, Roediger, and McDaniel. The definitive popular science book on learning. Explains retrieval practice, spacing, interleaving, elaboration with clear examples and research support.

- [Thinking, Fast and Slow](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/11468377-thinking-fast-and-slow) — Daniel Kahneman. Explains how our minds work: intuition vs. reasoning, biases, decision-making. Relevant to understanding how we learn and make mistakes.

- [Peak: Secrets from the New Science of Expertise](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/26312997-peak) — Anders Ericsson. The science of deliberate practice: how to get really good at something. Explains why some practice produces mastery and other practice doesn't.

- [Mindset: The New Psychology of Success](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/40615426-mindset) — Carol Dweck. The foundational work on growth vs. fixed mindset. How beliefs about learning shape effort and persistence.

- [Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/25744928-deep-work) — Cal Newport. Practical strategies for focused learning and work. Explains why distraction kills learning and how to protect focus.

- [How Learning Works: Seven Research-Based Principles for Smart Teaching](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/2931380-how-learning-works) — Ambrose et al. Comprehensive review of learning science research. More academic but highly useful for understanding the principles behind strategies.

## For Learners

Resources designed specifically for students and learners applying learning science to their own studying.

- [Learning Scientists Website](https://www.learningscientists.org) — Free, research-based articles and infographics on study strategies. Explains: retrieval practice, spaced practice, interleaving, elaboration, concrete examples, dual coding. Highly accessible.

- [Coursera: Learning How to Learn](https://www.coursera.org/learn/learning-how-to-learn) — Barbara Oakley's popular course on learning science. Video lectures, practical strategies, interviews with learning researchers. Free to audit.

- [Academic Tips: Study Skills Resources](https://learningcenter.unc.edu/tips-and-tools/) — UNC Learning Center. Practical study guides: reading strategies, note-taking, test preparation, memory techniques.

- [Study Skills Library: Retrieval Practice Guides](https://sites.google.com/site/retrievalpracticeguide/) — Detailed guides on implementing retrieval practice, spaced repetition, and other strategies.

- [Anki Documentation and Tutorials](https://docs.ankiweb.net/) — Complete guide to using Anki for spaced repetition flashcard learning. User guides, tips, and addon recommendations.

- [The Feynman Technique Explained](https://fs.blog/feynman-technique/) — Simple technique for understanding concepts: explain simply, identify gaps, refine, simplify. Practical guide with examples.

- [Learning Styles Myth: The Science Behind Effective Learning](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rhgCrVucAFY) — Veritasium video explaining why learning styles aren't real but dual coding is. Clear, engaging explanation of the science.

## For Parents & Mentors

Resources to help parents, teachers, and mentors support effective learning.

- [Mindset Works: Resources for Parents](https://www.mindsetworks.com/resources/for-parents/) — Tools and guides for helping kids develop growth mindset. Articles on praise, challenge, and resilience.

- [How to Support a Growth Mindset at Home](https://www.parentmap.com/article/how-to-support-a-growth-mindset-at-home) — Practical parenting strategies: growth mindset language, how to talk about effort and mistakes, creating a learning environment.

- [Teaching Students About Sleep and Learning](https://www.sleepfoundation.org/sleep-news/sleep-learning-and-school-success) — Why sleep matters for learning, typical adolescent sleep needs, how to support healthy sleep habits in students.

- [The Praise Handbook: How to Give Feedback](https://www.aeenet.org/Resources/Documents/Praising-Smart-People.pdf) — Research on how different types of praise affect learning: praise effort and strategy, not ability.

- [Creating a Distraction-Free Learning Environment](https://www.understood.org/articles/en/study-tips-reducing-distractions) — Practical tips for reducing distractions, managing technology, creating focus time.

- [Conversations About Learning: Questions to Ask Your Child](https://www.edutopia.org/article/questions-ask-your-child-about-learning) — Metacognitive questions parents can ask to develop awareness and reflection.

- [Understanding Test Anxiety and Productive Study](https://blog.prepscholar.com/test-anxiety) — How study strategies affect confidence and performance. Addressing anxiety through preparation and growth mindset.

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for learners who want to dive deeper into learning science research and theory.

- [Dunlosky et al. (2013): Improving Students' Learning With Effective Learning Techniques](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3775371/) — Major review of learning research. Comprehensive evaluation of which study techniques have strong research support (retrieval practice, spacing, interleaving) and which don't (highlighting, rereading).

- [The Power of Spacing for Retention](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2015.00004/full) — Research on spaced repetition, optimal spacing intervals, and memory consolidation.

- [Interleaving vs. Blocking in Learning](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/1745691615568883) — Research demonstrating why interleaving (mixing problems) produces better transfer than blocking (grouped problems).

- [The Generation Effect and Retrieval Practice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generation_effect) — Why generating answers (retrieval practice) is more powerful for learning than reading answers.

- [Elaborative Interrogation: Learning by Asking Why](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22812347) — Research on elaboration and the power of asking "why" questions to deepen understanding.

- [Growth Mindset Research: Carol Dweck's Latest Findings](https://www.mindsetworks.com/science/) — Latest research on growth mindset from the researcher who developed the theory.

- [The Learning Scientists Podcast](https://www.learningscientists.org/learning-scientists-podcast) — Research-based conversations about learning science with accessible explanations.

- [Journal of Educational Psychology](https://www.apa.org/pubs/journals/edu/) — Peer-reviewed journal publishing learning science research. High-quality studies on memory, cognition, and effective teaching.

## Evidence-Based Tools

Tools and applications that implement learning science research into practice.

- [Anki: Spaced Repetition Flashcard System](https://apps.ankiweb.net/) — Free, open-source spaced repetition tool. Widely used by language learners, medical students, and anyone learning from flashcards. Implements optimal spacing scientifically.

- [Quizlet](https://quizlet.com/) — Flashcard platform with built-in spaced repetition, multiple game modes, and community study sets. More accessible than Anki for beginners.

- [RemNote](https://www.remnote.com/) — Knowledge management and spaced repetition combined. Connect ideas, create concept maps, integrate spaced review.

- [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/) — Personal knowledge management tool. Build concept maps, link ideas across notes, visualize connections between concepts. Encourages elaboration and integration.

- [Miro](https://miro.com/) — Collaborative whiteboard for creating concept maps, mind maps, and visual learning artifacts.

- [Notion](https://www.notion.so/) — All-in-one workspace for organizing notes, schedules, goals, learning plans. Good for designing personal learning systems.

- [Forest: Focus and Productivity App](https://www.forestapp.cc/) — Gamified focus timer. Plant a tree, keep it alive by staying focused. Helps protect deep work and minimize distractions.

- [RescueTime: Focus and Productivity Tracker](https://www.rescuetime.com/) — Tracks how you spend time, identifies distractions, helps optimize learning schedule.

- [The Pomodoro Technique Timer](https://pomofocus.io/) — 25-minute focus intervals with breaks. Simple, evidence-based time management for sustained learning.

- [Spaced Repetition Calculators](https://supermemo.com/en/algorithms/sm2) — Calculate optimal spacing intervals. SuperMemo algorithm is one standard approach.

- [Feynman Technique Templates](https://www.scribd.com/document/427045266/Feynman-Technique-Template) — Templates for practicing explanation-based learning.

## Summary

The most important resource is your own experience. Use these materials to understand the principles, then apply them to your actual learning. Test strategies, track what works, and refine your personal system.

Start with [Learning Scientists](https://www.learningscientists.org) for quick, evidence-based summaries. Move to [Make It Stick](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/18770267-make-it-stick) for comprehensive understanding. Use [Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net/) or [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/) for tools. And remember: the best resource is your own learning journal where you track what's working for you.

LEARN-101 is the foundation. These resources are the scaffolding. Your personal learning system is the building.
