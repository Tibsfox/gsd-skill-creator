## Major World Religions: Content Alignment

### Christianity (M2, M3, M4, M5)
- Scripture: New Testament, Christian theology
- Major Denominations: Catholic, Orthodox, Protestant
- Ethics: Christian moral theology
- Impact: History, art, law

### Islam (M2, M3, M4, M5)
- Scripture: Qur'an, Hadith
- Major Traditions: Sunni, Shia, Sufi
- Ethics: Shari'ah, Islamic jurisprudence
- Impact: Civilization, science, mathematics

### Judaism (M2, M3, M4, M5)
- Scripture: Hebrew Bible, Talmud
- Traditions: Orthodox, Conservative, Reform
- Ethics: Jewish law and philosophy
- Impact: History, thought, ethics

### Buddhism (M2, M3, M4, M5)
- Scripture: Tipitaka, Mahayana texts
- Major Schools: Theravada, Mahayana, Vajrayana
- Concepts: Dharma, Sangha, enlightenment
- Impact: Philosophy, medicine, art

### Hinduism (M2, M3, M4, M5)
- Scripture: Vedas, Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita
- Philosophical Schools: Advaita, Dvaita, others
- Ethics: Dharma, karma, moksha
- Impact: Art, architecture, science

### Indigenous & Traditional Religions (M1, M2, M4)
- Global indigenous traditions
- Sacred relationships with nature
- Oral transmission of wisdom
- Contemporary practice

## Assessment Alignment

THEO-101 assessment aligns with **religious studies pedagogy standards**:

- **Knowledge:** Understanding core beliefs and practices of major traditions ✓
- **Comprehension:** Explaining why traditions teach what they do ✓
- **Analysis:** Comparing and contrasting traditions ✓
- **Application:** Applying historical context to understanding contemporary religious issues ✓
- **Synthesis:** Creating bridges across traditions through interfaith dialogue ✓
- **Evaluation:** Assessing claims made by and about religions critically ✓

## Pedagogical Commitment

Religious studies in THEO-101 follows the **academic study model**:
- Religions described from **insider perspective** (how practitioners understand themselves)
- Analyzed with **outsider scholarly tools** (comparative, historical, anthropological)
- Taught with **utmost respect** for all traditions
- Always **open to expert challenge** and correction from practitioners
