# THEO-101: Theology & Religion Studies -- Curated Resources

A collection of scholarly, educational, and accessible resources for teaching and learning about the world's religious traditions. All resources are selected for academic quality, balanced presentation, and respect for all traditions.

---

## Foundational Texts

Essential reading for educators and advanced learners building a framework for religious literacy.

- [The World's Religions by Huston Smith](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/the-worlds-religions-huston-smith) -- Classic introduction to major world religions; widely used in college courses; accessible prose with deep respect for each tradition.
- [God Is Not One by Stephen Prothero](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/god-is-not-one-stephen-prothero) -- Argues that religions are genuinely different (not all paths up the same mountain); excellent for understanding real diversity.
- [A History of God by Karen Armstrong](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/163860/a-history-of-god-by-karen-armstrong/) -- Traces the evolution of the idea of God across Judaism, Christianity, and Islam; scholarly yet readable.
- [The Sacred and the Profane by Mircea Eliade](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/the-sacred-and-the-profane-mircea-eliade) -- Foundational text on the phenomenology of religion; how the sacred manifests in human experience.
- [Dimensions of the Sacred by Ninian Smart](https://www.ucpress.edu/books/dimensions-of-the-sacred/paper) -- Smart's seven-dimension model for studying religion; an essential framework for comparative work.
- [Religious Literacy by Stephen Prothero](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/religious-literacy-stephen-prothero) -- Makes the case for why religious literacy matters in American civic life; includes core curriculum proposals.

## For Learners

Age-appropriate resources for students at various levels.

### Elementary & Middle School

- [BBC Bitesize: Religious Studies](https://www.bbc.co.uk/bitesize/subjects/zh3rkqt) -- Clear, balanced introductions to world religions for younger learners; excellent visuals and quizzes.
- [National Geographic: World Religions](https://education.nationalgeographic.org/resource/world-religions/) -- Photo-rich explorations of traditions; accessible for elementary and middle school students.
- [Religions of the World (DK Eyewitness)](https://www.dk.com/us/book/9781465475756-eyewitness-religion/) -- Beautifully illustrated reference book suitable for ages 8-14; balanced coverage of major traditions.
- [What Do You Believe? (DK)](https://www.dk.com/us/book/9781465429391-what-do-you-believe/) -- Interactive exploration of world religions, philosophies, and belief systems for ages 8-12.
- [One World, Many Religions by Mary Pope Osborne](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/28543/one-world-many-religions-by-mary-pope-osborne/) -- Introduces six major religions with photographs and respectful descriptions; ages 8-12.

### High School & College

- [CrashCourse: World Religions](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8dPuuaLjXtNUmMhDT1dH_CqfDaKqVkAU) -- Engaging video series covering major world religions; scholarly but accessible; free on YouTube.
- [Harvard Divinity School: Religious Literacy Project](https://rpl.hds.harvard.edu/) -- Frameworks, case studies, and teaching resources for developing religious literacy; college-level rigor.
- [Sacred Texts Online (Internet Sacred Text Archive)](https://www.sacred-texts.com) -- Vast digital library of sacred texts from traditions worldwide; free access to primary sources.
- [The Religion Teacher](https://www.thereligionteacher.com) -- Lesson plans and resources for teaching about religion; well-structured activities and discussion guides.
- [Khan Academy: Art and Religion](https://www.khanacademy.org/humanities/art-history) -- Explores religious art across traditions; connects visual art to theological and cultural context.

## For Parents & Mentors

Resources to support adults guiding learners through religious studies.

- [Harvard Pluralism Project](https://pluralism.org) -- Research on religious diversity in America; includes case studies, profiles, and teaching resources. Essential for understanding the pluralistic landscape.
- [Tanenbaum Center for Interreligious Understanding](https://tanenbaum.org) -- Resources for combating religious prejudice and building interreligious understanding; includes educator toolkits.
- [Interfaith Youth Core (IFYC)](https://ifyc.org) -- Resources for interfaith engagement among young people; discussion guides and activity ideas.
- [Teaching Tolerance (Learning for Justice): Religious Diversity](https://www.learningforjustice.org/topics/religion) -- Lesson plans and resources for teaching about religious diversity with respect and accuracy.
- [PBS: Religion & Ethics Newsweekly Archive](https://www.pbs.org/wnet/religionandethics/) -- Archived video segments on religion in contemporary life; excellent discussion starters for families.
- [Facing History and Ourselves: Religion](https://www.facinghistory.org/topics/religion) -- Resources connecting religious history to civic engagement and social justice; strong on Holocaust education and interfaith understanding.

## For Deeper Study

Academic and advanced resources for those pursuing specialized knowledge.

### Academic Journals & Publications
- [Journal of the American Academy of Religion](https://academic.oup.com/jaar) -- Leading scholarly journal in religious studies; peer-reviewed research across all traditions and methods.
- [Religious Studies Review](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/17480922) -- Book reviews and bibliographic essays covering the full range of religious studies scholarship.
- [Numen: International Review for the History of Religions](https://brill.com/view/journals/nu/nu-overview.xml) -- Comparative and historical study of religions; international scholarly perspective.

### Online Courses
- [World Religions Through Their Scriptures (Harvard/edX)](https://www.edx.org/learn/religion) -- Free online course reading sacred texts from five traditions; university-level scholarship.
- [Introduction to Religion (Yale Open Courses)](https://oyc.yale.edu) -- University lecture series on the academic study of religion; foundational for serious students.
- [The Science of Well-Being and Religion (Coursera)](https://www.coursera.org) -- Interdisciplinary courses connecting religion to psychology, philosophy, and human flourishing.

### Interfaith & Dialogue Resources
- [Religions for Peace](https://rfp.org) -- The world's largest interfaith organization; resources on interfaith cooperation for peace, justice, and sustainability.
- [Parliament of the World's Religions](https://parliamentofreligions.org) -- Global interfaith assembly resources; declarations, presentations, and dialogue frameworks.
- [United Religions Initiative](https://www.uri.org) -- Grassroots interfaith peacebuilding; local cooperation circles around the world.

### Sacred Texts & Primary Sources
- [Internet Sacred Text Archive](https://www.sacred-texts.com) -- Free access to sacred texts from hundreds of traditions; the most comprehensive online collection.
- [Quran.com](https://quran.com) -- Multiple translations and recitations of the Quran; useful for comparative textual study.
- [Sefaria](https://www.sefaria.org) -- Jewish texts library with Torah, Talmud, Midrash, and commentaries; interconnected digital library.
- [Access to Insight (Theravada Buddhist Texts)](https://www.accesstoinsight.org) -- Pali Canon translations and study guides; scholarly yet accessible.
- [BibleGateway](https://www.biblegateway.com) -- Multiple Bible translations searchable by passage; useful for comparative translation exercises.
