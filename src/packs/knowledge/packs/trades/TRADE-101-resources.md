# TRADE-101 Resources

Curated external resources for learning skilled trades and applied skills.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that explain why trades matter and how to approach trade education.

- [Shop Class as Soulcraft](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/293960/shop-class-as-soulcraft-by-matthew-b-crawford/) — Matthew Crawford's manifesto on the dignity and intellectual demands of skilled work
- [The Way Things Work Now](https://www.macmillanlearning.com/books/9781250011915/the-way-things-work-now-by-david-macaulay) — David Macaulay's visual guide to machines, mechanisms, and physics through trades
- [Braiding Sweetgrass](https://www.milkweed.org/book/braiding-sweetgrass-indigenous-wisdom-scientific-knowledge-and-the-teachings-of-plants/) — Robin Kimmerer on working with natural materials respectfully
- [The Craftsman](https://www.dnalc.org/view/10075-the-craftsman.html) — Richard Sennett on the ethics and methods of craft work
- [A Well-Made Birdhouse](https://www.popularwoodworking.com/) — Popular Woodworking Magazine — practical instructions and inspiration

## Important Notes

### Supervision & Safety

Many resources assume adult supervision or professional training environments. Always:
- Ensure adequate supervision for young learners
- Start with simpler activities and progress to complex ones
- Verify age-appropriateness before beginning any activity
- Establish clear safety protocols
- Have first aid available

### Community Resources

Many communities offer free or low-cost:
- Library tool lending programs
- Community maker spaces
- High school shop programs (open to community in some areas)
- Weekend workshops at community colleges
- Mentorship from retired tradespeople

Ask your local library, parks department, or community college about opportunities.
