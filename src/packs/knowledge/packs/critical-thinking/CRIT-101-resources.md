# CRIT-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Critical Thinking knowledge pack. All resources are selected for quality, accessibility, and alignment with evidence-based critical thinking education.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define the philosophy and pedagogy of critical thinking education.

- [Thinking, Fast and Slow](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780374533557/thinkingfastandslow) by Daniel Kahneman -- The definitive work on cognitive biases and dual-process theory: System 1 (fast, intuitive) vs. System 2 (slow, deliberate) thinking
- [The Demon-Haunted World](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/159731/the-demon-haunted-world-by-carl-sagan/) by Carl Sagan -- Essential reading on scientific skepticism and the "baloney detection kit" for evaluating claims of all kinds
- [Being Logical: A Guide to Good Thinking](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/102617/being-logical-by-d-q-mcinerny/) by D.Q. McInerny -- Clear, concise introduction to the principles of logical reasoning for general readers
- [Asking the Right Questions](https://www.pearson.com/en-us/subject-catalog/p/asking-the-right-questions/P200000003257) by M. Neil Browne and Stuart Keeley -- Practical guide to critical thinking in everyday situations organized around key questions
- [How to Think](https://convergentbooks.com/titles/alan-jacobs/how-to-think/9780451499608/) by Alan Jacobs -- Thoughtful, accessible guide to reasoning well in a polarized world

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, courses, and media for learners at various levels.

- [Your Logical Fallacy Is](https://yourlogicalfallacyis.com/) -- Clean, visual reference for 24 common logical fallacies with clear definitions, examples, and sharable cards
- [Your Bias Is](https://yourbias.is/) -- Companion site to Your Logical Fallacy Is, covering 24 common cognitive biases with accessible explanations and real-world examples
- [Crash Course Philosophy](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8dPuuaLjXtNgK6MZucdYldNkMybYIHKR) -- Engaging, well-produced video series covering logic, epistemology, ethics, and more; excellent for grades 8+
- [Khan Academy Philosophy & Logic](https://www.khanacademy.org/humanities) -- Free introductory philosophy and critical thinking content with structured learning paths
- [TED-Ed Logic Puzzles](https://ed.ted.com/lessons?category=logic-puzzles) -- Short, animated logic puzzles that build reasoning skills through enjoyable challenges
- [Wireless Philosophy (Wi-Phi)](https://www.wi-phi.com/) -- Free philosophy videos covering critical thinking, logic, and epistemology at an introductory level
- [Calling Bullshit Data Reasoning Course](https://www.callingbullshit.org/) -- Free university-level course materials on identifying and refuting misinformation; based on the University of Washington course
- [Logically Fallacious](https://www.logicallyfallacious.com/) -- Comprehensive database of over 300 logical fallacies and cognitive biases with academic and everyday examples

## For Parents/Mentors

Guidance for adults facilitating critical thinking development, regardless of their own background.

- [Philosophy for Children (P4C)](https://p4c.com/) -- International movement and teaching methodology for philosophical thinking with young learners through structured dialogue and community of inquiry
- [CriticalThinking.org](https://www.criticalthinking.org/) -- The Foundation for Critical Thinking: frameworks, articles, mini-guides, and professional development for teaching critical thinking
- [Calling Bullshit](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/563882/calling-bullshit-by-carl-t-bergstrom-and-jevin-d-west/) by Carl Bergstrom and Jevin West -- Practical, accessible guide to recognizing and resisting misinformation in the information age
- [Teach Your Kids to Think](https://www.thinkingclassroom.co.uk/) -- Practical strategies for fostering critical thinking at home through everyday conversations and activities
- [Thinking Together](https://thinkingtogether.educ.cam.ac.uk/) -- University of Cambridge program for developing children's collaborative reasoning through structured dialogue
- [The Fallacy Detective](https://www.christianlogic.com/product/the-fallacy-detective/) by Nathaniel and Hans Bluedorn -- Lighthearted, family-friendly introduction to logical fallacies for ages 12+

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for learners pursuing logic, epistemology, and decision science.

- [An Introduction to Formal Logic](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/an-introduction-to-formal-logic/080AED63A8F75B8E09EEB9C12E0BB4F4) by Peter Smith -- Rigorous but accessible introduction to propositional and predicate logic
- [Thinking and Deciding](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/thinking-and-deciding/3C2C4F7F8DA67DE39A4B63D0DC6E8AF3) by Jonathan Baron -- Comprehensive textbook on judgment, decision making, and the psychology of reasoning
- [Superforecasting](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/227815/superforecasting-by-philip-e-tetlock-and-dan-gardner/) by Philip Tetlock and Dan Gardner -- How the best forecasters think: calibration, updating, and intellectual humility applied to real prediction
- [Rationality: What It Is, Why It Seems Scarce, Why It Matters](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/602756/rationality-by-steven-pinker/) by Steven Pinker -- Tour of reasoning tools including logic, probability, game theory, and where human cognition fails
- [The Scout Mindset](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/555240/the-scout-mindset-by-julia-galef/) by Julia Galef -- How to see things as they are rather than as you wish they were; practical intellectual honesty
- [Predictably Irrational](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/predictably-irrational-dan-ariely) by Dan Ariely -- Engaging exploration of systematic cognitive biases through behavioral economics research

## Standards and Frameworks

Educational frameworks aligned with this pack's approach to critical thinking.

- [Common Core ELA Standards (Argumentation)](http://www.corestandards.org/ELA-Literacy/) -- National standards emphasizing evidence-based argumentation, source evaluation, and reasoning across reading and writing
- [Philosophy for Children (P4C) Framework](https://p4c.com/about-p4c/) -- International framework for philosophical inquiry with children emphasizing four types of thinking: critical, creative, caring, and collaborative
- [Association of American Colleges and Universities Critical Thinking VALUE Rubric](https://www.aacu.org/initiatives/value-initiative/value-rubrics/value-rubrics-critical-thinking) -- Rubric for assessing critical thinking at the college level across explanation, evidence, influence of context, student's position, and conclusions
- [Paul-Elder Critical Thinking Framework](https://www.criticalthinking.org/pages/our-conception-of-critical-thinking/411) -- Widely used framework organizing critical thinking around Elements of Thought, Intellectual Standards, and Intellectual Traits

## Digital Tools

Software and platforms that support critical thinking activities.

- [Kialo](https://www.kialo.com/) -- Collaborative debate and argument mapping platform that visualizes pro and con arguments in a tree structure; free for education
- [Kialo Edu](https://www.kialo-edu.com/) -- Education-specific version of Kialo with classroom management features, assignment tools, and student progress tracking
- [Snopes](https://www.snopes.com/) -- Long-running fact-checking website useful for media literacy exercises and evaluating real-world claims
- [AllSides Media Bias Ratings](https://www.allsides.com/media-bias/ratings) -- Media bias ratings and tools for comparing how different sources cover the same story; excellent for media literacy activities
- [FactCheck.org](https://www.factcheck.org/) -- Nonpartisan fact-checking of U.S. political claims; useful for media literacy and evidence evaluation exercises
- [Argument Ninja Podcast](https://argumentninja.com/) -- Podcast exploring critical thinking, argumentation, and rational persuasion with practical application
