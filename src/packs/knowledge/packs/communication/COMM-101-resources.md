# COMM-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Communication & Speech knowledge pack. All resources are selected for quality, accessibility, and alignment with the pack's emphasis on speaking, listening, presenting, and collaborating.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define the philosophy and pedagogy of communication education.

- [Talk Like TED](https://www.stmartinspress.com/) by Carmine Gallo -- Analysis of the most successful TED talks, extracting principles of clarity, storytelling, and audience engagement applicable at all levels
- [Crucial Conversations](https://www.vitalsmarts.com/crucial-conversations-book/) by Patterson, Grenny, McMillan & Switzler -- Framework for high-stakes communication: staying in dialogue when emotions run high, opinions differ, and stakes are great
- [You're Not Listening](https://www.katmurphy.com/) by Kate Murphy -- Exploration of why listening has become a lost art and how to reclaim it, with research on attention, empathy, and comprehension
- [The Art of Communicating](https://www.parallax.org/) by Thich Nhat Hanh -- Mindful approach to communication emphasizing deep listening, loving speech, and the connection between inner peace and outer expression
- [Made to Stick](https://heathbrothers.com/made-to-stick/) by Chip Heath & Dan Heath -- Why some ideas survive and others die: simplicity, unexpectedness, concreteness, credibility, emotions, and stories as communication principles
- [Nonviolent Communication](https://www.cnvc.org/) by Marshall B. Rosenberg -- Framework for expressing needs and feelings without blame, and listening empathetically to others, applicable to conflict resolution and everyday dialogue

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, courses, and media for learners at various levels.

- [TED-Ed](https://ed.ted.com/) -- Short, engaging educational videos with built-in discussion prompts and extension activities, modeling effective communication
- [Toastmasters Youth Leadership Program](https://www.toastmasters.org/resources/youth-leadership-program) -- Structured public speaking and leadership development program for young people, with progressive skill-building activities
- [National Speech & Debate Association](https://www.speechanddebate.org/) -- Resources for competitive and educational speech and debate, including event descriptions, practice materials, and tournament information
- [The Moth StorySLAM](https://themoth.org/education) -- True storytelling events with educational resources for developing narrative speaking skills and authentic audience engagement
- [Listenwise](https://listenwise.com/) -- Podcast-based listening comprehension platform with current events audio, comprehension questions, and discussion prompts
- [Voice and Speech Trainers Association](https://www.vasta.org/resources) -- Professional resources on vocal technique, projection, and articulation adapted for educational settings
- [Debate.org](https://www.debate.org/) -- Online debate platform where learners can practice argumentation in written format before applying skills orally

## For Parents/Mentors

Guidance for adults facilitating communication development, regardless of their own communication background.

- [Raise Your Hand (NSDA Family Resources)](https://www.speechanddebate.org/family-resources/) -- Resources from the National Speech & Debate Association for families supporting communication development at home
- [Edutopia: Teaching Communication Skills](https://www.edutopia.org/topic/communication-skills) -- Articles, videos, and strategies for developing speaking, listening, and collaboration skills in learners of all ages
- [Greater Good Science Center: Active Listening](https://greatergood.berkeley.edu/topic/empathy/definition#how-practice-empathy) -- Research-based practices for developing empathy and active listening in families and communities
- [Family Dinner Project](https://thefamilydinnerproject.org/) -- Research and resources on using family meals as opportunities for conversation, storytelling, and communication practice
- [Responsive Classroom: Morning Meeting](https://www.responsiveclassroom.org/what-is-morning-meeting/) -- Structured greeting, sharing, group activity, and morning message format adaptable for home and community use
- [Parenting Science: Conversation Skills](https://parentingscience.com/teaching-children-social-skills/) -- Research-based advice on helping children develop conversational skills, emotional vocabulary, and social communication

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for learners pursuing communication theory, rhetoric, and research.

- [A Rhetoric of Motives](https://www.ucpress.edu/) by Kenneth Burke -- Foundational work on rhetoric as identification, persuasion as creating common ground between speaker and audience
- [The Handbook of Communication Science](https://www.routledge.com/) edited by Charles Berger, Michael Roloff & David Roskos-Ewoldsen -- Comprehensive academic reference covering communication theory, research methods, and applications
- [Language and Social Interaction (Routledge)](https://www.routledge.com/Language-and-Social-Interaction/) -- Academic exploration of how language functions in social contexts, including conversation analysis and discourse studies
- [Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion](https://www.harpercollins.com/) by Robert Cialdini -- Research on the psychology of compliance and persuasion, essential for understanding how communication shapes behavior
- [The Charisma Myth](https://www.penguin.com/) by Olivia Fox Cabane -- Research-based exploration of charisma as a learnable set of communication behaviors, not an innate trait
- [Stanford Communication Program](https://undergrad.stanford.edu/programs/pwr) -- University-level resources on rhetorical analysis, argument construction, and multimodal communication

## Standards and Frameworks

Official educational frameworks aligned with this pack's approach.

- [Common Core State Standards: Speaking and Listening](http://www.corestandards.org/ELA-Literacy/SL/) -- National standards for comprehension, collaboration, and presentation of knowledge and ideas across K-12
- [National Communication Association K-12 Standards](https://www.natcom.org/academic-professional-resources/teaching-and-learning/k-12-instruction) -- Standards for communication competence including knowledge of processes, accomplishment of purposes, and application to diverse contexts
- [21st Century Skills Framework: Communication](https://www.battelleforkids.org/networks/p21/frameworks-resources) -- Communication as one of the 4 Cs (with Critical Thinking, Collaboration, and Creativity) in the P21 framework
- [CASEL Social-Emotional Learning Framework](https://casel.org/fundamentals-of-sel/) -- Social and emotional learning competencies that overlap with communication skills: self-awareness, social awareness, relationship skills, and responsible decision-making

## Digital Tools

Software and platforms that support communication skill development.

- [Flipgrid (now Flip)](https://info.flip.com/) -- Video discussion platform where learners record short video responses, building speaking skills with the safety of asynchronous, re-recordable responses
- [Padlet](https://padlet.com/) -- Collaborative digital boards for brainstorming, gallery walks, and sharing ideas visually before or after verbal discussions
- [Pear Deck](https://www.peardeck.com/) -- Interactive presentation tool that enables real-time audience participation, polling, and feedback during presentations
- [Canva for Education](https://www.canva.com/education/) -- Free design tool for creating presentation visuals, posters, and infographics that support but do not replace verbal communication
- [Otter.ai](https://otter.ai/) -- AI transcription service that can record and transcribe discussions, enabling learners to review and analyze their own communication patterns
- [Loom](https://www.loom.com/education) -- Asynchronous video messaging tool for practicing presentation skills with the ability to re-record before sharing

## Organizations and Competitions

Programs and communities that support communication development through practice and competition.

- [Toastmasters International](https://www.toastmasters.org/) -- Worldwide network of clubs for developing public speaking and leadership skills through structured practice and peer feedback
- [National Forensic League / NSDA](https://www.speechanddebate.org/) -- Largest interscholastic speech and debate organization, providing competitive and educational opportunities for communication development
- [Model United Nations](https://www.nmun.org/) -- Simulation program where students represent countries in UN-style debates, developing diplomacy, public speaking, and negotiation skills
- [Junior Achievement](https://www.juniorachievement.org/) -- Programs that develop professional communication skills including interviews, presentations, and workplace communication
- [TEDx Youth Events](https://www.ted.com/participate/organize-a-local-tedx-event/before-you-start/tedx-youth-events) -- Locally organized TED-style events for young people, providing authentic presentation opportunities with real audiences
