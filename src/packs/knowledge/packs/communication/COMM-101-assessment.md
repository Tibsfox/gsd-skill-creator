# COMM-101 Assessment Framework

## Overview

This assessment framework evaluates communication skills through performance, not recall. You cannot assess speaking ability with a written test. You cannot assess listening with a multiple-choice quiz. Communication is a doing skill, and it must be assessed by observing the doing. Progress is measured by the quality of communication -- clarity, responsiveness, adaptability, and impact -- not by factual knowledge about communication theory.

---

## Beginning

Learners at the Beginning level are developing foundational awareness of communication as a skill. They participate in structured activities, communicate basic messages, and are learning to attend to others.

**Speaking & Expression:**
- Speaks in complete sentences when prompted
- Shares personal experiences in show-and-tell format
- Uses appropriate volume for the setting (with reminders)
- Takes turns in conversation with guidance

**Listening:**
- Faces the speaker and makes some eye contact
- Sits quietly while others speak
- Responds to direct questions about what was said
- Begins to distinguish between hearing and listening

**Nonverbal Communication:**
- Identifies basic emotions from facial expressions (happy, sad, angry)
- Makes eye contact during one-on-one conversation
- Uses gestures naturally (pointing, waving) without intentional control
- Responds to obvious body language cues (smiling, frowning)

**Presentation:**
- Shares information with a small group using simple sentences
- Follows a basic structure (beginning and end) with scaffolding
- Speaks audibly to a small audience
- Uses a visual aid (drawing, object) to support a brief talk

**Discussion & Collaboration:**
- Takes turns speaking in small groups with facilitation
- Listens to a partner's idea before sharing their own
- Expresses agreement or disagreement simply ("I agree" / "I don't think so")
- Participates in structured group activities with clear roles

---

## Developing

Learners at the Developing level are building systematic communication habits. They organize ideas before speaking, listen with increasing attention, and participate in discussions with growing independence.

**Speaking & Expression:**
- Organizes ideas before speaking (beginning, middle, end)
- Uses appropriate vocabulary for the topic and audience
- Speaks with clear volume and reasonable pace
- Adjusts tone to match the situation (serious topic vs. fun topic)

**Listening:**
- Listens without interrupting for sustained periods
- Paraphrases a partner's message with reasonable accuracy
- Asks relevant follow-up questions after listening
- Identifies the main idea in another person's message

**Nonverbal Communication:**
- Identifies a range of emotions from nonverbal cues (nervous, excited, confused, bored)
- Uses eye contact and posture intentionally during communication
- Controls voice volume and pace with increasing consistency
- Notices when someone's body language contradicts their words

**Presentation:**
- Delivers 2-3 minute presentations with clear structure
- Makes eye contact with the audience during delivery
- Uses visual aids that relate to the message
- Manages basic nervousness (speaks despite anxiety)

**Discussion & Collaboration:**
- Contributes to group discussions without dominating
- Responds to others' ideas before adding new ones
- Gives simple feedback ("I liked how you explained..." / "Maybe you could add...")
- Follows discussion protocols with increasing independence

---

## Proficient

Learners at the Proficient level demonstrate independent communication competence. They adapt to different audiences, listen actively and accurately, deliver confident presentations, and engage productively in structured discussions.

**Speaking & Expression:**
- Adapts vocabulary, complexity, and style to different audiences
- Uses evidence and examples to support claims
- Speaks with vocal variety (emphasis, pausing, pacing for effect)
- Self-monitors during speaking and adjusts when the message is not landing

**Listening:**
- Paraphrases complex messages accurately and concisely
- Asks probing questions that deepen understanding
- Identifies assumptions and implications in others' statements
- Listens for understanding even when disagreeing with the message

**Nonverbal Communication:**
- Uses body language, gesture, and movement intentionally to reinforce messages
- Reads audience nonverbal feedback and adjusts communication accordingly
- Manages personal space and positioning for different communication contexts
- Demonstrates congruence between verbal and nonverbal communication

**Presentation:**
- Designs presentations with audience-appropriate content, structure, and visual aids
- Delivers with confident posture, natural movement, and engaging eye contact
- Handles audience questions by listening to the question, answering what was asked, and bridging
- Manages presentation anxiety through preparation and cognitive strategies

**Discussion & Collaboration:**
- Builds on others' ideas explicitly ("Connecting to what Maya said...")
- Disagrees respectfully with evidence and reasoning
- Gives constructive, specific, actionable feedback
- Facilitates small group discussions with equitable participation

---

## Advanced

Learners at the Advanced level demonstrate mastery of communication across contexts. They communicate persuasively, facilitate complex discussions, navigate conflict through dialogue, and mentor others in communication skills.

**Speaking & Expression:**
- Communicates persuasively using ethos, pathos, and logos appropriately
- Tells stories that illuminate complex ideas and move audiences
- Shifts register fluidly between formal and informal, technical and accessible
- Speaks with authenticity and authority, adapting to any audience or context

**Listening:**
- Listens with genuine curiosity, even to perspectives they initially resist
- Synthesizes multiple speakers' contributions into coherent summaries
- Detects logical fallacies, emotional manipulation, and unsupported claims in real time
- Models listening behavior that elevates the communication quality of the entire group

**Nonverbal Communication:**
- Integrates verbal, nonverbal, and visual communication seamlessly
- Reads complex emotional and social dynamics in group settings
- Adjusts nonverbal communication for cross-cultural contexts
- Coaches others on nonverbal communication with specific, actionable feedback

**Presentation:**
- Delivers compelling presentations that inform, persuade, and inspire
- Adapts presentations in real time based on audience response
- Designs visual communication that enhances understanding without creating dependency
- Mentors others in presentation design, delivery, and anxiety management

**Discussion & Collaboration:**
- Facilitates large group discussions with skill and equitable participation
- Navigates conflict through dialogue, de-escalation, and interest-based negotiation
- Guides groups toward genuine consensus without suppressing dissent
- Creates environments where all participants feel safe to contribute authentically

## Formative Assessment

Formative assessment occurs during learning and provides feedback that guides improvement. In COMM-101, formative assessment focuses on observable communication behaviors.

### Active Listening Checks

During pair and group activities, observe:
- Does the learner face the speaker and make appropriate eye contact?
- Does the learner paraphrase accurately before responding?
- Does the learner ask follow-up questions that show genuine comprehension?
- Does the learner wait for the speaker to finish before responding?
- Does the learner acknowledge others' ideas before adding their own?

### Speaking Observation

During presentations and discussions, assess:
- Does the learner organize ideas before speaking?
- Is the message clear and easy to follow?
- Does the learner use appropriate volume, pace, and tone?
- Does the learner adapt to the audience and context?
- Does the learner use nonverbal communication that supports the verbal message?

### Discussion Participation Tracking

During structured discussions, record:
- How often does each learner contribute?
- Does the learner build on others' ideas or only introduce their own?
- Does the learner use evidence to support claims?
- Does the learner disagree respectfully and respond to disagreement constructively?
- Does the learner share speaking time equitably?

### Reflection Journals

Periodic journal entries where learners reflect on:
- A communication challenge they faced and how they handled it
- A moment when active listening made a difference
- Something they noticed about their own nonverbal communication
- Feedback they received and how they plan to use it

## Summative Assessment

Summative assessment evaluates cumulative learning at the end of a unit or module. In COMM-101, summative assessment is performance-based.

### Communication Portfolio

A collection of work demonstrating growth in communication skills:

**Required components:**
- Audio or video recordings of speaking performances showing growth over time
- Written reflections on communication challenges, strategies, and growth
- Self-assessment using the rubric, comparing beginning and current skill levels
- Evidence of peer feedback given and received, with reflection on how it was incorporated

**Optional components:**
- A presentation delivered to a real audience (not just the classroom)
- A recording of a facilitated discussion with self-analysis
- A conflict resolution case study with communication analysis
- A cross-cultural communication reflection

### Capstone Projects

Projects that demonstrate integrated mastery across multiple modules:

1. **Communication Showcase:** Deliver a 5-7 minute presentation on a topic of personal significance to an authentic audience (community members, younger students, school board). Include visual aids, handle questions, and submit a written reflection analyzing your own communication strengths and areas for growth.

2. **Discussion Leadership:** Facilitate a 20-minute group discussion on a complex topic. Plan the discussion structure, prepare prompts, manage participation equitably, and submit an analysis of the discussion dynamics and your facilitation choices.

3. **Audience Adaptation Challenge:** Prepare the same core message for three different audiences and deliver all three versions. Submit a written analysis explaining what you changed for each audience and why, with specific reference to audience analysis principles.

4. **Conflict Resolution Case Study:** Analyze a real or realistic interpersonal conflict. Identify the communication breakdowns that caused or escalated the conflict. Propose specific communication strategies that could have prevented or resolved it. Role-play the resolution with a partner and reflect on the outcome.

## Portfolio Suggestions

### Foundation Level (K-2)
- Audio recordings of show-and-tell presentations (track growth over the year)
- Drawings of "good listener" behaviors
- Teacher observation notes on turn-taking and active listening
- Simple self-assessments (smiley face ratings on "I listened today" / "I spoke clearly")

### Elementary Level (3-5)
- Audio recordings of organized presentations showing structural development
- Paraphrasing accuracy logs from partner exercises
- Peer feedback notes given and received
- Written reflections on communication growth ("What I learned about listening this month")

### Middle School Level (6-8)
- Video recordings of presentations with self-assessment using rubric
- Discussion participation tracking data with personal analysis
- Structured debate performance recordings with judge feedback
- Cross-cultural communication reflection from real or simulated interactions

### High School Level (9-12)
- Portfolio of presentations to authentic audiences with audience feedback
- Facilitation recordings with analysis of discussion dynamics
- Conflict resolution case studies with communication analysis
- Register-shifting examples (same message for different audiences) with written rationale
- Peer mentoring logs showing communication coaching of younger learners
