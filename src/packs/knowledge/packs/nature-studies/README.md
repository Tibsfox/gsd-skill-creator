# NATURE-101: Nature Studies & Naturalism

## Quick Start

Welcome to NATURE-101, a specialized knowledge pack on nature studies and naturalism. This pack teaches the oldest form of science: going outside, observing carefully, keeping a nature journal, learning to identify the living things around you, and understanding how ecosystems work through direct experience.

## What You'll Learn

NATURE-101 spans five interconnected modules:

1. **Outdoor Observation** -- Field skills, nature journaling, using your senses, weather awareness, and the discipline of patient observation
2. **Plants & Fungi** -- Tree and wildflower identification, fungal ecology, life cycles, seeds, and seasonal change
3. **Animals & Insects** -- Bird watching, insect study, mammal tracking, animal behavior, and wildlife ethics
4. **Ecosystems & Connections** -- Food webs, water and nutrient cycling, soil ecology, ecological relationships, and succession
5. **Conservation & Citizen Science** -- Habitat stewardship, invasive species, data collection, restoration, and contributing to real science

## Who Should Use This Pack

- **Grades K-2** -- Sensory exploration, nature walks, simple identification, first nature journal entries
- **Grades 3-5** -- Systematic observation, plant and bird identification, food web understanding, habitat comparison
- **Grades 6-8** -- Advanced identification with keys, animal tracking, ecosystem analysis, citizen science participation
- **Grades 9-12** -- Research-quality field methods, statistical analysis, conservation biology, independent projects
- **College+** -- Professional field techniques, taxonomic specialization, ecological modeling, scientific contribution

## Outdoor-First Philosophy

This pack requires going outside. Activities cannot be completed from a screen or textbook. Nature study is about direct, personal observation of the living world. The sit spot practice, nature journaling, and species identification all demand time outdoors in all weathers and seasons.

## Learning Activities

Each module includes concrete, outdoor activities using freely available natural materials:

- **Observation:** Sit spots, sensory walks, nature journaling
- **Identification:** Tree walks, wildflower hunts, bird counts, fungus hunts
- **Investigation:** Decomposition stations, stream studies, ecosystem quadrats
- **Building:** Bug hotels, pollinator gardens, nest boxes
- **Citizen Science:** iNaturalist submissions, eBird counts, habitat surveys

## No Special Equipment Needed

Essential: a notebook, a pencil, and time outdoors. Optional but helpful: a hand lens ($5-10), binoculars ($20-100), and a regional field guide ($15-25). A smartphone camera is useful for identification with iNaturalist or Merlin Bird ID. Everything else is optional.

## How to Get Started

1. **Read the vision document** -- Understand the philosophy and scope
2. **Start with Module 1** -- Observation skills are the foundation for everything else
3. **Go outside** -- The first activity is sitting still for 15 minutes and noticing
4. **Keep a nature journal** -- Your journal is the primary learning artifact
5. **Use the assessment rubric** -- Track growth from Beginning through Advanced

## Key Concepts

**Observation** -- Learning to see what's actually there, not what you expect

**Identification** -- Knowing the names and habits of the living things around you

**Ecological thinking** -- Understanding how everything connects

**Journaling** -- Recording observations accurately and asking good questions

**Stewardship** -- Knowledge carries responsibility to care for wild places

## Prerequisites

No formal prerequisites. Recommended prior knowledge: SCI-101 (Scientific Inquiry) and ENVR-101 (Environmental Science & Sustainability). These packs provide helpful context but are not required -- nature study can begin with nothing more than curiosity and willingness to go outside.

## Time Requirements

Nature study is a lifelong practice:
- **Brief introduction** -- 5-10 hours of outdoor observation
- **Module focus** -- 20-40 hours per module depending on grade level
- **Complete pack** -- 80-300+ hours depending on depth and grade level
- **Lifelong practice** -- Naturalists never stop learning
