# NATURE-101 Resources & References

Curated external resources for teaching and learning nature studies and naturalism. All resources are free or low-cost, accessible, and well-established in naturalist education.

---

## Foundational Texts

### Nature Journaling & Observation

- [John Muir Laws - The Laws Guide to Nature Drawing and Journaling](https://johnmuirlaws.com) -- The definitive guide to nature journaling technique; teaches observation, sketching, and inquiry simultaneously; essential for any naturalist
- [Clare Walker Leslie - Keeping a Nature Journal](https://www.storey.com/books/keeping-a-nature-journal/) -- Practical guide to starting and maintaining a nature journal across all seasons and skill levels
- [Anna Botsford Comstock - Handbook of Nature Study](https://archive.org/details/handbookofnature00comsuoft) -- Classic 1911 reference for teaching nature study; still profoundly useful; free online at Internet Archive

### Naturalist Tradition

- [Charles Darwin - The Voyage of the Beagle](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/944) -- The original naturalist's field journal; free online at Project Gutenberg
- [Aldo Leopold - A Sand County Almanac](https://www.aldoleopold.org/about/aldo-leopold/sand-county-almanac/) -- The foundation of the land ethic and ecological thinking
- [Rachel Carson - The Sense of Wonder](https://www.rachelcarson.org) -- On cultivating a child's innate fascination with the natural world
- [Robin Wall Kimmerer - Braiding Sweetgrass](https://milkweed.org/book/braiding-sweetgrass) -- Indigenous ecological knowledge woven with Western science

### Nature & Childhood

- [Richard Louv - Last Child in the Woods](https://richardlouv.com/books/last-child/) -- Why nature time matters for children's development; coined "nature deficit disorder"
- [Jennifer Ward - I Love Dirt!](https://trumpeterbooks.com/book/i-love-dirt) -- 52 outdoor activities for kids organized by season
- [Charlotte Mason - Home Education, Volume 1](https://www.amblesideonline.org/CM/vol1complete.html) -- Nature study as core curriculum; free online

## For Learners: Field Guides & Identification

### Multi-Group Guides

- [National Audubon Society Field Guides](https://www.audubon.org/field-guide) -- Regional and topic-based guides for birds, trees, wildflowers, insects, mushrooms, mammals, and more
- [Peterson Field Guides](https://www.houghtonmifflinbooks.com/peterson/) -- Classic series with excellent illustrations; covers birds, wildflowers, trees, insects, mammals, reptiles, and amphibians
- [National Geographic Field Guide to the Birds of North America](https://www.nationalgeographic.com) -- Comprehensive bird guide with range maps

### Bird Identification

- [Cornell Lab - All About Birds](https://www.allaboutbirds.org) -- Free online bird guide with photos, sounds, range maps, and life history for every North American species
- [Merlin Bird ID App](https://merlin.allaboutbirds.org) -- Free AI-powered bird identification from photos or sound recordings; from Cornell Lab of Ornithology
- [eBird](https://ebird.org) -- Global bird observation database; explore species near you; submit your own observations
- [Audubon Bird Guide App](https://www.audubon.org/app) -- Free bird identification guide with photos, sounds, and range maps

### Plant & Fungi Identification

- [iNaturalist](https://www.inaturalist.org) -- AI-powered species identification from photos; community verification; covers all organisms
- [Seek by iNaturalist](https://www.inaturalist.org/pages/seek_app) -- Family-friendly nature identification app; real-time identification using camera
- [MushroomExpert.com](https://www.mushroomexpert.com) -- Comprehensive North American mushroom identification resource by Michael Kuo
- [Go Botany](https://gobotany.nativeplanttrust.org) -- Interactive plant identification for New England (model for regional tools)

### Insect Identification

- [BugGuide](https://bugguide.net) -- North American insect and spider identification with photos and expert community
- [Butterflies and Moths of North America](https://www.butterfliesandmoths.org) -- Species profiles, range maps, and sighting submissions

### Tracks & Signs

- [David Sibley - The Sibley Guide to Birds](https://www.sibleyguides.com) -- Authoritative bird identification reference with illustrations
- [Mark Elbroch - Mammal Tracks & Sign](https://www.stackpolebooks.com) -- Comprehensive field guide to North American mammal tracks, scat, and signs

## For Parents & Mentors: Teaching Nature Studies

### Getting Started

- [National Audubon Society - Family Activities](https://www.audubon.org/news/how-get-your-kids-outside) -- Outdoor observation activities for families; seasonal suggestions
- [National Wildlife Federation - Garden for Wildlife](https://www.nwf.org/garden-for-wildlife) -- Create certified wildlife habitat in your yard; family project
- [Nature Study Australia](https://www.naturestudyaustralia.com.au) -- Charlotte Mason-inspired nature study resources (applicable globally)
- [Handbook of Nature Study Blog](https://handbookofnaturestudy.com) -- Weekly nature study challenges and activity ideas following Comstock's classic

### Developing Naturalist Skills

- [John Muir Laws YouTube Channel](https://www.youtube.com/johnmuirlaws) -- Free video tutorials on nature journaling, observation, and field sketching
- [Project FeederWatch](https://feederwatch.org) -- Count birds at your feeder from November to April; family-friendly citizen science from Cornell Lab
- [Great Backyard Bird Count](https://www.birdcount.org) -- Annual four-day bird count each February; anyone can participate
- [Nature Journaling Week](https://johnmuirlaws.com/nature-journaling-week/) -- Annual event celebrating nature journaling worldwide

### Curriculum Resources

- [Project Wild](https://www.fishwildlife.org/projectwild) -- K-12 wildlife education activities and teacher training
- [Project Learning Tree](https://www.plt.org) -- Forest and land-based learning activities for K-12
- [Project WET](https://www.projectwet.org) -- Water education activities and watershed curriculum
- [Master Naturalist Programs](https://masternaturalist.org) -- State-based naturalist certification programs for adults (most U.S. states)

## For Deeper Study: Academic & Technical Resources

### Ecology & Natural History

- [E.O. Wilson - The Diversity of Life](https://www.hup.harvard.edu) -- Biodiversity, extinction, and conservation by one of the greatest naturalists
- [Bernd Heinrich - A Year in the Maine Woods / Winter World](https://www.eccobooks.com) -- Natural history observation at its finest
- [David George Haskell - The Forest Unseen](https://www.davidhaskell.com) -- A year of observation in one square meter of old-growth forest
- [Douglas Tallamy - Nature's Best Hope](https://www.douglastallamy.com) -- Why native plants matter for ecosystems; actionable conservation at home

### Ornithology & Bird Science

- [Cornell Lab of Ornithology](https://www.birds.cornell.edu) -- World-class bird research, education, and citizen science
- [Macaulay Library](https://www.macaulaylibrary.org) -- Largest archive of animal sounds and video; free access
- [Bird Academy](https://academy.allaboutbirds.org) -- Online bird biology courses from Cornell Lab (some free, some paid)

### Mycology

- [North American Mycological Association](https://namyco.org) -- Mushroom clubs, forays, and education across North America
- [Fungi Perfecti](https://fungi.com) -- Paul Stamets' mycology resources and research
- [MushroomExpert.com](https://www.mushroomexpert.com) -- Comprehensive identification resource with keys and descriptions

### Entomology

- [BugGuide](https://bugguide.net) -- Expert community for insect identification
- [Xerces Society for Invertebrate Conservation](https://xerces.org) -- Pollinator and invertebrate conservation science and resources
- [Monarch Watch](https://www.monarchwatch.org) -- Monarch butterfly research, tagging, and citizen science

## Citizen Science Platforms

### Biodiversity Observation

- [iNaturalist](https://www.inaturalist.org) -- Submit observations of any organism; AI and community identification; global biodiversity data
- [eBird](https://ebird.org) -- Global bird observation database; real-time data; hotspot maps; from Cornell Lab
- [GLOBE Observer](https://observer.globe.gov) -- NASA citizen science for clouds, land cover, mosquito habitat, and trees

### Specialized Monitoring

- [Project FeederWatch](https://feederwatch.org) -- Winter bird feeder monitoring; November-April; Cornell Lab
- [Monarch Watch](https://www.monarchwatch.org) -- Monarch butterfly migration tracking and tagging
- [Bumble Bee Watch](https://www.bumblebeewatch.org) -- Pollinator monitoring and identification
- [Frogwatch USA](https://www.aza.org/frogwatch) -- Frog and toad call monitoring for wetland health
- [CoCoRaHS](https://www.cocorahs.org) -- Community precipitation monitoring network
- [USA National Phenology Network](https://www.usanpn.org) -- Track seasonal events (leaf-out, bloom, migration) across the country

### Data Portals

- [GBIF - Global Biodiversity Information Facility](https://www.gbif.org) -- Aggregated biodiversity data from research institutions worldwide
- [IUCN Red List](https://www.iucnredlist.org) -- Global species conservation status assessments
- [NatureServe Explorer](https://explorer.natureserve.org) -- Conservation status of North American species and ecosystems

## Local & Community Resources

### Find These in Your Area

- Local Audubon chapters (bird walks, field trips, Christmas Bird Count)
- Native plant societies (plant identification walks, garden tours)
- Mycological societies (mushroom forays, identification workshops)
- State parks and nature centers with education programs
- University herbaria and natural history museums
- Master Naturalist programs (state-based certification)
- Land trusts and conservation organizations
- Botanical gardens and arboreta
- 4-H nature and outdoor programs

### Getting Involved

- Join a bird walk (most Audubon chapters offer free weekly or monthly walks)
- Volunteer for habitat restoration (trail maintenance, native planting, invasive removal)
- Participate in a BioBlitz (intensive species survey of one area)
- Take a field course at a local nature center or community college
- Start or join a nature journal club
- Attend a Master Naturalist training program
