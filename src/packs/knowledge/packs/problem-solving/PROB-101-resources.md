# PROB-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Problem Solving knowledge pack. All resources are selected for quality, accessibility, and alignment with strategy-based problem-solving education.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define the philosophy and pedagogy of problem-solving education.

- [How to Solve It](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691164076/how-to-solve-it) by George Polya -- The foundational work on problem-solving heuristics: understand, plan, execute, reflect. Essential reading for anyone teaching or learning problem-solving strategies.
- [Mathematical Problem Solving](https://www.elsevier.com/books/mathematical-problem-solving/schoenfeld/978-0-12-628870-4) by Alan Schoenfeld -- Research-based framework for teaching mathematical problem solving, demonstrating that explicit strategy instruction dramatically improves performance.
- [Thinking, Fast and Slow](https://us.macmillan.com/books/9780374533557/thinkingfastandslow) by Daniel Kahneman -- How human cognition works: System 1 (fast, automatic) and System 2 (slow, deliberate) thinking. Essential context for understanding why problem solving requires deliberate practice.
- [Mindset: The New Psychology of Success](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/44330/mindset-by-carol-s-dweck-phd/) by Carol Dweck -- The growth mindset research that underpins productive struggle: believing that ability improves with effort changes how learners approach challenges.
- [Lateral Thinking](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/331882/lateral-thinking-by-edward-de-bono/) by Edward de Bono -- Techniques for generating creative solutions by deliberately breaking conventional thinking patterns. The source of many creative problem-solving techniques.

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, puzzle collections, and courses for learners at various levels.

- [Art of Problem Solving (AoPS)](https://artofproblemsolving.com/) -- Online community and courses focused on mathematical problem solving through challenging problems, not formula memorization. Includes Alcumus adaptive practice.
- [Brilliant.org](https://brilliant.org/) -- Interactive problem-solving courses in math, science, and computer science that develop intuition through guided exploration rather than passive instruction.
- [NRICH](https://nrich.maths.org/) -- Free mathematical problem-solving resources from the University of Cambridge, featuring rich tasks, games, and investigations for all ages.
- [MathPickle](https://mathpickle.com/) -- Unsolved problems adapted for K-12 classrooms: real mathematical challenges that promote genuine problem solving and productive struggle.
- [Puzzle Baron](https://www.puzzlebaron.com/) -- Logic puzzles, cryptograms, and reasoning challenges that develop pattern recognition, deduction, and systematic elimination skills.
- [Project Euler](https://projecteuler.net/) -- Mathematical/computational problems requiring creative problem solving and programming. Excellent for developing decomposition and algorithm design skills.
- [Khan Academy Problem Solving](https://www.khanacademy.org/math) -- Free structured mathematics courses with step-by-step problem solving, useful for building foundational skills that support strategy application.
- [Scratch (MIT)](https://scratch.mit.edu/) -- Visual programming environment where learners solve creative challenges through computational thinking: decomposition, pattern recognition, abstraction, and algorithm design.

## For Parents/Mentors

Guidance for adults supporting problem solvers, regardless of their own problem-solving background.

- [Mathematical Mindsets](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Mathematical+Mindsets-p-9780470894521) by Jo Boaler -- How to create a mathematics learning environment that values struggle, depth, and creativity over speed and memorization. Directly applicable to problem-solving education.
- [Becoming the Math Teacher You Wish You'd Had](https://stenhouse.com/products/becoming-the-math-teacher-you-wish-youd-had) by Tracy Zager -- Practical guide to teaching mathematics as genuine problem solving, with classroom-tested approaches for fostering strategic thinking.
- [Helping Children Learn Mathematics](https://nap.nationalacademies.org/catalog/10434/helping-children-learn-mathematics) by National Research Council -- Evidence-based guidance for parents and educators on supporting mathematical development and problem-solving skills.
- [Exploratorium: Science Snacks](https://www.exploratorium.edu/snacks) -- Simple, effective investigation activities using common materials. Many naturally develop problem-solving strategies through hands-on exploration.
- [Family Math](https://lawrencehallofscience.org/) by Lawrence Hall of Science -- Activities designed for families to do together, building problem-solving skills through games, puzzles, and collaborative challenges.
- [PBS Parents STEM Activities](https://www.pbs.org/parents/learn-grow/all-ages/science) -- Age-appropriate activities and guides that embed problem-solving strategies in everyday STEM exploration.

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for learners pursuing problem-solving theory, cognitive science, and research methodology.

- [Problem Solving Through Recreational Mathematics](https://store.doverpublications.com/products/9780486409177) by Averbach and Chein -- Rich collection of problems organized by strategy, with detailed solutions that model expert problem-solving thinking.
- [The Art and Craft of Problem Solving](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/The+Art+and+Craft+of+Problem+Solving-p-9781119239901) by Paul Zeitz -- Advanced problem-solving techniques including mathematical investigation, combinatorics, number theory, and proof strategies.
- [Sources of Power: How People Make Decisions](https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262611466/sources-of-power/) by Gary Klein -- Research on how experts solve problems and make decisions in real-world, high-stakes environments. Reveals the role of pattern recognition and mental simulation.
- [An Introduction to Design Thinking](https://dschool.stanford.edu/) by Stanford d.school -- Free resources on the design thinking process: empathize, define, ideate, prototype, test. A formal problem-solving framework widely used in industry.
- [Conceptual Blockbusting](https://www.perseusbooksgroup.com/) by James Adams -- Classic text on recognizing and overcoming mental blocks that prevent creative problem solving. Addresses perceptual, emotional, cultural, and intellectual blocks.
- [Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs](https://mitp-content-server.mit.edu/books/content/sectbyfn/books_pres_0/6515/sicp.zip/index.html) by Abelson and Sussman -- Foundational computer science text that teaches computational thinking as a general problem-solving framework through programming.

## Puzzle and Game Resources

Collections of puzzles and games that develop specific problem-solving strategies.

- [ThinkFun Puzzles](https://www.thinkfun.com/) -- Physical puzzle games (Rush Hour, Gravity Maze, Circuit Maze) that develop spatial reasoning, planning, and strategic thinking
- [Simon Tatham's Portable Puzzle Collection](https://www.chiark.greenend.org.uk/~sgtatham/puzzles/) -- Free, open-source logic puzzles playable in a browser. Excellent for daily strategy practice.
- [Numberphile](https://www.youtube.com/numberphile) -- Video explorations of mathematical problems and puzzles that model expert problem-solving thinking and curiosity.
- [3Blue1Brown](https://www.youtube.com/3blue1brown) -- Visual mathematics videos that demonstrate how visualization and reframing can unlock deep understanding of complex problems.

## Standards and Frameworks

Official educational frameworks aligned with this pack's approach.

- [Common Core Mathematical Practices](http://www.corestandards.org/Math/Practice/) -- Eight mathematical practices emphasizing problem solving, reasoning, and strategic thinking across all grade levels.
- [ISTE Standards for Students](https://www.iste.org/standards/iste-standards-for-students) -- International technology standards including computational thinker (5a-5d) which formalize key problem-solving strategies.
- [PISA Collaborative Problem Solving Framework](https://www.oecd.org/pisa/) -- International assessment framework for collaborative problem solving, defining competencies and proficiency levels.
- [21st Century Skills Framework](https://www.battelleforkids.org/networks/p21) -- Partnership for 21st Century Learning framework including critical thinking, problem solving, and collaboration as core competencies.
