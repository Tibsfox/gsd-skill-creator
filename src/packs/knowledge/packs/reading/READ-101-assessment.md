# READ-101 Assessment Framework

## Overview

This assessment framework evaluates reading and literacy skills across the full arc of development: from phonemic awareness and decoding through vocabulary, comprehension, critical reading, and discipline-specific literacy. Progress is measured by the quality and independence of reading behavior, not by the quantity of texts consumed or vocabulary words memorized. A learner who reads more strategically, thinks more critically about texts, and adjusts their approach for different reading tasks is making progress -- regardless of their current reading level.

---

## Beginning

Learners at the Beginning level are developing foundational literacy skills. They are learning to connect sounds to letters, recognize common words, and understand that print carries meaning.

**Decoding & Fluency:**
- Recognizes most letters and their primary sounds
- Reads simple CVC (consonant-vowel-consonant) words by sounding out
- Recognizes a small set of high-frequency sight words (the, is, a, I, and)
- Follows along during read-alouds, pointing to words
- Reads simple sentences slowly with word-by-word pacing

**Vocabulary:**
- Understands basic conversational vocabulary
- Learns new words through direct instruction, repetition, and picture support
- Can point to pictures matching spoken vocabulary words
- Understands simple opposites (big/small, hot/cold, up/down)

**Comprehension:**
- Retells simple stories with beginning, middle, and end (with support)
- Identifies main characters in a story
- Answers literal "who" and "what" questions about a read-aloud
- Makes simple predictions based on pictures and titles
- Sequences 3-4 story events in order

**Critical Reading:**
- Understands that books are written by authors and illustrated by illustrators
- Distinguishes fiction (stories) from nonfiction (information)
- Identifies whether a book is trying to tell a story or teach something
- Expresses personal preferences ("I liked this book because...")

**Cross-Disciplinary Reading:**
- Recognizes that different books look different (storybook vs. textbook vs. recipe)
- Understands that pictures, diagrams, and labels carry information
- Follows simple picture-based instructions with guidance

---

## Developing

Learners at the Developing level are building fluency and beginning to use strategies for vocabulary acquisition and comprehension. They read independently with increasing confidence and can engage with grade-level texts.

**Decoding & Fluency:**
- Decodes multi-syllable words using phonics patterns and word parts
- Reads grade-level text with reasonable fluency (appropriate rate and accuracy)
- Uses word-attack strategies for unfamiliar words (sound it out, look for chunks, skip and return)
- Reads with some expression and attention to punctuation
- Self-corrects decoding errors that change meaning

**Vocabulary:**
- Uses context clues to determine the meaning of unfamiliar words (with some success)
- Recognizes common prefixes and suffixes (un-, re-, -ing, -tion)
- Growing academic vocabulary through reading and instruction
- Uses a dictionary or glossary when context clues are insufficient
- Understands that some words have multiple meanings depending on context

**Comprehension:**
- Summarizes texts, identifying main ideas and key supporting details
- Makes predictions before and during reading and checks them against the text
- Asks questions while reading ("Why did...?" "What will happen if...?")
- Identifies basic text structures (sequence, cause-effect, compare-contrast)
- Makes text-to-self and text-to-text connections

**Critical Reading:**
- Identifies author's purpose as to inform, persuade, or entertain
- Notices when two accounts of the same event differ
- Distinguishes between facts and opinions in simple texts
- Begins to evaluate whether claims are supported by evidence

**Cross-Disciplinary Reading:**
- Reads simple science and history texts with guidance
- Recognizes discipline-specific vocabulary (bold terms, glossary words)
- Uses text features (headings, captions, diagrams) to locate information
- Follows multi-step written instructions with some support

---

## Proficient

Learners at the Proficient level demonstrate independent, strategic reading across multiple text types. They employ comprehension strategies flexibly, evaluate texts critically, and adjust their reading approach for different disciplines.

**Decoding & Fluency:**
- Reads complex texts fluently across genres (fiction, nonfiction, poetry, technical)
- Automatic word recognition for grade-level and most academic vocabulary
- Reads with appropriate prosody (expression, pacing, emphasis) that reflects comprehension
- Efficiently applies morphological analysis to decode and understand unfamiliar multi-syllable words

**Vocabulary:**
- Rich academic vocabulary used naturally in speech and writing
- Sophisticated morphological analysis (Latin and Greek roots, complex affixes)
- Nuanced understanding of connotation, register, and domain-specific word usage
- Independently acquires new vocabulary through wide reading
- Understands and explains word relationships (analogies, nuanced synonyms)

**Comprehension:**
- Employs multiple comprehension strategies flexibly and independently
- Monitors comprehension and applies fix-up strategies without prompting
- Synthesizes information within and across texts to build integrated understanding
- Identifies theme, central argument, and organizational logic in complex texts
- Distinguishes between essential and supporting information with accuracy

**Critical Reading:**
- Evaluates the quality and relevance of evidence in nonfiction texts
- Detects bias in word choice, information selection, and framing
- Analyzes rhetorical strategies (appeals to emotion, authority, and logic)
- Compares multiple sources on the same topic and identifies agreements and contradictions
- Evaluates source credibility using specific criteria (author expertise, publication venue, evidence base)

**Cross-Disciplinary Reading:**
- Adjusts reading strategies for science, history, math, and technical texts
- Reads primary historical sources with appropriate sourcing questions (who, when, for whom, why)
- Integrates information from text, figures, tables, and captions in science texts
- Reads mathematical texts with precision and translates between verbal and symbolic representations
- Evaluates digital sources critically, navigating online information environments independently

---

## Advanced

Learners at the Advanced level demonstrate mastery of reading across all text types and disciplines. They read at high volume with strong retention, conduct sophisticated textual analysis, and guide others in developing reading skills.

**Decoding & Fluency:**
- Reads any text fluently, including academic, professional, and archaic texts
- Exceptional reading rate with maintained or improved comprehension
- Adjusts reading pace strategically (fast-scan for overview, slow for dense passages)
- Pronunciation and vocabulary extend well beyond grade level into specialized domains

**Vocabulary:**
- Extensive vocabulary across multiple domains and registers
- Teaches vocabulary strategies to others effectively
- Appreciates etymology, language evolution, and cross-linguistic connections
- Uses precise, nuanced vocabulary in both academic and creative contexts
- Recognizes and interprets figurative language, irony, and specialized jargon

**Comprehension:**
- Synthesizes across multiple complex texts to construct original understandings
- Evaluates arguments and evidence rigorously, identifying assumptions and gaps
- Reads at high volume with strong retention across varied text types
- Navigates complex text structures including academic papers, legal documents, and technical specifications
- Mentors others in comprehension strategy use

**Critical Reading:**
- Conducts sophisticated rhetorical and discourse analysis
- Evaluates research literature for methodology, evidence quality, and conclusions
- Identifies systemic bias, framing effects, and narrative construction in media and academic texts
- Synthesizes multiple sources into original, well-supported positions
- Recognizes the limits of textual evidence and what remains uncertain

**Cross-Disciplinary Reading:**
- Reads expertly across all disciplines with appropriate strategy adjustment
- Guides others in discipline-specific reading strategies
- Navigates complex digital information environments with discernment
- Conducts efficient research reading: skimming, scanning, deep reading as appropriate
- Integrates reading with writing, discussion, and independent research

## Formative Assessment

Formative assessment occurs during learning and provides feedback that guides improvement. In READ-101, formative assessment focuses on the strategies learners use and the quality of their comprehension, not just accuracy of word reading.

### Running Records

While a learner reads aloud, record:
- Words read correctly, errors, and self-corrections
- Types of errors (meaning-changing vs. non-meaning-changing)
- Strategies used at difficulty points (sounding out, rereading, using context)
- Fluency indicators (rate, accuracy, prosody)
- Pattern analysis: What does this reader do when they encounter an unfamiliar word?

### Comprehension Check-Ins

After reading, assess quickly through conversation:
- Can the learner summarize the main idea in one sentence?
- Can the learner identify what was confusing and what strategy they used?
- Can the learner make a connection to prior knowledge or another text?
- Can the learner generate a question about something the text left unanswered?
- Does the learner's response indicate surface-level or deep processing?

### Vocabulary Usage Monitoring

Track whether newly taught vocabulary appears in:
- Learner's spoken responses during discussion
- Written responses and reflections
- Spontaneous conversation (strongest indicator of true acquisition)
- Appropriate context (using the word correctly, not just repeating it)

### Strategy Self-Assessment

Periodically, learners rate their own use of comprehension strategies:
- Which strategies do I use most often?
- Which strategies do I rarely use?
- What do I do when I do not understand something?
- How has my reading changed since I started learning strategies?
- What is my biggest reading challenge right now?

## Summative Assessment

Summative assessment evaluates cumulative learning at the end of a unit or module. In READ-101, summative assessment should demonstrate the ability to read strategically, critically, and independently across text types.

### Reading Portfolio

A collection of work demonstrating growth in reading skills:

**Required components:**
- A fluency recording (audio or video) showing improvement in reading rate, accuracy, and expression over time
- Vocabulary entries showing successful use of context clues, morphological analysis, and word relationships
- Comprehension responses for at least three different text types (narrative, informational, argumentative) showing use of multiple strategies
- A critical analysis of a nonfiction text evaluating evidence quality, bias, and author's purpose
- A cross-disciplinary reading sample showing strategy adjustment for a non-English-arts text

**Optional components:**
- A comparative analysis of two or more texts on the same topic
- A fact-checking investigation of a real-world claim
- A "reading strategies guide" written for a younger learner
- A reflection on how the learner's reading habits and skills have changed

### Capstone Projects

Projects that demonstrate integrated mastery across multiple modules:

1. **Reading Strategy Teaching:** Teach a specific reading strategy to a younger learner. Plan the lesson, deliver it, and reflect on what worked and what you would change. Demonstrates deep understanding of the strategy itself.

2. **Source Investigation:** Select a controversial or complex topic. Find and evaluate at least five sources (varying in credibility and perspective). Create an annotated bibliography evaluating each source and write a synthesis paragraph combining findings.

3. **Cross-Disciplinary Reading Challenge:** Read texts from three different disciplines (science, history, and literature) on a related theme. Analyze how each discipline's text requires different reading strategies and write a comparative reflection.

4. **Fact-Check Report:** Select three claims circulating in media or online. Investigate each systematically: identify the original source, check the evidence, find corroborating or contradicting sources, and write a verdict with supporting reasoning for each.

## Portfolio Suggestions

### Foundation Level (PreK-2)
- Audio recordings of read-alouds showing fluency improvement
- Sight word recognition lists with dates showing growth
- Drawings of favorite story characters with dictated descriptions
- Simple story retelling recordings (verbal or drawn)
- "I Can Read" celebrations documenting books read independently

### Elementary Level (3-5)
- Reading response journals with predictions, questions, and connections
- Vocabulary collection notebooks with context clues and morpheme analysis
- Story maps and text structure diagrams
- Summaries showing ability to distinguish main ideas from details
- Reading log with brief reflections on books read independently

### Middle School Level (6-8)
- Annotated texts showing active reading strategies (highlighting, margin notes)
- Critical analysis of a news article or persuasive text
- Comparative reading chart for two sources on the same topic
- Vocabulary growth tracking with academic and domain-specific words
- Cross-disciplinary reading reflection (how reading science differs from reading novels)

### High School Level (9-12)
- Rhetorical analysis essays demonstrating close reading of complex texts
- Annotated bibliography evaluating sources on a research topic
- Synthesis essays combining information from multiple sources
- Cross-disciplinary reading portfolio with strategy reflections
- Source evaluation reports for digital and print sources
- Peer reading conference notes demonstrating ability to discuss texts analytically
