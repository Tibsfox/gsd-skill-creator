# PE-101: Physical Education & Movement — Assessment Framework

This assessment framework focuses on personal growth, effort, and understanding — not athletic performance or comparison to peers. Every learner is assessed relative to their own starting point, their willingness to engage, and their understanding of movement and wellness concepts.

---

## Beginning

Learners at the Beginning level are developing foundational awareness of movement and fitness.

| Domain | Indicators |
|--------|-----------|
| **Movement Skills** | Performs basic locomotor skills (running, jumping, hopping) with limited control; needs frequent reminders about spatial awareness; movement transitions are choppy |
| **Fitness Knowledge** | Can name some body parts involved in movement; beginning to understand that exercise makes the body stronger; limited vocabulary for fitness concepts |
| **Sports & Games** | Participates in simple cooperative games with support; still developing understanding of rules and turn-taking; may become frustrated with competition |
| **Dance & Rhythm** | Moves to music but may not match the beat consistently; willing to try creative movement with encouragement; may feel self-conscious |
| **Wellness** | Understands that movement is good for health in general terms; beginning to notice how exercise makes them feel; needs guidance to connect movement to other health habits |

**Assessment approach:** Observation during activities, brief conversations about what they noticed, simple self-assessment (smiley face scale), participation tracking. Focus on encouraging effort and building confidence.

---

## Developing

Learners at the Developing level show increasing control, understanding, and willingness to engage.

| Domain | Indicators |
|--------|-----------|
| **Movement Skills** | Performs locomotor and non-locomotor skills with improving control; demonstrates spatial awareness most of the time; can combine two movements in sequence |
| **Fitness Knowledge** | Identifies the five components of fitness; understands how exercise affects heart rate; can explain why warming up matters; beginning to use FITT vocabulary |
| **Sports & Games** | Follows game rules independently; participates in team activities with cooperation; shows sportsmanship in most situations; beginning to think strategically |
| **Dance & Rhythm** | Moves in rhythm with music most of the time; creates simple movement sequences; performs basic cultural dance steps; growing confidence in expressive movement |
| **Wellness** | Connects movement to specific health benefits (sleep, mood, energy); tracks fitness activities for short periods; sets simple personal goals; recognizes importance of rest |

**Assessment approach:** Skill demonstrations with self-reflection, fitness concept quizzes (not graded for speed), peer observation feedback, short journal entries about movement experiences. Focus on recognizing improvement and building self-assessment skills.

---

## Proficient

Learners at the Proficient level move with confidence, apply knowledge independently, and engage meaningfully in all domains.

| Domain | Indicators |
|--------|-----------|
| **Movement Skills** | Performs a wide range of movement skills with control and confidence; combines movements fluidly; adapts movements to different situations; demonstrates strong spatial awareness |
| **Fitness Knowledge** | Applies FITT principle to design personal workouts; explains exercise physiology at an age-appropriate level; monitors own fitness data; distinguishes between healthy challenge and risk |
| **Sports & Games** | Uses tactical thinking in game situations; demonstrates consistent sportsmanship; can modify games for inclusivity; resolves conflicts during play constructively |
| **Dance & Rhythm** | Moves expressively with rhythmic accuracy; creates original choreography; appreciates cultural dance forms with understanding; coordinates movement with partners and groups |
| **Wellness** | Designs and follows a realistic personal fitness plan; connects movement to nutrition, sleep, and stress management; prevents injuries through proper technique; plans for active living beyond school |

**Assessment approach:** Personal fitness plan portfolio, movement skill demonstration videos showing growth over time, peer coaching observations, written reflections connecting movement to wellness, game play analysis. Focus on application and transfer.

---

## Advanced

Learners at the Advanced level teach others, create new approaches, and sustain lifelong movement habits.

| Domain | Indicators |
|--------|-----------|
| **Movement Skills** | Performs specialized skills with precision; teaches movement skills to others effectively; creates new movement sequences and challenges; adapts movements for different abilities |
| **Fitness Knowledge** | Designs evidence-based fitness programs for self and others; understands training periodization; applies exercise science to real-world situations; evaluates fitness claims critically |
| **Sports & Games** | Coaches and officiates games fairly; designs inclusive games and activities; leads team strategy sessions; mentors younger or less experienced players |
| **Dance & Rhythm** | Choreographs complex pieces for groups; performs with expression and technical skill; teaches cultural dances with proper context; integrates dance with other art forms |
| **Wellness** | Maintains long-term fitness habits independently; serves as a wellness resource for peers; connects movement to community health; adapts fitness plans across life changes and seasons |

**Assessment approach:** Quality of peer teaching and coaching, fitness program design for others, leadership in inclusive activities, sustained fitness tracking over extended periods, community wellness contributions. Focus on leadership and lifelong application.

---

## Formative Assessment

Formative assessment in PE happens continuously during activities — not through tests or quizzes.

### Observation
- Watch for movement quality improvements over time (not comparing students to each other)
- Note participation levels and engagement (effort matters more than ability)
- Look for sportsmanship behaviors during games (helping teammates, encouraging opponents)
- Observe problem-solving during strategy activities (thinking, not just acting)

### Self-Assessment
- "Rate your effort today on a 1-5 scale. What did you try that was challenging?"
- "What movement skill improved this week? What evidence do you have?"
- "How did your body feel during and after exercise today?"
- Movement journals: brief written or recorded reflections after each session

### Peer Feedback
- Structured peer observations: "One thing you did well was... One thing to work on is..."
- Partner skill practice with specific observation tasks
- Team debriefs after games: what worked, what to improve (focused on tactics, not blame)

### Fitness Data
- Heart rate tracking over time (personal trends, not comparison)
- Fitness log consistency (are they tracking? are they reflecting?)
- Personal record improvements (flexibility, endurance, strength — compared to OWN baseline)

---

## Summative Assessment

Summative assessment provides evidence of growth and mastery at the end of modules or terms.

### Movement Portfolio
- Video recordings of key skills at the beginning and end of each module
- Side-by-side comparisons showing growth
- Learner narration explaining what improved and what they're still working on
- Portfolio is PRIVATE — shared only with teacher and family, never publicly ranked

### Personal Fitness Plan
- Complete plan using FITT principle with realistic goals
- Evidence of plan execution (tracking logs)
- Reflection on what worked and what was revised
- Assessed on quality of planning and reflection, not on fitness outcomes

### Sportsmanship & Teamwork Record
- Aggregated peer evaluations across multiple activities (not single-event scores)
- Self-assessment of sportsmanship growth over the term
- Teacher observations during games and team activities
- Conflict resolution examples and outcomes

### Wellness Reflection
- Written or recorded reflection connecting movement to overall health
- Evidence of understanding nutrition-exercise connection, sleep-exercise connection, stress-movement connection
- Personal wellness plan for staying active beyond the current class or program

---

## Portfolio Suggestions

A PE portfolio documents growth, effort, and understanding over time. Suggested components:

1. **Skill growth videos:** Before and after demonstrations of key movement skills
2. **Fitness data:** Heart rate charts, flexibility measurements, endurance logs over time
3. **Personal fitness plan:** The most recent version with revision history
4. **Reflection journal:** Selected entries showing deepening understanding
5. **Sportsmanship evidence:** Peer feedback, self-assessments, conflict resolution examples
6. **Creative movement piece:** A choreographed piece or movement sequence (video)
7. **Wellness connections:** Evidence of understanding movement-health connections
8. **Goal tracking:** Goals set, progress toward goals, revised goals

**Note:** Portfolios are assessed on completeness, reflection quality, and evidence of growth — never on athletic ability or body measurements.
