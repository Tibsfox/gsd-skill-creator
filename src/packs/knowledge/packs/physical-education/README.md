# PE-101: Physical Education & Movement

**Classification:** Specialized
**Domain:** Physical Education
**Recommended Prior Knowledge:** NUTR-101

## Overview

PE-101 is the Physical Education & Movement knowledge pack for the GSD Foundational Knowledge system. It covers movement literacy from kindergarten through college — not just "gym class" but a comprehensive understanding of how bodies move, how fitness works, and how to build lifelong active living habits.

## Philosophy

Every body is a moving body. This pack is built on the principle that physical education should be inclusive, joyful, and empowering for every learner regardless of athletic ability, body type, or prior experience. Assessment focuses on personal growth and effort, never on comparison to peers or athletic performance.

## What It Covers

| Module | Focus |
|--------|-------|
| M1: Movement Foundations | Locomotor, non-locomotor, and manipulative skills; spatial and body awareness |
| M2: Fitness & Body Science | Exercise physiology, fitness components, FITT principle, training principles |
| M3: Sports, Games & Teamwork | Individual and team sports, strategy, sportsmanship, adapted sports |
| M4: Rhythm, Dance & Creative Movement | Rhythm, cultural dance, choreography, expressive movement |
| M5: Lifetime Wellness & Active Living | Fitness planning, nutrition-movement connection, injury prevention, active habits |

## Key Principles

- **Inclusion first:** Every module includes adaptations for different abilities
- **Personal growth:** Assessment measures improvement relative to self, not peers
- **Science-based:** Exercise physiology and body mechanics are part of the curriculum
- **Joy in movement:** Finding activities each learner enjoys is the primary goal
- **Lifetime habits:** Building habits that sustain health across decades, not just passing a fitness test
