# PHYS-101: Curated Resources

A collection of high-quality, vetted resources for physics learning at all levels. Emphasis on freely available, interactive, and well-produced materials.

---

## Foundational Texts

Authoritative references that define the field and its pedagogy:

- [The Feynman Lectures on Physics](https://feynmanlectures.caltech.edu) --- Richard Feynman's legendary lectures, freely available online. The gold standard for physics education: deep, intuitive, and beautifully written. Best for high school and college learners.
- **Conceptual Physics** by Paul Hewitt --- Teaches physics concepts before mathematics. Builds intuition through everyday examples and thought experiments. Ideal for learners who are intimidated by math-heavy approaches.
- **Six Easy Pieces** by Richard Feynman --- Six of the most accessible Feynman Lectures, covering atoms in motion, basic physics, energy, gravity, quantum behavior, and the relation of physics to other sciences. A perfect entry point.
- **The Physics of Everyday Things** by James Kakalios --- Explains the physics behind familiar technology: GPS, MRI machines, microwaves, cell phones. Connects abstract principles to tangible applications.
- **Physics for Scientists and Engineers** by Serway & Jewett --- A comprehensive college-level textbook covering mechanics, thermodynamics, electromagnetism, optics, and modern physics. Standard reference for formal coursework.

## For Learners

Interactive, visual, and engaging resources organized by delivery format:

### Simulations & Interactive Tools
- [PhET Interactive Simulations](https://phet.colorado.edu) --- Free, research-based simulations from the University of Colorado Boulder. Covers every major physics topic with drag-and-drop interfaces that make abstract concepts tangible. Start here for any topic.
- [The Physics Classroom](https://physicsclassroom.com) --- Comprehensive tutorials, animations, and practice problems for high school physics. Clean, ad-free, and well-organized. Includes interactive simulations.
- [HyperPhysics](https://hyperphysics.phy-astr.gsu.edu) --- Concept maps linking physics topics with clear explanations, diagrams, and equations. Excellent as a reference tool for quick lookups.
- [oPhysics](https://ophysics.com) --- Interactive physics simulations focused on mechanics, waves, and optics. Clean interface with adjustable parameters.
- [Falstad Physics Applets](https://falstad.com/mathphysics.html) --- Java/HTML5 applets for circuits, waves, optics, and more. Particularly strong for electromagnetic simulations.

### Video Channels
- [Veritasium](https://youtube.com/veritasium) --- Science videos that start with common misconceptions and work toward correct understanding through experiments and expert interviews. Excellent for developing critical thinking.
- [MinutePhysics](https://youtube.com/minutephysics) --- Short, hand-drawn explanations of physics concepts. Perfect for quick conceptual overviews and "aha" moments.
- [SmarterEveryDay](https://youtube.com/smartereveryday) --- High-speed camera investigations of everyday physics phenomena. Engaging and curiosity-driven.
- [3Blue1Brown](https://youtube.com/3blue1brown) --- Beautiful mathematical animations that illuminate the math behind physics. Particularly strong on calculus, linear algebra, and differential equations.
- [PBS Space Time](https://youtube.com/pbsspacetime) --- Deep dives into astrophysics, relativity, and quantum mechanics. College-level content presented accessibly.
- [Dr. Physics A](https://youtube.com/drphysicsa) --- Clear, methodical explanations of A-level and college physics topics. Excellent for exam preparation.

### Open Textbooks
- [OpenStax Physics](https://openstax.org/subjects/science) --- Free, peer-reviewed college physics textbooks. Available in both algebra-based and calculus-based versions.
- [Light and Matter](https://lightandmatter.com) --- Free introductory physics textbook by Benjamin Crowell. Covers mechanics, waves, and electricity with a conceptual emphasis.

## For Parents & Mentors

Resources for adults who want to facilitate physics learning without being subject experts:

- **How Things Work: The Physics of Everyday Life** by Louis Bloomfield --- Explains physics through everyday objects: balls, cars, airplanes, roller coasters, musical instruments. Written for adults without technical backgrounds.
- **Physics for Future Presidents** by Richard Muller --- Covers the physics every citizen should understand: energy, nuclear power, space, climate change. Engaging and politically relevant.
- [Science Buddies: Physics Projects](https://sciencebuddies.org/science-fair-projects/project-ideas/physics) --- Curated science fair projects with step-by-step guides. Excellent for finding age-appropriate experiments.
- [Exploratorium: Science Snacks](https://exploratorium.edu/snacks) --- Quick, low-cost activities from San Francisco's Exploratorium museum. Each activity includes setup instructions, expected observations, and physics explanations.
- [NASA STEM Engagement](https://www.nasa.gov/stem) --- Space-themed physics activities and resources from NASA. Connects physics to space exploration, which many learners find motivating.

## For Deeper Study

Resources for advanced learners and those pursuing physics beyond the foundational level:

### University Courses
- [MIT OpenCourseWare: Physics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/physics/) --- Complete lecture notes, problem sets, exams, and video lectures from MIT physics courses. Free and comprehensive. Start with 8.01 (Classical Mechanics) and 8.02 (Electricity & Magnetism).
- [Khan Academy: Physics](https://khanacademy.org/science/physics) --- Video lessons covering AP Physics 1 & 2, plus introductory college physics. Includes practice problems with instant feedback.
- [Yale Open Courses: Fundamentals of Physics](https://oyc.yale.edu/physics) --- Professor Ramamurti Shankar's lecture series. Rigorous and engaging.
- [Stanford Online Physics](https://online.stanford.edu) --- Selected physics courses from Stanford, including Leonard Susskind's "Theoretical Minimum" series on classical mechanics, quantum mechanics, and relativity.

### Research & Current Science
- [Physics Today](https://physicstoday.scitation.org) --- Magazine of the American Institute of Physics. Current research news written for physicists and physics students.
- [arXiv Physics](https://arxiv.org/list/physics/recent) --- Preprint server for physics research papers. Not beginner-friendly, but invaluable for seeing what active researchers are working on.
- [Symmetry Magazine](https://symmetrymagazine.org) --- Particle physics news from Fermilab and SLAC. Beautiful writing and visuals about the frontiers of physics.

### Problem Collections
- **Problems in General Physics** by I.E. Irodov --- Challenging physics problems used in university courses worldwide. Builds deep problem-solving skills.
- **University Physics** by Young & Freedman --- Comprehensive problem sets organized by topic. The standard companion to university physics courses.
- [Brilliant.org](https://brilliant.org) --- Interactive problem-solving courses in physics and math. Uses guided discovery rather than lectures.

## Standards & Curriculum References

Official frameworks and curriculum guides:

- [Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS)](https://nextgenscience.org) --- The national science standards framework used across the United States. Organizes physics content by disciplinary core ideas, crosscutting concepts, and science/engineering practices.
- [AP Physics 1 & 2 Course Description](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses/ap-physics-1) --- College Board framework for algebra-based college physics. Useful for aligning high school physics instruction.
- [International Baccalaureate Physics Guide](https://ibo.org) --- IB Diploma Programme physics curriculum. Provides an international perspective on physics education.
- [College Board SAT Subject Test: Physics](https://collegereadiness.collegeboard.org) --- Topic list and practice materials for standardized physics assessments.
