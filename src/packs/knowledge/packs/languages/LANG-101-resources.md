# LANG-101: Language Learning Resources

This page curates resources for learners at different proficiency levels. The best language learning uses all categories: structured foundation + authentic input + interaction + feedback.

---

## Foundational Texts & Theory

For understanding how language works and language learning actually happens:

- [The Power of Babel: A Natural History of Language](https://www.mhebookreview.com/the-power-of-babel-a-natural-history-of-language/) by John McWhorter — Readable introduction to how languages change and evolve
- [Thinking with Words](https://www.amazon.com/Thinking-Words-Linguistics-Everyday-Life/dp/0393307816/) by Janet Ormrod — Linguistics concepts for the non-specialist
- [Second Language Acquisition](https://www.amazon.com/Second-Language-Acquisition-Linguistic-Perspectives/dp/0415725496/) by Lydia White — Academic but clear overview of language learning science
- [The Myth of the Critical Period for Language Learning](https://www.linguisticsociety.org/sites/default/files/e-learning/FAQ-Language-Learning-Critical-Period.pdf) — Myth-busting about age and language learning
- [Spaced Repetition and Language Learning](https://www.supermemo.com/en/articles/spaced-repetition-full-course/) — Scientific basis for why spacing works (Article by SuperMemo)

## For Learners: Structured Foundation

Apps and platforms that provide grammar explanation and controlled practice:

### Comprehensive Platforms
- [Duolingo](https://www.duolingo.com/) — Gamified daily practice; offers 30+ languages from English
- [Babbel](https://www.babbel.com/) — Structured lessons with conversational focus; 14 languages
- [Rosetta Stone](https://www.rosettastone.com/) — Immersion approach; 25+ languages
- [Busuu](https://www.busuu.com/) — Lessons plus community interaction with native speakers
- [Lingoda](https://www.lingoda.com/) — Live group and private classes with professional teachers

### Focused Skill Practice
- [LingQ](https://www.lingq.com/) — Learn through extensive reading and listening; vocabulary tracking
- [Pimsleur](https://www.pimsleur.com/) — Audio-based program focusing on speaking and listening; 50+ languages
- [Anki](https://apps.ankiweb.net/) — Open-source flashcard app with spaced repetition algorithm; free
- [Memrise](https://www.memrise.com/) — Flashcard-style learning with memory techniques; gamified
- [Speechling](https://www.speechling.com/) — Record yourself speaking; get feedback from community

### Specific Language Resources

- **Japanese**: [Genki Textbook](https://www.genkienglish.net/) (free online version), [NHK World Easy Japanese](https://www3.nhk.or.jp/nhkworld/en/ondemand/program/video/easyjapanese/)

### Podcasts for Language Learners

- [Easy Languages YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/EasyLanguages) — Street interviews with subtitles; real people speaking
- [Slow News in Slow [Language]](https://www.slowenewsinslowenglish.com/) — News stories at learner pace; multiple languages

- [TV5Monde](https://apprendre.tv5monde.com/) — Authentic French video clips with interactive exercises
- [DW Learn German](https://learngerman.dw.com/en/overview) — Authentic German videos with transcripts and exercises

## Speaking & Listening: Interaction & Practice

The most important part of language learning is actual communication:

### Language Exchange Partners
- [Tandem](https://www.tandem.net/) — App connecting learners with native speakers for text, voice, video chat
- [HelloTalk](https://www.hellotalk.com/) — Language exchange app; 150+ languages; supports voice calls and text correction
- [Conversation Exchange](https://www.conversationexchange.com/) — Match with language exchange partners; free
- [Speaky](https://www.speaky.com/) — Video chat with native speakers; structured lessons available
- [Italki](https://www.italki.com/) — Professional teachers and casual language partners; affordable lessons

### Live Classes & Tutoring
- [Preply](https://preply.com/) — Affordable tutors worldwide; book classes on demand
- [Verbling](https://www.verbling.com/) — Online tutoring with professional teachers
- [Italki](https://www.italki.com/) — Mix of professional teachers and conversation partners
- [Lingoda](https://www.lingoda.com/) — Structured live group classes; professional teachers
- [Local community colleges](https://www.google.com/search?q=community+college+language+classes+near+me) — In-person classes, often affordable

### Language Meetups & Conversation Clubs
- [Meetup.com](https://www.meetup.com/) — Search "[your city] + [language] conversation" to find local groups
- Local libraries — Many host language conversation hours
- Local international centers — Universities, cultural organizations often have language meetups
- Online Discord/Zoom groups — Many language communities have free, regular online conversation sessions

### Pronunciation & Speaking Feedback
- [Speechling](https://www.speechling.com/) — Record yourself; get feedback from native speakers
- [Forvo](https://forvo.com/) — Hear native speakers pronounce words; record and compare
- [Google Translate](https://translate.google.com/) — Speech recognition; have it listen while you speak to check comprehension
- [Pimsleur](https://www.pimsleur.com/) — Built-in speech recognition and feedback

## Getting Started Recommendations

**For beginners:**
1. Choose a language and set a specific goal (Why do you want to learn?)
2. Use Duolingo or Babbel 15 minutes daily for 2-4 weeks (builds habit and basic vocabulary)
3. Add one authentic input source after 2 weeks (podcast, YouTube channel, music)
4. Find a language exchange partner or conversation group within 4 weeks (even just texting is valuable)
5. Replace Duolingo with more authentic input and conversation as you progress

**For intermediate learners:**
1. Reduce app time; increase authentic input (news, podcasts, TV shows)
2. Find consistent conversation partner or class (for accountability)
3. Choose a specialization based on interest (travel, work, literature, music, etc.)
4. Read materials about your interests in target language
5. Track your proficiency level (ACTFL or CEFR) every 3-6 months

**For advanced learners:**
1. Use target language for actual work or study (not just practice)
2. Read literature, academic texts, specialized materials in your field
3. Engage with target culture deeply (travel, friends, media consumption)
4. Consider teaching others or pursuing specialization (translation, interpretation, teaching)
5. Enjoy! You've reached the point where language learning is pleasure, not work.

---

**The most important resource is consistency.** 15 minutes daily beats 2 hours once a week. Weeks and months of sustained effort beat marathon study sessions. Find what works for you and stick with it.
