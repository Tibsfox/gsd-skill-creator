# Bibliography

This page provides full citations for all sources referenced in essays and educational packs across the site. Citations are organized alphabetically by first author's surname. Each entry includes the original publication details and, where available, links to open-access editions or publisher pages.

## How Citations Work

Throughout the site, inline citations appear as superscript numbers that link to the corresponding entry in this bibliography. The citation system supports books, journal articles, conference papers, and web resources. All citations are maintained in a structured database and rendered consistently across the site.

## Referenced Works

The following works are cited across the essays and educational packs on this site. This list is generated automatically from the citation database and updated whenever new content is published.

### Books

Horowitz, P. and Hill, W. (2015). *The Art of Electronics*. 3rd edition. Cambridge University Press. ISBN 978-0-521-80926-9. The definitive reference for practical electronics, covering everything from basic circuits to advanced analog and digital design.

Meadows, D. H. (2008). *Thinking in Systems: A Primer*. Chelsea Green Publishing. ISBN 978-1-60358-055-7. An accessible introduction to systems thinking, covering feedback loops, leverage points, and the behavior of complex systems.

Ritchie, D. M. and Thompson, K. (1974). *The UNIX Time-Sharing System*. Communications of the ACM, 17(7), pp. 365-375. The original paper describing the design and implementation of Unix.

Tanizaki, J. (1933). *In Praise of Shadows*. Leete's Island Books. ISBN 978-0-918172-02-0. A meditation on aesthetics, the interplay of light and darkness, and the beauty found in subtlety and restraint.

Turkle, S. (1995). *Life on the Screen: Identity in the Age of the Internet*. Simon & Schuster. ISBN 978-0-684-80353-5. An exploration of how online environments shape identity, community, and the boundaries between the virtual and the real.

### Articles and Papers

These academic and technical sources provide the theoretical foundations referenced throughout the educational packs. Additional domain-specific references are included within each pack's own bibliography section.

### Web Resources

Online resources are cited with access dates and archived URLs where available. Given the impermanence of web content, we maintain local copies of all cited web pages through the Internet Archive's Wayback Machine when possible.

## Contributing

If you find a broken citation link or notice a missing reference, please open an issue on the project repository. The citation database is maintained as structured JSON and can be updated independently of the content that references it.
