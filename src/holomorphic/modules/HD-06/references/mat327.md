# MAT327: Introduction to Topology (Foundations)

## Core Definitions

### Topological Space
A **topological space** (X, T) consists of a set X and a collection T of
subsets of X (called **open sets**) satisfying:
1. The empty set and X are in T
2. Arbitrary unions of members of T are in T
3. Finite intersections of members of T are in T

### Closed Sets
A subset A of X is **closed** if its complement X \ A is open. A set can
be both open and closed (clopen), neither, or one but not the other.

### Continuous Maps
A function f: X -> Y between topological spaces is **continuous** if the
preimage f^{-1}(U) is open in X for every open set U in Y.

## Key Properties

### Hausdorff (T2)
X is **Hausdorff** if for any two distinct points x, y in X, there exist
disjoint open sets U, V with x in U and y in V. Consequence: limits are
unique. All metric spaces (including C and the Riemann sphere) are Hausdorff.

### Compact
X is **compact** if every open cover of X has a finite subcover. In R^n,
compact = closed and bounded (Heine-Borel). Key properties:
- Continuous images of compact sets are compact
- Closed subsets of compact spaces are compact
- The product of compact spaces is compact (Tychonoff)

### Connected
X is **connected** if it cannot be partitioned into two disjoint non-empty
open sets. Stronger: X is **path-connected** if any two points can be
joined by a continuous path.

## Relevance to Complex Dynamics

These abstract definitions become concrete tools in holomorphic dynamics:

- **Julia sets are compact**: J_c is a closed subset of the compact Riemann
  sphere, hence compact. This ensures the dynamics on J_c is well-defined
  and amenable to measure-theoretic analysis.

- **The Mandelbrot set is compact and connected**: Compactness follows from
  M being a closed bounded subset of C. Connectedness is the deep
  Douady-Hubbard theorem.

- **Hausdorff property**: Guarantees that when an orbit converges, the
  limit is unique — foundational for classifying fixed points and cycles.

- **Continuity of the Julia set**: The map c -> J_c is upper semicontinuous
  in the Hausdorff metric. Understanding this requires all three properties
  above.
