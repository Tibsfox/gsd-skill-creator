# PE-101: Physical Education & Movement — Resources

Curated resources for learners, parents, educators, and anyone seeking to deepen their understanding of physical education, movement science, and active living.

---

## Foundational Texts

- [SHAPE America National Standards & Grade-Level Outcomes](https://www.shapeamerica.org/standards/) — The definitive standards for K-12 physical education in the United States, defining what students should know and be able to do at each grade level
- [Physical Literacy: Throughout the Lifecourse](https://www.routledge.com/Physical-Literacy-throughout-the-Lifecourse/Whitehead/p/book/9780415487436) — Margaret Whitehead's foundational work on physical literacy as a lifelong concept, not just a school subject
- [Teaching Children Physical Education](https://us.humankinetics.com/products/teaching-children-physical-education) — George Graham's essential text on developmentally appropriate physical education instruction
- [Physical Best: Physical Education for Lifelong Fitness](https://www.shapeamerica.org/standards/guidelines/physicalbest.aspx) — SHAPE America's health-related fitness education program emphasizing personal improvement over competition
- [Meaningful Physical Education](https://us.humankinetics.com/products/meaningful-physical-education) — Tim Fletcher's framework for making PE personally meaningful to every learner

---

## For Learners

- [GoNoodle](https://www.gonoodle.com/) — Free movement and mindfulness videos for children, organized by age and activity type; great for classroom brain breaks and home movement
- [OPEN Physical Education](https://openphysed.org/) — Free, standards-based PE curriculum with video demonstrations, activity descriptions, and skill progressions for all grade levels
- [PE Central](https://www.pecentral.org/) — Thousands of lesson ideas, assessment tools, and student-friendly resources for physical education
- [Cosmic Kids Yoga](https://www.cosmickids.com/) — Yoga and mindfulness for children ages 3-12, using storytelling to make movement engaging and accessible
- [SPARK PE Programs](https://sparkpe.org/) — Evidence-based PE curriculum for K-12 with activity cards, assessments, and teacher guides
- [Darebee](https://darebee.com/) — Free fitness resources including no-equipment workouts, challenges, and programs for teens and adults

---

## For Parents/Mentors

- [Active for Life](https://activeforlife.com/) — Evidence-based articles and activity ideas for parents on developing physical literacy in children at every age
- [Adapted Physical Education National Standards (APENS)](https://www.apens.org/) — Resources and standards for adapted physical education, ensuring learners with disabilities can participate fully in movement education
- [International Physical Literacy Association](https://www.physical-literacy.org.uk/) — Research, resources, and advocacy for physical literacy as a lifelong concept beyond sport and PE class
- [CDC Physical Activity Guidelines for Children](https://www.cdc.gov/physical-activity-basics/guidelines/children-and-adolescents.html) — Science-based recommendations for how much and what types of physical activity children need
- [Special Olympics Unified Sports](https://www.specialolympics.org/our-work/sports/unified-sports) — Inclusive sports programs that pair athletes with and without intellectual disabilities on the same team

---

## For Deeper Study

- [American College of Sports Medicine (ACSM)](https://www.acsm.org/) — The leading professional organization for exercise science, with research summaries, position stands, and certification programs
- [Journal of Physical Education, Recreation & Dance (JOPERD)](https://www.shapeamerica.org/publications/journals/joperd.aspx) — Peer-reviewed research and practical articles on physical education pedagogy and practice
- [Kinesiology and Exercise Science Research](https://journals.humankinetics.com/) — Academic journals covering biomechanics, motor learning, sport psychology, and exercise physiology
- [National Association for Sport and Physical Education](https://www.shapeamerica.org/) — Professional development, research, and advocacy resources for PE professionals
- [The Aspen Institute Project Play](https://www.aspenprojectplay.org/) — Research and advocacy for youth sports that prioritize health, development, and inclusion over elite performance

---

## Adaptive & Inclusive Resources

- [National Center on Health, Physical Activity and Disability (NCHPAD)](https://www.nchpad.org/) — Resources for promoting health and physical activity for people with disabilities
- [Wheelchair Sports USA](https://www.wasusa.org/) — Information on wheelchair basketball, tennis, rugby, and other adaptive sports programs
- [Move United (formerly Disabled Sports USA)](https://www.moveunitedsport.org/) — Adaptive sports and recreation programs across the United States
- [Inclusive PE Strategies](https://www.pecentral.org/adapted/adaptedmenu.html) — PE Central's collection of strategies and activities for including learners with various abilities

---

## Movement Science & Wellness

- [ExRx.net Exercise Prescription](https://exrx.net/) — Comprehensive exercise directory with muscle anatomy, exercise demonstrations, and fitness testing protocols
- [Khan Academy Health and Medicine](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/health-and-medicine) — Free lessons on anatomy, physiology, and health science relevant to understanding how exercise affects the body
- [Harvard Health Exercise Resources](https://www.health.harvard.edu/topics/exercise-and-fitness) — Evidence-based articles on exercise, fitness, and the science of how movement supports health
- [Yoga with Adriene](https://www.youtube.com/user/yogawithadriene) — Free yoga videos for all levels, emphasizing mindfulness, flexibility, and the mental health benefits of movement
