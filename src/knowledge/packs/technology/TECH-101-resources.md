# TECH-101: Curated Resources

## Foundational Texts

Essential references for understanding technology education philosophy and practice.

- [The Way Things Work Now](https://www.davidmacaulay.com/) by David Macaulay -- Illustrated guide to how machines, devices, and systems work. Accessible for ages 8+ with deep technical accuracy. The gold standard for visual technology education.
- [The Design of Everyday Things](https://www.basicbooks.com/titles/don-norman/the-design-of-everyday-things/9780465050659/) by Don Norman -- User-centered design principles explaining why some tools work well and others frustrate. Essential reading for understanding the design process.
- [Technology Matters: Questions to Live With](https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/technology-matters) by David Nye -- Thoughtful examination of ten fundamental questions about technology's role in society. Excellent for Module 5 context.
- [Inventing Modern: Growing Up with X-Rays, Skyscrapers, and Tailfins](https://www.amazon.com/Inventing-Modern-Growing-Skyscrapers-Tailfins/dp/0195189515) by John H. Lienhard -- How invention and creativity drive technological change, told through personal and historical narrative.

## For Learners

Resources appropriate for direct use by students at various levels.

### Elementary (Grades K-5)

- [PBS Design Squad Nation](https://pbskids.org/designsquad) -- Video challenges and hands-on engineering design activities for young learners. Free online resources with printable activity guides.
- [Simple Machines Game (Museum of Science, Boston)](https://www.mos.org/mos-at-home) -- Interactive online exhibits exploring levers, pulleys, and inclined planes through virtual experiments.
- [How Stuff Works (Kids Section)](https://science.howstuffworks.com/) -- Age-appropriate explanations of how everyday technology works, from toasters to satellites.
- [Tinkercad](https://www.tinkercad.com) -- Free online tool from Autodesk for 3D design and simple circuit building. Excellent for prototyping and digital fabrication concepts.
- [CS Unplugged](https://www.csunplugged.org/en/) -- Computer science activities that teach digital technology concepts without requiring a computer. Ideal for Module 4 foundations.

### Middle School (Grades 6-8)

- [How Stuff Works](https://www.howstuffworks.com) -- Detailed explanations of technology systems from engines to the internet. Good for independent exploration.
- [MIT Scratch](https://scratch.mit.edu) -- Visual programming platform for understanding computational thinking and algorithms. Free and community-supported.
- [Crash Course Engineering](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8dPuuaLjXtO4A_tL6DLZRotxEb114cMR) -- YouTube series covering engineering design, materials, systems, and ethics. 10-15 minute episodes.
- [PhET Interactive Simulations (Engineering)](https://phet.colorado.edu/) -- Free interactive simulations from University of Colorado for exploring circuits, forces, and energy transformations.

### High School (Grades 9-12)

- [Code: The Hidden Language of Computer Hardware and Software](https://codehiddenlanguage.com/) by Charles Petzold -- Deep, accessible explanation of how computers work from first principles. Covers binary, logic gates, memory, and processors.
- [Khan Academy Computing](https://www.khanacademy.org/computing) -- Free courses on computer science fundamentals, internet architecture, and algorithms.
- [The Innovators](https://www.simonandschuster.com/books/The-Innovators/Walter-Isaacson/9781476708706) by Walter Isaacson -- History of the people and ideas behind the digital revolution, from Ada Lovelace to the internet.
- [3Blue1Brown](https://www.youtube.com/c/3blue1brown) -- Mathematical visualization videos that illuminate algorithms, data representation, and computational thinking.

## For Parents/Mentors

Resources for adults guiding learners through technology education.

- [ITEEA Standards for Technological and Engineering Literacy](https://www.iteea.org/stel) -- The International Technology and Engineering Educators Association standards framework. Defines what students should know about technology at each grade level.
- [Engineering is Elementary (Museum of Science, Boston)](https://www.eie.org/) -- Curriculum and professional development resources for teaching engineering design to children ages 4-11. Research-based and classroom-tested.
- [Instructables](https://www.instructables.com) -- Step-by-step project guides for building things with common materials. Searchable by difficulty, topic, and materials available.
- [Maker Ed](https://makered.org/) -- Resources for facilitating maker-centered learning. Includes project ideas, assessment frameworks, and community connections.
- [NGSS Engineering Design Standards](https://www.nextgenscience.org/topic-arrangement/engineering-design) -- Next Generation Science Standards for engineering, showing what students should be able to do at each grade band.

## For Deeper Study

Resources for advanced learners, educators designing curriculum, or adults seeking deeper understanding.

- [Structure in Nature Is a Strategy for Design](https://www.amazon.com/Structure-Nature-Strategy-Design-Pearce/dp/0262160641) by Peter Pearce -- How natural structures inform technological design. Connects biology, geometry, and engineering.
- [Cradle to Cradle: Remaking the Way We Make Things](https://www.cradletocradle.com/) by William McDonough and Michael Braungart -- Reimagining design for sustainability. Essential for Module 5's environmental impact content.
- [The Art of Systems Thinking](https://www.amazon.com/Art-Systems-Thinking-Essential-Creativity/dp/0722534426) by Joseph O'Connor and Ian McDermott -- Practical guide to systems thinking with exercises and real-world examples.
- [Technics and Civilization](https://www.amazon.com/Technics-Civilization-Lewis-Mumford/dp/0226550273) by Lewis Mumford -- Classic analysis of how technology and society shape each other across history. Dense but foundational.
- [The Existential Pleasures of Engineering](https://www.amazon.com/Existential-Pleasures-Engineering-Thomas-Dunne/dp/0312141041) by Samuel Florman -- Why people create technology and what it means to be an engineer. Good counterpoint to purely critical perspectives.

## Standards and Frameworks

Official standards documents for technology and engineering education.

- [ITEEA STEL (Standards for Technological and Engineering Literacy)](https://www.iteea.org/stel) -- Comprehensive K-12 standards for technology and engineering education
- [NGSS Engineering Design](https://www.nextgenscience.org/topic-arrangement/engineering-design) -- Engineering design standards within the Next Generation Science Standards
- [ISTE Standards for Students](https://www.iste.org/standards/iste-standards-for-students) -- Technology literacy standards from the International Society for Technology in Education
- [P21 Framework (Partnership for 21st Century Learning)](https://www.battelleforkids.org/networks/p21) -- Framework for integrating technology literacy with critical thinking and collaboration skills
