# ENGR-101: Engineering & Design -- Curated Resources

Resources are organized by audience and depth. All links verified as of February 2026.

## Foundational Texts

Essential reading for understanding engineering education philosophy and practice.

- [Engineering is Elementary (EiE)](https://eie.org) -- The gold standard curriculum for K-5 engineering education, developed by the Museum of Science, Boston. Research-backed, field-tested with thousands of classrooms. Free lesson plans and teacher guides available.
- [To Engineer Is Human by Henry Petroski](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/298426/to-engineer-is-human-by-henry-petroski/) -- How engineering failures drive progress. Accessible exploration of why things break and why that matters for all of engineering.
- [The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman](https://www.nngroup.com/books/design-everyday-things-revised/) -- Foundational text on human-centered design. Explains how design shapes user experience and why usability is an engineering concern.
- [Design Thinking for the Greater Good by Jeanne Liedtka](https://www.darden.virginia.edu/ibis/design-thinking) -- Applying engineering and design thinking to social challenges. Shows engineering as a force for equity and positive impact.

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, videos, and hands-on resources for students of all ages.

- [PBS Design Squad Nation](https://pbskids.org/designsquad) -- Video challenges and online activities for middle school engineers. Free, engaging, and well-produced. Great for inspiration and guided challenges.
- [Tinkercad by Autodesk](https://www.tinkercad.com) -- Free browser-based 3D design, electronics simulation, and coding tool. Perfect introduction to CAD and circuit design for ages 10 and up.
- [FIRST Robotics Programs](https://www.firstinspires.org) -- Competitive robotics programs from kindergarten (FIRST LEGO League Jr.) through college (FRC). The best hands-on engineering experience available for students.
- [Instructables](https://www.instructables.com) -- Community-contributed step-by-step engineering and making projects. Thousands of free project ideas with detailed instructions and photos.
- [Khan Academy Engineering](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/engineering) -- Free video lessons covering engineering principles, electrical engineering, and design. Self-paced with practice exercises.
- [How Things Work by Louis Bloomfield](https://howthingswork.virginia.edu/) -- Physics and engineering behind everyday objects. Connects abstract principles to real artifacts students encounter daily.

## For Parents and Mentors

Resources for adults supporting engineering learning, even without engineering backgrounds.

- [Maker Ed](https://makered.org) -- Resources and community for educators supporting maker-based learning. Practical advice for setting up engineering activities at home or school. Free facilitator guides.
- [Engineering is Elementary Family Resources](https://eie.org/families) -- Activities and guidance specifically designed for families learning engineering together. No engineering background required.
- [Teach Engineering](https://www.teachengineering.org) -- Free K-12 engineering curriculum aligned to national standards. Lesson plans, activities, and background information for non-specialist educators.
- [STEM Teaching Tools](http://stemteachingtools.org) -- Research-based practice briefs for supporting engineering learning. Short, actionable guidance documents.
- [The Art of Tinkering by Karen Wilkinson](https://www.exploratorium.edu/tinkering/art-of-tinkering) -- From the Exploratorium's Tinkering Studio. Shows how open-ended exploration develops engineering thinking naturally.

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for motivated high school students, college learners, and lifelong engineers.

- [MIT OpenCourseWare: Introduction to Engineering](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/engineering/) -- Free college-level engineering courses from MIT. Rigorous but accessible for motivated high school students.
- [Structures: Or Why Things Don't Fall Down by J.E. Gordon](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/323002/structures-by-je-gordon/) -- Accessible and witty introduction to structural engineering. Explains why bridges, buildings, and bones hold up or do not.
- [The Unwritten Laws of Engineering by W.J. King](https://www.asme.org/publications-submissions/books/find-book/the-unwritten-laws-of-engineering) -- Practical wisdom about engineering as a profession. Covers communication, ethics, teamwork, and career guidance.
- [Coursera Engineering Specializations](https://www.coursera.org/browse/physical-science-and-engineering) -- Structured multi-course programs in mechanical, electrical, civil, and software engineering from top universities.
- [NSPE Code of Ethics for Engineers](https://www.nspe.org/resources/ethics/code-ethics) -- The professional ethics code governing engineering practice. Essential reading for understanding engineering responsibility.

## Standards and Frameworks

Educational standards documents that inform this pack's structure.

- [Next Generation Science Standards: Engineering Design](https://www.nextgenscience.org/topic-arrangement/engineering-design) -- The NGSS engineering design standards covering K-12. Defines what students should know and be able to do at each grade band.
- [ITEEA Standards for Technological and Engineering Literacy](https://www.iteea.org/stel) -- Comprehensive standards for technology and engineering education. Broader than NGSS, covering technological literacy alongside design skills.
- [ABET Accreditation Criteria](https://www.abet.org/accreditation/accreditation-criteria/) -- Criteria for accredited engineering programs. Defines what a professional engineering education must include.
