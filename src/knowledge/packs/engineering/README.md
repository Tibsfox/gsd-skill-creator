# ENGR-101: Engineering & Design

**Classification:** Core Academic
**Status:** Alpha
**Version:** 1.0.0

Engineering as systematic problem-solving under constraints. The engineering design process -- define problems, brainstorm solutions, prototype, test, iterate -- is a universal approach to creating things that work in the real world.

## Modules

| ID | Module | Focus |
|----|--------|-------|
| M1 | The Engineering Design Process | Problem definition, requirements, brainstorming, decision-making |
| M2 | Materials & Structures | Material properties, structural principles, load paths, failure modes |
| M3 | Mechanisms & Systems | Simple machines, gears, circuits, control systems, systems thinking |
| M4 | Prototyping & Testing | Rapid prototyping, test design, failure analysis, iteration |
| M5 | Engineering Ethics & Impact | Safety, environment, accessibility, equity, professional ethics |

## Grade Levels

- **Foundation (PreK-2):** 30-50 hours -- Building, simple challenges, describing what happens
- **Elementary (3-5):** 50-80 hours -- Design process with guidance, measurement, documentation
- **Middle School (6-8):** 80-120 hours -- Independent design process, mechanisms, formal testing
- **High School (9-12):** 120-180 hours -- Complex systems, mathematical modeling, specialization
- **College+ (13+):** 180-240 hours -- Professional methodology, advanced analysis, research

## Prerequisites

- **Recommended:** MATH-101 (Mathematics), SCI-101 (Science)
- **Required:** None -- engineering starts with building and problem-solving

## Core Philosophy

Engineering is not about knowing facts -- it is about solving problems. Every module in this pack emphasizes the design cycle: define, ideate, prototype, test, iterate. Failure is not a mistake; it is data. The best engineers are not the ones who get it right the first time, but the ones who learn the most from each iteration.
