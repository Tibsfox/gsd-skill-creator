# PHILO-101: Philosophy -- Curated Resources

A collection of vetted, high-quality resources for learning and teaching philosophy at every level. Resources are organized by purpose and audience to help learners, parents, and mentors find what they need quickly.

---

## Foundational Texts

Classic works of philosophy that have shaped human thinking. Approachable in excerpt form for younger learners; essential reading for advanced students.

- [Plato's Dialogues (selections)](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/1497) -- The foundational texts of Western philosophy; Socratic method in action. Start with "Apology," "Meno," and "Republic" (Book VII, the Cave allegory). Free on Project Gutenberg.
- [Aristotle's Nicomachean Ethics](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/8438) -- The classic inquiry into what makes a good life, virtue, and happiness. Best approached through guided excerpts for high school students.
- [Marcus Aurelius: Meditations](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/2680) -- Stoic philosophy in practice: personal reflections on duty, reason, and living well. Remarkably accessible and immediately applicable to daily life.
- [Confucius: The Analects](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/3330) -- Foundational text of East Asian philosophy: virtue, relationships, social harmony, and self-cultivation.
- [Descartes: Meditations on First Philosophy](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/59) -- The starting point of modern epistemology: systematic doubt as a method for finding certainty. The "I think, therefore I am" argument.
- [John Stuart Mill: On Liberty](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/34901) -- Classic defense of individual freedom and the harm principle. Essential for political philosophy.

## For Learners

Resources designed to make philosophy accessible, engaging, and fun for learners at different levels.

- [Sophie's World by Jostein Gaarder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sophie%27s_World) -- A novel that teaches the history of Western philosophy through a mystery story. Excellent for ages 12+ and adults new to philosophy.
- [The Philosophy Book (DK Big Ideas Simply Explained)](https://www.dk.com/us/book/9781465458551/the-philosophy-book/) -- Visual, accessible overview of major philosophical ideas and thinkers. Good reference for middle school through adult.
- [Philosophy for Kids by David A. White](https://www.prufrock.com/Philosophy-for-Kids-P305.aspx) -- 40 fun questions that help children ages 8-12 think philosophically. Practical and classroom-ready.
- [CrashCourse Philosophy (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8dPuuaLjXtNgK6MZucdYldNkMybYIHKR) -- 46 episodes covering major philosophical topics in an engaging, accessible video format. Free.
- [Wireless Philosophy (Wi-Phi)](https://www.wi-phi.com) -- Free animated philosophy videos created by academic philosophers, covering logic, epistemology, ethics, and more.
- [The School of Life (YouTube)](https://www.youtube.com/c/theschooloflife) -- Philosophical ideas applied to everyday life: relationships, work, self-knowledge, and culture. Accessible and visually engaging.
- [Philosophy Experiments (The Philosophers' Magazine)](https://www.philosophyexperiments.com) -- Interactive thought experiments and philosophical games that test your intuitions and reasoning.

## For Parents/Mentors

Resources to help adults facilitate philosophical thinking even without formal philosophy training.

- [SAPERE -- Philosophy for Children (P4C)](https://www.sapere.org.uk) -- The leading organization for Philosophy for Children. Training, resources, and research on how to facilitate philosophical inquiry with young people.
- [The Philosophy Foundation](https://www.philosophy-foundation.org) -- UK-based organization providing resources, workshops, and training for teaching philosophy to children. Includes free session plans and activities.
- [Teaching Children Philosophy (Wartenberg Project)](https://www.teachingchildrenphilosophy.org) -- Guides for using picture books to teach philosophy. Each guide identifies philosophical themes in a children's book and provides discussion questions.
- [Big Questions from Little People (Gemma Elwin Harris, ed.)](https://www.penguin.co.uk/books/196284/big-questions-from-little-people-answered-by-some-very-big-people-by-harris-gemma-elwin/9780571288717) -- Experts answer children's philosophical questions in accessible language. Wonderful starting point for family philosophical discussions.
- [p4c.com -- Philosophy for Children Resources](https://p4c.com) -- Comprehensive resource hub for Philosophy for Children practitioners: lesson plans, stimuli, training materials, and research.
- [Plato Was Wrong! (A. C. Grayling)](https://www.acgrayling.com) -- Accessible philosophy writing for general audiences by a leading contemporary philosopher.

## For Deeper Study

Resources for advanced students and those pursuing philosophy at a higher level.

- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy (SEP)](https://plato.stanford.edu) -- The gold standard for philosophical reference. Free, comprehensive, peer-reviewed entries on every major philosophical topic. Written by experts, updated regularly.
- [Internet Encyclopedia of Philosophy (IEP)](https://iep.utm.edu) -- Peer-reviewed academic resource, complementary to SEP. Slightly more accessible writing style.
- [PhilPapers](https://philpapers.org) -- Comprehensive index and bibliography of philosophy research. Essential for anyone doing serious philosophical research.
- [Philosophy Bites (Podcast)](https://philosophybites.com) -- Short (15-20 minute) interviews with leading philosophers on specific topics. Over 400 episodes covering the full range of philosophy.
- [The Partially Examined Life (Podcast)](https://partiallyexaminedlife.com) -- In-depth philosophical discussions by philosophy enthusiasts. Accessible but substantive.
- [Think: A Compelling Introduction to Philosophy by Simon Blackburn](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/think-9780192854254) -- Clear, engaging introduction covering knowledge, mind, free will, God, ethics, and politics.
- [The Problems of Philosophy by Bertrand Russell](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/5827) -- Classic short introduction to philosophical problems. Free on Project Gutenberg. Still remarkably readable and thought-provoking.

## Online Courses & Platforms

Structured learning opportunities for those who want guided philosophical study.

- [Introduction to Philosophy (University of Edinburgh / Coursera)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/philosophy) -- Free university-level introduction covering epistemology, philosophy of science, philosophy of mind, political philosophy, moral philosophy, and metaphysics.
- [Justice (Harvard / edX)](https://www.edx.org/course/justice) -- Michael Sandel's famous course on justice and moral reasoning. Free. Excellent for high school and college students.
- [Khan Academy: Introduction to Philosophy](https://www.khanacademy.org/humanities/philosophy) -- Free philosophy lessons in Khan Academy's accessible format.
- [MIT OpenCourseWare: Philosophy](https://ocw.mit.edu/search/?q=philosophy) -- Free university-level philosophy course materials from MIT.

## Philosophy for Young Children

Specialized resources for introducing philosophy to the youngest learners (ages 4-8).

- [The If Machine by Peter Worley](https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/the-if-machine-9781472920102/) -- 30 philosophical session plans for children ages 5-11. Practical, classroom-tested, and engaging.
- [Thinking Together: Philosophical Enquiry for the Classroom by Paul Cleghorn](https://www.bloomsbury.com/uk/thinking-together-9781472918734/) -- Step-by-step guide to running philosophical enquiries with primary school children.
- [But Why? by Clive Gifford](https://www.quartoknows.com/) -- A book of philosophical questions for young readers, covering topics from truth and fairness to consciousness and beauty.
- [Thought Adventures (Sara Stanley & Steve Bowkett)](https://www.amazon.com/dp/1855390663) -- Activities and strategies for philosophical thinking with young children. Includes reproducible resources.

## Non-Western Philosophy

Resources for engaging with philosophical traditions beyond the Western canon.

- [Readings in Classical Chinese Philosophy (Ivanhoe & Van Norden)](https://www.hackettpublishing.com/readings-in-classical-chinese-philosophy-second-edition) -- Accessible translations of Confucius, Laozi, Mengzi, Zhuangzi, Xunzi, and Han Feizi.
- [An Introduction to Indian Philosophy (Sue Hamilton)](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/indian-philosophy/BB70F4C6CD58F0E5B35D9C3F9B4E0EE5) -- Clear introduction to Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain philosophical traditions.
- [African Philosophy: An Introduction (Samuel Oluoch Imbo)](https://www.rowman.com/ISBN/9780847688418/) -- Introduction to African philosophical thought: Ubuntu, communalism, and African approaches to ethics and metaphysics.
- [Indigenous Philosophies and Critical Education (George Sefa Dei, ed.)](https://www.peterlang.com/) -- Explores Indigenous ways of knowing and their philosophical foundations.
- [Philosophy in the Islamic World (Peter Adamson)](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/philosophy-in-the-islamic-world-9780199577491) -- Comprehensive introduction to the rich Islamic philosophical tradition.
