# PHILO-101: Philosophy -- Foundational Knowledge Pack

**Classification:** Specialized & Deepening
**Learning Domain:** Philosophical Inquiry
**Recommended Prior Knowledge:** CRIT-101, READ-101
**Enables:** THEO-101, LOG-101

---

## What This Pack Covers

Philosophy as the art of thinking well. Five modules guide learners from the youngest ages through college-level inquiry:

1. **Wonder & Questioning** -- The Socratic method, asking "why?", challenging assumptions, intellectual humility
2. **Logic & Reasoning** -- Valid arguments, deductive and inductive reasoning, common fallacies, argument evaluation
3. **Ethics & Values** -- Moral reasoning, ethical frameworks (consequentialism, deontology, virtue ethics), applied ethics
4. **Knowledge & Truth** -- Epistemology, evidence and justification, skepticism as a tool, philosophy of science
5. **Aesthetics & Meaning** -- Beauty, art, purpose, philosophy of mind, consciousness, the good life

## Who It's For

- **K-2:** Natural philosophers who ask "why?" -- this pack honors and develops that instinct
- **3-5:** Students ready to build arguments, explore moral dilemmas, and distinguish facts from opinions
- **6-8:** Developing thinkers who can study formal logic, ethical frameworks, and epistemology
- **9-12:** Advanced students engaging with philosophical traditions, applied ethics, and original argumentation
- **College+:** Students pursuing meta-philosophy, research traditions, and original philosophical inquiry

## How to Use It

Philosophy is best learned through *doing* -- discussing, questioning, arguing, reflecting -- not memorizing. Start with Module 1 (Wonder & Questioning) as the foundation, then follow whichever pathway suits the learner:

- **Linear:** M1 -> M2 -> M3 -> M4 -> M5
- **Ethics-First:** M1 -> M3 -> M2 -> M4 -> M5
- **Knowledge-Focused:** M1 -> M2 -> M4 -> M3 -> M5
- **Creative-Contemplative:** M1 -> M5 -> M3 -> M4 -> M2

## Key Principles

- **Philosophy is a practice, not a canon.** The goal is thinking well, not memorizing philosophers.
- **Every child is a philosopher.** This pack nurtures the questioning instinct children already have.
- **Process over answers.** Assessment values reasoning quality, not arriving at one "right" conclusion.
- **Global traditions.** Western, Eastern, African, and Indigenous philosophical traditions are all represented.
- **Discussion-driven.** Philosophy works best when people think together.
