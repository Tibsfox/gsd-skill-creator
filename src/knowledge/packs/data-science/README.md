# DATA-101: Data Science & Analysis — Foundational Knowledge Pack

Welcome to DATA-101, the foundational pack for data literacy and analysis.

## Quick Start

DATA-101 teaches students to read, work with, analyze, and argue with data. Rather than rote memorization of statistical formulas, we emphasize **reasoning with evidence, critical thinking, and responsible practice**.

The pack is organized in five modules:

1. **Data Collection & Organization** — Understanding where data come from and how to organize them
2. **Exploratory Data Analysis** — Summarizing, describing, and finding patterns in data
3. **Data Visualization & Communication** — Creating and interpreting graphs; telling stories with data
4. **Statistical Inference & Reasoning** — Reasoning about uncertainty, probability, and hypothesis testing
5. **Data Ethics & Real-World Applications** — Recognizing bias, privacy concerns, and responsible data use

## Key Features

### Hands-On Activities

50 activities grounded in real data and real questions:

- **Foundation (K-2):** Simple surveys, tally charts, comparing data sets
- **Elementary (3-5):** Collecting data, creating bar graphs, calculating mean and median
- **Middle School (6-8):** Designing surveys, exploring distributions, probability experiments
- **High School (9-12):** Hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, evaluating published research
- **College+:** Regression, machine learning, research ethics

### Real Data

Activities use or reference real data: weather data, census data, survey results, published research. Students work with authentic questions, not contrived examples.

### Critical Thinking

Assessment focuses on reasoning, not formula memorization. Can you ask a good question about data? Do you recognize potential bias? Can you explain findings to someone else?

### Ethical Reasoning

Module 5 explicitly addresses privacy, consent, algorithmic bias, and responsible data use. These are not optional add-ons — they are central to data literacy.

## Learning Outcomes

By the end of DATA-101, students can:

- **Collect data** thoughtfully, recognizing bias and quality issues
- **Explore data** using summary statistics and visualizations
- **Visualize data** clearly and critique misleading graphs
- **Reason statistically** about uncertainty, probability, and hypothesis testing
- **Address ethics** in data work: privacy, bias, fairness, and responsibility

## Grade Levels & Time

DATA-101 spans K-College with different depths at each level:

| Grade Band | Time | Focus |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Foundation (PreK-2) | 20-40 hours | Counting, simple tallies, comparing |
| Elementary (3-5) | 50-70 hours | Data collection, graphs, basic statistics |
| Middle School (6-8) | 70-100 hours | Distributions, probability, survey design |
| High School (9-12) | 100-150 hours | Hypothesis testing, confidence intervals, research |
| College+ | 150-200 hours | Advanced inference, programming, machine learning |

## Using DATA-101

### Start Here

1. Read **DATA-101-vision.md** for the pedagogical approach
2. Choose a **module** based on your students' interests or curriculum needs
3. Browse **DATA-101-activities.json** for hands-on activities
4. Use **DATA-101-assessment.md** to understand how to assess student learning

### Recommend Prerequisites

- **MATH-101** — Familiarity with numbers and operations
- **STAT-101** — If available, for foundation in probability (useful for Module 4)

### Follows Data Science Best Practices

- Start with data that answers real questions students care about
- Move from concrete examples (tallying votes) to abstract concepts (sampling distributions)
- Use technology (spreadsheets) to handle computation; focus on reasoning
- Emphasize the full cycle: ask → collect → explore → communicate → reflect

## Teaching Tips

### Make It Real

Use data from your students' lives: classroom surveys, school data, local data. Generic data sets are less engaging.

### Pair with Other Packs

- **MATH-101** — Quantitative foundation for all modules
- **CRIT-101** — Critical thinking complements skeptical data reasoning
- **READ-101** — Reading skills support interpreting research and news
- **STAT-101** — Advanced probability foundation for Module 4

### Build Slowly

Don't rush. Exploratory data analysis (Module 2) is harder than it looks — students may need multiple experiences with distributions before intuition develops. That's normal and expected.

### Highlight Uncertainty

Statistical reasoning is uncomfortable: we rarely know with certainty. Help students get comfortable with phrases like "The data suggest..." and "We can't rule out..." rather than confident claims.

## Assessment

Assessment in DATA-101 focuses on reasoning and communication:

**Formative:**
- Observe students as they work with data. Are they asking good questions? Considering limitations?
- Review artifact samples over time to see growth.
- Use think-aloud protocols to understand their reasoning.

**Summative:**
- **Data Exploration Project:** Given new data, students explore, visualize, find patterns, and present findings
- **Graph Critique:** Students identify what's misleading in a graph and redesign it
- **Hypothesis Test Report:** Students pose a question, collect or find data, test it, and explain results
- **Ethics Case Study:** Students identify ethical issues and propose responsible solutions

See **DATA-101-assessment.md** for rubrics.
