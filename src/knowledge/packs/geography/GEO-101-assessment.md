# GEO-101 Assessment Framework

## Overview

This assessment framework evaluates spatial thinking, systems understanding, and geographic reasoning -- not the memorization of place names, capitals, or dates. Progress is measured by the quality of spatial reasoning, not the quantity of geographic facts recalled. A learner who reads maps critically, connects Earth systems to human patterns, and reasons across scales is making progress -- regardless of whether they can name every country in Africa or recite the height of Mount Everest.

---

## Beginning

Learners at the Beginning level are developing foundational awareness of place, space, and the natural world. They notice features of their environment, create simple representations of familiar places, and are learning to connect weather to their daily experience.

**Earth Systems:**
- Notices obvious features of the landscape (hills, rivers, rocks, soil)
- Identifies basic weather conditions (sunny, rainy, snowy, windy)
- Recognizes that landscapes include both land and water features
- Understands that rocks, water, and air are parts of the Earth

**Human Geography:**
- Identifies different types of places (home, school, store, park)
- Recognizes that people live in different kinds of communities
- Notices that some areas are crowded and others are empty
- Understands that people need food, water, and shelter from their environment

**Maps & Spatial Thinking:**
- Draws simple maps of familiar places (bedroom, classroom, route to school)
- Understands concepts of near and far, above and below
- Uses simple directional language (left, right, up, down)
- Recognizes that maps represent real places

**Astronomy:**
- Observes the Sun, Moon, and stars in the sky
- Notices that the Sun appears to move across the sky during the day
- Recognizes that the Moon changes shape over time
- Understands the difference between day and night

**Climate & Weather:**
- Describes today's weather using basic vocabulary
- Recognizes seasonal patterns (it is colder in winter, warmer in summer)
- Understands that different places have different weather
- Connects weather to appropriate clothing and activities

## Developing

Learners at the Developing level are building systematic approaches to geographic observation and analysis. They read and create more detailed maps, understand basic Earth processes, recognize patterns in human settlement, and track celestial phenomena.

**Earth Systems:**
- Explains basic processes (water cycle, erosion, volcanic eruption) with diagrams
- Identifies major landform types and explains how they form
- Connects weather patterns to the water cycle
- Understands that Earth's surface is constantly changing through slow and fast processes

**Human Geography:**
- Explains why people settle near water, fertile land, and trade routes
- Identifies push and pull factors in simple migration scenarios
- Recognizes urban, suburban, and rural land use patterns
- Understands that cultures develop in response to their physical environment

**Maps & Spatial Thinking:**
- Reads maps using legend, scale, compass rose, and grid coordinates
- Locates places using latitude and longitude with guidance
- Understands that maps represent three-dimensional reality in two dimensions
- Creates maps with standard cartographic elements (title, legend, scale, orientation)

**Astronomy:**
- Explains day and night using Earth's rotation
- Tracks and records lunar phases over a full cycle
- Names the planets in order and understands relative distances
- Identifies several constellations and understands they appear to move through the year

**Climate & Weather:**
- Collects weather data systematically and creates simple graphs
- Distinguishes weather (daily conditions) from climate (long-term patterns)
- Identifies major climate zones on a world map
- Understands that temperature generally varies with latitude and altitude

---

## Proficient

Learners at the Proficient level demonstrate independent geographic thinking. They analyze Earth systems as interconnected processes, interpret human geography through spatial reasoning, use maps critically, understand astronomical phenomena through geometry, and evaluate climate evidence.

**Earth Systems:**
- Explains plate tectonics and connects it to the distribution of earthquakes and volcanoes
- Traces matter through the full rock cycle and connects it to landscape evolution
- Analyzes how changes in one Earth system (atmosphere, hydrosphere, lithosphere, biosphere) affect others
- Evaluates natural hazard risk based on geological and geographic evidence

**Human Geography:**
- Analyzes population distribution patterns using multiple geographic factors
- Applies push-pull frameworks to historical and contemporary migration
- Evaluates how urbanization creates both opportunities and challenges
- Reads cultural landscapes for evidence of historical and ongoing human modification

**Maps & Spatial Thinking:**
- Evaluates map projections for distortion and understands trade-offs
- Uses coordinate systems fluently and applies them to spatial problems
- Identifies and analyzes spatial patterns (clustering, dispersion, linear) in geographic data
- Applies basic GIS concepts to layer and query spatial information

**Astronomy:**
- Explains seasons, lunar phases, tides, and eclipses using orbital geometry
- Traces stellar evolution from nebula through main sequence to remnant
- Demonstrates accurate proportional reasoning about solar system scale
- Connects astronomical phenomena to observable effects on Earth

**Climate & Weather:**
- Explains atmospheric circulation patterns and their effects on climate zones
- Analyzes climate data using climographs and trend analysis
- Explains the greenhouse effect and traces carbon through the carbon cycle
- Evaluates evidence for climate change using scientific data and reasoning

---

## Advanced

Learners at the Advanced level demonstrate mastery of geographic analysis across all domains. They synthesize physical and human geography, apply spatial analysis to complex problems, engage with cutting-edge Earth and space science, and communicate geographic reasoning to diverse audiences.

**Earth Systems:**
- Synthesizes geological, atmospheric, hydrological, and biological processes into integrated Earth system models
- Evaluates geological evidence for deep time processes (continental drift, climate oscillation, mass extinction)
- Proposes evidence-based assessments of natural hazard risk for specific regions
- Teaches Earth system concepts to others effectively

**Human Geography:**
- Analyzes geopolitical issues through geographic reasoning about resources, borders, and power
- Evaluates urban systems using multiple models (concentric zone, sector, multiple nuclei)
- Synthesizes physical and human geography to explain regional development patterns
- Proposes geographic solutions to spatial problems (site selection, resource management, planning)

**Maps & Spatial Thinking:**
- Performs spatial analysis using GIS tools (buffer, overlay, proximity, interpolation)
- Evaluates maps as arguments, identifying bias, omission, and perspective
- Creates thematic maps that communicate complex spatial patterns effectively
- Applies critical cartography to question whose perspectives are represented and whose are excluded

**Astronomy:**
- Explains cosmological concepts (expanding universe, cosmic microwave background, nucleosynthesis)
- Evaluates planetary habitability criteria and applies them to exoplanet data
- Synthesizes astronomical timescales with geological and evolutionary timescales
- Communicates cosmic scale and significance to diverse audiences

**Climate & Weather:**
- Evaluates climate models, understanding assumptions, uncertainties, and projections
- Analyzes feedback loops (albedo, water vapor, carbon) in the climate system
- Proposes and evaluates evidence-based climate mitigation and adaptation strategies
- Synthesizes climate science with geographic, economic, and political analysis

## Formative Assessment

Formative assessment occurs during learning and provides feedback that guides improvement. In GEO-101, formative assessment focuses on the quality of spatial reasoning and geographic thinking, not the recall of geographic facts.

### Spatial Reasoning Checks

Observe learners during map and spatial activities. Look for:
- Do they orient maps correctly and use scale appropriately?
- Do they reason about direction, distance, and adjacency?
- Do they shift between different scales of analysis?
- Do they identify spatial patterns without prompting?
- Do they question map representations and projections?

### Systems Thinking Observations

During Earth systems activities, assess:
- Does the learner connect processes across different Earth spheres?
- Can they trace cause-effect chains (e.g., deforestation affects water cycle affects erosion)?
- Do they recognize feedback loops and unintended consequences?
- Can they predict how changing one variable affects the system?

### Geographic Inquiry Assessment

When learners conduct geographic investigations, evaluate:
- Is the geographic question spatial in nature (where, why there, what pattern)?
- Does the learner gather appropriate spatial data?
- Is the analysis geographically grounded (not just statistical)?
- Does the learner communicate findings with appropriate maps and visualizations?

### Observation Journals

Periodic journal entries where learners reflect on:
- What they noticed about spatial patterns in their daily life
- How their understanding of place and space has changed
- Connections they see between physical and human geography
- Questions they have about geographic phenomena

## Summative Assessment

Summative assessment evaluates cumulative geographic learning at the end of a unit or module. In GEO-101, summative assessment should demonstrate the ability to reason spatially, think in systems, and communicate geographic findings.

### Geographic Investigation Portfolio

A collection of work demonstrating growth in geographic thinking:

**Required components:**
- Maps created by the learner showing progression from simple to sophisticated cartographic skills
- A complete geographic investigation (question, data collection, spatial analysis, conclusion, reflection)
- An Earth systems analysis showing interconnections between at least two Earth spheres
- A spatial analysis of a human geography pattern with maps and data

**Optional components:**
- A critical evaluation of a map or spatial representation from the media
- A climate data analysis with climographs and trend interpretation
- A night sky observation journal with documented patterns
- A comparative geography study of two contrasting regions

### Capstone Projects

Projects that demonstrate integrated mastery across multiple modules:

1. **Geographic Investigation:** Identify a geographic question about your local area, collect spatial data through field observation, analyze patterns using maps and graphs, and present findings with cartographic visualizations and geographic reasoning.

2. **Earth Systems Case Study:** Select a natural event (volcanic eruption, flood, drought, hurricane) and analyze it as an Earth systems event: what physical processes caused it, how did it affect human populations, how was it connected to climate patterns, and what geographic factors determined its impact?

3. **Spatial Analysis Project:** Use GIS tools or manual mapping techniques to analyze a spatial problem: Where should a new park be located? Which areas are most vulnerable to flooding? How has land use changed over time? Present analysis with layered maps and spatial reasoning.

4. **Cosmic Context Report:** Create a presentation connecting Earth science to astronomical context: how does Earth's position in the solar system determine its habitability? How do stellar processes create the elements in Earth's crust? How does understanding cosmic scale change how we think about geographic problems?

## Portfolio Suggestions

### Foundation Level (K-2)
- Drawings of familiar places with basic map elements
- Weather observation charts with stickers or drawings
- "My neighborhood" map with landmarks labeled
- Sky watching journal with Moon and Sun observations

### Elementary Level (3-5)
- Maps with legend, scale, and compass rose
- Rock and mineral observation journals
- Weather data tables and simple graphs
- Cultural geography research presentations

### Middle School Level (6-8)
- Thematic maps using coordinate systems
- Earth systems interaction diagrams
- Climate zone research with climographs
- Human geography spatial analysis projects
- Night sky observation journals with pattern analysis

### High School Level (9-12)
- GIS-based spatial analysis projects
- Climate data analysis with statistical interpretation
- Geopolitical case studies using geographic reasoning
- Critical cartography evaluations of media maps
- Original geographic investigations with field data
