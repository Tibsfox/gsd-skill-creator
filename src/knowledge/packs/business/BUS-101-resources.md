# BUS-101: Business & Law -- Curated Resources

Resources are organized by audience and depth. All links verified as of February 2026.

## Foundational Texts

Essential reading for understanding business, economics, and legal education philosophy and practice.

- [Economics in One Lesson by Henry Hazlitt](https://fee.org/resources/economics-in-one-lesson/) -- Clear, concise introduction to economic thinking. Teaches the essential discipline of considering unseen effects and unintended consequences of economic decisions. Accessible to motivated high school readers.
- [Street Law: A Course in Practical Law](https://www.streetlaw.org) -- The standard textbook for teaching practical law to non-lawyers. Covers constitutional law, criminal law, consumer law, family law, and individual rights. Used in thousands of schools and community programs nationwide.
- [The Personal MBA by Josh Kaufman](https://personalmba.com/) -- Accessible overview of core business concepts without jargon or academic assumptions. Covers mental models for markets, products, finance, systems, and decision-making. Excellent foundation for self-directed business education.
- [Rich Dad Poor Dad by Robert Kiyosaki](https://www.richdadpoordad.com/) -- Personal finance classic that introduces the distinction between assets and liabilities, the importance of financial literacy, and the mindset shift from employee to entrepreneur. Read critically for its principles, not as prescriptive advice.

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, videos, and hands-on resources for students of all ages.

- [Khan Academy: Economics and Finance](https://www.khanacademy.org/economics-finance-domain) -- Free, comprehensive video lessons covering microeconomics, macroeconomics, finance, and capital markets. Self-paced with practice exercises. The best free resource for building economic and financial understanding.
- [Junior Achievement (JA)](https://www.juniorachievement.org) -- Volunteer-delivered programs in schools teaching financial literacy, work readiness, and entrepreneurship. Serves millions of students globally. Programs available for elementary through high school.
- [BizKids](https://bizkids.com) -- PBS-produced show teaching financial literacy and entrepreneurship to young people. Features real kid entrepreneurs running real businesses. Engaging video content free to stream online.
- [iCivics](https://www.icivics.org) -- Free educational games teaching civics, government, and legal concepts. Founded by retired Supreme Court Justice Sandra Day O'Connor. Excellent for middle and high school students learning about the legal system.
- [Investopedia Stock Simulator](https://www.investopedia.com/simulator/) -- Free virtual stock market where students can practice investing with no real money at risk. Learn how markets work, what affects stock prices, and the principles of diversification through experience.
- [SCORE Mentorship for Youth](https://www.score.org) -- Free mentoring from experienced business professionals available in person and online. Students working on business plans or entrepreneurial projects can get guidance from real business owners.

## For Parents and Mentors

Resources for adults supporting business and legal learning, even without business or legal backgrounds.

- [Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB) Educator Tools](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/educator-tools/) -- Free tools, lesson plans, and activity sheets for teaching financial literacy at every age. Produced by the federal agency responsible for consumer financial protection. Authoritative and practical.
- [EverFi Financial Literacy](https://everfi.com) -- Free digital financial literacy courses for K-12 students covering budgeting, banking, credit, and investing. Schools can enroll at no cost. Well-designed interactive modules.
- [Teach Children to Save (ABA)](https://www.aba.com/advocacy/community-programs/teach-children-to-save) -- American Bankers Association program with free resources and activities for teaching saving habits. Simple activities parents can use at home.
- [Money as You Grow](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/money-as-you-grow/) -- CFPB resource organizing age-appropriate financial milestones and activities. Tells parents what kids should understand about money at each age, with specific activities to build those skills.
- [Street Law Community Programs](https://www.streetlaw.org/programs) -- Legal education programs designed for community settings. Practical law education that does not require legal expertise to facilitate.

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for motivated high school students, college learners, and lifelong learners.

- [MIT OpenCourseWare: Principles of Economics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/economics/) -- Free college-level economics courses from MIT. Covers microeconomics, macroeconomics, and finance. Rigorous but accessible for motivated high school students.
- [Coursera: Business Foundations (Wharton)](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/wharton-business-foundations) -- University of Pennsylvania Wharton School business fundamentals. Covers marketing, accounting, operations, and finance. Free to audit, certificate available.
- [Legal Information Institute (Cornell Law)](https://www.law.cornell.edu) -- Free access to all federal and state laws, Supreme Court decisions, and legal encyclopedias. The best free legal research resource available. Essential for anyone doing legal analysis.
- [Small Business Administration (SBA) Learning Center](https://www.sba.gov/learning-center) -- Free courses and guides on starting and running a business. Covers business planning, financing, marketing, legal structure, and regulatory compliance.
- [Marginal Revolution University](https://mru.org) -- Free economics education platform created by Tyler Cowen and Alex Tabarrok. Short, clear video lessons on micro and macroeconomic concepts. Excellent for supplementing classroom learning.

## Standards and Frameworks

Educational standards documents that inform this pack's structure.

- [Council for Economic Education (CEE) National Standards](https://www.councilforeconed.org/national-standards/) -- The voluntary national content standards for economics education in K-12. Defines the 20 essential economic concepts every student should understand.
- [Jump$tart National Standards for Personal Finance Education](https://www.jumpstart.org/what-we-do/support-financial-smarts/national-standards/) -- Standards for K-12 personal finance education covering spending, saving, credit, investing, and risk management.
- [National Standards for Civics and Government](https://www.civiced.org/standards) -- Standards for civic education including the role of law, government, and citizenship. Relevant to the legal systems and constitutional rights content in this pack.
- [Common Core State Standards: Literacy in History/Social Studies](http://www.corestandards.org/ELA-Literacy/RH/introduction/) -- Reading and writing standards relevant to analyzing business cases, legal documents, and economic arguments.
