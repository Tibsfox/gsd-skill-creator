# WRIT-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Literature & Creative Writing knowledge pack. All resources selected for quality, accessibility, and alignment with deep reading, authentic writing, and literary thinking.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define the philosophy and pedagogy of literature and writing education.

- [In the Middle: A Lifetime of Learning About Writing and Reading](https://www.heinemann.com/products/in-the-middle-a-lifetime-of-learning-about-writing-and-reading/) by Nancie Atwell -- Foundational work on reader-writer workshops, showing how to build communities where reading and writing matter
- [The Art of Teaching Writing](https://www.heinemann.com/products/the-art-of-teaching-writing-second-edition/) by Lucy Calkins -- Process approach to writing instruction, emphasizing writing as thinking and discovery
- [You Can't Make This Stuff Up](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/You+Can%27t+Make+This+Stuff+Up%3A+The+Complete+Guide+to+Writing+Creative+Nonfiction-p-9780393080032/) by Lee Gutkind and Dinty W. Moore -- How to teach creative and authentic writing across forms
- [The Book Whisperer](https://www.scholastic.com/content/classroom/teaching-strategies/teaching-literacy/the-book-whisperer-lessons-in-reading-life.html) by Donalyn Miller -- Creating genuine reading lives, not compliance
- [Writing Territories](https://www.heinemann.com/products/writing-territories-teacher-practice-in-action/) by Penny Kittle -- Practical guidance on organizing reading and writing instruction
- [How to Read Literature Like a Professor](https://www.williammorrow.com/books/how-to-read-literature-like-a-professor-revised-edition/) by Thomas C. Foster -- Readable introduction to close reading and literary analysis

Interactive platforms, texts, and media for engaging with literature and writing.

- [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/) -- Community for readers to discover books, rate and review, connect with others

- [National Geographic Kids Stories](https://kids.nationalgeographic.com/stories) -- Age-appropriate narratives on science, exploration, and culture
- [Wattpad](https://www.wattpad.com/) -- Platform where writers share stories, poems, and creative work; readers can engage and provide feedback
- [Poetry Foundation](https://www.poetryfoundation.org/) -- Extensive poetry collection with extensive resources for teaching and learning
- [Scribd](https://www.scribd.com/) -- Digital library with access to books, audiobooks, magazines, and original writing

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for literary analysis and critical thinking.

- [Understanding Literary Interpretation](https://www.britannica.com/topic/literary-criticism) by Britannica -- Overview of literary criticism and interpretive approaches
- [Literary Theory: A Very Short Introduction](https://www.oup.com/academic/product/literary-theory-a-very-short-introduction-9780199685356) by Jonathan Culler -- Accessible introduction to critical frameworks
- [The Structure of Scientific Revolutions](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/S/bo13179781.html) by Thomas Kuhn -- Understanding paradigm shifts, applicable to literary movements
- [Close Reading: A Pocket Guide](https://www.oup.com/academic/product/close-reading-a-pocket-guide-9780190673475) by John Pennington -- Detailed guidance on close reading practices
- [Writing the Other](https://www.tor.com/books/writing-the-other-sf/) by Nisi Shawl and Cynthia Ward -- Writing with authenticity and avoiding stereotypes
- [JSTOR Daily](https://daily.jstor.org/) -- Access to scholarly articles about literature, culture, and ideas
- [The Millions](https://www.themillions.com/) -- Literary criticism and essays for thoughtful readers

## Recommended Books for Different Levels

### Foundation (K-2)
- *Where the Wild Things Are* by Maurice Sendak
- *Click, Clack, Moo* by Doreen Cronin
- *The Very Hungry Caterpillar* by Eric Carle
- *Charlotte's Web* by E.B. White (early elementary)
- *Ramona* series by Beverly Cleary

### Elementary (3-5)
- *Percy Jackson* series by Rick Riordan
- *Esperanza Rising* by Pam Muñoz Ryan
- *The Tale of Despereaux* by Kate DiCamillo
- *Pax* by Sara Pennypacker
- *Wings of Fire* series by Tui T. Sutherland

### Middle School (6-8)
- *The Outsiders* by S.E. Hinton
- *The Giver* by Lois Lowry
- *A Wrinkle in Time* by Madeleine L'Engle
- *Wonder* by R.J. Palacio
- *Monster* by Walter Dean Myers

### High School (9-12)
- *To Kill a Mockingbird* by Harper Lee
- *1984* by George Orwell
- *The Great Gatsby* by F. Scott Fitzgerald
- *Their Eyes Were Watching God* by Zora Neale Hurston
- *The House on Mango Street* by Sandra Cisneros

### Poetry Collections
- *Where the Sidewalk Ends* by Shel Silverstein
- *The Poet X* by Elizabeth Acevedo (contemporary verse novel)
- *Harlem* by Walter Dean Myers
- Collected poems by Emily Dickinson, Robert Frost, Langston Hughes
- Contemporary collections by Amanda Gorman, Ocean Vuong, Rupi Kaur
