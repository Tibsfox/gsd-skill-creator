# MFAB-101: Materials & Manufacturing

**Classification:** Core Academic
**Status:** Alpha
**Version:** 1.0.0

Understanding how materials are chosen based on properties and how objects are fabricated -- from artisanal craft to industrial processes. Material properties (strength, flexibility, conductivity, durability) determine what can be built and how.

## Modules

| ID | Module | Focus |
|----|--------|-------|
| M1 | Material Properties & Testing | Identifying and measuring material characteristics |
| M2 | Materials Families | Metals, polymers, ceramics, composites, natural materials |
| M3 | Fabrication & Manufacturing Processes | How things are made: cutting, joining, forming, casting, printing |
| M4 | Design for Manufacturing | Selecting materials and processes for a purpose under constraints |
| M5 | Sustainability & Lifecycle | Environmental impact, recycling, biodegradability, circular economy |

## Grade Levels

- **Foundation (PreK-2):** 25-40 hours -- Sorting, touching, naming materials, simple fabrication
- **Elementary (3-5):** 50-80 hours -- Property testing, material families, basic tool use
- **Middle School (6-8):** 80-120 hours -- Quantitative testing, manufacturing processes, DFM basics
- **High School (9-12):** 120-180 hours -- Material science fundamentals, advanced fabrication, LCA
- **College+ (13+):** 180-240 hours -- Characterization, process engineering, computational materials

## Prerequisites

- **Recommended:** SCI-101 (Science), ENGR-101 (Engineering & Design)
- **Required:** None -- material exploration starts with touching and sorting

## Core Philosophy

Everything we use is made of something, and that something was chosen for a reason. Material literacy means understanding what objects are made of, why those materials were chosen, how they were manufactured, and what happens at end-of-life. This pack teaches learners to read the physical world the way text literacy teaches them to read the written world.
