# STAT-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Accounting & Statistics knowledge pack. All resources are selected for quality, accessibility, and alignment with quantitative reasoning, financial literacy, and statistical thinking education.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define the philosophy and pedagogy of statistics and financial literacy education.

- [How to Lie with Statistics](https://wwnorton.com/books/How-to-Lie-with-Statistics/) by Darrell Huff -- The classic primer on statistical deception, misleading graphs, and critical evaluation of data claims. Essential reading for understanding how numbers can be manipulated.
- [Naked Statistics](https://wwnorton.com/books/Naked-Statistics/) by Charles Wheelan -- Engaging, accessible introduction to statistics that emphasizes intuition and real-world application over formulas and procedures.
- [The Signal and the Noise](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/305826/the-signal-and-the-noise-by-nate-silver/) by Nate Silver -- How prediction works and fails across domains from weather forecasting to election polling to financial markets. Demonstrates statistical thinking in action.
- [Accounting Made Simple](https://www.amazon.com/Accounting-Made-Simple-Explained-Pages/dp/0981454224) by Mike Piper -- Clear, concise introduction to accounting fundamentals for non-accountants. Demystifies financial statements in plain language.
- [The Richest Man in Babylon](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/183981/the-richest-man-in-babylon-by-george-s-clason/) by George S. Clason -- Timeless principles of personal finance told through parables. Accessible to middle school and older learners.
- [Thinking, Fast and Slow](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/313455/thinking-fast-and-slow-by-daniel-kahneman/) by Daniel Kahneman -- Nobel laureate's exploration of how cognitive biases affect probabilistic thinking and financial decisions.

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, courses, and media for learners at various levels.

- [Khan Academy Statistics & Probability](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/statistics-probability) -- Free, structured courses covering descriptive statistics, probability, random variables, and inference with practice exercises and progress tracking
- [Khan Academy Finance & Capital Markets](https://www.khanacademy.org/economics-finance-domain) -- Free courses on accounting, personal finance, interest, and investing with clear video explanations
- [Seeing Theory](https://seeing-theory.brown.edu/) -- Beautiful, interactive visualizations of probability and statistics concepts from Brown University. Makes abstract ideas tangible through manipulation and animation.
- [Practical Money Skills](https://www.practicalmoneyskills.com/) -- Free financial literacy curriculum from Visa with games, lesson plans, and interactive tools for K-12 learners
- [Investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com/) -- Comprehensive financial education platform with clear definitions, tutorials, simulators, and explainer articles on accounting, investing, and personal finance
- [Gapminder](https://www.gapminder.org/) -- Interactive data visualizations that challenge assumptions about the world. Excellent for developing data literacy and questioning preconceptions with real global data.
- [GeoGebra Probability Calculator](https://www.geogebra.org/probability) -- Free interactive tool for exploring probability distributions, computing probabilities, and visualizing statistical concepts
- [Next Gen Personal Finance](https://www.ngpf.org/) -- Free financial literacy curriculum with interactive activities, simulations, and case studies designed for middle and high school

## For Parents/Mentors

Guidance for adults facilitating statistics and financial literacy learning, regardless of their own background.

- [Jump$tart Coalition for Personal Financial Literacy](https://www.jumpstart.org/) -- National standards and resources for K-12 financial literacy education, including grade-level guides and best practices
- [NCTM Statistics and Probability Resources](https://www.nctm.org/Standards-and-Positions/Principles-and-Standards/Data-Analysis-and-Probability/) -- Standards-aligned materials for teaching data analysis and probability from the National Council of Teachers of Mathematics
- [Money as You Grow (CFPB)](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/consumer-tools/money-as-you-grow/) -- Age-appropriate financial milestones and conversation starters from the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
- [Council for Economic Education](https://www.councilforeconed.org/) -- Lessons, tools, and professional development for teaching economics and personal finance effectively
- [Steve Spangler Probability Activities](https://www.stevespanglerscience.com/) -- Engaging probability experiments and demonstrations with clear explanations for facilitating at home
- [Bedtime Math](https://bedtimemath.org/) -- Daily math and statistics puzzles for families, making quantitative thinking a natural part of everyday routine

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for learners pursuing statistics, probability theory, accounting, and financial analysis at college level and beyond.

- [OpenIntro Statistics](https://www.openintro.org/book/os/) -- Free, peer-reviewed statistics textbook suitable for AP Statistics and introductory college courses. Includes datasets, labs, and supplementary materials.
- [MIT OpenCourseWare: Probability and Statistics](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/mathematics/18-05-introduction-to-probability-and-statistics-spring-2014/) -- University-level course materials freely available, including lecture notes, problem sets, and exams
- [Coursera: Statistics with R (Duke University)](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/statistics) -- Modern statistical analysis using R, covering inference, regression, and Bayesian statistics
- [AICPA Resources for Students](https://www.aicpa.org/) -- Professional accounting standards, educational materials, and career guidance from the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants
- [R for Data Science](https://r4ds.had.co.nz/) by Hadley Wickham -- Modern data analysis tools and statistical computing for learners ready to use programming for statistical work
- [Against the Gods: The Remarkable Story of Risk](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Against+the+Gods%3A+The+Remarkable+Story+of+Risk-p-9780471295631) by Peter Bernstein -- History of probability theory and risk management, showing how statistical thinking transformed commerce, science, and society
- [AccountingCoach](https://www.accountingcoach.com/) -- Free, comprehensive accounting tutorials covering everything from basic bookkeeping through advanced financial analysis

## Interactive Tools & Simulators

Digital tools that support hands-on exploration of statistical and financial concepts.

- [Desmos Graphing Calculator](https://www.desmos.com/) -- Free graphing calculator for visualizing functions, plotting data, and exploring statistical relationships
- [StatKey](https://www.lock5stat.com/StatKey/) -- Free online tools for randomization and bootstrap methods, supporting modern statistics pedagogy
- [Compound Interest Calculator (Investor.gov)](https://www.investor.gov/financial-tools-calculators/calculators/compound-interest-calculator) -- Simple, authoritative compound interest calculator from the SEC for exploring growth over time
- [Random.org](https://www.random.org/) -- True random number generator for probability experiments and statistical sampling activities
- [Google Public Data Explorer](https://www.google.com/publicdata/directory) -- Access to large public datasets for statistical investigation projects with built-in visualization tools
