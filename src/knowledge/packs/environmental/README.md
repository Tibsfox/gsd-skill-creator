# ENVR-101: Environmental Science & Sustainability

## Quick Start

Welcome to ENVR-101, a foundational knowledge pack on environmental science and sustainability. This pack teaches evidence-based environmental reasoning: how to read data, understand Earth systems, evaluate claims critically, and make informed decisions.

## What You'll Learn

ENVR-101 spans five interconnected modules:

1. **Ecosystems & Biodiversity** — How living systems are organized, how energy flows, why biodiversity matters
2. **Earth Systems & Biogeochemical Cycles** — How water, carbon, and nitrogen cycle; how systems connect
3. **Human Impact & Environmental Health** — Sources of pollution, resource depletion, environmental justice
4. **Climate Science & Energy** — Greenhouse effect, climate evidence, energy tradeoffs and policy
5. **Sustainability & Action** — Designing sustainable systems, recognizing tradeoffs, proposing solutions

## Who Should Use This Pack

- **Grades K-2** — Observe nature, understand habitats, discover food relationships
- **Grades 3-5** — Build understanding of ecosystems and resource cycles
- **Grades 6-8** — Model Earth systems quantitatively, evaluate environmental claims
- **Grades 9-12** — Analyze climate science, energy policy, and complex environmental challenges
- **College** — Specialized research and integrative systems thinking

## Learning Activities

Each module includes concrete, hands-on activities using accessible materials:

- **Field work:** Backyard biodiversity surveys, water quality testing, pollution tracking
- **Experiments:** Water cycles in bags, greenhouse effect jars, energy audits
- **Games & models:** Food web card games, population simulations, climate data analysis
- **Design projects:** Sustainable communities, policy proposals, household energy audits

## No Special Equipment Needed

Activities use common household and classroom materials: jars, thermometers, magnifying glasses, rulers, graph paper, and recycled materials. No specialized lab equipment required.

## Key Concepts

**Systems thinking** — Understanding how parts interact and changes propagate

**Evidence evaluation** — Reading data, assessing credibility, recognizing uncertainty

**Quantitative reasoning** — Using numbers and models to understand processes

**Perspective-taking** — Acknowledging different values and legitimate tradeoffs

**Design thinking** — Proposing, testing, and iterating solutions

## A Note on Perspective

This pack teaches environmental science as a discipline, not as advocacy. It accurately presents evidence on ecosystems, pollution, climate, and sustainability. It acknowledges that people disagree on policy solutions because they hold different values — and that disagreement is legitimate.

The goal is to help learners read evidence, think critically, and make informed decisions.
