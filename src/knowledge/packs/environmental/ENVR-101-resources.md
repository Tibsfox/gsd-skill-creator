# ENVR-101 Resources & References

Curated external resources for teaching and learning environmental science. All resources are free, accessible, and well-established in environmental education.

---

## Foundational Texts & Educational Resources

### Learning Platforms

- [EPA Environmental Education Resources](https://www.epa.gov/students) — Official U.S. environmental education programs, classroom activities, environmental health information
- [Khan Academy - Natural Sciences](https://www.khanacademy.org/science) — Free video lessons on ecosystems, photosynthesis, cellular respiration, and energy
- [NOAA Education Resources](https://www.noaa.edu) — Ocean, atmosphere, and climate education materials from National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration

### Foundational Texts (for teachers/mentors)

- [Aldo Leopold - A Sand County Almanac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Sand_County_Almanac) — Classic essays on land conservation and ecological thinking
- [Rachel Carson - Silent Spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silent_Spring) — Foundational critique of pesticide impacts; sparked the environmental movement
- [Donella Meadows - Thinking in Systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thinking_in_Systems) — Systems thinking fundamentals (advanced)
- [E.O. Wilson - The Diversity of Life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Diversity_of_Life) — Biodiversity, extinction, and conservation

## For Learners: Data & Visualization

### Climate & Temperature Data

- [NASA Earth Observatory](https://earthobservatory.nasa.gov) — Satellite imagery and data on Earth systems, climate, land use, natural hazards
- [NOAA Climate Monitoring](https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/products/climate-data) — Global temperature records, sea level, ice extent, precipitation
- [Berkeley Earth](https://berkeleyearth.org) — Temperature analysis with interactive maps and uncertainty quantification
- [Global Carbon Project](https://www.globalcarbonproject.org) — CO₂ emissions by country and sector; historical data and projections
- [Our World in Data - Environment](https://ourworldindata.org/environment) — Interactive charts on climate, biodiversity, pollution, resources

### Biodiversity & Observation

- [iNaturalist](https://www.inaturalist.org) — Crowdsourced biodiversity observation; submit observations, get species identification
- [Merlin Bird ID](https://merlin.allaboutbirds.org) — AI-powered bird identification from photos or sounds
- [National Geographic Field Guides](https://www.nationalgeographic.com/animals/) — Species identification and ecology
- [Project eBird](https://ebird.org) — Global bird observation database; community science
- [Audubon](https://www.audubon.org) — Bird conservation, field guides, local birding groups

### Water & Ocean

- [NOAA Ocean Exploration](https://oceanexplorer.noaa.gov) — Ocean data, research, educational resources
- [USGS Water Resources](https://www.usgs.gov/mission-areas/water-resources) — Hydrological data, river conditions, groundwater
- [Aquatic Commons](https://aquaticcommons.org) — Open-access aquatic science literature

### Pollution & Environmental Health

- [EPA AirNow](https://www.airnow.gov) — Real-time air quality data and forecasts by region
- [WHO Air Quality Database](https://www.who.int/teams/environment-climate-change-and-health/air-quality-and-health) — Global air pollution data
- [USGS Pesticide and Contaminant Data](https://waterdata.usgs.gov/nwis/qw) — Water quality measurements nationwide

## For Deeper Study: Academic & Technical Resources

### Climate Science

- [IPCC Reports](https://www.ipcc.ch) — International Panel on Climate Change; comprehensive climate science synthesis
- [NASA GISS](https://www.giss.nasa.gov) — Goddard Institute for Space Studies; climate modeling and data
- [Skeptical Science](https://skepticalscience.com) — Climate science explanations for skeptical audiences; addresses common misconceptions
- [Carbon Brief](https://www.carbonbrief.org) — In-depth climate journalism and research analysis
- [Real Climate](https://www.realclimate.org) — Working climate scientists discussing climate science

### Ecosystems & Biodiversity

- [IUCN Red List](https://www.iucnredlist.org) — Global species threat assessment
- [Global Biodiversity Information Facility (GBIF)](https://www.gbif.org) — Aggregated biodiversity data from research institutions worldwide
- [World Wildlife Fund (WWF)](https://www.worldwildlife.org) — Conservation research and reports on ecosystem status
- [The Nature Conservancy - Conservation Science](https://www.nature.org/science) — Research on conservation effectiveness

### Sustainability & Solutions

- [David Mackay - Sustainable Energy Without the Hot Air](https://www.withouthotair.com) — Evidence-based energy analysis; quantifies renewable potential
- [Ellen MacArthur Foundation - Circular Economy](https://www.ellenmacarthurfoundation.org) — Circular economy concept and implementation
- [C2ES - Center for Climate and Energy Solutions](https://www.c2es.org) — Policy analysis on climate and energy solutions
- [World Bank - Environment](https://www.worldbank.org/en/topic/environment) — Global environmental data and development impact

### Data & Modeling

- [Google Earth](https://www.google.com/earth) — High-resolution satellite imagery; see land use change over time
- [Global Forest Watch](https://www.globalforestwatch.org) — Deforestation and forest monitoring data
- [Copernicus Climate Data](https://climate.copernicus.eu) — European climate monitoring data
- [NOAA Climate Prediction Center](https://www.cpc.noaa.gov) — Climate forecasts and monitoring

## Local & Community Resources

### Find These in Your Area

- Local naturalist groups (Audubon chapters, native plant societies)
- State parks and nature centers with educational programs
- University environmental programs and research
- Conservation nonprofits (local land trusts, river groups, wildlife centers)
- Science museums with environmental exhibits
- Community gardens and urban farms
- Water treatment plants and utility education programs

### Getting Involved

- Volunteer for monitoring programs (birds, water, invasive species)
- Participate in restoration work (trail building, native planting, invasive removal)
- Attend naturalist walks and field trips
- Take field courses at local colleges
- Connect online communities with local action (iNaturalist, eBird, etc.)

---

## Creating Your Own Resources

Don't forget: the best resources are often locally produced:

- Document your own biodiversity observations and share on iNaturalist
- Collect local water quality data and share with citizen science networks
- Document local environmental issues and solutions
- Interview local naturalists, conservation experts, and environmental justice advocates
- Map local food sources, energy systems, and waste pathways
