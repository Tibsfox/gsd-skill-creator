# MATH-101: Curated Resources

A collection of vetted external resources organized by category for learners, parents, and mentors working through the Mathematics Foundational Knowledge Pack.

---

## Foundational Texts

These texts provide the theoretical and pedagogical foundation for the approach taken in this pack.

- [How Children Learn Mathematics — Pamela Liebeck](https://www.routledge.com/) — Developmental progression of mathematical thinking from infancy through adolescence. Essential reading for understanding how learners build mathematical concepts.
- [Children's Mathematics: Cognitively Guided Instruction — Thomas Carpenter et al.](https://www.heinemann.com/) — Research-based framework for understanding children's mathematical thinking and using it to guide instruction.
- [Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning — Peter Brown et al.](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/) — Evidence-based learning strategies that apply directly to mathematics: spaced practice, interleaving, retrieval practice.
- [Mathematical Mindsets — Jo Boaler](https://www.youcubed.org/) — How beliefs about intelligence affect mathematical learning, and strategies for developing a growth mindset toward mathematics.
- [Adding It Up: Helping Children Learn Mathematics — National Research Council](https://nap.nationalacademies.org/) — Comprehensive review of research on mathematics learning, defining five strands of mathematical proficiency.

## For Learners (Elementary)

Interactive and engaging resources for young learners building foundational skills.

- [Khan Academy Early Math](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/early-math) — Free, structured lessons on counting, addition, subtraction, and basic geometry with practice exercises and progress tracking.
- [Beast Academy](https://beastacademy.com/) — Comic-based math curriculum for grades 2-5 with rich problem-solving and mathematical reasoning challenges.
- [Coolmath4Kids](https://www.coolmath4kids.com/) — Game-based exploration of number concepts, geometry, and basic operations for ages 3-12.
- [Math Playground](https://www.mathplayground.com/) — Logic games, word problems, and interactive math puzzles organized by grade level.
- [OpenStax Elementary Mathematics](https://openstax.org/) — Free, openly-licensed textbooks covering foundational arithmetic and early geometry.

## For Learners (Middle School and Above)

Resources that deepen understanding and build algebraic, geometric, and statistical reasoning.

- [3Blue1Brown — YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/3blue1brown) — Exceptional visual explanations of mathematical concepts from linear algebra to calculus, using beautiful animations.
- [Desmos Graphing Calculator](https://www.desmos.com/calculator) — Free interactive graphing tool for exploring functions, equations, and data visualization.
- [Desmos Classroom Activities](https://teacher.desmos.com/) — Curated interactive activities for algebra, geometry, and statistics that run in any browser.
- [GeoGebra](https://www.geogebra.org/) — Free dynamic mathematics software for geometry, algebra, calculus, and statistics with an extensive activity library.
- [OpenStax Algebra and Trigonometry](https://openstax.org/details/books/algebra-and-trigonometry-2e) — Free textbook covering algebra, functions, trigonometry, and analytic geometry.
- [Art of Problem Solving (AoPS)](https://artofproblemsolving.com/) — Mathematics education resources emphasizing problem-solving and mathematical creativity for motivated students.

## For Parents and Mentors

Resources for adults facilitating mathematical learning, especially those without formal math training.

- [YouCubed — Stanford University](https://www.youcubed.org/) — Research-based resources for parents and teachers on building positive mathematical mindsets and effective learning strategies.
- [NCTM Family Resources](https://www.nctm.org/Resources/For-Families/) — Grade-specific conversation starters, activity ideas, and guidance from the National Council of Teachers of Mathematics.
- [Bedtime Math](https://bedtimemath.org/) — Daily math problems designed to be fun conversation starters between parents and children at bedtime.
- [Math Before Bed](https://mathbeforebed.com/) — Visual prompts that spark mathematical conversations between parents and children.
- [How to Raise a Mathematician — Eugenia Cheng](https://www.basicbooks.com/) — Accessible philosophy of mathematical thinking for parents who want to support mathematical curiosity.

---

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for learners pursuing deeper mathematical understanding.

- [A Mathematician's Lament — Paul Lockhart](https://www.maa.org/) — Influential essay on the philosophy of mathematical education and the beauty of mathematical thinking.
- [What is Mathematics? — Richard Courant and Herbert Robbins](https://global.oup.com/) — Classic survey of mathematics from numbers to topology, accessible to motivated high school students and beyond.
- [Concrete Mathematics — Ronald Graham, Donald Knuth, Oren Patashnik](https://www.pearson.com/) — Discrete mathematics foundations with applications to computer science, covering sums, recurrences, number theory, and combinatorics.
- [Measurement — Paul Lockhart](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/) — Exploration of geometry and calculus through the lens of mathematical inquiry and discovery.
- [How Not to Be Wrong — Jordan Ellenberg](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/) — How mathematical thinking applies to everyday life, politics, and decision-making.

## Online Tools and Simulations

Interactive tools that support exploration across all four modules.

- [National Library of Virtual Manipulatives](http://nlvm.usu.edu/) — Java-based interactive manipulatives for number, algebra, geometry, measurement, and data analysis.
- [PhET Interactive Simulations](https://phet.colorado.edu/en/simulations/filter?subjects=math) — Free, research-based math and science simulations from the University of Colorado Boulder.
- [Polypad by Mathigon](https://mathigon.org/polypad) — Virtual manipulatives including algebra tiles, fraction bars, number lines, and geometric construction tools.
- [Wolfram Alpha](https://www.wolframalpha.com/) — Computational knowledge engine useful for checking calculations, graphing functions, and exploring mathematical concepts.

## Standards and Frameworks

Reference documents for alignment and scope.

- [Common Core State Standards for Mathematics](http://www.corestandards.org/Math/) — The mathematical standards framework used in most US states, organized by grade level and domain.
- [NCTM Principles and Standards for School Mathematics](https://www.nctm.org/) — Comprehensive mathematics education framework covering five content standards and five process standards.
- [Principles to Actions — NCTM](https://www.nctm.org/) — Research-based principles for effective mathematics teaching, including productive struggle and mathematical discourse.
