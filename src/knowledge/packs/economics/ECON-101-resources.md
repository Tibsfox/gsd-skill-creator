# ECON-101 Resources

A curated collection of resources for teaching and learning economics and personal finance. These resources support the five modules and provide real-world data, simulations, and expert guidance.

---

## Foundational Texts & References

### General Economics Education

- [Khan Academy Economics & Finance](https://www.khanacademy.org/economics-finance-domain) — Comprehensive video library covering microeconomics, macroeconomics, personal finance, and entrepreneurship across all levels. Free, high-quality, accessible.

- [Freakonomics](https://freakonomics.com) — Blog and podcast exploring economic thinking applied to everyday phenomena. Engaging, real-world focused. Great for sparking curiosity.

- [The Economy: Economics for Everyone](https://www.core-econ.org) — Free, open-source economics textbook emphasizing real-world applications and interactive simulations. Excellent supplementary text.

- [Capitalisn't Podcast](https://www.marketplace.org/shows/capitalisnt/) — Explores how capitalism is working and who it's working for. Nuanced, accessible, current.

### For Younger Learners (K-5)

- [The Story of Money](https://www.federalreserveducation.org/resources/resources-by-topic/money-and-banking) — From Federal Reserve Education. Visual stories about the history and functions of money.

- [Economics for Kids](https://www.iEcon.org) — Simple, illustrated explanations of economic concepts like scarcity, supply and demand, and money.

## For Learners: Interactive Tools & Simulations

### Markets & Supply/Demand

- [PhET Simulations: Trading Simulation](https://phet.colorado.edu) — Interactive marketplace where learners bargain, experience price changes, and understand market mechanisms. Free, in-browser.

- [Supply & Demand Simulator](https://www.learnbonds.com/supply-and-demand-simulator/) — Visualize shifts in supply and demand curves and see how prices change. Hands-on and visual.

### Personal Finance

- [iMoney: Financial Literacy Game](https://www.imoneyapp.com) — Interactive game where learners manage a budget, make financial decisions, and see consequences. Engaging and realistic.

- [GFLEC Money Smart](https://www.moneysmart.gov) — From the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau. Interactive tools for budgeting, managing debt, understanding credit, and more.

- [Mint.com Budgeting Tool](https://www.mint.com) — Real budgeting software (free or premium). Learners can use it for actual budget tracking, not just simulation.

### Investing & Market Data

- [Investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com) — Dictionary of financial terms, how-to guides, and articles. Searchable, comprehensive, accessible language.

- [Stock Market Simulator](https://www.stockmarketgame.org) — Learners invest virtual money in real stocks and compete with peers. Educational and engaging.

- [MarketWatch](https://www.marketwatch.com) — Real-time market data, stock quotes, and financial news. Good for current events and data analysis.

### Macroeconomics & Data

- [FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data)](https://fred.stlouisfed.org) — Comprehensive database of U.S. economic data. Filter by indicator, download data, create charts. Essential for analysis.

- [World Bank Data](https://data.worldbank.org) — Global economic data across countries. Compare economies, track trends, analyze development.

- [Gapminder](https://www.gapminder.org) — Interactive visualizations of global data (income, health, population). Beautiful and intuitive. Excellent for systems thinking.

Guides for having productive conversations.

- [NerdWallet](https://www.nerdwallet.com) — Comprehensive guides on credit, debt, mortgages, investing, insurance, and more. Written in accessible language.

- [Suze Orman's Financial Library](https://www.suzeorman.com/topics/financial-resources) — Personal finance advice, books, and resources from a well-known financial educator.

- [BrightScope Retirement Plans Database](https://www.brightscope.com) — Information about different retirement plan types and how they work.

### Parents' Financial Literacy

- [Get Rich Slowly](https://www.getrichslowly.org) — Blog and resources on building wealth, managing debt, and making smart financial decisions.

- [The Balance](https://www.thebalance.com) — Articles on personal finance, investing, banking, and money management for everyday people.

## For Deeper Study: Advanced Resources

### Economics Research & Analysis

- [NBER (National Bureau of Economic Research)](https://www.nber.org) — Peer-reviewed economic research papers. Advanced but important for evidence-based policy discussion.

- [Project MUSE: Economics Journals](https://muse.jhu.edu) — Access to academic economics journals. For advanced learners and educators doing deeper research.

- [SSRN (Social Science Research Network)](https://www.ssrn.com) — Preprints of economic and social science research. Often free and more current than published papers.

### Policy Analysis & Institutions

- [Congressional Research Service Reports](https://crsreports.congress.gov) — Balanced analysis of economic and policy issues prepared for Congress. Accessible and evidence-based.

- [Center for Economic and Policy Research (CEPR)](https://cepr.net) — Economic research and policy analysis with a focus on distributional effects and equity.

- [The Economist Magazine](https://www.economist.com) — Weekly publication analyzing global economic news and trends. Excellent for current events.

### Behavioral Economics

- [Daniel Kahneman's Work](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Kahneman) — Nobel Prize-winning economist on how humans actually make decisions (irrationally, systematically). Start with "Thinking, Fast and Slow."

- [APA: Behavioral Economics](https://www.apa.org/science/about/psa/behavioral-economics) — Resources on behavioral economics from the American Psychological Association.

### Environmental & Sustainable Economics

- [Our World in Data: Economics](https://ourworldindata.org/economics) — Data and research on economic development, inequality, and sustainability.

- [The Economics of Ecosystems and Biodiversity](https://www.ecoeco.org) — Research on valuing environmental goods and services.

## How to Use These Resources

**For Lesson Planning:**
- Use Khan Academy videos to supplement lessons or for learner self-study
- Pull data from FRED or World Bank for analysis activities
- Reference policy analysis from Congressional Research Service or CEPR

**For Learner Research:**
- Direct learners to Investopedia for definitions and concepts
- Use Gapminder for systems-thinking visualizations
- Have learners analyze real market data from MarketWatch or FRED

**For Current Events:**
- Clip news articles from Reuters, Bloomberg, or CNBC
- Share Marketplace podcast episodes
- Relate economic concepts to headlines

**For Differentiation:**
- Struggling learners: Khan Academy, simple simulations, visual tools
- Advanced learners: NBER papers, policy analysis, real data projects
