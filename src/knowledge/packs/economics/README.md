# ECON-101: Economics & Personal Finance

## Quick Start

**ECON-101** teaches economic thinking and personal financial literacy for learners from Foundation through College. It's practical, grounded in real-world decision-making, and designed to help young people understand how markets work and make sound financial choices.

### What's In This Pack

- **ECON-101-vision.md** — The pedagogical vision, problem statement, core concepts, and implementation approach
- **ECON-101-modules.yaml** — Definition of 5 modules with learning outcomes, topics, and activities
- **ECON-101-activities.json** — 10 concrete learning activities (2 per module)
- **ECON-101-assessment.md** — Assessment framework and rubric levels
- **ECON-101-resources.md** — Curated external resources for learning and teaching

### The Five Modules

1. **M1: Scarcity & Economic Thinking** — Why choices matter and what opportunity cost means
2. **M2: Markets & Exchange** — How supply and demand set prices and coordinate behavior
3. **M3: Money & Financial Systems** — History of money, banking, interest, and credit
4. **M4: Personal Financial Literacy** — Budgeting, saving, borrowing, investing, and financial decisions
5. **M5: Macroeconomics & Civic Economics** — GDP, inflation, policy, trade, and economic systems

### Key Features

- **Phenomena-first pedagogy** — Start with real situations, then build understanding
- **Personal relevance** — Activities use learners' own lives, families, and communities
- **Systems thinking** — Help learners see how individual incentives create patterns
- **Evidence-based** — Ground discussions in data and economic reasoning
- **Multi-age adaptable** — Same modules work for K-2, 3-5, 6-8, 9-12, and college

### Getting Started

**For educators:**
1. Read ECON-101-vision.md to understand the pedagogical approach
2. Choose modules appropriate for your learners' age and interests
3. Use activities from ECON-101-activities.json directly or as templates
4. Refer to ECON-101-assessment.md for grading rubrics and feedback strategies

**For parents/mentors:**
1. See the "Parent Guidance" section in ECON-101-vision.md
2. Involve your learner in family financial decisions (age-appropriately)
3. Discuss economic news and real-world prices
4. Play economic games (trading, investing simulations)

**For learners:**
1. Start with Module 1 (Scarcity) or a module matching your interests
2. Complete activities; they're designed to be engaging and practical
3. Apply what you learn to your own financial decisions
4. Ask questions about prices, markets, and money you encounter daily

### Learning Outcomes

By the end of ECON-101, learners will:

- Identify and articulate opportunity costs in personal decisions
- Apply supply and demand reasoning to understand real-world prices
- Calculate interest and compare borrowing costs
- Create a personal budget and make intentional financial decisions
- Measure and interpret macroeconomic indicators
- Evaluate policy proposals using economic reasoning and evidence

### Implementation Tips

- **Use current events** — Bring in recent news about prices, employment, inflation
- **Ground in local context** — Examine your community's local economy
- **Connect to learners' interests** — Economics applies to sports contracts, college costs, career choices, housing
- **Emphasize reasoning over memorization** — Economics is about thinking, not terminology
- **Acknowledge privilege** — Not all learners face identical economic circumstances
