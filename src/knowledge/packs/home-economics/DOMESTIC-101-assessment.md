# DOMESTIC-101 Assessment Framework

## Overview

This assessment framework evaluates practical competency: can you actually DO it? Not just describe it, not just explain the theory -- do it. Can you cook a safe meal? Can you sew a button that stays on? Can you create and follow a budget? Can you clean a bathroom? Can you fix a leaky faucet?

The proof of domestic skills is in the doing. A slightly crooked hem that holds is a success. A meal that is over-seasoned but safely prepared is a success. A budget that is approximately right but consistently used is a success. We celebrate doing over knowing, trying over avoiding, and progress over perfection.

---

## Beginning

Learners at the Beginning level are developing awareness of domestic skills and their importance. They notice what needs doing, ask questions, and are learning the basic vocabulary and safety requirements of household tasks.

**Kitchen Skills & Cooking:**
- Recognizes that food must be prepared and that there are safety rules
- Washes hands before handling food (with reminders)
- Can identify basic kitchen tools (spoon, bowl, cup, plate)
- Follows one-step instructions with guidance ("stir this," "pour that")
- Interested in what adults are doing in the kitchen

**Textiles, Clothing & Care:**
- Notices that clothes get dirty and need washing
- Sorts clothes into basic piles with guidance (light and dark)
- Attempts to fold simple items (towels, washcloths)
- Recognizes buttons and zippers as fasteners
- May be curious about sewing but has not yet tried

**Home Care & Organization:**
- Knows that spaces should be cleaned up
- Puts away belongings with reminders
- Helps with simple tasks (carrying items, holding dustpan)
- Beginning to develop routine habits (making bed with help)
- Notices when a space is messy

**Money Management:**
- Recognizes coins and bills as money
- Understands that things cost money
- Beginning to distinguish wants from needs
- May save coins but does not yet understand saving strategically
- Notices prices and that different things cost different amounts

**Self-Sufficiency & Practical Skills:**
- Curious about tools and how things work
- Notices when things are broken
- Asks adults for help with everything
- Beginning to understand that some things can be fixed
- Interested in nature and growing things

---

## Developing

Learners at the Developing level are building real skills. They can perform basic domestic tasks with guidance, understand the processes involved, and are gaining confidence through practice.

**Kitchen Skills & Cooking:**
- Follows simple recipes with help (reading steps, measuring ingredients)
- Practices food safety consistently (handwashing, clean surfaces, allergen awareness)
- Uses basic cooking tools (measuring cups, mixing bowls, spatulas)
- Prepares simple items independently (sandwiches, salads, smoothies)
- Beginning to understand meal planning basics (variety, nutrition, budget)

**Textiles, Clothing & Care:**
- Threads a needle with some difficulty
- Sews a button with guidance (may need help with tension and knots)
- Sorts laundry correctly by color and basic fabric type
- Reads care labels with reference chart
- Folds most garment types competently
- Beginning to recognize when clothes need mending

**Home Care & Organization:**
- Cleans personal space independently (tidying, wiping surfaces)
- Follows cleaning instructions for different areas
- Developing regular cleaning habits with reminders
- Organizes personal belongings in a basic system
- Understands cleaning product categories and basic safety

**Money Management:**
- Creates a simple budget with guidance (income, expenses, saving)
- Understands the concept of saving for a goal
- Compares prices of two similar items
- Recognizes that spending choices have trade-offs
- Beginning to understand the concept of interest

**Self-Sufficiency & Practical Skills:**
- Identifies common household tools by name and purpose
- Uses basic tools with supervision (screwdriver, measuring tape)
- Follows repair instructions with guidance
- Participates in gardening activities (planting, watering)
- Understands what an emergency kit contains and why

---

## Proficient

Learners at the Proficient level demonstrate solid, grade-appropriate competency in domestic skills. They can perform tasks independently, make good decisions, and apply their skills to real-life situations.

**Kitchen Skills & Cooking:**
- Prepares complete meals independently with appropriate complexity for age
- Plans meals for several days considering nutrition, budget, time, and preferences
- Adapts recipes for available ingredients, dietary needs, or desired portions
- Practices food safety consistently and automatically
- Shops for groceries with a list, comparing prices and quality
- Understands and respects diverse food traditions

**Textiles, Clothing & Care:**
- Sews buttons, hooks, and snaps securely and independently
- Mends small tears and holes using appropriate techniques
- Completes the full laundry cycle: sort, wash, dry, fold, put away
- Reads and follows care labels without reference chart
- Treats common stains with appropriate methods
- Makes informed clothing purchase decisions (quality, need, value)

**Home Care & Organization:**
- Cleans different rooms thoroughly using appropriate methods
- Follows a regular cleaning routine without reminders
- Organizes shared and personal spaces with practical systems
- Performs basic maintenance tasks (changing bulbs, tightening hardware)
- Understands cleaning product safety and chooses appropriate products
- Contributes to household responsibilities as a full participant

**Money Management:**
- Creates and maintains a realistic budget independently
- Saves regularly toward defined goals
- Calculates unit prices and compares value across options
- Understands how credit and interest work (conceptually and mathematically)
- Identifies predatory financial practices and marketing tactics
- Plans financially for near-term goals with a timeline

**Self-Sufficiency & Practical Skills:**
- Uses household tools safely and correctly
- Performs common repairs independently (tightening, patching, unclogging)
- Evaluates repair vs. replace decisions with reasoning
- Grows food in available space and maintains the garden
- Has assembled and maintains an emergency kit
- Approaches household problems with a "can I fix this?" mindset

---

## Advanced

Learners at the Advanced level demonstrate mastery that extends beyond personal competency into teaching, innovation, and integration across domains.

**Kitchen Skills & Cooking:**
- Plans and executes meals for multiple people considering complex constraints
- Cooks from diverse cuisines and adapts techniques to available resources
- Teaches cooking skills to others effectively
- Integrates nutrition science, budgeting, and cultural knowledge into food choices
- May develop a personal food philosophy informed by evidence and experience
- Manages food for a household including shopping, storage, preparation, and waste reduction

**Textiles, Clothing & Care:**
- Performs alterations and adjustments (hemming, taking in, letting out)
- Uses a sewing machine for basic projects
- Makes informed decisions about sustainable fashion and textile lifecycle
- Teaches sewing and mending skills to others
- Manages a complete wardrobe with minimal waste and maximum utility

**Home Care & Organization:**
- Manages a complete household: cleaning, maintenance, seasonal tasks, shared responsibilities
- Creates and implements systems for organization, maintenance, and improvement
- Performs intermediate repairs and knows when to call a professional
- Teaches household skills to others
- Evaluates and improves household systems over time

**Money Management:**
- Manages real finances independently: budgeting, saving, spending, investing basics
- Understands and uses financial products wisely (bank accounts, insurance, retirement)
- Creates long-term financial plans with realistic goals and timelines
- Teaches financial literacy to others
- Makes values-aligned financial decisions with full understanding of trade-offs

**Self-Sufficiency & Practical Skills:**
- Handles most common household repairs and maintenance independently
- Makes informed decisions about DIY vs. professional service
- Practices sustainable living through repair, reuse, gardening, and preservation
- Mentors others in practical skills and problem-solving
- Integrates self-sufficiency across all domestic domains

## Formative Assessment (Ongoing)

Formative assessment happens continuously, informing instruction and supporting learning.

### Practical Demonstration
- Can the learner perform the skill when asked?
- Is the result functional (even if not perfect)?
- Does the learner follow safety protocols?
- Is the learner improving over time?

### Planning & Organization
- Does the learner plan before acting (meal plans, budgets, cleaning schedules)?
- Are plans realistic and followed?
- Does the learner adjust plans when circumstances change?

### Problem-Solving
- When something goes wrong (burned food, stain, broken item), how does the learner respond?
- Does the learner attempt to solve problems before asking for help?
- Does the learner learn from mistakes and adjust approach?

### Safety Awareness
- Does the learner consistently follow safety practices?
- Can the learner identify hazards (knife safety, cleaning product dangers, financial risks)?
- Does the learner adapt safety practices to new situations?

### Real-Life Application
- Does the learner use skills outside of class (cooking at home, doing own laundry)?
- Does the learner take initiative on household tasks?
- Does the learner make decisions informed by what they have learned (price comparison, repair assessment)?

## Summative Assessment (End of Module/Unit)

Summative assessment evaluates what learners have learned after instruction.

### Portfolio
- Compilation of practical work: photos of meals cooked, mended items, cleaned spaces, budget spreadsheets, garden progress
- Learner self-selection of best work showing growth
- Written reflection: "What skills did I learn? What am I most proud of? What do I want to improve?"
- Demonstrates growth from beginning to proficient

### Practical Skills Test
- Cook a simple meal safely and independently
- Sew a button that stays securely attached
- Clean a designated area to standard
- Create a monthly budget for a given scenario
- Perform a basic repair task

### Life Skills Project
- Plan and execute a multi-day project integrating multiple modules:
  - Plan meals for a week, shop within budget, cook at least one meal
  - Create a cleaning and maintenance schedule for a living space
  - Build a personal budget for one month
  - Complete at least one repair or mending task
- Document the process and reflect on learning

### Teaching Demonstration
- Teach a domestic skill to another person (peer, younger student, family member)
- Demonstrate clear instruction, safety guidance, and patience
- Evaluate the learner's ability to perform the skill after instruction

### Emergency Preparedness Plan
- Create a comprehensive emergency plan for a household
- Include communication plan, supply kit, evacuation routes, and important documents
- Explain reasoning for each component

## Assessment Principles

### What We Value
- Practical competency: can you do it?
- Safety awareness: do you do it safely?
- Growth over time: are you improving?
- Problem-solving: can you figure it out?
- Real-life application: do you use it?
- Initiative: do you take responsibility?

### What We Do Not Measure
- Perfection (functional is the standard, not flawless)
- Speed (competency matters more than quickness)
- Conformity (there are many right ways to cook, clean, organize)
- Home circumstances (we assess skill, not privilege)
- Gender-based expectations (everyone learns everything)

### Avoiding Harm
- Never assume family financial situation
- Never compare learners' homes or living situations
- Respect diverse cultural practices and traditions
- Accommodate food allergies, dietary restrictions, and physical abilities
- Avoid gendered language or expectations
- Support learners from all family structures and circumstances

---

## Differentiation

### For Younger Learners (K-2)
- Focus on helping, observing, and developing habits
- Use play-based activities (pretend kitchen, sorting games)
- Keep tasks short and concrete
- Celebrate effort and participation
- Work alongside adults at all times

### For Elementary Learners (3-5)
- Introduce real skills with supervision
- Provide clear step-by-step instructions
- Allow for repetition and practice
- Connect to other subjects (math for measurement, science for cooking)
- Build confidence through achievable challenges

### For Middle School (6-8)
- Expect increasing independence
- Introduce more complex tasks (complete meals, full laundry, budgeting)
- Discuss real-world applications (apartment living, shared responsibility)
- Integrate critical thinking (consumer decisions, repair evaluation)
- Encourage teaching skills to younger students

### For High School (9-12)
- Real-world scenarios (apartment budgets, household management)
- Full independence in skill execution
- Complex projects (week-long meal planning, financial planning)
- Integrate across domains (cooking + budgeting + nutrition)
- Prepare for independent living

## Self-Assessment & Reflection

Throughout the unit, learners reflect on their own learning:

- "What domestic skills am I most confident in?"
- "What skill do I want to improve most?"
- "When have I used what I learned outside of class?"
- "What did I learn that surprised me?"
- "What would I teach someone else?"
- "How do these skills connect to the life I want to live?"

Reflection builds metacognition and ownership of learning.

---

## Family Connection

Assessment extends beyond the classroom. Family engagement supports skill development:

- Practice cooking together at home
- Involve learners in laundry, cleaning, and maintenance
- Discuss family budgeting and financial decisions openly
- Fix things together when possible
- Share family traditions and practical wisdom

---

## Final Note

The goal of DOMESTIC-101 assessment is not compliance or perfection. It is developing practical competency, safety awareness, and the confidence that comes from knowing you can take care of yourself. Assessment should support that goal through encouragement, honest feedback, and celebration of growth. Every meal cooked, button sewn, room cleaned, budget followed, and item repaired is a step toward independent, capable living.
