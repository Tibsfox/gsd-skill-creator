## About DOMESTIC-101

**Why home economics?**

The skills you actually need to live well -- cooking a meal, keeping a clean home, managing money, mending clothes, and planning for the future. Schools have largely abandoned these subjects, leaving young adults to figure out the basics of independent living on their own.

DOMESTIC-101 creates practical competency in:
- **Kitchen Skills & Cooking** -- from boiling water to meal planning and cultural foodways
- **Textiles, Clothing & Care** -- sewing, mending, laundry, wardrobe management
- **Home Care & Organization** -- cleaning, organizing, basic maintenance, seasonal tasks
- **Money Management** -- budgeting, saving, credit, debt avoidance, financial planning
- **Self-Sufficiency & Practical Skills** -- repairs, gardening, emergency preparedness, resourcefulness

## Gender-Neutral Philosophy

DOMESTIC-101 explicitly rejects gendered domestic expectations. Every skill is taught to every learner. Boys cook. Girls use tools. Everyone budgets, cleans, sews, and plans. These are not women's skills or men's skills. They are human skills.

## How to Use This Pack

### Option 1: Full Curriculum
Teach all 5 modules over a semester or year. Modules M1--M4 are independent entry points; M5 integrates skills from earlier modules.

### Option 2: Integrated
Pull individual activities into existing classes (math for budgeting, science for cooking, health for nutrition).

### Option 3: Focused Unit
Choose the module that addresses the most pressing need.

**Example sequences:**
- **Grades K--2:** M1 (kitchen basics) + M3 (cleaning and helping)
- **Grades 3--5:** M1 + M2 + M3 + M4 (build foundational skills)
- **Grades 6--8:** All 5 modules (developing independence)
- **High School:** All 5 modules with real-world application (apartment budgets, meal planning, maintenance)
- **College:** All 5 modules as preparation for independent living

## Assessment Philosophy

Assessment evaluates practical competency: can you actually DO it? A slightly crooked hem that holds is a success. A meal that is over-seasoned but safe is a success. A budget that is approximate but consistently used is a success. We celebrate doing over knowing and progress over perfection.

### Key Principles
- **Learn by doing:** Hands-on practice, not worksheets
- **Gender-neutral:** Every skill for every learner
- **Safety first:** Age-appropriate safety for all activities
- **Celebrate competency:** Progress over perfection
- **Real-life application:** Homework is cooking dinner, not filling in blanks
