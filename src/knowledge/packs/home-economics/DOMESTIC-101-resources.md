# DOMESTIC-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Home Economics & Life Skills knowledge pack. All resources are selected for quality, accessibility, practical application, and gender-neutral presentation. These skills are for everyone.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define the philosophy and practice of life skills education.

- [Home Comforts: The Art and Science of Keeping House](https://www.cherylmendelson.com/) by Cheryl Mendelson -- Comprehensive reference covering all aspects of household management: cleaning, laundry, cooking, organizing. Treats homemaking as a legitimate and satisfying body of knowledge.

- [Salt, Fat, Acid, Heat: Mastering the Elements of Good Cooking](https://www.saminnosrat.com/pages/books) by Samin Nosrat -- Teaches the fundamentals of cooking through understanding four essential elements. Combines science with cultural food wisdom.

- [The Total Money Makeover](https://www.ramseysolutions.com/) by Dave Ramsey -- Straightforward approach to personal finance: budgeting, debt elimination, saving. Popular entry point for financial literacy.

- [Make Do and Mend](https://www.iwm.org.uk/) -- World War II resource on repairing and extending the life of clothing and household items. Historical perspective on resourcefulness.

- [The Art of Fixing Things](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/25665178-the-art-of-fixing-things) by Lawrence Pierce -- Practical guide to common household repairs written for people with no experience.

## For Learners (Interactive & Video)

Platforms and resources designed for student engagement with life skills.

- [America's Test Kitchen Kids](https://www.americastestkitchen.com/kids) -- Science-based cooking for kids: recipes tested by kids, step-by-step instructions, cooking science explanations. Age-appropriate and engaging.

- [iFixit Repair Guides](https://www.ifixit.com/) -- Free, community-written repair guides for electronics, appliances, clothing, and household items. Teaches the repair mindset with clear instructions and photos.

- [Khan Academy Personal Finance](https://www.khanacademy.org/college-careers-more/personal-finance) -- Free courses covering budgeting, saving, credit, insurance, taxes, and investing. Clear explanations with practice exercises.

- [Crash Course: Economics](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL8dPuuaLjXtPNZwz5_o_5uirJ8gQXnhEO) -- Engaging video series covering economic thinking, personal finance, and consumer decisions. Well-produced and accessible.

- [Ready.gov Kids](https://www.ready.gov/kids) -- Emergency preparedness education for children and teens. Games, activities, and family planning tools from FEMA.

- [MyMoney.gov](https://www.mymoney.gov/) -- U.S. government financial literacy resources: earning, saving, protecting, spending, and borrowing. Age-appropriate content for teens and adults.

- [Tasty Cooking Videos](https://tasty.co/) -- Short, engaging cooking demonstration videos with step-by-step instructions. Good for visual learners.

- [YouTube: Dad, How Do I?](https://www.youtube.com/@DadhowdoI) -- Practical life skills videos: basic repairs, car maintenance, cooking, tying a tie. Created by a father for people who never had someone to teach them.

## For Parents & Mentors

Guidance for adults facilitating life skills learning at home.

- [Life Skills That Matter](https://www.lifeskillsthatmatter.com/) -- Resources for teaching practical skills to children: cooking, cleaning, organizing, budgeting. Organized by age group.

- [FamilyMint](https://www.familymint.com/) -- Free online tool for teaching kids about money management through virtual banking, budgeting, and goal setting.

- [Ellyn Satter Division of Responsibility](https://www.ellynsatterinstitute.org/how-to-feed-children/) -- Framework for feeding children: parents decide what, when, where; children decide whether and how much. Supports healthy food relationships.

- [NASAFACS Family & Consumer Sciences](https://www.nasafacs.org/) -- National standards and resources for family and consumer sciences education. Curriculum frameworks for all ages.

- [Council for Economic Education](https://www.councilforeconed.org/) -- Resources for teaching economics and personal finance at all grade levels. Lesson plans, activities, and assessments.

- [Repair Cafe Foundation](https://www.repaircafe.org/en/) -- International movement connecting people who need repairs with volunteers who have repair skills. Start or attend a repair cafe in your community.

- [Extension Service (USDA)](https://www.extension.org/) -- Cooperative extension programs offering free resources on cooking, food preservation, gardening, financial management, and home maintenance. Local offices throughout the United States.

## For Deeper Study (High School & College)

Advanced resources for learners pursuing life skills mastery.

- [The Joy of Cooking](https://www.thejoycooking.com/) by Irma Rombauer -- Comprehensive cooking reference covering techniques, recipes, and food science. A classic that belongs in every kitchen.

- [Personal Finance for Dummies](https://www.dummies.com/book/business-careers-money/personal-finance/general-personal-finance/personal-finance-for-dummies-282169/) by Eric Tyson -- Clear, comprehensive guide to personal finance: budgeting, investing, insurance, taxes, retirement planning.

- [iFixit Teardown Videos](https://www.ifixit.com/Teardown) -- Advanced repair content showing how products are designed and how to repair them. Develops understanding of how things work.

- [Investopedia](https://www.investopedia.com/) -- Comprehensive financial education platform covering investing, insurance, credit, debt, and financial planning. Clear explanations with examples.

- [Food Preservation Resources (USDA)](https://nchfp.uga.edu/) -- National Center for Home Food Preservation: science-based guides for canning, freezing, drying, and fermenting food safely.

- [Fine Homebuilding](https://www.finehomebuilding.com/) -- Expert resources on home maintenance, repair, and improvement. Detailed guides with professional techniques.

- [The Tightwad Gazette](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/41557.The_Complete_Tightwad_Gazette) by Amy Dacyczyn -- Classic resource on frugal living: stretching a budget, creative reuse, and finding value. Practical and non-judgmental.

- [Sustainable Fashion Resources](https://www.fashionrevolution.org/) -- Fashion Revolution: education and advocacy for sustainable, ethical fashion. Understanding the human and environmental cost of clothing.

## Digital Tools & Apps

Technology supporting life skills learning and practice.

- [Mint / Credit Karma](https://www.mint.com/) -- Free budgeting and expense tracking app. Visualize spending, set budgets, and track financial goals.

- [YNAB (You Need A Budget)](https://www.ynab.com/) -- Budgeting software teaching the envelope method. Free for students. Strong educational component alongside the tool.

- [Allrecipes](https://www.allrecipes.com/) -- Recipe database with reviews, ratings, and step-by-step instructions. Filter by ingredients, dietary preferences, and cooking time.

- [Mealime](https://www.mealime.com/) -- Meal planning app that generates recipes and grocery lists. Free tier available. Simplifies the meal planning process.

- [Google Sheets](https://sheets.google.com/) -- Free spreadsheet tool for creating budgets, meal plans, chore charts, and shopping lists.

- [iFixit App](https://www.ifixit.com/app) -- Mobile app with repair guides, troubleshooting tools, and parts sourcing. Put repair knowledge in your pocket.

- [Canva for Education](https://www.canva.com/education/) -- Free design tool for creating chore charts, meal plans, emergency plans, and visual guides.

## Frameworks & Standards

Educational frameworks and standards aligned with DOMESTIC-101.

- [Family & Consumer Sciences National Standards](https://www.nasafacs.org/national-standards-for-fcs) -- National standards for FCS education covering food, nutrition, housing, textiles, consumer economics, and family resources.

- [Jump$tart Coalition for Financial Literacy](https://www.jumpstart.org/) -- National standards for K-12 financial literacy education. Spending, saving, credit, insurance, and financial planning.

- [Council for Economic Education Standards](https://www.councilforeconed.org/standards/) -- Standards for economics and personal finance education including earning, saving, spending, and investing.

- [Ready.gov Emergency Preparedness](https://www.ready.gov/) -- Federal emergency preparedness guidelines and education resources for families, schools, and communities.

- [USDA Dietary Guidelines](https://www.dietaryguidelines.gov/) -- Official U.S. nutrition recommendations supporting meal planning and food education.

## Community & Skill-Sharing

Organizations and movements connecting people for practical skill development.

- [Repair Cafe International](https://www.repaircafe.org/en/) -- Free community repair events. Volunteers fix items while teaching repair skills. Find or start one in your area.

- [Maker Spaces & Fab Labs](https://www.makerspaces.com/what-is-a-makerspace/) -- Community workshops with tools, equipment, and expertise for making and repairing. Many offer classes.

- [Community Supported Agriculture (CSA)](https://www.localharvest.org/) -- Connect with local farms for seasonal produce. Learn where food comes from.

- [Master Gardener Programs](https://extension.org/master-gardener/) -- USDA extension programs training volunteer gardeners. Free gardening education and community support.

- [Habitat for Humanity](https://www.habitat.org/) -- Volunteer opportunities to learn construction, repair, and maintenance skills while helping build homes for others.

- [Tool Libraries](https://www.localtools.org/) -- Community lending libraries for tools. Borrow what you need without buying everything.

## Final Note

This resource list is a starting point. We encourage:
- **Educators** to add locally relevant resources (community organizations, extension services, local repair cafes)
- **Parents** to share family wisdom and practical traditions
- **Learners** to discover resources aligned with their interests
- **Communities** to contribute resources reflecting their culture and circumstances

The best resources for life skills are the people around you who know things. Ask your grandparents how they cooked. Ask your neighbor how they fix things. Ask your community how they manage. Real skills come from real practice with real people.
