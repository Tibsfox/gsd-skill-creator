# SCI-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Science Method & Nature knowledge pack. All resources are selected for quality, accessibility, and alignment with inquiry-based science education.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define the philosophy and pedagogy of scientific inquiry education.

- [A Short History of Nearly Everything](https://www.billbryson.com/) by Bill Bryson -- Accessible, engaging history of scientific discovery that makes the human side of science vivid and relatable for all ages
- [The Structure of Scientific Revolutions](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/S/bo13179781.html) by Thomas Kuhn -- The foundational work on paradigm shifts, normal science, and how scientific knowledge evolves through revolutions
- [Science in Action](https://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674792913) by Bruno Latour -- How science is actually practiced in laboratories and institutions, beyond the idealized textbook version
- [The Demon-Haunted World](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/159731/the-demon-haunted-world-by-carl-sagan/) by Carl Sagan -- Essential reading on scientific thinking, skepticism, and the "baloney detection kit" for evaluating claims
- [How to Lie with Statistics](https://wwnorton.com/books/How-to-Lie-with-Statistics/) by Darrell Huff -- Classic primer on data literacy, misleading graphs, and critical evaluation of statistical claims

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, courses, and media for learners at various levels.

- [Khan Academy Science](https://www.khanacademy.org/science) -- Free, structured science courses covering biology, chemistry, physics, and earth science with practice exercises and progress tracking
- [PhET Interactive Simulations](https://phet.colorado.edu/) -- Free, research-based interactive simulations from the University of Colorado Boulder for exploring scientific concepts and testing variables
- [SciShow on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/scishow) -- Engaging, accurate science communication covering diverse topics from microbiology to astrophysics
- [National Geographic Kids](https://kids.nationalgeographic.com/) -- Age-appropriate science content with stunning photography, exploration prompts, and activity ideas
- [Mystery Science](https://mysteryscience.com/) -- Structured inquiry lessons with engaging video introductions and hands-on investigation activities
- [CK-12 Foundation](https://www.ck12.org/student/) -- Free, customizable textbooks and interactive resources covering all major science domains
- [Crash Course Science](https://www.youtube.com/crashcourse) -- Fast-paced, well-produced video series covering biology, chemistry, physics, ecology, and more
- [Science Buddies](https://www.sciencebuddies.org/) -- Science fair project ideas with detailed procedures, background research guides, and assessment rubrics

## For Parents/Mentors

Guidance for adults facilitating science learning, regardless of their own science background.

- [NGSS Parent Guides](https://www.nextgenscience.org/resources/parent-guides) -- Grade-by-grade guides explaining what children should be learning and how parents can support scientific thinking at home
- [Exploratorium: Science Snacks](https://www.exploratorium.edu/snacks) -- Simple, effective science activities using common household materials, designed by the San Francisco Exploratorium
- [Science at Home (Smithsonian)](https://www.si.edu/kids) -- Activities, games, and exploration guides from the Smithsonian Institution, usable without specialized equipment
- [How to Raise a Mathematician](https://www.harpercollins.com/products/how-to-bake-pi-eugenia-cheng) by Eugenia Cheng -- Philosophy of curiosity-driven learning applicable to all STEM subjects
- [The Art of Doing Science and Engineering](https://www.stripe.press/p/the-art-of-doing-science-and-engineering) by Richard Hamming -- Philosophy of productive scientific and engineering thinking for mentors with technical backgrounds
- [Steve Spangler Science](https://www.stevespanglerscience.com/) -- Engaging experiments and demonstrations with explanations of the science behind them

---

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for learners pursuing scientific methodology, philosophy of science, and research skills.

- [The Logic of Scientific Discovery](https://www.routledge.com/The-Logic-of-Scientific-Discovery/Popper/p/book/9780415278447) by Karl Popper -- Foundational work on falsifiability, demarcation, and what distinguishes science from non-science
- [Against Method](https://www.versobooks.com/books/118-against-method) by Paul Feyerabend -- Provocative argument that science has no single method, challenging learners to think critically about methodology itself
- [Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman!](https://wwnorton.com/books/Surely-Youre-Joking-Mr-Feynman/) by Richard Feynman -- Autobiography demonstrating scientific curiosity, creativity, and integrity in practice
- [Bad Science](https://www.fourth-estate.co.uk/) by Ben Goldacre -- Critical examination of how science is misrepresented in media, medicine, and public policy
- [Understanding Science (UC Berkeley)](https://undsci.berkeley.edu/) -- Comprehensive online resource explaining how science works, common misconceptions, and the nature of scientific knowledge
- [Nature of Science Resources (NSTA)](https://www.nsta.org/topics/nature-science) -- Teaching resources from the National Science Teaching Association focused on understanding the nature and process of science

## Standards and Frameworks

Official educational frameworks aligned with this pack's approach.

- [Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS)](https://www.nextgenscience.org/) -- National framework emphasizing scientific practices, crosscutting concepts, and disciplinary core ideas
- [AAAS Benchmarks for Science Literacy](https://www.aaas.org/programs/project-2061/benchmarks) -- Statements of what all students should know and be able to do in science, mathematics, and technology
- [A Framework for K-12 Science Education](https://www.nap.edu/catalog/13165/a-framework-for-k-12-science-education-practices-crosscutting-concepts) -- The National Research Council framework that informed NGSS development
- [International Baccalaureate Science Guide](https://www.ibo.org/) -- International framework with strong emphasis on experimental skills and scientific investigation

## Digital Tools

Software and platforms that support scientific inquiry activities.

- [Desmos Scientific Calculator](https://www.desmos.com/scientific) -- Free, accessible calculator for data analysis and mathematical modeling
- [Google Sheets](https://sheets.google.com/) -- Free spreadsheet for data organization, graphing, and basic statistical analysis
- [Canva for Education](https://www.canva.com/education/) -- Free design tool for creating science posters, infographics, and visual presentations
- [Padlet](https://padlet.com/) -- Collaborative digital boards useful for gallery walks, peer review, and sharing investigation results
- [Hypothesis](https://web.hypothes.is/) -- Web annotation tool useful for collaborative analysis of scientific articles and peer review exercises
