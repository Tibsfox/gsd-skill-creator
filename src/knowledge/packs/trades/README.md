# TRADE-101: Trades & Applied Skills

## Quick Start

**TRADE-101** is the Foundational Knowledge Pack for skilled trades and applied skills. If you're interested in woodworking, electrical work, plumbing, welding, or construction, this pack gives you the foundation: tool safety, proper technique, hands-on projects, and career pathways.

## What You'll Learn

- **Module 1:** Tools, Safety & Workshop Practice — Hand tools, power tools, safety culture
- **Module 2:** Woodworking & Fabrication — Measuring, cutting, joining, finishing real projects
- **Module 3:** Electrical Fundamentals — Circuits, household wiring, troubleshooting
- **Module 4:** Plumbing & Mechanical Systems — Water, pressure, drainage, basic HVAC
- **Module 5:** Welding, Metalwork & Construction — Metal properties, joining, structure

## How to Use This Pack

### For Students

**Foundation Level (K-5):** Learn tool identification, basic safety, simple hand tools, and build your first projects. Activities focus on supervised practice with age-appropriate tools and projects (hammering, measuring, simple cutting, joining nails and screws).

**Exploration Level (5-8):** Expand across multiple trade domains. Learn measurement to 1/4 inch, basic power tools under supervision, simple electrical circuits, pipe assembly, and more complex projects. Decide which trade areas interest you most.

**Specialization Level (8-12):** Go deep into your chosen trade. Measure to 1/16 inch, read technical drawings, design and execute advanced projects, understand codes and standards, prepare for apprenticeship.

### For Teachers

1. **Read TRADE-101-vision.md** to understand the philosophy and pedagogy
2. **Select activities from TRADE-101-activities.json** appropriate to grade level
3. **Use the rubrics in TRADE-101-assessment.md** to evaluate student work
4. **Scaffold Module 1 (Tools & Safety)** — it's the foundation for everything else
5. **Provide real materials and real tools** — simulation reduces learning
6. **Connect with local tradespeople** for mentorship and apprenticeship pathways
7. **Emphasize safety constantly** — it's not negotiable

### For Parents

TRADE-101 teaches practical skills with real tools. Safety is paramount. Your child won't become a master electrician by age 12, but they'll understand electricity, respect it, and be ready for apprenticeship at 16+.

**Age-appropriate examples:**
- **Age 5-8:** Hammering nails, using hand screwdrivers, basic measurement
- **Age 8-12:** Saws under supervision, circuits with breadboards, pipe assembly
- **Age 12+:** Power tools under supervision, real electrical wiring (safe 12V), welding simulation
- **Age 16+:** Real apprenticeship with journeyperson supervision

But TRADE-101 can stand alone if taught by experienced instructor.

## What Makes This Pack Different

1. **Hands-on, supervised practice** — Real tools, real materials, real projects. Not simulations or videos.

2. **Safety-first culture** — Every activity rooted in preventing injury. Safety protocols are non-negotiable.

3. **Real outcomes** — Projects produce something functional: a working birdhouse, a light that turns on, plumbing without leaks, a welded piece that holds.

4. **For everyone** — Trades skills aren't gendered, aren't for "other kids," aren't a fallback. They're intellectually demanding and rewarding.

5. **Career pathways** — Preparation for 4-5 year apprenticeships leading to six-figure earning potential.

6. **Respect for work** — Skilled trades build civilization. This pack honors that.

## Learning Outcomes

After completing TRADE-101, students will be able to:

- Safely identify, select, and use hand and power tools
- Measure with precision and understand tolerances
- Read and create basic technical drawings
- Plan and execute multi-step projects from conception to completion
- Understand electrical circuits, plumbing systems, and structural principles
- Troubleshoot failures and solve problems in the physical world
- Recognize and prevent hazards
- Follow safety protocols consistently
- Explain why materials, methods, and safety rules matter

## Assessment

Assessment is **competency-based**: Can the student perform the task safely and correctly?

We use four rubric levels:

- **Beginning:** Identifies tools, follows safety with reminders, measures with guidance
- **Developing:** Selects appropriate tools, follows safety independently, measures within 1/4"
- **Proficient:** Plans projects, troubleshoots, measures within 1/16", works semi-independently
- **Advanced:** Designs original projects, mentors peers, reads complex drawings, masters specialization

**All assessment is safety-first.** A student cannot be "advanced" while cutting corners on safety.

## Implementation Requirements

To teach TRADE-101 effectively, you need:

1. **Experienced instructor** — Ideally with trades background or co-teaching with tradespeople
2. **Dedicated shop space** — Safe, equipped workspace with tools and materials
3. **Real tools** — Hand tools, power tools, measuring instruments (not simulations)
4. **Real materials** — Wood, metal, electrical components, pipe, PPE
5. **Safety culture** — Established protocols, emergency procedures, first aid
6. **Supervision** — Adult oversight for all tool use
7. **Connections** — Local tradespeople for mentorship and apprenticeship pathways
8. **Celebration** — Visible recognition of completed projects
