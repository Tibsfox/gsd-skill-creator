# NUTR-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Nutrition & Health Sciences knowledge pack. All resources are selected for quality, accessibility, and alignment with evidence-based, equity-centered nutrition education.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define the philosophy and science of nutrition education.

- [Food Politics: How the Food Industry Influences Nutrition and Health](https://www.nytimes.com/books/first/n/nestle-politics.html) by Marion Nestle -- Accessible analysis of how food industry influences nutrition policy and messaging; essential for understanding nutrition claims and marketing

- [In Defense of Food](https://michaelpollan.com/books/in-defense-of-food/) by Michael Pollan -- Exploration of what we've lost in modern food systems and how to eat well; addresses food culture, simplification, and return to whole foods

- [The Omnivore's Dilemma](https://michaelpollan.com/books/the-omnivores-dilemma/) by Michael Pollan -- Traces food systems from industrial corn to pasture-raised meat to farmers' market; reveals complexity of modern food production

- [Braiding Sweetgrass](https://www.robinkimmerer.com/) by Robin Wall Kimmerer -- Indigenous perspective on relationship with plants and food; wisdom about reciprocity and gratitude in food systems

- [Salt, Fat, Acid, Heat](https://www.saminnosrat.com/pages/books) by Samin Nosrat -- Teaches the fundamentals of good cooking through understanding ingredients; combines science with cultural food wisdom

## For Learners (Interactive & Video)

Platforms and resources designed for student engagement with nutrition science.

- [Khan Academy Health and Medicine](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/health-and-medicine) -- Free, structured courses covering anatomy (digestive system), nutrition basics, and health topics with practice exercises

- [PhET Interactive Simulations](https://phet.colorado.edu/) -- Free interactive simulations from University of Colorado Boulder; explore digestion, nutrient absorption, and body systems visually

- [TED-Ed Nutrition Videos](https://ed.ted.com/search?q=nutrition) -- Engaging animated videos on nutrition topics (how your gut works, nutrition claims, food systems) with discussion questions

- [National Geographic Kids](https://kids.nationalgeographic.com/) -- Age-appropriate science content with beautiful photography; includes food, farming, cooking, and nature topics

- [Crash Course Kids & Crash Course](https://www.youtube.com/crashcourse) -- Fast-paced, well-produced video series; Crash Course covers biology (digestion), chemistry (nutrients), ecology (food systems)

- [Science Buddies](https://www.sciencebuddies.org/) -- Hands-on science project ideas with detailed procedures; includes many nutrition and food-related investigations

- [Exploratorium: Science Snacks](https://www.exploratorium.edu/snacks) -- Simple, effective science activities using household materials; includes food science experiments

- [Common Sense Media](https://www.commonsensemedia.org/articles/teaching-kids-about-nutrition) -- Resources for teaching nutrition critically; addresses media literacy and marketing to children

## For Parents & Mentors

Guidance for adults facilitating nutrition learning without diet culture.

- [Ellyn Satter Division of Responsibility](https://www.ellynsatterinstitute.org/how-to-feed-children/) -- Framework for feeding children without power struggles; parents decide what/when/where, children decide whether/how much

- [Intuitive Eating](https://www.intuitiveeating.org/) -- Framework for non-diet eating and peaceful food relationship; resources for adults and some for teens

- [How to Raise an Intuitive Eater](https://www.amygamermanmsw.com/intuitive_parenting) by Amy Germann -- Parenting approach focused on respecting child's appetite and body

- [Healthy Eating for Families](https://www.eatright.org/health/wellness/family-resources) by Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics -- Science-based family nutrition resources

- [NGSS Parent Guides](https://www.nextgenscience.org/resources/parent-guides) -- Grade-by-grade guides explaining science learning; includes digestion and living systems

- [Steve Spangler Science](https://www.stevespanglerscience.com/) -- Engaging food science experiments and demonstrations with explanations

- [Simple Suppers & Family Cooking](https://www.budgetbytes.com/) -- Budget-friendly recipes and meal planning; emphasizes cooking from scratch and using whole ingredients

## For Deeper Study (High School & College)

Advanced resources for learners pursuing nutrition science, food systems, or related fields.

- [Molecular Gastronomy Textbooks](https://www.sciencedirect.com/topics/agricultural-and-biological-sciences/molecular-gastronomy) -- Scientific approach to cooking; explores how heat, chemistry, and technique affect food

- [The Logic of Scientific Discovery](https://www.routledge.com/The-Logic-of-Scientific-Discovery/Popper/p/book/9780415278447) by Karl Popper -- Foundational work on falsifiability and what distinguishes science; relevant to evaluating nutrition claims

- [Bad Science](https://www.fourth-estate.co.uk/) by Ben Goldacre -- Critical examination of how science is misrepresented in media and marketing, especially health and nutrition

- [Guns, Germs, and Steel](https://www.jared-diamond.com/) by Jared Diamond -- Historical perspective on how geography shaped agriculture and food systems globally

- [The Food Gap](https://www.karysommer.net/) by Kary Summers -- Explores food justice, food access, and inequities in food systems

- [Food Security & Food Justice Resources](https://www.foodispower.org/) -- Organization addressing food security, farming justice, and farm worker rights

- [USDA Nutrition Research Database](https://fdc.nal.usda.gov/) -- Official government database of nutrient composition of foods; primary source for nutrition data

- [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/) -- Database of peer-reviewed nutrition and health research; learn to search and evaluate studies

- [Cochrane Reviews](https://www.cochrane.org/) -- Systematic reviews of research on health topics; rigorous evaluation of evidence

## Learning Across Cultures

Resources celebrating diverse food traditions and nutritional wisdom.

- [Soul Food Nutrition Series](https://www.nal.usda.gov/fnic/diversity) -- Resources on traditional African-American food culture and nutrition

- [Latinx Food Culture & Nutrition](https://www.eatright.org/food-health-and-wellness/diverse-populations) by Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics -- Celebration of Latinx food traditions and nutritional wisdom

- [Asian Cuisines & Nutrition](https://www.epicurious.com/expert-advice/how-to-cook-healthy-asian-food) -- Cooking techniques and nutrition in diverse Asian food cultures

- [Indigenous Food Systems](https://www.fs.fed.us/managing-land/natural-resources/plants-animals/plants/traditional-uses) -- Understanding traditional indigenous foods and sustainable food systems

- [Slow Food Movement](https://www.slowfood.com/) -- International movement celebrating traditional foods, local agriculture, and food culture

- [Food & Culture Learning Platform](https://www.academia.edu/documents/in/Food_and_Culture) -- Academic resources on food's cultural significance

## For Food Security & Access

Resources supporting learners and families experiencing food insecurity.

- [Feeding America Food Bank Locator](https://www.feedingamerica.org/find-your-local-foodbank) -- Find local food banks and assistance programs

- [No Kid Hungry](https://nokidhungry.org/) -- Resources for families, schools, and communities to ensure children have access to nutritious food

- [SNAP/Food Stamp Program](https://www.fns.usda.gov/snap/state-directory) -- Federal nutrition assistance program; information on eligibility and application

- [WIC Program](https://www.fns.usda.gov/wic) -- Women, Infants, and Children program providing nutrition assistance to qualifying families

- [School Lunch Resources](https://www.fns.usda.gov/school-meals) -- USDA information on school meal programs and nutrition standards

- [Community Gardens & Urban Farming](https://www.communitygarden.org/) -- Find or start a community garden; grow food locally

- [Community Supported Agriculture (CSA)](https://www.localharvest.org/) -- Find local CSA farms; affordable access to seasonal produce

- [Food Rescue Programs](https://www.foodrescue.us/) -- Organizations connecting surplus food to hungry people

## Frameworks & Standards

Educational frameworks and standards aligned with NUTR-101.

- [Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS)](https://www.nextgenscience.org/) -- National standards for K-12 science education; includes human body systems and scientific inquiry

- [Health Education Standards](https://www.aahperd.org/sodhe/standards/) -- National standards for health education including nutrition and decision-making

- [Healthy Eating Standards](https://www.fns.usda.gov/nutrition-standards-school-meals) -- USDA nutrition standards for school meals

- [Academy of Nutrition & Dietetics Standards](https://www.eatright.org/) -- Professional organization setting evidence-based nutrition standards

- [Dietary Guidelines for Americans](https://www.dietaryguidelines.gov/) -- Official U.S. government nutrition recommendations (updated every 5 years)

## Final Note

This resource list is a starting point, not exhaustive. We encourage:
- **Educators** to add locally relevant resources (farms, nutritionists, markets, community programs)
- **Parents** to share family food wisdom and traditional recipes
- **Learners** to discover resources aligned with their interests and questions
- **Communities** to contribute resources reflecting their food culture and values

Food learning is lifelong and cultural. The best resources are those connected to real food, real people, and real communities.
