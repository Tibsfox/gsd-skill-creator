# DIGLIT-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Digital Literacy & Information Science knowledge pack. All resources are selected for quality, accessibility, and alignment with critical thinking about digital systems.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define digital literacy, information science, and critical thinking about digital systems.

- [Contagion: Why Things Catch On](https://www.jonah.com/) by Jonah Berger — How ideas and information spread; understand why misinformation goes viral
- [The Art of Critical Thinking](https://www.routledge.com/) — Developing judgment and evidence-based reasoning
- [Thinking, Fast and Slow](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/) by Daniel Kahneman — Understanding cognitive biases that affect decision-making online
- [The Age of Surveillance Capitalism](https://www.publicaffairs.com/) by Shoshana Zuboff — How data collection and algorithmic systems shape behavior
- [Algorithms of Oppression](https://nyupress.org/) by Safiya Noble — How algorithms reflect and amplify bias
- [The Misinformation Age](https://www.oup.com/) by Cailin O'Connor & James Owen Weatherall — Understanding how misinformation spreads and how to resist it

## Conclusion

These resources span foundational understanding, interactive practice, research-based learning, and practical tools. No learner needs to use all resources — different resources serve different learning styles and goals.

The goal is building critical thinking about digital systems, not consuming every resource. A single deep project using one or two resources is more valuable than superficial exposure to many.
