# DIGLIT-101: Digital Literacy & Information Science

## Quick Start

**DIGLIT-101** is a comprehensive foundational knowledge pack for developing digital literacy across all ages. This pack covers five core competencies essential for the 21st century:

1. **Digital Foundations** — How digital systems actually work
2. **Information Literacy** — Finding and evaluating reliable information
3. **Digital Communication** — Using tools responsibly and effectively
4. **Online Safety & Citizenship** — Making smart choices and protecting yourself
5. **Data & Algorithms** — Understanding how data and AI shape your world

## Key Features

- **Age-Appropriate Progression**: Foundation (K-2) through College+ (13-16) with spiral and linear pathways
- **Concept-Focused**: Teaches underlying concepts, not software tools that become obsolete
- **Critical Thinking**: Emphasizes judgment and decision-making over rule-following
- **Authentic Activities**: Real-world scenarios and investigations
- **Multiple Pathways**: Linear, spiral, or focused based on learner needs
- **Accessibility**: Unplugged alternatives and inclusive design throughout

## Getting Started

### For Educators

1. **Start with the vision document** (`DIGLIT-101-vision.md`) to understand the philosophy and goals
2. **Review the modules** (`DIGLIT-101-modules.yaml`) to see scope and structure
3. **Choose a pathway**:
   - **Linear**: M1 → M2 → M3 → M4 → M5 (comprehensive, sequential)
   - **Spiral**: Revisit all modules at increasing depth
   - **Focused**: Select 1-2 modules based on immediate needs
4. **Browse activities** (`DIGLIT-101-activities.json`) and pick ones that fit your context
5. **Use assessment rubrics** (`DIGLIT-101-assessment.md`) to evaluate student learning

### For Parents/Guardians

1. **Understand the pack's goals** — It's about judgment, not just rules
2. **Have conversations** — "What's something interesting you found online? How do you know it's true?"
3. **Model good practices** — Show how you evaluate sources, manage passwords, use technology thoughtfully
4. **Support their learning** — Activities in this pack often benefit from family involvement

### For Students

1. **Explore activities** in the areas you're interested in
2. **Try the challenges** — Password testers, fact-checking investigations, algorithm experiments
3. **Apply learning** to your own digital life
4. **Reflect** on how digital literacy affects your choices

## Learning Outcomes

By completing DIGLIT-101, learners will:

- Understand fundamental concepts of how digital systems work
- Evaluate credibility and bias in online information
- Use digital tools safely and responsibly
- Recognize online risks and know how to respond
- Understand how algorithms and data shape their experiences
- Make informed decisions about privacy and digital footprint

## Common Questions

**Q: Is this a coding course?**
A: No. Coding is valuable but separate. DIGLIT-101 focuses on understanding digital systems and making informed decisions, not programming.

**Q: What if we don't have much technology access?**
A: This pack includes unplugged activities and alternatives. You can explore networking with string and cards, evaluate sources with printed materials, and discuss digital citizenship without technology.

**Q: How long does this take?**
A: It depends on depth and age level. Foundation (K-2) might spend 25-40 hours over a year. High school might spend 80-120 hours for comprehensive coverage. You can also focus on specific modules.

**Q: Can we do just one module?**
A: Yes! The focused pathway allows selecting 1-2 modules based on immediate needs. However, modules are most powerful when connected.

**Q: How do we assess if it's working?**
A: Look for evidence of judgment and application:
- Can they evaluate a new source they haven't seen before?
- Do they explain their reasoning about online choices?
- Can they apply concepts to new situations?
- Do they recognize complexity and nuance?
