# MUSIC-101: Music & Sound

## Quick Start

**MUSIC-101** is a foundational knowledge pack about music as organized sound and universal human language. It covers rhythm, melody, harmony, listening, and musical creation across K-College.

This is not about producing virtuosos. It's about developing **musical literacy**: the ability to listen actively, create expressively, and understand how sound is organized.

### What's Inside?

- **5 Modules**: Rhythm & Movement → Melody & Voice → Harmony & Ensemble → Listening & Analysis → Creation & Technology
- **50+ Activities**: Body percussion, singing, composition, digital tools, ensemble work
- **Multi-Grade**: Foundation (K-2) through College (13-16)
- **No Equipment Required**: Start with voice and body percussion; add instruments and technology as desired

### For Learners

1. Start with **Module 1: Rhythm & Movement** — feel a steady beat, clap patterns, explore rhythm
2. Move to **Module 2: Melody & Voice** — sing, match pitches, create melodies
3. Build **Module 3: Harmony & Ensemble** — learn chords, sing parts, arrange
4. Explore **Module 4: Listening & Analysis** (any time) — understand music structure and style
5. Create with **Module 5: Creation & Technology** — compose, improvise, use digital tools

Or jump around based on your interests and what you want to make!

### For Teachers/Mentors

- Vision document explains the philosophy and pedagogy
- Modules file has all learning outcomes, topics, and example activities
- Activities JSON contains 50+ concrete lesson ideas with materials and timing
- Assessment framework focuses on understanding and expression, not performance anxiety
- Resources point to free tools: Chrome Music Lab, MuseScore, Audacity, Incredibox, GarageBand

### For Parents

Your child doesn't need to be "naturally talented" to benefit from music. Every human is musical. Music develops pattern recognition, emotional intelligence, fine motor control, and collaboration skills.

You can support music learning at home by:
- Listening together and talking about what you hear
- Singing (off-key is fine!)
- Making music with household objects
- Encouraging composition and experimentation
- Celebrating effort and curiosity
