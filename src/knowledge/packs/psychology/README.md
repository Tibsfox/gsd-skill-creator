# PSYCH-101: Psychology & Human Development

Welcome to the Psychology & Human Development foundational knowledge pack. This pack helps learners understand how humans think, feel, develop, and behave—from the inside out.

## Quick Start

**What This Pack Teaches:** The science of mind and behavior. Why you do what you do. How your brain works. How emotions guide behavior. How relationships shape who you become.

**Who It's For:** Anyone curious about how humans work. Students exploring self-understanding, empathy, and healthy relationships. Learners wanting to understand their own development and navigate challenges with evidence-based thinking.

**Time Commitment:**
- Foundation (K-2): 20-40 hours (spread over several years)
- Elementary (3-5): 50-70 hours
- Middle School (6-8): 70-100 hours
- High School (9-12): 100-150 hours
- College+: 150-200 hours

## Five Core Modules

### Module 1: Brain, Perception & Cognition
How your brain processes what you perceive, how attention works, why memory fails, and what cognitive biases shape your decisions.

**Key Activities:** Optical illusion gallery, attention blindness challenge, memory experiments, cognitive bias detector

### Module 2: Learning, Memory & Study Skills
How we learn (classical conditioning, operant conditioning, observation), how memory works, and what study strategies actually work based on research.

**Key Activities:** Study strategy experiments, memory palace construction, metacognition journal, retrieval practice

### Module 3: Emotion, Motivation & Well-being
What emotions tell us, how to regulate difficult feelings, what motivates us (intrinsic vs. extrinsic), resilience, and growth mindset.

**Key Activities:** Emotion thermometer journal, motivation mapping, emotion regulation toolkit, resilience challenges

### Module 4: Human Development Across the Lifespan
How we develop from infancy through aging. Attachment, cognitive stages, identity formation, moral development, and challenges at each stage.

**Key Activities:** Interviews across generations, Piaget's task investigations, perspective-taking exercises, life timeline reflection

### Module 5: Social Psychology & Relationships
How we behave in groups, how stereotypes form and can be countered, what makes relationships work, and how to build empathy.

**Key Activities:** Conformity observation challenge, empathy circle discussion, stereotype interrupt, bystander intervention scenarios

## How to Use This Pack

### For Self-Directed Learners
1. Start with Module 1 (how your brain works)
2. Choose Module 2 or 3 based on interest (learning or emotions)
3. Explore Modules 4 & 5 to understand development and relationships
4. Complete the portfolio assessment to demonstrate mastery

### For Guided Learning (with a mentor or parent)
1. Work through modules in order or follow a customized pathway
2. Engage in activities and discussions together
3. Keep reflection journals to notice psychology in daily life
4. Use the assessment rubrics to track growth

### For Classroom Use
- Each module is designed for flexibility
- Activities can be adapted for group or individual work
- Assessment rubrics support formative and summative evaluation
- Parent guidance helps families support learners at home

## Assessment & Growth

This pack uses a four-level rubric:

- **Beginning:** Can name and recognize concepts; understands basics
- **Developing:** Can apply concepts to familiar situations; sees psychology in daily life
- **Proficient:** Can explain complex ideas; applies understanding to real problems; teaches others
- **Advanced:** Can integrate concepts across domains; critiques and applies research; mentors others

See PSYCH-101-assessment.md for detailed rubrics.
