# LEARN-101: Learning to Learn

**The meta-pack that amplifies every other pack.**

## What Is This?

LEARN-101 teaches you how to learn. Not *what* to learn, but *how* your brain learns, what strategies actually work, and how to think about your own thinking.

This is the foundational pack for the entire 35-pack system. Every other pack (MATH-101, SCI-101, READ-101, and so on) becomes more effective when you understand the science of learning and apply evidence-based strategies.

## Why It Matters

Most students spend years in school without being explicitly taught *how* to learn. They default to ineffective strategies (cramming, re-reading, highlighting) and wonder why they work so hard but forget so much.

LEARN-101 changes that.

After completing this pack, you'll understand:
- **How memory actually works** — encoding, storage, retrieval, sleep consolidation
- **What study strategies actually work** — retrieval practice beats re-reading; spaced repetition beats cramming; interleaving builds transfer
- **How to monitor your own learning** — think about your thinking, distinguish understanding from familiarity
- **The power of growth mindset** — your abilities are developed through effort and strategy, not fixed at birth
- **How to transfer learning across domains** — apply a strategy learned in one pack to another pack

## The Five Modules

### Module 1: Your Learning Brain
Understand how memory works, the limits of working memory, why sleep is essential, and which brain myths are wrong.

### Module 2: Study Strategies That Work
Learn the evidence-based strategies: retrieval practice, spaced repetition, interleaving, elaboration, concept mapping. And learn what *doesn't* work (re-reading, highlighting, massed practice).

### Module 3: Thinking About Thinking
Develop metacognitive awareness. Distinguish fluency (easy to read) from understanding (can apply and teach). Learn to monitor and adjust your own learning.

### Module 4: Motivation, Mindset & Goals
Build growth mindset. Set SMART goals. Learn to push through productive struggle. View failure as data, not judgment.

### Module 5: Learning Across Domains
Apply strategies across subjects. Use analogies and teaching to deepen understanding. Design your personal learning system.

## How to Use This Pack

**Start with Module 1** to understand the science.

Then choose your own path:
- **Practice-focused?** Jump to Module 2 and start using strategies while you study other packs
- **Metacognitive-focused?** Move to Module 3 and develop deep self-awareness
- **Mindset-focused?** Go to Module 4 and build resilience and growth beliefs

**End with Module 5** to integrate everything and design your personal learning system.

## Key Activities

- **Working Memory Capacity Experiment** — Discover your limits and learn chunking strategies
- **Build a Spaced Repetition Schedule** — Design a spacing schedule and test it
- **Fluency Test** — Can you explain something without looking? Reveals gaps in understanding
- **SMART Goal Workshop** — Set specific, measurable learning goals
- **Cross-Pack Connection Map** — See how the same idea appears in multiple packs
- **Personal Learning System Design** — Integrate everything into your own system

## How This Amplifies Other Packs

When you study **MATH-101**, use retrieval practice on formulas, space reviews over weeks, and elaborate by connecting new formulas to ones you know.

When you learn **SCI-101**, use interleaving to mix experiment types, monitor your understanding of hypotheses, and transfer the scientific method to everyday reasoning.

When reading for **READ-101**, use elaboration strategies to connect ideas, monitor comprehension, and space reviews of difficult concepts.

When developing **CRIT-101** skills, interleave different fallacy types and use dual coding (examples + explanations).

This pattern applies to every pack. LEARN-101 is the multiplier.

## Assessment

In LEARN-101, you're not memorizing facts about learning science. You're **demonstrating that you can learn effectively**.

Can you:
- Design a learning plan for a real target (math concept, language skill, science content)?
- Monitor your own understanding accurately?
- Learn from mistakes and adjust strategies?
- Transfer a strategy from one context to another?
- Teach a peer how to learn something difficult?

These are the measures that matter.

## For Parents & Mentors

The most important thing you can do is model growth mindset. When you face something difficult, talk about it:
- "This is hard. I'm going to try a different approach."
- "I made a mistake. That's data I can learn from."
- "You worked hard on that. I noticed you adjusted your strategy halfway through."

Ask metacognitive questions:
- "Do you understand that or are you just recognizing familiar words?"
- "What strategy did you try? What will you try next?"
- "How is this like something you already know?"

Help your child see struggle as a sign of growth, not failure.
