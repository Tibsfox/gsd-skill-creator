# READ-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Reading & Literacy knowledge pack. All resources are selected for quality, accessibility, and alignment with research-based reading instruction that integrates phonics, vocabulary, comprehension, and critical analysis.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define the philosophy and pedagogy of reading and literacy education.

- [The Read-Aloud Handbook](https://www.penguin.com/) by Jim Trelease and Cyndi Giorgis -- The definitive guide to reading aloud, with decades of research on how reading aloud builds vocabulary, comprehension, and lifelong reading habits
- [Proust and the Squid](https://www.harpercollins.com/) by Maryanne Wolf -- The science of the reading brain: how humans learned to read, how reading changes neural pathways, and what happens when reading is difficult
- [Reading in the Brain](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/) by Stanislas Dehaene -- Neuroscience of reading: how the brain recognizes letters, assembles words, and constructs meaning from text at remarkable speed
- [The Knowledge Gap](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/562222/the-knowledge-gap-by-natalie-wexler/) by Natalie Wexler -- Why reading comprehension depends on background knowledge, and how the gap between good and struggling readers is often a knowledge gap, not a skills gap
- [How to Read a Book](https://www.simonandschuster.com/) by Mortimer Adler and Charles Van Doren -- The classic guide to analytical and critical reading, from elementary to syntopical reading levels, still relevant after seven decades

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, libraries, and media for learners at various reading levels.

- [ReadWorks](https://www.readworks.org/) -- Free, research-based reading comprehension passages with aligned questions, organized by grade level, topic, and skill focus
- [CommonLit](https://www.commonlit.org/) -- Free digital library of literary and nonfiction texts with comprehension activities, discussion questions, and paired text sets
- [Newsela](https://newsela.com/) -- Current events articles adapted to multiple reading levels on the same topic, perfect for developing comprehension at the right challenge level
- [Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/) -- Free access to over 70,000 public-domain books, including classics available for every reader and interest
- [Storyline Online](https://storylineonline.net/) -- Celebrity-read children's books with animation, building listening comprehension and love of stories for early and developing readers
- [Epic!](https://www.getepic.com/) -- Digital library with thousands of books for children, including read-to-me, audiobook, and independent reading options
- [Vocabulary.com](https://www.vocabulary.com/) -- Adaptive vocabulary practice using spaced repetition and context-rich definitions with a game-like interface
- [ReadTheory](https://readtheory.org/) -- Adaptive reading comprehension practice that adjusts difficulty based on learner performance

## For Parents/Mentors

Guidance for adults supporting reading development, regardless of their own reading background.

- [Reading Rockets](https://www.readingrockets.org/) -- Comprehensive, research-based resources for parents, teachers, and caregivers on teaching reading, including strategies, book lists, and expert interviews
- [PBS Parents Reading](https://www.pbs.org/parents/thrive/reading-tips-for-parents) -- Practical tips for parents on supporting reading development at home, from infancy through elementary school
- [Dolch Sight Word Lists](https://www.sightwords.com/) -- Classic high-frequency word lists organized by grade level for fluency practice, with games and activities
- [Read Aloud 15 Minutes](https://www.readaloud.org/) -- Campaign promoting daily read-aloud time with research summaries, book recommendations, and practical tips for busy families
- [Colorin Colorado](https://www.colorincolorado.org/) -- Bilingual literacy resources for families and educators working with English language learners
- [Book Trust](https://www.booktrust.org.uk/) -- Resources for fostering a reading culture at home, including age-appropriate book recommendations and reading activity ideas

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for learners and educators pursuing reading science, literacy theory, and teaching practice.

- [Why Johnny Can't Read](https://www.harpercollins.com/) by Rudolf Flesch -- The 1955 classic that launched the phonics debate and remains relevant to understanding the foundations of reading instruction
- [Understanding Reading](https://www.routledge.com/) by Frank Smith -- Theoretical framework for understanding reading as active meaning construction, emphasizing the role of prediction and prior knowledge
- [Readicide](https://www.stenhouse.com/) by Kelly Gallagher -- How schools kill the love of reading through overanalysis and underexposure, and practical strategies to revive authentic reading engagement
- [The Book Whisperer](https://www.josseybass.com/) by Donalyn Miller -- Building a community of readers through volume, choice, and authentic engagement rather than assignments and comprehension worksheets
- [Visible Learning for Literacy](https://www.corwin.com/) by Douglas Fisher, Nancy Frey, and John Hattie -- Evidence-based strategies for literacy instruction grounded in the largest meta-analysis of educational research
- [Understanding Science (UC Berkeley)](https://undsci.berkeley.edu/) -- Online resource explaining how science works, useful for teaching cross-disciplinary reading of scientific texts

## Standards and Frameworks

Official educational frameworks aligned with this pack's approach to reading instruction.

- [Common Core State Standards (ELA)](http://www.corestandards.org/ELA-Literacy/) -- National framework for English Language Arts with reading standards for literature, informational text, and foundational skills
- [Science of Reading Research](https://www.readingrockets.org/article/what-does-research-say-about-reading) -- Synthesis of decades of cognitive science research on how reading works and how it should be taught
- [NAEP Reading Framework](https://nces.ed.gov/nationsreportcard/reading/) -- The National Assessment of Educational Progress framework for measuring reading achievement across the United States
- [International Literacy Association Standards](https://www.literacyworldwide.org/) -- Professional standards for literacy educators emphasizing evidence-based practice and equity in literacy education

## Digital Tools

Software and platforms that support reading instruction and practice.

- [Google Docs](https://docs.google.com/) -- Free writing and annotation tool for collaborative reading responses, text annotation, and shared reading journals
- [Hypothesis](https://web.hypothes.is/) -- Web annotation tool for collaborative analysis of online texts, enabling shared close reading and discussion
- [Canva for Education](https://www.canva.com/education/) -- Free design tool for creating story maps, vocabulary posters, book reviews, and reading response visuals
- [Padlet](https://padlet.com/) -- Collaborative digital boards for sharing reading responses, book recommendations, and comparative analysis charts
- [Flipgrid](https://info.flip.com/) -- Video discussion platform where learners can record book talks, reading reflections, and think-aloud demonstrations

## Diverse Voices and Perspectives

Resources for building inclusive reading experiences that reflect diverse voices and experiences.

- [We Need Diverse Books](https://diversebooks.org/) -- Organization promoting diverse representation in children's and young adult literature with book lists and resources
- [Mirrors, Windows, and Sliding Glass Doors](https://scenicregional.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/Mirrors-Windows-and-Sliding-Glass-Doors.pdf) by Rudine Sims Bishop -- Foundational essay on why learners need books that reflect their own experience (mirrors), reveal others' experiences (windows), and invite them into new worlds (sliding glass doors)
- [Social Justice Books](https://socialjusticebooks.org/) -- Curated book lists addressing social justice themes across age levels and reading abilities
- [Lee & Low Books](https://www.leeandlow.com/) -- Publisher specializing in diverse children's literature with educator resources and discussion guides
