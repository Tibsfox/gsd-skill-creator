# CHEM-101 Resources

Curated resources for the Chemistry knowledge pack. All resources are selected for quality, accessibility, and alignment with hands-on, inquiry-based chemistry education.

---

## Foundational Texts

Resources that define the philosophy and pedagogy of chemistry education and chemical thinking.

- [The Disappearing Spoon](https://www.samkean.com/books/the-disappearing-spoon) by Sam Kean -- Engaging stories about every element in the periodic table. Makes the periodic table come alive through history, personality, and narrative. Accessible to middle school and up.
- [Stuff Matters](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/317568/stuff-matters-by-mark-miodownik/) by Mark Miodownik -- Exploration of the materials that make up our world: steel, paper, chocolate, plastic, glass, graphite. Chemistry through the lens of everyday materials.
- [Napoleon's Buttons](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/292513/napoleons-buttons-by-penny-le-couteur-and-jay-burreson/) by Penny Le Couteur & Jay Burreson -- How 17 molecules changed history. Connects chemistry to historical events in compelling narrative.
- [The Elements](https://periodictable.com/books/elements/) by Theodore Gray -- Beautiful photographic guide to every element with descriptions of properties and uses. The periodic table as art and science.
- [The Periodic Table](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/36909/the-periodic-table-by-primo-levi/) by Primo Levi -- Memoir linking chemistry to life experience. Each chapter named for an element. Beautiful and profound.

## For Learners

Interactive platforms, courses, and media for learners at various levels.

- [Khan Academy Chemistry](https://www.khanacademy.org/science/chemistry) -- Free, structured chemistry courses covering atoms, bonding, reactions, stoichiometry, and beyond. Practice exercises and progress tracking at every level.
- [PhET Chemistry Simulations](https://phet.colorado.edu/en/simulations/filter?subjects=chemistry) -- Free, research-based interactive simulations from the University of Colorado Boulder for building atoms, exploring molecular shapes, balancing equations, and investigating reactions.
- [Tyler DeWitt on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@tylerdewitt) -- Enthusiastic, clear chemistry explanations with humor and visual aids. Makes complex topics genuinely accessible and fun.
- [Periodic Videos](https://www.periodicvideos.com/) -- A video for every element in the periodic table from the University of Nottingham, featuring real experiments and demonstrations by Professor Martyn Poliakoff.
- [CrashCourse Chemistry](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLG61LF8I_OXoh2mhx2YNY9s4ekXiriMAf) -- Fast-paced, well-produced video series covering all major chemistry topics from atomic structure to organic chemistry.
- [AACT Resources](https://teachchemistry.org/) -- American Association of Chemistry Teachers lesson plans, activities, simulations, and resources organized by topic and grade level.
- [ChemCollective Virtual Labs](http://www.chemcollective.org/) -- Free virtual chemistry labs and scenario-based learning activities from Carnegie Mellon University.
- [Compound Interest Infographics](https://www.compoundchem.com/) -- Beautiful, accurate infographics explaining the chemistry of everyday things: food, drink, drugs, materials, and phenomena.

## For Parents/Mentors

Guidance for adults facilitating chemistry learning, regardless of their own chemistry background.

- [Royal Society of Chemistry: Learn Chemistry](https://edu.rsc.org/) -- Activities, experiments, and teaching resources suitable for home and classroom use. Searchable by topic and age range.
- [Steve Spangler Science](https://www.stevespanglerscience.com/) -- Engaging kitchen-safe experiments and demonstrations with clear explanations of the chemistry involved. Designed for parents and educators.
- [Science Buddies: Chemistry Projects](https://www.sciencebuddies.org/science-fair-projects/project-ideas/chemistry) -- Science fair project ideas with detailed procedures, safety notes, and chemical explanations. Excellent for guided home investigation.
- [Exploratorium: Chemistry Activities](https://www.exploratorium.edu/snacks/collection/chemistry) -- Quick, accessible chemistry activities using household materials from the San Francisco Exploratorium.
- [MEL Chemistry](https://melscience.com/) -- Subscription chemistry experiment kits with detailed scientific explanations. Good for families wanting structured, progressive chemistry experiences.
- [ACS Chemistry for Life](https://www.acs.org/education.html) -- American Chemical Society educational resources including safety guidelines, activity ideas, and career information for chemistry.

## For Deeper Study

Advanced resources for learners pursuing chemistry at college level and beyond.

- [Chemistry: The Central Science](https://www.pearson.com/en-us/subject-catalog/p/chemistry-the-central-science/P200000006802) by Brown, LeMay, Bursten -- Standard college-level textbook covering all fundamental chemistry topics with clear explanations and worked examples.
- [Organic Chemistry as a Second Language](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/Organic+Chemistry+as+a+Second+Language) by David Klein -- Accessible approach to organic chemistry that builds understanding rather than requiring memorization. Popular with students who find organic chemistry intimidating.
- [Why Chemical Reactions Happen](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/why-chemical-reactions-happen-9780199249732) by James Keeler & Peter Wothers -- Clear explanation of the thermodynamics and kinetics of chemical reactions. Bridges the gap between introductory and advanced chemistry.
- [The Art of Chemistry](https://www.wiley.com/en-us/The+Art+of+Chemistry%3A+Myths%2C+Medicines%2C+and+Materials-p-9780471071808) by Arthur Greenberg -- History of chemistry from ancient alchemy to modern materials science. Connects chemical discoveries to their cultural context.
- [MIT OpenCourseWare: Chemistry](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/chemistry/) -- Free college-level chemistry courses from MIT, including lecture videos, notes, and problem sets for general, organic, and physical chemistry.
- [Atkins' Physical Chemistry](https://global.oup.com/academic/product/atkins-physical-chemistry-9780198847816) by Peter Atkins -- The standard reference for thermodynamics, kinetics, and quantum chemistry at the university level.

## Standards and Frameworks

Official educational frameworks aligned with this pack's approach.

- [Next Generation Science Standards (NGSS): Physical Science](https://www.nextgenscience.org/search-standards?keys=&tid%5B%5D=100) -- National standards for matter and its interactions, covering structure, properties, and chemical reactions.
- [AAAS Benchmarks: Structure of Matter](https://www.aaas.org/programs/project-2061/benchmarks) -- Statements of what all students should know about atoms, molecules, and chemical reactions.
- [AP Chemistry Course Description](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses/ap-chemistry) -- College Board framework for advanced chemistry covering atomic structure, bonding, reactions, kinetics, thermodynamics, and equilibrium.
- [International Baccalaureate Chemistry Guide](https://www.ibo.org/) -- International framework with strong emphasis on experimental skills, data analysis, and real-world applications of chemistry.

## Digital Tools

Software and platforms that support chemistry learning activities.

- [PhET Build an Atom](https://phet.colorado.edu/en/simulations/build-an-atom) -- Interactive atom-building simulation. Add protons, neutrons, and electrons; see element identity and charge change in real time.
- [PhET Molecule Shapes](https://phet.colorado.edu/en/simulations/molecule-shapes) -- Explore VSEPR theory interactively. Build molecules and see how electron pairs determine three-dimensional shape.
- [PhET Balancing Chemical Equations](https://phet.colorado.edu/en/simulations/balancing-chemical-equations) -- Practice balancing equations with visual atom counts and instant feedback.
- [Molview](https://molview.org/) -- Free online 3D molecular viewer. Search for any molecule by name or formula and view it in ball-and-stick, space-filling, or wireframe modes.
- [Desmos Scientific Calculator](https://www.desmos.com/scientific) -- Free, accessible calculator for chemistry calculations (molar mass, concentration, stoichiometry).
- [Ptable Interactive Periodic Table](https://ptable.com/) -- Rich, interactive periodic table with properties, electron configurations, isotopes, and compound information for every element.

## Safety Resources

Essential safety information for hands-on chemistry activities.

- [ACS Safety Guidelines for Chemical Demonstrations](https://www.acs.org/education/policies/safety.html) -- Official safety guidelines from the American Chemical Society for demonstrations and experiments.
- [Flinn Scientific Safety Resources](https://www.flinnsci.com/safety/) -- Comprehensive safety data sheets, lab safety guidelines, and chemical disposal information.
- [NIH Household Products Database](https://householdproducts.nlm.nih.gov/) -- Look up the chemical composition and safety information of common household products used in kitchen chemistry.
