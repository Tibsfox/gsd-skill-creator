# Electronics Educational Pack

A complete journey through electronics, from your first resistor to designing radio receivers. Every concept is built on the one before it, every formula is derived instead of memorized, and every circuit is simulated before you build it.

## What You'll Learn

This pack covers the full spectrum of analog and digital electronics, structured as a progressive curriculum that you can work through at your own pace. Each module includes theory, worked examples, simulation exercises, and hands-on projects.

### Foundation Modules

- **DC Fundamentals** -- Voltage, current, resistance, and the laws that govern them. Ohm's law derived from first principles, Kirchhoff's voltage and current laws, and the mental models that make circuit analysis intuitive.
- **Component Library** -- Resistors, capacitors, inductors, diodes, and transistors. What each component does physically, how to read datasheets, and how to choose the right part for the job.
- **DC Circuit Analysis** -- Series and parallel circuits, voltage dividers, current dividers, Thevenin and Norton equivalents. The analytical tools that let you understand any DC circuit by inspection.

### Intermediate Modules

- **AC Fundamentals** -- Frequency, impedance, phase, and the complex plane. Why AC analysis requires different tools than DC, and how phasors make sinusoidal circuits tractable.
- **Filter Design** -- Low-pass, high-pass, band-pass, and notch filters. RC, RL, and RLC circuits as frequency-selective elements. Bode plots and the art of reading transfer functions.
- **Semiconductor Physics** -- How diodes and transistors actually work at the atomic level. Band theory, doping, PN junctions, and the physical basis for everything in modern electronics.

### Advanced Modules

- **Amplifier Design** -- Common emitter, common collector, common base. Gain, bandwidth, input and output impedance. The tradeoffs that every amplifier design must navigate.
- **Op-Amp Circuits** -- The ideal op-amp model, inverting and non-inverting configurations, integrators, differentiators, and active filters. Why the op-amp is the Swiss Army knife of analog design.
- **Digital Fundamentals** -- Logic gates, Boolean algebra, flip-flops, counters, and state machines. The bridge from analog physics to digital abstraction.
- **Radio Receiver Design** -- Tuned circuits, superheterodyne architecture, mixer theory, and IF filtering. The capstone project that ties together everything from DC fundamentals to filter design.

## Prerequisites

None. This pack starts from absolute zero. You should be comfortable with basic algebra, but all required mathematics (including complex numbers and basic calculus) is introduced as needed, in context, with worked examples.

## Tools

All simulation exercises use free, open-source tools. Circuit simulation is done with SPICE-compatible software (LTspice or ngspice). Digital logic is simulated with logisim-evolution. No proprietary software or paid licenses required.

## Estimated Duration

Working through the complete pack at a steady pace takes approximately 120 hours. Each module is self-contained enough to be completed in 2-4 sessions of focused study, so the full pack can be completed over 3-6 months of part-time study.
