## Cross-references — Adaptive Systems

**Department:** `.college/departments/adaptive-systems/`  
**Connection type:** Biological anchor bridge — Mind-Body practices are the experiential counterpart to the formal biological-roots treatment in Panel D. Homeostasis, interoception, and nervous-system regulation are the lived phenomena that Friston's free-energy principle and Kirchhoff's Markov-blanket formalism describe mechanistically.

**Relaxation wing — nervous system / homeostasis.**  
Homeostasis — the body's maintenance of stable internal temperature, blood glucose, pH, and other physiological variables — is the biological prototype for the Markov-blanket persistence theorem (Kirchhoff et al. 2018): any system that maintains a characteristic internal-state distribution over time necessarily possesses a Markov blanket partitioning its internal states (I) from the external environment (E) via sensory (S) and active (A) boundary states. The nervous system content in this wing is the organismal-scale instantiation of that partition. Formal extension: Adaptive Systems Panel D (`D-biological-roots.md`), §3 on the Markov blanket formalism.

**Relaxation wing — `mb-interoception` (proposed concept).**  
Interoception — the internal model of the body's physiological state — is the specific phenomenon that Friston's active inference account (Friston et al. 2013) was developed to model. The brain maintains a generative model of expected bodily states and minimises prediction error between expected and actual interoceptive signals. Mind-body practices that increase interoceptive awareness (breath awareness, body scan, yoga nidra) are, in the active-inference framework, practices that improve the precision of the generative model and reduce the free-energy cost of unexpected sensory signals. This concept (`mb-interoception`) is proposed for addition to the Relaxation wing as the formal anchor for the mind-body/adaptive-systems cross-reference. Formal extension: Adaptive Systems Panel D (`D-biological-roots.md`), §5 on active inference.

**Breath wing — physiological response to sustained stimulus.**  
Tachyphylaxis — receptor desensitisation under sustained ligand exposure — has a direct respiratory-system analogue: the CO2 chemoreceptors in the brainstem adapt to sustained elevated CO2 during breath-hold or shallow breathing. The net-shift equation (Lanzara 2023, Appendix III) describes this adaptation kinetics: K_H shifts toward K_L as the receptor population desensitises, reducing the net-shift signal ΔR_H even though CO2 concentration is unchanged. Breath practices that work with the desensitisation response (CO2 tolerance training, extended exhale holds) are engaging the tachyphylaxis mechanism. Biological-roots connection: Adaptive Systems Panel D (`D-biological-roots.md`), §2.
