# Writing Department

**Domain:** writing
**Source:** WRIT-101 Literature & Creative Writing Foundational Knowledge Pack
**Status:** Active
**Purpose:** Close reading, narrative craft, and authentic creative writing across K-College -- moving beyond comprehension questions toward genuine engagement with storytelling and authentic expression

## Wings

- Reading as Discovery -- Close reading practices, annotation, word choice, imagery, structure and pacing, multiple interpretations
- Story & Narrative -- Character development, point of view, conflict, dialogue, imagery, pacing, and original fiction
- Poetry & Language -- Forms (haiku, sonnet, free verse), imagery, figurative language, sound devices, rhythm
- The Writing Process -- Drafting, peer workshops, feedback, revision, editing, authentic voice development
- Literary Analysis -- Thematic analysis, symbolism, historical and cultural context, multiple interpretive frameworks

## Entry Point

writ-close-reading

## Concepts

### Reading as Discovery (4 concepts)
- writ-close-reading -- Slowing down to attend to how authors construct meaning through word choice and structure
- writ-textual-evidence -- Supporting interpretations with specific, relevant evidence from the text
- writ-word-choice-connotation -- How word selection shapes meaning and emotional tone
- writ-multiple-interpretations -- Recognizing that texts support multiple valid readings simultaneously

### Story & Narrative (4 concepts)
- writ-character-development -- Tracing how characters change and understanding their motivations
- writ-point-of-view -- How narrator perspective and distance shape reader understanding
- writ-conflict-types -- External vs. internal conflict and how tension drives narrative
- writ-dialogue-pacing -- How speech and sentence rhythm control story tempo

### Poetry & Language (4 concepts)
- writ-poetry-forms -- Haiku (5-7-5), sonnet (14-line iambic), free verse, villanelle -- form as meaning
- writ-figurative-language -- Metaphor, simile, personification, and how figures create meaning
- writ-imagery-sensory -- Appealing to the five senses in writing to create vivid experience
- writ-sound-devices -- Alliteration, assonance, rhyme, rhythm -- music in language

### The Writing Process (4 concepts)
- writ-drafting-discovery -- Writing without censorship to discover what you think and feel
- writ-peer-feedback -- Giving and receiving constructive workshop critique
- writ-revision-strategies -- Rethinking structure, argument, character -- not just fixing typos
- writ-voice-development -- Finding an authentic, distinctive writing voice through practice

### Literary Analysis (4 concepts)
- writ-thematic-analysis -- Identifying and tracing themes across a complete work
- writ-symbolism -- Objects, colors, characters that carry meaning beyond their literal function
- writ-historical-context -- How a text's time period shapes its meaning and reception
- writ-interpretive-frameworks -- Reader-response, feminist, historical materialist readings
