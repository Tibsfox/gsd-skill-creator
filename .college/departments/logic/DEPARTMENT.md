# Logic Department

**Domain:** logic
**Source:** LOG-101 Logic & Formal Systems Foundational Knowledge Pack
**Status:** Active
**Purpose:** Valid reasoning, arguments, fallacies, and formal proof from K through college -- logic as the science of valid reasoning foundational to mathematics, computer science, philosophy, and everyday decision-making

## Wings

- Logical Thinking Foundations -- Sorting, classification, if-then relationships, pattern recognition, rules and rule-following
- Arguments & Reasoning -- Argument structure, premises and conclusions, deductive validity, inductive strength, Socratic method
- Fallacies & Critical Thinking -- Common informal fallacies, propaganda techniques, cognitive biases, critical evaluation
- Formal Logic -- Propositional logic, truth tables, predicates and quantifiers, symbolic notation, proof techniques
- Applied Logic -- Logic in mathematics, programming, law, everyday decisions, paradoxes and their resolutions

log-classification-principles

## Concepts

### Logical Thinking Foundations (4 concepts)
- log-classification-principles -- Sorting objects by properties, Venn diagrams, set membership and subset relationships
- log-if-then-relationships -- Conditional statements, antecedent and consequent, contrapositive equivalence
- log-pattern-recognition -- Identifying logical patterns in sequences, grids, and argument structures
- log-rule-following -- Understanding rules as logical constraints and reasoning about rule application

### Arguments & Reasoning (4 concepts)
- log-argument-structure -- Premises, conclusions, inference rules -- anatomy of a logical argument
- log-deductive-reasoning -- Validity and soundness: structure guarantees truth when premises are true
- log-inductive-reasoning -- Strength and cogency: evidence supports but does not guarantee conclusions
- log-analogical-reasoning -- Arguing by similarity: when analogies are strong and when they break down

### Fallacies & Critical Thinking (4 concepts)
- log-informal-fallacies -- Ad hominem, straw man, false dichotomy, appeal to authority, slippery slope
- log-cognitive-biases -- Confirmation bias, availability heuristic, anchoring -- systematic reasoning errors
- log-propaganda-techniques -- Emotional appeals, bandwagon, loaded language, cherry-picking evidence
- log-argument-evaluation -- Distinguishing good from bad arguments using structured evaluation criteria

### Formal Logic (4 concepts)
- log-propositional-logic -- And, or, not, if-then operators and truth table analysis
- log-truth-tables -- Systematic evaluation of compound propositions using all truth-value combinations
- log-predicate-logic -- Extending propositional logic with for-all and there-exists quantifiers
- log-proof-techniques -- Direct proof, proof by contradiction, proof by induction

### Applied Logic (4 concepts)
- log-logic-in-mathematics -- How formal logic underpins mathematical proof and theorem structure
- log-logic-in-programming -- Boolean expressions, conditionals, loop invariants, formal verification
- log-logic-in-law -- Burden of proof, presumption of innocence, legal reasoning structure
- log-paradoxes -- Liar paradox, Russell's paradox -- boundaries of formal systems
