# Critical Thinking

**Domain:** critical-thinking
**Source:** CRIT-101 Foundational Knowledge Pack
**Status:** Active
**Purpose:** Foundational critical thinking skills spanning claims and evidence evaluation, logical argumentation, fallacy and bias recognition, applied media literacy, and metacognitive self-reflection -- the intellectual toolkit for reasoning well

## Wings

- Claims & Evidence -- Identifying claims, evaluating evidence quality, sourcing, and distinguishing facts from opinions
- Arguments & Logical Reasoning -- Deductive and inductive reasoning, argument structure, validity, soundness, charitable interpretation
- Fallacies & Biases -- Common logical fallacies, cognitive biases, and strategies for clearer thinking
- Applied Critical Thinking -- Media literacy, evaluating news and advertising, scientific literacy, decision-making frameworks
- Metacognition & Intellectual Humility -- Self-regulation of reasoning, intellectual virtues, calibrated confidence, beginner's mind

## Entry Point

crit-claims-facts-opinions

## Concepts

### Claims & Evidence (4 concepts)
- crit-claims-facts-opinions -- Distinguishing factual claims from opinions and evaluating what counts as evidence
- crit-evidence-quality -- Assessing source credibility, sample size, methodology, and relevance of evidence
- crit-sourcing -- Tracing claims to their origins and evaluating primary versus secondary sources
- crit-burden-of-proof -- Who bears responsibility for supporting a claim and why

### Arguments & Logical Reasoning (4 concepts)
- crit-argument-structure -- Identifying premises, conclusions, and the logical links between them
- crit-deductive-reasoning -- Validity, soundness, and reasoning where conclusions follow necessarily from premises
- crit-inductive-reasoning -- Probability-based reasoning, generalizations, and the limits of induction
- crit-charitable-interpretation -- Steel-manning: engaging with the strongest version of opposing arguments

### Fallacies & Biases (5 concepts)
- crit-ad-hominem-straw-man -- Attacking the person instead of the argument; misrepresenting an opponent's position
- crit-false-dichotomy -- Presenting only two options when more exist
- crit-appeal-to-authority -- When citing experts is valid and when it's fallacious
- crit-confirmation-bias -- Seeking information that confirms existing beliefs and ignoring contradictions
- crit-availability-anchoring -- How vivid examples and first impressions distort judgment

### Applied Critical Thinking (4 concepts)
- crit-media-literacy -- Evaluating news articles, headlines, and social media claims
- crit-advertising-persuasion -- Recognizing persuasion techniques in advertising and political messaging
- crit-scientific-literacy -- Reading and evaluating scientific studies, statistics, and expert claims
- crit-decision-frameworks -- Structured approaches to complex decisions under uncertainty

### Metacognition & Intellectual Humility (4 concepts)
- crit-metacognitive-monitoring -- Checking one's own reasoning for errors and updating beliefs on evidence
- crit-intellectual-humility -- Knowing what you don't know; being open to being wrong
- crit-calibrated-confidence -- Matching your confidence level to the actual strength of evidence
- crit-growth-mindset-thinking -- Seeing errors as learning opportunities and developing as a thinker
