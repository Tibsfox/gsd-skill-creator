# History

**Domain:** history
**Source:** HIST-101 Foundational Knowledge Pack
**Status:** Active
**Purpose:** Foundational historical thinking skills spanning source analysis, causation and consequence, continuity and change over time, historical perspective-taking, and argumentation -- developing disciplinary ways of investigating and interpreting the past

## Wings

- Sources & Evidence -- Primary and secondary sources, sourcing, corroboration, contextualization, reliability
- Causation & Consequence -- Immediate versus underlying causes, chains of causation, intended and unintended consequences
- Continuity & Change -- Periodization, turning points, rates of change, patterns, and revolutionary versus gradual change
- Perspectives & Empathy -- Historical empathy, multiple perspectives, inclusive narratives, avoiding presentism
- Historical Argumentation -- Thesis development, evidence use, counterarguments, conventions of historical writing

## Entry Point

hist-primary-secondary-sources

## Concepts

### Sources & Evidence (4 concepts)
- hist-primary-secondary-sources -- Distinguishing original documents from later interpretations and knowing when each is appropriate
- hist-sourcing -- Analyzing who created a source, when, why, and for what audience
- hist-corroboration -- Comparing multiple sources to build a more reliable historical picture
- hist-contextualization -- Placing sources and events in their broader historical, social, and political context

### Causation & Consequence (4 concepts)
- hist-immediate-underlying-causes -- Short-term triggers versus long-term structural causes of historical events
- hist-chains-of-causation -- How one event or decision leads to another in historical sequences
- hist-unintended-consequences -- Effects of historical actions that were not foreseen or intended
- hist-counterfactual-reasoning -- Imagining alternative histories to deepen understanding of real causes

### Continuity & Change (4 concepts)
- hist-periodization -- Dividing history into meaningful periods and debating where the boundaries belong
- hist-turning-points -- Identifying moments that fundamentally altered the direction of historical development
- hist-rates-of-change -- Distinguishing rapid transformation from gradual evolution in historical processes
- hist-patterns-trends -- Recognizing recurring patterns across different times and places in history

### Perspectives & Empathy (4 concepts)
- hist-historical-empathy -- Understanding past actors on their own terms without modern judgment
- hist-multiple-perspectives -- Seeing events through the eyes of different social groups, nations, and individuals
- hist-inclusive-narratives -- Identifying whose voices are absent from traditional accounts and why
- hist-avoiding-presentism -- Resisting the imposition of present-day values onto past actors and societies

### Historical Argumentation (4 concepts)
- hist-thesis-development -- Crafting a defensible historical claim that goes beyond description
- hist-evidence-selection -- Choosing and citing evidence that directly supports historical claims
- hist-counterargument -- Anticipating and addressing alternative interpretations in historical writing
- hist-historical-writing-conventions -- Structure, voice, citation, and the standards of disciplinary historical writing
