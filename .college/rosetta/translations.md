# Adaptive Systems — Cross-Domain Translations

This table maps six central Living Sensoria / Adaptive Systems concepts across all 13 Rosetta clusters. Each cell answers: "How does this concept read in this domain?" Cells are one to two sentences. Clusters receive substantive treatment where Thread C §§6.1–6.7 identified clear structural analogies; four clusters (Electronics/LED, Broadcasting/RBH, Business/BCM, Creative/FFA) receive light-touch cells per Thread C §6.8. The table is a quick-reference artefact; full derivations live in the corresponding Adaptive Systems department panels and primary-source citations in `concepts.md`.

Row order follows the six concept axes defined in the Living Sensoria v1.49.561 spec brief. Column order follows cluster numbers 1–13.

| **Gradient descent** | Logistic population growth: net growth rate r(1 − N/K) is the ecological gradient; population descends toward the K-attractor by reducing the error N − K. | Servo-loop error correction: a motor controller integrates position error and steps the drive signal in the direction that reduces the error; this is gradient descent on a quadratic cost in encoder counts. | MRAS parameter update: controller gain θ steps in proportion to tracking error e(t) = y_ref − y, descending the Lyapunov cost surface until e → 0 (Sastry 1989, Ch. 3). | Simulated annealing: the optimiser descends an energy landscape by accepting downhill moves always and uphill moves with probability exp(−ΔE/T); cooling T = gradient-descent learning-rate decay (Kirkpatrick 1983). | Iterative composition: a designer steps a layout toward a visual target by reducing the perceptual difference between current state and goal; each revision is a gradient step on an implicit aesthetic loss. | Margin optimisation: a business iteratively adjusts price or cost-structure to reduce the gap between target and actual margin; price-setting is gradient descent on a revenue loss surface. | Vision refinement: a roadmap is iteratively updated by reducing the gap between current-state and target-state; each revision is a step toward the stated vision, analogous to gradient descent on a strategy surface. | Adaptive bitrate: the ABR algorithm steps video quality up or down to reduce the error between target buffer level and measured buffer level; each step is a gradient update on a rebuffering-risk objective. | Free-energy minimisation: the brain steps its internal model parameters to reduce prediction error D_KL[q(I) ‖ p(I|S)]; neural weight update is gradient descent on variational free energy (Friston 2010, Fig. 1). | Harmonic resolution: a melodic phrase steps through intervals toward tonic resolution; each voice-leading move reduces the dissonance error, descending the harmonic-tension surface. | Orbital mechanics: trajectory correction burns step spacecraft velocity toward the target orbit by reducing the Δv budget error; each burn is a gradient step on the rendezvous cost function. | TD-error-driven weight update: δ = r + γV(s′) − V(s); actor weights step proportional to δ, descending the expected-return loss surface (Barto 1983, ACE). | Shader parameter fitting: a GLSL shader tuned to match a reference image applies iterative parameter updates proportional to the pixel-error gradient; GPU-parallelised per-fragment descent. |
| **Equilibrium / homeostasis** | Carrying-capacity attractor K: logistic dynamics make K a Lyapunov-stable equilibrium — disturbances produce transient deviations that decay back to K (net growth → 0 at N = K). | Voltage-regulator setpoint: a linear regulator holds V_out at a reference voltage by sourcing or sinking current proportional to the error V_ref − V_out; the setpoint is the regulator's Lyapunov equilibrium. | SLO steady-state: the system is at homeostasis when p99 latency and error rate stay within agreed bounds; an adaptive autoscaler restores this equilibrium after traffic spikes. | Thermodynamic equilibrium: a system at thermal equilibrium has zero net heat flux; temperature is uniform and entropy is maximised — the global Lyapunov minimum of the energy landscape (Lanzara 2023 net-shift derivation, Appendix III). | Stylistic signature: a mature artist's style is a stable attractor — individual works vary around it but the overall aesthetic signature persists; external critique may perturb but rarely shifts the equilibrium. | Market-clearing price: supply and demand converge to an equilibrium price at which quantity supplied equals quantity demanded; price deviations are self-correcting through market feedback. | Mission setpoint: a mature vision document holds a stable strategic equilibrium; the organisation's actions are pulled toward the stated mission the way a Lyapunov function pulls trajectories to its minimum. | Broadcast schedule steady-state: a station at equilibrium fills its schedule reliably with programmes, managing buffer levels and playout timing to maintain a stable audience service. | Markov blanket persistence: any system that maintains a characteristic internal-state distribution over time has a blanket partition {E, S, A, I} (Kirchhoff 2018, §2); persistence of the partition is the scientific definition of homeostasis. | Tonic resolution: the I chord is the Lyapunov minimum of Western harmonic space; all phrase arcs converge back to tonic, making tonic the musical homeostatic setpoint. | Orbital equilibrium: a satellite in a stable orbit is at gravitational-centrifugal equilibrium; perturbations (atmospheric drag, J2) are corrected by station-keeping burns to restore the homeostatic orbital state. | Value-function convergence: Q*(s, a) is the fixed point of the Bellman operator; policy evaluation converges to this equilibrium under mild regularity conditions (Barto 1983; Sastry 1989). | Render-state stability: a real-time renderer is at equilibrium when frame latency and GPU utilisation stay within the target performance envelope; dynamic LOD restores equilibrium after scene-complexity spikes. |
| **Noise as exploration** | Ecological dispersal: random individual movement across habitat patches is environmental noise that lets the population discover unoccupied niches — biological dispersal as stochastic exploration (Foraging theory). | Dithering: adding quantisation noise to a low-bit-depth signal prevents harmonic distortion by randomising rounding errors; noise spreads the error energy across the spectrum rather than locking it to harmonics. | Persistent excitation: an MRAS requires enough input variation (noise) to excite all system modes, ensuring the parameter estimator can identify the full plant; without it, identification converges to a low-rank subspace (Sastry 1989, Ch. 5). | Thermal noise as annealing: at high temperature T, a Boltzmann-distributed system accepts large uphill moves in energy; noise magnitude determines the breadth of state-space exploration before the system settles (Kirkpatrick 1983). | Sketch variance: a designer generates many rough sketches with deliberate variation before committing to a direction; sketch noise is structured exploration of the design space. | Market experimentation: A/B pricing tests introduce noise into the standard price point; the variance in outcomes reveals demand elasticity, turning noise into market information. | Strategic ambiguity as search: a vision document with deliberately open-ended language invites multiple interpretations, letting teams explore the solution space rather than converging prematurely on a fixed implementation. | Frequency agility: a radio transmitter randomises its carrier frequency across a spread-spectrum band; the noise in channel selection defeats jamming and also explores signal path diversity — security-driven noise as exploration. | Stochastic gradient Langevin dynamics (SGLD): the Langevin noise term injects Gaussian perturbations into the free-energy gradient, preventing variational collapse to a point estimate; noise drives posterior exploration (Friston 2010 epistemic term). | Improvisation: a musician exploring far from the tonal centre — high exploration — risks losing coherence but discovers novel harmonic combinations; noise in note choice drives the search across harmonic state space. | Debris-field navigation: a spacecraft in a debris field uses stochastic trajectory planning to avoid deterministic dead-ends; randomised search finds collision-free corridors that deterministic search misses. | ε-greedy / softmax sampling: training noise at finite batch size (Boltzmann temperature) allows the optimiser to escape shallow local minima; ε-greedy explicitly randomises action selection to maintain exploration (Barto 1983, ASE). | Stochastic sampling in path-tracing: Monte Carlo ray integration is structured noise that, when averaged, converges to the correct rendering integral; more samples reduce noise and narrow the exploration variance. |
| **Feedback loop** | Trophic cascade: predator population removes herbivores, allowing vegetation to recover, which supports more herbivores — a multi-trophic negative feedback loop that stabilises the ecosystem around a dynamic equilibrium. | Closed-loop amplifier: an op-amp with negative feedback drives its output until the differential input approaches zero; feedback reduces gain variability and extends the linear operating range (Bode 1945 feedback amplifier theory). | Adaptive control loop: controller measures plant output, computes error against reference model, updates gain parameters via the Lyapunov-derived update law, re-applies — a closed control loop that adapts its own gains (Sastry 1989, Ch. 3). | Heat engine cycle: a Carnot engine extracts work from a temperature differential; the working fluid absorbs heat at T_H, expands, expels heat at T_C, and repeats — thermodynamic feedback between heat reservoirs and working fluid. | Creative critique loop: an artist presents work to peers, receives critical feedback, revises, represents — iterative feedback loop on an aesthetic objective function (implicit in studio critique practice). | Management control cycle: plan → execute → measure → adjust is a business feedback loop; variance from plan drives the next period's corrective action, implementing a discrete-time control system. | Strategy-execution loop: a vision document is updated when execution reveals misalignment between stated goals and observed outcomes; the discrepancy is the feedback signal driving vision revision. | Automatic gain control (AGC): a broadcast receiver measures received signal strength and feeds back a correction to the RF amplifier gain, maintaining constant audio/video output level despite varying signal conditions. | Scientific method as inference loop: hypothesis → experiment → observation → model update is a feedback loop on a generative model of reality; Bayesian updating is the formal version (Friston 2010, free-energy as surprise minimisation). | Ensemble co-regulation: each musician's action generates an acoustic reward signal for every other player; the ensemble converges through reciprocal feedback — multi-agent actor-critic implemented acoustically (Barto 1983 multi-agent extension). | Orbital feedback control: a spacecraft's attitude control system measures orientation error (gyroscopes), computes corrective torque (reaction wheels), applies it, and re-measures — a closed-loop control system with millisecond cycle time. | Actor-critic feedback: the critic estimates V(s) and computes δ; δ is fed back to the actor as a learning signal; the actor's improved policy changes the state distribution, which changes what the critic learns — two coupled feedback loops (Barto 1983). | Rendering feedback in procedural systems: a shader writes to a buffer; the next frame samples that buffer as input texture; the read-write cycle creates a feedback loop that drives emergent visual dynamics (Shadertoy-style feedback). |
| **Specialised composition** | Niche partitioning: species in an ecosystem partition the resource space by specialising on different food sources, microhabitats, or activity times; specialisation reduces competition and increases total system throughput. | Amiga chipset architecture: the Copper, Blitter, and Agnus are dedicated co-processors, each responsible for one class of graphics task; heterogeneous specialisation removes the bottleneck of a single general CPU (Commodore-Amiga 1985). | Microservice decomposition: a distributed system decomposes functionality into independently deployable services, each specialised for one domain (auth, billing, routing); specialisation enables independent scaling and failure isolation. | Energy conversion specialisation: a power plant's boiler, turbine, and generator are specialised stages — each converts one energy form to another; no single component does all three, and each is optimised for its narrow task. | Production pipeline roles: a film crew partitions work into director, cinematographer, gaffer, editor roles — specialised composition where each specialist optimises a narrow concern within a shared creative objective. | Organisational specialisation: a firm's legal, finance, and engineering departments are specialised composants of the enterprise; each unit optimises a narrow function while the management layer integrates outputs. | Systems-of-systems architecture: a forward-looking ROF document partitions a complex system into specialised subsystems — each subsystem has bounded scope, and the whole emerges from their composition. | Broadcast workflow specialisation: acquisition, ingest, edit, playout, and monitoring are separate specialised stages in a broadcast chain; each stage is staffed by specialists and uses dedicated tooling (RBH operational architecture). | Modular experimental design: a scientific experiment partitions measurement, control, and analysis into specialised instruments; no single apparatus does all three, and each is optimised for precision on its narrow concern. | Ensemble roles: in an orchestra, strings, brass, woodwinds, and percussion are specialised groups; the conductor integrates their outputs into a unified performance — heterogeneous specialisation enabling polyphonic richness. | Mission subsystems: a spacecraft decomposes into propulsion, attitude control, communication, power, and payload subsystems — specialised hardware units composed by the mission operations team into a functioning whole. | Agentic decomposition: a skill-creator pipeline partitions an autonomous task into planner, executor, verifier, and integrator agents; each agent specialises on a narrow concern, and the pipeline composes their outputs (gsd-skill-creator architecture). | GPU shader pipeline: vertex, tessellation, geometry, and fragment shaders are specialised programmable stages; each processes one class of geometric or pixel data, and the pipeline composes their outputs into a rendered frame. |
| **Boundary condition** | Habitat boundary / ecotone: the edge between two biomes is a Markov blanket in ecological terms — species on either side have conditional independence of internal dynamics given the shared boundary interface (Kirchhoff 2018, §2). | PCB signal boundary: a printed circuit board edge-connector is the boundary between internal and external electrical domains; impedance matching at the boundary prevents signal reflection, enforcing the board's electrical blanket. | Network perimeter: a firewall defines the boundary between an internal subnet and the external internet; sensory states are ingress-filtered packets, active states are egress responses — a Markov blanket in network-security terms. | Phase boundary: the liquid-solid interface in a crystallising material is a boundary condition where internal lattice order and external melt disorder meet; the interface propagates at a rate determined by latent heat flux. | Gallery wall: the frame of a painting or the exhibition wall is the boundary condition that separates the work from its context; what is inside the frame is art, what is outside is world — a creative Markov blanket. | Regulatory boundary: a firm's compliance boundary defines which activities are internal (managed) versus external (regulated); the regulatory interface — audits, filings — is the sensory/active blanket surface. | Foxglove boundary condition: the Foxglove preface (pp. xxv–xxxii, tibsfox.com) frames the "boundary condition of being" — the cognitive surface at which an adaptive agent's internal model meets environmental evidence (Foxglove 2026). | Broadcast frequency limits: allocated spectrum bands are hard boundary conditions — frequencies outside the licensed band are external; adjacent-channel interference is the coupling at the blanket surface. | Markov blanket of a system: the {E, S, A, I} partition means the internal state I and external state E are conditionally independent given blanket states {S, A}; this is the formal definition of a system boundary (Kirchhoff 2018, §2–3). | Key signature boundary: the tonal key is the boundary condition of a harmonic system — notes within the key are internal states, chromatic alterations are excursions across the blanket; modulation is a blanket-shift event. | Magnetosphere as blanket: Earth's magnetosphere is the active-state boundary separating internal (protected atmosphere) from external (solar wind); its geometry is the planet's Markov blanket in the space-physics sense. | Skill activation perimeter: a skill's `trigger` clause is its Markov blanket boundary — it defines the sensory surface (context tokens that match) and active surface (actions the skill takes); contexts outside the trigger are external states (gsd-skill-creator architecture). | Framebuffer boundary: the framebuffer is the boundary between the GPU's internal render state and the display system's external pixel domain; a screen-space boundary condition (viewport clip) defines what crosses the blanket. |
