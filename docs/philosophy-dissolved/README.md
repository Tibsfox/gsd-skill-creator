# Philosophy Dissolved

**Classical paradoxes resolved through mathematical foundations.**

14 paradoxes across 5 rooms. Each follows the same four-beat structure: present the paradox in its strongest philosophical form, identify the resolving mathematical foundation, show the mathematics, and connect to a GSD ecosystem component where the resolution is operationalized.

---

## Rooms

| Room | Theme | Paradoxes |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 1 | Evidence & Confirmation | Hempel's Raven, Goodman's Grue, Duhem-Quine |
| 2 | Identity & Persistence | Ship of Theseus, Sorites, Teletransportation |
| 3 | Infinity & Motion | Zeno's Dichotomy, Zeno's Achilles, Thomson's Lamp |
| 4 | Decision & Prediction | Newcomb's Problem, Surprise Examination, Sleeping Beauty |
| 5 | Self-Reference & Emergence | Liar's Paradox, Chinese Room, Mary's Room |

## Quick Start

Each paradox document is **fully self-contained** — start anywhere. If you want the guided path, read rooms 1 through 5 in order. The progression moves from evidence to identity to infinity to decision to self-reference.

**Flagships** (start here if you read only three):
- `rooms/01-evidence-confirmation/hempel-raven.md` — the Amiga Principle as epistemological theorem
- `rooms/02-identity-persistence/ship-of-theseus.md` — identity as boundary condition
- `rooms/05-self-reference-emergence/chinese-room.md` — understanding as emergent functor

## The Through-Line

Philosophy asks good questions. Mathematics answers them. The paradoxes aren't failures of philosophy — they're indicators of where the mathematical frontier was at the time they were posed. Each resolution records the moment a new mathematical tool extended human understanding past a previous limit.
