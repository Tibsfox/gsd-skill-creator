# Who I Am

## Origin: SSSS.ME — Foxy's Playground (2010)

In December 2010, at the boundary between Minecraft Alpha and Beta, I started a
server called **SSSS.ME — Foxy's Playground**. Three dedicated servers, 24/7,
public, no whitelist. Anyone could walk in.

| Server | Port | What It Was |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Vanilla PVP/Survival | :25565 | Raw survival. PvP enabled. The real world. |
| RPG Multiworld | :25566 | mcMMO, Factions, iConomy with dynamic supply/demand pricing |
| Creative Build | :25575 | Towny protection, flight, no mobs, no PvP. The workshop. |

Plus the infrastructure that made it a community, not just a game server:

- **IRC:** ssss.me:6666
- **Mumble voice:** ssss.me:64738
- **Streaming music:** ssss.me:8000
- **Forums:** mc.tibsfox.com

Three different modes for three different kinds of people. Same community.
Same forest. Different doors.

### The Rules

Rule #1: *"Listen to Tibsfox, he pays the bills."*

The rest:
- No griefing (stealing allowed on PVP — that's the deal you accept when you walk in)
- No cheat/fly/speed/item mods
- No chat spam
- "Do not be a loser"
- Respect mods and fellow players

"Do not be a loser" was the honest version of what every community code of conduct
tries to say in 500 words.

### What the Server Taught Me

Running a Minecraft server for years teaches you things about trust that no
specification can capture:

- **You learn who shows up.** Not who says they will — who actually does.
- **You learn who builds for others.** Some people build for themselves. Some
  people build things the whole server uses. You can tell the difference in a week.
- **You learn who griefs.** And you learn that griefers are a small minority
  who take up a disproportionate amount of attention. The system shouldn't be
  designed around them.
- **You learn that trust is earned.** Not bought, not inherited, not declared.
  Someone who shows up every day and builds honestly earns trust. Someone who
  shows up with a hacked client and a stolen account does not. No algorithm
  needed — the community knows.
- **You learn that different people need different spaces.** The PvP player
  and the creative builder are both part of the community. They need different
  servers with different rules, connected by the same identity and the same
  social fabric.

## The Through-Line

The idea behind ssss.me is the same idea behind this project:

**Build a space. Open the doors. Let people create.**

A Minecraft server where you run three game modes with IRC and Mumble and
streaming music and forums is not that different from a repo where you run
ten research projects with muses and chipsets and a trust federation. The
tools changed. The principle didn't.

"Foxy's Playground" is the same name because it's the same place. The server
became the repo. The community became the forest. The trust rules I learned
from running a server for years became the trust axioms in the code.

## Trust Axioms

Three axioms that constrain the entire trust system. Learned from running a
Minecraft server, encoded into architecture:

1. **Respect does not equal trust.** The forest has elders still standing. I
   respect them. I do not hand them my trust vector for free. Seniority does
   not confer bilateral trust. Age of a tree is not the strength of its
   connections. I am not Old Growth. I am still a tree. There are those around
   me older than I. I have respect for my elders still standing. But that does
   not mean I trust them.

2. **Trust cannot be bought.** No mechanism exists to purchase, trade, or
   shortcut trust. Not with tokens, not with contribution volume, not with
   time served, not with status. The system has no currency. Any feature that
   creates a path from resources to trust is a design violation.

3. **Trust is earned through honesty and hard work.** Show up. Be honest.
   Do the work. Do it together. The system reflects what people actually build
   between each other — not what they claim, not what they pay for, not what
   they inherit. Honesty and hard work, observed by the people around you.
   That's it.

The work serves real communities:

  real art for real people. Creative community with deep trust networks built
  on shared experience and mutual support.
- **Salish heritage** — the Pacific Northwest is home. The PNW research series
  (Columbia Valley rainforest, Cascade Range, avian ecology, mammalian ecology,
  bio-physics sensing) is not borrowed metaphor. It is autobiography. The
  naming is true because it comes from someone who is these forests.
- **Creative arts and makers** — the crafters, builders, artists making things
  with their hands. The same spirit as the Minecraft creative server: here's
  a workshop, here are the tools, now make something.

## Open Source

This is all open source. Clone the repo, run your own instance, build your
own community. Same model as the Minecraft server: here's the world, here
are the rules, now make it yours. The patterns, rules, and architecture are
a gift. Once cloned, it's yours to reshape however you want.

That's who I am. A tree in the forest, still growing.
