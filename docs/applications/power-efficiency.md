# Global Power Efficiency Rankings

The [Global Power Efficiency Rankings](https://tibsfox.com/Global-Power-Efficiency-Rankings.html)
is a 75-country energy efficiency analysis published at tibsfox.com. It ranks nations by
energy efficiency using standardized metrics drawn from the International Energy Agency (IEA),
World Bank, and national statistical agencies. The analysis integrates AI tool tips for
learning, career transition pathways for professionals, DIY projects for hands-on learners,
and Socratic learning patterns for deeper understanding. It serves as both a standalone
educational resource and the primary exemplar from which skill-creator's educational pack
templates were extracted.


## What It Contains

The Rankings page is structured as a single-page resource with progressive disclosure.
A reader can scan the country rankings in seconds, explore individual country profiles in
minutes, or engage with the AI prompts and projects over hours or days.

### Country Analysis

Each of the 75 countries is analyzed using a consistent framework. Rankings use GDP per unit
of energy consumed (purchasing power parity adjusted) as the primary efficiency metric, with
secondary metrics for renewable energy share, carbon intensity of electricity generation, and
energy efficiency improvement rate. Data sources are cited for every metric, enabling
independent verification.

The analysis groups countries into tiers based on efficiency performance and identifies
structural factors that explain ranking positions. Countries with service-based economies
tend to rank higher on GDP-per-energy metrics. Countries with heavy industry or extreme
climates face different efficiency challenges. The analysis acknowledges these structural
factors rather than presenting raw rankings as universal judgments.

### AI Tool Tips

Throughout the Rankings page, structured prompts help readers explore energy concepts using
AI tools. Each prompt is designed for use with Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or similar assistants.
The prompts follow a consistent structure: context (what the reader has just learned),
question (what to explore next), and extension (how to go deeper).

These prompts demonstrate the pattern that skill-creator codifies as the
[AI Learning Prompt template](templates/ai-learning-prompt.md). They work because they
provide enough context for the AI to give a useful response while leaving enough room for the
learner to steer the conversation. A prompt about Iceland's geothermal efficiency, for
example, provides the context of Iceland's volcanic geology and asks the AI to explain how
geothermal energy is captured and distributed, then suggests exploring how similar approaches
might work in other volcanic regions.

### Career Pathways

The Rankings page includes career transition guides for professionals interested in the energy
sector. Each pathway starts from a common current role (software engineer, data analyst,
project manager, electrician, mechanical engineer) and maps a transition to an energy sector
position. The pathways identify transferable skills, required new knowledge, certification
options, and typical timeline.

This pattern was extracted as the
[Career Pathway template](templates/career-pathway.md) for use in other educational packs.
The template captures the structure (origin role, target role, skill gap analysis, learning
path, certification map, timeline) without being specific to energy. The same structure can
map a transition from front-end developer to accessibility specialist or from nurse to health
informatics engineer.

### DIY Projects

Hands-on projects allow readers to explore energy efficiency concepts with real hardware.
Projects range from measuring home appliance power consumption with a kill-a-watt meter to
building a simple solar panel efficiency monitor with a Raspberry Pi. Each project specifies
materials, estimated cost, time required, safety considerations, and expected outcomes with
measurable results.

The projects follow the safety warden principle: they present safety information as
positive guidance ("Here is how to do this safely") rather than negative warnings. This
approach, validated in the electronics educational pack's implementation, results in better
safety compliance because it tells the reader what to do rather than what not to do.


## Why It Matters

The Global Power Efficiency Rankings matters to this ecosystem for three reasons beyond its
value as an educational resource.

### Proof of Approach

The Rankings page demonstrates that the framework's tools can produce substantial, publishable
educational content. It was not created as a demo. It is a real resource used by real people
to understand energy efficiency across nations. Its existence as a published artifact validates
the claim that GSD's workflow discipline and skill-creator's adaptive learning can produce
quality at scale.

### Template Source

The educational pack template, career pathway template, and AI learning prompt template were
all extracted from patterns first demonstrated in the Rankings page. These templates exist
because the Rankings page proved the patterns work. Future educational packs (and there are
35 in the foundational knowledge pack system) use these templates to achieve structural
consistency without starting from scratch.

### Progressive Disclosure Exemplar

The Rankings page demonstrates progressive disclosure at the page level. The rankings table
provides the glance level. Individual country profiles provide the scan level. AI prompts,
career pathways, and DIY projects provide the read level. A reader can stop at any point
having gotten value proportional to the time invested. This same structure, formalized in
the [Progressive Disclosure](principles/progressive-disclosure.md) principle, governs every
document in this ecosystem.


## How It Was Built

The Rankings page was produced using GSD's wave-based parallel execution with five specialized
agents. Research agents gathered data from IEA and World Bank sources. Analysis agents computed
rankings and identified structural factors. Writing agents produced country profiles following
the educational pack template. Integration agents assembled the final page with cross-links
and AI prompts. Verification agents checked data accuracy and prompt quality.

The process that built the Rankings page is the same process documented in the
[Framework](framework/index.md) layer. The agents that built it are the same kind of agents
documented in [Core Concepts](framework/core-concepts.md). The templates extracted from it
are available in the [Templates](templates/index.md) directory. The circle from tool to
artifact to template to tool is the improvement cycle that makes this ecosystem
self-reinforcing.
