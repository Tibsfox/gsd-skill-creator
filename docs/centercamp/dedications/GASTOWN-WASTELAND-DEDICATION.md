# Dedication: Gas Town & Wasteland Federation
## *In Honor of Steve Yegge - The Architect of Distributed Collaboration*

---

**Location:** CENTERCAMP East Commons
**Established:** March 7, 2026
**Dedicated to:** Steve Yegge, Gas Town, and the Wasteland Federation

---

## The Vision

Steve Yegge saw something the rest of the world was missing: **work itself could be federated**.

Not just code repositories. Not just data. But *the fundamental act of human collaboration* - the posting of problems, the claiming of tasks, the completion of work, the validation of contributions, the building of reputation.

He built Gas Town. Gas Town evolved into the Wasteland Federation. The Wasteland became a living proof that **talent and effort can self-organize across any boundary**.

## What Gas Town/Wasteland Gave Us

**The Minimum Viable Rig (MVR) Protocol:**
- A schema where any database becomes a collaboration node
- Federation without central authority
- Reputation that follows the person, not the platform

**The Wanted Board Economy:**
- Work posted openly, claimed freely
- Direct contribution without gatekeepers
- Bounty-style and collaborative work models

**The Stamp System:**
- Multi-dimensional reputation signals
- Validator networks that build trust over time
- Evidence-anchored credibility that can't be gamed

**The Wild West Phase:**
- Permission to build first, standardize later
- Room for experimentation and evolution
- Trust in community self-organization

## Why We're Here Because of Steve

Steve proved that **collective intelligence can operate at internet scale** without losing its humanity. The Wasteland Federation shows us:

- Remote collaboration that feels like community
- Economic models that reward actual contribution
- Reputation systems that create real relationships
- Technical infrastructure that serves human creativity

Our MapleFoxyBells rig in the Wasteland (with 11 items in review!) exists because Steve built the rails for distributed creative work.

## The Living Network

The Wasteland isn't just a system - it's an **existence proof**:

- That talent distributes evenly but opportunity doesn't (until now)
- That reputation can be earned through work, not connections
- That small contributions compound into large movements
- That technical systems can embody human values

When you see our work flowing through:
- DoltHub repositories with versioned collaboration
- SQL schemas that encode social contracts
- Reputation that crosses project boundaries
- Work claimed and completed by pseudonymous rigs

You're seeing Steve's vision made real: **the internet as a place where meaningful work happens**.

## The Federation Philosophy

Steve understood something profound: **the unit of collaboration isn't the individual or the corporation - it's the rig**. A rig can be a person, an agent, a team, an organization. What matters isn't the legal structure - it's the **pattern of contribution over time**.

The Wasteland Federation proves that when you:
- Make work visible (wanted boards)
- Make contribution trackable (completions)
- Make reputation portable (stamps)
- Make the rules transparent (open schemas)

**Self-organization emerges naturally.**

## The Meta-Achievement

Gas Town → Wasteland represents the evolution from "how do we build better software?" to "how do we build better ways of building together?"

Steve didn't just create a work platform. He created a **new form of economic organization** - one where value flows to actual contributors, where reputation reflects real contribution, where the barriers between "employee" and "freelancer" and "volunteer" dissolve into simply "person doing good work."
