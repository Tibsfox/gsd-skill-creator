## What Worked

- **Explorer 1 as pre-institutional foundation aligned cleanly with Priester's pre-Seattle career formation.** Both are systems whose foundational identity was formed before the institution that later housed them, and the Van Allen belt discovery literally predates NASA the way Mwandishi and the ECM recordings predate Cornish. The structural parallel is exact and the prose lands it in a single paragraph of the Summary.
- **Doolittle & Brumm 2014 PNAS provided rigorous scientific grounding.** The Hermit Thrush claim -- that the bird sings harmonic-series intervals -- is not folk ornithology or poetic extrapolation; it is a statistically validated peer-reviewed result in the *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, and the release notes cite it. Peer-reviewed biology as primary source protects the claim against skepticism.
- **The Cornish-Doolittle coincidence emerged from real research, not invention.** Emily Doolittle was Cornish faculty when she co-authored the 2014 paper. Priester taught at Cornish from 1979 to 2011. The two overlapped institutionally for decades. The harmonic-series bird and the continuous-pitch trombonist shared the same jazz conservatory. This is a genuine structural resonance, not a stretch, and crediting it strengthens both halves.
- **The within-family Turdidae comparison (Varied Thrush at 2, Hermit Thrush at 7) activated a new engine capability.** First within-family comparison in SPS. The timbre-melody tradeoff emerges from the comparison, establishing a two-axis framework for future same-family pairs (chickadee / nuthatch, warbler / warbler, etc.).
- **The continuous-pitch principle unified four systems at one degree.** Trombone slide, Hermit Thrush syrinx, Lobaria folded thallus, Explorer 1 orbital sweep. Four mechanisms, one generative principle (complexity from continuous operation within physical constraints). Cross-syncing these in the Summary avoided the usual S36 / SPS segregation.

## What Could Be Better

- **The Central District thread's "diversification" framing needs reinforcement in later degrees.** Priester is the first non-CD primary, but the engine should not treat this as permanent departure. The Central District will return (Anderson-tier figures, Jackson Street hubs, Garfield alumni). The degree-7 prose uses "diversified, not abandoned" correctly but later degrees should echo the language until the thread demonstrably resumes.
- **The dedication to Bunsen is structurally correct but emotionally thin.** Bunsen's role as co-inventor of spectral analysis is real and relevant, but the dedication paragraph reads as background rather than a personal tribute. Future degrees should find dedications closer to the human stakes of the research (Grissom/White/Chaffee at degree 54 is the model; Bunsen is further from that register).
- **The PNW Hermit Thrush population is altitudinal-migrant, but the Doolittle & Brumm recordings were made in the Rocky Mountains.** The chain of inference (PNW Hermit Thrushes sing like Rocky Mountain Hermit Thrushes because they are the same species) is sound but not sourced. A follow-up should confirm whether PNW-specific Hermit Thrush recordings have been harmonically analyzed, or whether the claim rests on geographic range and vocal consistency assumptions.
- **The brass-preservation/expansion axis needs a third data point.** Two is a line; three is a pattern. The axis currently has Standifer (preserve) and Priester (expand). A future brass figure -- Floyd Standifer's students, Cornish's later brass faculty, or a secondary trumpet player -- should occupy a middle position to make the axis robust.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Central District lock broken.** Priester (born 1935 Chicago, DuSable -> Arkestra -> Roach -> Ellington -> Mwandishi -> Cornish) is the first primary S36 artist not rooted in the Central District. The seven-degree CD monopoly is diversified without being abandoned; the thread pauses, it does not close.

2. **The Pedagogue-Draw as tenth artist-city pattern.** A compound pattern -- drawn to Seattle from elsewhere (Draw) + expansion-oriented educator (Teacher). Irreducible to either component alone. The engine's first documented compound artist-city relationship.

3. **Brass lineage gains structural depth.** Trumpet (Standifer, 4, discrete/valved/preservation) vs. trombone (Priester, 7, continuous/slide/expansion) maps onto pedagogy, institution, and NASA mission role. Two instruments, two poles, one analytically dense family.

4. **Explorer 1 as pre-institutional foundation.** January 31, 1958, predates NASA itself. The Juno I launch, the Van Allen belt discovery by Geiger-Mueller counter saturation, and the ABMA/JPL pre-NASA status mirror Priester's pre-Seattle artistic formation: foundational identity formed before the institution that later housed it.

5. **ECM label thread activated.** Frisell (degree 1) + Priester (degree 7) -- two ECM artists, different instruments, shared aesthetic commitment to space and texture. The first label-level thread documented in the engine.

6. **Mountain-forest elevation opens the vertical axis.** The Hermit Thrush (Part B) breeds 500-1,800m, extending the engine's ecological range from sea level upward. Lobaria pulmonaria (Part A organism pairing) shares the mountain-forest substrate -- artist, organism, and species co-locate on the elevational gradient.

7. **Continuous-pitch access parallels continuous folded surface.** The trombone slide's microtonal continuity, the Hermit Thrush's harmonic-series intervals, and Lobaria's folded thallus all produce complexity from continuous (rather than discrete) generative mechanisms. Three systems, one generative principle.

8. **Harmonic series as cross-domain invariant.** The harmonic series is the mathematical skeleton of tonal music, the overtone structure of every resonant physical cavity, the generative pattern of the Hermit Thrush's song, and the emergent property of Priester's continuous slide. Degree 7 documents the harmonic series as a cross-domain invariant that appears independently in music theory, biology, physics, and engineering.

9. **First within-family bird comparison activates the SPS engine's taxonomic depth.** Varied Thrush (2) and Hermit Thrush (7) are both Turdidae but occupy opposite acoustic niches (timbre vs. melody). The comparison establishes that same-family pairs can document complementary allocation strategies and provides a template for future within-family comparisons.

10. **Peer-reviewed primary-source grounding strengthens structural claims.** Doolittle & Brumm (2014, *PNAS*) is not a metaphor source or a poetic reference; it is a statistically rigorous peer-reviewed biology paper, and citing it puts the harmonic-series-bird claim on scientific foundations rather than aesthetic assertion. Future degrees should prefer peer-reviewed primary sources over secondary summaries wherever the structural claim depends on scientific validity.
