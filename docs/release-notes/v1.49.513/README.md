Don Lanphere was born in Wenatchee, Washington, in 1928 -- the first eastern-Washington origin in the S36 engine and the engine's first artist from east of the Cascade crest. Wenatchee is one hundred fifty miles from Seattle across the rain-shadow wall, a working-class apple-and-packinghouse town where jazz was a radio phenomenon rather than a club-scene phenomenon. Lanphere picked up the alto saxophone and then the tenor saxophone as a teenager, developed his ear on Lester Young and Charlie Parker recordings, and at twenty-one walked onto 52nd Street in Manhattan and into the house bands at the Three Deuces and Club Carousel. In 1948 and 1949 he recorded on Blue Note with Fats Navarro -- the trumpet player who would die of tuberculosis and heroin complications in July 1950 at twenty-six -- and with Max Roach, J.J. Johnson, and Dexter Gordon. The recordings exist. The young tenor voice on them is the real thing: a player who had absorbed Lester Young's horizontal melodic line and Parker's chromatic approach and combined them into an early bebop voice with a distinctive tone. Lanphere was the bebop root node in the engine's genealogy because he was physically present, at a trackable date, at the creation site of the vocabulary that would branch into Hadley Caliman's hard bop (degree 9) and Bud Shank's cool jazz (degree 10). The root documented after the branches, the way Explorer 7 measured the radiation budget after Explorers 1 through 4 had already mapped the Van Allen belts.

And then the fall. Lanphere contracted heroin addiction the way so many of his 52nd Street contemporaries did -- Navarro, Parker, Gordon, Powell, Mingus at moments -- as the scene's occupational hazard. By 1951 he had returned to Wenatchee. The recording career stopped. The public career stopped. For approximately thirty years he worked outside of music, drifted in and out of the local scene in the Pacific Northwest, and carried the bebop vocabulary inside him without using it. The New York press forgot him. Jazz history wrote him out, the way it wrote out so many of the 52nd Street players who did not die dramatically. What remained was faith -- Lanphere became a committed Christian through his recovery -- and the intact vocabulary. In 1983 Fresh Sound Records released a recording. He started playing again. Through the 1990s he recorded steadily, toured the Pacific Northwest and Europe, taught at Cornish College of the Arts and at workshops, and emerged as the regional bebop elder. He died in 2003 at seventy-five, a full career late-stage in a sustainable equilibrium where the fire had been reduced to a slow burn rather than extinguished. The Prodigal: rise, fall, redemption. Both the fall and the return are required. The fall without the return is The Erasure (Patti Bown, degree 6). The return without the fall is The Stay (Ernestine Anderson, degree 3). Lanphere's arc is a fourteenth artist-city pattern and the only one in the engine so far that requires active destruction followed by recovery.

On October 13, 1959, at 15:22 UTC at Launch Complex 5 at Cape Canaveral, a Juno II rocket lifted off carrying Explorer 7. The satellite weighed forty-one and a half kilograms and carried five instruments, of which the most consequential was the Suomi-Parent Net Flux Radiometer. Verner Suomi at the University of Wisconsin and Robert Parent at the same institution had designed an instrument that measured the difference between incoming solar radiation and outgoing planetary thermal radiation -- the net flux that determines whether Earth's climate is in balance. The instrument was hemispherical: two thermopile mirrors, one pointed toward the Earth and one pointed toward space, with the output voltage proportional to the temperature difference. The measurement was the first of its kind from orbit. Prior to Explorer 7, the radiation budget had been estimated from ground-based sun photometers and high-altitude balloon measurements, which could only infer the planetary total. Explorer 7 measured it directly. The satellite operated for nearly two years in an elliptical orbit, returning the continuous global-scale measurement that confirmed the radiation budget was measurable, the climate system was in a near-equilibrium regime, and the tiny perturbations in net flux that would later prove central to climate science were real signals rather than artifacts. The mission was managed by Goddard Space Flight Center, which became NASA's Earth-observing institutional home and enters the engine at degree 12 as the fifth institutional sponsor after the Air Force (Vanguard), ABMA/JPL (Explorer 1), NASA/ARPA (Pioneer), and the Naval Research Laboratory (Vanguard payloads). The American space program was multi-institutional from the beginning. Goddard's presence extends the institutional diversity. Verner Suomi's radiometer became the ancestor of every Earth radiation budget instrument that followed, up through CERES and the modern fleet.

The Pine Siskin is the engine's thirteenth bird species and the first irruptive finch. *Spinus pinus*, Fringillidae, cardueline finch lineage. The siskin breeds in the boreal coniferous forest from Alaska across Canada and south through the northern Rockies, feeding primarily on spruce, pine, fir, hemlock, and cedar seeds. Its population dynamics are governed by the masting cycle of those conifers: a bumper seed crop produces abundant food and high reproductive success, and the population expands; a seed crop failure triggers a southward movement -- an irruption -- as tens of thousands of birds leave the boreal zone searching for alternative food. Puget Sound lowland feeders fill with Pine Siskins in irruption years and contain almost none in non-irruption years. The presence or absence of the bird in the lowlands is a direct proxy for the boreal seed-crop balance. The bird is the thermometer. It measures an ecological radiation budget the same way Explorer 7 measured the physical radiation budget. The siskin introduces the third temporal mode of presence to the engine: after resident (the Black-capped Chickadee at degree 3, the Chestnut-backed Chickadee at degree 9, the Pacific Wren at degree 11) and migratory (the Swainson's Thrush, the Western Tanager -- seasonal periodic presence driven by the solar calendar), irruptive is event-driven, stochastic, and tied to a distant system's imbalance. The siskin's call is the first "electronic" biological sound in the engine. The organism pairing is Usnea longissima, Methuselah's Beard Lichen. Usnea is a genus of filamentous fruticose lichens, and longissima is the longest-strand species in the genus, with individual thalli reaching three meters in length when the substrate allows. The lichen grows on the bark of old-growth conifers in humid Pacific Northwest forests -- Sitka spruce, western hemlock, western red cedar, Douglas-fir -- and is one of the most sensitive bioindicators of atmospheric pollution known to lichenology. Sulfur dioxide emissions, nitrogen oxide deposition, ozone exposure, and general air-quality degradation all disrupt Usnea's symbiotic algal partner, and a polluted atmosphere erases the lichen from its forest quickly. Presence of Usnea longissima means clean air. Absence means polluted air. The lichen is a radiation-budget-scale measurement instrument for atmospheric chemistry, and it is the oldest-growth sentinel in the Pacific Northwest bioindicator toolkit. ### Lessons Learned

1. **The root can be documented after the branches.** The engine's degree sequence does not have to follow jazz-historical chronology for the genealogy to be legible. Lanphere's bebop root at degree 12 after Caliman's hard bop at degree 9 and Shank's cool jazz at degree 10 demonstrates that the tree structure is independent of the order in which its nodes are discovered, and the out-of-order sequencing can even be preferred when the branches have more available surface material than the root.

The Erasure (Bown, degree 6) is the fall without return. The Stay (Anderson, degree 3) is the return without fall. The Prodigal is both, and neither alone is sufficient to qualify. Lanphere's E=1 at degree 12 is post-redemption quiet driven by bodily energy budget change. Same energy level, different origin pathway. The engine's energy scale is degree-descriptive, not cause-descriptive, and multiple origin pathways can produce identical energy values.

Irruptive is event-driven, tied to a distant system's imbalance, and stochastic in arrival. The three modes partition the temporal-presence space and are sufficient to cover all thirteen species in the SPS catalog. The partition is complete at degree 12.

5. **The synthesizer waveform set in the SPS is complete at seven waveforms across thirteen species.** Sine, sawtooth, pulse, noise, AM, FM, and additive synthesis are all represented in Pacific Northwest bird vocalizations. The forest is a complete synthesizer. The accumulating sub-thread from degree 2 closes at degree 12, and the closure is the engine's first demonstration that accumulating sub-threads do terminate cleanly inside a bounded degree window.

6. **Goddard Space Flight Center is the fifth NASA institutional sponsor and represents Earth-observing institutional culture.** The Air Force managed Vanguard. ABMA/JPL managed Explorer 1. NASA/ARPA managed Pioneer. NRL provided Vanguard payloads. Goddard manages Explorer 7 and will manage the subsequent Earth-observing fleet. The American space program was multi-institutional from the beginning, and Goddard's presence at degree 12 extends the institutional diversity tally to five lineages in twelve missions.

7. **Calendar-date dedication is now an explicit engine convention.** The Virchow-Explorer 7 October 13 match is the second instance of birth-date-to-launch-date dedication alignment after Campbell-Explorer 3 at March 26. With two instances the pattern is confirmed as deliberate selection rather than coincidence, and future engine dedications will be selected from the pool of candidate figures whose birth or death dates align with the paired mission's launch date.

8. **The Wenatchee origin opens a fifth pipeline city and documents the steepest artist ascent in the engine.** Seattle, Chicago, Detroit, and Los Angeles were the four pipeline cities through degree 11. Wenatchee's addition at degree 12 introduces eastern Washington, a working-class apple-packinghouse region with no jazz-club infrastructure, and Lanphere's five-year trajectory from Wenatchee to 52nd Street is the steepest documented origin-to-apex ascent.
