## Summary

**THE QUIET AFTER. FIRST TURDIDAE. DAVE GROHL FROM THE WRECKAGE. E=14 COMBINED. 87.5%.**

**Foo Fighters** arrive at E=7 -- the band formed by Dave Grohl in 1994 in the direct aftermath of Nirvana's dissolution, the deliberate act of continuing to make music from the center of the event that had ended the music. Grohl recorded the first Foo Fighters album alone in a studio in Seattle: all instruments, all vocals, all production, a one-man document of what happens when the drummer from the most important band in the generation decides to keep going. The Foo Fighters are post-grunge in the technical sense -- they emerged after the commercial grunge moment had restructured the industry that received them -- but they are grunge in the genealogical sense: the band contains the specific biography of having been there, of having been inside Nirvana, and the music carries that biography in the mode of having continued rather than the mode of having been present at the maximum.

E=7 for Foo Fighters is the post-grunge register in the summit context: lower than the Big Four entries, lower than the flanking heavy entries (TAD, Mudhoney, AIC at their sub-ceiling), but not low. E=7 is the energy of craft and continuation and the specific melody that Grohl brought to the post-grunge landscape -- cleaner hooks, cleaner production, the radio-accessibility that the immediate grunge canon had declined to pursue. The Foo Fighters' commercial success is larger in absolute terms than most of the entries that precede them in the summit; E=7 is not a reading of their commercial scale but of their sonic register in relation to the summit's prior arc. After three consecutive E=9 entries and the E=18 maximum, E=7 is the appropriate register for the descent: not silence, but the quiet after the storm that is not the storm.

**Varied Thrush in fog** arrives at E=7 on the SPS track -- *Ixoreus naevius*, the Varied Thrush, producing its single haunting note in the fog of a Pacific Northwest winter morning: a long, sustained, slightly wavering tone at a single frequency, produced once, followed by silence, then repeated at a different pitch. The Varied Thrush song is the most austere vocalization in the Pacific Northwest's bird catalog. Where the Swainson's Thrush produces an ascending spiral of complex intervals and the Hermit Thrush produces ethereal phrase-and-response sequences, the Varied Thrush produces a single note. One note. Held. Released. Silence. Another note at a different pitch. The fog carries the single tone further than the complexity of other thrushes' songs would carry, because a single sustained tone at the right frequency penetrates the water droplets of Pacific fog without the multi-harmonic scatter that breaks up more complex vocalizations.

E=7 for the Varied Thrush in fog is correct and it is the first TURDIDAE entry in the SPS track. The thrush family has been represented in the S36 track's ecological documentation but not in the SPS track's acoustic catalog until degree 315. The Varied Thrush arrives at the summit's descending arc as the first member of the family that contains some of the Pacific Northwest's most musically complex songbirds -- Swainson's Thrush, Hermit Thrush, Veery -- and it arrives at E=7 in fog, with one note, as the quietest possible introduction to the family. The FIRST TURDIDAE enters the SPS track not at E=9 but at E=7, not with complexity but with a single sustained tone in fog. The Thrush family begins here.
