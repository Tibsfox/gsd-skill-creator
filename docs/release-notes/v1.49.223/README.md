## Part A: Pearl Jam (Acoustic Context) — THE GRUNGE WHISPER

- **Seattle Origins.** Formed in **Seattle, Washington** in 1990 from the ashes of **Mother Love Bone**, with **Stone Gossard** and **Jeff Ament** recruiting **Mike McCready**, **Eddie Vedder**, and (initially) **Dave Krusen** on drums. The current lineup includes **Matt Cameron** on drums (since 1998). Pearl Jam are the most commercially successful Seattle band in history — over **85 million records sold worldwide**, with the debut album ***Ten*** (1991) eventually certified **13x Platinum** in the United States alone. Inducted into the **Rock and Roll Hall of Fame** in 2017. The band that, alongside **Nirvana**, **Soundgarden**, and **Alice in Chains**, defined the **early 1990s grunge movement** and made Seattle a global music capital.
- **MTV Unplugged 1992.** Recorded at **Kaufman Astoria Studios** in Astoria, Queens on **March 16, 1992**. The band performed *"Oceans,"* *"State of Love and Trust,"* *"Alive,"* *"Black,"* *"Jeremy,"* *"Even Flow,"* and *"Porch"* in fully acoustic arrangements. Never officially released as a standalone album during its era, it became one of the most bootlegged recordings in rock history. The acoustic *"Black"* — Vedder's baritone exposed without amplification, the emotional weight of the lyrics carried entirely by voice and acoustic guitar — is widely considered the **definitive version** of the song among fans, even relative to the studio version on ***Ten***.
- **Into the Wild 2007.** Eddie Vedder's solo acoustic soundtrack for **Sean Penn**'s film adaptation of **Jon Krakauer**'s book about **Christopher McCandless** ("Alexander Supertramp") and his fatal journey into the **Alaskan wilderness**. Entirely acoustic — ukulele and guitar. Tracks include *"Guaranteed,"* *"Hard Sun"* (a **Indio cover**), *"Society"* (a **Jerry Hannan cover**), *"Rise,"* and *"Long Nights."* The album won a **Golden Globe** for Best Original Song in 2008 and proved that Vedder's voice, fully detached from the band's production, could carry an entire 148-minute film. The soundtrack also marked Vedder's first solo album release at age 43.

## Part B: Deer Mouse — THE INVISIBLE ABUNDANT

- **Taxonomy.** ***Peromyscus maniculatus***. Genus name from Greek *pero* ("pouch" or "pointed") + *myscus* ("little mouse"), referencing the small pointed form of the genus. Species name from Latin "little-handed," referencing the small white forefeet. Described by **Wagner (1845)**. Class **Mammalia**, Order **Rodentia**, Family **Cricetidae**, Genus ***Peromyscus***, Species *P. maniculatus*. Common name **Deer Mouse** — named for the brown-and-white coloration resembling a deer's pelage. The most species-rich mammalian genus in North America, with over 50 *Peromyscus* species ranging from the Florida Keys to Alaska.
- **NEW FAMILY: Cricetidae.** The **36th family** in the engine. Cricetidae = New World mice, rats, voles, hamsters — over 600 species globally, the second-largest mammalian family after Muridae. The arrival of Cricetidae **breaks the Sciuridae octet** of eight consecutive entries (degrees 75 through 82): Yellow-bellied Marmot 75, Hoary Marmot 76, Olympic Marmot 77, Townsend's Ground Squirrel 78, Golden-mantled Ground Squirrel 79, Douglas Squirrel 80, Eastern Fox Squirrel 81, Northern Flying Squirrel 82. Eight Sciuridae across six genera and eight habitats. The longest family run in engine history. The Deer Mouse is the most appropriate first Cricetidae entry: most abundant, most widespread, most ecologically significant, most likely to be the first New World mouse a future engine reader thinks of.
- **First Ultrasonic Species.** The Deer Mouse is the engine's **FIRST ULTRASONIC SPECIES** — the first species whose primary vocalizations are entirely beyond the range of human hearing. **Ultrasonic calls** at **30,000 to 90,000+ Hz**, far beyond the ~20,000 Hz upper limit of human perception. Two primary call types: **pup isolation calls** (ultrasonic cries that attract the mother to displaced or distressed pups) and **courtship vocalizations** (ultrasonic signals during reproductive encounters between males and females). Plus **soft audible chip notes** at **5,000 to 15,000 Hz** — extremely quiet contact calls barely perceptible at close range. **E=2** — near-total inaudibility to human observers. The transition from nearly-ultrasonic (Northern Flying Squirrel at 82, 8,000-20,000+ Hz, E=3) to fully ultrasonic (Deer Mouse 83, 30,000-90,000+ Hz, E=2) crosses the perception threshold in one degree.
- **Most Abundant Native Rodent in North America.** Population densities reaching **20-30 individuals per hectare** in optimal habitat. Found in virtually every terrestrial habitat type: coniferous forest, deciduous forest, mixed forest, prairie, montane meadow, agricultural edge, desert scrubland, alpine tundra, and urban/rural building edges. Range extends from the **Arctic treeline** in northern Canada and Alaska through the lower 48 states to **central Mexico**. Total continental population likely in the hundreds of millions. Yet most people have never knowingly seen one. Secretive, nocturnal, nearly invisible. **The invisible abundant** — the most abundant mammal almost no one consciously sees.
- **Keystone Prey Species.** The Deer Mouse is a **keystone prey species** — the base of countless food webs. Documented prey items for the **Spotted Owl** (SPS-004), the **Red Fox** (SPS-071), the **Short-tailed Weasel** (SPS-067), the **American Marten** (SPS-065), plus owls (Great Horned, Barred, Northern Saw-whet), hawks (Sharp-shinned, Red-tailed), snakes (Western Rattlesnake, gopher snakes), coyotes, skunks, bobcats, and raccoons across the continent. **Remove the Deer Mouse and the predator community collapses**. The first entry in the engine explicitly identified as the prey base sustaining downstream species. The engine's first explicit **keystone prey** structural slot.
- **Habitat: Everywhere.** Coniferous forest (Douglas-fir, ponderosa pine, spruce, fir). Deciduous forest (oak woodland, riparian cottonwood). Mixed forest. Prairie and grassland. Montane meadow. Agricultural edge (fencerows, hedgerows). Desert scrubland (sagebrush steppe). Alpine zones above treeline. Urban/rural building edges (sheds, woodpiles, barns, attics). Range: Arctic treeline to central Mexico, coast to coast. **No single habitat defines this species** — "varied habitat" is the only accurate description. The most habitat-generalist mammal in the engine to date. The polar opposite of the alpine specialists in the Sciuridae quintet (75-79) and the Olympic-endemic specialist at degree 77.
- **Morphology and Identification.** **10-30 grams** — the **smallest mammal in the engine by weight**. Soft fine fur. Grayish-brown to reddish-brown above. **Clean white belly**. **White feet** (the species name *maniculatus* means "little-handed"). **Bicolored tail** (dark above, white below) — a key field identification feature distinguishing Deer Mice from house mice (uniform tail). Large dark eyes adapted for nocturnal vision. Prominent ears. Long whiskers. Average lifespan 1-2 years in the wild; up to 5-8 years in captivity. Sexual maturity at 35-49 days; multiple litters per year of 3-7 pups; the population turnover rate is among the highest of any mammal.
- **Cross-Quintet Predation and Disease Reservoir.** The Deer Mouse closes a predation network across multiple engine entries: **Spotted Owl** (4) hunts at night and consumes Deer Mice opportunistically; **Red Fox** (71) preys on Deer Mice as a primary winter food source; **Short-tailed Weasel** (67) is small enough to pursue Deer Mice into burrows and through tunnels; **American Marten** (65) takes Deer Mice from forest-floor litter. The Deer Mouse is also the **primary reservoir for Sin Nombre hantavirus** in the western United States — a structural ecological role that connects the species to human health surveillance and to the broader question of how invisible-abundant species shape regional disease ecology. Twenty-third consecutive original CSV species (degrees 61 through 83 with no substitutions).
