## Summary

**Helms Alee** returns at E=6 -- a **103-degree callback** to degree 111, where the trio entered the engine as the band whose member literally builds amplifiers. Dana James, Ben Verellen, and Hozoji Matheson-Margullis produce a sound that is simultaneously crushing and beautiful: walls of distorted guitar and bass overlaid with three-part harmonies that should not survive the noise surrounding them but somehow do. On Sargent House, records like *Sleepwalking Sailors* (2014) and *Noctiluca* (2019) document a band that treats volume as a compositional tool -- the loud parts are loud because the quiet parts are quiet, and the transitions between them are where the band lives. At E=6, Helms Alee returns at the same energy as their debut, matching Kinski's pattern of unchanging intensity. On the species side, the **Bobcat yowl** registers at E=5 -- the **third Felidae** entry in three consecutive degrees. The Bobcat (*Lynx rufus*) is Washington's most common wild cat, found from the San Juan Islands to the Blue Mountains, from sea level to alpine. The yowl is the Bobcat's most dramatic vocalization: a raspy, drawn-out caterwauling that sounds like a domestic cat amplified to nightmare scale. Produced primarily during the January-March breeding season, the Bobcat yowl carries through forest and suburban edge habitat alike, startling homeowners who have never considered what lives in the greenbelt behind their house.
