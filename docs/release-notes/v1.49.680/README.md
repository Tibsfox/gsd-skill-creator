Soyuz TM-3 launched 1987-07-22 03:59:17 UTC from Baikonur Cosmodrome LC-1 (Gagarin's Start) on a Soyuz-U2 (11A511U2) launch vehicle, carrying a three-cosmonaut crew on a 7-day visiting flight to Mir.

**Cosmonaut-Researcher Lt. Col. Muhammed Ahmed Faris** (محمد أحمد فارس; 1951-05-26 Aleppo Syrian Arab Republic → 2024-04-19 Istanbul Turkey) — Syrian Arab Air Force combat pilot (Yom Kippur War 1973 + Lebanon 1982; MiG-21 + MiG-23). Selected for Interkosmos 1985-09 from 60 Syrian candidates alongside backup Munir Habib Khalifa. Trained at Yuri Gagarin Cosmonaut Training Center Star City 1985-09 to 1987-07. **FIRST SYRIAN COSMONAUT + FIRST CITIZEN OF ARAB-MAJORITY NATION IN SPACE + FIRST ARABIC-LANGUAGE-SPEAKING COSMONAUT.**

**Commander Col. Aleksandr Stepanovich Viktorenko** (1947-03-29 Olginka Kazakh SSR → 2025-04-10 Star City Moscow Oblast Russia) — Soviet Air Force; rookie first flight at age 40. Subsequent career: 3 more long-duration Mir flights (Soyuz TM-8 EO-5 1989 + Soyuz TM-14 EO-11 1992 + Soyuz TM-20 EO-17 1994) cumulating ~489 days in space. Hero of the Soviet Union 1987.

**Flight Engineer Aleksandr Pavlovich Aleksandrov** (1943-02-20 Moscow Russian SFSR → still living 2026) — civilian Soviet cosmonaut + NPO Energia engineer; 2nd career flight (first = Soyuz T-9/EO-2 Salyut 7 1983). At v680 he serves as **relief substitute for medically-relieved Laveykin** — entering Mir on Soyuz TM-3 + staying as new EO-1 FE with Romanenko + returning on Soyuz TM-4 1987-12-29 (160 days; 309.72 days cumulative). Twice Hero of the Soviet Union (1983 + 1987). Distinct from Aleksandr-Pavlovich-Aleksandrov-Belorussian-cosmonaut Soyuz TM-13 1991.

Soyuz TM-3 docked Mir 1987-07-24 03:31 UTC at the aft axial port (Kvant-side; Kvant module attached at v678 1987-04). The mission demonstrated a substrate-novel **multi-flight Soyuz vehicle swap**: Aleksandrov stayed on Mir while Viktorenko + Faris + medically-relieved Laveykin returned aboard Soyuz TM-2 (Romanenko + Laveykin's original ferry vehicle) 1987-07-30. This **FIRST-COSMONAUT-RELIEF-VIA-INTERKOSMOS-VISITING-FLIGHT** substrate-form NEW LOCKED would later inform ISS crew rotation protocols.

During the 7-day visiting flight Faris performed:
- **Lyulin Bulgarian-Soviet radiation dosimeter experiment** (PI: Tsvetan Dachev IKI-BAS Bulgaria) — silicon PIN diode + charge-sensitive preamp + shaping amp + comparator + counter. Substrate-anchor for cumulative ISS exposure measurement cohort.
- **Hydrology Hand Earth-observation** — photography of Syrian agricultural terrain + Euphrates water-management infrastructure (Tabqa Dam 35.85°N 38.55°E + Lake Assad). Substrate-novel for ARAB-WORLD-EARTH-OBSERVATION-FROM-ORBIT cohort.
- **Mir Water Balance physiological experiments** — for Arab-region adaptation studies.
- **Cultural Payload** — Quran + Arabic literature samples + Syrian soil + Syrian flag. **FIRST QURAN IN ORBIT** substrate-form NEW LOCKED. Faris read Quran passages from near the Earth-observation window.

The **Interkosmos program**, founded 1967 by the Soviet Academy of Sciences, had previously flown 10 visiting-crew flights between 1978-1981 carrying cosmonauts from Czechoslovakia + Poland + East Germany + Bulgaria + Hungary + Vietnam + Cuba + Mongolia + Romania + France. The early cohort closed with Salyut 7 access in 1984. **The Mir-era Interkosmos cohort opens at v680** with Faris — substrate-anchor for the cumulative cohort that would extend through 7 more international flights to Mir between 1987-1991: Aleksandrov-Belorussian (Bulgaria 1988-06) + Mohmand (Afghanistan 1988-08) + Akiyama (Japan 1990-12; first journalist + commercial flight) + Sharman (UK 1991-05; first Briton) + Viehböck (Austria 1991-10) + Aubakirov (Kazakhstan 1991-10; immediately before Soviet Union dissolution).
