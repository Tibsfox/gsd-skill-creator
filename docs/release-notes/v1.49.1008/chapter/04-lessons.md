# Lessons — v1.49.1008

11 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **Imaging and spectroscopy of a layer together resolve it in both space and spectrum.**
   Rather than imaging the chromosphere or taking its spectrum, IRIS did both at high resolution at once, showing that pairing a slit-jaw imager with a spectrograph anchors every measurement to its place on the resolved Sun.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11761_

2. **A dedicated instrument turns a boundary into a resolved layer.**
   By building specifically for the interface region, IRIS resolved the chromosphere and transition region directly rather than inferring them from the photosphere below or the corona above, showing that a dedicated focus turns a boundary into a layer.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11762_

3. **Resolving the interface traces where the corona's energy begins.**
   By measuring the flows, waves, and brightenings of the interface, IRIS traced where the energy that heats the corona begins its journey upward, showing that the thin connecting layer carries the story of the heating.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11763_

4. **High resolution reveals the fine-scale dynamics a coarse view averages over.**
   By resolving the interface at about a third of an arcsecond in space, time, and spectrum, IRIS revealed the spicules, fast flows, and ultraviolet bursts of the layer, showing that fine resolution opens dynamics a coarse view sums away.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11764_

5. **A continuation can deepen an axis to a new layer of the same body.**
   By turning the solar watch from the corona to the interface below it, IRIS sustained the solar-observatory axis at obs#4 on the same target, showing that an axis can deepen to a new layer without rotating away.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11765_

6. **A shared laboratory heritage carries a method to a new layer.**
   By reusing the high-resolution solar instrumentation the Lockheed Martin Solar and Astrophysics Laboratory established with TRACE under Alan Title, IRIS carried the resolved-observation method from the corona to the interface, showing that a lineage of instrumentation can move down through the solar atmosphere.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11766_

7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across a distinct-palette mission.**
   Because IRIS reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.198 template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content to the interface-region imaging spectroscopy with a distinct interface-violet / line-magenta / plasma-teal palette, making the distinct-palette mission a clean build.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11767_

8. **The shader renders procedural structure rather than archived data.**
   The IRIS interface-region-spectrograph shader uses analytic geometry and procedural noise rather than loading actual IRIS observations from the LMSAL and NASA archives. A future revision could load encoded IRIS slit-jaw images and spectra for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to real observations.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11768_

9. **The spacecraft diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the spacecraft, its ultraviolet telescope, and the model of the layered solar atmosphere.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11769_

10. **The solar sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.**
   The sidebar lists representative lineage elements (IRIS, RHESSI, TRACE, OSO, SDO) but does not enumerate the full IRIS spectral-line roster with each formation height and temperature; a future revision could expand the table with per-line formation conditions.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11770_

11. **The interface energy flow is described rather than computed.**
   The way energy and plasma move up through the interface region is described but not run against archived data; a future ship could map the Doppler flows from a real IRIS observation.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11771_
