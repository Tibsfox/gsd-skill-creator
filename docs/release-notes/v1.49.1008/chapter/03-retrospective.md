A future revision could load encoded IRIS slit-jaw images and spectra for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to real observations.

## Surprises

- **A thin connecting layer holds the story of the corona's heating.** The interface region is a thin layer between the cool surface and the hot corona, yet IRIS showed that resolving it traces where the energy that heats the corona begins, so the catalog's interface-region entry is the layer where the corona's heating starts.
- **A wading bird that works the water's edge mirrors the interface imager.** The Great Blue Heron, which works the thin zone where water meets land, enters as the interface-working mirror to the instrument that resolved the interface region between the Sun's surface and corona, so the catalog pairs a boundary-working wading bird with an interface-imaging mission.
- **A named iris mirrors the spectrograph that spreads the light.** The Oregon Iris shares the mission's name and spreads its banded color across its petals, so the catalog's interface-region imaging spectrograph is paired with a native iris named, like the instrument, for the spread of color, the spread the spectrograph mirrors in wavelength.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Imaging and spectroscopy of a layer together resolve it in both space and spectrum.** Rather than imaging the chromosphere or taking its spectrum, IRIS did both at high resolution at once, showing that pairing a slit-jaw imager with a spectrograph anchors every measurement to its place on the resolved Sun.
2. **A dedicated instrument turns a boundary into a resolved layer.** By building specifically for the interface region, IRIS resolved the chromosphere and transition region directly rather than inferring them from the photosphere below or the corona above, showing that a dedicated focus turns a boundary into a layer.
3. **Resolving the interface traces where the corona's energy begins.** By measuring the flows, waves, and brightenings of the interface, IRIS traced where the energy that heats the corona begins its journey upward, showing that the thin connecting layer carries the story of the heating.
4. **High resolution reveals the fine-scale dynamics a coarse view averages over.** By resolving the interface at about a third of an arcsecond in space, time, and spectrum, IRIS revealed the spicules, fast flows, and ultraviolet bursts of the layer, showing that fine resolution opens dynamics a coarse view sums away.
5. **A continuation can deepen an axis to a new layer of the same body.** By turning the solar watch from the corona to the interface below it, IRIS sustained the solar-observatory axis at obs#4 on the same target, showing that an axis can deepen to a new layer without rotating away.
6. **A shared laboratory heritage carries a method to a new layer.** By reusing the high-resolution solar instrumentation the Lockheed Martin Solar and Astrophysics Laboratory established with TRACE under Alan Title, IRIS carried the resolved-observation method from the corona to the interface, showing that a lineage of instrumentation can move down through the solar atmosphere.
7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across a distinct-palette mission.** Because IRIS reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.198 template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content to the interface-region imaging spectroscopy with a distinct interface-violet / line-magenta / plasma-teal palette, making the distinct-palette mission a clean build.
