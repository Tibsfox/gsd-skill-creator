## Mission Overview

SWOT is the obs#2 sustainer of the EARTH-SYSTEM-SCIENCE-EXPLORATION axis — the joint NASA/CNES Earth-observing mission that circles Earth in a non-sun-synchronous orbit and reads the height and reach of the world's water, measuring the water-surface elevation and extent of rivers, lakes, reservoirs, and wetlands, and the two-dimensional shape of the ocean surface across the width of two wide swaths. ★ It was built by NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory with the French space agency CNES, with contributions from the Canadian Space Agency (the radar electronics) and the UK Space Agency. The observer launched ~16 December 2022 on a SpaceX Falcon 9 from Vandenberg Space Force Base (SLC-4E) and settled into a non-sun-synchronous orbit at roughly 890 km: an advanced surface-water and ocean-topography Earth observer. The story is the launch from Vandenberg, the climb into the ~890 km inclined orbit, KaRIn's two ~50 km swaths sweeping the surface, the interferometer reading height across the width of each swath, the rivers and lakes and wetlands resolved in height and extent, the fine-scale ocean revealed in two dimensions, and the long ongoing watch that continues today. The science is central: KaRIn provides the first global high-resolution survey of Earth's surface water — the water-surface elevation (height) and extent of rivers wider than ~100 m, lakes and reservoirs larger than ~250 m / ~6 ha, and wetlands, with height precision around ~10 cm over land — and the first fine-scale, two-dimensional, wide-swath map of ocean-surface topography, resolving sub-mesoscale eddies and currents and fronts down to feature scales around ~15 km, an order of magnitude finer than the heritage nadir altimeters could see. ★ The surface-water result is load-bearing: it is the first global HIGH-RESOLUTION survey of Earth's surface water (the height and extent of the world's rivers, lakes, reservoirs, and wetlands) — NASA's phrase "the first global survey of Earth's surface water" — NOT "first to measure surface water." ★ The ocean result is equally load-bearing: it is the first FINE-SCALE, 2D, WIDE-SWATH map of ocean-surface topography — distinct from the heritage nadir altimeters (TOPEX/Poseidon 1992, the Jason series) that measure sea-surface height along a single 1D track; SWOT's first is the fine-scale 2D wide-swath map (the ~15 km feature scale is a wavelength / feature-scale resolution, not a pixel resolution), NOT "first sea-surface-height." The scoping is load-bearing throughout: the first global high-resolution survey of Earth's surface water (KaRIn, the height and extent of rivers, lakes, reservoirs, wetlands) — not "first surface water"; the first fine-scale 2D wide-swath map of ocean-surface topography (KaRIn, the sub-mesoscale features) — distinct from the heritage nadir altimeters, not "first sea-surface-height"; and KaRIn as the first spaceborne wide-swath Ka-band radar interferometer for water/ocean-surface height (scoped to spaceborne wide-swath Ka-band interferometry — not "first radar," since Ka-band radars predate it, and not "first interferometric SAR," since SRTM/TanDEM-X used InSAR for topography). ★★ The mission is STILL OPERATING (since 2022), so the catalog frames its launch and completed milestones in the PAST (SWOT launched ~16 December 2022, reached its orbit, completed its commissioning / fast-sampling phase, and returned its first global surface-water and ocean-topography data) while its ongoing role is framed in the PRESENT (SWOT orbits Earth, continues to survey the world's water, has returned an enormous record, and remains operational — in Phase E, having exceeded its three-year prime mission). ## Surprises

- **KaRIn reads surface height across the width of two ~50 km swaths by interferometry.** The Ka-band Radar Interferometer carries two Ka-band (35.75 GHz) SAR antennas on a ~10 m boom, separated by a ~10 m baseline, and reads the surface height from the interferometric phase across the width of each of its two ~50 km swaths — a ~120 km total observation width with a ~20 km nadir gap — the first spaceborne wide-swath Ka-band radar interferometer, where heritage nadir altimeters read height only along a single track at nadir.
- **SWOT surveys the height and extent of the world's small rivers, lakes, and wetlands from orbit.** Where the heritage measurements reached only the largest features, KaRIn's high-resolution survey reaches rivers wider than ~100 m, lakes and reservoirs larger than ~250 m / ~6 ha, and wetlands worldwide, with height precision around ~10 cm over land — the first global high-resolution survey of Earth's surface water.
- **The fine-scale 2D ocean map resolves eddies an order of magnitude smaller than nadir altimeters could see.** Where the heritage nadir altimeters (TOPEX/Poseidon 1992, the Jason series) resolve ocean-surface features only down to ~75–100 km along a single 1D track, KaRIn's fine-scale 2D wide-swath map resolves sub-mesoscale eddies, currents, and fronts down to feature scales around ~15 km, with height precision improving to ~3 cm over the ocean with averaging — the first fine-scale, two-dimensional, wide-swath map of ocean-surface topography.
- **A small endemic fish that lives its whole life in the still, vegetated shallows.** The Olympic Mudminnow (*Novumbra hubbsi*), Washington State's only endemic freshwater fish, lives among the submerged vegetation of still or slow-moving, heavily vegetated wetland waters (swamps, marshes, sloughs, and bog ponds of the western Olympic Peninsula and the Puget lowlands), tolerant of low-oxygen still water, hovering in the quiet shallows for its whole life: a mirror of SWOT surveying the height and reach of the world's quiet surface waters (behavioral / ecological description only; a small freshwater fish, distinct from the introduced central mudminnow *Umbra limi* and from the sculpins *Cottus*).
- **An aquatic plant that stands up out of the quiet standing water.** The Common Mare's-tail (*Hippuris vulgaris*), an emergent aquatic flowering plant of shallow still and slow waters (pond and lake margins, marshes, slow streams), sends erect, whorled-leaf shoots up out of the water surface from a base rooted in the shallows, standing in the quiet standing waters (ecological description only; a flowering aquatic plant, distinct from the horsetails *Equisetum*, which it superficially resembles but is unrelated) — bound with the throughline of the quiet surface waters, the same standing shallows whose height and reach SWOT measures from orbit.

## Dedication

v1.282 SWOT is dedicated to the people across NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory and the French space agency CNES, with the Canadian and British partners, who built and still fly the observer that reads the height and reach of the world's water. The teams who lifted it from Vandenberg on a Falcon 9 and turned it into an inclined orbit, and the engineers behind the Ka-band Radar Interferometer — two antennas held apart on a long boom — who read the surface across the width of two wide swaths, so they can map the height of rivers and lakes and wetlands worldwide, and the fine ripple of eddies the older single-track instruments could never resolve. The partners in Canada behind the radar electronics, and in Britain, who helped raise this engineering. Together they survey the surface waters and the open ocean as one living world. They keep a long watch over the world's water, orbit after orbit, returning a record that grows by the day. They measure a world by the height and reach of its water, across the whole of its surface, and the watch they keep goes on.
