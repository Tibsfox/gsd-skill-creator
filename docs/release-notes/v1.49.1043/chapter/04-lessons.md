1. **An axis sustains when the next observation reads the same kind of source through a wholly new observable.**
   The X-ray-astronomy axis sustains at obs#4 because IXPE keeps the subject — neutron stars, black holes, magnetars, supernova remnants, pulsar wind nebulae, and blazar jets in the high-energy sky — but reaches it through the polarization rather than the image or the light curve; the substrate-form distinctness is the observable read, so the cadence continues the axis rather than rotating away from it.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12073_

2. **A third question lives in the orientation of the light.**
   The focusing telescopes answer *where* a photon came from and the timing observatory answers *when* it arrived; IXPE answers *how the light is oriented and ordered*, recovering the geometry of the emitting region and the structure of its magnetic field — the property neither the focused image nor the rapid light curve carries.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12074_

3. **A first can hinge on the qualifier.**
   IXPE is the first *imaging* X-ray polarimeter and the first observatory *dedicated* to X-ray polarimetry, not the first to measure cosmic X-ray polarization — a 1971 rocket, OSO-8 (1975), and PolarLight (2018) came earlier; the distinct firsts are imaging the orientation across a source, far better sensitivity, and a dedicated observatory, so the framing carries the "imaging / dedicated / many source classes" qualifier.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12075_

4. **A moderate focus is the right trade for a polarimeter.**
   A sub-arcsecond optic is not needed when the goal is the polarization; IXPE trades imaging sharpness (≤25-arcsecond focus) for an unobstructed beam onto an imaging photoelectric Gas Pixel Detector, the trade the polarization observable demands.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12076_

5. **One observatory reads the orientation across many kinds of source.**
   The same three telescopes recovered the radial magnetic field of the supernova remnant Cassiopeia A, the ~90° angle swing of the magnetar 4U 0142+61, the jet-aligned corona of the black-hole binary Cygnus X-1, the toroidal field of the Crab nebula, the shock-accelerated jets of Markarian 501 and 421, and the polarization of the accreting neutron star Scorpius X-1.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12077_

6. **The polarization sees the geometry the image and the light curve cannot.**
   A radial-versus-toroidal magnetic field, a disk-plane-versus-corona-above geometry, the QED vacuum birefringence of a magnetar's extreme field — all are written into the orientation and order of the light, recoverable only by reading the polarization degree and angle; the fourth observation is what the first imaging X-ray polarimeter sees that the focusing and timing telescopes could not.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12078_

7. **A continuation keeps the predecessors' vocabulary and references them by name.**
   Because IXPE sustains the X-ray-astronomy axis, the shared X-ray vocabulary is correct and kept, and Chandra (obs#1, soft/medium focusing), NuSTAR (obs#2, hard-band focusing), and RXTE (obs#3, timing) are referenced as the form complements — but IXPE's own facts (the Wolter-I Mirror Module Assemblies, the photoelectric Gas Pixel Detectors, the Falcon 9 launch, the equatorial orbit, the polarization results) and only its own six anchors are stamped; the predecessors' anchors and predecessor-specific hardware stay with their missions.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12079_

8. **Accuracy guardrails guard against the stale-infobox and fabricated-citation classes of error.**
   Using a Falcon 9 from Kennedy Space Center LC-39A (not Pegasus, not Cape Canaveral/Vandenberg), a ~600 km near-equatorial orbit, ~325–330 kg mass, ~US$188 million total cost, a two-year baseline, a 3.7 m boom distinct from the 4 m focal length, three flight telescopes with 24 shells per MMA, ≤25-arcsec resolution, the Cas A full-ApJ citation and the distinct Taverna/Krawczynski Science DOIs, and only the verified references — keeps the facts load-bearing and correct.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12080_

9. **The shader renders procedural geometry rather than archived data.**
   The X-ray-polarimetry shader uses analytic curves and procedural noise rather than loading actual IXPE event lists or a measured polarization map. A future revision could render a published IXPE polarization vector field (the Cassiopeia A radial field or the Crab nebula's toroidal field) for a higher-fidelity view of the data.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12081_

11. **The X-ray-polarimetry-continuation sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.**
   The sidebar lists the axis continuation and the IXPE mission elements (the three telescopes, the equatorial orbit, the science highlights) but does not enumerate the full roster of IXPE results or the prospective obs#5+ continuations; a future revision could expand the table.

12. **The polarimetry physics is described rather than computed.**
   The Stokes parameters, the modulation factor and minimum detectable polarization, and the cos²-modulated track-angle distribution are described but not run against published IXPE event data; a future ship could process a real IXPE observation to demonstrate a Q–U measurement or a polarization map directly.
