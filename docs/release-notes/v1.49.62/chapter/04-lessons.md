1. **The "musician's band" phenomenon is structural, not accidental.**
   King's X's combination of musical complexity (prog structures, jazz harmonics, gospel vocals) and accessibility (memorable melodies, tight arrangements) makes them immediately legible to other musicians and opaque to radio programmers. The same pattern appears in GGT (Geggy Tah): maximum musician respect, minimal commercial traction. The industry rewards simplicity of classification, not quality of execution.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #539_

2. **Resilience is its own legacy.**
   At 73, Doug Pinnick is still touring, still recording, still playing with the same band he co-founded 40+ years ago. Jerry Gaskill survived multiple heart attacks and returned to drumming. The band has outlasted every label that signed them. Persistence in the face of commercial indifference is a form of artistic statement that this research documents as fact, not sentiment.
