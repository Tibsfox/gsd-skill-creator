1. **The first space rendezvous validates Lunar Orbit Rendezvous.**
   NASA's chosen mission architecture for reaching the Moon -- Lunar Module separates, lands, ascends, and finds the Command Module in lunar orbit -- required rendezvous to work. If it failed, the crew died. Gemini 6A proved it worked. The docking comes at Gemini 8 (degree 44). But rendezvous is the harder problem -- finding another spacecraft in the void, matching its orbit, nulling relative velocity to centimeters per second. Solved.

2. **Gospel enters the engine.**

Degree 42 maps the upstream -- the root tradition from which all of it descended. Ray Charles (degree 30) secularized gospel into soul. Degree 42 maps the source.

9. **The improvisation model became the template.**
   NASA's pivot from Agena failure to Gemini 7 target in six weeks -- compressing pad turnaround from months to nine days -- established that the program could adapt to failure and still achieve objectives. Apollo 13's 1970 survival improvisation inherits directly from the Gemini 6A playbook.

11. **Pre-launch fuel-system inspection missed the dust cap.**
   The December 12 pad abort's proximate cause was an electrical umbilical disconnect; the deeper cause was a dust cap that had been left lodged in the Titan II's gas generator fuel line during pre-launch servicing. The quality-control process had not caught it. Post-mission reviews tightened inspection protocols, but the incident remains the textbook case for how a trivial foreign-object inclusion can threaten a multi-billion-dollar program.

12. **Gemini 7's fourteen-day endurance revealed unplanned sanitation and exercise gaps.**
   The mission duration was unprecedented, and post-flight medical debriefs documented crew deconditioning, sleep disruption, and hygiene problems the preflight planning had not fully anticipated. Subsequent Apollo missions benefited from the data, but the cost was a measurably uncomfortable fourteen days for Borman and Lovell.
