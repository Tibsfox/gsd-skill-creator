## Summary

**AMERICA'S BEST ROCK BAND. E=9 AGAIN.**

**Sleater-Kinney** arrive at E=8 -- the Olympia and Portland riot grrrl band formed in 1994 by Carrie Brownstein (guitar, vocals) and Corin Tucker (guitar, vocals), joined by Janet Weiss (drums) in 1996. Sleater-Kinney are, as Time magazine declared, America's best rock band -- a statement that is not hyperbole but assessment. The Sleater-Kinney catalog -- *Sleater-Kinney* (1995), *Call the Doctor* (1996), *Dig Me Out* (1997), *The Hot Rock* (1999), *All Hands on the Bad One* (2000), *One Beat* (2002), *The Woods* (2005), *No Cities to Love* (2015), *The Center Won't Hold* (2019) -- documents a band that began in the Olympia underground and became one of the defining acts in American rock music, full stop.

What Sleater-Kinney did technically is unusual: Carrie Brownstein and Corin Tucker play two guitars with no bass, and the band does not sound thin. The absence of bass is not a deficit; it is a structural choice that forces the two guitar lines to carry the entire harmonic weight of the music while Weiss's drumming provides the rhythmic foundation. The result is a guitar-driven rock sound that is simultaneously dense and open -- dense because both guitars are always doing something, open because the low end is not filled in. Tucker's vibrato-heavy vocals and Brownstein's more clipped delivery interlock in harmonies and counterpoint that are distinctive and unmistakable. Sleater-Kinney sound like themselves and no one else.

E=8 for Sleater-Kinney is the correct assignment. The band's catalog spans dynamics -- The Hot Rock is quieter than Call the Doctor, The Woods is louder than both -- but the core energy is high, the political commitment is consistent, and the musicianship is exceptional. E=8 reflects the full catalog, weighted toward the peaks. It is the right score.

Sleater-Kinney formed in Olympia during the riot grrrl period and carry the riot grrrl political inheritance -- feminist politics, DIY infrastructure, direct address -- into a musical context that grew beyond the punk underground to encompass the full range of American rock music. The band's trajectory from Olympia to international recognition represents a path that begins in the same infrastructure as Bikini Kill and Beat Happening -- Kill Rock Stars, K Records, the Olympia community -- and extends further than any other act in that infrastructure. Degree 277 closes the Olympia sequence. Four consecutive degrees (275-278) have documented the Olympia underground: Beat Happening's lo-fi foundation, Bikini Kill's riot grrrl politics, Sleater-Kinney's rock ascent, and Unwound's post-hardcore close. The sequence is complete.
