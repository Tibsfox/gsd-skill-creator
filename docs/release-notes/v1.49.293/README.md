## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Phasianidae (1st entry -- gamebird family opens)
- **Order:** Galliformes
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The Wild Turkey (*Meleagris gallopavo*) is the largest gamebird in North America and one of only two domesticated birds native to the New World (the other being the Muscovy Duck). In the Pacific Northwest, Wild Turkeys are an introduced but now established population, particularly in the oak woodlands and mixed forests of western Washington and Oregon. The **gobble** is one of the most recognizable sounds in North American birding -- a rapid, liquid, bubbling vocalization produced by males during the spring breeding season. The sound is generated by air forced through the syrinx and modulated by the fleshy snood and wattle, creating a cascading, rattling quality unlike any other avian vocalization. A full gobble carries over a mile in still air, making it one of the most far-reaching calls in the engine. At E=6, the Wild Turkey resets the energy level after the Columbidae quartet's gentle coos and claps, announcing the Phasianidae with maximum volume.
- **Geo arc:** Born Geologic (George Quibuyen's musical partner, born Prometeo Alexis Quibuyen -- note: Geo and Prometheus Brown are two different people despite both being Filipino-American MCs from Blue Scholars). Geo is the MC half of Blue Scholars alongside producer Sabzi, and his solo work through Mass Line Media extends the duo's concern with community organizing, immigrant identity, and Seattle's South End neighborhoods. Placing Geo immediately after Prometheus Brown at 152 creates a deliberate back-to-back pairing that foreshadows the Blue Scholars reunion at 156. Geo's 3rd appearance in the engine's orbit (Blue Scholars at 99, and now solo at 153).
- **Phasianidae preview:** The gamebird family will run through degrees 153-156 as a four-degree quartet -- Wild Turkey (153), Blue Grouse (154), Ruffed Grouse (155), Sooty Grouse (156). Four species, four distinct sound production methods, spanning the full range of gallinaceous acoustics from vocal gobble to subsonic boom to mechanical drumming.
