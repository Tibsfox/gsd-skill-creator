## Summary

**High Performance** arrives at E=6, the second entry in the Nastymix trilogy -- a Tacoma-connected act that brings the city back into the engine for the first time since its earlier appearances. High Performance's presence reinforces that Nastymix Records' reach extended beyond Seattle proper into the broader Puget Sound region. On the species side, the **Marbled Murrelet** calls at E=4 with a high, thin **keer keer keer** -- a rapid, piercing call heard at dawn and dusk as the bird commutes between its old-growth forest nest and the ocean. The Marbled Murrelet is one of the most extraordinary birds in the Pacific Northwest: a seabird that nests **in old-growth trees**, sometimes 50+ miles inland, on moss-covered branches high in the canopy. It is **ESA Threatened**, its fate tied directly to the fate of old-growth forests. Where the Common Murre (177) nests on bare cliff ledges in dense colonies, the Marbled Murrelet nests alone, in silence, in ancient trees. The 5th Alcidae, and the most ecologically urgent.

- ESA Threatened -- listed under the Endangered Species Act since 1992, the Marbled Murrelet's decline is driven by old-growth logging, nest predation (corvids attracted by human food waste), and marine prey shifts; its survival is a direct proxy for old-growth forest health
