## Summary

The twenty-fourth paired release and the first fresh dual-build -- the first degree where no pre-built NASA research.md existed, where both halves were constructed from catalog data and accumulated engine context. On October 3, 1962, at Launch Complex 14 at Cape Canaveral, Atlas 113-D ignited beneath Mercury spacecraft number 16. Its pilot had named it Sigma 7 -- Σ, the Greek summation symbol, the character that appears in every engineering equation where you add things up. Not Freedom, not Liberty Bell, not Friendship, not Aurora. The sum. Walter Marty Schirra Jr. was inside. He was thirty-nine years old, a Navy commander, an Annapolis graduate (class of 1945), a veteran of 90 combat missions in F-84E Thunderjets over Korea including an exchange tour with the 154th Fighter-Bomber Squadron where he shot down two MiG-15s while flying an Air Force airplane on loan from the Navy -- a fact that delighted his competitive sensibilities because it meant he had combat kills in another service's aircraft. He was the engineer of the Mercury Seven, the one who wanted to fly the mission exactly as designed. Not heroically. Not poetically. Not adventurously. Correctly.

NASA needed correct. John Glenn's Friendship 7 had been complicated by the heat shield indicator scare that nearly cost the mission its pilot. Scott Carpenter's Aurora 7 had been operationally messy -- fuel consumption far above the flight plan, science experiments prioritized over engineering data, a 250-mile overshoot that put the recovery fleet into sustained uncertainty for nearly an hour while Carpenter floated in a life raft beyond the planned landing ellipse. After one flight that survived problems and one flight that created problems, NASA needed a flight that had no problems. They needed an engineering validation flight that proved the Mercury spacecraft and its pilot could execute a mission profile with precision, conserve resources methodically, and return to the planned recovery point on target. They got Schirra. Six orbits -- doubling the three-orbit missions flown by Glenn and Carpenter. Nine hours and thirteen minutes. The key metric was hydrogen peroxide attitude control fuel: Glenn had landed with 61% remaining after three orbits; Carpenter had landed with 17% after three orbits; Schirra landed with 78% remaining after six orbits. He had twice as many orbits and four times Carpenter's fuel margin. He achieved this through drifting flight -- long periods of no active attitude control, letting the capsule tumble gently through space, using the automatic and manual systems only when the flight plan called for specific attitudes. The discipline of not touching the controls when the controls did not need to be touched. Splashdown was 4.5 miles from the recovery carrier USS Kearsarge in the central Pacific, southwest of Midway -- close enough that Schirra could see the ship from his capsule window as he descended under the parachute. The precision was absolute. Every parameter within spec. The textbook flight.

Schirra chose the name Sigma because he was the kind of man who thought in symbols. Not literary symbols -- mathematical ones. The sum of all the work. The capsule was the summation of McDonnell Aircraft's engineering, of the Space Task Group's mission planning, of the Worldwide Tracking Network's coordination across fifteen ground stations and five ocean-tracking ships, of the Atlas booster's gradually improving reliability, and of the five Mercury flights that preceded him. Glenn had proven the system could orbit. Carpenter had proven it could observe. Schirra proved it could operate -- routinely, efficiently, with the margins that would be required for longer missions. The Mercury program's penultimate flight was its most important for engineering: not the first orbit, not the science mission, but the demonstration that the machine worked as designed when flown as designed. The importance of boring. The precision flight that made Gemini possible because it proved Mercury was a production system, not a series of one-off adventures. The same way The Sonics' later garage rock refinement and Maktub's collective groove and Bill Frisell's hybrid jazz each required somebody, somewhere, to first demonstrate that the fundamental apparatus worked as designed -- so that subsequent work could extend rather than re-validate.

## Part A: Allen Stone -- THE PREACHER'S SON

The emotional directness of worship music became the foundation of a vocal style that earns comparisons to Stevie Wonder, Donny Hathaway, and Bill Withers. The preacher's son as a distinct artist-city pattern: rural, religious, self-taught, late-to-the-scene.

## Part B: Black-headed Grosbeak -- THE CONVERGENT VOICE

- **Species identity and Cardinalidae family entry.** The Black-headed Grosbeak (*Pheucticus melanocephalus*) is a large-bodied North American songbird in the family Cardinalidae, the cardinals and grosbeaks -- the fifteenth bird family in the engine. Males weigh 38-47 grams, females slightly larger. Breeding plumage on males features a black head, burnt-orange breast, and contrasting white wing-patches; females are streaked brown with a white supercilium. The bird is a common breeder in the mixed forests of the Pacific Northwest, wintering in Mexico.
- **Convergent vocal resemblance and the robin parallel.** The grosbeak's song is a rich, rolling series of slurred whistles at E=4, remarkably similar to the American Robin (*Turdus migratorius*, Turdidae, degree 25) in pitch range, phrase length, and melodic contour. The similarity is not mimicry -- the grosbeak does not learn from the robin and does not copy its repertoire. The two species diverged tens of millions of years ago. The resemblance is convergent evolution: two unrelated lineages arriving at similar acoustic solutions through independent optimization for shared habitat conditions (mixed forest canopy, open woodland edge, territorial broadcast from elevated perches).
- **Seed-crushing bill as first jaw-powered tool in SPS.** The grosbeak's conical bill is massive relative to its body -- the largest bill-to-body ratio of any songbird documented in the engine so far. The supporting jaw musculature is correspondingly hypertrophied. The bill can crack sunflower seeds, cherry pits, and pine-nut hulls that smaller passerines cannot process. The Cardinalidae bill is the engine's first documented vise-style jaw, distinct from the Picidae probing and drumming bills at degrees 21 and 22 and from the insectivorous warbler and thrush bills of earlier degrees.
- **Mixed-forest habitat and broad geographic range.** Black-headed Grosbeaks breed from southwestern British Columbia and central Alberta south through the western United States into central Mexico, occupying mixed-deciduous and coniferous forests with canopy cover 40-80% and open understory. The PNW breeding population occurs primarily in second-growth Douglas-fir / bigleaf-maple mixed stands at 100-1500m elevation. The species is a classic neotropical migrant, wintering in Mexico from Sinaloa south through Oaxaca.
- **Shared parental incubation and female singing behavior.** Unlike most Passeriformes, where males conduct nearly all territorial singing while females remain acoustically quiet, female Black-headed Grosbeaks sing during incubation -- a behavior documented by Ritchison (1983) and confirmed in multiple PNW breeding-ecology studies. Both sexes also share incubation duties in approximately equal time allocations, a rare trait among songbirds. The grosbeak is the first SPS species in the engine where females are documented vocal participants in territorial acoustic displays.
- **Diet as seasonal tool-switching.** Grosbeaks shift diet with season -- insects and arthropods during breeding (where the seed-crushing bill works less efficiently but still handles beetle and cicada exoskeletons), seeds and fruit during migration and wintering (where the vise-bill reaches its design optimum). The species uses the same tool for two different functions across the calendar, the same way Schirra's Mercury capsule executed both orbital operations and recovery procedures using the same hardware in different modes.
- **Convergence as degree-23 structural motif.** The grosbeak-robin acoustic convergence is one instance of a pattern that appears across every track at degree 23. Schirra flew the same capsule Glenn and Carpenter flew and achieved a different outcome through different methods. Stone developed a soul vocabulary that sounds like Stevie Wonder through independent autodidact paths rather than through Motown institutional training. The beaver builds infrastructure with its teeth the way Schirra's mission-planning team built flight plans with spreadsheets: jaw-powered engineering in one case, desk-powered engineering in the other, both producing reshaped environments through patient accumulation of precisely-placed material.
- **First family introduction since degree 19.** Cardinalidae at degree 23 is the first new family introduced in the engine since the Turdidae expansion at earlier degrees and the Parulidae quartet completion at degrees 14-17. Between degree 19 and degree 23, the engine cycled through Troglodytidae, Paridae, and Picidae species without introducing a new family. Cardinalidae entry at degree 23 re-starts the family-introduction cadence and foreshadows the Turdidae return at degree 25.

### What Could Be Better

- **The Allen Stone album trajectory was named but not unpacked.** *Allen Stone* (2011), *Radius* (2015), *Building Balance* (2019), and *Apart* (2021) are listed, but each album's specific harmonic and rhythmic contribution to Stone's evolving sound was not traced at the song or sideman level. A future chapter should produce a per-album ledger with producer credits, rhythm-section personnel, and representative tracks mapped against the gospel-to-soul vocabulary thesis.
- **The female-singing grosbeak behavior was asserted but not sourced in depth.** The Black-headed Grosbeak female singing behavior, while documented in ornithological literature, was referenced without full citation density. A future pass should cite Ritchison (1983), Johnson (1997), and Cornell Lab eBird behavior-track data to anchor the acoustic claim empirically.
- **The convergent-evolution mechanism stayed at the metaphor level.** The release claims grosbeak-robin acoustic similarity arises through convergent evolution, but it did not trace the specific selection pressures (canopy height, territorial-broadcast range, sexual selection for pitch range) that produced the convergence. A future chapter should unpack the mechanism rather than rely on the metaphor.

*Degree 23: the precision flight. A Navy pilot from Annapolis who shot down two MiG-15s while flying an Air Force airplane, who named his capsule after the summation symbol because he thought in equations rather than poetry -- who flew six orbits and landed with 78% of his fuel because he understood that the most important thing a pilot can do is not touch the controls when the controls do not need to be touched, who splashed down 4.5 miles from the carrier and could see the ship from his window, who flew the most boring and most important mission in the Mercury program because boring is what production systems do and the point was to prove that Mercury was a production system. A preacher's son from Chewelah, Washington -- population 2,600, one stoplight, no record stores -- who assembled an entire soul education from recordings alone at age fifteen, who led worship in his father's church, who sang "Unaware" in his mother's living room while a camera recorded and sixteen million people watched, the first career in the engine ignited by a YouTube video rather than a label or a venue or a teacher. A grosbeak that sounds like a robin through convergent evolution -- two unrelated lineages, same acoustic target, different paths, the precision of natural selection arriving at the same solution twice. A beaver that builds dams the way Schirra flew missions: not by heroics but by infrastructure, by the patient accumulation of material into structures that reshape the landscape. The first fresh dual-build. The engine proves it can build both halves from nothing. The textbook does not announce itself as a textbook. The discipline conceals its own mechanism. The sum adds up.*

*"Sigma 7: the summation. Six orbits, 78% fuel remaining, 4.5 miles from the ship. The precision that makes everything after it possible. The flight nobody remembers because nothing went wrong."*
