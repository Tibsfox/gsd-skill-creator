## Summary

**Tullycraft** arrives at E=3 -- the **FIRST TWEE POP** entry in the Seattle 360 engine, and the lowest-energy artist entry in Q3. Twee pop is the deliberate embrace of the small, the sweet, the handmade: lo-fi recordings, whimsical lyrics, and a principled rejection of rock machismo. Tullycraft -- Sean Tollefson's long-running project -- is Seattle's definitive twee band, releasing records on K Records and Mis Sounds with titles like *Old Traditions, New Standards* and *Every Scene Needs a Center*. At E=3, Tullycraft is the gentlest sound in the engine since the Harbor Porpoise (187, E=1), a deliberate whisper in a catalog that has been shouting. On the species side, the **Blue Whale infrasound** registers at E=5 -- but E=5 dramatically understates what is happening acoustically. The Blue Whale is the **largest animal that has ever lived on Earth** -- up to 100 feet long, 200 tons -- and its vocalizations at **188 decibels** are the **loudest sound produced by any living organism**. Blue Whale calls operate in the infrasonic range (10-40 Hz), below human hearing, and can travel across entire ocean basins. The E=5 rating reflects that much of this sound is imperceptible to human ears; the raw acoustic power is beyond anything else in the catalog.

## Key Features

- **Part A -- S36:** Tullycraft (Twee Pop, E=3) -- FIRST TWEE POP, K Records, gentlest Q3 entry
- **Part B -- SPS:** Blue Whale infrasound (deep pulse, E=5) -- largest animal EVER, 188 dB loudest biological sound
- FIRST TWEE POP -- Genre Stage 105 introduces twee pop; the genre is a philosophy as much as a sound: smallness as resistance, gentleness as aesthetic choice, lo-fi as ethical position; Tullycraft embodies every principle
- LARGEST ANIMAL EVER -- the Blue Whale exceeds every dinosaur, every prehistoric marine reptile, every organism that has ever existed; at 200 tons, it is larger than *Argentinosaurus*, larger than *Patagotitan*, larger than anything the fossil record has produced
- 188 dB LOUDEST BIOLOGICAL SOUND -- Blue Whale calls are louder than a jet engine at takeoff (140 dB) and can be detected by hydrophones thousands of miles away; the sound is so low-frequency that most of it falls below the threshold of human hearing
- Infrasonic communication -- Blue Whale calls at 10-40 Hz are below human hearing range; these signals propagate across ocean basins via the SOFAR channel (Sound Fixing and Ranging), a deep-water acoustic waveguide; two Blue Whales on opposite sides of the Pacific can theoretically hear each other
- E=3 to 188 dB -- the juxtaposition of Tullycraft's E=3 whisper and the Blue Whale's 188 dB infrasound is the most extreme energy contrast in the engine; the quietest artist paired with the loudest animal; perception vs. physics
