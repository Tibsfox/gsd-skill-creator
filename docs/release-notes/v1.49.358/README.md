## Lessons Learned

1. **Ceiling-raising events can occur along multiple axes simultaneously.** Degree 217 raises the callback-artist amplitude ceiling (PGMG E=5 → E=6) and the SPS acoustic-event amplitude ceiling (Grizzly roar to E=8 third-entry) simultaneously within a single degree. The engine's amplitude vocabulary should account for multi-axis ceiling-raising as a distinct pattern from single-axis ceiling-raising, because the interpretive weight of the combined event exceeds the sum of the individual events.
2. **Callback-with-amplitude-shift is a distinct structural register from floor-not-ceiling preservation.** When a callback artist returns at an amplitude different from their original appearance (PGMG E=5 → E=6), the shift is structurally meaningful rather than incidental. Future callback entries should be recorded with explicit shift-or-preservation tracking, and the engine's callback vocabulary should distinguish between floor-not-ceiling callbacks (Kinski, Helms Alee) and accumulated-context-warranted shift callbacks (PGMG) as separate first-class registers.
3. **Near-identity callback clusters are structurally distinct from strict callback-pair-identity.** Three consecutive callbacks at 103-103-102 degrees (Kinski, Helms Alee, PGMG) document near-identity rather than strict identity, and the one-degree variation is structurally meaningful. The engine's callback vocabulary should track cluster-near-identity patterns as distinct from pair-identity patterns, because the interpretive implications (callback distances are clustered rather than aligned to a specific number) differ.
4. **Infrasonic-component acoustic features are honestly measurable and should be documented at face value.** The Grizzly roar's sub-20 Hz acoustic energy is physically measurable and has specific propagation-and-perception consequences (felt before heard, propagates through terrain over longer distances than higher frequencies match). The engine's SPS vocabulary should document infrasonic components at face value rather than interpretively layering them, because the physical-acoustic reality is structurally sufficient without amplification.
5. **E=8 absolute-maximum-amplitude assignments require honest physical-acoustic justification.** The three E=8 entries (Cougar scream 212, Grizzly roar 217, The Blood Brothers 218) are each grounded in honest physical-acoustic output at the maximum-register limit of their respective domains. Future E=8 assignments should be constrained by similar honest-signal maximum-register criteria, because the interpretive weight of the absolute-maximum-amplitude tier depends on narrow occupancy grounded in physical reality.
6. **Family-internal amplitude range reflects body-mass-and-acoustic-physiology breadth within the family.** The Ursidae family's four-step internal amplitude range (Black Bear E=5 huff to Grizzly roar E=8) reflects the body-mass difference between the two North American species (Black Bear 70-270 kg, Grizzly 180-360 kg) and the corresponding acoustic-physiology difference. The engine's family-documentation vocabulary should track internal amplitude range as a structural feature alongside species-composition and habitat-distribution, because internal range is interpretively distinct from cross-family comparison.
7. **Keystone-species ecological role pairs with apex-amplitude acoustic signal at degree 217 in a way that previous degrees have not documented.** The Grizzly Bear's keystone-species function (soil bioturbation, salmon-nutrient transport, wildlife-corridor formation) pairs with the apex-amplitude dominance roar to produce a structural observation that a single species can occupy apex positions on both ecological and acoustic axes simultaneously. The engine's species-documentation vocabulary should track cross-axis apex occupancy as a distinct pattern, because the interpretive weight of simultaneous apex positions exceeds any single-axis apex occupancy.
8. **The voice-as-commanding-force cross-axis strengthens through cross-domain parity between human-musical-arrangement and biological-apex-terrestrial-vocalization domains.** Pretty Girls Make Graves' commanding mid-range post-punk-revival vocal authority and the Grizzly Bear roar's 100+ dB dominance display both instantiate voice-authority as a first-class organizing force, and the two instantiations in radically different domains demonstrate that the structural principle applies cross-domain rather than being confined to either human-musical or biological-acoustic contexts. The engine's cross-axis vocabulary is most productive when the two halves instantiate the register in genuinely different domains.
9. **Return-with-amplitude-shift callbacks document the engine's capacity for retrospective re-evaluation.** The PGMG return at E=6 raised from E=5 reflects the engine's re-evaluation of the band's post-punk-revival urgency through 102 degrees of accumulated context. The retrospective-re-evaluation capacity is structurally meaningful because it demonstrates that the engine's amplitude ratings are subject to continuing refinement as context accumulates rather than being fixed at first appearance. Future engine work should treat amplitude ratings as provisional until sufficient context has accumulated.
10. **Population-crisis context pairs with acoustic-amplitude-maximum in a structurally distinct way from population-abundance context.** The Grizzly Bear's fewer-than-10 North Cascades population paired with its E=8 absolute-maximum acoustic amplitude produces a structural juxtaposition where acoustic presence is inversely related to physical presence. This pattern is distinct from species with both acoustic and population abundance (Black Bear at degrees 200-202) or species with acoustic and population absence (near-extirpated species whose acoustic amplitude is also low). The engine's species-documentation vocabulary should track acoustic-amplitude-versus-population-density as a structural dimension.
