# Retrospective — v1.49.54

## What Worked

1. **Module 06 "Smoke Taint" is the most cross-domain module in the series.** Wildfire smoke (ECO/CAS fire ecology) + wine grapes (AGR viticulture) + air quality (AWF) + climate change (THE) — four domains colliding in one module. This is the Rosetta Stone framework working at its best: a phenomenon that can only be understood across multiple lenses.

2. **Module 04 "Grand Coulee" connects agriculture to every other infrastructure project.** The same dam that irrigates 671,000 acres also generates hydroelectric power (HGE/THE), blocks salmon migration (SAL), and displaced indigenous communities at Kettle Falls (TIBS). One piece of infrastructure, four competing uses, four Research projects with different perspectives on the same structure.

3. **The TeX source was compact (306 lines) but dense.** Four deep-dive modules with specific science (codling moth pheromone chemistry, Cosmic Crisp genomics, Jory soil mineralogy). The research agent expanded this to 8 modules totaling 1,663 lines. Expansion ratio of 5.4x — the highest yet. Dense source material produces the richest transformation.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Agriculture is the missing layer the ecology cluster needed.** COL, CAS, ECO map the wild landscape. GDN maps the garden. AGR maps the industrial agriculture that sits between — the orchards, vineyards, and irrigated fields that are neither wild nor garden. The bioregion map now has all three scales: wilderness, cultivation, and the managed landscape between.

2. **The Columbia River is the project's central thread.** It appears in SAL (salmon), HGE (hydroelectric), THE (energy), AGR (irrigation), COL (watershed), and WYR (timber transport). Six projects, one river. The Research series is a map of the Columbia Basin viewed from six different angles. The river connects them all.
