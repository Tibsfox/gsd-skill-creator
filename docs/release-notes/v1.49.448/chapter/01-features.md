- the salmon run at E=8 is not a species-level entry but an event-level entry; the engine documents the aggregate acoustic output of an entire river system at peak spawning density; this is the SPS track's most ecologically broad single entry -- not a species but a phenomenon; the aggregate designation is appropriate for a sound that exists only at the population level and not at the individual level; E=8 reflects the accumulated acoustic energy of thousands of fish simultaneously

## Salmon as ecological foundation


- the Pacific Northwest salmon run is the ecological event that structures the food web: it feeds the bears (documented at degrees 304-305), attracts the eagles (documented at degree 297), supports the seals and sea lions (documented at degree 306), and has structured human settlement, trade, and culture in the region for ten thousand years; the salmon run's acoustic presence at degree 307 is the engine acknowledging the food base that makes every apex predator in the arc possible; the sound is the foundation speaking

## River system acoustic ecology


- the Hoh River on the Olympic Peninsula is the defining Pacific Northwest salmon river: wild runs of chinook, coho, pink, and chum salmon in sequence through September-December produce the aggregate acoustic event documented at degree 307; the Snohomish and Skagit rivers provide similar aggregate runs in the Puget Sound drainage; the acoustic character of the salmon run -- the specific combination of splash, slap, and churn that varies with species, river gradient, and run density -- is one of the Pacific Northwest's most site-specific and temporally specific sounds; it exists for six to eight weeks per year and then is silent

## Ellensburg as grunge origin point


- the Screaming Trees came from Ellensburg, not Seattle; their inclusion in the grunge canon is accurate but their geographic origin is the interior Cascades, not the Puget Sound basin; the engine places them at degree 307 as a Seattle-orbit band rather than a Seattle-native band; the distinction is documented in the summary; Ellensburg's contribution to the grunge canon through the Screaming Trees is one of the less-discussed geographic expansions of the Seattle sound
