## Summary

The harmonic series made audible. Julian Priester's trombone is the only standard orchestral brass instrument that produces pitch through a continuous slide mechanism — no valves, no keys, no frets. The slide can stop anywhere between the seven standard positions, producing any frequency within its range, including all the microtonal intervals between the notes of the Western twelve-tone scale. The Hermit Thrush's song has been formally demonstrated by Doolittle and Brumm (2014, *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*) to follow the harmonic series — the sequence of frequencies produced when a fundamental is multiplied by successive integers (f, 2f, 3f, 4f...), whose ratios (2:1 octave, 3:2 perfect fifth, 5:4 major third) are the mathematical foundation of all tonal music. Both inhabit the space where physics becomes music. The slide has no frets. The bird sings the overtone series. Both prove that the mathematics of music was never invented — it was discovered.

Degree 7 breaks the Central District concentration that dominated degrees 0-6. Priester is the first S36 artist whose Seattle connection is not rooted in the Central District — he is associated with Cornish College of the Arts on Capitol Hill, not the Jackson Street corridor. He is the first artist born outside the Pacific Northwest (Chicago, South Side), the first whose formative pipeline passed through DuSable High School under Captain Walter Dyett (the same institutional pipeline that produced Nat King Cole, Dinah Washington, Gene Ammons, Johnny Griffin, and Von Freeman), and the first whose career bridges two of the most important experimental traditions in jazz history: Sun Ra's Arkestra (cosmic Afrofuturism, 1953-1956) and Herbie Hancock's Mwandishi sextet (electronic jazz, 1970-1973). Priester's arc reaches from the South Side of Chicago in 1935 to Cornish College of the Arts on Capitol Hill in 1979 and beyond — a forty-four-year span that covers hard bop (Max Roach's *We Insist! Freedom Now Suite*, 1960), orchestral jazz (Duke Ellington, late 1950s-1960s), Afrofuturist cosmic jazz (Sun Ra), first-wave electronic jazz (Mwandishi with Fender Rhodes, wah-wah pedal, Echoplex tape delay, and ARP synthesizer), and experimental pedagogy (Cornish as the expansion-oriented counterpart to Standifer's preservation-oriented University of Washington). The trombone's slide was the physical mechanism that let Priester move freely across these traditions — a continuous-pitch instrument matched the continuous-pitch aesthetics of both Afrofuturism and analog electronics.

For the species engine, degree 7 introduces the vertical dimension — the Hermit Thrush breeds in mountain forest at 2,000-7,000 feet, the first species defined by altitude. Every prior SPS entry (Great Blue Heron at wetland, Pacific Wren at understory, Varied Thrush at dense conifer, Black-capped Chickadee at urban edge, Spotted Owl at old-growth, Red-breasted Nuthatch at mid-canopy, Pacific-slope Flycatcher at lowland forest) occupied horizontal habitat gradients. The Hermit Thrush climbs. Its breeding range is subalpine fir, mountain hemlock, and Engelmann spruce in the Cascades and Olympics — habitats that only exist above a certain elevation and that compress as climate warming pushes vegetation zones upward. The species is an altitudinal migrant: it breeds at elevation in summer, descends to lowland in winter. Same individual bird occupies different acoustic niches by moving vertically rather than latitudinally. The thrush family inversion emerges as the key comparative insight: the Varied Thrush (degree 2) invests acoustic complexity in timbre (AM-modulated buzz, dense overtone spectrum per note, but only one note per phrase), while the Hermit Thrush invests complexity in melody (pure, flute-like, near-sinusoidal notes, but chained into phrases with harmonic-series interval structure). Timbre complexity and melodic complexity trade off. Acoustic bandwidth is finite. The Turdidae family demonstrates both allocation strategies in the same PNW forest.

The resonance between Part A and Part B is not analogical — it is mathematical. Priester's slide gives him direct physical access to the overtone series in a way that valved brass instruments do not. The slide can voice any frequency, which means it can voice the precise integer ratios of the harmonic series (2:1, 3:2, 4:3, 5:4, 6:5) without tempering. The Hermit Thrush's phrases preferentially select pitches from those same ratios, as demonstrated by the 2014 PNAS analysis of 71 songs from 14 individuals. When Priester plays an ascending line that arpeggiates the overtone series, and a Hermit Thrush sings a phrase that ascends through the same intervals, the two events are not related by metaphor. They are related by physics. The same mathematical structure is present in both signals, because the harmonic series is not a cultural artifact but a property of vibrating systems.

Degree 7 is also the first degree that makes the engine's operating hypothesis testable. Earlier degrees paired musicians and species on aesthetic and thematic grounds — Quincy Jones and the Great Blue Heron share a regal self-possession, Frisell and the Pacific Wren share a cascading intricacy. These parallels are productive but not falsifiable. The Priester/Hermit Thrush pairing is falsifiable. If the Doolittle and Brumm finding had failed peer review, the pairing would collapse. It did not fail. It is published in PNAS. The harmonic-series bird is real. The continuous-pitch trombonist is real. The degree holds.

The bridge concept deserves separate emphasis because it is the structural engine of Priester's career. He did not spend his life in any one tradition. He spent his life carrying material across traditions. Sun Ra to Max Roach. Max Roach to Duke Ellington. Ellington to Mwandishi. Mwandishi to Cornish. Each transition carried forward something from the previous context while absorbing the new one. The Arkestra taught him that jazz could be about anything. Roach taught him discipline under pressure. Ellington taught him orchestral precision. Hancock taught him that acoustic instruments could talk to electronic ones. Cornish gave him a room in which to teach students who might go anywhere next. Priester's students inherited a pedagogy of expansion, not preservation. The University of Washington under Standifer (degree 4) teaches the standards — "learn this." Cornish under Priester teaches the spaces between the standards — "go beyond this." Two Seattle institutions, two pedagogies, two brass players (Standifer trumpet, Priester trombone), the eighth and ninth degrees of the Seattle cultural circuit.

The degree also marks a geographic rotation that the engine had earned. Seven consecutive degrees centered on the Central District and Jackson Street (Quincy Jones, Bill Frisell with CD connections, Wayne Horvitz with Fremont/Capitol Hill drift, Ernestine Anderson, Floyd Standifer at UW but with deep CD roots, Overton Berry, and Patti Bown) documented the Central District cluster exhaustively. Degree 7 moves to Capitol Hill. Degree 8 (Gary Peacock) will move out of the city entirely (Yakima). The engine is not abandoning the CD — it will return — but it has earned the right to rotate. Rotation is not abandonment. The CD cluster remains the gravitational center; it is now large enough that the engine can orbit away and come back.

The Doolittle and Brumm finding deserves a final word. The paper analyzed 71 songs from 14 individual Hermit Thrushes recorded across multiple populations. Statistical analysis demonstrated that phrase pitches were not drawn from a continuous distribution but preferentially selected from the harmonic series of the phrase's fundamental. The result is not ambiguous. It is not a weak signal that might be an artifact. It is a published finding in *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences*, one of the world's most rigorously peer-reviewed journals. The harmonic series is a physical law. The Hermit Thrush arrived at it through natural selection. The human musical tradition arrived at it through cultural evolution — the Pythagorean monochord, the Aristoxenian tetrachord, Helmholtz's *On the Sensations of Tone*, Schoenberg's *Harmonielehre*, all three thousand years of Western theory. The convergence is the finding. Two completely different evolutionary pathways — biological and cultural — both converged on the same mathematical structure because the structure is imposed by physics, not by choice. Degree 7 is where the engine stops claiming "music resembles biology" and begins claiming "music IS biology." The shift is load-bearing for every subsequent degree. Future species with bioacoustic peer-reviewed research will be prioritized; future musicians with continuous-pitch instruments or electronic processing will be read through Priester's lens.

## Part A: Julian Priester — The Bridge Between Sun Ra and Hancock

Full deep research covering the complete Julian Priester arc from Chicago origins through the Arkestra, Max Roach and Duke Ellington, Mwandishi, and Cornish:

- **Chicago Origins and DuSable Pipeline:** Born June 29, 1935, in Chicago, Illinois. DuSable High School under Captain Walter Dyett — the institutional pipeline parallel to Garfield High School in Seattle. Same school produced Nat King Cole, Dinah Washington, Gene Ammons, Johnny Griffin, and Von Freeman. Dyett's pedagogy combined rigorous reading with improvisational freedom, a combination that shaped every DuSable graduate. Priester is the eighth degree, seventh jazz artist, first trombonist, and second brass player after Standifer (degree 4, trumpet).
- **Sun Ra's Arkestra (1953-1956):** Joined at eighteen. The Arkestra was not merely a band — it was a cosmology involving space travel, ancient Egypt, Afrofuturism, and systematic rejection of the entertainment-industry framework. Sun Ra demanded total commitment: rehearsals were daily, the repertoire was vast, and the aesthetic was uncompromising. Priester absorbed the premise that jazz could be about anything, could go anywhere, and was not obligated to satisfy conventional expectations. The Arkestra was his formative experience; everything after is shaped by it.
- **Max Roach and Duke Ellington (Late 1950s-1960s):** Hard bop discipline with Roach, including *We Insist! Freedom Now Suite* (1960) with Abbey Lincoln — one of the most politically charged jazz statements of the civil rights era. Orchestral precision with Ellington during the 1969-1970 period. Priester's ability to function in both contexts — Arkestra cosmic chaos and Ellington orchestral order, freedom and structure — demonstrates a technical and musical range few trombonists have matched. The slide let him sit inside a tight big-band section and also improvise against Sun Ra's free-form sheets.
- **Herbie Hancock's Mwandishi Sextet (1970-1973):** The hinge of Priester's career. Hancock (Mwandishi), Priester (Pepo Mtoto), Bennie Maupin, Eddie Henderson, Buster Williams, Billy Hart. Swahili names, Pan-African consciousness. First wave of jazz electronics: Fender Rhodes, wah-wah pedal, Echoplex tape delay, ARP synthesizer. Priester's trombone as both acoustic source and processing candidate — the slide's natural portamento matched the synthesizer's pitch-bending. Canonical recordings: *Mwandishi* (1971), *Crossings* (1972), *Sextant* (1973). These three albums are the engine's documentary evidence for electronic jazz first wave; they predate the fusion era proper and demonstrate a specifically Afrofuturist rather than rock-derived integration of electronics.
- **Seattle and Cornish College (1979-present):** Arrived as a mature artist with three decades spanning the full range of jazz. Cornish faculty — experimental, interdisciplinary, expansion-oriented pedagogy. Connected with Frisell (degree 1) and Horvitz (degree 2) to form the Priester-Horvitz-Frisell experimental jazz constellation. The advance party — arrived a decade before the others. Priester's long Cornish tenure made him the elder of Seattle's experimental jazz scene, a mentor figure whose students carried the Mwandishi and Arkestra lineages into the next generation.
- **Music Theory Mapping:** Five theory nodes — trombone slide as continuous pitch (the discrete-continuous spectrum: trumpet with valves vs. trombone with slide), microtonal blue note continuum (the slide gives direct physical access to the zone between major and minor third), modal jazz (Dorian, Mixolydian, Lydian modes across the Arkestra/Roach/Mwandishi arc), electronic jazz texture first wave (Fender Rhodes + wah-wah + Echoplex + ARP = electroacoustic jazz), and expansion-vs-preservation pedagogy (Standifer at UW teaches "learn this"; Priester at Cornish teaches "go beyond this").
- **Artist-City Pattern — The Pedagogue-Draw:** The eighth pattern. A hybrid of The Draw and The Teacher. Drawn to Seattle AND became a teacher, but oriented toward expansion rather than preservation. The first compound pattern in the taxonomy. Priester did not come to Seattle to document what had been; he came to teach students how to go somewhere new.
- **ECM Records Connection:** Priester's later solo recordings on ECM (*Love, Love*, 1974) connect him to the European avant-garde jazz aesthetic that also produced Frisell's later work. The ECM sound — spacious, reverberant, patient — suits Priester's legato slide phrasing. The ECM connection binds degrees 1 (Frisell) and 7 (Priester) through shared label identity and shared aesthetic values (patience, space, acoustic clarity).

## Part B: Hermit Thrush — The Harmonic Series Bird

Full deep research covering the PNW's mathematical singer:

- **Taxonomy:** *Catharus guttatus*, family Turdidae (thrushes). Genus *Catharus* from Greek *katharos* meaning "pure" — the taxonomy declares that purity of voice is the defining characteristic. Species *guttatus* means "spotted" (Latin *gutta*, drop). Up to 12 recognized subspecies; PNW populations primarily *C. g. guttatus* (coastal) and *C. g. nanus* (interior). The rufous tail is the single most diagnostic visual mark — contrasts sharply with the browner back. 6.5-7 inches, 23-37 grams.
- **Bioacoustics — The Doolittle and Brumm Finding (2014, PNAS):** Analysis of 71 songs from 14 individuals demonstrated that Hermit Thrush phrases preferentially select pitches from the harmonic series. The birds begin phrases with a clear fundamental and elaborate using notes whose frequencies correspond to overtone relationships. This is not metaphor. It is not anthropomorphic projection. It is a statistically validated finding published in one of the world's premier scientific journals. The harmonic series is not a human invention — it is a physical law. The bird arrived there by natural selection. The human arrived there by cultural evolution. They meet at the same place.
- **Song Structure:** Each phrase follows an arch form — ascending through several notes, reaching a peak, descending. Successive phrases start at different pitches, creating a spiral through pitch space. Fundamental range 1.5-4.0 kHz. Phrase duration 1.5-3.0 seconds. 5-12 notes per phrase. 6-12 distinct phrase types per individual. The timbre is pure, flute-like — strong fundamental, minimal upper harmonics per note. This pure-tone quality is the Hermit Thrush's signature and the reason human listeners consistently describe the song as "the most beautiful in North America."
- **The Thrush Inversion:** Varied Thrush (degree 2) = complex timbre (AM-modulated buzz), simple melody (one sustained pitch per phrase). Hermit Thrush (degree 7) = simple timbre (pure, flute-like), complex melody (multiple pitches per phrase, harmonic-series intervals). Timbre and melody are inversely related across the thrush family. One bird has rich notes in simple sequences. The other has simple notes in rich sequences. Acoustic bandwidth is finite. The Turdidae family demonstrates both allocation strategies.
- **Mountain Forest Habitat:** Breeds at 2,000-7,000 feet in the Cascades and Olympics. Subalpine fir, mountain hemlock, Engelmann spruce. The first species defined by altitude — the vertical dimension enters the engine. Altitudinal migrant: breeds at elevation in summer, descends to lowland in winter. Same species occupies different acoustic niches by moving vertically rather than latitudinally. The acoustic environment at elevation is quieter, colder, and more reverberant than lowland forest; the Hermit Thrush's song is shaped by that environment.
- **Conservation:** Least Concern (IUCN). Population ~72 million (Partners in Flight 2020). Overall stability with slight regional variation. Climate-driven upward migration is the long-term concern — mountains are pyramids, less land at the top as vegetation zones shift upward. The species' future depends on how quickly subalpine forest contracts and whether individual populations can track upward fast enough.
- **Arch-Form Phrase Architecture:** Each phrase is a miniature musical arch — ascent, peak, descent — a structural template that appears in many human musical traditions (the medieval *Dies Irae*, the blues riff, the pop-song chorus hook). The Hermit Thrush sings arches in series, each starting at a different pitch, creating a spiraling architecture across successive phrases. The result is a performance that is simultaneously local (each phrase complete in itself) and global (the sequence traces a larger shape across pitch space).
- **Cultural and Literary Resonance:** Walt Whitman chose the Hermit Thrush as the symbolic bird in *When Lilacs Last in the Dooryard Bloom'd*, his elegy for Abraham Lincoln. T.S. Eliot referenced the Hermit Thrush in *The Waste Land*. The species carries literary weight that exceeds its visual prominence — it is heard more often than seen, and the voice alone has been enough to anchor it in the American poetic canon.
- **Identification:** Distinguished from Swainson's Thrush (degree 18, to come) and Veery (degree 19, to come) by the rufous tail contrast and the characteristic phrase structure. Hermit Thrush phrases begin with a clear held note; Swainson's phrases spiral upward; Veery phrases spiral downward. The three North American *Catharus* species occupy three pitch-contour niches in the same forests.

## The Degree 7° Parallel

The resonance between Julian Priester and the Hermit Thrush emerged on four independent axes:

1. **Spiraling melodic architecture** — Priester's trombone lines orbit through pitch space rather than progressing linearly; the Hermit Thrush's successive phrases start at different pitches, creating a melodic spiral through the harmonic series.
2. **Continuous pitch as expressive medium** — the trombone slide produces any frequency within its range; the Hermit Thrush navigates the overtone series with continuous pitch selection, choosing which harmonics to voice and in what order.
3. **Bridge between traditions** — Priester bridges Sun Ra's cosmic Afrofuturism and Hancock's electronic methodology; the Hermit Thrush bridges the Turdidae family's two strategies (AM drone at degree 2, FM melody at degree 7).
4. **The shift from analogy to mathematics** — Priester's slide gives direct physical access to the microtonal continuum; the Hermit Thrush's song IS the harmonic series, measured and published in PNAS. Degree 7 is where the engine moves from "music resembles biology" to "music IS biology."

- **The Doolittle and Brumm citation** anchored the entire Part B research in peer-reviewed science. The shift from "music resembles biology" to "music IS biology" is the strongest theoretical advance in the circle so far. Having a PNAS paper as the foundation makes the claim testable and falsifiable.
- **The Sun Ra to Hancock bridge** produced the most architecturally complex career analysis in the S36 engine — three decades spanning the most radical (Arkestra) and the most electronic (Mwandishi) traditions in jazz, connected through one trombonist's career. The bridge concept is structurally productive.
- **Geographic rotation at degree 7** relieved the Central District concentration without abandoning it. Seven consecutive CD degrees documented the cluster exhaustively; now the engine has earned the right to rotate.
- **The thrush family inversion** (AM drone vs. FM melody, timbre vs. melodic complexity) produced a clean theoretical framework that applies beyond these two species to any system where bandwidth is finite and must be allocated between competing signal dimensions.
- **Elevation as habitat variable** opened the vertical axis — the engine's first acknowledgment that the PNW landscape rises, and that the soundscape changes as you climb.
- **The DuSable/Garfield parallel** connected the Chicago and Seattle jazz pipelines at the high-school level. Both institutions produced generational cohorts under a single band director's pedagogy. The parallel will recur whenever future degrees introduce DuSable or Garfield alumni.
- **The Pedagogue-Draw compound pattern** extended the artist-city taxonomy. Priester is both Drawn and Teacher, oriented toward expansion. The compound structure opens space for future hybrids.

### What Could Be Better

- **Mwandishi recording analysis** could include spectrographic data showing the trombone's electronic processing. Future releases involving electronic instrumentation should consider embedding acoustic analysis.
- **Mountain habitat quantification** — specific elevation bands, temperature profiles, and snowpack data would strengthen the habitat analysis.
- **The Priester-Frisell-Horvitz constellation** deserves a network analysis document showing the overlapping zones of experimentation. The research notes the constellation but does not map its internal structure.
- **Doolittle and Brumm statistical methodology** could be unpacked in more detail — the specific statistical tests (Kolmogorov-Smirnov against uniform distribution, chi-square against chromatic-scale nulls) would let future readers replicate the analysis with their own recordings.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Peer-reviewed science transforms the engine's claims.** The Doolittle and Brumm paper (PNAS 2014) moves the Part B analysis from analogy to evidence. The Hermit Thrush does not *resemble* a musician — it IS a musician operating on the same mathematical substrate. Future releases should prioritize species with published bioacoustic research, as the scientific literature provides a foundation that no amount of observational description can match.
2. **Career bridges reveal more than career snapshots.** Priester's value to the engine is not any single period (Arkestra, Roach, Ellington, Mwandishi, Cornish) but the connections between them. The bridge between Sun Ra and Hancock is the connective tissue of the entire post-bop experimental tradition. Degree 7 proved that artists whose careers span multiple traditions produce the richest analytical yield.
3. **The discrete-continuous spectrum is a universal principle.** Trumpet valves vs. trombone slide. Fretted guitar vs. fretless bass. Piano vs. human voice. Digital vs. analog. The spectrum applies to every instrument comparison in the engine and connects directly to signal processing theory. This principle will recur.
4. **Geographic rotation requires earning.** Seven consecutive CD degrees established the center of mass before the engine could orbit outward. Premature rotation would have fragmented the Central District documentation. Lesson: build the cluster first, then rotate.
5. **Compound artist-city patterns expand the taxonomy.** The Pedagogue-Draw is the first pattern that combines two prior patterns. Future compound patterns (e.g., Exile-Teacher, Prodigal-Builder) are now structurally possible. The taxonomy is closed under composition.
6. **Altitude is a first-class habitat variable.** Latitude and longitude have been implicit since degree 0. Elevation joined the model at degree 7. Future species in subalpine, alpine, or marine-depth niches can now inherit a vocabulary of vertical habitat. The engine's geographic coordinate system became three-dimensional.
7. **The thrush family is a trade-off laboratory.** Two Turdidae members, two inverse strategies. Future family groupings should be read through the same lens: where does the complexity go? Timbre or melody? Pitch or rhythm? Every family allocates limited acoustic bandwidth differently, and the allocation choices are the finding.
8. **Literary resonance is evidence of acoustic significance.** Whitman and Eliot chose the Hermit Thrush not arbitrarily but because its voice carries weight. When literary tradition has independently selected a species, the selection is data. Future degrees should survey literary and cultural citations as evidence of acoustic prominence.
9. **ECM as shared aesthetic anchor.** Priester and Frisell both recorded for ECM, an aesthetic coincidence that binds degrees 1 and 7 through label identity. Labels are not neutral distribution mechanisms — they are curatorial filters that shape both what is recorded and how it is heard. Future degrees should track label membership as a structural variable.

The Hermit Thrush carries the harmonic series into the mountain forest, where its flute-like phrases follow the same integer ratios that define the octave, the fifth, and the third in every musical tradition on Earth. Degree 7 adds the harmonic spiral to the circle — and with it, the strongest bridge between biology and music theory in the entire engine.*
