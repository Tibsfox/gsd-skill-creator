# Retrospective — v1.49.142

## What Worked

- **The Doolittle and Brumm citation** anchored the entire Part B research in peer-reviewed science. The shift from "music resembles biology" to "music IS biology" is the strongest theoretical advance in the circle so far. Having a PNAS paper as the foundation makes the claim testable and falsifiable.
- **The Sun Ra to Hancock bridge** produced the most architecturally complex career analysis in the S36 engine -- three decades spanning the most radical (Arkestra) and the most electronic (Mwandishi) traditions in jazz, connected through one trombonist's career. The bridge concept is structurally productive.
- **Geographic rotation at degree 7** relieved the Central District concentration without abandoning it. Seven consecutive CD degrees documented the cluster exhaustively; now the engine has earned the right to rotate.
- **The thrush family inversion** (AM drone vs. FM melody, timbre vs. melodic complexity) produced a clean theoretical framework that applies beyond these two species to any system where bandwidth is finite and must be allocated between competing signal dimensions.
- **Elevation as habitat variable** opened the vertical axis -- the engine's first acknowledgment that the PNW landscape rises, and that the soundscape changes as you climb.

## What Could Be Better

- **Mwandishi recording analysis** could include spectrographic data showing the trombone's electronic processing. Future releases involving electronic instrumentation should consider embedding acoustic analysis.
- **Mountain habitat quantification** -- specific elevation bands, temperature profiles, and snowpack data would strengthen the habitat analysis.
- **The Priester-Frisell-Horvitz constellation** deserves a network analysis document showing the overlapping zones of experimentation. The research notes the constellation but does not map its internal structure.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Peer-reviewed science transforms the engine's claims.** The Doolittle and Brumm paper (PNAS 2014) moves the Part B analysis from analogy to evidence. The Hermit Thrush does not *resemble* a musician -- it IS a musician operating on the same mathematical substrate. Future releases should prioritize species with published bioacoustic research, as the scientific literature provides a foundation that no amount of observational description can match.
2. **Career bridges reveal more than career snapshots.** Priester's value to the engine is not any single period (Arkestra, Roach, Ellington, Mwandishi, Cornish) but the connections between them. The bridge between Sun Ra and Hancock is the connective tissue of the entire post-bop experimental tradition. Degree 7 proved that artists whose careers span multiple traditions produce the richest analytical yield.
3. **The discrete-continuous spectrum is a universal principle.** Trumpet valves vs. trombone slide. Fretted guitar vs. fretless bass. Piano vs. human voice. Digital vs. analog. The spectrum applies to every instrument comparison in the engine and connects directly to signal processing theory. This principle will recur.
