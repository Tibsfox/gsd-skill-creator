## Summary

**Young Fresh Fellows** arrive at E=5 -- one of Seattle's most enduring and beloved indie rock bands, active since 1981 and still playing. Scott McCaughey's band emerged from the **Northgate** neighborhood, and with them, Northgate enters the engine's geographic map. The Young Fresh Fellows were the bridge between the 1980s garage rock underground and the grunge explosion: they played shows with Mudhoney before Mudhoney existed, shared stages with Nirvana before anyone cared, and kept going long after the cameras left. Their inclusion at degree 192 is a recognition of longevity, scene-building, and the unglamorous work of keeping a music community alive. On the species side, the **Gray Whale blow** registers at E=5 -- the **first Eschrichtiidae** in the engine. Eschrichtiidae is a monotypic family: the Gray Whale is its sole living member. The Gray Whale undertakes the **longest migration of any mammal on Earth** -- approximately 12,000 miles round trip between Arctic feeding grounds and Mexican breeding lagoons, passing through Puget Sound waters on the journey. The blow -- the explosive exhalation visible as a heart-shaped spout -- is the signature sound of this passage, audible from hundreds of meters away on a calm day.
