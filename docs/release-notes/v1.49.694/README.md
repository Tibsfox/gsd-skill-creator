## Summary

STS-32 Columbia launched 1990-01-09 12:35:00 UTC from Kennedy Space Center LC-39A; landed Edwards AFB Runway 22 1990-01-20 09:35:36 UTC. Mission duration 10d 21h 0m 36s = **first 10-day Shuttle mission**. **Columbia OV-102 ninth chronological flight** (STS-1 + STS-2 + STS-3 + STS-4 + STS-5 + STS-9 + STS-61-C + STS-28 + STS-32). 173 orbits at 296 x 361 km / 28.5 deg inclination; LDEF rendezvous at 340 km decayed orbit on flight day 4.

**Primary payload:** LDEF (Long Duration Exposure Facility) — 4,076 kg cylindrical aluminum structure (4.27 m diameter × 9.14 m length) housing 57 experiments + 86 trays + 1,394 individual samples. **LDEF retrieval first ever in space history** (substrate-novel; substrate-anchor RETRIEVAL-SUBSTRATE-FORM-NEW cohort opens). Deployed STS-41-C 1984-04-06 at 480 km altitude by MS Terry Hart; 1986 STS-51-L stand-down extended planned 12-month exposure into 5y 9m 3d substrate-form. Canadarm RMS grapple by MS Bonnie Dunbar at experiment-trays-support-frame on flight day 4; berthing in Columbia payload bay over approximately 6 hours.

**Secondary payload:** Syncom IV-5 (Leasat-5) deployed flight day 2 via Frisbee mechanism for US Navy Fleet Satellite Communications System (FLTSATCOM). Substrate-cumulative Syncom IV constellation: IV-1 v651 + IV-2 v660 + IV-3 v660 + LEASAT-3 v667 + IV-4 v667 + IV-5 v694.

**First Shuttle night landing** — Edwards AFB Runway 22 California predawn 01:35:36 PST 1990-01-20 = 09:35:36 UTC. Substrate-cohort-pair with v692 STS-33 first night Shuttle launch post-Challenger; NIGHT-OPERATIONS-POST-CHALLENGER-COHORT obs#2 cumulative substrate-pair opens.
