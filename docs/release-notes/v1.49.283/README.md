## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Strigidae (9th entry -- GHO 135, GHO duet 136, Barred 137, Barred caterwaul 138, Long-eared 139, Short-eared 140, Northern Saw-whet 141, Western Screech-Owl 142, Northern Pygmy-Owl 143)
- **TIES PICIDAE RECORD** -- 9 entries by a single family, matching the woodpecker count
- **Order:** Strigiformes (continuing, 9-degree owl run)
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The Northern Pygmy-Owl (*Glaucidium gnoma*) is a sparrow-sized owl (15-17 cm) and one of the smallest raptors in North America. Despite its diminutive size, it is a **fearless predator** -- regularly attacking and killing birds larger than itself, including robins, jays, and even quail. Active primarily at dawn and dusk (crepuscular), making it one of the more frequently seen owls in PNW forests. The **false eye-spots** (occipital spots) on the back of the head are black patches bordered by white, creating the illusion of a face watching from behind -- a predator deterrent. Call is a **tooting whistled series** -- evenly spaced, monotone, hollow whistles.
- **Geographic note:** Spaceman Scotty's Tacoma origin marks the first geographic expansion beyond Seattle city limits. Tacoma sits 30 miles south on the I-5 corridor -- a distinct city with its own music scene, its own identity, its own relationship to Puget Sound. The engine acknowledges the broader geography.
