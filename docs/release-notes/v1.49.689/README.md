## Summary

STS-30 Atlantis launched 1989-05-04 18:46:59 UTC from Kennedy Space Center LC-39B Florida — **4th post-Challenger US crewed spaceflight + FIRST US PLANETARY MISSION FROM SHUTTLE**. Mission duration 4 days 0 hours 56 minutes 27 seconds. Landed Edwards Air Force Base Runway 22 at 1989-05-08 19:43:26 UTC (same runway as STS-29 = EDWARDS-RUNWAY-22-USED-TWICE-IN-52-DAYS).

**52-day inter-flight gap** from STS-29 landing 1989-03-18 to STS-30 launch 1989-05-04 = third post-RTF substrate-room measurement. Lesson #10376 promoted ESTABLISHED at obs#15 with three substrate-cumulative measurements: 64d STS-26→STS-27 + 101d STS-27→STS-29 + 52d STS-29→STS-30.

**Primary payload: Magellan Venus radar mapper** — 3,449 kg launch mass + 2,414 kg dry mass; 12.6-cm wavelength Synthetic Aperture Radar; 75-100m horizontal resolution; ultimately mapped ~98% of Venus surface 1990-1994. **First US planetary mission since Pioneer Venus 1978-05 (11-year gap)**; substrate-cumulative with Soviet Venera 15/16 1983 radar mappers. Deployed via **IUS (Inertial Upper Stage) — first IUS planetary deployment** (substrate-distinct from prior IUS-TDRS deployments at STS-6 1983 + STS-26 v684 + STS-29 v688). **FIRST-US-PLANETARY-MISSION-FROM-SHUTTLE + MAGELLAN-VENUS-RADAR-MAPPER-DEPLOYMENT + IUS-PLANETARY-DEPLOYMENT-FIRST-OPERATIONAL-USE** substrate-forms NEW LOCKED.
