## Jack Endino -- producer of record


- Jack Endino's production work is among the most consequential in the history of American rock music; he recorded Nirvana's *Bleach* at Reciprocal Recording in Seattle for $606 over approximately 30 hours in December 1988; he recorded Mudhoney's *Superfuzz Bigmuff* (1988), Soundgarden's *Screaming Life* (1987), Green River's *Dry as a Bone* (1987), and dozens of other records that defined Sub Pop's early catalog; his production aesthetic -- raw, guitar-forward, minimal processing, maximum room sound -- became the aesthetic of the Seattle scene and influenced the production of alternative rock globally; he is sometimes called "the Godfather of Grunge," a label he has consistently resisted; the engine notes the label but endorses his own preference: he is a producer, a guitarist, a participant in a scene, and the man who recorded *Bleach*; that is sufficient

## Matt Cameron at Skin Yard


- Cameron played drums in Skin Yard from 1985 to 1986 before joining Soundgarden; his work in Skin Yard is the earliest entry in a drumming career that would span four decades and two of the largest bands of the 1990s; the Seattle 360 engine first encounters him here, at the beginning

## $606 and thirty hours


- the production budget and session length for Nirvana's *Bleach* are among the most cited statistics in rock history; they are cited here not as trivia but as a precise document of what was possible in Seattle in 1988; Reciprocal Recording was Jack Endino's studio, built on the model of doing excellent work at minimal cost, and *Bleach* is its most famous artifact; the engine documents this because Skin Yard is where Endino was developing the recording approach that made *Bleach* possible
