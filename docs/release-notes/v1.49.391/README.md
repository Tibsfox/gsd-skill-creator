**Wind Through Old-Growth Douglas Fir — The Second Wind Entry and the Structural Acoustic Contrast.** The SPS engine documented its first wind entry at degree 249 with **Wind Through Sitka Spruce** (*Picea sitchensis*), a pure aeolian organ moan produced by stiff circular needles approximately 1 mm in diameter acting as Karman-vortex generators in coastal storm wind. Degree 250 documents the second wind entry with **Wind Through Old-Growth Douglas Fir** (*Pseudotsuga menziesii*), and the contrast is deliberate rather than incidental. The two entries document fundamentally different acoustic character produced by fundamentally different plant morphology, branch architecture, canopy geometry, and geographic context. The leaf structure differs: Sitka spruce has true needles (sharp-pointed, stiff, individually very small, circular in cross-section), while Douglas fir has flat scale-like leaves approximately 2-3 cm long with flat cross-section and a groove along the upper surface. The documentation carries conservation weight: recording and characterizing the sound while it exists in recognizable form is part of what acoustic ecology does, and the engine's role in this register is to document the keynote sound as it persists rather than after it is lost.

- **The Central District continuation register documented the most structurally important element of the entry.** The engine's placement of Ayron Jones at degree 250 five engine degrees after Jimi Hendrix at 245-246 makes the Central District neighborhood continuity explicit. ### What Could Be Better

- **The Central District demographic transformation since Hendrix's era was acknowledged but not fully characterized.** The documentation notes that the Central District has undergone gentrification and community displacement since Hendrix grew up there in the 1950s, but does not fully characterize the specific demographic and economic changes — the percentage of African American residents in the Central District across the decades, the specific housing-cost and displacement patterns, the neighborhood-institution transformations (schools, churches, community organizations). Future Central District documentation should characterize the specific demographic trajectory so that the community-continuity register carries explicit documentation of what has persisted and what has changed.
- **The "Mercy" single and *Child of the State* album received less specific song-level analysis than their engine-position significance warrants.** The documentation registers "Mercy" as the national radio breakthrough but does not analyze the song's specific musical characteristics at the resolution the Hendrix entries at 245-246 received. Future artist documentation at engine-position-significant releases should provide song-level musical analysis (key, tempo, structure, notable passages, production details) alongside biographical and contextual documentation so that the musical specifics register as first-class documentation rather than as implied from contextual framing.
- **The acoustic contrast between Sitka spruce and Douglas fir wind would benefit from spectrogram comparison or frequency-range specification.** The degree 250 documentation characterizes the multi-harmonic resonance in qualitative terms and with specific mechanism descriptions but does not provide numerical frequency ranges for the major spectral components. Future wind-entry documentation should include specific frequency-range specifications (e.g., branch-sweep component 20-80 Hz, scale-leaf aeolian component 1000-4000 Hz) so that the acoustic documentation carries measurable specifics at the same resolution as the degree 249 aeolian-tone calculations.
- **The mycorrhizal network and mother-tree concepts were introduced but not connected to acoustic production.** The documentation introduces the mycorrhizal network and mother-tree concepts as structural features of old-growth Douglas fir ecosystems but does not connect these features to acoustic production directly. Future old-growth-forest documentation should explore whether underground mycorrhizal network structure affects above-ground acoustic environment (through tree-health effects on canopy density, through stand-scale carbon allocation affecting growth patterns) so that the ecosystem-complexity register integrates with the acoustic register rather than sitting alongside it.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Multi-generational community continuity can be documented through adjacent or near-adjacent engine positions rather than requiring long-gap registers.** The Central District entries at degrees 245 (Hendrix), 246 (Hendrix Early), and 250 (Ayron Jones) compress five decades of community continuity into five engine degrees. Future engine documentation should surface multi-generational community continuity through compact-sequence positioning when the community provides the constituting context, because compact sequencing carries interpretive weight that distant-entry cross-referencing does not replicate.
2. **Adjacent engine-degree pairs can document institutional diversity within a scene by placing contrasting artists on contrasting institutional infrastructure.** The Black Tones (249, Sub Pop independent) and Ayron Jones (250, Hollywood Records major) in adjacent degrees document the full institutional range of the current Seattle blues rock scene. Future scene-documentation should surface institutional diversity through adjacent-pair positioning when the scene spans significant institutional-infrastructure range, because adjacent sequencing forces the comparison in a way that single-artist documentation cannot.
3. **Wind-entry contrasts should be grounded in specific morphological measurements rather than generic descriptions.** The 249 (Sitka spruce) and 250 (Douglas fir) wind entries document their acoustic differences through specific morphological grounding — leaf structure (circular needle vs. flat scale leaf), leaf arrangement (spiral vs. pectinate), branch architecture (stiff vs. sweeping), canopy height (50-75 m vs. 70-90 m). Future wind-entry documentation should anchor acoustic contrasts in specific morphological measurements, because morphological grounding enables acoustic distinctions to register as physically mechanistic rather than impressionistic.
4. **Conservation context is constituting element of contemporary ecological-acoustic documentation rather than appendix.** The 85-90% old-growth Douglas fir loss register frames the degree 250 sound as remnant rather than baseline. Future SPS documentation of range-restricted or conservation-affected species should integrate conservation context as constituting element rather than adding it as appendix, because the loss history is part of what makes the current-moment sound interpretable.
5. **Acoustic-ecology frameworks like R. Murray Schafer's keynote-sound concept provide interpretive vocabulary for SPS documentation that standard natural-history descriptions do not.** The keynote-sound register applied to old-growth Douglas fir wind surfaces the difference between foreground sound and background sound, which in turn surfaces what has been silenced through ecosystem loss. Future SPS documentation should reference acoustic-ecology frameworks explicitly when they provide interpretive vocabulary that standard natural-history framing does not, because the discipline-specific vocabulary carries interpretive weight built up across decades of acoustic-ecology research.
6. **Long-term ecological research infrastructure (H.J. Andrews, Olympic National Park, Mount Rainier National Park) provides documented baselines that engine documentation should reference explicitly.** The degree 250 documentation names the H.J. Andrews Experimental Forest as the primary scientific baseline for old-growth Douglas fir wind sound. Future SPS documentation should reference specific long-term ecological research sites or monitoring infrastructure explicitly when they provide the scientific characterization the documentation draws on, because the specific-site reference makes the baseline concrete rather than abstract.
7. **Major-label versus independent-label contrasts carry structural weight when they frame adjacent engine positions.** The Hollywood Records (Ayron Jones, degree 250) versus Sub Pop (The Black Tones, degree 249) contrast is sharpened by the adjacent positioning. Future label-register documentation should surface major-to-independent or independent-to-major contrasts when they frame structurally significant engine positions, because the institutional contrast is part of what distinguishes artist reach and audience context.
8. **Round-number engine positions (50, 100, 150, 200, 250) carry structural weight that warrants placement of structurally weighty entries.** Degree 250 as the round-number midpoint of the extended engine run holds two structurally weighty entries — the Central District continuation representing the current Seattle blues standard-bearer, and the multi-harmonic keynote sound representing what was once the Pacific Northwest forest's omnipresent background. Future engine planning should consider round-number positions for entries with appropriate structural weight, because the numerical position carries interpretive meaning beyond mere sequence.
9. **Ecosystem-complexity features (mycorrhizal networks, nurse logs, canopy-height variation) are candidate contributors to acoustic-environment complexity and warrant investigation.** The old-growth Douglas fir documentation at degree 250 introduces ecosystem-complexity features but does not fully connect them to acoustic production. Future old-growth-forest documentation should investigate whether underground and structural ecosystem features affect above-ground acoustic environments, because the ecosystem-complexity register may integrate with the acoustic register in ways that enrich both.
10. **Time-compression (multi-decade community continuity, multi-century ecosystem development) is a first-class register for engine documentation rather than background.** The degree 250 entries compress five decades of Central District community continuity and 500-1,000+ years of old-growth ecosystem development into their present-moment documentation. Future engine documentation should surface time-compression dynamics explicitly when current-moment phenomena carry significant historical span, because the time-compression register documents continuity that present-moment description alone cannot capture.
