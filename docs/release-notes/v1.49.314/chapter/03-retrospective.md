Future hip-hop entries should document their neighborhood anchor as a first-class geographic coordinate parallel to the genre and sub-wing coordinates.
3. **Family-level pivots (Scolopacidae-to-Alcidae) mark structural transitions worth preserving explicitly.** The pivot from six consecutive sandpipers (168-173) to the first auk at degree 174 represented a fundamental ecological shift from wading/probing strategies to diving/wing-propelled strategies; the pivot framing entered the engine's vocabulary as a first-class structural-transition register. Future multi-family runs should document the transition coordinates explicitly rather than leaving the family change implicit.
4. **Convergent-evolution framing preserves biological context for behavioral-first events.** The wing-propelled underwater flight entry at degree 174 was preserved alongside its convergent-evolution parallel to Southern Hemisphere penguin wing-propulsion; the evolutionary-convergence framing provides biological context that distinguishes independently-evolved shared-ecology from shared-ancestry shared-ecology, and future behavioral-first events should receive the same convergence-or-homology framing where appropriate.
5. **The community-rooted conversational MC register establishes an accessibility pole in the Seattle hip-hop spectrum.** Grynch's accessibility-pole anchor at degree 174 complements the experimental pole (Butler), the lyricist pole (Gifted Gab, Nacho Picasso), and the producer-paradigm trio (Sabzi, Sango, Ryan Lewis); the multi-pole framing within the Seattle hip-hop spectrum provides a first-class framework for documenting genre-internal aesthetic diversity.
