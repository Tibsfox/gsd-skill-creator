1. **The most connected projects are the ones closest to home.**
   WYR's 24 cross-references happened naturally — the connections didn't need to be forced because Weyerhaeuser's timberlands ARE the PNW bioregion. The ecology cluster projects (COL, CAS, ECO) map species; WYR maps the industry that shaped those species' habitat. Same place, different layer. This is the Research series working as designed.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #487_

2. **Corporate history and environmental critique can coexist in one project.**
   WYR documents Weyerhaeuser's founding, growth, and innovations (Module 01, 02, 04, 06) alongside the destruction those activities caused (Module 03, 05, 07). Neither is suppressed. The "working forest" concept (Module 04) is presented as both a genuine improvement and an incomplete answer. Research that's honest about complexity is more useful than research that picks a side.
