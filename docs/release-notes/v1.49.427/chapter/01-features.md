## Alternative Tentacles and Northwest infrastructure


- Jello Biafra's Alternative Tentacles label, founded in San Francisco in 1979, modeled the kind of artist-controlled independent label that Northwest bands including C/Z Records and Empty Records built in the years that followed; the Dead Kennedys' label choice was as influential as their music in establishing that hardcore bands could own their output and distribute it independently at national scale; Northwest labels built on this model

## Political hardcore


- the Dead Kennedys combined extreme musical velocity with political content of unusual specificity -- critiques of California politics, of Reagan-era America, of suburban conformity -- and this combination of extreme music with explicit politics established a mode that Pacific Northwest hardcore bands inherited and adapted; the influence is not just sonic but rhetorical: the idea that hardcore could be both the loudest thing in the room and the most articulate thing in the room
