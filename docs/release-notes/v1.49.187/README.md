## Part B: Brown Creeper -- The Bark-Spiral

Full deep research covering the Brown Creeper as the engine's twentieth family, the E=1 return, the bark-spiral acoustic element, old-growth indicator ecology, and the complementary bark dyad:

- **Certhia americana -- "The Aristotle-bird of America."** Bonaparte 1838. Monotypic in North America. Nine species worldwide all in genus Certhia. *Certhia* from Ancient Greek *kerthios*, a small insectivorous tree-dwelling bird mentioned by Aristotle. *Americana* from Latin, "of America." Charles Lucien Bonaparte described the species in 1838, separating it from the Eurasian Treecreeper (*C. familiaris*). The simplest, most geographically direct species epithet in the engine. Certhiidae is the 20th family, entering as a singleton that will never gain a second member. The first genuinely new family since degree 31 (Bombycillidae) -- 20 degrees between new-family events. One genus, one species, one family, one degree. The engine adds families at a decreasing rate as the most common families fill first.
- **E=1 Returns After 38 Degrees of Absence -- Longest Drought of Any Energy Level.** Lower registers break through 50%. The invisible choir gains a fourth member. E=1 last seen at degree 12 (Pine Siskin). Degrees 13-50 had no E=1 entries. Thirty-eight consecutive degrees -- the longest absence of any energy level in the engine. E=1 rises from 3 to 4 entries (7.7%). E=1 + E=2 + E=3 = 28 degrees (53.8%) versus E=4 + E=5 = 24 degrees (46.2%). The quiet side holds a majority for the first time. The four E=1 species: Golden-crowned Kinglet (8), Bushtit (10), Pine Siskin (12), Brown Creeper (51). All small birds (5-14 g). All high-frequency. All easily overlooked. The invisible choir.
- **The Bark-Spiral -- Fifty-Second Acoustic Element (Species-Side).** First element defined by cryptic frequency. Acoustic inverse of physical spiral. Unit circle helix connection. A high, thin, descending series of sibilant notes that spirals through frequency space. Four to nine wispy high-frequency notes (3.0-8.5 kHz) descending overall. Named "the bark-spiral" for three resonances: (1) the foraging spiral -- the only North American bird that systematically spirals up tree trunks, the song's descending contour is the acoustic inverse; (2) the unit circle connection -- the bird traces a helix around cylindrical trunks, the first literal physical spiral in the engine; (3) the cryptic principle -- high, thin, easily missed, the acoustic expression of crypsis. Differs from the memory (degree 50) because the memory is defined by precision and the bark-spiral is defined by fragility. Differs from the threshold (degree 8) because the threshold pushes upward toward the edge of audibility while the bark-spiral retreats from the edge, spiraling down.
- **Old-Growth Indicator Species -- Twice as Abundant in Old-Growth.** 94% of nests in dead trees. Salvage logging paradox. Partners in Flight focal species. The Brown Creeper's presence and density correlate strongly with forest maturity and structural complexity. Three specific requirements: large trees (>50 cm DBH) with deeply furrowed bark for foraging, standing dead trees (snags) with loose bark for nesting, and forest interior conditions (avoids edges, requires continuous canopy). Hammock-shaped nest built behind a loose bark flap -- invisible from outside. Cup up to 6.4 cm deep and 15 cm across. Materials: spider egg cases, wood fibers, cocoon silk, bark strips, feathers, moss, lichens. The defining architectural innovation of the Certhiidae. Salvage logging removes the dead trees required for nesting. The trees that appear "wasted" to the timber economy are critical nesting infrastructure.
- **The Complementary Bark Dyad -- Brown Creeper Ascending (Degree 51) and Red-Breasted Nuthatch Descending (Degree 5).** Opposite in every behavioral dimension. 46 degrees apart. One goes up, the other comes down. Together they cover every surface of the bark. Bill: decurved probing versus straight chisel. Plumage: cryptic bark-pattern versus bold contrasting. Voice: high thin *see* at E=1 versus nasal *yank-yank* at E=2. Detection: invisible versus conspicuous. Social: solitary versus bold feeder visitor. The sharpest ecological complementarity documented in the engine. Both species forage on the same trees simultaneously in PNW forests. Contrary motion in counterpoint: two voices moving in opposite directions on the same staff.
- **Four-Level Crypsis -- Visual, Behavioral, Acoustic, Ecological.** The most comprehensively hidden species in the engine. (1) Visual: mottled brown-and-white upperparts match tree bark precisely. Presses flat and freezes when alarmed -- becomes invisible. (2) Behavioral: moves slowly and deliberately. Stays on far side of trunk from threats. No rapid jerky movements. (3) Acoustic: song and call at 6-8.5 kHz near the upper threshold of human hearing. Many people cannot detect them. Low survey detection rate. (4) Ecological: avoids edges, requires continuous canopy, forest interior specialist. No other species in the engine combines visual, behavioral, acoustic, and ecological invisibility to this degree. The bark-spiral is the first acoustic element where the defining quality is being hard to detect.
- **Six Phylogeographic Clades -- Most Complex Population Structure in the Engine.** Cryptic species debate. 25 note types on the West Coast. Virtually unstudied song variation. Manthey et al. (2011, *Molecular Phylogenetics and Evolution*) identified six well-supported geographically-structured clades. Northern-southern basal divergence likely represents cryptic species. Sympatry between clades in western North America. Up to 13 recognized subspecies. PNW subspecies: *C. a. occidentalis* (western Washington), *C. a. montana* (eastern Washington). Baptista & Johnson (1982) identified 25 different note types across West Coast populations. Eastern calls "relaxed, liquid"; western calls "buzzier." Sibley (2011): "Regional differences in song may offer the strongest support for splitting Brown Creeper into multiple species, but remain virtually unstudied." ## Lessons Learned

- **Record-extension is a second mode of milestone significance.** Degree 50 broke a record (six consecutive folk-adjacent, surpassing the five-degree quintets). Degree 51 extends the record to seven -- a different kind of milestone, neither breaking nor completing but amplifying the previous breakthrough. Future milestones should watch for three modes: record-breaking (exceeding a prior maximum), completing (reaching a pattern's final state), and extending (adding to a record already established). Each mode has distinct rhetorical requirements.
- **Genealogies compound across degrees.** The Carissa's Wierd genealogy was dormant at degree 48 (Sera Cahoone) and only became a structural feature at degree 51 (Mat Brooke). Three degrees apart, both Sub Pop, both folk-adjacent. The two-member genealogy is the deepest single-band diaspora the engine has documented. Future degrees should actively mine biographical data for band genealogies that span multiple engine entries. The Ben Bridwell / Band of Horses bridge is the next candidate.
- **Monotypic family entries are structurally significant despite their smallness.** The Brown Creeper is the only Certhiidae the engine will ever document -- one family, one species, one degree for the full 360. This is the opposite of the Parulidae (10 members) or Passerellidae (6 members) situation. Monotypic entries mark pattern-boundaries: the engine adds a family but that family will never grow. Future degrees should flag monotypic versus high-diversity family entries differently, because their cumulative weight is structurally different.
- **Literal mathematical embodiment is rare and worth naming explicitly.** The Brown Creeper is the first species in the engine whose physical behavior IS the unit circle (helical foraging = 3D unit circle). Most species relate to the engine's geometry metaphorically; the Brown Creeper does so literally. Future degrees should actively search for literal-embodiment instances and name them explicitly. Migratory paths, territorial rotation, circular flight, hive-waggle dances are all candidates.
- **Longest-drought returns are milestone-worthy in their own right.** E=1 returned after 38 consecutive degrees of absence -- the longest drought of any energy level in the engine. The return itself (not just the species that brought it back) is a structural event. Future degrees should flag drought-returns for any dimension: energy level, family, order, genre, gender, sound-production mechanism, geographic origin. A 30-plus-degree return is a first-order structural moment.
- **Complementary pairs can span the engine's full temporal extent.** Red-breasted Nuthatch (degree 5) and Brown Creeper (degree 51) -- 46 degrees apart -- form the tightest ecological complementary pair in the engine. The pair was not plannable in advance: the Red-breasted Nuthatch entered the engine at degree 5, and only at degree 51 did its complement arrive. Future complementary-pair events should watch for wide arcs. The engine's full temporal extent is in play.
- **Cryptic frequency as an acoustic category is new.** Previous acoustic elements named qualities of sound (intensity, pitch contour, rhythm, duration). The bark-spiral is the first element named by the listener's difficulty in detecting it rather than by the sound's properties per se. Future acoustic elements should consider listener-side categories alongside sound-side categories: detection threshold, attention requirements, habitat-matching, presbycusis-filtering. The listener is part of the acoustic system.
- **Seventh consecutive in a family run is a warning signal as well as a milestone.** The folk-adjacent heptad at degree 51 is a record, but it is also a potential structural liability. Seven consecutive degrees in one genre family risks monotony. Future streaks should actively plan for forced genre shifts at or before the seven-degree mark. The engine's CSV can be structurally adjusted if necessary. Record-extension is not an unqualified good.
- **Paired NASA releases at Pass 2 depth add genuine architectural value.** The Surveyor 3 pass-2 refinement document at `www/tibsfox/com/Research/S36/research/releases/051-grand-archives/pass2-refinement.md` and its species-side counterpart added the "fix after failure" theme that unified degree 51's artist and species sides under the broader NASA/Seattle 360 structural arc. Future paired releases should budget time for pass-2 refinement at the degree level, not just the pack level.
- **Redundancy-through-distribution is an engine-wide architectural principle.** Grand Archives' four-voice harmony degrades gracefully (three voices form a triad, two form an interval). The Brown Creeper's forest-wide nesting distribution (94% in dead trees, spread across snags throughout the old-growth landscape) degrades gracefully. The engine's 360-degree structure degrades gracefully (any single degree can be rewritten without collapsing the whole). Future degrees should look for redundancy-through-distribution as a recurring structural signature. It is the architecture of resilience on both sides and at engine level.
