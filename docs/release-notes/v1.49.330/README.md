**Release Date:** 2026-04-02

## Summary

**Mudhoney** arrives at E=7 and the **hip-hop era ENDS**. After 14 consecutive degrees of hip-hop and hip-hop-adjacent artists (degrees 175-188), the engine detonates the streak with the band that lit the fuse on grunge -- the genre that would define Seattle's global identity. "Touch Me I'm Sick" (1988) predates Nirvana's *Bleach* by a year, predates Pearl Jam by three years, and is arguably the single most important song in establishing the Sub Pop sound. Mudhoney never broke through commercially the way their peers did, which makes them the purest distillation of grunge: loud, messy, confrontational, and completely indifferent to mainstream acceptance. The transition from DoNormaal's E=8 Hip-Hop/Punk/Noise to Mudhoney's E=7 Grunge is the most significant genre boundary crossing since the engine entered Q3. On the species side, **Risso's Dolphin** clicks at E=4 -- the **second Delphinidae** in the engine and one of the most visually distinctive cetaceans in the world. Risso's Dolphins are born dark gray and accumulate white scars from squid beaks and teeth of other dolphins over their lifetime, so that old individuals appear almost entirely white -- a living record of every encounter written on the body.
