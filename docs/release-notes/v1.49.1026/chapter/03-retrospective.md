- **A held, still reference reads the faintest motion.** Gravity Probe B held its gyroscope spin axes fixed in inertial space and its telescope locked on a guide star, and against that steady reference read a spacetime drift of a few thousandths of an arcsecond per year — the same way a wind-hovering kestrel holds its head perfectly still to read the faintest movement of prey below.

## Lessons Learned

1. **An axis caps and the catalog rotates to a new way of seeing.** Because NASA's dedicated cosmic-microwave-background observatories after WMAP are limited, the cosmology axis caps at obs#2 and the forward continuation rotates to the fundamental-physics axis, showing that the catalog rotates to an under-served axis at obs#1 rather than forcing a thin third observation on a capped one.
2. **Testing the laws of the universe is a substrate-form distinct from observing its light.** By measuring the geodetic effect and frame-dragging directly, Gravity Probe B turns the catalog's gaze from observing the structure and light of the cosmos to testing the predictions of general relativity, showing that a measurement of the laws of physics opens a genuinely new substrate-axis.
3. **A geometric prediction becomes a measured angle.** By reading the geodetic drift at about 6,602 milliarcseconds per year, Gravity Probe B confirmed the curvature of local spacetime by Earth's mass to better than one percent, showing that Einstein's geometry of gravity could be turned into a direct laboratory measurement in orbit.
4. **A rotating mass twists the spacetime around it.** By reading the frame-dragging drift at about 37 milliarcseconds per year, consistent with the Lense-Thirring prediction, Gravity Probe B measured the faint sideways twist that the Earth's rotation gives to the local geometry, showing that frame-dragging is a measurable effect and not only a theoretical one.
5. **The most perfect spheres ever made carry the signal.** By machining four fused-quartz rotors round to within about forty atomic layers and coating them with niobium, the experiment built gyroscopes whose spin axes held steady enough to read a drift of a few thousandths of an arcsecond per year, showing that the precision of the instrument set the precision of the result.
6. **Flying drag-free gives a clean inertial reference.** By flying the spacecraft around a free-floating proof-mass gyroscope so that only gravity acted on the experiment, Gravity Probe B removed atmospheric drag and the pressure of sunlight, showing that a clean free-fall reference is what made the faint relativistic drift readable.
7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across an axis rotation.** Because Gravity Probe B reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.216 WMAP template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content from the precision-cosmology observatory to the general-relativity-test experiment while keeping the warm palette, making the axis rotation a clean intra-template rewrite.
