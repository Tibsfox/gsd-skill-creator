2. **Testing the laws of the universe is a substrate-form distinct from observing its light.**

3. **A geometric prediction becomes a measured angle.**
   By reading the geodetic drift at about 6,602 milliarcseconds per year, Gravity Probe B confirmed the curvature of local spacetime by Earth's mass to better than one percent, showing that Einstein's geometry of gravity could be turned into a direct laboratory measurement in orbit.

4. **A rotating mass twists the spacetime around it.**
   By reading the frame-dragging drift at about 37 milliarcseconds per year, consistent with the Lense-Thirring prediction, Gravity Probe B measured the faint sideways twist that the Earth's rotation gives to the local geometry, showing that frame-dragging is a measurable effect and not only a theoretical one.

5. **The most perfect spheres ever made carry the signal.**
   By machining four fused-quartz rotors round to within about forty atomic layers and coating them with niobium, the experiment built gyroscopes whose spin axes held steady enough to read a drift of a few thousandths of an arcsecond per year, showing that the precision of the instrument set the precision of the result.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11963_

6. **Flying drag-free gives a clean inertial reference.**
   By flying the spacecraft around a free-floating proof-mass gyroscope so that only gravity acted on the experiment, Gravity Probe B removed atmospheric drag and the pressure of sunlight, showing that a clean free-fall reference is what made the faint relativistic drift readable.
