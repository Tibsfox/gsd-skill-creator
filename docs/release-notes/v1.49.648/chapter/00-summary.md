## Top 5 structural firsts

1. **First untethered human spaceflight — Bruce McCandless II 1984-02-07 EVA-1 at 98 m (320 ft) maximum standoff from Challenger.** 5h 55m EVA via Manned Maneuvering Unit; nitrogen-cold-gas thruster backpack at 148 kg + 24 nozzle thrusters + six-degree-of-freedom control via dual hand controllers; McCandless's peak-standoff comment: "That may have been one small step for Neil, but it's a heck of a big leap for me." First human + MMU autonomous orbital free-flyer; substrate-anchor for entire MMU operational cohort STS-41-B + STS-41-C + STS-51-A.

2. **First and second consecutive STS satellite-deploy failures — WESTAR-VI Western Union 1984-02-03 ~8h MET + PALAPA-B2 Perumtel Indonesia 1984-02-06 Flight Day 4.** Both Hughes HS-376 spin-stabilized geosynchronous communications satellites with 24 C-band transponders; both lifted by Star 48B Payload Assist Module D upper stages; both PAM-D motors ignited as commanded ~45 min post-deploy then extinguished prematurely after <10 seconds of burn; root cause traced to **interply density variation in carbonized cloth of carbon-carbon nozzle exit cone** per post-flight Failure Investigation Committee final report September 1984; both satellites stranded in ~350 × 1,000 km useless orbits instead of intended geosynchronous transfer. Both retrieved on STS-51-A 1984-11-16 by Joseph Allen + Dale Gardner aboard Discovery using MMU + Stinger capture tool — substrate-anticipation forward-shadow at 9-month scale to first-ever in-orbit satellite retrieval.

3. **First new STS numbering scheme — STS-41-B = FY84 + 1=KSC-launch-site + B-of-FY second mission.** Supersedes simple STS-N enumeration after STS-9; new scheme classifies missions by fiscal-year + launch-site + alphabetical-FY-position; numbering scheme persists through STS-51-L Challenger 1986-01-28 disaster aftermath when NASA reverts to simple STS-N. Administrative-mechanism-design at the NASA-customer-allocation interface; the STS-41-B label is itself a coordinate-system label assigned by an internal NASA scheduling mechanism that allocates manifest slots among competing commercial + scientific + DoD customers.

4. **First KSC concrete-runway landing for an orbiter returning from orbit — 1984-02-11 12:15:55 UTC at Shuttle Landing Facility Runway 15.** First landing of any spacecraft at its launch site in human-spaceflight history; 10,815 ft rollout in 67 seconds; closes the runway-redundancy substrate arc from v645 Edwards-as-weather-contingency-divert + v646 Edwards-as-night-landing-capable + v647 Edwards-Runway-17-lake-bed to v648 KSC-as-primary-landing-site at fully-operational concrete-runway resolution.
