# Lessons — v1.49.1003

11 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **One standardized observatory can serve a broad community at once.**
   Rather than building a separate one-purpose spacecraft for each investigation, OGO flew a common observatory carrying a large and varied complement of instruments, showing that a standardized platform can serve many investigators on one design.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11706_

2. **A large instrument complement gives a broad simultaneous survey.**
   By carrying fields, plasma, and energetic-particle instruments together, the eccentric observatories surveyed many parameters of the deep magnetosphere at once, showing that a broad complement turns a focused measurement into a comprehensive survey.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11707_

3. **A polar orbit and the Earth's rotation give a global ground track.**
   The low polar POGO orbits, sweeping across all longitudes as the Earth turned beneath them, mapped the geomagnetic field across the entire globe, showing that a polar orbit gives a satellite the globe-spanning coverage a global field survey needs.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11708_

4. **A satellite survey turns scattered readings into a global model.**
   The POGO magnetometer survey turned scattered ground and aircraft readings into a globe-spanning satellite map of the geomagnetic field, showing that a consistent satellite survey gives a global picture that ground stations alone cannot.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11709_

5. **A standardized bus amortizes one design across many observatories.**
   By flying six observatories on a common modular bus, OGO needed only a new complement of experiments for each, showing that a shared platform spreads the design cost across the program and lets one design carry many investigations.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11710_

6. **A dual orbit-class architecture reaches two regions from one design.**
   By placing eccentric observatories in the deep magnetosphere and polar observatories over the ionosphere, OGO surveyed two distinct regions from one standardized design, showing that two orbit classes give one program a comprehensive reach.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11711_

7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across a distinct-palette mission.**
   Because OGO reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.193 template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content to the comprehensive geophysical survey with a distinct survey-amber / eccentric-blue / polar-teal / field-violet palette, making the distinct-palette mission a clean build.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11712_

8. **The shader renders procedural structure rather than archived data.**
   The OGO geophysical observatory survey shader uses analytic geometry and procedural noise rather than loading actual OGO survey datasets from the NASA Space Physics Data Facility. A future revision could load encoded OGO survey data for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to the real data.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11713_

9. **The standardized-observatory diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the standardized bus, its complement of instruments, and the two orbit classes.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11714_

10. **The comprehensive-survey sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.**
   The sidebar lists representative program elements (OGO, FAST, AMPTE, CRRES, ISEE-1/2, TWINS) but does not enumerate the full six-observatory roster with each instrument complement; a future revision could expand the table with per-observatory dates and instrument suites.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11715_

11. **The spherical-harmonic field model is described rather than computed.**
   The spherical-harmonic expansion that turns the polar survey into a global field model is described but not run against archived data; a future ship could fit the model from real POGO-era survey datasets.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11716_
