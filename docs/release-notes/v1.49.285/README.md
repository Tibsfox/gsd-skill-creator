## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Caprimulgidae (1st entry) -- STRIGIDAE STREAK BROKEN at 10 consecutive degrees (135-144)
- **Order:** Caprimulgiformes -- nightjars, a new order entering the engine
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The Common Poorwill (*Phalaenoptilus nuttallii*) is the **only bird known to hibernate**. First documented by Edmund Jaeger in 1946 in the Chuckwalla Mountains of California, where he found a torpid poorwill wedged into a rock crevice with a body temperature of just 6C (normal ~40C). The bird returned to the same spot for multiple winters. The Hopi people knew this long before Western science -- their name for the bird, *Holchko*, means "the sleeping one." The call is a soft, repeated "poor-will" or "poor-will-ip," delivered from the ground or a low perch at dusk and through the night. Crepuscular and nocturnal, nearly invisible by day thanks to cryptic plumage that perfectly matches leaf litter and rocky substrate.
- **Sabzi network:** Sabzi (Alexei Saba Mohajerjasbi) now connects to 5 engine entries -- Blue Scholars (101), and the DJ/production web that runs through Seattle's underground hip-hop. Common Market (RA Scion + Sabzi) released *Common Market* (2005) and *Tobacco Road* (2008) on Mass Line Media, defining the conscious hip-hop lane in the Pacific Northwest.
- **Rainier Valley:** First appearance of this neighborhood, home to the Rainier Beach and Columbia City scenes. Expands the engine's Seattle geography south from the Central District and Capitol Hill clusters.
