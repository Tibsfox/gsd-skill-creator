## Summary

The ground beneath the survey is what makes the survey possible. On September 11, 1967, at forty-six minutes and forty-four seconds past midnight Universal Time, Surveyor 5 touched down on the lunar regolith inside a small fresh crater on Mare Tranquillitatis. The spacecraft slid eight tenths of a meter down the nineteen-and-a-half-degree slope and came to rest with three footpads sunk two and a half centimeters into the bearing surface. Three days earlier, eighteen hours into the trans-lunar coast, a helium leak in the vernier-propulsion pressurization system had threatened the entire mission. At Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Pasadena, the JPL Real-Time Anomaly Response Team — propulsion engineers, guidance engineers, trajectory analysts, and operations controllers working in parallel across thirty-six continuous hours — redesigned the terminal descent profile in flight. They lengthened the main retrorocket burn, shortened the vernier-only descent phase, accepted a steeper terminal trajectory, and rewrote the autonomous sequence. The same hardware that had failed at Surveyor 4 fifty-three days earlier flew at Surveyor 5 and landed. The engineering lineage of the Surveyor program, which had absorbed the catastrophic Surveyor 4 loss at v1.59, demonstrated at v1.62 that the carrier had been rebuilt. Robert J. Parks at JPL and Howard H. Haglund at the Surveyor 5 project office held both sides of the transmission. Anthony L. Turkevich at the University of Chicago Enrico Fermi Institute had been waiting for this landing for seven years, ever since he and James H. Patterson and Ernest J. Franzgrote had begun the alpha-scattering instrument design. For eighty-three cumulative hours after touchdown the spectrometer measured the regolith composition with a curium-242 alpha source and two solid-state semiconductor detectors. The result, reported in *Science* 158:635-637 on November 3, 1967, was basaltic — silicon dioxide approximately fifty-eight to sixty-three percent, aluminum oxide fourteen to fifteen percent, iron-plus-magnesium oxides twelve to fourteen percent, calcium oxide six to seven percent. It was the first in-situ chemical analysis of another world. The substrate beneath the lander was the substrate of the Moon.

In Seattle in the same year that the Sub Pop Records ecosystem stabilized into the cluster that would later carry Fleet Foxes and Father John Misty and Modest Mouse, Mat Brooke left Band of Horses after the 2006 *Everything All the Time* tour, returned to the Ballard / Fremont side of the city he had been working from since the Carissa's Wierd years at the turn of the millennium, and assembled Grand Archives. The lineup was Brooke on lead vocals and guitar and primary songwriting, Curtis Hall on drums and percussion, Jeff Montano on guitar and bass and multi-instrumental backing, Thomas Wright on keyboards and organ, and Ron Lewis on bass. Sub Pop signed the band. Phil Ek — the same producer who had defined the Pacific Northwest indie-rock recording aesthetic at Fleet Foxes and at Built to Spill and at Modest Mouse and at Band of Horses itself — produced both of the Grand Archives albums. *The Grand Archives* shipped on February 19, 2008, ten tracks across forty-five minutes, with Pitchfork at 6.9 of 10 and NPR placing the band on the 2008 "new voices" coverage. *Keep In Mind Frankenstein* shipped on September 15, 2009, eleven tracks across forty-three minutes, with Pitchfork at 6.4 of 10. Then the band stopped. The two-album catalog was complete. Brooke had built a particular emotional register — a Pacific-Northwest grief-continuation indie-folk register that worked at the specific scale of two albums, and that did not require a third album to be measured. Grand Archives dissolved in 2011. The lineage from Carissa's Wierd through Band of Horses through Grand Archives runs in parallel with the Surveyor lineage. Both threads are about a craft team that built a small number of carefully measured artifacts and then stopped at the right place. Each one mirrors the other at its own scale. The Phil Ek production credit at Grand Archives is the third Phil Ek credit in the S36 corpus after Fleet Foxes at v1.53; the production lineage stabilizes at three exemplars exactly the way the §6.6 PRINCIPLE-TRINITY alignment variant stabilizes at three exemplars at v1.62.

The Pileated returned to the v1.62 corpus as the second appearance of the species. At v1.30 the species had been documented under the snag-drum behavioral channel — the broadband impulsive fifty-Hertz-to-five-kilohertz spectrum of the territorial drum produced by the pectoralis-bill-substrate motor system. At v1.62 the species returns under the laughing-call channel, a tonal one-to-two-kilohertz vocalization with harmonics to five or six kilohertz produced by the syringeal motor system at the tracheal bifurcation. Two distinct acoustic regimes from the same species, two different motor systems, one shared territory. ### Part B: Pileated Woodpecker (laughing call) -- THE BIOACOUSTIC INVENTORY OF OLD GROWTH

- **Second appearance of the species — different acoustic regime.** *Dryocopus pileatus* appeared at v1.30 under the snag-drum behavioral channel (broadband impulsive fifty-Hz-to-five-kHz spectrum, pectoralis-bill-substrate motor system). v1.62 documents the species under the laughing-call channel (tonal one-to-two-kHz with harmonics to five-six kHz, syringeal motor system at the tracheal bifurcation). Two distinct motor systems, two distinct acoustic regimes, one shared species and one shared territory. Second multi-appearance-with-behavioral-channel-differentiation exemplar after v1.2+v1.8 Polystichum munitum sword fern.
- **The laughing call as territorial vocal channel.** Loud falling rolling *kuk-kuk-kuk-kuk-kuk*, ten to fifteen notes per bout, two to four seconds per bout, peak energy one to two kilohertz with harmonics to five or six kilohertz, audible eight hundred meters in intact old-growth. Produced year-round by both sexes; peak January through April for breeding-territory establishment. The call is the species' primary acoustic territorial signal at long range.
- **Syringeal motor system — distinct from drumming.** The Pileated syrinx sits at the tracheal bifurcation and drives the laughing call through coordinated tympaniform-membrane vibration. The drumming motor system uses pectoralis-bill-substrate energy transfer instead. The two motor systems are anatomically and developmentally independent. The same individual produces both signals on different schedules for different functional purposes — a within-individual multi-channel transmitter.
- **Keystone-cavity-excavator — first explicit declaration.** Pileated Woodpeckers excavate fresh cavities each year, with eight-by-ten-centimeter entrances opening into thirty-to-sixty-centimeter chambers, in snags greater than fifty centimeters diameter at breast height (preferred seventy-five centimeters or more). The excavated cavities are then inherited by approximately twenty-plus secondary cavity users including Vaux's Swift, Northern Saw-whet Owl, Western Screech-Owl, Northern Flying Squirrel, Wood Duck, Bufflehead, American Marten, Silver-haired Bat, and several chickadee and nuthatch species. The Pileated is the keystone cavity excavator of Pacific Northwest old-growth — first explicit declaration in the SPS corpus.
- **Bioacoustic-inventory narrative thread — first in corpus.** The Pileated laughing call is used as an old-growth-forest-intactness proxy by Cornell Macaulay Library, BirdNET, Pacific Northwest Sound Atlas, and the USFS / OSU / UW joint monitoring programs. When passive acoustic monitors detect the laughing call, the forest beneath the monitor is functionally intact at the snag-density and canopy-continuity scales required for a Pileated breeding pair. The acoustic signal indexes the habitat substrate the way the Turkevich alpha-scattering signal indexed the lunar substrate.
- **Picidae third member in the corpus.** v1.62 brings the corpus Picidae count to three: Pileated v1.30 (drum), Red-breasted Sapsucker v1.56, Pileated v1.62 (laughing call). The family-level expansion is in parallel with the §6.6 PRINCIPLE-TRINITY three-exemplar stabilization at the same degree.
- **Northwest Forest Plan 1994 substrate.** The Pileated population is stable to recovering across the Pacific Northwest under the Northwest Forest Plan 1994 Late-Successional Reserve network. The plan wove the federal old-growth holdings of Oregon, Washington, and northern California into a continuous reserve network with snag-retention requirements; Pileated breeding success tracks the snag-retention compliance closely. The species is IUCN Least Concern with Partners in Flight estimating approximately 1.6 million individuals continent-wide.
- **The laughing call as acoustic element.** The sixty-second acoustic element on the species side is the Pileated laughing-call bioacoustic-inventory channel: a falling rolling territorial vocalization that, when present in a passive acoustic recording, tells the listener the substrate beneath the microphone has not been clear-cut. The acoustic element is parallel to the Grand Archives Pacific-Northwest grief-continuation register and parallel to the Turkevich alpha-scattering basaltic regolith result; each one indexes a different substrate at a different scale, and all three together close the §6.6 PRINCIPLE-TRINITY variant at v1.62.
