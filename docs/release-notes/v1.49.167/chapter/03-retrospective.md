# Retrospective — v1.49.167

## Lessons Learned

1. **The engine needs relay runners as much as sprinters.** The 360-degree rotation documents the full ecosystem of a musical tradition -- not just the peaks but the connective tissue between them. Eager's degree proves that the post-landmark position is structurally valuable.

2. **Non-residential artist-city connections are documentable and meaningful.** The Relay pattern demonstrates that a city's musical influence propagates beyond its borders through direct musical collaboration. The tradition travels through people, not geography.

3. **The mellow undertow follows the rough brrit as recovery follows rupture.** The three-element sequence (liquid brook, rough brrit, mellow undertow) maps flow-rupture-recovery -- a dramatic arc that mirrors the artist progression from steady professional (Boogaloo Joe Jones) to genre inventor (Ray Charles) to tradition-carrier (Brenda Lee Eager).

4. **Family transitions are timbral resets.** Moving from Hirundinidae to Bombycillidae reset the entire acoustic palette -- from liquid to sibilant, from complex warble to thin whisper, from river to wire. New families bring new sounds.

5. **Frugivory changes how the engine thinks about habitat.** Resource-defined ecosystems (fruiting trees) require different documentation than structure-defined ecosystems (old-growth, farmland). The habitat is a phenological event, not a geological feature. This expands the engine's ecological vocabulary permanently.

6. **Vocal sufficiency is adaptive.** Nomadic frugivores that do not defend territory do not need territorial song. The Cedar Waxwing's simple contact call is not a limitation -- it is an optimization. The engine's acoustic theory now includes the concept of "exactly enough."

7. **The energy plateau is independent of everything else.** E=4 has survived family transitions, timbral shifts, ecosystem changes, seasonal changes, and social structure changes. It is the one parameter that does not respond to structural boundaries. Breaking it will require deliberate attention at the degree-selection level.

Degree 31 is the aftermath of a landmark and the opening of a new chapter. The mellow undertow is not less than the rough brrit. The whisper is not less than the river. Brenda Lee Eager carried the tradition that Ray Charles created. The Cedar Waxwing whispers a contact call sufficient to hold a flock of hundreds together. The relay and the whisper. The tradition propagates. The flock coheres. The engine turns. And Puget Sound -- the actual water, the salt water, the tidal inland sea for which the project is named -- has still not spoken. Not once. Not a gull. Not a loon. Not a grebe. Thirty-one degrees of forest, field, farmland, and fruiting tree. The water is waiting.

*The engine's next step: degree 32 -- Bohemian Waxwing (Bombycilla garrulus), closing the waxwing pair. Same genus, different latitude. Boreal irruptive meets temperate resident. The pair explores geographic range contrast. After the pair closes, the water must finally speak.*
