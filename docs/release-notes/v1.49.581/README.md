**FIRST CONTROLLED LIFTOFF FROM ANOTHER CELESTIAL BODY.** Surveyor 6's vernier hop on November 17 1967 at 10:32 UT (2.5-second coordinated three-vernier burn at 150 lbf total thrust, ~4 m vertical, ~2.4 m lateral west, soft reland) is the first instance of a spacecraft commanded to lift off a planetary surface other than Earth under autonomous-sequenced control. The maneuver predates Apollo 11 ascent (July 21, 1969) by approximately 20 months and established the operational confidence basis for subsequent ascent maneuvers -- Apollo Lunar Module ascent stages, Mars sample-return ascent vehicles, Chang'e lunar-sample-return ascent. The vernier hardware that did the work was not redesigned. The same MMH/MON-10 bipropellant feed, the same throttle servos, the same propellant valves that had executed terminal descent on every previous Surveyor were commanded to a new function via new commanded sequences. The structural lesson -- that re-execution of existing hardware under new command sequences can produce a first -- generalizes beyond Surveyor 6 and beyond celestial mechanics, and is the same pattern that runs through Gibbard's solo *Former Lives* (DCFC's same authorial voice in a stripped-back vehicle) and the Green Frog's invasive establishment (the same ranid biology in a translocated context).

**FIRST STEREO IMAGE PAIR OF ANY PLANETARY SURFACE.** Pre-hop and post-hop imagery from the same Sinus Medii scene at vantage points displaced approximately 2.4 meters apart constitutes the first stereo image pair of any planetary surface. The displacement was sufficient for the depth perception that Apollo lunar-site selection would later need. Eugene M. Shoemaker (USGS), leading the Surveyor Television Experiment Team, treated the stereo pair as the program-level science objective for the hop, intended to establish stereo-photogrammetry technique for use in Apollo site selection. The technique that Shoemaker's team established with the November 17 1967 stereo pair would inform the Apollo Hasselblad stereo-photogrammetry program and, in parallel decades later, the Mars Exploration Rover Mastcam, MSL Curiosity Mastcam, and Mars 2020 Perseverance Mastcam-Z stereo-imaging programs. The same 2.4 m baseline is structurally identical to the human interpupillary distance scaled to lunar-scene viewing geometry -- a lift-and-reset that was simultaneously a measurement instrument.

- **BANJO-PLUNK ADVERTISEMENT CALL -- single broadband transient at 250-1000 Hz, 100 ms, 200-500 ms inter-event:** The Green Frog's advertisement call is a single broadband transient at 250-1000 Hz fundamental energy, ~100 ms pulse duration, 200-500 ms inter-event gap during active chorus. The call resembles a banjo string plucked once and damped. Each side of the chorus features alternating individual-male calls without overlap; the temporal mosaic is structurally similar to the kettle-drum punctuation of Pileated Woodpecker drum-roll terminations covered at v1.40. The acoustic signature is encoded as the cross-track Faust demo `green-frog-call-transcription.dsp` on the MUS side, paired bidirectionally with NASA-side `green-frog-banjo-plunk.dsp`.

- **AQUACULTURE/POND-STOCKING INTRODUCTION VECTOR -- 20th-century human-mediated translocation:** The Green Frog reached Pacific Northwest waters via 20th-century aquaculture and pond-stocking translocation, primarily for sport-fishing food-supplementation. The introduction was deliberate and human-mediated, not migratory. Naturalized populations established in lowland Puget Sound by approximately the 1930s-1940s. The aquaculture/pond-stocking vector is the **FIRST introduction-vector declaration** in the SPS corpus and is the structural primitive that the §6.6 LIFT-AND-RESET variant generalizes -- a controlled displacement that lands and persists.

- **RANIDAE FAMILY FIRST APPEARANCE -- prior anuran was Hylidae (Pacific Tree Frog):** The Green Frog is the **FIRST Ranidae family entry** in the Sound of Puget Sound corpus. The prior anuran was the Pacific Tree Frog (*Pseudacris regilla*), which is Hylidae. The two anuran families structurally divide PNW frog acoustic space: Hylidae (treefrogs, smaller, higher-frequency advertisement calls, climbing-adapted toe pads) versus Ranidae (true frogs, larger, lower-frequency calls, fully-aquatic adults). Both sides of the anuran family boundary now appear in the corpus.

- **GEOGRAPHIC LIFT-AND-RESET ON BIOLOGICAL SCALE -- origin to displacement-vector to established alien-context resident:** The Green Frog is the third instantiation of the §6.6 LIFT-AND-RESET temporal arc at v1.63, operating on the biological scale: ORIGIN (eastern North America native range east of the Mississippi-drainage divide) -> CONTROLLED-DISPLACEMENT (20th-century aquaculture and pond-stocking translocation, ~3000 km west) -> RELAND-WITH-PERSISTENCE (established naturalized invasive resident in lowland Puget Sound by 1930s-1940s, reproductively self-sustaining). In parallel with Surveyor 6's mechanical lift-and-reset (~4 m) and Gibbard's cultural lift-and-reset (single record), the Green Frog's biological lift-and-reset spans continents.

- **NATIVE-RANGE NOT PNW -- first SPS species whose geographic origin is explicitly NOT Pacific Northwest:** v1.63 introduces the first SPS species whose geographic origin is explicitly NOT the Pacific Northwest. The Mississippi-drainage divide is the western edge of the native range. This is a structural inversion of the prior SPS pattern (native PNW species). The forward-operational rule: future SPS subjects with non-PNW geographic origin should be tagged as such in `degree-sync.json` and the corpus-deltas, and the LIFT-AND-RESET §6.6 variant should be considered as the candidate alignment.

- **LAKE-EDGE HABITAT -- emergent vegetation, permanent/semi-permanent water:** The Green Frog inhabits lake edges, pond margins, slow-water shallows, and permanent-to-semi-permanent freshwater wetlands with emergent vegetation. The species is more aquatic than most ranids; adults rarely move far from water. The habitat preference structurally parallels the Sinus Medii lunar mare's flat-and-stable surface (the Surveyor 6 lift-target site was selected for its mare flatness) -- both sides of the lift-and-reset pattern require a stable substrate for the reland phase.

- **WDFW NON-NATIVE INVASIVE CLASSIFICATION -- documented stressor on native amphibian community:** The Washington Department of Fish and Wildlife (WDFW) classifies *Lithobates clamitans* as a non-native species and prohibits release or transport across watersheds. Documented impacts on native Pacific Tree Frog (*Pseudacris regilla*) populations and on Northwestern Salamander (*Ambystoma gracile*) larvae through direct predation and resource competition. The invasive-resident classification is the **FIRST invasive-resident narrative thread** in the SPS corpus and opens the structural register for future invasive entries.

- **NATURALIZED-BY-1930s-1940s -- Bury & Whelan 1984, Adams et al. 2003 documented baseline:** Naturalized populations established in lowland Puget Sound by approximately the 1930s-1940s. V-3 RESOLVED via Bury & Whelan 1984 (PNW herpetofauna baseline), Leonard, Brown, Jones, McAllister & Storm 1993 (PNW Amphibian Atlas), Adams, Pearl & Bury 2003 (*Ecology Letters* invasive-impact paper), and WDFW invasive-species records.
