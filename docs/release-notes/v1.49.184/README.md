Sera Cahoone -- born August 4, 1975, in Littleton, Colorado. Daughter of Bill Cahoone, a Rocky Mountain dynamite salesman, and Judy Cahoone, a business executive. Graduated from **Columbine High School** -- the school where, on April 20, 1999, twelve students and a teacher were killed, an event Cahoone watched unfold on television from Seattle, having moved there the year before. She began drumming at age twelve to combat anxiety. First stage performance at Ziggie's Saloon in North Denver during a blues open-mic night. Moved to Seattle in 1998 when a Colorado snowboarding shop opened a Washington location. Became the drummer for **Carissa's Wierd** (1998-2003), the sadcore band that produced three major subsequent careers: Ben Bridwell formed **Band of Horses** (Sub Pop, RIAA Gold), Mat Brooke formed **Grand Archives** (Sub Pop), and Cahoone became a solo singer-songwriter (Sub Pop). Played selected drum tracks on Band of Horses' *Everything All The Time* (2006, Sub Pop) -- received a **Gold Record** in 2019, thirteen years after the sessions. The transition from drummer to singer-songwriter was gradual and anxiety-ridden: she forced herself through open mic nights, recorded a self-titled debut playing guitar and drums herself, and sent a demo to **John Richards** at KEXP, who played it on air and launched her solo career. Sub Pop signed her in 2007. *Only as the Day Is Long* (2008, Sub Pop SP0759) -- NPR Tiny Desk Concert, July 10, 2008. *Deer Creek Canyon* (2012, Sub Pop SP1005, produced by Thom Monahan at Bear Creek Studios, Woodinville) -- named for the canyon in Littleton, Colorado, where her mother still lives. *From Where I Started* (2017, Lady Muleskinner Records, co-produced with John Morgan Askew at Flora Recording, Portland). *The Flora String Sessions* (2018, reimagined songs with Alex Guy string arrangements). Produced Margo Cilker's *Valley of Heart's Delight* (2023). First musicians to perform on the **Space Needle rooftop** (July 11, 2013, Sub Pop Silver Jubilee, broadcast live on KEXP, alongside Mudhoney). The slow bloom across twenty-five years.
