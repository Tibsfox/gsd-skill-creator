3. **Juno is a NASA / JPL New Frontiers solar-powered mission.**
   The mission is managed by NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory (like Galileo and the Cassini orbiter), part of NASA's New Frontiers program with principal investigator Scott Bolton of the Southwest Research Institute, and it is powered by three large solar arrays rather than nuclear generators; the catalog states this precisely throughout.

4. **Juno launched on an Atlas V 551 (5 August 2011); it used an Earth gravity assist (9 October 2013).**
   Juno launched aboard an Atlas V 551 from Cape Canaveral and used an Earth gravity assist on 9 October 2013 over a roughly five-year cruise to Jupiter; the catalog states this precisely.

5. **The Jupiter achievement is the first POLAR orbit, not the first orbit.**
   On 4–5 July 2016 Juno became the first spacecraft to orbit Jupiter pole-to-pole; the catalog scopes this strictly to the POLAR orbit, leaving the first Jupiter ORBIT to Galileo (1995, equatorial) — Galileo was the first spacecraft to orbit Jupiter at all, while Juno's first is the polar orbit.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12492_

6. **The deep-interior mapping is the first GLOBAL remote sounding from orbit — distinct from Galileo's single-point probe.**
