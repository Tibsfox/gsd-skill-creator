**Degree 240 marks the two-thirds point of the Seattle 360 engine: 240 of 360 degrees complete, 66.7% of the circle traveled.** This is the structural milestone of deep Quadrant III -- the engine has passed the second quarter-arc boundary and now begins the descent toward the final third. Paul Revere and the Raiders arrive at E=6 to mark the moment -- a band that carries the garage rock energy of the mid-1960s Pacific Northwest into a degree that is simultaneously a completion and a beginning.

**Paul Revere and the Raiders** formed in Boise, Idaho in 1960 and moved their operations to Portland, Oregon, becoming one of the defining acts of the Pacific Northwest garage rock explosion of the early-to-mid 1960s. The band's signature was theatrical: Revolutionary War costumes, showmanship, an almost cartoonish stage presence that sat in direct contrast to their underlying musical aggression. Mark Lindsay's vocals were raw, the guitars were fuzzed and loud, and the rhythm section was relentless. "Louie Louie" -- which the Raiders recorded before The Kingsmen's famous version -- was part of their early catalog, and while The Kingsmen's version is the one that became iconic, the Raiders' version demonstrates that this band was working in the same exact register at the same moment. The Raiders' connection to Portland and the Columbia River corridor makes them a crucial node in the engine's mapping of the PNW garage rock triangle: Tacoma, Seattle, and Portland all contributed to the mid-1960s garage explosion, and the Raiders are the Portland signal. At E=6, they arrive at the two-thirds mark with a sound that opens the **Garage Rock Era** arc that will run from degree 240 through 244.

On the species side, **Bull Trout** enters the Salmonidae arc at E=2 -- **silent/water**, the first of three consecutive near-silent entries that will define degrees 240-242 as the Silent Fish Trio. *Salvelinus confluentus* is federally **Threatened** under the Endangered Species Act, the only species in the current Salmonidae arc with that designation. Bull Trout require the coldest, clearest, least-disturbed water of any salmonid in the Pacific Northwest: water temperatures below 55°F, no sediment, no barriers, no compromises. The E=2 is not a mark of irrelevance -- it is the acoustic signature of a fish that lives in places where sound does not travel far, where water is cold and clear and the landscape is intact. Bull Trout are indicators of wilderness quality. Where they persist, the watershed is still working. Their near-silence at degree 240 anchors the two-thirds mark in the most ecologically honest way the engine could manage.
