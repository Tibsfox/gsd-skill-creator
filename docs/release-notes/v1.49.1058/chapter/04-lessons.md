   The Mars Exploration Rovers share the Mars-exploration vocabulary with the Mars Pathfinder predecessor (Mars, the red planet, the surface, regolith, the atmosphere, dust, in situ, planetary exploration, past water, outflow channel) — that vocabulary is shared and correct. 2. **A sustain has no axis rotation to frame — and 1.240 was itself a sustain, so there is no rotation framing to remove.**

The X-ray-axis vocabulary does not appear.

3. **The airbag landing and the wheeled rover are heritage REUSED / scaled up from Pathfinder, claimed as neither first.**
   The MERs used Pathfinder's airbag-cushioned bouncing landing scaled up ~17×, and the rover descends from Sojourner; the catalog stamps neither "first airbag-cushioned Mars landing" nor "first rover to operate on another planet" (both Pathfinder, obs#3). The MER firsts are in-situ water mineralogy, long-distance field geology, and record endurance / distance.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12273_

4. **The water evidence is in-situ MINERALOGY, not "discovered water," and is a different observable from the predecessors'.**
   Opportunity's Mössbauer and APXS identified jarosite, sulfates, and hematite concretions — minerals that form only in liquid water — the first in-situ mineralogical evidence of an ancient aqueous environment; this is distinct from Mariner 9's ORBITAL channels (obs#2) and Pathfinder's GROUND-LEVEL flood MORPHOLOGY (obs#3), and the orbital hematite signal that selected Meridiani was from Mars Global Surveyor, not Odyssey.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12274_

5. **The APXS on the MERs is "Alpha PARTICLE," a redesigned successor to Pathfinder's "Alpha Proton.**
   Same abbreviation (APXS), flipped expansion; the proton-scattering channel was de-emphasized (Rieder et al. 2003). The catalog does not conflate the two instruments and does not re-claim Pathfinder's mobile-rover rock-geochemistry first.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12275_

6. **The records are precise, separate numbers with careful scoping.**
   The marathon distance (42.195 km, 24 March 2015) and the final lifetime odometry (~45.16 km, 2018) are different numbers, never paired; the longest-operating record is "as of its end (2019)" (Curiosity surpassed the duration in 2023), while the farthest-driven (~45.16 km) off-world record still stands; the mark Opportunity surpassed was lunar (Lunokhod 2, ~39 km), not a Mars predecessor.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12276_

7. **The Mars Exploration Rovers are a long-traverse field-geology mission, not an orbiter, not the first Mars landing, and not the first rover.**
   They drove kilometers across the surface from rock to rock; they did NOT orbit, did NOT map the planet, and they are NOT the first Mars landing (Viking), the first orbiter (Mariner 9), or the first rover (Sojourner, Mars Pathfinder); they are distinct from Mariner 4 (1.36, the first Mars flyby).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12277_

8. **NASA / U.S.-mission framing is an accuracy requirement here.**
   The Mars Exploration Rover mission was a wholly NASA / United States mission, developed and operated by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory under NASA's Mars Exploration Program, with no foreign-partner caveat; the PI was Steven Squyres (Cornell) and the theme was "Follow the Water."
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12278_
