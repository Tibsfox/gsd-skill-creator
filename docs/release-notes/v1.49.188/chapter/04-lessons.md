4. **Environmental competition is a new category of acoustic element.**
   Before degree 52, acoustic elements were named by their sonic character (whisper, roar, trill, buzz), their structural function (preamble, bookend, resolution), or their cultural/biological role (dialect, language, memory). The torrent-song introduces environmental competition as a fourth category: sound shaped not by the performer's intent but by the acoustic demands of the habitat. Future environmentally-constrained elements (urban-noise adaptation, wind-shaped song, cavern reverberation) will extend this category.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5613_

5. **The champion pattern is a first-class relationship category, not a sub-type of influence.**
   Covering (tribute) reproduces a predecessor's work in the performer's own voice. Citing (influence) acknowledges a predecessor in the performer's new work. Collaborating (partnership) co-creates new work with a peer. Championing (restoration) uses the performer's platform to bring a predecessor back to the stage. Four categories, not three. Future intergenerational-labor research should track championship as a first-class relationship distinct from the traditional three.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5614_
