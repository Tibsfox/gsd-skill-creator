## Summary

**Flop** arrives at E=5 -- a quintessential early-'90s Seattle band that was too pop for grunge and too loud for pop, falling through the cracks of a scene that rewarded extremes. Rusty Willoughby's band released records on Frontier and Sony/Epic, toured with Mudhoney and Dinosaur Jr., and dissolved when the major-label machine found them unmarketable. Flop is the casualty story: talented, professional, in the right city at the right time, and still overlooked. Their inclusion at degree 195 is an act of recovery. The **first Cervidae** (deer family) enters the engine, and with it the SPS returns to land after the longest ocean passage in its history. Black-tailed Deer are the most commonly encountered large mammal in western Washington, spotted in suburban yards, forest edges, and island meadows from Olympia to Bellingham.
