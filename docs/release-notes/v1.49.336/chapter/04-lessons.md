# Lessons — v1.49.336

14 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **Winners-only documentation systematically omits the statistical majority of cases.**
   The popular-memory register of the early-1990s Seattle scene centers on the handful of breakthrough bands (Nirvana, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, Alice in Chains, Mudhoney). The Flop entry at degree 195 establishes that this register omits the majority of the scene — the bands that had every indicator of success and still did not break through. Similarly, adults-only documentation of Black-tailed Deer populations omits the majority of fawns, who do not survive their first year. The engine's commitment to the casualty and vulnerability registers is what distinguishes it from popular-memory accounts that retrospectively construct coherent success narratives.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7744_

2. **Niche-collapse is the causal structure of most casualty stories.**
   Flop's commercial failure was not a failure of the individual band but a failure of the market to support the niche the band occupied. The too-pop-for-grunge-and-too-loud-for-pop niche collapsed during the 1992-1994 market window, and bands in the niche had no commercial category to occupy. The niche-collapse-at-category-boundaries principle generalizes beyond the Flop case: casualty stories across domains are often driven by category-boundary collapse rather than by individual deficits. Future documentation should default to asking whether a casualty story fits the niche-collapse structure before attributing the outcome to individual causes.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7745_

3. **Phase-transition framing reshapes band-lifecycle documentation.**
   Treating Flop's dissolution as a phase transition within Rusty Willoughby's longer career arc (rather than as a terminal event) changes what the documentation emphasizes: the continuing artistic commitment, the multi-band career arc, the post-Flop work, the relationship between band-scale and individual-scale artistic lifecycles. The phase-transition framing is a more accurate structural description of what actually happened, and it surfaces the multi-band variant of sustained individual commitment that is complementary to the single-band variant.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7746_

4. **Sustained individual commitment expresses through either band-continuity or band-transition variants.**
   Tollefson's twenty-eight-plus-year Tullycraft commitment, Arm's forty-year Mudhoney commitment, and Willoughby's multi-band career arc are three distinct structural patterns of sustained individual artistic commitment. The first two express through band-continuity (the commitment is to a single continuing band); the third expresses through band-transition (the commitment is to continued artistic work across a sequence of dissolving-and-reforming bands). Both variants are first-class patterns and both express the same underlying sustained-commitment principle.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.351`) · lesson #7747_

5. **Predator-prey acoustic trade-off is a first-class vocalization-calibration register.**
   Prior species entries have documented vocalizations calibrated against mate-attraction (loudness-maximized), territorial display (distinctiveness-maximized), and alarm-signaling (urgency-maximized). The fawn maa bleat introduces a fourth calibration register: predator-prey trade-off (optimize doe-detection while minimizing predator-detection). The register is structurally distinct from the prior three and applies across a range of species whose vocalizations must balance multiple conflicting selection pressures.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.339`) · lesson #7748_

6. **Streak documentation must be constructed retrospectively when the streak breaks.**
   The fifteen-degree marine mammal streak was not explicitly documented as a streak during its progress; the streak's length and structural significance only became visible at the break-at-15 event. Future engine iterations should consider real-time streak tracking as a documentation feature, but in the current iteration the break event is the appropriate moment for retrospective streak documentation.

7. **Habitat-axis transitions can be more dramatic than individual-species entries.**

8. **Regional-subspecies distinction matters for taxonomic documentation.**
   Opening the Cervidae register with the Columbian Black-tailed Deer (*O. h. columbianus*) rather than with a generic Mule Deer or Black-tailed Deer entry preserves the regional-subspecies specificity that matches the engine's Pacific Northwest regional identity. The subspecies-level documentation establishes the template for future Cervidae entries and for other species where subspecies-level distinctions carry structural or ecological significance.

9. **First-year mortality is a cross-taxonomic vulnerability register.**
   Black-tailed Deer fawn first-year mortality exceeding 50% is the baseline vulnerability register at degree 195, but the high-first-year-mortality pattern applies across many Pacific Northwest species: salmon juveniles, songbird nestlings, many small-mammal juveniles. The engine's vulnerability register could extend systematically across these species as a cross-taxonomic vocabulary entry that unifies the documentation of life-stage mortality across domains.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7752_

10. **The casualty register and the vulnerability register are structurally parallel across music and biology.**

The cross-domain structural parallel is what converts the pairing from a thematic gesture into a multiplicative structural statement, and the engine's cross-sync vocabulary benefits from explicit attention to this kind of structural parallel across music and biology domains.
