# Features — v1.49.397

## SPS E=8 TIES MAXIMUM


- Winter Storm Surf at E=8 ties the engine's SPS maximum; this is the first time the SPS track has reached E=8; the Pacific coast storm surf is the acoustic event that the engine's SPS track has been building toward since the abiotic arc opened at degree 245; the wave crush of a 15-20 foot storm swell on a cobble or sand beach is one of the loudest sustained natural sound events in the Pacific Northwest; it is louder than any waterfall, louder than any wind event documented so far, and it registers at the full-body level that distinguishes acoustic events from ordinary sound

## Storm arc opens


- degree 256 is the first entry in the engine's five-degree storm arc (degrees 256-260); the arc documents Pacific Northwest storm conditions at escalating intensity; the arc begins at the coast (winter storm surf), moves to the ocean (Juan de Fuca surf at degree 257), to the mountains (Olympic wind storm at degree 258), to the Cascades (thunderstorm at degree 259), and reaches its maximum at degree 260 with a lightning strike; the arc is the most sustained passage through a single natural system in the SPS track's history
