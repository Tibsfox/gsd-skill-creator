1. **An axis sustains when the second observation is substrate-form-distinct.**
   The geodesy axis continued to obs#2 because LAGEOS-2 changed the geometry, the delivery, the program, and the measurement class while keeping the way of seeing — showing that intra-axis continuation is a deliberate test of distinctness, not a repeat.

2. **The pairing can be the instrument.**
   Two identical spheres at complementary inclinations measure what one cannot: the combination of their node rates cancels the dominant bulge uncertainty and exposes the relativistic drag — a measurement that exists only at the level of the constellation.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12002_

3. **A complementary inclination is a design decision, not a duplicate.**
   Placing the twin at 52.64 degrees against 109.84 completed the pair's geometric coverage and improved ranging of the Mediterranean and other seismically active regions — the second copy of a form adds the most when it is deliberately not in the same place.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12003_

4. **Repeating a proven form is itself an engineering choice.**
   Sixteen years after the original, the twin was built to the same design — brass core, aluminum shell, 426 corner cubes — because the benchmark's value lay in being identical: errors common to one orbit separate from signals common to both only if the targets match.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12004_

5. **An international twin doubles more than the hardware.**

   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12005_

6. **A crewed carrier and an expendable boost compose cleanly.**

7. **A geodesy constellation doubles as a relativity instrument.**
