## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Apodidae (1st entry) -- swift family enters the engine
- **Order:** Apodiformes -- a new order, which also contains hummingbirds (Trochilidae). Despite superficial resemblance to swallows, swifts are not related -- a textbook case of convergent evolution.
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** Vaux's Swift (*Chaetura vauxi*) is the smallest swift in North America and among the most aerial birds on Earth. The name Apodidae derives from Greek *apous* ("footless") -- their legs are so reduced they cannot perch on branches or walk on the ground. Instead, they cling to vertical surfaces with tiny, sharp claws. The **rapid chipping and twittering** is delivered in flight, a high-pitched, insistent chatter that fills the sky over forests and towns during breeding season. The **chimney roost spectacle** at Chapman Elementary School in Portland, Oregon, is one of the Pacific Northwest's great wildlife events: each September, up to 35,000 Vaux's Swifts spiral into a single large chimney at dusk while Cooper's Hawks patrol the edges. Historically nested in hollow old-growth trees; as old growth declined, they adapted to chimneys and similar structures. This dependence on both old-growth forest and aging human infrastructure makes them a species of conservation concern.
- **Vitamin D return:** Derrick Brown's 2nd entry spans the full range of his contributions. At degree 138, he entered as a DJ -- the turntablist and selector who shaped the sound of Seattle hip-hop radio and events. Here at 147, he enters as an artist: MC, producer, and label head (Conception Records). Few figures in Seattle hip-hop operated at this many levels simultaneously for this long. Connected to nearly every major act in the scene through production, collaboration, or mentorship.
