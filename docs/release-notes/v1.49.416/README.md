## Summary

**Olympia enters.**

**Beat Happening** arrive at E=5 -- the Olympia, Washington lo-fi punk and indie rock band formed in 1982 by Calvin Johnson (vocals, guitar, drums), Heather Lewis (vocals, drums, guitar), and Bret Lunsford (guitar). Beat Happening are the foundational act of a specific Pacific Northwest music culture that is not Seattle: the Olympia scene centered on K Records, the independent label Johnson founded in 1982, which would go on to release early records by Bikini Kill, Heavens to Betsy, Beck, and dozens of other acts across the 1980s and 1990s. Beat Happening's music is intentionally minimal, intentionally simple, and intentionally lo-fi: short songs, basic instrumentation, Calvin Johnson's deep baritone set against sparse rhythm guitar and drums, the production values of a living room recording. This is not incompetence. It is aesthetic commitment.

E=5 for Beat Happening is the correct assignment and one of the most important scores in the engine. E=5 means moderate energy -- not the E=8 of the Seattle HC arc that immediately precedes this entry, not the E=9 peak of the Refuzors/Orca Breaching pairing, but the sustained, moderate energy of a band that made a deliberate choice to operate at a different register from their contemporaries. Beat Happening's E=5 is not a lesser score. It is the correct score for a band whose entire aesthetic project was the rejection of excess -- of production excess, of instrumental excess, of volume excess -- in favor of something direct and small and honest.

Calvin Johnson's K Records -- and the broader Olympia music culture it anchored -- is the geographic and cultural counterweight to the Seattle music industry in the Pacific Northwest. Seattle had the clubs, the labels, the industry infrastructure. Olympia had K Records, basement shows, a commitment to community over commerce that produced an alternative to the alternative. Beat Happening is the first Olympia entry in the Seattle 360 engine, and their arrival at degree 275 -- immediately after the close of the Seattle HC arc and one degree before the engine's riot grrrl entry -- is the correct position. Olympia gave the world Beat Happening and Bikini Kill. Degree 275 is Beat Happening. Degree 276 is Bikini Kill.

**Orca Pec-Slap** arrives at E=7 on the SPS track -- the acoustic event produced when a Southern Resident Killer Whale lifts a pectoral fin -- up to 1.8 meters in length on an adult -- and slaps it against the water surface. The pec-slap is an SRKW social behavior: it produces a sharp, carrying acoustic signal that travels considerable distances both above and below the surface, functioning as a long-range social contact call or a signal of emotional state within the pod. Pec-slapping is associated with excitement, play, and social bonding; it is a behavioral expression of what might be called, with appropriate caution, orca mood -- a physical and acoustic expression of social engagement.

E=7 for the Orca Pec-Slap reflects the acoustic energy of the behavior -- sharp, impulsive, carrying, produced by a large pectoral appendage at surface impact -- at a level below the full breach (E=9) and the spy-hop (E=8) but above the resting acoustic behaviors. The pec-slap is body percussion at cetacean scale: a large animal using its body to make sound in a social context, which is a description that connects, however improbably, to the musical context of the Beat Happening pairing. Both are acts of using a body to make sound at intentional energy levels. Both are correct for this degree.
