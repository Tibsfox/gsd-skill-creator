## Olympia enters


- degree 275 is the first entry in the Seattle 360 engine from Olympia, Washington; Olympia sits 60 miles south of Seattle at the southern terminus of Puget Sound, the state capital, home to Evergreen State College, and the site of a music culture that developed in deliberate contrast to Seattle's; the Olympia scene centered on K Records and the International Pop Underground convention was not a Seattle satellite -- it was a distinct community with distinct values: small, community-oriented, explicitly DIY, committed to bypassing the music industry rather than working within it; Beat Happening is the correct entry point for Olympia in the engine because they are K Records' founding act and the template for what the Olympia aesthetic was

## Calvin Johnson and K Records


- Johnson founded K Records in 1982, the same year Beat Happening formed, making K one of the longest-running independent labels in American indie rock history; K's catalog -- Beat Happening, Bikini Kill, Heavens to Betsy, Beck (early releases), The Microphones, Mirah, Dub Narcotic Sound System -- documents a specific Pacific Northwest independent music culture with no equivalent in the Seattle major or Sub Pop independent scenes; Johnson's voice -- a distinctive low baritone, processed minimally, placed in front of sparse arrangements -- is one of the most immediately identifiable sounds in Pacific Northwest indie music

## Pec-slap as social percussion


- SRKW pec-slapping behavior is documented across all age classes and social contexts; calves pec-slap alongside adults; the behavior is contagious within pods; pec-slapping sessions in SRKW groups can last extended periods, multiple animals producing overlapping acoustic signals; in acoustic terms it is a percussion ensemble, each impact adding to a layered rhythmic surface -- a description that maps, with some imagination, to the deliberate simplicity of the Beat Happening sonic aesthetic
