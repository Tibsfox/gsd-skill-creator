## Lessons Learned

1. **Geographic-chapter openings in multi-region rotations warrant explicit structural marking rather than silent transitions.** The degree 275 placement as the first Olympia entry and the opening of the Olympia chapter is explicitly marked as an **OLYMPIA OPENING REGISTER** rather than being treated as an ordinary arc-continuation entry. The lesson is that chapter-level regional transitions in the Seattle 360 engine warrant explicit structural marking so that the transition is legible within the documentation rather than emerging as a silent structural shift. Future chapter openings — for example, a Tacoma chapter, a Bellingham chapter, or a Portland chapter if the rotation extends that far — should be explicitly marked as chapter-opening registers rather than integrated seamlessly into the preceding regional chapter.
2. **Label infrastructure warrants first-class documentation alongside band-level documentation when the label is structurally inseparable from the scene's aesthetic identity.** The degree 275 documentation of K Records as Beat Happening's institutional context treats the label as a first-class register rather than as background information. The lesson is that label infrastructure — when the label is structurally inseparable from the scene's aesthetic identity — warrants documentation on equal structural footing with band-level documentation. K Records is not incidental to the Olympia aesthetic but structurally central to it, and the documentation preserves this structural centrality.
3. **Scoring systems must hold to acoustic reality against positional-inflation pressure to maintain formal honesty.** The degree 275 E=5 scoring for Beat Happening against the surrounding E=7-8 hardcore arc register is a test of the engine's commitment to acoustic-reality scoring. The lesson is that formal scoring honesty is a structural property of the documentation practice and that the engine must hold to acoustic reality even when positional pressure would suggest a different score. Future lower-amplitude entries arriving in the midst of higher-amplitude arcs should be scored at their acoustic-reality level rather than inflated to fit the surrounding register.
4. **Penultimate positioning in extended multi-degree arcs carries structural weight distinct from middle-of-sequence positioning.** The degree 275 pec-slap placement as the sixth of seven arc degrees is documented as a penultimate-threshold position rather than a middle-of-sequence entry. The lesson is that extended multi-degree arcs have distinct structural positions — opening, early-mid, mid-arc-pivot, late-mid, penultimate, and closing — and that each position warrants structural documentation on its own terms rather than being reduced to "middle-of-arc" aggregate treatment.
5. **Biological behaviors with both individual-event acoustics and ensemble-behavior dynamics warrant dual-register documentation.** The degree 275 pec-slap entry documents both the individual-impact acoustic profile (E=7 from the 1.8-meter pectoral appendage) and the ensemble-percussion social dimension (pod-level contagion, layered rhythmic surface) at first-class structural resolution. The lesson is that biological behaviors with dual-dimensional acoustic-and-social structure warrant dual-register documentation where both the individual-event and the group-behavior dimensions are preserved. Future SPS documentation of multi-animal behaviors (pod calls, co-ordinated foraging, ensemble vocal sequences) should preserve both dimensions.
6. **Community-oriented production outside the commercial music-industry framework warrants first-class documentation on its own terms.** The Beat Happening documentation treats the Olympia scene's community-oriented production and explicit refusal of commercial mediation as a first-class structural element of the aesthetic rather than as background context. The lesson is that outside-commercial-framework community production warrants documentation on its own terms rather than being evaluated against commercial-success metrics. The Olympia scene's structural refusal of commercial mediation is not a limitation but a defining aesthetic commitment.
7. **Long-duration community institutions (labels, scientific authorities) warrant structural documentation at equivalent resolution to the acoustic events they produce or document.** The degree 275 treatment of K Records (1982-present) and the Center for Whale Research (1976-present) as long-duration community institutions documents them at structural resolution equivalent to the acoustic events they produce or document. The lesson is that community institutions spanning decades warrant first-class structural-documentation treatment where the institution's continuity is itself a structural property rather than a backdrop. Future Pacific Northwest music and biology documentation should preserve long-duration-institution context where it exists.
8. **Cross-sync axes that pair intentional body-use across cultural-musical and biological-percussion registers demonstrate that the engine's vocabulary applies to the common structural property rather than the surface modality.** The degree 275 cross-sync axis (deliberate body-use for sound-making at intentional energy levels within a community frame) pairs Beat Happening's lo-fi trio body-use with the SRKW pec-slap's pectoral-fin body-use as matched demonstrations that intentional body-use is a common structural property across otherwise-incommensurable surface modalities. The lesson is that the engine's cross-sync vocabulary can identify deep structural commonalities between cultural and biological acoustic production even when the surface modalities (voice/guitar/drum versus pectoral-fin-surface-impact) appear radically different, and that the cross-sync register should be keyed on the structural commonality rather than the surface modality.
