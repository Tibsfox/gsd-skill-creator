The distinction from Trumpeter Swan's individual-anatomy E=6 at degree 128 is clean — that species reaches E=6 through a single bird's tracheal-coil resonance; this species reaches E=5 through flock multiplication. The collective-vs-individual energy distinction is the structural argument for E=5, and the argument holds against the Trumpeter's immediately preceding individual-anatomy E=6 ceiling.

Four consecutive radio figures (126–129) now span two fundamentally different radio philosophies (independent FM vs. commercial AM) and two decades (1960s/1970s KJR + 1980s/1990s KCMU), with KEXP as the eventual synthesis. ## What Could Be Better

- **Specific KJR Arbitron/ratings numbers from the 1960s are not included.** Historical AM-radio ratings from the 1960s are difficult to locate in publicly available sources, and the engine research captured the dominance claim through secondary sources (O'Day's autobiography, Blecha's *Sonic Boom*, HistoryLink) rather than primary ratings data. The dominance claim is well-supported but the specific market-share numbers would have added a quantitative anchor. Tag: ACCEPTED.
- **Subspecies-level Canada Goose acoustic measurements are not detailed.** The research notes that honk pitch varies by subspecies (larger-bodied birds produce deeper honks) but does not provide subspecies-specific frequency measurements. Subspecies-level acoustic data is specialized research beyond the engine's scope for a single-degree release, and the general frequency range (200–2,500 Hz with most energy in 300–1,000 Hz) is sufficient for the E=5 classification. Tag: ACCEPTED.
- **Physical archives for both halves remain unsearched.** KJR station archives are not digitized to the extent that online research can fully substitute, and Pacific Northwest ornithological collections containing Canada Goose specimens are similarly bounded by physical access. The secondary-literature baseline is strong but not exhaustive. Tag: LIMITATION (inherited from earlier degrees).

## Lessons Learned

1. **Radio arc extension can reach backward as well as forward.** Four consecutive radio figures at 126–129 span two eras, and the engine documented the independent FM alternative (126–128 KCMU) before the commercial AM mainstream (129 KJR) that preceded it. Chronological ordering of the engine's releases is not the same as historical chronology of the subjects — the engine can document cultural dialectics in the order that makes structural sense rather than the order events happened.

2. **The mainstream and the alternative are mutually constitutive.** KJR AM and KCMU FM were not isolated phenomena. KCMU was founded (in part) because commercial stations like KJR were not serving certain audiences and genres — the alternative could not exist without the mainstream to define itself against. The thesis/antithesis/synthesis structure (KJR thesis, KCMU antithesis, KEXP synthesis) is the deep pattern of Pacific Northwest radio culture, and the engine's paired-axis design makes this dialectic visible by documenting both poles in adjacent degrees.

3. **Infrastructure-builder figures are as structurally important as artist figures.** O'Day is not a musician in the strict sense — he is a DJ, a concert promoter, and a cultural infrastructure builder. The engine's catalog of 130 artist-entries is richer for including figures like O'Day, Pavitt, Poneman, and Nasty Nes alongside the bands and solo artists, because infrastructure is what allows the art to reach audiences and without the infrastructure the artists do not exist.

4. **Flock-amplified collective power is a distinct energy category from individual anatomy.** The Canada Goose at E=5 reaches high-energy classification through flock multiplication rather than individual volume. The distinction from Trumpeter Swan's E=6 at degree 128 (which reaches ceiling through individual tracheal-coil resonance rather than flock effect) is clean and defensible. The engine's energy taxonomy now has two distinct mechanisms for reaching upper-register E values: individual-anatomy peak (Trumpeter) and flock-chorus collective (Canada Goose).

5. **Family streaks explore subfamilies and genera rather than repeating species types.** The Anatidae streak at twelve consecutive degrees spans three subfamilies (Anatinae ducks, Cygninae swans, Anserinae geese) and numerous genera. The streak is sustained precisely because each degree introduces a new branch of the family tree rather than repeating the prior branch. Future family streaks should be evaluated for internal taxonomic diversity, not just for consecutive count.

6. **Comparison pairs mirror prior SPS pair patterns and emerge from taxonomic splits.** The Canada/Cackling pair at 129–130 mirrors Hairy/Downy Woodpecker (110–111), Barrow's/Common Goldeneye (123–124), and Tundra/Trumpeter Swan (127–128). Comparison pairs typically emerge from taxonomic decisions (the 2004 AOU split) or from morphological-similarity identification challenges, and the engine treats these pairs as structurally significant sub-events within a family streak.

7. **Urban adaptation is a modern-ecological phenomenon worth documenting even without a rarity narrative.** Resident Canada Goose populations of 100,000–200,000+ birds in Washington State alone are not a conservation-concern rarity story — they are an abundance story. The engine's documentation of urban adaptation, behavioral plasticity, and the management challenges of overabundant species is important precisely because most species research focuses on rarity; the engine is also a catalog of dominance-and-abundance stories and the structural mechanisms that produce them.

8. **Parallel ubiquity is a cross-axis structural principle binding acoustic-dominance-by-presence cases.** O'Day's ubiquity on the Seattle dial and the Canada Goose's ubiquity in Seattle parks are structurally parallel — both achieve acoustic dominance through high-volume continuous output from a large population or a dominant broadcast platform rather than through rarity or peak intensity. The parallel-ubiquity principle binds both halves of degree 129 at the structural level and is a generalizable cross-axis pattern for future degrees where both halves are dominant-through-presence rather than rare-and-precious.

9. **Cooperative infrastructure underlies dominant presence on both media and ecological axes.** O'Day's dominance was collective (Lester Smith broadcasting group, PAMS Dallas jingle companies, Concerts West partnership, Seattle Center venue operators, regional teen-dance circuit) and the Canada Goose's V-formation flight is explicitly cooperative (10–14% energy savings, leader rotation, individual voice recognition). Dominant presence in the engine typically emerges from networked cooperation rather than individual exceptionalism, and this generalization holds across media and ecological domains.

10. **Compressed mid-range acoustic signatures are memorable precisely because they sacrifice spectral range for carrying power.** The KJR AM sound (5 kHz audio bandwidth, compressed mid-range) and the Canada Goose honk (200–2,500 Hz with energy concentrated in 300–1,000 Hz) share the same spectral structure. Both are instantly recognizable, both carry well over distance, and both sacrifice high and low frequencies for mid-range punch. This is a cross-axis pattern about how dominant acoustic signatures are engineered for durability and memorability, and it links AM broadcast engineering standards to waterfowl vocalization biology in ways the engine's paired design surfaces.
