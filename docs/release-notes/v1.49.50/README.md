## Summary

The 48th Research project — BLA (Business Law & Accounting), covering the legal and financial infrastructure from Luca Pacioli's 1494 double-entry bookkeeping through modern entity formation, cooperative accounting, tribal sovereign commerce, federal regulatory compliance, equity securities, and international expansion. Eight modules across six domains with 46 sources.

Named "Double Entry" — the foundational innovation that made modern commerce legible: every transaction recorded twice, debit and credit, 530 years and still the foundation. Double-entry is itself a Rosetta Stone — a single event encoded in two accounts simultaneously, translating between assets and obligations.

BLA strengthens the **Business cluster** (ACC, WSB, BCM, BLA) and creates new connections to TIBS/SAL (tribal sovereignty), WYR (corporate structure), and SAN (compliance frameworks). The Business cluster now covers: WA accounting law (ACC), WA small business startup (WSB), building/contractor compliance (BCM), and the full business law stack from formation to international expansion (BLA).

**Research series: 48 complete projects, 446 research modules, ~201,000 lines.**

## Retrospective

### Lessons Learned

1. **The Business cluster needed depth.** ACC and WSB were WA-specific. BLA provides the universal foundation — entity law, accounting principles, compliance frameworks — that ACC and WSB specialize. The cluster now has both breadth (6 domains) and depth (state + federal + international + tribal).

2. **Tribal sovereignty is not a footnote.** Module 04 documents a legal framework that's fundamentally different from state or federal law. Tribal enterprises operate under sovereign authority. The Research series gains nuance by documenting this parallel legal system alongside conventional business law — same domain, different jurisdiction, different principles.
