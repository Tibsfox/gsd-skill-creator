The Roosevelt elk rut occupies a six-to-eight week window from mid-September through late October with peak activity in the first week of October, during which bulls bugle to attract cows and to challenge competing bulls. The bugle is a frequency-sweeping call whose structure — opening low guttural growl in the 150-250 Hz range, rising through a sustained whistle at 600-800 Hz, concluding with a series of terminal grunts at 200-400 Hz — occupies a two-to-six second window and is repeated dozens of times per hour during peak rut activity.
