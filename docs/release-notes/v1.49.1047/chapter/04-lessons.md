1. **An axis can sustain by honoring its origin, not only by adding a new observable.**
   The X-ray-astronomy axis sustains at obs#8 because Einstein returns to the focusing-imaging branch the axis opened with Chandra at obs#1 and occupies its historical root — the first telescope to focus cosmic, non-solar X-rays into a true image, where every earlier instrument could only count photons. The distinction is the focusing-imaging origin, the arcsecond-class ancestor form, so the cadence continues the axis as a substrate-form-distinct sustain rather than rotating away.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12121_

2. **Continuation is by observable form, not by chronology.**
   Einstein launched in 1978 and Chandra in 1999, yet Einstein sits at obs#8 after Chandra at obs#1 because the axis is ordered by observable form. The same Van Speybroeck Wolter-I mirror lineage runs from Einstein's arcsecond-class optics to Chandra's sub-arcsecond optics — Einstein is the ancestor, Chandra the descendant, and the axis records the form's root after recording its perfection.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12122_

3. **The cosmic / non-solar qualifier is the single most load-bearing Einstein fact.**
   Grazing-incidence X-ray imaging of the SUN preceded Einstein by five years (Skylab's S-054 in 1973; solar X-ray rocket telescopes in 1963 and 1965), so the bare claim "first imaging X-ray telescope" is FALSE. Einstein was the first fully imaging X-ray telescope flown as an orbiting observatory for the cosmic, non-solar sky — the qualifier keeps the first true.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12123_

4. **Imaging and focusing are central, correct Einstein content — the error is only the Chandra-grade claim.**
   "Focusing," "Wolter Type-I," "grazing-incidence," "mirror," "imaging," and "telescope" are the very heart of Einstein and must not be over-scrubbed. The only imaging errors are crediting Einstein with sub-arcsecond / "sharpest" / "highest-resolution" imaging or the "X-ray Great Observatory" (Chandra, ~0.5 arcsec; Einstein is arcsecond-class, HRI ~2 arcsec) and citing "0.2–20 keV" as the imaging-optics band (that span is the full instrument suite via the non-imaging MPC; the imaging band is ~0.15–4.5 keV).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12124_

5. **A first can be an imaging/origin first without being a spectroscopy first.**
   Einstein carried the SSS and FPCS spectrometers, but they are mentioned as instruments only — Einstein stamps no spectroscopy first. Chandra owns high-resolution X-ray spectroscopy via dispersive gratings; XRISM owns the non-dispersive microcalorimeter form. Einstein's firsts are the cosmic-imaging telescope and the survey science that imaging unlocked.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12125_

6. **The survey science the focused image unlocked carries prior-art bounds.**
   Stellar coronae (Capella 1975 was the first single-source detection; Einstein owns the survey / norm-across-the-H-R-diagram result), normal galaxies (active galaxies detected 1967 and by Uhuru in 1971; Einstein owns the normal / non-active population-imaging result), and the cosmic X-ray background (Einstein resolved ~25 percent of the 1–3 keV band; ROSAT and Chandra/XMM resolved the majority later) — each first is stated with its qualifier so the result stays correct.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12126_

8. **Accuracy guardrails guard against the centennial, end-of-life, and Chandra-cross-attribution classes of error.**
   Stating the birth year as 1879 and the rename as "in honor of the 1879–1979 centennial" (not "on the centennial," never 1878); the launch as 13 November 1978 on an Atlas-Centaur from Cape Canaveral LC-36B (not Kennedy); operations ending April 1981 and reentry 25 March 1982 (~11 months apart, never collapsed); the sensitivity as "several hundred times" (never "1000×," a Chandra resolution statistic); the optics by SAO / Van Speybroeck and the spacecraft by TRW (not conflated); and the imaging band as ~0.15–4.5 keV (not "0.2–20 keV") — keeps the facts load-bearing and correct.
