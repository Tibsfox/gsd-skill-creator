## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Apodidae (2nd entry -- Vaux's Swift 147, Black Swift 148)
- **Order:** Apodiformes (continuing)
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The Black Swift (*Cypseloides niger*) is the largest swift in North America and one of its most enigmatic birds. Its **nest was the last of any North American bird species to be discovered** -- A.G. Vrooman found the first confirmed nest in 1901 behind a waterfall in Santa Cruz County, California, over a century after the species was first described. They nest almost exclusively on **cliff faces behind or beside waterfalls**, placing a single egg on a mossy ledge kept perpetually damp by spray. The waterfall provides a natural predator barrier -- few animals can reach nests through falling water. The **low chipping** call is given at colony sites, a soft, insistent sound barely audible over the roar of water. In flight, they are nearly silent. Black Swifts forage at extreme altitudes, sometimes above 4,000 meters, and their winter range remained unknown until 2012 when geolocator data revealed they migrate to the western Amazon basin in Brazil. A bird that hid its nest for a century and its wintering grounds for two.
