## Summary

**Brad** arrives at E=5 -- the band built around **Stone Gossard** of Pearl Jam, the side project that let one of grunge's architects explore everything that grunge would not allow. Brad is Gossard (guitar), Shawn Smith (vocals, who appeared solo at degree 41), Regan Hagar (drums), and Jeremy Toback (bass) -- a lineup that fuses funk, soul, psychedelia, and blues into a sound that is deliberately, luxuriously unhurried. Albums like *Shame* (1993) and *Interiors* (1997) on Epic Records document a band that exists in Pearl Jam's shadow by choice: Brad is where Gossard goes to play without arena-scale expectations, where songs breathe instead of pummel. At E=5, Brad occupies the warm, mid-energy space that defines a side project whose purpose is exploration rather than conquest. The **Pearl Jam connection** makes this entry a landmark -- Gossard is one of the founding architects of Seattle's most commercially successful band, and Brad's presence in the engine draws that lineage into the Seattle 360 for the first time. On the species side, the **American Badger** registers at E=5 -- and with this entry, **Mustelidae deepens** with a second family member after the Wolverine at degree 216. The American Badger (*Taxidea taxus*) is the Pacific Northwest's most tenacious burrower: a flat, wide-bodied mustelid whose digging speed is legendary -- a Badger can excavate faster than a human with a shovel, disappearing underground in minutes. The Badger's vocalization is a low, hissing growl delivered from the safety of its burrow entrance, a sound that says "I am here, I am not leaving, and you will regret this." Degree 219 is also **v1.49.360** -- the version number that completes the 360-degree cycle in the version string, a numeric milestone that resonates with the engine's own 360-degree architecture.
