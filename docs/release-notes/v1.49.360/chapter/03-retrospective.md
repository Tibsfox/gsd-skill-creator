## Lessons Learned

1. **Side-project entries can carry main-band lineage into the engine before direct main-band appearances.** The Brad entry at degree 219 brings the Pearl Jam founding-architect lineage into the Seattle 360 engine through Stone Gossard's membership, ahead of any direct Pearl Jam engine appearance. The side-project-as-lineage-entry pattern is a first-class documentation mode that organizes how arena-scale bands' lineages can cross the engine threshold, and future Seattle side-project entries should be evaluated for their lineage-entry value in addition to their intrinsic musical merit.
2. **Posthumous documentation lends elegiac weight that single-context living-artist documentation cannot supply.** Shawn Smith's death on April 5, 2019 and the Brad entry's documentation of his vocal contribution through the band context rather than through solo context produces interpretive weight that would not be present if Smith were still living. The engine's documentation of posthumous band contexts should acknowledge the elegiac weight explicitly rather than treating the death as incidental biographical detail, because the posthumous status shapes how the entry is read and what interpretive registers the entry occupies.
3. **Compact family-documentation windows are a distinct structural register from extended family-documentation runs.** The Mustelidae two-in-three window (216, 219) documents the family through contrasting ecological specialization without requiring an extended degree run, and the compact window is interpretively distinct from the Canidae quartet (208-211) or the Felidae three-degree sequence (212-214). Both compact and extended family-documentation registers should be preserved in the engine's documentation vocabulary, because families with few Pacific Northwest member species benefit from compact-window documentation while families with many member species benefit from extended-run documentation.
4. **Amplitude-trajectory resets from combined-peak events demonstrate deliberate narrative decompression.** The E=15 combined-amplitude peak at degree 218 followed by the E=10 combined-amplitude equilibrium at degree 219 is a -5 reset across a single degree, the sharpest combined-amplitude reset in the recent engine history. The reset documents that the engine's amplitude trajectory can deliberately reset to balanced-floor values when the narrative benefits from decompression, and the engine's amplitude-vocabulary should explicitly record amplitude-trajectory resets as first-class register additions rather than treating them as incidental amplitude changes.
5. **Monotypic-genus specialist positions carry distinctive interpretive weight.** The American Badger's monotypic-genus placement within Mustelidae means the species occupies a specialist grassland-fossorial niche that no other North American mustelid occupies, and the specialist-niche structural principle organizes the species' engine documentation differently from generalist-species documentation. The engine's SPS documentation vocabulary should record monotypic-genus placement explicitly when relevant, because the taxonomic isolation shapes how the species' register fits into family-level documentation.
6. **Arena-rock-to-mellow-groove register inversion documents main-band/side-project amplitude asymmetry.** Stone Gossard's Pearl Jam E=6-7 context versus Brad's E=5 mellow-groove context is the engine's clearest instantiation of the register-inversion principle that arena-rock architects operate side projects at amplitudes significantly below their main-band values. Future Seattle side-project documentation should anticipate register-inversion structures rather than assuming side projects will occupy the same amplitude registers as their parent bands, because the inversion is structural rather than incidental.
7. **Ecological-engineer species produce distinctive downstream-habitat registers.** The American Badger's 10-50-burrow territory maintenance and subsequent habitat re-use by burrowing owls, snakes, rabbits, and grassland rodents documents the species as a keystone ecological engineer whose downstream-habitat construction exceeds its direct predator-prey role in structural significance. 8. **Cross-degree amplitude comparisons constitute a first-class documentation mode.** The Brad E=5 register is interpretable only in contrast to the Pearl Jam E=6-7 register, and the American Badger E=5 register is interpretable only in contrast to the Wolverine E=6 register. Both cross-degree amplitude comparisons demonstrate that the engine's interpretive productivity often depends on inter-degree comparisons rather than on single-degree amplitude readings in isolation, and the engine's documentation vocabulary should treat cross-degree amplitude comparisons as a first-class documentation mode rather than as an analytical afterthought.
9. **Intermittent-but-sustained practice documents depth through discontinuous activity windows.** Brad's nearly-three-decade intermittent practice (1993-2019) with gaps of four to eight years between records documents a structural commitment to the side project that transcends any given release cycle, and the intermittent-but-sustained practice pattern is distinct from continuous-practice patterns documented elsewhere in the engine (Helms Alee's fourteen-year continuous practice is the contrast case). 10. **Combined-mode vocalization documentation should record component structures separately.** The American Badger growl-and-hiss combined-mode is two-part (low-growl at 100-600 Hz sustained + hiss at 2,000-8,000 Hz explosive), and the two components produce structurally distinct acoustic registers that the combined-mode aggregates.
