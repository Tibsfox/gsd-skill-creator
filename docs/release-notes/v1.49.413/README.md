## Lessons Learned

1. **Ecotype-level documentation within a multi-degree arc requires deliberate within-arc branching rather than uniform-taxon treatment.** The Orca Arc at degrees 270-276 documents two major Orcinus orca ecotypes rather than a single monolithic killer-whale entry, and the degree 272 Bigg's introduction is the within-arc branching point that makes the ecotype-level diversity legible at degree-by-degree resolution. The lesson is that multi-degree arcs covering single-taxon subjects should introduce within-taxon branching (ecotype, subspecies, population, lineage) at specific degree placements rather than treating the subject as uniform across the arc. Future multi-degree single-taxon arcs should follow the within-arc branching pattern when the taxon's actual biological diversity warrants the wider documentation breadth.
2. **Pre-visibility foundational-infrastructure documentation resists retrospective-narrative capture of the acoustic event.** The U-Men's Sub Pop early-catalog presence predates the 1988-1991 grunge breakthrough, and the engine's documentation preserves this pre-grunge-label distinction at first-class structural resolution rather than reducing the U-Men to a footnote in the grunge narrative. The lesson is that acoustic events sitting on foundational infrastructure before the infrastructure's later mainstream visibility should be documented at their pre-visibility resolution rather than through the lens of later mainstream-narrative capture. Future pre-visibility documentation should follow the same grain-size principle — document the acoustic event at the resolution of its actual moment rather than at the resolution of the later narrative that captured the moment.
3. **Restraint-based acoustic architecture is a first-class documentation subject rather than a negative-space artifact of maximum-output architecture.** The U-Men's post-punk structural discipline and the Bigg's ecotype's tactical silence are both restraint-based architectures that produce specific acoustic events, and the engine's degree 272 documentation treats them as first-class subjects rather than as absences of the maximum-output work nearby. The lesson is that restraint-based architecture deserves first-class documentation resolution because the restraint is itself the architecture — not the lack of architecture — and reducing restraint-based events to negative-space descriptions loses the structural specificity that the events actually possess. Future restraint-based documentation should follow the same first-class-subject principle.
4. **Within-arc turning points should be explicitly marked in the documentation rather than implied through sequence placement alone.** The degree 272 documentation explicitly marks both the Seattle HC arc post-hardcore turn and the Orca Arc ecotype turn as third-of-arc structural placements rather than leaving the turns implicit in the sequence. The lesson is that structural turns within multi-degree arcs benefit from explicit documentation markers — register names, placement labels, cross-references to prior and subsequent arc degrees — so that the reader can navigate the turn directly rather than having to infer the turn from comparison across degrees. Future multi-degree arc documentation should follow the same explicit-marker principle for within-arc structural turns.
5. **Foundational-figure continuity extends beyond the documented acoustic event and anchors the engine's cross-degree continuity architecture.** Tom Price's through-line from The U-Men to Gas Huffer and Michael Bigg's research legacy carried forward by Ford, Ellis, Olesiuk, and Balcomb both extend beyond the specific degree 272 acoustic events and anchor cross-degree continuity at first-class resolution. The lesson is that foundational-figure documentation should acknowledge the figure's broader continuity context — subsequent work, research legacy, institutional continuation — rather than reducing the figure to the single-degree event they contribute to. Future foundational-figure documentation should preserve the broader continuity at appropriate depth.
6. **Cross-ecotype behavioral-ecology documentation requires enumerating the specific ecological mechanisms that drive the behavioral divergence.** The resident-transient ecotype contrast is driven by specific ecological mechanisms — salmon-dependence versus mammal-dependence, acoustic-expressiveness versus tactical-silence, stable-matrilineal-groups versus fluid-smaller-groups, genetic divergence at the ecotype level — and the degree 272 documentation enumerates these mechanisms explicitly rather than treating the ecotype contrast as a descriptive label alone. The lesson is that cross-ecotype or cross-lineage behavioral documentation should enumerate the specific ecological mechanisms driving the divergence so that the behavioral claim is anchored to mechanism-level evidence rather than to descriptive-label shorthand alone.
7. **Hybrid-genre documentation should name the contributing traditions rather than reducing the hybrid to a single-label register.** The U-Men's post-punk-plus-blues-plus-no-wave-plus-hardcore-energy-budget hybrid register requires naming the specific contributing traditions (Chicago electric blues, Anglo-American post-punk, New York no wave, Seattle hardcore) rather than reducing the sound to a single genre label. The lesson is that hybrid-genre documentation should follow the same decomposition-into-contributing-traditions principle so that the hybrid nature of the sound is legible at first-class resolution. Future hybrid-act documentation should enumerate the contributing traditions rather than reducing the act to the nearest-label category.
8. **Scientific-authority citation should include both the foundational publications and the continuing-work institutional contexts.** The Bigg's ecotype documentation at degree 272 cites both the foundational publications (Bigg et al. 1987, Ford and Ellis 1999) and the continuing-work institutional contexts (Center for Whale Research, NOAA Fisheries, Orca Network, Pacific Biological Station) because both layers are necessary for the documentation to be scientifically anchored. The lesson is that scientific-authority citation in engine documentation should include both the publication layer and the institutional-continuity layer rather than citing one alone. Future scientific-authority citation should follow the same two-layer pattern.
