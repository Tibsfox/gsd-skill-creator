## Tom Price and Seattle underground continuity


- Price's career from the U-Men through Gas Huffer traces the full arc of Seattle's underground music community from 1980 to the mid-1990s; the U-Men are the starting point of that arc; their position on the Sub Pop early catalog connects them to the label infrastructure that would later define grunge globally; the U-Men were too weird, too blues-inflected, too post-punk to be captured by the grunge narrative, but they are foundational to the scene that produced grunge

## Bigg's ecotype and Southern Resident contrast


- the two ecotypes of *Orcinus orca* documented in the Pacific Northwest operate in the same waters with almost no acoustic or social overlap; Bigg's whales and Southern Residents share the Salish Sea but not the salmon; Bigg's whales are the hunters of harbor seals, Dall's porpoise, and gray whale calves; their acoustic suppression during hunts is a behavioral adaptation to the acoustic sensitivity of marine mammal prey; the contrast with the acoustically expressive SRKW -- pod calls, whistles, clicks, breaching -- is one of the most dramatic behavioral divergences within a single species documented in the cetacean record

## Sub Pop early catalog


- the U-Men released material on Sub Pop in its earliest configuration, before the label became the global distribution mechanism for Seattle grunge; their presence on Sub Pop situates them at the origin of the infrastructure that would make the Pacific Northwest music scene globally visible; the label's early catalog, which included the U-Men alongside Mudhoney, Soundgarden, and Nirvana, is the most important regional music catalog in American rock history
