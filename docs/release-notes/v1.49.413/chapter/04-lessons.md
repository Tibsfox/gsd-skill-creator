1. **Ecotype-level documentation within a multi-degree arc requires deliberate within-arc branching rather than uniform-taxon treatment.**
   The Orca Arc at degrees 270-276 documents two major Orcinus orca ecotypes rather than a single monolithic killer-whale entry, and the degree 272 Bigg's introduction is the within-arc branching point that makes the ecotype-level diversity legible at degree-by-degree resolution. The lesson is that multi-degree arcs covering single-taxon subjects should introduce within-taxon branching (ecotype, subspecies, population, lineage) at specific degree placements rather than treating the subject as uniform across the arc. Future multi-degree single-taxon arcs should follow the within-arc branching pattern when the taxon's actual biological diversity warrants the wider documentation breadth.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.457`) · lesson #8777_

2. **Pre-visibility foundational-infrastructure documentation resists retrospective-narrative capture of the acoustic event.**
   The U-Men's Sub Pop early-catalog presence predates the 1988-1991 grunge breakthrough, and the engine's documentation preserves this pre-grunge-label distinction at first-class structural resolution rather than reducing the U-Men to a footnote in the grunge narrative. The lesson is that acoustic events sitting on foundational infrastructure before the infrastructure's later mainstream visibility should be documented at their pre-visibility resolution rather than through the lens of later mainstream-narrative capture. Future pre-visibility documentation should follow the same grain-size principle — document the acoustic event at the resolution of its actual moment rather than at the resolution of the later narrative that captured the moment.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.472`) · lesson #8778_

3. **Restraint-based acoustic architecture is a first-class documentation subject rather than a negative-space artifact of maximum-output architecture.**
   The U-Men's post-punk structural discipline and the Bigg's ecotype's tactical silence are both restraint-based architectures that produce specific acoustic events, and the engine's degree 272 documentation treats them as first-class subjects rather than as absences of the maximum-output work nearby. Future restraint-based documentation should follow the same first-class-subject principle.
