1. **Memorial documentation for a murdered band member should document the artistry as first-class structural content and the loss as acknowledged fact rather than centering the band's entry on the circumstances of the loss.**
   The degree 280 **Mia Zapata** memorial documentation names the specific date (July 7, 1993), the specific age (27), the specific location (Central District), the ten-year unsolved period, the DNA-evidence identification and 2004 conviction of **Jesus Mezquia**, and then preserves the band's entry as first-class documentation of Mia Zapata's voice, the blues dimension, the emotional directness, *Frenching the Bully* (1992), the Central District community, and the Home Alive community response. The lesson is that memorial documentation should invert the implicit weighting — the artistry is the subject, the loss is the acknowledged fact — so that the band's entry is not reduced to the circumstances of the loss and the memorial register holds at first-class resolution rather than collapsing…

2. **Geographic differentiation at the neighborhood scale is structurally load-bearing when a thematic cluster spans multiple neighborhoods within a metropolitan scene.**
   The degree 280 documentation distinguishes **Central District Seattle** from **Capitol Hill Seattle** (degree 279) and from **Olympia** (degrees 275-278) rather than aggregating all into a homogenized Pacific Northwest movement. The lesson is that thematic clusters spanning multiple neighborhoods within a metropolitan or regional scene should preserve the neighborhood-level distinctions at first-class documentation resolution because the neighborhood-specific communities have distinct character (demographic context, institutional infrastructure, venue networks, rent economics) and the clusters become richer and more legible when the neighborhood-level differentiation is explicit.
