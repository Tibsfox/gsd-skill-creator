# Retrospective — v1.49.179

## Lessons Learned

1. **E=6 is structural, not inflationary.** The second E=6 was not assigned to validate a commercial career -- it was earned by a fifty-five-year trajectory of sustained maximum output that included instrument redefinition, barrier-breaking, and cultural permanence. E=6 appears only when an artist transforms what their instrument can be. Two E=6 modes, both Rock, both Seattle: this is a structural pattern, not a coincidence. The engine should track whether E=6 appears again and whether it remains exclusive to Seattle and to Rock.

2. **The genre map reaches rock only when the lineage is complete.** Forty-four degrees of jazz, soul, funk, R&B, blues, neo-soul, blues rock, and gospel preceded rock-primary. This was not avoidance -- it was honest sequencing. The engine documents what flows through Seattle, and the Black American musical traditions that preceded rock in Seattle's history had to be documented first. Rock enters the record only after its ancestors are in the record.

3. **Family changes do not guarantee energy changes.** The family changed from Parulidae to Fringillidae at degree 43, but the energy remained E=4 -- six consecutive E=4 across two families. The lesson: taxonomic diversity does not automatically produce dynamic diversity. The engine needs to track energy independently of taxonomy.

4. **State symbols add cultural depth.** The children's vote, the legislative history, the three names, the ecological paradox -- the American Goldfinch as Washington State Bird brings layers that pure ornithology cannot. Future degrees should identify any additional state or federal symbol designations.

5. **The lifer pattern was overdue.** Forty-three degrees of artist-city patterns, and the most obvious relationship -- the artist who simply lived in the city their entire career -- had not been named until now. Sometimes the simplest connection is the hardest to see because it is so fundamental. The lesson: check the taxonomy for gaps that feel like they should already be filled.
