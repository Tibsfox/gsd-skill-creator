## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Caprimulgidae (2nd entry -- Common Poorwill 145, Common Nighthawk 146)
- **Order:** Caprimulgiformes (continuing)
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The Common Nighthawk (*Chordeiles minor*) is not actually a hawk and is only loosely "common" -- populations have declined significantly across North America. Despite the name, it is often active at dusk and dawn (crepuscular) rather than strictly nocturnal. The **nasal "peent"** call is given in flight, a buzzy, electric sound that carries well over open ground. The **booming dive** is the signature behavior: males climb high, then plummet earthward in a steep dive, pulling up sharply at the bottom. As they pull up, air rushes through the outer primary feathers producing a deep, resonant **BOOM** audible from hundreds of meters. This is a **mechanical sound** -- no vocal apparatus involved -- making it one of the most dramatic non-vocal sounds in the avian world. Nests directly on flat gravel roofs in urban areas, a behavioral adaptation that expanded its range into cities. The wide, bristle-fringed gape is an aerial insect trap, scooping moths and beetles in continuous flight.
- **THEESatisfaction arc:** Formed in 2008 at Evergreen State College (Olympia), relocated to Seattle's Central District. Released *awE naturalE* (2012) and *EarthEE* (2015) on Sub Pop. Collaborated extensively with Shabazz Palaces -- appearing on *Black Up* and touring together. Their music resisted genre boundaries so completely that critics invented new terms to describe it. Dissolved in 2018, but the sonic footprint remains unique in Seattle's catalog.
- **Central District cluster:** THEESatisfaction joins Digable Planets (144) and the broader CD hip-hop lineage. The neighborhood's role as Seattle's Black cultural center and artistic incubator continues to thread through the engine.
