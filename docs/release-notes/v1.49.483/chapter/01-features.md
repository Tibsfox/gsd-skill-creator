## Steller sea lion scale


- the Steller sea lion (*Eumetopias jubatus*) is the Pacific Northwest's largest pinniped and the world's largest sea lion species; adult bulls reach 2,500 pounds and eleven feet in length; the species' primary Pacific Northwest populations occupy haul-out sites on offshore rocks from California to Alaska, with major rookeries on offshore Washington and Oregon rocks; the bull's vocalization -- a deep, resonant roar produced at maximum ventilatory capacity -- is the largest sea lion vocal output on Earth; the colony aggregate of many bulls plus the higher-frequency California sea lion layer creates an acoustic combination that spans a wide frequency range, from the Steller bull's low roar to the California sea lion's higher bark

## Pacific Northwest sea lion population dynamics
