# Retrospective — v1.49.117

## What Worked

- The six-module structure cleanly separates chronological eras from thematic threads (diversity, international, digital), avoiding the common trap of purely linear narrative
- The 36-test plan with 5 safety-critical BLOCK gates provides rigorous verification for a domain where dates, ratings, and attribution errors are common
- Fleet activation (17 roles) with explicit Opus/Sonnet/Haiku allocation matches the research depth required for 77 years of primary-source material

## What Could Be Better

- The podcast/YouTube native format landscape is evolving so rapidly that the Digital Reckoning module will need updating within months
- Late night in non-English markets (Japan, Latin America, India) is largely absent — a future extension could map the global format

## Lessons Learned

- The desk-couch-monologue-band format endured for 77 years not through innovation but through architectural elegance — a container simple enough to absorb any host personality or cultural era
- Arsenio Hall's significance extends far beyond "first Black late night host" — his show demonstrated that the audience the networks assumed did not exist was, in fact, enormous and underserved
- The Conan-Leno crisis of 2009 was not a personality conflict but a structural failure: NBC tried to run two late night shows in the same slot architecture, proving that the format's constraints are load-bearing, not decorative
