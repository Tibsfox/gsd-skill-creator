## Summary

**Tacocat** arrives at E=6 -- the **palindrome band**, whose name reads the same forwards and backwards, a fact they have leaned into with gleeful self-awareness across a career built on catchy punk-pop anthems about menstruation, astrology, the internet, and the absurdities of being alive in the 21st century. Tacocat is a Hardly Art cornerstone, releasing *NVM* (2014) and *Lost Time* (2016) as records that prove you can be funny, feminist, and musically rigorous all at once. At E=6, Tacocat matches Modest Mouse (199) for the highest energy in this stretch -- their live shows are chaotic, joyful events where the audience is expected to move. On the species side, the **Mountain Goat bleat** registers at E=4 -- and with this entry, the **first Bovidae** (cattle/goat/sheep family) enters the Sound of Puget Sound. Mountain Goats are not true goats but are more closely related to antelopes; they inhabit the alpine and subalpine zones of the Cascades and Olympics, living at elevations where few other large mammals venture. The bleat is the Mountain Goat's primary contact call: a nasal, wavering vocalization used between adults to maintain group cohesion on exposed ridgelines where visibility is limited by fog, rock, and snow.
