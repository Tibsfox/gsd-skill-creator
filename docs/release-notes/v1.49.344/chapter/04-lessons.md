1. **Cross-axes emerge from genuine independent research rather than from forced construction.**
   The misidentification-and-defiance cross-axis at degree 203 emerged from researching Tacocat's genre-defiance and the Mountain Goat's taxonomic-misidentification independently and noticing the structural overlap, not from deciding in advance to construct a cross-axis linking the two halves. Future degree documentation should prioritize independent research of each half and let cross-axes emerge through comparison, because organically-emerged cross-axes are structurally more robust than forced-constructed cross-axes and produce more interpretive depth per unit of documentation effort.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7861_

2. **Multi-degree label or family blocks deserve treatment as first-class interpretive units.**
   The Hardly Art four-degree streak (201-204) is more interpretive than any single Hardly Art entry, and the Bovidae family inauguration at 203 (with anticipated future Bovidae entries) is more interpretive than any single Bovidae entry. Future engine documentation should explicitly track multi-degree label, family, and cluster blocks as first-class units and provide cross-references, summary artifacts, and energy-or-feature arc documentation that captures the block-level structure rather than requiring individual-degree documentation to surface it piecemeal.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7862_

3. **Common-name misidentification is a productive taxonomy-vocabulary register.**
   The Mountain Goat's not-actually-a-goat status opens the naming-misidentification register as a first-class taxonomy-vocabulary category, and future species documentation should explicitly note when common names reflect early-European-naturalist misclassification, convergent-evolution false-cognate naming, or post-taxonomic-revision linguistic conservatism. The register is substantive because it surfaces the tension between functional-description names (which survive because they capture behavior-and-habitat) and phylogenetic-description names (which require taxonomic revision and public-communication uptake that common names often lack).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7863_

4. **Terrain-based predator-avoidance strategy is qualitatively distinct from speed, herd, or camouflage strategies.**
   The Mountain Goat cliff-fortress case establishes that predator-avoidance via occupation of terrain predators cannot enter is a first-class strategy category, not merely an extreme form of habitat selection. Future SPS documentation of habitat-specialist species should consider whether the habitat specialization functions as predator-avoidance-by-terrain-exclusion and document the strategy category explicitly when it applies, rather than treating habitat specialization as a category orthogonal to predator-avoidance.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7864_

5. **Convergent evolution provides a first-class biogeography register for Pacific Northwest species with trans-continental parallels.**
   The Mountain Goat / European chamois comparison establishes convergent evolution as a structural principle worth explicitly documenting for species whose ecological niche has been independently developed across separated lineages. Future species entries where convergent parallels exist (marine mammals with trans-oceanic parallels, forest specialists with trans-continental parallels) should develop the convergent-evolution register explicitly rather than treating such parallels as incidental observations.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.347`) · lesson #7865_

6. **Humor as political vehicle is distinct from irony and distinct from confrontation.**

Future music documentation should distinguish these three registers rather than collapsing them into a single "comedic" or "ironic" category, because the delivery mechanisms differ even when the political substrate overlaps.
