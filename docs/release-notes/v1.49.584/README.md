## Part B: Pioneer 9 + *Become Ocean* + Olympic Marmot Forward Triple

**Pioneer 9 closing the heliocentric constellation.** NASA Ames / TRW Systems Group designed the Pioneer 6/7/8/9 spacecraft as a deliberately-uniform four-platform constellation: identical 65.4 kg spin-stabilized common-bus deep-space probes deployed at one-year intervals into heliocentric orbits at structurally-distinct vantages. Pioneer 9 (1968-11-08 launch) closes the constellation by occupying the INNER vantage (~0.75 AU perihelion) — distinguishing it from Pioneer 6/7/8 which orbit near or just exterior to 1.0 AU. The constellation as a whole sees inner heliosphere + 1.0 AU torus + points exterior. Pioneer 9 carried 8 instruments (vs Pioneer 8's 5): an extended TRW/Scarf plasma wave detector with broader frequency range than the Stanford/Scarf E-field detector flown on Pioneer 8; cosmic-dust detector; celestial-mechanics experiment using DSN tracking precision. Operated 1968-11-08 through 1983 transmitter failure (~14 years 9 months); final NASA contact attempt 1987 unsuccessful. The 1983 failure mode is the central engineering data point: cumulative TID exposure across 15 years of inner-heliosphere transit caused graceful degradation of the bipolar power amplifier, helix erosion in the TWT, and high-voltage breakdown in the power-supply chain. Failure was *graceful* — not catastrophic — and the spacecraft continued to receive uplink commands and return basic engineering telemetry through ~1985 before final loss of useful link.

**John Luther Adams's *Become Ocean* as form-as-multiplicity-coordination primitive.** Adams (b. 1953, long-time Alaska resident 1978-2014) composed *Become Ocean* on commission from Seattle Symphony; premiere 2013-06-20 at Benaroya Hall conducted by Ludovic Morlot; recording Cantaloupe Music CA21101 (2014); Pulitzer Prize for Music 2014; Grammy 2015. The 42-minute work divides full symphony orchestra into THREE STRUCTURALLY-DISTINCT ORCHESTRAL CHOIRS at three structurally-distinct (incommensurable) temporal rates: strings choir at slowest rate (palindrome cycles ~6.25 minutes), woodwinds-and-harps choir at medium rate (~5.5 minutes), brass choir at fastest rate (~4 minutes). Each choir generates harmonically-related sustained-tone textures arcing through palindromic structure (slowly-building to peak, receding back to silence), but the three choirs are structurally OFFSET — peaks/troughs intersect at predetermined nodes producing three central climactic crests over the work's full duration. The form IS the work: structural primitive is COORDINATION across three palindromic arcs at three distinct rates. Remove the form (the timing structure) and what remains is harmonically-rich orchestral texture; the form is the identity-bearer.

**Olympic Marmot (Marmota olympus) colony coordination as bioacoustic analog.** Marmota olympus is ENDEMIC to the Olympic Peninsula of Washington — restricted to alpine and subalpine meadows of Olympic National Park between approximately 1500-2000 m elevation. Population has declined from a 1990s peak of approximately 7000 individuals to approximately 2000-4000 (Griffin & Taper 2008 Ecology 89:706-715), driven by coyote predation expansion and climate-driven habitat shifts; IUCN Least Concern but flagged species of concern. Three call types: (a) ascending whistle ('flat' or 'ascending' call) used as primary alarm; (b) trill ('connected whistles' call) used in higher-threat contexts; (c) descending whistle ('descending' or 'screaming' call) used as strong alarm. The species' acoustic identity is constructed not at the individual-bird scale but at the COLONY scale: a marmot colony occupies a structurally-distinct meadow with multiple territorial pockets, and the colony's whistle-calls coordinate temporally across the meadow such that an alarm propagates from initial-detector marmot through the colony in a structurally-fixed pattern (typically within 1-3 seconds), creating a colony-level chorus. The form of the chorus is the identity-bearer: a single marmot whistle is taxonomically diagnostic but does not establish meadow-state; the colony chorus, formed by coordination across multiple individuals at structurally-distinct positions, IS the meadow's communicated state.

**The trans-domain primitive: form-as-coordination-across-multiplicity-of-rates.** *Become Ocean* coordinates three orchestral choirs at three temporal rates intersecting at predetermined nodes. Olympic Marmot colony coordinates multiple whistle-calls at staggered intervals creating colony-level chorus. ## Lessons Learned

1. **Combined three-phase milestone (polish + citation + forward-build) is operationally productive when phases share substrate.** v1.49.584 demonstrates that doing polish + citation triage + forward-cadence build in the same milestone is more efficient than three separate milestones — the citation findings inform polish AND inform the new build, the polish makes the new build's template clean, and a single ship pipeline (manifests + release-notes + tag + push + RH refresh) covers all three workstreams.
2. **V-flag citation triage frequently surfaces fact corrections, not just page references.** 8 of 22 carried V-flags (~36%) revealed substantive factual errors when researchers checked NTRS / NASA history / AGU Wiley / Mudgway PDF. Future V-flag triage sprints should expect substantive corrections in a similar fraction.
3. **Mid-flight fact corrections should be applied by main context, not queued to finishing agents.** When citation-triage cluster agents surface corrections that affect another agent's in-flight work, queueing via SendMessage risks delivery-after-completion. Apply the correction inline.
4. **Cross-track scorer specs should be cross-checked for inconsistencies.** MUS scorer's regex `(?:\d+\.\s+)?` (numeric prefix optional) vs ELC scorer's bare-heading regex caused unnecessary scorer iteration. A meta-scorer would catch cross-track inconsistencies.
5. **Concept-registry .ts files for new MUS subject-specs must be authored alongside subject-spec.json.** MUS scorer's criterion 8 (concept-registry entry) walks `.college/departments/music/concepts/` looking for the `concept_id`. Build agent prompt template should include concept-registry .ts authoring as a Phase C deliverable, not discovered at scorer stage.
6. **GRACEFUL-ATTRITION is the operational complement of PERSISTENT-CONSTELLATION-LISTENER.** PCL describes the *sustained* operational mode; GA describes the *attritional* operational mode. Both modes are required for multi-platform multi-decade missions; design-discipline pairs them naturally.
7. **MUS Pass-1 closure at v1.66 unblocks Pass-2 backfill or Pass-3 frontier work for forward MUS degrees.** Future MUS subject-picks beyond v1.66 should default to Pass-2 backfill (advancing Domain target counts beyond Pass-1 minimums) or Pass-3 frontier (entirely new domain primitives) rather than continuing Pass-1 below-target closures.
8. **Era-anchoring `flight-hardware-mapping.csv` benign warning is a design-discipline early signal.** When ELC scorer reports "no year for degree in mapping" in a benign-warning state, that's a signal the CSV is drifting behind ship-cadence. Phase D back-fill discipline should include flight-hardware-mapping.csv update as a standard step, not on-discovery.
9. **Inner-heliosphere vantage (~0.75 AU) opens a new mission-design category.** Pioneer 9's INNER-vantage position distinguishes it from Pioneer 6/7/8 near-1.0-AU vantages.
