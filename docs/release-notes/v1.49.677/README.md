Soyuz T-15 (spacecraft serial 7K-ST No. 21) launched 1986-03-13 12:33:09 UTC from Baikonur Cosmodrome LC-1 (Gagarin's Start, Site 1/5) on a Soyuz-U2 launch vehicle, carrying CDR Col Leonid Denisovich Kizim (Soviet Air Force; 3rd career flight; born 1941-08-05 Krasny Liman Donetsk Oblast Ukrainian SSR; died 2010-06-14) and FE Vladimir Alekseyevich Solovyev (civilian engineer at NPO Energia; 2nd career flight; born 1946-11-11 Moscow Russian SFSR; died 2025-04-16). The crew were the SAME PAIR that had occupied Salyut 7 for 237 days in 1984 as the EO-3 expedition. Mir base block DOS-7 had launched 1986-02-20 21:28:23 UTC from Baikonur LC-200/39 on a Proton-K launch vehicle and was awaiting its first crew in empty orbit. Soyuz T-15 docked Mir's forward (-X) axial port on 1986-03-15 13:38 UTC = **FIRST-CREWED-VISIT-TO-MIR-CORE-MODULE-DOS-7 obs#1 first-instance NEW LOCKED**.

The crew configured Mir's atmospheric systems, deployed Earth-observation instruments delivered by Progress 25 + Progress 26, and commissioned the Argon-16B flight management computer + the Lyappa hand-rotating arm port-transfer capability that would later enable Mir's modular-expansion architecture (Kvant 1987 + Kvant-2 1989 + Kristall 1990 + Spektr 1995 + Priroda 1996). On 1986-05-05 12:12 UTC Kizim and Solovyev undocked Soyuz T-15 from Mir and flew the orbital-transfer trajectory to Salyut 7 — which had been in unattended slow-tumble survival mode since the Soyuz T-14 crew departed 1985-11-21 due to the medical evacuation of CDR Vladimir Vasyutin. Docking at Salyut 7's forward axial port followed on 1986-05-06 16:58 UTC. This was the **FIRST-INTER-STATION-CREW-TRANSFER-IN-SAME-SPACECRAFT obs#1 first-instance NEW LOCKED CLASS-OF-ONE** — no subsequent mission has accomplished a station-to-station transit within a single sortie, and Soyuz T-15 remains the **ONLY-SPACEFLIGHT-TO-VISIT-TWO-DIFFERENT-SPACE-STATIONS obs#1 first-instance NEW LOCKED CLASS-OF-ONE** in spaceflight history.

Kizim and Solovyev worked Salyut 7 for 51 days. Two EVAs (1986-05-28 and 1986-05-31; total 8h 50m) deployed the Soviet **Ferma-Postroitel** experimental truss-construction work — substrate-precursor to subsequent Mir module-deployment work — and assembled a 12-meter truss segment along with experimental welding + thermal-cycling exposure samples. The crew retrieved approximately 350 kg of scientific samples + equipment for return to Earth.

On 1986-06-25 14:58 UTC they undocked Soyuz T-15 from Salyut 7 — closing Salyut 7's crewed-operations history at its 9th and final expedition = **LAST-CREWED-VISIT-TO-SALYUT-7 obs#1 first-instance NEW LOCKED**. The station continued unattended until its uncontrolled reentry over Argentina on 1991-02-07. On 1986-06-26 19:46 UTC the crew re-docked at Mir's forward port, returning to the new core station. The remaining 20 days were spent completing first-stay Mir activation work and handing the station over to the long-duration expedition profile that would arrive as Soyuz TM-2 EO-1 on 1987-02-05.

Landing came at 1986-07-16 12:34 UTC near Arkalyk, Kazakh SSR. Total mission elapsed time: **125 days 0h 0m 56s** (75 days at Mir total + 51 days at Salyut 7 + transit). Kizim completed his career at **374.9 cumulative days in space** across three flights (Soyuz T-3 1980 + Soyuz T-10 1984 + Soyuz T-15 1986) — **first cosmonaut to spend cumulative >1 year in space at career close**. Solovyev completed his second career flight at **361.9 cumulative days** and subsequently became Mir + ISS Russian Segment Flight Director continuously from the late 1980s through ISS-72 — substrate-form COSMONAUT-TO-FLIGHT-DIRECTOR-CONTINUITY first-instance pattern with ~40 years of post-flight operational leadership.

**Context with the US Shuttle stand-down:** Soyuz T-15 launched 44 days after STS-51-L. ### Lesson #10385 candidate — SUB-AGENT-PROMPT-FILENAME-COORDINATION-DRIFT

- **Substrate observation:** At v677 W1+W2 two parallel sub-agents were dispatched concurrently — one for NASA 1.130 `index.html`, one for 13 artifact files. Each sub-agent received the MISSION-BRIEF.md + degree-sync.json as substrate inputs but neither received an explicit filename manifest. The two sub-agents independently chose mostly-divergent filenames for the same conceptual artifacts: index.html referenced 14 `href="artifacts/..."` URLs of which only 1 (`shaders/viewer.html`) actually matched the artifact sub-agent's chosen filenames. Inline correction required sed-driven mass-rename + removal of one stale shader reference + 5 minutes of operator time.
- **Substrate-form proposed:** SUB-AGENT-PROMPT-FILENAME-COORDINATION-DRIFT. Pattern for parallel multi-artifact dispatches: when two or more sub-agents are concurrently authoring complementary deliverables (index page referencing artifacts; manifest referencing files; catalog referencing pages), the dispatch coordinator must include an explicit shared filename manifest in BOTH prompts. Otherwise each sub-agent's authorial choice diverges and a post-dispatch reconciliation pass is required.
- **Operational mitigation:** the v677 inline fix-up cost ~5 minutes + ~3K tokens. The pre-dispatch coordination cost (compute the filename manifest, include in both prompts) would have been ~1 minute + ~500 tokens. Net token-economy + operator-time saving from the discipline is meaningful at scale.
- **Soak observation #1.** Applied at v677 first instance. ESTABLISHED candidate at any future parallel-multi-artifact dispatch milestone.
