## Summary

The Institutional Anchor and the ostinato. Degree 13 introduces Marc Seales -- a native-born Seattle jazz pianist, Doctor of Musical Arts, and director of the University of Washington jazz studies program, who represents the full formalization of jazz pedagogy in the Pacific Northwest. Seales is the fourteenth degree, the thirteenth jazz artist, and the first PhD-holding performer-scholar in the S36 engine. He is the third pianist in the series -- after Overton Berry at degree 5 and Patti Bown at degree 6 -- and the artist who completes the pedagogical arc that Floyd Standifer began at degree 4: from experiential teaching rooted in bandstand apprenticeship to doctoral-level academic jazz studies housed inside a flagship research university. Where Standifer taught by playing, Seales teaches by articulating the theoretical framework that generates the music, by designing curriculum, by directing student ensembles, by supervising graduate research, and by operating the full institutional apparatus of a degree-granting program. The distinction is structural: Standifer's teaching depended on his personal presence -- when he stopped, the teaching stopped. Seales's teaching is institutional -- the UW jazz program has a curriculum, degree requirements, faculty lines, and an administrative structure that can persist beyond any individual teacher. The program itself becomes the pedagogy vehicle, not the person.

The piano lineage deepens to three chairs spanning three distinct eras and three distinct educational models. Berry was self-taught -- an autodidact who learned the piano through listening and practice, playing stride and cocktail jazz from the 1940s through the 2020s, the definition of persistence. Bown studied at Seattle University, receiving formal classical and jazz training at the undergraduate level, deploying hard bop and orchestral density through two-handed voicings before being erased from the record despite Columbia Records sessions and a career that should have made her a household name. Seales holds the terminal degree -- a DMA from the University of Washington -- and teaches the contemporary modal language descended from Miles Davis, McCoy Tyner, Herbie Hancock, and Keith Jarrett: quartal voicings built from stacked fourths, extended chord structures reaching into the 9ths, 11ths, and 13ths, Lydian brightness with the raised fourth degree that is the single pitch distinguishing contemporary jazz harmony from bebop. The piano chair maps the entire educational spectrum of American jazz, from autodidact to doctorate, across three artists. The piano theory branch gains its eighth node at degree 13, surpassing saxophone/reed as the richest branch in the engine.

As a performer, Seales is a working musician whose piano vocabulary spans post-bop, contemporary jazz, and the modal language that defines modern jazz piano. He has recorded for Naim Records -- a UK-based audiophile label founded in Salisbury, England, by Naim Audio, manufacturers of high-end audio equipment -- the second international label in the engine after Fresh Sound at degree 12, and the first UK-based label. His collaborations with Seattle musicians -- including Julian Priester at degree 7, a fellow educator at Cornish College of the Arts -- position him at the center of Seattle's contemporary jazz network. Seales occupies the intersection of two worlds: the university program and the local performance scene, the professor who is also a gigging musician, the performer whose community includes both club audiences and graduate students. This node function is unique in the engine -- no previous artist has occupied the intersection of a university program and the local performance scene with equal presence in both.

The Hutton's Vireo sits in a Garry oak and sings the same two notes it sang an hour ago. Zu-wee. Zu-wee. Zu-wee. A small passerine of the family Vireonidae, 4.5 to 5 inches long, olive-green above, pale below, with a conspicuous broken white eye ring and two white wing bars -- a bird that looks, devastatingly and confusingly, almost exactly like a Ruby-crowned Kinglet. The resemblance is not mimicry. It is not shared ancestry. It is pure convergent evolution: two independent lineages separated by tens of millions of years of independent evolution arriving at the same physical solution because the solution works. Same size, same color, same wing bars, same habitat. Different families. Different songs. Different everything under the surface. The ear resolves what the eye cannot -- the vireo's repeated "zu-wee" is nothing like the kinglet's high-pitched chatter. The Golden-crowned Kinglet at degree 8 documented the Regulidae. The Hutton's Vireo at degree 13 documents the unrelated species that converges toward them. The engine now contains both sides of the convergent pair.

But the defining characteristic of the Hutton's Vireo is not its appearance. It is the repetition. The bird delivers its simple two-note phrase -- a rising inflection followed by a falling resolution, question-answer compressed to two syllables -- hundreds of times per hour with minimal variation. Studies have recorded individual males delivering the same "zu-wee" continuously for 10 to 20 minutes without significant change in pitch, timing, or phrasing. The bird does not develop its theme. It does not add ornaments. It does not transpose. It does not improvise. It repeats. This is the first species in the engine defined by repetition without variation. The Pacific Wren at degree 1 offered maximum complexity -- a cascading torrent, never the same twice. The Chestnut-backed Chickadee at degree 9 varied on a theme. The Oregon Junco at degree 11 repeated a single note above the fusion threshold, where repetition becomes a continuous tone -- the trill. The Hutton's Vireo repeats a two-note phrase below the fusion threshold, at a rate slow enough that each repetition is heard as a discrete event -- typically one phrase every 2 to 4 seconds. There is no fusion. Each "zu-wee" is a separate utterance. And each one is the same. Identity through persistence. The most conservative singer in the engine.

In music, an ostinato -- from the Italian for "obstinate" -- is a motif or phrase that repeats persistently throughout a composition, forming the structural foundation over which other elements play. The Hutton's Vireo's song IS an ostinato: a short melodic figure repeated without variation for extended periods. The riff in popular music operates the same way -- Chuck Berry's "Johnny B. Goode," the bass line in "Under Pressure," the opening figure of Beethoven's Fifth. These are structures that gain power through repetition. Each individual statement is simple. The cumulative effect creates the identity. The ostinato is structural, not decorative. It does not exist to be interesting in itself. It exists to create the stable temporal and harmonic framework that makes other musical events meaningful. The vireo's "zu-wee" is not interesting on the hundredth repetition. But it creates a territorial acoustic framework that is unambiguous, persistent, and exhaustively documented. The oak woodland knows this bird is here because the signal never ceases.

The Hutton's Vireo introduces a fundamentally new ecosystem to the engine: Pacific Northwest oak woodland. Every previous species was associated with conifer-dominated ecosystems -- old-growth understory, mature conifer canopy, mixed woodland, Douglas fir, spruce-fir. The vireo is tied to Garry oak -- Oregon white oak, *Quercus garryana* -- the only native oak of the Pacific Northwest, growing in the rain shadow of the Cascades and in scattered lowland pockets from southern Vancouver Island through the Puget Trough and the Willamette Valley. Garry oak woodlands are fire-dependent, drought-adapted, and structurally distinct from the surrounding conifer forests: open canopy, grassland understory, thick-barked trees with gnarled branches. They are a savanna ecosystem embedded in a landscape of temperate rainforest. And they have declined by 90 to 95 percent from their pre-European-settlement extent. Urban development, fire suppression, agricultural conversion, and invasive species have reduced the ecosystem to isolated fragments. The Hutton's Vireo is tied to the fate of these remnant oak groves. Where Hutton's Vireos persist, the oak woodland is functional. The bird is an indicator species for restoration success.

The resonance between Marc Seales and the Hutton's Vireo operates on three axes. First: both are persistent, systematic, academic -- defined by disciplined repetition of fundamentals. Seales the jazz pianist, the UW faculty member, the educator who shows up year after year, semester after semester, and teaches the tradition through institutional continuity. The vireo shows up and sings the phrase. Both are present consistently. Both are defined by what they faithfully repeat. Seales repeats the curriculum. The vireo repeats the "zu-wee." The institution and the territory are held by the same mechanism: sustained signal maintenance. Second: both are energy-2, the community-embedded presence. Seales's energy-2 is academic warmth -- the steady, sustained energy of institutional presence, the professor who is also a gigging musician. The vireo's energy-2 is territorial constancy -- always present, always audible, the resident that stays when all other vireos migrate south. The energy-2 cluster gains its eighth member. Third: both are anchored -- the Institutional Anchor pattern in the artist-city taxonomy, the resident specialist in the species engine. Seales is native-born, educated locally, holding a permanent institutional appointment. The vireo is the only resident vireo in the Pacific Northwest, the one that stays when the Cassin's, Warbling, and Red-eyed Vireos leave. Persistence is the defining trait at every level.

- **Modal Jazz and Post-Bop Harmony (Theory Deep Dive):** Five core theoretical elements documented: (1) Modal scales as compositional basis -- horizontal (scale-based) organization rather than vertical (chord-based), staying on one mode for extended periods. (5) Academic jazz pedagogy as integration point -- the musician who can both play the music and articulate the theoretical system that generates it. Thirteenth deployment of the chromaticism thread: modal chromaticism as structural modal choices, distinct from bebop (Lanphere), ornamental (Berry), and altered-voicing (Bown) chromaticism.

- **The Institutional Anchor (NEW -- Fourteenth Artist-City Pattern):** A native-born artist who is educated locally, holds an advanced degree, and anchors the music through a permanent institutional appointment. Requires three conditions: native-born, terminal degree, institutional appointment. Structurally distinct from all thirteen previous patterns. Not the Teacher (Standifer, experience-based), not the Persistence (Berry, no institutional support), not the Pedagogue-Draw (Priester, arrived from elsewhere), not the Salon Keeper (Brazil, informal home pedagogy). ### Part B: Hutton's Vireo -- The Ostinato That Never Stops

Full deep research covering the PNW's most persistent singer and the bioacoustics of repetition without variation:

- **Taxonomy -- The Kinglet's Double:** Family Vireonidae, order Passeriformes. Named for William Hutton (1826-1861), a California naturalist who collected the type specimen during the Pacific Railroad Surveys. Small: 4.5-5 inches, 10-14 grams. Olive-green above, pale below, broken white eye ring, two white wing bars, thick slightly hooked bill. Nearly identical in appearance to the Ruby-crowned Kinglet (Regulidae) -- two unrelated families separated by tens of millions of years of independent evolution arriving at the same physical solution through convergent evolution. Three reliable field marks separate them: bill shape (vireo thicker, hooked), foot color (vireo bluish-gray), wing bar pattern (dark area between vireo's wing bars). But the most reliable diagnostic is voice: the vireo's repeated "zu-wee" is nothing like the kinglet's chatter. The ear resolves what the eye cannot.

- **Bioacoustics -- Repetition Without Variation (NEW -- The Persistence Engine):** The signature vocalization is a simple two-note phrase -- rising-then-falling "zu-wee" or "zuit" -- approximately 0.4-0.7 seconds in duration, 2.5-5 kHz, repeated hundreds of times per hour with minimal variation. Individual males deliver the same phrase continuously for 10-20 minutes without significant change. The bird does not develop its theme. It does not ornament. It does not transpose. It repeats. This is the first species defined by repetition without variation. The progression: Pacific Wren (degree 1) = maximum complexity, Chestnut-backed Chickadee (degree 9) = variation on a theme, Oregon Junco (degree 11) = single-note repetition above fusion threshold (trill), Hutton's Vireo (degree 13) = phrase repetition below fusion threshold (ostinato). Each "zu-wee" is a discrete event. Identity through persistence.

- **The Monotony Threshold (NEW):** The point at which repetition without variation ceases to carry new information per repetition and instead establishes a persistent presence signal. The first "zu-wee" tells you the bird is there and what species it is. The fiftieth adds no new information content. But it adds temporal persistence -- the repetition becomes a clock, a pulse, an assertion that the territory is held. It is not conveying new data. It is maintaining a signal.

- **Oak Woodland Specialist (NEW -- First Oak-Associated Species):** Every previous species was associated with conifer-dominated ecosystems. The Hutton's Vireo introduces Pacific Northwest oak woodland -- Garry oak (*Quercus garryana*), the only native oak of the PNW, growing in the Cascades rain shadow and scattered lowland pockets. Fire-dependent, drought-adapted, savanna ecosystem embedded in temperate rainforest. Declined 90-95% from pre-settlement extent due to urban development, fire suppression, agricultural conversion, and invasive species (Scotch broom, English ivy, Himalayan blackberry). Washington state classifies Garry oak woodland as priority habitat. OCAP/CARE compliance: Coast Salish peoples actively managed oak ecosystems through controlled burning for millennia; the open park-like structure was the product of deliberate fire management. Loss of these fire practices following colonization is a primary driver of decline.

- **Convergent Evolution (STRUCTURAL -- Both Sides of the Pair):** The Hutton's Vireo (Vireonidae) and Ruby-crowned Kinglet (Regulidae) present one of North American ornithology's most instructive cases of convergent evolution. Two species in different families with nearly identical appearance: same size, same color, same wing bars, same habitat. Different ancestors, different songs, different phylogenetics. The resemblance is not mimicry or homology -- it is independent lineages arriving at the same morphological solution because the solution works. The engine now holds both sides of the convergent pair: the Golden-crowned Kinglet at degree 8 (Regulidae) and the Hutton's Vireo at degree 13 (Vireonidae, converging on Regulidae appearance). The parallel in music: independent discovery -- different artists in different traditions arriving at the same musical solution (pentatonic scale, blues form, subtractive synthesis architecture).

- **The Ostinato (MUSIC THEORY -- Progression Adds):** The vireo's song maps to the ostinato -- a motif or phrase that repeats persistently, forming structural foundation. The riff, the hook, the loop, the ground bass. Classical: chaconne, passacaglia. Popular: Chuck Berry's "Johnny B. Goode," Queen/Bowie "Under Pressure," Beethoven's Fifth. Electronic: the sequenced pattern that cycles unchanged while other layers modulate above it. The ostinato is structural, not decorative. It creates the stable framework that makes other musical events meaningful. The vireo's "zu-wee" is the ostinato of the oak woodland. The progression adds **ostinato** at degree 13.

- **Persistence Pair with Nuthatch (Degree 5):** The Red-breasted Nuthatch at degree 5 repeats a single note in steady rhythm -- the metronome, the temporal foundation. The vireo at degree 13 repeats a two-note phrase in steady rhythm -- the ostinato, the melodic foundation. The nuthatch is a point repeated. The vireo is a line repeated. Same persistence principle, elevated from rhythm to melody.

- **Conservation:** Least Concern (IUCN), approximately 3-4 million North American breeding population (Partners in Flight 2020). However, conservation status is directly tied to the fate of Garry oak woodland. Every acre of restored oak woodland is potential Hutton's Vireo habitat. The bird is an indicator species: where Hutton's Vireos persist, the oak woodland is functional.

### The Degree 13 Parallel

The resonance between Marc Seales and the Hutton's Vireo emerged on four independent axes:

1. **Persistence as identity** -- Seales shows up every semester, teaches the tradition, maintains the program. The vireo shows up every day, sings the phrase, maintains the territory. Both are defined not by virtuosic display but by disciplined repetition of fundamentals. The ostinato principle in both domains: a simple structure faithfully repeated produces cumulative effect. Seales's teaching is the ostinato of Seattle's jazz education pipeline -- the same curriculum, the same standards, the same institutional presence, semester after semester. The vireo's song is the ostinato of the oak woodland -- the same phrase, the same timing, the same territorial assertion, hour after hour. Neither develops. Both persist. And persistence IS the identity.

2. **Institutional anchor / resident specialist** -- Seales is the first artist in the S36 engine whose primary identity is institutional: native-born, terminal degree, permanent appointment. The vireo is the only resident vireo in the Pacific Northwest -- the Cassin's Vireo migrates, the Warbling Vireo migrates, the Red-eyed Vireo migrates. The Hutton's Vireo stays. Both are defined by staying when others leave. Both are the one member of their respective cohorts (jazz faculty / PNW vireos) that is permanently present. The anchor and the resident are the same structural position expressed in different domains.

3. **Academic warmth as energy-2** -- Seales's energy is not atmospheric (Frisell), not persistent-through-adversity (Berry), not salon-intimate (Brazil). It is steady institutional presence -- the professor who is also a gigging musician, warm because the teaching relationship is inherently connective, sustained because the institutional appointment provides stability. The vireo's energy-2 is territorial constancy -- always present, always audible, the resident that does not leave. Same energy level, same steadiness, different medium. Eight energy-2 artists. Eight energy-2 behaviors. The cluster continues to be the dominant energy in both engines.

4. **Convergent evolution / independent discovery** -- The Hutton's Vireo converges on the appearance of the Ruby-crowned Kinglet through entirely independent evolutionary paths. In music, independent discovery produces the same phenomenon: different traditions, separated by geography and culture, arriving at the same harmonic or structural solution because the solution works. The pentatonic scale. The 12-bar blues. The call-and-response form. Seales teaches the modal jazz vocabulary that was independently developed by Tyner, Hancock, Corea, and Jarrett working from the same theoretical root (Russell's Lydian Chromatic Concept, Davis's *Kind of Blue*). Multiple pianists, multiple approaches, same structural outcome. Convergent evolution writes the body. Independent discovery writes the harmony.

## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **The Standifer-to-Seales pedagogical arc is the most structurally significant development in the S36 engine's education dimension.** The engine has documented six pedagogical models across thirteen degrees, and the arc from Standifer's experiential teaching at degree 4 to Seales's doctoral academic program at degree 13 maps the complete formalization of jazz education in Seattle. This is not a simple linear progression -- the six models (self-taught, experiential teacher, salon keeper, elder-as-living-archive, working pedagogue-draw, university scholar-performer) coexist in the real world, and the engine documents all of them as equally valid knowledge transmission systems. But the Standifer-to-Seales arc is structurally complete: it spans the full spectrum from person-dependent to institution-dependent pedagogy, and it does so through two UW faculty members who occupy the same institutional position but embody fundamentally different teaching philosophies. This arc would not have been visible if the engine had documented Seales and Standifer in isolation. The arc emerges from the progressive accumulation of data points across degrees. The engine is revealing structures that no single degree could show.

- **The piano lineage at three chairs is the richest instrumental lineage in the engine.** Berry, Bown, Seales -- three pianists, three eras, three educational models, three city patterns (Persistence, Erasure, Institutional Anchor). The piano theory branch now has eight nodes, surpassing saxophone/reed at seven. This is unexpected: the S36 engine documented its first two pianists at consecutive degrees (5 and 6) and then waited seven degrees before adding the third. The gap between Bown (degree 6, 1950s-1970s hard bop) and Seales (degree 13, 1990s-present contemporary modal) mirrors the real gap in Seattle jazz piano history -- the generation between Bown's erasure and Seales's emergence is documented in the engine as absence, which is itself data. The three chairs tell a story about education (autodidact to doctorate), about recognition (persistence to erasure to institutional anchor), and about harmonic language (stride to hard bop to modal). No other instrument in the engine offers this depth of diachronic analysis.

- **The convergent evolution mapping between the Hutton's Vireo and the Ruby-crowned Kinglet closes a structural loop opened at degree 8.** When the Golden-crowned Kinglet was documented at degree 8, the engine held one member of the Regulidae. Now, at degree 13, it holds the unrelated Vireonidae species that converges on Regulidae appearance. Both sides of the convergent pair are in the engine. This is the first time the engine has documented a convergent evolution pair with both participants present, and it creates a new analytical capacity: the ability to compare two species that look the same but are structurally different, and to map that comparison onto the music domain where independent discovery produces the same phenomenon. The convergent pair is a template that the engine can apply to future entries whenever two unrelated subjects arrive at the same structural solution.

- **The ostinato as the degree's music theory contribution slots cleanly into the progression.** The progression from silence through cascade, drone, language, motif, metronome, question, harmonic spiral, ultrasonic threshold, variation, minimum signal, trill/tremolo, and sawtooth sweep now adds ostinato -- the repeated phrase as structural foundation. This is not a new concept (the riff, the hook, the loop are ubiquitous in music), but its placement at degree 13 is precise: the nuthatch at degree 5 introduced the metronome (single-note repetition as temporal foundation), the junco at degree 11 introduced the trill (single-note repetition above fusion threshold), and the vireo at degree 13 introduces the ostinato (two-note phrase repetition below fusion threshold). The repetition family now has three members differentiated by structure (point vs. line), rate (sub-fusion vs. supra-fusion), and perceptual result (discrete events vs. continuous tone vs. melodic pattern). The taxonomy of repetition is becoming one of the engine's most refined analytical tools.

- **Oak woodland as a new ecosystem opens the engine beyond conifer dominance.** Every species through degree 12 was tied to conifer-dominated habitats. The Hutton's Vireo's oak woodland association introduces a fundamentally different ecosystem: fire-dependent, drought-adapted, deciduous, savanna-structured. This is not a minor addition -- Garry oak woodland is one of the most threatened ecosystems in the Pacific Northwest, declined 90-95% from pre-settlement extent, and its active management by Coast Salish peoples through controlled burning for millennia makes it one of the most important examples of human-ecological co-management in the region. The engine needed this ecosystem. The conifer bias through twelve degrees was becoming a structural limitation. Degree 13 breaks it.

### What Could Be Better

- **Thirteen consecutive jazz degrees remains the dominant structural concern for S36.** The concern first flagged at degree 9, reiterated at every subsequent degree, is now at degree 13 with no resolution. Every S36 artist is a jazz musician. Seattle's musical identity extends far beyond jazz. The engine cannot credibly call itself a 360-degree rotation of Seattle music if all of its degrees to date are jazz. Genre diversification in subsequent degrees is not optional -- it is a structural requirement for the engine's integrity. The pedagogical arc from Standifer to Seales is complete. The piano lineage has three chairs. The bebop root node is documented. The jazz foundation is comprehensive. The engine needs to expand beyond it.

- **Seales's Naim Records discography deserves album-level analysis.** The research documents his recording relationship with Naim and the label's audiophile engineering ethos, but does not analyze specific albums or tracks. Naim releases are less widely documented than major-label sessions, but the recordings exist and represent the primary recorded evidence of Seales's performing voice. Future work should identify specific albums and map their harmonic vocabulary to the modal/quartal framework documented in the theory section.

- **The Garry oak ecosystem section could benefit from quantitative restoration data.** The research correctly identifies the 90-95% decline and cites ongoing restoration programs, but specific data on restoration acreage, species recovery rates, and Hutton's Vireo population responses to restoration would strengthen the causal chain between habitat restoration and species persistence. The Washington Department of Fish and Wildlife priority habitat data and the Puget Sound Partnership monitoring records contain this information. Integrating it is future work.

- **The convergent evolution analysis is complete at the morphological level but could extend to behavioral convergence.** The Hutton's Vireo and Ruby-crowned Kinglet share not only physical appearance but also foraging behavior -- both are active mid-canopy gleaners. Documenting the behavioral convergence in addition to the morphological convergence would strengthen the analysis and provide additional mapping points to the music domain (where independent discovery operates at both the structural level -- same chord progression -- and the behavioral level -- same performance practice).

## Lessons Learned

1. **Institutions outlast individuals, and both the music engine and the species engine now document this.** Seales's UW jazz program is the first pedagogy vehicle in the S36 engine that can survive the departure of its creator. Standifer's teaching died with Standifer. Brazil's salon died with Brazil. Seales's program has curriculum, degree requirements, faculty lines, and administrative structure. When Seales retires, the program continues. The Hutton's Vireo demonstrates the same principle from the other direction: the individual bird's song is ephemeral, but the species' acoustic signature persists because the population persists. The institution (the university program, the species population) is the persistence mechanism that individuals cannot provide alone. The lesson for system design: build structures that outlive their founders. Document the curriculum, not just the teacher. Write the schema, not just the query. The program IS the pedagogy vehicle. The population IS the acoustic signature. Individual brilliance is necessary but not sufficient. Institutional structure is what converts individual effort into persistent effect.

2. **Repetition without variation is a signal strategy, not a deficiency.** The Hutton's Vireo's monotonous song is consistently described as "boring" or "tiresome" by casual observers, but it is an optimal solution to the problem the bird is solving: territorial advertisement over long time periods in dense woodland where visual signals attenuate rapidly. The signal must be recognizable (simple), persistent (repeated), and unambiguous (invariant). Variation would add information content but reduce recognizability. The vireo maximizes recognizability at the cost of information content, and this is the correct tradeoff for its ecological niche. The same principle applies to institutional pedagogy: a curriculum that changes every semester loses institutional memory. A curriculum that persists -- the same core courses, the same foundational materials, the same degree requirements -- creates the stable framework that allows individual faculty to contribute their own variation within the structure. The ostinato is the curriculum. The improvisation is the individual teacher's contribution over the ostinato. Both are necessary. The ostinato comes first.

3. **Convergent evolution is the strongest evidence that a design solution is optimal.** When two independent lineages arrive at the same physical form, the form is not accidental -- it is the solution that the selection pressures of the niche produce. The Hutton's Vireo and Ruby-crowned Kinglet converge because small, olive-green, mid-canopy gleaning works in Pacific Northwest woodland. When two independent musical traditions arrive at the same harmonic structure (the pentatonic scale, the 12-bar blues, quartal voicings), the structure is not arbitrary -- it is the solution that the perceptual and physical constraints of music-making produce. When two independent software projects arrive at the same architecture, the architecture is worth studying. Convergence is nature's peer review. If two unrelated systems independently verify the same solution, the solution has been validated by the strongest possible test: independent replication. Design systems that actively look for convergent solutions across domains. The convergence IS the evidence.
