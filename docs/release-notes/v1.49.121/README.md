## Summary

A deep research study into the LHAASO collaboration's landmark December 2025 detection of gamma rays from Cygnus X-3 extending to 3.7 petaelectronvolts -- the most energetic radiation from any resolved point source in the LHAASO catalog. This compact binary system, a Wolf-Rayet star orbiting a probable black hole separated by less than one solar radius, operates as a super-PeVatron: accelerating protons to energies a thousand times beyond what human particle colliders can produce. The project traces the through-line from a 4.8-hour binary orbit to the large-scale structure of Galactic cosmic rays, one of the most remarkable scaling relationships in modern astrophysics.

## Retrospective

### What Worked
- The four-angle approach (system, observation, particle physics, multi-messenger) mirrors how the research community itself structures this problem and produces a complete reference
- Tracing the 40-year detection arc from unreliable 1980s air-shower experiments to the 10-sigma LHAASO confirmation gives genuine historical depth to the discovery narrative
- The photomeson production pathway (p + gamma -> pi0 -> gamma rays) provides a clean physical thread connecting all five modules

### What Could Be Better
- The neutrino non-detection remains the key open question -- future updates should track IceCube-Gen2 progress as it comes online
- The relationship between Cygnus X-3 and the broader Cygnus superbubble cosmic-ray factory deserves deeper treatment as more LHAASO data is published

## Lessons Learned

- The most energetic photons measured by any observatory on Earth emerge from an interaction region smaller than a city block -- extreme environments produce extreme physics at extreme scales
- Variability is the key diagnostic: unlike diffuse supernova remnants that grind out particles over millennia, Cygnus X-3's month-scale flux changes and 4.8-hour orbital modulation prove the emission is tied to the compact binary, not the surrounding nebula
- The cosmic-ray knee at ~3 PeV -- a feature measured for decades without clear explanation -- now has a plausible architect sitting inside the Cygnus superbubble
