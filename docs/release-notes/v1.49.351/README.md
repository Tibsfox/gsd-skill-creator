## Summary

**Telekinesis** arrives at E=4 -- Michael Benjamin Lerner's power-pop project that has quietly been one of the most consistent forces in Seattle indie for over a decade. Lerner is a **Merge Records** artist, and with this entry, one of America's most important independent labels enters the Seattle 360 engine for the first time. From *Telekinesis!* (2009) through *Ad Infinitum* (2015) to *Effluxion* (2019), Lerner has produced a body of work characterized by precise drumming (he plays every instrument but drums are his foundation), bright melodies, and an economy of arrangement that trusts the song over the production. At E=4, Telekinesis represents the restrained end of power-pop -- the hooks are there, but they're delivered with a lightness of touch that rewards attention over volume. On the species side, the **Gray Wolf howl** registers at E=7 -- the **highest SPS energy in this stretch** and a landmark entry on multiple levels. The Gray Wolf (*Canis lupus*) is an **EXTIRPATED species** in western Washington: wolves once ranged throughout the Puget Sound lowlands but were systematically eliminated by the early 20th century. Their slow return to Washington's northeast corner (with confirmed packs in the Cascades as of the 2020s) makes every howl an act of reclamation. The Gray Wolf howl is the deepest, most resonant Canidae vocalization in North America -- a low-frequency, sustained cry that can travel 10 miles across open terrain. At E=7, it is the most powerful mammalian vocalization since the Roosevelt Elk rut roar at degree 199.
