**The Power-Pop Lineage and Seattle's Position Within It.** The **power-pop** aesthetic that Telekinesis occupies at its restrained end has a specific historical lineage stretching back to early 1970s American pop-rock and extending through multiple regional and temporal branches. **Big Star** (Memphis, Tennessee) established the foundational power-pop canon through *#1 Record* (1972), *Radio City* (1974), and *Third/Sister Lovers* (1978), whose combined catalog fused British Invasion pop-song-structure with post-hippie American guitar-pop sensibilities to produce the power-pop template. **Teenage Fanclub** (Glasgow, Scotland) extended the power-pop tradition into the contemporary era through *Bandwagonesque* (1991), *Grand Prix* (1995), *Songs from Northern Britain* (1997), and subsequent albums whose melodic sophistication and harmonic richness established the contemporary power-pop canonical benchmark. **The Posies** (Seattle and Bellingham, Washington) established the specifically Pacific Northwest power-pop lineage through *Dear 23* (1990), *Frosting on the Beater* (1993), and adjacent catalog, and the Posies' Seattle-and-Cascadian regional presence established the regional power-pop precedent that Telekinesis's restrained-end register operates within. Telekinesis's degree 210 entry thus sits within a multi-decade multi-regional power-pop lineage spanning from Memphis through Glasgow through Seattle, with the specifically restrained-and-Merge-distributed Telekinesis register occupying a distinct niche that the engine now documents as a first-class musical-register vocabulary entry.

**The Economy-of-Arrangement Ethic and Multi-Instrumentalist Practice.** Telekinesis records are characterized by a sustained economy-of-arrangement ethic that operates at multiple levels simultaneously. At the arrangement level, the records feature restrained instrumental choices — typically drums, bass, guitar, and vocals with occasional keyboard or auxiliary-instrument additions — and avoid the maximalist multi-instrumental stacking that characterizes higher-amplitude power-pop registers. At the performance level, Lerner's multi-instrumentalist practice (he plays most of the instruments on Telekinesis records himself) produces an internally-consistent rhythmic-and-melodic signature whose drummer-composer foundation gives every track a specific percussive-forward character. At the production level, the records favor clarity over density, space over layering, and dry-close-microphone fidelity over reverb-heavy or effects-processed treatment. The combined economy-of-arrangement ethic produces a distinct restrained-power-pop register that the engine's degree 210 entry documents as a first-class musical-register vocabulary addition.

**The Gray Wolf and the Extirpation History.** The **Gray Wolf** (*Canis lupus*) is the largest extant wild canid in North America and historically the continent's most broadly distributed large predator. Prior to European-American settlement, Gray Wolves ranged continuously across the entire continent north of central Mexico, including continuous presence across the **Puget Sound lowland**, the **Cascade Range**, the **Olympic Peninsula**, the **Columbia Basin**, and the broader Pacific Northwest. Systematic extirpation proceeded through the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries via government-sponsored bounty programs, coordinated predator-control operations, and habitat fragmentation, and the last confirmed Gray Wolf in the western Cascades was killed in the 1930s. The extirpation was not an incidental loss but a deliberate program: state and federal wildlife-management agencies paid bounties for wolf carcasses, organized coordinated trapping and poisoning operations, and explicitly targeted the species for elimination in the interests of livestock-industry protection and what was then understood as rangeland management. The Gray Wolf's absence from western Washington between the 1930s and the present is thus a human-produced ecological wound rather than a natural range contraction, and the species' degree 210 entry marks the first **EXTIRPATED-species** register entry in the engine's SPS documentation.

**The Slow Recolonization and the Current State.** Gray Wolf recolonization of Washington state has proceeded since approximately 2008 through natural dispersal from **Idaho**, **British Columbia**, and **Montana** source populations. The first confirmed contemporary Washington pack (the **Lookout Pack**) was documented in Okanogan County in 2008, and subsequent pack documentation has expanded across the **northeastern Cascades** and the **Selkirk Mountains**, including confirmed packs in Ferry County, Stevens County, Pend Oreille County, and adjacent northeastern Washington counties. As of the 2020s, the Washington Department of Fish and Wildlife documents over thirty confirmed wolf packs across northeastern Washington, with recolonization of the **Cascade Range** proper proceeding more slowly owing to greater distance from source populations and greater intervening human-development pressure. No confirmed wolf packs currently exist west of the Cascade crest, meaning that the Puget Sound lowland, the western Cascades, and the Olympic Peninsula remain wolf-absent as of this degree's 2026 ship date despite ninety-plus years having passed since the 1930s extirpation. The Gray Wolf's Washington recolonization thus represents a decades-long recovery process whose western-Cascade completion remains pending and whose political, legal, and cultural framings continue to evolve alongside the ecological process.

**The Gray Wolf Howl and Its Acoustic Properties.** The **Gray Wolf howl** at degree 210 (E=7) is the deepest, most resonant, and most acoustically-complex Canidae vocalization in North America. Fundamental frequencies reach as low as 150 Hz (significantly lower than the Coyote howl's typical 300-600 Hz fundamental range), sustained durations reach up to 11 seconds per individual howl (significantly longer than the Coyote howl's typical 3-7 second sustained range), propagation distances exceed 10 miles across open terrain under favorable atmospheric conditions (significantly longer than the Coyote howl's typical 3-5 mile propagation), and multi-harmonic overtone structures produce rich timbre that carries through dense forest and across open terrain with remarkable fidelity. The E=7 amplitude rating reflects the howl's extraordinary acoustic properties across all four of these axes simultaneously — frequency, duration, propagation, and harmonic richness — and the rating ties the Gray Wolf howl to the Roosevelt Elk bugle (degree 197, E=7) and the Roosevelt Elk rut roar (degree 199, E=7) at the current Q3 amplitude ceiling. The ceiling-ties establish a three-degree cluster of high-amplitude Q3 mammalian vocalizations (197, 199, 210) that future documentation can frame as a structural observation about large-mammal vocal amplitude in the Pacific Northwest.

**The Beau-Geste Pack-Chorus Strategy.** The **beau-geste** pack-chorus strategy is a coordinated deceptive-vocalization technique in which pack members deliberately desynchronize their individual howls by offsetting pitch and timing to produce the acoustic impression of a larger pack than actually exists. The strategy operates at multiple acoustic levels simultaneously: individual pack members begin their howls at slightly different moments producing temporal-offset that masks the actual count, each pack member howls at a slightly different fundamental pitch producing pitch-offset that similarly masks the count, and the combined acoustic output contains more apparent individual voices than the actual pack contains. The strategy's functional purpose is territorial defense and intruder-deterrence: a small pack's beau-geste chorus can deter larger neighboring packs from territorial incursions by presenting as a much larger group. The strategy operates at both wolf and coyote scales (Coyotes use a similar pack-chorus strategy but at higher frequency and with less acoustic effect owing to smaller body size and higher fundamental frequencies), and the Gray Wolf's lower fundamental frequency and longer sustained duration produce particularly effective beau-geste results. The **beau-geste-pack-chorus register** enters the engine's acoustic-environment vocabulary at degree 210 as a first-class entry.

## Part B: Gray Wolf Howl — The Extirpated-Species Return and the Beau-Geste-Pack-Chorus Acoustic Register

- **Species Baseline and Taxonomic Identity:** **Gray Wolf** (*Canis lupus*) is a member of **Order Carnivora / Family Canidae / Genus Canis / species lupus**, the largest extant wild canid in North America and historically the continent's most broadly distributed large predator. Adult body mass in Pacific Northwest populations typically ranges from 70 to 145 pounds, with Washington wolves averaging 80-110 pounds and females generally smaller than males. The species is a pack-living apex predator whose social organization, territorial behavior, and vocal-communication repertoire are among the most studied and best-documented among North American mammals.
- **Historical and Contemporary Distribution:** Prior to European-American settlement, Gray Wolves ranged continuously across the entire continent north of central Mexico, including continuous presence across the **Puget Sound lowland**, the **Cascade Range**, the **Olympic Peninsula**, the **Columbia Basin**, and the broader Pacific Northwest. Systematic extirpation proceeded through the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries via government-sponsored bounty programs, coordinated predator-control operations, and habitat fragmentation. The last confirmed Gray Wolf in the western Cascades was killed in the 1930s, and the species was effectively extirpated from western Washington from approximately 1940 through 2008. Contemporary Washington distribution is restricted to the northeastern Cascades and Selkirk Mountains, with thirty-plus confirmed packs across **Okanogan**, **Ferry**, **Stevens**, **Pend Oreille**, and adjacent northeastern Washington counties as of the 2020s, and ongoing slow westward recolonization from the northeastern population core toward the Cascade crest.
- **Extirpation History and Governmental-Program Context:** The Gray Wolf's extirpation from western Washington was not an incidental loss but a deliberate governmental program. State and federal wildlife-management agencies paid bounties for wolf carcasses, organized coordinated trapping and poisoning operations, funded dedicated predator-control personnel, and explicitly targeted the species for elimination in the interests of livestock-industry protection and what was then understood as rangeland management. The extirpation program operated across multiple decades and involved coordination between the U.S. Biological Survey (predecessor to the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service), state wildlife agencies, livestock-industry associations, and local predator-control interests. The documented history of the extirpation program is an important context for understanding the contemporary Gray Wolf recovery process, which operates against a human-produced ecological wound rather than merely a natural range contraction.
- **Contemporary Washington Recolonization Since 2008:** Contemporary Gray Wolf recolonization of Washington state has proceeded since approximately 2008 through natural dispersal from **Idaho**, **British Columbia**, and **Montana** source populations. The first confirmed contemporary Washington pack (the **Lookout Pack**) was documented in Okanogan County in 2008, and subsequent pack documentation has expanded across the northeastern Cascades and the Selkirk Mountains. As of the 2020s, the **Washington Department of Fish and Wildlife** documents over thirty confirmed wolf packs across northeastern Washington. Recolonization of the Cascade Range proper proceeds more slowly owing to greater distance from source populations and greater intervening human-development pressure, and no confirmed wolf packs currently exist west of the Cascade crest.
- **The Gray Wolf Howl and Its Acoustic Properties:** The **Gray Wolf howl** at degree 210 (E=7) is the deepest, most resonant, and most acoustically-complex Canidae vocalization in North America. Fundamental frequencies reach as low as **150 Hz** (significantly lower than the Coyote howl's typical 300-600 Hz fundamental range), sustained durations reach up to **11 seconds** per individual howl (significantly longer than the Coyote howl's typical 3-7 second sustained range), propagation distances exceed **10 miles** across open terrain under favorable atmospheric conditions (significantly longer than the Coyote howl's typical 3-5 mile propagation), and multi-harmonic overtone structures produce rich timbre that carries through dense forest and across open terrain with remarkable fidelity. The E=7 amplitude rating reflects the howl's extraordinary properties across all four of these axes simultaneously.
- **The Beau-Geste Pack-Chorus Strategy:** The **beau-geste** pack-chorus strategy is a coordinated deceptive-vocalization technique in which pack members deliberately desynchronize their individual howls by offsetting pitch and timing to produce the acoustic impression of a larger pack than actually exists. The strategy operates at multiple acoustic levels simultaneously: individual pack members begin their howls at slightly different moments producing temporal-offset that masks the actual count, each pack member howls at a slightly different fundamental pitch producing pitch-offset that similarly masks the count, and the combined acoustic output contains more apparent individual voices than the actual pack contains. The strategy's functional purpose is territorial defense and intruder-deterrence: a small pack's beau-geste chorus can deter larger neighboring packs from territorial incursions by presenting as a much larger group. The strategy operates at both wolf and coyote scales, and the Gray Wolf's lower fundamental frequency and longer sustained duration produce particularly effective beau-geste results.
- **Four Functional Registers of the Adult Howl:** The adult Gray Wolf howl serves four distinct functional registers simultaneously. First, **territorial advertisement** — the howl signals the pack's presence and territorial claim to neighboring packs and to potential intruders, operating over the howl's ten-plus-mile propagation distance. Second, **pack-location coordination** — the howl allows dispersed pack members to maintain spatial awareness and coordinate movement across the pack's territory, which can span hundreds of square miles in lightly-populated habitat. Third, **social-bond maintenance** — the howl reinforces social bonds between pack members through synchronized group-howling behaviors that operate as a specifically pack-level communication form distinct from individual-to-individual communication. Fourth, **inter-pack communication** — neighboring packs can exchange information through howl-exchange behaviors that allow territorial negotiation without direct physical confrontation.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Return after long absence constitutes a distinct register from continuous presence and merits first-class documentation treatment across domains.** The Merge Records first engine entry and the Gray Wolf's Washington recolonization together establish return-after-absence as carrying distinct interpretive value that continuous-presence documentation cannot substitute for, across radically different domains. Future engine degrees should treat return-after-absence events (first engine entries of historically-significant institutions, returns of historically-present species, revivals of historically-significant aesthetic traditions) as first-class documentation subjects rather than as incidental context, and the return-after-absence cross-axis should be treated as a recurring documentation strategy rather than an ad-hoc observation at degree 210.

2. **Ecological-status registers like EXTIRPATED carry ethical-political weight exceeding their immediate technical definition and should be documented as first-class engine vocabulary entries.** The EXTIRPATED-species register's introduction at degree 210 establishes that the engine's SPS ecological-status vocabulary is differentiated rather than uniform, and the specific category of extirpated-species carries ethical-political weight (the extirpation is human-caused, the current absence is the result of deliberate governmental programs, the ongoing recolonization operates against historical loss rather than natural range dynamics) that exceeds the narrow ecological frame. Future SPS documentation should explicitly distinguish ecological-status registers (EXTIRPATED, vulnerable, threatened, endangered, secure, continuous-presence) as first-class vocabulary categories with their own documentation requirements and cross-axis analytical frames.

3. **Genus-or-family-level comparative arcs produce interpretive depth beyond what species-by-species documentation can achieve.** The Canidae quartet across degrees 208-211 demonstrates that multi-species family-level arcs produce cross-species acoustic-ecology observations (amplitude range comparison, life-cycle-stage variation, functional-register distribution) that individual species-by-species documentation cannot produce. Future SPS documentation of other multi-species families (Mustelidae, Cervidae, Ursidae, Sciuridae) should consider whether similar multi-species family-level arcs would produce comparable interpretive value, and genus-or-family-level comparative arcs should be treated as a deliberate documentation strategy rather than an incidental observation.

4. **E-value ceilings across quadrants are a first-class ledger dimension and should be explicitly tracked.** The three-degree Q3 E=7 cluster (Roosevelt Elk bugle at 197, Roosevelt Elk rut roar at 199, Gray Wolf howl at 210) surfaces an amplitude-ceiling structural observation that neither individual degree captures alone. Future engine documentation should explicitly track E-value ceilings across quadrants as a first-class ledger dimension, E-value-ceiling-ties should be treated as structural observations warranting cross-sync documentation, and quadrant-by-quadrant E-value-ceiling comparison should be developed as an analytical frame at the quadrant-completion mark.

5. **Independent-label ethics parallel artist-level creative practices in ways that deserve explicit documentation.** Merge Records' artist-ownership, profit-sharing, distribution-control-retaining ethics parallel Michael Benjamin Lerner's multi-instrumentalist solo-recording and song-over-production creative practices at the structural level, with both the label and the artist operating from principles of creator-level control over industry-standard commercial-optimization defaults. Future engine documentation of musical acts should consider whether label-relationship and creative-practice choices are treated as structural aesthetic choices warranting documentation rather than as merely commercial context, and the label-artist-ethics-parallelism pattern should be treated as a deliberate documentation strategy rather than an incidental observation.

6. **First-engine-entry documentation carries multiplicative vocabulary-addition weight and should be structured to maximize future reference-frame value.** Merge Records' first engine entry at degree 210 adds the full Merge historical-and-ethical context to the engine's label vocabulary, making the context available for subsequent Merge-artist degree entries without requiring from-scratch reintroduction. Future first-engine-entries for significant institutional or aesthetic references (record labels, venues, festivals, publications, aesthetic traditions) should be structured to maximize future reference-frame value by including comprehensive historical, ethical, and contextual documentation rather than only the immediate artist-level content.

7. **Pack-chorus acoustic strategies like beau-geste are first-class acoustic-environment register additions to the engine's vocabulary.** The Gray Wolf's deliberate within-pack pitch-and-timing desynchronization strategy enters the engine's acoustic-environment vocabulary as a specific pack-level coordination register distinct from single-source acoustic environments and from adult-group vocalizations without deliberate deception. Future SPS documentation of species with documented pack-chorus or group-vocalization strategies should consider whether similar acoustic-environment registers are documented or documentable, and group-vocalization acoustic strategies should be treated as first-class acoustic-environment documentation subjects.

8. **Restrained-and-maximalist registers within the same broader aesthetic tradition deserve explicit distinction as first-class vocabulary entries.** Telekinesis's restrained-power-pop register is structurally distinct from maximalist-power-pop registers within the broader power-pop tradition, and the register's documentation at degree 210 establishes restrained-versus-maximalist as a first-class within-tradition distinction. Future engine documentation of multi-register aesthetic traditions (power-pop, indie-rock, folk, jazz, electronic music) should explicitly distinguish restrained-versus-maximalist registers as first-class vocabulary entries rather than treating them as merely stylistic variations within a uniform tradition.

9. **Decade-plus catalog spans document sustained creative-practice ethics in ways that single-album documentation cannot.** Telekinesis's five-album arc across 2009-2019 documents sustained creative-practice consistency (economy-of-arrangement ethic, drummer-composer foundation, restrained-amplitude register) in ways that single-album documentation could not accomplish. Future engine documentation of musical acts with decade-plus catalog spans should foreground the catalog-level consistency-or-evolution pattern as a first-class documentation subject, and sustained-creative-practice documentation should be treated as a deliberate analytical frame rather than an incidental observation.

10. **Ethical-political weight in documentation exceeds the purely aesthetic or ecological frame and should be foregrounded rather than treated as incidental context.** Both halves of degree 210 carry ethical-political weight (Merge Records' artist-ownership ethics as deliberate industry-organization stance, Gray Wolf's Washington recolonization as contested recovery process involving multiple stakeholder interests) that exceeds the narrowly aesthetic or narrowly ecological frame, and the ethical-political weight is central to rather than incidental to the degree's documentation. Future engine documentation should foreground ethical-political stakes as first-class register content, treat ethical-political weight as a deliberate cross-axis analytical frame rather than as incidental context, and develop the ethical-political-weight cross-axis as a recurring documentation strategy across degrees where the stakes are present.
