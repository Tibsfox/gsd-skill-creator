## Artist-City Patterns

- **FIRST Shoegaze -- Genre Stage 94** -- Shoegaze has never appeared in the engine until now. Ninety-four genre stages in 118 degrees. Loomer's name itself references the shoegaze canon (My Bloody Valentine's *Loomer* track on *Loveless*). Self-released, Seattle. The genre is defined by its production -- walls of distorted guitar, buried vocals, heavy reverb and chorus effects, the "looking at your shoes" stage presence that gave the genre its name. E=3 reflects the genre's characteristic inward pull -- dense and enveloping but not aggressive.
- **E=3 steep drop** -- the energy crashes from E=5 (116, 117) to E=3. The steepest single-degree drop in the recent run. Shoegaze's sonic density registers as low energy because the dynamics are compressed -- everything is loud but nothing peaks. The wall of sound paradox: maximum sonic information, minimum dynamic contrast.
- **Self-released** -- Loomer operates without label infrastructure, continuing the self-released thread from Pony Time (116) and Gold Casio (98). The bedroom-to-venue pipeline. Shoegaze as a genre has always been production-intensive, making self-release a statement about what's now possible with home recording.

- FIRST Anatidae -- the duck family. Anatidae is one of the largest bird families, encompassing ducks, geese, and swans. The Harlequin Duck (*Histrionicus histrionicus*) is a dramatic opener -- boldly patterned in slate blue, chestnut, and white. Named after the Harlequin of commedia dell'arte for its theatrical coloring. The species name *histrionicus* means "actor" -- theatrical twice over.
- Harlequin Duck habitat: fast-flowing mountain streams in breeding season (connecting to the Dipper's habitat at 116-117), rocky coastline in winter. A bird of turbulent water. Squeak and high whistle are surprisingly delicate calls for a bird that navigates rapids.
