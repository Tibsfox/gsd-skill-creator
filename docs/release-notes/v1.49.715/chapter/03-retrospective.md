## Surprises

**Path (a) sub-agent dispatch under explicit audit produced a substrate-vocab-density comparable to path (c) main-context authorship.** The expectation was that sub-agent dispatch might produce higher substrate-vocab density due to the dispatch prompt's structural emphasis on substrate-anchors. In practice, the abstract behavioral guidance in the dispatch prompt (positive framing; vocabulary discipline; substrate-vocab density target similar to v714 baseline ~0.04/word) was sufficient to keep the deliverables in the healthy band. The brief carried the topic-specific framing, the dispatch prompt carried the behavioral discipline, and the sub-agent's authored output sat between them.

**The HMI-SOHO-MDI lineage thread is the strongest substrate-cumulative claim in the milestone, but the storytelling required pulling SOHO MDI history into the SDO narrative.** The expectation was that the lineage thread would be a structural-substrate observation rather than a narrative substrate. In practice, the lineage thread required ~1,500 words of MDI-and-then-HMI storytelling in the story artifact to communicate why the architectural continuity matters scientifically. The architectural choice (Michelson-tunable-filter interferometer) is itself the narrative subject — not merely a parameter.

**Three of three instruments share PI institute clustering around Stanford-Lockheed.** Pre-author expectation was that the 3-instrument suite would have three distinct PI institutes. In practice, AIA's PI substrate is LMSAL + Stanford-Lockheed Institute, HMI's is Stanford University + Stanford-Lockheed Institute, and only EVE's is at LASP (Boulder). This is substrate-form-distinct from v714 (where 9 instruments spread across 7 institutions including 2 countries) and is consistent with the SDO three-instrument suite being deliberately concentrated around Stanford + LMSAL solar-physics expertise.

**Atlas V 401 zero-strap-on configuration substrate is substrate-form-distinct from v714 Delta II 7920-8 by simplicity rather than complexity.** Pre-author expectation was that the Atlas V launch profile would be more complex than the Delta II profile because Atlas V is a larger family. In practice, the 401 configuration is the lowest-thrust Atlas V variant with zero solid-rocket boosters and a single-engine Centaur — operationally simpler than the v714 Delta II 7920-8 with nine GEM-40 strap-ons in two ignition phases. The launch substrate-form difference compounds at the spacecraft side: SDO's ~3,000 kg launch mass and trans-orbit injection requirement is well-matched to the Atlas V 401 envelope.

## Lessons Learned

**Path (a) sub-agent dispatch under explicit dispatch-prompt-density audit is the new default for milestones with operator capacity availability + Lesson #10407 candidate sustainment requirement.** v712 + v713 + v714 established path (c) main-context authorship as the standard primary path for clean topics + bandwidth. v715 establishes path (a) sub-agent dispatch under explicit audit as the standard primary path when Lesson #10407 candidate sustainment is the operator-capacity-driver. The four-path model now distinguishes (a) audited dispatch + (b) recovery dispatch + (c) main-context + (d) main-context recovery, with (a) and (c) as the standard primary paths under different operator-capacity-availability conditions.

**Lesson #10407 candidate sustained at obs#3 cumulative.** v713 first observation of dispatch-prompt-density failure; v714 first ship under codified discipline (path c); v715 first audit-anchor under explicit dispatch-prompt-density audit (path a). The candidate now has three observations: one failure, one path-c sidestep, one path-a clean audit-anchor. The promotion-to-ESTABLISHED threshold typically requires three-to-five clean observations under the discipline; one more clean ship under similar conditions would meet the promotion threshold.

**MEASUREMENT-ARCHITECTURE-CONTINUATION-THREAD is a new pattern within substrate-axes.** The HMI-SOHO-MDI lineage is the first measurement-architecture-continuation thread within the SOLAR-OBSERVATORY axis, but it is also the first measurement-architecture-continuation thread within any substrate-axis registered to date. The pattern is: two missions within the same substrate-axis deliberately share the architecture of a particular measurement (here Michelson-tunable-filter interferometer for Doppler + magnetogram), producing a substrate-cumulative archive across both. The pattern is registerable, traceable across forward axis entries (Solar Orbiter PHI), and methodologically distinct from substrate-form-distinct subform claims.

**Substrate-form-distinct dimensional analysis scales with axis-entry count.** The v713 analysis was 7 dimensions (one predecessor); v714 was 9 dimensions (two predecessors); v715 is 10 dimensions (three predecessors). The dimensional count growth suggests that as the axis accumulates substrate-form-distinct entries, the comparison-set expansion adds substrate-form-distinct dimensions rather than reducing them. The v715 added two dimensions over v714: HMI-SOHO-MDI lineage substrate-cumulative (substrate-cumulative not substrate-form-distinct, but registered as a dimension) and operations-center integration. Future axis entries will continue to add dimensions.

**Substrate-vocab density tuning continues to work as a forward control.** The v715 brief came in within the healthy band, deliberately tuned by selecting concise instrument descriptions and abbreviating cumulative substrate enumerations. The path (a) sub-agent dispatch under explicit audit then produced deliverables that inherited the brief's density discipline. The continuum mapping continues to predict trip risk reliably across v710-v715 brief authorship.
