## Structural firsts

**EROSITA-FIRST-INSTANCE:** eROSITA (extended ROentgen Survey with an Imaging Telescope Array) enters the catalog as the German MPE-built X-ray survey telescope (funded by DLR and the Max Planck Society, PI Peter Predehl at launch, Andrea Merloni from 2020) aboard the Russian Spektr-RG spacecraft (NPO Lavochkin Navigator bus, Roscosmos / IKI) — the first instance, the obs#1 first — sustaining the X-ray-astronomy axis at obs#12. COSPAR 2019-040A; SATCAT 44432. Launched 13 July 2019, 12:31 UTC, on a Proton-M / Blok DM-03 from Baikonur Cosmodrome Site 81/24, Kazakhstan, into a halo orbit around Sun-Earth L2 (~1.5 million km anti-solar). Seven co-aligned Wolter Type I X-ray mirror assemblies (54 nested shells each, Media Lario optics, 1600 mm focal length) feed back-illuminated frame-store pn-CCD detectors across 0.2–8 keV. eROSITA is a GERMAN instrument on a RUSSIAN spacecraft — NOT a NASA mission; NASA Marshall Space Flight Center fabricated the seven flight mirror modules for the COMPANION ART-XC hard-X-ray telescope under a reimbursable agreement with IKI, and NASA HEASARC archives the German western-sky data — eROSITA itself carries zero NASA hardware. The mission performed the eROSITA All-Sky Survey (eRASS1–eRASS4, Dec 2019 – Dec 2021; eRASS5 partial), the largest and deepest all-sky X-ray imaging survey to date, until German operations were placed in safe mode on 26 February 2022.

**LARGEST-ALL-SKY-X-RAY-SOURCE-CATALOG-SURVEY-FIRST-INSTANCE:** The eRASS1 western-Galactic-hemisphere catalog (Merloni et al. 2024, A&A 682, A34; DOI 10.1051/0004-6361/202347165) lists nearly 930,000 X-ray sources (930,203 exact; 903,521 point + 26,682 extended; 0.2–2.3 keV; detection-likelihood ≥ 6) over ~13,116 deg² — the largest X-ray source catalog ever compiled from a single all-sky survey instrument, and the first all-sky X-ray survey to exceed 100,000 sources in a single hemisphere. The hard-band eRASS1HARD catalog (2.3–5.0 keV, 5,466 sources) is the first true all-sky imaging survey of the sky above 2 keV. The complete eight-scan eRASS program (eRASS:8 design) was designed to reach ~25× the sensitivity of the ROSAT All-Sky Survey in the 0.2–2.3 keV band. Discipline: ~930,000 is the WESTERN HALF-SKY count (the eastern hemisphere belongs to IKI / Russia and has not been released) — always qualified as half-sky / western Galactic hemisphere; the ~25× factor is the FULL eRASS:8 design target, never eRASS1 alone (eRASS1 per scan is ~2.5–4× deeper than ROSAT RASS); the count is ~930,000, never ~927,000.

**GALACTIC-CENTER-X-RAY-BUBBLE-BILATERAL-SYSTEM-FIRST-INSTANCE:** eROSITA produced the first unambiguous detection and mapping of the complete bilateral hourglass X-ray bubble system centered on the Galactic center (Predehl et al. 2020, Nature 588, 227; DOI 10.1038/s41586-020-2979-0): two giant cavities each extending ~14 kpc above and below the Galactic plane (~80–85° angular extent), with sharp shock boundaries, revealing the X-ray thermal forward-shock counterpart (~0.3 keV shocked gas) to the gamma-ray Fermi Bubbles. The anchor is the detection of the COMPLETE BILATERAL SYSTEM — the southern counterpart bubble and the unified hourglass morphology are genuinely new; it is NOT "the first X-ray detection" of bubble emission (the North Polar Spur / Loop I had been visible in ROSAT data since the 1990s, and Sofue 2000 proposed a Galactic-center bipolar interpretation using ROSAT data). The eROSITA Bubbles and the Fermi Bubbles share a common Galactic-center outflow origin but are physically and morphologically distinct envelopes (thermal X-ray ~12–14 kpc vs non-thermal gamma-ray ~8–10 kpc) — never the same structure seen in two bands.

**CCD-IMAGING-ALL-SKY-X-RAY-SURVEY-FIRST-INSTANCE:** eROSITA performed the first purpose-designed all-sky X-ray survey with CCD-based focal-plane detectors: back-illuminated, fully-depleted, frame-store pn-CCDs delivering ~6% energy resolution at 1.5 keV (~92 eV FWHM at Al-K), a generation advance over the ROSAT PSPC gas proportional counter (~41% at 1 keV), and extending the imaging all-sky survey out to 8 keV where ROSAT (0.1–2.4 keV) was blind. ## Lessons Learned

1. **A CCD-detector all-sky survey is a distinct observable form from an imaging gas-proportional-counter survey.** ROSAT (obs#11) performed the first imaging all-sky survey with a Wolter telescope feeding a position-sensitive proportional counter (~41% energy resolution at 1 keV). eROSITA (obs#12) advanced the survey/catalog branch to back-illuminated frame-store pn-CCD focal planes (~6% at 1.5 keV) and extended it to 8 keV — a generation advance the four-way partition must keep distinct.
2. **The four-way partition (Uhuru / Einstein / ROSAT / eROSITA) must be stated explicitly in every survey-first claim.** "First all-sky survey" re-stamps Uhuru; "first imaging telescope" re-stamps Einstein; "first imaging all-sky survey" re-stamps ROSAT. The correct scope for eROSITA is always "first purpose-designed all-sky survey with CCD detectors" and "the largest all-sky X-ray source catalog to date."
3. **German-Russian framing is an accuracy requirement, not a qualifier.** eROSITA is a German MPE instrument aboard the Russian Spektr-RG spacecraft, not a NASA mission; NASA's hardware contribution is confined to the ART-XC mirror modules (reimbursable agreement). Every identification of eROSITA must open with "German MPE instrument aboard the Russian Spektr-RG" before naming the NASA ART-XC contribution.
4. **High-priority numeric and hardware errors recur in secondary sources.** Blok DM-03 (not Briz-M); Media Lario (not Carl Zeiss); pn-CCD (not proportional counter); CdTe DSSD for ART-XC (not silicon, not CCD); ~16–18″ HEW (not ~5–6″ pointed); 6 rev/day (4-hour period, not 4 rev/day); ~930,000 (not ~927,000). Each was stated correctly at every occurrence.
5. **Sensitivity and sky-coverage discipline prevents a recurring overclaim.** eRASS1 is ~2.5–4× deeper than ROSAT; the ~25× factor is the full eRASS:8 design. The ~930,000-source catalog is the WESTERN HALF-SKY count (eastern hemisphere belongs to IKI / Russia, unreleased). Both qualifiers are carried at every occurrence.
6. **The eROSITA Bubbles anchor must encode "complete bilateral system," not "first X-ray detection."** The North Polar Spur / Loop I was in ROSAT data; Sofue 2000 proposed a Galactic-center bipolar interpretation from ROSAT data. eROSITA's first is the unified bilateral hourglass system (the southern counterpart bubble + the unified morphology), and the eROSITA and Fermi Bubbles are co-origin but physically distinct.
7. **Boundary firsts belong to other missions.** The first X-ray detection of cosmic-web filaments belongs to ROSAT/Tanimura 2020 (eROSITA = highest significance only); the first cluster-cosmology constraints predate eROSITA (eROSITA = tightest to date); the CXB was discovered by the 1962 AS&E rocket and Einstein owns CXB source-resolution. None are re-stamped.
8. **A continuation keeps predecessors' shared vocabulary and references them by name.** The X-ray-astronomy vocabulary (Wolter Type I, grazing-incidence, focusing optics, soft X-ray, all-sky survey, source catalog, cosmic X-ray background, intracluster medium, galaxy cluster, AGN, neutron star, Scorpius X-1, Giacconi and Rossi, the 1962 founding) is correct and kept. Chandra through ROSAT are referenced as the form complements. Only predecessor-specific hardware and anchors stay with their missions.
9. **The suspension is operational, not terminal.** German operations were placed in safe mode on 26 February 2022; the hardware remains physically aboard and is not permanently powered off, and the companion ART-XC continued operating independently. The safe-mode event is stated as a suspension of German operations, never as a loss of the instrument.
10. **Clone source is ROSAT (gas proportional counter, LEO); eROSITA is CCD at L2.** The most structural replacement in the rewrite is removing ALL "Röntgensatellit," "Delta II / SLC-17A," "Carl Zeiss," "four Wolter shells," "PSPC / HRI / WFC," "RASS / 1RXS ~124,735," "EUV / WFC," "Hyakutake," "XDINS / Magnificent Seven," "Trümper," "1990 / LEO / spin-or-3-axis-LEO," and "tri-national German/NASA/UK" language and replacing with eROSITA's CCD detectors, seven 54-shell Media Lario mirror assemblies, Proton-M / Blok DM-03 / Baikonur, L2 halo orbit, eRASS1–eRASS4, ~930,000-source catalog, eROSITA Bubbles, ART-XC companion, and the German-Russian (NASA-ART-XC-mirrors-only) architecture.

## Surprises

- **A German instrument on a Russian spacecraft — not a NASA mission — produced the largest all-sky X-ray catalog to date.** eROSITA was built and operated by Germany's MPE (funded by DLR and the Max Planck Society) and flew aboard the Russian Spektr-RG, with NASA's contribution confined to the seven mirror modules of the companion ART-XC; the eRASS1 western-half-sky catalog lists nearly 930,000 sources, the largest ever compiled from a single all-sky survey instrument.
- **The all-sky survey came from a CCD focal plane a generation beyond ROSAT.** Back-illuminated, fully-depleted, frame-store pn-CCDs delivered ~6% energy resolution at 1.5 keV — versus the ROSAT PSPC's ~41% at 1 keV — and extended the imaging survey to 8 keV, where ROSAT (0.1–2.4 keV) was blind: a per-photon spectral capability that turned the focal plane into a wide-field imaging spectrometer.
- **A complete bilateral bubble system was hiding in the soft X-ray sky.** The North Polar Spur had been visible in ROSAT data for decades, but eROSITA's medium-energy imaging revealed the southern counterpart bubble and the unified hourglass morphology centered on the Galactic center — two ~14 kpc cavities of ~0.3 keV thermal gas, the X-ray thermal forward-shock counterpart to the gamma-ray Fermi Bubbles.
- **A whole class of recurring X-ray flares emerged once a survey could revisit the sky.** Before eROSITA only two quasi-periodic-eruption sources were known; eROSITA became the primary discovery engine, finding eRO-QPE1 through QPE5 — recurring soft X-ray flares from compact objects near galactic nuclei, a phenomenon a repeated all-sky survey is uniquely suited to catch.
- **An owl that surveys the whole dark field with a sound-focusing collector.** The Barn Owl (*Tyto alba*) quarters open ground in slow, systematic low sweeps, its heart-shaped facial disc collecting and focusing faint sounds onto asymmetric ear openings — the most acute directional hearing measured in any animal — to locate prey it cannot see, a faint-signal-focusing, systematic-survey architecture that mirrors eROSITA's Wolter mirrors focusing faint X-ray photons onto pn-CCDs across the whole sky.
- **A fern that catalogs its spores in a chain across every frond.** The Giant Chain Fern (*Woodwardia fimbriata*), the largest native fern of western North America, arrays its sori in a regular double row along each vein — a systematic, repeated, cataloged structure across the whole frond, evoking the scale and depth of eROSITA's largest all-sky X-ray source catalog, ~930,000 detections arrayed systematically across the western sky.

### What Could Be Better

- **The shader renders procedural geometry rather than archived survey data.** The CCD-survey shader uses analytic curves and procedural noise rather than loading actual eRASS1 catalog source positions on the celestial sphere. A future revision could render the real ~930,000-source eRASS1 positions (publicly available from HEASARC for the western sky) for a higher-fidelity view of the imaged sky.
- **The eROSITA diorama is a forthcoming artifact.** The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the Spektr-RG spacecraft with the seven hexagonally-packed Wolter mirror assemblies, the ART-XC concentrators, and the solar panels.
- **The eROSITA Bubbles are described but not computed.** The two ~14 kpc cavities, the ~0.3 keV thermal forward shock, and the bilateral hourglass morphology are described but not rendered as a computed all-sky map; a future ship could integrate a published bubble model into the shader's Bubbles mode for a historically accurate view.
- **The galaxy-cluster cosmology result is described rather than demonstrated.** The 5,259-cluster cosmology subsample and the tightest-to-date constraints (Ghirardini et al. 2024) are cited but not demonstrated as a model; a future ship could render the cluster-abundance constraint as a shader or interactive figure.

## Dedication

v1.234 eROSITA is dedicated to the people who built and flew the extended ROentgen Survey with an Imaging Telescope Array — the German telescope that scanned the whole sky in soft and medium X-rays with CCD focal planes for the first time: Peter Predehl at the Max-Planck-Institut für extraterrestrische Physik, who led the instrument from concept to launch, and Andrea Merloni, who carried it through the eRASS surveys and the 2024 catalog release; the MPE and HLL semiconductor teams, who built the back-illuminated frame-store pn-CCDs that turned the focal plane into a wide-field imaging spectrometer; Media Lario Technologies in Italy, who electroformed the 54-shell Wolter mirror assemblies; the Esther Bulbul and Vittorio Ghirardini cluster teams, who mapped 12,247 galaxy clusters and drew the tightest cosmological constraints to date from their abundance; Mikhail Pavlinsky and the IKI team, whose companion ART-XC carried NASA Marshall's mirrors into the hard band; and Riccardo Giacconi and Bruno Rossi, who founded the field with the 1962 rocket. From a halo orbit at Sun-Earth L2, the X-ray sky was cataloged anew — nearly 930,000 sources across the western half-sky, mapped in focused light.
