The streak at four demonstrates that moderate-continuation streaks can document real within-family diversity at meaningful depth without becoming formulaic, and the streak remains open for future extension with other Pacific Northwest Anatidae species.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Post-milestone normal-operation degrees are their own structural category deserving first-class documentation.** Degree 121 is the first degree past the one-third structural milestone at degree 120, and its job was not to mark or to inaugurate but to continue. Post-milestone normal-operation degrees demonstrate that engine cadence resumes after structurally weighted moments, and without normal-operation degrees the engine would collapse into undifferentiated weight. Future post-milestone degrees (the first degree past 180, 270, 360, and other milestones) should receive the same normal-operation documentation template.
2. **Moderate-continuation streaks extend beyond three when within-family diversity is genuine rather than artificial.** The Anatidae streak extended from three (118-120 at Hooded Merganser's entry) to four (118-121 at Wood Duck's entry) without becoming formulaic because each of the four consecutive waterfowl documented distinct genera, acoustic signatures, morphological profiles, and feeding ecologies. Future moderate-continuation streaks should extend only when within-family diversity is genuine; formulaic extension without real diversity would erode the streak-documentation pattern's analytical value.
3. **Neighborhoods are permanent engine features while artists are rotating populations.** Capitol Hill's return at degree 121 reinforced a structural principle: neighborhood infrastructure (venues, studios, apartments, sidewalks) persists across decades while the artists using the infrastructure rotate through careers. Future neighborhood documentation should treat the neighborhood as a recurring anchor rather than as a first entry, and the permanence-rotation distinction should be a consistent design principle across all geographic documentation.
4. **Ecological cohabitation networks extend across multiple degrees as species enter the catalog.** The cavity-nesting cohabitation network (Hooded Merganser + Wood Duck + Barrow's Goldeneye) extended from two to three species when Wood Duck entered at degree 121, and it will extend to full three-species documentation when Barrow's Goldeneye enters. Future ecological networks (forest-canopy networks, salmon-predator networks, pollinator-plant networks) should receive the same multi-degree network-extension treatment, documenting each entry as it joins the network rather than trying to introduce the whole network at a single degree.
5. **Energy recovery from extreme values takes multiple degrees rather than a single step.** The artist-axis four-degree recovery arc (Loomer 118 E=3 descent, Hollis Resnik 119 E=2 valley, Mark Lanegan 120 E=4 milestone recovery, Smokey Brights 121 E=5 normal-operation) documented that recovery from an E=2 extreme takes multiple degrees. Future recovery arcs from either direction (recovery from E=6 or E=7 peaks, recovery from E=2 valleys) should receive multi-degree documentation rather than being compressed into single-degree transitions.
6. **Deepening existing patterns is the post-milestone mode of operation rather than introducing new patterns.** Degree 121 extended the Capitol Hill neighborhood presence, the self-released DIY pipeline, the Anatidae streak, and the cavity-nesting cohabitation network — without introducing any wholly new patterns. The post-milestone emphasis on deepening is a design principle that distinguishes post-milestone degrees from inaugural degrees, and future post-milestone degrees should continue the deepening emphasis.
7. **Aesthetic surface beauty is a valid diagnostic category alongside acoustic intensity and morphological rarity.** Smokey Brights' shimmering synthesizer textures and Wood Duck's ornate plumage both document aesthetic surface beauty — warm production beauty on the artist side, painted-plumage beauty on the species side. The parallel documentation at both axes of a single degree established the category, and future entries can populate it at additional positions. Aesthetic surface beauty belongs alongside acoustic intensity (high-E degrees) and morphological rarity (fan-shaped crest type features) in the engine's diagnostic-category inventory.
8. **Conservation recovery is a first-class engine theme paired with energy recovery as thematic coherence.** Wood Duck's near-extinction-and-nest-box-enabled recovery paired with the artist-axis four-degree energy recovery arc documented recovery as a first-class engine theme. The coincidence of conservation recovery (species side) with energy recovery (artist side) at degree 121 is a structural finding — the engine documents resilience and return at both axes, and the parallel recovery narratives deepen thematic coherence. Future degrees should look for parallel recovery themes across the two axes as a consistent analytical frame.
9. **DIY production ecosystems are persistent engine features deserving multi-degree documentation.** The self-released DIY pipeline first documented at Gold Casio (degree 98) continued through numerous intermediate artists across twenty-three intermediate degrees to Smokey Brights at degree 121. The pipeline's continuity across twenty-three degrees establishes it as a persistent engine feature. Future DIY production ecosystems (community radio, mutual aid networks, open-source technical communities, small-press publishing networks) should receive the same multi-degree documentation treatment.
10. **Specific phenomenon documentation (cavity-leap duckling, shimmering synth, rising ooo-eek, etc.) adds to the engine's behavioral and acoustic catalog across degrees.** Each degree contributes specific phenomenon documentation — Wood Duck's cavity-leap duckling phenomenon, Smokey Brights' shimmering synth, the rising ooo-eek flight call — and the accumulating phenomenon catalog becomes a resource for future degree documentation. Future entries should reference prior phenomenon documentation when similar phenomena appear, and the phenomenon catalog itself should be periodically consolidated into retrospective artifacts.
