## Wood Duck -- The Most Beautiful Duck

The Wood Duck (*Aix sponsa*) is widely considered the most beautiful duck in North America. The male's plumage is extraordinary -- iridescent green and purple head with a swept-back crest, bold white chin strap, chestnut breast with white flecks, buff flanks, and an intricate face pattern that looks painted. The species name *sponsa* means "bride" or "betrothed" -- Linnaeus named it for its ornate plumage, as if dressed for a wedding.

The **rising ooo-eek** is the female Wood Duck's flight call -- a sharp, upward-inflected squealing whistle that carries across wooded ponds and flooded timber. Unmistakable once heard. The male's call is a thinner, rising whistle. Both are higher-pitched and more urgent than the merganser calls at degrees 119-120.

Habitat: **wooded ponds**. The Wood Duck is a tree-nesting duck -- it nests in cavities in trees near water, often quite high above the ground. Day-old ducklings leap from the nest cavity to the ground below, sometimes falling 50+ feet. The species nearly went extinct in the early 20th century due to habitat loss and hunting, then recovered dramatically through nest box programs and habitat protection. A conservation success story.

E=4. The rising ooo-eek has urgency but not volume. A carrying call, not a loud one.

Anatidae streak at 4: Harlequin Duck (118), Common Merganser (119), Hooded Merganser (120), Wood Duck (121). Four consecutive waterfowl, four different genera, four different acoustic signatures. The engine is working through the duck family systematically, each species a different voice.
