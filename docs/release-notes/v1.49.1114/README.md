## Dedication

v1.297 Aqua is dedicated to the people at NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center, at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory, and at the international partners — Brazil's INPE, who built HSB, and Japan's JAXA, who built AMSR-E — who designed and flew the second flagship of the Earth Observing System. The teams who placed six instruments on one afternoon-orbit platform — AIRS, AMSU-A, HSB, AMSR-E, CERES, and MODIS — so that the atmosphere's temperature and water vapor, the rain and the sea ice and the temperature of the sea surface, the radiation budget, and the daylit globe could all be read at once. The crews who carried the satellite aloft on a Delta II from Vandenberg into its afternoon orbit at the head of the A-Train, and the engineers who have kept it observing for more than two decades, well past its planned life. The scientists who built the long daily record of the planet's water — its vapor, its clouds, its precipitation, its sea ice — measurement after measurement, day after day. From 705 km the satellite still reads the afternoon side of the planet, and that long record of the living water belongs to them.
