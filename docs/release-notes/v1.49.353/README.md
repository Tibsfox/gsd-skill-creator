## Summary

**Cumulus** arrives at E=4 -- and with them, **FIRST DREAM POP enters the Seattle 360 engine as Genre 109**. Alexandra Niedzialkowski's project is built on layers of reverb-drenched guitars, ethereal vocals, and atmospheric production that transforms indie rock into something weightless and luminous. Albums like *I Never Meant It to Be Like This* (2017) capture the genre's essential quality: music that feels like it's being heard through fog, where melody dissolves into texture and texture dissolves into feeling. At E=4, Cumulus sits at dream pop's natural energy level -- the genre is defined by restraint, by the space between notes, by the willingness to let a chord ring until it disappears. Dream pop is the 109th genre in the engine, and its arrival here -- after the Canidae quartet's wild energy -- functions as a deliberate atmospheric reset. On the species side, the **Cougar scream** registers at E=7 -- and this entry is a **double landmark**: the **FIRST FELIDAE** (cat family) in the SPS engine and the return of E=7, matching the Gray Wolf howl (210) and Roosevelt Elk bugle/rut roar (197/199). The Cougar (*Puma concolor*) is Washington's largest native cat and the Pacific Northwest's apex solitary predator. The Cougar scream is one of the most blood-curdling sounds in North American wildlife: a high-pitched, wavering shriek that has been compared to a woman screaming, produced primarily by females during estrus to attract mates across vast territories. It is a sound that stops hikers in their tracks and has generated centuries of folklore. The Cougar scream at E=7 closes this eight-degree stretch with maximum predatory impact.

- Cougar scream acoustics -- the Cougar scream is produced at frequencies between 500-1500 Hz, with a duration of 1-2 seconds and a wavering quality caused by rapid modulation; the sound propagates effectively through forested terrain, carrying 2-3 miles under favorable conditions; it is most commonly heard during winter breeding season (December-March)
- Apex solitary predator -- the Cougar is the largest native cat in Washington, with adult males weighing 140-200 lbs and maintaining territories of 100-300 square miles; unlike wolves (who hunt cooperatively), Cougars are obligate solitary hunters, using ambush and pursuit over short distances; the scream is one of the few Cougar vocalizations that carries at landscape scale
