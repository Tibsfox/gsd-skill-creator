## Nasty Nes Rodriguez -- The First Radio Personality

Nasty Nes Rodriguez is the first entry in 126 degrees whose primary contribution to Seattle music is **radio broadcasting**. The engine has included musicians, producers, DJs, and label figures -- but never before a radio personality. Nasty Nes hosted **Fresh Tracks** on **KCMU** (the University of Washington's radio station, now KEXP), the first dedicated hip-hop program on Seattle radio.

Based in **Rainier Valley** -- the neighborhood that would become ground zero for Seattle hip-hop. The connection between Rainier Valley's emerging hip-hop scene and the broader city ran through KCMU's transmitter. Nasty Nes gave **Sir Mix-a-Lot** his first radio airplay. He championed local hip-hop when no one else on Seattle radio would touch it. The cultural math is simple: without the radio play, the scene stays in the neighborhood. With it, the city hears.

E=4. The energy level after E=7 (TAAS, degree 125) drops by 3 -- another significant descent. Radio energy is different from performance energy. It is the energy of curation, selection, advocacy. The voice behind the music, not the music itself.

**KCMU** now becomes a structural thread. Degree 126 begins a **radio trilogy**: Nasty Nes Rodriguez (126, hip-hop radio), Jonathan Poneman (127, KCMU DJ and Sub Pop co-founder), Bruce Pavitt (128, KCMU DJ and Sub Pop co-founder). Three figures, one radio station, three different pathways from the broadcast booth to the cultural infrastructure of Seattle music.

## Canvasback -- Speed and Silence

The Canvasback (*Aythya valisineria*) is a large diving duck found on marsh lakes and open water across the Pacific Northwest. The largest of the North American diving ducks, recognizable by its sloping profile -- the forehead runs in an unbroken line from the crown to the tip of the bill. Built for hydrodynamics.

The sound: low grunts. Quiet, infrequent vocalizations -- cooing and grunting during courtship, mostly silent otherwise. E=3. Another quiet Anatidae entry. The family's acoustic range in this streak has been remarkably varied in character but often modest in energy: squeaks, whistles, croaks, frog-calls, rising whistles, peeping, grunts, mechanical wing sounds, scaup calls, and now more grunts. Anatidae are not songbirds. Their sounds are functional, not performative.

The headline stat: **72 mph in flight** -- the fastest duck in North America. The Canvasback is built for long-distance migration. Large body, heavy breast muscles, tapered wings. The speed is not a behavioral choice; it is an aerodynamic consequence of the body plan. A bird optimized for covering distance.

**Anatidae streak at 9** -- tying the **Picidae nonet** (degrees 106-114, nine consecutive woodpecker-family species) for the **ALL-TIME FAMILY RECORD**. The waterfowl have now matched the woodpeckers. One more and they break the record.
