## Summary

**Presidents of the United States of America** arrive at E=6 -- the band that built its entire sonic identity around invented instruments and categorical refusal to take rock music too seriously, and in doing so created one of the most genuinely original sounds to emerge from Seattle in the 1990s. Chris Ballew played a **basitar** -- a two-string bass-guitar hybrid he invented -- and Dave Dederer played a **guitbass**, a three-string guitar tuned to bass range. The lineup was completed by Jason Finn on drums. The result was a stripped-down, almost cartoonish sound that had no precedent in the grunge world: no distortion wall, no weight, no angst. Songs about peaches, moving to the country, little yellow spiders, and lump. The debut album went platinum. The Presidents were the counter-move to the Seattle sound's dominant register -- proof that the same city could produce both the heaviest and the most playfully absurd rock records of the same era, and that both were authentically Seattle. Their arrival at E=6 is consistent with a band that is energetic and immediate without reaching the aggression levels of the post-grunge and proto-punk material surrounding them.

On the species side, **Coho Salmon** enters the Salmonidae arc with an **aggressive splash** at E=6, matching the Presidents' energy level in a direct acoustic correspondence. *Oncorhynchus kisutch* is the salmon of the spawning channel, the hatchery, the urban stream -- the species that adapted more readily than Chinook to the fragmented and modified watersheds of the lowland Pacific Northwest. But Coho carry their own crisis: **6PPD-quinone**, a tire rubber chemical that washes off roads into stormwater and into streams, kills adult Coho at concentrations below what any regulatory standard had predicted. The discovery of 6PPD-quinone toxicity in 2020 was one of the most significant environmental findings in recent Pacific Northwest history -- it explained why Coho were dying in urban streams during rain events, a phenomenon researchers had documented for years without identifying the cause. The Coho's aggressive splash in degree 238 is not only an acoustic entry; it is an entry that carries the weight of what urban development and road infrastructure have done to the salmon that managed to survive everything else the 20th century threw at them.

## Key Features

- **Part A -- S36:** Presidents of the USA (Alt Rock, E=6) -- basitar/guitbass invented instruments, platinum debut, playful counter-signal to Seattle grunge's dominant register
- **Part B -- SPS:** Coho Salmon (aggressive splash, E=6) -- Salmonidae arc continues, 6PPD-quinone urban stormwater crisis, hatchery adaptability
- Basitar and guitbass instruments -- Chris Ballew's basitar (two strings) and Dave Dederer's guitbass (three strings tuned to bass range) created a tonal space that no other Seattle band occupied; the instruments produced a sound that was simultaneously simpler and stranger than conventional guitar/bass arrangements; the thinness of the low-string approach gave PUSA their characteristic hollow, almost toy-like bottom end; this was not accidental -- Ballew has described the instrument choices as deliberate efforts to strip away everything that wasn't necessary, and the result was a band that sounded like nothing else on the radio in 1995
- 6PPD-quinone and the Coho stormwater problem -- researchers at the University of Washington and the Washington State Department of Ecology identified 6PPD-quinone as the toxicant responsible for coho salmon prespawning mortality in 2020; the chemical forms when 6PPD, an antiozonant used in tire rubber to prevent cracking, reacts with ozone; it washes off tires during rain events into stormwater and into streams at concentrations that kill adult Coho within hours of exposure; the discovery has prompted regulatory action and filter strip research, but the problem is structural -- it is built into the chemistry of every tire on every road in the region
- Coho ecology and urban adaptation -- Coho are the salmon most likely to be found in urban and suburban streams in the Puget Sound lowlands; they colonize smaller watersheds, tolerate higher water temperatures than Chinook, and use hatchery supplementation extensively; some urban streams in King County support natural Coho spawning within sight of apartment buildings and shopping centers; the species' urban presence made the 6PPD-quinone discovery both more visible and more urgently documented than it might have been for a species confined to remote wilderness rivers
- E=6 acoustic match -- Presidents of the USA at E=6 and Coho Salmon at E=6 create a rare exact energy correspondence between the engine's two tracks; both entries are loud without being aggressive, immediate without being heavy; the pairing reflects the engine's tendency to find acoustic resonances across its two parallel streams even when the sources are completely unrelated
