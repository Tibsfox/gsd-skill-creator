## Mission Architecture: Aditya-L1

Aditya-L1 launched **2023-09-02 06:50 UTC** (11:50 IST) from Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SDSC SHAR) Sriharikota Andhra Pradesh India on **PSLV-XL configuration PSLV-C57** from the Second Launch Pad. The substrate-form launch placed Aditya-L1 in an initial elliptical Earth orbit of approximately 235 × 19,500 km. Four earth-bound burn maneuvers (EBN-1 through EBN-4) progressively raised the apogee across mid-September 2023. On **2023-09-19** the Trans-Lagrangian Insertion burn (TL1) placed the spacecraft on its cruise trajectory toward Sun-Earth L1. After ~110 days of cruise, **L1 halo orbit insertion** completed on **2024-01-06** substrate-anchor (L1-HALO-ORBIT-INSERTION-2024-01-06 obs#1 first-instance NEW LOCKED at v712).

**Spacecraft prime:** U R Rao Satellite Centre (URSC) Bangalore ISRO in-house substrate-anchor — URSC-IN-HOUSE-SPACECRAFT-PRIME-SUBSTRATE-ANCHOR obs#1 first-instance NEW LOCKED at v712. ISRO's substrate-form spacecraft engineering centre handles bus design, subsystem engineering, and integration end-to-end for ISRO programs.

**Mission management:** Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). **Project Director:** Nigar Shaji ISRO URSC (NIGAR-SHAJI-PROJECT-DIRECTOR-ADITYA-L1 obs#1 NEW LOCKED at v712). **ISRO Chairman at launch:** S Somanath.

**Spacecraft mass + propulsion.** Total launch mass ~1,475 kg (~448 kg dry + ~1,027 kg propellant). The ~70% propellant fraction supports the substrate-form four-burn earth-bound apogee-raising sequence plus the Trans-Lagrangian Insertion burn plus the L1 halo orbit insertion burn plus periodic station-keeping across the 5-year nominal mission lifetime. **Propulsion:** liquid bipropellant; Liquid Apogee Motor (LAM) plus attitude-control thrusters.

**Solar power.** Three deployable solar panels generating ~440 W at 1 AU substrate. Sized for the substrate-form nominal L1 power budget across the 7-instrument science suite plus spacecraft bus plus communications.

**L1 halo orbit substrate.** The halo orbit measures approximately 200,000 × 600,000 km in the plane perpendicular to the Sun-Earth line. L1 substrate-form properties: continuous Sun observation (never eclipsed by Earth substrate); forward observation of solar-wind structures (~30-60 minutes before Earth substrate); gravitational metastability requiring periodic station-keeping (~once per several months substrate). Designed 5-year nominal mission lifetime.

**Communications.** S-band substrate for telemetry + telecommand; X-band substrate for science data downlink; routed via Indian Deep Space Network (IDSN) Byalalu near Bangalore + ISRO Telemetry Tracking and Command Network (ISTRAC) Bangalore substrate.

**Mission cost.** ~₹378.53 crore (~$48 million) substrate — approximately two orders of magnitude below prior axis cost envelopes (Europa Clipper ~$5.2 billion, JUICE ~$1.5 billion, Psyche ~$985 million).

## 7-Instrument Science Suite

- **Visible Emission Line Coronagraph (VELC)** — internally-occulted coronagraph + multi-slit spectrograph substrate; 1.05-3 solar radii field substrate; PI institute Indian Institute of Astrophysics (IIA), Bangalore substrate. Spectroscopic mode at 5303 Å (Fe XIV green line, ~2 million K plasma) + 7892 Å (Fe XI red line, ~1.3 million K plasma) + 10747 Å (Fe XIII near-infrared line, ~1.8 million K plasma). Primary VELC science: corona magnetic-field measurements + coronal eruption initiation diagnostics + coronal heating studies. Primary remote-sensing payload — VELC-VISIBLE-EMISSION-LINE-CORONAGRAPH-IIA-PRIMARY obs#1 NEW LOCKED at v712.
- **Solar Ultraviolet Imaging Telescope (SUIT)** — narrowband UV imaging substrate across 11 filter passbands spanning 200-400 nm; PI institute Inter-University Centre for Astronomy and Astrophysics (IUCAA), Pune. Photosphere + chromosphere ultraviolet imaging; chromosphere dynamics; transition-region characterization.
- **Solar Low Energy X-ray Spectrometer (SoLEXS)** — soft X-ray spectrometer substrate 1-30 keV; PI institute U R Rao Satellite Centre (URSC), Bangalore. Solar flare X-ray emission characterization.
- **High Energy L1 Orbiting X-ray Spectrometer (HEL1OS)** — hard X-ray spectrometer substrate 10-150 keV; PI institute ISRO Space Astronomy Group, URSC. Energetic flare physics; non-thermal particle acceleration diagnostics.
- **Aditya Solar Wind Particle Experiment (ASPEX)** — particle flux analyser substrate; electrons 0.1-5 keV + ions 100 eV to 20 keV + supra-thermal ions to 5 MeV/n; PI institute Physical Research Laboratory (PRL), Ahmedabad. Solar wind ion + electron flux measurements; in-situ solar-wind composition.
- **Plasma Analyser Package for Aditya (PAPA)** — plasma analyser substrate; PI institute Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre (VSSC), Thiruvananthapuram. Solar wind plasma composition + bulk velocity measurements.
- **Advanced Tri-axial High Resolution Digital Magnetometer (MAG)** — triaxial fluxgate magnetometer substrate; two sensors on 6-meter deployable boom for gradiometer-mode cancellation of spacecraft-generated field; PI institute Laboratory for Electro Optic Systems (LEOS), Bangalore. Interplanetary magnetic field measurements at L1.
