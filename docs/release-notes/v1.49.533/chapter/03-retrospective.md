# Retrospective — v1.49.533

## Lessons Learned

1. **E=5 crosses the S36 threshold.** After eleven consecutive E=4 degrees, the energy ceiling rises. Luther Rabb is the first artist the engine classifies above the plateau.

2. **The Ranger failure catalog collapses to one mode.** Ranger 4 and Ranger 6 share the same failure — cameras dead on arrival. A single mode repeating is worse than diversifying modes: it means no one has fixed it.

3. **Mercury Records takes a second artist.** The label thread accumulates not just in breadth (number of labels) but in depth (multiple artists per label).

4. **Central District 1970s is a documented layer.** Two consecutive historic-soul degrees (31, 32) prove the decade has independent weight alongside the 1960s (Dynamics) and the contemporary revival.

5. **Soul/R&B demands higher energy than jazz did.** The engine's pure-jazz degrees never crossed E=4; the R&B degrees break through immediately. The quadrant's acoustic character is intrinsically louder.

6. **Convergence can stall.** The Ranger program has converged toward the Moon (you can hit it now), but the science payload has not converged with the platform. Two separate subsystems, two separate convergence curves.
