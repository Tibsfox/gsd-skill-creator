# Retrospective — v1.49.533

## What Worked

- **Dual-threshold-crossing as a degree organizing principle.** Both Part A and Part B crossed E=5 on the same degree. Structurally coherent, narratively clean, and establishes a new ceiling without artifice.
- **The payload-activation / conditional-presence mirror.** The Ranger 6 cameras and the Bohemian Waxwing's irruptive presence share a structural condition -- reaching the target does not guarantee functional output. The mirror was present in the source material and the degree narrative successfully elevated it to explicit engine-level structure.
- **Central District 1970s layer documentation.** The Eager/Rabb pair (degrees 31-32) establishes the 1970s layer as independent strata, which had been missing from the neighborhood's musical archaeology.
- **Bombycillidae pair completion.** The family-pair pattern (Cedar at 31 + Bohemian at 32) extends cleanly from prior family treatments and produces the second engine-level family-pair instance.
- **Ranger failure catalog collapse framed as stall rather than progress.** The degree narrative successfully distinguishes progress-through-diverse-failures (early Ranger program) from stalling-through-repeated-failures (Ranger 4 + 6 sharing the camera-activation mode).

## What Could Be Better

- **Primary-source Rabb discography.** Luther Rabb's Mercury Records output deserves a discography verification pass beyond what fit into the degree window. Specific titles, session dates, and chart positions would strengthen the Part A prose.
- **Ranger 6 post-mortem detail.** The high-voltage arc in the camera power supply is the root cause per NASA's post-mission analysis, but the degree treatment could expand on the ground-test damage hypothesis and the design changes it triggered for Ranger 7.
- **Irruption-year quantification.** The Bohemian Waxwing's irruption cycle has decadal patterns that could be quantified (how many irruption years in the 1990-2020 window, typical flock sizes at PNW lowland arrival, return periods). Left qualitative here.
- **Central District 1980s gap.** The Central District 1970s layer is now documented but the 1980s and 1990s remain open. Future degrees should address the gap explicitly.
- **Aggregate-amplitude measurement.** The "collective E=5" call for the Bohemian Waxwing flock is a qualitative engine classification; an actual SPL measurement from a documented irruption flock would make the category quantitative.

## Lessons Learned

1. **E=5 crosses the S36 threshold.** After eleven consecutive E=4 degrees, the energy ceiling rose. Luther Rabb is the first artist the engine classifies above the plateau, and the break is categorical -- the R&B quadrant's true amplitude envelope was always wider than the first eleven E=4 R&B degrees had revealed.
2. **The Ranger failure catalog collapses to one mode.** Ranger 4 and Ranger 6 shared the same failure -- cameras dead on arrival. A single mode repeating is worse than diversifying modes: it means no one has fixed it. The collapse of the catalog is the signal the program had stalled.
3. **Mercury Records takes a second artist.** The label thread now accumulates not just in breadth (number of labels) but in depth (multiple artists per label). Two Mercury-signed Central District soul artists in consecutive degrees converts Mercury from a breadth data point into a depth thread.
4. **Central District 1970s is a documented layer.** Two consecutive historic-soul degrees (31, 32) prove the decade has independent weight alongside the 1960s (Dynamics) and the contemporary revival. The neighborhood's archaeology is now three-layered.
5. **Soul / R&B demands higher energy than jazz did.** The engine's pure-jazz degrees never crossed E=4; the R&B degrees break through at degree 32. The quadrant's acoustic character is intrinsically louder, and the distinction is structural rather than artist-specific.
6. **Convergence can stall.** The Ranger program has converged toward the Moon (you can hit it now), but the science payload has not converged with the platform. Two separate subsystems, two separate convergence curves, and the engine now documents them separately.
7. **The waxwing pair completes Bombycillidae.** Two species in two consecutive degrees -- the engine's family-pair pattern applied to a new family, and the second pair-level completion after the Turdidae work at earlier degrees.
8. **Collective loudness is distinct from individual loudness.** The E=5 Bohemian flock establishes a second axis on the loudness scale -- aggregate amplitude from many quiet voices. The flicker's E=4 solo vocalization and the Bohemian flock's E=5 aggregate operate on different acoustic architectures.
9. **Irruptive species category stabilizes.** Pine Siskin (12), Cedar Waxwing (31), Bohemian Waxwing (32) -- three species whose presence is conditional on external resource dynamics. The category has enough members to function as a structural pattern.
10. **Swarm communication recurs at higher amplitude.** Bushtit's quiet collective is echoed in the Bohemian's loud collective. Same architecture, different volume -- the engine now spans the full amplitude range of swarm-communication behavior.
11. **Conditional-activation mirror formalizes.** Ranger 6's cameras (existed but did not activate) and the Bohemian's southern appearance (exists but does not activate most years) share a structural match. Reaching the target is only half the mission.
