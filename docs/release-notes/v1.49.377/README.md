## Summary

**Queensryche** arrives at E=7 -- and with them, the **FIRST PROG METAL** entry in the Seattle 360 catalog, the **FIRST BELLEVUE BAND** in this genre's register, and the band that made *Operation: Mindcrime* (1988) and *Empire* (1990), two records that demonstrated that progressive rock's complexity and metal's force were not mutually exclusive. Geoff Tate (vocals), Michael Wilton and Chris DeGarmo (guitars), Eddie Jackson (bass), and Scott Rockenfield (drums) formed in Bellevue in 1980 and built a catalog that operated at a different intellectual register from most of what surrounded them in the Pacific Northwest: conceptual albums, operatic vocal arrangements, extended song structures that owed as much to Rush and Pink Floyd as to Black Sabbath. *Operation: Mindcrime* is a genuine rock opera, a complete narrative arc about a man manipulated into political assassination, and it is one of the most formally ambitious records that any Seattle or Bellevue band has produced. At E=7, Queensryche registers in the same range as Heart's hard rock entry (degree 235) and the harder S36 entries of the preceding ten degrees, but the E=7 reading means something different here: it is not punk velocity or hard rock mass but progressive complexity at full expression, the kind of E=7 that requires 8-minute songs to get there rather than two-minute blasts. **Queensryche is the second Bellevue band** in the engine -- Heart entered at degree 234, and now two degrees later the east side returns, establishing Bellevue as a genuine second node in the engine's geographic map. On the species side, the **Pacific Salmon** arrives at E=5 and delivers the **FIRST FISH** in the SPS engine, the **FIRST SALMONIDAE**, and one of the most consequential cross-reference events in the engine's history. Pacific Salmon -- the genus *Oncorhynchus*, encompassing Chinook, Coho, Sockeye, Pink, and Chum salmon -- are not a single species but an ecological category, and the sound they make -- the splashing and churning of spawning fish in shallow streams and rivers -- registers at E=5: present, distinctive, unmistakable once you know what you're hearing. The salmon spawn is one of the defining acoustic events of the Pacific Northwest autumn: a river full of spawning Chinook or Coho is a continuous churning sound, the surface broken by hundreds of fish simultaneously completing the final act of their lifecycle. The cross-reference implications are immediate: the **Bald Eagle** (degrees 221-222) congregates at salmon spawning rivers in winter to feed on exhausted and dead fish; the **Osprey** (degree 224) is a primary salmon hunter throughout the watershed; the **Grizzly Bear** (degrees 217-218) built its entire pre-hibernation fat reserve around the salmon run; every salmon-dependent species already in the engine now has its food source documented. Pacific Salmon are the keystone species of the Pacific Northwest ecosystem -- not a metaphor, but a measurable ecological fact: the nutrients in their bodies, brought from the ocean, fertilize the riparian forest, feed the birds, sustain the bears, and structure the predator guild in ways that cascade through every trophic level. The engine's first fish is the fish that builds the forest.

- **Part A -- S36:** Queensryche (Prog Metal, E=7) -- FIRST PROG METAL, FIRST BELLEVUE (second entry), Bellevue 1980, Operation: Mindcrime, Geoff Tate, conceptual albums
- **Part B -- SPS:** Pacific Salmon (splashing spawn, E=5) -- FIRST FISH, FIRST SALMONIDAE, cross-references Bald Eagle/Osprey/Grizzly, keystone species, ocean nutrients to forest
- FIRST FISH / FIRST SALMONIDAE -- the SPS engine crosses the vertebrate boundary at degree 236, moving from birds (degrees 221-235, fifteen consecutive degrees) to fish; the Pacific Salmon are the first non-avian, non-mammalian vertebrates in the engine, the first gill-breathing species, the first species documented by their aquatic behavior rather than aerial or terrestrial sound; the engine has documented birds and mammals from its opening through degree 235; degree 236 is the first widening of the taxonomic scope beyond tetrapods
- Salmon cross-reference: Bald Eagle (degrees 221-222) -- the Bald Eagle's winter concentration at rivers like the Skagit and Nooksack is made possible by the salmon run; the eagles arrive in November and December when spawned-out salmon are dying in the shallows, and they feed until the carcasses are gone; the engine documented the Bald Eagle's thin kee-kee-kee at degree 221 without yet having the salmon in the catalog; degree 236 retroactively completes that entry, giving the Bald Eagle's winter behavior its ecological context
- Salmon cross-reference: Osprey (degree 224) and Grizzly Bear (degrees 217-218) -- the Osprey hunts live salmon throughout the migratory season, plunging feet-first into rivers and coastal estuaries; the Grizzly Bear, documented at degrees 217-218, builds up to 30 pounds of pre-hibernation fat almost entirely from salmon during the August-October run; both species are structurally salmon-dependent, and neither their presence in the engine nor their ecological roles are fully legible without the salmon now entered at degree 236
- Keystone species mechanics -- the Pacific Salmon carry marine-derived nitrogen (the isotope N-15, enriched in ocean food webs) into freshwater and terrestrial ecosystems; when salmon die in streams after spawning, their bodies are eaten by bears, eagles, ravens, minks, and otters, and their carcasses are dragged into the riparian forest by predators who eat the fatty parts and leave the rest; the decomposing carcasses fertilize the forest floor; isotope studies have detected marine nitrogen in trees up to 300 meters from salmon streams; the salmon are a nutrient pipeline from the ocean to the forest, and the engine's documentation of their spawning sound is the documentation of that pipeline's audible end
- FIRST PROG METAL / Queensryche's conceptual catalog -- *Operation: Mindcrime* (1988) is the structural apex: a complete rock opera with a linear narrative, recurring themes, and character development across 15 tracks; *Empire* (1990) produced "Silent Lucidity," a genuinely unconventional radio hit that incorporated orchestral elements and a text spoken by Pamela Moore; the band's willingness to commit to extended forms and conceptual frameworks placed them outside the Seattle mainstream and connected them instead to the progressive rock tradition that runs from Yes and Genesis through Rush and Dream Theater; the engine has not previously documented a band working in this formal register, and Queensryche's entry at degree 236 opens that lineage
