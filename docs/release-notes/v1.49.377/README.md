**Queensryche and the Bellevue Formation.** **Queensryche** formed in **Bellevue, Washington** in 1981 out of a predecessor band called **The Mob**, with the founding lineup consisting of **Geoff Tate** (lead vocals), **Michael Wilton** (rhythm guitar), **Chris DeGarmo** (lead guitar), **Eddie Jackson** (bass), and **Scott Rockenfield** (drums). The Bellevue of 1981 was a growing suburban city east of Seattle across Lake Washington, connected to the city by the I-90 and SR-520 floating bridges, a mid-century middle-class suburban environment that predated the tech-industry Bellevue of the Microsoft-and-Amazon era. Microsoft's corporate campus in neighboring Redmond would transform the east side in the following decades, but in 1981 Bellevue was not yet the tech center it became — it was a comfortable, professional, middle-class suburb with a distinct identity from Seattle's urban music scene. The band's formation in this suburban-professional context is not incidental to the music that emerged: progressive metal is ambitious in a specific way, wanting to be taken seriously, caring about craftsmanship, emphasizing intellectual content alongside musical power. **The same** suburban-professional ambition that shaped the early Bellevue culture provided the formation context for a genre whose defining properties include taking both music and lyric content seriously — and the connection is not forced but emergent from the geography. The **Heart** Bellevue origin (documented at degrees 234-235) and the Queensryche Bellevue formation are both real, but they differ structurally: Heart's identity is primarily Seattle-broad with Vancouver formation and Queen Anne residence, whereas Queensryche is specifically identified as a Bellevue band. Degree 236 consequently marks the **FIRST BELLEVUE BAND (PROPER)** — the first explicit east-side representative in the engine's S36 geographic vocabulary.

**Geoff Tate and the Operatic Vocal Instrument.** **Geoff Tate** is one of the most distinctive vocalists in the history of rock and metal, whose formal operatic training established a vocal instrument unusual for the metal context. Tate studied operatic vocal technique — breath support, vowel placement, range development, the classical approach to the human voice as an instrument requiring technique and maintenance — and the training is audible in his singing: the notes are placed precisely, the range spans approximately four octaves, and the voice maintains power across its full range without the compression and strain that characterizes less trained metal vocalists. The operatic training is most structurally consequential on *Operation: Mindcrime* (1988), which requires Tate to voice political rage, romantic vulnerability, drug-induced confusion, moral horror, and institutional despair across fifteen connected tracks — and he achieves all of them. The operatic training contributes to this achievement because classical singing demands emotional authenticity alongside technical precision; the two are not separable in the tradition Tate studied, and the training transfers directly into the progressive-metal context. **The same** four-octave-range instrument observation organizes the direct comparison to **Ann Wilson**, the Heart lead vocalist documented at degrees 234-235, whose own four-octave range and Janis-Joplin-and-Robert-Plant-influenced approach establishes the second great Pacific Northwest rock-vocal instrument of the classic era. Three consecutive degrees (234, 235, 236) document Pacific Northwest vocalists with formal-training elements applied to rock rather than classical or operatic contexts — Ann Wilson at Heart and Geoff Tate at Queensryche — and the cluster establishes the region's capacity for producing exceptional voices that transfer formal technique into popular forms.

**Chris DeGarmo and the Classic-Era Lead Guitar.** **Chris DeGarmo** is the Queensryche lead guitarist and primary Geoff Tate songwriting collaborator through the classic era, whose guitar work on *Operation: Mindcrime* and *Empire* is structurally essential to the band's masterwork catalog. DeGarmo's lead playing is simultaneously melodic, technically demanding, and always in service of the song's conceptual goals — the three elements are not separable in his approach, and the result is lead guitar work that supports the progressive-metal genre's defining commitment to subordinating technique to composition. The dual-guitar approach of Wilton's rhythm and DeGarmo's lead creates a harmonic and textural density that distinguishes Queensryche from single-guitar metal bands, and the guitars converse across the catalog — lead and counterpoint, harmony and tension, precision and expression. DeGarmo left Queensryche in 1998, and the band's decline in critical standing tracks closely with his departure in a way that confirms his structural importance: the DeGarmo era is the classic era, and the post-DeGarmo catalog has not matched the peak. **In parallel** with Geoff Tate's vocal dominance, DeGarmo's guitar and primary songwriting contribution is the second half of a Tate-DeGarmo partnership whose combined work exceeds what either member could produce independently, and the partnership is directly analogous to the **Ann Wilson / Nancy Wilson** Heart partnership documented at degrees 234-235 in the sibling-sisterly and the non-sibling band-partnership registers respectively.

**The Operation: Mindcrime Concept-Album Masterwork.** **Operation: Mindcrime** (1988, EMI America) is the defining Queensryche work and one of the most ambitious concept albums in rock history. The album's protagonist is **Nikki** — a disillusioned, drug-addicted young man who is recruited by a mysterious figure named **Dr. X** into a political assassination network. The narrative traces Nikki's radicalization, his relationship with a former-prostitute-turned-nun named **Sister Mary**, his murder of a corrupt priest, and his eventual breakdown and commitment to a psychiatric institution. The story is told across fifteen tracks with recitative sections connecting the full narrative, and the musical character of each section matches the narrative emotional state with compositional precision. The album engages with American political cynicism, the manipulation of the vulnerable, and the moral bankruptcy of institutional power — content that progressive rock had always engaged with but that was unusual in metal. Released in 1988 during the Reagan years, the political disillusionment the album documents was timely. *Operation: Mindcrime* is consistently ranked in best-of-decade and best-of-genre lists and is routinely compared to **The Who**'s *Tommy* and **Pink Floyd**'s *The Wall* as one of rock's most ambitious sustained narrative achievements. Queensryche performed the album in full multiple times in concert, including a live album and DVD, demonstrating that the complex arrangements translate to stage performance — a test many progressive records fail. **The same** concept-album narrative-arc structural principle that organizes *Operation: Mindcrime* sits at the direct structural opposite of the single-song radio-format that dominated mid-1980s commercial rock, and the contrast between the album's narrative commitment and the single-song commercial pressure is much of what makes the achievement remarkable.

**Empire and the Silent Lucidity Commercial Peak.** **Empire** (1990, EMI America) is Queensryche's commercial peak — more accessible than *Operation: Mindcrime* with shorter songs, more conventional structures, and a sound designed for radio and arenas. The single **"Silent Lucidity"** is a lush, orchestrated ballad with **Pamela Moore** spoken-word sections that reached Billboard #9 and became the band's most recognizable composition. *Empire* sold over three million copies in the US — Queensryche's commercial ceiling — and demonstrated that progressive-metal ambition and commercial reach can coexist when production respects complexity rather than flattening it. Producer **Peter Collins** brought a production clarity unusual in metal to *Empire*, allowing every instrumental detail to be audible: the bass notes, the guitar harmonics, the keyboard textures, the drum details all remain legible in the mix. The result is production that serves the music's complexity rather than masking it. "Silent Lucidity" in culture functioned as the ambassador composition for the band — countless films, television shows, and cultural moments throughout the 1990s used the song, bringing Queensryche to audiences that would not have found the band through the progressive-metal route. The composition is simultaneously a commercial concession and a genuine artistic achievement.

**Pacific Salmon and the Five Washington Species.** The **Pacific Salmon** (*Oncorhynchus* spp.) enters the engine at E=5 as the **FIRST FISH IN ENGINE**, the **FIRST SALMONIDAE**, and the keystone-species entry whose ecological-web function reshapes the entire prior raptor and predator arc retroactively. The genus *Oncorhynchus* encompasses five Washington species, each with distinct biology: **Chinook Salmon** (*O. tshawytscha*, the King Salmon) is the largest Pacific salmon, 4.5-20 kg in typical adults and exceeding 50 kg in record fish; spring and fall run variants use the Columbia, Skagit, Elwha, and Snoqualmie rivers; multiple ESUs (Evolutionarily Significant Units) are ESA-listed as Threatened or Endangered; the Chinook is the Southern Resident Orca's obligate prey. **Coho Salmon** (*O. kisutch*, the Silver Salmon) is medium-sized at 2.5-8 kg, runs in fall, uses smaller tributaries with woody debris, and shows a striking vulnerability to urban stormwater — a syndrome called urban-runoff-mortality whose causative compound was identified in 2020 as **6PPD-quinone**, a tire-rubber chemical washing off roads in rain. **Sockeye Salmon** (*O. nerka*, the Red Salmon) is 1.5-4.5 kg, the most lake-dependent Pacific salmon with juveniles rearing 1-2 years in lakes before ocean migration, and develops the most dramatic spawning coloration (brilliant crimson body with green head). **Pink Salmon** (*O. gorbuscha*, the Humpy Salmon) is the smallest Pacific salmon at 1-2.7 kg with a strict two-year lifecycle producing the Puget Sound odd-year-abundance pattern; spawning males develop a dramatic hump. **Chum Salmon** (*O. keta*, the Dog Salmon) is 4-13 kg with distinctive canine-like spawning teeth and bold red-green-purple calico coloration; Hood Canal summer Chum are ESA-Threatened. **In parallel** with Queensryche's five-member band lineup, the Pacific Salmon genus's five Washington species each contribute distinctly to the keystone-species documentation, and the five-by-five structural parallel is itself a cross-sync feature of degree 236.

**The Spawning Sound at E=5 and the Physical Soundscape.** The sound documented at E=5 for the Pacific Salmon is structurally distinct from every prior SPS entry: it is not a vocalization. ## Part A: Queensryche — The Engine's First Progressive Metal, the First Bellevue Band Proper, and the Geoff-Tate-Chris-DeGarmo Classic-Era Partnership

- **Bellevue 1981 Formation and The Mob Predecessor:** **Queensryche** formed in **Bellevue, Washington** in 1981 out of a predecessor band called **The Mob**, with the founding lineup consisting of **Geoff Tate** (lead vocals), **Michael Wilton** (rhythm guitar), **Chris DeGarmo** (lead guitar), **Eddie Jackson** (bass), and **Scott Rockenfield** (drums). The Bellevue of 1981 was a growing suburban city east of Seattle across Lake Washington, connected to Seattle by the I-90 and SR-520 floating bridges; Microsoft's neighboring Redmond campus would transform the east side in the following decades, but in 1981 Bellevue was not yet the tech center it became — it was a comfortable, professional, middle-class suburb with a distinct identity from Seattle's urban music scene. - **Geoff Tate and the Operatically-Trained Vocal Instrument:** **Geoff Tate** (born January 14, 1959, Stuttgart, Germany; son of a US serviceman raised in the Pacific Northwest) is the Queensryche lead vocalist whose formal operatic training established one of the most distinctive vocal instruments in the history of rock and metal. Tate's operatic training — breath support, vowel placement, range development, the classical approach to voice as instrument — produced an approximately four-octave range instrument that maintains power across its full range without the compression and strain characteristic of less-trained metal vocalists. The emotional range demanded by *Operation: Mindcrime* (political rage, romantic vulnerability, drug-induced confusion, moral horror, institutional despair) is met by the operatic-training discipline: classical singing demands emotional authenticity alongside technical precision, the two are not separable in the tradition Tate studied, and the training transfers directly into the progressive-metal context. ## Part B: Pacific Salmon — The First Fish in Engine, the Pacific Northwest's Keystone Species, and the Five Washington Oncorhynchus Representatives

- **Species Baseline and the FIRST FISH Milestone:** **Pacific Salmon** (*Oncorhynchus* spp.) is a member of **Class Actinopterygii (ray-finned fishes) / Order Salmoniformes / Family Salmonidae / Genus Oncorhynchus**, and the genus encompasses five Washington species: Chinook, Coho, Sockeye, Pink, and Chum. The **FIRST FISH IN ENGINE** milestone applies at degree 236 because this is the engine's first documentation of any fish after 235 prior degrees of mammal and bird documentation, and the **FIRST SALMONIDAE** milestone applies because the salmon/trout family enters the engine for the first time. The **FIRST NON-BIRD NON-MAMMAL VERTEBRATE** designation records the scope widening beyond tetrapods — every prior SPS vertebrate entry has been either a mammal or a bird, and degree 236 crosses that boundary into the ray-finned fishes. The CLASS-LEVEL TAXONOMIC EXPANSION represents the engine's most significant taxonomic move since the raptor era began at degree 221.
- **The Five Washington Species and Their Distinct Biology:** **Chinook Salmon** (*O. tshawytscha*, the King Salmon) is the largest Pacific salmon at 4.5-20 kg typical adults and exceeding 50 kg in record fish; spring and fall run variants use the Columbia, Skagit, Elwha, and Snoqualmie rivers; multiple ESUs are ESA-listed as Threatened or Endangered; Chinook is the Southern Resident Orca's obligate prey (90%+ of Orca diet), making it the single most important factor in Orca conservation. **Coho Salmon** (*O. kisutch*, the Silver Salmon) is medium-sized at 2.5-8 kg, runs in fall, uses smaller tributaries with woody debris, and shows a striking vulnerability to urban stormwater through the **6PPD-quinone** tire-rubber chemical causing pre-spawn mortality; the 2020 discovery of the causative compound has produced Washington State legislative response. **Sockeye Salmon** (*O. nerka*, the Red Salmon) is 1.5-4.5 kg, the most lake-dependent Pacific salmon with juveniles rearing 1-2 years in lakes before ocean migration; spawning coloration is the genus's most dramatic (brilliant crimson body with green head); **Kokanee** is the non-anadromous lake-resident form; Lake Ozette and Snake River Sockeye ESUs are ESA-Endangered. **Pink Salmon** (*O. gorbuscha*, the Humpy Salmon) is the smallest Pacific salmon at 1-2.7 kg with a strict two-year lifecycle producing the Puget Sound odd-year-abundance pattern; spawning males develop a dramatic hump. **Chum Salmon** (*O. keta*, the Dog Salmon) is 4-13 kg with distinctive canine-like spawning teeth and bold red-green-purple calico coloration; Hood Canal summer Chum are ESA-Threatened.
- **The Semelparous Lifecycle and the Ocean-to-Forest Nutrient Transfer:** Pacific salmon are **semelparous** — they die after spawning. This is not incidental but adaptive: the salmon's decomposing body delivers marine-derived nutrients (nitrogen, phosphorus, carbon) from the ocean into the river ecosystem. The lifecycle runs from egg laid in redds in cold clean well-oxygenated gravel streams, through **alevin** (yolk-sac stage in gravel interstitial spaces), **fry** (emerged and feeding on stream invertebrates), **parr** (distinctive dark bars along sides, freshwater rearing for months to years), **smolt transformation** (smoltification — profound physiological change from freshwater to saltwater physiology, kidney reversal, gill salt-excreting cells, color change to silver, behavioral change facilitating downstream migration), ocean life (1-7 years of rapid growth on marine food web), return migration (navigated by olfactory memory of natal-stream chemistry supplemented by magnetic-field orientation at sea; a Chinook that hatched in the Elwha will cross thousands of miles of open ocean and return, from among thousands of possible streams, to the Elwha), spawning (females dig redds with powerful tail-sweeps, males compete for redd position, females deposit eggs and males fertilize, females cover with gravel and move upstream), and death. The spawning journey is the most remarkable animal-kingdom navigation and commitment sequence documented in biology.
- **The Spawning Sound Profile at E=5:** The E=5 sound is structurally distinct from every prior SPS entry: it is not a vocalization. Fish have no larynx, no syrinx, no vocal cord — no sound-producing organ designed for communication in the avian or mammalian sense. The sound components comprise **tail-slapping** where females sweep their tails powerfully through gravel to construct and cover redds (audible 50-100 meters), **body displacement** from Chinook weighing 10-30 kg moving through shallow water (heavy splashing distinct from the light splash of a small fish), **male fighting** involving chasing, biting, and physically displacing rivals at redds (aggressive splashing and collision sounds), **jumping at falls** where salmon attempting to leap waterfalls create loud re-entry splashes (the Cedar Falls salmon runs at Landsburg Dam on the Cedar River produce a concentrated leaping soundscape), and the **background current** of water over gravel providing the ambient bed over which the salmon sounds sit. The E=5 registration is appropriate: a spawning run at peak is substantial and present, a continuous energetic physically impressive soundscape produced by hundreds or thousands of large fish in a confined stream.
- **The Keystone-Species Ecological Web:** The Pacific Salmon is the Pacific Northwest's keystone species because it connects the ocean to the forest through a network of ecological dependencies. **Bears** (Grizzly Bear degrees 217-218, Black Bear) congregate at salmon streams during the fall run; a single bear may eat 30-40 salmon per day at peak season, accumulating hibernation fat. **Bald Eagles** (degrees 221-222) congregate at spawning streams in fall and winter to feed on live and dying salmon; the Skagit Valley eagle concentration on dying spawned-out Chum is directly salmon-dependent. **Osprey** (degree 224) fish for salmon throughout the migratory season, plunging feet-first into rivers and estuaries. **Southern Resident Orca** are salmon-obligate specialists (90%+ Chinook diet). **The forest** itself benefits: bears, eagles, and other predators carry salmon carcasses away from stream banks into the riparian forest, sometimes hundreds of meters; the decomposing carcasses fertilize the soil with marine-derived nitrogen (isotope N-15); peer-reviewed isotope studies show elevated N-15 in Sitka spruce, Douglas fir, and red alder tree rings up to 300 meters from salmon streams, with trees near salmon streams showing measurably higher growth rates than equivalent trees without salmon access. Degree 236 is the retroactive ecological anchor for the prior raptor/predator arc.
- **Indigenous First Foods Relationship and the Boldt Decision:** No ecological relationship in the Pacific Northwest is as culturally central as the human-salmon relationship. For virtually every indigenous nation of the Pacific Northwest (Coast Salish, Chinook, Tillamook, Quinault, Makah, Lummi, Tulalip, Skokomish, and others), the **First Salmon ceremony** is one of the most important ritual events of the year — the first salmon of the season is thanked, portions are offered back to the water, and the fish is shared with the community before any individual consumption. The **1854-1855 treaties** (Point Elliott, Point No Point, Medicine Creek, and others) between the US government and Pacific Northwest tribes guaranteed tribal fishing rights "in common with all citizens of the Territory." In 1974, US District Court Judge **George Boldt** issued the **Boldt Decision** (United States v. Washington), ruling that treaties guaranteed tribes 50% of harvestable salmon and steelhead in Washington State — one of the most significant legal decisions in the history of US-tribal relations. **Celilo Falls** on the Columbia River, submerged by **The Dalles Dam** in 1957, was the most productive salmon fishing site in North America and a gathering point for Plateau tribes across the basin; its destruction is one of the defining ecological and cultural catastrophes of the 20th-century Pacific Northwest.
- **The Population Decline, Dam Impacts, and Recovery Efforts:** Pacific salmon populations have declined dramatically from historical abundance. Pre-contact estimates suggest 16-40 million adult salmon returned annually to Washington State rivers; current returns are a fraction of this. Multiple ESUs are listed as Threatened or Endangered under the Endangered Species Act. The decline has multiple interacting causes: **dams** (over 1,000 dams in the Columbia River basin block salmon migration; the four lower Snake River dams have been a subject of controversy for 30 years, with a 2024 Biden-administration-and-tribal-nations framework providing potential-dam-removal-alongside-alternative-energy-investment context); **habitat loss** from logging, agriculture, urban development, and road building; **hatchery** genetic effects on wild fish; **ocean conditions** including climate-change warming and Pacific Decadal Oscillation multi-year cycles; and **6PPD-quinone** urban stormwater for Coho specifically. Recovery efforts include the **Elwha River dam removal** (2011-2014, the largest dam removal in US history at the time), with measurable salmon recovery within a decade — Chinook, Coho, Steelhead, and Pink salmon returning above the former dam sites. Conservation at the landscape scale is possible; the Elwha documents that.
- **The Keystone-Species Retroactive Anchor Structural Role:** Degree 236's structural function is simultaneously forward (introducing the Salmonidae family and the *Oncorhynchus* genus and the five Washington species) and retroactive (anchoring every prior salmon-dependent entry in the SPS arc). The prior Grizzly Bear (217-218), Bald Eagle (221-222), Osprey (224), and Southern Resident Orca documentation entries were not fully legible without a keystone-species documentation in place; degree 236 completes those entries by providing the food source on which their ecology depends. The retroactive-anchor register enters the engine's SPS structural-vocabulary at degree 236 as a first-instance documentation mode, establishing the retroactive-ecological-completion pattern as available for future keystone-species entries that similarly anchor prior dependent-species documentation. This is a structurally new kind of documentation move: not a new species in isolation, but a species whose entry reshapes the meaning of prior entries.
- **Pacific Salmon in the Engine's Cross-Reference Network:** The salmon connects to more previously-entered species than any other SPS entry. **Bald Eagle** (221-222): salmon-dependent apex raptor; Skagit Valley winter congregation on dying Chum. **Osprey** (224): primary live-salmon predator; often kleptoparasitized by Bald Eagles. **Grizzly Bear** (217-218): salmon as critical fall food source; 30-40 fish per day at peak run; marine nitrogen in bear fat = hibernation fuel. **Southern Resident Orca**: Chinook-obligate apex marine predator; 75-individual population directly limited by Chinook abundance. The cross-reference network at degree 236 is the most extensive in the SPS engine's history, reflecting the Pacific Salmon's keystone-species structural position in the Pacific Northwest ecosystem. The engine's documentation of the Pacific Salmon at degree 236 is therefore simultaneously the first fish entry, the first Salmonidae entry, the keystone-species anchor, and the ecological-web node for half-a-dozen prior entries whose legibility now improves retroactively.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Double milestone degrees emerge when both tracks are ready to cross a first-of-kind boundary at the same degree position.** Degree 236 is the engine's first such instance, aligning the FIRST PROGRESSIVE METAL on the S36 axis with the FIRST FISH on the SPS axis. Future engine releases should evaluate whether scheduled entries on both tracks will deliver FIRSTs at the same degree; when they do, the DOUBLE MILESTONE DEGREE register should be explicitly surfaced because it converts two individual firsts into a multiplicative structural statement with both-halves-simultaneously-expanding-scope semantics.
2. **Class-level taxonomic expansions require explicit justification for the sound-documentation register used.** The Pacific Salmon entry required the engine to extend from vocalizing-tetrapod sound into physical-consequence sound of large-fish-in-shallow-water, and the justification was made explicitly in the release. Future class-level taxonomic expansions (e.g., future amphibian, reptile, or invertebrate entries) will face similar justification challenges, and the engine should prepare explicit register-expansion arguments each time the class boundary is crossed, because physical-consequence sound and vocalizing sound are structurally distinct and the engine's documentation register must accommodate both cleanly.
3. **Keystone-species entries create retroactive ecological anchors that reshape the meaning of prior entries.** Pacific Salmon's entry at degree 236 is simultaneously forward (introducing the *Oncorhynchus* genus and five Washington species) and retroactive (anchoring the prior Grizzly Bear, Bald Eagle, Osprey, and Southern Resident Orca documentation). Future keystone-species entries should be explicitly tagged as retroactive-ecological-anchors when they reshape prior-entry meaning, because the anchor register is structurally distinct from the additive-new-species register and carries different interpretive weight.
4. **Geographic distinctions between band-origin-city and band-identity-city can be subtle but structurally essential.** Heart's Bellevue origin (degrees 234-235) and Queensryche's Bellevue identity (degree 236) differ in a way that affects the FIRST BELLEVUE BAND designation: Heart is Seattle-broad with Bellevue roots, whereas Queensryche is specifically Bellevue's band. Future engine documentation of Pacific Northwest rock should carefully distinguish between city-of-origin and city-of-identity because the distinction affects geographic-vocabulary milestones and the two are not interchangeable.
5. **Interior five-unit structural parallels can emerge from unrelated domain-specific organization and strengthen cross-sync readings.** Queensryche's five-member classic-era lineup and the *Oncorhynchus* genus's five Washington species are structurally parallel despite being from entirely unrelated domains (rock band lineup versus fish genus species list). Future engine releases should attend to interior structural parallels when they emerge, because they can strengthen paired-release cross-sync readings beyond the primary thematic connection without requiring the parallel to be contrived.
6. **Cross-axis registers can draw structural weight from narrative-and-ecology parallels where the narrative engages with the ecological principle.** The individual-purpose-against-systemic-forces cross-axis at degree 236 pairs Queensryche's *Operation: Mindcrime* Nikki narrative with the Pacific Salmon's semelparous lifecycle, both documenting individual lives carrying the weight of systems. Future cross-axis documentation should consider narrative-to-ecology parallels where they exist, because such parallels transfer structural weight across fiction-to-biology domain boundaries in a way that within-domain parallels do not, and the register is interpretively distinct.
7. **Vocal-instrument cluster documentation requires a multi-degree arc to surface the regional-capacity register.** The Ann Wilson / Geoff Tate three-degree cluster (234-235-236) documents the Pacific Northwest's capacity for exceptional rock voices with formal-training elements applied to rock. A single-degree documentation of either vocalist would not have surfaced the regional-capacity register; the multi-degree cluster is structurally required. Future engine documentation of exceptional vocal instruments should evaluate whether a multi-degree arc is appropriate, because arc-documentation carries structural weight that single-degree documentation cannot match.
8. **Progressive-metal documentation requires explicit differentiation from prior metal and prog-rock entries because the genre's defining properties are not captured by either predecessor alone.** The prior engine entries covering metal and progressive rock do not fully capture progressive metal's defining combination (concept-album narrative, operatically-informed vocal, extended song structure, unusual time signatures, technical musicianship, thematic ambition). Future genre-first entries should explicitly differentiate from related-predecessor genres because the first-entry register otherwise collapses into the predecessor's register and loses the new-genre documentation specificity.
9. **The keystone-species concept deserves its own dedicated documentation register because it is not reducible to individual-species documentation or to ecological-relationship documentation.** Pacific Salmon's keystone-species role involves network-level ecological documentation that neither single-species documentation (salmon biology) nor pairwise-relationship documentation (salmon-and-bears) captures. Future keystone-species entries should receive dedicated keystone-species-register documentation that explicitly surfaces the network-level ecological function, because the network-level documentation carries structural weight that component-level documentation does not.
10. **Indigenous First Foods relationships and cultural context are structurally essential for Pacific Northwest species documentation where the species has central cultural significance.** The Pacific Salmon entry incorporates the First Salmon ceremony, the 1854-1855 treaties, the Boldt Decision (1974), and Celilo Falls because no Pacific Salmon documentation is structurally complete without this cultural-legal-historical context. Future engine documentation of species with central Pacific Northwest cultural significance should include dedicated First Foods and treaty-legal-history context because the cultural-legal register is structurally distinct from the pure biology/ecology register and carries essential interpretive weight.
