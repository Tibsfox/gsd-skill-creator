## Summary

**RIOT GRRRL. IN MEMORY OF STEFANIE SARGENT. THE LARGEST ANIMAL.**

**Seven Year Bitch** arrive at E=8 -- the Seattle riot grrrl band formed in 1990 by Selene Vigil (vocals), Stefanie Sargent (guitar), Roisin Dunne (bass), and Vicky Insardi (drums). Seven Year Bitch released two studio albums: *Sick 'Em* (1992) on C/Z Records and *Viva Zapata!* (1994) on Atlantic Records, recorded and released after the death of Stefanie Sargent in June 1992. The band also contributed to the *Home Alive* compilation, a benefit album documenting the Seattle music community's response to violence against women, and released *¡Gato Negro!* in 1996 before dissolving that year.

Stefanie Sargent died on June 27, 1992, from an accidental drug overdose at age 24. She had recorded the material that would become *Sick 'Em* before her death, and the album was released posthumously. *Viva Zapata!* was named in memory of both Stefanie Sargent and Mia Zapata, the vocalist of The Gits who was murdered in July 1993. The name is an act of double mourning and a declaration of continued presence: the title asserts that the women who were lost are still being honored, still being spoken to, still part of the community that built the Seattle riot grrrl world.

The music of Seven Year Bitch is direct, loud, and politically committed in the riot grrrl mode -- Selene Vigil's vocals are unsparing, the guitars are distorted, the rhythm section is driving -- and the catalog carries the weight of what the band experienced without letting that weight slow the music down. *Viva Zapata!* is the document of a band that had lost one member, mourned another from a different band, and kept playing anyway. E=8 is the correct assignment. The energy is real and the commitment is complete.

This entry is offered with respect for Stefanie Sargent, for her work, and for the loss the Seattle music community sustained when she died. The engine documents her contribution through the music she made and the band she helped build.

**Blue Whale 20Hz Pulse** arrives at E=5 on the SPS track -- the low-frequency vocalizations of *Balaenoptera musculus*, the blue whale, the largest animal that has ever existed on Earth. The blue whale's primary vocalizations are infrasonic pulses centered around 20 Hz -- at the boundary of human hearing, sometimes below it -- and produced at sound pressure levels that make the blue whale the loudest animal on Earth. Blue whale 20Hz pulses have been recorded at 188 decibels, making them louder than a jet engine at close range, louder than any sound a terrestrial animal produces, louder than almost any natural phenomenon the surface environment generates. They propagate through the deep ocean sound channel (SOFAR) for thousands of kilometers.

E=5 for the blue whale 20Hz pulse reflects the listening experience, not the physical magnitude. The pulse is felt more than heard -- a subsonic presence, a vibration in the body and in the water, a phenomenon that operates below the range where human auditory processing assigns conventional energy. The largest animal on Earth makes the deepest sound in the Pacific Northwest marine record. It is also, in terms of auditory experience, among the most austere: a single, slow, subsonic pulse repeating in the dark of the deep ocean, at a frequency the listening ear cannot fully resolve. E=5 is correct. The scale is in the physics, not the perception.
