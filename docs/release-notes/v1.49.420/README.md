## Lessons Learned

1. **Cross-community documentation within a thematic cluster warrants first-class geographic-institutional differentiation.** The degree 279 documentation distinguishes the **Capitol Hill Seattle** riot-grrrl-adjacent register from the **Olympia** riot-grrrl register of degrees 275-278 rather than aggregating both into a homogenized Pacific Northwest movement. The lesson is that thematic clusters spanning multiple geographic-institutional communities should preserve the community-level distinctions at first-class documentation resolution — geographic location, label infrastructure, scene-internal institutional context — rather than collapsing the distinctions into thematic uniformity. The resulting documentation is richer for maintaining the differentiation.
2. **Memorial documentation for founding-member loss should be factual-and-respectful rather than sensationalist or anecdotal.** The degree 279 **Stefanie Sargent** memorial documentation names the specific date (June 27, 1992), the specific cause (accidental heroin overdose), the specific age (24), and the founding contribution to the band, and then preserves the memorial as a first-class structural element of the record without reducing the band to the circumstances of the loss. The lesson is that memorial documentation should be precise in the loss details and respectful in the handling, without sensationalism and without anecdotal padding, so that the memorial register holds at first-class resolution rather than collapsing into biographical footnote.
3. **Cross-degree grief-bridge documentation warrants first-class structural recognition when the connection is explicit at the artifact level.** The *¡Viva Zapata!* (1994) album title's double tribute to the Mexican revolutionary **Emiliano Zapata** and to the murdered **Mia Zapata** of **The Gits** (degree 280) is the most explicit cross-degree connection in the engine's riot-grrrl-adjacent documentation, and the engine's degree 279 documentation treats this bridge as a first-class structural element rather than as narrative background. The lesson is that cross-degree connections explicit at the primary-artifact level (album title, song lyric, liner-note dedication) warrant first-class structural recognition rather than being relegated to implicit narrative context, because the connection's documentation is what makes the cross-degree relationship legible at the engine-structural level.
4. **E-value decoupling from source-level requires explicit documentation at the first below-threshold entry.** The degree 279 blue whale documentation establishes that the E-value (perceptual-intensity score) is not the source-level (180-188 dB physical measurement) and that E=5 for a 20 Hz infrasonic pulse reflects the at-or-below-human-hearing-threshold reception rather than any decrease in physical energy. The lesson is that the E-scale's experiential-intensity grounding must be made explicit at the first entry where source-level and perceptual score decouple, because subsequent below-threshold entries will reference the decoupling-establishing entry as the precedent; leaving the decoupling implicit would allow future readers to conflate E with source-level incorrectly.
5. **Opposite-register family arcs can be structurally complete at two entries when the two anchors are the perceptual-accessibility extremes.** The Balaenopteridae arc across degrees 278-279 — the **Humpback Whale Breach** at E=9 (maximum spectacle) paired with the **Blue Whale 20Hz Pulse** at E=5 (maximum invisible) — is structurally complete for the family's documentation purpose with just two entries because the opposite perceptual-accessibility extremes are the anchors that make the family legible. The lesson is that arc length is not a uniform standard across families or communities: a family characterized by opposite-register documentation can be legible at two entries, while a family or community characterized by internal variation may require more extensive coverage (as in the Orca Arc at seven consecutive degrees). Arc planning should match the arc's length to the documentation purpose rather than to a uniform length standard.
6. **Losses carried structurally are distinct from losses registered anecdotally, and the distinction warrants first-class engine recognition.** Seven Year Bitch carries the **Stefanie Sargent** 1992 death and the **Mia Zapata** 1993 murder structurally through the *¡Viva Zapata!* (1994) title, through the continued *Gato Negro* (1996) catalog, and through the *Home Alive* compilation contribution; the **Blue Whale** species carries the twentieth-century commercial-whaling reduction structurally through the current **NE Pacific** population of approximately 1,000-2,200 individuals and through the slow **Endangered Species Act**-listed recovery. The lesson is that historical losses carried structurally — through continued artifact production, through population-scale documentation, through institutional frameworks — are distinct from losses registered as biographical or background detail, and the engine's documentation should preserve the structural dimension of carried losses at first-class resolution rather than relegating losses to footnote.
7. **Institutional distinctions within a thematic cluster should be documented at first-class resolution when they are structurally load-bearing.** The degree 279 documentation distinguishes **C/Z Records** (Seattle independent label) from **Atlantic Records** (major label) and from **Kill Rock Stars** (Olympia independent label) and **K Records** (Olympia independent label), and preserves all four as structurally distinct institutional anchors within the engine's 1990s Pacific Northwest independent-music-infrastructure picture. The lesson is that institutional distinctions are structurally load-bearing for the engine's documentation when they shape the catalog's production, distribution, and reception context, and the engine should preserve these distinctions at first-class resolution rather than aggregating all independent labels into a uniform category or all major labels into a uniform category.
8. **Cross-sync cross-axis registers should name the structural principle they document rather than simply pairing the A-side and B-side entries.** The degree 279 cross-sync axis — **presence beyond perception** — names the structural principle that acoustic presence and human perceptual accessibility are distinct structural axes, and the engine's documentation uses this name to organize both halves of the release as matched demonstrations of the principle rather than as unrelated A-side/B-side pairings. The lesson is that cross-sync registers should name the principle that organizes the pairing rather than simply describing the pairing, because the naming-of-principle is what makes the cross-axis register legible as an engine-level structural documentation rather than as a release-level coincidence of pairing.
