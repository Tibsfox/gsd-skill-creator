## Stefanie Sargent


- the engine acknowledges Stefanie Sargent's death in 1992 and her contribution to the Seven Year Bitch catalog with the same directness the band brought to their own mourning; she recorded *Sick 'Em*; *Viva Zapata!* carries her name in its title alongside Mia Zapata's; the Seattle music community's response to these losses -- *Home Alive*, continued performance, named mourning -- is itself a document of how a community processes loss while maintaining the activity that the lost were part of; Seven Year Bitch continued to play, and in doing so continued to insist that Stefanie Sargent's presence in the music was not ended by her death

## Largest animal, deepest sound


- the blue whale is the largest animal that has ever lived on Earth; larger than any known dinosaur, larger than any other cetacean, larger than anything the terrestrial fossil record has produced; it is also the source of the deepest primary vocalization in the Pacific Northwest marine record: 20Hz, at the infrasonic boundary, propagating through the SOFAR channel for thousands of kilometers; the conjunction of maximum physical scale and minimum auditory resolution is one of the engine's most unusual pairings -- the biggest thing makes the least perceptible sound

## E=5 and physical scale


- the blue whale 20Hz pulse scores E=5 not because it is quiet (it is the loudest biological sound ever recorded) but because the human auditory experience of an infrasonic pulse is unlike the experience of a sound in the conventional hearing range; the pulse is a pressure event, a whole-body sensation at close range, a barely-perceptible sub-bass at distance; the energy is in the physics; E=5 is the listening score; the physical score would be E=10 if the engine had such a rating
