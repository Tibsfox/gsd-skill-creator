## Summary

**The Frantics** arrive at E=6 -- a Seattle surf/garage band active from the late 1950s through the early 1960s, contemporaries of The Ventures and The Wailers, part of the founding generation of Pacific Northwest rock. The Frantics were a Seattle act -- not Tacoma, not Portland -- which makes them the Seattle node in the PNW garage rock triangle that degrees 240-244 are mapping. They were known for their live energy and their place in the Seattle ballroom circuit, the network of venues like the Spanish Castle Ballroom and the Eagles Auditorium that provided the infrastructure for the PNW rock scene before any record label infrastructure existed. The Frantics' E=6 arrival continues the Garage Rock Era arc without dropping from the range the arc established, and positions the engine correctly: Seattle was part of the 1960s PNW garage moment, just not the center of it.

On the species side, **Pacific Lamprey** enters at E=1 -- **silent/sucker**, a near-perfect silence that ties the engine's **absolute minimum**. E=1 has been registered only once before in the engine's history: at degree 187, when the Harbor Porpoise registered the lowest possible score. Pacific Lamprey ties that mark. The reasons for E=1 are structural to what Pacific Lamprey are: they are jawless fish, members of the class Petromyzontida, a lineage that diverged from the jawed vertebrate line approximately **450 million years ago**. They predate the dinosaurs by 220 million years. They predate the first land vertebrates. They were swimming through Devonian seas when the Pacific Northwest was underwater, before the Cascades existed, before the continent had its current shape. The "silent/sucker" sound is the sound of a lamprey attaching to a substrate or a host using its oral disc -- a specialized suction apparatus ringed with teeth -- and the sound is almost nothing: a wet contact, a pressure differential, a suction. **Degree 243 opens the FIRST PETROMYZONTIDAE entry in the engine's history.** The Salmonidae arc closed one degree ago; what follows is older by an order of magnitude.

Pacific Lamprey are also a crucial food and cultural resource for Columbia Plateau tribes -- the same tribes whose Celilo Falls salmon fishery was destroyed by The Dalles Dam in 1957. The Dam did not just block salmon passage; it eliminated the lamprey habitat at Celilo Falls as well. The Pacific Lamprey's entry at degree 243 is thus another thread in the engine's Celilo Falls documentation, begun at degree 236 with the salmon spawning entry and continuing through the Salmonidae arc and now into the ancient fish territory beyond it.

- The Spanish Castle Ballroom and PNW venue infrastructure -- the Spanish Castle Ballroom, located between Seattle and Tacoma on Highway 99, was the central live music venue for the PNW rock scene in the late 1950s and early 1960s; The Frantics played there regularly, as did The Wailers, The Sonics, The Ventures, and dozens of other regional acts; the venue was mythologized by Jimi Hendrix ("Spanish Castle Magic") and burned down in 1968; it was the physical infrastructure that made the PNW garage rock triangle function as a regional circuit rather than isolated local scenes
