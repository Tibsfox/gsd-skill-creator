# Lessons — v1.49.384

1. **The 450-million-year frame changes the engine's evolutionary-history vocabulary permanently.**
   Every SPS entry before degree 243 belonged to a lineage younger than 150 million years. Pacific Lamprey at 450 MYA establishes a new deep-time register that subsequent ancient-fish entries will extend (White Sturgeon at ~200 MYA, others to follow). The frame shift is not reversible; the engine now holds Paleozoic-lineage documentation alongside its Cenozoic-lineage documentation.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8404_

2. **Geographic trios work better than geographic singletons for regional-scene documentation.**
   The Northwest Sound three-city framework at degrees 241-244 documents the regional scene more faithfully than any single-city framework would. The Frantics' Seattle chapter is necessary precisely because Tacoma-plus-Portland alone would misrepresent the scene's geographic distribution. Future regional-scene documentation should default to multi-city frameworks.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8405_

3. **First-instance taxonomic registers carry disproportionate documentary weight.**
   The FIRST PETROMYZONTIDAE designation at degree 243 introduces an entire family to the engine's vocabulary in a single entry. Subsequent Petromyzontidae entries (if any) will add species-level detail, but the family-level register is established by the first entry. The pattern holds generally: first-instance family, order, or class entries require density in the initial documentation because they establish the register that subsequent entries will inherit.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8406_

4. **Absolute-minimum readings function as paired-anchor registers rather than singular anomalies.**
   The Harbor Porpoise (187) E=1 reading was documented as a singular event; the Pacific Lamprey (243) E=1 tie establishes E=1 as a recurring documentary feature with paired-anchor structure. Future absolute-minimum readings will participate in a three-or-more-instance network rather than being treated as novelties.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8407_

5. **Fish-ladder-design-failure is a transferable conservation-case register.**
   The specific insight that infrastructure built for one species cannot accommodate another species generalizes beyond lamprey-and-salmon. The register applies wherever mitigation infrastructure was engineered for a dominant-documented species but different-species navigation requirements were not considered. The register may prove reusable for documenting conservation failures in other management contexts.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8408_

6. **First Foods sacred-species documentation requires ceremonial-context honesty.**
   The First Foods status of Pacific Lamprey is not decorative — it is structural to how Columbia River tribes understand their relationship with the species and with the ecological system as a whole. The documentation responsibility extends beyond factual listing to ceremonial-context presentation that reflects the spiritual relationship rather than merely noting its existence.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8409_

7. **The ammocoete phase's 5-7-year invisibility is the engine's most striking life-history finding.**
   The longest larval phase of any Pacific Northwest vertebrate is spent blind, toothless, and buried in stream sediment filter-feeding. The invisibility of the phase makes it easy to omit from casual documentation, but the phase is biologically primary — without successful ammocoete habitat, the entire species has no future. Life-history documentation should foreground invisible-but-critical phases when they exist.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8410_

8. **Cross-degree Celilo Falls anchoring reinforces engine structural coherence.**
   The Celilo Falls 1957 double-loss spans Pacific Salmon (236) and Pacific Lamprey (243) — a seven-degree gap that the engine now bridges with paired documentation of the same historical event's dual cultural consequences. Future cross-degree anchoring opportunities should be identified proactively rather than discovered retroactively, and the degree 243 documentation establishes the explicit-cross-degree-anchoring pattern as a repeatable engine feature.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8411_

9. **The widest biological distance at a single degree is an engine-scale achievement.**
   Holding a 1959 Seattle surf-garage band and a 450-million-year-old jawless vertebrate at the same degree — six decades paired with 450 million years, a factor-of-7.5-million ratio — is the engine's widest single-degree biological span. The achievement is not about extremity for its own sake but about the engine's documentary capacity to sustain interpretive coherence across extreme temporal and biological distances.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.398`) · lesson #8412_

10. **Transitional positions in multi-degree arcs deserve full-weight documentation.**
   The Frantics at the middle of the four-degree garage-rock cluster and Pacific Lamprey at the opening of the ancient-fish arc both occupy transitional positions rather than anchor positions. The documentation principle is that transitional positions carry the same documentary weight as anchor positions; the engine does not concentrate interpretive richness at arc-opener-and-arc-closer endpoints.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8413_

11. **The Frantics are less documented than their Tacoma counterparts.**
   The Tacoma-centric mythology of the Northwest Sound means primary-source material on The Frantics (specific personnel rosters, full recording sessionography, detailed chart history) is thinner than for The Sonics or The Wailers. Future research may surface more specific personnel and recording history; the degree 243 documentation accepts this constraint and documents what is established from available sources.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8414_

12. **The Sea Lamprey (Great Lakes invasive) confusion has harmed Pacific Lamprey conservation.**
   The invasive *Petromyzon marinus* in the Great Lakes is subject to lampricide programs and has created a strongly negative public framework for "lampreys" generally that has sometimes contaminated management discussions of the native Pacific *Lampetra tridentata*. The engine documents this distinction but a more prominent public-communication component would strengthen the record.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8415_

13. **The oral-disc structure's cultural stigma requires careful handling.**
   The Pacific Lamprey's oral disc has generated strong negative cultural responses in European and Euro-American tradition where Indigenous traditions do not carry this stigma. The documentary responsibility is to present the morphology accurately while also presenting the Indigenous perspective that treats the same structure as the anatomy of a sacred food species; the degree 243 documentation does this but the balance requires ongoing attention in future lamprey-related releases.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8416_
