**The V-Shaped Rooster Tail and the Physics of the Bow Wave.** The rooster tail is the Dall's Porpoise's most distinctive observable behavior, and its formation is a direct consequence of the physics of a body moving rapidly just below the water surface. When the porpoise swims at maximum speed near the surface, its body displaces water at high velocity; the combination of the animal's speed, the shape of its body, and its proximity to the surface creates a bow-wave effect that pushes water upward and forward. As the water is pushed upward, it breaks the surface in a characteristic V-shaped plume that rises well above the waterline. The V-shape is a product of the angle at which the bow wave intersects the surface — the spray is not random but has a consistent shape determined by the physics of the animal's passage through the water. The height of the spray is proportional to the speed: the faster the porpoise swims, the taller and more dramatic the rooster tail. The acoustic component of this visual phenomenon is the hiss and rush of water displaced at high velocity, the percussive splash of the spray landing back on the surface, and the churning wake behind the animal — classified as a physical percussive wake rather than a biological vocalization, but acoustically real and audible to observers on nearby vessels or from the shoreline. For a human observer on a **Washington State Ferry** or in Puget Sound on a small boat, the rooster tail is often the first acoustic and visual indicator of a Dall's Porpoise at speed. **In parallel**, Wimps' live-performance register operates through a specific acoustic-and-visual signature as well: the three-piece configuration on a small Seattle club stage, the brisk-tempo compact songs delivered in rapid succession, the sardonic-observation lyrics that produce immediate laughter-and-recognition from the audience, the crunchy-not-saturated guitar tone and the deadpan vocal delivery that together create a recognizable band-identity signature well before any individual song title is identified. **The same** distinctive-signature-as-identity structural principle organizes both halves. **Each side** demonstrates that the engine can document identity-through-signature at first-class structural resolution across biological and cultural registers.
