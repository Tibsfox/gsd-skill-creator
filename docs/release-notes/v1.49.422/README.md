## Summary

**ENERGY DROP. FASTEST SMALL CETACEAN.**

**Wimps** arrive at E=6 -- the Seattle punk trio formed in 2011 by Rachel Ratner (vocals, guitar), Matt Nyce (bass), and Dave Ramm (drums). Wimps have released a consistent string of records on Kill Rock Stars since 2013: *Suitcase* (2013), *Repeat* (2015), *Garbage People* (2017), *Everyday Is Friday* (2020), and *Taste It* (2022). Their sound is fast, short, and deliberately unserious -- songs about being tired, about bad coffee, about the minor irritations of daily life, delivered with the tempo of hardcore and the sensibility of someone who has thought carefully about the joke they are about to tell and committed to telling it straight-faced.

Wimps are a different kind of Seattle punk band than the bands that preceded them in the engine's current sequence. The Gits, Seven Year Bitch, Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney -- these are bands with explicit political commitments, large emotional registers, and in several cases significant losses embedded in their histories. Wimps are not that. Wimps write songs about normal things and play them very fast. This is its own kind of discipline: to write a song about forgetting to call someone back and to make it funny and to play it at hardcore tempo without the song falling apart requires actual craft, and Wimps have it. Kill Rock Stars signing them in 2013 continued the label's practice of recognizing bands that are doing something specific well.

E=6 is the correct assignment. The energy is real -- Wimps play faster than their subject matter suggests -- but the overall intensity is lower than the E=8 run that preceded them. The energy drop from E=8 to E=6 is appropriate. The sequence needed a breath, and Wimps provide it without losing the punk tempo.

**Dall's Porpoise** arrives at E=6 on the SPS track -- *Phocoenoides dalli*, the fastest small cetacean in the Pacific Northwest, capable of sustained speeds of 55 km/h and bursts exceeding that. The Dall's porpoise is distinctive for the rooster tail of water spray it generates while sprinting at the surface -- a high-speed wake that is visible from a distance before the animal itself is clearly resolved. It is also a species that commonly bow-rides ferry and vessel wakes in Puget Sound and the Salish Sea, using the pressure wave ahead of a moving boat to accelerate without expending additional energy. The Dall's porpoise is a common sight on Puget Sound ferry crossings; it is also, in terms of raw speed, among the most impressive small marine mammals in the record.

E=6 for Dall's Porpoise is the correct assignment. The rooster tail sprint is energetic and visually dramatic, but it is not the physical extreme of a full whale breach or the social acoustic complexity of a multi-pod chorus. E=6 reflects the sustained high speed of a small, fast animal doing what small, fast animals do: moving through the water faster than most things its size, and making a visible signature in the process.

The energy alignment between Wimps (E=6) and Dall's Porpoise (E=6) is one of the engine's cleaner pairings: two entries at the same level, both fast by the standards of their context, both operating at a lower register than the extraordinary events that preceded them, and both correct for their position.
