## Summary

v1.49.999 advances the NASA degree catalog from 1.189 to 1.190, adding AMPTE — the Active Magnetospheric Particle Tracer Explorers — as the obs#5 INTRA-AXIS continuation of the reopened near-Earth magnetosphere substrate-axis. The magnetosphere axis reopened at v995 FAST (obs#1, the in-situ high-cadence auroral-acceleration-region snapshot science), was carried at v996 DE-1 (obs#2, the first global auroral imaging), at v997 IMAGE (obs#3, the first dedicated global imaging of the entire inner magnetosphere), and at v998 Polar (obs#4, the multi-wavelength auroral imaging and polar-cusp in-situ sampling), and at v999 AMPTE continues the axis at obs#5 with the first comprehensive in-situ ring-current ion composition joined to the first artificial ion-release tracer experiments. Where Polar imaged the aurora from above in three bands while sampling the polar plasma from within, AMPTE sampled the ring-current ions directly to read their composition by species while releasing lithium and barium ion clouds that traced the plasma flow, adding the in-situ composition and the active tracer-release substrates to the imaging and in-situ foundations the axis already holds.

AMPTE was a NASA Explorers mission flown as a cooperation of the United States, West Germany, and the United Kingdom, with NASA Goddard Space Flight Center managing the United States Charge Composition Explorer. The mission idea brought two methods to the inner magnetosphere at once: a spacecraft carrying charge-energy-mass spectrometers would sample the ring-current ions directly and resolve their composition by species, and a second spacecraft would release clouds of foreign ions into the solar wind and the magnetosphere to act as visible tracers of the plasma flow. AMPTE launched 1984-08-16 from Cape Canaveral on a single Delta 3924 vehicle that carried all three spacecraft to space together: the Charge Composition Explorer built by the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory, the Ion Release Module built by the Max Planck Institute, and the United Kingdom Subsatellite.

The signature of the mission is the first comprehensive in-situ measurement of the ring-current ion composition. The Charge Composition Explorer measured each ring-current ion's charge, energy, and mass together, so the instrument could resolve the ring current into its constituent species rather than treating it as a single population. For the first time the contributions of hydrogen, helium, and oxygen ions could be separated and the energy distribution of each species mapped across the inner magnetosphere, giving the direct-sampling composition picture that the remote ring-current imaging the catalog also holds complemented.

The composition data carried a notable finding: a substantial population of singly-charged oxygen of ionospheric origin within the ring current, establishing the ionosphere as a major source of ring-current material alongside the solar wind. Alongside the composition sampling, the Ion Release Module released clouds of lithium and barium ions in the solar wind and the magnetosphere, producing luminous tracer clouds that, picked up by the surrounding plasma, followed the plasma flow and made the otherwise-invisible motion visible and traceable. The United Kingdom Subsatellite flew near the Ion Release Module so the two spacecraft could measure each release from two nearby points at once, separating spatial structure from temporal change.

The science return joined the direct in-situ composition of the ring current to the active tracing of the plasma flow. The ring current was read directly and revealed as a mixed population drawn from two sources, and the plasma flow that carries and energizes the ring-current ions was made visible through the released clouds. By tracing where the released solar-wind ions traveled, AMPTE studied how mass and energy cross from the solar wind into the magnetosphere, turning a diffuse transfer process into a directly followed path.

The axis-relevant substrate of AMPTE at this degree is its continuation of the reopened near-Earth magnetosphere axis at obs#5 with the in-situ ring-current composition and the artificial tracer releases. AMPTE sustains the MAGNETOSPHERE-AXIS-INTRA-CONTINUATION thread at obs#5 cumulative (v995 FAST + v996 DE-1 + v997 IMAGE + v998 Polar + v999 AMPTE), it extends the RING-CURRENT-OBSERVATION-LINEAGE thread at obs#2 cumulative (v997 IMAGE remote + v999 AMPTE in-situ, AMPTE supplying the direct composition picture the remote imaging complemented), and it sustains the INTERNATIONAL-COOPERATION thread cumulatively (v1.174 Geotail + v1.185 Cassini-Huygens + v999 AMPTE). As the obs#5 entry on the reopened axis, AMPTE adds the in-situ composition and the active tracer-release substrates, and it sets the composition-and-tracer anchor from which the reopened magnetosphere axis will grow. The forward continuation favors further inner-magnetosphere, substorm, reconnection, and ionosphere-coupling missions that build on the foundations now established.

AMPTE is the first three-nation three-spacecraft cooperation of its kind in the catalog, and the cooperation records the joint mission of the United States, West Germany, and the United Kingdom. ## Lessons Learned

1. **An axis can be deepened by direct in-situ sampling after imaging.** DE-1, IMAGE, and Polar imaged the magnetosphere from space, and AMPTE then sampled the ring current in place to read its composition by species, showing that an axis can deepen by adding a direct in-situ measurement to the imaging foundations, an obs#5 continuation that complements remote views with a direct sample.
2. **Composition by species changes what a population is understood to be.** Before AMPTE the ring current was studied through its bulk energy and its magnetic effect, but resolving the ions by charge, energy, and mass revealed it as a mixed population of solar-wind and ionospheric sources, showing that a composition measurement can reshape the understanding of a population that bulk measurements treated as one.
3. **The ionosphere supplies the ring current from below.** AMPTE found singly-charged oxygen of ionospheric origin in the ring current, showing that the upper atmosphere feeds the energetic inner magnetosphere from below alongside the solar wind from outside, a source-attribution result that established a second source for the ring-current material.
4. **An active tracer makes a diffuse flow into a followed path.** The released lithium and barium clouds, picked up by the surrounding plasma, followed the plasma flow and made the otherwise-invisible motion visible, showing that an active experiment can turn a diffuse and otherwise-inferred process into a directly followed path, a method distinct from passive observation.
5. **Two nearby spacecraft separate space from time.** The Ion Release Module and the United Kingdom Subsatellite measured each release from two nearby points, showing that a paired vantage can separate the spatial structure of a feature from its change in time where a single spacecraft cannot, a forerunner of the later closely-spaced multi-spacecraft missions.
6. **A single launch can place complementary instruments in distinct regions.** The single Delta 3924 carried the composition sampler and the release-and-diagnostic pair to their separate regions at once, showing that a three-spacecraft single-launch architecture can put complementary instruments where each is needed and operate them together.
7. **An international cooperation can fly one mission across three nations.** AMPTE joined the United States, West Germany, and the United Kingdom in one mission of three coordinated spacecraft, showing that a shared mission can distribute the spacecraft and the science across several nations while operating as a single coordinated whole.
8. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across a distinct-palette mission.** Because AMPTE reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.189 template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content to the in-situ composition and active tracer releases with a distinct barium-green / lithium-red tracer-cloud and ring-current-amber palette, making the distinct-palette mission a clean build.

## Surprises

- **The ring current has an ionospheric face.** The ring current was long thought of as a solar-wind population, but AMPTE found a major share of singly-charged oxygen of ionospheric origin, so the catalog's first in-situ ring-current composition revealed the upper atmosphere feeding the energetic inner magnetosphere from below.
- **A current-tracing salmon mirrors a flow-tracing mission.** The Sockeye Salmon, which follows the moving currents of the ocean across years and returns to its natal stream, enters as the tracer-of-flow mirror to the active tracer-release mission, so the catalog pairs a current-tracing fish with a plasma-flow-tracing mission.
- **A released cloud can be seen across the sky.** The barium clouds AMPTE released glowed brightly in sunlight as they were ionized, so the otherwise-invisible plasma flow could be watched directly as a luminous drifting cloud, an active experiment that made the flow a visible path.

## Dedication

v1.190 AMPTE is dedicated to the team that built and flew the Active Magnetospheric Particle Tracer Explorers: the AMPTE science team who designed a mission to sample the ring-current composition directly while tracing the plasma flow with released ion clouds; the Charge Composition Explorer team at the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Laboratory who measured the ring-current ions by species and found the ionospheric-oxygen source; the Max Planck Institute Ion Release Module team who released the lithium and barium clouds that made the plasma flow visible; the United Kingdom Subsatellite team who provided the second nearby vantage on each release; and the NASA Goddard Space Flight Center team who managed the United States spacecraft. It is dedicated as well to the wider community of magnetospheric physicists whose work AMPTE advanced, the in-situ composition sampling and the active tracer releases that joined the ring current to its sources and the plasma flow to a visible path. AMPTE launched in 1984 on a single vehicle carrying three spacecraft of three nations, and it joined the ionosphere directly to the energetic inner magnetosphere.
