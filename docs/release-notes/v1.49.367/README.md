# v1.49.367 -- Degree 226: Wellwater Conspiracy + Cooper's Hawk

**Release Date:** 2026-04-03

## Summary

**Wellwater Conspiracy** arrives at E=6 -- the side project of **Matt Cameron**, Soundgarden's drummer and later Pearl Jam's drummer, making music that goes somewhere neither of his primary bands would travel. Formed in Seattle in the mid-1990s with Ben McMillan (vocals, formerly of Skin Yard and later Candlebox -- the Skin Yard connection links back to *Deep Six*, 1986, the founding document) and John McBain (guitar, Monster Magnet), Wellwater Conspiracy is the band that Matt Cameron made when he had ideas that didn't fit anywhere else. The music is psychedelic space rock -- heavy, slow, distortion-saturated, closer to Hawkwind or early Black Sabbath's cosmic side than to the grunge template that Cameron was otherwise operating within. *Declaration of Conformity* (1999) and *Brotherhood of Electric* (2002) are records that exist in a parallel universe from the commercial rock world, made by a world-class drummer who chose to spend his creative surplus on something genuinely strange. At E=6, the band sits in the committed middle register -- present and weighted, not explosive, not quiet, but occupying the space with conviction. The **Matt Cameron side project** designation makes this the third consecutive supergroup/side project in the engine (224: Mad Season, 225: Temple of the Dog, 226: Wellwater Conspiracy), completing the **GRUNGE SUPERGROUP CLUSTER** that began at degree 224 and documenting the creative ecosystem that surrounded the Seattle scene's major figures -- the bands they formed when the primary projects couldn't hold everything they were making. The cluster's three entries span Alice in Chains + Pearl Jam (Mad Season), Soundgarden + Pearl Jam + Mother Love Bone (Temple of the Dog), and Soundgarden + Skin Yard + Monster Magnet (Wellwater Conspiracy): a dense web of connections spanning the entire scope of the Seattle scene's founding generation. On the species side, the **Cooper's Hawk** registers at E=5 and continues the **ACCIPITER TRIO** at its middle position. *Accipiter cooperii* is the suburban accipiter -- the hawk most likely to be seen in a backyard, hunting at a bird feeder, navigating a city park, threading through a subdivision at full speed in pursuit of a starling or a house sparrow. Where the Northern Goshawk is a forest interior specialist, the Cooper's Hawk has adapted to human-modified landscapes more thoroughly than any other North American raptor of its size, and its success in cities and suburbs has made it one of the most frequently observed hawks in the Pacific Northwest. The *cak-cak-cak* alarm call is shorter and slightly lower than the Goshawk's *ka-ka-ka-ka*, reflecting the size difference: same rhythmic pattern, same rapid repetition, different frequency and carrying distance.

- Cooper's Hawk as urban adaptor -- the Cooper's Hawk's success in cities and suburbs is one of the more dramatic ecological stories of the late 20th century Pacific Northwest; populations that were suppressed or absent from urban areas in the 1970s have expanded dramatically as the hawk learned to exploit the concentrated prey available at bird feeders and in ornamental plantings; Seattle's Cooper's Hawks are now year-round urban residents, nesting in residential neighborhoods from Ballard to Rainier Valley; they are fast enough to pursue prey through dense shrubbery and agile enough to navigate between buildings at full hunting speed
- Space rock as genre position -- Wellwater Conspiracy's space rock orientation is the engine's first entry in this genre, and it arrives at a moment when the catalog has been building through post-punk, post-hardcore, and post-grunge toward something more experimental; the genre connects to the psychedelia that Love Battery introduced at degree 221, suggesting that the engine's movement through the grunge era is also documenting its outer edges, the bands that were always reaching beyond the template's limits
