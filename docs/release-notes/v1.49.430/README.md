## Summary

**FOURTH CONSECUTIVE E=9. THE STREAK CLOSES. FIRST LARIDAE.**

**Botch (return)** arrive at E=9 -- the Tacoma mathcore band returning to the engine for a second entry, this time marking the close of the four-degree E=9 hardcore crescendo. Botch formed in Tacoma in 1993 around Dave Verellen (vocals), Brian Cook (guitar), Tim Latona (bass), and Jade Devitt (drums), recording *We Are the Romans* (1999) on Hydra Head Records -- a record that became the definitional document of Pacific Northwest mathcore and one of the most technically demanding records in the American hardcore-derived music record. Botch disbanded in 2002 and reunited in 2022 with the lineup of Verellen, Cook, Latona, and Tim Utvich, recording *...And We Disappeared* (2023), the first new Botch material in two decades.

The return entry at degree 289 acknowledges the reunion and its significance: a mathcore band that defines a genre returning two decades later and producing new material that does not diminish the original catalog is an event in the Pacific Northwest music record. *...And We Disappeared* confirmed that the Botch sound is not a historical artifact but a living approach to music that the members can access and extend. The E=9 assignment holds.

**FOURTH CONSECUTIVE E=9 -- THE STREAK CLOSES.** Degrees 286-289 constitute the engine's first four-consecutive-degree E=9 streak on the S36 track: Dead Kennedys influence (286), Black Flag influence (287), Blood Brothers HC (288), Botch return (289). The four-entry streak is the most sustained high-energy sequence in the engine's S36 history. The streak closes at degree 289. What comes next (degree 290, These Arms Are Snakes, E=8) is not a collapse but a resolution: E=8 is high, the energy level drops one unit, and the post-hardcore sequence continues at a level that would have been the ceiling through degree 285. The streak is a distinct episode in the engine's energy record, bounded on both sides, and Botch closing it is correct.

Botch is from Tacoma. Tacoma's place in the Pacific Northwest music record is complicated by its proximity to Seattle -- close enough to be part of the same scene, distinct enough to maintain its own character. Botch's mathcore is Tacoma music in the sense that it carries the industrial, workingman's city quality that distinguishes Tacoma from Seattle's more explicitly cultural self-presentation. *We Are the Romans* is not an aspirational record; it is a record made by people who are working out something difficult, and the difficulty is the point.

**Glaucous-winged Gull** arrives at E=6 on the SPS track -- the colonial vocalizations of *Larus glaucescens*, the Glaucous-winged Gull, the most common large gull in the Pacific Northwest and the defining gull of Puget Sound, the Washington coast, and the Salish Sea. The Glaucous-winged Gull's vocalizations include the characteristic gull mew call and an alarm call used in colony defense, both produced in quantity at breeding colonies on offshore rocks, coastal cliffs, and flat-topped structures throughout the range. The Glaucous-winged Gull is the gull of the Pacific Northwest in the same way that the Herring Gull is the gull of the Atlantic coast -- ubiquitous, adaptable, loud, and present at every interface of land and sea.

**FIRST LARIDAE.** The Glaucous-winged Gull is the first member of family Laridae -- gulls and terns -- to enter the SPS track. Laridae will occupy four consecutive SPS entries (degrees 289-292): Glaucous-winged Gull, Herring Gull, Western Gull, and Common Tern. The Laridae arc is the longest single-family sequence in the seabird phase and documents the acoustic diversity within the gull-tern family from the Pacific Northwest's defining large gull to the colonial tern.

E=6 for the Glaucous-winged Gull's mew and alarm calls is correct. The individual gull call is familiar and present but not extreme; the colonial context adds acoustic density but does not elevate the energy to the higher scores in the SPS hierarchy. The gull acoustic environment is the ambient sound of the Pacific Northwest shoreline -- always present, always recognizable, and at E=6 correctly placed above the storm petrel's midnight chatter and equal to the Double-crested Cormorant's colonial display.
