## Douglas Fir structural acoustics


- the Douglas Fir's acoustic properties in high wind are functions of its structural architecture: the buttressed root system that allows the trunk to flex without toppling, the thick bark that produces a lower-frequency creak than thin-barked species, the crown's upswept branch structure that catches and disperses wind differently than flat-crowned species, and the sheer mass of an old-growth specimen that means the resonant frequencies of trunk flexion are measurable as low-frequency vibration through the ground as well as audible through the air; the Douglas Fir gale is a multi-frequency acoustic event spanning from infrasonic trunk resonance to the high-frequency hiss of wind through needles
