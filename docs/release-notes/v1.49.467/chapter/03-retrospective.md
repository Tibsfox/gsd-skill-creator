The lesson is that subject documentation benefits from multi-entry arcs at first-class resolution for subjects genuinely complex enough to require multiple entry points, and the arc structure (three positions, three calibrations, three distinct analytical frames) preserves the full subject at the engine's first-class resolution rather than collapsing the subject to a single entry's analytical frame.
2. **The mechanism of an acoustic property is analytically distinct from the category attribution of the property and should be documented at first-class resolution.** Truly's E=6 heaviness and Mudhoney's E=7 heaviness are both "heavy" at the category-attribution level but differ at the mechanism level: arrangement density vs. tonal aggression. The lesson is that documentation benefits from mechanism-specificity at first-class resolution rather than category-level attribution, and the mechanism-distinction preserves genre-boundary specificity that category-attribution would blur into a uniform "heavy" label.
3. **Compound acoustic events document through their simultaneous mechanisms at appropriate physics resolution rather than at narrative-description generality.** The Douglas Fir Crown in Gale is three simultaneous mechanisms (aerodynamic hiss, structural groan, Aeolian moan) with different frequency bands, different temporal behaviors, and different physics specifications.
