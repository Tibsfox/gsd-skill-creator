# Lessons — v1.49.1001

11 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **Two spacecraft a known distance apart separate structure from motion.**
   A single spacecraft crossing a moving boundary cannot tell a change in space from a change in time; two spacecraft a known distance apart resolve the ambiguity through the timing between their crossings, showing that the multi-point measurement turns a long-standing ambiguity into a directly measurable quantity.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11684_

2. **A controllable baseline tunes the resolvable scale.**
   By adjusting the separation between the mother and daughter spacecraft, ISEE-1/2 set the scale of structure it could resolve, closing in for a thin layer and spreading out for a wider feature, showing that a controllable baseline makes a two-spacecraft pair a tunable instrument for the boundaries.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11685_

3. **The two-point measurement reveals discrete reconnection events.**
   The two-point field data resolved the flux transfer events at the dayside magnetopause, showing that the multi-point measurement reveals magnetic reconnection proceeding in discrete bursts that carry plasma across the boundary, rather than only as a steady flow.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11686_

4. **A mother-daughter pair is the precursor of a constellation.**
   ISEE-1/2 flew two spacecraft in coordinated formation, the coordinated multi-point method the later four- and five-spacecraft constellations carried forward, showing that a two-spacecraft pair can pioneer a technique that scales to many spacecraft.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11687_

5. **A cooperation can fly one mission across two agencies.**
   ISEE-1/2 joined NASA and the European Space Agency in one mission, NASA Goddard leading the ISEE-1 mother spacecraft and the European Space Agency operating the ISEE-2 daughter spacecraft, showing that a shared mission can distribute the two spacecraft across two agencies while operating as a single coordinated pair.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11688_

6. **A distinct mission must be kept distinct from a similarly named one.**
   ISEE-1/2 is the near-Earth boundary-physics pair and is distinct from ISEE-3/ICE, the separate third ISEE spacecraft at the Sun-Earth L1 point already in the catalog at degree 1.99, showing that a program with several spacecraft needs each entry kept distinct in the catalog.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11689_

7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across a distinct-palette mission.**
   Because ISEE-1/2 reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.191 template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content to the variable-separation boundary physics with a distinct magnetopause-blue / bow-shock-teal / flux-transfer-violet / mother-gold palette, making the distinct-palette mission a clean build.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11690_

8. **The shader renders procedural structure rather than archived data.**
   The ISEE-1/2 two-point boundary-crossing shader uses analytic geometry and procedural noise rather than loading actual ISEE-1 and ISEE-2 magnetometer or plasma data from the NASA Space Physics Data Facility. A future revision could load encoded ISEE two-point field data for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to the real data.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11691_

9. **The formation diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the two spacecraft, the boundaries, and the close formation.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11692_

10. **The multi-spacecraft / boundary-physics sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.**
   The sidebar lists representative program elements (ISEE-1/2, AMPTE, CRRES, ISEE-3/ICE, THEMIS, MMS) but does not enumerate the full multi-spacecraft fleet; a future revision could expand the table with launch dates and spacecraft counts.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11693_

11. **The two-point timing is described rather than computed.**
   The two-point timing that recovers the boundary normal and velocity is described but not run against archived data; a future ship could compute the boundary parameters from real ISEE-1/2 crossing data.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11694_
