## Summary

**Pedro the Lion** arrives at E=4 -- David Bazan's one-man theological crisis rendered as spare, confessional indie rock. Pedro the Lion emerged from the **Greenwood** neighborhood and released early records on **Jade Tree**, the Philadelphia label that bridged hardcore and indie, giving Bazan a national platform from a distinctly local foundation. Albums like *It's Hard to Find a Friend* (1998) and *Control* (2002) are concept records about faith, doubt, alcoholism, and suburban despair, delivered with a deliberate quietness that makes every word carry moral weight. At E=4, Pedro the Lion occupies the same restrained register as 764-HERO (196) -- this is indie rock that whispers its crises rather than screaming them. On the species side, the **Roosevelt Elk cow call** registers at E=5 -- the middle entry in the elk trio (197-199). The cow call is fundamentally different from the bull's bugle: a nasal, barking **mew** used for contact between cows and calves in the herd. Where the bugle is a broadcast across valleys, the cow call is a conversation within the group -- directional, purposeful, and pitched to cut through the ambient noise of a rainforest understory.
