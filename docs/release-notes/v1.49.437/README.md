## Summary

**ANDREW WOOD PIVOT. PEARL JAM ORIGIN. RAPTOR BEHAVIORS BEGIN. 82.2%.**

**Mother Love Bone** arrive at E=7 -- the Seattle glam grunge band that existed for one complete album, one trajectory interrupted, and one loss that changed the history of Pacific Northwest rock. Mother Love Bone formed in 1987 when Andrew Wood (Malfunkshun, degree 295) joined Jeff Ament (Green River, degree 294) and Stone Gossard (Green River, degree 294) in the project that would synthesize the two proto-grunge trajectories into something new. The lineup also included Bruce Fairweather (guitar) and Greg Gilmore (drums), and the band signed to Polygram -- a major label deal that signaled the possibility of a post-Sub Pop future for Seattle rock. *Apple* was completed and was days from release when Andrew Wood died of a heroin overdose on March 19, 1990. He was 24. The album was released posthumously. The band dissolved. What came next, from the musicians who survived, was Pearl Jam.

Mother Love Bone is degree three of the proto-grunge origin arc (294-296). Green River (294) → Malfunkshun (295) → Mother Love Bone (296): this is the direct genealogy. The arc closes here. Ament and Gossard went forward from this point, eventually recruiting Eddie Vedder (from San Diego, before he was a Seattle musician) and forming the band that would become one of the best-selling rock acts of the 1990s. Chris Cornell (Soundgarden) processed Wood's death into the Temple of the Dog record (degree 225), which documented the scene's grief and introduced Vedder's voice to the world. Both downstream consequences are documented in the Seattle 360 engine; Mother Love Bone is the origin point they both radiate from.

The glam grunge designation is accurate. Mother Love Bone was not a grunge band in the simple sense: Wood's lyrical theatrics -- the glam influences from Malfunkshun carried forward, the Marc Bolan references, the flamboyant self-presentation -- were not part of grunge's aesthetic vocabulary. But the band's rhythm section (Ament on bass, Gossard and Fairweather on guitar) was the same rhythm section that would anchor Pearl Jam's early records, and the sound they were developing was the bridge between proto-grunge and what grunge became. E=7 is the correct assignment: the energy is high, the historical weight is extraordinary, and the glam-grunge hybrid places the band at the same level as Malfunkshun while occupying a different genre register.

**Peregrine Stoop** arrives at E=8 on the SPS track -- the acoustic event produced by a Peregrine Falcon (*Falco peregrinus*) in a high-speed stoop: the sonic crack generated as the bird reaches speeds approaching 240 mph in a vertical dive, the aerodynamic sound of controlled speed at its physical limit. The Peregrine previously entered the SPS track at degree 231 with its sharp *kak-kak-kak* alarm call at E=7. At degree 296 the engine documents a different Peregrine behavior and a significantly higher energy event: not the call, but the stoop itself, the physical act that makes the Peregrine the fastest animal on Earth. The sonic crack produced by a Peregrine at terminal dive velocity is not a vocalization -- it is a physical sound, the acoustic consequence of a body moving through air at a speed that generates audible aerodynamic phenomena. E=8 is the correct assignment: this is a new register, a new sound, and a new energy level for the Peregrine's SPS representation.

The raptor behaviors arc opens here. Degrees 296-298 document three consecutive raptor behavioral sounds rather than vocalizations: the Peregrine stoop (296), Bald Eagle talon combat (297), and Goshawk full alarm assault (298). Where the raptor arc earlier in the engine (degrees 221-235) documented raptor calls and vocalizations, this new sequence documents raptor physical behaviors -- the sounds raptors make through action rather than voice.
