# v1.49.368 -- Degree 227: Hovercraft + Sharp-shinned Hawk

**Release Date:** 2026-04-03
**Type:** Seattle 360 / Sound of Puget Sound -- Degree 227 of 360

## Summary

**Hovercraft** arrives at E=5 -- the band that made no concessions to accessibility, produced no vocals, left no bridges for the casual listener to cross, and documented one of the most pure expressions of noise rock and art rock that Seattle's mid-1990s underground produced. Beth Liebling (bass), Eddie Vedder's then-wife and later ex-wife, and Geoff Byrd (drums) and Talitha Borealis (electronics) made music that treated the rock format as raw material rather than template: the songs don't resolve, the noise doesn't peak and release on the standard timeline, the instrumental architecture is its own reward. Hovercraft released on Man's Ruin Records and built a reputation that exists almost entirely in the underground -- few mainstream reviews, no radio play, a catalog that rewards patience and attention rather than immediate engagement. At E=5, the band registers at the quieter end of the raptor arc's musical cluster, which suits the music: Hovercraft is not aggressive in the conventional rock sense, but in the more unsettling sense of art that doesn't behave. The **instrumental (no vocals)** designation is its own statement -- degree 227 is the engine's first explicitly instrumental entry in the Seattle 360 track in many degrees, and it arrives at a moment when the catalog has been densely populated with vocalists: Carrie Akre, Layne Staley, Chris Cornell, Eddie Vedder, Kevin Martin. Hovercraft removes the voice and asks what the music does without it. The answer, in their case, is considerable. The Beth Liebling / Eddie Vedder connection places Hovercraft in the Pearl Jam orbit without making it a Pearl Jam satellite -- Liebling's music is genuinely its own, made on her own terms, and the connection is biographical rather than musical. On the species side, the **Sharp-shinned Hawk** registers at E=5 and completes the **ACCIPITER TRIO** as its smallest member. *Accipiter striatus* is the smallest hawk in North America: a sparrow hawk in the literal sense, a bird that weighs as little as 3 ounces (males are significantly smaller than females in all accipiters, but the size gap is most extreme in the Sharp-shinned), that hunts songbirds through dense vegetation with a speed and agility that seems implausible for a vertebrate. The *high kik-kik-kik* call is lighter and faster than the Cooper's Hawk's *cak-cak-cak* -- same rhythmic pattern, higher pitch, shorter duration, the vocal signature of the smallest member of the genus. The Sharp-shinned Hawk is the most migratory of the accipiters, moving through the Pacific Northwest in concentrated migration pulses in September and October, sometimes numbering in the thousands at ridge-top hawkwatch sites in the Cascades. The accipiter trio is complete at degree 227: Goshawk (225), Cooper's (226), Sharp-shinned (227), moving from largest to smallest, forest interior to urban to migratory, high alarm to mid-alarm to high kik.

- **Part A -- S36:** Hovercraft (Art Rock, E=5) -- INSTRUMENTAL (no vocals), Beth Liebling, Man's Ruin Records, noise rock, Eddie Vedder connection
- **Part B -- SPS:** Sharp-shinned Hawk (high kik-kik-kik, E=5) -- ACCIPITER TRIO CLOSES, smallest North American accipiter, migratory, songbird specialist
- ACCIPITER TRIO CLOSES (225-227) -- degree 227 completes the three-degree accipiter sub-arc within the raptor arc; the trio documented all three North American accipiters in order from largest to smallest: Northern Goshawk (225, E=6, forest apex), Cooper's Hawk (226, E=5, suburban adaptor), Sharp-shinned Hawk (227, E=5, migratory specialist); the trio demonstrates how a single genus can partition ecological space across three size classes, three habitat types, and three migration strategies; the accipiter documentation is among the most detailed taxonomic coverage in the engine's history
- First instrumental entry -- Hovercraft's absence of vocals marks the first explicitly instrumental entry in the S36 track in recent memory; the engine has cataloged instrumentals before (Kinski's extended post-rock at degree 213 leans instrumental), but Hovercraft is the first band in the current run where the absence of vocals is a defining creative decision rather than an incidental feature; the contrast with the immediately preceding degrees -- Layne Staley (224), Chris Cornell and Eddie Vedder (225), Matt Cameron with Ben McMillan (226) -- is complete
- Beth Liebling and peripheral orbits -- the engine consistently documents not just the scene's central figures but the people in their immediate orbit who were making music independently; Liebling's presence in Hovercraft documents the creative ecology surrounding Pearl Jam without reducing her to a footnote in Vedder's biography; she was making genuinely distinctive art rock on her own terms, and degree 227 records that fact
- Sharp-shinned Hawk migration -- the Sharp-shinned Hawk is one of the most significant raptors in Pacific Northwest hawkwatch data; at sites like Chelan Ridge and the Cascades Raptor Center's monitoring stations, Sharp-shinned Hawks constitute the majority of counted raptors during fall migration; their migration is concentrated, predictable, and spectacular in scale; on peak days in October, hundreds can pass a single ridge-top observation point in an hour; the species that seems smallest and most easily overlooked is, in terms of migration volume, among the most abundant raptors in the western flyway
- Man's Ruin Records -- the San Francisco-based label that documented much of the late-1990s underground's intersection of stoner rock, psychedelia, and art rock; Hovercraft's presence on Man's Ruin connects the Seattle 360 catalog to a label that was simultaneously releasing bands like Fu Manchu, Queens of the Stone Age (early), and Nebula -- a cross-regional network of heavy, experimental underground rock that operated entirely outside the mainstream conversation
- Raptor arc penultimate -- degree 227 is the seventh of eight raptor entries; the arc is one degree from its conclusion; the Red-tailed Hawk at degree 228 will close both the accipiter arc's successor and the arc itself, and will deliver the Hollywood call correction that the engine planted at degree 221 with the Bald Eagle's thin *kee-kee-kee*
