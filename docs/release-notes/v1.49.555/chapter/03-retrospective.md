# Retrospective — v1.49.555

## Lessons Learned

1. **First successful Surveyor after failure.** Surveyor 3 proved that the enhanced valve testing and modified propellant routing developed after Surveyor 2's loss worked. All three vernier engines fired for every maneuver. The specific failure mode that destroyed Surveyor 2 was eliminated. The Surveyor program was back on track at two successes and one failure through three of seven missions.

2. **First robotic digging on the Moon.** The soil mechanics surface sampler -- a remotely operated scoop on a retractable arm -- was the first tool built by humans to manipulate the surface of another world. Four trenches, bearing strength tests (3-8 kPa), impact tests, pickup and dump trials. The surface was firm and cohesive, adequate for the Lunar Module.

3. **Triple bounce introduces new failure category.** Surveyor 2's failure was mechanical (valve). Surveyor 3's anomaly was informational (sensor provided incorrect data, system acted correctly on incorrect information). The radar altimeter/Doppler velocity sensor was confused by reflective surface. Fixing one problem revealed the next. The triple bounce led to radar modifications for Surveyor 5-7 and backup timer engine shutdown logic.

4. **The folk-adjacent run extends to seven consecutive degrees.** Degrees 45-51: Jurado (Folk), Votolato (Folk), Bazan (Indie Folk), Cahoone (Folk), Dawson (Folk Punk), Mirah (Indie Folk), Grand Archives (Indie Folk). The longest genre-family streak in engine history, surpassing the six-degree record set at degree 50. The communal harmony is the seventh distinct acoustic element in the run.

5. **The Carissa's Wierd genealogy deepens to two degrees from one band.** Sera Cahoone at 48 (drummer, solo folk, E=2) and Mat Brooke / Grand Archives at 51 (co-founder, Indie Folk, E=3). Same band, three degrees apart, both Sub Pop, both folk-adjacent. The deepest single-band diaspora in the engine.

6. **Third Sub Pop at three-degree intervals.** Jurado (45), Cahoone (48), Grand Archives (51). The label that launched grunge is the folk zone's metronome. Every third folk-adjacent degree is a Sub Pop release.

7. **First Certhiidae -- the 20th family, monotypic.** The Brown Creeper enters as the sole representative of its family in North America and in the engine. One genus, one species, one degree for all 360 degrees. The first genuinely new family since Bombycillidae at degree 31 -- a 20-degree gap between new-family events.

8. **E=1 returns after 38-degree absence.** The longest drought of any energy level in the engine. Lower registers (E=1 + E=2 + E=3) now hold 53.8% majority for the first time. The four E=1 species form the invisible choir: Golden-crowned Kinglet (8), Bushtit (10), Pine Siskin (12), Brown Creeper (51).

9. **Complementary bark dyad completed.** Brown Creeper ascending (degree 51) and Red-breasted Nuthatch descending (degree 5). Forty-six degrees apart, opposite in every behavioral dimension: foraging direction, bill shape, plumage strategy, voice, detection ease. Together: complete bark-surface coverage. The engine's deepest structural callback.

10. **The Conrad thread extends.** Pete Conrad flew Gemini 11 (degree 49), was eight days post-splashdown when Surveyor 2 failed (degree 50), and will walk to Surveyor 3 on Apollo 12 -- landing within 163 meters, retrieving the TV camera and surface sampler scoop. The pilot becomes the archeologist, visiting the artifact that proved the failure had been fixed.
