1. **Geology is architecture at planetary timescale.**
   The Gorge's acoustic properties are not an accident and not a design -- they are the emergent result of three geological processes (basalt deposition, Cascade uplift, catastrophic flooding) operating across three different timescales (millions of years, millions of years, thousands of years). The Amiga Principle: constraint produces the conditions for resonance. The basalt did not intend to focus sound. It does.

2. **Indigenous history is not context -- it is the primary story.**
   The Wanapum carved 300+ petroglyphs into the same basalt cliffs that form the amphitheatre's natural walls. They were present when Missoula's last floods carved the terrain. They greeted Lewis and Clark. They survived dam construction and displacement. Their Heritage Center stands 20 miles from the venue. Omitting this history from venue documentation is not a gap -- it is an erasure. This module corrects it.

3. **Acoustic serendipity is the signature of the Amiga Principle.**
   Dr. Bryan did not set out to build a concert venue. He was hiking his vineyard property and noticed he could hear every word spoken a thousand feet below. The entire 40-year arc of the Gorge Amphitheatre -- from 3,000-person plywood stage to 27,500-capacity Live Nation crown jewel -- originates from one person listening to what the landscape was already doing. The best architectures are discovered, not designed.

4. **Corporate ownership chains carry cultural consequences.**
   Bryan (family) -> MCA ($3.9M, corporate) -> House of Blues (entertainment brand) -> Live Nation (global monopoly). Each transition changed not just the business model but the cultural character of the venue. The progression from winery concert to Pollstar-winning amphitheatre is also a progression from local to global, from personal to corporate, from serendipity to strategy. Both stories are true simultaneously.
