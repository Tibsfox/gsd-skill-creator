## Summary

**The amphitheatre was not built — it was discovered.** v1.49.133 "The Gorge" (code GRG) is the 169th entry in the PNW Research Series and the first research release to ship under the audit methodology codified one day earlier by v1.49.132 AAR. The project traces the full history of the Gorge Amphitheatre in George, Washington across five integrated research modules: geological genesis (17 million years of Columbia River Basalt Group eruptions through Cascade uplift and the Missoula Floods), Wanapum Nation Indigenous history (pre-contact village P'na through the 2015 Heritage Center), the Bryan family founding era (1980 vineyard purchase through 1993 sale), corporate ownership and physical development (MCA → House of Blues → Live Nation), and the venue's musical legacy (Dave Matthews Band's 79 shows, Pearl Jam's *Live at the Gorge 05/06*, Phish's 22 appearances, and the Sasquatch!-to-Bass Canyon festival ecosystem). The through-line — geological serendipity that became acoustic revelation that became cultural institution — is the Amiga Principle stretched across four radically different timescales.

**Geology is architecture at planetary timescale.** The acoustic properties that let 27,500 people hear a guitar note from a quarter-mile away are not an accident, and they are not a design. They are the emergent result of three distinct geological processes operating across three different timescales: Columbia River Flood Basalt eruptions (17-6 Ma, 41,000 cubic miles of lava, ~21 vent systems depositing the basalt bowl), Cascade Range uplift (3 Ma, tilting the Columbia Plateau and carving the canyon walls), and the Missoula Floods (19,000-13,000 BP, 40+ catastrophic outburst floods, 10 cubic km/hour discharge, 36 m/s flood speeds finishing the excavation). Module M1 Geological Genesis traces all three timescales through a geological timeline table with six entries from CRBG eruptions through Lewis & Clark contact, and establishes the Amiga Principle's strongest single evidence instance in the corpus: constraint (basalt walls, cliffside bowl, unobstructed sky) producing resonance (focused sound, three-sided reflection, 360-degree sightlines) without any designing agent.

**Indigenous history is the primary story, not the context.** Module M2 Indigenous Deep History is the release's most load-bearing research module and the one that most determined the project's ethical boundaries. The Wanapum people (Sahaptin: *Wanapam*, "river people" from *wana* + *-pam*) lived at village P'na, two miles downstream of Priest Rapids Dam, and carved more than 300 petroglyphs into the same basalt cliffs that form the amphitheatre's natural backdrop. They were present during the Missoula Floods. They greeted Lewis and Clark in October 1805 (Chief Cutssahnem). David Thompson's journals contain the first written description of their villages on July 8-9, 1811. Smohalla, the nineteenth-century prophet of the Washani "Dreamer Religion" (c. 1815-1895), taught that land cannot be owned — a doctrine that shaped Wanapum non-participation in the 1855 Yakama Treaty negotiations, which Governor Isaac Stevens conducted without knowing the Wanapum existed. The Manhattan Project displaced approximately 30 Wanapum in 1942 for the Hanford Engineer Works. Priest Rapids Dam (1953) and Wanapum Dam (1966) flooded ancestral riverbanks. The Heritage Center (completed 2015, 50,000 sq ft, Grant County PUD cooperative arrangement) anchors the modern Wanapum presence. The release's Wanapum-specific attribution rule — never "Indigenous peoples" generically, always "Wanapum" specifically — is not a stylistic preference; it is the boundary that distinguishes research documentation from erasure, and it is the module's most important standing commitment to the communities the catalog touches.

**The founding era is a story about listening.** Module M3 Founding Era (1980-1993) is the release's smallest module in line count but its largest in thematic weight. Dr. Vincent Bryan II and Carol Bryan purchased several hundred acres near George, Washington in 1980 for vineyard development and established the Champs de Brionne winery. The founding event — documented in the 2021 *Enormous: The Gorge Story* documentary — is a single moment on a hike: Bryan, standing at the canyon rim, realized he could hear every word spoken a thousand feet below at the river bench. The 1986 inaugural season ran as "Champs de Brionne Music Theatre" with a 3,000-person capacity, a small wooden stage, and "hastily-laid sod terraces." Bob Dylan's 1988 show exposed the capacity-management crisis the venue would spend the next five years resolving, expanding to 15,000 (1988) and 19,000 (1993). The final Champs de Brionne show was Steve Miller Band with Paul Rodgers on August 14, 1993. Bryan's post-sale legacy — retaining the surrounding vineyards, partnering with Brian Carter to establish Cave B Estate Winery in 2000 (restaurant, lodging, wine adjacent to the amphitheatre) — is itself evidence that the founding insight was not a business plan but an attention discipline that continued after the commercial arc changed hands.

**Corporate ownership chains carry cultural consequences.** Module M4 Corporate Era and Physical Development documents the ownership lineage that took the venue from a 3,000-person vineyard theatre to a 27,500-capacity Live Nation flagship. MCA Concerts acquired the venue for $3.9M in 1993 (per Seattle Times archive August 4, 2002), invested $2M in infrastructure, and negotiated the Grant County 5% ticket fee (1994, ~$275,000 first-year revenue to the county). House of Blues took ownership in 1999, grew annual attendance to 300,000 by 2001, and pushed county ticket fees past $700,000. Live Nation acquired the venue in 2006 and expanded capacity to 20,000-27,500 depending on configuration. Mountain Productions installed the permanent stage structure that still stands today. White River Amphitheatre (2003, Muckleshoot Indian Reservation, Auburn, WA) opened as a 40-minute-from-Seattle competitor, but Seattle Times reporting confirmed the Gorge retained preferable "view" and "experience" rankings even at its 2h 20min drive time. The capacity evolution — 3,000 (1986) → 15,000 (1988) → 19,000 (1993) → 27,500 (current) — tells the arc in four numbers, and each transition carried a cultural shift that the module enumerates without editorial smoothing.

**The music is the proof.** Module M5 Musical Legacy and Cultural Impact is the largest research module and contains the release's densest concentration of primary-source data. Dave Matthews Band has played 79 shows through 2025, including the annual three-night Labor Day residency that has become a self-sustaining cultural institution. Three official live releases anchor the discography: *The Gorge* (2-CD/1-DVD, June 29, 2004), *Live Trax Vol. 44* (December 8, 2017), and *Take Me Back: Live From the Gorge* (February 6, 2026, recorded August 30, 2025). Pearl Jam's *Live at the Gorge 05/06* (Rhino/Warner, June 26, 2007) is a seven-disc box set that debuted at Billboard 200 #36 with 19,000 copies in its first week; the 1993 joint concert with Neil Young is the band's earliest Gorge appearance in the public record. Phish has played 22 shows since 1997. The festival ecosystem table documents 11 recurring events — Sasquatch!, Watershed, Bass Canyon, Beyond Wonderland, Paradiso, Lollapalooza, Lilith Fair, Ozzfest, H.O.R.D.E., Creation Festival, Vans Warped Tour, Pain in the Grass — each one a distinct cultural signature on the same basalt. Industry recognition is documented with sources: Pollstar "Best Outdoor Music Venue" nine times, 2013 Billboard Touring Award, ConcertBoom "Best Outdoor Concert Venues in America" ranking, 400,000 annual attendance (2013 reporting window). The 2023 Beyond Wonderland shooting (June 17, 2023) is documented factually per the release's safety rubric: two fatalities, two wounded, security guard struck by deflected bullet, suspect James M. Kelly (26, U.S. Army, Strongsville OH) shot by police. The *Enormous: The Gorge Story* documentary (2021, directed by Nic Davis) draws on Dave Matthews, Mike McCready, Dierks Bentley, Pat Monahan, John Oates, Steve Miller, and superfan Pat Coates as primary voices, and the release treats it as the canonical documentary source for the founding-era narrative.

## Part A: The Gorge Amphitheatre — Research Coverage

- **Seattle Origins:** George, Washington, Grant County, Columbia River benchland — the venue sits on the Columbia Plateau east of the Cascade Range, 150 road-miles from Seattle, 40-minute drive from Ellensburg, 2h 20min drive that Seattle Times reporting still ranks as preferable to the 40-minute White River Amphitheatre competitor for "view" and "experience."
- **Geological Substrate:** 17-6 Ma Columbia River Flood Basalts (41,000 cubic miles of lava, ~21 vent systems) deposited the basalt bowl; 3 Ma Cascade uplift tilted the plateau and cut the canyon; 19,000-13,000 BP Missoula Floods (40+ events, 10 km³/hour discharge, 36 m/s speeds) finished the excavation. All three timescales cited in Module M1's six-event geological timeline table.
- **Acoustic Properties:** Three-sided basalt wall reflection, downslope sound focus, unobstructed sky, 1,000-foot elevation differential — the venue's acoustic signature is documented in M1 and M3 as an emergent property of the landscape rather than a designed feature, corroborated by Bryan's 1980 acoustic-discovery hike and the *Enormous* documentary.
- **Indigenous History:** Wanapum Nation (Sahaptin *Wanapam*, "river people"), village P'na 2 miles downstream of Priest Rapids Dam, 300+ petroglyphs on basalt cliffs, Lewis & Clark contact October 1805 with Chief Cutssahnem, David Thompson July 8-9 1811, Smohalla and Washani religion c. 1815-1895, 2015 Heritage Center 50,000 sq ft.
- **Founding Era:** Dr. Vincent Bryan II and Carol Bryan purchased several hundred acres in 1980, established Champs de Brionne winery, discovered the acoustic bowl on a hike, ran 1986-1993 as Champs de Brionne Music Theatre, sold to MCA Concerts for $3.9M in 1993, retained surrounding vineyards, opened Cave B Estate Winery 2000 with Brian Carter.
- **Corporate Chain:** MCA Concerts (1993, $3.9M, $2M infrastructure investment) → House of Blues (1999, 300,000 annual attendance by 2001, $700,000+ county ticket fees) → Live Nation (2006, capacity expanded to 20,000-27,500, Mountain Productions permanent stage installation).
- **Musical Legacy:** Dave Matthews Band 79 shows (Labor Day residency, three live albums: *The Gorge* 2004, *Live Trax Vol. 44* 2017, *Take Me Back* 2026); Pearl Jam *Live at the Gorge 05/06* seven-disc box set, Billboard 200 #36; Phish 22 shows since 1997; 11-festival ecosystem (Sasquatch!, Watershed, Bass Canyon, Beyond Wonderland, Paradiso, Lollapalooza, Lilith Fair, Ozzfest, H.O.R.D.E., Creation Festival, Vans Warped Tour, Pain in the Grass).
- **Cultural Context:** 9x Pollstar "Best Outdoor Music Venue," 2013 Billboard Touring Award, 400,000 annual attendance, *Enormous: The Gorge Story* 2021 documentary (directed by Nic Davis, Dave Matthews, Mike McCready, Dierks Bentley, Pat Monahan, John Oates, Steve Miller, superfan Pat Coates), 2023 Beyond Wonderland shooting documented factually.
- **Safety Boundaries:** Wanapum-specific attribution (never "Indigenous peoples"), cultural-site GPS gating, 2023 shooting factual-only framing, petroglyph-coordinate gating, source quality rule (USGS / HistoryLink / Seattle Times / peer-reviewed only).

## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **The mission-pack PDF carried primary-source research rather than Wikipedia-derivative narrative.** USGS geological data, HistoryLink archival records, Seattle Times financial reporting (2002-08-04 MCA-acquisition article in particular), Grant County PUD cooperative records, and the *Enormous: The Gorge Story* 2021 documentary together provided a primary-source foundation that made the research modules genuinely authoritative. The project's citation discipline — every claim traced to a source document, every source document's publication date recorded — is the first evidence that the v1.49.132 AAR citation-discipline recommendation transfers to post-audit research.
- **The five-module architecture maps cleanly to the five natural layers of the venue's history.** Each module covers a distinct temporal and thematic domain (geological, Indigenous, founding, corporate, musical) with clear boundaries, minimal overlap, and explicit cross-module references. The architecture is not imposed on the material; it is recovered from the material. The module-count decision was made from the historical evidence, not from a template.
- **The geological-to-cultural through-line is structural insight, not metaphor.** "The amphitheatre was not built — it was discovered" is a literal description of the 17-million-year geological record, not a rhetorical flourish. The Amiga Principle at planetary timescale is the release's load-bearing thesis, and Module M1 provides the geological timeline table that makes the claim falsifiable rather than ornamental.
- **Wanapum-specific attribution held throughout.** Every claim about the Wanapum Nation is nation-attributed; the generic phrase "Indigenous peoples" does not appear. The release's most important ethical commitment — that specificity distinguishes research from erasure — is enforced module-by-module, not just in the safety rubric.
- **Single-commit mission-pack discipline shipped successfully.** The release landed as one commit (`89444e64e`) touching four files, mirroring the v1.49.82-131 batch pattern that v1.49.132 AAR audited and codified. This is the first evidence that the catalog-boundary architecture handles LaTeX-compiled mission-pack research releases without breaking the single-commit shape.

### What Could Be Better

- **The festival ecosystem documentation could be deeper.** Founding years, genre focus, and attendance figures for each of the 11 festivals would strengthen the cultural analysis. The current table documents existence and the fact of recurrence but does not quantify per-festival scale or trajectory. A follow-on enrichment pass is logged in lesson #777 and scheduled for the v1.49.195 release family.
- **The Mountain Productions stage installation deserves more technical detail.** The engineering of a permanent stage structure overlooking a 1,000-foot canyon is architecturally significant, and the module's current treatment (one paragraph) under-serves the topic. Structural engineering, wind-load calculations, and the specific construction timeline are absent.
- **The acoustic properties section could be strengthened with formal measurement data.** Reverb time, frequency response curves, and decibel fall-off measurements across the natural bowl would convert the current qualitative description into a quantifiable acoustic profile. If primary-source measurement data exists (industry sound-engineering reports, venue operations data), a follow-up pass should recover it.
- **The Dave Matthews Band section under-documents the Labor Day residency's economic impact on Grant County.** The specific cultural phenomenon of the three-night Labor Day weekend tradition — hotel bookings, campground revenue, per-show county sales-tax intake, the annual downstream effect on Ellensburg and Wenatchee businesses — is narratively referenced but not quantified, and the economic data would round out M5's cultural-impact argument.
- **The LaTeX source was not separated from the PDF for scoring.** `gorge_amphitheatre_mission.pdf` (193KB binary) and `gorge_amphitheatre_mission.tex` (1,025 lines) are both tracked, but the release notes did not originally catalog the relationship between them or explain why both are checked in. The uplift pass addresses this in the Files section; the original shipping shape left it implicit.

The corpus-shape problem — rich research on disk, thin release notes in the repository — is the opposite of the v1.49.132 AAR problem, and the uplift pass documented in this file closes the specific gap for v1.49.133.

## Lessons Learned

- **Geology is architecture at planetary timescale.** The Gorge's acoustic properties are not an accident and not a design — they are the emergent result of three geological processes (basalt deposition, Cascade uplift, catastrophic flooding) operating across three different timescales (tens of millions of years, millions of years, tens of thousands of years). The Amiga Principle: constraint produces the conditions for resonance. The basalt did not intend to focus sound. It does. This is the release's clearest single contribution to the Amiga Principle canon that v1.49.132 AAR Module M3 codified. (Lesson #773.)
- **Indigenous history is not context — it is the primary story.** The Wanapum carved 300+ petroglyphs into the same basalt cliffs that form the amphitheatre's natural walls. They were present when Missoula's last floods carved the terrain. They greeted Lewis and Clark. They survived dam construction and displacement. Their Heritage Center stands 20 miles from the venue. Omitting this history from venue documentation is not a gap — it is an erasure. This module corrects it. (Lesson #774.)
- **Acoustic serendipity is the signature of the Amiga Principle.** Dr. Bryan did not set out to build a concert venue. He was hiking his vineyard property and noticed he could hear every word spoken a thousand feet below. The entire 40-year arc of the Gorge Amphitheatre — from 3,000-person plywood stage to 27,500-capacity Live Nation crown jewel — originates from one person listening to what the landscape was already doing. The best architectures are discovered, not designed. (Lesson #775.)
- **Corporate ownership chains carry cultural consequences.** Bryan (family) → MCA ($3.9M, corporate) → House of Blues (entertainment brand) → Live Nation (global monopoly). Each transition changed not just the business model but the cultural character of the venue. The progression from winery concert to Pollstar-winning amphitheatre is also a progression from local to global, from personal to corporate, from serendipity to strategy. Both stories are true simultaneously. (Lesson #776.)
- **Primary-source density is the difference between research and summary.** USGS, HistoryLink, Seattle Times (2002-08-04 MCA article specifically), Grant County PUD, and the *Enormous* documentary together supplied the citations that let every numerical claim in the mission pack trace back to a specific document. Wikipedia and Grokipedia were used as indices into primary sources rather than as primary sources themselves — a discipline that v1.49.132 AAR Module M9's citation-discipline pass explicitly recommends and v1.49.133 operationalises.
- **The five-module architecture emerged from the material.** The decision to split the history into Geological / Indigenous / Founding / Corporate / Musical Legacy was not a template choice; it was recovered from the historical evidence. The alternative — a single chronological narrative — would have collapsed the Wanapum Deep History into a brief pre-contact introduction and lost the scale of the Indigenous presence on these cliffs. The module architecture is itself a research contribution.
- **Single-commit discipline scales to mission-pack releases.** The release shipped as one `feat(www)` commit touching four files (one line in `series.js`, one LaTeX source, one compiled PDF, one index.html), mirroring the v1.49.82-131 batch pattern that v1.49.132 AAR codified. This validates that the catalog architecture handles LaTeX-compiled mission-pack research without breaking the single-commit shape that the earlier 40-release batch established.
- **Safety rubrics belong in the release notes, not in private context.** The five-row safety-and-sensitivity table (Wanapum specificity ABSOLUTE, cultural-site GPS GATE, 2023 shooting ANNOTATE, petroglyph coordinates GATE, source quality ABSOLUTE) is a load-bearing artifact that future degree releases touching Indigenous cultural geography should treat as a reusable template. Publishing the boundaries makes them inspectable; hiding them makes them forgettable.
- **Through-lines are the deliverable, not the section headers.** The "The Gorge does not amplify music — it reveals it. The geology was always the architecture. The music was always the proof" closing line compresses the five-module architecture into three sentences. A through-line that holds across five modules across 17 million years across four industrial transitions is the actual research output. The modules are the evidence; the through-line is the finding.
- **Post-audit releases should cite the audit explicitly.** v1.49.133 is the first post-v1.49.132-AAR research release, and the release notes now explicitly reference the AAR methodology (single-commit discipline, primary-source-first citation rule, catalog-boundary architecture, cross-reference-graph participation). The audit is reusable only if the successor releases invoke its findings by name, and the naming discipline is itself evidence that the audit's feedback loop closed.
