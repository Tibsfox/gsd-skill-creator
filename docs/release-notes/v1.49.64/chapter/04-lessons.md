1. **Meaning concentrates at categorical boundaries.**
   The cusp controversy is really a debate about whether classification boundaries are real or arbitrary. In information theory, boundaries carry maximum information — they're where the system must decide. The liminal thesis applies far beyond astrology: in ecology (ecotones are more biodiverse than either adjacent biome), in music (genre boundaries produce the most creative work), and in the Research series itself (the most interesting projects cross Rosetta Stone cluster boundaries).

2. **Astronomical systems are coordinate systems, not belief systems.**
   The precession of the equinoxes is a measured physical phenomenon. The tropical and sidereal zodiacs are two valid coordinate systems for the same sky. The dispute about which one is "correct" is a dispute about purpose, not physics. This distinction — between measurement and meaning — is the thread that connects ATC to the broader Research series' commitment to precision.
