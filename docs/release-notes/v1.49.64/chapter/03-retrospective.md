# Retrospective — v1.49.64

## What Worked

1. **The FDR-ATC pair creates a cosmological diptych.** Two studies of zodiacal systems — one cyclical (Chinese sexagenary), one positional (Western ecliptic) — that illuminate each other through contrast. The structural connection (both are information architectures assigning meaning to temporal positions) is the through-line. Future cosmological studies should be designed in pairs.

2. **The cusp controversy module treats the debate seriously.** Module 05 doesn't advocate for or against cusp astrology — it maps three named positions, explains the astronomical mechanisms that each position relies on, and presents Goldschneider's empirical approach as a third path. This is the same methodology used throughout the Research series: document the territory, cite the sources, let the reader decide.

3. **Astronomical precision grounds the cultural content.** Starting with precession mathematics (1°/72 years, 25,772-year Great Year) and solar disc geometry (0.53° angular diameter) before discussing mythology and personality gives the study a quantitative foundation. The astronomical facts are not in dispute; the interpretive frameworks built on them are.

## What Could Be Better

1. **Indian (Jyotish), Arabic, and Mesoamerican zodiac traditions are absent.** ATC focuses on Western and briefly touches Mesopotamian origins. The sidereal zodiac systems used in Vedic astrology, the Arabic mansions of the moon, and the Mesoamerican calendar systems all define zodiacal boundaries differently.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Meaning concentrates at categorical boundaries.** The cusp controversy is really a debate about whether classification boundaries are real or arbitrary. In information theory, boundaries carry maximum information — they're where the system must decide. The liminal thesis applies far beyond astrology: in ecology (ecotones are more biodiverse than either adjacent biome), in music (genre boundaries produce the most creative work), and in the Research series itself (the most interesting projects cross Rosetta Stone cluster boundaries).

2. **Astronomical systems are coordinate systems, not belief systems.** The precession of the equinoxes is a measured physical phenomenon. The tropical and sidereal zodiacs are two valid coordinate systems for the same sky. The dispute about which one is "correct" is a dispute about purpose, not physics. This distinction — between measurement and meaning — is the thread that connects ATC to the broader Research series' commitment to precision.
