# Retrospective — v1.49.163

## What Worked

**Part A:**
1. **Northwest Music Archives is the authoritative source.** Peter Blecha's 1984 article in *The Rocket* and the comprehensive online archive provide the definitive account -- full discography with catalog numbers, personnel lists with dates, venue documentation. No other source matches this depth.
2. **PNW Bands provides complementary member roster data** including founding members not covered in the NW Music Archives article (Wally Griffin, Tracy Townsend, Bob Johnston, Jerry Strong).
3. **HistoryLink.org provides the infrastructure context** -- Seafair Records, Pat O'Day, Spanish Castle, Dave Lewis, Kearney Barton, Birdland, Jackson Street, EMP Grand Opening. The institutional history is exceptionally well documented.
4. **The Larry Coryell connection is the richest research thread.** Multiple sources (Wikipedia, All About Jazz, Guitar World, Vintage Guitar, the Louie Report) document his career from The Dynamics through jazz-rock fusion. His Seattle period is well-attested.
5. **The proto-fusion laboratory finding is the strongest analytical contribution.** Documenting the dispersal pattern (four members into four genres) reveals The Dynamics' true structural significance.
6. **The Central District genre bridge analysis is honest about complexity.** Documents the parallel circuits, the racial dynamics, the structural inequities of distribution, and the ethical nuance without reducing to simple categories.

**Part B:**
1. **The within-family contrast is clean and informative.** Every axis of variation between the martin and the Violet-green is well-defined and measurable. The engine's clearest comparative pair.
2. **The stream/rain metaphor for two liquid textures.** Continuous vs. discrete versions of the same timbral quality. Precise, audible, structurally grounded.
3. **The Tachycineta pair is well-positioned.** Same-genus species in consecutive degrees within a family cluster creates nested taxonomic hierarchy the engine has not attempted before.
4. **The name analysis is strong.** "The sea-colored swift-one" is one of the most evocative scientific names in ornithology. Name, taxonomy, and phenotype align.
5. **Size as master variable is a new analytical lens.** One morphological parameter drives five ecological outcomes. Powerful tool for future within-family comparisons.
6. **The western specialty paradox is honest.** Recording that a more common bird is less known because it is self-sufficient and quiet is accurate observation.

## Lessons Learned

**On temporal regression:** The engine is not a timeline. It is a map. Maps do not progress chronologically -- they fill in territory. The first backward temporal step at degree 27 reveals that the engine's degree sequence is organized by genre/energy/neighborhood, not by era. As the soul/R&B zone develops, it naturally reaches back to the historical R&B that preceded soul as a distinct genre. The regression is a structural feature, not a bug. Expect the engine to continue oscillating in time as it fills in the complete picture.

**On significance through dispersal:** The Dynamics challenge the assumption that a band's significance equals its own output. Sometimes a band is a laboratory -- its significance is what the members become after they leave. The Yardbirds analogy holds: Clapton to Cream, Page to Led Zeppelin, Beck to Jeff Beck Group. Coryell to fusion, Doubleday to Electric Flag, Woods to Hendrix. The band itself is the shell. The dispersed musicians are the seeds. Track dispersal patterns at subsequent ensemble degrees.

**On within-family contrast:** Placing the Purple Martin and Violet-green Swallow in consecutive degrees creates the engine's clearest before/after comparison on the axis of body size. Same family, same lifestyle, opposite everything else. One morphological variable (mass) drives five ecological outcomes (pitch, prey, cavity access, flight agility, conservation status). The engine has never had such clean demonstration that size is the master variable. Use this lens for future within-family comparisons.

**On the genre bridge:** The Central District genre bridge is the mechanism by which Black R&B crossed the racial divide in 1960s Seattle. The same songs played in both worlds -- the Black adult nightclubs and the white teen-dance venues -- but through different performers for different audiences with different commercial outcomes. The Dynamics were the conduit. This is not simple appropriation and it is not simple tribute -- it is the complex structural reality of American popular music in a racially segregated society. The research documents the mechanism without reducing it to simple categories. Honest complexity is better than tidy narratives.

**On two liquid textures:** The engine's vocabulary refines. "Liquid" alone is insufficient to describe a sound. The martin's liquid is legato (stream). The Violet-green's liquid is staccato (rain). The timbral adjective needs a temporal verb. This principle extends: every timbral quality can be expressed in multiple temporal structures, and the temporal structure changes the perceptual meaning. The engine gains a vocabulary distinction it needed.

**On the unrecognized majority:** The Violet-green Swallow outnumbers the Purple Martin 20:1 in the PNW but is far less known. Self-sufficiency is less newsworthy than dependency. The quiet, common, independent thing lives in the shadow of the loud, rare, managed one. This applies to music history too: The Dynamics were foundational -- they gave Coryell and Doubleday and Woods their stage -- but the bands those musicians joined afterward (fusion groups, Electric Flag, Hendrix sessions) are the ones that received the recognition. The laboratory is forgotten; the results are famous. The engine documents what was there first.
