The dispersed musicians are the seeds. Track dispersal patterns at subsequent ensemble degrees.

4. **On the genre bridge:**
   The Central District genre bridge is the mechanism by which Black R&B crossed the racial divide in 1960s Seattle. The same songs played in both worlds -- the Black adult nightclubs and the white teen-dance venues -- but through different performers for different audiences with different commercial outcomes. The Dynamics were the conduit. This is not simple appropriation and it is not simple tribute -- it is the complex structural reality of American popular music in a racially segregated society. The research documents the mechanism without reducing it to simple categories. Honest complexity is better than tidy narratives.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #1083_

6. **On the unrecognized majority:**
   The Violet-green Swallow outnumbers the Purple Martin 20:1 in the PNW but is far less known. Self-sufficiency is less newsworthy than dependency. The laboratory is forgotten; the results are famous. The engine documents what was there first.
