## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **Twelve-project simultaneous content landing stayed atomic across two mega-waves.** Each of BNY, LGW, SET, SGM, BHK, HEN, ANM, INP, OSC, PKD, EAZ, and NWZ landed as a single atomic content commit, and the release-notes stub commit was the thirteenth. Bisect across v1.49.87..v1.49.88 finds exactly thirteen clean state transitions rather than interleaved merges. The three-commit atomic-content discipline the Research series has used since WYR (v1.49.43) scales cleanly from single-project through five-project (MW4, v1.49.85) to twelve-project (this release) without modification — the pattern is robust under throughput pressure at 2.4× the prior mega-wave size.
- **Four-cluster establishment in one release is a natural consolidation.** MW10 + MW11 closes four distinct thematic threads (Science & Observation, Literature-author-centric, Animation, Zodiac) in one wave because the twelve projects actually belong to four coherent clusters rather than being an undifferentiated mega-bucket. Ending the wave at twelve with clean cluster boundaries is better than padding to fifteen or stopping short at nine; readers can now see four coherent clusters where before there was scattered material.
- **BHK's eight-module synthesis structure is the series' deepest single-project treatment.** The "spaces between" synthesis module (Module 08: Planetary Boundaries) ties black-hole cosmology back to earth-system science through scale translation — the module is the project's editorial argument, not an afterthought. The eight-module structure becomes the blueprint for future largest-scope projects where a single domain is not enough and synthesis across scales is the point.
- **SGM's complex-plane module is the moment the series turns and sees itself.** Module 02 (Complex Plane Architecture) explicitly cross-references the gsd-skill-creator skill-position architecture (v1.37 Complex Plane Learning Framework) that uses the same complex-plane substrate. The resonance between the Research subject and the engine architecture is now explicit, and the module makes the series' self-awareness a first-class editorial element.
- **LGW Module 01 (LIGO Hanford Interferometry) lands the PNW-specific layer at the highest-technology tier the series has attempted.** The 4-kilometer-arm interferometer at the Hanford Site is a PNW-infrastructure anchor that no other project in the Research series has matched for technological complexity; it also connects MW10 (cosmology) to the Tri-Cities industrial-operations community and the Pacific Northwest National Laboratory collaboration.
- **NWZ's OCAP/CARE cultural-care protocol is the series' cultural-sensitivity precedent.** Every nation is named specifically (K'iche' Maya, Apsaalooke, Dogon, Zulu) rather than folded into a generic category; the module-level discipline of naming the living communities whose knowledge is being documented is now the Research-series baseline for any project that touches indigenous, non-Western, or minoritized material. Future projects will inherit this precedent.
- **PKD is the most cross-referenced literature project in the series, and the density is earned rather than forced.** Reality-questioning connects directly to COI (dark electronic, v1.49.58), DPM (electronic emotion), STE (digital forensics, v1.49.85), ACE (audio compute engines), and MRW (simulated worlds, v1.49.85). Dick's themes have become infrastructure for the Research-series editorial voice, and the Contemporary Relevance module documents the connections explicitly.
- **OSC's controversy-documentation module is a template.** Module 05 (Faith, Controversy, Legacy) separates the literary work from the author's public record without either canceling or endorsing, documenting the positions and the responses as historical record. The module is the series' precedent for how to document controversy in any future project where the subject's public record is contested.

### What Could Be Better

- **Twelve projects is at the edge of reader-absorbability.** A reader cannot absorb twelve projects of this depth in a single sitting, and no reading-plan meta-document sequences the twelve for a new reader. A "How to Read v1.49.88" document would help readers enter the material; the same concern applies for each of the four clusters touched in the release (Science & Observation, Literature, Animation, Zodiac) now that each has multiple entries.
- **The PNW-specific layers have different depths across the twelve projects.** BNY Module 01 (Boeing stress analyst) and LGW Module 01 (Hanford) are full PNW-anchor modules; HEN Module 04 (Fraggle Rock Vancouver production) is embedded; ANM, INP, OSC, PKD, EAZ, and NWZ do not carry dedicated PNW-specific modules because the subjects are global. A future revision could add a standardized "PNW reception" or "PNW-community context" layer to the five projects where the subject-matter is non-PNW, documenting how the PNW fandom or scholarly community received the subject.
- **The mission-pack PDF rendering remains inconsistent.** None of the twelve MW10+MW11 projects pre-render PDFs (unlike MW4's GPG project, which shipped `gpu-gsd-mission.pdf` at 224,058 bytes). The decision to defer all PDFs to the build pipeline is cleaner than MW4's mixed discipline, but the series still needs an explicit "defer-all" policy document so the decision is made once rather than per-project.
- **Cross-cluster cross-references are thinner than they should be.** PKD cites STE, MRW, ACE, COI, and DPM — that density is right. But BHK-to-LGW-to-SET should form a dense science-cluster cross-reference matrix (multi-messenger-astronomy ties them together), and currently the cross-references are one-way rather than bidirectional. A cross-cluster cross-reference enrichment pass is queued for a follow-on revision before the Science Cluster gets a landing page.
- **The verification-matrix discipline was implicit on twelve simultaneous projects.** Each project ships with its own implicit 28-test matrix (4 safety + 12 core + 8 integration + 4 edge) but the mega-wave did not declare a cross-project consistency test layer; a 12 × 28 = 336 per-project-test aggregate plus a proposed 12-cross-project-consistency-test layer (one per project pair in the Science Cluster and one per project pair in the Animation triad) would have been explicit, and the lack of that declaration at wave-land time was an oversight. The verification-matrix template should be revised to declare mega-wave cross-project tests up-front.

### What Needs Improvement

- **The twelve-project commit choreography is underdocumented.** The commits land in four temporal bursts (19:12:17, 19:41:16, 19:42:01, and 20:10:02 windows), each burst landing 3-4 projects within seconds — a pattern that suggests the projects were pre-built and landed in serial groups from separate working trees. The specific workflow (parallel tree preparation, serial landing) needs to be written down in a mega-wave playbook document in `.planning/missions/release-uplift/` before the next twelve-plus wave attempt.
- **The `series.js` navigation file gains nine lines cumulative across twelve commits rather than twelve lines — some entries overwrite prior drafts.** The mismatch (12 project commits, 9 `series.js` line additions) indicates that three commits amended prior-wave stub entries rather than appending new ones. The ordering semantics of `series.js` remains implicit rather than explicit, and a comment block at the top of the file clarifying the append-order rule would prevent confusion on future mega-waves.
- **The NWZ project's cultural-care review trail is not captured in-repo.** The OCAP/CARE protocol was applied editorially — every nation named specifically, K'iche' spelling verified, Apsaalooke/Crow dual-naming convention observed — but the specific review trail (who reviewed, what changes resulted, what citations were added) is not documented in the project tree. ## Lessons Learned

- **Twelve-project mega-waves hold atomicity when every project is structurally complete at wave-land time.** Each of the twelve projects in MW10 + MW11 was developed to single-project completion before the wave landed, so the wave itself was pure assembly — twelve atomic drops plus one release-notes stub. The lesson is that mega-wave size scales with project independence; the pattern holds at 12× what single-project cadence would produce, provided that the projects share no unresolved dependencies at wave-land time. Future mega-waves should verify project-level completeness before attempting simultaneous landing, and the completeness criterion should include site-shell, research-modules, mission-pack LaTeX source, and per-project `series.js` entry.
- **Closing or extending four clusters in one release is worth the coordination cost.** The release establishes the Science & Observation Cluster, extends the LNV cluster with author-centric entries, adds three animation projects to the Creative cluster, and establishes the Zodiac Cluster — four thematic landings rather than one. The coordination cost (staging twelve projects in parallel trees) is paid back by the clarity that each cluster gains at wave-land: readers now see coherent cluster boundaries rather than scattered entries. Future releases should consider multi-cluster packaging when project batches naturally sort into multiple thematic clusters.
- **Eight-module synthesis projects need an explicit "spaces between" closer module.** BHK's Module 08 (Planetary Boundaries) is the synthesis module that ties black-hole cosmology back to earth-system science through scale translation — the module is the project's editorial argument, not an afterthought. Future projects of comparable scope should adopt the "spaces between" synthesis-closer convention explicitly rather than leaving synthesis to the reader.
- **When a Research project's subject resonates with the engine architecture, the cross-reference should be explicit and module-level.** SGM Module 02 (Complex Plane Architecture) references the gsd-skill-creator skill-position architecture (v1.37) because the two subjects share the same mathematical substrate. Future projects where Research-series content touches engine-architecture concerns should make the resonance a module-level editorial decision rather than a footnote; the explicit cross-reference is the series' self-awareness in action.
- **PNW-specific layers scale down gracefully when the subject is global but not to zero.** LGW's Module 01 (LIGO Hanford) is a full PNW-anchor module because LIGO Hanford is PNW infrastructure; HEN's Module 04 (Fraggle Rock Vancouver/BC production) is embedded because the Henson Company is not PNW-native; OSC and PKD have no PNW-specific modules because the authors were Utah-based (OSC) and Bay Area / Orange County (PKD). The lesson is that PNW layers scale with actual PNW-presence, and forcing a layer where none exists is worse than documenting "this subject has no significant PNW reception history" explicitly. Future projects should document the PNW-presence-or-absence decision rather than silently omitting the layer.
- **Cultural-care protocols are module-level disciplines, not release-level disclaimers.** NWZ's OCAP/CARE implementation names every nation specifically (K'iche' Maya, Apsaalooke, Dogon, Zulu) at the module level, not at the front of the project as a catch-all disclaimer. The specificity is the protocol; the disclaimer at the front would be a signal without substance. Future projects touching indigenous, non-Western, or minoritized material should adopt module-level naming-specificity as the cultural-care standard.
- **Controversy-documentation is a template, not a case-by-case judgment.** OSC's Module 05 separates the literary work from the author's public record without either canceling or endorsing — documenting positions, responses, and consequences as historical record. The module structure (biography → work → public-stance → community-response → legacy) generalizes to any future project where the subject's public record is contested. The template preserves research integrity while respecting that readers form their own judgments.
- **The mission-pack triad (LaTeX + index-HTML + deferred-PDF) is now the series default.** Twelve consecutive projects ship without pre-rendered PDFs; GPG (v1.49.85) was the only project in the Research series' history that pre-rendered, and the decision was not repeated. The series has effectively defaulted to defer-all-PDFs; the decision should be made explicit as series policy so future projects do not re-litigate the choice.
- **The multi-domain docroot scales to 128 projects without revision.** The v1.49.38 decision to reserve `www/tibsfox/com/` as a multi-domain docroot with `Research/<CODE>/` per-project directories has now survived 128 project additions, 0 naming collisions, multiple mega-wave simultaneous landings (MW4 five-project, MW10+MW11 twelve-project), and the gradual growth from a single-project cadence to a twelve-project-wave cadence. The decision is now load-bearing for the entire series and is unlikely to be revised before the forthcoming tibsfox.com publishing discipline lands.
- **Cluster establishment follows coverage, not count.** The Science & Observation Cluster closes at five projects (BNY, BHK, LGW, SET, SGM) not four or six because five is where popular-science communication (BNY), cosmology (BHK), gravitational-wave detection (LGW), SETI (SET), and the geometric pattern-substrate (SGM) form a coherent scale walk from public-pedagogy through observational-astrophysics to mathematical foundations. Future clusters should be sized by when coverage is complete rather than by round numbers; the Zodiac Cluster at four (ATC, FDR, EAZ, NWZ) and the Animation sub-cluster at three (HEN, ANM, INP) are the same lesson applied at different sizes.
- **Reality-questioning (PKD) is infrastructure, not genre-specific.** The cross-reference density from PKD to other Research projects (STE digital forensics, MRW simulated weather, COI dark electronic, DPM electronic emotion, ACE audio compute) shows that Dick's themes have become editorial infrastructure for the series rather than confined to the LNV cluster. The lesson is that some subjects behave like infrastructure — they are cross-domain conceptual substrate that every adjacent project references — and should be prioritized for early-in-series landing when detected. Reality-questioning as infrastructure explains why PKD took five modules to cover adequately, and why future infrastructure-candidate projects (e.g., a hypothetical consciousness-or-cognition anchor) should be given equal scope.
