## Horse Feathers -- Kill Rock Stars Enters

Horse Feathers is a Pacific Northwest indie experimental project -- quiet, textured, built on acoustic instruments (guitar, violin, banjo, cello) arranged with a folk musician's ear and an experimentalist's sense of space. The sound is intimate, spare, and precise. Whispered vocals, fingerpicked guitar, bowed strings that drift between melody and texture.

The label: **Kill Rock Stars**. Founded in Olympia, Washington in 1991 by Slim Moon. The label's roster reads like a history of Pacific Northwest independent music:

- **Bikini Kill** -- riot grrrl. Kathleen Hanna. The band that started a revolution.
- **Sleater-Kinney** -- Carrie Brownstein, Corin Tucker, Janet Weiss. One of the most critically acclaimed rock bands of the 1990s and 2000s.
- **Elliott Smith** -- before the DreamWorks records, Smith was on Kill Rock Stars.
- **Deerhoof** -- experimental rock. Decades of genre-defying work.
- **The Decemberists** -- early records, before Capitol.

Kill Rock Stars entering the engine is structurally significant. The label represents a tradition -- Olympia punk, riot grrrl, DIY ethics, feminist politics, experimental sound. Horse Feathers inherits this tradition in a quieter register -- experimental, independent, label-supported but small-scale.

E=4. After the E=6 peak of Wellwater Conspiracy (123), the energy drops by two levels. Horse Feathers is not quiet in the way Hollis Resnik (119, E=2) was quiet -- there is rhythmic presence, there are dynamics. But the instruments are acoustic, the vocals are soft, the arrangements are spare. Measured energy. The engine descends from the peak.

## Common Goldeneye -- The First Mechanical Sound

The Common Goldeneye (*Bucephala clangula*) is a medium-sized diving duck found across North America. More widespread than Barrow's Goldeneye (123) -- found on lakes, rivers, and coastal waters at lower elevations and across a broader range.

The species name *clangula* means "to resound" -- and the reason is the **rattling wing-whistle**. This is the engine's first **mechanical sound**: a distinctive, loud, whistling rattle produced by air passing through the modified inner primary feathers during flight. The sound is not a vocalization. It is not produced by the syrinx, the bill, or any deliberate behavior. It is a structural consequence of the wing's shape -- the bird cannot NOT make this sound when it flies.

This is a category breakthrough for the species axis. Every previous sound entry has been biological in origin:
- **Vocalizations** -- calls, songs, croaks, whistles, peeping (produced by the syrinx)
- **Percussive behavior** -- drumming (produced by deliberate bill strikes on resonant surfaces)
- **Behavioral sounds** -- splash + song (produced by deliberate action)

The wing-whistle is none of these. It is **aeroelastic** -- produced by the interaction between airflow and feather structure. The bird IS the instrument. The wing IS the resonator. Flight IS the performance.

E=4. The wing-whistle is audible at distance -- you hear a goldeneye coming before you see it. The rattling quality is distinctive, identifiable in flight even among mixed flocks. More energy than Barrow's Goldeneye's quiet grunts (E=3), which makes ecological sense -- the mechanical sound carries farther and is involuntary, while the grunts are voluntary and context-dependent.
