# Lessons — v1.49.264

17 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **Multiple structurally weighted events can converge at a single degree when the catalog order aligns.**
   Degree 124 simultaneously introduced the first mechanical sound, completed the *Bucephala* genus, extended the Anatidae streak to seven, inaugurated Kill Rock Stars as the engine's first riot-grrrl-lineage label, and resolved the E=6 Wellwater Conspiracy peak through a two-level descent. Five structurally weighted events at one degree is a dense convergence, and the convergence was not forced — it was produced by catalog order and engine design. Future degrees should recognize that multi-event convergences are possible and should not be artificially distributed across multiple degrees when the catalog order supports single-degree convergence.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.290`) · lesson #6655_

2. **Category-opening events carry engine-wide vocabulary impact beyond their individual-entry documentation.**
   The wing-whistle category opens with one entry (Common Goldeneye) but expands the engine's sound-production vocabulary from three categories to four. The vocabulary expansion will affect every future species-axis entry — all future entries will be positioned within the now-four-category taxonomy, and the fourth category exists because of degree 124. Category-opening events deserve recognition as engine-wide design changes, not merely as individual-entry additions.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #6656_

3. **Three-consecutive-degree genus completions are a valid documentation pattern when catalog order supports it.**
   The *Bucephala* genus completed in three consecutive degrees (122, 123, 124), the first three-consecutive-degree genus completion in the engine. The pattern demonstrates that tight taxonomic clusters are structurally valuable — readers walking the degrees in order encounter the three species together and can synthesize the genus-level patterns at read-time rather than cross-referencing widely scattered individual entries. Future genus completions should consider the three-consecutive-degree pattern as a deliberate option rather than defaulting to scattered documentation.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #6657_

4. **Synchronized energy convergences at structurally weighted degrees are a recurring engine pattern.**
   Both degree 120 and degree 124 produced synchronized E=4 convergences from opposite trajectories. Two instances are sufficient to recognize a pattern, and future structurally weighted degrees should consider energy-convergence design explicitly — when the catalog order supports a convergence, the engine should signal and document it rather than treating it as an accidental alignment.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.265`) · lesson #6658_

5. **Label-entry-as-lineage-proxy inaugurates a lineage before any individual artist in the lineage reaches their own degree.**
   Kill Rock Stars at degree 124 inaugurates the riot grrrl lineage in the engine even though Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney, and other core riot grrrl artists have not yet received their own degrees. The label-entry-as-lineage-proxy is a documented design pattern: labels can carry lineage structural weight, and entering a label before individual artists in the lineage reach their degrees is a valid sequencing choice. Future label entries should consider the lineage-proxy function explicitly.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.274`) · lesson #6659_

6. **Structural-versus-agentive philosophical parallels across both axes can anchor a degree's conceptual coherence.**
   The philosophical parallel between the Common Goldeneye's aeroelastic wing-whistle (sound emerges from structure, not from agency) and Horse Feathers' chamber-folk instrumental arrangement (sonic identity emerges from instrument choice and spatial placement, not from foreground-vocal-delivery performance) anchored degree 124's conceptual coherence. Future degrees should consider whether philosophical parallels between the two halves exist and can be surfaced in the documentation — not every degree will have such parallels, but when they exist, they add conceptual depth beyond the numerical convergences.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.269`) · lesson #6660_

7. **Peak-and-descent patterns on one axis should be paired with ascent-or-hold patterns on the other for engine breathing.**
   Degree 124's synchronized convergence happened because the artist axis descended from E=6 while the species axis ascended from E=3 — opposing trajectories meeting at E=4. The engine's breathing (peak-and-recovery across degrees) is healthier when opposing trajectories are arranged rather than when both axes ascend or descend together. Future degrees should consider trajectory-pairing as a design parameter.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.290`) · lesson #6661_

8. **The mechanical sound category's first entry establishes the category boundary cleanly when the first entry is unambiguous.**
   The Common Goldeneye's wing-whistle is unambiguously mechanical — it is clearly not a vocalization, clearly not a percussion sound, clearly not a deliberate behavioral action. The unambiguous nature of the first entry gave the new fourth category a clean boundary from the start. Future category-opening entries should be chosen for their unambiguity relative to existing categories, because ambiguous first entries blur category boundaries and force subsequent entries to clarify what should have been settled at the opening.
