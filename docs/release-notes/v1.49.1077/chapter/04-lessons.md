3. **Spitzer is managed by NASA JPL with science operations at the Spitzer Science Center — NOT Goddard/STScI and NOT Marshall/CXC.**

4. **Spitzer launched on an expendable Delta II from Cape Canaveral on 25 August 2003 and drifted into an Earth-trailing heliocentric orbit.**
   It launched 25 August 2003 (05:35 UTC) on a Delta II (7920H "Heavy") from Cape Canaveral (SLC-17B) — NOT the Space Shuttle — and drifted slowly behind Earth around the Sun into a distinctive Earth-trailing heliocentric orbit to keep the telescope cold; the catalog states this precisely.

5. **The observatory achievement is NASA's flagship infrared observatory, not "the first infrared space telescope.**

7. **The first detection of an exoplanet's LIGHT is distinct from Hubble's exoplanet ATMOSPHERE; the Earth-trailing orbit is a characteristic, not a "first.**

The Earth-trailing heliocentric orbit is kept as a described characteristic (the "first space telescope in such an orbit" superlative did not survive fact-verify), so the weather-map first is anchored in its place.
