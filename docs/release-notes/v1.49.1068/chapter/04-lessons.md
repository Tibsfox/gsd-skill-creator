1. **A sustain KEEPS the predecessor's shared domain vocabulary — it does not remove it.**
   Unlike a rotation, this is a SUSTAIN (mode=continuation): Pioneer 11 and its sister Pioneer 10 share almost all of their domain vocabulary, so the rewrite KEPT the shared outer-planets content (the outer solar system, deep space, the giant planets, Jupiter, Saturn, the asteroid belt, RTG power, the Pioneer plaque, the escape trajectory, the gravity assist, the flyby, NASA Ames, TRW) and replaced only the predecessor-specific facts — Pioneer 10 as the subject, the 2 March 1972 launch, the first asteroid-belt traverse, the first Jupiter flyby of December 1973, the first escape trajectory, the first message-to-ETI plaque, the 23 January 2003 last signal, and the Marbled Godwit + American Dunegrass pairing — with Pioneer 11's: the 6 April 1973 launch, the closer Jupiter flyby of December 1974…

3. **The Saturn result is the first FLYBY of Saturn — first to reach Saturn, not first to orbit.**
   Pioneer 11 made the first flyby of Saturn (1 September 1979); the catalog scopes this as the first to fly by / reach Saturn and notes that Voyager 1 (1980) and Voyager 2 (1981) came later, that Cassini was the first orbiter (2004), and that the Voyagers launched first (1977) but Pioneer 11 reached Saturn first.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12416_

4. **The F ring is a DISCOVERY by Pioneer 11, scoped against Voyager's later imaging.**
   Pioneer 11 discovered the narrow outer F ring in 1979; the catalog scopes this as "discovered Saturn's F ring" and notes that Voyager 1 imaged and characterized it in detail in 1980, but Pioneer 11 found it first.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12417_

5. **The magnetic-field result is the first DETECTION of Saturn's field and magnetosphere.**
   Pioneer 11 crossed Saturn's bow shock on 31 August 1979 and made the first detection of Saturn's intrinsic magnetic field and magnetosphere; the catalog scopes this as the first detection of Saturn's magnetic field / magnetosphere.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12418_

6. **0 re-stamps — Pioneer 11 shares Pioneer 10's journey but not its firsts.**

7. **The Jupiter→Saturn gravity assist is described, NOT anchored.**
   Pioneer 11 used Jupiter's gravity to redirect to Saturn; the catalog describes this rather than anchoring it, because Mariner 10 used a Venus assist to reach Mercury earlier (February 1974). Pioneer 11's Jupiter flyby is likewise the second (Pioneer 10 was first), described not anchored.
