1. **A sustain keeps the predecessor's axis vocabulary and replaces only the predecessor-specific content.**
   MRO shares the Mars-exploration vocabulary with the Phoenix Mars Lander predecessor (Mars, the red planet, the surface, the subsurface, water ice, dust, in situ, planetary exploration, the poles, the atmosphere, minerals) — that vocabulary is shared and correct. The rewrite removed only the Phoenix-specific content (the Phoenix name, the Mars Scout Program, PI Peter H. Smith, the Robotic Arm / TEGA / MECA / SSI / MARDI / MET instruments, the arctic landing / Vastitas Borealis / Green Valley, the perchlorate / touch-water-ice / snow-from-clouds science, the Mars Surveyor 2001 Lander / Mars Polar Lander heritage, the 2007/2008 dates, and the American Ermine + Mountain Avens pairing) and replaced it with MRO content.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12386_

2. **A sustain has no axis rotation to frame — and 1.248 was itself a sustain, so there is no rotation framing to remove.**
   The Mars axis opened at 1.238 (Viking, substrate-axis rotation #36); 1.239 through 1.249 are all SUSTAINS, so the clone source carries no rotation narrative, and at most a single distant note records that the Mars axis itself opened at 1.238 by rotating from the X-ray-astronomy axis (rotation #35, held complete at obs#15).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12387_

3. **The imaging first is the highest-RESOLUTION imaging from orbit — not "first images" or "first map.**
   HiRISE (~30 cm/pixel) returned the highest-resolution images of Mars from orbit; the catalog scopes this as the highest-RESOLUTION spatial imaging from orbit, notes that Mariner 9 (obs#2) made the first global IMAGE map and MGS (obs#9) the first global TOPOGRAPHIC map and thermal-infrared mineral survey, and never re-claims those firsts.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12388_

4. **The RSL first is the first detection of recurring slope lineae — not confirmed liquid water.**
   MRO's HiRISE discovered and identified recurring slope lineae, seasonal dark flows on warm slopes; the catalog scopes this as the first detection/identification of RSL and presents them as a newly identified seasonal surface phenomenon possibly involving briny or hydrated salts, without overclaiming confirmed flowing liquid water.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12389_

5. **CRISM and SHARAD are described but NOT anchored.**
   ESA's Mars Express OMEGA detected clays and phyllosilicates from orbit first (2004–06), so CRISM is described in the past tense (powered off 3 April 2023) and is not anchored; Mars Express's MARSIS was the first subsurface sounding radar at Mars (2005), so SHARAD is described only and is not anchored. ESA's Mars Express appears as the correct holder of those firsts — a scoping foil, not a leak.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12390_

6. **MRO is an ORBITER — never a lander or rover.**
   MRO works from orbit; it does not land or rove. The catalog never describes MRO landing or roving and never re-claims any lander or rover first.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12391_

7. **The mission is framed positively and in the present tense — MRO is still operating as of 2026.**
   MRO has worked at Mars for about twenty years and is still operating, with HiRISE having passed 100,000 images; the catalog frames the mission positively in the present tense. The one past-tense exception is CRISM, powered off on 3 April 2023, while MRO itself remains active.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12392_

10. **The imaging-resolution scope and the CRISM/SHARAD non-anchors are the load-bearing guards of this sustain.**
   Because Mariner 9 (obs#2) already made the first image map and MGS (obs#9) the first topographic map and thermal-IR survey, MRO's imaging claim must stay scoped to the highest-RESOLUTION spatial imaging; and because ESA's Mars Express detected clays (OMEGA) and sounded the subsurface (MARSIS) first, CRISM and SHARAD must stay described-only and unanchored. Both scopes were held throughout, yielding 0 predecessor re-stamps.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12395_

11. **The shader renders procedural geometry rather than archived mission imagery.**
   The MRO shader uses analytic curves and procedural noise rather than loading actual mission data or HiRISE imagery. A future revision could render a real HiRISE scene — a dune field, a fresh ice-exposing crater, or a documented recurring-slope-lineae site (publicly available from the Planetary Data System) — for a higher-fidelity view.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12396_

13. **The sharpest-eye mode is depicted rather than driven by real HiRISE data.**
