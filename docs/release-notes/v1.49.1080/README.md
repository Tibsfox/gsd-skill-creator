## Dedication

v1.263 TESS is dedicated to the people across NASA, the Goddard Space Flight Center, MIT and the MIT Kavli Institute, MIT Lincoln Laboratory, Orbital ATK and Northrop Grumman, and the mission's science teams who built and fly the survey that watches nearly the whole sky for the small worlds of the nearest, brightest stars. The engineers who built the four wide cameras, each a wide field of view, and stacked them into one tall strip that steps sector by sector around the sky; the launch team who sent it up on a Falcon 9 from Cape Canaveral in April 2018 and steered it, past the Moon, into a stable high orbit that swings out and back every two weeks; the principal investigator George Ricker and the teams who argued that the brightest, nearest stars were the right ones to watch, so their planets could be measured and their air read; the scientists who turn the faint, repeated dips into thousands of candidate worlds and hundreds of confirmed planets, near enough to follow up; and the operators who keep it surveying still, year after year. They watch the whole open sky, and the small worlds cross it one by one.
