The sustained crush and the ultra-thin hum.

Degree 91. The first degree of Quadrant II. The engine passes the quarter-mark and opens the second quadrant with extremes on both axes.

Hovercraft crushes. Sustained bass drones, guitar feedback loops, electronic distortion, and volume creating physical pressure. The sound presses down like weight. No vocals. No song structure. No beginning, middle, or end. The music starts, sustains, and stops. Beth Liebling and Ryan Campbell. Beth Liebling married to Eddie Vedder of Pearl Jam. The biggest grunge band and a two-person drone/noise project sharing a household but nothing else aesthetically. The spousal satellite -- artistic independence orbiting biographical fame. Genre Stage 73, Drone/Noise, the most extreme genre the engine has catalogued. E=2 -- the volume paradox: deafening in the room, invisible to the world. From the planetarium (degree 90, the ambient drift) to the bunker (degree 91, the sustained crush). From gentle immersion to sonic obliteration.

The mosquito swarm hums. Barely. A high-pitched, thin, persistent whine produced by wings beating 300-600 times per second. Not voice, not stridulation, not tymbal -- the first wing-produced sound. The first sound that is a mechanical byproduct of locomotion rather than a dedicated signal. NEW FAMILY Culicidae, 41st in the engine. NEW ORDER Diptera, fourth consecutive insect order. The ultra-thin hum cloud -- diffuse, barely there, at the very threshold of perception. E=2 -- the quietest sound the engine has catalogued. Wetland habitat. Standing water and mating columns at dawn and dusk. Thirty-first consecutive original CSV species.

Both E=2. Both at thresholds. Both drone. Hovercraft drones at maximum volume for minimum audience. The mosquito drones at minimum volume for maximum ubiquity. The sustained crush presses down. The ultra-thin hum barely registers. One pushes the upper limit of what an audience will endure. The other tests the lower limit of what an ear can detect. The engine opens Quadrant II at both boundaries simultaneously.
