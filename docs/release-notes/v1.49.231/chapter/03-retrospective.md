## Lessons Learned

- **Quadrant II opens with maximum extremity on the music side and minimum volume on the species side.** The engine does not transition gently between quadrants. Degree 91 is a statement: the second quadrant will be different from the first. Drone/Noise (the most extreme genre catalogued) at the precise transition point is a deliberate framing — the opening is structural, not accidental. Future quadrant boundaries (180/181, 270/271, 359/0) should be planned as statements rather than continuations.
- **The E=2/E=2 pairing reveals that E measures cultural and ecological penetration rather than acoustic intensity.** The matched energy with inverted volume (Hovercraft loud / mosquito quiet) is the first time both axes share E=2 at the same degree since early in the engine. This is a clarifying structural insight that resolves an ambiguity present since the framework was first deployed. E is a penetration metric, not a decibel metric. Future energy-level assignments should foreground penetration explicitly.
- **The spousal satellite pattern has specific applicability and will recur.** The pattern describes a real and recurring phenomenon in music scenes where one partner's fame overshadows the other's independent work. It complements the parallel scene (90) and the theatrical outsider (89) in a three-degree sequence exploring artistic positioning relative to dominant forces. Future Seattle entries with similar household-relationship structures (e.g., Carrie Brownstein / Fred Armisen, Krist Novoselic spouse projects) should be classified against the spousal satellite.
- **Genre Stage 73 maintains the ~0.80 genres-per-degree ratio established across the first quadrant.** Seventy-three genre stages in 91 degrees. The genre density is a stable structural feature of the engine, not a coincidence. The rate has held since the folk zone began at degree 45. The ratio is worth tracking forward as a long-term engine-health metric — if the ratio destabilizes, it signals either over-subdivision (rate climbs) or under-coverage (rate drops).
- **The first wing-produced sound is a genuine new sound category that expands the engine's taxonomy of sound production mechanisms.** Three insect mechanisms now: stridulation (physical rubbing), tymbal (membrane vibration), wing vibration (mechanical byproduct). Each order brought its own mechanism. The engine's biological sound taxonomy is growing more resolved. Future insect entries should be evaluated against the three-mechanism map; a fourth mechanism (e.g., pure substrate vibration, defensive hissing) would be its own structural milestone.
- **The contrast between degree 90 and degree 91 is one of the engine's sharpest consecutive transitions.** From shimmer to crush/hum. The engine produces dramatic movement between adjacent degrees. The 90/91 transition matches the 89/90 transition (baroque whisper to ambient drift) as the engine's most dramatic adjacent contrasts. Future degrees should be evaluated for transition sharpness; back-to-back sharp transitions indicate the engine is in a high-volatility phase.
- **Thirty-one consecutive original CSV species (61–91) extends the data reliability milestone.** The CSV has not needed substitution or correction in 31 consecutive entries. The data foundation remains solid into Quadrant II. The streak length is itself a structural fact about catalog quality — if substitution is required in Quadrant II, the streak break will be a notable event worth marking.
- **The wetland habitat network now spans four degrees (85, 86, 90, 91) and functions as an explicit ecological lineage.** Muskrat marsh, Pacific Treefrog wet habitat, spring peeper wet meadow, mosquito wetland. The lineage demonstrates the engine's capacity to track ecological networks across non-adjacent degrees. Future wetland entries (heron, marsh wren, salamander, bittern) should be classified against the existing network rather than treated as standalone entries — habitat-network tracking is a tested vocabulary worth applying to other shared-niche clusters (forest canopy, alpine, intertidal).
