- **The first wing-produced sound is a genuine new sound category that expands the engine's taxonomy of sound production mechanisms.** Three insect mechanisms now: stridulation (physical rubbing), tymbal (membrane vibration), wing vibration (mechanical byproduct). Each order brought its own mechanism. The engine's biological sound taxonomy is growing more resolved. Future insect entries should be evaluated against the three-mechanism map; a fourth mechanism (e.g., pure substrate vibration, defensive hissing) would be its own structural milestone.
- **The contrast between degree 90 and degree 91 is one of the engine's sharpest consecutive transitions.** From shimmer to crush/hum. The engine produces dramatic movement between adjacent degrees. The 90/91 transition matches the 89/90 transition (baroque whisper to ambient drift) as the engine's most dramatic adjacent contrasts. Future degrees should be evaluated for transition sharpness; back-to-back sharp transitions indicate the engine is in a high-volatility phase.

The streak length is itself a structural fact about catalog quality — if substitution is required in Quadrant II, the streak break will be a notable event worth marking.
