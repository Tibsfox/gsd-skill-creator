# Retrospective — v1.49.52

## What Worked

1. **The TeX source was the richest yet.** 1,087 lines with detailed product architecture, patent analysis, competitive mapping, and market projections. The research agent augmented this with web search rather than replacing it. TeX + web research dual-source continues to produce the deepest output.

2. **The competitive landscape module (04) is a genuine market analysis.** 10 companies mapped across 7 dimensions is more analytical depth than most of the creative/cultural projects. The Research series proves it can handle business analysis with the same rigor as ecology or music.

3. **Project #50 landed naturally.** No forced milestone — Synsor Corp is a real PNW company in a real Everett office building making real sensor products. The 50th project is about sensing and measurement, which is what the entire Research series does: measure, document, verify.

## Lessons Learned

1. **The PNW tech corridor extends north.** Seattle → Bellevue → Redmond → Everett. Synsor in Everett, Boeing in Everett. The manufacturing corridor that Weyerhaeuser built with timber is now building with sensors. The Research series maps the same geography through different eras.

2. **50 projects is a library.** What started as 16 PNW ecology studies is now a 50-project research library covering ecology, music, literature, conventions, cybersecurity, business law, timber, and industrial AI. The Rosetta Stone framework holds because the projects genuinely connect — not through forced metaphor but through shared geography, shared principles, and shared patterns.
