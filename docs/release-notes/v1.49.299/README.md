## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Odontophoridae (1st entry -- NEW FAMILY, New World quails)
- **Order:** Galliformes (continuing, 7th consecutive degree)
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The California Quail (*Callipepla californica*) is a New World quail native to the western United States, abundant throughout the Pacific Northwest in brushy edges, agricultural margins, suburban parks, and open woodlands. It is the **state bird of California** and one of the most widely recognized birds in western North America. The **chi-CA-go call** (also transcribed as *ka-KAA-ko* or *Chicago*) is a loud, three-note assembly call used by separated covey members to locate each other and regroup. The middle syllable is highest and loudest, creating a distinctive rising-falling pattern that carries well across open habitat. E=4 reflects the call's strong carrying power balanced against its relatively simple tonal structure. The **topknot plume** -- a forward-curving ornamental crest -- is actually a cluster of six overlapping comma-shaped feathers that bounce with each step, giving the bird its distinctive bobbing silhouette. Both males and females carry the plume, though the male's is larger and darker. California Quail are highly social, forming coveys of 10-75 birds outside the breeding season. They are ground-dwelling birds that prefer to run rather than fly, though they flush explosively when pressed. **Odontophoridae** (New World quails) are taxonomically distinct from Old World quails (Phasianidae) despite the shared common name -- they diverged roughly 40 million years ago and represent convergent evolution in body plan and lifestyle. The transition from Phasianidae (153-158) to Odontophoridae (159) at degree 159 keeps the engine within Galliformes while shifting to a fundamentally different evolutionary lineage. Where Phasianidae dominated with large-bodied species (turkey, grouse), Odontophoridae brings small-bodied, covey-forming, ground-dwelling birds with distinctive ornamental plumage.
- **Baile funk connection:** Sango's production style draws heavily from baile funk -- the high-energy dance music originating from the favelas of Rio de Janeiro. Characterized by rapid-fire percussion patterns, heavy bass, and vocal chops, baile funk rhythms infuse Sango's hip-hop and R&B production with a distinctly Brazilian pulse. This represents the first explicit global rhythmic influence in the Seattle hip-hop arc -- where earlier entries drew from jazz (Blue Scholars), soul (Geo), and experimental electronics (Shabazz Palaces), Sango brings an international dance-floor energy.
