## Mark Lanegan -- A Memorial

Mark William Lanegan. Born November 25, 1964, in Ellensburg, Washington. Died February 22, 2022, in Killarney, Ireland. Age 57.

**Screaming Trees** (1985-2000). Formed in Ellensburg -- not Seattle, but central Washington, the dry side of the Cascades. The band predated the grunge explosion, survived it, and outlasted most of their peers. Albums on Velvetone, SST, Sub Pop, and Epic. *Sweet Oblivion* (1992) broke through during the grunge wave with "Nearly Lost You" on the *Singles* soundtrack. But the Trees were never quite grunge -- too psychedelic, too raw, too Lanegan.

**Solo career** (1990-2022). Lanegan released his first solo album, *The Winding Sheet*, on Sub Pop in 1990 -- two years before Nirvana's *Nevermind*. The solo work was where the voice found its truest register: deep, ravaged, intimate. Whiskey-soaked baritone. The voice of someone who had been through something. Eleven solo albums. The late-period work moved into **darkwave** territory -- electronics, drum machines, gothic textures. *Somebody's Knocking* (2019) and *Straight Songs of Sorrow* (2020) are darkwave documents.

**Collaborations.** Queens of the Stone Age. Mad Season. The Gutter Twins (with Greg Dulli). Isobel Campbell. Soulsavers. Lanegan was a collaborator by nature -- his voice fit into other people's worlds while remaining unmistakably itself.

**The voice.** Low, damaged, beautiful. One of the three or four most recognizable voices in Pacific Northwest music history. A voice that carried its own weather.

E=4. The darkwave period brings measured intensity -- not the full-volume Trees, not the hushed solo whisper, but the middle ground where electronics and voice meet.

## Taxonomic State

- Classes: 2 (Aves, Mammalia)
- Orders: 8+ active in rotation
- Anatidae streak: 3 (118-119-120) -- Harlequin Duck, Common Merganser, Hooded Merganser
- Hooded Merganser (*Lophodytes cucullatus*): the smallest of the three merganser species in North America. The **fan-shaped crest** -- a large, erectile, black-and-white head crest that the male raises during courtship into a dramatic semicircular fan. When raised, the crest doubles the apparent head size. The most visually striking head ornament in North American waterfowl.
- The rolling frog-like call: a low, guttural, rolling croak that sounds more amphibian than avian. Unexpected from a bird with such dramatic visual display. The disconnect between visual spectacle and acoustic modesty.
- Sawbill adaptation continues: like the Common Merganser (119), the Hooded Merganser has serrated bill edges for gripping fish. Smaller bill, smaller fish. Crayfish and aquatic insects supplement the diet.
