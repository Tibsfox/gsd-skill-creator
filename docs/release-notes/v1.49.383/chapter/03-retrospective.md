# Retrospective — v1.49.383

## What Worked

- **The closure of the six-degree Salmonidae arc at degree 242 provided narrative resolution to a sustained multi-degree genus-level documentation project.** The Salmonidae arc ran from degree 237 through degree 242 and documented the full *Oncorhynchus* genus's Pacific Northwest presence across both salmon and trout species. The arc's closure at Cutthroat Trout — the smallest and quietest species in the group — provided a natural narrative endpoint that complemented the opening at Chinook (the largest and loudest). Future genus-level arcs can use the Salmonidae arc's six-degree length and its amplitude-descending structure as reference baseline.
- **The completion of the Silent Fish Trio across three consecutive E=2 degrees demonstrated the engine's capacity for sustained minimum-amplitude SPS entries.** Bull Trout (240), Dolly Varden (241), and Cutthroat Trout (242) all registered at E=2 while the S36 track ran at E=6, E=8, E=7. The three-degree stretch of minimum-amplitude SPS entries documented that the engine can maintain compositional balance even when one track stays substantially below the other's amplitude for an extended run. The register validates the engine's cross-track amplitude-independence and demonstrates that SPS minimums do not require S36 minimums to sustain the cross-track balance.
- **The Tacoma garage rock lineage (Wailers at 242 + Sonics at 241) corrected six decades of Seattle-centric Pacific Northwest rock historiography.** The conventional narrative places Pacific Northwest rock origins in Seattle; the chronological documentation places them in Tacoma. The engine's two consecutive Tacoma degrees (241, 242) establish the geographic correction with specificity that casual-reference documentation cannot provide, and the Wailers-Sonics pairing demonstrates that the engine's historiographic-correction register can operate through adjacent-degree documentation as well as through single-entry placement.
- **The Lewis-and-Clark-named-species register added a historical-scientific-record dimension to SPS taxonomic documentation.** The *Oncorhynchus clarkii* binomial and the *O. c. lewisi* subspecies designation carry the Corps of Discovery's scientific record permanently into the present. The register is interpretively productive because it documents that Pacific Northwest species nomenclature preserves historical-expedition scientific records, and the register extends to future species whose binomials carry comparable historical significance (e.g., *Lewisia* plants, *Clarkia* flowers, and other Lewis-and-Clark-era scientific descriptions).
- **The intra-Salish cross-axis register established shared-watershed geography as a valid cross-axis basis distinct from amplitude or acoustic quality.** The Wailers' Tacoma origin and the Cutthroat's Puget Sound drainage habitat share the same Salish Sea watershed at the most intimate possible geographic scale. The cross-axis register demonstrates that the engine's twelfth documented Q3 cross-axis can operate through shared-place rather than through shared-amplitude or shared-acoustic-quality, and the register opens the possibility of future cross-axes that document shared-geography structural relationships.

## What Could Be Better

- **The Wailers' second-phase Kent-Morrill-led R&B-vocal material is documented only in summary rather than in full depth.** The founding-era "Tall Cool One" instrumental receives substantial documentation, but the mid-1960s vocal-era material (including "Mau-Mau" in 1963 and the Kent Morrill-led subsequent recordings) is mentioned rather than explored in depth. Future Wailers-related documentation — whether through direct re-visitation or through related-artist entries — should develop the second-phase vocal-era material more fully, because the two-phase career structure is structurally central to understanding how Pacific Northwest rock's founding generation navigated the transition from instrumental to vocal-led rock.
- **The Etiquette Records infrastructure register is introduced at degree 242 but is documented primarily through its downstream effects rather than through direct Etiquette-era documentation.** The Wailers-Ormsby-Etiquette-Sonics infrastructure lineage is structurally important, but the specific Etiquette catalog, the label's business operations, and its role in sustaining Pacific Northwest garage-rock through the mid-to-late-1960s is mentioned rather than analyzed in detail. Future engine releases should consider whether Etiquette Records warrants its own dedicated label-focused entry, because the regional-independent-label register is structurally central to Pacific Northwest music history.
- **The cutbow hybridization conservation register is introduced but the full genetic-dilution ecology is deferred.** The degree 242 release introduces the cutbow hybridization challenge for Cutthroat genetic integrity but does not document the full genetic-dilution ecology (hybridization rates, backcross generations, geographic variation in hybridization pressure, introgression measurement methodologies). Future SPS documentation should expand the genetic-dilution-ecology register explicitly, because hybridization is structurally central to Pacific Northwest salmonid conservation across multiple species and the current surface-level documentation does not do justice to the conservation challenge.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Multi-degree arcs should have documented closure points that provide narrative resolution.** The Salmonidae arc's closure at degree 242 with Cutthroat Trout provided narrative resolution to the six-degree documentation project, and the closure position carries distinct documentary weight relative to the opening or middle positions. Future multi-degree arcs should be planned with explicit closure-position awareness rather than open-endedly extended without closure planning, because explicit closure improves interpretive clarity at the arc's endpoint.
2. **Sustained minimum-amplitude entries on one track do not require minimum amplitudes on the paired track.** The Silent Fish Trio's three consecutive E=2 SPS entries coexisted with S36 entries at E=6, E=8, and E=7. The sustained amplitude divergence validates the engine's cross-track amplitude-independence, and future engine releases should not treat extended amplitude divergence as a compositional problem requiring correction — it is a valid structural feature that documents the relationship between human cultural intensity and ecological quietness.
3. **Geographic correction of conventional narratives requires adjacent-degree documentation rather than single-entry placement alone.** The Wailers-Sonics Tacoma pairing at degrees 241 and 242 corrects Seattle-centric Pacific Northwest rock historiography through two consecutive degrees. A single Wailers or Sonics entry would have been easier to dismiss as an exception; two consecutive Tacoma-founded bands in the founding-era position force geographic reconsideration. Future engine releases should use adjacent-degree documentation when geographic-correction is the documentary intent.
4. **Historical-scientific-record registers can be surfaced through species nomenclature.** The *Oncorhynchus clarkii* binomial and the *O. c. lewisi* subspecies name carry the Corps of Discovery's historical record permanently. Future SPS documentation should attend to species nomenclature for historical-scientific-record implications, because the binomials themselves are primary-source records whose presence in the engine's documentation carries historical weight.
5. **Cross-axis registers can operate through shared-place rather than only through shared-amplitude or shared-acoustic-quality.** The intra-Salish cross-axis at degree 242 documents Wailers' Tacoma and Cutthroat's Puget Sound drainage as the same Salish Sea watershed. The shared-place cross-axis is structurally distinct from earlier Q3 cross-axes and demonstrates that the engine's cross-axis vocabulary can accommodate geographic-relationship bases as well as amplitude or acoustic bases. Future cross-axis documentation should attend to shared-place possibilities alongside amplitude and acoustic-quality parallels.
6. **Subspecies-diverse species require subspecies-specific conservation documentation rather than species-level aggregation.** Cutthroat Trout's more-than-a-dozen subspecies range from ESA-Threatened (Paiute, Lahontan, Greenback) to state species-of-concern (Westslope, Yellowstone) to recreational-fishery species (Coastal). Species-level conservation documentation would aggregate these differences into a misleading uniform status. Future SPS documentation of subspecies-diverse species should document conservation status at the subspecies level and avoid the misleading species-level aggregation that subspecies-unaware documentation would produce.
7. **Regional-independent-label infrastructure sustains successor bands across generations.** The Wailers-Ormsby-Etiquette-Sonics infrastructure lineage documents how the founding generation of Pacific Northwest rock built the recording infrastructure that sustained its successors. Future Pacific Northwest music documentation should trace label-infrastructure lineage explicitly, because the independent-label-infrastructure register is structurally central to Pacific Northwest music history and the continuity across generations is not adequately captured by single-band or single-era documentation alone.
8. **Dual-form life-history registers accumulate across genus-level arcs when multiple species share the register.** Steelhead at degree 239 established the first dual-form life-history register in the SPS vocabulary; Cutthroat Trout at degree 242 established the second through the Coastal Cutthroat's anadromous-and-resident dual-form expression. Multiple dual-form species within a single genus-level arc document that the register is a genus-level feature rather than a single-species idiosyncrasy. Future SPS documentation should track dual-form register accumulation across genus-level arcs explicitly, because the accumulation validates the register's structural generalizability.
9. **Founding-generation entries carry documentary weight distinct from mid-narrative entries.** The Wailers' founding-generation position in the Pacific Northwest rock narrative carries distinct documentary weight because it establishes the chronological origin of the tradition. Future engine releases should identify founding-generation entries explicitly when they occur and should document the chronological-origin status as a first-class register rather than treating the founding position as incidental to the artist's specific work.
10. **High-school-formation origin stories document a distinct Pacific Northwest rock-origin mode separate from jazz-club or parking-lot origins.** The Wailers' Stadium High School formation and The Sonics' comparable Tacoma-high-school origin together establish the high-school-teen-music-ecosystem as a distinct Pacific Northwest rock-origin mode. Future engine releases should track high-school-formation origin stories explicitly and should distinguish them from jazz-club, art-school, or parking-lot origins, because the origin-mode vocabulary organizes significant structural variation in how Pacific Northwest bands came into being across different eras.
