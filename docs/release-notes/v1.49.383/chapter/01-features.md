## SALMONIDAE ARC CLOSES


- six consecutive degrees (237-242) documented the core Pacific salmon and trout species of the Pacific Northwest: Chinook (E=6), Coho (E=6), Steelhead (E=5), Bull Trout (E=2), Dolly Varden (E=2), Cutthroat Trout (E=2); the arc moved from the largest, loudest, most culturally visible salmon species to the smallest, quietest, most interior trout species; it documented both the commercially fished species that built the PNW economy and the wilderness-indicator species that reveal the health of intact watersheds; the arc closes at degree 242 and will not reopen -- the next SPS entries will be outside the Salmonidae family entirely
