## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Strigidae (10th entry -- GHO 135, GHO duet 136, Barred 137, Barred caterwaul 138, Long-eared 139, Short-eared 140, Northern Saw-whet 141, Western Screech-Owl 142, Northern Pygmy-Owl 143, Flammulated Owl 144)
- **BREAKS PICIDAE RECORD** -- 10 entries, owls now hold sole record for most family entries in the engine
- **Order:** Strigiformes (continuing, 10-degree owl run)
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The Flammulated Owl (*Psiloscops flammeolus*) is a tiny (15 cm), strictly insectivorous owl of montane ponderosa pine and mixed-conifer forests. Its voice is **disproportionately deep** for its size -- a low, soft, ventriloquial hoot that can be nearly impossible to locate. The call carries poorly, audible only at close range, making this one of the hardest owls to detect in the field. E=2 sets a **new SPS minimum** -- the quietest entry in the entire Sound of Puget Sound series. The only small owl in North America with dark eyes (all others have yellow). Highly migratory -- winters in Mexico and Central America, the only truly migratory small owl in North America.
- **Ishmael Butler arc:** Digable Planets (degree 144) represents the smooth, accessible, Grammy-winning jazz rap of the early 1990s. Shabazz Palaces (degree 103) represents the radical reinvention -- avant-garde, experimental, Sub Pop-signed. Same artist (Ishmael Butler), separated by two decades and a complete artistic transformation. The engine captures both endpoints. *Reachin' (A New Refutation of Time and Space)* (1993) and *Blowout Comb* (1994) gave way to *Black Up* (2011) and *Lese Majesty* (2014). From Pendulum to Sub Pop. From jazz samples to electronic abstraction.
- **Genre Stage 99:** Jazz Rap enters the engine as the ninety-ninth genre classification. One away from 100.
