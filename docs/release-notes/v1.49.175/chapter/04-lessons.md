1. **Repeat entities reveal multidimensionality.**
   When the engine encounters the same person twice (Ron Artis II at 24, Ron Artis Jr at 39), the appearances do not collapse into redundancy. They reveal that one biographical point maps to multiple musical points. The 360-degree circle is not a line of unique artists -- it is a multidimensional space where a single person can occupy multiple positions viewed through different lenses. Track dual-degree candidates as a first-class structural category at every future degree, and expect that any artist whose career spans multiple genres or eras may become a dual-degree artist if their engine position moves.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5433_

2. **Patronyms carry colonial context.**
   Naming *Setophaga townsendi* for John Kirk Townsend documents 19th-century natural history's embedded assumptions: specimen collection that included human remains from Indigenous burial sites, expeditions funded by commercial ventures (Nathaniel Wyeth's 1834 fur trade expedition), westward colonial reach. The engine names the bird and the honoree but also acknowledges the context. Future patronym taxa should include this acknowledgment explicitly rather than passing it as neutral taxonomy.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5434_

3. **Consecutive genus pairs map dynamics, not diversity.**
   Previous family runs (Hirundinidae 26-30, Trochilidae 35-37) demonstrated that consecutive degrees could survey taxonomic space. The *Setophaga* pair at 38-39 demonstrates a different mode: two species in the same genus that actually interact in nature, hybridize where their ranges overlap, and compete via introgression. When future consecutive genus pairs appear, expect them to map ecological interactions rather than morphological or phylogenetic distribution, and budget research for hybridization dynamics rather than just type-species biology.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5435_

4. **Aeromechanical-to-vocal alternation continues.**
   The Trochilidae trio (35-37) introduced wing-generated sounds (the thrum, the sonic boom, the filament); degrees 38-39 returned to vocal sound (the drawl, the canopy buzz). The engine alternates between sound-production mechanisms as it moves through consecutive taxa. Future degrees should watch for the switch-back pattern and note whether sound-production mechanism correlates with family boundaries or with other structural axes.

7. **Return is accretion, not repetition.**

The musician expands his PNW circuit visit by visit, from one city to four. Track accretion patterns at all future recurrence degrees and distinguish stable recurrence (The Touring Anchor) from accelerating recurrence (The Returning Wave).
