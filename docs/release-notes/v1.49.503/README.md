## What's New in Pass 2

### S36 — Wayne Horvitz: Pass 2 Refinement (3,963 words)

Four new research sections not present in Pass 1:

1. **The Composed-Improvisation Method** — Horvitz's scores as a third paradigm between jazz lead sheets and Zorn's game pieces. Detailed compositional frameworks with specific zones designated for improvisation — the walls are fixed but the furniture can move. Gravitas Quartet recordings as exemplar. The three-degree genealogy: Jones (degree 0) = total compositional control, Frisell (degree 1) = total improvisational freedom, Horvitz (degree 2) = synthesis of both.

2. **The Royal Room and Community Infrastructure** — Full history of the Columbia City venue Horvitz co-founded: December 2011 opening, 5000 Rainier Ave S, 99 seats, $210K raised from 18 investors plus RVCDF loan and 4Culture grant. The South Hudson Music Project nonprofit (2016). Institution-building at three scales: Jones at the industry level (Mercury VP, Qwest Records), Frisell at the artistic level (solo concert model), Horvitz at the community level (neighborhood venue).

3. **Film Scoring as Compositional Laboratory** — Comprehensive catalog: Gus Van Sant sound design, PBS *Chihuly Over Venice*, Chaplin's *The Circus* (85-minute live score for 2 pianos/2 clarinets/violin), Ozu's *Woman of Tokyo*, *Cabinet of Dr. Caligari* at the Paramount Theatre. Scoring for Paul Taylor Dance Company and White Oak Dance Project. The discipline of writing to picture as constraint-driven composition feeding back into concert work. Doris Duke Performing Artist Award.

4. **Pioneer 1 Resonance** — Horvitz as Pioneer 1: failed to reach the mainstream but returned enormous data from the frontier. The sword fern parallel — Polystichum munitum persists through disturbance, provides structural continuity beneath the canopy. Three-organism vertical section across degrees: Armillaria underground (degree 0), fireweed on the surface (degree 1), sword fern in the understory (degree 2).

### SPS — Varied Thrush: Pass 2 Refinement (3,712 words)

Four new research sections not present in Pass 1:

1. **Winter Altitudinal Migration** — The Varied Thrush as a two-world bird: breeding in high-elevation montane forest (1,000-2,000m), descending to lowland urban areas in winter. The song heard in Seattle backyards is the echo of montane wilderness. Irruptive years when deep cold drives mass descents to sea level. BC Bird Atlas logo status. The vertical migration as seasonal marker.

2. **AM Synthesis Deep Dive** — Expanding on Pass 1's amplitude modulation discussion. Bilateral syringeal biomechanics producing the buzzy carrier+modulation signature. Beat frequency theory — two slightly offset oscillators producing AM. Individual acoustic fingerprints: each bird identified by its unique combination of carrier frequency, modulation rate, and modulation depth. Comparison to Edwin Armstrong's electronic AM synthesis.

3. **The Haunting-Sound Phenomenon** — Psychoacoustic analysis of why this simple song is consistently rated the most evocative in PNW forests. Ear canal resonance peak at 2.5-3 kHz, roughness perception in the 20-50 Hz modulation range, silence-framing enabling complete envelope processing, minor-quality pitch intervals between successive notes. Cultural resonance in PNW literature and film.

4. **Pioneer 1 Resonance** — The Varied Thrush's song "fails" at complexity but succeeds at atmospheric impact — the same inversion as Pioneer 1, which failed to reach the Moon but succeeded at returning data. The sword fern die-off at Seward Park (first observed 2013) revealing how the understory constant defines ecosystem character. Three baselines across degree 2: sword fern (ecological baseline), Varied Thrush (acoustic baseline), Pioneer 1 (empirical baseline).
