9. **Visually striking species can have acoustically modest signatures, and the engine should document both channels separately.**
   The Bufflehead's bold black-and-white tuxedo plumage and disproportionately large buffalo head make it one of the most visually conspicuous wintering ducks in Puget Sound, but its squeaky peeping at 2,000-6,000 Hz is among the softest calls in North American waterfowl. The visual-versus-acoustic disconnect at Bufflehead (documented earlier at Hooded Merganser 120) is now established as a recurring pattern, and future species entries should routinely document visual and acoustic channels separately rather than assuming correlation. The same lesson applies to artist entries where visual presence and acoustic presence operate on independent dimensions.
