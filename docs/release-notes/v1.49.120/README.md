**Released:** 2026-03-28

## Summary

A comprehensive research project mapping the intersection of neurodivergence, creativity, and genius across psychology, neuroscience, and education. The project challenges the deficit model of neurological difference, documenting how the same cognitive architectures that create challenges in one domain produce extraordinary capability in another -- from absolute pitch in autism (5-11% vs <0.01% in the general population) to hyperfocused creative surges in ADHD. The central model -- Cognitive Difference x Domain Alignment x Environmental Fit = Expressed Genius -- provides a rigorous framework for understanding why some minds produce breathtaking work under the right conditions.

### Research Modules

1. **M1: The Paradigm** — From deficit to difference: Judy Singer's 1998 concept, disability rights movement, diagnostic evolution DSM-III through DSM-5, medical vs. social model
2. **M2: The Neuroscience** — Divergent thinking in ADHD, absolute pitch-autism connection, local hyperconnectivity in savant syndrome, dyslexia's explorative bias, planum temporale asymmetry
3. **M3: The Prodigies** — Documented portraits of Nash, Tesla, Turing, Einstein, da Vinci, Mozart, van Gogh, Warhol, Boyle, Eilish, Barnett with retrodiagnosis epistemic framework
4. **M4: Twice-Exceptional (2e)** — Giftedness meets neurodivergence: three identification failure modes, masking and burnout dynamics, asynchronous development, SENG framework
5. **M5: Enabling Environments** — UDL, strengths-based assessment, workplace design, and the conditions that unlock vs. suppress neurodivergent potential

## Retrospective

### What Worked
- The five-thread pre-research approach (web research before packaging) produced rich, well-sourced content across all five modules
- The Track A (history/science) and Track B (portraits/education) parallel structure kept neuroscience separate from the humanistic portraits, preventing reductive framing
- The retrodiagnosis epistemic framework in M3 provides honest guardrails for attributing neurodivergence to historical figures

### What Could Be Better
- The intersection with creative practice (how neurodivergent artists actually work day-to-day) could be expanded with practitioner interviews and case studies
- Non-Western perspectives on neurodivergence and genius are underrepresented in the current module structure

## Lessons Learned

- The core model (Cognitive Difference x Domain Alignment x Environmental Fit) is deceptively simple but maps cleanly to the neuroscience: the neural architecture creates potential, the domain activates it, the environment permits or suppresses it. All three must be present.
- Twice-exceptional individuals (2e) represent the most underserved population in education because their giftedness masks their neurodivergence and vice versa -- systems designed for one pole cannot hold both simultaneously.
- The autistic brain that struggles with social inference achieves absolute pitch at rates ninety times higher than the general population -- the same wiring produces both the challenge and the capability.
