# Retrospective — v1.49.120

## What Worked

- The five-thread pre-research approach (web research before packaging) produced rich, well-sourced content across all five modules
- The Track A (history/science) and Track B (portraits/education) parallel structure kept neuroscience separate from the humanistic portraits, preventing reductive framing
- The retrodiagnosis epistemic framework in M3 provides honest guardrails for attributing neurodivergence to historical figures

## What Could Be Better

- The intersection with creative practice (how neurodivergent artists actually work day-to-day) could be expanded with practitioner interviews and case studies
- Non-Western perspectives on neurodivergence and genius are underrepresented in the current module structure

## Lessons Learned

- The core model (Cognitive Difference x Domain Alignment x Environmental Fit) is deceptively simple but maps cleanly to the neuroscience: the neural architecture creates potential, the domain activates it, the environment permits or suppresses it. All three must be present.
- Twice-exceptional individuals (2e) represent the most underserved population in education because their giftedness masks their neurodivergence and vice versa -- systems designed for one pole cannot hold both simultaneously.
- The autistic brain that struggles with social inference achieves absolute pitch at rates ninety times higher than the general population -- the same wiring produces both the challenge and the capability.
