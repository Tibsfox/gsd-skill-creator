## Lessons Learned

- **The cathedral harmony closes the nine-element folk-acoustic taxonomy.** The murmur, the confessional, the parable tone, the held rest, the lullaby subversion, the intimate architecture, the communal harmony, the testimony roar, and the cathedral harmony together cover the modes of folk-rooted sound the engine has documented across nine consecutive degrees. Future folk-adjacent entries will test whether any new mode is required or whether the nonad is structurally complete. Closure at nine is itself an achievement worth naming.
- **First entries of large families should be treated as beachheads, not endpoints.** Corvidae arrives as a singleton at degree 53, but unlike Certhiidae and Cinclidae (each monotypic in the PNW), Corvidae is a large family. American Crow, Common Raven, Gray Jay, Clark's Nutcracker, Black-billed Magpie are all plausible future entries. The jay is the first corvid, not the only corvid. Retrospectives should flag beachhead-vs-endpoint distinction for every new-family arrival.
- **Cognitive vocalization is a distinct acoustic category from physical vocalization.** The corvid alarm is the first element defined by what the call does (commands the forest) rather than by what it sounds like (pitch, rhythm, volume). This opens a new axis for future species entries: acoustic function as a first-class variable alongside acoustic structure. American Crow's funeral calls, Common Raven's problem-solving vocabulary, and Black-capped Chickadee's combinatorial alarm syntax all belong on this axis.
- **Retroactive biographical resonance (March 30 birthday / release date) is evidence of structural alignment.** The engine did not engineer the forty-year alignment between Pecknold's birthday and the release date. It discovered it during research. Future Part A research should actively search for birthday-release alignments, birthplace-collector alignments, and death-date alignments. Unplanned parallels are signals, not noise.
- **Paired-release cross-links enrich degrees beyond the S36/SPS pair.** The NASA 1.54 / Gemini 12 / Aldrin EVA / preparation-rendered-invisible connection added an entire second axis to degree 53 without conflicting with the primary pairing. Future degrees should surface the paired-release resonance in the main Summary, not only in pass-2 refinement. Treating the NASA track as a peer rather than as an afterthought strengthens both sides.
- **Name resonance (Fleet Foxes / Tibsfox) should be documented without over-reading.** The engine's 360-degree rotation selected Fleet Foxes for degree 53 based on Seattle 360 internal logic, not because of the name. The fox-in-the-project coincidence is real, and the engine logs it. But the documentation should not claim fate or design where there is only rotation. Honest noticing. No mysticism.
- **History-of-science biography deepens the species side as much as field-guide data.** Georg Wilhelm Steller's ten hours on Kayak Island, his recognition of Mark Catesby's Blue Jay from published illustrations, and his death at thirty-seven give the Steller's Jay a specific 1741 moment that field-guide data cannot provide. The Nice-Marler-Steller-Carlson lineage is now four generations deep. - **Carlson 2017 (Biology MDPI) on mimicry-as-mate-assessment overturns the popular competitor-deterrence reading.** Peer-reviewed recent science can replace received wisdom when the data are clean. The Steller's Jay's Red-tailed Hawk mimicry is most commonly deployed in breeding season with a mate present, not near predators or competitors. Future species entries should search for within-last-five-years peer-reviewed studies that overturn received explanations. The hawk mimicry was taught in every field guide as warning-off-competitors.
