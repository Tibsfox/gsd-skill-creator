## What Could Be Better

- **The 91-degree callback distance itself has no interpretive framework yet.** The callback records that TAAS returned from degree 125 at a 91-degree distance, but the engine has not yet produced a vocabulary for interpreting why TAAS's callback landed at 91 rather than at some other shorter distance. The 91-degree observation is structurally real but mechanistically opaque beyond the "shorter career produces more compressed callback window" heuristic, and future engine work may need to develop richer callback-distance-interpretation vocabulary.
- **The Wolverine's E=6 rating could be defended or challenged more rigorously.** The rating places the Wolverine at the same amplitude as Helms Alee's three-voice crushing harmonic and the Cougar scream's E=7-adjacent territory. The E=6 rating reflects the growl's sustained-territorial-defense amplitude rather than the Wolverine's peak vocalization (which would be the shriek at E=7+), but the distinction between growl-as-E=6 and shriek-as-E=7 is not formally documented in the engine's rating methodology. Future SPS entries may need to clarify how multi-vocalization species should have their engine amplitude rating selected from among their full vocal repertoire.
- **The climate-change-indicator register has only one instantiation at Wolverine.** The register is new at degree 216 and the Wolverine is its sole current instantiation. A single instantiation is not yet a pattern, and future SPS entries (Canada Lynx is another plausible climate-change-indicator, polar bear if the engine ever includes Arctic species) will need to either produce additional climate-change-indicator registrations or record the Wolverine as a one-off register addition rather than an emerging pattern.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Origin-band lineage tracking produces richer documentation when diaspora members enter in consecutive degrees.** The Botch diaspora consecutive-degree documentation (Knudson → Minus the Bear at 215, Cook → These Arms Are Snakes at 216) demonstrates that placing lineage-connected artists in consecutive degrees produces interpretively coherent origin-band tracking that scattered placement could not achieve. Future engine sequencing should consider grouping lineage-connected artists into consecutive-degree pairs or triads when possible.
2. **Label-level curatorial coherence is a first-class documentation register.** The Suicide Squeeze Records consecutive-degree pattern (Minus the Bear at 215, These Arms Are Snakes at 216) establishes label-level curatorial coherence as a distinct documentation register from origin-band lineage. Labels curate rosters that reflect aesthetic judgment, and tracking consecutive-degree same-label documentation captures the curatorial dimension that individual-artist entries would miss.
3. **Plateau-breaks should restore rather than rupture prevailing amplitude territory.** The E=7 plateau-break at TAAS demonstrates that effective plateau-break degrees restore prior peak-amplitude territory rather than introducing unprecedented extreme-amplitude territory. The distinction matters because pattern-management (restoration) preserves the engine's amplitude vocabulary while pattern-rupture (extreme extension) would exceed the engine's documented range without interpretive justification.
4. **Disproportionate-intensity cross-axis registers benefit from cross-domain parity across constraint types.** The pound-for-pound ferocity cross-axis at degree 216 is strengthened by having two clean instantiations in different constraint domains (temporal constraint for TAAS, physical constraint for Wolverine). Cross-domain parity demonstrates that the structural principle applies across different types of constraint, not only across different subject domains.
5. **Short-career dissolution-at-peak is a distinct documentation pattern from long-career sustained-practice.** TAAS's seven-year 2002-2009 active period with dissolution before diminishing returns establishes a short-career dissolution-at-peak documentation pattern distinct from the sustained-long-career patterns of Helms Alee (fourteen years) at degree 214 and Minus the Bear (seventeen years) at degree 215. The engine's biographical-trajectory documentation vocabulary benefits from recognizing both patterns as first-class registers rather than treating dissolution as a lesser documentation condition than sustained practice.
6. **Family-level return-after-extended-absence has interpretive weight proportional to absence duration.** The Mustelidae return at degree 216 (after a 31-degree absence since Sea Otter at 185) carries substantial interpretive weight because the intervening 31 degrees established what the absence meant. Return-entries should be documented with explicit reference to the absence-duration because the interpretive weight of the return depends on the absence.
7. **Acoustic-deception vocalizations represent a distinct SPS vocabulary register.** The Wolverine's guttural growl sounding larger than its source is a survival-strategy-by-design acoustic-deception, and the register is distinct from both loudness-as-size-accurate vocalizations (Cougar scream, Gray Wolf howl) and quietness-for-concealment vocalizations (smaller-carnivore stealth). The acoustic-deception register deserves first-class status in the SPS acoustic-environment vocabulary.
8. **Climate-change-indicator species entries should document both biological mechanism and conservation-status formalization.** The Wolverine's snow-dependent denning (biological mechanism) and the 2023 U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service ESA threatened-species listing (conservation-status formalization) together constitute the climate-change-indicator register. Both components are necessary: the biological mechanism establishes the vulnerability, and the conservation-status formalization validates the vulnerability as scientifically recognized rather than speculative.
9. **Combined-amplitude readings should be tracked across the engine's degree sequence.** The E=13 combined-amplitude value at degree 216 (TAAS E=7 + Wolverine E=6) extends the engine's combined-amplitude vocabulary beyond the prior E=11 (degree 214) and E=12 (degree 215) readings. Tracking combined-amplitude across consecutive degrees produces interpretively productive sequences that individual single-degree combined-amplitude readings cannot.
10. **Multi-vocalization species raise methodology questions about engine rating selection.** The Wolverine produces growls (E=6), shrieks (E=7+), barks (E=4), and kit chatters (E=3) — a vocal repertoire with substantially different amplitude readings across vocalization types. The engine's decision to rate the Wolverine at E=6 based on the growl (the most typical vocalization) rather than at E=7 based on the shriek (the peak vocalization) reflects a rating-methodology choice that future multi-vocalization species entries should make explicit rather than implicit.
