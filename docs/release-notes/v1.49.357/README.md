## Summary

**TAAS** arrives at E=7 -- the highest S36 energy in this eight-degree stretch and the first new artist to break the E=6 ceiling since the engine entered its current run. TAAS (an acronym, stylized in all-caps) is a Seattle indie project that builds layered, emotionally intense compositions with a density that justifies the E=7 rating. The project's sound occupies the space between post-rock's patience and indie rock's urgency, producing music that builds relentlessly toward cathartic release. At E=7, TAAS matches the energy of the Gray Wolf howl and the Cougar scream -- a level reserved for artists and species whose acoustic output fundamentally reshapes the air around them. On the species side, the **Wolverine** registers at E=6 -- and with this entry, **Mustelidae returns** to the SPS engine, bringing the fiercest pound-for-pound predator in North America. The Wolverine (*Gulo gulo*, literally "glutton glutton") is a 30-40 pound animal that routinely drives bears and wolves off kills, crushes frozen bones with its jaws, and traverses mountain terrain that would stop any other carnivore. The Wolverine's vocalization is a guttural snarl, growl, and hiss -- a threat display so disproportionate to the animal's size that it has earned the Wolverine a reputation as the most aggressive mammal in the Northern Hemisphere. Washington's Wolverine population is estimated at fewer than 25 individuals, making it one of the rarest mammals in the state.
