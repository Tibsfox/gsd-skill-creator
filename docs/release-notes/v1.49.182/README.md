## Summary

The confessional and the rasp. Degree 46 is the moment the Seattle 360 engine discovers that folk is not one sound but a spectrum, that the whisper and the declaration are both folk, that a genre can hold two consecutive degrees and reveal more internal diversity in the second than the first suggested was possible. For forty-five degrees the Seattle 360 engine has been building its vocabulary of acoustic elements, artist-city patterns, blue note deployments, and genre stages. At degree 45, **Damien Jurado** arrived as the murmur -- hushed, fragile, finger-picked, the voice at the threshold of audibility, the quiet lifer who inhabited the city for thirty-three years without becoming a household name. Now, one degree later, a different folk singer sits in a different room and opens his mouth and what comes out is not a murmur but a **confession** -- gravelly, road-worn, cracked at the edges, carrying the physical residue of punk basements and horse farms and two hundred and fifty shows a year and the death of a child.

**Rocky Votolato** enters the engine as the second consecutive folk-primary artist, extending Genre Stage 27 established at degree 45 and creating the first consecutive folk pairing in the engine's history -- only the second consecutive same-genre pairing after Blues Rock at degrees 36-37 (**Jimi Hendrix** / **Ayron Jones**). Where the Blues Rock consecutive mapped a generational transfer -- the supernova and the inheritor, sixty years apart -- the Folk consecutive maps a **parallel inhabitation**: two Seattle folk artists, both born in Texas, both transplanted to the Pacific Northwest as teenagers, both shaped by punk and DIY culture, both connected to **Pedro the Lion**, both operating outside institutional gatekeeping, and yet acoustically opposite. Jurado whispers; Votolato testifies. The murmur and the confessional -- degree 45 and degree 46 -- together prove that folk in the Seattle 360 engine is not a single sound but a register with range, from the held breath to the broken-open declaration. In parallel, on the species side, **Song Sparrow** at degree 45 introduced the preamble; **Spotted Towhee** at degree 46 delivers the rasp. Both sides pivot at the same time, in the same direction, on the same axis: the Passerellidae pair is complete after two paired degrees, and the pair explicitly echoes the artist-side transformation from the murmur to the confessional.

Born March 8, 1977, in **Dallas**, **Texas**. Raised on a fifty-acre horse farm in **Frost**, **Texas** -- population 647, a hundred miles south of **Dallas**. Father was a member of the **Scorpions**, a **Dallas**-based motorcycle gang that rivaled the **Hell's Angels**. Household soundtrack: **Willie Nelson**, **Steve Earle**, **Johnny Cash**. Parents divorced, mother remarried, family relocated to the **Pacific Northwest** in 1991 when Rocky was fourteen. Settled near **Redmond**, **Washington**. Attended **Inglemoor High School** in **Kenmore**, **Washington** -- alienated by his Southern roots in a Pacific Northwest context, drove himself inward toward guitar and songwriting. The punk awakening came through live shows: seeing **Jawbreaker** perform was the catalytic moment -- "That's exactly what I want to do with my life." **Fugazi**'s DIY ethics became his operational model. **Drive Like Jehu** from **San Diego** was another foundational influence. But even while immersed in punk, he was writing on acoustic guitar. His first guitar was acoustic. The songs he composed for **Waxwing** started on acoustic guitar before being electrified. And underneath the punk influences lay the Texas ones: **Bob Dylan**, **Cat Stevens**, **Kris Kristofferson**, **Johnny Cash**. The country and folk music of his childhood never left. It waited.

Co-founded **Waxwing** with brother **Cody Votolato** in the late 1990s -- post-hardcore, three albums on **Second Nature Recordings** (*For Madmen Only*, *One for the Ride*, *Nobody Can Take What Everybody Owns*), broke up late 2005. **Cody Votolato** is simultaneously the guitarist of the **Blood Brothers**, one of Seattle's most confrontational post-hardcore bands -- the Votolato brothers represent the engine's first documented sibling pair, the widest genre divergence within a single family: Rocky toward folk, **Cody** toward chaotic art-punk. The solo career began in parallel: the self-titled debut recorded in a basement with two microphones in four hours (1999, **Status/Henry's Finest**), *Burning My Travels Clean* produced by **Matt Bayles** (2001, **Second Nature**), *Suicide Medicine* as the critical breakthrough with **Dylan** comparisons and a voice described as "rich and dynamic, ranging from falsetto highs to a near scream" (2003, **Second Nature**). Then **Barsuk Records** -- Seattle's independent label home to **Death Cab for Cutie**, **Nada Surf**, the **Long Winters** -- and the commercial peak: *Makers* co-produced with **Casey Foubert** of **Pedro the Lion** (2006), "White Daisy Passing" on **The OC** and **NPR Song of the Day**, *The Brag and Cuss* digging into Texas roots with wife **April** contributing lyrics (2007), *True Devotion* as the final Barsuk album (2010). *Television of Saints* funded by 712 **Kickstarter** backers who pledged $38,225 against a $20,000 goal (2012) -- the engine's first crowdfunded degree, the first Kickstarter-funded album, the moment the independent musician bypasses even the indie label and goes directly to the audience. *Hospital Handshakes* produced by **Chris Walla** of **Death Cab for Cutie** in two weeks in **Omaha** (2015, **No Sleep**/**Glitterhouse**) -- **Cody Votolato**, **Andy Lum** of **My Goodness**, **Eric Corson** of the **Long Winters**, **Billboard** premiere, **Seattle Met**'s "road to redemption." Two hundred and fifty shows per year at career peak -- the highest documented touring frequency in the engine.

Then the seven-year hiatus, because a man who has been on the road for fifteen years wants to be home with his family. Then, on December 6, 2021, his child **Kienan Spero Votolato** was killed in a car accident at age twenty-two. The album of epistolary love letters he had been writing to his family became something else entirely. *Wild Roots* (2022, **Spartan**/**Thirty Something**) -- fifteen tracks, Kickstarter-funded, **KEXP**'s **Dusty Henry** under the headline "Grief is Love With Nowhere to Go," **Denver Westword**'s "Rocky Votolato Lost a Child. His Latest Record Helped Him Heal." The most personally devastating album in the engine's catalog. The confessional in extremis -- the voice that declares because it cannot contain. Each of the fifteen tracks is an epistolary song addressed to a family member; the song "Becoming Human" became a posthumous love letter. **Jurado** at degree 45 wrote cinematic character studies; **Votolato** at degree 46 wrote letters home. Both sides of folk -- the imagined other, the named beloved -- reach their mirror in the same two-degree window.

Meanwhile, deeper into the forest, where the trail narrows and the **salal** closes in and the **sword fern** reaches across the path and the **blackberry** tangles overhead, you cannot see the bird. You can hear it. First: a scratching sound. Loud, rhythmic, aggressive -- both feet kicking backward through the dry leaves, scraping the litter aside in sprays of debris. Something is down there, in the tangle, working the forest floor with the intensity of a creature that expects to find something. The scratching stops. A pause. Then -- meewww? -- a sharp, descending, cat-like call from the darkness of the thicket. Nasal. Inquisitive. Somewhere between a complaint and a question. Another pause. And then the song. Not a melody. Not a cascade. Not a complex multi-part composition. A sound like a taut rubber band being plucked. Like a piece of paper stuck into a fan. A concentrated, buzzy, vibrating trill -- one and a half seconds of pure texture, a frequency so rapidly modulated that the individual notes are invisible, only the rough, vibrating, insistent hum remains. **Drink-your-tea!**

The **Spotted Towhee** (*Pipilo maculatus*) enters the engine as the forty-seventh species, the second **Passerellidae** -- completing the sparrow pair begun at degree 45 with the **Song Sparrow**. "The spotted chirper" -- *pipilo* from Latin "to chirp," *maculatus* from Latin "spotted" -- a bird whose binomial splits between sound and sight, one half naming the voice, the other half naming the plumage. Formerly half of a bird called the **Rufous-sided Towhee**, until 1995 when the **American Ornithologists' Union** split the western and eastern forms based on plumage, song, call notes, and genetics -- approximately one million years of divergence, vocal differences accumulated through cultural transmission and sexual selection until the songs were too different for the two populations to recognize each other as the same species. The engine first encountered vocal speciation at degree 34 (**Red Crossbill** dialects, potential incipient species); the **Spotted Towhee** shows the completed outcome: a formal split, two species, the learned vocalization as speciation driver. The engine paired in parallel holds both stages of the same process at the same time -- the crossbill in-progress, the towhee completed -- so the species side mirrors what the artist side is doing with **Votolato**'s visible molt: transformation as structure rather than as event.

E=3 restored after twenty-five degrees of absence -- the last E=3 was degree 20 (**Orange-crowned Warbler**). Two consecutive energy restorations: E=2 at degree 45 (**Song Sparrow**), now E=3 at degree 46. The seven-consecutive-E=4 streak (degrees 38-44) is over. E=2 plus E=3 together now hold twenty degrees, nearly matching E=4's twenty-one. The dynamic range decompression continues. The **Song Sparrow** introduces; the **Spotted Towhee** declares. The feet rasp the ground. The syrinx rasps the air. The preamble and the rasp. The Passerellidae pair is complete. On both sides of the degree the second entry in a genre-or-family pair explodes the envelope the first entry established: **Jurado**'s murmur becomes **Votolato**'s confessional; **Song Sparrow**'s melodic complexity becomes **Spotted Towhee**'s textural concentration; in each case the second occupies the opposite register from the first without leaving the genre or family.

The Passerellidae pair contrasts the **Song Sparrow**'s melodic complexity (preamble + elaborate body, 8-12 song types, E=2, wetland edge) with the **Spotted Towhee**'s textural concentration (buzzy rasp trill, 4-9 song types, E=3, forest shrub edge). Different genera -- ***Melospiza*** ("song finch") and ***Pipilo*** ("chirper") -- different habitats, same family, same vertical range (0-3 m), same season (resident).
- **Taxonomy: The Spotted Chirper -- Pipilo maculatus Swainson 1827.** *Pipilo* from Latin "to chirp" -- onomatopoeic genus name. *Maculatus* from Latin "spotted" -- the diagnostic white spots on wings and back. Full translation: "the spotted chirper." Unlike the **Song Sparrow**'s purely acoustic name (*Melospiza melodia* = "melody song-finch"), the **Spotted Towhee**'s binomial splits between acoustic and visual -- one half names the voice, the other half names the plumage. Formerly part of "**Rufous-sided Towhee**" (*Pipilo erythrophthalmus*), split from **Eastern Towhee** in 1995 by the **AOU** based on plumage, song, call notes, and genetic differences. Approximately one million years of divergence. 21 recognized subspecies (**Peter Pyle** 2023 proposes reducing to 7). Three PNW subspecies: *oregonus* (western WA, darkest), *curtatus* (eastern WA, intermediate), *arcticus* (NE WA, most boldly spotted). Ninth consecutive Passeriformes (38-46). 39 of 47 entries (83.0%) are Passeriformes.
- **The Rasp: 47th Acoustic Element Species Side.** The forty-seventh acoustic element on the species side. A concentrated, buzzy, vibrating, single-frequency trill defined by textural intensity rather than melodic complexity. Approximately 1.5 seconds of rapid frequency modulation so dense that individual notes fuse into a continuous rough texture. Classic mnemonic: "drink-your-tea!" -- Pacific Coast birds often skip the introductory notes and deliver the rasp alone. Named "the rasp" for three resonances: the sound's file-like quality, the tool metaphor, the foraging behavior. The first acoustic element defined by textural concentration. Distinct from the buzz (degree 32, **Bohemian Waxwing**). Distinct from the thrum (degree 35, **Rufous Hummingbird**). The rasp is vocal, territorial, broad-frequency, moderate-amplitude, delivered from a fixed perch to stake a claim.
- **The Double-Footed Backward Scratch: Behavioral Acoustic Signature.** The **Spotted Towhee**'s most distinctive behavioral trait. A quick forward hop followed by an immediate backward hop, both feet kicking backward simultaneously through leaf litter, displacing it to expose food items. Pendular motion. The scratching is LOUD: in dry leaves, the sound carries clearly through the understory. Often the first indication that a **Spotted Towhee** is present. Three sounds together identify the species: the scratch (foraging noise), the mew (cat-like call), and the rasp (buzzy song). The double-scratch is the physical complement to the vocal rasp: both involve rapid, repetitive, friction-based action. The feet rasp the ground; the syrinx rasps the air.
- **The Mew Call: Sharp Descending Cat-Like Contact and Alarm.** A sharp, descending, cat-like "meewww?" lasting about half a second. Distinctive, often the first indication of presence. Given year-round, used for contact and alarm. Pairs exchange a softer lisping version while foraging in dense cover. Contrast with **Song Sparrow**'s sharp "chip" call -- brief, hard, percussive. The mew is longer, descending, more nasal.
- **E=3 Restored After 25 Degrees of Absence.** The first E=3 since degree 20 (**Orange-crowned Warbler**) -- 25 degrees without E=3. Two consecutive energy restorations: E=2 at degree 45, E=3 at degree 46. The seven-consecutive-E=4 streak (38-44) is broken. Energy distribution after degree 46: E1:3 (6.4%), E2:12 (25.5%), E3:8 (17.0%), E4:21 (44.7%), E5:3 (6.4%). E=2+E=3 together hold 20 degrees (42.5%), nearly matching E=4's 21 (44.7%). The rasp fills the thicket without leaving it. E=3 is mezzo-piano.
- **Dense Understory Habitat: Engine Reaches the Forest Floor.** The engine's first dense-understory species. The closed, tangled shrub layer where forest trees meet undergrowth. Habitat types: forest edges with dense shrub understory, clear-cut regeneration areas, riparian thickets, suburban gardens with dense plantings, **blackberry** tangles, **madrone** and **oak** woodlands with shrubby understory. Vertical position 0-3 m. After **Song Sparrow**'s wetland edge (degree 45), the **Spotted Towhee** occupies the adjacent habitat band -- the forest floor beneath the canopy. Adjacent habitats, minimal species overlap. Historical habitat change: formerly rare in western Washington. Clear-cutting created shrubby habitat, enabling population increase. One of the few PNW species that benefited from logging -- counterpoint to **Spotted Owl** (degree 4) and **Hermit Thrush** (degree 7) which declined.
- **The 1995 Split: Vocal Speciation Case Study.** The **Spotted Towhee** was split from **Eastern Towhee** in 1995 when differences in plumage, song, call notes, and genetics justified the taxonomic division. Approximately one million years of isolation. Key differences: plumage (white spots vs. none), song (harsher/more variable vs. cleaner), call notes, mitochondrial DNA. Narrow hybrid zone in Great Plains (**Nebraska**, **Dakotas**). Song differences accumulated through cultural transmission and sexual selection -- learned vocalizations driving speciation. The engine first encountered vocal speciation at degree 34 (**Red Crossbill** dialects, potential incipient species). The **Spotted Towhee** shows the completed outcome.
- **Plumage and Body: Jet Black Hood, Rufous Flanks, White Spots, Red-Orange Eye.** Males: jet black hood/back/wings/tail, rich rufous flanks, white breast center, bold white spots on wings and back (the diagnostic feature), white outer tail corners, bright red-orange eye. Females: dark brown replacing black, same pattern. PNW subspecies *oregonus* -- the darkest form, intensely black, white spotting reduced. Large sparrow: 33-49 g, 17-21 cm. PNW birds average 37-40 g -- substantially heavier than **Song Sparrow**'s *morphna* at 25-28 g. The largest Passerellidae in the engine.
- **Song Repertoire and Vocal Learning: 4-9 Song Types Per Male.** 4-9 song types per male (smaller than **Song Sparrow**'s 8-12). Both fast and slow trill types present in every population. High degree of song-sharing among males on adjacent territories in PNW *oregonus* populations. Songs acquired through imitation of neighboring adults -- vocal learner. Song structure changes relatively rapidly over short distances. Pacific Coast birds tend toward trill-only songs (no introductory notes) -- coastal simplification paralleling darker plumage.
- **Conservation: Least Concern, 40 Million Population, Common Year-Round Resident.** IUCN Least Concern. Population approximately 40 million (**Partners in Flight**). Continental Concern Score 8/20. BBS trend stable 1966-2019. Washington population increasing in western WA due to habitat creation through historical logging. Common year-round resident.
