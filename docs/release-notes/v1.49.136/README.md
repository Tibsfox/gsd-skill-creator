### Part A: Bill Frisell — Chromaticism as Space

- **Biography:** Born Baltimore 1951, studied at Berklee with Jim Hall and Pat Metheny. NYC downtown scene (1979-1989) with John Zorn, Paul Motian, Power Tools. Moved to Seattle's Fremont/Queen Anne in 1989 — "Seattle gave me space." One of the most recorded guitarists in jazz history (300+ albums).
- **The Seattle Draw:** Frisell was imported TO Seattle — reversing the export pattern of degree 0 (Jones exported FROM Seattle). This is the first inflection point in the S36 cultural geography.
- **Musical Style:** Signal chain as instrument (delay, volume swells, loops creating ensemble from solo guitar), open voicings (wide intervals, avoids close position), modal ambiguity (neither major nor minor), Americana deconstruction through avant-garde lens. Key albums: *Lookout for Hope* (1988, ECM), *Nashville* (1997, Nonesuch), *Guitar in the Space Age!* (2014), *Harmony* (2019, Blue Note).
- **Theory Mapping:** Chromatic harmony (extends Jones's ii-V-I into suspended territory), delay as counterpoint (one guitar → multiple voices = Amiga Principle), open voicings as negative space (the notes NOT played define the harmony), modal ambiguity (the threshold between major and minor), loop-based form (theme-and-variation through technology).
- **Carry-Forward from Degree 0:** All 4 IMMEDIATE items from Quincy Jones retrospective resolved — tritone lineage traced, Central District connection documented (Frisell lives 4 miles from Jones's Jackson Street), chromatic vocabulary mapped as divergent evolution, Berklee pipeline now has 2 data points.

### Part B: Pacific Wren — The Cascade Warble

- **Taxonomy:** *Troglodytes pacificus*, split from Winter Wren (*T. hiemalis*) in 2010. One of smallest PNW songbirds (~4 inches, 8-12g). The split was driven by vocal differences — the Pacific Wren's song is structurally distinct.
- **Bioacoustics — THE MAIN EVENT:** 100+ distinct notes per 5-10 second phrase. 36 notes per second at peak rate. Frequency range 2-10 kHz. The bilateral syrinx produces two independent sound sources simultaneously — biological polyphony. The song has been formally analyzed: musical intervals (scalar motion), repetition with variation, trills (rapid alternation = baroque ornament), micro-pauses creating rhythmic grouping.
- **Silence-to-Complexity Bridge:** Quantified contrast with degree 0: heron vocalizes 0-1 events/hour at 200-800 Hz; wren produces 36 notes/second at 2-10 kHz. From whole-note to sixty-fourth-note subdivision in one degree.
- **Old-Growth Dependency:** Strongly associated with old-growth and mature second-growth. The forest understory (root tangles, moss, fallen logs) provides both habitat and acoustic environment — the high canopy and dense understory create a reverb chamber that allows the complex song to propagate. The forest IS the studio.
- **Theory Mapping:** Complex melodic improvisation (each rendition differs — true improvisation over structural template), biological FM synthesis (syringeal muscle modulation parallels Chowning's 1973 FM synthesis algorithm), theme and variation (classical form in a 10-gram bird), trill as ornament (identical to baroque mordent/grace note), bilateral polyphony (two-voice counterpoint from one organism).
