1. **A sustain keeps the predecessor's axis vocabulary and replaces only the predecessor-specific content.**
   Mars Pathfinder shares the Mars-exploration vocabulary with the Mariner 9 predecessor (Mars, the red planet, the surface, regolith, the atmosphere, dust, in situ, planetary exploration, outflow channel, past water) — that vocabulary is shared and correct. The rewrite removed only the Mariner-9-specific content (Mariner Mars '71, the twin Mariner 8, the Atlas SLV-3C/Centaur from LC-36B, the 14 November 1971 Mars orbit insertion, the first-to-orbit anchor, the 7,329-image global map and ~85% coverage, the planet-encircling dust storm and the 2-month wait, the UVS/IRIS/infrared-radiometer payload, Masursky/McCauley/Leovy, the orbital revelation of Olympus Mons / Valles Marineris as the mission's anchor, the dry/wet 558.8/997.9 kg masses, the ~12-hour mapping orbit, the 27 October 1972…
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12256_

2. **A sustain has no axis rotation to frame — and 1.239 was itself a sustain, so there is no rotation framing to remove.**
   The Mars axis opened at 1.238 (Viking, substrate-axis rotation #36); both 1.239 (Mariner 9) and 1.240 (Mars Pathfinder) are SUSTAINS, so the clone source carries no rotation narrative, and at most a single distant note records that the Mars axis itself opened at 1.238 by rotating from the X-ray-astronomy axis (rotation #35, held complete at obs#15). The X-ray-axis vocabulary does not appear.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12257_

3. **The "first rover to operate on another planet" first carries the qualifier.**
   Lunokhod 1/2 operated on the MOON first (a satellite, not a planet), and PrOP-M aboard Mars 2/3 never operated — Sojourner was the first wheeled rover to operate on the surface of another planet (egress 6 July 1997); keep the verb "operate," never "first rover ever."
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12258_

4. **The "first airbag-cushioned Mars landing" first carries the qualifier.**
   Luna 9/13 used airbags on the MOON in 1966 (Luna 9 the conceptual antecedent, not a technical lineage); Pathfinder's airbag landing is scoped to Mars / another planet, distinct from Viking's retro-rocket terminal soft landing, reused by the MER rovers and dropped for MSL.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12259_

5. **The APXS is "Alpha PROTON," and the rock geochemistry first is mobile / rover-borne / individual-rock.**
   The instrument is the Alpha PROTON X-ray Spectrometer (not "Particle," the later MER/MSL name); Viking did the first FIXED-lander in-situ SOIL chemistry and never analyzed a rock, so this is not "first in-situ chemistry on Mars." Barnacle Bill was andesite-like (SiO₂ ~58%, quartz-normative); Yogi was more basaltic.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12260_

6. **The Ares Vallis flood evidence is the mission team's interpretation, not settled fact.**
   The rounded clasts, imbrication, and streamlined Twin Peaks were interpreted by the mission team as evidence of ancient catastrophic floods; a USGS study argued ice / mass-flow / glacial / eolian alternatives. The catalog never says Pathfinder "discovered water," and never re-claims Mariner 9's orbital channels — Pathfinder's contribution is ground-level in-situ sedimentology.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12261_

7. **Mars Pathfinder is a surface-mobility mission, not an orbiter and not the first Mars landing.**
   It did NOT orbit, did NOT map the planet, and did NOT reveal Olympus Mons / Valles Marineris (those belong to Mariner 9); it is NOT the first Mars landing (Viking); it is distinct from Mariner 4 (1.36, the first Mars flyby).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12262_

8. **NASA / U.S.-mission and "faster, better, cheaper" framing is an accuracy requirement here.**
   Mars Pathfinder was a wholly NASA / United States Discovery Program mission (JPL), the second Discovery mission to launch (after NEAR), ~US$265 million total — an early showcase of "faster, better, cheaper," NOT a flagship; attaching a foreign-partner caveat or calling it a flagship would be an error.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12263_

9. **A sustain references its predecessor exactly once, deliberately.**
   The only correct predecessor reference is a single nav-prev lineage note to v1.239 Mariner 9 (Mars obs#2), plus lineage references to Viking (1.238, obs#1) and Mariner 4 (1.36, the first Mars flyby). The predecessors' anchors stay with them — 0 re-stamps.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12264_

10. **Clone source is Mariner 9 (a NASA Mars orbital-reconnaissance mission, one surviving spacecraft of Mariner Mars '71, Atlas SLV-3C/Centaur, 1971–1972); Mars Pathfinder is a NASA Discovery surface-mobility mission (a lander plus the Sojourner rover, Delta II 7925, 1996–1997).**
   The most structural replacement in the rewrite is removing ALL Mariner-9-specific content — "Mariner 9," "Mariner Mars '71," "Mariner 8," "the Atlas SLV-3C/Centaur," "LC-36B," "the 14 November 1971 Mars orbit insertion," "the first-to-orbit anchor," "the 7,329-image global map / ~85% coverage," "the planet-encircling dust storm + 2-month wait," "the UVS / IRIS / infrared radiometer payload," "Masursky/McCauley/Leovy," "the orbital revelation of Olympus Mons / Valles Marineris as the mission's anchor," "the dry/wet 558.8/997.9 kg masses," "the ~12-hour mapping orbit," "the 27 October 1972 end," and "the White-tailed Kite + Spreading Phlox pairing" — and replacing with Mars Pathfinder's Discovery-Program origin, the Delta II 7925 from LC-17B, the airbag-cushioned EDL and the bounces, Ares…

11. **The shader renders procedural geometry rather than archived mission imagery.**

A future revision could render a real IMP "Presidential Panorama" mosaic or a Twin Peaks frame (publicly available from the Planetary Data System) for a higher-fidelity view.

13. **The airbag-bounce sequence is depicted rather than computed.**
   The bouncing arrival (first bounce 15.7 m, then a dozen and more diminishing bounces) is depicted in the shader but not driven from a measured or reconstructed bounce trajectory; a future ship could animate the bounce series from the reconstructed entry-descent-landing telemetry for a quantitatively faithful arrival.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12268_

14. **The rover traverse is depicted rather than driven by the real path.**
   The stop-and-go traverse from rock to rock is depicted in the shader but not rendered from the archived Sojourner traverse track and the rock-by-rock APXS sequence; a future ship could drive the traverse mode from the real ~100 m rover path and the dated rock analyses for a faithful surface-operations build-up.
