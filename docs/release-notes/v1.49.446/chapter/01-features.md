## URSIDAE PAIR (304-305)


- degrees 304 and 305 are the only consecutive Ursidae entries in the 360-degree engine; the pair documents the family's acoustic range: grizzly charge (E=9, kinetic, direct, threat-broadcast) → black bear tree-marking (E=7, behavioral, sustained, landscape-directed); the two mechanisms are acoustically distinct and ecologically complementary; the grizzly and black bear coexist in Pacific Northwest habitat through behavioral niche separation, and their acoustic signatures reflect that separation -- the grizzly dominates space through immediate force, the black bear maintains space through persistent territorial marking

## Mad Season as recovery document


- *Above* was recorded during a period when several of its members were in active recovery from substance dependence; the album's acoustic and emotional character reflects that context; the bass lines are sparse and supportive rather than propulsive; the guitar work is melodic rather than riff-driven; Staley's voice carries a quality of sustained effort that is present in all his recorded work but most audible in the Mad Season format; E=7 is the energy of the voice doing the emotional labor that the arrangement does not do for it

## Black bear acoustic ecology


- *Ursus americanus* is the most abundant large predator in Washington state, present in the Cascades, the Olympic Peninsula, and the eastern Washington highlands; the state population is estimated at 25,000-30,000 individuals; the black bear's tree-marking behavior -- standing upright to claw at the highest reachable point on a trunk, leaving claw marks, scent, and fur -- is a sustained behavioral sequence that produces the wood-crack of initial claw contact, the huff of physical exertion, and the vocalization that accompanies aroused bear behavior; E=7 documents the full acoustic package of the marking event

## Black bear vs. grizzly acoustic comparison


- the pair at 304-305 invites direct acoustic comparison: the grizzly charge combines roar (vocal), jaw-pop (percussive), brush-crash (kinetic), and ground impact (seismic) into a compound E=9 event; the black bear tree-marking combines wood-crack (percussive-kinetic), huff (exertive), and vocalization (vocal) into a sustained E=7 event; the same acoustic components appear -- vocal, percussive, kinetic -- but at different intensities and with different temporal structures; the pair is a controlled comparison within the same family

## The weight in the voice


- Layne Staley's vocal work on *Above* is documented at E=7 in the engine; what makes the E=7 carry its weight is not volume but the quality that the production allows to be audible; the Mad Season format, less compressed and less distorted than AIC, lets the voice's specific gravity register as the primary acoustic event; E=7 here is not a lower reading than it would be for a different vocalist -- it is the correct reading for what the voice is doing in this specific context
