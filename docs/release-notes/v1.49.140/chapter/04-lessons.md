1. **The Persistence is the hardest artist-city pattern to document because it has no inflection points.**
   Jones left — that is a story. Anderson left and returned — that is a story. Berry stayed. For sixty years. The narrative challenge is making persistence legible as achievement rather than stasis. The research succeeds by grounding Berry's persistence in the specific venues, the specific musical vocabulary, and the specific social function of the solo pianist. Persistence is not absence of change. It is presence through change.

3. **Self-taught mastery and the blue-note paradox illuminate a fundamental tension in music theory.**
   The piano is a fixed-pitch instrument. The blues tradition demands microtonal inflection. Berry resolves this paradox through techniques the tradition invented specifically to produce sounds the instrument was not designed to make — grace-note crushes, simultaneous major/minor thirds, rhythmic displacement. This is not a workaround. It is a creative response to constraint that IS the tradition. The engine should track these instrument-vs-tradition tensions as theory nodes in their own right.
