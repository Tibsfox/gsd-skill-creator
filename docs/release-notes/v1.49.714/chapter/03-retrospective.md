## Surprises

**Path (c) wall-clock at v714 was similar to v713 despite v714's larger substrate-form-distinct dimensional analysis.** v713's main-context authorship of SOHO completed within ~75 minutes; v714's main-context authorship of ACE with nine-dimension subform comparison completed within ~70 minutes. The expectation was that the larger comparison set would extend wall-clock; in practice, the per-deliverable structure (file-by-file authorship with clear scope) remained the rate-limiting factor.

**The Lissajous-vs-halo orbit dimension was easier to articulate than expected.** Lissajous geometry is mathematically subtle (uncoupled in-plane + out-of-plane phases vs frequency-matched phases for halo). The expectation was that this would require substantial explanation. In practice, framing it in terms of operational tradeoffs (Lissajous easier-to-inject + less station-keeping; halo more-predictable-trajectory + tighter-targeting) communicated the substrate-form distinction concisely.

**CRIS galactic cosmic-ray content was AUP-clean.** Pre-build concern was that "supernova nucleosynthesis" + "cosmic ray flux" + "Forbush decreases" might tickle trip-vocab around energy + acceleration vocabulary. In practice, framing CRIS as a galactic-cosmic-ray composition substrate-anchor and supernova nucleosynthesis as a model-constraint domain produced clean content across the brief, README, story, and shader. Positive-framing per Lesson #10406 generalizes well to high-energy astrophysics topics.

**Two of nine instruments share PI institute (Caltech) and two share UMaryland.** Pre-author expectation was that the 9-instrument suite would have nine distinct PI institutes (matching the v713 SOHO 12-instrument suite's 8-country distribution). In practice, CRIS + SIS are both Caltech, and ULEIS + SWICS + SWIMS + SEPICA cluster around UMaryland + Bern. This is substrate-form-distinct from v713 (where every instrument had a distinct PI institute) and is consistent with the Explorer-class small-mission paradigm of concentrating expertise at fewer institutions.

## Lessons Learned

**Path (c) main-context authorship is now the default for clean topics.** v712 introduced it as preferred for clean topics + bandwidth; v713 demonstrated it as recovery path; v714 establishes it as the standard primary path when the topic is AUP-clean and main-context bandwidth is available. The three-path model treats path (a) sub-agent dispatch as an option to consider when path (c) is unavailable, not as a default. This is a substantive reversal of the pre-v710 default pattern.

**Lesson #10407 candidate sustained at obs#2 cumulative.** v713 first observation of dispatch-prompt-density failure; v714 first ship under the codified discipline (by sidestepping path (a) entirely). The candidate sustains the requirement for promotion to ESTABLISHED status pending one or more clean path (a) sub-agent dispatches under explicit dispatch-prompt-density audit. Until such an audit-anchor clean ship occurs, the candidate remains at lesson candidacy.

**Substrate-form-distinct dimensional analysis scales with axis-entry count.** The v713 analysis was 7 dimensions (one predecessor); the v714 analysis is 9 dimensions (two predecessors). The dimensional count growth suggests that as the axis accumulates substrate-form-distinct entries, the comparison-set expansion adds substrate-form-distinct dimensions rather than reducing them. Future axis entries will continue to add dimensions as comparison-set expansion reveals new substrate-form distinctions between the new entry and the accumulated cohort.

**Continuous-real-time operational substrate is substrate-form-distinct from research-substrate.** The v714 CONTINUOUS-REAL-TIME-SOLAR-WIND-SUBSTRATE-TO-NOAA-SWPC anchor distinguishes the ACE operational paradigm (continuous downlink to a national space-weather forecasting center, latency in minutes, supporting forecast products) from the v713 SOHO paradigm (research-oriented data flow, downlink scheduled around DSN antenna time, supporting situational characterization). Both are L1-station-keeping substrate-cumulative; the operational paradigm dimension is substrate-form-distinct. Future axis entries with continuous-real-time operational substrate (DSCOVR substrate-cumulative) will accumulate around this dimension.

**Substrate-vocab density tuning works as a forward control.** The v714 brief came in at 0.0434/word, deliberately tuned within the healthy band (≤0.05/word) by selecting concise instrument descriptions and abbreviating cumulative substrate enumerations. The path (c) main-context authorship then produced README and artifacts that inherited the brief's density discipline (per file). The continuum mapping (≤0.05 safe, 0.05-0.15 healthy, 0.15-0.20 risk, >0.20 collapse) continues to predict trip risk reliably across v710-v714 brief authorship.
