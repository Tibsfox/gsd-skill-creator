### THE SOUND: PURE SUSTAINED TRILL -- 99th Species-Side Acoustic Element

The snowy tree cricket's call is a **pure, sustained trill**. The file-and-scraper wing mechanism produces an unusually pure waveform -- **nearly sinusoidal** with minimal harmonic content. Rare among stridulating insects. A sustained, crystalline trill at approximately 2-4 kHz. Clean, clear, continuous. One of the **purest tones produced by any animal**. Multiple crickets create a **shimmering curtain of pure sound**.

**Why E=3:** Pure but gentle. Individual calls quieter than field crickets. Sustained audibility through still night air. Lower than field cricket chorus (87, E=4). Higher than stonefly (94, E=2). Crystalline clarity compensates for volume.

Contrasts:
- **Cricket chorus** (87, Gryllidae) -- dense rhythmic chirping, percussive, E=4. Same family, opposite acoustic architectures.
- **Katydid rasp** (88, Tettigoniidae) -- harsh, buzzy. Tree cricket is clean and pure. Both Orthoptera, different textures.
- **Cicada throb** (89, Cicadidae) -- loud, pulsing, E=5. Tree cricket is quiet, pure, E=3. Both sustained; different amplitudes.
- **Toad chorus purring** (97, Bufonidae) -- sustained and musical, harmonic-rich. Tree cricket is sustained and pure, minimal harmonics. Both sustained; different timbres and taxa.

### ECOLOGY: DOLBEAR'S LAW AND THE THERMOMETER CRICKET

In 1897, physicist **Amos Dolbear** published the relationship:

**T(degrees F) = 40 + (number of chirps in 13 seconds)**

This is verified biophysics: ectothermic metabolism directly proportional to temperature. Higher temperature = faster metabolism = faster wing-rubbing = faster chirp rate. The relationship is linear within normal range. The cricket IS a biological thermometer.

**Shrub habitat:** Low trees, herbaceous plants, hedgerows. Tree crickets call from elevated positions -- not from the ground like field crickets. Elevated position helps pure tone propagate through night air.

**Acoustic purity:** Nearly sinusoidal waveform. Minimal harmonic content. Consistent frequency (temperature-dependent but stable). Nearby tree crickets often synchronize trills, creating phase-coherent acoustic fields.
