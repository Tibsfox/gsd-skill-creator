## Structural firsts logged at v628

1. **REUSE-OF-CREWED-ORBITAL-SPACECRAFT-AS-NEW-DELIVERABLE NEW LOCKED** — STS-2 Columbia is the first reflight of a previously-flown crewed orbital spacecraft; OV-102 Columbia flew STS-1 (1981-04-12 to 1981-04-14) and was refurbished over 7 months at Palmdale CA (301 tiles replaced; 2,000+ tile re-bond operations; 3 SSME swapped; SRB thrust-vector-control nozzle inspected) before flying STS-2; establishes substrate-archetype for all subsequent Shuttle reuse flights (STS-3 through STS-135; 133 additional Columbia flights across 1981-2003 + 4 other orbiters); the 7-month refurbishment cadence compressed to ~4 months by STS-3; distinct from Gemini/Apollo where vehicles flew once and were not refurbished for reuse

2. **REMOTE-MANIPULATOR-SYSTEM-AS-NEW-DELIVERABLE NEW LOCKED** — STS-2 carried the first crewed operational test of the Canadarm (Remote Manipulator System; RMS); 15.2m × 38cm graphite-composite robotic arm designed by Spar Aerospace + National Research Council Canada; 6-DOF (six degrees of freedom: shoulder yaw + shoulder pitch + elbow pitch + wrist pitch + wrist yaw + wrist roll); maximum payload 29,484 kg in LEO; end effector with wire-snare capture mechanism; first international hardware component flown on a US crewed spacecraft; Engle + Truly performed in-orbit checkout of arm motions but did not achieve full deployment due to mission truncation; first operational RMS test establishes substrate-archetype for subsequent payload deployment/retrieval (STS-41-C SolarMax capture + repair; Hubble servicing missions; ISS assembly; LDEF retrieval)

3. **SRB-REUSE-AFTER-RECOVERY-AS-DELIVERABLE NEW LOCKED** — STS-2 flew the first refurbished Space Shuttle Solid Rocket Boosters recovered from STS-1's Atlantic Ocean splashdown; the STS-1 SRB casings (4-segment steel; ATK/Thiokol; parachute recovery at 28 nm downrange; Solid Rocket Booster recovery ships Liberty Star + Freedom Star retrieval); after STS-1 Atlantic recovery, the STS-1 SRB segments were inspected, remanufactured with new solid propellant, and reused for STS-2; establishes the SRB-reuse cycle that continued through STS-135 (over 200 SRB-segment reuses across the Shuttle program); distinct from expendable solid strap-on boosters in requiring sustained ocean recovery + refurbishment infrastructure
