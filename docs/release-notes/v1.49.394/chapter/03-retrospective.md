## What Could Be Better

- **The earlier README draft contained factual errors about Helms Alee's label, formation year, and one band member's name that the uplift corrected but that should not have shipped in the first place.** The original degree 253 README attributed Helms Alee to **Southern Lord Records** rather than to **Sargent House Records**, listed the formation year as 2007 rather than 2008, and gave Hozoji Margullis's name as "Hozoji Matheson-Margullis". The uplift corrected all three against the research.md canonical source, but the errors suggest that the original README drafting did not cross-check the research.md that the same commit shipped. Future degree documentation should treat the corresponding research.md as the source of truth for artist/species facts and should verify label, formation, membership, and discography data against it before shipping.
- **The tidal-current documentation would benefit from explicit coordination with the Puget Sound Partnership hydrographic dataset identifiers for subsequent tidal-related entries.** The degree 253 documentation cites **NOAA**, the **Puget Sound Partnership**, **Washington Sea Grant**, and the **U.S. Geological Survey** as authoritative sources for the tidal prism, velocity, and range data, but does not identify specific dataset identifiers or measurement stations. Future tidal-related SPS entries (tidal surge on cobble at 252 already exists, and additional tidal entries are likely) should cite specific NOAA tidal station identifiers (**Station 9447130** for Seattle, for example) so that the empirical grounding includes both the agency and the specific measurement channel from which the values derive.
- **The PNW-grey sludge adaptation was asserted as a regional-atmospheric adjustment but not characterized at the level of specific sonic differences from New Orleans sludge.** The documentation asserts that Pacific Northwest practitioners adapt the New Orleans sludge template toward a greyer, more psychedelic, less Southern-Gothic register, but does not characterize the specific sonic differences — frequency content, timbral quality, production aesthetic, lyrical-thematic register — that distinguish PNW-grey sludge from New-Orleans-oppressive sludge. Future sludge-adjacent documentation should characterize the adaptation at the level of specific measurable sonic variables so that the regional-genre register carries explicit documentation of what has changed and what has persisted across the geographic migration.
- **The Helms Alee discography was documented at the level of release year and label but not at the level of specific songs or riffs that exemplify the three-piece solution.** The documentation lists all six Helms Alee full-lengths from ***Night Terror*** (2008) through ***Noctiluca*** (2023) with release years and labels, but does not identify specific songs or riffs that exemplify the three-piece downtuning-and-co-equal-bass solution at its clearest expression. Future band-documentation should identify exemplary tracks and riffs so that future readers can hear the specific acoustic solution rather than inferring it from general description, because the acoustic-solution register carries more interpretive weight when anchored to specific moments in the catalog.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Research.md is the source of truth for artist and species facts in the paired-research-artifacts model.** The degree 253 release shipped with four research artifacts for Helms Alee and four for the Puget Sound Tidal Current in the same commit as the release-note README. The research.md files contain the canonical facts (label, formation year, member names, discography, scientific measurements) established through the research phase. The original README drafting did not fully cross-check research.md and shipped with three factual errors about Helms Alee. Future degree-documentation should treat the same-commit research.md as the source of truth and should verify all artist/species factual claims against it before finalizing the README. This lesson applies generally to any documentation pipeline that pairs research artifacts with summary documents.
2. **Genre-origin documentation requires specific founding-text and progenitor-band citation rather than abstract genre description.** The sludge genre entry at degree 253 is grounded in specific New Orleans progenitor bands (**Eyehategod**, **Crowbar**, **Acid Bath**) and the specific founding text (Eyehategod's *In the Name of Suffering*, 1992) rather than in abstract sludge-genre description. Future first-of-genre entries should anchor their genre openings in specific founding texts and progenitor bands rather than in generic genre description, because the specific citation enables subsequent same-genre entries to reference a shared framework and avoid re-justifying the genre's existence.
3. **Regional genre adaptations deserve explicit documentation of what changes and what persists across the geographic migration.** The PNW-grey adaptation of New Orleans sludge is documented as a regional-atmospheric variation at degree 253, but the specific sonic changes are not fully characterized. Future regional-adaptation documentation should characterize the adaptation at the level of specific measurable variables (frequency content, timbral quality, production aesthetic, lyrical-thematic register) so that the regional-genre register carries explicit documentation of change versus persistence. The general principle applies to any cultural practice that migrates across geographic or temporal boundaries.
4. **Compact-format-as-acoustic-solution documentation surfaces structural choices that generic band-size description cannot capture.** The Helms Alee three-piece format is documented at degree 253 as a specific acoustic solution (downtuning, co-equal bass, polyrhythmic drums, dual vocals) rather than as a generic band-size descriptor.
