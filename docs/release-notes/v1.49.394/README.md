**The Cordilleran Ice Sheet and the Glacial Origin of Puget Sound.** Puget Sound was carved by the **Cordilleran Ice Sheet** during the last glacial maximum approximately 17,000 years ago. The ice sheet, at its maximum extent 3,000 to 6,000 feet thick over what is now Seattle, scoured the bedrock into the elongated north-south basin that is Puget Sound. ## Part B: Puget Sound Tidal Current — The First Tidal Bulk-Flow Entry, Cordilleran Glacial Origin, and the 25-Cubic-Kilometer Prism

- **Category Baseline and First Tidal Bulk-Flow Entry Position:** **Puget Sound Tidal Current** is the **FIRST TIDAL BULK-FLOW ENTRY** in the engine's SPS history — the first documentation of deep hydraulic movement of the full Sound water mass as distinct from the tidal interface phenomenon documented one degree earlier at degree 252 (tidal surge on cobble beach). The distinction is physically meaningful. The surge is the interface phenomenon — water meeting shore, a localized acoustic event audible at 200 meters distance. The tidal current is the bulk phenomenon — the movement of the entire water body, a broad acoustic event felt more than heard, with most of its energy in the infrasound register below 20 Hz. Together the two entries document the two acoustic dimensions of Puget Sound's tidal system at consecutive degrees, and the transition from 252 (interface, E=4) to 253 (bulk flow, E=2) documents the engine's movement from the noisy shore interface to the quiet bulk flow.
- **The Cordilleran Ice Sheet and the Glacial Origin of the Basin:** **Puget Sound** was carved by the **Cordilleran Ice Sheet** during the last glacial maximum approximately 17,000 years ago. The ice sheet, at its maximum extent 3,000 to 6,000 feet thick over what is now **Seattle**, scoured the bedrock into the elongated north-south basin that is Puget Sound. The Sound's characteristic depth (average approximately 65 meters; maximum approximately 280 meters near **Point Jefferson**) and its irregular channel system with multiple sub-basins are direct results of differential glacial erosion. The ice carved deep channels where the subglacial drainage was concentrated and left shallow sills where the erosion was less intense. Every measurable feature of the tidal-current acoustic phenomenon documented at degree 253 derives from this glacial geomorphology: the channel geometry, the constriction points, the depth profile, the basin volume, and the tidal prism itself are all direct legacies of the Cordilleran Ice Sheet's last glacial maximum presence.
- **Channel Geometry — Main Basin, Hood Canal, South Sound, Case Inlet, Carr Inlet, Colvos Passage:** **Puget Sound** is not a simple bay. It is a complex system of interconnected channels, basins, and passes. The **Main Basin** is the largest and deepest. **Hood Canal** is a long narrow fjord extending to the southwest, with its own distinct tidal behavior due to its elongated geometry. The **South Sound** is the shallow, narrow extension south of the **Tacoma Narrows**, with delayed tidal phase relative to the Main Basin because of the Narrows sill's restriction on exchange. **Case Inlet** and **Carr Inlet** are additional sub-basins within the South Sound region. **Colvos Passage** runs along the west side of **Vashon Island**, providing alternate routing for tidal exchange. This geometric complexity is essential to the tidal-current acoustic phenomenon because each constriction point generates localized high-velocity flow and turbulence, and the phase relationships between sub-basins create additional acoustic activity at the sub-basin boundaries.
- **Tacoma Narrows — Primary Constriction and 6-10 Knot Peak Currents:** The **Tacoma Narrows** — spanned by the **Narrows Bridge** — is a geological sill. The bedrock rises to within about 50 meters of the surface, compared to depths of 200+ meters in the Main Basin. This sill restricts the tidal exchange between the Main Basin and the South Sound, creating the strong tidal currents at the Narrows (6-10 knots at maximum) and a phase lag in the tidal cycle between the Main Basin and the South Sound. The Narrows is the most hydrodynamically active location in Puget Sound on a per-cross-section basis. During maximum flood and ebb, the surface shows visible turbulence: boils, whirlpools, standing waves. Below the surface, the turbulence is intense; the 6-10 knot flow past the bedrock sill scours the channel floor, re-suspends sediment, and generates broadband low-frequency noise that is the Sound's loudest underwater acoustic feature at the Narrows location.
- **Deception Pass — 8-9 Knot Peak Currents and the Most Visible Surface Turbulence:** **Deception Pass** is the secondary northern connection between the Strait of Juan de Fuca and the northern reaches of Puget Sound (Skagit Bay and beyond). Peak currents reach 8-9 knots during maximum flood and ebb, generating the most visible surface turbulence anywhere in the Sound's channel system. **Deception Pass Bridge** (built 1935, one of Washington state's most iconic bridges) spans the channel at approximately 180 feet above mean sea level, and the view from the bridge during maximum current shows the full expression of the Cordilleran Ice Sheet's legacy: a narrow passage between Whidbey Island and Fidalgo Island through which the tidal prism surges with sufficient velocity to produce visible surface chaos.
- **Admiralty Inlet — Primary Northern Entrance Between Point Wilson and Whidbey Island:** **Admiralty Inlet** is the primary northern entrance to Puget Sound, connecting the Sound to the **Strait of Juan de Fuca** through the passage between **Point Wilson** (on the Olympic Peninsula side) and **Whidbey Island** (on the Puget Sound side). The majority of the 25-cubic-kilometer tidal prism enters and exits through Admiralty Inlet on each tidal cycle. Peak currents are 3-5 knots — lower than the Narrows or Deception Pass due to the greater cross-sectional area, but the volume passing through is the largest in the Sound's channel system. Admiralty Inlet is the Sound's breath: the twice-daily respiration through which the entire system exchanges water with the open ocean.
- **The Tidal Prism — 25 Cubic Kilometers Per Cycle:** The "tidal prism" is the volume of water that enters and exits a tidal basin with each tidal cycle. For **Puget Sound**, the tidal prism is approximately 25 cubic kilometers (25 billion cubic meters) per complete tidal cycle. This is an enormous volume: for reference, this is roughly 10 times the volume of **Lake Washington**. This entire volume enters through Admiralty Inlet and the passes at the northern end of the Sound on the flood tide, and exits on the ebb. The movement of this volume through the inlets is the physical reality behind the tidal current. The tidal prism does not include the Sound's resident water mass, which is much larger than 25 km³; the prism is only the tidal exchange volume — the water that moves in and out rather than the water that stays.
- **Mixed Semidiurnal Tide Pattern and the 10-14 Foot Range:** **Puget Sound** experiences a **mixed semidiurnal tide**: two high tides and two low tides per day, but the two highs and two lows are of unequal height. This pattern distinguishes Puget Sound's tides from the simple semidiurnal tides of the Atlantic seaboard (two roughly equal highs and two roughly equal lows per day) and from the diurnal tides of parts of the Gulf of Mexico (one high and one low per day). The typical tidal range at Seattle is 10-14 feet (3-4 meters). The highest tides of the year (king tides) can reach 15-16 feet above mean lower low water. The lowest low tides (minus tides) can expose extensive intertidal areas that are normally underwater. The specific combination of mixed-semidiurnal pattern and 10-14 foot range produces the characteristic two-peak daily tidal curve that organizes every acoustic feature documented at degree 253.
- **Acoustic Character — Turbulence-Generated Infrasound Below 20 Hz:** The tidal current's acoustic character is defined primarily by turbulence — the irregular, multi-scale swirling of water as it flows past irregular channel geometry, through underwater features, past submerged objects. This turbulence generates broadband low-frequency noise typically below 100 Hz and predominantly below 20 Hz (infrasound, technically inaudible to human hearing). The sound is felt more than heard: the subsonic pulse of the moving water mass. Tide rips — the rough water at current convergence zones — are an audible surface expression of the tidal current; where the infrasonic tidal current is below human hearing, the surface expression at a tide rip is audible as the hissing of small waves at the convergence line, the sound of disturbed water, and the occasional splash of a seabird (**rhinoceros auklets**, **common murres**, **pigeon guillemots**) diving into fish concentrated at the rip.
- **E=2 Placement — Present as Background, Dominant as Physical Process:** The tidal current registers at **E=2**. The level is accurate: present as background, detectable by sensitive instruments and perhaps by the human body in prolonged proximity to major tidal current flows, but not dominant in the way that rain (E=3) or waterfall (E=5-6) are. The tidal current is the Sound's quietest major physical process, despite moving the largest volume of any water in the system. E=2 reflects this paradox: the largest-volume process in Puget Sound's acoustic ecosystem is also its quietest, because the mass moves slowly enough that the acoustic output is mostly in the infrasound register rather than in the audible band. The E=2 placement also marks the lowest documented amplitude in the abiotic soundscape phase (degrees 245-253), with the phase amplitude having descended from E=7 peaks (waterfalls at 246-247) through E=4-5 intermediate values (rain, wind, tidal surge) to E=2 at the tidal current.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Research.md is the source of truth for artist and species facts in the paired-research-artifacts model.** The degree 253 release shipped with four research artifacts for Helms Alee and four for the Puget Sound Tidal Current in the same commit as the release-note README. The research.md files contain the canonical facts (label, formation year, member names, discography, scientific measurements) established through the research phase. The original README drafting did not fully cross-check research.md and shipped with three factual errors about Helms Alee. Future degree-documentation should treat the same-commit research.md as the source of truth and should verify all artist/species factual claims against it before finalizing the README. This lesson applies generally to any documentation pipeline that pairs research artifacts with summary documents.
2. **Genre-origin documentation requires specific founding-text and progenitor-band citation rather than abstract genre description.** The sludge genre entry at degree 253 is grounded in specific New Orleans progenitor bands (**Eyehategod**, **Crowbar**, **Acid Bath**) and the specific founding text (Eyehategod's *In the Name of Suffering*, 1992) rather than in abstract sludge-genre description. Future first-of-genre entries should anchor their genre openings in specific founding texts and progenitor bands rather than in generic genre description, because the specific citation enables subsequent same-genre entries to reference a shared framework and avoid re-justifying the genre's existence.
3. **Regional genre adaptations deserve explicit documentation of what changes and what persists across the geographic migration.** The PNW-grey adaptation of New Orleans sludge is documented as a regional-atmospheric variation at degree 253, but the specific sonic changes are not fully characterized. Future regional-adaptation documentation should characterize the adaptation at the level of specific measurable variables (frequency content, timbral quality, production aesthetic, lyrical-thematic register) so that the regional-genre register carries explicit documentation of change versus persistence. The general principle applies to any cultural practice that migrates across geographic or temporal boundaries.
4. **Compact-format-as-acoustic-solution documentation surfaces structural choices that generic band-size description cannot capture.** The Helms Alee three-piece format is documented at degree 253 as a specific acoustic solution (downtuning, co-equal bass, polyrhythmic drums, dual vocals) rather than as a generic band-size descriptor. Future compact-format documentation (duos, trios, four-pieces working without conventional full-band instrumentation) should surface the specific acoustic and compositional solutions each format deploys, because the solution register documents structural choices that single-size description does not capture.
5. **Curated-label-roster-as-ecosystem documentation surfaces artistic-positioning elements that single-band documentation cannot capture.** The Sargent House roster register at degree 253 places Helms Alee within the curated heavy-art-music ecosystem (**Chelsea Wolfe**, **Russian Circles**, **Deafheaven**, **Marriages**, **Emma Ruth Rundle**, **Torche**) rather than treating the band as an isolated artifact. Future label-documentation should surface the full roster context when the label's curation shapes artistic trajectories in measurable ways, because the roster register documents an artistic-positioning element that single-band documentation does not capture.
6. **Glacial-origin documentation for hydrographic phenomena grounds contemporary measurements in deep-time geomorphology.** The Cordilleran Ice Sheet legacy basin register at degree 253 grounds every measurable feature of the Puget Sound tidal current (channel geometry, constriction points, basin volume, tidal prism) in the 17,000-year-ago glacial-maximum framework. Future hydrographic-SPS documentation should anchor contemporary acoustic measurements in deep-time geological origins when the origin is interpretively relevant, because the geological-origin register documents a deep-time structural element that contemporary-measurement documentation does not capture.
7. **Tidal-prism-as-volumetric-register provides the structural unit for scaling acoustic-output claims.** The 25-cubic-kilometer tidal prism register at degree 253 provides the structural volumetric unit (approximately ten Lake Washingtons) against which every acoustic-output claim about the tidal current can be scaled. Future tidal-SPS documentation should establish the specific volumetric unit early in the entry so that subsequent acoustic and velocity claims can be scaled against a shared structural reference, because the volumetric-register is a first-class structural element rather than an auxiliary detail.
8. **Below-audible-threshold acoustic phenomena deserve first-class documentation rather than being treated as ambient background.** The Puget Sound Tidal Current at E=2 operates predominantly in the infrasound register below 20 Hz, which is technically inaudible to human ears. The degree 253 documentation treats this sub-audible phenomenon as a first-class acoustic entry rather than as ambient background, documenting it through turbulence-mechanism, frequency-register, and mass-in-motion frameworks. Future sub-audible-acoustic documentation should maintain this first-class treatment rather than collapsing sub-audible phenomena into ambient-noise descriptions, because the engine's acoustic vocabulary is strengthened when it can hold inaudible-but-physical phenomena at the same structural resolution as audible phenomena.
