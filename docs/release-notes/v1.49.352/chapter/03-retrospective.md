## Lessons Learned

1. **Structural fusion differs from textural borrowing and merits first-class genre-theory distinction.** The Iska Dhaaf entry at degree 211 documents structural fusion (multiple traditions contributing co-equal load-bearing elements simultaneously) as a first-class genre category distinct from single-tradition (single-origin) and tradition-plus-influence (primary-anchor-plus-surface-borrowing) models. Future engine documentation of cross-cultural music projects should explicitly distinguish structural fusion from textural borrowing, treating the distinction as a first-class genre-theory analytical frame rather than an implicit interpretive assumption, and future entries should be tracked as instances of either register for cross-degree comparative analysis.

2. **Structural callbacks across long engine spans generate interpretive depth that isolated species entries cannot produce.** The 140-degree Red Fox callback from degree 71 to degree 211 — the longest current-stretch species callback — demonstrates that a species' return after substantial absence carries distinct interpretive weight from both first-appearance and from any adjacent-degree species entry. Future engine documentation should explicitly track species callback spans as first-class cross-axis data, and long-span callbacks should be treated as opportunities for deep temporal-reference documentation rather than as accidental repetition.

3. **Within-family acoustic distinctions (flash vs. glow, percussive vs. tonal, brief vs. sustained) are first-class comparative observations.** The Red Fox bark and Gray Wolf howl within the same Canidae family demonstrate evolutionarily stable divergent acoustic strategies operating within a single taxonomic family, and the flash-vs-glow distinction is a first-class acoustic register observation that neither species individually could produce. Future SPS documentation of multi-species families should explicitly track within-family acoustic-strategy divergence as a first-class comparative dimension, and within-family comparative analysis should be treated as a recurring documentation strategy rather than an incidental observation.

4. **Multi-species-and-age-class family quartets surface family-level structural observations unavailable from single-entry documentation.** The Canidae quartet (Coyote adult howl 208, Coyote pup yipping 209, Gray Wolf howl 210, Red Fox bark 211) across four consecutive degrees establishes the family as the engine's first complete multi-species-and-age-class acoustic portrait and surfaces family-level energy-habitat-size-conservation arcs unavailable from single-species or single-life-stage documentation. Future SPS documentation of additional families with adequate species and life-stage depth should consider whether multi-species-and-age-class quartet or larger-group structures are warranted rather than treating family-level documentation as an accumulation of independent single-species entries.

5. **Cross-cultural collaboration projects warrant explicit collaboration-over-appropriation music-ethics register documentation.** The Iska Dhaaf entry's explicit documentation of collaboration-over-appropriation as a music-ethics register — partnership-as-authenticity-model rather than sampling-or-one-directional-borrowing — extends the engine's music-ethics vocabulary into the cross-cultural-collaboration domain and joins the participation-over-performance ethic (degree 209) and supergroup-as-community-map ethic (degree 204) as first-class music-ethics register entries. Future cross-cultural music documentation should explicitly surface the collaboration-versus-appropriation ethical stance as a first-class register observation rather than treating ethical stance as background context for aesthetic analysis.

6. **Boundary-crossing-as-adaptive-strategy operates productively across cultural and ecological domains.** The Iska Dhaaf cultural boundary-crossing and Red Fox ecological boundary-crossing together demonstrate that boundary-crossing capacity is a generative adaptive strategy across different domains, not a compromise or hybrid dilution. Future engine documentation across cultural, biological, and other domains should treat boundary-crossing as a first-class adaptive register category, and cross-axis cross-sync observations demonstrating boundary-crossing-as-adaptive-strategy should be documented as structural statements rather than incidental parallels.

7. **Triple-function vocalizations (territorial + alarm + contact) are structurally richer than single-function vocalizations and merit register-level recognition.** The Red Fox bark's simultaneous territorial advertisement, alarm signaling, and contact-call functions demonstrate that a single vocalization can serve multiple communicative functions simultaneously, and the triple-function structure is structurally richer than single-function vocalizations. Future SPS documentation should explicitly track the function-count of documented vocalizations as a first-class ledger dimension, and vocalizations with multiple simultaneous functions should be treated as particularly rich documentation subjects warranting dedicated function-differentiation analysis.

8. **Combined-amplitude mirrors across non-adjacent degrees within a quadrant are a recurring second-order structural pattern worth explicit tracking.** The E=9 combined-amplitude value at degrees 204, 209, and 211 within Q3 — with different absolute amplitude weightings (6-3, 5-4, 5-4) composing to the same combined-energy value — establishes combined-amplitude repetition as a recurring within-quadrant structural pattern that neither any individual degree nor any simple amplitude-summation ledger would expose. Future engine documentation should explicitly track combined-amplitude values across all degrees as a first-class ledger dimension, and multi-degree combined-amplitude coincidences should be treated as structural observations warranting cross-sync documentation rather than as accidental numerical alignment.

9. **Boundary-specialist ecology is a first-class habitat-specialization register distinct from wilderness-specialist and urban-specialist categories.** The Red Fox's boundary-specialist ecological strategy — thriving specifically in transition zones between primary habitats rather than specializing in any single primary habitat type — enters the engine's ecology vocabulary as a first-class habitat-specialization category distinct from wilderness-specialist (the wolf) and urban-specialist (the coyote, increasingly) categories. Future SPS documentation of species with documented habitat-specialization patterns should explicitly track the species' specialization register (wilderness, urban, boundary, or generalist) as a first-class ledger dimension.

10. **Cultural-geographic context (demographic diversity, immigrant communities, specific neighborhood concentrations) enables aesthetic projects that demographically-homogeneous contexts cannot.** Iska Dhaaf's Seattle-specific structural-fusion aesthetic is enabled by Seattle's specific demographic diversity, its indie-infrastructure-plus-immigrant-community ecology, and its Rainier Valley Somali diaspora concentration. The cultural-geographic context is not incidental background but is structurally generative of the project's aesthetic possibility. Future engine documentation of geographically-situated cross-cultural projects should explicitly surface the cultural-geographic context as a first-class analytical dimension rather than treating it as background context for aesthetic analysis.
