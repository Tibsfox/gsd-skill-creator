## Summary

**Iska Dhaaf** arrives at E=5 -- the collaboration between **Mos Def** bassist Nathan Quiroga and Shabazz Palaces' Ishmael Butler, a project that fuses post-punk, Somali-influenced melodies, and Butler's abstract lyricism into something that has no genre peers. The name "Iska Dhaaf" comes from Somali, loosely translating to "let it go" or "don't worry about it" -- a fitting ethos for music that refuses categorization. Their self-titled debut (2014) on Sub Pop is a record that moves between angular guitar, programmed beats, and vocal textures that owe as much to East African music as to Seattle's experimental scene. At E=5, Iska Dhaaf represents the engine's cosmopolitan thread: Seattle as a city whose music is shaped by its immigrant communities, not just its rock lineage. On the species side, the **Red Fox bark** registers at E=4 -- and with this entry, the **Canidae quartet closes**. The Red Fox (*Vulpes vulpes*) is the most widely distributed wild carnivore on Earth, and its bark is one of the most distinctive sounds in Pacific Northwest woodlands: a sharp, hoarse, single-syllable cry that cuts through darkness with startling clarity. The Red Fox bark at degree 211 creates a **140-degree callback** to degree 71 -- the Red Fox's first appearance in the engine. This is the longest callback span in the series, connecting early engine foundations to the deep Quadrant III build with a single thread of vulpine sound.
