## Mount Rainier's glacier system


- Rainier's 26 named glaciers are the largest single-peak glacier system in the contiguous United States; the Emmons Glacier, covering 4.3 square miles, is the largest glacier by area; the Carbon Glacier, descending to the lowest elevation of any glacier in the lower 48 states, is one of the most accessible; glacial mass loss on Rainier has been accelerating since the 1980s, and calving events -- once confined to specific steep sections of the glacier faces -- now occur across a broader range of the system; the acoustic record of this mass loss is part of the climate record of the Pacific Northwest, and the engine documents it here as part of the geophysics arc
