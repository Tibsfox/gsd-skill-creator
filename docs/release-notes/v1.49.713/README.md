## Mission Architecture: SOHO

SOHO is a NASA-ESA joint heliophysics mission that launched on 1995-12-02 at 08:08:01 UTC from Cape Canaveral Air Force Station Launch Complex 36B on an Atlas IIAS configuration (Atlas-Centaur launch vehicle). The spacecraft prime contractor was Matra Marconi Space France (later Astrium / Airbus Defence and Space) under ESA-led industrial prime substrate-anchor. Spacecraft + science operations are conducted at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center Experimenters' Operations Facility (EOF), Greenbelt, Maryland.

Total spacecraft mass at launch was ~1,850 kg, including ~250 kg of monopropellant hydrazine for trans-L1 transit, L1 halo orbit insertion, and multi-decade station-keeping. Two deployable solar panel wings provide ~1,500 W at 1 AU. Communications are routed via S-band through the NASA Deep Space Network (DSN) Goldstone + Madrid + Canberra ground stations. The spacecraft is three-axis stabilized.

The Atlas IIAS launch vehicle performed a two-stage ascent. The Atlas booster, augmented by four solid-rocket strap-on boosters, provided first-stage thrust. The Centaur cryogenic upper stage performed a two-burn ascent — first burn to circular parking orbit, second burn to trans-L1 injection delivering ~3.2 km/s delta-v. Trans-L1 cruise duration was ~102 days. L1 halo orbit insertion completed 1996-03-14.

## Mission Profile

SOHO's mission profile unfolds across four canonical phases. The launch phase began 1995-12-02 08:08:01 UTC with Atlas IIAS ascent from CCAFS LC-36B. The Atlas booster + four solid-rocket strap-on boosters provided first-stage thrust; the Centaur cryogenic upper stage performed a two-burn ascent delivering ~3.2 km/s of trans-L1 injection delta-v. The trans-L1 cruise phase lasted ~102 days, with the spacecraft drifting toward the Sun-Earth L1 Lagrange point. L1 halo orbit insertion completed 1996-03-14, placing SOHO in a ~666,000 × 206,000 km halo geometry with ~6-month orbital period.

The nominal mission phase was designed for two years — sufficient to map a full solar cycle minimum and characterize the major heliophysics observable channels. SOHO completed nominal mission objectives during 1996-1998. Mission extensions have been approved continuously since, and operational lifetime has now reached ~30 years as of 2026-05-21. Eleven of the twelve original instruments produce ongoing science data; GOLF operates with reduced channel coverage but continues. Mission extensions are approved through 2025+ with potential further extensions.

Across that operational lifetime, SOHO has observed three solar cycles — Cycle 23 (~1996-2008), Cycle 24 (~2008-2019), and Cycle 25 (~2019-present, with the activity peak occurring in the 2025-2026 timeframe substrate). The mission profile substrate sustained across multiple solar cycles is the substrate-anchor for **LONG-DURATION-OPERATIONAL-SUBSTRATE-ANCHOR obs#1 NEW LOCKED at v713**.

Two substrate-anchor operational-adaptation episodes occurred during the mission. In 1998-06, a routine attitude-control sequence placed SOHO into an unintended attitude state; the ESA-NASA joint operations team conducted a ground-recovery sequence using the Arecibo radio telescope (Puerto Rico) for radar transmission and the Goldstone Deep Space Network station (California) for radar reception, characterizing SOHO's spin state and thermal condition over several months. Operations were fully restored 1998-09-16. This is the **GROUND-RECOVERY-OPERATIONAL-CONTINUITY-SUBSTRATE-ANCHOR obs#1 NEW LOCKED at v713**.

Subsequent to the 1998 sequence, two of three reaction-wheel gyroscopes reached end-of-design-life by 1998-12. The joint operations team developed and qualified gyroless attitude-control software during 1999, using star trackers and reaction wheels alone for attitude determination. SOHO became the first spacecraft to operate at L1 without gyroscope attitude reference. Gyroless operations continue through 2026, extending operational lifetime by ~25+ years. This is the **GYROLESS-OPERATIONS-ADAPTATION-SUBSTRATE-ANCHOR obs#1 NEW LOCKED at v713**.

**Long-duration-operational substrate magnitude is a substrate-form-distinct dimension.** SOHO's ~30-year-operational lifetime versus v712 Aditya-L1's ~2-year-operational substrate is sufficient to count as a distinct subform dimension within the SOLAR-OBSERVATORY axis.
