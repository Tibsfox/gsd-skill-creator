### SPS — Northern Spotted Owl: Pass 2 Refinement (3,844 words)

1. **Nocturnal Acoustic Ecology** — The owl occupies the nighttime temporal niche. Post-sunset acoustic transformation: 20-25 dB SPL ambient floor. Temperature inversions as acoustic waveguides (SOFAR channel analogy). The facial disk as biological parabolic antenna (~10 dB gain). Asymmetric ear placement for 1-2 degree 3D sound localization in total darkness.

2. **The Barred Owl Invasion** — Strix varia's westward range expansion (~100 km/decade). Acoustic frequency overlap (both species in the 300-700 Hz range). An ecological displacement with no clean solution.

3. **Survey Protocol Acoustics** — The USFWS callback protocol (Forsman 1983): play the four-note hoot, wait for response. 60-80% per-visit detection probability. Bayesian occupancy modeling (MacKenzie et al. 2002). The four-note hoot's journey from a tree branch to the Federal Register — the most consequential acoustic monitoring program in conservation history.

4. **Pioneer 3 Resonance** — Institutional framework as detection filter. The Army (ABMA/JPL) found the second Van Allen belt using different instruments than the Air Force would have deployed. Both cases show how what you detect depends on how you're organized to listen. Usnea and owl as parallel indicator species — their presence confirms, their absence condemns.
