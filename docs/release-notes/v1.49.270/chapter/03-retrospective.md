## What Could Be Better

- **Richards and Mazzone are documented at summary level but await their own degrees for full treatment.** The research mentions Richards' Morning Show personal/emotional dimension and Mazzone's Wo' Pop world-music dimension, but the substantive documentation of each host is deferred to their own degrees (131 and 132 respectively). This is correct discipline — each host deserves a full degree of research rather than a shared-degree summary — but the trio-opener position at degree 130 places Waters in explicit relation to two figures whose full documentation has not yet arrived.
- **The Willamette Valley agricultural-field ecosystem is documented at the wintering-concentration level but not at the full year-round economy level.** Tens of thousands of Cackling Geese winter in Willamette Valley agricultural fields on waste grain, grass, and clover. The documentation covers the wintering side but does not document the agricultural year (planting, harvest, post-harvest waste grain availability timing, farmer relations with goose presence). A fuller agricultural-ecosystem documentation is deferred.
- **The KCMU pre-1996 history is compressed.** KCMU began in 1972, and Waters joined in 1996 — 24 years into the station's history. Her tenure covers the station's final quarter-century of KCMU existence and all of its KEXP existence to date. The pre-1996 KCMU history is mentioned at summary level (launched Sub Pop through Poneman's and Pavitt's DJ work, introduced hip-hop through Rodriguez) but not documented in full. A dedicated KCMU history artifact would provide deeper context.
- **The Cackling Goose's acoustic behavior in mixed Canada-Cackling flocks is qualitative rather than quantitative.** The research describes the bimodal acoustic texture of mixed flocks (Canada honk wall with Cackling chattering layer overlaid) qualitatively but does not include quantitative data on call-rate ratios, flock size compositions, or acoustic dominance metrics. Field-recording analysis from Willamette Valley or Skagit Valley wintering grounds would provide quantitative grounding but was not incorporated into this degree's research scope.
- **The molecular genetic studies that grounded the 2004 split are named but not walked through.** The research notes that "molecular genetic studies in the late 1990s and early 2000s revealed that the small forms and the large forms represent distinct evolutionary lineages," but does not name specific studies or walk through the methodology. A fuller genetic-evidence documentation (which mitochondrial DNA markers, which sampling protocols, which phylogenetic tree topologies) would strengthen the split documentation but was outside the present scope.
- **The Aleutian fox-eradication recovery is mentioned but not fully documented.** The Aleutian Cackling Goose recovery through fox eradication on breeding islands is cited in relation to the Trumpeter Swan recovery at degree 128, but the specific recovery program details (which agency, which years, which islands, which population metrics before and after) are deferred. - **The KEXP in-studio session format is mentioned but the specific sessions featuring PNW artists are not catalogued.** KEXP's in-studio session platform (distributed via YouTube and KEXP Presents) features international and local artists in purpose-built-studio performances, and these sessions are central to the station's streaming-era brand. A catalog of in-studio sessions featuring Seattle/PNW artists — Fleet Foxes, Death Cab for Cutie, The Cave Singers, Mount Eerie, and many others — would be a dedicated artifact, but it exceeds degree-130's single-host scope.
- **The Capitol Hill versus Lower Queen Anne / Seattle Center geographic distinction is acknowledged but not resolved.** KEXP's purpose-built studio is technically at Seattle Center (Lower Queen Anne), but the station's cultural gravity is Capitol Hill. The documentation notes this tension but does not fully resolve it. Future KEXP-related documentation could develop this distinction further — institutional cultural identity does not always align with physical geography, and the mismatch is itself structurally interesting.

Degree-to-degree-adjacent contrast (129 O'Day to 130 Waters) is a structural event in the engine — commercial/independent dialectic concretely paired — and a fuller comparative treatment would strengthen the radio-era documentation.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Institutional memory is a structural asset that specific individuals embody.** Cheryl Waters' continuous presence across the 2001 KCMU-to-KEXP transition is the structural argument for why individual-human-continuity matters at institutions that undergo major transformations. Without a continuous on-air voice, the station would have risked losing its college-radio roots during the transition to professional independent public radio. Future entries documenting institutional transformations should identify the continuity-embodying individuals and treat their longevity as a first-class structural dimension rather than as biographical detail.
2. **Taxonomic splits are historical events with specific calendar dates and publishing authorities.** The **2004 AOU split** of *Branta canadensis* into *Branta canadensis* (Canada Goose) and *Branta hutchinsii* (Cackling Goose) is documented to a specific year, a specific publishing authority (Banks et al. in the 45th supplement to the AOU Check-list), and a specific genetic evidence base (molecular genetic studies of the late 1990s and early 2000s). Future taxonomic-split documentation should anchor to calendar dates, authority publications, and evidence bases rather than treating splits as ahistorical facts.
3. **Comparison pairs document same-category differentiation and reward adjacent-degree placement.** The Canada/Cackling Goose comparison pair (129-130) is the fifth comparison pair in SPS, and adjacent-degree placement allows direct acoustic and morphological contrast. Future comparison pairs should continue this adjacent-degree placement discipline, and the pattern of comparison pairs should be tracked as its own structural dimension alongside streak records and individual entries.
4. **Triads within longer arcs generate anticipatory structural weight.** The KEXP host trio (130-132: Waters, Richards, Mazzone) opens at degree 130 within the longer five-degree radio-figure arc (126-130). The trio structure creates anticipatory weight — future degrees have preordained structural roles — and the opener-middle-closer pattern becomes legible when a trio is recognized rather than just a sequence of related entries. Future sub-arc openings should be named as triads or tetrads when the structure supports it.
5. **Purely migratory species differ structurally from partially resident species even within the same genus.** The Cackling Goose (purely migratory) and Canada Goose (partially resident in PNW) share a genus but differ in migratory strategy, and the difference has ecological and observational consequences. Future genus-level comparisons should track migratory strategy as a first-class structural dimension alongside body size, habitat, and call.
6. **Subspecies-level documentation matters when the species contains distinct regional populations.** The Cackling Goose is not a single population but four subspecies (*minima*, *leucopareia*, *taverneri*, *hutchinsii*) with distinct breeding and wintering ranges. The aggregate species-level description obscures internal structure. Future polytypic species should be documented at subspecies level when the subspecies occupy distinct biogeographic ranges, and the within-species complexity should be treated as a structural dimension.
7. **Arctic-tundra breeders depend on compressed breeding-season ecology.** The Cackling Goose's Arctic tundra breeding requires quick pair formation, rapid nest building, tight incubation schedules, and compressed gosling rearing within the brief Arctic summer. This compressed ecology is itself a structural principle — it shapes the species' migration timing, population recovery rates, and vulnerability to climate change. Future Arctic-breeding entries should document the compressed-breeding-season ecology as a standard component.
8. **Human-curator-versus-algorithm framing is structurally significant for the radio/curation entries.** Waters at KEXP represents the **human curator** in the age of algorithmic recommendation, and the warm-and-knowledgeable presentation style is an implicit argument against algorithmic playlists. Future radio/curation entries (Richards 131, Mazzone 132, potential future public-radio hosts elsewhere) should document the human-curator-versus-algorithm dimension as a recurring structural theme.
9. **Infrastructure documentation should precede operator documentation.** The four-degree KEXP institutional arc (126-129: Rodriguez, Poneman, Pavitt, O'Day) preceded the three-degree operator trio (130-132: Waters, Richards, Mazzone). This ordering is structurally correct — operators require infrastructure context to be understood, and documenting voices without station-context would leave them ungrounded. Future institution-documentation sequences should follow this infrastructure-first, operators-second discipline.
10. **Descending post-climax energy arcs are clean patterns worth tracking.** After the Trumpeter Swan E=6 climax at degree 128, the energy arc descends cleanly: E=6 (128) → E=5 (129) → E=4 (130). The clean descent is a structural pattern distinct from random energy variation, and future post-climax arcs should be tracked as they emerge. The Anatidae streak's internal energy oscillation is rich structural substrate, and clean descents and ascents within the streak are worth naming as their own sub-events.
