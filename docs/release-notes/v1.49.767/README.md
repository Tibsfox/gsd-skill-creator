**Five-instrument suite per spacecraft.** Each Van Allen Probes spacecraft carries one full copy of all five science instrument suites — identical-payload twin-spacecraft substrate. ECT (Energetic Particle, Composition, and Thermal Plasma Suite) covers thermal-plasma to ultra-relativistic-electron energy ranges via HOPE + MagEIS + REPT three-instrument suite. EMFISIS (Electric and Magnetic Field Instrument Suite and Integrated Science) provides DC fluxgate magnetometer + waveform receiver (10 Hz – 12 kHz) + high-frequency receiver (10 kHz – 12 MHz). EFW (Electric Field and Waves Instrument) measures three-axis electric-field via double-probe + spin-plane booms + spin-axis stacer boom. RBSPICE (Radiation Belt Storm Probes Ion Composition Experiment) provides ion composition mass spectrometer 20 keV – 1 MeV per charge for H+ / He+ / O+ + electrons 25 keV – 1 MeV. RPS (Relativistic Proton Spectrometer) measures protons 50 MeV – 2 GeV (NRO Office of Space Launch funded — only non-NASA-funded instrument in suite). **Transient third radiation belt discovery substrate.** Within four weeks of EMFISIS + ECT instrument commissioning (September 2012), the Van Allen Probes observed a previously-unknown transient third radiation belt structure between the canonical inner and outer Van Allen belts; the transient belt persisted approximately four weeks before dissipating. Published Baker et al. 2013 *Science* 340:186-190 ("A Long-Lived Relativistic Electron Storage Ring Embedded in Earth's Outer Van Allen Belt"). Established that the inner-magnetosphere radiation environment can host previously-unsuspected long-lived storage structures and validated the twin-spacecraft simultaneous-measurement architecture.

**Electron acceleration mechanism substrate.** RBSP wave-particle measurements directly resolved the multi-decade open question of relativistic-electron acceleration mechanism in the outer radiation belt: local acceleration via wave-particle interactions (especially chorus-wave resonance) versus inward radial diffusion from a distant source. Twin-spacecraft simultaneous-two-point + EMFISIS wave + ECT electron + EFW electric-field measurements established that local chorus-wave acceleration dominates relativistic-electron energization during geomagnetic activity. **Plasma-wave radiation-belt dynamics substrate.** RBSP comprehensively characterized inner-magnetosphere plasma-wave populations — chorus waves (driving local electron acceleration), plasmaspheric hiss (driving slow electron loss), electromagnetic ion cyclotron (EMIC) waves (driving rapid relativistic-electron precipitation loss), ultra-low-frequency (ULF) waves (driving radial transport), and electrostatic structures.
