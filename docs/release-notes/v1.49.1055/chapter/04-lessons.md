# Lessons — v1.49.1055

14 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **A rotation replaces ALL of the predecessor's topical vocabulary.**
   Viking shares almost no subject vocabulary with the X-ray-astronomy predecessor; the rewrite removed the entire X-ray axis vocabulary (telescopes, detectors, energy bands, the focusing-imaging branch) and replaced it with Mars-exploration vocabulary (Mars, the red planet, regolith, Chryse/Utopia Planitia, the soft landing, the aeroshell/parachute/terminal-descent engines, in situ, astrobiology, habitability), keeping only neutral/shared spaceflight vocabulary and a single deliberate nav-prev lineage note to Suzaku.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12227_

2. **The four "first" traps must be stated in every claim of primacy.**
   The first SOFT landing on Mars was Mars 3 (1971); the first TRANSMISSION from the surface was Mars 3; the first Mars ORBITER was Mariner 9 (1971); Mars 2/3 were ALSO orbiter+lander systems. Viking owns the first FULLY SUCCESSFUL, sustained versions of the landing, the clear surface images, and the orbiter+lander system — every primacy claim carries the qualifier.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12228_

3. **The life-search result is scoped, not summarized.**
   The GCMS detected no organics at its 1976 threshold (a null later explained by perchlorate-driven pyrolysis destruction; Phoenix 2008; Curiosity/SAM indigenous organics) — never "no organics on Mars." The biology result is chemically active but biologically inconclusive — never "found life," never "ruled out life," never a "failure." It is a founding astrobiology result and a scientific surprise.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12229_

4. **NASA / U.S.-mission framing is an accuracy requirement here, not a qualifier.**
   Viking was a wholly NASA / United States program (NASA Langley + JPL + Martin Marietta) with no foreign partner — the foreign-partner caveats belonged to the X-ray axis the catalog rotates away from, and attaching one to Viking would be an error.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12230_

5. **Lander power and landing system are precise facts, not generic ones.**
   The landers were RTG-powered (two SNAP-19 plutonium-238 generators each), the orbiters solar-powered, and the landing was propulsive on three legs (aeroshell → parachute → terminal-descent engines) — NOT airbags, which belong to Pathfinder and the Mars Exploration Rovers.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12231_

6. **The paired lichen is scoped honestly to the clade.**
   The lichens actually flown in orbital exposure (EXPOSE-E/R2) and Mars-simulation chambers (BIOMEX) were *Xanthoria, Rhizocarpon, Circinaria, Buellia,* and *Pleopsidium* — not *Umbilicaria*. Rock Tripe (*Umbilicaria*) is a same-form Pacific Northwest bare-rock extremophile cited alongside the flown lichens as context about the clade, never claimed to have flown or been Mars-chamber-tested.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12232_

7. **The orbiter and lander are two halves of one architecture.**
   Viking is not "an orbiter" or "a lander" but a fully integrated orbiter+lander system: the orbiter certified the landing site, relayed lander data, and mapped the planet (52,663 images, ~97% of Mars, Phobos/Deimos), while the lander read the surface, soil, atmosphere, and possible biology in situ. The architecture is the unit of analysis.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12233_

8. **A rotation references its predecessor exactly once, deliberately.**
   The only correct predecessor reference is a single nav-prev lineage note to v1.237 Suzaku (the X-ray axis the catalog rotates away from), plus a lineage reference to Mariner 4 (1.36, the first Mars flyby). Everything else from the X-ray axis is a leak.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12234_

9. **Memorial-station naming is a distinct-pair fact.**
   The Viking 1 lander is the Thomas A. Mutch Memorial Station; the Viking 2 lander is the Gerald A. Soffen Memorial Station. The two are not interchangeable.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12235_

10. **Clone source is Suzaku (a JAXA/ISAS X-ray observatory, five XRT + XIS/HXD/XRS, M-V, 2005); Viking is a NASA Mars landing program (two orbiter–lander spacecraft, Titan IIIE–Centaur, 1975–1976).**
   The most structural replacement in the rewrite is removing ALL "Suzaku / ASTRO-EII," "the Vermilion Bird of the South," "JAXA / ISAS," "M-V-6 / Uchinoura," "XIS / HXD / XRS / XRT," "thin-foil X-Ray Telescopes," "175 foil shells," "micro-calorimeter," "60 mK," "lowest-background broadband sensitivity," "ICM to the virial radius," "X-ray CCDs," "Wolter Type-I," "grazing incidence," "keV bands," "Kokubun/Kelley/George 2007–2009," and "the Western Tanager + Western Red Columbine pairing," and replacing with Viking's two orbiter–lander spacecraft, NASA Langley / JPL / Martin Marietta, the Titan IIIE–Centaur launch, the Chryse/Utopia landings, the RTG-powered propulsive landing, the lander surface laboratory and the orbiter mapper/relay, the first clear surface images, the first in-situ search…

A future revision could render a real Viking surface panorama or a Viking-orbiter global mosaic (publicly available from the Planetary Data System) for a higher-fidelity view.
