# Lessons — v1.49.1015

11 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **A continuous survey turns a set of pointings into a film of the whole sky.**
   By sweeping the whole sky every few hours rather than stepping through pointed observations, Fermi built a continuous record of the high-energy sky, showing that a continuous sweep watches the variable sky in a way a sequence of pointings cannot.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11838_

2. **Sensitivity is bought with exposure.**
   By accumulating a deep continuous exposure, Fermi's faint sources emerged as their photon counts rose above the background, showing that the significance of a faint detection grows as the square root of the exposure and that the deepest map is the longest-observed one.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11839_

3. **A wide field gathers the faint signal across the whole reach.**
   By taking in a large fraction of the sky at once with its wide-field telescope, Fermi gathered the faint high-energy light across the whole sphere, showing that a wide field is what lets a survey cover and deepen the whole sky at once.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11840_

4. **Searching one channel for a regular signal reveals what other channels miss.**
   By searching the high-energy data for the regular pulse, Fermi found gamma-ray pulsars with no radio beam, showing that a population can be hidden until the right channel is searched for the right signal.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11841_

5. **An axis sustains at a substrate-form-distinct third observation.**
   By sweeping and mapping the whole sky continuously rather than catching individual bursts, Fermi sustained the gamma-ray-astronomy axis at obs#3, showing that an INTRA-AXIS continuation extends a domain with a distinct form rather than repeating the prior two.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11842_

6. **A deep whole-sky map reveals large-scale structure a narrow view would miss.**
   By mapping the whole sky deeply, Fermi revealed the two giant lobes above and below the galactic center, showing that faint large-scale structure emerges only when the whole sphere is seen deeply at once.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11843_

7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across an INTRA-AXIS continuation.**
   Because Fermi reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.205 Swift template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content to the continuous survey while keeping the warm palette, making the axis-sustaining continuation a clean build.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11844_

8. **The shader renders procedural structure rather than archived data.**
   The Fermi gamma-ray-all-sky-survey shader uses analytic geometry and procedural noise rather than loading actual Fermi-LAT source maps from the Fermi Science Support Center. A future revision could load encoded Fermi data for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to the real all-sky map.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11845_

9. **The two-instrument diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the observatory, its two instruments, and the low Earth orbit.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11846_

10. **The gamma-ray-observatory sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.**
   The sidebar lists representative lineage and observatory elements (Fermi, Swift, Compton, Chandra) but does not enumerate the full set of Fermi results per instrument; a future revision could expand the table with per-instrument energy coverage.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11847_

11. **The survey statistics are described rather than computed.**
   The photon-counting statistics and the power-law spectrum are described but not run against the archived catalog; a future ship could query a real Fermi-LAT source spectrum and fit the photon index directly.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11848_
