## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Phasianidae (5th entry -- Wild Turkey 153, Blue Grouse 154, Ruffed Grouse 155, Sooty Grouse 156, Spruce Grouse 157)
- **Order:** Galliformes (continuing)
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The Spruce Grouse (*Falcipennis canadensis*) is a boreal forest specialist found across northern North America, from Alaska through Canada and into the northern tier of the United States. In the Pacific Northwest, Spruce Grouse inhabit the high-elevation spruce and fir forests of the Cascades and northern ranges. The **wing claps and clucking** represent a dual acoustic signature -- males produce sharp wing claps during flutter-flight displays (short vertical flights with rapid wing beats that create audible percussive pops), combined with low clucking vocalizations during ground courtship. The wing clap is mechanical (5th mechanical sound in the engine), while the clucking is vocal -- the Spruce Grouse is the first Phasianidae to feature both mechanical and vocal elements in its primary display. E=4 reflects the moderate carrying power -- the wing claps are loud but brief, the clucking low and local. The Spruce Grouse is famously tame, often allowing humans to approach within a few feet without flushing. This tameness earned it the nickname "fool hen" among early settlers and hunters. The disposition is not foolishness but an evolved response to a predator landscape dominated by raptors -- freezing and relying on cryptic plumage is the correct strategy against hawks and owls, even if it fails against humans. As the **5th Phasianidae**, the Spruce Grouse extends this family run to unprecedented length -- five consecutive degrees, the longest single-family stretch in the engine. The Phasianidae sequence has now demonstrated the full range of gamebird acoustics: vocal gobble (Wild Turkey), subsonic pneumatic boom (Blue Grouse), mechanical wing-drum (Ruffed Grouse), ventriloquial muffled boom (Sooty Grouse), and now the dual wing clap + cluck display (Spruce Grouse).
- **Butler arc summary:** Ishmael Butler first appeared at degree 144 as half of **Digable Planets** -- the jazz-rap trio that won a Grammy in 1994 for "Rebirth of Slick (Cool Like Dat)." That entry represented Butler's first life in hip-hop: jazzy, smooth, 1990s New York. Shabazz Palaces represents Butler's second life: Afrofuturist, abstract, Seattle, 2010s. The 13-degree arc from 144 to 157 traces one of the most remarkable reinventions in hip-hop history. After Digable Planets dissolved, Butler relocated to Seattle, immersed himself in the Central District's creative community, and emerged with Shabazz Palaces -- a project that sounds nothing like his earlier work. *Black Up* (2011, Sub Pop) was a landmark in experimental hip-hop. Sub Pop's involvement was a statement: the label that defined grunge had identified Butler's project as the next frontier in Seattle music.
- **3 consecutive E=6 significance:** The Physics (155), Blue Scholars (156), and Shabazz Palaces (157) form the longest sustained E=6 run in engine history. This three-degree peak caps the hip-hop era -- a genre that entered the engine around degree 144 and has dominated through 157, producing a 13-degree arc that is one of the most sustained genre runs in the full 360.
