1. **A rotation opens when an axis caps.**
   The fundamental-physics axis held complete at obs#2 and rotation #34 opened geodesy / satellite-laser-ranging at obs#1, showing that the rotation discipline turns a capped depth into breadth rather than stretching an axis past its natural observations.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11989_

2. **The simplest spacecraft can be the steadiest instrument.**
   With no power, no electronics, and no moving parts, LAGEOS-1 has nothing aboard that can age, drift, or switch off, showing that subtracting every active system is itself a precision-engineering decision when the mission's job is to be exactly where gravity says.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11990_

3. **Inverting the architecture moves the upgrade path to the ground.**
   The precision instruments stay at the stations and the satellite is the reference target, so five decades of station improvements carried the measurement from centimeters to millimeters without touching the spacecraft.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11991_

4. **Density defeats perturbation.**
   The aluminum shell over a brass core packs about 407 kilograms into 60 centimeters, so atmospheric drag and radiation pressure barely move the orbit — the body's mass-to-area ratio is the quiet foundation of the benchmark's stability.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11992_

5. **A geodesy benchmark can double as a relativity instrument.**
   Decades of ranging to the LAGEOS pair yielded the 2004 Lense-Thirring frame-dragging measurement, showing that a sufficiently stable reference orbit serves measurements far beyond its design purpose — the single lineage thread back to the capped fundamental-physics axis.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11993_

6. **Plate tectonics passed from inference to measurement.**
   Station baselines changed by steady centimeters per year, agreeing with geologic-average rates, showing that a passive orbital target plus patient round-trip timing can settle a question the rock record could only imply.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11994_

7. **A reference frame is infrastructure.**
   The International Terrestrial Reference Frame realized in part through LAGEOS ranging underpins satellite navigation, ocean altimetry, and sea-level measurement, showing that the least visible deliverable — the frame itself — carries the broadest working legacy.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11995_
