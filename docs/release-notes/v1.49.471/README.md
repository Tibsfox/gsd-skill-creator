- **Cygnus buccinator Is North America's Largest Native Waterfowl at 11-14 kg and 2-2.4 m Wingspan.** The Trumpeter Swan (*Cygnus buccinator*) is the largest native waterfowl in North America and one of the world's largest flying birds. Adult males (cobs) weigh 11-14 kg (24-30 lbs) with wingspans of 2-2.4 meters (6.5-8 feet), and the species name *buccinator* is Latin for "trumpeter," referring directly to the deep resonant call. The call is produced by an elongated trachea that coils within a groove in the keel of the sternum, adding tube length up to 1.5 meters total without requiring a proportionally longer neck. The fundamental frequency of the call is 250-350 Hz with strong harmonics at 500-700 Hz, producing a smooth bell-like resonance with French-horn or tuba tonal quality rather than the brighter quality of a modern brass trumpet. The Pacific Coast subspecies *A. c. tabida* (Greater Sandhill Crane) is the subspecies most commonly encountered in western Washington, while Pacific Flyway migration concentrates Lesser Sandhill Cranes through the Columbia Basin and Palouse region of eastern Washington.
