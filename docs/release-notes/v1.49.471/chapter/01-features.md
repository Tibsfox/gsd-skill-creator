## Trumpeter Swan anatomy


- the Trumpeter Swan's resonant call is produced by a windpipe (trachea) that coils within the sternum, extending the resonating tube beyond what the neck would allow; the anatomy is an evolutionary parallel to the Sandhill Crane's tracheal coiling (which loops through the sternum differently but to the same acoustic effect); both species have evolved anatomical solutions to the same acoustic engineering problem: how to produce a louder, lower-frequency call from a bird-sized body; the resulting calls -- swan bugling and crane rattling -- are acoustically distinctive enough to be identified at significant distance, which is their primary function
