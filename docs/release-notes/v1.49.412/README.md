Where pod calls are the social announcement of group identity, whistles and clicks serve different communicative and sensory functions. SRKW whistles are continuous, frequency-modulated vocalizations used in close-range social interaction between individuals: they are the intimate acoustic layer of the pod's communication system, used in play, in bonding, in mother-calf interaction. Clicks are how orcas see.

The combination of whistles and clicks at E=8 reflects the acoustic richness of the SRKW vocal repertoire at close range, in active social and sensory engagement. This is not the pod call -- the formal, stereotyped vocalization that announces group identity.
