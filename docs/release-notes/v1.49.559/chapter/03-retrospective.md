# Retrospective — v1.49.559

## Lessons Learned

1. **Apollo 1 shadow week enters the engine.** Lunar Orbiter 3 launched L+9d from the fire that killed Grissom, White, and Chaffee. The engine carries forward the nine-day gap as a structural marker: Apollo 1 at degree 54 (Jan 27, 1967) into LO3 at degree 55 (Feb 5, 1967). The crewed program is paused. The robotic program is not. Every subsequent Apollo-era mission in the engine inherits this shadow.

2. **Folk-adjacent streak broken at ten (all-time record).** Degrees 45-54: Jurado, Votolato, Bazan, Cahoone, Dawson, Mirah, Grand Archives, Carlile, Fleet Foxes, Ivan & Alyosha. Ten consecutive degrees of folk-adjacent genre. Degree 55 breaks the streak with The Sonics at E=6-7. The longest genre-family run in engine history, and its termination at a proto-punk inflection.

3. **First garage rock and first proto-punk in S36.** Genre Stage 36 opens with The Sonics. Before this, S36 had stages for folk, indie folk, grunge, indie rock, hip-hop, electronic, jazz, punk, post-punk, alt-country, chamber-pop, shoegaze, and approximately twenty other genres -- but no garage rock, no proto-punk. Tacoma's 1960s contribution to the PNW musical canon enters the engine at degree 55.

4. **Biggest energy jump in Pass 2.** E=3 at degree 54 (Ivan & Alyosha, folk) to E=6-7 at degree 55 (The Sonics, garage rock). The amplitude-range of the engine expands to accommodate proto-punk. Previous energy envelope was E=1 to E=5; degree 55 pushes the ceiling to E=6-7 for the first time.

5. **First Turdidae in the engine.** Varied Thrush (*Ixoreus naevius*) introduces the thrush family at degree 55. Turdidae is one of the largest and most cosmopolitan bird families, historically notable for the American Robin and European Blackbird -- but the engine has reached 55 degrees without a thrush, and the Varied Thrush is a Pacific Northwest endemic (well, near-endemic; breeds from Alaska south to northern California). The 23rd bird family.

6. **Passeriformes resumes after Corvidae interlude.** Degrees 53-54 were Steller's Jay and Common Raven, both Corvidae but still Passeriformes. Degree 55's Varied Thrush is Passeriformes (Turdidae). The Passeriformes tally pulls forward to include the Corvidae plus the Turdidae. The engine's dominant avian order continues to dominate.

7. **Graceful partial failure as design discipline.** LO3's film advance jammed on February 23, 1967, stranding ~25% of exposed frames. Zero mission-critical data was lost because priority ordering transmitted the critical frames first. The engine documents graceful partial failure as an achievable mission architecture, not an accident. Future degrees will reference this pattern.

8. **First spacecraft-from-orbit photograph of a landed spacecraft.** LO3 imaged Surveyor 1 on February 22, 1967. This is a cartographic loop closure: the mapper photographs the lander, confirming that the lander is real, on real ground, ground that Apollo can land on. Structurally this parallels the engine's own method -- the engine documents its own artifacts (releases, research documents, cross-references) the way LO3 documented Surveyor 1.

9. **Clifford Nelson enters the engine.** Lunar Orbiter program manager, NASA Langley Research Center, 1963-1967. Five missions, five successes, 99% lunar surface coverage, 42-year record. Nelson joins the engine's dedication roster: the quiet managers whose discipline produced results the managers themselves were never credited for.

10. **February 1967 as synchrony month.** The Sonics recording *Boom* and LO3 mapping Apollo sites in the same calendar month. The engine now documents calendar-month synchrony as a structural device: when two tracks converge on the same month across different decades, the resonance is registered.
