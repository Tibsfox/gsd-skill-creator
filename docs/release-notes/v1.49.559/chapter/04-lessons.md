16. **The Apollo 1 pure-oxygen atmosphere should have been caught earlier.**
   The Block I Command Module's 16.7 psia pure-oxygen launch-pad atmosphere was a known combustion hazard; comparable concerns had been raised during Mercury and Gemini programs but dismissed in favor of weight savings. The Review Board convened on January 28 found that the cascade of ignition failures would have been unsurvivable even with perfect crew response. The unmanned work at degree 55 proceeds under the shadow of a preventable crewed accident, and the engine documents this asymmetry: the robotic side's discipline exists in part because the crewed side's discipline failed.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5154_

17. **The February 23 film advance jam indicates a mechanical-reliability gap.**
   The film advance mechanism that jammed on February 23, 1967 was a known single-point failure in the LO design. A redundant advance system had been considered during the 1964-1965 design phase and rejected on weight and complexity grounds. LO3's jam stranded ~25% of frames; LO4 and LO5 would fly with the same single-point mechanism and would not jam, but the probability profile was unacceptable by modern standards. Future engine retrospectives on mechanical reliability should cite the LO film advance as a counter-example.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5155_
