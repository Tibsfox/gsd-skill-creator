## Summary

**Chastity Belt** arrives at E=4 -- and with them, a **Hardly Art streak opens** that will run through degree 204. Chastity Belt formed in Walla Walla before relocating to Seattle, releasing records on Hardly Art (Sub Pop's sister label) that blend slack, deadpan humor with genuine emotional depth. *No Regerts* (2013) and *I Used to Spend So Much Time Alone* (2017) capture a band navigating post-college aimlessness with a shrug that conceals real songwriting craft. Julia Shapiro's vocals sit low in the mix, almost conversational, as if the songs are being told to you in confidence at a house show. At E=4, Chastity Belt continues the restrained energy band that has characterized degrees 196-201 -- six consecutive entries at E=6 or below, the longest such stretch in Quadrant III. On the species side, the **Black Bear jaw-pop** registers at E=6 -- the middle entry in the bear trio (200-202). The jaw-pop is one of the most startling sounds a Black Bear produces: a rapid, percussive clacking of the teeth that signals heightened stress and is typically accompanied by the huff (degree 200). The jaw-pop is louder and more aggressive than the huff, escalating the acoustic warning before any physical contact. It is a sound designed to make you stop and reconsider your approach.
