## Summary

The Builder-Stayer and the accelerando. Degree 15 introduces Thomas Marriott -- a Seattle-born jazz trumpeter who studied at the University of Washington under Marc Seales (degree 13), demonstrated national-level ability through performances with Maynard Ferguson and Benny Golson, and made the defining choice of his structural position in the engine: he stayed. Born in Seattle in 1979, Marriott is the sixteenth artist in the S36 engine, the sixteenth consecutive jazz artist, the second trumpeter after Floyd Standifer at degree 4, and the third UW pipeline product documented at consecutive degrees (13, 14, 15). He is the first artist born in the late 1970s, placing him squarely in Generation X, four years older than Aaron Parks (degree 14, born 1983) and a full generation younger than Marc Seales (degree 13). Where Parks was exported through the institutional pipeline at age 16 and never returned, Marriott completed his studies in Seattle, built his career in Seattle, and remains in Seattle. The UW pipeline produces both exports and anchors -- Marriott is the anchor product.

Marriott became one of the most active jazz trumpeters in the Pacific Northwest, recording extensively for Origin Records -- Seattle's premier independent jazz label, founded by John Bishop in 1997. Origin Records is the institutional infrastructure that connects the modern Seattle jazz scene, and it represents a third aesthetic position in the engine's label taxonomy: not Blue Note's national prestige (Parks, degree 14), not ECM's European spaciousness (Frisell 1, Peacock 8, Parks 14), but **documentary realism** -- a Seattle-based label documenting the Seattle jazz community with clean recordings, natural acoustic space, and minimal post-production processing. The label serves the scene rather than defining it. Marriott's discography on Origin includes *Flexicon* (2006), *Human* (2008), *Dialogue* (2010), and *Urban Folklore* (2013) -- a decade of consistent output documenting his evolution within the hard bop and post-bop vocabulary. He is not a radical stylist pushing genre boundaries (Parks, degree 14), but a deep practitioner refining a tradition. His trumpet vocabulary is rooted in the Clifford Brown lineage: rapid chromatic runs, arpeggiated chord tones at tempo, crisp articulation, and the physical endurance to sustain complex phrases across multiple choruses. The hard bop trumpet is an inherently projective instrument -- it leads the ensemble, states the melody, and commands the room. Marriott deploys this projection with the intensity that makes him the engine's first energy-3 artist.

The energy-3 threshold is structurally significant because it signals the engine's increasing intensity as the circle rotates through the jazz quadrant. For fifteen degrees, the S36 engine operated in the energy-1 to energy-2 range: two energy-1 artists (Peacock 8, Lanphere 12) and thirteen energy-2 artists (everyone else). The energy spectrum has been heavily weighted toward energy-2 -- the community-embedded, atmospheric, sustaining energy profile that characterizes Seattle's jazz tradition. Energy-3 has been absent. Marriott breaks the ceiling. His energy is not the meditative stillness of Peacock or the ambient warmth of Parks. It is the direct, assertive energy of a hard bop trumpeter: forward-projecting, rhythmically driving, harmonically declarative. The energy spectrum distribution shifts from 2:13:0 to **2:13:1** -- the first ternary distribution. As the circle rotates from jazz toward soul, R&B, and eventually rock, the average energy level must increase. Marriott at energy-3 is the transitional artist: still jazz, but at the intensity level that bridges to what comes next.

The Standifer-Marriott trumpet arc mirrors the Berry-Seales piano arc (degrees 5 to 13): elder to contemporary at the same instrument, separated by a generational and stylistic gap that reveals how the instrument's role has evolved in Seattle jazz. Standifer (degree 4, born 1929, Wilmington NC) was a multi-instrumentalist whose trumpet was one voice among several -- swing and bebop, experiential training on the Jackson Street corridor, energy-2, educational warmth. Marriott (degree 15, born 1979, Seattle) is trumpet-focused -- the instrument is the singular vehicle. Hard bop and post-bop, institutional training at UW under Seales, energy-3, assertive projection. The specialization trend is visible: from Standifer's generalist model (play everything, know everything) to Marriott's specialist model (master one instrument, go deep). The same pattern appears in the piano arc: Berry's cocktail versatility (degree 5) versus Parks's focused post-genre voice (degree 14). The trumpet in Seattle jazz has gained a full energy level across the arc.

The Builder-Stayer is the sixteenth named pattern in the artist-city taxonomy, and it is structurally distinct from all prior stay patterns. Berry (degree 5) stayed because he was embedded in a pre-institutional era -- no institutional alternative existed. Standifer (degree 4) stayed and taught experientially through bandstand mentorship. Seales (degree 13) stays and teaches within the university system -- institution-mediated. Marriott (degree 15) stays and builds infrastructure -- recording for a local label, performing at local venues, participating in the community -- without primary attachment to a teaching institution. The stay is scene-mediated, not institution-mediated. The Builder-Stayer requires four conditions: national-level ability (demonstrated through performances with Ferguson and Golson), conscious choice to remain in the origin city (not inability to leave -- ability plus choice), active investment in local infrastructure (Origin Records, Jazz Alley, Tula's, the Royal Room, Earshot Jazz Festival), and a career based locally. Marriott meets all four. He is the generational complement to The Persistence: Berry stayed because there was nowhere else to go. Marriott stays because there is somewhere else to go and he chooses not to. Passive retention versus active investment. Same outcome, different agency.

The three-degree UW sequence (13, 14, 15) now maps the full spectrum of the stayed-vs-left dynamic from a single institution: Seales the Institutional Anchor (stayed, teaches), Parks the Prodigy-Export (left, institutional pipeline), Marriott the Builder-Stayer (stayed, scene-mediated). Three consecutive degrees, all UW-connected, all structurally different. Two stay, one leaves -- but the two stays are structurally different (institutional vs. scene-mediated), and all three are products of the same pipeline. The UW pipeline is not a single mechanism. It is a complex system that produces multiple outputs: the anchor who teaches, the prodigy who leaves, and the builder who stays.

A Wilson's Warbler flits through a willow thicket along a mountain stream. Bright yellow, black cap, impossible to miss. *Cardellina pusilla* -- a small passerine of the family Parulidae, 4.3 to 5.0 inches long, 5 to 10 grams, one of the smallest warblers in North America. Males are bright olive-yellow above and vivid yellow below, with a conspicuous glossy black cap -- a round, neat skullcap that is the bird's most diagnostic field mark. The common name honors Alexander Wilson (1766--1813), the Scottish-American "Father of American Ornithology" who produced the nine-volume *American Ornithology* (1808--1814), the first comprehensive illustrated account of North American birds. Wilson described the species himself in 1811 as *Muscicapa pusilla*. The genus has shuffled through *Wilsonia* and landed in **Cardellina** after molecular phylogenetics (Lovette et al. 2010) shattered the old genus and revealed the Wilson's Warbler as sister to the Red-faced Warbler of the Mexican sky islands. Taxonomy reshuffles the genus, but Wilson's name stays on the bird.

This is the **second Parulidae representative** in the 360 Species engine, following the MacGillivray's Warbler at degree 14. Two consecutive warblers -- back-to-back Parulidae -- but fundamentally different in every behavioral dimension. The MacGillivray's hides; the Wilson's displays. The MacGillivray's resolves; the Wilson's accelerates. The MacGillivray's is a skulker that sings from concealment; the Wilson's is conspicuously active, constantly moving, flicking its tail, gleaning insects in the open understory. You will hear this bird, and you will see it. It does not hide. Sequential Parulidae, opposite strategies.

But the defining characteristic of the Wilson's Warbler is not its conspicuousness or its taxonomy. It is the song. The bird delivers a rapid series of 10 to 15 sharp, chattering notes -- "chi-chi-chi-chi-chi-chi-chet-chet-chet" -- over approximately 1.5 to 2.5 seconds, frequency range spanning roughly 3.5 to 7 kHz. The initial "chi" syllables are spaced approximately 0.1 to 0.15 seconds apart. By the terminal "chet" syllables, the inter-note interval compresses to approximately 0.06 to 0.08 seconds. The song also drops slightly in pitch toward the end -- the last few syllables lower and more emphatic than the opening. The overall trajectory: **faster and lower**. An accelerating descent. A ball rolling downhill. This is the first species in the engine defined not by its pitch, timbre, repetition pattern, or harmonic content, but by **TEMPO CHANGE WITHIN A SINGLE VOCALIZATION**. Every previous species maintained a constant or essentially constant tempo within its song. The Wilson's Warbler's song IS the tempo change.

In music, **accelerando** -- a gradual increase in speed within a phrase -- is the structural opposite of **ritardando**, which slows toward closure and finality. Most music (and most birdsong that varies in tempo at all) slows down at the end. The ritardando says "we are arriving." The accelerando says "we are NOT arriving -- we are building toward something that does not resolve." The Wilson's Warbler performs a natural accelerando: the song speeds up and then stops. There is no resolution. No final sustained note. No contrasting terminal element like the MacGillivray's "sweet." The song simply accelerates until it ceases. The ending is not an arrival -- it is an interruption. The momentum was still building when the sound stopped. Where the MacGillivray's Warbler at degree 14 introduced **resolution** (the song that arrives, A-A-A-B), the Wilson's Warbler at degree 15 introduces **anti-resolution** -- the song that builds momentum and stops before arriving. Sequential degrees, opposite approaches to ending. The engine now contains both: the song that arrives and the song that was still going somewhere when it stopped.

The Wilson's Warbler breeds in **willow riparian** habitat -- dense stands of willow (*Salix* spp.), alder, and other moist shrubby vegetation along streams, rivers, bogs, and wet meadows. This is the third riparian species in the engine, after the Pacific-slope Flycatcher at degree 6 (alder corridors) and the MacGillivray's Warbler at degree 14 (dense riparian shrub). The Wilson's Warbler is the most strictly riparian of the three -- it tracks the willow, the willow tracks the water, the water shapes the landscape. Willows are keystone riparian plants in the PNW: among the first woody species to colonize disturbed riparian areas, stabilizing stream banks, providing shade that moderates stream temperature for salmonid habitat, and creating the dense thicket structure that the warbler requires. Over 30 willow species occur in the PNW. The bird does not need any particular species; it needs the structure willows create. Dense, moist, shrubby, streamside. That is the Wilson's Warbler's address.

The energy-3 rating is structurally parallel to its Part A counterpart. For fourteen degrees, the SPS engine operated at energy levels 0 through 2. The Great Blue Heron's silence (energy 0). The Golden-crowned Kinglet's whispering (energy 1). Everything else at energy 2. Fifteen species, and the loudest were merely "present." The Wilson's Warbler breaks the ceiling. Energy 3. Not thunderous, not overwhelming, but noticeably more intense than anything before. The chattering chi-chi-chet carries. It is insistent, rapid, percussive. The tempo acceleration amplifies the perceived energy -- a signal that is getting faster feels louder and more intense than a signal at constant tempo, even at the same actual amplitude. Both engines reach energy-3 at degree 15. Both break ceilings that constrained the previous fourteen degrees. Both are Seattle-based, contemporary, and build momentum through forward-driving phrasing. Marriott's trumpet pushes through changes with double-time intensity. The warbler pushes through its song with accelerating tempo. The parallel is structural, not metaphorical.

The resonance between Marriott and the Wilson's Warbler operates on three primary axes. First: both are the **first energy-3** in their respective circles, breaking the quiet ceiling that dominated the foundational degrees. Second: both deploy **forward-driving momentum** as their primary structural device -- Marriott through hard bop rhythmic drive and double-time passages, the warbler through accelerating note density. Third: both are **contemporary and present** -- Marriott is an active working musician in Seattle today, the warbler is a breeding-season presence that arrives every spring. Neither is historical (Standifer, Berry) or distant (Peacock in NYC, Parks in NYC). Both are here now, in the Pacific Northwest, doing the work.

Conservation status: Least Concern (IUCN), approximately 60 to 70 million breeding individuals (Partners in Flight 2020), but declining at approximately 1.3% per year since 1966 -- a cumulative loss of roughly 50% over the past five decades. The paradox of abundance: sixty million birds and a downward trend. Primary threats include loss of riparian willow habitat through water diversion, livestock grazing, and development; Brown-headed Cowbird parasitism in fragmented habitats; climate-driven phenological mismatch between arrival timing and insect emergence peaks; wintering ground habitat loss in Mexico and Central America; and building collision mortality -- the Wilson's Warbler is among the top 10 species most frequently killed by window strikes in North America.

### Part B: Wilson's Warbler -- The Chi-Chi-Chet (Accelerando)

Wilson described the bird himself in 1811. Genus transfer from *Wilsonia* to *Cardellina* in 2011 after molecular phylogenetics (Lovette et al. 2010) shattered the old genus. Three subspecies: *pusilla* (eastern/boreal), *pileolata* (Pacific coast -- brightest, most prominent cap, the PNW form), *chryseola* (coastal California -- smallest, most golden).

Initial "chi" notes spaced 0.1--0.15 seconds apart. Terminal "chet" notes compress to 0.06--0.08 seconds apart. The song also drops slightly in pitch toward the end. The accelerando is the structural opposite of ritardando. Most music slows at the end (closure, arrival, resolution). The Wilson's Warbler speeds up and then stops. No resolution. No contrasting terminal element. The momentum was still building when the sound stopped. The progression adds **accelerando** at degree 15.

- **Anti-Resolution (NEW -- The Song That Doesn't End, It Just Stops):** Where the MacGillivray's Warbler at degree 14 resolved with a contrasting "sweet" (A-A-A-B, the song that arrives), the Wilson's Warbler at degree 15 accelerates and stops. No arrival. No cadence. The expectation of more notes is violated by silence. Sequential degrees, opposite endings. Degree 14: repetition-resolution. Degree 15: acceleration without resolution. The engine now contains both: the song that arrives and the song that was still going somewhere when it stopped. In music: the EDM build-up without the drop. The punk ending that crashes to a halt. - **Second Parulidae (Sequential Warblers, Opposite Strategies):** Back-to-back Parulidae entries with contrasting behavioral profiles. ## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **The UW three-product pipeline analysis is the most structurally complete institutional mapping the S36 engine has produced.** Three consecutive degrees (13, 14, 15), all connected to the same university program, all producing structurally different outcomes: the anchor who teaches, the prodigy who leaves, the builder who stays. This is not a simple "stayed or left" binary. It is a three-output typology emerging from a single institutional system. The UW jazz program appears at three consecutive degrees because the engine's rotational logic happened to place three UW-connected artists in sequence. The emergence is precisely what makes the engine valuable -- the structure was not planned. It was discovered. Seales alone tells you about an anchor. Parks alone tells you about an export. Marriott alone tells you about a builder. Together, they tell you about the pipeline itself. The pipeline produces all three. The institution does not determine which output it creates -- the individual does. Seales chose to teach. Parks was exported before he could choose. Marriott chose to stay. Three degrees of agency within one system.

- **The Builder-Stayer archetype fills the most conspicuous gap in the artist-city taxonomy.** Prior to degree 15, the engine documented musicians who stayed because the era gave them no alternative (Berry), who stayed to teach (Standifer, Seales), and who left (Jones, Parks). Missing was the musician who has national-level ability, who could leave, and who consciously chooses to remain and build. This is the most common real-world pattern in any local music scene -- the working professional whose investment in local infrastructure IS the scene. Without the Builder-Stayer, the engine's stayed-vs-left analysis was structurally incomplete. Every city has its Builder-Stayers, and documenting the archetype at degree 15 means the engine can now identify them at subsequent degrees as the genre palette expands beyond jazz.

- **The simultaneous energy-3 breakthrough at degree 15 in both engines is the strongest structural parallel the dual-engine architecture has produced.** Both Part A and Part B independently arrive at energy-3 at the same degree. This was not forced -- Marriott's energy-3 rating follows from his hard bop trumpet vocabulary and assertive projection, while the Wilson's Warbler's energy-3 rating follows from its insistent chattering and accelerating delivery. The parallel is emergent. It validates the dual-engine design: when two independent classification systems produce the same structural event at the same degree, the event is probably real rather than arbitrary. The energy ceiling was real in both domains, and both domains independently broke it at degree 15.

- **The accelerando mapping is the cleanest new bioacoustic concept since the fusion threshold at degree 11.** The Wilson's Warbler's song introduces tempo change within a single vocalization -- a phenomenon not present in any of the preceding fourteen species. Every previous species maintained constant or essentially constant tempo. The acceleration is not a refinement of an existing concept (the way repetition-resolution at degree 14 refined repetition at degree 13). It is a new dimension entirely: temporal dynamics. The progression now tracks not just what sound is produced (pitch, timbre, rhythm, structure) but how the production changes over time within a single utterance. This is the difference between a photograph and a video -- the previous species were snapshots of a sound at steady state. The Wilson's Warbler is a sound in motion.

- **The anti-resolution concept at degree 15 produces the strongest sequential contrast in the species engine.** Degree 14 introduced resolution (the MacGillivray's "sweet" -- the first song in the engine that arrives). Degree 15 immediately introduces anti-resolution (the Wilson's Warbler -- the song that builds momentum and stops before arriving). The engine now brackets the resolution concept in a single step: degree 14 is the arrival, degree 15 is the non-arrival. Setup and negation at consecutive degrees. This is structurally elegant because it prevents resolution from becoming the default assumption. Having introduced the concept of a song that ends, the engine immediately introduces the concept of a song that does not end. Neither is privileged. Both are valid structural strategies. The listener now has two models for what "ending" means: the arrival and the interruption.

- **Origin Records as institutional infrastructure fills a label-taxonomy gap.** Blue Note (national prestige, Parks degree 14) and ECM (European spaciousness, Frisell/Peacock/Parks) are documented but both are external to Seattle. Origin Records is the first label in the engine that IS Seattle -- founded in Seattle, documenting Seattle musicians, serving the local scene. The documentary realism aesthetic (clean, natural, minimal processing) is a third production position that the engine needed. Without a local label, the engine's institutional analysis implied that Seattle jazz is documented exclusively by external institutions. Origin Records corrects this: Seattle documents itself.

### What Could Be Better

- **Fifteen consecutive jazz degrees remains the dominant structural concern for S36.** The jazz foundation is now deeply documented -- four pianists, two trumpeters, three UW products, a full energy spectrum, sixteen artist-city patterns. The comprehensiveness of the jazz quadrant is becoming a liability: the engine risks appearing as a jazz encyclopedia rather than a 360-degree rotation. Genre diversification at subsequent degrees is not optional. The longer the jazz streak continues, the more abrupt the genre transition will feel when it arrives. The engine needs to begin rotating toward soul, R&B, and funk soon, and the rotation should feel like a natural evolution rather than a hard genre break.

- **Marriott's collaborative discography beyond Origin Records could expand the network analysis.** The research documents his Origin Records releases and his performances with Ferguson and Golson, but Marriott has also collaborated with numerous other Pacific Northwest musicians. A fuller collaborative network mapping would strengthen the Builder-Stayer thesis by showing how many local musicians he has recorded and performed with -- the network density that a Builder-Stayer creates is quantifiable, and the data exists in session credits and concert listings.

- **The Wilson's Warbler's population decline deserves quantitative comparison with other declining PNW warblers.** The 1.3% annual decline and 50% cumulative loss are documented, but context requires comparison: how does this compare to Yellow Warbler, Orange-crowned Warbler, and other PNW Parulidae trends? Is the Wilson's decline anomalous or part of a broader Parulidae trend? The BBS data for the PNW region contains this information and would contextualize the conservation narrative.

- **The riparian trio (degrees 6, 14, 15) would benefit from a synthesized riparian ecosystem section.** Three riparian species in fifteen degrees constitutes a pattern, but the engine does not yet have a dedicated analysis of riparian habitat as a recurring structural thread. A synthesized treatment -- comparing the three species' riparian requirements, their positions along the stream gradient, and their responses to riparian degradation -- would elevate the riparian thread from a coincidence to a documented structural pattern in the PNW acoustic landscape.

## Lessons Learned

1. **The Builder-Stayer is the most structurally important pattern for understanding how local scenes sustain themselves, and the engine needed fifteen degrees to reach it because the preceding degrees were building the context that makes the Builder-Stayer meaningful.** The Builder-Stayer only becomes legible against the taxonomy of other stay/leave patterns: The Persistence (staying by default), The Teacher (staying to educate), The Institutional Anchor (staying within the institution), The Export (leaving as career choice), The Prodigy-Export (leaving as institutional pipeline). Without those five patterns already documented, the Builder-Stayer would just be "a musician who stays in Seattle." With the taxonomy in place, the Builder-Stayer occupies a specific structural position: the musician with national ability who invests in local infrastructure through scene-mediated activity (recording, performing, community building) rather than through institutional attachment (teaching) or by default (no alternative). The taxonomy makes the pattern visible. The engine builds taxonomies progressively, each degree adding a pattern that illuminates the patterns already documented. Degree 15 retroactively sharpens degrees 0, 4, 5, 13, and 14 by providing the missing comparison point. The same is true across all engines: each new entry teaches you something about the previous entries that you could not have seen without the new data.

2. **Accelerando and anti-resolution are fundamentally different from all previous bioacoustic concepts because they are temporal dynamics rather than static properties.** Pitch, timbre, repetition pattern, harmonic content, fusion threshold -- all of these describe a sound's properties at any given instant. The accelerando describes how a sound's properties CHANGE over the duration of a single vocalization. This is a categorical expansion of the engine's descriptive vocabulary, not an incremental addition. The engine can now describe sounds in two dimensions: what they are (static properties) and how they change (temporal dynamics). The Wilson's Warbler is the first species that requires the temporal dimension to be understood. You cannot describe the chi-chi-chet by describing any single note. You can only describe it by describing the RELATIONSHIP between notes over time -- the compression, the acceleration, the trajectory. The individual note is not the unit of meaning. The rate of change is the unit of meaning. This has implications for subsequent species: the engine should now be alert to temporal dynamics (acceleration, deceleration, crescendo, decrescendo, rhythmic transformation) as primary descriptive categories, not just secondary modifiers.

3. **When two independent engines produce the same structural event at the same degree, the event is probably real and the classification system is probably capturing something genuine about the domain.** The simultaneous energy-3 breakthrough at degree 15 in both S36 and SPS was not coordinated. Marriott's energy-3 follows from his instrument, style, and performance character. The Wilson's Warbler's energy-3 follows from its chattering delivery and accelerating tempo. Two independent evaluation processes, two independent domains (human music, avian bioacoustics), same result at the same degree. This kind of convergence is the strongest evidence the engine can produce that its classifications are capturing real structural features rather than arbitrary labels. The energy scale is working because it maps to real perceptual phenomena -- assertive trumpet projection and insistent avian chattering both exceed the threshold that separates "present in the soundscape" from "demanding attention." When the categories converge across domains, the categories are probably measuring something true. The engine should track future convergences and divergences at shared degrees to continue calibrating whether the dual-engine architecture is producing genuine structural parallels or coincidences.
