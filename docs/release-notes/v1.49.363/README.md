## Summary

**Hammerbox** arrives at E=6 -- the band built around **Carrie Akre**, one of the most powerful and undersung vocalists the Pacific Northwest produced during the grunge era. Hammerbox formed in Seattle in 1990, releasing their self-titled debut on C/Z Records and later signing to A&M, and their music sits at the intersection of post-punk tension and melodic hard rock -- heavier than most of their contemporaries wanted to be, but with Akre's voice carrying it somewhere that pure aggression couldn't reach. *Numb* (1993) is the record that introduced them to a wider audience: dense, riff-forward, and anchored throughout by Akre's ability to modulate between vulnerability and ferocity in the span of a single phrase. C/Z Records, the label that released their debut, is one of the most important and least celebrated institutions in Seattle's independent music history -- the label that put out the *Deep Six* compilation in 1986 alongside Sub Pop, documenting the proto-grunge underground before it had a name. Hammerbox's presence on C/Z places them in a direct lineage with the founding moment of the entire scene. At E=6, the band occupies a decisive register: present, committed, harder-edged than the degrees immediately before them. Carrie Akre would go on to front Goodness, another essential Seattle band, keeping her voice in the Pacific Northwest's music through the post-grunge years when many of her contemporaries had moved on or burned out. On the species side, the **Bald Eagle's territorial call** registers at E=6 -- a step up in intensity from degree 221's contact call, and the engine's first look at Accipitridae behavior. The Bald Eagle's territorial display is more complex than its basic call: a sequence of piercing, rapid vocalizations delivered from a perch or in flight, sometimes accompanied by physical displays including head-bowing and wing-spreading. The territorial call is higher in intensity because it carries intent -- it is the Bald Eagle announcing ownership of nesting territory, fishing rights, and dominance in a local hierarchy that may involve dozens of individuals along a stretch of shoreline. At E=6, the territorial call registers closer to the bird's actual power than the thin contact *kee-kee-kee* of degree 221, though still far from the cinematic scream that Hollywood has assigned it. The two-degree treatment of the Bald Eagle -- contact call at 221, territorial call at 222 -- establishes the engine's pattern for the RAPTOR ARC: depth of coverage for significant species, not just a single entry and move on.
