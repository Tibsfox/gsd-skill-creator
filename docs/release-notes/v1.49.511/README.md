The first ten degrees of the engine have documented ten distinct structural commitments: silence against its opposite at degree 0 (Quincy Jones, Corvid silence), technological mediation at degree 1 (Bill Frisell, Anna's Hummingbird), community infrastructure at degree 2 (Wayne Horvitz), referential meaning at degree 3 (Ernestine Anderson, Black-capped Chickadee), indicator diagnostics at degree 4 (Floyd Standifer), network closure at degree 5 (Chuck Berry, Red-breasted Nuthatch), hard bop vocabulary at degree 6 (Patti Bown), institutional pipeline at degree 7 (Julian Priester, Cornish), perceptual threshold at degree 8 (Gary Peacock), and confirmation at degree 9 (Hadley Caliman, Chestnut-backed Chickadee, Explorer 3, *Marchantia polymorpha*). At degree 10, for the first time, the engine confronts a class of signals that individual instruments cannot resolve and that become meaningful only when aggregation is applied. Bud Shank's cool-jazz aesthetic -- lighter tone, arranged restraint, space between notes -- is the jazz tradition's quietest pole, the signal that rewards attention and punishes distraction. The Bushtit's continuous seep-tseep contact calls are individually inaudible beyond a few meters, but a flock of twenty to forty birds produces a collective acoustic presence that defines the shrub layer's soundscape. Four independent systems. Four instances of the same principle: scale transforms noise into signal.

Bud Shank was born Clifford Everett Shank Jr. on May 27, 1926, in Dayton, Ohio. He is the engine's first artist born in Ohio and the engine's first artist whose career trajectory moves through Southern California before settling in the Pacific Northwest in late career. Shank studied at the University of North Carolina in the late 1940s, then moved to Los Angeles in 1946, where he joined Stan Kenton's progressive jazz orchestra as a saxophonist and flautist in 1949. The Kenton Orchestra was not hard bop. It was not cool jazz either. It was progressive jazz -- an idiom that explicitly incorporated European classical harmony and orchestration into the big-band format, drawing from Ravel, Stravinsky, and Bartok while retaining jazz improvisation as a core organizing principle. Kenton's orchestra was Shank's conservatory. From Kenton, Shank moved to Howard Rumsey's Lighthouse All-Stars at Hermosa Beach, California, in 1953, and the Lighthouse All-Stars became the institutional home of West Coast cool jazz for the next decade. Where Caliman (degree 9) came up through the Dexter Gordon orbit of Los Angeles hard bop, Shank came up through the Kenton / Lighthouse orbit of West Coast cool jazz -- the two scenes coexisted in the same city during the same years but rarely interacted, and the Caliman-Shank pairing at degrees 9 and 10 documents the engine's sharpest within-instrument polarity to date: tenor saxophone / hard bop / blues-rooted / East Coast spiritual lineage (Caliman) versus alto saxophone / cool jazz / classical-influenced / West Coast architectural aesthetic (Shank). Shank signed with Pacific Jazz Records under Richard Bock's production. He recorded *Brazilliance* with Brazilian guitarist Laurindo Almeida in 1953 and 1958, an album that introduced bossa nova phrasing to the West Coast jazz canon five years before Stan Getz and Joao Gilberto's *Getz/Gilberto* made bossa nova a pop phenomenon. In 1966, Shank recorded a sixteen-bar alto saxophone solo on the Mamas and the Papas's "California Dreamin'" -- a track that sold more than twenty million copies over the following decades and which carried Shank's phrasing into the pop-music mainstream in a way no other West Coast jazz musician had previously achieved. Shank retired to the Pacific Northwest in late career, settling in Tucson briefly and Port Townsend, Washington, before his death in Tucson on April 2, 2009. The Port Townsend arc is the twelfth documented artist-city pattern in the engine -- the late-career PNW settler, an artist who built his reputation elsewhere and moved to the Pacific Northwest as a destination rather than an origin.

On July 26, 1958, at 15:00 UTC at Launch Complex 5 at Cape Canaveral, a Juno I rocket designated AM-19D lifted off carrying Explorer 4. It was the sixth American orbital attempt and the fourth American orbital success. The mission had a specific scientific objective that differentiated it from the preceding Explorer missions: Explorer 1 and Explorer 3 had discovered and confirmed the natural Van Allen radiation belt through measurements of background cosmic ray flux. Explorer 4's objective was the opposite. It would measure an artificial radiation belt -- one that the U.S. military was about to create intentionally, by detonating three 1.7-kiloton W25 nuclear warheads in low Earth orbit as part of Operation Argus. The Argus operation was the world's first true space weapons test. USS Norton Sound launched three X-17A rockets from the South Atlantic between August 27 and September 6, 1958, each carrying a W25 warhead that detonated at altitudes between two hundred and five hundred kilometers. The goal was to test a prediction that Greek-American physicist Nicholas Christofilos had made in 1957: that high-altitude nuclear explosions would inject charged particles into the geomagnetic field that would then be trapped in belts analogous to the natural Van Allen radiation belts. Christofilos was not a mainstream scientist. He was an elevator engineer who had taught himself particle physics from textbooks during the German occupation of Greece and whose theoretical work had already anticipated the strong-focusing principle used in the Cosmotron particle accelerator at Brookhaven. His Argus prediction was initially dismissed. The U.S. Atomic Energy Commission decided to test it anyway, and Explorer 4 was the measurement platform. Explorer 4's Geiger-Mueller counter and scintillation detectors recorded the three Argus detonations' particle injections against the natural Van Allen background, and the data confirmed Christofilos's prediction: the artificial radiation belts formed at the predicted altitudes, decayed with the predicted lifetimes (approximately three weeks for the first belt), and had the predicted spatial structure. The confirmation was one of the twentieth century's cleanest physics predictions, and Explorer 4 was the instrument that made it. The mission operated for 454 days, reentering the atmosphere on October 23, 1959. The Argus injection is the engine's first controlled intervention in a natural field. The Caliman-Shank saxophone polarity at degrees 9 and 10 is the engine's first stylistic controlled intervention -- cool jazz as deliberate injection of classical aesthetics into the blues-rooted jazz tradition, the way Argus was deliberate injection of artificial electrons into the natural magnetosphere.

In the dry shrub layer of the Puget Sound lowland oak-Douglas-fir savanna, at the height of a salal bush four feet above leaf litter, a flock of eighteen Bushtits moves through the branches in continuous rapid foraging motion. *Psaltriparus minimus*. Aegithalidae, the long-tailed tit family -- the only New World representative of a family otherwise distributed across Eurasia. Average mass 5 grams. The Bushtit is among the smallest passerines in North America, smaller than any chickadee documented in the engine at degrees 3 or 9. Its vocal repertoire is minimal: a thin, high-frequency seep-tseep contact call, repeated continuously at rates of two to five calls per second per individual. There is no song. There is no alarm call in the combinatorial sense that the Black-capped and Chestnut-backed Chickadees possess. There is no territorial broadcast. The Bushtit produces one kind of vocalization and produces it almost constantly while foraging. The call carries approximately six to eight meters before dropping below the ambient noise floor of the coastal shrub environment. An individual Bushtit, vocalizing alone, is effectively inaudible at any distance greater than a few meters. A flock of twenty to forty Bushtits, however, produces an aggregate acoustic presence that is clearly audible at thirty to fifty meters -- not because each bird is louder, but because each bird's call is below threshold individually and the flock's collective call density rises above threshold. The Bushtit is therefore the engine's first minimum-viable-signal species. The call carries only the information "I am here" -- no predator-threat encoding, no mate-attraction content, no territorial claim, no individual identification. It is the simplest possible signal that preserves flock cohesion, and it is the signal that Explorer 4 had to operate against: the Bushtit flock's aggregate acoustic presence is the biological analog of the Van Allen belt's aggregate cosmic ray flux -- individual particles too faint to measure, collective flux rising above the instrument's noise threshold.

The Bushtit flock's social structure is an engineered distributed system. Each bird maintains continuous acoustic contact with the flock through its seep-tseep calls, and the flock's collective output functions as a real-time map of the foraging front -- which branches are being explored, which birds are leading, which are trailing, where the flock is moving next. The system is fault-tolerant: if one bird's calls suddenly stop, the neighboring birds respond within seconds, either by intensifying their own calls or by moving toward the silence to investigate. The Bushtit flock is therefore a biological distributed sensor network, and it is the engine's first explicit distributed-system model at the species-biology level. Explorer 4 was a single node in the Explorer satellite network, which by July 1958 included Explorer 1, Explorer 3, and Explorer 4 orbiting Earth simultaneously with overlapping coverage -- no single satellite could map the full Argus-perturbed radiation environment alone, and the data from all three missions combined to produce the complete picture. The network principle at degree 10 is the network principle that will recur through the Explorer series and through the Pioneer series and through the Gemini series: no single instrument or platform is sufficient, and the aggregate measurement is the measurement that matters. The Bushtit flock demonstrates the principle at the five-gram scale. Explorer 4 demonstrates it at the radiation-belt scale. The two systems are structurally equivalent.

*Cladonia stellaris*, the star-tipped reindeer lichen, occupies the boreal and subalpine ground-cover niche across the Holarctic -- Alaska, Canada, Fennoscandia, Siberia -- and extends into the higher elevations of the Pacific Northwest Cascades and Olympic Mountains. Morphologically, it is a symbiotic composite organism: fungal hyphae (Mycobiont, *Cladonia*) hosting single-celled green algae (Photobiont, *Trebouxia*) in a stratified thallus approximately two to five centimeters tall, with stellate branching producing star-tipped apothecia at the growing terminals. The stellate branching is the lichen's distinguishing morphological feature and the feature that gives it its common name. Individually, a *Cladonia stellaris* thallus is a small shrub-like structure perhaps four centimeters across. Collectively, in undisturbed boreal and subalpine terrain, it forms mats of hundreds to thousands of square meters, with individual thalli tiled edge-to-edge across the forest floor. The mat is the organism that matters ecologically: *Cladonia stellaris* mats account for a substantial fraction of reindeer and caribou winter forage across the boreal zone, and the mats influence water retention, soil temperature, and understory plant succession across the landscapes they dominate. ## Part A: Bud Shank -- COOL RESTRAINT

- **Dayton, Ohio origin and University of North Carolina conservatory (1926-1946).** Bud Shank was born Clifford Everett Shank Jr. in Dayton, Ohio, on May 27, 1926 -- the engine's first Ohio-born artist and the engine's sixth non-Pacific-Northwest regional origin after Chicago (twice), Idaho, Idabel Oklahoma, and Wisconsin. The Kenton Orchestra was not hard bop and was not cool jazz in the narrow sense; it was progressive jazz, a Kenton-specific idiom that incorporated European classical harmony and orchestration (Ravel, Stravinsky, Bartok) into a big-band format while retaining jazz improvisation. The Kenton years were Shank's conservatory, and the classical-jazz hybridization that defined Shank's later playing was formed in Kenton's book. This is the engine's first documented Kenton Orchestra lineage.

This is the engine's first documented Lighthouse All-Stars lineage and the engine's first West Coast scene membership that operated in direct parallel (but rarely intersecting contact) with Caliman's degree-9 Los Angeles hard bop scene.
- **Pacific Jazz Records and Richard Bock production aesthetic.** Shank signed with Pacific Jazz Records under founder-producer Richard Bock, and the Pacific Jazz production aesthetic -- close miking, ensemble balance over soloist prominence, natural reverb, minimal post-production -- became Shank's sonic signature. This is the engine's first documented Pacific Jazz Records lineage and Shank is the engine's first Pacific Jazz artist. The flute is the engine's only body-disconnected wind instrument.

## Part B: Bushtit -- THE MINIMUM VIABLE SIGNAL

- **Species identity and Aegithalidae family isolation.** *Psaltriparus minimus*. Aegithalidae, the long-tailed tit family -- a family otherwise distributed across Eurasia with approximately a dozen species, of which Bushtit is the only New World representative. Year-round resident across western North America from southern British Columbia through central Mexico, concentrated in oak-chaparral, shrubby woodland edge, and suburban garden habitat. Average mass 5 grams, smaller than any chickadee (Black-capped 11 g at degree 3, Chestnut-backed 8 g at degree 9). The Bushtit is therefore the engine's smallest passerine to date and the engine's first sole-family-representative species.
- **Minimum-viable-signal vocalization -- seep-tseep contact call only.** Bushtit vocal repertoire consists almost entirely of a single call type: a thin, high-frequency seep-tseep contact call, repeated two to five times per second per individual, carrying approximately six to eight meters before falling below the coastal shrub-environment noise floor. There is no song. There is no combinatorial alarm system analogous to the Paridae D-note syntax. There is no territorial broadcast. The Bushtit encodes a single bit of information -- "I am present" -- and encodes nothing else. This is the engine's first minimum-viable-signal species and the engine's first documented case of a species whose vocal system operates at the lower bound of useful information content.
- **Flock aggregation as the signal-generating mechanism.** Individual Bushtit calls are below threshold for acoustic detection beyond a few meters. Bushtit flocks of twenty to forty individuals, however, produce an aggregate acoustic presence that is clearly audible at thirty to fifty meters. The flock's collective call density rises above threshold even as each individual stays below threshold. The Bushtit flock is therefore the engine's first explicit aggregation-based signal system -- the flock is the signal, the individual is the noise floor. This inverts the relationship the engine has documented in every prior species (Black-capped Chickadee individually audible, Red-breasted Nuthatch individually audible, Black-capped individual-level broadcaster).
- **Swarm communication as distributed sensor network.** The Bushtit flock operates as a biological distributed sensor network. Each bird is a mobile node. Each seep-tseep call is a status transmission. The collective output functions as a real-time map of the foraging front, coordinating flock movement through branch-to-branch continuous vocal contact. The system is fault-tolerant: one bird's silence triggers local search-and-recovery by neighboring birds, either through increased calling or through movement toward the silent bird's last-known position. This is the engine's first explicit distributed-system model at the species-biology level and a structural precursor to the Explorer / Pioneer / Gemini multi-satellite coordination patterns that the NASA thread will develop at higher degrees.
- **Free-rider role in the interspecies alarm network.** Bushtits occur in mixed-species foraging flocks alongside Black-capped Chickadees (where range overlaps), Chestnut-backed Chickadees (coastal), Ruby-crowned Kinglets, Yellow-rumped Warblers, and Red-breasted Nuthatches. The Bushtit benefits from the chickadees' combinatorial D-note alarm broadcasts (gaining threat information through eavesdropping, in the Templeton and Greene Davis 2005 *Science* framework) without contributing equivalent alarm information to the network. This is the engine's first documented free-rider role in the interspecies alarm network and the first asymmetric network participant: the alarm network's first pure receiver / non-broadcaster. Network-ecology implication: distributed alarm systems can support free-riders without collapsing, so long as the free-rider fraction stays below a threshold that the broadcaster species' fitness cost can tolerate.
- **Obligate social foraging and minimum flock size.** Bushtits do not forage alone. Flock size rarely drops below eight individuals and commonly reaches forty. The obligate-social behavior is the behavioral complement to the minimum-viable-signal vocal system: because each bird's calls are individually inaudible, flock cohesion requires continuous acoustic contact with multiple flock-mates simultaneously, and the minimum flock size is therefore approximately the minimum number of simultaneously-calling birds required to exceed the noise-floor threshold in the local habitat. Flock size is not social preference; it is acoustic necessity.
- **Sole New World Aegithalidae and biogeographic isolation.** The Bushtit is the only long-tailed tit in the Americas. Its closest relatives -- Long-tailed Tit (*Aegithalos caudatus*), Pygmy Tit, White-browed Tit-warbler -- occur in Eurasia across a range extending from Britain through Scandinavia to Japan and across the Himalayan foothills. The Bushtit's isolation in western North America is a biogeographic anomaly that dates to approximately 5-8 million years before present, when the Aegithalidae ancestor crossed the Bering Land Bridge from Eurasia and speciated in isolation. The engine's documentation of Bushtit at degree 10 is therefore also a documentation of one of the cleanest extant Beringian passerine biogeographic signatures, parallel to the eastern-hemisphere / western-hemisphere Paridae diversification that separates Black-capped and Chestnut-backed from their Eurasian cousins.
- **Cladonia co-location and subalpine habitat overlap.** Although Bushtits occupy lowland shrub and oak-savanna habitat in their core range, Pacific Northwest subalpine and higher-elevation Bushtit populations forage in habitats where *Cladonia stellaris* mats occupy the forest floor. The co-location at the subalpine elevation is less literal than the degree-9 Chestnut-backed Chickadee / Marchantia moss-mat co-location, but it continues the paired-engine method's pattern of habitat overlap between the SPS species and the NASA organism. Degree 10's co-location is at the seasonal-migration elevational scale rather than the continuous-habitat scale.
- **Noise floor as habitat -- the engine's first non-resolution-based signal system.** Every prior species in the engine has operated on resolution-based signaling: the Corvid silence (degree 0) is silence against a contrast; the Anna's Hummingbird (degree 1) is a complex hum at the edge of aural detection; the Black-capped Chickadee (degree 3) is combinatorial syntax; the Peacock perceptual threshold (degree 8) is the limit of human auditory discrimination. The Bushtit inverts this: the signal is not at the edge of resolution; it is below the edge of individual resolution, and it is meaningful only when aggregation is applied. This is the engine's first non-resolution-based signal system and the structural analog of Explorer 4's Argus measurement, which had to be extracted from a natural Van Allen background that was itself the signal of prior missions.

The detonation's measurement permanence (the solo exists on twenty million records) is the pop-field equivalent of Argus's permanent measurement record in physics archives.

**3. The Noise Floor as Habitat.** Explorer 4's noise floor (natural Van Allen belts + cosmic rays) was itself the signal of prior Explorer missions at degrees 1 and 9. The Bushtit's continuous seep-tseep calls are the acoustic noise floor of the coastal shrub layer -- noise to the birder listening for songs, signal to the flock maintaining cohesion. The noise floor is not absence of information. It is information at a different scale. Cladonia mats: individually invisible, collectively undeniable. Scale transforms noise into signal. The degree 10 principle is the scale-dependence-of-information principle, and all four tracks document it from independent angles.

**4. Swarm Communication as Distributed Sensor Network.** The Bushtit flock is a biological distributed sensor network. Each bird a mobile node, each seep-tseep a status transmission, the collective output a real-time map of the foraging front. Fault-tolerant: one bird's silence triggers local search-and-recovery. Explorer 4 as one node in the Explorer satellite network of 1958 -- no single satellite could map the full Argus-perturbed radiation environment alone; the complete picture emerged from aggregate data across Explorer 1, Explorer 3, and Explorer 4 orbits.

**5. Free-Rider on the Alarm Network.** The Bushtit benefits from chickadee alarm broadcasts in mixed-species flocks without contributing equivalent alarm information. First asymmetric participant in the interspecies alarm network: five nodes now documented, one free-rider. Network ecology therefore includes passive beneficiaries, not just broadcasters and receivers. The implication is that real-world alarm networks tolerate asymmetry and still function.

## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **The noise-floor theme carried all four tracks cleanly without forcing alignment.** Bud Shank's cool-jazz restraint (aesthetic signal at reduced amplitude), the Bushtit's minimum-viable seep-tseep contact call (acoustic signal below individual threshold), Explorer 4's Argus-measurement context (radiation signal against natural Van Allen background), and Cladonia stellaris's mat-forming growth habit (ecological signal through aggregation) each independently perform the noise-floor operation. The paired-engine method has now documented a scale-dependent signal phenomenon across jazz, biology, physics, and botany without any single track having to absorb thematic weight for the others.
- **The Caliman-Shank saxophone polarity at degrees 9 and 10 produced the engine's sharpest within-instrument contrast.** Tenor / alto, hard bop / cool, blues-rooted / classical-influenced, East Coast spiritual lineage / West Coast architectural aesthetic, Dexter Gordon orbit / Kenton and Lighthouse orbit, Catalyst Records / Pacific Jazz Records, Great Migration trajectory / late-career PNW settler trajectory. Seven dimensions of contrast, held constant at the instrument-family level (saxophone) and varying across style, origin, institution, production aesthetic, and career arc. This is the cleanest within-instrument polarity the engine has yet produced, and the two-degree sequence (9 then 10) demonstrates that adjacent degrees can produce sharper contrasts than widely-separated degrees when the instrument-family axis is held constant.
- **The flute entered the engine as the sixth tone-production mechanism with clean structural framing.** Edge-tone joins reed, lip-buzz, vocal cord, struck string, and plucked string to complete the engine's primary tone-production taxonomy. The flute is framed as the body-disconnected end of the body-mediation spectrum (Anderson's voice at degree 3 is the body-embedded end), and the Shank secondary-instrument framing preserves the degree 10 primary identity as alto saxophone while still introducing the new mechanism without forcing a standalone flute degree.
- **The July 26 calendar alignment of Carl Jung's birthday to Explorer 4's launch locked the dedication convention at its cleanest instance.** Jung (born July 26, 1875) and Explorer 4 (launched July 26, 1958) are separated by eighty-three years but share the exact calendar date, and Jung's theoretical commitment to the collective unconscious (signals below individual threshold, meaningful only in aggregate) structurally aligns the dedication to the degree's thematic commitment. The dedication framework is now an explicit engine convention rather than a coincidence-of-theme.
- **The noise-floor theme closed the opening decade of the engine at a natural thematic breakpoint.** Degrees 0-9 introduced ten distinct structural commitments (silence, complexity, infrastructure, meaning, diagnosis, network closure, hard bop, institutional pipeline, perceptual threshold, confirmation). Degree 10 introduces aggregation as the eleventh commitment and explicitly closes the first decade by documenting a principle that operates across the prior ten commitments rather than alongside them. The thematic arc from confirmation (degree 9, second-measurement proof) to aggregation (degree 10, below-threshold signals) is structurally coherent and positions the second decade of the engine to build on aggregation as the foundational operation.

### What Could Be Better

- **The Bushtit research did not integrate the obligate-social-foraging behavioral ecology with the minimum-viable-signal vocal ecology at sufficient depth.** The Bushtit's flock-size minimum (approximately eight individuals) is acoustic necessity rather than social preference -- the flock must exceed the noise-floor threshold in aggregate to maintain cohesion. This structural argument deserved a dedicated subsection with quantitative amplitude measurements, habitat-noise-floor data, and a comparative analysis of why chickadees can operate at flock sizes of two to four while Bushtits cannot drop below eight. The argument is present in the release but under-specified.
- **The Project Argus operational history was compressed into single-paragraph treatment when it deserves chapter-level depth.** Three 1.7-kiloton W25 nuclear warheads detonated at 200-540 km altitude in the South Atlantic magnetic anomaly zone represents the twentieth century's first space nuclear weapons test, the cleanest confirmation of Christofilos's belt-formation prediction, and the operational precursor to every subsequent high-altitude nuclear test (Starfish Prime 1962 is the direct successor). The release gestures at Argus without providing the full operational timeline, the warhead specifications, the USS Norton Sound launch platform context, or the post-test data analysis that confirmed the prediction.
- **The Laurindo Almeida / Brazilliance collaboration is mentioned but not documented with sufficient specificity.** The 1953 and 1958 *Brazilliance* sessions predate bossa nova's popular emergence by five years and represent a significant early documentation of Brazilian jazz integration with West Coast cool jazz. The release names the collaboration but does not cite specific tracks, does not place the sessions in the broader Pacific Jazz / Richard Bock production context, and does not engage Almeida's classical-guitar training (Heitor Villa-Lobos lineage) as a contributing factor to the Shank-Almeida aesthetic. Future Shank-related content should deepen this.
- **The Jung dedication relied on the calendar synchrony without engaging Jung's specific texts.** *Psychology and Alchemy* (1944), *Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious* (1959), and the Red Book (composed 1914-1930, published 2009) are the canonical texts for the collective-unconscious framework that the noise-floor theme structurally parallels. The dedication text establishes the July 26 alignment but does not quote Jung's specific structural claims about archetype detection requiring aggregation, and the dedication therefore reads as associational rather than citational (the same criticism that applied to the Campbell dedication at degree 9).

## Lessons Learned

1. **The noise floor is not absence of information; it is information at a different scale.** Degree 10's core principle is that signals existing below individual detection threshold become meaningful when aggregation is applied. The Bushtit flock's collective seep-tseep call density, Cladonia stellaris's mat-forming ground cover, Explorer 4's Argus measurement against Van Allen background, and cool jazz's collective-restraint aesthetic all operate at the same scale-transforms-noise-into-signal principle. This principle generalizes beyond the four tracks and is available as an engine-level methodology: future degrees will distinguish resolution-based signals (edge-of-detection) from aggregation-based signals (below-individual-detection) and treat them as structurally distinct.

2. **The saxophone family is the engine's sharpest within-instrument stylistic-polarity system.** Caliman (9, tenor, hard bop, blues-rooted, East Coast spiritual lineage) and Shank (10, alto, cool, classical-influenced, West Coast architectural aesthetic) invert across every measurable dimension while holding the instrument family constant. The engine produces its clearest stylistic contrasts when the instrument family is held constant and the style varies, and the Caliman-Shank pair at adjacent degrees demonstrates that the contrast can be produced without wide temporal separation. Future within-instrument polarities should be sought at similarly adjacent or near-adjacent degree positions.

3. **The flute diversifies the tone-production taxonomy to six categories and introduces the body-disconnected end.** Edge-tone (flute) joins reed (saxophone), lip-buzz (brass), vocal cord (voice), struck string (piano), and plucked string (guitar / bass). The flute is the only tone-production mechanism in which no vibrating element contacts the player's body -- the player's breath splits across the embouchure hole's edge and produces sound through edge-tone resonance in free air. This positions the flute at the extreme of the body-mediation spectrum opposite Anderson's voice at degree 3.

4. **Cool jazz's space-and-restraint aesthetic matches the engine's quiet-zone energy profile through degrees 5-10.** Degrees 5 through 10 have sustained combined energy levels of 2-4, the lowest sustained range in the engine to date. Cool jazz's aesthetic commitment to silence between notes and ensemble architecture over soloist display is not accidentally aligned with the engine's energy-profile shape; it reflects the artists and systems being documented in the current sequence. The quiet zone is a genuine property of the degree 5-10 subject matter, not a selection artifact.

5. **The "California Dreamin'" crossover is the first pop-music intersection in the engine and operates as an Argus-injection analog.** Shank's sixteen-bar alto saxophone solo brought West Coast cool-jazz phrasing to more than twenty million pop-record listeners -- a temporary detonation of jazz aesthetic in the pop-music field that persisted in the measurement record the way Argus's artificial radiation belts persisted in the physics literature after the belts themselves had decayed. Pop crossover is now an engine-recognized phenomenon, and future instances (Grover Washington Jr., Kenny G, Dave Koz) can be placed against the Shank precedent.

6. **Explorer 4 / Argus as controlled intervention is the engine's first deliberate-injection parallel across mission and aesthetic.** Three 1.7-kiloton nuclear devices detonated at 200-540 km altitude injected artificial electrons into the natural Van Allen magnetosphere. Cool jazz injects classical-music elements into the blues-rooted jazz tradition. Both are controlled interventions in a natural field, sustained by institutional infrastructure (Argus by USS Norton Sound and ABMA / JPL, cool jazz by Pacific Jazz Records and Lighthouse All-Stars), and both produced permanent measurement records despite the intervention itself being temporary. The controlled-intervention parallel is now available as an engine methodology.

7. **Cladonia stellaris introduces mat-forming collective-scale botany as the eleventh NASA organism layer.** The star-tipped reindeer lichen's stellate branching (individual scale) and mat formation (collective scale) make it the engine's first explicit two-scale botanical entry: visible individually through the star-tipped apothecia, visible ecologically through the hundreds-of-square-meters mats that dominate boreal and subalpine ground cover. The botanical profile now has a collective-scale layer complementing the degree 9 Marchantia deep-time layer (470 Myr persistence) and the earlier ephemeral and persistent-individual layers.

8. **Carl Jung's collective-unconscious framework is the engine's first dedicated aggregation-theory dedication.** Jung's July 26, 1875 birthday aligned to Explorer 4's July 26, 1958 launch locks the noise-floor degree to the psychologist whose central claim was that the most important signals in human psychology operate below individual detection threshold and become meaningful only when aggregated across populations. The archetypes of the collective unconscious are inaudible individually and unmistakable collectively -- the same principle the Bushtit flock demonstrates acoustically. The dedication is now the engine's clearest aggregation-theory anchor, and future aggregation-themed degrees can reference Jung structurally.

9. **The first decade of the engine is complete and the second decade begins on aggregation.** Degrees 0-9 introduced ten structural commitments; degree 10 introduces aggregation as the principle that operates across those commitments rather than alongside them. The opening decade closed at the natural breakpoint of confirmation (degree 9) and aggregation (degree 10), and the engine is now positioned to build the second decade's patterns on aggregation as the foundational operation. Degree 11 will open the second decade on whatever the next structural commitment is -- likely edge-habitat specialization or Central District return, based on the Seattle 360 engine's established trajectory.

10. **Free-rider roles in ecological networks are real and the Bushtit documents the first one in the engine.** The interspecies alarm network at degrees 3, 5, 7, and 9 accumulated nodes that were either broadcasters (chickadees) or receivers (nuthatches). At degree 10, the Bushtit introduces a purely asymmetric participant -- a species that benefits from the network's alarm broadcasts without contributing equivalent alarm information. Network ecology therefore includes passive beneficiaries, and the Bushtit establishes the pattern that real-world alarm networks tolerate asymmetric participation below a threshold that the broadcaster species' fitness cost can absorb.
