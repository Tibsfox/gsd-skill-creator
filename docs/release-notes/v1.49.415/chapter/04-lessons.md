# Lessons — v1.49.415

12 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **Arc-closing documentation at the end of a multi-degree sub-sequence should preserve the arc's structural unity rather than treating the closing entry as a standalone release.**
   The degree 274 documentation treats Vexed as the arc-closing entry of the five-degree Seattle HC arc rather than as an isolated single-band degree. The lesson is that arc-closing entries should be documented with explicit arc-level framing — naming the arc, naming the other arc entries, computing arc-level summary statistics, and surfacing the arc's structural unity — rather than being documented in the same format as isolated single-band degrees. The arc-level framing is what makes the closing entry legible as an arc-closure rather than a sequence-end.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.457`) · lesson #8801_

2. **Mid-arc pivot placements in extended multi-degree sub-sequences warrant first-class structural-fulcrum documentation.**
   The degree 274 documentation treats the Orca Spy-Hop as the mid-arc pivot of the seven-degree Orca Arc rather than as a middle-of-sequence entry. The lesson is that mid-arc placements in extended sub-sequences are structural fulcrums rather than simple sequential markers, and the documentation should surface the fulcrum function explicitly rather than treating middle-of-sequence entries as interchangeable with arc-opening or arc-closing entries.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8802_

3. **Cross-track arc-coordination at shared fifth-position placements creates legible cross-track structural registers.**
   The degree 274 alignment of the Seattle HC arc's fifth-and-closing position and the Orca Arc's fifth-and-pivot position at the same degree is structurally deliberate and interpretively productive. The lesson is that cross-track arc-coordination — when the engine can align arcs across tracks at shared structural positions — creates legible cross-track registers that are stronger than single-track arc documentation alone. Future arc planning should look for cross-track coordination opportunities where they exist.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8803_

4. **Sustained-ceiling amplitude across a multi-degree arc is a distinct register from single-degree peak amplitude.**
   The Seattle HC arc's weighted mean above E=7.5 across five entries at E=7-8 is a distinct register from single-degree E=8 peaks elsewhere in the catalog. The lesson is that the engine's amplitude vocabulary should distinguish sustained-ceiling arc amplitude from single-moment peak amplitude, and arc-level documentation should explicitly compute and surface arc-level amplitude statistics rather than reducing the arc to its highest-amplitude individual entry.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.456`) · lesson #8804_

5. **Outside-commercial-framework work warrants first-class documentation treatment rather than commercial-success-normalized evaluation.**

The lesson is that outside-commercial-framework work should be documented on its own terms — for its musical, cultural, and historical properties — rather than being evaluated against commercial-success metrics that the work itself was designed to operate outside of.
