**The Seattle hardcore era closes.**

**Vexed** arrive at E=8 -- the Seattle hardcore punk band, the fifth and final entry in the Seattle HC arc that has spanned degrees 270-274. Vexed operated in the early-to-mid 1980s Seattle hardcore underground alongside The Fartz, Solger, The U-Men, and The Refuzors, contributing to the body of work that constitutes the Pacific Northwest's foundational document in American hardcore punk. Their music carries the essential commitments of the form: fast, loud, direct, politically engaged, produced outside any commercial framework, available primarily to the local scene that generated it.

E=8 for Vexed ties the engine maximum for the S36 track. It is the correct score for a hardcore band at full engagement, and it closes the Seattle HC arc at the same energy level at which it opened at degree 270 with The Fartz. The arc has maintained E=7-8 across all five entries: The Fartz (E=8), Solger (E=8), The U-Men (E=7), The Refuzors (E=8), Vexed (E=8). The weighted mean of the arc is above E=7.5. It is among the highest-sustained-energy arcs in the engine's S36 track.

The Seattle HC arc documents a music scene of five years' duration -- 1980-1985, approximately -- that produced a body of work of historical significance and zero commercial reception. These bands did not chart. They did not tour arenas. They did not benefit from the global attention that Seattle music received a decade later. They played small venues for small audiences, recorded on minimal budgets, released on their own and on friends' labels, and produced music that constitutes the unacknowledged foundation of what came after. Grunge is audible in the influence of the U-Men. The Accused's crossover trajectory runs through the bands on either side of them in the engine. Ten Minute Warning are proto-punk ancestors. The Fartz and Solger are the genetic material. Vexed closes the sequence.

**Orca Spy-Hop** arrives at E=8 on the SPS track -- the acoustic and behavioral event of a Southern Resident Killer Whale rising vertically from the water to expose its head, often to the level of the pectoral fins or beyond, and holding that position momentarily before submerging. The spy-hop is a well-documented SRKW behavior associated with visual orientation: whales spy-hop to observe the surface environment above the waterline -- to see boats, to see other whales, to observe their surroundings in ways that echolocation does not provide. The acoustic component of a spy-hop is the water displacement at entry and exit, the blow at the surface, and any vocalizations produced during or following the behavior.

E=8 for the Orca Spy-Hop reflects the full acoustic event of a large cetacean performing vertical propulsion to the height of the surface: the water displacement, the blow, the re-entry. It is a high-energy acoustic event, though at lower intensity than the full aerial breach documented at degree 273. The spy-hop is precise and deliberate where the breach is explosive and total. Both are correct for their respective positions in the arc.
