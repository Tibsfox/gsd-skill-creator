**Cosmonaut-Researcher Maj. Aleksandr Panayotov Aleksandrov-Belorussian** (born 1951-12-01 Omurtag Bulgarian People's Republic; still living 2026) — Bulgarian People's Republic Air Force MiG-21 + MiG-23 fighter pilot. Selected for Interkosmos 1978 as backup to Georgi Ivanov.

The Bulgarian Interkosmos programme had launched its first cosmonaut Georgi Ivanov on **Soyuz 33 1979-04-10** with commander Nikolai Rukavishnikov, but the mission failed catastrophically: Soyuz 33's main engine ignited abnormally during the rendezvous burn with Salyut 6 (the redundant backup engine had to be used for a ballistic re-entry burn), and the crew returned to Earth 1979-04-12 without docking. Ivanov landed safely but the failed mission left a programmatic shadow on Bulgarian human spaceflight for **9 years 1 month 28 days**.

Aleksandrov-Belorussian's Soyuz TM-5 1988-06-07 visiting flight successfully docked Mir + completed 9d 20h Bulgarian-led scientific work: Spektr-256 Earth-observation spectrometer for atmospheric and surface mineralogy + ROZA-Mir biological adaptation experiment + Bulgarian cultural payload (Bulgarian icon + folk-art samples + Slavic-Cyrillic-script texts). **REDEMPTION-FLIGHT-AFTER-FAILED-FIRST-ATTEMPT** + **9-YEAR-INTERKOSMOS-PROGRAMME-GAP-CLOSURE** + **SECOND-BULGARIAN-COSMONAUT** substrate-forms all NEW LOCKED at v682.

**Patronymic-collision-disambiguation:** Aleksandrov-Belorussian is **NOT** the same individual as Aleksandr Pavlovich Aleksandrov the Soviet cosmonaut who flew v680 Soyuz TM-3 + v681 EO-1 return. The two share the given-name "Aleksandr" but have different patronymics (**Pavlovich** Soviet vs **Panayotov** Bulgarian), different nationalities, different career arcs. **Commander Col. Anatoli Yakovlevich Solovyev** (born 1948-01-16 Riga Latvian SSR; still living 2026) — Soviet Air Force; rookie first flight at age 40. Subsequent career: Soyuz TM-9 EO-6 1990 (179d) + Soyuz TM-15 EO-12 1992-1993 (188d) + STS-71 Atlantis 1995 (10d as first Shuttle-Mir docking commander) + Soyuz TM-26 EO-24 1997-1998 (197d). **Cumulative 651.8 days in space — at career close 5th most space-experienced cosmonaut overall.** Twice Hero of the Soviet Union + Hero of the Russian Federation.

**Flight Engineer Civ. Viktor Petrovich Savinykh** (born 1940-03-07 Berezkiny Kirov Oblast Russian SFSR; still living 2026) — civilian Soviet cosmonaut; 3rd career flight. Previous flights: Soyuz T-4 EO-1 Salyut 6 1981 (74d) + **Soyuz T-13 1985 Salyut 7 rescue mission with Dzhanibekov** (168d; restarted dead Salyut 7 after total power-system failure).
