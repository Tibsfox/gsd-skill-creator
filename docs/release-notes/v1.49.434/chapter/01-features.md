## Largest tern ecology


- the Caspian Tern is the world's largest tern at any latitude; in Washington it is a breeding species at the Columbia River mouth and a summer forager on Puget Sound; it feeds primarily on fish, plunge-diving in the manner of Sterna terns but with greater impact force due to its size; it also kleptoparasitizes smaller terns, taking fish they have caught -- a feeding strategy that places it in competition with the Forster's Tern, Common Tern, and other Laridae sharing the same estuarine habitat; its vocalizations are used in territorial defense and pair maintenance, and the *ka-arr* is its primary contact and alarm call
