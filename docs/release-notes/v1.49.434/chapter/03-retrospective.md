## Lessons Learned

1. **Second-wave arrivals warrant first-class structural documentation.** Documenting Swallow as a Sub Pop wave mid-wave entry rather than as a supplementary note to the founding bands validates the principle that second-wave and wave-era bands carry independent documentary significance. The wave extended the scene's breadth, the wave bands signed to Sub Pop during the label's peak authority period, and the engine's documentation preserves the wave register at first-class resolution rather than treating it as a footnote.
2. **Bounded episodes within broader categories preserve both the episode and the category registers.** The 1989-1992 pre-commercial artisan era within the broader Sub Pop catalog and the Caspian Tern's Laridae return within the broader Laridae family both demonstrate that the engine's documentation vocabulary can register bounded episodes without collapsing them into the broader category and without requiring the broader category to be re-established at each episode boundary.
3. **Family depth can be registered through genus-outlier entries as well as through arc-length sequences.** The Caspian Tern's *Hydroprogne* monotypic genus designation captures the species' outlier position within Laridae, and the engine's single-degree Laridae return at degree 293 demonstrates that the family has depth beyond the four-degree 289-292 arc. The engine uses that depth by registering the outlier species at a distinct degree rather than by extending the arc.
4. **Amplitude calibration distinguishes driving-but-bounded registers from peak-event registers.** Swallow's E=7 grunge/punk is distinct from Mudhoney's founding-band E=8 and from the hardcore E=9 streak, and the Caspian Tern's E=6 single-call register is distinct from the Common Tern colony's E=7 peak. The engine's E-scale preserves these distinctions at first-class resolution rather than collapsing them into a single high-amplitude category.
5. **Transition-edge degrees preserve both incoming and outgoing registers at equivalent structural resolution.** Degree 293 as the transition edge before the proto-grunge arc (294-296) and before the Alcidae family opening (294) demonstrates that transition degrees warrant first-class structural recognition, and the positioning preserves both the incoming wave-era and Laridae-return registers and the outgoing proto-grunge and Alcidae-opening registers.
6. **International press coverage can establish documentary visibility independent of commercial visibility.** The NME and Melody Maker coverage of Sub Pop wave bands including Swallow established international documentary visibility during the bands' active periods even when American commercial visibility was limited, and the engine's documentation reflects the international-press-documented reality rather than the American-commercial-visibility reality.
7. **Outlier scale is a structurally distinct documentary position.** The Caspian Tern's genus-outlier scale and Swallow's edge-position in the Sub Pop ecosystem are both outlier positions within their respective categories, and the engine's documentation registers outlier positions at first-class resolution rather than restricting first-class documentation to central or definitional examples.
8. **Cross-sync axes name documentation modes rather than describe incidental pairings.** The second-wave-arrival cross-sync axis at degree 293 names the structural principle that organizes both halves of the release rather than describing an incidental pairing of a band with a species. Naming the axis makes the shared structural mode legible across cultural and biological registers and positions the paired documentation for cross-reference against other cross-sync axes in the engine's documentary record.
9. **Venue-network geography registers scene interconnection at precise resolution.** The Central Tavern, Vogue, Moore Theatre, and OK Hotel as the Seattle scene venue network registers the 1989-1992 Seattle scene's interconnection at the venue-geography level rather than at the generic-Seattle level. The engine's documentation vocabulary preserves precise venue geography at first-class resolution rather than abstracting scene geography into a single placeholder.
10. **Colony-site specificity registers seabird ecology at first-class resolution.** The East Sand Island colony at the Columbia River mouth, the Nisqually River delta, Commencement Bay, and the Duwamish mouth as specific Caspian Tern sites register the seabird ecology at the site level rather than at the generic-estuary level.
