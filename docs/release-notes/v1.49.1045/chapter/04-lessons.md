# Lessons — v1.49.1045

12 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **An axis can sustain on a new observing strategy, not just a new instrument.**
   The X-ray-astronomy axis sustains at obs#6 because Swift reads the high-energy sky in a way the five focused, timing, and polarimetry observatories could not — by catching a fleeting transient as it happens, autonomously, across three bands. The distinction is the observing strategy and the multi-wavelength reach, not a new detector class, so the cadence continues the axis as a substrate-form-distinct sustain rather than rotating away.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12097_

2. **A multi-wavelength mission can still belong to an X-ray axis.**
   Swift is the first multi-wavelength member of an otherwise X-ray-only axis. Its focusing X-ray telescope and its X-ray-afterglow and X-ray-transient science place it on the axis; the gamma-ray and UV/optical instruments are intrinsic to its form and are correct, not off-axis contamination.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12098_

3. **A first can hinge on the conjunction of its qualifiers.**
   Swift's defining first is not any phenomenon-first (Vela, BATSE, BeppoSAX, and HETE-2 hold those) — it is the rapid, autonomous, routine, multi-wavelength FORM, the conjunction of all four properties. The short-burst, distance-record, and tidal-disruption firsts each carry their own qualifiers (the X-ray afterglow of a SHORT burst; the most distant burst AT THE TIME; the first RELATIVISTIC, JETTED tidal disruption event).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12099_

4. **A focusing telescope on an axis of focusing telescopes still needs the resolution stated.**
   The XRT genuinely focuses X-rays, but at a modest ~18-arcsecond half-power diameter — about 36× coarser than Chandra's ~0.5 arcsec. Stating the modest resolution keeps "focusing" and "imaging" correct Swift content while never claiming Chandra's first-focusing or sub-arcsecond imaging.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12100_

5. **The rapid response opened three classes of transient.**
   Because the focusing telescope was on the burst within minutes, Swift settled the short-burst merger question (GRB 050509B and the old-population host), set the early-universe distance record (GRB 090423), and found a new kind of event (the relativistic jetted tidal disruption Swift J1644+57) — each a consequence of the autonomous slew getting the narrow-field telescopes on target while the event was still bright.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12101_

6. **A continuation keeps the predecessors' vocabulary and references them by name.**
   Because Swift sustains the X-ray-astronomy axis, the shared X-ray vocabulary is correct and kept, and Chandra (obs#1, soft/medium focusing), NuSTAR (obs#2, hard-band focusing), RXTE (obs#3, timing), IXPE (obs#4, polarimetry), and NICER (obs#5, soft-band timing) are referenced as the form complements — but Swift's own facts (the three co-pointed instruments, the autonomous slew, the early-afterglow era, the short-burst / distance-record / tidal-disruption science) and only its own five anchors are stamped; the predecessors' anchors and predecessor-specific hardware (NICER's X-ray Timing Instrument, concentrators, silicon drift detectors, SEXTANT, Gendreau and Arzoumanian, the ISS mounting; IXPE's Gas Pixel Detectors; RXTE's PCA/HEXTE; NuSTAR's deployable mast; Chandra's sub-arcsecond…
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12102_

7. **Accuracy guardrails guard against the stale-source and fabricated-citation classes of error.**
   Using the 20 November 2004 launch on a Delta II 7320-10C from Cape Canaveral Air Force Station SLC-17A (not the slipped 17 November date, not a Delta II Heavy, not Vandenberg, not "Space Force Station"); the descriptive (non-acronym) name and the 2018 rename in honor of Neil Gehrels (who died in 2017, a different year); the MIDEX-3 / Explorer 84 selection in 1999 at ~US$163M; the ~585–600 km design orbit; the BAT coded-mask imager (not focusing), the XRT JET-X flight-spare focusing optics with the XMM CCD heritage, and the UVOT eleven-position filter wheel; and only the verified references — keeps the facts load-bearing and correct.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12103_

8. **The operations status is a twenty-year body of work, not a live state.**
   A two-year prime baseline operated for more than twenty years, with a reaction-wheel failure in January 2022 and a gyroscope degradation in March 2024 kept distinct (and not conflated), and a 2025–2026 reduced-operations posture — described as the body of work rather than asserted as a present-tense "fully operational" or "shut down" status.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12104_

9. **The shader renders procedural geometry rather than archived data.**
   The gamma-ray-burst transient shader uses analytic curves and procedural noise rather than loading an actual Swift-XRT light curve or a BAT coded-mask image. A future revision could render a published Swift X-ray afterglow light curve (the GRB 050509B or GRB 090423 curve) or a real coded-mask reconstruction for a higher-fidelity view of the data.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12105_

10. **The Swift diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the spacecraft with the co-aligned BAT, XRT, and UVOT in its observing configuration.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12106_

11. **The X-ray-astronomy-continuation sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.**
   The sidebar lists the axis continuation and the Swift mission elements (the three instruments, the autonomous slew, the science highlights) but does not enumerate the full roster of Swift results or the prospective obs#7+ continuations; a future revision could expand the table.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12107_

12. **The afterglow physics is described rather than computed.**
   The coded-mask deconvolution, the autonomous-slew time budget, the canonical light-curve power-law segments, and the redshift–distance relation are described but not run against published Swift event data; a future ship could process a real Swift observation to reconstruct a coded-mask image or fit an afterglow light curve directly.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12108_
