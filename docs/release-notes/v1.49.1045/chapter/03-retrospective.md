## Surprises

- **A wide-field trigger and a rapid, autonomous slew open a whole class of science.** Swift adds no sharper image and no new observable; by watching a sixth of the sky for a flash and then repointing its own narrow-field telescopes onto the new position within about a minute, with no human in the loop, it reads the early multi-wavelength afterglow of a cosmic explosion while it is still fading — and that single capability settled the short-burst merger question, set the early-universe distance record, and found the relativistic jetted tidal disruption event.
- **A short burst's host was an old elliptical galaxy.** GRB 050509B's X-ray afterglow sat at the edge of a luminous, non-star-forming elliptical at z=0.225 — an old stellar population with no massive young stars to collapse, which (with GRB 050724 and HETE-2's GRB 050709) made the case that short bursts come from compact-object mergers rather than the collapse that powers the long bursts, proved definitively in 2017 by GW170817.
- **A jumping spider already hunts the way Swift observes.** *Habronattus oregonensis* detects a fleeting target's motion with its wide-field secondary eyes, then rapidly swivels the movable retinae of its high-acuity, colour- and ultraviolet-sensitive principal eyes onto the target to examine it before the pounce — a wide-field motion trigger, a rapid autonomous reorientation, then a narrow-field, multi-wavelength examination, exactly Swift's BAT-trigger / autonomous-slew / XRT-and-UVOT pipeline.
- **A small annual fires its seeds the moment it is touched.** The Little Western Bittercress, the Pacific Northwest native called "shotweed," holds its ripe seed pods under tension and ballistically flings their seeds the instant a fleeting touch sets them off — an explosive, autonomous release triggered by a brief external event, mirror to Swift's rapid, self-actuated response to a transient trigger: a fleeting event firing an immediate response, the touch the burst and the seed launch the slew.

## Lessons Learned

1. **An axis can sustain on a new observing strategy, not just a new instrument.** The X-ray-astronomy axis sustains at obs#6 because Swift reads the high-energy sky in a way the five focused, timing, and polarimetry observatories could not — by catching a fleeting transient as it happens, autonomously, across three bands. The distinction is the observing strategy and the multi-wavelength reach, not a new detector class, so the cadence continues the axis as a substrate-form-distinct sustain rather than rotating away.
2. **A multi-wavelength mission can still belong to an X-ray axis.** Swift is the first multi-wavelength member of an otherwise X-ray-only axis. Its focusing X-ray telescope and its X-ray-afterglow and X-ray-transient science place it on the axis; the gamma-ray and UV/optical instruments are intrinsic to its form and are correct, not off-axis contamination.
3. **A first can hinge on the conjunction of its qualifiers.** Swift's defining first is not any phenomenon-first (Vela, BATSE, BeppoSAX, and HETE-2 hold those) — it is the rapid, autonomous, routine, multi-wavelength FORM, the conjunction of all four properties. The short-burst, distance-record, and tidal-disruption firsts each carry their own qualifiers (the X-ray afterglow of a SHORT burst; the most distant burst AT THE TIME; the first RELATIVISTIC, JETTED tidal disruption event).
4. **A focusing telescope on an axis of focusing telescopes still needs the resolution stated.** The XRT genuinely focuses X-rays, but at a modest ~18-arcsecond half-power diameter — about 36× coarser than Chandra's ~0.5 arcsec. Stating the modest resolution keeps "focusing" and "imaging" correct Swift content while never claiming Chandra's first-focusing or sub-arcsecond imaging.
5. **The rapid response opened three classes of transient.** Because the focusing telescope was on the burst within minutes, Swift settled the short-burst merger question (GRB 050509B and the old-population host), set the early-universe distance record (GRB 090423), and found a new kind of event (the relativistic jetted tidal disruption Swift J1644+57) — each a consequence of the autonomous slew getting the narrow-field telescopes on target while the event was still bright.
6. **A continuation keeps the predecessors' vocabulary and references them by name.** Because Swift sustains the X-ray-astronomy axis, the shared X-ray vocabulary is correct and kept, and Chandra (obs#1, soft/medium focusing), NuSTAR (obs#2, hard-band focusing), RXTE (obs#3, timing), IXPE (obs#4, polarimetry), and NICER (obs#5, soft-band timing) are referenced as the form complements — but Swift's own facts (the three co-pointed instruments, the autonomous slew, the early-afterglow era, the short-burst / distance-record / tidal-disruption science) and only its own five anchors are stamped; the predecessors' anchors and predecessor-specific hardware (NICER's X-ray Timing Instrument, concentrators, silicon drift detectors, SEXTANT, Gendreau and Arzoumanian, the ISS mounting; IXPE's Gas Pixel Detectors; RXTE's PCA/HEXTE; NuSTAR's deployable mast; Chandra's sub-arcsecond optics) stay with their missions.
