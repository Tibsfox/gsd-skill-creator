# Lessons — v1.49.1023

11 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **An axis caps when a third observation completes the range of a way of seeing.**
   By carrying the orbital exploration of a world from Venus and Mercury out to the small bodies of the belt, Dawn brought the planetary axis to a third, substrate-form-distinct observation and capped it at obs#3, showing that an axis earns a third entry and then rotates away rather than running indefinitely.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11926_

2. **Patient continuous thrust opens missions chemical rockets cannot fly.**
   By making its long journey on the gentle, tireless push of xenon ion engines, Dawn built up an enormous change in velocity over years and was able to orbit and then depart one body for another, showing that the choice of propulsion can open a class of mission — the multi-world orbital tour — that a chemical rocket could not.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11927_

3. **One explorer can compare two worlds at close range.**
   By orbiting Vesta and then climbing out of its gravity to orbit Ceres, Dawn let a single mission read two very different ancient bodies up close, showing that a body-to-body orbital tour brackets the range of a population better than two separate missions could.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11928_

4. **Orbit turns a distant point of light into a charted world.**
   By circling the two largest bodies of the belt where no spacecraft had orbited a belt body before, Dawn produced the first global image maps of Vesta and of Ceres, showing that staying in orbit is what makes a small distant body a mapped place rather than a glimpse.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11929_

5. **A surface can connect a distant body to rocks already in our collections.**
   By finding at Vesta a surface that matches the HED family of meteorites found on Earth, Dawn tied a body in the belt to fragments already in our hands, showing that orbital mineralogy can link a far world to the laboratory.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11930_

6. **An icy dwarf world can keep a record of water and salts.**
   By mapping the bright carbonate salt deposits in Occator crater, the lone mountain Ahuna Mons, and the signs of a briny interior at Ceres, Dawn read a small world that preserved a record of water and salts near its surface, showing that the smallest round worlds can carry an active geologic history.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11931_

7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across an intra-axis continuation.**
   Because Dawn reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.213 MESSENGER template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content from the Mercury orbiter to the Vesta-and-Ceres orbiter while keeping the warm palette, making the intra-axis obs#3 build a clean intra-template rewrite.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11932_

8. **The shader renders procedural structure rather than archived data.**
   The Dawn protoplanet-mapping shader uses fractal noise and analytic geometry rather than loading actual Framing Camera global mosaics, VIR mineral maps, or GRaND composition products from NASA's Planetary Data System. A future revision could load encoded Dawn imagery and composition products for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to real terrain, the Rheasilvia basin, the Occator salts, and Ahuna Mons.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11933_

9. **The ion-propelled-orbiter diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the spacecraft, the large solar arrays, the ion thrusters, and the two-world orbital tour.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11934_

10. **The Dawn-mission lineage sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.**
   The sidebar lists the planetary-axis and Dawn-mission elements (Dawn, Vesta, Ceres) but does not enumerate the full history of asteroid-belt exploration or the per-instrument coverage; a future revision could expand the table with the complete small-body mission roster and the per-instrument data products.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11935_

11. **The trajectory and gravity models are described rather than computed.**
   The low-thrust ion-propulsion trajectory and the gravity-field interior models are described but not run against the archived data; a future ship could process real Dawn tracking data to demonstrate the body-to-body transfer and the recovered interior structure directly.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11936_
