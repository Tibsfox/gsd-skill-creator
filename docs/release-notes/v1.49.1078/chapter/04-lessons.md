3. **CGRO is managed by NASA Goddard (GSFC) — NOT JPL/IPAC, Marshall/CXC-SAO, or STScI.**
   It is a NASA mission managed by NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center (GSFC, Greenbelt MD), with instrument teams at GSFC, NASA Marshall (MSFC), the Naval Research Laboratory, and international partners (the Max Planck Institute) — a different management center from Spitzer's JPL/IPAC, Chandra's Marshall/CXC-SAO, and Hubble's STScI; the catalog states this precisely throughout, as a correct intra-axis distinction (each Great Observatory had a different management center).

4. **CGRO launched aboard the Space Shuttle Atlantis on 5 April 1991 and was deployed into a low Earth orbit.**
   It launched 5 April 1991 aboard the Space Shuttle Atlantis on mission STS-37 — NOT an expendable rocket — and was deployed from the orbiter on 7 April 1991 (after a spacewalk freed a stuck high-gain antenna) into a low Earth orbit (about 450 km, below the Van Allen radiation belts), boosted periodically against atmospheric drag; the catalog states this precisely.

7. **BATSE's first all-sky GRB SURVEY showing isotropy is distinct from the first detection of GRBs; EGRET's first complete all-sky high-energy map is distinct from the first gamma-ray telescope.**
   BATSE made the first all-sky survey of gamma-ray bursts and found them isotropic — distinct from the first detection of GRBs by the Vela satellites (1967–1973); and EGRET produced the first complete all-sky map in high-energy gamma rays (above ~100 MeV) — distinct from the first gamma-ray telescope (SAS-2/COS-B) and from COS-B's first complete map of the Galactic plane. Both distinctions are held load-bearing.
