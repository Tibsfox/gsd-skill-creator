# Lessons — v1.49.1017

11 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **A wide survey of the nearest, brightest targets finds the most studiable worlds.**
   By surveying almost the whole sky's nearest bright stars rather than one deep field of faint distant ones, TESS found the worlds whose masses can be weighed and whose atmospheres can be read, showing that breadth and proximity together pick out the targets best suited to detailed study.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11860_

2. **A survey that hands its yield as a catalog multiplies its reach.**
   By producing a catalog of TESS Objects of Interest for the whole community, TESS turned its own discoveries into the source list for ground-based and space-based follow-up, showing that a survey's value grows when its yield is handed onward as a working target list.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11861_

3. **A bright host star is what makes detailed study possible.**
   By concentrating on bright nearby stars, TESS made its worlds amenable to the radial-velocity mass measurement and the transmission-spectroscopy atmosphere reading, showing that the brightness and proximity of a host star, not just the detection of a world, is what opens the world to detailed study.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11862_

4. **A resonant orbit can stay stable for decades on its own.**
   By flying a highly elliptical high-Earth orbit in a two-to-one resonance with the Moon, TESS gained long, uninterrupted views of each sky sector with little fuel, showing that a well-chosen resonance turns the Moon's gravity into a free, lasting stabilizer.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11863_

5. **An axis sustains at a substrate-form-distinct second observation.**
   By remaking the transit method as an all-sky survey of the nearest bright stars, TESS sustained the exoplanet-detection axis at obs#2 distinct from Kepler's single-deep-field census, showing that an INTRA-AXIS continuation extends an axis by giving its established method a new form.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11864_

6. **A small host star deepens a small world's transit.**
   Because TESS favors small, cool dwarf stars, a world of a given size makes a deeper, easier-to-measure dip against them, showing that the choice of host stars can make small worlds easier to find, not harder.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11865_

7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across an INTRA-AXIS continuation.**
   Because TESS reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.207 Kepler template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content to the all-sky survey while keeping the warm palette, making the axis-continuation a clean build.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11866_

8. **The shader renders procedural structure rather than archived data.**
   The TESS all-sky-transit-survey shader uses analytic geometry and procedural noise rather than loading actual TESS light curves from the Mikulski Archive for Space Telescopes. A future revision could load encoded TESS light curves for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to real transit signals.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11867_

9. **The four-camera-spacecraft-and-orbit diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the four-camera spacecraft, the camera strip over a hemisphere, and the lunar-resonant orbit.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11868_

10. **The observatory sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.**
   The sidebar lists representative axis-lineage and observatory elements (TESS, Kepler, IRAS, WISE) but does not enumerate the full set of TESS results; a future revision could expand the table with per-result highlights.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11869_

11. **The follow-up advantage is described rather than computed.**
   The radial-velocity and transmission-spectroscopy relations are described but not run against the archived catalog; a future ship could query a real TESS light curve and a real bright-star candidate and estimate a mass-measurement or atmosphere-reading feasibility directly.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11870_
