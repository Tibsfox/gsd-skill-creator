## Osceola Mudflow


- the Osceola Mudflow of approximately 5,600 years ago is the largest lahar in Mount Rainier's documented Holocene history; it traveled 70 miles from Rainier's summit to Puget Sound; it deposited up to 600 feet of volcanic debris in the upper Puyallup valley and created the alluvial plain on which the modern communities of Orting, Puyallup, Fife, and portions of Tacoma now stand; the Osceola event is not a historical curiosity -- it is the calibration event for current lahar hazard modeling in the Puyallup River watershed; the city of Orting is the primary concern in lahar planning because it sits in the valley bottom, 35 miles from Rainier, with a single road in and out; the Pierce County Emergency Management lahar evacuation plan is one of the most detailed volcanic emergency plans in the United States, and it is based on the Osceola profile
