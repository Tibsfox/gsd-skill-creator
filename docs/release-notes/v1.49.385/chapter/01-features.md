## FIRST ACIPENSERIDAE


- the White Sturgeon is the engine's first entry from the family Acipenseridae, the sturgeon family; the previous degree's Pacific Lamprey (450MYA) and this degree's White Sturgeon (200MYA) constitute back-to-back ancient fish entries that reach into deep evolutionary time; the Acipenseridae body plan -- the heterocercal tail, the bony scutes, the rostrum, the four barbels -- has been conserved across 200 million years of Earth history that included the extinction of the dinosaurs, the collision of India with Asia, the opening of the Atlantic Ocean, and the full duration of the Cenozoic Era; the engine documents not just the Pacific Northwest but the deep evolutionary history of the organisms that inhabit it
