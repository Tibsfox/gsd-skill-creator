# v1.49.373 -- Degree 232: Gruntruck + Prairie Falcon

**Release Date:** 2026-04-03

## Summary

**Gruntruck** arrives at E=7 -- the Seattle hard rock band that formed from the ashes of Skin Yard and brought guitarist Ben McMillan into a project that would eventually produce one of the harder-edged records in the mid-era Seattle catalog. McMillan (also of Skin Yard) and guitarist Turner Van Blarcum led a band on **Roadrunner Records** -- the first appearance of that label in the engine -- that pushed closer to metal than most of the Seattle contemporaries who were softening for mainstream appeal. Where Pond was melodic and Flop was hook-driven, Gruntruck was blunt: guitar-forward, rhythm-heavy, not interested in shoegaze or psychedelia or the R.E.M. college-radio lineage that influenced some of its peers. At E=7, Gruntruck matches the energy of Gas Huffer (degree 228) and Alcohol Funnycar (degree 229), contributing to the elevated band of E=7 readings that has characterized this section of the engine. **Roadrunner Records** enters as a new node in the label map: the Dutch-American metal and hard rock label that in the same period signed Type O Negative, Sepultura, Machine Head, and Fear Factory, documenting that Seattle's hard rock bands had connections into the wider metal ecosystem that ran through New York and Rotterdam rather than just through Sub Pop's offices on First Avenue. On the species side, the **Prairie Falcon** arrives at E=6, the second Falconidae in consecutive degrees and a bird that shares the Peregrine's open-sky hunting style while operating in a completely different landscape. The Prairie Falcon (*Falco mexicanus*) is a **canyon falcon** -- a bird of the arid interior West, of the Columbia Basin's rimrock coulees, the shrub-steppe canyons, the basalt cliffs above the Yakima River. It is a bird the engine finds not on the Sound's shoreline or in the Douglas fir forests of the western slope, but in the dry eastern half of Washington -- confirming the geographic expansion that began with the Ferruginous Hawk at degree 229. The Prairie Falcon's *kik-ki-ki* alarm is a rapid, assertive declaration from a bird that hunts Horned Larks and ground squirrels across vast open terrain. Where the Peregrine is a vertical specialist, a stoop-and-strike artist, the Prairie Falcon is a horizontal hunter, a low-altitude pursuer that chases prey at speed across the ground. Two falcons, same family, entirely different tactical philosophies.

## Key Features

- **Part A -- S36:** Gruntruck (Hard Rock, E=7) -- ROADRUNNER RECORDS ENTERS, Skin Yard lineage, Ben McMillan, metal-adjacent Seattle hard rock
- **Part B -- SPS:** Prairie Falcon (kik-ki-ki, E=6) -- canyon falcon, Columbia Basin rimrock, horizontal hunter vs. Peregrine's vertical stoop, Falconidae arc continues
- Roadrunner Records enters -- the label's Seattle connection through Gruntruck documents the band's place in the wider metal ecosystem of the early 1990s; Roadrunner was founded in the Netherlands in 1980 as a metal imprint and established its New York office in 1987; by the time it signed Gruntruck, it was the most significant metal-aligned independent label in the world; its presence in the engine alongside Sub Pop, Empty Records, Frontier Records, Epitaph, and Estrus maps the full range of the independent label universe that Seattle bands inhabited
- Prairie Falcon ecology -- the Prairie Falcon is Washington's resident falcon of the interior: it breeds on basalt cliff ledges in the Columbia Basin, the channeled scablands, and the Okanogan Highlands, and winters in the same terrain plus agricultural valley floors; unlike the Peregrine, which now also nests on urban high-rises, the Prairie Falcon remains a strictly wild-landscape species in Washington, rarely appearing west of the Cascades; its prey base is primarily small mammals and birds of open ground: Horned Larks, Western Meadowlarks, ground squirrels, and jackrabbits; it hunts low, fast, and laterally, closing on prey at angles the Peregrine would never attempt
- Hunting strategy contrast -- the Peregrine (degree 231) and the Prairie Falcon (degree 232) are the only two consecutive Falconidae in the engine and they document fundamentally different hunting strategies within the same family; the Peregrine stoops vertically from altitude, converting gravitational potential into kinetic energy; the Prairie Falcon pursues horizontally, using sustained powered flight and low angles; both are elite aerial predators, but the ecological conditions that produced them were different -- vertical ocean and coastal cliffs vs. horizontal open grassland -- and the divergence is visible in their body shapes: the Peregrine is heavier-chested for the stoop, the Prairie Falcon is longer-winged and lighter for the chase
- Hard rock cluster forming -- degrees 228 (Gas Huffer, E=7), 229 (Alcohol Funnycar, E=7), 232 (Gruntruck, E=7) form a cluster of three E=7 hard/punk rock entries in a five-degree window, the densest concentration of maximum-register S36 readings since the Blood Brothers at degree 218 (E=8); the cluster creates a plateau in the S36 track that will descend progressively through the Falconidae arc's musical partners
