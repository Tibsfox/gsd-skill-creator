## Oso, March 22, 2014


- the Oso landslide occurred at approximately 10:37 AM on a Saturday morning; the hillside that failed, known as the Hazel or Oso landslide site, had a documented history of previous failures -- geologists had identified the slope as unstable -- but the scale and speed of the 2014 event exceeded previous events by orders of magnitude; the slide crossed the North Fork Stillaguamish River, blocked it, and inundated a rural neighborhood; the Stillaguamish Tribe of Indians lost several members; rescue and recovery operations continued for weeks; 43 deaths make the Oso slide the deadliest single landslide event in United States recorded history; the engine treats this entry with the respect that 43 deaths and a devastated community require

## Acoustic record


- a mudslide at Oso's scale produces a deep, low-frequency rumble: the sound of saturated earth in rapid motion, the bass-frequency propagation of millions of cubic yards of material crossing a valley; survivors reported hearing the slide before it reached them; that sound -- the warning that came too late -- is what E=8 in the SPS track attempts to represent; it is not a score of aesthetics; it is a measure of acoustic energy; the Oso mudslide was an E=8 acoustic event
