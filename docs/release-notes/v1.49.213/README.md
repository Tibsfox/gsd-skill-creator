### STRUCTURAL EVENT: K RECORDS ENTERS -- The Heart of the International Pop Underground

Degree 73 introduces **K Records** into the engine. This is not merely another label entry. K Records is arguably the **most culturally significant independent label in Pacific Northwest history** -- more foundational than Sub Pop to the philosophy of independent music, more radical than Kill Rock Stars in its vision of what music could be. K Records is the heart of the **International Pop Underground**.

**K Records** was founded in **1982** by **Calvin Johnson** in **Olympia, Washington**. Johnson, the singer of **Beat Happening**, built K Records as more than a label -- it was a philosophy. The philosophy: music should be accessible to everyone, not just trained musicians. Anyone can make music. Anyone should make music. The pop song is a democratic form. Lo-fi production is not a limitation but a choice -- a rejection of the gatekeeping that professional studios and major labels impose.

The significance of K Records in the broader history of independent music cannot be overstated:

1. **The International Pop Underground Convention (1991).** K Records organized this five-day festival in Olympia, one week before Nirvana's *Nevermind* dropped. The convention's opening night was "Love Rock Revolution Girl Style Now" -- the event that crystallized riot grrrl as a movement. Bikini Kill, Bratmobile, and Heavens to Betsy performed. K Records did not just participate in the Pacific Northwest independent music revolution -- it sparked a parallel revolution that was quieter, warmer, and more radical.

2. **The K Records philosophy: anti-corporate, pro-community.** Where Sub Pop cultivated a brand identity and courted major-label distribution deals, K Records remained deliberately small. Calvin Johnson hand-numbered releases. The label operated from domestic spaces. K Records was not trying to become a bigger business. It was trying to prove that music did not need to be a business at all.

3. **The Beat Happening influence.** Calvin Johnson's band **Beat Happening** (1983-1992) was the K Records blueprint: three people who could barely play their instruments making jangly, minimal, deeply affecting pop songs. Beat Happening proved that technical limitation was not a barrier to great songwriting.

4. **The roster.** K Records has released music by **Beck** (early work), **Built to Spill** (early releases), **The Microphones** (Phil Elverum, degree 71), **Modest Mouse** (early singles), **Bikini Kill**, **Tiger Trap**, **Mirah**, **The Blow**, and dozens of other artists who defined Pacific Northwest independent music.

K Records is the **second Olympia label** in the engine after Kill Rock Stars (degree 70). KRS and K Records are the two pillars of Olympia's independent music culture: KRS (founded 1991, Slim Moon) is the political/punk pillar -- riot grrrl, Sleater-Kinney, political independence as aesthetic. K Records (founded 1982, Calvin Johnson) is the pop/communal pillar -- lo-fi sweetness, democratic music-making, the International Pop Underground. Same city. Different philosophies. Complementary visions. Olympia's two labels now both reside in the engine.

### THE LYNX-HARE CYCLE: ECOLOGY'S MOST FAMOUS PREDATOR-PREY DYNAMIC

The Snowshoe Hare is one half of the most famous predator-prey relationship in ecology:

- **The cycle:** Hare populations oscillate on an approximately **10-year cycle**. Population rises from ~1-2 hares per hectare to ~15-25 per hectare -- 10-25 fold increase -- then crashes. Documented for over 200 years through Hudson's Bay Company fur records.
- **The Canada Lynx follows:** Specialist predator (60-97% diet hares). As hares rise, lynx reproduce. When hares crash, lynx crash 1-2 years later. Coupled, time-lagged oscillation.
- **The textbook example:** The most commonly cited example of Lotka-Volterra predator-prey dynamics. The classic oscillating curves from fur records appear in every introductory ecology course.
- **Complexity:** Not purely predator-driven. Food availability (overbrowsing) and stress physiology also contribute.

The hare is a **keystone prey species** -- the foundation of the boreal and montane forest food web:

Predators: Canada Lynx (specialist, 60-97%), Coyote, Red Fox (SPS-071), American Marten (SPS-065), Great Horned Owl, Northern Goshawk, Golden Eagle, Bobcat, Ermine (SPS-067), Fisher (SPS-066).

### THE SNOWSHOE FEET

The defining physical feature:

- **Structure:** Hind feet disproportionately large (117-150 mm, nearly a third of body length). In winter, densely furred with stiff hairs that spread on snow, creating a natural snowshoe effect.
- **Function:** Distributes weight across larger surface area, preventing sinking into soft snow. Critical advantage over predators -- a lynx or coyote sinks deeper.
- **Seasonal:** Foot fur thickens in winter, enhancing the snowshoe effect.

### PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION

- **Length:** 363-520 mm total. Ears: 62-70 mm (moderate for a hare -- shorter than jackrabbits). Hind foot: 117-150 mm -- proportionally very large.
- **Weight:** 1.0-2.3 kg. Lighter than the Opossum (72, 1.8-6.4 kg) and much lighter than the Raccoon (70, 3.5-9.0 kg).
- **Speed:** Up to 45 km/h in short bursts. Zigzag through dense brush, using runway system knowledge.

### BEHAVIORAL ECOLOGY

- **Crepuscular/nocturnal.** Rests during day in shallow surface "form" under dense cover. No burrow.
- **Home range:** 2-8 hectares, overlapping, not territorial.
- **Runway system:** Network of well-worn trails through brush, used by multiple hares, providing known escape routes.
- **Reproduction:** 2-4 litters per year, 1-8 leverets per litter, precocial young. At peak density, 15-20 leverets per female per year.
- **Diet:** Strictly herbivorous. Summer: grasses, forbs, clovers. Winter: twigs, bark, buds of willow, birch, alder.
