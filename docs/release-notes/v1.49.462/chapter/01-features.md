- the Pacific Chorus Frog's breeding chorus is among the most acoustically prominent spring phenomena in the Pacific Northwest lowlands: male frogs congregate at ponds, marshes, and ditches from late January through April, producing chorus densities that can exceed painful decibel levels (90+ dB at pond edge) and that are audible for hundreds of meters; the species breeds in ephemeral ponds and roadside ditches that are often fish-free, maximizing tadpole survival; the frog's *krek-ek* call is the archetypal Pacific Northwest spring sound, so common that it has become acoustically invisible to longtime residents
