- **Pacific Chorus Frog Is the Dominant Layer in Density and Volume.** *Pseudacris regilla* (Baird and Girard, 1852), family Hylidae, is the most abundant and widespread frog in the Pacific Northwest. The range extends from British Columbia through Baja California and eastward to Montana, and the species occupies almost any still or slow-moving water: ponds, marshes, roadside ditches, and flooded fields from sea level up to 10,000 feet elevation. The small body length of 1.9–5 cm belies the species' acoustic output — a single male produces a call that carries 100+ meters, and at a productive breeding pond in April, 50–500 individuals may be calling simultaneously.
