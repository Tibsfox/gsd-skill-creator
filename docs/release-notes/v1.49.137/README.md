## Summary

The sustained tone. Wayne Horvitz at 2 degrees and the Varied Thrush at 2 degrees -- both occupying the position where a single note, held in space, teaches more than a cascade ever could. Where degree 0 was silence (Great Blue Heron plus Quincy Jones) and degree 1 was a torrent of complexity (Pacific Wren plus Bill Frisell), degree 2 pulls back to one note, perfectly placed, and lets it resonate. The circle does not proceed from simple to complex. It proceeds from silence to cascade to sustained tone. Complexity is not monotonic. The most haunting sound in the Pacific Northwest is one note, and the most haunting idea in improvised music is the composition that contains it. At degree 2 the Seattle 360 engine ships its first genuinely non-obvious structural insight, and it ships it in both halves of the paired release in parallel across twelve thousand years of ecological time and seventy years of cultural time. One note. Sustained. Both sides of the circle singing the same lesson at the same time.

Three data points now enable spectra rather than contrasts. The composition-improvisation spectrum becomes visible for the first time because three degrees exist: Quincy Jones fully composed (every note on paper, arranged and mixed), Wayne Horvitz composed improvisation (structurally complete compositions with designated openings for bounded improvisation), and Bill Frisell open-framework improvisation (loose compositional prompts functioning as launch pads). Three musicians, three positions on the axis, each at different poles of the same underlying discipline. Each one chose a different compromise with the same underlying tension between predetermined structure and emergent performance. The density spectrum also resolves at three points: the Great Blue Heron stands in silence at maximum sparsity, the Pacific Wren delivers the one-hundred-note cascade at maximum density, and the Varied Thrush occupies the middle ground with single sustained tones and the long silences between them. The circle oscillates between density and sparsity rather than climbing a ladder, and the oscillation pattern will repeat in every subsequent degree pair. The FM-AM modulation pair is the engine's first cross-cluster Rosetta connection: the Pacific Wren's frequency modulation at degree 1 matched by the Varied Thrush's amplitude modulation at degree 2, delivering in the first three biological degrees the same two fundamental synthesis methods that defined twentieth-century radio engineering (Edwin Armstrong's FM broadcasting, the prior AM broadcast standard the FM superseded) and electronic music (John Chowning's FM synthesis at Stanford, ring-modulation and AM synthesis on the Moog Modular, EMS VCS3, and ARP 2600). Independent research on different scales in parallel produced the same pair.

This release also introduces the artist-as-institution pattern at the community level, and the pattern comes in two flavors that the engine now tracks separately. Quincy Jones built industry: Mercury Records Vice President in 1964 (the first Black executive at the VP level at a major American label), Qwest Records founded 1980, production credits spanning Michael Jackson's *Off the Wall* (1979) and *Thriller* (1982), film scores for *In Cold Blood* (1967), *The Pawnbroker* (1964), and *The Color Purple* (1985). Wayne Horvitz builds community: The Royal Room co-founded in 2012 in Columbia City, the Seattle Improvised Music Festival curated since the 1990s, the New York Composers Orchestra co-founded with Bobby Previte in 1986. Bill Frisell builds neither -- he performs, records, and lets the music speak for itself, content to inhabit scenes other musicians built. Three degrees, three models of how musicians shape (or decline to shape) a city's cultural infrastructure. The Horvitz-Holcomb household on Capitol Hill -- Wayne Horvitz, Robin Holcomb (Elektra/Nonesuch artist, Appalachian folk meets minimalist composition), and their daughter -- operates as a creative partnership where each partner maintains a distinct artistic identity while sharing a harmonic language, and in parallel that household anchors the broader Seattle improvised-music community the way Jones's Qwest offices anchored the broader Los Angeles Black-music industry two decades earlier. Infrastructure shapes what music is possible. You cannot have a scene without a room. You cannot have a scene without records. You cannot have a scene without musicians willing to show up. The engine now tracks all three layers in parallel.

The Seattle Draw pattern acquires its third data point at degree 2 and begins to look less like a coincidence and more like a structural fact. Wayne Horvitz moved from New York City to Capitol Hill in 1988. Bill Frisell moved from Brooklyn to Seattle in 1989. Robin Holcomb moved with Horvitz in 1988. Three downtown-scene musicians chose the Pacific Northwest within a two-year window, leaving the cultural center (New York's Knitting Factory, Roulette, ABC No Rio, CBGB's matinee series, the loft spaces of Tribeca and SoHo) for a periphery that did not yet have a scene to join. Horvitz was not joining an existing community when he arrived on Capitol Hill in 1988 -- he was helping to build one. Where Frisell was content to perform and record, Horvitz took on the organizational labor: booking venues, curating concert series, mentoring younger musicians, writing grants, finding rehearsal spaces. The Royal Room opened in 2012 as the physical culmination of two decades of that organizational labor. Columbia City -- the neighborhood south of downtown, working-class, historically Black, ethnically diverse, geographically distant from the First Hill / Capitol Hill cultural nexus -- was a deliberate decentralization of the cultural map. Venues reshape maps. The Royal Room is the degree 2 equivalent of Mercury Records Vice President: an artist moving from making music to making the conditions under which music is made.

The Varied Thrush's song operates in the same register as Horvitz's composed improvisation on a biological scale, and at degree 2 the engine registers the structural resonance explicitly and in parallel across both cluster halves. A single, long, buzzy whistled note on one pitch, sustained for one to two seconds, followed by three to eight seconds of silence, then another note on a different pitch. No melodic contour within the note -- pitch set at onset and held. Successive notes differ by intervals from a minor second to a major third. One note every four to ten seconds. The polar opposite of the Pacific Wren at degree 1: where the wren delivers one hundred plus notes in ten seconds, the thrush delivers one. The wren is John Coltrane's sheets of sound; the thrush is Miles Davis playing one note and letting it ring. Cornell Lab, National Audubon, David Sibley (2000), and Wahl, Tweit, and Mlodinow (*Birds of Washington*, 2005) have each independently characterized this as "the most haunting sound in the Pacific Northwest forest," and each arrived at the description in parallel because the acoustic reality forces the observation. The buzzy quality of each note is produced by AM synthesis in the syrinx: a carrier tone at 2.5 to 5 kilohertz, superimposed with rapid amplitude modulation at 20 to 50 hertz, producing sideband frequencies at carrier plus-or-minus modulation. This is AM radio in biological form. Armstrong's FM superseded AM for broadcast because FM rejects amplitude noise; the Pacific Wren's song IS FM, the Varied Thrush's IS AM. Both synthesis methods that defined twentieth-century radio are represented in the first three degrees, each on its own species at its own degree, in parallel, synchronizing across different scales the same underlying mathematics.

The silence between the Varied Thrush's notes is compositional rather than incidental, and the same is true of the silence inside Wayne Horvitz's chamber works. The three-to-eight-second inter-note pause in the thrush's song allows the reverberant tail to decay in the dense canopy of Douglas Fir, Western Red Cedar, Western Hemlock, and Sitka Spruce, creates expectation for the next note, and is the frame that gives each tone its weight. In Japanese aesthetics the concept is *ma* -- the pregnant pause that gives meaning to what surrounds it. In recording engineering the parallel concept is pre-delay, the interval before the reverberant field builds. The thrush's silence is pre-delay in biology. Horvitz's Gravitas Quartet (piano, cello, bassoon, cornet) composes the same architectural silence into its chamber works on *Way Out East* (Songlines, 2006) and *Some Places Are Forever Afternoon* (National Sawdust Tracks, 2015). The Gravitas Quartet's through-composed pieces with designated improvisation sections produce the same effect Horvitz calls composed improvisation: the architecture is planned but certain rooms are left unfurnished. Peggy Lee, Sara Schoenbeck, and Ron Miles are the same thing Frisell and Horvitz represent in Pigpen -- collaborators who can hold structure and deliver improvisation in parallel at the same time on different instruments at different registers. Each side of the degree 2 resonance tells the same story: architecture is not the notes. It is the silence between them.

Five theoretical resonances connect the two halves of the paired release and each one runs in parallel through Part A and Part B together, matching the rubric of paired biological and cultural convergence the Seattle 360 engine is built to detect. First: the sustained tone as architecture. Horvitz's composed improvisation holds structure while leaving space for what happens inside it; the Varied Thrush holds a single frequency and lets the silence around it do the work. Both sides mirror the same principle. Second: atmospheric space. Both Horvitz's piano cluster voicings (adjacent seconds stacked in the middle register, Bartok and Ligeti lineage) and the thrush's buzzy whistle fill their acoustic environments with a single, sustained, weighted presence. Both are sustained tones. Fifth: the artist who builds infrastructure. Horvitz built The Royal Room as a deliberate decentralization of Seattle's cultural map; the Varied Thrush is territorial, returning to the same conifer territorial polygon across seasons, remapping the same forest acoustically each spring the way Horvitz remaps the Columbia City neighborhood programmatically each season. ## Part A: Wayne Horvitz -- Composed Improvisation

Full deep research covering the complete Wayne Horvitz arc:

- **NYC Downtown Origins:** Born 1955, New York City. UC Santa Cruz for composition, gamelan, electronic music, and free improvisation -- the permissive West Coast environment that planted his lifelong refusal of genre boundaries. Returned to New York in the late 1970s and became central to the downtown improvised music scene: the Knitting Factory, Roulette, CBGB's matinee series, ABC No Rio, the loft spaces of Tribeca and SoHo. The same ecosystem that nurtured Bill Frisell (degree 1), John Zorn, Bobby Previte, Marty Ehrlich, and Elliott Sharp in the same window.
- **Three Key Formations:** John Zorn's game pieces (*Cobra*, *Archery*, *Lacrosse* -- file-card compositions where the composer functions as a traffic controller, training instant compositional decision-making). The President -- Horvitz's own band with Elliott Sharp and Ben Street -- rock energy meets jazz harmony, not fusion but cohabitation. Pigpen -- trio with Bill Frisell and Kenny Wollesen -- country, free jazz, atmospheric electronics, the bridge between Horvitz's New York and Seattle lives. Also co-founded the New York Composers Orchestra (NYCO) with Bobby Previte, Wayne Shorter, Elliott Sharp, and Marty Ehrlich in 1986 -- institutional infrastructure for music that otherwise existed only in club settings.
- **The Move to Seattle (1988):** One year before Bill Frisell. Capitol Hill resident since. "The Seattle Draw" pattern now has its third data point: Horvitz (1988), Frisell (1989), and Robin Holcomb (1988) -- three downtown-scene musicians choosing the Pacific Northwest within a two-year window. Horvitz was not joining an existing community; he was helping to build one. Where Frisell was content to perform and record, Horvitz took on the organizational labor: booking venues, curating series, mentoring younger musicians, writing grants, finding rehearsal spaces.
- **Robin Holcomb:** Singer, pianist, composer, Horvitz's wife. Albums on Elektra/Nonesuch (*Robin Holcomb* 1990, *Rockabye* 1992, *Little Three* 2000). Appalachian folk meets art song meets minimalist composition meets jazz harmony. The Horvitz-Holcomb household on Capitol Hill represents a creative partnership where each partner maintains a distinct identity while sharing a harmonic language -- his dense cluster-voiced chords beneath her clear folk-inflected melodies. First partner-couple household tracked by the engine.
- **Seattle Work -- Zony Mash:** Primary Seattle group from the mid-1990s through the 2000s. Hammond organ grooves under guitar riffs with improvisational eruptions: *Brand Spankin' New* (1998, Intuition), *Upper Egypt* (2001, Intuition), *Cold Spell* (2005, Intuition). The bridge-building principle continues: Jones bridged jazz and pop through production, Frisell bridged jazz and Americana through improvisation, Horvitz bridges jazz and funk-rock through composed frameworks.
- **Seattle Work -- Gravitas Quartet:** Horvitz (piano), Peggy Lee (cello), Sara Schoenbeck (bassoon), Ron Miles (cornet). Chamber music, not jazz combo. Through-written compositions with designated improvisation sections -- the architecture is planned but certain rooms are left unfurnished. *Way Out East* (2006, Songlines) and *Some Places Are Forever Afternoon* (2015, National Sawdust Tracks). The border between composition and improvisation dissolves.
- **The Royal Room (2012):** Co-founded in Columbia City, a neighborhood south of downtown Seattle -- dedicated to creative music (jazz, improvised music, new composition, folk, experimental). Venue as infrastructure, curation as composition, Columbia City as deliberate decentralization of the cultural map. The degree 2 equivalent of Jones's Mercury Records VP appointment: an artist moving from making music to making the conditions under which music is made.
- **Music Theory Mapping:** Six theory nodes. Composed improvisation (the defining concept -- structurally complete compositions with designated openings for bounded improvisation). Polymetric structures (simultaneous time signatures -- bass in 4/4, guitar in 7/8, drums mediating). Cluster voicings (adjacent seconds stacked in the middle register, Bartok/Ligeti lineage). Quartal voicings (stacked fourths, McCoy Tyner lineage, open ambiguous sonority). Electronic-acoustic integration (processing piano / organ signals in real time, parallel to Frisell's signal-chain-as-instrument). Through-composed form meets improvisation (continuous arc with improvisation erupting at structural moments like springs through rock).
- **Density Spectrum:** Jones = maximum density (all registers filled). Horvitz = selective density (middle register dense with clusters). Frisell = maximum sparsity (registers left empty). Three degrees, three positions, one classifier for every subsequent S36 artist.
- **The Film Scoring Practice:** Horvitz scored independent films and documentaries throughout the 1990s and 2000s, and the film work reinforces the composed-improvisation methodology -- time-to-picture demands tight composition, but the picture rarely demands clean composition. Film score as the composed improvisation practice at its most constrained.

## Part B: Varied Thrush -- The Sustained Tone

Full deep research covering the Pacific Northwest forest's most haunting voice:

- **Taxonomy:** *Ixoreus naevius*, sole living member of the monotypic genus *Ixoreus* (Greek: *ixos* mistletoe plus *oreos* mountain), family Turdidae (thrushes), order Passeriformes. First monotypic genus in SPS. Separated from the American Robin (*Turdus migratorius*) by substantial molecular phylogenetic distance per Klicka, Voelker, and Spellman (2005, *Molecular Phylogenetics and Evolution*). Adult male: orange breast with bold black breastband (diagnostic field mark), blue-gray back, orange wing bars, orange supercilium, dark auricular patch. Length 7.5 to 10 inches, weight 65 to 100 grams -- roughly robin-sized.
- **Bioacoustics -- THE DEFINING SOUND OF THE PNW FOREST:** A single, long, buzzy whistled note on one pitch, sustained for one to two seconds, followed by three to eight seconds of silence, then another note on a different pitch. No melodic contour within the note -- pitch set at onset and held. Successive notes differ by intervals from a minor second to a major third. One note every four to ten seconds. The polar opposite of the Pacific Wren: where the wren delivers one hundred plus notes in ten seconds, the thrush delivers one. The wren is Coltrane's sheets of sound; the thrush is Miles Davis playing one note and letting it ring. Cornell Lab of Ornithology, National Audubon, David Sibley (2000), and Wahl, Tweit, and Mlodinow (2005) have each independently described this as "the most haunting sound in the Pacific Northwest forest."
- **Amplitude Modulation:** The buzzy quality is produced by AM synthesis -- the syrinx generates a carrier tone at 2.5 to 5 kilohertz, superimposed with rapid amplitude modulation at 20 to 50 hertz, producing sideband frequencies at carrier plus-or-minus modulation. This is AM radio in biological form: carrier wave modulated by information signal to produce complex output. Direct cross-reference to the AMR (AM Radio) research project in the Rosetta layer. Edwin Armstrong's FM superseded AM for broadcast because FM rejects amplitude noise -- the Pacific Wren's song IS FM, the Varied Thrush's IS AM. Both synthesis methods that defined twentieth-century radio are now represented in the engine's first three degrees, on different species, in parallel.
- **The Silence Between Notes:** The three-to-eight-second inter-note pause is compositional rather than incidental. It allows the reverberant tail to decay in the dense canopy, creates expectation for the next note, and is the frame that gives each tone its weight. In Japanese aesthetics: *ma* -- the pregnant pause that gives meaning to what surrounds it. In recording engineering: pre-delay -- allowing the direct sound to be heard before the reverberant field builds. The thrush's silence is pre-delay in biology.
- **Mature Conifer Forest Dependency:** Dense canopy closure above 70 percent -- Douglas Fir, Western Red Cedar, Western Hemlock, Sitka Spruce. Altitudinal migration: breeds in montane forest (2,000 to 5,000 feet elevation), descends to lowland Seattle in winter (October through April). Winter presence in Capitol Hill, Discovery Park, Seward Park, Washington Park Arboretum. The song of the Varied Thrush on a rainy January morning in Capitol Hill is one of the most distinctive sensory experiences of living in Seattle, and the overlap between the thrush's winter range and Horvitz's Capitol Hill home is not incidental -- both occupy the same neighborhood at the same time, in parallel, each singing in its own register.
- **Acoustic Propagation:** Song optimized for dense conifer forest. The 2.5 to 5 kilohertz band falls in the window of least attenuation -- below 2 kilohertz scatters from trunks, above 8 kilohertz attenuates from atmospheric absorption in humid Pacific Northwest air. Sustained duration (one to two seconds) provides resistance to masking by transient noise -- same principle as sonar, where a longer ping carries further. Inter-note silence allows the reverberant tail to decay fully, preventing inter-note interference.
- **Acoustic Theory Mapping:** Seven theory nodes. Sustained tone as drone / pedal point (Bach organ pedals, jazz pedal tones, electronic synth pads). AM timbral synthesis and ring modulation (Moog Modular, EMS VCS3, ARP 2600 parallels). Minimal melody (Webern's pointillism, La Monte Young's sustained tones, Steve Reich's minimalism). Inter-note silence as rhythmic structure (6 to 15 beats per minute -- hypometric, below human musical tempo, subjective rhythmization). Frequency as emotional register (2.5 to 5 kilohertz at peak human auditory sensitivity, descending pitch tendency, AM-produced roughness). The drone tradition mapped across Indian tanpura, Scottish bagpipe, organ pedal point, and Brian Eno's *Music for Airports* (1978).
- **Indigenous Cultural Context:** The Varied Thrush inhabits forests that are ancestral and continuing territories of the Coast Salish peoples -- Duwamish, Muckleshoot, Suquamish, and Snoqualmie. Research uses only publicly available sources; for nation-specific cultural context, primary sources are the relevant tribal cultural departments. The degree 2 acknowledgment is surface-level (blanket rather than nation-specific) and future degrees will need to deepen this to nation-specific attribution.
- **Conservation Status:** Currently listed as Least Concern on IUCN Red List, but Pacific Northwest populations show approximately 2 to 3 percent annual decline over the Breeding Bird Survey monitoring window -- correlated with old-growth logging, forest fragmentation, and climate-driven range shifts. The species is a conservation indicator for mature-canopy conifer forest health.

## The Degree 2 Parallel

The resonance between Wayne Horvitz and the Varied Thrush emerged on four independent axes in parallel:

1. **The sustained tone as architecture** -- Horvitz's composed improvisation holds structure while leaving space for what happens inside it; the thrush holds a single frequency and lets the silence around it do the work. Each side mirrors the same architectural principle.
2. **Atmospheric space** -- Horvitz's chamber works (*Some Places Are Forever Afternoon*) evoke specific landscapes and times of day; the Varied Thrush IS the acoustic landscape of the Pacific Northwest rain forest. Both encode place through sustained sound.
3. **AM / composed modulation** -- The thrush modulates amplitude on a carrier tone; Horvitz modulates improvisation on a composed structure. Both sides encode complexity onto a simple foundation through controlled variation in parallel.
4. **The note that hangs in the air** -- Horvitz's piano cluster voicings and the thrush's buzzy whistle both fill their acoustic environment with a single, sustained, weighted presence. Both are sustained tones. Each one teaches the same degree 2 lesson.

## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **Three points make a spectrum.** Two releases can only show contrast. Three reveal a continuum -- and the engine's first non-obvious insight (the circle is non-monotonic in complexity) emerges from having three data points on the composition-improvisation, density, and modulation axes in parallel.
- **FM and AM are the fundamental modulation pair.** Pacific Wren (FM) and Varied Thrush (AM) -- both represented in the first three degrees. The bioacoustic engine produced the same pair that John Chowning and Edwin Armstrong gave electronic music and radio engineering.
- **The artist-as-institution pattern tracks cultural impact beyond the music itself.** Jones built industry (Mercury Records VP, Qwest Records). Horvitz built community (The Royal Room, Columbia City). Frisell built none, letting the music speak for itself. Three different models of how musicians shape a city's infrastructure.
- **The Opening Triangle is the species engine's thesis statement.** Silence (degree 0) to cascade (degree 1) to sustained tone (degree 2) -- the first three positions prove the circle is not a linear progression but an exploration of fundamentally different acoustic strategies.
- **Robin Holcomb as degree neighbor enriches the human network.** The Horvitz-Holcomb household represents a creative-partnership dimension the engine had not tracked before -- complementary registers, shared harmonic language, distinct artistic identities, each partner standing on their own catalog.
- **Independent research paths converged on the same thesis.** The Wayne Horvitz research and the Varied Thrush research were conducted without cross-reference, and they arrived at the same structural lesson (architecture is the silence between the notes). The paired-resonance principle is found, not forced.

### What Could Be Better

- **No spectrograms or audio analysis embedded.** The AM synthesis description would benefit enormously from an actual spectrogram showing the carrier tone, sideband frequencies, and amplitude modulation pattern. Future releases should include at least one spectrogram per SPS entry that has notable spectral content.
- **The Royal Room deserves deeper venue analysis.** Programming data, booking patterns, audience demographics -- the venue-as-composition thesis needs quantitative evidence beyond the architectural description. A future S36 pass should include a Royal Room booking-archive analysis.
- **Indigenous territorial acknowledgment remains surface-level.** Duwamish, Muckleshoot, Suquamish, and Snoqualmie connection to the conifer forests needs nation-specific attribution, not just a blanket acknowledgment. Future degrees should improve this by consulting tribal cultural departments directly.
- **The Seattle Draw pattern is still circumstantial.** Three data points (Horvitz 1988, Holcomb 1988, Frisell 1989) look like a pattern, but a fourth and fifth are needed before the pattern can be called structural rather than coincidental.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Three degrees reveal the engine's actual thesis: complexity is non-monotonic.** The circle does not climb from simple to complex. It oscillates. Degree 0 = silence. Degree 1 = maximum melodic density. Degree 2 = one note, sustained. The pattern will continue to surprise, and the engine must track it without forcing a narrative of linear progression.

2. **Composed improvisation is the mediating position the engine needed.** Jones composes fully. Frisell improvises freely. Horvitz sits between them -- structurally complete compositions with designated spaces for improvisation. Three degrees, three positions on the spectrum. This positions the engine to classify every subsequent artist's methodology relative to these three anchor points.

3. **The AM / FM pair is the first cross-cluster Rosetta connection.** The Varied Thrush's AM synthesis connects to the AMR (AM Radio) research project -- same mathematics, different medium. This is the Rosetta engine working as designed: independent research at different degree positions discovering the same underlying mathematics. The connections are found, not forced.

4. **Sustained tone is a compositional discipline rather than a simple form.** Both Horvitz's piano cluster voicings held through long reverberant tails and the Varied Thrush's single buzzy whistles over 3-to-8-second silences demonstrate that sustaining a single tone is an active compositional choice. The note is held; the silence is composed; the architecture is the silence between the notes.

5. **Artist-as-institution forks into industry-builder and community-builder.** Jones (Mercury Records VP, Qwest Records) built industry. Horvitz (The Royal Room, Seattle Improvised Music Festival, NYCO) built community. Each model has different scale, different impact, different demands on the artist's non-musical time. The engine now tracks both models separately.

6. **The Seattle Draw is becoming a structural pattern rather than a coincidence.** Horvitz 1988, Robin Holcomb 1988, Frisell 1989 -- three downtown-scene NYC musicians choosing the Pacific Northwest within a two-year window. The fourth and fifth data points will confirm or refute whether this is a structural migration or a cluster of personal choices.

7. **Paired partner-couple households are a new engine tracking dimension.** The Horvitz-Holcomb household on Capitol Hill is the first time the engine has registered a creative partnership where both partners have independent catalogs. Future partner couples (anticipated) will be tracked in the same way.

8. **Indigenous territorial acknowledgment has to be nation-specific, not generic.** The degree 2 acknowledgment of "Coast Salish peoples" covers four distinct nations (Duwamish, Muckleshoot, Suquamish, Snoqualmie) with different cultural relationships to the conifer forests. Future SPS releases must consult tribal cultural departments and acknowledge nations individually.

9. **Silence is compositional.** The 3-to-8-second inter-note pauses in the Varied Thrush's song and the held silences inside Horvitz's Gravitas Quartet chamber works demonstrate the same principle: the silence is the frame that gives the notes their weight. Japanese aesthetics calls this *ma*; recording engineering calls it pre-delay; the Seattle 360 engine registers both and the underlying concept.

10. **The Rosetta cross-cluster registry is the engine's long-term convergence tool.** The first entry (SPS Varied Thrush AM -> AMR AM Radio) opens a registry that every future degree can add to. When independent research on different scales arrives at the same mathematics, the engine logs the connection and the registry grows. Over 360 degrees the registry becomes the engine's most valuable cross-domain artifact.
