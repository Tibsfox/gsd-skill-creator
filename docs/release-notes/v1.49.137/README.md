## Summary

Three degrees in, the circle reveals its first structural insight: the progression is non-monotonic. Degree 0 was silence (heron/Jones). Degree 1 was cascade complexity (wren/Frisell). Degree 2 is the sustained single tone — Wayne Horvitz's composed improvisation and the Varied Thrush's haunting one-note whistle. Less complex than degree 1 in density, but introducing entirely new dimensions: AM synthesis in the thrush's buzzy modulation, composed frameworks in Horvitz's scored-but-open structures, and the artist-as-institution pattern (The Royal Room in Columbia City).

Three data points now enable spectra rather than contrasts. The composition-improvisation spectrum (Jones → Horvitz → Frisell), the density spectrum (heron → thrush → wren), and the FM/AM modulation pair (wren = FM, thrush = AM) are all visible only because we have three degrees.

## Lessons Learned
- **Three points make a spectrum.** Two releases can only show contrast. Three reveal a continuum — and the engine's first non-obvious insight (the circle is non-monotonic) emerges.
- **FM and AM are the fundamental modulation pair.** Pacific Wren (FM) and Varied Thrush (AM) — both present in the first three degrees. The bioacoustic engine has produced the same pair that Chowning and Armstrong gave electronic music.
- **The artist-as-institution pattern tracks cultural impact.** Jones built industry, Horvitz built community (The Royal Room), Frisell built none (pure artist). Three different models of how musicians shape a city.
