**Mother-Pup Vocal Recognition and Acoustic Imprinting.** The Harbor Seal mother-pup bond is one of the most-studied parent-offspring recognition systems in marine mammal biology. Within hours of birth, mother and pup exchange vocalizations that encode individual identity. This acoustic imprinting is fast and durable — mothers and pups recognize each other's specific individual call signatures by frequency structure and timing, enabling identification across crowded haul-out beaches with hundreds of animals. In the noise and chaos of a summer pupping site, a mother returning from foraging can identify her pup's specific bleat among dozens, and a pup can identify its mother's return call. The acoustic imprinting happens fast because it must — in a crowded haul-out, misidentification means starvation or abandonment. The pup's **high nasal wavering bleat** is designed for this recognition: the higher fundamental frequency (300-1000 Hz) cuts through the lower-frequency ambient sound of waves, wind, and adult haul-out vocalizations, and the wavering frequency modulation creates a distinctive individual signature. The engine's documentation at degree 181 preserves the mother-pup vocal recognition system as first-class behavioral-ecology content, and the imprinting framework anchors the framework within which future mammalian parent-offspring acoustic recognition events (Orca calf-mother stereotyped call sharing, Gray Whale calf-mother moans, Roosevelt Elk cow-calf contact calls) can be documented.

**Precocial Pups, Lanugo Shed In Utero, and the 45-50% Fat Milk.** Harbor Seal pups are born **precocial** — eyes open, fur developed, capable of swimming within hours of birth. The developmental framework is distinct from many other pinniped species: the **lanugo** (fetal fur coat) is shed in utero, so newborn pups emerge with the adult gray-brown spotted coat rather than the white or silver lanugo visible in harp seal, hooded seal, and some other phocid pups. Pups nurse for **4-6 weeks** on milk that is approximately **45-50% fat** — one of the richest milks in nature and comparable to the milk of other phocid species — gaining approximately **0.5-1.0 kilogram per day** to reach weaning weight of approximately 24 kilograms. After weaning, the mother departs and the pup must forage independently, transitioning from full dependence to full independence abruptly.
