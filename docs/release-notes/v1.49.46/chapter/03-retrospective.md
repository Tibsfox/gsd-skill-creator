# Retrospective — v1.49.46

## What Worked

1. **Combined research+build pipeline.** Single Opus agent handled web research, document writing, 8 module creation, scaffold, and hub updates in one pass. No handoff between research and build agents — eliminated the wait time between phases.

2. **Convention-as-Rosetta-Stone (Module 07) is the conceptual payoff.** The Research series has been building toward the idea that every domain is a translation of the same underlying patterns. A convention is the physical proof — people who speak different creative languages meet in one space and find common ground. The module makes explicit what the hub has been doing implicitly.

3. **The conventions sub-cluster begins.** NWC is the first of three — the fixed node. WCN (Westercon, the traveling convention) and SMF (SMOFcon, the meta-layer) complete the stack. Three conventions, three architectural patterns, one community. Planning the cluster as a sequence rather than a batch means each one can reference the last.

## Lessons Learned

1. **The PNW creative ecosystem keeps revealing new layers.** Green River to Pearl Jam to Weyerhaeuser to Norwescon. Music, timber, conventions — all shaped by the same geographic isolation and DIY ethic. The research series maps the bioregion not just ecologically but culturally. Every project added makes the network more coherent.

2. **Forty-eight years of continuity is its own argument.** Norwescon has outlasted publishers, bookstores, magazines, and entire subgenres. The convention persists because the community persists. Documenting WHY it persists (Module 05's social graph, Module 04's hotel-as-architecture) matters more than documenting what happens there. Infrastructure outlasts content.
