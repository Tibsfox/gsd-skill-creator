## Summary

The 44th Research project — NWC (Norwescon), the Pacific Northwest's premier science fiction and fantasy convention, running continuously since 1978 at the DoubleTree by Hilton SeaTac. Eight modules trace 48 years of volunteer-run literary convention culture: from its founding through the Guest of Honor tradition, the legendary writing workshop, the DoubleTree as social architecture, community and inclusion evolution, the PNW convention ecosystem, and the convention as a Rosetta Stone for cross-domain creative translation.

Named "Room 101" — every convention has that one panel room where the deep conversations happen. Also an Orwell reference that fits a convention built on asking hard questions about technology, society, and imagination.

NWC connects directly to LNV (Larry Niven as past Guest of Honor), GRV/PJM (Seattle creative community), BRC (convention as temporary community infrastructure), FFA (masquerade/costume tradition), and the Rosetta Stone framework (conventions as cross-domain translation spaces where writers meet scientists meet artists meet gamers).

**Research series: 44 complete projects, 416 research modules, ~195,000 lines.**

## Retrospective

### What Worked

1. **Combined research+build pipeline.** Single Opus agent handled web research, document writing, 8 module creation, scaffold, and hub updates in one pass. No handoff between research and build agents — eliminated the wait time between phases.

2. **Convention-as-Rosetta-Stone (Module 07) is the conceptual payoff.** A convention is literally a space where people who speak different creative languages meet. Writers, scientists, artists, gamers, costumers — all in one hotel for a weekend. The parallel to the Research series hub (each project is a panel, the hub is the programming grid) writes itself.

### Lessons Learned

1. **The PNW creative ecosystem keeps revealing new layers.** Green River → Pearl Jam → Weyerhaeuser → Norwescon. Music, timber, conventions — all shaped by the same geographic isolation and DIY ethic. The research series maps the bioregion not just ecologically but culturally. Every project added makes the network more coherent.
