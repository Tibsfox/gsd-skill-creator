## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Phasianidae (2nd entry -- grouse trio begins)
- **Order:** Galliformes
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The Blue Grouse was split in 2006 into two species: the **Dusky Grouse** (*Dendragapus obscurus*) of the interior and western mountains, and the **Sooty Grouse** (*Dendragapus fuliginosus*) of the Pacific coastal ranges. Both species occur in Washington state, with the Dusky Grouse found in the eastern Cascades and Blue Mountains, and the Sooty Grouse along the western slopes and Olympic Peninsula. The engine uses the legacy common name "Blue Grouse" at degree 154 to represent the Dusky Grouse specifically, reserving the Sooty Grouse for degree 156. The **deep resonant booming** is one of the most remarkable sounds in the PNW avian world. Males establish display territories on ridges and hillsides, often in coniferous forest, and produce a series of low-frequency booms by rapidly inflating and deflating bright cervical air sacs -- yellow in Dusky Grouse, purple-red in Sooty. The fundamental frequency of this boom hovers around 50-100 Hz, placing much of the sound energy in the infrasonic or near-infrasonic range. Humans often feel the boom as much as hear it -- a deep pulsing that seems to come from everywhere and nowhere, the sound diffracting around trees and terrain. This ventriloquial quality makes the displaying male extraordinarily difficult to locate by ear alone. At E=5, the boom carries substantial energy despite its low frequency, filling mountain valleys with a sound more felt than heard.
- **Key Losada arc:** Key Losada represents the continuing depth of Seattle's independent hip-hop community -- artists who build careers outside major label infrastructure, releasing music on their own terms. The self-released streak now extends to 12 consecutive degrees of independent distribution.
- **Grouse sound comparison:** The three grouse species in the engine (154-156) each produce sound through fundamentally different mechanisms: Blue Grouse uses **air sac inflation** (pneumatic), Ruffed Grouse uses **wing drumming** (mechanical), and Sooty Grouse uses **air sac booming** (pneumatic, different frequency). Three species, three methods, one family.
