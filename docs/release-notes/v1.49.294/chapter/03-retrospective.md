## Lessons Learned

1. **Six consecutive degrees of the same category firmly establish a tier as a structural run.** The self-released underground tier at J. Pinder (149), Mr. Hill (150), Sol (151), Prometheus Brown (152), Geo (153), and Key Losada (154) consecutive degrees firmly establishes the tier as a structural run across six degrees. While two consecutive degrees was sufficient to move the pattern from "single-artist anomaly" to "emerging pattern," six consecutive degrees moves the pattern from "emerging" to "structurally established." Future tiers, families, or sub-genres that reach six-consecutive-degree runs should be recognized as structurally established categories.
2. **Progressively deeper-tier movement across consecutive-degree runs reveals ecosystem depth.** The six-degree self-released run (149–154) moves progressively deeper from documented underground (J. Pinder) through working underground (Mr. Hill, Sol) through solo-from-documented-group (Prometheus Brown, Geo) down to deep underground (Key Losada). The progressively deeper-tier movement across consecutive degrees reveals that the Seattle hip-hop ecosystem has real depth that the engine documents at each layer. Future consecutive-degree runs should be examined for progressive-depth patterns.
3. **First-of-its-kind acoustic registers should be explicitly named when introduced.** The subsonic display register at degree 154 was introduced as a first-of-its-kind acoustic category and explicitly named. Explicit naming at introduction establishes the vocabulary for future entries in the same register and preserves the structural significance of the introduction. Future first-of-its-kind registers (visual-acoustic integrated display, compound acoustic identity, multi-modal signal) should be explicitly named at introduction.
4. **Physical-acoustics principles should be named precisely rather than described vaguely.** The Helmholtz-resonator principle for the Dusky Grouse throat-sac booming was named precisely rather than described as vague "throat inflation." Precise physical-acoustics naming connects the biological phenomenon to the broader physics literature and provides a verifiable mechanism that future readers can investigate further. Future acoustic mechanisms should be named with their precise physical-acoustics terminology when applicable.
5. **Taxonomic splits documented at engine-structural level should preserve the split via non-adjacent degree placement.** The 2006 AOU Blue Grouse split into Dusky and Sooty Grouse was documented at engine-structural level with Dusky at 154 and Sooty at 156 and Ruffed Grouse at 155 between them. The non-adjacent placement with a between-species interposition mirrors the taxonomic relationship of close-but-distinct and preserves the split's structural significance. Future taxonomic splits should be examined for similar non-adjacent placement with appropriate between-species interpositions.
6. **The longest-run structural records should be tracked explicitly at each extension with named break-points when they occur.** The twenty-one-consecutive-hip-hop-era record was tracked at degree 154 with the explicit note that the plateau will break at degree 155 with The Physics at E=6.
