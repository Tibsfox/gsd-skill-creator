## Summary

**OLYMPIA CLOSES. FIRST BALAENOPTERIDAE. E=9.**

**Unwound** arrive at E=8 -- the Olympia post-hardcore band formed in 1989 by Justin Trosper (guitar, vocals) and Vern Rumsey (bass), joined by Sara Lund (drums) in 1993. Unwound operated for twelve years from within the Olympia underground, releasing nine albums on Kill Rock Stars before dissolving in 2002 and reforming in 2023. Their catalog -- *Fake Train* (1993), *New Plastic Ideas* (1994), *The Future of What* (1995), *Repetition* (1996), *Challenge for a Civilized Society* (1998), *Leaves Turn Inside You* (2001) -- documents a band that moved from hardcore intensity toward increasing structural complexity without losing the core energy that drove the early records.

*Leaves Turn Inside You*, Unwound's final studio album, is one of the most significant post-hardcore records made anywhere. It is a double album, slow in places, dense in others, with production that fills the stereo field and guitars that move in ways that do not announce themselves as memorable but are. Sara Lund's drumming across the Unwound catalog is among the most varied and compositionally integrated drum work in the post-hardcore record: she is not keeping time for the guitars; she is composing alongside them. Vern Rumsey's bass playing is the connective tissue that holds the Trosper guitar lines together across the band's increasingly complex structural experiments. Unwound worked for twelve years without significant commercial success, within the Kill Rock Stars infrastructure, in a city of fifty thousand people, and produced a body of work that is still being discovered.
