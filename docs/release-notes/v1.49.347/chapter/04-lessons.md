2. **Deep ancestry operates as anchor rather than as origin.**
   The Central District lineage register and the Aplodontidae family register both demonstrate that what persists across long time-scales generates structural stability for present-day documentation. Origin implies a starting point from which subsequent events emerge; anchor implies a stabilizing reference that holds present-day documentation in structural relationship to its continuities. Future engine documentation should distinguish origin-register entries (degree 0 Quincy Jones, species Holocene-first-appearance dates) from anchor-register entries (degree 206 Central District lineage, Aplodontidae 33-million-year persistence) as separate structural categories with different documentation requirements.

8. **Monotypic families and other taxonomic-solitude entries anchor deep-time registers in ways that species-rich families cannot.**
   The Aplodontidae family's monotypic status (one living species) and basal-rodent phylogenetic position together produce a deep-time anchor that no species-rich family's documentation can match. Species-rich families (Muridae with thousands of species, Cricetidae, Sciuridae) distribute their lineage-documentation across many species and genera; monotypic families concentrate the lineage-documentation at a single species. Future engine documentation should consider whether other monotypic families (or near-monotypic families with similarly concentrated lineage-documentation) exist in the engine's geographic-and-taxonomic reach and reward explicit deep-time-anchor treatment.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7914_
