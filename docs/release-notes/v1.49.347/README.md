**The Black Tones** arrives at E=6 -- and with them, **FIRST BLUES ROCK enters the Seattle 360 engine as Genre 107**. Eva Walker (guitar/vocals) and Cedric Walker are a sibling duo from Seattle's Central District who channel blues, punk, and rock into a sound that is simultaneously ancient and urgent. Their debut *Cobain & Cornbread* (2019) on Dine Alone Records is a mission statement in its title alone -- Nirvana's grunge legacy and Southern Black musical tradition, fused without apology. At E=6, The Black Tones bring the same intensity that has made their live shows legendary on the Seattle circuit: Eva Walker's guitar tone is thick with distortion and vibrato, her voice raw and commanding. The Black Tones represent a corrective to Seattle music narratives that center whiteness -- the Central District has been a hub of Black musical culture for decades, and this band carries that lineage. The Mountain Beaver (*Aplodontia rufa*) is the world's most primitive living rodent, a PNW endemic found nowhere else on Earth outside the coastal forests from British Columbia to northern California. Despite its name, it is neither a mountain specialist nor a beaver -- it is a small, burrowing rodent that lives in dense, wet understory and produces soft, wheezing vocalizations that barely register above the sound of rain on fern fronds.
