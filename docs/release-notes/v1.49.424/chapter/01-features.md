## FIRST OTARIIDAE


- the eared seals (Otariidae) are distinguished from the true seals (Phocidae) by the presence of external ear flaps and the ability to rotate their hind flippers forward for terrestrial locomotion; sea lions walk; true seals (harbor seals, elephant seals) undulate; this distinction is behavioral as much as anatomical; the Steller sea lion's terrestrial mobility makes it a different kind of marine mammal presence on the rocks and beaches of the Pacific Northwest than the harbor seal, which hauls out in a different posture; E=8 for the bull roar is the correct entry for the Otariidae into the engine

## Steller sea lion scale


- at 1,000 kg, the Steller sea lion bull is ten times heavier than a large harbor seal; it is also significantly heavier than the California sea lion (adult male: ~300 kg) and the Dall's porpoise (~200 kg); the Steller is the largest pinniped in the engine's marine mammal arc by mass, and among the largest in the North Pacific; the roar it produces is proportionate to its mass
