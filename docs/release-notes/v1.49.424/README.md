## Summary

**HARDLY ART SUPERGROUP. FIRST OTARIIDAE.**

**Childbirth** arrive at E=7 -- the Seattle punk pop supergroup formed by Emily Nokes (Tacocat), Julia Shapiro (Chastity Belt), and Bree McKenna (Tacocat), releasing music on Hardly Art. Childbirth have released two albums: *It's a Girl!* (2014) and *Women's Work* (2017), both on Hardly Art. The project assembles three musicians who are simultaneously active in their primary bands -- Nokes and McKenna in Tacocat, Shapiro in Chastity Belt -- into a side project with its own distinct humor register and political sensibility.

Childbirth are the result of the specific social and professional infrastructure that Hardly Art and the Seattle independent music scene provide: musicians who know each other well enough to collaborate, who share political commitments and aesthetic sensibilities, and who have the label infrastructure to release the result. The supergroup model -- three musicians from two active bands, operating in parallel with their primary work -- is a specific Seattle independent music phenomenon, and Childbirth is one of its clearest expressions.

The catalog is deliberately fast and funny, with political content about women's health, reproductive rights, and the experience of being a woman that is delivered at punk tempo without losing the humor that characterizes both Tacocat and Chastity Belt. *Women's Work* (2017) is particularly sharp -- the album title is already the joke and the statement simultaneously, and the songs fulfill both. E=7 is the correct assignment. The supergroup at its best is slightly more aggressive than either primary band in isolation, because the combination of these three specific musicians at this specific time produces something energetically above the sum of its parts.

Degree 283 is the second consecutive Hardly Art entry (Tacocat at 282, Childbirth at 283), documenting the label's capacity to produce a sequence of related but distinct acts from the same Seattle independent music infrastructure.

**Steller Sea Lion Bull Roar** arrives at E=8 on the SPS track -- the territorial vocalizations of *Eumetopias jubatus*, the largest member of Family Otariidae (the eared seals), produced by dominant males defending haul-out positions and breeding territories. The Steller sea lion bull is among the most physically imposing pinniped in the Pacific Northwest: adult males reach 1,000 kg and 3 meters in length. The territorial roar is produced from a massive chest cavity and is guttural, low-frequency, and carried over distance. At an active haul-out -- Protection Island, Destruction Island, the rocks of the outer coast -- the combined vocalizations of multiple bulls is a continuous, overlapping acoustic event of considerable intensity.

E=8 for the Steller sea lion bull roar is the correct assignment. The individual roar is the loudest vocalization any pinniped in the Pacific Northwest produces, and the combined haul-out acoustic event is among the most energetically intense biological sound events in the nearshore marine record. E=8 reflects both the individual roar and the collective site.

Degree 283 marks the engine's first entry for Family Otariidae -- the eared seals, the family that includes all sea lions and fur seals in the Pacific Northwest. The engine has now documented five marine mammal families across fourteen consecutive SPS degrees: Delphinidae (orcas), Balaenopteridae (humpback, blue whale), Physeteridae (sperm whale), Phocoenidae (Dall's porpoise), and now Otariidae.
