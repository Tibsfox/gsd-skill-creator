1. **A sustain keeps the predecessor's axis vocabulary and replaces only the predecessor-specific content.**
   MAVEN shares the Mars-exploration vocabulary with the InSight predecessor (Mars, the red planet, the atmosphere, the upper atmosphere, the ionosphere, the solar wind, dust, in situ, planetary exploration, past water, climate) — that vocabulary is shared and correct. The rewrite removed only the InSight-specific content (the InSight name, SEIS, HP3 "the mole," RISE, CNES, DLR, Elysium Planitia, Vandenberg and the first-West-Coast-launch note, marsquakes, the deep-interior / seismometer / interior-structure anchors, the crust/mantle/liquid-core result, and the American Badger + Big Sagebrush pairing) and replaced it with MAVEN content.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12330_

2. **A sustain has no axis rotation to frame — and 1.244 was itself a sustain, so there is no rotation framing to remove.**
   The Mars axis opened at 1.238 (Viking, substrate-axis rotation #36); 1.239 through 1.245 are all SUSTAINS, so the clone source carries no rotation narrative, and at most a single distant note records that the Mars axis itself opened at 1.238 by rotating from the X-ray-astronomy axis (rotation #35, held complete at obs#15).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12331_

3. **MAVEN is an ORBITER, not a lander or rover — it varies the form from the recent surface missions.**
   Where the prior several Mars degrees were landers and rovers, MAVEN circled high above and read the upper atmosphere; the catalog never describes MAVEN landing or roving and never re-claims any surface first.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12332_

4. **The escape measurement EXPLAINS where the water and air went — it does not "discover water.**
   MAVEN's first quantification of atmospheric escape concluded that escape to space removed the bulk of the early atmosphere — the dominant cause of the wet-to-dry climate change — answering the surface missions' "Mars was once wet" question; the catalog never writes that MAVEN discovered water and never contradicts the Mars Exploration Rovers' mineral water evidence or Curiosity's habitability finding.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12333_

5. **The metal-ion first is PERSISTENT layers beyond Earth — distinct from the Comet Siding Spring one-off.**
   NGIMS found persistent meteoric metal-ion layers (iron, magnesium, sodium); the catalog keeps the word PERSISTENT to distinguish them from the transient enhancement during the October 2014 comet pass, and notes that Earth has its own metal-ion layers, so the first is scoped "beyond Earth."
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12334_

6. **The aurora is NEW TYPES, never the first aurora at Mars.**
   IUVS characterized a deep diffuse aurora and a dayside proton aurora, but ESA's Mars Express/SPICAM discovered discrete Martian aurora earlier (2004–2005); the catalog frames MAVEN's aurora as new types and never writes "first aurora at Mars," and does not use the aurora as a canonical anchor.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12335_

7. **The mission's end is framed positively and mentioned once at low density.**
   MAVEN operated for about eleven years; its signal ended 6 December 2025 and NASA concluded the mission 3 June 2026. The catalog states this once and frames the mission positively as a landmark that achieved its goals, with no failure/loss vocabulary about the spacecraft — the "loss" the page features is the atmosphere escaping to space.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12336_

8. **NASA-with-university/industry-partners framing is an accuracy requirement here.**
   MAVEN was a NASA mission of the Mars Scout Program managed by NASA Goddard, with the spacecraft built by Lockheed Martin and instruments from CU/LASP (PI Bruce Jakosky), the UC Berkeley Space Sciences Laboratory, and Goddard — a NASA mission with major university and industry partners.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12337_

The predecessors' anchors stay with them — 0 re-stamps.

10. **Clone source is InSight (a NASA stationary solar-powered geophysics lander, Discovery, 2018–2022); MAVEN is a NASA upper-atmosphere orbiter (Mars Scout, 2013–2025).**
   The most structural replacement in the rewrite is removing ALL InSight-specific content — "InSight," "SEIS," "HP3," "the mole," "RISE," "CNES," "DLR," "Elysium Planitia," "Vandenberg," the first-West-Coast-launch note, "marsquakes," the deep-interior / seismometer / interior-structure anchors, the crust/mantle/liquid-core result, and the American Badger + Big Sagebrush pairing — and replacing it with MAVEN and the Mars Scout Program, Bruce Jakosky / LASP / CU Boulder, the Atlas V 401 launch from Cape Canaveral SLC-41 (18 November 2013), the 21/22 September 2014 Mars orbit insertion, the elongated elliptical orbit and the Deep Dip campaigns, the payload (Particles & Fields: SWIA/SWEA/STATIC/SEP/MAG/LPW; IUVS; NGIMS), the atmospheric-escape measurement and the wet-to-dry climate-loss…

11. **The shader renders procedural geometry rather than archived mission imagery.**
   The MAVEN shader uses analytic curves and procedural noise rather than loading actual mission data or IUVS imagery. A future revision could render a real IUVS ultraviolet map of the Martian upper atmosphere or aurora (publicly available from the Planetary Data System) for a higher-fidelity view.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12340_

12. **The MAVEN diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the orbiter with its solar panels, its gull-wing articulated panels, and the high-gain antenna.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12341_

13. **The atmosphere-escape mode is depicted rather than driven by real measurements.**
   The ions and atoms streaming off in the atmosphere-carried-off mode are depicted but not rendered from archived SWIA/STATIC or NGIMS escape-flux data; a future ship could drive the mode from the measured escape-rate time series for a quantitatively faithful reading.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12342_

14. **The metal-ion and aurora mode is depicted rather than computed.**
   The metal-ion layers and the aurora in the fourth mode are depicted rather than rendered from the published NGIMS metal-ion profiles or the IUVS aurora maps; a future ship could drive the mode from the real measured profiles for a quantitatively faithful view.
