## What Could Be Better

Future water-surface acoustic documentation should establish the terminology hierarchy explicitly, because the acoustic signature varies slightly across the sub-regions (Strait exposure differs from inner-sound protected water, and tidal current patterns differ across the Salish Sea subdivisions) and the register carries more interpretive weight when the geographic specificity is precise.
- **The Cfb oceanic climate classification was cited but the implications for diurnal and seasonal acoustic variation were not fully characterized.** The documentation cites the Köppen Cfb classification and the November-January rainfall peak but does not fully characterize the diurnal acoustic variation (rain on water sounds different at dawn versus midday versus dusk due to temperature-related air density and marine-layer interactions) or the seasonal variation within the winter peak (December rain sounds different from February rain due to different storm-system characteristics). Future meteorological-sound documentation should characterize these within-season acoustic variations, because the keynote-sound register carries more weight when the sub-variations are surfaced rather than flattened.
- **The Washington State Ferries acoustic-experience register cites passenger count but does not characterize the specific ferry-route acoustic variations.** The 24-million-passengers-per-year figure establishes the scale of the commuter acoustic exposure, but the documentation does not characterize how the acoustic experience varies across the ferry routes (Seattle-Bainbridge vs. Seattle-Bremerton vs. Edmonds-Kingston vs. Mukilteo-Clinton, etc.) where route-specific factors (crossing duration, terminal architecture, route exposure to open water vs. protected waters) shape the rain-on-water acoustic experience. Future ferry-acoustic documentation should characterize the route-level variation.
- **The atmospheric river events were mentioned but their specific acoustic contribution was not distinguished from everyday moderate rain.** The documentation notes the 10-15 annual atmospheric river events and their outsized contribution to annual precipitation, but does not explicitly distinguish the acoustic signature of an atmospheric-river heavy-rainfall event (> 10 mm/hour producing near-pure white noise) from the everyday moderate rainfall (1-5 mm/hour producing the textured white-noise keynote). - **The Naked Giants pre-2013-formation biography was thin and the high-school-formation context was documented but not fully situated.** The documentation establishes that Naked Giants formed while members were in high school in 2013 but does not fully characterize what that formation context looked like — which Seattle high schools, what music programs or lack thereof, what extracurricular venues exposed the members to the music they would go on to make. Future contemporary-band documentation should situate the pre-band-formation biographical context more fully, because the development-context register is interpretively meaningful and thin biographical framing leaves the formation register underspecified.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Keynote-background acoustic documentation requires explicit amplitude-softness framing rather than implicit amplitude-equals-importance assumption.** Pacific rain on Puget Sound at E=3 is the most culturally loaded sound in the SPS engine despite being the softest meteorological entry. The E=3 placement required explicit framing to avoid the reader conflating softness with unimportance. Future keynote-sound documentation should foreground the softness-as-keynote framing rather than treating amplitude as a proxy for documentary significance, because the keynote-background register is structurally distinct from the foreground-event register and its importance is not amplitude-equivalent.
2. **Four-stage acoustic sequences deserve explicit stage-by-stage documentation rather than summary aggregation.** The raindrop impact at degree 251 operates through four distinct physical stages (impact splash, crown splash, bubble entrainment, surface wave), each with its own frequency content and temporal profile. The documentation's explicit four-stage breakdown surfaces acoustic details that summary aggregation ("broadband white noise") would flatten. Future multi-stage acoustic documentation should surface the stages individually, because the documentary weight attaches to the specific mechanism sequence and the register carries more interpretive density when the stages are traceable.
3. **Long tradition-continuations with intervening interruptions should be documented as continuity-through-interruption rather than as reset.** The Northwest Sound documented at 239-244 and continued at Naked Giants 251 closes a 50-plus year arc with an apparent grunge-era hiatus in between. Future multi-decade tradition-documentation should frame the continuation as continuity-through-interruption rather than as revival or reset, because the continuity framing surfaces what persists across the interruption (the regional-tradition DNA) while the revival framing implies a reconstruction from external sources that may be less accurate.
4. **Three-piece all-singing democratic-ensemble configurations are a distinct band-structure category worth surfacing explicitly.** Naked Giants' three-piece all-singing format is structurally different from four- or five-piece lead-singer-plus-backing configurations in ways that affect the music substantively. Future engine documentation should surface democratic-ensemble structures explicitly when they organize significant band-structure dynamics, because the register documents a partnership category that generic band-member-listing documentation does not capture.
5. **Polar-contrast equilibrium pairings are a distinct cross-axis mode from matched-amplitude pairings and expand the engine's register vocabulary.** Degree 251's E=7 + E=3 = E=10 arrives at the equilibrium reading through polar contrast, demonstrating that equilibrium arithmetic can be satisfied by multiple pairing modes. Future combined-amplitude documentation should surface polar-contrast pairings as a distinct cross-axis mode rather than folding them into generic equilibrium documentation, because the polar-contrast mode carries different interpretive weight than matched-amplitude pairings and the register benefits from the distinction.
6. **Cultural loading is a first-class documentation object that can be surfaced at the same resolution as acoustic-physics content.** Pacific rain on Puget Sound carries the maximum cultural loading in SPS, and the documentation at degree 251 surfaces this through explicit references to keynote-sound theory, white-noise-machine neurological resonance, and universal PNW place-memory rather than leaving cultural loading as implicit. Future SPS documentation should surface cultural loading explicitly when it is structurally significant to the acoustic documentation, because the cultural register documents a relational property that generic acoustic documentation would miss.
7. **Institutional continuity across decades is documentable as first-class content rather than as incidental context.** Sub Pop's 35-plus year catalog continuity from Mudhoney through Naked Giants represents institutional infrastructure that shapes which bands exist and how they develop. Future engine documentation of label-signed artists should reference the label-catalog continuity explicitly, because the catalog register documents an artistic-positioning element that single-artist documentation does not capture.
8. **Commuter-scale cultural integration of acoustic environments warrants documentation beyond niche-observation framing.** The Washington State Ferries system placing 24 million passenger-experiences per year at the center of the rain-on-water acoustic exposure shifts the documentation frame from niche-nature-observation to daily-life-acoustic-exposure. Future acoustic-ecology documentation should characterize commuter-scale exposure when it is structurally significant, because the commuter register documents the acoustic-environment-as-daily-life integration that generic nature-observation documentation flattens.
