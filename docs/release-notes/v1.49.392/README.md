## Summary

**Naked Giants** arrive at E=7 -- the Seattle garage rock trio of Grant Mullen (guitar, vocals), Gianni Aiello (guitar, vocals), and Henry LaVallee (drums), formed in 2013 while the members were in high school. Naked Giants are a Sub Pop band -- signed to the label that defined the Seattle sound in 1989 and that has continued to function as the primary infrastructure for independent Seattle music across four decades. Their 2018 debut *SLUFF* and 2022 *Hifi* are records in the tradition of pure garage rock: energetic, melodically direct, not over-produced, guitar-forward in a way that connects to The Sonics and The Kingsmen as much as it connects to the 1990s. The E=7 reflects a band that plays at volume with enthusiasm and without irony, in a moment when irony is the default mode for guitar bands.

Sub Pop's role in the engine's narrative is cumulative. The label appears at every stage of the post-1986 Seattle music story: Nirvana, Mudhoney, Soundgarden, Sleater-Kinney, Fleet Foxes, Naked Giants. That a garage rock band with the direct simplicity of Naked Giants is on Sub Pop in 2018 tells you something about what the label has always been: a home for bands that play their instruments and mean it.

On the soundscape side, **Pacific Rain on Water** enters -- the **white noise** of sustained Pacific rain falling on the surface of Puget Sound or any of the region's large water bodies. When rain falls on open water, the acoustic result is a broadband white noise: every raindrop impact generates a small broadband sound, and when thousands of drops per second are falling across a large surface, the individual impacts merge into a continuous undifferentiated noise that covers the full frequency range. The E=3 rating reflects the low intensity of this sound relative to other precipitation entries -- it is ambient, pervasive, and flat in frequency content. It is the sound of the Pacific maritime climate in its most characteristic expression: persistent, gentle, covering everything.

The distinction between rain on a maple leaf (degree 248, patter-drip, E=4) and rain on open water (degree 251, white noise, E=3) is acoustic and ecological. Rain on a leaf is modified by the leaf's physical properties -- the impact point, the elasticity, the drainage pattern. Rain on water is absorbed without modification, and the sound is the direct acoustic product of the impact energy and the surface area. Open water rain is the purest acoustic expression of precipitation: no mediation, no structure, just the energy of falling water meeting the energy of standing water.
