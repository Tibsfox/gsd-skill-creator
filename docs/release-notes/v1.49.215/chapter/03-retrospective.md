# Retrospective — v1.49.215

## Lessons Learned

1. The second Anacortes act is a new and analytically productive category in the engine's artist-city taxonomy. It establishes that small PNW towns are not monolithic -- the same tiny town can produce radically opposite musical aesthetics. The pattern is relational: the second Anacortes act exists only in contrast to the first. Geography shapes but does not determine
2. TLE's second appearance transforms it from a data point to a pattern, paralleling Sub Pop's transformation at degree 74. One entry is a fact. Two entries create a trajectory. TLE and Sub Pop are now both recurring institutions in the engine
3. The Death Cab expanded universe at four degrees (58, 60, 67, 75) demonstrates how a single influential band radiates connections through multiple relationship types: the band itself, the solo artist, the departed member, the tour support act. Each connection type is structurally distinct
4. E=4 tying E=3 at 19 each creates a meaningful structural landmark -- the energy equilibrium at the middle. The engine's energy distribution is not skewed toward one energy level at the 3-4 range; it is balanced. The quiet majority (E1+E2+E3) still dominates at 61.8%, but the middle is equilibrated
5. Sciuridae's entry as the 35th family and sixth new family in seven degrees (69-75) confirms the extraordinary pace of family-level taxonomic expansion during the mammalian era. This rate will slow naturally when the engine returns to Aves
6. The first colonial mammal is a genuine behavioral landmark. Previous mammals were solitary, territorial, or family-unit social. The marmot's colonial living -- sentinel systems, harem structure, graded alarm calls -- represents a qualitatively different level of social complexity
7. Back-to-back alpine species (Pika 74, Marmot 75) with opposite winter strategies (hay storage vs fat storage) is one of the engine's most elegant ecological contrasts. The engine's alpine portrait deepens with each consecutive degree
8. Rodentia spanning from river (Beaver, 61) to summit (Marmot, 75) illustrates the order's extraordinary ecological range. Same order, opposite habitats, diverged ~50-60 million years ago. Taxonomic depth through ecological contrast
9. The anthem as acoustic element captures a dynamic structure (quiet verse to explosive chorus) rather than a timbre or production technique. This continues the expansion of the acoustic taxonomy beyond instrument categories into structural patterns
10. The engine is at ABSOLUTE CEILING for mammalian entries. Fifteen consecutive degrees. Return to Aves is non-negotiable at degree 76. The raptor gap at 76 entries is EMERGENCY level. The passeriform gap at 20 consecutive non-passerines is EXTREME
