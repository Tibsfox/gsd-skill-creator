## Mission Context

**Explorer 35** launched from Cape Canaveral LC-17B on 1967-07-19 at 14:19:02 GMT on a Thor-Delta E1 (TAD), five days after the Surveyor 4 mission loss (1967-07-17) and L+173 days after the Apollo 1 fire (1967-01-27). Three-day trans-lunar coast. Lunar orbit insertion 1967-07-22 via 23-second retrorocket burn. Selenocentric orbit 800 × 7,692 km altitude, 147.30° retrograde inclination, ~11.5-hour period. Operations 1967-07-19 through 1973-06-24 (2,167 days = 5 years 11 months 5 days). Commanded off (not failed); retired as newer IMPs took over.

**Spacecraft:** Built in-house at NASA Langley Research Center (Hampton VA). Dry mass 104.3 kg. Octagonal prism 71 cm × 20.3 cm. Four solar panels ~70 W. Spin-stabilized 25.6 rpm.

**Program Management:** NASA Goddard Space Flight Center. IMP Program Manager: Paul G. Marcotte (GSFC). Project Scientist: Norman F. Ness (GSFC, also magnetometer PI).

**Science Payload:** Nine instruments across three scientific channels (magnetic field, plasma flow, energetic particles) + four specialized experiments (micrometeorite, selenodetic gravity, bistatic radar, S-band telemetry).

**Scientific Findings:**
- Moon has no intrinsic magnetic field to <1 nT (Ness et al. 1967)
- Solar wind flows past Moon as electromagnetically transparent (field lines convected through unperturbed)
- Moon creates a cavity in the solar wind — the lunar wake — with Mach-cone internal structure (Colburn et al. 1971; Whang 1970)
- Six-year solar-cycle-modulated cosmic-ray time series (Van Allen et al. 1968-1974)
- Lunar gravity field harmonics refined to support Apollo landing-trajectory planning

**Institutional Legacy:** Explorer 35 is the architectural template for all subsequent anchored-orbital science missions — Pioneer Venus Orbiter (1978), Galileo (1989), Cassini-Huygens (1997), Mars Global Surveyor (1996), Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (2005), Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter (2009), ARTEMIS P1/P2 (2011-present direct lineage).

## Organism Context

**Pacific Harbor Seal** (*Phoca vitulina richardii*, Gray 1864) is the second marine mammal in the 60-degree corpus after v1.59 SRKW — closing the §6.4 FIRST-INSTANCE → CLASS TRANSITION for marine mammals.

**Taxonomy:**
- Family Phocidae (earless / true seals) — FIRST PHOCIDAE
- Superfamily Pinnipedia — FIRST PINNIPEDIA
- Order Carnivora — FIRST CARNIVORA
- Authority John Edward Gray, British Museum, 1864 — FIRST Gray 1864 authority
- Subspecies epithet honors Sir John Richardson (1787-1865), Scottish naval surgeon

**Salish Sea population:** ~50,000 individuals at ~3/km² density (among highest globally). Four subpopulations: San Juan Islands (~11,000, declining since 2013), Hood Canal (~1,500, +10-12% annually), South Puget Sound (~1,500, +10-12% annually), Outer WA/OR + BC (~36,000, stable at carrying capacity).

**Three vocalization classes:** underwater male territorial roar (100-500 Hz, 1-5 s, breeding season May-July), above-water short calls (barks, honks, grunts, growls, 200 Hz-2 kHz), mother-pup contact calls (pair-specific signatures during 4-6 week lactation).

**MMPA 1972 recovery arc:** ~3,000 seals (1960s bounty era) → ~50,000 seals (2020s carrying capacity) = 16.7× recovery. First MMPA-recovery-arc species in corpus.

**Predator relationships:** Harbor seals are NOT prey of Southern Resident Killer Whales (SRKW feed ~98% salmonid). Harbor seals are prey of Bigg's (transient) orcas, a genetically distinct ecotype with zero gene flow to residents (Hoelzel et al. 2007; Morin et al. 2024). First cross-ecotype predator-prey thread in 60-degree corpus.
