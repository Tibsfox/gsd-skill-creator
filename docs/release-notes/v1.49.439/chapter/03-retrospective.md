2. **Behavioral escalation from warning to sustained assault warrants a one-unit E elevation at the return entry.** The Northern Goshawk return at degree 298 elevates the energy reading from E=6 (single alarm at degree 225) to E=7 (sustained full alarm at degree 298). The lesson is that the engine's SPS amplitude vocabulary registers behavioral escalation at one-unit E elevation at first-class structural resolution, and that the E=6→E=7 boundary is anchored to the sustained-vocalization-plus-physical-engagement behavioral register rather than to a pure acoustic-amplitude-difference register.
