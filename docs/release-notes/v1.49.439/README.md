**Treepeople (return)** arrive at E=8 -- the second appearance of Doug Martsch's pre-Built to Spill Boise project, returning to the engine nine degrees after the geophysics-arc entry at degree 263. The first Treepeople entry established the band's identity: post-hardcore and alternative rock from Boise, Idaho, C/Z Records, the Doug Martsch developmental arc toward *There's Nothing Wrong with Love* (1994). The return entry at degree 298 shifts the energy reading upward from E=7 to E=8, reflecting a more aggressive or propulsive moment in the Treepeople catalog -- the faster, harder end of a band that was already fast and hard. E=8 for the return is a deliberate elevation that acknowledges the full range of the Treepeople catalog: the first entry placed the band at the middle of their register; the return places them at the top.

The C/Z Records connection is the label thread at degree 298. C/Z Records, the Seattle independent label founded in 1985 by Daniel House (also Skin Yard's bassist, degree 264/297) and Chris Hanzsek, released Treepeople alongside the Deep Six compilation (1986, which introduced Skin Yard, Green River, Soundgarden, Melvins, Malfunkshun, and U-Men to a wider audience) and other foundational Seattle records. C/Z's contribution to the proto-grunge era is enormous, and it is documented here through the Treepeople return: the label's commitment to releasing post-hardcore and alternative rock from both Seattle and the broader Pacific Northwest network is what made a Boise band (Treepeople) a natural fit for a Seattle label. Daniel House's simultaneous roles as Skin Yard bassist and C/Z co-founder placed him at the intersection of the band and the label infrastructure, and the engine documents that intersection through both Skin Yard entries and both Treepeople entries.

**Northern Goshawk Full Alarm** arrives at E=7 on the SPS track -- the third and final entry in the raptor behaviors arc, documenting *Accipiter gentilis* in sustained alarm mode: the *ka-ka-ka-ka-ka* assault that a nesting Goshawk produces when its territory is threatened, sustained for extended periods at high volume, directed at the intruder with the physical aggression that accompanies the call (Goshawks routinely strike humans, horses, and dogs who approach nest sites). This is a different acoustic event than the Goshawk's first SPS appearance at degree 225 (the alarm call at E=6, which opened the Accipiter trio). The full alarm at degree 298 is the sustained, nest-defense version: not a warning but an assault, maintained over minutes rather than seconds, escalating in intensity if the intruder does not retreat.

The raptor behaviors arc closes: Peregrine stoop (296, E=8) → Bald Eagle combat (297, E=7) → Goshawk full alarm (298, E=7). The arc is complete. Three consecutive degrees, three raptor species, three behavioral acoustic events. The Goshawk closes the arc with its maximum vocal expression -- the bird that was already the engine's most aggressive raptor by behavioral description now fully documented at its acoustic ceiling.

E=7 for the Goshawk full alarm is the correct assignment. The sustained *ka-ka* assault is louder, more sustained, and more physically intense than the single alarm call at degree 225 (E=6), and the E elevation from 6 to 7 reflects the behavioral difference between a single alarm and a sustained territorial assault. E=7 is accurate; E=8 would require a physical behavioral event of the Peregrine stoop's character.
