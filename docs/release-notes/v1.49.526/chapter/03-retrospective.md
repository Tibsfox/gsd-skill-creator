# Retrospective — v1.49.526

## Lessons Learned

1. **The convergence trio (23-24-25) is the engine's most elegant structural discovery.** Two acoustic ghosts before the archetype. The source preceded by its echoes. Three families, three textures, one convergent pattern. No previous degree sequence demonstrated inter-species acoustic convergence across consecutive releases.

2. **The jazz quadrant closes.** Twenty-five degrees of jazz-dominated labels. At degree 25, Jazz disappears from the genre label entirely. The genre gradient is complete. The soul quadrant opens.

3. **The Ranger program starts with failure.** The pattern holds for the third time: Pioneer (degree 1), Mercury (degree 16), Ranger (degree 25). New programs begin with failure. The failure altitude increases each time.

4. **The robin is the engine's most accessible species.** No expertise required. Everyone knows the robin. The archetype IS the baseline.

5. **The Central District thread persists through genre evolution.** Jazz (0-6) → Salon jazz (11) → Neo-soul collective (19) → Neo-Soul (25). Same neighborhood, four distinct musical eras. The geography is constant. The music transforms.

6. **Thrush quintet and Warbler quintet: two orthogonal quintets.** Both at 5 species. Thrushes map direction/timbre. Warblers map intensity. The two families together define the two-dimensional acoustic space of PNW songbirds.
