## Lessons Learned

1. **Complementary pairs bound the measurement space.** Venera 4 and Mariner 5 bound the Venus atmospheric profile; Shelby Earl and Brandi Carlile bound the PNW folk career space; Bewick's Wren and Pacific Wren bound the Troglodytidae ecological range. Each pair is two incomplete measurements whose joint evidence produces the complete picture. Future engine degrees with partial-measurement pairs should reference the twenty-four-hour template.

2. **Orthogonal biological axes emerge from single species entries.** The Bewick's Wren's cultural song learning forces a classification axis that every prior species can be labeled against. The axis exists retroactively. Future species with unusual biological features should be evaluated for new-axis potential before being filed under existing axes.

3. **Long family-return gaps measure the opening-quadrant territory covered.** Troglodytidae's 57-degree return from the Pacific Wren at degree 1 to the Bewick's Wren at degree 58 is the engine's new family-return benchmark. Returns across distances approaching a full quadrant (90 degrees) represent strong structural closure. Future returning families should be evaluated against the 57-degree benchmark.

4. **E=2 artists require honest qualitative-indicator classification.** Shelby Earl's critical recognition without chart data is the correct E=2 template. The classification rests on qualitative indicators (press outlets, producer-peer network, sustained multi-album career), flags the `chart_data` absence explicitly, and does not invent quantitative support. Future E=2 classifications should inherit the template.

5. **Methodological lineages transmit across engine degrees.** Kliore's Mariner 4 Mars occultation (1965) taught Mariner 5 Venus occultation (1967). Damien Jurado's Secretly Canadian artist work (engine degree 45) taught Shelby Earl's LUSIO debut (engine degree 58). Both are methodological inheritances transmitted across time through the practitioners' continuing work. Future releases should register methodological lineages as first-class engine artifacts.

6. **Post-reorder reconciliation is a documented process.** The 1.57 reconciliation demonstrates that when the NASA chronological reorder shifts an already-published release to a new slot, the fix is: (a) update the slot's metadata JSON to reflect post-reorder numbering, (b) annotate the original Pass-2 refinement files with a post-reorder position note, (c) correct any cross-references elsewhere in the catalog that pointed to the old slot. The release version stays as originally shipped. The pattern will be reused for any future chronological reorders.

7. **The cultural Allee effect is the first plausible cultural-extinction-cascade mechanism in the engine.** Bewick's Wren's eastern-US decline raises the possibility that song-dialect collapse precedes demographic extinction in culturally-transmitted species. The mechanism is unresolved but biologically plausible. Future species with cultural-transmission classifications should be evaluated for cultural-Allee-effect susceptibility, and the mechanism should enter the engine's ontology of extinction risks alongside habitat loss, competitive displacement, and climate-change-driven range shift.

8. **The infrastructure-beneficiary career is a distinct arc pattern.** Shelby Earl's career is sustained by a distributed network (LUSIO, NPR, Jurado, Fleet Foxes, community radio) rather than by a single load-bearing institution. The architecture is fault-tolerant: any single node can fail and the career persists. The engine registers the infrastructure-beneficiary arc as a distinct pattern, separate from institutional-sponsor careers (Carlile on Columbia) and breakthrough-independent careers (Fleet Foxes' Sub Pop to global recognition). Future artists will be classifiable as institutional, infrastructure-beneficiary, or breakthrough-independent.

9. **The Apollo 1 shadow sustains planetary work unbroken.** Mariner 5 launched L+138 days into the Apollo 1 shadow period and returned the most significant planetary-science results of the post-Apollo-1 era. The robotic-to-crewed ratio in the shadow quarter remains infinite: five robotic flights launched in the shadow period (Lunar Orbiters 3 and 4, Surveyor 3, Mariner 5, and Apollo 4's unmanned prep in November), zero crewed flights. The unmanned side's discipline is holding the American space program's scientific output through the crewed program's redesign period. The engine documents the asymmetry as a sustained pattern.

10. **Marine species absence has crossed the critical boundary.** Fifty-eight degrees, zero marine species in "The Sound of Puget Sound." Future retrospectives should commit to a specific marine species and degree number rather than repeating the "still 0" framing.
