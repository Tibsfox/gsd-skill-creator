## Lessons Learned

1. Five electronic-adjacent entries in nine degrees (86, 90, 91, 93, 94) demonstrate that Seattle's electronic landscape is deep enough to sustain this density without repetition. Five degrees, five purposes, one technology. The engine is mapping not just electronic music but the full range of what electronics do in a city's music ecology
2. The producer-as-artist lineage (Walla 67, Blood 94) is a genuine structural pattern in the Seattle music ecology -- recording engineers who become solo artists, bringing studio sensibility to creative work. With two instances 27 degrees apart, the lineage is confirmed as a pattern, not an anomaly
3. The stonefly as stream indicator introduces a fundamentally new type of contribution to the species engine: the diagnostic species. No prior species functioned explicitly as an indicator of ecosystem health. The stonefly's presence IS the data. This concept -- species as diagnostic tool -- may apply to other entries retroactively (the American Dipper at 52 is also a stream indicator, though not documented as such)
4. The return to streams after 37 degrees demonstrates that the engine can sustain long habitat gaps and then return with fresh perspective. The stonefly approaches the stream from an entirely different angle than the Belted Kingfisher (57) -- living IN the water rather than watching from above. Long gaps can create productive tension that makes the return more meaningful
5. The E=5-to-E=2 energy drop (hornet 93 to stonefly 94) mirrors the E=2-to-E=5 rise (bumblebee 92 to hornet 93). The species energy oscillates around extremes as the insect era deepens. This oscillation pattern -- minimum to maximum to minimum in three degrees -- creates dramatic structural texture
6. Eight insect entries in eight degrees (87-94) spanning six orders, seven families, and three sound mechanisms plus four acoustic postures (mating, advertising, incidental, defensive) plus one emergence sound is the deepest sustained exploration of any taxon class. The insect era is becoming a sub-engine within the engine
7. Thirty-four consecutive original CSV species (61-94) continues to demonstrate data reliability
8. The gap analysis persists: no raptor, no passerine since degree 56, no marine species. These gaps are now extreme and will need to be addressed
