Harbor seal, orca, sea lion, common loon, marbled murrelet — none have appeared. For an engine titled around Puget Sound, the marine omission grows more conspicuous with each non-marine degree.

## Lessons Learned

- **The producer-as-artist lineage is a tracked engine pattern.** With **Chris Walla** at degree 67 (indie rock production) and **Erik Blood** at degree 94 (experimental electronic and hip-hop production), two instances 27 degrees apart confirm the producer-as-artist as an engine lineage rather than an isolated case. Recording engineers who become solo artists, bringing studio sensibility to creative work, deserve dedicated lineage tracking alongside the Olympia lo-fi, Anacortes hermit, dream pop duo, and Death Cab orbit threads. Future producer-artist entries should be classified against this lineage.
- **Five electronic-adjacent entries in nine degrees demonstrate the Pacific Northwest's electronic depth.** Each entry maps a different relationship with electronics — pop structure (Postal Service), atmosphere (Sky Cries Mary), obliteration (Hovercraft), intimacy (NAVVI), experimentation (Erik Blood). Five degrees, five purposes, one technology. Not every region's music ecology would support this density without repetition. The Pacific Northwest's electronic landscape can sustain five distinct stages in nine degrees, which is itself a measure of source-community depth.
- **The stonefly introduces the diagnostic species as a category.** No prior species in the engine functioned explicitly as an indicator of ecosystem health. The stonefly's presence IS the data. This opens a new contribution category for the species engine: species as diagnostic tool. The concept can be applied retroactively (the American Dipper at degree 52 is also a stream indicator) and prospectively (future indicator species — keystone species, sentinel species, umbrella species — can be classified explicitly).
- **The engine can sustain long habitat gaps and return with fresh perspective.** Thirty-seven degrees without a stream species (57–94), then a return that adds the perspective the prior species could not — the stream as a place to inhabit for years rather than to hunt over. Long gaps can create productive tension that makes the return more meaningful. Future habitat returns should consider how they extend the engine's view of the habitat rather than merely revisiting it.
- **The species energy ledger oscillates around extremes as the Insect Era deepens.** The E=5-to-E=2 drop (hornet 93 to stonefly 94) mirrors the E=2-to-E=5 rise (bumblebee 92 to hornet 93). An E=2 → E=5 → E=2 oscillation across three consecutive degrees creates dramatic structural texture. Future degrees in deep taxon-class runs should expect energy oscillation around extremes rather than smooth interpolation. The pattern is now a tested engine behavior.
- **Eight insect entries in eight degrees is the new ceiling for taxon-class depth.** The Insect Era spans six orders, seven families, three sound mechanisms plus the emergence-rustle category, four prior postures plus the new passive-emergence posture. Plan for taxon-class runs of eight or more degrees rather than treating four-deep runs as the maximum. - **The studio-as-instrument philosophy is engine-trackable.** Erik Blood treats the studio as the instrument the music is played through. This philosophy distinguishes producer-artists from artists who work with producers and gives the producer's palette acoustic element its specific identity.
