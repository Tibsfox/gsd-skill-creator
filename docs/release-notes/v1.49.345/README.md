## Summary

**Childbirth** arrives at E=6 -- the **feminist supergroup** formed from members of Chastity Belt (Julia Shapiro), Tacocat (Bree McKenna), and Pony Time (Stacy Peck). Childbirth is the convergence point of the Hardly Art streak: three bands that shared stages, labels, and friendships collapsing into a single project that is louder, faster, and more deliberately confrontational than any of its constituent parts. *It's a Girl!* (2014) and *Women's Rights* (2015) on Hardly Art are punk records in the purest sense -- short, fast, funny, and angry, with song titles that function as manifestos. At E=6, Childbirth matches Tacocat (203) and closes the **Hardly Art streak at four consecutive degrees** (201-204), the longest single-label run in the engine. On the species side, the **Mountain Goat kid** registers at E=3 -- the lowest-energy mammal vocalization in this stretch and a deliberate mirror of the fawn entry at degree 195. The kid's call is a thin, high-pitched bleat produced by young Mountain Goats to maintain contact with their mothers on exposed alpine terrain. At E=3, the kid call is barely audible above the wind -- a sound that must compete with the harshest acoustic environment in the Pacific Northwest. The Mountain Goat pair (203-204) closes with vulnerability, matching the Black-tailed Deer pair's pattern of adult-then-young.
