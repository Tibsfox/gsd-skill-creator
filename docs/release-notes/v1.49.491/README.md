First, widespread weak-layer failure across multiple aspects — the snowpack must have developed a layer of weakness (often a buried surface-hoar layer or a crust interface) that is continuous across a large geographic area rather than local to a single slope. Third, sympathetic triggering — a large initial release on one slope can destabilize adjacent loaded slopes through mechanical shock or through the removal of a buttressing snow mass. The three conditions do not commonly align.
