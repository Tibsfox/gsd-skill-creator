## Darek Mazzone -- The Global Ear

Darek Mazzone has been at KEXP since **1996** -- like Cheryl Waters, he bridges the KCMU and KEXP eras. But where Waters curates independent music broadly and Richards curates with emotional intensity, Mazzone curates **globally**. His show **Wo' Pop** (World Population) is dedicated to music from around the planet -- African pop, Latin American folk, Middle Eastern electronic, South Asian classical, Pacific Island roots, and everything in between.

Wo' Pop is a statement. In a city whose music narrative is dominated by grunge, indie rock, and electronic music, Mazzone insists that the world is making music too, and Seattle should be listening. The show treats global music not as a novelty or an exotic curiosity but as a peer tradition -- equally vital, equally innovative, equally deserving of a dedicated time slot on a major independent station.

E=4. Higher than Waters (E=3) and Richards (E=3). Mazzone is a DJ as well as a host -- the "Radio/DJ" designation reflects his active mixing and sonic curation, particularly in Wo' Pop segments where he blends tracks from different traditions. The energy comes from the DJ craft: the transitions, the juxtapositions, the sonic collisions between traditions that wouldn't normally share airspace.

## Greater White-fronted Goose -- The Laughing Call

The Greater White-fronted Goose (*Anser albifrons*) is the first **Anser** genus goose in the engine. After three consecutive *Branta* geese (Canada, Cackling, Brant), the genus shifts. *Anser* is the "gray goose" genus -- taxonomically distinct from the dark-necked *Branta* geese, with different morphology, different plumage patterns, and different vocalizations.

The sound: a **musical laughing call** -- a high-pitched, yodeling *kow-kow-kow* or *kah-lah-ah-luck* that sounds distinctly like laughter. In flight, flocks of Greater White-fronted Geese produce a cacophony of laughing calls that is immediately distinguishable from the deeper honking of Canada Geese or the rolling murmur of Brant. The call has a joyful, almost celebratory quality -- the species sounds like it's having a good time.

E=4. Same energy as Cackling Goose and Brant. The laughing call is distinctive and carries well, but the Greater White-fronted Goose is not as loud as the Canada Goose (E=5) or the Trumpeter Swan (E=6). The musicality compensates for the volume -- it's a memorable call, not a powerful one.

**"Specklebelly."** Hunters call the Greater White-fronted Goose "specklebelly" for the irregular dark barring on its belly -- a distinctive field mark among North American geese. The white front (forehead patch) gives the species its official name; the speckled belly gives it its colloquial one. The species is more common in western North America, with significant populations passing through the Pacific Northwest during migration.

Three *Branta*, then one *Anser*. The genus shift is significant -- it means the engine has now sampled both major goose genera present in the Pacific Northwest. *Branta* (dark-necked geese) and *Anser* (gray geese) represent different evolutionary lineages within Anatidae.

**Field habitat.** The Greater White-fronted Goose is primarily a bird of open fields, agricultural land, and grasslands -- similar to the Cackling Goose but without the same degree of coastal association. During migration and winter, they forage in flooded fields, grain stubble, and wet meadows.
