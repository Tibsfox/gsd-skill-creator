6. **The first-comet-sample-return achievement is the first cometary samples returned to Earth — distinct from the earlier comet flybys.**
   Stardust captured coma dust of comet 81P/Wild 2 and returned it on 15 January 2006; the catalog scopes this strictly to "the first mission to return samples of a comet to Earth," and notes that earlier comet missions only flew PAST (Giotto, ICE, the Halley armada), returning no material. The qualifier is held load-bearing.

7. **The first-interstellar-dust achievement is the first capture-and-return of contemporary interstellar dust — scoped to capture-and-return.**
   Stardust caught interstellar dust on the B-side of its aerogel collector and brought it home (~7 candidate grains identified); the catalog scopes this strictly to "the first to capture and return interstellar dust" and notes that Ulysses and Galileo only DETECTED interstellar dust in situ. The qualifier is held load-bearing.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12742_

8. **The fastest-reentry achievement is the fastest reentry of a human-made object into Earth's atmosphere.**
   The Sample Return Capsule reentered at ~12.9 km/s on 15 January 2006, surpassing the Apollo 10 command module's ~11.1 km/s; the catalog scopes this as "the fastest reentry of any human-made object returning to Earth (a record at the time)," and the record still stood as of the mid-2020s, ahead of OSIRIS-REx's ~12.4 km/s reentry in 2023. The qualifier is held load-bearing.
