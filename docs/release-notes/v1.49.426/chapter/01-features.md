## PopLlama Records


- Conrad Uno's Ballard label released the Fastbacks, Young Fresh Fellows, and a series of Pacific Northwest acts that defined smart, melodic independent rock; the label's aesthetic is distinct from Sub Pop's grunge-adjacent identity: PopLlama bands were funnier, more overtly pop, and operated in a register of self-aware cheerfulness that is its own valid Seattle tradition; degree 285 introduces the PopLlama thread to the engine's current sequence

## Ballard as music geography


- the Fastbacks played their early shows in Ballard and maintained a Ballard identity through their career; Ballard's place in Seattle music geography is distinct from Capitol Hill (Sub Pop, grunge) and Olympia (K Records, riot grrrl); it is a neighborhood with its own culture, its own bars, and its own relationship to the independent music scene; the Fastbacks are the engine's Ballard entry
