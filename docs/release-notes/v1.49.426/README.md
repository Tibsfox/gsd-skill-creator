**The Fastbacks** arrive at E=7 -- the Seattle punk pop band formed in 1979 by Kim Warnick (bass, vocals) and Kurt Bloch (guitar, vocals), joined through various lineups over a career spanning more than two decades. The Fastbacks are a foundational entry in the Seattle independent music record: they predate the grunge era by a decade, they outlasted it, and they documented the Seattle punk underground through PopLlama Records and a series of albums that established the template for Pacific Northwest punk pop -- fast, melodic, committed, unpretentious. The Fastbacks' PopLlama connection is significant. PopLlama Records, the Seattle independent label founded by Conrad Uno in 1983, released the Fastbacks alongside Young Fresh Fellows and a constellation of Pacific Northwest acts that defined a particular strain of melodic, hook-driven independent rock. The PopLlama aesthetic -- smart, funny, fast, local -- runs through the Fastbacks catalog and through the Seattle independent record more broadly. Degree 285 is the first PopLlama entry in the current sequence; the label's contribution to Seattle music is documented here.

**Elephant Seal Bull Roar** arrives at E=8 on the SPS track -- the extraordinary vocalizations of the Northern Elephant Seal (*Mirounga angustirostris*) bull: the deep, rhythmic, percussive gargling belch produced by an adult male using an inflatable proboscis that amplifies and distorts the sound into one of the most distinctive and imposing vocalizations in the Pacific coast pinniped record. The elephant seal bull is the largest pinniped in the Northern Hemisphere -- adult males reach 2,000 kg and 5 meters in length -- and the bull roar is the acoustic expression of that size: a sound that carries across crowded breeding beaches, identifies the individual, and signals the capacity for serious violence. The proboscis is not merely ornamentation; it functions as a resonating chamber that shapes the call into the distinctive gargling quality that makes elephant seal vocalizations immediately recognizable at considerable distance.

**FIRST PHOCIDAE.** The Elephant Seal is the first true seal -- family Phocidae -- to enter the SPS track. The previous marine mammal entries have been Delphinidae (orca, dolphin, porpoise), Balaenopteridae (humpback, blue), Physeteridae (sperm whale), and Otariidae (Steller sea lion, California sea lion). Phocidae are the earless seals: they lack external ear flaps, move on land differently than sea lions (belly locomotion versus the sea lion's ability to walk on flippers), and include the elephant seal, harbor seal, and spotted seal. The elephant seal's entry as the first Phocidae representative opens a new family chapter in the SPS marine mammal survey, and it opens it at E=8.
