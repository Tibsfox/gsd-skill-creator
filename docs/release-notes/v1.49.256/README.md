## Artist-City Patterns

- **Post-streak reset** -- after the Capitol Hill sextet (110-115), Pony Time breaks the neighborhood streak. Seattle, but not Capitol Hill-specific. The duo format (Luke Beetham and Stacy Peck) is the smallest possible band -- guitar and drums, nothing else. Stripped down. DIY. Self-released. No label, no producer, no intermediaries. The anti-infrastructure statement.
- **DIY duo** -- Pony Time is a two-piece. Guitar and drums. The smallest unit of rock music that can still be called a band. Self-released everything. Booked their own shows. Made their own merch. The indie rock DIY ethic taken to its logical minimum. After Matador Records (115) and Suicide Squeeze (113) and Up Records (112), the engine returns to no-label territory.
- **Indie rock as genre** -- not math rock, not noise, not post-punk, not krautrock. Straight indie rock. The genre without a modifier. After a run of hyphenated genres (Math Rock/Electronic, Noise/Indie, Post-Punk), Pony Time is simply indie rock. The unadorned thing itself.
- **Energy stabilization** -- third consecutive E=5 on the artist axis (113-114-115... 114 was E=6, so: 113=5, 115=5, 116=5). The engine finds a middle register after the E=6 spikes.

## Taxonomic State

- Classes: 2 (Aves, Mammalia)

- Picidae nonet: CLOSED at 115 (107-115, 9 consecutive, all-time record)
- FIRST Cinclidae: American Dipper opens a new family
- American Dipper (*Cinclus mexicanus*): North America's only aquatic songbird. Walks underwater on stream bottoms to feed on aquatic invertebrates. Bobs constantly (the "dipping" behavior). Nictitating membrane for underwater vision. Nasal flaps to keep water out. Dense plumage with extra-large preen gland. Built for water. Sings year-round, even in winter, a loud bubbly song that carries over the sound of rushing water.
- Fire-to-water transition: Black-backed WP in burn forest (115) to American Dipper in mountain streams (116). The ecosystem shifts from fire-disturbed conifer to cold, fast-flowing water. Two extremes of Pacific Northwest habitat in consecutive degrees.
