1. **The first one-ninth of the circle closes with a milestone, not a pause.**
   Degree 40 = 1/9 of 360. The fortieth degree does not punctuate the engine; it synchronizes multiple cumulative progressions (40 acoustic elements, 40 blue note deployments, 40 artist-city patterns) to a natural-number landmark. Future multiples of 40 (80, 120, 160, 200, 240, 280, 320, 360) should be tracked as milestone synchronizations and used as natural audit points for cumulative statistics.

2. **Negative acoustic elements are a legitimate taxonomy expansion.**
   The shelved frequency is the first acoustic element defined by suppression rather than production. The thinning is the first acoustic element whose name carries ecological meaning. Both expand the acoustic taxonomy along axes the engine had not previously acknowledged. Future degrees should watch for elements that are defined by what is prevented, by what is ecological, or by what is meta-linguistic -- not just by what is produced as sound.

3. **Consecutive genus trios are structurally different from consecutive family runs.**

The *Setophaga* trio (38-39-40) maps a displacement drama: bystander, aggressor, victim. Future consecutive-same-genus runs should be classified as survey vs. drama vs. mechanism-map up front, because the narrative stakes are very different across those categories.

4. **The label-freedom dialectic is a five-stage arc, not a three-stage arc.**
   Alice Smith's Epic Records chapter documented the full cycle: independent debut, corporate capture, crowdfunded liberation, mature independence, and full-circle re-engagement on her own terms. All five stages are present in one career. The engine's prior label-thread degrees had each documented one or two stages; degree 40 is the first to show the complete arc. Future label-thread degrees should be coded against the five-stage frame.
