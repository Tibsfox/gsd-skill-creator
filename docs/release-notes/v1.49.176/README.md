## Summary

The shelved frequency and the thinning. Degree 40 is a milestone -- the completion of the first one-ninth of the 360-degree circle (40/360 = 1/9) -- and it arrives carrying the weight of suppression on both sides. **Alice Smith** is the neo-soul artist whose sophomore album was recorded, completed, and shelved by Epic Records, the engine's first Sony-family major label. **Hermit Warbler** is the species being displaced by the Townsend's Warbler documented one degree earlier -- the victim arriving immediately after the aggressor, the engine telling both sides of the same ecological drama across consecutive degrees. The shelved frequency (Part A's 41st acoustic element) is the engine's first **negative** acoustic element, defined not by a sound that is produced but by a sound that is prevented. The thinning (Part B's 41st acoustic element) is what happens to a song -- and a species -- that is being displaced: thinner, higher, stretched to the point of disappearance. Both sides of degree 40 document loss through constraint. One institutional. One biological. Both real.

Alice Smith -- born November 30, 1977, raised between Washington, D.C. and Augusta, Georgia. Four-octave vocal range forged in the Georgetown Visitation gospel choir, career launched at Fordham University in New York City where she performed with the **Black Rock Coalition** -- the organization founded by Vernon Reid (Living Colour), Greg Tate, and Konda Mason to promote Black artists in rock and experimental music. Her debut album *For Lovers, Dreamers & Me* (2006, BBE Records) earned a Grammy nomination for Best Urban/Alternative Performance ("Dream") and Rolling Stone named her one of "10 Artists to Watch in 2006," noting: "Smith could easily be lumped in with expressive chanteuses like Norah Jones and Alicia Keys, but she has a broader palette than either." Then Epic Records signed her. Then Epic Records shelved her. Between 2007 and 2012, an entire album's worth of material was recorded, re-recorded, delayed, subjected to label restructuring and attempts to reshape her sound toward commercial R&B formulas. Smith resisted. Years passed. The sophomore album was never released. This is the label-suppression pattern: the major-label system that promises amplification delivering suppression instead. The engine that has documented KEXP amplification (degrees 1, 24), self-released independence (the dominant label mode across forty degrees), and now documents the opposite -- institutional capture followed by creative constraint.

Smith left Epic and funded her second album ***She*** through Kickstarter (released March 19, 2013, Brooklyn Recordings). Seven years between albums. Track four -- "Another Love" -- caught the attention of **Prince**, who covered it on *Plectrumelectrum* (2014) with 3rdeyegirl. When Prince covers your song, the lineage is confirmed. Prince's own genre-fluid practice (funk, rock, pop, soul, jazz, psychedelia) mirrors Smith's. The Kickstarter liberation was the suppressed signal finding an alternative transmission path. After the self-released *Mystery* EP (2019) and a Nina Simone tribute contribution ("I Put a Spell on You," 2015), the full-circle moment arrived: the *Sinners* soundtrack (2025, Ryan Coogler's film starring Michael B. Jordan), released through **Sony Masterworks** -- Smith returned to the Sony corporate family that had shelved her, but now as a collaborator, not a contracted artist. At the **98th Academy Awards** (March 15, 2026), she performed "I Lied to You" alongside Miles Caton, Raphael Saadiq, Brittany Howard, Buddy Guy, Christone "Kingfish" Ingram, Misty Copeland, and Bobby Rush. An artist shelved by Sony subsidiary Epic Records performing on the biggest stage in entertainment, distributed by Sony Masterworks. The irony is structural. The arc is complete.

One word for three kinds of diminishment. The engine's first acoustic element that is simultaneously sonic, geographic, and genetic.

The *Setophaga* quartet -- Yellow (16), Black-throated Gray (38), Townsend's (39), Hermit (40) -- maps a chromatic, acoustic, and vertical gradient across the engine. **Plumage:** Yellow Warbler = maximum yellow everywhere; Black-throated Gray = near-achromatic, one yellow dot; Townsend's = bold yellow-and-black face with yellow breast; Hermit = entirely yellow face on plain gray body (all chromatic budget concentrated on the face beacon at the top of a 40-meter mountain hemlock). **Song:** Yellow = clear ascending crescendo; Black-throated Gray = husky flat drawl; Townsend's = buzzy ascending canopy buzz; Hermit = thin, high, reedy, variable, attenuated. Each step thins the sound. **Height:** Yellow at 3-12 m (riparian willow), Black-throated Gray at 5-15 m (oak-pine mid-canopy), Townsend's at 15-45 m (conifer crown), Hermit at 20-50 m (mountain conifer extreme crown). The Hermit Warbler pushes the warbler vertical gradient to its absolute limit. Above this, there are no warblers -- only sky. The displaced species occupies the highest point the family can reach, as if it has been pushed upward and outward by the species below it.

The competitive exclusion operates through behavioral dominance (Townsend's males respond more aggressively to territorial intrusions), hybrid mating asymmetry (offspring mate preferentially with Townsend's-like individuals), and allelic introgression (Townsend's alleles flow deeply into Hermit populations while the reverse flow is weaker). Both species occupy the upper conifer canopy with no clear niche separation allowing stable coexistence. The conservation question, acute at degree 40: if the Townsend's Warbler absorbs the Hermit Warbler through introgression, is that extinction? The genetic material is not destroyed -- Hermit alleles persist in hybrid genomes. The phenotype is being replaced -- fewer and fewer birds look like pure Hermit Warblers. The species concept depends on definition. The song changes in hybrids. If pure Hermit songs disappear, the thinning -- the acoustic element -- disappears from the landscape. The engine does not resolve this paradox. It documents both sides. Degree 39 presented the expanding species. Degree 40 presents the contracting species. The listener holds both.

The Hermit Warbler is a high-canopy specialist in mountain conifer forests: white fir, red fir, Douglas fir, mountain hemlock, incense cedar at mid to high elevations. Even stronger canopy affinity than the Townsend's -- one of the most canopy-bound warblers in North America, rarely descending to eye level during breeding season. Unlike Townsend's dual winter strategy (tropical + PNW coast), the Hermit Warbler is strictly a long-distance migrant wintering in highland pine-oak forests of Mexico and Central America. No PNW wintering. It abandons the PNW entirely each fall and returns each spring to a range that may have contracted slightly further south in its absence.
