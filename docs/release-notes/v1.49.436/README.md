**Malfunkshun** arrive at E=7 -- the Seattle glam punk band built around **Andrew Wood** (vocals, bass), one of the most singular figures in the history of Pacific Northwest rock. Malfunkshun formed in 1980 on Bainbridge Island -- making degree 295 the **FIRST BAINBRIDGE ISLAND** entry in the Seattle 360 engine -- and Wood developed the band's identity through the decade, arriving at a vision of rock performance that owed as much to Marc Bolan and David Bowie as to anything in Seattle's punk or grunge tradition. He called himself "L'Andrew the Love Child." He wore costumes. He wrote songs of theatrical extravagance in a scene that prized directness and unadorned honesty. He was, by all accounts of people who were present, extraordinarily charismatic -- a performer capable of transforming a small club into something larger than itself.

Malfunkshun is degree two of the proto-grunge origin arc (294-296). The sequence is: Green River (294) → Malfunkshun (295) → Mother Love Bone (296). Andrew Wood was the thread connecting the last two points. He played bass in Malfunkshun while simultaneously becoming the vocalist of the formed-from-Green River-fragments band that Ament and Gossard brought together around him. Malfunkshun and Mother Love Bone overlapped; Wood was central to both. At degree 295 the engine documents the earlier vessel -- the Bainbridge band, the glam punk original -- because what happened in Malfunkshun is what made Andrew Wood recognizable enough to become the front of Mother Love Bone. The charisma was already fully formed.

The glam punk register at E=7 is accurate. Malfunkshun's energy is high -- theatrical, committed, operating at the intersection of glam's visual theatrics and punk's velocity -- but the E=7 ceiling reflects the fact that glam punk, even at its most intense, operates in a different register from the hardcore that reached E=9 earlier in the sequence. E=7 for Malfunkshun is the correct acknowledgment of what the band achieved: a complete and fully realized vision of rock theatrics in a scene that initially didn't know what to do with it.

**Rhinoceros Auklet** arrives at E=6 on the SPS track -- the second consecutive Alcidae entry, documenting *Cerorhinca monocerata*, the alcid that nests in large burrow colonies on Washington's offshore islands and is the most abundant alcid breeding in Puget Sound proper. The Rhinoceros Auklet is named for the small horn at the base of its bill that appears during breeding season -- a feature unique within the alcid family. Its colony sounds are among the most distinctive of the alcid family: moaning calls produced from within burrows, audible above ground as a murmuring, subterranean chorus from a colony that may number in the thousands. The burrow moans are produced at night -- Rhinoceros Auklets return to their colonies after dark to avoid aerial predators -- giving the acoustic a nocturnal quality distinct from the diurnal seabird colonies.

The alcid arc continues: Tufted Puffin (294) and Rhinoceros Auklet (295) are two consecutive Alcidae entries on the SPS track. Both are Washington breeding alcids; both use burrow colonies; both produce colony sounds of modest individual register but significant aggregate acoustic presence. E=6 for the Rhinoceros Auklet is the correct assignment, matching the Tufted Puffin at the same score for similar reasons.
