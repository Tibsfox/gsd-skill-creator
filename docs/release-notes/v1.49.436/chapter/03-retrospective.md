## Lessons Learned

1. **Middle-of-arc entries are structurally load-bearing and should be preserved at first-class resolution rather than as transition points between the arc-opening and arc-closing entries.** The 294-296 Proto-Grunge Origin Arc's middle degree at 295 is **Malfunkshun**, and the middle position registers **Andrew Wood's** Bainbridge Island vessel as a first-class documentary subject rather than as a preliminary sketch for the Mother Love Bone band that would follow at degree 296. The lesson is that arc-middle entries carry structural weight that is not reducible to the arc-opening or arc-closing entries, and the engine's documentation vocabulary should preserve middle-entry subjects at first-class resolution with full biographical, compositional, and geographic documentation rather than treating them as transitions.

2. **Island-origin documentation registers at equivalent structural resolution to mainland-origin documentation and warrants first-class geographic treatment.** Malfunkshun's **Bainbridge Island** origin establishes the FIRST BAINBRIDGE ISLAND register in the engine's S36 documentation, and the 35-minute ferry crossing to the Seattle mainland is the physical arc of the band's Seattle-scene presence. The lesson is that island-origin geographic documentation is not subordinate to mainland-origin geographic documentation; the two categories register at equivalent structural resolution, and the engine's S36 geographic vocabulary should be expanded to include island-origin first-class treatment alongside the previously-dominant mainland-origin registers as the engine encounters subsequent island-origin subjects in the remaining degrees.

3. **Posthumous release documentation is structurally load-bearing for bands whose studio catalogs were bounded by the lead member's death during active career.** Malfunkshun's *Return to Olympus* (1995, **Loosegroove Records**) is the posthumous compilation assembled five years after **Andrew Wood's** March 1990 death, and the posthumous assembly is itself part of the historical record — a band whose primary documentary footprint is concentrated in a compilation appearance, demo recordings, and a posthumous compilation assembled by surviving personnel. The lesson is that posthumous releases should be preserved at first-class resolution alongside the life-released catalog, because the posthumous record is often the closest available document of the band's complete studio identity for bands whose life-active catalog was cut short by the lead member's early death.

4. **Nocturnal acoustic registers require temporally bounded documentation and warrant explicit notation of the temporal window.** The Rhinoceros Auklet's moaning chorus at 300-700 Hz is concentrated entirely in the nocturnal colony-return window (approximately two hours after sunset to approximately two hours before sunrise during the April-through-August breeding season at Pacific Northwest colonies), with minimal acoustic presence during daylight hours when the birds are at sea foraging. The lesson is that nocturnal-acoustic documentation should explicitly specify the temporal window rather than treating the species' acoustic register as continuously available, because the nocturnal concentration is structurally distinct from the diurnal-or-continuous registers that gull and tern colonies produce and the temporal specification is necessary for accurate acoustic documentation.

5. **Two-species family arcs with diurnal-and-nocturnal temporal complements produce complete family documentation at first-class resolution in a two-degree span.** The 294-295 Alcidae family arc covers the full temporal range of outer coast rocky island alcid colony behavior through the Tufted Puffin's diurnal register and the Rhinoceros Auklet's nocturnal register, and the two-degree span produces complete family documentation without requiring additional Alcidae entries in the near term. The lesson is that short family arcs (two degrees) can produce first-class family documentation when the selected species carry temporally or behaviorally complementary registers, and the engine's family-arc vocabulary should register two-species arcs as a productive mode alongside the longer three-degree or four-degree family arcs that the engine has previously documented.
