## ANDREW WOOD -- handled with respect


- Andrew Wood died of a heroin overdose on March 19, 1990, at 24, days before Mother Love Bone's debut album *Apple* was released. His death precipitated the creation of Temple of the Dog (degree 225) and directly led to the formation of Pearl Jam. The engine documents him here, in Malfunkshun, as the earlier version: the Bainbridge Island kid who built a band around theatrical ambition and arrived at a Seattle scene that didn't have a category for him. He made a category. The engine records this without sentimentality and without reduction. Andrew Wood was an extraordinary musician who died young. Both facts are true. The arc documents the work.

## FIRST BAINBRIDGE ISLAND


- Malfunkshun's Bainbridge Island origin makes degree 295 the first island-origin entry in the Seattle 360 engine. Bainbridge is not Seattle proper but it is the island 35 minutes by ferry from the downtown waterfront, connected to the city's cultural life through the ferry route and the shared understanding that the Sound's islands are part of the Seattle ecosystem. Wood crossed that water to play music in Seattle. The engine acknowledges the island.

## Proto-grunge origin arc degree 2 of 3 (294-296)


- Malfunkshun is the middle degree of the arc. Green River (294) is the founding; Malfunkshun (295) is the parallel development; Mother Love Bone (296) is the synthesis. Wood was present at the convergence point. His glam sensibility -- the theatrics, the literary lyrics, the costumes, the refusal to be merely loud -- altered the aesthetic direction of what became Mother Love Bone and left its mark on the Seattle scene's definition of what a rock front person could be.

## Bainbridge to Capitol Hill


- the geographic movement from Bainbridge Island (Malfunkshun's origin) to the Capitol Hill clubs where Wood performed is the physical arc of his career: from the ferry landing to the stage, the 35-minute crossing repeated until the island was a place of origin and Seattle was the arena
