## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Strigidae (7th entry -- GHO 135, GHO duet 136, Barred 137, Barred caterwaul 138, Long-eared 139, Short-eared 140, Northern Saw-whet 141)
- **Order:** Strigiformes (continuing, 7-degree owl run)
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The Northern Saw-whet Owl (*Aegolius acadicus*) is one of the smallest owls in the PNW -- robin-sized, roughly 18 cm. Named for its call's resemblance to the sound of a saw being sharpened on a whetstone. The tooting call is a monotonous, repetitive series -- too-too-too -- delivered at rates up to 130 per minute, sometimes for hours without pause. Fall migration is the great mystery: massive numbers detected at banding stations along mountain ridges, but the triggers, routes, and destinations remain poorly understood. A nocturnal migrant that moves in waves, invisible except to those running nets.
