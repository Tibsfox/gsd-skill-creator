1. **Genre superposition (two modes coexisting without merging) is a fundamentally different structural event than genre fusion (two genres merging into a new compound), and the engine must track both.**
   Lorber's Jazz Fusion is fusion: jazz AND funk become jazz-funk, a new compound with its own identity. Watts's Jazz/Comedy is superposition: jazz and comedy coexist without merging -- neither absorbs the other, neither is subordinate, both operate at full intensity simultaneously. Fusion produces a new genre. Superposition produces the absence of genre. Both are valid structural events. Both break the single-genre label. But they break it in different ways, and the engine should distinguish between them at subsequent degrees. When future artists combine modes, the question is: does the combination merge into a new identity (fusion) or coexist without merging (superposition)? The answer determines whether the engine creates a new genre label or declares the artist uncategorizable.
