# Retrospective — v1.49.78

## What Worked

1. **The cognition module establishes corvids as cognitive peers.** Marzluff's facial recognition, Clayton's episodic memory, and Bugnyar's theory of mind evidence — documented with specific researcher names and study citations — establishes that corvids are not just smart birds but cognitive beings with capabilities that parallel primate intelligence through convergent evolution.

2. **The cultural significance module names specific nations.** 8 named nations (Tlingit, Haida, Tsimshian, Kwakwaka'wakw, Heiltsuk, Coast Salish, Koyukon, Inuit) rather than generic "indigenous peoples." OCAP and CARE data sovereignty principles acknowledged. This is the same specificity that every cultural module in the series requires.

## Lessons Learned

1. **The watcher-watcher relationship is unique.** Corvids remember individual human faces and pass that information to offspring. No other PNW wildlife species has this documented capacity. The corvid studies the human studying the corvid. This reflexive relationship is why corvids hold such cultural significance — they are the one animal that watches back.
