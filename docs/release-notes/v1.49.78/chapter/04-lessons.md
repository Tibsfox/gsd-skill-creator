1. **The watcher-watcher relationship is unique.**
   Corvids remember individual human faces and pass that information to offspring. No other PNW wildlife species has this documented capacity. The corvid studies the human studying the corvid. This reflexive relationship is why corvids hold such cultural significance — they are the one animal that watches back.
