## Summary

The 78th Research project and the most cognitively rich wildlife study in the series. CRV (Corvid PNW) maps the crows and ravens of the Pacific Northwest — from the 2020 AOS taxonomic merger that split Northwestern Crow from American Crow through Marzluff's facial recognition research at UW, Clayton & Dickinson's episodic memory findings, Bugnyar's theory of mind evidence, the wolf-raven ecological partnership, the Redmond 2023-2025 roost migration, and the cultural significance across 8 named First Nations from Tlingit Raven Creator to Coast Salish Crow.

Six modules map the species: taxonomy and field identification (including the Slager et al. genomic evidence that dissolved Northwestern Crow), cognition and intelligence (convergent evolution of corvid and primate brains, tool use, consciousness framework), ecology and habitat (4 PNW zones, niche partitioning, cooperative vs. monogamous breeding), social behavior (UW Bothell roost analysis, 30+ raven call types, fission-fusion dynamics), cultural significance (8 nations, Bill Reid's sculpture, formline art, OCAP/CARE principles), and threats (West Nile Virus 45% decline data, raven predation impacts on Marbled Murrelet and Snowy Plover).

CRV extends the PNW wildlife cluster alongside AVI (Birds), MAM (Mammals), and FFA (Fur & Feathers). Where AVI surveys 250+ bird species across 7 ecoregions, CRV deep-dives into the corvid family — the species that watches the watcher. The TIBS (Animal Speak) connection is direct: Raven is the ultimate trickster-teacher in PNW indigenous traditions.

Named "Raven Steals the Sun" — the Tlingit creation narrative where Raven takes the form of a pine needle, is swallowed by the chief's daughter, is reborn as a child, and opens the boxes containing the sun, moon, and stars. The first act of light in the world was a corvid trick.

## Retrospective

### What Worked

1. **The cognition module establishes corvids as cognitive peers.** Marzluff's facial recognition, Clayton's episodic memory, and Bugnyar's theory of mind evidence — documented with specific researcher names and study citations — establishes that corvids are not just smart birds but cognitive beings with capabilities that parallel primate intelligence through convergent evolution.

2. **The cultural significance module names specific nations.** 8 named nations (Tlingit, Haida, Tsimshian, Kwakwaka'wakw, Heiltsuk, Coast Salish, Koyukon, Inuit) rather than generic "indigenous peoples." OCAP and CARE data sovereignty principles acknowledged. This is the same specificity that every cultural module in the series requires.

### Lessons Learned

1. **The watcher-watcher relationship is unique.** Corvids remember individual human faces and pass that information to offspring. No other PNW wildlife species has this documented capacity. The corvid studies the human studying the corvid. This reflexive relationship is why corvids hold such cultural significance — they are the one animal that watches back.
