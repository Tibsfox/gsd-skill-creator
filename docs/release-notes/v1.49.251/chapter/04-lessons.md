1. **Sustained corridors can appear after apparent correction.**
   After the E=6 three-peat ended at degree 110 and Kinski corrected to E=5, the expectation was that the maximum-energy corridor had closed. Helms Alee's return to E=6 at degree 111 reopened the corridor and established that a single-degree correction does not necessarily end a corridor, and that corridors operating at four of five degrees rather than at three of three are real structural phenomena. Future corridor documentation should track sustained-rate rather than consecutive-rate and should resist concluding corridors prematurely after first interruption.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #6446_

2. **Paradox-driven mechanisms can reach the energy ceiling.**
   Helms Alee's E=6 arrives through the collision of maximum heaviness and maximum beauty — a paradox-driven mechanism distinct from production saturation, punk combustion, or density-driven noise wall. The corridor's four mechanisms (production saturation, combustion, density, paradox) enumerate the catalog's paths to E=6, and the paradox path demonstrates that contradictions held at full intensity can generate the ceiling where a single element alone could not reach it. Future E=6 documentation should classify by mechanism rather than by genre.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.265`) · lesson #6447_

3. **Builder-player unification is a distinct structural pattern worth naming.**
   Ben Verellen's role as both Helms Alee's guitarist and Verellen Amplifiers' builder collapses the distinction between tool maker and tool user — a pattern that has no direct precedent in the engine and that extends the Hendrix-era amp-as-instrument move into amp-as-instrument-built-by-player. The pattern is rare but not unique (Leo Fender, Jim Marshall, Randall Smith as partial precedents), and future documentation should treat builder-player unification as a candidate cultural pattern worth naming explicitly when it appears.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.299`) · lesson #6448_

4. **Same-genus-consecutive documentation enables fine-resolution scaling comparison.**
   Genus *Dryobates* across degrees 110-111 (Hairy + Downy) as the engine's first same-genus-consecutive sister pair produced sharp scaling comparisons that would have been diluted if the two species had been placed at non-consecutive degrees. The mode is valuable whenever the catalog permits, and future planning should select sister-genus pairs for consecutive documentation rather than distributing them across the rotation when same-genus comparisons are available.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #6449_

5. **Family streaks can sustain without monotony through disciplined acoustic diversity.**
   The Picidae five-streak (107-111) documented five distinct acoustic profiles (colonial laughter, solitary primal scream, resonant machine-gun drum, sharp peek plus stuttering drum, soft pik plus gentle rattle drum) without the monotony risk that the family-streak format carries. The streak's sustainability depends on selecting species whose acoustic profiles differ substantially, and the Picidae range across the 107-111 window validates the approach. Future family streaks should target species diversity within family rather than species clustering.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.255`) · lesson #6450_

6. **Paired-engine degree format tolerates extreme contrast as structural feature.**

The engine's pairing logic operates in both parallel-synchronized mode (degree 110's dual E=5 correction) and in extreme-contrast mode (degree 111's E=6 vs E=4 gap), and both modes produce legitimate findings. Future degree planning should not force synchronization when extreme contrast itself is the finding.
