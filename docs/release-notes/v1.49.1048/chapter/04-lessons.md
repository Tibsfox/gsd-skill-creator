1. **An axis can sustain by going back to its root, not only by adding a new observable.**
   The X-ray-astronomy axis sustains at obs#9 because Uhuru goes one step deeper than Einstein, back to the survey/catalog founding root the whole axis descends from — the first satellite launched specifically for X-ray astronomy and the first to survey the entire X-ray sky and catalog it, where every earlier effort saw only minutes or fragments. The distinction is the survey/catalog origin, the non-imaging scanning ancestor form, so the cadence continues the axis as a substrate-form-distinct sustain rather than rotating away.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12133_

2. **Continuation is by observable form, not by chronology.**
   Uhuru launched in 1970, the earliest of the whole axis, yet it sits at obs#9 because the axis is ordered by observable form. Uhuru is the historical and technological root from which the HEAO program (HEAO-1, HEAO-2 / Einstein) and Chandra descend — the ancestor — exactly as Einstein (1978) sits at obs#8 as the focusing-imaging origin even though it predates Chandra (1999); the axis records the form's root after recording its later perfection.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12134_

3. **The 1962-rocket boundary is the single most load-bearing Uhuru fact.**
   "Uhuru discovered the first cosmic X-ray source / the X-ray background" is FALSE — both Scorpius X-1 (the first X-ray source beyond the Solar System) and the diffuse cosmic X-ray background were the 18–19 June 1962 Aerobee 150 sounding ROCKET flight by Giacconi, Gursky, Paolini & Rossi (PRL 9, 439), eight-plus years before Uhuru. Uhuru owns the first dedicated SATELLITE, the first all-sky SURVEY, and the first CATALOG.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12135_

4. **Non-imaging is central, correct Uhuru content — the error is only the imaging/focusing claim.**
   Uhuru is a scanning collimator of proportional counters with no focusing optics and no image; "imaging," "focusing," "Wolter," "mirror," "arcsecond," and "grazing-incidence optics" route to Einstein and Chandra, never to Uhuru. Position came from the scan-modulation geometry (~1 arcminute best for the bright 2U sources, ~30 arcminutes for extended sources), not from a focused image. The first cosmic imaging X-ray telescope is Einstein / HEAO-2 (1978).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12136_

5. **A first can be a survey/catalog first without being a timing or spectroscopy first.**
   Uhuru's variability / pulsation work (Cen X-3, Cyg X-1) is a survey by-product — the dedicated timing-observatory form is RXTE and NICER — and Uhuru carried only broadband proportional counters, so it stamps no spectroscopy first (Chandra owns grating high-res spectroscopy; XRISM the microcalorimeter form). Uhuru's firsts are the dedicated-satellite, all-sky-survey-and-catalog, X-ray-binary-characterization, and galaxy-cluster-diffuse firsts.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12137_

6. **The science the survey enabled carries its nuances.**
   Cen X-3 and Her X-1 established accreting X-ray binaries as a class (Schreier 1972 established the BINARY nature; the accretion-neutron-star model is later theory); Cyg X-1 was ENABLED by Uhuru's variability and the 1971 transition (radio gave the position, optical the black-hole mass — Uhuru did not alone "discover a black hole"); and Uhuru established the EXTENT of cluster X-ray emission and surveyed the population (it did not discover cluster X-rays — 1966–71 rockets — and the hot-gas iron-line confirmation came later, 1976–77).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12138_

7. **A continuation keeps the predecessors' vocabulary and references them by name.**
   Because Uhuru sustains the X-ray-astronomy axis, the shared X-ray vocabulary is correct and kept, and Chandra (obs#1, soft/medium focusing), NuSTAR (obs#2, hard-band focusing), RXTE (obs#3, timing), IXPE (obs#4, polarimetry), NICER (obs#5, soft-band timing), Swift (obs#6, rapid-response transient), XRISM (obs#7, non-dispersive spectroscopy), and Einstein (obs#8, focusing-imaging origin) are referenced as the form complements — but Uhuru's own facts (the two back-to-back banks of collimated proportional counters, the all-sky survey, the 4U catalog, the accreting-binary class, the galaxy-cluster diffuse emission) and only its own five anchors are stamped; the predecessors' anchors and predecessor-specific hardware (Einstein's Wolter-I optics / HRI / IPC / SSS / FPCS / Van Speybroeck;…
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12139_

8. **Accuracy guardrails guard against the launch-site, name, end-of-life, and people-role classes of error.**
   Stating the launch as 12 December 1970 on a Scout B (S-175C) from the Italian-operated San Marco platform off Kenya (not Cape Canaveral / Kennedy); the name "Uhuru" given after orbit on the seventh anniversary of Kenyan independence (not "on Kenyan independence day"); operations ending March 1973 and reentry early April 1979 (~six years apart, never collapsed); Giacconi paired with AS&E (not MIT), Townsend with GSFC as project manager, Rossi with MIT as founding figure, and the instrument (AS&E) / bus (APL) / management (GSFC) not conflated; the detector as two back-to-back banks of 12 proportional counters with the 0.52° / 5.2° fields and the ~2–20 keV detectors versus the 2–6 keV catalog band; and the 4U catalog as exactly 339 sources — keeps the facts load-bearing and correct.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12140_
