6. **The first-orbit achievement is the first spacecraft to orbit an asteroid — distinct from the earlier flybys.**
   NEAR entered orbit around 433 Eros on 14 February 2000, the first human-made object to orbit a minor planet; the catalog scopes this strictly to "the first spacecraft to orbit an asteroid," and notes that earlier missions only flew PAST asteroids (Galileo's Gaspra/Ida flybys, NEAR's own Mathilde flyby). 7. **The first-landing achievement is the first spacecraft to land on an asteroid — scoped to ASTEROID.**
   NEAR made the first controlled descent and touchdown onto an asteroid's surface (Eros, 12 February 2001); the catalog scopes this strictly to "the first spacecraft to land on an asteroid" and does NOT broaden it to "first U.S. landing on a celestial body" (the U.S. soft-landed on the Moon with Surveyor in 1966). NEAR was an ORBITER, not designed as a lander — the touchdown was an end-of-mission bonus that succeeded. The qualifier is held load-bearing.

The qualifier is held load-bearing.
