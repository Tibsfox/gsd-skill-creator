## Summary

**Temple of the Dog** arrives at E=7 -- the band that existed for one album, one purpose, and one person who was no longer alive to receive it. The project was conceived by **Chris Cornell** (Soundgarden) as a tribute to **Andrew Wood**, the charismatic vocalist of Mother Love Bone who died of a heroin overdose in March 1990 at 24, just before Mother Love Bone's debut album *Apple* was released. Cornell wrote the songs and brought in the musicians who had been closest to Wood: Stone Gossard (guitar), Jeff Ament (bass), and Mike McCready (guitar) -- the future core of Pearl Jam -- plus Pearl Jam's incoming vocalist **Eddie Vedder**, who was handed a microphone and proceeded to co-write and record what became "Hunger Strike," one of the definitive documents of the entire Seattle era. The self-titled album *Temple of the Dog* (1991) was recorded on A&M Records before Pearl Jam had released their debut, before "Black Hole Sun" existed, before anyone knew that Soundgarden and Pearl Jam would become two of the best-selling rock bands of the decade. It was made as a gift to a dead friend, and it became something larger than grief. At E=7, Temple of the Dog is the highest-rated band in the raptor arc and one of the highest-rated entries in the engine's recent run -- a register that reflects not just the music's quality but the weight of what it carries. "Hunger Strike" is the document at the center: two voices, Cornell's soaring tenor and Vedder's emerging baritone, trading lines over a riff that Gossard wrote while processing Wood's death. The song was a hit before anyone knew it was a hit, circulating on cassette dubs through the Seattle underground before the album was released. On the species side, the **Northern Goshawk** arrives at E=6 and opens the **ACCIPITER TRIO** -- three consecutive degrees (225-227) covering the three North American accipiters, from largest to smallest. *Accipiter gentilis* is the apex of the genus: the goshawk that medieval falconers called the "cook's hawk" because it could take game large enough to feed a household, a bird of dense forest interiors, territorial to the point of attacking humans who approach its nest, and loud in a way that the previous raptor entries have not been. The *ka-ka-ka-ka* alarm call is the accipiter sound: rapid, repeated, carrying through forest canopy, designed to flush prey and warn rivals simultaneously. The Northern Goshawk is the forest raptor where the Bald Eagle is the shoreline raptor and the Osprey is the water raptor -- it defines the remaining ecological niche, and its alarm call is the opening statement of the accipiter trio.

## Key Features

- **Part A -- S36:** Temple of the Dog (Rock Supergroup, E=7) -- Andrew Wood tribute, Cornell + Vedder + Gossard + Ament + McCready, "Hunger Strike," A&M Records
- **Part B -- SPS:** Northern Goshawk (ka-ka-ka-ka alarm, E=6) -- ACCIPITER TRIO OPENS, largest North American accipiter, forest apex raptor
- Andrew Wood tribute -- Mother Love Bone was the bridge between Green River and Pearl Jam, and Andrew Wood was its center: a vocalist of extraordinary charisma and range, writing lyrics that were flamboyant and literary where grunge was guttural and compressed, referencing Marc Bolan and glam rock in a scene that had rejected everything glam represented; Wood's death came days before *Apple*'s release, making the album simultaneously a debut and a posthumous record; Temple of the Dog is the scene's collective grief made audible
- "Hunger Strike" as origin document -- the song was not planned; Eddie Vedder was brought in to sing backup on other tracks, Cornell heard him in the studio and knew the song needed two voices; Vedder improvised his first verse on the spot; the result is one of the most fortuitous collaborations in the history of rock music, and it documents the moment when Pearl Jam's eventual vocalist was still unknown, still finding his instrument, still becoming the thing he would become
- ACCIPITER TRIO OPENS (225-227) -- degree 225 begins three consecutive entries documenting the North American accipiters: Northern Goshawk (225), Cooper's Hawk (226), Sharp-shinned Hawk (227); the accipiters are the three forest hawks of North America, all belonging to the genus *Accipiter*, all characterized by short wings, long tails, and the rapid, maneuvering flight style suited to hunting through dense tree canopies; the trio moves from largest (Goshawk) to mid-size (Cooper's) to smallest (Sharp-shinned), covering the full size range of the genus
- GRUNGE SUPERGROUP CLUSTER continues (224-226) -- Temple of the Dog is the second consecutive supergroup entry after Mad Season (224); where Mad Season brought together Alice in Chains and Pearl Jam through recovery, Temple of the Dog brings together Soundgarden and the future Pearl Jam through grief; the two motivations -- recovery and loss -- form the emotional spine of the grunge supergroup cluster, documenting the scene's capacity for music-making under extraordinary personal pressure
- Northern Goshawk ecology -- the Goshawk is Washington's most aggressive raptor, documented attacking humans, horses, and dogs who approach nest sites; it nests in old-growth conifer forests, particularly in mature Douglas fir and western red cedar, and is a year-round resident in the Cascades; its prey includes grouse, squirrels, snowshoe hares, and other medium-to-large prey items; the *ka-ka-ka-ka* alarm call is one of the most recognizable sounds of the deep forest, and experienced birders treat it as an indicator of healthy old-growth habitat
- Cornell and Vedder as a vocal pair -- the recording brought together what would become two of the decade's most recognized rock voices before either had fully established their public identity; Cornell's voice on *Temple of the Dog* is the bridge between *Louder Than Love* (1989) and *Badmotorfinger* (1991), in the period when Soundgarden was transitioning from underground metal to something larger; Vedder's voice is the voice of a 21-year-old who had just moved to Seattle from San Diego and was about to change everything
