## Lessons Learned

1. **Cross-regional institutional anchors deserve first-class structural treatment rather than being framed as biographical details.** The Rhymesayers Entertainment partnership at degree 162, like the Tacoma origin at degree 143 and the Spokane origin at degree 160, is a structural event rather than an incidental biographical detail. The lesson for future cross-regional institutional placements is that institutional anchors should be named, counted, and referenced in subsequent documentation ("first cross-regional indie-label anchor," "second cross-regional indie-label anchor," and so on) so that the engine's institutional footprint is trackable across the rotation.
2. **Hip-hop-era paradigm documentation benefits from four or more distinct registers rather than one dominant paradigm.** The hip-hop era (134-162) documents four paradigms (boom-bap, experimental, horn-driven-anthem, melodic-introspective) that together represent the breadth of Seattle hip-hop's sonic vocabulary. The lesson for future long-genre-era documentation is that paradigm-multiplicity should be framed as ecological breadth rather than as stylistic competition, and the engine should document as many distinct registers as the era genuinely contains rather than collapsing variation into a single dominant paradigm.
3. **Multi-part diagnostic vocalizations elevate acoustic identification beyond single-call thresholds.** The Sora's whinny + keek compound vocalization at degree 162, like the American Bittern's pump-er-LUNK at degree 163, demonstrates that multi-part calls can serve as first-class structural acoustic elements in the engine's species vocabulary. The lesson for future species with compound vocalizations is that multi-element call structures deserve explicit framing with functional-register contrast (territorial vs. alert, contact vs. alarm) so that the compound vocabulary becomes referenceable for future cross-species acoustic comparisons.
4. **Family comparison pairs (two-entry) work well when the family's PNW representation is compact.** The Rallidae pair (161-162), like the Odontophoridae pair (159-160), documents the family in two entries that suit the reality of only two species being commonly encountered in the Pacific Northwest. The lesson for future species-family documentation is that compact pairs work when the family's regional representation is compact, and the two-entry structure enables six-dimension contrast depth without the pacing demands of a six-entry arc. The paired-pair symmetry (Odontophoridae closes Galliformes; Rallidae opens wetland triad) also establishes a cadence for future family-comparison pair structures.
5. **Descending-pitch contour cross-sync parallels elevate paired-coordinate acoustic observations.** Both halves of degree 162 feature descending-pitch contour as a signature sonic element — Grieves's melodic-introspective sung hooks and the Sora's descending whinny cascade. The lesson for future cross-sync parallels is that shared acoustic-structural patterns at the level of pitch-contour, rhythmic structure, or tonal register elevate individual-axis observations into multiplicative structural statements that neither half alone could support. Acoustic-structural parallels should be tracked with the same rigor as biographical or habitat parallels.

The lesson for future long-distance-migrant species is that migration-distance should be framed as continental ecological connectivity rather than as an isolated species trait, and the engine's species documentation should preserve continental-scale network context for species whose ecology depends on long-distance migration.
