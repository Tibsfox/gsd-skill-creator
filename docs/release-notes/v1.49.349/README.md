## Summary

**Pickwick** arrives at E=6 -- and with them, **FIRST BLUE-EYED SOUL enters the Seattle 360 engine as Genre 108**. Galen Disston's voice is the centerpiece: a rich, full-bodied baritone that channels Otis Redding, Van Morrison, and Sam Cooke through the filter of a pale kid from the Pacific Northwest. Pickwick emerged from Seattle's Central District scene (following The Black Tones at 206) with *Can't Talk Medicine* (2012), a record that proved soul music could be made authentically in a city known for grunge. The band's horn section, vintage organ tones, and Disston's commanding vocal presence produce a sound that is warm, propulsive, and deeply unfashionable in the best possible way -- soul music in an indie rock town, delivered without irony. At E=6, Pickwick earns its rating through Disston's vocal power alone. On the species side, the **Coyote howl** registers at E=6 -- and **Canidae returns** to the SPS engine. The Coyote (*Canis latrans*, literally "barking dog") is the great survivor of North American predators: while wolves were extirpated from most of their range and bears retreated to wilderness, Coyotes expanded. The howl is the Coyote's signature: a rising, yipping, wavering cry that serves as territory announcement, pack coordination, and -- when multiple Coyotes howl together -- one of the most acoustically complex group vocalizations in the Pacific Northwest. A pair of Coyotes can sound like twenty. The Canidae quartet opens here and runs through degree 211.

- CANIDAE RETURNS -- the dog family re-enters the SPS engine; Canidae is one of the most acoustically sophisticated mammalian families, with vocalizations that range from the Coyote's complex howl to the wolf's bone-shaking bass; the Canidae quartet (208-211) will document four distinct Canidae voices across four degrees
- E=6 / E=6 MATCH -- like degree 205 (Naked Giants / Bighorn Sheep), degree 208 produces a matched-energy pair; Pickwick's soul-powered vocals and the Coyote's howl are both fundamentally about projection -- sounds designed to fill space, to be heard, to command attention across distance
- Acoustic multiplier -- Coyotes exploit a psychoacoustic phenomenon: by rapidly alternating between multiple frequencies and using yips, barks, and howls in quick succession, a pair of Coyotes can create the impression of a much larger group; this "beau geste" effect serves as a territorial bluff, convincing rival Coyotes that the territory is more heavily defended than it actually is
- Coyote expansion -- while most large predators contracted their range over the past two centuries, Coyotes expanded dramatically; they now occupy every habitat type in Washington state, from alpine meadows to downtown Seattle; their adaptability is unmatched among North American carnivores
- Soul in the Northwest -- Pickwick's existence challenges the narrative that Seattle is exclusively a rock town; the city has deep soul, R&B, and funk roots (Quincy Jones, the Jackson Street clubs, the Black Tones' Central District lineage); Pickwick's blue-eyed soul is a continuation of this thread, not an anomaly
