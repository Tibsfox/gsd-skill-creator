## Stonefly ecology and Pacific Northwest river health


- stoneflies (*Plecoptera*) are biological indicators of river water quality: their aquatic nymphs require high dissolved oxygen concentrations, cold water temperatures, and clean substrate, and their presence in a river system in large numbers indicates high ecological integrity; the Pacific Northwest's salmonfly populations are concentrated in the rivers with the coldest, cleanest water -- the upper Cascades tributaries, the Olympic Peninsula rivers, the Columbia River tributaries above major dam systems -- rivers that also support the strongest remaining wild salmon populations; a mass stonefly emergence at the scale documented at degree 355 is therefore not only an acoustic event but an ecological indicator: the river is clean enough, cold enough, and intact enough to support the nymph populations that produce the emergence; the acoustic event is an ecological health certificate issued in wingbeats
