## Lessons Learned

1. **Band-to-solo documentation arcs require a gap between band-unit entries and individual-solo entries to signal that the solo phase is distinct rather than duplicate-adjacent.** The Wilson Sisters Solo arc at degrees 259-260 arrives twenty-four degrees after the Heart band-unit entries at 234-235. The gap signals that the solo phase is a distinct structural phase rather than a direct follow-up to the band entry. Future band-to-solo documentation should maintain an appropriate gap rather than documenting solo work immediately adjacent to the band entry, because adjacent documentation risks collapsing the two phases into structural equivalence rather than preserving their distinction.
2. **Solo-capacity amplitude ceilings should be distinguished from band-capacity amplitude ceilings in the same artist's documentation across degrees.** Ann Wilson at degree 259 lands at E=7 in solo capacity; Heart at degrees 234-235 lands at the band-unit amplitude (E=8) one level higher. The placement is not a downgrade; it reflects the different acoustic contexts of solo-vocal versus full-band work. Future same-artist multi-context documentation should maintain the amplitude-context distinction rather than assigning a single amplitude to an artist across all contexts, because the amplitude-in-context register is a first-class structural element of the paired-engine amplitude vocabulary.
3. **Vocal technique documentation at the register's ceiling requires specific mechanism citation (breath support, pitched-center diction, register-shift) rather than abstract technique description.** The Ann Wilson degree 259 entry is grounded in specific vocal-technique elements (breath support below the diaphragm, pitched-center diction in higher registers, dynamic placement, chest-voice-to-head-voice register shift) rather than in generic rock-vocalist description. Future high-amplitude vocalist documentation should anchor technique claims in specific mechanism citation, because the mechanism-level register is what distinguishes high-technique work from uncontrolled high-amplitude work.
4. **Multi-degree meteorological arcs benefit from explicit documentation of the single-system continuity across progressively different geographies.** The storm arc 256-260 documents a single Pacific storm system moving through five progressively inland geographies (coast → strait → mountains → Cascades → lightning strike) rather than five independent weather events. Future multi-degree meteorological arcs should surface the single-system-continuity register explicitly, because the continuity is a first-class structural observation rather than an accidental coincidence of consecutive weather-themed entries.
5. **Acoustic-regime distinctions (episodic-plus-sustained versus continuous-broadband) should be documented at the level of acoustic structure rather than at the level of general atmospheric description.** The degree 259 Thunderstorm Cascades entry opens the episodic-plus-sustained acoustic-regime register to distinguish it structurally from the continuous-broadband regime of the Olympic Wind Storm at degree 258. Future SPS documentation should characterize acoustic-regime structures explicitly so that same-amplitude entries are not collapsed into structural equivalence despite their different underlying acoustic patterns.
6. **Lightning-and-thunder physics documentation requires thermodynamic citation (plasma channel temperature, supersonic expansion, shock-wave propagation) rather than generic loud-noise description.** The degree 259 documentation grounds the thunder acoustic event in the specific plasma-channel temperature (approximately 30,000 Kelvin), the supersonic expansion mechanism, and the shock-wave propagation physics. Future thunder-adjacent documentation should anchor acoustic claims in the thermodynamic and shock-wave physics rather than in generic loud-noise description, because the physics-grounding register is what distinguishes measurable thunder documentation from impressionistic weather description.
7. **Sustained E=N amplitude streaks across multiple consecutive degrees document ceiling-level amplitude as a first-class multi-degree structural register.** The four-consecutive-E=8 SPS streak across degrees 256-259 documents that the engine's amplitude vocabulary can sustain ceiling-level amplitude across multi-degree arcs rather than only at single-degree peaks. Future sustained-amplitude arcs should surface the streak-register explicitly when it spans multiple consecutive degrees, because the sustained-ceiling register is a first-class structural observation about amplitude-stability across mechanism variation.
8. **Cross-sync registers that operate across radically different acoustic mechanisms (vocal-fold modulation versus plasma-channel expansion) benefit from explicit mechanism-bridging in the cross-sync paragraphs.** The degree 259 cross-sync paragraphs explicitly bridge the vocal-technique and thunder-physics mechanisms by identifying the shared structural principle (power-at-the-upper-edge-held-under-precise-control) at the level of abstraction that holds for both mechanisms. Future cross-sync documentation of radically-different-mechanism pair-halves should operate at this level of mechanism-bridging abstraction, because the cross-sync register is strengthened when the shared structural principle is pitched at the level where both pair-halves are recognizable instances.
