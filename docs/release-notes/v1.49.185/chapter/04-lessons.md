# Lessons -- v1.49.185

10 lessons extracted from Degree 49 (Kimya Dawson + Fox Sparrow).

1. **Structural firsts should cluster, not stretch.** Degree 49 delivered five firsts in a single release because they all became structurally available at the same rotation. Attempting to ration them across later degrees would have produced thinner releases without making later degrees stronger. When the engine reaches a natural convergence, ship the whole convergence.

2. **Cultural definitions of energy levels beat mathematical ones.** E=3 had a structural definition before Dawson (between E=2 and E=4) but no cultural one. The Juno paradox makes E=3 legible as principled refusal. Future energy-level reference cases should be chosen to carry cultural meaning, not just mathematical placement.

3. **Transparency about engineered vs. surfaced resonance is mandatory.** The fox-Tibsfox naming coincidence surfaced from the CSV substitution process; the release note documents that it was not engineered. Every future resonance claim needs the same flag -- "designed" or "surfaced" -- so future readers can trust the engine's internal accounting.

4. **Second cities deserve their own acknowledgments.** Olympia gets Squaxin Island + Nisqually; Seattle kept Duwamish. The acknowledgment stack is now additive rather than collapsed into a single Seattle default. All future degrees in new cities must add to the acknowledgment stack rather than overwrite it.

5. **Five-consecutive-family streaks should be named "quintets" consistently.** Three quintets (Hirundinidae, Parulidae, Passerellidae) now tie the engine record. "Quintet" is the correct category name; five-consecutive-family streaks should be tracked as a first-class structural entity with its own ledger rather than reconstructed from the family table each time.

6. **Matched E=3 on both sides produces a resonance as strong as matched E=2.** Degree 45 established that matched E=2 produces the engine's strongest Part A / Part B resonance; degree 49 shows the same is true for E=3. The pattern is "matched-quiet" rather than "matched-E=2 specifically." Future degrees should track matched-energy resonance as a general property of the engine's paired architecture.

7. **CSV substitution practice has become a stable protocol.** Five consecutive amphibian replacements (45-49) without issue. The protocol is now default operating procedure: document the original species, state the reason, preserve energy and habitat, note the replacement transparently. The rule that seemed uncertain at degree 45 is settled by degree 49.

8. **Lo-fi as ethics is not lo-fi as deficiency.** The release note now distinguishes production-philosophy lo-fi (Dawson, K Records, Dub Narcotic) from low-budget-necessity lo-fi. The distinction matters for future independent-label degrees; the engine must not flatten aesthetic choice into resource constraint.

9. **Nonbinary identity tracking needs a richer taxonomy than non-male:male.** The twelve-of-fifty non-male counter is an improvement but still collapses distinct gender identities. The engine should design a proper identity taxonomy before the tally reaches 100 degrees so that retroactive re-coding remains small.

10. **Pass 2 NASA alignment is a real structural layer, not an ornament.** The Gemini 11 pairing (Pete Conrad, Dick Gordon, first-orbit rendezvous, tethered spaceflight, record altitude) added resonances that the Pass 1 research had not surfaced. Pass 2 pairings are now part of the expected deliverable, not optional enrichment.
