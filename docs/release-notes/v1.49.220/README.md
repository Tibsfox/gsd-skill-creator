The Douglas Squirrel (also called the **Chickaree**) is the **PNW's signature tree squirrel** -- a small, dark, aggressive, relentlessly vocal arboreal mammal of the conifer canopy. ### TAXONOMY: *Tamiasciurus douglasii*

- **Tamiasciurus** -- from Greek *tamias* ("steward" or "hoarder") + *skiouros* ("squirrel," from *skia* "shadow" + *oura* "tail"). *Tamiasciurus* = "hoarder squirrel" -- reflecting the species' intensive food-caching behavior.
- **douglasii** -- honors **David Douglas** (1799-1834), the Scottish botanist who explored the Pacific Northwest in the 1820s-1830s. The squirrel that lives in the tree named for the same person.

Classification:
- **Class:** Mammalia (19th mammalian entry; 17th species)
- **Order:** Rodentia -- SEVENTH Rodentia in the engine
- **Family:** Sciuridae -- SIXTH CONSECUTIVE (75-76-77-78-79-80). The Sciuridae sextet. Ties Mustelidae (63-68). TWO-WAY TIE AT SIX
- **Genus:** *Tamiasciurus* -- NEW GENUS. Fourth Sciuridae genus after *Marmota* (75-77), *Urocitellus* (78), *Callospermophilus* (79). Tree squirrels -- the first arboreal genus
- **Species:** *T. douglasii* -- the Douglas Squirrel. Pacific coast from British Columbia to central California

### THE SOUND: LOUD SCOLDING CHATTER RATTLE -- 81st Species-Side Acoustic Element

The Douglas Squirrel's vocalization is a **loud, aggressive, sustained scolding chatter** -- the most prominent mammal sound in PNW conifer forests:
- **Frequency range:** approximately 3,000-10,000 Hz. Broad range reflecting the complex chatter structure
- **Duration:** 2-10+ seconds per chatter bout. Individual notes within the chatter: 0.02-0.05 seconds each. The LONGEST sustained vocalization in the Sciuridae run
- **Structure:** Continuous rapid stream of sharp notes -- a machine-gun rattle. Not single notes (Golden-mantled 79) or short bursts (Townsend's 78) but CONTINUOUS RATTLING
- **Amplitude:** **E=5** -- LOUD. Audible at 200+ meters through dense conifer forest. The loudest and most persistent mammal vocalization in PNW forests
- **Function:** Primarily TERRITORIAL DEFENSE. The chatter IS the territory boundary

### ECOLOGY: THE CANOPY SENTINEL

**Habitat -- Conifer Canopy -- First in the Engine:** Douglas-fir, western red cedar, Sitka spruce, western hemlock, noble fir, subalpine fir. Sea level to treeline -- the broadest elevation range in the Sciuridae sextet. The engine's **first true canopy mammal**. The habitat progression across the sextet: alpine rock, alpine meadow, Olympic alpine, open grassland, forest edge, conifer canopy. Ground to treetops.

**Territory:** Aggressively territorial. The scolding chatter IS the territory defense. Middens -- massive piles of stripped cone scales at favorite feeding perches, meters wide and decades old.

**Diet:** Conifer seed specialist. Strips cone scales. Truffle disperser -- key mycorrhizal fungi inoculator. Caches green cones in cool, moist locations.

**Predator:** American Marten (degree 65) -- the PRIMARY PREDATOR. Both arboreal. **Cross-sextet predation**: Mustelidae sextet member (65) hunting Sciuridae sextet member (80). The canopy arms race.

### JOHN MUIR AND DAVID DOUGLAS

4. The no-guitar manifesto as blue note extends the engine's vocabulary for describing musical absence. Previous blue notes described what IS present (the baritone weight, the loose harmony). The no-guitar manifesto describes what is deliberately NOT present. The absence creates the sound
