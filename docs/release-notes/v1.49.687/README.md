## Summary

Buran 1.01 (ОК-1К1 "Orbital Ship 1, Kosmos 1") launched 1988-11-15 03:00:01 UTC from Baikonur Cosmodrome LC-110/37 on the **Energia heavy-lift launch vehicle (11K25)** — Energia's second flight + first successful payload delivery (substrate-cumulative with Energia maiden 1987-05-15 Polyus payload failure).

The spacecraft was **UNMANNED** (substrate-form UNMANNED-CREWED-SPACECRAFT-PROFILE NEW LOCKED — crewed-capable vehicle flew first orbital mission without crew; substrate-distinct from cargo-only spacecraft). Buran flew **two orbits** (206 minutes / 3 hours 25 minutes) at 256 × 252 km × 51.6° inclination, then performed deorbit burn + atmospheric entry + cross-range glide back to Baikonur.

**FIRST FULLY-AUTONOMOUS SPACE SHUTTLE LANDING IN HISTORY:** Buran touched down at Yubileyniy Airfield Runway 06 Baikonur 1988-11-15 06:25:24 UTC with the runway wet from morning precipitation and a 17 m/s (61 km/h) crosswind blowing across the centerline. The on-board landing system corrected for the crosswind autonomously — 3 meter centerline deviation at touchdown. US Space Shuttle STS-1 (1981-04-12) was crewed; the US Space Shuttle never made unmanned orbital landing.

**SOVIET-SHUTTLE-PROGRAM-CLOSURE substrate-form NEW LOCKED at v687.** Buran 1.01 was the first and only Buran orbital flight. Second flight-ready vehicle (Ptichka 1.02) never flew; preserved as museum exhibit.
