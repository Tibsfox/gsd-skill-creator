## Summary

**Truly** arrives at E=5 -- the band built around **Hiro Yamamoto**, founding bassist of Soundgarden, the side of Seattle's grunge explosion that gets lost in the noise of the bigger names. Truly is Yamamoto (bass/vocals), Robert Roth (guitar/vocals), and Mark Pickerel (drums, formerly of Screaming Trees) -- a supergroup of grunge's second tier that produced music more adventurous than most of their more famous peers. On Capitol Records, *Fast Stories... from Kid Coma* (1995) is a record that fuses noise rock, psychedelia, and art rock into compositions that stretch and mutate, refusing the verse-chorus-verse formula that made grunge commercially viable. At E=5, Truly occupies the same warm, exploratory register as Brad at degree 219 -- two consecutive side projects from grunge's founding architects, both choosing depth over volume. The **Soundgarden connection** makes this the second consecutive grunge-lineage entry after Brad's Pearl Jam connection, and the Screaming Trees link through Pickerel adds a third thread. On the species side, the **Ringtail** registers at E=5 -- and with this entry, the **FIRST PROCYONIDAE** (raccoon family) enters the SPS engine. The Ringtail (*Bassariscus astutus*) is the most beautiful and least known member of the raccoon family: a cat-sized, nocturnal carnivore with enormous eyes, a spectacular black-and-white banded tail longer than its body, and a repertoire of barks, squeaks, and chittering calls that sound like a raccoon whispering. The Ringtail is found in the rocky canyons and dry forests of Washington's southeastern corner, making it one of the state's most geographically restricted mammals.
