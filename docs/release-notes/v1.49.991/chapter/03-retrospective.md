The index trip-vocab page check returned PASS with zero primary and zero secondary classes in the title line and body, and the 2003 final telemetry contact was framed as a mission that far exceeded its design life.
- **Identifier-in-lists discipline preserved.** All substrate-anchor identifiers appear in bulleted lists, table cells, or anchor-pill spans rather than embedded in narrative paragraphs, satisfying the layout gate and the IDENTIFIER-NOT-PROSE-DISCIPLINE.
- **Dedication word-count discipline preserved.** Both the index.html dedication and the organism.html dedication are within the 200-word cap.
- **Reference template recognition.** The v1.181 Voyager 2 template files were used as the immediate reference — same CSS structure, same nav-card pair pattern, same sidebar and track-grid patterns — with palette tokens renamed for Pioneer 10 (--jovian-amber, --rust-copper, --belt-slate, --alder-green, --cruise-bronze) and a distinct Pioneer early-1970s deep-space palette, and the structural template preserved exactly.
- **Pairing files updated in both formats.** The 183rd canonical-pairings record was appended consistently to both the TSV (15-column tab-delimited) and the JSON (mirroring the existing entry structure), keeping the two data files in lockstep.

## What Could Be Better

- **The shader renders procedural structure rather than archived data.** The trailblazer-trajectory shader uses analytic geometry and procedural noise rather than loading actual Pioneer 10 plasma-analyzer, magnetometer, or cosmic-ray time series from the NASA Space Science Data Coordinated Archive. A future revision could load encoded Pioneer 10 archive intervals for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to the real outer-heliosphere cruise record.
- **The asteroid-belt-to-Jupiter trajectory diorama is a forthcoming artifact.** The 3D-printable trajectory STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the asteroid-belt traversal and the Jupiter gravity-assist path.
- **The Pioneer program-lineage sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.** The sidebar lists representative program elements (Pioneer 10, Pioneer 11, Voyager 1, Voyager 2, New Horizons) but does not enumerate the full outer-solar-system fleet; a future revision could expand the table with launch dates and mission outcomes.
- **The outer-heliosphere precursor comparison is asserted rather than computed.** The comparison between the Pioneer 10 cruise record and the later Voyager records is described as a comparator but not run; a future ship could compute and plot the Pioneer 10 solar-wind and cosmic-ray record beside the Voyager records as a function of heliocentric distance from archived data.

## Surprises

- **The earliest mission entered the axis sixth.** Pioneer 10 launched in 1972, five years before the Voyagers and decades before the remote-ENA missions, yet it entered the INTERSTELLAR-BOUNDARY axis as the sixth observation, so the chronologically-earliest mission is the most recent addition to the axis.
- **A two-year design lifetime stretched past three decades.** Pioneer 10 was designed for a two-year primary mission to reach Jupiter, yet it operated for more than three decades as an outer-heliosphere precursor, returning data far beyond what the design anticipated.
- **A first-of-its-kind plaque preceded the better-known Golden Records.** The Pioneer plaque, designed by Carl Sagan and Frank Drake, was the first physical interstellar message and a precursor to the more elaborate Voyager Golden Records that are now far more widely known, so the catalog records the earlier and simpler form after the later and more famous one.
