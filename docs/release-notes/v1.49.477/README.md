**The Gray Wolf Recovery Narrative Documents a Pacific Northwest Ecological Restoration at Acoustic First-Class Resolution.** The SPS track's Gray Wolf Howl component documents a recovered species and a recovered sound. *Canis lupus* (Gray Wolf, Order Carnivora, Family Canidae) was extirpated from Washington State by the 1930s through predator-control programs, and the species was absent from the state's acoustic landscape for seventy years. Natural recolonization from neighboring Idaho and Montana began in the 2000s, and the first modern Washington pack (the Lookout Pack) was documented in 2008 in the Kettle Range. As of 2024, Washington State supports 30+ packs primarily in the northeastern Cascades and the Okanogan-Wenatchee region, tracked continuously by the Washington Department of Fish & Wildlife. Every wolf howl in a Washington high meadow is therefore an ecological restoration event rather than a continuous historical phenomenon — the sound was absent for seven decades and is returning. The acoustic properties of the howl are themselves sophisticated: fundamental frequency 150-400 Hz sweeping and modulating across a single exhalation of 3-11 seconds, harmonics extending to several kHz, amplitude 90-105 dB at 1 meter from the source and 60-80 dB at 1 km in the open acoustic environment of the subalpine meadow, and detection ranges in excess of 10 km under favorable conditions of cold calm air over open terrain. Experienced field researchers can identify individual wolves by their howl characteristics alone, making the howl an individual acoustic signature like a fingerprint. **In parallel**, Alice in Chains' own recovery narrative plays out through the band's hiatus after Staley's death on April 5, 2002, and their reformation with William DuVall on vocals from 2005-2006 through *Black Gives Way to Blue* (2009), *The Devil Put Dinosaurs Here* (2013), and *Rainier Fog* (2018), carrying the band's musical identity forward without replacing its original emotional register. **The same** absence-followed-by-return narrative structure organizes both halves. **Each side** demonstrates that the engine's documentation vocabulary preserves the recovery-context dimension at first-class resolution rather than at continuous-presence attribution, and that the restoration of a species to a landscape and the continuation of a musical project after loss share a structural kinship in the engine's reading.

**The Thunder Crack Physics Ground the Atmospheric Source in Concrete Meteorological Acoustics.** The SPS track's lightning strike component arrives from a close cloud-to-ground or cloud-to-cloud lightning discharge at under 1-2 km from the listener position in the subalpine meadow. The lightning channel is heated to approximately 30,000 Kelvin — five times the surface temperature of the Sun — in less than one millisecond by the rapid electrical discharge between charged regions of a summer convective thunderstorm. The extreme rapid heating causes explosive expansion of the air channel, and the shock wave that propagates from the channel becomes thunder. At close range, thunder is heard as a sharp crack rather than as the rumble of distant thunder: a high-amplitude broadband transient at peak amplitude 110-120+ dB (and higher for extremely close strikes), frequency content from below 100 Hz through several kHz, peak duration under 0.1 seconds, and a 2-10 second decay as the sound reflects from the terrain and as later thunder from more distant sections of the same lightning channel arrives. The peak amplitude at close range is at or above the threshold of physical discomfort for human hearing and is believed to be at the threshold of auditory pain for many mammals. The 340 meter per second speed of sound means that thunder arrives 3-6 seconds after the lightning flash at the listener position for strikes within a kilometer, and the crack-before-rumble temporal structure is the signature of close proximity. The Cascades summer convective thunderstorm context is driven by daytime heating of the terrain through orographic convection sometimes enhanced by organized synoptic-scale lift from approaching weather systems, and the subalpine meadow's elevation at 5,000-6,500+ feet places the listener closer to the thunderstorm base than the forested valleys below. **In parallel**, Cantrell's guitar amplification architecture on *Dirt* and subsequent Alice in Chains records achieves its effect through a different mechanism — the G&L ASAT solidbody guitar (modeled on the Telecaster design) through Marshall amplifiers at heavy distortion producing the characteristic bright mid-range emphasis that makes the guitar-vocal harmony system audible against the down-tuned D-standard or C-standard guitar weight — but produces a similar acoustic effect in its register: sustained melodic expression against a background that carries the potential for dissonant interruption at any moment. **The same** held-expression-against-latent-interruption organizing principle unites both halves across the mechanism boundary. **Each side** demonstrates that the engine's acoustic vocabulary identifies the relationship between held beauty and latent interruption as a weight-producing architecture shared across human music and natural-acoustic domains at first-class resolution rather than at mechanism-isolated attribution.

**The Guitar-Vocal Harmony System Is Alice in Chains' Defining Compositional Contribution to the Pacific Northwest Heavy Music Tradition.** Jerry Cantrell's guitar writing on *Dirt* and across the Alice in Chains catalogue is the most immediately recognizable compositional approach in the Pacific Northwest metal/grunge tradition: the harmony is not performed over a separate guitar riff, as in most heavy rock music, but is composed as a single two-part texture in which the guitar riff and the vocal melody function as parts of the same harmonic structure. The intervals used are specifically chosen: thirds and fifths for the consonant anchor points, minor seconds and tritones for the dissonant tension points, the tritone specifically being the interval that classical harmony designated *diabolus in musica* (the devil in music) and the preferred interval of Cantrell's harmonic language. The harmony often sits in close dissonance, producing an unsettled quality even in slow passages, and the compositional approach is more common in vocal harmony groups than in metal bands. Cantrell inherited elements of this approach from the Texas metal tradition (Dimebag Darrell of Pantera is a cited influence) and fused them with the down-tuned heaviness of the wider grunge-adjacent Seattle heavy music tradition. The result on *Would?*, *Down in a Hole*, *Nutshell*, and *Them Bones* is a melodic register that is beautiful and dark in the same act, with the beauty held and the darkness always present in the intervals. **In parallel**, the wolf's territorial howl on the SPS track carries its own held-beautiful-with-latent-darkness structure: the fundamental frequency sweep and the harmonic overtones produce a tonally beautiful acoustic signal, and the sustained nature of the howl (3-11 seconds per exhalation) creates a held expression in the open meadow air, but the subalpine meadow's summer convective thunderstorm zone carries the latent potential for the close lightning strike that will interrupt the held expression. **The same** beauty-with-latent-interruption compositional principle organizes both halves. **Each side** demonstrates that the engine's aesthetic vocabulary preserves the held-beauty-against-darkness register at first-class resolution rather than at single-sided-attribution attribution, and that the Metal Summit's closing position specifically requires the pairing of two independent documents of this register.
