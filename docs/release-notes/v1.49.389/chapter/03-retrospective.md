## What Could Be Better

- **The specific acoustic-measurement data for Hoh rain-on-maple is available in peer-reviewed literature but has not been integrated into the degree 248 documentation at the measurement level.** The documentation of the patter-drip acoustic signature relies on qualitative description of the three-event dynamic rather than on specific decibel-level measurements, frequency-spectrum analyses, or duration-characteristic statistics. Peer-reviewed literature on temperate rainforest soundscape acoustics (including University of Washington acoustic ecology research) provides the quantitative data that could anchor the documentation with measurement-level specificity, and future SPS meteorology entries should integrate quantitative acoustic measurements when available, because the qualitative-only documentation is less reproducible than measurement-anchored documentation.
- **The Thunderpussy discography beyond the two studio albums is not comprehensively surveyed.** The degree 248 release documents the self-titled 2018 debut and the 2022 *Velvet Rut* sophomore album as the two primary studio releases, but does not comprehensively cover the band's singles, EPs, live releases, guest appearances, or side projects. Future Thunderpussy-related engine documentation (at any subsequent degree that references the band) should survey the complete discography and establish the catalog at measurement-level specificity, because the discography's full distribution would anchor the band's output within the broader Pacific Northwest hard rock context.
- **The epiphytic community on Hoh bigleaf maples is more diverse than the three species surveyed (cat-tail moss, hanging wing-moss, licorice fern).** The documentation of *Isothecium myosuroides*, *Antitrichia curtipendula*, and *Polypodium glycyrrhiza* covers the primary epiphytic species but omits secondary and tertiary epiphytes including additional moss species (*Neckera douglasii*, *Metaneckera menziesii*), liverwort species (*Porella navicularis*, *Frullania tamarisci*), and lichens (*Lobaria pulmonaria*, *Lobaria oregana*) that are also present on Hoh bigleaf maples and that contribute further acoustic modification to the rain-on-leaf signature. Future SPS botanical documentation of epiphytic communities on temperate rainforest trees should survey the complete epiphytic community at species-level specificity, because the partial-community documentation is less comprehensive than complete-community documentation.

Future engine releases should identify physical-system arc opportunities when they emerge from consecutive entries, because physical-system arcs extend the arc-vocabulary's applicability to abiotic documentation.
