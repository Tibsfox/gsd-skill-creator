1. **First docking achieved.**
   The fundamental operation for all future crewed spaceflight demonstrated. Twenty-seven minutes of stable docked flight before the emergency. The operation works. Gemini 8 proved it.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #2016_

2. **Armstrong's audition for Apollo 11.**
   Diagnosis of an unprecedented failure, irreversible decision under lethal time pressure, precise execution of recovery -- all in thirty seconds. The pattern that repeats at the Sea of Tranquility three years later.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #2017_

3. **Electronic music enters the engine.**
   Genre Stage 25. The DAW as primary instrument. The engine's first digital-native production methodology. After forty-four degrees of acoustic and analog traditions, the laptop arrives.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #2018_

4. **Self-release as survival strategy.**
   The independent artist as self-contained production unit. No label A&R, no distribution deal, no institutional dependency. Complete creative control at the cost of institutional support.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #2019_

7. **Thruster 8's failure was a quality assurance failure.**
   A chafed wire in the wiring harness, within specification but at the margin. Post-flight, McDonnell Aircraft Corporation revised inspection procedures for all remaining Gemini spacecraft.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #2022_

8. **Fail-safe systems should fail into safe states, not active states.**
   Gemini 8's normally-closed solenoid design meant a short-circuit energized the thruster. The post-flight redesign inverted the philosophy. The principle propagated to every subsequent Apollo safety-critical component.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5043_

9. **Small inputs, large effects.**
   Both the Lesser Goldfinch's fold-and-drop escape and Armstrong's RCS thruster pulses demonstrate how disproportionately small actions can produce survival-critical outcomes when timing and precision are correct.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5044_

10. **The Thruster 8 wire chafing was preventable.**
   Post-flight investigation traced the failure to a wire whose insulation had chafed against a structural member during the high-vibration launch phase, creating an intermittent short that energized the solenoid when spacecraft power came up for orbital operations. The quality-assurance process at McDonnell Aircraft Corporation had not caught the vulnerability, and the inspection procedure that would have identified the chafe was only formalized after the failure. The Gemini program carried the cost of learning that particular failure mode in flight.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5045_

11. **David Scott's EVA was cancelled.**
   Scott's planned three-and-a-half-hour EVA, including a test of the Astronaut Maneuvering Unit jet backpack, was deferred. The AMU test would not be attempted again during Gemini (cancelled outright after Gemini 9A's sun-visor fog problem). The backpack finally flew on Skylab missions in 1973 -- seven years after Scott's cancelled Gemini 8 test. The engineering program lost seven years of field experience because of the emergency.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5046_

12. **The solenoid design philosophy was inverted.**
   Gemini 8 revealed that using normally-closed solenoid valves (short circuit energizes = fires) exposes the mission to catastrophic failure from a single-point electrical fault. The post-flight redesign inverted the philosophy to normally-open valves (short circuit de-energizes = safe) for all remaining Gemini thrusters. The lesson generalizes: safety-critical components should fail into safe states, not active states. Gemini 8 was the cost of learning the principle.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5047_

13. **Chong the Nomad's six-year album gap.**
   The Love Memo EP (2018) and Do We Make of This? LP (2024) are separated by six years. The gap reflects real-world constraints -- pandemic shutdown, independent-artist economics, the self-release workflow -- but also underscores how fragile a solo electronic-music career is without institutional support. The engine documents the gap as the cost of the self-release model, not just its benefit.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #5048_
