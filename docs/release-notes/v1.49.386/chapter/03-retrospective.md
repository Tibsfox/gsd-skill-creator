## Lessons Learned

1. **Engine maximum assignments carry qualitative as well as quantitative weight.** The E=8 rating is a numerical calibration, but the third ENGINE MAXIMUM assignment at Hendrix is not structurally equivalent to the first two. Hendrix carries more historical-weight and global-influence density than The Blood Brothers or The Sonics despite all three being at E=8. Future engine documentation should explicitly distinguish quantitative-rating from qualitative-weight when ENGINE MAXIMUM assignments occur, because the rating vocabulary's numerical ceiling is not identical across all subjects that reach it.
2. **Category-boundary crossings are first-class documentation events.** The biotic-to-abiotic transition at degree 245 is as structurally significant as any species entry the engine has made, and the documentation explicitly frames the transition as such. Future engine releases should identify category-boundary crossings when they occur and should treat them as first-class documentation events rather than as ad-hoc transitions, because the engine's subject-matter vocabulary expands through these boundary crossings.
3. **Metadata schema extension can accommodate expanded subject matter without reengineering.** The Hydrology family opening at degree 245 expands the family field's vocabulary from taxonomic names to process categories without requiring any schema change — the field continues to accept single-string values, but the values now include both biological-taxonomic and physical-process categories. Future engine evolution should leverage existing schema flexibility when subject matter expands, because schema extension through controlled vocabulary changes is less disruptive than schema reengineering.
4. **Constraint structures can be productive of complexity across radically different generation mechanisms.** Hendrix's left-handed playing on a right-handed guitar restrung upside-down produced the thumb-over-bass innovations; River Current Over Boulders' water-flow encounter with boulder obstructions produced the polyrhythmic standing-wave soundscape. Both are constraint-produces-complexity demonstrations despite radically different generation mechanisms (human-musical versus physical-landscape). Future engine releases should surface constraint-produces-complexity dynamics when they organize significant outcomes, because the register documents that complexity can emerge from constraint structures rather than from unconstrained conditions across multiple subject domains.
5. **Multi-degree same-subject treatments are valid structural documentation modes.** The Hendrix split across degrees 245 (Experience peak) and 246 (early pre-Experience) is the first same-artist two-degree sequence at engine maximum in S36 history. The split is not padding — it is a structural documentation decision reflecting the subject's depth. Future engine documentation should consider multi-degree same-subject treatments when a single subject's depth exceeds single-degree accommodation, because the multi-degree treatment preserves documentation resolution that single-degree compression would lose.
6. **Arc-opening positions can carry as much weight as arc-closing positions.** The hydrological cycle arc opens at degree 245 with River Current Over Boulders as the arc's first entry. The arc-opening position documents the structural significance of the arc — the first single-physical-system arc in SPS history — as fully as an arc-closing position would. Future engine documentation should treat arc-opening positions with full documentary weight when they mark structural transitions, because the opening is as much a structural-significance marker as the closing.
7. **Cross-sync parallels surface structural isomorphisms across radically different subject domains.** The polyrhythm-at-both-scales cross-sync at degree 245 demonstrates that human-musical polyrhythm and physical-landscape polyrhythm are structurally isomorphic — both emerge from multiple independent parallel streams interacting without resolving — despite radically different generation mechanisms. 8. **Category-opening events are as structurally significant as category-closing events.** The first abiotic SPS entry at degree 245 opens the abiotic era; the last living-organism entry at degree 244 closed the biotic era. Both are structurally significant category boundaries. Future engine documentation should identify both category-opening and category-closing events when they occur, because the transition is a two-sided structural moment and both sides deserve explicit documentation.
9. **Place-specific acoustic character documents geographic identity as a first-class register.** The Cascade Range mountain streams — Skykomish, Sauk, Snoqualmie, Wenatchee, Elwha — each carry distinctive acoustic character shaped by their specific geography. The acoustic-geographic-identity register is a first-class structural vocabulary entry that complements the species-habitat register. Future Hydrology-family entries should develop place-specific acoustic characterization as standard metadata, because place-specific character is a first-class structural-significance marker for abiotic subjects.
10. **Outlast-your-generating-conditions dynamics organize both cultural and physical subjects.** Jimi Hendrix's recordings persist fifty-six years after his September 18, 1970 London death and continue to shape subsequent generations. River Current Over Boulders was present before biological life on Earth and will persist after the last living organism.
