## Summary

The erased and the difficult. Patti Bown at 6° and the Pacific-slope Flycatcher at 6° -- both occupying the same position on the unit circle, both teaching the same foundational lesson: that identity lives in what you do, not what you look like. Bown was one of the first Black women to lead a hard bop piano trio in the late 1950s, recorded a debut on Columbia Records, worked within the orbit of Quincy Jones and Oliver Nelson, performed session work for Motown -- and was erased from public memory. The flycatcher is literally named *difficilis* because you cannot identify it by sight. The 1989 taxonomic split that created this species was driven entirely by vocal differences. Remove the voice, and the taxonomy collapses back into one species. Both prove the same thing: identity is in the signal, not the surface.

Degree 6 introduces three structural firsts for the species engine: the first neotropical migrant (seasonal presence and absence -- arriving in April, departing by September, absent for six months), the first riparian habitat species (red alder corridors along streams, a new ecosystem distinct from conifer forest and estuary), and the principle that voice IS identity (the Pacific-slope Flycatcher exists as a recognized species *because of its call*). For the artist engine: the gender dimension deepens with the instrumentalist glass ceiling (Bown as female pianist faced higher barriers than Anderson as vocalist at degree 3), the seventh artist-city pattern emerges (The Erasure -- exported, succeeded, forgotten), and the piano lineage from Berry at degree 5 inverts across every dimension: stride versus hard bop, rooted versus rootless, solo versus trio, stayed versus left, recognized versus erased, male versus female.

The engine starts documenting what the industry dropped. This is not advocacy; it is inventory. You cannot map a city's musical geography if you only count the artists who survived the canon's filter. Bown did not survive the filter. She is degree 6 because the engine's job is to count everyone, including the ones the filter discarded.

### Part A: Patti Bown -- The Erasure

Full deep research covering the complete Patti Bown arc:

- **Seattle Origins:** Born June 26, 1931, in Seattle, Washington. Raised in the Central District -- the seventh consecutive degree connected to that neighborhood. Studied music at Seattle University, a Jesuit institution on Capitol Hill -- the first S36 artist connected to SU, adding a seventh educational pipeline to the engine's taxonomy (Garfield, Berklee, UC Santa Cruz, UW, bandstand apprenticeship, self-taught, Seattle University).
- **Career Arc:** Moved to New York City mid-1950s, arriving during hard bop's most competitive era. Columbia Records debut *Patti Bown Plays Big Piano* (1958) -- the most prestigious jazz label, home to Miles Davis, Brubeck, and Monk. One of the first Black women to lead a hard bop piano trio -- not as a vocalist who played piano, but as a pianist whose technique and authority demanded the bandstand. Worked with Quincy Jones (degree 0) and Oliver Nelson (*The Blues and the Abstract Truth*, 1961). Session pianist for Motown. Favorably reviewed in DownBeat. Shifted to education in later decades. Died March 21, 2008, in Philadelphia, age 76. No NEA Jazz Master. No Grammy nominations. Columbia debut out of print.
- **Music Theory Mapping:** Four new theory nodes -- hard bop rootless voicings (rootless left-hand chords, quartal harmony, collaborative voicings that assume a trio context), call-and-response with gospel influence (right-hand melodic call answered by left-hand chordal punctuation), hard bop rhythmic drive (percussive comping, blues-rooted insistence, the piano as rhythmic instrument), pianist-as-bandleader authority (the organizational Amiga Principle at small-group scale -- trio rather than big band).
- **The Gender Dimension:** Anderson (degree 3) was a vocalist -- the jazz industry recognized that role. Bown was an instrumentalist -- the piano bench was understood as a male position. Three layers of resistance: Black, female, pianist. The instrumentalist glass ceiling was higher than the vocalist ceiling. Berry (degree 5, male, stayed, recognized) and Bown (degree 6, female, left, erased) form the S36 engine's first instrument-specific consecutive pair, with every dimension inverted.
- **The Erasure Pattern:** The seventh artist-city pattern. Same departure as The Export (Jones), same destination (NYC), opposite outcome. The recordings go out of print. The reviews are not anthologized. The connections are not narrated in retrospectives. The artist becomes a footnote in other artists' discographies. The engine documents what the industry dropped.

### Part B: Pacific-slope Flycatcher -- Voice as Identity

Full deep research covering the PNW's canonical voice-first species:

- **Taxonomy:** *Empidonax difficilis*, family Tyrannidae (tyrant flycatchers -- largest bird family in the Americas, 400+ species). Genus *Empidonax* from Greek *empis* (gnat) + *anax* (king) -- king of the gnats. Species epithet *difficilis* means "difficult." Until 1989, it was lumped with the Cordilleran Flycatcher (*E. occidentalis*) as "Western Flycatcher." The AOU split was driven almost entirely by vocal differences -- the two species are virtually identical in plumage. Voice IS identity. 5.5 inches, 8-12 grams, olive-green, teardrop-shaped eye-ring, broad flat bill for snapping insects.
- **Bioacoustics:** Diagnostic call is a sharp, upslurred "ptseet" or "su-wheet" -- ascending from 2.5-3.0 kHz to 3.5-4.5 kHz over 0.3-0.5 seconds. Smooth continuous upward glide. The dawn song is a structured three-phrase sequence: ptseet (upslurred whistle), ptsik (short sharp downslurred chip), suweet (burry two-part note) -- a ternary form, the simplest complete formal structure in music. The upslurred note is acoustically a half cadence -- it rises and does not resolve. The first species in the circle whose primary vocalization ascends and remains unresolved.
- **Theory Mapping:** The upslur as musical question (half cadence on the dominant, creating tension that resolves only when the tonic arrives). Three-phrase dawn song as ternary form (A-B-C sections in fixed order -- the simplest complete song form). Voice as primary identity (the jazz concept of personal tone -- you recognize Coltrane by his sound, not his face). Seasonal presence as temporal form (the annual cycle maps to exposition, development, recapitulation, coda -- a movement that enters, sustains, and exits).
- **First Neotropical Migrant:** Arrives April, departs September. Breeds in PNW riparian corridors, winters in western Mexico (Sonora to Oaxaca). Round-trip 2,000+ miles. The first species with temporal presence and absence -- the acoustic niche goes silent for six months. The return of the "ptseet" in spring is an acoustic event, a seasonal marker.
- **Riparian Alder Ecosystem:** Red alder (*Alnus rubra*), bigleaf maple canopy, sword fern understory. The transition zone where water meets forest, deciduous replaces conifer. First deciduous riparian habitat in the circle. Indicator species for intact riparian corridors -- its presence confirms vegetative structure, moisture regime, and insect productivity.
- **Conservation:** Least Concern (IUCN) but declining -- estimated 2-3% annual decline since the 1970s per Breeding Bird Survey. Population ~6.5 million (Partners in Flight 2020). Causes: riparian forest degradation in PNW, tropical forest loss in Mexico.

### The Degree 6° Parallel

The resonance between Patti Bown and the Pacific-slope Flycatcher emerged on three independent axes:

1. **Identity through performance, not appearance** -- Bown's authority came from her playing, not her visibility; the flycatcher's species identity exists only through its call, not its plumage
2. **Known by those who listen, unknown to those who only look** -- Bown was reviewed favorably by critics who heard her play; the flycatcher is identified instantly by birders who hear the "ptseet" but remains unidentifiable to those relying on sight
3. **Underrecognized despite defining characteristics** -- Bown had a Columbia debut, arranger connections, session work, and favorable reviews, yet was erased; the flycatcher has the most diagnostic call of any Empidonax, yet is perpetually confused with its congeners by visual observers

## Retrospective

### What Worked
- **The Erasure pattern discovery** gave degree 6 its deepest structural contribution -- adding a seventh artist-city pattern that explains what happens when the canon's filter fails a genuinely accomplished artist. The engine gained a lens for documenting the forgotten.
- **Piano lineage inversion** across Berry and Bown proved analytically productive -- every dimension (style, voicings, context, left hand, role, training, city pattern, visibility, gender) inverts, creating a conversation between opposites rather than a progression.
- **Voice-as-identity** produced the strongest Part B concept since the silence principle at degree 0. The 1989 taxonomic split driven entirely by voice gave the species engine a definitive case study in acoustic identity.
- **Three-phrase dawn song as ternary form** extended the music theory mapping beyond single-note analysis into multi-section formal structure for the first time.
- **Neotropical migrant introduction** added the temporal dimension (seasonal presence/absence) that the resident-only circle lacked through degrees 0-5.

### What Could Be Better
- **Central District concentration at seven degrees** is analytically rich but geographically unsustainable. The engine acknowledged this in the research and flagged rotation as necessary starting at degree 7.
- **Bown source depth** is constrained by the erasure itself -- the available documentation is thinner than for any previous S36 artist, which is precisely the point but also limits the research density.
- **Riparian habitat analysis** could be deeper with specific stream corridor surveys and alder stand measurements. Future releases involving riparian species should quantify habitat structure.

## Lessons Learned

1. **The engine must document the erased, not just the celebrated.** If the S36 engine only maps artists who are already well-known, it reproduces the existing canon. The canon failed Patti Bown. The engine's purpose is inventory, not curation. Degree 6 proved that documenting the forgotten produces more interesting structural analysis (the Erasure pattern, the instrumentalist glass ceiling, the Berry-Bown inversion) than documenting the already-celebrated would.
2. **Voice-as-identity is testable and falsifiable.** The Pacific-slope Flycatcher's 1989 taxonomic split provides a concrete case: the species exists because of vocal differences. This is not metaphor. The connection between acoustic identity and taxonomic identity can be verified against the AOU supplement (37th, 1989). Testable claims produce stronger research.
3. **Temporal absence is as informative as temporal presence.** The flycatcher's six-month absence from the PNW soundscape is data -- it tells you what month it is, what the habitat condition is, and what migration ecology looks like. The species engine gained a new axis (seasonal vs. resident) that will apply to many subsequent degrees.
