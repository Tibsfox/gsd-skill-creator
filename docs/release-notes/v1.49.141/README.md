## Summary

She played. They forgot. It sings. You cannot see it. Patti Bown at 6° and the Pacific-slope Flycatcher at 6° occupy the same position on the unit circle and teach the same foundational lesson in two different languages: identity lives in what you do, not what you look like. Bown was one of the first Black women to lead a hard bop piano trio in the late 1950s, recorded a debut on Columbia Records in 1958 at twenty-seven years old, worked inside the professional orbit of Quincy Jones at degree 0 and Oliver Nelson on *The Blues and the Abstract Truth* (1961), served as a session pianist for Motown through the 1960s, received favorable reviews in *DownBeat*, and then was erased from public memory as thoroughly as any accomplished jazz musician has ever been. The Pacific-slope Flycatcher is literally named *difficilis* — "difficult" — because the 1989 AOU taxonomic split that created the species as a recognized taxon was driven entirely by vocal differences from the Cordilleran Flycatcher *E. occidentalis*. The two species are virtually identical in plumage. Remove the voice and the taxonomy collapses back into a single lump called "Western Flycatcher." The species exists as a distinguishable scientific entity because of its call. Both halves of degree 6 prove the same thing across cultural and biological registers: identity is in the signal, not the surface. The known-by-listening pattern. The filter-failure pattern. The degree where the engine stops counting only what survived.

Degree 6 introduces three structural firsts for the species engine that will reshape every subsequent SPS entry. The Pacific-slope Flycatcher is the engine's first neotropical migrant — arriving in the Pacific Northwest in April, departing by September, absent for six months while wintering in western Mexico from Sonora down to Oaxaca on a round-trip journey of 2,000+ miles. The six degrees preceding degree 6 were all year-round residents: the Great Blue Heron *Ardea herodias* at degree 0 in Puget Sound estuaries, the Pacific Wren *Troglodytes pacificus* at degree 1 in the understory, the Varied Thrush *Ixoreus naevius* at degree 2 in the conifer canopy, the Black-capped Chickadee *Poecile atricapillus* at degree 3 in mixed woodlands, the Northern Spotted Owl *Strix occidentalis caurina* at degree 4 in old-growth Douglas-fir, and the Red-breasted Nuthatch *Sitta canadensis* at degree 5 in coniferous forest. None of those species leaves. The flycatcher opens the temporal-presence axis that the resident-only circle lacked: the acoustic niche goes silent for half the year and the return of the "ptseet" in April is itself a seasonal marker, an event, a re-entry. The flycatcher is also the engine's first riparian habitat species — red alder *Alnus rubra* corridors along streams, bigleaf maple *Acer macrophyllum* canopy, sword fern *Polystichum munitum* understory. The transition zone where water meets forest, where deciduous replaces conifer. First deciduous riparian habitat in the circle. Indicator species for intact riparian corridors. And the flycatcher introduces the voice-as-taxonomic-identity principle: the Pacific-slope Flycatcher exists as a species *because of its call*, not despite plumage similarity but through vocal distinction that the AOU recognized in Supplement 37 (1989) as sufficient to split what had been the Western Flycatcher into two taxa.

For the artist engine at degree 6, the gender dimension deepens into the instrumentalist glass ceiling. Ernestine Anderson at degree 3 was a vocalist — the jazz industry recognized the woman-at-the-microphone role, from Billie Holiday and Ella Fitzgerald to Sarah Vaughan and Dinah Washington. Bown was an instrumentalist, and the piano bench in hard bop was understood as a male position occupied by Bud Powell, Thelonious Monk, Horace Silver, Wynton Kelly, Tommy Flanagan, Red Garland. Three layers of resistance — Black, female, pianist — stacked against her. The vocalist ceiling was high; the instrumentalist ceiling was higher. The seventh artist-city pattern emerges at degree 6: The Erasure. The Erasure is not the absence of accomplishment — it is the absence of memory. The engine's job at degree 6 is inventory, not curation, and that is why Bown is here.

The piano lineage from Overton Berry at degree 5 to Patti Bown at degree 6 is the engine's first instrument-specific consecutive pair, and every dimension inverts. Berry played stride and mainstream swing piano; Bown played hard bop. ## Part A: Patti Bown — The Erasure

- **Seattle Origins and Central District Root.** Born June 26, 1931, in Seattle, Washington. Raised in the Central District — the seventh consecutive S36 degree connected to that small neighborhood, the same blocks that produced Quincy Jones at degree 0, Ernestine Anderson at degree 3, and Overton Berry at degree 5. The Jackson Street corridor was active during her formative years — Black-and-Tan clubs, the Rocking Chair, the Washington Social and Educational Club. She came up in the corridor's terminal decade before urban renewal began the demolition. The Central District at degree 6 is saturated with the same three pipelines — church, public school, and club scene — that produced every prior S36 anchor, but Bown's path diverges through a fourth pipeline: Seattle University.
- **Seattle University — Seventh Educational Pipeline.** Studied music formally at Seattle University, a Jesuit institution on Capitol Hill. First S36 artist connected to Seattle University rather than Garfield High School, Berklee, UC Santa Cruz, the University of Washington, bandstand apprenticeship, or self-teaching. The engine's educational taxonomy now runs seven-deep at degree 6: Garfield (Jones), Berklee (multiple), UC Santa Cruz (Frisell), UW (Standifer, Seales), bandstand apprenticeship (Anderson, Berry), self-taught (various), Seattle University (Bown). Formal Jesuit music training is a distinct pedagogical environment — emphasizing sight-reading, theory, counterpoint, and classical technique alongside jazz practice — and produces a pianist with strong formal chops, not just a blues-and-swing player. Bown's voicings and chromatic approach bear the Jesuit-training fingerprint.
- **Columbia Records Debut 1958 — The Most Prestigious Label.** *Patti Bown Plays Big Piano* (1958) on Columbia Records — home of Miles Davis, Dave Brubeck, Thelonious Monk. At twenty-seven years old, Bown was recording as a leader on the label that defined the jazz mainstream. The debut featured original compositions and standards. The title itself — *Plays Big Piano* — is a declaration: full, muscular, authoritative touch, not the "lady pianist" softness that 1950s marketing would have expected. The Columbia debut is the single most significant surviving document of her artistry, and it is out of print. No reissue. No streaming-era resurfacing comparable to the Columbia reissues that Davis, Brubeck, and Monk have received. The label halo that at later degrees (degree 29, Prestige Records, "Boogaloo Joe" Jones) amplifies obscure catalogs did not apply to Bown. The debut sits in a Columbia vault and the vault has not been opened.
- **The Pianist-as-Bandleader Authority.** One of the first Black women to lead a hard bop piano trio on a major label. Not as a vocalist-who-plays-piano — Nina Simone's path at the same era, Shirley Horn's path, Diana Krall's later path — but as a pianist-first bandleader whose technique and authority demanded the trio. The distinction matters. The vocalist-who-plays-piano role was legible in 1950s jazz because Sarah Vaughan and Dinah Washington and Billie Holiday had established the woman-at-the-microphone position. The female-instrumentalist-as-bandleader was not legible. Mary Lou Williams had the chops and the legacy but was older and slotted into the swing-era historiography. Marian McPartland was a peer but was Irish-British and occupied a different racial cell in the industry's taxonomy. Bown was a Black woman leading a hard bop trio on Columbia in 1958. Nearly unprecedented. Engine-first female instrumentalist. Fourth theory node: pianist-as-bandleader authority — the organizational Amiga Principle at small-group scale, a trio of three rather than a big band of seventeen.
- **Quincy Jones Orbit and Oliver Nelson Sessions.** Worked professionally within the orbit of Quincy Jones (degree 0, same Central District origin) in his arranging and session work through the late 1950s and 1960s. Played on Oliver Nelson's *The Blues and the Abstract Truth* (Impulse! 1961), one of the canonical hard bop / post-bop documents with Eric Dolphy, Freddie Hubbard, Bill Evans, Paul Chambers, and Roy Haynes. Recorded session work for Motown through the 1960s. Favorably reviewed in *DownBeat*. These are not obscure sidemen credits — they are adjacencies to the most canonical names of the period. And each adjacency sat there for decades without anchoring her to the canon. Jones's arrangements, Nelson's Impulse! session, Motown's session book — the halos did not catch.
- **The Gender Dimension and Instrumentalist Glass Ceiling.** Anderson at degree 3 was a vocalist — the jazz industry had a legible woman-at-the-microphone role stretching from Holiday and Fitzgerald to Vaughan and Washington. Bown was an instrumentalist. The piano bench in hard bop was a male position: Bud Powell, Monk, Horace Silver, Wynton Kelly, Tommy Flanagan, Red Garland, Hank Jones, Cedar Walton, McCoy Tyner. Three layers of resistance — Black, female, pianist. The instrumentalist glass ceiling sat higher than the vocalist ceiling. Anderson's career was hard; Bown's was harder. The engine registers gender at two distinct heights after degree 6: vocalist ceiling (Anderson at 3, recognized) and instrumentalist ceiling (Bown at 6, erased). Gender gap running count after seven degrees: 2:5, and the two women occupy opposite recognition outcomes.
- **Music Theory Mapping — Four Hard Bop Nodes.** Hard bop rootless voicings (quartal harmony, left-hand chords that assume the bass plays the root, voicings designed for trio context where the pianist does not need to play bass notes). Call-and-response with gospel influence (right-hand melodic call answered by left-hand chordal punctuation — the Black church's antiphonal structure mapped into small-group jazz piano). Hard bop rhythmic drive (percussive comping, blues-rooted insistence, the piano as rhythmic instrument rather than merely harmonic accompanist). Pianist-as-bandleader authority (organizational leadership at small-group scale, the three-person trio as minimum viable jazz organization, the pianist both directing and soloing). All four nodes engine-firsts at degree 6.
- **The Erasure Pattern — Seventh Artist-City Pattern.** Same departure as The Export (Jones, degree 0). Same destination: New York City. Same professional context: the 1950s-1960s commercial jazz industry. Opposite visibility outcome. Jones became Quincy Jones — twenty-eight Grammys, *Thriller*, the name. Bown became a name most jazz listeners cannot place even when they have heard her playing on *The Blues and the Abstract Truth*. The Erasure is not the absence of accomplishment. The accomplishment was real: Columbia debut, Oliver Nelson session, Motown book, *DownBeat* review, trio leadership. The erasure is the absence of memory. Her recordings went out of print. Her reviews were not anthologized. Her connections were not narrated in retrospectives of Jones or Nelson. Her name became a footnote in other artists' discographies. The Erasure is the engine's first pattern where the canon's filter demonstrably fails — a fully accomplished career that the historiography declined to preserve.
- **Late Career and Philadelphia Death 2008.** Shifted focus toward education in later decades. Moved to Philadelphia. Died March 21, 2008, in Philadelphia at age 76. No NEA Jazz Master designation. No Grammy nominations in a seventy-six-year life. No major reissue campaign. The Seattle Times ran a short obituary. The Philadelphia outlets noted the death. *DownBeat* remembered. The canon did not. The engine, at degree 6, remembers.

## Part B: Pacific-slope Flycatcher — Voice as Identity

- **Taxonomy and the 1989 Split.** *Empidonax difficilis* Baird 1858, family Tyrannidae (tyrant flycatchers — the largest bird family in the Americas with 400+ species), order Passeriformes. Genus *Empidonax* from Greek *empis* (gnat) plus *anax* (king) — "king of the gnats." Species epithet *difficilis* — "difficult" — the most explicit naming in the engine so far about the identification problem itself. Until 1989, lumped with the Cordilleran Flycatcher *E. occidentalis* as a single "Western Flycatcher" species. The American Ornithological Union's 37th Supplement (1989) split the two taxa based almost entirely on vocal differences, a split that would be impossible to justify on plumage alone because the two species are virtually identical visually. Voice IS identity at the taxonomic level. The split is the engine's first species whose existence as a recognized taxon depends on bioacoustics.
- **Morphology and Field Identification.** 5.5 inches long, wingspan 7-9 inches, weight 8-12 grams. Olive-green above, yellowish below, diagnostic teardrop-shaped (almond-shaped) eye-ring, broad flat bill adapted for snapping insects in mid-air. Two pale wing bars. The teardrop eye-ring is the most useful visual field mark, but even that is shared with other *Empidonax* species. Most birders and field guides explicitly recommend identification by voice and habitat rather than plumage. The species refuses visual identification. A metaphor made physical: you cannot see Bown's Columbia debut back into print by looking harder, and you cannot see this species into existence by looking harder — both require listening.
- **The Diagnostic "Ptseet" Call — Upslurred Question, Half Cadence.** A sharp, clear upslurred call usually transcribed as "ptseet" or "su-wheet" — ascending from 2.5-3.0 kHz to 3.5-4.5 kHz over 0.3-0.5 seconds in a smooth continuous upward glide. Acoustically: a half cadence on the dominant. The note rises and creates harmonic tension that the ear expects to be resolved by a descending or terminal note. The resolution does not come. The "ptseet" hangs in the air, unresolved. First species in the circle whose primary vocalization ascends and remains unresolved. The engine's first question mark, the first half-cadence acoustic element. Every prior species vocalization either returned to a stable pitch (drone, motif, metronome) or did not establish a harmonic expectation at all (silence, cascade, language). The flycatcher creates expectation and declines to close it.
- **The Three-Phrase Dawn Song as Ternary Form.** Structured three-phrase sequence performed at dawn: "ptseet" (upslurred whistle, the question) + "ptsik" (short, sharp, downslurred chip, the rhythmic accent) + "suweet" (burry two-part note, the closing gesture). Performed in fixed A-B-C order. This is ternary form — the simplest complete formal structure in Western music, the compositional skeleton underlying thousands of sonata-allegro movements and song forms. The engine has not previously encountered multi-section formal structure in a bird vocalization. Degrees 0-5 delivered single-note analysis (Great Blue Heron croak, Pacific Wren cascade, Varied Thrush drone, Black-capped Chickadee language, Spotted Owl motif, Red-breasted Nuthatch metronome). Degree 6 is the first multi-section formal structure. The dawn song is the engine's first composition, not merely a call.
- **Voice as Primary Identity — Theory Mapping.** The flycatcher maps onto the jazz concept of personal tone — you recognize Coltrane by his sound, not by his face. Miles Davis's muted trumpet is identifiable in two notes. Monk's touch is identifiable in a single chord. The Pacific-slope Flycatcher's species identity operates the same way: its "ptseet" is diagnostic in one note. The 1989 AOU split made this taxonomically official. Voice-as-identity theory node applies to both Part A (Bown's piano touch — rootless voicings, rhythmic drive — is her audible signature independent of her physical appearance) and Part B (the flycatcher's call is its taxonomic signature independent of its plumage). The parallel is not metaphor — it is the same structural principle operating in two disciplines.
- **First Neotropical Migrant — Seasonal Presence as Temporal Form.** Arrives in the PNW in April. Departs by September. Absent for six months of the year. Winters in western Mexico from Sonora down to Oaxaca. Round-trip 2,000+ miles annually. The engine's first species with temporal presence and absence in the PNW soundscape — the acoustic niche goes silent for half the year. The return of the "ptseet" in April is itself a seasonal marker, an acoustic event. The annual cycle maps structurally onto the four-movement symphony (exposition: April arrival, development: May-July breeding, recapitulation: August-September late-summer calls, coda: migration and winter absence). First temporal-form species.
- **First Riparian Alder Ecosystem.** Breeding habitat: riparian red alder (*Alnus rubra*) corridors along PNW streams, with bigleaf maple (*Acer macrophyllum*) canopy and sword fern (*Polystichum munitum*) understory. The transition zone where water meets forest, where deciduous replaces conifer. First deciduous riparian habitat in the circle. The six preceding degrees occupied estuary (Heron), understory (Wren), conifer canopy (Thrush), mixed woodland (Chickadee), old-growth Douglas-fir (Owl), and coniferous forest (Nuthatch). None entered the riparian-alder band. The flycatcher is an indicator species for intact riparian corridors — its presence confirms vegetative structure, moisture regime, insect productivity, and stream temperature regulation. Riparian ecology enters the engine at degree 6 and the stream-corridor axis will carry forward.
- **Conservation Status and Declining Trend.** IUCN Least Concern, but Breeding Bird Survey documents approximately 2-3% annual decline since the 1970s. Partners in Flight 2020 population estimate: ~6.5 million. Threat vectors: riparian forest degradation in the PNW breeding range (stream corridor logging, agricultural conversion, urban expansion), tropical forest loss in western Mexico on the wintering grounds (Sonoran, Sinaloan, Oaxacan deforestation), climate-related phenology shifts that decouple arrival timing from peak insect emergence. The acoustic niche is shrinking. Six-month absence is becoming structural rather than purely cyclical.
- **Bioacoustics-as-Taxonomy — Epistemological Node.** The 1989 AOU split is the engine's clearest case study of an acoustic property serving as the primary taxonomic criterion. Macaulay Library holds the diagnostic recordings. Johnson 1980 *Empidonax* vocalization research established the framework. Kroodsma's acoustic-communication literature provides the theoretical frame. The engine's registered principle at degree 6: when voice differentiates cleanly and plumage does not, voice wins taxonomically. The AOU has since applied the same principle to multiple subsequent splits. Bioacoustics as a testable, falsifiable taxonomic method is now part of the engine's vocabulary.

## The Degree 6 Parallel: The Erasure and the Unseeable

The resonance between Patti Bown and the Pacific-slope Flycatcher emerged on five independent axes that run in parallel across the degree:

1. **Identity through performance, not appearance.** Bown's authority came from her playing — her touch, her voicings, her rhythmic drive. The flycatcher's species identity exists only through its call, not through olive-green plumage that is indistinguishable from its sibling species. Both refuse visual identification.
2. **Known by those who listen, unknown to those who only look.** Bown was reviewed favorably by critics who actually heard her on Columbia and with Oliver Nelson. The flycatcher is identified instantly by birders who hear "ptseet" but remains unidentifiable to observers relying on sight. Listening produces knowledge; looking does not.
3. **Underrecognized despite defining characteristics.** Bown had Columbia, Jones's orbit, Nelson's session, Motown session work, and *DownBeat* reviews — a fully accomplished career by every professional measure. She was erased anyway. The flycatcher has the single most diagnostic call in the *Empidonax* genus, and visual observers still confuse it with its congeners. In both cases the defining evidence was present and the recognition failed.
4. **The question that does not resolve.** The flycatcher's "ptseet" is a half cadence on the dominant — rises and does not resolve. Bown's career is a half cadence on the dominant of her own biography — Columbia debut, Oliver Nelson session, and then the arc simply ends without the recapitulation that the earlier movements suggested was coming. Both hang in the air.
5. **Seasonal and archival absence.** The flycatcher disappears from the PNW soundscape for six months each year and returns in April. Bown's career disappeared from public memory and has not returned. The seasonal absence is cyclical and recoverable; the archival absence is linear and has no April. The engine holds both in the same position at degree 6.

## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **The Erasure pattern discovery** gave degree 6 its deepest structural contribution — adding a seventh artist-city pattern that explains what happens when the canon's filter fails a genuinely accomplished artist. The engine gained a lens for documenting the forgotten that will apply forward to every subsequent degree where a professionally accomplished figure has fallen out of public memory. Inventory over curation.
- **Piano lineage inversion across Berry and Bown proved analytically productive.** Every dimension (style, voicings, context, left hand, role, training, city pattern, visibility, gender) inverts, creating a conversation between opposites rather than a progression. The engine's first instrument-specific consecutive pair revealed that two artists on the same instrument can be structurally opposite at every axis except the instrument itself.
- **Voice-as-identity produced the strongest Part B concept since the silence principle at degree 0.** The 1989 AOU taxonomic split driven entirely by voice gave the species engine a definitive case study in acoustic identity. The principle is testable and falsifiable against AOU Supplement 37 and Macaulay Library recordings.
- **Three-phrase dawn song as ternary form extended the music theory mapping beyond single-note analysis into multi-section formal structure for the first time.** A species vocalization is now recognized as ternary-form composition, not merely a call. The engine's formal vocabulary expanded from motif (degree 4) to complete three-movement structure (degree 6).
- **Neotropical migrant introduction added the temporal dimension** (seasonal presence and absence) that the resident-only circle lacked through degrees 0-5. The acoustic niche now has a twelve-month schedule, not merely a daily one — the frequency-time grid established at degree 4 (Spotted Owl nocturnal) gained a second axis (month-of-year migration) at degree 6.
- **First riparian ecosystem opened the deciduous-corridor habitat band.** Degrees 0-5 had not entered the stream-meets-forest transition zone. Red alder / bigleaf maple / sword fern is now registered as an indicator habitat type.
- **The parallel on five axes is the densest Part A / Part B resonance yet documented.** Identity-through-performance, known-by-listening, underrecognized-despite-defining-characteristics, unresolved-question, seasonal-and-archival-absence. Each axis is independently verifiable, and the five-axis density sets a new bar for subsequent degrees.

### What Could Be Better

- **Central District concentration at seven consecutive degrees is analytically rich but geographically unsustainable.** The engine acknowledged this in the research and flagged rotation as necessary starting at degree 7. The Central District pipeline is producing so many anchors that geographic diversity is suffering — Rainier Valley, Columbia City, Capitol Hill, Ballard, West Seattle, and the broader Puget Sound metro remain nearly untouched.
- **Bown source depth is constrained by the erasure itself** — the available documentation is thinner than for any previous S36 artist, which is precisely the point but also limits the research density. The Columbia debut is out of print, no reissue exists, no full-length biography has been written, and primary sources are limited to *DownBeat* archives, Oliver Nelson session credits, Seattle University records, and two short obituaries.
- **Riparian habitat analysis could be deeper with specific stream corridor surveys and alder stand measurements.** Future releases involving riparian species should quantify habitat structure: canopy height, alder stand age, stream order, water-temperature regime, insect productivity indices. Degree 6 documented the habitat type but did not quantify the habitat parameters.
- **The gender dimension deserves its own dedicated theoretical framework.** Anderson (vocalist, recognized) and Bown (instrumentalist, erased) together reveal a two-tiered gender ceiling in jazz, but the engine has not yet formalized a taxonomy of gendered industry roles. Future degrees with female artists should add to this framework rather than treat each woman as an individual case.
- **The Mexican wintering-ground research is thinner than the PNW breeding-ground research.** The Pacific-slope Flycatcher spends half its life in western Mexico, but the engine's documentation of Sonoran, Sinaloan, and Oaxacan ecology is notably less deep than the red alder corridor documentation. Neotropical-migrant species should receive balanced breeding / wintering-ground coverage.

## Lessons Learned

1. **The engine must document the erased, not just the celebrated.** If the S36 engine only maps artists who are already well-known, it reproduces the existing canon. The canon failed Patti Bown. The engine's purpose is inventory, not curation. Degree 6 proved that documenting the forgotten produces more interesting structural analysis — the Erasure pattern, the instrumentalist glass ceiling, the Berry-Bown inversion — than documenting the already-celebrated would. Future degrees should actively seek out the professionally accomplished but publicly forgotten.
2. **Voice-as-identity is testable and falsifiable.** The Pacific-slope Flycatcher's 1989 AOU taxonomic split provides a concrete case: the species exists as a recognized taxon because of vocal differences. This is not metaphor. The connection between acoustic identity and taxonomic identity can be verified against AOU Supplement 37 (1989) and Macaulay Library recordings. Testable claims produce stronger research than interpretive ones, and the engine should prioritize bioacoustics-based species where the voice is the primary taxonomic criterion.
3. **Temporal absence is as informative as temporal presence.** The flycatcher's six-month absence from the PNW soundscape is data — it tells you what month it is, what the habitat condition is, what migration ecology looks like, and how the annual acoustic calendar is structured. The species engine gained a new axis (seasonal vs. resident) at degree 6 that will apply to many subsequent degrees. Absence is a signal, not a gap.
4. **The instrument-specific consecutive pair is a structural finding, not a coincidence.** When degrees 5 and 6 share an instrument (piano) and invert on every other dimension, the pair becomes an analytical unit that reveals more than either member would alone. The engine should watch for instrument-specific pairs in later degrees and register them explicitly — an inversion between two pianists, two saxophonists, or two guitarists is a pattern, not a sequence.
5. **Multi-section formal structure marks a capability threshold, not merely a length increase.** The flycatcher's three-phrase dawn song is not a longer version of the Spotted Owl's four-note motif; it is a different class of compositional structure, moving from rhythmic cell (motif) to ternary form (composition). Future species with multi-section vocalizations should be classified by formal type, not merely by vocalization length.
6. **The first riparian habitat opens an axis that conifer-biased degrees had collapsed.** Six prior degrees in conifer, estuary, understory, and mixed woodland had implicitly defined the PNW as a conifer biome. The Pacific-slope Flycatcher in red alder corridor reveals that the PNW is actually a mosaic of conifer and deciduous riparian bands, and the engine should weight subsequent degrees toward deciduous, wetland, and freshwater ecosystems rather than defaulting to the conifer frame.
7. **Gender runs at two ceilings, not one.** Anderson and Bown prove that "woman in jazz" is not a single category — the vocalist ceiling and the instrumentalist ceiling sit at different heights, and the instrumentalist ceiling is demonstrably higher (Anderson was recognized; Bown was erased). Future engine degrees involving women should register their position on the vocalist-vs-instrumentalist axis as a first-order variable, not a footnote.
8. **The question mark is a legitimate acoustic element.** Degrees 0-5 had resolved elements (silence resolves to stillness, cascade resolves to end of fall, drone resolves to held pitch, language resolves to semantic completion, motif resolves to ostinato recognition, metronome resolves on every tick). Degree 6 is the first unresolved element — the upslur ascends and declines to close. The engine now registers resolution-vs-non-resolution as an acoustic property worth tracking. Not every element closes.
9. **Columbia Records out-of-print is not the same as Prestige Records in-catalog.** Label halos are selective. Bown's Columbia 1958 debut has not received the reissue treatment that Davis, Brubeck, and Monk's Columbia catalogs have received. The halo catches some artists and not others, and the selection mechanism deserves its own theoretical treatment at a future degree. The engine should register which artists the halo catches and which it lets fall.
10. **The AOU Supplement is a primary source for bioacoustics claims.** Future species where voice is the primary taxonomic criterion should cite the specific AOU Supplement (number, year, page) that established the taxon, not merely reference "the AOU split." The 37th Supplement (1989) is the Bown-equivalent canonical document for the Pacific-slope Flycatcher — the primary source where voice-as-identity became officially codified. Cite the document, not the summary.
