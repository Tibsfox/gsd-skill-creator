## What Worked

The symmetry was not forced; it was the natural reading of both phenomena, and the engine registered it cleanly in both Part A and Part B prose.
- **The three-convergence pattern synthesis completed the NASA track's first structural statement.** Pioneer at degree 5, Mercury at degree 24, Ranger at degree 33 -- the engine can now state as empirical fact that new NASA programs begin with failure and converge through accumulated fixes. The pattern was hypothesized after Pioneer and strengthened after Mercury; at degree 33 it is confirmed as a three-point trend line.
- **The CD R&B cluster documentation reached the density threshold where the cluster becomes its own engine concept.** Four artists in six degrees is not four data points; it is a cluster with internal structure (Dynamics = group, Eager = national relocation, Rabb = national band membership, English = pure local). Each artist maps a different mode within the same genre/geography combination.
- **The second consecutive E=5 confirmed the regime shift that Rabb at degree 32 opened.** One datapoint is noise; two consecutive are signal. The engine can now treat the E=4 decade (21-30) as definitively closed and the post-30 energy profile as a new regime.
- **The Mare Cognitum naming story gave the NASA track a linguistic payoff.** The past-participle Latin grammar ("the sea that has become known") and the self-referential naming logic (the sea named for the act of becoming known) gave the Ranger 7 section a second-order interpretive hook that lives beyond the first-order 4,308-image fact.

## What Could Be Better

- **Larry English's biographical detail is thinner than the cluster's other three artists.** Eager's Chicago relocation, Rabb's War tenure, and the Dynamics' indie chart penetration are all well-documented. English is the purest local, which is the engine's point, but the thinness of the primary-source biographical record means the Part A section leans on club-circuit ecology more than on English-specific events. A future pass could identify session recordings, specific album or single releases, and specific venues and dates to strengthen the artist-level documentation.
- **The Ranger Block III redesign timeline is compressed to a paragraph.** Schurmeier and Heacock's six-month redesign work at JPL is historically significant enough to warrant its own subsection, potentially with the six-month compression-of-schedule metric placed in direct comparison to other NASA engineering responses to failure (the Apollo 1 post-fire redesign, the Space Shuttle post-Challenger redesign).
- **The Cassin's Finch display-song duration claim is imprecise.** The 10- to 30-second flight duration range is a rough estimate pulled from field-guide summaries. A future pass should cite a specific Macaulay Library recording identifier with documented flight-track telemetry to pin the duration to a primary-source observation.
- **The three-convergence pattern lacks a predictive statement.** The engine now documents three convergences (Pioneer 5, Mercury 24, Ranger 33). The ~19 to 9 degree gaps between them do not yet yield a prediction for when the next convergence should arrive. A future pass could either make the prediction (e.g., the next new-program convergence falls at degree ~40-42) or explicitly acknowledge that the sample size is too small for prediction.
- **Western Hemlock as the NASA Organism was under-integrated with the Cassin's Finch biology.** The hemlock-canopy-forager connection is mentioned but not developed. A future refinement pass could elaborate the foraging-height preferences, the cone-crop interactions, and the winter altitudinal migration patterns that tie the finch to the hemlock zone over the full seasonal cycle.

## Lessons Learned

1. **The third NASA convergence confirms the structural pattern.** Pioneer at degree 5 (five missions to success), Mercury at degree 24 (seven flights to completion), Ranger at degree 33 (seven failures to 4,308 images). Three independent convergences in thirty-four paired releases. The engineering method -- failure, analysis, correction, iteration, convergence -- is now empirically validated across three programs, not hypothesized from two.

2. **Burst transmission is a cross-track structural principle.** Ranger 7's 4,308 images in 17 minutes and Cassin's Finch's aerial display song in 10-30 seconds both compress maximum information into a hard-deadlined window. Both are burst transmissions by identical logic. The principle applies cleanly to both mechanical data systems and biological acoustic performance, validating it as a cross-track rather than track-specific concept.

3. **Four artists in six degrees constitutes a genuine cluster.** The CD R&B sequence (Dynamics 27, Eager 31, Rabb 32, English 33) is dense enough at four-in-six that it becomes its own engine concept with internal structural variation -- group, national relocation, national band membership, pure local. Genre/geography clusters at this density reveal the scene's mode distribution, not just the scene's existence.

4. **Documentation arrives late in convergent systems.** Both the Ranger program (seven missions before usable imagery) and the Central District's R&B scene (decades of production before engine-level documentation) produced output continuously before the moment when the output was captured and registered. The convergence was already happening. The documentation arrived after the fact. The engine formalizes this as the late-arriving-documentation principle.

5. **The longest family-return gap in the engine is twenty-one degrees.** Fringillidae went from Pine Siskin at degree 12 to Cassin's Finch at degree 33. Turdidae returned after five, Parulidae and Hirundinidae after one. Twenty-one is an outlier that establishes Fringillidae as a sparse-but-significant engine family distinct from the cluster-dense warblers and thrushes.

6. **Two consecutive E=5 entries confirm a regime shift.** Rabb at degree 32 and English at degree 33 both register at E=5. One datapoint above the E=4 decade's ceiling is noise. Two consecutive datapoints are signal. The engine can now treat the E=4 decade as closed and the post-30 energy profile as a new regime.

7. **Data density progression spans three orders of magnitude across the NASA track.** Pioneer 4 delivered zero images over 82 hours. Mercury orbital flights delivered sustained photography over multi-hour missions. Ranger 7 delivered 4,308 images in 17 minutes. The progression from radio tracking to sustained photography to burst imagery is a three-order-of-magnitude acceleration that parallels the acoustic progression from silence through song to display burst in the SPS track.

8. **Mare Cognitum is the engine's first self-referential lunar name.** The Sea That Has Become Known was named for the act of becoming known -- specifically, becoming known through the Ranger 7 impact photography. The past-participle Latin grammar and the self-referential naming logic mark the first instance in the engine's NASA track of a mission's data product generating a new geographic name on the target body. Apollo landing sites and Surveyor touchdown points will extend this pattern.

9. **The Block III redesign compressed six months of engineering into a single convergence event.** Schurmeier's team at JPL rebuilt the Ranger camera subsystem between the Ranger 6 failure in January 1964 and the Ranger 7 launch in July 1964. Six months is the engine's tightest failure-to-convergence response documented so far. The engineering method accelerates under convergence pressure.

10. **The elevational oscillation synchronizes with program-level events.** Cassin's Finch returns the engine to high elevation at exactly the degree where the Ranger program converges. The vertical breakthrough in the SPS track aligns with the capability breakthrough in the NASA track. Elevation gain and program convergence are now a paired cross-track signal the engine will watch for in future degrees.
