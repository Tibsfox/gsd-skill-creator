## Surprises

- **A satellite that just spun and swept the sky with collimators surveyed it an order of magnitude deeper.** HEAO-1 added no focusing optics and no image — it simply spun slowly about the Earth–Sun line and let four large non-imaging instruments sweep great circles across the sky. That capability deepened and broadened Uhuru's founding survey: it covered the whole sky about three times and built the HEAO A-1 catalog of 842 sources, about an order of magnitude deeper than Uhuru's 4U catalog of 339, discovering on the order of 1,500 X-ray sources in all.
- **The diffuse cosmic X-ray background turned out to have a clean ~40 keV thermal shape and a striking isotropy.** The A-2 Cosmic X-ray Experiment made the first precise, definitive broadband (3–50 keV) measurement of the diffuse cosmic X-ray background spectrum — excellently fit by optically-thin thermal bremsstrahlung at kT = 40 ± 5 keV — and established it as highly isotropic at high Galactic latitude (the dipole consistent with the Local Group's motion), the key evidence for an extragalactic origin; the background itself had been discovered on the 1962 rocket, and resolving it into discrete sources came later, but HEAO-1 read its diffuse spectrum precisely.
- **A pair of fine wire grids could pin an X-ray source to arcseconds without forming an image.** The A-3 Scanning Modulation Collimator delivered arcsecond-class positions (~10–30 arcseconds for strong sources, e.g. ~20 arcseconds for the LMC X-1/2/3 sources) by modulating each source into a fringe pattern as the spacecraft scanned, precise enough to enable systematic optical and radio counterpart identification (SS Cygni, 4U1907+09, LMC X-1/2/3, Cygnus A as an extended source) — a clear precise-localization advance beyond Uhuru's plain scanning slat collimator, yet a position from grid modulation, never a focused image.
- **The survey reached all the way up into the hard X-ray and low-energy gamma-ray band.** The A-4 experiment carried the all-sky survey up to ~10 MeV with seven actively-shielded scintillators, producing the first all-sky hard X-ray source catalog (~77 sources, 13–180 keV) and measuring the diffuse cosmic hard X-ray background — a non-imaging hard-band survey, distinct from NuSTAR's later hard-band focusing telescope, and a band-extension rather than a span record (OSO-7 had already spanned ~1 keV–10 MeV).
- **A harrier already surveys a whole field by systematic low sweeping, guided by sound.** The Northern harrier (*Circus hudsonius*) hunts by "quartering" — flying low and slow in systematic transects, covering an entire field or marsh section by section to inventory what is there — and uniquely among hawks it has an owl-like facial disc of stiff feathers that funnels sound to its ears, so it hunts substantially by ear, locating prey acoustically as it sweeps. The resonance with HEAO-1 is behavioral: the harrier's systematic area-survey of the whole ground mirrors HEAO-1's slowly spinning instruments scanning great circles across the whole sky (the harrier does not image its surroundings or "see X-rays," and it is a partial migrant).
- **A fungus surveys an entire forest floor as one connected network.** The honey fungus (*Armillaria ostoyae*) — a wood-decay fungus, not a plant — grows mostly as a vast underground/under-bark network of mycelium and dark cord-like rhizomorphs ("bootlaces") that spread through the soil and forest floor, mapping and exploiting the whole root system of a stand; a single genetic individual in the Malheur National Forest of eastern Oregon covers roughly 9–10 square kilometers and is by area among the largest single organisms on Earth — mirror to the survey side, as HEAO-1's deep, comprehensive all-sky survey went about an order of magnitude deeper than its predecessor (the "largest organism" claim attributed to that Oregon *Armillaria ostoyae* individual by area).

## Lessons Learned

1. **An axis can sustain by deepening and broadening an existing form, not only by adding a new observable or returning to a root.** The X-ray-astronomy axis sustains at obs#10 because HEAO-1 is the adjacent-sustain of the survey/catalog form Uhuru originated — the deeper, broader-band, second-generation all-sky survey, the named survey-branch successor (Uhuru, then HEAO-1). Where Uhuru founded the survey at 339 sources in a narrow band, HEAO-1 deepened and broadened it to 842 sources across 0.25–25 keV, with the diffuse-background spectrum, the precise positions, and the hard-band extension besides; the cadence continues the axis as an adjacent-sustain rather than rotating away.
2. **Continuation is by observable form, not by chronology.** HEAO-1 launched in 1977, seven years after Uhuru, and sits at obs#10 as the adjacent-sustain of the survey/catalog form Uhuru rooted at obs#9 — exactly as Einstein (1978) sits at obs#8 as the focusing-imaging origin even though it predates Chandra (1999). The axis records the survey form's root (Uhuru) and then its deeper second generation (HEAO-1).
3. **"Sustain, not origin" is the single most load-bearing HEAO-1 fact.** "HEAO-1 conducted the first all-sky X-ray survey / built the first X-ray catalog" is FALSE — those firsts belong to Uhuru (1970, obs#9). HEAO-1 is the deeper, broader-band, second-generation SUSTAIN: the HEAO A-1 catalog = 842 sources vs Uhuru's 4U = 339, about an order of magnitude deeper, the deepest survey of its era.
4. **Non-imaging is central, correct HEAO-1 content — the error is only the imaging/focusing claim.** HEAO-1 carried collimated proportional counters, a wire-grid modulation collimator, and scintillators, with no focusing optics and no image; "imaging," "focusing," "Wolter," "mirror," "arcsecond image," and "grazing-incidence optics" route to Einstein and Chandra, never to HEAO-1. The A-3 modulation collimator gives a position from grid modulation, not a focused image. The imaging/focusing telescope of the same HEAO program is the separate HEAO-2 / Einstein (1978).
5. **A program name can hide two distinct missions.** HEAO-2 = Einstein is a separate mission in the same HEAO program (the imaging observatory, obs#8); HEAO-1 = HEAO-A = the survey mission. The shared "HEAO" name must not blur the two — HEAO-1 is not Einstein and is not imaging.
6. **A first can be a survey/spectrum/position/hard-band first without being a discovery, an imaging, a timing, or a spectroscopy first.** The A-2 measured the cosmic X-ray background spectrum precisely (it did not discover it — the 1962 rocket did — and did not resolve it into sources — Einstein → ROSAT → Chandra did); the A-3 gave precise positions from grid modulation (not a focused image); the A-4 carried the survey into the hard band as a non-imaging survey (not NuSTAR's hard-band focusing and not a span record — OSO-7 already spanned ~1 keV–10 MeV); and HEAO-1's broadband counters stamp no timing-observatory first (RXTE, NICER) and no high-resolution-spectroscopy first (Chandra gratings, XRISM microcalorimeter).
7. **A continuation keeps the predecessors' vocabulary and references them by name.** Because HEAO-1 sustains the X-ray-astronomy axis, the shared X-ray vocabulary is correct and kept, and Chandra (obs#1, soft/medium focusing), NuSTAR (obs#2, hard-band focusing), RXTE (obs#3, timing), IXPE (obs#4, polarimetry), NICER (obs#5, soft-band timing), Swift (obs#6, rapid-response transient), XRISM (obs#7, non-dispersive spectroscopy), Einstein (obs#8, focusing-imaging origin), and Uhuru (obs#9, survey/catalog origin) are referenced as the form complements / predecessor — but HEAO-1's own facts (the four large non-imaging instruments, the deep A-1 catalog, the A-2 background spectrum, the A-3 positions, the A-4 hard-band survey) and only its own five anchors are stamped; the predecessors' anchors and predecessor-specific hardware (Uhuru's two back-to-back banks of proportional counters / the 4U catalog as Uhuru's / Cen X-3 / Her X-1 / Cyg X-1 / the Coma–Perseus–Virgo diffuse-cluster first; Einstein's Wolter-I optics / HRI / IPC; XRISM's microcalorimeter / Resolve; Swift's BAT / coded mask / UVOT; NICER's concentrators; IXPE's Gas Pixel Detectors; RXTE's PCA / HEXTE; NuSTAR's deployable mast; Chandra's sub-arcsecond optics and gratings) stay with their missions.
8. **Accuracy guardrails guard against the launch-site, program, end-of-life, and people-role classes of error.** Stating the launch as 12 August 1977 on an Atlas-Centaur (Atlas SLV-3D / Centaur-D1AR) from Cape Canaveral LC-36B, a US site (not the Uhuru San Marco / Kenya / Scout launch); HEAO-1 as HEAO-A, the first of the three HEAO observatories (HEAO-2 = Einstein a separate mission); operations ending 9 January 1979 and reentry March 1979 (~two months apart, never collapsed); the management (Marshall Space Flight Center) and prime contractor (TRW) distinct from the A-1 (NRL/Friedman), A-2 (GSFC/Boldt + Garmire, NOT Gorenstein), A-3 (SAO + MIT/Schwartz + Bradt), and A-4 (UCSD + MIT/Peterson + Matteson) experiment teams; Giacconi, Rossi, and Marjorie Townsend kept as founders / Uhuru personnel, not HEAO-1's; the A-1 LASS modules as 1350–1900 cm² PER MODULE (not "1.65 m² total"); and the A-1 catalog as 842 sources and the A-4 as ~77 — keeps the facts load-bearing and correct.
