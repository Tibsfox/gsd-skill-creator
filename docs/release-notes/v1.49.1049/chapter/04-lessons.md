1. **An axis can sustain by deepening and broadening an existing form, not only by adding a new observable or returning to a root.**
   The X-ray-astronomy axis sustains at obs#10 because HEAO-1 is the adjacent-sustain of the survey/catalog form Uhuru originated — the deeper, broader-band, second-generation all-sky survey, the named survey-branch successor (Uhuru, then HEAO-1). Where Uhuru founded the survey at 339 sources in a narrow band, HEAO-1 deepened and broadened it to 842 sources across 0.25–25 keV, with the diffuse-background spectrum, the precise positions, and the hard-band extension besides; the cadence continues the axis as an adjacent-sustain rather than rotating away.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12145_

2. **Continuation is by observable form, not by chronology.**
   HEAO-1 launched in 1977, seven years after Uhuru, and sits at obs#10 as the adjacent-sustain of the survey/catalog form Uhuru rooted at obs#9 — exactly as Einstein (1978) sits at obs#8 as the focusing-imaging origin even though it predates Chandra (1999). The axis records the survey form's root (Uhuru) and then its deeper second generation (HEAO-1).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12146_

3. **Sustain, not origin" is the single most load-bearing HEAO-1 fact.**
   "HEAO-1 conducted the first all-sky X-ray survey / built the first X-ray catalog" is FALSE — those firsts belong to Uhuru (1970, obs#9). HEAO-1 is the deeper, broader-band, second-generation SUSTAIN: the HEAO A-1 catalog = 842 sources vs Uhuru's 4U = 339, about an order of magnitude deeper, the deepest survey of its era.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12147_

4. **Non-imaging is central, correct HEAO-1 content — the error is only the imaging/focusing claim.**
   HEAO-1 carried collimated proportional counters, a wire-grid modulation collimator, and scintillators, with no focusing optics and no image; "imaging," "focusing," "Wolter," "mirror," "arcsecond image," and "grazing-incidence optics" route to Einstein and Chandra, never to HEAO-1. The A-3 modulation collimator gives a position from grid modulation, not a focused image. The imaging/focusing telescope of the same HEAO program is the separate HEAO-2 / Einstein (1978).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12148_

5. **A program name can hide two distinct missions.**
   HEAO-2 = Einstein is a separate mission in the same HEAO program (the imaging observatory, obs#8); HEAO-1 = HEAO-A = the survey mission. The shared "HEAO" name must not blur the two — HEAO-1 is not Einstein and is not imaging.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12149_

6. **A first can be a survey/spectrum/position/hard-band first without being a discovery, an imaging, a timing, or a spectroscopy first.**
   The A-2 measured the cosmic X-ray background spectrum precisely (it did not discover it — the 1962 rocket did — and did not resolve it into sources — Einstein → ROSAT → Chandra did); the A-3 gave precise positions from grid modulation (not a focused image); the A-4 carried the survey into the hard band as a non-imaging survey (not NuSTAR's hard-band focusing and not a span record — OSO-7 already spanned ~1 keV–10 MeV); and HEAO-1's broadband counters stamp no timing-observatory first (RXTE, NICER) and no high-resolution-spectroscopy first (Chandra gratings, XRISM microcalorimeter).
