## Lessons Learned

1. **The dynamic range is the composition.** *We Are the Romans*'s primary innovation is the deployment of full dynamic range as structural tool rather than as decoration. The quiet sections are not respite from the loud; they are the tension accumulation that makes the loud a structural event. The lesson generalizes: in any artifact that deploys a parametric range (dynamic, frequency, rhythmic, lexical), using the range as architecture rather than as decoration is the path to the ceiling register, and the path is available across media.
2. **Non-coincidence is compositional.** Four independent voices operating simultaneously at individual maximums without resolving to common ground is not arrangement chaos but compositional intent. The lesson applies beyond music: in any system of multiple simultaneous voices (code, prose, ensemble work), the non-coincidence can be the structure rather than the problem, and distinguishing compositional non-coincidence from uncoordinated mess is the analytical skill that the ceiling register requires.
3. **Process is not rhythm and the distinction is analytically precise.** Rhythm has period and pulse that a body can entrain to; process is structured physics that continues regardless of entrainment. The Snoqualmie flood is process at acoustic maximum, and the geological-timescale calibration depends on keeping the process-versus-rhythm distinction sharp. Collapsing process into rhythm loses the impersonal-scale character that the calibration grounds in.
4. **The recording decision cannot be separated from the compositional decision.** *We Are the Romans* sounds like the genre at its best partly because Steve Albini recorded it at Electrical Audio under his minimal-processing philosophy. The recording approach is constitutive of the ceiling status, not incidental, and the lesson generalizes to any artifact where the production surface carries the compositional information: the surface is part of the composition, and treating production as downstream of composition loses the interaction that the ceiling register depends on.
5. **Voluntary dissolution preserves the ceiling.** Botch ended by choice in 2002 after the work was complete, and the dissolution preserved *We Are the Romans* as the ceiling by refusing to extend past the peak. The lesson generalizes to career-scale compositional decision-making: extending past the peak dilutes rather than extends, and the refusal to extend is itself a form of composition that the ceiling-preservation architecture requires.
