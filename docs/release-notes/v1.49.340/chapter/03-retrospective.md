Future SPS documentation should consider multi-degree arcs for species whose communication repertoires span functionally-distinct acoustic registers.
- **The Eastside-as-first-geography register expansion opens a substantial future documentation space.** Modest Mouse's Issaquah origin is not just a specific data point — it establishes the Eastside (Issaquah, Bellevue, Redmond, Sammamish, Kirkland, Mercer Island) as a first-class geographic register that prior S36 degrees have not carried. This opens future Eastside-origin documentation for bands, labels, and music-community institutions that have emerged from the Eastside across the Pacific Northwest music history. The Issaquah entry is the anchor for a geographic register that the engine will expand across subsequent Q3 and Q4 degrees.
- **The Isaac Brock sustained-commitment-at-manic-amplitude register complements prior sustained-commitment documentation across amplitude poles.** Mark Arm's forty-year Mudhoney commitment at the grunge-fuzz pole (189), Sean Tollefson's twenty-eight-year Tullycraft commitment at the twee-pop pole (194), and Isaac Brock's nearly-thirty-year Modest Mouse commitment at the manic-restlessness pole (199) together establish a three-point sustained-commitment documentation spanning the Pacific Northwest indie-rock amplitude spectrum from quiet (E=3) through moderate (E=5) to high (E=6-8). The engine's duration-to-identity vocabulary now operates across the full amplitude range rather than being concentrated at any single register.
- **The E=13 Q3 combined-energy high-watermark makes the amplitude-ceiling observable.** Prior Q3 degrees have accumulated combined-energy values below 13, but without an explicit high-watermark the amplitude-ceiling of Q3 was not observable. Degree 199's E=6+E=7=13 pairing establishes the observable ceiling and creates a reference point for subsequent Q3 degree interpretation. Future Q3 degrees can be evaluated relative to whether they match, exceed, or fall below the 13-ceiling, which makes the quadrant's amplitude-progression structure legible in ways that prior documentation did not support.
- **The cross-sync parallel structure forces productive structural observation across radically different time scales.** Modest Mouse's thirty-year human-career commitment and the Roosevelt Elk lineage's two-million-year species-commitment are separated by roughly five orders of magnitude in time scale, but the cross-sync parallel structure forces the observation that sustained-commitment-under-antagonistic-pressure operates across all time scales. The cross-sync is not a surface-level analogy; it is a structural statement about how commitment, pressure, and amplitude relate regardless of the specific time scale, and the pairing at degree 199 makes this scale-invariance legible in a way that single-half documentation could not.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Three-element arcs generate more interpretive depth than the sum of their individual entries.** The Roosevelt Elk trio across degrees 197-199 demonstrates that multi-degree documentation of related subjects produces emergent structural observations (the 7-5-7 amplitude arc, the cows-at-center-bulls-at-extremes social dynamic signature) that single-degree documentation could not surface. Future SPS and S36 documentation should consider whether three-element or longer arcs are available for subjects whose complete register requires multiple degrees, and prioritize such arcs where the substantive content supports them.

2. **Geographic-register expansion is a structural event, not just a location change.** Modest Mouse's Issaquah origin does not simply add a new data point; it opens the King County Eastside as a first-class geographic register that prior degrees have not carried. Future degrees should treat geographic-register expansion as a structural event that deserves explicit documentation rather than as a passing detail, and the engine's geographic vocabulary should accumulate registers (Seattle-proper, near-Seattle, Olympia, Eastside, and future Pacific Northwest regional registers) as first-class documentation anchors.

3. **Sustained commitment operates across all amplitude registers.** The three-point sustained-commitment documentation across degrees 189 (Mudhoney, E=high), 194 (Tullycraft, E=3), and 199 (Modest Mouse, E=6) establishes that duration-to-identity conversion is amplitude-independent. The vocabulary register is not amplitude-specific; it is duration-specific. Future engine documentation should look for sustained-commitment patterns across all amplitude ranges rather than treating commitment and loudness as correlated, and the engine's duration-to-identity vocabulary should accumulate examples across the full amplitude spectrum.

4. **Close-combat vocalization is a functional register distinct from long-distance challenge and intra-herd contact.** The Roosevelt Elk rut roar at degree 199 establishes a close-combat functional register in the engine's acoustic vocabulary that the bugle (197) and cow call (198) do not cover. Future species documentation for other animals that produce vocalizations during physical confrontation (male-male combat in various mammals, male-female agonistic interactions, predator-prey combat) can reference the close-combat register as a first-class functional category.

5. **E=13 combined-energy establishes Q3's amplitude ceiling as an observable reference point.** Prior Q3 degrees accumulated combined-energy values below 13, but without an explicit ceiling the quadrant's amplitude-progression structure was not observable. Degree 199's establishment of the E=13 high-watermark creates a reference point for subsequent Q3 evaluation. Future engine documentation should track combined-energy high-watermarks across quadrants as first-class structural statistics that enable quadrant-level interpretive depth.

6. **Label-vocabulary expansion accumulates across multiple degrees into a cluster structure.** Up Records' appearance at degrees 196 (764-HERO) and 199 (Modest Mouse) converts the label from a single-entry register to a two-band cluster, parallel to how K Records' accumulation across Tullycraft (194) and other Olympia-tradition entries builds a multi-band cluster. Future label documentation should track cluster formation explicitly as a structural event, with the third-entry in a label cluster potentially warranting special documentation as the point at which a label's register becomes structurally stable.

7. **Cross-sync structural parallels operate across radically different time scales.** Modest Mouse's thirty-year human-career commitment and the Roosevelt Elk lineage's two-million-year species-commitment differ by roughly five orders of magnitude in time scale, but the cross-sync parallel structure forces the observation that sustained-commitment-under-antagonistic-pressure is a scale-invariant structural principle. Future cross-sync pairings should consider whether substantive scale-invariance observations are available, and prioritize pairings where the substantive content supports cross-scale structural statements.

8. **Indie-to-major trajectory documentation requires dedicated cross-release treatment.** The Modest Mouse Up-to-Epic-to-platinum arc is a specific instance of a broader Pacific Northwest indie-to-major trajectory that defined the music industry of its era. A dedicated engine artifact documenting this trajectory across multiple bands (Modest Mouse, Death Cab for Cutie, The Shins, Fleet Foxes, Band of Horses, and others) would surface the structural pattern more effectively than piecemeal individual-degree documentation. Future engine iterations should accommodate multi-band trajectory documentation as a first-class artifact type distinct from individual-band-and-species degree entries.

9. **Theodore Roosevelt's Olympic Peninsula conservation legacy is embedded in the engine's species vocabulary.** The Roosevelt Elk subspecies naming is not incidental; it reflects the federal conservation intervention that preserved the species' core habitat. The engine's species documentation carries Roosevelt's conservation legacy implicitly through every Roosevelt Elk entry, and future documentation should consider whether other conservation-history connections (Olympic National Park, Mount Rainier National Park, North Cascades National Park, Glacier Peak Wilderness) deserve explicit documentation as the federal-conservation register that shapes Pacific Northwest species persistence.

10. **The engine's amplitude-ceiling concept requires explicit articulation rather than implicit accumulation.** Prior quadrants and engine documentation have accumulated amplitude values without explicit ceiling-tracking, leaving the quadrant-level amplitude-progression structure legible only in retrospect. Degree 199's E=13 establishment as Q3's combined-energy ceiling-so-far suggests that future engine documentation should track amplitude-ceiling events as first-class structural statistics, and the release history should carry amplitude-ceiling-by-quadrant summaries to make this structure observable rather than implicit.
