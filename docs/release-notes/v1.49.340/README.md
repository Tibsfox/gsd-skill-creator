## Summary

**Modest Mouse** arrives at E=6 -- and **Issaquah enters the engine**, making this the first **Eastside** origin in the Seattle 360. Isaac Brock's band formed in Issaquah in 1992, an unlikely launchpad for what would become one of the most influential indie rock acts of the '90s and 2000s. From *This Is a Long Drive for Someone with Nothing to Think About* (1996) through *The Lonesome Crowded West* (1997) to the mainstream breakthrough of *Good News for People Who Love Bad News* (2004), Modest Mouse charted a path from Up Records obscurity to platinum sales without ever losing the anxious, existential restlessness that defined them. At E=6, Modest Mouse is the highest-energy artist entry since DoNormaal (188, E=8) -- Brock's manic delivery and angular guitar work demand the elevation. This degree forms an **E=13 pair** with the Roosevelt Elk rut roar (E=7), the highest combined energy of any degree in Q3 so far. On the species side, the **Roosevelt Elk rut roar** closes the elk trio at E=7 -- matching the bugle's energy (197, E=7) but producing an entirely different sound. The rut roar is the vocalization of close combat: a deep, guttural bellowing that accompanies antler-locking and physical confrontation between rival bulls. Where the bugle carries across valleys as a challenge, the rut roar is produced at contact range, an expression of maximum exertion as 1,000-pound animals collide.
