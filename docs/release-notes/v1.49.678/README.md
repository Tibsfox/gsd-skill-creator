## Summary

Mir Kvant astrophysics module (Soviet designation 37KE No. 17501) launched 1987-03-31 00:06:16 UTC from Baikonur Cosmodrome LC-200/39 on a Proton-K (8K82K) heavy-lift launch vehicle — heaviest Soviet payload to LEO at that time at approximately 22,000 kg. Module designed by NPO Energia under general designer Valentin Glushko and Mir program lead Yuri Semenov; bus derived from the Soviet TKS military spaceplane Functional Cargo Block (FGB) — first civilian-program TKS-FGB use, establishing a design lineage that continues through Zarya FGB at ISS 1998.

Kvant carried the Roentgen Observatory consisting of five X-ray and UV instruments, four of which were Western contributions — **FIRST WESTERN INSTRUMENTS ON A SOVIET SPACE STATION**: the SIRENE-2 coded-aperture X-ray imager from CNES France (PI Jacques Paul, Service d'Astrophysique CEA Saclay); the Pulsar X-1 X-ray spectrometer from the Soviet Institute for Space Research IKI (PI Rashid Sunyaev); the HEXE High Energy X-ray Experiment from Max Planck Institute for Extraterrestrial Physics Garching West Germany (PIs Joachim Trümper + Hubertus Reppin); the TTM wide-field X-ray imager from University of Birmingham UK and University of Cologne West Germany (PI Gerald Skinner via UK Department of Education and Science); and the GPC-Mir broadband X-ray spectrometer from IKI Soviet. The Roentgen Observatory operated 1987-1997 (TTM through 1989; HEXE through 1997) and recorded the X-ray flux from Supernova 1987A in the Large Magellanic Cloud 1987-1989 — the only naked-eye supernova since SN 1604 Kepler.

Kvant's approach to Mir 1987-04-05 attempted forward-axial docking but failed: a plastic bag from Progress 28 hardware deployment had been pushed into Mir's aft docking node during prior cargo unloading, blocking the docking interface. The contingency EVA on 1987-04-11 by Soyuz TM-2 EO-1 crew Yuri Romanenko + Aleksandr Laveykin (3h 40m total; **FIRST CONTINGENCY EVA IN MIR ERA**) cleared the obstruction. Kvant successfully docked at Mir's aft port on 1987-04-12 12:36 UTC and remained attached through Mir's full 15-year operational life until 2001-03-23 controlled deorbit. Substrate-form FIRST-MODULE-FAILED-TO-DOCK-RECOVERED-BY-EVA obs#1 first-instance NEW LOCKED.

Supporting EO-1 crew: CDR Col Yuri Viktorovich Romanenko (Soviet Air Force; 3rd career flight after Soyuz 26/EO-1 Salyut 6 1977 + Soyuz 38 Cuba flight 1980; 326-day Mir mission 1987-02-05 to 1987-12-29; **430.32 cumulative days at career close** = career-record holder at that time; born 1944-08-01 Koltubanovsky Orenburg Oblast; died 2024-05-18 Saint Petersburg — substrate-coincident-distinct: exactly 2 years before v678 ship date in calendar coincidence). FE Aleksandr Ivanovich Laveykin (civilian NPO Energia engineer; rookie 1st flight; 174-day partial-mission to 1987-07-30; **first cosmonaut medically-relieved-mid-mission from Mir** due to cardiac arrhythmia 1987-06; born 1951-05-21 Moscow). Replacement: FE Aleksandr Pavlovich Aleksandrov (Soyuz TM-3 relief 1987-07-22; 2nd career flight after Soyuz T-9 EO-2 Salyut 7 1983; not to be confused with Belorussian-cosmonaut Soyuz TM-13 1991 of similar name).

Lyappa hand-rotating arm (commissioned by Kizim-Solovyev v677 = previous milestone) had its first operational use at Kvant berthing 1987-04-12 — **LYAPPA-ARM-FIRST-OPERATIONAL-USE-AT-MIR obs#1 first-instance NEW LOCKED**. The Lyappa arm subsequently enabled all 4 additional Mir module re-berthings (Kvant-2 1989 + Kristall 1990 + Spektr 1995 + Priroda 1996).
