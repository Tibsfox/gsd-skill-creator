\subsection{One-Paragraph Summary}
\label{sec:vision:summary}

This mission studies \emph{drift}: the space between the behaviour an LLM was
instructed to exhibit and the behaviour it actually produces, measured over the
trajectory of its work. The full executed package shipped here collects four
modules --- knowledge (A), alignment (B), retrieval (C), synthesis (D) --- each
grounded in published 2024--2026 arXiv literature and each audited for
numeric-attribution integrity, citation uniqueness, and quote-length discipline
via the hard-blocking CAPCOM gate defined in Phase~684 and invoked at the
final page of this document.

\texttt{drift-mission.tex} Stage~3 D8 (and Success Criterion~6): \textbf{SC}

\subsection{Citation and Numeric Audits}

Two additional audits accompany this artifact, land in \texttt{audits/}, and
are consumed by the release-note CAPCOM-GATE.md sign-off:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0.3em]

cited work inspected against the peer-review / arXiv / documented-standard
floor (SC-05), quote-length floor of 15 words (SC-02), at-most-one-quote-per-source

checks on the final-aggregated document.

\item \textbf{Numeric audit} (\texttt{audits/numeric\_audit.md}) --- every
numerical claim (\%, AUROC, pp, F1, scale factor) scanned for inline citation
within $\pm 50$ characters (SC-01). Reports the violation count and surfaces
any qualitative-only findings that are deliberately un-numerated (e.g.\
\texttt{rsd2026diffusion}).
\end{itemize}
