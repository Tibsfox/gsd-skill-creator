## What Could Be Better

- **Pavitt's post-Sub-Pop career as a music blogger and podcaster is not fully documented.** After stepping back from Sub Pop's day-to-day operations in the mid-1990s, Pavitt pursued music writing, blogging, and podcasting (the **Sub Pop U.S.A.** podcast documenting label history). The later-career arc is mentioned at the summary level but not developed at the depth that the 1980s fanzine/radio/label period receives. A dedicated post-Sub-Pop-career artifact would enrich the entry but was outside this degree's scope.
- **The Sub Pop 200 box set's full contents and the regional-scene surveys are compressed.** Sub Pop 200 (1988) included tracks by approximately 24 Pacific Northwest artists and functioned as a cross-label regional survey. The full contents and the curatorial rationale for each inclusion are mentioned at summary level but not at track-by-track depth. A dedicated Sub Pop 200 artifact walking through the regional-scene surveys encoded in the compilation would be valuable but was outside this single-degree scope.
- **The Evergreen State College peer network beyond Calvin Johnson and Slim Moon is underdeveloped.** The Evergreen network produced Pavitt, Calvin Johnson (K Records), Slim Moon (Kill Rock Stars), and additional figures in film (Matt Groening, Lynda Barry), politics, and environmental advocacy. The music-infrastructure peers (Pavitt, Johnson, Moon) are documented, but the broader peer network is mentioned at summary level. A dedicated Evergreen-alumni-network artifact would contextualize Pavitt's institutional substrate but was outside the scope.
- **The Rocket magazine's editorial structure and staff history are not catalogued.** Pavitt's Subterranean Pop column ran in The Rocket throughout the 1980s, and The Rocket was one of the primary institutional supports for Pacific Northwest music journalism. The magazine's editorial structure, staff, and broader coverage pattern are mentioned at summary level but not at the level of individual editors, writers, or issue-by-issue coverage. A dedicated Rocket artifact would enrich the journalism-infrastructure documentation but was deferred.
- **The specific Skagit Valley wintering sites for Trumpeter Swans are not catalogued.** The research documents Skagit Valley and Puget Sound as Pacific Northwest wintering grounds but does not list specific fields, wetlands, and refuges where wintering Trumpeter Swans are reliably observed. Specific site data varies year to year and is secondary to the ecological and acoustic profile, but a Pacific Northwest Trumpeter Swan wintering-site gazetteer would be useful for field observation.
- **The Trumpeter Swan Society's multi-decade coordination work is summarized rather than detailed.** The Trumpeter Swan Society (founded 1968) has coordinated multi-agency recovery work across state, federal, and tribal jurisdictions for over five decades. The society's specific programs, partnerships, and population-management initiatives are mentioned at the summary level but not at the level of individual projects. A dedicated society history artifact would document the institutional backbone of the recovery more fully but was outside the scope.
- **The lead poisoning threat to wintering Trumpeter Swans is noted but not quantified.** Spent lead shot ingestion from old hunting sites and lead fishing tackle remains a significant mortality factor for wintering Trumpeter Swans, particularly on Pacific Northwest wintering grounds with legacy contamination. The threat is mentioned but not quantified with specific mortality data or specific contaminated-site information. A dedicated lead-poisoning artifact would document the threat more fully but was beyond this degree's scope.
- **The Tundra Swan / Trumpeter Swan field identification challenge is documented qualitatively rather than with image-based reference.** The acoustic and morphological distinctions between the two species are documented in text, but the identification challenge (bill shape, neck posture, call structure) is not supplemented with reference imagery or spectrograms. Field guides cover this thoroughly, but a dedicated side-by-side comparison artifact would be useful for on-ground identification.
- **The Sub Pop roster's post-Pavitt evolution into the 2000s-2020s indie landscape is not traced.** Sub Pop's continued operation through the 2000s-2020s produced releases by The Shins, Iron & Wine, Beach House, Shabazz Palaces, and many others. The post-Pavitt roster evolution is mentioned at summary level but not developed at roster-by-decade depth. A dedicated Sub Pop roster evolution artifact would contextualize the label's continued vitality but was deferred.
- **The cross-reference to the Olympia Film Festival and related non-music Olympia infrastructure is underdeveloped.** Olympia's independent-music infrastructure sits within a broader independent-culture infrastructure including the Olympia Film Festival, the Evergreen State College's experimental-film programs, and a network of zines and small presses. ## Lessons Learned

1. **Diptychs of complementary partners at adjacent degrees structurally insist on co-equal contribution.** The Sub Pop co-founder diptych (Poneman 127 + Pavitt 128) and the swan comparison pair (Tundra Swan 127 + Trumpeter Swan 128) both produce their structural meaning through adjacent-degree pairing. Future diptychs (co-founders, collaborators, congeners, geographic or temporal pairs) should be presented at adjacent degrees when the engine's sequence permits, and the adjacency should be recognized as the structural vehicle for co-equal-contribution arguments.
2. **Multi-degree pattern closes produce the richest landing-point density when three or more patterns close at the same degree.** Degree 128 closes the Sub Pop co-founder diptych, the KCMU radio trio, and the Olympia label trilogy at a single landing point. The three-pattern close is the engine's highest-density pattern-close landing to date on the artist axis, and the structural density demonstrates that degrees can accumulate multiple closing patterns simultaneously. Future pattern-close degrees should be identified in advance where possible, and the engine should anticipate which degrees will function as multi-pattern-close landing points.
3. **Record-extending streaks can extend the record for multiple consecutive degrees rather than merely tying or passing once.** The Anatidae streak extended the all-time family-streak record at degree 126 (passing the Picidae nonet), again at degree 127, and again at degree 128. The three-degree extension pattern is structurally distinct from a single pass-and-move-on pattern. Future streaks approaching all-time records should be analyzed for multi-degree extension potential, and the three-degree extension should be recognized as its own structural category distinct from single-record-pass events.
4. **Infrastructure energy at E=3 is a structurally distinct register from performance energy at E=4-7.** The Poneman + Pavitt diptych codifies the E=3 infrastructure-energy register. Curatorial, editorial, critical, broadcast, and label-operational roles cluster at E=3, and the register should be applied consistently to future infrastructure-role entries (producers, A&R, journalists, DJs, label operators, venue owners, scene documentarians). The register's explicit establishment at the Sub Pop diptych makes future infrastructure-role classification easier.
5. **E=6 returns to the species axis are structurally significant when they end long lower-register runs.** Trumpeter Swan at E=6 is the first species-axis E=6 entry since American Dipper at degree 117, and the return occurs after eleven degrees of waterfowl at E=3 to E=5. Long lower-register runs on either axis accumulate anticipatory structural weight toward the register return, and the return should be recognized as a structural event distinct from the individual species' intrinsic characteristics.
6. **Congener pairs at adjacent degrees produce the richest species-comparison data.** The Tundra Swan + Trumpeter Swan pair at 127-128 demonstrates that adjacent-degree congener pairings produce comparison data across acoustic, habitat, morphological, and ecological dimensions simultaneously. Future congener pairs should be identified and presented at adjacent degrees when the engine's sequence permits, and the adjacency should be recognized as the structural vehicle for congener comparison.
7. **Conservation-recovery narratives and institutional-recovery narratives share structural similarity.** Sub Pop Records's recovery from near-bankruptcy (1990-1991) through strategic Warner Music partnership (1995) and Trumpeter Swan's recovery from near-extinction (1930s, fewer than 70 birds) through multi-agency coordination (Red Rock Lakes NWR, The Trumpeter Swan Society, state wildlife management) share the same narrative structure: depletion, persistent minimal-presence, coordinated recovery effort, restoration to prominence. Cross-axis recovery-narrative parallels should be made explicit when they co-occur at the same degree, as they reinforce the engine's argument that regional-institutional and species-population trajectories share structural similarities.
8. **Institutional infrastructure substrates span multiple decades and should be documented with temporal scope.** Bruce Pavitt's role depended on The Evergreen State College (founded 1971), KCMU (founded 1972), The Rocket (founded 1979), Sub Pop Records (founded 1988). Trumpeter Swan's recovery depended on Red Rock Lakes National Wildlife Refuge (founded 1935), The Trumpeter Swan Society (founded 1968), the North American Waterfowl Management Plan (founded 1986). In both cases, multi-decade institutional infrastructure is the substrate, and the substrate should be documented with founding dates to make the temporal scope legible. Future infrastructure-dependent entries should document the institutional substrate with founding dates and jurisdictional scope.
9. **Regional-scene documentation as critical practice is its own structural category.** Pavitt's Subterranean Pop fanzine and The Rocket column functioned as regional-scene documentation — region-by-region surveys of local scenes, reviews of small-run cassettes and seven-inch singles, interviews with label operators and band members. Regional-scene documentation is distinct from critical review of individual releases and distinct from biographical writing about individual artists; it is the practice of documenting scenes as systems. Future journalist and critic entries should note whether the figure practiced regional-scene documentation as a structural category.
10. **Three-college-labels from one college (Evergreen) demonstrate that pedagogical institutions can be music-industry substrates.** K Records (Calvin Johnson), Kill Rock Stars (Slim Moon), and Sub Pop (Pavitt + Poneman) all trace to The Evergreen State College's radical pedagogy and narrative-evaluation curriculum. Pedagogical institutions are not typically recognized as music-industry substrates, but Evergreen's production of three foundational Pacific Northwest independent labels demonstrates that interdisciplinary, self-directed academic programs can produce music-industry infrastructure. Future entries with college or university substrates should note the pedagogical-institution-as-substrate pattern when applicable.
