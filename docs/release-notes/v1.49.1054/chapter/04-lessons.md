# Lessons — v1.49.1054

1. **A lowest-background broadband-sensitivity observatory is a distinct observable form from a CCD-imaging-spectroscopy observatory.**
   ASCA (obs#14) carried the first union of imaging optics with X-ray CCDs. Suzaku (obs#15) advances the focusing-imaging branch by co-pointing the soft XIS CCDs (the refined ASCA SIS successors) and the hard collimated HXD on the lowest non-imaging hard-X-ray background then flown — the most sensitive simultaneous soft-to-hard broadband spectroscopy of its era — a distinct partition the focusing-imaging branch must keep.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12213_

2. **The Chandra/Einstein/XMM/ASCA partition must be stated in every imaging or spectroscopy claim.**
   "Sub-arcsecond imaging" and "first high-resolution X-ray grating spectroscopy" belong to Chandra (obs#1); "first imaging X-ray telescope / focusing-imaging origin" belongs to Einstein (obs#8); "largest pointed effective area" belongs to XMM-Newton (obs#13); "first X-ray CCD imaging spectroscopy" belongs to ASCA (obs#14). The correct scope for Suzaku is "first X-ray micro-calorimeter flown to orbit," "lowest non-imaging hard-X-ray background," and "first ICM profiles to the virial radius" — Suzaku is ~2 arcmin coarse and MODERATE XIS resolution.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12214_

3. **JAXA/ISAS-mission framing is an accuracy requirement, not a qualifier.**
   Suzaku is a JAXA/ISAS mission of Japan (Japan's 5th X-ray satellite, the recovery of ASTRO-E, M-V launch from Uchinoura), not a NASA mission; the NASA/US contribution is the GSFC-led XRT foil optics (co-developed with Nagoya and ISAS, "designed and led" not "built"), the GSFC-led XRS micro-calorimeter, and the MIT-led XIS CCDs (not GSFC), plus the GOF at GSFC. Every identification of Suzaku states the JAXA/ISAS framing before naming the GSFC / MIT / GOF contribution.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12215_

4. **The micro-calorimeter first requires scoping at every occurrence.**
   "First X-ray micro-calorimeter spectrometer flown to orbit aboard an observatory" is a bounded hardware-flight first; the helium was lost 8 August 2005, about four weeks after launch, so the XRS returned NO celestial science; never "first micro-calorimeter spectroscopy" (Hitomi/SXS 2016 = first spectroscopy; XRISM/Resolve obs#7 = sustained form). ASTRO-E's XRS (2000) never reached a usable orbit and XQC is suborbital.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12216_

5. **The HXD background first is scoped to non-imaging hard-X-ray instruments.**
   The HXD achieved the lowest residual non-X-ray background among past non-imaging hard-X-ray instruments (15–70 & 150–500 keV; Kokubun et al. 2007); a focusing hard-X-ray telescope reaches a lower background per resolution element; the HXD is collimated, NOT hard-X-ray focusing (NuSTAR, obs#2), and NOT the first hard-X-ray detector (Ginga, HEAO-1 earlier).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12217_

6. **The broadband capability is identity, never a first.**
   BeppoSAX (1996) flew coaligned simultaneous broadband on one observatory ~9 years before Suzaku; Suzaku is the most SENSITIVE simultaneous broadband (lowest background), NOT the first or widest. State this honestly; never stamp the broadband capability as a first.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12218_

7. **Firsts that are NOT Suzaku's belong to their owners.**
   The largest effective area (XMM-Newton, obs#13); sub-arcsecond + first high-res grating spectroscopy + NASA Great Observatory (Chandra, obs#1); the focusing-imaging telescope ORIGIN (Einstein, obs#8); the largest catalog + first CCD all-sky survey (eROSITA, obs#12); delivered non-dispersive micro-calorimeter high-res spectroscopy (XRISM, obs#7); the first X-ray CCD imaging spectroscopy (ASCA, obs#14); hard-X-ray focusing (NuSTAR, obs#2). None are re-stamped.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12219_

8. **A continuation keeps predecessors' shared vocabulary and references them by name.**
   The X-ray-astronomy vocabulary (Wolter Type I, grazing-incidence, focusing optics, soft and hard X-ray, effective area, CCD, intracluster medium, galaxy cluster, X-ray binary, neutron star, black hole, AGN, accretion disk, supernova remnant, Seyfert galaxy, iron K-alpha line, keV, Giacconi and the 1962 founding) is correct and kept. Chandra through ASCA are referenced as the form complements. Only predecessor-specific hardware and anchors stay with their missions.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12220_

9. **The Suzaku key papers are the PASJ 59 (sp1) special-issue set plus George 2009.**
   Mitsuda et al. 2007 (mission, PASJ 59, S1, DOI 10.1093/pasj/59.sp1.S1); Serlemitsos et al. 2007 (XRT, PASJ 59, S9); Koyama et al. 2007 (XIS, PASJ 59, S23); Takahashi et al. 2007 (HXD design, PASJ 59, S35); Kokubun et al. 2007 (HXD in-orbit, lowest-background result, PASJ 59, S53); Kelley et al. 2007 (XRS, PASJ 59, S77); George et al. 2009 (cluster outskirts, MNRAS 395, 657). Context (not Suzaku results): Boella et al. 1997 (BeppoSAX broadband); Tanaka et al. 1995 (ASCA iron-line discovery).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12221_

10. **Clone source is ASCA (first X-ray CCD imaging spectroscopy, two SIS + two GIS, Mu-3SII, 1993); Suzaku is broadband-sensitivity (five XRT, XIS + HXD + XRS, M-V, 2005).**
   The most structural replacement in the rewrite is removing ALL "Advanced Satellite for Cosmology and Astrophysics," "ASTRO-D / Asuka," "Japan's 4th X-ray satellite," "20 February 1993 Mu-3SII (M-3SII-7)," "~420 kg," "~525/615/31/96 orbit," "two SIS (Solid-state Imaging Spectrometer) + two GIS (Gas Imaging Spectrometer)," "first X-ray CCDs / first X-ray CCD imaging spectroscopy," "first thin-foil mirrors on a free-flying satellite," "first resolved relativistic iron line in MCG−6−30−15," "SN 1006 synchrotron," "14 July 2000 attitude loss / 2 March 2001 reentry," "American Kestrel + Western Sword Fern," and ASCA's four mission-specific anchors language, and replacing with Suzaku's five GSFC-led XRT, the XIS / HXD / XRS instruments, the M-V / Uchinoura launch, the near-circular low Earth…
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12222_

11. **The shader renders procedural geometry rather than archived observation data.**
   The broadband-spectroscopy shader uses analytic curves and procedural noise rather than loading an actual Suzaku XIS spectrum or HXD continuum. A future revision could render a real simultaneous XIS+HXD broadband spectrum (publicly available from the Suzaku archive at HEASARC / DARTS) for a higher-fidelity view.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12223_

12. **The Suzaku diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the spacecraft with the five co-aligned thin-foil XRT telescopes, the XIS / HXD / XRS focal-plane assemblies, and the solar arrays.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12224_

13. **The simultaneous two-band view is described but not computed.**
   The soft XIS image-plus-spectrum co-pointed with the hard HXD continuum is described but not rendered as a live two-panel broadband figure; a future ship could integrate an archived XIS+HXD joint spectral fit into the shader's two-band mode for a historically accurate view.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12225_

14. **The micro-calorimeter thermal pulse is described rather than demonstrated.**
   The XRS pixel registering the heat pulse of a single X-ray photon at ~6 eV resolution and 60 mK is cited but not demonstrated as an interactive pulse-height figure; a future ship could render the micro-calorimeter thermometer response as a shader or interactive plot.
