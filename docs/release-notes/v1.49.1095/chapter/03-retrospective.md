## Surprises

- **LADEE read the Moon's almost-nothing exosphere atom by atom.** The Moon's surface-boundary exosphere is a near-vacuum envelope of individual atoms rather than a true atmosphere; LADEE's Neutral Mass Spectrometer measured its composition and variability, finding helium, neon, and argon most abundant — and neon, long predicted, became the novel discovery that NASA press-released.
- **The dust around the Moon is a permanent, asymmetric cloud of meteoroid-impact ejecta.** The Lunar Dust Experiment found a permanent, dawn-enhanced, asymmetric cloud of fine dust around the Moon, produced not by electrostatic lofting but by ballistic ejecta thrown up by high-speed cometary meteoroid impacts — and it set stringent upper limits that argue against the lofted-dust explanation for the decades-old lunar horizon glow.
- **LADEE pulsed laser light across lunar distance and closed a two-way link at a record rate.** The Lunar Laser Communication Demonstration sent laser pulses across the dark between the Moon and Earth and achieved the first two-way laser communication from lunar orbit, with a record downlink of about 622 Mbps and a 20 Mbps error-free uplink — a first at lunar distance.
- **A night-flying moth that finds its mate by a few molecules on the thin air.** The Ceanothus Silkmoth (*Hyalophora euryalus*), a large saturniid of Pacific Northwest woodlands whose non-feeding adult lives only a week or two, finds a female across great distance in the dark when the male detects a few molecules of her pheromone carried on the thin night air with large feathered antennae, navigating to the faintest trace — a mirror of LADEE sensing the faintest traces in the thinnest medium (behavioral / ecological description only).
- **A lichen that draws its whole life from the thin air and the passing fog.** The Horsehair Lichen (*Bryoria fremontii*), a dark-brown filamentous lichen that hangs in fine threads from PNW conifer branches, is a fungus-and-alga symbiont with no roots that draws all its moisture and nourishment directly from the thin air and the fog, living on the bare surface of the branch (ecological description only) — bound with the throughline of drawing the faintest trace from the thinnest air.
