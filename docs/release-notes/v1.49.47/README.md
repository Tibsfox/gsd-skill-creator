## Summary

The 45th Research project — WCN (Westercon), the West Coast Science Fantasy Conference, running since 1948. The granddaddy of western US SF conventions and the traveling institution that taught an entire region how to build fandom communities. Seven modules cover 77 years: from LASFS founding through the traveling convention model, governance, the conventions it spawned, and the current decline-and-retirement debate.

WCN pairs with NWC (Norwescon) to tell the complete West Coast convention story. If Norwescon is the fixed server, Westercon is the traveling packet — carrying fandom culture from city to city, adapting to each host, maintaining protocol across decades. Together they document both architectures of community infrastructure: permanent and distributed.

Named "West of the Rockies" — the geographic boundary that defines Westercon's territory and identity since 1948.

**Research series: 45 complete projects, 423 research modules, ~196,000 lines.**

## Retrospective

### What Worked

1. **The NWC→WCN pair is architecturally clean.** Fixed node (Norwescon, 48 years at the DoubleTree) and traveling signal (Westercon, different city each year since 1948). Two conventions, two models, one community. The pair maps directly to distributed systems concepts (CMH) — centralized vs. decentralized infrastructure.

2. **Module 05 "The Question of When to Stop" is the most honest module in the series.** Documenting an institution's potential retirement requires respect for the people who built it and honesty about the challenges it faces. The module handles both without advocating either continuation or retirement.

### Lessons Learned

1. **Institutional lifecycles are research subjects.** WCN's 77-year arc (founding → growth → maturity → decline → retirement debate) is the same lifecycle every long-running project faces. The conventions sub-cluster (NWC + WCN) now documents both the thriving case and the declining case. Both are informative.
