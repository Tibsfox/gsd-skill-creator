- **The Southern Resident Killer Whale (Orcinus orca) Matriarchal Social Structure Organizes the Hunt.** The Southern Resident Killer Whale population — J, K, and L pods, ESA endangered 2005, resident to the Salish Sea (Puget Sound, Strait of Juan de Fuca, San Juan Islands) — is organized matrilineally, with pods structured around elder females and the matriline as the primary social bond. - **Post-Reproductive Female Longevity Is an Adaptation in Killer Whales, Not a Biological Artifact.** In most mammalian species, post-reproductive survival has no obvious fitness function; the individual continues to use resources without contributing reproductively. In killer whales, the calculation is different. The knowledge the matriarch carries increases pod survival during low-salmon years, and the increased survival is direct fitness because the pod members are the matriarch's descendants and relatives. Post-reproductive female longevity is therefore an adaptation in killer whales, not a biological artifact, and the recognition reframes the entire matriarch role.
