1. **The origin-point calibration is a stable E=10 route that the engine can apply consistently.**
   Bikini Kill at riot grrrl origin-point (degree 357) follows the same E=10 calibration logic as Solger at Seattle first-generation hardcore origin (degree 355) and earlier entries that occupy origin-point status. 2. **Political formations within a musical culture are analytically distinct from genres and must be described accordingly.**
   Riot grrrl is not a genre modification of punk; it is a political formation whose musical expression is a consequence rather than a cause. Describing it as a genre misses the specificity of what Bikini Kill was doing and understates the movement's structural effect on punk culture. Future degrees that document political formations within music traditions must use the political-formation frame consistently rather than defaulting to genre categorization.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #9790_

3. **Zines and textual infrastructure can precede musical output and must be chronologically preserved in the narrative.**
   The Bikini Kill zine was named before the band; Tobi Vail's *Jigsaw* co-founded the movement in parallel with it; the *Girl Power* pamphlet distributed with Huggy Bear preceded much of the music. The engine's narrative of the movement preserves the zines-first chronology because reversing it would misrepresent both the causal and the political structure of what the movement was. Textual-first political formations require textual-first documentation.
