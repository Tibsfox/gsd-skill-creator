## Lessons Learned

1. **The ethereal layering opens a new textural dimension in the engine's acoustic taxonomy.** Studio-crafted vocal layering as distinct from live looping (72), communal harmony (53), and unprocessed single voice (76). The distinction between studio and live methods is analytically productive -- both arrive at similar atmospheric results through different means, and the method IS the meaning. Mree's ethereal layering and Briana Marela's layered loop are mirror images of the same underlying impulse: the voice multiplied. The engine now distinguishes them taxonomically.
2. **The YouTube-to-artist pipeline is a genuinely new artist-city pattern.** The engine's first digital-first career pathway. The generational shift from physical venues to digital platforms changes not just how artists are discovered but how they develop artistic voice. Cover videos as apprenticeship. Reinterpretation as self-discovery. The platform shapes the art. The artist-city taxonomy is now wide enough to admit careers built when the city is partly virtual.
3. **The 30% gender threshold is ONE ARTIST AWAY.** The gender ratio at 23:55 (~29.5%) places the threshold one degree ahead. The next non-male artist will cross this milestone. Track at degree 78. The engine's gender trajectory has been a slow arc -- the threshold crossing will be a marker worth celebrating but not a finish line.
4. **The Asian-American thread is real but underrepresented.** Chong the Nomad (44, Filipino-American) and Mree (77, Taiwanese-American) are 33 degrees apart. The gap is large. Seattle's Asian-American music community is far larger than the engine has captured. The thread is nascent but now trackable, and degrees 78+ should look for opportunities to deepen it.
5. **The Sciuridae trio (75-76-77) establishes a three-consecutive-family template.** The trio structure -- simple (chirp), loud (whistle), complex (multi-note melody) -- provides a complete acoustic gradient within one genus. Volume peaks at the center (E=5 at 76). Complexity peaks at the end (multi-note at 77). The trio is the engine's most thorough treatment of any single genus to date and serves as the template for future genus completions.
6. **Endemic species are a new structural category.** The Olympic Marmot's geographic restriction (~500 km2 of total habitat) is qualitatively different from any prior species. The sky-island concept -- a population isolated on a mountaintop "island" surrounded by lowland "sea" -- is analytically rich and connects to evolutionary biology, conservation biology, and biogeography. Future endemic candidates should be tracked explicitly.
