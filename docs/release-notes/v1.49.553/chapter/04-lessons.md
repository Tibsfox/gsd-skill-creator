1. **First-orbit rendezvous achieved.**
   Conrad closed the distance to the Agena in one orbit -- 85 minutes, launch to docking. The M=1 profile Apollo would need for Lunar Module ascent rendezvous, proven operationally viable. The fastest rendezvous of the Gemini program. The technique that made lunar orbit rendezvous feasible without redesigning Apollo's consumables budget. Every subsequent LOR mission inherits this demonstration.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #2055_

2. **Highest crewed altitude: 1,374 km.**
   The Agena PPS burn boosted the docked stack to an apogee nearly twice the Gemini 10 record (763 km). More than three times the ISS orbit. The spacecraft passed through the inner Van Allen belt. Conrad could see from Africa to Australia in a single glance. The record stood for 26 months until Apollo 8 flew to the Moon in December 1968 and left Earth orbit entirely.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #2056_

3. **First tethered spaceflight generates first artificial gravity.**
   The 30-meter Dacron tether between Gemini 11 and the Agena, rotating at approximately one revolution per six minutes, produced 0.00015 g -- tiny but real, the first artificial gravity ever generated in space. Every subsequent tethered-spaceflight proposal (TSS-1, TSS-1R, space elevator concepts, rotating-habitat designs) traces back to this: 30 meters of Dacron rope and two spacecraft spinning slowly above the Earth.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #2057_

4. **First fully automatic reentry.**
   The onboard computer managed reentry without manual input, achieving 4.5 km accuracy -- the most precise Gemini landing, within visual range of the recovery carrier USS Guam. Validated the automated guidance approach Apollo would use for the more demanding return from lunar distance. Automated reentry became the default after this mission.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #2058_

5. **Dick Gordon's first flight -- Seattle native, UW class of 1951.**
   The deepest PNW thread in the engine's NASA track. Born in Seattle. Educated at UW with a chemistry degree. Bendix Trophy winner (1961, transcontinental speed record in an F-8U Crusader). Future Apollo 12 Command Module Pilot -- orbited the Moon alone in Yankee Clipper while Conrad and Bean walked. Assigned to command the cancelled Apollo 18. Never walked on the Moon.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #2059_

10. **Gemini program: 9 of 10 crewed flights complete.**
   One flight remains: Gemini 12 (Lovell/Aldrin, November 1966), where Buzz Aldrin will solve the EVA problem with underwater neutral-buoyancy training and the innovation of handholds and foot restraints everywhere. The program that proved every technique Apollo needed is approaching its final flight. 11. **The Dacron tether deserves deeper engineering treatment.**
   The 30-meter Dacron tether between Gemini 11 and the Agena is one of the most significant hardware firsts in the Gemini program, and its connection to later tethered-spaceflight proposals (space elevators, rotating habitats, TSS-1 and TSS-1R in the 1990s) is under-explored in this release. A future degree with a tether-relevant mission should treat the tether history as a dedicated thread.
