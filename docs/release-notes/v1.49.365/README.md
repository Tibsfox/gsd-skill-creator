## Summary

**Mad Season** arrives at E=6 -- the supergroup formed at the intersection of addiction, recovery, and creative desperation, bringing together **Layne Staley** (Alice in Chains, vocals), **Mike McCready** (Pearl Jam, guitar), **Barrett Martin** (Screaming Trees, drums), and John Baker Saunders (bass). The band formed in 1994 when McCready and Staley were both in treatment at the Hazelden Foundation in Minnesota -- a meeting that generated chemistry powerful enough to become music. *Above* (1995) was recorded and released on Columbia Records in a compressed window of time when Staley was in a period of relative stability, and the result is one of the most emotionally raw documents in the entire Seattle catalog: a record that sounds like four people who have seen the bottom, recorded at a moment when they were briefly back near the surface. At E=6, the band sits in the same register as several of their peers in this cluster -- present, committed, emotionally weighted -- but Mad Season carries a biographical gravity that distinguishes it from almost every other entry in the engine. Layne Staley would be largely reclusive after 1996 and died in 2002; *Above* is one of his last sustained recorded presences, and every listen carries that weight. The **Staley+McCready supergroup** designation matters structurally: degree 224 opens the **GRUNGE SUPERGROUP CLUSTER** -- three consecutive degrees (224-226) featuring supergroups and side projects that connect Alice in Chains, Pearl Jam, Soundgarden, Mother Love Bone, and Screaming Trees in a dense web of collaboration and mutual influence. Mad Season is the cluster's opening entry, and it is arguably the most emotionally significant. On the species side, the **Osprey** registers at E=5 and brings the **FIRST PANDIONIDAE** into the SPS engine -- a family with only one member worldwide. *Pandion haliaetus* is the only member of the family Pandionidae, a raptor so specialized and so distinct from all others that taxonomists placed it in its own family to avoid forcing it into the Accipitridae where it doesn't quite fit. The Osprey is the only raptor in North America that dives feet-first into water to catch fish, plunging from 30-130 feet above the surface and submerging completely; its reversible outer toe, barbed footpads, and closable nostrils are adaptations found in no other raptor. The *chewk-chewk-chewk* call is persistent and recognizable -- a rapid, repeated chirp delivered from nesting platforms and fishing perches that is one of the most distinctive sounds of the Puget Sound shoreline.

- **Part A -- S36:** Mad Season (Rock, E=6) -- Layne Staley + Mike McCready supergroup, Alice in Chains + Pearl Jam, *Above* (1995), Columbia Records
- **Part B -- SPS:** Osprey (chewk-chewk-chewk, E=5) -- FIRST PANDIONIDAE, sole member of its family, diving raptor, Puget Sound shoreline species
- GRUNGE SUPERGROUP CLUSTER OPENS (224-226) -- degree 224 begins three consecutive entries featuring supergroups and side projects: Mad Season (AIC+PJ+Screaming Trees), Temple of the Dog (Mother Love Bone tribute, Pearl Jam+Soundgarden), and Wellwater Conspiracy (Matt Cameron side project); the cluster documents grunge's collaborative ecology -- the way the scene's key figures moved between projects, forming and reforming combinations that produced music that no single band configuration could have made; this is the engine's most concentrated supergroup cluster
- Hazelden origin -- Mad Season formed from a chance meeting between Layne Staley and Mike McCready at the Hazelden Foundation, one of America's most well-known addiction treatment centers; the biographical context is inseparable from the music; *Above* is a record made by people in recovery, and the emotional content -- the urgency, the fragility, the gratitude -- is audible throughout; the record is not about addiction in a thematic or narrative sense, but it sounds like someone who has been close to not existing
- FIRST PANDIONIDAE -- the Osprey is the sole living member of the family Pandionidae, a classification that reflects its evolutionary distance from other raptors; while all other large raptors in North America belong to Accipitridae or Falconidae, the Osprey sits in its own family because its anatomical specializations -- the reversible outer toe, the barbed spicules on the footpads, the specialized nostril structure that closes during dives -- are unique; entering the engine through its own family, the Osprey is simultaneously part of the raptor arc and apart from it
- Osprey in Puget Sound -- the Osprey is one of the most consistently visible raptors on Puget Sound, nesting on channel markers, pier pilings, utility poles, and purpose-built nesting platforms throughout the Sound's inlets and bays; it is a migratory species, present from March through October and wintering in Central and South America; the *chewk-chewk-chewk* call is heard throughout the summer months from nesting pairs defending territories and communicating between mates; ferry passengers routinely see Osprey hovering over the Sound before plunge-diving
- Barrett Martin thread -- the Screaming Trees' drummer connects Mad Season to the third leg of grunge's founding triangle: Nirvana, Alice in Chains/Soundgarden, and Screaming Trees; Martin's presence also creates a link back to degree 219's Brad (Screaming Trees via Mark Pickerel) and degree 220's Truly (Screaming Trees via Pickerel again); the Screaming Trees have now appeared as a connective thread in multiple engine entries, establishing them as one of the scene's most underrecognized structural pillars
- Raptor arc position -- degree 224 is the fourth consecutive raptor entry; the arc has now documented Bald Eagle (contact, 221), Bald Eagle (territorial, 222), Golden Eagle (223), and Osprey (224); the next three entries will introduce the accipiter sub-arc with Northern Goshawk (225), Cooper's Hawk (226), and Sharp-shinned Hawk (227) before the Red-tailed Hawk closes the sequence at 228
