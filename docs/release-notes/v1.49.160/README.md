- **THE TANAGER THAT ISN'T A TANAGER -- Thraupidae to Cardinalidae, Molecular Phylogenetics Overwrites Morphology:** The Western Tanager was historically placed in Thraupidae (true tanagers). Molecular phylogenetic studies beginning in the 1990s moved *Piranga* out of Thraupidae and into Cardinalidae. DNA-DNA hybridization (Sibley and Ahlquist 1990), mitochondrial DNA (Klicka et al. 2007), multi-locus phylogenomics (Barker et al. 2013, 2015), and the AOS/AOU formal reclassification (2008) all confirm: *Piranga ludoviciana* is Cardinalidae. The common name "tanager" is retained by convention but is phylogenetically misleading. The Western Tanager is the second Cardinalidae in the engine (after the Black-headed Grosbeak at degree 23) -- two consecutive degrees, same family. The taxonomic reclassification lesson: names lag behind evidence, appearance does not equal relationship, molecular data can overturn a century of morphological classification. Science is a living process where conclusions update with evidence.

- **RHODOXANTHIN -- THE STOLEN PIGMENT, Honest Signaling Through Metabolic Constraint:** The male Western Tanager's red head is flame-orange to scarlet -- one of the most visually striking features of any PNW bird. The pigment responsible is rhodoxanthin, a rare ketocarotenoid the bird cannot synthesize. Most red plumage in birds comes from dietary yellow carotenoids enzymatically converted to red by the bird's own metabolism. Cardinals, House Finches, and Scarlet Tanagers all produce their red this way. The Western Tanager cannot. Rhodoxanthin is obtained directly from insect prey, specifically from hemipterans and possibly conifer-associated sources (yew arils, lichens). The red head is therefore an honest signal: a male with a bright red head has demonstrably found and consumed rhodoxanthin-rich prey, requiring effective foraging, quality territory, and sufficient body condition. The signal cannot be faked because the pigment cannot be manufactured. Dual-channel honest signaling: acoustic (song declares territory quality) and visual (plumage declares foraging quality). Key sources: Brush and Johnson (1976, *Auk*) first identified rhodoxanthin; Hudon (1991, *Canadian Journal of Zoology*) confirmed the bird cannot metabolically produce it; Hill (2002, *A Red Bird in a Brown Bag*) established the honest signaling framework.

- **THE HOARSE WARBLE -- Twenty-Fifth Acoustic Element (Part B Contribution), Timbral Distortion as Species Identity:** The Western Tanager's song is a series of hoarse, burry, slurred phrases in a rolling warble pattern. "Sounds like a robin with a sore throat." Fundamental frequency range ~1.5-4.5 kHz. 5-12 slurred phrases per bout, 2-4 phrases per second. The defining feature: additional spectral energy in the form of harmonic sidebands, frequency jitter, and amplitude irregularities that produce a rough, buzzy, burry quality. Spectrographically, wider frequency bands and less regular harmonic structure than either the grosbeak or the robin. This is the engine's first exploration of timbral distortion as acoustic identity. Every previous vocal species produced either pure tones (thrushes, warblers) or broadband noise (siskin, bushtit). The tanager produces a tone that is tonal but rough -- melodic content carried through a grainy medium. The audio equivalent of a photograph with visible film grain. The hoarseness IS the tanager. Remove it and you have a robin. Keep it and you have a species. The hoarseness may be an adaptation for acoustic robustness in conifer canopy: a broad-spectrum signal propagates more reliably through needle-dense canopy than a pure tone, because if some frequencies are attenuated by needle scatter, others persist.

- **CONIFER FOREST RETURN -- First Conifer Specialist Since Degree 9, Douglas Fir-Hemlock-Cedar Closed Canopy:** The Western Tanager breeds in Douglas fir, western hemlock, and western red cedar forest -- the cathedral forest that defines the PNW. It forages in the upper canopy at 15-30 m, sallying from branch tips to capture flying insects. The return to conifer forest after fifteen degrees (last at degree 9, Chestnut-backed Chickadee) fills a long gap. Conifer canopy acoustic properties shape the tanager's song: needle attenuation absorbs high frequencies above 4 kHz, favoring mid-frequency signals exactly where the tanager sings (2-4 kHz). Trunk reverberation softens sharp transients. Sound transmission is better vertical than horizontal. The hoarseness may actually improve detectability at ground level because the broad spectral distribution survives needle attenuation better than a pure tone.

- **LONG-DISTANCE NEOTROPICAL MIGRANT -- Up to 4,500 km One-Way, Farther Than the Grosbeak:** Wintering in Mexico through Central America to Costa Rica (occasionally Panama). Arrives in the PNW mid-May, later than the grosbeak (late April). Peak song late May through mid-July. One of the longest migration routes of any PNW songbird. Ten of 23 unique species in the engine are neotropical migrants -- 43%. The engine's summer chorus grows louder with each degree.

- **FOURTH CONSECUTIVE E=4 (Part B) -- Two Forms of Warble After Two Forms of Percussion:** Degree 21: metronomic vocal percussion (the hammer). Degree 22: broadband mechanical percussion (the drum). - **SECOND CARDINALIDAE IN ADJACENT DEGREES -- Tanager and Grosbeak as Kin:** Two consecutive Cardinalidae (degrees 23-24). The robin-like warble is a Cardinalidae family trait. Still 15 families in the engine, but Cardinalidae depth has doubled.

### The Part A/B Parallel: Long-Distance Migration, Rough Texture, Honest Signal

The degree 24 parallel operates on four axes with structural resonance between Part A and Part B:

1. **Island to forest -- the long migration.** Ron Artis II traveled from Hawaii to Seattle across the Pacific. The Western Tanager travels from Central America to the PNW, up to 4,500 km one-way. Both are long-distance migrants arriving from warm origins to inhabit the Pacific Northwest's summer soundscape. The tanager's migration is among the longest of any PNW songbird; Artis's journey crosses an ocean.

2. **Soul as texture, not formula.** Ron Artis II brings a raw, gritty, soulful quality -- not polished pop-R&B but something rougher, more honest, more textured. The Western Tanager sings a hoarse, rough warble -- not the smooth richness of the grosbeak or the clean brightness of the robin, but something grainy. Both find their identity in the rough edges. The hoarseness IS the signal.

3. **Energy 4 sustained through authenticity.** Fourth consecutive E=4. Neither needs studio processing or electronic amplification to be powerful. The raw vocal take. The live performance. The unprocessed signal. The honest surface.

4. **KEXP as conifer canopy.** KEXP is the broadcast platform through which Ron Artis II's music reaches Pacific Northwest audiences. The conifer canopy is the acoustic medium through which the tanager's song reaches forest listeners. Both are filtering media that shape the signal in transmission. What reaches the listener has been colored by the medium. The hoarseness in the canopy; the studio character on the airwaves.

### Retrospective

#### What Worked

- **Ron Artis II is well-documented through multiple high-quality sources.** SPIN interview (2024), Songwriters Hall of Fame profile, Grateful Web Maui fire coverage, KHON2 Hawaii, Ka Hui Ho'olauna family profile (2015, irreplaceable family context), Bread & Roses performer profile, multiple venue bios. The family story is unusually complete: all eleven siblings named, father's Thriller credits confirmed by multiple sources, mother's Lena Horne touring confirmed, Art...is venue confirmed by Yelp reviews and family profile.

- **The Motown lineage produced the richest structural finding.** The Thriller connection between degrees 0 and 24 is the engine's most concrete cross-reference -- literal album credits linking producer (Jones) and session player (Artis Sr.). This was not anticipated; it emerged from the research. The engine's circle has internal structure that reflects the real interconnections in the music industry.

- **The KEXP-as-label analysis fills a conceptual gap and extends the label taxonomy.** Four categories now documented (major, independent, self-released, media-institution proxy). The media-institution model is the purest amplifier -- zero contractual obligation, zero creative interference, merit-based and unilateral. The framework applies to all future label-field entries.

- **The Maui fire response documentation adds a dimension the engine has never captured.** Community service as artistic practice -- putting instruments in the hands of children who lost theirs. The instrument donation connects directly to the family's own origin story (Ron Artis Sr. and Victoria made sure every child had an instrument). Ron II replicates the model for the wider community. The engine now documents music's role as community infrastructure.

- **The Hawaii-to-Seattle pipeline analysis introduces cross-ocean cultural fusion.** The five-point framework (Hawaiian musical traditions, island jam culture, multi-ethnic fusion, Mick Fleetwood connection, Jack Johnson connection) provides structured analysis of what island culture brings to mainland soul music. New geographic category for the engine.

- **The rhodoxanthin story is genuinely unique biochemistry.** No previous species in the engine had a plumage pigment pathway this constrained. The honest signaling framework (Hill 2002) applies with particular force because the signaling molecule cannot be endogenously produced. Dual-channel honest signaling (acoustic + visual) is a new concept.

- **The convergence trio emerges naturally.** The engine did not force three robin-like warbles into adjacent degrees -- the species data dictated the placement. The grosbeak, tanager, and robin genuinely produce similar songs, and their proximity creates a natural cluster. The trio pattern was discovered, not designed.

- **Timbral distortion as acoustic identity enriches the engine's analytical vocabulary.** The distinction between the grosbeak's smooth warble and the tanager's hoarse warble demonstrates that timbre is an independent acoustic dimension. Same frequency range, same phrase architecture, different timbral character. The engine can now analyze timbre separately from melody, rhythm, and amplitude.

- **The taxonomic reclassification lesson (Thraupidae to Cardinalidae) models science as a living process.** The carry-forward context correctly flagged the taxonomy question. Verification against molecular evidence (Klicka et al. 2007, Barker et al. 2013, AOS/AOU 2008) confirms the reclassification. Names lag behind evidence. Appearance does not equal relationship.

### Lessons Learned

1. **The Motown dynasty is a new institutional pipeline category.** The engine's prior institutional categories were impersonal: schools (Garfield, Cornish, Berklee, UW), scenes (house shows, open mics), churches (worship training), autodidactic study (recordings). The dynasty pipeline is personal and permanent: the teacher is your parent, the ensemble is your siblings, the tradition is Motown. The family IS the institution. Lesson: track dynasty-mediated musical education at subsequent degrees. Distinguish between family musical backgrounds (common) and direct institutional lineage (rare).

2. **Literal cross-degree connections are a distinct category from thematic cross-references.** The Thriller circle (Jones at 0, Artis at 24) is verifiable through album credits. Every other cross-reference requires analytical inference. Lesson: track all literal connections (shared album credits, shared stages, producer-artist, teacher-student) as a separate category. They are facts, not interpretations.

3. **Media institutions can function as labels without being labels.** KEXP provides the same career services as a record label -- exposure, legitimacy, audience access, career amplification -- without any of the structural compromises (contracts, master ownership, creative direction). Lesson: the label field in the CSV accommodates non-traditional institutional relationships. Accept media-institution as a valid category.

4. **Community service as artistic practice expands what the engine documents.** The Maui fire instrument donation is not biography -- it is artistic practice. Putting instruments in the hands of children who lost theirs IS the music at its most fundamental level. Lesson: track community action as creative expression at subsequent degrees.

5. **Island origins bring fundamentally different cultural assumptions.** Hawaiian multi-instrumentalism as cultural default, kanikapila as communal practice, genre fluidity as norm, aloha as operating system. These are not mainland assumptions with tropical flavor -- they are a different cultural operating system entirely. Lesson: geographic origin is not just distance; it is cultural context.

6. **The convergence trio is richer than the planned convergence pair.** The carry-forward from degree 23 recommended avoiding robin-like species between degrees 23 and 25. The data overruled the recommendation: the Western Tanager IS robin-like, creating a trio rather than a pair. The trio structure (smooth, hoarse, clean) is analytically richer than a pair. Lesson: when the data contradicts the plan, the data wins.

7. **Timbral distortion adds an independent analytical dimension.** Same melody, different grain. The grosbeak and tanager prove that timbre can be analyzed separately from pitch, rhythm, and amplitude. Lesson: every future species comparison should include a timbral assessment.

8. **Taxonomic reclassification is itself a lesson.** The Western Tanager is a cardinal, not a tanager. The name persists by convention while the science has moved on. Lesson: common names are historical artifacts, not phylogenetic statements. Verify taxonomy at every degree.

9. **The second ADSR cycle accelerates faster than the first.** Groove reset (22) to testimony (23) to invocation (24) -- three degrees with rhythmic foundation, semantic content, and activation energy. The first cycle took 10+ degrees to build comparable complexity. Accumulated energy (four consecutive E=4) provides momentum the first cycle's E=1-2 degrees did not have.

10. **The engine's geographic model must now span oceans.** Continental United States is no longer sufficient. The Hawaii origin introduces Pacific Island as a geographic category, concurrent dual-geography (Island Export) as a trajectory type, and cross-ocean cultural fusion as an analytical framework. The model expands to match the data.
