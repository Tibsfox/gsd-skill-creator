The personnel overlaps are significant: Rusty Bryant (alto/tenor sax) across multiple albums; Bernard Purdie (drums, *No Way!*) -- one of the most recorded session drummers in American music, his "Purdie Shuffle" a foundational groove; Idris Muhammad (drums, *What It Is*) -- New Orleans second-line rhythms entering jazz-funk; Jimmy Lewis (bass) bridging jazz and R&B studio worlds. These are not arbitrary sidemen -- they are the rhythm section of the era, the same musicians who powered recordings by King Curtis, Aretha Franklin, Donny Hathaway, and Roberta Flack.

- **THE GRANT GREEN LINEAGE -- Soul-Jazz Guitar Vocabulary, Green-Jones-Benson Triangle.** Jones' guitar style places him in direct lineage with Grant Green (1935-1979), the Blue Note soul-jazz guitarist. Green's approach -- single-note lines, warm round tone, blues-inflected phrasing, rhythmic precision serving the groove rather than virtuosic display -- defined the soul-jazz guitar vocabulary. The Green-Jones-Benson triangle represents three primary paths: Green (Blue Note, purest expression, maximum swing), Jones (Prestige, funkiest expression, boogaloo dance-floor orientation), Benson (Prestige/CTI/Warner, most commercial expression, eventually crossing over to pop with "Breezin'" in 1976). All three connected through the Prestige/Blue Note ecosystem, sharing sidemen (Purdie, Muhammad, Lewis) and studios (Van Gelder's).

- **THE LIQUID BROOK -- THIRTIETH ACOUSTIC ELEMENT, Third Consecutive Liquid Element, Liquid Triad Complete.** The liquid brook is water that has found its bed. Not the ocean (too vast), not the waterfall (too dramatic), not the rain (too dispersed). A brook is water moving in a persistent, narrow channel with a characteristic sound: continuous, low-level, rhythmic murmur. Jones' soul-jazz guitar is structurally a brook -- continuous rhythmic flow, single-note lines following the groove's channel. The organ provides the streambed (harmonic foundation); the drums provide the gradient (rhythmic slope); the guitar is the water. The liquid triad: gurgle (unformed potential, The Dynamics' proto-fusion laboratory), twitter (rapid articulate song, Night Owls' crystallized supper-club repertoire), brook (continuous channeled flow, Jones' assembly-line soul-jazz production). Three states of the same substance, three degrees, three eras (1960s, 2010s, 1960s-1970s). The chronological inversion (the brook predates the twitter) is structurally correct -- Jones' Prestige recordings laid the grooves that soul and R&B bands would cover for decades.

- **GENRE RECURRENCE -- JAZZ/FUNK RETURNS FROM DEGREES 21-22, First Genre Return After Gap, Stage 11.** Jazz/Funk at degree 29 returns from degrees 21-22 (Polyrhythmics, Foxy Apollo) after a six-degree gap. This is the engine's first genre recurrence -- not a consecutive repeat (Soul/R&B at 27-28) but a return of a previously used genre label after an intervening gap. At degrees 21-22, Jazz/Funk described contemporary Seattle ensembles performing live original music. At degree 29, Jazz/Funk describes a 1960s-1970s recording artist who helped define the genre before it had a stable label. The genre label is the same, but the relationship has reversed: degrees 21-22 are the inheritors; degree 29 is the ancestor. Eleven genre stages now documented.

- **GROOVE-KINETIC E=4 -- NINTH CONSECUTIVE, Distribution 2:13:6:9, The Purest Functional Expression.** The groove-kinetic subtype is the purest expression of E=4 yet documented. Every prior E=4 degree generates energy through some combination of ensemble power, vocal intensity, or collective momentum. Jones generates energy through the groove itself -- the locked-in, repetitive, dance-floor-oriented rhythmic pattern that the boogaloo represents. The groove is not a means to energy; it IS energy. Nine E=4 subtypes: ensemble-kinetic (21), collective-kinetic (22), vocal-kinetic (23), dynastic-kinetic (24), congregational-kinetic (25), metamorphic-kinetic (26), incubator-kinetic (27), ambient-kinetic (28), groove-kinetic (29). Energy, sound, and career all follow the same structural pattern at degree 29: continuous, channeled, functional flow.

## Part B: Northern Rough-winged Swallow -- The Rough Buzzy Brrit

Full deep research covering the Northern Rough-winged Swallow as the liquid pattern breaker, the swallow quartet's dissenting fourth voice, the third Hirundinidae genus, the first below-surface nester, the unsolved serrated primary mystery, and the ninth consecutive E=4:

- **THE LIQUID PATTERN SHATTERS -- First Non-Liquid Swallow Sound, Family Timbral Space Revealed as Two-Dimensional.** The swallow trio at degrees 26-28 established a liquid trilogy -- stream (legato), rain (staccato), brook (mixed). Three liquid sounds in three degrees. The trilogy was complete, satisfying, structurally elegant. Degree 29 shatters it. The Rough-winged Swallow produces a rough buzzy brrit -- a harsh, dry, noisy vocalization with no liquid quality whatsoever. The sound is noise-dominated, not tone-dominated. Broadband buzz, not frequency-modulated sweep. Sandpaper, not water. The family contains a second timbral axis that the liquid trilogy did not explore. The acoustic space of PNW Hirundinidae is two-dimensional (liquid/rough x articulation), not one-dimensional. The liquid trilogy at degrees 26-28 filled three cells in the tonal/musical column. The Rough-winged Swallow occupies the first cell in the noisy/buzzy column -- and does so at the most compressed temporal scale (single burst, 0.2-0.5 seconds). Fundamental frequency 2.0-6.0 kHz, peak energy 3.0-5.0 kHz -- the most constrained frequency range of the four swallows, compressed and focused rather than expansive. The sound has no clear pitch center -- more noise than tone. In signal processing terms: the liquid swallows have high spectral purity (energy in harmonically related partials); the Rough-winged has low spectral purity (energy spread across non-harmonic frequencies, creating noise). The first noise-dominated acoustic element since the Pine Siskin's ascending sawtooth at degree 12. Thirteen degrees of tonal-to-liquid sound (16-28) scraped clean by one buzzy brrit.

- **THE SWALLOW QUARTET -- From Trio to Democratic Tension, Three Consonant Voices Plus One Dissonant Voice.** The trio was thesis-antithesis-synthesis -- a Hegelian resolution. The quartet is three liquid voices plus one dissenting voice. The structure shifts from resolution to friction. In a vocal quartet, one dissonant voice against three consonant voices creates tension. The three liquid swallows agree on timbre. The Rough-winged disagrees. The majority says "water." The dissenter says "sandpaper." The expansion from trio to quartet changes the structural grammar. The quartet has internal conflict the trio lacked. On every axis, the Rough-winged is the outsider: non-liquid (liquid gurgle/twitter/twittering gurgle vs. rough buzzy brrit), non-iridescent (iridescent purple/green-violet/blue-green vs. drab brown), non-musical (musical vs. non-musical), understudied (model organism/moderate/moderate vs. low research profile), unknown to the public (moderate/low/high vs. very low recognition). The un-swallow. The wallflower. Present at every gathering, overlooked at every one. The engine gives it a full degree because the overlooked member teaches more about the family's range than any additional study of the charismatic members.

- **FOUR CONSECUTIVE HIRUNDINIDAE -- Densest Family Run in Engine History, 100% Density.** Four species in four consecutive degrees with zero gaps. At degree 30 (Barn Swallow), the run extends to five. Comparison: warbler quintet spans seven degrees with a gap (71% density), thrush quintet spans twenty-four degrees with four gaps (21% density). The swallow run at 100% density is the most efficiently represented family by a wide margin. Sixteen families in the engine. Hirundinidae at four species now trails only Turdidae (5) and Parulidae (5). At degree 30, all three families will be tied at five.

- **THE THIRD SWALLOW GENUS -- *Stelgidopteryx*, Scraper-Wing, Naming Evolution from Mythology to Morphology.** *Stelgidopteryx* from Greek *stelgis* (scraper) + *pteryx* (wing). *Serripennis* from Latin *serra* (saw) + *penna* (feather). The full binomial: "the saw-feathered scraper-wing." Both genus and species names refer to the same anatomical feature -- the tiny hook-like serrations on the outer edge of the outermost primary feather. The common name "Rough-winged" also refers to these serrations. The entire nomenclature is built on one feature. The three genera trace a naming evolution: *Progne* (myth -- transformed into a swallow, says nothing about the wing), *Tachycineta* (behavior -- "swift-mover," describes how the wing moves), *Stelgidopteryx* (morphology -- "scraper-wing," describes the wing itself). Increasingly literal descriptions. The naming tradition became more empirical as ornithological science matured.

- **THE SERRATED PRIMARY MYSTERY -- Named Three Times, Understood Zero, Nearly Two Hundred Years Without an Answer.** The serrations on the outermost primary feather give the species its common name, its species epithet, and its genus name. The feature is: present in both sexes (not secondary sexual), present year-round (not seasonal), more pronounced in adults (age-dependent development), and located on the leading-edge feather that cuts the air during flight (aerodynamically significant position). Six hypotheses: (1) aerodynamic -- serrations modify airflow, analogy to owl feather serrations enabling silent flight, but no experimental confirmation; (2) sound production -- serrations vibrate during flight to produce the buzzy call, but vocalization appears to originate from the syrinx; (3) mate assessment -- roughness as age signal, but requires close contact not documented during courtship; (4) parasite reduction -- serrations dislodge feather mites, but other swallows have the same parasites without evolving serrations; (5) vestigial -- remnant of ancestral trait; (6) multiple minor functions simultaneously. The honest answer: we don't know. Every previous species name in the engine has been explicable -- "two-colored" (visible plumage), "swift-mover" (observable behavior), "golden-crowned" (identifiable marking). "Saw-feathered scraper-wing" names something we can see and touch but cannot explain. The engine's epistemological lesson: naming is not understanding. Description is not explanation. You can observe a feature for two hundred years and still not know what it does.

- **THE FIRST BURROWING NESTER -- Below-Surface Ecology, Erosion-Dependent Habitat, Three Nesting Substrates.** The Rough-winged Swallow nests in burrows excavated into vertical or near-vertical earth banks -- river cutbanks, road cuts, gravel pits, quarries. Burrow entrance 4-5 cm diameter, 30-60 cm deep, at heights of 1-5 meters above the waterline. Also uses drainage holes, expansion joints, and crevices in bridges and culverts; appropriates burrows from Belted Kingfishers and Bank Swallows. Solitary to loosely aggregated, never colonial (unlike the Bank Swallow, which nests in dense colonies). The engine's cavity economy now spans three substrates: wood excavation (Northern Flicker, degree 21-22), wood reuse (Tree Swallow, degree 28), earth excavation (Rough-winged Swallow, degree 29). The critical distinction: the Rough-winged Swallow depends on erosion for habitat. Actively eroding river banks create the nesting substrate. Most species depend on stability, not destruction. The river bank is negative space -- habitat created by the removal of material, not the addition of it. The first ecosystem in the engine that requires geomorphic disturbance. The first species whose housing depends on things falling apart.

- **THE UN-SWALLOW -- Drab, Quiet, Understudied, Overlooked, Present, Successful.** The Rough-winged Swallow is common but unknown, present but unnoticed, successful but understudied. It is the family's forgotten member. The martin has conservation concern (charismatic). The Tree Swallow has science (model organism). The Violet-green has beauty (iridescent gem). The Rough-winged has brown feathers and a mystery on its wing. Drab brown above, dingy white below, the color of the bank it lives in. No iridescence, no visual drama, no bold field marks. The least musical swallow in the engine -- the brrit is a contact call and mild alarm, not a song in the territorial/advertising sense. The engine, by giving it a full degree, honors the overlooked. Not every species can be the charismatic one. The plain, hardworking ones that nobody notices deserve their moment.

- **ROUGHNESS -- THE ARC WORD FOR DEGREE 29, From Water to Sandpaper, From Flow to Friction.** The circle has moved from flexibility (degree 28) to roughness (degree 29). The word captures the species' defining quality on every axis: rough sound (buzzy brrit), rough wing (serrated primary), rough habitat (eroding earth bank), rough plumage (drab brown, no polish). Nothing about this species is smooth. Nothing about it flows. It scrapes. It buzzes. It digs into dirt. It wears brown in a family of iridescent jewels. The opposite of the liquid polish that defined degrees 26-28. From flow to friction. From the aesthetic to the functional. The Rough-winged Swallow does not care about beauty. It cares about the burrow.

- **NINTH CONSECUTIVE E=4 (Part B) -- Energy Nonet, Dynamic Collapse Deepens, Must Break.** Nine degrees, nine acoustic textures, same energy level. The plateau survived the timbral shift from liquid to rough, proving it is independent of content. The engine has been at the same dynamic level for a quarter of all degrees. The energy is delivered through persistent, repeated brrit calls over foraging bouts rather than raw volume -- steady acoustic presence despite low individual amplitude. The dynamic range has collapsed. Nine is unsustainable. The break at degree 30 or 31 will be the most impactful dynamic event in the engine's history.

- **SEVEN CONSECUTIVE PASSERIFORMES (Degrees 23-29) -- Order Dominance Worsens.** The last non-passerine was the Northern Flicker (Piciformes) at degrees 21-22. Seven consecutive Passeriformes. The engine needs non-Passeriformes urgently after the swallow run closes.

- **CRITICAL GAPS PERSIST -- No Marine Species (29 Degrees), No Corvid (29 Degrees), No Raptor (29 Degrees), No Nocturnal Species Since Degree 4.** The project is called "The Sound of Puget Sound." Twenty-nine degrees without a species from the Sound itself. No gull, no grebe, no loon, no guillemot. No Steller's Jay, American Crow, or Common Raven. No Bald Eagle, Red-tailed Hawk, or Osprey. No nocturnal species since the Spotted Owl twenty-five degrees ago. The swallow quintet closes at degree 30. After that, the engine must turn to the water.

## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **The geographic transparency standard is the right approach.** Documenting the Seattle discrepancy honestly rather than fabricating a connection produced the engine's most important integrity finding. The Catalog pattern emerged directly from the gap -- a new way of understanding how recorded music creates presence independent of physical location. The transparency standard (established at degree 20) provided the framework. Lesson: when the data contradicts the assignment, document the contradiction. The finding is in the gap.

- **The Prestige Records research is rich and well-documented.** AllMusic, Discogs, and jazz discography databases provide complete session details for all seven Jones leader dates. The assembly-line nature of Prestige's production means the documentation is systematic. Personnel overlaps (Purdie, Muhammad, Lewis, Bryant) connect Jones to the broader R&B and soul recording infrastructure. These are not obscure sidemen -- they are among the most documented session musicians in American music.

- **The boogaloo-as-functional-naming finding is self-evident and well-sourced.** The name literally contains the word "boogaloo." The cultural context for the dance craze is available through Latin music scholarship. The connection between dance craze and performing name requires no speculation -- it is declared by the name itself.

- **The label halo effect is structurally significant and verifiable.** Browse Prestige-related content on any streaming platform: Jones is algorithmically adjacent to Davis, Coltrane, Monk, Rollins. The halo is not theoretical -- it is the current state of how streaming platforms organize catalogs.

- **The Grant Green / George Benson lineage provides reliable sonic context.** Even without listening to Jones' recordings, the lineage placement (between Green's purity and Benson's commercialism) describes the guitar style accurately.

- **The liquid pattern break is the right structural move.** Extending the liquid pattern to a fourth degree would have been gratuitous -- the trilogy was complete. The Rough-winged Swallow's non-liquid sound proves the engine knows when a timbral exploration is finished and can introduce a new axis within the same family.

- **The quartet structure is richer than the trio.** The trio was satisfying but closed. The quartet reopens the family and introduces internal conflict. The dissenter adds a dimension. The engine gained more from the fourth swallow than it lost in trio elegance.

- **The unsolved mystery is intellectually honest.** Most entries explain what is known. The Rough-winged Swallow entry is honest about what is NOT known. The serrated primary is named three times at three taxonomic levels, and the function is still unexplained. Science has gaps. The engine reflects them.

- **The burrowing ecology is genuinely novel.** Below-surface nesting has not appeared in the engine. The river bank as habitat -- created by erosion, maintained by disturbance -- introduces a new ecological dynamic. The engine's ecological vocabulary expanded.

- **The Part A/B parallel on four axes is clean.** Rough texture, unvarnished, overlooked, sustained energy. Both halves share the same structural thesis: roughness as functional quality, not defect.

## Lessons Learned

- **Geographic discrepancy is a structural finding, not a research failure.** The engine's first honest "we don't know if this artist belongs here" produced the Catalog pattern -- a genuinely new category in the taxonomy. The discrepancy forced the research to consider how recorded music creates presence independent of physical location. Intellectual honesty yields better theory than fabricated connections. The finding is in the gap.

- **The label halo effect is a real phenomenon with research implications.** Prestige Records amplifies Jones' discoverability by placing him algorithmically adjacent to canonical masters. This is not speculation -- it is how streaming platforms currently organize content. Future engine degrees should track label significance as a discovery variable.

- **Functional naming is rare and structurally significant.** "Boogaloo Joe" is the only name in 30 degrees that describes what the music does to the listener's body. The rarity makes it worth tracking. If another functional name appears, the pattern gains weight.

- **Assembly-line recording models produce the largest catalogs with the least critical attention.** Seven Prestige albums in five years is high-volume, low-profile production. The budget-record market generated enormous catalogs that jazz scholarship has largely ignored. The engine documents what criticism overlooked.

- **The dissenter carries the most information.** The first three swallows confirmed each other: all liquid, all iridescent, all musical. The fourth contradicts all three: non-liquid, non-iridescent, non-musical. In information theory, the member that differs from the group carries more information than the members that agree. When a family pattern feels complete, check whether you stopped because the exploration is genuinely exhausted or because the pattern is aesthetically pleasing. Aesthetic completion and actual completion are different things.

- **Naming is not understanding -- the serrated primary teaches epistemological humility.** A feature can be observed, described, classified, and named at three taxonomic levels without anyone knowing what it does. The engine should expect more unsolved mysteries. Science is not complete. Description should not be confused with explanation.

- **Roughness is a valid quality, not a defect.** Both halves of degree 29 are about unpolished, functional work. The boogaloo groove is rough because it prioritizes dance-floor momentum over harmonic sophistication. The Rough-winged Swallow is rough because it prioritizes burrow survival over aesthetic display. In both cases, roughness is not a failure to achieve polish -- it is a strategy that succeeds on its own terms.

- **The E=4 plateau at nine degrees is the engine's most urgent structural problem.** The variety within the plateau (nine different mechanisms, nine different subtypes) demonstrates that sustained energy does not require monotony. But the dynamic range has collapsed. A quarter of all degrees at one energy level. The break will define the next arc.

- **Erosion as habitat creation inverts the usual conservation logic.** Most conservation focuses on preserving stability -- old-growth trees, undisturbed wetlands, intact prairies. The Rough-winged Swallow needs active destruction -- eroding banks, collapsing road cuts, abandoned quarries. Conservation for this species means allowing disturbance, not preventing it. The engine's ecological vocabulary must include habitats maintained by removal, not just growth.

- **The organ trio is the minimum viable band for full-spectrum jazz.** Three musicians producing a complete rhythmic, harmonic, and melodic texture. The engine has documented organizational models from duo through large ensemble. The organ trio adds the baseline -- the smallest configuration that works without electronic augmentation.
