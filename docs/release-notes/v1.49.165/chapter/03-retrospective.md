## Lessons Learned

1. **Geographic discrepancy is a structural finding, not a research failure.** The engine's first honest "we don't know if this artist belongs here" produced the Catalog pattern -- a genuinely new category in the taxonomy. The discrepancy forced the research to consider how recorded music creates presence independent of physical location. Intellectual honesty yields better theory than fabricated connections. The finding is in the gap.

2. **The label halo effect is a real phenomenon with research implications.** Prestige Records amplifies Jones' discoverability by placing him algorithmically adjacent to canonical masters. This is not speculation -- it is how streaming platforms currently organize content. Future engine degrees should track label significance as a discovery variable.

3. **Functional naming is rare and structurally significant.** "Boogaloo Joe" is the only name in 30 degrees that describes what the music does to the listener's body. The rarity makes it worth tracking. If another functional name appears, the pattern gains weight.

4. **Assembly-line recording models produce the largest catalogs with the least critical attention.** Seven Prestige albums in five years is high-volume, low-profile production. The budget-record market generated enormous catalogs that jazz scholarship has largely ignored. The engine documents what criticism overlooked.

5. **The dissenter carries the most information.** The first three swallows confirmed each other: all liquid, all iridescent, all musical. The fourth contradicts all three: non-liquid, non-iridescent, non-musical. In information theory, the member that differs from the group carries more information than the members that agree. Lesson: when a family pattern feels complete, check whether you stopped because the exploration is genuinely exhausted or because the pattern is aesthetically pleasing. Aesthetic completion and actual completion are different things.

6. **Naming is not understanding -- the serrated primary teaches epistemological humility.** A feature can be observed, described, classified, and named at three taxonomic levels without anyone knowing what it does. The engine should expect more unsolved mysteries. Science is not complete. Description should not be confused with explanation.

7. **Roughness is a valid quality, not a defect.** Both halves of degree 29 are about unpolished, functional work. The boogaloo groove is rough because it prioritizes dance-floor momentum over harmonic sophistication. The Rough-winged Swallow is rough because it prioritizes burrow survival over aesthetic display. In both cases, roughness is not a failure to achieve polish -- it is a strategy that succeeds on its own terms.

8. **The E=4 plateau at nine degrees is the engine's most urgent structural problem.** The variety within the plateau (nine different mechanisms, nine different subtypes) demonstrates that sustained energy does not require monotony. But the dynamic range has collapsed. A quarter of all degrees at one energy level. The break will define the next arc.

9. **Erosion as habitat creation inverts the usual conservation logic.** Most conservation focuses on preserving stability -- old-growth trees, undisturbed wetlands, intact prairies. The Rough-winged Swallow needs active destruction -- eroding banks, collapsing road cuts, abandoned quarries. Conservation for this species means allowing disturbance, not preventing it. The engine's ecological vocabulary must include habitats maintained by removal, not just growth.

10. **The organ trio is the minimum viable band for full-spectrum jazz.** Three musicians producing a complete rhythmic, harmonic, and melodic texture. The engine has documented organizational models from duo through large ensemble. The organ trio adds the baseline -- the smallest configuration that works without electronic augmentation.
