## Summary

**206 Zulu** is the **first collective entry** in the Seattle 360 -- not a band, not a solo artist, but a collective dedicated to the four elements of hip-hop: DJing, MCing, breaking, and graffiti. Named for Seattle's area code and the Zulu Nation tradition, active from 1997 to present, Central District rooted. The engine has cataloged solo artists, duos, bands, producers -- now it adds collectives. On the species side, the **Western Screech-Owl** delivers a **descending whinny trill** -- a bouncing-ball call that accelerates and drops in pitch. 8th Strigidae, continuing the owl run's quiet descent at E=3.

- **Species note:** The Western Screech-Owl (*Megascops kennicottii*) is a small, cavity-nesting owl of low-elevation woodlands throughout the PNW. Its primary vocalization is a **descending whinny trill** -- a series of short whistled notes that accelerate and drop in pitch, like a bouncing ball coming to rest. Also gives a double trill. Strictly nocturnal, relying on exceptional hearing and silent flight. Common in suburban areas with mature trees -- one of the owls most likely heard (but never seen) in Seattle neighborhoods.

A collective is a different organizational form -- a community institution preserving and transmitting cultural knowledge across generations.
