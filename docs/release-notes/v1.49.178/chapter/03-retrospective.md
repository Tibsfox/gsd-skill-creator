# Retrospective — v1.49.178

## Lessons Learned

1. **Gospel's arrival required a new connection pattern.** The circuit rider was necessary because gospel's geography operates through church networks rather than secular music industry infrastructure. The engine's prior 42 artist-city patterns all assumed secular connection modes. Gospel's institutional geography is real and mapable -- but invisible to the mainstream music industry's documentation systems. When the genre changes, the connection mode must change with it.

2. **The source enters last.** For forty-one degrees, the engine documented genres that descended from gospel without documenting gospel itself. This is structurally analogous to the Yellow-rumped Warbler's position: the generalist that appears after the specialists. The source tradition and the common ancestor both entered the engine at degree 42, and both entered after their derivatives had been mapped. The lesson: the foundation is often the last thing you document because it is so foundational you forget it needs documenting.

3. **The warbler quintet proved that family runs can tell narrative arcs.** The swallow quintet (26-30) was a family survey. The warbler quintet (38-42) is a story -- displacement, disturbance, generalist triumph. The engine now has two models for consecutive family runs: survey and narrative. Narrative is richer but requires more structural justification at each degree.

4. **Physiology is a valid structural dimension.** The wax-digestion adaptation is the engine's first metabolic innovation highlighted as a structural feature. Future degrees can document physiological traits alongside acoustic, ecological, and behavioral ones. The engine's vocabulary expands.

5. **The E=5 return confirms the sustained high-energy sector.** The one-degree E=4 recalibration at degree 41 was a pause, not a collapse. The blues/soul/R&B/gospel quadrant of the engine sustains E=5 as its baseline with occasional E=4 recalibrations. This is now a confirmed structural feature, not an anomaly.

The altar call and the shimmer. Both stayed. Both adapted. Both found a way to persist where others chose the easier crossing. The headwater and the generalist. The altar call and the shimmer. The source enters the record.
