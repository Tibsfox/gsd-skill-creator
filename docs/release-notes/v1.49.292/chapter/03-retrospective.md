The degree-152 release notes should have included a cross-reference clarification pointing forward to the degree-153 entry and noting the naming relationship explicitly, and future uplift should reconcile the 292–293 naming presentation across both readmes.
- **The Mass Line Media independent-label context could have been documented more specifically.** Mass Line Media as the Blue Scholars co-founded independent label that houses the duo's releases and Geo's solo work was mentioned but not developed in depth. A future uplift could document Mass Line Media's roster, release history, and role in Seattle hip-hop infrastructure more specifically, and the label's relationship to the broader independent-label tier (Conception Records, 206 Zulu) could be mapped more explicitly.
- **The urban ecology literature context for the Rock Pigeon could have been expanded beyond the taxonomic and behavioral sources.** The research cited Johnston & Janiga 1995 *Feral Pigeons* and Goodwin 1983 *Pigeons and Doves of the World*, but the urban-ecology literature on Rock Pigeon flock dynamics, human-food-dependence metrics, and cliff-to-city pre-adaptation mechanisms could have been expanded. A future uplift could add references from the urban-ecology research literature (e.g., Johnston 1985, Soldatini et al. 2006, Sacchi et al. 2002) to deepen the urban-synanthropic documentation.
- **The dual-function wing-clap courtship-and-alarm documentation could have been supported with primary research references.** The wing-clap dual-function framing (courtship display and predator alarm) was stated but not grounded in specific primary research references. The engine's Mourning Dove wing-whistle documentation at degree 150 cited Coleman (2008) and Hingee & Magrath (2009) for the alarm-signal function; the Rock Pigeon wing-clap documentation did not cite comparable primary research. A future uplift could add primary references on the wing-clap's dual-function and anchor the interpretation at the same level of research support as the Mourning Dove wing-whistle.

## Lessons Learned

1. **The solo-of-duo-member bridging position documents what the duo entry cannot.** Prometheus Brown's solo entry at degree 152 documents the individual MC's voice, identity, and artistic vision separated from the Blue Scholars duo production partnership. The bridging position is structurally informative because it preserves the individual artistic identity that the duo format alone cannot fully express, and the engine uses solo-of-duo-member placements to extend collective-subject documentation across multiple degrees rather than compressing all documentation into the duo's original entry. Future collective-subject arcs should consider the solo-of-member bridging pattern as a deliberate documentary device.
2. **Name multiplicity is identity content, not naming variation.** The three-name identity (George Quibuyen / Geologic / Prometheus Brown) is itself Filipino-American identity content — multiple names activated in different contexts reflects the complexity of bilingual / bicultural identity. The engine preserves the three-name multiplicity as legitimate register variation rather than collapsing it to a single canonical name, and the approach extends to other artists whose identity includes birth names, stage names, cultural names, and persona names operating in different dimensions.
3. **Family quartet documentation at four consecutive degrees enables settlement-pattern structural completeness.** The 149–152 Columbidae quartet's native-native-invasive-feral structure documents the family at structural depth that no single-degree entry could capture. Four consecutive family-degree documentation is a deliberate choice that supports settlement-pattern structural analysis, and the engine should consider similar multi-degree family arcs where the family's biological and ecological range supports it (e.g., the Phasianidae 153–156 quartet opening at degree 153).
4. **Mechanical sounds warrant a dedicated inventory register alongside vocal sounds.** The Goldeneye flight whistle (124), Mourning Dove wing whistle (150), and Rock Pigeon wing clap (152) together document three distinct non-vocal sound-production mechanisms. The three-mechanism inventory is itself a structural feature of the engine, and mechanical sounds should be tracked in a dedicated inventory register alongside the vocal-sound catalog. Future mechanical sounds (woodpecker drumming, grouse drumming, etc.) should be integrated into the inventory and documented with full mechanism specification.
5. **Cliff-to-city pre-adaptation explains urban-synanthropic success.** The Rock Pigeon's cliff-nesting ancestry pre-adapted the species for urban buildings whose ledges and cornices replicate the rocky cliff substrate. The pre-adaptation framing is a foundational principle in urban ecology and should be applied to other urban-synanthropic species entries where the pre-adaptation story is relevant (e.g., Peregrine Falcon urban nesting on skyscrapers, Chimney Swift urban roosting in chimneys).
6. **Political fire, performance intensity, identity navigation, and activism each generate legitimate E=5 energy.** Prometheus Brown's E=5 calibration draws on four distinct energy sources specific to solo political MC artistry: political fire (conviction and urgency), performance intensity (declarative delivery), identity navigation energy (creative tension of bilingual / bicultural identity), and activist energy (community organizing flowing into artistic practice). The four-source energy calibration should be applied to future political-MC entries and preserves E=5 as a structurally earned placement rather than a default hip-hop-era calibration.
7. **Back-to-back duo-member solo pairings build narrative tension toward reunion entries.** The engine's deliberate placement of Prometheus Brown (152) and Geo (153) as back-to-back solo entries immediately before the Blue Scholars reunion at 156 is a first-of-its-kind structural device that builds narrative tension toward the reunion. Future collective-subject reunion entries should consider the back-to-back member-solo pattern as a deliberate narrative device where the collective has multiple members whose individual identities can be documented separately.
8. **The feral-global human-synanthropic category closes taxonomic family arcs at the most human-dependent end.** The Rock Pigeon as feral-global human-synanthropic closes the Columbidae quartet at the family's most human-dependent end. Closing a family arc at the most human-dependent / most anthropogenic member is a structural principle that provides narrative closure and completeness, and future family arcs should consider whether the feral-global / human-synanthropic position is available for the closing entry (e.g., European Starling as feral-invasive for a starling family arc, House Sparrow for a passerid arc).
9. **The engine's taxonomic arcs benefit from intentional settlement-category structural planning.** The Columbidae quartet's native-native-invasive-feral structure at 149–152 was the engine's first intentional four-position settlement-pattern taxonomic arc. Intentional settlement-category planning (rather than degree-ordering by research convenience or availability) supports deeper structural analysis and should be applied to future family arcs where the settlement-pattern logic provides documentary value.
10. **The Filipino-American identity documentation at Seattle specificity anchors the community representation.** Prometheus Brown's Filipino-American identity documentation at degree 152 is grounded in Seattle-specific community context (Beacon Hill, CID, Manong generation labor history, 1898–1946 colonial legacy, Tagalog–English code-switching), and the Seattle specificity anchors the community representation against generic ethnic-identity framing. The Seattle-specific anchor preserves the engine's Pacific Northwest contextual discipline and honors the community's local particularity rather than flattening it into a generalized diaspora representation.
