The **global population** is estimated at **300 million or more** feral birds, with additional tens of millions of domesticated racing pigeons, fancy show pigeons, and utility pigeons kept by humans for racing sport, exhibition, and meat.

**The Wing Clap and the Third Mechanical Sound.** Beyond any vocal cooing, the Rock Pigeon's engine-defining acoustic is the **loud wing clap** — a sharp percussive slap produced when the **wings are clapped together above the body** during display flights and alarm takeoffs. The wing clap is generated not by the syrinx but by **feather-on-feather contact** — the physical collision of flight feathers above the body that transmits a crack audible across urban canyons and echoing off building facades and apartment-block brick. The sound falls in the **1000–2000 Hz** range with a duration of a few tens of milliseconds, and it carries surprisingly far for a non-amplified avian sound. The wing clap serves two distinct functions: **courtship display**, where males clap during ascending display flights to attract females and establish territorial boundaries above urban roofscapes, and **predator alarm**, where the explosive takeoff clap alerts the flock to danger and triggers rapid mass flight that confuses aerial predators like Peregrine Falcons and Cooper's Hawks. The wing clap stands as the engine's **third mechanical (non-vocal) acoustic event** following the **Common Goldeneye** flight whistle at degree 124 (modified inner primary feathers) and the **Mourning Dove** wing whistle at degree 150 (modified ninth primary feather), and it completes a three-mechanism inventory of non-vocal avian acoustic communication: **primary-feather whistle** in the Goldeneye, **modified-ninth-primary whistle** in the Mourning Dove, and **feather-on-feather clap** in the Rock Pigeon. Three distinct mechanisms, three distinct communicative functions, all within the engine's growing catalog of non-vocal avian sounds.

1. **The solo-of-duo-member bridging position documents what the duo entry cannot.** Prometheus Brown's solo entry at degree 152 documents the individual MC's voice, identity, and artistic vision separated from the Blue Scholars duo production partnership. The bridging position is structurally informative because it preserves the individual artistic identity that the duo format alone cannot fully express, and the engine uses solo-of-duo-member placements to extend collective-subject documentation across multiple degrees rather than compressing all documentation into the duo's original entry. Future collective-subject arcs should consider the solo-of-member bridging pattern as a deliberate documentary device.

The engine preserves the three-name multiplicity as legitimate register variation rather than collapsing it to a single canonical name, and the approach extends to other artists whose identity includes birth names, stage names, cultural names, and persona names operating in different dimensions.

Four consecutive family-degree documentation is a deliberate choice that supports settlement-pattern structural analysis, and the engine should consider similar multi-degree family arcs where the family's biological and ecological range supports it (e.g., the Phasianidae 153–156 quartet opening at degree 153).
4. **Mechanical sounds warrant a dedicated inventory register alongside vocal sounds.** The Goldeneye flight whistle (124), Mourning Dove wing whistle (150), and Rock Pigeon wing clap (152) together document three distinct non-vocal sound-production mechanisms. The three-mechanism inventory is itself a structural feature of the engine, and mechanical sounds should be tracked in a dedicated inventory register alongside the vocal-sound catalog. Future mechanical sounds (woodpecker drumming, grouse drumming, etc.) should be integrated into the inventory and documented with full mechanism specification.
5. **Cliff-to-city pre-adaptation explains urban-synanthropic success.** The Rock Pigeon's cliff-nesting ancestry pre-adapted the species for urban buildings whose ledges and cornices replicate the rocky cliff substrate. The pre-adaptation framing is a foundational principle in urban ecology and should be applied to other urban-synanthropic species entries where the pre-adaptation story is relevant (e.g., Peregrine Falcon urban nesting on skyscrapers, Chimney Swift urban roosting in chimneys).
6. **Political fire, performance intensity, identity navigation, and activism each generate legitimate E=5 energy.** Prometheus Brown's E=5 calibration draws on four distinct energy sources specific to solo political MC artistry: political fire (conviction and urgency), performance intensity (declarative delivery), identity navigation energy (creative tension of bilingual / bicultural identity), and activist energy (community organizing flowing into artistic practice). 7. **Back-to-back duo-member solo pairings build narrative tension toward reunion entries.** The engine's deliberate placement of Prometheus Brown (152) and Geo (153) as back-to-back solo entries immediately before the Blue Scholars reunion at 156 is a first-of-its-kind structural device that builds narrative tension toward the reunion. Future collective-subject reunion entries should consider the back-to-back member-solo pattern as a deliberate narrative device where the collective has multiple members whose individual identities can be documented separately.
8. **The feral-global human-synanthropic category closes taxonomic family arcs at the most human-dependent end.** The Rock Pigeon as feral-global human-synanthropic closes the Columbidae quartet at the family's most human-dependent end. Closing a family arc at the most human-dependent / most anthropogenic member is a structural principle that provides narrative closure and completeness, and future family arcs should consider whether the feral-global / human-synanthropic position is available for the closing entry (e.g., European Starling as feral-invasive for a starling family arc, House Sparrow for a passerid arc).
9. **The engine's taxonomic arcs benefit from intentional settlement-category structural planning.** The Columbidae quartet's native-native-invasive-feral structure at 149–152 was the engine's first intentional four-position settlement-pattern taxonomic arc. Intentional settlement-category planning (rather than degree-ordering by research convenience or availability) supports deeper structural analysis and should be applied to future family arcs where the settlement-pattern logic provides documentary value.
10. **The Filipino-American identity documentation at Seattle specificity anchors the community representation.** Prometheus Brown's Filipino-American identity documentation at degree 152 is grounded in Seattle-specific community context (Beacon Hill, CID, Manong generation labor history, 1898–1946 colonial legacy, Tagalog–English code-switching), and the Seattle specificity anchors the community representation against generic ethnic-identity framing. The Seattle-specific anchor preserves the engine's Pacific Northwest contextual discipline and honors the community's local particularity rather than flattening it into a generalized diaspora representation.
