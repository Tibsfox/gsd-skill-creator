## Taxonomic State

- **Family:** Columbidae (4th and final entry -- Band-tailed 149, Mourning 150, Collared 151, Rock Pigeon 152)
- **Order:** Columbiformes (quartet complete)
- **Class:** Aves
- **Species note:** The Rock Pigeon (*Columba livia*) is the most familiar bird on Earth -- domesticated approximately 5,000 years ago in the Middle East, it now inhabits every continent except Antarctica and thrives in every city on the planet. But the **wing clap** that earns its place in SPS is not a coo or call -- it is a **loud, sharp percussive slap** produced when the wings are clapped together above the body during display flights and alarm takeoffs. The sound carries surprisingly far, a crack that echoes off building walls in urban canyons. This is a **true mechanical sound**, produced by the physical collision of flight feathers, not by the syrinx. It serves as both courtship display (males clap during ascending display flights to attract females) and predator alarm (the explosive takeoff clap alerts the flock). As the engine's **3rd mechanical sound**, it joins the Common Nighthawk's diving wing boom (146) and the Mourning Dove's wing whistle (150) in demonstrating that birds communicate through structure as much as voice. The Rock Pigeon closes the **Columbidae quartet** -- four consecutive degrees spanning the pigeon and dove family from the native Band-tailed (149) through the iconic Mourning Dove (150), the invasive Collared-Dove (151), to the globally ubiquitous Rock Pigeon (152). One native, one beloved, one invader, one everywhere.
- **Prometheus Brown arc:** Born George Quibuyen, Prometheus Brown is a Filipino-American MC and one half of Blue Scholars, the duo that appeared at degree 99 -- over 50 degrees ago. Blue Scholars were one of the most important acts in Seattle hip-hop's mid-2000s renaissance, fusing Sabzi's jazz-inflected production with Prometheus Brown's politically charged, literary lyricism. His solo work extends that project's concerns -- immigration, identity, community organizing, the Filipino-American experience in the Pacific Northwest -- into a more introspective register. Seattle's Filipino-American community, centered in the Beacon Hill and South End neighborhoods, is one of the largest in the United States, and Prometheus Brown's music gives that community explicit representation in the engine.
- **Columbidae quartet summary:** 149 Band-tailed Pigeon (native, owl-like coo, mineral springs), 150 Mourning Dove (native, mournful coo + wing whistle, most hunted), 151 Eurasian Collared-Dove (invasive, hoo-HOO-hoo, fastest avian invasion), 152 Rock Pigeon (introduced, wing clap, most ubiquitous bird on Earth). Four species, four stories, spanning thousands of years of human-bird coexistence.
- **Mechanical sounds complete set:** 1. Common Nighthawk wing boom (146) -- air through primaries in dive, 2. Mourning Dove wing whistle (150) -- air through modified feathers on takeoff, 3. Rock Pigeon wing clap (152) -- feathers striking together above body. Three distinct mechanisms, three distinct communicative functions.
