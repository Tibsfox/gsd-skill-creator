## What Could Be Better

- **Criminal Nation's specific discography and release chronology was preserved at the genre-corridor level without track-and-album detail.** The documentation preserved the gangsta-rap corridor opening and the Genre Stage 101 framework but did not include specific release dates, album titles, track listings, producer credits, or label-of-record details for Criminal Nation; future Seattle hip-hop documentation could benefit from more complete discographic detail anchored to specific tracking sources (Genius, Discogs, Allmusic, regional press archives like *The Rocket* and *The Stranger*).
- **The regional Seattle gangsta-rap scene beyond Criminal Nation was mentioned but not enumerated.** The documentation noted that Seattle carried a gangsta-rap corridor distinct from the Nastymix party-rap register, but did not enumerate other regional gangsta-rap artists active in the same era (for example, **Rap Pages** coverage of Seattle artists, **Mix-a-Lot's Posse** members with harder material, Tacoma-scene artists); future Seattle hip-hop documentation should preserve the full regional gangsta-rap scene vocabulary where relevant.
- **The western DPS Steller Sea Lion decline causes were listed but not weighted by recent scientific consensus.** The documentation noted prey-base shifts, fisheries interactions, killer whale predation, and regime shifts as hypothesized causes of the ~80% decline but did not weight the hypotheses by recent scientific consensus (which has converged on prey-quality reductions and ecosystem regime shifts as primary, with predation and fisheries as secondary); future Mammalia documentation on declining populations should include consensus-weighting where available.
- **The Columbia River estuary Steller Sea Lion foraging on migrating salmon carries a conservation-conflict subtext that was not elaborated.** The documentation mentioned Steller Sea Lion foraging at the Columbia River estuary without elaborating the conservation-conflict context: Steller Sea Lions prey on ESA-listed salmon runs (Chinook, Steelhead) at the Bonneville Dam fish ladders, which has produced management conflict (limited lethal removal authorized by NOAA for specific documented-predation individuals) and illustrates the complex interplay of two ESA-listed taxa in the same ecosystem; future marine-mammal documentation should preserve such conservation-conflict contexts explicitly.
- **The Great Northern Expedition's broader historical context (Russian expansion, trans-Bering colonization, fur trade) was not fully elaborated.** The Steller 1741-1742 documentation was placed in the context of the Bering expedition and the Steller naming lineage but did not extend to the broader Russian imperial context (the expedition's fur-trade rationale, subsequent Russian colonization of Alaska 1784-1867, and the trans-Pacific fur-seal hunting that decimated the Northern Fur Seal and indirectly affected Steller Sea Lion populations); future pinniped documentation could preserve the fur-trade historical context where relevant.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Second-sub-category openings in rapid succession demonstrate that a new vocabulary can support multiple distinct corridors.** The gangsta-rap corridor opening at degree 182 two degrees after the halfway mark and the Otariidae family opening at degree 182 two degrees after the Phocidae opening at degree 180 both demonstrate that the engine's vocabulary-expansion capacity continues beyond the initial opening at a rotation landmark; the second-sub-category-opening principle generalizes to future vocabulary expansions where multiple corridors or sub-categories will be documented in rapid succession.
2. **Multi-corridor architecture is the correct documentation framework for regional scenes with diverse parallel voices.** Seattle hip-hop's multi-corridor architecture — party rap, conscious rap, experimental, independent-Grammy, noise-rap, gangsta rap — requires a parallel-corridor documentation framework rather than a sequential-genre narrative; the framework preserves the genuine stylistic diversity of regional scenes that national narratives often compress into single dominant voices, and the principle applies broadly to future regional-scene documentation (indie rock corridors, folk-revival corridors, electronic corridors).
3. **Chronological precedence over mainstream recognition is a first-class structural documentation event.** Criminal Nation's pre-1992-mainstream activity and Steller's 1742 description predating federal frameworks by ~230 years both illustrate the chronological-precedence principle: regional scenes and foundational naturalist observations carry precedence over the institutional frameworks that later recognized them; the principle supports future documentation of foundational regional activity that predates mainstream consolidation.
4. **The Distinct Population Segment (DPS) regulatory concept enables sub-species-level population-listing documentation.** The ESA's DPS mechanism allows conservation action to target specific genetically and geographically distinct populations even when the species as a whole remains less threatened; the DPS vocabulary enters the engine at degree 182 through the Steller Sea Lion and is available for future Mammalia entries with multi-DPS status, particularly the Orca (Southern Resident Killer Whale) and the salmon runs (multiple ESUs).
5. **Ceiling-breach intensity (E=7) is a recurring Q3 feature rather than a Quadrant II artifact.** The second E=7 entry in Quadrant III (Criminal Nation at degree 182, two degrees after Macklemore & Ryan Lewis at 181) confirms that the engine's maximum-intensity register is sustained in the second half of the rotation; future Quadrant III documentation should anticipate E=7 entries at similar rhythm and should avoid treating E=7 as a first-half exclusive register.
6. **Pinniped family taxonomy requires both Otariidae and Phocidae baselines for future entries.** The engine's pinniped vocabulary now supports both major families (Phocidae at 180, Otariidae at 182), and future pinniped entries (California Sea Lion, Northern Fur Seal, Northern Elephant Seal, possibly Guadalupe Fur Seal) can reference established family-level vocabulary; the three-degree window for establishing both families provides a template for future taxonomic-sub-category-pair openings.
7. **Size-and-intensity extremes are efficient opening-entry selections for new register categories.** The Steller Sea Lion's selection as the First Otariidae (largest sea lion species on Earth) and Criminal Nation's E=7 gangsta-rap selection both demonstrate that opening a new register category with a size-or-intensity extreme provides readers with an accessible anchor for the new vocabulary; future new-register openings should prefer extreme-anchor entries where available.
8. **Regional or regulatory frameworks at the degree-level connect the engine to national-or-international contexts.** Criminal Nation's West-Coast gangsta-rap national framework and the Steller Sea Lion's DPS-ESA-North-Pacific regulatory framework both illustrate that degree-level documentation opens onto larger contexts beyond the immediate Puget Sound geography; the framework-connection principle provides vocabulary for future documentation where regional-national or species-international frameworks are structurally significant.
9. **Naming lineages preserve multi-species taxonomic-history context efficiently.** The Georg Wilhelm Steller naming lineage — six Pacific-Rim species carrying his name from the 1741-1742 Great Northern Expedition — efficiently anchors multiple species to a single foundational natural-history episode; the lineage-based documentation supports future entries where a naturalist's name is carried across multiple species and provides historical depth without duplicating per-species biography.
10. **Q3's reshape work is sustained through rapid degree-to-degree structural events rather than stabilizing at the halfway mark.** The pattern of degrees 180, 181, and 182 (halfway mark, duo-plus-pup follow-through, first-gangsta-rap-plus-first-otariidae) demonstrates that Quadrant III is sustaining the reshape-for-the-second-half density rather than allowing the opening degrees to settle; the documentation framework should anticipate continued Q3 density with major structural events at most degrees rather than a return to steadier pace.
