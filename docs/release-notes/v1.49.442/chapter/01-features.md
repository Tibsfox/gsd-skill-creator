# Features — v1.49.442

## APEX PREDATOR ARC OPENS (301-308)


- the final eighth arc of the engine begins at degree 301 with the Roosevelt Elk bugle at E=8; the arc will run for eight consecutive degrees through the complete apex predator food web of the Pacific Northwest: elk (prey signal) → cougar → wolf → grizzly → black bear → sea lion → salmon → marten; the arc is not organized by habitat or taxonomy but by the relational logic of predation -- each species is connected to the others through food, territory, and ecological pressure; the bugling bull elk at E=8 sets the threshold that the arc's three E=9 entries will surpass

## The bugle as ecological signal


- the Roosevelt Elk bugle is not merely a vocalisation but a broadcast signal that activates predator attention; the bull in full rut is at maximum acoustic output, maximum hormonal state, and minimum vigilance -- the combination that makes the rut the primary hunting season for wolves and cougars; the bugle announces the arc's predator logic before a single predator has appeared in the sequence; the prey speaks first

## Noise rock as terminal sixth register


- Hovercraft's noise rock at E=6 is the appropriate register for the opening of the engine's final sixty degrees; the terminal sixth will contain some of the engine's highest-energy entries (the three E=9 streak at 302-304, the E=8 entries at 306-308) and some quieter resolutions; opening with Hovercraft at E=6 sets the stage for escalation without frontloading the energy; the noise rock texture -- abrasive, sustained, resistant to easy resolution -- is the acoustic register of a sequence that will not resolve easily

## Beth Liebling / Rachel Carns lineage


- Hovercraft's membership connects to the broader Seattle experimental and noise community of the 1990s; Liebling's later work under her married name (Beth Orton) moved toward different registers entirely, but the Hovercraft output is noise rock of genuine conviction -- not a side project but a central project for both members during the period of the engine's primary documentation window

## Olympic Peninsula ecology


- the Roosevelt Elk is the defining large mammal of Olympic National Park, the Hoh Rain Forest, and the Olympic Coast; the bugling heard on a foggy October morning in the Hoh is one of the primary acoustic experiences of the Pacific Northwest old-growth; its presence at degree 301 anchors the apex predator arc in the western Cascades and Olympic Peninsula ecosystem that is the SPS track's ecological spine
