## Green River genealogy


- the dissolution of Green River in 1987 produced two distinct musical lineages that together account for a substantial fraction of Pacific Northwest rock's commercial and artistic impact in the 1990s: Mark Arm and Steve Turner took their punk-rock noise orientation into Mudhoney, which became Sub Pop's flagship band and the most important band for the label's identity; Jeff Ament and Stone Gossard joined Andrew Wood in Mother Love Bone, whose dissolution after Wood's death produced Pearl Jam; Green River is therefore the common ancestor of Sub Pop's punk-metal identity and of the Pacific Northwest rock sound that reached the widest commercial audience; both lineages are present at the Metal Summit -- degree 337's TAD represents the Sub Pop heavy weight, degree 340's Nirvana represents the commercial breakthrough -- but Green River precedes both

## Pacific Northwest snowpack hydrology


- the Cascade Range and Olympic Mountains function as the Pacific Northwest's primary water storage system: winter precipitation falls as snow at high elevations, accumulates in snowpack at depths of 60-120 inches snow water equivalent in the highest basins, and releases as meltwater in spring; the timing and magnitude of snowmelt determines the summer baseflow in every major Pacific Northwest river system -- the Columbia, the Skagit, the Snoqualmie, the Green, the Nisqually -- and therefore the availability of cold, clear water for Pacific salmon, steelhead, and bull trout summer migration and spawning; the acoustic event at degree 339 is the sound of the annual water release that sustains the ecological systems the SPS track has documented throughout
