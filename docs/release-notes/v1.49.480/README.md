**Snowmelt flood surge** arrives at E=7 on the SPS track -- the acoustic event of Pacific Northwest snowpack releasing into river systems during spring thaw: the annual cycle in which the Cascade and Olympic mountain snowpack, accumulated over winter at rates of 60-120 inches of snow water equivalent in high-elevation basins, begins releasing into river systems as air temperatures rise in April and May, producing flood surges that transform Washington's rivers from their winter base flows to their spring maximum flows over periods of days to weeks.
