7. **The cratering-experiment achievement is the first controlled cratering experiment on a comet nucleus — framed positively.**

The newly-formed crater was obscured by the bright dust at the time, and Stardust-NExT (1.272) later imaged it in February 2011. The qualifier is held load-bearing.

8. **The self-guided-probe achievement is the first self-guided probe to reach a comet's nucleus, and the copper fraction is "about half," NOT "largely copper.**
   The autonomously-navigated probe steered itself with its own targeting camera to the sunlit nucleus at ~10.3 km/s; the catalog scopes this as "the first self-guided probe to reach a comet's nucleus." The 372 kg probe had a copper core, about half copper by mass (~49%, with a ~113 kg all-copper cratering mass), chosen because copper is absent from comet spectra; the catalog says "copper-core" / "about half copper," NOT "largely copper." The qualifiers are held load-bearing.
