## What's New in Pass 2

### S36 — Bill Frisell: Pass 2 Refinement (4,156 words)

Four new research sections not present in Pass 1:

1. **Guitar-as-Orchestra** — How Frisell builds multi-voice counterpoint from a single Telecaster through the signal chain. Volume-swell ADSR envelope shaping (eliminating percussive attack to mimic bowed instruments), asynchronous loop layering on the Line 6 DL4 (half-speed transposition creating cello-register drones), the Electro-Harmonix Freeze as sustained-chord organ, and stereo amplifier staging as orchestral seating. One guitarist producing four or five independent voices simultaneously — the Amiga Principle at its most literal.

2. **Nashville Skyline** — The 1997 Nonesuch album recorded at Sound Emporium with Viktor Krauss, Jerry Douglas, Ron Block, and Adam Steffey. How a jazz musician walked into a Nashville studio with bluegrass instrumentalists and found shared harmonic ground — open voicings, melodic primacy, respect for space. The album as proof that genre boundaries are administrative zoning lines, not walls. Frisell as fireweed: first into the burned territory between genres, two years before the Americana Music Association existed and thirteen years before the Grammy category.

3. **ECM Records Aesthetic** — The production philosophy that shaped Frisell's formative years (1983-1988). Jan Erik Kongshaug's engineering at Oslo studios — natural room ambience captured through microphone placement rather than artificial reverb. Manfred Eicher's silence directive: the space between notes as compositionally significant. The structural parallel between ECM's studio chain (instrument → room → microphone → minimal processing) and Frisell's live chain (guitar → volume pedal → delay → reverb → amp → room). Both treat every acoustic event between creation and the listener's ear as music.

4. **Pioneer 0 Resonance** — The T+77s flight as the aesthetic of incomplete sustain. Pioneer 0 transmitted valid data for 77 seconds before the Thor exploded — the note was correct, it just didn't sustain as long as planned. Frisell's volume-swelled phrases arc toward beauty and then decay without resolving — the incomplete arc as aesthetic principle. The discipline of compression: Pioneer 0's 77-second data burst, the Pacific Wren's 10-second territorial declaration, Frisell's four-note harmonic narratives — maximum information in minimum time. Fireweed as first colonizer: degree 0's Armillaria builds networks in existing forests; degree 1's fireweed colonizes bare ground where no forest yet exists.

### SPS — Pacific Wren: Pass 2 Refinement (3,999 words)

Four new research sections not present in Pass 1:

1. **Song Complexity Analysis** — Element inventory (40-50 types per individual, 36+ elements per second), comparison with Brown Thrasher (repertoire breadth vs. within-song density), Marsh Wren (comparable element density but lower spectral bandwidth), and Common Nightingale (comparable complexity at lower peak information rate). Recombinatorial syntax as generative grammar — finite elements combined by probabilistic rules into theoretically infinite unique utterances. Trill architecture: frequency-sweep oscillator elements producing the buzzy, mechanical quality that distinguishes Pacific from Winter Wren.

2. **Acoustic Adaptation to Old-Growth** — The Sound Window Hypothesis (Morton 1975): the 2-8 kHz transmission window where old-growth understory propagates sound most efficiently. Low frequencies attenuated by trunk scattering, high frequencies absorbed by humid air, the sweet spot between. The wren sings in the key the forest resonates. The reverb-chamber effect: dense understory vegetation creating closely spaced reflective surfaces that add natural reverb — the same principle Manfred Eicher captured in ECM's Oslo studios.

3. **Territory Mapping Through Song** — Repertoire-based individual recognition: each male identified by his unique recombinatorial patterns (individual Markov models). Counter-singing as negotiated boundary: repertoire matching signals heightened territorial intent. Neighbor-stranger discrimination: the "dear enemy" effect depends on song complexity providing sufficient individual information. The dawn chorus as landscape-scale acoustic map — overlapping territorial broadcasts encoding the population's entire social structure in real time.

4. **Pioneer 0 Resonance** — The 77-second data burst parallel. The wren's song-to-territory ratio: each 10-second bout maintains a 180-day breeding territory (1,555,200:1 function-to-signal ratio). The data-in-failure principle: most song bouts are sung to an empty forest, but their cumulative effect constructs acoustic infrastructure. Fireweed and the colonization of disturbed silence: the wren's dawn song fills the acoustic void the way fireweed fills burned ground — first complex signal into empty territory, structuring the environment for everything that follows.
