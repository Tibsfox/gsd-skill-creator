## Surprises

- **Two instruments image the changing land from one sun-synchronous platform.** Landsat 9 carries OLI-2 and TIRS-2 together — OLI-2 imaging the land in nine reflective bands at 30 m multispectral and 15 m panchromatic with 14-bit radiometry, TIRS-2 reading the land's thermal infrared in two bands at about 100 m. The pairing is the point: the reflective and thermal views of the land read together from the same sun-synchronous orbit, paired with Landsat 8 for the 8-day repeat.
- **OLI-2 carries 14-bit radiometry, up from OLI's 12-bit.** OLI-2 quantizes the reflective bands at 14 bits, up from OLI's 12 bits, for improved sensitivity over dark targets — a measurable improvement in the radiometric quality of the land record over the ninth Landsat's predecessor.
