## Summary

**Fastbacks** arrive at E=5 -- the **longest-running Seattle band**, active since **1979**. Formed two years before the Young Fresh Fellows (192), the Fastbacks hold the undisputed title of Seattle's most enduring band. Kurt Bloch, Kim Warnick, and Lulu Gargiulo built a catalog of hook-laden, buzzsaw punk-pop across four decades, releasing records on Sub Pop, PopLlama, and No Threes, and playing with virtually every significant Seattle band that followed them. The Fastbacks were the foundation: they were playing Seattle clubs before Black Flag toured the West Coast, before the Melvins moved to town, before anyone imagined that Seattle would become the center of the rock universe. On the species side, the **Gray Whale breach** registers at E=7 -- a dramatic escalation from the blow at degree 192 (E=5). The breach is one of the most spectacular behaviors in the animal kingdom: a 40-ton animal launching its entire body out of the water and crashing back in an explosion of sound and spray. The acoustic impact of a Gray Whale breach -- the thunderclap of re-entry -- can be heard underwater for miles. This is the second Gray Whale entry, forming a **blow/breach comparison pair** that documents the same species at two radically different energy states.
