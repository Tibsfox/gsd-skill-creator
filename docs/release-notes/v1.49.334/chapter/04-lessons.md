1. **Foundation-layer documentation requires pre-scene bands.**
   The engine's documentation of the Seattle music scene needs entries for bands that existed before the scene had identity, before the infrastructure existed, and before external attention focused on Pacific Northwest music. Fastbacks at degree 193 establishes this register by carrying the 1979-formation-date foundation-layer role; future degrees should consider whether similarly pre-scene entries exist for other regional music scenes in the engine's coverage and document them with comparable structural weight.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7717_

2. **Consecutive-degree label streaks with personnel bridges are structurally distinct from isolated label coincidences.**
   The PopLlama streak across degrees 192-193 with the Kurt Bloch dual-band bridge demonstrates that a label connection plus a human connection creates a doubly-coupled continuity that is stronger than either alone. Future degrees should look for opportunities to document similar doubly-coupled consecutive-degree relationships, and the engine's documentation vocabulary should preserve the distinction between single-coupled and doubly-coupled streaks.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7718_

3. **Within-species multi-entry documentation requires genuinely distinct acoustic and behavioral registers.**
   The Gray Whale blow at 192 (E=4) and breach at 193 (E=7) both ship without redundancy because the blow is routine respiration and the breach is explosive physical impact — two entirely different sound types, two entirely different behavioral contexts, and a +3 energy delta. Future multi-entry species documentation should meet all three criteria (distinct sound types, distinct behavioral contexts, significant energy contrast) to justify the structural decision; documenting two similar-energy same-behavior events of the same species would not earn the multi-entry architecture.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.398`) · lesson #7719_

4. **Elided connections in mainstream histories are structural documentation opportunities.**
   The Duff McKagan early-Fastbacks-drummer connection is entirely elided in most rock histories of Guns N' Roses, which treat McKagan's trajectory as a Los Angeles story that begins with his arrival in the city. The engine's documentation at degree 193 corrects this omission by preserving the Seattle punk roots of GNR's bassist. Future degrees should prioritize documenting connections that mainstream histories elide — the structural information value of preserved-but-unknown connections is higher than the information value of reiterating already-documented connections.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7720_

5. **Geographic nodes expand the engine's spatial documentation incrementally.**
   Ballard enters the engine at degree 193 as a new geographic neighborhood node, distinct from previously-documented Capitol Hill, University District, Central District, and Rainier Valley. The engine's neighborhood map grows one node at a time, and each new node carries its own historical-ethos register — Ballard's working-class Scandinavian-American fishing-and-shipyards identity aligns with punk DIY in ways that Capitol Hill's art-school bohemia does not. Future degrees should consider which neighborhoods remain undocumented and identify the bands whose origin neighborhoods would expand the engine's spatial coverage.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.384`) · lesson #7721_

6. **Permission lineages are as important as direct influence lineages.**
   Kim Warnick's role as one of the first prominent women in Seattle punk established permission for the riot grrrl generation that emerged through Olympia a decade later — Bikini Kill (1990), Sleater-Kinney (1994), and the broader movement — without direct musical influence. Permission lineages operate structurally differently from direct-influence lineages: they create cultural space rather than transmit specific techniques, and they require sustained-presence-in-a-role rather than charismatic-moment-of-breakthrough. The engine's documentation should preserve both kinds of lineages and distinguish them explicitly.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7722_

7. **Broadband percussive impulse is a distinct acoustic register from vocalizations.**
   The Gray Whale breach at E=7 is the first physical-impact-sound register in the engine, distinct from the narrow-band vocalizations (whistles, clicks, songs, blows, grunts) that dominate prior marine mammal entries. The 20-5,000 Hz broadband signature with peak energy in the 20-200 Hz infrasonic register represents a different class of sound entirely — not a vocalization but the sound of physical impact on water. The engine's acoustic vocabulary should recognize this distinction explicitly and may develop further physical-event-sound entries (tail slaps, body rolls, feeding vocalizations that incorporate physical sounds) in future degrees.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.339`) · lesson #7723_

8. **Long-duration specialist careers convert individual persistence into infrastructure.**
   The Fastbacks played continuously for 22 years (1979-2001), and this duration is not incidental — it is the structural precondition for the foundation-layer register to exist. A band that plays for three years cannot build an ecosystem; a band that plays for twenty-two years can. Duration converts to infrastructure, and infrastructure converts to ecosystem. The engine's documentation should preserve this duration-to-infrastructure-to-ecosystem conversion pattern and recognize that sustained narrow commitment is structurally distinct from broad adaptation.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7724_

9. **Monotypic lineages are a recurring engine taxonomic vocabulary entry.**

The monotypic-lineage register documents lineages where only one surviving species represents the entire family or genus, and the recurrence across multiple degrees establishes monotypic status as a first-class taxonomic vocabulary category in the engine's documentation. Future Mammalia entries should preserve monotypic status when it applies.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7725_

10. **Cross-sync operations require both halves to genuinely embody the shared principle, not force the parallel.**
   The foundation-and-spectacle cross-sync at degree 193 works because Fastbacks' 22 years of quiet persistence genuinely built the Seattle ecosystem whose spectacular moments followed, and Gray Whale's decades of routine migration genuinely build the 40-ton body whose rare breach produces the spectacular acoustic impact. Neither half is forced into the cross-sync pattern; both halves actually carry the foundation-enables-spectacle relationship. The engine's cross-sync documentation should always meet this genuine-embodiment criterion and should avoid forced parallels that merely look structurally symmetric without operating substantively.
