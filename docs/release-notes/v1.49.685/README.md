## Summary

STS-27 Atlantis launched 1988-12-02 14:30:34 UTC from Kennedy Space Center LC-39B Florida — **second US crewed spaceflight after STS-51-L Challenger** (64 days after STS-26 landing). DoD-classified payload **Lacrosse-1** (codenamed Indigo/Onyx; NRO-managed) = **FIRST SYNTHETIC-APERTURE-RADAR (SAR) RECONNAISSANCE SATELLITE**; substrate-anchor for 5-vehicle Lacrosse constellation 1988-2005.

**Near-loss tile damage event.** Ablative insulation from RH SRB nose cap struck Atlantis at ~T+85 seconds: ~707 individual tile damage sites + 1 tile completely missing exposing aluminum substructure plate adjacent to L-band antenna. Commander Gibson and MS Mullane separately concluded reentry would kill the crew. Mission classification prevented public discussion for years; Mullane published the declassified-era account in *Riding Rockets* (Scribner 2006). Antenna mounting plate acted as heat sink during reentry, sparing the airframe.

**Substrate-form forward-shadow to STS-107 Columbia loss 2003-02-01** = 15-year forward-shadow with identical root-cause class (ascent-debris strike of orbiter TPS during powered flight). **FOAM-STRIKE-ASCENT-DEBRIS-NEAR-LOSS-AND-LOSS substrate-cohort opens at v685** with STS-27 as substrate-anchor and Columbia STS-107 as substrate-realization at v-future.

Mission duration 4 days 9 hours 5 minutes 37 seconds. Landed Edwards Air Force Base Runway 17 at 1988-12-06 23:36:11 UTC.

**Primary payload: Lacrosse-1 (USA-34 / 1988-106B)** — ~14,500 kg synthetic-aperture-radar reconnaissance satellite; designator codenamed Indigo / Onyx; National Reconnaissance Office (NRO) managed. Direct deployment from payload bay at 437×447 km × 57° inclination. **LACROSSE-1-FIRST-SAR-RECONNAISSANCE-SATELLITE** substrate-form NEW LOCKED — substrate-anchor for 5-vehicle Lacrosse constellation: Lacrosse-1 (1988-12 STS-27) + Lacrosse-2 (1991-03 STS-39) + Lacrosse-3 (1997-10 Titan-IV) + Lacrosse-4 (2000-08 Titan-IVB) + Lacrosse-5 (2005-04 Titan-IVB).
