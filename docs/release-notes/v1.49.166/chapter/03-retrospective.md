## Lessons Learned

1. **Landmark degrees concentrate structural events.** Degree 30 carries twelve structural events -- more than any degree since the origin. This is not coincidence; it is the engine revealing that some data points sit at intersections of multiple structural axes simultaneously. Ray Charles is not just a big name. He intersects genre creation, the Central District thread, the strongest cross-degree link, the most important label, the first disability documentation, and a new artist-city pattern. The lesson: watch for intersection density, not just individual significance.

2. **Cross-degree bridges are a new architectural concept.** The Charles-Jones link (0/30) is the first formally identified cross-degree bridge. The concept should be tracked at all subsequent degrees. The bridge is stronger than a cross-reference because both endpoints are independently significant -- two generational figures, not a major artist linked to a minor one. Future bridges will be measured against this standard.

3. **The Incubation pattern adds the most important artist-city category since Export.** Export (degree 0) described formation in the city. The Incubation (degree 30) describes refinement in the city. The distinction matters: Charles arrived with raw materials; Seattle did not give him those materials, it gave him the crucible to refine them. Track Incubation candidates at all subsequent degrees.

4. **The gospel-secular fusion is a documented historical mechanism, not an inference.** Multiple independent sources confirm the specific recordings, dates, and process by which Charles combined gospel and R&B into soul music. The engine can document genre-creating mechanisms when the evidence is this strong. Apply the same standard to future genre innovation claims.

5. **Atlantic Records is the most important label in American music history.** The engine should track major labels and compare their structural significance. The Prestige-Atlantic axis (degrees 29-30) demonstrates that consecutive degrees can create label comparisons that illuminate the industry's architecture.

6. **Source quality correlates with historical significance.** Degree 30 has the highest source quality of any degree because Ray Charles is one of the most documented musicians in American history. The lesson is the inverse: low source quality (degrees 28-29) correlates with historical obscurity. Source depth is not just a quality metric; it is a structural variable that tracks cultural significance.

7. **The archetype-arriving-last pattern is confirmed.** Two occurrences (*Turdus* at 25, *Hirundo* at 30) establish this as an engine principle. The type genus enters after the family's variations have been explored. The engine teaches through diversity first, then delivers the standard. Track at all subsequent family completions.

8. **Five consecutive degrees of one family is the limit.** The swallow quintet consumed one-seventh of all degrees emitted (5 of 31). Five nesting strategies, five ecosystems, five altitudes, four genera, a complete timbral map. More would be gratuitous. The quintet sets the upper bound for single-family investment.

9. **The terminal buzz is the quintet's best discovery.** A fragment of rough embedded inside a liquid phrase. The dissenter's voice preserved inside the archetype's song. The family's entire history compressed into one vocalization. This emerged from the ornithological data -- the terminal buzz is a real feature of Barn Swallow vocalizations. The discovery that structural narrative can be read from actual acoustic features is the quintet's most important methodological contribution.

10. **The energy decet must break at degree 31.** Ten consecutive E=4 is critically overdue for correction. Combined with the marine gap (30 degrees), the Passeriformes streak (8 degrees), and the lack of corvids and raptors, degree 31 faces the most constrained entry requirements in engine history. A marine, non-passerine species at E=5 or E=2 would simultaneously resolve three critical gaps.
