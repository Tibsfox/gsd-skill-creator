## Summary

**Campfire OK** arrives at E=5 -- the indie-folk project built around the warm, communal energy that its name promises. Campfire OK's sound is exactly what happens when Pacific Northwest musicians who grew up on Sub Pop decide to pick up acoustic instruments: the songs are campfire-ready in the literal sense, built on acoustic guitars, group vocals, and arrangements that invite participation rather than observation. Their debut *Strange Like We Are* (2013) documents a band that values togetherness over virtuosity, with harmonies that sound like they were worked out in a living room at 2 AM. At E=5, Campfire OK occupies the warm center of the engine -- a gentle exhale after the E=6 intensity of Pickwick. On the species side, the **Coyote pup yipping** registers at E=4 -- forming an **adult-pup pair** with the Coyote howl at degree 208. This mirrors the engine's established pattern of adult-then-juvenile vocalizations (Black-tailed Deer at 195-196, Mountain Goat at 203-204). Coyote pups begin yipping at approximately three weeks of age, producing a high-pitched, staccato vocalization that is equal parts practice and social bonding. The pup yip is recognizably Coyote but lacks the adult howl's range and control -- it is the sound of an animal learning the most important tool in its acoustic repertoire.
