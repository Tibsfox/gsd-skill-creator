## What Could Be Better

- **The shader renders procedural structure rather than archived data.** The CRRES radiation-belt-environment shader uses analytic geometry and procedural noise rather than loading actual CRRES energetic-particle flux maps or dose maps from the NASA Space Physics Data Facility. A future revision could load encoded CRRES belt-flux and dose data for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to the real data.
- **The transfer-orbit diorama is a forthcoming artifact.** The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the spacecraft, the radiation belts, and the transfer orbit.
- **The radiation-belt / inner-magnetosphere sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.** The sidebar lists representative program elements (CRRES, AMPTE, Polar, IMAGE, DE-1, FAST, RBSP/Van Allen Probes) but does not enumerate the full inner-magnetosphere fleet; a future revision could expand the table with launch dates and mission outcomes.
- **The storm-time belt formation is described rather than computed.** The radial transport that builds the storm-formed second proton belt is described but not run against archived data; a future ship could model the transport from real CRRES storm-time flux measurements.

## Surprises

- **The radiation belts are not a fixed background.** The belts were long thought of as a stable feature that varies slowly, but CRRES captured the rapid formation of a whole new proton belt during a single storm, so the catalog's first comprehensive belt-environment map also recorded the belts reorganizing in real time.
- **An enduring alpine mammal mirrors an enduring spacecraft.** The Mountain Goat, which endures and crosses the harshest exposed alpine terrain as a matter of course, enters as the endure-the-environment mirror to the mission that swept through and endured the intense radiation-belt environment, so the catalog pairs a high-exposure alpine mammal with a radiation-belt-environment mission.
- **A lichen literally draws a map.** The Map Lichen's growth pattern spreads across bare rock like a chart, so the catalog's radiation-belt-environment mapping mission is paired with a lichen that endures extreme exposure while its growth maps the surface it grows on.

## Lessons Learned

1. **An axis can be deepened by a focused environment map after composition sampling.** AMPTE sampled the ring-current ions to read their composition, and CRRES then mapped the radiation-belt environment in detail, showing that an axis can deepen by adding a focused environment map to the composition foundation, an obs#6 continuation that complements the composition sample with a belt-environment picture.
2. **A repeated-traversal orbit builds a time-resolved environment map.** The geosynchronous-transfer orbit carried CRRES through the belts twice per revolution, so the same region could be measured over and over as conditions changed, showing that a repeated-traversal orbit can turn a single spacecraft into a time-resolved survey of the inner magnetosphere.
3. **A single strong storm can build a new radiation belt rapidly.** CRRES captured the rapid formation of a new proton belt during the great geomagnetic storm of March 1991, showing that the belt structure is dynamic and can be reshaped quickly by a single storm, a result that reshaped the understanding of the radiation belts.
4. **Measuring the environment and its effect together links cause to consequence.** Because the same spacecraft mapped the particle environment and measured its effect on the electronics, CRRES tied the measured belt environment directly to its effect on technology, showing that measuring an environment and its consequence on the same platform links the two directly.
5. **A dose map turns a particle population into an engineering model.** The dosimeters mapped the accumulated dose alongside the particle measurements, showing that mapping the dose a population deposits turns a measured particle environment into a usable engineering model of the conditions a spacecraft encounters.
6. **A joint partnership can fly one mission across two organizations.** CRRES joined NASA and the United States Air Force in one mission, NASA Marshall leading the NASA element and the Air Force Geophysics Laboratory providing the payload, showing that a shared mission can distribute the science and the payload across two organizations while operating as a single coordinated whole.
7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across a distinct-palette mission.** Because CRRES reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.190 template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content to the radiation-belt environment mapping with a distinct proton-belt-amber / electron-belt-blue / dose-violet palette, making the distinct-palette mission a clean build.
