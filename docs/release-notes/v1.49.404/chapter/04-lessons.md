1. **Multi-band developmental arcs should be documented across multiple engine positions rather than collapsed into a single entry.**
   The Treepeople-to-Built to Spill arc documents **Doug Martsch**'s developmental trajectory across two structurally distinct bands (post-hardcore versus melodic alternative rock), and the engine's decision to place Treepeople at degree 263 and Built to Spill at a later engine position preserves the distinct stylistic centers of the two bands without collapsing either into the other. Future multi-band developmental arcs (Mark Arm across Green River and Mudhoney, Kurt Bloch across multiple bands, and others) should follow the same multi-position template rather than single-position summary.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.414`) · lesson #8666_

2. **Regional music-infrastructure registers should explicitly acknowledge geographic breadth beyond administrative boundaries when the documented activity operated at the broader scope.**
   The Treepeople entry at degree 263 explicitly extends the Pacific Northwest alternative-rock regional network beyond Seattle, Olympia, Bellingham, and Portland to include Boise, Idaho via the I-5 corridor and the Cascade-crossing routes. Future regional-infrastructure documentation should acknowledge similar geographic breadth when the documented activity genuinely operated at the broader scope, because collapsing the broader scope to narrower administrative boundaries would misrepresent the actual infrastructure that supported the activity.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8667_

3. **First-arrival acoustic signals should be documented at amplitudes that reflect their first-arrival character rather than at the peak amplitudes of the larger structures they precede.**
   The P-wave at E=5 and Treepeople at E=7 both reflect first-arrival amplitudes appropriate to the first-arrival character of each signal. The P-wave is not the peak amplitude of a Cascadia earthquake sequence; Treepeople is not the peak amplitude of Doug Martsch's developmental arc. Future first-arrival documentation should register amplitudes that match the first-arrival character rather than projecting the eventual peak amplitude backward onto the first-arrival entry.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.437`) · lesson #8668_

4. **Paleoseismology documentation benefits from citation of multiple independent evidence lines when they independently converge on the same conclusion.**
   The January 26, 1700 Cascadia full-rupture date is confirmed by two independent evidence lines: **Japanese Hōei-era** tsunami records from across the Pacific and **Brian Atwater** and collaborators' coastal paleoseismology record from the regional coast. Future paleoseismology-adjacent documentation should explicitly cite the multiple independent evidence lines when they exist, because the convergence of independent lines is what distinguishes rigorous paleoseismology from speculative date-assignment.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #8669_

5. **Geophysical-hazard documentation should anchor acoustic claims in specific physics parameters (propagation speed, wave mode, frequency range) rather than in generic low-frequency description.**

Future geophysical-hazard documentation should anchor acoustic claims in specific measurable physics parameters rather than in impressionistic description, because the physics-grounding register is what distinguishes measurable geophysical documentation from impressionistic weather-style description.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.405`) · lesson #8670_
