3. **The sky crane is heritage, not a Perseverance first — it is Curiosity's first (obs#5).**
   Perseverance reused Curiosity's sky crane (the first sky-crane landing was Curiosity's, in 2012) and added Terrain-Relative Navigation for autonomous hazard avoidance and the Range Trigger to shrink the landing ellipse. The catalog never re-stamps the sky crane and never calls Perseverance's landing a "first" landing of any kind except the Terrain-Relative-Navigation precision and hazard-avoidance addition (the first use at Mars).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12303_

4. **The biosignature search is not life detection, not Viking's extant-life search, and not Curiosity's habitability assessment.**
   Perseverance seeks signs of ANCIENT microbial life and caches samples — the organic matter and textures are potential biosignatures, not life. The catalog states throughout that Perseverance has NOT detected or found life, that this is distinct from Viking's in-situ extant-life search (obs#1) and from Curiosity's habitability assessment (obs#5), and that the cached samples have not yet been returned to Earth. All astrobiology prose is hedged ("signs of," "could record," "potential biosignatures").
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12304_

5. **Perseverance is NUCLEAR (MMRTG), like Curiosity — nuclear power is not a first here.**
   Perseverance is powered by a radioisotope generator like Curiosity, so the catalog does not claim nuclear power as a first. Perseverance's distinct firsts are the first collection and caching of Mars samples for return, the first powered flight on another planet (Ingenuity), and the first in-situ resource production (MOXIE); the payload is Mastcam-Z, SuperCam, PIXL, SHERLOC with WATSON, MEDA, RIMFAX, and MOXIE plus the Sampling and Caching System (no CheMin / SAM; ChemCam becomes SuperCam).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12305_

6. **Sample caching for return is the distinct mission first — the samples are cached, not yet returned.**
   Perseverance is the first mission to drill, hermetically seal, and cache rock and regolith cores on Mars for a future return to Earth (the first step of the NASA-ESA Mars Sample Return campaign), with the first rock core ("Montdenier," 6 September 2021) and the first sample depot on another world ("Three Forks," early 2023). The catalog scopes this as the first to COLLECT and CACHE samples FOR RETURN and never claims the samples have been returned to Earth; this is distinct from all prior in-situ analysis (Viking, Pathfinder, the MERs, and Curiosity analyzed samples on Mars).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12306_

7. **Ingenuity's flight and MOXIE's oxygen are scoped firsts.**
   Ingenuity made the first POWERED, CONTROLLED flight on another planet (19 April 2021, 72 flights through 18 January 2024) — Mars's thin air made it a hard first, distinct from unpowered or uncontrolled parachute or aeroshell descent. MOXIE made the first usable resource (oxygen) on another world (first oxygen 20 April 2021, ~122 g across 16 runs) — the first demonstration of in-situ resource utilization (ISRU) on another planet, a demonstration-scale proof of concept.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12307_
