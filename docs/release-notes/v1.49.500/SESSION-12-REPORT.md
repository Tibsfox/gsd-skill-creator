## What Was Built

The Seattle 360 / Sound of Puget Sound engine pairs **360 Seattle-area musicians** (S36) with **360 PNW species, soundscapes, and natural phenomena** (SPS) across a full unit circle rotation. Each degree produces:

- **research.md** — Deep research document (2,000-4,000 words)
- **index.html** — Styled web page with sidebar statistics
- **knowledge-nodes.json** — Structured knowledge graph data
- **retrospective.md** — Reflection and carry-forward lessons
- **README.md** — Verbose release notes (in docs/release-notes/)

That's **9 files per degree** (4 S36 + 4 SPS + 1 release note), plus a git commit, git tag, merge to main, push to origin, and GitHub release — for every single degree.

### S36 Coverage

360 entries covering the full arc of Seattle-area music: jazz (Quincy Jones, degree 0) through proto-punk (The Sonics), garage rock (The Kingsmen, "Louie Louie"), the grunge explosion (Nirvana, Soundgarden, Alice in Chains, Pearl Jam), riot grrrl (Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney), mathcore (Botch), and the quiet dissolution of Unwound at degree 359.

### SPS Coverage

360 entries covering: songbirds, raptors, owls, woodpeckers, marine mammals (orcas, whales, seals, sea lions), fish (salmon, lamprey, sturgeon), ancient species, abiotic soundscapes (waterfalls, wind, rain, tides), geophysics (volcanoes, earthquakes, lahars), storms, apex predator convergences, multi-species synthesis events, and finally — silence.

### Energy Arc

The engine's energy scale (E=1 to E=10) traced a complete dramatic arc:

- **E=1** minimum: Harbor Porpoise (187), Pacific Lamprey (243)
- **E=9** first appearances: Lightning (260 SPS), Dead Kennedys (286 S36)
- **E=10** first appearance: Botch (344) — the ceiling breaks
- **E=20** combined maximum: Degrees 344, 346-348, 349-359

The final 16 degrees (344-359) sustained E=10 on both tracks — the engine's maximum output held through its closing arc.
