## Lessons Learned

1. **An axis sustains when a new way of seeing the same substrate enters the catalog.** By mapping the same relic radiation COBE first detected at about thirty-three times the resolution and turning it into precise numbers, WMAP sustained the cosmology / cosmic-microwave-background axis at obs#2 rather than rotating away, showing that an axis continues when a successor observation is genuinely substrate-form-distinct — finer resolution, precise parameters, a new vantage — even on the same target.
2. **A detection becomes a precise measurement with a sharper instrument.** By resolving the fine structure of the temperature variations down to about 13 arcminutes, WMAP turned the blurred few-degree pattern COBE detected into a detailed map of the early universe, showing that the next step after a first detection is the resolution that makes it a measurement.
3. **The basic numbers of cosmology are written in the angular power spectrum.** By reading the sizes and strengths of the temperature variations — the acoustic peaks of the power spectrum — WMAP measured the age of the universe at about 13.77 billion years, a flat geometry, and the rate of cosmic expansion, showing that the detailed pattern of the relic radiation encodes the basic parameters of the cosmos.
4. **Ordinary matter is only a small part of the universe.** By measuring the relative heights of the acoustic peaks, WMAP found the universe to be about 4.6 percent ordinary matter, about 24 percent dark matter, and about 71 percent dark energy, showing that the precise accounting of the cosmic energy budget reveals most of the universe in forms still being studied.
5. **A distant, quiet vantage reads the faint sky best.** By observing from the Sun-Earth L2 Lagrange point, about 1.5 million kilometers from Earth, WMAP read the faint background far from the planet's own microwave emission, showing that a stable, distant vantage is itself an engineering achievement that opened a place later cosmology observatories would also use.
6. **Many frequency bands separate the foreground from the cosmic signal.** By measuring the sky in five frequency bands from 23 to 94 gigahertz, WMAP separated the emission of our own galaxy from the cosmic background, showing that reading the faint relic radiation cleanly depends on distinguishing the nearby foreground from the distant signal.
7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across an intra-axis continuation.** Because WMAP reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.215 COBE template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content from the first-detection observatory to the high-resolution precision-cosmology successor while keeping the warm palette, making the intra-axis continuation a clean intra-template rewrite.
