## Lessons Learned

1. **Round-number engine completion milestones carry documentary weight when they coincide with structural arc closures.** Degree 252's 70.0% engine completion milestone lands at the exact moment three multi-degree arcs close simultaneously. Future engine sequencing should target round-number completion points as arc-closure moments when possible, because the combination of round-number milestone + arc-closure is interpretively stronger than either phenomenon in isolation. The 70.0% threshold as a batch-closing, streak-closing, arc-closing moment is documented at higher structural resolution than a single-degree milestone would be.
2. **Commercial-institutional spectrum documentation requires all positions to be named, not only the commercially visible ones.** The 249-252 batch documents Sub Pop, Hollywood Records, Sub Pop, and self-released in sequence. Future batch-level documentation should explicitly name the institutional position of each entry so that the commercial spectrum can be read as a documentary element rather than being absent from the record. The engine's documentary function requires surfacing peripheral positions (self-released, underground) at documentary grade rather than treating them as lesser forms of the commercially visible positions.
3. **Sustained single-energy streaks document generational consistency as meaningful data.** The 249-252 E=7 streak documents the current Seattle rock generation's consistent energy across four genres and four institutional positions. Future sustained-energy streaks should be surfaced explicitly as documentary evidence of generational consistency rather than treated as coincidental sequences, because the streak register carries interpretive weight about scene-wide amplitude norms that single-entry energy placement does not capture.
4. **Multi-phase acoustic sequences require explicit phase-by-phase decomposition rather than single-event description.** The cobble beach sound is a four-phase sequence (uprush → maximum uprush → backwash → settlement); noise rock sound is a multi-stage process (pick attack → string vibration → distortion saturation → amplifier stages → speaker resonance → feedback oscillation). Future acoustic documentation of multi-phase sound sources should decompose the phase sequence explicitly rather than treating the source as a single-event impulse, because the multi-phase structure is constitutive of the characteristic sound rather than a minor elaboration on it.
5. **Backwash-as-signature identification requires understanding which phase of a multi-phase sequence is the identifier.** The signature cobble beach sound is the backwash clatter, not the uprush hiss or the maximum-uprush broadband percussion. Future multi-phase acoustic documentation should identify the specific phase that serves as the recognizable identifier, because the identifier phase is what makes the sound distinguishable in recordings and in memory rather than merely present.
6. **Natural lithophones are a first-class acoustic category parallel to biological vocalizations and hydrodynamic sounds.** Stone percussion under wave action is as constitutive of the PNW acoustic landscape as bird vocalizations or waterfall roar. Future SPS documentation should treat lithophonic landscapes as first-class categories rather than as subcategories of water or beach sound, because the acoustic mechanism (stone-on-stone impact under applied force) is distinct from either aeolian vibration or hydrodynamic flow.
7. **Geological explanations for landscape character are acoustically constitutive, not incidentally contextual.** The absence of sandy beaches in the PNW is geologically explained (volcanic/metamorphic bedrock, lack of quartz-sand supply) and acoustically consequential (stone percussion where other coasts have silent sand absorption). Future SPS documentation should surface geological explanations explicitly when they are acoustically constitutive, because the geology is not a backdrop to the sound — it is the reason the sound exists.
8. **Self-release as a philosophical position warrants documentation equal to major-label positioning.** The engine's documentation of No-Anchor at degree 252 treats self-release as a philosophical commitment with specific implications (ownership, artistic freedom, audience directness) rather than as a lesser institutional status. Future self-released-artist documentation should carry the same analytical depth as major-label-artist documentation, because the institutional position is a documentary element rather than a default condition, and the engine's documentary function requires treating philosophical choices at documentary grade rather than as absences of institutional support.
9. **Arc closures at threshold moments create structural resonance that arc closures at mid-sequence points do not.** The three arc closures at degree 252 (E=7 streak, meteorological/hydrological arc, abiotic soundscape arc) coincide with the 70.0% engine completion milestone. Future arc sequencing should consider threshold alignment when possible, because the closure-at-threshold structural resonance is interpretively stronger than closure-at-mid-sequence, and the engine's documentation can surface this resonance as documentary evidence of deliberate sequencing rather than coincidental alignment.
10. **Indigenous cultural landscapes embedded in millennia of use are constitutive contexts for abiotic acoustic phenomena.** The Coast Salish, Lummi, and Samish use of the San Juan Islands and northern Puget Sound cobble beach zone for butter clam and littleneck clam harvest across millennia (documented by clamshell middens) makes the cobble beach soundscape a cultural landscape rather than an unpeopled natural feature. Future abiotic-soundscape documentation should surface indigenous cultural landscapes explicitly when the acoustic phenomena occur within tribal homelands with archaeologically documented continuous use, because the cultural-landscape register documents a relational element that generic geographic documentation does not capture.
