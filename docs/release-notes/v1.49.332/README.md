## Summary

**The Posies** arrive at E=5 -- the **FIRST POWER POP** entry in the Seattle 360 engine. Jon Auer and Ken Stringfellow formed The Posies in **Bellingham** in 1987, making this the first entry with roots outside the Seattle-Tacoma metropolitan corridor. Power pop -- tight harmonies, jangly guitars, concise song structures -- is the polar opposite of the grunge that surrounded The Posies in the early 1990s, and their persistence in the Seattle scene despite never fitting the prevailing aesthetic is itself a statement about the city's musical depth. Bellingham enters the engine's geographic map, extending the reach 90 miles north of Seattle. On the species side, the **Humpback Whale song** registers at **E=8** -- **TYING THE SPS MAXIMUM** set by DoNormaal at degree 188. The Humpback Whale song is arguably the most famous animal vocalization on Earth: a complex, structured sequence of moans, cries, and howls that can last up to 20 hours, travel thousands of miles through the ocean, and evolve across the breeding season as whales learn and modify phrases from each other. The Humpback's song was included on the Voyager Golden Record launched into interstellar space in 1977 -- making it one of a handful of Earth sounds selected to represent our planet to the universe. E=8 is not given lightly. The Humpback Whale song earns it.
