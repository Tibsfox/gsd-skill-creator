STS-26 Discovery launched 1988-09-29 15:37:00 UTC from Kennedy Space Center LC-39B Florida — **first US crewed spaceflight after the loss of STS-51-L Challenger on 1986-01-28**. The 32-month-1-day stand-down period saw NASA's adoption of nearly all Rogers Commission recommendations (v675), redesign of the Solid Rocket Booster field joints with capture-feature + heaters + composite o-ring backup, recertification of orbiter thermal protection systems, overhaul of crew + ground procedures, and the deliberate selection of an all-veteran 5-crew for the return-to-flight mission.

Mission duration 4 days 1 hour 0 minutes 11 seconds. Landed Edwards Air Force Base Runway 17 at 1988-10-03 16:37 UTC.

**All-veteran crew:**

- **CDR Capt. Frederick Hamilton "Rick" Hauck** (born 1941-04-11 Long Beach California; still living 2026) — Navy A-4/F-4/F-14 pilot; Naval Academy 1962; NASA Group 8 1978. Prior flights: STS-7 PLT 1983-06 (6d 2h) + STS-51-A CDR 1984-11 (7d 23h; Westar 6 + Palapa B-2 retrieval — first satellite-retrieval). **Selected as STS-26 commander 1986 immediately post-Challenger — FIRST EXPLICITLY-NAMED RETURN-TO-FLIGHT COMMANDER** substrate-form NEW LOCKED. Cumulative 18d 2h 9m. Distinguished Service Medal + Defense DSM.

- **PLT Col. Richard Oswalt Covey** (born 1946-08-01 Fayetteville Arkansas; still living 2026) — USAF F-4/F-15 pilot; Air Force Academy 1968; NASA Group 8 1978. Prior: STS-51-I PLT 1985-08 (LEASAT-3 rescue v667). Subsequent: STS-38 CDR 1990 + STS-61 CDR 1993 (Hubble first servicing). Cumulative 26d 21h 7m.

- **MS Col. David Carl Hilmers** (born 1950-01-28 Clinton Iowa; still living 2026) — USMC A-6 pilot; Naval Academy 1972; NASA Group 9 1980. Prior: STS-51-J 1985-10 (DoD classified Atlantis maiden v668). Subsequent: STS-36 1990 + STS-42 1992. Post-NASA career: pediatrician. **Substrate-pair with v683 Polyakov physician-cosmonaut = HILMERS-FUTURE-PHYSICIAN-DUAL-CAREER-PAIR-WITH-POLYAKOV** substrate-form NEW LOCKED. Cumulative 20d 17h 47m.

- **MS Col. George Driver "Pinky" Nelson** (born 1950-07-13 Charles City Iowa; still living 2026) — civilian-USAF; PhD astronomy University of Washington 1978; NASA Group 8 1978. Prior: STS-41-C 1984-04 (Solar Max repair) + STS-61-C 1986-01 (Chang-Díaz at v672). **Substrate-coincident-distinct: STS-61-C landed 1986-01-18 = 10 days before Challenger loss; Nelson among last astronauts to land pre-Challenger + first crew post-Challenger.** Cumulative 17d 2h 42m.

**Primary payload: TDRS-3 (TDRS-C "Hermes")** — 2,268 kg Tracking and Data Relay Satellite deployed via IUS (Inertial Upper Stage) two-stage solid rocket booster + reached geosynchronous orbit at 41°W. Replaces TDRS-B that was lost on Challenger; substrate-cumulative with TDRS-1 STS-6 1983-04. TDRS-3 continued operational service 1988 through 2020s. **TDRS-C-HERMES-DEPLOYMENT** substrate-form NEW LOCKED.
