## Summary

Yuri's Night documents the World Space Party -- the global celebration of human spaceflight held every April 12 to mark both Gagarin's first orbit (1961) and the first Space Shuttle launch (1981). From a Space Generation Advisory Council concept at UNISPACE III in 1999 to UN Resolution A/RES/65/271 designating the International Day of Human Space Flight in 2011, this project traces the organizational history, the spaceflight milestones, and the global outreach infrastructure of an event that has grown to 320+ events across 50+ countries on all seven continents.

## Retrospective

### What Worked
- The safety considerations table explicitly addresses the Russia/Ukraine framing, Soviet vs. Russian attribution, and the STS-1 date coincidence -- demonstrating careful sensitivity handling for a topic with active geopolitical context
- Connecting Gagarin's call sign "Kedr" (cedar) to the project's own muse naming creates a subtle but genuine resonance within the series
- The organizational history from SGAC to SpaceKind Foundation provides a useful case study in how grassroots space advocacy organizations scale

### What Could Be Better
- The connection to commercial spaceflight (SpaceX, Blue Origin, Virgin Galactic) and their impact on the Yuri's Night event ecosystem could be explored further
- Audio/video archives from Gagarin's actual flight communications are referenced but not deeply cataloged

## Lessons Learned

- The April 12 date alignment between Vostok 1 (1961) and STS-1 (1981) was genuinely unplanned -- an IBM System/4 Pi AP-101 timing skew caused a launch scrub on April 10, pushing STS-1 to the 12th by technical accident.
- Yuri's Night demonstrates that a shared calendar date can become institutional infrastructure -- from informal parties to UN resolution in 12 years (1999-2011), with no central budget or mandatory participation.
- The SpaceKind Foundation's navigation of the 2022 Ukraine invasion (honoring Gagarin as a human symbol while acknowledging geopolitical reality) is a real-world case study in how cultural organizations handle contested heritage.
