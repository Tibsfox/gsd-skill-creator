## Summary

The engineering lineage is what outlasts the mission. On July 17, 1967, at two minutes and forty seconds past two o'clock in the morning Universal Time, the radio from Surveyor 4 stopped. The spacecraft had been two and a half minutes from a soft landing at Sinus Medii, the Central Bay of the Moon. The main retrorocket had been firing for forty-one seconds. The vernier engines had been performing nominally. The solar panels were producing nominal power. The telemetry had been nominal. And then it stopped. At Hughes Aircraft Company in El Segundo, California, and at Jet Propulsion Laboratory in Pasadena, and at the Tidbinbilla tracking station in Australia, and at Honeysuckle Creek, engineers listened to silence. The failure mode was never conclusively determined. Fifty-three days later Surveyor 5 launched from the same pad &mdash; Launch Complex 36B at Cape Canaveral &mdash; with the same hardware and landed successfully in Mare Tranquillitatis, conducting the first lunar alpha-scattering chemistry experiment. The engineering lineage of the Surveyor program &mdash; the institutional knowledge transmitted across a team of engineers from senior to junior, from mission to mission, from one failed spacecraft to its successor &mdash; continued. In the same way, at the same time in the year 1967, the American unmanned lunar reconnaissance proceeded continuously through the shadow period of Apollo 1, while the crewed program itself remained grounded for redesign. Two programs paired in parallel, running at opposite tempos. Robert J. Parks at JPL and Benjamin Milwitzky at NASA Headquarters held the engineering lineage together across the Surveyor 4 loss, both sides of the transmission holding the line.

Forty-nine years later, approximately to the week, on July 9, 2016, in Anacortes, Washington, Geneviève Castrée Elverum died of pancreatic cancer at age thirty-five. She had been diagnosed in 2015. She was Canadian, born in France and raised in Quebec, a cartoonist and illustrator and musician who had recorded eight albums under the names Woelv and Ô Paon and drawn the graphic novel *Susceptible*. She had met Phil Elverum in 2008 and married him in Anacortes, the small island town on Fidalgo Island in Skagit County, Washington, where Elverum had returned from Olympia to build his own record label, P.W. Elverum & Sun, Limited. Six weeks after her death Phil Elverum began to record in the room where she had died, using her instruments. The album was called *A Crow Looked at Me*. It was released on March 24, 2017, to a 9.0 Best New Music from Pitchfork and a Metacritic aggregate of 93 across twenty-four critic reviews. The lyrics did not transform the death into symbol. They named the chair. They named the backpack. They named the mail that arrived after her death with her things in it. Most grief-songs compress an experience into metaphor: the bird as soul, the empty room as absence, the ashes scattered as peace. The album refused the compression. It accepted the larger representation as the price of the fidelity. The follow-up, *Now Only*, was released on March 16, 2018. *The Atlantic* described it as &ldquo;a progression from *A Crow Looked at Me*, not an experience of total sadness, featuring flashes of irony, hope, and love.&rdquo; The grief arc continued. The register widened. *Lost Wisdom pt. 2*, a 2019 collaboration with Julie Doiron, followed. *Microphones in 2020*, a single forty-five-minute track returning to Elverum's earlier project name, came out in August 2020. *Night Palace*, his most recent album, was released in November 2024. The label continues to ship from Anacortes. The daughter Agathe is being raised. The recording project, which began in 1997 as The Microphones on K Records out of Olympia and transitioned in 2003 to Mount Eerie on P.W. Elverum & Sun, remains active thirty years after its first album. The lineage of the household did not end with one of its makers. The Jurado-Fleet-Foxes-Earl producer-friend web established at degrees 45, 53, 58 in the engine's accumulation now extends to Elverum at degree 59, the same way the Surveyor engineering team extended forward into Pioneer Venus. Both lineages run in parallel at opposite scales, each mirror of the other at its own tempo.

In the Salish Sea in the fall of 2016 &mdash; the same year Geneviève Castrée died &mdash; Granny disappeared from observation. She was the oldest southern resident orca on photo-identification record. She had been catalogued at the start of the Center for Whale Research census in 1976 as an already-adult female and assigned the identifier J2. Her age estimate, derived retroactively from reproductive-timing analysis of her descendants, placed her between eighty and one hundred and five years old in the mid-2010s. The scientists who had observed her continuously for forty years called her Granny. She had led J pod through summer after summer, through the Chinook salmon runs in the Strait of Georgia and Haro Strait and Puget Sound, through the superpod gatherings when J, K, and L pods would sometimes come together. Her matriline's discrete calls &mdash; the stereotyped one-to-two-second tonal vocalizations that John Ford had catalogued in 1987 as S1, S2, S3, S4, S5, S8, S10, S16, S17, S18, S22, and others &mdash; were the calls of J pod because the pod descended from her matrilineal line. She disappeared from observation in October 2016. The Center for Whale Research made the determination, after weeks of repeated non-sightings at encounter locations where J pod reliably appeared, that Granny was deceased. Her body was never recovered. Her passing was announced in early 2017. Her calls did not die with her. In 2025, nine years after her disappearance, J pod has twenty-seven individuals per the Center for Whale Research July 2025 census &mdash; a slight increase from twenty-five at July 2024, with two surviving Christmas calves born in December 2024 and a female calf born in April 2025. Each of them can produce calls S1, S4, S10, S16 &mdash; the J pod signature repertoire. Each of them learned those calls during development, in the two-thousands or two-thousand-tens, by copying their mothers and matrilineal relatives, many of whom had copied Granny or Granny's daughters. The acoustic signature of J pod in 2025 is the accumulation of multiple generations of matrilineal copying, of which Granny's specific contribution is one threadline among many. When a hydrophone records J pod today, it records Granny's calls, transmitted forward through the lineage, preserved by the copying mechanism that stores culture in conspecific behavior instead of genetic sequence. The same way Geneviève Castrée's influence persists in Phil Elverum's recording technique &mdash; the production aesthetic, the tracking approach, the arrangement instincts that they developed together &mdash; Granny's influence persists in J pod's acoustic signature. Both are lineages that outlasted the individual who originated them.

These three instances are not the same event. Surveyor 4 is a spacecraft that failed under unclear circumstances and whose engineering program continued on the same cadence. Phil Elverum is a person who lost his wife and kept making records. J pod is a matriline that lost its senior matriarch and kept producing the calls she had contributed to during her life. The scales are different. The media are different. The mechanisms are different. But the structural axis is the same. In each case, a carrier &mdash; a spacecraft, a person, an individual animal &mdash; is lost, and what persists is the transmitted practice: the engineering knowledge in the team, the recording-project method in the artist, the matrilineal call-repertoire in the pod. The carrier is fragile. The lineage, when it has been built and transmitted before the carrier is lost, survives. Aristotle, in the *Nicomachean Ethics*, distinguished between *poiesis*, which is making, and *praxis*, which is doing. A *poiesis* is an object &mdash; the spacecraft, the record, the dorsal fin. A *praxis* is an action &mdash; the engineering, the recording, the call. The *poiesis* can be lost. The *praxis*, when it has been passed to other practitioners, continues. Surveyor 4 is gone; the Hughes-and-JPL Surveyor-program engineering *praxis* continued to Surveyor 5 and onward to Viking and to every subsequent three-leg soft-lander. Geneviève Castrée is gone; the Mount Eerie recording *praxis* continued to *Now Only* and onward. Granny is gone; the J pod matrilineal-dialect *praxis* continues in the twenty-seven orcas who carry her calls. The engineering lineage, the artistic project, the matrilineal dialect &mdash; three different names for what they share.

## Part A: Mount Eerie -- THE ENGINEERING LINEAGE

- **Anacortes refuge and the Fidalgo Island anchor.** Mount Eerie operates out of Anacortes, Washington, on Fidalgo Island in Skagit County — approximately 77 miles north of Seattle, west of the Skagit River delta, at the eastern edge of the San Juan Archipelago. Population approximately 17,000. The project is named after Mount Erie (1,273 ft, the highest point on Fidalgo Island, named 1841 by the United States Exploring Expedition under Charles Wilkes for the Battle of Lake Erie) with a deliberate homophone-spelling variant. This is the first Anacortes anchor, first Fidalgo Island anchor, and first Skagit County anchor in the S36 engine; the first non-Seattle-metropolitan Washington anchor in 59 engine degrees; the first island-based S36 artist.
- **The Microphones to Mount Eerie career arc.** Phil Elverum's recording project began in 1997 as The Microphones on K Records (Olympia, Washington) under Calvin Johnson. The Microphones period produced *Don't Wake Me Up* (1999), *It Was Hot, We Stayed in the Water* (2000), *The Glow pt. 2* (2001), and *Mount Eerie* (2003) — the fourth Microphones album serving as the transition point. In 2003 Elverum transitioned to the Mount Eerie project name and established his own label, P.W. Elverum & Sun, Ltd., in Anacortes. The Mount Eerie catalogue now spans two decades: *No Flashlight* (2005), *Lost Wisdom* (2008, with Julie Doiron), *Wind's Poem* (2009), *Clear Moon* (2012), *A Crow Looked at Me* (2017), *Now Only* (2018), *Lost Wisdom pt. 2* (2019), *Microphones in 2020* (2020), and *Night Palace* (2024).
- **Geneviève Castrée and the biographical hinge of 2016.** Geneviève Elverum (née Gosselin, known by pen name Geneviève Castrée) was a Canadian cartoonist, illustrator, and musician from Quebec, born April 9, 1981 in Quimper, France. She recorded eight albums under the names Woelv and Ô Paon and produced graphic-novel work including *Susceptible* (2013). She married Phil Elverum in 2008 in Anacortes. Their daughter Agathe was born in 2015. Castrée was diagnosed with pancreatic cancer in 2015, eleven months after Agathe's birth, and died at home on July 9, 2016, age 35. The 2015 diagnosis and 2016 death is the biographical hinge of Phil Elverum's subsequent work.
- **A Crow Looked at Me (2017) and the refusal of metaphor.** Six weeks after Castrée's death, Elverum began recording what became *A Crow Looked at Me*. The album was recorded in the room where Castrée died, using (mostly) her instruments, over a six-week period. It was released on March 24, 2017. Pitchfork awarded it 9.0 out of 10 and Best New Music. Metacritic aggregated a 93 across 24 critic reviews. The album refuses the grief-songwriting convention of metaphor: lyrics name specific objects (the chair, the backpack, the closet) and specific moments (packing up the art studio, receiving her belongings in the mail, watching their daughter grow up without her mother) that most songwriters would transform into symbol. The album accepts the larger representation as the price of the fidelity.
- **Now Only (2018) and the widening register.** *Now Only* was released on March 16, 2018, as the direct follow-up to *A Crow Looked at Me*. *The Atlantic* described it as "a progression from A Crow Looked at Me, not an experience of total sadness, featuring flashes of irony, hope, and love." ## Part B: Southern Resident Killer Whale -- THE MATRILINEAL DIALECT

- **First marine species in 59 engine degrees — closes critical gap.** *Orcinus orca* (resident-ecotype subspecies candidate *O. orca ater* per Morin et al. 2024) is the first marine species in the 59-degree engine history. This closes the 0/58 marine gap flagged as CRITICAL in the v1.58 handoff at two-quartile boundary crossing. Closed at degree 59 with 9 degrees of margin before the two-quartile deadline of degree 68. FIRST MARINE SPECIES. First marine mammal. First cetacean. First critically-endangered species (ESA Endangered November 2005). First species with documented post-reproductive matriarch life-history pattern. Five first-instance declarations from a single species entry.
- **J, K, L pods and the J-Clan single-clan structure.** The SRKW community consists of three pods: J pod with 27 individuals, K pod with 14 individuals, L pod with 33 individuals (July 2025 Center for Whale Research census total 74). All three pods share a common acoustic clan — J-Clan — evidenced by overlapping discrete-call repertoires at the clan level. Northern Resident Killer Whales (NRKW) include three clans (A, G, R) across approximately 13 pods; Bigg's (transient) killer whales use a separate T-series call catalogue. SRKW's single-clan structure makes it the most genetically and acoustically bottlenecked known killer whale population.
- **Matrilineal social structure — sons stay with natal matriline.** A matriline is a female SRKW and all of her direct descendants — offspring of both sexes, daughters' offspring, and further generations. Critically, sons do NOT leave the natal matriline at sexual maturity. A male SRKW remains with his mother and matrilineal relatives for his entire life. He mates with females from other matrilines (typically outside the pod to avoid close-kin mating) but returns after mating. Matriline membership is for-life. This is structurally unusual among mammalian social systems and is characteristic of resident-ecotype killer whales.
- **Post-reproductive matriarch life-history — rarest mammalian pattern.** Female SRKW can live 30+ years past their last calf — a post-reproductive lifespan pattern shared with only two other mammalian species: humans (*Homo sapiens*) and short-finned pilot whales (*Globicephala macrorhynchus*). The grandmother hypothesis explains this: post-reproductive females increase descendant fitness through ecological knowledge transfer (foraging routes, seasonal prey patterns, social coordination), and the kin-selection benefit exceeds the alternative fitness cost of non-reproducing lifespan. Adult sons of deceased mothers suffer elevated mortality in the year following mother death, a measurable effect of matriarchal social function.
- **Granny (J2) as reference matriarch.** Granny, catalogued as J2 by the Center for Whale Research, was the oldest SRKW on photo-identification record. First catalogued in 1976 at already-adult age; age estimates from reproductive-timing analysis place her between 80 and 105 years old at death in late 2016. She led J pod through summer after summer, through the Chinook salmon runs in the Strait of Georgia and Haro Strait and Puget Sound, through the superpod gatherings when J, K, and L pods would sometimes come together. Her matriline's discrete calls — the stereotyped vocalizations Ford catalogued in 1987 — were the calls of J pod because the pod descended from her matrilineal line.
- **Discrete-call repertoire (Ford 1987) and cultural transmission (Ford 1991).** J pod catalogued discrete calls include S1, S2, S3, S4, S5, S8, S10, S16, S17, S18, S22, S24 per John K. B. Ford's 1987 Canadian Data Report catalogue. Each call is a 1-2 second tonal vocalization with distinct harmonic structure. Calf SRKW acquire their discrete-call repertoire through matrilineal cultural transmission during development: the calf copies calls of its mother and matrilineal relatives; it does NOT copy the father's calls (who belongs to a different matriline) or unrelated adults' calls. Ford 1991 (Canadian Journal of Zoology 69:1454-1483) established that call-repertoire similarity between pods correlates with matrilineal-ancestry distance. Call repertoires are stable over decades within a matriline.
- **Chinook salmon dependence and the conservation crisis.** SRKW diet is approximately 80% Chinook salmon (*Oncorhynchus tshawytscha*) in summer Salish Sea. The near-monospecific dependence creates a critical conservation vulnerability: SRKW body condition and reproductive success track Chinook abundance. Chinook populations across the Pacific Northwest have declined dramatically through the 20th century due to dam construction, hydrosystem operations, habitat loss, ocean conditions, and fishery harvest. The SRKW community has not recovered to the pre-live-capture-era baseline (140-200 individuals) despite the live-capture era ending in 1977; other Pacific Northwest orca ecotypes (NRKW, Bigg's) have grown substantially in the post-capture era, but SRKW has not.
- **Cultural-transmission axis second exemplar with v1.58 Bewick's Wren.** The cultural-transmission axis opened at degree 58 with Bewick's Wren (neighborhood-scale dialect, horizontal male-copying, single-generation stability) is extended at degree 59 with SRKW (matriline-scale dialect, vertical matrilineal transmission, multi-decade stability). Two species, two taxa (Troglodytidae avian + Delphinidae cetacean), two transmission mechanisms, one shared axis. The axis is now beyond single-exemplar status and is a first-class chain pattern. Future candidates: humpback whale song culture, chimpanzee tool use, parrot vocal learning.
- **The Matrilineal Dialect as acoustic element.** The fifty-ninth acoustic element on the species side is The Matrilineal Dialect: discrete-call repertoire that persists matrilineally across decades of individual matriarch death. Each SRKW carries the calls of its mother and matrilineal relatives; when a matriarch dies, her calls do not die with her — they were already transmitted to her descendants during their development. The acoustic signature of J pod in 2025 is the accumulation of multiple generations of matrilineal copying; Granny's calls persist in the pod nine years after her disappearance. The matrilineal dialect is the acoustic equivalent of the engineering lineage at Hughes Aircraft and JPL: the practice that outlasts the carrier.

## Lessons Learned

1. **First-hard-gated-forward-degree operational discipline differs from retro-pass walker cadence.** Retro-pass work benefits from parallel subagent fleets across multiple missions simultaneously; forward-build at hard-gate cadence benefits from sequential single-mission focus where each artifact is verified against the spine before the next one begins. v1.60+ should follow the v1.59 template of single-context build rather than reverting to retro-pass parallel patterns.

2. **Inline governance bump is the correct v1.4-era pattern.** CHAIN-CONVENTIONS extensions accumulated during the W5 wave were ready to ship at v1.58 but were deferred inline to v1.59 per user direction. This paid off: v1.59 declarations reference stable governance, and the v1.4 bump landed on disk as a precondition for the mission ship rather than as a post-hoc patch.

3. **Multi-dimension first-instance declarations benefit from an explicit format.** v1.59 carries five first-instance declarations (marine species, marine mammal, cetacean, critically-endangered, Anacortes/non-Seattle WA). A canonical format for packaging multiple first-instances into a single header is a v1.5 CHAIN-CONVENTIONS candidate — the MULTI-DIMENSION-FIRST-INSTANCE sub-form.

4. **CHANNEL-PARALLELISM is the right alignment for multi-entity topologies.** The three-parallel-entity topology of v1.59 (Surveyor 4 + Mount Eerie + SRKW) maps cleanly to CHANNEL-PARALLELISM. Future multi-entity missions (Mariner 6+7, Apollo CSM+LM, Viking 1+2) should plan for CHANNEL-PARALLELISM with v1.58 and v1.59 as founding exemplars.

5. **Cultural-transmission axis is now beyond single-exemplar.** Two species, two taxa, two mechanisms, one shared axis. Future species candidates where social learning is the primary mode (humpback whale song culture, chimpanzee tool use, parrot vocalizations) can cite v1.58 + v1.59 as the axis founders.

6. **Sourced-fact discipline under hard gates requires primary-source verification budget.** Several `confidence:to-verify` flags remain in v1.59 documentation (Ford 1987 exact publication details; Morin et al. 2024 DOI; Phil Elverum birth year; Mariner 5 / Surveyor program-level personnel cross-references). Future ships should budget primary-source resolution time for each flagged claim before it accumulates.

7. **Forest-module-api amendment-5 SOCIAL-LEARNING-DRIVEN DSP class gained second exemplar.** Scheduled for formalization at harness v1.1.0 minor bump. The v1.59 orca-matriline DSP matches the v1.58 bewicks-wren DSP authoring pattern — primary knob is a cultural-transmission coefficient; acoustic contour is derived from social-input cardinality. Third exemplar or v1.60+ scheduled review would trigger the bump.

8. **Surveyor-program engineering-lineage framing validated the thematic motif.** The "engineering lineage survives the loss of the individual" theme holds at three independent scales (aerospace / personal / ecological).
