# Chapter 00 — Summary (v1.49.564 Degree 59)

v1.59 ships the fifty-ninth paired release under the post-reorder NASA chronological numbering and the FIRST HARD-GATED FORWARD DEGREE per NASA-DEGREE-CANONICAL §13. Three parallel entities encode a single theme at divergent scales: Surveyor 4 (aerospace / institutional), Mount Eerie / Phil Elverum (personal / artistic), Southern Resident Killer Whale (ecological / cultural-transmission).

**Surveyor 4** was launched July 14, 1967 on Atlas-Centaur AC-11 from LC-36B at Cape Canaveral. Telemetry ceased abruptly at 02:02:40 UT on July 17, 1967 — 41 seconds into the Thiokol TE-364-1 main retrorocket burn, 2.5 minutes before planned touchdown at Sinus Medii. The failure was never conclusively diagnosed. Fifty-three days later Surveyor 5 launched from the same pad with identical hardware and succeeded, conducting the first lunar alpha-scattering chemistry experiment. The engineering lineage of the Surveyor program survived its second loss.

**Mount Eerie** is the musical project of Phil Elverum from Anacortes, Washington. His wife Geneviève Castrée died of pancreatic cancer on July 9, 2016. Six weeks later he began recording *A Crow Looked at Me* (March 2017, Pitchfork 9.0 Best New Music, Metacritic 93) in the room where she died. The project continued: *Now Only* (2018), *Lost Wisdom pt. 2* (2019), *Microphones in 2020* (2020), *Night Palace* (2024). First Anacortes / Fidalgo Island / Skagit County anchor in the S36 engine; first non-Seattle-metro Washington anchor.

**Southern Resident Killer Whale** (*Orcinus orca*, resident-ecotype candidate subspecies *O. orca ater*) is the first marine species in 59 engine degrees, closing the critical 0/58 marine gap with 9 degrees of margin. 74 individuals per Center for Whale Research July 2025 census (27 J + 14 K + 33 L). J-Clan single-clan structure. Discrete-call repertoire catalogued by Ford 1987; matrilineal cultural transmission per Ford 1991. Granny (J2, ~80-105 years at death in late 2016) was the oldest documented SRKW matriarch; her calls persist in the J pod matrilineal dialect nine years later.
