# Lessons — v1.49.1063

1. **A sustain keeps the predecessor's axis vocabulary and replaces only the predecessor-specific content.**
   MGS shares the Mars-exploration vocabulary with the MAVEN predecessor (Mars, the red planet, the surface, the atmosphere, dust, in situ, planetary exploration, crust, topography, mineralogy) — that vocabulary is shared and correct. The rewrite removed only the MAVEN-specific content (the MAVEN name, the Mars Scout Program, Bruce Jakosky, LASP, the upper-atmosphere / ionosphere / atmospheric-escape anchors, the solar-wind stripping, the persistent metal-ion layers, the aurora, SWIA/SWEA/STATIC/SEP/LPW/IUVS/NGIMS, Deep Dip, the Atlas V 401, the 2013/2014 dates, and the Violet-green Swallow + Showy Milkweed pairing) and replaced it with MGS content.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12344_

2. **A sustain has no axis rotation to frame — and 1.245 was itself a sustain, so there is no rotation framing to remove.**
   The Mars axis opened at 1.238 (Viking, substrate-axis rotation #36); 1.239 through 1.246 are all SUSTAINS, so the clone source carries no rotation narrative, and at most a single distant note records that the Mars axis itself opened at 1.238 by rotating from the X-ray-astronomy axis (rotation #35, held complete at obs#15).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12345_

3. **MGS is the first FULLY SUCCESSFUL U.S./NASA Mars orbiter since Viking — never "the first Mars orbiter.**
   Mariner 9 was the first spacecraft to orbit another planet, the Soviet Phobos 2 orbited Mars in 1989 before failing, and Mars Observer never reached orbit; the catalog scopes MGS as the first fully successful U.S./NASA orbiter since Viking (recovering the 1993 Mars Observer loss) and never re-claims Mariner 9's first-to-orbit.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12346_

4. **The topography first is the first global TOPOGRAPHIC / elevation map — distinct from Mariner 9's IMAGE map.**
   MOLA measured precise elevation and relief by laser ranging, quantifying the hemispheric dichotomy, the depth of Hellas, and the heights of Olympus Mons and Tharsis; the catalog keeps "topographic / elevation" and never writes "first global map of Mars," which belongs to Mariner 9's first global image map.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12347_

5. **The magnetism result is the discovery of an ancient, now-extinct global field — not a present-day field.**
   MAG/ER found remanent fossil banding in the ancient crust; the catalog scopes this as evidence of a global field once generated by an early core dynamo, now extinct, notes that Mars has no global field today, and presents the early-plate-tectonic-like reading as an interpretation where mentioned.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12348_

6. **The TES Meridiani-hematite note is a forward cross-link that GUIDED a rover landing site — not a re-stamp.**
   TES produced the first global thermal-IR mineral survey and found the Meridiani hematite; the catalog presents the hematite as the deposit that later guided the choice of Opportunity's landing site (MGS/TES 1999 preceded the Mars Exploration Rover Opportunity in 2004) and never re-claims the Mars Exploration Rovers' (obs#4) in-situ mineral-water-evidence first.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12349_

7. **Aerobraking is MGS's orbit-attainment technique, never an anchor.**
   MGS used aerobraking to shrink and circularize its orbit into the mapping orbit; the catalog mentions it only as the technique and never anchors "first aerobraking," because Magellan demonstrated aerobraking at Venus earlier.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12350_

8. **The mission's end is framed positively and mentioned once at low density.**
   MGS operated for about ten years — the longest-serving Mars spacecraft of its era at the time; contact was lost in November 2006 and NASA ended recovery efforts in January 2007. The catalog states this once and frames the mission positively as a long, successful mapping mission, with no failure / loss dwelling.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12351_

9. **A sustain references its predecessor exactly once, deliberately.**
   The only correct predecessor reference is a single nav-prev lineage note to v1.245 MAVEN (Mars obs#8), plus lineage references to InSight (1.244, obs#7), Perseverance (1.243, obs#6), Curiosity (1.242, obs#5), the Mars Exploration Rovers (1.241, obs#4), Mars Pathfinder (1.240, obs#3), Mariner 9 (1.239, obs#2), Viking (1.238, obs#1), and Mariner 4 (1.36, the first Mars flyby). The predecessors' anchors stay with them — 0 re-stamps.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12352_

10. **Clone source is MAVEN (a NASA upper-atmosphere orbiter, Mars Scout, 2013–2025); MGS is a NASA mapping orbiter (JPL / Lockheed Martin, 1996–2006).**
   The most structural replacement in the rewrite is removing ALL MAVEN-specific content — "MAVEN," "Mars Scout Program," "Bruce Jakosky," "LASP," the upper-atmosphere / ionosphere / atmospheric-escape anchors, the solar-wind stripping, the persistent metal-ion layers, the aurora, "SWIA/SWEA/STATIC/SEP/LPW/IUVS/NGIMS," "Deep Dip," "Atlas V 401," the 2013/2014 dates, and the Violet-green Swallow + Showy Milkweed pairing — and replacing it with Mars Global Surveyor, the recovery of the 1993 Mars Observer loss, the Delta II 7925 launch from Cape Canaveral SLC-17A (7 November 1996), the 12 September 1997 Mars orbit insertion, the aerobraking into the Sun-synchronous mapping orbit, the instruments MOC, MOLA, TES, and MAG/ER, the first precise global topographic map, the ancient crustal magnetism…
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12353_

11. **The shader renders procedural geometry rather than archived mission imagery.**
   The MGS shader uses analytic curves and procedural noise rather than loading actual mission data or MOLA topography. A future revision could render a real MOLA global elevation grid or a TES mineral map (publicly available from the Planetary Data System) for a higher-fidelity view.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12354_

12. **The MGS diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the orbiter with its solar panels, its high-gain antenna, and the nadir-pointing instrument deck.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12355_

13. **The relief-mapping mode is depicted rather than driven by real altimetry.**
   The topographic relief in the mapping mode is depicted but not rendered from the archived MOLA elevation grid; a future ship could drive the mode from the real gridded topography for a quantitatively faithful reading of the hemispheric dichotomy, Hellas, and Olympus Mons.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12356_

14. **The fossil-magnetism mode is depicted rather than computed.**
   The remanent magnetic bands in the third mode are depicted rather than rendered from the published MAG/ER crustal-field maps; a future ship could drive the mode from the real measured magnetic-anomaly maps for a quantitatively faithful view.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12357_
