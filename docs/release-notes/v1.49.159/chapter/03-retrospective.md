## Lessons Learned

1. **The church can function as a conservatory without institutional infrastructure.** Stone's church training provided the two essential elements of formal music education: vocal technique (thousands of hours of singing) and audience comfort (leading worship services). What it did not provide -- theory, peer musicians, professional networks -- Stone built himself. The lesson: institutional music training is one pathway, not the only pathway. The engine should track alternative training pipelines at all subsequent degrees.

2. **The independent label is the sweet spot for soul music.** Stone's career spans self-released, independent (ATO), and major (Capitol). He chose the independent model twice. The major label wanted hits; the soul singer wanted authenticity. The lesson: track label affiliation at all subsequent degrees. Compare independent vs. major models when data permits. ATO's amplifier-not-director model is now the engine's baseline for healthy label relationships.

3. **Internet-mediated career ignition changes the velocity equation.** A viral YouTube video compressed what previously took years of live performance into months. The lesson: track whether subsequent artists show internet-mediated careers. The distinction between pre-internet and post-internet career trajectories may be the engine's most important temporal variable.

4. **Rural origins add a dimension that urban-only analysis cannot capture.** The concept of "the scene" that drove 22 prior degrees does not exist in Chewelah. Stone's trajectory forces the engine to model what happens when there IS no scene -- when the artist must assemble everything from recordings, in isolation, with no institutional support. The lesson: rural origin is not an absence of data; it is a different kind of data.

5. **Acoustic convergence across families is a testable prediction.** The grosbeak sounds like the robin. When the robin arrives at degree 25, the engine can compare them directly -- same acoustic solution, different evolutionary paths. The lesson: the convergence pair concept generates hypotheses that future degrees can test. Track all cross-family vocal resemblances.

6. **Both-sex singing challenges the male-dominated paradigm.** Every previous species with song had male-dominated or male-exclusive singing. The grosbeak breaks this. The lesson: the engine's behavioral vocabulary should not assume male-only song as default. Track female song at all subsequent degrees.

7. **The ADSR cycle's second iteration builds faster than the first.** The first cycle: silence (0) to cascade (1) to drone (2) -- slow accumulation. The second cycle: groove reset (22) to testimony (23) -- two degrees and already has rhythmic foundation plus semantic content. The acceleration maps to accumulated energy: three consecutive E=4 degrees provide momentum the first cycle's E=1-2 degrees did not have.

8. **Scene ecology continues to produce structural insights from consecutive degrees.** Degrees 18-23 (Maktub, FMR, Monge, Polyrhythmics, Foxy Apollo, Stone) now document six consecutive aspects of how a music scene operates: workshop, collective, individual, anchor, inheritor, rural arrival. The lesson: consecutive degrees in the same genre zone generate emergent structural analysis that no single degree can produce.

9. **The bill as dual-purpose instrument (ecological tool + acoustic resonator) is the most elegant morphological finding since the flicker pair.** The same anatomical structure that cracks seeds, processes fruit, and ejects cowbird eggs also shapes the spectral character of the song. The lesson: form follows function follows form. Track dual-function anatomy at all subsequent degrees.

10. **Source depth recovers with career maturity.** 32 sources at degree 23 vs. 21 at degree 22. The emerging-artist documentation gap identified at degree 22 was structural, not universal. Labeled, nationally-touring artists generate more press. The lesson: source depth is itself a variable that tracks career stage. Document it explicitly.
