## Lessons Learned

1. **Subsidiary-label registers warrant first-class documentation distinct from parent-label main-catalog registers.** The degree 282 documentation distinguishes **Hardly Art** (the 2007-founded **Sub Pop** subsidiary) from the Sub Pop main catalog rather than conflating both into a single Sub Pop institutional entry. The lesson is that subsidiary labels operate on distinct curatorial and aesthetic axes from their parent labels — even when the subsidiary shares distribution infrastructure and institutional connection with the parent — and the subsidiary's roster, founding context, and curatorial identity warrant first-class documentation resolution rather than being aggregated into the parent label's coverage. The resulting institutional record is richer for preserving the distinction.
2. **Humor as political-delivery-mechanism is distinct from humor as presentation-decoration, and the distinction warrants first-class register recognition.** The degree 282 **feminist-humor-as-competency** register documents Tacocat's humor as a specific developed skill that delivers the politics rather than as decoration layered on top of already-articulated politics. The lesson is that humor in politically committed music can operate at either the delivery-mechanism level (where the humor carries the political content into the listener's reception) or at the decoration level (where the humor accompanies already-delivered political content), and these are structurally distinct registers rather than equivalent variations. Documentation should identify which register is operating when humor is present in politically committed music, because the two registers have different structural consequences for how the music works and for how the politics land.
3. **Consecutive-degree personnel bridges are a distinct cross-degree-relationship type from artifact-level cross-degree connections, and both warrant first-class documentation.** The degree 282 **Tacocat-Childbirth McKenna personnel bridge** is the engine's first musician-level consecutive-degree cross-degree link, extending the cross-degree-relationship vocabulary beyond the artifact-level connections (such as the *¡Viva Zapata!* title at degree 279 bridging to Mia Zapata at degree 280) into personnel-level connections. The lesson is that cross-degree relationships can operate at multiple structural levels — artifact (album title, song lyric, dedication), personnel (shared musicians), institutional (shared labels or venues), geographic (shared neighborhoods), temporal (shared time periods) — and the engine's documentation should track each level's cross-degree relationships at first-class resolution rather than collapsing them into a single cross-degree-relationship category.
4. **Family-return registers after single-species arcs extend family documentation to internal-range representatives at first-class resolution.** The degree 282 **Delphinidae return register** documents the family through *Lagenorhynchus obliquidens* after the seven-degree **Orca Arc** (*Orcinus orca* only, degrees 270-276) and the **Phocoenidae** excursion at **Dall's Porpoise** at degree 281. The lesson is that cetacean-family documentation can extend to multiple species at first-class resolution when the family's behavioral and ecological range is broad enough to support distinct register-documentation for different representatives; the Orca Arc documented Delphinidae through a single species at its maximum complexity, and the Pacific White-Sided Dolphin Pod at degree 282 documents the family through a distinct mid-size acrobatic-social representative. Family-level documentation should match the number and selection of representative species to the family's internal range rather than to a uniform single-species standard.
5. **Compound acoustic signatures combining multiple components warrant first-class register recognition when the components together constitute the structural event.** The degree 282 **aerial-acrobatic + call-burst compound acoustic register** combines aerial-impact-splash percussive signatures with tripartite pod vocalization (whistles, echolocation clicks, burst-pulse calls) into a single compound acoustic event. The lesson is that compound acoustic signatures — where multiple distinct acoustic components together constitute the structural event rather than any single component alone — warrant first-class register recognition rather than being decomposed into separate single-component entries. The compound-signature-vocabulary precedent extends the engine's acoustic documentation beyond single-source maximum-force entries into coordinated multi-source registers where the compound is the event.
6. **Collective-generated E-values are structurally distinct from single-source E-values at the same numeric level, and the distinction warrants first-class cross-axis register recognition.** The degree 282 **collective exuberance at E=7** cross-axis register documents both halves of the release as group-generated E=7 events — Tacocat's four-piece hook-driven collective warmth, the Pacific White-Sided Dolphin pod's coordinated aerial display — rather than as single-source E=7 events. The lesson is that E-values at the same numeric level can be structurally distinct depending on whether the intensity is generated through group-level coordination or through single-source maximum performance, and the engine's documentation should track the single-source-vs-collective distinction at first-class register resolution rather than treating all E=7 entries as structurally equivalent.
7. **Warmth-and-humor registers after high-intensity cluster arcs are structurally productive as cluster-cooldown documentation.** The degree 282 Tacocat warmth-and-humor register follows the four-consecutive-E=8 riot-grrrl-cluster streak (degrees 276-279) and The Gits at E=8 at degree 280 and the Wimps sardonic E=6 at degree 281. The lesson is that cluster-cooldown documentation — where register intensity steps down from a high-intensity arc through transitional registers toward a distinct mode — is a structurally productive documentation pattern that the engine should register explicitly rather than letting the cooldown emerge implicitly. The Tacocat entry at degree 282 documents the warmth register after the riot-grrrl cluster's high-intensity arc, and the structural cooldown is a first-class documentation element rather than an incidental feature.
8. **Cross-axis registers should name the structural principle they document rather than simply pairing the A-side and B-side entries.** The degree 282 cross-sync axis — **collective exuberance at E=7** — names the structural principle that group-generated acoustic-and-visual energy is a first-class register distinct from single-source maximum force, and the engine's documentation uses this name to organize both halves of the release as matched demonstrations of the principle rather than as unrelated A-side/B-side pairings. The lesson is that cross-sync registers should name the principle that organizes the pairing rather than simply describing the pairing, because the naming-of-principle is what makes the cross-axis register legible as an engine-level structural documentation rather than as a release-level coincidence of pairing; the degree 282 cross-sync name (collective exuberance at E=7) extends the cross-sync-naming precedent established at degrees 279 (presence beyond perception) and earlier.
