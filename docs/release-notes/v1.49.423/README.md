**FIRST HARDLY ART. FEMINIST HUMOR. ACROBATICS.**

**Tacocat** arrive at E=7 -- the Seattle punk pop band formed in 2007 by Emily Nokes (vocals), Eric Randall (guitar), Lelah Maupin (drums), and Bree McKenna (bass). Tacocat have released four studio albums: *Shame Spiral* (2011, self-released), *NVM* (2014, Hardly Art), *Lost Time* (2016, Hardly Art), and *This Mess Is a Place* (2019, Sub Pop). Their catalog spans a decade of Seattle independent music, their sound moving from lo-fi punk pop to increasingly polished production without losing the core sensibility that distinguishes them: songs about the texture of contemporary life, delivered with feminist politics and genuine comedic intelligence.

Tacocat are funny. This requires acknowledgment because humor is not treated as a distinct musical competency, and Tacocat's humor is a specific, developed competency: the songs are funny in the way that requires timing, economy of language, and the willingness to commit fully to the premise. Emily Nokes' lyrics observe the specifics of contemporary experience -- period pain, internet culture, the mechanics of catcalling, the minor indignities of being a woman in a world that was not designed with women in mind -- with the deadpan precision of someone who has thought carefully about what is actually happening and chosen to describe it exactly as it is. The jokes land because the observations are accurate. The politics work because the jokes work.
