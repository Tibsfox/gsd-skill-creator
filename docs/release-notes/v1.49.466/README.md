**Love Battery** arrive at E=7 -- the Seattle heavy psych-grunge band formed in 1989 by Ron Nine (vocals, guitar), Kevin Whitworth (guitar), Kory Clarke (bass), and Jason Finn (drums), releasing *Between the Eyes* (1992) and *Dayglo* (1993) on Sub Pop and producing some of the label's most psychedelically inflected heavy guitar work: fuzz-dense, distortion-saturated grunge rooted in the heavy psych tradition of Blue Cheer and Stooges-lineage American rock, but filtered through the Pacific Northwest's characteristically wet and heavy production aesthetic. Love Battery occupied the Sub Pop roster's heavy psych niche -- heavier and more psychedelic than Mudhoney's punk-inflected energy, less metal-adjacent than TAD's blunt weight, positioned at the intersection of the Nuggets-era fuzz-psych tradition and the Seattle underground's early 1990s production sensibility.

**Salmon-bear-eagle convergence** arrives at E=8 on the SPS track -- the Pacific Northwest's most documented keystone species synthesis event: the simultaneous presence of Pacific salmon (*Oncorhynchus* spp.), black bear (*Ursus americanus*), and bald eagle (*Haliaeetus leucocephalus*) at a spawning stream during the fall salmon run, the event in which three of the Pacific Northwest's defining keystone species occupy the same acoustic space at the same time. The spawning salmon fill the stream with their acoustic signature -- the thrashing, splashing, and low-frequency hydraulic disturbance of thousands of fish in spawning behavior, their bodies deteriorating but their reproductive drive undiminished. The bears generate the acoustic signature of large-mammal salmon predation: the water entry, the splash and catch, the wet crunch of a successful strike, the low vocalizations of bears in close proximity competing for stream access. The eagles contribute aerial acoustics: the wingbeat signature of large raptors landing on streamside branches, the territorial calls of competing eagles, the sharp impact sounds of talons striking water on a strike attempt.

E=8 for the salmon-bear-eagle convergence is the correct assignment for the Pacific Northwest's emblematic three-species synthesis event. Each species is individually documented in the SPS track -- salmon are the riparian ecology foundation, bears are the apex omnivore, eagles are the raptor apex -- but their simultaneous convergence at the spawning stream produces a combined acoustic environment that no single species can generate alone. The convergence event is not merely three sounds playing simultaneously; it is three species in active ecological relationship, each acoustically present because of what the other two are doing. The salmon are there because the stream is their spawning ground. The bears are there because the salmon are there. The eagles are there because the bears and the salmon are there. The three acoustic signatures are ecologically entangled, not merely co-present.

**SYNTHESIS EVENT.** The salmon-bear-eagle convergence is marked as a synthesis event in the SPS track because it documents ecological function rather than isolated species behavior. The fall salmon run is the Pacific Northwest's most important nutrient cycle event: spawning salmon carry marine-derived nutrients (nitrogen, phosphorus, carbon from their time at sea) into freshwater riparian systems, where their decomposing bodies fertilize streamside vegetation. Bears accelerate this nutrient transfer by dragging salmon carcasses into the forest. Eagles further distribute remains. The acoustic event at the spawning stream is the sound of the Pacific Northwest's primary nutrient cycle in operation -- the marine-to-terrestrial transfer that sustains the old-growth forest ecosystem the engine has been documenting throughout the SPS track.
