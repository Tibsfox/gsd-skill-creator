## Summary

**FIRST CANIDAE. WOLF RECOVERY. E=9. 84.2%.**

**Brad** arrive at E=5 -- the Seattle post-grunge band formed in 1992 as a side project among Shawn Smith (vocals, keyboards), Stone Gossard (guitar, Pearl Jam), and Regan Hagar (drums, Malfunkshun). Brad's music occupied a distinctly different position from the bands their members came from: where Pearl Jam operated at arena scale and Malfunkshun had been proto-grunge with theatrical edge, Brad was quiet, soul-influenced, melodically precise, operating at the post-grunge register with an R&B undertow. Shawn Smith's voice was one of the most distinctive in the Seattle scene -- a high, pure tenor capable of sustained melodic expression that had no analog in the grunge vocal tradition -- and Brad's records built around that voice rather than around guitar distortion.

E=5 for Brad is the appropriate lower-register assignment for a band that was fundamentally not a rock band in the energy sense. The keyboard-forward, voice-forward compositions of *Shame* (1993) and subsequent records operate at the E=5 level of emotional directness and compositional quietness. Brad is the Seattle scene's most prominent evidence that the scene contained multitudes -- that Stone Gossard, who could play the riff behind "Even Flow," chose to build something measured and soulful in his side project. E=5 at degree 303, alongside the SPS track's first Canidae entry at E=9, is a deliberate contrast: the S36 track at its quietest in the final-sixth context, paired with the SPS track at maximum energy.

**Gray Wolf pack howl** arrives at E=9 on the SPS track -- *Canis lupus*, the gray wolf, producing the coordinated multi-voice pack howl that is the most ecologically and culturally resonant large predator vocalization in North America. The wolf howl is not a single voice but a harmonic structure: each wolf in the pack contributes a different frequency layer, the fundamental tones of the adults underpinning the higher harmonics of the yearlings, the whole assembly producing a chord that can sustain for several minutes, carrying up to ten kilometers across open terrain and through forested ridges. The pack howl is simultaneously a territorial declaration, a rallying call, a coordination signal, and an acoustic map of the pack's location and composition.

E=9 for the wolf pack howl is **FIRST CANIDAE** at maximum energy. The wolf is the Pacific Northwest's recovered apex predator: absent from Washington west of the Cascades for most of the twentieth century, returning via natural dispersal from Idaho and British Columbia beginning in the 2000s, with established packs now in the North Cascades, the Okanogan Highlands, and the Blue Mountains. The howl documented here is the multi-voice harmonic of a full pack -- not a single animal's territorial call but the coordinated acoustic output of a social predator announcing itself to the landscape. E=9 is confirmed by the multi-voice harmonic complexity, the ten-kilometer carry, and the pack's social acoustic function.

**THREE E=9 STREAK CONTINUES.** Cougar (302, Felidae) → Wolf (303, Canidae). Two consecutive E=9 entries across two distinct families. The streak has sustained through the shift from solitary apex predator to pack predator, from ambush hunter to pursuit hunter, from Felidae to Canidae. The wolf pack howl continues the ceiling established by the cougar scream from a completely different anatomical and social position. One more degree -- the grizzly at degree 304 -- will complete the three-family, three-degree E=9 sequence.

**WOLF RECOVERY IN WASHINGTON.** The wolf's return to Washington is one of the Pacific Northwest's most significant ecological events of the twenty-first century. The Lookout Pack, the Smackout Pack, the Teanaway Pack and others represent the leading edge of natural recolonization that has altered predator-prey dynamics, vegetation structure, and the acoustic environment of the North Cascades. The pack howl at degree 303 is not merely an acoustic entry but a documentation of recovery: the wolf's voice returning to the landscape after a century of absence.
