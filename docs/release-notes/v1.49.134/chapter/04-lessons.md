1. **The most consequential creative decisions often begin as accidents.**
   The Moody Blues were assigned to record a Dvorak symphony demonstration record for a stereo format promotion. They convinced their producer to let them record original material instead. Decca executives were not informed until the album was completed. That album, *Days of Future Passed*, founded progressive rock. The Amiga Principle in its purest form: constraint (stereo demo assignment) transformed into opportunity (genre-defining concept album) through architectural insight (the day-as-allegory structure was original material that served the demo purpose better than Dvorak would have).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #781_

2. **Engineering decisions create musical signatures.**
   Derek Varnals' removal of the tape-splice transient from the Mellotron's playback mechanism was not a musical decision -- it was a signal processing decision. But it created the "orchestral wave" sound that defined the Moody Blues' sonic identity and, through their influence, the entire progressive rock genre. The boundary between engineering and art is itself a threshold -- a *space between* where the Moody Blues consistently found their most important innovations.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #782_

3. **Three chart lives for a single song is a phenomenon that only FM radio could produce.**
   "Nights in White Satin" charted three separate times across twelve years (1967, 1972, 1979) without being re-recorded. The 1972 breakout originated organically from FM DJs in Seattle -- the same city that would later incubate grunge through the same independent-DJ/regional-breakout mechanism. The pattern is structural, not accidental: decentralized media gatekeepers discover material that centralized promotion misses.

5. **Critical reassessment takes exactly as long as it takes.**
   From "ponderous mound of thought-jello" (1967) to Rolling Stone essential albums list (2007) to Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (2018): 51 years. The work didn't change. The context did. FM radio made the recording audible. Time made the influence visible. Punk's rejection of prog was itself eventually reassessed. The Moody Blues' critical trajectory is a case study in how canonical status is earned -- not through immediate acclaim but through persistent influence that outlasts its detractors.
