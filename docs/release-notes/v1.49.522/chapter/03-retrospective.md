# Retrospective — v1.49.522

## Lessons Learned

1. **Godspeed -- the orbit and the groove.** Friendship 7 orbits Earth three times, four hours 55 minutes, on an Atlas LV-3B that was a pressurized steel balloon with walls thinner than a dime. Polyrhythmics' eight-piece instrumental funk locks a groove that sustains like orbital mechanics -- eight instruments as gravitational bodies holding each other in trajectory. Once Glenn's Atlas ignited, the options were orbit or ocean. Once the band locks into the pocket, breaking the groove is catastrophic. Orbit as groove, groove as orbit.

2. **First E=4 S36 and combined energy of 8.** Polyrhythmics deploys E=4 on the S36 track and the Northern Flicker deploys E=4 on the SPS track. Combined energy of 8 matches degree 1 (Frisell E=3 + Wren E=5) as the joint all-time maximum. The sustained E=3 zone (degrees 15-20, combined 6 for six straight degrees) breaks upward. Volume as territory -- funk demands volume, woodpeckers demand volume, Glenn's televised launch claimed the nation's attention through maximum-volume public broadcast.

3. **The large ensemble as fourth creative unit type.** Polyrhythmics introduces the large ensemble (eight pieces, no vocalist, groove as gravitational center) after solo (0-17), fixed band (18, Maktub), and collective (19, Fly Moon Royale). The organizational gradient -- solo → band → collective → large ensemble -- tracks increasing complexity, increasing personnel, and decreasing reliance on a single voice. The groove is the center.

4. **Fremont enters as the sixth Seattle neighborhood.** Polyrhythmics is rooted in Fremont, extending the geographic map beyond Central District, Queen Anne, Capitol Hill, Cornish/general, Pike Place Market area, and Rainier Valley. The Cornish pipeline extends to four members -- Priester (faculty), Caliman (faculty), Watts (student), Grant Schroff (graduate). Two faculty, one student, one graduate.

5. **The Mercury program completes across six degrees.** MR-1 four inches (16) → MR-2 Ham suborbital (17) → MA-5 Enos orbital (18) → Freedom 7 Shepard suborbital (19) → Liberty Bell 7 Grissom suborbital/sank (20) → Friendship 7 Glenn orbital (21). Six flights, six degrees, converging from false start to orbital success. Pioneer (degrees 1-5) needed five missions; Mercury needs six flights. The convergence pattern is fractal -- it repeats at different scales across different programs.

6. **Genre gradient -- Jazz increasingly secondary.** Jazz/Funk at degree 21 pushes Jazz further toward modifier status while Funk asserts primary identity. The genre trajectory documented through the late teens and into the twenties shows Jazz as the consistent modifier with different primary genres (Fusion, Comedy, Soul, Funk) asserting themselves in turn.

7. **Chinook salmon as total commitment -- burning itself to finish.** *Oncorhynchus tshawytscha*, the King, makes the longest Pacific salmon migration. Once in freshwater, it does not eat -- it burns muscle, fat, and bone calcium to complete the upstream swim. No return. Glenn's orbital commitment was identical (orbit or ocean once the Atlas lit). The groove's commitment is identical (lock or collapse once eight musicians enter the pocket). Three systems, three kinds of total commitment: biological (salmon), mechanical (Atlas), rhythmic (groove).
