## Surprises

- **One spacecraft left Earth faster than anything before it.** New Horizons launched on 19 January 2006 aboard an Atlas V 551 with a STAR 48B third stage, leaving Earth at the highest launch / Earth-departure speed ever achieved (about 58,000 km/h, roughly 36,000 mph relative to Earth) — fast enough to pass the Moon's distance in about nine hours, the fastest launch speed ever set.
- **A flyby probe returned the first close-up reconnaissance of Pluto.** On 14 July 2015 New Horizons flew about 7,800 km / 4,800 miles above Pluto's surface, returning the first close-up data on Pluto and its moons (Charon, Styx, Nix, Kerberos, and Hydra) — the first reconnaissance of the Pluto system, revealing the heart-shaped plain (Tombaugh Regio) and the icy mountains.
- **A spacecraft explored a small, pristine body at the far edge of the Kuiper Belt.** On 1 January 2019 New Horizons flew within about 3,500 km of Arrokoth (formerly 2014 MU69), at about 44 AU from the Sun — the first close flyby of a small, primordial cold-classical Kuiper Belt object and the most distant object ever explored up close, a two-lobed contact binary preserved from the early solar system.
- **The small moons of Pluto were found from Earth before the flyby, not by the spacecraft.** New Horizons characterized Charon, Styx, Nix, Kerberos, and Hydra up close, but Styx and Kerberos were discovered by the Hubble Space Telescope during the cruise (in 2012 and 2011), so the spacecraft characterized rather than discovered the small moons.
- **A swift tree bat travels far through the dark of the forest night.** The Silver-haired Bat (*Lasionycteris noctivagans*), a swift, agile tree bat of Pacific Northwest forests and a monotypic genus, migrates long distances and forages on the wing through the dark, roosting alone in loose bark, cavities, and foliage (behavioral / ecological natural history only) — a mirror of New Horizons reaching swiftly and far across the dark of the outer solar system.
- **A small cushion wildflower clings at the cold far upper edge of where plants can grow.** Tolmie's Saxifrage (*Micranthes tolmiei*), a small mat- or cushion-forming alpine wildflower of cold, rocky high Pacific Northwest summits, talus, and snowbeds (the Cascades and Olympics), holds the cold far upper edge of where plants can grow, with small white star-flowers and fleshy succulent leaves (ecological description only) — a mirror of New Horizons reaching the coldest, most distant small worlds at the frontier of the planetary system.

7. **New Horizons holds the fastest LAUNCH speed — not the fastest spacecraft ever.** It left Earth at the highest launch / Earth-departure speed ever achieved; the catalog scopes this STRICTLY to the launch speed and explicitly does NOT call it the fastest spacecraft ever (the Parker Solar Probe reaches far higher speeds via solar gravity assists).
