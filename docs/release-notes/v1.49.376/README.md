# v1.49.376 -- Degree 235: Heart (hard rock) + Turkey Vulture

**Release Date:** 2026-04-03
**Type:** Seattle 360 / Sound of Puget Sound -- Degree 235 of 360

## Summary

**Heart** returns at E=7 -- the second entry in the Heart pair, the harder-edged face of the same Bellevue band, and the highest S36 reading that Heart carries in the engine. Where degree 234 documented the classic rock Heart -- melodic, arena-polished, the catalog that sold 35 million copies -- degree 235 documents the hard rock Heart: the band that could play "Barracuda" like a demolition event, the band that preceded and outlasted the grunge era without losing its own identity, the band that Ann Wilson sang through with a force that most rock vocalists of any gender have never approached. The E=7 reading is the correct reading for this face of Heart: not the ballads, not "Alone," not the glossy mid-1980s production, but the band at its most physical, most raw, the version that influenced every Seattle rock musician who came after them. The **HEART PAIR** is complete: E=6 (classic, degree 234) → E=7 (hard rock, degree 235). The pair documents two legitimate readings of the same band, both accurate, neither complete without the other. The one-step E elevation maps a real distinction: Heart is a band whose catalog contains genuine range, and the engine acknowledges that range rather than collapsing it into a single data point. On the species side, the **Turkey Vulture** arrives at E=2 -- the **FIRST CATHARTIDAE** in the engine, the **near-minimum acoustic reading**, and the species that creates the **widest E-gap in the SPS track**. The Turkey Vulture (*Cathartes aura*) does not sing. It does not call. It does not communicate vocally in any meaningful sense. It lacks a true syrinx -- the vocal organ that gives birds their songs -- and what it produces instead is a hissing grunt: a low, breathy exhalation that is less a call and more evidence that the bird is present. E=2 is the lowest SPS reading that is not absolute silence. The Turkey Vulture is functionally a silent bird, a vast soaring presence above ridgelines and thermals, a bird that you see before you hear and that you may never hear at all. The gap between degree 234's American Kestrel (E=5) and degree 235's Turkey Vulture (E=2) is three E-units. The gap between degree 235's Turkey Vulture (E=2) and degree 234's Heart (E=7) in the S36 track is five E-units -- the **widest simultaneous E-gap** between the two tracks in the engine's history. Heart at maximum force, Turkey Vulture at near-silence, in the same degree: the engine has never before placed entries this far apart on the acoustic scale at the same position. The **FIRST CATHARTIDAE** entry also marks a family that stands in a different position from every raptor the engine has documented: the New World Vultures (*Cathartidae*) are not closely related to the Old World Vultures or to the hawks and falcons; they are an ancient lineage, and their ecological role is decomposition rather than predation.

## Key Features

- **Part A -- S36:** Heart (Hard Rock, E=7) -- HEART PAIR CLOSES, E=6→E=7 across pair, hard rock face, "Barracuda" register, Bellevue's hardest entry
- **Part B -- SPS:** Turkey Vulture (hissing grunt, E=2) -- FIRST CATHARTIDAE, near-minimum E, silent soarer, no true syrinx, widest E-gap
- WIDEST E-GAP -- degree 235 contains the largest simultaneous S36/SPS E-value spread in the engine's history: Heart at E=7 (S36) vs. Turkey Vulture at E=2 (SPS), a difference of 5; no previous degree has placed two entries this far apart on the acoustic scale simultaneously; the gap is not a measurement error but a structural reality: a hard rock band at maximum register paired with a bird that cannot sing; the engine has placed these two entries together, and the contrast is the story
- Turkey Vulture acoustics -- the absence of a syrinx is the mechanism: the syrinx is the avian vocal organ, located at the base of the trachea where the bronchi diverge, and it is what gives birds the ability to produce complex, modulated sound; the Turkey Vulture's syrinx is vestigial, non-functional for song production; what it produces -- the hissing grunt -- is a simple exhalation through the glottis, audible only at close range; the Turkey Vulture is one of the few bird species in North America where you can be standing ten feet away from the animal and hear nothing unless it exhales directly at you; E=2 is the appropriate registration
- FIRST CATHARTIDAE -- the New World Vultures are the engine's first entry from an order that is entirely separate from the raptors (Accipitriformes) and falcons (Falconiformes); Cathartiformes is an ancient order with seven living species, distributed across the Americas; in Washington only the Turkey Vulture is found regularly, with the California Condor historically present and now the subject of a Pacific Northwest recovery consideration; the Turkey Vulture's arrival at degree 235 opens the engine's documentation of the scavenger guild -- a functional role, not a taxonomic one, that will expand as the engine progresses
- Turkey Vulture ecology in Washington -- a summer resident of western and eastern Washington, arriving from wintering grounds in California and Mexico in March and departing by October; over Puget Sound it is commonly seen riding thermals above the Cascade foothills and the Olympic Peninsula, sometimes in groups of dozens during migration; it locates carrion primarily by smell rather than vision -- it has a highly developed olfactory sense unique among North American raptors -- and this mechanism is the evolutionary explanation for the large, bald red head: a feathered head would accumulate bacteria and decomposing tissue, so the vulture's solution is no feathers where the work is done
- Heart pair structural analysis -- the E=6/E=7 pair across degrees 234-235 is the engine's most nuanced band documentation to date, acknowledging that a major artist's catalog is not acoustically homogeneous; the classic rock reading (E=6) and the hard rock reading (E=7) are both correct and both incomplete; taken together they describe a band whose range spans multiple rock genres, whose live performance was consistently harder-edged than studio production suggested, and whose influence on Pacific Northwest rock runs deeper than the record sales alone indicate
