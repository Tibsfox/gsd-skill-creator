## Lessons Learned

1. **Pair-structure closure at elevated amplitude maps catalog duality empirically.** Heart's one-step E elevation from E=6 at degree 234 to E=7 at degree 235 maps the band's genuine hard-rock-versus-classic-pop duality through observable amplitude shift rather than through assertion. Future catalog-spanning-artist documentation should consider pair-structure when single-degree treatment would inadequately capture the catalog, because the two-degree structure gives each era its own frame without forcing era-selection and the amplitude-shift across the pair maps the duality empirically.
2. **Mechanism-level acoustic-register justification strengthens near-minimum and near-maximum readings.** The Turkey Vulture's E=2 reading rests on the vestigial-syrinx mechanism — a specific anatomical documentation that explains the near-minimum registration. Future SPS near-minimum and near-maximum acoustic-register readings should rest on mechanism-level justification wherever possible, because mechanism documentation is structurally defensible against impressionistic readings and provides replicable criteria for future similar registrations.
3. **Amplitude-contrast cross-sync is interpretively productive when extreme positions coincide in a single degree.** The five-E-unit simultaneous spread between Heart (E=7) and the Turkey Vulture (E=2) at degree 235 documents that cross-sync structures are not limited to parallel-amplitude pairing. Future cross-axis documentation should recognize amplitude-contrast as a legitimate cross-sync category when the engine stages extreme positions simultaneously, because contrast itself can be the documented subject and the amplitude-gap magnitude carries structural weight.
4. **Family-opener entries anchor future taxonomic coverage and should include lineage-context documentation.** The Turkey Vulture as FIRST CATHARTIDAE entry opens the New World Vulture family documentation, and the entry includes lineage-context documentation (evolutionary distinctness from Old World Vultures, convergent evolution, seven living species across the Americas). Future family-opener entries should include similar lineage-context documentation, because the opener sets the frame for subsequent family entries and provides the taxonomic orientation readers need to interpret later family-member entries.
5. **Functional-ecology registers (guilds) enrich species documentation beyond taxonomy.** The scavenger-guild register opens at degree 235 as a cross-taxon functional-ecology category distinct from the predator and pursuit-hunter guilds. Future SPS documentation should include functional-ecology-guild registers where applicable, because guild documentation organizes species by role rather than only by taxonomy and cross-taxon guilds (scavenger, pollinator, nutrient-cycler) provide interpretive frames that pure taxonomic documentation does not capture.
