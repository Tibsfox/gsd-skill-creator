## Summary

**Minus the Bear** arrives at E=6 -- the Seattle band that made math rock danceable without ever admitting they were a math rock band. Jake Snider (vocals), Dave Knudson (guitar), Erin Tate (drums), Cory Murchy (bass), and Alex Rose (synths) built a sound defined by Knudson's two-handed tapping technique and the band's refusal to choose between complexity and accessibility. On Suicide Squeeze Records, albums like *Menos el Oso* (2005) and *Planet of Ice* (2007) fuse intricate guitar work, electronic textures, and Snider's conversational vocals into something that fills dance floors and satisfies music theorists simultaneously. The band's name comes from a joke about an episode of *CHiPs* featuring a bear -- a perfect encapsulation of their refusal to take themselves seriously while making deeply serious music. At E=6, Minus the Bear earns its rating through density: every measure contains more notes, more rhythmic ideas, more timbral variation than most bands manage in a full song. On the species side, the **Canada Lynx** registers at E=5 -- and with this entry, the **Felidae quartet closes**. The Canada Lynx (*Lynx canadensis*) is the snow ghost of the North Cascades: a medium-sized cat with massive snowshoe paws, ear tufts like antennae, and a vocalization that splits the difference between the Cougar's power and the Bobcat's rasp. The Canada Lynx is a **Washington state threatened species**, its range restricted to the highest, snowiest forests of the Okanogan and North Cascades. Its voice closes the Felidae portrait with the rarest and most elusive of Washington's three wild cats.
