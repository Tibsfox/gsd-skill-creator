## Summary

**Kinski** returns at E=6 -- a **103-degree callback** to degree 110, where the band entered the engine as the FIRST KRAUTROCK act. Chris Martin, Matthew Reid-Schwartz, and Lucy Atkinson's instrumental project has spent two decades building vast, motorik compositions that owe as much to Neu! and Can as to anything from the Pacific Northwest. On Sub Pop, records like *Airs Above Your Station* (2003) and *Down Below It's Chaos* (2007) document a band that refuses vocals, refuses verses, refuses anything that interrupts the relentless forward motion of their sound. At E=6, Kinski returns at the same energy level as their first appearance -- the krautrock engine does not fatigue. On the species side, the **Cougar chirp** registers at E=4 -- forming a **comparison pair** with the Cougar scream at degree 212. Where the scream is a blood-curdling territorial shriek at E=7, the chirp is something shockingly domestic: a birdlike, high-pitched trill that Cougars use for close-range communication between mothers and cubs. The E=7-to-E=4 drop across a single degree is a 3-point energy descent that reveals the Cougar's full acoustic range -- from apex predator to attentive parent.
