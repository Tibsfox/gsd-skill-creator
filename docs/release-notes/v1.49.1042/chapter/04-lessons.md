1. **An axis sustains when the next observation reads the same kind of source through a wholly new substrate.**
   The X-ray-astronomy axis sustains at obs#3 because RXTE keeps the subject — accreting compact objects in the high-energy sky — but reaches it through the time domain rather than the image; the substrate-form distinctness is the domain read, so the cadence continues the axis rather than rotating away from it.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12061_

2. **Abandoning the image is what buys the time domain.**
   A focusing telescope collects only the photons its mirror area gathers and cannot deliver enough per millisecond to time the brightest variable sources; RXTE gives up the picture for large-area collimated detectors that time-tag every photon to the microsecond, the trade the time domain demands.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12062_

3. **A first can hinge on the qualifier.**
   RXTE is the first satellite *dedicated* to high-time-resolution X-ray timing, not the first to do timing at all — EXOSAT and Ginga came earlier; the distinct first is being built around timing with the area, microsecond resolution, and all-sky monitoring to reach the kHz regime, so the framing carries the "dedicated/purpose-built" qualifier.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12063_

4. **Microsecond timing and a large area reach phenomena an image cannot resolve.**
   The kilohertz QPOs of Scorpius X-1, the 363 Hz burst oscillation of 4U 1728-34, and the 401 Hz pulse of SAX J1808.4-3658 all live in the time domain — signals at the dynamical and spin timescales of matter at the edge of a compact object that no imaging optic, however sharp, can capture in time.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12064_

5. **One observatory reads many kinds of variable compact object in time.**
   The same fast-timing detectors found the inner-flow oscillations of an accreting neutron star, the spin frequency revealed in a thermonuclear burst, the first accretion-powered millisecond pulsar, the dozen-plus variability classes of the microquasar GRS 1915+105, and the high-frequency QPOs of stellar-mass black holes.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12065_

6. **The time domain sees the dynamics the image freezes.**
   The orbital frequency at the inner edge of an accretion flow, the spin of a neutron star, the precession that probes frame-dragging — all are timing signatures the focusing telescopes cannot resolve; the third observation is what the first dedicated timing observatory hears that the focusing telescopes could not.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12066_

7. **A continuation keeps the predecessors' vocabulary and references them by name.**
   Because RXTE sustains the X-ray-astronomy axis, the shared X-ray vocabulary is correct and kept, and Chandra (obs#1, soft/medium focusing) and NuSTAR (obs#2, hard-band focusing) are referenced as the focusing complements — but RXTE's own facts (the PCA/HEXTE/ASM collimated detectors, the Delta II launch, the in-house Goddard build, the kHz QPOs) and only its own six anchors are stamped; the predecessors' anchors and predecessor-specific facts stay with their missions.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12067_

8. **Accuracy guardrails guard against the stale-infobox and fabricated-citation classes of error.**
   Using orbit ~580 km / ~23°, mass ~3,200 kg / ~7,055 lb, deactivation 5 January 2012, operating life ~16 years, launch site Cape Canaveral SLC-17A, the Explorer-69 program, PCA ~6,500 cm², the distinct 363 Hz and ~1,100 Hz phenomena, the ASM DOI 10.1086/310260 kept distinct from the Strohmayer 310261, and only the verified citations — keeps the facts load-bearing and correct.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12068_

9. **The shader renders procedural geometry rather than archived data.**
   The X-ray-timing-variability shader uses analytic curves and procedural noise rather than loading actual RXTE event lists or a measured power-density spectrum. A future revision could render a published RXTE light curve or power spectrum (the Scorpius X-1 kHz QPO peaks or the GRS 1915+105 variability classes) for a higher-fidelity view of the timing data.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12069_

10. **The RXTE diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the observatory, the five PCA units, the two HEXTE clusters, and the three All-Sky Monitor cameras.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12070_

11. **The X-ray-timing-continuation sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.**
   The sidebar lists the axis continuation and the RXTE mission elements (the PCA, HEXTE, and ASM, the low orbit, the science highlights) but does not enumerate the full roster of RXTE results or the prospective obs#4+ continuations; a future revision could expand the table.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12071_

12. **The timing physics is described rather than computed.**
   The Fourier power-density spectrum, the epoch-folding period search, and the orbital and precession frequencies at the inner accretion flow are described but not run against published RXTE event data; a future ship could process a real RXTE light curve to demonstrate a QPO peak or a folded pulse profile directly.
