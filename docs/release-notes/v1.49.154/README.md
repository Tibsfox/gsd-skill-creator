## Summary

**The descending spiral and the neighborhood that refuses to disappear.** Degree 19 introduces Fly Moon Royale -- a Seattle-based neo-soul/jazz collective rooted in the Central District, led by vocalist and bandleader Parker and the first collective-type creative unit to enter the S36 engine. Where Maktub at degree 18 was a fixed four-piece with irreplaceable chemistry -- Reggie Watts, Kevin Goldman, Alex Veley, and Davis Martin -- Fly Moon Royale is structurally different: a rotating ensemble of musicians drawn from Seattle's jazz and soul scenes who orbit a central creative vision. The collective is the third organizational type in the S36 engine after the solo artist (degrees 0-17) and the fixed band (Maktub, degree 18), and its arrival completes a clean three-entry typology of creative-unit configurations. A solo artist's identity IS the person -- Quincy Jones, Ernestine Anderson, Wayne Horvitz, Jeff Lorber. A band's identity is the irreplaceable chemistry of specific individuals -- Maktub. A collective's identity is the aesthetic itself -- Parker's vocal authority, the neo-soul harmonic vocabulary, the groove-first commitment -- around which different instrumentalists cycle, creating a different ensemble at each performance. The sound is both consistent (Parker's voice, the shared vocabulary, the groove) and variable (different musicians bring different inflections, different improvisation instincts, different timbral colors). The collective is the most fluid organizational type in the engine: the identity is the aesthetic, not the personnel. Nineteen degrees in, the S36 engine now contains three organizational types arrayed along a gradient of creative-unit fluidity.

**THE CENTRAL DISTRICT RETURNS AFTER SEVEN DEGREES OF ABSENCE.** The Central District location is the single most structurally significant geographic fact about degree 19. The Central District has been the S36 engine's founding geography -- the Jackson Street jazz corridor of degrees 0 through 6, the Black cultural infrastructure that sustained Seattle jazz from the postwar era through the 1960s, the neighborhood where Quincy Jones, Ernestine Anderson, Ray Charles, Buddy Catlett, Floyd Standifer, Overton Berry, and Patti Bown built the engine's foundational cohort. After a brief return at degree 11 with Joe Brazil and his Detroit-to-Central District salon model, the Central District disappeared from the engine for seven consecutive degrees -- the longest single-neighborhood absence across nineteen degrees. Degrees 12 through 18 documented Pine Siskin, Hutton's Vireo, MacGillivray's Warbler, Wilson's Warbler, Yellow Warbler, Common Yellowthroat, Swainson's Thrush, and on the S36 side Julian Priester, Gary Peacock, Hadley Caliman, Joni Metcalf, Don Lanphere, Bill Frisell, Jeff Lorber, Reggie Watts, and Maktub -- a sweep across Chicago, Idaho, Oklahoma, Ohio, and Capitol Hill, but never back to the Central District. Degree 19 brings the CD back, but in a transformed state. The Central District of 2012 is not the Central District of 1950. Gentrification restructured the neighborhood profoundly through the 1990s and 2000s -- displacement by economic pressure, demographic shift, the closing of the Jackson Street nightclubs that had sustained Overton Berry and Patti Bown. The Black community that powered the postwar corridor has been fragmented and reduced. The nightclubs are gone. But the music continues. Fly Moon Royale represents a new generation of Central District music, the tradition persisting through transformation as the genre evolves from straight jazz to neo-soul and R&B. The thread does not break; it transforms. The neighborhood changes but artists still root their work there, still draw from the community's musical DNA, still create live music that reflects the place. The geographic persistence through gentrification is the most poignant thread in the engine, and it operates in parallel to the Veery's persistence at the western edge of its breeding range -- both are rare continuations of something that was once abundant, both are threads that transformation cannot fully break.

**SYNCOPATION IS THE TECHNIQUE THAT DEFINES NEO-SOUL AS DISTINCT FROM ITS ANCESTORS.** The music-theory mapping at degree 19 is syncopation -- the off-beat rhythmic emphasis that separates soul and R&B from their jazz and blues ancestors. Syncopation displaces rhythmic accents from strong beats (1 and 3 in 4/4 time) to weak beats (2 and 4) or to the subdivisions between beats. Neo-soul extends this displacement further than traditional R&B: the kick drum lands on the "and" of 4 instead of beat 1, the bass line anticipates the chord change by a sixteenth note, the vocal phrase starts a beat early or lags behind the harmonic rhythm. The aggregate effect is what producers and drummers call the rhythmic haze -- everything feels slightly off from where rigid notation would place it, and the off-ness IS the groove. D'Angelo's *Voodoo* (2000) is the national landmark for this technique: Questlove and Pino Palladino playing the simplest drum-and-bass groove with every attack microscopically displaced, creating a feel no quantized drum machine can replicate. Fly Moon Royale operates in this rhythmic tradition. The collective model makes syncopation harder, not easier: rotating musicians must share an understanding of where the beat is supposed to not be, without the benefit of years playing together. The syncopation vocabulary serves as an audition criterion. If you cannot feel where the beat is supposed to not be, you cannot play in this collective. The technique connects across the engine to Berry (degree 5, groove syncopation in jazz piano), to Maktub (degree 18, funk-inflected collective syncopation as a fixed band), and to Anderson (degree 3, vocal phrasing across the bar line).

**THE VEERY IS THE THIRD CATHARUS AND THE CATHARUS TRIPTYCH IS NOW COMPLETE.** A Veery sings from a willow thicket at the western edge of its range. *Catharus fuscescens* -- the species epithet means "becoming dusky," from Latin *fuscus* (dusky, dark brown) plus the inchoative suffix *-escens*. The common name is onomatopoeic -- *veer-veer-veer* -- the bird that named itself. Named by James Francis Stephens in 1817. Family Turdidae, order Passeriformes. This is the third *Catharus* thrush to enter the circle, completing the Catharus triptych: three species from the genus whose name means "pure" (Greek *katharos*), each with a fundamentally different approach to organizing pitch. The Hermit Thrush at degree 7 spirals through the harmonic series -- vertical, chordal, static thinking, the notes drawn from the overtone series and related within each phrase rather than between phrases. The Swainson's Thrush at degree 18 spirals upward -- horizontal, melodic, ascending, tension, each phrase pitched higher than the last, the melody climbing through pitch space. The Veery at degree 19 spirals downward -- horizontal, melodic, descending, resolution, each phrase lower than the last, the melody settling into repose. Static, ascending, descending -- these three directions exhaust the possibilities for pitch motion. There are no other directions pitch can go. The genus named pure maps the complete pitch space of music through three biological voices. The triptych is not merely complete. It is closed. The Swainson's Thrush asked the question at degree 18. The Veery provides the answer at degree 19. Ascending tension followed by descending resolution. This is the fundamental shape of tonal music -- tension and release, question and answer, dominant and tonic, half cadence and authentic cadence. Placed at adjacent degrees, the ascending and descending spirals form the most fundamental pairing in tonal music, the tension-resolution pair that degree 18 set up and degree 19 completes. The matched pair is closed. The engine has been building toward this pairing since degree 7. Eleven degrees later, the Swainson's asked the question. One degree after that, the Veery answers it.

**BILATERAL SYRINX POLYPHONY RECREATES THE CHORUS EFFECT IN BIOLOGICAL FORM.** The Veery's ethereal quality -- that shimmering, underwater texture that makes the song the most otherworldly voice in North American birdsong -- comes from bilateral syrinx polyphony. Two closely-spaced tones produced simultaneously by the left and right halves of the syrinx create beat frequencies: periodic amplitude fluctuations perceived as shimmer, wavering, pulsing. This is the chorus effect in biological form. A chorus pedal duplicates a signal, detunes the copy by a few cents, and mixes them -- the slight frequency difference creates beat frequencies that produce a spacious, shimmering sound. The Veery has been doing this naturally for millions of years. The comparison across the engine is structurally elegant: the Pacific Wren at degree 1 uses bilateral syrinx polyphony for melodic complexity -- two interleaved voices producing 100 notes in 10 seconds, two-part counterpoint, a Bach fugue from an inch-long bird. The Veery at degree 19 uses bilateral syrinx polyphony for atmospheric shimmer -- two closely-spaced tones creating beat frequencies, the chorus pedal, the opposite of counterpoint. Same organ, two completely different applications. Information density versus atmospheric texture. The first quadrant of the engine opens with bilateral polyphony for complexity at degree 1 and closes with bilateral polyphony for atmosphere at degree 19, and the bookend is the most structurally satisfying pairing in the first twenty degrees.

### Part B: Veery -- The Descending Spiral (CATHARUS TRIPTYCH COMPLETE, Matched Pair Closed)

Full deep research covering the third Catharus thrush, the descending spiral as bioacoustic and musical structure, the completion of the Catharus triptych, bilateral syrinx as chorus effect, the tension-resolution pair, and lowland riparian ecology:

- **Taxonomy -- The Third Catharus: THE TRIPTYCH COMPLETE (Three Pitch Directions Exhaust the Space):** Family Turdidae (thrushes), order Passeriformes. Genus *Catharus* from Greek *katharos*, "pure." The Veery (*Catharus fuscescens*) becomes the third *Catharus* in the circle after the Hermit Thrush (degree 7) and the Swainson's Thrush (degree 18). No other genus in the engine has three representatives. The species epithet *fuscescens* means "becoming dusky" (Latin *fuscus* + inchoative *-escens*). Named by James Francis Stephens in 1817. The common name is onomatopoeic -- an attempt to transcribe the descending *veer-veer-veer*. Unlike "Hermit Thrush" (behavior) or "Swainson's Thrush" (naturalist), the Veery's name IS the sound. The bird named itself. Physical: 6.5-7.5 inches, 26-54 grams. The warmest-toned of the three Catharus: uniform warm cinnamon-brown upperparts, faint blurred breast spots, no bold eyering, no rufous tail contrast. The least marked, warmest, gentlest of the three. The least common Catharus in the PNW -- core breeding range is the northeastern US and southern Canada, the Pacific Northwest represents the western edge.

- **Bioacoustics -- THE DESCENDING SPIRAL: The Song That Falls (PROGRESSION ADDS):** The Veery's song is the mirror image of the Swainson's Thrush. Where the Swainson's spirals upward, the Veery spirals downward: ethereal, reedy phrases, each phrase lower than the last, creating a descending helical staircase through pitch space. Frequency range: 1.5-4.5 kHz. Starting pitch: ~3.0-4.5 kHz (highest in sequence). Terminal pitch: ~1.5-2.5 kHz (lowest). Phrase duration: 0.5-1.5 seconds. Song duration: 2-5 seconds (shorter, more compact than the Swainson's 4-12 seconds). The quality is "dreamy," "underwater," "otherworldly" -- the most ethereal voice in North American birdsong. The defining feature is the inter-phrase pitch trajectory: each successive phrase begins at a lower pitch. The Swainson's ascending spiral creates the feeling of climbing; the Veery's descending spiral creates the feeling of settling, resolving, coming home. Dusk and dawn calling, with strong dusk bias.

- **THE CATHARUS TRIPTYCH COMPLETE -- Three Pitch Directions Map the Complete Pitch Space:** Hermit Thrush (degree 7): harmonic series, STATIC (vertical/chordal -- notes relate within a phrase), musical function = drone / harmony / the chord. Swainson's Thrush (degree 18): ascending spiral, ASCENDING (horizontal/melodic upward -- tension), musical function = departure / question / tension. Veery (degree 19): descending spiral, DESCENDING (horizontal/melodic downward -- resolution), musical function = arrival / answer / resolution. Static, ascending, descending -- these three directions exhaust the possibilities. There are no other directions pitch can go. The triptych maps to a complete grammar of pitch motion: the Hermit Thrush is the NOUN (the thing itself, what the chord IS), the Swainson's Thrush is the VERB of departure (ascending, going out, tension, the question), the Veery is the VERB of return (descending, coming home, resolution, the answer). Every melody in every tradition can be decomposed into these three motions: staying, rising, falling. The genus *Catharus* provides all three.

- **BILATERAL SYRINX POLYPHONY -- The Chorus Effect in Biological Form:** The Veery produces two independent fundamental frequencies simultaneously through the left and right halves of its bilateral syrinx. The two tones are closely spaced -- separated by a few hertz to a few tens of hertz -- producing beat frequencies: periodic amplitude fluctuations perceived as shimmering, pulsing, wavering. This is the chorus effect. A chorus pedal duplicates a signal, detunes the copy, and mixes them. The Veery has been doing this for millions of years. The bilateral syrinx comparison: Pacific Wren (degree 1) uses bilateral polyphony for MELODIC COMPLEXITY (two interleaved voices, 100 notes in 10 seconds -- two-part counterpoint). The Veery uses it for ATMOSPHERIC SHIMMER (two closely-spaced tones creating beat frequencies -- the chorus pedal). Same organ, opposite applications: information density vs. atmospheric texture. The Veery modulates the frequency difference dynamically, controlling shimmer rate as a musical parameter in real time.

- **THE TENSION-RESOLUTION PAIR -- Swainson's + Veery, Matched Pair CLOSED:** The Swainson's Thrush (degree 18) spirals UPWARD: aspiration, tension, the question. The Veery (degree 19) spirals DOWNWARD: resolution, release, the answer. Adjacent degrees, both *Catharus*, opposite trajectories. Together they form a complete narrative arc -- the oldest story in music: you go out, and then you come home. Half cadence (hanging -- degree 18) and authentic cadence (resolved -- degree 19). The matched pair that degree 18 set up is now closed. The engine has been building toward this pairing since degree 7. Eleven degrees later, the Swainson's asked the question. One degree after that, the Veery answers it.

- **LOWLAND RIPARIAN -- The Thicket at the Edge of Range:** Dense, deciduous undergrowth along streams and rivers at lower elevations than the Swainson's closed-canopy riparian forest. Willow-alder thickets, cottonwood bottomlands, red-osier dogwood and snowberry. The Veery is uncommon in the PNW -- western margin of its breeding range, concentrated in northeastern Washington with scattered records west of the Cascades. The descending spiral at the edge of range. Distinct from the Swainson's habitat in structure: lower, denser, more deciduous, less vertically stratified. The Swainson's sings from mid-canopy; the Veery sings from the thicket interior. The ascending spiral exploits vertical complexity; the descending spiral fills the horizontal thicket.

- **Conservation -- Significant Long-Term Decline, Steeper Than Swainson's:** Least Concern (IUCN). Approximately 12 million breeding population (Partners in Flight 2020) -- roughly one-eighth the Swainson's. Breeding Bird Survey shows significant long-term decline of approximately 1.5% per year since 1966 -- steeper than the Swainson's 0.7%. Cumulative loss over six decades approaches 55-60%. Climate change models predict northward range shifts that could further reduce the PNW's already marginal breeding population.

- **Neotropical Migration at the Western Edge -- Bolivia to the Willow Thicket:** The Veery is a long-distance neotropical migrant whose non-breeding range concentrates in the Amazon Basin of Brazil and Bolivia -- one of the most southerly wintering destinations of any PNW breeding songbird. Individual Veerys tracked by geolocator travel more than 7,000 kilometers each way, sometimes in a single non-stop flight of over 3,000 kilometers across the Caribbean. The PNW's marginal breeding population carries the descending spiral across a full hemisphere and back each year. The conservation implication is direct: protecting the lowland riparian thickets of northeastern Washington requires protecting Amazonian forest thousands of miles south, and vice versa.

Where Maktub (degree 18) and the Swainson's Thrush both climbed -- layered ascent, collective staircase -- FMR and the Veery both arrive. The groove IS the resolution. The descending spiral IS the answer. Both are about the homecoming, not the journey.

2. **Both are structurally gentler versions of their immediate predecessors, completing a tension-resolution pair at the paired-degree level.** Maktub was a fixed four-piece with irreplaceable chemistry and high-energy live shows. Fly Moon Royale is a rotating collective with ambient communal energy -- consistent aesthetic, variable personnel, gentler. The Swainson's Thrush ascends with tension. The Veery descends with resolution -- shorter songs, tighter phrases, the melody unwinding rather than building. The paired degree (18-19) enacts tension-resolution in both engines simultaneously: the ascending/high-energy entry followed by the descending/resolving entry.

3. **Both represent persistence through transformation -- the thing that endures not because it stays the same but because it adapts.** The Central District persists through gentrification: different demographics, different genre, same geographic commitment to music. The Veery persists at the edge of its range: uncommon, declining, but still singing from the lowland thickets. Both are the rare continuation of something that was once abundant. Both are the thread that does not break.

## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **The THIRD ORGANIZATIONAL TYPE at degree 19 completes a clean three-entry typology (solo, band, collective) that emerged naturally from the data rather than being imposed.** Solo artists dominated degrees 0-17 because the jazz tradition is individual-centered. The first band appeared at degree 18 because neo-soul shifted toward collective groove. The first collective appears at degree 19 because the rotating-ensemble model is a natural extension of the collective principle into a more fluid form. Each type appeared when the genre context demanded it. The typology was not planned -- it was discovered. This is the engine's most reliable structural signal: when a new category appears, it appears because the data requires it, not because the analyst anticipated it.

- **The Central District oscillation pattern -- present 0-6, absent 7-10, back 11, absent 12-18, back 19 -- is now the engine's clearest example of geographic persistence through social transformation.** The CD's musical thread has survived gentrification not because the same people stayed (they largely didn't) but because the geographic identity of the neighborhood continues to attract and sustain musicians who root their work in the place. The pattern is poignant because each return is in a different genre and a different era. The thread does not repeat; it transforms. Jazz piano (Berry, 5) became salon teaching (Brazil, 11) became neo-soul collective (FMR, 19). The neighborhood's musical identity is not a genre -- it is a commitment to groove-first, community-rooted music-making. The genre changes. The commitment persists.

- **The CATHARUS TRIPTYCH is the most analytically productive taxonomic structure in either engine because it maps the complete pitch-direction space through three species from the same genus.** Static (harmonic series, degree 7), ascending (ascending spiral, degree 18), descending (descending spiral, degree 19). These three directions exhaust the possibilities for pitch motion. The triptych is closed -- there is nothing left to add. The genus named "pure" provides the pure forms of each pitch direction. This is not metaphor -- it is acoustic measurement. The Hermit Thrush navigates the overtone series (Doolittle & Brumm 2014). The Swainson's Thrush ascends measurably. The Veery descends measurably. Biology and music theory converge on the same three directions because both are constrained by the same physics: pitch either stays, rises, or falls.

- **The tension-resolution pair (Swainson's Thrush ascending at degree 18 + Veery descending at degree 19) is the engine's first completed matched pair, and it closes with the most fundamental structure in tonal music: question and answer.** The matched pair was set up explicitly at degree 18 and resolved at degree 19. Adjacent degrees. Same genus. Opposite trajectories. This is the engine's cleanest structural demonstration: two entries designed to illuminate each other through contrast. The ascending spiral creates tension. The descending spiral provides resolution. Together they enact the oldest musical story: departure and homecoming. The matched pair should become a template for future adjacent-degree pairings where two entries from the same taxonomic group employ opposite strategies.

- **The bilateral syrinx comparison (Pacific Wren at degree 1 vs. Veery at degree 19) bookends the first quadrant with two uses of the same organ for completely different purposes.** Degree 1: bilateral polyphony for melodic complexity, two-part counterpoint, information density. Degree 19: bilateral polyphony for atmospheric shimmer, chorus effect, beat frequencies. Same biological structure, eighteen degrees apart, opposite applications. The bookend is structurally elegant: the first quadrant opens with bilateral polyphony for complexity (cascade) and closes with bilateral polyphony for atmosphere (descending spiral). The organ's dual nature maps to the dual purpose of music itself: saying something (complexity/information) and feeling something (atmosphere/emotion).

- **The parallel between Fly Moon Royale and the Veery -- both operating at the resolution end, both gentler versions of their predecessors, both persisting through transformation -- is structurally clean because it operates on the same axis as the degree 18 parallel (layered ascent) but in the opposite direction (settling resolution).** Degree 18's parallel: both build through layered ascent. Degree 19's parallel: both resolve through settling descent. The consecutive degrees form a meta-parallel: the Part A / Part B resonance at degree 18 was about climbing, and the Part A / Part B resonance at degree 19 is about arriving. The parallels themselves enact tension and resolution.

### What Could Be Better

- **The energy-3 streak at FIVE consecutive degrees (15-19) is now the most prominent unexplained structural feature in the engine.** Five consecutive high-energy entries, five distinct subtypes, no explanation for why the streak persists or what will reset it. Is it a property of the jazz-to-soul transition zone? A selection artifact? A property of the genres being documented? The question has been asked at every degree since 16 and remains unanswered. Degree 20 will either extend the streak to six (unprecedented) or break it (providing the first energy reset in five degrees).

- **The gender tracking shows FOURTEEN consecutive male or male-led artists (degrees 7-19).** This is the longest single-gender run in the engine by a significant margin. The last female or female-led entry was Ernestine Anderson at degree 3. The streak warrants active attention at future degrees. Neo-soul has a rich tradition of female artists nationally (Badu, Scott, Hill) and locally. The engine's gender distribution needs rebalancing.

- **The neo-soul thread at two entries (Maktub 18, FMR 19) in consecutive degrees could benefit from more distance.** Two consecutive neo-soul entries risk making the genre feel over-represented at this arc of the circle. The diversity of the engine's first 17 degrees (where no consecutive degrees shared a genre) is a structural strength that two consecutive neo-soul entries slightly erode. Future placement should ensure genre variety returns.

- **The collective model's harmonic and improvisational analysis relies on convention-based (lingua franca) improvisation vs. history-based (shared language) improvisation as a theoretical distinction, but the research could ground this more concretely with specific harmonic examples.** What specific chord voicings constitute the neo-soul lingua franca? How do they differ from gospel extensions? The harmonic vocabulary remains abstractly described.

## Lessons Learned

- **The three organizational types (solo, band, collective) form a gradient of creative-unit fluidity, and the gradient correlates with genre evolution.** Jazz (individual-centered, virtuoso tradition) naturally produced solo artists. Neo-soul (groove-centered, collaborative tradition) naturally produced bands and then collectives. The organizational type is not independent of the genre -- certain genres structurally favor certain creative-unit configurations. Future degrees should track how organizational type correlates with genre. The engine may discover that each major genre transition brings a new organizational type.

- **Geographic persistence through gentrification is documented through the CD oscillation pattern, and the mechanism is not continuity of community but continuity of place-identity.** The Central District's musical thread survives not because the same people stayed but because the neighborhood's identity as a musical place continues to attract artists who root their work there. This is a structural insight about how neighborhoods sustain creative cultures: the place carries meaning independent of its current residents. Musicians choose the CD not because of who lives there now but because of what the CD means in Seattle's musical history. The geographic thread is carried by narrative identity, not demographic continuity.

- **The Catharus triptych demonstrates that the most powerful taxonomic comparisons exhaust a parameter space rather than merely sampling it.** Three species, three pitch directions, complete coverage. The triptych is powerful precisely because there is nothing left to add -- static, ascending, and descending are the only options. Future multi-species comparisons within a genus should aim for completeness: not "here are three interesting species" but "here are all the strategies this genus uses, and together they cover the full space of possibilities." Exhaustive coverage turns a comparison into a framework.

- **Tension-resolution pairs placed at adjacent degrees are the engine's most efficient structural device because they encode a complete musical argument in the minimum number of entries.** Degrees 18 and 19 together say more about pitch direction than either says alone. The ascending spiral only achieves its full meaning when the descending spiral answers it. The principle: when the engine encounters an entry that embodies one half of a fundamental duality (tension/resolution, major/minor, consonance/dissonance), it should actively seek the complementary entry for an adjacent degree. Matched pairs at adjacent degrees maximize analytical yield per degree.

- **The first quadrant (degrees 0-19) is the jazz arc, and its completion provides the engine's first opportunity for retrospective structural analysis across a full arc.** Twenty degrees is enough data to identify patterns that individual degrees cannot reveal: the CD oscillation, the energy-3 streak, the gender imbalance, the genre transition mechanism (soften-break-shift), the Catharus triptych. These patterns emerged over multiple degrees and are only visible in aggregate. The engine should perform arc-level analysis at each quadrant boundary (degree 19, 89, 179, 269, 359). Each quadrant boundary is a natural checkpoint for structural review.

- **Bilateral syrinx polyphony has two independent biological applications, and both are documented in the first twenty degrees.** The Pacific Wren at degree 1 uses bilateral polyphony for information density (counterpoint, 100 notes in 10 seconds, the Bach fugue). The Veery at degree 19 uses bilateral polyphony for atmospheric texture (beat frequencies, chorus shimmer, the underwater quality). Same organ, opposite applications. The engine should track whether every anatomical feature that supports dual applications eventually acquires both within the 360 degrees -- a principle of exhaustive coverage at the organ level complementing the exhaustive coverage at the pitch-direction level.

- **The collective organizational type is more resilient but less unique than the fixed band.** Maktub survives as a specific memory because it was Watts + Goldman + Veley + Martin and nobody else. Fly Moon Royale survives as a continuing project because the aesthetic survives any individual instrumentalist's departure. The trade-off is fidelity of moment vs. longevity of project. Neither mode is superior; they are different answers to the question of what a creative unit wants to prove. The engine should expect future collectives and future fixed bands and should use the distinction as a taxonomic dimension.

- **A single-degree's research can support an entire-quadrant retrospective when the structural signals converge.** Degree 19's combination of the Catharus triptych's completion, the Central District's third return, the energy-3 streak's extension to five, the first tension-resolution pair's closure, and the first quadrant's closure means one degree's research supports analysis of twenty degrees' worth of structure. The principle: the engine should treat degrees that close multiple running ledgers as natural checkpoints and should mine them for retrospective structural observations rather than treating them as just another pair of research releases.
