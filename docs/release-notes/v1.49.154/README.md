## Summary

The descending spiral and the neighborhood that refuses to disappear. Degree 19 introduces Fly Moon Royale -- a Seattle-based neo-soul/jazz collective rooted in the **Central District**, led by vocalist and bandleader Parker. The project is not a band in the fixed-lineup sense documented at degree 18 (Maktub) -- it is a **collective**, a rotating ensemble of musicians drawn from Seattle's jazz and soul scenes who orbit a central creative vision. This is the **third organizational type** in the S36 engine after solo artist (degrees 0-17) and fixed band (degree 18): the **collective**. The distinction is structural. A solo artist's identity IS the person. A band's identity is the irreplaceable chemistry of specific individuals. A collective's identity is the **aesthetic** -- the central creative authority (Parker's vocals and artistic direction) around which different instrumentalists cycle, creating a different ensemble at each performance. The sound is both consistent (Parker's voice, the neo-soul harmonic vocabulary, the groove-first approach) and variable (different instrumentalists bring different inflections, different improvisation instincts, different timbral colors). The collective is the most fluid organizational type: the identity is the aesthetic, not the personnel.

The Central District location is the most structurally significant geographic fact about degree 19. The CD has been the engine's **founding geography** -- the Jackson Street jazz corridor of degrees 0-6, the Black cultural infrastructure that sustained Seattle jazz from the postwar era through the 1960s. After a brief return at degree 11 (Joe Brazil), the Central District disappeared from the engine for **seven consecutive degrees** (12-18), the longest absence of any neighborhood thread. Degree 19 brings the CD back -- but in a transformed state. The Central District of 2012 is not the Central District of 1950. Gentrification has restructured the neighborhood profoundly. The Black community that sustained the Jackson Street corridor has been displaced, fragmented, reduced by economic pressure and development. The nightclubs are gone. But the music continues. Fly Moon Royale represents a **new generation of Central District music** -- the tradition persists but the genre has evolved from straight jazz to soul/R&B fusion. The thread does not break; it transforms. The neighborhood changes, but artists still root their work there, still draw from the community's musical DNA, still create live music that reflects the place. The CD oscillation pattern is now fully visible: present 0-6, absent 7-10, back 11, absent 12-18, back 19. Long absences punctuated by single-degree returns. The geographic persistence through gentrification is the most poignant thread in the engine. The music transforms, the community transforms, but the geographic thread persists.

The music theory mapping at degree 19 is **syncopation** -- the off-beat rhythmic emphasis that defines soul and R&B as distinct from their jazz and blues ancestors. Syncopation displaces rhythmic accents from strong beats (1 and 3 in 4/4 time) to weak beats (2 and 4) or to subdivisions between beats. Neo-soul takes this displacement further than traditional R&B: the kick drum might land on the "and" of 4 instead of beat 1; the bass line might anticipate the chord change by a sixteenth note; the vocal phrase might start a beat early or lag behind the harmonic rhythm. The aggregate effect is a **rhythmic haze** -- everything feels slightly off from where rigid notation would place it, and the "off-ness" IS the groove. D'Angelo's *Voodoo* (2000) is the national landmark for this technique. Fly Moon Royale operates in this rhythmic tradition. The collective model demands that every incoming musician understand the syncopation vocabulary implicitly -- if you can't feel where the beat is supposed to not be, you can't play in this collective.

Degree 19 extends the energy-3 streak to **five consecutive degrees** (15-19) -- the longest single-energy streak at level 3 in the engine. Five subtypes: acoustic-assertive (Marriott, 15), electronic-propulsive (Lorber, 16), performative-explosive (Watts, 17), groove-collective (Maktub, 18), and now **soul-communal** (Fly Moon Royale, 19). The distribution shifts to **2:13:5**. The fifth subtype is distinct because the energy source is not just the band's internal dynamics but the **relationship between the collective and the audience** -- the neo-soul live performance as communal ritual, the groove drawing the audience into participatory response, the audience's energy feeding back into the performers.

A Veery sings from a willow thicket at the western edge of its range. *Catharus fuscescens* -- the species epithet means "becoming dusky" (Latin *fuscus*, "dusky, dark brown" + the inchoative suffix *-escens*). The common name is onomatopoeic -- *veer-veer-veer* -- the bird named itself. This is the **third Catharus thrush in the circle**, completing the **Catharus triptych**: three species from the same genus, each with a fundamentally different approach to organizing pitch. The Hermit Thrush (degree 7) spirals through the harmonic series -- vertical, chordal, static. The Swainson's Thrush (degree 18) spirals **upward** -- horizontal, melodic, ascending, tension. The Veery spirals **downward** -- horizontal, melodic, descending, resolution. Static, ascending, descending -- these three directions exhaust the possibilities. There are no other directions pitch can go. The genus named "pure" (*katharos*) maps the complete pitch space of music through three biological voices. The triptych is not merely complete -- it is **closed**.

The Swainson's Thrush (degree 18) asked the question. The Veery (degree 19) provides the answer. Ascending tension followed by descending resolution. This is the fundamental shape of tonal music: tension and release, question and answer, dominant and tonic. Placed at adjacent degrees, the ascending and descending spirals form the most fundamental pairing in tonal music -- the **tension-resolution pair** that degree 18 set up and degree 19 completes. The **matched pair is closed**. Call and response. Half cadence (hanging) and authentic cadence (resolved). The engine built toward this pairing since degree 7. Eleven degrees later, the Swainson's asked the question. One degree after that, the Veery answers it.

The Veery's ethereal quality -- that shimmering, underwater texture -- comes from **bilateral syrinx polyphony**. Two closely-spaced tones produced simultaneously by the left and right halves of the syrinx create **beat frequencies** -- periodic amplitude fluctuations perceived as shimmer, wavering, pulsing. This is the **chorus effect** in biological form. A chorus pedal duplicates a signal, detunes the copy, and mixes them. The slight frequency difference creates beat frequencies that produce a spacious, shimmering sound. Every guitar player who uses a chorus pedal is recreating what the Veery's syrinx does naturally. The bilateral syrinx comparison across the engine: the Pacific Wren (degree 1) uses its two voices for melodic complexity (two-part counterpoint, a Bach fugue). The Veery uses its two voices for atmospheric shimmer (two detuned voices, a chorus pedal). Same organ, completely different purpose. Information density vs. atmosphere.

The progression adds **descending spiral** at degree 19.

### Part A: Fly Moon Royale -- Neo-Soul Collective (THIRD ORGANIZATIONAL TYPE, CD Returns, Syncopation)

Full deep research covering Fly Moon Royale as the first collective in the S36 engine, the Central District's return after seven degrees of absence, syncopation as primary technique, neo-soul harmony in a groove context, and the live show as primary medium:

- **THIRD ORGANIZATIONAL TYPE -- The Collective (Rotating Musicians Around a Central Creative Authority):** The engine now documents three organizational types for creative units. Solo artist / bandleader (degrees 0-17): one creative authority, music orbits a single identity. Fixed band (degree 18, Maktub): permanent members, collective identity, emergent composition. **Collective** (degree 19, Fly Moon Royale): central creative authority (Parker) plus rotating musicians, variable ensemble, consistent aesthetic. The collective occupies a space between the solo artist and the fixed band. Like a solo artist, there is a central creative identity. Like a band, the music is genuinely collaborative. Unlike a solo artist, the collaborators change. Unlike a fixed band, the sound is defined by the bandleader's aesthetic choices, not by the irreplaceable chemistry of specific individuals. The collective is more resilient (survives personnel changes) but less unique in moment-to-moment expression (the magic of four irreplaceable people playing together is not available). The distinction from Maktub (degree 18) is instructive: when Maktub played, it was always Watts, Goldman, Veley, and Martin. When Fly Moon Royale plays, it is Parker plus whoever Parker has assembled for that particular show.

- **THE CENTRAL DISTRICT RETURNS -- Geographic Persistence Through Gentrification (CD Oscillation Pattern Confirmed):** The CD oscillation pattern is now fully visible across nineteen degrees: PRESENT 0-6 (Jackson Street corridor, postwar jazz, founding geography), ABSENT 7-10 (four consecutive degrees, Chicago/Idaho/Oklahoma/Ohio origins dominate), RETURNS at 11 (Joe Brazil, Detroit-to-CD transplant, salon model), ABSENT 12-18 (seven consecutive degrees, the longest gap), RETURNS at 19 (new generation, neo-soul/R&B, gentrification context). Long absences punctuated by single-degree returns. Each return is in a different era and a different genre. The pattern demonstrates geographic persistence -- the Central District does not disappear from Seattle's musical identity even as its demographics change. The connection to Overton Berry (degree 5) and Patti Bown (degree 6) is particularly poignant: Berry and Bown represented the CD's piano tradition, the groove and the swing that the nightclub circuit sustained. Fly Moon Royale inherits that groove tradition in a neo-soul vocabulary rather than a jazz one. The lineage from Berry's groove-based piano to FMR's groove-based collective is traceable through the CD geography: different decades, different genres, same neighborhood commitment to groove-first music-making.

- **SYNCOPATION AS PRIMARY TECHNIQUE -- The Off-Beat Emphasis That Defines Neo-Soul and R&B:** Syncopation displaces rhythmic accents from strong beats to weak beats or to subdivisions between beats. Neo-soul extends this further than traditional R&B -- the rhythmic haze where everything feels slightly off from the grid, and the off-ness IS the groove. D'Angelo's *Voodoo* is the national benchmark: Questlove and Pino Palladino playing the simplest groove with every attack microscopically displaced, creating a feel no quantized drum machine can replicate. Fly Moon Royale operates in this tradition. The collective model makes syncopation harder: rotating musicians must share an understanding of where the beat ISN'T without years of playing together. The syncopation vocabulary serves as an audition criterion. Connects to Berry (degree 5, groove syncopation in jazz piano), Maktub (degree 18, funk-inflected collective syncopation), and Anderson (degree 3, vocal phrasing across the bar line).

- **Neo-Soul Harmony in a Collective Context -- Jazz Extensions Deployed for Atmosphere:** Jazz-informed chord progressions -- extended voicings, altered tensions, suspended structures -- deployed in a groove context where harmony provides atmosphere rather than functional direction. The Erykah Badu / D'Angelo vocabulary: minor 9ths floating over a deep pocket, harmonic motion through voice-leading within the chord rather than chord-to-chord progression. The collective model changes how harmony is deployed: without years of shared voicing development (as Maktub had), FMR relies more on harmonic convention (shared neo-soul vocabulary) and less on harmonic idiosyncrasy (unique voicings from long collaboration). More generic but more resilient and more accessible to new participants.

- **COLLECTIVE IMPROVISATION WITH ROTATING PERSONNEL -- Lingua Franca Model:** Improvisation in the collective relies on shared conventions rather than shared history. The musicians may be meeting for the first time at soundcheck. The neo-soul/R&B/jazz vocabulary serves as a lingua franca that enables strangers to create coherent music together. The collective model selects for musicians who are fluent in the shared language; the fixed band model selects for musicians who develop a private language together. This is the improvisational equivalent of a common tongue.

- **THE LIVE SHOW AS PRIMARY MEDIUM -- Every Performance Is a Different Creation:** The live show IS the art, and every show is unique because the ensemble is potentially different. The recording documents one configuration at one moment; the next performance will be a different configuration, a different creation. This extends the live-improvisation-as-composition thread from Maktub (degree 18) but adds the variable-personnel dimension: not only is the improvisation different every night, the improvisers are different every night.

- **FIFTH CONSECUTIVE ENERGY-3 ARTIST (Longest Streak EXTENDS to Five):** Distribution shifts from 2:13:4 to **2:13:5**. Soul-communal subtype added. The energy source is the relationship between collective and audience -- neo-soul live performance as communal ritual.

### Part B: Veery -- The Descending Spiral (CATHARUS TRIPTYCH COMPLETE, Matched Pair Closed)

Full deep research covering the third Catharus thrush, the descending spiral as bioacoustic and musical structure, the completion of the Catharus triptych, bilateral syrinx as chorus effect, the tension-resolution pair, and lowland riparian ecology:

- **Taxonomy -- The Third Catharus: THE TRIPTYCH COMPLETE (Three Pitch Directions Exhaust the Space):** Family Turdidae (thrushes), order Passeriformes. Genus *Catharus* from Greek *katharos*, "pure." The Veery (*Catharus fuscescens*) becomes the third *Catharus* in the circle after the Hermit Thrush (degree 7) and the Swainson's Thrush (degree 18). No other genus in the engine has three representatives. The species epithet *fuscescens* means "becoming dusky" (Latin *fuscus* + inchoative *-escens*). Named by James Francis Stephens in 1817. The common name is onomatopoeic -- an attempt to transcribe the descending *veer-veer-veer*. Unlike "Hermit Thrush" (behavior) or "Swainson's Thrush" (naturalist), the Veery's name IS the sound. The bird named itself. Physical: 6.5-7.5 inches, 26-54 grams. The warmest-toned of the three Catharus: uniform warm cinnamon-brown upperparts, faint blurred breast spots, no bold eyering, no rufous tail contrast. The least marked, warmest, gentlest of the three. The least common Catharus in the PNW -- core breeding range is the northeastern US and southern Canada, the Pacific Northwest represents the western edge.

- **Bioacoustics -- THE DESCENDING SPIRAL: The Song That Falls (PROGRESSION ADDS):** The Veery's song is the **mirror image** of the Swainson's Thrush. Where the Swainson's spirals upward, the Veery spirals **downward**: ethereal, reedy phrases, each phrase lower than the last, creating a descending helical staircase through pitch space. Frequency range: 1.5-4.5 kHz. Starting pitch: ~3.0-4.5 kHz (highest in sequence). Terminal pitch: ~1.5-2.5 kHz (lowest). Phrase duration: 0.5-1.5 seconds. Song duration: 2-5 seconds (shorter, more compact than the Swainson's 4-12 seconds). The quality is "dreamy," "underwater," "otherworldly" -- the most ethereal voice in North American birdsong. The defining feature is the inter-phrase pitch trajectory: each successive phrase begins at a lower pitch. The Swainson's ascending spiral creates the feeling of climbing; the Veery's descending spiral creates the feeling of **settling, resolving, coming home**. Dusk and dawn calling, with strong dusk bias.

- **THE CATHARUS TRIPTYCH COMPLETE -- Three Pitch Directions Map the Complete Pitch Space:** Hermit Thrush (degree 7): harmonic series, STATIC (vertical/chordal -- notes relate within a phrase), musical function = drone / harmony / the chord. Swainson's Thrush (degree 18): ascending spiral, ASCENDING (horizontal/melodic upward -- tension), musical function = departure / question / tension. Veery (degree 19): descending spiral, DESCENDING (horizontal/melodic downward -- resolution), musical function = arrival / answer / resolution. Static, ascending, descending -- these three directions exhaust the possibilities. There are no other directions pitch can go. The triptych maps to a complete grammar of pitch motion: the Hermit Thrush is the NOUN (the thing itself, what the chord IS), the Swainson's Thrush is the VERB of departure (ascending, going out, tension, the question), the Veery is the VERB of return (descending, coming home, resolution, the answer). Every melody in every tradition can be decomposed into these three motions: staying, rising, falling. The genus *Catharus* provides all three.

- **BILATERAL SYRINX POLYPHONY -- The Chorus Effect in Biological Form:** The Veery produces two independent fundamental frequencies simultaneously through the left and right halves of its bilateral syrinx. The two tones are closely spaced -- separated by a few hertz to a few tens of hertz -- producing beat frequencies: periodic amplitude fluctuations perceived as shimmering, pulsing, wavering. This is the chorus effect. A chorus pedal duplicates a signal, detunes the copy, and mixes them. The Veery has been doing this for millions of years. The bilateral syrinx comparison: Pacific Wren (degree 1) uses bilateral polyphony for MELODIC COMPLEXITY (two interleaved voices, 100 notes in 10 seconds -- two-part counterpoint). The Veery uses it for ATMOSPHERIC SHIMMER (two closely-spaced tones creating beat frequencies -- the chorus pedal). Same organ, opposite applications: information density vs. atmospheric texture. The Veery modulates the frequency difference dynamically, controlling shimmer rate as a musical parameter in real time.

- **THE TENSION-RESOLUTION PAIR -- Swainson's + Veery, Matched Pair CLOSED:** The Swainson's Thrush (degree 18) spirals UPWARD: aspiration, tension, the question. The Veery (degree 19) spirals DOWNWARD: resolution, release, the answer. Adjacent degrees, both *Catharus*, opposite trajectories. Together they form a complete narrative arc -- the oldest story in music: you go out, and then you come home. Half cadence (hanging -- degree 18) and authentic cadence (resolved -- degree 19). The matched pair that degree 18 set up is now closed. The engine has been building toward this pairing since degree 7. Eleven degrees later, the Swainson's asked the question. One degree after that, the Veery answers it.

- **LOWLAND RIPARIAN -- The Thicket at the Edge of Range:** Dense, deciduous undergrowth along streams and rivers at lower elevations than the Swainson's closed-canopy riparian forest. Willow-alder thickets, cottonwood bottomlands, red-osier dogwood and snowberry. The Veery is uncommon in the PNW -- western margin of its breeding range, concentrated in northeastern Washington with scattered records west of the Cascades. The descending spiral at the edge of range. Distinct from the Swainson's habitat in structure: lower, denser, more deciduous, less vertically stratified. The Swainson's sings from mid-canopy; the Veery sings from the thicket interior. The ascending spiral exploits vertical complexity; the descending spiral fills the horizontal thicket.

- **Conservation -- Significant Long-Term Decline, Steeper Than Swainson's:** Least Concern (IUCN). Approximately 12 million breeding population (Partners in Flight 2020) -- roughly one-eighth the Swainson's. Breeding Bird Survey shows significant long-term decline of approximately 1.5% per year since 1966 -- steeper than the Swainson's 0.7%. Cumulative loss over six decades approaches 55-60%. Climate change models predict northward range shifts that could further reduce the PNW's already marginal breeding population.

### The Degree 19 Parallel

The resonance between Fly Moon Royale and the Veery operates on three primary axes:

1. **Both operate at the resolution/groove end of their respective arcs -- the arrival, not the departure.** Fly Moon Royale settles into pocket grooves -- the music resolves into feel rather than escalating through ascent. The Veery's descending spiral resolves into repose rather than climbing through aspiration. Where Maktub (degree 18) and the Swainson's Thrush both climbed -- layered ascent, collective staircase -- FMR and the Veery both arrive. The groove IS the resolution. The descending spiral IS the answer. Both are about the homecoming, not the journey.

2. **Both are structurally gentler versions of their immediate predecessors, completing a tension-resolution pair at the paired-degree level.** Maktub was a fixed four-piece with irreplaceable chemistry and high-energy live shows. Fly Moon Royale is a rotating collective with ambient communal energy -- consistent aesthetic, variable personnel, gentler. The Swainson's Thrush ascends with tension. The Veery descends with resolution -- shorter songs, tighter phrases, the melody unwinding rather than building. The paired degree (18-19) enacts tension-resolution in both engines simultaneously: the ascending/high-energy entry followed by the descending/resolving entry.

3. **Both represent persistence through transformation -- the thing that endures not because it stays the same but because it adapts.** The Central District persists through gentrification: different demographics, different genre, same geographic commitment to music. The Veery persists at the edge of its range: uncommon, declining, but still singing from the lowland thickets. Both are the rare continuation of something that was once abundant. Both are the thread that does not break.

## The First Quadrant Complete

Degree 19 is the twentieth species and the twentieth artist in the circle (degrees 0-19). The first 20 degrees of 360 constitute the **first quadrant** -- not a quarter of the circle geometrically (that would be 90 degrees) but the first **jazz quadrant**, the foundational arc where every artist has carried "Jazz" somewhere in their genre label (as primary, compound, superposition, or modifier). From Quincy Jones at degree 0 through Fly Moon Royale at degree 19, the engine has mapped the full trajectory of jazz as practiced in Seattle: from postwar big-band arranging through free improvisation, composed improvisation, West African roots, piano groove, vocal ballad, cool jazz, experimental electronics, organ trio, cocktail jazz, salon teaching, guitar meditation, classical crossover, spiritual jazz, acid jazz, fusion, genre superposition, neo-soul, and now the collective.

The first quadrant tells a complete story: **jazz arrived, evolved, expanded, and ultimately dissolved its own boundaries**, becoming a method rather than a genre. The transition happened gradually over twenty degrees, but the critical zone was degrees 16-18: fusion softened the boundary, superposition broke it, and Soul/Jazz shifted the center of gravity. At degree 19, Jazz remains in the genre label as a modifier, but the music is now neo-soul with jazz technique -- the inversion is complete. The first quadrant is the jazz arc: everything from the founding at Jackson Street through the dissolution into soul.

The next degree -- degree 20 -- enters the **soul/R&B transition zone**. What comes next will determine whether the genre shift at degree 18 was a local event (soul music as a one-time destination) or a structural transition (the circle moving permanently into territory where soul, R&B, gospel, funk, and hip-hop are the primary genres and jazz is the historical substrate). The first quadrant planted the roots. The second quadrant will grow from them. Twenty artists. Twenty species. Twenty sounds. The jazz quadrant is closed.

## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **The THIRD ORGANIZATIONAL TYPE at degree 19 completes a clean three-entry typology (solo, band, collective) that emerged naturally from the data rather than being imposed.** Solo artists dominated degrees 0-17 because the jazz tradition is individual-centered. The first band appeared at degree 18 because neo-soul shifted toward collective groove. The first collective appears at degree 19 because the rotating-ensemble model is a natural extension of the collective principle into a more fluid form. Each type appeared when the genre context demanded it. The typology was not planned -- it was discovered. This is the engine's most reliable structural signal: when a new category appears, it appears because the data requires it, not because the analyst anticipated it.

- **The Central District oscillation pattern -- present 0-6, absent 7-10, back 11, absent 12-18, back 19 -- is now the engine's clearest example of geographic persistence through social transformation.** The CD's musical thread has survived gentrification not because the same people stayed (they largely didn't) but because the geographic identity of the neighborhood continues to attract and sustain musicians who root their work in the place. The pattern is poignant because each return is in a different genre and a different era. The thread does not repeat; it transforms. Jazz piano (Berry, 5) became salon teaching (Brazil, 11) became neo-soul collective (FMR, 19). The neighborhood's musical identity is not a genre -- it is a commitment to groove-first, community-rooted music-making. The genre changes. The commitment persists.

- **The CATHARUS TRIPTYCH is the most analytically productive taxonomic structure in either engine because it maps the complete pitch-direction space through three species from the same genus.** Static (harmonic series, degree 7), ascending (ascending spiral, degree 18), descending (descending spiral, degree 19). These three directions exhaust the possibilities for pitch motion. The triptych is closed -- there is nothing left to add. The genus named "pure" provides the pure forms of each pitch direction. ## Lessons Learned

1. **The three organizational types (solo, band, collective) form a gradient of creative-unit fluidity, and the gradient correlates with genre evolution.** Jazz (individual-centered, virtuoso tradition) naturally produced solo artists. Neo-soul (groove-centered, collaborative tradition) naturally produced bands and then collectives. The organizational type is not independent of the genre -- certain genres structurally favor certain creative-unit configurations. Future degrees should track how organizational type correlates with genre. The engine may discover that each major genre transition brings a new organizational type.

2. **Geographic persistence through gentrification is documented through the CD oscillation pattern, and the mechanism is not continuity of community but continuity of place-identity.** The Central District's musical thread survives not because the same people stayed but because the neighborhood's identity as a musical place continues to attract artists who root their work there. This is a structural insight about how neighborhoods sustain creative cultures: the place carries meaning independent of its current residents. Musicians choose the CD not because of who lives there now but because of what the CD means in Seattle's musical history. The geographic thread is carried by narrative identity, not demographic continuity.

3. **The Catharus triptych demonstrates that the most powerful taxonomic comparisons exhaust a parameter space rather than merely sampling it.** Three species, three pitch directions, complete coverage. The triptych is powerful precisely because there is nothing left to add -- static, ascending, and descending are the only options. Future multi-species comparisons within a genus should aim for completeness: not "here are three interesting species" but "here are all the strategies this genus uses, and together they cover the full space of possibilities." Exhaustive coverage turns a comparison into a framework.

4. **Tension-resolution pairs placed at adjacent degrees are the engine's most efficient structural device because they encode a complete musical argument in the minimum number of entries.** Degrees 18 and 19 together say more about pitch direction than either says alone. The ascending spiral only achieves its full meaning when the descending spiral answers it. The principle: when the engine encounters an entry that embodies one half of a fundamental duality (tension/resolution, major/minor, consonance/dissonance), it should actively seek the complementary entry for an adjacent degree. Matched pairs at adjacent degrees maximize analytical yield per degree.

5. **The first quadrant (degrees 0-19) is the jazz arc, and its completion provides the engine's first opportunity for retrospective structural analysis across a full arc.** Twenty degrees is enough data to identify patterns that individual degrees cannot reveal: the CD oscillation, the energy-3 streak, the gender imbalance, the genre transition mechanism (soften-break-shift), the Catharus triptych. These patterns emerged over multiple degrees and are only visible in aggregate. The engine should perform arc-level analysis at each quadrant boundary (degree 19, 89, 179, 269, 359). Each quadrant boundary is a natural checkpoint for structural review.
