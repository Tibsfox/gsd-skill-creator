## Lessons Learned

1. **The invisible band is the engine's most important methodological advance.** Previous degrees assumed that documented output (albums, press, criticism) would exist. Degree 28 proves that an artist can be structurally significant -- can play 150+ gigs per year, employ a rotating roster of musicians, sustain the live-performance infrastructure of an entire region -- and leave no archival trace. The research methodology had to adapt: when there are no secondary sources, first-party promotional materials and venue listings become the primary archive. The absence of documentation IS the finding. Lesson: the engine should expect invisible bands at subsequent degrees and be prepared to document them.

2. **Continuity agents are the engine's richest cross-reference type.** Jeff Davies connecting Berry (degree 5) to Night Owls (degree 28) is more structurally significant than any single-degree finding at degree 28. The chain reveals the connective tissue of Seattle's music ecosystem: the same musicians reappearing across decades, carrying harmonic vocabulary and institutional memory from one era to the next. Lesson: track continuity agents at all subsequent degrees. The pattern is likely universal in any city's music scene.

3. **Hemispheric expansion was inevitable and should be tracked.** All prior degrees documented American musical DNA. As the engine moves into the contemporary era, international lineages become more visible. Seattle is a Pacific Rim port city. Christian Fattoruso's Latin American inheritance is the first non-North-American DNA. Asian, African, and other lineages will follow. Lesson: flag hemispheric expansion when it occurs and track the DNA pool's diversification.

4. **The centrist is not average -- it is flexible.** The Tree Swallow lesson applies to Night Owls as well. The centrist has capabilities the extremes lack: the Tree Swallow eats berries (no other swallow can); Night Owls play every genre (no headlining act would). Being in the middle frees the organism from the constraints that trap the extremes. Flexibility is the centrist's strategy. Lesson: when documenting a middle-position artist or species, look for the unique capability, not just the moderate values.

5. **Three consecutive liquid elements is the maximum before the engine must change timbre.** The liquid trilogy at degrees 26-28 is the densest timbral cluster in engine history. The water cycle is complete: stream, rain, brook. Degree 29 must be a non-liquid sound. Lesson: three is the maximum consecutive run for any single timbral family.

6. **The E=4 plateau at eight degrees is now the engine's defining structural tension.** The variety within the plateau (eight different mechanisms, eight different subtypes) demonstrates that sustained energy does not require monotony. But the dynamic range of the engine is collapsing. The break at degree 29 will be one of the most structurally significant events in the engine. Lesson: the plateau must break, and the break direction (up to E=5 or down to E=2/E=3) will define the next arc.

7. **The Aurora Avenue thread demonstrates geographic continuity in venue infrastructure.** Parker's (1964) and Aurora Borealis (2010s) on the same highway. The specific rooms change but the highway keeps hosting music. Lesson: track geographic continuity across degrees. Infrastructure persists even when the institutions that occupy it turn over.

8. **The owl resonance opens a new analytical dimension.** First direct name echo between artist and species tracks. The resonance is structural, not coincidental: both Night Owls and the Spotted Owl (degree 4) are defined by territorial persistence. Lesson: track artist-species name resonances at all subsequent degrees.

9. **First consecutive genre repeat signals zone stability.** The genre has found its center at Soul/R&B. The label that accurately describes the tradition no longer needs modification. Lesson: consecutive genre repeats may signal that the engine has entered a stable zone. Track how long the repetition lasts before the next genre shift.

10. **The swallow trio demonstrates that three degrees is enough to resolve a family.** The warbler quintet used five degrees over seven. The thrush quintet used five degrees over twenty-four. The swallow trio achieves comparable resolution in three consecutive degrees. The minimum viable family sample: one extreme, the opposite extreme, the synthesis. Lesson: future family clusters should aim for three-degree density when the data supports it.
