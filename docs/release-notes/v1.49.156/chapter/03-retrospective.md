## Lessons Learned

1. **Structural debt accumulates invisibly until the degree that resolves it reveals how much was owed.** The Duwamish acknowledgment was flagged at degree 3 and escalated repeatedly for seventeen degrees without implementation. The resolution at degree 20 is clean, but the debt was real: twenty-one releases on Duwamish land without acknowledgment. The lesson is not that the debt was resolved but that it should never have accumulated. Future engine-level obligations (gender tracking, territorial acknowledgment, conservation status updates) should be resolved within one degree of identification, not deferred through eighteen.

2. **Three data points transform a coincidence into a thread.** With two women in the engine (degrees 3 and 6), the patterns were invisible: both Black, both departed, both vocal -- but with N=2, any shared property could be coincidental. With three women (adding degree 20), the shared properties become patterns: all Black, all departed, all vocal-primary or multi-instrumentalist. The gender thread is now the engine's most persistent cultural pattern. The lesson: the third instance of any structural category is the one that reveals the pattern's character. Track aggressively after the second instance; analyze structurally after the third.

3. **The twenty-first acoustic element required both engines to name because the ADSR envelope's Sustain and Release parameters are two halves of the same acoustic truth.** Part A contributes sustain (the held tone, the busker's projection). Part B contributes the release (the fade, the trill's dissolution). Together they describe the complete arc: everything that sustains eventually releases. This is the first acoustic element in the engine that is inherently bilateral -- it cannot be fully described from either engine alone. Future elements may similarly require both engines to articulate. The bilateral naming is a feature, not a limitation.

4. **Closed sets (the Parulidae quintet's five endings, the Catharus triptych's three pitch directions) are the engine's most powerful analytical structures because they provide completeness proofs.** Five song endings exhaust the possibilities. Three pitch directions exhaust the space. Once a set is closed, no further entries can add to it -- only confirm or challenge its completeness. The engine should seek closed sets actively: when three or four entries from the same taxonomic group reveal a pattern, ask whether one more entry would close the set. Closing a set is more analytically valuable than adding another open-ended data point.

5. **The Street Laureate pattern demonstrates that the engine's artist-city taxonomy grows by necessity, not by design.** Twenty-one degrees, twenty-one patterns. No two artists relate to Seattle the same way. The taxonomy was not planned at degree 0 -- it emerged as each new artist demanded a category that didn't yet exist. This is the engine's most reliable structural signal: when a new category appears, it appears because the data requires it. The taxonomy will continue to grow. By degree 360, it may contain 360 patterns. Each one will have been discovered, not invented.

6. **The warbler quintet and the hidden-crown motif demonstrate that even the plainest, most overlooked entry can contribute the most structurally significant material.** The Orange-crowned Warbler is the plainest warbler in North America -- identification by absence of field marks, the bird nobody notices. Yet it completes the warbler quintet, introduces the ADSR release envelope, extends the hidden-feature motif, breaks the riparian streak, introduces the partial migrant category, and expands the interspecies eavesdropping network. The plainest bird delivered the most structural advances. The parallel to Whitney Monge is exact: the most understated artist broke the gender gap, introduced a new neighborhood, established a new pattern, and resolved the engine's most overdue debt. Plainness is not absence. Hidden quality is still quality.
