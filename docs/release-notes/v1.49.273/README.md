## Kevin Cole -- The Architect

Kevin Cole is the **KEXP Program Director** -- the person who shapes the station's identity at the structural level. Where Cheryl Waters (130), John Richards (131), and Darek Mazzone (132) are the *voices* of KEXP, Cole is the *architect*. He hires the hosts. He sets the curatorial direction. He decides what kind of station KEXP is going to be. The hosts execute a vision; Cole shapes it.

This is a different kind of radio contribution. Pat O'Day (129) built AM radio empires through personality and promotion. Poneman and Pavitt (127-128) built a label and a zine that amplified Seattle's underground. Cole builds an *institution* -- a station that outlasts any single host or show. KEXP's survival through the transition from KCMU, through the move to the new building in the Seattle Center campus, through the streaming era -- that's institutional leadership, and Cole is central to it.

**Tablam** -- Cole's own music project. The detail matters because it reveals what kind of person runs KEXP: a musician. Not a media executive, not a business strategist, but someone who makes music and also happens to run a radio station. This is the KEXP ethos distilled: the station is run by people who love music enough to make it themselves.

E=3. Same energy as Waters and Richards. Cole's role is structural and curatorial, not performative. The energy is in the design of the system, not in the output of the system. Low E, high impact.

**KEXP at 90.3 FM, Capitol Hill.** Cole is the fourth consecutive KEXP figure (130-133) and the eighth consecutive radio/media figure (126-133). The radio sequence that began with Nasty Nes Rodriguez continues to deepen.

## Snow Goose -- The Shrill Flock

The Snow Goose (*Anser caerulescens*) delivers one of the most distinctive sounds in the entire engine: **the flock call**. A single Snow Goose produces a shrill, high-pitched, nasal *honk* that is already more dissonant than other geese. But Snow Geese don't travel alone. They travel in flocks of thousands -- tens of thousands. And when 30,000 Snow Geese lift from a field simultaneously, the sound is a wall of shrill dissonance. Not musical. Not harmonious. Just overwhelming.

E=5. The highest energy among the recent geese, matching Canada Goose (129). The energy comes from sheer volume -- not the volume of a single bird, but the cumulative volume of thousands. The Skagit Valley staging grounds north of Seattle host one of the Pacific Northwest's great wildlife spectacles: massive flocks of Snow Geese wintering in the agricultural flats, visible from the roads, audible from a mile away.

**Conservation irony.** The Snow Goose is a conservation success story that has become a conservation problem. Populations have recovered so dramatically from historic lows that the species now causes significant damage to its Arctic breeding habitat. Too many geese stripping too much tundra vegetation. Wildlife managers have liberalized hunting seasons to try to reduce populations -- the rare case where *more* hunting is the conservation strategy. Success breeding its own crisis.

**16th consecutive Anatidae.** Degrees 118-133. The streak: Harlequin Duck, Common Merganser, Hooded Merganser, Wood Duck, Bufflehead, Barrow's Goldeneye, Common Goldeneye, Greater Scaup, Canvasback, Tundra Swan, Trumpeter Swan, Canada Goose, Cackling Goose, Brant, Greater White-fronted Goose, Snow Goose. Sixteen species. The engine's waterfowl survey continues.

**Delta habitat.** Snow Geese favor river deltas, estuarine flats, and agricultural fields during winter -- the Skagit Valley delta is the iconic Pacific Northwest location. The habitat association is similar to the Greater White-fronted Goose (132) but at vastly greater density.
