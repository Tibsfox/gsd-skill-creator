## Summary

**Kid Sensation** arrives at E=6, opening the **Nastymix trilogy** -- three consecutive artists connected to Sir Mix-a-Lot's Nastymix Records label, the foundational infrastructure of Seattle hip-hop before Sub Pop existed for grunge. Kid Sensation was Nastymix's breakout MC beyond Mix himself, with "Back 2 Boom" and "Seatown Ballers" establishing a Seattle-specific hip-hop identity in the late 1980s. On the species side, the **Common Murre** calls at E=5 with a growling, nasal **murrrr** -- the vocalization that gave the bird its name. The Common Murre is the most abundant alcid in the Pacific Northwest, breeding in dense cliff colonies where birds stand shoulder-to-shoulder on bare rock ledges. Its egg is **pear-shaped** -- pointed at one end so it rolls in a tight circle rather than off the narrow ledge, one of the most elegant evolutionary solutions in ornithology. The 4th Alcidae, and the densest colony bird yet.
