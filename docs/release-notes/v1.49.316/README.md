## Part B: Tufted Puffin — The Third Alcidae and the Conservation Crisis

- **Golden Head Tufts Seasonal Display.** The elongated pale-yellow feather plumes extending backward from above the eyes during the breeding season — the "tufts" that give the species its common name — enter the engine's anatomical vocabulary as a seasonal-display morphology feature unique among Pacific Northwest seabirds alongside the Rhinoceros Auklet's breeding-season bill horn at the preceding degree; the tufts emerge in late winter through early spring and are shed at the conclusion of breeding.
- **Triangular Orange Bill Morphology.** The massive carotenoid-pigmented breeding-plate bill — triangular in silhouette, vivid orange-red at the base, pale-yellow in the midsection, red-orange at the tip — operates as a breeding-display feature whose outer keratin plates shed after the breeding season to reveal a smaller and duller post-breeding bill underneath; the bill morphology is unique among Pacific Northwest seabirds and enters the engine's anatomical vocabulary at degree 176.
- **Low Groaning Arrr Call.** The defining acoustic signature is a low, throaty, groaning **arrr** vocalization delivered primarily from within burrows or from rock crevices at colony sites, operating at E=4 clarity-and-carry calibration at the individual-burrow scale and carrying a different harmonic structure and shorter fundamental duration than the Rhinoceros Auklet's moaning uurrr at the preceding degree.

- **Washington Colony Sites and Collapse Drivers.** Remaining Washington Tufted Puffin colonies concentrate at **Protection Island** (Strait of Juan de Fuca), **Smith Island** (Strait of Juan de Fuca), **Destruction Island** (outer Pacific coast), and **Tatoosh Island** (Cape Flattery); the collapse is driven by **marine heatwave warming** (including the 2013-2016 Northeast Pacific "Blob"), **Pacific sand lance** and **Pacific herring** prey decline, **colony disturbance** from human activity, and broader **climate change** effects on the oceanographic regime.
