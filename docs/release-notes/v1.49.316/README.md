On the species side, the **Tufted Puffin** calls at E=4 with a low, groaning **arrr** from deep within its nesting burrow -- one of the most iconic and most endangered seabirds in Puget Sound. The Tufted Puffin's population in Washington has collapsed by over 90% since the 1970s, with breeding colonies on islands like Smith and Protection islands reduced to a handful of pairs. The golden head tufts and massive orange bill make it the most visually striking alcid in the Pacific, but its conservation crisis is acute. The third Alcidae, and now the most urgent.
