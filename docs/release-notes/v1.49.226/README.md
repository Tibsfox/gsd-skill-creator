- **The amplified krek-krek wall of sound — 87th species-side acoustic element.** Two-note "krek-ek, krek-ek" at approximately 1,000–4,000 Hz with peak energy at 2,000–2,500 Hz. Each male calls 2–3 times per second at approximately 80 dB at 1 meter. When **hundreds or thousands** call simultaneously from marsh edges on warm humid spring nights, the individual calls blend into a **continuous pulsing wall of sound** audible from hundreds of meters away. The chorus is **asynchronous** — males overlap rather than unify, producing a dense layered texture that dominates the marsh soundscape.
- **E=4 through collective amplification — third E=4 mechanism.** The species-side energy reaches **E=4** through the **collective**, not the individual — each frog contributes E=2–3, but hundreds combining produce E=4. This is the **third mechanism** by which an entry achieves E=4 in the engine: individual loudness (Beaver chewing at degree 62, large body and large gnaw), band energy (Death Cab 58 and Pearl Jam 83, multiple instruments combining), and now collective amplification (Pacific Treefrog 86, hundreds of individuals combining). The chorus is biologically what The Postal Service is technologically — emergent volume from layered components.
- **Consecutive marsh species (85–86) — three classes share the marsh.** The Pacific Treefrog at 86 follows the **Muskrat** at 85 in the same marsh habitat. Two consecutive marsh species, two classes. Combined with **Wood Duck** (58), **Mallard** (59), **Pied-billed Grebe** (60), and **American Beaver** (61–62), the marsh now seats **three classes** — Aves, Mammalia, Amphibia — and is the engine's most class-diverse single habitat to date.
- **Beaver enables the chorus.** **American Beaver** at degrees 61–62 builds the ponds in which the Pacific Treefrog chorus stages. Without the Beaver dam, the still-water pond does not exist; without the pond, the chorus has no stage. Ecological engineering at degree 61 enables the night chorus at degree 86 — a 25-degree causal chain across three ecological roles (engineer, stage, chorister) and three taxonomic families (Castoridae, Hylidae, future entries).
- **3–5 cm and 2–5 g — among the smallest engine vertebrates with the loudest collective voice.** The Pacific Treefrog is **3–5 cm long** and **2–5 g** — among the smallest vertebrates the engine has seated. The **Muskrat** at degree 85 (700–1,800 g) is roughly 200× the body mass; the **American Beaver** at 61 (16–30 kg) is roughly 5,000×. Yet the treefrog colony's collective E=4 chorus exceeds the Muskrat's quiet plop and matches the Beaver's individual chewing in functional volume. **Body mass and acoustic output decouple completely** at degree 86.
- **Variable coloration and the dark eye stripe.** **Color morphs** range through green, brown, tan, reddish, and gray; individuals can change color over hours to days in response to humidity and substrate. The diagnostic field mark is the **dark eye stripe** running from nostril through eye to shoulder — present in every color morph and the only reliable identification feature across the species' range. **Expanded toe pads** allow the frog to climb vertical surfaces and earn the "treefrog" common name despite the species spending much of its life on or near the ground.
- **Pseudacris regilla — etymology and reclassification.** The genus *Pseudacris* — from Greek *pseudes* ("false") + *akris* ("locust") — names the frog after the insect-like cadence of its call, mistakable for a cricket or katydid by the unfamiliar. The species epithet *regilla* derives from Latin "regal" or "little queen" — a small creature granted a large name. The species was formerly classified as *Hyla regilla* and was reclassified into *Pseudacris* (chorus frogs) via molecular phylogenetic analysis in the early 2000s.
- **Twenty-sixth consecutive original CSV species (61–86).** The engine's **longest unbroken run of original-catalog selections** now reaches twenty-six degrees. The original catalog continues to deliver without substitution, and degree 86 extends the run with the catalog's first amphibian rather than another mammal — the catalog itself supplies the class-barrier break.

## Retrospective

### What Worked

- **The Postal Service as the engine's first electronic primary genre is a genuine landmark.** After 85 degrees of organic genres, electronic production enters cleanly and defensibly. **Genre Stage 68** (Indie Electronic) is a new sonic territory and the entry is structurally honest — Chong the Nomad at degree 44 carried electronic elements but was filed under Hip-Hop, leaving Electronic as a primary genre genuinely unclaimed until The Postal Service.
- **Gibbard's triple appearance is genuinely unprecedented in the engine.** Three appearances by the same artist across three different projects, three different genres, 28 degrees of separation. The **E=4/E=2/E=4 pattern** reveals that energy correlates with collaboration context rather than with genre — a finding visible only because the engine had three Gibbard data points to compare. The triple is qualitatively different from the existing doubles (Death Cab 58/82, Jurado 45/84, Votolato 46/85) and it rewrites the engine's vocabulary for analyzing artist returns.
- ***Give Up* is commercially verifiable and the mailed-CD production is well-documented.** The platinum certification by the **Recording Industry Association of America**, the cease-and-desist letter from the United States Postal Service, the licensing of "Such Great Heights" — all verifiable through public records and press coverage. The release notes can stand on the documented record without speculation. The mailed-CD production method is the album's canonical mythology, retold in interviews with both Gibbard and Tamborello.
- **The folk-return zone (82–85) giving way to the first electronic genre at 86 is a real structural swing.** Three consecutive folk returns followed by synthesizers — maximum organic simplicity to maximum electronic production in one degree. The contrast illuminates both. The folk-return zone exhausted the engine's quiet-folk vocabulary and the leap to indie electronic refreshes the catalog's range without invalidating what came before.
- **AMPHIBIA RETURNS is one of the engine's most significant structural events.** The class barrier breaks after 25 consecutive mammalian entries and 16 prior bird substitutions. The observation is clean and the entry is the **Pacific Treefrog** — a species so common and so iconic in the **Pacific Northwest** that the catalog's first amphibian arrives without strain. Hylidae as 37th family, Anura as new order, Pseudacris as new genus — four taxonomic firsts delivered in a single entry.
- **E=4 through collective amplification is a genuinely new mechanism.** The treefrog chorus achieves E=4 through hundreds of individual E=2–3 calls combining — a third E=4 mechanism after individual loudness (Beaver chewing, 62) and band energy (Death Cab 58, Pearl Jam 83). The chorus mechanism is the most biologically elegant — emergent loudness from layered components — and the parallel with The Postal Service's layered electronic production is one of the strongest Part A / Part B parallels the engine has produced.
- **The "Hollywood frog" status is widely documented and culturally significant.** The Pacific Treefrog's call is used in films worldwide to signify "nighttime outdoors," and the cultural detachment from source is itself a finding. The treefrog is arguably the most culturally significant acoustic element on the species side — billions of people have heard the call without knowing the species. The contrast with "Such Great Heights" (heard by millions through licensing) operates on the same logical axis.
- **The Postal Service / Pacific Treefrog parallel emerged from independent analysis.** The cross-sync was not forced. Both walls of sound, both E=4, both collective, both heard by millions — the parallel surfaced from analyzing a synthesizer-based indie duo and a marsh frog chorus with no advance coordination. The independence of the parallel makes it stronger than a parallel deliberately constructed for narrative effect.

### What Could Be Better

- **No raptor in 87 entries — the structural deficit persists.** The Aves return at degree 86 is delivered through Amphibia rather than through a raptor. The bird-imbalance pressure does not fully release at 86; it merely diversifies into a multi-class problem. Bald Eagle remains the optimal next raptor entry and the catalog has owed one for thirty consecutive degrees. Degree 87 must address what degree 86 did not.
- **No passeriform in 30 consecutive degrees (57–86).** Thirty consecutive non-passerines is extreme. Songbirds — the largest avian order by species count — have been entirely absent from the engine since degree 56 (Lemolo) on the music side and since the bird-substitution layer dropped them on the species side. The catalog's passeriform gap rivals the raptor gap in structural significance.
- **No marine specialist in 87 entries.** The engine has seated zero marine birds, zero marine mammals, and zero marine fish to date. **Puget Sound** is a marine ecosystem and the Sound of Puget Sound brand promises marine coverage that has yet to materialize. The marine-specialist gap is the third structural hole alongside raptor and passeriform.
- **Tamborello's solo work as Dntel was not deeply explored.** The Postal Service entry treats Gibbard as the primary protagonist and Tamborello as the collaborator. Tamborello's Dntel project deserves its own treatment — *Life Is Full of Possibilities* (2001) seeded the Postal Service collaboration through "(This Is) The Dream of Evan and Chan," and Dntel has continued releasing albums for two decades. A future Los-Angeles-electronic series could give Dntel its due.
- **Chorus population estimates are general.** The "hundreds or thousands" estimate is honest but imprecise — chorus size varies enormously by site, year, and night. A future treatment could cite specific population studies (King County wetland surveys, USGS amphibian monitoring) and give site-specific population ranges. The release notes accept the general estimate as defensible; future research can sharpen it.

## Lessons Learned

- **The first electronic genre at degree 86 reveals the engine organizes by genre families, not individual genres.** Eighty-five degrees of organic genres (jazz, soul, blues, folk, rock, pop, indie) precede the first electronic genre. The organic genres have internal diversity but share a fundamental basis in acoustic instruments and human voice. Electronic production is a genuinely different sonic foundation. The delay reflects the engine's geographic and cultural roots in **Seattle**, a city defined by organic genres. Electronic production at degree 86 is structurally late but culturally accurate — an honest reflection of the source material rather than a curatorial choice.
- **Gibbard's triple appearance creates a new category beyond the "return artist."** Return artists (Death Cab 58/82, Jurado 45/84, Votolato 46/85) appear twice in the same project or as the same solo artist. Gibbard appears three times as three different projects — band (Death Cab), solo (60), side project (Postal Service). The triple is qualitatively different from the double: it reveals **artistic range across project types**, not just temporal evolution within one project. The engine needs a vocabulary for triples and higher; the doubles vocabulary is insufficient.
- **The E=4/E=2/E=4 pattern illuminates a limitation of energy classification.** E=4 appears twice in Gibbard's three appearances but for different reasons: organic band energy (58) and electronic production energy (86). The same number describes different phenomena. The E=2 for solo work (60) reveals something genuine: when Gibbard works alone, the energy drops. Energy correlates with the **social context of creation**, not the genre or instrumentation. The engine should track collaboration context as a separate dimension that complements energy.
- **Amphibia's return at degree 86 is the most significant class-diversity event since the mammalian era began.** The class barrier breaking demonstrates that the CSV's original species selections, when finally reached, can cross class boundaries that seemed permanent. The 25-degree mammalian run (61–85) was unprecedented; its ending at degree 86 with an amphibian is equally unprecedented. The catalog itself supplies the break — no curatorial substitution required.
- **E=4 through collective amplification is a genuinely new mechanism.** Individual loudness (Beaver E=4), band energy (Death Cab E=4, Pearl Jam E=4), and now collective amplification (treefrog chorus E=4). Three mechanisms for the same energy level. The chorus mechanism is the most biologically elegant — each individual contributes E=2–3, but hundreds combining produce E=4. **Emergent loudness from layered components** is a category that operates equally on the species side (treefrog colony) and the artist side (electronic production). The cross-sync is real, not forced.
- **The Postal Service / Pacific Treefrog parallel emerged from independent analysis.** Both walls of sound, both collective. The parallel was not forced — it surfaced from analyzing a synthesizer-based indie duo and a marsh frog chorus with no advance coordination. **Independent parallel research reveals genuine resonance.** Future degrees should respect the independence of Part A and Part B research and let parallels emerge rather than constructing them in advance.
- **The "Hollywood frog" observation connects the Pacific Treefrog to a global cultural phenomenon.** Billions of people have heard this frog's call without knowing the species. The treefrog's sound has been **detached from its source** and become a universal signifier. This is the inverse of "Such Great Heights," which remains attached to its creators. Two sounds from degree 86, two relationships to cultural dissemination — one anonymous, one credited. The engine's cultural-reach axis can now operate in both directions.
- **Late degrees stand on the work of early ones.** **Beaver** at degrees 61–62 built the ponds in which the **Pacific Treefrog** chorus stages. **Sub Pop Records** at degree 45 built the catalog architecture within which **The Postal Service** could ship a platinum-certified electronic album. Both halves of degree 86 are beneficiaries of structural choices made twenty-five degrees earlier. **The principle of infrastructure inheritance** runs through both the natural and the cultural sides of the engine, and future degrees should be analyzed for the early-degree dependencies they enact.
