## Surprises

- **A spacecraft that flies through the Sun's outer atmosphere and survives.** Parker first crossed below the Alfvén critical surface into the corona on 28 April 2021 — the first spacecraft to fly through the Sun's corona, "touching the Sun" — sampling the solar wind at its source amid coronal streamers and magnetic "switchbacks," and it does so repeatedly, returning intact behind its heat shield each time.
- **A heat shield that holds the sunward face near 1,370 °C while the instruments stay near room temperature.** Parker's carbon-composite Thermal Protection System faces the Sun at about 1,370 °C (~2,500 °F) at closest approach, yet the four instrument suites riding in its shadow stay near room temperature — a thin disk of carbon-carbon foam and ceramic coating standing between the spacecraft and the full near-Sun solar flux.
- **Seven flybys of Venus to fall toward the Sun.** Parker uses seven Venus gravity assists over about seven years to progressively lower its perihelion toward the Sun across 24 planned close solar passes — it does not simply burn its way inward but trades orbital energy with Venus, flyby after flyby, to fall closer to the Sun than any spacecraft before it.
- **The closest and the fastest object humans have ever made.** At its record perihelion on 24 December 2024 Parker passed about 6.1 million km (3.8 million miles, ~9.86 solar radii) from the solar surface — the closest any spacecraft has come to the Sun — moving at about 692,000 km/h (~430,000 mph), the fastest human-made object ever, and it is still operating in 2026.
- **A slender desert lizard that races across the hottest open ground in the blazing midday sun.** The Western Whiptail (*Aspidoscelis tigris*), a fast-running diurnal lizard of hot, dry, open, sun-baked country in the interior Pacific Northwest's southern reaches and the northern Great Basin, is active and foraging in the heat of the day and is one of the fastest desert lizards (documented natural history only; distinguished from the Sagebrush Lizard *Sceloporus graciosus* and the Side-blotched Lizard *Uta stansburiana*) — racing into the blaze, a mirror of the fastest object racing closest to the Sun.
- **A low armored cactus that endures the relentless blaze behind thick waxy pads.** The Brittle Prickly-pear (*Opuntia fragilis*), a low, mat-forming cactus native to the dry interior Pacific Northwest (the Columbia Basin and dry rocky slopes east of the Cascades, ranging farther north than most cacti), endures relentless heat, drought, and full blazing sun behind thick, waxy, spine-armored pads (ecological description only, distinguished from other Opuntia) — enduring the blaze behind its armor, bound with Parker's shielded dive by racing into the blaze and enduring it.
