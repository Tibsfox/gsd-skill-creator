# Lessons — v1.49.1019

11 lessons extracted. Classification source: ⚙ rule-based · 🤖 LLM tiebreaker (needs review) · 👤 human.

1. **An axis grows by extending its subject, not only by opening new axes.**
   By extending the systematic Earth observation Landsat established for the land surface to the whole Earth system, Terra sustained the earth-observation axis at obs#2 with a substrate-form-distinct observation, showing that an axis can continue at a new substrate-form rather than always rotating to a new axis.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11882_

2. **Many instruments on one platform measure the connections between the parts.**
   By carrying five instruments that view the same scene at the same time, Terra let the land, the clouds, the aerosols, the energy budget, and the air be measured together and related to one another, showing that simultaneous, co-located measurement reads the connections that separate instruments cannot.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11883_

3. **A flagship opens an era and prepares the way.**
   By being the first of the great EOS observatories, Terra opened the era of multi-instrument whole-Earth-system observatories and prepared the way for the companions that followed, showing that a flagship sets the form the program builds on.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11884_

4. **A wide swath buys a near-daily cadence at moderate resolution.**
   By imaging a wide swath from a morning orbit, MODIS tiled the whole planet in a day or two, showing that breadth of coverage and frequency of revisit trade against fine resolution — a different point on the same design space from a high-resolution land imager.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11885_

5. **The energy budget is read by measuring both what comes in and what goes out.**
   By measuring the incoming sunlight against the outgoing reflected light and emitted heat, CERES read the radiation balance directly, showing that the budget that drives the climate system is a measurement of both halves at once.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11886_

6. **An international partnership joins many contributions into one observatory.**
   By carrying instruments from the United States, Japan, and Canada on one platform with open data, Terra joined many contributions into one flagship, showing that a shared platform and open data turn separate instruments into one observatory of the whole planet.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11887_

7. **A reference template carries forward cleanly across an intra-axis continuation.**
   Because Terra reuses the canonical card structure of the v1.209 Landsat template, the build preserved the structural template exactly and swapped the content to the flagship while keeping the warm palette, making the intra-axis continuation a clean build.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11888_

8. **The shader renders procedural structure rather than archived data.**
   The Terra earth-system-observatory shader uses fractal noise and analytic geometry rather than loading actual Terra MODIS scenes from NASA Worldview. A future revision could load encoded Terra imagery for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to real land cover, ocean color, and clouds.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11889_

9. **The five-instrument-platform diorama is a forthcoming artifact.**
   The 3D-printable diorama STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the platform, the five instruments, and the morning ground track over a whole-Earth globe.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11890_

10. **The observatory sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.**
   The sidebar lists representative axis-lineage and observatory elements (Terra, Landsat, IRAS/Spitzer, the afternoon companion) but does not enumerate the full EOS observatory set or the per-instrument specifications; a future revision could expand the table with per-instrument highlights.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11891_

11. **The energy budget and the synergy are described rather than computed.**
   The radiation balance and the multi-angle geometry are described but not run against the archived data; a future ship could query real Terra measurements of one scene and combine them to demonstrate the synergy directly.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #11892_
