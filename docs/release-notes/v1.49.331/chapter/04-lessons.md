1. **The post-hip-hop era is a multi-genre era, not a single-genre era.**
   The fourteen-degree hip-hop corridor (175-188) ended at degree 189 with Mudhoney's grunge return, and the question at degree 190 was whether the post-hip-hop axis would consolidate around any one genre. The answer — indie pop entering at degree 190 — confirms that the era is diversified by design. Grunge and indie pop together establish that the axis will document genre breadth rather than depth, and subsequent degrees should continue the pattern.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7679_

2. **First-in-category entries do not require peak intensity.**
   Harvey Danger at E=5 and Minke Whale at E=4 are both mid-range energy ratings, yet both anchor first-in-category registers — FIRST INDIE POP and FIRST BALAENOPTERIDAE respectively. The inauguration of a register is a structural event independent of intensity, and the engine's documentation should distinguish category-entry from intensity-entry. Category entries can be modest and still carry full structural weight; intensity entries can occur later in the register without displacing the anchor.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7680_

3. **The understated carries more significance than its size suggests.**
   Both halves of degree 190 are understated in their domains — Harvey Danger is not the largest indie pop band, and Minke Whale is not the largest rorqual. Yet both carry the first-in-category anchor weight for their respective registers. The understated-entry pattern is a recurring engine vocabulary entry, and the cross-sync at degree 190 establishes the pattern as a first-class structural principle.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7681_

4. **Priority claims matter for release-model innovations.**
   Harvey Danger's September 2005 pay-what-you-want release of *Little by Little...* predated Radiohead's October 2007 *In Rainbows* by two full years, yet the historical record generally credits Radiohead with the innovation. The engine's documentation preserves the Harvey Danger priority as a structural anchor, and similar priority claims in other degrees (early Seattle grunge predating industry-level recognition, early West Coast indie predating East Coast press attention) should receive similar engine-level priority preservation.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7682_

5. **The toothed-to-baleen transition is a fundamental feeding-strategy shift.**
   Degrees 180-189 documented ten consecutive toothed marine mammals with varying phylogenetic positions but consistent feeding apparatus. At degree 190, the Minke Whale introduces baleen plates, and the feeding mechanism changes from teeth-to-prey to filter-to-prey. This is a more fundamental transition than any of the family-level changes within the toothed group, and the engine's documentation should mark such feeding-strategy transitions as first-class structural events distinct from taxonomic-position transitions.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7683_

6. **The Capitol Hill ecosystem is distinct from the Central District / Ballard DIY ecosystem.**
   Seattle indie music of the 1990s had at least two distinct geographic and cultural anchors — the Central District / Ballard DIY-house-show-studio ecosystem that produced Mudhoney and grunge, and the Capitol Hill venue-bookstore-literary-ecosystem that produced Harvey Danger and indie pop. Both are Seattle, but they are two different Seattles, and the engine's geographic vocabulary should preserve the distinction rather than collapsing Seattle into a single scene.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.347`) · lesson #7684_

7. **The family of giants opens through its smallest member.**
   Balaenopteridae contains the largest animals in Earth history, yet the engine enters the family through the 10-meter / 10-tonne Minke Whale rather than the 30-meter / 150-tonne Blue Whale. This is a deliberate entry-point choice with structural rationale: entering through the smallest member anchors the family at modest intensity and leaves ascending trajectory for subsequent entries. The pattern should generalize — family registers across the engine's taxonomic vocabulary should enter through modest-scale members and extend through larger-scale members.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7685_

8. **Mechanical-sounding biological signals are a recurring misidentification pattern.**
   The Minke Whale "boing" was recorded by the U.S. Navy in the 1960s and misidentified as non-biological for decades before Thompson & Friedl (1982) established the biological origin. The pattern — biological signal initially considered non-biological because of mechanical acoustic qualities — recurs across cetacean research history (blue whale calls were initially considered non-biological; some deep-water fish sounds were considered mechanical for decades). The engine's acoustic vocabulary should preserve such misidentification histories as first-class documentation rather than relegating them to footnote status.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #7686_
