Indie pop as a genre represents a fundamental shift in the engine: away from volume, distortion, and confrontation and toward melody, cleverness, and wry observation. The energy drops from Mudhoney's E=7 grunge to Harvey Danger's E=5, and the engine exhales. Balaenopteridae contains the largest animals that have ever lived on Earth, and the Minke Whale is the smallest member of this giant family. At 25-30 feet, the Minke is the "small" baleen whale that feeds in Puget Sound waters, producing low-frequency boings and grunts that puzzled Navy sonar operators for decades before being attributed to whales. The rorqual family gate is open, and the engine now has access to the largest acoustic producers in the ocean.
