### What Could Be Better

- **The shader renders procedural structure rather than archived data.** The Saturn-flyby shader uses analytic geometry and procedural noise rather than loading actual Pioneer 11 plasma-analyzer, magnetometer, or cosmic-ray time series from the NASA Space Science Data Coordinated Archive. A future revision could load encoded Pioneer 11 archive intervals for a higher-fidelity rendering keyed to the real Saturn encounter and outer-heliosphere cruise.
- **The Jupiter-to-Saturn trajectory diorama is a forthcoming artifact.** The 3D-printable trajectory STL files are referenced as forthcoming rather than provided; a future ship could include the actual STL geometry of the Jupiter flyby, the gravity-assist redirect, and the Saturn ring-plane traversal.
- **The Pioneer program-lineage sidebar table is illustrative rather than exhaustive.** The sidebar lists representative program elements (Pioneer 10, Pioneer 11, Voyager 1, Voyager 2, Cassini, New Horizons) but does not enumerate the full outer-solar-system fleet; a future revision could expand the table with launch dates and mission outcomes.
- **The paired-precursor two-direction comparison is asserted rather than computed.** The comparison between the Pioneer 10 and Pioneer 11 cruise records along their two directions is described as a comparator but not run; a future ship could compute and plot the two records as a function of heliocentric distance from archived data.

## Dedication

v1.183 Pioneer 11 is dedicated to Charles Hall, the NASA Ames Research Center Project Manager who carried the Pioneer project leadership forward through the Jupiter flyby and the first Saturn encounter, to John Wolfe, the Project Scientist who guided the fields-and-particles science program and the plasma-analyzer team through the Saturn encounter and the long outer-heliosphere cruise, to the Saturn-encounter science leads who planned the ring-plane traversal and read the first close observations of the Saturn system, to the TRW engineers who built the spin-stabilized companion spacecraft, and to the NASA Ames Research Center teams who operated the mission for more than two decades. Pioneer 11 launched 1973-04-05 on an Atlas-Centaur, flew past Jupiter, used the first Jupiter gravity assist to reach Saturn, made the first Saturn flyby and ring-plane traversal, and carried the paired outer-heliosphere precursor record outward along a complementary direction. After far exceeding its design lifetime, Pioneer 11 returned its final telemetry in 1995 and continues outbound toward the constellation Aquila, the companion that completed the Pioneer pair.
