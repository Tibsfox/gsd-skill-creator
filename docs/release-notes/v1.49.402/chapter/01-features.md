# Features — v1.49.402

## Ellensburg enters


- degree 261 is the first entry in the Seattle 360 engine from east of the Cascades; the Screaming Trees are from Kittitas County, Ellensburg -- a city defined by the Columbia Plateau high desert, the Yakima River, Central Washington University, and the Kittitas Valley cattle culture; their music carries this geographic origin in its texture and temperament; the engine's documentation of Pacific Northwest rock is incomplete without the east side of the Cascades, and the Screaming Trees are the correct entry point; Ellensburg's music culture is smaller than Seattle's but real, and it produced one of the most important bands in the regional canon

## Geophysics arc opens


- degree 261 opens the most geologically coherent arc the SPS track has yet produced; the arc will document seven consecutive entries from the Cascade volcanic and seismic system, moving from the continuous tremor of Rainier's hydrothermal interior to the discrete events of lahar simulation, earthquake P-wave, submarine canyon flow, glacier calving, rockslide, and mudslide; the arc is a systematic tour of what Cascade geology sounds like at the infrasonic and subsonic frequencies where geophysical energy propagates; it opens here with volcanic tremor because tremor is the baseline -- the constant, ongoing signal that Mount Rainier produces not during eruptions but during ordinary operation
