## Lessons Learned

1. **Multi-degree species arcs can resolve in low-amplitude registers and this resolution pattern captures different structural information than high-amplitude resolution.** The bear trio's 5-6-4 amplitude arc concludes with a low-amplitude vulnerability register (cub distress), which is structurally inverse to the Roosevelt Elk trio's 7-5-7 arc that concluded with high-amplitude combat. This contrast establishes that arc-shape is a first-class vocabulary in the engine's multi-degree-arc documentation, and that different species require different arc shapes to capture their full acoustic personality. Future engine documentation should track arc-shape patterns (descending, ascending, bracketing, rising-falling) as structural statistics that enable species-level-register comparison.

2. **Genre-vocabulary expansion at mid-quadrant boundaries is a structural event that marks where the engine's documentation continues to grow rather than saturate.** Genre Stage 106 (surf rock) opens at degree 202, establishing that the engine's genre-vocabulary continues to expand at meaningful structural intervals even 202 degrees into the 360-degree run. Future engine iterations should track genre-stage-openings as first-class structural events, with explicit attention to whether the opening at a specific degree boundary reflects substantive vocabulary expansion or merely incremental genre-count increase.

3. **Family-level-register-closure events (like the Ursidae closure at degree 202) are structural events that deserve explicit documentation rather than passing mention.** The black bear cub distress call at degree 202 closes the Ursidae family register for Pacific Northwest species documentation since grizzlies are restricted to remote areas and the black bear is the only lowland bear. This family-level closure is a structural event that establishes degree 202 as a family-register-boundary, and future engine iterations should track family-level-closure events as first-class structural statistics parallel to genre-stage-openings and other register-boundary documentation.

4. **Label-aesthetic-breadth across multi-degree clusters requires density to become observable as a structural pattern.** The Hardly Art streak's four-consecutive-degree cluster at 201-204 makes the label's curatorial breadth from indie rock to surf rock to hook-pop (forthcoming) to more experimental registers (forthcoming) legible as deliberate aesthetic breadth rather than as genre monoculture or single-band-dominant label pattern. Without the four-in-four-degrees density, the label's curatorial range would not be observable at the same structural level. Future label-vocabulary documentation should consider whether specific label streaks warrant explicit density-tracking and breadth-documentation as first-class structural events.

5. **Sound-thrown-outward-into-empty-space operates as a general geometric principle across aesthetic and biological domains.** La Luz's reverb and the black bear cub's high mew are both outward-reaching vocalizations but in radically different domains, and the cross-sync parallel at degree 202 establishes that outward-reach-into-empty-space operates as a general structural principle regardless of domain. Future cross-sync pairings should consider whether substantive domain-invariance observations are available, and prioritize pairings where the substantive content supports cross-domain structural statements rather than surface-level analogies.

6. **PNW-as-coherent-regional-idiom observations require grounding in actual geographic and acoustic reality rather than stylistic relocation.** La Luz's surf rock is a genuine Pacific Northwest idiom because it is built on the actual cold-water, wetsuit-based surf culture of Ocean Shores, Westport, and Long Beach, with the reverb evoking fog and the tremolo suggesting cold swells. The coherent regional idiom is possible because the geographic and acoustic reality supports it, rather than because a stylistic relocation of California surf has been mapped onto Seattle geography. Future PNW-aesthetic documentation should prioritize grounding observations in actual regional geographic and cultural reality rather than in stylistic-relocation framings.

7. **Organizing-consciousness documentation across amplitude poles establishes a three-point bandleader-as-identity register.** Isaac Brock's Modest Mouse organization (199, manic-restlessness pole, E=6), Sean Tollefson's Tullycraft organization (194, twee-pop pole, E=3), and Shana Cleveland's La Luz organization (202, reverb-drenched-melody pole, E=5) together establish a three-point bandleader-as-organizing-consciousness documentation pattern across the Q3 run. The three-point pattern makes the organizing-consciousness register legible across the full Q3 amplitude range rather than being concentrated at any single register, and future engine documentation should look for additional organizing-consciousness examples across amplitude poles not yet represented in the three-point pattern.

8. **Infant-contact-call registers extend across multiple species and warrant cross-species vocabulary documentation.** The black bear cub distress call (E=4) and the black-tailed deer fawn maa bleat (E=3) are both infant contact calls with species-specific amplitude and character variation.
