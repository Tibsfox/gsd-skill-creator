## Artist-City Patterns

- **E=2 NEW ENGINE MINIMUM** -- the energy floor drops again. E=2 is the quietest the engine has ever run on the artist axis. Ambient music by definition resists energy spikes -- it is sound designed to be inhabited rather than performed to. Brian Eno's original definition: "as ignorable as it is interesting." Hollis Resnik's ambient work operates in this tradition -- texture over rhythm, atmosphere over melody, duration over structure.
- **FIRST Ambient -- Genre Stage 95** -- Ambient enters the engine at degree 119, the ninety-fifth genre stage. The genre that's been conspicuously absent through 118 degrees of a Seattle music engine. Seattle has a deep ambient and drone scene -- Stars of the Lid connections, Lawrence English collaborations, the Kranky Records orbit. The genre arrives late but meaningfully, at the engine's quietest moment.
- **Energy descent arc** -- three consecutive drops: E=5 (117, Deep Sea Diver) to E=3 (118, Loomer) to E=2 (119, Hollis Resnik). A descent of 3 energy points in 2 degrees. The engine is approaching a valley. The species axis holds steady at E=4, creating a widening gap between the axes.
- **Local/self-released** -- no label infrastructure. The ambient tradition of self-release continues from the shoegaze entry at 118. Quiet genres, quiet distribution.

## Taxonomic State

- Classes: 2 (Aves, Mammalia)
- Orders: 8+ active in rotation
- Anatidae streak: 2 (118-119) -- Harlequin Duck + Common Merganser
- Common Merganser (*Mergus merganser*): the largest of the mergansers. The sawbill adaptation -- serrated bill edges for gripping slippery fish. A diving duck that hunts by sight underwater, chasing salmon and trout through clear water. Guttural croak is a low, harsh call, functionally opposite to the Harlequin's delicate squeak and whistle.
- Sawbill as adaptation: the merganser bill is one of the most specialized in Anatidae. Unlike the flat, filter-feeding bill of dabbling ducks, the merganser has narrow, hooked, serrated mandibles -- convergent with fish-eating mammals. A duck that hunts like an otter.
