## Through-line

> *Last crewed Moon landing. First geologist on lunar surface. Orange soil discovery at the margin of mission. Maximum profile within Apollo constraint — every metric maxes pre-existing record. The Apollo program closes with its highest-yield single mission.*

Apollo 17 launched 1972-12-07 05:33:00 UTC from KSC LC-39A — the only nighttime Apollo launch. Crew: Eugene Cernan (CDR), Ronald Evans (CMP), Harrison "Jack" Schmitt (LMP, PhD geology Harvard 1964 — first scientist-astronaut on lunar surface). LM Challenger landed 1972-12-11 19:54:57 UTC at Taurus-Littrow valley (20.19°N, 30.77°E). 3 EVAs at 22h04m cumulative; 75h surface time (longest of any Apollo); 110.5 kg samples (heaviest); LRV-3 traverse 35.7 km (longest). Orange soil discovery at Shorty crater during EVA-2 1972-12-12 was identified as 3.64 Ga pyroclastic volcanic ash glass (only direct sample of pyroclastic lunar volcanism). Iconic Earth photograph (NASA AS17-148-22727) taken 1972-12-07 ~10:39 UTC at ~45,000 km from Earth during translunar coast became the most-reproduced photograph in human history. Evans deep-space EVA 1972-12-17 ~65 min outside spacecraft at ~290,000 km. Splashdown 1972-12-19 19:24:59 UTC USS Ticonderoga.
