## Lessons Learned

1. **The FIRST HAEMATOPODIDAE entry and simultaneous CHARADRIIFORMES order opening demonstrate that multi-register first events can coincide within a single species entry.** When a species enters that simultaneously opens a new family (Haematopodidae), a new order (Charadriiformes), and a new habitat environment (rocky marine coastline), the structural significance exceeds any single-register framing. The engine's documentation at degree 166 established the multi-register first-event pattern as a documentation template, and future multi-register entries should inherit the same pattern (separate framings for family-first, order-first, and habitat-pivot events even when they coincide).
2. **The ROCKY SHORE PIVOT demonstrates that habitat-environment transitions deserve their own structural-event register separate from taxonomic first-entries.** The engine's documentation at degree 166 registered the habitat pivot from inland wetlands to rocky marine coastline as a first-class structural event rather than absorbing it into the Haematopodidae entry. The pivot framing accommodates future habitat transitions (desert, alpine, deep forest, etc.) as recurring structural-event types, and the template establishes that habitat environment is a first-class axis independent of taxonomic first-entries.
3. **The third mode of non-Seattle geographic expansion (small-town Washington via Monroe) demonstrates that origin-diversity registers should distinguish at least three modes: urban, regional, and small-town.** The engine's documentation at degree 166 established Monroe as structurally distinct from Tacoma (urban) and Spokane (regional), and the three-mode framing opens the possibility for additional modes (mid-size town, resort-community, rural-agricultural) as future non-Seattle entries appear. The geography-theory vocabulary now includes mode-distinction as a first-class register, and future origin entries should place themselves within or beyond the established three-mode taxonomy explicitly.
4. **The small-town-to-Seattle pipeline concept illustrates that regional music scenes operate through a foundation-and-infrastructure split that neither pole can provide alone.** The observation that small towns supply foundation (origin, hunger, perspective) while cities supply infrastructure (venues, collaborators, audience) — and that neither is sufficient alone — enters the scene-theory register at degree 166 as a functional analysis of regional-scene operation. The framing respects that scene depth depends on pipeline flow rather than interior production alone, and future small-town-origin artists should be documented within this pipeline framework explicitly.
5. **The cultural-distance-vs-geographic-distance observation demonstrates that migration distance is measured in relationships rather than kilometers.** The 30-mile Monroe-to-Seattle distance producing cultural distance comparable to 280-mile Spokane-to-Seattle demonstrates that scene membership is a social-geographic phenomenon rather than a physical-geographic one. The engine's documentation at degree 166 enters this as a first-class geography-theory observation, and the framing applies to any scene-migration documentation where the network distance exceeds the map distance.
6. **The red-orange bill and three learned feeding techniques demonstrate that combined morphology-plus-learning registers exceed what either component alone would support.** The Black Oystercatcher's inherited bill anatomy combined with multi-month parentally-learned feeding techniques produces a capability whose output requires both components to function. The engine's documentation at degree 166 entered the combined morphology-plus-learning register as a first-class observation, and the framing establishes a template for species whose capabilities depend on both inherited anatomy and learned behavioral skills.
7. **The frequency-shift acoustic adaptation (piping at 3,000-5,000 Hz tuned to surf noise) illustrates that habitat-specific selection pressure drives acoustic evolution independently of body size or taxonomic position.** The Black Oystercatcher's piping frequency is adapted to cut through low-frequency surf ambient noise rather than determined by body-size prediction or family-typical vocal range. The engine's documentation at degree 166 entered this frequency-shift adaptation as a first-class acoustic-adaptation observation, and the template applies to other species with habitat-specific acoustic adaptations (forest-density compensation, urban-noise compensation, etc.) that future species entries may document.
8. **The intertidal indicator species concept illustrates that narrow ecological specialization produces both vulnerability and indicator value as paired consequences of the same underlying specialization.** The Black Oystercatcher's rocky-shore exclusivity produces both high vulnerability (population declines with rocky shore degradation) and high indicator value (presence signals healthy rocky shore ecosystems). The engine's documentation at degree 166 preserved this paired-consequence framing as a first-class conservation-ecology register, and future specialist-species entries should be documented with attention to the vulnerability-indicator-value coupling.
9. **The permanent 100-500 meter territories held for decades illustrate that spatial-persistence registers can extend across timescales that exceed most passerine patterns by an order of magnitude.** The Black Oystercatcher's multi-decade territory-holding far exceeds the seasonal or annual territorial patterns of most songbirds, and the engine's documentation at degree 166 entered spatial-persistence at multi-decade timescales as a first-class behavioral register. The framing applies to other species with extended territorial persistence (raptors, corvids, some seabirds) that future species entries may document.
10. **The paired-coordinate cross-sync readings transform individual observations into multiplicative structural statements about edge specialization.** The five cross-sync parallels at degree 166 (specialist-from-the-edge, unique-tools-from-edge-position, geographic-expansion, capability-acquisition-through-learning, new-structural-territory) each converted an individual per-axis observation into a multiplicative paired observation whose structural weight exceeds the sum of the component parts. The engine's documentation at degree 166 demonstrated that systematic cross-sync reading preserves paired-coordinate meaning, and the template applies to all future degree documentation where structural resonance between Part A and Part B merits explicit synchronization framing.
