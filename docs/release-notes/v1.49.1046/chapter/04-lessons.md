1. **An axis can sustain on a new detector class, not just a new pointing strategy.**
   The X-ray-astronomy axis sustains at obs#7 because XRISM reads the high-energy sky in a way the six focusing, timing, polarimetry, precision-timing, and rapid-response observatories could not — by weighing each photon's energy as the tiny heat it deposits in a detector held at 50 mK, resolving emission-line shapes and Doppler velocities even on extended sources. The distinction is the energy-resolving detector class, not a new way of pointing, so the cadence continues the axis as a substrate-form-distinct sustain rather than rotating away.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12109_

2. **Non-dispersive spectroscopy is distinct from dispersive spectroscopy on an axis that already has both.**
   Chandra's HETG/LETG and XMM-Newton's RGS did high-resolution X-ray spectroscopy first, but dispersively, on point sources — a grating smears on an extended source. XRISM's first is the operational, sustained, non-dispersive microcalorimeter form, whose ~5 eV resolution is independent of source size, so it works on the diffuse hot gas of clusters and remnants. The qualifier keeps XRISM's spectroscopy first distinct from Chandra's grating-spectroscopy first.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12110_

3. **A first can hinge on the conjunction of its qualifiers.**
   XRISM's defining first is not the microcalorimeter device (Suzaku flew it first) nor the first microcalorimeter result (Hitomi returned the Perseus measurement) — it is the operational + sustained + non-dispersive microcalorimeter observatory, the conjunction of all three properties. The guest-observer, ultra-fast-wind-structure, and extended-source-velocity-field firsts each carry their own qualifiers (open competed program; resolve the clumpy structure, not detect the wind; extended / diffuse source, not point source).
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12111_

4. **A focusing telescope on an axis of focusing telescopes still needs the resolution stated.**
   The X-ray Mirror Assemblies genuinely focus X-rays, but at arcminute-class half-power diameter (~1.3′ for the Resolve mirror, ~1.5′ for the Xtend mirror) — roughly 150–180× coarser than Chandra's ~0.5″. Stating the modest resolution keeps "focusing," "Wolter-I," "mirror," and "imaging" correct XRISM content while never claiming Chandra's first-focusing or sub-arcsecond imaging.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12112_

5. **The microcalorimeter capability opened a resolved-velocity era of science.**
   Because Resolve measures each photon's energy directly at ~5 eV, the bulk motions and turbulence of hot plasma can be read straight from emission-line shapes and shifts — the first non-dispersive velocity field of an extended source (N132D), the resolved Fe-Kα structure of an active nucleus (NGC 4151), the quiescent core of a galaxy cluster (Centaurus), and the resolved clumpy ultra-fast wind of a quasar (PDS 456), each a consequence of energy-resolving spectroscopy on extended and point sources alike.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12113_

6. **A continuation keeps the predecessors' vocabulary and references them by name.**
   Because XRISM sustains the X-ray-astronomy axis, the shared X-ray vocabulary is correct and kept, and Chandra (obs#1, soft/medium focusing), NuSTAR (obs#2, hard-band focusing), RXTE (obs#3, timing), IXPE (obs#4, polarimetry), NICER (obs#5, soft-band timing), and Swift (obs#6, rapid-response transient) are referenced as the form complements — but XRISM's own facts (the two co-aligned instruments, the single-photon heat-pulse measurement, the cold chain, the as-flown 1.7–12 keV band, the resolved velocity fields) and only its own five anchors are stamped; the predecessors' anchors and predecessor-specific hardware (Swift's BAT / coded mask / XRT / UVOT / autonomous slew; NICER's X-ray Timing Instrument / concentrators / SEXTANT; IXPE's Gas Pixel Detectors; RXTE's PCA/HEXTE; NuSTAR's…
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12114_

7. **Accuracy guardrails guard against the design-vs-operational and fabricated-citation classes of error.**
   Distinguishing the Resolve design band (0.3–12 keV) from the as-flown operational band (1.7–12 keV, the gate valve never opened — the single most error-prone XRISM fact); attributing the Perseus 164 km/s measurement to Hitomi (2016), not XRISM; keeping the Centaurus bulk-streaming (~130–310 km/s) and turbulent-dispersion (≤~120 km/s) figures distinct; stating XRISM resolved the PDS 456 wind's structure rather than discovering ultra-fast outflows; framing it as a JAXA-led mission with NASA and ESA (the recovery of Hitomi, formerly XARM); using the 6 September 2023 H-IIA launch from Tanegashima alongside SLIM and the ~550 km / 31° orbit; and using only the verified references — keeps the facts load-bearing and correct.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12115_

8. **The status is a body of work, not a single live state.**
   XRISM is operating and conducting its science program (first light early 2024, Guest Observer Cycle 2 from ~1 November 2025, Cycle 3 ~mid-2026), with the Resolve gate valve closed (September 2025 reopening attempts unsuccessful) — described as the body of work and the honest degradation rather than asserted as a single present-tense status.
   _⚙ Status: `investigate` · lesson #12116_

9. **The shader renders procedural geometry rather than archived data.**
   The high-resolution spectroscopy shader uses analytic curves and procedural noise rather than loading an actual XRISM/Resolve heat-pulse trace or a published N132D or PDS 456 spectrum. A future revision could render a published Resolve spectrum (the N132D iron-line region or the PDS 456 wind) or a real pulse-height record for a higher-fidelity view of the data.
