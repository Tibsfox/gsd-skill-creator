2. **Comparative superlative framing is more useful than isolated superlative citation.**
   The Northern Fur Seal's 300,000 hairs/sq in is more meaningful when framed against the Sea Otter (100-150K/sq in) and the chinchilla (~20K/sq in) than when cited in isolation. The comparative framing reveals the magnitude of the adaptation (2-3× Sea Otter, 15× chinchilla) in terms the reader can evaluate. Future extreme-adaptation documentation should provide comparative framing against the nearest-competitor taxon and against a relevant non-marine or land counterpart.

9. **International treaties can predate domestic statutes and establish international-cooperation frameworks that domestic frameworks later adopt.**
   The 1911 North Pacific Fur Seal Convention predates the 1972 Marine Mammal Protection Act by sixty-one years and established the principle that international cooperation could prevent species extinction — a principle the MMPA later operationalized at the domestic level. The engine's conservation vocabulary now preserves this historical layering, and future treaty-and-statute entries can reference the international-precedes-domestic temporal pattern.
