## RIOT GRRRL


- Bikini Kill's degree 276 entry is the engine's first riot grrrl entry; riot grrrl as a cultural movement was centered in Olympia and Washington, D.C. in the early 1990s, and Bikini Kill was its defining musical act; the movement's explicit feminist politics, its zine culture, its community organizing practices, and its confrontational live performance ethic constitute a body of cultural production that is historically significant beyond the music itself; Kathleen Hanna's later work -- Le Tigre, The Julie Ruin -- continued the project that Bikini Kill began; the engine documents Bikini Kill at their origin, the Olympia riot grrrl moment, because that is where the contribution to the Pacific Northwest music record is located

## Orca Arc closes -- full portrait


- the seven-degree Orca Arc (270-276) has documented the complete behavioral acoustic range of *Orcinus orca* in the Salish Sea: pod call (social identity), whistles and clicks (close-range social and sensory), transient hunt (Bigg's ecotype, tactical silence), breaching (maximum physical energy), spy-hop (surface observation), pec-slap (social percussion), echolocation (prey search); the arc is complete; no species in the SPS track has received seven consecutive entries; the Southern Residents received this because their situation is urgent and their acoustic culture is extraordinary

## Kill Rock Stars and Olympia infrastructure


- Slim Moon founded Kill Rock Stars in Olympia in 1991, the same year Bikini Kill released *Revolution Girl Style Now!*; the label's early catalog -- Bikini Kill, Heavens to Betsy, Sleater-Kinney, Elliott Smith, Unwound -- is among the most important bodies of work in Pacific Northwest independent music; Kill Rock Stars and K Records together constituted the Olympia music infrastructure that sustained riot grrrl, lo-fi punk, and the broader Olympia underground through the 1990s; this infrastructure was built by and for the community it served, without major label involvement or commercial targeting

## SRKW salmon dependency


- Southern Resident Killer Whales require approximately 10-19 Chinook salmon per day per individual; with fewer than 75 individuals in the population, the pod requires hundreds of Chinook daily; Chinook salmon runs in the Salish Sea and the rivers that feed it have declined dramatically due to dam construction, habitat destruction, and climate change; the SRKW echolocation documented here is a whale searching for a fish that has become harder to find; the click trains that propagate through the water column are the sound of a population under nutritional stress, and the Orca Arc closes with this acoustic fact in full view
