## Summary

**RIOT GRRRL. ORCA ARC CLOSES.**

**Bikini Kill** arrive at E=8 -- the Olympia and Washington, D.C. riot grrrl band formed in 1990 by Kathleen Hanna (vocals), Tobi Vail (drums), Billy Karren (guitar), and Kathi Wilcox (bass). Bikini Kill are among the most important bands in the history of American punk rock, and the most important band to emerge from the Olympia, Washington music scene: their music, their politics, and the cultural movement they catalyzed -- riot grrrl -- constitute one of the defining moments of feminist punk music and one of the most significant cultural events in Pacific Northwest music history. The Bikini Kill catalog -- *Revolution Girl Style Now!* (1991), *Bikini Kill* (1992), *Pussy Whipped* (1993), *Reject All American* (1996) -- documents a band at the intersection of punk energy, feminist politics, and community organizing, producing music that was simultaneously a call to action, a community document, and a body of work with aesthetic commitments that outlasted the cultural moment that produced it.

E=8 for Bikini Kill is the correct assignment. Kathleen Hanna's vocal delivery is among the most energetic and politically committed in the Pacific Northwest music record: her voice on Bikini Kill recordings is loud, demanding, direct, and unwilling to be ignored. The band's rhythm section drives at hardcore energy. The guitars are raw. The production is intentionally rough. E=8 is what Bikini Kill sounds like, and it is the correct score.

Bikini Kill formed in Olympia and operated within the K Records and Kill Rock Stars infrastructure that defined the Olympia music community. Kill Rock Stars -- founded in 1991 by Slim Moon -- was the label that released much of Bikini Kill's recorded output and that became one of the primary distribution mechanisms for riot grrrl music broadly. Kill Rock Stars and K Records together constitute the Olympia music industry: two small labels, a community of artists committed to feminist and DIY principles, and a body of recorded work that constitutes one of the most politically significant chapters in Pacific Northwest music history.

Riot grrrl as a cultural movement extended beyond music: it included zine culture, consciousness-raising practices, community organizing, and an explicit feminist politics that was unprecedented in the punk world at the time. Bikini Kill was at the center of this movement, and their music is its most recognized document. Degree 276 is the engine's first riot grrrl entry, the second Olympia entry in two consecutive degrees, and the correct position for the band that makes the movement audible.

**Orca Echolocation** arrives at E=8 on the SPS track -- the full, active click train echolocation of Southern Resident Killer Whales engaged in prey search, the most acoustically intensive expression of SRKW biosonar, produced when a whale is actively scanning the water column for Chinook salmon. SRKW echolocation in full prey-search mode generates click trains of hundreds of pulses per second, directed forward into the water column, reflected from fish targets, and processed by the whale's auditory system into a three-dimensional acoustic image of the underwater environment at ranges of hundreds of meters. This is the SRKW's primary sensory system for finding food -- and finding food is survival, because the Southern Residents depend on Chinook salmon to a degree that makes their fate inseparable from the fate of the salmon.

The Orca Arc closes at degree 276. Seven consecutive SPS entries have documented the Southern Resident Killer Whales: pod calls, whistles and clicks, Bigg's transient hunt, full aerial breach, spy-hop, pec-slap, and echolocation. Together they constitute a complete acoustic portrait of *Orcinus orca* in the Salish Sea -- the social acoustic layer, the sensory layer, the behavioral expression layer, the maximum-energy event layer, and the observational layer. The arc has moved from the social announcement of group identity at degree 270 to the active prey search of degree 276: from the pod saying *we are here* to the pod asking *where is the salmon*. Both questions are the same question. The SRKW are here, and they are hungry, and the salmon they need are in decline.

The arc closes. The engine continues. But the orcas remain, in the water, clicking into a sea that answers with less than it used to.
