## Part A: Bikini Kill — First Riot Grrrl Entry, Second Olympia Entry, Kill Rock Stars Foundational Band, Kathleen Hanna Feminist Punk Vocal Delivery

- **First Riot Grrrl Entry on the S36 Track:** **Bikini Kill** enter the engine at degree 276 as the first riot grrrl entry on the S36 track. Riot grrrl as a cultural movement was centered in Olympia and Washington, D.C., in the early 1990s, and Bikini Kill was its defining musical act. The movement's explicit feminist politics, zine culture, consciousness-raising practices, and community organizing constitute a body of cultural production that is historically significant beyond the music itself, and the engine's opening of the riot grrrl register at degree 276 anchors the movement within the S36 track at its defining musical act.
- **Second Olympia Entry in Two Consecutive Degrees:** Bikini Kill's placement at degree 276 immediately follows **Beat Happening** at degree 275, establishing a two-degree Olympia sub-sequence that documents the two most influential Olympia musical acts in immediate sequence. The sub-sequence covers the full Olympia aesthetic range from Beat Happening's deliberate lo-fi aesthetic restraint at E=5 to Bikini Kill's hardcore-energy feminist punk at E=8, demonstrating that the Olympia register is not a single-aesthetic register but a community register within which multiple aesthetic practices coexist.
- **Formation, Founding Members, and Timeline:** Bikini Kill formed in Olympia in 1990 when **Kathleen Hanna** met **Tobi Vail** at Evergreen State College. The band added **Kathi Wilcox** on bass and **Billy Karren** on guitar, and by 1991 they had recorded *Revolution Girl Style Now!* and released it themselves. *Bikini Kill* (1992), *Pussy Whipped* (1993), and *Reject All American* (1996) followed on Kill Rock Stars. The band disbanded in 1998, with members continuing through **Le Tigre**, **The Julie Ruin**, and other projects; the band reformed in 2017 for a touring reunion that continues into the mid-2020s period. The engine documents Bikini Kill at their Olympia origin because that is where the contribution to the Pacific Northwest music record is located.
- **Kill Rock Stars Foundational Band and the Slim Moon 1991 Label Founding:** Bikini Kill's releases on **Kill Rock Stars** — founded in Olympia in 1991 by **Slim Moon** — anchor the engine's first Kill Rock Stars documentation. The Kill Rock Stars early catalog — Bikini Kill, **Heavens to Betsy**, **Sleater-Kinney**, **Elliott Smith**, **Unwound** — is among the most important bodies of work in Pacific Northwest independent music, and together with **K Records** the two Olympia labels constitute the music industry infrastructure that sustained riot grrrl, lo-fi punk, and the broader Olympia underground through the 1990s. This infrastructure was built by and for the community it served, without major-label involvement.

Kathleen Hanna's vocal delivery is among the most energetic and politically committed in the Pacific Northwest music record — her voice is loud, demanding, direct, and unwilling to be ignored. ## Part B: Orca Echolocation — Seventh and Final Orca Arc Degree, Prey-Search Biosonar, SRKW Salmon Dependency, Chinook-Runs-Decline Context

- **Seventh and Final Degree of the Orca Arc:** The **Orca Echolocation** at degree 276 closes the seven-degree **Orca Arc** (270-276) — the most extended single-taxon sub-sequence in the engine's catalog. The arc has now documented the complete behavioral acoustic repertoire of *Orcinus orca* in the **Salish Sea**: pod call (social identity) at degree 270, whistles and clicks (close-range social and sensory) at degree 271, transient hunt (Bigg's ecotype tactical silence) at degree 272, breaching (maximum physical energy) at degree 273, spy-hop (surface observation) at degree 274, pec-slap (social percussion) at degree 275, and echolocation (prey search) at degree 276. No species in the SPS track has received seven consecutive entries.
- **Full-Click-Train Prey-Search Biosonar:** SRKW echolocation in full prey-search mode generates click trains of hundreds of pulses per second directed forward into the water column. The whale's auditory system processes the reflections from fish targets into a three-dimensional acoustic image of the underwater environment at ranges of hundreds of meters. This is the SRKW's primary sensory system for finding food — the biosonar apparatus that allows a whale to detect individual salmon in the water column, identify species, estimate target size, and navigate toward prey across distances at which purely visual orientation would be insufficient.
- **SRKW Chinook Salmon Dependency and the 10-19 Fish Per Day Requirement:** Individual **Southern Resident Killer Whales** require approximately 10-19 **Chinook salmon** (*Oncorhynchus tshawytscha*) per day. With fewer than 75 individuals across the three-pod population (**J pod**, **K pod**, **L pod**), the aggregate population requires hundreds of Chinook daily. The SRKW are Chinook-specialist predators; the population's dietary dependence on Chinook is among the most specialized diet-to-prey-species relationships documented in the North Pacific marine mammal record, and the fate of the SRKW is directly and inseparably linked to the fate of the Chinook runs.
- **Chinook-Runs-Decline Context and the Resource-Declining Environment:** Chinook salmon runs in the Salish Sea and its tributary rivers — the **Fraser River**, the **Columbia River**, and the Puget Sound tributary rivers — have declined dramatically across the past century because of dam construction, habitat destruction, climate change, and upstream water-use pressure. The echolocation click trains that propagate through the water column at degree 276 are the acoustic signature of a population under sustained nutritional stress, searching for a prey species whose availability has been reduced by structural pressures external to the marine environment itself.
- **E=8 as Correct Amplitude for Full-Click-Train Prey-Search Intensity:** E=8 for the Orca Echolocation reflects the full acoustic event of sustained high-frequency click-train production at prey-search intensity. The assignment sits one degree below the E=9 **Orca Breaching** at degree 273 (single-moment maximum-energy aerial event) as a lower-peak but higher-duration-of-sustained-output register. The click trains are not a momentary pulse but a sustained sensory transmission lasting through the duration of an active prey-search sequence, and the E=8 assignment reflects the sustained-acoustic-output character of the behavior.
- **Biosonar Acoustic Imaging and Three-Dimensional Environmental Reconstruction:** SRKW biosonar is capable of three-dimensional acoustic imaging at ranges of hundreds of meters. The whale's forward-directed click trains and the reflection-processing capabilities of the auditory system together produce an image of the underwater environment that includes fish targets, substrate features, and other acoustic-relevant objects. This capability is not merely a sense equivalent to vision; it is a sense that allows the whale to perceive its environment across dimensions and ranges that vision alone would not support.
- **Cross-Pod Echolocation Documentation Baseline:** The echolocation behavior is documented across all three Southern Resident pods (**J pod**, **K pod**, **L pod**) with similar frequency and similar contextual associations, making it a population-wide behavioral register. The cross-pod documentation baseline is anchored in decades of **Center for Whale Research** hydrophone and behavioral observation work led by **Ken Balcomb** since 1976, and the degree 276 entry preserves the cross-pod baseline as a first-class element of the record.
- **Population-Under-Nutritional-Stress Documentation as First-Class Element:** The engine's documentation at degree 276 explicitly registers that the SRKW echolocation is the acoustic signature of a population under nutritional stress. The documentation is not neutral behavioral description; it is documentation of a survival-sensory-apparatus operating in a resource-declining environment. The nutritional-stress context is preserved as a first-class element of the record rather than being treated as secondary context, and the engine's Orca Arc closing entry thus carries the full weight of the population's survival situation as part of the formal documentation.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Arc-closing documentation at the end of an extended single-taxon sub-sequence should place the subject's most structurally significant behavioral or sensory register at the arc-closing position.** The degree 276 documentation places the SRKW echolocation — the population's primary survival-sensory-apparatus — at the seven-degree Orca Arc's closing position rather than reserving the position for a more-elaborated-register entry. The lesson is that arc-closing entries should carry the subject's most structurally significant behavioral or sensory register, surfacing the closing note as the structural statement of the arc rather than treating arc-closing as a simple sequence-end marker.
2. **Genre-register openings in the engine should anchor to the movement's defining act rather than to a later or secondary act.** The degree 276 documentation opens the riot grrrl register through Bikini Kill — the movement's defining musical act — rather than through a later riot grrrl act or a secondary representative. The lesson is that genre-register-opening documentation should anchor to the defining act because the register's foundational significance is what the opening-entry documentation is responsible for preserving.
3. **Geographic-territory documentation can establish territory-level first-class register status through consecutive-degree placement of the territory's most influential subjects.** The two-degree Olympia sub-sequence (275 Beat Happening, 276 Bikini Kill) establishes Olympia as a first-class S36 track register distinct from Seattle proper through the consecutive-degree placement of the territory's two most influential musical acts. The lesson is that territory-level register status can be established through consecutive-degree placement at the territory's most significant subjects, and future geographic-territory documentation should consider consecutive-degree placement as a register-establishment technique.
4. **Population-under-structural-pressure documentation should preserve the pressure context as a first-class element of the record rather than as secondary background.** The degree 276 documentation explicitly frames the SRKW echolocation as the acoustic signature of a population under nutritional stress, searching for a prey species whose availability has been reduced by structural pressures. The lesson is that pressure context should be preserved as first-class documentation rather than as background detail — the pressure is part of what the behavior is, and the documentation should reflect that rather than treating the behavior as neutral description.
5. **Biosonar and other primary-sensory-apparatus registers warrant first-class SPS documentation because the sensory apparatus is structurally central to the species' survival.** The SRKW echolocation is the population's primary sensory system for finding food, and the degree 276 documentation treats the biosonar at first-class structural resolution rather than as a secondary behavioral detail. The lesson is that primary-sensory-apparatus registers — biosonar, vision, chemoreception, whichever sense the species uses for survival-critical tasks — warrant first-class SPS documentation because the sensory apparatus is not incidental to the species but structurally central to its survival.
6. **Non-commercial-infrastructure documentation should be preserved as a first-class element of both cultural and biological subjects because the infrastructure is structurally central to what the subject is.** The Kill Rock Stars and K Records Olympia label infrastructure and the Center for Whale Research and associated SRKW documentation infrastructure are both preserved at first-class resolution in the degree 276 entry. The lesson is that non-commercial infrastructure is not incidental to the subject but structurally central — the Olympia music culture is the community that Kill Rock Stars and K Records built, and the SRKW scientific record is the documentation that the Center for Whale Research and its partners produced — and the documentation should preserve the infrastructure as a first-class element rather than treating it as background.
7. **Cultural-movement documentation should surface the movement's extra-musical dimensions (zines, organizing, politics) as first-class elements rather than as secondary context.** The riot grrrl movement's zine culture, consciousness-raising practices, community organizing, and explicit feminist politics are preserved at first-class resolution in the degree 276 documentation rather than being treated as background to the Bikini Kill music entry. The lesson is that cultural-movement documentation should preserve the movement's extra-musical dimensions as first-class because the movement is not just the music — the movement is the full cultural formation within which the music operated.
8. **Cross-axis documentation at the close of an extended sub-sequence can carry survival-pressure weight equivalent to the opening of a new register on the paired track.** The degree 276 cross-axis register (acoustic expressions of survival under decline) carries survival-pressure weight equivalent to the Orca Arc's closing and to the riot grrrl register's opening. The lesson is that cross-axis documentation is not a neutral pairing mechanism but can carry substantive structural weight that is equivalent to or greater than the individual-entry documentation on each track, and future cross-axis documentation should aim for substantive structural weight rather than simply pairing surface similarities.
