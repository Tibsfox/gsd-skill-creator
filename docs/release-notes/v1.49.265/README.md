## These Arms Are Snakes -- E=7 Breaks the Ceiling

These Arms Are Snakes was a Capitol Hill post-hardcore band active from 2003 to 2009. Formed from the ashes of **Botch** and **Kill Sadie**, TAAS combined angular, dissonant guitar work with rhythmic ferocity and screamed/sung vocal dynamics. The sound is abrasive, precise, and relentlessly intense -- post-hardcore pushed to its structural limits.

The lineup included members of some of Seattle's most significant heavy bands. **Brian Cook** (bass) is the connective thread: Botch (1993-2002, math-metal, Hydra Head Records), These Arms Are Snakes (2003-2009, post-hardcore, Suicide Squeeze), and Russian Circles (2004-present, post-rock/post-metal, Sargent House). One musician spanning three bands, three genres, three labels, three decades. The arc traces heavy music's evolution in the Pacific Northwest from math-metal through post-hardcore to post-rock.

**Suicide Squeeze Records** enters the engine. Founded in Seattle in 1999, Suicide Squeeze is a Capitol Hill independent label with a roster that spans punk, indie rock, and experimental music -- Minus the Bear, The Album Leaf, early Modest Mouse singles. Another structurally significant Pacific Northwest label.

E=7. The engine has never been here before. Every previous maximum was E=6 (Haunted Horses at 114, Wellwater Conspiracy at 123). TAAS doesn't merely match the ceiling -- it breaks through. The delta from Horse Feathers (124, E=4) is 4 energy levels -- the widest single-degree gap in engine history. The engine doesn't climb to the new maximum; it detonates.

## Greater Scaup -- The Scaup Call and the Anatidae Octet

The Greater Scaup (*Aythya marila*) is a medium-large diving duck found in saltwater bays, harbors, and coastal waters across the Pacific Northwest. A sea duck -- more marine than most of the Anatidae entries so far. The name "scaup" is onomatopoetic -- derived from the bird's soft, low-pitched call.

The soft scaup call: a low, breathy, descending note. Quiet and unassuming. E=3. After the wing-whistle's mechanical novelty at degree 124, the Greater Scaup returns to a conventional vocalization -- but a gentle one. The Anatidae family continues to demonstrate its acoustic range: squeaks, whistles, croaks, frog-like calls, rising whistles, peeping, grunts, mechanical wing sounds, and now the soft scaup.

**Anatidae streak at 8** -- tying the **Sciuridae octet** for the longest family streak in engine history. Eight consecutive waterfowl species. The family's dominance of this section of the engine is structural: Puget Sound IS waterfowl habitat. The saltwater bays, the marshes, the lakes, the rivers -- these are Anatidae landscapes.
