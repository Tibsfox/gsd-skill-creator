**The *Deep Six* Compilation as the Founding Document of Seattle Grunge.** Before **Sub Pop Records** was a commercial enterprise and before grunge had a name, the *Deep Six* compilation (1986, **C/Z Records**) gathered six Seattle heavy-rock bands into one recording at **Reciprocal Recording** with **Jack Endino** engineering. The six bands were **Green River**, the **Melvins**, **Soundgarden**, **Skin Yard**, the **U-Men**, and **Malfunkshun** — and the compilation is the earliest collected statement of what would become the Seattle sound. The compilation received modest attention at the time — several thousand copies sold, mostly through Seattle record stores and mail order to music journalists — but its historical status has been established retrospectively as the bands on it became famous. *Deep Six* is now recognized as one of the most historically significant compilations in American rock history: the first gathering of the sounds that would transform American popular music in 1991-1992 when **Nirvana's** *Nevermind* (September 1991) and **Pearl Jam's** *Ten* (August 1991) reached mass audiences. **In parallel**, the Tufted Puffin's presence at the rocky island colony network — **Tatoosh Island**, **Destruction Island**, **Quillayute Needles**, **Three Arch Rocks NWR** — establishes the species as the outer coast Pacific Northwest seabird whose breeding sites have been occupied continuously for decades through colony site fidelity. **The same** founding-document structural principle organizes both halves. **Each side** demonstrates that the engine's documentation vocabulary preserves first-gathering records (the six bands in one room in one year on one compilation) and long-fidelity sites (the rocky island colonies occupied across generations of breeding pairs) at first-class structural resolution.

**The Green River Split as the Most Consequential Event in Seattle Rock.** Green River dissolved in 1987. The reasons involve a creative fault line between two factions with incompatible visions for the band: **Mark Arm** and **Steve Turner** wanted to stay closer to punk — fast, raw, anti-commercial, ideologically committed to underground aesthetics — while **Stone Gossard** and **Jeff Ament** had broader rock ambitions that produced bigger songs, wider audiences, and a larger sonic palette. Both positions were legitimate. The tension between them is not a conflict between good and bad but a conflict between two different visions of what rock music could be. Turner left first over the aesthetic disagreement; when the band finally dissolved, the split was clean: Arm and Turner formed **Mudhoney** in 1988, and Gossard and Ament met **Andrew Wood** of **Malfunkshun** and formed **Mother Love Bone** in 1987. Mudhoney became the band that **Sub Pop's** founders **Bruce Pavitt** and **Jonathan Poneman** considered the truest expression of the label's aesthetic; Mother Love Bone became a glam-inflected grunge band with Wood's extraordinary charisma at the center. Mother Love Bone ended with Wood's death in March 1990, and the response to that death — **Temple of the Dog** (1991, documented at degree 225), a tribute album featuring **Chris Cornell** of **Soundgarden** alongside Gossard and Ament and the musicians who would become Pearl Jam — brought Cornell into contact with the Mother Love Bone survivors. **Eddie Vedder** was recruited, the lineup solidified, and Pearl Jam recorded *Ten* (1991). The chain runs: **Green River** splits (1987) → **Mother Love Bone** forms → **Andrew Wood** dies (1990) → **Temple of the Dog** (1991) → **Pearl Jam** records *Ten* (1991). Green River's 1987 split is the first event in this chain. **In parallel**, the Tufted Puffin's seasonal plumage cycle is one of the most dramatic in North American birds: the breeding adult has a pure white face with two long golden-yellow tufts streaming past the eye, a laterally compressed orange-red bill with a yellow interior gape, and pale yellow to amber eyes on an entirely black body; the non-breeding winter adult is entirely dark brown-black with the golden tufts shed and the outer bill sheath shed to reveal a smaller, duller bill underneath. **The Jack Endino Production Signature and the Sub Pop Sonic Identity.** *Dry as a Bone* EP (1987, Sub Pop) was produced by **Jack Endino** at **Reciprocal Recording** in Seattle, and the production work established the sonic template that defined Sub Pop's subsequent catalog. The Endino signature — a specific relationship between room ambience and close-mic'd drums, a snare that cracks rather than booms, kick drums that are physical without being triggered, and guitar distortion that retains dynamic range rather than compressing into wall-of-sound mush — is the sonic identity applied to **Nirvana's** *Bleach* (1989), **Mudhoney's** *Superfuzz Bigmuff* (1988), **Soundgarden's** *Screaming Life* (1987), and dozens of other Sub Pop recordings from 1987-1990. The template begins with the Endino-Green River sessions at Reciprocal Recording. *Dry as a Bone* is the document at the center of the Sub Pop sonic origin: raw, honest, heavy, fast, recorded in Seattle by Seattle musicians on a tiny label budget; it established the template for everything Sub Pop released in the following three years; it is the founding sonic document of what the music press would later call grunge, made before anyone called it grunge. Endino is not only the producer — he was also a member of **Skin Yard**, the band that appeared alongside Green River on *Deep Six* and that is documented at degree 264 in the engine, and his position as both producer and musician inside the same scene made the Sub Pop sonic identity a producer-and-musician continuity rather than an external imposition from outside the scene. **In parallel**, the Tufted Puffin colony's acoustic signature combines low-frequency grunting calls (200-800 Hz, guttural and not piercing, used as contact calls within pair bonds and between colony members at burrow entrances) with the physically audible wingbeat sound as birds launch from cliff edges (a rapid thwap-thwap-thwap of wings too small for the body mass, compensating with frequency rather than with span). **The First Alcidae Entry and the Two-Octave Frequency Drop.** The **Tufted Puffin Colony** at degree 294 is the first member of family **Alcidae** — the auks — to enter the SPS track. **Burrow Nesting and Colony Site Fidelity as Alcidae Structural Signature.** Tufted Puffins are burrow nesters: they excavate burrows in the soil of island tops or cliff edges, or appropriate existing burrows and rock crevices. The burrow entrance is used for decades by the same pair. This site fidelity means that Tufted Puffin colonies are stable in a way that open-nesting colonial species are not: the same burrow entrances are occupied year after year, the same cliff-edge locations used for generations of breeding pairs, and the site's acoustic and ecological continuity runs across multi-decade windows. The breeding cycle is singular: one chick per year (rarely two), both parents incubating and provisioning, the chick remaining in the burrow for approximately 45 days fed on fish delivered by parents from foraging trips that may extend 50-150 km offshore, the colony occupied continuously from late April through mid-August. Outside the breeding season, Tufted Puffins are entirely pelagic: they spend September through April at sea over the continental slope and in offshore Pacific waters, and Pacific Northwest sightings during winter are rare except from offshore pelagic trips. The dramatic breeding-season presence at coastal colonies contrasts with complete offshore disappearance in winter. Population trends are a conservation concern: Pacific Northwest Tufted Puffin populations have shown concerning declines over recent decades at some major colony sites (including **Protection Island** in the Strait of Juan de Fuca, previously documented in the engine as a large seabird colony site), with climate change effects on prey availability, introduced predators at island colonies, and ocean productivity shifts as the primary hypothesized drivers; the species' global population remains large but the regional trend warrants conservation attention. **In parallel**, Green River's 1984-1988 active period was similarly bounded: the band formed, recorded four releases (*Come On Down*, the *Together We'll Never* single, *Dry as a Bone*, *Rehab Doll*), appeared on *Deep Six*, and dissolved, and the members then dispersed into the subsequent bands that carried their work forward. - **Capitol Hill Formation 1984: Arm, Turner, Gossard, Ament, Fairweather, Vincent.** Green River formed in **Capitol Hill** in 1984 around **Mark Arm** (vocals), **Steve Turner** (guitar), **Stone Gossard** (guitar), **Jeff Ament** (bass), **Bruce Fairweather** (guitar, early lineup), and **Alex Vincent** (drums). The lineup is the most consequential personnel configuration in Seattle rock history: five people (six counting Fairweather) in one band whose subsequent dispersal after the 1987 split produced **Mudhoney** (Arm and Turner) and the **Mother Love Bone** → **Pearl Jam** lineage (Gossard and Ament). Mark Arm was born Mark McLaughlin in 1962 and adopted the name Arm from a line in the **Easybeats** song "Peculiar Hole in the Sky" — a detail that reveals the band's relationship to rock history: irreverent, literate about influence, deliberately not taking itself too seriously. Steve Turner's guitar playing in Green River and Mudhoney is the sound of punk-informed rock guitar: not technically showy, not metal-influenced, but rhythmically aggressive and sonically heavy. Stone Gossard's guitar sensibility was different from Turner's — interested in dynamics, riff-building, and rock-architecture structural movement — and the tension between these two guitar sensibilities was the band's creative fault line. Bruce Fairweather's later work includes founding membership in **Love Battery** (documented at degree 221 in the engine), extending the Green River personnel network to the U-District psychedelia register.
- ***Dry as a Bone* (1987): The Founding Document of the Sub Pop Sonic Identity.** *Dry as a Bone* EP (1987, **Sub Pop Records**) was recorded at **Reciprocal Recording** in Seattle with **Jack Endino** producing and engineering. The EP is the most complete statement of the mature Green River sound: heavy but not metal, fast but not punk, raw but not unproduced. The Endino production signature — a specific relationship between room ambience and close-mic'd drums, a snare that cracks rather than booms, kick drums that are physical without being triggered — was established in these sessions and subsequently applied to **Nirvana's** *Bleach* (1989), **Mudhoney's** *Superfuzz Bigmuff* (1988), **Soundgarden's** *Screaming Life* (1987), and dozens of other Sub Pop recordings from 1987-1990. *Dry as a Bone* is the founding sonic document of what the music press would later call grunge, recorded in Seattle by Seattle musicians on a tiny label budget before anyone called the sound grunge. Tracks "Unwind" and "Baby Takes" are the standout compositions. *Rehab Doll* EP (1988, Sub Pop) followed, released after the band had dissolved and produced again by Endino; the cover art by **Charles Burns** is one of the canonical images of the Sub Pop visual aesthetic.
- ***Deep Six* (1986): The First Collected Statement of the Seattle Sound.** *Deep Six* compilation (1986, **C/Z Records**, run by **Daniel House** who also played in **Skin Yard**) is the founding document of Seattle grunge and the first collected statement of the sound that would transform American popular music in 1991-1992. The compilation gathered six Seattle heavy-rock bands — **Green River**, the **Melvins**, **Soundgarden**, **Skin Yard**, the **U-Men**, and **Malfunkshun** — into one recording at **Reciprocal Recording** with **Jack Endino** engineering. Green River and Malfunkshun are both present in the engine at consecutive degrees (294 and 295) because both bands were on *Deep Six* and because the Proto-Grunge Origin Arc that opens at Green River continues through Malfunkshun's Andrew Wood register. *Deep Six* received modest attention at the time — several thousand copies sold, mostly through Seattle record stores and mail order — but its historical status has been established retrospectively as the bands on it became famous. The compilation is now recognized as one of the most historically significant records in American rock history: the first gathering of the sounds that would reach mass audiences with **Nirvana's** *Nevermind* and **Pearl Jam's** *Ten* in 1991.
- **First Sub Pop Release: *Come On Down* (1985).** Green River's "Come On Down" (1985, **Homestead Records** / **Sub Pop**) was among **Sub Pop's** earliest releases, appearing on the *Sub Pop 100* compilation (1986) — the first Sub Pop compilation — and establishing Green River's position at the material origin of the label that defined Seattle grunge. The *Together We'll Never / Ain't Nothin' to Do* single (1985, **Tasque Force** / Sub Pop) was among Sub Pop's first single releases. Green River's position at Sub Pop's origin is literal: they were there before Sub Pop was a fully commercial entity and before **Bruce Pavitt** and **Jonathan Poneman** had formalized the label's operation. Without Green River, Sub Pop's early catalog is incomplete, and the Sub Pop identity that defined Seattle grunge from 1988 forward has its first material in the Green River sessions.
- **The 1987 Split: Creative Fault Line Between Arm / Turner and Gossard / Ament.** Green River dissolved in 1987. The creative fault line ran between two factions with incompatible visions for the band: **Mark Arm** and **Steve Turner** wanted to stay closer to punk (fast, raw, anti-commercial, ideologically committed to underground aesthetics), while **Stone Gossard** and **Jeff Ament** had broader rock ambitions that produced bigger songs, wider audiences, and a larger sonic palette. Both positions were legitimate. Turner left first over the aesthetic disagreement; when the band finally dissolved, the split was clean. Arm and Turner formed **Mudhoney** in 1988 and Mudhoney became the band that **Sub Pop's** founders considered the truest expression of the label's aesthetic — **Bruce Pavitt** and **Jonathan Poneman** have said that Mudhoney was the band that most completely embodied what they were trying to do. Gossard and Ament met **Andrew Wood** of **Malfunkshun** and formed **Mother Love Bone** in 1987 — the band that would become glam-inflected grunge with Wood's extraordinary charisma at the center and would end with Wood's death in March 1990. The split is the most consequential dissolution event in Seattle rock history because the two trajectories from one origin point are among the most consequential splits in American rock history.
- **The Chain from Green River Split to Pearl Jam *Ten*.** The chain runs: Green River splits (1987) → Mother Love Bone forms with Wood → Wood dies (March 1990) → **Temple of the Dog** records (1991, documented at degree 225 in the engine, a tribute album featuring **Chris Cornell** of **Soundgarden** alongside Gossard and Ament) → Pearl Jam forms with **Eddie Vedder** and records *Ten* (August 1991). Each step in the chain is a direct consequence of the preceding step. The chain traces from the 1987 Green River dissolution to the Pearl Jam debut that reached mass audiences in 1991-1992 — a four-year window during which the Gossard / Ament trajectory moved through Mother Love Bone's formation, Wood's death, the Temple of the Dog tribute, and the Pearl Jam lineup solidification. The Pearl Jam lineage cross-references in the engine include **Mad Season** at degree 224 (**Layne Staley** + **Mike McCready**, the second chapter of Pearl Jam side projects), **Temple of the Dog** at degree 225 (the tribute album and the direct descendant of the Mother Love Bone → Wood's death event chain), and the forthcoming Mother Love Bone entry at degree 296.
- **The Proto-Grunge E=7 Sonic Register.** Green River's E=7 placement is structurally correct for the proto-grunge formulation. The proto-grunge sonic register combines punk velocity (from the **Stooges**, **Black Flag**, and hardcore influence), heavy-rock weight (from **Black Sabbath**, **Neil Young** Crazy Horse era, and **Aerosmith**), and Pacific Northwest ground-level aesthetics (deliberately unglamorous, anti-metal, refusing metal's excesses while retaining heaviness) into a register that sits one unit below the E=8 grunge-peak amplitude that **Mudhoney** and the fully-formed grunge acts occupy. The E=7 placement accurately registers the energy of the 1984-1988 Green River catalog without claiming the higher amplitude that the 1988-onward Mudhoney catalog established. The energy score does not diminish the historical importance of Green River — the historical weight is immense — but it accurately describes the acoustic register of the proto-grunge formulation as distinct from the subsequent fully-formed grunge register.

## Part B: Tufted Puffin Colony — First Alcidae at E=6, *Fratercula cirrhata*, Outer Coast Rocky Island Burrow Colonies, Deep Grunts at 200-800 Hz and Wingbeat Mechanical Sound, Tatoosh Island and Destruction Island and Quillayute Needles and Three Arch Rocks NWR, the Two-Octave Frequency Drop from the Preceding Laridae Colonies

- **First Alcidae Entry and Alcidae Family Arc Opening.** The **Tufted Puffin Colony** at degree 294 is the first member of family **Alcidae** — the auks — to enter the SPS track. Alcidae will occupy two consecutive SPS entries (degrees 294-295): **Tufted Puffin Colony** at 294 (diurnal colony register) and **Rhinoceros Auklet** at 295 (nocturnal colony register). The two-degree opening span establishes the Alcidae presence in the engine across the two most visually and acoustically distinctive Pacific Northwest alcids, with inverted temporal regimes (day versus night) covering the full temporal range of the outer coast rocky island colony. The Alcidae are the Northern Hemisphere's ecological analog to the Southern Hemisphere's penguins: diving seabirds, wings adapted for underwater propulsion, colonial nesting on rocky islands and cliff faces, pelagic foraging for fish and invertebrates. Unlike penguins, alcids retain flight, but the tradeoff is visible in the small wing-to-body ratio and the resulting rapid whirring wingbeat. The Pacific Northwest hosts one of the most diverse alcid assemblages in North America, including **Tufted Puffin**, **Rhinoceros Auklet**, **Common Murre**, **Pigeon Guillemot**, **Marbled Murrelet**, **Cassin's Auklet**, and **Ancient Murrelet**.
- ***Fratercula cirrhata* Morphology: White Face, Golden Tufts, Laterally Compressed Bill.** The Tufted Puffin (*Fratercula cirrhata*) is immediately recognizable. Breeding adults have a pure white face contrasting with an entirely black body; two long golden-yellow tufts stream past the eye from the bill back to the nape — the "tufted" in the common name; the bill is massive, laterally compressed, and brightly colored with the outer sheath orange-red and the interior gape yellow; the eye is pale yellow to amber. The full breeding-plumage face is one of the most dramatically patterned of any North American seabird: black, white, gold, orange-red, pale yellow, all in one compact face. Outside the breeding season, the Tufted Puffin loses the white facial patch and the golden tufts and the winter bird is entirely dark brown-black above and below with a smaller, duller bill. The breeding-plumage bill's outer coloring is not permanent: the outer sheath is shed after the breeding season and a smaller, duller bill is exposed underneath. The seasonal bill-sheath is shared with the **Atlantic Puffin** and the **Horned Puffin** and is a characteristic of the *Fratercula* genus (the Latin name *fratercula* means "little brother" — possibly referring to the friar's robes suggested by the bird's coloring). Body length 14-17 inches, wingspan 25 inches, weight 560-800 grams.
- **Deep Grunts at 200-800 Hz: The Alcidae Acoustic Signature.** Tufted Puffins are not particularly vocal compared to gulls or terns, but the calls they do produce are distinctive: deep, low-pitched grunts and moaning calls, somewhat like soft purring or growling. These are primarily contact calls within the pair bond and between colony members at the burrow entrance. The frequency range is roughly 200-800 Hz — distinctly lower than any Laridae entry in the engine. The quality is guttural, not piercing. At an active Tufted Puffin colony in peak summer, the ambient acoustic character is low-frequency grunting from burrow entrances, the constant mechanical whirring of wingbeats as birds launch and land, wind over the rocky cliff, and the more distant background of ocean and wave sound. The overall acoustic texture is deep, busy, and textured without the sharp piercing quality of tern colonies. The E=6 placement reflects this acoustic character: present and substantial, with real presence in the deep grunts and physically audible wingbeat sound, but not at the sharp piercing high-frequency intensity of the tern entries.
- **The Two-Octave Frequency Drop from the Preceding Laridae Colonies.** The acoustic transition from the preceding Laridae arc to the opening Alcidae arc is the engine's most dramatic frequency shift in the coastal seabird sequence. **Common Tern** keerr at 2,000-4,000 Hz (degree 292) drops to **Caspian Tern** ka-arr at 800-2,000 Hz (degree 293) and drops again to **Tufted Puffin** grunt at 200-800 Hz (degree 294). A two-octave drop over three consecutive entries. The taxonomy is audible in the frequency progression: the family-level difference between Laridae and Alcidae registers as a frequency register shift that listeners can perceive in direct comparison between recordings. The engine's documentation preserves the frequency progression at first-class resolution because the frequency register is structurally load-bearing for distinguishing the two families' acoustic signatures.
- **Outer Coast Rocky Island Colony Network: Tatoosh, Destruction, Quillayute Needles, Three Arch Rocks NWR.** Major Pacific Northwest Tufted Puffin colonies are distributed across the outer coast rocky islands: **Tatoosh Island** at **Cape Flattery** (the northwestern tip of the contiguous United States), **Destruction Island** offshore of Jefferson County (also a **Steller Sea Lion** haul-out, documented at degree 283 in the engine, establishing Destruction Island as a shared-species Pacific Northwest habitat register), **Quillayute Needles** on the Olympic Peninsula outer coast, and **Three Arch Rocks National Wildlife Refuge** on the northern Oregon coast (Oregon's most significant seabird nesting colony). The San Juan Islands and Gulf Islands host smaller breeding groups. The rocky island colony context is structurally important for the degree 294 documentation because the deep grunts and wingbeat sounds are colony-specific: the vocalizations are produced in and near the burrows, and the wingbeat sounds are physical byproducts of cliff-edge launches and landings that are concentrated at the colony. Away from the colony, Tufted Puffins are largely silent and are pelagic foragers whose acoustic presence is minimal.
- **Burrow Nesting, Site Fidelity, and the 45-Day Chick-Rearing Window.** Tufted Puffins are burrow nesters: they excavate burrows in the soil of island tops or cliff edges, or appropriate existing burrows and rock crevices. The burrow entrance is used for decades by the same pair. Tufted Puffins raise one chick per year (rarely two), both parents incubating and provisioning, and the chick remains in the burrow for approximately 45 days fed on fish delivered by parents from foraging trips that may extend 50-150 km offshore. The colony is occupied continuously from late April through mid-August. The long provisioning period and the decades-long site fidelity make Tufted Puffin colonies stable in ways that open-nesting colonial species are not: the same burrow entrances are occupied year after year, the same cliff-edge locations used for generations of breeding pairs.
- **Pelagic Winter: September-April Offshore over the Continental Slope.** When not breeding, Tufted Puffins are entirely pelagic: they spend the non-breeding season from September through April at sea, primarily over the continental slope and in offshore Pacific waters. Pacific Northwest Tufted Puffins winter at sea far from land; sightings are rare except from offshore pelagic trips. The dramatic breeding-season presence at coastal colonies contrasts with complete offshore disappearance in winter, and the seasonal transformation in plumage (breeding white-face-gold-tufts to winter all-dark) accompanies the seasonal transformation in habitat (summer colonies to winter pelagic). The species' Pacific Northwest acoustic signature is therefore entirely a breeding-season phenomenon — the deep grunts and wingbeat sounds at the colonies — and the engine's documentation preserves the colony acoustic register at first-class resolution for the degree 294 entry.
- **Conservation: IUCN Least Concern Globally, Regional Decline Concern.** The Tufted Puffin is listed as **IUCN Least Concern** with a stable global population, but Pacific Northwest populations have shown concerning declines over recent decades at some major colony sites (including **Protection Island** in the Strait of Juan de Fuca, which was previously documented in the engine as a large seabird colony site). Climate change effects on prey availability, introduced predators at island colonies, and ocean productivity shifts are the primary hypothesized drivers. The species is protected under the **Migratory Bird Treaty Act (MBTA) of 1918** in the United States. Colony monitoring programs by the **Washington Department of Fish and Wildlife (WDFW)**, the **US Fish and Wildlife Service (USFWS)**, and the **Washington Ornithological Society (WOS)** track the regional population trends. The species' global population remains large, but the regional trend warrants conservation attention and establishes the Tufted Puffin as a species whose Pacific Northwest presence is measurably diminished from historical baselines even as the global population is not at risk.

## Lessons Learned

1. **Origin arcs and influence arcs are structurally distinct documentation modes and should be preserved separately.** The 294-296 Proto-Grunge Origin Arc documents the musicians who actually played in the three bands (Green River, Malfunkshun, Mother Love Bone), while the 286-287 influence arcs (**Dead Kennedys (influence)** and **Black Flag (influence)**) documented external foundational influences on the Pacific Northwest scene. The two modes are not interchangeable, and conflating them would flatten the engine's documentation into a generic "musical influences" register that loses the distinction between actual personnel continuity and external foundational texts. The lesson is that arc-type distinctions are structurally load-bearing for the engine's S36 documentation, and origin arcs that document personnel continuity should be preserved at first-class resolution distinct from influence arcs that document external foundational texts.
2. **Band splits that generate multiple subsequent bands are among the most consequential dissolution events in a scene's history and warrant first-class documentation as generative events rather than as mere endings.** Green River's 1987 split generated **Mudhoney** (from Arm and Turner) and, through Mother Love Bone and Andrew Wood's death, the **Pearl Jam** lineage (from Gossard and Ament). The split is not a failure — it is the mechanism through which both visions (the Arm / Turner punk commitment and the Gossard / Ament broader rock ambition) found their proper forms. The lesson is that scene-shaping band dissolutions should be documented as generative events that produced specific subsequent outcomes at first-class resolution, and the chain from dissolution to subsequent band formation should be traced explicitly rather than left implicit.
3. **Producer-at-source documentation is structurally load-bearing for sonic-identity continuity across a label's catalog.** **Jack Endino's** production work on *Dry as a Bone* (1987) established the sonic template subsequently applied to **Nirvana's** *Bleach* (1989), **Mudhoney's** *Superfuzz Bigmuff* (1988), **Soundgarden's** *Screaming Life* (1987), and dozens of other Sub Pop recordings from 1987-1990. Endino was also a member of **Skin Yard** (documented at degree 264), making the Sub Pop sonic identity a producer-and-musician continuity rather than an external imposition. The lesson is that producer documentation should be preserved at first-class resolution alongside the band and album documentation, because the producer-at-source continuity is structurally load-bearing for understanding how a label's sonic identity propagated across its catalog.
4. **Family-level taxonomic boundaries can be registered audibly through frequency progression across consecutive SPS entries.** The 292-294 Laridae-to-Alcidae transition registers as a two-octave frequency drop (Common Tern keerr at 2,000-4,000 Hz → Caspian Tern ka-arr at 800-2,000 Hz → Tufted Puffin grunt at 200-800 Hz) that listeners can perceive in direct comparison between recordings. The lesson is that frequency progression is a first-class documentation register for family-level taxonomic boundaries and should be preserved at first-class resolution in the SPS track, because the acoustic register carries information about family-level biology that is structurally load-bearing for the engine's seabird documentation and cannot be fully captured through species-level identification and morphological descriptions alone.
5. **Compilation appearances can be the founding documents of a scene even when the compilations themselves receive modest initial attention.** *Deep Six* (1986, **C/Z Records**) sold only several thousand copies at release but is now recognized as the founding document of Seattle grunge because the six bands on it (**Green River**, **Malfunkshun**, **Soundgarden**, **Skin Yard**, **Melvins**, **U-Men**) collectively define what the scene sounded like before it had a name. The lesson is that compilation appearances warrant first-class documentation in the engine even when the compilations themselves are not commercially significant, because the compilations often serve as the first collected statement of a scene's sound and provide structurally load-bearing cross-references between the bands that appear on them.
6. **Burrow-nesting with decades-long site fidelity produces stable colony sites that are structurally distinct from open-nesting colonial species.** The Tufted Puffin's burrow-nesting behavior and decades-long site fidelity means that colonies at **Tatoosh Island**, **Destruction Island**, **Quillayute Needles**, and **Three Arch Rocks NWR** are occupied continuously across generations of breeding pairs, with the same burrow entrances used year after year. The lesson is that nesting-behavior distinctions are structurally load-bearing for the engine's SPS documentation because they determine colony stability and site-level ecological continuity, and the engine should preserve nesting-behavior documentation at first-class resolution rather than treating all colonial seabirds as acoustically equivalent.
7. **Shared-site Pacific Northwest habitat registers establish cross-species ecological continuity that spans the engine's documentation arc.** **Destruction Island** is both a Tufted Puffin colony and a Steller Sea Lion haul-out (documented at degree 283), and the shared-site designation establishes Destruction Island as a multi-species Pacific Northwest habitat register. The lesson is that geographic documentation should preserve shared-site designations at first-class resolution because the shared-site registers carry information about Pacific Northwest ecological density that is structurally load-bearing for the engine's regional documentation scope, and the shared-site registers should be surfaced explicitly in cross-reference documentation rather than treated as incidental coincidence.
8. **Asymmetric cross-sync amplitudes preserve the structural role at equivalent resolution to symmetric-amplitude cross-sync registers.** The 294 cross-sync pairing — Green River at E=7 (proto-grunge origin arc opening) paired with Tufted Puffin at E=6 (Alcidae family arc opening) — operates at asymmetric amplitudes but preserves the arc-opening structural role at equivalent resolution to the symmetric-amplitude cross-sync pairings previously documented. The lesson is that cross-sync structural roles are not dependent on amplitude symmetry; the engine's cross-sync documentation vocabulary can register matched structural roles (two firsts at one degree, bounded episodes occupying available space, unceremonious presence, riot-grrrl amplitude ceiling) across asymmetric amplitudes at first-class resolution.
9. **Simultaneous two-arc-opening at the same degree is a structurally productive documentation mode that preserves documentary density across transitions.** Degree 294 simultaneously opens the 294-296 Proto-Grunge Origin Arc (Part A) and the Alcidae family arc (Part B), and the simultaneous opening is the structural inflection point that preserves the documentary density through the transition from the preceding Laridae / hardcore sequences. The lesson is that the engine's documentation vocabulary can register simultaneous arc-openings as a distinct structural mode that preserves the documentary density across transitions at first-class resolution, and the simultaneous mode is structurally productive when the opening arcs are thematically unrelated and operate at distinct amplitude registers.
