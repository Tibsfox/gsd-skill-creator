## Summary

**THE PROTO-GRUNGE BAND. FIRST ALCIDAE. 81.7%.**

**Green River** arrive at E=7 -- the Seattle band that is not merely proto-grunge but the direct ancestor of grunge itself. Green River formed in 1983 around **Mark Arm** (vocals, guitar) and **Steve Turner** (guitar), with a lineup that at various points included **Jeff Ament** (bass), **Stone Gossard** (guitar), and Alex Vincent (drums). This was not a band that pointed toward grunge -- this was the band from which grunge directly descended, through the split that produced Mudhoney (Arm and Turner) and Mother Love Bone / Pearl Jam (Ament and Gossard). Green River released two Sub Pop records before dissolution: *Dry as a Bone* (1987), produced by Jack Endino, and *Rehab Doll* (1988). These two records are the founding documents of what Sub Pop would become. *Dry as a Bone* arrived before Sub Pop was Sub Pop in its commercial sense; it arrived as the label was still learning what it was. The record told the label what it was.

Green River's E=7 is the correct assignment for the proto-grunge formulation. The energy is high and the historical weight is immense, but the proto-grunge sonic register -- the mixture of punk velocity and heavy rock influence, the Stooges and Aerosmith and Black Flag processed through a Seattle lens -- sits at E=7 rather than the E=8+ of the bands that followed and exceeded it. The energy score does not diminish the historical importance; it accurately describes the acoustic register.

The proto-grunge origin arc begins here. Degrees 294-296 trace a continuous lineage: Green River to Malfunkshun to Mother Love Bone. Three bands, three degrees, the origin story of grunge laid out in sequence. Green River is degree one of that arc -- the point at which the founding musicians found each other and found a sound. What came after was consequential enough to change popular music globally, and it started here, with Mark Arm and Steve Turner and Jeff Ament and Stone Gossard in Seattle in 1983.

**Tufted Puffin** arrives at E=6 on the SPS track -- the most visually dramatic alcid of the Pacific Northwest and the **FIRST ALCIDAE** entry in the SPS track. *Fratercula cirrhata* is a pelagic seabird of the open North Pacific that comes ashore only to breed, and in Washington it breeds on offshore rocks and islands -- Protection Island, Tatoosh Island, the Three Arch Rocks area south of the state line. The Tufted Puffin's colony sounds are a mix of deep grunts and rhythmic wingbeat sounds: not melodic, not alarm-based, but the functional vocalizations and physical sounds of a colonial breeding aggregation operating in tight space. The grunts are low, the wingbeats audible at close range, the overall colony acoustic a dense mix of bird presence.

**FIRST ALCIDAE.** The Tufted Puffin opens the alcid family in the SPS track. The Alcidae are the alcids -- puffins, auklets, murres, murrelets, dovekies -- the diving seabirds that evolved in convergence with penguins, using their wings to swim underwater with the same efficiency that penguins achieve but retaining flight. The family is the ecological equivalent of penguins in the Northern Hemisphere: dense-bodied, short-winged (for their size), built for underwater pursuit. Alcidae's arrival at degree 294 opens a new family chapter in the SPS seabird survey, one that will extend through degree 295 with the Rhinoceros Auklet. The Tufted Puffin enters first because it is the most visually and acoustically dramatic member of the family present in Washington waters -- the bird that most people picture when Pacific Northwest alcids are mentioned.

E=6 for the Tufted Puffin colony is accurate. The individual calls are modest in isolation; the colony aggregate registers higher than a single bird but does not reach the extended urgency of the gull and tern colonies that preceded it. E=6 places the puffin colony in the mid-range of the seabird acoustic hierarchy.

- **Mark Arm and Steve Turner** -- Arm (born Mark McLaughlin, 1962) was the vocalist and guitarist who would go on to front Mudhoney through four decades; Turner played guitar in Green River and Mudhoney and is the melodic intelligence behind the Mudhoney sound; their collaboration in Green River is the compressed form of what they would expand in Mudhoney; Green River is where both artists arrived at their full sonic identities
