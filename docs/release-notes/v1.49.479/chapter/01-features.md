## Jack Endino's dual role


- Skin Yard's Jack Endino holds a unique position in the Pacific Northwest music history: he is simultaneously a performer (Skin Yard guitarist) and the producer who recorded the foundational Sub Pop catalog; Endino produced *Bleach* (1989), *Dry as a Bone* / *Rehab Doll* (Green River, 1986), *God's Balls* (TAD, 1989), *Touch Me I'm Sick* (Mudhoney, 1988), and dozens of other Sub Pop-era recordings; the production philosophy he developed -- minimal processing, direct sound, preserving the natural acoustic output of the room and the band -- is the aesthetic that defines Sub Pop's sonic identity; Endino's guitar work in Skin Yard informed his sense of what a heavy guitar sound should feel like, which informed every production decision he made at the founding of the Sub Pop era

## Old-growth forest snag acoustics


- snags -- standing dead trees -- are among the most acoustically active elements of old-growth forest structure; dry wood under lateral load (wind) produces higher-frequency cracking and snapping sounds than live wood, which flexes and returns; a snag under sufficient wind load will produce a sequence of acoustic events as internal wood fibers fail -- first cracking, then a sustained groan as the failure propagates, then the percussion of the fall if the wind exceeds the snag's remaining structural strength; the old-growth windstorm's acoustic environment includes this snag percussion as a layer unavailable in forests too young to have produced standing dead trees of substantial size
