## Artist-City Patterns

- **Post-punk returns** -- Pretty Girls Make Graves brings post-punk back to the engine with sharp, driving energy. Andrea Zollo's vocals, angular guitars, urgent tempos. Capitol Hill, 2001-2007. Named after a Morrissey lyric (itself from a Jack Kerouac line). The short-lived band that burned bright and stopped before it faded.
- **Matador Records enters** -- sixteenth label on the map. Founded 1989, New York. The first major non-PNW indie label in the engine's recent run. Matador signed PGMG for *The New Romance* (2003) and *Elan Vital* (2006). The label that also holds Pavement, Yo La Tengo, Cat Power. National infrastructure reaching into Seattle.
- **Capitol Hill sextet closes** -- Kinski (110), Helms Alee (111), 764-HERO (112), Minus the Bear (113), Haunted Horses (114), Pretty Girls Make Graves (115). Six consecutive Capitol Hill artists spanning krautrock, noise, indie/electronic, math rock, noise/indie, and post-punk. The streak ends here. Six genres, one neighborhood, one streak.
- **2001-2007 lifespan** -- PGMG existed entirely within the Bush-era post-9/11 window. Formed in the ashes of The Hookers. Disbanded before the 2008 crash. A band shaped by its moment: urgent, political, finite.
