1. **Two streak closures at a single degree amplify structural significance beyond the sum of their parts.**
   The Capitol Hill sextet and Picidae nonet both closed at degree 115 — not a coincidence but a deliberate structural alignment. The double closure produces a peak rather than two independent findings, and the engine should continue to align major closures at single degrees when the catalog permits. Multi-record convergence at a single degree is the template established by degree 115 and validated by degree 110's multi-milestone convergence.

2. **Taxonomic family streaks can surpass previous records when the family's acoustic diversity supports extended runs.**
   The Picidae nonet (9 consecutive) surpassed the Sciuridae octet (8 consecutive) because Picidae holds sufficient acoustic and ecological diversity across nine species to sustain the streak without repetition. Future family streaks should select families whose internal diversity can support extended runs, and the Picidae example sets the bar: four energy levels, oak-savanna-to-burn-forest habitat range, colonial-to-solitary behavioral range, four-to-three toes morphological range. Diversity within family, not just family membership, enables record-length streaks.
   _⚙ Status: `applied` (applied in `v1.49.287`) · lesson #6512_
