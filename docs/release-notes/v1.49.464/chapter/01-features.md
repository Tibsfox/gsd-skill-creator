## Deception Pass hydraulics


- Deception Pass, the tidal channel between Whidbey Island and Fidalgo Island at the north end of Puget Sound, is one of the Pacific Northwest's most dramatic tidal constrictions; the channel is 457 meters wide at its narrowest and the tidal range in Puget Sound forces enormous water volumes through this width twice daily; at peak flood and ebb tides, the currents exceed 8 knots and produce standing waves, whirlpools, and hydraulic surges that are audible from the state park bridges 180 feet above the water; the Deception Pass tidal bore is the Salish Sea's most accessible large-scale tidal hydraulic acoustic event
