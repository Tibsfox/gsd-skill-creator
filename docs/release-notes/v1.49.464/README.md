**Ten Minute Warning** arrive at E=8 -- the Seattle proto-grunge band formed in 1981 by Paul Solger (guitar), Steve Verwolf (bass), Duff McKagan (drums), and Gary Minkler (vocals), who operated in the early-1980s Seattle punk scene and produced a sound that was already moving toward what would become grunge: heavier than punk, slower in sections, with a guitar weight that would not be fully realized until the late 1980s but that was audible in embryonic form in Ten Minute Warning's brief recorded output. The band is a historical document before it is a musical one: Ten Minute Warning is where Duff McKagan played drums before he moved to Los Angeles and joined Guns N' Roses in 1984, and the band's existence and sound are a primary record of the Seattle punk scene's evolution toward the heavier register that produced grunge. The music itself is good -- energetic, harder than most of the early-1980s Seattle punk that surrounded it -- and the E=8 energy reading reflects both the music's genuine intensity and its historical position as an origin document.

E=8 for Ten Minute Warning is the post-summit cooldown's only E=8 S36 entry (excluding Gruntruck at degree 316, which is the summit's closing degree). Ten Minute Warning were making something new in 1981-1984, even if they couldn't name it yet. That newness earns E=8.

**Puget Sound tidal bore** arrives at E=8 on the SPS track -- the rhythmic hydraulic surge produced at the constriction points in Puget Sound's complex tidal channel geometry, where the twice-daily tide forces enormous volumes of water through narrow passages (the Tacoma Narrows, Deception Pass, Swinomish Channel) at velocities that produce standing waves, hydraulic jumps, and the roaring surge sound of water moving faster than the adjacent water column. The Puget Sound tidal bore is one of the Pacific Northwest's most powerful and most regular hydraulic acoustic events: at Deception Pass, the tidal bore can be heard from both bridge viewpoints as the tide forces through the channel at velocities exceeding 8 knots, producing a sustained roaring sound that is neither fully analogous to a river rapid nor to an ocean wave but that combines the sustained duration of a rapid with the saline power of an ocean tidal event.

E=8 for the Puget Sound tidal bore is the correct assignment for the SPS track's final high-energy hydrological event before degree 324's comprehensive Hoh rainforest soundscape. The tidal bore is rhythmic -- it occurs on the tidal schedule, predictable to the hour -- but the sound it produces is not gentle. At the Tacoma Narrows, where currents can exceed 5 knots, the bore sound at peak flow is a sustained roar. At Deception Pass, the hydraulic surge at the constriction is one of the loudest natural acoustic events in the Salish Sea region outside of major storms. E=8 is the correct reading for rhythmic geological-scale water movement at this velocity.
