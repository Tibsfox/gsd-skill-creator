## Summary

**The Sonics** arrive at E=8 -- and with that, the engine ties its absolute maximum. E=8 has been reached only once before in the engine's history: at degree 218, when The Blood Brothers registered a score that the engine has not matched in the 23 degrees since. The Sonics tie that mark. This is not a coincidence of the scoring system; it is an accurate reflection of what The Sonics actually were and what they actually sound like. Formed in Tacoma in 1960, The Sonics are one of the most important bands in the history of rock music, and they are consistently underrepresented in canonical rock histories written from a non-Pacific Northwest perspective. Gerry Roslie's vocals are the engine that makes The Sonics what they are: he screamed. Not the performative scream of hard rock, not the emotional scream of punk -- a raw, uncontrolled, genuinely alarming scream that sounds like something is actually wrong. "Have Love Will Travel," "The Witch," "Psycho," "He's Waitin'" -- the songs are built around a rhythm section that is punishing in a way that anticipates heavy metal, and Roslie's vocals cut through all of it with a quality that no studio production could smooth. **The Sonics are proto-punk** -- they arrived before punk, before the Stooges, before the New York Dolls, before the Ramones, and they were doing what all of those bands would later do. The engine's absolute maximum is a correct assessment.

On the species side, **Dolly Varden** enters the Salmonidae arc at E=2 -- **silent/ripple**, the second entry of the Silent Fish Trio. *Salvelinus malma* is a charr, as are Bull Trout, and the two species are so closely related that they were considered the same species until 1980. Dolly Varden are named, allegedly, after a character in a Dickens novel who wore a spotted dress -- the fish's red and orange spots on an olive-green body making the comparison seem reasonable to some 19th-century naturalist. They are fish of the near-shore marine environment and the cold coastal streams, more tolerant of salt water than Bull Trout, with a range that extends from Puget Sound north along the coast to Alaska and across the Pacific. Their ripple at E=2 is the sound of a fish that lives in places where the water is still cold enough and clean enough that silence is the natural state.

The cross-track energy contrast at degree 241 is the engine's most extreme: E=8 on the S36 track, E=2 on the SPS track. This is a six-point spread -- the maximum possible divergence between an absolute maximum and a near-minimum. The Sonics screaming at the top of the range while Dolly Varden barely disturbs the surface of a cold stream is one of the engine's defining compositional moments.
