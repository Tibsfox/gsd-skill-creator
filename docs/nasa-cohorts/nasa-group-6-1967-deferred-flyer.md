# NASA Astronaut Group 6 (1967) — Deferred-Flyer Cohort

## Cohort substrate-form

The substrate-form **SELECTED-1967 + DEFERRED-FIRST-FLIGHT-15-PLUS-YEARS** describes
NASA Astronaut Group 6, the "Excess Eleven" (XS-11) scientist-astronauts announced
1967-08-04. Of the eleven selected, **seven eventually flew** — none of
them sooner than 15 years after selection. Four resigned before flying. The cohort
is anchored by two complementary sub-forms:

- **DEFERRED-15-YEARS-TO-FIRST-FLIGHT** — Allen + Lenoir flew STS-5 (1982-11-11),
  precisely 15y 3m 7d after selection; first members of the group to reach orbit.
- **SELECTED-NEVER-FLEW** — Llewellyn, O'Leary, Chapman, and Holmquest all resigned
  before flight assignment. Llewellyn is the canonical anchor (earliest departure,
  August 1968, within thirteen months of selection).

Adjacent / cross-cohort substrate-form anchor:

- **LONGEST-WAIT-TO-FIRST-FLIGHT** is held by **Don L. Lind** — but Lind was
  selected in **NASA Group 5 (1966-04-04)**, *not* Group 6. His 19y 0m 25d wait
  to STS-51-B (1985-04-29) is the longest observed in NASA history for a
  continuously-serving astronaut. The Group 6 cohort *brackets* this record:
  Group 6 followed Group 5 by sixteen months, and Group 6's eventual flyers
  experienced the same Apollo-era flight famine that produced Lind's wait.
  This file documents Group 6 specifically; Lind is referenced here as the
  cross-cohort upper-bound on the DEFERRED-FLYER substrate-form.

## Selection context

NASA Astronaut Group 6 was selected on **1967-08-04** as NASA's second
scientist-astronaut group (Group 4, "The Scientists", was the first, 1965).
NASA Director of Flight Crew Operations Deke Slayton had planned to hire
20–30 new scientist-astronauts; only 11 of the 923 applicants were judged
qualified, and NASA hired all 11.

The group's "Excess Eleven" / "XS-11" nickname came from Slayton himself: when
the group reported to the Manned Spacecraft Center on 1967-09-18, he told them
"We don't need you around here" and offered to accept resignations. With Apollo
Applications being absorbed into Skylab and the Apollo program winding down,
there were no near-term scientist-astronaut flight slots. This is the structural
root cause of the cohort's DEFERRED-FLYER substrate-form: a PhD-track group
selected into an institution that had no flights waiting for them.

Professional composition of the eleven:

- 3 medical doctors
- 3 astronomers
- 2 physicists
- 1 geophysicist
- 1 electrical engineer
- 1 chemist (chemical engineer / bioelectrochemist)

## Cohort members

### John Anthony "Jack" Llewellyn (never-flew)
- **Discipline:** Chemical engineer / bioelectrochemist (PhD)
- **Selection:** 1967-08-04 (NASA Group 6)
- **Departure:** Resigned 1968-08-23 after completing the first phase of jet
  flight training at Reese Air Force Base; difficulties with flight training
  cited. One of NASA's first naturalized U.S. citizens of foreign birth
  (born in Wales).
- **Flight assignments:** None.
- **Substrate-form:** **SELECTED-NEVER-FLEW** (cohort anchor — earliest
  Group 6 departure)

### Joseph Percival "Joe" Allen IV (STS-5 + STS-51-A)
- **Discipline:** PhD physics (Yale, 1965). BA mathematics + physics
  (DePauw, 1959); MS physics (Yale, 1961). Prior research at Yale Nuclear
  Structure Laboratory and University of Washington Nuclear Physics Laboratory.
- **Selection:** 1967-08-04 (NASA Group 6)
- **First flight:** **STS-5** Columbia, 1982-11-11 — **15y 3m 7d deferment**
- **Second flight:** **STS-51-A** Discovery, 1984-11-08 — first space salvage
  mission (Palapa B-2 and Westar VI satellite retrieval)
- **Mission role:** Mission Specialist (MS) both flights
- **Career total:** 2 flights, 314 hours
- **Departure from NASA:** 1985
- **Substrate-form:** **DEFERRED-15-YEARS-TO-FIRST-FLIGHT** (joint cohort
  anchor with Lenoir for the STS-5 15-year-deferment pair)

### William Benjamin "Bill" Lenoir (STS-5 only)
- **Discipline:** PhD electrical engineering (MIT, 1965). BS + MS EE (MIT,
  1961 + 1962). MIT assistant professor of EE 1965–1967; research in remote
  sensing.
- **Selection:** 1967-08-04 (NASA Group 6)
- **First flight:** **STS-5** Columbia, 1982-11-11 — **15y 3m 7d deferment**
- **Mission role:** Mission Specialist (MS)
- **Career total:** 1 flight, 122+ hours
- **Departure from NASA:** 1984-09 (to Booz Allen Hamilton)
- **Death:** 2010-08-26 (bicycle accident, Sandoval County, NM)
- **Substrate-form:** **DEFERRED-15-YEARS-SOLE-FLIGHT** (joint cohort anchor
  with Allen for the STS-5 15-year-deferment pair; sole-flight sub-form
  distinguishes Lenoir from Allen)

### Karl Gordon Henize (STS-51-F)
- **Discipline:** PhD astronomy (Michigan, 1954). BA mathematics + MA astronomy
  (Virginia, 1947 + 1948). Northwestern University professor.
- **Selection:** 1967-08-04 (NASA Group 6)
- **First flight:** **STS-51-F** Challenger, 1985-07-29 — **17y 11m 25d**
  deferment (Spacelab-2)
- **Mission role:** Mission Specialist (MS)
- **Career total:** 1 flight
- **Death:** 1993-10-05 (high-altitude cerebral edema, Mt. Everest expedition)
- **Substrate-form:** **DEFERRED-18-YEARS-SOLE-FLIGHT** (~18y rounded)
- **Cluster-entry milestone:** v1.49.663

### Anthony Wayne England (STS-51-F)
- **Discipline:** PhD geophysics. From 1972-08 to 1979-06 served as research
  geophysicist with the United States Geological Survey.
- **Selection:** 1967-08-04 (NASA Group 6) — **but with a resign-and-rejoin
  nuance**
- **First resignation:** 1972-08 (with Philip Chapman), citing the dearth of
  flight opportunities in the post-Apollo era
- **Return to NASA:** 1979-06 as senior scientist-astronaut (MS), assigned to
  the Operations Mission Development Group
- **First flight:** **STS-51-F** Challenger, 1985-07-29 (Spacelab-2)
- **Mission role:** Mission Specialist (MS)
- **Career total:** 1 flight
- **Substrate-form:** **DEFERRED-18-YEARS-SOLE-FLIGHT** *(with resign-rejoin
  nuance: ~18 years from original 1967 selection wall-clock; or ~6 years from
  1979 rejoin if measured from continuous-service start. The wall-clock
  selection-to-flight figure is preserved here for cohort consistency, with
  the rejoin caveat surfaced)*
- **Cluster-entry milestone:** v1.49.663

### Don Leslie Lind (*adjacent — NASA Group 5, 1966*) — STS-51-B
- **Discipline:** PhD high-energy nuclear physics (UC Berkeley, 1964;
  Lawrence Radiation Laboratory pion-nucleon scattering). Worked at NASA
  Goddard 1964–1966 as space physicist before astronaut selection.
- **Selection:** **1966-04-04** (NASA Group 5, the "Original Nineteen") —
  *NOT Group 6*
- **First flight:** **STS-51-B** Challenger, 1985-04-29 — **19y 0m 25d
  deferment** (Spacelab-3, payload commander)
- **Mission role:** Mission Specialist / Payload Commander
- **Career total:** 1 flight
- **Substrate-form:** **LONGEST-WAIT-TO-FIRST-FLIGHT** (anchor; longest
  observed continuously-serving NASA wait)
- **Cluster-entry milestone:** v1.49.661
- **Cross-cohort note:** Lind is the upper-bound anchor for the DEFERRED-
  FLYER substrate-form but is **not a member of NASA Group 6**. He is the
  Group-5 sibling whose career was shaped by the same Apollo-Skylab-Shuttle
  flight famine that defined Group 6.

## Cohort statistics

- **Selection date:** 1967-08-04 (announcement)
- **Reporting date:** 1967-09-18 (Manned Spacecraft Center)
- **Original cohort size:** **11** (confirmed; "Excess Eleven" / "XS-11"). Eleven
  selected from 923 applicants; the eleven were Allen, Chapman, England, Henize,
  Holmquest, Lenoir, Llewellyn, Musgrave, O'Leary, Parker, and Thornton.
- **Flew:** 7 (Allen, England, Henize, Lenoir, Musgrave, Parker, Thornton)
- **Never-flew:** 4 (Chapman 1972-07, Holmquest 1973-09, Llewellyn 1968-08,
  O'Leary 1968-04)
- **Multi-flight subset:** Parker × 2, Allen × 2, Thornton × 2, Musgrave × 6
- **Range of deferment to first flight (flyers only):**
  - Shortest: ~15y 3m (Allen, Lenoir — STS-5, 1982-11)
  - Longest within Group 6: ~28y 9m (Musgrave — STS-6, 1983 first; STS-80,
    1996 last)
  - Group-6 spread of first-flight years: 1982–1985 (the 1996 figure above is
    Musgrave's *final* flight; his first flight was STS-6 1983-04)
- **Cross-cohort longest-wait (adjacent Group 5):** Lind 19y 0m 25d
