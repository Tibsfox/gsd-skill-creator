## Section 2: Co-op Legal Structure — RCW 23.86

### Washington Worker Cooperative Statute
**RCW 23.86** is Washington State's dedicated cooperative corporation statute. Key provisions for this mission:

- **Formation:** Articles of incorporation filed with Washington Secretary of State. Minimum 3 incorporators. Filing fee ~$180.
- **Member definition:** Workers who have completed a probationary period (typically 6 months) and purchased a membership share.
- **Equity structure:** Member equity (shares) is typically non-transferable outside the worker membership. This is the structural acquisition protection — a buyer cannot purchase controlling interest without the consent of the worker-members.
- **Governance:** One member, one vote. Board of directors elected by members.
- **Patronage dividends:** Profits distributed to members in proportion to labor contribution (hours or wages), not capital investment.

### Federation Model
A federated structure consists of:
1. **Individual node co-ops** — each a separate RCW 23.86 entity, owned by its own worker-members
2. **Federated purchasing/contracting entity** — a second co-op (or LLC owned by the node co-ops) that holds the master client contracts and shared engineering assets
3. **Inter-co-op agreement** — governing how production work and revenue is distributed across nodes

**Precedent:** The PNW plywood co-ops (Washington and Oregon, 1921–present) operated this way at scale — 80–450 worker-owners per plant, multiple plants operating under shared industry agreements. The model is proven in PNW manufacturing specifically.

### NWCDC (Northwest Cooperative Development Center)
- Headquarters: Olympia, WA
- Primary co-op formation support for OR/WA/ID
- Has assisted manufacturing, food service, home care, and retail co-ops in the region
- **Recommended first step:** Contact NWCDC before filing to review bylaws and federation structure

### Capitalization Pathways
1. **CDFI loans:** Community Development Financial Institutions (e.g., Craft3, a PNW CDFI) offer favorable loan terms to worker co-ops. Craft3 operates across WA/OR with a manufacturing co-op focus.
2. **SBA 7(a) loans:** Available to worker co-ops; NWCDC can connect to SBA-preferred lenders with co-op experience.
3. **Member equity buy-in:** Standard model — each founding worker purchases a membership share (typically $1,000–$10,000). This builds initial capitalization and aligns ownership incentive from day one.
4. **USDA Rural Development:** If Node North qualifies as rural (Snohomish County has rural-designated areas), USDA Business & Industry loan guarantees apply.

## Section 3: Production Methodology — AWI Standards and Lean Manufacturing

### Architectural Woodwork Institute (AWI) Standards
The AWI Quality Standards Illustrated (current edition) is the baseline spec for all commercial millwork. Three grades:
- **Economy Grade:** Basic commercial; adequate for back-of-house
- **Custom Grade:** Standard commercial millwork; appropriate for most retail and hotel applications
- **Premium Grade:** Highest quality; required for luxury hotel lobbies, flagship retail

Starbucks and Alaska Airlines fixture contracts will specify AWI Custom or Premium grade. The production model must be built to this standard from day one.

### BOM-First Engineering (Synsor Methodology Reconstructed)
Based on the documented production profile from the 16-year Synsor employee:

1. **SOW intake:** Client provides scope of work (drawings, specs, quantity, schedule)
2. **BOM generation:** Engineering converts SOW to complete bill of materials — every part, every material, every hardware item, every finish
3. **CNC program generation:** CAD/CAM software converts BOM components to CNC router programs
4. **Material procurement:** BOM-driven purchase orders to timber and sheet goods suppliers
5. **Production scheduling:** Pull scheduling — work orders released against confirmed material delivery
6. **CNC production:** Panel processing, routing, profiling, carving
7. **Finish room:** Spraying, staining, topcoat — the quality differentiator for identical-unit rollouts
8. **Assembly and staging:** Final assembly, hardware installation, quality inspection
9. **Delivery and installation:** For commercial clients, white-glove delivery and installation is often included

### CNC Equipment Baseline for Node North
- **Primary tool:** CNC router (4x8 or 5x10 table, 3-axis minimum) — panel processing, routing, carving
- **Secondary:** Wide-belt sander, edge bander, dado/rabbet saw
- **Finish room:** Spray booth with VOC controls (OSHA 29 CFR 1910.94), full downdraft or side-draft ventilation
- **Software:** CAD/CAM (Vectric, Mastercam Wood, or similar); ERP for BOM and scheduling

### Lean Manufacturing Principles (Applied to Millwork)
- Pull scheduling: no unit goes to production without a confirmed order
- Standard work: every CNC program documented and version-controlled
- Visual management: shop floor status boards tracking units through each production stage
- 5S: Sort, Set, Shine, Standardize, Sustain — critical for sawdust-heavy environments
- Kaizen events: quarterly improvement reviews with all worker-owners participating

## Section 4: Client Pipeline — Confirmed Anchor Targets

### Starbucks (Priority 1)
- **Why:** Starbucks is the largest commercial millwork client in the PNW by volume. They are in continuous store buildout and renovation mode globally.
- **Relationship entry point:** Former Synsor Materials Manager served as Starbucks Casework Manager 1997–2004. This individual is confirmed active on LinkedIn as owner of International Millwork & Fixtures (founded 2015). **This is the primary warm outreach target.**
- **What Starbucks needs:** Consistent, spec-compliant casework for espresso bars, service counters, shelving systems. AWI Custom grade. Rollout quantities of 50–500 units for regional builds.
- **LEED/FSC requirement:** Starbucks has active sustainability commitments. FSC-certified timber and LEED-compliant finishes are likely required.

### Alaska Airlines (Priority 2)
- **Why:** Confirmed Synsor client. Alaska Airlines is headquartered in Seattle; gate and cabin fixture refreshes are cyclical. PNW-based supplier has logistics advantage for Seatac-based operations.
- **Relationship entry point:** Alaska Airlines facilities procurement team. Former Synsor employees in the region may have contacts.
- **What Alaska needs:** Gate fixtures (millwork counters, signage surrounds, seating surrounds), cabin wood components. Aerospace-adjacent precision tolerance.

### Hotel Renovation Chains (Priority 3)
- **Why:** Hotel renovation (PIPs — Property Improvement Plans) operate on 7–10 year cycles. Marriott, Hilton, and IHG all have significant PNW hotel footprints. Lobby and in-room millwork (headboards, nightstands, desks, closet systems) is high-volume, repeatable work.
- **Relationship entry point:** Hotel FF&E (Furniture, Fixtures & Equipment) procurement — typically managed through design firms (interior design studios that spec the work, then go to bid). Target PNW-based hospitality interior design firms first.
- **What hotels need:** Identical units across 200+ rooms. AWI Custom grade. FSC certification increasingly required for brand standards.

### Charlotte Russe / Retail Chain Fixtures (Priority 4)
- **Why:** Confirmed Synsor client. Retail chain store rollouts are the bread-and-butter volume work. Charlotte Russe is defunct but the retail fixture market is not — Target, REI, Nordstrom Rack all have PNW connections and ongoing store fixture needs.
- **Relationship entry point:** Store design / visual merchandising procurement teams at PNW-headquartered retail brands.

## Section 5: Supply Chain — PNW Timber and Materials

### Primary Hardwood Supplier: Emerson Hardwood Company
- Established 1907 in Portland, OR
- 4 divisions, 7 locations in Oregon and Washington
- Carries domestic and imported hardwood lumber, plywood, veneers, flooring, decking, mouldings
- Has in-house CNC and millwork department (EM2) — potential subcontract relationship, not just supply
- **Recommended approach:** Open a wholesale account as a co-op; negotiate volume pricing once first anchor contract is in hand

### Specialty Hardwood: Crosscut Hardwoods
- Locations: Seattle, Portland, Eugene
- Known as "the woodworker's candy store" — specialty species, custom cuts
- Appropriate for premium-grade work requiring unusual species or figured wood

### Sheet Goods: OrePac Building Products
- I-5 corridor distribution from 10 branches across western US
- Fabricates and distributes door and building components; new MDF manufacturing facility operational 2024
- **Recommended approach:** Set up a commercial account; they serve contractors and manufacturers at volume

### FSC Certification Pathway
- FSC Chain of Custody (CoC) certification is required by most major hotel and retail clients
- Certification body: Rainforest Alliance or SCS Global Services (both operate in PNW)
- Timeline: 3–6 months from application to certification
- Cost: ~$1,500–$3,000/year for a small manufacturer
- **Critical path item:** Apply for FSC CoC in parallel with co-op formation — client contracts will require it

### Finish Materials
- Stains, topcoats, sealers: Sherwin-Williams has commercial millwork finish programs with volume pricing
- VOC-compliant finishes: Required for LEED compliance; Sherwin-Williams Catalyst and Resisthane product lines meet California Air Resources Board (CARB) standards (the de facto national standard)

### Sno-Isle TECH Skills Center
- Location: Everett, WA (and additional locations in Snohomish County)
- Existing programs include: Advanced Manufacturing, Welding, Building Construction Technology
- No dedicated commercial millwork or CNC woodworking track currently documented
- **Opportunity:** Propose a dedicated Precision Woodworking / Commercial Millwork track in partnership with Node North
- **Model:** Students do classroom work at Sno-Isle, hands-on production work at Node North shop floor; earn co-op equity from first day of apprenticeship

### Apprenticeship Structure
- **Year 1:** Classroom (AWI standards, wood science, CAD basics) + shop floor (material handling, sanding, assembly)
- **Year 2:** CNC operation, BOM reading, finish room work

## Section 7: Safety Considerations

| Condition | Recommendation | Boundary Type |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| CNC router operation | OSHA 29 CFR 1910.212 (machine guarding); operator certification required | ABSOLUTE |
| Finish room operations | OSHA 29 CFR 1910.94 (ventilation); VOC monitoring; explosion-proof electrical | ABSOLUTE |
| Wood dust exposure | OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1000 (PEL for wood dust); dust collection system mandatory | ABSOLUTE |
| FSC certification lapse | Notify all active clients immediately if certification lapses; do not ship FSC-claimed product | GATE |
| Worker-owner equity transfer | Any request to transfer equity outside worker membership triggers board review | GATE |
| Out-of-spec production units | Do not ship units failing AWI grade inspection; document and rework | GATE |
