## Objective

Build a concrete client acquisition strategy for the PNW Millwork Co-op Network with named outreach targets, relationship rationale, and ready-to-use templates. Done looks like a founding worker-owner can open this document, identify the first three calls to make, and have a draft message ready for each one.

## Context

The most important data point from Synsor's history: a Synsor Materials Manager (1990–1994) went directly to Eddie Bauer as Asst. Purchasing Manager (1994–1997), then to Starbucks as Casework Manager (1997–2004), and is now active as owner of International Millwork & Fixtures (founded 2015). This single career arc is the primary warm outreach target — a person who was trained by Synsor, managed casework procurement at Starbucks for seven years, and is now in the fixture industry independently.

Confirmed Synsor clients: Starbucks, Alaska Airlines, AT&T, Charlotte Russe. These are the priority target industries. The co-op does not cold-pitch; it approaches through the alumni network and shared PNW history.

Client contracts will require FSC certification and likely LEED/CARB compliance. These must be in place before a contract can be executed — the supply chain component (05) handles this, but the client pipeline must reference the requirement.

## Technical Specification

### Required Outputs
1. **Priority target list** — minimum 3 named targets with: client name, entry point rationale, specific contact strategy, what they need, compliance requirements
2. **Warm outreach templates** — one per priority target; not cold pitches; references shared PNW history, Synsor legacy, co-op ownership model
3. **SOW response template** — structured response to a commercial fixture SOW: acknowledgment, BOM review timeline, AWI grade commitment, FSC/LEED status, production capacity, delivery timeline
4. **Contract intake checklist** — questions to ask before accepting a bid: AWI grade, FSC/LEED requirements, unit quantity, delivery schedule, installation required?

### Priority Targets (pre-populated from research)
- **Starbucks:** Former Synsor Materials Manager / Starbucks Casework Manager is the entry point. She is now owner of International Millwork & Fixtures and has ongoing industry relationships. The pitch: co-op-owned PNW supplier with direct Synsor lineage, FSC-certified, AWI Custom/Premium, Starbucks rollout experience baked in.
- **Alaska Airlines:** Confirmed Synsor client. PNW headquarters. Gate and cabin fixture refresh cycles. Entry point: Alaska Airlines facilities/procurement, or via interior design firms working on Alaska's terminal refresh projects.
- **Hotel chains (PNW properties):** Entry via PNW-based hospitality interior design firms. FF&E specification is done by the design firm; the co-op wins by being on the approved vendor list for 2–3 active PNW hospitality designers.
