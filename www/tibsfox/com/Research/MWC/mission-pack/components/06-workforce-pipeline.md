## Objective
Design the workforce pipeline that feeds Node North and eventual additional nodes with certified precision woodworkers — from Sno-Isle TECH students through apprenticeship to full worker-owner equity. Done looks like a curriculum outline and apprenticeship structure that a Sno-Isle TECH program director and a Node North founding worker-owner can both sign off on.
## Context
Sno-Isle TECH Skills Center (Everett, WA) has existing Advanced Manufacturing programs but no dedicated commercial millwork or CNC woodworking track. The opportunity is to propose one in partnership with Node North.
The Fox Infrastructure Group Master Business Plan identifies "Advanced Fabrication & Manufacturing" as one of seven FoxEdu workforce tracks. The Sno-Isle partnership and Node North apprenticeship are a direct implementation of that track — the first concrete PNW workforce node for the broader corridor manufacturing education network. This connection must be documented explicitly.
Former Synsor employees remain in the region. 10–20 year Synsor veterans are in their 40s–60s today. They carry institutional knowledge that no curriculum document can fully capture. Identifying 2–3 as founding master instructors is critical.
Washington State apprenticeship registration is governed by WAC 296-05 (WA Apprenticeship and Training Council). Registered apprenticeships provide access to apprenticeship wages and, in some cases, federal training funds.
## Technical Specification
### Required Outputs
1. **Sno-Isle TECH partnership proposal outline** — specific curriculum track name, contact pathway, Node North as the employer-partner, proposed start timeline
2. **3-year apprenticeship structure** — Year 1/2/3 curriculum and shop floor progression; AWI-aligned skill milestones
3. **Co-op equity accrual model** — how an apprentice purchases their membership share; vesting schedule; what happens if they leave before completion
4. **Master instructor strategy** — LinkedIn search approach for former Synsor employees; role definition for founding instructors; compensation model in a worker co-op
5. **WAC 296-05 registration pathway** — steps to register the apprenticeship with WA Apprenticeship and Training Council
6. **FoxEdu integration documentation** — explicit connection to Fox Infrastructure Group "Advanced Fabrication & Manufacturing" workforce track; how this program feeds the broader corridor network
### Apprenticeship Curriculum Outline (Year by Year)
- **Year 1:** Wood science and AWI standards (classroom) + material handling, sanding, assembly (shop floor)
- **Year 2:** CNC router operation, BOM reading, finish room entry-level (spray prep, masking)
- **Year 3:** CNC programming, project management basics, quality inspection to AWI grade, finish room lead
