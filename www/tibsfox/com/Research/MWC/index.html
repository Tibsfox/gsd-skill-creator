PNW Millwork Co-op

Cooperative Economics · Timber Heritage · Craftwork · Supply Chain · Workforce Development
Overview Co-op Economics Timber Heritage Craftsmanship Supply Chain Workforce Client Pipeline Verification Mission Pack
There was a shop in Everett that built the insides of Starbucks stores, Alaska Airlines gates, and hotel lobbies across the Pacific Northwest for forty-six years. Synsor Corporation was not a craft shop and not a furniture factory — it was a precision manufacturing operation embedded in its own community. Then a New York consolidator bought it and the Everett operation wound down. The gap it left is real. The reconstruction is a federated worker co-op network along the I-5 corridor — owned by the workers who build it, acquisition-proof by design.

Research Modules

M1: Cooperative Economics & Legal Structure

Legal Track B · RCW 23.86

Worker cooperative formation under Washington State law. RCW 23.86 federation structure, acquisition-protection bylaws, patronage dividends, one-member-one-vote governance. NWCDC formation pathway. The PNW plywood co-op precedent. Capitalization through CDFI, SBA 7(a), and member equity.

M2: PNW Timber Heritage & Industrial Memory

Heritage Track A · Synsor lineage

Synsor Corporation's 46-year operating history (1971–2017). The Everett/Mukilteo millwork corridor. Boeing-adjacent precision manufacturing culture. The acquisition trap and community ownership as defense. PNW plywood co-ops (1921–present) as structural precedent.

M3: Millwork Craftsmanship & Production Engineering

Production Track A · BOM-first

BOM-first CNC production methodology reconstructed from Synsor's documented workflow. SOW-to-BOM pipeline, CNC programming, AWI Quality Standards (Custom and Premium grade), lean manufacturing, finish room protocols, multi-node production coordination.

M4: I-5 Corridor Supply Chain Network

Supply Track C · Timber sourcing

Hardwood, sheet goods, finish materials, and hardware supply mapping along the I-5 corridor. Emerson Hardwood (est. 1907), OrePac Building Products, Crosscut Hardwoods. FSC Chain of Custody certification. CARB-compliant finishes. Supply resilience and backup protocols.

M5: Workforce Development & Apprenticeship

Workforce Track C · Sno-Isle TECH

Three-year apprenticeship pipeline from Sno-Isle TECH through Node North to full worker-owner equity. AWI-aligned skill milestones, master instructor recruitment from Synsor alumni, WAC 296-05 registration, FoxEdu integration.

M6: Client Pipeline & Commercial Strategy

Business Track B · Anchor contracts

Anchor client acquisition through the Synsor alumni network. Starbucks casework, Alaska Airlines fixtures, hotel FF&E renovation cycles. Warm outreach strategy, SOW response templates, contract intake checklists. The alumni-to-purchasing pipeline.

M7: The Joinery Report — Verification Matrix

Integration Wave 2 · NWCDC package

Integration audit across all components. 10 success criteria verification. Gap and conflict resolution log. Critical path from founding cohort to first production run. NWCDC presentation package assembly. The complete formation proposal.
