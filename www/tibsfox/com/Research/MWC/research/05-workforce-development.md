## 1. The Workforce Problem

Synsor Corporation operated for 46 years. During that time, it developed precision woodworkers through on-the-job training -- an informal apprenticeship that transmitted institutional knowledge from senior workers to junior workers through years of side-by-side work on the shop floor. When Synsor closed, that pipeline stopped.

No current institution in Snohomish County offers a dedicated commercial millwork or CNC woodworking curriculum. Sno-Isle TECH Skills Center has Advanced Manufacturing and Building Construction Technology programs, but nothing specifically targeting the precision CNC woodworking skills that commercial millwork demands [1].

The workforce gap has three dimensions:

1. **Skills gap.** The specific skills of BOM-first manufacturing engineering, CNC woodworking at AWI Custom/Premium grade, and production finish room work are not being taught anywhere in the corridor.
2. **Knowledge gap.** The institutional knowledge of Synsor's production methodology -- the informal know-how that only 10-20 year veterans carry -- is aging out. Former Synsor workers in their 40s-60s are the last generation who can transfer this knowledge directly.
3. **Pipeline gap.** Even if someone wanted to enter commercial millwork, there is no clear pathway from interest to employment. No curriculum, no apprenticeship structure, no employer partner.

The co-op's workforce pipeline solves all three by creating a formal apprenticeship program in partnership with Sno-Isle TECH, staffed by former Synsor master instructors, and connected to the broader Fox Infrastructure Group education network.

## 3. Three-Year Apprenticeship Structure

The apprenticeship is structured as three progressive years, each building on the previous. The design follows AWI-aligned skill milestones and the Washington State apprenticeship framework (WAC 296-05).

### Progression Overview

| Year | Focus | Shop Floor Role | Classroom Content | AWI Skill Level |
|------|-------|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Foundation | Material handling, sanding, basic assembly | Wood science, AWI standards, CAD, safety | Helper |
| 2 | Production | CNC operation, BOM reading, finish room prep | CNC programming basics, material science, finish chemistry | Operator |
| 3 | Leadership | CNC programming, project management, quality inspection | Advanced CNC, management, cost estimation | Journey-level |

### Hours Split

| Year | Classroom Hours | Shop Floor Hours | Total Hours |
|------|----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 | 400 | 1,600 | 2,000 |
| 2 | 200 | 1,800 | 2,000 |
| 3 | 100 | 1,900 | 2,000 |
| **Total** | **700** | **5,300** | **6,000** |

The ratio shifts from 20% classroom / 80% shop in Year 1 to 5% classroom / 95% shop in Year 3, reflecting the progression from learning foundations to applying them under production conditions.

## 4. Year 1: Foundation

### Classroom Curriculum

| Module | Content | Duration | Assessment |
|--------|---------|----------|-----------|
| Wood Science | Species identification, grain structure, moisture content, wood movement | 40 hrs | Written exam + species identification practical |
| AWI Standards | Quality Standards Illustrated overview, Economy/Custom/Premium grades, inspection criteria | 40 hrs | Grade identification practical |
| CAD Basics | 2D drafting, dimensioning, basic 3D modeling, BOM output | 80 hrs | CAD project (design a simple cabinet, produce BOM) |
| Blueprint Reading | Plan views, elevations, sections, detail drawings, architectural notation | 40 hrs | Blueprint reading practical |
| Shop Math | Fractions, decimals, board foot calculation, material estimation | 40 hrs | Math practical (calculate BOM quantities) |
| OSHA Safety | 29 CFR 1910.212 (machine guarding), 1910.94 (ventilation), 1910.1000 (dust), PPE | 40 hrs | OSHA 10-hour certification |
| Material Handling | Forklift, material storage, rack systems, inventory management | 40 hrs | Forklift certification |
| Introduction to Finishing | Wood preparation, stain types, sealer types, topcoat types, VOC awareness | 40 hrs | Stain sample project |
| Co-op Governance | Cooperative principles, RCW 23.86, membership rights and responsibilities | 40 hrs | Written exam |

### Shop Floor Activities (Year 1)

- Material receiving and inspection (grade checking lumber deliveries)
- Material staging (pulling and organizing material for production orders)
- Sanding (wide-belt sander operation, hand sanding techniques)
- Basic assembly (gluing, clamping, fastening, squareness checking)
- Shop maintenance (5S implementation, dust collection, clean-up)
- Observation of CNC operations (standing with the CNC operator, learning the workflow)

### Year 1 Milestones

| Milestone | Assessment | Pass Criterion |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| Species identification | 10 species, 30 seconds each | 8/10 correct |
| AWI grade assessment | 5 sample panels, grade each | 4/5 correct |
| CAD project | Design and BOM a simple 3-shelf bookcase | Complete BOM, correct dimensions |
| OSHA 10 | OSHA 10-Hour General Industry certification | OSHA card issued |
| Sanding quality | Sand 10 panels to 180-grit finish | 9/10 pass touch test |
| Assembly accuracy | Assemble 5 practice units | 4/5 square within 1/32" |

## 6. Year 3: Leadership

### Classroom Curriculum

| Module | Content | Duration | Assessment |
|--------|---------|----------|-----------|
| CNC Programming | CAD/CAM toolpath generation, nesting, optimization | 60 hrs | Program a 5-part fixture from drawings |
| Project Management | Scheduling, client communication, deadline management | 20 hrs | Lead a small project start-to-finish |
| Cost Estimation | Material costing, labor hour estimation, overhead allocation | 20 hrs | Estimate a sample project within 10% of actual |

### Shop Floor Activities (Year 3)

- CNC programming (writing new programs under engineering lead supervision)
- Quality inspection (performing AWI grade inspections)
- Production scheduling (managing the daily work queue)
- Client interaction (participating in SOW review meetings)
- Leading kaizen events (identifying and driving improvement actions)
- Mentoring Year 1 apprentices (the teaching-learning cycle begins)

### Year 3 Milestones and Completion

| Milestone | Assessment | Pass Criterion |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| CNC programming | Program a complete 5-part fixture from architectural drawings | Program runs, parts fit, AWI Custom pass |
| AWI inspection | Inspect 20 units for AWI Custom grade | 19/20 correct grade determination |
| Project lead | Lead a small project (10 units) from SOW to delivery | On-time, on-spec, within budget |
| Journey-level certification | Comprehensive practical and written assessment | Pass all components |

Upon completion of Year 3, the apprentice is eligible for:
- **Journey-level certification** from the Washington State Apprenticeship and Training Council
- **Full worker-owner status** in the co-op (if not already achieved through equity accrual)
- **Independent CNC programming and quality inspection authority** on the production floor

## 7. Co-op Equity Accrual Model

The apprentice begins accruing cooperative equity from day one. This is a non-negotiable principle: if you are working in the shop, you are building ownership.

### Accrual Structure

```
EQUITY ACCRUAL TIMELINE
================================================================

  Year 0 (enrollment)
  |  Initial equity contribution: $500 (from first paycheck, installment OK)
  |
  Year 1
  |  Accrual: $150/month (deducted from wages, credited to equity account)
  |  Year 1 total: $1,800 + $500 initial = $2,300
  |
  Year 2
  |  Accrual: $200/month
  |  Year 2 total: $2,400 -> Cumulative: $4,700
  |
  Year 3
  |  Accrual: $250/month
  |  Year 3 total: $3,000 -> Cumulative: $7,700
  |
  Completion
  |  Full membership share value: $5,000 (reached during Year 2)
  |  Excess accrual builds additional equity stake
  |  Full voting rights from day one of membership
```

### Key Provisions

- **Accrual from day one.** There is no probationary period for equity accrual. The apprentice begins building ownership immediately.
- **Voting rights.** Full voting rights attach upon completion of the probationary period (6 months) and payment of the initial equity contribution. This means most apprentices have voting rights within their first year.
- **Departure before completion.** If an apprentice leaves before completing the three-year program, their accrued equity is refunded at book value, minus any amounts owed to the cooperative. They do not forfeit accrued equity.
- **Partial vesting.** There is no cliff vesting. Equity accrues linearly. An apprentice who completes 18 months has 18 months of accrued equity.
- **Wage structure.** Apprentice wages start at the entry-level rate and increase annually. The equity accrual is in addition to wages, not a substitute for wages.

### Wage and Equity Schedule

| Year | Hourly Wage (est.) | Equity Accrual/Month | Annual Total Comp |
|------|-------------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | $18-22/hr | $150 | $37,440 - $45,760 + $1,800 equity |
| 2 | $22-26/hr | $200 | $45,760 - $54,080 + $2,400 equity |
| 3 | $26-32/hr | $250 | $54,080 - $66,560 + $3,000 equity |
| Journey | $32-40/hr | Standard member rate | $66,560 - $83,200 + patronage |

## 8. Master Instructor Recruitment

The most critical element of the workforce pipeline is not the curriculum or the institutional partnership -- it is the master instructors. These are the people who carry the institutional knowledge that no document fully captures.

### Target Profile

- Former Synsor Corporation employees with 10+ year tenure
- Roles: CNC operator, finish room lead, production manager, manufacturing engineer
- Currently in their 40s-60s, likely still in the PNW
- May be working at other millwork shops, in procurement roles, retired, or in adjacent industries

### Recruitment Strategy

1. **LinkedIn search.** Search "Synsor Corporation" to identify former employees by role and tenure. Map their current positions and locations.
2. **Direct outreach.** Contact identified alumni with a clear message: we are rebuilding Synsor under worker ownership, and we need master instructors to transfer the knowledge. This is not a job listing; it is an invitation to rebuild something they helped create.
3. **Role definition.** Master instructor is a worker-owner position, not a contractor role. They are full members of the co-op with voting rights and equity.
4. **Compensation.** Master instructor compensation should reflect their expertise: journey-level wage plus a training stipend. Their equity accrual begins immediately (they are not apprentices; they are founding members).
5. **Knowledge documentation.** Part of the master instructor role is documenting what they know. The production playbook, finish room protocols, and quality inspection procedures should all be co-authored with master instructors.

### Expected Recruitment Timeline

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| LinkedIn mapping | Identify 5-10 former Synsor employees in target roles | Month 1 |
| Initial outreach | Contact top 5 candidates | Month 1-2 |
| Interest assessment | In-person meetings with interested candidates | Month 2-3 |
| Founding commitment | 2-3 master instructors commit to founding cohort | Month 3-6 |
| Knowledge documentation begins | Co-authoring production playbook | Pre-launch |

## 9. WAC 296-05: Apprenticeship Registration

Washington State apprenticeship registration is governed by WAC 296-05 (Washington Administrative Code) and administered by the Washington State Apprenticeship and Training Council (WSATC) under the Department of Labor & Industries [2].

### Why Register

- **Legal recognition:** Registered apprenticeships are recognized by the U.S. Department of Labor
- **Apprentice wage provisions:** Registered programs may pay apprentice wages (a percentage of journey-level wage) that increase with each year
- **Federal training funds:** Some registered programs qualify for federal training grants
- **Credential value:** Journey-level certification from a registered program carries more weight than informal training

### Registration Process

1. **Contact WSATC:** Washington State Apprenticeship and Training Council, Department of Labor & Industries (Olympia, WA)
2. **Program standards submission:** Submit program standards including: curriculum outline, on-the-job training plan, related supplemental instruction (classroom) plan, wage scale, supervision ratio
3. **Advisory committee:** Establish a program advisory committee including employer representatives (Node North), employee representatives (worker-owners), and educational institution representative (Sno-Isle TECH)
4. **Review and approval:** WSATC reviews standards and issues program registration
5. **Individual apprentice registration:** Each apprentice is individually registered with WSATC upon enrollment

### Timeline

| Step | Duration |
|------|----------|
| Program standards preparation | 1-2 months |
| WSATC submission and review | 2-4 months |
| Approval and first enrollment | 1-2 months after approval |
| **Total** | **4-8 months** |

## 10. FoxEdu Integration

The Fox Infrastructure Group Master Business Plan identifies seven workforce education tracks under the FoxEdu umbrella. The "Advanced Fabrication & Manufacturing" track is the direct connection point for the millwork co-op's apprenticeship program [3].

### Alignment Map

| FoxEdu Track | MWC Implementation |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Advanced Fabrication & Manufacturing | Precision Woodworking & Commercial Millwork program at Sno-Isle TECH + Node North |
| Core skills: CNC operation, CAD/CAM, production engineering | Mapped directly to Year 1-3 apprenticeship curriculum |
| Workforce pipeline: regional skills centers + employer partners | Sno-Isle TECH (skills center) + Node North (employer partner) |
| Credential: industry-recognized certification | Journey-level certification via WAC 296-05 + AWI knowledge assessment |

### How the MWC Program Feeds FoxEdu

The Sno-Isle TECH partnership and Node North apprenticeship are the first operational instance of the FoxEdu Advanced Fabrication & Manufacturing track. They establish:

1. **The curriculum template.** The three-year program structure can be adapted for other fabrication disciplines (metalwork, composites, advanced materials).
2. **The employer partnership model.** The co-op-as-employer-partner model demonstrates that worker cooperatives can serve as apprenticeship hosts.
3. **The equity accrual model.** Day-one equity accrual is the FoxEdu innovation: education that builds ownership, not just skills.
4. **The geographic template.** Sno-Isle TECH + Node North is the Snohomish County instance. The same model can replicate along the I-5 corridor: Clark College + Node South (Portland), Renton Technical College + Node Central (South King County).

## 11. Safety Training Requirements

All apprentices and worker-owners must complete safety training before operating any equipment. Safety is not a module within the curriculum -- it is a precondition for the curriculum.

### Required Certifications

| Certification | Timing | Provider | Cost |
|--------------|--------|----------|------|
| OSHA 10-Hour General Industry | Year 1, first month | OSHA-authorized trainer | $25-75/person |
| Forklift operator | Year 1, first quarter | OSHA-compliant training provider | $50-150/person |
| Respirator fit test | Before finish room work | Occupational health provider | $50-100/person |
| First Aid / CPR | Year 1 | American Red Cross or equivalent | $50-100/person |

### Equipment-Specific Training

| Equipment | Training Required | Sign-Off Authority |
|-----------|------------------|-------------------|
| CNC router | Manufacturer training + 40 hours supervised operation | CNC lead or master instructor |
| Wide-belt sander | 8 hours supervised operation | Production lead |
| Table saw | 16 hours supervised operation + SawStop or equivalent safety system training | Production lead |
| Edge bander | 8 hours supervised operation | Production lead |
| Spray booth | 24 hours supervised operation + respirator training | Finish room lead |
| Compressed air system | 4 hours supervised operation | Production lead |

> **SAFETY WARNING:** No apprentice operates any equipment without completed training and sign-off by the designated authority. No exceptions. Equipment operation without training is an OSHA violation and a co-op safety policy violation subject to immediate work stoppage.

### Shop Safety Protocol

- **Emergency stops:** Every worker must know the location and operation of every emergency stop in the shop. Test: during the first week, apprentices must locate and identify all emergency stops.
- **PPE requirements:** Safety glasses at all times in the shop. Hearing protection near CNC equipment. Respirator in finish room. Steel-toe boots in material handling areas.
- **Dust collection:** Dust collection system must be running whenever any cutting, sanding, or routing equipment is operating. Wood dust PEL: 5 mg/m3 for hardwood dust (OSHA 29 CFR 1910.1000).
- **Incident reporting:** All incidents (injury, near-miss, equipment malfunction) reported immediately. Worker-owners participate in incident review and corrective action.
