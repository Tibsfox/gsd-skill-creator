# I-5 Corridor Supply Chain Network

> **Domain:** Materials Procurement & Supply Chain Management
> **Module:** 4 -- Timber, Sheet Goods, and Finish Supply for Commercial Millwork
> **Through-line:** *The supply chain is not a cost center. It is the network's circulatory system.* When a purchasing manager at Node North opens a wholesale account with Emerson Hardwood, they are not just buying lumber -- they are connecting the co-op to a supply infrastructure that has operated along the I-5 corridor since 1907. The reliability of the supply chain is what enables the reliability of the production promise. Without it, the BOM is a wish list.

## 1. Supply Chain Architecture

The supply chain for the PNW Millwork Co-op Network maps directly onto the I-5 corridor. Primary and backup suppliers for each material category are selected for geographic proximity, product quality, FSC certification status, and established commercial wholesale programs.

```

================================================================

  SUPPLIER NORTH                    SUPPLIER CENTRAL
  +-----------------------+        +-----------------------+
  | Emerson Hardwood      |        | Crosscut Hardwoods    |
  | (Seattle location)    |        | (Seattle)             |
  | Hardwood lumber,      |        | Specialty species,    |
  | plywood, veneer       |        | figured wood, custom  |
  +-----------+-----------+        +-----------+-----------+
              |                                |
              v                                v
       +------+------+                 +-------+-------+
       | NODE NORTH  |                 | NODE CENTRAL  |
       | Everett     |                 | South King    |
       +------+------+                 +-------+-------+
              |                                |
              +--------------------------------+
                             |
                             v
  +-----------------------+        +-----------------------+
  | Emerson Hardwood      |        | OrePac Building       |
  | (Portland HQ,        |        | Products              |
  | est. 1907)            |        | (I-5 corridor,        |
  | 4 divisions,          |        | 10 branches)          |
  | 7 locations           |        | MDF, plywood, doors   |
  +-----------+-----------+        +-----------+-----------+
              |                                |
              v                                v
       +------+------+                        |
       | NODE SOUTH  | <----------------------+
       | Portland    |

```

### Supply Chain Principles

1. **Primary + backup for every category.** No material category depends on a single supplier. If the primary is out of stock, the backup activates without delay.
2. **BOM-driven procurement.** No speculative ordering. Every purchase order traces to an approved BOM line item.
3. **FSC-first sourcing.** FSC-certified material is the default. Non-FSC material requires explicit approval and cannot be used on FSC-claiming contracts.
4. **I-5 corridor preference.** Suppliers within the I-5 corridor between Bellingham and Eugene get preference for delivery speed and shipping cost.

## 4. Specialty Hardwoods

### Primary Supplier: Crosscut Hardwoods

Crosscut Hardwoods is known as "the woodworker's candy store" -- a specialty hardwood retailer and wholesaler with deep inventory of unusual species, figured wood, and custom cuts [2].

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Locations | Seattle, Portland, Eugene |
| Specialty | Unusual species, figured wood (curly, quilted, birdseye), live-edge slabs |
| Account type | Retail and wholesale; volume discounts for commercial accounts |
| Use case | Premium-grade millwork requiring unusual species or figured wood |

**When to use Crosscut instead of Emerson:**

- Client specifies a species not in Emerson's standard inventory
- Premium-grade project requires figured or character wood
- Small-quantity specialty runs where Emerson's minimum order is too large
- Custom veneer matching for feature panels

### Exotic and Imported Species

For projects requiring exotic species (teak, mahogany, ipe, etc.):

- **Emerson Hardwood:** Carries some imported species; verify availability per species
- **Specialty importers:** For rare species, contact Pacific Woodworks (Portland) or J. Gibson McIlvain (national, ships to PNW)
- **FSC requirement:** Exotic species are more likely to have FSC sourcing issues. Verify FSC certification before quoting.

## 7. FSC Chain of Custody Certification

FSC (Forest Stewardship Council) Chain of Custody (CoC) certification is required by Starbucks, most hotel chains, and all LEED-certified projects. It verifies that FSC-certified timber maintains its certified status through every step of the supply chain [5].

### Certification Process

| Step | Action | Timeline | Cost |
|------|--------|----------|------|
| 1 | Choose certification body | Week 1 | Free |
| 2 | Application submission | Week 2 | $500-$1,000 |
| 3 | Document review | Week 3-6 | Included |
| 4 | On-site audit | Week 6-10 | $1,000-$2,000 |
| 5 | Audit report review | Week 10-14 | Included |
| 6 | Certificate issued | Week 14-16 | Included |
| 7 | Annual surveillance audits | Annually | $800-$1,500/year |
| **Total initial cost** | | **3-4 months** | **$1,500-$3,000** |

### Certification Bodies Operating in PNW

- **Rainforest Alliance (RA-Cert):** Most widely recognized FSC certification body. Office in Portland, OR.
- **SCS Global Services:** Based in Emeryville, CA; active in PNW. Offers combined FSC/PEFC certification.

### FSC CoC Requirements for the Co-op

Once certified, the co-op must:

1. **Track FSC material separately.** FSC-certified lumber and sheet goods must be identifiable from receiving through production to finished product.
2. **Maintain purchase records.** Every FSC-certified material purchase must include the supplier's FSC CoC certificate number.
3. **Label FSC product.** Finished products sold as FSC-certified must carry the FSC label.
4. **Annual audit.** The certification body conducts an annual surveillance audit to verify compliance.
5. **No mixing.** FSC-certified and non-certified material of the same species cannot be mixed in a single production run unless using the FSC percentage or credit system (consult certification body).

> **SAFETY WARNING:** Shipping product labeled as FSC-certified when the CoC has lapsed or the material is not actually certified is a certification violation that can result in decertification, contract termination by clients, and reputational damage. If the CoC certificate lapses, immediately notify all active clients and halt all FSC-labeled shipments.

## 8. Lead Time Management

Production scheduling depends on reliable material lead times. The following table represents standard lead times from established accounts:

| Material Category | Primary Supplier | Lead Time | Buffer Recommendation |
|-------------------|-----------------|-----------|----------------------|
| Standard hardwood lumber | Emerson Hardwood | 3-5 business days | 2 days |
| Specialty hardwood | Crosscut Hardwoods | 5-10 business days | 3 days |
| Exotic hardwood | Various | 10-21 business days | 5 days |
| Hardwood plywood (standard) | OrePac | 1-3 business days | 1 day |
| Hardwood plywood (custom veneer) | Emerson Hardwood | 10-15 business days | 3 days |
| MDF, particleboard | OrePac | 1-2 business days | 1 day |
| Finish materials | Sherwin-Williams | 2-5 business days | 2 days (keep common stock on hand) |
| Hardware (standard) | Hafele | 3-7 business days | 2 days |
| Hardware (specialty) | Various | 7-21 business days | 5 days |

### Critical Path Items

The following items have the longest lead times and must be ordered earliest:

1. **Exotic hardwoods** -- 10-21 business days. Order immediately upon BOM approval.
2. **Custom veneer layups** -- 10-15 business days. Order with exotic hardwoods.
3. **Specialty hardware** -- 7-21 business days. Confirm availability before quoting the project.

### Inventory Policy

- **MDF and standard plywood:** Maintain 2-week buffer stock (these are used on nearly every project)
- **Common stain colors and topcoat:** Maintain 1-month stock of top 5 colors
- **Hardwood lumber:** Order per-project from BOM. No speculative inventory.
- **Hardware:** Common items (European hinges, drawer slides) maintain shelf stock. Specialty items per-project.

## 9. Supply Chain Resilience

### Stockout Response Protocol

When a primary supplier cannot fill an order within the required timeline:

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Contact primary supplier: confirm stockout, expected restock date | Same day |
| 2 | Contact backup supplier: confirm availability and lead time | Same day |
| 3 | If backup can fill: redirect PO to backup supplier | Same day |
| 4 | If backup cannot fill: contact tertiary sources (other regional distributors) | Day 1-2 |
| 5 | If no supplier can fill: notify client, propose timeline adjustment or material substitution | Day 2-3 |
| 6 | If material substitution: revise BOM, obtain client approval, reissue to production | Per process |

### Supplier Redundancy Matrix

| Material Category | Primary | Backup | Tertiary |
|-------------------|---------|--------|----------|
| Hardwood lumber | Emerson Hardwood | Crosscut Hardwoods | Pacific Woodworks |
| Hardwood plywood | OrePac | Emerson (Plywood Div.) | Columbia Forest Products |
| MDF / sheet goods | OrePac | Roseburg Forest Products | Georgia-Pacific (regional) |
| Specialty hardwood | Crosscut Hardwoods | Emerson Hardwood | J. Gibson McIlvain |
| Stains and topcoats | Sherwin-Williams | Mohawk Finishing | ML Campbell |
| Hardware | Hafele | Blum (through distributor) | Wurth |

### Supply Chain Risk Assessment

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|-----------|
| Primary hardwood supplier stockout | Medium | Medium | Backup supplier relationship maintained; 2-week buffer on common species |
| FSC-certified material unavailable | Low-Medium | High | Multiple FSC suppliers; early-order protocol for FSC jobs |
| Finish product formulation change | Low | High | Maintain reference panels; test new batches before production |
| Shipping disruption (weather, labor) | Low | Medium | 2-day buffer in production schedule; emergency pickup option |
| Price increase (lumber market) | Medium | Medium | Fixed-price quotes valid 30 days; BOM cost recalculated at order time |

## 10. Account Establishment Protocol

### Emerson Hardwood Account Opening

1. **Contact:** Call Portland headquarters or Seattle branch. Request commercial wholesale account.
2. **Documentation:** Provide business license (RCW 23.86 incorporation), EIN, resale certificate (WA state).
3. **Credit application:** Complete Emerson's commercial credit application. Net-30 terms standard for established accounts.
4. **Minimum order:** Typically none for wholesale accounts, but volume pricing tiers start at ~$1,000/order.
5. **FSC discussion:** Request a meeting with their FSC compliance team to discuss CoC documentation requirements.
6. **EM2 introduction:** Request introduction to EM2 (their in-house millwork division) for potential subcontract or overflow partnership.

### OrePac Account Opening

1. **Contact:** Call nearest branch office (Seattle, Portland, or I-5 corridor location).
2. **Documentation:** Business license, EIN, resale certificate.
3. **Credit application:** Complete commercial account application. Net-30 terms.
4. **Delivery setup:** Establish delivery route and schedule. OrePac operates regular delivery routes along the I-5 corridor.
5. **Product catalog:** Request current product catalog with sheet goods specifications and pricing.

### Sherwin-Williams Industrial Account Opening

1. **Contact:** Call Sherwin-Williams industrial wood coatings sales (Pacific Northwest territory).
2. **Documentation:** Business license, EIN.
3. **Shop visit:** Schedule a finish room consultation. Their rep will assess equipment and recommend product systems.
4. **Account setup:** Commercial account with net-30 terms.
5. **Training:** Request spray technique training for the finish room team (Sherwin-Williams offers this as a value-add service).
