In Music

The ii-V-I is the most important chord progression in jazz and one of the most fundamental patterns in all of Western harmony. It works because each chord has a specific gravitational role: the ii chord (typically a minor seventh) creates gentle tension, the V chord (dominant seventh) creates maximum tension through its tritone interval, and the I chord (major or tonic) resolves everything back to home.

In the key of C major, this is Dm7 → G7 → Cmaj7. The bass moves down in fourths (D → G → C), which is the strongest root motion in tonal music. Every jazz standard leans heavily on this pattern — "Autumn Leaves," "All the Things You Are," and hundreds more are essentially chains of ii-V-I progressions in different keys.

Quincy Jones mastered this progression across decades of work — from his big band arrangements through Michael Jackson's Thriller. The ii-V-I gives music a sense of journey: you leave home, build tension, and arrive. It is the harmonic equivalent of storytelling's beginning-middle-end.

What makes it so powerful is voice leading. The third of the ii chord steps down to become the seventh of the V chord, which then resolves down by half-step to the third of the I chord. These tiny chromatic motions — sometimes just one fret or one piano key — create the feeling of inevitability. The resolution doesn't just happen; it must happen.

In the Forest Simulation

The ii-V-I maps directly to the agent foraging cycle: scan (ii — the agent surveys its environment, building potential), pursue (V — the agent locks onto a target with maximum energy expenditure), and consume (I — the agent reaches the resource and feeds, returning to rest state).

This three-phase envelope is modeled as a sinusoidal function where the period T scales with the agent's energy level. A high-energy agent cycles faster — scanning, pursuing, and consuming in rapid succession. A low-energy agent stretches the cycle, spending more time in the scan phase, conserving resources before committing to pursuit.

Rosetta Connections

Ecology

Predator-prey pursuit cycle: detect → chase → capture. The three phases map to metabolic states: resting scan, anaerobic burst, post-prandial recovery.

Infrastructure

Request lifecycle: DNS resolution (ii, finding the address) → TCP handshake (V, establishing connection) → data transfer (I, delivering the payload). Every network call is a ii-V-I.

Electronics

Charge-discharge-steady cycle in an RC circuit. The capacitor charges (building tension), reaches peak voltage (dominant), then settles to steady state (resolution).

The Math

VariableMeaning
activity(t)Agent's current behavioral intensity at time t, ranging from 0 (rest) to 1 (peak pursuit)
tCurrent simulation time (in frames or seconds)
TPeriod of the foraging cycle, varies inversely with energy level — higher energy = shorter T = faster cycling
2π/TAngular frequency — how quickly the agent moves through the scan-pursue-consume phases
sin()The sinusoidal function creates smooth transitions between phases rather than abrupt state changes, mimicking biological rhythm

The three phases of ii-V-I map to the sinusoid's shape: the rising portion (0 to π/2) is the scan/approach phase, the peak (π/2) is the dominant/pursuit moment, and the descent (π/2 to π) is the resolution/consumption. The second half of the cycle (where sin goes negative) represents the rest period before the next foraging bout.
