History & Origins

The Signal That Wouldn't Stop

History

From Larry Adams's 100-milliwatt AM experiment in December 1969 through 56 years of format evolution, survival crises, and power increases to the world's oldest continuously operating dance music station.

Education

Room 1204

Education

The Electronic Media curriculum at Nathan Hale High School: a four-course CTE sequence where teenagers learn production, programming, and on-air hosting to compete with and outperform commercial stations worldwide.

Technical Infrastructure

100 Milliwatts to Cougar Mountain

Technical

Nine documented power levels from 100 mW AM to 8,500 W ERP HD Radio. Antenna engineering, Cougar Mountain transmitter site, digital workflow evolution, and internet streaming since 1997.

Cultural Influence

The Tastemaker

Cultural

Billboard Dance Radio Airplay panel member. First non-commercial station on the panel. Lady Gaga on a high school stage in 2008. Café Chill syndicated nationally. 9+ specialty shows shaping electronic music culture.

Funding & Sustainability

The Architecture of Survival

Funding

87% self-funded through listener donations and underwriting. Post-CPB dissolution impact analysis: $175K annual loss plus satellite, royalties, and EAS infrastructure. Four survival crises in 56 years.
