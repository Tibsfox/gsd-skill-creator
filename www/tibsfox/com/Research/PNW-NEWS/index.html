Artemis II Flight Day 2 — Crew Prepares for Trans-Lunar Injection

Space NASA • April 2, 2026

The four-person crew of Orion Integrity completed proximity operations with the ICPS upper stage last night and is now in a 115 × 43,760 mile orbit around Earth. A perigee raise burn is scheduled this morning, followed by a crew rest period. The trans-lunar injection (TLI) burn — the six-minute firing that sends them to the Moon — is targeted for approximately 5 PM PDT today.

This will be the first time humans have left Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in December 1972 — 53 years and 4 months ago.

M4.6 Earthquake Rattles Boulder Creek, Santa Cruz Mountains

Seismic USGS • April 2, 2026 01:41 PDT

A magnitude 4.6 earthquake struck 1 km ESE of Boulder Creek at 1:41 AM, depth 10.85 km, on the San Andreas fault system. Over 25,000 people filed "Did You Feel It?" reports. Felt from Sacramento to Fresno. No damage or injuries. This is the largest event on the Loma Prieta fault segment since the M6.9 in 1989.

The segment has re-accumulated approximately 72% of the strain released by Loma Prieta, based on GPS geodetic slip rates of 33–37 mm/year over 36.5 years.

Everett Emergency Response Exercise Scheduled April 6

Emergency City of Everett • April 6, 2026

The City of Everett Emergency Management, partnering with Snohomish County Department of Emergency Management, will conduct a full-scale emergency response exercise at Everett Memorial Stadium on Monday, April 6, from 8 AM to 5 PM. The exercise will test multi-agency coordination and response capabilities.

This exercise is particularly relevant given the seismic hazard profile of the Puget Sound region: 85% probability of a M6.5+ deep earthquake, and 10–15% probability of a Cascadia M9.0, within the next 50 years.

Post-Frontal Rain Continues — 1.48" and Counting

Weather KPAE METAR • April 2, 2026

The atmospheric river that arrived during the Artemis II launch window continues to deliver rain across the Puget Sound. KPAE has recorded 0.97" on April 1 and 0.51" so far on April 2, with peak intensity of 0.22"/hr at 3 AM. Pressure has fallen from 30.00" to 29.73" over 48 hours. Temperatures hovering at 41°F with near-saturation (96% humidity).

This rain delivers approximately 40,000 gallons per acre — significant soil recharge heading into planting season. Soil temperatures remain around 45–50°F, good for cool-season crops.
