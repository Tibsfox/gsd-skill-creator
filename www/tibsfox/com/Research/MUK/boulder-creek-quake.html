Context: The San Andreas at Boulder Creek

Boulder Creek sits on the Santa Cruz Mountains segment of the San Andreas Fault, where the Pacific Plate grinds northwest past the North American Plate at roughly 46 mm/year. This segment last ruptured catastrophically in the 1989 Loma Prieta earthquake (M6.9), which collapsed the Cypress Structure on I-880, damaged the Bay Bridge, and interrupted the World Series at Candlestick Park.

The 2026 M4.6 event occurred at 10.85 km depth with strike-slip + reverse motion — the same mechanism as Loma Prieta. At this magnitude, no structural damage is expected, but it serves as a reminder that this fault segment remains active and accumulating strain.

Concurrent Events

This earthquake occurred during Artemis II Flight Day 2, with the Orion spacecraft Integrity in a 115 × 43,760 mile orbit around Earth. While the crew prepared for trans-lunar injection, the ground beneath their former training facilities shifted. The space between the Moon and the Earth includes the fault lines below our feet.
