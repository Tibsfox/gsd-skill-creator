The rain arrived at KPAE around 10 AM on April 1 as predicted in the Day 1 sweeps. Heaviest precipitation fell 2–4 PM (0.10–0.12"/hr) during the approach of the warm front. Rain eased during the launch window (6–7 PM) then resumed overnight, peaking at 0.22"/hr at 3 AM April 2. This is a classic Pacific frontal passage — pre-frontal rain, brief clearing at the front, then post-frontal showers.

Weather Pattern Analysis

Pressure Trace

Barometric pressure fell steadily from 30.00" (1015.9 hPa) at midnight March 31 to 29.41" (996.2 hPa) by 6 PM April 1 — a drop of 0.59" (20 hPa) in 42 hours. This is a moderate deepening rate consistent with a Pacific frontal system, not a bomb cyclone but enough to drive sustained rain and gusty south winds.

Wind Shift

March 31 was dominated by light northerlies (post-high-pressure clearing). April 1 brought the classic SE→S→SW wind progression of a frontal passage: SE 8-14 kt during the warm sector, shifting S 15-18 G32 at the frontal boundary (7 PM), then SW 5-10 post-frontal. This progression is textbook PNW meteorology.

Temperature & Dewpoint

March 31 peaked at 56°F (13.3°C) with dewpoint 26°F — dry northerly flow. April 1 barely reached 47°F (8.3°C) with dewpoint 45°F — the marine air mass had fully replaced continental air. Dewpoint spread collapsed from 30°F to 2°F, indicating near-saturation.

Gardening & Agriculture Notes

This 1.48" rain event delivered approximately 40,000 gallons per acre — significant soil recharge after a dry late March. Soil temperatures in the Everett area are typically 45–50°F in early April, warm enough for cool-season crops (lettuce, peas, radishes, kale) but still below the 60°F threshold for warm-season planting (tomatoes, peppers, squash). Last frost date for Everett: typically April 15–25. A more comprehensive planting guide is coming.
