Artemis II Mission Profile

Artemis II will be the first crewed mission beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in 1972. The crew of four will fly a free-return trajectory — a figure-eight path that loops behind the Moon and returns to Earth without requiring an engine burn for the return trip.

Key mission events:

Trans-Lunar Injection (TLI) — At T+1h 57m, the ICPS upper stage fires for ~18 minutes, accelerating Orion from 7.8 km/s to ~10.8 km/s, sending it toward the Moon.

Lunar Closest Approach — At roughly T+4d, Orion passes ~8,900 km behind the far side of the Moon. The Moon's gravity bends the trajectory back toward Earth.

Entry Interface (EI) — At ~T+10d, Orion hits Earth's atmosphere at 11.0 km/s (Mach 32). The heat shield endures temperatures over 2,760 C.

The Vis-Viva Equation

The velocity at any point on a Keplerian orbit is: v = sqrt(GM * (2/r - 1/a)), where r is the current distance from the central body, a is the semi-major axis, and GM is the gravitational parameter. This simulation uses a simplified patched-conic approximation: Earth dominates near Earth, the Moon dominates near the Moon.
