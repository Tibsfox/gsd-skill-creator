Beam Physics

A circular aperture produces a diffraction-limited beam with half-angle divergence: θ = 1.22 λ / D, where λ is wavelength and D is aperture diameter. Shorter wavelengths and larger apertures produce tighter beams.

At distance L, the beam footprint diameter is approximately 2L tan(θ) ≈ 2.44 λ L / D. The power per unit area (irradiance) drops as the square of the footprint radius.

Shannon-Hartley capacity: C = B log2(1 + S/N), where B is bandwidth and S/N is signal-to-noise ratio. Laser links achieve enormous S/N ratios because all the power is concentrated into a tiny spot, enabling data rates of 200+ Mbps from lunar distance.

Artemis II will use both O2O (Optical to Orion) laser communication at 1550 nm and traditional S-band radio for redundancy. The laser link achieves 100x the data rate with a telescope 30x smaller than the radio dish.
