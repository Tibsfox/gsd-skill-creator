A region defined by water needs transit that crosses it. Light rail, commuter rail, buses, and ferries weave together into a multimodal network that connects islands, peninsulas, and cities across the Puget Sound basin.

Sound Transit Link Light Rail

1 Line (Blue) — Lynnwood to Angle Lake

Route

Lynnwood City Center → Mountlake Terrace → Shoreline → Northgate → Roosevelt → U District → University of Washington → Capitol Hill → Westlake (downtown) → Pioneer Square → International District → Stadium → SODO → Beacon Hill → Mount Baker → Columbia City → Othello → Rainier Beach → Tukwila International Blvd → Sea-Tac Airport → Angle Lake

Travel time: ~45 min Lynnwood to Sea-Tac · Frequency: 8 min peak, 10 min off-peak

Fare: $2.25-3.50 depending on distance · ORCA card accepted

The 1 Line is the backbone of regional transit, connecting the northern suburbs through downtown Seattle to the airport. It opened in stages: the initial segment from Westlake to Sea-Tac in 2009, the University Link extension in 2016, Northgate in 2021, and the Lynnwood extension in 2024. Each extension dramatically increased ridership and changed commuting patterns.

From Mukilteo: Community Transit Route 113 connects the Mukilteo ferry terminal to Lynnwood City Center station. Transfer to the 1 Line for destinations throughout Seattle and south to the airport.

2 Line (Red) — Cross-Lake Service Opened Mar 28, 2026

Route

Lynnwood City Center → [shared with 1 Line to Judkins Park] → Mercer Island → South Bellevue → East Main → Bellevue Downtown → Wilburton → Spring District → Bel-Red → Overlake Village → Downtown Redmond

Travel time: ~55 min Lynnwood to Redmond · 25 stations, 33 miles total

The 2 Line is historic: the first light rail crossing of Lake Washington. The line runs through the I-90 tunnel under Mercer Island, connecting Seattle's east side to the regional rail network for the first time. Microsoft's Redmond campus, Bellevue's growing downtown, and the Spring District development are all directly served.

The project was decades in the making. Approved as part of Sound Transit 2 in 2008, the $3.6 billion East Link extension required tunneling through downtown Bellevue, building a new floating bridge rail crossing (the first of its kind), and navigating complex environmental and property negotiations. It opened March 28, 2026, just days before the Artemis II launch.

Deep dive: Light rail history covers the full story from Forward Thrust (1968) to the 2 Line opening, including the political battles, engineering challenges, and ridership projections.

Sounder Commuter Rail

N Line — Everett to Seattle (Waterfront Route)

Route & Schedule

Everett → Mukilteo → Edmonds → King Street Station (Seattle)

Travel time: ~60 min Everett to Seattle

Schedule: Limited — 2 AM southbound trains, 2 PM northbound trains (weekdays only)

Mukilteo station: Adjacent to the ferry terminal — one of the region's best multimodal connections (train + ferry + bus)

The Sounder N Line runs along the BNSF Railway tracks directly on the Puget Sound waterfront, offering some of the most scenic commuter rail views in the country. The tracks hug the shoreline from Everett through Mukilteo and Edmonds, passing through the same coastal landscape visible from the ferry crossings and waterfront parks.

Ridership on the N Line has been limited since its 2003 launch, primarily due to the restricted schedule (peak hours only) and competition with express buses on I-5. Sound Transit has studied expanding service but faces challenges with BNSF track sharing and landslide-prone sections of the coastal route between Edmonds and Seattle.

King County Metro (Seattle & King County)

All Link light rail stations have King County Metro bus connections. Once you reach the Link system via Community Transit Route 113 from Mukilteo, the entire Metro network is accessible with a single transfer.

Key Metro services:

  • RapidRide A — Federal Way to Tukwila (connects to Link)
  • RapidRide C — West Seattle to downtown
  • RapidRide D — Ballard to downtown (connects to Shilshole Bay Marina, boating guide)
  • RapidRide E — Aurora Village to downtown (connects to Swift Blue)
  • RapidRide H — Delridge/White Center to downtown

Future Expansion

Everett Link Extension (Target: 2037)

Sound Transit 3 (approved 2016) includes extending Link light rail from Lynnwood north to Everett Station, with stations in the Paine Field area. This will bring light rail directly to the Boeing factory area and dramatically improve transit access to Mukilteo. The Paine Field station will be less than 3 miles from the ferry terminal.

Stride Bus Rapid Transit (2026-2028)

S1 Line: I-405 BRT from Lynnwood to Bellevue to Burien. 37 miles, 12 stations. Will connect with Link at Lynnwood and Bellevue.

S3 Line: SR-522 BRT from Shoreline to Bothell to Woodinville. Connects with Link at Shoreline.

Community Transit Swift Expansion

Additional Swift BRT lines planned for Snohomish County corridors. The Swift network is designed to provide frequent, reliable bus service on major corridors while feeding riders to Link light rail stations.
