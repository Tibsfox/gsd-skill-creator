Timeline

  • 1969 Founded in December as AM 1210 at 100 milliwatts, broadcasting from Nathan Hale High School in northeast Seattle.
  • 1971 Moved to FM in January at 10 watts, transmitting from Wedgwood Elementary School.
  • 1972 Power increase to 320 watts.
  • 1974 Power increase to 1,500 watts.
  • 1981 Power increase to 3,000 watts. Format shifts from light rock/pop toward R&B and urban contemporary.
  • 1983 "C89" branding begins. Jack Straw Foundation initiates attempt to acquire the station license from Seattle Public Schools.
  • 1988 Jack Straw Foundation takeover effort defeated after five-year campaign. Station remains under school district control.
  • 1991 Major power increase to 30,000 watts, dramatically expanding coverage across the Puget Sound region.
  • 1997 Internet streaming launches, making C89.5 one of the earliest radio stations with an online presence.
  • 2002 Transmitter relocated to Cougar Mountain for improved signal coverage.
  • 2003 Village Voice names KNHC "best high school radio station in America."
  • 2004 Added to Billboard's Dance Radio Airplay panel, becoming the first non-commercial station on the panel.
  • 2009 New digital studios installed at Nathan Hale High School.
  • 2015 Becomes eligible for Corporation for Public Broadcasting Community Service Grants.

Format History

KNHC has undergone a remarkable format evolution over its five decades. The station began in 1969 as a modest AM carrier-current operation playing light rock and pop music. Through the 1970s, student DJs shaped programming around the music they were listening to, giving the station an organic, youth-driven identity.

The pivot came in 1981 when format shifted toward R&B and urban contemporary. By the mid-1980s, as electronic dance music began emerging, student programmers gravitated toward house, techno, and freestyle. The "C89" brand crystallized in 1983 around this dance-forward identity.

Today C89.5 is recognized as the world's oldest dance music radio station. The format is programmed entirely by high school students enrolled in Nathan Hale's Mass Communications magnet school program. The station's HD sub-channels extend the mission: HD2 "Save The Wave" preserves new wave and synthpop, while HD3 "Cafe Chill" offers downtempo and ambient electronic music.

Mass Communications Program

Nathan Hale High School's Mass Communications magnet program is the educational backbone of C89.5. Students learn radio production, audio engineering, on-air presentation, music programming, and broadcast operations. The program produces working professionals: graduates have gone on to careers at commercial radio stations, record labels, and in live event production across the Pacific Northwest and beyond.

Connection to PNW Research

C89.5 represents a unique intersection of education and broadcast infrastructure in the Seattle radio landscape. While the Seattle 360 Engine traces music history through artists and venues, KNHC demonstrates how the next generation of the PNW music community learns its craft. The station's dance/EDM format fills a gap in the regional dial, connecting to the electronic music tradition that runs from early rave culture through the modern festival circuit. Its 200,000 weekly listeners make it one of the most successful non-commercial stations in the country, and its Billboard panel membership ensures Seattle's dance music pulse reaches national industry metrics.
