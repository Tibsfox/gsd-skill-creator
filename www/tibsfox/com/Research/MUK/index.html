Scoring Methodology

Temperature MAE

Accuracy

Mean absolute error in °C between predicted and actual temperature. Lower is better. Scored over rolling 30-day window. Accounts for microclimate variation between airport and waterfront.

Wind Accuracy

Accuracy

Combined direction error (degrees) and speed ratio. Marine wind channeling up Possession Sound creates local patterns that regional models often miss.

Precipitation F1

Accuracy

F1 score for binary rain prediction (did it rain when they said it would?). Puget Sound Convergence Zone makes this the hardest metric — rain can fall on Mukilteo while Everett stays dry.

Fog Prediction

Accuracy

Dewpoint spread < 2.5°C predicts fog. Score: did fog actually form? Radiation fog on calm clear nights, advection fog off Possession Sound. Binary classification F1.

Confidence Calibration

Accuracy

Brier score — when they say 80% chance of rain, does it rain 80% of the time? Measures whether probability forecasts are well-calibrated, not just whether they're right.

Timing Accuracy

Accuracy

Hours off on front arrival. When the forecast says rain starts at 2 PM, does it? Convergence Zone events are notoriously hard to time — this metric reveals which models handle them.

Microclimate Features

Puget Sound Convergence Zone

Weather

When marine air splits around the Olympic Mountains, the two streams converge over north King County / south Snohomish County. This creates a narrow band of heavy precipitation that can dump rain on Mukilteo while Everett stays dry. The PSCZ is the primary source of forecast error for this location.

Possession Sound Marine Layer

Weather

Cool marine air funnels up Possession Sound from the Strait of Juan de Fuca. Creates morning fog, moderates temperatures, and channels wind differently than inland stations report. KPAE sits at the airport, 2 miles from the waterfront — the microclimate differs.

Cascade Rain Shadow Edge

Weather

Mukilteo sits at the western edge of the Cascade rain shadow transition. Annual precipitation drops sharply east of the Cascades. The foothills to the east receive more orographic lift precipitation than the waterfront. Elevation matters at every scale.

Tidal Influence on Air Temperature

Tide Weather

Large tidal exchanges in Possession Sound affect nearshore air temperature and humidity. Low tide exposes mudflats that warm differently than open water. The 12.4-hour tidal cycle overlays the 24-hour diurnal cycle, creating beat frequencies in temperature and fog formation.

Waterfront Parks & Beaches (North to South)

Puget Sound shoreline from Kayak Point south through Mukilteo and Edmonds to Seattle. Tides affect beach access, fishing, and boat launching at all locations. Full guide with history, access, and ecology →

Kayak Point County Park

Tide-dependent 15mi N of Mukilteo

300-foot fishing pier, boat launch, beach access. Best crabbing pier in Snohomish County. Low tide exposes extensive tidal flats. Camping available.

Mukilteo Lighthouse Park

Tide-dependent Mukilteo

Historic lighthouse (1906), accessible beach, picnic area, boat launch. Adjacent to the new Mukilteo ferry terminal fishing pier. Coho and Pink salmon when in season.

Picnic Point Park

Tide-dependent 3mi S of Mukilteo

Snohomish County park with 700 feet of shoreline, beach access at low tide, trail through ravine to the water. Quiet, less crowded than Mukilteo.

Meadowdale Beach Park

Tide-dependent 5mi S of Mukilteo

1.25-mile trail through old-growth forest to beach. Loons Creek estuary. Best low-tide beachcombing south of Mukilteo. No vehicle access to beach.

Edmonds Waterfront

Tide-dependent Edmonds

Fishing pier (219 Railroad Ave), Brackett's Landing (underwater dive park), marina, beach. Marine sanctuary protects tidal zone. Ferry terminal adjacent.

Carkeek Park

Tide-dependent North Seattle

Beach access via pedestrian bridge over railroad tracks. Piper's Creek salmon spawning (Nov-Dec). Tide pools at low tide. 220 acres of forest trails.

Golden Gardens Park

Tide-dependent Ballard, Seattle

Largest public beach in north Seattle. Boat launch, fire pits, off-leash dog area. Views of Olympic Mountains. Popular sunset spot.

Port of Everett Boat Launch

Launch 10th St & W Marine View Dr

13-lane launch, direct access to North Puget Sound. $15/day includes trailer parking. Gateway to Possession Sound crabbing, salmon fishing, and Whidbey Island waters.

Mukilteo Lighthouse Boat Launch

Launch Mukilteo

Single-lane ramp at Lighthouse Park. Free. Tide-dependent — check water level before launching. Direct access to Possession Sound.

Edmonds Marina

Launch Edmonds

Public boat launch at Port of Edmonds. Access to central Puget Sound. Close to Edmonds-Kingston ferry route — watch for ferry traffic.

Shilshole Bay Marina

Launch Ballard, Seattle

Port of Seattle marina with boat launch. Access to Puget Sound via Shilshole Bay. Gateway to the Ship Canal and Ballard Locks for Lake Washington access.

Ballard Locks & Lake Washington

The Hiram M. Chittenden Locks connect Puget Sound (salt water) to Lake Washington (fresh water) via the Ship Canal. The locks handle more boat traffic than any other lock in the US. Full boating guide →

Hiram M. Chittenden Locks (Ballard Locks)

Locks 3015 NW 54th St, Seattle

Open daily 7 AM – 9 PM. Free passage for all vessels (kayaks to 760-foot ships). Water level drops up to 26 feet. 10-15 minute transit. Fish ladder for salmon viewing (Jun-Nov). Carl S. English Jr. Botanical Garden on grounds. Over 1 million visitors/year.

Crabbing & Shrimping

Salt Water

Possession Sound and Port Gardner are productive Dungeness crab grounds accessible from the Everett boat launch. WDFW sets seasonal openings (typically summer/fall). Shrimp pots in deeper water (100-300 ft).

Safety & Navigation

Boating

NOAA Chart 18443 (Puget Sound, northern part). Watch for ferry traffic on Mukilteo-Clinton and Edmonds-Kingston routes. Possession Sound has commercial shipping traffic to/from Port of Everett. Tidal currents strongest at Admiralty Inlet and Deception Pass.

Light Rail & Transit

Sound Transit Link light rail connects Lynnwood to Seattle to Bellevue to Redmond. Community Transit buses connect Mukilteo/Everett to Lynnwood station. Full transit guide with trip planning →

Sound Transit 1 Line

Light Rail

Lynnwood City Center → Northgate → U District → Capitol Hill → Westlake (downtown) → Pioneer Square → Stadium → SODO → Rainier Beach → Tukwila → Sea-Tac Airport → Angle Lake. Frequent service, ~45 min Lynnwood to Sea-Tac.

Sound Transit 2 Line (Crosslake)

Light Rail Opened Mar 28

Lynnwood → Northgate → U District → Capitol Hill → Judkins Park → Mercer Island → Bellevue Downtown → Spring District → Bel-Red → Overlake → Downtown Redmond. 25 stations, 33 miles. First light rail across Lake Washington.

Sounder N Line (Commuter Rail)

Rail

Everett → Mukilteo → Edmonds → King Street Station (Seattle). Waterfront route along Puget Sound. Peak hours only (AM southbound, PM northbound). ~60 min Everett to Seattle.

Bus Connections to Light Rail

Bus

Community Transit serves Mukilteo, Everett, Lynnwood. Route 113 connects Mukilteo ferry terminal to Lynnwood Transit Center (1 Line station). Swift Blue Line (BRT) connects Everett to Shoreline/Aurora.
