## Key Researchers & Research Sites

### Andrews Forest Long-Term Ecological Research (LTER)
- Located in the Western Cascades of Oregon (Blue River Ranger District)
- Operated by Oregon State University and USDA Forest Service
- Continuous ecological research since 1948
- Key mycorrhizal studies: Luoma (1988), Smith et al. (2000), Amaranthus et al. (1994)
- Source of foundational data on old-growth forest ecology, fungal diversity, logging legacy effects

### North Cascades National Park Complex
- 504,781 acres of protected wilderness
- 1,630 documented vascular plant species (GOV-04)
- Estimated 236,000 acres of old-growth forest
- Non-vascular plants and fungi could more than double species count
- Site of grizzly bear reintroduction program (NPS/USFWS EIS, April 2024)
- Greatest concentration of active alpine glaciers in lower 48 states

### Northwest Forest Plan Region
- Federal lands management framework since 1994
- Focused on northern spotted owl but covers 1,000+ species
- Adaptive management component not fully implemented
- Reserve network gaps along elevation and latitudinal gradients

## Source Quality Notes

1. **Government agency data (GOV-01 through GOV-09):** Highest reliability. Based on systematic field inventories, long-term monitoring, and regulatory authority. Use as primary source for all quantitative claims.

2. **Peer-reviewed research (PR-01 through PR-07):** High reliability. Published in journals with editorial review. Provides specific quantitative data and analytical frameworks not available in agency reports.

3. **Professional organizations (CON-01 through CON-04):** Good reliability for regional context and conservation status. Data often synthesized from agency and academic sources. Use for conservation program descriptions and regional assessments.

4. **Temporal currency:** All primary sources published within the last 15 years where possible. Foundational studies (Luoma 1988, Amaranthus 1994) are exceptions — they remain the benchmark datasets for Cascade mycorrhizal ecology and have not been superseded.
