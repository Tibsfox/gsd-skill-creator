Indigenous Broadcasting PNW

Sovereign Signals · KWSO · KYNR · Daybreak Star · Tribal Airwaves
Overview Station Profiles Language & Culture Infrastructure Programming Sovereignty Mission Pack
There are signals moving through the air over the Pacific Northwest right now that most people never hear. They carry birthday greetings and wildfire alerts, powwow schedules and obituaries, Ichishkíin phrases and salmon-run news. These are tribal radio signals — sovereign frequencies operating outside the mainstream media ecosystem, built by and for nations whose right to communicate in their own voice predates every FCC license by ten thousand years.

Research Modules

M1: Station Profiles

Stations Track A · Sovereign infrastructure

Documentary profiles of KWSO 91.9 FM (Confederated Tribes of Warm Springs), KYNR 1490 AM (Confederated Tribes and Bands of the Yakama Nation), and Daybreak Star Radio (United Indians of All Tribes Foundation). Technical specifications, founding histories, governance structures, staff, and tribal government integration.

M2: Language & Cultural Transmission

Language Track B · Revitalization

Multilingual broadcasting at KWSO (Ichishkíin Sínwit, Kiksht, Northern Paiute). Yakama language sovereignty through KYNR. Daybreak Star Radio's pan-Indigenous music curation. Oral tradition preservation and archival capacity. AAPB historical holdings.

M3: Infrastructure, Funding & Crisis

Infrastructure Track A · CPB funding crisis

2025 CPB rescission impact on KWSO (40% budget at risk). KYNR 2018 burglary case study. Emergency Alert System function. Digital divide on reservation lands. Equipment vulnerability patterns. Native Public Media network-wide analysis (57 stations, 20 states).

M4: Programming Ecosystem & Networks

Programming Track B · Syndication

Native Voice One / Koahnic Broadcast Corporation syndication architecture. National Native News (400+ stations). Native America Calling. Nation-specific local production: birthday announcements, obituaries, powwow schedules, language programming. Urban vs. reservation broadcasting models.

M5: Sovereignty, Governance & Futures

Sovereignty Synthesis · UNDRIP framework

UNDRIP Article 31 applied to tribal broadcasting. FCC licensing paradox (tribe-licensed vs. nonprofit). 1855 Treaty with the Tribes of Middle Oregon. Tribal council as board of directors. OCAP principles. Digital expansion strategies. Sustainability pathways and risk analysis.
