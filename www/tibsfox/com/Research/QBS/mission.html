Mission Package

Quantum sensing is where single-molecule biophysics was in the early 1990s. It took about 15 years of scientific and engineering development to go from detecting individual pentacene molecules to super-resolution microscopy. We are partway through this trajectory. — Peter Maurer

Mission Objectives

  1. Map the measurement frontier — understand where quantum precision meets biological complexity across six research domains
  2. Connect Dartmouth interdisciplinary engineering with Max Planck focused fundamental research as complementary institutional models
  3. Assess technology readiness from TRL 2 (protein qubits) through TRL 7+ (Josephson amplifiers in astronomy) with clear bottleneck identification
  4. Extract cross-domain patterns — signal-to-noise as universal principle, modularity as scaling strategy, measurement as the foundation of understanding

DIY Sessions

Session 1: Blackbody to Quantized Energy

Visual demonstration of blackbody radiation using candle, incandescent, and LED light sources with a DVD diffraction grating. Measure spectral peaks, compare to Planck’s law. Model LC oscillator quantization. ~$50, 2 hours.

Session 2: Biological Age Calculator

Build a simplified biological age estimator using public DNA methylation datasets. Implement Horvath clock CpG sites, extend with metabolomic features. Visualize UMAP of metabolite-disease associations. Python/R, 3 hours.

Session 3: Metamaterial Design with ML

Design and optimize 2D lattice structures for target Poisson’s ratio using FEA and scikit-learn. Parametric unit cells, inverse design network, transfer learning. Python + FEA, 4 hours.
