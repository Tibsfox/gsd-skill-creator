Silicon to Evidence

Storage Silicon · RAID/HBA · Ceph · Data Recovery · Digital Forensics · Optical Inspection
Overview Silicon Arrays Ceph Recovery Forensics Optical Mission Pack
Every enterprise storage incident is, at bottom, a physics problem. Somewhere in the space between a spinning platter or a NAND flash cell and the bit pattern a forensic examiner needs in court, something went wrong. The path from silicon to evidence runs through the same sequence: understand the chip, understand the array, understand the distributed system, understand the failure modes, and only then — with precision, with chain of custody intact, with write-blocking in place — begin recovery. Silicon to evidence is not a miracle. It is applied architecture.

Research Modules

M1: Storage Silicon Substrate

Silicon Track A · Controller families

Samsung NVMe/SAS enterprise SSDs, Philips/NXP legacy controllers, Broadcom MegaRAID/HBA Tri-Mode SerDes, AMD/Xilinx FPGA computational storage. Die architectures, firmware paths, known forensic failure modes per family.

M2: Storage Array Architecture

Array Track A · RAID topology

RAID levels 0/1/5/6/10/50/60/5EE and JBOD topology. Stripe geometry, parity distribution, write-hole vulnerabilities, MegaRAID metadata formats, DDF standard, HBA passthrough vs. IT-mode comparison for Ceph OSD.

M3: Ceph Distributed Storage

Distributed Track A · Object store

Ceph RADOS architecture, BlueStore on-disk format, CRUSH placement algorithm, erasure coding profiles, stretch cluster operations, OSD journal/WAL/DB separation, and forensic challenges unique to distributed object stores.

M4: Data Recovery Engineering

Recovery Track B · Failure modes

15+ failure mode taxonomy across controller, firmware, NAND, and protocol layers. Professional toolchain: Ace Lab PC-3000, VNR, FE Flash-Extractor, IR6500 BGA rework. NVMe chip-off procedure, RAID superblock reconstruction.

M5: Digital Forensics & Legal Chain

Forensics Track B · Evidence standards

NIST SP 800-86 methodology, write-blocking hardware and procedures, forensic imaging standards, hash verification, chain of custody documentation, court-admissible reporting, Ceph-specific forensic procedures.

M6: Optical Imaging & VLSI Inspection

Optical Track B · Evidence documentation

Pentax/Ricoh industrial macro lenses for die-level documentation, Bosch AOI semiconductor inspection, brightfield vs. darkfield techniques, EXIF metadata forensics, BGA bond inspection, burn trace documentation.
