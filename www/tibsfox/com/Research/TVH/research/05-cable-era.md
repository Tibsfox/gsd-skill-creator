# Cable Era & Streaming -- MTV, HBO, YouTube & Cord-Cutting

## CATV Origins (1948)

Community Antenna Television began in Pennsylvania mountain towns where terrain blocked Philadelphia's broadcast signals. John Walson of Mahanoy City is widely credited with the first commercial CATV system (1948): a large antenna on a nearby mountaintop, connected to homes via coaxial cable.

## HBO and the Satellite Revolution (1975)

Home Box Office launched in 1972 as a cable service in Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania. On September 30, 1975, HBO began satellite distribution via SATCOM 1, fundamentally changing cable television. Any cable system with a satellite dish could now receive HBO's feed, creating the first nationally distributed cable network.

## The Cable Explosion (1980-1990)

Cable grew from 16 million to 50 million subscribing households:
- **CNN** (June 1, 1980): First 24-hour news network
- **ESPN** (September 7, 1979): All-sports network
- **Nickelodeon** (April 1, 1979): Children's programming
- **MTV** (August 1, 1981): "Video Killed the Radio Star" -- music television

MTV's impact was cultural as much as commercial. It created a new visual language for television, influenced fashion, music, and youth culture globally, and proved that narrow-format programming could command massive audiences.

## Regulatory Battles

- **1984 Cable Communications Policy Act:** Deregulated cable pricing, leading to rapid expansion but also price increases
- **1992 Cable Television Consumer Protection Act:** Re-regulated cable, mandated retransmission consent
- **1996 Telecommunications Act:** Further deregulated ownership limits, enabled media consolidation

## Peak Cable and Cord-Cutting

Cable subscriptions peaked in 2000 at approximately 68.5 million US households. The decline began slowly but accelerated:

| Year | Cable Subs (M) | Streaming Milestone |
|---|---|---|
| 2000 | 68.5 | Peak cable |
| 2005 | 65.0 | YouTube launches |
| 2007 | 63.0 | Netflix streaming begins |
| 2010 | 59.0 | "Cord-cutting" enters vocabulary |
| 2015 | 50.0 | Netflix has 75M global subs |
| 2020 | 38.0 | COVID accelerates streaming |
| 2023 | 33.0 | 5M households cut cable in single year |
| 2025 | ~28.0 | Streaming surpasses cable viewing |
