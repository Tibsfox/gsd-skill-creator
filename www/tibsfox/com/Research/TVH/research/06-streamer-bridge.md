# Streamer Bridge -- LPTV, ATSC 3.0 & Community Voice

## The Convergence Thesis

The Streamer Bridge is the central innovation this mission investigates: a pathway for internet-native creators to acquire LPTV licenses and use ATSC 3.0's IP-native architecture to serve terrestrial audiences. The signal returns to the air.

## PEG Access Television

Public, Educational, and Government (PEG) access television was mandated by the FCC in 1969 and codified in the 1984 Cable Act. At peak, 2,500 PEG access operations managed over 3,000 cable channels. As of 2026, approximately 1,600 remain active.

PEG channels represent untapped infrastructure:
- Studio facilities in most US markets
- Community relationships and training programs
- First Amendment protections for public access
- Declining cable franchise fee funding base

## LPTV as Community Platform

LPTV's original mission was community broadcasting. With 5,300+ licensed stations, the LPTV ecosystem serves:
- Ethnic and language-specific communities
- Religious broadcasters
- Local news in rural markets
- Educational institutions

The December 2025 rule changes make LPTV more accessible to new entrants.

## ATSC 3.0 Hybrid Architecture

ATSC 3.0's IP-native backbone enables a hybrid delivery model:

```
INTERNET PRODUCER
     |
     v
ATSC 3.0 ENCODER (IP packets)
     |
     +---> BROADBAND (internet delivery)
     |
     +---> LPTV TRANSMITTER (over-the-air delivery)
     |
     +---> 5G BROADCAST (cellular delivery)
```

The same content reaches audiences via whichever path is available: internet, antenna, or cellular.

## The Streamer-to-Terrestrial Pathway

A step-by-step operational model:

1. **Build audience online:** Establish streaming presence (YouTube, Twitch, etc.)
2. **Spectrum search:** Identify available LPTV channels in home market
3. **FCC Form 2100:** Apply for construction permit
4. **Equipment:** ATSC 3.0 encoder (~$15K-50K), transmitter (~$5K-25K), antenna
5. **Construction:** Install at suitable tower site or rooftop
6. **Testing:** Verify coverage area and signal quality
7. **License grant:** Apply for station license after successful construction
8. **Hybrid operation:** Stream internet content AND broadcast OTA simultaneously
9. **Class A upgrade:** If qualifying criteria met, apply for primary spectrum protection

## Who This Serves

The Streamer Bridge serves audiences that broadband economics have left behind:
- Rural households beyond cable/fiber reach
- Low-income households where $60+/month streaming bundles are unaffordable
- Elderly viewers who use antennas by preference
- Emergency communication: OTA broadcasting works when internet goes down

A single creator with a streaming audience and a Class A LPTV license can reach every home with an antenna in a 30-mile radius.
