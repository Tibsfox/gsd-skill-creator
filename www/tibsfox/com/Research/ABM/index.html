Overview Paradigm Neuroscience Prodigies 2e Environments Mission Pack
Neurodivergent minds are not deficient versions of neurotypical minds. They are specialized architectures that, in the right context, route perception and cognition through pathways that standard configurations cannot access. The autistic brain that struggles with social inference is precisely the brain that achieves absolute pitch at rates ninety times higher than the general population.

Research Modules

M1: The Paradigm

Paradigm Track A · History

From deficit to difference: Judy Singer's 1998 concept, the disability rights movement, diagnostic evolution DSM-III through DSM-5, medical model vs. social model, and where the paradigm shift remains contested.

M2: The Neuroscience

Neuroscience Track A · Cognitive Architecture

Divergent thinking in ADHD, absolute pitch-autism connection (5-11% vs <0.01%), local hyperconnectivity in savant syndrome, dyslexia's explorative bias, and the planum temporale asymmetry.

M3: The Prodigies

Prodigies Track B · Portraits

Documented portraits: Nash, Tesla, Turing, Einstein, da Vinci, Mozart, van Gogh, Warhol, Boyle, Eilish, Barnett. Epistemic framework for retrodiagnosis applied throughout.

M4: Twice-Exceptional (2e)

2e Track B · Education

Giftedness meets neurodivergence: three identification failure modes, masking and burnout dynamics, asynchronous development, SENG framework, and evidence-based support systems.

M5: Enabling Environments

Environments Synthesis · Systems

Cognitive profile x domain alignment x environmental fit = expressed genius. UDL, strengths-based assessment, workplace design, and the conditions that unlock vs. suppress neurodivergent potential.
