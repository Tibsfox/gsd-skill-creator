Research Modules

M1 The Paradigm History of neurodiversity from Judy Singer (1998) through today. Medical model vs. social model. Diagnostic evolution DSM-III → DSM-5.
M2 The Neuroscience Cognitive architecture of creativity. ADHD divergent thinking. Absolute pitch in autism (5–11% vs <0.01%). Savant hyperconnectivity theory. Dyslexia explorative bias.
M3 The Prodigies Documented portraits: Nash, Tesla, Turing, Einstein, da Vinci, Mozart, van Gogh, Warhol, Andersen, Dickinson, Grandin, Barnett, and more. Epistemic framework applied throughout.
M4 Twice-Exceptional (2e) Giftedness meets neurodivergence. Three identification failure modes. Masking and burnout. Adult 2e experience. SENG framework. Evidence-based support systems.
M5 Enabling Environments Synthesis: cognitive profile × domain alignment × environmental fit. UDL, strengths-based assessment, workplace design. Conditions that unlock vs. suppress neurodivergent genius.
