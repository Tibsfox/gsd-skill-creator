# The Neuroscience: Cognitive Architecture of Creativity

## Divergent Thinking and ADHD

A 2024 peer-reviewed study (Pasarin-Lavin et al., *Applied Neuropsychology*) tested 181 secondary school students. Students with ADHD significantly outperformed neurotypical peers on Verbal Creativity, particularly Originality. Research in *Cognitive Psychology* shows ADHD individuals demonstrate a more intuitive cognitive style -- unconsciously comparing new situations against past experiences for rapid, non-linear responses.

ADHD-linked traits significantly correlated with entrepreneurship (Logan, 2009; Verheul et al., 2016).

## Absolute Pitch, Autism, and Local Hyperconnectivity

Absolute pitch (AP) -- identifying musical tones without external reference -- occurs in fewer than 0.01% of the general population. In autistic populations, prevalence estimates range from **5% to 11%** (PMC, 2024), making it 500-1,100 times more common.

A PLOS ONE study (Dohn et al., 2012) found significantly higher autism-spectrum quotient scores in AP musicians (r = .46, p = .003). The neuroscience involves *local hyperconnectivity*: AP musicians show enhanced bilateral superior temporal lobe connectivity. The "veridical mapping" theory proposes that the same neurocognitive mechanism underlies AP, synesthesia, and hyperlexia.

## Autism and Creativity

A 2021 meta-analysis (Baron-Cohen lab) found autistic individuals inhibited in fluency and flexibility but displaying **high levels of detail and originality**. Verbal originality in autistic individuals exceeds neurotypical norms.

Research on neurodiverse pairs (PMC, 2023) found that mixed autistic/non-autistic pairings produced more innovative designs than single-neurotype pairs -- among the first empirical evidence that neurological diversity enhances collective innovation.

## Savant Syndrome

Savant syndrome occurs in approximately 10% of the autistic population. The most supported model involves altered local/global brain connectivity -- disruption of long-range connectivity produces heightened specialization in specific perceptual domains.

## Dyslexia: The Explorative Bias

A 2022 Cambridge University study (Taylor et al.) proposed that dyslexia represents an evolutionary *explorative bias* -- a cognitive style specialized for discovery and exploration. Professionals with dyslexia were better at identifying black holes from noisy astrophysical data (Schneps, Harvard-Smithsonian).

**Important caveat:** A 2016 meta-analysis of 36 studies found "little consistent evidence" for spatial advantages in dyslexia overall. A 2018 meta-analysis found higher *variance* -- a subset shows spatial strengths, but it is not universal.
