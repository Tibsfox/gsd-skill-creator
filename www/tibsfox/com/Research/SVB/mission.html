Mission Package

A radio transmitter broadcasting at 30 kW is a node. A student at the console at seventeen, aware for the first time that 200,000 people can hear them, is a moment in the space between. The signal travels; something happens in the person who sent it. That something is what institutions cannot document and what oral history is designed to preserve. The student voice is the signal that never quite made it to tape. This mission is the antenna pointed at what was broadcast between the lines.

The Transcript

The space between signal and listener is where the GSD ecosystem's human layer lives. Understanding this space — from the moment a student keys the microphone to the moment a listener three counties away hears a voice that is simultaneously seventeen and infinite — is what transforms a documentation project into an oral history. A 30-kW transmitter broadcasting from a high school in Seattle's Meadowbrook neighborhood is a remarkable civic achievement. What it did to the students who lived inside it, year after year across five decades, is a different kind of remarkable, and it is at serious risk of being lost. Alumni are aging. Memory is imperfect. The tapes are in attics. This mission builds the vessel to gather what the institutional record was never designed to hold: the felt history of what it means to be young and powerful and heard.
