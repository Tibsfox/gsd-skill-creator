The Chandra X-ray Observatory has spent 26 years watching black holes across every mass class — from the 6.6-solar-mass V616 Mon at 3,300 light-years to TON 618 at 66 billion solar masses and 10.4 billion light-years. This catalog maps them all: the confirmed, the contested, the predicted. Where BHK maps the theoretical spacetime, BHC maps the observed population. The territory, not the map.

Research Modules

M1: Stellar-Mass Black Holes

Stellar Track A · X-ray binaries & LIGO

Confirmed X-ray binary catalog: Cygnus X-1, V404 Cygni, GRS 1915+105. LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA gravitational wave detections. Gaia astrometric BH series (BH1, BH3). Estimated 100 million in the Milky Way.

M2: Intermediate-Mass Black Holes

IMBH Track A · The missing link

GW190521 remnant (142 solar masses). Omega Centauri IMBH (8,200+ solar masses). GCIRS 13E candidate. The gap between stellar and supermassive. DESI early data expanding the sample.

M3: Supermassive Black Holes

SMBH Track B · Galactic centers

The two imaged black holes: Sgr A* and M87*. TON 618, NGC 1277, J0529-4351. Chandra Deep Field South: 5,000 AGN in deepest X-ray image. Population statistics from deep surveys.

M4: Detection Methods

Detection Track B · Instrumentation taxonomy

X-ray binary dynamics, gravitational waves, stellar orbital mechanics, direct EHT imaging, reverberation mapping, megamaser disks, astrometric displacement, tidal disruption events.

M5: Dynamics & Phenomena

Dynamics Synthesis · Cross-catalog

Tidal disruption events. Relativistic jets. Growth regulation and feedback. Black hole mergers and pairs. Accretion modes from quiescent to super-Eddington. Chandra archive cross-reference.
