## 1. The Missing Link Problem
Most known black holes are either below 100 Msun (stellar) or above 10^6 Msun (supermassive). IMBHs in the 100-10^5 range are scarce "missing links."

## 2. Known and Candidate IMBHs

| Object | Mass (Msun) | Host | Evidence |
|--------|-------------|------|----------|
| GW190521 remnant | 142 | N/A (merger) | First GW-confirmed IMBH |
| Omega Cen IMBH | >=8,200 | Omega Centauri | 7 fast-moving stars (Haberle et al. 2024, Nature) |
| GCIRS 13E | ~1,300 | MW Galactic Center | First reported MW IMBH (2004); debated |
| 3XMM J215022.4 | ~50,000 | Distant galaxy | Tidal disruption event |
| GW231123 remnant | IMBH-range | N/A (merger) | LIGO-Virgo-KAGRA 2024-25 |

## 3. DESI Early Data
By early 2025, DESI uncovered the largest samples ever of IMBHs and dwarf galaxies hosting active black holes, substantially expanding the candidate pool.
