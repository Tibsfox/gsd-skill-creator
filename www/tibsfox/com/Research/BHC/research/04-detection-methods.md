## 1. Detection Method Taxonomy

| Method | Instrument | BH Class | Confidence & Limitations |
|--------|-----------|----------|------------------------|
| X-ray binary dynamics | Chandra, XMM, NICER | Stellar | High; requires companion; ~50 confirmed |
| Gravitational waves | LIGO, Virgo, KAGRA | Stellar, IMBH | Direct; mass/spin from waveform; 90+ events |
| Stellar orbital mechanics | VLT/GRAVITY, Keck | SMBH | Highest precision for Sgr A* |
| Direct imaging (EHT) | Event Horizon Telescope | SMBH | Gold standard; only 2 imaged |
| Reverberation mapping | Optical spectrographs | SMBH/AGN | Moderate accuracy; hundreds of AGN |
| Megamaser disk | VLBI | SMBH | Sub-percent mass; Keplerian rotation |
| Astrometric displacement | Gaia | Stellar (dormant) | No X-ray required; growing sample |
| Tidal disruption events | Chandra, Swift | All classes | Indirect mass from flare luminosity |

## 2. Method Comparison
Gravitational waves provide the most direct measurement. EHT provides the most dramatic visualization. Astrometric detection via Gaia is the newest and fastest-growing method, revealing the dormant population invisible to X-ray surveys.
