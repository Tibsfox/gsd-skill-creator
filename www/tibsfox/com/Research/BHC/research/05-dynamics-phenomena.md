## 1. Tidal Disruption Events
Stars disrupted by tidal forces of SMBHs. Chandra documented: "Black Hole Caught Red-Handed in a Stellar Homicide" (May 2012); "A Giant Black Hole Destroys a Massive Star" (Aug 2023); a black hole that destroyed one star then targeted another (Oct 2024).

## 2. Relativistic Jets
"Black-Hole-Powered Jets Forge Fuel for Star Formation" (Feb 2017); "Gigantic Jet Spied From Black Hole in Early Universe" (Mar 2021); "Surprisingly Strong Black Hole Jet at Cosmic Noon" (Jun 2025).

## 3. Growth Regulation
Chandra's March 24, 2026 press release: why black holes hit the brakes on growth. New evidence for self-limiting feedback mechanism by which SMBHs regulate their own accretion.

## 4. Mergers and Pairs
Giant black holes on collision course (Feb 2023); supermassive pair (Sep 2024 with Hubble); three on collision course (Sep 2019).

## 5. Accretion Modes
Sgr A* feeds at extremely low rate relative to mass (sub-Eddington). J0529-4351 represents the other extreme (super-Eddington, hyperluminous).
