The Through-Line

Black holes are the universe's most extreme objects, yet they are also among its most common. The Milky Way alone may host 100 million stellar-mass black holes. Only about 50 are confirmed via X-ray binaries, and fewer than a dozen intermediate-mass candidates exist. At the supermassive end, the Chandra Deep Field South revealed 5,000 AGN in a single pointing. This catalog maps what is known, what is contested, and what is predicted — across 26 years of Chandra press archive data, three LIGO observing runs, two EHT images, and the emerging Gaia astrometric series.
