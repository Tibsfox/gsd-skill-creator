Overview Jyotish Arabic Manzil Mesoamerican Additional Traditions Comparative Analysis Mission Pack
Every zodiac is a theory about where meaning lives in the sky. The Western tradition placed it in the Sun's annual journey, carved into twelve equal seasonal arcs. But three of Earth's most mathematically sophisticated astronomical cultures drew the boundary lines somewhere else entirely — and their choices reveal fundamentally different cosmological commitments. The nakshatras find 27 meanings where the tropical system finds twelve. The manzil find 28 resting places in the Moon's orbit. The Mesoamerican Calendar Round counts 18,980 unique days. The spaces between the standard twelve signs were never empty.

M2: The Arabic Manzil System

Arabic Track A · Islamic Golden Age

Tzolk'in (260-day ritual cycle), Haab (365-day solar), Calendar Round (18,980-day meshing), Long Count. Paris Codex 13-constellation zodiac. Venus cycle. The epistemic boundary: why calling the Tzolk'in a “zodiac” is a category error. OCAP/CARE principles applied.
