Kings Creek Road runs deep into the redwoods above Boulder Creek. This is where Sam and I lived for many years — where he ran through the giants and I learned what it means to be still. At 1:41 in the morning on Flight Day 2 of Artemis II, the San Andreas shifted beneath our old home. The ground remembers.

ShakeAlert early warning was activated. UC Berkeley issued drop-cover-hold alerts at 01:41 and cleared all-clear by 02:20. Some residents reported receiving phone alerts approximately 30 seconds before feeling the tremor. One Berkeley resident on Reddit: "Shook my whole house."

Damage: No structural damage reported. UC Berkeley confirmed minimal campus impact. No injuries.

The strike-slip + reverse mechanism is characteristic of the San Andreas Fault in the Santa Cruz Mountains, where the fault bends slightly, producing transpressional motion. This is the same mechanism that produced the 1989 Loma Prieta earthquake (M6.9) — the deadliest California earthquake since 1906.

Historical Context: This Fault Segment

M6.9 Loma Prieta — October 17, 1989

The defining earthquake of this fault segment. Epicenter near Loma Prieta peak, ~9 miles NE of Santa Cruz — approximately 10 km from tonight's M4.6. Six feet of right-lateral strike-slip and four feet of reverse-slip. 63 deaths, 3,757 injuries, $6 billion damage. Cypress Structure collapse killed 41. Bay Bridge span failure. Marina district liquefaction. World Series interrupted at Candlestick Park.

Boulder Creek experienced severe property damage. The town sits directly on the ruptured segment. Highway 9 was blocked by landslides. Power was out for days. The redwoods absorbed the shaking better than the structures did.

I was 13 years old, watching the aftermath on IRC from Everett, Washington. Years later I would live on Kings Creek Road above Boulder Creek with Sam, on the same ground that shook that day.

M7.9 Great San Francisco Earthquake — April 18, 1906

The 1906 earthquake ruptured 477 km of the San Andreas Fault from San Juan Bautista to Cape Mendocino. The Loma Prieta segment was part of the southern extent of this rupture. 3,000+ deaths, 80% of San Francisco destroyed by fire. The 1989 Loma Prieta event re-ruptured the southernmost 40 km of the 1906 break.

Seismic Gap Theory

The Loma Prieta segment was designated a "seismic gap" — a section of an active fault that had been quiet for an unusually long time, building up strain. It had been relatively inactive since 1906 until two moderate foreshocks in June 1988 and August 1989 preceded the M6.9 mainshock. Tonight's M4.6 is a reminder that this segment remains active, 37 years after Loma Prieta.

Concurrent: Artemis II Flight Day 2

This earthquake occurred at MET +05:06 — five hours and six minutes after the launch of Artemis II from Pad 39B. The Orion spacecraft Integrity was in a 115 × 43,760 mile orbit around Earth with the crew asleep after completing proximity operations. While four humans orbited higher than anyone since 1972, the ground beneath the Santa Cruz Mountains shifted along the same fault that has been moving for 28 million years. The space between the Moon and the Earth includes everything underneath us.
