Parks & Green Spaces

Forest parks, waterfront trails, and athletic fields across a sprawling city.

Jennings Memorial Park & Nature Park

60+ acres combinedtrails

30 acres of forest, wetlands, and trails in Jennings Memorial Park connect to 31 acres of nature trails, open space, and wetland overlooks in the adjacent Nature Park. Together they form the city's largest green space — a forested retreat with playgrounds, ball fields, picnic shelters, and creek trails through native woodland.

Access: 6915 Armar Rd, north Marysville

Ebey Waterfront Park

sloughtrail

A waterfront trail and park along Ebey Slough, connecting to the Snohomish River system. The trail follows the historic waterway that was once Marysville's primary transportation corridor. Bird watching for herons, eagles, and migratory waterfowl along the riparian corridor.

Comeford Park

historiccommunity

Named for city founder James P. Comeford, this central park provides community gathering space with playground, picnic areas, and sports facilities. Located near downtown Marysville.

Strawberry Fields Athletic Complex

sportsheritage

A multi-field athletic complex honoring Marysville's strawberry farming heritage. Hosts youth and adult sports leagues, tournaments, and community athletic events. Named for the Japanese-American strawberry farms that once covered the area.

Lakes & Waterways

Lake Goodwin & Wenberg State Park

lakestate park

Wenberg State Park sits on the shore of Lake Goodwin, north of Marysville. A 46-acre park with swimming beach, boat launch, camping (75 sites), and fishing. Lake Goodwin covers 550 acres and supports bass, perch, and stocked trout. A popular summer destination for families.

Access: E Lake Goodwin Rd, ~10 miles north of downtown

Ebey Slough & Snohomish River

estuarysalmon

The Ebey Slough is a distributary of the Snohomish River that defines Marysville's southern boundary. The slough and river system support all five species of Pacific salmon and are critical habitat for the region's ecological health. Trail access along the waterfront.

Notable Landmarks

Tulalip Resort Casino

Tulalip Tribesresort

Operated by the Tulalip Tribes, the resort casino is one of the largest employers in north Snohomish County. The complex includes a hotel, multiple restaurants, entertainment venues, and conference facilities. The Tulalip Tribes are a federally recognized government operating from the adjacent Tulalip Reservation.

Marysville Strawberry Festival

June92+ years

Running since 1932, the Strawberry Festival is a week-long celebration in June featuring a grand parade along State Avenue, carnival, live music, car show, golf tournament, strawberry shortcake eating contest, and community events. The festival honors the strawberry farming heritage that defined the community before WWII.

Getting Here from Mukilteo

Marysville is 18 miles north of Mukilteo along the I-5 corridor, beyond Everett.

By Bus (via Everett Station)

Mukilteo → Everett Station: Sounder N Line (1 stop, 8 min, peak only) or Community Transit routes north.

Everett Station → Marysville: Community Transit routes serve the I-5 corridor north to Marysville, including service to Smokey Point and the Tulalip area.

Total transit time: approximately 40–55 minutes with transfer at Everett Station. Weekend service available but with reduced frequency.

By Car

I-5 north from Mukilteo (via SR-525/I-5) to Marysville exits. Approximately 20–25 minutes without traffic. The drive passes through Everett along the I-5 corridor.
