"Paula had four DMA channels. A Finnish programmer in 1989 saw those four channels and wrote a software mixing layer that gave composers eight voices — doubling the instrument's polyphony at the cost of forty percent of a 7.14 MHz processor's time. Every tracker effect command — the arpeggio, the portamento, the pattern jump — is an instruction written in the spaces between note triggers. The music lives there. The GSD ecosystem names its orchestration chip Agnus, its display chip Denise, its I/O chip Paula. These are not decorative names. They are a claim about architecture." — Stage 1 Through-Line, OctaMED & Mod Trackers Mission Package
