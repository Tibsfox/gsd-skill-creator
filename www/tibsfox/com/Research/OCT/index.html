The Commodore Amiga shipped in 1985 with a sound chip called Paula — four DMA-driven 8-bit PCM channels, two panned hard-left and two panned hard-right. That was it. The hardware was defined, finite, and unchangeable. And yet from those four channels, an entire musical culture emerged. Hardware constraint × software leverage = cultural explosion.

Research Modules

M1: Paula Architecture

Paula Track A · Hardware

MOS 8364 register-level architecture. Four DMA-driven 8-bit PCM channels with 6-bit volume. Hardware low-pass filter (~4.4 kHz at 3.58 MHz). The 14-bit hack: combining two channels for higher resolution audio. Agnus DMA timing and CPU zero-cost playback.

M2: MOD Format & Tracker Paradigm

MOD Track A · Format

The module file concept: samples + patterns + order list in a single portable object. Binary layout: header, sample definitions, pattern table, raw PCM. Effect vocabulary: arpeggio, portamento, volume slide, vibrato, pattern jump. VSync timing model. Zero-CPU playback design.

M3: OctaMED Deep Dive

OctaMED Focal · Software mixing

Teijo Kinnunen's software mixing breakthrough: routing two virtual channels through each Paula channel to double polyphony to 8 voices at ~40% CPU. Native MIDI support, arbitrary-length patterns, MED/OctaMED version history from 1989 to OctaMED SoundStudio. The composition tool that transcended the demoscene.

M4: Tracker Family Tree

Genealogy Track B · Evolution

The complete genealogy: SoundMonitor (C64) to Ultimate Soundtracker to NoiseTracker to ProTracker (Amiga) to ScreamTracker 3/S3M to FastTracker 2/XM to Impulse Tracker/IT. What each format added and why. The MED/OctaMED branch as parallel evolution.

M5: Demoscene & Cultural Ecology

Culture Track B · Community

BBS and FidoNet distribution networks. Demoparty competitive structure. MOD file sharing culture and modarchive.org. Keygen music as genre. UK rave/hardcore scene's adoption of Amiga trackers. Crossover artists: Calvin Harris, Venetian Snares, DJ Zinc. UNESCO intangible cultural heritage recognition.

M6: Modern Tracker Ecosystem

Modern Synthesis · Living culture

Renoise (professional VST-capable tracker DAW), OpenMPT (format museum and composition tool), MilkyTracker (FT2 clone), Furnace (multi-chip tracker), FamiTracker, SunVox (modular+tracker). Hardware: Polyend Tracker, Teenage Engineering PO series. Demoparties in 2026.
