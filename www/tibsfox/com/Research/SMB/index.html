Why Black Holes Hit the Brakes

Cosmic Noon · Wedding-Cake Survey · Accretion Mechanics · AGN Feedback · Cosmological Implications
Overview Cosmic Noon Survey Accretion Feedback Implications Mission Pack
Ten billion years ago the universe was at its most ravenous. Supermassive black holes were gorging on cold gas reserves, blazing as quasars that outshone entire galaxies. Everything since has been a long, slow exhale. A 2025 study marshaling data from 1.3 million galaxies and 8,000 growing SMBHs reveals the verdict: black holes are consuming material less rapidly because there is simply less cold gas available.
