# AGN Feedback & Galaxy Co-evolution

## Feedback Modes

AGN feedback operates in three principal modes:

**Quasar mode (radiative/wind):** During rapid accretion, the AGN drives powerful outflows that expel cold gas at velocities exceeding escape speed. Direct, violent gas removal. Gravitational energy release becomes comparable to the host galaxy's binding energy (Silk & Rees, 1998).

**Radio mode (jet/kinetic):** During lower accretion, the AGN drives jets that heat circumgalactic and halo gas, preventing cooling and condensation. "Maintenance mode" keeping massive galaxies quenched over long timescales.

**Preventative mode:** Ongoing AGN energy output prevents cold gas from reaching the interstellar medium, gradually reducing star formation without dramatic expulsion.

## Black Hole Mass as Quenching Predictor

Research using machine learning across multiple cosmological simulations (Eagle, Illustris, IllustrisTNG) finds that **black hole mass** -- not current accretion rate or AGN luminosity -- is the most predictive parameter for galaxy quenching across all redshifts (Bluck et al., ApJ 2023).

The implication: the *history* of AGN activity, encoded in black hole mass, determines quenching outcomes. Present-day AGN luminosity is nearly irrelevant. The fossil record of cosmic noon AGN activity determines the quiet universe we inhabit today.

## Galaxy Co-evolution

| Epoch | SMBH State | Galaxy State | Relationship |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| z > 3 | Rapid growth | Active star formation | Co-fueled by cold gas |
| z ~ 2 (Noon) | Peak accretion | Peak star formation | Shared gas supply; feedback begins |
| z ~ 1 | Declining | Green valley transition | AGN feedback driving quenching |
| z ~ 0.5 | Near-dormant | Red and dead | Feedback complete; gas exhausted |
