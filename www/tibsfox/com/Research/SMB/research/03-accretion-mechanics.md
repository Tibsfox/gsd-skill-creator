# Accretion Mechanics & Cold Gas Depletion

## The Three Hypotheses

Before Yu et al., three explanations competed:

1. **Lower accretion efficiency** -- Black holes became less efficient at converting material into growth
2. **Smaller typical masses** -- Average mass of active black holes declined, reducing X-ray output
3. **Fewer active black holes** -- The AGN fraction declined over time

## The Verdict

By analyzing brightness distributions and active fractions across redshift, the team concluded: **black holes are consuming material less rapidly** because cold gas availability has declined. Niel Brandt (Penn State): "Black holes' consumption of material has greatly slowed as the universe has aged, probably because the amount of cold gas available has decreased since cosmic noon."

## Cold Gas Depletion Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Description |
|-----------|------------|
| AGN feedback | Past accretion events heat and expel gas; self-starvation |
| Declining merger frequency | Mergers drive fresh gas inflow; fewer mergers = less fuel |
| Cosmological structure evolution | Star formation consumed vast reserves at cosmic noon |
| Gravitational shock heating | Gas in massive halos shock-heats, cannot cool |
| Cosmic expansion | Structure transitions toward isolation; gas inflow decreases |

## What "Cold Gas" Means

In this context, "cold gas" refers to molecular and atomic hydrogen at temperatures low enough to spiral into an accretion disk and sustain black hole growth. This distinguishes it from hot, virialized halo gas that cannot efficiently cool and fall inward.
