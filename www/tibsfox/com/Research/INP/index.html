Before the cursor blinked and before the render farm hummed, there was a room full of women in white smocks and hairnets, bent over transparent acetate sheets with the finest nibs available, tracing lines fine as spider silk. The ink and paint department was the last human station on a vast assembly line of dreams — and it is where animation truly became animation. Every frame of every hand-painted cel passed through their hands: over 100,000 per feature film, each one a miniature painting made to be photographed once and, in most cases, never seen again as a physical object.

Research Modules

M1: Origins and Technical Evolution

History Track A · 1906–1990

From Blackton's blackboard to CAPS: the full arc of cel animation technology. Cel patent, material substrate evolution, multiplane camera, rotoscoping, xerography, APT, and the digital transition. Production artifact hierarchy and peg registration systems.

M2: The Ink & Paint Department

Labor Track A · Human history

The overwhelmingly female, systematically uncredited workforce behind every frame. Gender division, department hierarchy, the art of inking, Disney's paint laboratory. Key individuals: Hazel Sewell, Mary Weiser, Ruthie Tompson, Phyllis Craig, Retta Scott. WWII opening and xerography displacement.

M3: Studio Ecosystems

Studios Track B · West + Japan

Comparative study of Disney, Warner Bros., Fleischer, MGM, Hanna-Barbera, Don Bluth, and the Japanese tradition: Toei, Mushi, Sunrise, Gainax, Madhouse, Studio Ghibli. The genga/douga system. Television transition and global outsourcing pipeline.

M4: Collecting & Authentication

Market Track C · Valuation

Production art taxonomy from key cels to sericels. Valuation hierarchy, auction records, Courvoisier Gallery, Disneyland Art Corner. Six-point authentication framework. Screen-matching techniques. Japanese anime collecting and market risks.

M5: Preservation Science

Science Track B · Conservation

Cellulose acetate chemistry and the autocatalytic vinegar syndrome feedback loop. A-D strip detection protocols. Storage environment standards (4 tiers). Getty/Disney ARL joint research program. Mixed-media conservation challenges. Emergency response protocols.

M6: Digital Cataloging & Archives

Archive Track C · Standards

VRA Core, CCO, CDWA standards mapping. 20-field minimum viable metadata schema for animation production art. Controlled vocabularies, provenance recording, condition assessment. Existing databases: ARL, SCAD, Rubberslug, CelNexus. Private and institutional implementation.
