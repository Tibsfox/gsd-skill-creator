## Discoveries

1. **Camp Muir's guide wires and tent structures are the above-treeline equivalent of the forest's Aeolian branch resonances.** Below treeline, branch cylinders produce Aeolian tones (75-500 Hz) through vortex shedding. Above treeline, the same physical mechanism applies to the guide wires (much smaller diameter, much higher Aeolian frequency: 1,000-4,000 Hz) and tent fabric edge structures. The mountain replaces the forest's Aeolian sources with human-made structures. The mechanism is identical; the structures are different.
2. **The acoustic distinction between "no rhythmic pulse" (above treeline) and "gust cycle 5-15 sec" (below treeline) is experientially significant.** Below treeline in a gale, a person can follow the gust cycle -- the alternation of gust and lull is perceptible and even calming (the lulls provide brief acoustic relief). Above treeline in a storm, the wind is nearly continuous at extreme velocity -- there is no lull, no cycle, no rhythm. The absence of rhythmic structure makes the above-treeline wind experientially more disorienting than the below-treeline gale even when the amplitude is similar.
