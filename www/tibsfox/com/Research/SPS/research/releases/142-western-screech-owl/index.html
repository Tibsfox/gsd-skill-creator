Western Screech-Owl

Megascops kennicottii (Strigidae) — E=3 — Bouncing-Ball Trill
Descending whinny trill — E=3 bouncing-ball rhythm. Eighth consecutive Strigidae (135–142). ONE AWAY from tying the Picidae nonet record. New genus: Megascops (5th). Camouflaged. Suburban. Permanent resident.

The Bouncing-Ball Trill

A series of whistled notes that accelerate and descend in pitch, like a ball bouncing to rest. 400–600 Hz, 3–5 seconds. The rhythm is the diagnostic feature: widely spaced notes speeding to a rapid, low terminal trill. Not a screech despite the name. E=3 — soft, mellow, neighborhood-scale.

Bark Mimicry — The Invisible Owl

Gray-brown plumage with intricate dark streaking and crossbarring that perfectly mimics tree bark. When alarmed, raises ear tufts and narrows its body to resemble a broken branch stub. Pressed against a trunk, it disappears. The most suburban owl — nesting in backyard trees and nest boxes while homeowners sleep unaware.

Eighth Consecutive — ONE Away from the Record

Eight consecutive Strigidae (135–142). Five genera. Six species. The Picidae nonet record stands at nine. One more owl at degree 143 would TIE the record. Two more would BREAK it.
