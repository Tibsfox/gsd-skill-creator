## What Worked

1. **The Rainbow Trout-Steelhead relationship is the entry's most important biological clarification.** The same species in two life-history forms is not widely understood outside fisheries biology -- documenting it precisely enriches the engine's treatment of *O. mykiss*.
2. **The acoustic distinction from Chinook (both episodic leaps, different energy levels) is well-grounded.** The lower mass + cleaner body shape + steeper reentry angle = higher-frequency, briefer, less energetic sound. E=5 vs. E=6 for Chinook is the correct assessment.
3. **The Olympic Peninsula crisis documentation is urgent and necessary.** Emergency closures on the Hoh, Sol Duc, and Quillayute system rivers represent one of the most serious acute conservation failures in Washington State fisheries management. The engine's permanent record holds it.
4. **The iteroparity distinction is important.** Steelhead are iteroparous (potentially spawn multiple times); Pacific salmon (Chinook, Coho, Sockeye, Pink, Chum) are semelparous (spawn once and die). This is a biologically significant distinction that affects conservation strategy.

## Discoveries

1. **The kelt ecology is underappreciated.** A post-spawning Steelhead (kelt) returning to the ocean is one of the most physically stressed fish in freshwater -- emaciated, nearly spent. Those that survive ocean recovery return as exceptionally large fish. The kelt cycle is extraordinary but rarely discussed.
2. **Summer Steelhead warming vulnerability is accelerating.** Summer fish holding in rivers through warm months are increasingly stressed. As river temperatures rise with climate change, summer Steelhead are holding in water approaching thermal tolerance limits for weeks at a time.
