## What Worked

1. **The cultural transmission parallel is the entry's most analytically significant contribution.** Both species have culturally transmitted vocalizations: Humpback song spreads laterally through social learning at 1,500 km/year; SRKW pod dialects are family-specific and transmitted generationally. The simultaneous presence of two independently evolving cultural traditions in one acoustic space is the engine's richest cultural-acoustic event. No previous entry has documented two culturally transmitted vocal systems in the same space.
2. **The Humpback recovery context is essential, not incidental.** The acoustic event would not have been possible during the post-whaling period (1970s-early 2000s) when Humpbacks were rare in the Salish Sea. The simultaneous presence is partly a conservation restoration story: the species is back in numbers that make regular summer encounters possible. The acoustic ecology is being restored along with the population.
3. **The overlap zone (100-500 Hz) is specifically documented.** The multi-species entry is not simply "two species in the water at the same time" -- the acoustic data grounds it in specific frequency overlap. The 100-500 Hz zone where both species' primary vocal energy is present is where the convergence event is acoustically defined. The behavioral modification research (SRKW reducing low-frequency calls, apparent turn-taking) is documented as a research frontier.
4. **The E=8 calibration is well-supported.** Five factors: combined output, Humpback song complexity, SRKW in non-ceremony context (E=8 vs. E=9 at greeting), cultural dual-tradition significance, conservation significance. The multi-species event reaches E=8 through superposition of two E=7-8 components plus the unique convergence quality.

## Discoveries

1. **The Humpback song is the animal kingdom's most complex sustained vocal sequence.** This is not hyperbole -- it is a research finding. No other non-human organism produces a comparably long, hierarchically structured, culturally evolving vocal sequence. The song is organized into units -> phrases -> themes -> song, with cultural variation possible at each level. The engine documents a vocal event here that has no parallel at any other degree.
2. **The SRKW acoustic situation in 2024 is an endangered-species acoustic ecology.** ~73 animals in J/K/L pods. The call catalog documents 100+ types across the three pods. If the SRKW population declines further or becomes extinct, those 100+ culturally transmitted call types disappear permanently. The engine's documentation of SRKW calls (degrees 311 and 317) is, among other things, an acoustic record of an endangered cultural tradition.
