## What Worked

1. **The air bubble mechanism is the entry's most important acoustic fact.** The musical quality of rain-on-water comes from bubble oscillation at ~14,000 Hz, not from the impact itself. This is counterintuitive and genuinely interesting.
2. **The "heard most clearly when it stops" observation is the entry's best cultural insight.** Auditory habituation makes constant sounds inaudible; the silence after rain is louder than the rain. This is the lived experience of rain in the PNW.
3. **E=3 is right.** The softest meteorological entry in the arc. Moderate, persistent Pacific rain is not a dramatic sound -- it is a constant one. The energy placement is accurate.
4. **The ferry system context grounds the entry in Seattle daily experience.** 24 million ferry passengers per year experience rain-on-water as a regular commute backdrop. The sound is not abstract.

## Discoveries

1. **The white noise machine phenomenon confirms the acoustic resonance.** Commercial white noise machines that approximate rain sound are widely used for sleep. The appeal is not coincidental -- the acoustic profile of moderate rain (textured white noise) has a specific effect on the human nervous system. The PNW is the white noise capital of the continent.
2. **Drop size distribution explains PNW rain's specific character.** Maritime 0.5-2mm drops vs. continental convective 3-5mm drops. The smaller, gentler drops produce the persistent, moderate character that distinguishes PNW rain from dramatic thunderstorm downpours.
