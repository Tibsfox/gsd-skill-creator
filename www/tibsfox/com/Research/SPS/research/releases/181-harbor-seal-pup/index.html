Harbor Seal Pup — High Bleat

Phoca vitulina (Phocidae) — E=4 — Second Harbor Seal Entry
Second Harbor Seal entry. The pup’s high bleat is distinct from the adult bark (degree 180). Mother-pup vocal recognition bond. Fifth species with double entries. Second Phocidae. Sandy beaches, summer pupping season. The pair bond.

The Pup’s Voice — A Different Sound

The adult Harbor Seal (degree 180) produces a low, guttural bark for alarm and territory. The pup produces a high-pitched, nasal bleat — a wavering, urgent call to locate its mother. As different as a dog’s bark and a lamb’s cry. 500–3000 Hz (vs adult 200–2000 Hz). The sound of need, separation, and recognition.

Mother-Pup Vocal Recognition

Within hours of birth, mother and pup exchange calls that encode individual identity. In a colony of hundreds, a mother recognizes her pup’s specific bleat. Each pup’s call has a unique frequency signature — frequency contour, timing, harmonic structure. The bond is acoustic. The call connects two into one.
