Sea Otter — High Whine & Growl

Enhydra lutris (Mustelidae) — E=4 — MUSTELIDAE RETURNS
MUSTELIDAE RETURNS (weasel family, last at degrees 63–68 river otter). The smallest marine mammal. The only marine mammal that uses tools — cracking shellfish with rocks on its belly. Densest fur of any animal: up to 1 million hairs per square inch. Kelp forest keystone species. Resident.

The Tool-User — Rocks on the Belly

The Sea Otter floats on its back and places a flat rock on its chest as an anvil. It smashes sea urchins, clams, and mussels against the rock to crack them open. Individual otters keep favorite rocks stored in a skin pouch under the forearm. True tool use — selecting, transporting, and using an object. The only tool-using marine mammal.

Kelp Forest Keystone — The Trophic Cascade

Otters eat urchins. Urchins eat kelp. With otters: kelp forests thrive. Without otters: urchin barrens — underwater deserts. Documented by Estes & Palmisano (1974), this is ecology’s most famous trophic cascade. Kelp forests sequester 5–8x more carbon than otter-absent barrens. One species controls an entire ecosystem.

The Densest Fur — 1 Million Hairs Per Square Inch

No blubber. The Sea Otter relies entirely on fur for insulation. Up to 1,000,000 hairs per square inch — a human head has 100,000 total. Two layers: waterproof guard hairs + insulating underfur trapping air against skin. 10–15% of the day grooming. If fouled by oil, the otter dies of hypothermia. The Exxon Valdez spill killed ~2,800 sea otters.
