*Degree 185 retrospective complete. SEA OTTER at the one hundred eighty-fifth degree -- THE TOOL-USER. Mustelidae returns to the engine after 117 degrees -- the weasel family last seen with the river otter at degrees 63-68, now expressed in the marine realm. The Sea Otter is the smallest marine mammal, the only marine mammal that uses tools, and the owner of the densest fur of any animal on Earth. One million hairs per square inch. No blubber -- fur is the only insulation. Rocks on the belly as anvils, cracking sea urchins and clams. Favorite rocks stored in skin pouches under the forearm. The otter-urchin-kelp trophic cascade, documented by Estes and Palmisano in 1974, is ecology's most famous example: otters eat urchins, urchins eat kelp, so otters maintain kelp forests. Remove the otter and the forest becomes an urchin barren. Add the otter and the forest returns. One species controls an entire ecosystem. From near-extinction (fewer than 2,000 by 1911, hunted for that extraordinary fur) to partial recovery (125,000 globally, 2,500 in Washington, still ESA Threatened).
