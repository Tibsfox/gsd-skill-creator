## Structural Event 2: Granary Trees -- Community Infrastructure

- **Situation:** The Acorn Woodpecker drills thousands of individually sized holes in trees, each storing a single acorn. 50,000+ acorns per granary. Maintained across generations by the colony.
- **Root Cause:** The granary-tree behavior is an evolved adaptation for acorn storage and winter survival. The behavior is species-specific and extraordinary in its scale.
- **Recommendation:** Document the granary tree as community infrastructure. Note the scale (50,000+ acorns, decades of use). Compare with nutcracker caching (105, dispersed) -- two caching strategies at massive scale.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Granary trees. Community infrastructure. 50,000+ acorns. Track.

## Structural Event 3: Colonial Living -- Cooperative Breeding

- **Situation:** The Acorn Woodpecker lives in colonial groups of 15+ individuals with cooperative breeding, communal nest defense, and shared granary maintenance. One of the most social woodpeckers in the world.
- **Root Cause:** Cooperative breeding is an evolved social strategy for granary defense and winter survival. The colonial system is rare among woodpeckers.
- **Recommendation:** Document the colonial system. Connect to Yellow-bellied Marmot (75, colonial parallel) and Common Raven (100, intelligence). Track.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Colonial. Cooperative breeding. Social intelligence. Track.

## Structural Event 4: Oak Savanna Habitat

- **Situation:** The Acorn Woodpecker occupies oak savanna and oak woodland in southern Oregon at the northern edge of its range. Obligate relationship with oaks.
- **Root Cause:** The species requires oaks for acorn production and suitable trees for granary construction. Without oaks, no acorns, no Acorn Woodpecker.
- **Recommendation:** Document the oak savanna niche. Note the limited PNW distribution (southern Oregon). The habitat is specific and declining.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Oak savanna. Southern Oregon. Obligate. Track.
