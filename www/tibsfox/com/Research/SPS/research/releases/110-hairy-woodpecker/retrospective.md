## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The Hairy-Downy comparison framework is well-established.** The call (PEEK! vs. pik), bill proportion, size, and drum differences are well-documented in ornithological literature. The framework sets up degree 111 effectively.

2. **E=5 is defensible.** The Hairy's peek! is loud and emphatic but not physically overwhelming. The stuttering drum is rapid and sharp but not the booming resonance of the Pileated. E=5 correctly captures this: powerful but not maximum.

3. **The genus etymology contrast (tree-beater vs. tree-treader) is elegant.** Dryocopus (108-109) to Dryobates (110-111) captures the size and force differential in the genus names themselves.

4. **The Picidae streak documentation is accurate.** Four consecutive, halfway to the Sciuridae record, each entry acoustically distinct.

5. **Cross-references to SPS-109 (Pileated drumming), SPS-108 (Pileated laughing), SPS-107 (Acorn), SPS-021 (Flicker), and SPS-111 (Downy preview) are all defensible.**

6. **Hairy Woodpecker is extremely well-documented.** Jackson, Ouellet & Jackson 2002 is the definitive species account. Cornell Lab comprehensive. Stark et al. 1998 for drum analysis. Strong source base.
