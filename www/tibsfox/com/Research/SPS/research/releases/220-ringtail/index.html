FIRST PROCYONIDAE. The raccoon family enters. Sharp bark at 1,000–4,000 Hz + rapid chitter at 2,000–6,000 Hz. 0.7–1.5 kg — cat-sized with an enormous banded tail. E=5. Nocturnal specialist. FIRST PROCYONIDAE — Raccoon Family Enters

Raccoons, coatis, ringtails, kinkajous. New World family. Plantigrade. Omnivorous. Dexterous. The ringtail enters first: Bassariscus astutus — “cunning little fox.” Not a cat, not a fox, not a raccoon. Its own remarkable creature.

The Nocturnal Observer

Strictly nocturnal. Enormous dark-adapted eyes. Rotating rear ankles for headfirst cliff descent. 14–16 alternating black and white tail rings. Tail as long as the body — balance organ for rocky terrain. Rare in Washington: Columbia River Gorge, southeastern WA. Historical “miner’s cat” — kept as mousers in frontier camps.
