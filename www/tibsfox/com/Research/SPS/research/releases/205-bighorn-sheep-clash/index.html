The Clash — 800 lbs of Impact

Two rams charge from 20–40 feet apart, reaching 20–40 mph. Combined closing speed exceeds 60 mph. Impact: up to 800 lbs of force. The crack is instantaneous — a sharp, resonant CRACK that echoes off canyon walls. Audible for miles. Contests involve dozens of clashes over hours.

Skull Engineering — Built to Collide

Double-layered skull: outer layer absorbs initial impact, cancellous bone struts distribute force, inner layer protects brain. Frontal sinuses act as pneumatic shock absorbers — larger in rams than ewes, evolved specifically for combat. Cervical reinforcement: massive tendons, thick vertebral processes. The entire anterior body is an impact-absorption chain.

Horn Curl — The Measure of a Ram

3/4 curl, full curl, beyond. A full-curl ram has completed a 360-degree arc — typically at 7–8 years. Horns can weigh over 30 lbs combined (12% of body weight). The curl directs impact force into the horn base where bone and keratin absorb it. Growth is continuous but slows after full curl.
