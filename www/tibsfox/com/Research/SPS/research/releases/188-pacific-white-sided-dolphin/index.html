Pacific White-sided Dolphin — Squawks & Whistles

Lagenorhynchus obliquidens (Delphinidae) — E=5 — FIRST DELPHINIDAE
FIRST DELPHINIDAE — the dolphin family enters. Third cetacean family. The silence breaks — after two porpoises (E=5 rooster-tail, E=1 silence), the dolphin arrives with audible squawks, whistles, and clicks. Acrobatic leaps. Pods of 10–100+. 9 consecutive marine mammals (180–188) — ties Picidae nonet.

The Silence Breaks — Squawks, Whistles, Clicks

After two silent porpoises (Dall’s 186, Harbor 187), the dolphin arrives with full audible voice. Squawks: rapid click bursts merging into buzzy vocalizations. Whistles: frequency-modulated tonal sounds — the acoustic hallmark that distinguishes dolphins from porpoises. Clicks: broadband echolocation in audible range. A rich, diverse, hearable vocal repertoire. E=5.

The Acrobat — Aerial Displays

Spectacular leaps, belly flops, side slaps, somersaults. Launches completely out of the water, rotating in mid-air. Multiple dolphins leaping simultaneously. Speeds up to 25 mph. The progression from cryptic surfacing (Harbor Porpoise 187) to full aerial display (188) mirrors the acoustic progression from silence to song.

Marine Mammal Era — 9 Consecutive — Ties Record

9 consecutive marine mammals (degrees 180–188). Ties the Picidae nonet record for longest taxonomic streak. 5 families: Phocidae (2), Otariidae (3), Mustelidae (1), Phocoenidae (2), Delphinidae (1). Extraordinary diversity within a single streak. Energy range: E=1 to E=6 across 9 entries.
