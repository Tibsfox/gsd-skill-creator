## E=4 -- THE NOCTURNAL TWITTER

### Why E=4

The Ancient Murrelet's sound registers at E=4:

- **Nocturnal colony sound:** The shrill twittering is given at night, when the birds are active at colony. The nocturnal timing creates an eerie, disorienting soundscape -- shrill bird calls in total darkness.
- **Colony aggregate:** Like the Common Murre (177, E=5), the colony sound is more impressive than individual calls. But the Ancient Murrelet's colony is smaller and the individual calls are less powerful.
- **Character:** The calls are high-pitched, rapid, trilling -- a "twitter" that is sharp and penetrating but not loud. Multiple birds calling create a shimmering chorus.
- **Why not E=5?** The individual volume is modest. The colony effect is notable but not overwhelming like the murre colony. E=4 reflects a penetrating, contextually eerie sound that gains power from the nocturnal setting.

## TWO-DAY-OLD CHICKS AT SEA

### The Most Precocial Seabird

The Ancient Murrelet's chick departure is one of the most remarkable events in bird biology:

- **Two days old:** Ancient Murrelet chicks leave the nest burrow when they are only ONE TO THREE DAYS OLD -- typically at two days. They have never eaten in the burrow. They have never seen the ocean. They cannot fly. They weigh about 25-30 grams.
- **Nighttime departure:** The departure happens at NIGHT. The parents call from the sea below the colony, and the chicks respond -- running, tumbling, falling through the dark vegetation toward the sound of their parents' voices.
- **The run to the sea:** Chicks emerge from the burrow and scramble downhill toward the ocean, guided by parental calls and the sound of waves. They may travel hundreds of meters through forest and undergrowth. Predation during this journey is a major mortality source.
- **Open ocean:** Once the chicks reach the water, the family group swims away from the island -- potentially traveling tens or hundreds of kilometers at sea. The chicks are fed at sea by both parents for several weeks.
- **Why so early?** The precocial strategy minimizes time at the vulnerable colony. Burrow-nesting seabirds on islands are especially vulnerable to introduced predators (rats, cats, raccoons). By leaving at two days, the chick spends the minimum possible time at the most dangerous location.
- **Comparison:** Most seabirds spend weeks or months in the nest. The Common Murre's chick leaves at 15-25 days. The Ancient Murrelet's chick at 1-3 days is dramatically earlier -- one of the most precocial departure times of any bird.

(Sources: Cornell Lab of Ornithology; Gaston 1992; Gaston & Jones 1998; engine internal analysis)

## THE "ANCIENT" NAME

### An Elder's Shawl

The name "Ancient" refers to the bird's plumage:

- **Gray back:** The breeding adult has a gray back that suggested, to early naturalists, an old person's gray shawl. The "ancient" appearance gave the species its name.
- **White head streaks:** Fine white streaks on the black head add to the "aged" appearance -- like gray hair.
- **Contrast with other Alcidae:** The Ancient Murrelet's plumage is subtler and more "dignified" than the dramatic puffin or guillemot. The gray-and-black-and-white palette is understated.
- **Scientific name:** *Synthliboramphus antiquus* -- *antiquus* meaning "ancient" in Latin. The genus name means "compressed bill."

(Sources: Cornell Lab of Ornithology; Sibley 2014; etymology; engine internal analysis)

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of the Ancient Murrelet

**Nocturnal Colony Twitter:**
- **Trigger:** Nighttime colony activity. Arriving and departing birds. Contact between mates.
- **Mechanism:** Vocal (syringeal).
- **Character:** High-pitched, rapid, shrill twittering. A trilling, chattering vocalization. Multiple birds create a shimmering nocturnal chorus.
- **Frequency:** Approximately 4000-8000 Hz. High-pitched.
- **Duration:** Individual twitter bursts 0.5-2.0 seconds. Colony sound continuous during peak nocturnal activity.
- **Amplitude:** E=4. Penetrating but not loud. Colony aggregate creates atmosphere. The nocturnal context amplifies the eeriness.

**Parental Call (Chick Departure):**
- Parents call from the sea during chick departure nights. The calls guide chicks from burrow to ocean. Specific, recognizable to individual chicks.
