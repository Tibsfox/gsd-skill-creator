*Degree 179 retrospective complete. ANCIENT MURRELET at the one hundred seventy-ninth degree -- THE FOUNDING ELDER. The Ancient Murrelet is named for its gray back that resembles an old person's shawl -- *antiquus*, ancient. Fine white streaks on the black head add to the elder's appearance. And this elder has the most extraordinary parenting strategy of any seabird: its chicks leave the nest at one to three days old. Two days is typical. The chick has never eaten in the burrow. It cannot fly. It weighs 25 to 30 grams. At night, it scrambles from the burrow toward the sea, guided by the calls of its parents from the water below. The journey through dark vegetation may be hundreds of meters. Once the chick reaches the ocean, the family swims away -- potentially traveling hundreds of kilometers at sea. The parents feed the chick at sea for weeks. This is the most precocial chick departure of any seabird. For comparison, the Common Murre (177) keeps its chick on the ledge for 15 to 25 days. The Ancient Murrelet's two-day departure is seven to twelve times faster. The strategy minimizes time at the vulnerable colony -- where introduced predators (rats, cats, raccoons) are the primary threat. Get out fast. Get to the safe ocean. The Alcidae streak closes at 179 -- six consecutive entries (174-179) documenting six species with six distinct nesting strategies: crevice, nocturnal burrow, cliff burrow, open cliff ledge, old-growth tree branch, and island burrow. The longest family streak in the engine. And across the degree pair, Sir Mix-a-Lot is the founding elder of Seattle hip-hop -- the one who built the label, wrote the anthem, achieved #1, won the Grammy, and launched the careers. Both are founders who launch the next generation into the unknown. The founding elder. v1.49.319.*
