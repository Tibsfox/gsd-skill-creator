Ancient Murrelet — Shrill Twitter

Synthliboramphus antiquus (Alcidae) — E=4 — The Founding Elder
SIXTH ALCIDAE (174–179) — streak closes. Chicks leave the nest at TWO DAYS OLD, leaping into the dark sea. Named for its gray “elder’s shawl.” Nocturnal colony. Shrill twittering in darkness. E=4. v1.49.319.

The “Ancient” Name — An Elder’s Gray Shawl

The breeding adult’s gray back suggested to early naturalists an old person’s gray shawl. Fine white streaks on the black head add to the “aged” appearance. Synthliboramphus antiquus — antiquus meaning “ancient” in Latin. An understated, dignified plumage among the flashy puffins and guillemots.

Two-Day-Old Chicks at Sea — The Most Precocial Seabird

At 1–3 days old, Ancient Murrelet chicks emerge from their burrows at night and scramble toward the sea, guided by parental calls. They have never eaten. They cannot fly. They weigh 25–30 grams. Once in the water, the family swims away — potentially hundreds of kilometers. The most dramatic departure in seabird biology.
