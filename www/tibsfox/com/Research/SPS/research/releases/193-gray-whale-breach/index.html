Gray Whale Breach — 40 Tons Airborne

Eschrichtius robustus (Eschrichtiidae) — E=7 — HIGHEST SPS ENERGY
E=7 — HIGHEST SPS ENERGY. A 40-ton animal launches itself out of the water and crashes back. ~10,000 watts of acoustic energy on impact. The most spectacular physical event in the engine. 14 consecutive marine mammals (180–193). Second Gray Whale entry (first at 192, blow E=4).

The Breach — ~10,000 Watts of Impact

The whale accelerates vertically from depth. 40 tons leave the water. The body rotates 90–180 degrees in mid-air. Then the crash: 500,000–1,000,000 newtons of impact force. Broadband percussive sound at 20–5,000 Hz. Splash column 10–15 meters high. Detectable by other whales at 5–20+ km. The loudest single-event sound in the engine. E=7.

Why They Breach — Five Theories

Communication: acoustic impact carries kilometers. Parasite removal: dislodges barnacles and whale lice. Play: calves breach more than adults. Acoustic exploration: broadband echo-sounding. Social display: fitness demonstration. These theories are not mutually exclusive — a single breach may serve all five functions.

Marine Mammal Era — 14 Consecutive

14 consecutive marine mammals (degrees 180–193). 7 families: Phocidae, Otariidae, Mustelidae, Phocoenidae, Delphinidae, Balaenopteridae, Eschrichtiidae. The longest streak in engine history. Energy range: E=1 to E=7 across 14 entries.
