Mountain Chickadee

Poecile gambeli
fee-bee-bay descending whistle — the memory, 3rd Paridae in the engine, 3rd Poecile — completes the three-chickadee elevation gradient (lowland / coastal / mountain), BREAKS the 5-consecutive Passerellidae streak (45–49), spatial memory specialist — caches tens of thousands of seeds and remembers every one, hippocampus larger than any other chickadee, Common Bird in Steep Decline (~39% population loss since 1966), white eyebrow diagnostic, E=2

CSV Substitution

The original CSV had Rough-skinned Newt (Taricha granulosa) at degree 50. Because the SPS Part B engine is birds-and-mammals only, Mountain Chickadee replaces it. The substitution preserves the original’s E=2 energy level and forested habitat. This is the sixth consecutive amphibian-class replacement (Pacific Chorus Frog at 45, Columbia Spotted Frog at 46, Red-legged Frog at 47, Tailed Frog at 48, Western Toad at 49, Rough-skinned Newt at 50) — the engine remains deep in the amphibian belt of the original CSV.

The Passerellidae Streak Ends at Five

Mountain Chickadee is Paridae, NOT Passerellidae. This BREAKS the five-consecutive Passerellidae run (degrees 45–49). The sparrow quintet is complete. A new family enters the sequence for the first time in six degrees. Paridae was last seen at degree 9 (Chestnut-backed Chickadee) — 41 degrees ago.

Three Chickadees, Three Elevations

The Mountain Chickadee completes the engine’s three-Poecile elevation gradient. Black-capped Chickadee (degree 3, lowland, mixed woodland). Chestnut-backed Chickadee (degree 9, mid-elevation, wet coastal conifer). Mountain Chickadee (degree 50, high elevation, dry montane conifer). Three elevation bands, three acoustic environments, three cognitive breakthroughs: language, variation, memory. The only genus with three representatives from the same family. Degrees 3, 9, and 50 span 47 degrees of the engine.

The 51st Acoustic Element: The Memory

The Mountain Chickadee’s fee-bee-bay is a descending pure-toned whistle of spatial precision — 3–4 notes stepping downward in frequency, ~4,170 Hz to ~3,200 Hz, each note clean and sustained. Named “the memory” for three resonances: the bird’s extraordinary spatial memory (tens of thousands of cached seeds, barcode neurons in the hippocampus), the recall of two previous Poecile in the engine (degrees 3 and 9), and the 50-degree milestone — looking back across half the circle.

The memory differs from the ballad (49) as precision differs from beauty. The ballad says “listen to how beautiful this is.” The memory says “I know exactly where I am.”

The Hippocampal Specialist

Mountain Chickadees cache tens of thousands of food items across the mountain landscape and retrieve them weeks or months later. Research (Chettih et al. 2024, Cell) revealed that each cache is individually encoded by unique “barcode” firing patterns in hippocampal neurons. Higher-elevation birds have larger hippocampi with more and larger neurons. A 2024 Current Biology study identified ~100 genes linked to spatial memory. Birds with better memory live two years longer. Natural selection acts directly on cognition.

Common Bird in Steep Decline

Partners in Flight designates the Mountain Chickadee as a Common Bird in Steep Decline — ~1% annual decline since 1966, cumulative loss of ~39%. Population ~7.9 million (vs. Black-capped ~42M, Chestnut-backed ~10M). Climate change may compress the mountain habitat upslope, and the memory-survival link that defines the species’ strategy may become less effective in unpredictable conditions. The most vulnerable of the three engine chickadees.

E=2 — All Three Poecile Are Quiet

E=2 at degree 50. All three engine chickadees are E=2 — a genus-level characteristic. E=2 + E=3 together now hold 24 degrees (47.1%), further widening the gap over E=4’s 21 degrees (41.2%). Chickadees are quiet birds whose survival strategy is cognition, not acoustic power.

The Variegated Bird of Gambel

Poecile gambeli — from Greek poikilos (“variegated”) + the honorific gambeli for William Gambel (1823–1849), a Philadelphia naturalist who collected the type specimen during his California expeditions. Gambel died of typhoid fever at age 27 while crossing the Sierra Nevada during the Gold Rush — a young naturalist lost in the same mountain forests this bird calls home. The species name commemorates a life cut short in the mountains.

The Sound: Fee-Bee-Bay

Song: 1–2 seconds, ~3,200–4,200 Hz. Three to four pure-toned whistled notes descending in a clean staircase. The mnemonic “cheeseburger” captures the rhythm. Purer and more sustained than the Black-capped’s two-note fee-bee (degree 3) — the Mountain Chickadee has elaborated the ancestral song with additional descending steps.

Chick-a-dee call: Six note types (A, A/B, B, C, Dh, D) — an expanded repertoire compared to the Black-capped’s four types (degree 3). Hundreds of unique combinations encoding information about urgency, topic, subject, and threat level.

Spatial Memory Research

The Mountain Chickadee is among the most intensively studied spatial memory systems in any wild animal. Key findings:

  • Barcode neurons — each cache encoded by a unique hippocampal firing pattern (Chettih et al. 2024, Cell)
  • ~100 memory-linked genes identified, concentrated in hippocampal neurogenesis pathways (Current Biology 2024)
  • Larger hippocampus at higher elevations — more neurons, larger neurons, more neurogenesis (Pravosudov lab, UNR)
  • Memory predicts lifespan — best-memory birds live ~2 years longer than worst-memory birds
  • Natural selection on cognition — spatial memory ability directly affects overwinter survival

Theory Nodes

M

The Memory foundational

The 51st acoustic element. A descending pure-toned whistle of spatial precision. Named for the hippocampal memory specialist, the recall of two previous Poecile (degrees 3, 9), and the 50-degree milestone — looking back across half the circle.

E

Three-Chickadee Elevation Gradient foundational

Lowland (degree 3) → mid-elevation coastal (degree 9) → high-elevation montane (degree 50). Three Poecile, three elevation bands, three cognitive breakthroughs: language, variation, memory. The only genus represented three times from the same family.

H

The Hippocampal Specialist foundational

Tens of thousands of caches, barcode neurons, ~100 memory-linked genes, natural selection on cognition. The most thoroughly researched spatial memory system in any wild songbird. Memory quality predicts survival — better memory, longer life.

B

Passerellidae Streak Broken foundational

Five-consecutive Passerellidae (45–49) ends at degree 50. Paridae replaces Passerellidae. The sparrow quintet is complete. A new family enters for the first time in six degrees.

E

E=2 — Genus-Level Quiet foundational

All three Poecile are E=2. A genus-level characteristic. E=2 + E=3 = 24 degrees (47.1%) vs. E=4 = 21 (41.2%). Chickadees survive through cognition, not volume.

W

The White Eyebrow intermediate

The diagnostic field mark. A white supercilium bisecting the black cap — unique among North American chickadees. Visible in fresh plumage (fall/winter), may fade by late summer as white feather tips wear away.

C

Mountain Conifer Forest intermediate

Ponderosa pine, Douglas fir, Engelmann spruce at 600 m to timberline. Dry, open, sun-warmed — acoustically different from the wet coastal conifer of the Chestnut-backed’s habitat. Low sound attenuation favors the fee-bee-bay’s pure tones.

SPS-003: Black-capped Chickadee

SAME GENUS, DIFFERENT ELEVATION. *Poecile* at lowland (degree 3) and mountain (degree 50). Fee-bee (2 notes) vs. fee-bee-bay (3–4 notes). Combinatorial syntax (4 note types) vs. expanded repertoire (6 note types). Language vs. memory. 47 degrees apart, same genus.

SPS-009: Chestnut-backed Chickadee

SAME GENUS, MID-ELEVATION. The third point on the elevation gradient. Coastal conifer specialist (degree 9) between the lowland Black-capped and the mountain Mountain. Lost the fee-bee song entirely. Syntax conserved across speciation but song diverged.

SPS-049: Fox Sparrow

FAMILY TRANSITION. The ballad (degree 49, Passerellidae) yields to the memory (degree 50, Paridae). The quintet ends, a new family begins. Melodious completeness yields to spatial precision. Dense thicket gives way to open mountain forest.

SPS-045: Song Sparrow

SPS-004: Northern Spotted Owl

CONSERVATION PARALLEL. The Mountain Chickadee’s “Common Bird in Steep Decline” status echoes the Spotted Owl’s Near Threatened ranking. Two species declining in PNW conifer forests. Different causes, same habitat under pressure.

SPS-034: Red Crossbill

CONIFER SPECIALIST PARALLEL. Both are montane conifer specialists with complex population structure. Red Crossbill has 10+ call types; Mountain Chickadee has two phylogeographic clades (potential species split). Conifer-dependent birds with unresolved taxonomy.

AVI: Wings of the Pacific Northwest

Mountain Chickadee documented in eastern Washington. Two subspecies populations in the state: eastern Cascades (P. g. baileyae) and Blue Mountains / northeast (P. g. gambeli). Rare in western Washington except during winter irruptions.

ECO: Living Systems of the PNW

Montane conifer forest ecology. Ponderosa pine and Douglas fir as primary habitat trees. Elevation-dependent species interactions. Climate change impacts on montane bird communities — upslope habitat compression, altered phenology.
