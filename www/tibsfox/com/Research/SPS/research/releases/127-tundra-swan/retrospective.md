## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **E=5 is defensible.** The individual Tundra Swan call is moderate (E=3-4). But the species' acoustic identity is the flock baying, and the flock multiplier (50-200 swans) produces E=5 volume. The melodious quality adds emotional intensity above raw decibel level.

2. **The Picidae record break is accurately documented.** The Picidae held 9 consecutive (107-115). The Anatidae are now at 10 (118-127). The record is broken.

3. **The Skagit Valley wintering ecology is well-documented.** Cornell Lab, WDFW, and field guides confirm the Skagit Flats as a primary PNW wintering ground for Tundra Swans.

4. **The Tundra/Trumpeter comparison preview is accurate.** Size, bill shape, voice, and population differences are well-established in the literature. Voice is consistently cited as the most reliable field mark.

5. **Cross-references to SPS-128 (Trumpeter), SPS-118 (Harlequin), SPS-126 (Canvasback), SPS-107-115 (Picidae), and SPS-124 (Common Goldeneye) are all defensible.**

### Source Quality

6. **Tundra Swan is well-documented.** Cornell Lab, Limpert & Earnst 1994, Sibley 2014. Migration routes, wintering ecology, and acoustics are thoroughly covered in the literature.
