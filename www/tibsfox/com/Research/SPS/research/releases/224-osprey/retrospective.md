## Discoveries

1. **The "head-forward fish carry" is a documented instinct, not a learned behavior.** The Osprey reorients the fish in flight almost immediately after the catch, consistently orienting the fish head-forward to reduce aerodynamic drag. This is one of the most precisely studied behavioral adaptations in raptor biology.
2. **The Osprey is one of the few birds that can submerge completely and survive.** The plunge into water feet-first can submerge the bird to its shoulders or completely. The oily plumage allows it to shake off and fly. Documented cases of Osprey being held underwater by large fish they could not release (due to the spicule grip) are among the more dramatic moments in raptor natural history.
3. **Lake Washington (urban Seattle) supports a documented breeding Osprey population.** The engine's geographic scope includes urban Seattle. The Osprey at Lake Washington -- visible from the Burke-Gilman Trail, from the SR-520 bridge -- is a PNW urban wildlife success story.
