## Purpose

This is the fourteenth release of the PNW 360 Species engine and the third species in the **second decade** (degrees 11--20). The Hutton's Vireo introduces **three new structural dimensions** to the circle: (1) repetition without variation -- the first species whose defining acoustic characteristic is the monotonous repetition of a simple phrase, crossing the monotony threshold into persistent presence signaling; (2) oak woodland -- the first non-conifer-dominated habitat in the engine, introducing Garry oak ecosystem as a distinct, critically threatened PNW landscape; (3) convergent evolution -- the first species whose visual identity is defined by near-identical resemblance to an unrelated species (Ruby-crowned Kinglet, family Regulidae), demonstrating independent lineages arriving at the same morphological solution.

The Hutton's Vireo also introduces the **ostinato** as a synthesis type -- a two-note phrase repeated below the fusion threshold, where each repetition is heard as a discrete event. This contrasts with the Oregon Junco's trill (degree 11, repetition ABOVE the fusion threshold, perceived as continuous) and the Chestnut-backed Chickadee's variation (degree 9, same structure with modified delivery). The vireo is the most conservative singer in the engine: it repeats and does not vary.

## Three Structural Advances

### 1. Repetition Without Variation: The Ostinato

The Hutton's Vireo is the first species in the engine defined by **monotonous repetition** of a simple phrase. The two-note "zu-wee" repeats hundreds of times per hour without melodic, rhythmic, or timbral variation. This crosses the **monotony threshold**: the point at which repetition ceases to carry new information and instead maintains a persistent presence signal.

The repetition taxonomy is now complete through several modes:
- **Degree 1** (Pacific Wren): No repetition. Maximum improvisation.
- **Degree 5** (Nuthatch): Single-note steady beat. The metronome.
- **Degree 9** (CB Chickadee): Variation on a theme. Same structure, modified delivery.
- **Degree 11** (Oregon Junco): Single-note repetition ABOVE fusion threshold. The trill -- discrete events perceived as continuous.
- **Degree 13** (Hutton's Vireo): Two-note repetition BELOW fusion threshold. The ostinato -- each repetition a discrete, identical event. No variation.

### 2. Oak Woodland: The First Non-Conifer Habitat

Twelve previous species were associated with conifer-dominated ecosystems. The Hutton's Vireo introduces **Garry oak woodland** -- the only native oak habitat of the Pacific Northwest, a fire-dependent savanna that has declined 90--95% from pre-settlement extent. This is a structural habitat advance: the engine now represents both the dominant conifer landscape and the rare, threatened oak alternative.

The oak woodland connection also introduces the Coast Salish fire management story: the open, park-like structure of pre-contact oak savannas was maintained by deliberate burning for millennia. The loss of these fire practices is the primary driver of Garry oak decline. The vireo's habitat is a human-ecological partnership, not a "natural" wilderness.

### 3. Convergent Evolution: The Double

The near-identical appearance of the Hutton's Vireo (Vireonidae) and Ruby-crowned Kinglet (Regulidae) -- two unrelated families that have converged on the same physical solution -- introduces **convergent evolution** as a documented phenomenon in the engine. The Golden-crowned Kinglet at degree 8 documented the Regulidae family. The Hutton's Vireo at degree 13 documents the unrelated species that looks like them. The engine now contains both sides of the convergent pair.

The music-theory parallel is **independent discovery**: different traditions arriving at the same solution because the underlying pressures are the same. Convergent evolution and independent discovery are the same process operating in biological and cultural domains.

## The Seales Parallel: The Academic

The Part A parallel at degree 13 maps to **Marc Seales** from S36. The parallel operates on four axes:

1. **Academic persistence.** Seales is a professor at the University of Washington, a jazz educator who sustains the tradition through teaching. The vireo is a territorial broadcaster, sustaining its presence through repetition. Both maintain something larger through disciplined practice.
2. **Systematic approach.** Seales works within the tradition systematically -- modal jazz, harmonic fundamentals, the discipline of jazz education. The vireo works within its two-note phrase systematically -- no improvisation, no variation. System over spontaneity.
3. **Energy 2.** Both are energy-2 -- present, audible, engaged, but not explosive. The consistent, reliable middle ground.
4. **Resident / persistent.** Both are always there. Seales at UW, the vireo in the oak woodland. Neither disappears unpredictably like Lanphere (degree 12). Both are constants.

## What Worked

### 1. The ostinato concept lands cleanly
The distinction between repetition modes -- no repetition (wren), variation (chickadee), trill above fusion (junco), ostinato below fusion (vireo) -- is structurally crisp. Each mode has a clear acoustic and perceptual identity. The repetition taxonomy is now well-populated.

### 2. The convergent evolution pair is instructive
Having both the Regulidae (degree 8) and the Vireonidae mimic (degree 13) in the engine creates a concrete demonstration of convergent evolution with a direct cross-reference. The engine can now point to two specific releases that document both sides of the convergent pair.

### 3. The oak woodland introduces genuine habitat diversity
Thirteen degrees of conifer was becoming monotonic. The Garry oak woodland is a structurally different ecosystem -- open canopy, grassland understory, fire-dependent -- that brings new ecological vocabulary to the engine. The Coast Salish fire management connection enriches the cultural dimension.

### 4. The 12/13 contrast (noise vs. persistence) is the strongest adjacent-degree contrast yet
The Pine Siskin (irruptive, stochastic, unpredictable) followed immediately by the Hutton's Vireo (resident, constant, monotonously repetitive) creates a powerful juxtaposition. The engine's strongest statement about temporal diversity is made by placing these two species side by side.

### 5. The monotony threshold concept has broad applicability
The idea that repetition shifts from information-carrying to presence-maintaining past a certain count is a useful analytical tool. It applies to territorial birdsong, musical loops, ambient music, advertising jingles, and persistent signals of all kinds. The vireo provides a clear biological exemplar.

### 6. The minimal melody (two notes: up then down) is the irreducible kernel
The "zu-wee" as the minimum possible melody -- question-answer compressed to two notes -- is a clean structural observation. Every melody contains this motion. The vireo delivers it raw.

## What Could Be Better

### 1. STILL no high-energy species (now 14 degrees without one above energy 2 -- CRITICAL x6)

This is now a **six-release critical deficiency**. Fourteen degrees. Three energy levels used: 0 (none -- the heron's silence), 1 (bushtit, kinglet, siskin), and 2 (everything else). No energy 3, 4, or 5. The recommendation has been at the top of the list for six consecutive releases. The energy-2 ceiling MUST break. The engine cannot credibly represent "The Sound of Puget Sound" without loud sounds.

### 2. Still only one non-passerine (the heron at degree 0)

Thirteen of fourteen species are passerines. Taxonomic diversity remains narrow. A woodpecker, raptor, seabird, or waterfowl would broaden the foundation.

### 3. Still no marine or shoreline species

"The Sound of Puget Sound" -- fourteen degrees about a Sound with no Sound in them. This gap is conspicuous.

### 4. Still no predator species

The alarm network has broadcasters and receivers but no trigger species.

### 5. The energy distribution is heavily skewed low

Three species at energy 1, ten at energy 2, zero at 3/4/5 (plus the heron at 0). The engine needs high-energy species urgently.

### 6. The vireo's scolding chatter deserves more analysis

The alarm/agitation vocalizations were described but not deeply analyzed. The scolding chatter near nests or predators is acoustically distinct from the territorial zuit and may carry information about threat type or intensity.
