## Structural Event 2: Whitebark Pine Mutualism -- Obligate Seed Disperser

- **Situation:** Clark's Nutcracker caches up to 98,000 whitebark pine seeds per year. Unretrieved seeds germinate, making the nutcracker the primary dispersal agent. Obligate mutualism.
- **Root Cause:** The nutcracker's specialized sublingual pouch, long bill, and extraordinary spatial memory enable seed extraction, transport, and caching at scale. The mutualism evolved over millennia.
- **Recommendation:** Document the obligate mutualism. Note the crisis facing whitebark pine (blister rust, beetles, climate change). The decline of either species threatens the other.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Whitebark pine mutualism. Obligate. 98,000 seeds/year. Crisis documented. Track.

## Structural Event 3: Seed Caching at Scale -- 98,000 Seeds Per Year

- **Situation:** The nutcracker's caching behavior is the most extreme of any corvid in the engine. 98,000 seeds/year. Sublingual pouch carries 150 seeds. Transport distance up to 22 km. Thousands of caches.
- **Root Cause:** Enlarged hippocampus for spatial memory. Specialized sublingual pouch. Preferential cache placement on south-facing slopes for early snow-melt recovery.
- **Recommendation:** Document as the most extreme caching behavior in the engine. Compare with Gray Jay (104, thousands in tree bark) and Raven (100, hundreds in ground). An order of magnitude beyond other cachers.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- 98,000 seeds/year. Sublingual pouch. Greatest spatial memory in corvid family. Track.
