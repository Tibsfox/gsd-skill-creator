## THE SOUND: SHACK-SHACK-SHACK RAUCOUS ALARM

### The Alarm Call

The Steller's Jay's **"shack-shack-shack"** alarm call is one of the most recognizable sounds in Pacific Northwest conifer forests [1, 2]:

- **Character:** Harsh, rasping, metallic staccato bursts.
- **Structure:** Series of 3-8 rapid harsh notes, each 0.1-0.2 seconds long.
- **Frequency:** Broad-spectrum, centered around 2,500-4,000 Hz. Not tonal -- noisy, penetrating.
- **Amplitude:** E=5. One of the loudest sounds in the conifer forest. Projects across hundreds of meters of dense canopy.
- **Rate:** 4-8 notes per second during intense alarm. Rate increases with threat proximity.
- **Timbre:** Harsh. Rasping. Metallic. Penetrating. Designed to startle and alert.

### The Sentinel Function

The Steller's Jay is the **sentinel species** of PNW conifer forests [1, 2]:

- **Community broadcast:** Other forest species (Douglas Squirrel, small passerines, deer) respond to the jay's alarm calls as reliable predator warnings.
- **Multi-level protocol:** Different alarm patterns for different threat types. Aerial predators (hawks, owls) provoke one alarm pattern; terrestrial threats (cats, weasels) provoke another.
- **Mobbing behavior:** Jays gather and mob detected predators, calling continuously to alert the forest community and harass the predator.
- **Hawk mimicry as deception:** Steller's Jays mimic Red-tailed Hawk calls to scatter competitors from food sources -- using the alarm system deceptively [2].

## CONIFER FOREST HABITAT

The Steller's Jay is the **dominant corvid of the conifer canopy** [1]:

- **Habitat:** Douglas fir, western red cedar, western hemlock, Sitka spruce forests. Sea level to timberline.
- **Niche:** The corvid of the closed canopy -- contrasts with the American Crow (open/urban) and Common Raven (open forest/cliffs).
- **Range:** Year-round resident throughout the Pacific Northwest conifer belt.
- **Food:** Omnivorous. Seeds, nuts, insects, berries, eggs, nestlings. Caches food like other corvids.
- **Intelligence:** Problem-solving, tool use, social learning. Corvid intelligence confirmed for Steller's Jay populations.

### Conifer Canopy Community

The Steller's Jay shares the conifer canopy with the **Douglas Squirrel** (degree 80). Both are vocal, territorial canopy species. The squirrel's territorial chatter and the jay's alarm call are the two most common sounds in PNW conifer forests. Together they form the acoustic foundation of the conifer canopy soundscape.
