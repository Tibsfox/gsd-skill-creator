## Purpose

This is the eleventh release of the PNW 360 Species engine and the close of the **first decade** (degrees 0--10). The Bushtit introduces **four new structural dimensions** to the circle: (1) the minimum viable signal -- a 1-bit contact call that encodes only presence; (2) swarm intelligence -- the first species whose primary significance is collective behavior, not individual vocalization; (3) the noise floor -- the acoustic threshold where signal barely exceeds environmental noise; (4) a parallel communication network -- the forest now has two simultaneous communication systems (alarm broadcast and swarm cohesion) operating alongside each other.

The bushtit is the **minimum of everything**: minimum size (2.8--3.1 inches, 4--6 g), minimum volume (barely audible), minimum information (1 bit per call), minimum energy (energy 1). But the flock behavior is **maximum coordination**: 30+ birds moving as a self-organizing superorganism through three-dimensional space without a leader, without complex signals, without any individual directing the collective. This is the Amiga Principle's most extreme biological expression in the species engine.

## Four Structural Advances

### 1. Minimum Viable Signal: The Information Floor

The bushtit's contact call encodes **1 bit of information**: present/absent. "I am here." That is all. No predator encoding (chickadee, degrees 3/9). No frequency modulation (wren, degree 1). No amplitude modulation (thrush, degree 2). No pitch contour (flycatcher, degree 6). No harmonic structure (hermit thrush, degree 7). No combinatorial syntax. No referential content. Just presence.

This is the **Shannon information floor**: the minimum non-zero unit of information (1 bit). The bushtit proves that this minimum is sufficient for complex collective behavior. The lesson is profound: you do not need complex communication to produce complex behavior. You need **enough communicators**. Information per signal is less important than the number of simultaneous signals.

### 2. Swarm Intelligence: The First Collective Species

Every previous species has been analyzed as an **individual vocalist** -- one bird, one song, one acoustic strategy. The bushtit cannot be understood as an individual. Its call is meaningless in isolation. A single bushtit producing a single "tsit" in empty space communicates nothing. The call only has function when produced by many individuals simultaneously, each monitoring neighbors, each adjusting position. The bushtit flock is the meaningful unit -- the superorganism -- and the call is the flock's coordination protocol, not an individual's expression.

This is the first time the circle has analyzed a species where the **collective is the instrument** and the individual is merely a component. The wren is a soloist. The chickadee is a broadcaster. The bushtit is a node in a swarm.

### 3. The Noise Floor: Where Signal Meets Noise

The bushtit's call lives at the boundary between signal and noise. It is barely audible. It is easily confused with environmental sound. An observer standing near a bushtit flock may hear nothing, or may hear a faint insect-like sibilance indistinguishable from wind or crickets. The call does not project. It does not carry. It exists only at close range, within the flock.

This is the **noise floor** of the species engine. Degree 0 is silence (no signal). Degree 10 is the first sound above silence that barely qualifies as communication. The noise floor is not a failure -- it is a threshold. Below it: silence. Above it: the beginning of everything. The bushtit marks that threshold.

### 4. Parallel Communication Network: Two Systems

The forest now has **two simultaneous communication networks**:

| Network | Mediating Species | Signal | Content | Volume | Range |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Alarm broadcast** | Chickadees (degrees 3, 9) | A-B-C-D combinatorial calls | Referential threat information | Loud, carrying | 30+ meters |
| **Swarm cohesion** | Bushtit (degree 10) | Contact seep | Presence only (1 bit) | Barely audible | A few meters |

The two networks operate simultaneously in mixed-species winter flocks. The chickadees provide the alarm infrastructure. The bushtits provide the cohesion infrastructure. Different signals, different content, different volumes, different ranges, different functions. The forest has depth in its communication architecture.

## The Bud Shank Parallel: Quiet Jazz at the Noise Floor

The Part A parallel at degree 10 maps to **Bud Shank** from S36. The parallel operates on four axes:

1. **Volume as aesthetic.** Shank's alto and flute work is defined by its quietness -- the opposite of hard bop. The bushtit is defined by its quietness -- the opposite of the carrying alarm call. Both operate at the noise floor of their respective domains.
2. **West Coast specificity.** Shank was Pacific Jazz Records, the sound of the West Coast. The bushtit is the backyard bird of the Pacific coast. Both are thoroughly western, thoroughly local.
3. **Ensemble over soloist.** Cool jazz values the collective sound. The bushtit flock values the collective movement. Neither privileges the individual over the group.
4. **Restraint as signal.** What is NOT done is as important as what IS done. Shank does not overplay. The bushtit does not over-signal. The space is the message.

## What Worked

### 1. The minimum viable signal concept is the strongest new theoretical contribution since variation form

The insight that 1 bit is sufficient for swarm intelligence -- that complex collective behavior does not require complex individual communication -- is a genuine theoretical finding that connects bioacoustics to information theory, cellular automata, and distributed computing. This is not analogy. It is isomorphism: the same mathematical structure (emergence from simple local rules) operating in multiple domains.

### 2. The noise floor position perfectly complements the silence at degree 0

Degree 0 was silence -- absence. Degree 10 is the noise floor -- the first presence above absence. Together they bracket the threshold where nothing becomes something. The engine now has both endpoints: the zero (silence) and the epsilon (minimum signal). The space between them is the noise floor. The bushtit lives in that space.

### 3. The swarm intelligence mapping to cellular automata is technically rigorous

Conway's Game of Life, Wolfram's Rule 110, Reynolds' boids algorithm -- all three are properly cited and structurally parallel. The bushtit flock genuinely satisfies the definition of a self-organizing system: simple local rules, no centralized coordination, complex emergent behavior. This is not metaphor. It is classification.

### 4. The parallel communication network concept adds architectural depth

Moving from one network (the alarm broadcast) to two parallel networks (alarm + swarm cohesion) is a genuine structural advance. The two networks have different properties (referential vs. non-referential, loud vs. quiet, long-range vs. short-range) and serve different functions. The forest's communication architecture now has layers.

### 5. The Part A parallel with Bud Shank achieves biographical specificity

Pacific Jazz Records, West Coast sound, quiet aesthetics, ensemble over soloist -- four resonance axes with genuine biographical grounding in Shank's career and artistic identity. This is stronger than the more conceptual parallels at some previous degrees.

### 6. The Amiga Principle finds its most extreme expression

The instruction text identified this as key: "This is the Amiga Principle's most extreme expression in the species engine." The release delivers on this. Simpler individual signal is not possible (1 bit). More complex collective behavior from simpler components is difficult to find in vertebrate biology. The bushtit flock is the biological minimum of the Amiga Principle.

## What Could Be Better

### 1. STILL no high-energy species (now 11 degrees without one above energy 2)

This is now the **single most overdue structural gap** in the circle. Eleven degrees. Two energy levels (1 and 2). No energy 3, 4, or 5. The dynamic range still has no ceiling. Degree 11 MUST introduce a high-energy species. A Steller's Jay, Pileated Woodpecker, or Bald Eagle -- something loud, something carrying, something that breaks the energy-2 barrier. This gap is no longer a note -- it is an architectural deficiency that urgently needs correction.

### 2. Still only one non-passerine (the heron at degree 0)

Ten of eleven species are passerines. The taxonomic diversity of the circle is narrow. A woodpecker (Piciformes), raptor (Accipitriformes), or seabird (Charadriiformes) would broaden the foundation.

### 3. Still no marine or shoreline species

"The Sound of Puget Sound" has spent eleven degrees entirely on land. Not a single species of the Sound itself -- no gull, no alcid, no shorebird, no marine mammal sound. This is a conspicuous absence.

### 4. The bushtit's role in mixed-species flocks could be explored more deeply

The release documents the bushtit's participation in mixed-species winter flocks but does not fully develop the interaction dynamics between the alarm network and the swarm cohesion network when both are operating simultaneously in the same flock. How do bushtits respond to chickadee alarm calls? Do they freeze, scatter, or tighten their flock spacing? Future releases should explore this interaction.

### 5. No experimental verification of the 1-bit claim

The characterization of the bushtit call as "1 bit: present/absent" is a theoretical interpretation, not an experimentally demonstrated result. No controlled playback experiment has tested whether bushtit contact calls carry any graded information beyond presence. The call may encode subtler information (identity, age, sex, motivational state) that spectrographic analysis alone cannot detect. This should be acknowledged as an inference.
