## Discoveries

1. **The Cougar-Wolf encounter is an underappreciated acoustic event in PNW biology documentation.** Most Pacific Northwest wildlife acoustics literature focuses on single-species vocalization studies. Dual-apex inter-predator encounter acoustics are rarely systematically documented. The engine is recording something that the field of wildlife acoustics treats as incidental.
2. **The asymmetry of the encounter (Cougar typically initiates; Wolf pack responds collectively) is acoustically significant.** The Cougar is a solitary apex predator producing individual threat vocalizations. The Wolf responds as a collective (pack-present responses are louder and more complex than solo-Wolf responses). The encounter pairs solitary felid acoustics with collective canid acoustics: different acoustic social structures colliding at the boundary.
3. **Washington State Wolf packs (Smackout, Togo, Wedge, Sherman) are documented into specific territories that overlap established Cougar range.** The encounters are not random -- they occur at predictable geographic interfaces (ridgeline saddles, riparian corridors). The engine's geographic specificity (northeast Washington, Okanogan Highlands) is correct based on pack territory documentation.
