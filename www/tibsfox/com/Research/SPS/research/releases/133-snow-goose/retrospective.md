## Structural Event 3: Blue Morph and Conservation Irony

- **Situation:** The blue morph (single-gene polymorphism) and conservation irony (population explosion destroying Arctic habitat) are the Snow Goose's most distinctive biological and ecological features.
- **Root Cause:** The blue morph represents one of the best-documented examples of simple Mendelian genetics in wild birds. The conservation irony -- agricultural food subsidies driving population explosion driving Arctic habitat destruction -- is one of the most instructive cases in conservation biology.
- **Recommendation:** Document both as advanced theory nodes. These are the Snow Goose's most significant contributions beyond the acoustic.

## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **E=5 is defensible.** The flock scale (10,000-50,000+) is uniquely large among PNW geese. The acoustic impact at close range is well-documented as overwhelming. E=5 matches Canada Goose (129, flock-driven) without reaching E=6 (reserved for the Trumpeter Swan's individual vocal power).

2. **The Skagit Valley spectacle is extremely well-documented.** WDFW surveys, Skagit Audubon Society data, eBird hotspots, and tourism media all confirm the flock sizes and visual/acoustic descriptions.

3. **The blue morph genetics are well-established.** Cooke & Rockwell (1988) and subsequent genetic studies confirm the single-gene, incomplete-dominance model.

4. **The conservation irony is supported by peer-reviewed literature.** Abraham, Jefferies & Alisauskas (2005) and others document the Arctic habitat destruction. The Light Goose Conservation Order is a federal policy response.

5. **The goose quintet acoustic comparison (129-133) extends cleanly from the quartet (129-132).**

### Source Quality

6. **Snow Goose is one of the most studied waterfowl in North America.** Extensive literature from ornithology, genetics, conservation biology, and wildlife management. Source base is excellent.
