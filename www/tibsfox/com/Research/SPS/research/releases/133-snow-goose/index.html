Shrill Dissonant Flock Call — E=5

The Snow Goose’s individual call is a high-pitched, nasal bark — “honk” or “whouk” at 600–4,000 Hz. Shrill, somewhat harsh. But multiply by tens of thousands and you get one of the most overwhelming avian sounds in the PNW: a continuous, roaring, dissonant wall of sound. Mass liftoffs — tens of thousands of wingbeats plus collective shrieking — are physically immersive, audible for more than a mile across the flat Skagit Valley.

White and Blue Morphs

White morph: entirely white with black wingtips, pink bill with black “grinning patch.” The classic Snow Goose. Blue morph: dark gray to blue-gray body with white head — once considered a separate species (“Blue Goose”). Controlled by a single gene with incomplete dominance. Blue morphs are uncommon but present in Skagit Valley flocks — a birding prize among the white mass. caerulescens = “becoming blue” — the species was named from the dark form first.

Conservation Irony — Too Many Geese

Saved from overhunting by conservation measures. Now 15 million+ birds — populations exploded from agricultural food subsidies. The exploding population is destroying Arctic breeding habitat — overgrazing tundra to bare mud, causing “eat-outs” that take decades to recover. The 1999 Light Goose Conservation Order liberalized hunting to reduce numbers. A conservation success that became an ecological crisis.

Sixteenth Consecutive Anatidae (118–133)

Sixteen consecutive. E=5 breaks the E=4 plateau (130–132) through sheer flock scale. The goose era reaches five entries (129–133). One more goose remains: Ross’s Goose at 134 (the FINAL Anatidae!). The streak spans ducks (118–126), swans (127–128), Branta geese (129–131), and Anser geese (132–133). Nearly doubled the Picidae record (9).
