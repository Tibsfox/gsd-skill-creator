## What Worked

1. **The ancient fish arc (243-244) is the engine's most evolutionarily dramatic consecutive pairing.** Pacific Lamprey at 450 MYA (E=1, jawless) followed by White Sturgeon at 200 MYA (E=4, armored cartilaginous). Two living fossils, two consecutive degrees, two species that have survived everything the Earth has thrown at them.
2. **The Celilo Falls triple loss is the entry's most important cross-reference.** Pacific Salmon (236), Pacific Lamprey (243), and White Sturgeon (244) all had tribal fishing traditions destroyed by The Dalles Dam in 1957. The engine has now documented this triple loss across three entries spanning eight degrees. One dam; three species; irreplaceable cultural losses.
3. **The century-scale life is the entry's most philosophically resonant biological fact.** A 100-year-old White Sturgeon was born in 1926. It has experienced the entire industrialization of the Columbia River within one individual's lifetime. This framing makes the abstract history concrete.
4. **The global sturgeon crisis context is important.** By documenting that 50%+ of worldwide sturgeon species are critically endangered, the entry frames the Columbia River White Sturgeon not as a species without conservation concerns but as a relative success story in a globally devastated family.

## Discoveries

1. **The breach behavior's cause is incompletely understood.** Proposed explanations include parasite removal (lamprey are known parasites of sturgeon -- a real cross-reference between degrees 243 and 244), spawning communication, and buoyancy adjustment. The engine documents honest scientific uncertainty.
2. **The reservoir fragmentation creates independent population genetics over time.** Each dam pool contains a population that cannot exchange genes with adjacent pools. Over multiple generations, this will create distinct subpopulations adapted to the specific conditions of their reservoir. This is evolution on a human timescale, visible within the lifespan of individual sturgeon.
3. **Don Gallucci's age (14-15 at the time of "Louie Louie") creates a remarkable pairing subtext.** The keyboard player on the most famous garage rock song was a teenager; the White Sturgeon he pairs with (via the engine pairing at 244) can live over 100 years and weigh as much as a car. Youth and ancient age, fame and obscurity, speed and patience.
