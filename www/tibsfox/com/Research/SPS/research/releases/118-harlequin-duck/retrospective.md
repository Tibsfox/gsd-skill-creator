## Structural Event 2: Commedia Dell'Arte Naming -- Theater in Taxonomy

- **Situation:** The Harlequin Duck is named after Harlequin (Arlecchino) of Italian commedia dell'arte. Scientific name Histrionicus histrionicus = "actor actor" (Latin histrio = stage player). The most theatrical naming in North American ornithology.
- **Root Cause:** The male's plumage is so elaborately patterned -- geometric marks that look painted on -- that taxonomists recognized the theatrical quality and named it accordingly. Nature imitating art. The intersection of natural history and performance art.
- **Recommendation:** Document the commedia dell'arte connection in full. Note the etymological richness: histrio, Arlecchino, the costumed trickster character. This is unique cultural content in the engine.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Commedia dell'arte naming documented. Theater in taxonomy. Unique.

## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The painted player concept accurately captures the species' visual and cultural significance.** The commedia dell'arte connection is well-established in ornithological literature. The plumage description is accurate and vivid. Verified.

2. **E=4 is defensible.** The Harlequin's calls are thin and high-pitched -- carrying over turbulent water by frequency placement rather than volume. E=4 correctly reflects moderate energy: audible in chaos but not dominating it. Lower than the dipper's E=5 (which must out-sing a river with sustained, powerful song).

3. **The turbulent water specialization is well-documented.** Mountain stream breeding + rocky coast wintering. Dual-habitat lifecycle. Turbulence at every stage. Cornell Lab and Robertson & Goudie 1999 provide comprehensive documentation.

4. **The habitat overlap with American Dipper is ecologically significant.** Both breed on the same mountain streams. Both forage for the same invertebrates (caddisfly, stonefly, mayfly larvae). The connection is real and meaningful.

5. **Cross-references to SPS-116 (Dipper song), SPS-117 (Dipper dunking), SPS-115 (Black-backed WP), SPS-021 (Flicker), and SPS-004 (Spotted Owl) are all defensible.**

### Source Quality

6. **Harlequin Duck ecology is well-documented.** Robertson & Goudie 1999 is the definitive reference. Cornell Lab of Ornithology provides comprehensive life history. Macaulay Library has audio recordings. Strong source base.
