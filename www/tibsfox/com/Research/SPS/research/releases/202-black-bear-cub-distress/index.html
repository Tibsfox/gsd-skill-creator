Black Bear Cub Distress

Ursus americanus (Ursidae) — E=4 — Bear Trio 3/3
Third and final Black Bear entry. Bear trio completes (200–202). High, mewling “meeew, meeew” at 300–800 Hz. Cub separated from sow. Summer forest. E=4 — infant vulnerability. The inversion: adult intimidation gives way to infant need.

The Distress Call — High Mewling Squall

“Meeew, meeew.” High, thin, sustained. 300–800 Hz. Repeated every 2–5 seconds. The cub has lost sight of its mother. The mew brings her running — crashing through brush with urgent intensity. Carries 50–150 meters. Enough for the sow. E=4.

The Inversion — Bear Trio Completes

200: Huff (E=5) — “I know you’re there.” 201: Jaw-pop (E=6) — “Back off. Now.” 202: Cub distress (E=4) — “Where are you, mom?” Escalation then inversion. E=6 to E=4. Adult dominance gives way to infant vulnerability. Three voices. One species. Complete.

Maternal Response — The Most Dangerous Encounter

A mewing bear cub on a trail means the sow is nearby. She will respond to the cub’s distress with defensive aggression. This is the highest-risk bear encounter for hikers. Leave immediately. Do not approach the cub. The sow is coming.
