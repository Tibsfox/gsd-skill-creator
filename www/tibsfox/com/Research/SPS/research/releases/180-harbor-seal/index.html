First Marine Mammal — The Engine Enters the Ocean

For 179 degrees, the SPS engine documented primarily birds. At degree 180, the engine enters the marine mammal realm. The Harbor Seal is the most common marine mammal in the PNW — 30,000–40,000 in Washington State. Resident year-round. Protected under the Marine Mammal Protection Act (1972). The taxonomic scope of the engine expands dramatically at the midpoint.

The Threshold — Between Land and Sea

The haul-out behavior is living at the threshold. On rocks: a terrestrial animal, breathing air, warming in the sun. In water: a sleek, powerful predator, diving to 500+ meters, holding breath for 25+ minutes. The same animal in two worlds. At degree 180 — the midpoint — the Harbor Seal embodies the boundary between what was and what comes next.

The Liquid Gaze — Large Dark Eyes

Disproportionately large, dark eyes adapted for dim underwater light. The round face, the whiskers, the expression humans read as curiosity — the “liquid gaze” of a harbor seal surfacing near a kayak is one of the most compelling wildlife encounters in the PNW. Thick vibrissae (whiskers) detect fish by water turbulence.
