Gray Whale Blow — Powerful Explosive Blow

Eschrichtius robustus (Eschrichtiidae) — E=5 — FIRST ESCHRICHTIIDAE
FIRST ESCHRICHTIIDAE — a monotypic family (one species, one family). Longest mammal migration: 10,000–12,000 miles/year, Alaska to Mexico. V-shaped blow visible from shore. Benthic suction feeder. 13 consecutive marine mammals (180–192). 7 families in the streak.

The Blow — Powerful and Visible

A forceful exhalation at up to 300 mph. V-shaped or heart-shaped spout, 3–4 meters tall. Audible from hundreds of meters over water. Because Gray Whales migrate within 1–5 miles of shore, the blow is often the first sign — heard before the whale is seen. The most audible cetacean from PNW land. E=5.

Longest Mammal Migration — Alaska to Mexico

10,000–12,000 miles round trip per year. Arctic feeding grounds to Baja California calving lagoons. Migration follows the PNW coast closely. Southbound: Oct–Jan. Northbound: Mar–Jun. PCFG: ~250 whales feed along the PNW coast in summer instead of continuing to the Arctic. The long road.

Marine Mammal Era — 13 Consecutive — 7 Families

13 consecutive marine mammals (degrees 180–192). 7 families: Phocidae, Otariidae, Mustelidae, Phocoenidae, Delphinidae, Balaenopteridae, Eschrichtiidae. Energy range: E=1 to E=8. 5 feeding strategies: hauling, cracking, echolocation, lunge feeding, benthic suction.
