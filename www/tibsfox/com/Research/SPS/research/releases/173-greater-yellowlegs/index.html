Tringa melanoleuca (Scolopacidae) — E=5 — The Sentinel
SIXTH AND FINAL SCOLOPACIDAE — sentinel species, first to call alarm. Loud, ringing “klew-klew-klew” carries across the entire tidal flat. Straight bill returns. E=5. v1.49.313.

The Sentinel — First to Call Alarm

The Greater Yellowlegs is the sentinel of the shorebird community. First to detect threats. First to call. Its ringing “klew-klew-klew” triggers flight in every shorebird on the flat — Dunlin flocks (169), godwits (172), curlews (171). One bird’s alarm becomes the community’s alarm. The loud solo voice.

Scolopacidae Streak Complete — Six Entries (168–173)

Six consecutive Scolopacidae. Bill morphology circle: straight (168) → decurved (169) → down-curved (170) → extremely down-curved (171) → upturned (172) → straight again (173). The circle closes. Six calls. Six energies. Longest family streak in the engine.
