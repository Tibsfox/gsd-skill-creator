Cerorhinca monocerata (Alcidae) — E=4 — Nocturnal Horn-Bill
SECOND ALCIDAE — nocturnal colony visitor. Horn-like casque at bill base. Burrow nester. Eerie grunting/moaning chorus at night. Protection Island: 36,000 pairs. E=4. v1.49.315.

Nocturnal Colony — The Night Shift

Returns to the colony only at night. Carries fish in total darkness. Navigates to the correct burrow among thousands. The darkness is protection — avoiding Bald Eagles, Peregrine Falcons, and gulls that hunt by sight. After dark, the colony comes alive with eerie grunting and moaning — an otherworldly chorus.

The Rhinoceros Horn — Bill Casque

A vertical, horn-shaped casque of keratinized tissue at the bill base. Breeding adults only. Orange bill with white facial plumes. The name “Rhinoceros” comes directly from this structure. Cerorhinca from Greek keras (horn) + rhynchos (bill).

Protection Island

Washington’s Protection Island (Strait of Juan de Fuca) hosts ~36,000 nesting pairs — one of the largest colonies in the world. National Wildlife Refuge. Burrows dug 1–5 meters into island soil. Strict island-breeder — requires predator-free islands with deep soil.
