## Structural Event 3: Cavity Nesting Chain -- Picidae to Anatidae

- **Situation:** The Wood Duck is an obligate cavity nester, as is the Hooded Merganser (degree 120). Both depend on cavities created by woodpeckers (Picidae, degrees 107-115). The ecological chain is: fire creates snags, woodpeckers excavate cavities, waterfowl nest in them.
- **Root Cause:** The engine has traced an ecological succession from fire (degree 115) through cavity creation (Picidae nonet, 107-115) through cavity occupation (Anatidae streak, 118-121). This is the longest ecological chain in the engine.
- **Recommendation:** Document the Picidae-Anatidae cavity chain as a major engine pattern. The chain spans 15 degrees (107-121).
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Cavity chain documented. 15-degree ecological succession (107-121).

## Structural Event 4: Conservation Success -- Nest Boxes

- **Situation:** The Wood Duck is one of the greatest conservation success stories in North American waterfowl management. Nest box programs reversed severe population decline.
- **Root Cause:** The Wood Duck's cavity-nesting requirement made it uniquely susceptible to habitat loss (mature forest destruction) and uniquely responsive to habitat restoration (nest boxes). The conservation story is genuine and significant.
- **Recommendation:** Document the conservation success as a theory node. The lesson: providing habitat infrastructure (artificial cavities) can reverse population declines.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Conservation success documented. Nest box programs.
