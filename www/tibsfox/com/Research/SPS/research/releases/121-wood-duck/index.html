The Most Beautiful Duck in North America

The male Wood Duck in breeding plumage: iridescent green-to-purple head with a swept-back crest, two thin white racing stripes from bill to nape, bright red eyes, chestnut-maroon breast with white speckles, warm buff flanks with vermiculated patterns. Every surface carries color, pattern, or iridescence. The species name sponsa means “betrothed” — dressed for a wedding. An argument for extravagance in nature.

Anatidae Streak — Four Consecutive Waterfowl

118: Harlequin Duck (turbulent stream, E=5) → 119: Common Merganser (river, E=4) → 120: Hooded Merganser (wooded pond, E=4) → 121: Wood Duck (wooded pond, E=4). Four consecutive Anatidae. Four genera: Histrionicus, Mergus, Lophodytes, Aix. The streak deepens. The wooded pond holds for a second degree.

Cavity Nesting — The Leap of Faith

Obligate cavity nester — nests in tree cavities created by large woodpeckers or natural decay. Also uses nest boxes — the Wood Duck nest box program is one of the greatest conservation success stories in North American waterfowl. Ducklings hatch in the cavity and at one day old, leap to the ground from heights of 15+ meters. The “leap of faith”: 20-gram downy ducklings bouncing off the ground on their mother’s call.

The Rising Ooo-Eek Flight Call

A sharp, rising, squealed “ooo-eek!” — thin, high-pitched, ascending. 1,500–5,000 Hz, sweeping upward over 0.3–0.5 seconds. Plaintive, almost electronic in quality. Primarily an in-flight contact call that penetrates forest canopy. The female’s call is louder and more emphatic. Not a quack or honk — a squealing whistle. E=4: piercing and carrying despite moderate volume. Same energy as the Hooded Merganser (120), but radically different frequency profile — high rising squeal vs. low rolling frog-call.

Genus Aix — Two Beautiful Ducks

Only two living species: Wood Duck (Aix sponsa, North America) and Mandarin Duck (Aix galericulata, East Asia). Both renowned for extraordinary plumage. Both cavity nesters. Both perching ducks. A biogeographic mirror: parallel species on two continents, both evolved through sexual selection, both cultural symbols for centuries.
