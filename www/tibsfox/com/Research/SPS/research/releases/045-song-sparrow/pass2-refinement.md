The Song Sparrow introduces Passerellidae — the New World sparrows — to the engine. It is *Melospiza melodia*, "melody song-finch," the bird whose scientific name says "song" twice. The most studied songbird in North America: Margaret Morse Nice tracked individual Song Sparrows across their entire lives in her Columbus, Ohio backyard from 1928 to 1943, inventing modern population ecology methodology. Peter Marler used Song Sparrows to establish the foundational principles of avian vocal learning. The species is to ornithology what *Drosophila* is to genetics — the model organism, exhaustively documented, the reference against which all other songbirds are measured. Its structured song always opens with 2-4 clear introductory notes (the preamble) before launching into a complex body of trills and buzzy phrases. Energy drops to E=2 — the gentlest approach in 31 degrees.

Surveyor 1 achieved the first American soft landing on the Moon on June 2, 1966, setting down in Oceanus Procellarum and transmitting 11,237 photographs of the lunar surface back to Earth. Before Surveyor, no American spacecraft had landed gently on anything. Every previous lunar attempt — the Ranger series — was a crash. Surveyor 1 did the opposite: it approached slowly, fired retrorockets, and touched down at walking speed. The Song Sparrow's E=2 is the engine's own soft landing — after 31 degrees at E=3 and E=4, the energy level drops to the gentlest register. The Song Sparrow does not crash into the soundscape. It alights.

The documentation parallel is the deepest structural alignment. Surveyor 1's 11,237 photographs were an exhaustive survey — not of the whole Moon, but of one small patch of regolith, catalogued frame by frame, rock by rock, shadow by shadow. Margaret Morse Nice's Song Sparrow studies were the same kind of work: not a survey of all birds, but an exhaustive life-history catalog of one species in one backyard, individual by individual, territory by territory, song by song. Both Surveyor 1 and the Song Sparrow represent the transition from impact to study — from crashing through a domain to settling down and documenting it completely. The Song Sparrow is the bird that stays in one place and lets science come to it. Surveyor 1 is the spacecraft that stayed in one place and let the camera do the rest. Exhaustive documentation from a fixed position. The soft landing as a prerequisite for seeing clearly.
