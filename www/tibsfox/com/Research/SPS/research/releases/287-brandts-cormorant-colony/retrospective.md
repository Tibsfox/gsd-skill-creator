## What Worked

1. **Opening Phalacrocoracidae with the colony rather than the individual correctly captures the acoustic phenomenon.** Brandt's Cormorant's significance in the engine is the colonial sound mass -- not the individual call. This mirrors the storm-petrel colony choice at degree 286.
2. **The blue gular pouch as honest signal is the correct primary biological frame.** The blue pouch is both the diagnostic identification mark and the central courtship mechanism. Female choice based on pouch brightness is one of the cleaner honest signal examples in Pacific Northwest wildlife.
3. **El Niño sensitivity is the ecology story.** No other PNW seabird is as tightly coupled to California Current upwelling. This is the species to cite when explaining how oceanographic conditions translate directly to seabird breeding success.
4. **The 287-288 Phalacrocoracidae chapter structure is correct.** Outer coast specialist (287) followed by inland generalist (288) documents the full range of the Pacific Northwest cormorant ecology in two consecutive entries.

## Discoveries

1. **The genus change from Phalacrocorax to Urile reflects recent molecular work.** *Urile penicillatus* is correct current taxonomy. The engine documents this change rather than using the older name.
2. **Georg von Brandt (1799-1868) named several Pacific Northwest species.** The naming history of Pacific coast wildlife runs heavily through 18th-19th century European naturalists accessing Russian expedition collections. Brandt is one of the prominent names in this tradition.
