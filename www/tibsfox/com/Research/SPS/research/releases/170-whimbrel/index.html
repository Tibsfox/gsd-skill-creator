Whimbrel — Rippling Curlew Call

Numenius phaeopus (Scolopacidae) — E=4 — The Hemispheric Voyager
THIRD SCOLOPACIDAE — first curlew in the engine. Down-curved bill (7–9 cm). Rippling 7-note call: “pi-pi-pi-pi-pi-pi-pi” — one of the most diagnostic shorebird calls. One of Earth’s great migrants. E=4. v1.49.310.

First Curlew — Down-Curved Bill Enters

7–9 cm, strongly down-curved. Adapted for reaching into crevices and crab burrows. The first curlew bill in the engine. Bill morphology progression: straight (snipe, 168) > slightly decurved (Dunlin, 169) > down-curved (Whimbrel, 170). At degree 171, the Long-billed Curlew arrives with the longest bill of any North American shorebird — up to 21 cm.

Incredible Migration — Hemispheric Voyager

One of the most widely distributed shorebirds on Earth. Breeds on Arctic tundra, winters in the tropics. Individual Whimbrels fly 4,000–8,000+ km. Satellite-tracked birds have made 5,000 km nonstop transoceanic flights. Year after year, the same birds return to the same stopover sites.
