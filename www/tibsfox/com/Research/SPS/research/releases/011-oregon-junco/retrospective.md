## Purpose

This is the twelfth release of the PNW 360 Species engine and the opening of the **second decade** (degrees 11--20). The Oregon Junco introduces **three new structural dimensions** to the circle: (1) the trill as tremolo -- rapid single-note repetition above the temporal fusion threshold, creating the perception of a continuous tone from discrete pulses (the analog-to-digital boundary in biological acoustics); (2) contested taxonomy as a subject of research -- the lumping/splitting history of the junco complex makes classification itself a lesson, not just a label; (3) edge ecology as musical threshold -- the forest-edge habitat maps to the cadence point, the trill to the cadential ornament, the ecotone to the transition between sections.

The Oregon Junco's trill creates the deepest cross-reference in the circle: the **continuous vs. discrete** connection to the Varied Thrush (degree 2). Both produce sounds perceived as sustained tones at a single pitch. But the thrush's tone is genuinely continuous (analog -- solid spectrogram line). The junco's trill is structurally discrete (digital -- row of individual dashes perceived as continuous above the fusion threshold). Same output, opposite architecture. This is the Nyquist-Shannon sampling theorem demonstrated in biology.

## Three Structural Advances

### 1. The Trill as Analog-to-Digital Boundary

The junco's trill creates discrete pulses that are perceived as a continuous tone. This is:
- **Tremolo** in music (rapid bow strokes perceived as sustained note)
- **Persistence of vision** in film (24 discrete frames perceived as continuous motion)
- **PWM dimming** in LEDs (on/off flashes perceived as steady light)
- **AM radio** (carrier on/off switching perceived as audio tone)
- **Nyquist-Shannon sampling theorem** (discrete samples reconstructing continuous signal when sample rate exceeds 2x bandwidth)

The junco IS the biological demonstration of all of these principles. The trill rate is the sampling rate. The fusion threshold is the Nyquist limit. The perceived buzz is the reconstructed signal. This connects directly to BPS, LED, and every signal-processing domain in the research series.

### 2. Contested Taxonomy as Research Subject

Every previous species had an uncontested taxonomic identity. The Oregon Junco does not. It was a species until 1973, was lumped into the Dark-eyed Junco complex based on hybridization evidence, and molecular phylogenetics now suggests the lump may have been premature. The bird's identity is in flux.

This makes taxonomy itself a lesson: classification is a human decision applied to biological continua. Where you draw the line on a gradient depends on which criteria you prioritize. The junco complex is the species-concept problem made visible. The bird doesn't change. The label does.

### 3. Edge Ecology as Musical Threshold

The junco lives at the forest edge -- the ecotone where forest meets clearing. The trill in classical music ornaments the cadence point -- the note at the boundary between phrases. The mapping is structural, not metaphorical: the junco trills at the boundary between habitats, the trill ornaments the boundary between musical sections, and both serve as signals that something is transitioning.

This connects to MDB's threshold architecture and extends the edge ecology thread from the bushtit (degree 10). The bushtit occupied the shrub canopy of the edge. The junco occupies the ground layer. Both are edge species. They partition the ecotone vertically.

## The Joe Brazil Parallel: The Neighborhood Edge

The Part A parallel at degree 11 maps to **Joe Brazil** from S36. The parallel operates on four axes:

1. **Edge-dwelling.** Brazil worked the boundary between the jazz club and the neighborhood -- not the touring circuit, not the living room, but the threshold between them. The junco works the boundary between the forest and the yard. Both are threshold figures.
2. **Community-embedded.** Brazil was a fixture -- the musician who was always there, always playing, always part of the local fabric. The junco is the most common backyard bird in Seattle -- always at the feeder, always trilling from the edge perch. Persistent local presence.
3. **Persistent presence.** Both define their locale through repeated, consistent presence. Brazil played the same venues year after year. The junco trills the same trill from the same edge year-round. Resident. Reliable. The sound of the place.
4. **Threshold signal.** Brazil's music carried between inside and outside. The junco's trill carries between forest and clearing. Both signals cross the boundary in both directions.

## What Worked

### 1. The continuous vs. discrete connection to degree 2 is the strongest cross-reference in the circle

The Varied Thrush (genuinely continuous tone) and Oregon Junco (discrete pulses perceived as continuous) are architecturally opposite species producing the same perceptual result. This is not analogy -- it is the same psychoacoustic principle (temporal fusion) demonstrated by its presence in one species and its absence in the other. The solid-line vs. dotted-line spectrogram comparison is immediately graspable and technically rigorous.

### 2. The trill-as-tremolo mapping connects biology to music, engineering, and signal processing simultaneously

Tremolo (music), persistence of vision (film/LED), AM radio (signal processing), and the Nyquist-Shannon theorem (information theory) all describe the same principle: discrete events above a fusion threshold are perceived as continuous. The junco's trill is the biological demonstration. Five domains, one principle, one bird.

### 3. The taxonomy section makes classification itself a subject of research

The Oregon Junco's lumping/splitting history is genuinely instructive about how species concepts work, how classification decisions are made, and how molecular phylogenetics is reshaping those decisions. This adds a dimension that no previous release has explored.

### 4. Edge ecology extends naturally from degree 10

The bushtit occupied the shrub canopy of the ecotone. The junco occupies the ground layer. The vertical partitioning of the edge habitat is a clean ecological thread that connects two consecutive degrees through shared habitat, different niches.

### 5. The return to mid-range frequency is structurally important

Degrees 8--10 were all in the ultra-high or noise-floor register (6--9 kHz). Degree 11 returns to 3--6 kHz -- comfortably within human hearing range. The circle needs register variety, and this return to accessible mid-range after three consecutive high-frequency degrees provides it.

### 6. Energy 2 maintains the gap but grounds the bird accessibly

Degree 11 does NOT address the still-overdue high-energy species. But the junco at energy 2 is appropriate for what it is -- a moderately voiced bird. The energy gap remains the single most pressing structural deficiency. Twelve degrees without a loud voice is now critical.

## What Could Be Better

### 1. STILL no high-energy species (now 12 degrees without one above energy 2 -- CRITICAL)

This is now a **critical architectural deficiency**. Twelve degrees. Two energy levels (1 and 2). No energy 3, 4, or 5. The dynamic range has no ceiling. The recommendation has been at the top of the list for four consecutive releases. Degree 12 MUST introduce a loud species. This is non-negotiable.

### 2. Still only one non-passerine (the heron at degree 0)

Eleven of twelve species are passerines. The taxonomic diversity is narrow. A woodpecker, raptor, or seabird would broaden the foundation significantly.

### 3. Still no marine or shoreline species

"The Sound of Puget Sound" has spent twelve degrees entirely on land. Twelve releases about a Sound with no Sound in them. A marine species is overdue.

### 4. Still no predator species

The alarm network has broadcasters (chickadees) and receivers (nuthatch, kinglet) but no trigger species -- the predator whose arrival causes the alarm. Adding a predator would complete the information loop.

### 5. The chip note deserves more development

The junco's "tick" chip note is a significant vocalization -- sharper and more individually distinctive than the bushtit's contact seep, potentially encoding more information than 1 bit. Future work should compare chip note structure across degrees to build a taxonomy of contact calls.
