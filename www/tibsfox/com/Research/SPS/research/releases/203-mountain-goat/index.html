Mountain Goat

Oreamnos americanus (Bovidae) — E=4 — FIRST BOVIDAE
FIRST BOVIDAE — the cattle/goat/sheep family enters. Not actually a goat — a goat-antelope related to chamois. Nasal “baaah” at 200–600 Hz. Alpine cliffs. Above treeline. E=4 — steady on stone. The highest-elevation mammal in the engine.

Not Actually a Goat — Taxonomic Misidentification

Oreamnos americanus — not in genus Capra (true goats). Closest relative: chamois (Rupicapra) of the European Alps. Only living species in its genus. Early naturalists called it a goat because it looks like a goat and lives like a goat. The name is a lie that describes the behavior. Convergent evolution, not shared lineage.

The Impossible Terrain — Cliff-Dwelling Adaptations

Split hooves with rubbery pads that grip rock like climbing shoes. Low center of gravity. Powerful shoulders. Dense double-layered wool — warmest coat in North America. Withstands -50°C and 150 km/h winds. Both sexes carry dagger-like horns (permanent, not shed). The cliff is the fortress: predators cannot follow.

The Bleat — Sound Across Stone

“Baaah.” Nasal, resonant, 200–600 Hz. Contact call across rocky terrain. The nasal quality cuts through wind. Rock surfaces reflect and amplify the sound. Carries 200–500 meters across alpine basins. E=4 — moderate sound, amplified by stone.
