*Degree 203 retrospective complete. MOUNTAIN GOAT at the two hundred third degree -- FIRST BOVIDAE. NOT ACTUALLY A GOAT. The cattle/goat/sheep family enters the engine with its most misidentified member. Oreamnos americanus -- the Mountain Goat -- is not in the genus Capra (true goats). It's a goat-antelope, the only living species in its genus, more closely related to the chamois of the European Alps than to any goat. Early naturalists called it a goat because it looks like a goat and lives like a goat. The name is a lie that happens to describe the behavior. Convergent evolution. In the Cascades, Mountain Goats occupy alpine and subalpine zones above treeline -- 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Split hooves with rubbery pads grip rock like climbing shoes. Dense double-layered wool withstands -50 C. Both sexes carry permanent dagger-like horns. The cliff is the fortress: predators cannot follow where the Mountain Goat walks. The nasal bleat -- "baaah" at 200-600 Hz -- carries across rocky basins, amplified by rock surfaces, cutting through constant alpine wind. E=4: moderate, steady, sufficient. Alpine acoustics extend the effective range. Across the degree pair, Tacocat -- palindrome punk pop defying genre labels. Misidentification and defiance. v1.49.344.*
