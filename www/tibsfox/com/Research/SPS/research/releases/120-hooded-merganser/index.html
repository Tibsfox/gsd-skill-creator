The Fan-Shaped Crest — Black and White Display

The male Hooded Merganser’s fan-shaped crest is one of the most dramatic features of any North American duck. When raised, it expands into a large, rounded fan — black at the edges with a triangular white center. The bird’s head appears to double in size. Courtship display, territorial signaling, alarm response. When relaxed, the crest lies flat — a completely different bird. The transformation is startling.

Anatidae Streak — Three Consecutive Waterfowl

118: Harlequin Duck (turbulent stream, E=5) → 119: Common Merganser (river, E=4) → 120: Hooded Merganser (wooded pond, E=4). Three consecutive Anatidae. Three ecosystems: rapids to river to pond. Three genera: Histrionicus, Mergus, Lophodytes. The water calms to stillness.

Water Culmination — From Torrent to Still Pond

The water ecosystem theme spans degrees 116–120: fast stream (dipper) → turbulent rapids (harlequin) → river (common merganser) → wooded pond (hooded merganser). Five degrees of progressive calming. The full hydrological spectrum from mountain torrent to woodland pool. The water is still. The engine has reached silence.

The Rolling Frog-Like Call

The Hooded Merganser produces one of the most unusual calls in North American duck taxonomy: a rolling, frog-like “crrroo” — a resonant, almost mechanical sound more like an amphibian than a bird. Low, rolling, guttural. 400–2,000 Hz. Primarily during courtship display. If you hear what sounds like a large frog from a wooded pond and see a duck, it is likely a Hooded Merganser. E=4: moderate, unusual quality over volume.

Smallest Merganser, Sole Genus

The Hooded Merganser is North America’s smallest merganser (450–880g, roughly half the Common Merganser’s mass) and the only member of genus Lophodytes. The generic separation reflects early evolutionary divergence from the Mergus mergansers. Smaller body, larger proportional crest, shorter bill, preference for wooded ponds over rivers. The sole genus representative at the one-third mark — taxonomically unique.
