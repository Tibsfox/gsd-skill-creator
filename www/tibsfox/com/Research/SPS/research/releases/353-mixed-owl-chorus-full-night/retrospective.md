## What Worked

1. **The four-phase temporal structure is the correct organizational model for the full-night owl chorus.** Dusk (territorial establishment), early night (full calling), deep night (diminished activity), pre-dawn (resurgence). Each phase has a distinct acoustic character. The experience of the full night is the trajectory through these phases, not any single peak.
2. **The frequency architecture of the four species is the correct acoustic organization.** Great Horned (200-700 Hz), Barred (300-900 Hz), Northern Spotted (400-900 Hz), Western Screech-Owl (700-1,500 Hz). The four species occupy distinct primary frequency ranges with some overlap. The winter owl community is frequency-organized.
3. **The Barred/Spotted interaction as ecological documentation is the correct framing.** The simultaneous occurrence of both species in the winter chorus documents an ecological transition: old-growth forest with an unresolved competitive interaction in progress. The acoustic event is an ecological document, not just an acoustic one.
4. **The E=9 temporal development calibration correctly distinguishes the full-night owl chorus from the E=10 simultaneous-maximum entries.** The owl chorus is not less impressive -- it is differently structured. No single moment is at the ceiling of what the forest can produce. The E=9 is the correct energy for an event that is about depth and duration rather than peak intensity.
5. **The pairing with TAAS is structurally exact.** TAAS: temporal development within songs (intrasong variation, songs develop through phases). Owl chorus: temporal development through the night (four phases, each with distinct character). Both E=9. Both about trajectory over time. The arc resolves at E=18 with a temporal development pairing.

## Discoveries

1. **The Northern Spotted Owl's presence as old-growth quality indicator.** The winter owl chorus in truly old-growth Pacific Northwest forest includes the Spotted Owl. The presence of its four-note call is a direct indicator of old-growth forest quality -- the forest is old enough and structurally complex enough to support a specialist that cannot adapt to fragmented secondary forest. The Spotted Owl's call is the sound of protected old growth.
2. **The Great Horned Owl as the earliest PNW breeding species.** December-January calling is peak territorial activity because Great Horned Owls begin breeding in the winter, before any other PNW bird species. The winter night is the season of maximum Great Horned Owl vocal activity, not a quiet period -- it is their breeding season.
3. **The pre-dawn resurgence as the closing arc's acoustic analogue.** The owl chorus increases activity before dawn, then transitions to the diurnal chorus as light arrives. This pre-dawn resurgence is the temporal analogue of the closing arc's structure: maximum energy followed by resolution, with a brief return of activity before the final close. The full night ends with an increase before it ends.
