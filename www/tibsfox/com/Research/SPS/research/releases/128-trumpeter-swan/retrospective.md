## Structural Event 5: Conservation Triumph

- **Situation:** From fewer than 100 Trumpeter Swans in the contiguous US (1935) to approximately 63,000 today. Red Rock Lakes NWR established specifically for the species. Reintroduction programs across historic range.
- **Root Cause:** 19th-century market hunting, egg collection, and habitat loss drove the species to the brink. Federal protection, dedicated refuges, and reintroduction programs over eight decades achieved the recovery. One of the defining conservation achievements of the 20th century.
- **Recommendation:** Document the conservation narrative. Note the contrast with Tundra Swan's stable population (never near-extinction). The two swans have different conservation histories.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Conservation triumph documented. <100 to 63,000 over eight decades.
