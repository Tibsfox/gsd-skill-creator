Deep Resonant Bugling — E=6 Maximum Energy

The Trumpeter Swan’s bugle is produced by an extra-long trachea (over 1 meter) coiled within the keel of the sternum — a resonating chamber analogous to the tubing of a brass instrument. The result: a deep, brassy, commanding call at 200–1,500 Hz, audible at 1–3 kilometers. A single bird fills a valley. E=6 through individual anatomical power — no flock amplification needed. The most powerful avian voice in North America. Buccinator = “the trumpeter.”

Conservation Triumph — From <100 to ~63,000

In 1935, fewer than 100 Trumpeter Swans survived in the contiguous United States, all at Red Rock Lakes, Montana. Market hunting (feathers, skins), egg collection, and habitat loss had driven the species to the brink. Red Rock Lakes National Wildlife Refuge was established specifically to protect them. Through decades of protection, reintroduction programs, and habitat management, the population has recovered to approximately 63,000 birds — one of the defining conservation achievements of the 20th century.

North America’s Largest Native Bird

Males up to 17.2 kg (38 lbs) — heavier than California Condor, Bald Eagle, or Sandhill Crane. Wingspan up to 250 cm (over 8 feet). The largest nest of any North American bird — up to 3.5 meters (11 feet) in diameter. The size drives the sound: large body cavity houses the long trachea, large lungs power the bugle. The largest bird produces the deepest, loudest voice.

Swan Comparison Pair Complete (127–128)

Tundra Swan (127): high melodious baying, E=5, flock-driven, yellow loral spot, concave bill, smaller (~6.5 kg avg), ~170,000 population. Trumpeter Swan (128): deep resonant bugling, E=6, individual power, no yellow spot, straight bill, larger (~11 kg avg), ~63,000 population. Voice is the most reliable separator. Collective vs. individual. Whistling vs. commanding. The pair is complete.

Eleventh Consecutive Anatidae (118–128)

The Anatidae streak extends to eleven. The energy arc across the streak: E=5, E=4, E=4, E=3, E=3, E=3, E=4, E=3, E=4, E=5, E=6. Maximum energy arrives at the streak’s eleventh degree through the most powerful voice. The progression from Harlequin Duck (stream specialist, E=5) to Trumpeter Swan (wetland giant, E=6) spans the full ecological and acoustic range of the Anatidae. The streak culminates in the continent’s largest bird and its most commanding call.
