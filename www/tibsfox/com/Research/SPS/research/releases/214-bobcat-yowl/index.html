Bobcat Yowl

Lynx rufus (Felidae) — E=5 — Third Felidae
Third Felidae. The smaller predator. Raspy, caterwauling yowl at 400–1,500 Hz. 1–3 seconds. Territorial and mating. 7–14 kg — a fraction of the cougar. But fierce. E=5 — mid-range predator energy. 20,000–30,000 in Washington. More common than you think.

The Yowl — Raspy Aggression

400–1,500 Hz. 1–3 seconds. Drawn-out, scratchy, caterwauling. “RRAAAAOWWW.” Sounds like a large angry house cat — because it is. Breeding season: January–March. Males yowl to attract, females respond. Mating chorus: multiple bobcats in sequence. Memorable and unnerving. Carries 200–500 meters.

The Adaptable Predator

Most habitat-generalist felid in North America. Forest, brush, desert, swamp, suburban edge. 20,000–30,000 in Washington — 10x the cougar population. Diet: rabbits, rodents, squirrels. Occasionally young deer. Stalks and pounces like a scaled-down cougar. Most PNW residents live near bobcats without knowing it.
