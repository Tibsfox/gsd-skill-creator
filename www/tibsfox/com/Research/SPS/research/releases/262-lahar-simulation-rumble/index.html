THE MOST DESTRUCTIVE VOLCANIC HAZARD IN THE PACIFIC NORTHWEST

A lahar is a flowing mixture of water and volcanic debris moving through a river valley from a volcanic source — the consistency of wet concrete, carrying boulders of thousands of tons, moving at 20-40 mph in steep sections. The Osceola Mudflow (approximately 5,600 years ago) originated at Rainier's summit, traveled the White River valley, and spread volcanic debris across an area now occupied by Auburn, Kent, Sumner, Puyallup, Orting, and portions of Tacoma. Volume: 3.8 cubic kilometers. The next lahar will not need to be this large to be catastrophic. The deep ground vibration at E=7 is the acoustic imagination of this event.

The Osceola Mudflow — The Historical Record

~5,600 YEARS AGO — THE LAST MAJOR RAINIER LAHAR

Source: eastern flank collapse of Mt. Rainier triggered by hydrothermal weakening of the volcanic edifice. Volume: 3.8 cubic kilometers (~2-3x the Mt. St. Helens 1980 eruption volume). Path: White River valley → Puget Sound lowlands. Reach: the current shoreline near Tacoma. The modern agricultural lowlands of the Puyallup River delta are built on Osceola deposit. The land beneath Orting is Osceola deposit. The historical record says this will happen again.

"The question is not whether a major lahar will occur from Mt. Rainier but when and how large it will be. The Osceola Mudflow is the geological record. The next lahar will be the present-tense record."

Real Hazard Zones — Orting, Puyallup Valley

Orting, WA

Extreme

Population ~8,000. Located entirely within lahar inundation zone. Built on Osceola deposit. Warning time: 30-45 minutes. Vehicle evacuation inadequate — vertical evacuation planning. Signage directing residents to high ground. Evacuation drills practiced.

Puyallup, WA

High

Population ~45,000. Puyallup River valley primary lahar pathway. Valley-floor portions in hazard zone. Elevated portions relatively safe.

Auburn / Kent

High

White River lahar pathway. Industrial and commercial infrastructure in inundation zones. Kent Valley contains significant manufacturing and warehouse facilities.

Port of Tacoma

Elevated

Major West Coast shipping facility near Puyallup River delta. Fuel storage and port infrastructure in potential inundation area.

Sea-Tac Airport

Elevated

Located between White River and Green River valleys. Near edge of some lahar hazard mapping zones. Proximity raises concern in comprehensive hazard planning.

USGS Detection Network

Monitoring

Automated sensors in river channels. Detect sudden flow change indicating approaching lahar. Automated alert to Pierce County Emergency Management. Integration with summit volcanic monitoring.

The Acoustic Signature — E=7

A lahar produces a distinctive acoustic sequence: first, low-frequency ground vibration (infrasonic, detectable at several kilometers before the flow arrives); then, as the flow approaches, a low-frequency rumble often compared to a fast freight train but lower in pitch and more sustained; at close range, turbulent flow noise -- boulders grinding against bedrock and against each other, waves within the flow, material crushing and breaking. E=7 captures the full-volume sustained presence of this sound: massive, direction-locked, impossible to ignore at any audible range.
