American Coot — Clucking + Grunting Flock

Fulica americana (Rallidae) — E=4 — The Not-a-Duck
THIRD RALLIDAE. Rallidae cluster complete (161–162, 164). Looks like a duck. Not a duck. White bill, lobed toes (not webbed), head-bobbing swim. Clucking + grunting flock sound. Aggressive territorial foot-fighting. E=4. v1.49.304.

Not a Duck — The Taxonomic Surprise

Black body, swims on open water, found in flocks. Every visual cue says “duck.” But the American Coot is Rallidae — more closely related to the Virginia Rail and Sora than to any duck. White chicken-like bill (not flat). Lobed toes (not webbed). Head-bobbing swim (ducks glide). One of the most frequently misidentified birds in North America.

The Aggressive Waterbird

Coots fight fiercely — foot-fighting with lobed toes as weapons. Birds rear up, strike, lock feet, push across water. Loud cackling “KA-KA-KA-KA!” during territorial disputes. Also defend against intraspecific brood parasitism — other coots lay eggs in neighbors’ nests. Parent coots count their own eggs.

Theory Nodes

F

Flock Vocalization

Advanced

Continuous clucking, grunting, cackling stream. 500-5000 Hz. Flock of 50-500+ birds. Collective sound, not individual calls. Unmistakable ambient.

L

Lobed Toes

Intermediate

Scalloped flaps on each toe. Not webbed. Swim thrust + land walking + diving. More versatile than duck webbing. Rail foot plan modified for open water.

A

Aggressive Territorial

Intermediate

Foot-fighting with lobed toes as weapons. Rearing, striking, locking feet. Brood parasitism defense. Egg counting. Flock dominance hierarchy.

N

Not a Duck

Foundational

Rallidae, not Anatidae. White bill, lobed toes, head-bobbing swim. Most frequently misidentified waterbird. A rail in duck habitat.
