## Structural Event 2: The Eelgrass-Brant-Puget Sound Connection

- **Situation:** Brant feeds almost exclusively on eelgrass (*Zostera marina*). Eelgrass is a foundational species in Puget Sound's shallow marine ecosystem, supporting juvenile Chinook salmon, herring, and Dungeness crab. Brant is an indicator species for eelgrass health.
- **Root Cause:** The dietary specialization creates a direct link between bird, plant, and marine ecosystem. Eelgrass decline = Brant decline. Protecting eelgrass for Brant also protects the broader marine food web. The Brant is a charismatic entry point for ecosystem conservation.
- **Recommendation:** Document the ecosystem connection as a theory node. This is the first species in the engine with a direct, documented link to Puget Sound marine ecosystem health through a specific habitat dependency.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Eelgrass-Brant-Puget Sound connection documented. Ecosystem indicator.
