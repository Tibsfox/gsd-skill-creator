Dendragapus fuliginosus (Phasianidae) — E=5 — Ventriloquial Deep Booming
FOURTH AND FINAL PHASIANIDAE. The gamebird quartet completes. Blue Grouse split complete: Dusky (154, interior) + Sooty (156, coastal). The most ventriloquial bird sound in the PNW — impossible to localize. Yellow cervical air sacs. Wet coastal mountain forest. 50–100 Hz. Seems to come from everywhere.

Ventriloquial Booming — From Everywhere and Nowhere

The most directionally ambiguous bird sound in the PNW. 50–100 Hz fundamental — wavelengths of 3–7 meters that diffract around trees. Cervical air sacs produce damped Helmholtz resonance with minimal upper harmonics. Tree-perch display position diffuses sound downward through canopy. Listeners standing directly below cannot determine direction. The sound is a presence, not a point source.

Blue Grouse Split Complete

Dusky Grouse (degree 154) — interior mountains, purplish-red air sacs, drier forest.
Sooty Grouse (degree 156) — coastal mountains, yellow air sacs, wetter forest.
Ruffed Grouse drumming (degree 155) between them — the mechanical sound separating the split vocal pair. Both species documented. The split is complete.
