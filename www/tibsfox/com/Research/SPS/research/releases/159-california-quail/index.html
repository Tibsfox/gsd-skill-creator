California Quail — Chi-CA-Go Call

Callipepla californica (Odontophoridae) — E=4 — The Assembly Call
FIRST ODONTOPHORIDAE. A new family enters the engine. The iconic “chi-CA-go!” assembly call — three syllables, stressed second, instantly recognizable. Forward-curving topknot plume. Covey behavior. Brushy edges. E=4.

First Odontophoridae — New World Quail Enters

Phasianidae (153–158, 6 entries) is complete. Odontophoridae begins. New World quail — exclusively Western Hemisphere. Smaller bodies. Social coveys. Ornamental head plumes. A different branch of the Galliformes tree. The engine enters new taxonomic territory.

“Chi-CA-Go!” — The Assembly Call

Three syllables. First quick and quiet. Second loud and stressed. Third dropping. 100–300 meter carry. The call that brings the covey back together. Male on elevated perch — fence post, brush top, rock. Repeated dozens of times. One of the most recognizable bird calls in the PNW.

Theory Nodes

C

Chi-CA-Go Assembly Call

Advanced

Three syllables, stressed second. 100-300m carry. Assembly function: brings scattered covey together. One of the most recognizable PNW bird calls.

V

Covey Social Behavior

Intermediate

10-200+ birds. Communal roosting (rosette formation). Group foraging. Flush-scatter-reassemble cycle. The covey is the fundamental social unit.

O

First Odontophoridae

Advanced

New World quail family enters. Distinct from Old World Phasianidae. Smaller body. Social coveys. Head plumes. Western Hemisphere endemic.

T

The Topknot Plume

Foundational

Forward-curving cluster of 6 feathers. Comma-shaped. Bobs when walking. Both sexes but male larger. Cultural icon. State bird of California.
