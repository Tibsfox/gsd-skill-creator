## Discoveries

1. **The dual function of the monitoring network.** The same seismometers and infrasonic sensors that detect background volcanic tremor (the SPS degree 261 sound) will be the first instruments to detect increased pre-eruptive activity. The background sound and the warning signal are the same category of signal at different amplitudes.
2. **Hydrothermal alteration as collapse mechanism.** Rainier doesn't need to erupt to produce a catastrophic lahar. The hydrothermal alteration of the edifice rock has weakened large sections of the mountain. An earthquake, or a modest increase in hydrothermal activity, could trigger an edifice collapse. The Osceola Mudflow (5,600 years ago) reached Puget Sound. The next one may not need a full eruption to repeat this.
