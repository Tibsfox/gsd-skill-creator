Tahoma — The Mountain

Mt. Rainier (Tahoma: "the mother of all waters" in Lushootseed/Coast Salish) stands at 14,411 feet, the highest peak in Washington and the Cascade Range. A stratovolcano with 26 named glaciers covering approximately 35 square miles — the most glaciated terrain of any peak in the contiguous United States outside Alaska. Classified as active by the USGS. The mountain has not erupted significantly in historical time, but it is not dormant: it has a persistent hydrothermal system, produces volcanic tremor continuously, and is classified by USGS Volcanic Threat Assessment as one of the most dangerous volcanoes in the United States.

"The mountain communicates at frequencies we cannot hear. The infrasound from a volcanic tremor episode at Rainier can be detected at stations tens to hundreds of kilometers away. The machine is running. We just cannot hear it without instruments."

Volcanic Tremor — The Sound

Volcanic tremor is a sustained seismic signal produced by fluid movement through volcanic conduits — magma, hydrothermal water, volcanic gases flowing through cracks and conduits within the edifice. As fluid moves, it vibrates the surrounding rock, producing a continuous or episodic resonance analogous to sound produced when air moves through a pipe. The tremor at Rainier is typically below 1-10 Hz — infrasonic, below the 20 Hz lower threshold of human hearing. Detected by seismometers and infrasonic pressure sensors, not conventional microphones.

Frequency rangeBelow 1-10 Hz. Infrasonic. Inaudible without instrumentation. Detectable by seismometers and infrasonic arrays.
MechanismFluid movement through volcanic conduits. Magma, hydrothermal water, volcanic gases. Conduit resonance as pipe analog.
Harmonic tremorEpisodes with harmonic frequency structure (fundamental + overtones). Associated with increased magmatic activity. Monitored by PNSN.
Hydrothermal sourcePrimary source at Rainier is the hydrothermal system. Hot springs and fumaroles at summit. Cave network under summit ice.
Trigger sensitivityRegional earthquakes elsewhere in the PNW can trigger tremor episodes at Rainier. The mountain is sensitive to regional stress changes.

The Most Dangerous Volcano in the United States

USGS Volcanic Threat Assessment ranks Mt. Rainier among the highest-priority hazard sites in the United States. Two factors: volcanic hazard (severe: large lahars, pyroclastic flows, lava flows possible) and exposure (extreme: millions of residents in lahar pathways through Puyallup River valley, White River valley, Carbon River valley).

Lahar Pathway Cities

Orting (entirely within lahar inundation zone), Puyallup, Sumner, Auburn, Kent. Hundreds of thousands of residents. Port of Tacoma. Seattle-Tacoma International Airport near White River pathway.

Warning Time: Orting

As little as 30-45 minutes before lahar arrival. Not enough time for vehicle evacuation given modern traffic patterns. Orting has vertical evacuation planning.

The Glacial Factor

26 named glaciers, ~35 sq miles of ice. Glacial melt in eruption or major hydrothermal event produces lahars regardless of eruption volume. Ice is the multiplier.

Monitoring System

USGS Cascades Volcano Observatory + Pacific Northwest Seismic Network. Seismometers, GPS, gas sensors, tiltmeters. The tremor monitoring network IS the early warning system.

The Geophysics Arc

The Geophysics Arc (261 onward) documents the underground physical machinery of the Pacific Northwest. Degree 261 opens with volcanic tremor (periodic, E=5). Degree 262 continues with lahar simulation (episodic, E=7). Degree 263 enters seismic territory with P-wave earthquake rumble (episodic, E=5). Degree 264 descends to the deep water column (continuous, E=2). The arc moves from volcanic to seismic to hydrological — from the mountain down through the crust and into the sea.

E=5 is the correct energy for the background volcanic tremor: not the maximum, not quiet. The mountain produces this sound continuously. It is the steady state of an active stratovolcano. The Geophysics Arc opens at medium energy because the underground is always operating, not only when it is at maximum.
