## What Worked

1. **The first geophysical entry expands the SPS engine's scope in a significant and appropriate direction.** All 317 previous SPS entries documented biological acoustic events. The CSZ entry adds the geological acoustic baseline beneath all PNW biological events. The expansion is defensible: the Cascadia Subduction Zone generates real, documented, instrumented acoustic signals (ETS tremor, deep ocean infrasound) that are as acoustically real as any biological vocalization in the engine, even if infrasonic.
2. **The scale-vs-intensity distinction is the entry's most important calibration contribution.** The CSZ is the engine's largest object by absolute physical scale. E=5 is the correct calibration because E reflects perceptual acoustic intensity, not absolute physical scale. Documenting the distinction explicitly as a calibration principle is the entry's analytical core: a megathrust earthquake releases more energy than all biological vocalizations in the engine combined, but the continuous between-event baseline signal is E=5. The engine must distinguish these.
3. **ETS as the 14-month geological clock is the entry's most technically distinctive fact.** The recurrence interval for CSZ ETS events is measured in months -- a timescale that bridges the geological (measured in years and millions of years) and the human-event (measured in days and months). The ETS clock is the CSZ's most tractable temporal feature for the engine's documentation purposes.
4. **The 1700 megathrust context is correctly framed as "what this entry is the prelude to."** The between-event baseline (degree 318) and the megathrust event (hypothetical future entry beyond the scale) are paired conceptually. The engine documents what the CSZ does continuously, not what it does catastrophically. The catastrophic future entry is noted as open.

## Discoveries

1. **The CSZ is the only SPS entry where the generating system's scale exceeds the E-scale's maximum.** A full-margin M9.0 Cascadia rupture would release energy orders of magnitude above any biological acoustic event in the PNW or anywhere. The E-scale (1-9) was designed for biological acoustic events. The CSZ megathrust would require a special calibration convention outside the biological scale. The engine notes this as an open calibration problem.
2. **The acoustic documentation of the CSZ exists and is accessible.** NEPTUNE Canada (now Ocean Networks Canada) and the Ocean Observatories Initiative have deployed seafloor hydrophone networks in the NE Pacific that continuously record the CSZ's acoustic baseline. ETS tremor signals are in the published scientific record. The engine is documenting a real acoustic system with real instrumented data, not a theoretical construct.
