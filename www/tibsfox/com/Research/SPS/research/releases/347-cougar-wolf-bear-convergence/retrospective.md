## The Rarity Context

The triple apex predator convergence is the SPS entry with the lowest probability of natural occurrence in any given night in the Pacific Northwest. Each element is individually rare:

- **Cougar vocalization:** Cougars are extremely widespread in Washington State (~1,500-2,000 adults) but almost never heard. The species hunts silently. The scream is heard primarily during mating season or in territorial encounters.
- **Gray wolf howl:** Wolves were absent from Washington for most of the 20th century. Recolonization from Idaho began around 2008; the current population (~200+ individuals) is concentrated in northeast Washington. Pack howls occur but require locating an active pack.
- **Grizzly bear vocalization:** The Washington grizzly population is among the smallest in the lower 48. The Selkirk population is confirmed; the North Cascades recovery is ongoing. Finding a vocalizing grizzly in the same acoustic space as the other two is extraordinarily improbable.

The convergence is possible. It is documented here not as a common event but as the acoustic maximum -- the ceiling -- of the PNW terrestrial ecosystem's biological voice.

## Discoveries

1. **The gray wolf recolonization timeline (2008 Washington recolonization from Idaho) is significant for ecosystem completeness.** For most of the engine's documented period (1960s-2000s), Washington lacked a gray wolf population. The apex predator triad has only been possible in Washington State since the mid-2010s when wolf packs became established in multiple ranges. The convergence entry documents a wildlife recovery success story alongside the acoustic maximum.
2. **The 80-4,000 Hz combined frequency range of the three voices almost perfectly spans human conversational hearing.** Human speech is 80-3,500 Hz. The triple apex predator convergence occupies essentially the full range of human hearing sensitivity -- from the lower frequency limit of conversational speech to the upper limit of most vocal production. The acoustic event would be maximally salient to the human ear, activating every auditory frequency band simultaneously.
3. **Inter-predator competition dynamics are poorly documented in Washington State** compared to Yellowstone (where wolf-grizzly interactions have been studied extensively since wolf reintroduction in 1995). The convergence entry draws on Yellowstone-based research for some behavioral context, with the acknowledgment that Washington-specific data is more limited.
