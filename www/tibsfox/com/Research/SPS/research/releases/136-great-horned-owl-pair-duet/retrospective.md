## Structural Event 2: Size-Pitch Paradox

- **Situation:** The female Great Horned Owl is approximately 25% heavier than the male (1.4-2.5 kg vs. 0.9-1.8 kg) but produces a higher-pitched hoot (300-500 Hz vs. 250-400 Hz). This violates the typical size-pitch relationship (larger body = deeper voice).
- **Root Cause:** The size-pitch relationship is driven by syrinx morphology, which may be influenced by hormonal and developmental factors independent of overall body size. The pitch dimorphism likely evolved for communicative reasons -- allowing reliable sex identification by voice.
- **Recommendation:** Document the size-pitch paradox as a theory node. This is a significant biological finding with implications for acoustic dimorphism research. First within-species gendered acoustic analysis in the engine.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Size-pitch paradox documented. New analytical dimension for the engine.

## Structural Event 3: Winter Nesting and Pair Duet Function

- **Situation:** Great Horned Owls nest in January-February -- the earliest of any North American bird. The pair duet peaks during this period, serving as both territorial declaration and pair bond reinforcement. Lifelong pair bonds.
- **Root Cause:** Early nesting provides first access to best nest sites (which are appropriated from other species) and maximum development time for young. The duet coordinates nesting behavior, reinforces the pair bond, and defends the territory during the most vulnerable period.
- **Recommendation:** Document winter nesting and pair bond duration. The duet's functional context is integral to understanding its acoustic significance.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Winter nesting documented. Pair bond function documented.
