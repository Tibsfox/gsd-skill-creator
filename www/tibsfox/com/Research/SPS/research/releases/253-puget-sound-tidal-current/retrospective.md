## What Worked

1. **The 252-253 pair as the complete tidal acoustic picture.** Tidal Surge on Cobble Beach (252, E=4, shore interface, audible) and Tidal Current (253, E=2, bulk flow, infrasonic). Two acoustic expressions of the same tidal system: the noisy where water meets stone, and the quiet where the entire water mass moves. Documenting both is the correct approach.
2. **The tidal prism number.** 25 cubic kilometers per tidal cycle, approximately 10x the volume of Lake Washington. This is the entry's key datum: the relationship between physical scale (enormous) and acoustic energy (E=2, minimal). Volume does not equal acoustic energy.
3. **The glacial origin.** The Cordilleran Ice Sheet as the physical explanation for Puget Sound's channel geometry, depth, and tidal behavior. The Sound's tidal current is a product of glacial carving -- the ice created the container; the tide fills and empties it.
4. **The infrastructure/maritime arc opening.** Degrees 253-255 bridge from the natural abiotic sounds (245-252) to the storm arc (256-260): tidal current (253, Hydrology), fog horn (254, Maritime), log truck (255, Anthropogenic). The arc captures the Sound's infrastructure as sound.

## Discoveries

1. **Infrasound as the entry's defining acoustic concept.** The tidal current produces primarily infrasound -- below 20 Hz, technically below human hearing threshold. This places it in a unique acoustic category: the largest physical process in the Sound is the quietest acoustically. The body feels it before the ear registers it.
2. **Tide rips as the audible expression of the current.** Where the infrasonic bulk flow becomes audible, it is at tide rips -- the convergence zones where opposing currents meet. These are also the primary feeding locations for diving seabirds: the current maps the fish; the birds follow. The acoustic and ecological functions of the tidal current are inseparable.

## Pair Analysis: Puget Sound Tidal Current + Helms Alee

- **Energy:** E=2 + E=7 = E=9. Above equilibrium.
- **Theme:** MASS IN MOTION, MEASURED IN BULK NOT VELOCITY. Helms Alee moves sonic mass slowly -- the sludge riff at 60 BPM, enormous through downtuning and fuzz. The tidal current moves water mass slowly -- 25 billion cubic meters at 0.5-1.0 knots in the open basins. Both operate at the scale of geological process. Both are low-frequency phenomena measured in bulk rather than speed. The E rating asymmetry (7 vs 2) reflects acoustic reality: sludge is audibly massive; the tidal current is physically massive but acoustically quiet. They are the same phenomenon in different registers: one made by musicians, one made by the moon.
