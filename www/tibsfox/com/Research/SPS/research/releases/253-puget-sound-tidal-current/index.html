25 BILLION CUBIC METERS — TWICE DAILY — SUBSONIC — THE SOUND BREATHING

The tidal current of Puget Sound is not the wave, not the shore impact, not the cobble percussion of degree 252. It is the bulk flow: 25 cubic kilometers of seawater entering and exiting the Sound through Admiralty Inlet twice daily. The acoustic signature is infrasonic — below 20 Hz, technically below human hearing. The tidal current is felt more than heard. The Sound breathing. E=2.

The Glacial Origin

Puget Sound was carved by the Cordilleran Ice Sheet (~17,000 years ago): 3,000-6,000 feet of ice, scoured the bedrock into elongated north-south channels and deep sub-basins. Average depth: 65 meters. Maximum depth: ~280 meters near Point Jefferson. The irregular geometry of channels, sills, passes, and basins — Main Basin, Hood Canal, South Sound, Case Inlet, Colvos Passage — is the product of differential glacial erosion. The tidal current navigates this geometry twice daily.

"The Tacoma Narrows sill rises to within 50 meters of the surface. The Main Basin drops to 200+ meters. This geometry creates the strongest tidal currents in the Sound: 6-10 knots through the Narrows, 8-9 knots through Deception Pass."

The Mechanics of 25 Billion Cubic Meters

Tidal Prism

~25 cubic kilometers per tidal cycle. ~10x the volume of Lake Washington. All enters/exits through Admiralty Inlet and northern passes.

Tidal Pattern

Mixed semidiurnal: 2 highs + 2 lows per day, unequal heights. Range at Seattle: 10-14 feet. King tides: 15-16 feet. Minus tides expose extensive intertidal.

Current Velocities

Open basins: 0.5-1.0 knots. Deception Pass: 8-9 knots. Tacoma Narrows: 6-10 knots. Admiralty Inlet: 3-5 knots.

Acoustic Character

Turbulence broadband below 100 Hz. Often below 20 Hz (infrasound). Felt as subsonic pulse more than heard. E=2.

The Underwater World

Below the surface of the tidal current, Puget Sound has a complex acoustic environment: snapping shrimp (crackling broadband noise), Pacific cod and rockfish vocalizations, herring spawning sounds, harbor seals, Steller sea lions, orca. The tidal current's infrasound is the foundation beneath all biological sound. At tide rips — the convergence zones where opposing currents meet — the tidal current is audible: hissing surface disturbance, diving seabirds, concentrated fish. Rhinoceros auklets, common murres, and pigeon guillemots aggregate at tide rips to feed on fish compressed there by the current.

Traditional Navigation

For Coast Salish peoples — Lekwungen, Saanich, Lummi, Samish, Suquamish — the tidal currents were navigational infrastructure. Traditional knowledge of the tidal cycle: its timing, velocity, direction at specific locations. Departing at the right tidal phase, the current carried canoes from Admiralty Inlet to the Main Basin without paddle effort. The current mapped the fish: forage aggregations at convergence zones and sills. Traditional fishers followed the current to find them. This knowledge, accumulated over millennia, represents the most detailed human understanding of Puget Sound's tidal system ever assembled.
