Mourning Dove

Zenaida macroura (Columbidae) — E=3 — Mournful oo-WOO-woo-woo-woo + Wing Whistle
SECOND COLUMBIDAE. The most recognized bird call in America. Dual-sound species: mournful five-note coo + wing whistle on takeoff. America’s most hunted game bird (20 million/year). 300–400 million population. Everywhere. Unnoticed.

The Mournful Call

“oo-WOO-woo-woo-woo” — five notes, second highest, descending. Fundamental 350–550 Hz. Repeated with 3–8 second pauses. Named for the sound: “mourning” — early settlers heard grief in the cooing. The most frequently misidentified bird sound (often called an owl). Embedded in American cultural memory: summer mornings, rural nostalgia, peaceful melancholy.

The Wing Whistle — Second Mechanical Sound

A sharp, ascending whistle on takeoff — produced by the modified 9th primary feather. Not a vocalization but a feather-generated alarm signal. The whistle alone triggers flight in nearby doves. 1000–3000 Hz. Second mechanical sound in the engine (after Common Goldeneye at 124). Gentle coo → sharp whistle.

America’s Most Hunted Game Bird

20 million Mourning Doves killed by hunters annually. Legal in 40 of 50 states. More than all duck species combined. Washington opened its first Mourning Dove season in 2019. Yet the population sustains: up to 6 broods per year, 14-day incubation, 14-day nestling period. The paradox: America’s most beloved backyard bird and most hunted game bird are the same species.
