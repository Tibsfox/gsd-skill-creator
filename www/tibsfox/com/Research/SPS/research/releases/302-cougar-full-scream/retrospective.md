## Discoveries

1. **The escalating structure is what separates the cougar scream from other high-amplitude wildlife sounds.** Most alarming sounds startle at onset. The cougar scream builds. This structural property makes it qualitatively different: sustained dread, not sudden shock. E=9 is justified by this property alone, separate from the timbre.
2. **The female-estrus reproductive context is the primary vocalization setting, not predation or territorial defense.** The most commonly recorded cougar screams are produced by females in estrus seeking males. The most terrifying sound in the Pacific Northwest forest is, in its primary context, a mating call. This is one of the engine's more interesting biological ironies.
