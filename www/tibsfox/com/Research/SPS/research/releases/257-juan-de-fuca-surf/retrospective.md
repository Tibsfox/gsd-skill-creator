## What Worked

1. **The infrasound argument is the most important factual contribution.** Open coast surf produces substantial energy below 20 Hz. Recordings miss this. The E=8 rating reflects the full embodied acoustic experience including the physical pressure component. This is why surf at this scale feels overwhelming in a way that recordings do not convey.
2. **The swell origin documentation grounds the sound in its physical source.** North Pacific storms, trans-Pacific travel, Gulf of Alaska contribution, 12-18 second wave periods at the coast -- these facts explain why January surf in the Strait of Juan de Fuca is categorically louder than July surf. The sound is the product of the specific oceanographic conditions of the North Pacific winter.
3. **The Makah and Quileute cultural contexts reframe the sound.** E=8 surf is not just loud weather; it is the medium of thousand-year-old livelihoods. The Makah's treaty right to hunt whales exists because they maintained an ocean-going culture through this sound environment. The surf is information, not background.
4. **"The storm heard before it arrives" is the correct characterization.** Juan de Fuca Surf (257) leads the Storm Arc because it is the storm's advance announcement. Coast surf → ridge winds → Cascade thunder → lightning. The arc follows the storm system's progression from coast to interior.

## Discoveries

1. **The sustained quality is the defining acoustic feature.** Most natural sounds are episodic or rhythmic. Open coast surf is sustained -- the wall of sound never closes. New breaking events constantly replace completing ones. This gives the sound a different quality from everything that precedes it in the engine: not rhythm, not pulse, but continuous presence.
2. **The Cape Flattery exposure is genuinely extreme.** Cape Flattery at the northwest corner of the contiguous US has no fetch obstruction to the west or northwest. Pacific swell arrives here without any reduction. The surf at Cape Flattery, in major winter swell, is about as loud as Pacific Northwest coastal sound gets at E=8.

## Pair Analysis: Juan de Fuca Surf + Wellwater Conspiracy (Heavy)

- **Energy:** E=8 + E=7 = E=15. Above equilibrium. The coast is louder than the music.
- **Theme:** SUSTAINED HEAVY PRESENCE. Both Juan de Fuca Surf and Wellwater Conspiracy's stoner rock are sustained, large, low-frequency presences. The guitar riff repeats; the surf repeats. Both are products of physics applied to mass: wave energy applied to water produces the surf roar; guitar pick applied to string (with fuzz pedal converting clipping to tone) produces the stoner rock riff. The mechanisms differ; the acoustic character aligns. The music is slightly below the coast; the coast does not notice.
