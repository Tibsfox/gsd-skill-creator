First Alcidae — The Auk Family Enters

After six consecutive Scolopacidae (168–173) — shorebirds probing tidal flats — the engine shifts to Alcidae. The auks. Wing-propelled divers that “fly” underwater. The Northern Hemisphere’s ecological penguins. From shore to sea. From walking to diving. From loud to quiet.

Bright Red Feet — Urban Nesting

Vivid red-orange feet against dark plumage and white wing patch. Nests in crevices under wharves, piers, ferry terminals — including Seattle’s Elliott Bay waterfront. Breeds within meters of heavy human activity. An urban seabird.

Wing-Propelled Diving

Pigeon Guillemots dive to 10–45 meters (up to 60+), using their wings to fly through water. Short, stiff wings — a compromise between aerial flight and underwater propulsion. Fast, buzzy flight over water. Carry fish crosswise in bill to feed chicks.
