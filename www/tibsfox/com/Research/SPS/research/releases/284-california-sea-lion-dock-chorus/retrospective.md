## What Worked

1. **The Steller-California contrast is the engine's cleanest family comparison.** Two entries in two consecutive degrees, same family, almost opposite ecological and acoustic profiles. The contrast documents the Otariidae family's range in the Pacific Northwest far more completely than a single entry would. This is correct design.
2. **The Ballard Locks conflict is essential PNW documentation.** The California Sea Lion as the protagonist in the ESA vs. MMPA tension at the Chittenden Locks: a recovering protected species preying on an endangered one. Management required lethal removal. This is part of the California Sea Lion's Pacific Northwest story.
3. **"The most culturally familiar" is a meaningful category.** The California Sea Lion's bark is what people mean when they say "sea lion sound." Documenting this in the engine positions the entry correctly within the broader SPS project: the species that most people already know is documented alongside the one they don't.
4. **Urban waterfront as habitat category is new to the SPS catalog.** Previous entries have used ocean, channel, open water, rookery. "Urban waterfront" is the California Sea Lion's specific niche. This expands the SPS ecosystem taxonomy.
5. **E=7 is correct.** The dock chorus is loud, constant, and overlapping -- but the individual bark is shorter and less massive than the Steller roar. The step-down from E=8 to E=7 is the right calibration.
