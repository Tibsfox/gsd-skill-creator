Three consecutive instances of that maximum. E=9 through concentration rather than convergence.

The sound is an indicator of salmon availability. This connects the acoustic entry directly to the Pacific Northwest conservation story (Chinook salmon, Snake River dams, habitat restoration) without overstating the case.

## Discoveries

1. **The orca's deliberate back/side re-entry to maximize impact area is documented behavioral choice, not incidental.** The animal orients for maximum impact -- the splash and pressure wave are, in part, a social signal to other pod members. The breach is communication as well as behavior.
2. **The pre-breach acceleration signature is detectable on hydrophone before the surface event is visible.** Research vessels and NOAA hydrophone arrays can identify an imminent breach acoustically before it occurs. The breach is preceded by its acoustic announcement: the subsurface acceleration that changes the water pressure around the animal.
3. **Breach cascade frequency decreases with nutritional stress, making it a diagnostic tool for salmon monitoring.** NOAA NWFSC and the Center for Whale Research use breach and porpoising rates as behavioral health indicators correlated with prey availability. The degree-340 sound is a salmon abundance proxy.
