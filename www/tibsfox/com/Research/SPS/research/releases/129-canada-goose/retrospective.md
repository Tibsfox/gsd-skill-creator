## Structural Event 4: Urban Adaptation -- The Dominant Species

- **Situation:** The Canada Goose is the most visible, most audible, and most numerous large bird in many PNW urban landscapes. Resident populations of 100,000-200,000+ in Washington State. Parks, golf courses, corporate campuses.
- **Root Cause:** Mid-20th century transplant programs created resident populations from migratory stock. The mild PNW climate, abundant mowed grass near water, and reduced predation in urban environments created ideal conditions. The species' behavioral plasticity and loss of wariness completed the urban adaptation.
- **Recommendation:** Document the urban adaptation as an ecological phenomenon. Note the parallel with the degree pair partner (Pat O'Day) as acoustic dominant of their respective landscapes.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Urban adaptation documented. Acoustic dominance in urban environments parallels ecological dominance.

## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **E=5 is defensible.** Flock-amplified collective power. Individual honk is moderate; flock of 50-200 creates wall of sound. One step below E=6 (Trumpeter Swan individual anatomy). The collective-vs-individual energy distinction is clean.

2. **The genus shift documentation is accurate.** Cygnus (127-128) to Branta (129) is a real taxonomic transition. The engine tracks this correctly.

3. **The 2004 species split is well-documented.** Banks et al. 2004 (AOU 45th supplement) is the authoritative source. The split was based on size gap, genetics, and ecological differences.

4. **V-formation aerodynamics are well-sourced.** Cutts & Speakman 1994, Portugal et al. 2014. The 10-14% energy savings figure is robust.

5. **Cross-references to SPS-130 (Cackling), SPS-128 (Trumpeter), SPS-127 (Tundra), SPS-118 (Harlequin), and SPS-107-115 (Picidae) are all defensible.**

### Source Quality

6. **Canada Goose is exceptionally well-documented.** Cornell Lab, Mowbray et al. 2002, Sibley 2014, WDFW management plans. Perhaps the most documented waterfowl species in North America. Source base is excellent.
