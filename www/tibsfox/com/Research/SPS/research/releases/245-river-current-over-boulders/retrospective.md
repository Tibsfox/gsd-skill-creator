## Discoveries

1. **The river-as-acoustic-spine concept is generative.** In any mountain watershed, the river is the constant acoustic background against which all other sounds occur. This framing -- the abiotic as context for the biotic -- is an important lens for the abiotic era.
2. **The seasonal variation of river sound is significant.** Spring snowmelt = fuller, deeper sound (more boulders submerged, longer standing waves). Late summer = higher-pitched, fragmented (exposed boulders, lower flow). The river is not a single sound but a year-round acoustic arc.
3. **The Elwha River post-dam-removal is the most current and consequential river data in the engine.** The largest dam removal in US history at the time (2011-2014) has produced measurable habitat recovery, and the river is actively creating new boulder-field environments as it works through reservoir sediment. This is live geological process observable in real time.
