FIRST CATHARTIDAE — NEW WORLD VULTURES ENTER THE ENGINE. Degree 235 is the engine's first Cathartidae. The family includes Turkey Vulture, Black Vulture, California Condor, Andean Condor, and three others. Cathartidae is NOT related to Old World vultures (Accipitridae) — they are a convergent evolution. Turkey Vulture is more closely related to storks than to hawks.
WIDEST S36/SPS E-GAP IN ENGINE. Turkey Vulture (E=2) paired with Heart Hard Rock (E=7) = E-spread of 5. No previous degree has an S36/SPS gap greater than 4. The most nearly-silent bird in the raptor arc meets the loudest female-fronted rock in the engine. E=2 + E=7 = E=9 combined.

The Bird Without a Voice

The Turkey Vulture has no functional syrinx — the specialized vocal organ found in all other bird families covered in this engine. Without a syrinx, it cannot produce vocalizations in the bird sense. What it can produce: hisses, grunts, and low groaning sounds by forcing air through the esophagus. At the nest when threatened, a sustained hiss. Otherwise: silence.

E=2 is the honest placement. The Turkey Vulture soars over PNW ridges in complete silence, holding its V-shaped wings in the dihedral, tilting in thermals. No sound from above. The voice is a hiss, not a song. The engine has never encountered a bird this quiet.

The Non-Voice — Acoustic Profile

No syrinx — no true vocalization
Hiss: broadband noise, 200–2,000 Hz
Grunt: low, guttural, non-directional

Sound produced by forcing air through the esophagus, not the syrinx. Functions: defensive hiss at nest (threat response), low grunts during feeding competition. E=2 reflects the near-absence of intentional vocalization. The Turkey Vulture communicates primarily through posture and flight.

The Nose That Finds the Dead

The Turkey Vulture is the only regularly soaring bird that finds carrion primarily by smell. The olfactory bulb is approximately 4 times larger (relative to body size) than in a hawk or eagle. The target compound is dimethyl sulfide (DMS) — produced within hours of an animal's death, detectable at concentrations of 1 part per billion. A Turkey Vulture can find a carcass hidden under dense forest canopy by smell alone.

"The pipeline industry discovered Turkey Vultures congregating above underground gas leaks before sensors could detect them. The vultures were smelling the same sulfur compounds added to natural gas that they use to find carrion."

Thermal Soaring — The Silent Circuit

The Turkey Vulture's metabolic rate in soaring flight is barely above resting rate — the thermal does the work. The characteristic V-wing dihedral and rocking flight motion allow the bird to ride rising air columns across the PNW landscape without flapping. Coverage of 100-200 km per day on virtually no caloric expenditure. The architecture of effortless travel.
