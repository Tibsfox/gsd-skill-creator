## What Worked

1. **Opening Hydrobatidae with the colony (rather than a single bird) captures the correct acoustic phenomenon.** The storm-petrel's signature soundscape is the colony chorus, not the individual call. A single Leach's Storm-Petrel vocalization would not represent what makes this family acoustically significant. The colony approach is correct.
2. **The nocturnal restriction is the central ecological fact.** Everything about the storm-petrel's breeding biology flows from temporal predator avoidance: night arrival, night departure, darkness as habitat. This is the correct primary frame.
3. **The petrel etymology is one of natural history nomenclature's most evocative stories.** "Petrel" from "Pierre" (Peter) walking on water -- the bird's pattering foraging behavior named for a Biblical miracle. This deserves prominence.
4. **Triangle Island's size is striking.** 50,000-100,000 breeding pairs on a single BC island is extraordinary. Most people have no idea this colony exists. The engine documents it.

## Discoveries

1. **The cryptic species issue (Pacific vs. Atlantic population divergence) is current active taxonomy.** "Leach's Storm-Petrel" may represent multiple species upon completion of DNA analysis. The engine documents this uncertainty.
2. **Island biosecurity as the primary management tool is a Pacific Northwest conservation story.** The eradication of introduced predators from seabird nesting islands is one of the most successful wildlife management approaches in the region. Storm-petrels are the beneficiary.
