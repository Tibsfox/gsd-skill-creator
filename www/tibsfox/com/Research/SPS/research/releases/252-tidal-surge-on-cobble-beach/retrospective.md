## What Worked

1. **The backwash clatter is the correct identification of the signature sound.** Not the wave approach, not the maximum uprush -- the retreating water dragging cobbles downslope is the most characteristic and most distinctive element of cobble beach acoustics.
2. **The lithophone concept provides the best analytical framework.** Cobble beach as natural percussion instrument. Wave energy = player. Geology = instrument design. The absence of sandy beaches is an acoustic presence: stone percussion where other coasts have silence.
3. **Deception Pass as primary location is well-grounded.** Most-visited Washington State Park, strongest tidal currents in inner Puget Sound, cobble beaches on both sides of the passage. The geography supports the acoustic claim.
4. **The geological explanation for why PNW beaches are cobble is the entry's most important factual contribution.** This is not obvious to most visitors. Knowing why the beach is stone and not sand explains both the visual and acoustic character of the place.

## Discoveries

1. **The clamshell midden connection grounds the acoustic experience in millennia of human use.** These beaches are not scenic backdrop -- they are working landscapes with archaeological depth. The sound of cobble underfoot and underwater has been part of human experience in this place for thousands of years.
2. **The wave period (5-15 seconds) creates the rhythm.** Regular enough to constitute a beat; irregular enough to never be metronomic. This is the tidal beat of the PNW coast -- as characteristic as the 4/4 beat is to rock music, but rooted in physics rather than convention.

## Pair Analysis: Tidal Surge on Cobble Beach + No-Anchor

- **Energy:** E=4 + E=7 = E=11. Above equilibrium.
- **Theme:** FORCE AGAINST HARD MATERIAL, INTENTIONAL AND ACCIDENTAL. Noise rock: the musician deliberately places the guitar pick against the string with the intention of producing a specific sound from the collision of hard material against hard material. The distortion pedal, the feedback -- all deliberate applications of force against resistance. Tidal surge on cobble: the wave places kinetic energy against the cobble beach with no intention at all -- pure physics, no musician, no choice. Both produce complex, irregular, unpitched percussion. The pairing holds the intentional and the accidental at the same E=4/E=7 level, asking whether the deliberate and the physical produce the same category of sound. They do.
