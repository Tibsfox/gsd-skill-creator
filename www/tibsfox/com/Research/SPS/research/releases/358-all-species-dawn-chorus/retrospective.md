## What Worked

1. **The coastal edge as the geographic enabling condition is the key insight.** Inland dawn chorus locations are acoustically rich but lack the marine mammal layer. The coastal edge -- tidal flat + estuary + forest + open water within hearing range -- is the only Pacific Northwest location where all four trophic layers (birds, frogs, insects, harbor seals) are geographically co-present at dawn. The location is the condition that makes E=10 possible.
2. **The three simultaneous conditions (spring + coastal edge + dawn) as the E=10 structure is the correct calibration frame.** Any one of these conditions produces notable acoustic events. The convergence of all three at simultaneous peak -- spring breeding maximum for all taxa, coastal edge for maximum layer presence, civil twilight for bird chorus maximum -- is E=10 through the convergence principle consistent with the closing arc's other entries.
3. **Full-spectrum coverage (100 Hz to 15+ kHz) as the second instance in the engine (after degree 351) is the correct cross-reference.** The Hoh Rainforest entry achieved full spectrum through insect + bird + rain in old-growth. The coastal dawn achieves the same through the addition of harbor seal low-frequency vocalization extending the range downward. Two routes to full spectrum; the coastal edge route is enabled by the marine mammal layer.
4. **Pacific Tree Frog as the Hollywood generic frog sound from a Pacific Northwest species is the correct specificity.** The meta-observation (this specific PNW frog call became the de facto global representation of frog sound through Hollywood recording practice) grounds the species' documentation in a broader cultural frame that distinguishes it from pure taxonomy.
5. **The bird layer frequency stratification is the correct acoustic organization.** Not just a list of species but an organized description of which frequency ranges they occupy: thrushes (1-3 kHz), warblers (2-5 kHz), wrens (4-8+ kHz), red-wing blackbirds (1-4 kHz marsh). The frequency architecture of the dawn chorus is the analytical structure that connects to the Sleater-Kinney pairing.

## Discoveries

1. **Civil twilight (6 degrees below horizon) as the specific moment of maximum dawn chorus density.** The maximum is not at sunrise -- it is before sunrise, when most species are simultaneously active but the light is not yet sufficient for foraging to compete with singing. The precise timing: the convergence has a specific moment, determined by light physics and species visual acuity thresholds. The dawn chorus maximum is an astronomically defined event.
2. **The frog chorus and bird chorus interact acoustically by occupying the same frequency range (2-4 kHz for frogs, overlapping with warblers and sparrows).** The overlap is not interference -- it creates a sustained background against which the more complex bird songs are superimposed. The Pacific Tree Frog chorus provides the structural background; the birds provide the structured events. This interaction is the acoustic organization of the 2-4 kHz mid-frequency zone of the dawn chorus.
3. **Individual dawn chorus components calibrate at E=5-8 alone; the intersection is E=10.** The explicit enumeration of individual component calibrations (bird alone E=7-8, frog alone E=5-6, seals alone E=4-5) and the argument that the intersection is E=10 is the clearest statement of the convergence principle in the closing arc. The intersection is the event.
