The Alpine Ghost — Year-Round Above Treeline

The only bird in North America that lives above treeline year-round. Never descends. Endures −40°C, roosts in snow burrows, subsists on willow buds. The smallest grouse (300–400g). Its soft cackling and clucking are the quiet sounds of survival at the edge of what is habitable.

Pure White in Winter — Complete Plumage Transformation

Summer: mottled brown and gray. Cryptic against alpine rocks.
Winter: pure white. Entire bird — body, wings, head — turns white. Only bill and eyes remain dark. Against the snowfield, virtually invisible. The white tail (diagnostic) provides complete camouflage. 6–8 weeks per transition. Driven by photoperiod. An evolutionary masterpiece.
