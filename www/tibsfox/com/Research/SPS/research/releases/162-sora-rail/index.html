Sora — Descending Whinny + Keek

Porzana carolina (Rallidae) — E=4 — The Common Invisible
SECOND RALLIDAE. Virginia Rail / Sora comparison pair completes (161–162). Most common North American rail — almost never seen. Descending whinny (musical, horse-like) + sharp “keek!” Short, stubby, chicken-like bill. E=4. v1.49.302.

Virginia Rail / Sora Comparison (161–162)

Virginia Rail (161): long bill, whinny grunt (pig-like), probes mud, deeper marsh.
Sora (162): short bill, whinny (musical) + keek, surface-picks, varied marsh.
Both secretive. Both E=4. Same marsh, different niches. The Rallidae comparison pair.

The Abundance Paradox

700,000–1,000,000+ breeding in North America. Yet one of the least-seen birds relative to population. Voice-to-sight detection ratio: 20:1 or higher. The most common rail. Almost never seen. The abundance-invisibility paradox defines the species.

Theory Nodes

W

Descending Whinny

Advanced

Rapid descending whistled notes. 2000-5000 Hz. Horse-like, musical. Higher-pitched than Virginia Rail. 8-15 notes in 1-2 seconds.

K

Sharp Keek

Advanced

Explosive, single-syllable "keek!" 3000-6000 Hz. Cuts through marsh soundscape. Most diagnostic call. Virginia Rail lacks this call type.

B

Short-Billed Rail

Intermediate

Short, stubby, chicken-like bill. Surface-picking adaptation. Seeds from water surface. Contrast with Virginia Rail's long probing bill.

A

Abundance Paradox

Foundational

Most common rail in N.A. (700K-1M+). Almost never seen. Detection ratio 20:1 (voice to sight). Abundance-invisibility paradox.
