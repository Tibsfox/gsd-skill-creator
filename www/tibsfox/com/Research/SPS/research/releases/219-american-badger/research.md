## THE AMERICAN BADGER -- THE TENACIOUS DIGGER

### Built to Dig

The American Badger is defined by excavation:

- **Digging adaptations.** Massive front claws (up to 5 cm), powerful forelimbs, low-slung body, small ears set close to the head to prevent dirt entry, and a nictitating membrane (third eyelid) to protect eyes while digging. Every feature serves the burrow.
- **Burrow systems.** Badgers dig complex burrow systems with multiple entrances, sleeping chambers, and escape tunnels. A single badger may maintain 10-50 burrows across its territory. The digging is constant -- badgers modify the landscape.
- **Prey excavation.** The primary hunting strategy: detect ground squirrels, gophers, and prairie dogs in their burrows, then dig them out. The badger digs faster than its prey can escape. The hunt is an engineering project.
- **Ecological engineer.** Abandoned badger burrows are used by dozens of other species: burrowing owls, snakes, rabbits, foxes, mice. The badger's digging creates habitat for the entire grassland community.
- **Size.** 6-12 kg (14-26 lbs). Low-slung, wide, powerful. The body shape is optimized for entering and excavating burrows.
- **Washington habitat.** Badgers in Washington are found primarily in eastern Washington's grassland and sage-steppe -- the dry, open country east of the Cascades. They are uncommon but present. Loss of grassland habitat threatens the population.

(Sources: Badger biology; digging adaptations; burrow systems; prey excavation; ecological engineering; WA habitat)

## THE GROWL AND HISS

### Defensive Vocalization

The badger's vocal profile is a two-part defense:

- **The growl.** Low, sustained, guttural -- "GRRRRR" -- 100-600 Hz. Similar in character to the wolverine's growl (216) but less intense. The growl communicates: "I am here, I am agitated, do not approach."
- **The hiss.** Sharp, explosive exhale -- broad spectrum, 2,000-8,000 Hz. The hiss is the badger's escalation -- added to the growl when the threat persists. The hiss says: "I warned you. Now I'm threatening."
- **Combined defense.** The growl-and-hiss combination is the badger's standard defensive display, often accompanied by arched back, raised fur, bared teeth, and a wide stance. The display is convincing: few predators will press an attack against a badger in full defensive mode.
- **Mustelid aggression.** The badger shares the mustelid family's reputation for disproportionate aggression. Like the wolverine, the badger will stand its ground against much larger animals. The vocalizations back up the attitude.

(Sources: Badger vocalization; growl-hiss combination; defensive behavior; mustelid aggression)

## Acoustic Profile

**Defensive Growl:**

- **Frequency:** Broad spectrum, 2,000-8,000 Hz. Sharp, explosive.
