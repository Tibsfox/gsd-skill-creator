## THE DEER MOUSE: THE INVISIBLE ABUNDANT

The Deer Mouse is a small, secretive, nocturnal rodent -- the **most abundant and most widespread native rodent in North America**. At 10-30 grams, it occupies nearly every terrestrial habitat from coast to coast, from sea level to alpine tundra. Despite this extraordinary abundance, most people have never knowingly seen one. It is secretive, nocturnal, and nearly invisible [1, 2, 3].

This is the engine's **NEW FAMILY: Cricetidae** -- the **36th family**. The Sciuridae streak of EIGHT consecutive entries (75-82) FINALLY BREAKS. Cricetidae = New World mice, rats, voles, hamsters. A new family after the octet.

This is also the engine's **first ultrasonic species** -- the first species whose primary vocalizations are entirely beyond human hearing (above 20,000 Hz).

And this is the **twenty-third consecutive original CSV species**.

## TAXONOMY: *Peromyscus maniculatus*

### Etymology and Nomenclature

- **Peromyscus** -- from Greek *pero* ("pouch" or "pointed") + *myscus* ("little mouse"). A reference to the small, pointed form of these mice [1, 2].

- **maniculatus** -- from Latin "little-handed," referring to the small white forefeet. Described by Wagner (1845) [1, 2].

- **Common names:** Deer Mouse (standard). Named for the brown-and-white coloration resembling a deer's pelage [1, 2].

### Classification

- **Kingdom:** Animalia
- **Phylum:** Chordata
- **Class:** Mammalia (twenty-second mammalian entry; twentieth mammalian species)
- **Infraclass:** Placentalia
- **Order:** Rodentia -- TENTH Rodentia in the engine.
- **Family:** Cricetidae -- **NEW FAMILY** (36th in engine). Cricetidae = New World mice, rats, voles, hamsters. The Sciuridae streak of eight (75-82) BREAKS.
- **Genus:** *Peromyscus* -- NEW GENUS in the engine. The most species-rich mammalian genus in North America (over 50 species).
- **Species:** *P. maniculatus* -- the Deer Mouse. The most abundant and widespread native rodent in North America [1, 2, 3].

## PHYSICAL DESCRIPTION: THE SMALL NOCTURNAL MOUSE

### Body Form

The Deer Mouse is small and adapted for nocturnal life [1, 2, 3]:

- **Length:** 12-17 cm (5-7 inches) total including tail.
- **Weight:** 10-30 g (0.35-1 oz). The smallest mammal in the engine by weight.
- **Ears:** Large, prominent, rounded. Excellent hearing.
- **Eyes:** Large, dark. Adapted for nocturnal vision.
- **Tail:** Bicolored -- dark above, white below. Shorter than head-body length.
- **Feet:** White. All four feet white ("maniculatus" -- little hands).

### Pelage

- **Body:** Soft, fine fur. Grayish-brown to reddish-brown above, varying by subspecies and habitat.
- **Belly:** Clean white. Sharply demarcated from dorsal color.
- **Tail:** Bicolored -- dark above, white below. The key field mark.

## THE SOUND: ULTRASONIC + SOFT CHIP -- 84th ACOUSTIC ELEMENT

### The Ultrasonic Threshold

The Deer Mouse produces **ultrasonic vocalizations** -- frequencies above 20,000 Hz, entirely beyond the range of human hearing [1, 2, 3, 4]:

- **Ultrasonic calls:** 30,000-90,000+ Hz. Far beyond human perception.
- **Types:** (1) Pup isolation calls -- ultrasonic cries that attract the mother. (2) Courtship calls -- ultrasonic vocalizations during reproductive encounters.
- **Function:** Social and reproductive communication in frequencies that predators (owls, foxes) may not detect as readily.

The Deer Mouse also produces **soft audible chip notes** -- extremely quiet contact calls barely perceptible even at close range:

- **Frequency:** approximately 5,000-15,000 Hz. Audible but very soft.
- **Duration:** 0.05-0.1 seconds. Brief.
- **Context:** Contact calls between individuals. Very rarely heard by humans.

### Why E=2

1. **Primary vocalizations are ULTRASONIC** -- humans cannot hear them at all.
2. **Audible calls are extremely soft** -- low amplitude, brief, barely detectable.
3. **Secretive and nocturnal** -- the animal itself is rarely encountered.
4. **E=2** reflects the near-total inaudibility to human observers.

The Northern Flying Squirrel (degree 82) was **nearly** ultrasonic (8,000-20,000+ Hz, E=3). The Deer Mouse **crosses the threshold** -- primary vocalizations are fully ultrasonic (30,000-90,000+ Hz). From nearly ultrasonic to fully ultrasonic in one degree. The acoustic frontier pushes beyond human perception.

## ECOLOGY: THE INVISIBLE ABUNDANT

### Habitat: Everywhere

The Deer Mouse is the **most habitat-generalist mammal** in the engine [1, 2, 3]:

- **Forests:** Coniferous, deciduous, mixed. Understory and ground level.
- **Grasslands:** Prairie, meadow, agricultural edges.
- **Deserts:** Arid scrublands of the interior West.
- **Alpine:** Above treeline in mountain regions.
- **Urban edges:** Sheds, woodpiles, rural buildings.
- **Range:** From the Arctic treeline to central Mexico. Coast to coast.

No single habitat defines this species. "Varied habitat" is the only accurate description.

### Abundance: The Most Abundant Native Rodent

The Deer Mouse is the **most abundant and most widespread native rodent in North America** [1, 2, 3]:

- Population densities can reach 20-30 individuals per hectare.
- Found in virtually every terrestrial habitat type.
- Total population likely numbers in the hundreds of millions.
- Yet most people have never knowingly seen one -- secretive, nocturnal, rarely seen.

### Diet: Omnivorous Opportunist

Seeds, insects, fungi, fruits, flowers. Caches seeds for winter. An omnivorous opportunist that exploits whatever food source is locally available [1, 2].

### Predators: Everything

The Deer Mouse is a **keystone prey species** -- the base of countless food webs [1, 2, 3]:

- **Spotted Owl** (SPS-004) -- preys on Deer Mice. Nocturnal predator-prey.
- **Red Fox** (SPS-071) -- preys on Deer Mice. The elemental predator-prey relationship.
- **Short-tailed Weasel** (SPS-067) -- small enough to pursue mice into burrows.
- **Owls, hawks, snakes, coyotes, skunks, bobcats, raccoons** -- nearly every predator eats Deer Mice.

The Deer Mouse is the prey that sustains the predator community. Remove the Deer Mouse and the food web collapses.
