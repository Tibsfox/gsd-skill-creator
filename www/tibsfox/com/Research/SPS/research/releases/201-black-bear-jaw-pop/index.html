Black Bear Jaw-Pop

Ursus americanus (Ursidae) — E=6 — Bear Trio 2/3
Second Black Bear entry. Bear trio (200–202): huff, jaw-pop, cub distress. Loud, explosive tooth-clacking — “POP.” Defensive intimidation. Trail conflict. E=6 — explosive percussive display. Energy contrast with Chastity Belt (E=4 + E=6).

The Jaw-Pop — Percussion of the Skull

“POP.” Sharp, explosive, percussive. Not a vocalization — a mechanical sound. The bear rapidly snaps its jaws shut, teeth clacking together, amplified by the resonant cavity of the mouth. Broadband: 100–5,000 Hz. Carries 50–100 meters through forest. Often repeated: pop-pop-pop. E=6.

Escalation Ladder — Huff to Jaw-Pop

Degree 200: Huff (E=5). “I know you’re there.” Degree 201: Jaw-pop (E=6). “Back off. Now.” The +1 energy increase matches behavioral escalation. Accompanied by head swaying, ground slaps, blowing. A multi-modal threat display. The jaw-pop is the loudest component.

Bear Trio — 200-202

200: Huff (E=5) — awareness. 201: Jaw-pop (E=6) — threat. 202: Cub distress (E=4) — need. Escalation then inversion. Adult intimidation gives way to infant vulnerability. Three voices. One species. The full emotional range.

Jaw-Pop Mechanics — Percussion Not Vocalization

Teeth clacking, mouth cavity amplification. A percussive instrument played by the bear's own skull. Broadband transient. Unique mechanism in the engine.

Trail Conflict — Defensive Not Predatory

Foundational

Bears that jaw-pop want distance. A bear that intends to attack is silent. The louder the display, the more stressed the bear. Back away slowly.
