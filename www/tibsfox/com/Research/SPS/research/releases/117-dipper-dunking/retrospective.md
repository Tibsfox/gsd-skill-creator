## Structural Event 2: Wing Hydrofoils -- Biomechanical Marvel

- **Situation:** The dipper walks underwater against currents of 0.5-2.0 m/s by using partially opened wings as hydrofoils that generate downforce.
- **Root Cause:** The wing hydrofoil solution is the key adaptation enabling the dunking behavior. Faster current = more downforce = more grip. The bird uses the river's own energy against itself. This is an inverted airplane wing principle applied underwater by a 60-gram bird.
- **Recommendation:** Document as a biomechanical marvel. Note the physics: negative lift generation, current-proportional grip, combined with claw adhesion and streamlined posture. This is engineering-grade evolutionary design.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Wing hydrofoils documented. Physics explained. Biomechanical marvel.

## Structural Event 3: Splash-Song Integration -- Three Acoustic Layers

- **Situation:** The dipper's alternation between singing and dunking creates a three-layer acoustic signature: stream drone + bubbly song + splash transients. Unique in the PNW soundscape.

## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The wing hydrofoil physics is accurate and compelling.** The principle of partially opened wings generating downforce against current is well-documented in the ornithological and biomechanics literature. The analogy to inverted airplane wings is accurate. Verified.

2. **E=5 is defensible.** The physical energy expenditure of underwater foraging in fast, cold current is enormous for a 60-gram bird. E=5 correctly reflects the athletic intensity of the dunking behavior. Consistent with degree 116 (E=5 for the song).

3. **The splash-song three-layer model is original and well-supported.** The separation of stream noise, song, and splash into three acoustic layers is analytically sound and provides a compelling framework for understanding the degree 117 acoustic signature.

4. **The double-entry pattern is well-paralleled with Pileated Woodpecker.** The structural mirror between the two double-entries (vocal/physical separation) is clean and defensible.

5. **The salmon connection is ecologically significant.** The dipper foraging for salmon eggs connects it to the salmon cycle that is central to PNW ecology -- a meaningful cross-ecosystem link.

### Source Quality

6. **Dunking behavior is well-documented.** Sullivan 1973, Kingery 1996, and Bakus 1959 all provide detailed observations. Cornell Lab of Ornithology and Macaulay Library provide supplementary data. Strong source base for a well-studied behavioral phenomenon.
