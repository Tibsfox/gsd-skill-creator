## Structural Event 1: Vespidae Enters -- 43rd Family

- **Situation:** Vespidae enters as the 43rd family. Wasps and hornets. Same order Hymenoptera as bumblebee (92).
- **Root Cause:** Hymenoptera is a large order (bees, wasps, ants). The bumblebee opened the order at degree 92; the hornet follows immediately with the aggressive side.
- **Recommendation:** Document Vespidae as 43rd family. Note the Hymenoptera pair -- consecutive same-order entries with opposite acoustic postures.
- **Carry Item:** Vespidae at degree 93. 43rd family. Hymenoptera now has two families in engine. Track.

## Structural Event 2: First Aggressive/Defensive Insect Sound

- **Situation:** Every prior insect sound was mating (cricket chirp, katydid rasp), advertising (cicada throb), or incidental flight (mosquito whine, bumblebee drone). The hornet nest buzz is the first sound with aggressive/defensive intent.
- **Root Cause:** Colony defense produces a fundamentally different acoustic posture. The sound is designed to intimidate and repel, not attract or advertise.
- **Recommendation:** Document as a new acoustic posture category: aggressive/defensive. The engine now has mating calls, advertising signals, incidental flight sounds, and defensive displays in its insect repertoire.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- First aggressive/defensive insect sound. New acoustic posture category. Track.

## Structural Event 3: Hymenoptera Pair -- E=2 to E=5

- **Situation:** Bumblebee (92, E=2) and hornet (93, E=5). Same order, consecutive degrees, energy gap of 3 levels.
- **Root Cause:** Hymenoptera encompasses both pollinators and predators. The acoustic range within the order reflects the behavioral range.
- **Recommendation:** Document the Hymenoptera pair as the widest energy gap within a consecutive same-order pair. The E=2 to E=5 jump demonstrates that taxonomic proximity does not imply acoustic similarity.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Hymenoptera pair. E=2 to E=5. Widest consecutive same-order energy gap. Track.

## What Worked

### 1. The Hymenoptera Pair Is a Genuine Structural Achievement
Consecutive same-order entries with opposite acoustic postures (gentle vs aggressive) and extreme energy contrast (E=2 vs E=5). The pair demonstrates the order's range and enriches the engine's understanding of insect sound diversity.

### 2. First Aggressive/Defensive Sound Is a Real Category Addition
The engine had mating, advertising, and incidental flight sounds from insects. Adding the aggressive/defensive posture is a genuine new category, not a variation on existing ones. The hornet nest buzz functions differently from every prior insect sound.

### 3. E=5 Is Defensible
A disturbed hornet colony is LOUD. The collective buzzing of hundreds of hornets is clearly audible at distance and overwhelming at close range. E=5 matches the cicada (89) as the highest insect energy. The assessment is defensible.

### 4. The Papery Quality Is a Genuine Acoustic Distinction
The wood-fiber nest resonates with the collective buzzing, adding a papery, rustling quality not present in any other insect sound. The acoustic coupling between nest material and wing vibration is a real phenomenon.

### 5. Thirty-Third Consecutive Original CSV
Thirty-three consecutive original species. No substitution since degree 61. The engine's own data remains reliable.
