Conservation Crisis — Over 90% Decline

Washington’s Tufted Puffin breeding population has declined by over 90% since the 1970s. Many colonies abandoned. San Juan Islands puffins functionally extinct. State Endangered (2015). Causes: ocean warming, prey collapse, Bald Eagle predation, introduced predators. The most recognizable seabird in the PNW is disappearing.

The Icon — Most Recognizable Seabird

White face. Entirely black body. Golden tufts sweeping back from the eyes. Massive orange-red bill. Unmistakable. On postcards, t-shirts, children’s books. The charismatic megafauna of the seabird world. The Pacific counterpart to the Atlantic Puffin. Known by everyone. Endangered.

Fish Carrying

Puffins carry 5–20 small fish crosswise in their bill. Held by tongue against upper mandible serrations. Dive to 60+ meters. Deepest diver of the three Alcidae in the engine. The prey availability crisis means fewer fish per trip, fewer trips per day, lower chick survival.
