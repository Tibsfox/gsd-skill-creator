Northern Pygmy-Owl

Glaucidium gnoma (Strigidae) — E=3 — Deliberate Tooting
Tooting whistled series — E=3 slow, deliberate. TIES PICIDAE NONET RECORD — ninth consecutive Strigidae. False eyes on back of head. Diurnal predator. PNW’s smallest owl. Hunts birds its own size. Fearless.

TIES THE RECORD — Nine Consecutive Strigidae

Nine consecutive owl entries (135–143). Six genera. Seven species. TIES the Picidae nonet for the all-time family streak. From the 1400g Great Horned Owl to the 62g Pygmy-Owl — a 22:1 mass ratio within one family. Degree 144 will break the record.

False Eyes — Occipital Eyespots

Dark spots bordered by white on the back of the head create a convincing illusion of a face looking back at you. Anti-predator function: the owl appears to be always watching. May also confuse mobbing songbirds. Found in several small owl species worldwide — convergent evolution.

Fearless Micro-Predator

15–18 cm. 54–73 g. Smaller than a robin. But hunts birds its own size or larger. Active pursuit through forest canopy. Diurnal. Triggers intense mobbing from dozens of songbirds. The most feared small owl. Caches multiple kills.
