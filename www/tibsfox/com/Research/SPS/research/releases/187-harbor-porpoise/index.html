Harbor Porpoise — Silence

Phocoena phocoena (Phocoenidae) — E=1 — NEW ABSOLUTE MINIMUM
E=1 — NEW ENGINE ABSOLUTE MINIMUM. Below previous E=2 (degrees 119, 144). No audible vocalization. Echolocation at >120 kHz — 6–7x above human hearing. No whistles. No social sounds audible to humans. The quietest entry in the entire engine. Second Phocoenidae. Silence is the sound.

E=1 — The Absolute Minimum

The Harbor Porpoise produces no sounds that humans can hear. Echolocation at 120–150 kHz — far above the ~20 kHz ceiling of human perception. No whistles. No audible social sounds. Brief surfacing. No breaching. No splashing. E=1. The floor. The signal exists — we simply cannot hear it.

Silence as Survival — Anti-Predator Adaptation

NBHF echolocation above orca hearing range. No whistles (unlike dolphins). No broadband sounds. The Harbor Porpoise has eliminated ALL audible sound from its repertoire. Orcas cannot hear it. Humans cannot hear it. Silence = survival. The cost: limited social communication. Group coordination relies on proximity and visual cues, not voice.

Present but Unseen — The Hidden Cetacean

10,000–15,000 in Washington inland waters. Year-round resident. Recolonizing southern Puget Sound since the 2000s. Small body (1.4–1.9 m). Brief surfacings. Avoids boats. Does not bow-ride. Rarely breaches. Present in the Salish Sea every day of the year — and most people have never seen one.
