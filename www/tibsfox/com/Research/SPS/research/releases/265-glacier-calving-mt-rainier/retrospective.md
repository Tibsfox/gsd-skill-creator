## What Worked

1. **Mt Rainier's glaciological significance is historically grounded.** 25 named glaciers, ~35 square miles of ice, most glaciated peak in the lower 48 -- these are documented USGS and NPS figures. The research foundation is solid.
2. **The multi-phase acoustic signature (crack + cascade) is physically accurate.** Ice fracture mechanics, fall and impact, rumble cascade with valley reverberation -- each phase is distinct and documentable.
3. **The Carbon/Emmons contrast is productive.** Two very different glaciers (lowest elevation / largest area) with distinct acoustic environments (enclosed valley / open flank) -- this contrast enriches the research.
4. **The summer calving thermal dynamics are well-explained.** Solar loading, meltwater penetration, diurnal cycles -- the physics grounds the seasonal designation.

## Discoveries

1. **Carbon Glacier is remarkably low.** 3,500-foot terminus elevation at this latitude is exceptional. The glacier exists in territory where no glacier "should" be by typical expectations -- the maritime climate and the mountain's relief make it possible.
2. **The valley geometry is integral to the acoustic signature.** Glacier calving is not just about the ice -- the terrain the ice exists in determines what the sound becomes. This is a recurring theme in the Geophysics arc.
