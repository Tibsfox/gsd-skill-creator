## What Worked

1. **The 6PPD-quinone documentation is the entry's most significant contribution.** The 2020 discovery of a tire rubber chemical as the cause of urban Coho pre-spawn mortality is a major scientific finding -- one of the most important conservation discoveries in Pacific Northwest salmon biology in decades. The engine's permanent record now contains the full account: discovery, mechanism, species-specific toxicity, mortality rates, and the legislative response.
2. **The acoustic distinction between Chinook (E=6) and Coho (E=6) is important and well-documented.** Same energy level, completely different acoustic character: Chinook = episodic heavy impacts at a waterfall; Coho = continuous chaotic splashing in shallow riffles. The shallow-water amplification rationale is physically grounded.
3. **The Beaver-Coho ecology link is a genuine connection across the engine.** American Beaver (61-62) creates the slow, deep pond habitat that Coho juveniles require for winter rearing. The cross-reference from degree 238 back to degrees 61-62 is ecologically valid and enriches both entries.
4. **The stream-specialist framing distinguishes Coho from Chinook accurately.** Both are Pacific salmon; their ecological roles in the watershed are genuinely different because of habitat range.

## Discoveries

1. **The Washington State SB 5804 (2023) is the first legislation anywhere in the world directly targeting tire rubber chemistry for wildlife protection.** This is a significant first in environmental law -- worth recording in the engine as a legal-historical milestone.
2. **The DHODH mechanism explains the species-specific toxicity.** Coho lack a protective mutation at the DHODH enzyme active site that other salmonids have. The toxicity is not arbitrary -- it has a specific molecular explanation. This is important for understanding which salmon populations are at risk.
