## Discoveries

1. **The orca breach as intentional communication over distance is the most interesting behavioral observation.** Surface slaps from full-body breaches can be heard by pods separated by several kilometers. In the SRKW, breach rates increase during social gatherings. The breach is not random exuberance -- it is a calculated acoustic event with communicative function. This is the detail that makes the pairing with the Blood Brothers structurally coherent: neither is chaos. Both are structured.
2. **The wave mechanics of colonial alarm propagation are genuinely fascinating.** Each tier of birds responds to both the original stimulus and the response of the tier below simultaneously. The alarm is not instantaneous across the whole colony -- it propagates, tier by tier, from the lowest ledges upward. The result is a temporal layering in the acoustic event: first the lowest-ledge birds, then each successive tier, creating a sweeping crescendo rather than an instantaneous all-at-once. This temporal structure means the full colony alarm has internal dynamics that persist for several seconds after the initial trigger.
