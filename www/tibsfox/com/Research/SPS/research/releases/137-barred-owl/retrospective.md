## The One Hundred Thirty-Seventh Degree: The Eastern Invader in PNW Old-Growth

### The Barred Owl at degree 137 introduces a new species to the Strigidae era -- the third consecutive owl entry (135-136-137). The "Who cooks for you?" mnemonic is the most recognizable owl phrase in North American birding: 8 notes at 400-550 Hz, rich and hoarse timbre. E=4 reflects measured, deliberate phrasing. The Barred Owl is an eastern species that has expanded westward across the continent into PNW old-growth, creating a conservation crisis for the Northern Spotted Owl (SPS degree 4). This is the first species in the engine documented as ecologically antagonistic to a previously documented species.

- **Situation:** Barred Owl at degree 137. E=4. Third consecutive Strigidae. New species. Eastern invader. Spotted Owl conflict.

## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **E=4 is defensible.** The "Who cooks for you?" hooting is measured, deliberate, and rhythmic -- not the explosive energy of E=5 or E=6. It matches the GHO solo hoot (135, E=4) in energy level but for different acoustic reasons. The caterwauling (138, E=6) gets maximum energy separately.

2. **The Spotted Owl conflict is well-documented.** Wiens et al. 2014, Livezey 2009, and USFWS sources provide comprehensive documentation of the competitive exclusion, hybridization, and removal programs.

3. **The range expansion chronology is accurate.** BC in 1940s-1960s, Washington by 1970s-1980s, now abundant throughout PNW. Livezey 2009 provides the definitive range expansion analysis.

4. **The vocal mnemonic analysis is accurate.** Cornell Lab and Sibley confirm the 8-note phrase structure and 400-550 Hz frequency range.

5. **Cross-references to SPS-004 (Spotted Owl), SPS-135/136 (GHO), and SPS-138 (caterwauling) are all defensible.**
