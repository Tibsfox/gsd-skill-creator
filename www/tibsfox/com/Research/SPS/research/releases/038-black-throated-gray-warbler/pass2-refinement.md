The Black-throated Gray Warbler is the sixth Parulidae — the warbler quintet (degrees 14-17, 20) extends to a sextet. This warbler occupies oak-pine woodland — a dry habitat distinct from the riparian and shrub habitats of the previous five warblers. The habitat diversification within Parulidae continues: dense shrub (14), willow riparian (15), riparian willow (16), marsh edge (17), shrubby slope (20), and now oak-pine (38). Six warblers, six habitats.

The "lazy buzzy wheeze" is the most relaxed vocal delivery of any warbler in the engine — a slow, drawled, buzzy song that sounds like the bird is singing through a yawn. Where the Wilson's Warbler accelerated (degree 15) and the Yellow Warbler crescendoed (degree 16), the Black-throated Gray Warbler decelerates — or rather, never accelerates. The tempo is slow from start to finish. The laziest warbler song in the engine.

Gemini 3 performed the first orbital maneuver by a crewed spacecraft — changing its orbit, proving that humans could steer in space. The Black-throated Gray Warbler's oak-pine habitat is the first dry-side habitat for any warbler in the engine — the previous five warblers all occupied moist habitats. The warbler has maneuvered to a new orbital zone: dry instead of wet, oak instead of willow, lazy instead of urgent. The orbital maneuver as habitat shift.
