## What Worked

1. **FIRST CORVIDAE is correctly identified as the engine's most significant avian milestone entry.** All previous avian entries have been documented as acoustic events -- the sound a bird makes and its ecological context. The Raven entry adds a cognitive dimension that is new to the engine: the vocalization is an expression of active cognition. Theory of mind, tool use, future planning, play -- these are not incidental to the entry; they are the explanatory framework for why the Raven has 30+ call types and produces extended maximum vocabulary displays. The acoustic complexity is a product of the cognitive complexity.
2. **The theory of mind documentation (Bugnyar food-cache study) is the entry's most analytically important scientific reference.** The re-caching behavior when observed -- moving a food cache after the observer has seen the original location -- requires the caching bird to model the observer's knowledge state. This is not conditioned behavior; it is perspective-taking. It is one of the strongest demonstrations of non-primate, non-ape theory-of-mind precursors in the scientific record. It is the single most important cognitive fact the engine can document about the Raven.
3. **The wolf-Raven vocalization strategy is the entry's most behaviorally striking contribution.** Using vocal mimicry to recruit a mammal to do mechanical work (carcass opening) is a cross-species cognitive strategy that has no parallel in the engine's biological entries. The vocalization is not communication for its own sake; it is a tool for manipulating another species' behavior. The Raven uses sound as a cognitive tool. This is the degree-320 SPS entry's most memorable single fact.
4. **The FIRSTS pairing (Raven + Alcohol Funnycar) correctly identifies the degree-320 theme.** Both are first appearances. Both reach E=7 through opposite acoustic philosophies: maximum vocabulary (Raven, 30+ types, 5-20 minute session) vs. maximum economy (Alcohol Funnycar, 2-minute punk songs, minimum form). The same energy level reached through cognitive complexity and through formal minimalism simultaneously. The degree-320 pairing is the engine's clearest demonstration that E=7 contains multiple acoustic philosophies.
5. **The PNW First Nations cosmological connection is correctly positioned.** The Raven's cultural significance in Haida, Tlingit, and other Pacific Northwest Indigenous cosmologies is commensurate with its actual cognitive and acoustic standing. The trickster/creator framework is not mysticism -- it is accurate empirical observation of a bird that deceives, plans, plays, and adapts. The engine documents both the scientific cognitive record and the cultural recognition that preceded it.

## Discoveries

1. **The Raven is the engine's first entry where the acoustic complexity directly reflects cognitive complexity.** Previous complex acoustic events (Orca greeting, E=9; Humpback song, E=8) reflect behavioral complexity and cultural transmission. The Raven's acoustic complexity reflects individual cognitive processing: a bird that has theory of mind, plans for the future, and uses vocal mimicry as a cognitive tool will naturally produce the most varied and complex vocal repertoire in the PNW. The acoustic and cognitive complexities are not independent; they are causally linked.
2. **The full Corvidae family arc is now opened.** FIRST CORVIDAE is not just a Raven entry; it is the beginning of the engine's most cognitively rich bird family arc. American Crow (15-20 call types, widespread, urban/rural), Steller's Jay (alarm specialist, forest ecology), Clark's Nutcracker (spatial memory specialist for whitebark pine seed caching -- one of the most documented spatial memory capacities in birds), Canada Jay (aggressive food-caching, boreal forest), Northwestern Crow (coastal Pacific coast specialist) -- all enter in future degrees with the cognitive register established here.
3. **The wolf-Raven relationship is the most complex cross-species relationship in the PNW engine.** Wolf and Raven share a documented ecological relationship that spans behavioral ecology (Ravens following wolf packs to exploit kills), acoustic interaction (Raven mimicking wolf to recruit wolves to carcasses), and Native cosmological association in many Pacific Northwest cultures. The engine has documented wolves (degree 303, E=8, full pack howl) and now documents the Raven-wolf acoustic strategy. The two entries are connected.
