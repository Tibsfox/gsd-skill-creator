## Discoveries

1. **The frequency separation principle is the key insight for multi-species simultaneous soundscapes.** Species that coexist acoustically in the same temporal space tend to occupy different frequency registers -- this is acoustic niche partitioning. The degree-321 three-layer stack is a clear example: 300-500 Hz (owl), 500-3000 Hz (frogs), 4000-8000 Hz (crickets). The three species avoid masking each other by broadcasting on different channels. This principle recurs in multi-species acoustic entries throughout the engine.
2. **Spatial separation adds a directional dimension to the frequency separation.** The soundscape at degree 321 is not only spectrally distributed; it is spatially distributed. The frogs call from in front (the water); the owl calls from behind or to the side (the woodland); the crickets call from below (the ground). A listener at the pond edge experiences the layers as coming from different directions.
