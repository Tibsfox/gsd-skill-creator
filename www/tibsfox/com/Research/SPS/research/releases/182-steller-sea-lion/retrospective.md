*Degree 182 retrospective complete. STELLER SEA LION at the one hundred eighty-second degree -- FIRST OTARIIDAE. The eared seals enter the engine, and they enter BIG. Eumetopias jubatus -- the Steller Sea Lion -- is the largest sea lion in the world. Males reach 3.3 meters and 1,120 kilograms (2,500 pounds). That is roughly the weight of a grizzly bear. On a rocky haul-out, the visual impact is extraordinary -- an animal that seems to belong to a different geological era. The colony roar at breeding rookeries creates a wall of sound: bulls roaring with deep, sustained, thunderous vocalizations (100-3000 Hz), cows calling, pups bleating, hundreds of massive animals in an intense social environment. This is E=6 -- the SPS ceiling -- because no PNW pinniped is louder. Named for Georg Wilhelm Steller, the naturalist on Vitus Bering's 1741 expedition to the North Pacific, the Steller Sea Lion carries the legacy of European exploration of the PNW coast. But it also carries the weight of conservation crisis: the western DPS has declined approximately 80% and is ESA Endangered, while the eastern DPS (including PNW populations) has recovered sufficiently for delisting in 2013. And across the degree pair, Criminal Nation's gangsta rap confrontation matches the Steller's raw power -- E=7 + E=6 = E=13, the highest combined energy in the engine. Both are raw power announcing its presence. The roar that shakes the ground. v1.49.323.*
