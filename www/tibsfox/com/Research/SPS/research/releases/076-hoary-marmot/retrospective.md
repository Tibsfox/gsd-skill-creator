## Structural Event 1: Back-to-Back Sciuridae -- The Marmot Pair

- **Situation:** Yellow-bellied Marmot (75, Sciuridae) and Hoary Marmot (76, Sciuridae) are consecutive Sciuridae -- same family, different species, consecutive degrees. The marmot pair. Fourth family pair after Anatidae (58-59), Castoridae (61-62), Mustelidae (67-68).

## Structural Event 3: Largest North American Marmot

- **Situation:** At 5-7 kg, the Hoary Marmot is the largest marmot in North America and the largest rodent in the engine after the American Beaver (61-62, 11-32 kg).
- **Root Cause:** The Hoary Marmot's large body mass is an adaptation to extreme alpine cold -- larger body size provides better thermoregulation (reduced surface-area-to-volume ratio) for surviving long alpine winters.
- **Recommendation:** Document as the largest marmot. Note the size contrast with Yellow-bellied Marmot (2-5 kg) and Pika (120-185 g) -- three size classes in three consecutive degrees.
- **Carry Item:** Largest rodent in engine: Beaver (11-32 kg). Second largest: Hoary Marmot (5-7 kg). Track.

## Structural Event 4: Longest Hibernation in the Engine

- **Situation:** 7-8 months of hibernation. The Hoary Marmot is active for only 4-5 months per year. The longest dormancy period of any species in the engine.
- **Root Cause:** Alpine winters at 1,500-3,000+ meters are too severe for surface activity. The Hoary Marmot's hibernation strategy is the opposite of the Pika's hay-gathering strategy -- fat storage vs. food storage, dormancy vs. year-round activity.
- **Recommendation:** Document as the longest hibernation. Note the contrast with Pika (74, NO hibernation) -- same alpine habitat, opposite survival strategies.
- **Carry Item:** Longest hibernation: Hoary Marmot (76), 7-8 months. Contrast: Pika (74), no hibernation. Track.

## Structural Event 6: Whistler Mountain -- The Marmot That Named a Place

- **Situation:** Whistler Mountain and the city of Whistler, BC, are named for the Hoary Marmot's whistle. No other species in the engine has named a major geographic feature through its vocalization.
- **Root Cause:** The Hoary Marmot's whistle is so characteristic of alpine travel in the PNW/BC that early visitors named the mountain for the sound. The sound became a place name.
- **Recommendation:** Document the toponymic significance. Note the BC connection extending engine geography beyond the US border. Track vocalization-to-place-name connections.
- **Carry Item:** Toponymic species: 1 (Hoary Marmot -- Whistler Mountain/Whistler, BC). BC enters engine geography. Track.
