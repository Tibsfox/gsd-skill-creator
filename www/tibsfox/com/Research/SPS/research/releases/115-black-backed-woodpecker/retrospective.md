## Structural Event 4: Second Fire-Specialist -- Obligate vs. Opportunistic

- **Situation:** Lewis's Woodpecker (112, opportunistic fire-dependent) and Black-backed Woodpecker (115, obligate fire-dependent). Two fire-specialists in the Picidae streak.
- **Root Cause:** Fire ecology has a spectrum of dependence. Lewis's uses burned forest but can also use other open habitats. Black-backed requires burned forest -- it is essentially absent from unburned forest. The two fire-specialists document this spectrum.
- **Recommendation:** Document the spectrum of fire-dependence. Lewis's = opportunistic. Black-backed = obligate. Track fire ecology connections in future degrees.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Fire-specialist spectrum: opportunistic (Lewis's) to obligate (Black-backed).

## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The fire ghost concept accurately captures the species' ecology.** The Black-backed Woodpecker's fire-dependence is well-documented in the literature. The term "fire ghost" reflects the species' behavior: appearing in burned landscapes, exploiting the beetle bonanza, disappearing as the burn ages. Verified.

2. **E=5 is defensible.** The Black-backed Woodpecker's rattle is loud and assertive, carrying across open post-fire landscapes. Not the overwhelming E=6 of the Pileated, but louder and more emphatic than the Three-toed (E=4) or Downy (E=3). E=5 correctly reflects high energy in an open acoustic environment.

3. **The conservation paradox is well-documented.** Fire suppression, salvage logging, and the tension between fire management philosophy and species conservation are extensively covered in the literature (Hutto 2008, Siegel et al. 2018).

4. **The three-toed adaptation pair (114-115) is structurally perfect.** The only two three-toed woodpeckers in North America, documented consecutively. The biomechanical advantage is well-established.

5. **Cross-references to SPS-114 (Three-toed), SPS-112 (Lewis's), SPS-108 (Pileated laughing), SPS-107 (Acorn), and SPS-004 (Spotted Owl) are all defensible.**

### Source Quality

6. **Black-backed Woodpecker ecology is extensively studied.** Hutto 2008 is the foundational paper on fire-following woodpeckers. Nappi et al. 2015 and Siegel et al. 2018 provide detailed population and habitat data. Cornell Lab of Ornithology provides comprehensive life history. Strong source base.
