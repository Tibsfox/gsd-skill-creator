## What Worked

1. **FIRST OTARIIDAE is a genuine family milestone.** After Delphinidae (orca arc, then Pacific White-sided Dolphin) and Phocoenidae (Dall's Porpoise), Otariidae enters. The family distinctions are clear and documented: external ear pinnae, rear flipper rotation, front flipper swimming, more complex social and vocal behavior.
2. **The Steller naming correction is important.** Steller, not Stellar. Named for a specific person: Georg Wilhelm Steller (1709-1746), who died at 37 and is now attached to four PNW species including the extinct Sea Cow. The naming history is PNW natural history.
3. **E=8 as arc peak for 281-284 is correctly calibrated.** Dall's Porpoise E=6, Pacific White-sided Dolphin E=7, Steller Sea Lion E=8, California Sea Lion E=7. The Steller bull roar is the sustained maximum of this four-degree block.
4. **The western stock crisis is essential documentation.** ~80% decline in two decades. ESA listed. The same conservation context as the SRKW at 271 -- the Pacific Northwest's marine mammals are documented with their conservation reality intact.
5. **The mane connection to both the roar and the combat protection is genuine biology.** The same anatomy that produces the low-frequency resonance of the roar also protects the neck during territorial combat. One structure, two functions.
