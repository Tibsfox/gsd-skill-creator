Mountain Beaver

Aplodontia rufa (Aplodontidae) — E=2 — Living Fossil
FIRST APLODONTIDAE — the most primitive living rodent family. Sole surviving species. ~50 million year lineage. Found nowhere on Earth except the PNW. Thin whistle at 2,000–6,000 Hz. Barely audible. E=2 — quietest mammal in the engine. NOT a beaver.

Living Fossil — ~50 Million Years

Aplodontidae dates to the Eocene. Every other living rodent family evolved after this one. Sole surviving species — all other aplodontids extinct. Primitive kidneys. Primitive thermoregulation. Ancient skull. These are not deficiencies — they are the original condition.

NOT a Beaver — The Misnomer

No relation to Castoridae. 10–20x smaller (0.8–1.5 kg vs. 15–30 kg). No dams. No lodges. No flat tail (vestigial stub). Early settlers saw gnawing + water proximity and made assumptions. Some prefer “sewellel” (Chinook Jargon) or simply Aplodontia.

Dense Understory — The Invisible Mammal

Lives beneath the canopy in the darkest, most dense forest layer. Sword fern. Salal. Devil’s club. Burrow systems with 10–20+ entrances. Cut fern fronds at a burrow entrance = occupancy sign. Nocturnal. Rarely seen. Water-dependent (primitive kidneys require constant moisture).
