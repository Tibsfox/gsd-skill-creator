Long-billed Curlew — Rising “Curleee!”

Numenius americanus (Scolopacidae) — E=5 — The Extreme Curlew
FOURTH SCOLOPACIDAE — longest bill of any North American shorebird: up to 21 cm (8.5 in). Largest shorebird on the continent. Loud rising “curleee!” carries 1+ km. Curlew pair with Whimbrel (170) COMPLETE. E=5. v1.49.311.

Extreme Bill — 21 cm, Longest in North America

Up to 21 cm (8.5 inches) — nearly as long as the bird’s body. Extremely down-curved. Reaches deep into ghost shrimp burrows, crab tunnels, prairie soil. Females have longer bills than males. The Whimbrel’s bill (7–9 cm) is less than half this length. Moderate vs extreme.

Grassland Giant — Conservation Concern

Grassland-obligate breeder — dependent on native Great Plains prairie. Habitat loss (conversion to agriculture) threatens populations. Near Threatened. The warm cinnamon plumage and bright cinnamon underwings are diagnostic.
