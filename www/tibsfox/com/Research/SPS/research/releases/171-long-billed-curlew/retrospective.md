*Degree 171 retrospective complete. LONG-BILLED CURLEW at the one hundred seventy-first degree -- THE EXTREME EXTENSION. The longest bill of any North American shorebird -- up to 21 centimeters, 8.5 inches, nearly as long as the bird's body. Extremely down-curved, following a smooth, elegant arc. The bill reaches deep into ghost shrimp burrows, crab tunnels, and prairie soil. The Long-billed Curlew is also the largest shorebird on the continent -- 50 to 65 centimeters long, up to 950 grams, heavy-bodied and warm cinnamon-buff in plumage, with bright cinnamon underwings that flash diagnostic in flight. And the call: a loud, clear, rising whistle -- "curleee!" or "cur-LEE!" -- that carries over a kilometer across open grassland. The name "curlew" is onomatopoeic, derived from this very call.
