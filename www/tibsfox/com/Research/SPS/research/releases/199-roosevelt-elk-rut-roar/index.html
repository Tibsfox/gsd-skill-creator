Roosevelt Elk Rut Roar

Cervus canadensis roosevelti (Cervidae) — E=7 — Elk Trio Complete
Third Roosevelt Elk entry. Elk trio completes (197–199). Deep sustained bellowing at 80–400 Hz. Combat vocalization during antler contests. E=7 — raw power, primal, the contest itself. Combined E=13 with Modest Mouse — highest pair energy.

The Rut Roar — Deep Sustained Bellowing

80–400 Hz. Deep, raw, guttural. Not the announcing whistle of the bugle but the direct challenge — face to face, 10 meters apart. The roar vibrates from the chest of a 300–500 kg bull. Sustained 3–8 seconds. The sound of maximum physical intensity. “I will not back down.”

Antler Combat — When Roars Aren’t Enough

Escalation: bugle → parallel walk → roar → antler lock. Two bulls push, 300–500 kg each. Contests last 30 seconds to 30 minutes. The winner controls the harem: 10–30 cows. Injuries possible. Fatalities documented but rare. The roar continues during combat — grunting, bellowing, exertion.

Elk Trio Complete — 197-199

197: Bugle (E=7) — display, announcement. 198: Cow call (E=5) — alarm, maternal contact. 199: Rut roar (E=7) — combat, direct challenge. E=7, E=5, E=7. Symmetrical. Display, connection, combat. The full vocal repertoire of the Roosevelt Elk in three degrees.
