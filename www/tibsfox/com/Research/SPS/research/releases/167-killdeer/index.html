Killdeer — Kill-Dee Alarm

Charadrius vociferus (Charadriidae) — E=5 — The Broken-Wing Actor
FIRST CHARADRIIDAE — plover family enters. The Killdeer’s broken-wing distraction display is the most famous deception in birding. Its “kill-DEE! kill-DEE!” alarm is loud, insistent, and the source of its name. Species name vociferus — the vociferous plover. E=5. v1.49.307.

The Broken-Wing Display — Most Famous Bird Deception

When a predator approaches the nest, the Killdeer leaves and drops to the ground with one wing dragging, appearing injured. It moves erratically, calling loudly, luring the predator away. When the predator has been led far enough, the bird “recovers” and flies back. The display is graduated — intensity calibrated to threat level. Effective against foxes, coyotes, cats, dogs, crows, and humans.

Vociferus — The Vociferous Plover

Both names reference the voice. “Killdeer” is onomatopoeia of the call. Vociferus means “carrying voice” — loud and insistent. Named by Linnaeus in 1758. The name has stuck for nearly 270 years because it remains perfectly accurate. The bird has not become quieter.

First Charadriidae — Plover Family Enters

67 species worldwide. Plovers, lapwings, dotterels. Short stout bills. Run-stop-peck foraging. Open habitat specialists. The Killdeer is the most adaptable — nesting on parking lots, gravel rooftops, and construction sites alongside natural habitats. A Walmart parking lot = a gravel bar on a river.

Kill-Dee Alarm

Loud, insistent, repeated kill-DEE! kill-DEE! 2500-4500 Hz. Piercing. Can continue for minutes. Insistence is the defining characteristic. E=5.

Broken-Wing Display

Advanced

Most famous bird deception. Feigns injury to lure predators. Graduated response -- intensity matches threat level. Effective against diverse predators. Performance as defense.

Intermediate

Scrape in gravel. No concealment. Parking lots, rooftops, construction sites. Cryptic eggs. Precocial chicks. Defense entirely behavioral. Double-brooding.
