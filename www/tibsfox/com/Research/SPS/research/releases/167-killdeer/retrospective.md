*Degree 167 retrospective complete. KILLDEER ALARM at the one hundred sixty-seventh degree -- THE BROKEN-WING ACTOR. On the open ground of the PNW -- parking lots, gravel rooftops, agricultural fields, mudflats, construction sites -- a medium-sized plover with two black breast bands and a red eye-ring runs across the bare surface, stops, tilts its head, and begins screaming: "Kill-DEE! kill-DEE! kill-DEE!" The call is loud, shrill, insistent, and impossible to ignore. Linnaeus named it *Charadrius vociferus* in 1758 -- the vociferous plover -- and the name remains perfectly accurate 270 years later. Both names reference the voice: Killdeer is onomatopoeia; vociferus means loud-voiced. When a predator approaches the nest -- a shallow scrape in open gravel, concealed by nothing but the cryptic coloring of the eggs -- the Killdeer performs the most famous deception in birding. It leaves the nest and runs away, then drops to the ground with one wing extended and dragging, as if broken. It moves erratically, calling loudly, appearing to be an easy catch. The predator follows. When it has been led far enough, the bird suddenly "recovers," takes flight, and circles back. The broken-wing display is graduated: the intensity matches the threat level, from mild alarm for distant disturbance to full theatrical performance for close approach. Effective against foxes, coyotes, cats, dogs, crows, and humans. Performance as defense. The chicks are precocial -- running and foraging within hours of hatching, scattering and freezing when the parent gives the alarm. No nest structure. No concealment. Just cryptic coloring, precocial development, and the loudest, most insistent alarm call on open ground. The Charadriidae enters the engine at degree 167: the plover family, 67 species worldwide, and the Killdeer is its most adaptable representative. And across the degree pair, ShowYouSuck brings joyful intensity to Capitol Hill -- another performer who is loudly visible in open space, using performance to maintain position. The loudest voice in the open: celebration and alarm. v1.49.307.*
