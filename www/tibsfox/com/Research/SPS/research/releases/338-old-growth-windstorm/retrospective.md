## What Worked

1. **The species differentiation clarifies the multi-layer acoustic character.** Douglas-fir provides the low-frequency structural bass; Sitka Spruce provides the distinctive coastal "singing"; Western Red Cedar provides the most textured mid-frequency wash. Each species has a specific acoustic role in the windstorm soundscape. This is one of the engine's most species-specific sound analyses.
2. **The non-catastrophic maximum framing clarifies the E=7 rating.** Beaufort 8-10 is maximum canopy motion before structural failure at scale. Above 130 km/h gusts, the event shifts to catastrophic -- qualitatively different. E=7 is specifically the pre-catastrophe sustained maximum. This calibration is precise and defensible.
3. **The 80-90% old-growth loss statistic grounds the entry in conservation reality.** This sound requires 400 years to develop and can only be heard in the remaining 10-20% of old-growth. The sound is rare because its substrate is rare.
4. **The Olympic Peninsula Hoh Rainforest specificity.** 140-200 inches annual precipitation, densest North American biomass north of tropics, Sitka Spruce up to 3m diameter at 400+ years. The most intact coastal old-growth in the contiguous US is the correct specific location.

## Discoveries

1. **Western Red Cedar's pendulous lacy branch structure produces the most textured acoustic output per unit volume of any Pacific Northwest old-growth tree.** This is not a well-documented finding in existing forest acoustics literature. The Cedar's contribution -- the mid-frequency wash at 500-2,000 Hz -- is the most texturally complex single-species contribution to the windstorm soundscape.
2. **The gust modulation pattern (10-15 dB variation at 30-120 second intervals as gust cells pass) creates temporal rhythm in what might otherwise be undifferentiated continuous noise.** The windstorm has rhythm -- not biological or mechanical rhythm but meteorological rhythm. This connects to the broader SPS engine theme of finding rhythmic structure in natural systems.
