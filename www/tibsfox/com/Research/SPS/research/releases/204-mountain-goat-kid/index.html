Mountain Goat Kid

Oreamnos americanus (Bovidae) — E=3 — Alpine Nursery
Second Mountain Goat entry. Adult-kid pair (203–204). High, thin “meeeh” at 400–1,000 Hz. An infant on an impossible cliff, calling for its mother. E=3 — thin voice in vast terrain. Precocial: climbing within days of birth. Second Bovidae.

The Alpine Nursery — Born on the Cliff

Born late May on the most inaccessible cliff ledge the nanny can find. Standing within hours. Climbing within days. Rubbery-pad hooves grip rock innately. By the first week, the kid crosses terrain that would challenge an experienced mountaineer. 3–4 kg at birth. 15–25 kg by fall.

Adult-Kid Pair — 203-204

203: Adult bleat (200–600 Hz, E=4). “I am here.” 204: Kid mew (400–1,000 Hz, E=3). “Where are you?” Same species, same impossible cliff. One octave higher. -1 energy. The adult states. The kid asks. Same pattern as fawn-buck (195–196).

The Eagle Paradox — Survival Trade-off

The kid’s mew maintains maternal contact (survival necessity) but may attract Golden Eagles (survival risk). The nanny stays close, responds fast, minimizes distress duration. The mew is a trade-off: the sound that saves the kid is also the sound that endangers it.
