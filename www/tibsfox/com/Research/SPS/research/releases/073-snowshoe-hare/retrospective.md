1. **NEW ORDER: LAGOMORPHA (4th MAMMALIAN ORDER).** Lagomorphs diverged from rodents ~80-90 million years ago. Not rodents. Two families: Leporidae (rabbits and hares) and Ochotonidae (pikas). ~92 species worldwide.

2. **NEW FAMILY: LEPORIDAE (33rd FAMILY).** Fifth new family in five consecutive degrees. The most intense period of family-level taxonomic expansion in engine history extends by one degree. Unprecedented five-degree streak.

3. **FIRST PURE PREY MAMMAL.** Every mammal since degree 61 has been a predator or omnivore. The Snowshoe Hare is defined entirely by its prey status. Its biology is shaped by the pressure of being hunted.

4. **THUMP ON GROUND / SQUEAL IF CAUGHT -- 74th acoustic element.** Alarm hind-foot percussion (ground-coupled warning) and distress squeal (upon capture). E=3. The first pure prey vocalization. The first ground-coupled alarm.

5. **SEASONAL COAT CHANGE (second after SPS-067).** Brown in summer, white in winter. Predator-prey parallel with the Short-tailed Weasel: same adaptation, opposite purposes (hunting vs. hiding).

6. **LYNX-HARE POPULATION CYCLE.** The 10-year cycle. 200+ years of documentation. Ecology's most famous predator-prey dynamic. The textbook example.

7. **SNOWSHOE FEET.** Disproportionately large, densely furred hind feet that function as natural snowshoes. The defining physical adaptation. The trait that gives the species its name.

8. **KEYSTONE PREY SPECIES.** The Snowshoe Hare feeds 10+ predator species. When the hare crashes, the entire predator community is affected. The hare IS the food web.

9. **THIRTEENTH MAMMALIAN ENTRY.** Eleventh species. Four orders. Seven families. Two infraclasses. 17.6% of entries (13 of 74). Thirteen consecutive mammalian degrees.

- **Situation:** The Snowshoe Hare introduces order Lagomorpha -- the 4th mammalian order. Lagomorphs diverged from rodents ~80-90 million years ago. Not rodents -- key differences include 4 upper incisors, strictly herbivorous diet, cecotrophy, and ecological role as pure prey.
- **Root Cause:** The CSV places the Snowshoe Hare at degree 73. The species is a native year-round resident in Washington. Its inclusion introduces a new mammalian order, adding taxonomic depth to the engine.
- **Recommendation:** Document as a significant taxonomic event. Four mammalian orders in thirteen degrees shows genuine breadth: Rodentia (engineering), Carnivora (predation), Didelphimorphia (marsupial), Lagomorpha (prey). The engine's mammalian sampling covers four distinct ecological strategies.
- **Carry Item:** Mammalian orders: 4 (Rodentia 2, Carnivora 9, Didelphimorphia 1, Lagomorpha 1). Four orders across thirteen entries.

## What Worked

### 1. The First Pure Prey Mammal Is Structurally Transformative
The engine needed a prey species. Twelve consecutive predators and omnivores created a lopsided food web. The Snowshoe Hare completes the picture -- now the engine has what gets hunted, not just what hunts. The prey perspective transforms the predator entries retroactively.

### 2. The Lynx-Hare Cycle Is a Perfect Ecological Story
The most famous predator-prey dynamic in ecology entering the engine. 200+ years of data. The textbook example. This is the kind of ecological content the engine was built for.

### 3. The Seasonal Coat-Change Parallel Is Excellent
The weasel (67) and the hare (73) as a matched pair -- same adaptation, opposite purposes. Predator and prey, both white in winter, both brown in summer. One of the strongest ecological connections in the engine.

### 4. Five New Families in Five Degrees Is an Unprecedented Streak
Mephitidae, Procyonidae, Canidae, Didelphidae, Leporidae. Five consecutive new families. The engine's taxonomy is expanding at an extraordinary rate.

### 5. E=3 Is Correct
The hare is moderately audible -- more than the secretive opossum (E=2) but not conspicuously vocal. The thump is loud but brief. The squeal is piercing but rare. E=3 captures the alarm-event energy.

### 6. The LAKE Parallel Works
Both in the understory. Both quiet until startled. Both communal. Both seasonal. The parallel captures the hare's essential character: hidden, communal, attuned to cycles.
