## Structural Event 1: Ranidae Enters -- 46th Family

- **Situation:** Ranidae enters as the 46th family. True frogs. Second anuran family (after Hylidae at degrees 86 and 90). Third amphibian entry in the engine.
- **Root Cause:** The engine returns to amphibians after nine insect entries (87-95). Ranidae (true frogs) is a distinct family from Hylidae (tree frogs). The wood frog is one of the most widespread frogs in North America and the northernmost-ranging North American frog.

## Structural Event 2: Freeze Tolerance -- First Freeze-Tolerant Species

- **Situation:** The wood frog survives being frozen solid -- up to 65% of body water as ice, heart stops, thaws in spring to resume normal activity. First freeze-tolerant species in the engine.
- **Root Cause:** Freeze tolerance is one of the most remarkable survival strategies in vertebrate biology. The wood frog produces cryoprotectant glucose at 100x normal levels, preventing intracellular ice damage. This is not hibernation or torpor -- the frog is functionally dead and revives.
- **Recommendation:** Document freeze tolerance as a core ecological contribution. The wood frog introduces the concept of survival through apparent death -- a fundamentally new strategy in the engine.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Freeze tolerance. First freeze-tolerant species. Cryoprotectant. Track.

## Structural Event 3: Vernal Pool -- First Temporary Habitat

- **Situation:** The vernal pool is the first explicitly temporary habitat in the engine. Prior aquatic habitats (streams, ponds, lakes) were permanent. The vernal pool fills with snowmelt, exists for weeks to months, then dries completely.
- **Root Cause:** Temporary habitats are a distinct ecological category. They are fishless (too ephemeral for fish), which makes them safe for amphibian larvae. The wood frog's entire larval development must complete before the pool dries.
- **Recommendation:** Document vernal pool as the first temporary habitat. Note the fishless advantage and the developmental constraint.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Vernal pool. First temporary habitat. Fishless. Ephemeral. Track.

## What Worked

### 1. Freeze Tolerance Is a Genuine Biological Phenomenon
The wood frog's freeze tolerance is well-documented in the scientific literature. Storey and Storey (1988) and subsequent research confirm the 65% body water as ice, the cryoprotectant mechanism, the cardiac arrest, and the revival. This is not speculative biology.

### 2. The Duck-Like Clucking Is a Distinctive and Well-Documented Sound
The wood frog's call is consistently described as "duck-like" in field guides and herpetological literature. The acoustic character is genuinely distinctive from other anuran calls in the engine.

### 3. Vernal Pool as First Temporary Habitat Is Structurally Novel
No prior entry used a temporary habitat. The vernal pool introduces the concept of ephemeral habitat -- a space that exists only seasonally. This is ecologically significant and structurally new.

### 4. Return to Amphibians After Nine Insect Entries
The engine spent degrees 87-95 in insects (nine entries, seven orders). The wood frog breaks the insect streak and returns to Amphibia. The transition is clean and the Ranidae entry is taxonomically significant.

### 5. Thirty-Sixth Consecutive Original CSV
Thirty-six consecutive original species. The engine's reliability continues.
