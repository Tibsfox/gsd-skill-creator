## What Worked

### 1. The Caw Complex Is Well-Documented
The American Crow's vocal repertoire is extensively studied, especially by Marzluff and colleagues at UW. Alarm caw, assembly caw, scolding caw -- all documented. The "most recognized bird sound" claim is defensible.

### 2. Face Recognition Is Peer-Reviewed
Marzluff et al. (2010) documented lasting face recognition in crows. Published in *Animal Behaviour*. Not speculative.

### 3. The Corvidae Trio Is Structurally Powerful
Three consecutive degrees from the same family. Individual, pair, social. Three dimensions of corvid life. Unprecedented and well-motivated.

### 4. E=4 Is Defensible
Loud, persistent, familiar. Not at the raven pair's E=5. E=4 captures the crow's strong voice without overstating.

### 5. Cross-References Are Strong
Raven (100, same genus), Raven pair (101, trio), Mallard (59, familiar/ubiquitous), American Robin (25, familiar bird), Raccoon (70, urban intelligence). All defensible.
