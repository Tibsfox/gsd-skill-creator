Falcipennis canadensis (Phasianidae) — E=4 — The Fool Hen
FIFTH PHASIANIDAE. Beyond the quartet. The “fool hen” — so tame it can be approached by hand. Wing claps from short flutter flights + soft clucking contact calls. Boreal spruce forest specialist. E=4. The quietest Phasianidae.

The Fool Hen — Quiet Intensity in the Boreal Forest

The “fool hen” earned its name by refusing to flee. Evolved in vast boreal forests where its cryptic plumage and motionless sitting defeat visual predators, the Spruce Grouse will sometimes allow humans to approach within arm’s reach. Its wing clap display is brief and percussive — 1–2 second flutter flights. Its clucking is soft and intimate — audible at 10–20 meters. The quietest gamebird in the engine.
