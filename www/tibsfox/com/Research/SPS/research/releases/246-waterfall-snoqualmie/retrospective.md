## Discoveries

1. **The falls is 100 feet taller than Niagara.** This is a commonly cited fact but worth documenting in context: when people think of famous waterfalls, Niagara dominates the imagination. Snoqualmie is significantly larger by height. The Pacific Northwest's most culturally famous waterfall is larger than the country's most famous waterfall.
2. **The 1899 underground powerhouse is a genuine engineering landmark.** One of the first underground powerhouse designs in the world -- generators inside the cliff, tunneled into the basalt. This is 19th-century engineering achievement at a scale worth documenting.
3. **The Point Elliott Treaty (1855) created the legal framework for the tribal-falls relationship.** The treaty guaranteed fishing rights and cultural access even as it ceded most territorial sovereignty. The falls' status as a sacred site that the tribe must negotiate access to through non-tribal ownership structures is a continuing dimension of the treaty's legacy.
