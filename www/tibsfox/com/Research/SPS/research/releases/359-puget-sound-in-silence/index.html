The Acoustic Layers — What Remains

Snapping Shrimp

2–15 kHz — Dominant Biological Signal

Alpheidae. The dominant acoustic signal in warm shallow Puget Sound when vessel noise is absent. Not a single snap — a continuous crackling background rising and falling with temperature and shrimp density. Millions of years of Pacific coastal evolution.

Harbor Seal + SRKW

Harbor Seal: 100–8,000 Hz complex FM underwater calls. SRKW: 1–15 kHz ambient discrete calls (pod dialect) at non-foraging rate, propagating through fjord waveguide. Biological presence in baseline state.
