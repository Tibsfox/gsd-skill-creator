Anatidae Streak BROKEN — 17 Consecutive (118–134)

Seventeen consecutive Anatidae — ducks (118–126), swans (127–128), geese (129–134). Six genera. Four subfamilies. Nearly doubled the Picidae record (9). Strigidae enters at degree 135 — the owl family. First new family since Cinclidae at degree 116, nineteen degrees ago. The engine shifts from water to forest, from prey to predator, from diurnal to nocturnal, from flock to solitary.

Deep Resonant Hoot — E=4

The quintessential owl sound: “hoo-h’HOO-hoo-hoo” at 250–600 Hz fundamental. Four to five deep notes with characteristic rhythm. Far-carrying — audible at one mile on calm nights. Low frequency travels through forest canopy with minimal attenuation. Nocturnal amplification: stable night air conducts the sound further. The deep hooting on a cold winter night is one of the most evocative sounds in the PNW.

The Tiger of the Woods — Apex Predator

Talon grip: 300 psi — comparable to a large dog’s bite force. The most diverse diet of any North American owl: mice to skunks to other raptors. Predator of predators — regularly kills Red-tailed Hawks, Barred Owls, and juvenile Peregrine Falcons. One of the few predators that takes skunks (poor sense of smell). Earliest nester in North America — eggs laid January–February, incubated through ice storms and sub-zero temperatures.
