## Structural Event 1: ANATIDAE STREAK BROKEN at 17 (118-134)

- **Situation:** Seventeen consecutive Anatidae: Harlequin Duck (118) through Ross's Goose (134). Six genera. Four subfamilies. The all-time record by massive margin (Picidae was 9, nearly doubled). The streak ran for nearly a fifth of the first 135 degrees.
- **Root Cause:** The Anatidae family's extraordinary generic diversity sustained the streak through ducks, swans, and geese. The streak ended naturally when the PNW waterfowl catalog was exhausted. Seventeen entries sampled the family comprehensively.
- **Recommendation:** Mark the Anatidae streak as COMPLETE at 17. This record is likely permanent -- no other PNW family has comparable generic diversity to sustain a longer run.

## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **E=4 is defensible.** The deep hoot is individually powerful and far-carrying (1+ mile) but intermittent. Not the sustained flock-chorus volume of Canada Goose (E=5) or the tracheal resonance of Trumpeter Swan (E=6). E=4 reflects the balance between individual power and intermittent delivery.

2. **The streak break documentation is accurate.** Seventeen consecutive Anatidae is verifiable through the engine's own records. The Picidae nonet (9) was the previous record.

3. **The predatory ecology is well-documented.** 300 psi talon grip, diverse diet, predator-of-predators status, skunk predation, earliest nesting -- all confirmed in Houston, Smith & Rohner 1998 and Cornell Lab.

4. **The hoot description is accurate.** "Hoo-h'HOO-hoo-hoo" at 250-600 Hz is well-documented in ornithological literature. Far-carrying range (1+ mile) is supported by field observations.

5. **Cross-references to SPS-134, SPS-118, SPS-116, SPS-004, and SPS-136 are all defensible.**

### Source Quality

6. **The Great Horned Owl is one of the best-studied owls in North America.** Houston, Smith & Rohner 1998, Cornell Lab, Sibley 2014. Source base is excellent.
