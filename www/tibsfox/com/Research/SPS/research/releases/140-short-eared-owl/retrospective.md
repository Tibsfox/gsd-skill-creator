## What Worked

1. **E=5 is defensible.** The bark + wing-clap combination is more complex and impactful than the Long-eared Owl's single-note pumping (E=4). The multi-modal display (vocal + mechanical + visual) justifies the step up.

2. **The Asio comparison pair is the most complete in the engine.** Same genus, opposite everything. The parallel documentation at 139 and 140 creates a clean analytical pair.

3. **Grassland decline documentation is accurate.** NABBS, WDFW, and Cornell Lab data confirm long-term population decline.

4. **Six consecutive Strigidae is the deepest family streak.** Three genera, four species, seven total entries. Comprehensive owl family documentation.

5. **The wing-clap display documentation is accurate.** Wiggins et al. 2006 and Cornell Lab confirm the 5-20 clap sequences and aerial display behavior.

---

## What's New

1. **Multi-modal display (vocal + mechanical + visual).** First non-vocal acoustic component in the engine. The wing-clap is a mechanical sound, not a vocalization. This expands the engine's acoustic vocabulary.

2. **Diurnal owl.** The most diurnal owl in the engine. All previous Strigidae were nocturnal or crepuscular.

3. **Open grassland habitat.** First owl documented in open-country habitat. All previous Strigidae were forest species.

4. **Ground nesting.** First ground-nesting owl in the engine. Previous owls nest in cavities, nests, or tree platforms.

5. **Conservation decline due to habitat loss.** The Short-eared Owl's decline is driven by grassland conversion -- a different conservation narrative from the Spotted Owl (habitat loss from logging) or the Barred Owl conflict.
