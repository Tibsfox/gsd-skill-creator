## What Worked

1. **FIRST ACCIPITRIDAE is the correct milestone to lead with.** The engine's transition from carnivore mammals (212-220) to raptors (221+) is one of the most significant structural moments in the post-200 era. Opening with the national bird is architecturally correct.
2. **The Hollywood substitution (Red-tailed Hawk call) is one of the engine's most interesting findings.** The gap between visual/cultural power and acoustic thin-ness is genuinely surprising and documents a cultural misconception at scale.
3. **DDT conservation story is fully documented.** 1963 nadir (417 pairs), DDT ban 1972, delisting 2007. The recovery arc is one of North America's great conservation successes.
4. **The Skagit Valley winter congregation is properly noted.** Hundreds of eagles, spent salmon -- one of the PNW's signature wildlife spectacles. Geographically important for the engine's PNW scope.

## Discoveries

1. **Benjamin Franklin's Turkey argument is more ecological than it appears.** Franklin's complaint that the eagle "steals" (kleptoparasitism) is verified by science. The eagle does routinely steal fish from Osprey. Franklin's "bad moral character" accusation maps onto the ecological concept of kleptoparasitism three degrees before Osprey (224) enters the engine.
2. **The genus Haliaeetus (sea eagles) has 8 species worldwide -- a global distribution that makes the Bald Eagle the New World representative of a family that includes the African Fish Eagle and Steller's Sea Eagle.** The engine's PNW entry connects to a global ecological guild.
