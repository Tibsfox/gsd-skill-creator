Blue Whale Infrasound — 188 dB Below Hearing

Balaenoptera musculus (Balaenopteridae) — E=5 — THE LARGEST ANIMAL EVER
THE LARGEST ANIMAL THAT HAS EVER LIVED. Up to 200 tons. 188 dB — the loudest sound of any animal. At 10–40 Hz, below human hearing. Calls travel hundreds of miles through the deep sound channel. The absolute superlative — invisible to the ear. 15 consecutive marine mammals (180–194).

The Absolute Superlative

30 meters long. 200 tons. Heart the size of a Volkswagen Beetle. Tongue weighs as much as an elephant. No larger animal has existed in 4.5 billion years of Earth history. The calf gains 90 kg per day. The absolute superlative of biological scale.

188 dB — Across Ocean Basins

188 dB re 1 μPa at 1 m. The loudest animal sound. At 10–40 Hz — below human hearing. Propagates through the SOFAR deep sound channel at ~1,000 m depth. Detectable at 500–1,600 km. Before industrial shipping, blue whales may have communicated across entire oceans.

The Paradox of Inaudibility — E=5

188 dB is the loudest animal sound on Earth. But at 10–40 Hz, humans cannot hear it. The largest animal that has ever lived speaks at a frequency invisible to us. E=5 honors the extraordinary power while acknowledging the inaudibility. The loudest sound on Earth is, to human ears, silence.
