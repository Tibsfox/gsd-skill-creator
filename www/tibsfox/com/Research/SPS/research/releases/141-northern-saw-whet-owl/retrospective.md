## The One Hundred Forty-First Degree: The Tiny Tooting Phantom -- Monotonous Whistle in the Dark Forest

### E=3 metronomic minimalism. Seventh consecutive Strigidae. Fourth genus (Aegolius). Robin-sized -- smaller than a robin. Most common but least seen owl. Migration mysteries revealed by banding. Tooting at ~1000 Hz, ~2 notes/sec, sustained for minutes to hours. Ventriloquial. The quietest energy in the Strigidae run.

## What Worked

1. **E=3 is defensible.** The monotonous, single-note, low-amplitude tooting is genuinely less energetic than the Long-eared Owl's pumping (E=4). The step down to E=3 is calibrated.
2. **Fourth genus adds diversity.** Aegolius is distinct from Bubo, Strix, and Asio. The Strigidae run is not repetitive -- each genus brings a new ecological strategy.
3. **Migration mystery documentation is strong.** Banding station data from Project Owlnet and published research confirm the surprising abundance and irruptive migration.
4. **Robin-sized comparison is accurate and vivid.** 18-21 cm vs. robin's 23-28 cm. Genuinely smaller.
5. **Ventriloquial quality well-documented.** The pure tone makes localization difficult -- confirmed in Rasmussen et al. 2008.

1. **E=3 -- new low in Strigidae run.** Energy descent continues: peak E=6 at 138, now E=3 at 141.
