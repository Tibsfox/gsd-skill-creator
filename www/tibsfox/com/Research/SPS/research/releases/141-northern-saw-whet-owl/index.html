Northern Saw-whet Owl

Aegolius acadicus (Strigidae) — E=3 — Monotonous Tooting
Too-too-too-too-too — E=3 minimal, metronomic. Robin-sized — one of the smallest owls. Seventh consecutive Strigidae (135–141). New genus: Aegolius. Migration mysteries. Most common, least seen.

The Tooting Call — Metronomic Minimalism

A monotonous series of whistled notes: “too-too-too-too-too” at ~1000 Hz, ~2 notes per second, sustained for minutes to hours. No variation. No phrase structure. No climax. The owl equivalent of a metronome. E=3 — the quietest energy in the Strigidae run. Ventriloquial — difficult to localize.

Robin-Sized Predator — Smaller Than You Think

18–21 cm long. 54–151 g weight. Actually smaller than a robin. Large head, yellow eyes, no ear tufts. Roosts pressed against tree trunks in dense cover. So tame it may not fly when discovered — trusts its camouflage. Hunts deer mice from low perches. Caches frozen prey and “incubates” it to thaw.

Migration Mysteries — The Invisible Movement

Nocturnal migration was essentially unknown until banding stations began capturing Saw-whets in the 1990s. Some stations net hundreds per season — population densities far higher than expected. Irruptive pattern linked to rodent cycles. May be one of the most numerous raptors in North America — hidden by small size and strictly nocturnal habits.
