Long-eared Owl

Asio otus (Strigidae) — E=4 — Deep Pumping Hoots
Deep “whooo” pumping at 300–400 Hz. Single notes every 2–4 seconds. E=4 — measured, minimal, meditative. Fifth consecutive Strigidae (135–139). Asio genus enters. The most secretive owl in the PNW. Cryptic roosting. Communal winter roosts. “Ghost owl.” First half of the Long-eared/Short-eared pair (139–140).

The Ghost Owl — E=4 Minimal Hooting

The Long-eared Owl is the PNW’s most secretive owl. During the day, it roosts in dense vegetation, pressing against tree trunks with raised ear tufts, becoming nearly invisible. At night, it reveals itself with deep “whooo” pumping — single notes at 300–400 Hz, spaced every 2–4 seconds. Meditative, persistent, minimal. The simplest owl vocalization in the engine.

Communal Winter Roosts

In winter, Long-eared Owls congregate in communal roosts — 20–50+ birds in a single stand of dense conifers. These spectacular aggregations are among the least-known wildlife events in the PNW. The same sites may be used for decades. Detection often comes from pellet carpets beneath roost trees, not from seeing the owls themselves.
