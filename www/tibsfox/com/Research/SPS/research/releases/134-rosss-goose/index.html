Ross’s Goose

Anser rossii (Anatidae) — E=4 — 17th Consecutive Anatidae — THE FINAL ANATIDAE
higher nasal call — E=4 — North America’s smallest goose. About two-thirds the size of a Snow Goose. Higher-pitched, squeakier call at 800–5,000 Hz. Short stubby bill without grinning patch. Named after Hudson’s Bay Company factor Bernard Rogan Ross. Seventeenth consecutive Anatidae (118–134) — THE FINAL ENTRY. THE ALL-TIME RECORD. Sixth and final goose in the goose era (129–134). The streak ends with the smallest, quietest goose of all.

The Final Anatidae — Seventeen Consecutive (118–134)

The streak is closed. Seventeen consecutive Anatidae entries — nearly doubling the Picidae record (9). The streak sampled the family comprehensively: ducks (118–126), swans (127–128), Branta geese (129–131), Anser geese (132–134). Three subfamilies, five+ genera, seventeen entries. Energy arc: opening → E=6 climax → descent → E=5 surge → E=4 gentle coda. Degree 135 (Great Horned Owl) begins a new family.

North America’s Smallest Goose — E=4

About two-thirds the size of a Snow Goose, roughly half the weight (1.0–1.6 kg vs. 2.0–3.5 kg). Short, stubby triangular bill without the Snow Goose’s black grinning patch. Warty caruncles at bill base in adult males. Rounder, more compact head. Higher-pitched, weaker call: squeaky “kak” at 800–5,000 Hz. Often found mixed in Snow Goose flocks — a sought-after birding find.

Snow/Ross’s Comparison Pair (133–134)

Mirrors Canada/Cackling (129–130): larger species (Snow, E=5) followed by smaller version (Ross’s, E=4). In both pairs: deeper call → higher call, higher energy → lower energy, larger body → smaller body. The pattern repeats: the engine pairs large and small versions of closely related species. Three comparison pairs in the goose era: Tundra/Trumpeter (swans), Canada/Cackling (Branta), Snow/Ross’s (Anser).
