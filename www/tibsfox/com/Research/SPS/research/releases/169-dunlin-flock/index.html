Dunlin Flock — Sighing Collective Wind-Sound

Calidris alpina (Scolopacidae) — E=3 — The Murmuration Shorebird
SECOND SCOLOPACIDAE — thousands of Dunlin wheeling in unison over tidal flats — the collective wind-sound of thousands of wings creating a sighing, rushing sound. NOT vocal. The sound of the flock itself. E=3. v1.49.309.

Murmuration — Thousands Turning as One

Each bird responds to its nearest 6–7 neighbors. There is no leader. The coordination emerges from simple local rules applied simultaneously by thousands of individuals. The result is a fluid, organic, pulsating shape that moves as one organism — ribbons, spheres, pancakes, tornadoes. Turn waves propagate at 3× individual flight speed. At close range, the sound: a rushing, sighing, whooshing collective wind-sound.

Tidal Flat — The Stage

Padilla Bay, Skagit Bay, Nisqually NWR — extensive mudflats provide winter habitat for 5,000 to 50,000+ Dunlin. Feeding at low tide, probing with slightly decurved bills. Rising tide pushes the flock to air — triggering murmuration. The murmuration is the tidal transition.

The Collective Sound

The research focus is not the individual Dunlin’s call but the collective sound of the flock in murmuration. Thousands of small wings beating and turning simultaneously create a rushing, sighing sound — a collective wind-sound that is the voice of the flock itself. The sound rises and falls with the flock’s movements. It is the sound of air being displaced by the collective body.

Theory Nodes

M

Murmuration — Collective Aerial Display

Advanced

Thousands wheeling as one. 6-7 nearest neighbors. No leader. Emergent coordination. Shape-shifting. Turn waves at 3x speed. Predator defense.

S

Collective Wing-Sound — The Sighing Rush

Advanced

Aggregate of thousands of wings. Rushing, sighing, whooshing. Not vocal. Atmospheric. Rises and falls with flock movements. The voice of the flock.

F

Predator Defense — Falcon Trigger

Intermediate

Peregrine Falcon or Merlin triggers most dramatic murmurations. Predator cannot target individual in coordinated mass. Defense through collective movement.

T

Tidal Flat Cycle

Foundational

Feed at low tide. Rising tide triggers flight and murmuration. Falling tide draws birds back. Winter phenomenon (Oct-Apr). Puget Sound estuaries.
