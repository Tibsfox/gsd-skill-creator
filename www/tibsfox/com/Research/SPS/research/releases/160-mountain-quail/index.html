Mountain Quail — Loud Quee-Ark

Oreortyx pictus (Odontophoridae) — E=4 — The Secret Giant
SECOND AND FINAL ODONTOPHORIDAE. The pair completes. Largest New World quail. Most secretive — rarely seen, always heard. Loud “quee-ARK!” carries 200–400m through dense brush. Longest head plume of any quail: two straight feathers pointing backward. E=4. v1.49.300.

The Secret Giant — Heard More Than Seen

The largest New World quail (240–290g) is also the most secretive. It selects the densest brush available — manzanita, ceanothus, impenetrable tangles. When approached, it runs deeper into cover rather than flying. Yet its “quee-ARK!” carries 200–400 meters through the brush. The detection paradox: loud call, invisible bird.

California / Mountain Quail Comparison (159–160)

California Quail (159): forward topknot, chi-CA-go, open edges, large coveys, common, easily seen.
Mountain Quail (160): straight backward plumes, quee-ark, dense brush, small coveys, secretive, rarely seen.
Equal energy (E=4 + E=4). Different in every other dimension. The Odontophoridae comparison pair.

Theory Nodes

Q

Quee-Ark Call

Advanced

Loud, emphatic, single-note whistle. 200-400m carry through dense brush. Called from concealed position. Detection paradox: loud call, invisible bird.

S

Most Secretive Quail

Intermediate

Densest brush. Runs rather than flushes. Rarely seen despite being common. Largest N.A. quail yet most elusive. Small coveys (5-20).

P

Longest Head Plume

Foundational

Two straight feathers pointing backward. 6-8 cm. Longest of any quail. Contrast with California Quail's forward topknot. May aid brush navigation.

W

Walking Migration

Intermediate

One of few quail that migrates. Walks downslope in autumn, upslope in spring. Miles through dense brush. Traditional routes.
