## Discoveries

1. **The sub-bass body vibration component (20-100 Hz) is a physically significant aspect of the tidal bore experience that distinguishes it from most biological acoustic events.** At close range to a major tidal rip, the low-frequency pressure fluctuations in the water column are felt as body vibration before they are heard as sound. The human body responds to sound pressure in the 20-100 Hz range as vibration (the "felt-before-heard" quality) at sufficient amplitude. This is not infrasound; it is the sub-bass limit of audible hearing where perception crosses from acoustic to somatic.
2. **Puget Sound's multiple sill system creates hydraulic lag effects that are acoustically important.** The Admiralty Inlet sill (50m depth) restricts the tidal exchange between the main Puget Sound basin and the deeper northern channels, creating differential tidal timing across the Sound. This differential timing means that maximum bore conditions at different passages do not all occur simultaneously -- the tidal bore event at Deception Pass may peak 30-60 minutes before or after the bore at Tacoma Narrows.
