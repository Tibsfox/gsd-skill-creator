Deep Rhythmic Surge — E=8 — The Sound Has No Biology

The tidal bore is not a single impact (the rock avalanche, degree 322) but a sustained, rhythmic, deep surge: millions of kilograms of water per second moving through a 150-meter passage at 8–9 knots. The acoustic event lasts 2–4 hours per tidal cycle, not 30–90 seconds. E=8 through sustained hydraulic mass, not through brief peak intensity. Deception Pass, Tacoma Narrows, the San Juan Islands passages — all simultaneously active at spring tide maximum exchange.

surge —— surge —— surge —— surge
Standing waves build and release (5–30 sec rhythm) → tidal current approaches maximum (20–40 min variation) → tidal cycle (6 hours) → three nested rhythms, all operating simultaneously in the water column

Deception Pass — The Primary Site

Deception Pass: 150 meters wide, 20–30 meters deep, separating Whidbey Island from Fidalgo Island. At spring tide maximum exchange, current reaches 8–9 knots (4–4.5 m/s) — among the highest tidal velocities in Puget Sound. White water in the main channel, boils rising from depth, whirlpools at the current boundaries, standing waves that maintain their position in the flow. The sound carries several hundred meters on calm days. Washington State’s most visited state park (~2 million annual visitors): the visual drama of maximum exchange is the public face of the acoustic event.

Acoustic Profile — E=8 Through Hydraulic Mass

Mass flow rate: At Deception Pass maximum: 5,000–10,000 m³/sec × 1,025 kg/m³ = 5–10 million kg/sec. Fundamentally different order of magnitude from any biological source.

Frequency profile: Sub-bass 20–100 Hz (felt as body vibration); Bass 100–300 Hz (dominant roar); Mid 300–1,000 Hz (wall turbulence); Upper-mid 1,000–2,000 Hz (cavitation, surface turbulence).

Duration: 2–4 hours per tidal cycle at near-maximum current. The bore acoustic event is sustained for hours — not seconds or minutes. The E=8 of duration, not of peak intensity.

Rhythmic structure: Three nested timescales simultaneously: standing wave oscillation (5–30 sec), current approach/recession (20–40 min), tidal cycle (6 hours). The “deep rhythmic surge” is real — the bore has structure, not just noise.

The Sites

Deception Pass

150m wide. 8–9 knots max. Standing waves, boils, tidal rip. Deception Pass Bridge 50m above water. WA’s most visited state park.

Tacoma Narrows

1,600m wide. 5–6 knots max. Deeper, lower-frequency acoustic profile. Galloping Gertie resonance principle (1940). The bass register of the degree-323 event.

Cattle Pass

South entrance to San Juan Islands. 5+ knots max. Visible from False Bay, San Juan Island.

Current Pass

San Juan–Lopez passage. 4–5 knots max. Standing waves. Part of the distributed San Juan Islands tidal bore system.
