## Structural Event 2: Echolocation as Sensory Infrastructure

- **Situation:** Echolocation clicks at 10,000-80,000+ Hz: sensory not primarily social. The SRKW uses echolocation to detect and catch Chinook salmon. Boat noise reduces effective range.
- **Root Cause:** The echolocation system is the SRKW's primary foraging tool. The acoustic disturbance threat is directly relevant: if echolocation effectiveness is impaired by boat noise, foraging success is reduced during an already nutritionally stressed period. The acoustic disturbance threat and the echolocation system are the same story.
- **Recommendation:** State the acoustic disturbance-echolocation connection explicitly. The boat noise threat is not abstract -- it directly impairs the sensory system used to catch food. Document.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Boat noise reduces echolocation effectiveness. Direct foraging impairment. Acoustic disturbance threat mechanistically documented.

## Structural Event 3: Signature Whistles -- Individual Identity

- **Situation:** Individual Southern Residents develop signature whistles -- individually distinctive acoustic patterns. Not genetically encoded; developed through social interaction with mother and pod mates.
- **Root Cause:** Signature whistles are an extension of the cultural acoustic theme established at degree 270. The discrete calls are pod-specific (cultural). The signature whistles are individually specific (cultural at the individual level). Both demonstrate that SRKW acoustic identity is learned, not encoded.
- **Recommendation:** Document signature whistles as the individual-level complement to pod-level discrete calls. Note the parallelism: pod identity (discrete calls) and individual identity (signature whistles) are both culturally transmitted.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Signature whistles = individual acoustic identity, culturally developed. Parallel to pod-level discrete calls.
