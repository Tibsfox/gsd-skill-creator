# Release 032 Retrospective -- Bohemian Waxwing (Degree 32)

**Release:** 032 of 360
**Species:** Bohemian Waxwing (*Bombycilla garrulus*)
**Sound:** Buzzy bzeee calls flock chorus
**Date:** 2026-03-29

---

## Purpose

This is the thirty-third release of the PNW 360 Species engine (degrees 0-32), the second and final Bombycillidae in the engine, and the closing voice of the waxwing pair (degrees 31-32). The engine needed to complete the waxwing pair, break the E=4 energy plateau, introduce a new seasonal category (irruptive), and demonstrate how the same genus can produce qualitatively different sounds at different energy levels.

Three structural contributions:

1. **The waxwing pair closes.** Cedar Waxwing (31, E=4, resident, whisper) + Bohemian Waxwing (32, E=5, irruptive, buzz) = the engine's third same-genus consecutive pair, after Catharus (18-19) and Tachycineta (27-28). The first same-genus pair with different energy levels. The pair separates genus-level constants (frugivory, flock behavior, wax tips, sibilant vocalization) from species-level variables (body size, range, energy, call texture, seasonal pattern). Bombycillidae is CLOSED at 2 of 3 species worldwide (66.7% coverage).

2. **E=5 breaks the E=4 plateau.** Eleven consecutive degrees at E=4 (degrees 21-31) -- the longest unbroken energy plateau in engine history. It ends here. E=5 is used for the first time ever. The full five-level energy palette (E=1 through E=5) is now active. The dynamic range has expanded upward. The ground bass lifts.

3. **"Irruptive" enters the seasonal vocabulary.** The engine's sixth seasonal category, after resident, migrant, partial migrant, summer, and spring. Irruption is not migration -- it is unpredictable, food-driven displacement from boreal breeding grounds. The Bohemian Waxwing is the engine's second irruptive species (after Pine Siskin at degree 12). The irruptive species is a messenger from elsewhere.

## The Transition: Whisper to Buzz

### Within the Same Genus

The transition from degree 31 to degree 32 is the most structurally dense transition in the engine since degrees 30-31 (Barn Swallow to Cedar Waxwing). But where 30-31 crossed a family boundary, 31-32 stays within one genus while changing nearly every other parameter:

| Dimension | Degree 31 (Cedar Waxwing) | Degree 32 (Bohemian Waxwing) |
|---|---|---|
| Genus | *Bombycilla* | *Bombycilla* (SAME) |
| Family | Bombycillidae | Bombycillidae (SAME) |
| Energy | 4 | **5 (NEW -- first E=5)** |
| Season | Resident | **Irruptive (NEW category)** |
| Sound | High thin see-see-see (whisper) | Buzzy bzeee flock chorus (buzz) |
| Frequency | 6-10 kHz | 5-8.5 kHz (lower) |
| Call duration | 0.3-0.8 s | 0.5-1.5 s (longer) |
| Amplitude | Low-moderate | Moderate-high (louder) |
| Mass | 30-36 g | 45-70 g (nearly 2x) |
| Range | Temperate Nearctic | Holarctic boreal |
| PNW status | Common year-round | Rare winter visitor |
| Flock scale | 50-400 | 200-5,000+ |
| Ecosystem | Fruiting trees | Berry stands |
| Undertail | White | Cinnamon |

The genus holds: both are silk-tailed frugivores in flocks, both produce sibilant trills, both eat berries, both lack territorial song, both have wax tips. Everything else shifts. The pair is a controlled experiment with one variable held constant (genus) and everything else free to vary. What emerges is the distinction between what the genus requires and what the species chooses.

### The Energy Contrast Within the Pair

Previous same-genus pairs held energy constant:
- Catharus (18-19): E=3, E=3
- Tachycineta (27-28): E=4, E=4

Bombycilla breaks this pattern: E=4, E=5. The pair itself contains the energy transition that breaks the eleven-degree plateau. This is structurally elegant -- the plateau does not break at a family boundary or a random degree. It breaks within a genus pair, where the comparison is maximally informative. The listener can hear exactly what E=5 means because they just heard E=4 from the same genus.

## The Luther Rabb Parallel

The Part A parallel at degree 32 maps to **Luther Rabb** from S36. The parallel operates on five axes:

1. **Energy jump.** Brenda Lee Eager (31, E=4) to Luther Rabb (32, E=5). Eager was smooth, silky Mercury Records polish. Rabb was explosive Fillmore energy, bass-driven funk, opening for Hendrix. The artist pair mirrors the bird pair: silk whisper to bass buzz, E=4 to E=5.

2. **Irruptive career.** Rabb appeared suddenly on the national scene with Ballin' Jack in 1970, burned through venues opening for Hendrix, toured with Santana, joined War -- an irruptive career trajectory. Like the Bohemian Waxwing: intense while present, unpredictable in duration.

3. **Bass as buzz.** Rabb was a bass player. The bass guitar's low-frequency energy = the Bohemian Waxwing's lower-pitched bzeee. Cedar Waxwing = Eager's vocal (high, smooth). Bohemian Waxwing = Rabb's bass (low, buzzy).

4. **Seattle roots, national reach.** Born and raised in Seattle. Played nationally and internationally. Holarctic reach from a local origin -- like the Bohemian Waxwing's breeding range spanning both continents from boreal forests.

5. **Career interrupted.** Car accident (1986), stroke (2002). The irruptive career arc mirrors the irruption cycle: explosive arrival, intense activity, sudden interruption, gradual fading.

## What Worked

### 1. The E=4 plateau breaking is the right structural event at the right moment
After eleven degrees of sustained E=4, the break to E=5 is seismic. Placing it within a same-genus pair maximizes the contrast -- the listener can hear exactly what the energy jump means because they just heard the same genus at E=4. The break is not arbitrary; it is earned by the flock dynamics of an irruptive species.

### 2. The waxwing pair is the most internally contrastive genus pair yet
Previous pairs held energy constant. This pair breaks that pattern. The pair explores range, energy, seasonal pattern, call texture, body size, and PNW status -- more dimensions of contrast than either previous pair. The pair is a comprehensive lesson in within-genus variation.

### 3. Irruptive as a seasonal category enriches the engine's temporal vocabulary
Migration is predictable. Irruption is not. Adding unpredictability as a temporal dimension gives the engine new ecological language. The irruptive species is not just a bird with a different schedule -- it is a bird with no schedule, a messenger from conditions far away.

### 4. The whisper-to-buzz transition within one genus is acoustically informative
The engine has never before demonstrated how the same basic vocal architecture (Bombycilla's sibilant trill) produces qualitatively different sounds in closely related species. The pair reveals the relationship between body size, flock scale, range, and acoustic texture.

### 5. The Luther Rabb parallel is strong
The energy contrast (Eager E=4 to Rabb E=5) mirrors the bird pair exactly. The bass-as-buzz metaphor works. The irruptive career arc works. The parallel is the most dimensionally aligned in recent degrees.

## What Could Be Better

### 1. CRITICAL: No marine species in 32 degrees of "The Sound of Puget Sound"
Thirty-two degrees. The water has not spoken. NOT ONCE. After the waxwing pair closes here, degree 33 is the absolute non-negotiable deadline for a marine species. The project is named for Puget Sound. The Sound must speak. A gull, a loon, a grebe, a cormorant, an alcid -- anything from the water. **THIS IS NOT OPTIONAL.**

### 2. CRITICAL: Ten consecutive Passeriformes (degrees 23-32)
The longest order-level streak in the engine has extended by one more degree. A marine species at degree 33 would almost certainly be non-Passeriformes, solving both the marine gap and the order streak simultaneously.

### 3. CRITICAL: No corvid in 32 degrees
No Steller's Jay, American Crow, or Common Raven. The most intelligent, acoustically complex PNW birds remain absent.

### 4. CRITICAL: No raptor in 32 degrees
No Bald Eagle, Red-tailed Hawk, or Osprey. Top predators are still absent.

### 5. No nocturnal species since degree 4
Twenty-eight degrees since the Spotted Owl. The night has one voice.

## Recommendations for Release 033

The waxwing pair is closed. Bombycillidae is done. The engine must now address its most critical structural gaps. The following are not suggestions -- they are requirements:

1. **A MARINE SPECIES (NON-NEGOTIABLE -- 32 DEGREES OVERDUE).** The project is called "The Sound of Puget Sound." The water must speak at degree 33. A gull (Glaucous-winged Gull, Charadriiformes), a loon (Common Loon, Gaviiformes), a grebe (Western Grebe, Podicipediformes), a cormorant (Double-crested Cormorant, Suliformes), or an alcid (Pigeon Guillemot, Charadriiformes). ANY of these would solve multiple problems simultaneously: marine species, new order, break Passeriformes streak.

2. **A NON-PASSERINE (CRITICAL -- 10 consecutive).** Ten straight Passeriformes. A marine species (most are non-Passeriformes) would break this immediately.

3. **A CORVID (URGENT -- 32 degrees).** Steller's Jay, American Crow, or Common Raven. The most acoustically complex PNW birds are absent.

4. **A RAPTOR (URGENT -- 32 degrees).** Bald Eagle, Red-tailed Hawk, or Osprey. Top predators are absent.

5. **A NOCTURNAL SPECIES (OVERDUE -- 28 degrees).** Great Horned Owl, Barred Owl, or Common Nighthawk.

6. **Calibrate E=5 frequency.** Now that E=5 exists, the engine must decide: is E=5 rare (fortissimo accent) or available (new normal)? The next few degrees will establish the precedent.

Nobody predicted them. Nobody scheduled their arrival. They appeared in the mountain ash and stripped it bare in hours. Bombycilla garrulus. The talkative silk-tail. The Bohemian -- the vagabond, the wanderer, the nomad from the boreal north who follows no calendar and answers to no one but the berries. Fifty-five grams of gray-brown silk and cinnamon, buzzing louder than the Cedar Waxwing ever whispered, in a flock five times the size, at energy level five -- the first five the engine has ever seen. The whisper became a buzz. The plateau broke. Eleven degrees of E=4, holding steady through the hammer, the groove, the tanager's hoarseness, the robin's familiarity, the swallows' river of liquid song, and the Cedar Waxwing's crystalline thread -- all at the same energy. And then the Bohemian arrived and the energy jumped. Not because a single bird was so much louder, but because a thousand of them buzzed at once, because they arrived without warning, because their presence was an event, because the irruption was the sound. The whisper carried. The buzz overwhelms. And after the buzz, the waxwing pair is closed. Two silk-tails. Two degrees. One genus. The third same-genus pair in the engine, and the first where the energy levels differ. The glass harmonica and the kazoo choir. The resident and the vagabond. E=4 and E=5. Degree 31 and degree 32. And now -- thirty-two degrees of The Sound of Puget Sound, and the Sound itself -- the actual water, the salt water, the tidal inland sea -- has still not spoken. Not once. Not a gull. Not a loon. Not a grebe. Thirty-two degrees of forest, field, farmland, fruiting tree, and berry stand. The swallows filled the sky. The waxwings filled the mountain ash. But the Sound is waiting. It has been waiting for thirty-two degrees. It will not wait any longer.*
