The Bohemian Waxwing is the second consecutive Bombycillidae — completing the waxwing pair (Cedar degree 31, Bohemian degree 32). The Bohemian is larger, grayer, and more northern than the Cedar — a boreal species that irrupts southward in winter when berry crops fail, appearing in the PNW unpredictably. Second irruptive species (after Pine Siskin degree 12). The stochastic-presence thread continues: species whose appearance is event-driven rather than calendar-driven.

The "buzzy bzeee" calls produced by a Bohemian Waxwing flock create a collective acoustic event — hundreds of birds calling simultaneously produce a wall of high-frequency buzzing that dominates any environment the flock occupies. At E=5, this is the loudest collective vocalization in the engine — not the loudest single voice (the flicker's wick-wick at E=4) but the loudest aggregate (hundreds of simultaneous calls). The swarm-communication model from the Bushtit (degree 10) returns at a higher amplitude: individually quiet calls producing collectively overwhelming sound.

Ranger 6's cameras failed on a Moon-impacting spacecraft — the instruments existed but didn't activate, the same way the Bohemian Waxwing's irruptive presence "exists" in the PNW but doesn't "activate" in most years. Both are systems that reach their target zone (the Moon / the PNW lowlands) but whose functional output (photography / acoustic presence) is conditional on factors beyond the organism's control (timer activation / berry crop failure elsewhere).
