### 3. Spotted Owl Prey Link Is Ecologically Critical
The Northern Spotted Owl's dependence on flying squirrels is well-documented in conservation biology. This is a key ecological link that connects degree 4 to degree 82 across 78 degrees.

### 4. Truffle Mutualism Is Scientifically Important
The truffle-squirrel-forest mutualism is a cornerstone of old-growth ecology. Maser et al. (1978) documented this relationship. The connection to American Marten (65) adds depth.

### 5. Nearly Ultrasonic Call Is Genuine
The "tseet" contact call at the upper edge of human hearing is documented in Wells-Gosling & Heaney (1984). E=3 reflects presence but extreme subtlety.

### 6. Old-Growth Dependence Links to Pacific Wren (1) and Pileated Woodpecker (54)
Three old-growth specialists across 82 degrees. The ecological network expands.
