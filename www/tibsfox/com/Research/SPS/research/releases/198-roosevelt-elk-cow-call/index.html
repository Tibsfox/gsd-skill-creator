Roosevelt Elk Cow Call

Cervus canadensis roosevelti (Cervidae) — E=5 — Fourth Cervidae
Second Roosevelt Elk entry. Elk trio middle (197–199). Sharp alarm bark (300–800 Hz) and plaintive maternal mew (400–1,200 Hz). Year-round. Essential communication. Cow-calf vocal fingerprint. E=5 — powerful and purposeful, not performative.

The Mew — Maternal Contact

“Meeew.” Drawn-out, nasal, 400–1,200 Hz. Individually recognizable — each cow has a distinctive call, and each calf knows its mother’s voice. Established within hours of birth. Calving season May-June: cow separates, calf hidden in vegetation, mew for nursing contact. The sound of connection.

The Bark — Alarm

“BARK.” Sharp, explosive, 300–800 Hz. Carries 500 m to 1 km through forest. One cow barks and the entire herd responds — heads up, ears forward, ready to flee. Year-round. Predators, humans, any threat. 0.2–0.5 seconds, repeated 2–5 times.

Elk Trio — The Quieter Middle

197: Bugle (E=7) — the screaming whistle. Display. 198: Cow call (E=5) — bark + mew. Connection. 199: Rut roar (E=7) — deep bellowing. Combat. The cow’s voice is the essential middle — E=7, E=5, E=7. The valley between two peaks.
