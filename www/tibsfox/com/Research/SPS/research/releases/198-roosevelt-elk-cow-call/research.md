## THE BARK -- ALARM AND WARNING

### Explosive Alert

The cow's bark is a sharp, explosive vocalization:

- **Character.** A short, forceful "BARK" -- a percussive exhalation through the mouth and nose. Not unlike a dog's bark in character (sharp, attention-getting) but lower and more powerful.
- **Frequency.** 300-800 Hz. Mid-range. Designed to carry through forest and be immediately recognized by other elk.
- **Function.** Alarm call. Used when the cow detects a predator (cougar, bear, wolf) or a human approach. The bark alerts the herd to danger. When one cow barks, the entire herd responds -- heads up, ears forward, ready to flee.
- **Duration.** 0.2-0.5 seconds per bark. May be repeated 2-5 times in rapid succession.
- **Carry distance.** 500 meters to 1 km in forest. The sharp character cuts through ambient forest noise.
- **Both sexes bark.** While the bark is primarily associated with cows (who are more vigilant due to maternal duties), bulls also bark. The cow's bark is higher than the bull's.

(Sources: Elk alarm vocalization; cow bark studies; herd communication; predator response)

## THE MEW -- MATERNAL CONTACT

### The Sound Between Mother and Calf

The mew is the essential maternal vocalization:

- **Character.** A drawn-out, nasal "meeew" -- high for an elk, plaintive, searching. The quality is similar to a domestic cow's moo but higher-pitched and more nasal. The sound carries a quality of calling -- "where are you?" or "here I am."
- **Frequency.** 400-1,200 Hz. Higher than the bark, with more harmonic complexity. The mew has overtones that make it individually recognizable.
- **Individual recognition.** Each cow has a distinctive mew, and each calf recognizes its mother's specific call. In a herd of dozens, a cow's mew will bring only her calf running. This vocal fingerprint is established within hours of birth.
- **Calving season.** May-June. Cows separate from the herd to give birth. The calf is hidden in vegetation for the first 1-2 weeks. During this period, the cow returns to nurse several times per day, using the mew to locate and call the calf. The calf responds with its own high-pitched mew.
- **Duration.** 0.5-2.0 seconds per mew. Longer than the bark. The drawn-out quality carries through forest.
- **Emotional quality.** The mew has an unmistakably emotional quality -- urgency when the calf is lost, contentment when nursing, agitation when danger is near. Human observers consistently describe the cow's mew as "plaintive" or "searching."

(Sources: Cow-calf vocal recognition; elk calving behavior; maternal communication; acoustic individuality)

## BUGLE vs. COW CALL -- THE COMPARISON

### Display vs. Connection

The bugle (197) and cow call (198) represent fundamentally different vocal strategies:

- **The bugle is performance.** The bull bugles to be noticed by everyone in the valley -- cows, rival bulls, the landscape itself. Maximum volume, maximum frequency range, maximum reach. Display.
- **The cow call is precision.** The cow calls to reach one individual -- her calf, or the nearest herd member. Recognizable, specific, targeted. Connection.
- **Public vs. private.** The bugle is the most public sound in the PNW forest -- you hear it from kilometers away. The cow's mew is a private conversation -- audible at close range, meaningful only to the intended recipient.
- **Seasonal vs. year-round.** The bugle is seasonal spectacle. The cow call is daily necessity.
- **Energy.** E=7 (bugle) vs. E=5 (cow call). The cow's voice is powerful but purposeful. The bull's bugle is powerful and performative.

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of a Roosevelt Elk Cow

**Bark (Alarm):**
- **Frequency:** 300-800 Hz. Sharp, percussive.
- **Character:** Explosive "BARK." Short. Attention-getting. Cuts through ambient noise.
- **Duration:** 0.2-0.5 seconds. Repeated 2-5 times.
- **Context:** Predator detection, human approach, any threat. Year-round.
- **Carry distance:** 500 m to 1 km in forest.

**Mew (Contact/Maternal):**
- **Frequency:** 400-1,200 Hz. Nasal, drawn-out.
- **Character:** Plaintive "meeew." Searching. Individually recognizable. Cow-calf vocal fingerprint.
- **Duration:** 0.5-2.0 seconds.
- **Context:** Cow-calf contact (calving season May-June), herd cohesion, nursing.
- **Carry distance:** 200-500 m. Sufficient for cow-calf communication in dense forest.

**Calf Response:**
- **Frequency:** 600-1,800 Hz. Higher than cow's mew.
- **Character:** Thin, high mew. Similar to fawn bleat in quality. Vulnerable.
- **Context:** Responding to mother's call. First 2-4 weeks of life.

**Overall Amplitude:** E=5. The cow's vocal repertoire is powerful and purposeful -- the bark commands attention, the mew establishes connection. Essential communication, not display.
