Minke Whale — Low Thump & Ratchet

Balaenoptera acutorostrata (Balaenopteridae) — E=4 — FIRST BALAENOPTERIDAE
FIRST BALAENOPTERIDAE — the rorqual whale family enters. The smallest rorqual (7–10 m). Baleen filter feeder. Distinctive thump trains + ratchet sounds — mechanical, rhythmic, unlike anything else in the ocean. 11 consecutive marine mammals (180–190). Toothed-to-baleen transition.

First Balaenopteridae — The Great Whales Arrive

The rorqual family enters. Balaenopteridae includes the largest animals ever to live on Earth: blue whales, fin whales, humpbacks. The Minke Whale is the smallest rorqual — the entry point to the family of giants. Baleen plates instead of teeth. Ventral pleats for lunge feeding. Fourth cetacean family.

The Thump & Ratchet — Mechanical Music

Thump trains: repetitive, rhythmic, low-frequency pulses. Ratchet: pulsed, mechanical, zipper-like sound. The “boing” — first recorded by the U.S. Navy in the 1960s, initially misidentified as non-biological. Downsweeps: 80–200 Hz for long-range contact. Mechanical. Rhythmic. Unique. E=4.

Marine Mammal Era — 11 Consecutive

11 consecutive marine mammals (degrees 180–190). 6 families: Phocidae (2), Otariidae (3), Mustelidae (1), Phocoenidae (2), Delphinidae (2), Balaenopteridae (1). Toothed-to-baleen transition at degree 190. Energy range: E=1 to E=6.
