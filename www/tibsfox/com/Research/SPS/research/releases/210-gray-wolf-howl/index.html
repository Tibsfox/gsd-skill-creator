Gray Wolf Howl

Canis lupus (Canidae) — E=7 — EXTIRPATED — Apex Voice
EXTIRPATED. Eliminated from Washington by the 1930s. Now returning from BC and Idaho. 30+ packs confirmed. Deep sustained howl at 150–780 Hz carrying 5–10 km. E=7 — the most powerful voice in the Canidae family. Third Canidae. Canidae quartet apex.

The Howl — Landscape-Scale Voice

150–780 Hz fundamental, 3–11 seconds sustained. Chest-amplified. Individual acoustic signature. Carries 5–10 km through mountain terrain. Functions: territory, pack rally, social bonding, mourning. When pack howls, each wolf chooses a different pitch — the acoustic illusion of a larger pack. E=7.

EXTIRPATED — 70 Years of Silence

Systematically eliminated: bounty hunting, trapping, poisoning. Gone by the 1930s. For 70 years, no wolf howl in Washington. The wilderness was quieter by its most powerful voice. Now returning — natural dispersal from BC and Idaho. 30+ confirmed packs in northeastern Washington. Every pack is a negotiation between wildness and agriculture.

Trophic Cascade — The Wolf Changes the River

Wolf presence reshapes ecosystems. Wolves reduce elk browsing, riparian vegetation recovers, stream banks stabilize, fish habitat improves, bird populations benefit. The “landscape of fear”: elk change behavior in wolf presence. The wolf doesn’t need to kill every elk — its presence alone changes how elk use the landscape.
