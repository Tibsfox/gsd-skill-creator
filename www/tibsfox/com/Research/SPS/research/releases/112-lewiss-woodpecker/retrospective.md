## Structural Event 2: The Flycatching Woodpecker -- Behavioral Anomaly

- **Situation:** Lewis's Woodpecker catches insects on the wing like a flycatcher. Rarely drums. Rarely drills. The most behaviorally anomalous woodpecker in North America.
- **Root Cause:** Open ponderosa pine and burned forest habitat provides the aerial space for flycatching. The habitat drove the behavioral evolution -- in open forest, the space between trees becomes a hunting ground. Lewis's Woodpecker exploited the niche that other woodpeckers ignore.
- **Recommendation:** Document the flycatching behavior as a major research finding. Connect to the Northern Flicker (degree 21, ground-foraging) as another non-standard Picidae foraging strategy. Track whether other behaviorally anomalous species appear in the engine.
