The Lesser Goldfinch is the smallest North American *Spinus* at 8-11.5 grams — the "lesser" in body only. Its song is the most complex of the three engine *Spinus*, a mimicry mosaic woven from fragments of 39 or more other species' vocalizations stitched together with the bird's own bright phrases. Named *Spinus psaltria* — "female lute player of the thorn bush" — this is a bird whose scientific name explicitly references a musical instrument and a human performer. It is also the engine's first active range-expansion species: historically a bird of the American Southwest, the Lesser Goldfinch has pushed northward into Washington since 1975, its population increasing 110.5% in the state between 2012 and 2022 as climate warms and habitat shifts.

Gemini 8 achieved the first docking in space on March 16, 1966 — Neil Armstrong and David Scott maneuvered their spacecraft to dock with an unmanned Agena target vehicle. The docking succeeded, but within minutes a stuck thruster on the Gemini capsule began an uncontrolled roll that accelerated to nearly one revolution per second. Armstrong undocked from the Agena, used the reentry control system to stabilize the tumble, and made an emergency splashdown in the western Pacific — cutting the three-day mission to under eleven hours. It was Armstrong's first spaceflight, the mission that proved he could think and act under lethal pressure, the lesser-known flight that preceded his walk on the Moon. The Lesser Goldfinch is the lesser-known goldfinch that precedes the familiar one in public awareness. Both are defined by what comes after: Armstrong by Apollo 11, the Lesser Goldfinch by the American Goldfinch that overshadows it.

The mimicry mosaic maps to Gemini 8's composite character. The Lesser Goldfinch's song is half autobiography and half anthology — original phrases woven with borrowed fragments from dozens of species. Gemini 8 was similarly composite: the first mission to combine rendezvous, docking, and EVA objectives in a single flight plan (Scott's planned EVA was canceled after the emergency). The mission assembled multiple firsts into one timeline, just as the Lesser Goldfinch assembles multiple species into one song. And both encounters with complexity ended abruptly: the thruster malfunction forced early termination, the way the Lesser Goldfinch's song breaks off mid-phrase when a predator passes. Thin margins. The smallest goldfinch, the shortest Gemini mission, both carrying more complexity in their compressed duration than their larger, longer counterparts.
