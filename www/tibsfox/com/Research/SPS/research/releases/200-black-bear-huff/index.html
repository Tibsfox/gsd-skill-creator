The Huff — Explosive Warning Exhalation

“WHOOSH.” Sharp, explosive exhalation through nose and mouth. 100–600 Hz, broadband. Startling at 5–20 meters. Duration: 0.3–1.0 seconds, repeated 2–5 times. The bear lowers its head, sways. Ears forward (alert, not aggressive). The huff is de-escalation — not attack. “I am aware of you. I would prefer you to leave.”

More Common Than You Think

25,000–30,000 Black Bears in Washington. In the forests surrounding every major city. Diet: 80–90% plant (berries, grasses, roots). Generally shy and avoidant. The bear is always closer than you think. Understanding the huff as non-aggressive is essential for coexistence.

FIRST URSIDAE — Degree 200

The bear family enters the engine at the milestone degree. After Cervidae (deer and elk, 195–199), Ursidae opens a new family. Apex omnivore. Solitary. Intelligent. Powerful but avoidant. The huff starts the repertoire with the most commonly encountered sound.

Intermediate

The huff is the opposite of aggression. An aggressive bear charges or jaw-pops. The huffing bear wants you to leave. Warning, not threat.
