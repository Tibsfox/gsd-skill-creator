## What Worked

1. **FIRST URSIDAE at degree 200 milestone is structurally strong.** New family at the milestone number. Bears are a major ecological presence.
2. **Starting with the huff rather than the growl is the right choice.** The huff is the most commonly encountered bear sound. De-escalation, not aggression. Sets the correct tone for the family's entry.
3. **The de-escalation framing is essential.** Bears that huff are not attacking -- they want you to leave. Correcting the common misinterpretation serves both research and public education.
4. **"More common than you think" is a strong theme.** 25,000-30,000 bears in Washington. In forests surrounding every city. The bear is always closer than you think.
5. **E=5 correctly calibrates the huff.** Explosive and startling at close range but short and directed. Not sustained like the elk bugle (E=7). The energy of measured response.
