## FIRST URSIDAE -- THE BEAR FAMILY ENTERS

### A New Family at Degree 200

The Ursidae (bear family) enters the engine at the milestone degree:

- **Ursidae.** The bear family. In the PNW, two species: American Black Bear (*Ursus americanus*) and Grizzly Bear (*Ursus arctos*, extremely rare in Washington, limited to the North Cascades). The Black Bear is the common bear.
- **FIRST URSIDAE.** After Cervidae (deer and elk, 195-199), Ursidae is the next major mammalian family. Bears represent something different in the PNW landscape: apex omnivore, solitary, intelligent, powerful but generally avoidant of conflict.
- **Why the huff?** Not the growl (aggressive) or the jaw-pop (threatening) but the huff -- the most common bear vocalization that humans encounter. The huff is the sound of a bear that has detected you and wants you to leave. It's a measured response: "I see you. Back off." The huff starts the bear's vocal repertoire in the engine with the most representative, most commonly heard, and most misunderstood sound.
- **More common than you think.** This is a key research finding: Black Bears are far more common in the PNW than most people realize. Washington has an estimated 25,000-30,000 Black Bears. They live in the forests surrounding every major city. Suburban encounters are increasingly common. The bear is always closer than you think.

## THE HUFF -- ACOUSTIC ANATOMY

### An Explosive Exhalation

The Black Bear huff is a specific vocalization with a specific purpose:

- **Character.** A sharp, explosive exhalation through the nose and mouth. "WHOOSH" or "HUFF." It sounds like someone blowing out forcefully -- a short, loud breath. The sound is startling at close range (bears typically huff at 5-20 meters from the perceived threat).
- **Frequency.** 100-600 Hz, with a broad spectral profile. Not a pure tone -- it's a noise-like sound (broadband exhalation) with low-frequency emphasis. The huff carries information through its acoustic character rather than its pitch.
- **Duration.** 0.3-1.0 seconds. Short and percussive. May be repeated 2-5 times with 1-3 second intervals.
- **Physical mechanism.** The bear forcefully expels air through the nose and mouth, often while lowering the head and swaying. The exhalation is powered by the diaphragm and the bear's powerful chest muscles. The sound is an honest signal of the bear's size and physical power -- a larger bear produces a louder, deeper huff.
- **Context.** The huff occurs when a bear detects a potential threat (human, dog, another bear) at moderate range. It is a warning -- not an attack signal. Bears that huff are typically deciding whether to flee or stand their ground. The huff says: "I am aware of you. I would prefer you to leave."
- **Often misinterpreted.** Humans who hear a bear huff often interpret it as aggression. In reality, the huff is the opposite of aggression -- it is an attempt to avoid conflict. An aggressive bear does not huff; it charges or jaw-pops. The huff is de-escalation through warning.

(Sources: Black Bear vocalization studies; huff acoustic analysis; bear communication; misinterpretation research)

## BLACK BEAR BEHAVIOR -- THE AVOIDANT OMNIVORE

### More Afraid of You

Black Bears in the PNW are generally avoidant:

- **Diet.** Omnivorous. 80-90% plant material: berries (huckleberries, blackberries, salal), grasses, roots, nuts. Also insects (ants, yellowjacket larvae), fish (salmon when available), carrion, and occasionally small mammals. The Black Bear is primarily a forager, not a hunter.
- **Personality.** Solitary except for mothers with cubs. Generally shy, avoidant of humans and conflict. Most bear encounters in the PNW end with the bear fleeing. The huff is the intermediate response -- the bear has decided not to flee immediately but is communicating that it wants space.
- **Intelligence.** Bears are among the most intelligent mammals in the PNW. They have excellent long-term memory (remembering food sources across years), problem-solving ability, and social intelligence. The huff is part of a sophisticated communication system.
- **Seasonal activity.** Active spring through fall. Winter denning (not true hibernation -- bears in PNW can be active during warm winter spells). The huff is most commonly heard in spring (when mothers with cubs are vigilant) and fall (when bears are actively foraging before denning).
- **Urban-wildland interface.** As development expands into bear habitat, encounters increase. Bears raid garbage, bird feeders, fruit trees. The huff is increasingly heard in suburban settings. Understanding bear communication -- including the huff as non-aggressive -- is essential for coexistence.

(Sources: Black Bear diet and behavior; PNW bear ecology; intelligence studies; urban-wildland interface; bear coexistence)

## E=5 -- THE MEASURED WARNING

### Calibration

The Black Bear huff at E=5:

- **Why E=5?** The huff is explosive and startling at close range. At 5-20 meters, the sound is unmistakable and attention-getting. But it is not a sustained vocalization (like the elk bugle E=7 or rut roar E=7). It is a brief exhalation. The huff is a controlled release of energy -- just enough to communicate the warning, not so much as to trigger a confrontation.

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of a Black Bear (Warning)

**Huff (Warning/De-escalation):**
- **Frequency:** 100-600 Hz. Broadband exhalation. Low-frequency emphasis.
- **Character:** Explosive "WHOOSH" or "HUFF." Short, percussive, startling. Breath-like.
- **Duration:** 0.3-1.0 seconds per huff. Repeated 2-5 times.
- **Context:** Warning. Bear has detected threat at 5-20 m. De-escalation. "I see you. Back off."
- **Carry distance:** 50-200 m. Short-range by design. Not a broadcast -- a directed message.

**Accompanying Behaviors:**
- **Head lowering.** The bear lowers its head during the huff.
- **Swaying.** Side-to-side body sway. Assessing the threat.
- **Bluff charge.** Sometimes the huff precedes a bluff charge -- the bear runs toward the threat but stops short. The bluff charge is another de-escalation attempt (not attack).
- **Ear position.** Ears forward (alert/curious) during huff, not flattened (aggressive).

**Overall Amplitude:** E=5. Explosive, startling, directed. The sound of a powerful animal that does not want a fight. The measured warning.

(Sources: Black Bear huff acoustics; warning behavior; bluff charge studies; engine internal analysis)
