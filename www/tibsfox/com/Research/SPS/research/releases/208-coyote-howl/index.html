CANIDAE RETURNS — last seen at degree 71 (Red Fox). 137-degree gap. The ascending yip-howl group chorus: 3–7 animals that sound like twenty. Frequency-hopping acoustic deception. E=6 — carries 3–5+ miles. Most iconic mammalian sound in North America. Found in every habitat.

The Group Howl — Ascending Yip-Howl Chorus

Begins with sharp staccato yips at varied pitches. Escalates to long, ascending, frequency-modulated howls. The entire pack vocalizes simultaneously, each animal at different pitches and rhythms. Carries 3–5+ miles in open terrain. Duration: 30 seconds to several minutes. The most recognized wildlife sound on the continent.

Acoustic Deception — Three Sounds Like Twenty

Each Coyote frequency-hops — rapid pitch and tone changes that make one animal sound like two or three. A pack of 3–7 sounds like 15–20+. The deception serves territorial defense: rival packs overestimate group size and stay away. The howl is a force multiplier. Social intelligence: each member adjusts in real-time.

The Ultimate Generalist — Thriving Everywhere

Every US state except Hawaii. Every Canadian province. Forest, grassland, urban, suburban, alpine, desert. Expanded range after wolf extirpation. Persecution resilience: a century of systematic killing has increased populations (larger litters, range expansion). The more you kill, the more there are.
