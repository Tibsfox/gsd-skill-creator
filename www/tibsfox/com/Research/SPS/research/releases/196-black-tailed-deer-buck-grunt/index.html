Black-tailed Deer Buck Grunt

Odocoileus hemionus columbianus (Cervidae) — E=4 — Fawn-Buck Pair
Second Black-tailed Deer entry. Fawn-buck pair (195–196). Low, guttural “uurrh” at 50–200 Hz. Fall rut. Territorial claim. Antler displays. E=4 — controlled assertion. Energy match with 764-HERO (E=4 + E=4).

The Territorial Claim — Low Guttural Grunt

“Uurrh.” Low, guttural, 50–200 Hz. Repeated at intervals during the fall rut. The buck patrols his territory, grunting to announce his presence to does and rival bucks. Carries 100–300 meters through forest. The sound of controlled assertion. E=4.

The Rut — November-December

Antlers fully hardened by November. Buck rubs on trees. Scrapes on the ground. Scent marking. The grunt is the vocal component: attracting does, warning rivals. When two bucks meet, antlers lock and they push — contests usually brief, rarely lethal.

Fawn-Buck Pair — 195-196

195: Fawn bleat (500–1,500 Hz, E=3). “Where are you?” 196: Buck grunt (50–200 Hz, E=4). “I am here.” Same species, opposite life stages. High to low. Need to claim. The full vocal range of the PNW’s most common large mammal in two consecutive degrees.
