## What Worked

1. **The Glaucous-winged / Western pair across 289 and 291 is the arc's internal dialogue.** The two great large gulls of Washington State occupy separate arc positions -- 289 (Glaucous-winged, Salish Sea, E=6) and 291 (Western, Pacific coast, E=7). Reading them together gives the full picture: habitat differentiation, hybrid zone, acoustic differentiation. The arc is not just a sequence of birds; it is a structured document of PNW gull ecology.
2. **The E=7 vs. E=6 differentiation is justified and documented.** Western Gull calls are louder and more assertive per bird than Glaucous-winged calls. Rocky island habitat creates acoustic reflectance that amplifies the colony. Both factors support the E step. The calibration is grounded in biology and physical acoustics.
3. **The Pacific coast rocky island habitat is a genuine geographic expansion of the SPS arc.** Many previous SPS entries have been Salish Sea or Puget Sound focused. The Western Gull introduces the outer coast -- the exposed Pacific coast of Washington, different in character, ecology, and acoustic environment from the protected inland waters.
4. **Audubon's naming is historically significant.** John James Audubon named this species in 1839 during his Pacific coast survey work. The name *occidentalis* -- simply "western" -- reflects the species' defining geographic character. This is one of the most geographically literal names in the arc.

## Discoveries

1. **Mixed seabird colony acoustic environments are richer than gull-only colonies.** Western Gull colonies often share space with Brandt's Cormorant, Common Murre, Pigeon Guillemot, and others. The multi-species soundscape of a Pacific coast rocky island colonial site -- Murre growling, cormorant grunts, guillemot whistles, and Western Gull wauuuw calls simultaneously -- is qualitatively different from the single-species gull colony. The SPS engine documents species but the ecological reality is multi-species.
2. **The Farallon Islands Western Gull colony is one of the largest single gull colony concentrations in the eastern Pacific.** While the engine focuses on PNW species, the Western Gull's range extends to California, where the largest colonies are. The Farallons context gives scale to what a maximum Western Gull colony sounds like.
