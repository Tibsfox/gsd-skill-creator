## Discoveries

1. **The "breathing" quality of wind-in-tree sound is amplitude modulation.** The gustiness of coastal storms creates periodic swelling and diminishing of the aeolian tones. What the ear interprets as "breathing" is frequency-modulated amplitude modulation -- a specific acoustic phenomenon, not a metaphor.
2. **The trunk diameter is an amplifier.** The Bernoulli effect around a 3-meter trunk accelerates wind velocity into the canopy, driving higher aeolian frequencies. The size of the tree is not incidental to the sound -- it determines the acoustic character.
