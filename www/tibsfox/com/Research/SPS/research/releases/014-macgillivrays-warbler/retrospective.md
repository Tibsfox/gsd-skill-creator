## Purpose

This is the fifteenth release of the PNW 360 Species engine and the fourth species in the **second decade** (degrees 11--20). The MacGillivray's Warbler introduces **three new structural dimensions** to the circle: (1) repetition with resolution -- the first species whose song contains an explicit narrative arc (A-A-A-B: setup followed by payoff), transforming the vireo's endless loop into a phrase with a destination; (2) behavioral invisibility -- the first species that has distinctive visual field marks but CHOOSES concealment in dense shrub, singing from hiding while possessing the plumage to be identified by sight; (3) Parulidae entry -- the first New World warbler in the engine, from the most species-rich warbler family in the Americas, and only the second neotropical migrant (after the Pacific-slope Flycatcher at degree 6).

The MacGillivray's Warbler also introduces the **ternary / AAB** synthesis type -- repeated identical syllables followed by a contrasting resolution syllable, where the repetition builds anticipation and the contrast releases it. This is the most fundamental structural principle in Western music (tension-release) delivered at minimum scale. It directly evolves from the Hutton's Vireo's ostinato (degree 13): same repetition, one structural addition (the resolution), fundamentally different meaning.

## Three Structural Advances

### 1. Repetition With Resolution: The Minimal Narrative

The MacGillivray's Warbler is the first species in the engine whose song has a **beginning, middle, and end** within a single utterance. The A-A-A-B structure (churry-churry-churry-sweet) is the smallest possible narrative arc: repetition builds anticipation (setup), contrast releases it (payoff).

The resolution taxonomy now spans:
- **Degree 1** (Pacific Wren): No repetition. Maximum improvisation. No resolution because there is no pattern to resolve.
- **Degree 5** (Nuthatch): Single-note steady beat. The metronome. No resolution -- the beat continues.
- **Degree 9** (CB Chickadee): Variation on a theme. Same structure, modified delivery. Resolution through variation, not contrast.
- **Degree 11** (Oregon Junco): Single-note trill above fusion. No resolution -- continuous tone.
- **Degree 13** (Hutton's Vireo): Two-note ostinato below fusion. No resolution -- the phrase repeats indefinitely.
- **Degree 14** (MacGillivray's Warbler): Repeated syllables + contrasting terminal element. **RESOLUTION through contrast.** The first song in the engine that arrives at a destination.

### 2. Behavioral Invisibility: The Chosen Hidden Singer

The MacGillivray's Warbler has distinctive visual field marks (gray hood, yellow belly, broken white eye arcs) but **chooses** to stay hidden in dense shrub. This is distinct from the Pacific-slope Flycatcher at degree 6, which MUST be heard because Empidonax flycatchers are morphologically cryptic. The warbler's concealment is behavioral, not physical -- a performance choice. The bird that could be seen but chooses to be a voice.

This maps to music performance traditions where the maker is invisible by choice: session musicians, ghost writers, producers, film composers. The sound carries the identity. The maker stays behind the thicket.

### 3. Parulidae Entry and Second Migrant

The MacGillivray's Warbler is the **first Parulidae** (New World warbler) in the engine -- the most species-rich warbler family in the Americas, entering through one of its most secretive members. It is also the **second neotropical migrant** (after the flycatcher at degree 6), reinforcing the seasonal layering pattern: resident species form the year-round foundation, migrants add summer voices.

The East/West species pair (MacGillivray's west, Mourning east) introduces the biogeographic concept of sibling species divided by continental geography -- a pattern repeated across many North American bird pairs.

## The Parks Parallel: The Next Generation

The Part A parallel at degree 14 maps to **Aaron Parks** from S36. The parallel operates on four axes:

1. **Contemporary inheritor.** Parks is a young jazz pianist carrying forward the tradition of his predecessors. The warbler carries forward the repetition principle from degree 13 with a new structural addition. Both are "next generation" -- same roots, new growth.
2. **Resolution as transformation.** Parks' harmonic resolutions transform standard jazz phrases. The warbler's sweet syllable transforms repeated churry syllables. In both cases, the ending changes the meaning of the beginning.
3. **Energy 2.** Both are energy-2 -- present, audible, engaged, but not flashy. Understated presence with structural depth.
4. **Hidden depth.** Parks' playing has more happening under the surface than shows at first listen. The warbler has more distinctive plumage than you will ever see. Both reveal their full nature only to careful attention.

## What Worked

### 1. The 13/14 sequential advance (ostinato to narrative) is the strongest structural progression yet
The transition from "repetition without resolution" (vireo, degree 13) to "repetition with resolution" (warbler, degree 14) is the cleanest single-step structural advance in the engine. One addition -- the contrasting terminal syllable -- transforms the loop into a story. The progression is immediately comprehensible and musically powerful.

### 2. The behavioral invisibility vs. morphological crypticity distinction is precise
Distinguishing the warbler's chosen concealment from the flycatcher's imposed crypticity creates a clear taxonomy of invisibility with different musical parallels. The session musician who COULD step to the front but stays behind the board vs. the ensemble player who is genuinely indistinguishable from peers.

### 3. The East/West species pair introduces biogeography
The MacGillivray's/Mourning pair is a concrete example of a major North American biogeographic pattern, with a clean musical parallel (regional variants of the same tradition). This concept will recur as more East/West pairs enter the engine.

### 4. The Parulidae entry through a skulking species is fitting
The most species-rich warbler family in the Americas enters the engine through one of its most secretive members. This is structurally appropriate -- the family's diversity is vast, but the engine meets it through concealment, not display. The warbler is the ambassador who does not show its credentials.

### 5. The ternary form concept connects to familiar musical structures
AAB, AABA, 12-bar blues, call-and-response -- all map cleanly to the warbler's song. The musical theory connections are the most accessible so far because tension-resolution is the most universally understood principle in Western music.

### 6. The dense shrub habitat adds successional dynamics
The disturbance-dependent habitat introduces ecological concepts (succession, disturbance ecology, the paradox of conservation through managed disturbance) that enrich the engine's ecological vocabulary beyond "protect old growth."

## What Could Be Better

### 1. STILL no high-energy species (now 15 degrees without one above energy 2 -- CRITICAL x7)

This is now a **seven-release critical deficiency**. Fifteen degrees. Three energy levels used: 0 (heron's silence), 1 (bushtit, kinglet, siskin), and 2 (everything else). No energy 3, 4, or 5. The recommendation has been at the top of the list for seven consecutive releases. The energy-2 ceiling MUST break. The engine cannot credibly represent "The Sound of Puget Sound" without loud sounds.

### 2. Still only one non-passerine (the heron at degree 0)

Fourteen of fifteen species are passerines. Taxonomic diversity remains narrow. A woodpecker, raptor, seabird, or waterfowl would broaden the foundation.

### 3. Still no marine or shoreline species

"The Sound of Puget Sound" -- fifteen degrees about a Sound with no Sound in them. This gap is conspicuous. Gulls, cormorants, auklets, oyster catchers -- the water birds are absent.

### 4. Still no predator species

The alarm network has broadcasters and receivers but no trigger species. A raptor or predator would complete the information loop and provide the alarm network's reason for existence.

### 5. The energy distribution is heavily skewed low

Three species at energy 1, eleven at energy 2, zero at 3/4/5 (plus the heron at 0). The engine needs high-energy species urgently.

### 6. Two neotropical migrants but no altitudinal migrants

Both migrants in the engine travel to Mexico/Central America. The PNW also has altitudinal migrants -- species that move downslope in winter rather than leaving the region entirely. This pattern is undocumented.

6. **Consider a species with demonstrable mimicry or vocal learning.** The Steller's Jay can mimic hawk calls and other sounds. Corvid vocal learning would introduce a new dimension: sound as acquired behavior rather than innate pattern.
