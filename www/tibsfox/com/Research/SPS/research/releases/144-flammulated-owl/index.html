BREAKS THE RECORD — Ten Consecutive Strigidae

Ten consecutive owl entries (135–144). Seven genera. Eight species. SURPASSES the Picidae nonet. New all-time longest family streak. Energy: E=6 (peak) to E=2 (minimum) — the widest range. Mass: 1400g to 54g — 26:1 ratio. The most comprehensive single-family documentation in the engine.

E=2 — The Quietest Voice in the Engine

A single, soft, low-pitched “boop” at 300–450 Hz. Barely audible at 30–50 meters. Researchers standing directly below a calling bird sometimes cannot hear it. The most ventriloquial sound in the engine. A distant, muffled heartbeat. New SPS minimum.

Dark Eyes — Unique Among Small Owls

The only small owl in North America with dark eyes. All other small owls have bright yellow eyes. Dark brown-black iris. Linked to strictly insectivorous diet. The single most reliable field mark. If it’s a small owl with dark eyes, it’s a Flammulated Owl.

Ponderosa Pine — East-Side Specialist

Open ponderosa pine forests east of the Cascades. Summer breeder only — winters in Mexico/Central America. The only migratory owl in the run. Strictly insectivorous: moths, beetles, grasshoppers. When insects disappear, the owl must leave.
