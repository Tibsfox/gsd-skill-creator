## Structural Event 2: Stream Indicator Species

- **Situation:** Stoneflies are among the most reliable stream health indicators in freshwater ecology. Their presence indicates clean, cold, well-oxygenated water with rocky substrate.
- **Root Cause:** Stonefly nymphs have strict environmental requirements -- low pollution, cold temperature, high dissolved oxygen. They are intolerant of degradation. This makes them ideal diagnostic species.
- **Recommendation:** Document the stream indicator role as a core ecological contribution. The stonefly hatch is not just a sound event -- it is a health diagnosis. The engine now contains a species whose presence IS the data.

## What Worked

Stoneflies as stream health indicators is well-established in freshwater ecology. The diagnostic role -- presence confirms stream health -- adds a fundamentally new type of contribution to the engine. No prior species functioned explicitly as an indicator.

### 2. The Return to Streams Fills a Genuine Gap
37 degrees without a stream species. The engine needed to return to water. The stonefly returns from the insect side, adding a new perspective -- the nymph that lives IN the stream rather than the bird that watches it from above.

### 3. Plecoptera as a New Order Is a Real Taxonomic Achievement
Sixth insect order. First with an aquatic life stage. The engine's insect diversity now spans terrestrial, aerial, and aquatic habitats.

### 4. The Energy Drop Is Dramatically Effective
E=5 (aggressive hornet) to E=2 (barely audible wing rustle) in consecutive degrees. The contrast is extreme and structurally resonant. The engine goes from maximum insect volume to minimum insect volume in one step.

### 5. The Wing Rustle Is a Genuine Acoustic Distinction
Not a buzz, not a chirp, not a call, not a whine. A physical sound of wings on rock and water surface. A new category of insect sound -- passive emergence rather than active signaling.

Thirty-four consecutive original species. The engine's reliability continues.
