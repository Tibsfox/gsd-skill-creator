North American Porcupine

Erethizon dorsatum (Erethizontidae) — E=3 — 30,000 Quills
FIRST ERETHIZONTIDAE — New World porcupine family. 30,000 barbed quills. Eerie mating moans (mistaken for Bigfoot). Teeth-chattering warning signal. Nocturnal tree-climber. E=3 — the quality unsettles, not the volume. 30–40% show healed fractures from falls.

30,000 Quills — The Ultimate Deterrent

Modified hairs with microscopic barbed tips that expand in warm tissue. Not projectiles — release on contact. Barbs work deeper with each muscle contraction. Antibiotic coating reduces infection (self-quilling from tree falls is common). Only the Fisher has evolved a counter-technique: repeated face attacks.

The Mating Moans — Eerie Night Sounds

Fall mating season: long, wavering, almost human moans at 300–2,000 Hz. Males calling for females in dark forest. Mistaken for human distress calls, Bigfoot vocalizations, injured animals. 2–8 seconds per moan. Audible 50–200 m. The sound you hear at 2 AM and never forget.

Nocturnal Tree-Climber — Life in the Canopy

5–14 kg of clumsy-looking climber. Feeds on inner bark (cambium) year-round. May spend days in a single tree in winter. 30–40% of adults show healed bone fractures from falls. Slow metabolism. No need for speed when you carry 30,000 barbed spears.
