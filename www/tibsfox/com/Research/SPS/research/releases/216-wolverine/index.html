Wolverine

Gulo gulo (Mustelidae) — E=6 — Mustelidae Returns — Fiercest Relative to Size
Mustelidae returns. Gulo gulo — “glutton glutton.” Guttural growl at 100–800 Hz. 10–25 kg — drives bears and wolves from kills. E=6 — disproportionate for body size. 25–50 in Washington. North Cascades. Wilderness indicator. The fiercest predator relative to its size.

Pound-for-Pound Ferocity

10–25 kg. Drives bears and wolves from kills. Attacks prey 5–10x its size. Jaws crush frozen bone. Acoustic deception: growl sounds like much larger animal. 100–500+ km² territories. Travels 30–50 km/day through deep snow. Where wolverines persist, the wilderness is still wild.

Wilderness Indicator

Requires vast, undeveloped territory. 25–50 in Washington. North Cascades. Snow-dependent denning — females require deep snow into May. Climate change vulnerable. As snow recedes, denning habitat shrinks. Food caching: surplus meat in snowbanks, defended against all predators.
