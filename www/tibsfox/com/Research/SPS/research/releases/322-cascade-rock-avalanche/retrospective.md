## What Worked

1. **The self-amplifying cascade principle is the entry's defining physical insight.** Unlike most acoustic events that peak immediately and then decay, the rock avalanche grows. Each impact dislodges additional material; additional material produces more impacts; the mass grows and the velocity increases. E=8 is reached not at the initiation but at the event's developing maximum. This is acoustically distinct from all previous E=8 entries in the engine.
2. **The summer thermal trigger explanation is scientifically accurate and specific.** Freeze-thaw progressive fracture opening + summer thermal diurnal cycle + dry-season cohesion reduction = a loaded system that triggers during the afternoon thermal maximum. The physical explanation is complete without requiring geological expertise to understand.
3. **The distinction between volcanic (hydrothermal alteration) and non-volcanic (glacial oversteepening) Cascade cliff types is important.** Mt. Rainier's weak clay-mineral alteration zones behave differently from the North Cascades' crystalline granite. Both produce avalanches but through different geological mechanisms.
4. **The Mt. St. Helens 1980 note is essential context.** The largest rock avalanche in recorded North American history was the triggering event for the eruption. The degree-322 cliff face event is a scaled-down version of the same physical principle that catastrophically altered the landscape 46 years ago.

## Discoveries

1. **The glacial valley reverberance effect is acoustically significant.** A rock avalanche in a U-shaped glacial cirque or valley produces wall reflections that extend the perceived duration of the acoustic event considerably beyond the actual mass movement duration. The mountain becomes an acoustic chamber. This reverberant quality is a defining characteristic of Cascade alpine avalanche acoustics that distinguishes them from avalanches in non-glacial topographies.
2. **The episodic vs. sustained distinction in E=8 events is important for engine calibration.** The rock avalanche (degree 322) reaches E=8 through brief peak intensity. The tidal bore (degree 323) reaches E=8 through sustained hydraulic mass. The Hoh winter storm (degree 324) reaches E=8 through complete spectral simultaneity. Three consecutive E=8 SPS events, three different mechanisms.
