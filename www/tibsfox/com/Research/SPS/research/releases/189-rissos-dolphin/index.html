Risso’s Dolphin — Low Grunts & Clicks

Grampus griseus (Delphinidae) — E=4 — Second Delphinidae
Second Delphinidae — the scarred dolphin. Deep-water squid specialist. Born dark gray, ages to nearly white through accumulated scarring. Low grunts + clicks. Monotypic genus. 10 consecutive marine mammals (180–189) — BREAKS Picidae nonet record.

The Scarred One — A Living Record

Born dark gray. Accumulates white scars throughout life: squid tentacle rakes, conspecific tooth rakes, parasites. Older individuals become nearly entirely white. Each dolphin carries a unique record of its life on its skin. The scars enable individual identification. E=4.

Deep-Water Specialist — Squid Hunter

Dives to 300–1,000+ meters. Primary prey: deep-water squid. Reduced dentition (2–7 pairs, lower jaw only) — adapted for sucking in squid, not biting. Nocturnal feeding follows squid vertical migration. The diet that shapes the scarred body.

Marine Mammal Era — 10 Consecutive — NEW RECORD

10 consecutive marine mammals (degrees 180–189). BREAKS the Picidae nonet record — now the longest taxonomic streak in engine history. 5 families: Phocidae (2), Otariidae (3), Mustelidae (1), Phocoenidae (2), Delphinidae (2). Energy range: E=1 to E=6.
