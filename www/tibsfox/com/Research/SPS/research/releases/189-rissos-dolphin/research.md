## SECOND DELPHINIDAE -- THE SCARRED DOLPHIN

### The Deep-Water Specialist

Degree 189 continues the Delphinidae sequence:

- **Second Delphinidae.** Following the Pacific White-sided Dolphin (188, E=5), the Risso's Dolphin is the second dolphin in the engine. Where the first dolphin was acrobatic and vocal, the Risso's is deep-diving and scarred.
- **Monotypic genus.** *Grampus* contains only one species: *Grampus griseus*. The Risso's Dolphin is alone in its genus -- taxonomically isolated within the largest cetacean family.
- **The scarred appearance.** Risso's Dolphins are born dark gray and accumulate white scarring throughout their lives -- from squid tentacle rakes, from interactions with other Risso's Dolphins (tooth rakes), and from parasites. Older individuals can appear nearly entirely white. The scars record a lifetime.
- **Deep-water specialist.** Unlike the Pacific White-sided Dolphin that rides the continental shelf, the Risso's Dolphin prefers deep water -- submarine canyons, steep continental slopes, waters 400-1,000 meters deep. A deep-water specialist.

## E=4 -- LOW GRUNTS AND CLICKS

### Why E=4

The Risso's Dolphin registers at E=4:

- **Low grunts.** Risso's Dolphins produce distinctive low-frequency burst-pulse sounds often described as "grunts" or "barks." These are lower in pitch than the Pacific White-sided Dolphin's squawks. The grunts are social and functional but less varied than the previous dolphin's repertoire.
- **Clicks.** Broadband echolocation clicks for deep-water navigation and prey detection. The clicks are powerful -- adapted for detecting squid in deep, dark water.
- **Less whistle-dominant.** While Risso's Dolphins can whistle, their vocal repertoire is less whistle-dominant than many other delphinids. The grunt + click combination is more characteristic.
- **Why E=4?** The vocal repertoire is real and functional but less varied and less demonstrative than the Pacific White-sided Dolphin (E=5). The deep-water lifestyle means less surface vocalization. The grunts are distinctive but not loud by dolphin standards. E=4 reflects a competent but restrained vocal presence -- appropriate for a deep-water specialist that communicates through grunts and clicks rather than the full squawk-whistle-click chorus of the previous dolphin.

## THE SCARRED BODY -- A LIVING RECORD

### Aging from Gray to White

The Risso's Dolphin's scarring is its most distinctive feature:

- **Born dark gray.** Calves are born medium to dark gray, smooth-skinned. The dorsal surface is darker than the ventral.
- **Accumulating scars.** Throughout life, the skin accumulates linear white scars from three primary sources:
  - **Squid tentacles.** Risso's Dolphins feed primarily on squid. The squid's tentacle suckers and hooks rake the dolphin's skin during capture. Every meal leaves marks.
  - **Conspecific tooth rakes.** Risso's Dolphins rake each other with their teeth during social interactions -- dominance displays, play, mating competition. The parallel linear scars are characteristic.
  - **Parasites.** Cookie-cutter sharks and lampreys leave circular scars. Parasitic copepods leave smaller marks.
- **Aging to white.** As scars accumulate and the underlying pigment fades, older Risso's Dolphins become progressively lighter -- from dark gray to light gray to nearly entirely white. The oldest individuals are almost ghostly white with dense networks of overlapping scars.
- **Individual identification.** The unique scarring patterns allow researchers to identify individual dolphins -- each animal carries a unique record of its life on its skin.

(Sources: Risso's Dolphin dermatology; scarring patterns; photo-identification studies; engine internal analysis)

## DEEP-WATER FEEDING -- SQUID SPECIALIST

### The Diet That Shapes the Body

- **Primary prey: squid.** Cephalopods (primarily squid) constitute the bulk of the Risso's Dolphin diet. Histioteuthis, Dosidicus, Loligo, and other deep-water squid species.
- **Deep diving.** Forages at depths of 300-600 meters, with recorded dives to 1,000+ meters. The deep diving allows access to mesopelagic and bathypelagic squid that other dolphins cannot reach.
- **Nocturnal feeding.** Squid migrate vertically -- ascending at night, descending by day. Risso's Dolphins often feed more actively at night when squid are shallower and more accessible.
- **Reduced dentition.** Risso's Dolphins have fewer teeth than most dolphins: typically only 2-7 pairs in the lower jaw, none in the upper jaw. The reduced dentition is adapted for squid capture -- squid are sucked in rather than bitten. The teeth are used primarily for social interactions (tooth raking).

(Sources: Risso's Dolphin diet studies; cephalopod ecology; deep-diving behavior; dental morphology; engine internal analysis)

## PUGET SOUND PRESENCE

### Visitor Status

- **Outer coast and offshore.** The Risso's Dolphin is primarily an offshore species in Washington waters -- continental shelf edge, submarine canyons, deep channels.
- **Strait of Juan de Fuca.** Occasional visitor to the Strait of Juan de Fuca, typically associated with deep-water prey concentrations.
- **Rare in inner Puget Sound.** Less likely to enter inner Puget Sound than the Pacific White-sided Dolphin. The Risso's Dolphin prefers deeper, more oceanic water.
- **Population.** Eastern North Pacific population estimated at 16,000-20,000. Washington waters: intermittent presence, seasonal, numbers variable.

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of the Risso's Dolphin

**Grunts (Burst-Pulse Sounds):**
- **Frequency:** 1-20 kHz (lower range than Pacific White-sided Dolphin squawks).
- **Character:** Low-frequency burst-pulse sounds -- buzzy, grunting quality. Social and functional.
- **Context:** Social interaction, feeding coordination, group cohesion.
- **Duration:** 0.1-1 second per burst.

**Clicks:**
- **Frequency:** Broadband, 2-120 kHz.
- **Character:** Powerful echolocation clicks adapted for deep-water prey detection. Longer inter-click intervals for deep targets.
- **Context:** Deep-water navigation, squid detection, obstacle avoidance.

**Whistles:**
- **Frequency:** 4-22 kHz.
- **Character:** Less prominent than in Pacific White-sided Dolphin. Present but not the primary vocalization type.
- **Context:** Social contact, group coordination.

**Overall Amplitude:** E=4. Functional, lower-frequency, more restrained than the acrobatic Pacific White-sided Dolphin. Deep-water specialist with deep-water voice.

(Sources: Soldevilla et al. 2010; Madsen et al. 2004; Risso's Dolphin acoustics; engine internal analysis)
