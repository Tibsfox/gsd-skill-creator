## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **E=4 is defensible.** The musical laughing call is individually distinctive and musically complex but not acoustically dominant in the way that Canada Goose flocks (E=5) are. Flock-driven. Consistent with Cackling Goose and Brant at E=4 despite different acoustic character.

2. **The genus shift documentation is accurate.** Branta (black geese, black heads, black bills) to Anser (gray geese, gray heads, orange bills). The visual and acoustic differences are well-documented.

3. **The laughing call description is accurate.** Cornell Lab, Sibley, and Ely & Dzubin 1994 all describe the distinctive yodeling/laughing quality. The frequency range (500-3,000 Hz) is supported by acoustic studies.

4. **The "specklebelly" nickname is widely used.** Field guides, hunting literature, and birding communities all use this name. Well-documented.

5. **Cross-references to SPS-131, SPS-129, SPS-118, SPS-128, and SPS-107-115 are all defensible.**
