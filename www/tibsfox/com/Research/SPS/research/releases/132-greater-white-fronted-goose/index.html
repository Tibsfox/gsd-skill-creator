Musical Laughing Call — E=4

The Greater White-fronted Goose produces the most musically complex call of any PNW goose — a high-pitched, yodeling “kla-ha” or “kow-yow” at 500–3,000 Hz. Liquid, laughing cadence. Genuinely resembles human laughter, especially when multiple birds call simultaneously. A flock produces a musical chattering chorus — lighter, higher, more cheerful than any Branta goose. Often detected by voice first in mixed flocks.

First Anser Genus — The Gray Geese

Genus shift from Branta (black geese, 129–131) to Anser (gray geese, 132). Visually distinct: gray-brown head (no black), pink-orange bill with white band at base, orange legs. Dark belly markings = “specklebelly” nickname. The visual shift parallels the acoustic shift: laughing vs. honking/cackling/murmuring. A fundamentally different goose.

Fifteenth Consecutive Anatidae (118–132) — Unprecedented

Fifteen consecutive. The Picidae nonet (107–115, nine consecutive) is now dwarfed — nearly doubled. Energy post-climax: E=6, E=5, E=4, E=4, E=4 — a plateau at E=4 for three entries (130–132). The streak spans ducks (118–126), swans (127–128), Branta geese (129–131), and now Anser geese (132). Four genera. Three subfamilies. Unprecedented diversity.
