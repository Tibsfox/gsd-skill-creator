## What Worked

1. **The matriarch post-reproductive role as knowledge center is the correct E=10 analysis.** The key insight is not that elder females lead the hunt -- it is why: decades of accumulated knowledge about salmon locations (specific rivers, timing, water column depth preferences) that becomes survival-critical during low-Chinook years. The Brent et al. 2015 Current Biology correlation (pod survival vs matriarch age) is the scientific anchor for the claim that this is knowledge transmission, not just social hierarchy.
2. **The five-phase hunt sequence (detection → coordination → approach → capture → post-capture) as the acoustic architecture is the correct analytical frame.** Each phase has a distinct acoustic signature. The full acoustic event of the hunt is not a single climactic moment but a sequential deployment of different acoustic systems. This is the correct parallel to the S36 pairing (Bikini Kill's systematic deployment of vocal, instrumental, and community-building approaches).
3. **Chinook specialization as the reason the hunt acoustics are maximally complex is the correct causal analysis.** The specialization produces the acoustic event: Chinook are large and individually powerful, requiring maximum coordinated approach and the most complex echolocation deployment. If SRKW hunted small schooling fish in herding behavior, the acoustic event would be less complex. The Chinook cultural specialization is what makes this entry E=10.
4. **The pairing with Bikini Kill on the structural basis of female expertise at operational center is exact.** Not metaphorical -- both are maximum-energy systems whose operational logic runs through female expertise. The matriarch organizes the hunt through accumulated knowledge and vocalization; Hanna organizes the movement through precision and urgency of articulation. The pairing is structural.
5. **The three-acoustic-system convergence (echolocation + discrete calls + breathing) as the E=10 calibration is solid.** Each system alone is not uniquely E=10; the convergence of all three at maximum-foraging rate, organized by matriarch coordination, during peak Chinook season, is E=10. The convergence principle is consistent with the closing arc's earlier SPS entries.

## Discoveries

1. **Post-reproductive female longevity as reproductive fitness (not just social) in killer whales.** In most mammalian species, post-reproductive survival has no obvious fitness function. In killer whales, it does: the knowledge the matriarch carries increases pod survival during low-salmon years. Post-reproductive female longevity is an adaptation, not a biological artifact. This is the scientific discovery that reframes the entire matriarch role.
2. **The Chinook cultural specialization being transmitted through the matriarchal social structure across generations.** The prey preference is not genetic -- it is cultural. The matriarch teaches the next generation what to hunt, where, and when. The cultural transmission of the specialization through the social structure means that the loss of elder matriarchs degrades the pod's knowledge base, not just its social structure. The ESA endangered status becomes comprehensible through this frame.
3. **Food sharing (males sharing with mothers) as a documented post-capture acoustic context.** The post-capture sharing behavior extends the acoustic event beyond the hunt itself: the social interactions around food distribution are an acoustic event in their own right, distinct from the foraging calls.
