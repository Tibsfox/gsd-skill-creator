## Structural Event 2: Dome-Shaped Nests -- Unique Among Corvids

- **Situation:** The Black-billed Magpie builds enclosed, dome-shaped nests of sticks and mud. 60-120 cm diameter. 40-50 days to build. Unique among corvids. Reused by other species.
- **Root Cause:** The dome structure is an evolved adaptation for predator and weather protection in open-country habitat. The architecture is a species-specific behavior not shared by any other corvid.
- **Recommendation:** Document the dome nest as unique among corvids. Note the community-infrastructure function (reuse by other species). The nest is architecture.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Dome nests. Unique among corvids. Community infrastructure. Track.

## Structural Event 3: Bold Black-and-White Plumage -- The Long Tail

- **Situation:** The magpie's bold black-and-white plumage and extraordinarily long tail (longer than the body) make it the most visually striking corvid in the engine.
- **Root Cause:** The plumage and tail are species-specific. The iridescence (green-bronze-purple) is produced by feather microstructure. The long tail serves both flight-maneuverability and display functions.
- **Recommendation:** Document the visual distinctiveness. Note the long tail as diagnostic. The magpie is unmistakable.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Bold plumage. Long tail. Iridescent. Unmistakable. Track.

## Structural Event 4: Open Country Habitat -- East of the Cascades

- **Situation:** The Black-billed Magpie occupies open country primarily east of the Cascades -- sagebrush steppe, agricultural fields, riparian corridors.
- **Root Cause:** The magpie requires open ground for foraging and scattered trees for nesting. The Cascade Range divides the magpie's open-country habitat from the wetter, forested habitat of the western corvids.
- **Recommendation:** Document the open-country niche. Connect to Red Fox (71, open country) and Townsend's Ground Squirrel (78, open country). Note the Cascades as dividing line.
