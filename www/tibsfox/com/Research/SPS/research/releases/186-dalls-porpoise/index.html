Dall’s Porpoise — Rooster-Tail Splash

Phocoenoides dalli (Phocoenidae) — E=5 — FIRST PHOCOENIDAE
FIRST PHOCOENIDAE — the porpoise family enters the engine. First cetacean. No audible vocalization — echolocation above human hearing (>100 kHz). The “rooster tail” — a V-shaped spray at 35 mph, visible from a mile away. Fastest porpoise on Earth. Stealth echolocation.

The Rooster Tail — 35 mph on the Surface

At top speed the Dall’s Porpoise creates a distinctive V-shaped cone of spray — the “rooster tail.” Visible from over a mile away. No other cetacean creates this pattern. The primary field identification marker. 35 mph (55 km/h) — the fastest porpoise in the world. Silent voice. Explosive presence.

Stealth Echolocation — Above Predator Hearing

Echolocation at >100 kHz — far above human hearing (~20 kHz) AND above orca hearing (~100 kHz ceiling). Narrow-Band High-Frequency (NBHF) clicks: a Phocoenidae family trait. The porpoise navigates and hunts invisibly to its primary predator. Silence as survival strategy.

Black and White — The Tuxedo Cetacean

Black body, white flank patch. Dorsal fin often white-tipped. Thick, muscular, built for power. Frequently bow-rides boats in Puget Sound. Groups of 2–10, occasionally 100+ during feeding. 2,000–4,000 in Washington inland waters.
