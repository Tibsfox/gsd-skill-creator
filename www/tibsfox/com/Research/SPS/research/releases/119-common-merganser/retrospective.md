## Structural Event 2: The Sawbill -- Serrated Bill Adaptation

- **Situation:** The Common Merganser's serrated bill is one of the most specialized feeding adaptations in North American waterfowl. Backward-pointing tomia create a one-way trap for fish.
- **Root Cause:** Mergansers diverged from dabbling ducks to exploit piscivory. The serrated bill is the defining adaptation: narrow, hooked, serrated. Catches fish up to 30 cm.
- **Recommendation:** Document the adaptation in detail. Note the four key features: backward-pointing serrations, narrow shape, hooked tip, red-orange coloration.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Sawbill adaptation documented. Track whether merganser bill comparisons arise at degree 120 (Hooded Merganser).
