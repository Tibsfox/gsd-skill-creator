Cougar Chirp

Puma concolor (Felidae) — E=4 — Second Felidae
Second Felidae. Cougar scream/chirp comparison pair (212–213). Bird-like contact chirp at 1,000–3,000 Hz. 0.1–0.3 seconds. Mother-kitten communication. The gentle side of the apex predator. E=4 — intimate, close-range, tender. Carries 5–30 meters. The whisper after the scream.

The Chirp — Bird-like and Gentle

1,000–3,000 Hz, 0.1–0.3 seconds. Short, bird-like, whistling. “PEEP” or “CHIRP.” Field biologists have confused it with bird calls. Used between mothers and kittens for close-range contact. Also exchanged during courtship — solitary predators signaling non-aggression. Carries 5–30 meters.

Life in the Den — The Vulnerable Apex

1–6 kittens (typically 2–3), born blind and spotted. Hidden dens: rock overhangs, dense brush, hollow logs. 40–70% first-year mortality. Each chirp: the kitten is alive. The mother is near. 12–24 months of dependency. The spots fade. The chirp fades. Adulthood is silence.
