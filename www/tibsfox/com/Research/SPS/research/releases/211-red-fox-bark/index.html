Red Fox Bark

Vulpes vulpes (Canidae) — E=4 — 140° Callback — Quartet Complete
140-degree callback from degree 71. Sharp, explosive “WRAHH!” at 500–2,000 Hz. The most adaptable canid. Forest, field, suburb, urban edge — the fox thrives in boundary zones. E=4 — explosive but brief. Flash, not glow. Fourth Canidae. Canidae quartet COMPLETE (208–211).

The Bark — Sharp and Explosive

“WRAHH!” — 0.2–0.5 seconds. Percussive, not tonal. Begins and ends abruptly. 500–2,000 Hz, peak at 800–1,500 Hz. Territory, alarm, contact. Carries 200–500 m. Plus the breeding scream — a blood-curdling wail often mistaken for a human in distress.

140-Degree Callback — The Fox Returns

First appeared at degree 71. Returns at degree 211. 140 degrees — more than a third of the full 360. At 71, the engine was still establishing. At 211, the fox closes a family quartet with comparative context that didn’t exist before. The fox bridges time within the engine itself.

Canidae Quartet COMPLETE — 208-211

Energy arc: 5–4–7–4. Moderate, dip, spike, return. Habitat arc: everywhere, den, wilderness, edges. Size arc: coyote (10–16 kg), pup (0.25–5 kg), wolf (30–80 kg), fox (4–7 kg). Four strategies for being a canid. The quartet closes.
