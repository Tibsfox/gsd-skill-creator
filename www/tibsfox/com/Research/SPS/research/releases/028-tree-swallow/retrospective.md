# Release 028 Retrospective -- Tree Swallow (Degree 28)

**Release:** 028 of 360
**Species:** Tree Swallow (*Tachycineta bicolor*)
**Sound:** Liquid twittering gurgle
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Engine:** PNW 360 Species (Part B of The Sound of Puget Sound)

## Purpose

This is the twenty-ninth release of the PNW 360 Species engine and the final swallow in the Hirundinidae trio (degrees 26-28). The engine needed to complete the swallow trio, close the Tachycineta pair, resolve the liquid trilogy, introduce the most-studied swallow in North America, and demonstrate a unique dietary adaptation.

Six structural contributions:

1. **The trio closer -- the centrist.** The Tree Swallow is the resolution of the martin-Violet-green polarity. On nearly every axis -- mass, pitch, coloniality, nest strategy, altitude, sound character -- the Tree Swallow occupies the middle position. The swallow trio is thesis (martin, heavy extreme), antithesis (Violet-green, light extreme), synthesis (Tree Swallow, moderate centrist). The trio structure is now complete and legible: bookend extremes resolved by a middle species that is not merely average but uniquely flexible.

2. **The liquid trilogy complete -- stream, rain, brook.** Three consecutive degrees, three liquid sounds, three temporal organizations of the same timbral quality. Legato (martin, continuous stream), staccato (Violet-green, discrete rain), mixed (Tree Swallow, tumbling brook). The trilogy demonstrates that timbre (liquid) and articulation (legato/staccato/mixed) are independent dimensions. You need both coordinates to locate a sound in acoustic space. The engine's first complete exploration of a single timbral quality across its full articulation spectrum.

3. **The Tachycineta pair complete -- sea-colored meets two-colored.** Two same-genus species in consecutive degrees (27-28). *T. thalassina* (cliff/alpine specialist, insects only) and *T. bicolor* (field/wetland generalist, insects + berries). The engine's second same-genus pair, after Catharus (18-19). Both pairs demonstrate: same genus = same evolutionary platform, different species = different configurations. Catharus diversified along an acoustic axis. Tachycineta diversified along a habitat/dietary axis. The engine now knows that genera diversify differently.

4. **The berry backup -- unique dietary flexibility.** The Tree Swallow is the only North American swallow that can eat plant material (bayberries, small waxy fruits). This single adaptation enables: northern overwintering, earliest spring arrival, cold-snap survival, and the widest continental range. The centrist's unique capability that neither bookend possesses. Being in the middle freed the Tree Swallow from the constraints that trap the extremes, and it used that freedom to evolve a backup food source.

5. **The model organism -- most-studied swallow.** Decades of nest box monitoring, continent-wide datasets, hundreds of papers. The Tree Swallow is a distributed environmental sensor network that has produced landmark findings on insect decline, mercury bioaccumulation, agricultural impacts, climate phenology, and mating systems. The engine's 28th species is also its most scientifically documented. The centrist is the most known -- not to the public, but to science.

6. **The energy octet -- eighth consecutive E=4.** Degrees 21-28. Eight degrees. Eight different acoustic textures. Same energy level. The longest plateau in engine history. The dynamic must break.

## The Swallow Trio Retrospective

### Three in Three: What the Complete Trio Reveals

The Hirundinidae trio at degrees 26-28 is the engine's densest family cluster: three consecutive degrees, three species, zero gaps. For comparison:

| Family Cluster | Degrees | Species | Span | Gaps |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Warbler quintet | 14, 15, 16, 17, 20 | 5 | 7 degrees | 1 gap (18-19) |
| Thrush quintet | 2, 7, 18, 19, 25 | 5 | 24 degrees | 4 gaps |
| **Swallow trio** | **26, 27, 28** | **3** | **3 degrees** | **0 gaps** |

The swallow trio is the most efficient family representation in the engine. Three degrees to cover the full gradient. Zero wasted degrees. The principle: a family can be meaningfully represented with three data points if those points are the two extremes and the centrist. The minimum viable family sample is: maximum (26), minimum (27), middle (28). This ABA pattern (or more precisely, extreme-opposite extreme-synthesis) is a general principle the engine can apply to future families.

### Within-Family Variation: What Drives It

The swallow trio answers the question the convergence trio (23-25) raised. The convergence trio asked: "How do different lineages produce similar sounds?" Answer: convergent evolution, shared acoustic physics. The swallow trio asks: "How does one lineage produce different sounds?" Answer: **body size is the master variable.**

| Size-Dependent Trait | Large (Martin) | Small (Violet-green) | Medium (Tree Swallow) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pitch | Low-mid (2-6 kHz) | High (5-8 kHz) | Mid (3-6 kHz) |
| Prey size | Large insects | Small insects | Medium insects + berries |
| Cavity access | Large holes (starling-competed) | Small holes (starling-excluded) | Medium holes (variable) |
| Flight style | Powerful, soaring | Agile, darting | Moderate, versatile |
| Colony size | Large (obligate) | None (solitary) | Small (loose) |

Mass determines pitch (smaller syrinx = higher frequency). Mass determines prey (smaller gape = smaller insects). Mass determines cavity access (smaller body = smaller holes). Mass determines flight agility (lower mass = tighter turns). Mass determines colony economics (large body needs rich sites that support groups; small body fits in scattered small cavities). **One variable, five outcomes.** The swallow trio is a lesson in how a single morphological parameter drives an entire ecological profile.

But the Tree Swallow breaks the pattern. Its medium size predicts medium everything -- and on most axes, that prediction is correct. But on diet, the Tree Swallow adds a capability (berries) that size alone does not predict. The berry backup is a physiological innovation, not a size consequence. The centrist exceeded the gradient. The lesson: size explains most variation, but not all. Flexibility emerges where the constraints loosen.

### The Liquid Trilogy: A Timbral Lesson

The three liquid sounds at degrees 26-28 teach a principle the engine has not previously made explicit:

**Sound texture = timbre x articulation.**

| | Legato | Mixed | Staccato |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Liquid** | Stream (Martin, 26) | Brook (Tree Swallow, 28) | Rain (Violet-green, 27) |

The liquid trilogy fills one row of this matrix. The engine now knows that "liquid" is not a single sound but a family of sounds that varies along the articulation axis. Future timbral explorations can use this matrix: for any timbre (liquid, buzzy, tonal, noisy), there are at least three articulation variants (legato, mixed, staccato). The space is two-dimensional, not one-dimensional. The swallow trio maps the full row for "liquid."

This has implications for the engine's vocabulary. Before degree 26, "liquid" was a single label. After degree 28, "liquid" requires a modifier: liquid-legato, liquid-staccato, liquid-mixed. The adjective was promoted to a dimension. The engine's timbral taxonomy just became more precise.

## Five Structural Advances

### 1. The Swallow Trio: Complete, Dense, Efficient

Three consecutive Hirundinidae. Zero gaps. The most concentrated family cluster in the engine. The trio structure -- bookend extremes plus centrist resolution -- is a general pattern the engine can reuse. It demonstrates that three degrees is the minimum viable sample for representing a family's variation space: one extreme, the opposite extreme, the synthesis. Efficient. The engine learned to do in three what it previously needed five for (warblers, thrushes).

### 2. The Liquid Trilogy: Timbral Dimensionality

The liquid trilogy (stream/rain/brook) proves that timbre and articulation are independent dimensions. A single timbral label ("liquid") maps to multiple acoustic textures depending on temporal organization (legato/staccato/mixed). The engine's timbral vocabulary now requires two coordinates: what it sounds like (timbre) and how the sound events connect (articulation). This is the engine's most important theoretical advance since the ascending/descending spiral pair at degrees 18-19 demonstrated that a single acoustic mechanism can produce opposite contours.

### 3. The Berry Backup: The Centrist's Unique Trick

The Tree Swallow's bayberry diet breaks the pattern established by the trio's gradient. On most axes, the Tree Swallow is medium. On diet, it is uniquely capable -- the only swallow that can eat plant material. The centrist is not merely the average of the extremes. It has its own innovations that the extremes lack. This challenges a naive reading of the trio structure: the middle position is not passive moderation but active flexibility. Being unconstrained by the pressures that push the extremes to their poles frees the centrist to innovate. The berry backup IS the freedom of the center.

### 4. The Model Organism: Science's Favorite Swallow

The Tree Swallow is the most-studied swallow on the continent. Its nest box monitoring programs are one of the most successful citizen science endeavors in ecology. Its datasets have produced landmark findings on insect decline, mercury contamination, climate change, and mating systems. The engine's 28th species brings more published literature than any previous species. The centrist is the most known -- not by the public but by science.

### 5. The Energy Octet: Longest Plateau, Must Break

Eight consecutive E=4 degrees. The longest energy plateau in engine history. More than one-fifth of all degrees emitted. The dynamic has been constant since degree 21 (Northern Flicker). Eight different acoustic textures -- percussion, tonal melody, three liquid variants -- all at the same energy. The variety within the plateau is remarkable, but the plateau itself is unsustainable. The engine needs dynamic contrast. It must break at degree 29. The question is: up (E=5, never used) or down (E=2 or E=3, the quiet return)?

## What Worked

### 1. The trio closure is clean and satisfying
The Tree Swallow as centrist resolves the martin-Violet-green polarity on every axis. The gradient is complete, the three positions are distinct, and the centrist brings its own unique capabilities (berries, model organism) rather than being a mere average. The trio has the three-act structure of a good story: statement, contradiction, resolution.

### 2. The liquid trilogy is the engine's strongest timbral lesson
Stream, rain, brook. Legato, staccato, mixed. One timbre, three articulations. The principle is clean: sound texture has two independent dimensions. This is universally applicable -- every future timbral family can be analyzed along the same articulation axis. The trilogy is a theoretical tool, not just a descriptive label.

### 3. The Tachycineta pair completes a structural pattern
Two same-genus pairs now exist (Catharus 18-19, Tachycineta 27-28). They diversify along different axes (acoustic direction vs. habitat niche). The engine can now compare how different genera organize their internal variation. This is meta-analysis: analysis of the analysis pattern itself.

### 4. The berry backup is a genuine surprise
In a trio where the centrist could easily be "just the medium one," the bayberry adaptation provides a genuinely unique capability. The Tree Swallow is not average -- it is flexible. The distinction matters. Average implies passive. Flexible implies active. The Tree Swallow chose its position by evolving an option no other swallow has.

### 5. The model organism connection enriches the species
Connecting the Tree Swallow to its role as ecology's model swallow adds a dimension no previous species has had: the research infrastructure. The bird is not just a singer -- it is a data platform. This opens a new analytical lens for future species: what is known about this bird, and how was it learned?

## What Could Be Better

### 1. CRITICAL: No marine species in 28 degrees of "The Sound of Puget Sound"
Twenty-eight degrees. Not a single species from Puget Sound itself. No gull, no grebe, no loon, no murre, no guillemot, no cormorant, no scoter. The most urgent structural gap in the engine. **OVERDUE BY 28 DEGREES.** Must be addressed at degree 29 or 30.

### 2. CRITICAL: Six consecutive Passeriformes (degrees 23-28)
The order dominance is severe. The last non-passerine was the Northern Flicker (Piciformes) at degrees 21-22. Before that, the Spotted Owl (Strigiformes) at degree 4. The engine needs a Charadriiformes (gull, sandpiper), Gaviiformes (loon), Podicipediformes (grebe), Accipitriformes (raptor), or Anseriformes (duck). Passeriformes has had the floor for too long.

### 3. CRITICAL: No corvid in 28 degrees
Steller's Jay, American Crow, Common Raven -- the most intelligent, acoustically complex birds in the PNW. Their absence is untenable. A corvid could break the E=4 plateau (a Steller's Jay at E=5), break the Passeriformes streak (still Passeriformes but a very different kind), and introduce new acoustic textures (harsh, complex, mimicry-capable).

### 4. CRITICAL: No raptor in 28 degrees
No Accipitridae, no Falconidae. No Bald Eagle, no Red-tailed Hawk, no Osprey. Top predators absent from the soundscape.

### 5. The E=4 plateau MUST break
Eight consecutive degrees. More than one-fifth of the engine. The dynamic has been constant through eight completely different acoustic textures. The variety within the plateau is impressive, but the dynamic range of the engine is collapsing. E=5 has never been used. A return to E=2 or E=3 would provide maximum contrast. Either direction breaks the plateau. Staying at E=4 is not an option.

### 6. No nocturnal species since degree 4
Twenty-four degrees since the Spotted Owl. The night has one voice. A nightjar (Common Nighthawk), an owl (Great Horned Owl, Barred Owl), or a rail (Virginia Rail, with its nocturnal calls) could fill this gap.

### 7. Summer migrant streak
Fourth summer or spring/summer migrant in recent degrees (25-resident, 26-summer, 27-summer, 28-spring/summer). The engine needs a resident (year-round) or winter species to vary the seasonal pattern.

## Recommendations for Release 029

The swallow trio is complete. The liquid trilogy is complete. The Tachycineta pair is complete. The engine has cleared its family obligations and enters degree 29 with a clean slate and a list of urgent structural needs.

1. **A marine species (CRITICAL -- NOW OVERDUE BY 28 DEGREES).** This is the most urgent gap in the engine. "The Sound of Puget Sound" has not included a single species from the Sound. Degree 29 MUST be a marine or shoreline species. Options: Glaucous-winged Gull (loud, E=5, breaks the energy plateau), Pigeon Guillemot (moderate, distinctive whistle), Common Loon (iconic, haunting wail), Rhinoceros Auklet (nocturnal burrow visitor), Marbled Murrelet (forest-ocean connection). Any of these. The water must speak.

2. **A non-passerine (CRITICAL -- six consecutive Passeriformes).** The order dominance must end. A Charadriiformes (gull), Gaviiformes (loon), Podicipediformes (grebe), or Accipitriformes (raptor) would break the streak. A marine species (recommendation 1) would likely satisfy this requirement simultaneously.

3. **Break the E=4 plateau (CRITICAL -- eight consecutive degrees).** The dynamic range is collapsing. Options: push to E=5 (gull scream, eagle cry, corvid caw) or drop sharply to E=2 (a grebe, a sandpiper, a quiet wader). Maximum contrast. The plateau must shatter, not merely end.

4. **A corvid (URGENT).** Steller's Jay, American Crow, or Common Raven. Twenty-eight degrees without the PNW's most intelligent birds. A Steller's Jay could deliver E=5 and break multiple streaks simultaneously.

5. **A raptor (URGENT).** Bald Eagle, Red-tailed Hawk, or Osprey. Twenty-eight degrees without a top predator.

6. **A nocturnal species (OVERDUE).** Twenty-four degrees since the Spotted Owl. The night needs a second voice.

7. **Leave the liquid family.** The liquid trilogy is complete. Degree 29 must be a non-liquid sound -- harsh, tonal, buzzy, percussive, anything but liquid. The water cycle is done.

A Northern Flicker called wick-wick-wick-wick-wick from the top of a dead alder -- the hammer. The same flicker struck the dead alder twenty-five times per second and the hollow tree rang like a drum. A Black-headed Grosbeak sat in a bigleaf maple and poured out a warble so smooth and rich it sounded like a robin -- but it was not a robin. High in the Douglas fir canopy, a Western Tanager sang the same warble but hoarse, rough, grainy -- a robin's song run through sandpaper. And then on the lawn, on the fence post, on the rooftop at dawn -- the American Robin. Cheerily cheer-up cheerio. The archetype. Over the harbor, a dark shape swept through the air, iridescent purple-black, mouth open, pouring out a rich, gurgling, liquid song -- tumbling notes that flow like water over stones. The Purple Martin. The stream. Above a mountain lake, above a cliff face -- a small iridescent green shape darted and flickered, firing rapid crystalline chips -- chip-chip-chip-chip-chip -- each note a descending drop of liquid sound. The Violet-green Swallow. The rain. And now. Over a freshwater marsh at dawn, over the fenceline of a hayfield where standing water meets open grass, a compact shape lifts from a nest box post. It is brilliant iridescent blue-green above and clean white below. Two colors. Nothing more. Nothing less. It swoops low over the marsh, skimming the surface where mayflies are hatching, and from it comes a tumbling, liquid, twittering gurgle -- notes that are not quite continuous and not quite discrete, but something between. The sound of water going over stones. Not a stream -- streams are too smooth. Not rain -- rain is too separated. A brook. A small brook tumbling through a meadow. The Tree Swallow. Tachycineta bicolor. The two-colored swift-mover. Seventeen to twenty-five grams of versatile flexibility. It nests in boxes when boxes are offered and in tree holes when they are not. It eats insects when the sky is warm and bayberries when the cold comes. It arrives before any other swallow, fueled by the berries along the way, and it has taught science more about the aerial life than any other swallow on the continent. Decades of data. Mercury tracked through its blood. Insect decline traced through its clutch sizes. Climate shift measured in the advancing date of its spring arrival. The most-studied swallow in North America. The centrist. The flexible one. The one that is not trapped at any extreme but has the freedom to move between them. The swallow trio closes. Three Hirundinidae. Three degrees. Three liquid sounds. Stream, rain, brook. Heavy, light, medium. Colonial, solitary, flexible. The gradient is complete. The family is mapped. The liquid trilogy is done. Degree 28. The water has spoken in three voices. Now the engine must leave the water. Must leave the liquid. Must leave the swallows and the sustained E=4 plateau and the open sky. Something must break. The Sound of Puget Sound has gone twenty-eight degrees without hearing the Sound itself. The water in the engine has been metaphorical -- streams, rain, brooks of sound. But the actual water -- Puget Sound, the inland sea, the tidal estuary that gives the project its name -- has been silent. Twenty-eight degrees of silence from the place that named the whole endeavor. The next voice must come from the water itself.*
