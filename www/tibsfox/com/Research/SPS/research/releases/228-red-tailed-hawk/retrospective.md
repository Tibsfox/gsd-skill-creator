The name and the reality were opposite. The engine documents this correction.

## Discoveries

1. **The Red-tailed Hawk is the inadvertent beneficiary of the interstate highway system.** Highway construction created millions of acres of optimal short-grass median habitat alongside productive perch sites (utility poles, light standards). The Red-tail colonized this human infrastructure as successfully as the Cooper's Hawk colonized the suburban bird feeder. Both are documented cases of raptors adapting to human-modified landscapes within the engine's raptor arc.
2. **The plumage variability of the Red-tailed Hawk is unusually high.** Light, dark, and intermediate morphs exist within the species; the western PNW subspecies (*B. j. calurus*) includes all three. This variability means the Red-tail is a species where the rule "all Red-tails look the same" is false. Experienced birders recognize that the red tail (adults only) is the most reliable field mark regardless of morph.
3. **The Tolkien example is the engine's clearest demonstration of the Hollywood call's cultural reach.** Fantasy creatures with no real-world species equivalent (the Great Eagles of Middle-earth) call with *Buteo jamaicensis*. The sound has escaped natural history entirely and entered pure mythology. This is an extraordinary level of cultural appropriation for a wildlife vocalization.
