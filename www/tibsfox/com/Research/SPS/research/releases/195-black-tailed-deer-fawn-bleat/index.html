First Cervidae — Deer Enter the Engine

For 15 consecutive degrees (180–194), every SPS entry was a marine mammal. 7 families. Seals, sea lions, otters, porpoises, dolphins, whales. At degree 195, the streak breaks. Cervidae enters — the deer family, one of the most successful mammal families on Earth. The engine returns to land.

The Fawn Bleat — A Sound of Need

A thin, high “maaaa” — wavering, plaintive, 500–1,500 Hz. The fawn calls when separated from its mother. Every bleat is a calculated risk: it attracts the mother but could alert predators. 40–60% fawn mortality in the first year. The sound of vulnerability. E=3.

Marine Mammal Streak — Ended at 15

15 consecutive marine mammals (degrees 180–194). 7 families: Phocidae, Otariidae, Mustelidae, Phocoenidae, Delphinidae, Balaenopteridae, Eschrichtiidae. Energy range: E=1 to E=7. The streak falls 2 short of the Anatidae record (17). At 195, the engine returns to land.
