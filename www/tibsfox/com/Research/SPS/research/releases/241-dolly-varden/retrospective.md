## What Worked

1. **The char genus introduction is taxonomically important.** *Salvelinus* is distinct from *Oncorhynchus* in key ways (light spots on dark background; cold-water specialization; different evolutionary lineage within Salmonidae). Introducing the genus here, following Bull Trout at 240, provides the taxonomic context for the entire char arc.
2. **The Dickens name origin is one of the engine's more surprising cultural tangents.** A 19th-century fashion style derived from a Dickens character naming a 21st-century conservation priority fish is the kind of historical serendipity the engine should document.
3. **The E=2 acoustic profile is philosophically consistent.** The engine's willingness to document near-silence as a valid entry -- the barely-audible ripple of a cold-water predator -- is important. Sound has a full spectrum, and E=2 is as legitimate as E=8.
4. **The transition flag (to Petromyzontidae at 243) is the entry's most important structural note.** Dolly Varden closes the Salmonidae arc. One more entry (Cutthroat Trout, 242) remains before the engine crosses into jawless fish territory.

## Discoveries

1. **The Brook Trout threat is underappreciated.** The introduction of eastern Brook Trout (*Salvelinus fontinalis*) throughout the West for sport fishing has had severe consequences for native chars (Dolly Varden, Bull Trout) through competition and hybridization. This is a conservation story driven by human error -- introducing a native fish to the wrong region.
2. **Climate change as primary threat is a new pattern in the fish arc.** Unlike salmon (whose primary threats include dams and fishing), Dolly Varden's primary threat is glacial retreat. There is no mitigation for this. The entry documents a species whose habitat is vanishing in the most fundamental possible way.
3. **The Bull Trout / Dolly Varden taxonomic separation history is complex.** Both species were called "Dolly Varden" until the 1980s-90s. Management programs designed for one species were actually affecting two different species with different ecology. The 1980s taxonomic work was genuinely important for conservation.
