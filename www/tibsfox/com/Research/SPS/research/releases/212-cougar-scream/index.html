Cougar Scream

Puma concolor (Felidae) — E=7 — FIRST FELIDAE — Apex Land Predator
FIRST FELIDAE. The cat family enters. Piercing, drawn-out “AAAAEEEEIII” at 500–2,500 Hz. Almost human-sounding. The most terrifying sound in the forest. E=7 — same energy as the wolf, utterly different strategy. The ghost cat. Solitary apex predator. 2,000+ in Washington. Rarely seen. Rarely heard. Never forgotten.

The Scream — Terror in the Forest

500–2,500 Hz, overtones to 4+ kHz. 1–3 seconds. Piercing, drawn-out, wavering wail. Used by females in estrus to attract mates across territories of 50–400 km². Overlaps human distress frequencies — triggers primal freeze response. Carries 1–3 km through forest canopy. Plus chirps and whistles (mother-kitten, surprisingly bird-like) and purrs (the largest cat that can purr).

FIRST FELIDAE — The Cat Family Enters

41 living species. Retractable claws. Binocular vision. Obligate carnivores. Solitary hunters. The cougar: 50–100 kg, 1.5–2.4 m nose to tail. Also called mountain lion, puma, catamount, panther — most common names of any animal in English. Range: Yukon to Patagonia. PNW’s apex land predator.

The Ghost Cat — Unseen Apex Predator

Large animals moving through human-populated areas unseen. Motion cameras: suburban backyards, urban parks, commuter trails. Ambush predator: stalk, sprint, neck bite. The kill is quick. 2,000+ cougars in Washington. Encounters increasing. Fatal attacks extremely rare. Proximity fear: knowing the predator is there but never seeing it.
