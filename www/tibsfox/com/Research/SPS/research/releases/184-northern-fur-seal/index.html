Northern Fur Seal — Colony Growl

Callorhinus ursinus (Otariidae) — E=5 — Densest Fur
Densest fur of any mammal — up to 300,000 hairs per square inch. Third and final Otariidae. Nearly hunted to extinction for this fur. 1911 conservation treaty. Pelagic lifestyle. Colony growl/bleat texture. Marine mammal block closes (180–184).

Densest Fur — 300,000 Hairs per Square Inch

Two layers: dense, waterproof underfur trapping air against the skin, and coarser guard hairs for protection. 2–3x denser than sea otter fur. The fur provides insulation instead of blubber — maintaining body temperature in the frigid North Pacific without the mass penalty. The adaptation that nearly caused extinction.

Nearly Hunted to Extinction

From millions to fewer than 300,000 by the early 20th century. The North Pacific Fur Seal Convention (1911) — one of the first international conservation treaties — banned pelagic sealing. Partial recovery, then a 50% decline since the 1950s. Currently Vulnerable (IUCN) and Depleted (MMPA).

Pelagic Lifestyle — Months at Sea

Outside breeding season, Northern Fur Seals live in the open ocean — rarely touching land. Months at sea, feeding and swimming. Unusual among pinnipeds. Breeds on Pribilof Islands (Alaska). Passes through PNW waters during migration.
