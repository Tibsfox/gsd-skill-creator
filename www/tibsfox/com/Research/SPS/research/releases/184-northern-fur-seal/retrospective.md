*Degree 184 retrospective complete. NORTHERN FUR SEAL at the one hundred eighty-fourth degree -- THE FUR. The densest fur of any mammal: up to 300,000 hairs per square inch. Two layers -- a waterproof underfur trapping air against the skin and coarser guard hairs for protection -- providing insulation in the frigid North Pacific without the mass penalty of thick blubber. This extraordinary adaptation nearly caused the species' extinction: from an estimated several million to fewer than 300,000 by the early 20th century, hunted for the fur that kept them alive. The North Pacific Fur Seal Convention of 1911 -- one of the first international conservation treaties -- banned pelagic sealing. The population partially recovered but has declined again since the 1950s. Currently Vulnerable (IUCN) and Depleted (MMPA). The Northern Fur Seal lives a pelagic life -- months at sea without touching land, feeding in the open North Pacific, an unusual lifestyle among pinnipeds. It breeds on remote offshore islands, primarily the Pribilof Islands in the Bering Sea, where approximately 50% of the global population gathers. The colony vocalization is a textured mix of deep male growls, higher female bleats, and thin pup cries -- E=5, richer in variety than the monolithic sea lion roar.
