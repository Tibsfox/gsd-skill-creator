## Purpose

This is the thirteenth release of the PNW 360 Species engine and the second species in the **second decade** (degrees 11--20). The Pine Siskin introduces **three new structural dimensions** to the circle: (1) the electronic sound -- the first species whose vocalizations sound distinctly like a synthesizer, with buzzy timbre mapping to a sawtooth waveform and ascending pitch sweep mapping to an LFO on oscillator pitch; (2) irruptive migration -- the first species with stochastic (non-resident, non-migratory) presence, driven by conifer seed crop failure, introducing noise/randomness into the temporal model; (3) information carrier -- the siskin's presence at a given location signals ecological conditions in distant forests, making the bird a mobile data point about ecosystems you cannot see.

The Pine Siskin completes the **three temporal modes** of the engine: resident (DC signal -- constant presence), migrant (periodic signal -- calendar-driven arrivals, established at degree 6), and irruptive (stochastic signal -- event-driven, seed-crop-determined presence). These three modes exhaust the major categories of animal movement patterns. All three are now represented.

## Three Structural Advances

### 1. The Electronic Sound: Biological Sawtooth

The Pine Siskin's zhreee is the first vocalization in the engine that sounds distinctly like a synthesizer. The acoustic structure maps to a **sawtooth waveform with ascending frequency sweep**: all harmonics present, amplitude decreasing as 1/n, fundamental sweeping from low to high. On the spectrogram, multiple parallel frequency bands ascend simultaneously -- the visual signature of harmonically rich frequency modulation.

This is distinct from every previous sound type:
- The Varied Thrush (degree 2) produces a pure tone -- sine wave. Clean, single frequency.
- The Oregon Junco (degree 11) produces a trill -- rapid discrete pulses at constant pitch.
- The Pine Siskin produces a **buzzy ascending sweep** -- sawtooth wave with LFO on pitch. The synthesizer in feathers.

The contrast with the Pacific-slope Flycatcher (degree 6) is particularly instructive. Both sweep upward in pitch. But the flycatcher's upslur is a clean sine wave sweep (single ascending line on spectrogram). The siskin's zhreee is a sawtooth sweep (multiple ascending lines on spectrogram). Same direction. Radically different harmonic content. The flycatcher asks a question. The siskin fires an electrical signal.

### 2. Irruptive Migration: The Third Temporal Mode

Every previous species had predictable presence: either resident (always there) or migrant (calendar-driven). The siskin is the first **irruptive** species -- present in some years, absent in others, driven by conifer seed crop failure rather than seasonal cues.

This completes the three temporal modes:
- **Resident (DC):** Present year-round. A constant. 10 of the first 12 species.
- **Migrant (periodic):** Arrives and departs on calendar schedule. The flycatcher at degree 6.
- **Irruptive (stochastic):** Present or absent based on external resource conditions. The siskin at degree 12.

In signal-processing terms: DC signal, periodic signal (sine wave), and stochastic signal (colored noise). The engine now has all three fundamental signal types represented in its temporal model.

### 3. Information Carrier: The Bird as Data Point

The siskin's presence at a given location carries information about ecosystem conditions elsewhere. If siskins are flooding into Seattle feeders, it means conifer seed crops have failed in the boreal/montane forests of British Columbia or Alberta. The bird is a mobile signal about distant resources -- an ecological telegraph.

No previous species carried this kind of distributed information. Residents tell you about local conditions. Migrants tell you what time of year it is. Irruptives tell you what's happening in forests you can't see.

## The Lanphere Parallel: The Quiet Return

The Part A parallel at degree 12 maps to **Lanphere** from S36. The parallel operates on four axes:

1. **Unpredictable presence.** Lanphere's appearances were not on a fixed schedule -- periods of visibility alternated with periods of absence. The siskin's presence is similarly stochastic -- driven by external conditions, not a calendar.
2. **Defined by absence.** Both carry more significance when they appear because the appearance cannot be taken for granted. A resident (or a musician who's always at the same venue) becomes background. An irruptive (or a musician who surfaces unpredictably) commands attention when present.
3. **Energy 1.** Both are quiet. The siskin's zhreee is distinctive but not loud. Lanphere's energy level is similarly restrained -- not a powerhouse but a recognizable signal at close range.
4. **Redemption arc.** Present, absent, present. The return is not guaranteed. When it happens, it resets the context.

## What Worked

### 1. The three temporal modes are now complete

Resident, migrant, and irruptive: the three fundamental categories of animal presence are all represented in the engine by degree 12. This is a structural completion -- not a new feature but the final piece of a framework that was implicit since degree 6 introduced the first migrant. DC, periodic, stochastic.

### 2. The sawtooth/synthesizer mapping is acoustically grounded

The zhreee genuinely shows multiple parallel harmonic bands on the spectrogram -- this is not metaphor but measured acoustic reality. The mapping to sawtooth waveform is technically rigorous. The siskin really does produce a sound whose spectral structure matches synthesizer terminology more closely than any previous species.

### 3. The flycatcher contrast (degree 6 vs. degree 12) illuminates harmonic content

Both sweep upward. One is a sine wave (clean). The other is a sawtooth (buzzy). The comparison between the two ascending sweeps, distinguished by harmonic content alone, is the cleanest demonstration of what "timbre" means in the engine so far.

### 4. The conifer seed crop connection grounds the irruptive behavior in specific ecology

The research traces the chain: masting dynamics --> seed crop failure --> irruptive dispersal --> presence at observation site. This is not hand-waving about "unpredictable birds" -- it is a documented ecological mechanism with specific literature (Koenig & Knops 2001).

### 5. The energy-1 pair (bushtit vs. siskin) reveals temporal contrast within the same energy level

Same energy, opposite temporal behavior. The bushtit whispers constantly (DC quiet). The siskin whispers when it shows up at all (stochastic quiet). This demonstrates that energy level and temporal mode are independent dimensions.

### 6. The Xenakis/Cage/free jazz connections are structurally accurate

Stochastic composition (individual events unpredictable, aggregate behavior deterministic) maps directly to irruptive migration (individual bird paths unpredictable, population movement follows resource gradient). Free jazz timing (events when conditions demand, not on a grid) maps to irruptive timing (arrivals when seed crops fail, not on a calendar). These are genuine structural parallels, not loose analogies.

## What Could Be Better

### 1. STILL no high-energy species (now 13 degrees without one above energy 2 -- CRITICAL x5)

This is now a **five-release critical deficiency**. Thirteen degrees. Three energy levels used: 0 (none -- the heron's silence), 1 (bushtit, siskin), and 2 (everything else). No energy 3, 4, or 5. The dynamic range has no ceiling. The recommendation has been at the top of the list for five consecutive releases. Degree 13 MUST break the energy-2 ceiling. The engine cannot credibly represent "The Sound of Puget Sound" without loud sounds.

### 2. Still only one non-passerine (the heron at degree 0)

Twelve of thirteen species are passerines. Taxonomic diversity remains narrow. A woodpecker, raptor, seabird, or waterfowl would broaden the foundation.

### 3. Still no marine or shoreline species

"The Sound of Puget Sound" -- thirteen degrees about a Sound with no Sound in them. A gull, cormorant, pigeon guillemot, or orca would connect the engine to its namesake. This gap is becoming conspicuous.

### 4. Still no predator species

The alarm network has broadcasters and receivers but no trigger species. Adding a predator (Cooper's Hawk, Barred Owl, Bald Eagle) would complete the information loop.

### 5. The energy distribution is skewed low

Two species at energy 1, ten at energy 2, zero at 3/4/5 (plus the heron at 0). The engine needs high-energy species to demonstrate the full dynamic range. A Steller's Jay (energy 4--5), Pileated Woodpecker (energy 4), or Bald Eagle (energy 3--4) would reset the balance.

### 6. The warble deserves more structural analysis

The extended buzzy warble (5--15 seconds) is a significant vocalization that was described but not deeply analyzed structurally. Future work should decompose the warble into its component phrase types and map its internal organization.
