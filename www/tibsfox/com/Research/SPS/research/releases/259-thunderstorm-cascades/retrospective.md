## What Worked

1. **The mountain reflection mechanism is the key acoustic insight.** Flat-land thunder decays in a few seconds. Cascade valley thunder lasts 30-60 seconds because valley walls, ridge faces, and rock outcrops return reflected arrivals at different delays. The 'rolling' character of mountain thunder is the terrain speaking. This is not obvious and it is the correct explanation.
2. **The 30,000 K temperature is the most arresting physical fact.** The lightning channel temperature (approximately 5x the sun's surface temperature) contextualizes why the acoustic energy is E=8. The explosive thermal expansion from 30,000 K in a fraction of a millisecond is a genuine physical extreme.
3. **The fire weather connection documents a direct ecological chain.** Summer Cascade thunderstorms are the primary natural wildfire ignition source. Thunder at degree 259 connects directly to Lightning Strike Tree at degree 260. The arc from weather to ignition is the engine documenting a natural causal sequence.
4. **"Environment to event" is the correct framing for the 259→260 transition.** Thunder (259) is the storm surrounding the observer. Lightning strike (260) is the storm's specific action at a specific place. This transition in character is as significant as the transition in energy (E=8→E=9).

## Discoveries

1. **Mount Rainier as the highest point in Washington extends above most cumulonimbus tops.** This means Rainier's summit is not in the clouds during many thunderstorms; it rises above them. The reflection geometry at Rainier -- the volcanic cone returning thunder from multiple angles -- is the most extreme in the state.
2. **Alpine Lakes Wilderness is simultaneously the most-visited wilderness in Washington and one of the most dangerous locations in summer.** The beauty and the danger are the same thing: open lakes above treeline = the most spectacular scenery and the most exposed position in a lightning storm.
