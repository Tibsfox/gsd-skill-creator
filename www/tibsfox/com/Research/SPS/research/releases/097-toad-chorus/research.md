## THE SOUND: SUSTAINED MUSICAL PURRING -- 97th ACOUSTIC ELEMENT (species side)

### The Call

The toad's call is a **sustained, musical purring trill** [1, 2]:

**The mechanism:** Males inflate the vocal sac and produce a long, continuous trill by rapidly vibrating the laryngeal muscles. The trill can last **10-30 seconds or more** -- far longer than any other amphibian call in the engine. The vocal sac acts as a resonating chamber, amplifying and enriching the harmonic content.

**The acoustic character:** A musical, purring tone. Harmonic-rich, with a fundamental frequency typically between 1-3 kHz depending on species. The trill is smooth and continuous -- no gaps, no clicks, no percussive elements. A **sustained note** rather than a repeated call. Multiple males calling simultaneously create a **dense, layered chorus** where individual trills overlap and interweave, producing a continuous wall of musical purring.

**PNW character:** The Western Toad (*Anaxyrus boreas*) produces a softer, higher-pitched trill compared to the Eastern American Toad. The sustained, musical quality is the same [1].

### Why E=4

1. **LOUD AND PERSISTENT.** The toad chorus is loud. Multiple males calling simultaneously create a dense wall of sound audible hundreds of meters from the pond.
2. **SUSTAINED.** Individual trills last 10-30+ seconds. The chorus is continuous for hours during peak breeding.
3. **COMPARABLE TO CRICKET CHORUS.** E=4 at degree 87 (cricket chorus) for amplitude and persistence. The toad chorus matches in sustained intensity.
4. **HIGHER THAN WOOD FROG.** The wood frog chorus (96, E=4) is percussive and intermittent. The toad chorus is sustained and continuous. Both E=4 but different temporal structures.
5. **DOMINATES THE NIGHTTIME SOUNDSCAPE.** Near breeding ponds, the toad chorus IS the soundscape.

### Contrast with Prior Anura Acoustic Elements

- **Pacific Treefrog "kreek-ek"** (degree 86, Hylidae) -- sharp, high-frequency, repeated calls. The toad trill is sustained, lower, purring.
- **Spring Peeper peep** (degree 90, Hylidae) -- high-pitched ascending peep, repeated. The toad trill is a continuous purring note.
- **Wood Frog clucking** (degree 96, Ranidae) -- percussive, duck-like quacking. The toad trill is smooth, musical, sustained.

## ECOLOGY: THE BREEDING POND AND THE SUSTAINED TRILL

### Pond Habitat

**Habitat:** Shallow, still or slow-moving water -- ponds, lake margins, seasonal pools, beaver ponds [1, 2]. The Western Toad breeds in a variety of aquatic habitats from sea level to subalpine elevations. Outside of breeding season, toads are largely terrestrial, living in forests, meadows, gardens, and other moist habitats.

### Chorus Dynamics

**Calling behavior:** Males gather at breeding ponds and call in sustained trills to attract females [1, 2]. The chorus dynamics:
- **Individual trills** last 10-30+ seconds, far longer than any treefrog or ranid call.
- **Chorus density** can include dozens to hundreds of males at a single pond.
- **Temporal pattern** peaks at dusk and continues through the night during breeding season.
- **Sustained wall of sound** -- individual trills overlap continuously, creating a musical drone.

### True Toads vs Other Anurans

Bufonidae (true toads) are distinguished from other Anura by [1, 2]:
- **Dry, warty skin** (vs smooth skin in most frogs).
- **Parotoid glands** -- large glands behind the eyes that produce defensive toxins.
- **Short legs** adapted for walking rather than leaping.
- **Sustained trilling call** -- the longest continuous calls in Anura.
