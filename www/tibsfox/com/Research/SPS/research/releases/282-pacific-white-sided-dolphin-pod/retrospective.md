## What Worked

1. **Delphinidae returning at 282 after Phocoenidae at 281 confirms the family progression.** The engine is systematically documenting marine mammal families: Delphinidae (orca, 270-276) → Phocoenidae (Dall's Porpoise, 281) → Delphinidae again (Pacific White-sided Dolphin, 282) → Otariidae (Steller Sea Lion, 283). The family arc is structured.
2. **The aerial acrobatics + call burst is the correct acoustic entry mode.** Not just the echolocation (ultrasonic, primarily inaudible) or just the whistles (social but quieter at E=5-6). The combined event -- impact splash of multiple leaping animals + burst of vocalizations -- is what a pod in full social activity sounds like. E=7 is correct.
3. **The cooperative foraging connection to Steller Sea Lion (283) is a genuine ecological link.** Both species aggregate at herring schools. The engine's consecutive-degree pairing reflects actual co-occurrence in Pacific Northwest marine ecology.
4. **The burst-pulse vocalization is the right acoustic feature to foreground.** Burst-pulse calls increase during excitement and arousal -- they are the animal's acoustic expression of the energy state that produces the aerial behaviors. The burst-pulse is the sound of E=7 in a dolphin pod.
