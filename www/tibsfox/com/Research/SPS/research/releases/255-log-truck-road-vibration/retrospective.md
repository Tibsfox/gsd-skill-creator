## Discoveries

1. **The spotted owl listing (1990) as acoustic history.** The dramatic reduction in federal old-growth logging post-1990 transformed the forest road soundscape on federal lands. Fewer log trucks on fewer forest roads -- the acoustic consequence of a major conservation decision. The engine captures this historical context.
2. **Acoustic masking range (100-500m).** The log truck's noise can mask biophony within this range. This connects the industrial sound to the ecological acoustics of the working forest: the truck's passage temporarily silences the birds.
3. **Forest road as wildlife corridor.** The paradox: the road that carries the log truck also creates edge habitat and travel routes for deer, elk, and bear. The industrial scar and the wildlife corridor are the same feature.
