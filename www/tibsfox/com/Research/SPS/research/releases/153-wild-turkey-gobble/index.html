153°

Wild Turkey — Gobble

Meleagris gallopavo (Phasianidae) — E=6 — Powerful Liquid Gobble
FIRST PHASIANIDAE. The gamebird family enters the engine. E=6 maximum energy returns after a 45-degree gap (since degree 108). The gobble is audible at over one mile — one of the most powerful bird vocalizations in North America. 100–9000 Hz. Liquid thunder. Ben Franklin’s preferred national bird. Reintroduced to the PNW. Restoration success.

The Gobble — Liquid Thunder

~30 pulses in ~1 second — so rapid they merge into a continuous rolling, liquid sound. Syringeal production with crop/throat sac resonance amplification. Broad spectrum: 100–9000 Hz. Peak energy: 1000–4000 Hz. 100+ dB at close range. Audible at 1+ mile. Amplitude modulation aids long-distance propagation. Dawn chorus: chain-reaction gobbles from multiple toms. Acoustically overwhelming.

PNW Restoration — Not Native, Now Established

Wild Turkeys were absent from Washington when settlers arrived. Introduction programs by WDFW: Eastern, Rio Grande, and Merriam’s subspecies placed in eastern Washington oak-pine habitats. Now established in Klickitat, Yakima, Okanogan Counties and expanding. National recovery: from 30,000 birds (1930s) to 7+ million today. One of the great wildlife restoration successes in North American history.

Phasianidae Quartet (153–156)

Four gamebirds enter the engine:
153: Wild Turkey gobble (E=6, liquid thunder).
154: Dusky Grouse deep booming (E=5, subsonic).
155: Ruffed Grouse drumming (E=5, mechanical).
156: Sooty Grouse muffled booming (E=5, ventriloquial).
Vocal to mechanical. Loudest to most mysterious.
