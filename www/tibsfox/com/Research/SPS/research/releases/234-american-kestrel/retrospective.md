## What Worked

1. **E=5 consistency across all four Falconidae entries is a real pattern.** The four falcons are all E=5 despite substantial size and ecological differences. The energy consistency reflects the family's characteristic controlled, focused calling pattern -- not the maximum aggression of E=7-8, not the near-silence of E=2.
2. **UV vision is the Kestrel's most surprising fact.** That a commonly-seen roadside bird has a sensory advantage invisible to all competing raptors (and to humans) is the kind of discovery that distinguishes deep research from surface documentation.
3. **The population decline mystery is worth flagging.** A common, familiar bird declining for unclear reasons -- with proposed causes from Cooper's Hawk recovery to pesticides to climate -- is an open question that the engine should track.

## Discoveries

1. **The Peregrine-to-Kestrel size ratio is extraordinary.** Female Peregrine at 1,500g to female Kestrel at 165g = 9x weight ratio within the same family across four degrees. The Falconidae contains genuinely extreme size diversity.
2. **The nest box acceptance is exceptional in the raptor world.** Most raptors resist nest boxes or use them only reluctantly. The Kestrel's acceptance of precisely-dimensioned boxes has made it one of conservation's most tractable species.
