### The Second Swallow and the Missed Target: Aerial Grace Near the Objective

The Violet-green Swallow is the second consecutive Hirundinidae (after the Purple Martin at degree 26) — the engine's first swallow pair. Like the martin, the Violet-green Swallow sings in flight, feeds in flight, and lives its active life in three-dimensional aerial space. But where the martin is the largest North American swallow (heavy, powerful, deep-voiced), the Violet-green is lighter, more agile, higher-pitched — the swallow that flickers through forest openings and over water with a grace that the bulkier martin cannot match.

Ranger 3 reached the Moon's neighborhood but missed by 36,793 km — close but not close enough, the spacecraft passing through the target zone without making contact. The Violet-green Swallow's aerial foraging is a continuous series of near-misses and contacts — the bird swoops toward flying insects, sometimes catching them, sometimes missing, each pass through the insect cloud a trajectory that may or may not connect. The swallow's success rate is high but not perfect. Each miss is immediately followed by another pass. The bird does not dwell on missed targets. It adjusts and tries again. Ranger 3 did not have this luxury — one pass, one miss, one failure.

The swallow pair (degrees 26-27) mirrors the Ranger pair progression: Ranger 1-2 (stuck in orbit) → Ranger 3 (escaped orbit, missed Moon). Purple Martin (degree 26, heavy, powerful, stationary-colony-based) → Violet-green Swallow (degree 27, light, agile, mobile). The heavier species appeared with the heavier failure (stuck in orbit). The lighter species appears with the lighter failure (missed target but reached the vicinity). The progression toward success continues.

### Liquid Chips and the Aerial Acoustic Palette

The Violet-green Swallow's "twitter and liquid chips" extends the aerial-song vocabulary introduced by the Purple Martin's "rich gurgling liquid song." Two Hirundinidae, both singing in flight, both producing liquid-timbre vocalizations — the swallow family's acoustic signature is fluid, flowing, continuous, produced while the body is in motion. The Hirundinidae pair establishes liquid timbre as a family trait, the way the Turdidae established melodic beauty and the Parulidae established intensification strategies.
