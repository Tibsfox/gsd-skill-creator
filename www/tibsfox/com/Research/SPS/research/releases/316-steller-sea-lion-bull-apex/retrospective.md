4. **The single-bull vs. rookery distinction preserves a future E=9 entry.** This degree documents the single bull apex roar (E=8). ## Discoveries

1. **The Steller Sea Lion is the only marine mammal entry in the SPS engine where the acoustic event is generated primarily by anatomy rather than by behavioral intensity.** Orca (degree 311): complex social vocalization, behavioral. Cougar-Wolf (degree 313): territorial threat, behavioral. Elk bugle (degree 314): rut competition, behavioral. Steller Sea Lion: the 80-500 Hz roar is generated by the body's resonant properties at maximum body mass. The acoustic event is anatomical. This is a new calibration category.
2. **Cape Flattery / Carroll Island is the engine's most remote acoustic documentation site.** The westernmost point of the contiguous US. No road to the promontory (tribal land, Makah Nation). The haul-out sites require boat access or observation from the headland. The geographic remoteness of the documentation site is part of the Steller Sea Lion entry's character: this acoustic event happens at the edge of the continent, where the Pacific begins.
3. **The 80 Hz lower bound is significant: at 100m, the fundamental frequency produces enough pressure to be felt before it is heard.** The Steller Sea Lion bull apex roar produces infrasonic-adjacent frequencies (80 Hz) at sufficient amplitude that an observer at close range experiences the roar as a physical vibration before perceiving it as sound. This is not infrasound (below 20 Hz), but it approaches the body-vibration threshold. The E=8 calibration includes this felt-before-heard quality as a factor.
