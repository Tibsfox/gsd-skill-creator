Acoustic Profile — The Apex Bull Roar

Frequency: 100–1,500 Hz primary energy. Peak 200–800 Hz. Harmonics to 3,000+ Hz. The sub-bass content (100–400 Hz) is perceived as pressure at close range.

Amplitude: 110–120 dB at 10–20 meters. Dominant over rookery ambient at 100+ meters. Comparable to a moderately loud rock concert at close range.

Resonance anatomy: The massive chest and neck cavity of a 500+ kg bull amplifies specific harmonics, producing the deepest, most resonant roar in the family Otariidae. The largest resonance chamber = the deepest roar = the E=8 individual maximum.

Duration: 2–8 seconds per roar event. Hundreds of events per day during peak breeding season. The highest sustained individual acoustic output of any Pacific Northwest pinniped.

The Species

Eumetopias jubatus, Schreber, 1776. The largest member of Otariidae. Adult bulls 700–1,200 lbs, body length 9–11 feet. Extreme sexual dimorphism: bulls 2–3 times larger than cows. The characteristic mane of coarser fur around the neck and chest (the “Steller” of the common name) provides physical neck protection during combat. Range: Sea of Japan through the Gulf of Alaska south to California. Washington colonies: San Juan Islands haul-outs, Olympic Peninsula coast. Eastern stock population recovery (~40,000) is a Pacific Northwest marine mammal conservation success.

Polygynous breeding: dominant beachmaster bulls control prime rock and maintain harem access during the June-July peak. Beachmasters hold their position for 2–3 breeding seasons before being displaced by younger, larger competitors. The apex bull documented at degree 316 is at the peak of his physical and competitive development.
