## THE SOUND: PURE SUSTAINED TRILL -- 98th ACOUSTIC ELEMENT (species side)

### The Call

The snowy tree cricket's call is a **pure, sustained trill** [1, 2]:

**The mechanism:** Stridulation -- rubbing specialized wing structures together. The file-and-scraper mechanism of *Oecanthus fultoni* produces an unusually pure waveform. The wing morphology generates a **nearly sinusoidal tone** with minimal harmonic content -- rare among stridulating insects, which typically produce complex, harmonic-rich sounds [2].

**The acoustic character:** A sustained, crystalline trill at approximately **2-4 kHz**. The tone is clean, clear, and continuous -- no buzzing, no rasping, no percussive elements. One of the **purest tones produced by any animal**. Individual crickets produce a gentle, ringing tone. Multiple crickets create a **shimmering curtain of pure sound** that defines the late-summer night.

**Duration:** Each trill is sustained for several seconds. Calling continues for hours through the night during peak season.

### Why E=3

1. **PURE BUT GENTLE.** The tree cricket's call is remarkably clear but not loud. Individual calls are quieter than field crickets.
2. **SUSTAINED AUDIBILITY.** The pure tone carries well through still night air. Audible at moderate distances.
3. **LOWER THAN FIELD CRICKET CHORUS.** The field cricket chorus (87, E=4) is louder through sheer density and volume. The tree cricket is pure but gentle.
4. **HIGHER THAN STONEFLY.** The stonefly (94, E=2) is barely audible. The tree cricket is clearly audible.
5. **CRYSTALLINE CLARITY COMPENSATES FOR VOLUME.** The purity of the tone makes it perceptible even at low amplitude.

## ECOLOGY: DOLBEAR'S LAW AND THE THERMOMETER CRICKET

### Dolbear's Law

In 1897, physicist **Amos Dolbear** published the relationship between snowy tree cricket chirp rate and ambient temperature [3]:

**T(degrees F) = 40 + (number of chirps in 13 seconds)**

This relationship is linear and reliable because [1, 2, 3]:
- **Ectothermic metabolism:** The cricket's metabolic rate is directly proportional to ambient temperature.
- **Wing-rubbing speed:** Higher temperature = faster metabolism = faster wing-rubbing = faster chirp rate.
- **Linear correlation:** The relationship is approximately linear within the normal temperature range.
- **Verified biophysics:** This is not folklore. Multiple studies have confirmed the relationship. The cricket IS a biological thermometer.

### Shrub Habitat

**Habitat:** Shrubs, low trees, herbaceous plants, hedgerows [1, 2]. Tree crickets live and call from elevated positions on vegetation -- not from the ground like field crickets. The elevated calling position helps the pure tone propagate through the night air without ground-level obstruction.

Contrast with field cricket habitat: dry meadow (degree 87) vs shrub/low tree (degree 98). Different ecological niches within the same family.

### Acoustic Purity -- An Outlier Among Stridulators

The snowy tree cricket's wing morphology is unusual among Gryllidae [1, 2]:
- **File-and-scraper mechanism** produces a nearly sinusoidal waveform.
- **Minimal harmonic content** -- most stridulating insects produce complex, harmonic-rich sounds. The tree cricket is an acoustic outlier.
- **Consistent frequency** -- individual crickets maintain remarkably stable pitch (temperature-dependent but stable at a given temperature).
- **Synchronization** -- nearby tree crickets often synchronize their trills, creating phase-coherent acoustic fields.
