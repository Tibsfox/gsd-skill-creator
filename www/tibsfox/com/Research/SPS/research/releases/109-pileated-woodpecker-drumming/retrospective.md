## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The dual-entry approach is analytically sound.** The Pileated's two sound-production systems are genuinely independent phenomena. Documenting them separately allows proper analysis of each: the vocal system (syrinx, high-frequency, primal scream) and the percussive system (bill strikes, low-frequency, machine-gun). Verified.

2. **E=6 is defensible for the drumming.** The Pileated's drum is the loudest of any North American woodpecker. The resonant dead snag amplifies the sound to carry over 1 km through forest. The physical force (1,000-1,200 g deceleration per strike) is extraordinary. E=6 is the only appropriate level.

3. **The dead-snag-as-instrument concept is well-supported.** The literature documents the Pileated's selection of resonant drum trees and its repeated return to favored trees. The acoustic properties of the snag directly shape the drum's character. The concept is defensible.

4. **The Picidae streak documentation is accurate.** Three consecutive Picidae (107-108-109), each acoustically distinct. The streak tracks toward the Sciuridae record.

5. **Cross-references to SPS-108 (Pileated laughing), SPS-054 (Pileated original), SPS-107 (Acorn), SPS-021 (Flicker), and SPS-082 (Flying Squirrel) are all defensible.**
