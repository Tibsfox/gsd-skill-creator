## What Worked

### 1. Dolbear's Law Is a Perfect Detail
Temperature-dependent chirp rate is a well-documented, mathematically described, verifiable phenomenon. T(°F) = N/4 + 40. The cricket as a living thermometer. No other engine species has a mathematically described environmental relationship. Unique and verifiable.

### 2. The Treefrog-Cricket Chorus Pair Is Ecologically Real
Back-to-back chorus species (86-87). Both collective breeding sounds. Both E=4. But different habitats (marsh vs meadow) and different textures (wall-of-sound vs rhythmic). The parallel was not forced -- it emerged from the data.

### 3. Insecta Is a Genuine Landmark
After 86 degrees of vertebrates (birds, mammals, amphibian), an invertebrate enters. The class barrier expands. Four classes now. The observation is clean.

### 4. The Rhythmic Quality Distinguishes Cricket from Treefrog
The treefrog chorus (86) is a wall of sound -- no gaps, continuous. The cricket chorus retains its rhythmic pulsing quality even in large choruses. This distinction is audible and defensible.

### 5. Family-Level Entry Is Appropriate
The cricket chorus is genuinely a family-level phenomenon -- multiple species contribute. A single-species entry would be less accurate. The family-level approach is honest.
