## Discoveries

1. **The Caspian Tern's management story is specifically Pacific Northwest.** The species is globally common and globally stable. Its management controversy in the PNW arises from the particular combination of the Columbia River salmon recovery program's scale and the Caspian Tern's exceptional colony size at East Sand Island. This is not a bird conservation problem -- it is an ecological consequence of simultaneously protecting two species at their functional limits.
