## What Worked

1. **FIRST FALCONIDAE is the correct milestone to lead with.** The engine has been in Accipitridae since degree 221. Ten degrees of hawks, eagles, and ospreys. The family shift at 231 is the raptor arc's most significant structural event, and the Peregrine Falcon is the correct species to open Falconidae.
2. **The convergent evolution finding is the degree's most important scientific content.** Falcons are not closely related to hawks. They are more closely related to parrots and songbirds. The shared raptor morphology is convergent evolution -- two lineages, one aerodynamic solution. This is counterintuitive, robustly supported, and exactly the kind of finding the engine exists to document.
3. **The stoop speed (389 km/h, 242 mph) is one of the engine's most concrete superlatives.** Most superlatives in natural history involve ranges or estimates. Ken Franklin's 2005 instrumented measurement of 389 km/h is a specific, documented, measured number. The engine has an exact figure for the fastest self-powered movement in the animal kingdom.
4. **The tomial tooth is the anatomical key.** The upper mandible notch that distinguishes Falconidae from Accipitridae is both a structural distinction and an ecological adaptation (prey dispatch by spinal cord severance). Form following function in the most literal sense.
5. **The urban reintroduction discovery is one of the recovery story's most interesting chapters.** The Peregrine Fund expected cliff-site reintroductions to succeed better than urban reintroductions. The opposite was partially true. The city provided better food, better predator protection, and less disturbance. Urban ecology producing better raptor habitat than natural cliff sites is a genuinely surprising finding.
6. **Seattle urban Peregrines are documented.** The degree places the species in the Seattle context explicitly -- not just "the PNW" but the downtown Seattle nest sites. This is local specificity at the engine's geographic home.

## Discoveries

1. **The two PNW subspecies have meaningfully different DDT impact histories.** *F. p. pealei* (Peale's, seabird diet) suffered less DDT impact than *F. p. anatum* (songbird diet) because seabirds accumulated less DDT than pesticide-affected songbirds. The prey chain determines the toxin accumulation. This is a precise ecological finding that goes beyond the general DDT story.
2. **The nictitating membrane and nostril tubercles are specific adaptations to the stoop.** These are not general falcon features -- they are mechanical solutions to the specific problems created by traveling at 240+ mph: wind blast to the eye, and air pressure to the lungs. The stoop created selection pressure for specific anatomical responses.
3. **The molecular phylogeny finding (Hackett et al. 2008) is one of the most surprising results in recent avian systematics.** Before molecular analysis, falcons were classified with Accipitriformes based on morphology. Molecular data overturned this entirely. The engine documents a case where molecular data contradicted a century of morphological classification.
