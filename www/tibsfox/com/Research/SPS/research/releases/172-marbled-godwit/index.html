FIFTH SCOLOPACIDAE — bill curves UP after four degrees of down-curved bills. The counter-curve. Loud, insistent “godwit! godwit!” repeated call. Cinnamon plumage. Bicolored upturned bill. E=5. v1.49.312.

The Counter-Curve — Bill Direction Reverses

After four degrees of down-curved bills (168–171), the Marbled Godwit’s bill curves UP. 10–13 cm, slightly upturned, bicolored (pink-orange base, dark tip). Adapted for lateral sweeping through soft mud and shallow water. The curlew probes DOWN; the godwit sweeps UP. Opposite curves. Different prey zones. Same tidal flat.

Cinnamon Shorebird — Warm-Toned Giant

Warm cinnamon-buff plumage. Intricate marbled pattern on back and wings. Bright cinnamon underwings in flight (shared with Long-billed Curlew). Large — 42–48 cm. Breeds on Great Plains. Regular on PNW tidal flats fall through spring.
