Hundreds of birds calling simultaneously: mewing contact calls, alarm waves, territorial long calls, chick begging from late June through August. ## What Worked

1. **FIRST LARIDAE is a genuine taxonomic milestone.** The gull and tern family enters the SPS engine for the first time. Four consecutive degrees (289-292) document the family across four ecological contexts. Glaucous-winged Gull is the correct founding species: the most common, most abundant, most ecologically central gull in the Pacific Northwest.
2. **The emergent colonial soundscape as a new acoustic category is the key insight.** Previous SPS entries have been individual-scale or small-group sounds. The colony soundscape is different: it does not belong to any single bird. Hundreds of simultaneous callers produce a continuous, layered, multi-pitch acoustic environment that is not reducible to any individual's call. This is genuinely new in the engine.
3. **The hybrid zone with Western Gull is important natural history.** Larus glaucescens x Larus occidentalis hybrids are common throughout Puget Sound. One of the most studied gull hybrid zones in North America. This establishes the species relationship that will persist into SPS 291 (Western Gull colony).
4. **E=6 calibration is correct.** The colony chorus is sustained and multi-layered, but it is an ambient ecological background -- not an explosive single event. The E=6 anchor at the opening of the Laridae arc is right. Subsequent entries (290-292) step up to E=7.

## Discoveries

1. **The alarm call propagation is the colony's most dramatic acoustic event.** One bird's alarm triggers adjacent birds in seconds. A wave of alarm calling sweeping a 500-pair colony can cover the entire site in under a minute. The colony's collective predator detection system is literally acoustic: the alarm sound is the mechanism, not just the symptom.
2. **Rooftop nesting increase since mid-20th century is an unacknowledged urban wildlife story.** Glaucous-winged Gulls have colonized flat commercial rooftops throughout coastal PNW cities as traditional rocky island habitat has been disturbed. The colony sound now occurs above urban streets, not just on remote island rocks.
3. **The four-year plumage maturation means that any colony contains birds in four distinct plumage states simultaneously.** A colony is not a group of identical adults. First-winter, second-winter, third-winter, and adult birds all nest at the same site. The acoustic diversity mirrors the plumage diversity.
