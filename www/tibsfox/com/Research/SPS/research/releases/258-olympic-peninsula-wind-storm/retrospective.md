## Discoveries

1. **The Hoh Rainforest 140-170 inch precipitation is the clearest evidence of Olympic Mountain weather intensity.** The same frontal systems that strip the ridges of branches deposit this enormous precipitation on the windward slope. The mountain's weather extremity is expressed both as precipitation (Hoh) and as wind (ridge). Both records come from the same source.
2. **Sitka spruce shallow roots explain the blowdown pattern.** Waterlogged soil limits root depth; dense competition limits lateral root spread. The result is a large, heavy, shallow-rooted tree that blows over rather than breaks. This is why Olympic blowdowns are so large-scale: not scattered broken snags but entire stands of uprooted trees lying in the same direction.
