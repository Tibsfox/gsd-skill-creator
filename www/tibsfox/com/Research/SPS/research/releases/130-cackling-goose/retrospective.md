### Cackling Goose at degree 130 extends the Anatidae record to thirteen consecutive entries and completes the goose comparison pair (129-130). The higher-pitched cackling call -- rapid "cack-cack-cack" at 400-2,000 Hz, thinner and squeakier than Canada Goose -- registers E=4 through flock amplification. Split from Canada Goose in 2004 by the AOU. Purely migratory -- Arctic tundra breeder with no PNW resident populations. The overlooked counterpart to North America's most ubiquitous waterfowl.

## Structural Event 3: The 2004 Species Split

- **Situation:** One of the most significant recent taxonomic decisions in North American ornithology. Four smallest subspecies elevated to *Branta hutchinsii*. Based on molecular genetics, bimodal size distribution, and different breeding ecology.
- **Root Cause:** Molecular studies revealed deep genetic divergence between the large and small forms. The split was inevitable once the genetic data was available.
- **Recommendation:** Document the split as a landmark taxonomic event. Note its relevance to the comparison pair structure.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- 2004 split documented. Taxonomic landmark.
