## The Two Hundred Thirty-Sixth Degree: The Keystone

### FIRST FISH IN THE ENGINE. FIRST SALMONIDAE. The Pacific Salmon is the PNW's keystone species -- the ecological center around which bears, eagles, orcas, and forests organize. Five species in Washington (Chinook, Coho, Sockeye, Pink, Chum). The spawning sound is not a vocalization but a physical soundscape: tail-slapping gravel, body displacement, fish fighting, jumping at falls. E=5. The lifecycle that moves marine nutrients into the forest. The species that connects the ocean to the trees.

## What Worked

1. **The ecological web documentation is the entry's strongest element.** The cross-references to Bald Eagle (221-222), Osprey (224), Grizzly Bear (217-218), and the implied Orca connection create the retroactive anchor that degree 236 provides for nearly everything that came before in the raptor and predator arc.
2. **The five-species structure works well.** Covering all five Washington species as distinct entities (rather than treating Pacific Salmon as a single entry) preserves the genuine biological and conservation distinctions. Each species has different ecology, timing, conservation status, and cultural significance.
3. **The spawning sound justification is important.** The argument that physical sounds (tail-slap, body displacement) belong in SPS alongside vocalizations is philosophically essential for the engine's expansion into fish. The "Sound of Puget Sound" includes spawning runs.
4. **The marine nutrient cycle is the entry's most surprising science.** Trees near salmon streams growing faster because of marine-derived nitrogen in fish carcasses carried by bears and eagles is a genuine ecological discovery (documented in peer-reviewed literature) that is under-known outside ecology circles.

## Discoveries

1. **The 6PPD-quinone Coho story (2020) is recent and consequential.** The identification of a tire rubber chemical as the cause of urban Coho pre-spawn mortality is a major conservation finding with significant implications. Washington State has already passed legislation. The research continues.
2. **The Boldt Decision (1974) is the most important legal event in the engine's documented geographic history.** The ruling that tribal treaties guarantee 50% of harvestable salmon has shaped every salmon management decision in Washington State for fifty years. It belongs in the engine's legal-historical record.
3. **The Elwha dam removal produced measurable results within a decade.** Salmon returning above former dam sites, habitat recovering, sediment redistribution normalizing. This is conservation success at the landscape scale.
