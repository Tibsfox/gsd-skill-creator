## What Worked

1. **The size gradient arc (Peregrine to Prairie Falcon to Merlin) is structurally elegant.** Large to medium to small within three degrees. The Merlin correctly closes the three-degree sequence before the Kestrel provides the fourth and final entry.
2. **The assertiveness paradox is the key insight.** A bird that weighs 125-300 grams attacking crows and ravens many times its size is a behavioral fact that the ki-ki-ki chatter documents vocally. Size and ferocity are not correlated in this family.
3. **The black Merlin (*suckleyi*) adds PNW-specific depth.** This nearly-black resident coastal subspecies is under-documented relative to the taiga winter visitor. Its year-round presence and dark coloration are specific to the wet Pacific coast.

## Discoveries

1. **The rapid-fire tempo is distinctive.** The Merlin's ki-ki-ki is faster than the Peregrine's kak-kak-kak and more staccato than the Kestrel's killy. The 10-15 notes per second in alarm mode is genuinely machine-gun-like. The sound has mechanical urgency.
2. **Urban adaptation trend is notable.** Merlins are increasingly nesting in Canadian and northern US cities, hunting House Sparrows. The urban Merlin is a documented 21st-century behavioral shift.
