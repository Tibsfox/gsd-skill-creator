## Structural Event 2: Ecotype Contrast (Southern Resident vs. Bigg's)

- **Situation:** Southern Residents (degrees 270-271): salmon-eating, continuously vocal, endangered. Bigg's (degree 272): mammal-eating, acoustically silent during hunting, not endangered. Same genus. Same waters. No interbreeding for 100,000+ years.
- **Root Cause:** The ecotype contrast is one of the most dramatic examples of cultural divergence in animal biology. Diet and acoustic strategy are both culturally transmitted (calves learn from mothers) and have maintained the populations as behaviorally distinct without significant genetic exchange. The contrast is the engine's most striking within-genus ecological divergence.
- **Recommendation:** Document the contrast table. State the cultural transmission mechanism. Note that the divergence is maintained by cultural inheritance, not by geographic separation.
