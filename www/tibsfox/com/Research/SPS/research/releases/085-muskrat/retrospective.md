## What Worked

### 1. The Quiet Plop Is Genuinely the Muskrat's Signature Sound
The plop -- the sound of the animal entering water -- is the primary detection sound. You hear muskrats before you see them. Widely described in field guides and natural history literature. Verifiable.

### 2. First Marsh Species Is a Genuine Habitat Milestone
After the alpine/forest era (mountain goat, marmots, squirrels, mice, woodrats), the engine returns to aquatic habitat. The marsh is a new habitat type.

### 3. Cricetidae Habitat Diversity Is Remarkable
Three consecutive Cricetidae in three radically different habitats: varied (83), rocky outcrop (84), marsh (85). This family diversity is itself an observation -- Cricetidae are ecological generalists.

### 4. Lodge Builder Parallel with Beaver (61) Is Sound
Both semi-aquatic rodents building structures in water. Different materials (cattails vs. sticks), different scales, same principle. The parallel is genuine.

### 5. NOT a Rat Is Taxonomically Correct
*Ondatra zibethicus* is Cricetidae, not Muridae. The common name "muskrat" is misleading. This is established taxonomy.

### 6. E=3 Calibration Is Accurate
The plop is clearly audible at moderate range in quiet marsh conditions. Not loud (not E=4) but distinctive and recognizable. E=3 is appropriate.
