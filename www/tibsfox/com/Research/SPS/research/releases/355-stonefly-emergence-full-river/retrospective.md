## Discoveries

1. **The Skwala emergence as seasonal signal is important context.** The smaller Skwala species emerge first, before the major Pteronarcys and Hesperoperla emergences. Their appearance is the river's announcement that spring has begun for the aquatic community. The emergence season is not a single event but a progression: Skwala first, then the larger species following in sequence over weeks. The "full river covered" event is the aggregate of this progression at its peak.
2. **The water quality indicator significance is the most important conservation observation.** A major full-river stonefly emergence at E=10 scale can only occur in a river system with intact ecological function. The emergence is self-measuring: its scale announces the river's health. In damaged systems, stonefly populations are the first major invertebrate group to decline. Documenting the full-river emergence at E=10 is implicitly documenting a healthy river.
