THE CASCADIA SUBDUCTION ZONE — THE REALLY BIG ONE

The Juan de Fuca plate drives beneath North America at 40 mm/year. The locked zone accumulates strain. When it slips: a megathrust earthquake, M8.0-9.2. The most recent full-margin rupture: January 26, 1700. A tsunami crossed the Pacific and struck Japan, documented in historical records. Tree-ring analysis of drowned coastal forests confirms the date. 41 CSZ earthquakes in 10,000 years. Average recurrence: 200-500 years. The current gap: 326 years. The question is when, not if. The P-wave rumble at degree 263 is the first acoustic signal of the event.

The P-wave — The Sound That Arrives First

THE ANNOUNCEMENT, NOT THE EVENT

A P-wave is a compressional wave traveling through rock at 5-7 km/second (>10,000 mph). It arrives before the S-waves and surface waves that produce damaging shaking. When it reaches the surface, a portion of its energy converts to acoustic energy: a low, rolling, directional rumble, often felt as a brief "boom" below the feet before the shaking begins. Duration: seconds to 10 seconds at moderate distance. Frequency: primarily below 20 Hz, some energy to 100 Hz. E=5: the announcement of what is coming. The ShakeAlert early warning system detects the P-wave arrival, computes the earthquake parameters, and broadcasts alerts in the seconds before damaging waves arrive. The P-wave rumble and the early warning system are the same physical phenomenon.

The Fault Systems Below

Cascadia Subduction Zone

700 miles long. Juan de Fuca plate subducting at 40 mm/yr. Locked zone accumulates strain for centuries. Megathrust events M8-9.2. Last rupture 1700. The dominant hazard.

Seattle Fault Zone

Runs east-west through the Seattle metro area. Last major rupture ~1,100 years ago (estimated M7.0-7.5). Produced a Puget Sound tsunami. Runs under present-day Seattle neighborhoods.

Intraslab Earthquakes

Within the subducting Juan de Fuca slab, 30-70 km depth. Moderate-to-significant events (M4-6.8). The 2001 Nisqually earthquake (M6.8, 52 km depth) was intraslab. Wide felt area, moderate intensity.

South Whidbey Island Fault

Through Whidbey Island into eastern Puget Sound. Less characterized than Seattle Fault. Potential for significant earthquakes. Part of the complex Puget Lowland tectonic system.

The 2001 Nisqually Earthquake — The Modern Record

"Many people in the region reported a brief rumble or vibration that preceded the main Nisqually shaking by 5-10 seconds. This is consistent with the physics: at ~50 km distance from Olympia, the P-S wave differential was approximately 6-7 seconds. The rumble was the announcement; the shaking arrived seconds later."

ShakeAlert — The P-wave Window

ShakeAlert (USGS, deployed across WA/OR/CA) detects P-wave arrivals, computes location and magnitude in near-real-time, and broadcasts alerts before damaging S-waves and surface waves arrive. For a CSZ full-margin rupture, Seattle may receive 30-90 seconds of warning. The P-wave rumble that degree 263 documents is the physical trigger of the entire ShakeAlert system. The acoustic rumble heard by humans and the seismic network's P-wave detection are the same physical event processed by different sensors.
