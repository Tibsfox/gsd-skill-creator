Historical Timeline

From unincorporated timber country to a light rail terminus in 65 years.

1900s Interurban electric railway runs through the area, connecting Seattle and Everett
1937 Alderwood Manor established as a planned logging-to-farming community
1959 Incorporated as the City of Lynnwood — rapid suburban growth follows I-5
1979 Alderwood Mall opens, becoming a regional retail anchor
2005 Lynnwood Convention Center opens on the city center campus
2009 Lynnwood Transit Center becomes Community Transit's busiest hub
2024 Link light rail opens to Lynnwood City Center — 1 Line and 2 Line terminus

Parks & Green Spaces

Urban parks, lake boardwalks, and athletic fields in a densifying suburban core.

Lynnwood City Park

communityplayground

A large municipal park with playground, sports fields, picnic shelters, and walking paths. Hosts community events and summer programs. The park anchors a residential neighborhood north of the city center with mature trees and open lawn areas.

Scriber Lake Park

boardwalkwetland

A boardwalk trail loops around Scriber Lake through wetland habitat with interpretive signs on local ecology. The elevated walkway passes through marshy areas with cattails, red-winged blackbirds, and seasonal waterfowl. A peaceful retreat in the center of the city.

Access: 52nd Ave W & 198th St SW

Meadowdale Playfields

sportsfields

Multi-field athletic complex with baseball diamonds, soccer fields, and a covered play area. Hosts youth sports leagues and community athletics. Adjacent to the Meadowdale neighborhood in southwest Lynnwood.

Daleway Park

neighborhoodplayground

A neighborhood green space with playground equipment, picnic areas, and open lawn. Provides local recreation access for the residential communities in north-central Lynnwood.

Sprague's Pond

pondnatural area

A small natural area centered on a retention pond surrounded by native plantings. Walking paths circle the pond, providing a short nature loop in the eastern part of the city. Storm water management meets urban green space.

Lakes & Waterways

Scriber Lake

urban lakeboardwalk

A small urban lake in central Lynnwood, notable for its boardwalk trail and wetland ecology. The lake and surrounding marsh support frogs, dragonflies, and migratory birds. Scriber Lake Park provides the primary public access.

Martha Lake

lakeresidential

A residential lake in the south Lynnwood / Martha Lake area. Primarily surrounded by private homes with limited public access. The Martha Lake neighborhood is one of Lynnwood's most established residential areas.

Lake Serene (nearby)

lake

A small lake near Lynnwood's eastern boundary, surrounded by residential development. The Lake Serene community straddles the Lynnwood-Mountlake Terrace border area.

Notable Landmarks

Lynnwood Convention Center

eventsconference

A 40,000-square-foot event facility hosting conferences, trade shows, and community gatherings. Located on the city center campus near the transit center, making it accessible by light rail from across the region.

Alderwood Mall Area

retaildining

One of the largest shopping centers in the Pacific Northwest, anchored by major retailers and surrounded by restaurants and entertainment. The Alderwood area is Lynnwood's primary commercial district, generating significant retail tax revenue for the city.

Getting Here from Mukilteo

Lynnwood Transit Center is THE transfer point from Snohomish County to the light rail network.

Major Multimodal Hub

Lynnwood City Center Station

The southern gateway for Snohomish County transit. Opened August 30, 2024, this station is the northern terminus for both the Link 1 Line and 2 Line. A brand-new 1,670-stall parking garage serves commuters. Community Transit buses, Sound Transit Express buses, and local routes all converge here. From Lynnwood City Center, you can reach UW, Capitol Hill, downtown Seattle, Sea-Tac Airport, Bellevue, and Redmond — all on one train, no transfers.

From Mukilteo to Lynnwood

Route 117: Mukilteo Ferry → Harbour Pointe → Lynnwood City Center Station

Direct bus service, approximately 32 minutes. Runs 7 days a week (reduced weekend frequency). This is the primary connection from Mukilteo to the light rail network.

Link Light Rail (from Lynnwood City Center Station)

1 Line: Lynnwood → Mountlake Terrace → Shoreline → Northgate → U District → Capitol Hill → Westlake → Pioneer Square → SODO → Rainier Beach → Sea-Tac Airport → Angle Lake

2 Line: Lynnwood → U District → Capitol Hill → Judkins Park → Mercer Island → Bellevue → Spring District → Overlake → Redmond Technology Station

Trains run every 8–10 minutes during peak, every 15 minutes off-peak, 7 days a week.
