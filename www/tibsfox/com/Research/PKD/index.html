Philip K. Dick

Reality · Identity · Gnosis · Androids · The Black Iron Prison
Overview Life & Career Philosophy VALIS & Gnosis Legacy Relevance Mission Pack
Reality is that which, when you stop believing in it, doesn't go away. Philip K. Dick wrote forty-five novels and roughly one hundred twenty-one short stories asking what that means — and spent his final eight years producing a million-word theological journal after experiencing what he called a divine communication. He was a Gnostic who distrusted his own gnosis, a philosopher who worked in the trashiest genre available to him, and through it produced some of the most searching explorations of consciousness in American literature. The trap doors in the floor of everyday experience are not bugs but features.

Research Modules

M1: Life and Literary Career

Biography Track A · 1928–1982

From the twin sister's death through Berkeley, the prolific pulp period, Hugo-winning breakthrough, drug-shadowed masterworks, the 1971 break-in, Orange County exile, and posthumous canonization. All 45 novels, 121 short stories, and the arc from genre obscurity to Library of America.

M2: Philosophical Architecture

Philosophy Track A · Ontological uncertainty

Five core themes mapped to key works: nature of reality, human vs. machine, identity and self, political control, entropy and negentropy. Philosophical lineage from pre-Socratics through Kant and Jung to Baudrillard. Dick's fiction as philosophical argument wearing narrative clothing.

M3: The 2-3-74 Experience & the Exegesis

Theology Track B · Mystical experience

The pink beam, anamnesis, VALIS, the Black Iron Prison, and the million-word Exegesis. Valentinian Gnosticism, Neoplatonism, Kabbalah. Multi-perspectival analysis: religious, psychological, literary. The VALIS trilogy as fictionalized theology. "The Empire never ended."

M4: Cinematic and Cultural Legacy

Legacy Track B · Adaptation & reception

Blade Runner to Electric Dreams: every major film and television adaptation. What Hollywood preserves and what it strips away. The LOA volumes, SF Hall of Fame, the PKD Award, "Dickian" as critical adjective. $1B+ box office from an author who died in poverty.

M5: Contemporary Relevance

Relevance Synthesis · Dick in 2026

LLMs and the android question. Deepfakes and manufactured reality. Algorithmic surveillance and Flow My Tears' checkpoint state. Simulation theory from Dick to Bostrom. The PKD android at CES. "Fake realities will create fake humans" as diagnostic for the age of synthetic media.
