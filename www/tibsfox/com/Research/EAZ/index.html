East Asian Zodiac Variations

Twelve Animals · Five Elements · Four Nations · Two Millennia of Cultural Drift
Overview Origins Substitutions Myths Five Elements Living Traditions Mission Pack
The Chinese zodiac is not one system. It is a root system — a cosmological framework originating in Han Dynasty China that spread across East Asia over two millennia and took root differently in every soil it touched. Japan received it via Korean scholars and swapped the wild boar for the domestic pig. Vietnam received it under Han military occupation and traded the rabbit for the cat — an animal that guarded rice paddies from the rats that threatened the harvest. The spaces between these national variations are the story.

Research Modules

M1: Origins and Transmission

Historical Track A · Chronological spine

The ganzhi system from Shang oracle bones through Han formalization. Transmission routes and dates for Korea, Japan, and Vietnam. The Kitora Tomb evidence. Why the Earthly Branches permitted animal substitution.

M2: Animal Substitutions

Cultural Track B · Drift atlas

Vietnam’s Cat and Buffalo, Japan’s Boar, the Sheep-Goat ambiguity. Broader survey: Cambodia’s Naga, Malaysia’s Mousedeer, Turkey’s Leopard. Nine-nation master substitution table with cultural logic.

M3: Origin Myth Divergence

Mythology Track A · Narrative layer

The Great Race told four ways. China: the Cat overslept. Vietnam: the Cat swam. Japan: the Boar overshot. Korea: swimming race. The Rat as universal trickster. Myth as calendrical ratification.

M4: Five Elements & Calendrical Adaptation

Elements Track B · Wuxing transmission

Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water — the elemental layer that transforms twelve animals into sixty combinations. Japan’s 1873 Gregorian shift. Onmyodo. Buddhist deity pairings. Korean saju Four Pillars.

M5: Living Traditions Today

Contemporary Track B · Practice layer

Korean tti social grammar and gunghap compatibility. Japanese nengajo card tradition and kanreki 60th birthday. Vietnamese Tet decorations and zodiac birth booms. PNW diaspora connections.
