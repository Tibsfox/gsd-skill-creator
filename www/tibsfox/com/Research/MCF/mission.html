The Through-Line

Kubernetes was designed to be remarkable at one thing: orchestrating containers inside a single administrative domain. But the FoxCompute architecture is not a single administrative domain. It is a federation of islands — BC coast nodes, valley compute clusters, edge inference boxes, a future spine stretching south — each with different ownership, different connectivity, different regulatory context, different thermal and power profiles. The right model is what the internet itself figured out decades ago: peers who speak a common protocol, maintain their own sovereignty, and cooperate on terms they negotiate. The federation layer is the chip bus. The service mesh is the DMA channel. The result, like the Amiga, is something that punches far above its weight.
