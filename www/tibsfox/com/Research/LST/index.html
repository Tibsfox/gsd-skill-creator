Lake Stevens — The Lake

The largest natural lake in Snohomish County, 1,013 acres, 150 feet deep.

Lake Stevens is a 1,013-acre natural lake with a maximum depth of 150 feet, formed by glacial action during the last ice age. The lake supports populations of largemouth bass, smallmouth bass, yellow perch, rainbow trout, kokanee salmon, crappie, catfish, bluegill, rock bass, and pumpkinseed sunfish — ten species in total. Public swimming beaches, boat launches, and waterfront parks ring the shoreline. The lake is the heart of the community and the reason the city exists.

Fishing

10 speciesyear-round

Largemouth bass, smallmouth bass, yellow perch, rainbow trout, kokanee salmon, crappie, channel catfish, bluegill, rock bass, and pumpkinseed sunfish. Bass tournaments run through summer. Trout stocked by WDFW. Best bass fishing May through September.

Swimming & Paddling

beachkayak

Public swimming beaches with lifeguards in summer. Kayak, canoe, and paddleboard access at multiple launch points around the lake. The warm shallow areas near the south shore are popular for families. Wakeboarding and water skiing on the main body.

Parks & Green Spaces

Lake Stevens City Park

waterfrontswim beach

The primary public park on the lake with a swimming beach, playground, picnic shelters, sports courts, and boat launch. Summer events and community gatherings center here. The park provides the best public access to the lake's western shore.

Access: North Lakeshore Dr

North Cove Park

waterfrontboat launch

A waterfront park on the north end of the lake with a public boat launch, fishing dock, and picnic area. Popular with anglers targeting bass in the north cove's shallow waters and weed beds.

Lundeen Park

waterfrontneighborhood

A lakefront neighborhood park with dock access, lawn areas, and views across the lake. Provides a quieter alternative to the main city park for swimming and picnicking.

Catherine Creek Park

trailsnatural area

A nature park along Catherine Creek with walking trails through wetland and forest. The creek is a tributary of the Pilchuck River and supports salmon habitat restoration efforts by the city and Snohomish County.

Frontier Heights Park

playgroundsports

A neighborhood park with playground, basketball court, and open fields in the eastern part of the city. Serves the growing residential communities on the upland plateau above the lake.

Getting Here from Mukilteo

Lake Stevens is 15 miles northeast of Mukilteo, inland from the coast. Transit requires a transfer through Everett.

By Bus (via Everett Station)

Mukilteo → Everett Station: Community Transit Route 117 to Lynnwood, transfer to Swift Blue or local routes north, OR Sounder N Line north one stop to Everett.

Everett Station → Lake Stevens: Community Transit Route 201/202 serves the Lake Stevens corridor from Everett Station.

Total transit time: approximately 45–60 minutes with transfer. Weekend service is limited; weekday service runs hourly on the Lake Stevens routes.

By Car

Mukilteo Speedway east to SR-527, then north on SR-9 to Lake Stevens. Approximately 20–25 minutes depending on traffic. The drive follows the eastern edge of the Everett urban area into the foothills transition zone.
