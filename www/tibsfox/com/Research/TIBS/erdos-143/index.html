Erdős #143 — Transfer-of-Method Analysis

Status: OPEN ($500 prize) — candidate for AI-assisted reduction following the #1196 breakthrough. Field: Primitive sets (arithmetic number theory). Last reviewed: 2026-04-23. Related: Erdős #1196 — AI-Assisted Proof.

Claim

The techniques that resolved #1196 (Erdős–Sárközy–Szemerédi primitive-sum conjecture, solved April 13, 2026 by GPT-5.4 Pro in a single ~80-minute reasoning trace prompted by Liam “Eric” Price) may transfer to the adjacent primitive-sets problem #143 with modest adaptation. This page is the transfer-of-method analysis: what exactly carried the #1196 trace, which of those ingredients are independent of the specific primitive-sum target, and what a directed #143 attempt would look like.

Evidence

  1. Same problem family. Both #143 and #1196 concern primitive sets (subsets of \(\mathbb{N}\) where no element divides another). #1196 is the sum-limit question; #143 is a structurally adjacent density question. See ERDOS-TRACKER.md entry for #143 (“Related to #220 (proved) and Lichtman’s 2022 work”). The primitive-set universe itself — the restriction that no element divides another — is common to both; what changes is the functional of the set one bounds.

  2. Reasoning-trace method transfer. #1196 fell to a single long-reasoning session that (a) built the extremal construction, (b) verified Lichtman’s 2023 bound \(e^{\gamma}\pi/4 \approx 1.399\) was tight by re-deriving the analysis from first principles, and (c) closed the gap via a primes-in-arithmetic-progressions argument that had apparently not been attempted in ninety years of work on the problem. The bone structure — a long coherent chain of extremal-function manipulation that terminates in a concrete combinatorial argument — is the kind of method that, once found, generalizes across a problem family.

  3. Post-#1196 Erdős-tracker AI-attempt status for #143: none directly, with an adjacent note recording #1196’s solved status (see ERDOS-TRACKER.md entry for #143, line 222). The attempt surface is open; as of 2026-04-22, no lab has publicly tried.

  4. Structural adjacency. #143 sits immediately below #138 and #146 in the same TIER-2 block of the tracker. #138 has a partial AI attempt (DeepMind prover agent + Sothanaphan + GPT-5.4 Thinking, 10 Apr 2026) also concerning an additive-combinatorics question. The density of recent AI activity in this neighborhood is itself evidence that the primitive-set family is currently tractable to long-reasoning LLMs.

Proposed Transfer

A directed prompt modeled on the #1196 prompt, adapted for the #143 density question:

  1. Load the #1196 reasoning trace as one-shot context.
  2. Substitute the #143 target quantity (primitive-set density bound instead of sum bound).
  3. Ask the model to re-apply the extremal construction + tight-bound strategy — start from the extremal family \(A_k\) of integers with exactly \(k\) distinct prime factors, then optimize over \(k\) for the #143 target.
  4. Verify the chain against Lichtman 2022 (which is closest to #143’s native literature).
  5. If the chain breaks at step 3, widen the prompt to include Gorodetsky, Lichtman, Wong 2024 refinement of the lower-order correction \(1 - (c + o(1))\,k^2 2^{-k}\) with \(c \approx 0.0656\).

Expected outcome: not a full proof on first pass, but a narrowed candidate attack. The #1196 trace itself took about eighty minutes of reasoning; the #143 analog should be budgeted at \(\geq\) two hours, with a human spot-check at each inflection point where the chain invokes a non-obvious number-theoretic identity.

Risk Factors

  • Less constructive target. #143’s statement is less constructive than #1196’s (density vs. extremal value). Direct method transfer may fail where the #1196 reasoning relied on a concrete extremal structure to anchor its extremal-function work. In that case the transfer is a partial transfer — the combinatorial reductions carry but the terminal analytic step has to be redone.
  • Bounty calibration. There is no bounty pool for incremental #143 progress (the $500 is for the resolution, not incremental work). Effort against #143 is speculative in a way that #1196 work was not, because #1196 was already a recognised flagship open problem with public bounty history.
  • Literature lag. Jared Duker Lichtman (prior-best holder on the primitive-sum family) has not published on #143 specifically since 2022; a recent paper pipeline is possible but unconfirmed as of 2026-04-22. A #143 result could collide with an unpublished Lichtman paper, though this is already the standard risk for any frontier primitive-set work.
  • Survivorship bias in the wiki. The teorth/erdosproblems wiki warns explicitly that only successful AI runs are reported. The base rate of AI attempts on arithmetic-combinatorial open problems is unknown. A negative result on #143 would not be published on the wiki at all, so prior-negative evidence is absent.
