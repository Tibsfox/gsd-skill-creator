Mission Pack

The original research brief and mission package that initiated the SANS Institute research project. A comprehensive three-stage TeX document — Vision, Research Reference, Mission Package — covering the full pipeline from institutional analysis to structured execution.

Source Documents

The mission pack comprises a multi-hundred-line TeX mission document covering the complete SANS Institute research scope: organizational history from 1989 founding through Alan Paller’s legacy, the GIAC certification pipeline across six practice areas, the global conference and summit model, community programs including the Holiday Hack Challenge and Internet Storm Center, workforce context grounded in ISC2 2025 data, and career outcome mapping aligned to the NIST NICE framework.

Research Scope

  • Organizational Foundation: History from 1989 founding, non-profit/for-profit structure, Alan Paller’s vision and legacy, practitioner-instructor model, relationship to CIS, NIST, and standards bodies.
  • Training & Certification: Six practice areas (Cyber Defense, Offensive Operations, DFIR, Cloud Security, ICS, Leadership), GIAC certification tiers, course numbering conventions, pricing structure, three delivery modes.
  • Conferences & Summits: Flagship events (Orlando, San Diego, London, Amsterdam), specialized summits (ICS, AI, Leadership, DFIR, Security Awareness), event anatomy, NetWars tournaments.
  • Community Programs: Holiday Hack Challenge (21+ years, 15,000+ annual participants), NetWars cyber ranges, Internet Storm Center, SANS Reading Room, Work-Study program, free webcasts and newsletters.
  • Workforce Context: ISC2 2025 Workforce Study (4.8M gap, skills > headcount shift), GIAC-certified salary benchmarks, AI impact on workforce, NIST NICE framework alignment, career outcome data.

Key Connections

SANS Institute connects directly to SYS (systems administration and security operations), SHE (network security for IoT/smart home), CMH (network infrastructure security), BRC (trust systems — GIAC as trust certification), SMF (knowledge transfer — preserving operational expertise), WAL (Rosetta Stone — practitioner-first principle across domains), BCM (professional certification paths), and TSL (skill quality and validation).
