The Through-Line

Every system that moves data from the world to a human mind passes through at least three layers: a representation layer that gives data shape, a linking layer that connects representations into navigable networks, and a display layer that collapses time into a comprehensible now. The Amiga Principle is alive in every layer of this stack.

“The meaning lives between the nodes — in the link, the frame, the delta, the graph edge, and the rendered pixel.”
