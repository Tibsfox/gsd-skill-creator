# Origins & Format -- Almost Live! Founding 1984-1993

## The Pilot (September 23, 1984)

The pilot episode of Almost Live! (initially called "Take 5") aired September 23, 1984, on KING-TV, Seattle's NBC affiliate. The show was created by KING VP of Programming Bob Jones. The title "Almost Live!" was coined by original head writer Jim Sharp, who described it as "subtle, really easy, humorous if you think about it."

## The Letterman Model

The show was initially patterned after Late Night with David Letterman at the suggestion of director Steve Wilson. Ross Shafer, who had been running a Puyallup stereo store and pet shop while doing stand-up comedy, was selected as host. The early format featured local celebrity guests, a live band, and video pieces.

## Early Success and the Louie Louie Campaign

Almost Live! became popular quickly, winning nearly 40 Northwest Emmy Awards in its first seasons. KING-TV expanded it to one hour and aired it twice a week. Shafer's most famous publicity stunt was a campaign to make "Louie Louie" the Washington State song, drawing an estimated 5,000 people to the State Capitol on April 12, 1985.

## The Keister Transition (1988)

When Shafer left in 1988 to host the Fox Network's The Late Show, John Keister assumed the host role. Keister -- a Franklin High School alumnus who had covered music for The Rocket and hosted KING's music video program REV -- brought a fundamentally different comedy sensibility.

Keister's first season attempted to maintain the talk show format, with results he later described as "falling flat." The guest interviews and live band segments were gradually dropped.

## The Sketch Comedy Transformation (1990)

The pivotal transformation came in April 1990, when the show moved to Saturday 11:30 PM -- a slot requiring special permission from NBC, since it delayed Saturday Night Live by half an hour in the Seattle market. The trial was meant to last six months.

Almost Live! won a national award from the National Association of Television Programming Executives (best local show in America) while SNL "tanked" in local ratings. The trial was extended indefinitely.

The show was shaved to a half hour and converted fully to sketch comedy. This format would define it for the remaining nine seasons.

## Comedy Central Syndication (1992-1993)

In summer 1992, the show taped 65 special episodes for Comedy Central, bringing Almost Live! to a national cable audience. The same year, Microsoft, Starbucks, and the national grunge explosion were all amplifying Seattle's profile.
