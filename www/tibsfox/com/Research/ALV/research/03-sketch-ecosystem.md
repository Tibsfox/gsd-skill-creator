# Sketch Comedy Ecosystem -- Cast, Formats & Improv

Almost Live!'s cast was drawn from Seattle's comedy community -- not Hollywood casting agencies. This produced an ensemble of teachers, engineers, improv performers, and local media professionals.

## Recurring Sketches

The sketch format developed a vocabulary of recurring characters and premises:

- **Speed Walker** -- John Keister as a crime-fighting competitive speed walker
- **High-Fivin' White Guys** -- Satire of Seattle's white middle-class enthusiasm
- **Cops in...** -- Cops franchise parody set in various Seattle neighborhoods (Ballard, Wallingford, etc.)
- **Mind Your Manners with Billy Quan** -- Darrell Suto as an etiquette enforcer
- **The Worst Girlfriend in the World** -- Relationship comedy
- **Lame List / Almost Live! Top 10** -- Weekly satirical lists targeting Seattle culture
- **Uncle Fran's Tips for Teens** -- Pat Cashman as a well-meaning but clueless advice-giver
- **Qwik Fishin'** -- Parody of Seattle's outdoor recreation culture

## Unexpected Productions / Theatresports

The parallel improv ecosystem at Pike Place Market was a critical training ground. Unexpected Productions has operated Theatresports at Pike Place Market since 1985, providing:

- Performance space for developing comedy skills
- Cross-pollination with Almost Live! cast members
- Joel McHale performed at Theatresports concurrently with his Almost Live! tenure (1993-97)
- Improv training complemented the show's sketch writing process

## The Seattle Stand-Up Circuit

Seattle's comedy clubs in the 1980s-90s included:
- **The Comedy Underground** -- Pioneer Square, primary venue
- **Giggles** -- University District
- Various open-mic nights at bars throughout Capitol Hill and Fremont

This circuit fed talent into Almost Live!'s writing room and cast.
