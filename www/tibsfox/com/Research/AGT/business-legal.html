1. Damodaran Corporate Finance

Five lecture recordings from Aswath Damodaran’s corporate finance and valuation courses at NYU Stern. Damodaran — widely regarded as the foremost authority on equity valuation education — provides a rigorous, data-driven framework for understanding how companies create and destroy value.

The Three Drivers of Cash Flow Value

DriverWhat It MeasuresDirection
Revenue GrowthGrowth potentialHigher = more valuable
Operating MarginsProfitabilityHigher = more valuable
Sales-to-Capital RatioCapital efficiencyHigher = more valuable (less reinvestment)

The Perfect Company: High revenue growth + high operating margins + high sales-to-capital ratio. Many companies achieve this at the $50M stage. The Magnificent Seven (Apple, Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta, Nvidia, Tesla) did it at $500B scale — that is what makes them exceptional.

DCF Valuation Framework

Value = ∑ [Expected Cash Flowt / (1 + Discount Rate)t]

The three drivers feed directly in: revenue growth drives the numerator (future cash flows), operating margins convert revenue to cash, and the sales-to-capital ratio determines reinvestment drag on free cash flow.

Common-Size Analysis

Damodaran demonstrates cross-company comparison using vertical analysis: every income statement line as a percentage of revenue (revenue = 100%), every balance sheet line as a percentage of total assets. This enables comparison between companies of different sizes — the same principle behind our normalized metrics across research projects of different scales.

2. Accounting & Financial Statements

Seven transcripts spanning the full arc of business finance education — from introductory accounting through CPA-level tax planning to quantitative finance at MIT.

The Accounting Equation

Assets = Liabilities + Shareholders’ Equity

This identity is the foundation of double-entry bookkeeping. Every transaction must maintain this balance. Accounting is “the language of business” — not math, but language.

Four Interconnected Financial Statements

StatementPurposeKey Line Items
Income StatementProfitability over a periodRevenue, COGS, gross profit, operating expenses, net income
Statement of Changes in EquityHow equity evolvedBeginning equity, issued shares, net income, dividends
Balance SheetFinancial position at a point in timeAssets, liabilities, shareholders’ equity
Cash Flow StatementWhere cash came from and wentOperating, investing, financing activities

Critical insight: These are an integrated system, not independent documents. The income statement feeds into changes in equity, which feeds into the balance sheet, which feeds into cash flow.

Cap Table Mechanics for Startups

  • Cap table = authoritative ledger of all current and pending shareholders
  • Spreadsheets are “insufficient” — professional software (Aista, Carta) handles the complexity
  • 409A valuations required for stock option pricing — certified by NACV team
  • Entity-specific taxation: sole proprietorship, LLC, S-Corp, partnership each have distinct mechanics for quarterly estimated payments and withholding

3. Legal Technology & IP

Thirteen sources spanning IP fundamentals, Stanford CodeX legal technology research, and practical legal AI deployment.

The Four Pillars of IP

TypeProtectsDurationRegistration
CopyrightOriginal works of authorship (code, art, music, text)Life + 70 yearsExists at creation; registration enables federal suit
TrademarkSource identifiers (words, symbols, sounds, colors)Infinite (if maintained)Common law from use; federal registration adds nationwide protection
Trade SecretInformation with economic value from secrecyAs long as secret is maintainedNo registration; DTSA (2016) provides federal cause of action
PatentNovel, non-obvious, useful inventions20 years (utility), 15 years (design)USPTO prosecution; $10K–$30K+ per utility patent

IP Strategy for Startups

The monopoly right is not the most important function of patents for startups. Patents serve as:

  • Valuation signal — investors use patent portfolio as proxy for innovation depth
  • Defensive moat — cross-licensing leverage against larger companies
  • Acquisition currency — patent portfolio often drives M&A pricing
  • Provisional applications — 12-month placeholder at low cost ($320 small entity) to establish priority date

4. Safety-Critical Fintech

Nigeria Ajo/Esusu: Zero-Tolerance Financial Software

Traditional rotating savings groups (Ajo/Esusu) digitized into mobile platforms serving populations with no formal banking access. The engineering constraints are absolute:

  • Zero tolerance for financial errors — a single miscalculated contribution or missed payout destroys trust in a community where trust is the only collateral
  • Offline-first architecture — network connectivity is intermittent; the system must function without persistent connection and reconcile when connectivity returns
  • Minimal device capability — feature phones with USSD interface, not smartphones with apps
  • Cultural protocol as business logic — the rotation order, contribution schedule, and dispute resolution follow social norms that vary by community and must be configurable

This is the hardest class of financial software: safety-critical systems where the users have no recourse to customer support, no backup payment method, and no regulatory protection. Every design decision is a trust decision.

5. Enterprise AI Governance

AmpGPT Organizational Model

Enterprise AI governance requires treating model drift as a first-class risk category. The AmpGPT pattern establishes organizational structure around AI deployment:

Governance LayerFunctionRisk Addressed
Policy BoardApprove use cases, set boundaries, review incidentsStrategic misalignment, reputational risk
Technical ReviewEvaluate model selection, integration architecture, data flowsTechnical debt, security vulnerabilities
Operational MonitoringTrack drift metrics, usage patterns, cost, latencyModel degradation, cost overrun
Compliance AuditRegulatory alignment, bias testing, data lineageLegal liability, discrimination claims

Drift as Risk

Model drift in enterprise AI is analogous to configuration drift in infrastructure — it happens invisibly and compounds. The governance framework must include:

  • Baseline metrics — established at deployment, tracked continuously
  • Drift detection thresholds — statistical triggers for human review
  • Rollback procedures — ability to revert to a known-good model version
  • Incident classification — severity levels for drift events (cosmetic, functional, safety-critical)

This maps to our trust system: trust is earned through consistent behavior over time, and drift erodes trust. The trust-relationship provider’s decay function models exactly this phenomenon.
