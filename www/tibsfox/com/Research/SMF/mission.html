Mission Pack

The original research brief and mission package that initiated the SMOFcon research project. A comprehensive three-stage document — Vision, Research Reference, Mission Package — covering the full pipeline from institutional analysis to structured execution.

Source Documents

The mission pack comprises a multi-hundred-line TeX mission document covering the complete SMOFcon research scope: etymology of “SMOF” from Chalker’s 1963 coinage through NYT citation to the founding of the convention series in 1984, convention operations and programming structure, the knowledge ecosystem anchoring Worldcon governance, WSFS constitutional mechanics, internationalization challenges including visa barriers and bursary programs, and the horizon toward the 2039 Worldcon centennial.

Research Scope

  • Etymology & Identity: “Secret Masters of Fandom” from conspiracy humor to professional self-identifier. Chalker’s coinage (1962–1963), NYT citation (1971), Tucker’s Neo-Fan’s Guide (1973). The culture of convention runners as a distinct community within fandom.
  • Convention History: Smofcon 0 (1970s precursor) through Smofcon 43 (Lisbon, 2026). The shift from 27 years of US-only gatherings to three European events in six years. MCFI, SFSFC, SWOC as recurring hosts.
  • Operations: Hotel negotiation, programming scheduling, volunteer management, safety and code of conduct, accessibility, crisis management. The Worldcon Q&A as informal accountability mechanism. The consuite and zoo con.
  • Knowledge Transfer: The Worldcon Runners’ Guide, ConRunner wiki, Fancyclopedia 3, Smofs mailing list, File 770. Oral tradition vs. codified knowledge. The fragility of volunteer institutional memory.
  • Governance: WSFS as unincorporated literary society. Site selection mechanics, Business Meeting procedure, Mark Protection Committee. SMOFcon as the informal back-channel for fan governance.
  • Internationalization: Geographic expansion, visa barriers (ConKigali), bursary programs (SFSFC, CanSMOF, Glasgow 2024), virtual participation, the 2039 horizon.

Key Connections

SMOFcon completes the conventions sub-cluster: NWC (the convention), WCN (the traveling convention), SMF (the convention about conventions). The meta-layer that keeps West Coast fandom’s infrastructure alive. Connects to SYS (systems administration), BCM (project management), BRC (volunteer infrastructure), TIBS (knowledge transmission), WAL (Rosetta Stone — translation between creative communities), GSD2 (meta-orchestration), TSL (meta-review), WYR (institutional memory), DDA (community persistence).
