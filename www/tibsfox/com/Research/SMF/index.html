Since 1984, roughly 150 people have gathered each December in hotel function rooms across two continents — not to celebrate science fiction, but to argue about how science fiction celebrations should be organized. They call themselves “smofs” — Secret Masters of Fandom — a name that began as a conspiracy joke and evolved into the self-identifier of an entire volunteer culture. SMOFcon is their annual parliament, their knowledge exchange, and their recruiting office.

Identity & Origins

The Secret Masters

Identity

The term “Secret Masters of Fandom” — from Chalker’s 1963 coinage to a volunteer culture’s self-identifier. The tongue-in-cheek humor of calling yourself a master when your primary job is making sure the function rooms have enough chairs.

Since 1984

History

From a New York precursor gathering through 43 numbered conventions across two continents. A small convention that shaped how an entire culture organizes itself — and its recent expansion from US-only to international.

Operations & Knowledge

How to Run a Convention

Operations

What SMOFcon teaches: hotel contracts, volunteer management, safety protocols, accessibility, crisis response. Systems engineering performed by volunteers on a deadline with no budget.

The Oral Tradition

Knowledge

Why convention knowledge is fragile: volunteer turnover, burnout, generational gaps. The documentation challenge — most convention knowledge exists only in people’s heads. SMOFcon as the mechanism for preserving what can’t be written down.

Governance & Synthesis

Where Bids Are Made

Governance

SMOFcon as the informal governance layer for Worldcon, Westercon, and NASFiC. Where democratic voting meets insider knowledge — the pre-game that ensures the candidates are prepared.

The Convention About Conventions

Connections

NWC + WCN + SMF: the complete conventions stack. The recursive pattern — a convention about conventions, a skill-creator about skill-creation. Three layers: application, network, infrastructure.

Verification

The Con Chair’s Checklist

7/7 PASS

Named for the checklist every convention chair maintains — every item checked is a disaster prevented. Module completion, source registry, cross-link audit, cultural sensitivity review.
