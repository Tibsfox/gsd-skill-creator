Long-Term Memory Systems for AI Agents

BEAM · Cognee · Memori · Mem0 · Honcho — SOTA Analysis & Our Architecture Evolution
Overview BEAM Mem0 Cognee Memori+ Benchmarks Our Arch RAM Disk Custom Eval Proposals Roadmap
The BEAM paper (ICLR 2026) demonstrates that even 1M-token context LLMs — with or without RAG — struggle as dialogues lengthen. Cognee hits 93% correctness with graph+vector hybrid memory. Memori achieves 81.95% on LoCoMo with 95% token reduction, beating Mem0 (62.47%) substantially. Our current 6-tier LOD architecture (5,087 lines, 167 tests) scored LongMemEval R@5 at 96.6% — but we need to measure ourselves against BEAM’s 10 abilities, benchmark ourselves on our own 129-session 16,928-turn conversation corpus, and engineer a persistent RAM disk to eliminate local storage thrashing. This research digest surveys the state of the art, identifies our gaps, and proposes concrete improvements.

Module Overview

BEAM

01. BEAM Benchmark Deep Dive

ICLR 2026 paper by Tavakoli et al. 100 conversations at 128K/500K/1M/10M tokens, 2,000 probing questions, 10 memory abilities (abstention, contradiction, event ordering, info extraction, instruction following, knowledge update, multi-session reasoning, preference, summarization, temporal). LIGHT framework: 3.5-12.7% improvement.
Mem0

02. Mem0 Architecture & Research

Mem0ai papers and architecture. Fact extraction pipeline, LLM-based memory management. 62.47% on LoCoMo (lowest of comparison set). Why it underperforms against Memori and Zep, and what their approach gets right.
Cognee

03. Cognee: Graph + Vector Hybrid

Hybrid graph + vector memory with Chain-of-Thought optimization. 0.93 human-like correctness on HotPotQA. +314% F1 improvement, +1618% EM improvement over prior versions. Graph-completion retrievers, ontologies, knowledge graph integration.
Memori+

04. Memori, Honcho, Zep, LangMem Survey

Comparative architectures. Memori: 81.95% LoCoMo, 95% token reduction, dual-layer (semantic triples + summaries). Zep: 79.09%. LangMem: 78.05%. Mem0: 62.47%. Honcho evaluation framework. Architectural patterns common to the winners.
Benchmarks

05. LongMemEval, LoCoMo, HotPotQA, MSC

Academic benchmark methodology deep dive. LongMemEval conversation synthesis. LoCoMo multi-session structure. HotPotQA multi-hop reasoning. MemBench patterns. What each measures, limitations, how to replicate methodology on our own data.
Our Arch

06. Our Current Architecture: Strengths & Gaps

6-tier LOD review: RAM cache → Index → Files → Chroma → PostgreSQL → Corpus. 5,087 lines, 167 tests. Prior measured scores (LongMemEval R@5 96.6%). What we do well, what we miss from SOTA approaches (graph reasoning, semantic triples, CoT-optimized retrieval).
RAM Disk

07. Persistent RAM Disk Design

tmpfs engineering for working memory. 4GB initial, dynamic 4GB growth chunks. Sync strategies (write-behind, periodic snapshot, event-driven). Crash recovery. Memory pressure handling. The stability dividend — leveraging long system uptime.
Custom Eval

08. Custom Benchmark from Our Session Data

Methodology for using 129 sessions + 16,928 turns as an evaluation dataset. Automated question generation across BEAM’s 10 ability categories. Scoring rubric. How to prove our architecture works on OUR actual workload, not synthetic benchmarks.
Proposals

09. Improvement Proposals & Architecture Evolution

Concrete enhancements synthesized from SOTA: graph+vector hybrid (Cognee), semantic triples layer (Memori), LIGHT-style scratchpad (BEAM), RAM-backed working memory. Prioritized, sized, and measured against custom benchmarks.
Roadmap

10. Implementation Roadmap & Risk Assessment

Phased implementation plan. Phase 1: persistent RAM disk. Phase 2: custom benchmark infrastructure. Phase 3: graph layer. Phase 4: semantic triples. Risk assessment, dependencies, measurement gates, rollback strategy. Benchmark-driven development.
