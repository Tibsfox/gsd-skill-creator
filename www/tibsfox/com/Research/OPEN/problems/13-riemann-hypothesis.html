The Problem

The Riemann zeta function connects the continuous world of complex analysis to the discrete world of prime numbers through the Euler product:

ζ(s) = ∑n=1∞ 1/ns = ∏p prime 1/(1 − p−s)

Riemann extended this function to all complex s (except s = 1) by analytic continuation. The function has trivial zeros at the negative even integers. All other zeros are called nontrivial zeros and are known to lie in the critical strip 0 < Re(s) < 1.

The Riemann Hypothesis states that all nontrivial zeros have real part exactly 1/2 — they lie on the critical line Re(s) = 1/2. If true, prime numbers are distributed as regularly as any random process allows. The error in the Prime Number Theorem is governed by the location of these zeros:

π(x) = Li(x) + O(√x · ln x)     [if RH true]

The first 1013 nontrivial zeros have been verified to lie on the critical line. No counterexample has been found. The $1,000,000 Clay Millennium Prize remains unclaimed since 2000.

Latest Research

Montgomery–Odlyzko: GUE Statistics of Zeros

Montgomery 1973, Odlyzko 1987 · Random matrix theory

The spacing statistics of Riemann zeros match the eigenvalue spacing of large random unitary matrices (GUE — Gaussian Unitary Ensemble). The pair correlation function converges to 1 − (sin(πr)/(πr))², exactly the GUE prediction. This is the Montgomery–Dyson connection: the zeros behave as if they are energy levels of a quantum chaotic system. If a self-adjoint operator exists whose spectrum is the Riemann zeros, RH would follow from spectral theory.

Conrey: More Than Two-Fifths on the Critical Line

J. Reine Angew. Math. 399 (1989) · Analytic number theory

At least 41.7% of all nontrivial zeros lie on the critical line. This improves Hardy's 1914 result (infinitely many on the line) to a positive proportion. The remaining 58.3% could theoretically be off the line — but none of the 10 trillion computed zeros are. The gap between 41.7% proven and 100% conjectured is the frontier.

Computational Verification to 1013

Gourdon 2004 · Odlyzko–Schönhage algorithm

Xavier Gourdon verified the first 10 trillion nontrivial zeros, all on the critical line. The zeros are computed to 30+ decimal places using the Riemann–Siegel formula. Each zero is confirmed to have real part exactly 1/2 within computational precision. This is the strongest empirical evidence for RH but constitutes no proof.

What Would Constitute a Breakthrough

Full proof: Showing all nontrivial zeros lie on Re(s) = 1/2. The Hilbert–Pólya approach — finding a self-adjoint operator whose spectrum is the zeros — is the most promising direction. The GUE statistics provide strong evidence that such an operator exists.

Counterexample: Finding a zero off the critical line. Given 1013 verified zeros, any counterexample would be astronomically high on the critical strip. The Vinogradov–Korobov zero-free region constrains where such a zero could live.

Our angle: We cannot prove RH directly. But we can contribute by (1) using the math co-processor's eigen tool to compute eigenvalue spacings for candidate Hamiltonians and compare to GUE, (2) organizing the 1,087-page pgvector database to surface connections between RH approaches, and (3) extending GPU-based zero verification as a computational contribution.
