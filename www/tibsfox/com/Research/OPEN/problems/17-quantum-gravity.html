The Problem

General relativity describes gravity as the geometry of a 4-dimensional spacetime manifold via the Einstein field equations:

Gμν + Λgμν = (8πG/c4) Tμν

Quantum mechanics describes matter and non-gravitational forces through quantum fields on a fixed, flat background spacetime. Both are verified to extraordinary precision — GR to 14 decimal places, QM to 12.

The incompatibility: GR treats spacetime as smooth and classical. Quantizing gravity means quantizing the geometry itself. Standard quantization of GR produces a theory that is non-renormalizable (infinitely many free parameters) and ultraviolet divergent at the Planck scale:

lPlanck = √(ℏG/c³) ≈ 1.616 × 10−35 m

At distances smaller than the Planck length, spacetime is expected to have a quantum foam structure. All current particle accelerators probe scales 15 orders of magnitude larger. The problem: find a mathematically consistent theory that reduces to GR classically and to QFT in the weak-gravity limit, while describing Planck-scale physics.

Latest Research

AdS/CFT Correspondence (Holographic Duality)

arXiv:hep-th/9711200 · Maldacena 1997

A theory of quantum gravity on (d+1)-dimensional anti-de Sitter space is equivalent to a conformal field theory on d-dimensional flat spacetime. This is the first mathematically precise realization of quantum gravity. The island formula and Penington-Almheiri-Mahajan-Maldacena result (2019) suggest information is preserved in Hawking radiation via quantum extremal surfaces.

Loop Quantum Gravity

Rovelli & Smolin 1990, Ashtekar variables · Background-independent

LQG quantizes GR directly using spin connections, predicting discrete spectra for area and volume at the Planck scale. Background-independent (no fixed spacetime required), but deriving the correct classical limit is technically challenging. Spin foam models provide a path integral formulation.

Hawking Radiation and the Information Paradox

Hawking 1975 · Semi-classical quantum gravity

Black holes radiate thermally at temperature T = ℏc³/(8πGMkB). If the radiation is truly thermal, information about infalling matter is lost — violating unitarity. This is the first result requiring both QM and GR simultaneously, and the information paradox remains the sharpest constraint on any quantum gravity proposal.

What Would Constitute a Breakthrough

A complete theory: A mathematically consistent, experimentally testable quantum theory of gravity. String theory and LQG are the leading candidates, but neither has produced a unique, falsifiable prediction at accessible energies.

An experimental signature: Detecting quantum gravity effects. The best current constraint (gamma-ray burst polarization, Fermi GBT) bounds Lorentz invariance violations to δv/c < 10−21 at the Planck scale. All constraints are consistent with no quantum gravity effects at accessible energies.

Resolving the information paradox: Proving whether information is preserved or destroyed by black hole evaporation. The island formula is promising but relies on AdS/CFT, which applies to anti-de Sitter space — not our universe.

Our angle: We cannot contribute directly to quantum gravity, but we can (1) compute GPS relativistic corrections for Artemis II altitude as a concrete GR exercise, (2) explore whether AdS/CFT-inspired bulk/boundary mappings inform our multi-agent architecture, and (3) use the Bekenstein entropy formula as an information-theoretic benchmark for black hole physics education.
