The Twin Prime Conjecture

Are there infinitely many prime pairs {p, p+2}?

The Problem

A twin prime is a pair (p, p+2) where both are prime: (3,5), (5,7), (11,13), (17,19), (29,31), (41,43), ... The conjecture states there are infinitely many such pairs.

The quantitative form (Hardy-Littlewood, 1923) predicts how many:

π2(x) ~ 2C2 · x / (ln x)²     where C2 ≈ 0.6602

Zhang's 2013 breakthrough proved that prime gaps less than 70,000,000 occur infinitely often. The Polymath8/Maynard-Tao collaboration reduced the bound dramatically:

The parity problem in sieve theory is the fundamental barrier: sieves cannot distinguish numbers with an odd number of prime factors from those with an even number. Overcoming this likely requires arithmetic input beyond standard sieve techniques.

Latest Research

Zhang: Bounded Gaps Between Primes

Annals of Mathematics 179(3) · 2014

Yitang Zhang, a University of New Hampshire lecturer, proved that infinitely many consecutive prime pairs differ by less than 70 million. The paper was accepted in three weeks (typical review: 1-2 years). Zhang's bound was not tight; 70 million is a byproduct of the method's parameters. The result opened the floodgate for all subsequent improvements.

Maynard-Tao Sieve and Polymath8

arXiv:1407.4897 · Polymath8b 2014

James Maynard and Terence Tao independently developed a more efficient sieve reducing the gap to 600 (Maynard) and then 246 (Polymath8b) unconditionally. Maynard's approach also proves that prime k-tuples of any size occur infinitely often. Under the Elliott-Halberstam conjecture, the bound reaches 6. Maynard won the 2022 Fields Medal partly for this work.

Brun's Theorem: Convergent Reciprocals

Bull. Sci. Math. 43 (1919) · Classical sieve theory

The sum of reciprocals of twin primes converges: ∑(1/p + 1/(p+2)) = B ≈ 1.902. This contrasts with the harmonic series over all primes (which diverges). Twin primes are sparse enough for their reciprocals to sum to a finite number — but convergence of the sum does not settle whether the list is finite or infinite.

Erdős tracker and GPU pipeline. The ERDOS-TRACKER.md tracks 105 prize problems, several involving prime gaps and distributions. The same sieve-filter-verify structure applies to finding anomalies in the pgvector database (1,087 pages, all-MiniLM-L6-v2 embeddings). Brun's sieve, the Maynard-Tao sieve, and our embedding-based search are all instances of reducing a large search space to a subset satisfying a local constraint.

RH cross-reference (P13). Under the Riemann Hypothesis, prime gaps near x are typically O(√x · ln x). The Hardy-Littlewood twin prime conjecture is consistent with RH and follows from stronger unproven hypotheses. Better knowledge of zeta zeros sharpens prime gap distributions.

What Would Constitute a Breakthrough

Proving the conjecture: Showing infinitely many twin primes exist. This likely requires overcoming the parity problem — the deepest barrier in sieve theory. No known sieve-based approach can reach a gap of 2; new arithmetic input is needed.

Reducing the bound below 6: Even without the full conjecture, any improvement below the Elliott-Halberstam conditional bound of 6 would require fundamentally new ideas. The gaps 4 and 2 correspond to cousin primes and twin primes respectively.

Our angle: We can contribute by (1) extending GPU-parallel twin prime verification and computing Brun's constant to additional precision, (2) visualizing prime gap sequences as time series to reveal structural patterns, and (3) exploring whether the Maynard-Tao sieve structure has analogues in semantic clustering of our research corpus.
