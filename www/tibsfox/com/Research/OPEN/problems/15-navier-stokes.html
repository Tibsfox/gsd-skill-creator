Navier-Stokes Existence & Smoothness

Do smooth solutions to the equations of fluid motion always exist in 3D?
Open · $1M Clay Prize
OPEN Live Research

The Problem

The incompressible Navier-Stokes equations describe the velocity field u(x,t) and pressure field p(x,t) of a viscous fluid in three spatial dimensions:

∂u/∂t + (u · ∇)u = −∇p + νΔu + f     [momentum]
∇ · u = 0     [incompressibility]

Given smooth, divergence-free initial data u0 with all derivatives bounded, does a smooth solution exist for all time t > 0? And does the kinetic energy ½∫|u|² dx remain bounded?

Either proving global existence (no finite-time blowup) or constructing a smooth initial condition that leads to singularity wins the $1,000,000 Clay Prize. These equations describe every fluid on Earth — blood in veins, wind over Paine Field, water in Puget Sound — yet the mathematical question of well-posedness remains unanswered since Leray's foundational work in 1934.

Latest Research

Leray Weak Solutions and Partial Regularity

Leray 1934, Caffarelli-Kohn-Nirenberg 1982 · Foundational

Leray proved that weak solutions (satisfying the equations in a distributional sense) exist globally. Caffarelli-Kohn-Nirenberg proved the strongest regularity result: the set of spacetime singularities has Hausdorff dimension at most 1. If singularities exist, they form at most a line in spacetime — not a surface or volume.

Tao: Averaged Navier-Stokes Blowup

arXiv:1402.0290 · JAMS 2016

Terence Tao showed that a suitably modified version of Navier-Stokes (with a more nonlinear advection term) can exhibit finite-time blowup. This suggests the nonlinearity is the key difficulty, but the true NS equations remain open. The viscous dissipation (νΔu) fights against blowup, and the question is whether it always wins.

Beale-Kato-Majda Blowup Criterion

Comm. Math. Phys. 94 (1984) · Vorticity criterion

If the maximum vorticity |ω|L∞ stays bounded for all time, smooth solutions exist globally. Blowup requires |ω| → ∞ in finite time. The vortex stretching mechanism — the term (ω·∇)u in the vorticity equation — is the primary suspect for potential 3D blowup.

What Would Constitute a Breakthrough

Global existence proof: Showing smooth solutions exist for all time in 3D. This would likely require a new energy estimate that controls vortex stretching. The 2D case is solved (Ladyzhenskaya, 1959) because vortex stretching doesn't occur in 2D.

Blowup construction: Finding smooth initial data that produces a singularity in finite time. Tao's averaged NS blowup suggests the approach, but the true equations have stronger viscous dissipation. Any blowup scenario would need to overwhelm the dissipation with concentrated vortex stretching.

Our angle: We can contribute by (1) using the math co-processor's FFT tool for spectral decomposition of simulated flows and verifying the Kolmogorov energy spectrum, (2) computing Reynolds numbers for our forest simulation to determine whether we operate in the "safe" regime, and (3) analyzing KPAE wind data for vortex stretching proxies (wind shear across stations) in the MUK convergence zone.
