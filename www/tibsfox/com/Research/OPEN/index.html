AI Reasoning & Reliability

P1: Chain-of-Thought Faithfulness

AI Active Reasoning transparency

When a language model shows its reasoning, does the displayed chain of thought actually reflect the computation that produced the answer? Or is the scratchpad a post-hoc rationalization — a confabulated narrative draped over opaque internal states? Turpin et al. (2023) and Lanham et al. (2023) show the gap is real and measurable.

P2: Thinking Token Divergence

AI Active Extended thinking

Extended thinking models (o1, Claude with thinking blocks) use hidden reasoning tokens. When the internal reasoning diverges from the visible output, which one is “correct”? This is not a philosophical question — it is an engineering problem with safety implications for agentic systems that take real-world actions based on model outputs.

P3: CoT Monitoring at Scale

AI Testing Verification systems

If chain-of-thought is unreliable, how do we monitor it at production scale? Classical software testing assumes deterministic behavior. LLM reasoning is stochastic, context-dependent, and path-dependent. We need new verification architectures — and they must work within the convoy model's multi-agent execution framework.

Multi-Agent Systems & Distributed Intelligence

P4: Distributed Intelligence Emergence

AI Emergent Multi-agent orchestration

When multiple AI agents collaborate through structured protocols (GSD convoy, GUPP/DACP), does the collective exhibit capabilities that no individual agent possesses? This is the central question of the Gastown model: whether orchestration creates genuine distributed intelligence or merely distributes labor.

P5: Nondeterministic System Testing

Testing Active Verification theory

How do you write tests for a system whose outputs are stochastic? The gsd-skill-creator runs 21,298 deterministic tests, but the agents it orchestrates are fundamentally nondeterministic. Property-based testing, metamorphic testing, and statistical verification all offer partial answers. None is complete.

P6: Confidence-Based Routing

AI Security Trust calibration

When a model reports confidence in its output, is that confidence calibrated? Poorly calibrated confidence in a multi-agent system is worse than no confidence at all — it creates false trust. The trust system (trust-relationship.ts, 95 tests) must account for this fundamental uncertainty in every routing decision.

Pure Mathematics

P7: The Collatz Conjecture

Math Open since 1937 Number theory

Start with any positive integer. If even, halve it. If odd, triple and add one. Does every starting value eventually reach 1? Proposed by Lothar Collatz in 1937, verified computationally to 268, yet completely unproven. Erdős said “Mathematics may not be ready for such problems.”

P8: Chromatic Number of the Plane

Math Open since 1950 Combinatorial geometry

What is the minimum number of colors needed to color every point of the Euclidean plane such that no two points at distance exactly 1 share the same color? Known to be between 5 and 7 since de Grey (2018) improved the lower bound. The gap between 5 and 7 remains one of the most tantalizing open problems in combinatorics.

P9: The Komlós Conjecture

Math Open since 1981 Discrepancy theory

Given n vectors in Rd, each of Euclidean norm at most 1, can we always assign signs ±1 to achieve a signed sum with L∞ norm bounded by an absolute constant independent of d? Banaszczyk (1998) proved an O(sqrt(log d)) bound. The conjectured O(1) bound remains open.

Emergent Behavior & Complex Systems

P10: AI-Assisted Mathematical Discovery

AI Math Discovery frontier

Can AI systems generate genuinely novel mathematical conjectures — not just verify human conjectures or search parameter spaces, but identify structural patterns that humans have missed? FunSearch (Romera-Paredes et al., 2024) and AlphaProof suggest the boundary is shifting, but the epistemological question remains open.

P11: Kuramoto Synchronization

Emergent Active Network dynamics

When does a network of coupled oscillators synchronize? The Kuramoto model provides a clean mathematical framework, but the general problem on arbitrary graph topologies is unsolved. This maps directly to the helium corridor cooperative model: distributed nodes with heterogeneous coupling, partial observation, and network resilience requirements.

P12: Machine Unlearning

AI Security GUPP/DACP alignment

Can a trained model provably forget specific training data without full retraining? This is not just a privacy regulation compliance question — it is fundamental to the GUPP (Generalized User Privacy Protocol) and DACP (Distributed Agent Consent Protocol). If unlearning is impossible to verify, consent frameworks lose their enforcement mechanism.
