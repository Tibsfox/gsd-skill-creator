Millennium Prize Problems & Classical Mathematics

P13: The Riemann Hypothesis

Math Open since 1859 $1M Clay Prize

All nontrivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function lie on the critical line Re(s) = 1/2. The distribution of prime numbers — and every cryptographic key ever generated, including agent identity keys in the GSD trust system — depends on this being true. 10 trillion zeros verified, zero counterexamples found.

P14: P vs NP

Math Open since 1971 $1M Clay Prize

If P = NP, verification is no easier than search, and the entire witness-observer pattern collapses. The GSD verifier, the convoy model, and paper #34's Curry-Howard framework all rest on the practical assumption that checking a proof is easier than finding one. Three known barriers prevent all current proof techniques from resolving this.

P15: Navier-Stokes Existence & Smoothness

Math Open since 1934 $1M Clay Prize

The equations governing every fluid on Earth — blood, wind over Paine Field, water in Puget Sound — and we cannot prove smooth solutions always exist in 3D. Our forest simulation, the KPAE weather pipeline, and the MUK convergence zone all run on numerical approximations to equations we haven't proven are well-posed.

P16: The Twin Prime Conjecture

Math Classical Number theory

Are there infinitely many pairs of primes that differ by 2? Zhang (2013) proved bounded gaps exist; Maynard-Tao reduced the bound to 246. The gap from 246 to 2 remains open. Our GPU pipeline for Erdős verification connects directly to the sieve methods and prime gap computation this problem demands.

Fundamental Physics

P17: Quantum Gravity

Physics Open since 1930s GR + QM reconciliation

General relativity describes spacetime curvature at cosmic scales. Quantum mechanics describes particle behavior at atomic scales. Both are spectacularly successful, and they are mathematically incompatible. Reconciling them is the deepest open problem in physics — and Artemis II's cislunar navigation requires relativistic corrections that brush against this boundary.

P18: Dark Energy & the Cosmological Constant

Physics Open since 1917 Accelerating expansion

68% of the universe's energy density is something we cannot see, detect directly, or explain from first principles. The cosmological constant problem — a 120 orders of magnitude discrepancy between quantum field theory predictions and observation — is the worst prediction in the history of physics.

P19: Turbulence

Physics Classical The last unsolved problem

Kolmogorov's 1941 theory predicts the energy cascade in turbulent flow, yet fully rigorous derivation from Navier-Stokes remains elusive. Turbulence appears in our forest simulation (canopy wind), weather data (MUK convergence zone), and — by analogy — in context degradation patterns in long LLM conversations. The -5/3 power law is everywhere.

Collatz Conjecture (3n+1)

Math

Take any positive integer. If even, halve it. If odd, triple and add 1. Does this always reach 1? Verified computationally to 2.95 × 10²&sup0; but unproven. Terence Tao (2019) proved "almost all" orbits reach values close to 1.

Connection: L-systems are iterative rewriting. Collatz is iterative transformation. Can we visualize Collatz orbits as L-system trees?

TSPB Layer 8 (L-Systems) · Tao 2019 arXiv:1909.03562
WATCHING — visualization may reveal structure

Chromatic Number of the Plane

Math

How many colors do you need to color every point in the plane so that no two points exactly 1 unit apart share a color? Known: between 5 and 7. The lower bound was raised from 4 to 5 in 2018 by amateur mathematician Aubrey de Grey.

Connection: Unit distance = unit circle. The problem lives on our foundational object. Computational search + graph theory.

TSPB Layer 1 (Unit Circle) · de Grey 2018 arXiv:1804.02385
OPEN — computational approaches viable

Komlós Conjecture

Math

Given n vectors in R&sup d; with Euclidean norm at most 1, can you always assign signs ±1 to achieve a signed sum with infinity norm O(1)? Best known bound is O(√log n). Closing the gap is a major open problem in discrepancy theory.

Connection: Vector balancing = resource allocation = agent task distribution. If we solve it, our convoy model gets optimal load balancing.

TSPB Layer 4 (Vector Calculus) · Ben Green's Open Problems list

Emergent Behavior & Complex Systems

Kuramoto Synchronization Thresholds Updated

Math ML

When does a population of coupled oscillators synchronize? The Kuramoto model gives critical coupling strength K_c, but for heterogeneous populations with non-uniform natural frequencies, the transition dynamics are poorly understood.

April 2 update: Kuramoto himself published a 50-year retrospective (arXiv:2602.20505). New paper on launch day (arXiv:2603.29648): proportional coupling where K_ij scales with frequency detuning dramatically lowers K_c. Help the ones who need it most — the hardest-to-sync pairs get the strongest coupling. Direct implication for agent model profiles.

Connection: Our firefly simulation implements Kuramoto coupling. Proportional coupling variant is testable today in vehicle.js. Maps to agent orchestration: budget-tier agents should get more oversight than quality-tier.
