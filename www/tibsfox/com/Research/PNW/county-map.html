Education Pipeline from MUK

The transit network maps directly to the educational pipeline: community college, university, research.

MUK → EVCC (Everett Community College): 6 miles north. Bus via Mukilteo Speedway/SR-526 or Sounder to Everett. ~20–30 min. Programs: CS, IT Networking, Welding, Advanced Manufacturing, Business.
MUK → EDCC (Edmonds College): 5 miles south. Route 117 toward Lynnwood, then short transfer. ~25–35 min. Programs: CIS, Cybersecurity, Engineering Tech, Business, Construction Management.
MUK → UW Seattle: 30 miles south. Route 117 to Lynnwood City Center → Link 1 Line to U District Station. ~60–75 min total. Also: Sounder N Line to King Street → Link north to UW. Programs: CS, Aerospace Engineering, Applied Math, Information School.
MUK → UW Bothell: 15 miles southeast. Route 117 to Lynnwood → bus east, or drive via I-405. ~45–60 min by transit. Programs: Computing & Software Systems, Engineering, Business, Data Science.
MUK → WSU Everett: 7 miles north, at 915 N Broadway in downtown Everett. Bus or Sounder to Everett Station, then walk. ~25 min. WSU Everett campus offers engineering, data analytics, and business programs — a direct pipeline from EVCC to a 4-year WSU degree without leaving the county.
ORCA fare: $2.75 with free transfers within 2 hours. Students at EVCC, EDCC, Shoreline CC, and most community colleges receive ORCA cards through their institutions. UW students get a U-PASS providing unlimited transit rides.
