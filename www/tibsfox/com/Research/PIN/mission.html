Mission Package

Categories strain, crack and sometimes break, under their burden — step out of the space provided. The Nurse With Wound List opened with those words in 1979. Forty-seven years later, the network that list partially mapped is still generating connections, still producing meaning in the spaces between artists. The post-industrial network was built by people who understood that the edges carry the intelligence, not the nodes. This study maps those edges.

The Through-Line

The network this study maps was built by people who understood that the spaces between artists are where the actual intelligence lives. Not the nodes. The edges. The collaborations, the departures, the deliberate refusals. Small, principled components, faithfully iterated, producing staggering complexity from the connections between them. Throbbing Gristle invented industrial music and then deliberately terminated themselves so it could not be reduced to a style. Coil never released a consistent album. Current 93 changed its sound every few years. The network sustained itself through constant self-disruption — with no node large enough to calcify the whole. That is what the post-industrial network was. That is why it matters.
