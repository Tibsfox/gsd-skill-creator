Introduction

Language models drift. Not in the way a satellite drifts from its planned orbit — there is no single corrective burn, no ground controller who can uplink a fix. The drift is subtler: a model answers one question slightly less accurately than it did before, agrees with a user who pushed back a bit too hard, or retreats to memorized facts when the retrieved document says something different. Each episode is small. Accumulated across millions of interactions, across months of deployment, across the full surface of a system that touches knowledge, behavior, and retrieval simultaneously, the drift becomes consequential.

This mission catalogs that drift across three surfaces:

  • Module A — Knowledge & Factual Drift: How facts erode in long-form generation. The SD score (Spataru 2024), misinformation-induced belief shift (Fastowski 2024), natural context drift against pretraining snapshots (Wu 2025), and the DRIFT probe for intermediate-layer factual tracking (2026).
  • Module B — Alignment & Task Drift: How instruction fidelity degrades. Prompt-injection task drift (Abdelnabi 2024), TraceAlign behavioral monitoring (Das 2025), multi-turn equilibria (Dongre 2025), goal drift under adversarial pressure (2025), agent stability across networks (Rath 2026), and SAIL mid-layer intervention (2025).
  • Module C — Context & Retrieval/SSoT Drift: How retrieved context and the single source of truth degrade. Response semantic drift in diffusion-LM RAG (2026), event-evolution graphs for temporal retrieval (Liu 2026), the Semantic Grounding Index (2025), and context-entropy dilution in BEE-RAG (2025).

Drift Taxonomy — Surface Map

The mission defines a 7-field record schema for each drift phenomenon: {phenomenon, surface, metric, typical_magnitude, citation_id, mitigations, couplings}. The 19 records accumulated across Waves 1 and 2 cover all three surfaces plus cross-surface coupling events. The central quantitative result: SD scores cluster between 0.77 and 0.79 across all major LLMs tested, suggesting drift is an architectural property rather than a model-family artifact.

Key mitigations by surface:

  • Knowledge: Layer-wise semantic dynamics (LSD) early stopping; DRIFT probe as real-time factuality gate
  • Alignment: TraceAlign fine-tuning (40% → 6.2%); SAIL focal-attention LoRA adapter; ASI monitoring for multi-agent systems
  • Retrieval: Contextual normalization; BEE-RAG entropy-aware retrieval; Chronos temporal grounding
