Research Scope

  • Mythological Convergence: Structural analysis of the Osiris-Horus succession myth (c. 2400 BCE), Shakespeare’s Hamlet (c. 1600 CE), the Epic of Sundiata (c. 1235 CE), and biblical parallels. Eight or more structural elements mapped across all four traditions with specific textual citations.
  • The Griot Tradition: The jali system of Mandé societies. Hereditary roles: genealogist, historian, musician, counselor, diplomat, praise-singer. The Kouyaté lineage’s seven-hundred-year stewardship of the Sundiata epic. Oral tradition as sophisticated technology with built-in error correction.
  • Deep Roots — Paleoanthropology: Ardi (4.4 Ma), Lucy (3.2 Ma), the Laetoli footprints (3.6 Ma), the Turkana Boy (1.5 Ma). The Great Rift Valley as crucible of human emergence. The biomechanical revolution of upright walking.
  • Circle of Life: African cosmological foundations — Osiris agricultural-resurrection cycle, Mandinka badenya/fadenya duality. Ecological science: nutrient cycling, trophic cascades, keystone species. Connection to the “spaces between” philosophy.
  • The Voice of Africa: Cultural authenticity across thirty years of Lion King adaptations. The 1994 original’s Swahili names and Lebo M.’s Zulu chants. Barry Jenkins’ collaborative process for Mufasa (2024) with John Kani and Kagiso Lediga.
  • Roots & Return: Three-layer framework connecting deep-time origins (“we all come from Africa”), cultural heritage (griot as genealogist), and personal identity recovery (reconnecting-descendant protocols).

Key Connections

LKR connects to TIBS (Animal Speak — indigenous knowledge systems and respectful documentation), FDR (Fire Dragon — mythological fire traditions across cultures), ARC (Shapes & Colors — symbolic visual language in African art), ECO (Living Systems — ecological cycles and trophic cascades), DAA (Deep Audio — the kora, balafon, and oral musical tradition), FFA (Fur & Feathers — African wildlife and cultural symbolism), and MAM (Mammals — lion ecology and apex predator dynamics).
