Mythological Roots

Mythological Convergence

Convergence

Four traditions converge in one story: the Osiris-Horus succession myth (c. 2400 BCE), Shakespeare’s Hamlet, the Epic of Sundiata — the literal Lion King of Mali — and the biblical narratives of exile and return. Structural parallels mapped and sourced.

The Griot Tradition

Jeliya

The jali of West Africa: genealogist, counselor, musician, and living archive. Seven hundred years of the Kouyaté lineage. Oral tradition as a sophisticated knowledge-preservation technology with built-in error correction.

Deep Roots — Paleoanthropology

East Africa

Ardi, Lucy, the Laetoli footprints, the Turkana Boy. The Great Rift Valley as crucible of human emergence. The biomechanical revolution of upright walking and the body that opened the savanna as habitat.

Circle of Life

Ecology

Not a Disney song but a paleoanthropological fact. African cosmological foundations, the Osiris agricultural cycle, Mandinka badenya and fadenya, ecological trophic cascades, and the mathematical spaces between.
