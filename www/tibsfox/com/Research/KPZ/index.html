Origins & Frequency Heritage

Origins & Frequency Heritage (1959--1978)

Origins

KETO-FM signs on in 1959 with a pelican logo and easy listening. Gene Autry's Golden West Broadcasters acquires in 1976, flips to pop-rock Top 40 as "The FM KVI." Frank Colbourn earns twelve gold records. Meanwhile, 104.9 exists only as translator K285AE. The geological bedrock of Seattle radio heritage.

The Radio Wars

The Radio Wars (1978--1994)

Radio Wars

K-Plus 101 rides the disco wave and crashes with it. Hot Hits returns KPLZ to Top 40 in 1983. The definitive KPLZ-vs-KUBE battle: Hit Election, Stereo Wars, Rick Dees affiliation. KNBQ provides a third contender. By 1994, the wars end with "Waiting for a Star to Fall."

Star 101.5 & Personalities

Star 101.5 & the Personalities (1994--2024)

Star 101.5

Kent Phillips and Alan Budwill: 32 years together, the longest-running morning team in Seattle radio history. Star 101.5 survives three ownership transfers. Christmas music becomes a regional institution. On April 1, 2024, "Good Riddance" ends the Star era and Hank FM begins.

The 104.9 Frequency

The 104.9 Frequency (1988--present)

104.9

A 17,000-watt signal from Eatonville punching above its weight. The beloved Funky Monkey era (1999--2010). GenX's catastrophic 0.3 share. iHeart's four-station shuffle exiles KUBE from 93.3. Contemporary Worship closes the chapter. Nine identities on one frequency.

Industry Context & Cultural Legacy

Industry

The Telecommunications Act of 1996 and the consolidation wave that reshaped Seattle radio. From locally owned competitive stations to corporate cluster management. The death of local radio as cultural commons, and what the KPLZ/104.9 stories tell us about the national narrative.
