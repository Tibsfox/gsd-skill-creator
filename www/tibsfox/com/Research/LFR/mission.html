Research Scope

  • Old-Growth Forest Ecology: What defines old-growth in the PNW. The big four conifers. Multi-layered canopy structure. Snags, nurse logs, canopy gaps. Why old-growth is an ecosystem state, not just an age. How much remains from the original 15–19 million acres.
  • Mother Tree / Mycorrhizal Networks: Suzanne Simard’s research at UBC. The common mycorrhizal network (CMN). Carbon and nutrient transfer between trees. Hub trees provisioning seedlings. The Wood Wide Web metaphor and the Karst et al. controversy. Western red cedar as Grandmother Cedar.
  • Terrain Genesis: Cascadia Subduction Zone, volcanic arc (Tahoma, St. Helens, Hood, Baker), Cordilleran Ice Sheet glaciation, the Missoula Floods. How geology determines soil, soil determines hydrology, hydrology determines what grows where.
  • Canopy Ecology: Eight vertical layers from emergent to subterranean. Epiphyte loads of 35 kg per tree. Marbled murrelet nesting ecology. Light gradients from 100% to <2%. Nalini Nadkarni’s canopy science.
  • Fire Ecology: Low-severity vs. high-severity fire regimes. The 1910 Great Fire and a century of suppression. The 2020 Oregon Labor Day fires. Indigenous cultural burning by Kalapuya, Yakama, Chinook, Salish nations. Prescribed fire restoration.
  • Climate and the Forest: Douglas fir range shifts, bark beetle outbreaks, snowpack decline, carbon storage argument for old-growth preservation, assisted migration debate.
  • Mesh Telemetry: RAWS weather stations, dendrometer bands, camera traps, passive acoustic monitoring, Landsat/Sentinel-2 satellite imagery, LiDAR canopy structure mapping, IoT sensor networks, digital twin concept.
