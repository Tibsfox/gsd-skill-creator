Bay Area House Music

Queer Disco · Warehouse Culture · Wicked · Deep House · Burning Man · PNW Connection
Overview Queer Disco Rave Era Labels & Sound Venues Outdoor & BM Mission Pack
San Francisco has always been a city of arrivals — people coming to the edge of the continent to escape, to invent, to become. The house music that took root here carries that same directional charge. It did not simply transplant itself from Chicago or London; it mutated, psychedelicized, queered, and salt-aired itself into something specific to this geography and its people. To study SF Bay Area house music is to study an unbroken chain of chosen communities using rhythm as a technology for survival, joy, and radical belonging.

Research Modules

M1: Queer Disco Pre-History (1975–1990)

History Track A · Foundations

Patrick Cowley, Sylvester, Megatone Records, and the hi-NRG revolution. The Trocadero Transfer, The End Up, and the Castro/SoMa gay discotheque network. The catastrophic rupture of the AIDS epidemic and the cultural continuity that survived it.

M2: The UK Invasion & Rave Golden Era (1991–1999)

Scene Track A · Golden era

Wicked Sound System's arrival from London. The Hardkiss Brothers and the psychedelic West Coast sound. Baker Beach full moon parties, warehouse raves, and the complete ecosystem of 1990s Bay Area dance music culture.

M3: Labels, Records & the SF Sound

Sound Track B · Discography

Hardkiss Music to Dirtybird Records. Om Records, Dark Entries, HNYTRX, Grayhound Recordings. The label genealogy of the Bay Area and how each expressed and transmitted the West Coast psychedelic deep house identity.

M4: Clubs, Venues & Warehouse Culture

Venue Track B · Spaces

1015 Folsom, The Mezzanine, Public Works, The Midway, The Great Northern. Sound system culture from Turbosound to Funktion-One. SoMa warehouse lineage. Gentrification pressures and the scene's adaptive responses.

M5: The Outdoor & Festival Continuum

Culture Track C · Continuum

Baker Beach to Black Rock Desert. Wicked's full moon parties, Sunset Sound System, Dirtybird BBQ and Campout. The Bay Area–PNW rave connection. Decompression, How Weird, and the contemporary queer revival. Future trajectory vectors.
