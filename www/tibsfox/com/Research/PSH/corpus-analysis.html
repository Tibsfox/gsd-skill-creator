Convergence Patterns

Modules converged in 2–8 passes. The pattern reveals two distinct groups:

  • Fast convergence (2 passes): Shipwrecks (C) and Forts (I) — highly specific vocabulary with few corpus overlaps. “Shipwreck,” “salvage,” “battery,” and “fortification” are nearly unique to PSH. These modules contribute novel vocabulary to the corpus rather than connecting to it.
  • Slow convergence (8 passes): Defense (H), Community (L), and Picnic Point (M) — broad vocabulary that touches many domains. Terms like “Boeing,” “school,” “community,” and “infrastructure” have deep cross-domain resonance.

Cross-Reference Distribution

The 1,044 cross-references distribute unevenly across modules:

The low aggregate magnitude confirms that Possession Sound Heritage is a corpus expansion project rather than a synthesis project.
