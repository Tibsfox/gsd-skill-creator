1. Vacuum Tube Amplifier Build

Advanced ELE HFE

Build a single-ended Class A vacuum tube amplifier based on the CastlePast series. This project connects analog audio heritage to modern electronics understanding.

High voltage warning: Tube amplifiers operate at 250–400V DC plate voltage. Lethal voltages are present even when unpowered (capacitor discharge). This project requires experience with high-voltage circuits.

Bill of Materials

  • 12AX7 preamp tube, EL84 or 6V6 power tube, 5Y3 rectifier tube
  • Output transformer (5K primary, 4/8/16 ohm secondary)
  • Power transformer (250V secondary, 6.3V filament winding)
  • Electrolytic capacitors (47uF/450V), film coupling caps, resistors
  • Chassis, tube sockets, terminal strips, hook-up wire

What You Learn

  • Thermionic emission and vacuum tube operating principles
  • Class A bias, load lines, and impedance matching
  • Power supply design with tube rectification and LC filtering
  • Audio signal path from grid to plate to output transformer

2. LED Controller with Arduino/ESP32

Beginner LED SHE

Build a programmable LED strip controller using an ESP32 microcontroller with WiFi control, animations, and home automation integration.

Bill of Materials

  • ESP32 development board (NodeMCU-32S or similar)
  • WS2812B addressable LED strip (5V, 60 LEDs/m)
  • 5V power supply rated for LED current (60mA per LED at full white)
  • Logic level shifter (3.3V to 5V), 470 ohm data resistor, 1000uF capacitor

What You Learn

  • PWM signal generation and addressable LED protocols (WS2812B timing)
  • ESP32 WiFi stack and web server for remote control
  • Animation algorithms: rainbow, chase, fire, breathing patterns
  • Home Assistant or MQTT integration for smart home control

3. 555 Timer Circuit Projects

Beginner T55 ELE

Three progressive builds with the NE555 timer IC — the most versatile analog IC ever made. From the Philips semiconductor heritage (the 555 was designed at Signetics, later Philips).

Three Builds

  1. Astable oscillator: Variable-frequency square wave generator with LED blinker
  2. Monostable pulse generator: Debounced button input with adjustable pulse width
  3. PWM motor controller: Variable-speed DC motor control using 555 PWM

What You Learn

  • Internal comparator, flip-flop, and discharge transistor architecture
  • RC timing networks: frequency = 1.44 / ((R1 + 2*R2) * C)
  • Duty cycle control and PWM generation without microcontrollers
  • Reading datasheets and calculating component values from specifications

4. IoT Sensor Networks — Security-First Design

Intermediate SHE AGT

Deploy a mesh of environmental sensors with security as the primary design constraint, not an afterthought. Connects to the Artemis II LIVE page infrastructure.

Bill of Materials (per node)

  • ESP32-C3 (RISC-V core with hardware crypto acceleration)
  • BME280 (temperature, humidity, pressure) or SHT31 sensor
  • SSD1306 OLED display (128x64), solar panel + LiPo battery
  • Weatherproof enclosure (IP65 rated)

What You Learn

  • TLS 1.3 on embedded devices with hardware crypto offload
  • MQTT with client certificates — no password-based auth
  • ESP-NOW mesh networking for local sensor-to-sensor communication
  • OTA firmware updates with signed image verification
  • Power budgeting for solar-powered deployment (target: indefinite runtime)

5. BESS Battery Monitoring Dashboard

Intermediate ENE SHE

Build a monitoring dashboard for Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS), tracking cell voltage, temperature, state of charge, and cycle count. Connects to the Energy Systems research cluster.

Bill of Materials

  • ESP32 with INA219 current/voltage sensor (per cell or pack)
  • DS18B20 temperature sensors (one per cell group)
  • InfluxDB + Grafana for time-series visualization
  • Relay module for emergency disconnect (over-voltage/over-temp protection)

What You Learn

  • Coulomb counting and open-circuit voltage for state-of-charge estimation
  • Cell balancing fundamentals (passive resistive balancing)
  • Thermal runaway detection and emergency disconnect protocols
  • Time-series data architecture with InfluxDB retention policies

6. RTL-SDR Radio Receiver Projects

Beginner AMR CBC

Use a $25 RTL-SDR dongle to receive everything from FM radio to aircraft ADS-B transponders, weather satellites, and the hydrogen line at 1420 MHz.

Bill of Materials

  • RTL-SDR Blog V4 dongle (0.5 MHz – 1.7 GHz)
  • Dipole antenna kit (included with V4) or DIY ground plane antenna
  • Optional: LNA (low-noise amplifier) for weak signals, bandpass filters
  • Software: SDR++ or GQRX (Linux), CubicSDR (cross-platform)

Progressive Projects

  1. FM broadcast reception: Listen to local stations, visualize in waterfall display
  2. ADS-B aircraft tracking: Receive 1090 MHz transponder data, feed to FlightAware
  3. NOAA weather satellite: Decode APT images from NOAA 15/18/19 at 137 MHz
  4. Hydrogen line 1420 MHz: Detect galactic hydrogen for radio astronomy (see DIY Science module)

7. Photonic Experiments with Fiber Optics

Intermediate ELE HPC

Explore photonic computing concepts with hands-on fiber optic experiments, connecting to the AGT research on WDM micro-resonator comb lasers and photonic integration.

Bill of Materials

  • Plastic optical fiber (POF) 1mm diameter, 10m length
  • LED transmitter and photodiode receiver modules
  • Fiber cleaver, polishing kit, and ST/SC connectors
  • Arduino for signal generation and measurement

What You Learn

  • Total internal reflection, numerical aperture, and modal dispersion
  • Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) concepts with colored LEDs
  • Optical power measurement and loss budgeting
  • Signal modulation: OOK, pulse-position, and analog intensity modulation
  • Connection to photonic computing research — light as a compute medium
