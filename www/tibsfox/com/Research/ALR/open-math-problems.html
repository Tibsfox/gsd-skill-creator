1. Riemann Hypothesis

Millennium $1M OPEN

All non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function have real part equal to 1/2.

Current State

  • Verified computationally: first 1013 zeros all lie on the critical line (Platt, 2021)
  • Montgomery-Dyson conjecture: zero spacing statistics match eigenvalue distributions of random Hermitian matrices (GUE)
  • Known consequences: prime counting function error bounds, Lindelöf hypothesis, distribution of primes in arithmetic progressions

Artemis II Connection

The RH/Montgomery-Dyson research thread explores eigenvalue spacing vs zeta zeros using GPU computation on RTX 4060 Ti. Arbitrary precision arithmetic via MPFR library for high-zero verification.

2. Collatz Conjecture

Open OPEN

For any positive integer n: if n is even, divide by 2; if odd, compute 3n+1. The conjecture states all starting values eventually reach 1.

Current State

  • Verified computationally: all integers up to 268 (~2.95 × 1020) reach 1
  • Tao (2019): proved almost all Collatz orbits attain almost boundedly low values (logarithmic density result)
  • Known difficulty: Erdős offered $500 — “mathematics is not yet ready for such problems”

Computational Approach

GPU-accelerated verification using CUDA on RTX 4060 Ti. Binary representation optimization: trailing zeros handled by bit shift, odd step is (3n+1)/2 combined. Target: extend verification frontier.

3. P vs NP

Millennium $1M OPEN CSP

Can every problem whose solution can be verified in polynomial time also be solved in polynomial time?

Current State

  • Consensus: most complexity theorists believe P ≠ NP (Gasarch survey: 81%)
  • Barriers: relativization (Baker-Gill-Solovay), natural proofs (Razborov-Rudich), algebrization (Aaronson-Wigderson)
  • Practical impact: cryptography, optimization, AI planning all depend on this separation

CS Connection

The open CS problems module explores related questions about agentic reasoning limits and code generation correctness, both of which touch complexity-theoretic boundaries.

4. Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture

Millennium $1M OPEN

The rank of an elliptic curve over the rationals equals the order of vanishing of its L-function at s = 1.

Current State

  • Rank 0 and 1: proved by Gross-Zagier and Kolyvagin (1980s)
  • Higher ranks: wide open; largest known rank is 29 (Elkies, 2024 update)
  • Computational evidence: extensive numerical verification for curves of moderate conductor

5. Navier-Stokes Existence and Smoothness

Millennium $1M OPEN

Do smooth solutions to the 3D incompressible Navier-Stokes equations always exist, and do they remain smooth for all time?

Current State

  • 2D case: solved (Ladyzhenskaya, 1960s) — smooth solutions exist globally
  • 3D case: open; partial results for small data, short time, or with additional regularity
  • Tao (2016): constructed finite-time blowup for an averaged version of the equations

6. Yang-Mills Mass Gap

Millennium $1M OPEN

Prove that quantum Yang-Mills theory exists on R4 and has a positive mass gap.

Current State

  • Lattice QCD: numerical evidence strongly supports the mass gap
  • Constructive QFT: rigorous construction exists in 2D and 3D; 4D remains open
  • Physical evidence: glueball mass spectra from lattice calculations agree with experimental bounds

7. Twin Prime Conjecture

Open OPEN

There are infinitely many pairs of primes that differ by 2 (twin primes: 3–5, 5–7, 11–13, ...).

Current State

  • Zhang (2013): proved infinitely many prime pairs with gap ≤ 70,000,000
  • Maynard-Tao (2014): reduced gap bound to 246 using Selberg sieve optimization
  • Polymath8b: current best is gap ≤ 246 (unconditionally); gap ≤ 6 under Elliott-Halberstam
  • Gap of 2: remains open and may require fundamentally new techniques

8. Goldbach Conjecture

Open OPEN

Every even integer greater than 2 can be expressed as the sum of two primes.

Current State

  • Ternary Goldbach: proved by Helfgott (2013) — every odd integer > 5 is the sum of three primes
  • Binary Goldbach: verified computationally to 4 × 1018
  • Chen (1966): every sufficiently large even number is the sum of a prime and a semiprime (product of at most two primes)

9. ABC Conjecture

Disputed OPEN

For coprime positive integers a + b = c, the product of distinct prime factors of abc is usually not much smaller than c.

Current State

  • Mochizuki (2012): claimed proof via Inter-universal Teichmüller theory (IUT); 600+ pages
  • Controversy: proof remains disputed; Scholze and Stix identified a gap in Corollary 3.12 (2018)
  • Published (2021): PRIMS journal accepted the paper, but mathematical community consensus remains divided
  • Implications if true: would resolve Fermat-Catalan, Szpiro, and many other conjectures

10. Hodge Conjecture

Millennium $1M OPEN

Every Hodge class on a projective complex algebraic variety is a rational linear combination of classes of algebraic cycles.

Current State

  • Known cases: true for divisors (Lefschetz 1-1 theorem) and in dimension ≤ 3
  • Counterexample attempts: Grothendieck generalized Hodge conjecture has known counterexamples
  • Approach: heavily algebraic geometry; connects to motives, derived categories, and intersection theory
