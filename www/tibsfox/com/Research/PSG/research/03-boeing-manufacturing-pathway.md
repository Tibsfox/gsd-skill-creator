## Overview

Boeing's Everett complex is the anchor of Snohomish County's manufacturing economy. With 30,000+ workers across three shifts operating seven days a week in the world's largest building by volume, the factory represents both the demand side of the workforce pipeline and the economic engine that justifies the Pacific Spine corridor concept. This module traces the complete pathway from pre-employment training through journey-level Boeing employment, documenting each institutional node and the gaps between them.

> The Boeing Everett complex accounts for approximately $19 billion in economic output. This is not an industry cluster -- it is a single-employer economic gravity well. The workforce pipeline that feeds it determines the region's economic health.

---

## Paine Field and the Boeing Everett Complex

### The World's Largest Building

The Boeing Everett Factory is the world's largest building by volume at over 472 million cubic feet, covering 98.3 acres within a 1,000-acre complex adjacent to Seattle Paine Field International Airport.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Volume | 472+ million cubic feet |
| Footprint | 98.3 acres |
| Total Complex | ~1,000 acres |
| Separate Buildings | Up to 200 |
| Workforce | 30,000+ across 3 shifts |
| Operating Schedule | 7 days/week |
| Aircraft Delivered | 5,000+ widebody since 1967 |
| Adjacent Airport | Seattle Paine Field International (PAE) |

### Paine Field Evolution

Paine Field was renamed Seattle Paine Field International Airport in July 2023 and handled 580,000 passengers in 2024. The airport's dual identity -- commercial passenger service alongside Boeing's manufacturing operations -- reflects the density of aerospace activity in the Paine Field corridor.

### Manufacturing Industrial Center (MIC)

The Paine Field/Boeing Everett Manufacturing Industrial Center accounts for approximately 42,000 covered jobs. This designation by the Puget Sound Regional Council (PSRC) formalizes the complex's role as a regional employment center and enables infrastructure investment coordination.

## Current Production Programs

### Active Production Lines (2026)

| Aircraft | Status | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| 767 Freighter | Active -- ending 2027 | Final wide-body freighter orders |
| 777 | Active | Ongoing deliveries to global airlines |
| 777X | In development | 500+ orders, next-generation widebody |
| MAX 10 | Hiring began January 2026 | New Everett production line |

### 767 Freighter Wind-Down

The 767 freighter line is scheduled to end production in 2027. This wind-down will free production space and workforce capacity, creating a transition window where workers from the 767 line can be retrained for 777X or MAX 10 production. The timing aligns with the Machinists Institute's training ramp-up in Everett.

### MAX 10 Everett Launch

As of January 2026, Boeing began hiring for the MAX 10 production line in Everett. This represents a significant expansion of narrow-body production at the Everett site, which has historically focused on wide-body aircraft. The MAX 10 hiring creates immediate demand for trained machinists and structures mechanics -- exactly the roles that BPET and the Machinists Institute prepare workers for.

> Boeing began hiring for the MAX 10 line in Everett in January 2026. The Machinists Institute opened in June 2025. The timeline is not coincidental -- the training infrastructure is being built to feed the production expansion.

## Boeing Pre-Employment Training (BPET)

### Program Structure

BPET is a certified training pathway where students completing approved courses receive direct offers of employment as Aircraft Structures Mechanics in Everett and Renton. This is not a referral program or a job fair -- it is a guaranteed employment pipeline for qualified completers.

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Program Name | Boeing Pre-Employment Training (BPET) |
| Outcome | Direct offer of employment |
| Role | Aircraft Structures Mechanic |
| Locations | Everett and Renton |
| Entry Pay | $25.32/hour |
| Journey Pay | Up to $54.76/hour (CBA) |

### Role Responsibilities

Aircraft Structures Mechanics hired through BPET perform:

- Drilling and fastening complex structures including composite materials
- Operating hand and machine tools including automated equipment
- Verifying kitted parts against installation plans and bill of materials
- Assembly of structural components per engineering specifications
- Quality inspection at work station level

### Compensation Structure

| Level | Hourly Rate | Annual (est.) |
|-------|-------------|---------------|
| Entry (BPET hire) | $25.32 | ~$52,700 |
| Mid-level | ~$38.00 | ~$79,000 |
| Journey-level (top) | $54.76 | ~$113,900 |

The pay progression from entry to journey-level represents a 116% increase. This trajectory -- from $52,700 to $113,900 -- is achievable without a four-year degree. For students from Sno-Isle TECH's Aerospace Manufacturing program, this represents a concrete economic outcome: hands-on training at 16-17 years old leading to six-figure earning potential by their late twenties.

### BPET-Sno-Isle Connection

The direct connection between Sno-Isle TECH's Aerospace Manufacturing program and BPET qualification is the most important workforce pipeline junction in the Pacific Spine corridor. A student who enrolls in Aerospace Manufacturing at Sno-Isle TECH as a junior, completes the program, meets BPET requirements, and receives a direct Boeing employment offer has traversed the complete education-to-employment pipeline in approximately 2-3 years.

## IAM Local 751 Machinists Institute

### Everett Facility

The Machinists Institute opened a 20,000-square-foot, three-story training facility in Everett in June 2025. IAM Local 751 represents approximately 33,000 Boeing machinists in Washington.

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Location | Everett, WA |
| Size | 20,000 sq ft, 3 stories |
| Opened | June 2025 |
| Operator | IAM Local 751 |
| Members Represented | ~33,000 Boeing machinists |
| Significance | First Local 751 Everett training program |

### Training Capabilities

The Machinists Institute's Everett facility provides:

- **VR-equipped training rooms** with robotic arms for immersive skill development
- **Virtual reality spray-painting simulators** for aircraft finishing techniques
- **Virtual reality welding simulators** for joint fabrication practice
- **Manual machining equipment** including milling machines for hands-on metal work
- **Blueprint reading curriculum** for interpreting engineering drawings
- **Assembly line quality control** for Boeing production standards
- **Large auditorium** for union meetings and community events

### Direct Employment Pathway

The Everett Machinists Institute program offers a direct Boeing employment pathway -- the first such program Local 751 has operated in Everett. Previously, Machinists Institute training was available only at other locations, requiring Everett-area workers to travel for training. The new facility places training infrastructure within the same geographic corridor as the employment destination.

> The Machinists Institute opened in Everett in June 2025. This is the first time Local 751 has operated a training program in Everett -- the city where 30,000+ Boeing workers are employed. The institutional gap between training location and employment location has been closed.

### VR Training as Force Multiplier

The virtual reality training capabilities at the Machinists Institute represent a significant advance over traditional classroom instruction. VR spray-painting allows students to practice aircraft finishing techniques without consuming materials or risking defects on actual aircraft. VR welding provides infinite practice repetitions with immediate feedback. These capabilities reduce the time from enrollment to competency, compressing the training pipeline.

## The Machinist Shortage

### National Projections

A projected U.S. shortfall of over 2 million machinists by 2030 was documented in a 2021 Deloitte / Manufacturing Institute study. This projection was cited by U.S. Senator Maria Cantwell at the Machinists Institute's Everett facility opening in June 2025.

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Projected Shortfall (2030) | 2+ million machinists |
| Source | Deloitte / The Manufacturing Institute (2021) |
| Context | Aging workforce, insufficient pipeline |
| Regional Impact | Direct threat to Boeing production capacity |

### Why the Shortage Matters

The machinist shortage is not a hypothetical future concern. Boeing's Everett facility needs machinists today. The 777X production ramp, the MAX 10 Everett launch, and ongoing 777/767 operations all require trained machinists. Every unfilled machinist position represents either delayed production, overtime costs, or quality risk from undertrained workers.

The Pacific Spine corridor's workforce pipeline -- from Sno-Isle TECH through BPET and the Machinists Institute to Boeing employment -- is the regional response to a national crisis. The infrastructure exists. The question is whether the pipeline can scale to meet demand.

## Regional Training Ecosystem

### Training Institutions at or Near Paine Field

The Boeing Everett complex has attracted a constellation of training institutions to the Paine Field corridor:

| Institution | Programs | Connection |
|-------------|----------|------------|
| Washington Aerospace Training & Research Center | Aerospace manufacturing, composites | Paine Field campus |
| Center of Excellence for Aerospace & Advanced Manufacturing | Industry coordination, curriculum alignment | Statewide with local presence |
| Aerospace Joint Apprenticeship Committee (AJAC) | Registered apprenticeship for aerospace trades | Statewide, Boeing-aligned |
| Edmonds Community College | Aerospace and advanced manufacturing programs | 10 miles south |
| Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University (Everett) | Associate through master's degrees in aviation/aerospace | Paine Field campus |
| Sno-Isle TECH Skills Center | 22 CTE programs including Aerospace Manufacturing | Adjacent to Paine Field |
| IAM Local 751 Machinists Institute | Boeing-direct machinist training | Everett facility |

### Ecosystem Density

The concentration of aerospace training institutions within a 10-mile radius of Paine Field is remarkable. No other corridor in the United States has this density of aerospace-specific training infrastructure adjacent to a major manufacturing facility. The challenge is not capacity -- it is coordination. These institutions serve overlapping populations with partially aligned curricula, and no single entity manages the end-to-end pipeline.

> Seven training institutions within 10 miles of Paine Field. Each with its own enrollment process, its own curriculum, its own industry advisory structure. The connective tissue between them -- a shared enrollment pipeline, credential stacking, transparent pathways -- does not yet exist at scale.

### Pipeline Gaps

| Gap | Description | Impact |
|-----|-------------|--------|
| Credential Fragmentation | Each institution issues separate credentials | Students navigate multiple enrollment systems |
| Housing Barrier | No residential option for rural students | Limits Sno-Isle TECH access for distant districts |
| Information Asymmetry | Students unaware of full pipeline | Underutilization of BPET and Machinists Institute |
| Scheduling Conflicts | Split-day model limits immersion | Students cannot do full-day intensive training |
| Post-18 Transition | No structured handoff from Sno-Isle to BPET | Students must self-navigate the next step |

## Economic Impact

### The $19 Billion Engine

The Boeing Everett complex represents approximately $19 billion in economic output for Snohomish County. This figure encompasses direct Boeing employment, supply chain spending, and multiplier effects across the regional economy.

| Category | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Direct Employment | 30,000+ Boeing Everett workers |
| MIC Employment | 42,000 covered jobs |
| Economic Output | ~$19 billion |
| Supply Chain | 600+ companies in Snohomish County |
| Aerospace Workforce Share | 46% of Washington State |

### Single-Employer Concentration

The concentration of economic activity around a single employer creates both opportunity and risk. The opportunity is clear: a reliable demand signal for workforce development investment. The risk is equally clear: any disruption to Boeing production -- strikes, regulatory delays, supply chain failures, program cancellations -- reverberates through the entire regional economy.

## 777X and Future Production

### 777X Status

The 777X program has 500+ orders and represents Boeing's next-generation widebody offering. Production ramp in Everett will require significant workforce expansion, creating sustained demand for trained machinists and structures mechanics throughout the late 2020s and into the 2030s.

### Production Transition Window

The convergence of 767 wind-down (ending 2027), 777X ramp-up, and MAX 10 Everett launch creates a production transition window where workforce development investment has maximum leverage. Workers transitioning from the 767 line need retraining. New 777X and MAX 10 lines need fresh hires. The Machinists Institute and BPET pipeline must operate at full capacity during this window.

## Integration with Pacific Spine

### The Three Conversations

The Boeing manufacturing pathway is central to all three of the Pacific Spine's founding conversations:

1. **Sno-Isle TECH conversation** -- How can the skills center's Aerospace Manufacturing program better align with BPET requirements and Machinists Institute offerings?
2. **Boeing conversation** -- What does Boeing need from the workforce pipeline, and how can the corridor integrate housing and training infrastructure to meet that need?
3. **Tulalip Tribes conversation** -- Tribal workforce development programs can connect to the Boeing pipeline through the same training institutions, creating economic opportunity within tribal communities.

### FoxEdu Design Reference

The Boeing manufacturing pathway provides the concrete template for FoxEdu design. A functioning education-to-employment pipeline that produces workers earning $25-55/hour without requiring a four-year degree is the model the corridor should replicate across other sectors.
