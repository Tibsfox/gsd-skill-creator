## Overview

Sno-Isle TECH Skills Center is a functioning public technical high school at 9001 Airport Road in Everett, Washington, near Paine Field. It is not an abstraction in a workforce development report. It is a building where juniors and seniors from 14 school districts learn aerospace manufacturing, maritime vessel operations, welding, nursing, and 18 other trade programs. Understanding what Sno-Isle TECH actually is -- its programs, its constraints, its geographic reach -- is prerequisite to any conversation about workforce pipeline development in Snohomish County.

> This module documents the institution as it exists today. The gap between what Sno-Isle TECH produces and what Boeing, the trades, and the healthcare system need is the space where Fox Infrastructure Group's educational vision must operate.

## Career Pathway Architecture

### Five Pathways Model

Sno-Isle TECH organizes its 22 programs into five career pathways. This architecture maps to Washington State's Career and Technical Education (CTE) framework and connects to both postsecondary education and direct employment.

```
INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY (4 programs)
    |
    +-- Networking → CompTIA → IT careers
    +-- Electronics → Engineering tech → Manufacturing
    +-- Game Design → Digital media → Software
    +-- Animation → Visual effects → Entertainment

BUSINESS & MARKETING (2 programs)
    |
    +-- Fashion → Retail → Business management
    +-- Culinary → Hospitality → Restaurant/food service

HUMAN SERVICES (4 programs)
    |
    +-- Early Childhood → Education → K-12 teaching
    +-- Cosmetology → Licensing → Salon ownership
    +-- Criminal Justice → Academy → Law enforcement
    +-- Fire Fighting → EMT → Fire department

SCIENCE & HEALTH (6 programs)
    |
    +-- Nursing → CNA → RN pathway
    +-- Dental/Medical Assisting → Clinical roles
    +-- Pharmacy Tech → Certification → Pharmacy
    +-- Veterinary → Vet tech → Animal care
    +-- Healthcare Careers → Exploration → Specialization

TRADE & INDUSTRY (6 programs)
    |
    +-- Aerospace Mfg → BPET → Boeing employment
    +-- Advanced Mfg → CNC/CAM → Manufacturing
    +-- Construction → Apprenticeship → Building trades
    +-- Welding → Certification → Fabrication
    +-- Diesel/Auto → ASE certification → Automotive
    +-- Maritime → USCG licensing → Marine careers
```

### Dual Credit Pipeline

Programs offer dual credit through partnerships with community and technical colleges in the region. Students can earn college credits while completing high school, reducing the total time to a postsecondary credential. This pipeline is particularly significant for the Aerospace Manufacturing program, where dual credits can count toward the coursework required for Boeing Pre-Employment Training (BPET) eligibility.

### The Commute Problem

The geographic spread of member districts creates a significant barrier. A student from Darrington departs at 6:25 AM and returns by 11:15 AM for the AM session. Students from Whidbey Island (Oak Harbor) must cross water to reach the campus. This commute burden falls disproportionately on rural students -- precisely the students who often have the most to gain from trade and technical education.

> The Darrington bus departs at 6:25 AM. That is a 55-mile commute each way on mountain roads. A residential education model using modular housing near Paine Field could eliminate this barrier entirely. See Module 4 (Barracks Housing Model) for the analysis.

## Foundation and Support Model

### Sno-Isle Skills Center Foundation

The Sno-Isle Skills Center Foundation is a separate nonprofit entity that supports students through scholarships and resources, with the explicit mission of eliminating financial barriers to career and technical education.

Key functions:
- **Scholarships** for students who cannot cover lab fees or equipment costs
- **Tool and equipment grants** for programs requiring personal tool sets
- **Industry partnerships** connecting foundation funding to employer needs
- **Community outreach** building awareness of CTE pathways

> The foundation model matters because it demonstrates that the institution has a support structure beyond tax funding. When financial barriers prevent a student from rural Darrington from participating in Aerospace Manufacturing, the foundation exists to close that gap.

Sno-Isle TECH relaunched its summer school program in the 2025/2026 cycle, offering 3-week introductory courses for incoming 9th through 12th grade students. This expansion is significant because it extends the institution's reach to younger students (freshmen and sophomores) who are not yet eligible for regular programs.

| Feature | Detail |

| Eligibility | Incoming 9th-12th grade students |

## Advisory Committee Structure

### Industry-Education Connection

Each Sno-Isle TECH program maintains an advisory committee composed of industry professionals, educators, and community representatives. These committees ensure that curriculum remains aligned with current industry practices and employer needs.

Advisory committee functions include:
- **Curriculum review** -- ensuring program content matches current industry standards
- **Equipment recommendations** -- advising on tools, software, and machinery that reflect workplace reality
- **Employment connections** -- facilitating direct pipelines from program completion to hiring
- **Industry trend monitoring** -- identifying emerging skills and technologies for program evolution

The advisory committee structure is the institutional mechanism that keeps Sno-Isle TECH's programs relevant. Without it, curricula drift away from employer needs. With it, the skills center produces graduates who are employable on day one.

## Workforce Pipeline Analysis

### The Gap

Sno-Isle TECH produces skilled graduates. Boeing needs skilled workers. The Machinists Institute trains for specific Boeing roles. But these three institutions operate independently without a unified pathway from high school enrollment through journey-level employment.

```
CURRENT STATE (fragmented):

Student → Sno-Isle TECH → [GAP] → BPET → [GAP] → Boeing Employment
                                     |
                          Machinists Institute → [GAP] → Boeing Employment

DESIRED STATE (integrated):

Student → Sno-Isle TECH → BPET/Machinists → Boeing Employment
              |                    |                    |
         Credential            Direct-hire         Journey-level
         Stacking              Pipeline            Progression
```

### Quantifying the Pipeline

| Stage | Current Capacity | Bottleneck |
|-------|-----------------|------------|
| Sno-Isle TECH enrollment | 22 programs, ~1,000+ students/year | Rural access (commute barrier) |
| BPET qualification | Unknown pass rate | Program awareness, prerequisite alignment |
| Machinists Institute intake | New facility (June 2025), capacity ramping | First Everett operation; pipeline not yet established |
| Boeing hiring | 30,000+ Everett workforce | Economic cycles, production schedules |

## Integration with Pacific Spine

### Role in the Corridor

Sno-Isle TECH is the entry point of the Pacific Spine's workforce pipeline. The institution already exists, already trains in the right disciplines, and already sits adjacent to the Boeing Everett complex. The question is not whether to create a workforce development system -- it is how to integrate the one that already exists with the manufacturing pathway (Module 3) and the housing infrastructure (Module 4) needed to remove access barriers.

### FoxEdu Alignment

The Fox Infrastructure Group's FoxEdu layer envisions corridor-trained builders with credentials recognized across the Pacific Spine. Sno-Isle TECH's existing dual-credit and industry certification model provides the institutional foundation for this vision. The gap is credential portability -- ensuring that certifications earned at Sno-Isle TECH are recognized and valued throughout the corridor.
