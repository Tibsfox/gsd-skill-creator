EnviSmart: Robust Multi-Agent Workflows for Environmental Data

arXiv: 2604.01647

Authors: Guan et al.

Date: April 2026 · Categories: cs.AI, cs.DB · Venue: PEARC 2026

Abstract

EnviSmart introduces a three-track knowledge architecture with role-separated multi-agent workflows and deterministic validators for environmental data management. Evaluated on SF2Bench (2,452 stations, 8,557 files, 39 years of data), the system detects coordinate errors before publication. The three-track architecture separates data ingestion, quality validation, and knowledge synthesis into independently verifiable pipelines.

Key Findings

  1. Three-track knowledge architecture: ingestion, validation, synthesis
  2. Role-separated multi-agent design with deterministic validators
  3. 2,452 stations, 8,557 files, 39 years in SF2Bench
  4. Coordinate error detection catches geospatial mistakes before publication
  5. Deterministic validators provide guarantees that LLM-based validators cannot
  6. PEARC 2026 publication validates the approach for high-performance environmental computing

Implications for Our Architecture

The three-track architecture maps to our sweep.py weather data pipeline: we ingest from METAR/NWS/CWOP/NDBC sources, validate coordinates and values, then synthesize into the weather-map. The deterministic validator concept validates our use of hooks (deterministic) alongside LLM-based agents (non-deterministic). SF2Bench's scale (39 years, 8,557 files) is comparable to our PNW data sources (46 datasets, 14 categories). The role-separated multi-agent design aligns with our GSD executor/verifier/planner separation.
