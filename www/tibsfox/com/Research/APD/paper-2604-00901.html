HERA: Experience as Compass for Multi-Agent RAG Orchestration

arXiv: 2604.00901

Authors: Li, Ramakrishnan

Date: April 2026 · Categories: cs.AI, cs.IR

Abstract

HERA introduces a hierarchical framework for jointly evolving multi-agent RAG topology and agent prompts. Reward-guided sampling generates query-specific topologies rather than fixed architectures. Achieves 38.69% improvement across six benchmarks through emergent self-organization in sparse networks. The key insight is that RAG orchestration should adapt per-query, not be fixed at design time.

Key Findings

  1. 38.69% improvement across six RAG benchmarks
  2. Joint evolution of topology and agent prompts outperforms fixed architectures
  3. Reward-guided sampling generates query-specific topologies
  4. Emergent self-organization produces sparse, efficient networks
  5. Hierarchical framework separates topology selection from agent behavior
  6. Fixed architectures underperform adaptive orchestration by significant margins

Implications for Our Architecture

Validates the adaptive dispatch pattern in our mayor-coordinator and sling-dispatch skills. Our current orchestration selects agents based on task type — HERA suggests we should evolve the agent topology per-query. The 38.69% improvement is substantial enough to justify implementing reward-guided agent selection in our fleet-mission pattern. The emergent self-organization finding supports our convoy model's observation that optimal team composition emerges through iteration, not upfront design.
