BibTeX Citation Hallucinations in Scientific Publishing Agents

arXiv: 2604.03159

Date: April 2026 · Categories: cs.CL, cs.DL

Abstract

A taxonomy of citation fabrication patterns in LLM-generated BibTeX entries used by scientific publishing agents. The paper provides agent-by-agent comparison of hallucination rates, develops a citation verification pipeline, and proposes mitigation strategies for research automation. Fabrication patterns include plausible-but-nonexistent papers, wrong-author attribution, incorrect venue/year combinations, and DOI fabrication.

Key Findings

  1. Fabrication taxonomy: nonexistent papers, wrong authors, incorrect venues, DOI fabrication
  2. Agent-by-agent comparison reveals significant variation in hallucination rates
  3. Citation verification pipeline catches most fabrications but adds latency
  4. Plausible-but-nonexistent papers are the hardest to detect automatically
  5. DOI fabrication exploits the fact that DOI format is predictable
  6. Mitigation strategies reduce but cannot fully eliminate citation hallucinations

Implications for Our Architecture

Directly applicable to our research-engine and publish-pipeline. Our 190+ completed research missions generate thousands of citations — each is a potential hallucination vector. We should add a BibTeX verification stage to the publish-pipeline that resolves DOIs, checks CrossRef, and flags plausible-but-unverified entries. The fabrication taxonomy should be encoded in the data-fidelity skill's detection patterns. Priority: DOI resolution is cheap and catches the easiest fabrications first.
