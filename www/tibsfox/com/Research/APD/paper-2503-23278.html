MCP Landscape: Security Threats & Future Research

arXiv: 2503.23278

Authors: Hou et al.

Date: March 2025 · Categories: cs.CR, cs.AI

Abstract

A landscape survey of the MCP ecosystem identifying 12 distinct threat categories, 16 threat scenarios, and 47 specific attack vectors. The survey maps the gap between MCP's rapid adoption (110M SDK downloads, 3,600x growth since November 2024) and the maturity of its security tooling. Categorizes attackers into four types: malicious developers, external attackers, users, and security flaws. Proposes safeguards organized by lifecycle phase.

Key Findings

  1. 110M MCP SDK downloads as of early 2025
  2. 3,600× adoption growth since November 2024
  3. 16 distinct threat scenarios across 4 lifecycle phases
  4. 47 specific attack vectors identified
  5. 4 attacker categories: malicious developers, external attackers, users, security flaws
  6. Supply-chain compromise and credential leakage have the highest real-world impact scores
  7. Security tooling remains at “early prototype” maturity for most categories

Implications for Our Architecture

The adoption-security gap is directly relevant to our skill marketplace design. With 110M SDK downloads and growing, MCP will replicate npm/PyPI supply-chain crises at a larger scale due to higher privilege levels. Our skill-integration system should implement lifecycle-phase-aware security: creation-time validation, discovery-time provenance checking, operation-time sandboxing, and update-time dependency analysis. The four attacker categories should map to threat models in our security-hygiene skill.
