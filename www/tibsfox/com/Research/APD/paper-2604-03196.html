Code Review Agents: Industry Claims vs Empirical Reality

arXiv: 2604.03196

Authors: Chowdhury et al.

Date: April 2026 · Categories: cs.SE, cs.AI

Abstract

An empirical study of 19,450 pull requests on the AIDev platform comparing code review agent performance against human reviewers. CRA-only PRs achieve 45.20% merge rate versus 68.37% for human-only reviews. Signal-to-noise ratio sits in the 0-30% range for most agents, with 60.2% of CRA-generated PRs being closed without merge. 12 of 13 evaluated agents fall below 60% signal quality.

Key Findings

  1. 19,450 PRs analyzed on AIDev platform
  2. 45.20% merge rate for CRA-only PRs vs 68.37% for human-only
  3. 60.2% of CRA-generated PRs closed without merge
  4. 12/13 agents fall below 60% signal quality threshold
  5. Signal-to-noise ratio in 0-30% range for most agents
  6. Industry marketing claims significantly overstate actual performance

Methodology

Large-scale empirical study on AIDev platform analyzing 19,450 PRs across three conditions: CRA-only (agent reviews without human involvement), human-only (traditional review), and CRA-assisted (agent findings reviewed by humans). Signal-to-noise measured as the ratio of actionable comments to total comments. Merge rate tracked as the primary outcome metric.

Implications for Our Architecture

Validation

Validates our adversarial-pr-review and code-review skill design: human-in-the-loop is essential, not optional. The 45.2% vs 68.37% gap quantifies the cost of fully automated review.

Design Principles

  • Use agents for signal generation (finding issues), humans for signal evaluation (deciding on fixes)
  • The 0-30% signal-to-noise range means most agent comments are noise — present as suggestions, never auto-merge
  • 12/13 agents below 60% signal quality confirms the need for adversarial review of the reviewer itself
  • Our issue-triage-pr-review skill already follows this pattern; this paper quantifies why it's right
