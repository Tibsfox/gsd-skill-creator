Skills in the Wild: Benchmarking Agentic Skills in Realistic Settings

arXiv: 2604.04323

Authors: Liu et al.

Date: April 2026 · Categories: cs.AI, cs.SE

Abstract

A large-scale empirical study of 34,000 real-world agentic skills across progressive challenge levels. Performance degrades as skill complexity increases, approaching no-skill baseline performance at the highest challenge tiers. Query-specific refinement recovers performance from 57.7% to 65.5%, demonstrating that skill curation and adaptation are essential for maintaining effectiveness at scale.

Key Findings

  1. 34,000 real-world skills benchmarked across progressive challenge levels
  2. Performance degradation approaches no-skill baseline at highest complexity
  3. 57.7% baseline performance before query-specific refinement
  4. 65.5% recovered performance with refinement — 7.8 percentage point improvement
  5. Progressive challenge levels reveal where skill abstractions break down
  6. Skill curation and adaptation are essential at scale — raw skill count is insufficient

Methodology

Large-scale benchmark with 34K real-world skills collected from public repositories and marketplace platforms. Progressive challenge levels test skills at increasing complexity: simple tool invocations, multi-step workflows, error recovery, and adversarial inputs. Performance measured against a no-skill baseline (agent without any skill definitions) and an oracle (manually curated skill set). Query-specific refinement adapts skill descriptions per-query before invocation.

Implications for Our Architecture

Skill Philosophy Validation

Directly validates our skill design philosophy: we have 34 curated skills, not 34,000 uncurated ones. The degradation-to-baseline finding confirms that skill quality matters more than quantity.

Specific Insights

  • Our skill-integration system's bounded learning guarantees prevent the complexity explosion this paper documents
  • The 57.7% to 65.5% recovery via query-specific refinement maps to our session-awareness skill's context-dependent activation
  • Progressive challenge levels provide a testing framework for our own skill suite
  • The no-skill baseline comparison should be run against our system to measure actual skill contribution
