Security Threat Modeling: MCP, A2A, Agora, ANP

arXiv: 2602.11327

Authors: Anbiaee et al.

Date: February 2026 · Categories: cs.CR, cs.DC

Abstract

Comparative threat modeling across four agent communication protocols using STRIDE-based analysis. MCP optimizes for tool integration but lacks built-in mutual authentication. A2A provides stronger identity guarantees at higher latency. Agora enables marketplace discovery at the cost of trust-on-first-use vulnerabilities. ANP offers the most complete security model but has the lowest adoption. The paper identifies 12 protocol-level risks across creation, operation, and update lifecycle phases.

Key Findings

  1. 12 protocol-level risks identified across 4 agent communication protocols
  2. 4 lifecycle phases analyzed: creation, operation, update, and discovery
  3. MCP scores worst on Spoofing and Elevation of Privilege due to trust-the-transport design
  4. ANP scores worst on Denial of Service due to complex handshake requirements
  5. No single protocol provides complete security — layering is required
  6. First protocol-centric risk assessment framework for agentic systems

Implications for Our Architecture

Validates our multi-protocol approach: MCP for tool binding, with trust-relationship.ts providing the mutual authentication layer that MCP lacks. The wrong-provider tool execution measurement directly applies to our skill loading — we should verify skill provider identity, not just skill content. The lifecycle phase model (creation/operation/update) maps to our skill installation, execution, and update workflows.
