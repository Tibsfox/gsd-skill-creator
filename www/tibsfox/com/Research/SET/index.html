Research Modules

M1: Foundations & Institutions

History Track A · Timeline & institutions

Project Ozma (1960) through 3I/ATLAS (2025). The Wow! signal, SETI Institute, Breakthrough Listen, Allen Telescope Array, Berkeley SETI, Penn State PSETI, NASA Technosignatures SAG, SETI@home, and the COSMIC system at the VLA.

M2: Detection Methodologies

Detection Track A · Instruments & algorithms

Radio SETI and the water hole. Narrowband Doppler-drifting signals, TurboSETI, COSMIC at the VLA, AI-accelerated processing (600x speed, 10x false-positive reduction), LaserSETI, optical SETI, and RFI mitigation strategies.

M3: Technosignature Science

Techno Track B · Passive detection

Dyson sphere detection and Project Hephaistos. Seven M-dwarf candidates, dust-obscured galaxy contamination, interstellar object 3I/ATLAS search, the Earth Detecting Earth framework, waste-heat cosmology, and Kardashev scale classification.

M4: Biosignatures & Astrobiology

Bio Track B · Life detection

JWST atmospheric spectroscopy, K2-18b DMS/DMDS controversy (Cambridge vs. NASA/JPL), Hycean worlds, 15 biosignature gases, Europa Clipper, the Extremely Large Telescope (2030), and the Habitable Worlds Observatory (2040s).

M5: Theoretical Frameworks

Theory Sequential · Mathematical models

The Drake Equation and Stern/Gerya 2024 plate-tectonics update. Fermi Paradox resolution hypotheses, the Great Filter, Rare Earth, Zoo and Dark Forest hypotheses, AI as Great Filter, and the Kardashev energy scale.

M6: Synthesis — Signal, Silence, and the Spaces

Synthesis Sequential · Cross-module integration

What the collective null results mean. Information-theoretic framing of SETI as signal-in-noise. The Amiga Principle applied to cosmic search architecture. DACP parallels. The Great Filter's position and what 2025 data suggests about our future.
