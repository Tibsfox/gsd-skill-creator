City of Shoreline

Light rail arrived in 2024 — two stations, a saltwater beach, and an electric railway's ghost
47.7557°N 122.3426°W · King County · Elevation: 480 ft

Shoreline was unincorporated King County until 1995 — a strip of neighborhoods between Seattle and Edmonds that didn't even have its own name until the school district gave it one. Then light rail arrived in 2024, and suddenly two new stations turned a quiet suburb into a transit destination. The Interurban Trail remembers what Shoreline forgot: there was a railway here once before.

Historical Timeline

Named for a school district, incorporated by vote, transformed by light rail.

1906 Seattle-Everett Interurban electric railway begins service through the area
1927 Shoreline School District established — the name that sticks
1939 Interurban railway ceases operation; corridor later becomes a trail
1964 Shoreline Community College founded
1995 Incorporated as the City of Shoreline after voter approval
2000s Aurora Avenue (SR-99) corridor development and improvement
2024 Shoreline South/148th and Shoreline North/185th Link stations open (Aug 30)

Parks & Green Spaces

Saltwater beach access, neighborhood parks, and a historic trail corridor.

Richmond Beach Saltwater Park

saltwater beach40 acres

A 40-acre park descending through forest to a Puget Sound beach at the foot of the bluffs. The beach offers views of the Olympic Mountains, passing ships, and spectacular sunsets. Trails wind through second-growth forest with interpretive signs. One of the few public saltwater beaches in King County north of Seattle. Restrooms and picnic facilities at the beach level.

Access: NW 196th St, Richmond Beach neighborhood

Interurban Trail

regionalhistoric corridor

A paved multi-use trail following the corridor of the former Seattle-Everett Interurban electric railway (1906–1939). The trail runs north-south through Shoreline, connecting to Lynnwood to the north and Seattle to the south. A living memorial to the electric railway era — the original light rail that connected the region a century before Link.

Hamlin Park

foresttrails

An 80-acre forested park with trails through mixed conifer and deciduous woodland. One of the largest forested parks in Shoreline, providing habitat for owls, pileated woodpeckers, and native songbirds. A quiet refuge in the city's eastern neighborhoods.

Shoreview Park

sportsplayground

A community park with sports fields, playground, basketball courts, and picnic areas. The park hosts youth sports leagues and community events. Located in central Shoreline with easy access from residential neighborhoods.

Cromwell Park

neighborhoodplayground

A neighborhood park with playground, open lawn, and walking paths. Part of Shoreline's network of distributed neighborhood parks that provide local recreation access within walking distance of most homes.

Lakes & Water Features

Echo Lake

lakeneighborhood

A small urban lake near Aurora Avenue surrounded by residential development. Echo Lake provides a green respite along the SR-99 corridor. Limited public access but visible from surrounding streets and the Aurora transit corridor.

Twin Ponds Park

pondsplayground

A park centered on two retention ponds with a playground, sports courts, and walking paths. The ponds support urban wildlife including ducks, frogs, and dragonflies. A community gathering space with covered picnic areas.

Ronald Bog

wetlandnatural area

A preserved wetland area providing urban wildlife habitat and storm water management. The bog supports a unique lowland wetland ecosystem within the developed suburban landscape. Trail access around the perimeter.

Notable Landmarks

Link Light Rail Stations

1 Lineopened 2024

Two Link stations opened on August 30, 2024: Shoreline South/148th and Shoreline North/185th. Both serve the 1 Line connecting Lynnwood to Angle Lake (and eventually Federal Way). The stations are catalyzing major development — over 3,000 housing units near 148th and 1,700 near 185th are completed or in the pipeline.

Shoreline Community College

educationest. 1964

A two-year community college serving approximately 10,000 students with transfer programs, workforce training, and continuing education. The campus sits on a 83-acre wooded site in central Shoreline. Programs include nursing, dental hygiene, automotive, and clean energy technology.

RapidRide E Line

BRTAurora Ave

King County Metro's RapidRide E Line runs along Aurora Avenue (SR-99) through Shoreline, connecting Aurora Village in the north to downtown Seattle. The E Line is one of Metro's highest-ridership BRT routes, providing frequent service 7 days a week.

Getting Here from Mukilteo

Shoreline sits between Lynnwood and Seattle — two Link stations make it easy to reach by rail.

By Link Light Rail (via Lynnwood City Center Station)

Route 117 from Mukilteo Ferry to Lynnwood City Center Station (~32 min). Then 1 Line south to Shoreline North/185th (1 stop, ~4 min) or Shoreline South/148th (2 stops, ~7 min).

Total transit time: approximately 40–45 minutes to Shoreline North/185th. Service runs 7 days a week, every 8–10 minutes peak, every 15 minutes off-peak.

By RapidRide E Line

From Lynnwood, connect to the RapidRide E Line along Aurora Avenue. Frequent service runs approximately every 10 minutes during peak hours.
