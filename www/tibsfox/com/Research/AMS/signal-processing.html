Key Mathematical Concepts

Fourier Analysis in Instruction Scheduling

F(ω) = ∫-∞∞ f(t) e-iωt dt

DFT: X[k] = ∑n=0N-1 x[n] e-i2πkn/N

Any periodic signal decomposes into sinusoidal components.

VLIW (Very Long Instruction Word) architectures use trace scheduling to pack multiple operations into each instruction word. The scheduling problem has a frequency-domain interpretation: instruction streams exhibit periodic patterns (loop bodies, pipeline stages) that Fourier analysis can decompose. Identifying the dominant frequencies reveals the natural parallelism of the workload. CppCon’s coverage of these “impossible computers” connects directly to this analysis.

Noise vs Signal in LLM Threat Modeling

SNR = Psignal / Pnoise
SNRdB = 10 log10(Psignal / Pnoise)

Detection threshold: SNR > SNRmin

LLM threat modeling faces a fundamental signal-to-noise problem: adversarial inputs (signal) are embedded in legitimate traffic (noise). The DDIPE bypass rates of 11.6–33.5% represent a low-SNR regime where malicious prompts are difficult to distinguish from benign ones. The 1.6% cross-model bypass rate suggests that ensembling multiple detectors (analogous to matched filtering) dramatically improves detection SNR.

Sound Propagation Over Water

L(d) = L0 - 20 log10(d/d0) - αd

Attenuation: 6–10 dB over water (frequency-dependent)
Speed of sound in water: ~1,480 m/s (vs 343 m/s in air)

Sound propagates differently over water than over land. The 6–10 dB attenuation range is frequency-dependent — lower frequencies travel farther. This connects to the Possession Sound Heritage project (PSH, |z| = 0.281) and The Sound of Puget Sound (SPS) in the PNW corpus. The physics of sound propagation over Puget Sound directly inform the weather station and environmental monitoring research.

Photonic WDM and Micro-Resonator Frequency Combs

WDM channels: λ1, λ2, ..., λN
Channel spacing: Δν = c / (λ2 · Δλ)

Comb laser: fn = f0 + n · frep
where frep = free spectral range of micro-resonator

Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) packs multiple data channels onto a single optical fiber by assigning each channel a different wavelength of light. Micro-resonator comb lasers generate hundreds of equally-spaced frequencies simultaneously, enabling massively parallel optical computing. Asianometry’s coverage of photonic computing connects this to the memory-centric computing research — moving data as photons eliminates the 60–90% energy cost of electronic data movement.

Schumann Resonance

fn ≈ (c / 2πR) √(n(n+1))

Fundamental: f1 ≈ 7.83 Hz
R = Earth radius, c = speed of light
Cavity: Earth surface ↔ ionosphere

The Schumann resonance is the electromagnetic resonance of the Earth-ionosphere cavity. The fundamental frequency of 7.83 Hz falls within the range of brain alpha waves (8–12 Hz), leading to the brain-as-transmitter hypothesis explored in the Nic Rouleau consciousness research. While speculative, the frequency overlap raises questions about electromagnetic coupling between biological and geophysical systems — a cross-domain connection between signal processing and neuroscience.
