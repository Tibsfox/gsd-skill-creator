Overview Versioning Upstream Tracking Governance Change Mgmt Quality Gates Mission Pack
A version number is a promise. A governance framework is the system that ensures promises are kept. When Module 05 grew to 706 lines with TypeScript interfaces, ASCII diagrams, and token budget analysis inside a container sized for 400 lines, the content wasn't wrong — the container was. Module governance is the practice of drawing boundaries that respect what things are, not just how big they are. The Amiga didn't achieve its reputation by cramming everything into one chip. The magic was in the boundaries.

Research Modules

M1: Module Versioning Foundations

Versioning Track A · Semantic contracts

Semantic versioning as a communication protocol. Module identity and namespacing. Version pinning strategies. Drift detection and currency monitoring. The frozen snapshot problem. Automated version sentinels and their design principles.

M2: Upstream Dependency Tracking

Supply Chain Track A · Dependency intelligence

Dependency graphs as infrastructure. Direct vs. transitive dependencies and the transitive risk multiplier. Supply chain attacks and trust boundaries. SBOM standards (SPDX, CycloneDX). The npm and Cargo ecosystems as case studies.

M3: Governance Frameworks for Modular Systems

Governance Track B · Policy architecture

Change management frameworks (ITIL, Kotter). Approval workflows and HITL gates. Quality gates in CI/CD. The governance problem in open ecosystems. CNCF graduation model. RFC processes. Ostrom's commons governance principles.

M4: Change Management & Approval Workflows

Change Mgmt Track B · Promotion pipelines

The promotion problem. Document class taxonomy. Size contracts as governance instruments. The promotion pipeline from detection through validation. Pointer reference architecture. Content integrity during promotion. Module 05 case study.

M5: Quality Gates & the Governance Problem in Open Ecosystems

Quality Track B · Enforcement & health

Gate design principles and taxonomy. The Governance Ledger as verification matrix. Ecosystem health monitoring. License and security governance. SLSA build integrity framework. Governance patterns from Kubernetes, Rust, Apache, and Debian.
