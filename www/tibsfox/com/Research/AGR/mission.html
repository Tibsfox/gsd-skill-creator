Mission Pack

The original research brief and mission package that initiated the PNW Agriculture research project. A comprehensive TeX document covering four modules of expanded research — apple cultivar science, viticulture and terroir, water infrastructure, and pest/disease/smoke taint science — with a fifth module on labor, sustainability, and economic resilience.

Source Documents

The mission pack comprises a multi-page TeX research companion covering the complete PNW agriculture scope: apple genomics and high-density orchard systems, Willamette Valley soil geology and Washington AVA architecture, Grand Coulee Dam and the Columbia Basin Project, the Odessa Aquifer crisis, codling moth IPM, apple maggot quarantine, wildfire smoke taint chemistry, and agricultural labor economics.

Research Scope

  • Apple Cultivar Science: WSU Tree Fruit Research Center. Cosmic Crisp (WA 38) development from 1997 cross to 2019 launch. RosBREED genomic-assisted breeding. High-density Tall Spindle orchards on dwarfing rootstocks. Geneva series fire blight resistance.
  • Viticulture & Terroir: Three soil types of the Willamette Valley (Jory, Willakenzie, Laurelwood). Washington’s 21 AVAs and hierarchical structure. Missoula Floods as terroir driver. Climate change as opportunity and threat.
  • Water Infrastructure: Grand Coulee Dam and the Columbia Basin Project (671,000 acres). Banks Lake and the canal system. Odessa Aquifer depletion and $200M+ replacement program. Prior appropriation doctrine. Snowpack decline and irrigation timing mismatch.
  • Pest & Disease Science: Codling moth degree-day models and mating disruption. Apple maggot quarantine zones. Fire blight and Geneva rootstock resistance. Wildfire smoke taint: thiophenol discovery, Gordonia alkanivorans bioremediation, $7.65M USDA-NIFA “smoke to glass” program.
  • Labor & Economics: 40,000–50,000 seasonal workers, H-2A visa dependence. Mechanization progress and harvest platforms. LIVE certification (27,000+ vineyard acres). Ste. Michelle Wine Estates acquisition. Oregon enotourism ($207.5M).

Key Connections

AGR connects directly to GDN (food production at garden scale), ECO (elevation bands and agricultural ecology), CAS (rain shadow and volcanic soils), AWF (water quality and agricultural runoff), SAL (salmon vs. irrigation water rights), THE (energy-water nexus from shared dams), HGE (volcanic geology as terroir driver), COL (Missoula Floods), WYR (timber-to-farmland conversion), BPS (pheromone sensing and genetic markers), NND (Columbia Basin Project as New Deal infrastructure), and AVI (bird predation on orchard pests).
