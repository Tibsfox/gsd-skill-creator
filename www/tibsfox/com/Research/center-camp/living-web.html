The Living Web

Foxy's Installation — the mycorrhizal network beneath the research forest
“The network is the forest. Grandmother Cedar provisions the seedlings.”

Seven threads connect thirteen research projects into a single living system. The salmon carry nitrogen from the ocean into the forest. The mycorrhizal network distributes it underground. The vertical gradient determines where each species can thrive. Fire resets and regenerates. Pollinators move genetic material between plants. Water connects watershed to ocean. And the heritage thread reminds us that Indigenous peoples have understood these connections for millennia — the documentation is catching up to what was already known.
