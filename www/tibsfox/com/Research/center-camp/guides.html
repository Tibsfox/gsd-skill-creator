The Guides

Eleven species, eleven paths — choose a trail and let your guide lead you through the research
Center Camp sits at the heart of the forest. Eleven trails lead outward into the research — ecology, electronics, infrastructure, energy, creative arts, business, vision, music, radio, community, and science fiction. Each trail has a guide — a PNW species that knows the way. Pick a trail. The guide will introduce themselves and show you what’s ahead.
Every guide was chosen from the research itself — species documented in the ecology, biology, and natural history of the Pacific Northwest. They guide because guiding is what they do in the wild: elk make trails, moles build tunnels, swans follow thermal gradients, loons carry sound across water. The forest teaches through its own inhabitants.
