Mission Package

The retrospective found the seam in Module 06 — a 484-line module carrying two distinct bodies of knowledge that need their own homes. Module 04 had the framework but not the numbers. A housing proposal that cannot answer “how much per bed, amortized over twenty years” cannot be taken to a co-op board, a tribal council, or a development finance institution. This mission removes both obstacles. The spaces between good intention and executable plan are where projects go to die. This mission closes two of those spaces.

The Through-Line

The Amiga Principle holds that remarkable outcomes emerge from architectural elegance — specialized execution paths producing staggering complexity through faithful, principled iteration. Module 06 grew long because it was doing two things well simultaneously. The retrospective named the seam. This mission cuts along it, not because the content was wrong, but because navigability is a design value. A document that cannot be used as a reference is not a reference — it is a monument. The housing gap is a simpler story: specificity is kindness. A proposal without cost data asks an institutional partner to imagine the numbers themselves. That is too much friction. This mission removes it by going to the ground-truth sources and bringing those numbers back in a form that survives a board meeting.
