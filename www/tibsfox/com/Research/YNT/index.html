On April 12, 1961, Yuri Gagarin became the first human to orbit the Earth. Twenty years later to the day — by technical accident, not design — STS-1 launched the Space Shuttle era. Yuri's Night celebrates both: the audacity of leaving the planet and the cosmic coincidence that two of humanity's greatest spaceflight milestones share a calendar date. The World Space Party has grown to 320+ events in 50+ countries across all seven continents.

Research Modules

M1: Vostok 1 & Gagarin's Flight

Vostok Track A · Historic flight

April 12, 1961: 108 minutes, one orbit, 27,400 km/h. Vostok 3KA-3 spacecraft, modified R-7 ICBM. Call sign Kedr (cedar). Space Race context: 25 days before Shepard, 10 months before Glenn. Global impact and FAI records.

M2: STS-1 & the Space Shuttle Era

Shuttle Track A · April 12, 1981

First Space Shuttle orbital flight: Columbia, Commander John Young, Pilot Robert Crippen. The April 12 coincidence (IBM System/4 Pi timing skew). 37 orbits, 2 days. Columbia's 27 missions before STS-107.

M3: Organization History

Org Track B · Founding to SpaceKind

UNISPACE III origins (1999). Founders: Loretta Hidalgo Whitesides, George T. Whitesides, Trish Garner. SGAC to independent 501(c)(3) to SpaceKind Foundation. 2022 Ukraine war navigation.

M4: Global Events & Outreach

Events Track B · STEM programming

Event diversity: NASA centers, museums, bars, hackathons, raves. AstroAccess, Cosmic Odyssey Scholarship, Spirit of Yuri's Night Award, Moon Trees. 2011 peak: 100,000+ attendees, 567 events, 75 countries.

M5: UN Recognition & Policy

Policy Synthesis · International framework

UN Resolution A/RES/65/271: International Day of Human Space Flight. Outer Space Treaty (1967). UNOOSA framework. SDG linkages. Cosmonautics Day history. Global policy architecture.
