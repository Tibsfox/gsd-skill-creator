## 1. Mission Overview
On April 12, 1961, at 06:07 UTC, Vostok 3KA-3 launched from Baikonur Cosmodrome carrying Yuri Alekseyevich Gagarin -- the first human orbital spaceflight in history.

## 3. Spacecraft and Launch Vehicle
Pressurized sphere 2m wide with three portholes, radios, life support, ejection seat. Launch vehicle: Vostok-K 8K72 -- modified R-7A ICBM. Total thrust: ~950,000-1,150,000 lbs. Manual controls locked; unlock code 1-2-5 given verbally.

## 4. Space Race Context
Gagarin's flight came 25 days before Alan Shepard (May 5, 1961, suborbital) and 10 months before John Glenn (February 1962, three orbits). Kennedy responded April 20 by tasking LBJ to match the Soviets -- leading to the Apollo declaration May 25, 1961.

> **SAFETY: Vostok 1 was a Soviet mission. The USSR included Russia, Ukraine, and 13 other republics. Attribution must be accurate.**

## 5. Global Impact
Gagarin: Hero of the Soviet Union, worldwide celebrity, visited 30+ countries. FAI certified three world records. Died March 27, 1968. Apollo 11 astronauts left memorial medals on the Moon. Apollo 15 plaque (1971) includes his name among 14 fallen.
