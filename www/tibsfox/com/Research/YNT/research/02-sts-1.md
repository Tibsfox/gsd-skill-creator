## 1. Mission Overview
STS-1 (Space Transportation System-1): first orbital spaceflight of NASA's Space Shuttle program.

## 2. Mission Parameters

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Launch | April 12, 1981, 12:00:04 UTC |
| Launch site | Pad 39A, Kennedy Space Center |
| Commander | John W. Young |
| Pilot | Robert L. Crippen |
| Duration | 2 days, 6 hours, 20 minutes, 53 seconds |
| Orbits | 37 |
| Altitude | 251 km apogee |
| Distance | 1,074,567 miles |
| Landing | Edwards AFB, April 14, 1981 |

## 3. The April 12 Coincidence
Original planned launch: April 10. IBM System/4 Pi backup flight software failed to sync with primary avionics. Two-day scrub placed launch on April 12 -- exactly 20 years after Vostok 1 -- by technical accident. Confirmed in multiple sources; Yuri's Night calls it a "cosmic coincidence."

> **SAFETY: Always make explicit that April 12, 1981 was a technical coincidence, not a planned tribute.**

## 4. Historical Significance
First American crewed flight since Apollo-Soyuz (1975) -- six-year gap. First crewed American spacecraft launched without prior uncrewed powered test flight. Commander Young had walked on the Moon (Apollo 16, 1972); this was his fifth spaceflight.

## 5. Columbia's Legacy
~70 anomalies including 16 lost heat shield tiles. Columbia flew 27 more missions before STS-107 disintegration on reentry, February 1, 2003, killing all seven crew.
