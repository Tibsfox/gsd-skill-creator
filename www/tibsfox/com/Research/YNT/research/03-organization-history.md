## 1. Founding (1999-2001)
Concept: Space Generation Forum during UNISPACE III, Vienna, 1999. Founders: **Loretta Hidalgo Whitesides**, **George T. Whitesides**, **Trish Garner**. First event: April 12, 2001 (40th anniversary of Vostok 1) in Los Angeles.

## 2. Organizational Evolution

| Period | Organization | Status |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| 2001-2010 | Space Generation Advisory Council | Project under SGAC |
| 2010 | Yuri's Night | Independent 501(c)(3) |
| 2022-present | The SpaceKind Foundation | Renamed; Yuri's Night brand retained |

## 3. Key Milestones

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 2001 | First global Yuri's Night -- LA anchor event |
| 2004 | Ray Bradbury, Dennis Tito, Peter Diamandis, Lance Bass, Nichelle Nichols attend LA |
| 2007 | NASA Ames hosts Bay Area event |
| 2011 | 50th anniversary: 100,000+ attendees, 567 events, 75 countries, 7 continents; ISS crew greeting |
| 2022 | SpaceKind Foundation issues Ukraine solidarity statement |
| 2024 | 320+ events in 50 countries across 7 continents |

## 4. 2022 Ukraine War Navigation
Space Foundation renamed its 2022 celebration from "Yuri's Night." SpaceKind Foundation condemned the invasion while maintaining Gagarin's flight was "in the name of the entire Soviet Union" (including Ukraine). The organization continued the Yuri's Night name.

> **SAFETY: Follow SpaceKind Foundation's own framing. Distinguish Gagarin as human symbol vs. Russia as geopolitical actor.**
