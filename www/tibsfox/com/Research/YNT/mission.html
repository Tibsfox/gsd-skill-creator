Mission Package

April 12 belongs to the cosmos. In 1961, Gagarin orbited the Earth. In 1981, Columbia launched the Shuttle era — on the same date, by technical accident. Yuri's Night celebrates both: the audacity and the coincidence. From a Space Generation Forum concept in 1999 to 320+ events across 50+ countries, the World Space Party demonstrates that space exploration is a shared human inheritance.

The Through-Line

Yuri's Night is proof that a shared date on the calendar can become a global institution. The April 12 coincidence — Gagarin's orbit in 1961 and Columbia's launch in 1981 — was unplanned, but the celebration was not. Loretta Hidalgo Whitesides, George Whitesides, and Trish Garner chose to build something that honors the human dimension of spaceflight: not the rockets, but the audacity of the people who ride them. The UN recognized April 12 as the International Day of Human Space Flight in 2011. The event continues to grow.
