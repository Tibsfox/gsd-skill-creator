Mission Pack

The original research brief and mission package that initiated the Year of the Fire Dragon research project. A comprehensive three-stage TeX document covering the complete Fire Dragon scope: Wuxing cosmological foundations, Dragon mythology from Neolithic jade through Qing dynasty imperial law, Fire element in Traditional Chinese Medicine, the Bǐng Chén archetype constructed from first principles, and peer-reviewed demographic evidence of Dragon-year birth booms across Singapore, Taiwan, Hong Kong, and mainland China.

Three-Stage Pipeline

  • Stage 1 — Vision Document: Narrative vision, problem statement, five-module architecture, domain map, chipset configuration, twelve success criteria, through-line. The “why” and “what.”
  • Stage 2 — Research Reference: Compiled findings from scholarly, government, museum, and peer-reviewed sources organized by module. Wuxing theory, dragon mythology, Fire in TCM, Bǐng Chén archetype, demographic evidence, source bibliography with 30+ citations.
  • Stage 3 — Mission Package: Squadron activation profile (12 roles), 4-wave execution plan (Foundation, Research, Synthesis, Publication), 3 parallel tracks, 10 components, 32 tests (6 safety-critical), token budget of 60.5K across Opus/Sonnet/Haiku, dependency graph, verification matrix.

Research Scope

  • Cosmological Foundations: Wuxing (Five Phases) from Shang oracle bones through Han dynasty correlative cosmology. Heavenly Stems, Earthly Branches, sexagenary cycle mathematics (LCM of 10 and 12 = 60). Planetary correspondences (Mars = Fire). The critical “phases not elements” translation.
  • Dragon Mythology: Archaeological evidence from Xinglongwa (6200 BCE) through Erlitou turquoise mosaics. Imperial symbolism: five-claw rule, dragon robes, 10,000+ dragon designs in the Forbidden City. Four cosmic dragons, nine dragon sons, composite anatomy from nine animals.
  • Fire in TCM: Heart as Emperor organ, Shen (spirit/consciousness), Small Intestine as separator of pure and impure, Pericardium as Heart protector, Triple Burner as thermostat. Generating/controlling/exhaustion cycles. Fire personality in balance and imbalance. Seasonal medicine.
  • Bǐng Chén Archetype: Yang Fire (sun) stem + Dragon (Earth) branch. Hidden stems within Chén: Yang Earth (stability), Yin Water (depth), Yin Wood (fuel). Self-regulating internal architecture. All five elemental Dragon variants compared. Four Pillars context.
  • Demographic Impact: Dragon-year birth booms documented with peer-reviewed evidence: ~30% Singapore (Agarwal/Qian, AEA 2018), Taiwan reversal (Goodkind 1991/1993), Shenzhen birth timing manipulation (ScienceDirect 2021). The Dragon Paradox: auspicious birth → cohort crowding → structural disadvantage.
