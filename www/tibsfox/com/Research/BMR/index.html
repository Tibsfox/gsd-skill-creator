Bob Marley & Reggae

Music, Resistance · Rastafari · The Rhythm of Liberation
Overview Origins & Roots Life & Career Rastafari Musical Architecture Global Legacy Mission Pack
In the spaces between a Trench Town government yard and a global stage, something extraordinary was born. Bob Marley did not invent reggae the way an engineer invents a machine — he became the vessel through which a suffering people's deepest wisdom, spiritual hunger, and indestructible joy found its way into the world's ears. Reggae is, at its core, an architecture of resistance: a musical operating system that transforms pain into groove, oppression into prophecy, and raw human longing into something so structurally elegant it has outlasted the man who gave it its face.

Research Modules

M1: Origins & Roots

Origins Track A · Musical genealogy

Jamaican mento folk music through ska and rocksteady. Sound system culture, Kingston independence-era energy, rude boy subculture. Key producers: Coxsone Dodd, Duke Reid, Prince Buster. The infrastructure that made reggae possible.

M2: Bob Marley — Life & Career

Biography Track B · Full biographical arc

Nine Miles to Trench Town. The Wailers formation (1963). Island Records breakthrough. Assassination attempt and London exile. Exodus. Africa tours. Cancer, death at thirty-six, state funeral. All ten studio albums profiled.

M3: Rastafari & Spirituality

Theology Track B · Spiritual framework

Marcus Garvey's pan-Africanism. Leonard Howell's founding theology. Haile Selassie's 1930 coronation. Three Mansions: Nyabinghi, Bobo Ashanti, Twelve Tribes. Ganja as sacrament. Marley's conversion arc through Ethiopian Orthodoxy.

M4: Musical Architecture

Technical Track A · Sonic structure

One-drop rhythm, skank guitar on beats 2 and 4, bass as lead melody. Lee "Scratch" Perry's studio innovations. King Tubby's dub production. The Barrett Brothers as rhythmic engine. I-Threes vocal architecture.

M5: Global Impact & Legacy

Legacy Synthesis · Cross-genre influence

UNESCO 2018 inscription. Hip-hop's Jamaican sound-system roots. UK punk and 2 Tone. Reggaeton's dancehall DNA. Legend compilation (28+ million copies). One Love Peace Concert. Contemporary reggae revival.
