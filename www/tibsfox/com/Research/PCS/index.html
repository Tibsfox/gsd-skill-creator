Research Modules

M1: Computing with Physical Systems

Photonics MPI/Princeton · Fundamental Limits

Physical neural networks where the input, transduction, and measurement are all physical. Shot noise as the defining feature. Hilbert space of functions with eigentask decomposition. Capacity formula: C = Σ[1/(1 + βk²/S)]. Correlated encoding outperforms product states. Reservoir computing equivalence.

M2: Trapped-Ion Quantum Hardware

Ion Trap MPI · Scaling to Millions

Single trapped ions as “the best qubit you can find.” Calcium-40 optical qubits with >1 second coherence. The 100x trapping frequency challenge (1 MHz to 100 MHz). Modular architecture with optical and physical links. Anomalous surface noise scaling as D-4. Cryogenic mitigation of an unknown mechanism.

M3: Solid-State Hydrogen Energy Storage

Energy Synapse/Lumen · $1T Market

Metal hydride batteries that never degrade. High-entropy alloys enabling solid-state hydrogen storage. Behind-the-meter energy arbitrage displacing peaker plants. Linear scaling: double vessels for double capacity. From “Lunar Manufacturing” to terrestrial grid resilience. The $20–50M cold storage blackout insurance case.

M4: Circular Sanitation & Nutrient Recovery

Ecology Synapse/Wasted · NPK from Humans

Humans are the only animals that mix waste streams. 25% of global fertilizer needs replaceable from human waste. Source separation preserves stream integrity. 32 billion gallons of water wasted annually in US portable sanitation. Urine = 1% of waste volume but majority of nutrients. PFAS avoidance through source separation.

M5: Synthesis — Noise, Signal, and Modularity

Synthesis Cross-seminar · Universal Principles

Understand your noise floor, then engineer your signal above it. The physical computing stack from trapped ions to nutrient cycles. Modularity as the universal scaling pattern: ion modules, eigentask spaces, hydrogen vessels, portable sanitation units. Technical founders solving infrastructure problems.
