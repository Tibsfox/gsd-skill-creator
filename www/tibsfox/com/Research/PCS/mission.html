Mission Package

Understand your noise floor, then engineer your signal above it. This principle unifies quantum physics, energy storage, and nutrient cycling. Four talks, four domains, one universal lesson: computation, storage, and cycling are all forms of signal preservation against noise.

Mission Objectives

  1. Map the physical computing stack from trapped ions (quantum layer) through physical neural networks (computation layer) to energy storage and nutrient cycling (infrastructure layer)
  2. Identify the modularity pattern that enables scaling across all four domains: ion modules, eigentask function spaces, hydrogen vessels, portable sanitation units
  3. Connect noise theory to practice — shot noise, anomalous surface noise, chemical contamination, and the universal signal-to-noise principle
  4. Extract the founder pattern — technical founders solving infrastructure problems by following the science rather than the original vision

DIY Sessions

Session 1: Optical Reservoir Computer

Build a simplified optical computing system: laser pointer through ground glass diffuser, smartphone camera as detector array. Train a linear classifier on stochastic speckle features. Demonstrates physical neural network principles. ~3 hours.

Session 2: Electrochemical Hydrogen Storage

Disassemble NiMH batteries to expose metal hydride electrodes. Measure charge/discharge curves, calculate Wh/kg, compare to diesel and lithium-ion. Observe temperature rise during hydrogen desorption. ~4 hours.

Session 3: Urine-to-Fertilizer Extraction

Struvite precipitation from synthetic urine. Add MgCl2 at pH 9.0–9.5, filter precipitate, calculate phosphorus recovery efficiency. Compare cost to commercial fertilizer. ~2 hours.
