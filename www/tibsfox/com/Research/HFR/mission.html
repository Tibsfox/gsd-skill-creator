Mission Package

Sound is physics made audible. A pressure wave — a disturbance in air molecules propagating outward at 343 meters per second at sea level — becomes a voltage through a transducer, travels along a conductor shaped by the physics of resistance, capacitance, and inductance, is amplified by circuits whose behavior is governed by quantum mechanics at the junction level, and is finally reconverted to pressure through a driver pushing a cone against the resistance of air. Every step in that journey is a physical transformation. Every transformation introduces constraints. High fidelity is the art and science of minimizing those constraints.

The Through-Line

The space between the recording and the ear is where fidelity lives or dies. Every component in the chain — DAC, amplifier, crossover, driver, room, ear canal — performs a physical transformation on the signal. The physics at each stage is well understood: Shannon's sampling theorem, Ohm's law, the Lorentz force equation, the wave equation, the Sabine reverberation formula, Fletcher-Munson equal-loudness curves. The art of hi-fi is not in any single stage but in the integration — ensuring that the transformations chain cleanly, that each stage's output is well-matched to the next stage's input, and that the cumulative error budget stays below the threshold of human perception. The measurement-subjectivist debate resolves here: measurements predict most of what matters, but the final arbiter is the listener in the room, hearing the complete chain as a single experience.
