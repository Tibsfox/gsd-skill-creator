Sound is physics made audible. A pressure wave — a disturbance in air molecules propagating outward at 343 meters per second — becomes a voltage through a transducer, travels along a conductor shaped by resistance, capacitance, and inductance, is amplified by circuits whose behavior is governed by quantum mechanics at the junction level, and is finally reconverted to pressure through a driver pushing a cone against the resistance of air. High fidelity is the art and science of minimizing every transformation loss until the reproduction and the original are indistinguishable to the most demanding instrument we possess: the human auditory system.

Research Modules

M1: Speaker Physics & Transducer Design

Physics Track A · Electromechanical

Moving-coil driver physics, Thiele-Small parameters, crossover network theory, enclosure acoustics (sealed, ported, transmission line), electrostatic and planar magnetic drivers, ribbon tweeters, horn loading, and the complete loudspeaker as a system.

M2: Amplifier Topology & Circuit Design

Electronics Track A · Power stages

Class A, AB, B, D, G, H amplifier topologies. Vacuum tube physics (triode, pentode, push-pull). Solid-state output stages. Negative feedback theory. Damping factor and speaker control. Distortion mechanisms: THD, IMD, TIM. Power supply design for audio.

M3: DAC Architectures & the Digital Path

Electronics Track B · Conversion

R-2R ladder, delta-sigma, multibit, and hybrid DAC architectures. Nyquist-Shannon sampling theorem. Oversampling and noise shaping. Digital filter topologies. Jitter analysis and reclocking. USB audio class, I2S, S/PDIF interfaces. Vinyl vs digital: the complete technical comparison.

M4: Room Acoustics & Psychoacoustics

Physics Track B · Listening environment

Room modes, standing waves, RT60 reverberation time, Sabine and Eyring equations. Absorption, diffusion, and reflection. Psychoacoustic foundations: Fletcher-Munson equal-loudness contours, critical bands, auditory masking, localization. Measurement vs subjective listening: the measurement-subjectivist debate.

M5: Headphone Technology & Personal Audio

Physics Track C · Personal listening

Dynamic, planar magnetic, electrostatic, and balanced armature driver technologies. Open-back vs closed-back acoustic design. Headphone amplifier requirements: impedance matching, voltage swing, current delivery. HRTF and binaural audio. IEM design and crossover architectures. Headphone measurement standards (IEC 60268-7).
