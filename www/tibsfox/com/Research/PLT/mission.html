Mission Pack

The original research brief and mission package that initiated the PNW Planting Intelligence project. A comprehensive three-stage TeX document covering the full Vision to Research to Mission pipeline — weather intelligence, crop science, orchard management, companion ecology, and slug defense for the week of March 26, 2026.

Source Documents

The mission pack comprises a 30-page TeX research companion covering the complete PNW micro-climate planting scope: real-time weather fusion from NOAA, NWS, and AccuWeather; ENSO phase transition analysis; 20+ cool-season crop profiles with soil temperature thresholds; warm-season indoor start timing with the “May Mistake” avoidance protocol; established fruit tree management for apple, pear, and plum; integrated slug defense for maritime PNW; and orchard companion planting ecology including nitrogen fixers, mineral accumulators, pest confusers, and pollinator attractors.

Research Scope

  • Weather Intelligence: 7-day forecast table (March 26 – April 1, 2026), ENSO La Niña-to-neutral transition, soil temperature estimation by bed type, frost risk curves for 5+ PNW sub-regions, wet-March-to-dry-April inflection analysis.
  • Cool-Season Crops: 20+ direct-sow crops — legumes (peas, favas), salad greens (lettuce, arugula, spinach, kale, mache, chard), root vegetables (radish, carrot, beet, turnip, parsnip), alliums (onion sets, shallots, garlic, chives), potatoes, hardy herbs (cilantro, dill, parsley, chervil, oregano, mint, sorrel).
  • Warm-Season Planning: Indoor start guide for tomatoes, peppers, eggplant, basil, brassicas. Germination temperature requirements, transplant date targeting, succession planting through April.
  • Orchard Management: Dormant pruning deadline, dormant oil spray timing, codling moth trap deployment, fire blight inspection, fertilization, mulch management, bare root planting window for apple, pear, plum in western Washington.
  • Defense & Companion Ecology: Integrated slug management (iron phosphate, copper barriers, beer traps, evening patrols, watering timing). Companion planting matrix: chives for scab, daffodils for rodent deterrence, comfrey for minerals, clover for nitrogen, nasturtiums as trap crops, calendula/borage/yarrow for pollinators.

Key Connections

PLT connects directly to AGR (PNW agriculture at regional scale), GDN (food production at garden scale), ECO (elevation bands and growing zones), COL (Columbia Valley weather patterns), CAS (Cascade rain shadow and precipitation), AWF (water quality and soil health), SAL (salmon heritage and watershed ecology), and WYR (forest-to-farmland conversion and timber ecology).
