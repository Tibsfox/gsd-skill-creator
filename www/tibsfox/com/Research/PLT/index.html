The Pacific Northwest spring does not arrive as a single event. It arrives in the spaces between rain systems — two-day windows where the soil is workable, the air temperature cooperates, and the gardener who knows the rhythm of the season can get seeds into ground that will be watered for free by the next front rolling off the Pacific. The companion planting pattern is the garden-scale Amiga Principle: chives, comfrey, clover, and yarrow each doing one thing well, their combined effect creating a self-renewing ecosystem that reduces external inputs every season.
