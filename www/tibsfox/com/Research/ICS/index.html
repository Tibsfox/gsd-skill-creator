There is a spring terminal on a piece of cardboard. Push the lever, insert a wire, release. The spring clamps down with a force of 0.5–0.8 Newton, transmitted through a brass body alloyed from copper (Z=29) and zinc (Z=30). That terminal costs approximately fifteen cents. Inside the Large Hadron Collider, superconducting niobium-titanium magnets bend proton beams traveling at 0.999999991c around a 27-kilometer ring. The spring terminal and the superconducting dipole are the same thing seen at different scales: a connection made precise, held by a force calibrated to preserve information through a mechanical interface.

Research Modules

M1: Modular Construction Principles

Engineering Track A · Foundations

What is a module? The spring terminal as minimal module. Interface contracts and coupling. The periodic table as parts inventory. Standardization, hierarchical decomposition, failure modes at module boundaries. The fidelity principle across ten layers.

M2: Building Blocks as Computation

Computation Track A · Theory

Construction as computation. Combinatorial explosion from finite parts. The LEGO computation model. Boolean logic from physical parts. Cellular automata, von Neumann self-reproduction, Turing completeness. Assembly complexity and information content.

M3: LEGO, Meccano & Construction Set History

History Track B · 124 Years

Meccano (1901), Erector Set (1913), LEGO (1958). Lincoln Logs, Tinkertoy, Fischer Technik. LEGO Technic, Mindstorms, and the programmable brick. Modern platforms: Arduino, Raspberry Pi, 3D printing. Design principles that endure.

M4: Construction Set Theory

Mathematics Track B · Formal systems

Algebraic structure of construction sets. Group theory and symmetry. Combinatorics of composition. Category theory and interface contracts. Graph theory, type theory, information theory. The fidelity metric as mathematical object. Infinite composability from finite parts.

M5: Compositional Design

Engineering Track C · Architecture

Composition vs. assembly. The Amiga Principle formalized. Separation of concerns. Design patterns as compositional templates. Layered and pipeline architecture. Emergent properties. The Standard Model as compositional design. GSD as compositional system.

M6: Software Architecture Metaphor

Software Track C · Integration

Parts and packages. Interfaces and APIs. Build systems as assembly manuals. Quality control and testing. Versioning and backward compatibility. Package registries, containers, and infrastructure as code. The GSD construction set.
