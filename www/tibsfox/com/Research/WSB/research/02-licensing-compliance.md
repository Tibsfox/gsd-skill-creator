## 1. The Washington Business Licensing Service

The **Business Licensing Service (BLS)** is the central portal for Washington business licensing, administered by the Department of Revenue. A single BLS application simultaneously:
- Registers the business with the Department of Revenue (DOR) — establishing B&O tax and sales tax accounts
- Registers with the Employment Security Department (ESD) — establishing the unemployment insurance account
- Registers with Labor & Industries (L&I) — establishing workers' compensation accounts
- Applies for the state business license (the "master business license")

This one-application approach is the state's version of dependency resolution: instead of three separate agency visits, one application populates all three accounts.

**Portal:** [https://bls.dor.wa.gov](https://bls.dor.wa.gov)

---

## 2. When a State Business License Is Required

A **Washington State Business License** is required when the business meets any of the following conditions:

| Condition | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|
| Annual gross income | $12,000 or more |
| Sales tax collection | Any sales tax obligation |
| Trade name use | Operating under a name other than owner's legal name |
| Employee hiring | Plans to hire within 90 days |
| DOR tax/fee obligation | Any tax or fee payable to the Department of Revenue |

Note: a sole proprietorship below the $12,000 threshold that does not collect sales tax and operates under the owner's legal name technically does not require a state business license — though licensing is recommended as it establishes formal business identity.

## 3. The State Business License Application Process

### 3.1 Information Required

The BLS application collects:
- Legal entity type and SOS filing number (UBI)
- Business name and any trade names
- Principal business activity (NAICS code)
- Location(s) of business activity
- Owner/officer information
- Employment estimates (if hiring)
- Workers' compensation classification (estimated)

### 3.2 Processing Time

| Filing Method | Processing Time |
|---------------|----------------|
| Online at BLS portal | 5 business days (typical) |
| Paper application | Up to 21 business days |

Online filing is strongly recommended. Paper filing risks delay of all downstream agency account creation.

### 3.3 Annual Renewal

The state business license renews annually. Renewal notices are sent to the business's registered address. Failure to renew results in license deactivation, which can trigger compliance issues with DOR, ESD, and L&I simultaneously.

## 4. City and County Endorsements

Most Washington cities and counties require separate **local business licenses** in addition to the state license. These are called "endorsements" when processed through the BLS system.

### 4.1 How Endorsements Work

The BLS system includes city and county endorsement options. When applying for a state license, businesses can simultaneously apply for local endorsements that the BLS system supports. Not all jurisdictions are integrated — some require separate local applications.

### 4.2 Finding Local Requirements

The **Business Licensing Wizard** ([https://secure.dor.wa.gov/gteunauth/_/](https://secure.dor.wa.gov/gteunauth/_/)) generates a customized checklist of required licenses and permits based on:
- Business activity type (NAICS code or description)
- Business location (city/county)
- Specific industry indicators (food, construction, alcohol, cannabis, etc.)

### 4.3 Consequences of Missing Local Licenses

Operating without required local licenses can result in:
- Fines and penalties from the local jurisdiction
- Backdated license fees
- Business interruption orders
- Complications with city contracts and permits

## 5. Everett-Specific Requirements

For businesses operating in Everett, Washington (Snohomish County):

### 5.1 City of Everett Business License

Everett requires a city business license for businesses operating within city limits. Contact the City of Everett Finance Department for current requirements, fees, and the application process. Requirements may vary by business type and location.

### 5.2 Home Occupation Permits

Businesses operating from a home in Everett require a **Home Occupation Permit** from the City of Everett if:
- Customers or clients visit the home
- Employees other than residents work at the home
- Signage is placed on the property
- The business activity generates significant traffic or impacts neighbors

### 5.3 Snohomish County Requirements

For businesses operating outside city limits in unincorporated Snohomish County:
- Snohomish County does not have a general business license requirement
- Specific permits (construction, septic, land use) may apply
- Contact Snohomish County Planning and Development Services for land use permits

## 6. Industry-Specific Permits

Certain business activities require additional permits and licenses beyond the general state business license:

### 6.1 Food Service

| Activity | Permit | Agency |
|----------|--------|--------|
| Restaurant or food service | Food establishment permit | Snohomish County Health District |
| Food manufacturing/processing | Manufacturing plant license | WA Department of Agriculture |
| Mobile food vendor | Mobile food unit permit | Snohomish County Health District |
| Food handling employees | Food handler card | Snohomish County Health District |

### 6.2 Alcohol

All businesses selling or serving alcohol require a license from the **Washington State Liquor and Cannabis Board (LCB)**. License types include:
- Retailer (grocery, convenience, liquor stores)
- On-premises (restaurant, bar, tavern)
- Off-premises (stores, delivery)
- Manufacturer (brewery, winery, distillery)

LCB license fees and requirements vary significantly by license type. Applications are submitted through LCB's online portal and typically take 60–90 days to process.

### 6.3 Construction and Trades

Washington requires **contractor registration** through L&I for anyone who builds, alters, repairs, or improves residential or commercial structures — even just marketing or bidding for such work. Requirements include:

| Requirement | General Contractor | Specialty Contractor |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Registration with L&I | Required | Required |
| Surety bond | $12,000 (general) | $6,000 (specialty) |
| Liability insurance | $200,000 minimum | $200,000 minimum |
| Training certification | Required (certain trades) | Required (certain trades) |

A business card describing "home improvement" or "remodeling" services without a valid contractor registration is a violation.

### 6.4 Cannabis

Cannabis businesses require a license from the **Washington State Liquor and Cannabis Board**. The licensing process includes background checks, location approval, financial disclosure, and compliance with extensive security, testing, and labeling requirements. The LCB imposes strict controls on who may hold a cannabis license.

### 6.5 Healthcare and Social Services

Licensed healthcare providers, daycare facilities, and social service agencies require licenses from the **Washington State Department of Health** or the **Department of Children, Youth, and Families (DCYF)** in addition to general business licensing.

**Source:** Business.WA.gov Small Business Guide — Start chapter; Washington Department of Licensing; WA Liquor and Cannabis Board.

## 7. Professional Licensing (Non-CPA)

Many professions in Washington require individual professional licenses from the **Department of Licensing (DOL)** or relevant professional boards. This is separate from the business entity license:

| Profession | Licensing Body |
|------------|---------------|
| Real estate agent/broker | WA Department of Licensing |
| Insurance producer | WA Office of the Insurance Commissioner |
| Cosmetologist/barber | WA Department of Licensing |
| Electrician | WA Department of Labor & Industries |
| Plumber | WA Department of Labor & Industries |
| Structural pest inspector | WA Department of Agriculture |
| CPA | WA Board of Accountancy (see ACC module) |
| Attorney | WA State Bar Association |

A business providing professional services must ensure that all practitioners hold current individual licenses in addition to the entity's business license.

## 8. The Business Licensing Wizard

The **Business Licensing Wizard** is the state's compliance discovery tool. It generates a personalized license and permit checklist based on inputs:

**Input:** Business activity description or NAICS code + location (city/county)

**Output:** Customized checklist of required:
- State licenses and endorsements
- City and county licenses
- Professional licenses if applicable
- Industry-specific permits
- Environmental permits (if applicable)

**URL:** [https://secure.dor.wa.gov/gteunauth/_/](https://secure.dor.wa.gov/gteunauth/_/)

The Wizard is updated as licensing requirements change. It is the most practical starting point for understanding the complete licensing stack for a specific business.

## 9. Maintaining Compliance Across Multiple Jurisdictions

Businesses with multiple Washington locations face compounded compliance:
- Each city location may require a separate city endorsement
- Some counties add county-level requirements
- Professional licenses must cover all jurisdictions where services are offered

### 9.1 License Tracking System

A basic license tracking system should record:
- License name, issuing agency, license number
- Issue date, expiration date, renewal notice date
- Renewal fee and payment method
- Contact information for the issuing agency

No single government system tracks all licenses for a business — the business owner is responsible for maintaining this inventory.

### 9.2 Municipal Research and Services Center (MRSC)

The MRSC ([https://mrsc.org](https://mrsc.org)) maintains a directory of all Washington cities and counties with their licensing requirements, contact information, and regulatory summaries. It is the most comprehensive reference for multi-jurisdiction compliance research.
