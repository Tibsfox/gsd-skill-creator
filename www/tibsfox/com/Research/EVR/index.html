Historical Timeline

From Rockefeller-backed speculation to the world's largest airplane factory.

1890 Founded with investment from John D. Rockefeller — lumber mills and a smelter
1891 Incorporated as a city; becomes Snohomish County seat
1893 Great Northern Railway arrives; Panic of 1893 slows growth
1901 Weyerhaeuser Timber Co. establishes major mill operations
1916 Everett Massacre — IWW labor conflict at the city dock
1943 Paine Field built as military airfield during WWII
1967 Boeing breaks ground on Everett factory for the 747 program
1994 Naval Station Everett commissioned — home port for carrier strike group
2003 Sounder N Line commuter rail begins service at Everett Station
2019 Angel of the Winds Arena (now Prov. Arena) hosts AHL Silvertips
2024 Swift Orange Line BRT extends transit coverage

Parks & Green Spaces

197-acre urban forest, riverfront trails, and bluff-top beach parks.

Forest Park

197 acresswim center

Everett's oldest and largest park — 197 acres of wooded trails, a swim center with diving pool (opened 1975), water playground, tennis and basketball courts, and event venues including historic Floral Hall. The former animal farm is permanently closed, but the trails and forest canopy remain one of the finest urban woodland walks in Snohomish County.

Access: 802 E Mukilteo Blvd

Langus Riverfront Park

riverfronttrails

A waterfront park along the Snohomish River estuary with paved trails, bird-watching platforms, and views of Spencer Island. The trail extends along the river through wetlands that support salmon habitat, great blue herons, and migrating shorebirds.

Access: Smith Island Rd, off Hwy 529

Howarth Park

beachrailroad bridge

A bluff-top park with trails descending through forest to a Puget Sound beach. The pedestrian bridge over the BNSF railroad tracks is a landmark — the only at-grade beach access in the city. Views of Whidbey Island and the Cascade foothills from the beach.

Access: Olympic Blvd, west Everett

Lowell Riverfront Trail

trailriver

A paved multi-use trail along the Snohomish River in the historic Lowell neighborhood. Connects to the Langus Riverfront system, providing continuous waterfront walking and cycling. The Lowell area was historically a separate town, absorbed into Everett in the early 1900s.

Thornton A. Sullivan Park

Silver Lakeswim

Located on the shore of Silver Lake, this park features a swimming beach, fishing dock, playground, sports fields, and picnic shelters. Silver Lake is a 105-acre urban lake stocked with trout and home to bass, perch, and crappie. Public boat launch available.

Access: Silver Lake Rd, south Everett

Lakes & Waterways

Silver Lake

105 acresswimmingfishing

A 105-acre lake in south Everett with public swimming beach, fishing dock, and boat launch. Stocked with rainbow trout; also holds largemouth bass, yellow perch, and crappie. Thornton Sullivan Park provides the primary public access on the north shore.

Port of Everett Marina

marinaport

The largest public marina on the West Coast with over 2,300 slips. Jetty Landing development includes restaurants, shops, and waterfront promenade. The port handles both recreational boating and commercial cargo, with views of Naval Station Everett across the waterway.

Notable Landmarks

Boeing Everett Factory

98.3 acresmanufacturing

The largest building in the world by volume at 472 million cubic feet. Built in 1967 for the 747 program, expanded for the 767, 777, and 787. The facility covers 4.3 million square feet of floor area — large enough to contain 75 football fields or all of Disneyland. Tours of the factory are available through the Future of Flight Aviation Center.

Funko Headquarters

retailpop culture

The global headquarters of Funko, makers of Pop! vinyl figures, located in downtown Everett. The Funko HQ store is a major attraction with exclusive figures, interactive displays, and a warehouse-scale retail experience.

Providence Arena (Angel of the Winds)

AHL hockeyevents

Home of the Everett Silvertips (AHL/WHL) hockey team. A 10,000-seat arena in downtown Everett hosting hockey, concerts, and community events. The Silvertips are a pipeline team for the Seattle Kraken NHL franchise.

Naval Station Everett

militarynaval

The newest naval station in the US (commissioned 1994), home port for a carrier strike group including guided-missile destroyers and a Nimitz-class aircraft carrier. The base is a major economic driver for the region, employing thousands of military and civilian personnel.

Getting Here from Mukilteo

Everett is 7 miles north of Mukilteo — the north anchor of the regional transit network.

Major Multimodal Hub

Everett Station (3201 Smith Ave)

The north anchor of regional transit. Everett Station serves Amtrak Cascades, Sounder N Line, Community Transit local and express buses, Swift Blue BRT, Greyhound intercity service, and the future Everett Link light rail extension. From here, the entire Puget Sound and Pacific coast corridor is accessible. Parking garage, staffed waiting area, and ticket office on site.

Sounder N Line (Commuter Rail)

Everett → Mukilteo → Edmonds → King Street Station (Seattle)

One stop north from Mukilteo. Approximately 8 minutes. Weekday peak hours only (4 morning southbound, 4 evening northbound). Everett Station is the northern terminus.

Amtrak Cascades (Intercity Rail)

Vancouver BC → Bellingham → Mt Vernon → Stanwood → Everett → Edmonds → Seattle → Portland → Eugene

Two daily trains each direction. Everett Station is a full-service Amtrak stop on the Pacific coast corridor connecting British Columbia through Washington and Oregon.

  • Southbound to Seattle: ~40 minutes
  • Southbound to Portland: ~4 hours
  • Northbound to Bellingham: ~1.5 hours
  • Northbound to Vancouver BC: ~3.5 hours

Community Transit & Swift BRT

Route 117: Mukilteo Ferry → Lynnwood City Center Station (connects to light rail). Transfer at Seaway Transit Center or Everett Station for Everett local routes.

Swift Blue Line: Everett Station → Aurora Village (connects to King County Metro). High-frequency BRT running every 10–12 minutes, 7 days/week.

Swift Orange Line: McCollum Park P&R → Boeing/Paine Field → Mill Creek. Connects to the Boeing campus and surrounding employment centers.

Future: Everett Link Extension

Sound Transit's Everett Link extension will bring light rail north from Lynnwood to Everett Station, creating a continuous rail link from Everett to Sea-Tac Airport and beyond. Projected opening in the 2030s as part of ST3.
