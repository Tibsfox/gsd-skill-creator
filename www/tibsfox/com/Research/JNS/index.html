Seattle 1967 — from Cascade trails to every campus on earth
Overview Origins SuperBreak Design Corporate Cascades Sustainability Culture Verification Mission Pack
In 1967, above a transmission shop on Aurora Avenue in Seattle, two young people who couldn’t afford much of anything except ambition began hand-sewing backpacks from aluminum frames and nylon. Murray Pletz had won a design contest with a flexible-frame pack; Jan Lewis knew how to sew. The company they built carried her name and, eventually, the books of half the students in America. The SuperBreak endures not because it was engineered to dominate but because it was designed to fit — structurally, economically, culturally — into the life of anyone who needed to carry something from one place to another.

Origins & Product

The Garage on the Ave

Origins

Founded 1967, Seattle, near the University of Washington. Murray Pletz, Jan Lewis, and Skip Yowell. The D-2 external frame pack. Cascade Range as testing ground. Three people in a garage who changed what everyone carries.

The SuperBreak

Product

How mountaineering packs became campus packs. The SuperBreak: most sold backpack in the world. Ubiquity as invisibility — so common nobody notices. The best design is the one that disappears.

Design & Corporate

Simple, Durable, Affordable

Design

Key products: SuperBreak, Right Pack, Big Student, Adaptive Collection. The lifetime warranty — repair or replace, no questions, forever. Materials: Cordura nylon, recycled polyester, construction that outlasts ownership.

From Garage to VF Corp

Corporate

Four ownership transitions: founders → K2/Cummins → Blue Bell → VF Corporation. The $775 million acquisition. How the brand outlasted its founders’ ownership and the warranty outlasted corporate structure.

Place & Planet

Designed for These Mountains

Cascades

The packs were made for PNW trails. Cascade Range as original testing ground. The PNW outdoor gear ecosystem: JanSport, REI, Eddie Bauer, Filson — all from the same place, all shaped by the same weather.

Where the Pack Comes From

Sustainability

Manufacturing in Vietnam, recycled materials, 236 million plastic bottles diverted, lifetime warranty as anti-disposability. The tension between mass production and environmental responsibility.

Culture & Verification

On Every Back

Culture

JanSport in hip-hop, film, on every campus. The SuperBreak as the democratic backpack — no status, just function. #LightenTheLoad mental health campaign. How a hiking pack became invisible infrastructure.
