Chinook Facts
Common name: King Salmon
Scientific: Oncorhynchus tshawytscha
Max size: 150 cm, 61 kg
Lifespan: 3-7 years
Range: Alaska to California
Status: Threatened (many runs)

Navigation: Chinook salmon use Earth's magnetic field as a map during ocean migration, then switch to olfactory (scent) homing to find their natal river. They can detect their home stream's unique chemical signature at parts per billion. Smolts imprint on their river's scent during downstream migration. Adults travel up to 3,000 km through the Pacific before returning to spawn in the exact gravel bed where they hatched.

Columbia River: Once supported runs of 10-16 million salmon annually. The river's 1,243-mile length and extensive tributary system made it the greatest salmon-producing river system on Earth.
