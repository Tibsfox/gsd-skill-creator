## Primary Technical Sources

1. **NASA SP-6 "Results of the Second United States Manned Orbital Space Flight, May 24, 1962."** NASA Special Publication, 1962. Complete mission report including trajectory reconstruction, spacecraft systems analysis, retrofire anomaly investigation, and scientific experiment results. Documents the 402 km overshoot root cause analysis.

2. **Carpenter, M. Scott. "Pilot's Flight Report — Mercury-Atlas 7 (Aurora 7)."** In *Proceedings of the Third National Conference on the Peaceful Uses of Space*, NASA, 1963. Carpenter's own account of the mission, including the firefly investigation methodology and the fuel management timeline.

3. **Kraft, Christopher C. Jr. "Mercury Mission Control Operations — Lessons Learned."** NASA internal report, 1963. Kraft's post-mission analysis documenting the flight director's perspective on fuel management, communication protocols, and the decision-making framework for real-time mission modifications.

## Organism Sources

4. **Lantz, Trevor C. and Turner, Nancy J. "Traditional Phenological Knowledge of Aboriginal Peoples in British Columbia."** Journal of Ethnobiology, Vol. 23, No. 2, pp. 263-286, 2003. Documents seasonal harvesting practices for Oplopanax horridus among First Nations of British Columbia.

5. **Inui, Taichi et al. "Antiproliferative Activity of Oplopanax horridus: The role of Sesquiterpenes."** Planta Medica, Vol. 76, No. 4, pp. 364-369, 2010. Modern pharmacological validation of devil's club bioactive compounds.

6. **Moerman, Daniel E. "Native American Ethnobotany."** Timber Press, 1998. Comprehensive ethnobotanical database documenting 38+ nations' use of Oplopanax horridus.

7. **Hitchcock, C. Leo and Cronquist, Arthur. "Flora of the Pacific Northwest."** University of Washington Press, 1973. Taxonomic description, distribution, and habitat notes for Oplopanax horridus.

## Biographical Sources

8. **Washington, Booker T. "Up from Slavery: An Autobiography."** Doubleday, Page & Co., 1901. Washington's own account of building Tuskegee Institute and his philosophy of practical education.

9. **Harlan, Louis R. "Booker T. Washington: The Making of a Black Leader, 1856-1901."** Oxford University Press, 1972. Definitive biography covering Washington's early life and Tuskegee founding.

## Bird Sources

10. **Moore, William S. "Inferring Phylogenies of Flicker Subspecies."** Molecular Phylogenetics and Evolution, Vol. 4, pp. 335-350, 1995. Genetic analysis of Northern Flicker subspecies including the Red-shafted (C. a. cafer) and Yellow-shafted (C. a. auratus) forms.

11. **Stark, R. D., Dodenhoff, D. J., and Johnson, E. V. "A Quantitative Analysis of Woodpecker Drumming."** Condor, Vol. 100, pp. 350-356, 1998. Acoustic analysis of woodpecker drumming rates, confirming species-specific temporal patterns including the flicker's ~25 beats/second rate.
