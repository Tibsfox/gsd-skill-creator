## 1. Mission Overview

Artemis II is the first crewed flight of NASA's Space Launch System (SLS) and Orion spacecraft. It is the first crewed mission beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in December 1972 — a 53-year gap. Four astronauts will fly a free-return lunar trajectory over approximately 10 days, testing Orion's life support, navigation, and communication systems in the deep space environment before subsequent Artemis missions land humans on the Moon.

The Orion crew module is designated **CM-003 "Integrity"**.

### Mission Firsts and Records

| Record | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| First woman beyond LEO | Christina Koch |
| First person of color beyond LEO | Victor Glover |
| First non-US citizen beyond LEO | Jeremy Hansen (Canadian) |
| Most people in deep space simultaneously | 4 (exceeds Apollo 8's record of 3) |
| Fastest crewed reentry | ~40,000 km/h (Mach 32, 25,000 mph) |
| Greatest crewed flight distance from Moon | ~7,600 km (4,700 mi) beyond the far side |
| First crewed test of optical comms from deep space | O2O system, 260 Mbps downlink |

## 3. Vehicle Specifications

### 3.1 Space Launch System (SLS) Block 1

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Total height | 98 m (322 ft) |
| Liftoff mass | 2,610,000 kg (5,750,000 lb) |
| Total thrust at liftoff | 39 MN (8,800,000 lbf) |
| Payload to TLI | >27,000 kg (59,500 lb) |
| Payload to LEO | 95,000 kg (209,000 lb) |
| Launch pad | LC-39B, Kennedy Space Center |

**Core Stage:**
- 4x RS-25D engines (E2047, E2059, E2062, E2061)
- Sea-level thrust per engine: 2,281 kN (513,000 lbf)
- Combined vacuum thrust: 9.1 MN (2,049,200 lbf)
- Propellant: LH2 (144,000 kg) + LOX (840,000 kg)
- Burn time: 480 seconds
- Core stage height: 64.6 m (212 ft), diameter: 8.4 m (28 ft)

**Solid Rocket Boosters (2x 5-Segment):**
- Combined sea-level thrust: 29.2 MN (6,560,000 lbf)
- Combined vacuum thrust: 32 MN (7,200,000 lbf)
- Booster height: 54 m (177 ft)
- Burn time: 126 seconds
- Separation at ~3,100 mph, ~30 miles altitude

**Interim Cryogenic Propulsion Stage (ICPS):**
- 1x RL10C-2 engine
- Thrust: 110.1 kN (24,800 lbf)
- Max burn time: 1,125 seconds
- Provides orbit insertion and TLI burn

### 3.2 Orion Spacecraft (CM-003 "Integrity")

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Crew module diameter | 5.02 m (16 ft 6 in) |
| Crew module length | 3.3 m (10 ft 10 in) |
| Crew module shape | 57.5-degree frustum, blunt spherical aft |
| Launch mass (CM) | 10,400 kg (22,900 lb) |
| Landing mass (CM) | 9,300 kg (20,500 lb) |
| Pressurized volume | 19.56 m3 (690.6 ft3) |
| Habitable volume | 8.9 m3 (316 ft3) — 50% more than Apollo |
| Parts count | 355,056 individual parts, 77,150 types |

**European Service Module (ESM-2):**
- Built by Airbus Defence and Space for ESA
- Launch mass: 15,461 kg (34,085 lb)
- Dry mass: 6,185 kg (13,635 lb)
- Provides propulsion, power (solar arrays), oxygen, water
- Combined spacecraft mass: 26,520 kg (58,467 lb)
- Total with Launch Abort System: 33,446 kg (73,735 lb)
- Delta-v capability: up to 1,050 m/s (3,445 ft/s) after ICPS separation

**Heat Shield:**
- Material: AVCOAT ablative (silica fibers + resin in fiberglass-phenolic honeycomb)
- Heritage: Apollo and Shuttle programs
- **Issue from Artemis I:** Unexpected char loss — gas trapped in AVCOAT caused cracking during reentry
- **Resolution:** Modified reentry trajectory (steeper descent angle reduces thermal exposure time). Heat shield NOT replaced. Independent review (Dec 2024) confirmed safety margins. Orion structure validated under conditions exceeding expected extremes. Artemis III heat shield will incorporate permeability design improvements.

**Life Support:**
- Design life: 21.1 days undocked, 6 months docked
- Atmosphere: N2/O2 at 101.3 kPa (sea level) or reduced 55.2-70.3 kPa
- Payload return: 100 kg (220 lb)
- Glass cockpit derived from Boeing 787

**Recovery:**
- Water landing only — parachutes of Nomex cloth (Apollo/Shuttle heritage)
- Recovery vessel: US Navy San Antonio-class amphibious transport dock
- Splashdown zone: Pacific Ocean near San Diego

## 4. Mission Timeline

### 4.1 Pre-Launch Sequence

| Time | Event |
|------|-------|
| T-48h | Launch countdown begins |
| T-24h | Crew health checks, suit-up prep |
| T-6h | Crew ingress to Orion |
| T-2h | Close hatch, cabin leak check |
| T-10m | Terminal countdown autosequence |

### 4.2 Launch & Ascent

| Time | Event |
|------|-------|
| T-0 | Liftoff — SLS ignition, 39 MN thrust |
| T+2m | SRB separation (twin 5-segment boosters at ~3,100 mph, ~30 mi altitude) |
| T+8m | Core stage MECO + stage separation |
| T+18m | ICPS burn — orbit insertion |

### 4.3 Earth Orbit & TLI

| Time | Event |
|------|-------|
| ~T+2h | ICPS perigee raise burn — high Earth orbit (~70,000 km apogee, ~23.5 hr period) |
| ~24h | Earth orbit operations — life support checkout, proximity ops with ICPS |
| | CubeSat deployment (5 international payloads) |
| ~T+24-48h | Trans-Lunar Injection (TLI) burn via ESM OMS engine |
| Post-TLI | ICPS separation, Orion free flight on free-return trajectory |

### 4.4 Outbound Transit

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| Day 2-3 | Outbound coast, mid-course correction if needed |
| Day 3-4 | Approaching lunar sphere of influence |

### 4.5 Lunar Flyby

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| Day 4 | Closest approach — ~6,513 km (4,047 mi) from lunar far side surface |
| Day 4+ | Free-return trajectory gravity assist — 7,600 km (4,700 mi) beyond Moon |

### 4.6 Return Transit

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| Day 5-8 | Return coast, trajectory refinement burns |
| Day 9 | Approaching Earth |

### 4.7 Entry, Descent, Landing

| Day | Event |
|-----|-------|
| Day ~9.5 | Service module jettison |
| Day ~9.5+ | Atmospheric reentry at ~40,000 km/h (Mach 32) |
| Day ~10 | Parachute deploy + splashdown in Pacific Ocean near San Diego |

**Note on Reentry Profile:** Originally planned as skip reentry (bounce off atmosphere then re-enter). Changed to steeper direct-entry profile due to Artemis I heat shield char loss findings. Reduced thermal exposure time while maintaining safety margins.

## 5. Science Payloads & Technology Demonstrations

### 5.1 AVATAR (A Virtual Astronaut Tissue Analog Response)
- Organ-on-chip investigation mimicking individual astronaut organs
- First deep space test outside ISS and Van Allen Belt
- Studies radiation and microgravity effects on human health

### 5.2 ARCHAR (Artemis Research for Crew Health And Readiness)
- Movement and sleep monitors worn before, during, and after mission
- Saliva samples to test immune biomarkers
- Radiation exposure and isolation effects assessment

### 5.3 O2O Optical Communications System
- 4-inch (100 mm) telescope with two gimbals mounted on Orion
- Modem and control electronics integrated into spacecraft
- Ground stations: California and New Mexico
- Downlink rate: up to **260 megabits per second** (vs ~2 Mbps for S-band radio)
- First crewed deep space test of optical/laser communications

### 5.4 CubeSats (5 International Secondary Payloads)

| CubeSat | Country | Purpose |
|---------|---------|---------|
| TACHELES | Germany | Electrical component impact studies in deep space |
| ATENEA | Argentina | Radiation shielding, GPS, communication systems |
| K-RadCube | South Korea | Dosimeter testing in deep space |
| Space Weather CubeSat-1 | Saudi Arabia | Space weather measurement in high Earth orbit |
| Avionics Unit | TBD | Avionics technology demonstration |

## 8. Current Tracker Assessment

The existing `index.html` tracker includes:

**Working well:**
- Live countdown to 2026-04-01T22:24:00Z (confirmed correct)
- Mission phase detection (pre-launch → launch → transit → lunar → return)
- Crew roster with roles and agencies
- Vehicle specifications
- Mission profile data
- Moon phase calculation (synodic month, accurate)
- KSC weather from NOAA API (KXMR station, verified working)
- Canvas trajectory visualization with Bezier curves
- Orion position marker interpolated along trajectory
- Responsive design

**Updates needed based on research:**
1. Lunar closest approach: tracker says ~8,900 km — research says **~6,513 km** (4,047 mi from far side surface). Need to verify which figure is canonical. The 8,900 km may be from an earlier trajectory design.
2. Max distance from Earth: tracker says ~380,000 km — research says 7,600 km **beyond** the Moon (~392,000 km from Earth). Minor discrepancy.
3. Missing: Heat shield reentry profile change (skip → direct entry)
4. Missing: O2O optical communications system details
5. Missing: Science payloads (AVATAR, ARCHAR)
6. Missing: CubeSat secondary payloads
7. Missing: Backup crew (Douglas, Gibbons)
8. Missing: JPL Horizons integration for post-launch real tracking
9. Missing: DSN dish status integration
10. Missing: Launch coverage schedule
11. Timeline could be more detailed (Earth orbit phase, CubeSat deploy, TLI via ESM not just ICPS)

**Trajectory model refinement:**
- Current model: simple 42%/58% outbound/return split on Bezier curves
- Reality: ~24h in high Earth orbit before TLI, then ~3-4 day outbound, flyby, ~4-5 day return
- The HEO phase (23.5-hour orbit at ~70,000 km apogee) is not represented
