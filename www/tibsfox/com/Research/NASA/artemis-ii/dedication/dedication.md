## Why Sophie Germain

Sophie Germain taught herself mathematics from books in her father's library during the French Revolution, when she was locked in the house for safety. She couldn't attend lectures at the Ecole Polytechnique, so she obtained lecture notes and submitted work under the pseudonym "Monsieur Le Blanc." Lagrange discovered her true identity and became her mentor. She corresponded with Gauss under the pseudonym for years.

Her work on elastic surfaces — how materials vibrate, how they hold under stress — won the grand prize from the Paris Academy of Sciences in 1816. That mathematics underlaid the calculations used to build the Eiffel Tower. Her contributions to Fermat's Last Theorem provided foundations for centuries of subsequent work.

She was never given a degree. She was never appointed to a position. She did the work anyway.

Artemis II sends the first woman beyond low Earth orbit. Sophie Germain did mathematics beyond every boundary placed in front of her. The AVCOAT heat shield protecting Orion's crew at Mach 32 is an elasticity problem. The resonance is structural.
