#31 Half a Century of the Theory of Synchronization — Yoshiki Kuramoto

arXiv:2602.20505, Feb 2026 · arXiv
The master speaks: Kuramoto himself reviews 50 years of coupled oscillator theory — from the original model through chimera states to modern applications. Published February 2026. Our firefly simulation implements the model he invented. Our circadian drift research (ARCHeR, paper #27) uses his framework. The creator of the theory is still writing about it.
TSPB Layer 1 (Unit Circle):Kuramoto model: dθ_i/dt = ω_i + (K/N) Σ sin(θ_j - θ_i) Each oscillator lives on the unit circle. Phase coupling via sine. Order parameter r = |1/N Σ e^(iθ_j)| measures synchronization. When K > K_c, spontaneous order emerges from disorder.
The man who discovered how fireflies synchronize is still finding new things in the equation. Fifty years and counting.

#32 Enhanced Synchronization with Proportional Coupling in Kuramoto Networks

arXiv:2603.29648, March 31, 2026 · arXiv
Published on launch day: A new coupling scheme where interaction strength scales with frequency detuning between oscillator pairs. Higher detuning = stronger coupling. This is the opposite of the standard model (uniform K). Result: synchronization threshold drops dramatically. Direct application to our vehicle.js firefly model and to multi-agent coordination — agents that are most different should communicate most.
TSPB Layer 3 (Trigonometry):Proportional coupling: K_ij = K_0 × |ω_i - ω_j| Standard: K_ij = K/N (uniform) Proportional lowers K_c because the hardest-to-sync pairs get the most help. Application: agent model profiles — budget agents get more oversight than quality agents.
Help the ones who need it most. The math confirms the intuition.

#33 HERA: Multi-Agent RAG with Evolving Orchestration

arXiv:2604.00901, April 1, 2026 · arXiv
Published during TLI countdown: Hierarchical framework where agent topologies evolve through experience accumulation. Global level optimizes which agents work together; local level refines individual agent behavior via credit assignment. 38.69% improvement over baselines on 6 benchmarks. Sparse exploration yields compact, high-utility agent networks — the system learns to use fewer agents, not more.
TSPB Layer 8 (L-Systems):HERA topology: tree of agents, pruned by experience GSD parallel: convoy model, where mayor assigns agents based on past performance Key insight: emergent self-organization → sparse networks outperform dense ones Our evidence: 4 agents for synthesis beats 8 agents with crosstalk
More agents is not better agents. The system that learns to use fewer, better-matched agents wins. We proved this empirically with GSD convoys.

#34 Typed Chain-of-Thought: A Curry-Howard Framework for Verifying LLM Reasoning

arXiv:2510.01069, submitted ICLR 2026 · arXiv
Formal verification of reasoning: Introduces Proof-Carrying Chain-of-Thought (PC-CoT) — each reasoning step generates a typed faithfulness certificate, like a type-checked proof. Typed Reasoning Graphs represent dataflow as bipartite graphs. This is the Curry-Howard correspondence applied to LLM output: proofs are programs, types are propositions, CoT steps are proof steps. First rigorous bridge between type theory and LLM reasoning verification.
TSPB Layer 7 (Proofs):Curry-Howard: propositions = types, proofs = programs PC-CoT: reasoning_step : StepType certifies the step is valid If the type checks, the reasoning is faithful (by construction) Connection to our Nyquist verification: coverage = type completeness
If you can type-check a proof, you can type-check a chain of thought. The bridge between formal verification and AI safety is type theory.
