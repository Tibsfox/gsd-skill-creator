# TSPB Mathematics — Artemis II

*Populated post-mission with real parameters from flight data.*

## Primary Layer: Layer 4 — Vector Calculus

**Concept:** Free-return trajectory mechanics — the mathematics of a spacecraft that uses lunar gravity to curve its path and return to Earth without a propulsive burn.

### Topics to Develop

1. **Free-return trajectory** — restricted 3-body problem (Earth-Moon-Orion), patched conics, sphere of influence transitions
2. **Trans-lunar injection** — Tsiolkovsky rocket equation with real Orion parameters (Isp, mass ratio, delta-v)
3. **Optical link budget** — O2O laser communications: photon flux at 384,400 km, diffraction limit of 100mm aperture, 260 Mbps at lunar distance
4. **Heat shield ablation** — thermal flux as function of entry angle, AVCOAT char rate, direct vs skip reentry comparison
5. **Orbital mechanics** — HEO period calculation (23.5 hours at 70,000 km apogee), vis-viva equation

## Secondary Layers

- **Layer 7 (Information Theory):** O2O optical link capacity, Shannon limit at lunar distance
- **Layer 3 (Trigonometry):** Flyby geometry, closest approach angle, gravity assist deflection
- **Layer 1 (Unit Circle):** Free-return as closed curve, trajectory as parametric function of time
