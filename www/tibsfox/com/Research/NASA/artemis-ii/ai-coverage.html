Anthropic published a radar chart showing the gap between theoretical AI capability and observed AI usage across 22 occupational categories. The gap is enormous — AI could theoretically cover most knowledge work, but observed usage barely registers. We have 213 research projects and 10 months of AI-assisted production. The Three-Layer Finding

Finding 1: The Anthropic Gap is real — but it's a choice, not a limit

Anthropic's data shows observed AI usage at 5-40% of theoretical capability across most categories. Our project shows observed coverage at 60-91% in our active domains. The gap isn't technical — it's adoption. When you actually commit to AI-assisted research production across domains, the coverage approaches the theoretical ceiling.

Finding 2: The embodiment boundary is sharp

Categories where our coverage drops to near zero — Healthcare, Protective Service, Personal Care, Construction — share one trait: the work requires a body. AI can write about healthcare but can't examine a patient. AI can research construction materials but can't pour concrete. The theoretical-vs-observed gap in physical domains is real and structural, not a matter of adoption.

Finding 3: Cross-domain coverage is our actual contribution

Our Rosetta Stone framework maps 31 concepts across 10 clusters. The same AI session that writes ecology research also writes circuit analysis, music theory, and legal compliance. This cross-domain fluency is what the Anthropic chart can't show — it measures categories independently, but AI's real power is connecting them.

Methodology

How we scored

Theoretical (Anthropic): Values estimated from the published radar chart. These represent what percentage of tasks in each occupation AI could theoretically perform based on task-level analysis.

Observed (Anthropic): Actual AI usage rates observed in their study. The gap between theoretical and observed is the adoption gap.

Our Coverage: Measured from actual project data. For each category, we counted: research projects with published content, source code files, college department curricula, and documentation. Score = (active projects in category / total possible scope) normalized to 0-1, weighted by content depth (file count, line count, cross-reference density). A score of 0.91 for Computer & Math means we have deep, published, AI-assisted content covering 91% of the tasks AI could theoretically handle in that domain.
