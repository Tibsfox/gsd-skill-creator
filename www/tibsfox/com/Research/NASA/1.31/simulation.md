### A2. Python: Venus Transfer Orbit Visualizer

**What it is:** Top-down view of the inner solar system showing the Hohmann transfer ellipse from Earth to Venus, with the 1962 launch window highlighted. Shows where Mariner 1 should have gone and where it ended up (the Atlantic Ocean, 290 miles from Cape Canaveral).

### A3. Godot 4: Range Safety Decision Game

**What it is:** The player is the Range Safety Officer. A rocket launches and begins deviating. The player must decide when to send the destruct command — too early wastes a good mission, too late risks populated areas. Score is based on optimal timing.

### A4. GLSL Shader: Barn Swallow Aerial Pursuit

**What it is:** A fragment shader simulating a Barn Swallow chasing insects in flight. The swallow's forked tail generates differential aerodynamic forces visualized as flow lines. The insect's path is random; the swallow's pursuit demonstrates real-time guidance corrections — the biological smoothing that Mariner 1's software lacked.

### A5. Blender: T+293 Seconds — The Destruction of Mariner 1

**What it is:** A 60-second animation of Mariner 1's launch and destruction. The Atlas-Agena B lifts off, flies normally for 4.5 minutes, then begins oscillating as the guidance error amplifies. At T+293s, the destruct command fragments the vehicle. Debris falls to the Atlantic.

### A6. Arduino: Smoothing Filter Demonstrator ($25)

**What it is:** Physical demonstration of the moving average filter. A noisy analog signal (from a potentiometer with deliberate jitter) feeds into an Arduino. One output shows the raw signal driving an LED (oscillating wildly). The other shows the smoothed signal (stable). A toggle switch enables/disables smoothing — the "overbar switch."

## B. Machine Learning

### B1. Code Bug Detection with Transformer

**What it is:** Fine-tune a small code model to detect missing mathematical operations in Fortran-like pseudocode. Training data: pairs of (correct code with smoothing, buggy code without smoothing). The model learns to flag missing averaging operations.

### B2. Nest Success Prediction from Construction Parameters

**What it is:** Train a classifier on simulated Barn Swallow nest data to predict structural failure from construction parameters (mud ratio, fiber content, surface roughness, drying time, pellet count). The student discovers that single-point failures (one bad pellet) are harder to predict than systematic failures (wrong mixture throughout).

## C. Creative Arts

### C1. Story: "The Overbar"

**What it is:** Short fiction from the perspective of the overbar — the symbol that was never written, watching the equation it should have been part of run without it, knowing that the smoothing it represents will never happen. The story is about absence and consequence.

### C2. Audio: Mariner 1 Destruct Sequence (FAUST DSP)

**What it is:** Sound design piece. Three phases: (1) clean launch — engine roar, stable telemetry beeps; (2) guidance oscillation — telemetry beeps become erratic, pitch and frequency modulated by the feedback instability; (3) destruct — sharp silence, then falling debris tones, fading to ocean waves. The Barn Swallow's call enters as the waves recede — the rebuilder arrives.

### C3. Circuit: Analog Smoothing Filter ($12)

**What it is:** An RC low-pass filter that physically demonstrates the overbar. A noisy input signal (generated by a 555 timer with random variations) passes through the filter. The oscilloscope (or Arduino ADC + OLED) shows the raw input (noisy) and filtered output (smooth). The filter IS the overbar — the physical circuit that the software was supposed to implement.
