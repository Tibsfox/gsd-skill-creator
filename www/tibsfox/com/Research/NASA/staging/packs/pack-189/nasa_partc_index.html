Learning Tiers

Novice — age 10+, no prerequisites, metaphor-first
Intermediate — high school / intro college, mechanism-first
Advanced — undergraduate / enthusiast, formalism available

The Four-Stage Teaching Arc

A
Explain
Plain language + one analogy per tier. The thing a student can say after this stage.
B
Demonstrate
Real NASA data from the mission. What the instrument measured. How to read it.
C
Practice
Try Session (Novice) or problem set (I/A). Student produces an observable result.
D
Assess
2–4 mastery questions. One misconception corrected. Advancement condition stated.

Sample Deliverables

Study Guide Sample — Novice Tier — Enceladus Subsurface Ocean (Cassini Part C)

Analogy: Imagine cracking the shell of a hard-boiled egg and finding the white inside still liquid. The shell is Enceladus's ice crust. The liquid inside is the ocean. The crack at the south pole is where pressure from below pushes water out into space.

One thing to remember: Enceladus has a liquid ocean beneath its ice — and that ocean contains the chemical ingredients that life uses on Earth.

Try Session — Feel the Pressure — Enceladus Geysers · Novice · 20 min · Materials: plastic bag + water

Fill a zip-seal bag halfway with water. Squeeze from the sides and watch where the water tries to go. Press a dimple into the surface and feel the pressure push back. The Connection: Warm liquid water inside Enceladus, heated by Saturn's tidal forces, pushes against the ice from below. Where the ice is thinnest, the water escapes as vapor — the geysers Cassini flew through. What to think about: If you were designing a spacecraft to find an ocean under ice, what would you look for?

How to Run Part C After Part B

  1. Confirm Part B is complete: v1.50.{MISSION_SLUG} release exists in .planning/outputs/missions/{MISSION_SLUG}/.
  2. Verify {MISSION_SLUG}.ck.yaml contains a populated science_results list (produced by Part B Wave 3).
  3. Hand the template and Part B output to gsd-skill-creator:
    gsd run skill-creator --template nasa_partc_template.pdf --input {MISSION_SLUG}.ck.yaml
  4. Wave 0 runs immediately: concept inventory built, learning graph constructed, cross-mission overlaps detected.
  5. Waves 1A and 1B run in parallel: study guides and exercises produced for all concepts at all tiers.
  6. Wave 2 (Opus): Claude teaching scripts produced and analogy quality reviewed across all concepts.
  7. Wave 3: Assessments, CK YAML entries, and cross-mission links finalized.
  8. Approve CAPCOM Wave 3B gate. Release tagged as v1.50.{MISSION_SLUG}.EDU.

Recompile the Template

xelatex -interaction=nonstopmode nasa_partc_template.tex
xelatex -interaction=nonstopmode nasa_partc_template.tex
xelatex -interaction=nonstopmode nasa_partc_template.tex

Custom boxes: samplebox (study guide, green), trybox (Try Session, amber), claudebox (teaching note, brown). Modify colors via \definecolor in preamble.
