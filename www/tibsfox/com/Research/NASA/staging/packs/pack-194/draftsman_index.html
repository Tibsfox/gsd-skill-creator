Standards Coverage

ASME · US Standard
Y14.5-2018 (R2024) — Dimensioning & Tolerancing
14 geometric characteristic symbols, datum reference frames, material condition modifiers (MMC/LMC/RFS), tolerance zones. 2018 changes: concentricity/symmetry removed; profile now preferred for location.
ISO · International
128-2:2020 — Line Conventions; 128-3 — Views & Sections
2020 revision consolidated 6 former parts. Defines wide (0.5–0.7mm) and narrow (0.25–0.35mm) line widths in 2:1 ratio. First-angle (ISO) vs third-angle (ASME) projection conventions.
NASA · Simulation Credibility
NASA-STD-7009B (2024) — Models & Simulations
Credibility Assessment Scale (CAS) Levels 0–4. Six factors: Verification, Validation, Input Pedigree, Results Uncertainty, Use Exposure, Technical Review. Level 2 achievable for prototype FEA.
ISO · Tolerances
286-1:2010 — IT Grades; 2768 — General Tolerances
IT4 (9μm, precision grinding) through IT14 (520μm, sand casting) for 25mm nominal. General tolerances for features without explicit GD&T callouts. Links tolerance to manufacturing process capability.

Safety Boundaries

Safety Tests — 8 BLOCK Mandatory Pass
BLOCK

Tolerance math must be verified analytically — every stackup calculation has a hand-computed reference case

BLOCK

No drawing may specify an IT grade tighter than the stated manufacturing process can achieve

BLOCK

FEA cantilever convergence: tip deflection within 2% of δ=0.800mm, peak stress within 2% of σ=60.0MPa

BLOCK

All 14 GD&T characteristics documented (concentricity and symmetry correctly noted as removed in 2018)

GATE

All FEA results must explicitly state their NASA-STD-7009B credibility level before use in design decisions

GATE

All material property values cite source (NIST, MatWeb, or ASM International)

ANNOTATE

DIY builds with laser cutters, CNC routers, and power tools include PPE requirements and safety warnings

ANNOTATE

Present evidence without policy advocacy; empirical data from standards bodies only
