The Tape Recorder Revolution: Explorer 1 could only transmit data in real-time when visible from ground stations (~10% of each orbit). Explorer 3 added a miniature tape recorder designed by George Ludwig at the University of Iowa. It recorded continuously, then played back compressed at 4x speed over ground stations. This gave scientists the first complete radiation profile of an orbit, confirming the Van Allen belts.

Controls: RECORD simulates one orbit of data collection. PLAYBACK replays the tape at 4x speed. COMPARE shows what Explorer 1 would have seen (gaps where no ground station was visible).
