Why Coverage Matters: Explorer 1 relied on real-time telemetry — data transmitted directly to ground stations. With stations spread around the equator, each station could receive data for only ~5-10 minutes per 115-minute orbit. Total coverage: ~10-15%. The gaps were exactly where the radiation was highest (over regions with no stations). Explorer 3's tape recorder stored data for the entire orbit, achieving 100% coverage and confirming what Explorer 1 could only suggest.
