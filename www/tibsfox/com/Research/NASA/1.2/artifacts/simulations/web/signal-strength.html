Pioneer 1 Signal Propagation — Pioneer 1 transmitted at 300 milliwatts (0.3 W) on 108.06 MHz. As the spacecraft receded from Earth, signal strength dropped following the inverse-square law. The animation shows power density at the ground station decreasing with distance. Key altitudes are marked: the inner Van Allen belt (3,500 km), the slot region (8,000 km), the outer belt (16,000 km), and apogee (113,854 km). The 76-meter Lovell Telescope at Jodrell Bank had sufficient sensitivity to receive the signal even at apogee — the combination of dish gain and low receiver noise temperature provided a link margin that kept the signal well above the noise floor throughout the 43-hour mission. Without Jodrell Bank's large aperture, some of Pioneer 1's data would have been lost.
