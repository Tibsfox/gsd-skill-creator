Pioneer 1 Van Allen Belt Oscilloscope — This display simulates the radiation count rate as Pioneer 1 flew through the Van Allen belts during its 43-hour mission. The Y-axis shows Geiger-Mueller tube clicks per second. The trace draws the radiation profile as the spacecraft ascends through the inner belt (peak ~3,500 km), crosses the slot region, passes through the outer belt (peak ~16,000 km), coasts to apogee at 113,854 km, and returns. Enable audio to hear the GM tube clicks — faster clicking indicates higher radiation. The detector saturates in the inner belt core, causing the measured count rate to drop despite increasing radiation — the phenomenon that initially confused the Pioneer team.
