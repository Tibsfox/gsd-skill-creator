Simulation & Creative Arts

Build, Sink, Recover, Create — what to build, train, visualize, and compose

Hardware: RTX 4060 Ti, 60GB RAM, i7-6700K Organism: Enteroctopus dofleini Bird: Harlequin Duck

A. Simulations — What to Build Locally

A1. Python: Capsule Flooding and Sinking Rate Calculator

Libraries: numpy, matplotlib  |  Difficulty: Beginner-Intermediate  |  Duration: 2–3 hours

Models the complete post-hatch-failure sequence: water ingress through the open hatch, rising capsule mass, helicopter load limit comparison, the moment the capsule becomes unliftable, release, and terminal sinking velocity through 4,900 meters. Adjustable parameters include hatch diameter, sea state, helicopter payload, and ocean depth.

4 plots: (1) Capsule mass vs time — the blue area crosses the red helicopter limit at t≈9s, with the hook attachment time at t=60s far to the right. (2) Depth vs time with GPO range shaded at 0–1500 m and Harlequin Duck range at 0–20 m. (3) Velocity vs depth showing terminal velocity of ~3.5 m/s reached within 100 m. (4) Pressure vs depth — the linear simplicity is the point.

Key learning: The helicopter needed 60+ seconds to hook up. The capsule became unliftable in ~9 seconds. The gap between 9 and 60 is mathematical proof that Liberty Bell 7 was lost the moment the hatch blew.

A2. Python: Explosive Bolt Failure Probability Analysis (Monte Carlo)

Libraries: numpy, matplotlib  |  Difficulty: Intermediate  |  Duration: 3–4 hours

Monte Carlo simulation modeling probability of premature hatch detonation under varying temperature, vibration, and electrical noise. Defines probability distributions for environmental parameters and a detonator activation threshold model. Runs 100,000 simulated post-splashdown scenarios.

4 plots: (1) Pie chart of failure causes — thermal/vibrational, electrical, pilot mechanical. (2) Probability vs temperature showing sharp rise above 40°C. (3) Histogram of thresholds at failure. (4) Cumulative probability over time after splashdown.

Key learning: The Monte Carlo consistently attributes the majority of premature detonations to thermal + electrical mechanisms, with pilot error as a statistically unlikely minority cause.

A3. Web: “Save Liberty Bell 7” — Helicopter Recovery Game

Technology: HTML5 Canvas + JavaScript  |  Target: 1920×1080  |  <1200 lines

A physically accurate helicopter recovery game where the player must hook the capsule and lift it before it floods and sinks. The capsule floods at ~100 L/s. The helicopter has limited payload. The game is unwinnable under actual conditions — proving viscerally that no pilot could have saved the capsule. Scoring: “Lewis’s Choice” (pilot saved, capsule lost) is the historically accurate correct outcome.

Key learning: The moment the player hooks the capsule and sees the lift gauge go deep red — the engine chip light flashing, the capsule pulling the helicopter down. This is not a skill problem. It is a MATH problem.

A4. Web: Harlequin Duck Turbulence Navigator

Technology: HTML5 Canvas + JavaScript  |  Target: 1920×1080  |  <1000 lines

Interactive fluid dynamics simulation: control a Harlequin Duck navigating a PNW mountain stream. Rocks, rapids, eddies, varying current velocities. Dive to the stream bottom for caddisfly larvae, manage energy, find eddy resting spots behind boulders. Real-time Reynolds number display (~461,000, fully turbulent). Force balance panel shows drag, propulsion, current, buoyancy, and net force.

Key learning: The first time the player rests in an eddy and watches flow particles stream past, then ventures into the main current and is immediately swept downstream. The Harlequin Duck’s survival strategy is reading turbulence to find eddies. Liberty Bell 7 had no eddies.

A5. SPICE: Explosive Bolt Firing Circuit

Tool: ngspice  |  Difficulty: Intermediate

Models the electrical initiation circuit for the Mercury hatch. Includes the bridgewire detonator (R=1.0Ω), the 28V spacecraft bus, the firing relay, and the salt water fault path. Normal operation: 8.75A through bridgewire, fires in 0.65 ms. Salt water fault: with Rsalt=100Ω across the open relay, leakage current of 0.271A delivers 50 mJ firing energy in ~0.7 seconds. The capsule was in salt water for minutes.

A6. SPICE: Squelch Circuit — Progressive Radio #20

Tool: ngspice  |  Difficulty: Beginner-Intermediate

Carrier-operated squelch circuit: mutes audio output when no signal is present, eliminating the loud hiss between transmissions. Uses a comparator-driven JFET audio switch with adjustable threshold. Mercury ground stations used squelch circuits on their tracking receivers. The last telemetry from Liberty Bell 7 was silence — the squelch muting a signal that was no longer there because the antenna was 4,900 meters below the surface.

B. Machine Learning — What to Train

B1. Fault Classification from Sensor Telemetry

Libraries: scikit-learn or PyTorch  |  Difficulty: Intermediate  |  Duration: 3–4 hours

Multi-class classifier (CNN or Random Forest) distinguishing “normal,” “thermal anomaly,” “electrical fault,” and “mechanical impact” from simulated capsule telemetry. 8 features per sample at 100 Hz, 5,000 windows per class. Expected: Mechanical impact is the easiest to classify (sharp, distinctive transient) — which means the ABSENCE of a mechanical signature in the actual data is significant evidence against pilot error.

C. Computer Science — FMEA for Recovery Systems

C1. Failure Mode and Effects Analysis

The FMEA reveals the explosive hatch as the highest-risk component in the recovery chain:

  • Severity: 10 (total loss of capsule)
  • Detection: 10 (no sensor warned of impending premature detonation)
  • Occurrence: 3 (unlikely but not impossible)
  • RPN = 10 × 3 × 10 = 300 (action threshold: 100)

If this FMEA had been performed before MR-4 with accurate occurrence estimates, the hatch system would have been flagged for redesign before the flight.

D3. FAUST Synth: Liberty Bell 7 Splashdown

180-second real-time synthesis: splashdown impact (broadband burst + 40 Hz thud), surface floating (wave-modulated noise, metallic hull creaks), hatch detonation (sharp <1 ms transient at 2+ kHz), flooding (water rush through resonant lowpass, air bubbles), sinking (deep 20–30 Hz drone decreasing in frequency, hull compression groans), ocean floor impact (15 Hz thud, sediment settle, silence).

D4. FAUST Synth: Whitewater Harlequin

60-second PNW mountain stream soundscape: continuous stream flow (pink-filtered noise, 800–2000 Hz), rock splash (irregular noise bursts), Harlequin Duck calls (squeaky whistles at 2–4 kHz), forest ambient. At T+30: dive sequence — lowpass filter sweeps from 4 kHz to 500 Hz as the duck submerges, underwater rumble and paddling pulses at ~4 Hz, then return to surface.

E. Problem-Solving Methodology — Failure-Mode-First Design

The inversion: Traditional design asks “How do we make it work?” Failure-mode-first asks “How can it break?”

For the Liberty Bell 7 hatch, redesigned with failure-mode-first thinking:

  • Prevention: Thermally insulate the MDF. Add a second independent safety interlock. Shield the electrical firing path from salt water. Add a mechanical lock on the plunger.
  • Detection: Temperature sensor on hatch frame. Current monitor on firing circuit. Plunger position sensor in telemetry.
  • Mitigation: Secondary flotation collar around hatch opening. Automatic bilge pump. Pre-positioned inflatable pontoon.

The goal is not to prevent all failures. The goal is to ensure that no single failure propagates to total system loss — which is exactly what happened when a single hatch failure cascaded to capsule loss because there was no prevention, no detection, and no mitigation.

F. GSD Improvements — Chipsets

F1. Failure Mode Analysis Chipset

4 chips: fmea-scanner (scan workflow steps for failure modes before execution), cascade-analyzer (trace downstream effects of each failure), mitigation-planner (propose retry/fallback/rollback for high-RPN modes), pre-flight-check (abort if any failure mode exceeds RPN threshold). The pre-flight review that Liberty Bell 7 needed for its explosive hatch.

F2. Octopus Architecture (Distributed Adaptive Response)

4 chips: arm-autonomy (agents handle local problems without central approval), brain-strategy (coordinator sets goals, intervenes only on conflicts), severed-arm-protocol (continue working if disconnected from coordinator), chemoreception (continuous local environment sampling for informed autonomous decisions).

Hemingway wrote the old man and the sea because he understood that the ocean does not negotiate. Santiago's marlin was magnificent. And the sharks took it anyway. Grissom's flight was successful. The hatch blew and the ocean came. The Giant Pacific Octopus in its PNW den understands something about the ocean: the ocean is not an opponent. It is an environment. You do not defeat an environment. You accommodate it. The octopus flows. It adapts. The capsule could not become smaller than the ocean. Grissom could. He squeezed through the hatch, shed the capsule like the octopus sheds a spent spermatophore, and swam for his life. The bell sank. The man swam. The octopus, 4,900 meters below and 1,000 miles northwest, reached one arm out of its den and tasted the cold Pacific water and did not know that a capsule was falling through the Atlantic at 3.5 meters per second, settling toward the floor of an ocean that takes what it takes and does not negotiate the terms.
