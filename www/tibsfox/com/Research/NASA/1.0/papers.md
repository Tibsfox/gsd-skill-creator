### Historical Paper (Connected to Mission)

**Jacobs, Eastman N., Ward, Kenneth E., and Pinkerton, Robert M. (1933). "The Characteristics of 78 Related Airfoil Sections from Tests in the Variable-Density Wind Tunnel." NACA Report 460.**

- **URL:** [https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/19930091108](https://ntrs.nasa.gov/citations/19930091108)
- **Published:** 1933
- **Significance:** This is NACA's defining publication — the report that established the 4-digit and 5-digit airfoil series used worldwide for the next century. Tested in Langley's Variable-Density Tunnel (the same tunnel described in the Minecraft build spec), Report 460 systematically characterized 78 airfoil shapes by varying camber, camber position, and thickness. The report's methodology — systematic parameter variation with rigorous measurement — IS the NACA culture. Every aerospace engineer who has ever looked up an airfoil has used the data from this report or its descendants.

**Connection to Mission 1.0:** Report 460 is what NACA DID for 43 years. It is the fruiting body of the hidden network — the visible evidence of decades of underground work. When the National Aeronautics and Space Act of 1958 transformed NACA into NASA, the institutional culture that produced Report 460 (systematic, empirical, pragmatic) transferred intact into the new agency.

### State-of-the-Art Review

**Anderson, John D. Jr. (2011). "NACA Airfoils: A Historical Perspective." In: *Fundamentals of Aerodynamics*, 5th edition, McGraw-Hill, Chapter 4.12.**

- **Significance:** Anderson's textbook treatment of the NACA airfoil legacy shows that the 4-digit and 5-digit series are still in active use, still taught in every aerodynamics course, and still the default starting point for wing design. The parametric approach NACA pioneered in the 1930s has been extended to computational methods (CFD optimization), but Report 460's data remains the benchmark.
- **Connection:** 93 years after Report 460, NACA's airfoils are still flying. The hidden network's product has outlived the institution that created it.
