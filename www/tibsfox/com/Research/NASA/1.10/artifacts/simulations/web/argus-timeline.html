The Christofilos Effect

Greek physicist Nicholas Christofilos predicted in 1957 that nuclear detonations at high altitude would inject electrons into Earth's magnetic field, creating artificial radiation belts. The electrons would spiral along field lines, trapped between magnetic mirror points. The belts would persist for days to weeks depending on injection altitude. Explorer 4 confirmed every prediction.
