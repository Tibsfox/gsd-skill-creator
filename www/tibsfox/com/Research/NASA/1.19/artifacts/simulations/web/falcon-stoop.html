Physics
Terminal velocity occurs when drag force equals gravitational force. More wing tuck = less drag area = higher terminal velocity.

Drag equation: F = 0.5 × rho × v^2 × Cd × A
Mass: ~1.0 kg (female peregrine)
Cd (tucked): ~0.04 (bullet-like)
Cd (spread): ~0.4 (parachute-like)
Area (tucked): ~0.008 m^2
Area (spread): ~0.12 m^2

At full tuck: terminal velocity = ~107 m/s (240 mph)
Wings spread: terminal velocity = ~12 m/s (27 mph)
