The Complete Ballistic Trajectory of Freedom 7

Mercury-Redstone 3 was a planned suborbital flight. The trajectory is a textbook ballistic arc: boost phase (powered flight to burnout), coast phase (unpowered parabolic arc to apogee and back), and reentry phase (atmospheric deceleration to splashdown). Every number in the parameter table was predicted before launch and confirmed during flight.

SUBORBITAL BALLISTIC TRAJECTORY:

  PHASE 1: POWERED FLIGHT (0 to ~142 s)

    Thrust equation:
      F = m * a + m * g * sin(gamma) + D

    At burnout:
      h_bo     = ~60 km
      v_bo     = 2,294 m/s
      gamma_bo = ~45 degrees

    Velocity components:
      v_horizontal = v_bo * cos(45) = 2,294 * 0.7071 = 1,622 m/s
      v_vertical   = v_bo * sin(45) = 2,294 * 0.7071 = 1,622 m/s

  PHASE 2: COAST / BALLISTIC ARC (~142 s to ~562 s)

    Equations of motion (negligible drag above atmosphere):
      x(t) = x_bo + v_horizontal * t
      y(t) = y_bo + v_vertical * t - (1/2) * g * t^2

    TIME TO APOGEE:
      t_apogee = v_vertical / g = 1,622 / 9.5 = ~170 s

    ALTITUDE AT APOGEE:
      h_max = 60,000 + 1,622 * 170 - 0.5 * 9.5 * 170^2
            = 60,000 + 275,740 - 137,275
            ~ 198 km (actual: 187.5 km)

    DOWNRANGE DISTANCE:
      R = v_bo^2 * sin(2 * gamma_bo) / g
        = (2,294)^2 / 9.8
        = 537 km (actual: 486 km)

  PHASE 3: REENTRY (~562 s to 928 s)

    Peak reentry g: 11.6g
    Duration of peak loading: ~30 seconds

    Total flight: 142 + 420 + 366 = 928 s = 15 min 28 sec

Deposit 2: Manual Control — Human in the Loop (Layer 7)

MERCURY MANUAL ATTITUDE CONTROL:

  Thruster specs: 18 nozzles (6 per axis), ~1 lbf (~4.4 N) each
  Moment arm: ~0.5 m
  Torque per nozzle: ~2.2 N*m

  Angular acceleration:
    pitch: 2.2 / 100 = 0.022 rad/s^2 = 1.26 deg/s^2
    roll:  2.2 / 40  = 0.055 rad/s^2 = 3.15 deg/s^2

  10-degree pitch maneuver time: ~5.64 seconds

HUMAN-IN-THE-LOOP CONTROL BANDWIDTH:

  Sense (visual):    ~200 ms
  Decide (cognitive): ~100 ms
  Act (motor):        ~100 ms
  Vehicle response:   ~50 ms
  Feedback (visual):  ~200 ms
  Total loop:         ~650 ms

  Control bandwidth: f = 1 / (2 * 0.65) = 0.77 Hz

SHANNON INFORMATION RATE:

  3 axes * log2(10 positions) * 0.77 Hz
  = 3 * 3.32 * 0.77
  = ~8 bits/second

  In 5 min of manual control: ~2,400 bits transmitted
  The "spam in a can" argument was refuted in 2,400 bits.

Deposit 3: The Stoop — Peregrine Falcon Terminal Velocity (Layer 4b)

TERMINAL VELOCITY:
  v_t = sqrt(2 * m * g / (rho * C_d * A))

  SOARING: C_d = 0.4, A = 0.06 m^2
    v_t = sqrt(19.6 / 0.0294) = 25.8 m/s = 93 km/h

  STOOP: C_d = 0.07, A = 0.008 m^2
    v_t = sqrt(19.6 / 0.000686) = 169 m/s = 608 km/h (theoretical)
    Actual max: ~390 km/h (limited by structure + control)

  PULLOUT G-FORCE:
    g_pullout = v^2 / (r * g_0)
    At 390 km/h, 50 m radius: (108)^2 / (50 * 9.8) = 23.8g

Fifteen minutes. Nine hundred and twenty-eight seconds from launch to splashdown, and inside those seconds Alan Shepard proved that a human brain is a flight computer. Three animals, three mediums, three trajectories: the rocket through vacuum on a parabolic arc, the falcon through air on a hyperbolic dive, the salamander through water on a ballistic lunge. Each one converting stored energy into kinetic energy into contact with a target.
