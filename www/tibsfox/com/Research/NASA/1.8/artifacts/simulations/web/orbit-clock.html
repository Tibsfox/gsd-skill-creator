Vanguard 1 was launched by the Naval Research Laboratory as part of the International Geophysical Year. It was the first satellite to use solar power and the first whose tracking data revealed that Earth is slightly pear-shaped. Nikita Khrushchev called it "the grapefruit satellite." It has outlasted every spacecraft of its era and will remain in orbit for approximately 240 more years. It does nothing now. It carries no functioning instruments. It simply endures — a 1.47 kg aluminum sphere, smaller than a basketball, tumbling silently through the thermosphere at 8 km/s, catching sunlight on six small silicon rectangles that once powered a transmitter and now power nothing at all.

Organism: Polypodium glycyrrhiza (licorice fern) — an epiphyte that outlasts the branch it grows on.
Dedication: Gottlieb Daimler — who proved that small engines change the world.
