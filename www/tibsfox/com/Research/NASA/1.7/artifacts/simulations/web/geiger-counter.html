Mission Context

Explorer 1 carried a Type 314 Geiger-Mueller tube built by James Van Allen at the University of Iowa.

As the satellite passed through the radiation belt at ~1,500 km altitude, the counter registered increasing hits… then suddenly read ZERO.

The tube’s dead time (100 µs) was longer than the interval between particles. Every particle arrived while the tube was still recovering. Saturation.

Slide the intensity to maximum and watch the count rate peak, then drop to zero. The silence IS the signal.

The Paradox

Nmeasured = Ntrue × e−Ntrue×τ

At high true rates, measured rate approaches zero. Zero counts = maximum radiation.
