# NASA Missing Milestones Research
## Human Stories and Institutional Milestones for the 552-Entry Catalog

*Research Date: 2026-03-29*
*Current catalog: 552 entries (versions 1.0 through 1.202)*
*Philosophy: "We start with the simple idea of counting. We never leave people behind."*

---

## Summary of Findings

The current catalog is strong on spacecraft launches and missions but thin on the PEOPLE, INSTITUTIONS, and COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT that make space exploration meaningful. This research identifies **~150 missing milestones** across 10 categories. These are not spacecraft -- they are the humans who built, flew, supported, dreamed about, and were excluded from spacecraft. Adding them transforms the catalog from a launch manifest into a story about who we are.

## CATEGORY 1: Astronaut Selection Classes

The story of who gets chosen -- and why the criteria change -- is the story of America deciding what "the right stuff" actually means.

### Already in catalog:
- Individual astronaut flights are covered (Mercury through Artemis)
- STS-8 notes Bluford as first African American in space
- STS-7 notes Sally Ride as first American woman in space

## REFLECTIONS ON THE CATALOG'S TRANSFORMATION

The current 552-entry catalog answers: *"What did NASA launch?"*

With these 98 additions, it begins to answer: *"Who built NASA, who was kept out, who got in, who died trying, who watches from Earth, and why does any of it matter to someone who will never ride a rocket?"*

The arc of every category is the same: **expansion of participation**. From 7 test pilots to 18,300 applicants. From segregated computing pools to Congressional Gold Medals. From "women can't be astronauts" to a woman circling the Moon. From classified military launches to citizen scientists classifying galaxies from their kitchen tables.

That is the story worth telling. The spacecraft are how we got there. The people are why we went.
