Explorer 6 traversed both Van Allen radiation belts on every orbit. Its highly elliptical trajectory took it from 237 km (skimming the atmosphere) to 42,400 km (beyond geosynchronous). Kepler's second law: fast at perigee, slow at apogee. Most time spent in the outer belt.
