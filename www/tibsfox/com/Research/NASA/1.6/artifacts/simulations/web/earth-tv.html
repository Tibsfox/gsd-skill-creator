Explorer 6 — Spin-Scan TV Camera — August 1959

EXPLORER 6 TV CAMERA

First Photograph of Earth from Orbit

Status: STANDBY
Spin Rate: 2.8 RPM
Revolution: 0 / 24
Altitude: 237 km
Signal: 100%
Ground Station: South Point, Hawaii
Photocell: Single-element scanner
Scan method: Spin-stabilized sweep
Period: 21.4 sec/revolution
Image: Sunlit crescent of Earth
Date: August 14, 1959
Each revolution of the spacecraft sweeps the photocell across 360° of sky. Where Earth's sunlit crescent falls in the field of view, brightness increases. The signal is transmitted to the ground station and recorded line by line. The image builds up slowly, degrading as the spacecraft climbs toward apogee.
