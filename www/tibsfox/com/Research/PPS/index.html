Picnic Point Parking Structure Feasibility

Ground Level + 2-Floor Parking Structure with Overpass Integration — Snohomish County, WA
Overview Geotechnical Structural Permitting Stormwater Cost Transit Corpus
Mission 2 of 2 in the Picnic Point series. This engineering feasibility study builds on the ecological and cultural baseline established in PPP (Mission 1), examining whether a 3-level parking structure at Picnic Point Park is structurally, geotechnically, and regulatorily viable.

The Structure as an Engineering Problem

Picnic Point Park's parking area sits atop a glacial till bluff overlooking Puget Sound, separated from the beach by the BNSF Railway mainline. The existing surface lot constrains park capacity and offers no integration with the pedestrian overpass that provides the sole beach access. This study evaluates a 3-level parking structure — ground level plus two elevated decks — with a Level 3 deck-to-overpass tie-in bridge, examining the full engineering feasibility envelope: subsurface conditions, seismic design, structural system selection, regulatory pathway, stormwater management, and order-of-magnitude cost.

The study is scoped as a Class 4 pre-design feasibility assessment. All structural and geotechnical claims require licensed professional engineers; all cost figures carry ±50% accuracy bands. The goal is to determine whether the concept merits advancement to schematic design — not to produce construction documents.

Research Modules

Module A: Site Survey & Geotechnical

LiDAR topographic survey, subsurface borings (SPT/CPT), slope stability, foundation system for coastal bluff glacial till, Seismic Design Category D.

WSDOT Geotech USGS MF-2346 IBC 2021

Module B: Structural Engineering

Open-air precast concrete, IBC 2021 seismic Zone D, ASCE 7-22 loads, Level 3 deck-to-overpass tie-in bridge, ADA vertical circulation, 30% drawings.

IBC 2021 ASCE 7-22 Clark Pacific

Module C: Permitting

Snohomish County PDS building permit, SEPA checklist, Shoreline SDP, Critical Areas, BNSF Railway coordination, LDA permit, pathway flowchart.

Snohomish County PDS SEPA BNSF

Module D: Stormwater

Impervious surface calc, LID BMPs, Drainage Manual MR 1–9, oil/water separator, bioretention, permeable pavement, NPDES Phase I, shellfish closure nexus.

WA Ecology NPDES Snohomish County

Module E: Cost & Funding

Class 4 engineer's estimate, RSMeans PNW calibration, $80–100/sqft precast, $28K–35K/stall, two-phase construction, RCO/FHWA/WA WRP grants, 20-year life-cycle.
