Knowledge Gaps

PPS introduces a set of knowledge domains that are almost entirely new to the 239-project corpus. These gaps define the frontier that future infrastructure projects would connect to:

  • Geotechnical engineering — subsurface investigation, bearing capacity, slope stability, foundation design. Zero prior corpus presence. PPS Module A is the founding entry.
  • Permitting and regulatory framework — SEPA, Shoreline Management Act, Critical Areas Regulations, BNSF LDA process. The governance domain is the most underrepresented in the corpus, and Module C is the most isolated module in PPS.
  • Stormwater engineering — LID BMPs, Drainage Manual requirements, oil/water separators, NPDES compliance. Module D connects to ecology through water quality but the engineering methodology is novel.
  • Construction economics — RSMeans cost data, per-stall metrics, life-cycle cost analysis, grant funding mechanisms. Module E's 2-pass convergence reflects the complete absence of financial and economic vocabulary in the corpus.
  • ADA/accessibility engineering — PROWAG, ADAAG, accessible route design. No existing project addresses physical accessibility standards or universal design.
  • Seismic structural design — the USGS connection exists through seismology but structural engineering practice (ductile detailing, lateral force-resisting systems, diaphragm design) is new.

These gaps are not weaknesses — they represent the precise knowledge expansion that PPS contributes to the research series. The infrastructure cluster, anchored by BCM and now expanded by PPS, is the fastest-growing domain in the corpus.

Mission Dependency: PPP → PPS

PPS is Mission 2, dependent on PPP Mission 1 data in three specific areas:

  1. Shellfish closure baseline — PPP's ecology module documents the existing shellfish closure at Picnic Point. PPS Module D (Stormwater) must demonstrate that the parking structure does not worsen this condition.
  2. BNSF overpass context — PPP documents the existing pedestrian overpass as the sole beach access route. PPS Module B (Structural) proposes integrating the parking structure with this overpass via a Level 3 bridge.
  3. Bluff geology — PPP's geology module covers the glacial bluff sediments and coastal processes. PPS Module A (Geotechnical) extends this into engineering-grade subsurface characterization for foundation design.

The corpus cross-reference pipeline detects these dependencies automatically — PPP appears as a connected project in Modules B, C, and D of PPS, confirming the mission dependency relationship through independent keyword analysis.

Pipeline Architecture

The analysis was performed by the Research Co-Processor, a three-layer system:

  1. Unison core — Typed pipeline with 4 IO handlers (handleKnowledgeIO, handleResearchIO, handleOrchestrateIO, handleContextIO), 33 pure tests, 6 IO smoke tests. Content-addressed code ensures pipeline reproducibility.
  2. Python bridge — research-coprocessor-bridge.py connects Unison types to filesystem IO. Scans the 239-project corpus, runs multi-pass keyword expansion with convergence detection, produces HTML output with complex plane metadata.
  3. Corpus scoring — 6-signal OOPS relevance algorithm (keyword overlap, type prior, recency, path proximity, continuity bonus, branch relevance). Each concept mapped to the complex plane where |z| = research depth and arg(z) = breadth/depth balance.

Convergence: the pipeline expands search terms from each pass's discoveries and stops when no new cross-references are found. PPS modules converged in 2–8 passes (PPP: 2–8). The permitting module's 4-pass convergence with only 8 results is the starkest example of novel vocabulary entering an unprepared corpus — the pipeline ran multiple passes but found almost nothing to expand into.

Key insight from PPS: Engineering and regulatory vocabulary occupies a different corpus region than ecology and science. PPP demonstrated that specific keywords (project names, species, agency acronyms) produce rich cross-referencing within ecology. PPS demonstrates that engineering vocabulary (geotechnical, precast concrete, LID BMPs, SEPA) produces sparse but frontier-expanding connections. Both patterns are valuable — depth within existing clusters versus breadth into new domains.
