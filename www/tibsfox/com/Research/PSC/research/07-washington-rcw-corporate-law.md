# Module 7 — Washington State Corporate Law Reference

## RCW Analysis for Washington Profit Corporations

*This module compiles Washington State statutory references relevant to forming and maintaining a profit corporation under Washington law. Covers the full lifecycle from incorporation through annual compliance, share structure, governance, and dissolution.*

**Key Statutes Covered:**
- RCW 23.95 — Uniform Business Organizations Code (registered agents, entity names, annual reports)
- RCW 23B — Washington Business Corporation Act (incorporation, shares, directors, mergers)
- RCW 23.95.400-460 — Registered Agent requirements (13 sections)
- RCW 23B.02 — Articles of Incorporation requirements
- RCW 23B.06 — Share structure and authorization
- RCW 23B.08 — Directors and Officers duties
- RCW 23B.11A — Mergers and share exchanges
- RCW 23B.13 — Dissenters' rights

---

## Incorporation Requirements (RCW 23B.02)

To form a Washington profit corporation, articles of incorporation must include:

1. **Corporate name** — must contain "Corporation," "Incorporated," "Company," "Limited," or abbreviation (RCW 23.95.305)
2. **Number of authorized shares** — at least one class specified
3. **Registered agent** — name and address of an agent in Washington (RCW 23.95.415)
4. **Incorporator** — name and address of at least one incorporator

Optional provisions: purpose, par value, preemptive rights, director liability limits, cumulative voting.

**Filing:** Submit to Washington Secretary of State with $180 filing fee. Processing typically 1-3 business days online.

## Registered Agent Requirements (RCW 23.95.400-460)

Every Washington corporation must maintain a registered agent at all times (RCW 23.95.405). The agent:

- Must have a physical street address in Washington (no PO boxes)
- Accepts service of process on behalf of the corporation
- Must consent to the appointment (RCW 23.95.415)
- Can be the incorporator, a resident, or a commercial registered agent service
- Change of agent requires filing with SoS (RCW 23.95.430)
- Agent may resign with 31 days' notice (RCW 23.95.445)

**Commercial registered agents** (e.g., Northwest Registered Agent, CT Corporation) are listed separately under RCW 23.95.420 and serve many entities simultaneously.

## Annual Compliance

| Requirement | Deadline | Fee | RCW |
|------------|----------|-----|-----|
| Initial annual report | 120 days from incorporation | $70 | 23.95.255 |
| Subsequent annual reports | Anniversary of filing date | $70 | 23.95.255 |
| Maintain registered agent | Ongoing | — | 23.95.405 |
| Keep registered office current | Ongoing | — | 23.95.410 |
| File change of agent/office | Within 30 days of change | $10 | 23.95.430 |

**Failure to file:** Administrative dissolution after notice period (RCW 23.95.605-615). Reinstatement possible within 5 years with back fees and penalties.

## Share Structure (RCW 23B.06)

Washington allows flexible share structure:
- One or more classes of shares
- Par value or no par value
- Preferred shares with specified rights
- Up to 1 billion authorized shares (no additional filing fee regardless of count)
- Board may issue shares without shareholder approval up to authorized amount
- Preemptive rights only if stated in articles

## Directors and Officers (RCW 23B.08)

- Minimum one director required
- Directors owe duty of care and duty of loyalty
- Business judgment rule protects good-faith decisions
- Officers appointed by board (president, secretary, treasurer typical)
- Indemnification of directors/officers permitted (RCW 23B.08.510-580)
- Director liability can be limited in articles (RCW 23B.02.020(2)(d))

## Mergers and Acquisitions (RCW 23B.11A)

Washington permits:
- Merger with domestic or foreign entities
- Share exchanges
- Short-form mergers (parent-subsidiary, 90%+ ownership, no shareholder vote required)
- Dissenters' rights for shareholders who object (RCW 23B.13 — right to fair value appraisal)

## Pacific Coast Business Law Comparison

| Factor | Washington | Oregon | California |
|--------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Filing fee | $180 | $100 | $100 |
| Annual report | $70/year | $100/year | $25 min tax + $800 franchise tax |
| Registered agent | Required (RCW 23.95) | Required (ORS 63.114) | Required (Corp Code 1502) |
| No income tax | Yes (no corporate income tax) | No (6.6-7.6% corporate) | No (8.84% corporate) |
| B&O tax | Yes (0.471-3.3% gross receipts) | No B&O equivalent | No B&O equivalent |
| Name reservation | $30, 180 days | $10, 120 days | $10, 60 days |

Washington's combination of no corporate income tax + B&O gross receipts tax makes it attractive for high-margin, low-revenue businesses (like research institutions) but less favorable for high-revenue, low-margin operations.

## Complex Plane Reading

Corporate formation maps to the complex plane framework from Module 04:

- **Real axis (R):** Legal compliance, filing status, registered agent, annual reports — measurable, verifiable facts
- **Imaginary axis (X):** Institutional trust, market perception, brand credibility — the experienced reality of being incorporated

A newly filed corporation sits at approximately z = 0.1 + 0.05i — minimal real-axis presence (just the filing), near-zero imaginary-axis trust. The path to z = 1.0 + 1.0i requires:
1. Annual report compliance (R grows steadily)
2. Business activity and revenue (R accelerates)
3. Institutional memberships like ORCID (X grows — academic credibility)
4. Published research corpus (X grows — intellectual contribution)
5. Community engagement (X grows — social capital)

A typical trajectory for a new Washington research-focused corporation:
- At filing: z ≈ 0.1 + 0.05i
- Year 1 (compliant, active): z ≈ 0.3 + 0.15i
- Year 3 (established, recognized): z ≈ 0.5 + 0.3i
- Year 10 (institutional): z ≈ 0.8 + 0.7i
