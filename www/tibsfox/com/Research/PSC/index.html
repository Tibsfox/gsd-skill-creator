History is not a timeline. It is a trajectory through a complex plane. Every political event has two components: the real part — what happened, who held power, what laws were enacted — and the imaginary part — what it felt like to be alive in that moment, how people narrated their own experience. The two axes are not redundant. They are orthogonal. Together they define where a society actually is.
