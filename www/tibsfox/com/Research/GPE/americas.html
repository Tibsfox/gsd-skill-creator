Americas Strategy Highlights

🌴
California: The Efficiency Benchmark
Title 24 building codes • CPUC programs • 40% below national intensity

California has kept per-capita electricity consumption flat since the 1970s while the rest of the US increased by 50%. The state's Title 24 building energy codes, the strictest in the nation, are estimated to have saved Californians $100 billion in energy costs. The CPUC's energy efficiency programs have avoided the need for 30 GW of power plant capacity. California's ZNE (Zero Net Energy) mandate requires all new residential construction to produce as much energy as it consumes.

⚡
Quebec: Hydroelectric Advantage
99% renewable electricity • Lowest rates in North America

Quebec's massive hydroelectric infrastructure (Hydro-Quebec operates 63 plants with 37 GW capacity) provides 99% renewable electricity at some of the lowest rates in North America. This clean, cheap energy has attracted data centers and aluminum smelters while maintaining one of the lowest carbon electricity grids globally. Quebec demonstrates how geography and long-term infrastructure investment create lasting competitive advantages in the energy transition.

🏙️
Sao Paulo: Tropical Efficiency
Brazil's economic engine • Ethanol economy • Smart grid modernization

Sao Paulo state accounts for 33% of Brazil's GDP and leads its efficiency programs. Brazil's unique ethanol economy (flex-fuel vehicles dominate) reduces petroleum dependence, while ANEEL's smart grid modernization program targets 100% smart meter coverage by 2030. The state's PMVA (Environmental Monitoring Program) uses AI-assisted satellite monitoring to track deforestation impacts on the energy-water nexus, since Brazil depends on hydropower for 65% of electricity.
