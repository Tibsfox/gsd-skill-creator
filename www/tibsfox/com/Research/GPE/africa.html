Kenya: Geothermal Pioneer
93% renewable electricity • Olkaria geothermal complex • M-KOPA solar

Kenya generates 93% of its electricity from renewables, primarily geothermal (45%) and hydropower (30%). The Olkaria geothermal complex in the Rift Valley is the largest in Africa, with over 800 MW capacity. Kenya's energy efficiency ranking (#8 globally) reflects both this clean grid and innovative off-grid solutions like M-KOPA, which has deployed over 3 million solar home systems using mobile money payments, bringing clean energy to households beyond the grid.

🇿🇦
South Africa: The Load-Shedding Crisis
Coal dependency • Eskom restructuring • Renewable IPP program

South Africa ranks #69 globally with an intensity of 5.70, the worst in Africa's ranked countries. Aging coal plants operated by Eskom have led to years of rolling blackouts (load-shedding), with over 200 days of load-shedding in 2023. The crisis has paradoxically accelerated renewable adoption: the REIPPPP (Renewable Energy Independent Power Producer Procurement Program) has attracted over $20 billion in private investment, and South Africa added more rooftop solar in 2023 than in the previous decade combined.

🇪🇹
Ethiopia: Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam
6.45 GW capacity • Africa's largest hydropower • Green industrialization

Ethiopia's Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam (GERD) on the Blue Nile, with 6.45 GW capacity, will more than double the country's electricity generation. Ethiopia plans to use this clean power base to industrialize sustainably, attracting manufacturing that would otherwise use fossil fuels. The country's current Elite ranking (#6, intensity 0.84) reflects very low consumption (17 TWh for 126 million people), but GERD could maintain efficiency while dramatically expanding access.
