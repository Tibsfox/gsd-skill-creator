MENA Strategy Highlights

🌞
Souss-Massa, Morocco: Solar Ambition
Noor Ouarzazate CSP • 52% renewable target by 2030 • Africa's solar leader

Morocco's Souss-Massa region hosts the Noor Ouarzazate Solar Complex, one of the world's largest concentrated solar power (CSP) installations at 580 MW. The complex includes molten salt thermal storage providing up to 7.5 hours of dispatchable solar energy after sunset. Morocco targets 52% renewable electricity by 2030 and has positioned itself as a clean energy hub connecting African solar resources to European demand via the planned UK-Morocco power cable (3.6 GW, 3,800 km undersea).

🇦🇪
UAE: From Oil to Nuclear & Solar
Barakah nuclear plant • Mohammed bin Rashid Solar Park • Vision 2050

The UAE's Barakah nuclear power plant (5.6 GW, four APR-1400 reactors) provides 25% of the country's electricity with zero carbon emissions, making it the first nuclear plant in the Arab world. Combined with the Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum Solar Park (target: 5 GW by 2030), the UAE is diversifying away from gas faster than any other Gulf state. Dubai's Demand Side Management strategy targets a 30% reduction in energy demand by 2030.

🇮🇱
Israel: Tech-Driven Efficiency
Startup Nation • Solar water heating mandate • Desalination innovation

Israel ranks #17 globally with an intensity of 1.36, the most efficient economy in the MENA region. The country's mandatory solar water heating law (since 1980) saves an estimated 8% of total electricity demand. Israel's water-energy nexus innovation is world-leading: the Sorek B desalination plant produces freshwater at $0.40/m3 using only 2.5 kWh per cubic meter, half the energy of 2000-era plants. These technologies are exported throughout the region.
