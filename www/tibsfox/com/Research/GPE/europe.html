EU Green Deal & REPowerEU
55% emission reduction by 2030 • Energy independence from Russian gas

The European Green Deal targets a 55% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions by 2030. Following Russia's invasion of Ukraine, REPowerEU added urgency with plans to end dependence on Russian fossil fuels. The combined policy response drove the EU's energy intensity improvement to 5.0%/year from 2020-2024, the fastest improvement of any major region.

Key mechanisms: Renovation Wave (35 million buildings by 2030), EU Energy Efficiency Directive (binding 11.7% reduction target), and the EU ETS carbon price reaching record levels above EUR 100/tonne in 2023.

Bavaria: Energiewende Leader
Germany's southern powerhouse • 52% renewable electricity

Bavaria demonstrates how a wealthy industrial region can lead the energy transition. With 52% of electricity from renewables (primarily solar and hydropower), Bavaria has maintained its industrial competitiveness while dramatically reducing energy intensity. The state's 10,000 Houses Program provides grants for energy-efficient retrofits, and its building code exceeds EU minimums.

Scotland: Wind Power Pioneer
97% renewable electricity • Net-zero by 2045

Scotland generated enough wind energy to power every home in the country twice over in 2024. With 97% of electricity from renewables, Scotland is the EU's (and UK's) leading example of how policy commitment translates to rapid transition. The ScotWind offshore leasing round attracted GBP 700 million in investment, targeting 25 GW of new offshore wind capacity.
