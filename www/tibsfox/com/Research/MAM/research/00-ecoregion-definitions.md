# PNW Mammalian Taxonomy — Ecoregion Zone Definitions

**Version:** 1.0 | **Zones:** 8 (7 shared with AVI + 1 cross-cutting fossorial)

---

## Zone 1: Alpine / Subalpine (>5,000 ft)
- **Indicator:** American pika (*Ochotona princeps*) — climate sentinel, talus specialist
- **Keystone:** Mountain goat (*Oreamnos americanus*) — alpine browser, wool source for Coast Salish weaving
- **Characteristic:** Hoary marmot, wolverine (100+ mi circuits), white-tailed ptarmigan (AVI cross-link)
- **Threats:** Climate-driven upslope contraction, pika population declines at lower elevations

## Zone 2: Montane Conifer Forest (2,500–5,000 ft)
- **Indicator:** American marten (*Martes americana*) — old-growth structure dependent
- **Keystone:** Northern flying squirrel — mycorrhizal fungus disperser, Spotted Owl prey (AVI cross-link)
- **Characteristic:** Black bear, fisher (reintroduced WA/OR), red tree vole, deer mouse (first recolonizer after disturbance)
- **Key interaction:** Squirrel-fungus-forest feedback loop (Maser 1998)

## Zone 3: Lowland Forest / Urban (<500 ft)
- **Indicator:** Douglas squirrel (*Tamiasciurus douglasii*) — conifer seed specialist
- **Keystone:** Raccoon — dominant urban mesopredator
- **Characteristic:** Virginia opossum (introduced), deer mouse, Townsend's chipmunk, coyote (urban expansion)

## Zone 4: Foothill / Oak Woodland (500–2,500 ft)
- **Indicator:** Western gray squirrel (*Sciurus griseus*) — Conservation Strategy Species in OR/WA
- **Characteristic:** Brush rabbit, gray fox, California ground squirrel

## Zone 5: Riparian / Wetland / Estuarine
- **Indicator:** American beaver (*Castor canadensis*) — watershed architect
- **Keystone:** Beaver — dam effects on hydrology, fish habitat, riparian vegetation, water storage
- **Characteristic:** River otter, muskrat, Pacific water shrew, mink
- **Key interaction:** Beaver-salmon-bear-eagle nutrient cascade (AVI cross-link to Module 5)

## Zone 6: Coastal / Marine / Pelagic
- **Indicator:** Southern Resident killer whale (*Orcinus orca ater*) — ESA endangered, 73 individuals (2024)
- **Keystone:** Sea otter — kelp forest engineer, controlling sea urchin populations
- **Characteristic:** Harbor seal, Steller sea lion, gray whale, humpback (396 catalogued 2022), harbor porpoise
- **Ecotype differentiation:** Resident (salmon), Bigg's/transient (marine mammals), offshore orcas

## Zone 7: East-Side Steppe / Shrubland
- **Indicator:** Pygmy rabbit (*Brachylagus idahoensis*) — ESA-listed, Columbia Basin population critically endangered
- **Characteristic:** Badger, black-tailed jackrabbit, pronghorn, kit fox
- **Key sites:** Malheur Basin (beaver, river otter in riparian corridors)

## Zone 8: Fossorial / Subterranean (Cross-Cutting)
- **Indicator:** Townsend's mole (*Scapanus townsendii*) — largest North American mole
- **Keystone:** Pocket gophers (5 spp. in OR) — significant soil engineers
- **Characteristic:** American shrew-mole (PNW endemic, sole genus member), coast mole, various shrews
- **Ecological role:** Below-ground ecosystem engineering, soil turnover, mycorrhizal network maintenance
