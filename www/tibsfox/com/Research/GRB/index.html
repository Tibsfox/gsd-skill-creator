A neutron star merger inside the tidal tail of a galaxy merger inside a galaxy group — a merger within a merger within a merger. The r-process elements synthesized in this event — gold, platinum, europium — may escape to the circumgalactic medium, enriching the next generation of stars in the halo. The GSD ecosystem shares this geometry: knowledge lives not in one context window, but in the handoffs between sessions. The tidal tail is a handoff channel.

Research Modules

M1: Burst Properties

Burst Track A · Prompt emission

Fermi GBM detection, IPN triangulation, T90 = 0.94s. Band function spectral fit, Epk = 440 keV. Energetics at three redshift scenarios (z = 0.45, 1.2, 3.0). Amati classification in short-GRB region.

M2: Multi-wavelength Afterglow

X-ray Track A · Localization chain

Swift/XRT to Chandra/ACIS-S: 27x improvement in positional accuracy. X-ray temporal and spectral analysis. Optical/NIR non-detections. Faint host G* identification via HST WFC3/F160W.

M3: Host Galaxy Environment

Host Track B · Galaxy group

Putative host G* characterization (F160W = 26.00 mag). Galaxy group at z = 0.453 confirmed via VLT/MUSE. Tidal tail morphology extending 180 kpc. Formation timescale ~700 Myr.

M4: Merger Demographics

Demographics Track B · Population context

69-galaxy short-GRB host population from Fong/Nugent surveys. Optical selection effects. Galaxy-group occurrence rate (Pcc ~ 3-4%). Delay-time distribution implications.

M5: r-Process Enrichment

r-Process Synthesis · CGM implications

Ejecta dispersal in tidal tails. Fade radius calculation. Cosmic context: Reticulum II, AT2017gfo kilonova, Milky Way halo stars. Future instrumentation: NewAthena/X-IFU redshift degeneracy breaking.
