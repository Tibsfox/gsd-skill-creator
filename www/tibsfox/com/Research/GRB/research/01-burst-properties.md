## 1. Classification and Detection

GRB 230906A was detected by the Fermi Gamma-ray Burst Monitor (GBM) on 2023 September 6 at 12:55:07.15 UT. Simultaneously recorded by INTEGRAL SPI-ACS, Konus-Wind, and Mars Odyssey HEND. InterPlanetary Network (IPN) triangulation refined the Fermi localization from 2.8 degrees down to a narrow error box of 12' x 2', enabling Swift/XRT follow-up.

## 3. Energetics at Three Redshift Scenarios

The distance degeneracy requires presenting results at each plausible redshift:

| z | E_gamma,iso (1-10000 keV) | Jet angle lower limit | Origin scenario |
|---|---------------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| 0.45 | ~2 x 10^51 erg | theta_j >= 6.7 deg | Galaxy group |
| 1.2 | ~1.4 x 10^52 erg | theta_j >= 4.5 deg | Typical host luminosity |
| 3.0 | ~6.8 x 10^52 erg | theta_j >= 2.9 deg | Flat SED / Balmer break |

Collimation-corrected total energy at all three redshifts: (0.8-5) x 10^50 erg, well within the NS merger energy budget. Jet break time lower limit t_j > 6 days (90% confidence) from XMM-Newton.

## 4. Light Curve Morphology

Two-component structure: first soft peak followed by 0.3 s quiescence, then main emission episode. This morphology is consistent with NS merger models showing precursor emission from magnetospheric interaction.
