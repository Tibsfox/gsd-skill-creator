Research Scope

  • Historical & Astronomical Foundations: The Babylonian MUL.APIN catalogues (c. 1000 BCE) and the “Three Stars Each” texts. The Hired Man (LÚ.HUÑ.GÁ) becoming Aries the Ram. The Bull of Heaven (GU4.AN.NA) as Taurus. The formalization of the twelve-sign zodiac (5th century BCE). Hipparchus, Ptolemy, and the tropical system. Goldschneider’s personology (1994).
  • Astronomical Mechanics: Earth’s axial precession — 1° every 72 years, full Great Year of ~25,772 years. The solar disc’s half-degree diameter and physical cusp straddling. Tropical vs. sidereal zodiac systems and their ~23–24° current offset. IAU constellation boundaries vs. equal-30° astrological signs. The First Point of Aries in Pisces.
  • Mythological & Cultural Dimensions: Mars (Ares/Nergal) across Babylonian, Greek, and Roman traditions. Venus (Aphrodite/Ishtar/Inanna) with dual Taurus-Libra rulership. The cosmic lovers and their offspring (Cupid, Harmonia, Phobos, Deimos). The Bull of Heaven in the Epic of Gilgamesh. Spring equinox rites. The alchemical marriage of fire and earth.
  • Personality & Archetypal Analysis: The “Cusp of Power” profile from Goldschneider, Tarot.com, Astrology.com, and LoveToKnow. Six trait domains (leadership, determination, strategic timing, humor, sensuality, independence). Six shadow traits (controlling, blunt, ego-absorbed, emotionally blind, stubborn, mercenary). Fire-to-earth elemental transition. Cardinal-to-fixed modal shift.
  • The Cusp Controversy: Three positions mapped — mathematical denial (Desimone, AstroTwins), narrow 12-hour window (Robbins), and broader chart dynamics (McBride). The progressed Sun mechanism. Goldschneider’s 48-period personology. What cusp belief reveals about meaning-making at categorical boundaries.
