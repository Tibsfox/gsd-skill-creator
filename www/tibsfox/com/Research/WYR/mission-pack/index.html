Contents

Stage 1 — Vision Document

The working forest question: Can Weyerhaeuser's model of private timberland management at scale align profit with ecological integrity? Vision narrative, 5 core problems, 6 research modules, 12 success criteria, chipset configuration, and the through-line connecting to the Amiga Principle.

Stage 2 — Research Reference

Six-module evidence base: Corporate profile & history (125 years, $7.1B revenue, 10.4M acres), forest management & SFI certification (140+ indicators, 3 environmental controversies), carbon & climate solutions ($100M NCS target, IFM credits at $29/credit), mass timber & wood products (30–60% lower embodied carbon), biodiversity & ecosystem services (Mt. St. Helens restoration, watershed protection), and economic & community impact ($5.3B returned to shareholders 2021–2024). Full bibliography from USFS, GAO, peer-reviewed journals, and professional organizations.

Stage 3 — Mission Package

Fleet activation profile: 11 components across 4 waves with 3 parallel tracks. 134,000 estimated tokens. 17-agent crew manifest. 38-test plan (8 safety-critical / mandatory-pass). Dependency graph, cache optimization strategy, token budget, and verification matrix mapping all 12 success criteria to specific tests.

Usage Instructions

  1. Download the PDF mission package
  2. Feed to gsd-skill-creator via Claude Code: the document contains everything needed for autonomous execution
  3. The orchestrator should begin with Wave 0 (schema + source index), then launch 3 parallel tracks in Wave 1
  4. Human approval (CAPCOM) required at wave boundaries
  5. Safety-critical tests are mandatory-pass gates — any failure blocks progression
