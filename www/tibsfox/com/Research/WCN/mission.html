Mission Pack

The original research brief and mission package that initiated the Westercon research project. A comprehensive three-stage document — Vision, Research Reference, Mission Package — covering the full pipeline from institutional analysis to structured execution.

Source Documents

The mission pack comprises a multi-hundred-line TeX mission document covering the complete Westercon research scope: 78-year institutional history from 1948 founding through proposed 2028 retirement, LASFS governance structure, the Westercon bylaws system, site selection mechanics, cultural impact analysis including the birth of furry fandom, convention traditions catalog, and decline/retirement analysis. Squadron activation profile with parallel research tracks.

Research Scope

  • History & Origins: Founded 1948 by “Doc” Daugherty and LASFS. One-day event with 77 attendees grew into a 78-year rotating institution. July 4th weekend tradition. Combined Worldcon events. Growth decades through peak and decline.
  • The Traveling Model: Site selection by member vote, two years in advance. Geographic eligibility west of 104th meridian or Hawaii. The bidding process, host committee bootstrapping, and the advantages and costs of a convention with no permanent address.
  • Governance: LASFS service mark, bylaws system, business meeting tradition, failsafe provisions, Kevin Standlee’s governance stewardship. Relationship to WSFS/Worldcon. Democratic fan governance as a living system.
  • Cultural Legacy: Birthplace of organized furry fandom (1985 Prancing Skiltaire party). The Other Change of Hobbit bookstore (1977). Convention governance template. Regency dance tradition. C/FO and anime fandom cross-pollination.
  • Decline & Retirement: COVID disruption, repeated bid failures (2023-2025), volunteer exhaustion, the Allen/Deneroff assessment, bylaws repeal motion (first passage July 2025), ratification vote July 2026, projected final convention 2028.

Key Connections
