## 1. When a Firm License Is Required

Washington requires a **CPA firm license** when a firm performs or offers to perform attest services or compilation services AND has an office in Washington. The trigger is the combination of three factors:

1. The firm has **an office in Washington** (physical presence in the state)
2. The firm **performs attest services** (RCW 18.04.025(1)) OR **compilation services** (RCW 18.04.025(5))
3. The firm uses the CPA title in connection with those services

A firm providing tax preparation, advisory, or consulting services under the CPA title does NOT require a Washington firm license — provided the services are performed through individuals with valid practice privileges and the firm is licensed in the home state of the performing individual.

## 2. Firm License Requirements (RCW 18.04.195)

### 2.1 License Conditions

A CPA firm license requires:
- At least one partner/officer/shareholder who is a Washington CPA licensee responsible for supervising attest services
- A majority of firm ownership (financial interest and voting control) held by licensed CPAs
- Non-CPA owners registered with the Board (see Section 3)
- The firm must maintain a current valid license in each jurisdiction where it has offices

### 2.2 License Renewal

CPA firm licenses renew on the same three-year cycle as individual licenses, with renewal April 30 of the renewal year. At renewal, the firm must certify:
- Peer review status and results (see Section 4)
- Changes in ownership structure or responsible supervisory licensees
- Compliance with all Board rules

### 2.3 Services Triggering the Firm License Requirement

| Service | Firm License Required? |
|---------|----------------------|
| Audit (financial statement) | Yes |
| Review engagement | Yes |
| Compilation | Yes (though peer review exempted as of Nov 2024) |
| Agreed-upon procedures | Yes |
| Tax preparation | No |
| Bookkeeping | No |
| Advisory/consulting | No |
| Using "CPA" in firm name only | No (but individual licensure required) |

## 3. Non-CPA Firm Owners (WAC 4-30-110)

Washington allows non-CPAs to hold ownership interests in CPA firms. This is a significant feature that recognizes the business reality that accounting firms may have investors, business managers, or IT specialists who are not licensed accountants.

### 3.1 Registration Requirements

Non-CPA owners must:
- Register with the Board as nonlicensee firm owners
- Meet good character requirements (no disqualifying criminal history or professional misconduct)
- Comply with Washington state laws and all Board rules
- Not hold a majority of financial interest or voting control (CPA majority required)

### 3.2 Restrictions

Non-CPA owners:
- May not perform or supervise attest or compilation services
- May not hold themselves out as CPAs
- May not use the CPA title in any capacity
- Are subject to Board jurisdiction for conduct affecting the firm's compliance

**Source:** WAC 4-30-110; RCW 18.04.195.

## 4. Peer Review (WAC 4-30-130)

### 4.1 The Peer Review Requirement

All Washington CPA firms performing attest services must participate in a **board-approved peer review program**. Peer review is an independent evaluation of a firm's accounting and auditing practice to assess compliance with professional standards.

### 4.2 Board-Approved Peer Review Programs

| Program | Administered By | Covers |
|---------|----------------|--------|
| PCAOB Inspection | Public Company Accounting Oversight Board | Public company audits |
| AICPA Peer Review | AICPA through administering entities (e.g., Colorado Society of CPAs) | Non-public company attest work |
| Other Board-approved programs | Varies | Board-case-by-case approval |

### 4.3 Enrollment Timing

Firms must enroll in a peer review program **before issuing their first attest report**. This is a pre-condition, not a post-hoc requirement. A firm that issues its first audit report and then enrolls in peer review is already out of compliance.

### 4.4 Reporting at Renewal

Every firm must certify peer review status and results with each firm license renewal application by April 30 of the renewal year. The certification includes:
- Current peer review program enrollment
- Date of most recent peer review
- Peer review rating (pass, pass with deficiency, fail)
- Any remedial actions required

### 4.5 Consequences of Peer Review Failure

If the Board determines through peer review or otherwise that a firm's practices do not conform to professional standards, it may:
- Require **remedial CPE** for the firm's personnel
- Mandate submission of **future work for monitoring review**
- Take disciplinary action including **license revocation**

**Source:** WAC 4-30-130; WBOA QAR Program page.

## 5. The November 2024 Compilation Exemption

Effective **November 21, 2024**, the Board amended WAC 4-30-130 to exclude compilation services from the mandatory peer review requirement.

### 5.1 What Changed

**Before November 21, 2024:** Firms performing compilations were required to participate in the Board's peer review program.

**After November 21, 2024:** Compilations are excluded from mandatory Board peer review. A firm that performs only compilation work (no audits, no reviews) is no longer required to be enrolled in a peer review program as a Board licensing condition.

### 5.2 The AICPA Carve-Out

AICPA members who are enrolled in the AICPA Peer Review program may still be required to undergo peer review for compilation work through their **AICPA membership obligations** — separate from the Board's licensing conditions. The November 2024 change only affects Board requirements; AICPA membership requirements operate independently.

### 5.3 Practical Impact

Smaller firms that perform compilations but no higher-level attest services now have reduced peer review administrative burden. However, firms that are also AICPA members must verify whether their AICPA membership still requires peer review for compilation work.

**Source:** WBOA QAR Program page; WSCPA peer review changes advisory; WAC 4-30-130 (amended November 2024).

## 6. Quality Assurance Review (QAR) Program

The Board's own Quality Assurance Review program is an additional oversight layer beyond standard peer review. The QAR program allows the Board to directly evaluate firms' compliance with professional standards when:
- A firm's peer review reveals systemic deficiencies
- A complaint or investigation triggers enhanced oversight
- The Board exercises discretionary oversight authority

QAR reviewers are Board-authorized practitioners who conduct on-site evaluations of work papers, engagement files, and quality control systems.

## 7. Firm Mobility and Out-of-State Firms

### 7.1 Firm Mobility Provisions

Out-of-state CPA firms may exercise all privileges of a Washington-licensed firm without obtaining a Washington firm license if:
- The firm holds a valid CPA firm license in its home state
- All individuals performing work in Washington hold active CPA licenses
- The firm meets QAR requirements
- The firm consents to comply with the Washington Public Accountancy Act

The firm must cease Washington operations immediately if its home-state license becomes invalid or is revoked.

### 7.2 Temporary Practice

Firms from non-reciprocal states may apply for temporary practice privileges for specific engagements, subject to Board approval.

**Source:** WBOA Practice Privilege page; RCW 18.04.195; RCW 18.04.350.

## 8. Firm Name Rules and DBA Prohibition

### 8.1 Name Registration

Every licensed CPA firm must operate under a name registered with the Board. The name on the firm's letterhead, reports, and any public-facing materials must match the Board-registered name.

### 8.2 DBA Prohibition

**No licensed firm may operate under an alias or DBA (doing business as) that differs from the registered name.** This prevents firms from presenting under trade names that obscure their licensed identity.

### 8.3 Succession

When a firm undergoes a name change (partner retirement, merger, rebranding), the firm must notify the Board and update its license accordingly. A firm that has been operating under an old name without updating its registration may face compliance issues at renewal.

## 9. Decision Tree: Does My Firm Need a License?

```
Does the firm have an office in Washington?
├── NO → No WA firm license required
│         (Individual practice privilege may apply)
└── YES → Does the firm perform or offer:
          ├── Attest services (audit, review, AUP)? → YES → Firm license REQUIRED
          ├── Compilation services?                 → YES → Firm license REQUIRED
          └── Only tax/advisory/consulting?         → NO firm license required
                (Individual CPA licensure still required for CPA title use)
```
