## 1. Washington State Board of Accountancy

The Washington State Board of Accountancy (WBOA) is the regulatory body established under the Public Accountancy Act (RCW 18.04) to govern the practice of public accounting in Washington. The Board consists of nine members appointed by the governor: six licensed CPAs (each holding a license for 10 or more years of continuous practice) and three public members who represent the consumer perspective.

The Board meets quarterly — the last Friday of January, April, and July — plus additional meetings as needed. Its physical office is at 711 Capitol Way S., Suite 400, Olympia, WA 98501. All Board decisions, publications, license verifications, and consumer alerts are accessible at [https://acb.wa.gov](https://acb.wa.gov).

### 1.1 Regulatory Authority

The Board exercises authority over:
- CPA individual licensure (initial and renewal)
- CPA firm licensing
- Continuing professional education requirements
- Practice standards and professional conduct
- Disciplinary proceedings and enforcement
- Consumer protection and complaint resolution

The Board's rules are codified in WAC 4-30. When WAC 4-30 conflicts with professional standards published by the AICPA or other bodies, **Board rules prevail** — this override principle is critical and is formally stated in WAC 4-30-048.

## 2. The Three Licensure Pathways (2025)

On October 17, 2025, the Board conducted a public hearing resulting in amendments to WAC 4-30-060 and WAC 4-30-070, effective November 20, 2025. These amendments created three distinct pathways to CPA licensure. All three pathways share a common accounting concentration requirement: **24 semester hours (36 quarter hours) in accounting subjects** AND **24 semester hours (36 quarter hours) in business administration** at the undergraduate or graduate level.

| Pathway | Education | Credit Hours | Experience | Expiration |
|---------|-----------|-------------|------------|------------|
| **1** | Post-baccalaureate (master's or higher) with accounting concentration | 150 semester hrs | 1 year / 2,000 hrs | None |
| **2** | Baccalaureate with accounting concentration | 120 semester hrs | 2 years / 4,000 hrs | None |
| **3 (Legacy)** | Baccalaureate + 30 additional semester hrs with accounting concentration | 150 semester hrs | 1 year / 2,000 hrs | December 31, 2035 |

### 2.1 The Innovation in Pathway 2

Pathway 2 is the key innovation of the 2025 amendments. It allows candidates with a standard bachelor's degree and accounting concentration (120 credits) to pursue licensure without completing additional post-baccalaureate hours — provided they complete **two years** (4,000 hours) of qualifying experience instead of one. This change directly addresses the national pipeline shortage of CPA candidates by reducing the education barrier while maintaining experience standards.

### 2.2 The Expiration Clock on Pathway 3

Pathway 3 is the legacy pathway that existed before the 2025 amendments. It expires December 31, 2035. Candidates who begin under Pathway 3 must complete all requirements before that date; after December 31, 2035, only Pathways 1 and 2 will be available.

**Source:** WBOA, WAC 4-30-060, WAC 4-30-070 (amended October 2025).

## 3. The CPA Examination

### 3.1 Structure

The Uniform CPA Examination consists of **four sections**: three core sections (mandatory for all candidates) and one discipline section (candidate's choice):

| Section | Code | Type | Content |
|---------|------|------|---------|
| Auditing and Attestation | AUD | Core | Audit process, professional standards, assurance services |
| Financial Accounting and Reporting | FAR | Core | GAAP, GASB, financial statements, governmental accounting |
| Regulation | REG | Core | Federal taxation, business law, ethics |
| Information Systems and Controls | ISC | Discipline | IT audit, cybersecurity, data analytics |
| Business Analysis and Reporting | BAR | Discipline | Managerial accounting, financial analysis, reporting |
| Tax Compliance and Planning | TCP | Discipline | Advanced taxation, planning strategies |

Each section is scored on a scale of 0–99. The passing threshold is **75**. All four sections must be passed; the candidate selects exactly one discipline section.

### 3.2 Washington's 36-Month Window

Washington grants candidates a **36-month window** from the first passing score to complete all four sections — one of the longest windows in the nation. Scores achieved outside this window expire and must be retaken.

### 3.3 Application and Fees

Applications are submitted through NASBA's Okta-based CPA portal. Fees as of 2026:
- Education evaluation fee: $96
- Exam application fee: $103
- Per-section fee: $262.64

Total first-attempt cost for all four sections: approximately $1,254 (evaluation + application + 4 sections).

**Source:** WBOA Exam page; NASBA; UWorld CPA Requirements guide.

## 4. Ethics Requirement

### 4.1 Initial Licensure Ethics Exam

All Washington CPA candidates must pass the **AICPA Professional Ethics exam** — an 8-hour self-study course covering the AICPA Code of Professional Conduct — with a score of **90% or better**. This is a one-time requirement for initial licensure; it does not repeat at renewal.

### 4.2 Renewal Ethics Course

For each three-year renewal cycle, CPAs must complete a **4-hour Board-approved Washington State ethics and regulations course**. At least 60% of the course content must cover:
- The Public Accountancy Act (RCW 18.04)
- Board rules and policies (WAC 4-30)
- Variances between Washington law and the AICPA Code of Conduct

The purpose of this ongoing requirement is to keep practitioners current with state-specific rules, which may differ materially from national standards.

**Source:** WBOA; WAC 4-30-132.

## 5. Experience Verification

### 5.1 Who May Verify

Experience must be verified by an **active Washington CPA licensee** who has held their license for a minimum of five years. The verifying CPA must determine — through interview or course completion — that the applicant is knowledgeable of the Public Accountancy Act and Board Rules.

### 5.2 Record Retention

Both the applicant and the verifying CPA must maintain documentation for **a minimum of three years** (WAC 4-30-072).

### 5.3 Qualifying Experience Types

Qualifying experience may be obtained through:
- Public accounting practice
- Industry or government employment
- Academic employment (in certain cases)
- One or more employers, with or without compensation
- Combination of full-time and part-time work

### 5.4 Hour Requirements by Pathway

| Pathway | Hours Required | Equivalent Duration (Full-Time) |
|---------|---------------|--------------------------------|
| Pathway 1 | 2,000 hours | ~1 year |
| Pathway 2 | 4,000 hours | ~2 years |
| Pathway 3 | 2,000 hours | ~1 year |

**Source:** WBOA Experience page; WAC 4-30-070, WAC 4-30-072.

## 6. Continuing Professional Education (CPE)

### 6.1 The 120-Hour Cycle

Washington requires **120 hours of CPE** per three-year renewal cycle, with the following constraints:
- Minimum **20 hours per year** (not frontloaded)
- Maximum **24 hours** may be non-technical subjects
- **4-hour Board-approved ethics course** mandatory per cycle

### 6.2 Acceptable CPE Formats

The Board accepts a wide range of learning modalities:

| Format | Duration | Credit Hours |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Nano learning | 10–50 minutes | 0.2–1.0 |
| Formal learning (with attendance tracking) | 50+ minutes | Standard |
| Self-study | Variable | Variable |
| University courses | Semester/quarter | Converted |
| Peer review committee service | Variable | Variable |

**Not permitted:** CPA exam review courses do not qualify for CPE credit.

### 6.3 Acceptable Subject Areas

CPE subjects must fall within recognized professional competence areas:
- Accounting and auditing
- Taxation
- Management advisory services
- Information technology
- General professional competence

### 6.4 Renewal Mechanics

License renewal occurs every three years, with renewal open **January 1 through April 30** of the renewal year.
- Standard renewal fee: $230
- Late renewal (May 1–June 30): $230 + $100 late fee = $330

**Source:** WAC 4-30-132, WAC 4-30-133, WAC 4-30-134; WBOA CPE Guidelines.

## 7. License Renewal, Inactive Status, and Reinstatement

### 7.1 Active vs. Inactive Status

As of July 1, 2024, Washington converted inactive certificates to **inactive license status**. Inactive licensees:
- Must attach the term "inactive" whenever using the CPA title
- May **not** practice public accounting
- Maintain the credential at reduced fee

### 7.2 Reinstatement

CPAs who allow their license to lapse may apply for reinstatement. Requirements include:
- Completing all CPE hours that would have been required during the lapsed period
- Paying back fees and any applicable penalties
- Board approval for extended lapses

### 7.3 Retirement Status

CPAs who have permanently retired from practice may apply for retired status, which allows use of the CPA title without renewal or CPE requirements — provided they do not perform or offer to perform accounting services.

## 8. Interstate Reciprocity and CPA Mobility

### 8.1 Individual Mobility (RCW 18.04.350)

A CPA licensed in another state may practice in Washington without obtaining a Washington license under the **practice privilege** provisions of RCW 18.04.350, provided:
- The individual holds a valid active license in good standing in their home state
- They have passed the Uniform CPA Exam and met experience requirements equivalent to Washington's
- They consent to comply with the Public Accountancy Act
- They cease practicing in Washington if their home-state license becomes invalid

### 8.2 Reciprocal Licensure

CPAs from other states who wish to obtain a Washington license (rather than using practice privilege) may apply for reciprocal licensure, with requirements varying based on whether their home state's requirements are substantially equivalent to Washington's.

## 9. The Protocol Stack Model

The ACC through-line describes accounting regulation as a layered protocol stack:

```
Layer 4: Professional Standards (Application Layer)
  → AICPA Code, PCAOB standards, FASB/GASB codifications
  → Local application rules, specialty standards

Layer 3: Board Rules (Operational Layer)
  → WAC 4-30 (all practice rules, ethics, CPE, enforcement)
  → OVERRIDE: Board rules prevail over Layer 4 when in conflict

Layer 2: State Statute (Jurisdictional Authority Layer)
  → RCW 18.04 (Public Accountancy Act)
  → Defines who may practice and under what constraints

Layer 1: Federal Standards (Base Layer)
  → GAAP hierarchy (GASB/FASB Codification)
  → Federal tax law (IRC), securities regulations
```

The key override rule: when Layer 3 (WAC 4-30) conflicts with Layer 4 (AICPA standards), **Layer 3 wins**. CPAs cannot assume AICPA compliance equals Washington compliance.

This architecture mirrors the GSD skill-creator system: global defaults exist, but local rules (the Board, the safety warden) override them when they conflict.
