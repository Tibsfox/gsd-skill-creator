## 1. GAAP Hierarchy for Washington Practitioners

Washington practitioners follow the GAAP hierarchy as established by GASB Codification Section 1000. The hierarchy defines which sources are authoritative and in what order they are consulted when preparing financial statements.

### 1.1 For Governmental Entities

| Category | Sources |
|----------|---------|
| **Category A** (Authoritative) | GASB Statements and Interpretations |
| **Category B** (Authoritative) | GASB Technical Bulletins, Implementation Guides, AICPA literature specifically cleared by GASB |
| **Other literature** (Non-authoritative) | FASB, FASAB, IASB, AICPA — considered only in absence of Category A/B treatment |

Washington local governments use the Washington State Auditor's Office **BARS (Budgeting, Accounting and Reporting System)** manuals — both the GAAP Manual and the Cash Basis Manual — which implement GASB standards in Washington's specific governmental context.

### 1.2 For Private-Sector Entities

For private-sector businesses in Washington:
- **FASB Accounting Standards Codification** is the authoritative source of U.S. GAAP
- GASB standards do not apply
- BARS manuals do not apply

### 1.3 The Annual Reporting Requirement for Local Governments

Under RCW 43.09.230, Washington local governments must file annual financial reports with the State Auditor's Office. These reports must comply with GAAP (GASB) or cash-basis accounting as prescribed by the Auditor. This creates a mandatory GASB compliance obligation for every county, city, school district, fire district, and public utility district in Washington.

**Source:** SAO BARS GAAP Manual, Section 3.1.2; GASB Codification Section 1000; RCW 43.09.230.

## 2. The Board-Rule-Prevails Principle (WAC 4-30-048)

WAC 4-30-048 states: when Board rules differ from professional standards published by AICPA or other standard-setting bodies, **Board rules prevail**.

This is the most critical compliance point in Washington accounting practice and is frequently misunderstood. The practical implications:

### 2.1 What This Means for Practitioners

1. **AICPA compliance is necessary but not sufficient.** A practitioner who follows the AICPA Code of Professional Conduct exactly may still violate WAC 4-30 if Washington's rules are stricter.

2. **Active monitoring required.** CPAs must independently verify that their practice conforms to WAC 4-30, not just AICPA standards. The Board publishes newsletters and updates when Washington rules diverge from national standards.

3. **Historical example:** The November 2024 change exempting compilations from mandatory peer review is a Washington-specific rule change. It is not reflected in AICPA Peer Review program requirements, which may still apply to AICPA members independently of Board licensing conditions.

### 2.2 The Override Architecture

```
AICPA Code of Professional Conduct
         |
         ▼ (applied unless...)
WAC 4-30 (Board Rules)
         |
         ▼ (override trigger: conflict → WAC prevails)
RCW 18.04 (Statute)
         |
         ▼ (constitutional floor)
Washington State Constitution
```

The architecture mirrors the GSD safety warden pattern: local rules (the Board, the jurisdiction) override global defaults (AICPA standards) whenever they conflict.

**Source:** WAC 4-30-048; WBOA December 2017 Newsletter.

## 3. Independence (WAC 4-30-042)

A Washington licensee must maintain independence in mental attitude in all professional services for which independence is required by professional standards.

### 3.1 Scope of the Independence Requirement

The independence requirement applies to:
- Individual Washington CPA licensees
- CPA firms licensed under RCW 18.04.195
- Nonlicensee firm owners
- Employees of CPA firms
- Out-of-state individuals exercising practice privileges under RCW 18.04.350
- Out-of-state firms operating under RCW 18.04.195

### 3.2 When Independence Is Required

Independence is required for **attest services** — audits, reviews, and certain other engagements. It is not required for all accounting services; tax preparation and advisory services do not inherently require independence in the WAC sense, though conflicts of interest and objectivity requirements still apply.

### 3.3 Independence Impairment

Common independence impairment situations:
- Financial interests in the client (ownership of stock, loans)
- Preparing source documents that will then be audited
- Serving as an officer or director of the client
- Bookkeeping or financial statement preparation for audit clients (self-review threat)
- Close family member relationships with client management

**Source:** WAC 4-30-042.

## 4. Integrity and Objectivity (WAC 4-30-040)

WAC 4-30-040 requires that in **any professional service** — not just attest services — a licensee must:

1. **Maintain objectivity and integrity** in all professional judgments
2. **Be free of conflicts of interest** that could impair objectivity
3. **Not knowingly misrepresent facts** or mislead clients or the public
4. **Not subordinate professional judgment** to the judgment of others when doing so would violate professional standards

### 4.1 Practical Application

Unlike the independence requirement (which is service-specific), the integrity and objectivity requirement applies universally. A CPA preparing tax returns, providing business advisory services, or preparing management reports must maintain integrity and objectivity in each engagement.

The "subordination of judgment" clause is notable: a CPA who signs off on work they know to be incorrect because a supervisor or client pressured them has violated WAC 4-30-040, regardless of the service type.

**Source:** WAC 4-30-040.

## 5. Accounting Principles Compliance (WAC 4-30-049)

A licensee may not express an opinion that financial statements conform with GAAP — nor state that they are unaware of material modifications needed — if the statements contain a material departure from applicable accounting principles.

### 5.1 The Departure Exception

A departure from GAAP is permissible only if:
1. Due to unusual circumstances, the financial statements would be **misleading without the departure**, and
2. The departure is **fully disclosed** in the financial statements

This is a narrow exception. It does not apply to convenience departures, client preferences, or cost-benefit judgments.

### 5.2 Material Departure Consequences

Issuing an unqualified opinion on statements that contain a material GAAP departure is a prohibited act under WAC 4-30-142, subject to disciplinary action including license revocation and fines up to $30,000.

**Source:** WAC 4-30-049.

## 6. Attest and Compilation Services Defined

### 6.1 Attest Services (RCW 18.04.025(1))

Attest services are professional services in which a practitioner **expresses an assurance conclusion** about subject matter or an assertion, based on evidence:
- Financial statement audits (highest level of assurance)
- Reviews (moderate assurance — limited procedures)
- Agreed-upon procedures (no overall conclusion, but specific procedures)
- Other assurance engagements

Attest services require both an **individual CPA license** AND a **CPA firm license** under RCW 18.04.195.

### 6.2 Compilation Services (RCW 18.04.025(5))

A compilation is a service in which a CPA presents financial statements in conformity with financial reporting standards, **without** expressing any assurance. The accountant does not audit or review the statements — they compile them from client-provided data.

As of November 21, 2024, compilations are **excluded** from the Board's mandatory peer review requirements. Note: AICPA members may still face peer review obligations for compilation work through their AICPA membership independently of Board licensing conditions.

### 6.3 Non-Attest Services

Services that do not require a CPA license or firm license (though may use the CPA title):
- Tax preparation and planning
- Bookkeeping and write-up services
- Payroll services
- Business advisory and consulting
- Management accounting

## 7. The Compliance Hierarchy in Practice

For a Washington CPA performing an audit engagement, the compliance stack from top to bottom:

```
1. Washington Public Accountancy Act (RCW 18.04)
   → Statutory authority for everything below

2. WAC 4-30 (Board Rules)
   → Practice standards, CPE, firm licensing, enforcement
   → OVERRIDE: Prevails over AICPA when in conflict

3. AICPA Auditing Standards (GAAS / PCAOB for public companies)
   → Detailed engagement procedures, documentation standards
   → Apply fully unless WAC 4-30 overrides

4. GASB/FASB GAAP
   → Financial reporting standards for the subject matter

5. Firm-level Quality Control Standards
   → SQCS (Statements on Quality Control Standards)
```

## 8. Professional Standards by Service Type

| Service | Licensing Required | Standards | Peer Review |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|-------------|
| Financial statement audit | Individual CPA + firm license | GAAS/PCAOB | Required |
| Review engagement | Individual CPA + firm license | SSARS | Required |
| Compilation | Individual CPA + firm license | SSARS | **Not required (as of Nov 2024)** |
| Tax preparation | PTIN (IRS) | IRC; Circular 230 | Not applicable |
| Bookkeeping | None | None | Not applicable |
| Advisory/consulting | None (but CPA title requires active license) | Varies | Not applicable |
