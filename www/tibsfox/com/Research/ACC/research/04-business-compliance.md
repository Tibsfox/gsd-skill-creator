## 1. The Scope-of-Practice Boundary: Who Needs What Credential

The most common point of confusion for Washington business owners and aspiring practitioners is the boundary between services requiring a CPA license and services open to unlicensed practitioners. The boundary is sharper than most people assume.

### 1.1 Service Classification Table

| Service | Credential Required | Notes |
|---------|-------------------|-------|
| Financial statement **audit** | WA CPA license + CPA firm license | Only licensed CPAs within licensed firms |
| Financial statement **review** | WA CPA license + CPA firm license | Subject to SSARS; peer review required |
| **Compilation** | WA CPA license + CPA firm license | Firm license required; peer review exempted Nov 2024 |
| **Agreed-upon procedures** | WA CPA license + CPA firm license | Specific procedures, no overall assurance |
| **Federal tax preparation** | IRS PTIN | No WA state license required |
| **WA state tax preparation** | No state-specific license | WA does not require state-level tax preparer registration |
| **Bookkeeping** | No license required | General business activity; CPA restriction does not apply |
| **Financial advisory / consulting** | No CPA license required | Unless using "CPA" title (requires active license) |
| **Payroll services** | No license required | Industry-specific software competence only |

### 1.2 Washington's Tax Preparer Exception

Washington does **not** require a state-level tax preparer registration — unlike Oregon, California, New York, Maryland, and Connecticut, which have their own state registration systems. Any individual with a valid IRS PTIN may prepare federal tax returns in Washington without additional state registration.

However, the type of credential determines IRS representation rights:

| Credential | Representation Rights Before IRS |
|------------|----------------------------------|
| CPA (licensed) | Unlimited representation |
| Enrolled Agent (EA) | Unlimited representation |
| Attorney | Unlimited representation |
| Annual Filing Season Program (AFSP) | Limited representation (own clients, returns prepared) |
| PTIN only (no other credential) | Limited representation only |

**Source:** RCW 18.04.345; IRS Tax Professional Credentials guide; WBOA Use of Title page.

## 2. Title Protection (RCW 18.04.345)

Under RCW 18.04.345, **no unlicensed individual or firm** may use the following titles or abbreviations:

- "certified public accountant" / "CPA"
- "certified accountant"
- "chartered accountant"
- "licensed accountant" / "LA"
- "licensed public accountant" / "LPA"
- "public accountant" / "PA"
- "CA"
- Any other designation that implies CPA licensure

**Violation is a misdemeanor.** The Board publishes consumer alerts for unlicensed title use and actively investigates complaints.

### 2.1 Inactive License Title Use

Inactive certificate holders (converted to inactive license status as of July 1, 2024) must attach the term **"inactive"** whenever using the CPA title:
- Permitted: "Jane Smith, CPA (inactive)"
- Prohibited: "Jane Smith, CPA"

Inactive licensees may not practice public accounting or offer accounting services to the public.

## 3. Record Retention Master Table

No single source consolidates Washington's overlapping retention requirements. The table below synthesizes DOR, L&I, IRS, and WBOA requirements into a single reference:

| Record Type | Retention Period | Authority | Notes |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|-------|
| Tax records (B&O, sales, use) | **5 years** | WAC 458-20-254 | Gross receipts, deductions, ledgers, journals, invoices |
| Financial statements | **5 years** | WAC 458-20-254 | Included in DOR record-keeping requirements |
| Payroll records | **3 years** | WA L&I | Employee hours, wages, deductions, workers' comp contributions |
| Personnel records | **3 years post-termination** | WAC 296-17-35201 | Longer than federal 1-year minimum |
| Chemical exposure records | **30 years** | WA L&I / OSHA | Safety data sheets, exposure monitoring |
| Federal income tax returns | **3–7 years** | IRS | 3 years from filing; 7 years if loss deduction claimed |
| Unreported income (>25% of gross) | **6 years** | IRS | Extended statute of limitations |
| Fraudulent returns | **No limit** | IRS | No statute of limitations on fraud |
| CPE documentation | **3 years** | WBOA | CPAs must retain for audit verification |
| CPA experience affidavit | **3 years** | WAC 4-30-072 | Both applicant and verifying CPA |
| Corporate governance docs | **Permanent** | RCW 23B / 24.03A | Articles, bylaws, meeting minutes |
| Bank statements | **7 years** | Best practice | Supports IRS and DOR audit defense |

## 4. Washington DOR Record Retention (WAC 458-20-254)

The Department of Revenue requires that every person required to collect or pay Washington taxes maintain records for **five years** from the date of the transaction or the date the return was filed, whichever is later.

### 4.1 Required Records

DOR-required records include:
- Gross receipts records (sales, income, revenue by classification)
- Records of all deductions and exemptions claimed
- Purchase invoices and supporting documentation
- General ledgers, journals, and sub-ledgers
- Financial statements (balance sheets, income statements)
- Tax returns filed with DOR

### 4.2 Access by DOR

Washington businesses must make DOR records available for examination upon request. The DOR may conduct field audits, desk audits, or correspondence audits within the 5-year window. Records outside the 5-year window are generally protected.

**Source:** Washington DOR, WAC 458-20-254.

## 5. Labor and Industries Payroll Records

### 5.1 Payroll Record Retention

Washington L&I requires employers to maintain payroll records for **three years**. Required records include:
- Employee names, addresses, and Social Security numbers
- Hours worked each day and week
- Hourly rates of pay
- Gross and net wages
- Workers' compensation contributions
- Deductions and the basis for each deduction

### 5.2 The 30-Year Chemical Exposure Exception

For employees exposed to chemicals, Washington requires **30-year** retention of:
- Safety data sheets (SDS / MSDS)
- Exposure monitoring records
- Medical surveillance records related to chemical exposure

This exception applies to any Washington employer with chemical exposure in the workplace — relevant to manufacturing, construction, agriculture, healthcare, and laboratory settings.

**Source:** WAC 296-17-35201; WA L&I Recordkeeping Requirements.

## 6. IRS Federal Record Retention

### 6.1 The 3-Year General Rule

The IRS can generally audit a tax return for **three years** from the due date of the return or the date filed, whichever is later. Business records supporting the return should be retained for at least three years beyond this window.

### 6.2 Extended Periods

| Situation | Retention Period |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Standard return | 3 years from filing |
| Substantial understatement of income (>25% unreported) | 6 years |
| Business bad debt deduction or worthless securities loss | 7 years |
| Fraudulent return | No limit (fraud has no statute of limitations) |
| Employment tax records | 4 years after tax due or paid |

### 6.3 Asset Records

Records relating to the **cost basis of assets** — real estate, equipment, securities — must be retained for as long as the asset is owned plus the applicable retention period after sale. A building purchased in 2010 and sold in 2030 requires records from 2010 through 2033 (3-year post-sale rule).

**Source:** IRS Publication 583 — Starting a Business and Keeping Records.

## 7. Board of Accountancy Professional Records

CPAs must maintain documentation of CPE for **three years** after the renewal period to which the CPE applies. The WBOA may conduct CPE audits and require CPAs to produce certificates, transcripts, and completion records.

The three-year experience documentation requirement (WAC 4-30-072) applies separately: both the applicant and the verifying CPA must retain experience records for three years after CPA licensure.

## 8. Corporate Governance Records (RCW 23B / 24.03A)

Washington corporations (for-profit under RCW 23B, nonprofit under RCW 24.03A) must retain **permanent** records of:
- Articles of Incorporation (or Certificate of Formation for LLCs)
- Bylaws (and operating agreements for LLCs)
- Board minutes and written consents in lieu of meetings
- Shareholder/member records and transfer histories
- Major contracts, leases, and financing agreements

"Permanent" means indefinitely — these records survive the active life of the business and should be retained even after dissolution until the applicable statute of limitations has run on any claims.

## 9. Annual Government Reporting (RCW 43.09.230)

Washington local governments (counties, cities, school districts, special purpose districts) must file **annual financial reports** with the State Auditor's Office. These reports must be prepared in accordance with GAAP or the cash-basis standards prescribed by the Auditor.

### 9.1 Filing Requirements

| Entity Type | Report Type | Deadline |
|-------------|------------|---------|
| Cities over 200 population | Annual financial report | 150 days after fiscal year end |
| Counties | Annual financial report | 150 days after fiscal year end |
| School districts | Annual financial report | 150 days after fiscal year end |
| Special purpose districts | Annual financial report | Varies by district type |

The State Auditor's Office provides BARS manuals (GAAP and Cash Basis versions) to guide preparation and ensure consistency across jurisdictions.

## 10. Practical Compliance Checklist

For a Washington small business (1–20 employees, services industry):

- [ ] Retain DOR tax records for 5 years (WAC 458-20-254)
- [ ] Retain payroll records for 3 years (L&I)
- [ ] Retain employee personnel files for 3 years post-termination (WAC 296-17-35201)
- [ ] Retain federal tax records for minimum 3 years; 7 years for loss claims (IRS)
- [ ] Keep corporate governance documents permanently (RCW 23B)
- [ ] Verify that accounting service provider has appropriate credentials
- [ ] If using "CPA" title: verify current active license at acb.wa.gov
- [ ] If hiring a CPA firm for attest work: verify both individual license and firm license
