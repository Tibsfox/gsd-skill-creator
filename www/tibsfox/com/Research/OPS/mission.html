Mission Package

NASA achieved mission success not through heroic improvisation but through disciplined process. The Systems Engineering Handbook exists because missions matter — because a spacecraft that fails due to a preventable oversight is not just an engineering failure but a failure of process. OpenStack exists because NASA chose to share. This project carries that commitment forward: applying NASA's engineering rigor to cloud infrastructure, then publishing everything so that a person who wants to understand cloud computing does not need a corporate certification budget to learn.

The Through-Line

This project is where the metaphor becomes the method. Since its inception, GSD has drawn on NASA's engineering philosophy as a design guide. The mission crew manifest adapts the flight control room. The vision-to-mission pipeline mirrors the SE Engine. But philosophy without practice is just decoration. When GSD deploys an OpenStack cloud using its own mission management architecture, documented with the same methodology NASA uses for spacecraft, the metaphor stops being a metaphor. It becomes evidence. And because OpenStack itself was born from NASA's commitment to public knowledge infrastructure, the project carries that commitment forward. Everything produced is open — not because open source is a business strategy, but because knowledge created through collective effort toward shared understanding has a moral character that demands sharing.
