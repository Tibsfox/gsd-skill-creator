Origins & Formation

Origins and Formation

Module 1

From the CoCu collective in 1987 Los Angeles through the demo tape that David Byrne called "truly like nothing else." The ten-member experimental workshop, Bono's studio time, jazz training under Jaki Byard, and the baby sisters who gave the band its name.

The Music

Discography

Module 2

Three albums on Luaka Bop: Grand Opening (1994), Sacred Cow (1996), and Into the Oh (2001). Track-level analysis, the story of "Whoever You Are" from a child's traffic song to Billboard #16, and the Mercedes-Benz commercial that revived it five years later.

The People

The Creative Network

Module 3

Susan Rogers (Prince's engineer), Pamelia Stickney (theremin pioneer), James Gadson (legendary session drummer), David Byrne, Laurie Anderson, Glen Phillips. The creative constellation that orbited two musicians from Pomona.

Post-Geggy Tah Trajectories

Module 4

Greg Kurstin's rise from indie band keyboardist to nine-time Grammy winner. Tommy Jordan's steel drums on Jack Johnson's "Flake." Pamelia Stickney's path from a studio introduction to Bob Moog's collaborator. Where the inclusionary wave went next.

The Context

Cultural Context

Module 5

Luaka Bop as curatorial project, the 1990s alternative rock landscape, the "world music" paradox, postmodern inclusionary aesthetics, and why an American band belonged on a label built for the world's outsider music.
