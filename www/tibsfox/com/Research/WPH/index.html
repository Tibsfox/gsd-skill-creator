Alexander Graham Bell's patent #174,465 described a method of transmitting vocal sounds telegraphically. What he actually built was the first node in a network that would reshape human civilization. Every phone call since March 10, 1876 — from the first words “Mr. Watson, come here” through 4.5 billion smartphones alive today — traces a continuous engineering lineage: transducers converting pressure waves to electrical signals, switching systems routing those signals to their destination, and protocols ensuring the conversation arrives intact. The telephone is not a device. It is a protocol stack that learned to fit in your pocket.

Research Modules

M1: Telephone History & Switching Systems

History Track A · Foundations

Bell's patent to Strowger step-by-step. Manual switchboards, crossbar exchanges, #1ESS through #5ESS electronic switching. Signaling System 7 (SS7). The PSTN architecture that carried a century of voice. Electromechanical relay logic as the first programmable network.

M2: Cellular Evolution — 1G through 5G

Network Track A · Wireless generations

AMPS analog cellular through 5G NR. TDMA, CDMA, GSM air interfaces. OFDM and MIMO antenna systems. Frequency reuse geometry, handoff algorithms, cell splitting. Spectrum allocation and the physics of propagation. Each generation's defining engineering trade-off.

M3: VoIP & SIP Convergence

Network Track B · Packet voice

Circuit-switched to packet-switched voice. H.323 to SIP protocol evolution. RTP media transport, codec negotiation (G.711, G.729, Opus). NAT traversal (STUN/TURN/ICE). QoS mechanisms, jitter buffers, MOS scoring. The engineering of making packets sound like circuits.

M4: Smartphone Architecture & Baseband Processors

Hardware Track B · Device internals

Application processor vs. baseband processor dual-core architecture. Qualcomm Snapdragon, MediaTek Dimensity, Samsung Exynos SoC families. RF front-end design, antenna tuning, power amplifier efficiency. Mobile OS kernel architecture (Linux, XNU). Baseband firmware as the most privileged code on your phone.

M5: PNW Telecom Heritage

PNW Track C · Regional history

Pacific Northwest Bell and the Bell System in Oregon, Washington, and Idaho. The Kingsbury Commitment and AT&T divestiture impact on PNW service. US West, Qwest, CenturyLink lineage. Rural electrification and telephone cooperatives. Submarine cable landings at Pacific City and Nedonna Beach. The Seattle Kingdome as early cellular deployment site.

M6: Mesh Communications

Mesh Track C · Decentralized networks

Mesh network topology and routing protocols (AODV, OLSR, B.A.T.M.A.N.). LoRa/Meshtastic long-range mesh. Wi-Fi mesh (802.11s). Delay-tolerant networking for disaster response. Community mesh networks (Guifi.net, NYC Mesh). Phone-to-phone mesh without infrastructure. Burning Man mesh deployments as proof of concept.
