# Era-by-Era History -- Six Epochs of SNL 1975-2025

## Era 1: The Golden Age (1975-1980)

The founding era established every convention that would define SNL for fifty years: the cold open, the host monologue, Weekend Update, the musical guest, the "Live from New York" catchphrase. The Not Ready for Prime Time Players -- Belushi, Aykroyd, Radner, Chase, Murray, Curtin, Newman, Morris -- became the most famous comedy ensemble in television history.

**Signature moments:** Samurai (Belushi), Roseanne Roseannadanna (Radner), Weekend Update (Chase, then Curtin/Aykroyd), Land Shark (Chase), the Blues Brothers (Belushi/Aykroyd).

## Era 2: The Dark Years (1980-1985)

### Jean Doumanian (1980-1981)

Michaels' departure left the show in the hands of Jean Doumanian, whose single season is widely regarded as the worst in SNL history. The cast lacked chemistry, the writing was inconsistent, and ratings cratered. NBC fired Doumanian after 12 episodes.

### Dick Ebersol (1981-1985)

Ebersol stabilized the show by signing Eddie Murphy, whose commanding presence single-handedly justified the show's continued existence. Murphy's characters -- Gumby, Mr. Robinson's Neighborhood, Buckwheat, Velvet Jones -- delivered the show's highest-rated episodes of the early 1980s. When Murphy left for film stardom in 1984, the show's quality dropped again.

## Era 3: Michaels Renaissance (1985-1995)

Michaels returned in 1985, rebuilding the show around a new generation:
- **Phil Hartman:** "The Glue" -- master impressionist, reliable ensemble anchor
- **Dana Carvey:** Church Lady, George H.W. Bush, Garth (Wayne's World)
- **Mike Myers:** Wayne Campbell, Dieter (Sprockets), Simon
- **Chris Farley:** Physical comedy genius, Matt Foley ("van down by the river")
- **Adam Sandler:** Opera Man, Canteen Boy

Wayne's World (1992 film) became the first successful SNL sketch-to-movie adaptation, grossing $183 million.

## Era 4: The Peak Era (1995-2010)

The show entered its most commercially successful period:
- **Will Ferrell (1995-2002):** George W. Bush, Cheerleaders, More Cowbell
- **Tina Fey:** First female head writer (1999), Weekend Update anchor
- **Amy Poehler:** Weekend Update co-anchor with Fey
- **Jimmy Fallon, Tracy Morgan, Horatio Sanz:** Core ensemble
- **The Lonely Island (2005-2012):** Digital shorts that went viral ("Lazy Sunday," "Dick in a Box")

Tina Fey's 2008 Sarah Palin impression drew 10 million YouTube views in 48 hours -- the moment SNL became a digital-era phenomenon.

## Era 5: The Digital Age (2005-2015)

The Lonely Island (Andy Samberg, Akiva Schaffer, Jorma Taccone) pioneered SNL's digital comedy:
- "Lazy Sunday" (December 2005): One of YouTube's first viral hits
- "Dick in a Box" (2006): Emmy-winning digital short
- The digital shorts proved SNL could thrive beyond the live broadcast

Key cast: Bill Hader, Kristen Wiig, Fred Armisen, Jason Sudeikis, Kate McKinnon (joined 2012).

## Era 6: The Contemporary Era (2015-Present)

Defined by political satire reaching new intensity:
- **Alec Baldwin as Donald Trump:** Became the show's most politically charged recurring impression
- **Kate McKinnon:** Versatile impressionist, LGBTQ representation milestone
- **Weekend Update:** Colin Jost and Michael Che established as the longest-running anchor duo
- **Bowen Yang:** First featured player to earn an Emmy nomination, AAPI representation
- **SNL50 season (2024-25):** 22.8 million viewers for the anniversary special
