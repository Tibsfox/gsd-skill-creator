# The Production Machine -- The Six-Day Weekly Cycle

## Overview

SNL's six-day production cycle is one of the most sophisticated live creative workflows in television history. Every week, the show is written, cast, designed, rehearsed, and performed from scratch. The cycle has remained essentially unchanged since 1975.

## Monday: The Pitch Meeting

The host arrives at 30 Rock on Monday for a meeting in Lorne Michaels' office. Writers pitch sketch ideas directly to the host. The host's strengths, interests, and willingness to experiment shape the week's direction.

**Key dynamic:** Writers compete for the host's attention and for Michaels' approval. The Monday meeting sets the tone -- a host who engages enthusiastically generates better material.

## Tuesday: The All-Night Writing Session

Tuesday is the marathon. Writers work through the night, individually or in pairs, producing 40-50 sketches. The writers' room on the 17th floor of 30 Rock fills with coffee, takeout, and the sound of keyboards. Scripts are due Wednesday morning.

**The numbers:** 40-50 sketches written. 12-15 will make it to the table read. 8-10 will air.

## Wednesday: The Table Read

The full cast, host, writers, and Michaels gather around a long table in the studio. Every submitted sketch is read aloud, with writers performing parts not assigned to cast members.

**The cut:** After the table read, Michaels and the head writer select which sketches advance. This is the show's most brutal quality filter -- two-thirds of the week's work is eliminated in a single afternoon.

## Thursday-Friday: Development

Surviving sketches enter production:
- **Set design and construction:** The art department builds sets in 48 hours
- **Costume and makeup:** Wardrobe designs costumes for all characters
- **Rehearsal:** Cast and host rehearse sketches on the studio floor
- **Rewrites:** Writers revise based on rehearsal feedback
- **Music rehearsal:** The musical guest sound-checks with the house band

## Saturday: The Day

### Dress Rehearsal (8:00 PM)

A full run-through performed before a studio audience. The dress rehearsal runs long (often 2+ hours for a 90-minute show), providing one final data point for cuts. Sketches that don't play well with the dress audience may be cut before air.

### Live Broadcast (11:29:30 PM)

The show goes live at 11:29:30 PM Eastern. The 90-minute broadcast includes:
- Cold open (political sketch or topical opener)
- Host monologue
- 5-7 sketches
- Weekend Update (10-12 minutes)
- Musical performances (2)
- Goodnights

**The contract with the audience:** You are watching something that could go wrong. That vulnerability is the show's beating heart.
