## The SNL Effect

Political scientists have identified the "SNL Effect" -- the measurable influence of political parody on voter perception:

### Academic Evidence

- **Purdue University (2008):** Research demonstrated that Tina Fey's Sarah Palin impression influenced voter perceptions of Palin's competence
- **CUNY Academic Works (2025):** Isabel Fox's historical analysis documented SNL's consistent engagement with presidential politics across all 50 seasons
- **IndieWire analysis:** Documented correlation between SNL political sketches and social media political discourse

### The Fey-Palin Phenomenon (2008)

Tina Fey's September 2008 impression of Sarah Palin became a cultural event:
- The phrase "I can see Russia from my house" (an SNL invention) was widely attributed to Palin herself
- 10 million YouTube views within 48 hours
- The sketch drew the highest SNL ratings in 14 years
- Fey won an Emmy for the portrayal

## Weekend Update

Weekend Update has served as SNL's anchor of political commentary since 1975:

### Anchor Succession

| Anchors | Years | Style |
|---|---|---|
| Chevy Chase | 1975-1976 | Deadpan, pratfall |
| Jane Curtin / Dan Aykroyd | 1977-1980 | Point-counterpoint debate |
| Various (Ebersol era) | 1981-1985 | Inconsistent |
| Dennis Miller | 1985-1991 | Sardonic, reference-heavy |
| Kevin Nealon | 1991-1994 | Understated |
| Norm Macdonald | 1994-1998 | Anti-comedy, confrontational |
| Colin Quinn / Tina Fey | 1998-2006 | Transition to journalist style |
| Amy Poehler / Tina Fey | 2004-2006 | First female co-anchor team |
| Seth Meyers | 2006-2014 | Smart, affable |
| Colin Jost / Michael Che | 2014-present | Longest-running duo |

Weekend Update reaches approximately 30 million viewers in its early era and continues to be the show's most consistently viewed segment in the streaming age.

## Politicians on SNL

A unique dynamic: politicians appearing on the show that satirizes them:

- **Hillary Clinton** appeared alongside Kate McKinnon's impression of her
- **Donald Trump** hosted in November 2015 (controversial, drew protests)
- **Barack Obama** appeared via cold open
- Multiple presidential candidates have done cameos to demonstrate "good humor"
