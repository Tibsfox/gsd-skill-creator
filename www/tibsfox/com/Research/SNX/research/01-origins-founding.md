# Origins & Founding -- NBC Mandate, Lorne Michaels & Studio 8H

## The NBC Mandate (1975)

In early 1975, NBC had a problem: Johnny Carson's Tonight Show reruns were filling Saturday late night, but the reruns were declining in ratings and the network wanted live programming. NBC president Herb Schlosser asked his head of late-night programming, Dick Ebersol, to develop a new show.

Ebersol recruited Lorne Michaels, a 30-year-old Canadian comedy writer who had worked on Rowan and Martin's Laugh-In and several variety specials. Michaels was given a modest budget and a mandate: create a live variety show for the post-Watergate generation.

## Assembling the Cast

Michaels drew from two primary talent pools:
- **Second City (Chicago/Toronto):** John Belushi, Dan Aykroyd, Gilda Radner, Bill Murray (joined Season 2)
- **National Lampoon:** Chevy Chase, who had written for the National Lampoon Radio Hour

The founding cast -- officially "The Not Ready for Prime Time Players" -- also included Laraine Newman, Jane Curtin, and Garrett Morris.

## Studio 8H

NBC's Studio 8H at 30 Rockefeller Center became the show's permanent home. Originally built as a radio studio for Arturo Toscanini and the NBC Symphony Orchestra, the $250,000 renovation transformed it into a live television theater. The studio's relatively intimate 300-seat capacity creates the energy that defines SNL's atmosphere -- close enough to the performers that the audience's reactions are genuine, not amplified.

## The Premiere (October 11, 1975)

The first episode aired on October 11, 1975, with George Carlin as host and musical guests Billy Preston and Janis Ian. The cold open featured Michael O'Donoghue and John Belushi in the "Wolverines" sketch. The show was initially titled "NBC's Saturday Night" because ABC's Saturday Night Live with Howard Cosell held the name.

Key moments from the first season:
- Chevy Chase's "Weekend Update" established the fake news desk format
- Andy Kaufman's Mighty Mouse lip-sync (Episode 1) defined the show's avant-garde comedy edge
- The Samurai sketches (Belushi) became the first recurring character franchise
- Richard Pryor hosted Episode 7, in one of the most celebrated episodes of the first season

## The Name Change

The show became "Saturday Night Live" in March 1977 after ABC's Howard Cosell show was canceled, freeing the name.

## The First Cast Exodus (1980)

After five seasons, the entire original cast departed. Michaels himself left after a salary dispute with NBC. The show's survival was in immediate doubt -- it had been built so completely around its founding ensemble and producer that continuation seemed impossible.
