Mission Pack

The original research brief and mission package that initiated the Business Law & Accounting research project. A comprehensive three-stage TeX document — Vision, Research Reference, Mission Package — covering the full pipeline from legal architecture analysis to structured execution.

Source Documents

The mission pack comprises a multi-hundred-line TeX mission document covering the complete business law and accounting research scope: entity formation under Washington and federal law, cooperative accounting under Subchapter T, tribal sovereign governance and joint venture structures, federal regulatory compliance (Section 106, NAGPRA, CDFI), equity system design and securities registration, and international expansion frameworks across CPTPP member states.

Research Scope

  • Entity Formation: LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp, partnership, sole proprietorship. Washington PBC formation, cooperative corporation formation under RCW 23.86, formation sequencing for multi-entity architectures, the choice matrix across liability, taxation, and fundraising.
  • Double-Entry Bookkeeping: Luca Pacioli’s 1494 system. Debits and credits, the accounting equation, chart of accounts, journal entries, financial statements. GAAP vs. IFRS. Accrual vs. cash basis. 530 years of continuous use.
  • Cooperative Accounting: Worker, consumer, producer cooperatives. Patronage dividends, member equity, internal capital accounts. Subchapter T taxation. REI, Mondragon, Land O’Lakes. The cooperative difference from standard corporations.
  • Tribal Sovereignty: Section 17 federal corporations, tribal LLCs, tribally chartered entities. Sovereign immunity, arm-of-the-tribe doctrine, joint venture structuring. IGRA basics. The Harvard Project on American Indian Economic Development.
  • Federal Compliance: SEC registration, Regulation D (506(b), 506(c)), accredited investor definitions. SOX basics. AML/KYC. The compliance stack from formation to public markets.
  • Equity & Securities: Stock options, RSUs, phantom equity. Cap table management, 409A valuations. Vesting schedules. Common vs. preferred stock. Convertible notes and SAFEs.
  • International Expansion: Subsidiaries vs. branches, transfer pricing, IFRS vs. GAAP, FCPA, VAT/GST, cross-border employment.

Key Connections
