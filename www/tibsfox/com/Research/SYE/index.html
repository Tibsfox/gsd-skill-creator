Systems Engineering

The Discipline That Built Apollo, the Shuttle, Artemis & the SLS — INCOSE · NASA SE · ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288
Overview Requirements Architecture V&V Thinking Risk CM Interfaces Trades Lifecycle HSI
Systems Engineering is the discipline that made it possible to land humans on the Moon, fly the Space Shuttle for thirty years, build the International Space Station, and send Perseverance to Mars. It is not a single technique but an integrated practice: requirements capture, architecture decomposition, verification and validation, risk management, configuration control, interface definition, and rigorous lifecycle processes. The INCOSE handbook, NASA SE Handbook (SP-2016-6105), and ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288 codify seventy years of lessons learned from programs where failure meant lost lives. This research digest surveys the full discipline across ten modules.

Module Overview

Requirements

01. Requirements Engineering

Elicitation, analysis, specification, validation. SRS/SyRS structure. EARS (Easy Approach to Requirements Syntax). INCOSE requirements rules. Traceability matrices. Requirement quality attributes. The cost of requirements defects (10x per phase).
Architecture

02. System Architecture & Design

Functional decomposition. Logical and physical architecture. Model-Based Systems Engineering (MBSE) with SysML. Architecture frameworks (DoDAF, TOGAF, MODAF). Arcadia/Capella methodology. Views and viewpoints.
V&V

03. Verification & Validation

The V&V distinction (did we build it right vs did we build the right thing). Four methods: Inspection, Analysis, Demonstration, Test. V-model. TRR/FRR gates. Acceptance criteria. Compliance matrices. V&V planning.
Thinking

04. Systems Thinking & Modeling

Emergence and system-level properties. Hierarchies and decomposition. Feedback loops. Systems of Systems (SoS). STAMP/STPA for hazard analysis. System dynamics (Forrester). Soft systems methodology (Checkland).
Risk

05. Risk Management

Risk identification methodology. Probabilistic Risk Assessment (PRA). FMEA/FMECA. Fault tree analysis. Risk registers and matrices. NASA risk-informed decision making (RIDM). Continuous risk management. Residual risk acceptance.
CM

06. Configuration Management

Baselines (functional, allocated, product). Change Control Boards. Configuration items. Version control for hardware. As-designed vs as-built vs as-maintained. Engineering change proposals (ECPs). Status accounting and audits.
Interfaces

07. Interface Management & ICDs

Interface Control Documents structure and contents. Interface definition and verification. N² diagrams. Subsystem boundaries. Mechanical, electrical, data, thermal interfaces. Interface agreements between organizations. The interface failure mode problem.
Trades

08. Trade Studies & Decision Analysis

Trade study methodology. Weighted decision matrices. Pugh concept selection. Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP). Multi-Attribute Utility Theory (MAUT). Cost-effectiveness analysis. Sensitivity analysis. Documenting decisions for traceability.
Lifecycle

09. Lifecycle Processes

The V-model and Vee. Waterfall origins (Royce 1970, misunderstood). Agile SE. Spiral model. ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288 processes. NASA 7120.5 project lifecycle. Stage-gate reviews: MCR, SRR, PDR, CDR, TRR, FRR, ORR, DR.
HSI

10. Human Factors & Systems Integration

Human-System Integration (HSI). Ergonomics and anthropometrics. Cognitive workload and situational awareness. Human error taxonomy. HSI domains (NASA and DoD). Systems integration at scale. Integration test and verification.
