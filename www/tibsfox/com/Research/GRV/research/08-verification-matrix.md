## 7. The Through-Line

The research confirms the model proposed in the vision document: Seattle's musical history operates as a continuous feedback loop where geographic isolation concentrates creative energy, independent infrastructure captures and amplifies it, and periodic "eruption events" project it globally. Green River serves as the pivotal eruption that transformed local ferment into worldwide phenomenon.

The same structural conditions -- isolation, density, cross-pollination, DIY necessity -- produced jazz on Jackson Street in the 1930s, garage rock in Tacoma in the 1960s, grunge in Seattle in the 1980s, and indie rock and hip-hop in the 2000s. The specific content changes. The generative mechanism persists.

Six bands on one compilation. One word in a fanzine letter. A name borrowed from a serial killer. One band split into two. The sound the forest made was grunge. Green River was the first band to record it.
