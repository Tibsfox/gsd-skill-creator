Project management is the discipline of initiating, planning, executing, monitoring, and closing work to achieve specific goals within constraints. This research series is a comprehensive survey of PM from ancient construction projects through modern AI-native orchestration, covering the methodologies, metrics, tools, and organizational patterns that determine whether projects succeed or fail.

Special focus is given to GSD (Get Shit Done) as an AI-native project management system for Claude Code — a system where AI plans the work, AI executes the work, and humans serve as gate-keepers at phase boundaries. GSD represents a fundamental rethinking of PM: instead of managing human teams through ceremonies and sprints, it manages AI agents through phases, waves, and verification criteria.

Research method: Primary sources (PMBOK Guide, Scrum Guide, Agile Manifesto, Standish Group CHAOS reports), canonical textbooks (Brooks, Goldratt, DeMarco & Lister, Kim/Behr/Spafford), practitioner literature, cross-reference against the PNW Research Series corpus.

Module Overview

History

History & Origins

Pyramids to AI. Frederick Taylor’s scientific management, Henry Gantt’s charts, PERT and CPM from the Cold War, the founding of PMI, the software crisis of the 1960s–80s, the Agile revolution of 2001, and the AI-native turn of the 2020s.

Key: Taylor, Gantt, PERT, CPM, PMI, Agile Manifesto
Methodologies

Methodologies

Waterfall, Agile (Scrum, Kanban, XP), Lean, PRINCE2, Six Sigma, SAFe, Hybrid approaches. What each methodology assumes about requirements, teams, and change. When each works, when each fails, and why the industry keeps inventing new ones.

Key: Waterfall, Scrum, Kanban, XP, Lean, SAFe, Hybrid
Metrics

Metrics & Performance

The Standish Group CHAOS reports and their controversial methodology. The Iron Triangle (scope, time, cost). Earned Value Management (CPI, SPI, EAC). Agile velocity, burndown charts, and cycle time. OKRs and DORA metrics. What gets measured gets managed — and what gets measured wrong gets managed wrong.

Key: CHAOS, Iron Triangle, EVM, velocity, DORA
Risk

Risk & Stakeholders

Risk identification, qualitative and quantitative analysis, Monte Carlo simulation for schedule risk. Stakeholder analysis and engagement strategies. Conflict resolution frameworks. Why most projects fail from people problems, not technical problems.

Key: Risk matrix, Monte Carlo, stakeholder mapping
AI & Tools

AI & Tools

The PM tools landscape: Jira, Asana, Linear, Monday, Notion, Shortcut, ClickUp. The evolution from wall charts to digital boards to AI-assisted planning. AI agents as team members: automated code review, AI-generated test plans, autonomous execution. The shift from “tool-assisted humans” to “human-gated AI.”

Key: Jira, Linear, AI agents, autonomous execution
GSD — Flagship

GSD Deep Dive

GSD (Get Shit Done): the AI-native PM system. The lifecycle: discuss → plan → execute → verify. Wave architecture for parallel execution. GSD vs Scrum, Kanban, and SAFe. The three paradoxes of AI-native PM. Why ceremony overhead drops to near-zero when the executor is an AI agent, and why human gates become more important, not less.

Key: Lifecycle, waves, phases, CAPCOM, AI-native philosophy
Rosetta

Rosetta Integration

Waterfall vs Agile vs Hybrid vs GSD: a side-by-side comparison across 12+ dimensions. Detailed mapping tables for GSD vs Scrum, GSD vs Kanban, GSD vs SAFe. Decision matrix: which methodology fits which project context.

Key: Paradigm comparison, methodology selection
Education

Learning Resources

A staged path from the Agile Manifesto through PMBOK, Earned Value Management, and practical tooling to GSD and the Cynefin framework. The canonical books: Brooks, Goldratt, DeMarco & Lister, Kim/Behr/Spafford, Forsgren/Humble/Kim. Free resources and the PM community.

Key: Books, Scrum Guide, PMBOK, GSD docs

The Arc of Project Management

YearWhatWhy it mattered
~2560 BCEGreat Pyramid of Giza completed20+ years, 100,000 workers, the oldest surviving evidence of large-scale project coordination.
1911Frederick Taylor: The Principles of Scientific ManagementWork decomposition, time studies, efficiency optimization. The intellectual ancestor of every PM framework.
1910sHenry Gantt develops the Gantt chartVisual scheduling. Still used in every PM tool 110 years later.
1956DuPont develops the Critical Path Method (CPM)Mathematical scheduling for chemical plant construction.
1958US Navy develops PERT for Polaris missile programProbabilistic scheduling. Managed 3,000+ contractors and shaved two years off the schedule.
1965IPMA founded (International Project Management Association)First professional PM body.
1968NATO Software Engineering Conference (Garmisch)The “software crisis” formally identified. Software projects fail at alarming rates.
1969PMI (Project Management Institute) foundedThe professional body that would standardize PM through PMBOK.
1970Winston Royce publishes “Managing the Development of Large Software Systems”Ironically described the waterfall model as flawed, but the diagram was adopted as a methodology.
1975Fred Brooks: The Mythical Man-Month“Adding manpower to a late software project makes it later.” Brooks’s Law.
1986Hirotaka Takeuchi & Ikujiro Nonaka: “The New New Product Development Game”The rugby metaphor that would become Scrum.
1987PMBOK Guide 1st edition publishedProject management codified as a body of knowledge.
1995Standish Group publishes the first CHAOS ReportOnly 16.2% of software projects succeed (by their controversial definition). The industry panics.
1995Ken Schwaber & Jeff Sutherland present Scrum at OOPSLAThe framework that would dominate agile adoption.
1996Kent Beck begins Extreme Programming (XP) at Chrysler C3TDD, pair programming, continuous integration, short iterations.
1997Eliyahu Goldratt: Critical ChainTheory of Constraints applied to project scheduling.
2001Agile Manifesto signed at Snowbird, Utah17 signatories. Four values, twelve principles. The revolution.
2004David Anderson introduces Kanban for software developmentFlow-based PM borrowed from Toyota Production System.
2011SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework) 1.0 launchedEnterprise agile at scale. Controversial but widely adopted.
2013Gene Kim et al.: The Phoenix ProjectDevOps as a novel. The bridge between PM and ops.
2018Forsgren, Humble, Kim: AccelerateDORA metrics: deployment frequency, lead time, MTTR, change failure rate.
2021PMBOK Guide 7th edition shifts to principle-based approachPMI acknowledges that prescriptive processes are insufficient.
2024–26AI-native PM emerges (GSD, AI agents as team members)AI plans, AI executes, humans gate. The ceremony overhead drops to near-zero.
