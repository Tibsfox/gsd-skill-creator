Research Modules

M1: Biography and Origins

Biography Track A · Engineering roots

From Washington D.C. childhood through Cornell engineering and Carl Sagan's astronomy course to Boeing's hydraulic suppressor tube. The comedy career, Almost Live! on Seattle's KING-TV, and the moment a mispronounced gigawatt launched a persona that would teach science to a generation.

M2: The Television Show (1993–1998)

Television Track A · Media artifact

KCTS-TV production, PBS/Buena Vista dual distribution, NSF/DOE underwriting, 100 episodes, 18 Emmy Awards. Format analysis: Mr. Wizard meets MTV. KCTS outreach studies proving viewers explained science better than non-viewers. The first show on both PBS and commercial television.

M3: Science Communication Pedagogy

Education Track A · Methodology

Humor as cognitive lubricant, demonstrations over descriptions, replicability as epistemic engagement. The Sagan lineage. The pivot from children to adults. Critical thinking as the meta-skill. Digital-era science communication and the challenge of platform fragmentation.

M4: Science Advocacy and Controversy

Advocacy Track B · Public engagement

Climate change advocacy, the Ken Ham creationism debate before 3 million live viewers, Bill Nye Saves the World on Netflix, the March for Science. The methodological tension: does engaging denialists in public forums help or harm scientific literacy?

M5: The Planetary Society and Space Legacy

Space Track B · Orbital demonstration

Charter member to CEO of the world's largest non-profit space organization. The LightSail solar sailing program: from Cosmos 1's failure to LightSail 2's three-year mission on sunlight. Mars Rover sundials, citizen-funded science, and the $7M crowdfunded spacecraft.
