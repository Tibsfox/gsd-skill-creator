Voices from the Fir Trees

PNW Radio Broadcast History — Pioneers · Golden Age · Community Radio · Grunge · Digital
Overview Mission Pack
Radio, at its essence, is about the spaces between — between transmitter and receiver, between voice and ear, between isolation and community. In the Pacific Northwest, where mountains and water fragment geography and dense forests swallow signals, broadcasting has always been an act of bridging.

Research Modules

01 — Pioneers and Spark (1915–1929)

KJR · KGW · KFAE · 7XF · Kraft

Experimental wireless stations, the Class of 1922, Vincent Kraft's Ravenna garage, KGW and the Oregonian, Washington State College's KFAE, and the dawn of licensed broadcasting in the Pacific Northwest.

02 — Golden Age and War (1929–1945)

Hoot Owls · Mel Blanc · Murrow · NBC · CBS

Network affiliation era, KGW's Hoot Owls as pioneering variety radio, Mel Blanc's Portland career, Edward R. Murrow's Skagit County roots and CBS trajectory, and wartime broadcasting.

03 — Media Empires and FM (1945–1962)

Bullitt · KING · FM Revolution · KMED

Dorothy Bullitt and King Broadcasting Company, the KING call letters legend, FM radio arrives in the Northwest, Blanche McBride Virgin as first woman station owner, and the five-year TV monopoly.

04 — Community and Counterculture (1962–1984)

KRAB · KBOO · KAOS · Milam · Freeform

Lorenzo Milam's donut shop revolution, the KRAB Nebula of 40+ community stations, KBOO Portland, KAOS at Evergreen State, punk radio, and Reagan-era funding collapse.

05 — College Radio and Grunge (1972–2001)

KCMU · KEXP · Sub Pop · Nirvana · Soundgarden

KCMU's founding, the Sub Pop Records genesis at the station, Soundgarden DJs, Kurt Cobain's dedication, the KCMU-to-KEXP transformation via Paul Allen, and pioneering internet streaming.

06 — Public Broadcasting and Digital (1986–Present)

NWPB · KUOW · KEXP Digital · KING-FM

Northwest Public Broadcasting's 19-station network, KEXP as digital pioneer, KING-FM's nonprofit preservation, community radio persistence, diversity reckoning, and the 2025 federal funding crisis.
