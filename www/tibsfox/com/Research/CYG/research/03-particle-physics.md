# Particle Acceleration & Photomeson Physics

## The Photomeson Production Mechanism

The photomeson process:

```
p + gamma -> Delta+ -> pi0 + p
pi0 -> gamma + gamma (observed UHE gamma rays)

p + gamma -> Delta+ -> pi+ + n
pi+ -> mu+ + nu_mu -> e+ + nu_e + anti-nu_mu + nu_mu (neutrinos)
```

For UV photons (~10 eV) from the WR star, the Delta+ resonance condition requires proton energies of ~10-100 PeV. The gamma-ray photon from pi0 decay carries roughly 10% of the parent proton energy, placing observed 1-3.7 PeV gamma rays as secondaries from protons at 10-40 PeV.

## Acceleration Mechanisms

Three scenarios evaluated by Kachelriess et al. (2025):

| Mechanism | Description | PeV Capable? |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| Magnetic reconnection | Reconnection in relativistic jet | Yes |
| Diffusive shock acceleration (DSA) | Fermi-I; protons scattered across jet shocks | Yes |
| Second-order Fermi (stochastic) | Particle-wave scattering; slower but persistent | Yes |

All three can accelerate protons beyond 1 PeV. Required CR acceleration efficiency: 10^-3 to 10^-2 -- extremely modest given jet kinetic luminosity exceeding 10^39 erg/s.

## Target Photon Fields

Two photon interaction channels:

- **UV photons from WR star:** Dominant at PeV energies. Produces orbital modulation because target density varies with orbital phase. Spectral contribution peaks near 1 PeV
- **Stellar wind gas (hadronic pp):** Harder secondary spectrum. Contributes more below 1 PeV; minimal orbital modulation

X-ray photons from the compact object are a potential third target but predict no orbital modulation, which is disfavored by LHAASO evidence.

## Why the Spectrum Is So Hard

Cygnus X-3 has the hardest spectral index of any UHE source in the LHAASO catalog. The combination of the photomeson pileup near 1 PeV and the efficient proton acceleration in the inner jet produces a spectrum that rises where most other sources are falling -- a distinctive signature that strongly favors the hadronic interpretation over leptonic alternatives.
