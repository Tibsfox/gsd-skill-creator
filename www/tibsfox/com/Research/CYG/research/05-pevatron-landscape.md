# Galactic PeVatron Landscape & CR Knee

## LHAASO's PeVatron Census

LHAASO has detected over 100 ultra-high-energy gamma-ray sources, with 12 confirmed PeVatrons (sources emitting above 100 TeV). Cygnus X-3 is unique among these as the hardest spectral source and the only confirmed *variable* PeVatron -- its emission fluctuates on month timescales, unlike the steady emission from supernova remnants.

## The Cygnus Superbubble

Cygnus X-3 sits inside the Cygnus superbubble, a vast stellar-wind-carved cavity approximately 150 parsecs across. The superbubble has long been suspected as a cosmic-ray factory -- LHAASO detects diffuse UHE emission from the region. The confirmed PeVatron inside this bubble provides a plausible source for some of this diffuse emission.

## The Cosmic-Ray Knee

The "knee" in the cosmic-ray energy spectrum at approximately 3 PeV has been measured for decades without clear explanation. This spectral feature -- a steepening of the power-law distribution -- suggests that whatever sources produce cosmic rays at these energies have a characteristic maximum energy near 3 PeV.

Cygnus X-3's confirmed emission to 3.7 PeV places it precisely at the energy scale of the knee. If microquasars like Cygnus X-3 contribute significantly to the Galactic cosmic-ray population, they could be responsible for the knee feature -- a possibility that was largely dismissed before the LHAASO detection.

## The 40-Year Detection Arc

- **1970s-1980s:** Multiple air-shower experiments reported possible detections from Cygnus X-3 direction at extreme energies
- **1990s:** Better-calibrated detectors found nothing; claims shelved
- **2009:** AGILE satellite detected transient GeV emission during radio flaring
- **2009-2012:** Fermi-LAT confirmed GeV emission correlated with radio states
- **2025:** LHAASO reports 10-sigma detection extending to 3.7 PeV

The 40-year arc from tentative claims to confirmed super-PeVatron detection represents one of the most dramatic vindications in gamma-ray astronomy.

## Architecture, Not Brute Force

The PeV photons from Cygnus X-3 emerge from a region smaller than a city block, powered by a jet requiring barely one part in a thousand of its available kinetic energy. This is the astrophysical expression of the principle that staggering complexity can emerge from small, principled building blocks operating within elegant architectural constraints.
