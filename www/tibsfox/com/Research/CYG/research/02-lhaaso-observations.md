# LHAASO Observations & Spectral Analysis

## The LHAASO Observatory

LHAASO (Large High Altitude Air Shower Observatory) operates at 4,400 m elevation in Sichuan Province, China. Three detector components:

- **KM2A** (Kilometer Square Array): 10 TeV - 10 PeV. Primary detector for this discovery
- **WCDA** (Water Cherenkov Detector Array): 100 GeV - 20 TeV
- **WFCTA** (Wide Field-of-View Cherenkov Telescope Array): Complementary stereo imaging

## Detection Summary

| Observable | Measured Value |
|-----------|---------------|
| Detection significance | ~10 sigma (KM2A) |
| SED energy range | 0.06 - 3.7 PeV |
| Power-law spectral index | 2.18 +/- 0.14 |
| Spectral character | Hardest UHE source in LHAASO catalog |
| Spectral feature | Hardening above 1 PeV |
| Low-energy | No significant excess below 20 TeV in WCDA |
| Quiescent flux | Nearly one order of magnitude below flaring state |
| CMB absorption | Accounted for in intrinsic SED reconstruction |
| Orbital modulation | 3.2 sigma evidence for 4.8-hr period |
| Variability timescale | Month-scale |

## The 1 PeV Spectral Pileup

The SED shows a pronounced rise toward 1 PeV even after CMB absorption correction. This "pileup" is characteristic of photomeson production near the Delta+ resonance threshold (~145 MeV pion rest mass). The UV photon field of the WR star naturally places this resonance in the observed energy range, providing strong support for the hadronic interpretation.

## Month-Scale Variability

Cygnus X-3 shows order-of-magnitude flux variability between flaring and quiescent states on month timescales. This variability distinguishes it from steady sources like supernova remnants and confirms the emission is tied to active accretion/jet processes. The possible 40-year history of bright-phase/dark-phase cycling has no consensus physical model.

## Orbital Modulation

The 3.2-sigma evidence for 4.8-hour orbital modulation links the UHE emission directly to the binary system's geometry. As the compact object orbits the WR star, the photon target density and jet-star angle vary, creating periodic changes in photomeson production efficiency.
