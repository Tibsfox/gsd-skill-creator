# Bibliography and Source Index

> **Document 10 of the FWL (Fowl) research series**
>
> This document compiles and organizes the source material underpinning the FWL (Fowl) research project. Sources are categorized by domain: History, Breed Standards, Genetics, Husbandry, Conservation, Wild Galliformes, and Show Culture. Each entry includes bibliographic details, a brief annotation describing the source's relevance to FWL, and cross-references to FWL documents where the source is cited or relevant.
>
> **Source quality standard:** All sources are traceable to published books, peer-reviewed journals, government agencies, established breed organizations, or recognized agricultural extension services. Web resources are included only for organizations with demonstrated editorial standards and institutional accountability. No anonymous blogs, social media posts, or unverified web content.
>
> **Citation format:** Entries follow modified APA style. Where publication dates span multiple editions, the most recent edition consulted is cited with the original publication date noted in brackets.

## How to Use This Bibliography

Sources are organized by category and numbered sequentially within each category (e.g., H1-H14 for History, B1-B11 for Breed Standards). Where a source is relevant to multiple categories, it is listed in its primary category with cross-references to secondary relevance. FWL document numbers (01-09) indicate where each source is most directly relevant.

## Notes on Source Availability

Historical sources (H3-H6) are digitized via Google Books, HathiTrust, and the Biodiversity Heritage Library. Hutt's *Genetics of the Fowl* (G1) was reprinted in 2003 by Norton Creek Press. The BNA species accounts (W4, W5, W7-W12) are incorporated into *Birds of the World* (OR5). *Poultry Science* provides open access to articles older than 12 months. USDA and state wildlife agency publications are generally freely available online. Victorian-era breed standards and nomenclature differ substantially from modern usage.
