# FWL Learning Pathway: From Curious to Keeper

A structured learning journey through the world of poultry -- domestication, breeds, genetics, husbandry, and heritage conservation. You don't need to be an expert to start. You don't need a flock. You don't even need a yard. You just need to start.

This pathway organizes all FWL research documents, try-sessions, study-guides, breed profiles, and guides into a coherent sequence. Each level builds on the previous one, but you can skip ahead or circle back whenever you want. Learning isn't linear -- it just helps to have a map.

**Total estimated time:** 40-60 hours across all five levels (spread over weeks or months)
**Prerequisites:** None. Curiosity is the only entry requirement.
**College of Knowledge connections:** Nature Studies, Science, History, Home Economics

---

## Level 1 -- Curious (1-2 hours)

*"I've seen chickens. I think they're interesting. Tell me more."*

You're starting from zero, or close to it. Maybe you've met a chicken at a petting zoo, or you've noticed your neighbor's coop, or you just think roosters are absurdly beautiful. This level gives you the foundation: where chickens came from, how to tell breeds apart, and what it actually means to keep a flock.

### Reading

1. **[01-domestication-history.md](../01-domestication-history.md)** -- Start here. The 8,000-year story of how the Red Junglefowl became the most numerous bird on Earth. This sets the context for everything else. (~20 min)

2. **[guides/starting-a-flock.md](../guides/starting-a-flock.md)** -- A practical overview of what flock-keeping actually involves. Read this even if you have no plans to keep chickens -- it grounds the rest of the research in reality. (~15 min)

### Try-Session

3. **[try-sessions/breed-identification.md](../try-sessions/breed-identification.md)** -- An interactive exercise in identifying chicken breeds from descriptions and characteristics. This is where the abstract becomes visual. You'll start building the vocabulary that makes the rest of the pathway click. (~30 min)

### What You'll Learn
- The evolutionary and cultural story of domestic poultry
- Basic breed classification concepts (classes, types, varieties)
- What keeping a flock actually requires in terms of time, space, and commitment
- How to distinguish common breeds by appearance

### What You'll Be Able to DO
- Identify at least 5 common chicken breeds on sight
- Explain why there are so many breeds (different climates, purposes, cultures)
- Have a conversation about chickens that goes beyond "they lay eggs"

### College Connections
- **Nature Studies:** Bird biology, domestication as ecological relationship
- **History:** Agricultural history, human-animal co-evolution

## Level 2 -- Exploring (1 day)

*"Okay, I'm hooked. I want to know about specific breeds, and I definitely want to understand the egg thing."*

Now you're building breadth. You'll read breed profiles, learn about the science of eggs, and start understanding the genetics that create the astonishing variety of domestic poultry. Pick the three breeds that interest you most -- there's no wrong answer.

### Reading -- Pick 3 Breed Profiles

Choose three from the breed directory. Read them in any order. Each profile covers history, appearance, temperament, egg production, and suitability for different climates. Here are the options:

- **[breeds/orpington.md](../breeds/orpington.md)** -- The gentle giant. A great starter breed for families. Fluffy, calm, good layers.
- **[breeds/plymouth-rock.md](../breeds/plymouth-rock.md)** -- The American classic. Hardy, friendly, the bird that fed a nation.
- **[breeds/australorp.md](../breeds/australorp.md)** -- The egg-laying champion. Holds the world record (364 eggs in 365 days).
- **[breeds/wyandotte.md](../breeds/wyandotte.md)** -- The laced beauty. Stunning feather patterns, cold-hardy.
- **[breeds/silkie.md](../breeds/silkie.md)** -- The oddball. Fur-like feathers, blue skin, five toes. Unforgettable.
- **[breeds/brahma.md](../breeds/brahma.md)** -- The "King of Chickens." Enormous, feather-footed, surprisingly gentle.
- **[breeds/marans.md](../breeds/marans.md)** -- The chocolate egg layer. French heritage, James Bond's breakfast egg.
- **[breeds/cream-legbar.md](../breeds/cream-legbar.md)** -- The auto-sexing blue egg layer. A genetics marvel.
- **[breeds/ameraucana-easter-egger.md](../breeds/ameraucana-easter-egger.md)** -- The blue/green egg layers. Where egg color gets interesting.

(~15-20 min each, ~45-60 min total)

### Reading -- Egg Science & Color

4. **[05-egg-science.md](../05-egg-science.md)** -- The chemistry and biology of eggs: shell formation, the bloom, nutritional content, why a fresh egg tastes different from a store-bought one. (~20 min)

5. **[guides/egg-color-rainbow.md](../guides/egg-color-rainbow.md)** -- The genetics behind egg color. Blue, green, chocolate, olive, pink, white -- and why you can't always predict what you'll get. (~15 min)

### Try-Session

6. **[try-sessions/egg-taste-test.md](../try-sessions/egg-taste-test.md)** -- A structured blind taste test comparing eggs from different sources. You'll need some eggs for this one. The results may surprise you (or confirm what egg snobs have always claimed). (~45 min including prep)

7. **[try-sessions/chicken-math-project.md](../try-sessions/chicken-math-project.md)** -- Calculate the REAL cost of your backyard eggs. Startup costs, monthly operating expenses, production curves, and the honest spreadsheet that reveals why backyard eggs cost $4-8/dozen. Plus: the chicken math phenomenon (you start with 6, you end up with 20). (~1-2 hours)

### Study Guide

7. **[study-guides/genetics-study-guide.md](../study-guides/genetics-study-guide.md)** -- The fundamentals of poultry genetics: dominant/recessive traits, sex-linked inheritance, how feather color is determined. This is the key that unlocks Level 4. (~30 min)

### What You'll Learn
- Detailed knowledge of at least 3 specific breeds
- The science of egg formation and egg color genetics
- Basic genetic principles as they apply to poultry
- The difference between heritage and production breeds

### What You'll Be Able to DO
- Explain why different breeds lay different colored eggs
- Recommend breeds for specific climates and purposes
- Taste the difference between fresh and commercial eggs (maybe)
- Read a basic Punnett square for poultry traits

### College Connections
- **Science:** Genetics, biology, chemistry (egg formation)
- **Nature Studies:** Breed diversity, selective breeding
- **Home Economics:** Food sourcing, egg quality assessment

## Level 3 -- Planning (1 week)

*"I'm thinking about getting chickens. Or I already have a few and I want to do this right."*

This is where theory meets practice. You'll design a coop, learn husbandry, understand health basics, and face the reality of predators in the Pacific Northwest. This level is heavier -- give it a week, do a section per day, and let the knowledge settle.

### Try-Session

8. **[try-sessions/coop-design-challenge.md](../try-sessions/coop-design-challenge.md)** -- Design a chicken coop from scratch, considering your climate, flock size, predator risks, and budget. This is an engineering project disguised as a chicken exercise. (~2-3 hours over multiple sessions)

### Reading -- Husbandry

9. **[06-husbandry-guide.md](../06-husbandry-guide.md)** -- The comprehensive care guide: feeding, watering, bedding, seasonal management, molting, egg collection, and the daily rhythm of flock-keeping. This is the single most practical document in the project. (~45 min)

### Study Guide

10. **[study-guides/poultry-health-basics.md](../study-guides/poultry-health-basics.md)** -- Common diseases, parasites, injury treatment, and when to call a vet. Nobody wants to read about bumblefoot -- but when your hen has bumblefoot, you'll be glad you did. (~30 min)

### Reading -- PNW-Specific

11. **[guides/predator-defense-pnw.md](../guides/predator-defense-pnw.md)** -- Hawks, raccoons, coyotes, bears, and the neighborhood dog: the predators that will try to eat your flock in the Pacific Northwest, and how to stop them. This connects directly to the RCN (Raccoon) research project. (~20 min)

### What You'll Learn
- How to design a safe, functional coop for your specific situation
- Daily, weekly, and seasonal flock management routines
- How to identify and respond to common health problems
- The specific predator threats in the Pacific Northwest and how to mitigate them

### What You'll Be Able to DO
- Design a coop on paper (or in reality) that meets your flock's needs
- Keep chickens healthy through all four PNW seasons
- Recognize the early signs of common poultry diseases
- Build a predator defense strategy appropriate to your environment

### College Connections
- **Trades:** Construction (coop building), materials selection
- **Science:** Veterinary basics, parasitology
- **Nature Studies:** Predator-prey ecology, PNW wildlife
- **Home Economics:** Food production, sustainable living

## Level 4 -- Keeping (Ongoing)

*"I have a flock, I'm comfortable with the basics, and I want to go deeper."*

Now you're a keeper. The flock is alive and healthy and laying. This level takes you into the fascinating deeper waters: hatching your own chicks, understanding show bird culture, the intricate world of feather genetics, and the urgent work of heritage breed conservation. There's no timeline here -- this is ongoing study that you dip into as curiosity and experience pull you forward.

### Try-Session

12. **[try-sessions/incubation-project.md](../try-sessions/incubation-project.md)** -- Hatch eggs from start to finish. 21 days of temperature control, candling, humidity management, and the incredible moment of pip and zip. This is the most rewarding try-session in the entire project. (~21 days elapsed, ~30 min/day active time)

### Reading -- Culture & Community

13. **[03-show-bird-culture.md](../03-show-bird-culture.md)** -- The world of poultry shows: judging standards, show preparation, the community of breeders who dedicate their lives to perfecting a breed. Surprisingly competitive. Surprisingly warm. (~30 min)

### Reading -- Deep Genetics

14. **[04-feather-genetics.md](../04-feather-genetics.md)** -- The deep dive into feather color genetics: E-locus, pattern genes, dilution, sex-linked barring, and the molecular biology behind why a Silver-Laced Wyandotte looks the way it does. This builds on the genetics study guide from Level 2. (~45 min)

### Reading -- Conservation

15. **[07-heritage-conservation.md](../07-heritage-conservation.md)** -- Why heritage breeds matter, which ones are endangered, and how small-flock keepers are the last line of defense against genetic extinction. This is the "why it matters" chapter. (~30 min)

### Reading -- Wild Relatives

16. **[08-wild-galliformes-pnw.md](../08-wild-galliformes-pnw.md)** -- The wild relatives of domestic chickens that live in the Pacific Northwest: grouse, quail, pheasants. Understanding the wild birds deepens your understanding of the domestic ones. (~20 min)

### What You'll Learn
- The full incubation and hatching process
- How poultry shows work and why they matter for breed preservation
- Advanced feather color genetics (enough to predict offspring colors)
- The conservation status of heritage breeds and how to contribute
- The connection between domestic poultry and wild PNW galliformes

### What You'll Be Able to DO
- Successfully incubate and hatch eggs
- Evaluate a bird against breed standard criteria
- Predict feather color outcomes from breeding pairs
- Contribute to heritage breed conservation through informed breeding choices
- Identify wild galliformes during PNW hikes

### College Connections
- **Science:** Advanced genetics, embryology, conservation biology
- **Nature Studies:** Wild galliformes, ecological connections
- **History:** Agricultural heritage, breed preservation as cultural work
- **Community:** Show bird culture, breeder networks

## Level 5 -- Showing (Mastery)

*"I want to breed to standard. I want to contribute to the breed. I want to show."*

This is the mastery level. You're not just keeping chickens anymore -- you're stewarding a breed. This level has no endpoint because mastery in breeding is a lifelong pursuit. You'll need deep knowledge of breed standards, an eye for conformation, understanding of line breeding, and patience measured in generations of birds.

### Deep Study

17. **Return to [03-show-bird-culture.md](../03-show-bird-culture.md)** with fresh eyes -- now read it as a participant, not an observer. Study the APA Standard of Perfection for your chosen breed. (~ongoing)

18. **Return to [04-feather-genetics.md](../04-feather-genetics.md)** -- Now you're reading for breeding program design. How do you fix a desired trait? How do you breed out a fault? What are the tradeoffs between genetic diversity and breed type? (~ongoing)

19. **[02a-american-class.md](../02a-american-class.md)** through **[02f-ornamental-bantams.md](../02f-ornamental-bantams.md)** -- Read the complete class survey for your breed's class. Understand where your breed fits in the larger taxonomy. (~2-3 hours across all class documents)

### Cross-References

20. **[09-cross-references.md](../09-cross-references.md)** -- Connections to other research projects, including RCN (raccoon ecology for predator management), WDC (woodworking for coop construction), and the broader College of Knowledge. (~15 min)

21. **[10-bibliography.md](../10-bibliography.md)** -- Primary sources, breed club publications, and further reading for ongoing study. (~reference)

### What You'll Learn
- How breed standards work at a detailed, practical level
- Line breeding, outcrossing, and genetic management for small flocks
- The politics and community dynamics of the poultry show world
- How to evaluate your own birds honestly

### What You'll Be Able to DO
- Prepare and show birds at poultry shows
- Design a multi-generation breeding program for your chosen breed
- Mentor new keepers (give back to the community that taught you)
- Contribute meaningfully to heritage breed conservation

### College Connections
- **Science:** Population genetics, conservation biology
- **Community:** Breed clubs, mentorship, agricultural fairs
- **History:** Living agricultural heritage preservation
- **Art:** The aesthetic dimension of breed standards (yes, really)

## How to Use This Pathway

**Go at your own pace.** The time estimates are rough guides, not deadlines. Some people spend a year at Level 2, happily reading breed profiles and tasting eggs. Some people blast through to Level 3 in a weekend because they've already ordered chicks. Both approaches are valid.

**Circle back.** After you've kept chickens for a season, re-read the husbandry guide. It'll hit differently when you've lived it. After you've hatched your first clutch, re-read the genetics study guide. Suddenly it's not abstract -- it's your birds.

**Follow your curiosity.** If you're at Level 1 and the feather genetics document calls to you, read it. The levels are a suggestion, not a gate.

**Connect to other projects.** The research network is richest when you cross boundaries:
- **[RCN](../../RCN/research/)** -- Raccoon ecology (your #1 predator in PNW urban areas)
- **[WDC](../../WDC/research/)** -- Woodworking (build your own coop)
- **[PNT](../../PNT/research/)** -- Painting (paint your favorite hen)
- **[FSR](../../FSR/research/)** -- First responders (animal first aid principles)

**Use the cross-project learning paths.** This project appears in:
- [The PNW Naturalist](../../../learning-paths/pnw-naturalist.md) -- wild galliformes and backyard birds in ecosystem context
- [The Creative Maker](../../../learning-paths/creative-maker.md) -- understanding the animals you create art of
- [The Community Responder](../../../learning-paths/community-responder.md) -- animal first aid basics
