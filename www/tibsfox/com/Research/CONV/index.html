The five convergent patterns

The most architecturally interesting finding is not any single paper but the extent to which independently-pursued research converges on design choices gsd-skill-creator has been making. Five patterns in particular have been arrived at by multiple research groups working from different motivations:

  1. Dual verification: Surrogate plus Oracle. The CoEvoSkills paper's Surrogate + Ground Truth Oracle loop is precisely the CAPCOM + Safety Warden split; their 40.5pp over-baseline gain and 35-45pp cross-model transfer validate the pattern empirically.
  2. Memory / skills / rules as a compression spectrum. Shen et al. identify the “missing diagonal” gap: no surveyed system supports adaptive cross-level compression. gsd-skill-creator's memory-in-CLAUDE.md / skills-in-SKILL.md / rules-in-hooks split is exactly that diagonal.
  3. Capability evolution decoupled from identity. Qin et al.'s Embodied Capability Modules (ECMs) give the Amiga Principle its formal name, with a measured 2.3ms runtime-safety overhead and 100% enforcement.
  4. Two-Gate guardrail. Wang and Dorchen prove distribution-free PAC learnability is preserved under self-modification iff the policy-reachable family has bounded capacity. CAPCOM + Safety Warden stated as a theorem.
  5. Harness as a first-class systems object. The Natural-Language Agent Harnesses paper's control+contracts+state decomposition maps onto the Wave/CAPCOM/Squadron/Fleet stack. CAMCO further formalizes the K_max-then-escalate gate as a theorem.

The five gap-closure candidates
