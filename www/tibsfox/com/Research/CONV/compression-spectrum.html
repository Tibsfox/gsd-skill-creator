The axis (Shen et al., 2026)

The Experience Compression Spectrum paper frames three storage models (episodic memory, procedural skills, declarative rules) as points on a single compression axis:

Storage levelCompression ratiogsd-skill-creator analog
Episodic memory5–20×session memory, CLAUDE.md
Procedural skills50–500×SKILL.md files, cartridge format
Declarative rules1000×+hooks, CAPCOM gate definitions

The paper's citation analysis across 1,136 references in 22 papers reports a cross-community citation rate below 1% — the three storage levels are traditionally studied in isolation. This means papers on episodic memory rarely cite papers on procedural skills, and neither cites the rules literature.

The missing diagonal

The architecturally interesting finding: no system in the 22-paper survey supports adaptive cross-level compression, where material flows between levels based on usage. The paper calls this the “missing diagonal.”

It is precisely the diagonal gsd-skill-creator has been implementing implicitly. The observe-detect-suggest-apply-learn-compose loop routinely promotes session-memory patterns to SKILL.md entries, and sometimes further to hooks. Material flows freely between levels based on usage patterns. The architectural move Shen et al. recommend is what gsd-skill-creator has de-facto been doing.

Formalizing the diagonal

The implication for gsd-skill-creator: formalize the cross-level adaptive compression explicitly, then cite Shen et al. as the canonical motivation. This turns an intuited design decision into a publishable architectural contribution — the paper documents that nobody ships what we have shipped.

A concrete Half-B candidate is src/compression-spectrum/: a single subsystem that provides adapters into the existing session memory, SKILL.md loader, and hooks, and exposes a per-concept compression-level query. Each concept in the College of Knowledge carries a compression-level tag; promotion/demotion moves along the spectrum become observable events the drift-audit CLI can track.
