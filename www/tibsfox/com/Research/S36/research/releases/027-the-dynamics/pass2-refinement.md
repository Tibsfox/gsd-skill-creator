### The Third Ranger Failure and the Central District's Historic Soul

Ranger 3 launched January 26, 1962, aimed at the Moon, and missed by 36,793 kilometers — a guidance system error that sent the spacecraft wide of its target. Unlike Rangers 1 and 2 (stranded in orbit), Ranger 3 escaped Earth — it reached the Moon's neighborhood but couldn't hit the target. Progress through failure: stuck in orbit (Rangers 1-2) → escaped orbit but missed (Ranger 3). The trajectory converges. Each failure is at higher altitude.

The Dynamics are a historic Central District soul group from 1960-1970 — the engine's first artist from the pre-soul-revival era. Every prior soul-identified degree (18-26) was contemporary (2000s-present). The Dynamics reach back to the 1960s when the Central District's musical identity was diversifying from jazz into soul, R&B, and funk. This is the Jackson Street corridor's second act: the same neighborhood that produced Jones's jazz arrangements now producing soul vocal groups during the decade when Motown, Stax, and Atlantic were defining the national soul landscape.

The CD's musical timeline in the engine: Jazz (degrees 0-6, 1940s-2000s) → Salon Jazz (degree 11, 1960s-2000s) → Neo-Soul Collective (degree 19, 2012-present) → Neo-Soul (degree 25, 2010s) → **Historic Soul (degree 27, 1960-1970)**. The engine has been documenting the CD's present first, then reaching back into its past. Degree 27 is the archaeological dig — uncovering the historic soul layer beneath the contemporary neo-soul surface. The neighborhood's soul tradition didn't start in the 2010s. It started in the 1960s. The Dynamics prove it.

Ranger 3 missed the Moon but proved the spacecraft could escape Earth orbit — the problem was aim, not power. The Dynamics missed the national soul mainstream (they never achieved the commercial success of Motown or Stax acts) but proved the Central District could produce soul music — the capability was there, the infrastructure was there, the talent was there. The miss wasn't in the music. It was in the industry's aim — the distribution networks, the label infrastructure, the national promotion that Seattle's soul scene lacked. The guidance error was institutional, not artistic.
