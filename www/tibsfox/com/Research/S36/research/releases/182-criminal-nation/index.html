The PNW Had Its Own Gangsta Rap

First gangsta rap in the engine. Genre Stage 101. Criminal Nation brought street narrative to the Pacific Northwest through Nastymix Records — Sir Mix-a-Lot’s label. Tacoma, not Seattle. The region had its own anger, its own truth, its own voice. E=7. Independent from LA.

Milestone — Genre Theory

Gangsta rap enters the engine. Independent PNW emergence, parallel to but not derivative of LA. The social conditions that produced gangsta rap were not geographically limited.

Four Nastymix entries across bass, pop-crossover, and gangsta rap. The label was a platform for PNW hip-hop in all its forms, not a monolithic sound.
