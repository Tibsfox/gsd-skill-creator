The Return: E=7 to E=8

Treepeople first appeared at degree 263 — the Boise-Seattle pipeline, Doug Martsch before Built to Spill, C/Z Records, post-hardcore in the Northwest tradition. The energy was E=7: high, demanding, melodically complex. The return at degree 298 escalates to E=8. The escalation is documented in the music: Just Kidding (1993), the band's final album, is more extended, more harmonically adventurous, and more rhythmically demanding than any previous Treepeople recording. The band developed. The energy tracks the development.

"The guitar vocabulary that defines Built to Spill was developed in Treepeople's rehearsal rooms in Boise — unexpected harmonic choices that resolve in non-obvious ways, melodic independence between guitar and bass, songs that let themselves develop over longer time spans than the post-hardcore tradition typically allowed."

Doug Martsch — Five Years of Development

The five-year Treepeople arc (1988-1993) documents Martsch's development as a songwriter in detail. The early recordings show the vocabulary already present but unfinished. Guilt, Regret and Embarrassment (1992) is the band in mature form. Just Kidding (1993) is the final synthesis — the most extended, most harmonically specific work the band recorded. By the time of its release, Martsch had already begun forming Built to Spill.

Understanding Built to Spill requires understanding Treepeople first. The longer songs, elaborate guitar arrangements, rhythmic complexity, emotional directness — all of this was present in Treepeople and was carried directly into the new context. Treepeople is the laboratory. Built to Spill is the result.

The Boise-Seattle Corridor — Confirmed

The return confirms what the first entry established: Boise's isolation produces a specific kind of musician. Martsch developed a guitar vocabulary without constant exposure to the trends of a larger scene — it does not sound like it was developed in the Capitol Hill bar scene. It sounds like it was developed alone with records, in Boise, finding a personal harmonic language before arriving in Seattle with something distinct to offer.

Built to Spill has confirmed this corridor across three-plus decades. Martsch remains Boise-based. The Boise-Seattle connection that Treepeople established in 1988 persists.
