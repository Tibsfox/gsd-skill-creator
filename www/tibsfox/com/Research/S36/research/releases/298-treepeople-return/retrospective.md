## What Worked

2. **The C/Z double-return structure is a real pattern.** Skin Yard (297) and Treepeople (298) returning in adjacent degrees mirrors their first appearances at 263 and 264. The label is documented in two symmetrical pairs. This is engine architecture.
3. **The Built to Spill future-entry setup is complete.** Two Treepeople entries (263, 298) provide the full pre-Built foundation. When Built to Spill enters the engine, the context exists.
4. **The post-hardcore tradition is fully located.** Treepeople at E=8 is the Northwest version: melodic exploration within rhythmic complexity. Differentiated from DC (Fugazi, political directness) and East Coast (Jawbreaker, confessional lyric) versions. The Northwest character is documented.

## Discoveries

1. **E=8 in post-hardcore is distinct from E=8 in metal or noise.** The Treepeople E=8 is maximum melodic and rhythmic ambition -- not aggression or noise. The engine's energy scale measures intensity, not genre character. E=8 in post-hardcore and E=8 in metal-adjacent bands are both valid and distinct.
2. **The Boise isolation hypothesis is confirmed by Martsch's guitar vocabulary.** The harmonic choices do not sound like music developed in the Capitol Hill bar scene. They sound like music developed by a person who spent years alone with records, finding a personal language before bringing it to a larger audience.
