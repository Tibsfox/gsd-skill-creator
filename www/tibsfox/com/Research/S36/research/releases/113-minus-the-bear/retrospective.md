## Structural Event 3: Dave Knudson's Two-Hand Tapping -- A Technique as Sonic Identity

- **Situation:** Dave Knudson's two-hand tapping technique is the defining sonic element of Minus the Bear. Both hands on the fretboard simultaneously, building song structures from tapping. Combined with delay and loop pedals for cascading layers. Previously developed in Botch (Tacoma mathcore) in an aggressive context, then transformed for Minus the Bear's warm, melodic application.
- **Root Cause:** The technique is notable for two reasons: (1) it is used structurally, not ornamentally -- entire songs are built from tapping, not just solos; (2) the Botch-to-Minus the Bear transition demonstrates the technique's genre-agnostic versatility.
- **Recommendation:** Document the tapping technique as a major research finding. The Botch connection provides historical depth. This is one of the most distinctive individual instrumental techniques documented in the engine.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Two-hand tapping technique documented. Botch connection documented. Track.
