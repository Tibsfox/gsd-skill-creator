Small-Town Hunger

From Monroe — 30 miles northeast of Seattle, at the base of the Cascade foothills, a small town of 20,000 in Snohomish County. Travis Thompson brings the hunger of an artist who had to leave home to be heard. The small-town-to-Seattle trajectory: Monroe provides the foundation, Seattle provides the infrastructure.

The Pipeline

Monroe joins Tacoma (143) and Spokane (160) as non-Seattle origins in the engine. But Monroe is different — not a major city like Spokane, not an urban center like Tacoma. Monroe is small-town Washington: highway exits, agricultural land, Cascade foothills. 30 miles from Seattle but a world away culturally. The outsider status is real.
