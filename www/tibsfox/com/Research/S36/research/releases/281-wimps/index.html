The Deadpan Punk

Wimps is a Seattle three-piece punk band formed in 2011: Rachel Ratner (guitar/vocals), Dave Ramm (bass), Matt Nyce (drums). The sound is brisk, compact, sardonic. The subject matter is everyday life observed with a dry, precise eye. The songs are short, the playing is tight, the observations are exact.

This is the tradition of deadpan punk — the Buzzcocks, Wire, early Talking Heads in their punk mode — bands that make you recognize something true rather than feel something large. Wimps at E=6 is not asking for your catharsis. It is asking you to notice what you already know.

"The humor does not undercut the music; it is the music's method."

Kill Rock Stars — The Lineage Continues

Kill Rock Stars entered the engine at degree 276 with Bikini Kill. Now, at degree 281, the label returns with Wimps. Two women-fronted punk bands, separated by thirty years of Seattle scene history, on the same Olympia-rooted independent label.

Kill Rock Stars was founded in 1991 by Slim Moon as the commercial infrastructure for riot grrrl and post-K Records indie punk. Its founding roster — Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney, Elliott Smith, Deerhoof — established its identity. Wimps on Kill Rock Stars in 2014 is the label in its contemporary form: evolved, still independent, still committed to the Pacific Northwest punk tradition.

The lineage is not coincidental. Woman-fronted punk band, Seattle geography, Kill Rock Stars institutional home. The ground that Bikini Kill prepared made Wimps possible as simply a punk band, not a movement. The values persisted; the temperature changed.

Rachel Ratner

Ratner's guitar is the defining sound of Wimps: crunchy rather than saturated, clear enough to let the riffs land, played with efficiency and no excess. Her vocals deliver the band's observations in a matter-of-fact tone — close to talking, never overselling the joke or the critique. In a lineage that includes Kim Warnick, Kathleen Hanna, Corin Tucker, and Mia Zapata, Ratner’s guitar belongs. Not in the same mode — each is distinctive — but in the same tradition.

Energy = 6 — Precision Over Volume

E=6 after the E=7-8 streak is the correct calibration for Wimps. The intensity here is not in volume or ferocity but in precision: the exactly right word in the exactly right song at exactly the right tempo. The Gits screamed. Seven Year Bitch confronted. Wimps observes with a dry eye and a perfectly timed chord change. All three approaches are valid punk responses to the world.
