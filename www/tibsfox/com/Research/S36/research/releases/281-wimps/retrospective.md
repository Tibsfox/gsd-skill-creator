## What Worked

1. **The E=6 drop is structurally correct.** After four consecutive entries at E=7-8 (Sleater-Kinney, Unwound, Seven Year Bitch, The Gits), the energy needed to change. Wimps provides the right tamp: still punk, different temperature, same Seattle geography.
2. **Kill Rock Stars returning at 281 confirms the lineage.** Bikini Kill (276) and Wimps (281): five degrees apart, thirty years of scene history apart, same label. The pattern is visible and meaningful.
3. **Deadpan punk is a real tradition and Wimps is its current Seattle representative.** The Buzzcocks-Wire lineage in a 2010s Seattle context. Observation over confrontation.
4. **Rachel Ratner's guitar is distinct.** The crunchy-not-saturated tone is a deliberate aesthetic choice that places Wimps outside the grunge and riot-grrrl-adjacent distortion aesthetics of the preceding degrees.

## Discoveries

1. **Wimps documents the post-riot-grrrl normalization.** Woman-fronted punk band on Kill Rock Stars in 2011 doesn't require a manifesto. The ground was prepared. The normalization is itself historically significant.
2. **The deadpan mode requires as much craft as the confrontational mode.** Precision is harder than volume. Wimps' compact observations are the product of disciplined songwriting.

## Pair Analysis: Wimps + Dall's Porpoise Rooster Tail

- **Energy:** E=6 + E=6 = **E=12.**
