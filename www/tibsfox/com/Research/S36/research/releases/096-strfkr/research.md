## STRFKR -- Danceable Joy, DIY Spirit

### Formation and Career

**STRFKR** (also known as Starfucker) is the project of **Joshua Hodges**, founded in **Portland, Oregon** in 2007. Originally conceived as a solo project, STRFKR grew into a full band known for infectious synth-pop and explosive live performances. Hodges intended STRFKR as a one-off project -- the deliberately provocative name reflects the assumption it would never need to be a "real" band name.

Key albums:
- *STRFKR* (2008) -- the self-titled debut. DIY production. Instant underground following.
- *Reptilians* (2011) -- expanded sound, wider audience. Polyvinyl Records.
- *Miracle Mile* (2013) -- the commercial breakthrough. Synth-pop at its catchiest.
- *Being No One, Going Nowhere* (2016) -- meditation-influenced. Still danceable.

STRFKR's live shows are legendary in the Portland scene -- high-energy, costumed, joyful chaos. The band regularly performs in costume, with confetti, inflatables, and a general atmosphere of uninhibited celebration.

### Portland and DIY Infrastructure

STRFKR is a product of **Portland's indie infrastructure** -- cheap rehearsal spaces, DIY venues, an audience that values independent production, and a distribution network (Polyvinyl) that allows bands to stay independent while reaching a national audience. Portland provides the economic conditions for the DIY maximalist: low overhead, high creative freedom, and an audience that rewards authenticity over polish.

### The Synth-Pop Pulse

STRFKR's defining sonic quality is the **synth-pop pulse** -- driving synth bass lines, pulsing electronic rhythms, catchy melodies layered over danceable beats. Where ODESZA (95) builds to euphoric drops, STRFKR maintains **constant forward motion** -- relentless groove, no dramatic arc, just joy engineered for movement. The pulse is the composition.
