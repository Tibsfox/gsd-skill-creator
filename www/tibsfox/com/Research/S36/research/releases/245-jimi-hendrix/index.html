The Central District — Where It Began

James Marshall Hendrix was born in Seattle and grew up in the Central District — historically Seattle's Black neighborhood, concentrated there in part by racial housing covenants that restricted where African American families could live. The CD was a dense, vibrant cultural environment: Black-owned businesses, COGIC churches whose gospel tradition runs through all of Hendrix's music, Garfield High School (also the school of Quincy Jones), the community structures that provided partial stability during a difficult childhood.

His mother Lucille died when Jimi was fifteen. His father Al raised him with help from grandmother Nora. The family moved frequently. Jimi received his first guitar — an acoustic, played upside-down by a left-handed boy in a right-handed world — around 1958. The physical constraint of playing a right-handed instrument would produce the most distinctive technique in rock history.

"Playing upside-down and restrung, Hendrix could not use standard chord shapes. He developed his own — and in doing so, invented a harmonic vocabulary that funk, soul, and rock guitar still speak today."

London 1966 — The Explosion

After years on the Chitlin' Circuit (see degree 246 for the early career), bassist Chas Chandler discovered Hendrix at the Cafe Wha? in Greenwich Village in July 1966 and brought him to London. The British blues scene — Cream, the Yardbirds, the Rolling Stones — thought it represented the frontier of electric guitar. Hendrix arrived and moved the frontier.

Chandler assembled the Jimi Hendrix Experience: Noel Redding on bass, Mitch Mitchell on drums. The power trio gave Hendrix maximum sonic freedom. "Hey Joe" reached #6 in the UK in late 1966. "Purple Haze" introduced him to the US in 1967. The debut album Are You Experienced reached #5 on the Billboard 200. In nine months, Jimi Hendrix had gone from anonymous sideman to the most talked-about guitarist on earth.

The Three Albums

Are You Experienced (1967)

Reprise / Track

"Purple Haze," "Foxy Lady," "The Wind Cries Mary," "Red House," "Fire." The debut. Full range established immediately: blues, psychedelia, R&B, pop songwriting.

Axis: Bold as Love (1967)

Reprise / Track

"Little Wing," "Spanish Castle Magic," "Castles Made of Sand." "Little Wing" is one of the most beautiful guitar compositions ever recorded — chord melody, voice-leading, open strings, impossible nuance.

Electric Ladyland (1968)

Reprise / Track

"All Along the Watchtower," "Voodoo Child (Slight Return)," "Machine Gun." The double album. Dylan's "All Along the Watchtower" made definitive. "Voodoo Child" recorded in one take. The greatest display of electric guitar ever committed to tape.

Band of Gypsys (1970, live)

Capitol

Fillmore East, NYE 1969. All-Black band: Buddy Miles (drums), Billy Cox (bass). "Machine Gun" — 12-minute improvised anti-war blues. The political record.

Woodstock — August 18, 1969

The final act at Woodstock, performing at dawn for what remained of the half-million crowd. The set included solo electric "The Star-Spangled Banner" — Hendrix playing the national anthem through controlled feedback, dive-bomb whammy bar, and pitch manipulation that evoked rockets, bombs, and the sonic texture of American violence. No words. No political statement explicit. The music said everything that needed saying about the country in 1969.

It remains the most significant political act in the history of popular music performed without a single spoken word.

The Technique — What No One Else Has Done

Hendrix's technical innovations are too numerous to fully catalogue. The core: thumb-over bass notes that allowed simultaneous bass and melody; chord voicings that became the harmonic vocabulary of funk and soul (Prince, Stevie Ray Vaughan, John Mayer descend directly from him); whammy bar integration as melodic element rather than decoration; feedback control as pitchable, sustainble musical texture; tone knob mastery during performance. He played a Fender Stratocaster upside-down and restrung, developing technique that no left-handed converted player has replicated.
