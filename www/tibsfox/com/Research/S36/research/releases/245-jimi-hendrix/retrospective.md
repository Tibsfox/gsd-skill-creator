## What Worked

1. **The Central District origin is the engine's most important geographical data point.** The CD -- formed in part by racial housing covenants -- produced the greatest guitarist in rock history. The neighborhood and the artist are inseparable. Documenting this is essential.
2. **The two-phase Hendrix entry (245 full career, 246 early career) is correct.** The formation years on the Chitlin' Circuit are distinct enough, and important enough, to deserve their own degree. The early career is not a footnote to the Experience era -- it is the school that made the Experience possible.
3. **"The Star-Spangled Banner" at Woodstock deserves its own extended treatment.** The most significant political act in popular music history, performed without a single spoken word. The guitar as political voice.
4. **The technique documentation captures the innovations correctly.** Thumb-over bass, chord voicings that became funk vocabulary, whammy bar as melodic element, feedback control. These are specific, verifiable innovations that changed the instrument permanently.

## Discoveries

1. **The left-handed constraint as innovation source is the engine's most compelling example of constraint-driven creativity.** Playing upside-down and restrung was a physical limitation. The chord voicings it forced became the harmonic vocabulary that Prince, Stevie Ray Vaughan, and John Mayer still use. The limitation was the gift.
2. **Dylan's acknowledgment of Hendrix's "All Along the Watchtower" is one of the most gracious acts of artistic recognition in pop history.** Dylan told Rolling Stone that Hendrix's version is the definitive recording of the song -- that he himself plays it now the way Hendrix played it. This is extraordinary: the composer deferring to the interpreter.
3. **The Band of Gypsys' political dimension is under-documented in mainstream Hendrix narratives.** The all-Black band in 1969-1970, at the height of Black Power, responding to criticism about his white audiences and white bandmates -- this is a deliberate and courageous artistic-political choice.
