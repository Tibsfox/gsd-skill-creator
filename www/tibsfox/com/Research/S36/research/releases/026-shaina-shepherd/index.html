The song moves outward. Shaina Shepherd is a Tacoma-born, gospel-raised, classically trained, opera-injured, grunge-baptized soul singer who carries her voice from church choir to Soundgarden reunion to community ceremony. She names Nina Simone and Quincy Jones as influences. Jack Endino — the godfather of grunge — produced her band’s debut. The surviving members of Soundgarden chose her to sing Chris Cornell’s parts. The genre label is one word: Soul. The Transmuter.

The Classical-to-Soul Arc

Tacoma — Gospel Foundation

Born and raised in Tacoma, WA. Father: military, then federal intelligence. Mother: teacher. Grew up around gospel music and not much else. Watched her mother sing in the church choir. Youngest sibling. Studied vocals under an older sister. The church was everything.

NYU — Classical Training

Passion for vocal technique led to opera, then musical theater, then back to opera. Studied at NYU Steinhardt (first East Coast conservatory in the engine). Influenced by Leontyne Price (opera) and Kurt Carr (gospel).

Age 22 — The Injury

Vocal cord injury destroyed her opera career. Turned to contemporary music. Discovered rock through a library card and Joan Jett’s “Cherry Bomb” — what her gospel upbringing called “the Devil’s music.”

New York — Symphony Administrator

After NYU, worked as an administrator in the symphony world. Worked closely with classical musicians. This insider view of music industry structure later informed the founding of The ARM.

2016 — BEARAXE

Returned to the Pacific Northwest. Started playing piano in November 2017. Became front-woman of BEARAXE, a soul-grunge band. Debut EP Last Call (2018) produced by Jack Endino at Soundhouse Studios. “Like Sharon Jones fronting Mötley Crüe.”

2020–Present — Solo Career

Debut solo single The Virus (2020, Freakout Records). Sub Pop release Never Be Another You (2021, SP1496). Nordstrom holiday campaign. Proceeds to Africatown Community Land Trust (Central District). Nina Simone tribute show at the Royal Room (2019). JAMUARY album (2025). Soundgarden reunion at SMooCH benefit (December 2024).

The Transmuter — 27th Artist-City Pattern

Shepherd’s relationship to Seattle is alchemical: she fuses the city’s multiple genre traditions through a single voice. Her collaborators span grunge (Endino, Soundgarden, McKagan), rock (Heart, Pearl Jam), indie (Acid Tongue), electronic (Chong the Nomad), classical (Andrew Joslyn), and soul. No prior engine artist has working relationships across this many genre worlds. The city is the crucible; Shepherd is the transmuting agent.

The liturgical sequence deploys outward: groove reset (22) → testimony (23) → invocation (24) → congregation (25) → caroling (26). The congregation gathered; the caroling carries the song into the world. Shepherd moves from gospel choir to opera stage to rock club to symphony hall to Soundgarden reunion to community ceremony. Community Work

The ARM (Artist Reclamation Movement)

Co-founded to reclaim the artist industry to serve creators first, shift culture to respect creative professionals, and build an artistic middle class. First artist-founded advocacy organization for industry reform in the engine.

Sunday Sessions

Weekly ceremony at Tim’s Tavern. Modern vaudevillian series. Family-friendly, donation-based. Created from a personal journey navigating mental health: “I needed to do this to force myself to be a listener.”

Jam Jar

Weekly event at Cafe Racer, Capitol Hill. All-Star backing band hosts notable local artists. Shepherd as curator and host, not performer. Creating conditions for others to explore.
