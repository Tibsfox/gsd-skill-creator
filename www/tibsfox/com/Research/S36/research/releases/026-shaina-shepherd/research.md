## Biography & The Classical-to-Soul Arc

Shaina Shepherd is a **singer, songwriter, vocal teacher, and community organizer** based in Seattle, Washington. She was **born and raised in Tacoma** -- making her the engine's first artist from the south Puget Sound corridor rather than Seattle proper. She grew up in a household shaped by **service and faith**: her father served in the military before becoming a federal intelligence agent, her mother was a teacher, and the family's life centered on church. Shaina grew up around **gospel music and not much else**. She watched her mother sing with the church choir and emulated the steady richness of her tone. She was the youngest of her siblings and eventually joined the choir herself, studying under one of her older sisters.

This gospel foundation is structurally significant for the engine. Allen Stone (degree 23) came through the church-worship pipeline from Chewelah, Washington. Ron Artis II (degree 24) inherited gospel sensibility through a Motown-infused family dynasty. Shaina Shepherd represents a **third gospel pathway**: not rural church worship (Stone) or dynastic inheritance (Artis), but **urban gospel as the sole childhood musical environment** -- gospel as the water she breathed before any other influence entered her world.

### The Classical Detour and the Injury That Changed Everything

Shepherd's passion for vocal technique led her from gospel to **opera**, then to **musical theater**, then back to opera. She studied both at **New York University (NYU Steinhardt School of Culture, Education, and Human Development)** -- a prestigious conservatory pathway that places her in the engine's institutional pipeline alongside Berklee (Jones 0, Frisell 1, Lorber 16, Stone 23 visiting), Cornish (Priester 7, Caliman 9, Watts 17), and UW (Standifer 4, Seales 13, Parks 14). NYU is the engine's first **East Coast conservatory** -- every prior institutional pipeline has been Pacific Northwest-based (Garfield, Cornish, UW) or nationally distributed (Berklee).

At **age 22**, Shepherd suffered a **vocal cord injury** that forced her to abandon operatic training. This is the engine's most dramatic career-altering physical event. No prior degree documents an artist whose original artistic practice was destroyed by injury. The vocal cord injury did not end her career -- it **redirected it**. Unable to sustain the demands of classical opera, Shepherd turned to contemporary music. In college she discovered rock and roll -- a genre her gospel upbringing had framed as **"the Devil's music."** Her gateway was a library card and a Joan Jett record: "Cherry Bomb" by The Runaways.

The Nina Simone parallel is impossible to miss and Shepherd herself has named it. **Nina Simone** -- Shepherd's primary influence, cited "above all others" -- was a classically trained pianist who was rejected from the Curtis Institute of Music, an experience Simone attributed to racial discrimination. Simone's pivot from classical piano to jazz/soul/protest music defined the twentieth century's most important artist-activist voice. Shepherd's pivot from classical opera to soul/rock is the same structural arc: **classical training interrupted by circumstance, redirected into popular music, producing a voice that carries classical technique into spaces where it is not expected**.

Shepherd's stated influences confirm this lineage: **Fanny** (the pioneering all-women rock band of the 1970s), **Kurt Carr** (gospel), **Jeff Buckley** (art-rock/soul), **Leontyne Price** (opera -- the first Black woman to achieve international stardom at the Metropolitan Opera), **Quincy Jones** (the engine's degree 0), and above all, **Nina Simone**.

The **Quincy Jones** citation is a direct link to the engine's origin. Shepherd names Jones as an influence -- the same Quincy Jones who launched the engine at degree 0, the Garfield High School graduate who became the most successful producer in American music history. Twenty-six degrees later, his influence surfaces explicitly in a Tacoma-born soul singer's self-described influences. The circle begins to curve.

(Source: shainashepherdmusic.com; Sub Pop; South Seattle Emerald; KEXP; CanvasRebel; KNKX; Sonic Guild; LoudSwell)

### From Brooklyn Back to the Northwest

After graduating from NYU, Shepherd was **immersed in the symphony world as an administrator** in New York City, working closely with classical musicians. This administrative experience is unique in the engine -- no prior artist has worked behind the scenes in institutional music before finding their voice as a performer. The administrative role gave her an insider's view of how the classical music industry operates: its hierarchies, its funding structures, its gatekeeping. This institutional knowledge later informed her founding of **The ARM (Artist Reclamation Movement)**.

Shepherd eventually returned to the Pacific Northwest. She started **playing piano in November 2017** -- a remarkably late instrumental start for someone who had been a vocalist for most of her life. She describes being inspired by the classical musicians she worked with as an administrator: if they could dedicate their lives to mastering an instrument, she could pick one up and see where it led. The piano became the vehicle for her songwriting.

(Source: shainashepherdmusic.com; CanvasRebel; KNKX)

## BEARAXE: Soul-Grunge and the Jack Endino Connection

While finding her voice on the piano, Shepherd became the **front-woman of BEARAXE**, a Seattle-based band described as **"soul-grunge"** -- a genre hybrid that is unprecedented in the engine. BEARAXE is rooted in **prog-rock and soul** but combines **funk, jazz, and proto-punk**. They have been active since **summer 2016**, rocking venues around the Northwest.

BEARAXE's debut EP, ***Last Call*** **(2018)**, was recorded, mixed, and mastered by **Jack Endino** at **Soundhouse Studios**. This is the single most structurally significant production credit in the engine since degree 0.

**Jack Endino** is the **"godfather of grunge."** He recorded **Nirvana's debut album *Bleach*** in 30 hours for $606.17 at Reciprocal Recording in 1988. He produced seminal albums for **Mudhoney, Soundgarden, Screaming Trees, Tad, Green River**, and hundreds of other bands that defined the Seattle grunge movement. Since 1986, he has worked on over **700 records** for bands from 14 countries. Sub Pop recorded most of their early music at Reciprocal Recording with Endino behind the board, and his raw, unpolished production aesthetic essentially *defined* the grunge sound.

Jack Endino producing BEARAXE's debut EP means that the sonic DNA of Seattle's grunge revolution runs directly through Shaina Shepherd's first recorded work. The production pipeline:

| Node | Era | Significance |
|------|-----|--------------|
| Reciprocal Recording / Endino | 1986-1990s | Defined grunge sound (Nirvana, Mudhoney, Soundgarden) |
| Soundhouse Studios / Endino | 2018 | Produced BEARAXE *Last Call* EP |
| BEARAXE / Shepherd | 2016-present | Soul-grunge: gospel voice + grunge production = new hybrid |

The EP is described as an **"unapologetically chaotic story of Black Feminism."** Shepherd's vocal approach in BEARAXE pushes past soul territory into what the South Seattle Emerald described as **"shreds" -- "like Sharon Jones fronting Motley Crue."** The comparison captures the impossible hybrid: a gospel-trained, classically educated, opera-injured voice screaming through the same production chain that gave the world "Smells Like Teen Spirit."

(Source: Bandcamp; KING5; South Seattle Emerald; LoudSwell; Jack Endino official site; Wikipedia)

### The Soundgarden Reunion: December 14, 2024

On **December 14, 2024**, at the **13th annual SMooCH benefit** for Seattle Children's Hospital, the three surviving members of **Soundgarden** -- **Kim Thayil** (guitar), **Matt Cameron** (drums, also of Pearl Jam), and **Ben Shepherd** (bass) -- reunited on a Seattle stage for the first time since **Chris Cornell's death in 2017**. Billed as **Nudedragons** (an anagram of "Soundgarden"), they invited **Shaina Shepherd** to fill the vocal role that Chris Cornell had occupied.

Shepherd shouted **"This is for Chris!"** before launching into a six-song set drawn from Soundgarden's earliest and heaviest material:

| Song | Album | Year |
|------|-------|------|
| "Hunted Down" | *Screaming Life* EP | 1987 |
| "Flower" | *Ultramega OK* | 1988 |
| "Beyond the Wheel" | *Ultramega OK* | 1988 |
| "Outshined" | *Badmotorfinger* | 1991 |
| "Kickstand" | *Superunknown* | 1994 |
| (one additional track) | | |

**Duff McKagan** (Guns N' Roses) joined for the final songs. The event raised over **$2.2 million** for Seattle Children's Hospital's uncompensated care fund.

The structural significance cannot be overstated. Soundgarden is one of the **four founding bands of grunge** (alongside Nirvana, Pearl Jam, and Alice in Chains). Chris Cornell is considered one of the greatest rock vocalists in history. The surviving members chose **Shaina Shepherd** -- a Tacoma-born, gospel-raised, classically trained, opera-injured, soul-singing Black woman -- to stand in his place. The Seattle Times called the reunion "heavy and heartfelt." Rolling Stone and Revolver covered it internationally.

This is the engine's first direct connection to **grunge royalty**. Prior degrees have documented jazz legends (Jones, Anderson, Standifer, Berry, Bown, Priester, Peacock), soul/funk pioneers (Maktub, Polyrhythmics), and indie/alternative artists (Watts, Fly Moon Royale). But no prior degree has placed an engine artist on stage with the founding members of Soundgarden. The grunge thread -- Seattle's most globally recognized musical export -- enters the engine at degree 26.

Shepherd has also collaborated with **Nancy Wilson** (Heart), **Mike McCready** (Pearl Jam), and **Dave Matthews** -- a constellation of rock royalty that no prior engine artist has touched.

(Source: Rolling Stone; Seattle Times; Revolver; American Songwriter; Loudwire; The Prp; NME; mxdwn)

## Community Work: The ARM, Sunday Sessions, Jam Jar

### The Artist Reclamation Movement (The ARM)

Shepherd is the co-founder of **The Artist Reclamation Movement (The ARM)**, an organization working to:
1. **Reclaim the artist industry** to serve creators first
2. **Shift the culture at large** to respect the contributions of creative professionals
3. **Build up an artistic middle class**

The ARM is structurally significant because it addresses the economic infrastructure of artistic practice -- not the art itself but the conditions under which art is made and compensated. No prior engine artist has founded an organization explicitly focused on **artist economics and industry reform**. The ARM is the engine's first **advocacy organization** -- distinct from educational organizations (The Residency at degree 25), cultural preservation organizations (Langston Hughes PAI), or grant-making organizations (Sonic Guild, Artist Trust).

### Sunday Sessions

Shepherd created **Sunday Sessions** at **Tim's Tavern** in Seattle -- a weekly ceremony infused with music and community. The event is described as a "modern vaudevillian style series" that is **family-friendly and donation-based**, featuring special guests and celebrating music and friendship. Shepherd has said she created Sunday Sessions out of a personal journey navigating **mental health challenges** -- "I needed to do this to force myself to be a listener."

Sunday Sessions is the engine's first documented **recurring community music event** created and curated by an engine artist. Joe Brazil (degree 11) held sessions in his living room, but those were informal and private. Shepherd's Sunday Sessions are public, regular, and intentionally structured as **community ritual** -- the word "ceremony" in the description is deliberate.

### Jam Jar

Shepherd curates **Jam Jar** at **Cafe Racer** on Capitol Hill every Wednesday -- an event where an **All-Star backing band** hosts notable local artists, diving into their songwriting world and expanding it through high-level improvisation. The format places Shepherd not as performer but as **curator and host** -- the person who creates the conditions for other artists to explore.

## Music Theory: The Metamorphic Voice

### Vocal Architecture: The Shapeshifter

Shaina Shepherd's voice is the engine's first **metamorphic instrument** -- a voice that is not characterized by a single technique or style but by its ability to **transform between styles within a single performance**. The South Seattle Emerald described her BEARAXE performances as sounding like "an AC/DC or Alabama Shakes recording session" or "a Heart or Sharon Jones show" -- four completely different sonic worlds invoked to describe one voice. This is not versatility (the ability to switch between modes) but **metamorphosis** (the voice actually becoming something different in each mode).

The metamorphic quality comes from the layered training: gospel (the foundation), opera (the technique), rock (the release), soul (the integration). Each layer is not abandoned when the next is added -- they accumulate. When Shepherd screams through a grunge song, the scream carries operatic breath control. When she sings a soul ballad, the ballad carries gospel call-and-response instinct. The voice is a **palimpsest** -- every layer visible through the ones above it.

### Blue Note Deployment (Twenty-Sixth)

Shepherd's blue note is the **metamorphic** blue note -- a bend that changes character mid-bend. It begins as a gospel melisma, passes through operatic vibrato, and lands as a rock scream. Twenty-six deployments across twenty-seven degrees. The metamorphic blue note is the first that **changes identity during execution** -- it is not one type of bend but a bend that transforms as it unfolds, reflecting the artist's own genre-crossing practice.
