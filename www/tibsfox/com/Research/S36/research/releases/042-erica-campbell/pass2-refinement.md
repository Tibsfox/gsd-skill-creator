---

Gemini 6A launched December 15, 1965 — Wally Schirra and Tom Stafford, chasing Gemini 7 (Borman and Lovell, already in orbit for eleven days). The original Gemini 6 mission had been scrubbed when its Agena target vehicle exploded during launch on October 25. NASA improvised: instead of docking with an unmanned target, Gemini 6A would rendezvous with the already-orbiting Gemini 7 — two crewed spacecraft finding each other in the void. Schirra flew Gemini 6A to within one foot of Gemini 7. Thirty centimeters. Two spacecraft, each traveling at 28,000 kilometers per hour, holding station a ruler's length apart. No physical docking — just proximity, precision, and the proof that two vehicles could find each other and stay together. The first rendezvous in space.

Erica Campbell's career is the story of rendezvous — the convergence of sacred and secular, gospel and R&B, the church and the charts. Mary Mary's "Shackles (Praise You)" reached #28 on the Billboard Hot 100 and #5 on R&B charts in 2000 — the first gospel song in five years to crack the R&B top ten. The song did not dock with pop music. It did not fuse or compromise. It held station alongside it, thirty centimeters apart, matching velocity. The gospel was still gospel — praise music, church-rooted, lyrically devotional. The R&B production was still R&B — Warryn Campbell's hip-hop-fluent beats, the rhythmic architecture he learned from sessions with DJ Quik and Death Row Records. Two traditions traveling at full speed, holding proximity without collision. Sacred and secular, one foot apart.

The Gemini 6A rendezvous proved that docking was possible before docking was attempted — the proof of concept that you could find your target and match its orbit. Campbell's work proved that gospel could find a mainstream audience and match its cultural velocity without surrendering its identity. The original Gemini 6 plan failed when its Agena target exploded. The improvised Gemini 6A — rendezvous with a crewed vehicle instead — was better than the original plan. Campbell's own trajectory followed the same pattern: raised in the Evangelistic Church of God in Christ in Inglewood, trained through the gospel show circuit, working as backup for Brandy in the R&B machine — then the improvisation, the convergence of sacred training and secular production knowledge that became Mary Mary. The planned trajectory (gospel stays in the church) failed. The improvised trajectory (gospel finds R&B in orbit and holds station) changed everything.
