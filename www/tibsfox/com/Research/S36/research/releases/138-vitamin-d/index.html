Producer-DJ — The Dual Threat

Vitamin D is both producer and DJ — creating the beats in the studio and performing them live. His production catalog spans three decades of Seattle hip-hop, serving as a sonic history of the Central District. Sample-based, crate-digging, vinyl-rooted. The beats document the sound of a neighborhood and a community.

As founder of Conception Records, Vitamin D built a platform for the Seattle underground — fully independent, physical media, complete creative control. The label is cultural infrastructure for the Central District hip-hop community.

Three Decades in the Central District

Vitamin D has been at the center of Seattle’s hip-hop underground since the early 1990s. His production credits span the Central District ecosystem — from underground mixtapes to polished albums. He has witnessed the entire arc of gentrification from inside the CD. His continued presence represents cultural persistence in the face of displacement.

The relationship between producer and neighborhood is reciprocal: the CD shapes Vitamin D’s sound, and Vitamin D’s sound documents the CD. Funk, soul, jazz, and R&B samples from the crates — the vinyl warmth of a neighborhood’s musical heritage chopped and flipped into new compositions.
