## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The wall dissolves accurately captures the shoegaze acoustic quality.** Dense textural guitar, submerged vocals, diffuse energy. The concept maps precisely to the genre's defining characteristics. Verified.

2. **E=3 is defensible.** Shoegaze is loud in absolute terms but the energy is diffuse. There is no attack, no urgency. The wall of sound envelops rather than confronts. E=3 correctly reflects the experiential energy: the listener is immersed, not impacted. The descent from four consecutive E=5 is structurally necessary.

3. **The MBV/Loveless connection is verifiable.** The name "Loomer" is a direct reference to the opening track of Loveless. The genre lineage is well-established.

4. **The grunge-vs-shoegaze historical narrative is accurate.** The parallel decline of shoegaze with the rise of grunge and Britpop in 1991-93 is well-documented in music history. The irony of shoegaze appearing in Seattle is genuine.

5. **Cross-references to degrees 117 (Deep Sea Diver), 116 (Pony Time), 110 (Kinski), 1 (Nirvana), and 115 (PGMG) are all defensible.**

### Source Quality

6. **Shoegaze as a genre is well-documented.** Extensive critical literature. MBV and Slowdive are canonical. The Seattle scene is smaller but documented through Bandcamp and KEXP.
