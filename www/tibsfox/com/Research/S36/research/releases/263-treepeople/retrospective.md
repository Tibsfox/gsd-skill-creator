## What Worked

1. **Boise as first Idaho entry is historically grounded.** Treepeople genuinely formed in Boise. The Boise-Seattle creative corridor is a real infrastructure that has sustained Pacific Northwest independent music. This is not Seattle-centrism with an Idaho label; it is accurate documentation of a geographic network.
2. **Martsch's development in Treepeople is the correct framing.** The melodic guitar voice that defines Built to Spill's best work is fully present in Treepeople. Documenting this is a contribution to understanding how Martsch developed.
3. **C/Z Records and the Deep Six compilation is historically essential.** Deep Six (1986) is the root document of the grunge lineage. C/Z was there. The label's presence in the engine through Treepeople and Skin Yard connects the engine to this origin moment.
4. **E=7 streak three-mechanism analysis is productive.** Psychedelic density (261), speed (262), post-hardcore complexity (263): three mechanisms, same energy. The engine's E-scale is a measure of presence and impact, not a single axis.

## Discoveries

1. **The Boise-Seattle corridor is a persistent structural feature of Pacific Northwest indie rock.** Treepeople established it; Built to Spill extended it. The corridor has been active since the late 1980s. This is worth tracking as the engine continues.
2. **C/Z's role is underappreciated relative to Sub Pop.** Sub Pop gets the canonical narrative; C/Z gets the Deep Six credit when it is remembered at all. But C/Z was operating at the origin of the grunge ecosystem, and its catalog documents that origin in ways Sub Pop does not.
