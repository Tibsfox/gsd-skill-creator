## What Worked

2. **The drag racing metaphor is structurally useful.** A funny car built for maximum performance over minimum distance, burning alcohol, is an exact metaphor for underground punk band operation: maximum energy, no commercial longevity concern, fuel-intensive and unsustainable by design.
3. **E=7 is correct for the underground floor.** Underground bands carry higher energy because intensity is the currency of credibility when commercial success is absent. The inverse relationship between commercial apparatus and raw energy is documented correctly at degree 229.

## Discoveries

1. **The underground label tier (Empty Records) sits lower commercially but higher energetically than the mid-tier.** This is counterintuitive -- smaller label, higher E. The relationship between commercial scale and musical energy is inverse in the underground. More resources = more production polish = lower raw energy.
2. **The funny car name is one of the most precise band name choices in the engine.** Most band names are evocative but imprecise. Alcohol Funnycar is a specific mechanical reference that maps exactly onto the band's operating philosophy.
