EMPTY RECORDS ENTERS THE ENGINE. Degree 229 brings the most deliberately underground label of the Seattle ecosystem into the engine. The funny car runs on alcohol — maximum performance, minimum distance, no concern for fuel economy.

The Underground Floor

Alcohol Funnycar represents the structural base of Seattle's early-1990s scene — the underground floor below Sub Pop's mid-tier and the major label commercial surface. The band formed around 1990 in Seattle and operated on Empty Records, one of the least commercially visible labels in the ecosystem. Their grunge-punk hybrid burned fast and loud, built for impact rather than longevity.

The name is programmatic: an alcohol funny car is a drag racing machine that burns methanol at maximum power output, is structurally minimal, and runs at the edge of controllable. This is the correct description of the music and the band's operating philosophy. Run hot. Run fast. Don't worry about the fuel economy.

"The underground can sustain E=7 because energy is what it has to offer in place of commercial infrastructure. Maximum effort, minimum apparatus."

Empty Records — The Label Map Completes

Empty Records was founded in Seattle in the late 1980s by figures closely affiliated with the underground scene — a deliberate anti-commercial operation. Where Sub Pop packaged grunge for export and Amphetamine Reptile provided noise-punk with distribution, Empty Records stayed underground by design. The catalog skews abrasive, fast, and sloppy in the best sense.

With degree 229, the engine's Seattle label map is complete: Sub Pop (221-227), Amphetamine Reptile (228), and now Empty Records. The full spectrum of early-1990s independent label geography is documented.

Punk Roots of Grunge

Alcohol Funnycar embodies the punk substrate from which grunge grew. The Wipers — Portland's Greg Sage — are the most direct Pacific Northwest ancestor of this form: fast tempos, melodic guitar lines over distorted rhythm, DIY production ethic. Hüsker Du demonstrated that punk could be complex. The Replacements showed that controlled sloppiness was an art. Alcohol Funnycar inherits all three traditions and operates at the E=7 end of the spectrum where noise and urgency are primary values.

Energy = 7 — The Underground Ceiling

E=7 is the high-energy zone before the absolute noise ceiling (E=8-9). At E=7, songs function like short detonations — built for impact, not development. Alcohol Funnycar operates fully in this zone: fast, distorted, minimal structure, maximum urgency. The grunge-punk overlap pushes tempo into punk territory while maintaining Seattle's characteristic guitar noise.
