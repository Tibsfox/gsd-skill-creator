## Discoveries

1. **The Sub Pop Singles Club is underemphasized in the engine's documentation relative to its historical importance.** The Singles Club was the real-time distribution mechanism that put Sub Pop's supporting roster music into the hands of the dedicated international grunge audience. Swallow appeared in the Singles Club. Without the Singles Club, the supporting roster was invisible outside Seattle.
2. **The 1989-1992 founding window is the most important period in Seattle music history for the engine's purposes.** Every degree in this window -- whether summit (309-316) or secondary roster (318-322, 324) -- is documenting a single cohort. The same city, the same neighborhood (Capitol Hill and adjacent), the same bars, the same label. The engine's grunge sequence is one dense cultural moment seen from multiple positions within it.
