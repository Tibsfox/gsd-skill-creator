## What Worked

1. **FIRST BAINBRIDGE ISLAND is a genuine geographic milestone.** No previous engine entry has come from Bainbridge Island. The island's combination -- ferry distance from Seattle, island isolation during Wood's formation years, the Wood family's specific cultural context -- produced an aesthetic that the Seattle mainland scene was not developing. Geographic isolation produced the unique synthesis.
2. **Andrew Wood's persona and the glam tradition are documented fully.** L'Andrew the Love God: the name, the costumes, the sustained power-rock tenor, the theatrical commitment, the refusal of irony. In the flannel-and-denim scene, Wood chose gold lamé and meant it. The documentation captures both what he was and why it was distinctive.
3. **The Deep Six placement (295) consecutive with Green River (294) is historically correct.** Both bands are on *Deep Six* (1986). The engine places them consecutively because they are consecutive steps in the same story. Hearing degrees 294 and 295 back-to-back is hearing the two bands whose convergence produced Mother Love Bone.
4. **The synthesis analysis is the essential contribution.** Glam (Bowie, Bolan, Plant) + proto-grunge (heaviness, rawness) = something new. No other Seattle band was doing this. Malfunkshun is the invention; Mother Love Bone is the invention at full scale with Gossard and Ament's architecture supporting it.

## Discoveries

1. **Bruce Fairweather connects Malfunkshun to Love Battery (221) in the engine.** Fairweather, who played in the early Malfunkshun and Green River configurations, became a founding member of Love Battery. The engine's guitarist web extends: degree 221 (Love Battery, U-District psychedelia) is connected through Fairweather to degrees 294-295 (the grunge origin arc).

## Pair Analysis: Malfunkshun + Rhinoceros Auklet Colony Night

- **Energy:** E=7 (Malfunkshun) + E=6 (Rhinoceros Auklet) = **E=13.**
- **Theme:** THE ISLAND NIGHT. Andrew Wood grew up on Bainbridge Island, developing the glam punk synthesis in the ferry-accessible but daily-separated island environment. The Rhinoceros Auklet arrives at its rocky island colony only after dark, navigating by sound and memory in complete darkness. Both are island-derived. Both carry their full identity into the night. Malfunkshun's theatricality is nocturnal in spirit: full commitment, not diminished by the absence of an audience, operating from an island outpost.
