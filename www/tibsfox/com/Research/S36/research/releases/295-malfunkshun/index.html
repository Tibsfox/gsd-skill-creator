First Bainbridge Island — Andrew Wood — Deep Six (1986)

Bainbridge Island enters the engine for the first time. Andrew Wood grew up there — the island across the ferry from Seattle, the home of an aerospace engineer's son who absorbed David Bowie and T. Rex from records while the mainland scene developed its grunge aesthetic. Malfunkshun is Andrew Wood's first band: glam punk/grunge, the synthesis of theatricality and heaviness that Wood brought to the Seattle scene. Stone Gossard and Jeff Ament left Green River (294) to join Wood in Mother Love Bone (296). Deep Six (1986) places Wood in the origin document alongside Gossard, Ament, and Cornell.

Andrew Wood — L'Andrew, the Love God

Wood called himself "L'Andrew, the Love God" on stage. He performed in scarves, tights, ruffled shirts — full glam regalia. In the denim-and-flannel Seattle scene, this was not eccentricity but a principled aesthetic statement: rock music could be performed with the full visual amplification of the Bowie and Bolan tradition. The voice was natural, extraordinary — a sustained power-rock tenor capable of Plant's range and the emotional openness that distinguished him from heavy metal's aggressive tradition. The stage persona and the music were not two things: they were one thing.

Wood is remembered by everyone who knew him as someone of unusual generosity, warmth, and enthusiasm about other musicians. His presence made people around him feel they were witnessing something that mattered. This quality was not separable from the music — it was the music's source.

"In the Seattle scene that was choosing flannel and mud-colored album covers, Wood chose gold lamé and called himself the Love God. This was not irony. He meant it."

The Glam Punk/Grunge Synthesis

Wood's primary influences were glam: Bowie (Ziggy Stardust era), T. Rex (Marc Bolan), Led Zeppelin, Aerosmith (the early blues-rock-glam hybrid). These are not the punk-and-metal combination that defines most Seattle grunge. Wood arrived at the scene from a completely different direction. The collision of his glam sensibility with the scene's heavy guitar tradition produced something new: the glam punk/grunge synthesis. Not one or the other. Both simultaneously.

When Gossard and Ament from Green River joined Wood in Mother Love Bone, they brought their proto-grunge riff-heaviness into contact with Wood's theatrical vocal approach. The Mother Love Bone sound is what happens when those two directions meet. Malfunkshun is where the synthesis was invented — on Bainbridge Island, before anyone else heard it.

Cassette recordings and self-released material from the Bainbridge Island and early Seattle years. The analog history of the Wood brothers developing the Malfunkshun sound before the Seattle scene formalized.
