## SELF-RELEASED -- THE MOST FUNDAMENTAL INDEPENDENCE

### No Label. No Infrastructure. Just the Artist.

The self-released model in Seattle hip-hop:

- **The independence spectrum.** The engine has documented a spectrum of independence:
  - Nastymix Records (177-179, 182): Independent label with infrastructure.
  - Macklemore LLC (180-181): Self-owned LLC with business operations.
  - Cold Recordings (183): Artist-built label and brand platform.
  - Self-released (184): No label at all. Direct to platform.
- **Digital platforms as infrastructure.** SoundCloud, Bandcamp, Spotify, YouTube -- these platforms provide the distribution infrastructure that labels once controlled. A self-released artist can reach listeners without any intermediary.
- **The trade-off.** Self-release means total control but also total responsibility. No label handles promotion, distribution logistics, playlist placement, or branding. The artist does everything or it doesn't get done.
- **Scale vs authenticity.** Self-released music tends to reach smaller audiences but maintains total artistic control. In the Seattle hip-hop tradition, this authenticity is valued.

(Sources: Self-release platforms; digital distribution analysis; Seattle hip-hop independence spectrum; engine internal analysis)
