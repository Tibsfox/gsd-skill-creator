The Proto-Grunge Foundation Revisited

Skin Yard first appeared at degree 264 — anchored to Jack Endino's production legacy, to Matt Cameron's early career, to C/Z Records and the Deep Six compilation. The return at degree 297 arrives with thirty-three degrees of accumulated engine context: the Blood Brothers, Botch, These Arms Are Snakes, Swallow, Green River, Malfunkshun, Mother Love Bone. The full arc of Seattle's heavy underground is now visible. Skin Yard at 264 was the origin. Skin Yard at 297 is the rearview.

Proto-grunge can now be defined precisely. In 1985, there was no such category. There was heavy music made by young Seattle musicians in a punk/metal interzone — riff-based, dark, direct, anti-pretentious. The category "grunge" was created in 1991-1992 by the commercial apparatus that responded to Nirvana's success. Proto-grunge is the retroactive label for what Skin Yard was doing six years before that naming.

"The band taught the producer. The production philosophy is a direct extension of what Skin Yard practiced as a band: minimum interference, document the live performance, preserve the energy in the room."

Jack Endino — The Guitarist Inside the Producer

At degree 264, Endino appeared primarily as producer — the man who recorded Nirvana's Bleach for $606.17, who produced Mudhoney's Superfuzz Bigmuff, who shaped the sonic character of Seattle rock across two decades. The return demands the other half of that story: Endino was in the band. He played guitar in Skin Yard for seven years while running Reciprocal Recording and developing the production philosophy that would define the Seattle sound.

His guitar work in Skin Yard is heavy, textured, and deliberately anti-showy — he plays the way he records: the guitar serves the song, the song serves the energy. The production philosophy is derived from performance experience. He knew what it felt like to play heavy music and what it should sound like on tape because he had done both, in the same years, with the same people.

The Complete Arc: 1985–1992

Formation (1985)

Origin

Endino (guitar), McMillan (vocals), House (bass), Cameron (drums). All four became structurally important to Seattle in different domains.

Deep Six compilation (1986)

C/Z Records

Daniel House founds C/Z and releases the original document of the Seattle heavy underground. Six bands: Skin Yard, Soundgarden, Melvins, Green River, Malfunkshun, U-Men.

Cameron departs for Soundgarden (1986)

Personnel

The first major arc: Skin Yard (1985-86) to Soundgarden (1986-97) to Pearl Jam (1998-present). Skin Yard is Cameron's origin.

Core albums on C/Z (1987–89)

Discography

Skin Yard (1987), Hallowed Ground (1988), Blood and Candle Smoke (1989). The proto-grunge catalog. Endino's philosophy in action.

Dissolution (1992)

End

McMillan departs for Gruntruck. Band dissolves. Inside the Eye (1993) released posthumously. Seven years complete.

Daniel House — The Infrastructure Man

House's role extends far beyond bass guitar. He founded C/Z Records and curated Deep Six — one of the most consequential label and compilation decisions in Pacific Northwest music history. Without C/Z, the Seattle underground had no self-documentation at the moment it was forming. House made the infrastructure that preserved the origin.
