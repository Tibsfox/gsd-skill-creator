## SKIN YARD -- THE COMPLETE ARC

### Seven Years: 1985-1992

The full Skin Yard timeline is now documentable:

- **1985: Formation.** Jack Endino (guitar), Ben McMillan (vocals), Daniel House (bass), and Matt Cameron (drums). The initial lineup is extraordinary in retrospect: Endino would become Seattle's most important producer; Cameron would become Soundgarden's drummer and then Pearl Jam's drummer; McMillan would co-found Gruntruck; House would found C/Z Records and release the *Deep Six* compilation. Four people in a band, all of whom would become structurally important to the Seattle scene in different ways.
- **The *Deep Six* compilation (1986).** C/Z Records released the *Deep Six* compilation in 1986 -- one of the most important documents in Seattle music history. Six bands: Skin Yard, Soundgarden, Melvins, Green River, Malfunkshun, U-Men. This is the compilation that identified the Seattle heavy underground as a community. Daniel House (Skin Yard bassist) founded C/Z Records and produced this document. Skin Yard is simultaneously a *Deep Six* band and the home of the label founder. The compilation is the origin document of the scene.
- **Matt Cameron leaves for Soundgarden (1986).** Cameron's departure for Soundgarden is the first major personnel shift. His replacement is Todd Kimball. Cameron's trajectory -- Skin Yard (1985-1986) → Soundgarden (1986-1997) → Pearl Jam (1998-present) -- is one of the most significant careers in Seattle rock. Skin Yard is the beginning.
- **C/Z Records releases: *Skin Yard* (1987), *Hallowed Ground* (1988), *Blood and Candle Smoke* (1989).** The core albums on C/Z. The sound develops across these three records: heavy guitar, dark lyrical imagery, rhythmic drive. Ben McMillan's vocals have a quality that sits between metal's operatic ambition and punk's directness. Endino's production is characteristic -- live energy documented rather than transformed.
- **Gruntruck formation begins (1990).** Ben McMillan and Jack Endino's parallel activities begin to diverge. McMillan co-founds Gruntruck. Skin Yard continues but the internal energy is shifting.
- **Final recordings: *Fist Sized Chunks* (1991), *Inside the Eye* (1993, posthumous).** The band records and releases final material. By 1992 the band dissolves. The seven-year run is complete.

(Sources: Skin Yard timeline 1985-1992; founding lineup: Endino, McMillan, House, Cameron; Deep Six compilation 1986: six bands, C/Z Records; Matt Cameron departure to Soundgarden 1986; C/Z albums: Skin Yard 1987, Hallowed Ground 1988, Blood and Candle Smoke 1989; Gruntruck formation; Fist Sized Chunks 1991; Inside the Eye 1993 posthumous)

## JACK ENDINO -- THE GUITARIST INSIDE THE PRODUCER

### The Man Who Made the Records Was Also in the Band

The critical fact about Skin Yard that differentiates it from any other Seattle band:

- **Endino the guitarist.** At degree 264, the engine documented Endino primarily as a producer. At degree 297, the return demands accounting for Endino as a guitarist -- the player inside the band that became a production philosophy. Endino's guitar work in Skin Yard is heavy, textured, and deliberately anti-showy. He plays the way he would later record: the guitar serves the song; the song serves the energy; the energy is the point.
- **Production philosophy derived from performance.** Endino has said in interviews that his production approach is shaped by being a musician: he records bands the way he would want to be recorded. That approach -- minimum interference, document the live performance, preserve the room's energy -- is a direct extension of what Skin Yard practiced as a band. The band taught the producer.
- **Reciprocal Recording as home studio.** Endino founded Reciprocal Recording in Seattle and did much of Skin Yard's own recording there, alongside the work he did for Mudhoney, Nirvana, Soundgarden, and hundreds of others. The studio that shaped Seattle rock was operated by the same man who was playing guitar in one of the foundational proto-grunge bands simultaneously.
- **The conflict of interest that wasn't.** Endino being both a participant in and a documenter of the Seattle scene is unusual. Most producers are outside the bands they record. Endino was inside. This insider position gave his production an empathy that outside producers rarely achieve: he understood what it felt like to play the music because he was playing it himself, in a different context. Skin Yard is the source of that empathy.
- **After Skin Yard.** Endino's post-Skin Yard career continued with constant production work. He produced records for bands across the Pacific Northwest and internationally for decades following the band's dissolution. The production career outlasted the band by thirty-plus years. But the philosophical foundation -- laid in Skin Yard's rehearsal rooms and the Reciprocal Recording sessions -- never changed.

(Sources: Endino as guitarist in Skin Yard; production philosophy derived from performance experience; minimum interference philosophy; Reciprocal Recording; insider producer position; Skin Yard self-production through Endino; post-Skin Yard production career continuity)

## DANIEL HOUSE -- THE INFRASTRUCTURE MAN

### Bass Player, Label Founder, Scene Builder

Daniel House's role in Skin Yard extends far beyond bass guitar:

- **Founding C/Z Records.** House founded C/Z Records, the label that released the *Deep Six* compilation and Skin Yard's own catalog. A bassist founding an independent label and using it to document his own scene is not unusual in independent music history -- but House's execution at C/Z was consequential. Without C/Z, the *Deep Six* compilation may not have happened; without *Deep Six*, the Seattle underground had no self-documentation at the moment it was forming.
- **The *Deep Six* curation decision.** House chose the six bands on *Deep Six*: Skin Yard, Soundgarden, Melvins, Green River, Malfunkshun, U-Men. This is one of the most consequential curatorial decisions in Pacific Northwest music history. The six bands represent the full range of the Seattle heavy underground at the moment of its formation. The compilation sold modestly at the time but became historically essential as documentation.
- **Bass playing in Skin Yard.** House's bass work is the grounding element in Skin Yard's sound. Where Endino's guitar explores harmonic depth and McMillan's vocals push against the ceiling, House's bass provides the structural foundation. Proto-grunge's heaviness is substantially a bass-and-drums phenomenon -- the weight comes from the low end.
- **Scene infrastructure as legacy.** House's contribution to Seattle music is infrastructure: the label, the compilation, the distribution, the documentation. Musicians make music; House made the conditions in which music could be documented and distributed. This is a different but equally essential contribution.

(Sources: Daniel House biography; C/Z Records founding; Deep Six compilation curation: six bands, 1986; bass playing in Skin Yard; scene infrastructure as distinct contribution; independent label foundation in context of scene development)

## THE SOUND -- PROTO-GRUNGE, E=7

### Before the Category Existed

Skin Yard's music at E=7 occupies a specific position that can now be defined precisely:

- **Proto-grunge vs. grunge.** Grunge, as a commercial category, did not exist in 1985-1986. What existed was heavy music made by young Seattle musicians in a punk/metal interzone. Skin Yard's music at this moment has elements of heavy metal (riff-based song structures, downtuned guitars, dark lyrical imagery) and punk (directness, anti-pretension, live energy prioritized over studio polish). It is neither genre; it is the space between them that would eventually be labeled "grunge."
- **The riff as architecture.** Skin Yard songs are built around guitar riffs the way a building is built around a frame. The riff is not decoration; it is structure. This is a heavy metal tradition applied with punk directness. The result is songs that feel inevitably constructed -- each riff leading to the next in a progression that feels necessary.
- **Ben McMillan's vocals.** McMillan's vocal style in Skin Yard is an important component of what makes the band's sound distinctive. He does not shout in a punk mode and does not sing in a metal mode. He occupies the space between: melodically aware but rough-edged, emotionally direct but not theatrically overwrought. The vocals over Endino's heavy riffs produce the proto-grunge character.
- **E=7 calibration.** The engine places Skin Yard at E=7 -- not maximum (E=8-9 represents the most intense entries in the catalog) but high. This is correct. Skin Yard is heavy but the heaviness is structural rather than aggressive. The music does not attack; it presses. The distinction is felt: E=7 is the weight of a large stone, not the impact of a collision.
- **Comparison to contemporaries.** At the same energy level: Green River (proto-grunge, also E=7), Mother Love Bone (E=8, higher). Skin Yard's E=7 is the appropriate position in the energy landscape: heavier than the ambient and melodic entries, lighter than the maximum-aggression entries.

(Sources: Proto-grunge genre definition; heavy metal / punk interzone 1985-1986; riff-based architecture; McMillan vocal style; E=7 calibration; energy landscape comparison: Green River, Mother Love Bone; structural vs. aggressive heaviness distinction)

## BEN McMILLAN -- AND GRUNTRUCK

### The Voice That Left and Continued

Ben McMillan's trajectory after Skin Yard is one of the more complex stories in Seattle rock:

- **Gruntruck formation (1990-1991).** McMillan co-founded Gruntruck with guitarist Turner Van Blarcum (ex-Bundle of Hiss). Gruntruck was signed to Roadracer Records and released two albums: *Push* (1992) and *Crazy Love* (1992). The band achieved some commercial presence in the post-Nirvana commercial grunge moment but dissolved by 1994.
- **Gruntruck's sound.** Gruntruck moved Skin Yard's proto-grunge template toward more explicitly commercial production -- cleaner recording, more attention to radio-ready song structure. This is the expected response to the commercial grunge moment: the underground bands that had been doing this music since 1985 suddenly had a market, and many of them adjusted their production approach accordingly.
- **McMillan's health.** Ben McMillan struggled with addiction and health problems following Gruntruck's dissolution. He continued performing with various projects through the 1990s and 2000s but never achieved the sustained commercial presence that the Skin Yard platform might have predicted. He died in 2008.
- **The voice in context.** McMillan's vocals in Skin Yard are the most important element for understanding what proto-grunge sounded like before "grunge" was commercially legible. His approach -- melodic but raw, emotionally direct but not performed -- is the vocal style that the grunge commercial wave would package in a cleaner form. McMillan was doing the original version.

(Sources: Ben McMillan biography; Gruntruck formation 1990-1991; Turner Van Blarcum; Roadracer Records; Push 1992; Crazy Love 1992; commercial grunge adjustment; McMillan health struggles; death 2008; vocal style as proto-grunge archetype)

## LEGACY AND SIGNIFICANCE

### The Band That Produced the Producer

Skin Yard's legacy in Seattle music is indivisible from Jack Endino's production legacy:

- **The original document.** Skin Yard's *Deep Six* appearance (1986) is the original document of the Seattle heavy underground. Before Nirvana's *Bleach*, before Mudhoney's *Superfuzz Bigmuff*, there was *Deep Six* -- and Skin Yard was one of its six bands and the home of its label founder.
- **The Matt Cameron origin.** Skin Yard is Matt Cameron's first band of consequence. Cameron's trajectory -- Skin Yard → Soundgarden → Pearl Jam -- is one of the great careers in Seattle rock. The beginning is here.
- **The production philosophy origin.** Jack Endino's approach to recording -- live energy, minimum interference, the band in the room -- was not developed in a vacuum. It was developed in Skin Yard's rehearsal rooms and on Skin Yard's recording sessions, where Endino learned what it felt like to play heavy music and what it should sound like when captured on tape. The philosophy precedes the career.
- **Quiet influence.** Skin Yard is not a band that the mainstream history of Seattle rock discusses prominently. They are overshadowed by Nirvana, Soundgarden, Pearl Jam -- bands that Endino produced or that Cameron drummed for later. But the foundational work -- the seven years of proto-grunge, the *Deep Six* compilation, the C/Z infrastructure -- is part of the foundation those larger careers were built on. Quiet influence is still influence.

(Sources: Deep Six 1986 as original document; Matt Cameron origin in Skin Yard; Endino production philosophy origin; quiet influence pattern; mainstream vs. underground historical documentation)
