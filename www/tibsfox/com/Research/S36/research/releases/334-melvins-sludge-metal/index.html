The Houdini Era — Atlantic Records 1993-1996

Houdini (1993, Atlantic)

Two Cobain-Produced Tracks

The most commercially hostile major-label debut of the grunge era. "Hooch" as the near-commercial moment. "Hag Me" as feedback nightmare. Kurt Cobain produced "Cophead" and "Spread Eagle Beagle." Atlantic expected Nirvana-scale results. The Melvins delivered the Melvins.

Stoner Witch (1994, Atlantic)

Second Atlantic Album

More melodic than Houdini, no less heavy. "Queen" and "Magic Pig Detective" demonstrate strange pop structure within extreme context. Atlantic keeps them one more year.

Stag (1996, Atlantic)

Final Atlantic Album

Most experimental of the three. Atlantic relationship concludes. The Melvins move to Amphetamine Reptile, then Ipecac. Four years on a major label, no compromise, three records.

Aberdeen at Maximum

The Metal Summit spans Aberdeen (Melvins, degrees 308 and 334), the U-District (Soundgarden, degree 335), and Beacon Hill (Alice in Chains, degree 336). Aberdeen is the engine's most peripheral geographic entry — 100 miles from Seattle, a declining timber town on the coast — and it produces the engine's maximum energy degree. The periphery contains the maximum. This is one of the engine's defining geographic statements: the center of the scene (Capitol Hill, the U-District) produces important music; the periphery produces the heaviest music in the entire rotation.
