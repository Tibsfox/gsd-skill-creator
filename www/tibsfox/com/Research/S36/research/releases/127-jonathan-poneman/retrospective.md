## Structural Event 3: The Nirvana Deal as Survival Mechanism

- **Situation:** Poneman negotiated a royalty percentage on future Nirvana albums when the band left Sub Pop for DGC Records. When Nevermind sold 30 million copies, the royalties saved Sub Pop from bankruptcy.
- **Root Cause:** The royalty clause was unusual for a band departing an indie label. Poneman's business instinct recognized that Nirvana's potential value extended beyond any one-time buyout. This single negotiation determined Sub Pop's survival.
- **Recommendation:** Document the Nirvana deal as a structural inflection point. The moment where business acumen saved artistic infrastructure.
