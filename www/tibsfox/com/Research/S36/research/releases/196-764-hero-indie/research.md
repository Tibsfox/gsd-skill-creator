## UP RECORDS -- THE LABEL

### Olympia to Seattle

Up Records is a significant label in the PNW indie ecosystem:

- **Up Records.** Founded in 1994 in Seattle by Chris Takino. An indie label that released some of the most critically regarded PNW indie rock of the 1990s-2000s. Artists included 764-HERO, Modest Mouse (early releases), Built to Spill, Lync, and others.
- **Aesthetic.** Up Records had a distinctive aesthetic: understated, emotionally intelligent, sonically adventurous but never flashy. The label's roster shared a quality of restraint -- these were bands that communicated through precision and mood rather than volume.
- **764-HERO as Up Records core.** 764-HERO was one of Up Records' foundational artists. John Atkins (764-HERO frontperson) was deeply embedded in the Up Records community.
- **Up Records and Kill Rock Stars.** 764-HERO also released on Kill Rock Stars (Olympia-based). The two labels shared an ethos: independent, artist-centered, quality over commerce. The 764-HERO presence on both labels reflects their position at the intersection of Seattle and Olympia indie scenes.

## JOHN ATKINS AND THE 764-HERO SOUND

### Restraint as Method

764-HERO's indie rock sound is defined by restraint:

- **John Atkins.** Vocalist, guitarist, songwriter. Atkins' guitar style is deliberate -- notes chosen for maximum emotional effect with minimum flash. The guitar lines are melodic but sparse. Chords ring and decay. Space is as important as sound.
- **Polly Johnson.** Bassist, vocalist. Johnson's bass provides melodic counterpoint to Atkins' guitar. The interplay between guitar and bass is the core of the sound -- two melodic voices in conversation.
- **Vocal delivery.** Atkins sings in a measured, slightly detached register. Not emotionless but emotionally controlled. The restraint makes the moments of openness more powerful. The vocal style is conversational -- speaking melodically rather than performing.
- **Song structures.** The songs follow conventional verse-chorus structures but with extended instrumental passages, gradual builds, and unexpected textural shifts. Not post-rock in the Mogwai/Godspeed sense, but post-rock-adjacent in the attention to dynamics and texture.
- **Connection to Modest Mouse.** 764-HERO and Modest Mouse shared members, shows, and aesthetic territory. Isaac Brock (Modest Mouse) and John Atkins existed in the same scene. The two bands are branches of the same Up Records tree.

(Sources: 764-HERO discography; John Atkins; Polly Johnson; Up Records aesthetic; Modest Mouse connection)

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of 764-HERO (Indie Rock Context)

- **Tempo.** Moderate. 90-120 BPM typically. Not rushed. Space for notes to ring.
- **Guitar.** Clean to lightly overdriven. Melodic lines, not chord-strumming. Notes chosen precisely. Open tunings occasionally. The guitar BREATHES -- space between notes is intentional.
- **Bass.** Melodic, independent. Not just root notes. A second voice that sometimes leads the arrangement.
- **Vocals.** Measured, slightly detached, conversational. Not shouted, not whispered. Pitched in a comfortable middle register. The emotional content is in the word choice and phrasing, not in vocal fireworks.
- **Drums.** Restrained but dynamic. Quiet passages with rimshots and light cymbals. Louder passages with full kit. The drums serve the dynamics.
- **Dynamics.** The key feature. 764-HERO songs move between quiet and loud, spare and dense. The builds are gradual. The climaxes are earned through patience.
- **Production.** Clear, warm, modest. Not lo-fi, not polished. The production serves the songs without drawing attention to itself.
