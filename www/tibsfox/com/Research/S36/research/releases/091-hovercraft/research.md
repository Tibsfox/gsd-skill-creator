## HOVERCRAFT -- The Drone/Noise Duo

### Formation and History

Hovercraft was a Seattle drone/noise duo formed in **1993** by **Beth Liebling** (bass) and **Ryan Campbell** (guitar, electronics). The band made heavy, hypnotic, instrumental drone/noise -- no vocals, no conventional song structure, no verses, no choruses. Just bass, guitar, electronics, and volume.

Active from 1993 to 2001. Releases on **Tag Records** and **DGC Records** (a Geffen subsidiary). The fact that a drone/noise duo ended up on a major-label subsidiary reflects the post-Nirvana economics of the 1990s -- major labels signed extreme Seattle acts hoping lightning would strike twice.

### Beth Liebling and Eddie Vedder

**Beth Liebling** was married to **Eddie Vedder** of Pearl Jam (degree 83). This is a direct personal connection between degrees 83 and 91 -- the biggest band in Seattle's grunge era and a two-person drone/noise project that rejected everything grunge stood for commercially. Vedder occasionally performed with Hovercraft. The connection shows how Seattle's musicians moved fluidly between commercial and experimental worlds.

### The Sound

Hovercraft's music is built from:
- **Sustained bass drones** -- Liebling's bass provides the low-frequency foundation, held and looped into continuous tones.
- **Guitar feedback loops** -- Campbell's guitar generates feedback that sustains and evolves over long durations.
- **Electronic distortion** -- effects pedals, processing, and amplification push the sound toward its physical limits.
- **Volume** -- the music is meant to be experienced at high volume, where the physical vibrations become part of the experience.

The result is a **wall of sound** that does not move forward in time like a song but presses down like weight. No beginning, middle, or end in any conventional sense. The music starts, sustains, and stops.
