## What Worked

1. **The Cameron consecutive degrees (225-226) is the engine's tightest dual-appearance arc.** One musician, two consecutive degrees, two entirely different emotional registers: Temple of the Dog's grief monument (225) and Wellwater Conspiracy's orbital space rock (226). The contrast documents Cameron's range.
2. **The Monster Magnet connection imports a cross-regional element.** McBain's Monster Magnet history brings a New Jersey/East Coast space rock vocabulary into the Seattle context. The synthesis (Cameron's Soundgarden-forged power + McBain's space rock approach) is distinctive and could not have been produced by either musician alone.
3. **The Hollywood Records anomaly is well-documented.** Disney subsidiary signing a heavy space rock duo from the Seattle underground is one of the more absurd consequences of the post-Nirvana major label expansion. The engine correctly identifies this as an anomaly rather than a natural fit.
4. **The Soundgarden-as-center analysis gives coherence to Cameron's multi-project career.** Temple of the Dog and Wellwater Conspiracy are both satellites of the central Soundgarden orbit. This organizational logic makes the career arc readable.

## Discoveries

1. **Hollywood Records' willingness to sign experimental acts during the mid-1990s was more extensive than commonly remembered.** The label's post-Nirvana Seattle-adjacent signings included acts that had essentially no commercial probability. This reflects either genuine A&R adventurism or institutional pressure to appear relevant to the alternative rock market -- probably both.
2. **Wellwater Conspiracy's Sub Pop period (2001, *The Scroll and Its Combinations*) connects the band to the engine's established underground network.** Love Battery (221) was Sub Pop. Wellwater joins at 226. Sub Pop as underground community appears across multiple degrees.
3. **The space rock genre's relationship to heavy rock is clarified by Wellwater Conspiracy.** Space rock is not ambient (E=1-3) and not aggressive (E=7-8) -- it is heavy but atmospheric, orbiting rather than driving. The E=6 rating is correct: present and assertive but organized around space and sustain rather than urgency.

## Pair Analysis: Wellwater Conspiracy + Cooper's Hawk

- **Energy:** E=6 + E=5 = E=11. Combined pair slightly above equilibrium.
- **Theme:** THE MIDDLE GROUND. Wellwater Conspiracy is the middle project in the current S36 sequence -- not the apex supergroup of Temple of the Dog (225), not the minimal wordless duo of Hovercraft (227). It is a substantial creative enterprise operating between those extremes. The Cooper's Hawk (SPS 226) is the middle accipiter -- between the apex Goshawk and the smallest Sharp-shin, and the one that has most successfully colonized the middle ground between wilderness and suburb. Both are defined by adaptability and middle-position utility.
