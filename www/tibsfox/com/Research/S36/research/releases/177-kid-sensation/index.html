The First Generation

Kid Sensation was there before the infrastructure existed. Part of the posse on “Posse on Broadway.” One of the first Seattle MCs to release a full-length album. Nastymix Records. Central District. “Back to Boom.” The earliest commercial Seattle hip-hop.

Nastymix Records & the Posse

Nastymix Records was founded by Sir Mix-a-Lot and Ed Locke in 1985 — the first significant hip-hop label in the Pacific Northwest. Kid Sensation was a flagship artist on the label, appearing on Mix-a-Lot’s defining track “Posse on Broadway” (1988). His debut album Rollin’ with Number One (1990) demonstrated that Seattle hip-hop could sustain full-length projects. The Nastymix sound: bass-heavy, funk-influenced, synthesizer-driven.

Central District as Hip-Hop Origin

Foundational — Geography

The Central District was the heart of Black Seattle and the origin point of Seattle hip-hop. Kid Sensation documented Central District life before anyone outside the region was listening.
