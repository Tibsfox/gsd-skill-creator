The Garage Tradition Continues

The engine documented the original Northwest Sound at degrees 241-244: The Sonics, The Wailers, The Frantics, The Kingsmen — the 1963-1965 Pacific Northwest garage rock moment. Naked Giants arrive at degree 251 as the same tradition's current practitioners. The format has evolved: three-piece instead of five, Sub Pop instead of Etiquette Records, psychedelic flourishes that the original Northwest bands didn't have. But the essential approach — loud guitars, insistent rhythm, short songs, physical energy — is the same.

"Sub Pop in 2013 is the institutional equivalent of Etiquette Records in 1964 — the label that gives Pacific Northwest garage rock bands their platform. The institution changes; the tradition persists."

Three Voices, Three Instruments, One Sound

Grant Mullen (guitar/vocals), Gianni Aiello (bass/vocals), Henry LaVallee (drums/vocals) — all three sing, all three contribute to the ensemble without a dominant frontperson. This democratic structure creates a different kind of rock band than the vocalist-plus-backing-band model. The songs hold three textures and can produce three-part harmony when the arrangement calls for it.

The three-piece format strips the band to essentials: one guitar carries all harmonic weight, the bass carries melodic and harmonic context, the drums drive the physical energy. In a smaller band, every person's contribution is more exposed and more necessary. Naked Giants' three-piece is a deliberate choice, not a limitation.
