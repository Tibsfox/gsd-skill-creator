## What Worked

1. **The garage arc connection (241-244 → 251) is the entry's most useful structural element.** Seven degrees between the original Northwest Sound and its current practitioners. The arc is genuine, not engineered.
2. **The E=7 streak is now at three degrees.** 249, 250, 251. The contemporary Seattle rock scene's energy level is consistent. This pattern is worth tracking through degree 252.
3. **The three-piece all-singing format is distinctive within the engine.** Most rock bands have a designated vocalist. Naked Giants' democratic structure is a specific approach that produces a specific sound.
4. **Sub Pop continuity across 50+ years of garage rock.** Etiquette/Wand (1963-1965) to Sub Pop (2013-present): the institutional evolution of PNW garage rock. The label names change; the function persists.

## Discoveries

1. **The psychedelic and surf layers are what distinguish Naked Giants from straight revivalism.** A band that only played Northwest Sound-style garage in 2013 would be a tribute act. The incorporation of psychedelic texture and surf vocabulary makes them contemporary practitioners rather than recreationists.
2. **The three-piece all-singing format requires more from each member than a larger band.** No bass player hiding in the back, no second guitarist covering for the lead. Every decision is visible. This creates accountability that shows in the band's consistency.

## Pair Analysis: Naked Giants + Pacific Rain on Water Surface

- **Energy:** E=7 + E=3 = E=10. Equilibrium reached.
- **Theme:** ATTACK AND DIFFUSION. Naked Giants' garage rock is about attack: the initial impact of the guitar on the pickup, the first hit of the kick drum, the sharp onset of each note. Pacific rain on Puget Sound is about diffusion: millions of individual water drops, each with its own impact, the overall effect a continuous white noise that has no sharp onset and no sharp attack. The two sounds are structural opposites. The pairing holds the most kinetic and the most ambient, and they meet at E=10 equilibrium.
