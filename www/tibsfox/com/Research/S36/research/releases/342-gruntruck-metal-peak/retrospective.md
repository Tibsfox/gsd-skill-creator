## What Worked

1. **The Ben McMillan documentation is handled correctly.** Born 1966, died January 20 2008, age 41, complications from type 1 diabetes. Documented once, factually, with respect. The music -- Push (1992) and Inside Yours (1993) -- is the primary subject. The biographical facts are context, not spectacle.
2. **The Skin Yard lineage connection (degree 338 to degree 342) is the engine's most direct vocalist continuity.** McMillan was Skin Yard's vocalist, then Gruntruck's vocalist. Four degrees separate the two entries. The same voice in two different projects at the same energy level (E=8 for both).
3. **The Pearl Jam / Gruntruck contrast (consecutive Capitol Hill E=8 entries) is analytically valuable.** Both at E=8, same neighborhood, same era. Pearl Jam: 35+ years, 13 million debuts, still recording. Gruntruck: 4 years, two albums, dissolved 1994. The engine documents both without privileging commercial scale over historical significance. The energy calibration is not a chart.
4. **The Roadrunner Records placement is correct and illuminating.** Gruntruck is more accurately metal than grunge by the standards of the Roadrunner roster. The label placement identifies the band's genre position more precisely than the grunge umbrella.
5. **E=8 holding for two consecutive degrees (341, 342) before rising is the correct ramp structure.** The base step is two degrees wide, not one. The escalation requires a platform.

## Discoveries

1. **The Swallow solo project (mid-1990s) demonstrates McMillan's musical range beyond the Gruntruck context.** Swallow moved toward gothic and atmospheric territory -- a deliberate departure from the metal of Gruntruck. The range documented in the Gruntruck/Swallow arc is wider than the metal peak would suggest.
2. **Inside Yours (1993) is retrospectively the stronger of the two albums in the metal press assessment.** The wider dynamic range and compositional variation of the second album outlast the more direct impact of Push. The pattern -- second album as fuller creative statement -- recurs across the engine's heavy catalog (compare to Melvins: Ozma to Bullhead; Blood Brothers: early records to Crimes).
3. **The 1990-1991 independent/live period built the audience that the Roadrunner deal inherited.** Gruntruck's reputation was built on Capitol Hill stages before the studio catalog existed. The live identity preceded and enabled the label identity.
