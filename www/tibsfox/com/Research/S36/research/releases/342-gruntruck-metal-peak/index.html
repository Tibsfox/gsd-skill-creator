Ben McMillan — 1966–2008

Ben McMillan died January 20, 2008, in Seattle, Washington. He was 41 years old. Cause of death: complications from type 1 diabetes. He was the vocalist for Skin Yard (1986–1992), Gruntruck (1990–1994), and the solo project Swallow. His voice was one of the most distinctive instruments in the Seattle metal/grunge underground. The engine documents this biographical fact once, completely, with respect. The music is the primary subject.

The Skin Yard Lineage

Gruntruck does not appear in isolation. It is the continuation of a specific thread: the Capitol Hill metal lineage that runs from Skin Yard through Gruntruck. Ben McMillan was Skin Yard’s vocalist from 1986, through the Deep Six compilation (C/Z Records, 1986), through the band’s most significant albums (Hallowed Ground 1988, 1000 Smiling Knuckles 1991). The engine documented Skin Yard at degree 338. Gruntruck at degree 342 is the same voice, the same Capitol Hill geography, and a harder sonic edge.

Scott McCcloud’s guitar is the element that distinguishes Gruntruck from the Skin Yard template. Where Skin Yard had Jack Endino’s more dissonant, atmospheric guitar approach, McCcloud brings a precision-metal riffing style: palm-muted patterns with specific rhythmic architectures that owe more to Roadrunner’s 1992 roster (Sepultura, Fear Factory) than to the Sub Pop underground. The collision between McCcloud’s metal precision and McMillan’s Seattle underground vocal sensibility is Gruntruck’s defining sound.

"Metal in Seattle in 1992 was not arena metal. It was something smaller and angrier and less interested in spectacle than the mainstream metal of the decade. Gruntruck was that."

The Capitol Hill Contrast: Pearl Jam and Gruntruck

Degrees 341 (Pearl Jam) and 342 (Gruntruck) are consecutive Capitol Hill entries at E=8 in the same era. The contrast is stark:

Pearl Jam: formed 1990, still active in 2026, 35+ years of continuous operation, 13 million US copies of the debut, Epic Records, globally recognized. Gruntruck: formed 1990, dissolved 1994, four years, two albums, Roadrunner Records, recognized within the metal underground, the engine of Ben McMillan’s most fully realized heavy project.

The engine documents both at E=8. The energy calibration does not privilege commercial scale over historical significance. Gruntruck’s two albums are at E=8 because the music is E=8, not because the band’s commercial footprint matched Pearl Jam’s. The engine is not a chart.

Discography

Push (1992)

Roadrunner Records

The debut. Roadrunner’s metal production clarity applied to Seattle underground material. Tribe, Above Me. McCcloud’s riff architecture at its most immediate. McMillan’s voice at full projection. The record that made the Roadrunner placement make sense.

Inside Yours (1993)

Roadrunner Records

The follow-up. More compositionally varied than Push, with wider dynamic range. Songs breathe before returning to the heavy sections. Retrospectively assessed as the stronger of the two records within the metal press. The band’s most complete creative statement.

1990–1991 Independent Period

Capitol Hill live era

Before Roadrunner, Gruntruck built their reputation on Capitol Hill stages. The live reputation preceded the studio work. The transition from Skin Yard’s existing audience to a Roadrunner metal audience was navigated through this pre-label touring period.
