## THE SKIN YARD LINEAGE -- WHERE GRUNTRUCK CAME FROM

### The Genealogy from Skin Yard to Gruntruck

Gruntruck emerges directly from Skin Yard (degree 338):

- **Skin Yard (1985-1993).** Skin Yard was one of the founding bands of the Seattle metal/grunge underground -- appearing on the Deep Six compilation (1986, C/Z Records) alongside the Melvins, Green River, and three other defining bands. Jack Endino played guitar in Skin Yard, then left the band to become the definitive engineer of the early Sub Pop sound (producing Nirvana's *Bleach*, Mudhoney's debut, and scores of other foundational records). Skin Yard's catalog -- particularly *Hallowed Ground* (1988) and *1000 Smiling Knuckles* (1991) -- represents one of the most consistently heavy and compositionally sophisticated bodies of work in the Seattle underground.
- **Ben McMillan's role in Skin Yard.** Ben McMillan was the vocalist for Skin Yard from 1986 through their dissolution. His voice -- a powerful, distinctive instrument with a lower center of gravity than the tenor scream common in the era -- was one of Skin Yard's defining features. McMillan left Skin Yard to form Gruntruck in 1990, taking the band in a more explicitly metal direction.
- **Scott McCcloud in Gruntruck.** Scott McCcloud (guitarist) brought Gruntruck's most technically distinctive element: a guitar approach that combined the metal precision of the Roadrunner Records catalog with the Seattle underground's comfort with dissonance and noise. McCcloud's riffs are architecturally complex -- they do not resolve in predictable patterns, and the rhythm section (Tim Paul on bass, William Flatt on drums) provides the foundation for this complexity.
- **The Capitol Hill context.** Gruntruck operated from Capitol Hill, the same neighborhood as Pearl Jam (degree 341). Two E=8 bands from the same neighborhood in consecutive degrees: Pearl Jam's arena rock lineage and Gruntruck's metal lineage. The Capitol Hill scene at E=8 contains multitudes.

## BEN McMILLAN -- WITH RESPECT

### A Careful and Complete Accounting

Ben McMillan (born 1966, died January 20, 2008, Seattle) is the primary biographical subject of this entry. The engine documents with respect:

- **The biographical facts.** Ben McMillan was born in 1966. He died on January 20, 2008, in Seattle, Washington. He was 41 years old. The cause of death was complications from type 1 diabetes. He had been diagnosed with diabetes and had struggled with managing the condition, compounded by other health challenges during his lifetime.
- **The musical career summary.** McMillan was the vocalist for Skin Yard (1986-1992), then Gruntruck (1990-1994), then his solo project Swallow (mid-1990s). His voice was one of the most distinctive instruments in the Seattle metal/grunge underground: not the tenor scream of the most extreme metal, not the melodic baritone of the grunge mainstream, but a powerful mid-range instrument with considerable emotional weight. He could project at full volume without losing pitch control.
- **The Swallow project.** After Gruntruck's dissolution, McMillan formed Swallow, a more gothic and atmospheric project that moved away from the heavy metal context of Gruntruck into a darker, more melodic territory. The Swallow work demonstrates the range of McMillan's musical imagination beyond what Gruntruck's metal context accommodated.

The engine documents his death's biographical facts (date, cause, age) and keeps the primary focus on the music. This is the approach taken for all sensitive biographical subjects in the engine.
- **The music's standing.** *Push* (1992) and *Inside Yours* (1993) are documents of a band at the metal peak of the grunge era. They stand on their own as creative achievements independent of the biographical circumstances surrounding them.

## THE SOUND -- METAL/GRUNGE AT E=8

### What Gruntruck Sounds Like

Gruntruck's music at its peak:

- **The genre position.** Gruntruck occupies a specific position in the Seattle metal/grunge spectrum: heavier than Pearl Jam or Alice in Chains in texture, more musically organized than the noise/sludge end (Melvins, Mudhoney's heaviest material). Their sound is closer to the heavy end of Roadrunner Records' roster (which at the time also included Sepultura, Fear Factory, and Obituary) than to the Sub Pop aesthetic. The Roadrunner signing was appropriate: Gruntruck was genuinely metal in a way that most Seattle grunge was not.
- **The guitar approach.** Scott McCcloud's guitar combines heavy palm-muted riffing (a metal technique) with the more open, feedback-tolerant approach of the Seattle underground. The riffs are not the pure-metal riffing of Metallica or Pantera -- they have a rhythmic openness that allows the songs to breathe at Seattle tempos rather than at pure-metal velocity. The result is a guitar sound that is recognizably from the same tradition as Skin Yard while moving in a more explicitly metal direction.
- **The dynamics.** Gruntruck uses dynamic contrast in the grunge tradition -- quiet verses, heavy choruses -- but the heavy parts are heavier than most grunge, and the quiet parts retain a tension that does not fully release. This is a formal characteristic: the songs do not resolve harmonically or dynamically in satisfying conventional ways. The unresolved tension is the point.
- **The production.** *Push* (1992, Roadrunner) was produced with a clarity that served the guitar's technical precision. The metal production approach on a Seattle band creates a useful collision: the Seattle underground's preference for rawness meets the metal tradition's preference for precision.

## DISCOGRAPHY

### Push and Inside Yours

Gruntruck's complete catalog is short by design:

- ***Push* (1992, Roadrunner Records).** The debut. Produced with Roadrunner's characteristic metal precision. Tracks like "Tribe" and "Above Me" demonstrate the band at their most immediately effective: heavy riffs, McMillan's voice at full commitment, McCcloud's guitar in sharp relief. The album established Gruntruck as one of the heavier entries in the Seattle catalog and made a case for their Roadrunner placement over a Sub Pop alignment.
- ***Inside Yours* (1993, Roadrunner Records).** The follow-up. More compositionally varied than *Push*, with a wider dynamic range and more willingness to let songs breathe before returning to the heavy sections. The production is slightly more spacious. The album was received well within the metal community; it remains the better of the two albums in retrospective assessment by the metal press.
- **The 1990-1991 period.** Before the Roadrunner deal, Gruntruck released independent material and played Capitol Hill venues extensively. The live reputation preceded the studio work. The transition from the Skin Yard audience (who knew McMillan's voice) to a Roadrunner audience (who encountered Gruntruck on its own terms) was one of the challenges of the catalog's trajectory.

(Sources: Push 1992 Roadrunner; Tribe Above Me heavy tracks; Inside Yours 1993 Roadrunner; more compositionally varied than Push; 1990-1991 pre-Roadrunner independent releases; Capitol Hill live reputation)
