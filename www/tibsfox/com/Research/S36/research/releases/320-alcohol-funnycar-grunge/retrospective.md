## What Worked

1. **The E=7-from-velocity mechanism completes the four-mode E=7 analysis across degrees 317-320.** The post-summit cooldown has held at E=7 for four consecutive degrees, each through a distinct mechanism: grief (Temple), commercial accessibility (Candlebox), emotional precision (Hammerbox), punk velocity (Alcohol Funnycar). This is the engine's most complete analysis of what a single energy level can mean across different aesthetic approaches. The cooldown is not monotone; it explores the full range of E=7.
2. **Empty Records is correctly positioned as the underground punk wing of the Seattle indie ecosystem.** Fred Canby's label is the engine's smallest label context in the current sequence -- smaller than Sub Pop, smaller than the independents that had Sub Pop-scale commercial aspirations. The Empty aesthetic (fast, raw, cheap, DIY) is the punk end of the spectrum. The label's presence in the engine documents the underground circuit that ran parallel to the commercially documented grunge era.
3. **The Dave Dederer trajectory (Empty Records -> PUSA/Maverick) is the engine's most dramatic underground-to-mainstream before-and-after.** From Empty Records punk-grunge to a 1M+ debut on Madonna's label. The punk velocity developed in Alcohol Funnycar is audible in PUSA's economy of arrangement -- shorter, faster, more direct than standard alternative rock. The before-and-after is stark; the thread between them is the punk economy of form.
4. **The grunge spectrum is now complete.** The engine has documented from metal end (Melvins, degree 308, E=8) through commercial center (Soundgarden, Pearl Jam, AIC) through Sub Pop punk (Mudhoney) to underground punk-grunge (Alcohol Funnycar, degree 320, E=7). The full spectrum from sludge metal to underground punk is now in the record.

## Discoveries

1. **The Empty Records / PUSA / Maverick thread connects through Dave Dederer and Jason Finn simultaneously.** Dederer: Alcohol Funnycar (Empty, underground) -> PUSA (Maverick, 1M+). Finn: Love Battery (Sub Pop, psychedelic, degree 221) -> PUSA (Maverick, 1M+). Both pre-PUSA projects are in the engine. Both pre-PUSA labels (Empty and Sub Pop) are in the engine. The PUSA future entry will be the convergence point of both threads. The network of pre-PUSA connections is richer than a single-band analysis captures.
2. **"Punk end of grunge" is a useful classification that the engine had not formally documented before degree 320.** The spectrum analysis (metal end / commercial / Sub Pop / underground punk) is now complete. Alcohol Funnycar at the punk end confirms that the grunge spectrum is as wide as the classical continuum of post-punk to metal that it draws from -- 1990s Seattle grunge contains the full range.
