## Kill Rock Stars: Legendary Label Enters

### Olympia as Sixth I-5 Corridor Node

Degree 70 introduces Kill Rock Stars into the engine.

- **Situation:** Kill Rock Stars, founded 1991 in Olympia by Slim Moon. Home of Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney, Elliott Smith. First KRS artist in the engine. Olympia becomes the sixth I-5 corridor node.
- **Root Cause:** The engine's label geography expands with KRS -- the political independent label, representing artistic freedom and DIY ethics. KRS adds a philosophical dimension to the label ecology: Sub Pop (commercial tastemaker), Barsuk (band ecosystem), Tender Loving Empire (community retail), KRS (political independence). Olympia enters as a geographic and ideological anchor.
- **Recommendation:** Document KRS as a new label entry. Note Olympia as sixth I-5 node. Track whether future KRS artists appear. Note the political independent label as a distinct category from commercial (Sub Pop) and community (TLE) labels.

## The Corridor Wanderer: Multi-Nodal Identity

### Portland, Seattle, Olympia as Connected Ecosystem

Degree 70 introduces the corridor wanderer pattern.

- **Situation:** Horse Feathers has Portland origin, Seattle presence, Olympia label. Three I-5 corridor nodes contributing to the band's identity. First genuinely multi-nodal artist in the engine.
- **Root Cause:** The corridor wanderer treats the I-5 corridor as a single ecosystem rather than a series of discrete cities. Previous corridor artists (Y La Bamba, Laura Gibson) had primary cities with secondary connections. Horse Feathers' identity is genuinely distributed across three nodes.
- **Recommendation:** Document the corridor wanderer as a new artist-city pattern. Note the distinction from working musician (one city), visiting artist (institutional), and Export (leaves). Track whether future degrees include other corridor wanderers.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Corridor wanderer pattern established. 71st artist-city pattern. First multi-nodal identity. Track at future degrees.

## First Prominent Violin and Banjo

### Engine Instrumental Palette Expands

Degree 70 introduces violin and banjo as primary instrumental voices.

- **Situation:** Through sixty-nine degrees, the engine's instrumental palette has been overwhelmingly guitar-centric. Horse Feathers brings violin and banjo as lead/structural voices, not ornamental layers. Three string instruments braiding: violin, banjo, guitar.
- **Root Cause:** The engine has implicitly been a study of guitar-based music. Horse Feathers introduces a string-ensemble model where the guitar is one voice among equals. The fiddle line carries melodies and emotional atmosphere. The banjo adds brightness and rhythmic counterpoint.
- **Recommendation:** Document the expansion of the instrumental palette. Note violin and banjo as first prominent non-guitar string instruments. Track whether future degrees include other non-guitar-centric artists.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Violin and banjo enter as primary voices. Engine instrumental palette expanded. Track at future degrees.

## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **Kill Rock Stars concept clearly established.** The label's significance -- Olympia geography, political independence, riot grrrl/punk origins, Elliott Smith lineage -- provides rich structural material. The distinction from Sub Pop (commercial tastemaker), Barsuk (band ecosystem), and TLE (community retail) is analytically productive.

2. **Corridor wanderer pattern well-articulated.** The distinction from working musician (one city), visiting artist (institutional), and Export (leaves) is clear. Horse Feathers as the first genuinely multi-nodal artist provides a clean pattern entry.

3. **The fiddle line acoustic element grounded in audible characteristics.** The fiddle line is described through specific instrumental behavior -- the violin as lead voice, the counterpoint with hushed vocals, the emotional continuity across spaces between words. Audible on *Thistled Spring* and *Broken Birthday*.

4. **Indie Folk architectural contrast with Fleet Foxes productive.** The choir versus chamber ensemble distinction (wall of voices vs. woven strings) reveals the genre's range. Same genre label, opposite sonic realities.

5. **Multi-territorial acknowledgment documented.** The corridor wanderer's multi-nodal identity naturally produces a multi-territorial footprint. Squaxin Island/Nisqually added through institutional geography. Seven territories mapped.

6. **Cross-references well-established.** Five cross-references: Y La Bamba (61, corridor), Beam (68, hushed folk), Fleet Foxes (53, indie folk), Sykes (64, folk atmospheric), Gibson (57, Portland folk). All analytically productive.

7. **Energy calibration accurate.** E=3 correctly captures the chamber ensemble energy -- more textured than solo E=2 but still intimate. The distinction from the back-to-back E=2 (68-69) is clear.

8. **KRS context strengthens the entry.** Placing Horse Feathers within the KRS catalog (Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney, Elliott Smith) contextualizes the band's aesthetic and philosophical position.

### Source Quality

9. **KRS catalog provides institutional context.** The label's history, roster, and Olympia geography are well-documented and verifiable.

10. **Discography documented.** Four key albums: *House with No Home* (2006), *Thistled Spring* (2008), *Appreciation* (2012), *Broken Birthday* (2016). Label progression from self-released to KRS documented.
