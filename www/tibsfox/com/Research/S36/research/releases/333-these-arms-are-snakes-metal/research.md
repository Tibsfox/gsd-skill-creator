## THESE ARMS ARE SNAKES -- THE BAND

### Personnel and the Collective Architecture

The core lineup that defined the band's seven-year run:

- **Steve Snere (vocals).** Snere's vocal approach is the band's most distinctive surface feature. He operates across a range that includes melodic passages, spoken-word intensity, and screamed delivery -- all within the same song, sometimes within the same verse. The flexibility is not stylistic inconsistency; it is a compositional tool, using vocal register shifts to mark the music's architectural transitions. His delivery is literate and emotionally complex -- the lyrics are not blunt-force heavy metal statements but genuine literary engagements with anxiety, alienation, and the difficulty of navigating an incomprehensible world.
- **Brian Cook (bass).** Brian Cook is the band's most significant biographical connection point. Cook was the bassist of Botch (Tacoma, Hydra Head Records) -- the immediately preceding degree in the engine, S36-332. When Botch dissolved in 2002, Cook joined These Arms Are Snakes as bassist, bringing the Botch mathcore tradition's rhythmic precision and compositional rigor into the band's post-metal framework. Cook is also a founding member of Russian Circles, making him one of the most multiply-connected musicians in the PNW metal tradition.
- **Ryan Frederiksen (guitar).** Frederiksen's guitar work defines the band's post-metal architecture. He uses standard and extended tunings, processed tones, and both clean and fully distorted sounds -- often transitioning between them within a single song. The guitar is not simply a riff-delivery vehicle; it is a textural and structural tool, responsible for the band's characteristic density and atmospheric weight.
- **Chris Common (drums).** Common's drumming is precise and powerful -- he holds the rhythmic complexity together through the band's frequent dynamic shifts and unusual song structures. His background spans multiple styles, and his ability to modulate from quiet to crushing supports the band's full dynamic range.

(Sources: Steve Snere vocals; vocal range melodic to spoken to screamed; literate lyrics; Brian Cook bass; Botch dissolution 2002; Cook joins These Arms Are Snakes; Russian Circles founding member; Ryan Frederiksen guitar; extended tunings and processing; Chris Common drums; dynamic modulation)

## THE BRIAN COOK THROUGHLINE -- BOTCH TO SNAKES TO RUSSIAN CIRCLES

### The Most Connected Bassist in the PNW Metal Tradition

Brian Cook's trajectory is one of the engine's great cross-reference stories:

- **Botch (1993-2002, Tacoma).** Cook was the bassist of Botch for the band's entire later active period. His bass playing in Botch -- metric precision, compositional participation, willingness to syncopate against the guitar rhythm -- defined the mathcore band's bottom end. *We Are the Romans* (1999) is the document; Cook's bass is throughout it.
- **These Arms Are Snakes (2003-2009, Seattle/Capitol Hill).** After Botch, Cook brought the mathcore tradition's compositional rigor into a more post-metal framework. The transition from Botch to Snakes is instructive: both bands play at extreme intensity, but Snakes' music is more textural and atmospheric, less purely metric. Cook's bass in Snakes serves a different function than in Botch -- less rhythmic structural anchor, more textural foundation for the band's atmospheric architecture.
- **Russian Circles (2005-present, Chicago).** Cook co-founded Russian Circles with guitarist Mike Sullivan and drummer Dave Turncrantz in Chicago in 2005, while still active with These Arms Are Snakes. Russian Circles became one of the defining post-metal instrumental bands of the 2000s and 2010s, releasing records on Sargent House (the same label as Helms Alee, S36-331). The three-way connection -- Botch (332) via Cook to Snakes (333) via Cook to Russian Circles to Sargent House (331's Helms Alee's label) -- is the metal arc's most elegant documentary thread.
- **The significance.** Cook's presence connects three consecutive degrees in the metal arc (331 via label, 332 via direct membership, 333 via direct membership) into a single biographical narrative. One musician's career traces the post-metal tradition from its foundations (Botch, mathcore) through its atmospheric development (Snakes, post-metal) and into its instrumental sophistication (Russian Circles, post-metal instrumental).

## THE SOUND -- POST-METAL ARCHITECTURE

### What These Arms Are Snakes Built

Post-metal as practiced by These Arms Are Snakes:

- **Post-metal definition.** Post-metal is the long-form, textural, atmospheric development of metal's heaviness -- less concerned with riff velocity than with accumulated weight, less interested in aggression per se than in the sustained pressure that precedes and follows aggression. The genre's defining bands (Neurosis, Isis, Pelican, Cult of Luna) share a tendency toward long song structures (8-15 minutes or more), dynamic range exploited to its extremes (from near-silence to full overwhelming distortion), and a compositional logic more akin to modern classical or ambient music than to conventional rock song structure.
- **These Arms Are Snakes' version.** Snakes operate within the post-metal tradition but with a more aggressive and urgent quality than many of its practitioners. The band maintains the architectural ambition -- the long-form structures, the extreme dynamic range, the textural processing -- but integrates a hardcore urgency that prevents the music from becoming purely atmospheric. The combination is specific to Capitol Hill in 2003-2009: too heavy for the indie underground, too textured for the metal scene, occupying the space between categories.
- **The dark complex texture.** The band's characteristic sound involves layered guitar processing, bass-heavy bottom end, and Snere's dynamic vocal approach creating a dense but internally differentiated texture. The music is dark -- not in the cartoonish gothic sense, but in the more meaningful sense of operating in registers that feel genuinely heavy and difficult. The weight is earned.
- **Song structure.** These Arms Are Snakes frequently use songs that build from quiet or moderate passages into maximum distortion, then recede, then build again -- the standard post-metal architecture, but executed with enough melodic and structural intelligence that the builds feel inevitable rather than formulaic. The band also uses sections of relative stasis -- music that rests at medium intensity rather than always moving toward the next climax -- that create genuine tension through stillness.
- **E=8 calibration.** E=8 is the second-highest energy in the engine, below the Melvins' E=9 (at degree 334, the immediately following entry). The calibration reflects the band's post-metal architecture: not purely velocity-based aggression (The Accused, 329, is also E=9 through speed), but accumulated textural weight at the highest sustainable intensity. E=8 is the maximum for a band whose heaviness is as much about architecture as about impact.

## DISCOGRAPHY -- THE THREE RECORDS

### A Focused Seven-Year Catalog

These Arms Are Snakes released three full-length albums and several EPs during their active period:

- ***Oxeneers or the Lion Sleeps When Its Antelope Go Home* (2004, Initial Records).** The debut album. Introduces the band's core approach: aggressive post-metal with literate lyrics, dynamic range between quiet and full distortion, Brian Cook's precise bass work. The record establishes the Capitol Hill post-metal vocabulary that the band would develop across their catalog. Title is characteristically literary -- an unusual noun-phrase construction that signals the band's engagement with language as a compositional element.
- ***Easter* (2006, Suicide Squeeze Records).** The Suicide Squeeze debut and the band's most critically recognized record. *Easter* refines the debut's approach into something more compositionally coherent -- the dynamic shifts are more deliberate, the atmospheric passages are longer and more developed, and Snere's vocals are integrated more fully into the overall texture. The production is clearer, allowing the layered guitar and bass work to be audible simultaneously. Widely considered the band's definitive statement.
- ***Tail Swallower and Dove* (2008, Suicide Squeeze Records).** The final studio album before the band's dissolution in 2009. More experimental and varied than the previous two records, with greater dynamic range and more extended quiet passages. Some listeners found this more demanding; others consider it the band's most fully realized work. The title references the Ouroboros symbol (the snake eating its own tail) -- a characteristically literary choice that frames the record's themes of cycles, returns, and endings. In retrospect, an appropriate conclusion.
- **Dissolution (2009).** These Arms Are Snakes dissolved in 2009 without a public announcement of the reasons. The dissolution was clean -- no acrimony documented publicly. Brian Cook continued with Russian Circles; other members pursued various projects. The band left three full-length records and a reputation as one of Capitol Hill's most architecturally serious heavy bands.

(Sources: Oxeneers or the Lion Sleeps When Its Antelope Go Home 2004 Initial Records; Easter 2006 Suicide Squeeze; Tail Swallower and Dove 2008 Suicide Squeeze; Ouroboros title reference; dissolution 2009; catalog: three albums plus EPs; Cook continues Russian Circles)

## LEGACY -- THE ARCHITECTURE THAT REMAINED

### Post-Metal Capitol Hill's Most Serious Band

- **Critical reception.** These Arms Are Snakes were critically recognized during their active period, particularly by music press covering extreme and independent music. *Easter* received substantial attention as a serious post-metal record that distinguished itself through its literary quality and architectural ambition. The band was not commercially significant -- they never had a mainstream moment -- but within the underground, they were respected as one of the more serious heavy bands of their era.
- **The Suicide Squeeze anomaly.** Suicide Squeeze is not typically associated with heavy music; their catalog skews toward indie rock, post-rock, and experimental music. These Arms Are Snakes are one of the heaviest bands in the label's history. The fact that the label supported them is part of Capitol Hill's broader character: the neighborhood contains the full range from quiet introspection to maximum intensity, and the label reflects that range.
- **The connection density.** Few bands in the engine are connected to as many other entries as These Arms Are Snakes, via Brian Cook: directly to Botch (332), indirectly to Russian Circles and the Sargent House ecosystem (connecting to Helms Alee, 331). The Cook throughline makes degree 333 one of the engine's most deeply cross-referenced entries.
- **The Metal Summit opening.** Degree 333 opens the four-degree Metal Summit with an E=8 entry that establishes architectural complexity as the first principle. The Summit then escalates through Melvins' sludge primogeniture, Soundgarden's heavy metal evolution, and Alice in Chains' grunge-metal synthesis -- each a different approach to maximum intensity. These Arms Are Snakes set the architectural frame for everything that follows.
