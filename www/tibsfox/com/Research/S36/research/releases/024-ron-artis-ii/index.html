Biography & The Motown Lineage

Ron Artis II was born on August 31, 1986 on the mainland United States. His father Ron Artis Sr. was a Motown session player — keyboards on Michael Jackson’s Thriller. His mother Victoria toured as a vocalist with Lena Horne. When Ron II was approximately four, the family moved to Oahu, Hawaii, settling on the North Shore in Haleiwa.

The second eldest of eleven children — Stephanie, Ron II, Victor, Stevon, PraiseJesus, Spirit, Martis, Kailua, American, Thunderstorm, and Artis — Ron grew up in the Ron Artis Family Band, learning piano by age four and receiving a guitar at thirteen. The family operated Art...is, a gallery and music venue in Haleiwa that became a community institution.

Brother Thunderstorm Artis reached the finals of NBC’s The Voice Season 18 (2020), turning all four coaches’ chairs. Brother Stevon plays drums in Ron II’s band The Truth.

Ron II has performed and collaborated with Mick Fleetwood (Fleetwood Mac), Jack Johnson, Jake Shimabukuro, Booker T. Jones, Eric Krasno (Soulive/Lettuce), G Love, John Oates (Hall & Oates), and Joan Baez. Blues & Soul Magazine called his voice “a voice from heaven, a cross between Eric Bibb and Al Jarreau.”

In December 2023, following the devastating Maui wildfires, Ron II returned to Hawaii and donated over 200 instruments to children who had lost theirs — featured on ABC’s Good Morning America. His song “No Weight”, performed with daughter Ida Marie Artis, became the relief anthem.
