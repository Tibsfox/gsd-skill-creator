The Arc: Central District to the World Stage

September 7, 1942 — Seattle, Washington

Born Luther James Rabb. Son of a minister. Raised in Seattle’s Central District. Learns piano on grandmother’s instrument. Begins saxophone at age ten.

Late 1950s — The Velvetones

Plays tenor and baritone saxophone in The Velvetones alongside Jimi Hendrix (guitar), Pernell Alexander (guitar), Walter Jones (drums), Robert Green (piano). Teen dances at Yesler Terrace in the Central District. Hendrix’s first band.

Early 1960s — Garfield High School

Attends Garfield High School with Hendrix and Ronnie Hammon. Passes through The Stags, The Nite Sounds, and The Emergency Exit. Switches from saxophone to bass guitar.

1969 — Ballin’ Jack Founded

Co-founds Ballin’ Jack with Ronnie Hammon in Seattle. Horn-rock/jazz-rock fusion. Signed to Columbia Records with encouragement from Hendrix. Debut album (1970) charts at No. 180 Billboard. “Super Highway” reaches No. 93 Hot 100.

1970 — Hendrix Cry of Love Tour

Ballin’ Jack opens for Hendrix across the United States. Fillmore East, Fillmore West, large halls, outdoor festivals. Band relocates from Seattle to Los Angeles.

1973–1974 — Mercury Records

Ballin’ Jack releases Special Pride (1973) and Live And In Color (1974) on Mercury Records. Band disbands in 1974 due to poor sales.

1976 — Santana

Lead vocalist for Carlos Santana on European tour (Amigos Tour). Replaces Greg Walker. Arena-scale performances across Europe.

1979 — Joins War

Replaces B.B. Dickerson as bassist and vocalist for War. Completes album sessions. Plays on The Music Band 2 (MCA), Outlaw (RCA, 1982), Life (Is So Strange) (RCA, 1983).

1986 — Car Accident

Neck injury causes numbness in hands. Unable to play bass. Transitions to management and production work.

2002 — Stroke

Stroke paralyzes Rabb from the chest down. Four years of declining health.

January 22, 2006 — Los Angeles

Luther James Rabb dies at age 63. Buried at Forest Lawn Memorial Park (Hollywood Hills), Los Angeles, California.
