A Vessel for Storytelling

Damien Jurado is Damien Troy Jurado — born November 12, 1972 in Seattle, Washington. His childhood was itinerant: Houston, Texas, where he discovered punk rock and skateboarding around age ten; the Olympic Peninsula coast, a small timber town of 8,000 people where music arrived by mail order and fanzine; and finally Seattle, where the family settled in the fall of 1988. He met David Bazan (future leader of Pedro the Lion) in high school. They played in bands together — The Guilty, Linus, Coolidge — before each launched a solo career.

Before music, Jurado wanted to be a visual artist. He painted and drew from middle school until Sub Pop signed him at age 22. The visual training persists: his songs are scenes, not arguments. Each track is a portrait, a landscape, a room. He started making lo-fi folk cassettes on his own label, Casa Recordings (founded 1995 with Eben Haase), gaining a cult following that reached Jeremy Enigk of Sunny Day Real Estate through a recreational baseball team. Enigk passed the demos to Sub Pop. The rest is thirty years of quiet, relentless work.

“He doesn’t sing as much as he emotes, but when this young talent spins his altruistic tales of unrequited love lost and found, depression doesn’t sound any sweeter than this.” — Times Colonist, on Rehearsals for Departure

Selected Discography

Waters Ave S.

1997 · Sub Pop

Debut. Produced by Steve Fisk. 13 songs. “A tentative but thoroughly charming debut” (Trouser Press). Sub Pop’s shift from grunge to folk begins here.

Rehearsals for Departure

1999 · Sub Pop

Produced by Ken Stringfellow. “Uncanny reminders of the late Nick Drake” (The Guardian). The voice arrives in full.

Ghost of David

2000 · Sub Pop

Bleakest, most personal record. Found-sound collage elements from Postcards and Audio Letters bleed in. Darker, more alien.

Where Shall You Take Me?

2003 · Secretly Canadian

First Secretly Canadian release. Return to folk ballads. Begins a 15-year partnership with the Bloomington, Indiana label.

Saint Bartlett

2010 · Secretly Canadian

First Richard Swift production. The collaboration that transforms Jurado’s sound. Live-to-tape at National Freedom.

Maraqopa

2012 · Secretly Canadian

Maraqopa trilogy, part 1. Protagonist enters a visionary space. Car accident. Swift’s production breathes. “Expand and retract like a great beast’s breath.”

Brothers & Sisters of the Eternal Son

2014 · Secretly Canadian

Maraqopa trilogy, part 2. The car accident propels deeper into Maraqopa. Spiritual imagery. Psychedelic warmth.

Visions of Us on the Land

2016 · Secretly Canadian

Maraqopa trilogy, part 3. Silver Katharine alone in Maraqopa. The others depart on a spaceship. Cosmic folk.

In the Shape of a Storm

2019 · Secretly Canadian

Sparsest album. Recorded in two hours. Voice, guitar, Josh Gordon on Nashville-tuned guitar. Tribute to Swift. Archive of abandoned songs.

The Monster Who Hated Pennsylvania

2021 · Maraqopa Records

First release on his own label. Total independence declared. The cassette kid founds a record company.

Private Hospital

2025 · Maraqopa Records

22nd studio album. Released as a book with download code. Completes the Reggae Film Star pentalogy. Found sounds, minimal synths, piano vignettes.

The Richard Swift Partnership

The pivotal creative partnership of Jurado’s career began in 2010 with producer Richard Swift at National Freedom Studios in Cottage Grove, Oregon. Swift’s live-to-tape ethos and psychedelic sensibility transformed Jurado’s sound. Together they produced Saint Bartlett (2010), Maraqopa (2012), Brothers and Sisters of the Eternal Son (2014), Visions of Us on the Land (2016), and the covers album Other People’s Songs, Vol. 1 — recorded in a single weekend on a 4-track with one Coles 4038 ribbon microphone.

Richard Swift died in July 2018, at age 41, from complications of alcoholism. In the Shape of a Storm (2019) was described as Jurado’s tribute to Swift — stripped of all the production complexity they had built together, returning to voice and guitar alone. National Freedom Studios remains open, maintained by engineer Chris Colbert and Swift’s family.

Music Theory Contributions

The Murmur

Advanced

46th acoustic element. The sonic phenomenon produced when a voice operates at the threshold between speech and song, so quiet the listener must lean in. The murmur is the acoustic signature of hushed folk singing — the voice confiding inward toward a single listener rather than projecting outward into a room. First intimacy-based acoustic element. Jurado’s vocal delivery is the murmur in its purest form.

The Quiet Lifer

Advanced

46th artist-city pattern. An artist who inhabits a city for decades without seeking the spotlight, building a body of work so large it becomes inseparable from the city’s identity, yet who remains unknown to casual inhabitants. 33 years in Seattle. 22+ albums. Every venue from bars to cathedrals. The musician’s musician.

Blue Note: The Held Breath

Intermediate

45th blue note deployment. The blue note that exists in the space between notes — the pause before a vocal line resolves, the silence where a more conventional singer would fill with sound. First silence-based blue note. The ache of the absent note. The quietest blue note in the engine.

Genre Stage 27: Folk

Advanced

First folk-primary genre stage. After jazz, soul, funk, R&B, blues, neo-soul, gospel, rock, and electronic, the engine strips down to its most elemental form: one person, one guitar, one story. Electronic/R&B (44) to Folk (45) — the engine’s second-largest single-degree genre leap.

E=2 Returns

Intermediate

14th E=2 deployment. Returns after a 31-degree absence (degree 13 to degree 45) — the longest gap for any energy level. E=2 retakes the outright lead. Distribution: 2:14:7:12:10:2. The engine’s quiet register re-asserts itself after 31 degrees of E=3+ energy.
