## The Two Hundred Forty-Second Degree: The Founding Generation

### Tacoma, 1959. "Tall Cool One." #36 Billboard Hot 100 -- the first Pacific Northwest rock band to chart nationally. The Fabulous Wailers. Not Bob Marley's Wailers. E=7. Buck Ormsby in two roles simultaneously -- musician and label infrastructure builder. The Northwest Sound template before The Sonics existed.

---

## What Worked

1. **The disambiguation is essential and should be front-loaded.** The Tacoma Wailers have been chronically underappreciated because their name was absorbed into Bob Marley's global fame. The entry establishes the distinction immediately: Tacoma, 1959, formed four years before the Jamaican group.
2. **"Tall Cool One" as the first nationally charting PNW rock single is historically significant.** The milestone gives the Wailers a permanent place in the engine beyond their intrinsic musical merit. They opened the national door.
3. **Buck Ormsby's dual role is the entry's most structurally important insight.** Musician and label executive simultaneously -- the person who was making the Northwest Sound and building the infrastructure to preserve it. This is a rare historical role.
4. **The E=7 placement accurately reflects the relationship to the Sonics.** The Wailers established the template; the Sonics pushed it to E=8. E=7 is one step below the maximum -- appropriate for the founding generation.

## Discoveries

1. **The Golden Crest Records New York connection is the commercial mechanism.** Without national distribution, "Tall Cool One" never reaches the Billboard Hot 100. The label deal is what separates the Wailers from dozens of equally talented Northwest bands that never left the region.
2. **"Out of Our Tree" (1966) is the Wailers' most underappreciated track.** A decade after their formation, approaching the Sonics' energy level, showing a band that evolved through the British Invasion rather than being destroyed by it.
3. **The Etiquette Records connection runs through Buck Ormsby.** The Wailers (Buck Ormsby bass) and the Sonics (Etiquette Records, Buck Ormsby) are connected through a single individual who built both the art and the infrastructure.
