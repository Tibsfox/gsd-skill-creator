NOT BOB MARLEY'S WAILERS. Tacoma, Washington, 1959. "The Fabulous Wailers." Four years before the Jamaican group formed. First PNW rock band to chart nationally. "Tall Cool One" — #36 Billboard Hot 100, 1959.
FIRST NATIONALLY CHARTING PNW ROCK BAND. "Tall Cool One" (1959) reached #36 on the Billboard Hot 100 — the first national chart success for Pacific Northwest rock music. The Wailers opened the door.

Tacoma's Founding Band

The Wailers formed in Tacoma in 1959 — one year before The Sonics, in the same city, in the same working-class milieu that produced the Northwest Sound. Where the Sonics would push to maximum extremity, the Wailers built a foundation: instrumental excellence, a band with actual chops, and the ambition to reach beyond the regional market.

"Tall Cool One" (1959) reached #36 on the Billboard Hot 100 — an independent label record from Tacoma, Washington competing in the national Top 40 one year before The Sonics had even formed.

The Wailers were fronted by Robin Roberts ("Rockin' Robin Roberts") in the early years, backed by Rich Dangel on guitar, Kent Morrill on organ, and Buck Ormsby on bass — the same Buck Ormsby who would found Etiquette Records and become the Northwest Sound's primary infrastructure builder. The dual role Ormsby played — musician and label executive — is one of the scene's most important structural facts.

Golden Crest Records & National Reach

The Wailers' national success was enabled by Golden Crest Records — a New York independent label with national distribution. Having a New York deal was the difference between regional obscurity and Billboard chart positions. This distribution infrastructure is what separates "Tall Cool One" from the dozens of Northwest Sound singles that never left the regional market. The Wailers had the sound, the ambition, and the label connection to break nationally.
