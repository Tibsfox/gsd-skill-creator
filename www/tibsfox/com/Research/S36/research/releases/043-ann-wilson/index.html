The Voice That Defined Rock

Ann Dustin Wilson is one of the most powerful rock vocalists in history. Born June 19, 1950, in San Diego, California, to a Marine Corps Major and a concert pianist, she grew up on military bases in Panama and Taiwan before her family settled in Bellevue, Washington, in the early 1960s. She graduated from Sammamish High School in 1968, sang in Seattle bar bands, and in 1970 answered a newspaper ad that led her to a band that would become Heart.

Heart sold over 35–50 million records worldwide, placed 29 singles on the Billboard Hot 100, scored two #1 hits (“These Dreams,” “Alone”), and took their self-titled 1985 album to #1 on the Billboard 200 (quintuple platinum). Wilson was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame (2013), received the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award (2023), and a Hollywood Walk of Fame star (2012).

“I usually hate it when people try to cover ‘Stairway to Heaven.’ But I like your version.” — Robert Plant to Ann Wilson, after Heart’s 2012 Kennedy Center Honors performance

Solo Career: Immortal, Fierce Bliss, Another Door

Wilson’s solo career launched with Hope & Glory (2007, Rounder/Zoe) — a covers album featuring Elton John, k.d. lang, and Alison Krauss. The Ann Wilson Thing! EPs (2015–2016) declared independence from Heart’s commercial machinery.

Immortal (2018, BMG) paid tribute to departed friends and influences: David Bowie, Amy Winehouse, Tom Petty, Chris Cornell (another Seattle voice). Fierce Bliss (2022, Silver Lining Music) was Wilson’s first solo album with original material, featuring Vince Gill, Kenny Wayne Shepherd, and Warren Haynes. Another Door (2023, LouJon) was the first album where Wilson was the sole lyricist on every track.

In July 2024, Wilson revealed a cancer diagnosis and underwent surgery and chemotherapy. Heart’s Royal Flush Tour resumed February 2025. She performed seated with a broken elbow. The voice did not diminish.
