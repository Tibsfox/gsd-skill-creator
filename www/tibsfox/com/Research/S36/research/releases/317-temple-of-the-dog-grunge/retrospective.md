## What Worked

1. **The Andrew Wood grief-as-catalyst frame is the entry's structural foundation.** The album is not analyzed as a product or a brand exercise. It is analyzed as a memorial record, and the memorial origin determines everything: the personnel (Cornell's friends), the songs (written in direct grief), the energy (E=7 -- the grief controls the throttle), the production schedule (completed before any of these people became commercially famous).
2. **The Eddie Vedder arrival story is one of the engine's most consequential personnel cross-references.** The cassette tape mechanism -- Jack Irons as conduit, Vedder recording in San Diego, Gossard playing it for Cornell, Cornell inviting Vedder to Seattle -- is a documented chain of events that produced Pearl Jam's existence. The Temple sessions are where Vedder met the future Pearl Jam lineup. This is not incidental; it is the structural origin of one of the top-selling bands in rock history.
3. **The Pearl Jam sonic prototype analysis is correct.** Same studio (London Bridge), same producer (Rick Parashar), same personnel except Vedder leading rather than backing. Ten is Temple's sonic sibling. This is documented in production records and personnel overlap.
4. **E=7 calibration is correct for a grief record.** Cornell at E=8 is Soundgarden: maximum force, maximum range, maximum attack. Cornell at E=7 is Temple: same instrument but restrained by grief's weight. "Say Hello 2 Heaven" is the clearest E=7 anchor in the album.

## Discoveries

1. **The delayed commercial recognition pattern applies to memorial records generally.** Temple of the Dog sold modestly in 1991 and accelerated to Platinum in 1993 via Pearl Jam's commercial breakthrough. The record's cultural significance preceded its commercial performance by two years. This is a pattern worth tracking: the most emotionally authentic records often require commercial context (the fame of their participants) to reach their audience.
2. **"Hunger Strike" contains one of the grunge era's most unlikely vocal pairings.** Cornell's upper register and Vedder's baritone are at opposite ends of the male vocal range. The duet structure (trading verses at different registers) was not planned as a commercial single; it emerged from the session as a natural division of material. The track's success is a demonstration that formal contrast can produce commercial result without commercial calculation.
