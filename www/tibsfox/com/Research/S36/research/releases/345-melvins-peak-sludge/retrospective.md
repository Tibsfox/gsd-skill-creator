## What Worked

1. **The Bullhead/Lysol-specific framing is essential.** Not "the Melvins at degree 345" but the Melvins during the 1991-1992 two-record corridor. The rest of the catalog (including the Atlantic period, Ipecac years, and ongoing work) is genuinely more varied and less extreme. The E=9 calibration lives here and only here.
2. **The riff-as-architecture framing for "Boris" is the right analytical lens.** One riff developed through 14 minutes via slight rhythmic and dynamic variation. Not a song in the conventional sense. Not a loop either. An architectural composition using a single musical idea. This framing correctly identifies what makes "Boris" extreme without reducing it to "just a long riff."
3. **The Lysol/drone metal ancestor analysis adds essential context.** Lysol's influence on Sunn O))), Earth, and Boris (the Japanese band named after the Bullhead track) is real and documentable. The Melvins are the genetic origin of a significant strand of subsequent heavy music.
4. **The acoustic pairing with Full Pacific Infrasound is the strongest thematic pairing in the summit.** Shared frequency range (guitar fundamentals 30-62 Hz overlapping Pacific infrasound upper range), shared geography (Aberdeen 20-30 miles from Grays Harbor), shared compositional philosophy (continuous, enormous, relentless, no concession). The engine's pairing logic reaches full coherence at degree 345.
5. **The "they made the others possible" framing is correct and important.** Cobain learned guitar from Buzz Osborne. Grohl was influenced by Crover. The entire Aberdeen-to-Seattle heavy tradition flows from the Melvins' example. The summit cannot be documented honestly without this acknowledgment.

## Discoveries

1. **The "Boris" influence vector is more significant than expected.** The track named a major Japanese drone metal band, which in turn influenced a generation of experimental heavy music globally. A single 14-minute track on a Boner Records release in 1991 has a traceable influence line running through Japanese experimental music to the present day.
2. **The Lysol/Atlantic signing timeline reveals an extraordinary industry moment.** Atlantic Records signed the Melvins after Lysol -- the most uncommercialized, most extreme, most deliberately anti-commercial record a major label had ever signed. The post-Nevermind feeding frenzy made Atlantic willing to sign a band whose most recent record was a 30-minute single-track drone monolith with "The Rose" embedded in the middle. The grunge boom was genuinely remarkable.
3. **Slow-tempo drumming as the rarest skill in heavy music is an important observation.** Most analyses of the Melvins focus on Buzz Osborne's guitar. Dale Crover's contribution -- generating genuine rhythmic authority at 50 BPM across 14 minutes -- is equally defining. Crover's influence on Grohl is direct and should be credited more consistently in accounts of Nirvana's sound.

## Pair Analysis: Melvins Peak Sludge + Full Pacific Infrasound

- **Energy:** E=9 + E=9 = E=18. The summit foundation.
- **Theme:** LOW-FREQUENCY MAXIMUM FROM SHARED GEOGRAPHY. The Melvins developed their sound in Aberdeen, Washington, 20-30 miles from the Pacific coast. The Pacific's infrasound output fills the Grays Harbor basin with low-frequency content in the 1-100 Hz range. The Melvins' guitar fundamentals in the *Bullhead* era occupy the 30-62 Hz range -- directly overlapping the upper infrasound and lower swell noise bands. The pairing is not imposed; it is acoustic. The compositional philosophy is also shared: both the storm and the band generate their maximum output through sheer scale, duration, and relentlessness. Neither accommodates the listener. Both are simply what they are at maximum amplitude.
