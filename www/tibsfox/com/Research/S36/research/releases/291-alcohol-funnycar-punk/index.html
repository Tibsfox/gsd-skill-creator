The Punk Wing of Seattle's Heavy Years

Alcohol Funnycar occupied a specific position in Seattle's early-to-mid 1990s music ecosystem — the punk wing of the heavy alternative scene. While Nirvana, Pearl Jam, and Soundgarden were moving toward major labels and arena rock, Alcohol Funnycar remained rooted in the punk tradition: faster, rawer, less commercially oriented. Their six-year run (1990-1997) covered the full arc of grunge's rise and commercial peak, and they operated throughout on Empty Records' independent punk infrastructure.

Empty Records — The Punk Infrastructure

Empty Records enters the engine for the first time through Alcohol Funnycar's return at degree 291. Founded in Seattle, Empty operated in the punk sphere of the city's independent label ecosystem — a distinct niche from Sub Pop's grunge catalog, Hydra Head's hardcore approach, or Suicide Squeeze's eclectic Capitol Hill output. Empty's catalog documented the punk wing of Seattle's heavy music scene during its most productive period: raw, community-rooted, not seeking commercial crossover.

The label ecology of Seattle in the 1990s was diverse. Sub Pop is the most famous name, but Estrus, C/Z, Empty, and others maintained the full range of the scene's output. Alcohol Funnycar operating on Empty rather than Sub Pop reflects a specific community position: the punk community inside the larger alternative scene.
