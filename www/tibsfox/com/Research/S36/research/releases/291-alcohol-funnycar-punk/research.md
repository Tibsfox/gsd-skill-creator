## THE PUNK CONTINUUM IN SEATTLE

### A Thread Through the Scene

Seattle's punk history is often overshadowed by grunge's commercial narrative, but the punk tradition ran continuously through the city's music scene:

- **Pre-grunge Seattle punk.** Seattle had a functioning punk scene before grunge's rise -- bands like The Fartz, The Fastbacks, The Lewd, and others in the early 1980s. This pre-history established the infrastructure (venues, record shops, zine culture) on which grunge built.
- **Punk alongside grunge.** In the early 1990s, punk and grunge coexisted in Seattle. They shared venues (the Central Tavern, the Vogue), audiences, and in some cases personnel. The distinction between a punk band and a grunge band was not always clear to participants; it was clearer to the music press that needed genre categories for marketing purposes.
- **Alcohol Funnycar's position.** Alcohol Funnycar represents the continuation of the punk thread through Seattle's heaviest years. While grunge was being packaged for national consumption, punk bands like Alcohol Funnycar maintained the community-rooted, independent infrastructure that punk requires. The band's six-year run (1990-1997) covers the full arc of grunge's rise and commercial peak.
- **Post-grunge punk persistence.** After grunge's commercial moment passed (1995-1996), punk's infrastructure in Seattle proved more durable. The independent label system, the small venues, the community networks -- these persisted after the major-label money withdrew. Alcohol Funnycar operated in this persistent infrastructure.
- **The Fastbacks connection.** The Fastbacks are the emblematic Seattle punk band -- active from 1979, never commercially successful, never stopping, releasing records across multiple decades. Alcohol Funnycar occupies a similar position in the 1990s: the punk band doing the work without commercial reward, sustained by the music and the community.

(Sources: Seattle punk history: The Fartz, Fastbacks, The Lewd; punk and grunge coexistence 1990-1994; punk infrastructure persistence post-grunge; The Fastbacks as template; Central Tavern, the Vogue as shared venues)
