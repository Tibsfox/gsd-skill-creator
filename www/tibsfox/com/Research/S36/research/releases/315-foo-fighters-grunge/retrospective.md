## What Worked

1. **The Nirvana genealogy documentation is the engine's primary purpose for this entry.** Grohl was not adjacent to Nirvana -- he was its drummer, the physical foundation of Nevermind's commercial power. The Foo Fighters formation in the weeks after Cobain's death is documented as a specific act: continuation through work as grief response. The genealogy is direct, not inferential.
2. **E=7 is correct and the calibration is defensible against E=8.** The distinction between Pearl Jam (E=8) and Foo Fighters (E=7) is the distinction between sonic density and melodic directness. Pearl Jam maintains E=8 through full-band collective commitment and Vedder's vocal weight. Foo Fighters achieves E=7 through melodic commitment and emotional directness without the harmonic/sonic density of the Summit bands. The one-unit difference is real.
3. **Everlong (1997) is the best single-song calibration anchor in the post-Summit zone.** Open-D tuning, Hawkins' structural drumming, Grohl's most melodically complete vocal. The acoustic version demonstrates the song is not dependent on production -- it is a compositional achievement. This is E=7: maximum melodic quality at accessible sonic weight.
4. **Taylor Hawkins is documented with appropriate biographical weight.** Born February 17 1972, died March 25 2022 Bogotá Colombia, age 50. His death preceded the scheduled Estéreo Picnic performance. But Here We Are (2023) is a grief record in part for Hawkins. The engine documents this fact with respect, parallel to how it documented Cornell (degree 312) and Staley (degree 313).

## Discoveries

1. **Pat Smear's inclusion as the first hire is architecturally significant.** Grohl's first post-Nirvana decision was to recruit a 35-year-old Los Angeles punk elder (Smear was born 1959, the Germs' 1977 formation). The first move in building Foo Fighters was to reach backward to first-wave punk, not forward to post-grunge. The Germs connection runs through Smear from 1977 to 1994.
2. **Wasting Light's recording method is the engine's most deliberate act of genealogical reconnection.** Grohl's Encino garage, analog tape (no digital recording), Butch Vig (who produced Nevermind), Krist Novoselic (Nirvana's bassist on White Limo). The choices are programmatic: reduce production mediation, involve the Nevermind lineage, return to physical analog. The result (four Grammys including Best Rock Album) validates the method. The engine records both the intent and the outcome.
3. **The Foo Fighters' post-Grohl multi-album output (But Here We Are, 2023, post-Hawkins) adds a second grief record to their catalog.** Self-titled (1994) was formed from grief for Cobain. But Here We Are (2023) processes grief for Hawkins (and Grohl's mother). The band's most significant creative acts are each catalyzed by loss. The engine records this structural pattern without overreading it.

- **Theme:** THE ISOLATED DIRECT STATEMENT. Everlong's primary melodic gesture is a single clear melodic event -- the descending open-D riff -- followed by space for it to land, then the vocal entrance. The Foo Fighters' most enduring moments are direct, melodically clear statements with room to resonate. The Varied Thrush in fog operates identically: one pure unmodulated tone, held, then silence for it to land. Two or three seconds of tone; three to eight seconds of silence. Then a new tone at a different pitch. Each statement isolated, direct, and given space to exist before the next arrives. Both E=7 through isolated direct statement rather than collective density or maximum volume.
