## Biography: Mree and the Ethereal Layering

### The YouTube Origins

Mree (Marie Hsiao) built her initial audience through **YouTube cover videos** -- ukulele and vocal covers of popular songs that showcased her voice and arrangement sensibility. The covers were not imitations but **reinterpretations** -- stripping songs to their melodic essence, rearranging them for ukulele and voice, finding intimacy in pop material. The YouTube channel became a platform for demonstrating artistic voice before releasing original music.

The YouTube-to-artist pipeline is a distinctly 2010s phenomenon. Where earlier artists in the engine built audiences through live performance (the club circuit, the folk circuit, the queer folk circuit), Mree built hers through digital presence. The internet IS the venue. The bedroom IS the studio. The camera IS the stage.

### The Sound

Mree's signature sound is **ethereal layering** -- delicate vocals layered over ukulele and soft instrumentation, creating a gauzy, dreamlike texture. The voice is not a single thread but a woven fabric of multiple vocal takes, harmonies with herself, whispered overdubs that float beneath the primary melody. The production is intimate but not raw -- each layer placed with care, the result closer to painting than performance.

The ukulele is Mree's primary instrument -- an instrument associated with intimacy and warmth, its soft nylon strings producing a gentle, rounded tone. The ukulele in Mree's music is both foundation and texture, providing harmonic structure while contributing to the overall gauzy atmosphere.

### Connection to Briana Marela (Degree 72)

Mree's ethereal layering connects to **Briana Marela** at degree 72 -- both create layered vocal textures from a single voice multiplied. But the method differs: Marela uses **live looping technology** to build vocal layers in real time during performance. Mree uses **studio layering** to craft vocal textures in post-production. Live process versus studio craft. Both arrive at similar atmospheric results through different means.

## Acoustic Element 78: The Ethereal Layering

The **ethereal layering** is the seventy-eighth acoustic element in the Seattle 360 engine. It describes the acoustic quality of delicate vocals layered over acoustic instrumentation to create a gauzy, dreamlike texture -- multiple vocal takes woven together, harmonies with oneself, whispered overdubs floating beneath the primary melody. The result is intimacy multiplied rather than diminished.

The ethereal layering is distinct from the layered loop (degree 72, Briana Marela) in method (studio versus live looping) and from the communal harmony (degree 53, Fleet Foxes) in scale (one voice multiplied versus many voices unified). It is closest to the manuscript page (degree 57, Laura Gibson) in its literary care -- both are crafted, deliberate, precise -- but where Gibson's element is about text, Mree's is about texture.

### Contrasts with Prior Acoustic Elements

- **The androgynous register** (degree 76, Chris Pureka) -- a single unprocessed voice resisting categorization. The ethereal layering is a voice multiplied, embracing processing as aesthetic choice.
- **The layered loop** (degree 72, Briana Marela) -- real-time vocal multiplication through technology. The ethereal layering is studio-crafted, deliberate, not performative.
- **The communal harmony** (degree 53, Fleet Foxes) -- many voices dissolving into collective sound. The ethereal layering is one voice creating the illusion of community.
- **The murmur** (degree 45, Damien Jurado) -- whispered male intimacy. The ethereal layering is whispered female intimacy, multiplied.
- **The ache** (degree 58, Death Cab for Cutie) -- emotional precision under indie rock. The ethereal layering is emotional diffusion under folk/pop.

## Blue Note 77: The Delicate Suspension

The **delicate suspension** is the seventy-seventh blue note deployment. The blue note that sounds in the tension between the fragility of the sound and the resilience of the artistic practice -- between how delicate the music sounds and how much determination it takes to sustain a career at this scale. Mree's music sounds like it might dissolve at any moment, but it persists. The delicacy is structural, not accidental.

The delicate suspension also plays in the layering itself: each vocal layer is fragile alone, but woven together they create something stronger than any single thread. The blue note is the space between the threads -- the gaps in the weave where silence shows through.

### Contrasts with Prior Blue Notes

- **The coming-out** (degree 76, Chris Pureka) -- gap between assumed and actual identity. The delicate suspension is the gap between apparent fragility and actual resilience.
- **The reverb trail** (degree 74, Poor Moon) -- spatial dissolution. The delicate suspension is textural dissolution -- the music always on the edge of disappearing.
- **The thread** (degree 70, Horse Feathers) -- two instruments approaching the same pitch. The delicate suspension is many layers approaching transparency.
