Third Asian-American Artist: Taiwanese-American Representation

Degree 77 introduces the engine’s third Asian-American artist. Mree (Marie Hsiao) is Taiwanese-American — a multi-instrumentalist who sings, plays ukulele, guitar, and piano, and layers her own vocals into ethereal arrangements. The Asian-American thread in the engine: Chong the Nomad (44, Filipino-American, Electronic/Hip Hop) and now Mree (77, Taiwanese-American, Folk/Pop). Different backgrounds, different genres, different energies, same underrepresented demographic in PNW music.

Mree’s Taiwanese-American identity inflects her music subtly — not through explicit cultural reference but through the bicultural sensibility of navigating two worlds, the precision and delicacy of arrangement that reflects a dual aesthetic tradition.

The Ethereal Layering: 78th Acoustic Element

Mree’s signature sound is ethereal layering — delicate vocals layered over ukulele and soft instrumentation, creating a gauzy, dreamlike texture. The voice is not a single thread but a woven fabric of multiple vocal takes, harmonies with herself, whispered overdubs that float beneath the primary melody. The 78th acoustic element is defined by this layered delicacy.

Contrast with prior elements: the androgynous register (77, Pureka) is a single unprocessed voice resisting categorization; the layered loop (72, Briana Marela) uses technology to multiply voice in real time. Mree’s ethereal layering is studio-crafted, deliberate, intimate — each layer placed with care, the result closer to painting than performance.

The YouTube-to-Artist Pipeline: 78th Pattern

Mree built her initial audience through YouTube cover videos — ukulele covers of popular songs that showcased her voice and arrangement sensibility. The covers were not imitations but reinterpretations — stripped down, rearranged, made intimate. The YouTube audience became the foundation for original music.

The YouTube-to-artist pipeline is the 78th artist-city pattern — distinct from the identity artist (77, Pureka) who builds audience through lived identity, and distinct from the side project (74, Poor Moon) where existing fame provides the platform. Mree’s pipeline is digital-first: the internet IS the venue.

Folk/Pop at Genre Stage 59

Mree’s music sits at the intersection of folk and pop — acoustic instrumentation (ukulele, guitar) with pop song structures and melodic sensibility. Genre Stage 59. The folk element is textural (acoustic instruments, intimate scale). The pop element is structural (verse-chorus, hooks, accessible melody). The combination creates a bedroom pop quality — music that sounds like it was made in a personal space for a personal audience.

The ethereal layering is not production excess — it is intimacy multiplied. Each vocal layer brings the listener closer, not farther. The bedroom becomes a cathedral of whispered harmonies.
