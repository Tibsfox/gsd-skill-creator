## The Fiftieth Degree: The Incubator Graduate

### Mirah Breaks the Record and Marks the Milestone

Degree 50 is structurally significant on two axes: it breaks the swallow quintet record with the sixth consecutive folk-adjacent degree, and it marks the fiftieth rotation -- the first one-seventh of the circle. Mirah is the engine's second K Records artist, first Evergreen State College student, and the artist whose four-album production partnership with Phil Elverum (The Microphones/Mount Eerie) establishes a bridge to one of the PNW's most important undocumented artists.

- **Situation:** Mirah (Mirah Yom Tov Zeitlyn) is an Olympia-formed, Brooklyn-based indie folk singer-songwriter, born September 17, 1974, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. Moved to Olympia in 1992 at seventeen to attend The Evergreen State College. Self-taught guitar. First song was a class assignment. Part of K Records renaissance. Four solo albums produced by Phil Elverum (2000-2009) at Dub Narcotic Studio. Pitchfork Best New Music for C'mon Miracle (8.5/10). Advisory Committee (AllMusic 4.5/5, Pitchfork 8.3/10). "Cold Cold Water" -- signature track. Billboard Top Heatseekers #46 for (a)spera. Founded Absolute Magnitude Recordings (2014). Six solo albums. Openly queer, Jewish. Moved Olympia to Portland (2004) to Brooklyn (2013). Active through 2026.
- **Root Cause:** Degree 50's structural events converge because Mirah occupies the intersection of multiple engine threads: K Records continuation (from degree 49), Olympia geography (second consecutive), folk-adjacent genre (sixth consecutive), Evergreen State College (first student), and the Phil Elverum production partnership (first documentation). Each event is independently sourced and verified.
- **Recommendation:** Document all structural events. Acknowledge degree 50 as both the record-breaking degree (six consecutive folk-adjacent) and the fifty-degree milestone.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Record broken. Milestone reached.

## First Evergreen State College Student

### The School That Made Olympia's Music

Degree 50 introduces the first artist who actually attended The Evergreen State College.

- **Situation:** Mirah moved to Olympia in 1992 at seventeen to attend Evergreen. She taught herself guitar at the school. She wrote her first song as a class assignment. The school's ethos (no grades, student-directed learning, experimental curriculum) became the template for her career. While degree 49 (Dawson) acknowledged Evergreen's role in Olympia's culture, Dawson did not attend the school as a student.
- **Root Cause:** Evergreen State College is foundational to Olympia's music scene. Calvin Johnson (K Records, Beat Happening) attended. Kathleen Hanna (Bikini Kill) attended. Tobi Vail (Bikini Kill) attended. Bret Lunsford and Heather Lewis (Beat Happening) attended. Slim Moon (Kill Rock Stars) attended. Mirah's attendance and development at Evergreen is documented through Wikipedia, the Olympia Music History Project, Boston Hassle interview, and The Creative Independent profile.
- **Recommendation:** Establish Evergreen State College as a formative institution in the engine. Document Mirah as the first student. Note the school's role in creating the K Records renaissance.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- First Evergreen student. Formative institution documented.

## Phil Elverum: The Bridge to The Microphones/Mount Eerie

### Four Albums, One Decade, One Partnership

Degree 50 documents the most sustained producer-artist relationship in the engine.

- **Situation:** Phil Elverum produced Mirah's first four solo albums: You Think It's Like This But Really It's Like This (2000), Advisory Committee (2002), C'mon Miracle (2004), and (a)spera (2009). All four recorded on or near K Records' Olympia infrastructure. AllMusic called the debut "a masterpiece of lo-fi beauty." PopMatts called the partnership "one of those rare perfect meeting of the minds." Elverum also performed on The Old Days Feeling and recorded vocals for Share This Place (2007). In 2020, Elverum contributed a cover of "Of Pressure" to the debut's 20th anniversary reissue. They are documented as "dear old friends and collaborators."
- **Root Cause:** Phil Elverum (The Microphones, Mount Eerie) is one of the most important PNW artists. His production work with Mirah is documented across Wikipedia, AllMusic, PopMatts, Pitchfork, Discogs, Talkhouse Podcast, and the Polyvinyl reissue. The partnership spans ten years and four albums. Elverum's production technique -- panoramic, lo-fi textures used as building materials -- is the sonic architecture of Mirah's entire K Records catalog.
- **Recommendation:** Document the Elverum partnership in full. Establish Elverum as a bridge to The Microphones/Mount Eerie -- a future degree candidate whose groundwork is laid through production credits at degree 50.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Elverum partnership. Four albums. Bridge to future degree.
