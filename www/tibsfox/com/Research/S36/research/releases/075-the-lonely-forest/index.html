The Second Anacortes Act: Same Town, Opposite Energy

Degree 75 introduces The Lonely Forest — the engine’s second band from Anacortes, Washington. The first was Phil Elverum at degree 71, whose hermetic lo-fi aesthetic defined Anacortes as a place of solitary, inward-looking music. The Lonely Forest reverses everything: where Elverum whispers, The Lonely Forest shouts. Where Elverum reduces, The Lonely Forest amplifies. Where Elverum creates music for one listener in a quiet room, The Lonely Forest creates music for a crowd with their arms in the air.

Anacortes is a town of fewer than 20,000 people on Fidalgo Island in the San Juan Islands archipelago, approximately 80 miles north of Seattle. That this tiny town has produced two radically different musical aesthetics is one of the engine’s most striking geographic revelations. Anacortes is not one sound. It is a place where opposite musical energies coexist — the hermetic and the anthemic, the whispered and the shouted, the lo-fi and the hi-fi.

The second Anacortes act pattern establishes that small PNW towns are not monolithic. A town can be the birthplace of both Mount Eerie and The Lonely Forest. Geography does not determine aesthetic. The same rain, the same ferries, the same Puget Sound fog — and one artist retreats inward while another reaches outward.

The second Anacortes act proves that a town is not a genre. Anacortes is not “lo-fi” any more than Seattle is “grunge.” A place contains multitudes. The hermit and the anthem-maker share the same zip code.

Tender Loving Empire Returns: Second TLE Artist

Degree 75 marks the second appearance of Tender Loving Empire in the engine. The first TLE artist was Y La Bamba at degree 61. TLE is a Portland-based record label and artist collective — a label that operates also as a retail store, a community hub, and a curatorial platform for Pacific Northwest indie music.

TLE at degree 61 was Y La Bamba — bilingual Latin-inflected indie folk from Portland. TLE at degree 75 is The Lonely Forest — anthemic indie pop from Anacortes. The label connects Portland and Anacortes, folk and pop, bilingual and anglophone. TLE becomes a recurring institution in the engine alongside Sub Pop (degrees 68, 74) — a label that hosts multiple artists across the engine’s arc.

TLE’s return contrasts with Sub Pop’s return: Sub Pop is Seattle’s gravitational center, the defining label of the city’s musical identity. TLE is Portland’s community-oriented alternative — smaller, more intimate, more curatorial. Both independent. Different scales of independence.

The Anthemic Uplift: Arms in the Air

The Lonely Forest’s music is defined by the anthem — big, uplifting choruses designed to make a crowd sing along. The songs build from quiet verses to explosive, arms-in-the-air refrains. The energy is earnest, positive, unironic — The Lonely Forest believes in the chorus.

The anthem is the 76th acoustic element. It is distinct from the reverb wash (degree 74, Poor Moon) — where Poor Moon dissolves into atmospheric haze, The Lonely Forest crystallizes into a singalong. The anthem is distinct from the communal harmony (degree 53, Fleet Foxes) — the communal harmony is many voices blending; the anthem is one voice inviting many to join. The communal harmony dissolves individuality. The anthem celebrates it.

The anthemic quality connects The Lonely Forest to Ivan & Alyosha (degree 54) and Pickwick (degree 62) — all three share a positive, uplifting energy that contrasts with the introspective melancholy more common in Seattle indie. The Lonely Forest is the anthem at E=4: louder than Ivan & Alyosha’s gentle warmth (E=3), matched with Pickwick’s stage energy (E=5).

Death Cab Connection: Tour Support

The Lonely Forest opened for Death Cab for Cutie on tour — the engine’s third connection to Death Cab after the band itself (degree 58) and Chris Walla (degree 67). The Death Cab expanded universe grows to four degrees: Death Cab (58), Ben Gibbard (60), Chris Walla (67), and now The Lonely Forest (75, tour support).

Tour support is a specific kind of artistic relationship: the opening act is selected by the headliner, implying aesthetic alignment and endorsement. Death Cab chose The Lonely Forest as a support act, connecting Bellingham (Death Cab’s origin) and Anacortes (The Lonely Forest’s origin) through the touring circuit. Two small Washington towns linked by the road.

Indie Pop at Genre Stage 57

Advanced — Genre Stage 57

Indie Pop enters the engine. Pop sensibility with independent production and distribution. Catchy, melodic, hook-driven. Distinct from Indie Folk/Rock (74, atmospheric) and Art Pop (72, conceptual). Indie Pop is the accessible face of independence — songs designed to be loved on first listen.

Key Discography

Arrows

2009 · Trans Records

The debut album. Anthemic indie pop/rock from Anacortes. The songs that caught Death Cab’s attention and earned The Lonely Forest a tour support slot. Earnest, energetic, hook-driven.

We Sing the Body Electric!

2011 · Trans / Tooth & Nail

The breakthrough. Bigger production, bigger hooks, the anthem fully realized. The title references Walt Whitman — literary ambition meeting pop accessibility. Arms-in-the-air choruses.

Adding Up the Wasted Hours

2014 · Tender Loving Empire

The TLE album. More mature, slightly more restrained, but the anthemic core remains. The album that connects The Lonely Forest to TLE’s Portland ecosystem.
