Biography & The Young Funk Collective

Foxy Apollo is a jazz/funk collective founded in 2016 by guitarist and vocalist Sam Ashkenazy while still in high school. Ashkenazy and drummer Satchel Schwartz reconnected through their school’s jazz band and started making music together, quickly recruiting bassist Jack Christian. What started as a teenage jazz-funk trio has grown into a six-piece ensemble with keyboards, trumpet, and saxophone.

Current members: Sam Ashkenazy (guitar, vocals), Zachary Schmidt (bass), Dallas Quick (drums), Shaun Quick (guitar), Jackson Cruz (keys/trumpet), Andrew Hewitt (saxophone). Earlier members include Satchel Schwartz (drums), Jack Christian (bass), and TyeLeah Nanto (credited on Sweet Talk).

Members hail from Seattle, Bellingham, and Anacortes — the first degree with a dispersed I-5 corridor geographic identity rather than a single Seattle neighborhood. Ashkenazy relocated from Seattle to Bellingham. Saturated, Faded was recorded at Champion Street Studios in Bellingham.

The Daily UW described the early sound as evoking “an easy ’70s feel, with bluesy guitar licks and vocals in the style of the late, great Jimi Hendrix.” The Cascadia Daily News captured the mature sound: “pop melodies, jazz textures, R&B grooves or psychedelic rock twists.”

Career Timeline

2016 · Formation

Sam Ashkenazy and Satchel Schwartz form Foxy Apollo in high school. Original trio with bassist Jack Christian. U-District house shows begin.

2017 · Foxy Apollo EP

Debut EP (5 tracks). Raw garage-funk: “I Think I’m Mad,” “Mental Breakdown,” “Free Steppin,” “Hey Sexy,” “Fucked Beyond Belief” (feat. Brennan Tien, trumpet). Genre tags: funk, indie, jazz-funk, rock, garage.

2018 · Tangie EP

Second EP (4 tracks). “Palomino” first appears here (later re-recorded for Saturated, Faded). Credits: Ashkenazy and Schwartz only.

2019 · House Shows to Venues

Daily UW interview. Shows at Chop Suey, The Rendezvous. Live at the Moth Manor and Live at Louie G’s recordings. Lineup shifts: Quick replaces Schwartz, Schmidt replaces Christian.

2020 · Sweet Talk

Debut full-length (9 tracks). Six-piece lineup. Recorded by Alex Dillard. Released during pandemic — unable to tour. “Someday” and “Boatplane” become best-known tracks.

2021 · New Shade of Blue

First post-pandemic single. Ashkenazy: “It represents turning a new leaf and expanding your perspective over and over again.”

2022 · Tractor Tavern

Show at Seattle’s Tractor Tavern with Alfredo Ghosts and Crazy Eyes. Significant venue graduation for the young band.

2023 · Saturated, Faded

Second full-length LP (9 tracks). Recorded at Champion Street Studios (Bellingham). Mixed by Ben McLeod, mastered by Chris Longwood. West Coast tour from Wild Buffalo. Themes: “seizing opportunities, embracing change.”

Vocal Funk as Platform

Intermediate

The funk groove serves as a delivery platform for vocals, pop melodies, jazz textures, and psychedelic exploration. The groove carries rather than constitutes. Distinct from Polyrhythmics’ funk-as-sovereign-groove where rhythm IS the composition. The polarity of Jazz/Funk revealed by consecutive same-genre pair.

Acoustic Element #23: The Groove Reset

The ADSR envelope completed at degree 21 (the release). Degree 22 begins a new cycle — but not from silence. The groove reset is the moment after the release when a new groove locks in from the residual energy of the previous cycle. The sound never fully stops; the new attack begins from a non-zero amplitude.

In Foxy Apollo’s practice: the transition between songs in a live set — one funk groove ends, the drums snap into the next pattern, the bass locks to a new pocket, the horns introduce a new theme. The groove transforms without stopping. Generationally: the engine completed one ADSR cycle spanning twenty-two degrees of veteran musicians. The youngest act in the engine resets the groove.

Artist-City Pattern #23: The Inheritor

A young artist who grew up inside an existing music scene, absorbed its values through direct participation (house shows, local venues, school jazz bands), and carries the scene’s DNA into the next generation. Did not build the scene, did not arrive at the scene — was born inside it and metabolized it as part of growing up.

The Inheritor is the generational complement to The Ensemble Anchor (Polyrhythmics, 21°): one creates the conditions, the other emerges from them. Together they demonstrate how a music scene reproduces itself.
