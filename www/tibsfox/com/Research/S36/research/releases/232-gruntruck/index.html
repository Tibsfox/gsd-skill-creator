The Underground Metal Lineage

Gruntruck forms from the most credentialed underground metal lineage in Seattle. Ben McMillan comes from Skin Yard (1985-1993) — one of the city's most important pre-grunge heavy bands, whose production history through Jack Endino fed directly into Sub Pop's aesthetic. Tim Paul comes from The Accused — Seattle crossover thrash veterans since 1981. Tom Niemeyer brings the guitar vocabulary. The result is Capitol Hill hard rock with deep underground roots and a Roadrunner Records deal.

"Gruntruck is what happens when the underground metal tradition tries to find its commercial expression in the grunge moment. Two albums. Roadrunner. Ben McMillan's voice at the center. The Skin Yard lineage preserved."

Ben McMillan -- The Defining Voice

McMillan is Gruntruck's defining element and the degree's biographical center. His voice — deep, powerful, capable of both melody and aggression — is one of the most underrated in the Seattle scene. He developed it in Skin Yard over eight years before Gruntruck. He occasionally toured with the Screaming Trees, connecting him to Mark Lanegan and Eastern Washington's rock tradition. After Gruntruck dissolved in 1994, McMillan continued in the Seattle scene until his death in 2008 — a loss that makes the Gruntruck catalog more historically significant in retrospect.

Roadrunner Records — The Metal Label Map Completes

Roadrunner Records was founded in the Netherlands in 1980 and became the world's most important metal-specific independent label. At the moment Gruntruck was on Roadrunner, the label also released Type O Negative's Bloody Kisses (1993) — one of goth metal's landmark records. Roadrunner's international distribution and metal credibility gave Gruntruck access to a different audience than Sub Pop bands reached. The engine now maps the full independent label spectrum: Sub Pop, Amphetamine Reptile, Empty Records, Frontier, and Roadrunner.
