## What Worked

1. **The sludge genre etymology is the entry's most important conceptual contribution.** "Sludge" as description: thick, viscous, slow, heavy. Not aggression but mass. The New Orleans origin (Eyehategod, Crowbar) and PNW adaptation (greyer, more psychedelic) establishes the lineage clearly.
2. **Dana James as co-equal instrument.** The dual vocal approach and bass-as-lead-instrument distinguishes Helms Alee from simpler sludge. Worth foregrounding: the clean voice floating above the crushing instrumentation is the genre's most distinctive aesthetic device.
3. **The Sargent House roster context.** Brian Cook's trajectory (Botch → Russian Circles → Sargent House) creates an unexpected bridge to degree 254. This is the engine doing what it does best: finding the thread that connects.
4. **Six album consistency is the legacy.** 2008 to 2023: six albums on one label. In the indie heavy music world, this is unusual persistence.

## Discoveries

1. **The slow avalanche as energy metaphor.** E=7 sludge is physically different from E=7 rock -- the energy derives from low-frequency mass at high volume rather than speed or melodic intensity. This distinction matters for understanding the engine's energy scale across genres.
2. ***Noctiluca* (bioluminescent plankton) as album title.** The Pacific Northwest coast glowing blue at night: specific, natural, regional. This is exactly the kind of geographic/natural imagery that characterizes the best PNW music.

- **Energy:** E=7 + E=2 = E=9. Slightly above equilibrium.
