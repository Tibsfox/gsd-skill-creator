## What Worked

1. **Amphetamine Reptile enters the engine cleanly.** AmRep is a necessary counterweight to the Sub Pop mythology that has dominated the engine's grunge degrees. Gas Huffer as the Seattle chapter of the AmRep catalog connects the engine to the noise-rock underground that existed alongside grunge without being absorbed by it.
2. **Fremont's debut is historically grounded.** The neighborhood's eccentric identity (Troll, Center of the Universe, Solstice Parade) is the correct social context for Gas Huffer's anti-polish irreverence. The two are well-matched.
3. **Tom Price's U-Men lineage provides pre-grunge depth.** The connection from Gas Huffer (1990) back to U-Men (1981-1989) extends the engine's pre-grunge genealogy and demonstrates the continuous underground that grunge emerged from.
4. **Thirteen-year run (1990-2003) is rare in the post-summit sequence.** Gas Huffer outlasted the commercial grunge moment by eight years. This longevity is a form of artistic commitment that the engine acknowledges through the full era documentation.

## Discoveries

1. **The AmRep visual identity is as important as the sonic identity.** Tom Hazelmeyer's design work for AmRep -- deliberately crude, hand-drawn, maximum information -- matches the sonic aesthetic precisely. Gas Huffer records are design objects as well as music objects.
2. **Fremont in the 1990s is a specific character neighborhood that produces a specific type of band.** Cheaper than Capitol Hill, blue-collar-adjacent, weird on purpose. Gas Huffer is the sonic product of that specific cultural environment.
