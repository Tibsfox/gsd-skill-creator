## VERONICA LIPGLOSS -- PUNK/ELECTRONIC -- SEATTLE

### The Artist

Veronica Lipgloss is a Seattle punk/electronic artist whose work fuses the raw, confrontational energy of punk with electronic production -- synths, drum machines, noise, and distortion. The aesthetic is DIY, the energy is maximum, and the result defies easy genre classification. Punk/Electronic captures the essential character: punk attitude routed through electronic circuitry.

### The Punk/Electronic Intersection

The punk/electronic intersection is not electronic music that references punk, nor punk that uses electronic instruments. It is a **genuine collision** -- both elements are present at full force simultaneously:

- **Punk**: raw vocals, aggressive delivery, confrontational attitude, short-sharp-loud structures, DIY production ethos.
- **Electronic**: synths, drum machines, sequencers, noise generators, distortion processing, electronic textures.
- **Collision**: the punk elements and the electronic elements do not blend smoothly -- they **combust**. The friction between the organic (voice, attitude) and the synthetic (machines, circuits) produces the explosive energy. E=6.

### Seattle Context

Veronica Lipgloss emerges from Seattle's punk and electronic underground -- a scene that has always valued both punk's DIY ethos and electronic music's experimentation. The engine has documented both lineages: punk-adjacent energy (Pearl Jam 83), noise (Hovercraft 91), electronic production (Erik Blood 94, Chong the Nomad 44/107), experimental hip-hop (Knife Knights 106). ### The Sound

The punk circuit manifests as:
- **Vocal aggression + electronic processing** -- raw punk vocals routed through distortion, delay, and electronic processing. The voice is not clean; it is **electrified**.
- **Machine beats + punk tempo** -- drum machines running at punk speed. The mechanical precision of electronic drums combined with punk's breakneck tempo produces a relentless rhythmic assault.
