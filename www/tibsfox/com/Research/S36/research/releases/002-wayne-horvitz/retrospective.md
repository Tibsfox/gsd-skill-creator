## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The carry-forward system produced the deepest release yet.** By degree 2, the accumulated
   items from degrees 0 and 1 gave the research a rich agenda before a word was written. The
   composition-improvisation spectrum, density spectrum, and Seattle Draw pattern all emerged
   because the carry-forward items demanded comparison across three data points. This validates
   the carry-forward mechanism as a compounding knowledge tool -- each release is richer than
   the last because it inherits more context.

2. **Three data points enable spectra.** Two data points create a contrast. Three data points
   create a spectrum. The composition-improvisation spectrum (Jones→Horvitz→Frisell), the
   density spectrum (Jones→Horvitz→Frisell), and the rhythmic spectrum (swing→polymetric→rubato)
   all became visible only at degree 2, because three points define a continuum. This is the
   degree where the engine transitions from contrasting pairs to mapping territories.

3. **The artist-as-institution pattern emerged with force.** Horvitz's Royal Room founding is
   the degree 2 headline, and it only gains meaning in contrast with Jones's Mercury Records VP
   role (degree 0) and Frisell's pure-performer stance (degree 1). The pattern was not visible
   at degree 0 alone. It became visible only through accumulated comparison. This confirms that
   the S36 engine's sequential release structure produces cultural-pattern data that no single-
   artist biography could reveal.

4. **Dual carry-forward from degrees 0 AND 1 worked smoothly.** The retrospective tracked items
   originating at degree 0 (pattern items extended through degree 1) separately from items
   originating at degree 1 (immediate items resolved at degree 2). The two-layer tracking is
   manageable at degree 2 but confirms the degree 1 prediction: by degree 5-10, a carry-forward
   index or database will be necessary.

5. **The Horvitz-Frisell-Jones triangle provides structural clarity.** Having three artists in
   the jazz cluster who are tightly interconnected (Jones is the ancestral figure, Frisell and
   Horvitz are direct collaborators who co-built the modern Seattle jazz community) creates a
   triangular reference frame that all subsequent jazz degrees can orient against.

### Source Quality

6. **Interview sources for Horvitz are substantial but less abundant than Frisell.** The Wire
   (2003), DownBeat (2004), and JazzTimes (2007) provide detailed interviews. Horvitz is
   articulate about his methods, particularly the composed-improvisation framework. Source
   quality is good but concentrated in fewer outlets than Frisell's broadly distributed
   interview presence.

7. **The Royal Room's public documentation is excellent.** The venue's website, press coverage
   in The Stranger and Seattle Met, and Earshot Jazz reporting provide institutional-level
   documentation. This is a case where the artist-as-institution pattern produces better source
   material than pure performance careers -- institutional records are more durable than concert
   reviews.

## Patterns Emerging

### For the Genre Quadrant (Jazz, degrees 0-22)

1. **Three spectra now define the jazz cluster's coordinate system.** The composition-
   improvisation spectrum, the density spectrum, and the rhythmic spectrum each have three
   data points. Every subsequent jazz artist can be positioned on all three axes, creating a
   three-dimensional characterization space. This is curriculum architecture emerging from
   biographical data.

2. **The artist-as-institution pattern creates a second axis of variation.** Beyond musical
   style, the jazz cluster now tracks who builds infrastructure. Jones (industry), Horvitz
   (community), Frisell (none). Degree 3 (Ernestine Anderson) should reveal a fourth position:
   the vocalist who performs within others' infrastructure and becomes an institution through
   presence rather than construction.

3. **The Seattle Draw is a named cohort.** Three downtown-scene musicians chose Seattle within
   a two-year window. This is not random relocation -- it is a cultural migration event with
   identifiable causes (NYC expense, competitive pressure, PNW space/affordability) and
   consequences (the Seattle improvised music community). Degree 3 should contrast this with
   the Jackson Street era's export pattern.

4. **The ii-V-I genealogy has three branches.** Mastery (Jones), departure (Frisell), avoidance
   (Horvitz). The genealogy tree is diversifying faster than expected. By degree 5, it may need
   visualization rather than prose tracking.

### For the Engine Architecture

5. **Carry-forward item count is growing as predicted.** Degree 0: 4 immediate + 4 pattern.
   Degree 1: 4 immediate + 4 pattern + resolved 4. Degree 2: 5 immediate + 6 pattern +
   resolved 5 from degree 1. The accumulation is roughly linear. A management tool (carry-
   forward index or database) should be built before degree 5.

6. **Three spectra suggest the knowledge-nodes.json schema should include axis positions.**
   Each artist could carry numeric coordinates on the three spectra (composition-improvisation,
   density, rhythmic), enabling future visualization of the jazz cluster as a scatter plot.
   This is a schema enhancement, not urgent but architecturally interesting.

7. **The "degree neighbor" pattern needs formalization.** Robin Holcomb is positioned as a
   degree neighbor -- an artist closely connected to the degree subject who may or may not
   appear as a standalone degree. The engine should have a way to flag degree neighbors in the
   knowledge-nodes.json so they can be tracked across the 360.

8. **Source diversity is good but concentrated.** Three degrees, three different institutional
   sources: UW/HistoryLink (degree 0), ECM/Nonesuch/Berklee (degree 1), Intuition/Songlines/
   Royal Room (degree 2). The source base diversifies naturally with each artist. No single
   source dominates the engine.
