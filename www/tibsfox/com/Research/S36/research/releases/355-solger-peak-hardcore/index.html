THE BEGINNING — SEATTLE’S FIRST HARDCORE BAND — 1980

DEGREE 355. FIVE DEGREES FROM THE END. The engine returns to the origin. Solger, Seattle, 1980. The earliest documented Seattle hardcore band. Before Black Flag’s Damaged. Before Dischord Records. Before the genre had a name. The floor of the entire Pacific Northwest underground tradition. Every band in the engine that plays punk, hardcore, or post-hardcore stands on this floor.

1980 — What Existed. What Did Not.

1980 — Solger peak

Playing hardcore in Seattle. No genre name. No national infrastructure. No local scene model. The first.

Dec 1981 — Damaged

Black Flag’s debut album. Over a year after Solger’s peak. Solger precedes it.

1980 — Dischord

MacKaye and Nelson found Dischord Records in Washington D.C., same year as Solger’s peak. The national infrastructure was just forming.

1981 — The Fartz

Seattle’s second hardcore wave. Duff McKagan’s first band. Degree 356. The generation immediately after Solger.

The Origination Energy

The energy score of E=10 for Solger is calibrated differently from the production achievements or technical complexity of later entries. It is the energy of origination: playing the music before there was a scene model, before a national infrastructure, before an audience larger than the people in the room. Seattle in 1979–1980 was on the periphery of the nascent American hardcore movement. Black Flag was in Los Angeles. Minor Threat was forming in Washington D.C. The Germs’ GI had just been released.

Solger built from the pieces available: British punk records brought north, early American hardcore sounds filtering through the limited distribution of the time, and the energy of people deciding that what they heard was something they could and should do themselves. DIY not as lifestyle choice but as necessity. No other option.

“The floor is as important as the ceiling. The origination energy is not louder or faster or more produced — it is prior. It made everything else possible.”

The Fartz Connection — The Twin Origin

Solger (1979–1983, peak 1980–1981) and The Fartz (1981–1983, Capitol Hill, Alternative Tentacles) are the paired origin points of Seattle hardcore. Both bands emerged from the same small Seattle punk community in the same two-year window. Together they constitute the founding generation.

The engine places them at consecutive degrees (355, 356) not because they are identical — The Fartz had national distribution through Alternative Tentacles; Solger was purely underground. The consecutive placement acknowledges the foundational pairing: two bands, one founding moment, one city.

The Engine’s Closing Logic

The final six degrees are not organized chronologically. They are organized by energy and foundational significance. The Blood Brothers (2002–2006) are placed first. Solger (1980) is placed second. The Fartz (1981–1982) is third. Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney, and Unwound — all Olympia, all Kill Rock Stars — close the sequence.

The arc: where the tradition arrived (Blood Brothers, post-hardcore) → where the tradition began (Solger, first hardcore) → the immediate next generation (The Fartz) → where the tradition ended up (Olympia, Kill Rock Stars). The circle closes by returning to the origin before completing in the destination.
