## The Retro-Soul Voice: Anachronism as Aesthetic

### Disston's Voice as Temporal Displacement

Degree 62's defining characteristic is Galen Disston's powerful retro-soul voice in an indie-folk context.

- **Situation:** Disston's voice drew comparisons to Van Morrison, Otis Redding, Sam Cooke. The voice was genuinely powerful and soulful -- not an impression but the instrument he had. Deployed in 2010s indie-folk arrangements, the voice created a productive anachronism. The voice exceeded the container. The excess was the energy.
- **Root Cause:** The disjunction between voice and arrangement is a matter of context. Disston's natural vocal quality was soulful; the band's natural musical context was indie-folk. The collision was not designed but discovered. The aesthetic emerged from the gap.
- **Recommendation:** Document the voice-arrangement disjunction. Note the Allen Stone (23) contrast -- integration vs disjunction. Document the Van Morrison parallel. Analyze the cultural questions around a white singer with a soul voice.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Retro-soul voice documented. Integration vs disjunction analyzed. Cultural context acknowledged.
