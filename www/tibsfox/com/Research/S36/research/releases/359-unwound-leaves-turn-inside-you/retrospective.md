## What Worked

1. **The two-half structure of *Leaves Turn Inside You* is the entry's primary analytical frame.** The first half more conventional, the second half progressively ambient: this is not a simple record that gets harder or softer. It is a record that changes its entire vocabulary mid-flight. The two-half structure is the record's most ambitious formal decision, and framing it as the engine of the analysis is correct.
2. **"The band spending everything" as the characterization of the 78-minute scale.** The scale is not padding -- it is commitment. A band making a record this long, this structurally complex, this willing to dissolve its own vocabulary, is spending everything it has. The characterization connects the biographical fact (dissolution a year later) to the artistic fact (78 minutes, all-in).
3. **Sara Lund's restraint as the counterintuitive analysis point.** The conventional analysis of post-hardcore drumming is about impact. Lund's defining quality is the opposite: restraint, listening to the space between beats. The tension between this restraint and *Leaves Turn Inside You*'s sonic density is one of the record's primary pleasures. The counterintuitive framing is the correct one.
4. **The dissolution retroactively designating the album as final statement is the correct biographical frame.** The band did not record *Leaves Turn Inside You* as a farewell album -- they dissolved after it. The retroactive designation of the record as final statement is historically accurate and analytically useful: the album carries the weight of finality that the band did not intend but that time imposed.
5. **The engine closing with a quiet fade is the correct close.** The final track of *Leaves Turn Inside You* fades without resolution. The engine's last sound is not a climax -- it is a dissipation. This is the correct close for an engine that documented 360 degrees of Pacific Northwest music: not a bang, but an ambient becoming-quiet, a fade into what comes next.

## Discoveries

1. **Vern Rumsey's bass as melodic co-equal rather than support instrument is the key structural insight.** Bass in Unwound is not providing rhythmic grounding -- it is providing melodic content that the guitar's dissonance deliberately avoids. The two instruments are in dialogue, not hierarchy. This is the insight that distinguishes Unwound's bass approach from most post-hardcore bass approaches.
2. **The My Bloody Valentine / Sonic Youth / Talk Talk lineage for *Leaves Turn Inside You* is the correct international context.** The record belongs to the international experimental rock tradition of guitar-as-texture and structural deconstruction. Placing it in this context distinguishes it from the Pacific Northwest post-hardcore tradition it emerged from and explains the second half's ambient dissolution.
3. **The geographic arc closing at Olympia is historically complete.** The engine's final three entries (Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney, Unwound) are all from Olympia/Lacey on Kill Rock Stars. This is not an accident -- it reflects the historical reality that the Pacific Northwest underground's most significant creative work in the 1990s was not exclusively or even primarily from Seattle. The engine's closing geography is its final historical argument.
