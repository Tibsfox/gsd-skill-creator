DEGREE 359. THE FINAL DEGREE. THE CIRCLE IS COMPLETE. Unwound’s final album before dissolution. Leaves Turn Inside You (Kill Rock Stars, 2001). 78 minutes. 16 tracks. The most ambitious record of the Kill Rock Stars tradition. Post-hardcore vocabulary dissolving to ambient in the second half. The last note is a quiet fade. The engine closes here.

Degree 000: Quincy Jones (Seattle jazz, born 1933). Degree 359: Unwound (Olympia experimental, dissolved 2002). The circle is complete.

The Band

Justin Trosper

Vocals, Guitar

Guitar as textural and harmonic resource. Dissonance, structural complexity, extended techniques. Vocals at edge of comfort — discomfort of delivery is the delivery’s content.

Vern Rumsey

Bass, Vocals

Bass as co-equal melodic voice. On longer tracks, bass lines provide melodic content guitar’s dissonance deliberately avoids. Dialogue with guitar, not hierarchy.

Sara Lund

Drums

Restraint and listening as defining qualities. Space between beats as carefully attended as the beats themselves. Tension between restraint and album’s sonic density is a primary pleasure.

Leaves Turn Inside You — The Two-Half Structure

First Half

More conventionally structured (for Unwound). Tracks with recognizable beginnings and ends. Clear transitions. “Terminus” (12 minutes) opens. “Papier Mache” at peak energy. Wall-of-sound approach. Maximum sonic density.

Second Half → Quiet Fade

Progressively ambient and experimental. Post-hardcore vocabulary dissolves. “Radio Receiver”: the turn. “Finished”: final track, near-ambient, quiet fade without resolution. The last note is not a climax — it is a dissipation.

“The dissolution of the band’s post-hardcore vocabulary in the second half is the sonic enactment of the transition the band was making in its final creative period. Not dilution — the most ambitious structural decision they made.”
