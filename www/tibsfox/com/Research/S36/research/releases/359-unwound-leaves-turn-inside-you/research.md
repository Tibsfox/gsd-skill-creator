## UNWOUND -- THE BAND

### Justin Trosper, Vern Rumsey, Sara Lund. Lacey/Olympia. 1989.

Unwound formed in Lacey, Washington -- a small city immediately adjacent to Olympia -- in 1989, when its members were in high school. The band's early lineup included Justin Trosper (vocals, guitar) and Vern Rumsey (bass, vocals). Sara Lund joined on drums and became the band's most durable third member through the final years.

**Justin Trosper (vocals, guitar).** Trosper's guitar playing is the most technically ambitious in the Kill Rock Stars stable of the 1990s. Not ambitious in the direction of guitar heroics -- ambitious in the direction of dissonance, structural complexity, and the use of guitar as a textural and harmonic resource rather than primarily a rhythmic or melodic one. On *Leaves Turn Inside You*, Trosper's guitar work incorporates extended techniques, unusual tunings, and a relationship with feedback and sustain that places the guitar in the experimental rock tradition more than the post-hardcore tradition.

Trosper's vocals match the guitar: not conventionally skilled, but deployed with total precision in service of the song's emotional and textural requirements. The voice is at the edge of comfort -- sustained at intensities that most vocalists would reduce to stay within a safe register. The discomfort of the delivery is the delivery's content.

**Vern Rumsey (bass, vocals).** Rumsey's bass work on *Leaves Turn Inside You* is among the most significant in the Pacific Northwest underground. The bass in Unwound is not a support instrument -- it is a co-equal melodic voice. On the album's longer tracks, Rumsey's bass lines provide melodic content that the guitar's dissonance deliberately avoids. The two instruments are in dialogue, not hierarchy.

**Sara Lund (drums).** Lund's drumming is one of the most distinctive in 1990s Pacific Northwest underground rock. Her approach is the opposite of the conventional post-hardcore drummer: where most post-hardcore drumming maximizes impact (the crash, the downbeat, the physical assertion), Lund's drumming is frequently restrained, precise, and listening to the space between the beats as carefully as to the beats themselves. On *Leaves Turn Inside You*, the interplay between her restraint and the album's sonic density creates a tension that is one of the record's primary pleasures.

(Sources: Unwound formation Lacey Washington 1989; Justin Trosper guitar vocals; guitar dissonance structural complexity extended techniques unusual tunings feedback sustain experimental rock; vocals at edge of comfort discomfort as content; Vern Rumsey bass co-equal melodic voice not support instrument; bass melodic content dialogue with guitar; Sara Lund drums restraint precision; space between beats; tension between restraint and density)

## *LEAVES TURN INSIDE YOU* (2001) -- THE FINAL ALBUM

### The Ambition at Maximum. The Last Record.

*Leaves Turn Inside You* was released in 2001 on Kill Rock Stars. It is Unwound's seventh album and their longest, running approximately 78 minutes across 16 tracks. The album was recorded in the band's final creative period, shortly before their dissolution.

The album's structure is one of its most significant features: it is organized in two approximately equal halves that function differently from one another. The first half is more conventionally structured (for Unwound's advanced version of "conventional") -- tracks with recognizable beginnings and ends, clear transitions, accessible entry points. The second half becomes progressively more ambient, experimental, and less structured in the conventional sense. The final tracks are near-ambient soundscapes that bear almost no sonic resemblance to the band's post-hardcore origins.

This is not dilution or loss of focus -- it is the most ambitious structural decision the band made in their career. The album asks to be experienced as a journey from the recognizable to the unfamiliar, from the structured to the open. The dissolution of the band's post-hardcore vocabulary in the album's second half is the sonic enactment of the transition the band was making in its final creative period.

**Key tracks:**

**"Terminus."** The album's opening. A twelve-minute track that establishes the album's scope immediately: this is not a conventional-length record and it is not structured around three-to-four minute songs. The opening track is itself an argument about what the album will be.

**"Papier Mache."** Mid-album. The band at peak energy convergence -- Trosper's guitar at maximum density, Rumsey's bass providing melodic counter-movement, Lund's drums creating the rhythmic architecture that holds both.

**"Cooked."** One of the album's most sonically extreme tracks. The wall-of-sound approach at Unwound's most sustained.

**"Radio Receiver."** The album's turn toward the ambient. The post-hardcore vocabulary begins to dissolve here. The frequency range narrows; the attack softens; the temporal scale expands.

**"Finished."** The final track. A quiet, near-ambient conclusion that ends the album not with a resolution but with a sustained sound that fades. The engine's last note is quiet.

(Sources: Leaves Turn Inside You 2001 Kill Rock Stars; seventh album 78 minutes 16 tracks; two-half structure first half conventional second half ambient experimental; ambition of structural decision; Terminus 12 minutes opening; Papier Mache peak energy convergence; Cooked wall-of-sound; Radio Receiver post-hardcore vocabulary dissolves; Finished final track quiet ambient fade; last note quiet)

## THE EXPERIMENTAL TRADITION -- BEYOND POST-HARDCORE

### The Pacific Northwest Underground's Experimental Wing

Unwound occupies a specific position in the Pacific Northwest underground that differs from all previous engine entries: they are the representative of the tradition that looked toward experimental music, noise, and ambient music alongside the punk tradition, rather than the tradition that radicalized punk through political content (Bikini Kill) or architectural innovation (Sleater-Kinney).

*Leaves Turn Inside You* places Unwound in the lineage of bands who used the rock format to approach the sonic territory of experimental music: My Bloody Valentine's use of guitar as texture, Sonic Youth's use of alternate tunings and dissonance, Talk Talk's late-career deconstruction of pop structure. These are not Pacific Northwest bands -- but *Leaves Turn Inside You* participates in the same investigation they conducted. The Pacific Northwest underground produced, in its final Kill Rock Stars entries, a record that belongs to the international experimental rock tradition.

The dissolution: Unwound broke up in 2002, shortly after *Leaves Turn Inside You*. The timing suggests that the album was the band's final statement -- the fullest possible development of what they were doing, completed before dissolution. Unlike bands that dissolve because they have run out of ideas, Unwound dissolved after their most ambitious achievement. The album and the dissolution together form a complete arc.

(Sources: Unwound experimental tradition vs political content vs architectural innovation; Leaves Turn Inside You My Bloody Valentine Sonic Youth Talk Talk lineage; guitar as texture alternate tunings deconstruction; international experimental rock tradition; dissolution 2002 after Leaves; fullest development before dissolution not exhaustion; album and dissolution complete arc)

## THE E=10 CALIBRATION -- WHY *LEAVES TURN INSIDE YOU* IS THE CEILING

### The Most Ambitious Record. The Last Sound. The Ceiling Is the Correct Close.

The E=10 calibration for *Leaves Turn Inside You* rests on the record's specific achievement: the most ambitious record produced by the Kill Rock Stars tradition, completed as the band's final statement before dissolution, representing the Pacific Northwest underground's fullest development toward experimental rock.

**Scale and ambition.** At 78 minutes, *Leaves Turn Inside You* is the longest final-arc entry in the engine. The scale is not padding -- every minute is justified by what the album is attempting. The ambition of a band making a record this long, this structurally complex, this willing to dissolve its own vocabulary in the second half, is not common in the Pacific Northwest underground. This is a band spending everything.

**Final statement status.** The dissolution of Unwound a year after the album's release retroactively designates *Leaves Turn Inside You* as a final statement. The band did not intend it this way -- the dissolution was not announced before the album's release -- but the result is that the album carries the full weight of finality. The last record is never just a record; it is the document of what the band knew how to do at the end.

**Closing the engine.** The engine closes with a quiet fade. The final track of *Leaves Turn Inside You* ends without resolution. The engine's last sound is not a climax -- it is a dissipation. This is the correct close: an engine that documented 360 degrees of Pacific Northwest music should not close with a bang. It should close as the Pacific Northwest itself closes winter -- not with a dramatic event but with a gradual transition, an ambient becoming-quiet, a fade into what comes next.

(Sources: Leaves Turn Inside You 78 minutes most ambitious Kill Rock Stars record; scale not padding band spending everything; dissolution 2002 final statement status retroactive; last record carries weight of finality; engine closes with quiet fade; final track no resolution dissipation; correct close ambient becoming-quiet)

## THE PAIRING -- E=20

### Unwound + Puget Sound in Silence

The pairing at degree 359: Unwound *Leaves Turn Inside You* (S36 359, E=10) + Puget Sound in Silence (SPS 359, E=10). Combined E=20. The engine's final paired entry.

The structural parallel: both are about maximum depth at minimum surface disturbance. The silence of Puget Sound -- or the approach to silence in conditions of low human acoustic disturbance -- is not the absence of sound. It is sound organized at different scales: the sub-audible infrasound of the water column, the faint biological sounds of the benthic community, the distant and infrequent calls of marine mammals in undisturbed water. The sound is there; the surface is quiet.

*Leaves Turn Inside You* works the same way in its final tracks: the sonic density of the album's first half dissolves into tracks that approach silence without achieving it. The sound is still there; the surface is quiet. Both are about what remains when the most audible things are removed.

The engine's final pair: maximum depth. E=10 + E=10 = E=20. The last sound. The circle is complete.
