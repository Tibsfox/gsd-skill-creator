## Discoveries

1. **The fuzz-as-compression technique is what distinguishes E=7 from E=5 Love Battery.** At E=5, fuzz is texture -- it adds shimmer and depth to the guitar sound. At E=7, fuzz is pushed to the point where individual notes are encased in harmonic overtones -- the guitar becomes a saturated wall rather than a shimmering texture. The transition from texture to compression is the technical explanation for the energy difference.
2. **Jim White's bass is the key variable in the heavy material.** On the heavy tracks, the bass is not accompaniment but primary structure. Without the bass providing the weight-bearing foundation, the fuzz guitar would become undifferentiated noise. The bass makes the heaviness intelligible. This is a technique shared with doom metal -- the bass defines the riff's mass.
