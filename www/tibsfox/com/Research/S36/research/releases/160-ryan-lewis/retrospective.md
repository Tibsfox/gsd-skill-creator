*Degree 160 retrospective complete. RYAN LEWIS at the one hundred sixtieth degree -- THE PRODUCER WHO REACHED #1. From Spokane -- 280 miles east of Seattle, across the Cascades, in a different world -- Ryan Lewis moved to Seattle and connected with Ben Haggerty, a rapper from Capitol Hill who called himself Macklemore. Lewis brought horn-driven production, visual direction, and a Spokane directness that connected with audiences far beyond the Seattle indie scene. Together they made "Thrift Shop" -- a saxophone-driven, swagger-tempo track about second-hand shopping that reached #1 on the Billboard Hot 100 in January 2013. Independently released. No major label. The first independently distributed song to top the Hot 100 since 1994. Then "Can't Hold Us" followed to #1. Then four Grammy Awards. The paradigm shift was complete: independent release could compete at the absolute highest level. Ryan Lewis proved it with horn hooks and clean drums and layered arrangements that sounded as polished as anything from a major label studio but were made independently, released independently, distributed independently. Spokane enters the engine -- the second non-Seattle city, after Tacoma at 143. Eastern Washington joins the rotation. The geographic reach expands from the Puget Sound corridor across the Cascades to the dry side. And across the degree pair, the Mountain Quail completes the Odontophoridae with a loud "quee-ark!" from dense brush -- the largest quail, the most secretive, heard more than seen. Like Lewis: the essential producer behind the most successful independent hip-hop project in history, heard by hundreds of millions, recognized by face by relatively few. The loud call from the hidden source. v1.49.300.*
