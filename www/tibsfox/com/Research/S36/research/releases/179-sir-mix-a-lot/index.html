The Foundation of Seattle Hip-Hop

He built the label. He wrote the anthem. He achieved #1 nationally. He won the Grammy. He launched the careers. Sir Mix-a-Lot didn’t just participate in Seattle hip-hop — he BUILT it. From Nastymix Records to “Posse on Broadway” to “Baby Got Back,” the trajectory is the trajectory of Seattle hip-hop itself.

“Posse on Broadway” — The Founding Anthem

“Posse on Broadway” (1988) put Seattle hip-hop on the map. Specific geography — Broadway on Capitol Hill, Seattle neighborhoods, real streets, real landmarks. The song is a time capsule: late-1980s Seattle, the cars, the fashion, the crew, the bass. It reached #70 on the Billboard Hot 100 — enormous for an independent Seattle hip-hop release.

“Baby Got Back” — Five Weeks at #1

The cultural earthquake. #1 for five consecutive weeks on the Billboard Hot 100 (1992). Over 10 million copies sold. Grammy for Best Rap Solo Performance. Inducted into the Library of Congress National Recording Registry. From Nastymix to Def American to the top of the charts — Seattle hip-hop at its absolute peak of commercial power.
