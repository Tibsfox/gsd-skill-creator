Electronic Music on Independent Radio

Indica Jones has been a fixture of KEXP’s electronic music programming since the mid-2000s, bringing deep knowledge of electronic subgenres to the station’s airwaves. House, techno, ambient, IDM, breakbeat, UK bass, dubstep, garage, drum and bass, downtempo, experimental electronic — the breadth of subgenres reflects the genre’s own diversity.

For many KEXP listeners, Indica Jones’s programming is their primary exposure to electronic music beyond mainstream EDM. The programming serves as a gateway to the deeper electronic underground — the independent labels, the DIY producers, the subgenres that don’t get algorithmic promotion.

Capitol Hill Electronic Culture

Electronic music is inherently nocturnal. Capitol Hill’s late-night culture — clubs, warehouse parties, after-hours sets — is the social infrastructure that electronic music requires. Indica Jones brings this nocturnal energy to daytime listeners through KEXP.

Capitol Hill is the epicenter of Seattle’s electronic music scene. Kremwerk, Timbre Room, Re-bar, and rotating warehouse venues have hosted electronic music since the rave era of the early 1990s. The neighborhood’s density, walkability, and late-night culture create the conditions for electronic music to thrive. Indica Jones connects KEXP to this local scene while programming international electronic music — the local-global bridge, specific to electronic.
