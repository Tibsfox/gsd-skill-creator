## CENTRAL DISTRICT -- DISPLACEMENT AND RESISTANCE

### The Neighborhood Under Pressure

The Central District is central to this entry:

- **Historical Black community.** The Central District was the center of Seattle's African American community from the 1940s through the 2000s. Redlining and racial covenants in other neighborhoods concentrated Black residents in the CD. The community built institutions: churches, businesses, cultural organizations, music venues.
- **Gentrification.** Starting in the 2000s and accelerating through the 2010s-2020s, the CD has experienced massive gentrification. Property values skyrocketed. Black residents were displaced. Black-owned businesses closed. The demographic transformation has been dramatic -- the CD has gone from majority-Black to majority-white in approximately two decades.
- **Musical displacement.** The Central District was the birthplace of Seattle hip-hop (Sir Mix-a-Lot, degree 179; Criminal Nation, degree 182). The gentrification that displaced residents also displaced the music scene -- venues closed, gathering places disappeared, the sonic landscape changed.
- **Resistance.** Stas THEE Boss's activist work is an act of resistance -- using music to document displacement, challenge gentrification, affirm Black identity, and maintain cultural presence in a neighborhood being erased.

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of Stas THEE Boss (Activist)

The activist-era sonic profile is distinct from the solo and THEESatisfaction work:

- **Vocal delivery.** More direct, sharper, more confrontational. Less melodic abstraction than THEESatisfaction, less introspective flow than solo work. The activist delivery is declarative -- statements delivered with force.
- **Production.** Harder. Heavier bass, sharper percussion, more aggressive compression. The beats hit harder than the more ambient THEESatisfaction production or the exploratory solo material.
- **BPM range.** 90-130 BPM. Mid-tempo to up-tempo. The tempos match protest energy -- walking pace to march pace.
- **Lyrical content.** Police violence, systemic racism, Central District gentrification, queer liberation, Black power, community organizing. Political without being preachy -- the personal is political and vice versa.
- **Mix approach.** Vocals forward and sharp. Production supporting but aggressive. Less layered than THEESatisfaction, more focused than solo work. The mix serves the message.
