## THE POWER TRIO -- THREE MAKES SIX

### Maximum Output, Minimum Personnel

Naked Giants is the distillation of garage rock to its irreducible form:

- **Grant Mullen.** Guitar/vocals. Mullen's guitar work fills the sonic space that most bands need two guitarists to occupy. Heavy riffs, melodic leads, and rhythm work -- all from one player. The power trio demands this: every instrument must do double duty.
- **Gianni Aiello.** Bass. Aiello's bass is not a support instrument -- it's a second lead. In the power trio format, the bass fills the harmonic gap left by a single guitar. Fat, distorted, melodic bass lines that carry as much of the song as the guitar.
- **Henry LaVallee.** Drums. LaVallee's drumming is the engine -- aggressive, dynamic, pushing the trio forward. In a power trio, the drummer is the anchor and the accelerator simultaneously.
- **The power trio principle.** Three musicians generating E=6 energy. No hiding behind layers. Every note audible. Every instrument essential. The format is a trust exercise: each member trusts the others to fill their space completely.

## NAKED GIANTS -- THE SOUND

### Garage Rock Distilled

Naked Giants' sound is defined by economy and volume:

- **Speed and energy.** Fast tempos, loud dynamics, songs that hit hard and end quickly. The garage rock ethos: get in, make noise, get out. No extended jams, no atmospheric passages, no filler.
- **Guitar tone.** Mullen's guitar is thick and fuzzy -- heavy distortion with a vintage edge. The tone references 1960s garage (The Sonics, a Seattle garage ancestor) filtered through modern indie production.
- **Rhythmic drive.** The bass and drums lock into a groove that pushes forward relentlessly. The rhythm section is not accompaniment -- it's propulsion. Aiello's bass and LaVallee's drums are the engine; Mullen's guitar rides on top.
- **Vocal delivery.** Shouted, energetic, sometimes buried in the mix. The vocals are part of the texture, not above it. In the power trio format, the voice is a fourth instrument, not the lead.
- **Production.** Loud but not lo-fi. Naked Giants' recordings have more fidelity than Childbirth's basement aesthetic (204) but maintain the garage rawness. Sub Pop production: professional enough to hear everything, raw enough to feel the room.

(Sources: Naked Giants discography; garage rock production; Sub Pop recording aesthetic)

## THE SONICS LINEAGE -- SEATTLE GARAGE ROCK

### A Long Tradition

Naked Giants connects to Seattle's garage rock origins:

- **The Sonics (1960s).** Tacoma/Seattle. The original Pacific Northwest garage rock band. "Have Love Will Travel," "Psycho," "Strychnine." Raw, loud, aggressive. The Sonics established that the PNW could produce music as loud and fast as anyone.
- **The power trio tradition.** From Jimi Hendrix (Seattle-born, power trio pioneer with the Experience) through grunge (many started as trios or near-trios) to Naked Giants. Seattle's music history is studded with small bands making enormous noise.
- **Naked Giants as inheritors.** Naked Giants doesn't imitate The Sonics or Hendrix -- the band inherits the principle. Three people, maximum volume, minimum pretension. The garage ethos transmitted across six decades.

(Sources: The Sonics history; Jimi Hendrix Experience; Seattle garage rock lineage; power trio tradition)

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of Naked Giants

- **Tempo.** Fast. 140-180 BPM. Garage rock tempos -- not as extreme as Childbirth's punk velocity, but relentless.
- **Guitar.** Thick fuzz distortion. Power chords and melodic riffs. Vintage garage tone through modern pedals. One guitar filling the space of two.
- **Bass.** Fat, distorted, melodic. Not just root notes -- Aiello's bass carries melody and harmonic content. The bass is a co-lead instrument.
- **Vocals.** Energetic, shouted, sometimes competing with the instruments for space. Part of the texture. Not pristine.
- **Drums.** Aggressive, dynamic, driving. LaVallee's fills are punctuation marks. The drums set the pace and the others follow.
- **Production.** Professional garage. Clear enough to distinguish all three instruments, raw enough to feel the room. Sub Pop fidelity.
- **Song length.** Short to medium. 2:00-3:30 minutes. Compact but not as compressed as Childbirth.
