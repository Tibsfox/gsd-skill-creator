Three findings emerge:

1. **All three women in the engine are Black.** This is not coincidental -- it reflects the historical centrality of Black women to Seattle's music scene (and American music broadly) combined with the structural invisibility of their contributions in documented histories and institutional pipelines.

2. **All three women eventually left Seattle.** Anderson left and returned. Bown left permanently (The Erasure). Monge left permanently. The departure thread connecting all three women is troubling: the city builds careers on their talent and then does not retain them. This is a pattern the engine must track but cannot solve.

The fourteen-degree gap is not a failure of research -- it reflects the actual gender imbalance in the documented jazz and soul scenes. The engine should not retroactively insert women to correct the ratio; it should document the gap honestly and note it explicitly.

**Distribution shift:** 2:18 (women:men) to **3:18** (women:men). Still profoundly unbalanced (14.3% women in a city with far more women musicians). Track whether degree 21+ begins to close the gap or whether the 14-degree absence was an anomaly.

| External cities | 7, 8, 9, 10, 12, 16, 17 | 7 | Chicago, Idaho, Oklahoma, Ohio, Wenatchee, Portland, Stuttgart |

Rainier Valley's character differs fundamentally from the Central District. The CD had a concentrated, historically Black musical infrastructure -- nightclubs, jam sessions, the Jackson Street corridor. Rainier Valley has no equivalent jazz infrastructure. Its musical identity is polyglot and DIY, reflecting its status as one of the most ethnically diverse neighborhoods in the United States. Monge's career fits this: she did not emerge from neighborhood musical institutions but from public space (Pike Place Market, which is in downtown Seattle, not Rainier Valley). The Rainier Valley connection is residential rather than institutional.

- **Situation:** Rainier Valley enters as the fifth neighborhood but with a residential rather than institutional connection.
- **Root Cause:** The engine's geographic threads have been institution-dependent (Jackson Street clubs, UW, Capitol Hill venues). Monge's career was built at Pike Place Market (downtown) and venues scattered across multiple neighborhoods.
- **Recommendation:** Track whether Rainier Valley produces additional artists or whether Monge's connection is an outlier. The neighborhood's polyglot character may produce artists in genres beyond the engine's current jazz-adjacent scope.
- **Carry Item:** IMMEDIATE -- Rainier Valley geographic thread. Track duration and depth.

## The Street Laureate: A New Artist-City Pattern

### Twenty-First Named Pattern

Whitney Monge's relationship to Seattle does not fit any of the twenty existing artist-city patterns. She arrived with no institutional pathway, built her career from the most exposed position possible (busking on the street), earned recognition through sheer persistence and talent, reached the city's highest institutions (Seattle Symphony), and then departed.

The Street Laureate pattern has five phases:
1. **Arrival without pathway** -- no institutional support, no connections, no plan
2. **Public-space development** -- building the career in the most exposed, unprotected environment
3. **Community-built audience** -- the audience finds the artist through direct encounter
4. **Institutional recognition** -- the city's formal institutions eventually recognize what the street produced
5. **Departure** -- the artist leaves, carrying the city's mark but not staying

The pattern differs from Export (Jones, degree 0) because the departure comes after deep embedding, not early career flight. It differs from The Erasure (Bown, degree 6) because the artist's work remains visible and recognized after departure. It differs from Builder-Stayer (Marriott, degree 15) because the artist does not stay.

- **Carry Item:** PATTERN -- The Street Laureate, twenty-first artist-city pattern. Track subsequent instances.

## Sustain as Acoustic Element: The Twenty-First in the Progression

### What Emerges from the Research

The acoustic progression through twenty degrees (silence, cascade, drone, language, motif, metronome, question, harmonic spiral, ultrasonic threshold, variation, minimum signal, trill/tremolo, sawtooth sweep, ostinato, repetition-resolution, accelerando, crescendo, the hook, ascending spiral, descending spiral) reaches its twenty-first element: **sustain**.

Sustain emerges directly from the research: a busker's instrument must sustain in hostile acoustic environments. The voice holds a note over street noise. The guitar rings out against ambient sound. Sustain is the portion of a sound's envelope between attack and release -- the duration over which the sound maintains its presence.

The placement after ascending spiral (18) and descending spiral (19) is structurally apt: after the spirals have wound and unwound, sustain is the held tone at the bottom -- the note that does not resolve further but simply persists. This maps Monge's career arc: ascent through the Seattle scene, descent (departure, displacement), and now sustain (Nashville, continuing to write and perform).

- **Carry Item:** PATTERN -- Sustain, twenty-first acoustic element. Track progression at degree 21.
