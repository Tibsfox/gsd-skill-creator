## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The Garfield-Central District thread produced the deepest biographical continuity yet.**
   Degrees 0 and 3 share specific, verifiable overlap: same school, same era, same teachers,
   same neighborhood, same bandleader (Hampton). This is not analogical connection — it is
   documented institutional overlap. The S36 engine's sequential structure makes this visible:
   degree 3 enriches degree 0 retroactively.

2. **Vocal theory nodes opened a genuinely new branch.** Degrees 0-2 were all instrumental
   (keyboard, guitar, keyboards). The introduction of the voice as instrument required five
   new theory concepts that do not overlap with the instrumental nodes. This validates the
   engine's ability to expand its theory tree as new instrument families enter the sequence.

3. **The four cultural patterns (Export, Draw, Exile, Stay) form a complete taxonomy.** This
   taxonomy emerged only through sequential release — it was not designed in advance. The
   fact that Anderson exhibits three of the four patterns in a single career demonstrates that
   the patterns are not mutually exclusive categories but phases that an artist can pass through.

4. **The composition-improvisation spectrum gained its most clarifying position.** "Interpretation"
   as a fourth position illuminates something that degrees 0-2 could not: the vocalist's
   relationship to musical material is different in kind from the instrumentalist's. Anderson
   does not compose, does not freely improvise, and does not compose-then-improvise. She
   interprets. This is a creative act that the spectrum was missing.

5. **The carry-forward system handled its first "not applicable" cases gracefully.** Several
   carry-forward items from degrees 0-2 (signal chain, Amiga Principle, Berklee pipeline)
   do not apply to Anderson. Documenting "not applicable" is as valuable as resolving items —
   it reveals the boundaries of each concept's applicability and identifies which patterns are
   universal vs. instrument-specific.

### Source Quality

6. **The NEA Jazz Masters biography is authoritative and concise.** The NEA designation (2002)
   produced a well-researched biographical summary that serves as a primary reference. For a
   vocalist who did not write an autobiography, the NEA materials fill a critical gap.

7. **Paul de Barros's coverage in the Seattle Times and *Jackson Street After Hours* provides
   the strongest local sourcing.** De Barros has been the authoritative chronicler of Seattle
   jazz for decades, and his coverage of Anderson is consistent, detailed, and reliable.

8. **Source quantity for Anderson is lower than for degrees 0-2.** Jones has an autobiography,
   a global profile, and extensive institutional records. Frisell has abundant interview material
   from multiple publications. Horvitz has detailed interviews in The Wire and DownBeat. Anderson
   has fewer primary-source interviews and no autobiography. The NEA, de Barros, and HistoryLink
   materials carry more weight as a result. Future vocalists may face similar source constraints.

## Patterns Emerging

### For the Genre Quadrant (Jazz, degrees 0-22)

1. **The theory tree now has two main branches: instrumental and vocal.** Degrees 0-2 built the
   instrumental branch (harmony, rhythm, composition, production). Degree 3 opens the vocal
   branch (phrasing, call-and-response, blues tonality, swing rhythm, interpretation). Future
   jazz vocalists will extend the vocal branch; future instrumentalists will extend the
   instrumental branch. The two branches will cross-reference (e.g., call-and-response connects
   to composed improvisation) but remain structurally distinct.

2. **The Garfield-Central District thread is the strongest institutional lineage in the engine.**
   Two confirmed alumni (Jones, Anderson), same era, same teachers, different career paths.
   Floyd Standifer (degree 4) will likely add a third perspective on the same geographic and
   institutional context. By degree 6, the thread may have enough data points to constitute
   a standalone case study of a single neighborhood's musical output.

3. **The four cultural patterns form a complete artist-city taxonomy.** Export, Draw, Exile,
   Stay. Anderson proves these are not exclusive categories but sequential phases. This taxonomy
   should be applied to every subsequent degree, with the expectation that most artists will
   exhibit one or two patterns, and a few (like Anderson) will exhibit three or more.

4. **The composition-improvisation spectrum is complete at four positions.** Full composition,
   composed improvisation, loose frameworks + free improvisation, interpretation. These four
   positions cover the full range of creative relationships to musical material. Subsequent
   degrees may add nuance (e.g., singer-songwriters who both compose and interpret) but are
   unlikely to add a fifth fundamental position.

5. **The rhythmic spectrum is complete at four positions.** Regular pulse (Jones), polymetric
   (Horvitz), rubato (Frisell), elastic swing (Anderson). Four relationships to the beat,
   each representing a different temporal philosophy.

### For the Engine Architecture

6. **"Not applicable" carry-forward resolutions are as valuable as resolved ones.** Degree 3
   produced five "not applicable" resolutions, each revealing that a carry-forward item is
   instrument-specific rather than universal. This information is valuable for the engine's
   self-understanding: it defines the boundaries of each concept's applicability.

7. **Source quantity varies significantly by artist.** Jones (abundant — autobiography, global
   profile), Frisell (abundant — interview-rich living artist), Horvitz (moderate — focused
   in specialist publications), Anderson (lower — no autobiography, fewer interviews). The
   engine should expect source-quantity variation and plan accordingly: institutional sources
   (NEA, HistoryLink, local journalism) compensate for missing primary sources.

8. **The carry-forward accumulation is nearing the limit of prose tracking.** Degree 3
   resolved 5 immediate items, extended 5 pattern items, produced 5 new immediate items,
   produced 7 new pattern items, and logged 4 architectural items. Total active items across
   all degrees is now approximately 25-30. A structured carry-forward index (JSON or table)
   should be built before degree 5 to prevent items from being dropped.

## Accumulated Wisdom: Degrees 0 -> 1 -> 2 -> 3

This section tracks the compounding lessons across the first four releases.

1. **The carry-forward mechanism is the engine's most valuable feature.** (Reconfirmed, degree 4.)
   Each release inherits context that makes it richer. Degree 3 is the richest yet because it
   loops back to degree 0's institutional context while introducing an entirely new theory branch.

2. **Three data points create spectra; four create taxonomies.** Degree 2 showed that three points
   define a continuum. Degree 3 shows that four points define a classification system — the
   cultural patterns, the composition-improvisation positions, and the rhythmic spectrum are all
   complete taxonomies at four positions.

3. **"Not applicable" resolutions define concept boundaries.** When a carry-forward item does not
   apply to a new artist, the mismatch reveals whether the concept is universal or instrument-
   specific. This boundary information improves the engine's self-understanding.

4. **The Garfield-Central District thread is the institutional spine of the jazz cluster.** Two
   confirmed alumni, both from the same era, both launched through the same band, both on the
   same label. This is the densest institutional connection in the engine. Degree 4 (Standifer)
   will test whether it extends further.

5. **Source quantity varies; institutional sources compensate.** Not every artist has an
   autobiography or abundant interview material. NEA, HistoryLink, and local journalism fill the
   gaps. The engine should expect and plan for varying source density.

6. **Vocal theory is structurally distinct from instrumental theory.** The five vocal nodes
   introduced at degree 3 do not overlap with the instrumental nodes from degrees 0-2. This is
   correct — the voice is a different instrument with different theory. The two branches will
   cross-reference but remain distinct.

7. **The export/draw/exile/stay taxonomy is the engine's strongest cultural-analysis tool.** It
   classifies every artist's relationship to Seattle and reveals that these relationships can
   change over a career. Anderson exhibits three of four patterns. Most artists will exhibit one
   or two.

8. **Prose-based carry-forward tracking is approaching its limit.** With ~30 active items across
   four degrees, a structured index is necessary before degree 5.
