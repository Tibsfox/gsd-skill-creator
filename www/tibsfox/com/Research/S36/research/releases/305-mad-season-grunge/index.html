The Return — Grunge at E=7

Mad Season first appeared at degree 224, framed as Rock at E=6: the supergroup, the recovery narrative, the meditative soulfulness of Above. The return at degree 305 shifts frame and raises energy. The grunge classification foregrounds the physical weight — the distorted guitars, the density of Staley's voice in full cry, the connection to the collapsing Seattle scene of 1994-1995. Above is a grunge album in the most essential sense: it emerged from the same underground, the same addiction histories, the same gravitational field that produced Soundgarden, Alice in Chains, Pearl Jam.

"My pain is self-chosen — at least, so the prophet says." — "River of Deceit," 1995

Layne Staley (1967–2002)

Layne Thomas Staley (August 22, 1967 – April 5, 2002) is the emotional center of Mad Season. His baritone-to-tenor vocal range, distinctive vibrato, and extraordinary emotional control produced one of the most recognizable voices in rock. By the time of the Above sessions (1994), Staley was already in the grip of severe heroin addiction — the addiction that would define his final decade and end his life at 34.

Above's lyrical content — "River of Deceit," "Wake Up," "I Don't Know Anything" — reports directly from the interior of that experience. The lyrics are not metaphorical. Staley died April 5, 2002, eight years to the day after Kurt Cobain's death, of a combined cocaine and heroin overdose. He had been dead approximately two weeks before he was found in his Seattle apartment.

Above (1995) — The Album

"River of Deceit"

Permanent Standard

The emotional core. Written by Staley and McCready. Meditative, searching guitar over lyrics that navigate resignation and transcendence. The Seattle scene's most direct engagement with mortality as subject.

"Wake Up"

Album Opener

Acoustic build to full arrangement. A deliberate awakening. Sets the album's dynamic range: quiet passages that make the heavy ones heavier.

"Lifeless Dead"

Maximum Grunge

The most aggressive track. Staley's vocal closest to his Alice in Chains register. This is where Above most fully qualifies as grunge at E=7.

"I'm Above"

Album Closer

Ten-minute epic. Multiple movements: quiet, loud, quiet, final statement. The album's structural capstone.

The Tragic Accounting

Of the original four Mad Season members:

  • Layne Staley (vocals): Died April 5, 2002. Heroin and cocaine overdose. Age 34.
  • John Baker Saunders (bass): Died January 17, 1999. Drug overdose. The first to go.
  • Mike McCready (guitar): Continues with Pearl Jam. Recovery. Above remains a personal touchstone.
  • Barrett Martin (drums): Continues as musician, composer, and ethnomusicologist. Writing about PNW sound cultures.

The album is a document of people who did not all survive to see its lasting impact. Two of four original members are gone. Above was completed; the band was not.

The 2011 Reunion — Mark Lanegan

Barrett Martin and Mike McCready organized Mad Season reunion shows in 2011. Mark Lanegan — Screaming Trees vocalist, one of the few voices in rock capable of inhabiting Staley's material without imitation — sang in Staley's place. The shows were received with deep respect. Lanegan's voice: deep, worn, authoritative. The 2011 shows demonstrated that Above was substantial enough to survive the loss of its primary voice.
