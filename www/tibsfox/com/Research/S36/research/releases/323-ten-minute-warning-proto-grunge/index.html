Ten Minute Warning

E=8 RETURNS — THE LAST HIGH-ENERGY PRE-SUMMIT ECHO. Ten Minute Warning: proto-grunge at maximum energy, 1982–1986. The vocabulary is fully formed. Sub Pop does not exist yet. Nirvana has not formed. The commercial moment arrives four years too late. Steve Turner goes to Green River, then Mudhoney. Greg Gilmore goes to Mother Love Bone. The band that seeded two summit entries never had a summit.

Before the Myth

Ten Minute Warning formed in Seattle in 1982 — before Sub Pop (1986), before Green River (1984), before the mythology that would market the sound they were already making. The band operated with the full grunge vocabulary: heavy, down-tuned guitar; mid-tempo rhythm with downbeat emphasis; aggressive non-melodic vocals; riff-organized song structures; dark, confrontational lyrical content. In 1982, this sound had no genre name and no commercial infrastructure. By 1989–1991, it would be called grunge and would sell millions of records.

“The mountain was already there. The commercial world caught up by 1989.”

Ten Minute Warning (1982) is the oldest branch of the grunge family tree. No other single pre-grunge band seeds as many summit entries through direct personnel.

Capitol Hill in 1982

In 1982, Capitol Hill was Seattle’s bohemian neighborhood — cheaper, denser, producing bands from the post-punk tradition. No Sub Pop, no commercial infrastructure, no touring circuit for heavy independent Seattle bands. Ten Minute Warning played locally, released independently, and existed entirely outside any system that could have rewarded them commercially. This is Capitol Hill before it knew what it was becoming.

Members and Connections

Paul Solger (vocals)

Founding member

Raw, confrontational vocal delivery. Screams, shouts, intones — the full range of aggressive vocal expression that grunge would later codify.

Steve Turner (guitar)

Green River → Mudhoney

The most consequential alumni. Turner’s guitar vocabulary from 1982 is the direct ancestor of Mudhoney’s approach. “Touch Me I’m Sick” begins here.

Greg Gilmore (drums)

Mother Love Bone → Pearl Jam lineage

Gilmore went to Mother Love Bone with Jeff Ament and Andrew Wood. Wood’s death led to Temple of the Dog, which led to Pearl Jam’s formation.
