Ten Minute Warning

E = 8 Proto-Punk / Rock Independent
DUFF McKAGAN ORIGIN POINT. Before Guns N’ Roses, before Los Angeles, before Appetite for Destruction sold 30 million copies — there was Capitol Hill, 1982. Ten Minute Warning is where one of rock history’s most recognizable bass players learned to play.

The Capitol Hill Origin Story

Michael Andrew McKagan — Duff McKagan — was born in Seattle on February 5, 1964, and grew up in Capitol Hill. Ten Minute Warning was his band before Los Angeles. He played drums here — his original instrument — and brought the rhythmic discipline of Capitol Hill punk into every bass line he later played for GN’R.

The line from "Welcome to the Jungle" to Capitol Hill, Seattle, 1982 runs through Ten Minute Warning.

Proto-Punk from Capitol Hill, E=8

Ten Minute Warning formed in 1982 in Seattle’s Capitol Hill neighborhood — before grunge existed as a concept, before Sub Pop was a cultural force, before the world knew Seattle as a music city. The band operated in the proto-punk ecosystem that was Seattle’s first coherent musical tradition: fast, loud, independent, rooted in the same DIY energy that would later produce Mudhoney and Nirvana.

E=8 ties the engine maximum for a Seattle rock band. The designation is earned. Ten Minute Warning was not gentle or polished — this was Capitol Hill in the early 1980s, an affordable and culturally marginal neighborhood where punk made sense as a natural expression. The songs are fast, McKagan’s drumming is relentless, Steve Pearson’s guitar uncompromising.

"The drummer who became the world's most recognizable rock bassist. What he learned at the kit in Capitol Hill, he applied to every GN'R bass line he ever played."

The Band: McKagan and Pearson

Ten Minute Warning is primarily a McKagan-Pearson band. Duff McKagan played drums — his original instrument before the Los Angeles conversion to bass. Steve Pearson handled guitar and vocals. Pearson’s guitar work anchors the band in proto-punk territory: less pure hardcore than the Fartz (McKagan’s earlier band), more structured and melodic, with an approach that acknowledges rock history while operating at punk velocity. The two-guitar setup with Paul Solger gave the band density and textural range.

The band name itself is historically situated: "Ten Minute Warning" carried nuclear-apocalypse connotations in the early Cold War 1980s. A ten-minute warning was the time between detection and impact. Punk chose this language deliberately — the nihilism was culturally resonant.

The Seattle Proto-Punk Ecosystem

The Fartz

Predecessor

Pure hardcore. McKagan's earlier band, representing maximum aggression in the Seattle scene. Ten Minute Warning is the more mature evolution: same energy, more song structure.

The Fastbacks

Peer

Power-pop punk formed 1979. Kim Warnick and Kurt Bloch. McKagan briefly passed through. The Fastbacks lasted decades; they represent Seattle punk's melodic wing.

U-Men

Peer

Post-punk, art-adjacent. More influenced by Nick Cave and Birthday Party than Black Flag. The atmospheric complement to Ten Minute Warning's direct aggression.

Green River

Successor

Mark Arm, Steve Turner, Jeff Ament, Stone Gossard. The direct link from proto-punk to grunge. Ten Minute Warning is upstream of Green River in the Seattle genealogy.

The Departure: Capitol Hill to Los Angeles

McKagan left Seattle for Los Angeles in 1983–1984. Los Angeles was the center of the American rock industry — the Sunset Strip, the major labels, the industry infrastructure that Seattle did not yet have. A young musician who wanted to break through nationally had limited options. McKagan chose Los Angeles.

In Los Angeles he connected with Slash, Axl Rose, Izzy Stradlin, and Steven Adler. Guns N’ Roses formed in 1985. Appetite for Destruction was released in 1987 and went on to sell approximately 30 million copies worldwide. McKagan joined as bassist — converting from his Seattle role as drummer. His bass playing in GN’R has the rhythmic authority of a drummer: driving, locked-in, foundational. Capitol Hill trained this quality into him.
