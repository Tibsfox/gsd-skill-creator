## What Worked

1. **The island geography milestone is properly documented.** 266 degrees before the engine leaves the mainland. Whidbey Island is genuinely significant -- the largest island in Puget Sound, the second-largest in the contiguous US. The Deception Pass context adds geographic drama appropriate to the band.
2. **Martha Splatterhead is fully documented as a cultural object.** The horror-punk theatrical tradition, the splatter film connection, the theatrical framing of extreme music -- all documented. The character is more durable than the records.
3. **The Nastymix / Combat dual label identity is the research discovery.** Sir Mix-a-Lot's hip-hop label hosting a crossover thrash band from Whidbey Island. This institutional cross-pollination is exactly the kind of historical surprise that makes research worth doing.
4. **E=8 is correct.** Crossover thrash at The Accused's level is maximum energy. No compromise.

## Discoveries

1. **The Nastymix connection will matter again.** Sir Mix-a-Lot enters the engine in his own right eventually. The Accused's presence on his label is a preview and a cross-reference point. Track.
2. **Geographic isolation shapes the sound.** Oak Harbor's distance from the mainland scene -- ferry or Deception Pass -- may have contributed to The Accused's distinctive crossover approach. When you're already outside the scene geographically, genre boundaries matter less.
