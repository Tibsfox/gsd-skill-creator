Oak Harbor, Whidbey Island — First Island Entry

Whidbey Island is the largest island in Puget Sound and the second-largest in the contiguous United States. Oak Harbor sits on the island’s north end, adjacent to Naval Air Station Whidbey Island. The engine reaches the island for the first time at degree 266. To get here from mainland Seattle you cross Deception Pass — 180 feet above turbulent tidal currents — or take the Mukilteo ferry. The Accused formed in this geographic isolation and built one of the most important crossover thrash catalogs in American punk history.

Martha Splatterhead — The Horror-Punk Persona

Martha Splatterhead is The Accused’s signature creation: a horror-punk alter ego drawn from the splatter film tradition (John Carpenter, Dario Argento) that gives the band’s maximum-energy music a theatrical frame. The Martha Splatterhead character says: this is performance, this is cinema, this is horror movie entertainment. The extreme music is not nihilism — it is theater at E=8 velocity.

The Return of Martha Splatterhead (1986) is the defining statement. One of the most memorable personae in Northwest punk history.

Crossover Thrash: The Genre

Crossover thrash emerged in the mid-1980s at the intersection of hardcore punk and thrash metal. Hardcore brought fast tempos, short songs, punk politics, DIY ethics. Thrash metal brought technical guitar playing, palm-muted riffing, metal production. Crossover thrash is the synthesis — songs at hardcore velocity with metal’s guitar technique.

Among the defining bands: D.R.I. (Houston), Suicidal Tendencies (Los Angeles), Corrosion of Conformity (North Carolina), and from the Northwest — The Accused. The Accused tend toward the punk end of the crossover spectrum: shorter songs, rawer production, more hardcore directness than technical showmanship. The Martha Splatterhead theatrical element distinguishes them from all other crossover thrash bands.

"Nastymix Records: Sir Mix-a-Lot's hip-hop label. The Accused: crossover thrash from Whidbey Island. The same label. This is Seattle music in the 1980s -- small enough that all the misfits knew each other, big enough to matter."

Nastymix / Combat: Two Worlds, One Band

The Accused’s label history is one of the engine’s more surprising institutional facts. Nastymix Records was founded in 1985 by Sir Mix-a-Lot — Seattle hip-hop artist, not yet nationally famous, running an independent label on the strength of regional success. The Accused, a crossover thrash band from a Puget Sound island, signed with Nastymix. This is not an accident of categorization; it is a document of Seattle’s music scene in the mid-1980s.

The Accused also released material through Combat Records, the heavy metal independent that also released D.R.I., Corrosion of Conformity, and Megadeth’s early material. Nastymix + Combat: punk enough for the hip-hop label, metal enough for the metal label. A band that doesn’t fit neatly anywhere fits everywhere.

Discography

The Return of Martha Splatterhead (1986)

Nastymix

The breakthrough and the definitive statement. Martha Splatterhead fully realized. Crossover thrash from Oak Harbor at maximum energy. The album that established The Accused's reputation.

More Fun Than an Open Casket Funeral (1987)

Nastymix / Combat

Second album. Tighter production, more developed arrangements. The horror-punk aesthetic continues in the title. Both Nastymix and Combat involvement.

The Curse of Martha Splatterhead (1988)

Combat

Most metal-influenced recording. Longer songs, more complex arrangements, thrash metal production values. Combat Records home for the most technically ambitious work.

Straight Razor (1990)

Combat

Late-period original lineup. The band through the decade's end. Some lineup evolution but the core Martha Splatterhead identity maintained.
