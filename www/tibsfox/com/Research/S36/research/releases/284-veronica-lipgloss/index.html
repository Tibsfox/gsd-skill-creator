The Underground Entry

Veronica Lipgloss is a Seattle garage punk band operating in self-release mode: no label infrastructure, no institutional support, music released on their own terms and timeline. In the context of degrees 281–283 — which include Kill Rock Stars and a Sub Pop subsidiary — degree 284 is the radical underground contrast. E=7 in a garage rather than a studio.

"Self-release is not a consolation prize. It is a tradition as old as punk itself. The Buzzcocks' 'Spiral Scratch' in 1977 established the principle: make the record, press it, distribute it yourself. Veronica Lipgloss is in this tradition, seventy degrees into the engine's second quadrant."

The Name

Veronica Lipgloss is a feminine-coded name — deliberately so. Veronica: a saint's name, an Archie Comics character, classically feminine and slightly old-fashioned. Lipgloss: a cosmetic product associated with femininity and surface presentation. Together as a "band name": it sounds like a character from a teen novel, not a punk band. This is the subversion. The content — garage punk — belies the surface. The gap between the name's register and the music's register is itself a punk move.

The Garage Punk Tradition

Garage punk is one of the oldest traditions in rock: The Sonics (Tacoma, earlier in the engine), the Stooges, MC5, the Buzzcocks. The Pacific Northwest has been part of this tradition since the 1960s. Veronica Lipgloss operates in the raw end of it: no studio polish, guitars crunchier and more abrasive, production closer to a room than a facility. E=7 in the garage mode sounds like E=7 in the building, not E=7 through the mixing board.
