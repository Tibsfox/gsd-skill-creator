Born Out of Addiction and Grief

Mad Season formed in late 1994 — months after Kurt Cobain's death, four years after Andrew Wood's. McCready was in rehabilitation at Hazelden (Minnesota). Staley was simultaneously in treatment elsewhere. The two musicians connected through their shared experience of addiction, shared scene, shared losses. The album Above was recorded under these conditions.

The Seattle rock community in late 1994 had buried Andrew Wood (March 1990, Mother Love Bone) and Kurt Cobain (April 1994, Nirvana). The survivors were counting who remained. Mad Season is the record that Seattle's grief made.

"My pain is self-chosen / at least, so the prophet says" — River of Deceit. The moral judgment of addiction, the counter-argument, and the exhaustion of the debate, in eleven words.

Above (1995) — Track by Track

01

Wake Up

Mid-tempo meditation on consciousness and numbness. Staley's vocal enters over McCready's guitar like surfacing.

02

X-Ray Mind

Harder, more driving. McCready's guitar in full voice. The album's most direct rock moment.

03

River of Deceit

The signature. Opens with Saunders' bass alone. Staley's lyric about self-deception and the river. McCready builds. Resignation as resolution.

05

Long Gone Day

Horn section. The album's most melodic and accessible track. Unusual instrumentation for Seattle rock.

06

I Don't Know Anything

The admission of uncertainty. Staley stripped of pretense. The album's most honest moment.

09

I'm Above

The closing track. The title statement: aspirational, fragile, uncertain whether it is true.

Aquatic Imagery — The Lyric World

Staley's lyrics on Above are saturated with water and sky: the river of deceit, surfacing from numbness, the day going long and light, the question of being above. Seattle is a water city — Elliott Bay, Lake Washington, the Puget Sound. The aquatic imagery is geographically grounded.

The Osprey (SPS 224) lives in the zone between water and sky. It hunts by diving from flight into water to catch fish. It occupies the surface boundary — neither fully submerged nor fully in the air. Staley's narrator in Above occupies the same zone: neither underwater in addiction nor fully above it. The pairing is exact.

The Losses

Andrew Wood (March 1990)

Mother Love Bone

Heroin overdose. The first major tragedy. Pearl Jam formed from his band's ashes. Candlebox wrote "Far Behind" as tribute.

Kurt Cobain (April 1994)

Nirvana

Death by suicide. The most visible loss. Mad Season forms in the months immediately after. Above is the response.

John Baker Saunders (Jan 1999)

Mad Season bassist

Heroin overdose. First member of Mad Season to die. His bass opens "River of Deceit."

Layne Staley (April 2002)

Alice in Chains

Found April 19, 2002 — exact 8th anniversary of Cobain's death. Drug overdose. The final loss.
