## What Worked

1. **The supergroup framing is the correct structural anchor.** AIC + Pearl Jam + Screaming Trees. This is the engine's densest intersection of established band lineages to date. Three bands, one album, one moment.
2. **Aquatic imagery as pairing rationale for Osprey is exact.** "River of Deceit," "Wake Up" (surfacing), "Above" (elevation over water). Staley wrote a water album in a water city. The Osprey is the fish hawk. The pair is precise.
3. **The Seattle grief timeline (Wood 1990, Cobain April 1994) provides essential context.** *Above* cannot be understood without knowing what preceded it. The engine tracks these threads.
4. **"River of Deceit" lyric analysis ("my pain is self-chosen / at least, so the prophet says") is the engine's most focused lyric work to date.** The emotional density of those words deserves the attention.

## Discoveries

1. **John Baker Saunders is one of the engine's most underrepresented musicians.** His bass opens "River of Deceit." He died in 1999. He has received almost no documentation in the standard Seattle music histories. The engine corrects this.
2. **Barrett Martin's academic music training is audible on *Above*.** The jazz-inflected rhythmic vocabulary distinguishes the album from conventional grunge drumming. His later ethnomusicological work (Amazon basin music, indigenous traditions) makes him one of Seattle's most intellectually broad musicians.
3. **Layne Staley was found dead on April 19, 2002 -- the exact 8th anniversary of Kurt Cobain's death (April 5, 1994, found April 8).** The dates do not align precisely, but the April anniversary is a recurring Seattle grief marker.

## Pair Analysis: Mad Season + Osprey

- **Energy:** E=6 + E=5 = E=11. Combined pair slightly above equilibrium.
- **Theme:** THE ZONE BETWEEN WATER AND SKY. The Osprey hunts by diving from flight into water -- it inhabits the surface boundary, neither submerged nor aerial. Staley's lyrics in *Above* position the narrator in the same zone: above the addiction but not free of it, surfacing but not yet above. The album's title is the aspiration; the lyrics are the honest report of not yet achieving it. The Osprey dives, catches, resurfaces. Above is about the dive and the surfacing. FIRST PANDIONIDAE.
