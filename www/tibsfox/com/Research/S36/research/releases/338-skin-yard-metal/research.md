Jack Endino played guitar in Skin Yard before becoming the Sub Pop production engineer. Ben McMillan sang for Skin Yard before co-founding Gruntruck and then Candlebox. The band's position in the metal summit arc is as the historical precedent: they were doing proto-metal in Seattle before it had a name. ## U-DISTRICT -- THE SECOND APPEARANCE

### The Ave Returns to the Metal Summit

The U-District in the metal summit context:

- **U-District in the engine.** The University District entered the engine previously with Love Battery (degree 221, Psychedelic Rock, E=5) -- the Ave's first appearance was in the psychedelic wing of Sub Pop, Ron Nine's fuzz and reverb. Now the U-District returns in a completely different context: proto-metal, pre-Sub Pop, heavier and more historically foundational. The same neighborhood produced two bands on opposite ends of the spectrum -- hazy psychedelia and metallic precision.
- **The U-District as incubator for heaviness.** The University District's cheap rent and proximity to the University of Washington provided the rehearsal space and the scene infrastructure for early heavy bands. Before the Capitol Hill venues (the Central, the Vogue) became the primary grunge circuit, the U-District's bars and all-ages spaces were part of the Seattle heavy music ecosystem.
- **Skin Yard's connection to the neighborhood.** Skin Yard formed in 1985 in the broader Seattle north end context, with the U-District as a key part of their scene geography. The band predated the Sub Pop commercial explosion and operated in the smaller, more self-contained world of the mid-1980s Seattle heavy music underground.

## SKIN YARD -- THE BAND

### Personnel and the Two Futures

Skin Yard's lineup contained two members whose subsequent careers would shape the Seattle story:

- **Ben McMillan (vocals).** McMillan is the critical cross-reference. His vocal approach in Skin Yard was heavy and direct -- not the melodic reach of Chris Cornell (who McMillan would briefly work alongside in the Seattle scene) but a grounded, powerfully physical delivery suited to proto-metal. After Skin Yard, McMillan co-founded Gruntruck with Jack Endino -- which brings Endino's composition role forward. McMillan then co-founded Candlebox (degrees 318, the engine's grunge-peak entry), the band that achieved multi-platinum success with their self-titled 1993 debut. McMillan died in 2008. His trajectory from Skin Yard's proto-metal underground to Candlebox's mainstream success is one of the more complete arcs in the Seattle story.
- **Jack Endino (guitar).** Before Jack Endino became the production engineer who defined the Sub Pop sound -- before Bleach, before Mudhoney, before TAD at degree 337 -- he played guitar in Skin Yard. This is the origin point of Endino's Seattle musical involvement. His guitar work in Skin Yard preceded his production career and demonstrates that Endino's understanding of heavy guitar sound came from being a practitioner before he became a documentarian. The producer of the Sub Pop era learned his understanding of guitar weight by playing it himself.
- **Matt Cameron (drums).** Before Matt Cameron joined Soundgarden (where he played through their entire active period), he played drums in Skin Yard. Cameron's presence in Skin Yard's lineup provides the direct connection from the proto-metal underground to Soundgarden's metal context -- a through-line from the C/Z underground to A&M Records. Cameron is one of the most consequential drummers in Seattle rock history: Skin Yard, Soundgarden, and Pearl Jam (where he has played since 1997).
- **Daniel House (bass).** House was the band's bass player and also the co-founder of C/Z Records with Chris Hanzsek. House's dual role -- as Skin Yard's bassist and as one of C/Z's founders -- made Skin Yard both a participant in and a creator of the label infrastructure that preceded Sub Pop's dominance.

(Sources: Ben McMillan vocals; McMillan Gruntruck co-founder with Endino; McMillan Candlebox co-founder; McMillan death 2008; Jack Endino guitar Skin Yard before production career; Matt Cameron drums Skin Yard; Cameron Soundgarden full active period; Cameron Pearl Jam 1997-present; Daniel House bass; House C/Z Records co-founder with Chris Hanzsek)

## C/Z RECORDS -- THE PRE-SUB POP LABORATORY

### The Label That Documented the Proto-Scene

C/Z Records and its significance:

- **C/Z Records founding.** C/Z Records was co-founded by Daniel House (Skin Yard's bassist) and Chris Hanzsek in Seattle in 1986. The label predates Sub Pop's commercial expansion and represents the earlier, more underground phase of the Seattle heavy music scene. C/Z documented the proto-grunge and proto-metal bands that were defining the Seattle sound before Sub Pop turned it into an internationally recognized aesthetic.
- **The Deep Six compilation (1986).** The most important document of the proto-Seattle scene is the *Deep Six* compilation, released on C/Z in 1986. The compilation features: Skin Yard, Mudhoney's predecessor (Green River, at degree 339 immediately following), Melvins, U-Men, Malfunkshun, and Soundgarden. This one compilation contains essentially all the ingredients of the Seattle sound in a single document, released four years before the grunge commercial breakthrough.
- **C/Z vs. Sub Pop.** Sub Pop (founded 1986 as a label, growing from Sub Pop fanzine) became the commercially dominant label of the grunge era. C/Z remained smaller, more underground, more focused on documenting the scene than marketing it. Skin Yard's association with C/Z places them in the scene's underground wing -- the laboratory rather than the commercial operation.
- **The label legacy.** C/Z Records was eventually absorbed into Sup Pop's orbit, with some C/Z artists moving to Sub Pop. The label's historical significance is in its early documentation: *Deep Six* is the single most important artifact of pre-commercial Seattle heavy music.

(Sources: C/Z Records co-founded Daniel House Chris Hanzsek 1986; Deep Six compilation 1986; Deep Six artists: Skin Yard Green River Melvins U-Men Malfunkshun Soundgarden; Sub Pop vs. C/Z commercial vs. underground distinction; Deep Six as primary pre-commercial Seattle document; C/Z eventual absorption into Sub Pop orbit)

## JACK ENDINO'S DUAL ROLE

### Guitar Player Then Production Engineer

The most significant individual cross-reference in the metal summit:

- **The chronology.** Endino played guitar in Skin Yard from approximately 1985 through the band's active period. While playing in Skin Yard, he began his production work at Reciprocal Recording -- first as a recording engineer, then increasingly as the primary production voice of the Sub Pop sound. The transition from Skin Yard guitarist to Sub Pop production architect is one of the most consequential career pivots in Seattle music history.
- **What guitar playing taught the engineer.** Endino's production approach -- loud, guitar-dense, drum-forward, physically present -- reflects a practitioner's understanding of what heavy guitar should sound like. He knew how the amp sounds in the room; he knew how the riff is supposed to feel. This embodied knowledge is what made his production work credible to the heavy bands he recorded. He was not an external observer; he was someone who had played the same music.
- **The Endino network.** From Skin Yard (as player) to TAD (degree 337, as producer) to Nirvana's Bleach to Mudhoney's early records -- Endino is the connective tissue of the Seattle heavy music scene across its entire arc. The Skin Yard appearance at degree 338 is where Endino's Seattle story begins in the engine.

(Sources: Endino Skin Yard guitar timeline circa 1985; Endino Reciprocal Recording production transition; Endino production philosophy from practitioner understanding; Endino network: Skin Yard, TAD, Nirvana Bleach, Mudhoney; Endino as connective tissue Seattle heavy music arc)

## DISCOGRAPHY

### The Proto-Metal Documents

- ***Skin Yard* (1987, Toxic Shock Records).** The self-titled debut. Raw, heavy, and historically important as one of the first fully realized proto-metal documents from the Seattle scene. The production captures the band's live character without the refinement that later Sub Pop-era records would achieve.
- ***Hallowed Ground* (1988, C/Z Records).** The first C/Z release. Produced in part by Jack Endino, who was simultaneously building his production career. The album demonstrates the proto-grunge heavy template: the guitar is dense, the rhythm section is physical, McMillan's vocals are front and direct.
- ***Blood and Candle Smoke* (1989, C/Z Records).** The second full-length on C/Z. The production is more refined, the songs more structured. The band is finding its voice within the proto-metal tradition while simultaneously watching that tradition transform into something with broader commercial reach (Sub Pop's expansion was occurring in real time around them).
- ***Inside the Eye* (1990, C/Z Records).** The third C/Z record. By this point, Soundgarden and Mudhoney are internationally known; Skin Yard continues in the C/Z underground. The record demonstrates artistic persistence in the face of commercial obscurity.
- ***1000 Smiling Knuckles* (1991, Cruz Records).** The final studio album. Cruz Records (a different label) for the last document. The band dissolves in 1992 as its members move on to the bands that will define the peak grunge era.

(Sources: Skin Yard self-titled 1987 Toxic Shock; Hallowed Ground 1988 C/Z Endino production; Blood and Candle Smoke 1989 C/Z; Inside the Eye 1990 C/Z; 1000 Smiling Knuckles 1991 Cruz Records; band dissolution 1992)

The energy of origination is different from the energy of execution.
