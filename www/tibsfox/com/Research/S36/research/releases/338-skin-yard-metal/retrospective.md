## What Worked

1. **The Endino dual-role story is the entry's defining contribution.** Jack Endino as Skin Yard guitarist, then Sub Pop production architect -- the practitioner-to-documentarian transition. His production philosophy (loud, guitar-dense, drum-forward, physically present) derives from having played the music himself. This is a foundational understanding of how the Sub Pop sound was constructed.
2. **The Deep Six compilation connection provides exact historical grounding.** Degrees 338 and 339 are consecutive Deep Six alumni. This is not coincidence -- the engine is documenting the proto-Seattle scene in sequence, and the consecutive placement of Skin Yard and Green River mirrors their Deep Six co-appearance.
3. **Matt Cameron's throughline from Skin Yard to Soundgarden to Pearl Jam is one of the engine's most complete individual arcs.** One drummer's complete career through three phases of the Seattle story: underground proto-metal (338), grunge peak (Soundgarden, future entry), post-grunge legacy (Pearl Jam, past entry).
4. **Ben McMillan's arc from underground to mainstream and its personal cost is documented.** Skin Yard (338, proto-metal underground) to Candlebox (318, multi-platinum mainstream). McMillan died 2008. The engine documents both endpoints of his career.

## Discoveries

1. **C/Z Records as the laboratory that preceded Sub Pop is underappreciated.** The standard narrative positions Sub Pop as the origin. C/Z predates it. The Deep Six compilation (1986) is the actual first document of the full Seattle heavy music scene. Daniel House co-founded both the band and the label that documented the band.
2. **The U-District appears twice in the metal arc: Love Battery (221, psychedelic) and Skin Yard (338, proto-metal).** The same neighborhood produced opposite acoustic approaches. The Ave is more musically diverse than the grunge mythology suggests.
