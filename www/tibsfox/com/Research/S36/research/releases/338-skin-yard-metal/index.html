The Proto-Metal Historical Record

Skin Yard formed in 1985 — four years before TAD's debut, six years before Nevermind. They were doing proto-metal in Seattle when it had no commercial name, no internationally recognized label, and no mainstream audience. The band operated in the C/Z Records underground, releasing records before the Sub Pop era transformed the Seattle heavy music scene into a global phenomenon.

The band's significance is structural: Skin Yard contained two musicians who would go on to define the Seattle story at its peak. Jack Endino played guitar here before becoming the Sub Pop production engineer who recorded Bleach and defined the label sound. Matt Cameron played drums here before joining Soundgarden for their entire active period and then Pearl Jam in 1997. This is the origin point.

"Jack Endino learned what heavy guitar should sound like by playing it himself. The producer of the Sub Pop era was first a practitioner."

Direct vocal delivery suited to proto-metal. After Skin Yard: Gruntruck (with Endino), then Candlebox (engine degree 318, multi-platinum). McMillan died 2008. One of the more complete arcs in the Seattle story: underground proto-metal to mainstream breakthrough.

Jack Endino (guitar)

Sub Pop Producer →

Played guitar in Skin Yard before his production career. His practitioner's understanding of heavy guitar informed the Sub Pop production aesthetic. The engineer of Bleach and TAD's God's Balls started here, playing the music rather than recording it.

Matt Cameron (drums)

Soundgarden / Pearl Jam →

Played drums in Skin Yard before joining Soundgarden for their entire active period and Pearl Jam from 1997. One of the most consequential Seattle drummers. The proto-metal origin point for a career that spans the full Seattle story.

Daniel House (bass)

C/Z Records co-founder

Co-founded C/Z Records with Chris Hanzsek. Simultaneously a band member and the label creator. Skin Yard was a participant in and creator of its own scene infrastructure.

C/Z Records and Deep Six

C/Z Records (co-founded by Daniel House and Chris Hanzsek, 1986) predates Sub Pop's commercial dominance. Its most important document is the Deep Six compilation (1986), which captures the proto-Seattle scene in a single release: Skin Yard, Green River (the band that would become Mudhoney and Pearl Jam), Melvins, U-Men, Malfunkshun, and Soundgarden. Every major strand of the Seattle heavy music story is present on this one compilation, four years before the commercial breakthrough.

Degrees 338 (Skin Yard) and 339 (Green River) are consecutive Deep Six alumni — the engine places the two foundational Deep Six proto-metal bands back-to-back, which is historically precise.

E=7 — Organized Heaviness

Skin Yard's E=7 in the metal summit is a different kind of heaviness than TAD's E=9 (degree 337). Where TAD sustains maximum mass and velocity simultaneously with almost no dynamic relief, Skin Yard's proto-metal is more structurally organized: the songs have shape, dynamic variations are present, the heaviness is applied within a compositional framework rather than uniformly across the record. This is proto-metal vs. heavy grunge — organized heavy vs. sustained maximum heavy.

The energy drop from E=9 (337) to E=7 (338) reflects the summit's approach to its close. The arc does not maintain maximum throughout; it creates space for the re-escalation at degrees 339 (Green River, E=7) and 340 (Nirvana heavy, E=9).
