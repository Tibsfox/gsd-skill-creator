## Biography: Jesse Marchant and the Cinematic Dark

### The Artist

**Jesse Marchant** is a Canadian-born singer-songwriter who has also recorded under the name **JBM**. His music is atmospheric, cinematic folk with baritone vocals -- dark and meditative. The baritone is the anchor: low, weighted, gravitational. The arrangements are sparse: guitar, ambient textures, occasional strings, and the vast space between notes.

### The Sound

Marchant's music evokes **landscape** -- not specific places but the feeling of being in a vast, quiet space. The cinematic quality comes from pacing: his songs move at the speed of film, not pop. Long passages of near-silence. Gradual builds. The baritone carrying weight across empty space.

Albums:

- **Not Even in July** (2014, independent) -- atmospheric folk with cinematic scope. The silence between notes carries meaning. Winter-weight. Dark but not despairing.
- **Illusion of Love** (2019, independent) -- more expansive, still dark. Strings and atmosphere. The baritone anchors everything. Love examined through a lens of melancholy.

### PNW Connection

Marchant's connection to the PNW scene comes through performances and collaborations. The dark, atmospheric quality of his music fits naturally within Seattle's tradition of meditative, introspective folk -- the lineage that includes Phil Elverum (71), Jesse Sykes (64), and the broader Pacific Northwest folk continuum.
