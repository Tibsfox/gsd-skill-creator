Data Discrepancy: Label Correction

The engine’s CSV data lists Tender Loving Empire (TLE) as the label for Ivan & Alyosha. This is incorrect. TLE is a Portland, Oregon-based gift shop and record label with no documented connection to Ivan & Alyosha. Actual labels: Dualtone Records (Nashville) and Nettwerk Music Group (Vancouver).

The Brothers Karamazov in Capitol Hill

Tim Wilson (vocals, guitar) and Ryan Carbary (guitar, vocals) formed Ivan & Alyosha in 2007 in Seattle, Washington, based in the Capitol Hill neighborhood. The name came from producer Eli Thomson, who suggested naming the band after characters from Dostoevsky’s The Brothers Karamazov (1880) — Ivan (the intellectual skeptic) and Alyosha (the spiritual believer). The first literary-named band in the engine.

The five-piece lineup: Tim Wilson, Carbary, Pete Wilson (Tim’s brother, bass, vocals), Tim Kim (guitar, vocals), and Cole Mauro (drums). The sibling bond — Tim and Pete Wilson as actual brothers — deepens the Dostoevsky resonance: a band named for fictional brothers, containing real brothers.

The Dualtone Era: National Distribution

After self-released EPs — The Verse, The Chorus (2009, produced by Eli Thomson in LA) and Fathers Be Kind (2011) — Ivan & Alyosha signed with Dualtone Records, a Nashville-based independent label also home to The Lumineers.

All the Times We Had (2013, Dualtone) — first full-length. Five-piece at full strength: jangly electric and acoustic guitars, multi-voice harmonies, propulsive drums. Bright, optimistic, hook-driven folk-pop. “Running for Cover.” “Don’t Wanna Die.”

It’s All Just Pretend (2015, Dualtone) — the philosophical turn. The title echoes Ivan Karamazov’s doubt: if it’s all just pretend, what grounds the faith? Slightly darker undertone beneath the jangle.

Benaroya Hall: The Symphony Collaboration

Live at Benaroya Hall (2022) was recorded with the Seattle Symphony, arranged by Lee Mills and Andrew Joslyn. Joslyn — arranger for Macklemore and Mary Lambert — expanded Ivan & Alyosha’s folk-pop to orchestral scale in a 2,500-seat concert hall.

The collaboration demonstrates that the band’s harmonic vocabulary is rich enough to sustain symphonic treatment. Not all folk-pop translates to orchestra; Ivan & Alyosha’s layered harmonies provide natural anchor points for string, brass, and woodwind arrangements.

All We Ever Had: The Return as Duo

All We Ever Had (2023, Nettwerk Music Group) marked Ivan & Alyosha’s return as a two-piece: Wilson and Carbary, the founding duo, stripped to essence. The title echoes and transforms the 2013 debut: All the Times We Had becomes All We Ever Had. The times became everything. Five became two.

The Nettwerk signing moved the band from Nashville (Dualtone) to Vancouver (Nettwerk) — from the American South’s indie infrastructure to the Pacific Northwest’s broader independent ecosystem. Nettwerk’s roster includes Passenger, Old Crow Medicine Show, and Angus & Julia Stone.

The cathedral harmony is five people building a structure of sound. The fraternal jangle is two people remembering why they started.

The Fraternal Jangle

Advanced — 55th Acoustic Element

Bright, ringing, optimistic harmonic texture from two voices and two guitars — simple in form, complex in emotional resonance. Not the cathedral’s overwhelming density (53) but the warmth of intimately familiar voices: brothers, lifelong collaborators. The jangle is the guitar’s contribution; the fraternal is the relationship’s. Trust expressed as music.
