## What Worked

1. **Oak Harbor as geographic origin is both specific and historically correct.** The Accused actually come from Oak Harbor -- not Seattle, not a suburb. The island, the naval station, the Growlers. This is the most remote geographic origin point in the engine.
2. **The Naval Air Station Whidbey Island context provides acoustic grounding.** The EA-18G Growler as background noise for Oak Harbor's daily life is not a metaphor -- it is the actual acoustic environment. The loudest bands come from the loudest places.
3. **Combat Records positions The Accused correctly in the national extreme music context.** The label connects Oak Harbor to Megadeth, Exodus, Possessed. The Northwest extreme music voice in the company it kept.
4. **Martha Splatterhead as 40-year persistent character is a genuine achievement.** Brand persistence in extreme music is rare. Martha has survived multiple hiatuses and lineup changes.
5. **1981 formation year is the engine's pre-history moment.** The Accused predate the Sub Pop narrative, the grunge moment, the alternative rock era. They are the origin point, not the culmination.

## Discoveries

1. **The Nastymix connection (1990) is the engine's most unexpected label placement.** Nastymix was a Seattle hip-hop label -- home to Sir Mix-a-Lot. The Accused appearing on Nastymix documents the cross-genre fluidity of the Seattle scene at its peak, when a crossover thrash band from a naval island could share a roster with the architect of "Baby Got Back."
2. **Crossover thrash tempo (180-220+ BPM) is the engine's highest musical velocity.** No previous S36 entry operates at this speed. The metal arc opens at the maximum tempo the engine has documented.
3. **The Accused's longevity (40+ years) contrasts sharply with the brief active periods of most crossover thrash contemporaries.** D.R.I. and C.O.C. have also maintained long careers, but many crossover thrash bands burned out quickly. The Accused's persistence is the outlier.
