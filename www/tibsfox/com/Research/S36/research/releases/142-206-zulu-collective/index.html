First Collective Entry — The Four Elements — PNW Zulu Nation

206 Zulu is the Pacific Northwest chapter of the Universal Zulu Nation. The first collective/organization entry in the S36 engine. DJing + MCing + Breaking + Graffiti = the four pillars. Central District. Active since ~1997. DJ Riz (degree 136) is a member.

The Four Elements

DJing

Turntablism. Vinyl-based. Skill-focused. DJ Riz (136) represents this element. Community service over commercial success.

MCing

Lyricism. Freestyle cyphers. Battle rap. Conscious rap. The vocal element. Democratic — anyone can enter the cypher.

Breaking

B-boying/B-girling. Physical expression. Competitions, practice sessions, community circles. Connects to Bronx origins.

Graffiti / Aerosol Art

Visual culture. Legal mural projects. Exhibitions. Workshops. The aesthetic dimension of hip-hop.

Community Practice

206 Zulu is not a label. It is not a venue. It is a network of practitioners connected by shared cultural commitment. Jams, cyphers, workshops, battles, anniversary celebrations. The infrastructure that supports individual artists like DJ Riz, DJ Sounds, and the next generation. Cultural preservation through active practice.
