The E=7 Ceiling Breach

From degree 1 through degree 124, the energy scale operated on a 1–6 range. E=6 was the absolute maximum — reached at degrees 107 (Knife Knights), 108 (Acorn Woodpecker), 109 (No-Anchor), and 123 (Wellwater Conspiracy). These Arms Are Snakes at degree 125 breaks that ceiling. E=7 is not an incremental step — it is a redefinition of the scale itself. The energy spectrum is now 1–7. Every previous rating exists in a new context.

E=7 is earned through sustained maximum intensity. Where E=6 bands spike to maximum in their peaks, TAAS sustains that intensity as its baseline and then exceeds it. Angular, dissonant guitars slash rather than chug. Brian Cook’s bass is a low-frequency weapon. Steve Snere’s vocals oscillate between melody and unhinged confrontation. The drums are violent, syncopated, unpredictable. Even the “quiet” passages register at E=5–6 — what would be maximum for other artists is the resting state for These Arms Are Snakes.

Botch → TAAS → Russian Circles

Brian Cook is the thread through three eras of heavy music: Botch (mathcore, Everett, 1993–2002), These Arms Are Snakes (post-hardcore, Capitol Hill, 2002–2009), Russian Circles (instrumental post-metal, Chicago, 2004–present). One bass player, three bands, three answers to the same question: how do you make music that is uncompromisingly heavy? Botch: through complexity. TAAS: through relentlessness. Russian Circles: through dynamics.

Cook’s bass work in These Arms Are Snakes carries the technical precision of Botch into a new emotional register. The bass is not background — it is a second attack vector, doubling the guitar’s intensity while adding low-end weight that makes the music physically felt. The Botch connection places TAAS in the lineage of Northwest heavy music’s most extreme practitioners.

Capitol Hill Post-Hardcore

Capitol Hill in the early 2000s was the epicenter of Seattle’s heavy music underground. The Comet Tavern, Neumos, and the house show circuit provided the infrastructure. The neighborhood was not yet tech-gentrified — it was affordable enough for musicians, with a density of bars, clubs, record shops, and rehearsal spaces that created critical mass. TAAS operated alongside The Blood Brothers, Pretty Girls Make Graves, Minus the Bear, and Murder City Devils — the post-hardcore/post-punk/noise axis of early 2000s Seattle.

Discography

This Is Meant to Hurt You (EP, 2003) — five tracks of concentrated aggression, title as statement of intent. Oxeneers or the Lion Sleeps When Its Antelope Go Home (2004) — the debut full-length, expanded sonic palette, oblique and confrontational. Easter (2006) — darker, more atmospheric, more controlled; the album where E=7 is most fully realized, sustained maximum intensity with textural variation. Tail Swallower and Dove (2008) — final album, longest compositions, most experimental, Cook’s most inventive bass work. The band dissolved in 2009. Six years, four releases, no compromise.
