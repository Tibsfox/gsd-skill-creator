The Most Critically Acclaimed Band of the 2000s

Robin Pecknold was born in Seattle on March 30, 1986. His father Greg played in the 1960s Seattle soul band The Fathoms. His great-grandfather Theodor Valaas immigrated from Norway in 1905. Pecknold grew up in Kirkland, Washington, attending Lake Washington High School alongside Skyler Skjelset. They bonded over Bob Dylan and Neil Young. At fourteen, Pecknold wrote his first song. In 2006, they co-founded Fleet Foxes in Seattle.

Seattle producer Phil Ek (Modest Mouse, Built to Spill, The Shins, Band of Horses) heard the demos and helped record the self-released Fleet Foxes EP (2006). By late 2007, over 250,000 Myspace plays in two months caught Sub Pop’s attention. The band signed on January 18, 2008 — the fourth Sub Pop artist in the folk zone (after Jurado 45, Cahoone 48, Grand Archives 51).

Fleet Foxes: Pitchfork #1 of 2008

The self-titled debut, Fleet Foxes, was released June 3, 2008, on Sub Pop. Produced by Phil Ek. Recorded at Avast! Recording Company and London Bridge Studio in Seattle. The album cover: a detail from Pieter Bruegel the Elder’s Netherlandish Proverbs (1559) — Dutch Renaissance, a village of fools.

“A landmark in American music — an instant classic.” — The Guardian, 5/5 stars

Pitchfork: 9.0/10. Metacritic: 87/100 (universal acclaim, 31 reviews). Rolling Stone: 4/5 — compared to Beach Boys, Animal Collective, CSNY. Mojo: “Instant Classic.” Uncut: First-ever Music Award winner. Pitchfork #1 Album of 2008. The Times: #1 of 2008. Billboard: Critics’ Choice Album of the Year.

“White Winter Hymnal” — a vocal round of interlocking harmonies, Pecknold’s lyrics “fairly meaningless” by his own admission. The song is pure vocal architecture. Covered by Pentatonix and distributed worldwide as choral sheet music. “Mykonos” — the band’s most-played concert song, a soaring folk anthem from the Sun Giant EP. Billboard 200 #36. UK #3.

Helplessness Blues: Billboard #4

Helplessness Blues (May 3, 2011, Sub Pop) debuted at #4 on the Billboard 200 — the band’s commercial peak. UK #2. Norway #1. Produced by Phil Ek across Avast!, Bear Creek Studios, Dreamland Studios, and Reciprocal Recording. Pitchfork: 8.8/10. Metacritic: 85. Grammy nomination: Best Folk Album (2012).

Josh Tillman joined as drummer in 2008. Morgan Henderson (formerly of The Blood Brothers) joined in 2010, adding upright bass, woodwinds, violin, and saxophone. After touring, Tillman departed in January 2012 to become Father John Misty — one of the decade’s most distinctive solo voices. Pecknold: “My relationship with Josh was fully deteriorated by that point.”

Fleet Foxes went on indefinite hiatus. Pecknold enrolled at Columbia University (2013), majoring in English. The most critically acclaimed indie folk band of the 21st century simply stopped.

Crack-Up & Shore: Return and Renewal

After six years, Crack-Up (June 16, 2017, Nonesuch) marked the return. Co-produced by Pecknold and Skjelset. Experimental, orchestral, progressive. Pitchfork compared it to Joanna Newsom’s Ys and Grizzly Bear’s Yellow House. Pitchfork: 8.7/10. Billboard 200: #9. Metacritic: 81.

Shore (September 22, 2020, Anti-) was released on the autumnal equinox — announced one day in advance, dropped at the moment of astronomical transition. Brighter and more celebratory than Crack-Up. Recorded across five studios (New York, Paris, Los Angeles). Pitchfork: 8.3/10. Metacritic: 87. Grammy nomination: Best Alternative Music Album (2022).

Four albums. Four Pitchfork scores between 8.3 and 9.0. Four Metacritic scores between 81 and 87. Universal acclaim on every release. No other band in the engine can claim this level of sustained critical consensus.

Music Theory Contributions

The Cathedral Harmony

Advanced — 54th Acoustic Element

Layered vocal harmonies that achieve such density and reverberance the sound transforms from voices singing into an architectural space the listener inhabits. “White Winter Hymnal” is a vocal round where seams disappear and the listener perceives not singers but a structure made of voices. The communal harmony (51) is the campfire; the cathedral harmony is the nave.

The Prodigy Return

Advanced — 54th Artist-City Pattern

The artist who emerges from a city’s suburban orbit young, achieves world recognition, departs for education, and returns to create from the original city network. Pecknold: Kirkland to Seattle to Pitchfork #1 to Columbia University to Crack-Up at Avast! and Anacortes. The circle closes.

Blue Note: The Ancient Longing

Intermediate — 53rd Deployment

The blue note that arrives from temporal displacement — music that sounds centuries old, Bruegel cover art, Renaissance mandolin figures, shape-note harmonies. The listener feels homesick for a place they have never been. Nine consecutive folk-adjacent blue notes (45–53).
