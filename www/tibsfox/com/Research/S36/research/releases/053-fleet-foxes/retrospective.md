## The Father John Misty Genealogy

### The Drummer Who Became a Star

Degree 53 establishes the Fleet Foxes-Father John Misty genealogy.

- **Situation:** Josh Tillman joined Fleet Foxes as drummer in 2008, replacing Nicholas Peterson. He performed on Helplessness Blues (2011) and its extensive world tour. Tillman joined because he was dating Pecknold's sister and "had very little interest in being a drummer." He wanted to leave before the Helplessness Blues tour but stayed at Sub Pop's urging. Pecknold: "My relationship with Josh was fully deteriorated by that point." Final show Tokyo, January 20, 2012. Tillman became Father John Misty -- releasing Fear Fun (2012), I Love You Honeybear (2015), Pure Comedy (2017), Chloe and the Next 20th Century (2022). Both Fleet Foxes and Father John Misty are critically acclaimed and folk-adjacent. Opposite aesthetic valences: earnest pastoral versus sardonic literary.
- **Root Cause:** The divergence is a natural consequence of two fundamentally different creative temperaments sharing a band. Pecknold's vision is communal, harmonic, reverberant. Tillman's vision is solitary, ironic, confrontational. The shared origin (Fleet Foxes, Sub Pop, Seattle) produced opposite futures.
- **Recommendation:** Document the Fleet Foxes-Father John Misty genealogy. Track Tillman as a future degree candidate. Note the parallel to the Carissa's Wierd genealogy (degrees 48, 51).
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Fleet Foxes-Father John Misty genealogy. Tillman as future degree candidate. Opposite valences from same origin.
