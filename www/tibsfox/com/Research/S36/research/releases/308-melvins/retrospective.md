## What Worked

1. **The Cobain connection is the most important influence relationship in the engine's history.** The Melvins are not adjacent to Nirvana; they are its precondition. Buzz Osborne gave Cobain his musical education. Dale Crover played on Nirvana's first demo. Cobain tried to join the Melvins and was rejected -- the rejection forced him to form Nirvana. This is documented and central.
2. **E=8 is correct and the first in the engine.** There is no heavier band in the Pacific Northwest. The Melvins' sludge metal -- 40-60 BPM, down-tuned to dissolution, distortion as low-frequency smear -- fills the E=8 calibration completely. The maximum is earned.
3. **Aberdeen as geographic entry is historically significant.** The engine has documented Seattle neighborhoods and geographic outliers (Ellensburg, degree 307). Aberdeen/Montesano is the engine's most important geographic entry outside the city: the timber country that produced both the Melvins and Kurt Cobain.
4. **The Marten equivalence pairing (SPS 308, E=9) is deliberate and correct.** Both from Aberdeen-adjacent timber country. Both at maximum energy on their tracks. Both from the deep Pacific Northwest. The forest exceeds the music by one point: this is the engine's correct ecological principle.

## Discoveries

1. **The Dale Demo (1988) is the engine's most significant unanticipated cross-reference.** Dale Crover played on Nirvana's first recording. The Melvins are literally inside the earliest Nirvana document. The influence is not just inspirational; it is physical -- Crover's drums are on tape.
2. **The Atlantic period (Houdini, Stoner Witch) is the most principled major-label behavior in the engine's history.** Most bands modulate toward commercial accessibility when signing to major labels. The Melvins made their most deliberately inaccessible records with Atlantic's budget. The refusal to adapt is the defining behavior.
3. **Dave Grohl's precision-power combination traces directly to Dale Crover.** Nevermind's rhythm section carries Crover's influence. The Melvins are in Nevermind through Grohl's education. The influence is not just Cobain's -- it runs through Grohl too.

## Pair Analysis: Melvins + Pacific Marten scream

- **Energy:** E=8 + E=9 = E=17. **THE ENGINE'S DOUBLE MAXIMUM ZONE.** S36 maximum (Melvins E=8) + SPS maximum (Marten E=9). The degree-308 pairing is the most energetically loaded in the engine to date.
- **Theme:** THE DEEP TIMBER COUNTRY MAXIMUM. Both the Melvins and the Pacific Marten come from the same landscape: Aberdeen's surrounding old-growth and mature forest, the timber country of southwestern Washington. The Melvins' sound carries the weight of that landscape -- the logging culture, the economic pressure, the isolation of the coast. The Marten's territorial scream fills the old-growth forest with the same kind of uncompromising assertion. Both say: this is my territory, at maximum volume, without apology. The forest exceeds the music by one: E=9 vs. E=8. The engine's ecological principle holds even at maximum.
