## What Worked

1. **The two-degree Hendrix structure is the correct choice.** The early career is not prologue -- it is a complete and independent subject. The Chitlin' Circuit years, the apprenticeships, the exploitation contracts, the Army years -- all of these deserve dedicated documentation.
2. **The PPX contract documentation is important.** Systematic exploitation of young Black musicians in the music industry is part of the story. The contract is documented factually without editorializing. The consequence -- the Band of Gypsys album as legal settlement -- is one of the more unexpected outcomes of an exploitative music industry relationship.
3. **Billy Cox as the through-line.** Meeting Cox at Fort Campbell in 1961 and having him in the final touring band in 1970 -- nine years of partnership across the full arc of Hendrix's career -- is one of the engine's better narrative threads.
4. **The E=7 framing is accurate.** The early career Hendrix is not at maximum. He is playing in support, constrained, developing. E=7 is the intensity of potential, not yet of full expression.

## Discoveries

1. **The Chitlin' Circuit's physical showmanship traditions are the direct source of Hendrix's legendary stage moves.** Playing guitar with the teeth, behind the head, between the legs -- these were Black entertainment traditions from the circuit, refined and pushed to an extreme by Hendrix. They were not invented from nothing.
2. **Little Richard's firings of Hendrix are the engine's best illustration of excess as virtue.** Being fired for being too expressive is not a failure -- it is the earliest evidence that Hendrix's expressive capacity was beyond containment. The constraint that the sideman role demanded was the crucible in which the drive to express fully was tested.
3. **The Green Book is the correct frame for the Chitlin' Circuit.** The network of venues was the musical equivalent of the Green Book's travel infrastructure -- a parallel economy sustaining Black professional life in a segregated country.
