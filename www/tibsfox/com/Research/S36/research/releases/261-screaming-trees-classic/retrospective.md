## What Worked

1. **Ellensburg as geographic milestone is historically grounded.** The Trees are genuinely from Ellensburg. The dry, slightly isolated quality of their sound has a geographic logic that the engine can document. This is not the first band from outside Seattle -- it is the first band from east of the Cascades.
2. **Lanegan's voice as primary analytical lens is correct.** The Screaming Trees are a very good band; with Lanegan, they are irreplaceable. The voice is the differentiating element. The documentation of Lanegan's post-Trees career (QOTSA, Gutter Twins, solo work) extends the legacy beyond the band's 2000 dissolution.
3. **SST-to-Epic arc is historically significant.** Very few bands made the crossing from underground independence (SST) to major-label presence (Epic) while retaining artistic credibility. Sweet Oblivion and Dust demonstrate this integrity.
4. **Jack Endino thread established.** Endino co-produced Sweet Oblivion (261) and will be central to Skin Yard (264). The Endino thread through the Seattle network is a recurring structural element.

## Discoveries

1. **The isolation of Ellensburg as a sonic influence is real.** High desert, far from the coast, college town. The Trees' sound -- slightly out-of-time, heavy with roots in the 1960s -- makes geographic sense in this context. The engine documents geography as a sonic force.
2. **Lanegan's death (2022) retrospectively frames everything.** His voice was always weighted with something; in retrospect it is weighted with a life that was genuinely difficult and genuinely productive. The memoirs (Sing Backwards and Weep, Devil in a Coma) are documents of that weight.
