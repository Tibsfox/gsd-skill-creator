## PROGRAM DIRECTOR -- BUILDING THE KEXP SOUND

### The Curatorial Architect

Kevin Cole has served as KEXP's **Program Director** since the station's transformation from KCMU to KEXP in the early 2000s. His role is the most structurally significant at the station:

- **Programming philosophy:** Cole shaped KEXP's core programming philosophy: **human curation over algorithm, discovery over repetition, depth over breadth**. Every show on the station reflects this philosophy -- from the Morning Show's eclectic mix to Wo' Pop's global scope. Cole built the framework that enables these individual expressions.
- **Talent development:** Cole hired, mentored, and supported the on-air talent that defines KEXP's personality. The host trio (130-132) exists because Cole built the programming structure that gives each host space to develop their unique voice. A program director's greatest achievement is the quality of the people they empower.
- **Station identity:** KEXP's identity as "the listener-supported radio station that treats music as art, not product" is largely Cole's architectural achievement. The station's global reputation -- built through live sessions, streaming, and social media -- reflects programming decisions Cole made years before the results were visible.
- **Playlist philosophy:** Cole developed KEXP's approach to playlists: DJs curate their own shows with genuine freedom, but within a framework that ensures the station maintains coherence. This balance -- freedom within structure -- is the architectural achievement. Too much freedom produces chaos; too much structure produces format radio. Cole found the middle.
- **Live sessions:** KEXP's in-studio live sessions, which have become the station's most visible global product (hundreds of millions of YouTube views), are a programming innovation that Cole championed and expanded. The sessions bring artists into the studio for intimate, high-quality performances that are then distributed globally. This turned a Seattle radio station into a worldwide music discovery platform.

### The KCMU-to-KEXP Transition

Cole was instrumental in the station's transformation:

- **KCMU era:** The station operated as the University of Washington's student radio station -- freeform, experimental, underfunded. The programming quality was real but the reach was limited to the Seattle FM signal.
- **KEXP transition (2001):** The renaming and relaunch as KEXP, backed by Paul Allen's donation, required someone to define what the new station would be. Cole was central to this definition: maintaining the KCMU spirit of freeform curation while professionalizing the programming and expanding the audience.
- **The new building (2016):** KEXP's move to a purpose-built facility at Seattle Center was both a physical and programming milestone. The new building's design -- with a street-level studio visible to passersby, state-of-the-art recording facilities, and a gathering space -- reflects Cole's vision of radio as community institution, not just broadcast technology.
- **Global streaming:** Under Cole's programming direction, KEXP embraced streaming and social media early, turning the station into a global platform. The live session YouTube channel -- now one of the most-watched music channels in the world -- is a direct extension of the programming philosophy Cole built.

## TABLAM -- THE PROGRAM DIRECTOR AS ARTIST

### Kevin Cole's Musical Project

Beyond his programming role, Cole performs music under the name **Tablam**:

- **Sound:** Tablam's music is atmospheric, experimental, and textured. Electronic and ambient elements blend with guitar-based composition. The aesthetic is closer to post-rock and ambient electronica than to the indie rock or folk that dominates KEXP's core rotation.
- **Artistic identity:** Tablam reveals a dimension of Cole that the programming role doesn't fully express -- the artist's impulse to create, not just curate. The project demonstrates that Cole's relationship with music is not purely administrative; he is a musician who also happens to be a program director.
- **Low profile:** Tablam is not a high-profile project. Cole doesn't use his KEXP platform to promote Tablam -- the separation between his curatorial role and his artistic output is maintained. This restraint is itself revealing: the program director's ethics prevent self-promotion through the station's airwaves.
- **Aesthetic alignment:** Tablam's atmospheric, experimental aesthetic aligns with KEXP's curatorial values: music that rewards attention, that is compositionally interesting, that doesn't chase commercial trends. The program director's artistic output reflects the same values he curates for.

## THE KEXP CURATORIAL MODEL -- COLE'S LEGACY

### What Cole Built

Kevin Cole's legacy is the **KEXP curatorial model** itself -- a framework for how a radio station can function as a cultural institution in the streaming age:

- **Human curation as competitive advantage:** In an era of algorithmic recommendation (Spotify Discover Weekly, YouTube recommendations, Apple Music For You), KEXP's argument is that a human curator can discover and contextualize music in ways algorithms cannot. Cole built the programming framework that makes this argument concrete -- every show on KEXP is a demonstration of human curatorial superiority.
- **Local-to-global pipeline:** KEXP discovers local and regional artists (Pacific Northwest and beyond), gives them exposure through airplay and live sessions, and then distributes those sessions globally through YouTube and streaming. Cole's programming architecture created this pipeline -- the most effective artist-discovery-to-global-audience mechanism in independent radio.
- **Radio as community institution:** Cole's vision of KEXP extends beyond broadcasting to community gathering. The new building's public spaces, the station's events programming, and the integration with Seattle's cultural calendar all reflect a vision of radio as community institution. The program director's role becomes community architect.
- **Sustainability model:** KEXP's listener-supported funding model, which Cole's programming makes viable, demonstrates that quality curation can be financially sustainable without corporate ownership or advertising-driven format restrictions. The programming quality drives the listener support.
