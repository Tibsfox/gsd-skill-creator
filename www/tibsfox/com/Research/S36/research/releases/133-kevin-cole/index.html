The Programming Architect

Kevin Cole has served as KEXP’s Program Director since the station’s transformation from KCMU in the early 2000s. Where the host trio (130–132) documented KEXP’s on-air performers, degree 133 documents the architect — the person who built the stage those performers work on. Cole shaped the station’s core philosophy: human curation over algorithm, discovery over repetition, depth over breadth.

Every show on KEXP reflects Cole’s programming framework. He hired and mentored the on-air talent, developed the playlist philosophy (genuine DJ freedom within coherent station identity), championed the live in-studio sessions that became KEXP’s most visible global product (hundreds of millions of YouTube views), and guided the station through the KCMU-to-KEXP transition and the move to the purpose-built Seattle Center facility in 2016.

Tablam

Tablam — The Program Director as Artist

Tablam reveals the dimension of Cole that the programming role doesn’t fully express — the artist’s impulse to create, not just curate. Atmospheric, experimental, textured. Electronic and ambient elements blended with guitar-based composition. The sound of a person who listens deeply and creates from that listening.

Cole maintains strict separation between his curatorial role and his artistic output — Tablam is not promoted through KEXP’s airwaves. This restraint is itself revealing: the program director’s ethics prevent self-promotion through the station. The aesthetic aligns with KEXP’s values: music that rewards attention, that is compositionally interesting, that doesn’t chase commercial trends.
