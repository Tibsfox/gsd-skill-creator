## HIP-HOP ON KEXP -- THE CONTINUING MISSION

### Carrying the Nasty Nes Lineage

DJ B-Mello's hip-hop programming on KEXP is the direct continuation of a mission that Nasty Nes Rodriguez (degree 126) started on KCMU in the late 1980s:

- **The KCMU hip-hop foundation:** Nasty Nes was the first to bring dedicated hip-hop programming to KCMU, proving that the genre had a place on community radio. His show "Fresh Tracks" introduced Seattle listeners to hip-hop in an era when the genre was largely ignored by commercial radio in the Pacific Northwest. This was pioneering work.
- **The transition gap:** During the KCMU-to-KEXP transition and the station's identity refinement, hip-hop programming's status on the station evolved. The question was whether KEXP -- which was building a global identity around indie rock, live sessions, and eclectic curation -- would maintain its commitment to hip-hop as a programming pillar. B-Mello's presence answered this question affirmatively.
- **B-Mello's role:** DJ B-Mello hosts KEXP's hip-hop programming blocks, bringing the genre's breadth to the station's audience. This includes classic hip-hop, underground and independent hip-hop, Pacific Northwest hip-hop (connecting to Seattle's significant hip-hop scene), and contemporary releases. The programming is curated with the same depth and respect that characterizes all KEXP programming -- applying the station's curatorial ethos to hip-hop specifically.
- **Genre bridge:** B-Mello bridges multiple hip-hop eras and subgenres. His programming connects golden-age hip-hop (the late 1980s and 1990s that Nasty Nes first brought to KCMU) to contemporary releases, underground movements, and local artists. This breadth is essential -- KEXP's hip-hop programming is not a nostalgia trip but a living, evolving curatorial mission.

### Seattle's Hip-Hop Context

B-Mello's KEXP programming exists within Seattle's rich hip-hop ecosystem:

- **Local scene:** Seattle has produced significant hip-hop talent: Sir Mix-A-Lot, Macklemore & Ryan Lewis, Shabazz Palaces, Ishmael Butler (formerly of Digable Planets), Blue Scholars, Gifted Gab, and many others. KEXP's hip-hop programming connects the station to this local scene, providing exposure for Seattle hip-hop artists who might otherwise struggle for radio airplay.
- **Community connection:** Hip-hop programming connects KEXP to communities that the station's indie rock core might not reach. Hip-hop audiences are demographically and culturally broader than indie rock audiences, and B-Mello's programming expands KEXP's community engagement.
- **Cultural significance:** In a city whose musical identity is dominated by grunge, indie rock, and the Sub Pop narrative, KEXP's hip-hop programming is a correction -- a reminder that Seattle's musical identity includes hip-hop as a major component. B-Mello is the on-air voice of this correction.
