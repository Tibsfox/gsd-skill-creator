The Ventures

E = 5 Surf Rock / Instrumental Dolton Records
FIRST TACOMA BAND IN THE ENGINE — 100 MILLION RECORDS SOLD. The Ventures formed in 1958 in Tacoma, Washington — not Seattle. They sold an estimated 100 million records worldwide, triggered the electric guitar boom in Japan, and are the most globally impactful Pacific Northwest band of all time by units sold.

The Sound -- Walk Don't Run

The Ventures recorded “Walk Don't Run” in 1960 — a cover of a Johnny Smith jazz composition filtered through Chet Atkins’ country guitar style, then transformed into electric rock. The result is one of the most recognizable guitar instrumentals in history: three minutes of clean, reverb-drenched melodic guitar with nothing extraneous. It reached #2 on the Billboard pop chart. It has been covered more than 200 times.

“The Ventures’ guitar sound is the anti-grunge of the Pacific Northwest: clean where grunge is distorted, melodic where grunge is aggressive, open where grunge is compressed. Both sounds come from the same region. They could not be more different.”

Tacoma — First Non-Seattle Location

The Ventures bring Tacoma into the engine. 32 miles south of Seattle, at the south end of Puget Sound. Working-class city, port city, a different character than Capitol Hill or the U-District. Don Wilson was a used car salesman before the band; Bob Bogle worked construction. They formed the Ventures in a parking lot. The origin is as practical and working-class as Tacoma itself.

The Sound -- E=5, Surf Rock

E=5 is the clean middle of the engine’s scale. Surf rock requires space — the notes need room to ring and decay. The signature elements: reverb (clean, bright, notes sustaining slightly beyond their natural duration), tremolo (amplitude modulation giving the “watery” quality of surf rock), and clean tone (no distortion, articulate pick attack, crisp chord changes). Fender guitars and amplifiers are the instruments of this sound. The Ventures defined what Fender was for in the 1960s.

The Members

Don Wilson (born 1933, Tacoma)

Co-Founder

Rhythm guitar and business manager. The organizational spine. Longest-tenured member. Still active.

Bob Bogle (1934–2009)

Co-Founder

Bass and lead guitar. Original lead voice on “Walk Don't Run.” Later switched roles with Edwards. Died 2009.

Nokie Edwards (born 1935)

Lead Guitar

Primary lead guitarist on most 1960s recordings. Diverse stylist: surf, country, jazz. Left and rejoined multiple times.

Mel Taylor (1933–1996)

Drums (1962–1996)

One of the great 1960s rock drummers. Precise, swinging, authoritative. Son Leon Taylor now drums for the band.

Japan -- The Largest Audience in the World

The Band That Taught Japan to Play Electric Guitar

The Ventures were discovered in Japan through US Armed Forces radio in the early 1960s. The “Ventures guitar boom” followed: tens of thousands of Japanese musicians learned electric guitar by playing Ventures songs from tablature songbooks published in Japan in massive print runs. The Ventures have sold an estimated 30–50 million records in Japan alone. They are more popular in Japan than in the US. They continue to tour Japan to large audiences. Their influence on Japanese rock is analogous to the Beatles’ influence on American rock — they are the originary model. Rock and Roll Hall of Fame: 2008.
