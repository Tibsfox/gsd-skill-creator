## What Worked

1. **The Japan connection is the entry's most important finding.** 30-50 million records in Japan, the Ventures guitar boom, tens of thousands of Japanese musicians learning guitar from Ventures songbooks -- this is a cultural impact of extraordinary scale that is underrepresented in American rock narratives. The engine now has it.
2. **The working-class origin is correctly documented.** Don Wilson as used car salesman, Bob Bogle in construction, a duo in a Tacoma parking lot -- this origin story is the Ventures' character. It is not Capitol Hill bohemia; it is working-class Pacific Northwest practicality.
3. **The Tacoma milestone is properly noted.** First non-Seattle location in the engine. 32 miles from Seattle; a different city with a different character. The engine now spans the Puget Sound region more broadly.
4. **The anti-grunge observation is accurate and useful.** Clean tone, reverb, melodic precision -- these are the aesthetic opposite of distorted, compressed, aggressive grunge. Both sounds come from the same region. The engine now holds both poles.

## Discoveries

1. **"Walk Don't Run" was a cover three times over.** Johnny Smith (jazz guitar, original) --> Chet Atkins (country guitar) --> The Ventures (electric rock). Each iteration transformed the composition's character. The Ventures' version is now the primary version in cultural memory.
2. **The Ventures have over 100 albums and have been active for 66+ years.** The longevity is extraordinary and underappreciated. Most acts fade within a decade; the Ventures have persisted across six decades by following their audience (primarily Japan) rather than chasing an American mainstream that moved on in the late 1960s.

## Pair Analysis: The Ventures + Steelhead leap

- **Energy:** E=5 + E=5 = E=10. **EQUILIBRIUM.**
- **Theme:** METALLIC CLEAN. The Steelhead is the "metallic" salmon -- its name refers to the steel-grey of its ocean-phase coloration. Its leap is described as a "metallic splash clean jump" -- precise, clean, bright. The Ventures' guitar sound is the metallic equivalent in music: bright, clean, articulate, reverb-drenched, nothing blurred or distorted. Both are E=5: present, engaged, controlled, not excessive.
- **The anadromous connection.** Steelhead crosses the ocean and returns. The Ventures crossed the Pacific culturally -- they are an American band that built their largest audience in Japan, established a cultural presence that persists across decades, and in a real sense "returned" to the Pacific Northwest as global figures. Both organisms have a journey that goes out over open water and returns transformed.
