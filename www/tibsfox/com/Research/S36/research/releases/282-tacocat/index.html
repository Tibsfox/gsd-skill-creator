TACOCAT
A palindrome — reads the same forwards and backwards. The name is an aesthetic blueprint: structural symmetry, playful wit, the mundane made clever.

Capitol Hill Punk Pop

Tacocat formed in Capitol Hill, Seattle in 2007: Emily Nokes (vocals/guitar), Bree McKenna (bass), Eric Randall (guitar), Lelah Maupin (drums). The sound is bright punk pop — hooks that stay, feminist content embedded in subject matter rather than manifesto, and a consistent sense of humor that is the band's primary political tool.

Where Wimps at degree 281 is deadpan and dry, Tacocat at degree 282 is warm and hook-driven. Both are punk, both are feminist, both are Capitol Hill. The difference is temperature: Wimps observes with a dry eye; Tacocat delivers the observation with a melody that won't leave your head.

"The humor is not a retreat from the politics; it is the politics' delivery mechanism. The right hook makes the right observation land harder."

Hardly Art — Sub Pop's Subsidiary

Hardly Art was founded in 2007 as a Sub Pop Records subsidiary dedicated to indie pop, punk pop, and Pacific Northwest acts that existed in a different sonic space from Sub Pop's grunge-rooted main catalog. The roster — Tacocat, Childbirth (degree 283), Chastity Belt, La Luz, King Tuff — defines a specific Capitol Hill aesthetic: indie pop with feminist or politically aware sensibilities and a sense of humor.

Sub Pop has been a presence in the engine since the early degrees. Its main roster established the grunge canon; Hardly Art extends the institutional story into the 2000s-2010s indie pop moment. The label range — Mudhoney to Tacocat via Hardly Art — documents thirty years of Seattle independent music evolution.

The Hardly Art roster is also connected by personnel: Bree McKenna plays in both Tacocat and Childbirth (degree 283). The label is a network as well as a catalog.

The Feminist Content

"Crimson Wave" addresses menstruation matter-of-factly, with a chorus that stays. "Men Explain Things to Me" addresses mansplaining directly. "Dana Katherine Scully" is a tribute to The X-Files’ skeptical scientist — the woman in the room who insists on evidence. The politics in Tacocat are in the subject matter, delivered in the tone of a hook-driven punk pop song.

This is not confrontation-mode feminism — it is normalization-mode feminism. The riot grrrl tradition (Bikini Kill at degree 276) made it safe to be explicit; Tacocat makes the explicit casual. Casual is a different kind of political act.

The Bree McKenna Connection — Childbirth at 283

Tacocat’s bassist Bree McKenna co-founded Childbirth (degree 283), the Capitol Hill punk pop supergroup that also includes Julia Shapiro (Chastity Belt) and Stacy Peck. Two consecutive engine degrees share a musician — one of the tighter cross-reference links in the Capitol Hill chapter.

Discography

Strangles (2013)

Hardly Art

First Hardly Art release. Tacocat fully formed in the label relationship. Clean production, tight songs, integrated feminist content.

NVM (2014)

Hardly Art

The breakthrough. "Bridge to Hawaii," "Crimson Wave," "Psychedelic Quinceañera." The songs that established Tacocat as a defining Seattle punk pop band.

Lost Time (2016)

Hardly Art

Expanded arrangements, more dynamic range. Sharon Van Etten collaborates. The band growing within its form.

This Mess Is a Place (2019)

Sub Pop

The band moves to Sub Pop main label. Larger platform, polished production. Still Tacocat: funny, feminist, hook-driven.
