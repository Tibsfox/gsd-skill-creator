## What Worked

1. **FIRST HARDLY ART is a genuine institutional milestone.** Sub Pop's subsidiary enters the engine for the first time. The label's range -- from grunge-era founding catalog to 2007 Capitol Hill indie pop subsidiary -- is now visible in the engine's structure. Important.
2. **The palindrome as aesthetic blueprint is a real argument.** TACOCAT reads the same forwards and backwards. The structural symmetry, the playful wit, the everyday-made-clever: the name is the band in miniature.
3. **Feminist humor as normalization strategy is correctly distinguished from riot grrrl's confrontation mode.** Both traditions are feminist; they operate differently. Tacocat's "Crimson Wave" makes menstruation casual in a punk pop hook; Bikini Kill's "Suck My Left One" confronts directly. Both political; different registers.
4. **The Bree McKenna link to Childbirth (283) is one of the engine's tighter cross-references.** Two consecutive degrees share a musician. Capitol Hill micro-network documented.

## Discoveries

1. **"Dana Katherine Scully" is underappreciated as a feminist statement.** Scully is a medical doctor and FBI scientist who is perpetually rational and perpetually under-credited. A tribute song is exactly the right form for this tribute.
2. **Hardly Art's roster connections by personnel (McKenna, Shapiro across bands) make it a network as much as a label.**
