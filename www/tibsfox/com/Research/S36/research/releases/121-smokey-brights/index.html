Smokey Brights

E = 5 Indie Electronic Self-Released Capitol Hill

The Moody Pulse — Synth-Forward Atmosphere

Kim West (vocals, synthesizers) and Ryan Devlin (guitar, production). Capitol Hill, 2014–present. Dense synthesizer layers, breathy vocals, guitar-as-texture, programmed rhythms. The music is smokey (dark, hazy, atmospheric) and bright (melodic hooks, vocal clarity, harmonic luminescence). Self-released. DIY. The moody pulse throbs beneath everything.

The Sound

Smokey Brights builds from synthesizer-forward production: analog-inflected pads, arpeggiated sequences, sub-bass warmth creating atmospheric beds. Kim West’s smoky, breathy vocals anchor the human center — intimate delivery against dense electronic architecture. Ryan Devlin’s guitar is textural, not rhythmic: reverb-soaked clean tones, tremolo-picked ambience, closer to dream-pop than indie rock. Drum machines pulse at moderate tempo. The sound is dark, melodic, and immersive.

The name captures the aesthetic: something hazy and obscured (“smokey”) combined with something luminous and vivid (“brights”). Dark atmospheres lit by bright melodic hooks. Murky synthesizer beds punctuated by clear vocal lines. The tension between obscurity and clarity.

Capitol Hill — The DIY Approach

Smokey Brights is a Capitol Hill band in the deepest sense — emerged from the neighborhood’s creative ferment, playing the small venues (Barboza, Neumos, Sunset Tavern, house shows) that constitute its musical ecosystem. All releases self-released through Bandcamp and streaming platforms. Home production. No label mediation. KEXP sessions for broader exposure. The visual aesthetic is band-controlled: dark, moody, atmospheric with flashes of saturated color.

Capitol Hill returns at degree 121 after a one-degree break. The Capitol Hill streak (110–115) ended at 116 (Seattle proper). Degrees 117–119 were Seattle generic. Degree 120 reached Ellensburg (east of the Cascades). At 121, the engine returns to its gravitational center. Capitol Hill is home base — the engine orbits it, departing and returning.

Discography

Wild Rites (2016) — debut full-length. Core sound established: synth-forward, atmospheric, Kim West’s vocals as human center. I Love You But Damn (2018) — the refinement. Darker, denser. Guitar recedes further into the synth wash. Time Is a Crooked Bow (2020) — most atmospheric. Spacious, reverberant, introspective. Each release deepens the atmospheric commitment. The trajectory: greater density, darker mood, more sophisticated synthesis.
