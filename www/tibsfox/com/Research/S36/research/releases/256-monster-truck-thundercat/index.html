The Name

Monster Truck Thundercat is a compound of two 1980s cultural references. Monster trucks — oversized vehicles on enormous tires, born from Bob Chandler's Bigfoot (1975) and institutionalized by Monster Jam — are a distinctly American form of arena spectacle: enormous, loud, deliberately excessive. Thundercats (1985-1987, Rankin/Bass) were the humanoid cat aliens of 1980s animated television, carrying the Sword of Omens, shouting "ThunderCats, Ho!" from Third Earth. The compound name is knowingly excessive: the genre convention of stoner rock naming — which runs toward the grandiose, the nostalgic, and the deliberately silly — fully embraced.

"Stoner rock band names are their own genre: Earthride, Riffmaster General, Bongzilla, Greenleaf, Stoned Jesus, Wo Fat. Monster Truck Thundercat belongs to this tradition. The name is the manifesto."

Self-Released in the Bandcamp Era

Self-released stoner rock is not marginal -- it is one of the genre's primary distribution modes. Bandcamp (founded 2008) transformed the stoner/doom underground: a band in Seattle can now have listeners in Germany, Sweden, and Italy without a label. Limited vinyl pressings (300-1000 copies, often colored vinyl) move through Bandcamp, underground record stores, and merch tables at shows. The small-venue circuit — Tractor Tavern, Highline, Barboza, and similar Seattle spaces — provides the live infrastructure without label booking. Monster Truck Thundercat operate entirely within this system.
