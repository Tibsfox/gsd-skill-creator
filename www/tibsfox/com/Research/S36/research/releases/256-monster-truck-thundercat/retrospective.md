## What Worked

1. **The name analysis is the entry's strongest contribution.** Monster trucks (Bigfoot, 1975) and Thundercats (1985-1987) as compound references situates the band precisely within stoner rock's naming tradition -- knowingly excessive, 1980s nostalgic, deadpan humorous. The naming tradition itself is documented.
2. **The institutional spectrum (253-256) as analytical frame.** Sargent House → Hydra Head → Heavy Psych Sounds → Self-Released: the four-degree arc documents the complete institutional range of Pacific Northwest heavy music. This is one of the engine's more useful sequential analyses.
3. **The double stoner degree documented.** Two consecutive Seattle stoner bands at E=6, different institutional positions. The engine demonstrates: institutional position does not determine musical quality or energy.
4. **The storm arc opening noted.** The asymmetric pairing with Winter Storm Surf (SPS 256, E=8) is intentional. The storm is bigger than the music at this moment. The engine acknowledges this.

## Discoveries

1. **Bandcamp as stoner distribution infrastructure.** The 2008 founding of Bandcamp transformed the self-released heavy psych underground more than any other platform. International reach for self-released bands is now normal, not exceptional.
2. **Limited vinyl pressing circuit.** 300-1000 copy vinyl runs through Bandcamp and underground record stores constitute a real distribution network. This is the physical infrastructure of the stoner underground.

## Pair Analysis: Monster Truck Thundercat + Winter Storm Surf

- **Energy:** E=6 + E=8 = E=14. Well above equilibrium. The highest combined S36+SPS energy in the current arc.
- **Theme:** THE STORM IS BIGGER THAN THE MUSIC (FOR NOW). Monster Truck Thundercat are playing their riffs in a rehearsal space or a small venue in Seattle. Outside, on the outer coast of Washington, 150 miles west, the Pacific Ocean is generating 25-foot swells that drive onto the beach at Cape Disappointment or the Long Beach Peninsula with the full force of a winter Pacific storm. The E=8 of the storm surf is not just louder than the E=6 of the stoner riff -- it is a different category of force entirely. The ocean does not compare itself to the music. The music does not compare itself to the ocean. The engine holds both at the same degree and asks: what does it mean to be heavy in the presence of the Pacific winter? The answer is: you continue playing, because the riff is the riff and the storm is the storm, and both are real.
