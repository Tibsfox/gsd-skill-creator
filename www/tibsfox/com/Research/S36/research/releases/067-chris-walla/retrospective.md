## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **Three-degree Death Cab study thoroughly documented.** The structural progression from band (58) to vocalist (60) to producer (67) is analytically productive. The different energy levels (E=2, E=2, E=3) reveal how each member's contribution operates at a different register.

2. **Producer-as-artist concept clearly established.** The distinction between performer-primary and producer-primary degrees is grounded in Walla's actual career: his production credits are his primary artistic statement. The cross-degree production credits are verifiable through discography databases.

3. **Clean room acoustic element grounded in audible characteristics.** The clean room is described through specific production qualities: transparency, dynamic range, emotional precision, space. These are audible in Walla's production work -- listening to Death Cab's first four albums and comparing with post-Walla albums demonstrates the clean room aesthetic.

4. **Cross-degree production credits analytically productive.** The links from degree 67 to degrees 58 and 59 reveal the producer as invisible architecture -- connecting the engine's separate studies through a single sonic aesthetic.

5. **Departed member pattern analytically distinct from prior patterns.** The departed member is not the Export (geographic departure) or the wanderer (city accumulation). It is creative departure -- leaving a band, not a city. The distinction is clear and productive.

6. **Barsuk diaspora documented as structural concept.** Three trajectories from one label: major (Death Cab), self-release (Gibbard), self-release (Walla). The pattern reveals how artists respond differently to the same institutional starting point.

7. **Controlled variable blue note grounded in production analysis.** The blue note of precision encountering uncontainable emotion is distinct from the howl at the edge (performer's failure to contain) and other prior blue notes. The producer's blue note is a new category.

8. **Energy calibration accurate.** E=3 correctly captures the middle register of Walla's solo work -- neither the ache of E=2 (Death Cab, Gibbard) nor the power of E=4 (Case). The producer's energy level.

9. **Production credits extensively documented.** Death Cab (4 albums), The Decemberists (2), Tegan and Sara (1), Nada Surf (1), The Long Winters (1), Hot Hot Heat (1), Ra Ra Riot (1). All verifiable through AllMusic and discography databases.

10. **Cross-references well-established.** Five cross-references: Death Cab (58, band vs. departed), Gibbard (60, parallel solo), Long Winters (59, production), Sykes (64, Barsuk), Earl (55, producer network). All productive analytically.

### Source Quality

11. **AllMusic and Pitchfork provide comprehensive Walla documentation.** Production credits, biography, solo discography extensively available.

12. **Death Cab departure coverage provides context.** The 2014 departure was widely covered in music press. Amicable but definitive.

13. **Production credits verifiable through multiple sources.** AllMusic, Discogs, and album liner notes confirm all production credits cited.
