Born August 23, 1986, in Seattle. Raised in the Central District — same neighborhood as Hendrix. Same middle school (Washington). Adopted by his aunt at age four after parents’ drug addiction. A child of the state — literally, legally, emotionally. Self-taught guitarist. Gospel and soul household. Grunge and Hendrix through the speakers. Discovered by Sir Mix-a-Lot at a dive bar, 2012. A decade of club shows in Seattle. Child of the State (2021) — “Mercy” No. 1 Mainstream Rock. Two chart-toppers. Fender Next class. Played Hendrix’s Woodstock Stratocaster at the Experience Hendrix Tour, Seattle, 2024. “Rock and roll is a creation of Black America.” The Inheritor — the tradition didn’t die. It went underground for a generation. He brought it back.

Career Timeline

1986 — Born

August 23, University of Washington Medical Center, Seattle. Mother age 19. Father absent. Both parents struggled with drug addiction.

1986–1993 — Foster Care & Adoption

Placed under state supervision by age four. Adopted by aunt and uncle. Legal adoption finalized around age seven. “Child of the State” — literally.

Childhood — Central District

Raised in gospel and soul household. Learned piano and drums. Guitar as birthday gift from biological mother. Attended Washington Middle School — same school as Hendrix. First saw Hendrix mural on the wall.

2000s — Self-Taught Guitarist

Absorbed Hendrix, Nirvana, Soundgarden, Michael Jackson, Freddie King, B.B. King, Roy Buchanan, Mike McCready, Kim Thayil. Seattle as “a living, breathing master class for the aspiring artist.”

2010 — Ayron Jones and the Way

Founded power trio with DeAndre Enrico (bass) and Kai Van De Pitte (drums). Named after Hendrix Experience / Cream / SRV format.

2012 — Discovered by Sir Mix-a-Lot

University District dive bar, sleepy Wednesday night. Mix heard the music from outside: “three 50-year-old white dudes” based on the sound. Found three young Black men playing blues and rock.

2013 — Dream

Self-released debut album, produced by Sir Mix-a-Lot.

2010–2020 — The Seattle Circuit

Hundreds of club shows. Opening slots for B.B. King, Rolling Stones, Jeff Beck, Living Colour, Guns N’ Roses, Slipknot. A decade of apprenticeship in one city.

2020 — Big Machine / John Varvatos

Signs to Big Machine Label Group / John Varvatos Records. “Take Me Away” debuts Top 5 on Active Rock chart.

2021 — Child of the State

Major-label debut (May 21). “Mercy” reaches No. 1 on Billboard Mainstream Rock Airplay. Fender Next class of 2021. One of the few Black solo rock artists to top the chart.

2023 — Chronicles of the Kid

Second major-label album. “Blood in the Water” reaches No. 1 Mainstream Rock. Covers Elmore James’ “The Sky Is Crying.”

2024 — Experience Hendrix Tour

Participates in the Experience Hendrix Tour. At the Seattle kickoff, plays Hendrix’s actual Woodstock Fender Stratocaster. The living Seattle inheritor, playing the dead legend’s instrument, in the city they share.
