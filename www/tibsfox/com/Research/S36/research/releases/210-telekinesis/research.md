## MERGE RECORDS ENTERS -- LABEL STAGE 210

### The Durham Connection

Merge Records' appearance at degree 210 is a major label milestone:

- **Merge Records.** Founded in 1989 by Mac McCaughan and Laura Ballance (both of Superchunk) in Durham, North Carolina. One of the most respected independent labels in American music. Merge's roster includes Arcade Fire, Neutral Milk Hotel, Spoon, The Magnetic Fields, and Caribou.
- **The significance.** Merge is not a Seattle label. It's a Durham label that signed a Seattle artist. This is the reverse of the usual pattern -- instead of a local label discovering local talent, a distant label reached across the country for Telekinesis. The quality signal: Merge doesn't sign casually.
- **Label theory impact.** After the Hardly Art streak (201-204), which documented a Sub Pop subsidiary's community curation, Merge enters as an example of national indie label reach. Hardly Art curates locally. Merge scouts nationally. Both models work. Different scales, different networks.
- **One-man-band on a label.** Telekinesis is essentially Michael Benjamin Lerner's solo project, though he uses collaborators live and in studio. Merge signing a one-man project signals confidence in the songwriter rather than the band dynamic.

(Sources: Merge Records founding; Mac McCaughan/Laura Ballance; Superchunk; Merge roster; label theory)

## MICHAEL BENJAMIN LERNER -- THE SONGWRITER

### One Person, Many Instruments

Telekinesis is a project centered on one songwriter:

- **Michael Benjamin Lerner.** The creative core of Telekinesis. Lerner writes, records, and often performs all instruments on the recordings. The project name ("Telekinesis") reflects moving things with the mind -- an apt metaphor for a solo artist who creates the illusion of a full band.
- **Capitol Hill roots.** Lerner is based in Capitol Hill, Seattle -- the same neighborhood that produced Chastity Belt, Tacocat, Childbirth, and dozens of other entries in the 360. But where those bands are products of the feminist punk network, Telekinesis is a solo operation. Same neighborhood. Different mode.
- **Power pop lineage.** Lerner's songwriting draws from power pop tradition: big hooks, tight arrangements, melodic precision. Think Big Star, Cheap Trick, Matthew Sweet. The songs are carefully constructed to deliver maximum melody in minimum time.
- **Studio craft.** Despite being largely a solo recording project, Telekinesis recordings sound full and polished. Lerner's multi-tracking creates dense, layered arrangements that belie their one-person origin. The craft is in making the solo sound communal.

(Sources: Michael Benjamin Lerner biography; Telekinesis project structure; power pop lineage; Capitol Hill scene)

## THE SOUND OF TELEKINESIS

### Indie Pop as Craft

Telekinesis delivers precision pop:

- **Hooks.** The hooks come first. Every Telekinesis song is built around a melodic hook -- usually in the guitar line, reinforced by the vocal melody. The hook is the load-bearing element. Everything else supports it.
- **Drums.** Lerner is primarily a drummer, and the drum parts in Telekinesis recordings are exceptional -- tight, dynamic, and driving. The drumming gives Telekinesis more momentum than typical indie pop. The rhythm section pushes.
- **Guitars.** Clean to moderately distorted. Jangly in verses, fuller in choruses. The guitar tone is bright and present -- not buried in reverb like shoegaze, not distorted like punk. This is pop guitar: clear, melodic, purposeful.
- **Vocals.** Lerner's vocals are warm and slightly earnest. Not ironic. Not detached. The vocal delivery is direct and emotionally transparent -- a contrast to the deadpan style common in Seattle indie.
- **Song length.** Short. 2:30-3:30 minutes. Efficient. The songs arrive, deliver the hook, develop briefly, and end. No extended outros. No ambient padding. Pop economy.
- **Production.** Clean and full. Multiple guitar tracks, layered harmonies, precise drum sounds. The production values are higher than typical DIY indie -- this is where the Merge investment shows.

(Sources: Telekinesis discography; power pop production; indie pop songwriting; drum-forward arrangements)

## E=4 -- RESTRAINED POWER

### Calibration

Telekinesis at E=4 is melodic energy held in check:

- **Why E=4?** Telekinesis is energetic but controlled. The hooks are catchy, the drums are driving, but the overall dynamic stays moderate. This is not the explosive energy of punk (E=6-7) or the quiet intensity of folk (E=2-3). E=4 is pop energy: bright, engaging, never overwhelming. The power is in the precision, not the volume.
- **Power pop paradox.** Power pop is paradoxical: it's about delivering maximum emotional impact at moderate volume. The "power" is in the songwriting, not the amplifier. E=4 captures this -- powerful hooks delivered with restraint.
- **Comparison.** E=4 matches Chastity Belt (201) -- both are E=4 Capitol Hill acts. But Chastity Belt's E=4 is slack and ironic, while Telekinesis's E=4 is tight and earnest. Same energy, opposite postures. The E=4 spectrum is wide.

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of Telekinesis

- **Tempo.** Moderate to upbeat. 120-150 BPM. Pop tempos. Moving but not frantic.
- **Guitar.** Clean to moderately distorted. Jangly verses, fuller choruses. Bright, present, melodic. Multiple tracked layers.
- **Bass.** Melodic, supportive. Following the chord changes with movement. Not flashy but well-crafted.
- **Vocals.** Warm, slightly earnest, emotionally transparent. Layered harmonies in choruses. Not ironic. Not detached.
- **Drums.** Exceptional. Tight, dynamic, driving. Lerner is a drummer first. The drums are the engine of each song.
- **Production.** Clean, full, polished. Merge-level quality. Multiple guitar tracks. Layered harmonies. Precise drum sounds. Studio craft that makes solo recording sound like a band.
- **Song length.** Short. 2:30-3:30 minutes. Pop economy. Hook delivery systems.
