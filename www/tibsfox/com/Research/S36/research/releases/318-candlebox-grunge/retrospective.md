## What Worked

1. **The Maverick Records paradox is the entry's most analytically productive frame.** Grunge defined itself against the mainstream pop industry. Candlebox signed to a label founded by the most commercially successful pop artist of the era. The paradox is not incidental; it is structural. The label context explains the commercial reach and the critical skepticism simultaneously.
2. **The commercial record is documented without the critical disqualification logic.** 4 million copies. This is a historical fact. The engine records it alongside the critical establishment's dismissal, presenting both as facts in the record, without applying the critical logic to discount the commercial achievement.
3. **Kevin Martin's classic-rock vocal inheritance is correctly identified as the acoustic basis for commercial reach.** Robert Plant and Paul Rodgers are the lineage. This heritage gave Candlebox dual accessibility: heavy enough for the grunge audience entering from punk-metal, melodic enough for the classic-rock FM audience that Nirvana's rougher edges couldn't reach. The 4 million is partly an acoustics story.
4. **E=7 consecutive cooldown is correctly calibrated.** The post-summit sequence (317: Temple of the Dog, E=7; 318: Candlebox, E=7) is a stable cooldown from E=8 (Gruntruck, 316). ## Discoveries

1. **Candlebox and Alanis Morissette on the same label in 1993-1995 is the Maverick commercial context.** Maverick's commercial breakthrough records were Candlebox (1993, 4M) and Jagged Little Pill (1995, 33M). Both are grunge-adjacent artists whose commercial success exceeded the critical establishment's expectations. Maverick's model was not credibility-first; it was commercial reach first, and both artists delivered. The label's success is partly the story of signing artists the critical establishment undervalued.
2. **The commercial-critical inversion in grunge is most visible with Candlebox.** Alice in Chains' Dirt (6M) is canonical; Candlebox's debut (4M) is not. The musical and commercial difference is small (6M vs. 4M). The critical difference is vast. The only explanation is non-musical: Alice in Chains' critical capital (Seattle royalty, Layne Staley's suffering, post-punk lineage) vs. Candlebox's critical deficit (classic-rock roots, Maverick label). The engine documents the inversion without resolving it.
