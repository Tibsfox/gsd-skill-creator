## What Worked

1. **Green River's historical significance requires depth, and the depth is warranted.** The split is the most consequential event in Seattle rock history. The personnel in the band at one time contained the founding members of Mudhoney and the founding members of Pearl Jam. This is not a small claim -- it is documented fact, and the documentation at degree 294 provides the full context.
2. **The *Deep Six* compilation is correctly treated as the founding document.** Before Sub Pop was commercial, before grunge had a name, before the music press arrived from London -- *Deep Six* (1986, C/Z Records) is the scene in one recording. Green River is in it. Degrees 294 and 295 (Green River and Malfunkshun) are both *Deep Six* bands, consecutive in the engine because consecutive in the story.
3. **The creative fault line analysis is essential.** Mark Arm/Steve Turner and Stone Gossard/Jeff Ament had genuinely incompatible visions for the band. Both visions were legitimate. The conflict was creative, not personal. The outcome was not a failure -- it was the mechanism through which both visions found their proper form. Mudhoney is the Arm/Turner vision fully realized; Mother Love Bone and Pearl Jam are the Gossard/Ament vision fully realized. The split made both possible.
4. **Capitol Hill as provenance is correctly maintained.** Green River from Capitol Hill; the engine's most productive neighborhood, the densest concentration of creative friction in Seattle's music history. This is where proto-grunge was built.
