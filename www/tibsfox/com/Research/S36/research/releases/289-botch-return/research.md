## WE ARE THE ROMANS -- IN FULL CONTEXT

### The Record That Grows

*We Are the Romans* (1999, Hydra Head) was covered in the degree 254 entry. The return at 289 places it in additional context:

- **The record's growth since 1999.** When *We Are the Romans* was released in 1999, it was recognized immediately as technically exceptional. But its full stature emerged gradually -- by 2005, it was cited constantly by younger bands as the template. By 2010, it appeared on every serious list of essential heavy music. By 2022 (Botch reunion shows), it was a fully canonical document, its influence pervasive across the entire landscape of extreme music. A record that gets bigger over time is a record that was ahead of its moment.
- **The closing track.** "Queen of the Surface Streets" -- the album's final track and longest -- is one of the most remarkable closers in post-hardcore. The track uses every element of Botch's technical vocabulary: the time signature displacement, the contrapuntal bass, the vocal ferocity. And then it ends not with resolution but with interruption, as if the music has exceeded its container. The album does not conclude; it stops, mid-momentum. This is a compositional decision: Botch refuse to offer the listener the satisfaction of a tidy close.
- **"Mondrian Was a Liar."** Named after Piet Mondrian, whose rectilinear color field paintings are among the most rigidly geometric in 20th-century art. The irony of the title -- Mondrian, who dedicated his life to geometric truth, is named a liar in a track characterized by the most rigorous mathematical application of rhythm -- is characteristic of Botch's intelligence. They mock the intellectual pretension they simultaneously embody.
- **Tacoma as recording context.** *We Are the Romans* was recorded at Avast! Recording Co. in Seattle (formerly Egg Studios), working with producers Stuart Hallerman and Matt Anderson. The studio's location in Seattle while the band came from Tacoma is characteristic: Tacoma bands recorded in Seattle because Seattle had the infrastructure. The Tacoma-Seattle axis runs through the album's production history.
- **The lyrics.** Botch's lyrical approach is Catholic, apocalyptic, and oblique. The imagery runs to water, religious figures, and confrontation. Dave Verellen does not explain -- the words arrive as physical events alongside the instrumental attacks. The lyrical opacity is not evasion; it is correct match with the music's refusal to be immediately legible.

(Sources: *We Are the Romans* 1999; "Queen of the Surface Streets" analysis; "Mondrian Was a Liar" title reference; Avast! Recording Co. Seattle; Stuart Hallerman and Matt Anderson; lyrical analysis; Catholic and apocalyptic imagery; canonical status 2005-2022)

## THE BRIAN COOK BRIDGE

### From Botch to These Arms Are Snakes

The most important cross-reference at degree 289 is the Brian Cook connection to degree 290:

- **Cook's role in Botch.** Brian Cook's bass in Botch is not a support instrument -- it is a contrapuntal voice that sometimes operates at a different rhythmic layer from the guitars, adding polyrhythmic complexity in an already complex sonic environment. Cook plays bass lines that are rhythmically independent from the drums and guitars without losing the music's coherence. This is extremely difficult to execute.
- **Cook's departure and arrival.** After Botch dissolved in 2002, Cook co-founded These Arms Are Snakes in Seattle. The move from Botch to TAAS is a move from Tacoma's industrial mathematical ferocity to Capitol Hill's more dynamic, post-punk-influenced post-hardcore. The music changes; the technical intelligence and commitment to the genre remain.
- **These Arms Are Snakes (2003-2009).** TAAS occupied a different sonic space than Botch: more dynamic range, more groove, more influence from post-punk and noise rock. Where Botch operated primarily at maximum intensity, TAAS varied their approach -- aggressive when the song demanded it, quiet when the song demanded that. The Brian Cook trajectory shows how the mathcore tradition could evolve without losing its core intelligence.
- **Russian Circles (2007-present).** After TAAS dissolved in 2009, Cook joined Russian Circles -- a Chicago post-metal instrumental trio. Russian Circles' music is the meditation version of Botch's attack: the same technical intelligence, the same commitment to structure and composition, deployed at lower energy and longer time scale. Cook has now been the connective tissue between three bands that, taken together, map the evolution of Pacific Northwest post-hardcore from its mathcore peak to its post-metal diffusion.
- **The cook trajectory as music history.** Botch (1993-2002) → These Arms Are Snakes (2002-2009) → Russian Circles (2007-present). This is a career that demonstrates how musical intelligence can evolve across genres without losing its fundamental character.

(Sources: Brian Cook bass in Botch; polyrhythmic bass role; These Arms Are Snakes 2003-2009; Capitol Hill TAAS; Russian Circles Chicago post-metal; Cook trajectory as genre map; Botch to TAAS dynamic range comparison)

## DAVE KNUDSON AND MINUS THE BEAR

### The Second Arc of Influence

Dave Knudson's post-Botch work is equally revealing:

- **Minus the Bear (2001-2018).** Knudson founded Minus the Bear in Seattle in 2001 -- before Botch had even dissolved -- with vocalist Jake Snider and drummer Erin Tate. Where Botch was mathematical aggression, Minus the Bear is melodic, intricate, indie rock with technical guitar work. The same angular riff construction that makes Botch's music mathematically complex appears in Minus the Bear's songs -- but applied to hook-driven, accessible music.
- **The genre distance.** From *We Are the Romans* to *Highly Refined Pirates* (2003, Minus the Bear debut): both records feature extremely precise guitar work, unusual time signatures applied without academic self-consciousness, and a drummer who can navigate complex rhythmic territory. The instruments know what they are doing in both cases. The emotional register is completely different.
- **Knudson's guitar vocabulary.** The angular riffs, the rhythmic displacement, the pattern-based approach to the guitar as a rhythmic and harmonic instrument simultaneously: these techniques appear in both Botch and Minus the Bear. Knudson did not abandon what he learned in Botch; he applied it to different music. This is what technical musicianship enables.
- **Technical guitar as a value.** The Knudson arc demonstrates that the technical guitar tradition in Pacific Northwest music is not genre-limited. The same musician can produce mathcore's most extreme examples and indie rock's most intricate melodic work. The technical vocabulary is transferable.

(Sources: Dave Knudson guitar biography; Minus the Bear 2001 formation; *Highly Refined Pirates* 2003; Jake Snider; Erin Tate; technical guitar transfer between Botch and Minus the Bear; Pacific Northwest guitar tradition)

## MATHCORE -- THE FULL PICTURE

### Technical Extreme Music at Its Peak

The degree 289 return is an opportunity to assess mathcore in full:

- **Mathcore's defining characteristics.** Unusual time signatures (7/8, 11/8, 5/4, and rapid changes between them); polyrhythm; extreme tempo variation within a single track; the riff as rhythmic attack rather than melodic hook; the voice as instrument rather than melodic vehicle; loud-quiet-loud dynamics used at extreme range. All of these appear in Botch's music.
- **Botch vs. Dillinger Escape Plan.** These are the two peaks of the mathcore genre. DEP (New Jersey, 1997-2017) is more relentlessly technical -- every moment is at maximum complexity. Botch is more compositionally controlled -- the complexity serves the song's emotional arc. DEP impresses; Botch devastates. Both are essential documents; they achieve their effects through different applications of the same technical vocabulary.
- **The Converge parallel.** Converge's *Jane Doe* (2001, Deathwish Inc.) is the East Coast equivalent of *We Are the Romans*: both are extreme post-hardcore records of technical complexity and emotional devastation, released within two years of each other. Converge and Botch are frequently cited together as the twin peaks of the genre's creative moment. Both bands were active at the same time; neither was directly influenced by the other -- they arrived at similar places through different paths.
- **The genre's Pacific Northwest roots.** Botch is the Pacific Northwest's primary contribution to mathcore. The genre has roots in Washington, DC (Fugazi, Dischord Records), Louisville (Slint), and Providence (Lightning Bolt) -- but its most technically demanding and emotionally devastating instantiation came from Tacoma. This is unexpected. Tacoma is not a city associated with avant-garde musical intellectualism. But Botch made it the home of American mathcore's peak.

(Sources: Mathcore defining characteristics; Dillinger Escape Plan comparison; DEP vs. Botch emotional vs. technical; Converge *Jane Doe* 2001; Deathwish Inc.; Fugazi Dischord; Slint Louisville; Lightning Bolt Providence; Tacoma as mathcore home)

## REUNION CONTEXT -- 2016 AND 2022

### The Returns That Confirmed the Stature

- **2016 reunion shows.** Botch reunited in 2016 for a limited run of shows. The response was extraordinary: *We Are the Romans* had grown so far in stature in the fourteen years since dissolution that the band's return was treated as a major cultural event in heavy music. The shows sold out. The music, performed live, was as technically demanding and emotionally devastating as the record. No new studio material.
- **2022 reunion shows.** A second reunion in 2022. By this point Botch's status was beyond argument: the twenty-three-year gap between *We Are the Romans* (1999) and the 2022 shows had only confirmed the record's place in heavy music's canon. The band performed *We Are the Romans* in its entirety at some shows.
- **The significance of no new studio material.** Both reunion tours produced no new recordings. This is a deliberate and correct decision: Botch's catalog is complete and finished. The reunion shows allow audiences to experience the music live without risking the dilution that often comes with late-career studio recordings. The catalog remains as it was. This is integrity.
- **The influence confirmed.** By the 2022 shows, the musicians in the audience who cited Botch as a primary influence were themselves established artists: members of Converge, Deafheaven, Portrayal of Guilt, Full of Hell, and dozens of other significant heavy bands were in the rooms. Botch influenced the bands that influenced the next generation. The transmission is documented.

(Sources: 2016 Botch reunion shows; 2022 Botch reunion; *We Are the Romans* performed live; no new studio recordings; audience at reunion shows; influence chain Botch → multiple generations)
