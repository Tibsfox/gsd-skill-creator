## What Worked

1. **The no-vocals analysis is the most formally precise entry in the accipiter arc.** The decision to have no vocalist is not a limitation but an aesthetic position -- it displaces the entire communicative weight onto the instruments. Documenting this correctly frames Hovercraft as a formal choice rather than a deficiency.
2. **The post-rock / shoegaze / drone lineage is accurately placed.** My Bloody Valentine, Slowdive, Sonic Youth, Glenn Branca, Tortoise, Slint -- these are the correct reference points for understanding what Hovercraft was doing within the 1990s experimental rock tradition. The lineage gives the band a context larger than the Seattle scene.
3. **The DGC anomaly analysis extends the label-history thread.** DGC (Nirvana's major label) signing a no-vocals art rock duo is one of the post-Nirvana expansion's more extreme examples. The Tag subsidiary model (DGC's experimental imprint) explains the mechanism.
4. **The accipiter trio closes at 227.** Degree 225 (largest), 226 (medium), 227 (smallest). The structural descent from Goshawk to Sharp-shin mirrors the S36 sequence from supergroup to space rock to wordless duo. The parallelism is the engine's most elegant four-degree architecture to date.

## Discoveries

1. **The album title *Akathisia* rewards analysis.** Akathisia is a medical condition of motor restlessness -- inability to remain still. Applied to a band whose music is dense, sustained, and deliberately patient, the title is an ironic inversion: the name names restlessness while the music embodies stillness-within-density. This kind of clinical-vocabulary-as-title is consistent with a band interested in formal precision.
2. **Beth Liebling's personal connection to Pearl Jam (marriage to Eddie Vedder) gave Hovercraft visibility within the Capitol Hill community.** This biographical fact does not define the music, but it is a factual element of the band's context. The engine documents it without overweighting it.
3. **The live experience of instrumental rock at concert volume is different from vocal rock at the same volume.** Without lyrical content, the listener's response is more somatic -- physically immersive without the cognitive mediation of words. Hovercraft's live performances are documented as specifically high-volume and physically engaging. This is a different listener mode than even the loudest vocal rock.
