## What Worked

1. **U-District as first entry is historically grounded.** The Ave produced psychedelic rock, not grunge. Love Battery is the correct band for this neighborhood's debut.
2. **Jason Finn dual membership is one of the engine's more surprising cross-references.** The distance between Love Battery's heavy psychedelia and PUSA's melodic pop-rock comedy is vast. Finn navigated it.
3. **Sub Pop diversity is documented.** The label is not just grunge -- Love Battery proves the range.
4. **E=5 streak continues (219-221, three degrees).** The post-predator arc holds its energy: spacious, weighted, present.

## Discoveries

1. **Ron Nine's guitar approach is distinct from all previous Seattle guitarists in the engine.** Not Thayil's metal precision, not Peterson's punk immediacy. Psychedelic expansiveness. A third vocabulary.
2. **Love Battery's commercial obscurity is proportionate to their artistic specificity.** The more specific the sound, the smaller the commercial ceiling. This is a pattern across the engine.
