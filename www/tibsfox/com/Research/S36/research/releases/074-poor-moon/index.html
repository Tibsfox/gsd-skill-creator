The Side Project: Fleet Foxes Connection Returns

Degree 74 introduces Poor Moon — a band whose significance is inseparable from its relationship to Fleet Foxes (degree 53). Poor Moon is a side project: a band formed by members of a bigger act, creating music outside their primary group’s identity. Christian Wargo and Casey Wescott are both members of Fleet Foxes — the band that gave the engine its communal harmony at degree 53.

This is the engine’s second connection to degree 53. The first was through genre and aesthetic influence. Degree 74 makes the connection literal — same musicians, different band. This is the strongest inter-degree connection in the engine: not influence, not genre overlap, not label shared, but shared personnel.

Where Fleet Foxes built soaring multi-part vocal harmonies that dissolved individual voices into collective sound, Poor Moon creates dreamy, reverb-heavy indie folk/rock — spacier, more psychedelic, more atmospheric. The communal harmony (53) is replaced by the reverb wash. The choir becomes the echo.

The side project suggests that an artist is not a fixed entity but a collection of possibilities — different band configurations unlock different musical registers. Wargo in Fleet Foxes is part of a choir. Wargo in Poor Moon is part of a duo. Same voice, different architecture.

Sub Pop Returns: Second Sub Pop Artist

Degree 74 marks the second appearance of Sub Pop Records in the engine. The first Sub Pop artist was Sam Beam (Iron & Wine) at degree 68. Sub Pop at degree 68 was an institutional connection — a Southern artist connected to Seattle through the label. Sub Pop at degree 74 is an organic connection — a Seattle band on Seattle’s defining label.

That Poor Moon — a Fleet Foxes side project — would release on Sub Pop is almost gravitational: Fleet Foxes are Sub Pop artists, and the side project orbits the same label. Sub Pop becomes a recurring institution in the engine — a label that hosts multiple artists across the engine’s arc.

Sub Pop at degree 74 contrasts with Jagjaguwar at degree 72: Jagjaguwar is the national indie label (Indianapolis). Sub Pop is the local indie label (Seattle) — rooted in the city, defining the city, inseparable from Seattle’s musical identity. Both independent. Different geographies of independence.

One-Album Band: The Temporal Artifact

Poor Moon released one self-titled album: Poor Moon (2012, Sub Pop). The band is no longer active. This makes Poor Moon the engine’s second defunct/ended artist after Pickwick (degree 62).

Poor Moon’s brevity is explained by its side-project nature. The band formed in the gap between Fleet Foxes’ Helplessness Blues (2011) and Crack-Up (2017). When Fleet Foxes resumed, Poor Moon’s creative space closed. The side project is a temporal artifact: it exists in the gaps between a bigger act’s work, filling the silence with different music.
