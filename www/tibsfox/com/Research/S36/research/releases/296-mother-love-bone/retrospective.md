## What Worked

1. **The four-degree arc (293-296) functions as a complete origin story.** Swallow (293) establishes the Sub Pop wave context. Green River (294) is the origin band. Malfunkshun (295) is the synthesis and the voice. Mother Love Bone (296) is the convergence and the pivot. Four consecutive degrees tell the complete story from the wave to the event that generated Pearl Jam and Temple of the Dog.
2. **The *Apple* album requires full track analysis.** "Stardog Champion," "Crown of Thorns," "Stargazer," "Gentle Groove" are not background details -- they are evidence of what Mother Love Bone was, what the Seattle scene could have become commercially with Wood at the center, and what the Cornell-recorded "Crown of Thorns" on *Temple of the Dog* preserved. The album is the artifact.
3. **The chain documentation table is the right format for the lineage.** Four rows: Green River (294), Malfunkshun (295), Mother Love Bone (296), Temple of the Dog (225), Pearl Jam. The chain is traceable from the engine at consecutive degrees. The table makes the structure visible.
4. **Andrew Wood's death is handled correctly: factual, brief, focused on the consequences for the music.** The fact: March 19, 1990, heroin overdose, age 24. The consequence: Cornell wrote Temple of the Dog. Cornell recorded "Crown of Thorns." Pearl Jam formed. The engine's obligation is documentation of the music and its history, not lingering on loss. The documentation is honest about what happened and focused on what followed.
5. **Stardog Records is correctly documented.** Mercury creating a dedicated imprint for Mother Love Bone was a genuine statement of major-label commitment at a scale the Seattle proto-grunge scene had not previously received. The counterfactual -- what *Apple* would have achieved commercially if Wood had lived, into the same market that received *Ten* -- is worth noting without dwelling on.

## Discoveries

1. **Terry Date's involvement places Mother Love Bone in the major-rock production tradition.** Date went on to produce Soundgarden (*Superunknown* co-production), Pantera, and dozens of major heavy rock albums. His work on *Apple* is the earliest evidence of his partnership with Seattle heavy rock -- and it produces the best-sounding record the proto-grunge scene made before the commercial era.
