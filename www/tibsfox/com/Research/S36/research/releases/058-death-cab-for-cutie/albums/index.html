Death Cab for Cutie — Discography

Ten studio albums across twenty-six years, from a four-track bedroom project in Bellingham, Washington to one of the most enduring bands in American indie rock. The arc traces the entire history of independent music's relationship with the mainstream: Barsuk Records to Atlantic Records and back again. Chris Walla's production shaped the first six records. Ben Gibbard's songwriting moved from lo-fi confessionals to widescreen emotional landscapes. The band never stopped being a Pacific Northwest band — the geography is in the sound.

Era I — Barsuk Records (1998–2003)

Something About Airplanes

1998 · Barsuk Records

The lo-fi debut. Four-track recordings from Bellingham, Washington. Ben Gibbard's bedroom project becomes a real band. Raw, overdriven, unmistakably Pacific Northwest.

Barsuk

We Have the Facts and We’re Voting Yes

2000 · Barsuk Records

The leap from bedroom to studio. Clarity emerges from the fuzz. Gibbard's lyrical voice crystallizes around themes of romantic failure and self-deception. The indie rock album as argument.

Barsuk

The Photo Album

2001 · Barsuk Records

New wave edges sharpen the sound. Drum machines and angular guitars signal ambition beyond the indie-folk template. The band as studio project. Walla's production takes the foreground.

Barsuk

Transatlanticism

2003 · Barsuk Records

The masterpiece. Eleven songs about distance, longing, and the impossible gap between people. The album that changed everything — for the band, for Barsuk, for indie rock itself.

Barsuk

Era II — Atlantic Records (2005–2015)

Plans

2005 · Atlantic Records

The major-label debut. Mortality, marriage, and the passage of time. The sound expands to fill arenas without losing the emotional core. "I Will Follow You into the Dark" becomes the band's most-known song. Commercial peak.

Atlantic

Narrow Stairs

2008 · Atlantic Records

The dark turn. Longer songs, heavier textures, deliberate difficulty. A band pushing back against the accessibility of Plans. "I Will Possess Your Heart" as eight-minute statement of intent.

Atlantic

Codes and Keys

2011 · Atlantic Records

Synthesizers, texture, light. The brightest-sounding Death Cab record. Written during a period of personal happiness for Gibbard. The departure that divided fans.

Atlantic

Kintsugi

2015 · Atlantic Records

The final Chris Walla album. Named for the Japanese art of repairing broken pottery with gold. Divorce, departure, the beauty in broken things. Rich Costey produces alongside Walla.

Atlantic

Era III — Independent Return (2018–present)

Thank You for Today

2018 · Atlantic Records

First album without Chris Walla. New guitarist Dave Depper and touring member Zac Rae reshape the sonic palette. The band continues, reconfigured. Memory and persistence as theme and practice.

Atlantic

Asphalt Meadows

2022 · Atlantic Records

Pandemic-era album. The most sonically adventurous Death Cab record since Narrow Stairs. Noise, beauty, the persistence of making art when everything has stopped. The late-career reinvention.

Atlantic
