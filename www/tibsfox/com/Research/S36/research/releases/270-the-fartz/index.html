Duff McKagan — The Three-Quarters Link

The Fartz’s drummer was Duff McKagan — born Michael Andrew McKagan in Seattle, 1964. After The Fartz, McKagan joined Ten Minute Warning (degree 265). Then he relocated to Los Angeles and co-founded Guns N’ Roses with Axl Rose, Slash, Izzy Stradlin, and Steven Adler. Appetite for Destruction (1987) sold over 30 million copies.

The arc: The Fartz (Capitol Hill hardcore, 1981) → Ten Minute Warning (Seattle hardcore, 1983) → Guns N’ Roses (Los Angeles, 1985) → global rock stardom. The engine documents the first two steps at degrees 265 and 270.

Alternative Tentacles

The Fartz releasing on Alternative Tentacles is not incidental. Alternative Tentacles was founded in 1979 by Jello Biafra — co-founder of the Dead Kennedys, the most politically significant American punk band of the era. The label’s catalog includes the Dead Kennedys, Nomeansno, MDC, DOA, The Crucifucks: the defining bands of American political hardcore.

The Fartz appearing on Alternative Tentacles places them in this company explicitly. Biafra chose to release their music because he considered it significant. The political alignment was also genuine: Alternative Tentacles was anti-corporate, anti-authoritarian, committed to documenting hardcore across North America. The Fartz shared these commitments. This is worth stating: Seattle’s hardcore scene had real political content, not just aesthetic aggression.

Capitol Hill, 1981

Capitol Hill in 1981 was cheaper than it would become by the 1990s. The combination of affordable rent, proximity to downtown, and a community of artists, queers, and political activists made it the natural center for Seattle’s punk scene. The Fartz were rooted here — not just geographically but socially and politically. The community that produced them was the same community that would later produce the alternative rock venues, the zine culture, and the DIY infrastructure that grunge inherited.

The engine has returned to Capitol Hill many times: The Fartz (270), The U-Men (272), Ten Minute Warning (265), The Accused (266), Love Battery (221). The neighborhood’s density of cultural production is the engine’s most consistent geographic pattern. Hardcore is its earliest and rawest expression.

The Sound -- Hardcore Punk, E=8

The Fartz play fast. The defining characteristic of early 1980s American hardcore is tempo — songs at the maximum velocity the instruments can maintain. Song length: 90 seconds to two and a half minutes. Guitar: downstroke-driven power chords. Vocals: rhythmically precise aggression, syllables as percussion at maximum speed.

Blaine Cook’s vocals are not melodic in the traditional sense. They function as a percussion instrument delivering confrontational content at tempo. This is a genuinely distinct vocal tradition — different from blues-influenced rock shouting, different from punk-pop singing. Hardcore vocals are their own category, and Cook is among the best practitioners of early 1980s West Coast hardcore.
