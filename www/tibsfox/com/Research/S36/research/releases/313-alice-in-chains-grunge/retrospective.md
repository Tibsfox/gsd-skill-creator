## What Worked

1. **The dual vocal analysis is the most harmonically significant new theory in the post-Summit zone.** Cantrell and Staley built harmonics in minor thirds and tritones -- not the thirds-above harmony standard in rock. The dissonance is held, not resolved. This is the technical foundation that separates Alice in Chains from every other grunge act and establishes the E=9 calibration on sonic grounds before considering lyrical content or cultural significance.
2. **E=9 is correct.** The metal foundation (drop-D, palm muting, Cantrell's riff architecture) plus the Staley vocal weight plus Dirt's complete emotional document equals E=9. The calibration is not a sentiment -- it is structural. Soundgarden was E=9 at degree 312; Alice in Chains is E=9 at degree 313. Both entries at the metal edge of grunge deserve maximum calibration.
3. **The April 5 thread is documented with appropriate weight.** Kurt Cobain: April 5 1994. Layne Staley: April 5 2002. Same calendar date, eight years apart. Both Seattle. Both voices of irreplaceable character in their generation. The engine records this without elaboration; the coincidence speaks for itself.
4. **Dirt (1992) analysis at track level is the correct depth.** Rooster (Cantrell's father, Vietnam, anti-war through personal portrait), Would? (Andrew Wood tribute, Mother Love Bone thread), Down in a Hole (depression as spatial metaphor), Angry Chair (addiction as interior monologue). The album is a complete emotional document and warrants this treatment.

## Discoveries

1. **Alice in Chains is the engine's first documented case of a band maintaining identity after the death of its irreplaceable voice.** William DuVall joined in 2006; Black Gives Way to Blue (2009) is a legitimate continuation. Most bands cannot survive this transition. AIC's survival raises a structural question: what is the band -- the name, the music, the lineup, or the voice? The engine documents both periods with equal respect.
2. **The AIC-Mother Love Bone thread (via Would?) is the engine's strongest track-to-band cross-reference.** Would? is specifically an Andrew Wood tribute. The degree-296 Mother Love Bone entry and the degree-313 Alice in Chains entry are connected by a single song. This kind of specific, documented cross-reference is the engine's preferred form of connection.
3. **The Cantrell guitar approach was built for Staley.** The tritone substitutions, the Lydian inflections in clean passages, the harmonic structure -- all optimized for the specific interaction with Staley's voice. After 2002, Cantrell maintained the compositional approach rather than imitating the lost voice, and DuVall brought different qualities that nonetheless worked within that framework. The guitar architecture survived the vocal transition.
