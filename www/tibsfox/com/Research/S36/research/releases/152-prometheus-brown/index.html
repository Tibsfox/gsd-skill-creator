Filipino-American Identity

Seattle has one of the oldest and largest Filipino-American communities in the United States. Prometheus Brown centers this identity: colonial legacy (1898–1946), labor history (cannery workers, farm workers), Tagalog code-switching, manong generation, transnational connections (balikbayan boxes, long-distance family). The art takes the raw material of displacement and creates something new.

Beyond Blue Scholars

Without Sabzi’s production, the individual voice stands alone. More experimental. More Filipino-specific. Community projects: benefit albums, collaborative mixtapes with other Filipino-American artists. Visual art: graphic design, posters, zines. The artist-activist model with roots in the Carlos Bulosan tradition.
