Punk/Psych — The Genre Synthesis

Punk and psychedelia share 1960s roots that lead to the same place through different routes. Psychedelia found that heavy amplification and studio experimentation could produce intensity through immersion. Punk found that directness and maximum energy at minimum complexity could produce intensity through impact. Love Battery sits in the overlap: fuzz and reverb used with punk directness, songs short enough for punk economy but dense enough for psychedelic weight.

The canonical reference: the Stooges' Fun House (1970). Saxophone freakouts, distorted guitar, physical performance energy — punk and psychedelia synthesized before either category was fully named. Love Battery filters this inheritance through the Sub Pop 1990 context and U-District geography. The connection is direct.
