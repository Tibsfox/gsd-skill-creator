## What Worked

1. **The "detonation" framing as the third E=10 calibration is the correct analytical structure.** E=10 through detonation is different from E=10 through completeness (Soundgarden) or depth (Alice in Chains). *Nevermind* is not the most technically complex or the longest or the most emotionally harrowing of the three. It is the most consequential. Cultural maximum earns E=10 by a third independent route.
2. **The "displacement of Michael Jackson" as the most efficient summary of the cultural impact is correct.** It is a single verifiable fact that communicates the scale of what happened without requiring elaborate argument. The most commercially dominant artist of the 1980s was displaced by a band from Aberdeen, Washington, on a DGC subsidiary label. The sentence does the work.
3. **The "transition record problem" framing for Butch Vig's production is analytically precise.** The challenge of a transition record is to be heavy enough to be authentic and accessible enough to cross to FM radio simultaneously. Vig and Andy Wallace solved it. Most attempts fail on one dimension. *Nevermind* succeeded on both, which is why it was the transition artifact rather than any of the dozens of other grunge records that could have occupied that position.
4. **The compression-release vocal physics is the correct characterization of Cobain's vocal approach.** Not four-octave range (Cornell) or autobiography from the inside (Staley). The specific emotional physics: verse = compressed and withdrawn, chorus = full projection and urgency. The listener's body responds to the release. This is what made the songs radio-viable without losing their underground authenticity.
5. **The Aberdeen genealogy from Melvins to Nirvana is the summit's most important structural observation.** Cobain took guitar from Buzz Osborne. Grohl was influenced by Crover. The same geography that produced the summit's foundation (degree 345) produced the summit's terminal entry (degree 348). The periphery produces the maximum, twice, from the same city.

## Discoveries

1. **The "proving the unrepeatable" observation is the most important industry analysis.** The signing frenzy that followed *Nevermind* mostly failed. This is analytically significant: it demonstrated that *Nevermind*'s success was specific to Cobain's songwriting and the historical moment, not a template that could be industrially reproduced. The detonation proved its own uniqueness by the failure of every attempt to repeat it.
2. **The *MTV Unplugged* record as confirmation of songwriting strength is important.** The acoustic setting removed all of *Nevermind*'s production context (the Vig distortion, the Andy Wallace mix density, the Grohl drum power). The songs held. This is the mark of genuine songwriting: the structural and harmonic content is strong enough to survive the removal of its production context. *MTV Unplugged in New York* (recorded November 1993, released November 1994) is the confirmation of what the songs actually were.
3. **The *Nevermind* / *Dirt* consecutive September timing (1991/1992) is a genuine historical concentration.** Both albums arrived in September (September 24, 1991 for *Nevermind*; September 29, 1992 for *Dirt*) in consecutive years, and both became two of the most significant albums of the decade. The concentration of E=10 documentation into a 12-month window is historically remarkable.
