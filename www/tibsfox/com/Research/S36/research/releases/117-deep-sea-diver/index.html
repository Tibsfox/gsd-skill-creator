The Sideplayer’s Own Voice

Jessica Dobson spent years as a touring guitarist for some of indie rock’s most prominent acts — Beck (experimental pop, genre fluidity), The Shins (jangly melodic indie), Conor Oberst (emotional directness, folk-inflected rock), and Yeah Yeah Yeahs (angular art-rock). Each gig was an education. Deep Sea Diver is the synthesis — everything Dobson absorbed, redirected into her own voice.

The sideplayer-to-frontperson trajectory inverts the usual band narrative. Most musicians start with their own project and then collaborate. Dobson proved herself in other people’s bands first, then stepped to the front. The result: a sound that carries the accumulated weight of multiple musical vocabularies, expressed as a single, layered, hook-driven voice.

The band’s sound is built on three pillars: layered guitars (multiple tracks creating dense, shifting textures — overdrive and clean tones coexisting, effects used architecturally), big hooks (melodies that cut through the textural density like a signal through noise), and Dobson’s vocals (powerful, clear, emotionally expressive — the same person controlling voice and guitar gives the music internal coherence). Sub Pop. E=5. Dense and powerful.

Sub Pop Returns

After the self-released reset at degree 116 (Pony Time — no label, DIY absolute), Sub Pop Records returns at degree 117. The label trajectory: Matador (115) → Self-Released (116) → Sub Pop (117). From prestigious national indie to no label at all to Seattle’s own. Sub Pop at degree 117 is a homecoming — the engine returns to its institutional center of gravity. The label that launched Nirvana at degree 1 is here again, 116 degrees later, still signing the most interesting music in Seattle.

Jessica Dobson — Guitar Hero Who Sings

Dobson’s guitar work is the sonic signature. Her playing draws from the extended vocabulary developed touring with Beck (experimentalism), The Shins (melodic precision), Conor Oberst (emotional directness), and Yeah Yeah Yeahs (angular energy). These influences don’t compete — they’re synthesized into a style that is melodic but layered, technically accomplished but emotionally present, structured but capable of explosive release.

Key albums: History Speaks (2020, Sub Pop) — full realization. Impossible Weight (2022, Sub Pop) — expanded ambition. Each album expands the sonic palette while maintaining the hook-driven core. Guitar layers become more elaborate; production becomes more layered; emotional range deepens.
