## What Worked

1. **The Melvins-Nirvana connection chain is the most important structural contribution of this cluster.** Degrees 308 (Melvins) and 311 (Nirvana) are connected by: Buzz Osborne's education of Cobain; the Dale Demo (1988, Dale Crover on drums); Cobain's attendance at the Melvins' first show; the rejection that forced Nirvana's formation; and the Crover-Grohl influence chain. This is not casual cross-referencing; it is a complete causal chain from one band to the other. The engine documents it fully.
2. **The E=9 calibration is defensible on three simultaneous grounds: commercial impact (30M sales, Michael Jackson displaced), cultural impact (ended hair metal, created 1990s rock market), and artistic quality (*Nevermind* songwriting, *In Utero* artistic ambition, *MTV Unplugged* emotional depth).** The calibration is not based on scale alone; it accounts for all three dimensions simultaneously.
3. **The treatment of Cobain's death is factual, respectful, and concise.** The engine does not sensationalize. The facts are stated: April 5, 1994; self-inflicted; age 27; chronic pain and addiction as context. The artistry is the subject. The death is acknowledged. Both are true. The engine moves on.

## Discoveries

1. **The Olympia period (1988-1989) is underrepresented in most Nirvana histories.** Cobain's time at Evergreen State's orbit, the K Records connection, the riot grrrl movement's political influence, the Tobi Vail relationship &mdash; all of these shaped the political and cultural awareness visible in *In Utero*'s lyrics in ways that the standard Aberdeen-to-Seattle narrative obscures.
2. **The *MTV Unplugged* staging choice ("like a funeral") is Cobain's most significant aesthetic statement about mortality.** He chose candles, lilies, and draped fabric months before his death. The staging was intentional. The posthumous weight of the recording is not entirely accidental; Cobain was engaging with the aesthetics of finality in real time.
3. **The *In Utero* DGC tension is more complex than usually described.** The Scott Litt re-mixes of "Heart-Shaped Box" and "All Apologies" are the only tracks that were modified; the rest of Albini's production was released intact. The compromise was surgical. The resulting album is more coherent than it would have been if DGC had demanded broader changes.
