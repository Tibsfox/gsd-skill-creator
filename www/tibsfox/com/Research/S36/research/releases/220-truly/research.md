## THE SOUNDGARDEN CONNECTION

### Hiro Yamamoto's Post-Soundgarden Life

Truly represents Soundgarden's founding bassist in a different context:

- **Hiro Yamamoto.** Founding bassist of Soundgarden (1984-1989). Yamamoto was with Soundgarden during the band's formative years -- the early Sub Pop releases, the first major-label signing. He left Soundgarden in 1989 to return to school, and was replaced by Ben Shepherd. Yamamoto's departure predates Soundgarden's commercial breakthrough.
- **From Soundgarden to Truly.** Where Soundgarden was heavy, dark, and powerful (Chris Cornell's voice, Kim Thayil's riffs), Truly was spacious, textured, and experimental. Yamamoto's bass in Truly is less aggressive, more atmospheric. The transition mirrors Gossard's Pearl Jam-to-Brad shift at degree 219.
- **Robert Roth.** The primary creative force in Truly. Roth's guitar work and songwriting shaped the band's sound: expansive, effects-heavy, somewhere between alternative rock and space rock. Roth's vision placed Truly in the experimental wing of Seattle alternative rock.
- **The 219-220 arc.** Brad (219, Stone Gossard/Pearl Jam) followed by Truly (220, Hiro Yamamoto/Soundgarden). Two degrees, two side projects, two of Seattle's most important bands. The engine traces the quieter paths that branch from the loudest sources.

## THE SOUND -- SPACIOUS ALTERNATIVE

### Experimental Adjacent

Truly's music occupies a space between alternative rock and experimental:

- **Spacious.** The music breathes. Long sustained notes, reverb-drenched textures, space between the instruments. Truly is not dense -- it's open.
- **Effects-heavy guitar.** Robert Roth's guitar uses effects extensively: reverb, delay, tremolo, phasing. The guitar creates atmospheric textures rather than riffs. The sound owes as much to shoegaze and space rock as to grunge.
- **Yamamoto's bass.** In Soundgarden, Yamamoto's bass was the low-end anchor beneath heavy riffs. In Truly, the bass is more melodic, more independent, more atmospheric. The bass roams.
- **Dynamic range.** Truly's songs move between quiet, atmospheric passages and louder, more driven sections. But even the louder sections are restrained compared to Soundgarden. The dynamics serve mood rather than force.
- **Underrated.** Truly is one of the most underrated bands in Seattle's alternative rock history. Their albums (*Fast Stories...from Kid Coma*, 1995; *Feeling You Up*, 1997) received critical praise but limited commercial attention. The Island/Capitol label connection brought resources but not breakthrough.

(Sources: Truly recordings; Robert Roth guitar technique; Yamamoto bass evolution; critical reception)

## THE ALBUMS

### Two Records on Major Labels

- ***Fast Stories...from Kid Coma* (1995, Capitol Records).** The debut. Expansive, textured, ambitious. Long tracks (several exceed 6 minutes). The album sounds like Seattle alternative rock stretched and pulled into something wider, stranger, more atmospheric.
- ***Feeling You Up* (1997, Island Records).** The second album. More focused but still spacious. Tighter songs but the same atmospheric DNA. Critical praise, limited sales. Island Records was beginning its corporate contraction, and Truly was not a priority.
- **Dissolution.** Truly continued as a project through the early 2000s but gradually became dormant. No dramatic breakup -- just a quiet fading, matching the band's aesthetic.

(Sources: Truly discography; Capitol Records; Island Records; critical reception; career trajectory)

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of Truly

- **Tempo.** Slow to mid-tempo. 80-110 BPM. Spacious tempos. Songs breathe.
- **Guitar.** Effects-heavy. Reverb, delay, tremolo, phasing. Atmospheric textures rather than riffs. Clean to lightly overdriven. Sometimes raw and distorted in louder sections.
- **Bass.** Melodic, independent, atmospheric. Yamamoto roams. The bass is not an anchor -- it's a participant.
- **Drums.** Dynamic, varied, sometimes sparse. Serving the mood.
- **Vocals.** Robert Roth: mid-range, slightly raw, emotional. Not powerful like Cornell or Smith. Intimate, searching.
- **Production.** Spacious, atmospheric, professional (major label resources). Room for the reverb to breathe.
- **Song length.** Medium to long. 4:00-7:00+. Songs take their time.

(Sources: Truly recordings; production analysis; atmospheric alternative rock)
