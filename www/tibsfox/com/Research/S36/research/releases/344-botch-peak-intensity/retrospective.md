## What Worked

1. **The four-dimension E=10 framework is the right analytical structure.** E=10 is not one dimension at maximum. It is the simultaneous maximum of all four: rhythmic complexity (Latona's irregular time signatures), guitar technique (Knudson's dissonant technical riffs), vocal intensity (Verellen's raw-scream at full projection), and production density (Common's walls-of-sound). Any one of these at maximum = E=7-8. All four simultaneously = E=10.
2. **The "ceiling exhausting itself" framing is correct.** E=10 is not "slightly more intense than E=9." It is the point at which the scale can no longer represent the differentiation between this and what exceeds it -- because nothing in the engine's scope exceeds it. Botch fills the ceiling completely.
3. **The Tacoma origin as periphery-produces-the-maximum is analytically correct and well-supported.** Melvins (Aberdeen, E=8). Nirvana (Aberdeen, E=9). Screaming Trees (Ellensburg). Botch (Tacoma, E=10). The engine's most extreme entries consistently come from outside Seattle's urban core. The pattern is real.
4. **We Are the Romans as the specific E=10 document is correct.** The 1999 Hydra Head record with Chris Common producing is the clearest single document of all four dimensions at maximum. The opening track "C. Thomas Howell as the Soul Man" demonstrates immediate full capability with no warmup. The record earns the calibration.
5. **The final ascent ramp (341 E=8, 342 E=8, 343 E=9, 344 E=10) is correctly structured and documented.** The ramp is complete. The energy profile is: 8, 8, 9, 10 -- base holds for two degrees, then rises by one, then breaks through the ceiling. The structure is deliberate and documented from its first step.

## Discoveries

1. **Brian Cook's post-Botch trajectory (Russian Circles) is the most unexpected continuation in the engine's heavy catalog.** From Botch's mathcore to Russian Circles' instrumental post-rock is a genuine artistic range. The bass sophistication that Cook was developing in Botch's framework became the foundation for a different kind of compositional complexity in Russian Circles.
2. **The mathcore moment (1997-2002) produced multiple defining records simultaneously across multiple geographies.** Converge (Massachusetts), Dillinger Escape Plan (New Jersey), Cave In (Massachusetts), and Botch (Tacoma) all reached their creative peaks within a five-year window. We Are the Romans (1999) is the Pacific Northwest entry in a genre that was being defined nationally and internationally in the same period.
3. **E=10 being from Tacoma rather than Seattle is the engine's most significant final-geography surprise.** After 344 degrees of predominantly Seattle-geography music, the absolute maximum comes from 30 miles to the south. The engine documents the full region, not just the city.
