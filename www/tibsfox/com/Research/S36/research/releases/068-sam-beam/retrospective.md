## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **Sub Pop structural event thoroughly documented.** The label's history, significance, and institutional role are grounded in verifiable facts -- founding date, key signings, cultural impact. The distinction between Sub Pop's prior presence (Fleet Foxes at 53) and its structural entry at degree 68 is clearly drawn.

2. **Visiting artist pattern clearly established.** The distinction from Export, wanderer, and departed member is analytically productive. The expansion of belonging from geographic to institutional is a genuine conceptual contribution to the engine's framework.

3. **The hush acoustic element grounded in audible characteristics.** The hush is described through specific vocal qualities -- whispered volume, breath as compositional element, microphone proximity, physical mechanics of quiet singing. These are audible on The Creek Drank the Cradle and Our Endless Numbered Days.

4. **Distinction from the whisper (degree 31) clearly articulated.** The whisper is technique; the hush is condition. Beam does not choose to whisper -- the whisper is the voice's native register. This distinction is analytically productive and verifiable through the catalog.

5. **Genre Stage 50 milestone documented.** The fiftieth genre stage is a round-number milestone. The label-imported folk as a new deployment of the genre is a productive distinction.

6. **Sub Pop's evolution documented.** From grunge (Nirvana, Soundgarden) to folk (Iron & Wine, Fleet Foxes) -- the label's reinvention mirrors Seattle's music scene evolution. This is verifiable through the Sub Pop catalog.

7. **The held breath blue note grounded in recording analysis.** The breath between phrases on The Creek Drank the Cradle is audible. The four-track recording captures breathing, lip sounds, and physical proximity. The blue note is not abstract -- it is audible as the space between whispered phrases.

8. **Energy calibration accurate.** E=2 correctly captures Iron & Wine's intimate, hushed quality. The three-degree descent from E=4 to E=3 to E=2 (degrees 66-67-68) is a clean structural progression.

9. **Cross-references well-established.** Five cross-references: Jurado (45, quiet folk), Gibbard (60, E=2 folk), Sykes (64, dark folk), Fleet Foxes (53, Sub Pop), Aaberg (65, quiet spectrum). All productive analytically.

10. **Discography thoroughly documented.** Six Iron & Wine albums plus two Calexico collaborations. All on Sub Pop except Ghost on Ghost (Nonesuch). The label progression (Sub Pop to Nonesuch to Sub Pop return) is factual and documented.

### Source Quality

11. **AllMusic and Pitchfork provide comprehensive Iron & Wine documentation.** Discography, biography, reviews extensively available.

12. **Sub Pop Records history well documented.** Michael Azerrad's *Our Band Could Be Your Life*, multiple institutional histories, catalog documentation.

13. **Recording history for The Creek Drank the Cradle documented.** The home-recording origin, four-track process, and Tallahassee location are part of the standard Iron & Wine narrative.
