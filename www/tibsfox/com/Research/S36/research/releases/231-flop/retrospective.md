The engine should document both the lottery winners and the overlooked with the same depth. Degree 231 does this.

4. **E=6 across 230-231 creates a stable pair.** Two consecutive degrees at E=6 hold the melodic middle without stagnating. The genre shifts (grunge/indie to alt rock) while the energy holds. This is correct engine behavior.

## Discoveries

1. **Lisa Fancher's curatorial trajectory -- from SoCal hardcore to PNW alt rock -- is a story worth telling.** The same label that documented the Circle Jerks and T.S.O.L. also signed Flop. The traverse from hardcore to alt rock in one label's lifetime is a complete picture of the West Coast independent music evolution from 1980 to 1993. Frontier's history is richer than the Flop degree alone conveys.
2. **Rusty Willoughby's post-Flop work in the Seattle scene is an open thread.** The engine notes this as a carry-forward item without resolving it -- Willoughby continued in the scene, and future degrees may surface his later work.
