## What Worked

1. **E=8 calibration is correct and requires no modification for the gender of the band's vocalist.** The engine's energy calibration is neutral. Seven Year Bitch's heaviest material is genuinely E=8 -- the guitar is downtuned and fully distorted, Vigil's voice is at maximum aggressive deployment, the production (Atlantic Records resources applied to riot grrrl-adjacent metal) is massive. E=8 without qualifier.
2. **The grief-as-fuel framing is the most important contextual contribution of this entry.** Stefanie Sargent's death in 1992 and Kurt Cobain's death in 1994 (during the recording of *Viva Zapata!*) are part of the historical record. The emotional weight of both losses is audible in the band's heaviest material. Documenting this is not speculative -- it is historically grounded.
3. **The riot grrrl adjacent position is precise.** Not riot grrrl (extends beyond punk intensity into metal). Not merely heavy rock (the feminist political content and Capitol Hill scene membership connect to riot grrrl). The "adjacent" designation is the correct categorization.
4. **The E=6-6-8 sequence reveals the post-summit return pattern.** Two consecutive degrees at E=6 (Truly, Brad) followed by the sharp rise to E=8 (Seven Year Bitch) mirrors the energy accumulation and release pattern seen elsewhere in the engine. The pattern: moderate-moderate-intense. The intensity is more dramatic because of the two-degree plateau preceding it.

## Discoveries

1. **The 279-328 arc documents the full Seven Year Bitch energy range.** E=7 (riot grrrl/punk, 279) to E=8 (metal/heavy punk, 328). One unit of energy separates the punk mode from the metal mode. The heavy/metal side requires the Atlantic Records resources, the post-loss emotional intensity, and the slower doom-influenced tempos that the punk mode does not use.
2. **Valerie Agnew's drums are understated in the engine's documentation relative to their importance.** Agnew's drumming on the heaviest Seven Year Bitch tracks is what makes the metal heaviness intelligible rather than chaotic. The rhythm section's precision at E=8 intensity is not a given -- it requires a drummer who can maintain groove and control at maximum volume and aggression. Agnew does this.
