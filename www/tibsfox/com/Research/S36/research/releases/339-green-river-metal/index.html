THE BAND THAT BECAME TWO BANDS. Green River (1984–1987) contains the genetic material of both Mudhoney and Pearl Jam. Mark Arm and Steve Turner → Mudhoney (Sub Pop's grunge template). Stone Gossard and Jeff Ament → Mother Love Bone → Pearl Jam (Ten, 13 million US copies). No other Seattle band produced two acts of this importance from a single dissolution. The Genetic Material of Seattle Rock

Green River formed in 1984 and dissolved in 1987 — three years, two EPs, and the entire genetic material of the Seattle grunge story. The band appeared on the Deep Six compilation (1986, C/Z Records) alongside Skin Yard (degree 338), documenting the pre-commercial Seattle heavy music scene before Sub Pop's commercial expansion changed everything.

The dissolution was not a failure. It was a bifurcation — a single band becoming two bands that would go in opposite commercial directions, both of them defining the grunge era in different ways. The band held, in the same lineup, the people who would become Mudhoney and the people who would become Pearl Jam. When it split, it split perfectly along the aesthetic line.

"One band, two futures, and the complete genetic map of the Seattle grunge story held in a single 1984-1987 Capitol Hill lineup."

The Bifurcation — One Band Becomes Two

Mudhoney Wing

Mark Arm (vocals) and Steve Turner (guitar) founded Mudhoney in 1988. First single "Touch Me I'm Sick" became the template for Sub Pop grunge: fast, loud, irreverent, deliberately uncommercial.

Mudhoney remained on Sub Pop for their entire career. Still active as of 2026. One of the most consistent and principled bodies of work in Seattle rock history.

Commercial profile: critical favorite, Sub Pop stalwart, never compromised for mainstream

Pearl Jam Wing

Stone Gossard (guitar) and Jeff Ament (bass) co-founded Mother Love Bone (1988). When vocalist Andrew Wood died March 19, 1990, they reorganized with Eddie Vedder as Pearl Jam.

Ten (1991, Epic): 13 million US copies. One of the best-selling rock albums of the 1990s. Pearl Jam still active as of 2026, over 30 years of continuous operation.

Commercial profile: multi-platinum, stadium rock, sustained 35-year career

Deep Six and the Pre-Commercial Record

Green River appeared on the Deep Six compilation (1986, C/Z Records) — the most important document of pre-commercial Seattle heavy music. Degrees 338 (Skin Yard) and 339 (Green River) are consecutive Deep Six alumni, placed in the engine back-to-back as they appeared in the original compilation context. The engine's placement mirrors the historical record: both bands were there at the beginning, before the world was paying attention.

Dry As a Bone EP (1987, Sub Pop) is Green River's primary document: five tracks of proto-grunge containing both the Mudhoney aesthetic and the Pearl Jam aesthetic in the same band, in the last moment before the split separated them forever.
