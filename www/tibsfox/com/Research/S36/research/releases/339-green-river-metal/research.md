## CAPITOL HILL -- THE FOUNDATIONAL NEIGHBORHOOD RETURNS

### Where the Engine Began

Capitol Hill in the metal summit:

- **Capitol Hill's engine history.** Capitol Hill has been the engine's most productive neighborhood -- the bohemian, punk, queer, artist neighborhood that generated band after band throughout the 1970s, 1980s, and 1990s. The neighborhood produced the earliest Seattle punk entries, the mid-grunge-era entries, and now returns at degree 339 as the origin of the band that contains the genetic material of both major grunge strands.
- **The geography of dissolution.** Green River dissolved in 1987. The members who continued west -- toward the more commercial end of the sub-pop sound -- ended up in Pearl Jam via Mother Love Bone. The members who continued in the Sub Pop grunge aesthetic -- toward the punk-informed, more abrasive end -- ended up in Mudhoney. Capitol Hill produced both trajectories from a single origin point.
- **Green River's Capitol Hill identity.** Green River played Capitol Hill venues throughout their brief active period. The neighborhood's infrastructure -- the Central Tavern, the Vogue, the early all-ages spaces -- provided the performance circuit for the proto-grunge scene. Green River's audience in 1984-1987 was the Capitol Hill underground music community.

## GREEN RIVER -- THE BAND

### The Personnel and the Two Futures

Green River's lineup is the engine's most consequential personnel arrangement:

- **Mark Arm (vocals).** Mark Arm is the defining vocalist and lyricist of the proto-grunge aesthetic. His vocal approach -- raw, confrontational, rooted in the Stooges/MC5 tradition of aggressive rock performance -- is the template for Sub Pop grunge vocals. After Green River's dissolution, Arm co-founded Mudhoney (1988) with Steve Turner, producing one of the most consistent bodies of work in Seattle rock history. Mudhoney remained on Sub Pop throughout the grunge era and beyond, never compromising their aesthetic for commercial considerations.
- **Stone Gossard (guitar).** Gossard's guitar work in Green River established his compositional approach: riff-based, melodic within the heavy structure, accessible beneath the noise. After Green River, he co-founded Mother Love Bone (1988) with Jeff Ament. When Mother Love Bone vocalist Andrew Wood died of heroin overdose in March 1990, Gossard and Ament reorganized with vocalist Eddie Vedder to form Pearl Jam (1990). Gossard's trajectory from Green River to Pearl Jam is the most commercially consequential individual arc in Seattle rock history.
- **Jeff Ament (bass).** Ament's bass playing in Green River demonstrated the melodic approach that would characterize Pearl Jam's rhythm section. Like Gossard, Ament moved through Mother Love Bone to Pearl Jam. Ament also played in Three Fish (a side project) and has been involved in multiple charitable and cultural initiatives in the Pacific Northwest.
- **Steve Turner (guitar).** Turner's guitar approach in Green River was noisier, more punk-influenced than Gossard's. After Green River, Turner founded Mudhoney with Arm. Turner's approach established the more abrasive wing of the grunge guitar vocabulary.
- **Alex Shumway (drums).** The rhythm foundation of Green River's recorded output. Less a figure in subsequent Seattle history than the band's other members, but essential to the proto-grunge template's physical character.

(Sources: Mark Arm vocals; Mudhoney co-founding 1988 with Steve Turner; Stone Gossard guitar; Mother Love Bone co-founding 1988; Andrew Wood death heroin overdose March 1990; Pearl Jam formation 1990 Eddie Vedder; Jeff Ament bass; Three Fish side project; Steve Turner guitar; noisy punk-influenced approach; Mudhoney aesthetic; Alex Shumway drums)

## THE DRY AS A BONE EP

### The Sub Pop Debut Document

- ***Dry As a Bone EP* (1987, Sub Pop).** Green River's Sub Pop debut and arguably their most important release. The EP captures the band at their creative peak: five tracks of proto-grunge that contain both the aggressive noise-rock of the Mudhoney wing and the riff-based accessibility of the Pearl Jam wing, before the two aesthetics were separated by the band's dissolution. The EP is produced with Jack Endino's characteristic directness -- loud, guitar-forward, drum-physical.
- **"Unwind" and "Baby Takes."** The standout tracks. "Unwind" demonstrates the proto-grunge heavy approach: slow, dense, Mark Arm's vocals raw and confrontational. "Baby Takes" demonstrates the pop-rock accessibility that Gossard's guitar instincts would develop further in Pearl Jam. Both tracks exist in the same band, the same EP, the same moment before the inevitable divergence.
- **The Sub Pop packaging.** *Dry As a Bone* appeared in the Sub Pop limited-edition packaging format that would define the label's aesthetic: limited pressing, hand-numbered, designed for collectors. This packaging approach was Sub Pop's early commercial strategy -- creating scarcity and collectibility around music from local bands.
- ***Rehab Doll* (1988, Sub Pop).** The second EP, released after the band had effectively already dissolved. The record documents a band in its final moments -- the personnel would be in the process of forming their next projects even as this record was being completed.

(Sources: Dry As a Bone EP 1987 Sub Pop; Jack Endino production; "Unwind" and "Baby Takes" standout tracks; Sub Pop limited-edition numbered packaging; Rehab Doll 1988 Sub Pop; band dissolution in progress during Rehab Doll)

## THE DISSOLUTION AND ITS CONSEQUENCES

### One Band Becoming Two of Seattle's Most Important Acts

The most consequential band dissolution in Seattle music history:

- **The split.** Green River dissolved in 1987 over tensions between the band's two aesthetic directions. Mark Arm and Steve Turner wanted to maintain the punk-noise-grunge aesthetic, the confrontational anti-commercial stance. Stone Gossard and Jeff Ament were interested in a more melodic, stadium-accessible direction. These two positions were incompatible within a single band, and the dissolution was the result.
- **Mother Love Bone (1988-1990).** Gossard and Ament founded Mother Love Bone with vocalist Andrew Wood, guitarist Greg Gilmore, and other members. Wood's theatrical, glam-influenced vocal style was radically different from Mark Arm's confrontational delivery. Mother Love Bone were recording their debut album *Apple* (1990) when Wood died of a heroin overdose on March 19, 1990, the day the album was mastered. *Apple* was released posthumously. The band could not continue without Wood.
- **Pearl Jam (1990-present).** Gossard and Ament reconvened with guitarist Mike McCready, drummer Dave Krusen, and vocalist Eddie Vedder. Vedder sent a demo tape to Gossard and Ament that became the basis for three Pearl Jam songs. The band formed in 1990, signed to Epic Records, and released *Ten* in 1991. *Ten* sold 13 million copies in the United States alone -- making Pearl Jam one of the most commercially successful rock bands of the 1990s.
- **Mudhoney (1988-present).** Arm and Turner founded Mudhoney, signing to Sub Pop. Mudhoney's first single "Touch Me I'm Sick" (1988) became the template for the Sub Pop grunge sound -- fast, loud, irreverent, and deliberately uncommercial. Mudhoney is still active and still on Sub Pop as of 2026, representing an extraordinary commitment to the original aesthetic over decades.
- **The asymmetry.** The dissolution of Green River produced: one band (Pearl Jam) that became one of the best-selling rock acts of the decade; and one band (Mudhoney) that remained a critical and fan favorite while consciously avoiding commercial compromise. The same original band, two completely different commercial destinies. This asymmetry is one of the defining stories of Seattle rock.

(Sources: Green River dissolution 1987 aesthetic tension; Arm/Turner punk-noise direction vs. Gossard/Ament melodic direction; Mother Love Bone 1988: Wood Gossard Ament Gilmore; Andrew Wood death March 19 1990; Apple posthumous release; Pearl Jam 1990: McCready Krusen Vedder; Vedder demo tape; Epic Records; Ten 1991 13 million US copies; Mudhoney 1988: Touch Me I'm Sick; Sub Pop grunge template; Mudhoney still active 2026)

### The Transitional Energy

Green River at E=7 in the metal summit context:
