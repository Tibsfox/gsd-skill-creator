## What Worked

1. **The bifurcation framework is the entry's defining contribution.** One band, two futures: Arm + Turner → Mudhoney (Sub Pop stalwart, still active 2026); Gossard + Ament → Mother Love Bone → Pearl Jam (Ten, 13 million US copies). The split was along the aesthetic line -- punk-noise-grunge vs. melodic-accessible -- and both wings became defining bands. This is documented clearly in the personnel section.
2. **The Deep Six consecutive placement (338, 339) mirrors historical accuracy.** The engine's sequential placement of Skin Yard and Green River mirrors their co-appearance on the Deep Six compilation (1986). This is one of the engine's most historically precise structural decisions.
3. **The E=7 proto-form energy analysis connects clearly to the summit energy profile.** 9 (TAD) → 7 (Skin Yard) → 7 (Green River) → 9 (Nirvana). The dip-and-return structure mirrors the historical arc. Green River's E=7 is understood correctly as potential-form energy pointing toward Nirvana's E=9 realization.
4. **Andrew Wood's death is documented respectfully.** Wood died March 19, 1990. The entry notes the fact and its consequence (Mother Love Bone dissolves, Pearl Jam forms) without excessive detail. The tone is appropriate.

## Discoveries

1. **The aesthetic split within Green River was complete and clean.** The two wings of the band did not produce overlapping aesthetics -- Mudhoney and Pearl Jam are as different from each other commercially and aesthetically as two bands could be while sharing the same original lineup. This suggests the aesthetic tension in Green River was genuine, not manufactured in retrospect.
2. **Mudhoney's longevity (still active 2026) is one of the engine's most important implicit statements.** Mudhoney chose the Sub Pop aesthetic and has maintained it for nearly 40 years. This is not inertia -- it is commitment. The commercial success of Pearl Jam created no pull on the Mudhoney wing of the Green River dissolution.
