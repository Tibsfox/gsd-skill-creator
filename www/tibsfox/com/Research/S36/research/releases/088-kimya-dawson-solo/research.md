## K Records -- Second Artist

**K Records** is Calvin Johnson's Olympia, Washington label. Founded in 1982. The heart of Pacific Northwest lo-fi indie. Home to Beat Happening, the Microphones (Phil Elverum), and a generation of artists who believed that imperfection was authenticity. "Lo-fi" is not a compromise at K Records -- it's an aesthetic choice, an ideological position.

Kimya Dawson's solo releases on K Records fit the label's aesthetic perfectly: handmade, intimate, anti-corporate, lo-fi as ideology. The K Records catalog values rawness over polish, personality over perfection. Dawson is a natural K Records artist.

**LAKE** (degree 73) was the first K Records artist. Dawson is the second. Both share the Olympia connection. Both are lo-fi. Both reject polish. **Phil Elverum** (degree 71) is K Records-adjacent (Microphones releases on K). The Olympia lo-fi lineage runs through K Records: Beat Happening -> Microphones -> LAKE -> Dawson.

(Sources: K Records; AllMusic)

## The Solo Work

### Solo Discography (Selected)

Kimya Dawson's solo work is extensive and deliberately lo-fi:

- **I'm Sorry That Sometimes I'm Mean** (2002, K Records) -- raw, confessional, acoustic.
- **My Cute Fiend Sweet Princess** (2004, K Records) -- longer songs, more confidence.
- **Remember That I Love You** (2006, K Records) -- the *Juno* album. The *Juno* soundtrack (2007) brought Dawson's music to a mass audience. "Tire Swing" and "Anyone Else But You" (Moldy Peaches) became ubiquitous.
- **Thunder Thighs** (2011, Great Crap Factory) -- post-*Juno*, more experimental.

### The *Juno* Connection

The 2007 film *Juno* used several Kimya Dawson songs and one Moldy Peaches song on its soundtrack. The soundtrack went platinum. Dawson's lo-fi aesthetic reached millions of listeners who had never heard of K Records or the Moldy Peaches. The bedroom strum went mainstream -- but Dawson didn't change. The music stayed lo-fi.

(Sources: K Records; AllMusic)

## Acoustic Element 89: The Bedroom Strum

The **bedroom strum** is the eighty-ninth acoustic element in the Seattle 360 engine. It describes the sound of Kimya Dawson's solo work -- a single acoustic guitar, strummed simply, recorded without production.

The voice is close-mic'd or room-mic'd -- you can hear the room. The guitar is not perfectly in tune. The performance is not perfectly timed. The imperfections are the point -- they signal authenticity, intimacy, the handmade. This is the K Records aesthetic translated into a single person's recording practice.

### Contrasts with Prior Acoustic Elements

- **The emerging twang** (degree 87, Neko Case) -- folk-country structures, pedal steel, genre infrastructure. The bedroom strum strips away all genre infrastructure. No pedal steel, no band, no production. Just guitar and voice in a room.
- **The electronic pulse** (degree 86, Postal Service) -- maximum production. The bedroom strum is minimum production. The engine swings from maximum to minimum.
- **The stripped confession** (degree 85, Votolato) -- both stripped-down, both voice-forward. But Votolato's stripping is dramatic (removing from a fuller context). Dawson's is natural (she was always this minimal).

The 89th acoustic element.

## Pattern 89: The Solo Distillation

The **solo distillation** is the eighty-ninth artist-city pattern in the Seattle 360 engine. It describes a return artist whose solo work strips away everything from the original band context -- the Moldy Peaches' communal Anti-Folk energy, the irony, the duo dynamic -- leaving only the essential: one voice, one guitar, the songs.

The solo distillation is the artist reduced to their minimum. The Moldy Peaches were a duo, a scene, an attitude. Kimya Dawson solo is just Kimya Dawson. The solo distillation reveals what remains when everything else is removed: the songs themselves, unadorned, unprotected.

Contrast with prior patterns:
- **The early chapter** (degree 87, Neko Case) -- temporal movement to an earlier era. The solo distillation is format reduction (band to solo), not temporal movement.
- **The side project** (degree 86, Postal Service) -- lateral movement to a new collaborator. The solo distillation moves away from collaboration entirely.
