## GOLD CASIO -- Retro Synths, Modern Sensibility

### The Project

**Gold Casio** is a Seattle indie electronic project built on **vintage synthesizer aesthetics** -- warm analog pads, arpeggiated sequences, retro drum machines, and melodic synth lines filtered through modern production. The name itself evokes retro electronic hardware -- the gold Casio watch as an icon of 1980s consumer electronics, now reframed as aesthetic object.

Gold Casio operates **entirely self-released** -- no label, no management. The bedroom-to-Bandcamp pipeline: produce at home, release directly to streaming platforms. Seattle's electronic community provides the audience; the internet provides the distribution.

### The Retro-Synthetic Aesthetic

Gold Casio's sound is defined by its relationship to electronic music's past:
- **Analog synthesizer tones** -- warm pads and arpeggios that reference 1980s synth-pop.
- **Retro drum machines** -- programmed rhythms that evoke early electronic production.
- **Modern production values** -- clean mixing, spatial effects, contemporary dynamics.
- **Nostalgia as creative fuel** -- not reproduction of the past but reinterpretation through contemporary sensibility.

The tension between retro source material and modern execution creates the distinctive **retro-synthetic shimmer** -- warm, hazy, wistful.

### Self-Released and Self-Directed

Gold Casio represents the **purest expression of DIY** in the electronic era. Where STRFKR (96) uses indie label infrastructure (Polyvinyl) and the Postal Service (86, 97) uses Sub Pop, Gold Casio eliminates all intermediaries. The self-released synthesist: bedroom production, direct distribution, zero infrastructure overhead.
