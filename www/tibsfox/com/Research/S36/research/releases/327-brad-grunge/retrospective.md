## What Worked

1. **The Pearl Jam satellite framing is accurate and provides the clearest context for Brad in the grunge world.** Gossard's presence in both Pearl Jam and Brad is the most direct connection between the summit bands and the post-grunge supporting cast. The framing of Brad as a parallel creative project -- same musicianship, different scale and aesthetic goal -- is analytically correct.
2. **The soul-grunge fusion is documented as a coherent aesthetic, not a contrast.** The temptation is to frame Brad as "grunge with unexpected R&B vocals" (as if the R&B element is strange or incongruous). The correct framing is: Brad developed a specific fusion aesthetic in which soul-inflected vocals and heavy rock are not in conflict. The grunge tradition is wide enough.
3. **E=6 calibration is correct.** The soul inflection in Smith's voice prevents the heaviest Brad tracks from reaching E=7. The warmth and the groove reduce the confrontational directness that E=7 requires. E=6 is accurate.
4. **The longevity documentation is necessary.** "1992-present" is unusual in the post-grunge return sequence. Most bands in this sequence are historically bounded. Brad's continued activity is part of the record.

## Discoveries

1. **The Gossard lineage (Green River → Mother Love Bone → Pearl Jam → Brad) is one of the longest continuous lineage threads in the engine.** The same musician documents the full arc from proto-grunge (294) to pre-grunge (296) to summit (314) to post-grunge (327). No other single musician in the engine spans this much of the Seattle music history arc.
2. **Smith's vocal adaptation to the grunge context is specific and deliberate.** On the heaviest Brad tracks, the melismatic gospel phrasing is controlled, the phrases are shorter, the delivery is more direct. Smith does not eliminate the warmth but manages it. This is vocal craft, not genre capitulation.

## Pair Analysis: Brad (grunge) + Mt. Rainier Wind Above Treeline

- **Energy:** E=6 + E=7 = E=13. The soul-grunge band at intimate scale against the summit wind at E=7. Brad's controlled heaviness vs. the ultra-high-velocity howl above treeline. Both involve operating near a threshold: Brad at the edge of grunge (E=6, not E=7); the Rainier wind at the edge of the tree zone (above treeline, exposed summit).
- **Theme:** THE THRESHOLD CONDITION. Brad operates at the edge of the grunge tradition -- close enough to be genuinely grunge-adjacent, far enough to maintain the soul aesthetic. The Mt. Rainier wind above treeline operates at the threshold of the vegetated zone -- above the trees, exposed to the unmediated Pacific wind. Both entries document their subjects at the threshold condition: not fully inside the most extreme category, but operating at the edge where the extreme condition defines the experience.
