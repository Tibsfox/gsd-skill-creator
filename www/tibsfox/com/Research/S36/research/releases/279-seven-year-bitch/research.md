## CAPITOL HILL: THE SEATTLE CONTEXT

### Not Olympia -- Capitol Hill

Seven Year Bitch is the first riot grrrl-adjacent entry in the engine that is specifically rooted in Seattle rather than in Olympia:

- **Capitol Hill.** Capitol Hill is Seattle's historic bohemian district: dense with bars, music venues, independent businesses, and a long tradition of LGBTQ+ community and alternative culture. In the early 1990s, Capitol Hill was the center of Seattle's feminist punk and riot grrrl-adjacent scene. The same energy that Bikini Kill produced in Olympia found its Capitol Hill expression in Seven Year Bitch.
- **The Seattle-Olympia distinction.** The relationship between the Olympia scene (Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney) and the Seattle Capitol Hill scene (Seven Year Bitch) is one of proximity and influence rather than institutional overlap. Seven Year Bitch was not on Kill Rock Stars (the Olympia label) but on C/Z Records (a Seattle label). The geographic and institutional distinction is real.
- **C/Z Records.** C/Z Records was a Seattle-based independent label that was associated with the Northwest alternative music scene. The label also released work by Mudhoney, Love Battery, and other Seattle acts. Seven Year Bitch's first two albums were on C/Z.
- **The Atlantic signing.** "¡Viva Zapata!" (1994) was released on Atlantic Records, a major label -- an unusual move for a riot grrrl-adjacent band. The signing brought Seven Year Bitch to a much wider audience without, by critical assessment, compromising the music's character.

(Sources: Capitol Hill Seattle history; LGBTQ+ and feminist punk community on Capitol Hill; Seattle vs. Olympia scene distinction; C/Z Records history; Atlantic Records signing; "¡Viva Zapata!" major label release)

## THE MEMBERS

### Selene Vigil, Stefanie Sargent, Valerie Agnew, Elizabeth Davis

- **Selene Vigil (vocals).** Vigil is the band's primary vocalist and central public presence. Her voice is powerful and direct -- not technically operatic in the way of some riot grrrl-adjacent singers, but immediately present and committed. She delivers the band's lyrics -- which are often fierce, often grief-inflected, often darkly funny -- with full conviction. Vigil has spoken publicly about the band's history, its losses, and its significance.
- **Stefanie Sargent (guitar, 1990-1992).** Sargent was a founding member and the band's original guitarist. She died in June 1992 from a heroin overdose, at age twenty-four. She had recorded the band's debut album before her death. The loss of Sargent is a defining event in the band's history -- not one that the band was defined *by* in a passive sense, but one that the subsequent work consciously acknowledges and responds to.
- **Roisin Dunne (guitar, 1992-1997).** Dunne joined the band after Sargent's death and is the guitarist on "Viva Zapata!" and "¡Viva Zapata!" and "Gato Negro." Her playing is the guitar voice that the world primarily knows as Seven Year Bitch.
- **Valerie Agnew (drums).** Agnew's drumming is a constant across the band's history: powerful, direct, propulsive. Her contribution to the band's sound is substantial and consistent.
- **Elizabeth Davis (bass).** Davis provides the low-end foundation for the band's guitar-and-drums attack.

(Sources: Selene Vigil biography; Stefanie Sargent biography and death June 1992; Roisin Dunne joining; Valerie Agnew drumming; Elizabeth Davis bass)

## STEFANIE SARGENT: A FOUNDING MEMBER REMEMBERED

Stefanie Sargent died on June 27, 1992, from a drug overdose. She was twenty-four years old. She had been a founding member of Seven Year Bitch and had recorded the band's debut material.

The engine documents Sargent's death factually and with care. The loss was a formative event for the band and for the Seattle independent music community. The response of Seven Year Bitch to this loss is documented in their subsequent music: they continued, they grieved through their work, and they named their most important album after another woman who died violently -- Mia Zapata of The Gits (degree 280).

The connection between Sargent's death and the naming of "¡Viva Zapata!" is the engine's most important cross-reference between degrees 279 and 280. Two deaths in the Seattle feminist punk community within a year of each other: Sargent in June 1992, Zapata in July 1993. Seven Year Bitch responded to both.

(Sources: Stefanie Sargent death June 27, 1992; age 24; founding member; debut album recorded; community response; connection to "¡Viva Zapata!" naming)

## THE DISCOGRAPHY

### Three Albums, Seven Years

- ***Scratch It Out* (1992, C/Z Records).** The debut. Recorded in part before Sargent's death. Raw, fast, feminist punk -- the Capitol Hill version of the Olympia sound. The album documents the band in its original formation.
- ***Viva Zapata!* (1994, Atlantic Records).** The major label debut and the central statement. The album title is named in tribute to Mia Zapata of The Gits, who was murdered in July 1993 (degree 280). The music is fierce, direct, and grief-inflected -- it knows what has been lost and refuses to be gentled by the knowledge. "Rock N Roll Junkie," "The History of Utah," "Hip Like Junk," "No Fucking War." The band at its most fully realized. The Atlantic signing brought the music to a wider audience without diluting it.
- ***Gato Negro* (1996, Atlantic Records).** The final album. The band continues its development; the sound evolves. The dissolution comes in 1997.

(Sources: Seven Year Bitch discography; "Scratch It Out" 1992; "¡Viva Zapata!" 1994 title tribute to Mia Zapata; "Gato Negro" 1996; Atlantic Records major label arc)

## "¡VIVA ZAPATA!": THE TITLE AND THE TRIBUTE

### A Name That Carries Weight

The album title "¡Viva Zapata!" is one of the most significant acts of tribute in the Seattle independent music scene:

- **Emiliano Zapata.** "¡Viva Zapata!" echoes the cry associated with the Mexican revolutionary Emiliano Zapata -- "¡Viva Zapata! ¡Viva la revolución!" The exclamation points are the album's own addition; the revolutionary association is deliberate.
- **Mia Zapata.** The album is named in tribute to Mia Zapata of The Gits (degree 280), who was murdered on July 7, 1993. Zapata was Selene Vigil's friend and a prominent figure in the Capitol Hill Seattle feminist punk scene. The album, recorded in 1994 in the aftermath of Zapata's murder, carries the tribute in its title.
- **The double meaning.** "¡Viva Zapata!" means "Long live Zapata!" -- both the revolutionary and the murdered singer. The album title refuses to accept that Mia Zapata can be silenced. The exclamation points insist on life.
- **The community response.** The Capitol Hill feminist punk community's response to Mia Zapata's murder was collective and sustained. Home Alive -- a self-defense and community organization founded in the aftermath of the murder -- was one institutional expression of this response. "¡Viva Zapata!" is Seven Year Bitch's artistic expression of the same response.

(Sources: "¡Viva Zapata!" title; Emiliano Zapata reference; Mia Zapata tribute; Selene Vigil and Mia Zapata friendship; Home Alive organization; community response to murder; double meaning of the title)

## THE SOUND: RIOT GRRRL-ADJACENT SEATTLE

### E=8 in the Capitol Hill Register

Seven Year Bitch's E=8 is riot grrrl-adjacent rather than strictly riot grrrl: the band shares the feminist punk values and the energy level of the Olympia scene but was shaped by a different community and institutional context:

- **The guitar approach.** Where Bikini Kill's guitar is raw and direct (Billy Karren's power-chord punk), Seven Year Bitch's guitar -- particularly in Roisin Dunne's playing on "¡Viva Zapata!" -- is heavier and more riff-driven. The Capitol Hill sound is adjacent to grunge in its guitar weight, even as the feminist punk values are distinct.
- **Vigil's vocals.** Selene Vigil's vocal delivery is direct and committed. The lyrics are sharp, often darkly funny, sometimes grief-inflected without being mournful. The emotional range includes defiance, humor, anger, and sorrow -- but the defiance always wins.
- **The rhythm section.** Agnew on drums and Davis on bass form a propulsive foundation. The band's rhythm section drives the music with the same physical commitment as the vocal delivery.
- **The grief dimension.** Seven Year Bitch's music after Sargent's death and after Zapata's murder is inflected with grief in a way that is rare in punk music. Most punk handles grief through anger (which is appropriate) but rarely through the specific combination of anger, dark humor, and defiant continuation that Seven Year Bitch achieves.

(Sources: Capitol Hill vs. Olympia musical character; grunge adjacency in guitar weight; Vigil vocal approach; defiance-grief emotional range; dark humor in Seven Year Bitch lyrics)
