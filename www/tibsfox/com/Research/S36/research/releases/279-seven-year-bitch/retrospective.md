## What Worked

1. **The handling of Stefanie Sargent's death is factual and respectful.** Documented accurately (June 27, 1992, age 24, drug overdose), acknowledged as a defining event, and then the analysis moves forward with the band -- because the band moved forward. The death is not sensationalized; it is the necessary context for everything that follows.
2. **The "¡Viva Zapata!" tribute connection is documented fully.** The bridge between degrees 279 and 280 -- between Seven Year Bitch and The Gits, between Selene Vigil and Mia Zapata, between one death and another -- is one of the most important cross-degree connections in the engine. It is stated explicitly.
3. **C/Z Records entry noted as milestone.** Seattle's independent label infrastructure enters the engine's riot grrrl documentation at degree 279. The institutional geography is now complete: Kill Rock Stars (276-278), C/Z (279-280).
4. **Capitol Hill vs. Olympia distinction maintained.** The geographic distinction between the Olympia scene and the Capitol Hill scene is real and important. Seven Year Bitch is adjacent to riot grrrl but rooted in a different community.
5. **The "grief through defiance" framing is correct.** Seven Year Bitch does not surrender to grief. They carry it through continued action. "¡Viva Zapata!" is the most obvious expression of this: naming the album for a murdered friend is not wallowing; it is insisting on life.
6. **E=8 streak fourth consecutive documented.** The engine has now documented four consecutive E=8 entries (276-279). The pattern is noted without requiring more than the observation.

## Discoveries

1. **The 279-280 bridge is the most explicit cross-degree bridge in the riot grrrl section.** Seven Year Bitch names their album for Mia Zapata; The Gits is the next degree. The connection is direct, communal, and deeply moving. The engine's structure places them side by side; the "¡Viva Zapata!" title makes explicit what the structure implies.
2. **The Capitol Hill feminist punk scene has its own institutional identity.** C/Z Records is not Kill Rock Stars; Capitol Hill is not Olympia. The engine's documentation is richer for maintaining this distinction rather than collapsing both into "Pacific Northwest riot grrrl."
