Stefanie Sargent — Remembered

Stefanie Sargent, founding guitarist of Seven Year Bitch, died on June 27, 1992. She was twenty-four years old. She had recorded the band’s debut material before her death.

The band continued. The subsequent work — particularly “¡Viva Zapata!” — is made in full knowledge of this loss, without wallowing in it. The music carries grief the way defiance carries it: by continuing, by insisting, by being loud and alive. Stefanie Sargent is part of Seven Year Bitch’s history, not a footnote to it.

¡Viva Zapata! (1994) — The Central Statement

The album title is tribute to Mia Zapata of The Gits (degree 280), who was murdered on July 7, 1993. Selene Vigil and Mia Zapata were friends. The Capitol Hill feminist punk community lost two women in the space of a year: Sargent in June 1992, Zapata in July 1993.

“¡Viva Zapata!” means “Long live Zapata!” — both the Mexican revolutionary and the murdered singer. The exclamation points insist on life. The album refuses to accept that Mia Zapata can be silenced. Released on Atlantic Records — a major label, a wider audience, without dilution.

The defiance always wins. Grief is present; it does not defeat the music. The music defeats the grief, or at least it refuses to yield to it.

The Sound — E=8, Capitol Hill

Selene Vigil

Vocals

Direct, powerful, present. Dark humor and defiance. The lyrics are fierce and grief-inflected without being mournful. Vigil delivers them with full conviction. Capitol Hill feminist punk in its vocal fullest expression.

C/Z Records

Label Entry

Seattle-based independent label (also: Mudhoney, Love Battery). Enters the engine at degree 279. The first Seattle-based non-Sub Pop, non-Kill Rock Stars label in the riot grrrl register. C/Z then Atlantic for “¡Viva Zapata!”

Capitol Hill vs. Olympia

Geography

Seattle Capitol Hill feminist punk ≠ Olympia riot grrrl. Same energy level (E=8), different community, different institutional infrastructure, different relationship to the movement. Both valid. Both important.

Grief and Defiance

Character

Seven Year Bitch carries grief (Sargent 1992, Zapata 1993) through defiance, not through melancholy. The music responds to loss by being more alive, not less. This is the specific emotional achievement of “¡Viva Zapata!”
