The Lo-Fi Signal — Indie Rock Through Electronic Haze

764-HERO’s sound is a lo-fi signal: warm, rough, deliberately unpolished indie rock woven with electronic textures — loops, samples, synthesizer washes, drum machine patterns. The music sounds like a transmission arriving through static and haze, carrying a clear melodic and emotional signal underneath. John Atkins (guitar, vocals, electronics) and Polly Johnson (bass, vocals) built songs where indie rock and electronic elements coexist without hierarchy. Neither dominates. Both are lo-fi. Both are handmade.

Up Records — New Label in the Engine

764-HERO introduces Up Records to the label map — a Bellingham/Seattle indie label founded by Chris Takino in 1994. Up Records was home to Modest Mouse (early releases), the Microphones, Mirah, and other essential PNW indie acts. The label represented the lo-fi, experimental, DIY end of the Pacific Northwest indie spectrum. Up Records joins Sub Pop, Kill Rock Stars, K Records, Suicide Squeeze, Barsuk, and Sargent House in the engine’s label map.

Ahead of Its Time — The Indie/Electronic Crossover Before the Postal Service

764-HERO’s Salt Sinks and Sugar Floats (1997) incorporated electronic elements into lo-fi indie rock six years before the Postal Service’s Give Up (2003) defined the indie/electronic crossover. 764-HERO was experimenting with the formula — indie songwriting + electronic production as compositional tool — before it had a name. The pioneers are not always the beneficiaries. The band dissolved circa 2003, just as the genre they anticipated exploded.

Key Albums

Salt Sinks and Sugar Floats (1997, Up Records) — the definitive 764-HERO album. Lo-fi indie rock with electronic textures woven through. Songs that feel simultaneously handmade and electronic, warm and distant, intimate and abstracted.

Weekends of Sound (2001, Up Records) — the most ambitious album. More electronic, more expansive, more experimental. If Salt Sinks was indie rock with electronic touches, Weekends of Sound is closer to electronic music built on indie rock foundations. Critically acclaimed but arrived as the landscape shifted toward garage rock revival.
