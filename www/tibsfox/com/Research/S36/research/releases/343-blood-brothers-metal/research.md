## SHORELINE, WASHINGTON -- THE ORIGIN

### Not Seattle -- the Suburb North of the City

The Blood Brothers' geographic origin:

- **Shoreline, Washington.** Shoreline is a suburban city immediately north of Seattle, incorporated in 1995. Population approximately 55,000. It occupies the area previously known as the "North End" of Seattle before incorporation. Shoreline is a residential suburb of modest houses and strip commercial corridors -- the kind of place that produces bands through boredom and proximity to Seattle rather than through neighborhood-specific cultural infrastructure. The Blood Brothers are Shoreline's most notable contribution to rock music.
- **The DIY suburban context.** The Blood Brothers formed in the mid-1990s DIY scene -- house shows, small venues, Kinko's-printed flyers, 7-inch singles on small labels. The suburban context is relevant: the band's extremity (the screaming, the musical complexity, the lyrical darkness) is in part a reaction to the suburban environment. The aesthetics of post-hardcore are frequently the aesthetics of suburban alienation given musical form.
- **The Shoreline to Seattle pipeline.** Shoreline's proximity to Seattle means the band had access to Seattle's venues, labels, and scene without being from the Capitol Hill or U-District neighborhoods that anchor most of the engine's Seattle entries. The Blood Brothers are the first Shoreline band in the engine, and their entry at E=9 (one below the E=10 maximum) establishes Shoreline's place in the engine at near-maximum intensity.

(Sources: Shoreline Washington suburban city north of Seattle; incorporated 1995; population approximately 55,000; formerly North End Seattle; DIY scene house shows 7-inch singles small labels; post-hardcore suburban alienation aesthetics; Shoreline proximity to Seattle venues and scene)

## THE SOUND -- POST-HC/METAL AT E=9

### The Most Texturally Intense Vocals in the Engine

The Blood Brothers' sonic architecture:

- **The dual-vocalist screaming.** Johnny Whitney and Cody Votolato both scream simultaneously, in parallel, at different pitches and with different timbral qualities. This is not the one-screamer, one-melodic-vocalist dynamic that characterizes most post-hardcore -- both vocalists operate in the extreme register. Whitney's scream is higher-pitched, with a sharp, piercing quality. Votolato's scream is lower-pitched and rawer. The dual extreme vocal creates a texture of maximum density that is unique in the engine's S36 catalog.
- **The keyboard integration.** Johnny Whitney played keyboard alongside his vocal role. The keyboard textures in Blood Brothers' music are not background or ambient -- they are aggressive, dissonant, sometimes melodic, always present as a structural element. The keyboard-and-screaming combination is unusual in metal and post-hardcore contexts; it gives the Blood Brothers a distinctive textural identity that separates them from pure guitar-based metal.
- **The rhythmic complexity.** Mark Gajadhar's drumming and Morgan Henderson's bass provide the rhythmic foundation for the vocal and guitar complexity. The rhythmic patterns are not conventional rock drumming -- they use irregular accents, polyrhythmic figures, and tempo shifts that match the compositional ambition of the overall music. Henderson's bass (he joined the band later in the career) adds another layer of independent melodic and rhythmic movement.
- **The guitar approach.** Cody Votolato and Jordan Billie's guitar work is dissonant, technically precise, and harmonically ambiguous. Riffs do not settle into comfortable tonal centers; the harmonic language is angular and resistant. Combined with the dual-screaming and the keyboard, the guitar creates a total sonic environment of maximum density and maximum unease.

(Sources: Johnny Whitney dual-vocalist screaming higher-pitched sharp piercing; Cody Votolato dual-vocalist screaming lower-pitched rawer; dual extreme vocal -- not one-screamer one-melodic; Whitney keyboard alongside vocals; keyboard as aggressive structural element not background; Mark Gajadhar drums irregular accents polyrhythmic tempo shifts; Morgan Henderson bass independent melodic/rhythmic movement; Jordan Billie guitar; dissonant harmonically ambiguous guitar riffs)

## CRIMES (2004) -- THE CAREER-DEFINING RECORD

### The Blood Brothers at Their Peak

*Crimes* is the album that defines the Blood Brothers' position in the engine:

- **The production.** *Crimes* (2004, Artist Direct) was produced by Ross Robinson, who had previously produced records for Korn, Slipknot, and Glassjaw. Robinson's production approach is maximalist and close-miked, capturing the physical intensity of the performance rather than cleaning it up. The result is one of the most sonically overwhelming records in the engine's documented catalog: the dual screaming, the keyboard textures, the drums, and the guitars are all given full presence in the mix.
- **The tracks.** "Ambulance vs. Ambulance" opens the record with immediate intensity -- the dual screaming, the driving rhythm section, the keyboard fragments. "Rats and Rats and Rats for Candy" is the most melodically accessible track, demonstrating the band's capacity for hooks within the extreme context. "Love Rhymes With Hideous Car Wreck" is the post-hardcore peak. "My First Kiss at the Public Execution" exemplifies the lyrical approach -- the theatrical, surreal imagery that runs through the Blood Brothers' catalog.
- **The lyrical world.** Johnny Whitney's lyrics are surreal, violent, and theatrical. They do not describe the world realistically -- they describe it through distorted, nightmarish images that function as emotional amplification rather than narrative. "Love Rhymes With Hideous Car Wreck." "Your Rooftop is Burning and You're Standing on it (Singing and Clapping Your Hands)." The titles alone signal the aesthetic program: melodrama made literal, disaster as metaphor, the body as a site of extreme circumstance.
- **The Ross Robinson context.** Robinson's production work on *Crimes* is one of the engine's most significant producer-artist alignments. He brought the same close-miked physical intensity to the Blood Brothers that he had brought to nu-metal (Korn, Slipknot) and alternative metal (Glassjaw), but the Blood Brothers' compositional ambition and vocal complexity produced results that are unique in his catalog.

(Sources: Crimes 2004 Artist Direct; Ross Robinson production; Korn Slipknot Glassjaw previous Robinson productions; maximalist close-miked production approach; Ambulance vs Ambulance opens the record; Rats and Rats and Rats for Candy most melodically accessible; Love Rhymes With Hideous Car Wreck; My First Kiss at the Public Execution; Whitney lyrics surreal violent theatrical; Your Rooftop is Burning EP title)

## DISCOGRAPHY -- THE FULL ARC

### 1997-2007: Ten Years of Maximum Intensity

- **The early period (1997-2000).** *Discord Harmony* (1999), *Spit Shine Your Black Clouds* (2000). The earliest recordings are more conventionally post-hardcore than the later catalog -- the dual-screaming template is being established, the keyboard integration is emerging, but the musical architecture is not yet at the complexity of the mid-period work.
- ***This Adultery Is Ripe* EP (2000).** A transitional document showing the band moving toward the denser, more dissonant approach of the later albums. The EP was released on Chewbacca Kaboom, a small label, and documented within the hardcore underground before the band reached a wider audience.
- ***...Burn Piano Island, Burn* (2003, Artist Direct).** The record that brought the Blood Brothers to national attention. Produced with the density that would characterize *Crimes*. "Cecilia and the Silhouette Saloon," "Guitarmy" -- the tracks that established the band's profile in the post-hardcore and metal press.
- ***Crimes* (2004, Artist Direct).** The career peak. Ross Robinson producing. Dual screaming at maximum. Keyboard textures fully integrated. The record the engine documents at E=9.
- ***Young Machetes* (2006, V2/Equal Vision).** The final studio album before the band's 2007 dissolution. More varied in approach than *Crimes*, with more quiet passages and more explicit melody. Some critics found it a disappointment after *Crimes*; others found it the most compositionally mature record. The band dissolved after this record's tour cycle.

(Sources: Discord Harmony 1999; Spit Shine Your Black Clouds 2000; This Adultery Is Ripe EP 2000 Chewbacca Kaboom; Burn Piano Island Burn 2003 Artist Direct; Cecilia and the Silhouette Saloon Guitarmy; Crimes 2004; Young Machetes 2006 V2 Equal Vision; band dissolution 2007)

## E=9 -- THE PENULTIMATE STEP

### One Below the Historic Maximum

- **Why E=9 and not E=10.** The Blood Brothers are at E=9 and not E=10 because Botch (degree 344) is the engine's first and only E=10 -- the absolute ceiling, the historic maximum. The Blood Brothers are penultimate in the escalation because their music is genuinely extreme but does not reach the mathcore technical intensity of *We Are the Romans*. The calibration is: Blood Brothers E=9 = the most extreme post-hardcore in the engine; Botch E=10 = the most intense mathcore in the engine and the most intense any S36 entry reaches.
- **The ramp transition.** E=8 (Gruntruck) → E=9 (Blood Brothers) → E=10 (Botch). The ramp rises by one at this step. The dual screaming and compositional complexity of the Blood Brothers is a genuine increase in intensity over Gruntruck's metal approach, calibrated as one step closer to the ceiling.
- **The SPS pairing.** Peregrine stoop through tern colony (SPS 343, E=9): coastal cliff, summer, dive-bomb through screaming colony. The Peregrine's stoop at 200+ mph through the tern colony -- which erupts in screaming chaos at the moment of the stoop -- is the appropriate SPS entry for the Blood Brothers' E=9: sudden explosive intensity, screaming from multiple sources, the moment when everything goes from tense to chaotic. The Blood Brothers' music sounds like what it would feel like to be a tern when the Peregrine stoops.
