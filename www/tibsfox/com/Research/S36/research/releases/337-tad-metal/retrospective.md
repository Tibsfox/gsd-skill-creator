## What Worked

1. **Georgetown as the metal summit's opening location is precise.** The industrial south end -- Duwamish, Boeing Field, railyards -- provides the physical analog to TAD's sonic approach. The neighborhood that ships and processes things produced the band that processed heaviness.
2. **The mass vs. velocity distinction clarifies TAD's E=9 mechanism.** Mudhoney maximizes velocity, not mass. Soundgarden uses dynamics. TAD sustains both mass and velocity with almost no dynamic relief. This is the specific E=9 formula.
3. **The Jack Endino connection links TAD to the Sub Pop production ecosystem.** Endino produced both TAD and Nirvana's Bleach. The 1988-1989 Sub Pop peer relationship, and the dramatic commercial divergence after 1991, is one of rock history's most instructive comparisons.
4. **The physical dimension of TAD's sound is documented.** Sub-bass frequency combination of bass and dual low-tuned guitars creates a physical acoustic event at live volume. This is the E=9 quality that recorded work only partially captures.

## Discoveries

1. **Eight Arms to Hold You's title origin is an unexpectedly rich cultural artifact.** The title was originally intended for the second Beatles film before it became Help!. TAD's appropriation of this discarded Beatles title demonstrates cultural intelligence beneath the heavy exterior.
2. **Butch Vig produced both 8-Way Santa (1991) and Nevermind (1991).** In the same year, Vig produced the heaviest Sub Pop record and the most commercially successful grunge record. The producer's versatility, and the divergent commercial outcomes, is a significant finding.
