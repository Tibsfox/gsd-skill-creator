## The Central District Concentration: 5 of 6 Degrees

This is the retrospective's primary structural observation and it must be stated plainly: **five of the first six degrees of the Seattle 360 engine are Central District jazz artists.** This is an extraordinarily concentrated geographic/genre cluster. The numbers:

| Degree | Artist | Central District | Jazz |
|--------|--------|:---:|:---:|
| 0 | Quincy Jones | Yes | Yes |
| 1 | Bill Frisell | No (settled in Seattle, not CD specifically) | Yes (experimental/downtown) |
| 2 | Wayne Horvitz | No (settled in Columbia City) | Yes (experimental/composed) |
| 3 | Ernestine Anderson | Yes | Yes |
| 4 | Floyd Standifer | Yes | Yes |
| 5 | Overton Berry | Yes (native-born) | Yes |

Five Central District-connected artists. Five jazz artists (all six if you count Frisell and Horvitz as jazz-adjacent). The sole degree that is neither Central District nor conventional jazz is degree 1 (Frisell), who is still Seattle-based and still jazz-rooted.

**Is this a problem?** No. It is the documented reality of Seattle's music history. The Central District was the city's Black cultural center for most of the twentieth century. The Jackson Street corridor was one of the most significant jazz scenes on the West Coast. The concentration of talent in this neighborhood is historical fact, not selection bias.

**But it does raise the question: when does the circle leave the Central District?** The S36 engine is a 360-degree rotation. It cannot spend its entire first quadrant (degrees 0-89) in one neighborhood playing one genre. The engine needs to demonstrate that it can map Seattle's full musical geography -- Capitol Hill, Ballard, the University District, downtown, the suburbs -- and its full genre range -- rock, grunge, hip-hop, electronic, folk, classical. Degree 6 is the natural rotation point.

**The emerging question is not whether the Central District cluster is valid. It is whether the engine's sequencing has the discipline to leave it.** The Central District has been thoroughly documented: six artist-city patterns, five directly connected artists, three Jackson Street participants, one native-born. The thread is rich. But the circle must rotate.

## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The Persistence pattern emerged naturally from Berry's biography.** Born in the Central District. Never left. Still playing. Sixty years. The pattern is not forced — it is the simplest possible description of Berry's career. The S36 engine's sequential structure made the emergence visible because the previous five patterns (Export, Draw, Exile, Stay, Teacher) all involve some form of movement or institutional activity. Berry's pattern is the absence of both: pure persistence.

2. **The Amiga Principle bracket (Jones 0° - Berry 5°) is a genuine structural discovery.** It was not planned. The degree 0 research identified the Amiga Principle as arranging-as-architecture. Three consecutive degrees found it inapplicable. Degree 5 found it fully realized in a completely different form — physical rather than organizational. The bracket creates a structural symmetry across the first six degrees that validates the engine's sequential-accumulation method.

3. **Blue notes as piano-specific theory opened a new technical dimension.** The micro-tonal inflection problem — how a fixed-pitch instrument evokes a between-the-keys pitch — is genuinely interesting music theory that no previous degree addressed. The connection to Anderson (vocal bend) and Standifer (embouchure lip) creates a three-instrument cross-reference on the same conceptual problem. This is the engine working as designed.

4. **The formal/informal pedagogy axis was predicted at degree 4 and confirmed at degree 5.** This is a carry-forward success — the degree 4 retrospective named the axis and identified Berry as the informal pole. Degree 5 confirmed it with full documentation. The carry-forward mechanism is producing real predictive value.

5. **Cocktail jazz harmony as serious music theory was worth the space.** The research pushes back against the pejorative connotation of "cocktail jazz" by demonstrating that the harmonic vocabulary is as complex as bebop. This is a genuine critical contribution — revaluing a style that jazz criticism has historically dismissed.

### Source Quality

6. **Source density for Berry is comparable to Standifer — local, adequate, not abundant.** Two consecutive local-label artists confirms the pattern: Central District "stay" musicians have local documentation. The engine's methodology — compensating for source sparsity with analytical depth — continues to work.

7. **Paul de Barros's *Jackson Street After Hours* remains the spine source.** Six degrees, all referencing it. The dependency risk noted at degree 4 is real but manageable: the book is authoritative and no alternative comprehensive source exists for Seattle jazz history at this depth.

## The Central District Concentration — Full Analysis

Five of six degrees are Central District jazz artists. This section provides the full analysis that the degree 4 retrospective began.

### Why the Concentration is Historically Valid

The Central District's musical output between 1940 and 1970 was extraordinary by any measure. The Jackson Street corridor was the primary jazz venue district on the West Coast north of San Francisco. The neighborhood produced or hosted Quincy Jones, Ray Charles, Ernestine Anderson, Floyd Standifer, Overton Berry, Buddy Catlett, Jimi Hendrix, and dozens of other musicians whose careers span the full range of American popular music. The Garfield High School music program was a conservatory-grade pipeline that produced nationally significant artists from a single public school. The S36 engine's concentration of Central District artists in its first six degrees is not a distortion of history — it IS the history.

### Why the Engine Must Rotate

Historical validity is necessary but not sufficient. The S36 engine is designed to map Seattle's complete musical geography across 360 degrees. At the current rate, the first quadrant (degrees 0-89) would be entirely Central District jazz. This would produce an extraordinary case study of one neighborhood — and fail as a map of the city.

The rotation question is not "should the engine leave the Central District?" (yes, obviously) but "when, and to what?" The degree 4 retrospective predicted that degree 5 would extend the thread. It has. The thread is now six degrees deep, five artists thick, and documented from every angle: incubator, destination, homecoming, permanent post, birthplace. There is nothing left to prove. The Central District thread is established. It can be revisited at any subsequent degree. It does not need to be continuous.

### Recommendation for Degree 6

Degree 6 should either:
- **Rotate genre:** Move to rock, grunge, hip-hop, electronic, folk, or classical.
- **Rotate geography:** Move to Capitol Hill, Ballard, University District, downtown, or the suburbs.
- **Rotate both:** Ideally, degree 6 represents a different neighborhood AND a different genre, demonstrating the engine's range.

If degree 6 is another Central District jazz artist, the retrospective will need to justify the seventh consecutive jazz degree and the sixth Central District connection. The bar for that justification is very high.
