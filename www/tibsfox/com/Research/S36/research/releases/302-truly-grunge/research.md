## TRULY -- THE BAND REVISITED

### Hiro Yamamoto and the Post-Screaming Trees Lineage

Truly's formation history connects to one of the engine's significant threads -- the Screaming Trees and the Eastern Washington connection to the Seattle scene:

- **Hiro Yamamoto.** The bass player who most directly connects Truly to the engine's prior arc is Hiro Yamamoto, who had previously played with Soundgarden. The Soundgarden-Truly connection through Yamamoto is one of the engine's more significant member crossovers -- Soundgarden representing the commercial peak of the Seattle sound, Truly representing a more crystalline, less commercially successful but artistically distinct direction.
- **Robert Roth (guitar/vocals).** Truly's primary songwriter and vocalist. Roth's guitar work is characteristically clean for a grunge-era Capitol Hill band -- not the fuzz-heavy distortion of Mudhoney or the metal-influenced tone of Soundgarden, but a cleaner, more atmospheric sound that incorporates melody as a primary value alongside heaviness.
- **Mark Pickerel (drums).** Previously of Screaming Trees. The Screaming Trees produced multiple threads in the Seattle alternative ecosystem: Gary Lee Conner and Van Conner in Trees, Mark Pickerel moving to Truly, Mark Lanegan's solo work. Pickerel's drumming in Truly is rhythmically sophisticated.
- **The Capitol Hill base.** Like many of the engine's most artistically specific entries, Truly was rooted in Capitol Hill. The neighborhood's experimental community provided the audience and context for music that was too melodic for the noise crowd and too clean for the grunge mainstream.

## THE ISLAND RECORDS DEAL

### Major Label and the Post-Grunge Moment

Truly's relationship with Island Records tracks the major-label expansion into Seattle's alternative scene:

- **Island Records context.** Island was one of the most artistically credible major labels of the 1990s -- the home of U2, PJ Harvey, and later Pulp. Truly's Island deal placed them in distinguished company but did not translate to commercial breakthrough. The label had the credibility to attract artistically serious bands but could not guarantee commercial success for acts outside the mainstream alternative spectrum.
- **The post-grunge commercial problem.** By the time Truly was fully established on Island, the grunge moment had peaked commercially. *Nevermind* (1991), *Ten* (1991), and *Superunknown* (1994) were the commercial peak. By 1995-1996, the commercial landscape was shifting toward post-Oasis Britpop influences, electronic music, and the consolidation of alternative radio around a narrower sound. Truly's crystalline melodic grunge was not the sound the market was looking for.
- **Artistic integrity vs. commercial timing.** Truly's situation is one of the engine's clearest examples of artistic quality decoupled from commercial success. The Island deal gave them resources and distribution; the commercial moment had already moved on.

(Sources: Island Records artist roster; Truly Island deal; post-grunge commercial shift 1994-1996; commercial timing vs. artistic quality; label credibility)

## THE SOUND -- GRUNGE/ALT, E=6

### Crystalline Heaviness and Melodic Sophistication

Truly's sound is one of the most distinctive in the engine's Capitol Hill sequence:

- **The crystalline production aesthetic.** Where most grunge bands of the Capitol Hill scene leaned into rawness -- rough-edged production, drums forward, vocals pushed hard -- Truly produced a sound that was cleaner, more melodically defined, with guitar tones that sang rather than ground. This is post-grunge in the original sense: taking grunge's emotional weight and applying it to more production-conscious, melodically sophisticated song structures.
- **Melody as primary value.** Truly's songs are melodic in a way that separates them from grunge's blues-rock roots. The melodies are prominent and developed -- not incidental to the heaviness but load-bearing structures that carry the songs. This made Truly an unusual band in the Capitol Hill context: too clean for noise rock, too melodic for grunge, too heavy for alternative pop.
- **The atmospheric quality.** Truly's music has an atmospheric quality -- a sense of space in the production, reverb and delay used to create depth -- that is closer to British shoegaze (Ride, Slowdive, Lush) than to Pacific Northwest grunge. This connection is not coincidental: Truly was absorbing influences beyond the immediate Seattle scene.
- **E=6 calibration.** E=6 is correct for Truly: the music has genuine weight and emotional engagement, but the crystalline production and melodic sophistication prevent it from reaching the raw impact of E=7 (Hammerbox, ahead) or E=8 (the apex predators now entering in SPS). The beauty mediates the force.

(Sources: Truly sound analysis; crystalline production aesthetic; melody as primary value; shoegaze influence; British alternative comparison; E=6 calibration)

## DISCOGRAPHY

### Core Albums

- ***Fast Stories from Kid Coma* (1995, Island).** The definitive Truly statement. Recorded with producer Jack Endino -- the producer most associated with the early grunge sound (Nirvana's *Bleach*, Mudhoney's debut) -- the album produces a distinctive combination of Endino's raw tracking approach and Truly's melodic sophistication. The result is one of the best albums in the Capitol Hill alt-rock catalog of the mid-1990s.
- ***Feeling You Up* (1996, Island).** EP and follow-up material. Continuing the melodic direction of *Fast Stories*.
- ***Sunset Gun* (2002, Jetpak).** Later period on an independent label, the Island deal over. *Sunset Gun* documents Truly in a post-commercial moment, still making music that is artistically coherent and melodically sophisticated without the major-label infrastructure. This is commitment to the sound beyond the moment.

(Sources: Fast Stories from Kid Coma 1995 Island; Jack Endino production; Nirvana Bleach; Mudhoney Endino connection; Sunset Gun 2002 Jetpak; Truly discography)

## JACK ENDINO AND THE PRODUCTION QUESTION

### The Raw Producer Meets the Crystalline Sound

The choice of Jack Endino to produce *Fast Stories from Kid Coma* is one of the more interesting production decisions in the Seattle alt-rock catalog:

- **Endino's credentials.** Jack Endino is the father of the Seattle Sound -- the engineer and producer who tracked Nirvana's *Bleach*, Mudhoney's first singles, Soundgarden early records, and dozens of the grunge era's foundational recordings. His aesthetic is raw, immediate, and guitar-forward.
- **The Truly-Endino tension.** Truly's melodic sophistication and crystalline production aesthetic would seem to be in tension with Endino's rawness. The production of *Fast Stories* resolves this tension productively: Endino's directness disciplines Truly's atmospheric tendencies, while Truly's melodic intelligence prevents the record from becoming another raw-grunge document. The combination is the record's most distinctive quality.
- **Endino as engine thread.** Jack Endino has appeared as a production credit throughout the engine's core grunge entries. His presence at Truly reinforces the engine's compositional principle: Seattle's music ecosystem was small enough that the same key figures (Endino, Sub Pop, Jonathan Poneman, Bruce Pavitt) appear across multiple artistic contexts.

(Sources: Jack Endino production history; Nirvana Bleach; Mudhoney; Soundgarden; Truly Fast Stories production; Endino raw aesthetic; tension and resolution with Truly melodic direction)

## LEGACY AND SIGNIFICANCE

### What Truly Represents

- **The melodic grunge gap.** Truly represents a space in the grunge landscape that existed but was underrepresented commercially: heavy guitar music with genuine melodic sophistication, atmospheric production, and emotional nuance. This space was occupied most commercially by bands like Alice in Chains (in their slower moments) and The Smashing Pumpkins (in their alt-rock mode). Truly occupied it more quietly and more specifically.
- **The Capitol Hill melodic tradition.** Not every Capitol Hill band was about aggression or abrasion. Truly represents a tradition of melodic, atmospheric rock that ran through the neighborhood alongside the heavier and noisier bands. This tradition is worth preserving in the engine's record.
- **Retrospective significance.** In the years since their commercial moment, Truly's *Fast Stories from Kid Coma* has gained retrospective appreciation as one of the better records of the mid-1990s Capitol Hill scene. The engine's return at degree 302 reflects this reappraisal.

(Sources: Melodic grunge tradition; Alice in Chains and Smashing Pumpkins comparison; Capitol Hill melodic thread; retrospective appreciation; Fast Stories critical legacy)
