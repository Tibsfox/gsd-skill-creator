The Crystalline Grunge Return

Truly returns at degree 302 for a final-sixth reappraisal. The Capitol Hill band — Robert Roth (guitar/vocals), Hiro Yamamoto (bass, former Soundgarden), Mark Pickerel (drums, former Screaming Trees) — occupies one of the most specific niches in the Seattle alt-rock landscape: melodic, atmospheric grunge that is too clean for the noise crowd, too heavy for alternative pop, too sophisticated for the raw grunge mainstream. Island Records distributed the sound broadly; the commercial moment had already moved on.

The return in the final sixth is a reappraisal: what does Truly mean now, when the music can be heard without the genre's commercial noise? The answer is that it holds. Fast Stories from Kid Coma (1995, produced by Jack Endino) remains one of the better records in the Capitol Hill alt-rock catalog.

"The melody is not incidental to the heaviness — it is load-bearing. The crystalline production disciplines the grunge impulse without domesticating it."

The Soundgarden and Screaming Trees Lineage

Truly's personnel tracks two of the engine's major bands. Hiro Yamamoto brought the Soundgarden thread — one of the engine's commercial peaks — into a post-commercial direction. Mark Pickerel brought the Screaming Trees thread: Eastern Washington roots in the Seattle mainstream, rhythmically sophisticated playing. The combination produced a band that could not have existed without the Seattle ecosystem's interconnectedness.

Jack Endino and the Production Tension

The choice of Jack Endino — the engineer/producer most associated with raw grunge (Nirvana's Bleach, Mudhoney's debut) — to produce Truly's most melodic and sophisticated record is one of the engine's more interesting production decisions. Endino's directness disciplined Truly's atmospheric tendencies; Truly's melodic intelligence prevented the record from becoming another raw-grunge document. The tension is the record's most distinctive quality.

E=6 -- The Melodic Weight

E=6 is correct for Truly: genuine weight and emotional engagement, but the crystalline production and melodic sophistication mediate the force. Below E=7 (Hammerbox, ahead) where the rawness overtakes the melody. Above E=5 where the energy becomes spacious and ambient. Truly occupies the middle with precision: heavy enough, beautiful enough, specific enough to hold its place in the engine's energy sequence.
