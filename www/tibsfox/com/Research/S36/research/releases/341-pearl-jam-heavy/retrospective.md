## Discoveries

1. **Jack Irons as the connective tissue between San Diego and Seattle is often understated.** Irons was the person who passed the demo tape to Gossard. Without Irons, the Vedder-to-Seattle connection does not happen in the way it did. The Chili Peppers → Irons → Pearl Jam connection is a genuine cross-scene influence vector.
2. **The Ticketmaster confrontation (1994) is more significant than its outcome suggests.** The DOJ investigation was real. The precedent of a major act filing an antitrust complaint against the dominant ticketing monopoly was genuinely novel. The confrontation failed commercially (too few accessible venues) but documented the structural problem three decades before Live Nation monopoly concerns became mainstream public discourse.
3. **Vitalogy's vinyl-first release (1994) is an underappreciated institutional decision.** Releasing on vinyl one week before CD was a deliberate rebuke of the CD commercial apparatus at the moment of maximum CD dominance. The decision cost money. Pearl Jam made it.
