## Structural Event 2: KCMU-to-KEXP Pipeline Begins

- **Situation:** KCMU (freeform college radio, UW, 1972) became KEXP (independent powerhouse, 2001). Fresh Tracks on KCMU in the 1980s is early DNA of KEXP's willingness to platform unheard music. Degrees 126-128 all have KCMU/radio connections.
- **Root Cause:** KCMU/KEXP is one of the most important music institutions in Seattle history. Its freeform ethos enabled programming (like Fresh Tracks) that commercial radio would never air. The pipeline from college radio to global independent station is a structural arc in the engine.
- **Recommendation:** Document the KCMU-to-KEXP pipeline. Track at degrees 127-128.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- KCMU-to-KEXP pipeline begins at 126. Track at 127-128.

## Structural Event 3: Rainier Valley Enters the Engine

- **Situation:** Rainier Valley is a new neighborhood in the engine. Southeast Seattle. One of the most ethnically diverse corridors in the United States. The base for Seattle hip-hop (with the Central District).
- **Root Cause:** The geography of genre in Seattle maps to neighborhoods. Capitol Hill = punk/indie rock. Rainier Valley/Central District = hip-hop. This is not random; it reflects demographics, economics, and cultural history. The engine's geographic scope now includes Rainier Valley.
