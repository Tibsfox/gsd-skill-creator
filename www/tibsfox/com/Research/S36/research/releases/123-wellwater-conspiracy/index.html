The Supergroup Orbit

The Wellwater Conspiracy was a side project of Matt Cameron (Soundgarden, Pearl Jam) and John McBain (Monster Magnet), formed in 1994 as a vehicle for unfiltered musical energy outside their main-band commitments. Cameron is the connective tissue of grunge — the only musician who was a permanent member of both Soundgarden and Pearl Jam, plus Temple of the Dog. McBain’s Monster Magnet pedigree brought crushing space rock guitar to the partnership.

E=6 at degree 123 makes this the fifth E=6 in the engine’s history — Hendrix (36), Chong the Nomad (107), Veronica Lipgloss (108), No-Anchor (109), and now Wellwater Conspiracy (123). Five E=6 entries, five different genres: Rock, Electronic/R&B, Punk/Electronic, Noise/Experimental, Garage/Space Rock. E=6 continues to transcend genre — it is an energy level, not a style. The quiet corridor (119–122: E=2, E=4, E=5, E=5) acted as decompression between the E=6 three-peat (107–109) and this return.

Cameron: The Hub of the Supergroup Network

Matt Cameron is the only musician to have been a permanent member of both Soundgarden and Pearl Jam — two of the four core grunge bands. Add Temple of the Dog and the Wellwater Conspiracy, and Cameron appears in four cross-band configurations. No other Seattle musician connects the scene’s biggest names as directly.

Cameron’s drumming in the Wellwater Conspiracy is feral compared to his main-band work. In Soundgarden, his playing was metronomically complex. In Pearl Jam, it is powerful and groove-oriented. In the Wellwater Conspiracy, the restraint is gone. The drums hit harder, the fills are wilder, the tempo is more elastic. This is what E=6 sounds like when a world-class drummer removes all filters.

McBain’s guitar — heavy, distorted, feedback-drenched from his Monster Magnet years — fills the sonic space with warm, enveloping psychedelic distortion. The guitar is not accompaniment; it is an environment. Three albums document the arc: Declaration of Conformity (1997), the self-titled (1999, with Ben Shepherd of Soundgarden on bass), and Brotherhood of Electric (2001).

Hollywood Records — The Unlikely Label

The Wellwater Conspiracy released through Hollywood Records, a label owned by the Walt Disney Company. This is the first Disney-owned label in the engine — a structural anomaly. Hollywood Records was primarily known for mainstream pop and soundtracks. A garage/space rock supergroup on a Disney label reflects the late-1990s era when major labels signed anything connected to the Seattle scene. Cameron’s name — the drummer from Soundgarden and Pearl Jam — was sufficient to attract corporate interest, even for deliberately uncommercial music.
