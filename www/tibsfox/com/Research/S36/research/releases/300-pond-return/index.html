Pond — The Second View

Pond first appeared at degree 230 — the Portland-Seattle axis, Charlie Campbell's melodic grunge, Fremont, Sub Pop's geographic scope. The return adds the retrospective: Campbell's full career across Pond, Brad, and Satchel is now complete as an engine narrative. The melodic weight of Pond was preparation for Brad's soulful sophistication. Stone Gossard, Shawn Smith, Regan Hagar — the Brad connection places Pond's alumni in the center of Seattle's mid-1990s respected (but non-commercial) alt-rock scene.

Pond (1993, Sub Pop)

Debut

Grunge/indie debut. Portland origin, Fremont relocation. Melodic riffs anchored by heavy rhythm section. Campbell's voice-forward songwriting established.

Fremont at the Five-Sixths Mark

Fremont: the Center of the Universe, the Fremont Troll, the Lenin statue, the artisanal and the absurdist in community partnership. In the 290s, Fremont appeared twice: Gas Huffer (E=8, punk-garage, humor as structure, degree 292) and now Pond (E=6, melodic grunge, indie sophistication, degree 300). The neighborhood's range — from the loudest garage punk to the most melodically sophisticated indie rock — is documented across these two entries. The five-sixths mark belongs to the quieter end of that range.
