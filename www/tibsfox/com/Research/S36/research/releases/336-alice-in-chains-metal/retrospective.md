## What Worked

1. **The guitar-vocal harmony system is the correct differentiating focus.** The AIC harmonic structure (guitar riff + vocal melody in harmonic relationship, minor seconds and tritones in the intervals) is what makes their sound distinct from all other Metal Summit entries. This is the right emphasis.
2. **Staley handled with full respect and complete biography.** Death date, cause, context (April 5 = Cobain date), and the documented isolation of his final years -- presented factually without romanticization or sensationalism.
3. **The Metal Summit summary table in the index.html is the right closing gesture.** Degrees 333-336 together, geography, energy -- the arc complete and visible.
4. **Beacon Hill as geographic completion of the arc.** Capitol Hill (333) to Aberdeen (334) to U-District (335) to Beacon Hill (336). The full traverse of Seattle-Aberdeen geography. The arc's completion makes geographic sense.
5. **Cobain-Staley April 5 date connection.** Both 1994 and 2002. Noted as historical fact, not used for dramatic effect.

## Discoveries

1. **"Would?" (AIC, Dirt) and "Hunger Strike" (Soundgarden, Temple of the Dog) are both tributes to Andrew Wood.** Two consecutive engine degrees (335, 336) both have documented tributes to the same person. This is one of the engine's more remarkable consecutive cross-references.
2. **Cantrell's G&L ASAT guitar is a specific differentiator.** The Telecaster-derived bright tone through Marshall distortion creates a mid-range emphasis that makes the guitar-vocal harmony system more audible than darker, bass-heavy tones would. The gear choice serves the compositional system.

- **Energy:** E=9 + E=9 = E=18. **THE METAL SUMMIT'S DRAMATIC CLOSE.**

The wolf's sustained howl, building in the high mountain meadow, cut by the sudden crack of close lightning -- the most dramatically interrupted natural event in the engine. Alice in Chains' held guitar-vocal harmonies, the most melodically beautiful element in the heaviest music, carrying the weight of autobiography and grief -- suddenly darkened by the dissonant intervals that are always there in the AIC harmonic system.
