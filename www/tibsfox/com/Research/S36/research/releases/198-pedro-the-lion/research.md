## GREENWOOD ENTERS -- NEW NEIGHBORHOOD

### North Seattle

Greenwood is a new neighborhood in the engine:

- **Greenwood.** A residential neighborhood in north Seattle. Not as famous as Capitol Hill or Ballard in the music geography, but home to a quiet creative community. Less nightlife, more studios and practice spaces. A place where artists live and work without the scene pressure of Capitol Hill.
- **Why Greenwood for Pedro the Lion?** David Bazan has been associated with north Seattle throughout his career. Greenwood reflects the band's aesthetic: residential, understated, not performing cool. The music happens in living rooms and small clubs, not in the spotlight.
- **Engine geography update.** Greenwood is the first north Seattle neighborhood to enter the engine. The geography has been dominated by Capitol Hill, Ballard, and the University District. Greenwood opens a new quadrant -- quieter, more residential, further from the waterfront.
- **Neighborhood energy.** Greenwood at E=4 matches its character: present but not loud. Measured. A place where things happen without calling attention to themselves.

## DAVID BAZAN -- THE SOLE CONSTANT

### One Voice, Many Configurations

Pedro the Lion is David Bazan:

- **David Bazan.** Singer, guitarist, songwriter, the only constant member across all eras of Pedro the Lion. His voice: warm, measured, conversational. Not a showman. A storyteller. The voice of someone thinking out loud, working through ideas in real time.
- **Band as project.** Pedro the Lion has had many members over the years, but the creative vision is Bazan's. He writes, he sings, he often plays most of the instruments on recordings. The band name is a container for Bazan's songwriting.
- **Solo career overlap.** During Pedro the Lion's hiatus (2006-2017), Bazan released music under his own name. The solo work is continuous with Pedro the Lion -- same voice, same concerns, same aesthetic. The distinction between "Pedro the Lion" and "David Bazan" is a formality.
- **Storytelling approach.** Bazan's songs are often narrative -- characters, situations, moral dilemmas. Not confessional diary entries but constructed stories that examine human behavior. The *Control* album (2002) is a concept album about a man descending into corruption. *Winners Never Quit* (2000) explores suburban disillusionment.

(Sources: David Bazan career; Pedro the Lion discography; solo work; storytelling approach; band membership)

## *CONTROL* (2002) -- THE NARRATIVE ALBUM

### Jade Tree Records

*Control* is Pedro the Lion's most acclaimed album:

- **Concept album.** *Control* tells the story of a man who rises in the corporate world, cheats on his wife, and spirals into moral collapse. Each song advances the narrative. The songwriting is precise, economical, devastating.
- **Jade Tree Records.** Released on Jade Tree Records, a Philadelphia-based indie label that specialized in emo and post-hardcore. Jade Tree also released Promise Ring, Jets to Brazil, Lifetime. The label's identity -- emotionally intelligent, musically restrained -- fit Bazan perfectly.
- **The sound.** Sparse. Guitar, bass, drums, voice. Minimal overdubs. The production is clean but not polished -- you can hear the room. The restraint of the arrangements mirrors the restraint of the storytelling: every note, every word, every silence is intentional.
- **Key tracks.** "Priests and Paramedics" -- the album's centerpiece, the protagonist's moment of crisis. "Magazine" -- pop culture's empty promises. "Options" -- the small decision that starts the descent.
- **Legacy.** *Control* is regarded as one of the finest indie/emo albums of the early 2000s. Its influence runs through the "slowcore" and "sadcore" traditions -- quiet music with heavy emotional content.

(Sources: *Control* (2002); Jade Tree Records; concept album structure; critical reception; emo canon)

## FAITH AND DOUBT -- THE EMO THEOLOGY

### Searching in Song

Pedro the Lion exists at the intersection of faith and doubt:

- **Christian upbringing.** Bazan grew up in a devout Christian household. His early work is informed by faith -- not as certainty but as question. The songs wrestle with belief, morality, meaning.
- **Loss of faith.** Over the course of his career, Bazan moved from questioning faith to abandoning organized religion. This journey is documented across albums: early work with theological language, later work with explicit doubt and rejection.
- **The faith-doubt thread in emo.** This connects directly to Sunny Day Real Estate (degree 197). Jeremy Enigk's conversion to Christianity. Bazan's departure from it. Two emo artists, opposite trajectories, both wrestling with the same fundamental questions. The emo genre's emotional vulnerability has a spiritual dimension -- the searching, the yearning.
- **Not preachy.** Pedro the Lion's engagement with faith is never preachy or prescriptive. The songs observe, question, struggle. Even the early "Christian" songs are full of doubt. The later "secular" songs are full of yearning. The tension is the point.

(Sources: David Bazan spiritual journey; Pedro the Lion lyrics; faith in emo; Enigk comparison; religious upbringing)

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of Pedro the Lion

- **Tempo.** Slow to mid-tempo. 70-110 BPM. The pace of walking, thinking, narrating. No rush.
- **Guitar.** Clean or lightly overdriven electric guitar. Simple chord progressions. Arpeggiated figures. The guitar supports the voice -- it does not compete with it. Occasional distorted passages provide contrast.
- **Bass.** Simple, supportive, melodic when it needs to be. The bass holds the harmonic foundation without drawing attention.
- **Drums.** Restrained. Often brushed or played with soft sticks. Dynamic but controlled. The drums breathe -- never cluttered.
- **Vocals.** The center of everything. Bazan's voice: warm, slightly husky, conversational. He sings like he's talking to you in a room. The phrasing is natural, not performed. The emotional content comes from the words and their delivery, not from vocal gymnastics.
- **Dynamics.** Narrower range than SDRE. Pedro the Lion moves between quiet and moderately loud. The dynamics serve the narrative -- quieter for intimacy, slightly louder for emphasis. No explosions.
- **Production.** Clean, warm, room-tone. You can hear the space. Minimal effects. The production philosophy: transparency. Let the songs be heard as they are.

(Sources: Pedro the Lion discography; production analysis; Bazan vocal style; indie emo sonic characteristics)
