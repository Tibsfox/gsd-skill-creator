## The One Hundred Ninety-Third Degree: The Foundation Layer -- Since 1979

### Indie Rock/Pop, E=5, Ballard, PopLlama Records. Seattle's longest-running punk/pop band. 1979-2001. Predates Sub Pop by 7 years, grunge by 10. Kurt Bloch, Kim Warnick, Lulu Gargiulo. PopLlama streak continues (192-193). Duff McKagan (early drummer). v1.49.334.

## What Worked

1. **E=5 is right for the Fastbacks.** Energetic punk/pop with melodic precision. Not the raw intensity of E=7+ hip-hop, not the quiet restraint of E=2-3. The power-pop sweet spot.
2. **Foundation layer narrative is compelling.** 1979 is EARLY. The timeline argument is irrefutable: Sub Pop 1986, Nirvana 1989, Pearl Jam 1990. The Fastbacks were there first.
3. **PopLlama streak through Kurt Bloch is elegant.** The same human in both bands (192-193) creates a personnel bridge that elevates the label connection.
4. **Ballard as a new geographic node works.** Working-class punk fits Ballard's identity. The engine's neighborhood map continues to expand.
5. **Duff McKagan connection adds unexpected depth.** Seattle punk to Guns N' Roses is a remarkable trajectory.
6. **Dual female/male harmonies are a distinctive sonic element.** Warnick and Gargiulo's voices are not typical punk vocals.

## What's New

1. **Foundation layer concept.** Pre-scene infrastructure. The band that existed before the scene.
2. **PopLlama streak (192-193).** Two consecutive PopLlama degrees with a personnel bridge.
3. **Ballard enters the engine.** Working-class, Scandinavian, maritime. New geographic node.
4. **Duff McKagan connection.** Seattle punk drumming roots for GNR's drummer.
5. **22-year continuous operation.** Longest span for any Seattle band in the engine.
