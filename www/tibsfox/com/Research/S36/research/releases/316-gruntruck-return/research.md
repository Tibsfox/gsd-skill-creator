## BEN McMILLAN -- THE VOICE OF THE SUMMIT'S CLOSE

### Vocalist, Capitol Hill, January 9, 2008

Ben McMillan was born June 21, 1963, in Seattle, Washington. He died January 9, 2008, in Seattle, at age 44, from a combination of insulin-dependent diabetes complications and substance abuse. He was one of the most powerful vocalists in Seattle's grunge scene:

- **The voice.** McMillan was a baritone-to-tenor singer with raw physical power: less technically controlled than Staley or Cornell, less melodically wide-ranging than Vedder, but with an urgency and intensity of delivery that is immediately recognizable. His singing style is uncompromised -- aggressive when the songs require aggression, with moments of surprising melody that emerge from the surrounding heaviness like light through a break in clouds.
- **The Skinyard connection.** Before Gruntruck, McMillan was the vocalist in Skinyard (degree 297, S36 engine), one of the early Seattle grunge bands. Skinyard formed in 1985 and was active until 1991, overlapping with Gruntruck's formation. The progression from Skinyard to Gruntruck traces the development of the Capitol Hill heavy rock sound through the late 1980s into the grunge era proper.
- **After Gruntruck: Selfishgene.** After Gruntruck dissolved in 1994, McMillan formed Selfishgene with Scott Vanderpool, releasing a single album (*Monster Truck Explosions*) in 1997. The project was less commercially visible than Gruntruck but continued McMillan's creative engagement with heavy rock.
- **The death.** Ben McMillan died January 9, 2008. He was 44. His death from complications of diabetes and substance abuse adds to the list of losses in the Seattle grunge scene: Cobain (1994), Wood (1990), Starr (2011), Cornell (2017), Staley (2002). McMillan is the least publicly known of these losses but equally representative of the personal cost that ran through the scene.

(Sources: Ben McMillan biography; born June 21 1963 Seattle WA; died January 9 2008 Seattle; age 44; insulin-dependent diabetes complications and substance abuse; vocal style analysis; Skinyard connection formed 1985; Selfishgene Monster Truck Explosions 1997; Seattle grunge mortality thread)

## THE BAND -- FORMATION AND MEMBERSHIP

### Capitol Hill, 1990-1994

Gruntruck formed in Seattle in 1990 from the remnants of Skinyard and other Capitol Hill band connections:

- **Ben McMillan** (vocals). See above.
- **Tom Niemeyer** (guitar, born August 3, 1962). Former Skinyard guitarist. His guitar approach is the defining sonic element of the Gruntruck sound: heavy, down-tuned, direct. Not technically flashy but rhythmically authoritative, with a tone that sits in the Melvins-to-AIC-to-Soundgarden continuum of Capitol Hill heavy rock.
- **Tim Paul** (bass). Solid, appropriately registered, supporting the guitar's heaviness without competing with it.
- **Tim Haney** (drums). The rhythm section foundation. Powerful at the tempos Gruntruck operates at.
- **Jack Endino production.** Both Gruntruck albums were produced by Jack Endino, the Sub Pop house producer who made *Bleach* (Nirvana), *Superfuzz Bigmuff* (Mudhoney), and dozens of other defining Seattle grunge records. Endino's production style for Gruntruck is consistent with his broader approach: direct, dry, drums prominent, guitar tone preserved rather than processed into something it isn't. The Endino aesthetic is Capitol Hill heavy rock unadorned.

(Sources: Gruntruck member biographies; Tom Niemeyer born August 3 1962; Tim Paul bass; Tim Haney drums; Jack Endino production both albums; Endino discography: Bleach, Superfuzz Bigmuff; Endino production style analysis)

## INSIDE YOURS AND PUSH -- THE COMPLETE DISCOGRAPHY

### Two Albums, Four Years

Gruntruck made two studio albums. Both are complete statements of the E=8 Capitol Hill heavy rock approach:

***Inside Yours* (1992, Roadrunner Records).** The debut. Produced by Jack Endino. Nine tracks. The sound is established immediately on the opening track: McMillan's aggressive baritone over Niemeyer's down-tuned riffs, the rhythm section locking in with the guitar rather than competing with it. The production is direct: this is a band playing in a room, recorded to tape, without excessive layering or processing. Tracks from *Inside Yours* received significant college radio and MTV Headbangers Ball airplay, which placed Gruntruck in a slightly different commercial context than the Sub Pop bands -- more metal-adjacent, less punk-rooted. Roadrunner was at the time primarily a metal label (Sepultura, Obituary, Slayer releases), and Gruntruck's placement on Roadrunner reflects their sonic positioning between grunge and metal.

***Push* (1993, Roadrunner Records).** The second and final album. Also produced by Jack Endino. More developed than *Inside Yours*: slightly wider dynamic range, songs with more internal contrast, McMillan's vocals finding moments of melodic openness inside the heavy context. "Tribe" is the signature track from *Push*: the riff, the McMillan vocal approach at its most complete, the Endino production framing the song without over-dressing it. *Push* represents Gruntruck's creative peak; the band dissolved the following year.

The complete Gruntruck catalog -- two albums, approximately 80 minutes of music -- is a complete and coherent statement of Capitol Hill grunge/metal at E=8. The brevity of the catalog is typical of the non-crossover Seattle bands: four years, two albums, genuine creative achievement, no commercial breakthrough sufficient to sustain the band.

(Sources: Inside Yours 1992 Roadrunner Records; Jack Endino production; college radio and MTV Headbangers Ball airplay; Roadrunner Records roster: Sepultura, Obituary, Slayer; Push 1993; Tribe as signature track; Gruntruck dissolution 1994; catalog analysis)

## ROADRUNNER RECORDS -- THE METAL CONTEXT

### A Different Label Path in the Seattle Scene

Most significant Seattle grunge bands were on Sub Pop (Mudhoney, Nirvana, Soundgarden pre-A&M, TAD), Columbia (Alice in Chains), Epic (Pearl Jam), or A&M (Soundgarden). Gruntruck on Roadrunner is the outlier:

- **Roadrunner Records (1980, Netherlands).** Founded in 1980 in the Netherlands, Roadrunner was by the early 1990s one of the most important heavy metal labels in the world. Their 1990s roster included Sepultura (*Chaos A.D.*, 1993; *Roots*, 1996), Obituary, Type O Negative, Slayer (distributed), Machine Head, and Fear Factory. Gruntruck was the most explicit Seattle grunge band on the Roadrunner roster.
- **The positioning.** Gruntruck on Roadrunner placed the band in the metal section of record stores rather than the alternative section, which is where Mudhoney, Nirvana, and Pearl Jam lived commercially. This positioning reflects the sonic reality: Gruntruck's music is heavier and less punk-influenced than most Sub Pop grunge. The Roadrunner path was appropriate to the sound.
- **The commercial context.** Roadrunner in 1992-1993 was distributing through Atlantic Records in the US, which gave Gruntruck broader distribution than the independent Sub Pop model but less commercial infrastructure than the major labels (Columbia, Epic) that AIC and Pearl Jam were on. The result was college radio and metal-adjacent airplay without the major-label commercial push that gave *Ten* and *Dirt* their multi-platinum certifications.

(Sources: Roadrunner Records history; founded 1980 Netherlands; early 1990s roster; Sepultura on Roadrunner; Atlantic distribution for Roadrunner US; alternative vs. metal store positioning; Gruntruck commercial context vs. Sub Pop and major label bands)

## THE CAPITOL HILL ECOSYSTEM -- THE SUMMIT'S GEOGRAPHIC CENTER

### Why Most of the Summit Lives Here

Of the eight bands in the grunge summit (degrees 309-316), six have Capitol Hill connections:

- **TAD (309):** Capitol Hill.
- **Mudhoney (310):** Capitol Hill.
- **Nirvana (311):** Aberdeen (origin), but Capitol Hill when in Seattle.
- **Soundgarden (312):** Capitol Hill.
- **Alice in Chains (313):** Beacon Hill / Skyway (slightly south).
- **Pearl Jam (314):** Capitol Hill.
- **Foo Fighters (315):** Capitol Hill (origin, Robert Lang Studios nearby).
- **Gruntruck (316):** Capitol Hill.

Capitol Hill's concentration of the grunge summit reflects the neighborhood's function in the late 1980s and early 1990s: affordable rent, walkable venue density, a music-permissive culture that tolerated loud, heavy, uncommercial music in small clubs. The Central Tavern, the Vogue, Moe's Mo'Roc'N Cafe, the Weathered Wall -- these Capitol Hill venues were the proving grounds for the bands that would become the grunge summit. By the time *Nevermind* broke in September 1991, these clubs had hosted every significant band in the summit for years before commercial recognition arrived.

Gruntruck as the closing entry of the grunge summit is geographically appropriate: the last band in the cluster lived in the neighborhood that hosted most of the cluster. The summit opens in Aberdeen (TAD, connected to that community) and closes on Capitol Hill, where most of the bands lived and played.
