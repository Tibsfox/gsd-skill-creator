## What Worked

1. **FIRST EMO at E=5 is the right calibration.** Intense, dynamic, emotionally raw -- but structured. Not E=6 (grunge wall-of-sound). Not E=4 (restrained confessional). E=5 is the energy of emotional release within form.
2. **Genre Stage 105 marks a genuine new genre.** Emo is distinct from indie rock, grunge, and punk. The engine now has emo as a category.
3. ***Diary* (1994) as the defining album is well-supported.** Sub Pop release, Brad Wood production, critical consensus, fan consensus, historical consensus.
4. **The Foo Fighters connection is a strong structural element.** Mendel's 28-year tenure in the Foo Fighters after leaving SDRE is one of the most significant member connections in PNW music history.
5. **Enigk's spiritual journey adds depth.** Faith and doubt as an emo theme connects forward to Pedro the Lion (198).
6. **E=5/E=7 pair energy of 12 is the highest in recent degrees.** Emotionally intense emo paired with the elk's screaming bugle.

*Degree 197 retrospective complete. SUNNY DAY REAL ESTATE at the one hundred ninety-seventh degree -- FIRST EMO. Genre Stage 105. The voice that breaks. Jeremy Enigk's aching, soaring vocal -- cracking from whisper to scream, transparent in its emotion, the template for a generation of emo singers. *Diary* (1994) on Sub Pop Records, produced by Brad Wood: the album that defined what Pacific Northwest emo could be. "In Circles" -- spiraling guitars and the repeated lyric that goes nowhere and everywhere. "Seven" with its ascending figure. The dynamics: quiet to overwhelming, restraint to release. Nate Mendel on bass, who would leave for the Foo Fighters in 1995 -- the emo-to-arena-rock pipeline, running for nearly three decades through one musician. William Goldsmith's dynamic drumming. Dan Hoerner's interlocking guitar architecture. Enigk's spiritual journey -- faith emerging in the middle of Seattle's secular indie world, surprising and alienating some, adding a dimension of searching that connects forward to Pedro the Lion at degree 198. Capitol Hill, 1992. Formed during the grunge peak but doing something different. E=5: emotional release within form. Across the degree pair, the Roosevelt Elk bugle at E=7 -- a high screaming whistle that breaks into grunting, the bull's declaration during the fall rut. Both voices that cannot be contained.
