The Voice That Breaks

Sunny Day Real Estate. *Diary* (1994). The album that defined what Pacific Northwest emo could be. Jeremy Enigk’s aching, soaring voice — cracking and surging, whispering and screaming. Nate Mendel on bass (later Foo Fighters). Dan Hoerner and William Goldsmith. Capitol Hill, 1992. Sub Pop. The FIRST EMO entry. Genre Stage 105. The emotional intensity of a voice that cannot be contained. E=5.
