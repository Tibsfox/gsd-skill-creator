We Are the Romans — 1999 — Hydra Head Records

Eight tracks. 37 minutes. Canonical. Every serious list of essential heavy music includes it. The technical ambition of mathcore combined with emotional devastation not commonly attempted in extreme music. The riffs change time signature within a single phrase. The bass is a contrapuntal voice, not support. The drums maintain multiple simultaneous meters. The vocals are instruments. Together they produce something that cannot be categorized by any single genre label.

The Band

Botch formed in Tacoma in 1993. Dave Verellen on vocals: not a singer in the melodic sense but a physical presence, his voice an instrument operating at the same technical demand as the guitars. Brian Cook on bass: contrapuntal, operating at a different rhythmic layer from the guitars, creating polyrhythmic complexity in an already polyrhythmic environment. Cook's post-Botch trajectory — These Arms Are Snakes, then Russian Circles — traces how the Botch approach diffused into post-metal and post-rock internationally. Dave Knudson on guitar: angular riffs that interrogate and disrupt meter rather than establishing and maintaining it. Knudson later founded Minus the Bear. Tim Latona on guitar: interlocking with Knudson to create the time-signature displacement that defines the band. Tim Meskers on drums: the most technically demanding role, maintaining multiple simultaneous meters at high volume.

"The dissolution in 2002 is an act of artistic integrity. They made the records they could make and stopped. The catalog is complete, finished, and fully realized. This is rare in rock music."

Mathcore -- The Genre Context

Mathcore is extreme music with unusual time signatures (5/4, 7/8, 11/8), rapid meter changes, polyrhythm, and rhythmic displacement — combined with hardcore volume and aggression. The "math" refers to the apparent precision required: the musicians must simultaneously track multiple rhythmic layers while maintaining physical attack. The lineage runs through Slint's Spiderland (Louisville, 1991), Fugazi's rhythmic intelligence (Washington DC, 1987), and Refused's The Shape of Punk to Come (Sweden, 1998). Botch's closest contemporaries: Dillinger Escape Plan (New Jersey, 1997) and Converge (Salem MA, 1990). The Converge parallel is exact: Jane Doe (2001) is the East Coast equivalent of We Are the Romans — both technically demanding, emotionally devastating, and canonical.

Hydra Head Records

Hydra Head Records was founded by Aaron Turner in Boston in 1993 — the same year Botch formed. Turner founded the label as a vehicle for his own band Isis (post-metal, 1997-2010) and expanded it into the defining label for intelligent heavy music in the late 1990s and 2000s. The roster: Botch, Isis, Cave In, Pelican, Oxbow, Harvey Milk, Khanate, Converge (briefly), Deadguy. Non-commercial by philosophy. Closed in 2012. The 20-year catalog represents post-hardcore, sludge, post-metal, and noise rock at their creative peak. *American Nervoso* (1998) and *We Are the Romans* (1999) are the two major Botch releases on Hydra Head.

Tacoma -- Industrial Port City

Tacoma is 30 miles south of Seattle on Puget Sound: a port city, more industrial and working-class than Capitol Hill Seattle. The Tacoma waterfront is a working port. The Tacoma Narrows Bridge spans some of the strongest tidal currents in the Sound. The South Sound, the industrial waterfront, the specific combination of working-class Tacoma and the University of Puget Sound's liberal arts environment above the city — this geography shapes Botch. The technical ambition (UPS intellectual culture) and the physical aggression (Tacoma's industrial ground level) are both in the music.
