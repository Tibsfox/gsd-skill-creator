## First Vocal Looping Artist: Technology as Instrument

### The Loop Pedal as Defining Instrument

Degree 72 introduces the first vocal looping artist.

- **Situation:** Briana Marela is the first vocal looping artist in the engine. The loop pedal is her primary instrument -- technology that transforms the voice from a linear medium into an architectural medium. One voice becomes a choir of itself.
- **Root Cause:** Through seventy-one degrees, the engine has tracked acoustic and electronic instruments as the tools of music-making. Vocal looping introduces a new category: technology as process rather than sound source. The loop pedal does not produce sound -- it captures, stores, and replays sound. It transforms the voice into something it could not be without the technology.
- **Recommendation:** Document vocal looping as the first technology-as-instrument entry. Note the distinction from Chong the Nomad (44, studio-based electronic production). Track whether future degrees include other technology-as-instrument artists.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- First vocal looping artist. Technology as instrument category established. Loop pedal as defining instrument. Track at future degrees.

## The International Student: Training Abroad

### Iceland Academy of the Arts

Degree 72 introduces the international student pattern.

- **Situation:** Briana Marela studied at the Iceland Academy of the Arts in Reykjavik. Nordic ambient tradition (Bjork, Sigur Ros) shaped her vocal looping aesthetic. Seattle is home. Iceland was school. The music synthesizes Pacific Northwest indie and Nordic ambient aesthetics.
- **Root Cause:** The international student pattern reflects the reality of contemporary arts education: artists travel to study in other countries and return carrying those influences. Marela's Iceland training is audible in the music's ethereal quality, ambient patience, and treatment of voice as sonic object. The international student brings global influences into the local ecosystem.
- **Recommendation:** Document the international student as a new artist-city pattern. Note the distinction from corridor wanderer (local), Export (leaves permanently), and visiting artist (institutional). Track whether future degrees include other internationally trained artists.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- International student pattern established. 73rd artist-city pattern. Iceland connection documented. Track at future degrees.
