## HAUNTED HORSES -- NOISE/INDIE -- SEATTLE

### The Duo

Haunted Horses is a Seattle noise duo consisting of:

- **Colin Dawson** -- guitar, vocals, electronics. Dawson is the primary sonic architect. His guitar work ranges from heavily distorted drone to angular, atonal riffing to layered feedback textures. The electronics include noise generators, effects pedals, and processing that pushes the guitar signal from recognizable instrument to pure texture. Dawson's vocals are raw, shouted, sometimes buried in the mix -- more texture than melody, more expression than lyrics in the traditional sense.

- **Jessica Jaworski** -- drums, drum machines, electronics. Jaworski provides the rhythmic foundation, blending acoustic drums with drum machines and electronic percussion. The drum machine element is central to Haunted Horses' sound -- it provides the industrial, mechanical pulse that drives the songs. Jaworski's approach is not to choose between acoustic drums and drum machines but to **layer them**: the organic unpredictability of live drumming combined with the relentless, inhuman regularity of the machine. The combination creates a tension that is essential to the band's character.

### The Sound

Haunted Horses' sound is **industrial noise processed through indie rock instrumentation**. The genre classification **Noise/Indie** captures the hybrid:

- **Noise** provides: extreme distortion, feedback, harsh textures, volume, industrial rhythms, confrontational sonic assault. The noise element is not incidental; it is the foundation. Haunted Horses starts with noise -- heavy, distorted, mechanical noise -- and builds songs within it.

- **Indie** provides: song structures (verse-chorus or through-composed, but recognizable structures), vocal presence (Dawson's vocals add a human element to the mechanical textures), guitar-and-drums instrumentation (the basic rock duo format), and a connection to the broader Seattle independent music ecosystem. The "indie" qualifier distinguishes Haunted Horses from pure noise acts (who may work entirely in texture and abstraction) -- there are songs here, with beginnings and endings and identifiable parts, even if those songs are buried in distortion.

The sound draws from a lineage of industrial and noise acts:

- **Big Black / Shellac (Steve Albini):** Drum machines + angular guitar + shouted vocals. Big Black pioneered the format in the 1980s: a drum machine providing the industrial pulse, a guitar providing angular, distorted riffs, a vocalist shouting over the top. Haunted Horses inherits this template directly.
- **Swans (early):** Crushing repetition, extreme volume, industrial textures. Early Swans (1982-1986) pursued heaviness through repetition and volume rather than traditional metal riffing. Haunted Horses shares this approach: the heaviness comes from sustained distortion and relentless rhythm, not from complex riffs.
- **Lightning Bolt:** Noise duo format (bass + drums). Lightning Bolt demonstrated that two musicians can generate the sonic force of a full band through extreme amplification and rhythmic intensity. Haunted Horses extends this principle with guitar instead of bass and the addition of drum machines.
- **No Anchor (degree 109):** Direct Seattle noise duo parallel. Both are two-person noise acts operating at E=6 maximum in Seattle. No Anchor and Haunted Horses are part of the same resurgent noise/industrial underground.

### The Industrial Underground Resurgence

Haunted Horses exists within a broader **resurgence of industrial and noise music** in Seattle during the 2010s and 2020s. After the grunge era and the subsequent indie rock dominance of the 2000s, Seattle's underground shifted partly toward noise, industrial, and experimental music. This resurgence includes:

- **Haunted Horses** -- noise/industrial duo
- **No Anchor** (degree 109) -- noise duo
- **Wolf Eyes** (Detroit-origin, PNW-connected) -- noise, industrial
- **Various DIY venues and spaces** that host noise shows, often in non-traditional venues (warehouses, basements, art spaces)
- **KEXP support** for experimental and noise acts through their programming
- **Bandcamp** as the primary distribution platform for the underground

The resurgence is not a mass movement -- noise and industrial music remain niche genres with small but dedicated audiences. But the infrastructure exists: the venues, the platforms, the community. Seattle's noise underground in the 2020s is a direct descendant of the city's long tradition of extreme music, from the Melvins and Tad in the late 1980s through noise rock (Kinski, degree 110) and post-metal (Helms Alee, degree 111) to the current industrial/noise scene.

## Acoustic Profile: The Machine Signal

### The Sound

The **machine signal** is the acoustic signature of Haunted Horses: a heavily distorted, drum-machine-driven, industrially textured sonic assault in which human performance and machine rhythm merge into a single, crushing sound. The "machine signal" describes the fundamental quality: this music sounds **mechanical** -- relentless, rhythmically rigid, tonally harsh -- even though it is created by two human musicians. The drum machines are the machine; the guitar distortion is the signal; the combination is the machine signal.

### Key Sonic Characteristics

- **Guitar tone:** Extreme distortion -- the guitar signal is processed through multiple distortion pedals, creating a dense, saturated wall of overtones. The original guitar tone is barely recognizable beneath the processing. The distortion is not the warm overdrive of blues or the focused distortion of metal; it is **harsh, buzzing, industrial distortion** that treats the guitar as a noise generator rather than a melodic instrument.
- **Drum machines:** The relentless, mechanical pulse that drives the songs. The drum machines provide industrial-grade rhythms: simple, repetitive, high-impact patterns that hit with mechanical precision. The machines do not swing; they do not breathe; they do not vary. The inhuman regularity is the point.
- **Live drums (layered):** Jaworski layers acoustic drumming over and around the drum machine patterns. The live drums add organic variation -- fills, dynamic changes, human timing fluctuations -- to the mechanical foundation. The tension between human and machine is essential: neither fully organic nor fully electronic, but a **hybrid** that is more unsettling than either alone.
- **Vocals:** Dawson's vocals are raw, shouted, often buried in the mix. The vocals function as another textural layer rather than as the primary melodic element. Words are present but not always intelligible through the distortion. The vocals express urgency and intensity without requiring lyrical clarity.
- **Volume:** Loud. Haunted Horses is a live-volume band -- the recorded music is loud, but the live experience is designed for extreme volume. The physical impact of the sound is part of the expression.

### Energy: E=6

E=6 -- maximum energy. The combination of extreme guitar distortion, drum machine assault, shouted vocals, and industrial textures creates the maximum sonic force achievable by two musicians. E=6 at Haunted Horses is different from E=6 at Kinski (instrumental ascent) or Helms Alee (beauty/destruction) -- Haunted Horses' E=6 is **mechanical, industrial, confrontational**. The machine signal is not beautiful; it is powerful. The energy is not ascending; it is relentless. The E=6 comes from sustained maximum intensity rather than dynamic variation.

This is the fifth distinct expression of E=6 in eight degrees: punk/electronic combustion (108), industrial noise duo (109), instrumental noise rock (110), beauty/destruction (111), and now **industrial machine signal** (114). The engine has demonstrated that E=6 is not a single sound -- it is a level that can be expressed through radically different sonic approaches.
