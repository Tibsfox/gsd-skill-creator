The Machine Signal — Human Performance Meets Industrial Rhythm

Drum machines provide the relentless, mechanical pulse. Extreme guitar distortion treats the instrument as a noise generator. Shouted vocals add urgency without lyrical clarity. The combination sounds mechanical — relentless, rhythmically rigid, tonally harsh — even though two human musicians create it. Lineage: Big Black (drum machine + angular guitar), early Swans (crushing repetition), Lightning Bolt (duo force). The machine signal is not beautiful; it is powerful.

E=6 Returns — Fifth Maximum in Eight Degrees

Five E=6 entries in eight degrees (108–114). Each expresses maximum differently: punk/electronic combustion (108), industrial noise duo (109), instrumental ascent (110), beauty/destruction (111), machine signal (114). E=6 is not a single sound — it is a level with radically different expressions. The engine’s baseline has shifted upward.

Self-Released — Pure DIY Infrastructure

First fully self-released artist in the Capitol Hill streak. No label intermediary. Bandcamp as primary distribution. The most extreme expression of DIY independence in the engine’s label ecosystem. Noise music’s economics don’t support traditional label overhead — the audience is small, dedicated, and accustomed to buying directly from artists. Self-release is not a limitation; it is the natural infrastructure for noise music.

The Duo

Colin Dawson (guitar, vocals, electronics) — sonic architect. Heavily distorted guitar from drone to angular atonal riffing. Shouted vocals buried in the mix. Electronics push the guitar signal from instrument to pure texture.
Jessica Jaworski (drums, drum machines, electronics) — rhythmic foundation. Layers acoustic drums over drum machine patterns. The organic unpredictability of live drumming combined with the relentless regularity of the machine. The tension between human and machine is essential.

The Noise/Industrial Resurgence

Haunted Horses exists within a broader resurgence of industrial and noise music in Seattle during the 2010s–2020s. After grunge and 2000s indie rock dominance, the underground shifted toward noise, industrial, and experimental music. Alongside No Anchor (degree 109), Haunted Horses represents the current generation of Seattle noise. DIY venues, Bandcamp distribution, KEXP support. Descended from the Melvins, Tad, Kinski, Helms Alee — Seattle’s long tradition of extreme music continues.
