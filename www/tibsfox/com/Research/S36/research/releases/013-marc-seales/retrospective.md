## The Standifer-to-Seales Arc: The Most Significant Pedagogical Finding

Degree 13's most important contribution is completing the **Standifer-to-Seales pedagogical arc** -- the full formalization of jazz education at the University of Washington, from experiential teaching (Standifer, degree 4) to doctoral-level academic program direction (Seales, degree 13).

### The Arc

```
EXPERIENTIAL (Standifer, 4) -- UW faculty, teaching by playing
  |
  | (9 degrees between, multiple pedagogical models documented)
  |
ACADEMIC (Seales, 13) -- UW faculty, teaching by scholarship + playing
```

This arc maps a historical transition in jazz education:

1. **Standifer's model (degree 4):** Learn by doing. The teacher plays, the student watches and imitates. The UW appointment provides institutional backing, but the teaching method is pre-institutional -- it is the bandstand apprenticeship model transported into a university setting. When Standifer stops teaching, the specific knowledge he transmitted through presence is at risk.

2. **Seales's model (degree 13):** Learn by understanding. The teacher articulates theory, designs curriculum, directs ensembles, and supervises research. The DMA provides scholarly depth. The UW program has structure that can survive faculty turnover. When Seales retires, the program continues -- different teachers, same institutional framework.

### The Institution-Dependency Spectrum

The arc introduces a new analytical axis: the **institution-dependency spectrum**.

| Position | Artist | Dependency |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Fully person-dependent | Brazil (11) | Salon dies when Brazil dies |
| Mostly person-dependent | Berry (5) | Performance IS teaching; no institution |
| Partially institutional | Standifer (4) | UW appointment, but experiential method |
| Institutional with performance credential | Priester (7), Caliman (9) | Cornish appointments, working-musician authority |
| Fully institutional | **Seales (13)** | UW program with curriculum, degrees, administrative structure |

This spectrum is the degree's most significant structural contribution. It provides a framework for classifying all future educators in the engine.

## Piano Branch Becomes Richest: Structural Significance

The piano theory branch extends to **eight nodes** at degree 13, surpassing the saxophone/reed branch (seven nodes at degree 12) as the richest in the engine. The three pianists -- Berry (stride), Bown (hard bop), Seales (modal/contemporary) -- map three distinct eras and three distinct educational paths:

| Dimension | Berry (5) | Bown (6) | Seales (13) |
|-----------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Era | 1940s-2020s | 1950s-1970s | 1990s-present |
| Style | Stride, cocktail | Hard bop, orchestral | Modal, post-bop |
| Training | Self-taught | Undergraduate | Doctoral |
| Harmonic vocabulary | Triads, 7ths, stride patterns | Altered voicings, orchestral density | Quartal voicings, extended chords, modal scales |

The piano trio is structurally richer than the saxophone pair (Caliman/Shank at degrees 9/10) because three data points define a trajectory: the evolution from oldest to newest jazz piano vocabulary, AND the parallel evolution from no formal education to the terminal degree. No other instrument family in the engine has this dual-trajectory documentation.

## The Institutional Anchor: Fourteenth Artist-City Pattern

Degree 13 adds the fourteenth named pattern: **The Institutional Anchor** -- native-born, terminal degree, permanent institutional appointment, active performer.

The Institutional Anchor fills a specific gap in the taxonomy. Previous patterns covered:

- Musicians who teach informally (Brazil's salon, degree 11)
- Musicians who teach at institutions they arrived at from outside (Priester and Caliman at Cornish, degrees 7 and 9)
- Musicians who teach through experience at their local institution (Standifer at UW, degree 4)

None covered the native-born musician who earns a terminal degree locally and anchors the local institution permanently. Seales fills this gap.

The pattern requires three conditions that no previous artist meets simultaneously:
1. Native-born (or long-resident from formative years)
2. Terminal degree (DMA, PhD, or equivalent)
3. Permanent institutional appointment at a degree-granting institution

This is the most institutionally embedded position in the taxonomy. It represents the endpoint of the professionalization of jazz -- from informal art form taught on the bandstand to formal academic discipline taught at the doctoral level.
