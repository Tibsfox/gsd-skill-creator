## What Worked

1. **The Brian Cook genealogical chain is the key structural insight.** The engine documents bands and the people who carry approaches from one band to the next. Cook is a direct biological link from Botch (289) to TAAS (290). Degrees 289-290 are not just adjacent entries -- they are parent and child.
2. **Suicide Squeeze's first engine appearance is correctly placed.** The label is a Capitol Hill institution. Its first appearance through TAAS, at degree 290, is historically accurate. The label's eclectic catalog and Capitol Hill roots make it a natural fit for the 2000s Seattle post-hardcore context.
3. **E=8 as expansion rather than reduction is the correct framing.** After four degrees at E=9, E=8 is a step down in ceiling but a step up in available territory. TAAS uses the space: atmospheric passages, dynamic variance, textural exploration. This is only possible when not operating at the absolute intensity maximum.
4. **2000s Capitol Hill as a legitimate engine chapter.** The engine has spent significant time in the grunge era (late 1980s-mid 1990s). The 2000s post-hardcore scene is a continuation, not an appendix. TAAS documents this chapter directly.

## Discoveries

1. **Chris Common's subsequent production career is an untracked cross-reference.** After TAAS, Common became a significant producer and engineer in post-hardcore and experimental rock. His work with Pelican, Chelsea Wolfe, and others represents a production lineage that starts in TAAS's drum seat.
2. **Brian Cook's move to Russian Circles extends the genealogy further.** Post-TAAS, Cook joined Chicago's Russian Circles (a band with deep Pacific Northwest connections). The Cook lineage: Botch → TAAS → Russian Circles. Three bands across three cities (Tacoma, Seattle/Capitol Hill, Chicago), each distinct, all connected through one bassist.

## Pair Analysis: These Arms Are Snakes + Herring Gull Flock Alarm

- **Energy:** E=8 (TAAS) + E=7 (Herring Gull) = **E=15.**
- **Theme:** ANGULAR VOLATILITY MEETS RAUCOUS ALARM. The Herring Gull flock alarm (SPS 290) is a winter beach scene -- a flock suddenly alarmed, dozens of birds producing overlapping raucous screams simultaneously, no coordination, pure reactive cacophony. TAAS's post-hardcore is angular and volatile but controlled -- every shift is intentional, every dynamic change is musical decision. The pairing: controlled volatility meets uncontrolled alarm. Both are loud, both shift abruptly, both layer multiple simultaneous sound events. Different generating mechanisms, similar acoustic results.
