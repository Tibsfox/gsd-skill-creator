## What Worked

1. **The E-gap record is structurally productive.** The contrast of Heart Hard Rock (E=7) with Turkey Vulture (E=2) forces the engine to examine what "power" means across categories. Maximum vocal power + minimum vocalization = the engine's most extreme pairing.
2. **Barracuda's backstory anchors the E=7 placement.** "Authentic anger makes E=7 music" is a principle the engine can carry forward. The song was written in genuine fury at real exploitation. The E=7 energy is emotional truth, not performance.
3. **The Mushroom Records legal dispute as engine context.** The label dispute that produced both the unauthorized Magazine release and the authorized Portrait version documents the commercial exploitation Heart faced and navigated.

## Discoveries

1. **Roger Fisher's departure in 1980 is a meaningful marker.** The hard rock era effectively ends when Fisher leaves -- the dual-guitar density that defines the E=7 material requires both Wilson and Fisher. The subsequent lineup changes coincide with the shift toward E=6 pop-rock.
2. **Dreamboat Annie's transatlantic release timeline is important.** The 1975 Canadian / 1976 US release creates a publishing complication in the discography. The album reached #7 US despite the delayed rollout.

## Pair Analysis: Heart (Hard Rock) + Turkey Vulture

- **Energy:** E=7 + E=2 = E=9. E-gap of 5 -- ENGINE RECORD. No prior degree has an S36/SPS gap greater than 4.
- **Theme:** TWO KINDS OF POWER. Ann Wilson's hard rock vocal is maximum-deployment power -- four octaves pushed to their limit, emotion as pure acoustic force. The Turkey Vulture circles in silence, finding death by smell, transforming the dead into nutrients. Both are more powerful than their cultural reception suggests: Heart was commercially underestimated (women don't do this); the Turkey Vulture is culturally undervalued (vultures are associated with ugliness and death). Both prove the cultural reception wrong through their actual performance.
- **The FIRST CATHARTIDAE connection.** The Turkey Vulture at SPS 235 introduces a new family with an old ecological role: the ecosystem's sanitation department, the purifier. The pairing of the "purifier" with Heart's hard rock -- genuinely cleansing in its emotional directness -- is not accidental.
