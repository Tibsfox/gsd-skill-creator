Matt Cameron — The Origin Document

Matt Cameron (born November 28, 1962, San Diego) was Skin Yard's drummer before joining Soundgarden in 1986. His drumming in Skin Yard is already technically proficient with strong dynamic sense — not just a timekeeper but a compositional contributor. The drummer who would execute the complex rhythmic patterns of Badmotorfinger and the dynamic nuance of Superunknown developed his approach here, in a band most Soundgarden fans have never heard.

After Soundgarden's original dissolution in 1997, Cameron joined Pearl Jam in 1998 and has been their drummer ever since — playing on every Pearl Jam album from Binaural (2000) to the present. He has been with Pearl Jam for more than twice as long as he was with Soundgarden. Skin Yard is where it began.

The Sound — Proto-Metal at E=7

Skin Yard's music is heavy and dark — riff-based, drawn from the metal tradition (Black Sabbath as the obvious reference), but faster and more direct. Metal's heaviness with punk's velocity. No extended solos; no excess. Get in, deliver the riff, get out. This is the proto-grunge sound: the music existed before the commercial label. When Nirvana broke in 1991, the ecosystem they were named after had been active since 1985. Skin Yard is 1985.
