The Producer Web — Three Degrees, One Figure

99°: Blue Scholars (Sabzi + Geo) → 101°: Common Market (Sabzi + RA Scion) → 122°: Sabzi (solo). Three nodes, one connecting figure. The producer is the constant; emcees are the variables. The production DNA is recognizable across all three entries. 23 degrees separate the first and third nodes.

The Production Style

Jazz sampling as foundation. Sabzi is a crate digger — productions built from jazz, soul, funk, and international records. Not simple loop-and-layer: he deconstructs and reconstructs, isolating phrases, chopping into new patterns, rebuilding into compositions that honor the source while creating something new.

Layered beat construction. Multiple sample sources stacked and blended. Primary loops with secondary harmonic support. Dense but clear. Boom-bap drums: punchy kick, snapping snare, shuffling hi-hat. Melodic production: beats carry harmonic progressions and hooks even without vocals. Electronic expansion: solo work adds synthesizers and digital processing beyond pure sampling.

The overall character is warm. Analog-leaning. The production sounds like records, like rooms, like instruments played by people — even when constructed from samples and electronic elements. The warmth is the signature.

Iranian-American Identity

One of the most prominent Iranian-American musicians in hip-hop. Cross-cultural synthesis: Middle Eastern melodic sensibility within American hip-hop and jazz sampling. Minor modes, ornamental figures, a certain melancholy warmth. The immigrant narrative — social justice, community, navigating between cultures — informed by his perspective as a child of Iranian immigrants.

Seattle Hip-Hop Production

Part of a crate-digging lineage alongside Vitamin D, Jake One, Bean One. The UW pipeline: Sabzi and Geo met at the University of Washington. DIY infrastructure: Mass Line Media and artist-owned operations. KEXP as accelerant — in-studio sessions gave exposure beyond the live circuit. Seattle hip-hop is overwhelmingly independent.
