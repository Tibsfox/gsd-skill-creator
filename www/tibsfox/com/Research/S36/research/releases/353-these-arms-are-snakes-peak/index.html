Easter (2006) — The Peak Record

Easter was produced by Toshi Kasai (who also produced the Melvins — the production genealogy runs through the engine). Eleven tracks. The album’s primary sonic signature is intrasong dynamic variation: not just quiet sections between loud sections, but tempo, texture, and intensity changing within individual songs. Songs begin in one mode and arrive in another without the transition being predictable.

This is the post-hardcore compositional challenge: maintain the urgency of hardcore punk while introducing the structural complexity of post-rock and noise rock. Easter succeeds. It is more dynamically active within songs than We Are the Romans (which uses dynamics as between-section architecture). The temporal development is the experience.

The Band

Steve Snere
Vocals

Register-switching: spoken, sung, shouted within the same song, sometimes the same phrase. Not stylistic affectation — a compositional tool. The voice that is not sure whether it is singing or shouting is expressing ambivalence. The delivery is semantic.

Ryan Frederiksen
Guitar

Tonal range: clean passages, texture-based playing, feedback sections, and aggressive distorted passages. Not always distorted. The full palette, deployed within single songs.

Brian Cook
Bass — ALSO IN BOTCH (352)

Same musician, consecutive degrees, different approach. In Botch: compositionally independent, contrapuntal. In TAAS: rhythmically supportive, closer to drum alignment. The comparison documents a bassist’s range across band contexts.

Chris Common
Drums

Momentum not impact. Forward motion rather than percussion events. The drums hold the rhythmic foundation without dominating the mix, providing the platform for the guitar and vocal departures.

The Brian Cook Connection

Brian Cook appears at degree 352 (Botch, We Are the Romans) and degree 353 (These Arms Are Snakes, Easter). The same musician in consecutive engine degrees, in different bands, in different sonic contexts. His Botch bass is a compositionally independent contrapuntal voice that defined a genre precedent. His TAAS bass is a rhythmically supportive foundation that enables the other instruments’ complexity. Both approaches are correct for their contexts. The consecutive placement documents the lineage.
