## What Worked

1. **The historical context is the essential frame.** Temple of the Dog cannot be understood without Andrew Wood -- the Mother Love Bone vocalist who died March 19, 1990, before his debut album shipped. The tribute is the reason the record exists. Without that frame, it is a well-made rock album. With it, it is a monument.
2. **The accidental supergroup analysis is the engine's most historically specific entry to date.** The album was recorded before Pearl Jam existed publicly. Every future Pearl Jam member (Gossard, Ament, McCready, Vedder) is documented here at a pre-fame moment. This is an extraordinary archival fact.
3. **The Chris Cornell-Andrew Wood roommate relationship gives the grief a specificity that makes the music credible.** "Say Hello 2 Heaven" is not a professional tribute. It is a personal address. The engine correctly identifies this distinction.
4. **The accipiter arc opens with the largest species.** Pairing Temple of the Dog (apex supergroup, E=7) with the Northern Goshawk (apex accipiter, E=6) is structurally precise. Both at top of their domains.

## Discoveries

1. **Eddie Vedder's session at Temple of the Dog was his first significant Seattle recording.** He was auditioning for the band that would become Pearl Jam. His vocal on "Hunger Strike" was not planned -- it was simply what happened in the studio with a talented singer present. The most famous duet of the early 1990s was an accident of availability.
2. **The album's initial sales were modest.** It became platinum only after Nirvana's *Nevermind* broke commercially and the world turned to Seattle. Temple of the Dog is thus a pre-wave document heard primarily by a post-wave audience. The historical inversion is significant.
3. **After Cornell's death in 2017, the album acquired a second grief layer.** Made to mourn Wood (1990), it now serves also as a document of Cornell's voice before Cornell's own loss (2017). The monument to one death became, in time, a monument for two.
