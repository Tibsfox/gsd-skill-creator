The Morning Show (2001–Present)

John Richards has hosted The Morning Show on KEXP 90.3 FM since 2001, making him one of the longest-running morning hosts in Seattle radio. He joined KEXP (then transitioning from KCMU) in 1997 and took over the morning slot four years later. The show airs weekday mornings (6–10 AM) and has become the station’s signature program — music-forward with deeply personal commentary, an intuitive curatorial approach, and a voice that tens of thousands of Seattle-area listeners consider part of their daily ritual.

Richards’ style is conversational, not performative. He speaks to the listener as an individual. He plays music that “feels right” — reading the emotional temperature of the morning and curating accordingly. Indie, electronic, hip-hop, folk, international — the coherence comes from mood and quality, not genre. The Morning Show is what an algorithm cannot be: personal, vulnerable, contextual, and surprising.

Music Heals — Mental Health as Broadcast Mission

Music Heals was ahead of its time in normalizing mental health conversation in public media. Before mental health awareness became a mainstream priority, Richards was using his morning show to create space for these conversations. The radio becomes a support group.

Music Heals is Richards’ signature initiative — an annual on-air event (typically October, coinciding with Mental Health Awareness Month) that explicitly connects music listening with mental health and emotional well-being. Listeners share stories of how music helped them through grief, depression, anxiety, addiction, and loss. The stories are read on air, creating a public space for vulnerability. The campaign has grown from a single-day event to a year-round ethos that permeates the show. Music Heals has become one of KEXP’s defining programs — proving that a music radio station can serve a community function beyond entertainment.

KEXP’s Public Face

Richards’ social media presence has made him KEXP’s most visible ambassador. His online voice matches his on-air voice: personal, enthusiastic, occasionally vulnerable. At festivals, concerts, and public events, he is the person most likely recognized as “the KEXP guy.” His personal brand and the station’s brand have become intertwined to a degree no other KEXP personality has achieved.

In the streaming era, Richards represents the human alternative to the algorithm. A curator with taste, context, emotional intelligence, and the ability to read a city’s mood. The parasocial relationship — the feeling of knowing the host — is the Morning Show’s core value proposition. Algorithms don’t have bad days, share personal stories, or dedicate songs to grieving listeners. Richards does.
