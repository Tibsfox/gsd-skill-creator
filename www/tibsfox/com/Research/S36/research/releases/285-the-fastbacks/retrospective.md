## What Worked

1. **Twenty-two years is the engine's longest sustained run.** The Fastbacks span the entire history of Seattle alternative music from its pre-scene beginnings to its post-grunge tail. No other band in the engine matches this temporal arc.
2. **Ballard's working-class character provides correct context.** The maritime, Scandinavian-heritage neighborhood is the right social substrate for the band's unaffected, direct approach to punk pop.
3. **PopLlama vs. Sub Pop contrast is analytically productive.** Conrad Uno's label documents the non-grunge corner of Seattle indie. Both labels are in the engine now; the full picture is richer.
4. **Kurt Bloch's dual role (Fastbacks + production) extends the band's reach into the broader engine ecosystem.** His production work for Mudhoney connects the Fastbacks genealogically to grunge's canonical bands.
5. **The drum turnover (~30 over 22 years) is a genuine structural fact**, not just a curiosity. The trio model was the organizational innovation that enabled the band's longevity.

## Discoveries

1. **E=7 is correct but unusual for punk.** Most punk bands cluster at E=8-9 in the engine. The Fastbacks' melodic content brings them to E=7 rather than higher -- the hooks are real, the tempo is fast, but the music is not about maximum aggression.
2. **PopLlama is a significant gap-fill.** Sub Pop has been in the engine since early degrees. PopLlama's absence until degree 285 is notable -- the melodic indie alternative to grunge had not been documented. The Fastbacks fix this.
