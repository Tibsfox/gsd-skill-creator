## RHYMESAYERS ENTERTAINMENT -- MIDWEST INDEPENDENT HIP-HOP

### The Minneapolis Connection

Rhymesayers Entertainment connects Seattle hip-hop to the Midwest independent tradition:

- **Founded 1995:** Rhymesayers was founded in Minneapolis by Slug (Sean Daley) of Atmosphere, Ant (Anthony Davis), Musab (Beyond), and Siddiq (Brokn Inglish). The label grew from the Minneapolis hip-hop scene's Fifth Element record store and Headshots open mic nights.
- **Label philosophy:** Rhymesayers prizes lyrical substance, emotional honesty, and artistic independence. The label's roster is characterized by MCs who write personal, introspective, narrative-driven hip-hop. Production is varied but always serves the lyrics.
- **Key artists:** Atmosphere (Slug and Ant) -- the label's flagship act, known for deeply personal storytelling. Brother Ali -- social commentary and vulnerability. Eyedea & Abilities -- abstract, philosophical rap. Aesop Rock -- dense, literary wordplay. Prof -- charismatic party rap as counterbalance.
- **Seattle connection:** Grieves on Rhymesayers represents the Seattle-Minneapolis axis in independent hip-hop. Both cities have strong indie hip-hop scenes that prize substance over spectacle. The Rhymesayers connection validates Seattle's emotional hip-hop tradition within the broader national independent scene.
- **Regional independence:** Rhymesayers, like Sub Pop (degree 163 upcoming with Porter Ray), is a regional independent label that achieved national significance. The Midwest and PNW independent circuits share values: authenticity, community, artist-first economics.

## THE EMOTIONAL MC

### Grieves's Artistic Identity

Benjamin Laub as Grieves occupies a specific position in hip-hop:

- **Vulnerability as strength:** Grieves's lyrics address mental health, relationships, self-medication, recovery, and personal growth with an honesty that is sometimes uncomfortable. The music does not flinch from difficulty. Depression is not romanticized but confronted.
- **Singing + rapping:** The ability to sing adds a dimension that most MCs lack. Grieves uses melody to carry emotional weight -- the singing sections often express what straight rapping cannot. The vocal switching creates dynamic range within tracks.
- **Production partnerships:** Grieves has worked with producers including Budo (early work) and later self-produced material. The production tends toward melodic, atmospheric beats that support emotional delivery -- piano loops, ambient textures, warm bass.
- **Live performance:** Capitol Hill venues. Smaller rooms. The emotional intensity translates directly -- Grieves's live shows are intimate and raw. The audience is close. The vulnerability is palpable.
- **Albums:** *Irreversible* (2010, Rhymesayers), *Together/Apart* (2011), *Winters & the Wolves* (2014), *Running Wild* (2017), *If I Could Only Be Well* (2019). The discography maps a personal journey -- growth, struggle, recovery, acceptance.

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of Grieves

Grieves's sonic signature balances rap and melody:

- **Vocal delivery:** Rapid, precise rapping alternating with melodic singing. The transition between modes is fluid -- sometimes within a single bar. The voice carries emotional weight in both modes.
- **Production style:** Melodic, atmospheric. Piano loops, ambient pads, warm bass, crisp drums. The production creates space for emotional delivery. More polished than boom-bap, less anthemic than Ryan Lewis. A middle ground: personal and intimate.
- **BPM range:** 80-120 BPM. Varied. Slower tracks for introspective moments, faster tracks for energetic emotional release. The tempo serves the emotional intent.
- **Lyrical density:** High. Grieves packs syllables but maintains clarity. The lyrics are meant to be heard and understood, not obscured by density. Every word carries weight.
- **Mix approach:** Clean but warm. The mix supports the vocal -- the voice is always front and center. Production elements frame the delivery without competing.
- **Hook construction:** Sung hooks. Melodic and memorable. The hooks are the emotional anchors of the tracks -- the moments where the vulnerability is most exposed.
