## Biography: Chris Pureka and the Androgynous Register

### The Voice

Chris Pureka's voice is the first thing you notice and the last thing you forget. It is a rich, deep, androgynous voice -- a register that resists gendered categorization. Not a "woman's voice" and not a "man's voice" but a **voice** -- full, resonant, unadorned, carrying the weight of personal experience without the shield of production or genre convention. The voice is the instrument. The guitar is accompaniment.

The androgynous register is not a novelty -- it is the acoustic expression of an identity that exists outside the gender binary. When Pureka sings about love, loss, and identity, the voice itself embodies the themes. The lyrics are about navigating a world built on binaries; the voice navigates the same territory acoustically. The personal is the sonic.

### Career

Pureka has released several albums, each deepening the confessional folk practice:

- **Driving North** (2006, self-released) -- the debut. Raw, direct, acoustic folk. Songs about love, loss, and the landscapes of the Pacific Northwest. The title itself is geographic -- driving north, toward the PNW, toward home.
- **How I Got Here** (2010, self-released) -- the second album. More polished but no less intimate. The title is autobiographical -- the journey that produced the artist. Songs about relationships, identity, the process of becoming.
- **Back in the Ring** (2016, self-released) -- the third album. Pureka returned after a hiatus, the title signaling resilience. The most musically diverse of the three, with fuller arrangements while maintaining the acoustic core.

The discography is small and intentional. Three albums across ten years. No filler, no contractual obligations, no label pressure to release on a schedule. Each album is a complete statement. The self-released model means every decision -- recording, mixing, artwork, distribution -- passes through the artist's hands.

### The Queer Folk Circuit

Pureka's career operates within the **queer folk circuit** -- a network of venues, festivals, house concerts, and online communities that sustain queer artists outside the mainstream music industry. This circuit is particularly strong in the Pacific Northwest, where progressive politics and DIY music culture create infrastructure for artists whose identities place them outside the mainstream.

The queer folk circuit is not a ghetto -- it is a community. Artists like Pureka draw audiences who come not just for the music but for the representation, the shared identity, the space where queerness is centered rather than marginalized. The circuit sustains careers that the mainstream industry would not.

### Connection to Mirah (Degree 50)

Pureka's entry connects to **Mirah** at degree 50 -- another queer artist in the engine. Mirah (Mirah Yom Tov Zeitlyn) is a queer singer-songwriter associated with the K Records/Olympia scene. The Mirah-Pureka connection strengthens the engine's queer artist thread: degree 50 (Mirah, Indie Folk, Olympia) and degree 76 (Pureka, Folk, Seattle/PNW). Different cities, different scenes, same identity thread.

But Pureka's androgynous register is distinctive because it is explicitly connected to a nonbinary identity. The voice is not "unusual" -- it is accurate. It sounds like the person singing.
