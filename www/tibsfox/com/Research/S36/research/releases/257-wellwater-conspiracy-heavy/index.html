Matt Cameron's Other Band

Wellwater Conspiracy is the record of what happens when one of the world's most accomplished rock drummers chooses to spend his time in the Space/Stoner Rock tradition. Matt Cameron — drummer for Soundgarden (1986-1997, 2010-2017) and Pearl Jam (1998-present) — co-founded Wellwater Conspiracy with guitarist John McBain, formerly of Monster Magnet. The collaboration announces its direction immediately: Monster Magnet are one of the defining stoner rock bands in American music history. When McBain left and took his riff orientation to Seattle, the result was a band that placed cosmic heavy rock in the same city that produced grunge.

"When you are the drummer for Soundgarden and Pearl Jam, your musical context is largely predetermined. Wellwater Conspiracy is the space where Cameron defines the terms himself."

Heavy Mode: Space/Stoner Rock

Stoner rock and space rock meet at a specific coordinate: riffs that repeat and deepen over extended passages, guitar tones thick with fuzz and sustain, production that is wide and reverb-soaked, lyrics (when present) that address cosmic scales. Wellwater Conspiracy occupies this coordinate precisely. McBain's Monster Magnet vocabulary — heavy, science-fictional, riff-driven — combined with Cameron's locked, precise drumming produces something that most stoner rock bands cannot achieve: rhythmic airtightness beneath the fuzz.

The McBain-Cameron combination is the engine: McBain's heavy riffs drive the structure; Cameron's precision makes the structure unimpeachable. Many stoner rock bands have loose rhythm sections (the genre can tolerate it; the riff is the main event). Wellwater Conspiracy has Cameron, which means the groove never loosens. The cosmic riff floats on a perfect foundation.
