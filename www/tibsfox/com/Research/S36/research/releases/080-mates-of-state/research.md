## Biography: Mates of State and the Organ Explosion

### The Duo

**Kori Gardner** (keyboards, organ, synthesizer, vocals) and **Jason Hammel** (drums, vocals) are a married couple who formed Mates of State in Lawrence, Kansas in 2000. They are a true duo -- two people, two instruments, no additional members, no guitar. The constraint is the creative engine: everything the band does comes from keyboards and drums.

### The Sound

Mates of State's music is **exuberant indie pop driven by organ/keyboard and drums**. The sound is dense and bright -- Gardner's keyboards fill the harmonic space that a full band would normally occupy with guitars, bass, and keyboards combined. The organ is not accompaniment -- it IS the band's harmonic structure. Combined with Hammel's drums and both voices, the duo generates a sound bigger than most four-piece bands.

The **no-guitar manifesto** is central: by removing the default instrument of rock, Mates of State forces the organ into a role it rarely occupies -- the entire melodic, harmonic, and textural foundation. The result is the **organ explosion**: walls of keyboard sound that are simultaneously melodic and rhythmic, propulsive and harmonic.

Albums:

- **My Solo Project** (2000, Polyvinyl) -- the debut. Organ and drums. The manifesto established.
- **Our Constant Concern** (2002, Polyvinyl) -- exuberant pop. The formula refined.
- **Team Boo** (2003, Polyvinyl) -- hooks sharpen. Pop precision with punk energy.
- **Bring It Back** (2006, Barsuk) -- Barsuk debut. Bigger production. The organ wall expands.
- **Re-Arrange Us** (2008, Barsuk) -- more expansive arrangements. Still keyboard-and-drums core.
- **Mountaintops** (2011, Barsuk) -- the most polished. Duo pop perfected.

(Sources: Bandcamp; Barsuk Records; AllMusic; KEXP; engine internal analysis)
