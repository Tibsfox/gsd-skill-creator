The Darker Sister Band

Childbirth is the Capitol Hill punk pop supergroup formed in 2013: Stacy Peck (drums, vocals), Julia Shapiro (guitar, vocals — also of Chastity Belt), Bree McKenna (bass — also of Tacocat, degree 282). Two consecutive Hardly Art degrees share a bassist, a neighborhood, and a label.

Tacocat at degree 282 is warm, hook-driven, feminist-content-via-subject-matter. Childbirth at degree 283 is darker, more confrontational, feminist-content-via-irony. Both are E=7. Both are Hardly Art. Both are Capitol Hill. The temperature is the difference.

"The band's name claims contested territory. The discomfort is the point. The dark humor that follows is the method."

Tacocat vs. Childbirth — Same Label, Different Temperature

Emily Nokes. Warm. Hook-driven. "Crimson Wave" is matter-of-fact. Feminist content delivered with brightness. The palindrome. The Scully tribute. The politics via the hook that stays.

283 — Childbirth

Three voices. Darker. More confrontational. Women's Rights (2015) uses the title's sincerity against itself. The politics via irony that makes you uncomfortable. The discomfort is deliberate.

Bree McKenna's bass plays in both. The same hands; two temperatures of the same feminist punk pop tradition.

Hardly Art — Two Consecutive Degrees

No label has produced two consecutive engine entries before degrees 282-283. Hardly Art, founded 2007 as a Sub Pop subsidiary, now claims both. The density of the Capitol Hill indie scene in the 2010s is visible in the engine's structure: two bands, one label, one neighborhood, one shared bassist.

Childbirth and Tacocat on Hardly Art in the 2010s is the contemporary echo of Bikini Kill and Sleater-Kinney on Kill Rock Stars in the 1990s: the label as the institutional home of a scene, a moment, a cluster of feminist punk values.

The Chastity Belt Connection

Julia Shapiro's primary band is Chastity Belt — formed at Whitman College (Walla Walla, WA) in 2010, relocated to Seattle, now a central Hardly Art act. Chastity Belt's music is indie rock with sardonic feminist observation: "Time to Go Home," "Drone," "Seattle Party." The Whitman-to-Seattle pipeline is a recurring PNW pattern.

Shapiro brings Chastity Belt's songwriting depth to Childbirth: more structural complexity, more willingness to sit in uncomfortable musical spaces when the subject requires. The Hardly Art triple (Chastity Belt + Tacocat + Childbirth) is connected by personnel, geography, and aesthetic values.
