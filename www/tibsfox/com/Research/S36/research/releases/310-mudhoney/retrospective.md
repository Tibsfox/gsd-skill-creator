## The Three Hundred Tenth Degree: The Band That Stayed

### Central District, Seattle. Sub Pop's longest-running major act. E=8. "Touch Me I'm Sick" (1988): the first grunge single. "Superfuzz Bigmuff" (1988): the EP that exported Sub Pop to the world. Green River split: Arm + Turner chose fuzz over melody. Mark Arm, Steve Turner, Matt Lukin, Dan Peters. Reprise 1992-1998. Back to Sub Pop. Active as of 2026. 35+ years. The band that stayed.

---

## What Worked

1. **The Green River split is Mudhoney's most important origin story and the grunge era's most productive dissolution.** The two-direction split (noise/fuzz to Mudhoney; melodic to Mother Love Bone/Pearl Jam) from a single band's end is the most efficient origin story in the grunge cluster. Degrees 294, 296, 310, and future Pearl Jam degrees are all consequences of a single 1988 event.
2. **"Touch Me I'm Sick" as the first grunge single is the cleanest single-sentence summary of Mudhoney's historical position.** The timing (August 1988, same month as Sub Pop's official launch) makes the band and the label's origin simultaneous and mutually defining.
3. **"The band that stayed" is the right frame for the Sub Pop loyalty narrative.** In a cluster where every other major act left (Nirvana, Soundgarden, Pearl Jam, TAD, Screaming Trees), Mudhoney's ultimate return to Sub Pop is the most important single fact about their legacy relationship with the label.

## Discoveries

1. **The *Superfuzz Bigmuff* title-as-aesthetic-statement is underappreciated.** Naming the record after your tools (Univox Super-Fuzz + Big Muff Pi) is a radical act of transparency: the record is what it says it is, which is the application of these specific tools at maximum commitment. No mystification. No artistic distance. The tools are the art.
2. **Matt Lukin's Melvins connection is important.** Lukin was the Melvins' bassist before joining Mudhoney. His presence connects Mudhoney directly to the Melvins (degree 308) at a personnel level, not just an influence level. The Melvins-Mudhoney connection runs through the bassist's actual biography.
3. **The Reprise period is better than its reputation.** *My Brother the Cow* (1995) is a strong record that has been undervalued because it occupies the "grunge hangover" period between commercial breakthrough and scene dissolution. The entry's identification of *My Brother the Cow* as the Reprise peak is a defensible critical position.
