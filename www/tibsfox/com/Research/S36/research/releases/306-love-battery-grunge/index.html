Grunge/Psych — The Compound Genre

The Grunge/Psych classification is the engine's first compound genre assignment for Love Battery. It is not a compromise — it is a more accurate description than either genre alone. Both traditions share roots in late-1960s electric rock (Black Sabbath for grunge; Blue Cheer and early Cream for psychedelia). Both use volume as texture. Both emerged from the same Seattle underground. Love Battery is where the traditions most explicitly coexist.

Dayglo (1992) is the compound genre's clearest document: "Out of Focus" is the signature track — heavy riff, dreamy middle section, heavy return. The structure is grunge; the texture is psychedelic. Applied at degree 306 in the grunge cluster, the compound classification makes explicit what was always present.

U-District in the Grunge Cluster

Most of the grunge cluster (294–308) is Capitol Hill music: Green River, Malfunkshun, Mother Love Bone, Skin Yard, Treepeople, Mad Season. Love Battery represents the U-District within the cluster — a reminder that the grunge scene was not limited to one neighborhood. Sub Pop's reach into the U-District through Love Battery demonstrates the label's geographic spread. The Ave (University Way NE): bars, record stores, cheap rent. The same infrastructure that produced psychedelia also produced the grunge-adjacent sound that reads as Grunge/Psych in the cluster context.
