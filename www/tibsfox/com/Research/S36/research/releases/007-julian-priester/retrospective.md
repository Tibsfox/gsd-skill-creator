## The Geographic Rotation: Central District Thread Paused

This is the retrospective's primary structural contribution. For the first time in eight degrees, the engine has produced an artist who is NOT connected to the Central District. Priester is associated with Seattle generally, Cornish College specifically, and his formative musical life was entirely in Chicago. The seven-degree Central District concentration (degrees 0-6) is broken.

### What the Rotation Reveals

The rotation was mandated at degree 5 (retrospective said "unacceptable" to continue without justification), reluctantly permitted at degree 6 (Bown justified on gender dimension and Erasure pattern grounds), and executed at degree 7 (Priester). The three-degree resolution timeline demonstrates the carry-forward mechanism's value: the engine identified the problem, permitted one exception with explicit justification, then enforced the rotation.

The rotation reveals something the concentration obscured: Seattle's musical geography is not reducible to one neighborhood. The Central District was the historical center of Black musical life, and the engine documented it exhaustively. But the city's experimental jazz scene — the scene that gave Seattle national jazz reputation from the 1980s onward — was NOT centered in the CD. It was centered in arts colleges (Cornish), concert halls, and the informal networks that connected musicians who had arrived from elsewhere. Priester, Frisell, and Horvitz built Seattle's jazz reputation, and none of them are Central District artists.

The geographic diversification does not diminish the CD thread. It contextualizes it. The CD produced the foundation (Jones, Anderson, Standifer, Berry, Bown). The city-wide scene built the superstructure (Priester, Frisell, Horvitz). Foundation and superstructure are both necessary. The engine has now documented both.

## The Brass Pair: Standifer Trumpet to Priester Trombone

Degrees 4 and 7 form the S36 engine's first brass-specific pair, analogous to the Berry-Bown piano pair (degrees 5-6). The comparison is rich because the two instruments represent opposite ends of the pitch-production spectrum:

| Dimension | Standifer (4) | Priester (7) |
|-----------|---------------|--------------|
| **Instrument** | Trumpet | Trombone |
| **Pitch mechanism** | Valves (3) — discrete positions | Slide — continuous, any frequency |
| **Pitch type** | Discrete — locked to harmonic series via valve combinations | Continuous — analog slide stops anywhere |
| **Microtonal access** | Lip-bending (embouchure) — limited range, difficult to sustain | Slide movement — direct, physical, unlimited within range |
| **Jazz tradition** | Bebop/swing (rapid chord changes, precision articulation) | Post-bop/avant-garde (modal fields, electronic texture, sustained tones) |
| **Institution** | University of Washington (formal, university) | Cornish College (formal, arts college) |
| **Pedagogical orientation** | Preservation — teach the canon | Expansion — teach boundary-crossing |
| **Era** | 1950s-2000s | 1970s-present |

The discrete-continuous distinction is the brass pair's defining axis. The trumpet's valves produce pitch through fixed plumbing — you press combinations and get specific notes. The trombone's slide produces pitch through analog positioning — you can stop anywhere. This is not merely a mechanical difference; it is a philosophical one. The trumpet assumes that music is built from discrete pitches (the Western twelve-tone temperament). The trombone is compatible with that assumption but not bound by it — it can produce all the pitches between the notes, all the microtonal zones that the tempered scale ignores.

The brass pair is the second instrument-specific pair in S36 (after the Berry-Bown piano pair). Like the piano pair, every dimension inverts. Discrete vs. continuous. Valved vs. slide. Preservation vs. expansion. University vs. arts college. The engine is building a library of instrument contrasts that map to broader cultural and philosophical distinctions.

## The Sun Ra / Hancock Bridge: A Career-Length Structural Insight

Priester's career demonstrates something the engine has not previously captured: an artist whose significance lies not in one period or one ensemble but in the CONNECTION between multiple traditions. Jones (degree 0) was defined by his architectural role — the arranger. Anderson (degree 3) was defined by her voice. Standifer (degree 4) was defined by his permanence. Berry (degree 5) was defined by his persistence. Bown (degree 6) was defined by her erasure.

Priester is defined by his BRIDGE function. He carried Sun Ra's cosmic permission into Hancock's electronic methodology. He carried Mwandishi's electroacoustic vocabulary into Cornish's pedagogical practice. He carried Chicago's DuSable pipeline into Seattle's arts-college ecosystem. At every stage, Priester connected one tradition to another — not by fusing them into something new, but by being present in both and allowing the transmission to occur through his person.

This is a new category for the artist-as-institution spectrum: **The Bridge** — an artist whose institutional significance lies in connecting traditions that would otherwise remain separate. The spectrum now has eight positions:

1. **Industry** (Jones, 0) — shapes the entire music industry from above
2. **Community** (Horvitz, 2) — builds institutions for other musicians
3. **Performer** (Anderson, 3) — the voice, the stage, the recording
4. **Presence** (Frisell, 1) — inhabits the scene, shapes it by being there
5. **Educator** (Standifer, 4) — transmits the tradition through teaching
6. **Persistence** (Berry, 5) — the fact of still being there IS the institution
7. **Erasure** (Bown, 6) — significant during career, absent from memory
8. **Bridge** (Priester, 7) — connects traditions through participation in both

## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The geographic rotation was worth enforcing.** Three degrees of escalation (mandated at 5, permitted-with-justification at 6, executed at 7) produced a clean rotation that opened new analytical dimensions. The engine can now document city-wide phenomena, not just neighborhood phenomena.

2. **The trombone's continuous-pitch mechanism is the richest single-instrument theoretical contribution since Anderson's vocal theory at degree 3.** The discrete-continuous spectrum (trumpet vs. trombone) maps to a fundamental distinction in music — and in information theory. Discrete vs. analog. Digital vs. continuous. The trombone is the analog instrument in a digital world.

3. **The Sun Ra / Hancock bridge is the first career-length structural insight.** Previous degrees analyzed artists through their peak period or their most prominent role. Priester's significance requires understanding the full career arc — from Arkestra (1953) to Cornish (present). The bridge function only becomes visible when you see the entire trajectory.

4. **The ECM label connection between Frisell and Priester establishes the first label-pair beyond Mercury.** ECM's aesthetic (space, texture, contemplation) is a thematic thread that connects two S36 artists through shared sensibility, not just shared business arrangement. The label thread is gaining analytical weight.

5. **The Priester-Horvitz-Frisell constellation diagram shows the temporal structure of Seattle's experimental scene.** Priester arrived ~1979, Frisell and Horvitz in the late 1980s. The scene was not created by simultaneous arrival — it was built in stages, with Priester as the advance party. This temporal dimension was not visible from degrees 1 and 2 alone.

### Source Quality

6. **Mwandishi documentation is excellent.** Bob Gluck's *You'll Know When You Get There* (2012) provides detailed documentation of the ensemble's personnel, recordings, and sonic methodology. Hancock's autobiography *Possibilities* (2014) corroborates from the bandleader's perspective. The Mwandishi period is one of the best-documented ensembles in the S36 engine.

7. **Sun Ra scholarship (Szwed's *Space Is the Place*) provides the philosophical framework.** The Arkestra's cosmology is not a marketing gimmick — it is a systematic worldview documented in academic scholarship. Priester's participation is confirmed in multiple sources.

## Accumulated Wisdom: Degrees 0 -> 1 -> 2 -> 3 -> 4 -> 5 -> 6 -> 7

1. **The carry-forward mechanism is the engine's most valuable feature.** (Reconfirmed, degree 8.)
   Eight releases deep. The geographic rotation mandate (queued at 5, permitted-with-justification
   at 6, executed at 7) demonstrates the mechanism's enforcement power. Items don't just accumulate;
   they create pressure that eventually forces resolution.

2. **The engine documents connections, not just artists.** Priester's primary significance is the
   BRIDGE between Sun Ra and Hancock — two traditions connected through one trombonist. Previous
   degrees documented individual artists. Degree 7 documents a structural relationship. The engine
   is becoming a map of how traditions connect, not just a catalog of who played what.

3. **Geographic diversification opens new analytical dimensions.** The Central District concentration
   produced the foundation — seven degrees of neighborhood-specific analysis. The rotation to
   Seattle-general opened the city-wide experimental scene, the arts-college pedagogical model,
   the Chicago-to-Seattle pipeline, and the ECM label thread. Concentration and diversification
   are both productive; the engine needs both.

4. **Instrument pairs produce the richest analysis.** The Berry-Bown piano pair (degrees 5-6) and
   the Standifer-Priester brass pair (degrees 4 and 7) both exhibit total inversion across every
   dimension. This pattern is now confirmed across two instrument families. The engine should
   continue looking for instrument pairs that create these contrast conversations.

5. **Compound patterns are a sign of engine maturity.** Degrees 0-6 produced simple patterns (one
   label each: Export, Draw, Erasure, etc.). Degree 7 produced the first compound pattern
   (Pedagogue-Draw). As the engine accumulates more data, simple categories become insufficient.
   Compound patterns acknowledge that artists occupy multiple positions simultaneously. This is
   not a failure of taxonomy; it is its growth.

6. **The trombone's continuous pitch mechanism extends the engine's deepest thread.** The blue note
   thread has been tracked since degree 0 — seven deployments across eight degrees, each using a
   different instrument-specific mechanism. The trombone slide is the most literal because the
   mechanism itself is continuous. The thread is the engine's most persistent and most analytically
   productive.

7. **Seattle's experimental jazz reputation was built by artists from elsewhere.** Priester (Chicago),
   Frisell (Denver/Berklee/NYC), Horvitz (NYC) — none of the three artists who define Seattle's
   experimental jazz scene are from Seattle. They were all Draws. The city's jazz reputation rests
   on its ability to attract and support experimental musicians, not on a local pipeline. This is
   the opposite of the Central District pattern, where the reputation rested on a local pipeline
   (Garfield, Jackson Street).
