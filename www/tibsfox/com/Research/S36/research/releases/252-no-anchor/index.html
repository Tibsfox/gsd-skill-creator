What Noise Rock Is

Noise rock uses the rock format — guitar, bass, drums, often vocals — but applies extreme volume, dissonance, and structural disruption to create music that is both physically aggressive and sonically complex. It is not about pleasant listening experience. It is about physical impact and the productive destruction of conventional rock aesthetics.

"The dissonance is not accidental. The feedback is not a mistake. The structural disruption is the content. Noise rock is rock music that has decided to be honest about what loud electric instruments can do."

The lineage runs from No Wave (New York, late 1970s) through SST Records (Black Flag, Sonic Youth), Touch and Go (Big Black, Shellac), and the 1990s independent underground (Unwound, Jesus Lizard). Steve Albini's production philosophy — all signals equal, no hiding in reverb — is the aesthetic standard for how noise rock is recorded. No-Anchor exists within this tradition.

Self-released artists own their recordings outright. No label buyout, no advance to recoup, no percentage claimed. Complete ownership.

No A&R mediation

DIY ethics

No label notes, no commercial pressure to soften the music. For noise rock especially, this matters: the genre exists partly because of institutional rejection of its extreme qualities.
