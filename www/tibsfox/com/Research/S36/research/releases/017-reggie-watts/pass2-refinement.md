### The Test Subject and the Uncategorizable: Ham Validates the System, Watts Breaks It

Mercury-Redstone 2 carried Ham — a chimpanzee trained at Holloman Air Force Base to perform lever-pulling tasks during spaceflight. Ham was the test subject: the living organism sent through the system to validate that the system could support life. Ham's flight (January 31, 1961) reached 253 km altitude and 9,426 km/h. He performed his tasks. He survived. His success qualified the Mercury capsule for human spaceflight — Alan Shepard flew five months later. Ham did not choose to fly. He was chosen. His function was to prove that the vehicle worked by surviving it.

Reggie Watts is the opposite of a test subject. He does not validate the system — he breaks it. The genre classification system (jazz, rock, pop, comedy, performance art) is the vehicle, and Watts refuses to ride in any single capsule. His Jazz/Comedy designation is not a compound genre — it is a superposition, two modes coexisting without merging. When Watts performs, the audience cannot determine whether they are at a jazz concert or a comedy show because both are happening simultaneously at full intensity. The classification system fails. The test subject (Ham) proves the system works by surviving within it. The uncategorizable artist (Watts) proves the system's limits by refusing to fit within it.

Taricha granulosa — the rough-skinned newt — is one of the most toxic animals in North America. Its skin contains tetrodotoxin, the same neurotoxin found in pufferfish, produced by symbiotic bacteria in the newt's skin glands. The toxin is a defense mechanism: any predator that bites the newt experiences paralysis and potentially death. The newt's bright orange belly is aposematic coloring — a warning signal that says "I am dangerous, do not eat me." The toxin and the warning coexist in the same organism. Ham was the test subject who proved the system safe. The newt is the test subject that proves the system dangerous — touch me and find out. Watts is the artist who proves the genre system insufficient — categorize me and find out.

The Ham-Watts-newt triangle maps three responses to a system:
- **Ham:** Conforms to the system, validates it, is released
- **Watts:** Refuses the system, breaks it, transcends it
- **Newt:** Weaponizes against the system, poisons those who try to consume it

### The Cornish Pipeline at Three: Faculty → Faculty → Student

Pass 1 documented Watts as the third Cornish connection and the first student (after Priester and Caliman as faculty). Pass 2 notes the pipeline's inversion and connects it to Mercury-Redstone 2's role in the pipeline from animal testing to human spaceflight.

Mercury-Redstone 2 was the validation flight — the test that proved the system could support a living occupant. After Ham, the next Mercury flights carried humans (Shepard, Grissom). The pipeline ran: unmanned test (MR-1, degree 16) → animal test (MR-2, degree 17) → human flight (upcoming degrees). The Cornish pipeline ran: faculty input (Priester/7, Caliman/9) → student output (Watts/17). Both pipelines have a validation step in the middle — Ham validates the capsule for humans; Watts validates the Cornish curriculum for interdisciplinary artists. The student who emerges from the institution IS the test of whether the institution works.

Watts studied jazz piano AND acting at Cornish — the first dual-discipline artist in the engine. The acting training is structural, not incidental: his performances are theatrical, his comedy is embodied, his audience interaction is staged. Cornish is a college of the arts (plural), and Watts used that breadth. The Cornish pipeline's stylistic range now spans experimental jazz trombone (Priester), hard bop saxophone (Caliman), and performance-art beatbox looping (Watts) — the widest spread of any institutional pipeline except Berklee.

### The Loop Pedal Lineage: Frisell to Watts, Contemplation to Absurdism

Pass 1 documented the Frisell-to-Watts technological lineage (delay pedal → loop station). Pass 2 develops this as a bracket around the first jazz quadrant of the circle.

Frisell (degree 1) uses delay and looping to create contemplative atmospheres — sustained guitar tones layered into ambient textures. Watts (degree 17) uses a loop station to create absurdist collages — beatbox rhythms, vocal harmonies, bass lines, comedy, all captured and stacked in real time. Same technology (capture → layer → build). Opposite aesthetic (meditation vs. chaos). The loop pedal lineage at degrees 1 and 17 brackets the jazz quadrant: Frisell at the entrance, Watts near the exit. Sixteen degrees apart, one technological thread connecting them. The technology evolved from analog delay (Frisell's Line 6 DL4) to digital looping (Watts's loop station). The aesthetic evolved from introspective to confrontational. The body-as-sound-source thread reaches its extreme: Watts's voice is simultaneously the instrument, the synthesizer, the drum machine, and the comedy script.
