## What Worked

1. **Tom Price / U-Men lineage is historically grounded.** The U-Men (1981-1989) are a genuinely important pre-grunge Seattle band. Price's membership provides Gas Huffer with underground roots that predate the grunge moment by nearly a decade. The lineage is documentable and significant.
2. **Amphetamine Reptile as primary AmRep entry in the engine is correct.** AmRep's catalog is one of the defining documents of 1990s American underground music. Gas Huffer's placement there gives the engine an entry point into the noise-rock tradition that otherwise might be underrepresented.
3. **E=7 streak analysis (261-262) is productive.** Comparing how Screaming Trees and Gas Huffer both achieve E=7 through different mechanisms (density vs. speed) is a genuine insight into how the energy scale works.
4. **Humor as deliberate mode is documented correctly.** Gas Huffer's irreverence is not incidental; it is a distinct artistic stance in a scene often dominated by serious emotional content.

## Discoveries

1. **Fremont as neighborhood has a specific sonic logic.** The absurdist carnivalesque character of Fremont maps onto Gas Huffer's approach better than any other Seattle neighborhood. The band did not happen to be from Fremont; the neighborhood and the band share an aesthetic DNA.
2. **E=7 via speed and E=7 via density are two distinct acoustic experiences that produce the same engine reading.** This reveals something about the limits of any single-number energy metric: it collapses different acoustic experiences into the same value. The two mechanisms are both correct at E=7; they just sound different.
