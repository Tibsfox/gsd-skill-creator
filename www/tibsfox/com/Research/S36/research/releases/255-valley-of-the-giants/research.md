## STONER ROCK -- THE GENRE

### Riffs, Fuzz, Psychedelic Extension

Stoner rock is the genre that frames Valley of the Giants:

- **The genre definition.** Stoner rock (also: stoner metal, desert rock, heavy psych) is a genre that combines the heavy guitar riffs of Black Sabbath-era metal with the psychedelic extended-play aesthetic of late 1960s rock. The name "stoner" refers to the genre's thematic and sonic association with cannabis: slow, heavy riffs that feel heavier and more spacious under altered perception; extended instrumental passages designed for drift; lyrics that often address inner states, desert imagery, or cosmic themes. The genre is not about aggression (unlike thrash, death metal, or hardcore) but about sustained weight and atmospheric texture.
- **The Sabbath foundation.** All stoner rock traces back to Black Sabbath's early records (*Black Sabbath* 1970, *Paranoid* 1970, *Master of Reality* 1971). Tony Iommi's guitar tuning -- downtuned by multiple semitones from standard, played through a Laney amplifier with sustain pushed to maximum -- created the prototype stoner rock sound. The guitar tone is not bright or cutting; it is warm, dark, and enormous. The riffs are slow enough to feel like geological events.
- **Kyuss and the desert rock tradition.** The American desert rock movement emerged from Palm Desert, California in the early 1990s, with Kyuss (1987-1995) as the primary document. Guitarist Josh Homme (later Queens of the Stone Age) developed a specific California desert rock aesthetic: Sabbath tunings combined with psychedelic extension, outdoor generator parties in the Coachella Valley, a sun-baked spaciousness that is the geographic and cultural opposite of Seattle's grey atmospheric rock. Queens of the Stone Age refined this: tighter songs, broader commercial appeal, but the same Iommi-derived guitar DNA.
- **Fu Manchu, Monster Magnet, Sleep.** Other defining stoner rock bands: Fu Manchu (California, '87-present) for their tight, fast stoner; Monster Magnet (New Jersey, '89-present) for their lysergic heavy rock; Sleep (San Jose, '90-present) for their doom-stoner synthesis -- particularly *Dopesmoker* (aka *Jerusalem*), a 63-minute single-track album that represents the extreme endpoint of stoner rock's riff-repetition-as-mantra philosophy.
- **The PNW adaptation.** Pacific Northwest stoner rock tends toward the atmospheric and psychedelic rather than the purely riff-forward. The grey climate, the forested geography, and the existing psychedelic tradition (going back to Jimi Hendrix) makes PNW stoner rock more introspective and atmospheric than California desert rock. Valley of the Giants exist in this PNW stoner tradition.

(Sources: Stoner rock genre definition; Black Sabbath Tony Iommi tuning; Kyuss Palm Desert California 1987-1995; Josh Homme Queens of the Stone Age; Fu Manchu; Monster Magnet; Sleep *Dopesmoker*; PNW adaptation of stoner)

## VALLEY OF THE GIANTS -- THE BAND

### Seattle Heavy Psych

Valley of the Giants are a contemporary Seattle heavy psych/stoner rock band:

- **The name.** "Valley of the Giants" is a reference to a grove of ancient Douglas firs in the Oregon Coast Range -- one of the old-growth forests that define the Pacific Northwest's ecological identity. The name situates the band firmly in the PNW landscape: ancient trees, deep forest, the scale of things that have been growing for centuries. This is not desert rock naming; this is rain forest naming.
- **The sound.** Valley of the Giants operate in the heavy psych / stoner tradition with a PNW atmospheric quality: thick, downtuned guitar riffs combined with extended instrumental passages that use reverb and feedback to create the sense of being inside something very large. The band is in the tradition of Sleep, Windhand, and YOB -- doom-adjacent stoner with a meditative, psychedelic quality.
- **Seattle context.** Heavy psych bands in Seattle operate in a specific niche: not the mainstream rock circuit, not the post-hardcore underground (Botch's territory), not the sludge tradition (Helms Alee), but the specific subset of heavy music that uses long, riff-forward songs with psychedelic extension. The audience for this music in Seattle is dedicated and specific.
- **E=6 placement.** E=6 in heavy psych means: present, heavy, riff-forward, but not at the maximum energy of post-hardcore (E=8) or even sludge (E=7). The songs move at moderate tempo, the riffs are the primary content, and the psychedelic extension -- the second half of each song that moves away from the riff into more atmospheric territory -- constitutes a significant portion of the running time. Heavy enough to be physically present; psychedelic enough to create interior states.

(Sources: Valley of the Giants band research; Valley of the Giants Oregon Coast Range Douglas fir grove; heavy psych sound; Windhand; YOB; Seattle heavy psych circuit; E=6 in heavy psych context)

## THE HEAVY PSYCH SOUNDS LABEL

### An Italian Independent for Global Heavy Psych

Heavy Psych Sounds Records is Valley of the Giants' label home:

- **Founded.** Heavy Psych Sounds Records (HPS) was founded in Italy in 2012 by Davide Sferra. The label began as a distribution network for European heavy psych and expanded into a genuine international label for stoner rock, doom, and psychedelic heavy music.
- **The roster.** HPS has a substantial catalog of heavy psych and stoner rock bands: Dead Meadow, The Cosmic Dead, Earthless, Causa Sui, Acid King, Wo Fat, Elder, Sergeant Thunderhoof, Mothership, and many others. The label's aesthetic is explicitly retro-referencing: many HPS records are designed to look and feel like 1970s records -- warm analog production, illustrated covers, vinyl-first releases.
- **The significance of an Italian label.** Valley of the Giants being on an Italian heavy psych label is characteristic of the genre's international community: stoner rock and heavy psych have a disproportionately large European audience relative to their American commercial profile. The European underground heavy music scene (Germany, Italy, Scandinavia, Netherlands) has been a significant market for American stoner and doom bands since the early 1990s. HPS is part of this infrastructure.
- **The vinyl-first philosophy.** Heavy psych is the genre most likely to be released on vinyl first, for reasons that are simultaneously cultural and sonic: the genre is explicitly retro-referencing (it sounds like it was made in 1972), and the vinyl listening experience -- the warmth, the physicality, the ritual of playing a record -- matches the genre's meditative aesthetic. Valley of the Giants' presence on an international vinyl-first label is characteristic of where stoner rock exists in the current music market.

(Sources: Heavy Psych Sounds Records founded 2012 Davide Sferra; HPS roster; Earthless; Acid King; Elder; European heavy psych market; vinyl-first philosophy in stoner rock; retro-referencing aesthetic)

## THE SOUND -- STONER ROCK TECHNIQUE

### Fuzz, Extended Play, and the Riff as Structure

Valley of the Giants' sonic approach in detail:

- **Fuzz guitar.** The fuzz pedal is the central tool of stoner rock. Where grunge uses high-gain amplifier distortion (the Mudhoney/Soundgarden approach), stoner rock uses fuzz -- a softer, warmer, more harmonically rich form of distortion that adds overtones to the guitar signal without the harsh clipping of high-gain. The fuzz tone makes the guitar sound warm, enormous, and slightly out of focus -- the desired effect.
- **Downtuning.** Like sludge (Helms Alee, 253), stoner rock uses extended downtuning: guitars tuned to C, B, or lower. The downtuning moves the guitar's fundamental frequencies into the bass range, adding physical weight to the low end. When a downtuned fuzz guitar and a conventional tuned bass play the same riff, the low-frequency content is enormous.
- **Extended song structures.** Stoner rock songs are long. A typical stoner rock track might be 8-12 minutes: a few minutes of riff establishment, a few minutes of development, and then a long psychedelic extension section where the band drifts into improvised or semi-improvised territory. This is the genre's most distinctive structural characteristic -- the willingness to stay with a riff, or to abandon the riff entirely for pure atmosphere, for longer than any other rock genre.
- **The riff as mantra.** The long riff repetition in stoner rock creates a meditative state: the same pattern repeated many times at slow tempo triggers a trance-like listener response. Sleep's *Dopesmoker* (63 minutes, one track) is the extreme version; Valley of the Giants operate at a more accessible end of the same principle.
- **Vocal approach.** Stoner rock vocals tend toward the mid-range, buried somewhat in the mix, letting the guitars carry the primary load. The voice is present but not dominant -- unlike post-hardcore (where the voice is an instrument) or classic rock (where the voice is the primary melodic element). The buried vocal is characteristic: the voice adds another texture rather than being the focal point.

(Sources: Fuzz pedal vs. amplifier distortion; downtuning in stoner rock; extended song structures; riff repetition as mantra; Sleep *Dopesmoker*; vocal approach in stoner rock; typical track length analysis)

## LEGACY AND SIGNIFICANCE

### The Living Heavy Psych Tradition

- **Stoner rock as living tradition.** Unlike some genres that had a peak moment and now exist primarily as nostalgia, stoner rock is an actively evolving genre. New bands emerge constantly; labels like Heavy Psych Sounds expand their rosters annually; the European festival circuit (Desertfest, Psychedelic Circus, Freak Valley Festival) maintains a substantial live audience. Valley of the Giants exist within a living tradition, not a historical artifact.
- **The name as PNW identity.** "Valley of the Giants" is one of the most explicitly Pacific Northwestern band names in the engine. It references old-growth forest -- one of the PNW's most contested and powerful ecological landscapes. The naming locates the band in the specific geography of the region in a way that few other band names do.
- **The Heavy Psych Sounds connection.** Being on HPS gives Valley of the Giants access to the international heavy psych market: European distribution, festival booking, visibility within the genre's dedicated global audience. This is more valuable for a stoner rock band than a Sub Pop deal would be -- the genre's audience is not the same as grunge's audience, and the label infrastructure that serves it is international and independent.

(Sources: Stoner rock as living tradition; Desertfest; Psychedelic Circus; Valley of the Giants band name as PNW identity; old-growth forest contest and significance; HPS international market access)
