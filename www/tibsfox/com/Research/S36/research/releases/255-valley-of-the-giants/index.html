RIFFS. FUZZ. OLD-GROWTH SCALE. — PNW STONER ROCK — HEAVY PSYCH SOUNDS (ITALY)

Valley of the Giants take their name from one of the great old-growth Douglas fir groves of the Oregon Coast Range. The scale of ancient forest is in the music: thick, warm, enormous riffs at slow tempo, extended into psychedelic drift. Black Sabbath's tuning. Kyuss's California sun translated into PNW grey. Sleep's meditative repetition at accessible length. Heavy Psych Sounds Records: the Italian independent that serves the international heavy psych community. Paired with the log truck's road percussion — the working forest's low-frequency industrial voice.

Stoner Rock -- The Genre

Stoner rock combines the heavy riffing of early Black Sabbath with the psychedelic extended-play aesthetic of late 1960s rock. The name describes the sonic and thematic territory: slow, warm, enormous riffs that reward altered or meditative listening; extended instrumental passages designed for drift; lyrics addressing inner states and cosmic themes. The genre traces from Tony Iommi's downtuned Laney amp tone through Kyuss's Palm Desert generator parties (California, 1987-1995) to Sleep's Dopesmoker (63 minutes, one track, the genre's extreme endpoint).

"The riff as mantra: repeated slowly, at high volume, until it creates a meditative state. Sleep understood this. Valley of the Giants operate in the same tradition at a more accessible entry point."

The PNW Stoner Tradition

Pacific Northwest stoner rock adapts the California desert model for a greyer, more atmospheric climate. Where Kyuss and Queens of the Stone Age evoke sun, sand, and wide open Coachella Valley distance, PNW stoner bands evoke forest, fog, and the specific interior feeling of being inside something very large and old. The fuzz guitar sounds different in the rain. The reverb has more atmosphere to work with. Valley of the Giants' name says it precisely: this is the sound of standing inside a grove of ancient Douglas fir, the canopy 200 feet up, the forest floor in perpetual shadow.

Heavy Psych Sounds Records

Heavy Psych Sounds Records was founded in Italy in 2012 by Davide Sferra. Beginning as distribution and expanding into a label, HPS now has a roster that reads as a comprehensive map of international heavy psych: Dead Meadow, Earthless, Acid King, Elder, Causa Sui, Sergeant Thunderhoof, Mothership. The label is vinyl-first and retro-referencing -- many HPS records are designed to look and feel like 1972. The European heavy psych festival circuit (Desertfest, Psychedelic Circus, Freak Valley Festival) provides the live context. Valley of the Giants on HPS means access to this international infrastructure: a Seattle band with an Italian label in a genuinely international heavy psych market.

Energy = 6 -- The Psychedelic Middle-High

E=6 in stoner rock is the sustained weight of something moving steadily. Not the maximum ferocity of post-hardcore (E=8) or the slow avalanche of sludge (E=7), but a mid-to-high energy that carries physical presence and psychedelic extension in equal measure. The riff moves at moderate tempo; the fuzz makes it enormous; the song continues past the point where conventional rock would stop; the psychedelic extension begins. E=6 is the energy of the long haul rather than the sudden attack.
