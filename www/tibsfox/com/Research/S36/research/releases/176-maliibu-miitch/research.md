## STRUCTURAL EVENT 2: FIRST TRAP -- A NEW GENRE

### Trap Enters the Engine

Trap is hip-hop's most dominant subgenre of the 2010s-2020s. Its defining characteristics:

- **808 bass:** Deep, sustained 808 bass that defines the low end. The bass is felt physically. This is body music.
- **Hi-hat patterns:** Rapid-fire hi-hat rolls and patterns. Triplet hi-hats. The rhythmic signature of trap.
- **Snare patterns:** Hard-hitting snares, often with heavy reverb. Snares that crack.
- **Tempo:** Typically 130-170 BPM (though half-time feel makes it feel like 65-85 BPM). The duality of fast and slow simultaneously.
- **Vocal style:** Often Auto-Tuned or processed. Melodic rap. Ad-libs. The voice as texture.

That trap enters at degree 176 -- not earlier -- reflects the engine's focus on Seattle's local scene rather than national trends. Seattle's relationship with trap is distinctive: the city's hip-hop identity has been built on lyrical and experimental traditions (Shabazz Palaces, Ishmael Butler, DoNormaal). Trap arrives as an import that local artists reshape.
