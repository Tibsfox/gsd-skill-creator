## The Fifty-Sixth Degree: The Dream Haze

### Lemolo Extends the Folk Zone to Twelve and Reaches Gender Parity

Degree 56 is structurally significant on six axes: it extends the folk-adjacent record to twelve consecutive degrees (from eleven at degree 55), it returns Indie Folk to the streak as the fifth use of that classification, it maps Capitol Hill for a third time (first triple neighborhood), it delivers consecutive E=2 with degree 55 (first back-to-back E=2 in the folk zone), it activates a Portland-Seattle PNW corridor through Tender Loving Empire, and it records the sixteenth non-male artist -- pushing the folk zone to exactly 50% gender parity. Lemolo is the folk zone's dream haze -- reverb-drenched layered vocals dissolving the boundaries between voice, room, and electronic texture into a single luminous field. The duo collective: two women, one atmosphere.

- **Situation:** Lemolo is a dreampop/indie folk duo based in Seattle, Washington, in the Capitol Hill neighborhood. Meagan Grandall (vocals, guitar, keyboards) and Kendra Cox (drums, percussion, vocals). Named after Lemolo Falls in Oregon's Umpqua National Forest -- Chinook Jargon for "wild." Three albums: *The Kaleidoscope* (2012, self-released), *Swirl* (2014, Tender Loving Empire), *Today, I Am Well* (2018, self-released). KEXP sessions. The Stranger, Seattle Weekly, City Arts Magazine coverage.
- **Root Cause:** Degree 56's structural events converge because Lemolo occupies a unique intersection: the folk-adjacent streak extension (from 45), Indie Folk's return, Capitol Hill's triple, consecutive E=2, the PNW corridor activation, and the gender-parity milestone. Each event is independently sourced.
