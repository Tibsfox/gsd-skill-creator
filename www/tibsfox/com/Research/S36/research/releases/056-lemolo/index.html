The PNW Corridor: Portland – Seattle via Tender Loving Empire

Tender Loving Empire (Portland-based independent label) released Swirl (2014). Named after Lemolo Falls in Oregon’s Umpqua National Forest — a Chinook Jargon word meaning “wild.” The duo bridges Seattle and Portland through the I-5 corridor: the PNW as a single creative market.

The Dream in the Falls

Lemolo is a dreampop/indie folk duo based in Seattle, Washington, in the Capitol Hill neighborhood. The duo consists of Meagan Grandall (vocals, guitar, keyboards) and Kendra Cox (drums, percussion, vocals). Lemolo fuses folk songwriting foundations with dreampop atmosphere: heavy reverb, layered vocals, shimmering electronic textures, and a sense of vast sonic space.

Named after Lemolo Falls in Oregon’s Umpqua National Forest — the word derives from Chinook Jargon, meaning “wild” or “untamed.” The name plants the duo’s identity in the Pacific Northwest landscape: waterfalls, old-growth forests, and the natural reverb of falling water.

The Kaleidoscope: The Debut (2012)

The Kaleidoscope (2012, self-released) — folk songs refracted through dreampop production. A kaleidoscope refracts a single image into overlapping patterns; Lemolo’s sound refracts the voice through reverb and layering into shimmering multiplicities. Acoustic guitar anchors; electronic textures rotate.

Self-released via Bandcamp. No label, no distribution network. The debut as self-birth: two people making an album and putting it into the world themselves. KEXP sessions and The Stranger coverage provided initial visibility.

Swirl: Tender Loving Empire (2014)

Swirl (2014, Tender Loving Empire) deepened the dreampop production while maintaining the folk core. The title suggests water motion — swirl, vortex, spiral. Water imagery connects to the band name (Lemolo Falls), to the PNW landscape, and to the production aesthetic: the swirl of reverb around the voice.

Coverage from KEXP, The Stranger, Seattle Weekly. The Portland label extended reach along the I-5 corridor — the PNW as a single creative market.

Today, I Am Well (2018)

Today, I Am Well (2018, self-released) — a return to independence after the Tender Loving Empire partnership. The title is present-tense affirmation: not “I was well” or “I will be well” but today, specifically, in this moment. The comma isolates the temporal marker. The declaration must be renewed each day.

Three albums across seven years — identical arc to Shelby Earl (degree 55): three albums, seven years, widening intervals. Two consecutive folk-adjacent degrees with mirrored discographic structures.
