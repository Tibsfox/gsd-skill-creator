## What Worked

1. **The *Dirt* framing is the entry's most important contribution.** The distinction between *Dirt* as heavy album and *Dirt* as autobiographical document of addiction is the correct frame. No other Big Four album is directly about the illness that would kill its vocalist. This is not commentary -- it is factual description.
2. **The Staley-Cantrell vocal harmony documentation is technically specific and accurate.** Minor seconds, tritones, parallel minor thirds -- these are verifiable interval choices that explain the distinctive dissonant quality of their harmonics. The music theory is correct.
3. **The 245-247 corridor framing is the engine's most important structural observation in this session.** Three consecutive degrees at E=7 or E=8 -- Hendrix, Hendrix early, Alice in Chains -- will not be matched. Documenting this as a unit is the correct approach.
4. **The Big Four differentiation is clean.** Nirvana: pop melody under noise. Pearl Jam: classic rock scope. Soundgarden: technical range. AIC: *Dirt*. Each has its specific identity.

## Discoveries

1. **"Rooster" is the PNW's most important veteran song.** Jerry Cantrell's father served in Vietnam; the song documents his experience and uses the nickname he earned there. The PNW has Fort Lewis, multiple military installations, and a large veteran community. "Rooster" connects the band's personal history to a regional reality.
2. **The *Jar of Flies* EP achievement is under-recognized.** The first EP to debut at #1 on the Billboard 200 -- an acoustic EP from a band with a reputation for heaviness. This is a demonstration of genuine musical range, not a commercial calculation. Alice in Chains could have followed *Dirt* with another heavy album. They made *Jar of Flies* instead.
3. **Layne Staley's death timeline is important to document correctly.** Died April 5, 2002. Discovered April 19. Alone. The two-week gap between death and discovery is one of the most haunting facts about the end of the Staley era, and it documents the isolation that addiction produces.

## Pair Analysis: Alice in Chains + Sol Duc Falls

- **Energy:** E=8 + E=7 = E=15. The engine's second-highest combined degree energy (behind Hendrix 245 + river current at E=13... actually E=13 is lower, so 247 is higher). The canyon roar of Sol Duc Falls pairs with the resonant, canyon-deep guitar tone of Alice in Chains. Both are sounds that bounce off rock walls, that accumulate and intensify in enclosed spaces. Both are in the Olympic temperate rainforest's acoustic environment -- one natural, one made in a recording studio that sought to recreate that weight.
- **Theme:** RESONANT ENCLOSURE. Sol Duc Falls roars in an Olympic canyon. Alice in Chains' guitar tones are deep, warm, amplified -- the sound of a large room with stone walls. Both sounds require space to fully develop; both fill whatever space they're given. The pairing is sonically accurate.
