## POST-HARDCORE IN SEATTLE

### Beyond the Hardcore Template

TAAS exemplifies post-hardcore's expansion beyond punk's boundaries:

- **Post-hardcore defined.** The genre takes hardcore punk's intensity and adds complexity: unusual time signatures, dissonant harmonies, dynamic range, and compositional ambition that exceeds punk's three-chord template. Post-hardcore bands play with a punk's energy but a progressive's sophistication.
- **Seattle post-hardcore.** The city's post-hardcore tradition includes Botch, TAAS, Blood Brothers (degree 218), Minus the Bear (tangentially), and earlier bands like Unwound. The scene was centered on Capitol Hill and the all-ages venue The Crocodile Cafe, Neumos, and house shows.
- **The TAAS sound.** Angular guitar riffs that cut rather than flow. Brian Cook's bass is the rhythmic anchor -- thick, distorted, providing the weight beneath the angular guitars. Steve Snere's vocals alternate between melodic singing and harsh screaming. The keyboard/synth work (often from Snere or later members) adds an unsettling, almost horror-movie textural element.
- **Short career, intense output.** TAAS existed for only 7 years (2002-2009) but released three albums: *This Is Meant to Hurt You* (2003), *Easter* (2006), and *Tail Swallower and Dove* (2008). Each album intensified the formula. The band burned bright and ended before diminishing.

(Sources: Post-hardcore genre history; Seattle post-hardcore scene; TAAS discography; venue history; sound analysis)

## THE BOTCH FAMILY TREE

### Two Branches from One Root

The Botch diaspora is one of Seattle's most productive:

- **Botch (1993-2002).** The mathcore/metalcore band that produced two radically different offshoots.
- **Branch 1: Minus the Bear (2001-2018).** Dave Knudson took Botch's technical precision and channeled it into accessible math rock. Shimmer. Groove. Electronic textures. E=6.
- **Branch 2: These Arms Are Snakes (2002-2009).** Brian Cook took Botch's aggressive energy and channeled it into angular post-hardcore. Dissonance. Violence. Unsettling textures. E=7.
- **The contrast.** Same origin (Botch), different mutations. MTB made the math beautiful. TAAS kept the math violent. Both are recognizable as Botch descendants -- the technical precision, the rhythmic complexity -- but the aesthetic destinations are opposite.
- **Brian Cook's career.** After TAAS, Brian Cook continued in Russian Circles (an instrumental post-metal trio on Sargent House -- connecting to Helms Alee's label, degree 214). Cook's trajectory: mathcore (Botch) to post-hardcore (TAAS) to post-metal (Russian Circles). Three genres, one bass player, consistent excellence.

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of These Arms Are Snakes

- **Tempo.** Fast to mid-tempo. 120-170 BPM. Punk-adjacent speed with progressive-influenced pauses.
- **Guitar.** Angular, dissonant, atonal passages alternating with heavy riffs. The guitar cuts rather than flows. Unusual voicings and chord shapes that avoid standard rock harmony.
- **Bass.** Brian Cook's bass is thick, distorted, often playing riffs independent of the guitar. The bass provides the weight and the low-end anchor while guitars slash above.
- **Drums.** Dynamic, precise, powerful. Blast beats, complex fills, sudden time signature changes. The drums drive the intensity.
- **Vocals.** Alternating clean melodic singing and harsh screaming. Steve Snere's voice moves between modes rapidly, sometimes within a single phrase. The contrast is jarring by design.
- **Keyboards/Synth.** Unsettling textures, horror-movie ambience, industrial noise. The electronic elements add a dimension that pure guitar bands lack.
- **Production.** Dense but clear. The angular riffs need to be heard, the time signature changes need to be felt. Not muddy -- precise even in chaos.
- **Dynamic range.** Extreme. Quiet-loud-quiet at punk speed. The music punishes complacency.

(Sources: TAAS recordings; post-hardcore production; angular guitar technique; Brian Cook bass style; vocal dynamics)
