## The Three Hundred Twelfth Degree: The U-District at E=9

### The first Seattle grunge band signed to a major label (A&M, 1989). Chris Cornell: four-octave voice, songwriter of extraordinary gift. Kim Thayil: the most theoretically sophisticated guitar in grunge. Matt Cameron: metric complexity with power-precision balance. Hiro Yamamoto: original bassist, later to Truly (degrees 220, 302). *Badmotorfinger* and *Nevermind* on the same day, September 24, 1991. *Superunknown*: #1, "Black Hole Sun," 9 million copies. Temple of the Dog (degree 225): Cornell-Vedder-Cameron tribute to Andrew Wood. Chris Cornell died May 18, 2017. He is remembered through the artistry.

## Discoveries

1. **Badmotorfinger and Nevermind on the same day (September 24, 1991) is the engine's most striking release-date coincidence.** Two of the three most important grunge records of the 1990s entered the market simultaneously. One broke commercially in weeks; the other built gradually. The different commercial trajectories from the same starting point document how commercial success in rock operates: not quality alone, but the specific combination of quality, timing, and the particular commercial moment.
2. **Superunknown was released 28 days before Cobain's death.** This fact is almost never surfaced. The commercial peak of grunge (Soundgarden reaching #1, March 8, 1994) and the end of Nirvana (April 5, 1994) are 28 days apart. The grunge era's commercial peak and its most significant ending are essentially simultaneous.
3. **Kim Thayil's theoretical sophistication is systematically underrecognized in grunge histories.** Most histories discuss the Seattle sound in terms of volume and attitude; Thayil's actual music theory knowledge &mdash; the specific deployments of alternate tuning, modal harmony, odd time signatures &mdash; is at a level that most rock guitarists never approach. Soundgarden was the most musically sophisticated of the major grunge bands by a significant margin.
