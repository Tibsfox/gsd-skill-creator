## What Worked

1. **Rainier Valley return is geographically significant.** 76 degrees between appearances. The neighborhood's proximity to the Central District -- where Hendrix grew up -- is the engine's most important geographic resonance for this entry.
2. **The twin sibling dynamic is the entry's defining structural fact.** The two-piece format works because of the communication depth that comes from shared lives, not despite the limited personnel.
3. **Sub Pop as ongoing institution.** The Black Tones are a current Sub Pop act. The label's presence in the engine is not only historical.
4. **The blues thread from Hendrix (245-246) to Black Tones (249) is the entry's most important narrative line.** Three degrees of separation; the tradition is continuous even through different genres.

## Discoveries

1. **Eva Walker's guitar is the most significant aspect of the entry's cultural weight.** Female blues rock guitarists are rare in any era; the tradition is dominated by male musicians. Her visibility and quality challenge a long-standing exclusion.
2. **The album title *Cobain* is a notable artistic statement.** Naming a blues rock debut album after Kurt Cobain -- not to cover his music but to acknowledge the lineage -- is a sophisticated positioning. It says: Seattle rock, Sub Pop, but the blues end of the tradition.
