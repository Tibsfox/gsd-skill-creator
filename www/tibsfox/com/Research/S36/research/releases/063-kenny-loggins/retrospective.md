## Everett Enters: The Fourth I-5 Corridor Node

### Departure City vs Workspace City

Degree 63 introduces Everett to the engine -- the fourth I-5 corridor node.

- **Situation:** Everett, Washington -- industrial port city thirty miles north of Seattle. Boeing, Navy, timber. Kenny Loggins born here 1948. Family moved to California when Loggins was young. Everett is the birth city, not the career city. The I-5 corridor now has four nodes: Portland, Seattle, Bellingham, Everett.
- **Root Cause:** Everett is the engine's first "departure city" -- a location that produces an artist and exports them elsewhere. Unlike Capitol Hill (workspace), Bellingham (launchpad), or Portland (scene base), Everett's contribution is a birth certificate.
- **Recommendation:** Document Everett as a departure city. Note the I-5 corridor with four nodes. Track whether future degrees add additional corridor nodes.

## The E=3 Calibration: Local Footprint vs Commercial Success

### Why Not E=5 for a Global Star?

Degree 63 assigns E=3 to one of the most commercially successful PNW-born artists.

- **Situation:** Kenny Loggins had #1 hits, Grammy wins, arena tours, and global cultural impact through movie soundtracks. By any measure of commercial energy, Loggins was E=5 or E=6. The engine assigns E=3.
- **Root Cause:** The engine measures local cultural footprint, not global commercial success. Loggins left the PNW as a child. His career was Los Angeles-based. He did not play local venues, did not receive KEXP rotation, was not part of the indie ecosystem. His PNW connection is biographical, not cultural. E=3 reflects the attenuated local tie.
- **Recommendation:** Document the E=3 calibration decision. The principle is now explicit: energy measures local connection, not global volume. Note the Ann Wilson contrast (E=5, stayed connected) as the benchmark. E=3 is becoming the "connected but not embedded" level.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- E=3 calibration documented. Local footprint principle explicit. Track E=3 as "connected but not embedded" at future degrees.

## The Soundtrack King: Unique Phenomenon

Degree 63 documents a career uniquely defined by movie soundtracks.
