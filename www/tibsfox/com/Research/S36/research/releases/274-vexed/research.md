## CAPITOL HILL HARDCORE

### The Neighborhood at Full Speed

Capitol Hill is the central bohemian neighborhood of Seattle, and in the early 1980s it was one of the most productive sources of punk and hardcore music in the city:

- **The neighborhood.** Capitol Hill runs east of downtown on an elevated plateau. Its commercial spine -- Broadway -- has been a center of Seattle's alternative culture since the 1970s. By the early 1980s, the neighborhood was home to a dense concentration of punk and hardcore bands, venues, and DIY operations. The rents were low enough for bands to operate; the foot traffic and community density supported shows.
- **Vexed as Capitol Hill band.** Vexed were identified with Capitol Hill in the way that the neighborhood's bands always are -- not through marketing but through where they lived, rehearsed, and played. The Capitol Hill hardcore scene in 1982-1986 was distinct from the broader Seattle punk scene: tighter, more geographically concentrated, more likely to involve people who lived within walking distance of each other.
- **The venue ecosystem.** Capitol Hill in the early 1980s had a network of venues, house shows, and practice spaces that supported hardcore music. The Central Tavern, various rented halls, and house basements formed the infrastructure. Vexed operated in this ecosystem.
- **Community density.** What made Capitol Hill hardcore distinct from other Seattle scenes was density -- the musicians, the audience, and the infrastructure were all in close proximity. This created a tighter feedback loop between bands and their community than the more diffuse city-wide scene.

## VEXED -- THE BAND

### Formation and Operation

- **1982 formation.** Vexed formed in Seattle in 1982, two years after The Refuzors (273) and in the same early hardcore generation as The Fartz (270). The early 1980s Seattle hardcore scene was small enough that bands of the same generation knew each other and shared shows, spaces, and audiences.
- **The name.** Vexed -- to be annoyed, troubled, agitated. In the context of hardcore punk, the name functions as a statement of emotional state: perpetually vexed, in response to the political and social conditions of the early Reagan era. Hardcore names in this period often operated this way -- naming the emotional content directly.
- **Capitol Hill specificity.** While other Seattle hardcore bands (The Refuzors, The Fartz) operated city-wide, Vexed were identified specifically with Capitol Hill. This neighborhood specificity is significant in the engine -- Capitol Hill has appeared throughout the S36 catalog as a source of Seattle's most adventurous music across multiple genres.
- **The four-year run.** 1982-1986 is a tight window. Four years of active operation, then dissolution. In the hardcore tradition, short runs are common -- the music burns at high intensity and bands often dissolve after a few years rather than compromise or continue past the point of genuine creative investment. Vexed's four-year run produced a body of work that is a significant local document.

## THE HARDCORE MOMENT: 1982-1986

### Peak Seattle Hardcore

- **The national context.** By 1982, American hardcore had established its infrastructure: Minor Threat (DC) had released *Out of Step* (1983), Black Flag was on *Damaged* (1981) and touring constantly, the Dead Kennedys were at their creative peak. The national hardcore scene had solidified around a set of values: speed, aggression, DIY, political consciousness, and short songs that made their point and got out.
- **Seattle's contribution.** Seattle hardcore in 1982-1986 was not nationally prominent in the way that DC, LA, or Bay Area hardcore was -- but it was a real and serious local scene. Bands like Vexed, The Fartz, The Refuzors, and others were creating original work in response to their specific local conditions.
- **The independent infrastructure.** By 1982, the national hardcore underground had developed a functional DIY infrastructure: indie labels pressing records, fanzines documenting the scene, touring circuits connecting cities. Vexed operated within this infrastructure, releasing independent records that circulated through the hardcore network.
- **Reagan-era politics.** Like all early 1980s hardcore, Vexed's music was formed in explicit response to the Reagan administration's policies. Nuclear anxiety, economic inequality, militarism, and the sense of a fraudulent American promise are the political content of the era's hardcore punk. Vexed were a Capitol Hill band with Capitol Hill's political character: more explicitly leftist, more urban, more queer-adjacent than the suburban punk scenes of other cities.
- **The end: 1986.** The dissolution of Vexed in 1986 coincides with a broader shift in Seattle's music scene. The mid-1980s saw hardcore's national peak beginning to pass; alternative rock (what would become grunge) was beginning to emerge as the next dominant local genre. Some members of early hardcore bands moved into the emerging alternative scene. Others simply stopped.

(Sources: National hardcore 1982-1986; Minor Threat; Black Flag; Dead Kennedys; Seattle hardcore infrastructure; Reagan-era politics in hardcore; 1986 scene transition)

## THE SOUND -- HARDCORE PUNK, E=8

### Speed, Economy, Impact

- **E=8 in the Capitol Hill register.** Capitol Hill hardcore at E=8 has a particular quality: urban, dense, community-embedded. The music reflects the neighborhood's political consciousness -- more explicitly leftist and queer-aware than suburban hardcore, more engaged with the specific conditions of living in the city.
- **Song economy.** Hardcore songs in the 1982-1986 era tend toward brevity -- 60 to 90 seconds, sometimes less. The songs make their point at maximum velocity and stop. This is not limitation; it is a compositional choice rooted in punk's anti-indulgence ethic. Vexed operated in this tradition.
- **The rhythm section.** In early 1980s hardcore, the rhythm section (drums and bass) drives the tempo and provides the physical momentum of the music. The guitarist provides the power chord aggression above it; the vocalist delivers the text. Vexed's sound is built on this foundation.
- **Recording quality.** Independent hardcore recordings of this era have a characteristic sonic quality: rough, direct, often recorded quickly in cheap studios or practice spaces. The production values are minimal by design -- the energy of the performance is the point, not studio polish. These recordings are documents, not productions.
