Capitol Hill Hardcore

Vexed formed in Seattle in 1982, on Capitol Hill — the neighborhood that has appeared throughout the S36 engine as a source of Seattle’s most adventurous music. In the early 1980s, Capitol Hill was a dense, politically engaged, community-embedded punk scene. Low rents, a network of venues and practice spaces, and a neighborhood culture that supported alternative expression produced a series of hardcore bands including Vexed.

The name signals the emotional content directly: perpetually vexed, agitated, troubled by the conditions of the early Reagan era. Hardcore names in this period often operated this way. Vexed were not vexed about abstract things — they were vexed about nuclear anxiety, economic inequality, and the fraudulent American promise that the 1980s political moment was selling.

"The scene is its bands, not just its stars. Without bands like Vexed, the scene that produced the stars would not have existed."

The Hardcore Moment: 1982–1986

By 1982, American hardcore had established its infrastructure: Minor Threat in DC, Black Flag in LA, Dead Kennedys in the Bay Area. The national scene had consolidated around speed, aggression, DIY ethics, and political consciousness. Seattle’s contribution was not nationally prominent but was genuinely serious. Vexed, The Fartz (270), The Refuzors (273), and others were creating original work in response to specific local conditions.

Capitol Hill in 1982 was more explicitly leftist and queer-adjacent than suburban hardcore scenes. The neighborhood’s political character shaped the music: urban, dense, engaged with the specific conditions of city life. Vexed’s four-year run ended in 1986, coinciding with the mid-1980s transition from hardcore to the emerging alternative rock scene that would become grunge.
