Lunar Orbiter 1 launched August 10, 1966, and entered lunar orbit on August 14 — the first American spacecraft to orbit the Moon. Its mission was cartographic: photograph potential Apollo landing sites with enough resolution to determine whether a Lunar Module could land safely. The spacecraft carried two cameras — a wide-angle for context and a telephoto for detail — and returned 42 high-resolution and 187 medium-resolution photographs over 77 orbits. On August 23, Lunar Orbiter 1 captured the first photograph of Earth from the vicinity of the Moon — the pale blue disk hanging above the lunar horizon. The mission was not about the Moon. It was about mapping the terrain so that someone else could land on it.

Pedro the Lion is the cartographic mission of the engine's folk sector. David Bazan met Damien Jurado (degree 45) at Shorewood High School in Shoreline, Washington — they recognized each other in a hallway because they were both carrying the same Crucified cassette tape. Bazan drummed for Jurado's early bands (the Guilty, Linus, Coolidge). Bazan produced Jurado's *I Break Chairs* (2002). Casey Foubert, Pedro the Lion's multi-instrumentalist, produced four Rocky Votolato (degree 46) albums across the years that defined Votolato's commercial peak. Three consecutive folk-adjacent degrees — 45, 46, 47 — all orbiting Bazan. Pedro the Lion did not land on the Moon. Pedro the Lion mapped the terrain so that Jurado and Votolato could find their landing sites.

Lunar Orbiter 1's two-camera system — wide-angle for context, telephoto for detail — mirrors Bazan's songwriting method. The wide-angle: Pedro the Lion's concept albums (*Control*, *Winners Never Quit*, *Achilles Heel*) survey entire moral landscapes — capitalism, marriage, faith, violence — in narrative arcs that span twelve songs. The telephoto: Bazan's autobiographical album cycle (*Phoenix*, *Havasu*, *Santa Cruz*) zooms into specific cities, specific years, specific memories with forensic precision. The Greenwood coffee shop where he worked during Pedro the Lion's early years. The evangelical upbringing he eventually left. Lunar Orbiter 1's photographs determined where Apollo would land. The orbiter maps. The lander follows. Degree 47 maps the territory that degrees 45 and 46 already inhabit.
