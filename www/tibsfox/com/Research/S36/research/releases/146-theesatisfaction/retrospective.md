*Degree 146 retrospective complete. THEESATISFACTION at the one hundred forty-sixth degree -- GENRE DISSOLUTION. Stasia Irons and Catherine Harris-White: rapper and singer, producer and visual artist, two Black women creating music that refuses to be categorized. Not hip-hop. Not R&B. Not electronic. Not neo-soul. All of these and none of these. On Sub Pop -- the grunge label reborn as something wider, joining Shabazz Palaces in a Central District mini-ecosystem of experimental Black music. awE naturalE dissolved genre boundaries. EarthEE made the dissolution complete. The Afrofuturist vision: cosmic, communal, joyful, Black, femme, unapologetic. THEESatisfaction existed during the Central District's most intense gentrification -- the neighborhood that made this art was being erased even as the art was being created. The music carries the weight of that disappearing place. Thirteen degrees into the hip-hop era, the engine has documented DJs, producers, collectives, organizations, cities, genres, and now genre dissolution itself. The engine is not just cataloging music -- it is tracking the evolution of what music can be.*
