## HELMS ALEE -- NOISE/EXPERIMENTAL -- CAPITOL HILL

### The Band

Helms Alee is a Seattle noise rock trio formed in 2007, active from 2008 to the present. The band consists of:

- **Ben Verellen** -- guitar, vocals. Also the founder and builder of **Verellen Amplifiers**, a boutique amplifier company based in Seattle. The amplifiers he builds are the amplifiers the band plays through. The instrument-maker and the instrument-user are the same person. This is not a trivial detail -- it means Helms Alee's guitar tone is literally designed from the circuit board up by the person playing it.
- **Dana James** -- bass, vocals. James provides both low-end heaviness and ethereal vocal harmonics. The bass work is central to the band's crushing sound -- thick, distorted, and relentless.
- **Hozoji Matheson-Margullis** -- drums, vocals. Matheson-Margullis is one of the most powerful drummers in Seattle's heavy scene, driving the band's rhythmic intensity with precision and force. Also contributes vocals, making Helms Alee a **three-voice band**.

All three members sing. This is the defining characteristic of Helms Alee's sound: **three voices** -- sometimes screaming, sometimes harmonizing in angelic unison, sometimes layering guttural growls under pristine melody. The vocal approach is unprecedented in heavy music. Most noise rock bands have one vocalist. Helms Alee has three, and they use all possible combinations: unison harmony, call-and-response, simultaneous screaming, whispered passages over crushing riffs.

### The Sound

Helms Alee's sound is built on a fundamental contradiction: **maximum heaviness with maximum beauty**. The riffs are among the heaviest in Seattle's music scene -- low-tuned, massively amplified (through Verellen amps), distorted, and physically overwhelming. But layered over and woven through these crushing riffs are vocal harmonies of startling beauty -- three-part harmonies that recall Appalachian folk music or church choir singing, delivered in the middle of a sonic avalanche.

The genre classification is **Noise/Experimental** because no single existing genre captures what Helms Alee does:
- **Noise rock** captures the heaviness and distortion but misses the vocal beauty.
- **Sludge metal** captures the tempo and weight but misses the harmonic sophistication.
- **Post-rock** captures the dynamic range but misses the aggression.
- **Experimental** captures the genre-defying quality and the willingness to combine elements that conventional wisdom says don't belong together.

The result is music that is simultaneously crushing and gorgeous, abrasive and tender, maximally heavy and maximally melodic. **E=6** because the paradox itself is overwhelming -- the contrast between beauty and destruction generates more energy than either alone.

### The Verellen Amplifiers Connection

Ben Verellen designs and hand-builds amplifiers under the **Verellen Amplifiers** brand. These are high-powered, boutique tube amplifiers designed for extreme volume and tonal density. The amps are used by heavy bands across the country, but their first and most important user is Helms Alee itself.

This creates a unique feedback loop in the history of amplified music: the amplifier designer is the guitarist. The tone is not found -- it is **engineered from the ground up**. Verellen knows what he wants to hear because he plays it every night. He knows what the amp needs to do because he's the one pushing it to its limits. The circuit design informs the composition, and the composition demands new circuit designs.

The Verellen connection places Helms Alee in a lineage that includes Leo Fender (who couldn't play guitar but built the instruments), Jim Marshall (who built amps for the Who and Hendrix), and Randall Smith (Mesa/Boogie, designed amps for Carlos Santana). But Verellen is unique: he is both builder and player. The amp and the music are **one system**.

### Discography

- **Weatherhead** (2008) -- debut album. Established the template: crushing riffs, ethereal harmonics, dynamic shifts. Released on Hydra Head Records.
- **Boats** (2009) -- EP. Further refinement of the heavy/beautiful dynamic.
- **Night Terror** (2011) -- second full-length. Heavier, more confident, the three-vocal approach fully realized. Hydra Head Records.
- **Sleepwalking Sailors** (2014) -- Sargent House. The breakthrough. Wider recognition, the sound fully matured. The album where the beauty/destruction paradox reaches its peak.
- **Stillicide** (2016) -- Sargent House. Continued evolution. More experimental, more dynamic range, the quiet passages quieter and the loud passages louder.
- **Noctiluca** (2019) -- Sargent House. Named after bioluminescent dinoflagellates. The album is the band's most atmospheric and expansive, with longer compositions and more textural exploration while maintaining crushing heaviness.
- **Tremolos** (2022) -- Sargent House. Most recent full-length. The title references the guitar technique (rapid repetition of a single note), and the album explores tremolo as both a musical technique and a metaphor for sustained vibration.

### Sargent House

Helms Alee has been on **Sargent House** since 2014. Sargent House is a Los Angeles-based independent label run by Cathy Halstead that specializes in heavy, experimental, and genre-defying music. The label's roster includes Deafheaven, Russian Circles, Emma Ruth Rundle, and Marriages -- all acts that, like Helms Alee, combine heaviness with beauty or experimentalism. Sargent House is the **right home** for Helms Alee because the label's entire identity is built around the same paradox the band embodies: heavy music that is also beautiful.

## The Heavy Music Ecosystem of Capitol Hill

Helms Alee exists within Capitol Hill's deep tradition of heavy music. The neighborhood has been home to:
- **The Melvins** -- Montesano/Capitol Hill. The godfathers of sludge metal. Helms Alee inherits the Melvins' willingness to be heavy and weird simultaneously.
- **Kinski** (degree 110) -- Capitol Hill. Instrumental post-rock/noise. Helms Alee shares Kinski's commitment to volume and dynamics but adds the three-voice vocal dimension.
- **Hovercraft** (degree 91) -- Noise. Beth Liebling and Eddie Vedder. Instrumental drone. Helms Alee is more structured and more vocal than Hovercraft but shares the commitment to overwhelming volume.
- **Big Business** -- Capitol Hill. Sludge/noise duo. Jared Warren and Coady Willis (who also played in the Melvins). Helms Alee shares the sludge lineage and the Capitol Hill address.
- **Sunn O)))** -- Stephen O'Malley is from Seattle. Drone/doom. Helms Alee shares the commitment to extreme low-end but is rhythmically active where Sunn O))) is static.

Helms Alee is the **synthesis** of Capitol Hill's heavy tradition: the Melvins' sludge, Hovercraft's volume, Kinski's dynamics, Big Business's rhythmic intensity, and a vocal approach that belongs to no one but Helms Alee. The three-part harmonics over crushing noise is the band's unique contribution to Seattle's heavy music lineage.

## Acoustic Profile: The Crushing Harmonic

### The Sound

The **crushing harmonic** is the acoustic signature of Helms Alee: the paradoxical combination of maximum heaviness (crushing riffs, distorted bass, pounding drums) with maximum beauty (three-part vocal harmonics, melodic passages, dynamic stillness). The crushing harmonic is not a compromise between heavy and beautiful -- it is **both at full intensity simultaneously**. The beauty does not soften the heaviness; the heaviness does not diminish the beauty. Both exist at maximum, and the contrast generates E=6.

### Key Sonic Characteristics

- **Guitar tone:** Verellen-designed. Thick, saturated, low-tuned. The distortion is not fuzzy or buzzy but **dense and physical** -- you feel it in your chest before you hear it in your ears. The Verellen amp circuit is designed for this specific quality: maximum density without loss of clarity.
- **Bass tone:** Distorted, thick, occupying the low-frequency spectrum with authority. Dana James's bass is not just a rhythm instrument -- it is a **second wall of distortion** that doubles the band's tonal weight.
- **Drums:** Powerful, precise, dynamic. Hozoji Matheson-Margullis drives the band with force and variety -- pounding sludge grooves, explosive fills, and moments of complete silence that make the return of sound hit harder.
- **Vocals:** Three voices. The defining element. Sometimes all three in angelic harmony over the heaviest possible riffs. Sometimes screaming in unison. Sometimes one voice clean and two distorted. The vocal combinations are the band's compositional palette.
- **Dynamics:** Extreme. Helms Alee uses dynamics as a weapon. The quietest passages are genuinely quiet -- whispered vocals, clean guitar, restrained drums. The loudest passages are genuinely overwhelming. The distance between the quietest and loudest moments is the **maximum dynamic range** in Seattle heavy music.

### Production Approach

Helms Alee's recordings capture the live energy of the trio while expanding the spatial and textural possibilities. The production tends toward **clarity within density** -- you can hear all three instruments and all three voices even when the band is at maximum volume. This clarity-within-density is partly a function of the Verellen amp design (optimized for dense-but-clear distortion) and partly a function of careful mixing that gives each element its own frequency space.
