E=6 — Fourth Maximum in Five Degrees (107-111)

E=6 sustained corridor. Helms Alee is the fourth E=6 in five degrees (108, 109, 110, 111). The engine has entered unprecedented maximum-energy density. Prior to degree 108, there was only one E=6 in 107 degrees (Hendrix at 36). Now there are four in five degrees. Each E=6 is distinct: punk/electronic combustion (108), doom-sludge ferocity (109), instrumental ascent (110), and now the crushing harmonic (111) — beauty colliding with destruction at maximum intensity.

Three Voices — Beauty in the Noise

All three members of Helms Alee sing. Ben Verellen, Dana James, Hozoji Matheson-Margullis — three voices that harmonize in angelic unison over the heaviest possible riffs. The vocal approach is unprecedented in heavy music. Most noise rock bands have one screamer. Helms Alee has three harmonizers. The contrast between the crushing instrumental weight and the ethereal vocal beauty is the defining paradox — and the source of E=6 energy. Neither the beauty nor the heaviness compromises. Both exist at maximum.

Verellen Amplifiers — Builder and Player, One Person

Ben Verellen designs and hand-builds boutique tube amplifiers under the Verellen Amplifiers brand. The amplifiers Helms Alee plays through are the amplifiers Verellen builds. The instrument-maker and the instrument-user are the same person. The tone is not found — it is engineered from the circuit board up. In the lineage of Leo Fender, Jim Marshall, and Randall Smith — but Verellen is unique: he is both builder and player. The amp and the music are one system.

The Crushing Harmonic

Helms Alee’s acoustic signature is the crushing harmonic: the paradoxical combination of maximum heaviness (crushing riffs, distorted bass, pounding drums through Verellen amplifiers) with maximum beauty (three-part vocal harmonics, melodic passages, dynamic stillness). The crushing harmonic is not a compromise — it is both at full intensity simultaneously.

The guitar tone is thick, saturated, low-tuned — dense and physical through the Verellen circuit. Dana James’s bass adds a second wall of distortion. Hozoji Matheson-Margullis drives with force and precision. Over this sonic weight, three voices harmonize with startling beauty — recalling Appalachian folk singing delivered in the middle of a sonic avalanche.

The dynamics are extreme. The quietest passages are genuinely quiet — whispered vocals, clean guitar, restrained drums. The loudest are genuinely overwhelming. The distance between the two is the maximum dynamic range in Seattle heavy music.

Discography

Weatherhead (2008, Hydra Head) — debut. Established the template. Night Terror (2011, Hydra Head) — heavier, more confident, three-vocal approach fully realized. Sleepwalking Sailors (2014, Sargent House) — the breakthrough. Beauty/destruction paradox at peak. Stillicide (2016, Sargent House) — more experimental, wider dynamic range. Noctiluca (2019, Sargent House) — most atmospheric and expansive. Named after bioluminescent dinoflagellates. Tremolos (2022, Sargent House) — sustained vibration as technique and metaphor.

Capitol Hill Heavy Music Lineage

Helms Alee exists within Capitol Hill’s deep tradition of heavy music: the Melvins (sludge), Kinski (degree 110, instrumental post-rock/noise), Hovercraft (degree 91, drone/noise), Big Business (sludge/noise). Helms Alee is the synthesis — the Melvins’ sludge weight, Hovercraft’s volume, Kinski’s dynamics, plus a three-voice vocal dimension that belongs to no one but Helms Alee. Second consecutive Capitol Hill act at E=6 (110-111).
