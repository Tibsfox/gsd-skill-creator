## Windham Hill Records: The New Age Label

### First Appearance

Degree 65 introduces Windham Hill Records -- the quintessential New Age label.

- **Situation:** Windham Hill founded by William Ackerman in Palo Alto, CA, 1976. Defined the New Age sound. Roster: George Winston (piano, *December* sold 3+ million), Michael Hedges (guitar), Liz Story (piano), Alex de Grassi (guitar), Philip Aaberg (piano). Clean acoustic recording, contemplative atmosphere, no vocals, no drums.
- **Root Cause:** Windham Hill represents a different model of independent label than Barsuk (degrees 57-64). Barsuk curates across genres (folk to indie rock). Windham Hill curates within a single aesthetic (acoustic contemplation). Barsuk signs bands. Windham Hill signs soloists. Both are independent labels with specific curatorial identities, but the curatorial models diverge.
- **Recommendation:** Document Windham Hill entry. Note the curatorial model contrast with Barsuk. Note the label's commercial success (Winston's *December* at 3+ million copies). Track label at degree 66.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Windham Hill enters. First New Age label. First appearance. Curatorial model contrast with Barsuk. Track at future degrees.
