## What Worked

1. **Ten's track-level analysis is the correct depth for the engine's first Pearl Jam entry.** Jeremy, Alive, Black, Even Flow -- each track has a distinct structural role in the album. Alive as three-act narrative (mortality-survival-perseverance). Jeremy as school shooting portrait. The album is not a collection of songs; it is a document of a particular emotional reckoning with mortality, violence, and persistence. This architecture makes Ten the correct starting point.
2. **The E=8 studio / E=9 live distinction is the most precise calibration note in the post-Summit sequence.** Pearl Jam live 1992-1996 is documented at levels that would calibrate E=9 in a live-performance entry. The studio record is E=8. The studio-to-live gap is a real acoustic and energetic distinction that the engine documents correctly. No other grunge act in the engine has a larger studio-to-live energy gap.
3. **The Mother Love Bone formation story is complete at degree 314.** Gossard and Ament (degree 296, Mother Love Bone -> degree 225, Temple of the Dog -> degree 314, Pearl Jam) form the structural continuity thread from 1985 through the present. The formation story closes the Mother Love Bone thread that opened at degree 296.
4. **The Ticketmaster conflict is documented with appropriate precision.** 1994 DOJ complaint, attempted Ticketmaster-free touring, practical failure, principled foundation for later artist advocacy. The engine does not inflate the outcome (the fight was mostly lost) but records the principle as historically significant.

## Discoveries

1. **Mike McCready born April 5 1966: the April 5 thread has a third entry.** Cobain died April 5 1994; Staley died April 5 2002; McCready was born April 5 1966. The date connects the engine's most significant loss pair (Cobain and Staley) with the birthday of a member of the most commercially successful surviving band of the generation. The connection is biographical coincidence, but the engine documents coincidences as data points.
2. **Pearl Jam's 11-album output (1991-2024) is the most sustained major creative output of any grunge-generation band.** Soundgarden: 6 studio albums. Nirvana: 3. Alice in Chains: 7 (including DuVall era). Pearl Jam: 11, with no decade without a studio release. The longevity distinguishes them from every Seattle peer. The engine records this as fact, not as comparative judgment.
3. **The Matt Cameron transfer from Soundgarden (degree 312) to Pearl Jam (from 1998) is the engine's most significant late-career personnel event in the grunge zone.** Cameron played on Superunknown (1994, degree 312) and has played on Pearl Jam (2006) through Dark Matter (2024). His drumming style connects both bands through specific rhythmic vocabulary. The cross-band calibration point.
