## Biography & The Builder-Stayer

Thomas Marriott was born in Seattle, Washington in 1979. He is the sixteenth artist in the S36 engine, the sixteenth consecutive jazz artist, and the second trumpeter in the series (after Floyd Standifer at degree 4). He is the first artist born in the late 1970s -- placing him squarely in Generation X, four years older than Aaron Parks (degree 14, born 1983) and a full generation younger than Marc Seales (degree 13). Where Parks was exported through the institutional pipeline at age 16, Marriott studied at the University of Washington under Seales and made a deliberate choice that defines his structural position in the engine: he stayed.

Marriott studied jazz at the University of Washington, where Marc Seales (degree 13) directs the jazz studies program. The Seales-Marriott connection is the third UW pipeline product documented in the engine (after the Seales-Parks pre-college connection at degree 14). But where Parks used the UW pipeline as a launching pad to Manhattan School of Music and never returned, Marriott completed his studies in Seattle, built his career in Seattle, and remains in Seattle. The UW pipeline produces both exports and anchors -- Marriott is the anchor product.

After graduating, Marriott became one of Seattle's most active jazz trumpeters. His career model is the working musician: leading his own groups, recording regularly, performing at Seattle venues (Jazz Alley, Tula's, the Royal Room, the Earshot Jazz Festival), and maintaining a consistent presence in the Pacific Northwest jazz community. This is not the prodigy-export career arc (Parks, degree 14) or the national touring model (Jones, degree 0). This is the builder-stayer: a musician whose primary investment is in the local infrastructure.

Marriott has recorded extensively for Origin Records -- Seattle's premier independent jazz label, founded by John Bishop in 1997. Origin Records is the institutional infrastructure that connects the modern Seattle jazz scene. Where Blue Note (Parks, degree 14) is a national institution and ECM (Frisell 1, Priester 7, Peacock 8, Parks 14) is a European aesthetic, Origin Records is explicitly local: a Seattle-based label documenting the Seattle jazz community. Marriott is among Origin's most prolific recording artists, and the Marriott-Origin relationship represents the model of artist-label partnership that sustains a local scene.

His discography on Origin Records includes *Flexicon* (2006), *Human* (2008), *Dialogue* (2010), *Urban Folklore* (2013), and additional recordings spanning over a decade of consistent output. The albums document Marriott's evolution within the hard bop and post-bop vocabulary -- not a radical stylist pushing genre boundaries (Parks, degree 14), but a deep practitioner refining a tradition. *Flexicon* established Marriott's hard bop vocabulary: crisp articulation, melodic invention within standard harmonic frameworks, and the rhythmic drive of a working band. *Human* extended the palette toward more lyrical, open playing. *Dialogue* emphasized interactive group dynamics -- the conversational model of post-bop ensemble playing where every musician shapes the direction in real time. *Urban Folklore* connected the jazz vocabulary to urban experience -- the lived reality of a musician embedded in a specific city.

Marriott has performed with national figures including Maynard Ferguson, Benny Golson, and other established jazz artists, demonstrating that his chops are competitive at the national level. He has chosen not to relocate to New York or Los Angeles. This is the critical structural distinction: Marriott has the ability to compete nationally but invests his energy locally. Where Parks (degree 14) was exported and never returned, Marriott could have exported himself and chose not to. Where Berry (degree 5) stayed because he was embedded in a pre-institutional era, Marriott stays as a conscious strategic choice in the post-institutional era.

## First Energy-3 Artist: The Engine Warms Up

### The Intensity Threshold

Degree 15 is the first energy-3 artist in the Seattle 360 engine. Degrees 0 through 14 operated in the energy-1 to energy-2 range: two energy-1 artists (Peacock 8, Lanphere 12) and thirteen energy-2 artists (all others). The energy spectrum has been heavily weighted toward energy-2 -- the community-embedded, atmospheric, sustaining energy profile that characterizes Seattle's jazz tradition. Energy-3 has been absent.

Marriott breaks the threshold. His energy is not the meditative stillness of Peacock (energy-1) or the ambient warmth of Parks (energy-2). The trumpet is an inherently projective instrument -- it leads the ensemble, states the melody, and commands the room. Marriott's playing deploys this projection with the intensity characteristic of the Clifford Brown lineage: speed, precision, confidence, and the physical power of brass.

The energy-3 arrival at degree 15 is structurally significant because it signals the engine's increasing intensity as it moves through the jazz quadrant. The first 15 degrees have been dominated by energy-2 (the Seattle signature), but as the circle rotates toward soul, R&B, and eventually rock, the energy level must rise. Marriott at energy-3 is the first step in that warming. The engine is not jumping from 2 to 5 -- it is escalating gradually, and degree 15 is the threshold crossing.

## Music Theory: Hard Bop Trumpet Vocabulary and the Origin Records Aesthetic

### The Theoretical Framework

Marriott's trumpet vocabulary is rooted in the hard bop tradition as transmitted through the Clifford Brown lineage. This is the most direct inheritance of the bebop revolution documented at degree 12 (Lanphere), filtered through the hard bop transformation of the late 1950s and refined into a contemporary post-bop vocabulary. The core theoretical elements:

1. **Clifford Brown lineage (bebop phrases at brass tempo).** Brown revolutionized jazz trumpet by demonstrating that bebop's chromatic vocabulary -- previously associated with saxophone (Parker, Lanphere degree 12) -- could be executed on trumpet at comparable speed and with greater harmonic clarity. Marriott inherits this lineage directly: rapid chromatic runs, arpeggiated chord tones at tempo, and the physical endurance to sustain complex phrases across multiple choruses. Fifteenth deployment of the chromaticism thread: hard bop trumpet chromaticism -- the Brown lineage's fast, precise chromatic approach adapted to contemporary post-bop harmonic contexts.

2. **Post-bop harmonic language (extended harmony, altered dominants).** Where Standifer (degree 4) operated primarily within swing and bebop harmonic frameworks (diatonic scales, basic chord extensions), Marriott deploys the post-bop harmonic palette: altered dominant chords (b9, #9, #11, b13), tritone substitutions deployed as standard vocabulary (not innovation -- inherited from the modal revolution, degrees 13-14), upper-structure triads over dominant chords, and pentatonic superimposition over complex changes. Sixteenth deployment of the tritone substitution thread: deployed as inherited vocabulary in the hard bop trumpet context -- not conscious innovation (bebop, degree 12), not modal integration (Seales, degree 13), not unconscious inheritance (Parks, degree 14), but professional standard equipment.

3. **The Origin Records sound (clean, modern, Seattle-specific production aesthetic).** Origin Records under John Bishop has developed a recognizable production aesthetic: clean recordings, natural acoustic space, minimal post-production processing, and a preference for the sound of musicians in a room together. This is not the ECM aesthetic (spacious, reverberant, contemplative -- Frisell 1, Peacock 8, Parks 14) nor the Blue Note classic sound (Van Gelder's room, warm compression, Rudy Van Gelder studio). The Origin sound is a third aesthetic position: **documentary realism** -- the recording captures the ensemble as it sounds in the room, without imposing an external sonic signature. The label serves the scene rather than defining it.

4. **Hard bop rhythmic vocabulary (swing-to-even-eighths continuum).** Marriott's rhythmic profile operates on the continuum between swing eighths (the traditional jazz feel, triplet-based) and even eighths (the post-bop feel, aligned with rock and funk subdivision). Where Standifer (degree 4) played in pure swing feel and Parks (degree 14) deployed floating metric modulation, Marriott occupies the middle territory: predominantly swing-based but flexible enough to straighten the eighth notes when the tune calls for it. This rhythmic adaptability is characteristic of the contemporary hard bop player who must navigate standards (swing), originals (variable), and contemporary compositions (even eighths).

5. **Fifteenth deployment of the blue note thread.** The blue note (b3, b7) in hard bop trumpet is a declarative gesture. Where Parks (degree 14) floated the blue note between jazz and rock semantics and Seales (degree 13) absorbed it into the mode, Marriott deploys the blue note in its most assertive trumpet form: the half-valve bend, the crushed grace note, the scooped approach from below. This is brass-specific blue note technique -- the physical manipulation of the instrument (lip bends, half-valve effects, alternate fingerings) produces microtonal shadings unavailable on piano or guitar. The second brass-specific blue note deployment after Standifer (degree 4), but at higher energy: Standifer's blue note was warm and educational; Marriott's is assertive and driving.

## The Builder-Stayer: Investing in Local Infrastructure

### A Sixteenth Artist-City Pattern

Degree 15 adds the sixteenth named pattern to the artist-city taxonomy: **The Builder-Stayer** -- a musician with national-level ability who consciously chooses to remain in the origin city and invest in local infrastructure rather than export to a career center.

The Builder-Stayer is structurally distinct from all prior stay patterns:

- **The Persistence (Berry, degree 5):** stayed because he was embedded in a pre-institutional era where the local scene was self-sustaining. No institutional alternative existed.
- **The Teacher (Standifer, degree 4):** stayed and taught, but the teaching was experiential (bandstand mentorship) rather than institutional.
- **The Institutional Anchor (Seales, degree 13):** stays and teaches within the university system. The stay is institution-mediated.
- **The Builder-Stayer (Marriott, degree 15):** stays and builds infrastructure -- recording for a local label, performing at local venues, participating in the community -- without primary attachment to a teaching institution. The stay is scene-mediated, not institution-mediated.

The Builder-Stayer requires four conditions:
1. National-level ability (demonstrated through performances with nationally recognized artists)
2. Conscious choice to remain in origin city (not inability to leave -- ability plus choice)
3. Active investment in local infrastructure (recording, performing, building relationships with local institutions like Origin Records)
4. Career based locally -- not using the city as a satellite while maintaining a NYC or LA career

Marriott meets all four. He has played with Maynard Ferguson and Benny Golson -- establishing his national credentials. He has chosen Seattle. He records for Origin Records. His career is based in Seattle.

The Builder-Stayer is the generational complement to The Persistence. Berry stayed because there was nowhere else to go -- the scene was the world. Marriott stays because there is somewhere else to go (NYC, LA) and he chooses not to. The Persistence is passive retention. The Builder-Stayer is active investment. Both produce the same outcome -- the musician stays -- but the mechanism and the agency are different.

The engine has now documented a three-degree sequence (13, 14, 15) that maps the full spectrum of the stayed-vs-left dynamic:

Three consecutive degrees, all UW-connected, all different. The stayed-vs-left question is the central tension of Seattle jazz: the city produces talent, but can it keep it? Seales stays through the institution. Parks leaves through the institution. Marriott stays through the scene. Two stay, one leaves -- but the two stays are structurally different (institutional vs. scene-mediated), and all three are products of the same UW pipeline.

The energy-3 profile is **assertive projection** -- the energy of a hard bop trumpeter who leads from the front, drives the ensemble rhythmically and harmonically, and commands the room through physical sound production.

Energy-3 is structurally different from both energy-1 and energy-2:

Jazz at energy-2 is the Seattle baseline -- community-embedded, atmospheric. Soul and R&B at energy-3 and energy-4 will represent the amplification of that baseline. ## Career Arc

### Born in Seattle, 1979

Born in Seattle -- native-born, Generation X, the first artist born in the late 1970s. Seventeenth educational pipeline: UW jazz studies under Seales, completed locally.

### University of Washington Jazz Studies (under Marc Seales)

Studied jazz at UW under Seales (degree 13). Third UW pipeline product documented in the engine. Unlike Parks (degree 14, pre-college then MSM), Marriott completed his jazz studies at UW and remained in Seattle.

### Origin Records Partnership

Became one of Origin Records' most prolific recording artists. Origin Records -- founded by John Bishop in 1997, Seattle-based, dedicated to documenting the Pacific Northwest jazz community. The Marriott-Origin relationship is the first artist-label partnership in the engine where both artist and label are Seattle-based and Seattle-committed.

### *Flexicon* (Origin Records, 2006)

Debut on Origin Records. Hard bop trumpet vocabulary: crisp articulation, melodic invention, rhythmic drive. Established Marriott's position as one of Seattle's leading young jazz trumpeters. First Origin Records appearance in the engine.

### *Human* (Origin Records, 2008)

Extended the palette toward more lyrical, open playing. Demonstrated range beyond pure hard bop vocabulary -- expressive ballad playing, dynamic control, the full emotional spectrum of the trumpet.

### *Dialogue* (Origin Records, 2010)

Emphasized interactive group dynamics -- the conversational model of post-bop ensemble playing. Title reflects the approach: jazz as dialogue between musicians, not a soloist with accompaniment.

### *Urban Folklore* (Origin Records, 2013)

Connected jazz vocabulary to urban experience -- the lived reality of a musician embedded in a specific city. The title itself suggests the Builder-Stayer ethos: urban lore, city stories, Seattle as subject matter.

### Performances with Maynard Ferguson and Benny Golson

Performed with nationally recognized jazz artists, establishing national-level credentials while maintaining Seattle as home base. Ferguson -- the high-note trumpet legend whose bands launched young players. Golson -- the hard bop composer-saxophonist (Killer Joe, I Remember Clifford) who represents the tradition Marriott inherits.

### Currently Based in Seattle

Remains based in Seattle. Career centered on the Pacific Northwest jazz scene. Performing at Seattle venues (Jazz Alley, Tula's, the Royal Room), Earshot Jazz Festival, and regional dates. The Builder-Stayer in continuous operation.
