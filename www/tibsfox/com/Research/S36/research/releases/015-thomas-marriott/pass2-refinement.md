### The Builder-Stayer and the Weather Eye: Continuous Local Observation

Pass 1 documented Thomas Marriott as the builder-stayer — a nationally competitive trumpeter who chose to invest his energy locally rather than export himself to New York. Pass 2 connects this to TIROS-1's mission: the first satellite to provide continuous weather observation from a fixed orbital perspective, and to moon jellyfish's transparent body as a lens for observing internal processes.

TIROS-1 launched on April 1, 1960, and took 22,952 cloud-cover photographs in 78 days. It did not travel to exotic destinations. It orbited Earth at 700 km altitude, looking down at the same planet from the same orbit, day after day, image after image. The value was not in any single photograph. It was in the continuous coverage — the accumulation of thousands of observations from a consistent vantage point that revealed weather patterns invisible to ground-based observation. The weather eye's power was persistence from a fixed position.

Marriott's career model is the same. He does not tour internationally. He does not record for a European label from a New York base. He orbits Seattle at a fixed distance, performing at the same venues (Jazz Alley, Tula's, the Royal Room, Earshot Jazz Festival), recording for the same local label (Origin Records), building the same local infrastructure, year after year. The value is not in any single performance or album. It is in the continuous coverage — a decade-plus of consistent musical output from a fixed local position that reveals the Seattle jazz community's structure and evolution in a way that a passing national touring artist cannot.

Aurelia aurita — the moon jellyfish — is nearly transparent. Its bell (the dome-shaped body) is composed of 95% water, with thin layers of tissue that are translucent enough to see internal structures: the four horseshoe-shaped gonads, the gastric pouches, the radial canals that distribute nutrients. The jellyfish is a lens — looking through it reveals what is inside. Marriott's Origin Records discography functions similarly: looking through the albums (*Flexicon*, *Human*, *Dialogue*, *Urban Folklore*) reveals the internal structure of Seattle's contemporary jazz community — the sidemen, the venues, the harmonic vocabulary, the aesthetic choices that define the scene. The discography is transparent to the community it documents.

### The UW Pipeline at Three: Anchor, Export, Builder

Pass 1 documented the Seales-Parks-Marriott UW triad. Pass 2 maps this as the engine's most fully documented institutional pipeline — three consecutive degrees, three structurally different outputs, one institution.

TIROS-1 was the first in a series — TIROS-2 through TIROS-10 followed, each building on the previous mission's engineering and expanding the weather observation capability. The TIROS program was iterative: each satellite improved on the last, and the accumulated series produced a comprehensive weather observation system that transformed meteorology. The UW jazz pipeline is similarly iterative: Seales (degree 13) built the program, Parks (degree 14) was its most visible product (exported nationally), and Marriott (degree 15) is its most locally invested product (trained locally, performing locally, documenting locally). Three iterations, each building on the previous, collectively producing a comprehensive jazz education-to-performance system.

The three UW products map to three TIROS functions:

| UW Product | TIROS Function | Role |
|-----------|---------------|------|
| Seales (13) — Anchor | The camera — the instrument that captures | Builds the observation capability |
| Parks (14) — Export | The transmitted image — the data sent to distant receivers | The output that reaches national/global audiences |
| Marriott (15) — Builder | The continuous orbit — the sustained vantage point | The local presence that accumulates observations over time |
