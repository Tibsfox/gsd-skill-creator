## Structural Event 2: Concert Infrastructure Foundation

- **Situation:** Concerts West (O'Day co-founder). Beatles at Seattle Center Coliseum (August 21, 1964, 14,300 fans). Spanish Castle circuit (Hendrix connection). Teen dance network throughout Puget Sound.
- **Root Cause:** O'Day proved that Seattle was a viable major concert market. Before Concerts West demonstrated this, national acts often skipped Seattle. O'Day's infrastructure -- proven venues, trained audiences, established production -- was inherited by every promoter who followed, including those who booked grunge-era shows.
- **Recommendation:** Document concert infrastructure as a foundational layer for Seattle's live music ecosystem. Track connections to concert venues that appear later in the engine.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Concert infrastructure foundation documented. Beatles, Spanish Castle, teen dances.

## Structural Event 3: KJR/KCMU Dialectic

- **Situation:** KJR AM (mainstream, commercial, Top 40, mass audience) vs. KCMU FM (independent, freeform, niche audience). The two stations represent opposite approaches to radio.
- **Root Cause:** The alternative could not exist without the mainstream to define itself against. O'Day's KJR is the thesis. KCMU is the antithesis. KEXP (the renamed, expanded KCMU) is the synthesis: independent ethos with professional infrastructure and global reach.
- **Recommendation:** Document the dialectic as a structural pattern in Seattle radio history. The thesis/antithesis/synthesis arc may complete if KEXP DJs appear in subsequent degrees.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- KJR/KCMU dialectic documented. Track KEXP synthesis at future degrees.
