The Band

Unwound formed in Olympia/Tumwater, Washington in 1991. Three members: Justin Trosper (guitar, vocals), Vern Rumsey (bass), Sara Lund (drums). The band is the sum of three specific contributions that create something none of the individuals could create alone.

Trosper’s compositional intelligence drives the music: angular chord voicings, dissonant harmonics, rhythmic complexity that refuses convention. Rumsey’s bass is active, independent, compositionally present — post-hardcore bass that does its own thing rather than following the guitar. And Sara Lund: one of the most important drummers in independent American music, technically demanding and physically committed, the element that makes the complexity cohere.

2002: A Completed Body of Work

Unwound dissolved in 2002. They have not reunited. The body of work is closed. This completeness changes how the catalog is received: no reunion tour to anticipate, no new release to reframe the past. The records exist in the past tense, fixed and complete.

“Leaves Turn Inside You” (2001) — the final album, released one year before the dissolution — sounds like an ending. In retrospect, it sounds like exactly that: a band completing something definitive and then stopping.

“Leaves Turn Inside You” (2001) — The Final Statement

A double album. The angular post-hardcore of the middle period gives way to something more spacious, more melancholy, more ruminative. The dissonance is still present but diffuse — environmental rather than confrontational. “September.” “Below the Salt.” Songs that are genuinely beautiful in a way the band’s earlier work rarely attempted. The album rewards close listening over time. It sounds like an ending because it is one.

The Sound — Post-Hardcore, E=8

The Dissonance

Sound

Chord voicings that don’t resolve. Melodic lines that cut against the harmonic background. The dissonance is the point: it creates a tension that is emotional as well as harmonic. The music never resolves into comfort.

Sara Lund

Drums

One of the most important drummers in independent American music. Rhythmically complex, physically committed, compositionally sophisticated. She makes the complexity cohere. Her contribution to Unwound’s sound is as important as Trosper’s compositional vision.
