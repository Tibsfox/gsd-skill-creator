## The Fifty-First Degree: The Serial Founder

### Grand Archives Extends the Record and Deepens the Genealogy

Degree 51 is structurally significant on three axes: it extends the folk-adjacent record to seven consecutive degrees (broken at degree 50 with six), it deepens the Carissa's Wierd genealogy with a second engine degree from the same band (joining Sera Cahoone at degree 48), and it introduces the serial founder pattern through Mat Brooke's career of building, running, and setting down a series of distinct projects across music and business.

- **Situation:** Grand Archives is a Seattle indie folk band formed in September 2006 by Mat Brooke, with Curtis Hall (drums, the Jeunes), Jeff Montano (bass, the New Mexicans), Ron Lewis (keyboards/guitar, Ghost Stories), and Thomas Wright (guitar). Five-piece with four-part vocal harmonies. Brooke previously co-founded Carissa's Wierd (1995-2003, with Jenn Champion, Ben Bridwell, and Sera Cahoone) and played guitar on Band of Horses' Everything All the Time (2006, Sub Pop) before leaving. Signed to Sub Pop. Debut The Grand Archives (February 2008): Pitchfork 7.5/10, Metacritic 72, AllMusic 3.5/5. NPR: "soft-rock redux." Second album Keep in Mind Frankenstein (September 2009) recorded at Paradise Sound Studios, Index, WA. Villains demos 2010-2011, third album never completed. Band inactive by mid-2011. Brooke opened The Redwood (Capitol Hill bar), The Oak, and The Spruce (Port Angeles restaurant).
- **Root Cause:** Degree 51's structural events converge because Grand Archives occupies the intersection of multiple engine threads: Carissa's Wierd genealogy (from degree 48), Sub Pop continuation (third artist after 45 and 48), folk-adjacent genre (seventh consecutive), return to Seattle (from Olympia at 49-50), and the serial-founder career pattern unique to Mat Brooke. Each event is independently sourced and verified.
- **Recommendation:** Document all structural events. Acknowledge degree 51 as the record-extending degree (seven consecutive folk-adjacent), the Carissa's Wierd genealogy deepening, and the third Sub Pop folk-zone artist.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Record extends. Genealogy deepens. Third Sub Pop.

## The Carissa's Wierd Genealogy Deepens

### Two Engine Degrees from One Band

Degrees 48 and 51 derive from the same band -- Carissa's Wierd.

- **Situation:** Carissa's Wierd was formed in Tucson, Arizona, in 1995 by Mat Brooke and Jenn Champion. The band relocated to Seattle and grew to include Ben Bridwell and Sera Cahoone. Three albums of slowcore, chamber-inflected indie rock (1999-2002). Disbanded 2003. Cahoone (degree 48) was the drummer; Brooke (degree 51) was the co-founder, guitarist, and vocalist. Both landed on Sub Pop as folk-adjacent solo/band artists. Three degrees apart.
- **Root Cause:** The Carissa's Wierd diaspora is a natural consequence of a band whose members had distinct creative identities. Cahoone went quieter (E=2, Folk). Brooke went brighter (E=3, Indie Folk). Bridwell went louder (Band of Horses, projected E=5). The slowcore band's dissolution produced three distinct trajectories along the energy spectrum.
- **Recommendation:** Document the Carissa's Wierd genealogy as the engine's deepest single-band diaspora. Track Ben Bridwell / Band of Horses as a future degree candidate.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- Two degrees from one band. Three apart. Both Sub Pop. Both folk-adjacent. Bridwell as future candidate.

## First Band with Recent Defined End Date

### The Five-Year Lifespan

Grand Archives (2006-2011) is the engine's first band with a recent defined end date.

- **Situation:** Formed September 2006. Debut February 2008. Second album September 2009. Villains demos 2010-2011. Then silence. Five-year lifespan. Never officially disbanded -- simply stopped. Brooke's career pattern is serial: Carissa's Wierd (1995-2003), Band of Horses (2004-2006 for Brooke), Grand Archives (2006-2011). Three bands in fifteen years, each ending when its purpose was served.
- **Root Cause:** Mat Brooke's creative mode is not persistence but iteration. He builds things, runs them, and moves on. The pattern continued into the restaurant industry. The five-year band lifespan is not failure but design -- the band served its purpose and concluded.
- **Recommendation:** Document the five-year lifespan and the serial-creation pattern. Note as a new temporal type in the engine.
