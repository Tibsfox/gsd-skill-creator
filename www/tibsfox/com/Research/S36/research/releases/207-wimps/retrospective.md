## What Worked

1. **E=6 streak breaking at 207 is the right structural note.** Four consecutive E=6 entries needed a reset. Wimps at E=5 provides dynamic range without dropping dramatically. The engine breathes.
2. **Kill Rock Stars return shifts the label axis meaningfully.** After Sub Pop/Hardly Art dominance (200-206), the Olympia lineage provides a different perspective. Different label, different tradition, different community.
3. **Lo-fi as commitment framing is accurate and respectful.** Wimps' production is a choice, not a limitation. The GBV/Beat Happening lineage gives historical context.
4. **Catchy hooks buried in distortion is the right distinguishing feature.** Lo-fi punk that sticks in your head. The pop sensibility beneath the noise. This separates Wimps from mere noise.
5. **Deterrence pairing with Porcupine is clever.** Both survive by being unappealing to the wrong audience. Lo-fi deters polish-seekers. Quills deter predators. The parallel works at the strategic level.
6. **Punk economy concept (fast, short, no bridges, no solos) connects to the broader engine narrative.** Maximum efficiency in songwriting mirrors the power trio economy (205) and duo economy (206).

## Pair Analysis: Wimps + Porcupine

- **Energy:** E=5 (S36) + E=3 (SPS) -- Total E=8. **MODERATE CONTRAST.** Wimps higher by 2.
- **Theme:** DEFENSE THROUGH DETERRENCE. Wimps' lo-fi aesthetic is a form of defense: the rough production deters listeners who need polish, filters for those who value authenticity. You hear the room, the imperfections, the cheap-amp tone -- and you either stay for the hooks or you leave. The Porcupine's 30,000 quills are the ultimate physical deterrent: barbed, painful, potentially fatal to predators who ignore the warning. The moaning and teeth-chattering is the acoustic warning before the quills engage. Both survive by making themselves unappealing to the wrong audience. The lo-fi filter and the quill defense are parallel strategies: don't try to be for everyone. Be for the right audience. Deter the rest.
