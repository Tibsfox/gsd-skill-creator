## KILL ROCK STARS RETURNS

### The Olympia Connection

Kill Rock Stars' return at degree 207 carries historical weight:

- **Kill Rock Stars history.** Founded in Olympia, Washington in 1991. One of the foundational Pacific Northwest indie labels alongside Sub Pop and K Records. Kill Rock Stars was the label of Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney, Elliott Smith, The Decemberists, and many others. The label's identity: fiercely independent, feminist-aligned, uncompromising on artistic vision.
- **Previous KRS entries.** Kill Rock Stars has appeared earlier in the engine. Its return at 207 after the Sub Pop/Hardly Art dominance of recent degrees (200-206) shifts the label axis. Kill Rock Stars represents a different lineage: Olympia riot grrrl rather than Seattle Sub Pop.
- **Wimps on KRS.** Wimps' lo-fi punk is a natural fit for Kill Rock Stars' aesthetic. The label has always favored artists who prioritize energy and authenticity over production polish. Wimps is exactly this: fast, catchy, deliberately rough.

## WIMPS -- THE SOUND

### Lo-Fi as Commitment

Wimps' lo-fi punk is intentional, not accidental:

- **Speed and brevity.** Songs are fast (140-180 BPM) and short (1:00-2:30 minutes). The punk economy: get in, deliver the hook, get out. No bridges, no extended outros, no guitar solos. Maximum efficiency.
- **Lo-fi production.** Deliberately rough recording quality. The lo-fi aesthetic is a choice -- a commitment to the idea that the energy matters more than the fidelity. The room is in the recording. The imperfections are features.
- **Catchy hooks.** Despite the lo-fi production, Wimps writes hooks. Short, memorable melodies buried in distortion. The pop sensibility underneath the punk delivery. This is the distinguishing feature: lo-fi punk that sticks in your head.
- **Vocal delivery.** Direct, conversational, sometimes deadpan. Not screaming, not crooning. Talking at volume. The vocal approach matches the lo-fi aesthetic: unpretentious, immediate, human-scaled.
- **Guitar tone.** Thin, buzzy distortion. Not the thick fuzz of Naked Giants (205) or the blues crunch of The Black Tones (206). Wimps' guitar tone is deliberately cheap-sounding -- small amps, basic pedals, room sound. The tone is part of the aesthetic.

## LO-FI PUNK IN CONTEXT

### The Tradition

Wimps connects to a specific lo-fi punk tradition:

- **Guided By Voices lineage.** Robert Pollard's Guided By Voices pioneered the lo-fi rock aesthetic: four-track recordings, song fragments, hooks buried in tape hiss. Wimps is not GBV, but the principle is shared: the recording quality is part of the art.
- **Beat Happening.** Calvin Johnson's Beat Happening (Olympia, K Records) was lo-fi before the term existed. Simple instruments, minimal production, maximum sincerity. The Olympia lo-fi tradition that Kill Rock Stars inherited.
- **Wimps as synthesis.** Wimps synthesizes the lo-fi tradition (GBV, Beat Happening) with Seattle's punk energy (Childbirth, Tacocat). The result is lo-fi punk that is specifically Pacific Northwest: influenced by both the Olympia minimalist tradition and the Seattle punk maximize.

(Sources: Lo-fi rock history; Guided By Voices; Beat Happening; Olympia-Seattle continuum)

## Acoustic Profile

### The Sound of Wimps

- **Tempo.** Fast. 140-180 BPM. Punk tempos. Relentless but not extreme.
- **Guitar.** Thin, buzzy distortion. Cheap-amp aesthetic. Basic power chords with melodic hooks. The tone is deliberately lo-fi.
- **Bass.** Simple, driving, following the guitar. Punchy but not prominent in the lo-fi mix.
- **Vocals.** Direct, conversational, occasionally deadpan. Not screamed. Not polished. Human-scaled.
- **Drums.** Fast, simple, driving. Snare-forward. No fills. Punk economy.
- **Production.** Lo-fi. Room sound. Tape quality by choice, not necessity. The imperfections are the aesthetic.
- **Song length.** Very short. 1:00-2:30 minutes. Maximum compression. Hooks that land fast.
