Capitol Hill — The Geographic Base

Capitol Hill is the neighborhood the engine has documented throughout as Seattle’s most consistently radical cultural geography. The dense, walkable, counter-cultural center of the city — clubs, record stores, houses, small venues. The Fartz operated here in 1980–1982, playing in the small club spaces that constituted the entire Seattle hardcore infrastructure at the time.

Because This Fuckin’ World Still Stinks (1982, Alternative Tentacles). The title announces the aesthetic completely: not ironic, not metaphorical, genuinely and directly confrontational with the world.

The Duff McKagan Genealogy

The Path: Capitol Hill → Los Angeles → Global
The Fartz, Seattle (1980–1984) → Los Angeles underground (1984–1985) → Guns N’ Roses (1985) → Appetite for Destruction (1987) → Arena rock / mainstream breakthrough → Return to Seattle (later career, writing, chronicling)

McKagan born February 5, 1964, Seattle. Active in local punk/hardcore scene from adolescence. His trajectory from Capitol Hill hardcore to arena rock is one of the most complete genealogies of underground-to-mainstream absorption in Pacific Northwest music. His book It’s So Easy (and other lies) (2011) documents the full arc with characteristic directness.

Alternative Tentacles — The National Infrastructure

Founded by Jello Biafra in 1979 in San Francisco. Home label for Dead Kennedys. In 1982, one of the two or three most important independent hardcore labels in America. Being on Alternative Tentacles meant:

National distribution through the same channels as Dead Kennedys records. Credibility within the underground hierarchy. Community connection to the national hardcore scene. For a Seattle band in 1982, this was the mechanism by which local founding became legible nationally.
