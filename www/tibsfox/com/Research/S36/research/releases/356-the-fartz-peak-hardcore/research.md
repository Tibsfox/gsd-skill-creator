## THE FARTZ -- THE BAND

### Capitol Hill, Seattle. 1980. The Founding Generation's Second Band.

The Fartz formed in Seattle around 1980, with Capitol Hill as their geographic base. Capitol Hill was the neighborhood the engine has documented throughout as Seattle's most consistently radical cultural geography: the dense, walkable, counter-cultural center of the city, home to the punk and early hardcore scene that operated in clubs, record stores, and houses throughout the early 1980s.

The Fartz lineup included Duff McKagan on bass and drums -- his first significant band. McKagan would later join Guns N' Roses (1985) and become one of the most recognized bass players of the late 1980s arena rock era. His presence in the Fartz is the most frequently cited biographical connection between the Seattle hardcore underground of 1980-1981 and the mainstream rock world that followed.

The band also included Paul Solger (connecting to the Solger tradition documented at degree 355, though the relationship between the two bands/individuals requires careful documentation -- Paul Solger was not the same as the band Solger, but the shared name is historically noted), vocalist Loud Walt (Walt Wagner), and other members across the band's active period.

The Fartz played fast, aggressive, politically direct hardcore. The Capitol Hill base gave them access to the small but growing Seattle punk scene; the Alternative Tentacles connection gave them distribution to the national hardcore underground.

(Sources: The Fartz Capitol Hill Seattle formation 1980; Duff McKagan bass drums early member; Paul Solger guitarist; Loud Walt Walt Wagner vocalist; Capitol Hill Seattle geography; fast aggressive politically direct hardcore; national distribution through Alternative Tentacles)

## BECAUSE THIS FUCKIN' WORLD STILL STINKS (1982) -- THE RECORD

### Alternative Tentacles. The Document of the Seattle Hardcore Peak.

*Because This Fuckin' World Still Stinks* was released in 1982 on Alternative Tentacles Records. The release is the document of the Fartz at their peak: fast, loud, direct, politically confrontational. The title announces the aesthetic completely: not ironic, not metaphorical, not self-consciously transgressive as performance -- genuinely, directly confrontational with the world as the band encountered it.

The Alternative Tentacles context is significant. Jello Biafra's label -- founded in 1979 in San Francisco, home to Dead Kennedys releases and a distribution network that reached the entire American hardcore underground -- was one of the two or three most important independent labels in American hardcore in 1982. The Fartz on Alternative Tentacles placed them in the same distribution context as Black Flag's early releases, Minor Threat, the Dead Kennedys themselves, and other foundational hardcore bands. The national distribution did not make The Fartz a national band -- but it made their music available to anyone connected to the hardcore underground anywhere in the country.

The music: short songs (most under two minutes), maximum velocity, minimal production, vocals that prioritized volume and directness over melody or technical precision. The production was not a limitation -- it was the correct sonic expression of the form. Hardcore in 1982 was not supposed to sound polished. The rough edges were the point.

(Sources: Because This Fuckin' World Still Stinks 1982 Alternative Tentacles; Jello Biafra Alternative Tentacles 1979 San Francisco; Dead Kennedys same label; national hardcore underground distribution; short songs under 2 minutes; maximum velocity minimal production; rough production as intentional expression)

## DUFF MCKAGAN -- THE CONNECTION

### From Capitol Hill Hardcore to Guns N' Roses to Seattle Rock Legend

Duff McKagan's biography is the most documentable cross-reference in the Fartz entry. Born Michael Andrew McKagan in Seattle on February 5, 1964, he grew up in Seattle and was active in the local punk/hardcore scene from an early age. The Fartz was his first significant band; he played bass (and reportedly drums in some configurations) with the band in the early 1980s.

In 1984, McKagan moved to Los Angeles and joined Guns N' Roses in 1985. The band released *Appetite for Destruction* in 1987, which became one of the best-selling rock albums of the decade. McKagan's bass line on "Welcome to the Jungle," "Sweet Child O' Mine," and "Paradise City" defined the sonic character of late-1980s arena rock alongside Slash's guitar work and Axl Rose's vocals.

The genealogy: Capitol Hill Seattle hardcore (1980-1984) → Los Angeles hard rock underground → Guns N' Roses mainstream breakthrough → one of the most commercially successful rock bands of the 1980s. The path from The Fartz to *Appetite for Destruction* traces the absorption of the underground into the mainstream -- the same absorption that Nirvana reversed (bringing the underground back to the surface) seven years later.

McKagan returned to Seattle later in his life, writing books (*It's So Easy (and other lies)*, 2011) and columns, becoming one of the most articulate chroniclers of the Pacific Northwest rock tradition. The full arc: Capitol Hill hardcore → arena rock → return to Seattle. The engine acknowledges the full arc.

(Sources: Duff McKagan born February 5 1964 Seattle; Fartz first significant band; bass drums early configuration; moved Los Angeles 1984; Guns N' Roses 1985; Appetite for Destruction 1987; Welcome to the Jungle Sweet Child O'Mine Paradise City; arena rock commercial success; return to Seattle; It's So Easy book 2011; underground to mainstream genealogy)

## ALTERNATIVE TENTACLES -- THE LABEL CONTEXT

### Jello Biafra's Infrastructure and What It Meant to be on It

Alternative Tentacles was founded in 1979 by Jello Biafra (Eric Reed Bouchard) as the label for Dead Kennedys releases and, subsequently, the broader American hardcore underground. The label's significance in 1982:

- **Distribution.** Alternative Tentacles had a genuine national distribution network -- something that independently functioning Seattle bands rarely had access to in the early 1980s. Being on AT meant your record could be ordered through the same channels as Dead Kennedys records.
- **Credibility.** In the underground hierarchy of 1982, being on Alternative Tentacles was a specific kind of credibility -- associated with Biafra's confrontational politics, the Dead Kennedys' theatrical extremity, and the San Francisco hardcore scene's particular aesthetic position.
- **Community.** The AT catalog connected The Fartz to a national community of bands and listeners who were operating in the same cultural space: hardcore as explicitly political music, DIY as institutional principle, confrontation as method.

For a Seattle band in 1982, this connection was not incidental. It was the mechanism by which the local founding of Seattle hardcore became legible to the national underground.

(Sources: Alternative Tentacles founded 1979 Jello Biafra Bouchard; Dead Kennedys home label; national distribution 1982; underground credibility hierarchy; AT catalog connects to national hardcore community; SF hardcore scene aesthetic position; local to national legibility mechanism)
