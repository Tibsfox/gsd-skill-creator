## What Worked

1. **The "output-to-capacity ratio" E=10 calibration is the correct framework for the Fartz.** Not output-to-volume (by that measure, the Fartz is a small club band with thousands of records distributed). Output-to-capacity: the environment they had -- Capitol Hill clubs, Alternative Tentacles distribution, small underground audience -- was filled completely at maximum output. E=10 where the capacity is fully realized, regardless of the scale of the capacity. This parallels the Pacific Wren pairing exactly.
2. **The Duff McKagan genealogy (Capitol Hill hardcore → LA underground → Guns N' Roses → arena rock → Seattle return) is the most complete individual biography in the final six.** The path from The Fartz to Appetite for Destruction traces the arc of underground-to-mainstream absorption that Nirvana would reverse seven years later. The engine documents both ends of this absorption: the Fartz as origin, Nirvana (348) as the reversal.
3. **The Solger + Fartz founding pair is the correct structural unit.** Placing them at consecutive degrees (355-356) acknowledges the historical pairing: two bands, one founding generation. The engine's closing structure honors the founding pair as a unit.

## Discoveries

1. **Paul Solger (guitarist, The Fartz) connecting to the Solger band name is worth careful documentation.** Paul Solger was a guitarist in The Fartz, and the band Solger at degree 355 had its own independent existence. The shared name is historically present and worth noting without conflating the two. This is a nuance in the documentation that the entry handles carefully.
2. **The Alternative Tentacles distribution mechanism is the most important infrastructure observation.** In 1982, there was no national infrastructure for punk or hardcore music outside of three or four labels: SST, Dischord, Alternative Tentacles, and a few others. Being on AT was not incidental -- it was the difference between existing as a purely local phenomenon and being legible to the national underground. This is the specific role The Fartz played that Solger (independent) could not.
