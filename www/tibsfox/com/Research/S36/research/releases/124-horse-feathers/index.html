The Chamber Quiet

Horse Feathers is the creative vehicle of Justin Ringle, a singer-songwriter whose work lives at the intersection of folk, chamber music, and experimental arrangement. Fingerpicked acoustic guitar provides the skeleton; violin, banjo, and cello add melodic counter-lines and harmonic depth; experimental textures (tape loops, found sounds) create an atmosphere of controlled unpredictability. The result is music that is simultaneously intimate and orchestral — the chamber quiet.

E=4 reflects the balance: quiet foundation with dynamic swells. The music never approaches the volume or aggression of higher-energy entries, but its chamber richness and emotional intensity lift it above the whisper-quiet register. Ringle’s vocals are quiet, slightly rough, close-miked — imperfections preserved rather than corrected. The vocal cracks at emotional peaks, and those cracks are the music’s most honest moments.

Kill Rock Stars — Independence as Principle

Kill Rock Stars was founded in Olympia in 1991 during the explosion of riot grrrl and DIY culture. The label’s name is a statement: the democratization of music, the rejection of celebrity, the elevation of community over hierarchy. Bikini Kill, Sleater-Kinney, Elliott Smith, Unwound, The Decemberists — a roster that reads like a canon of PNW independent music.

Horse Feathers on Kill Rock Stars is the label’s first direct entry in the engine. The chamber folk/experimental approach is not commercially optimized — too quiet for radio, too experimental for mainstream folk, too folky for experimental audiences. KRS provides the infrastructure for music that exists between categories, which is precisely where Horse Feathers lives. The Olympia hub sits on I-5 between Portland and Seattle, embodying the corridor that connects the two cities’ music scenes into one ecosystem.

The Portland–Seattle Corridor

Horse Feathers originated in Portland and connects to Seattle through Kill Rock Stars and the broader cultural corridor. Portland brings the literary folk tradition — DIY house shows, Powell’s Books, the Elliott Smith/Decemberists ecology. Seattle brings the label infrastructure and national visibility. Olympia sits between them, and KRS channels both cities’ energies. The corridor is not two separate scenes — it is one scene with two centers of gravity.

Discography

House With No Home (2006) — spare acoustic debut, fingerpicked guitar and hushed vocals. Thistled Spring (2008) — chamber instrumentation enters, the project’s defining balance between folk intimacy and orchestral richness. Appreciation (2012) — atmospheric production, banjo prominence, experimental textures appear. Cynic’s New Year (2014) — the fullest expression, full chamber ensemble, integrated experimental elements, quiet/loud dynamics most pronounced. The arc moves from spare folk to full chamber experimental across eight years.
