The Laptop as Instrument

Chong the Nomad is Alda Agustiano — an Indonesian-American producer, DJ, and vocalist who grew up in the Renton/Kent area of the Seattle metro, the daughter of Indonesian immigrants who led a worship group at their church and ran the Bumbu Truck (Indonesian street food). She began making beats at age 14 on FruityLoops, studied music composition and gamelan ensemble at Cornish College of the Arts, and built a career entirely outside the traditional label system — self-releasing music on Bandcamp while licensing to Apple, Nike, Singapore Airlines, and Marvel Studios.

Her debut EP Love Memo (Valentine’s Day 2018) earned “Top Album” recognition from The Seattle Times, Seattle Weekly, The Stranger, and KEXP. She opened for ODESZA and Death Cab for Cutie, co-produced “Lazy Susan” on the Marvel Shang-Chi soundtrack (featuring 21 Savage, Rich Brian, Warren Hue), and released her debut LP Do We Make of This? in June 2024 — called “one of the greatest local records of the year” by WASH Magazine.

The Gamelan Bridge

Agustiano studied gamelan ensemble at Cornish — the traditional Indonesian bronze percussion orchestra that operates outside Western musical theory. Gamelan uses non-tempered tuning systems (slendro and pelog), cyclical rhythmic structures (gongan), and interlocking melodic patterns (kotekan) where composite melodies emerge from the combination of individual parts. She intentionally translates these structures into her electronic productions. Indonesian friends report recognizing gamelan textures in her beats.

This is the engine’s first non-Western musical tradition embedded in the production DNA of an artist. Forty-four degrees of jazz, soul, blues, gospel, and rock — all Western traditions — and now gamelan enters through an Indonesian-American producer who studied it at Cornish and carries it into FruityLoops.

Music Theory Contributions

The Grain

Advanced

45th acoustic element. The sonic phenomenon produced when sound is decomposed into microscopic grains, individually processed, and reassembled into a texture that shimmers between recognition and abstraction. The chopped vocal samples, stuttering noise fragments, and layered synthesizer textures of Chong the Nomad’s productions are grain events. First digital-production-physics acoustic element.

The Bedroom Architect

Advanced

45th artist-city pattern. The artist who builds an entire sonic world from a bedroom or home studio, without institutional scaffolding. The city provides cultural context (KEXP, Capitol Hill, Crane City); the creative work happens in private with a laptop and headphones. First technology-mediated city pattern.

Blue Note: The Chopped Bend

Intermediate

44th blue note deployment. The blue note captured in a vocal sample, sliced into fragments, pitch-shifted, and embedded in an electronic beat so that the emotional content of the blues bend persists even though the original vocal is unrecognizable. First digitally mediated blue note.
