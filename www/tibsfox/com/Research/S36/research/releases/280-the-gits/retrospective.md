## What Worked

1. **The documentation prioritizes the artistry correctly.** The instruction to handle Mia Zapata's murder with utmost respect and focus on the music is followed throughout. The murder is documented factually and briefly in one specific section, then the focus returns immediately to the music, the voice, and the community's response. This is the correct approach.
2. **Mia Zapata's voice is documented at appropriate depth.** The blues dimension, the emotional directness, the range, the note-bending, the silence as technique -- these are the specifics that make the claim "one of the most gifted vocalists of the Pacific Northwest punk scene" more than a statement: it is a documented assessment.
3. **"Frenching the Bully" documented as primary artistic statement.** Steve Albini's production approach applied to The Gits produces exactly what it should: the natural sound of the instruments, Zapata's voice at the center. The album is what endures.
4. **Home Alive documented as a milestone.** The community's organized response -- founding a self-defense organization in the immediate aftermath of the murder -- is one of the most significant community responses in Seattle music history. It is documented as a milestone, not as a footnote.
5. **The 279-280 bridge is confirmed and stated explicitly.** "¡Viva Zapata!" named for Mia Zapata; The Gits is the next degree. The bridge is the most explicit cross-degree connection in this section of the engine.
6. **E=8 fifth consecutive documented.** The most sustained E=8 streak in the Pacific Northwest documentation of the engine. Five bands, five consecutive degrees, five entirely different characters within E=8.
7. **Joan Jett's lineage fully documented.** From Bikini Kill's "Rebel Girl" production (276) to The Gits tribute collaboration (280): the same musician's sustained commitment to the feminist punk community documented across four degrees.
