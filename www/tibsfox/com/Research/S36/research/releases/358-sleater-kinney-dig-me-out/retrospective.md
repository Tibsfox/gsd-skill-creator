## What Worked

1. **The no-bass two-guitar architecture as the entry's primary analytical frame is correct.** The absence of bass is not merely a biographical curiosity -- it is the structural decision that defines the band's entire sonic architecture. Every aspect of how the three instruments relate to each other is shaped by the no-bass decision. The analytical frame that treats it as architecture rather than absence is the right one.
2. **Describing the two guitars as "both functions simultaneously" rather than "rhythm and lead" is the correct formulation.** The standard rhythm/lead split is the vocabulary of a guitar architecture that Brownstein specifically avoided. "Both functions simultaneously" captures what the guitar actually does on *Dig Me Out* in a way that "creative use of rhythm guitar" or "non-conventional lead" would not.
3. **Corin Tucker's vibrato as "the sound of pitch's instability under pressure" is the correct characterization.** Not ornamental, not stylistic -- it is the direct acoustic manifestation of the emotional content of the songs. The vibrato is what happens to the pitch when the song's emotional pressure is sufficient. This framing connects the technical description to the artistic content.
4. **Janet Weiss's kick drum as the low-frequency substitute for absent bass is the correct technical analysis.** The physics: without bass, the sub-100 Hz range has to be carried by the kick drum or left empty. Weiss's drumming being heavier and more present than typical rock drumming is not a stylistic preference -- it is a structural consequence of the no-bass decision.
5. **Peak-period specificity and durability as the two E=10 calibration components is solid.** *Dig Me Out* is neither the debut nor the late-career ambitious record. It is the mid-period peak where all elements converged at maximum. The record that holds longest in retrospective assessment is the record that got most right. Both calibration arguments are documentable.

## Discoveries

1. **John Goodmanson as the common producer across multiple Kill Rock Stars final-arc entries.** Goodmanson produced *Dig Me Out* (Sleater-Kinney 358) and is documented as working with Unwound and the Blood Brothers. His production transparency -- present enough to define the sonic space without inserting between listener and band -- is a consistent Kill Rock Stars aesthetic.
2. **The interplay being the content (no background, only foreground) is the key insight for the two-guitar architecture.** In most rock guitar arrangements, there is a background (rhythm guitar) and a foreground (lead guitar). In Sleater-Kinney, both guitars are in perpetual motion and the interplay between them is itself the content. There is no background. This formulation distinguishes the architecture precisely.
3. **The Village Voice citation as real-time recognition of *Dig Me Out* distinguishes it from Bikini Kill's DIY distribution context.** Bikini Kill (357) operated entirely within underground distribution. Sleater-Kinney (358), with Kill Rock Stars' commercial distribution reach, received alternative press attention that the preceding riot grrrl generation largely did not. This is a meaningful historical transition in how the Olympia tradition was received.

- **Energy:** E=10 + E=10 = E=20. Combined maximum.
