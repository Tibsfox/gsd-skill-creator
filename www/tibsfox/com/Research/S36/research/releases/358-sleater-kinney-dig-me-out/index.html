DIG ME OUT — OLYMPIA, 1997 — THREE VOICES, NO BASS

DEGREE 358. THE OLYMPIA SEQUENCE CONTINUES. Carrie Brownstein and Corin Tucker as a two-guitar instrument. Janet Weiss as the third voice that completed the architecture. No bass — not a deficiency, an architectural decision. The most fully realized two-guitar ensemble in Pacific Northwest indie rock. Dig Me Out (Kill Rock Stars, April 22, 1997). 34 minutes. 13 tracks. E=10.

Three Voices

Carrie Brownstein

Guitar, Vocals

Both functions simultaneously: low end provides rhythmic grounding; upper register provides melodic commentary; continuous rapid transition between. Not rhythm guitar or lead guitar — both at once.

Corin Tucker

Guitar, Vocals (primary)

Organic vibrato — the sound of pitch’s instability under the song’s emotional pressure. Not ornamental; structural. Rhythmically direct guitar complementing Brownstein’s architectural complexity.

Janet Weiss

Drums (joined 1996)

Third voice in three-voice ensemble. Kick drum carries low-frequency absent bass. Heavier and more present than typical rock drumming by structural necessity, not stylistic choice.

The Architecture — No Bass

Frequency Distribution Without Bass

Sub-bass Weiss kick drum: carries low-frequency grounding that absent bass would provide. Heavier by necessity.
Lower register Tucker guitar: rhythmically direct lower-register parts; architectural grounding in the lower strings.
Mid / Lead Brownstein guitar: both rhythm and lead simultaneously. Neither exclusively. Wide-register transition as technique.
Vocal / High Tucker primary voice at full vibrato. Brownstein secondary voice structural not decorative. Two-voice vocal system.
“There is no background. Both guitars are in perpetual motion. The interplay between them is itself the content of the music.”

Key Tracks — Dig Me Out (1997)

“Start Together” Album opens. Two-guitar architecture in first 10 seconds before full band. The architecture announced immediately.
“One More Hour” Emotional peak for many listeners. Tucker vibrato at maximum deployment. Interplay at maximum complexity.
“Turn It On” Speed and directness. Album’s most energetically immediate track. Brownstein guitar providing momentum.
“Dig Me Out” Title track. Assertion of presence. Demand for recognition. Full ensemble synchronized at maximum.
