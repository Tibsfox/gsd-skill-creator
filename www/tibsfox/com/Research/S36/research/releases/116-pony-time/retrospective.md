## What Worked

### Research Process

1. **The raw transmission accurately captures the acoustic quality.** Two-person garage rock with no mediation. Overdriven guitar + pounding drums + vocals. Lo-fi production. Short songs. The description matches the sound. Verified.

2. **E=5 is defensible.** Garage rock is high energy -- loud, fast, immediate. E=5 correctly reflects the intensity of a two-piece band playing at full volume. Not the maximum E=6 (reserved for overwhelming force) but genuinely powerful.

3. **The post-streak reset is structurally accurate.** The contrast with degree 115 is dramatic across every dimension except energy. The pattern is clear and defensible.

4. **Self-released status is verifiable.** Pony Time's discography is available on Bandcamp as self-released. No label credits. Confirmed.

5. **Cross-references to degrees 115 (PGMG), 110 (Kinski), 108 (Veronica Lipgloss), 28 (Mudhoney), and 1 (Nirvana) are all defensible.**

### Source Quality

6. **Pony Time has a smaller source footprint than major-label artists.** Bandcamp, KEXP sessions, The Stranger mentions. Source base is adequate but thinner than for bands with label press infrastructure. This is appropriate -- the DIY approach means less institutional documentation.
