A Dynamite Salesman’s Daughter Who Drummed Her Way to Song

Sera Cahoone was born August 4, 1975. She grew up in Littleton, Colorado, in the foothills southwest of Denver. Her father, Bill Cahoone, was a Rocky Mountain dynamite salesman. Her mother, Judy Cahoone (née Ahrendts), was a business executive. Sera was raised primarily by her mother. She has a sister, Kal Cahoone, and a brother, Nathan Cahoone — both musicians.

Cahoone graduated from Columbine High School in Littleton. She began drumming at age twelve to combat anxiety — a condition she has discussed openly across her career. Her first stage performance was at Ziggie’s Saloon in North Denver during a blues open-mic night, while she was still a teenager. In 1998, she moved to Seattle when the snowboarding shop where she worked opened a Washington location. She quit retail after two years, worked in a coffee shop, and began playing music full-time.

“I remember one day I turned on TV and I saw my principal. It was just a surreal, crazy thing. I felt horrified of course, but also the fact that I was so far away and there’s my whole hometown on TV just made it worse.” — Sera Cahoone, on seeing the Columbine shooting from Seattle

The Carissa’s Wierd Genealogy

After arriving in Seattle, Cahoone became the drummer for Carissa’s Wierd, the slow, sad, string-laden indie rock band founded by Mat Brooke and Jenn Champion. The band played their final shows at the Crocodile Cafe in November 2003. From that single band emerged three major subsequent projects:

  • Ben Bridwell — formed Band of Horses (Sub Pop debut 2006, RIAA Gold)
  • Mat Brooke — formed Grand Archives (Sub Pop, 2008)
  • Sera Cahoone — solo singer-songwriter (Sub Pop, 2008)

Cahoone played session drums on Band of Horses’ debut album Everything All The Time (2006, Sub Pop) — the record that became the band’s commercial breakthrough. She received a Gold Record in 2019 for her contributions. The Carissa’s Wierd genealogy parallels the Pedro the Lion triangle (degrees 45–47): a single band producing multiple solo careers that scatter across the Seattle scene.

The Slow Bloom: From Drums to Voice

Cahoone’s transition from drummer to singer-songwriter was gradual and anxiety-ridden. She had been writing songs privately but was terrified of performing them. She described herself as “a super shy kid” who found it agonizing to have people hear her voice and lyrics. She forced herself through open mic nights to overcome the fear. The process took years. There was no breakthrough moment — no viral hit, no magazine cover, no festival-slot explosion. The career is the sum of twenty-five years of showing up.

In July 2013, Cahoone and bandmate Jason Kardong became the first musicians to perform on the rooftop of Seattle’s Space Needle, as part of Sub Pop’s Silver Jubilee Celebration, broadcast live on KEXP. The quiet folk singer from Ballard played on the same rooftop where Mudhoney played that same day. Sub Pop’s range: grunge and folk on the same landmark.

By 2023, Cahoone had transitioned into a producer role, producing Margo Cilker’s sophomore album Valley of Heart’s Delight — also playing drums and singing backing vocals. The drummer became a songwriter who became a producer: three career stages, each building on the last.

Music Theory Contributions

The Held Rest

Advanced

49th acoustic element. The silence within a song that functions as a rhythmic event rather than an absence. When a drummer leaves a beat empty, the space becomes part of the groove. Cahoone’s spare arrangements are built on held rests — the songwriter who hears silence the way a drummer hears rests. Extends the folk-vocal taxonomy to four elements: murmur (45), confessional (46), parable tone (47), held rest (48).

The Slow Bloom

Advanced

49th artist-city pattern. The career that develops gradually over many years without a single breakthrough moment. No viral hit, no explosion — just steady work, word of mouth, and incremental accumulation of respect. Cahoone moved to Seattle in 1998, drummed for others until 2006, self-released a debut in 2005, signed to Sub Pop in 2007, transitioned to producing by 2023. Twenty-five years of slow growth. The opposite of the supernova (37).

Blue Note: The Drummer’s Ghost

Intermediate

48th blue note deployment. The rhythmic muscle memory of a musician who has changed instruments but cannot leave their first instrument behind. Cahoone writes on guitar but hears drums. Her pacing, vocal phrasing, and spare arrangements carry the pulse of a rhythm section that isn’t there. Four consecutive folk-adjacent blue notes mapping absence: held breath (45), crack (46), suspended resolve (47), drummer’s ghost (48).
