## What Worked

1. **Cornell handled respectfully and completely.** Full biographical treatment including his death (May 18, 2017, Detroit, suicide following Soundgarden concert), his complete body of work (Temple of the Dog, Audioslave, solo records), and the significance of his vocal range and approach. The memorial note in the HTML is appropriate and calibrated.
2. **Thayil's Eastern modal influence is the right differentiating feature.** Most Metal Summit analysis would focus on vocal power and guitar distortion; the Eastern modal influence (South Asian-American background, Indian classical music) is what makes Thayil's approach distinct and is the correct emphasis.
3. **The Badmotorfinger/Nevermind/Ten same-week context is the right historical anchor.** The densest six weeks in American alternative music -- October 1991 -- and Soundgarden's position in it.
4. **Temple of the Dog as community document.** Andrew Wood's death, Cornell's tribute, Vedder's participation -- this is the scene's emotional infrastructure, handled with the right weight.

## Discoveries

1. **Matt Cameron's post-Soundgarden joining Pearl Jam (1998) creates a direct Seattle arc.** Cameron connects three entries: Soundgarden (335), Pearl Jam (via the Temple of the Dog-Pearl Jam connection), and indirectly the entire grunge ecosystem. His career is a biographical through-line of the Seattle scene.
2. **The Badmotorfinger/Nevermind timing is a recurring engine pattern.** Multiple consecutive degrees have contemporaneous release dates (AIC Dirt 1992 and Nevermind 1991, Badmotorfinger 1991). The Seattle commercial moment was genuinely compressed into 1991-1994.
