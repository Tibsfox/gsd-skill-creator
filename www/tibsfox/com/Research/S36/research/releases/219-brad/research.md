## THE PEARL JAM CONNECTION

### Stone Gossard's Other Band

Brad represents Pearl Jam's guitarist in a completely different context:

- **Stone Gossard.** Founding guitarist of Pearl Jam. Gossard is one of the most important figures in Seattle music history -- he was in Green River (proto-grunge), Mother Love Bone (Andrew Wood), Temple of the Dog, and Pearl Jam. His role in Brad shows the breadth of his musical interests.
- **The contrast.** Pearl Jam is arena rock at E=6-7. Brad is mellow alternative/soul at E=5. Gossard in Pearl Jam plays driving, rhythm-heavy riffs. Gossard in Brad plays laid-back, groove-based, atmospheric guitar. Same musician, different energy state.
- **Side project as creative outlet.** Brad allowed Gossard to explore music that didn't fit Pearl Jam's template. Funk, soul, R&B, and jazz influences that would have been out of place in Pearl Jam found a home in Brad.

## SHAWN SMITH (1965-2019) -- RIP

### The Voice That Defined Brad

Shawn Smith's passing in 2019 was a significant loss to Seattle music:

- **The voice.** Shawn Smith had one of the most soulful voices in Seattle music -- a warm, rich baritone/tenor that could move from gentle croon to passionate wail. His vocal style drew from R&B, soul, and jazz more than from rock. In a city dominated by guitars, Smith's voice was the instrument.
- **Vocal range.** Smith's range was extraordinary -- he could move from a low, breathy whisper to a powerful falsetto within a single phrase. The control was effortless. The emotion was always present.
- **Multiple projects.** Beyond Brad, Smith fronted Satchel (funk-rock), Pigeonhed (electronic), and released solo material. Each project showcased a different facet of his voice. Brad was the mellowest, most soul-inflected context.
- **The loss.** Smith died on April 5, 2019, at age 53. His death was less publicly mourned than other Seattle losses (Cobain, Cornell, Staley) but was deeply felt in the Seattle music community. Smith was a musician's musician -- respected by peers, loved by collaborators.

(Sources: Shawn Smith biography; vocal analysis; Satchel; Pigeonhed; death April 2019)

## THE SOUND -- MELLOW GROOVE

### After the Storm

Brad's music is the antithesis of the Blood Brothers:

- **Groove-based.** Where post-hardcore attacks, Brad grooves. The music is built on bass and drum patterns that swing and sway. Funk and R&B influences. The rhythm section provides a warm foundation.
- **Soulful vocals.** Shawn Smith's voice carries the melody over relaxed instrumental arrangements. The vocals are the center -- everything else supports the voice.
- **Atmospheric guitar.** Gossard's guitar in Brad is restrained, textural, atmospheric. Clean tones, reverb, effects. No power chords. No distortion. The guitar paints rather than drives.
- **Laid-back energy.** The music doesn't demand attention -- it invites it. This is music for late nights, slow mornings, and the spaces between more intense experiences.
