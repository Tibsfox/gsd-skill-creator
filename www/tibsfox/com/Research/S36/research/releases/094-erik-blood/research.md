## ERIK BLOOD -- The Studio as Instrument

### Formation and Career

Erik Blood is a Seattle **producer, multi-instrumentalist, and recording engineer** whose solo work sits at the intersection of electronic, experimental, and atmospheric music. He is also a **visual artist**, bringing a multi-disciplinary practice to music. Blood has produced albums for some of Seattle's most adventurous artists, including **Shabazz Palaces** (Sub Pop, experimental hip-hop), **THEESatisfaction** (Sub Pop), and others in Seattle's forward-looking music community.

His solo work is **atmospheric electronic/experimental** -- the music of a producer working for himself, applying the techniques and sensibilities developed through engineering and producing other artists' records to his own creative vision. The studio is the instrument. The production IS the composition.

### The Producer-As-Artist Lineage

Erik Blood joins **Chris Walla** (degree 67) in the engine's producer-as-artist lineage. Walla defined Death Cab for Cutie's sound through production; Blood has shaped the sound of Seattle's experimental and hip-hop scenes. Both are engineers who became artists, both approach music through the studio, both have influenced the sound of Seattle from behind the mixing console. Walla's domain was indie rock; Blood's domain is experimental electronic and hip-hop.

### The Shabazz Palaces Connection

Blood's production work for **Shabazz Palaces** (Ishmael Butler's experimental hip-hop project on Sub Pop) places him at the intersection of Seattle's electronic and hip-hop communities. This cross-genre position is central to his identity -- he is not an electronic artist who happens to produce hip-hop, nor a hip-hop producer who happens to make electronic music. He is a producer-artist who moves between genres because the studio permits all of them.

### Visual Art Practice

Erik Blood is also a **visual artist** -- painting, design, and visual work that parallels his music practice. The multi-disciplinary approach is characteristic of Seattle's experimental community, where the boundary between music, visual art, and production is intentionally porous.

The **producer's palette** is the ninety-fifth acoustic element in the Seattle 360 engine. It describes the sound produced when a recording engineer and producer makes music for themselves -- the full vocabulary of studio techniques (compression, effects chains, spatial processing, synthesis, sampling, layering) deployed not in service of another artist's vision but in service of the producer's own. The producer's palette is the sound of the studio itself as a creative instrument, with no genre obligation and no client to satisfy.
