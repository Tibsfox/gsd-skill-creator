Botch — We Are the Romans

E = 10 Mathcore Hydra Head Records

THE MATHCORE CEILING — THE ALBUM — 1999

We Are the Romans (1999). Eight tracks. Thirty-seven minutes. No filler. The record against which every subsequent mathcore album is measured. Brian Cook’s compositionally independent bass. Dynamic architecture as structural tool. Non-coincident independent voices. E=10.

Pairs with River + Percussion + Wind Geological (SPS 352, E=10) — Snoqualmie Falls in flood, geological timescale. E=20.

The Dynamic Architecture

Most extreme music operates in a narrow dynamic range — maximum volume most of the time, with occasional quieter moments. We Are the Romans uses the full dynamic range as a compositional tool.

Quiet Sections

Genuinely quiet. Tension accumulation. Not respite — preparation. The silence before the release.

→

Violent Sections

Sudden. Unprepared. Maximum intensity. The quiet made this an event, not a baseline. The loud is the release of accumulated tension.

Without the quiet, the loud is just loud. With the quiet, the loud is an event. The quiet sections are structural, not decorative. This is the album’s primary compositional innovation.

The Tracks

To Our Friends in the Great White North

Opens with clean guitar. Establishes the album’s dynamic range before the first violence. The deceptive opening that announces what kind of album this will be.

C. Thomas Howell as the Soul Man

The album’s most recognized track. Stop-start rhythmic structure. Verellen’s scream as rhythmic instrument. Cook’s bass operating independently of the guitar riffs.

Transitions from Persona to Object

The album’s longest and most structurally complex track. Extended quiet sections followed by abrupt violence. Dynamic architecture at its most deliberately disorienting.

We Are the Romans

Closes the album. Begins quietly. Arrives at the most sustained violent passage. The arc from opening clean guitar to closing sustained intensity completes here.

The Four Independent Voices

Brian Cook
Bass — THE INNOVATION

Compositionally independent. Not harmonic support for the guitars. Composes in the spaces the guitars leave. Contrapuntal. The lasting technical precedent every subsequent mathcore band responded to.

Dave Knudson
Guitar

Angular, dissonant riffs not in standard metal vocabulary. Irregular time signatures at high tempos. Composing independently from Latona rather than doubling.

Tim Latona
Guitar

Non-coincident with Knudson. Each guitar playing different riffs simultaneously, creating composite texture more complex than either alone.

Dave Verellen
Vocals — Rhythmic Instrument

Scream as percussion voice. Phrasing compositionally determined by rhythmic structure, not lyrical narrative. The voice liberated from its conventional support role.

“The album operates at the timescale of geological processes. The rhythmic structures do not resolve at the human pulse. They move like the Snoqualmie River in flood: continuous, impersonal, enormous.”

Tacoma — Industrial Periphery Confirmed

Botch formed in Tacoma, Washington — 35 miles south of Seattle, Port of Tacoma container port, ASARCO copper smelter Superfund site. The fourth consecutive confirmation of the engine’s pattern: Aberdeen (Melvins, Nirvana), Oak Harbor (The Accused), Georgetown (TAD), Tacoma (Botch). The industrial periphery produces the maximum technical extreme.
