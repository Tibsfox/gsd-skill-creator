## What Worked

1. **Fremont as the tonal correction to Capitol Hill.** Eight consecutive Capitol Hill-anchored degrees (221-227) -- grief, supergroups, art rock, heavy atmospheric music. Gas Huffer arrives from Fremont with irreverence, humor, and fast punk. The neighborhood shift is a genuine tonal shift, and the engine benefits from it.
2. **The U-Men connection grounds Gas Huffer in Seattle's deepest underground history.** Tom Price in the U-Men (1981-1989) predates grunge by nearly a decade. Gas Huffer is not a product of the post-Nirvana signing frenzy -- it is an expression of a Seattle underground tradition that predates the commercial wave. Price's career arc is the longest continuous underground commitment documented in the engine so far.
3. **The Amphetamine Reptile analysis correctly identifies the label as a cross-regional network.** AmRep is Minneapolis-based; Gas Huffer is Seattle-based. The connection places Gas Huffer in a noise rock underground that extends beyond the Seattle scene and into the broader American heavy underground of the early 1990s.
4. **The raptor arc closes correctly at E=7.** The arc opened at E=5 (Bald Eagle contact call, 221), ranged across E=6, E=4, and back, and closes at E=7 with Gas Huffer. Not exhausted, not at the ceiling -- energized and forward-moving. The correct tonal note on which to end.

## Discoveries

1. **The album title *Integrity Technology and Service* (1995) is one of the engine's best documented cases of absurdist corporate parody.** Mission statement language applied to a punk band: the humor of incongruity. Gas Huffer's consistent use of this kind of title framing is a literary as well as musical aesthetic decision.
2. **The "borrowed energy" parallel between Gas Huffer and the Red-tailed Hawk (SPS 228) is one of the engine's most conceptually interesting cross-pair analyses.** The Red-tail's scream is used for every other raptor in cinema; Gas Huffer's punk energy type, as a genre, was borrowed and processed into commercial grunge. Both are the source of borrowed energy distributed into a larger cultural context without full attribution.
3. **Fremont's "Freedom to Be Peculiar" slogan is a documented neighborhood identity.** The slogan captures the neighborhood's character precisely: not the earnest artistic solemnity of Capitol Hill, but playful self-importance and comfort with the eccentric. Gas Huffer embodies this slogan in band form.

## Pair Analysis: Gas Huffer + Red-tailed Hawk

- **Energy:** E=7 + E=6 = E=13. Combined pair above equilibrium. The raptor arc closes with force.
- **Theme:** BORROWED ENERGY. The Red-tailed Hawk's scream is the most borrowed sound in the history of American filmmaking -- used for Bald Eagles, Golden Eagles, Ospreys, Peregrine Falcons, and fantasy raptors worldwide. Gas Huffer's punk energy, as a genre type, was borrowed and processed into commercial grunge without the genre receiving full credit for what it supplied. Both are the source that powers something larger than themselves in the cultural ecosystem. Both are more present in the actual world (real PNW forests, real Fremont bars) than in the cultural mythology that appropriated their energy.
