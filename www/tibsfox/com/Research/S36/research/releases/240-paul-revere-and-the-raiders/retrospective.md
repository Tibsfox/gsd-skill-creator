## What Worked

1. **The two-thirds mark acknowledgment is structurally appropriate.** Degree 240 is a genuine milestone. The engine marks it with a band that earned the position through commercial success and historical significance.
2. **"Kicks" as anti-drug song is correctly framed.** The song's directness -- confrontational, melodically strong, commercially successful -- is a genuine achievement. It belongs in the engine's permanent record.
3. **The Columbia Records thread (238-240) is complete.** PUSA (238) and Raiders (240) close the label thread in this four-degree run. The documentation of label patterns adds depth to the engine's music-industry record.
4. **The Pacific Northwest 1960s context is important.** The Raiders were the first Pacific Northwest act to achieve sustained national commercial visibility. This predates the grunge mythology by 25 years. The engine now holds the record.

## Discoveries

1. **Paul Revere Dick's actual name was Paul Revere Dick.** The coincidence with the historical figure is what triggered the whole Revolutionary War concept. The name came first; the costumes followed the name. This is a more interesting origin than "they decided to wear costumes."
2. **Terry Melcher produced the Raiders' best work.** Melcher's later associations (Manson, Beach Boys) tend to overshadow his earlier production work. The Raiders records he produced are genuinely well-crafted.

## Pair Analysis: Paul Revere and the Raiders + Bull Trout

- **Energy:** E=6 + E=2 = E=8. **MOST DRAMATIC CONTRAST in the 237-240 run.** The other pairings were E=7+6=13, E=6+6=12, E=5+5=10. This is the lowest combined energy and the widest internal gap.
- **Theme:** THE LOUD AND THE SILENT. The Raiders are theatrical, costumed, organ-driven, E=6 garage rock -- the loudest visual presence in 1960s Pacific Northwest music. Bull Trout is E=2: near-silent, cold-water-dependent, hiding in the dark recesses of pristine mountain streams. The contrast is deliberately extreme at the two-thirds mark. Both the loud and the silent exist in the Pacific Northwest. The engine documents both.
- **The milestone resonance.** At the two-thirds mark, the engine holds both the theatrical loudness of the Raiders and the near-silence of the Bull Trout. Both are real. Both are Pacific Northwest. The circle turns.
