### The New Program and the Central District's Return: Starting Over at Higher Altitude

Ranger 1 launched August 23, 1961 — the first mission of the Ranger program, NASA's first dedicated lunar exploration effort. The mission failed: the Agena upper stage did not reignite for translunar injection, and the spacecraft was stranded in a low Earth parking orbit where it reentered the atmosphere within a week. The Ranger program begins the way the Pioneer program began — with failure. But this failure is at higher altitude: where Pioneer 0 (degree 1) exploded at T+77 seconds from the surface, Ranger 1 reached orbit and simply couldn't leave it. The failure mode has evolved. The starting altitude is higher. The program is new.

Black Stax returns the Central District to the engine — the fourth CD return (after degrees 0-6, 11, 19, now 25). Each return is in a different era and genre: postwar jazz (0-6), salon jazz (11), neo-soul collective (19), and now Neo-Soul as primary genre identity (25). The Central District's musical DNA evolves with each return. Jazz → Soul → Neo-Soul. The thread does not repeat — it spirals upward, the same geography producing different music at different altitudes, the way the Ranger program operates at a higher orbital altitude than the earlier Explorers.

Neo-Soul at degree 25 is the engine's first genre label without "Jazz" anywhere in it. For twenty-five degrees, every artist had "Jazz" in at least a modifier position. Black Stax's "Neo-Soul" is pure — no jazz qualifier, no compound label, no hedge. The genre transition that began at degree 16 (Jazz Fusion) and progressed through Jazz/Comedy (17), Soul/Jazz (18-19), Jazz/Funk (21-22), Soul/Jazz (23-24) has now reached its destination: the first non-jazz primary genre. The jazz quadrant of the circle is complete. Neo-Soul opens the soul quadrant.

### The Band as Central District Statement

Black Stax is a band — a collective of Seattle musicians rooted in the Central District whose music is explicitly soul-identified. The band's Central District base is significant because it connects the CD's neo-soul present to its jazz past: the same neighborhood that produced Quincy Jones's arranging vocabulary now produces neo-soul groove. The harmonic sophistication is inherited (jazz voicings under soul melodies), but the genre identity has shifted. Jazz was the primary; now soul is.

Ranger 1's failure was a beginning, not an ending — the Ranger program would eventually succeed (Rangers 7-9 returned the first close-up photographs of the Moon's surface in 1964-1965). Black Stax's Neo-Soul is a beginning too — the first pure soul genre in an engine that has been building toward it for 25 degrees. The jazz foundation was laid (degrees 0-15). The genre gradient was traversed (degrees 16-24). The soul destination is reached at degree 25. Like Ranger 1, the first mission of the new program doesn't reach its target. But the program has started.
