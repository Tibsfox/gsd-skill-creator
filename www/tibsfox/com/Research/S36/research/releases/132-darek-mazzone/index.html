Wo’ Pop — The Show That Opens the Map

Darek Mazzone has hosted Wo’ Pop on KEXP 90.3 FM since the late 1990s, having joined the station (then KCMU) in 1996. The name contracts “World Pop,” and the show has become one of KEXP’s most distinctive programs — a genuinely global musical journey that takes listeners from West African highlife to Colombian cumbia to Moroccan gnawa to Japanese city pop, connected by rhythm, mood, and the host’s adventurous ear.

Mazzone does not treat world music as a homogeneous category. Each regional tradition is approached with specificity and respect — a Malian kora recording comes with context about the griot tradition, a cumbia track is placed in the history of tropical dance music. For many KEXP listeners, Wo’ Pop is their primary exposure to non-Western music.

Expanding KEXP Beyond Indie Rock

If KEXP can guide you through Moroccan gnawa and make it feel accessible, it can guide you through anything. Wo’ Pop is the strongest version of the station’s argument that human curation matters — because the music is the most unfamiliar to the average Western listener.

Wo’ Pop breaks the genre bubble. KEXP’s identity is rooted in indie rock and alternative music — Mazzone’s program demonstrates that the curatorial ethos (quality, discovery, context) applies to all music, not just the Western indie canon. The show brings in listeners who might not otherwise engage with KEXP, serving as a gateway to the station’s broader programming. World music enthusiasts discover KEXP through Wo’ Pop; then they explore everything else.
