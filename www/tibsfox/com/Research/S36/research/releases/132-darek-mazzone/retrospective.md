## Structural Event 2: Global Music Curation as KEXP Identity Expansion

- **Situation:** Wo' Pop expands KEXP beyond its indie rock core into genuinely global territory -- Africa, South America, Middle East, Asia, Caribbean, Eastern Europe. The show demonstrates that KEXP's curatorial ethos applies to all music.
- **Root Cause:** The freeform tradition inherited from KCMU has no genre boundaries. Mazzone applies this tradition to the widest possible musical territory. The result is a program that is uniquely KEXP -- no other Seattle radio station (or most American stations) offers comparable world music programming.

## Structural Event 3: The DJ as Translator

- **Situation:** Mazzone's role goes beyond curation to translation -- making unfamiliar music accessible without reducing it to exotic novelty. Context, connection, and selection as translation tools.
- **Root Cause:** World music programming faces a unique challenge: bridging the gap between the listener's familiar sonic world and unfamiliar traditions. Mazzone's approach (specificity, respect, context) is the model for how to do this well.
- **Recommendation:** Document the DJ-as-translator as a media theory node. Note that this is the strongest version of the human curator vs. algorithm argument: if a human can guide you through gnawa, no algorithm can replicate that.
- **Carry Item:** CONFIRMED -- DJ-as-translator documented. Strongest human-curation argument.
