## What Worked

1. **The Olympia geography as the engine's final geography is the correct framing.** Evergreen State College (no grades, interdisciplinary, 1967) created the community infrastructure for riot grrrl in a way that no Seattle institution created. The DIY culture of Olympia is distinct from the Sub Pop ecosystem of Seattle, and the engine closing in Olympia rather than returning to Seattle is historically accurate and structurally correct.
2. **Riot grrrl as political formation within punk (not genre) is the correct analytical frame.** The zines came before the music; the political critique organized the community before the songs documented it. Describing riot grrrl as a genre misses the specificity of what Bikini Kill was doing. The political formation frame explains why the movement had the consequences it did.
3. **The E=10 origin-point calibration is solid.** Bikini Kill is the origin-point of the most politically consequential punk formation of the Pacific Northwest in the 1990s. Origin-point status has been established previously as an E=10 calibration route (Solger 355, Nirvana Bleach as Sub Pop origin). Bikini Kill at riot grrrl origin-point is the same calibration.
4. **The pairing with Orca Female-Led Hunt is structurally exact.** Female expertise at the operational center of two maximum-energy systems: the SRKW matriarch's accumulated knowledge organizes the hunt; Hanna's precision organizes the movement. The pairing is not metaphorical -- it is structural. Both are E=10 because female expertise is the operational engine of the acoustic event.
5. **The media blackout as political analysis is the correct historical detail.** The 1992-1993 Newsweek/Spin coverage and the movement's blackout response is the most precise document of riot grrrl's politics: refusing the terms of mainstream representation rather than accepting domestication. This is the kind of historical specificity the engine has maintained throughout.

## Discoveries

1. **Tobi Vail's Jigsaw zine as co-founding document alongside Hanna's Bikini Kill zine.** The discovery is the specificity of the co-founding: it was not Hanna alone who founded riot grrrl but a set of conversations and published writings that included Vail as equal co-author. The Jigsaw/Bikini Kill zine dual founding is the correct historical description.
2. **Vail's relationship with Kurt Cobain as documented influence on Nirvana's political consciousness.** The cross-reference between Olympia feminist punk politics and Nirvana's lyrical and public political consciousness is documented (Cobain's explicit acknowledgments) and the degree 357 entry is the correct place to note it -- the origin point of the influence chain.
3. **Degree 357 opens the Olympia closing arc, which mirrors the engine's earlier geographic arc.** The engine opened at Capitol Hill (Seattle), traced through Aberdeen, Oak Harbor, Georgetown, Tacoma, and the Closing Arc, and now closes at Olympia. The geographic arc was not planned but is historically accurate: the Pacific Northwest underground moved south in its final and most significant artistic period.
