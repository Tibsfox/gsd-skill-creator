In the moment of truly understanding an enemy, you also love them. This is the paradox of the Speaker for the Dead: to speak someone's life truthfully requires inhabiting their perspective so completely that judgment dissolves into comprehension. Card's career is a sustained exploration of empathy's architecture — how understanding another consciousness transforms both the knower and the known.

Research Modules

M1: Biography & Formative Influences

Biography Track A · Formation

From Richland, Washington (1951) through Mormon pioneer heritage, Brazil mission, theater career, early publications in Analog, Campbell Award, Greensboro relocation, 2011 stroke, and Southern Virginia University. Formative reading, family musical tradition, the transition from playwright to novelist.

M2: The Ender Universe

Literary Track A · Core achievement

The Ender Saga, Shadow Series, Formic Wars prequels, and 2013 film adaptation. Games as reality, empathy as weapon, child soldiers, xenocide ethics, the Speaker vocation. Hugo/Nebula consecutive wins, Radford's critique, Kessel's analysis, and the boycott.

M3: Beyond Ender — Major Series & Works

Literary Track A · Full range

Tales of Alvin Maker (Joseph Smith reimagined), Homecoming Saga (Book of Mormon as SF), Mithermages, Pathfinder, Women of Genesis, standalone novels, video game dialogue, comics, and cross-genre range from horror to historical fiction.

M4: Craft Philosophy & Pedagogy

Craft Track B · Teaching legacy

The MICE quotient (Milieu, Idea, Character, Event). How to Write Science Fiction and Fantasy. Literary Boot Camp alumni: Sanderson, Meyer, Wolverton, Stone, Ford, McClellan. InterGalactic Medicine Show. Writers of the Future. Southern Virginia University teaching.

M5: Faith, Controversy & Legacy

Faith Track B · The full picture

Mormon theology as structural architecture in fiction. The controversy timeline from 1990 through 2013. NOM board, DC Comics cancellation, Ender's Game boycott. The art-versus-artist question with evidence from multiple perspectives. Professional consequences and legacy assessment.
