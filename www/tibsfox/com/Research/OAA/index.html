Every office has workflows that somebody built by hand and nobody documented. Approval chains live in email threads, document pipelines live in somebody's cron job, and project status lives in a spreadsheet that three people update differently. The GSD orchestration layer — convoy routing, document generation, token-budget enforcement — is office automation at machine scale. The patterns are identical; the execution speed is not.

Research Projects

BPM Convoy Workflow Orchestration

OAA-CWO · GUPP/Sling/Mayor-Coordinator · 15 study topics · 3 try sessions

How GSD's convoy dispatch model maps to Business Process Management. GUPP work assignment as process instantiation, sling-dispatch as a task router, mayor-coordinator as an orchestration engine. Compared to Camunda, Temporal.io, and Apache Airflow.

Pipeline Document Pipeline Automation

OAA-DPA · Sweep daemon + publish-pipeline + FTP sync · 13 study topics · 3 try sessions

Scheduled document generation, multi-format output, and automated publishing. The sweep daemon as a cron-driven content pipeline, transcript-compactor as document summarization, publish-pipeline as release management. Compared to Power Automate, n8n, and Zapier.

Coord Multi-Agent Task Coordination

OAA-MTC · Beads-state + token-budget + event-log · 14 study topics · 3 try sessions

Task assignment, progress tracking, resource allocation, and budget constraints at agent scale. Beads-state as a project ledger, token-budget as resource leveling, event-log as an audit trail. Compared to Jira, Asana, and Monday.com.

PLM/ERP PLM & ERP Systems

OAA-PES · BOM, digital thread, cloud-native PLM, ERP modules · 15 study topics · 3 try sessions

Product Lifecycle Management and Enterprise Resource Planning as enterprise workflow backbones. Cloud-native PLM (Duro, First Resonance), agentic PLM (Propel), ERP fundamentals and migration triggers. BOM as convoy manifest, token-budget as financial controls, event-log as audit trail.

Lean Lean Operations & Digital Transformation

OAA-LDT · Factory Physics, Toyota Production System, MLOps, seven wastes · 15 study topics · 3 try sessions

Factory Physics meets agent orchestration. Toyota’s JIT and Kanban as pull systems, Little’s Law applied to convoy WIP, Boeing’s $250M lean failure, seven wastes mapped to agent work, CMU SLAs for operational quality. Digital transformation patterns from proprietary to open.
