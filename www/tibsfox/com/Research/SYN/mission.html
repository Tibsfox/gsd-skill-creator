Mission Pack

The original research brief and mission package that initiated the Synsor Corp research project. A comprehensive three-stage TeX document — Vision, Research Reference, Mission Package — covering the full pipeline from competitive intelligence to structured execution.

Source Documents

The mission pack comprises a multi-hundred-line TeX mission document covering the complete Synsor.ai research scope: company founding and corporate evolution, product architecture (turnkey hardware kit plus AI software platform), patent analysis of the granted German patent on temporal trend analysis via compressed feature retention, competitive landscape mapping across 10+ companies, market sizing with five normalized analyst projections, and GSD ecosystem integration architecture mapping to chipset patterns.

Research Scope

  • Company & Product: Munich founding (May 2021), Nico Engelmann and Benjamin Gosse, UG-to-GmbH conversion, APX seed funding (April 2022), SCE affiliation, six-component turnkey hardware/software system, edge-first deployment.
  • Patent & IP: Granted German patent on production process optimization via visual information. Three key claims: real-time capture, creeping deterioration detection, compressed feature temporal retention. Prior art comparison against Utechzone US10964004B2.
  • Competitive Landscape: Direct (Scortex, Relimetrics, elunic), adjacent (Landing AI, Instrumental, Neurala), incumbent (Cognex, Keyence, Omron, KLA-Tencor). Two-axis mapping: AI-native vs. AI-augmented, point solution vs. platform.
  • Market Dynamics: Five analyst projections ($2.3B–$34.2B range) with methodology normalization. Predictive quality subsegment: $2.9–4.3B globally. Adoption drivers: 41.9% industrial AI adoption in 2024, IoT sensor cost decline, 30% downtime reduction.
  • GSD Integration: Chipset pattern mapping (Paula I/O, Denise display, Event Dispatcher, Silicon Manifest). Camera-edge pipeline as GSD event source. Compressed features as observation log pattern.

Key Connections

Synsor Corp connects directly to BPS (bio-physics sensing — sensor technology and signal processing), SHE (smart home sensors and IoT integration), LED (signal processing and measurement), SYS (infrastructure and edge computing), MPC (mathematical computation for sensor data processing), GSD2 (GSD integration architecture and chipset patterns), and the PNW manufacturing ecosystem centered on Everett’s aerospace corridor.
