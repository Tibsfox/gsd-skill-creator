Mission Package

Space is not silent. Galaxy clusters contain plasma that propagates real sound waves. LIGO detects spacetime vibrations in the audio frequency range. The CXC "A Universe of Sound" program has produced over 35 sonifications, accumulating 4 million+ listens. This mission maps the science, the technique, the tools, and the human impact of turning telescope data into sound.

The Through-Line

In 2003, astronomers discovered that the supermassive black hole at the center of the Perseus galaxy cluster produces real acoustic pressure waves in the surrounding hot plasma — 57 octaves below B-flat above middle C. The CXC "A Universe of Sound" program, launched in 2020 by Kimberly Arcand, Matt Russo, and Andrew Santaguida, translates multi-wavelength telescope data into audio using inverse spectrogram techniques. Peer-reviewed research (Arcand et al. 2024, Frontiers in Communication) demonstrates that both blind/low-vision and sighted participants show high learning gains from sonification. The tools are open-source, the data is public, and the cosmos is accessible to anyone who can hear.
