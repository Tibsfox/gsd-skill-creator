Overview Physics Pipeline Objects Access Tools Mission Pack
In 2003, astronomers found that the supermassive black hole at the center of the Perseus galaxy cluster was producing real sound waves in the surrounding plasma — 57 octaves below B-flat above middle C. Space is not silent. It simply requires the right medium, the right instrument, and the willingness to listen. The CXC "A Universe of Sound" program translates telescope data into audio, making the cosmos accessible to anyone who can hear.

Research Modules

M1: Sonification Physics

Physics Track A · Sound in space

Sound propagation in galaxy cluster plasma. The Perseus calculation: 57 octaves below B-flat. LIGO gravitational wave acoustics: the chirp of GW150914. Real pressure waves vs. data translation.

M2: Chandra & Multi-Telescope Pipeline

Pipeline Sequential · CXC program

The CXC "A Universe of Sound" program (est. 2020). Core team: Arcand, Russo, Santaguida, Malec. Inverse spectrogram technique. Multi-wavelength data layering (M87 example).

M3: Landmark Sonified Objects

Objects Track A · Catalog

Perseus Cluster, M87, Galactic Center, Crab Nebula, Cassiopeia A, SN 1987A, LIGO GW150914, Pillars of Creation, Stephan's Quintet, Hubble Ultra Deep Field. 15+ documented sonifications.

M4: Accessibility & Human Perception

Access Track B · BLV research

Arcand et al. (2024) peer-reviewed study. Gould et al. (2024) transit detection accuracy. Dr. Wanda Diaz-Merced. Psychoacoustic foundations: temporal sensitivity, signal-in-noise, multidimensionality.

M5: Tools & Open Ecosystem

Tools Track B · Software survey

SYSTEM Sounds, sonoUno, STRAUSS, StarSound, xSonify, MAST Python Library, NASA Space Jam, SN1987A Interactive, Sonification Symphony. Open-source and research-grade tools.
