## 1. Arcand et al. 2024 Study
First peer-reviewed BLV sonification study (Frontiers in Communication, doi: 10.3389/fcomm.2024.1288896):
- Both BLV and sighted participants showed high learning gains
- Strong emotional responses across both groups
- Participants wanted to learn more after exposure
- BLV category spans wide range of sight loss; future work must recruit broadly

## 2. Transit Detection Accuracy
Gould et al. 2024 (Astronomical Journal, doi: 10.3847/1538-3881/ad24aa):
- Above-chance transit detection using auditory stimuli alone
- Visual stimuli had higher overall accuracy
- Complementary bias profiles suggest combined analysis could improve detection

## 3. Dr. Wanda Diaz-Merced
Blind astrophysicist who continued research using data sonification after losing eyesight. Analyzed gamma-ray burst light curves. TED Talk on the topic. Associated with Harvard-Smithsonian CfA at time of LIGO detection.

## 4. Psychoacoustic Foundations
- **Temporal pattern sensitivity:** Hearing is exquisitely sensitive to pattern variation over time
- **Signal-in-noise detection:** Focused hearing can identify desired signals amidst noise
- **Multidimensionality:** Sound carries more simultaneous dimensions than a 2D plot
- **Optimal range:** 4,000-6,000 Hz, threshold resolution 0.7-1.8% (Sulis 2024)
