Historical Timeline

From shingle mills on the river to a university town in the tech corridor.

1870s David Bothell settles near the Sammamish River; logging begins
1886 Town of Bothell established; shingle and lumber mills on the river
1909 Incorporated as a city; interurban railway connects to Seattle
1940s Lumber era fades; Bothell transitions to residential community
1970s Burke-Gilman Trail opens on former rail corridor
1990 University of Washington Bothell campus established on former wetland site
2000s Canyon Park tech corridor grows; AT&T, Philips, and tech firms cluster
2010s Downtown revitalization; Bothell Landing amphitheater, new restaurants

Parks & Green Spaces

Old-growth forest, river trails, and an amphitheater on the Sammamish.

Blyth Park

23 acresold-growth

23 acres of old-growth and mature second-growth forest in the heart of Bothell. Trails wind through towering Douglas fir and western red cedar, some over 200 years old. A ravine with a seasonal creek adds topographic drama. One of the last old-growth stands in the lower Sammamish River corridor.

Access: 104th Ave NE & Riverside Dr

North Creek Forest

64 acrestrails

A 64-acre urban forest with trails through wetlands, mixed forest, and riparian habitat along North Creek. The forest was preserved through community advocacy and provides critical habitat connectivity between the Sammamish River and the upland forests to the east.

Park at Bothell Landing

amphitheaterriver

A waterfront park at the historic center of Bothell with an amphitheater hosting concerts and events, a playground, historical buildings (including the Beckstrom log cabin), and direct access to the Sammamish River Trail. The park is the cultural heart of downtown Bothell.

Access: 9919 NE 180th St, downtown Bothell

Sammamish River Trail

10+ milesriver

A paved multi-use trail following the Sammamish River from Bothell south to Marymoor Park in Redmond, connecting to the Burke-Gilman Trail westward to Seattle. The combined trail system provides continuous car-free travel from Bothell to the UW campus and beyond — over 27 miles of connected trail.

Burke-Gilman Trail

regionalbike

The Burke-Gilman Trail passes through Bothell on its way from Seattle to the Sammamish River Trail. Built on a former railroad corridor, the paved trail is one of the most-used cycling and walking paths in the region. The Bothell segment connects downtown to the UW Bothell campus.

Wayne Golf Course

golfpublic

A public 18-hole golf course operated by the City of Bothell. The course sits in the Sammamish River valley with views of the surrounding forested hills. Open year-round, one of the more affordable public courses in the north King County area.

Waterways

Bothell is a river town. The Sammamish connects it to Lake Washington without needing a lake of its own.

Sammamish River

paddle routesalmon

The Sammamish River flows through Bothell on its way from Lake Sammamish to Lake Washington. The river is navigable by kayak and canoe, providing a paddle route from Bothell to the north end of Lake Washington. Salmon return to the river and North Creek, with restoration projects improving spawning habitat.

North Creek

tributaryrestoration

A major tributary of the Sammamish River flowing through eastern Bothell and the Canyon Park area. North Creek supports coho and chinook salmon habitat and has been the focus of significant riparian restoration work. The creek corridor provides green space through the tech district.

Notable Landmarks

UW Bothell / Cascadia College

universitycommunity college

The University of Washington Bothell and Cascadia College share a 135-acre campus east of downtown, near I-405 and SR-522. UW Bothell serves approximately 6,000 students with programs in computing and software systems, engineering, business, nursing, and education. Cascadia College (2-year) provides transfer pathways and workforce training. The campus incorporates a restored wetland as a living laboratory.

Canyon Park Tech Corridor

techemployment

The Canyon Park area along Bothell-Everett Highway hosts technology companies, biotech firms, and corporate offices. The corridor grew from the 1990s tech boom and continues to attract employers seeking north-end alternatives to the Eastside office market.

Country Village Shops

shoppinglocal

A collection of independent shops, boutiques, and eateries in a village-style setting near downtown Bothell. The center preserves a small-town retail feel in a region increasingly dominated by big-box stores.

Getting Here from Mukilteo

Bothell is southeast of Mukilteo, across the I-405 corridor — reachable via Lynnwood transfer.

By Bus (via Lynnwood City Center Station)

Route 117 from Mukilteo Ferry to Lynnwood City Center Station (~32 min). Transfer to Community Transit or Sound Transit routes serving the Bothell corridor via SR-522.

Total transit time: approximately 50–70 minutes with transfer. Service frequency is better on weekdays; weekend connections may require longer waits.

By Car

Mukilteo Speedway east to I-5 south, then I-405 south to Bothell (Exit 26 or SR-522). Approximately 20–30 minutes depending on traffic.
