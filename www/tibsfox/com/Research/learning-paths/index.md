# Learning Paths -- Your Guide to the Research Network

The research network contains over 190 projects across 13 Rosetta clusters. That's a lot of knowledge. Learning paths organize it into journeys -- structured sequences that take you from "I'm curious" to "I can do this" to "I can teach this."

There are two kinds of paths:

**Per-project learning pathways** go deep into a single research project. They organize all of that project's documents, try-sessions, study-guides, and deep-dives into a 5-level sequence from beginner to mastery. If you know what you want to learn, start here.

**Cross-project learning paths** go wide across multiple research projects. They weave documents from 4-5 projects into a unified 10-module journey around a theme. If you want to build a broader competence that connects multiple interests, start here.

You don't need to be an expert to start any path. You just need to start.

## Choosing Your Path

**If you know your interest:** Pick a per-project pathway and start at Level 1.

**If you want breadth:** Pick a cross-project path and start at Module 1.

**If you want depth:** Start with [The Systems Thinker](systems-thinker.md). It teaches you to see patterns across domains.

**If you want to push the frontier:** Start with [The Research Frontier](research-frontier.md). It teaches you to derive and test new hypotheses.

**If you're not sure:** Start with [The PNW Naturalist](pnw-naturalist.md). It touches five projects and the skills are immediately applicable to your daily life in the Pacific Northwest.

**If you want the most immediate impact:** Start with [The Community Responder](community-responder.md), Module 1. In 3 hours, you'll learn skills that could save a life.

**If you want to make things:** Start with [The Creative Maker](creative-maker.md). It goes from zero craft experience to multi-medium creative practice.

Every path connects to the others. The PNW Naturalist who learns to paint moves naturally into the Creative Maker. The Community Responder who learns woodworking can follow the WDC pathway to furniture making. The paths are a network, not a hierarchy.
