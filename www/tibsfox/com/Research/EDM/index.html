Historical Timeline

From timber camps on the shore to a vibrant arts district overlooking the Sound.

1860s George Brackett settles at the future townsite, builds logging operations
1884 Town platted as "Edmonds" after Senator George F. Edmunds (Vermont)
1890 Incorporated as a city — lumber and shingle mills drive the economy
1900 Great Northern Railway arrives, connecting Edmonds to regional trade
1947 Washington State Ferry service begins at Edmonds terminal (to Kingston)
1970s Downtown revitalization begins — lumber era fades, arts community grows
2003 Sounder commuter rail N Line service begins at Edmonds station
2017 Edmonds Waterfront Center opens, anchoring the renewed downtown

Parks & Green Spaces

Coastal parks, old-growth forest, and urban green spaces across 8.9 square miles.

Brackett's Landing

underwater parkbeach

Home to the Edmonds Underwater Park — a 27-acre marine preserve and one of the top dive sites in the Pacific Northwest. The park flanks the ferry terminal with tide pools, sandy beach, and views of the Olympic Mountains. Protected since 1970, it hosts lingcod, octopus, and over 200 species of marine life.

Access: Waterfront, adjacent to WSF Edmonds terminal

Marina Beach Park

beachoff-leash

A popular off-leash dog beach south of the marina, with sweeping views of Puget Sound, the Olympics, and passing ferries. Sandy shoreline with driftwood, picnic areas, and a paved path connecting to the waterfront trail system.

Access: Admiral Way, south of Edmonds Marina

Yost Park

old-growthtrails

48 acres of second-growth and old-growth forest with a trail network following Shell Creek down to the beach at Puget Sound. The ravine canopy includes western red cedar, Douglas fir, and bigleaf maple. One of the few remaining urban old-growth stands in south Snohomish County.

Access: 96th Ave W, trails to beach

Olympic Beach

beachviewpoint

Public beach north of the ferry dock with unobstructed views of the Olympic Range. Low-tide walking exposes tide pools and sandy flats. The beach connects to the Edmonds waterfront promenade, linking Brackett's Landing to the marina district.

Access: Railroad Ave, north of ferry terminal

Edmonds City Park

sportscommunity

Edmonds' central community park with sports fields, playground, spray park, and picnic shelters. Hosts community events, summer concerts, and the annual Fourth of July celebration. Surrounded by residential neighborhoods within walking distance of downtown.

Access: 6th Ave N & Pine St

Sierra Park

trailsneighborhood

A quiet neighborhood park in the Westgate area with wooded trails, play equipment, and a covered picnic area. The forested setting provides a sheltered walking loop through mixed conifers and deciduous understory.

Access: Sierra Dr, Westgate neighborhood

Lakes & Waterways

Edmonds fronts Puget Sound and shares one significant freshwater lake with its neighbor to the east.

Lake Ballinger

lakefishing

A 104-acre lake shared with Mountlake Terrace, stocked with rainbow trout and home to largemouth bass, perch, and catfish. Public access via the Lake Ballinger Park on the southeast shore. The lake drains via McAleer Creek to Lake Washington. Swimming, fishing, and small-craft boating.

Puget Sound Waterfront

saltwaterferry

Edmonds' 3+ miles of Puget Sound shoreline include the Kingston ferry terminal, a working marina with over 600 slips, a public fishing pier, and continuous waterfront promenade. The marina hosts both recreational and commercial vessels, with views across the Sound to Kingston and the Olympics.

Notable Landmarks

Edmonds Center for the Arts

performing arts

A 700-seat performing arts venue in the restored 1930s-era Edmonds School District building. Hosts concerts, theater, dance, and community events year-round. The renovation preserved the original art deco exterior while creating a modern performance space.

Edmonds-Kingston Ferry

WSFcrossing

Washington State Ferries route connecting Edmonds to Kingston on the Kitsap Peninsula. A 30-minute crossing with views of the Cascades, Olympics, and Whidbey Island. One of the busiest routes in the WSF system, carrying vehicles and walk-on passengers.

Downtown Edmonds

shopsdining

A walkable downtown core along Main Street and 5th Avenue with independent shops, galleries, restaurants, and tasting rooms. The Salish Sea brewing and wine scene anchors the nightlife. Art Walk events and the Edmonds Farmers Market bring the community together regularly.

Getting Here from Mukilteo

Edmonds is 7 miles south of Mukilteo along the waterfront — the closest Amtrak station to MUK.

Sounder N Line (Commuter Rail)

Everett → Mukilteo → Edmonds → King Street Station (Seattle)

Direct service, one stop south from Mukilteo. Approximately 10 minutes. Weekday peak hours only (4 morning southbound, 4 evening northbound trains). Edmonds station is at the waterfront, steps from the ferry terminal.

Amtrak Cascades (Intercity Rail)

Vancouver BC → Bellingham → Mt Vernon → Stanwood → Everett → Edmonds → Seattle → Tacoma → Portland → Eugene

Edmonds is the closest Amtrak stop to Mukilteo. Two daily trains each direction on the Cascades corridor, connecting the entire Pacific coast from British Columbia to Oregon. The Edmonds station sits directly on the waterfront — the same platform serves both Sounder and Amtrak.

  • Southbound to Seattle: ~30 minutes
  • Southbound to Portland: ~3.5 hours
  • Northbound to Everett: ~20 minutes
  • Northbound to Vancouver BC: ~4 hours

Amtrak Coast Starlight

Seattle → Tacoma → Portland → Sacramento → Oakland → San Jose → Los Angeles

Daily long-distance service connecting Seattle to California. Board at Seattle King Street Station via Sounder N Line from Edmonds (~30 minutes to King Street).

Community Transit Bus

Route 116: Edmonds → Lynnwood Transit Center. Connects to Link light rail (1 Line and 2 Line) at Lynnwood City Center Station.

Route 130: Local service connecting Edmonds neighborhoods.

Weekend service available but less frequent. Weekday buses run approximately every 30 minutes.
