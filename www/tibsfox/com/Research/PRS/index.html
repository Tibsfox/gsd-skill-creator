A polyrhythm is not two rhythms played at the same time. It is one rhythm with two clocks. The 50/60 Hz power grid is a planetary polyrhythm that shaped television, broadcast, and display technology for eight decades. The West African bell pattern is a 7-in-12 Euclidean rhythm that traveled the Middle Passage and became the clave, the jazz ride cymbal, and the backbone of every groove-based music on earth. The same mathematics — ratio theory, LCM, phase coupling — governs both. Rhythm connects every domain because rhythm IS the domain.

Research Modules

M1: Polyrhythm Theory

Theory Track A · Foundations

Ratio theory, resultant patterns, perceptual grouping and streaming, LCM composite cycles, irrational rhythm, entropy analysis, notation systems, Euclidean pedagogy. The mathematical and cognitive framework for everything that follows.

M2: Cross-Rhythm Mathematics

Math Track A · Geometry & combinatorics

Euclidean rhythm theorem, maximally even sets, necklace theory, Toussaint's clock diagrams, rhythm canons and tiling, modular arithmetic, spectral analysis (DFT), distance metrics, algorithmic composition. From Bjorklund to TidalCycles.

M3: Clave & West African Traditions

Ethnomusicology Track B · Lineage

The standard pattern, Ewe agbadza, Yoruba bata, Akan adowa, drum ensemble architecture, the Middle Passage diaspora, son and rumba clave, Brazilian samba and maracatu, Caribbean traditions, the clave in jazz and pop.

M4: Hemiola & Metric Modulation

Perception Track B · Temporal illusion

The 3:2 pivot, Baroque to Romantic hemiola, African hemiolic structures, Elliott Carter's metric modulation, modulation chains, rhythmic Necker cubes, time signatures across cultures, aksak meters, phase shifting (Reich).

M5: Rhythm & Frequency (Hz)

Signal Track C · The continuum

The rhythm-pitch continuum, the 20 Hz boundary, polyrhythm as frequency ratio, 50/60 Hz infrastructure polyrhythm, SMPTE drop-frame as Euclidean rhythm, BPM clusters, electronic music grids, sub-bass perception, groove physics, tuning systems.

M6: Polyrhythm as Synchronization Protocol

Sync Track C · Systems

Coupled oscillators, biological clocks, firefly synchronization, network clock distribution, MIDI clock and Ableton Link, consensus mechanisms, phase-locked loops, swarm behavior, the conductor problem, rhythm as error correction.
