Mission Package

Backyard astronomy is the oldest distributed sensor network humanity ever built. Millions of telescopes pointed at the same sky, on six continents, speaking incompatible protocols. This mission wires them together — anchored to Yuri's Night as the annual convergence event where the world's largest distributed science experiment happens every April 12.

The Through-Line

On that first orbit in 1961, Gagarin saw the whole planet at once. Every April 12, we can look back — millions of eyes pointed at the same sky, each seeing their own slice of the cosmos. The Global Telescope Mesh does not add new eyes. It connects the ones that are already open. The spaces between telescopes are where the science lives. The Amiga Principle at planetary scale: extraordinary output through connecting existing capability via elegant architecture.

“The Earth is blue... How wonderful. It is amazing.” — Yuri Gagarin, April 12, 1961
