Architecture

The render farm is built from 42 identical 4U rack units, each a Mercury GPU400-TX01 containing dual Xeon X5570 CPUs and 8 ATI V9800 GPU accelerators. The design is pure horizontal scale — every rack is a self-contained compute node that connects to the same network fabric.

The Mercury GPU400-TX01

Mercury Computer Systems

Mercury Computer Systems (now Mercury Systems) specialized in GPU-accelerated compute for defense, intelligence, and scientific applications. The GPU400-TX01 was their 4U GPU compute server, designed to pack maximum GPU density into a standard rack unit. Each unit held 8 GPU accelerators in a custom PCIe backplane with liquid cooling capability. The platform was used for signal processing, radar simulation, and molecular dynamics — and, hypothetically, fractal rendering at datacenter scale.

The same principle — embarrassingly parallel workloads across commodity hardware — scales from $2,500 to $10.5M. The innovation is not in architecture but in recognizing that GPU stream processors are the natural unit of parallel compute for this class of problem.

Computing Context: July 2011

The TOP500 #1 in June 2011 was Japan's K Computer at 8.16 PFLOPS (LINPACK). At 9.1 PFLOPS peak, the render farm would have been competitive on raw throughput, though LINPACK performance would be lower. The ATI V9800 (Evergreen architecture, 40nm) was ATI/AMD's professional compute card. GPU computing was transitioning from niche to mainstream, with NVIDIA's Tesla and AMD's FireStream competing for the HPC market. The $10.5M price tag was fraction of the K Computer's $1.2B.

Where This Fits

The render farm is the apex of the archive — the most expensive, most powerful, and most ambitious design. It represents the logical endpoint of the white-box philosophy: even at datacenter scale, the system is composed of identical commodity units replicated 42 times. No custom silicon, no proprietary interconnects. Just racks of GPU accelerators and standard Xeon hosts.

It is also, notably, still built from off-the-shelf components. The Mercury GPU400-TX01 was a catalog product. The principle holds at every scale in the archive.
