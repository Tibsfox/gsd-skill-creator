Computing Context: March 2008

Intel's Xeon E5400 series (Harpertown, 45nm) had just launched. Quad-core was the new frontier for server CPUs. The Tyan S2692ANR was a dual-socket LGA771 board — workstation-class hardware at commodity prices. 8GB of RAM was substantial for the era; most consumer systems shipped with 2-4GB.

Where This Fits

At $140/core, this sits near the efficiency sweet spot for its era. Compare to the same-day Gaming Extreme at $646/core — the workstation delivers the same core count at 78% less cost per core by choosing Xeon E5410 over Core 2 Extreme QX9775. The trade-off: no discrete GPU, no gaming. Pure floating point.
