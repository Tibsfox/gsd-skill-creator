The 2026 DRAM Crisis

The single most impactful factor on all 2026 builds. Memory cost is the story of this moment.

The Numbers

DDR5 desktop: 32GB DDR5-6000 kits: $80 (mid-2025) → $400-430 (Apr 2026) = 5x increase
DDR5 server: 64GB ECC RDIMMs: ~$250 (early 2025) → ~$1,000 (Apr 2026) = 4x increase
128GB/256GB modules: Prices roughly doubled from 2025 levels
Samsung: Raised DDR5 prices 60% in late 2025

Root Cause & Forecast

Root cause: AI datacenter buildout consuming all available DRAM fab capacity. Every H100/B200 server rack needs 10-20TB of HBM and DDR5, diverting wafer starts from consumer DRAM.

Forecast: Prices expected to remain elevated through 2026, with potential relief in 2027 as new Samsung/SK Hynix/Micron fabs come online.

At 125 TFLOPS FP32, 96GB GDDR7 ECC, and $8,000, it sits in the sweet spot between consumer RTX (cheap but small VRAM) and datacenter H100/B200 (expensive, NVLink-dependent).

3. A $655K Rack Delivers 3.77 PFLOPS

Enough to have ranked #1 on TOP500 as recently as 2012. For $971K you get 4.5 PFLOPS — approximately the compute power that powered the entire Human Genome Project.

4. Memory Is the New Bottleneck

In every tier, DDR5 RAM has become the disproportionate cost driver. GPU $/TFLOP has plummeted, CPU $/core has plummeted, but $/GB of RAM has spiked. This inverts decades of trends where storage and RAM got cheaper faster than compute.

5. Reference Platform Is a Closeout

The MZ72-HB0 + 2x 7T83 should not be the basis for new system design. SP3/DDR4 is two generations behind, and the Zen 5 EPYC 9755 delivers 2x the IPC in a single socket for less money.
