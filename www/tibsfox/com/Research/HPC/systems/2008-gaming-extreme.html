In Their Own Words

"bad ass gaming.. omg expensive" — LJ #45973, March 18, 2008

The comment thread included a Mac vs PC comparison: a comparable Mac Pro configuration came to $6,099 versus the PC at $4,211 (without GPUs). The Apple tax was real and quantified.

The Core 2 Extreme QX9775 was Intel's top-bin desktop quad-core at 3.2GHz — $1,504 each. The GeForce 8800 Ultra was NVIDIA's halo product. This build maximizes per-thread performance rather than thread count, the opposite philosophy of the same-day Budget Workstation.

Computing Context: March 2008

The GeForce 8800 Ultra was the last of the G80 architecture cards, launched in May 2007 at $829 MSRP. By March 2008 it had dropped to $657. CUDA had launched with the G80, but GPU compute was still in its infancy — these cards were specced for gaming, not GPGPU. The dual-GPU SLI configuration here foreshadows the 3-Way SLI build that would come 14 months later.

Where This Fits

At $646/core, this is the most expensive per-core build in the 2008 sprint (excluding the $1,355/core High Memory server). The premium buys clock speed (3.2GHz vs 2.33GHz on the workstation) and GPU capability. This is the only 2008 build with discrete graphics — it marks the first time GPU entered the system design vocabulary of this archive.
