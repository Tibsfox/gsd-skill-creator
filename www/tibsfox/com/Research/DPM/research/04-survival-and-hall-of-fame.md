# Reinvention and Persistence (2001-2023)

> **Domain:** Music History / Electronic Music
> **Module:** 4 -- Exciter to Memento Mori, and the Question of Endurance
> **Through-line:** *The last two decades of Depeche Mode are a study in what happens when a band outlives its era without becoming a nostalgia act. Each album was a statement that the project was still alive, still evolving, still unwilling to simply replay Violator for stadium audiences.*

## Exciter and the Quiet Reset

*Exciter* (2001) was produced by Mark Bell (LFO). The album was deliberately sparse -- stripped-back arrangements, minimal layering, emphasis on space and texture. After the crisis of the mid-1990s, *Exciter* was a convalescence album: proof of life, not proof of dominance.

"Dream On" opened with acoustic guitar, a first for DM. "I Feel Loved" was a trance-influenced pulse. The album divided fans -- some heard restraint, others heard exhaustion. It reached #8 in the US, #9 in the UK. Respectable, not triumphant.

The Exciter Tour sold well. DM's live audience had become self-sustaining -- not dependent on album cycles or radio play but on the accumulated power of four decades of devotion. The concert experience was the product; the albums were the renewal.

## Playing the Angel: Return to Form

*Playing the Angel* (2005) was the comeback. Produced by Ben Hillier, it restored the aggressive edge that *Exciter* had softened. Gahan co-wrote three songs -- his first DM songwriting credits -- signaling a new creative balance.

"Precious" combined pop structure with electronic muscle. "A Pain That I'm Used To" was industrial in spirit. "John the Revelator" was as close to gospel rock as DM would ever get. The album reached #1 in 19 countries.

The Tour of the Universe (2009-2010) accompanying *Sounds of the Universe* became DM's largest -- 97 shows, 2.7 million tickets. They were now a touring institution.

---

## Sounds of the Universe

*Sounds of the Universe* (2009) was another Hillier production, recorded at DM's own studio in Santa Barbara. The band had invested in vintage synthesizers -- the album prominently featured analog instruments from the 1970s and 1980s, creating a warm texture distinct from the digital precision of *Violator* or *Ultra*.

"Wrong" was the lead single -- fast, angular, aggressive. The album sold over 1 million copies in its first week globally. Critical reception was mixed, but commercial performance demonstrated that DM's audience was loyal beyond any single album cycle.

## Delta Machine and Spirit

*Delta Machine* (2013) leaned into the blues-electronic fusion that "Personal Jesus" had pioneered 23 years earlier. The title referenced the Mississippi Delta -- Gore explicitly connecting electronic music to its spiritual ancestor in the blues. "Heaven" was a ballad that earned comparisons to "Enjoy the Silence." "Should Be Higher" featured a choir.

*Spirit* (2017) was explicitly political -- rare for DM. "Where's the Revolution" addressed populism and political disengagement. "Going Backwards" was environmental commentary. The album reached #1 in several countries and #5 in the US. DM had never been a political band, and the pivot felt both genuine and slightly uncomfortable.

## The Loss of Andrew Fletcher

Andrew Fletcher -- co-founder, keyboardist, the self-described "person who makes sure everything is alright" -- passed away on May 26, 2022, at age 60. His role in DM had always been the least visible but perhaps the most essential: he was the stabilizer, the mediator, the one who kept the band functional through decades of creative tension.

Fletcher's death raised the question of DM's continuation. Gore and Gahan chose to proceed, dedicating the next album to his memory.

### Cross-references
- **B52** faced the same question after Ricky Wilson's passing -- how does a band continue after a foundational member is gone?
- **TKH** dissolved rather than continue, making DM's choice to persist the less common path

## Memento Mori

*Memento Mori* (2023) was DM's fifteenth studio album and their first as a duo. The title -- Latin for "remember that you will die" -- was both a dedication to Fletcher and a statement of artistic intent. Produced by James Ford (Simian Mobile Disco, Arctic Monkeys), the album was meditative, autumnal, and unexpectedly warm.

"Ghosts Again" became a Top 10 hit across Europe. "Wagging Tongue" and "My Favourite Stranger" demonstrated that Gore's songwriting had not dimmed. The album debuted at #1 in the UK and several European countries, #14 in the US.

The Memento Mori World Tour (2023-2024) sold over 3 million tickets across 77 shows. The production was a tribute to the band's entire career -- visuals spanning from *Black Celebration* through *Memento Mori*, with Fletcher acknowledged at every show.
