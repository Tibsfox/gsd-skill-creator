Systems Administration

From 1950s Mainframes to Self-Healing Infrastructure — 70+ Years of Keeping Systems Running — Artemis II Mission Research
Overview History Linux Networking Storage Monitoring Security Automation Cloud AIOps Practice
From the first operators loading punch cards into IBM mainframes in the 1950s to autonomous AI agents remediating production incidents in closed-loop pipelines, systems administration has undergone one of the most dramatic professional transformations in computing history. This research digest traces that entire arc — seven decades of practice, 10 modules, 60,186 words of deep research, 640 cross-references across a 249-project corpus. Deep research expansion (Session 007) added 8x content depth with real configurations, case studies, and primary source citations across every module. Along the way, we lost our only professional guild: LOPSA dissolved in December 2025, leaving sysadmins as one of the few engineering disciplines without a dedicated professional organization.
