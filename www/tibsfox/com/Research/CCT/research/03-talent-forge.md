## The John Oliver Retention Failure

In summer 2013, Stewart took a twelve-week hiatus. Correspondent John Oliver guest-hosted to critical acclaim. Comedy Central had not locked Oliver to a contract. HBO observed Oliver's performance and offered him creative control and his own show.

*Last Week Tonight with John Oliver* premiered on HBO on April 27, 2014. Comedy Central lost its best succession candidate precisely because the guest-hosting period functioned as an HBO audition. This case is studied in business literature as a cautionary talent-management example.

## The Colbert Report as Architecture

The Colbert Report deserves analysis because it was a scheduled architecture decision. Comedy Central placed it at 11:30 PM immediately following TDS, creating a two-hour satirical news block. Stewart produced it through Busboy Productions. The two shows shared writers and studio space with explicit on-air interaction. This programming block was Comedy Central's peak linear cable offering.
