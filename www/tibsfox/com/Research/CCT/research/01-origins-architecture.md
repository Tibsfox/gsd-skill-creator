# Origins & Architecture (1989-1997)

## The Dual-Failure Origin Story

On November 15, 1989, Time Warner -- through its HBO subsidiary -- launched The Comedy Channel as the first cable network dedicated exclusively to comedy. The channel aired twelve hours per day, with programming originating from HBO Downtown Studios at 120 East 23rd Street in Manhattan. Initial subscriber base was approximately 4.2 million.

On April 1, 1990, Viacom (owner of MTV, VH1, and Nickelodeon) launched a rival channel, HA! TV Comedy Network, with approximately 4.0 million subscribers. HA!'s format relied heavily on licensed reruns -- including Saturday Night Live seasons 6-16 acquired from NBC.

Both channels faced the same structural problem: cable system operators saw no reason to carry two comedy channels. After initial outlays of close to $50 million for both ventures, they were primarily operating at a loss.

## The Merger

On April 1, 1991, the two networks merged in a 50/50 joint venture between Viacom and Time Warner, launching as CTV: The Comedy Network with 12.5 million combined subscribers. On June 1, 1991, the channel renamed itself Comedy Central to avoid confusion with Canadian broadcaster CTV.

In April 2003, Viacom bought out AOL Time Warner's 50% stake for $1.23 billion, making Comedy Central a wholly-owned Viacom property.

## Early Programming Identity

Comedy Central's early programming identity was shaped by three pillars:

- **Mystery Science Theater 3000** (MST3K): Won a Peabody Award in 1994, received two Emmy nominations and six CableACE nominations
- **Politically Incorrect with Bill Maher**: Became CC's flagship topical show before defecting to ABC in fall 1996
- **Convention coverage**: In 1996, CC dispatched satirical correspondents to the Republican National Convention -- a precursor to TDS's "Indecision" specials

## The Daily Show Launch

When Politically Incorrect moved to ABC, Comedy Central needed a replacement for daily political commentary. The result was *The Daily Show*, premiering July 22, 1996 with Craig Kilborn as host. Co-created by Lizz Winstead and Madeleine Smithberg.

## South Park

In 1997, Comedy Central premiered *South Park* (creators Trey Parker and Matt Stone), which would become the channel's longest-running scripted series (still airing as of 2026, season 28). South Park established that Comedy Central could produce culturally significant original content beyond talk and sketch formats.
