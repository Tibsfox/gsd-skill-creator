When you stand at dusk on the UW Bothell campus and watch sixteen thousand crows pour out of the sky like a river of dark intelligence, something ancient stirs. These are not just birds. They are minds — each one carrying a map of faces, a memory of slights, a vocabulary of calls that researchers are only beginning to decode.

Natural Science

Taxonomy & Identification

Module 1

American Crow, Common Raven, and the former Northwestern Crow. The 2020 AOS taxonomic merger, Slager et al. genomic analysis, field identification keys, subspecies variation across the PNW.

Cognition & Intelligence

Module 2

Convergent evolution of corvid and primate intelligence. Tool use, facial recognition (17-year memory), episodic-like memory, theory of mind, and the “feathered apes” framing.

Ecology & Habitat

Module 3

Niche partitioning: crows in the lowlands, ravens in the mountains. Diet ecology, breeding strategies, territorial dynamics across four PNW habitat zones.

Culture & Conservation

Social Behavior & Communication

Module 4

The UW Bothell mega-roost and its 2023–2025 migration. Vocalization research, individual voice signatures, cooperative breeding, raven fission-fusion dynamics.

Cultural Significance

Module 5

Nation-specific Raven traditions: Tlingit, Haida, Tsimshian, Kwakwaka’wakw, Heiltsuk, Coast Salish, Koyukon. Bill Reid’s sculpture. Formline art. OCAP/CARE principles.

Threats & Conservation

Module 6

West Nile virus: near-100% laboratory fatality, 45% population decline 1999–2020. The dilution effect. Raven impacts on species of concern. Urban ecology and coexistence.

The Through-Line

The spaces between are where meaning lives. Between the crow and the raven lies a landscape-scale division of ecological labor. Between Tlingit creator-Raven and Tlingit trickster-Raven lies the full spectrum of what it means to bring a world into being through accident, appetite, and grace. Between the Northwestern Crow that existed for 160 years in our field guides and the American Crow it always was lies a lesson about the boxes we build and the organisms that refuse to stay in them.
