Research Scope

  • Taxonomy & Identification: American Crow (*Corvus brachyrhynchos*), Common Raven (*Corvus corax principalis*), former Northwestern Crow (*C. b. caurinus*). The 2020 AOS merger based on Slager et al. genomic analysis across 900 km of hybrid zone. Field identification keys: size, bill shape, tail shape, vocalizations, flight style, habitat.
  • Cognition & Intelligence: Convergent evolution with primate intelligence. New Caledonian crow metatool problems, PNW shellfish-dropping behavior, Marzluff’s 17-year facial recognition study, Clayton & Dickinson episodic-like memory, Bugnyar raven theory of mind, corvid consciousness assessment.
  • Ecology & Habitat: Landscape-scale niche partitioning — crows dominate urban lowlands, ravens hold mountains and old-growth. Diet ecology, cooperative breeding in crows, lifelong monogamy in ravens, territorial dynamics across Cascades divide.
  • Social Behavior & Communication: UW Bothell mega-roost (up to 16,000 birds), 2023–2025 migration to Redmond, pre-roost staging, unihemispheric sleep, Wacker/Abadi acoustic research, 30+ raven call types, displacement communication.
  • Cultural Significance: Nation-specific Raven traditions: Tlingit dual creator/trickster, Haida Eagle/Raven moiety and Bill Reid’s sculpture, Tsimshian Transformer, Kwakwaka’wakw ceremonial masks, Coast Salish artistic traditions, Koyukon and Inuit subarctic traditions. OCAP/CARE principles.
  • Threats & Conservation: West Nile virus (near-100% laboratory fatality, 45% national decline 1999–2020, dilution effect). Raven predation on Marbled Murrelet, Snowy Plover, Sage-Grouse. Urban crow-raven competitive dynamics. Climate change habitat boundary shifts.

Key Connections

CRV connects to AVI (Birds — complete PNW avian taxonomy including corvid order), MAM (Mammals — wolf-raven ecological partnerships), ECO (Living Systems — trophic cascades and niche partitioning), TIBS (Animal Speak — indigenous knowledge systems and respectful documentation), COL (Columbia Valley — lowland crow habitat), CAS (Cascade Range — alpine raven territory), FFA (Fur & Feathers — wildlife cultural symbolism), and LKR (Lion King Roots — mythological animal traditions across cultures).
