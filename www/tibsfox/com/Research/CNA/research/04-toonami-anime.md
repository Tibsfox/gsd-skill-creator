# Toonami & the Anime Bridge (1997-Present)

## Toonami's Founding

Toonami launched March 17, 1997 as a weekday afternoon action-animation block on Cartoon Network, hosted by the AI character TOM (voiced by Steve Blum). The block's mission was explicit: bring action-oriented animation -- particularly anime -- to mainstream American cable audiences.

## Dragon Ball Z and the Mainstreaming of Anime

*Dragon Ball Z* became Toonami's signature series and arguably the single most important show in mainstreaming anime in the United States. Its serialized storytelling, dramatic fights, and character development drew audiences far beyond the traditional children's animation demographic.

## Cowboy Bebop's Midnight Premiere

On September 2, 2001, *Cowboy Bebop* premiered on Adult Swim at midnight -- the same day Adult Swim launched. The show's uncensored late-night broadcast represented a watershed: anime presented as adult entertainment rather than children's programming. Cowboy Bebop's jazz-infused soundtrack, mature themes, and cinematic quality demonstrated that anime could compete with (and surpass) Western adult animation on its own terms.

## Cancellation and Revival

Toonami was cancelled in 2008 as part of broader Cartoon Network programming changes during the CN Real era. Fan outcry was significant.

On April 1, 2012, Adult Swim aired a surprise Toonami block as an April Fools' joke. The response was so overwhelming that Toonami was revived as a permanent Saturday night Adult Swim block on May 26, 2012, where it continues to air.

## Original Anime Commissions

In its Adult Swim era, Toonami has moved beyond curation to production:

- *Fena: Pirate Princess* (2021): Co-produced with Crunchyroll
- *Blade Runner: Black Lotus* (2021): Co-produced with Crunchyroll and Alcon Entertainment
- *Rick and Morty: The Anime* (2024): Adult Swim-commissioned anime adaptation

These commissions represent a new phase: Toonami as a US commissioner of anime content, not merely a licensee and broadcaster.

## The Cultural Bridge Mechanism

Toonami and Adult Swim's anime programming accomplished something specific: they bridged the gap between anime as niche import and anime as core component of American pop culture. The mechanism was:

1. **Accessible scheduling** (afternoon, then late-night)
2. **Quality curation** (selecting shows with broad appeal)
3. **Cultural framing** (TOM's introductions positioned anime as worthy of attention)
4. **Uncensored presentation** (Adult Swim airings preserved original content)
