# Foundations & Corporate Genealogy (1986-2001)

## The Library Acquisition Strategy

Ted Turner's animation empire was built through two pivotal acquisitions. In March 1986, Turner Broadcasting System purchased MGM/UA from Kirk Kerkorian for approximately $1.5 billion. Financial pressure forced Turner to sell back the MGM name, but he retained the entire pre-May 1986 MGM film and animation library -- including Tom and Jerry, Droopy, and the pre-1948 Warner Bros. Looney Tunes and Merrie Melodies library.

In 1991, Turner acquired Hanna-Barbera Productions for $320 million, outbidding MCA Inc. and Hallmark Cards. Hanna-Barbera's catalog comprised over 3,000 half-hour episodes -- The Flintstones, Yogi Bear, The Jetsons, Scooby-Doo, and hundreds of others. Combined with the MGM and Warner libraries, Turner now held a self-sustaining animation archive capable of supporting a 24-hour dedicated channel.

## Channel Launch (October 1, 1992)

Betty Cohen, previously Senior VP at Turner Network Television, was appointed founding president. The channel launched October 1, 1992 at noon Eastern Time with the 1946 Looney Tunes short "The Great Piggy Bank Robbery." At launch: approximately 2 million households through 233 cable systems.

Turner modeled Cartoon Network explicitly on CNN: single genre, 24 hours, no compromise. Internal research found 46% of cable cartoon viewers were children and 44% were adults.

## The Turner-Time Warner Merger (1996)

The October 10, 1996 merger gave Cartoon Network access to Warner Bros. Animation's post-1950 catalog. This coincided with Cartoon Network Studios' formal establishment and the transition from library-only to active original production.
