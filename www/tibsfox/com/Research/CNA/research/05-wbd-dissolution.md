# WBD Dissolution & Current Crisis (2022-2026)

## The Discovery-WarnerMedia Merger

In April 2022, AT&T spun off WarnerMedia, which merged with Discovery to form Warner Bros. Discovery under CEO David Zaslav. The merger was driven by debt reduction and streaming consolidation, not creative strategy.

## The Content Purge

Beginning in 2022, WBD systematically removed content from streaming platforms:

- *Infinity Train* (2019-2021): Removed from HBO Max despite critical acclaim
- *Summer Camp Island* (2018-2023): Removed mid-production
- *OK K.O.! Let's Be Heroes*: Removed from all platforms
- Multiple other Cartoon Network originals made unavailable for streaming

The removals were driven by accounting strategy -- removing content from the balance sheet as a tax write-off -- rather than audience data or creative evaluation.

## Institutional Restructuring

- **October 2022:** Cartoon Network Studios merged with Warner Bros. Animation, eliminating CNS as a distinct creative entity
- **Ongoing 2022-2025:** Multiple rounds of layoffs at Atlanta studios
- **August 2024:** CartoonNetwork.com shut down entirely
- **Cartoon Network's broadcast window** reduced to 6am-5pm; Adult Swim expanded to 5pm-6am

## What Has Been Lost

The WBD era is instructive because it dismantles the architecture while keeping the nodes:

- The shows still exist (mostly)
- The channel still broadcasts
- But CartoonNetwork.com -- the connective tissue between generations of fans and their shows -- is gone
- The Williams Street creative culture that required physical separation from corporate is gone
- The CNS development team that specialized in original voices is merged into a franchise-delivery operation

You can remove the spaces between and still have nodes. But you no longer have architecture.

## The Current State (2026)

As of early 2026:

- Cartoon Network continues to broadcast but with a reduced original programming slate
- Adult Swim maintains its block with Rick and Morty as the anchor franchise
- Toonami continues on Saturday nights
- No significant new original series development has been announced for Cartoon Network
- The institutional knowledge and creative culture that produced three decades of landmark animation is largely dispersed

The question facing Cartoon Network and Adult Swim is existential: can the channels survive as creative institutions when the institutional conditions that made them exceptional have been systematically eliminated?
