PNW Commercial Helium Distribution Network

Overview

The Pacific Northwest I-5 corridor has an established network of industrial gas suppliers providing helium in various purities and quantities. Understanding this existing infrastructure is essential for evaluating any helium supply chain improvements in the region.

Primary Suppliers

Central Welding Supply

The largest regional independent provider with over 30 locations across Washington, Oregon, and Alaska. Their 10,000 sq ft fill plant in Smokey Point, WA serves as a central hub for high-purity and specialty gas distribution throughout the region.

  • Seattle: 5401 4th Ave S
  • Portland: 2313 NE Martin Luther King Jr Blvd
  • Coverage: Washington, Oregon, Alaska
  • Capabilities: High-purity gas, specialty gas mixtures, cylinder and bulk delivery

Airgas (Air Liquide)

An Air Liquide company with extensive storefront presence nationally and regionally. Offers high-pressure gas and liquid cylinders, as well as specialized isotope supply including Helium-3.

  • Seattle: 4401 Airport Way S
  • Portland: 7232 SE 82nd Ave
  • Spokane: 4230 E Trent Ave
  • Parent company note: Air Liquide recently invested $60 million in Phoenix, AZ specifically for ultra-high purity hydrogen, helium, and CO2 supply to semiconductor fabs — demonstrating the parent company’s active investment in fab gas infrastructure.

Industrial Source

An independent distributor focused specifically on the I-5 corridor with seven locations across Oregon.

  • Locations: Portland, Salem, Eugene/Springfield, Medford
  • Significance: Already optimized for I-5 corridor logistics with a spoke network covering western Oregon

OXARC (Meritus Gas Partners)

Part of the Meritus Gas Partners network, providing industrial gases including helium across the eastern Washington and Portland metro regions.

  • Spokane: 4003 E Broadway Ave
  • Gresham/Portland: 19310 NE San Rafael St
  • Coverage: Extends the regional network east along I-90

Linde Welding Gas & Equipment

Offers industrial supply and helium services through localized service centers.

  • Seattle: 944 W Leary Wy

Supply Chain Context (March 2026)

As of late March 2026, global helium markets are experiencing significant volatility following geopolitical disruptions affecting Middle Eastern exports. This has increased the strategic importance of domestic supply chains and regional distribution networks.

PNW distributors have historically relied on helium sourced from: - Federal Helium Reserve (Amarillo, TX) — now privatized as of 2024 - Riley Ridge (Wyoming) — produces approximately half of remaining US supply - Various smaller US producers — growing in importance as traditional sources contract

The existing distribution network provides the “last mile” infrastructure for any regional helium supply chain improvements. These companies have the relationships, the delivery routes, the safety certifications, and the customer base already in place. Any new helium production or purification in the PNW would naturally integrate with — not replace — this existing network.

Observations

  1. The I-5 corridor is already served by a mature distribution network
  2. Smokey Point is the largest regional fill/processing facility
  3. East-west coverage extends to Spokane via OXARC and Airgas
  4. Seven Oregon locations (Industrial Source) provide dense southern corridor coverage
  5. No regional purification/liquefaction — all suppliers currently purchase from out-of-region sources
  6. The gap is upstream, not downstream — distribution works, but the region has no local production or purification capacity
