## Scenario Analysis

### Scenario A: Crisis Pricing Holds 3+ Years

| Year | Revenue | COGS | Gross Margin | Operating Exp | Net |
|------|---------|------|-------------|--------------|-----|
| 1 | $3.5M | $1.5M | $2.0M | $800K | $1.2M |
| 2 | $5.5M | $2.2M | $3.3M | $900K | $2.4M |
| 3 | $6.5M | $2.5M | $4.0M | $1.0M | $3.0M |

**Payback: <2 years.** Patronage dividends begin Year 2. Expansion capital available Year 3.

### Scenario B: Qatar Resumes in 18 Months (Prices Normalize)

| Year | Revenue | COGS | Gross Margin | Operating Exp | Net |
|------|---------|------|-------------|--------------|-----|
| 1 | $3.5M | $1.5M | $2.0M | $800K | $1.2M |
| 2 | $2.7M (prices drop) | $1.8M | $0.9M | $900K | $0 (breakeven) |
| 3 | $2.7M | $1.6M | $1.1M | $900K | $200K |

**Payback: ~4 years.** Tight but viable. The co-op survives on Research Grade premium pricing and recycling service contracts. Grant funding reduces debt service burden.

### Scenario C: Worst Case — Market Collapse + Equipment Issues

| Year | Revenue | COGS | Gross Margin | Operating Exp | Net |
|------|---------|------|-------------|--------------|-----|
| 1 | $2.0M (delayed start) | $1.2M | $0.8M | $800K | $0 |
| 2 | $2.0M (market crash) | $1.5M | $0.5M | $900K | -$400K |
| 3 | $2.2M (recovery) | $1.4M | $0.8M | $850K | -$50K |

**Payback: 7+ years.** Co-op draws on reserves, defers patronage dividends, may need additional member capital calls. Not fatal but stressful. Grant funding is the difference between survival and failure in this scenario.

## Key Sensitivities

| Variable | Impact on Year 2 Net Income |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Helium price ±$20/L | ±$1.2M |
| Electricity cost ±$0.02/kWh | ±$40K |
| Crude helium cost ±20% | ±$360K |
| Volume ±20% | ±$540K |
| Equipment downtime (1 month) | -$300K |

**Helium price is the dominant variable.** Everything else is manageable.

## Breakeven Analysis

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Breakeven helium price (Grade-A) | ~$22/L equivalent |
| Breakeven monthly volume | ~3,500 L equivalent |
| Breakeven customer count | ~5 regular accounts |
| Minimum viable revenue | ~$2.5M/year |

At pre-crisis prices (~$25–35/L), the co-op is marginally profitable. At crisis prices ($50–100/L), it is highly profitable. At any price above $22/L, it covers all costs including debt service.

## The Bottom Line

The financial model works across all scenarios except total market collapse — and even then, grant funding provides a lifeline. The co-op is not a speculative venture; it is a known technology applied to a known market with a known funding source (CHIPS Act).

The question isn't whether the numbers work. The question is whether five people are willing to put in the work to make it happen.
