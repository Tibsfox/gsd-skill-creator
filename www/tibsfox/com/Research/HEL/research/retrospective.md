# HEL Research Retrospective

**Project:** HEL — Helium Supply Chain & Pacific Rim Semiconductor Infrastructure
**Initiated:** 2026-03-31
**Documents:** 14 research papers + knowledge graph (31 nodes, 60+ connections)
**Scope:** Full supply chain from geological formation to fab delivery to Pacific Rim trade

## What Went Right

### Cross-Domain Synthesis
This project demonstrates the value of the Rosetta Stone framework. Helium supply chain research requires simultaneous understanding of geology, chemistry, cryogenic physics, semiconductor manufacturing, international trade, maritime logistics, cooperative business law, and geopolitics. No single discipline covers this — the connections between them are where the insight lives.

### Timing
Research initiated during an active crisis (Qatar force majeure, March 2, 2026) means every finding has immediate relevance. The economic window analysis (document 09) captures a moment where the economics of action are dramatically different from the economics of normal times.

### Four New Documents (Refinement Pass)
The initial 10 documents covered the core supply chain but had gaps. The refinement pass added:
- **Helium-3** (11) — the rare isotope that constrains quantum computing scaling
- **Shortage History** (12) — the five-crisis pattern proving this is structural, not temporary
- **Silicon Forest** (13) — the specific customer ecosystem that makes PNW infrastructure relevant
- **Regulatory Landscape** (14) — the legal framework showing there are no unusual barriers

These four transform the project from "here's a supply chain" to "here's a supply chain with historical proof of recurring failure, a specific customer base, a scaling constraint, and a clear legal path."

## What Could Be Deeper

### Technical Specifications
The modular PSA system descriptions are general. Future research should include specific manufacturer product lines, throughput curves at different concentrations, power consumption per unit output, and maintenance requirements.

### Financial Modeling
Document 09 provides ranges and estimates. A proper financial model with Monte Carlo simulation across price scenarios, demand curves, and deployment timelines would strengthen the economic case.

### Specific Regulatory Pathways
Document 14 identifies the relevant laws but doesn't map the complete permit application timeline for a specific site. A "regulatory roadmap" for a hypothetical Smokey Point or Portland hub would be valuable.

### Geological Survey
No analysis of whether helium-bearing formations exist in the PNW specifically. The corridor model assumes crude helium is purchased from other regions — but are there any local sources? The Cascadia subduction zone geology might hold surprises.

## Key Insights

1. **The pattern is the finding.** Five crises since 2006, each worse, intervals shrinking. This isn't bad luck — it's structural. The same root cause (concentrated production, depleting reserves) has been identified and ignored five times.

2. **The PNW advantage is processing, not extraction.** Cheap electricity + port access + customer proximity = value-added processing zone. Buy crude, purify locally, distribute fast.

3. **He-3 is the hidden constraint.** Everyone knows about He-4 shortages. Few outside quantum computing research understand that He-3 — produced only from nuclear weapons tritium decay — is an even harder constraint. The PNW hosts both the demand (Microsoft Quantum) and the only lunar extraction company (Interlune).

4. **The Ring of Fire is a system.** This is not a metaphor. The nations around the Pacific Rim are participants in a shared system where each nation's prosperity depends on the others' stability. Infrastructure should reflect this mutual dependence.

5. **The law is not the obstacle.** Standard industrial gas regulations. Standard environmental review. Straightforward co-op formation (five people, one vote). The obstacle was always the false sense of abundance from cheap federal and Qatari helium. That illusion ended March 2, 2026.
