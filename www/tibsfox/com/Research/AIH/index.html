Research Modules

M1: Historical Foundations (1943–2017)

History Track A · Origins

McCulloch-Pitts neurons (1943) through AlexNet (2012) to the Transformer (2017). The Dartmouth Conference. Minsky's perceptron critique. AI Winter I and II. Backpropagation's return (1986). Convolutional neural networks and ImageNet. 15+ documented milestones with primary source citations tracing the conceptual arc.

M2: The Transformer Era (2017–2023)

Transformer Track A · Architecture

"Attention Is All You Need" (2017) to ChatGPT (2022). Scaling laws: Kaplan et al. (2020) and Chinchilla (2022). GPT-3 few-shot learning. InstructGPT and RLHF. DALL-E and diffusion models. Open-weight movement: LLaMA, Mistral, Qwen. The emergence of in-context learning as a computational phenomenon.

M3: Frontier Models & Reasoning (2023–present)

Frontier Track B · Capabilities

Claude 3.5/4, GPT-4o/o1/o3, Gemini 2.5, DeepSeek-R1, Llama 4. Reasoning models winning IMO gold (2025). Inference-time compute scaling. Extended thinking and chain-of-thought. Multimodal fusion. The open-weight frontier: DeepSeek, Qwen, Mistral. Cost collapse: 1000x price reduction in 18 months.

M4: Agentic Architecture & Protocols

Agentic Track B · Composition

Model Context Protocol (MCP) and Agent-to-Agent (A2A) as TCP/IP for agents. Claude Code and Devin as agentic developer tools. Multi-agent orchestration patterns: swarm, hierarchical, market-based. The DACP three-part bundle as a GSD-native protocol. Seven-hour autonomous agent runs. Tool use and computer use capabilities.

M5: Alignment, Safety & Governance

Alignment Deep · Safety layer

Constitutional AI and RLHF/RLAIF. Mechanistic interpretability: probing transformer internals. Alignment faking research (Anthropic 2024). Red-teaming and model evaluations. EU AI Act, US executive orders, frontier model governance. The alignment gap: capability research outpacing safety by orders of magnitude. Cross-evaluation between Anthropic and OpenAI.

M6: College of Knowledge Integration

College Capstone · Synthesis

Mapping the eight-layer mathematical progression of The Space Between to AI/ML concepts. Unit Circle as activation geometry. Set theory as tokenization. Category theory as model composition. L-Systems as generative recursion. The Complex Plane of Experience as navigation instrument for AI practitioners. Rosetta Core cross-domain bridges.
