Research Modules

M1: Cooking as Teaching Methodology

Method Mise en place · Recipe as algorithm

The kitchen as classroom. Mise en place as project management. The recipe as algorithm. Feedback loops and the Calibration Engine. Progressive disclosure in cooking instruction. Numerical Recipes and the cookbook pattern across domains. The Rosetta Core in culinary translation.

M2: Food Science & Transformation

Science Maillard · Thermodynamics

The Maillard reaction and Hodge mechanism. Caramelization, protein denaturation, emulsification, starch gelatinization, fermentation biology. Heat transfer modes. Specific heat and thermal mass. Altitude effects. Newton's cooling law as the Calibration Engine's mathematical model.

M3: Knife Skills & Tool Mastery

Technique Grip · Cuts · Equipment

The chef's knife as API. Pinch grip and claw grip mechanics. The cut taxonomy from rough chop to brunoise. Steel metallurgy and edge geometry. Kitchen equipment as architecture. Thermometer calibration. Tool maintenance as systems administration. The four levels of competence.

M4: PNW Cuisine & Ingredients

Regional Terroir · Seasonal

Pacific Northwest terroir. Five species of Pacific salmon. Wild mushrooms and mycorrhizal ecology. Coastal shellfish harvest. The berry seasons. PNW herbs and wild aromatics. Indigenous food traditions and first foods. Cedar plank cooking. The PNW pantry. Seasonal cooking calendar.

M5: Food Safety as Systems Engineering

Safety HACCP · Absolute boundaries

Food safety as critical systems design. The temperature danger zone. USDA minimum internal temperatures. Cross-contamination as input validation. HACCP as CI/CD pipeline. Allergen management as type safety. Safe storage as data retention. Home economics and waste reduction. The Safety Warden architecture.
