The Through-Line

Cooking with Claude is the Rosetta Core's flagship proof of concept. The same translation engine that converts a user's “it came out too dry” into a thermodynamics adjustment is the engine that converts “this code feels slow” into a complexity diagnosis, or “I don't understand derivatives” into a geometric visualization. The concept is the same. The expression changes. The user sees a friendly colleague. The engine sees math. And the meal comes out right.

The kitchen is the proof because everyone cooks. Everyone understands the journey from “I burned the garlic again” to “I can make this with my eyes closed.” That journey — from unconscious incompetence through conscious practice to unconscious mastery — is universal. It's the same journey in every domain. Cooking with Claude maps it, measures it, and accelerates it through the Calibration Engine's Observe→Compare→Adjust→Record cycle. The recipe is the algorithm. Mise en place is the project plan. The Safety Warden is the non-negotiable constraint. And the partnership — cooking with, not for — is the whole point.
