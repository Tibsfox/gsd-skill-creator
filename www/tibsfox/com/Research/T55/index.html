555 Timer IC

Analog IC Design — Timer Circuits · Oscillators · Pulse Width Modulation · Timing Design
Overview Mission Pack
The 555 timer is a voltage-controlled bistable engine that converts RC time constants into precisely timed digital output transitions. Hans Camenzind designed it with 25 transistors, 2 diodes, and 15 resistors — and it became the most manufactured integrated circuit in history.

Hans Camenzind's biography, the Signetics contract, the 9-to-8 pin evolution, the naming by Art Fury, and the extraordinary market adoption.

Comparators · Flip-Flop · Pin Config

Six functional blocks analyzed: voltage divider, two comparators, SR flip-flop, discharge transistor, and output stage. Complete pin-by-pin documentation.

03 — Operating Modes & Mathematics

Monostable · Astable · Bistable · Derivations

Rigorous derivation of timing equations from RC exponential curves. T = 1.1RC explained, not just stated. Duty cycle analysis and VCO operation.
