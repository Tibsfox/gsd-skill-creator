## 1. Federal Energy Funding Programs

**Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act (IIJA, 2021):**
The IIJA provides approximately $62 billion for clean energy infrastructure. Relevant provisions for the Fox corridor:
- $500M for demonstration of advanced nuclear and geothermal projects
- $2.5B for transmission siting and routing studies
- $3B for grid resilience and innovation
- Clean hydrogen electrolysis pilot programs (relevant for surplus hydro monetization)

**DOE Loan Programs Office (LPO):**
The LPO provides debt financing for innovative clean energy projects. EGS geothermal projects are eligible under the ATVM and Title 17 programs. LPO loans have funded first-of-a-kind clean energy projects at rates that commercial banks will not match -- critical for EGS projects that lack a commercial track record in PNW geology.

**DOE FORGE Program:**
The Frontier Observatory for Research in Geothermal Energy is DOE's dedicated EGS research program. FORGE funding has supported the Utah FORGE site and the NEWGEN partnership at Newberry Volcano. Cascade Range EGS development could qualify for FORGE follow-on funding as the program scales from research to commercial demonstration.

## 2. IRA Tax Credits and Direct Pay

The Inflation Reduction Act (2022) transformed the economics of clean energy development through expanded tax credits and a new "direct pay" mechanism that allows tax-exempt entities (including tribal governments) to receive credits as cash refunds:

**Investment Tax Credit (ITC) for Geothermal:**
- Base credit: 30% of qualified investment
- Bonus credit for tribal lands: +10% (additive)
- Domestic content bonus: +10% (if equipment manufactured in US)
- Energy community bonus: +10% (if in distressed community like Grant County)
- Maximum potential ITC: 60% of qualified investment

**Production Tax Credit (PTC) for Geothermal:**
- Base rate: 2.75 cents/kWh for first 10 years
- Inflation-adjusted annually
- Applicable for facilities entering service before 2035

**Direct Pay mechanism:**
Tribal governments and other tax-exempt entities can receive ITC and PTC as direct cash payments rather than as tax credits -- critical because tribal governments typically do not have federal tax liability to offset. This makes the full ITC/PTC value accessible to the Colville Confederated Tribes without requiring a tax equity partnership structure (which has historically extracted value from tribal energy projects).

**IRA extension (January 2025):** The IRA's clean energy tax credit provisions were extended through 2035, providing investment certainty for the Fox corridor's multi-phase development timeline.

## 3. Tribal-Specific Funding

**DOE Office of Indian Energy:**
- Grants for tribal clean energy feasibility studies: up to $200,000
- Grants for tribal clean energy project development: up to $3 million
- Grants for tribal energy code development: up to $500,000
- Technical assistance for tribal energy project economics and permitting

**BIA Tribal Energy Resource Agreements (TERA):**
TERA allows tribes to enter energy development agreements without individual BIA approval for each transaction. For the Fox corridor, TERA streamlines the process of Colville Confederated Tribes entering long-term power purchase agreements with data center operators -- reducing transaction costs and improving deal velocity.

**Indian Health Service (IHS) Sanitation:**
For geothermal development that could affect water quality, IHS infrastructure funding is available for water system improvements.

**EDA Economic Development Grants:**
The Economic Development Administration provides grants to distressed communities for economic development infrastructure. Grant County's distressed designation makes the Electric City site eligible for EDA funding toward site preparation, workforce training, and infrastructure improvements.

## 4. Electric City Site-Specific Funding

The former Vanalco aluminum smelter site qualifies for multiple funding streams based on its specific characteristics:

**EPA Brownfields Program:**
- The aluminum smelter left environmental contamination (industrial residuals)
- EPA Brownfields provides up to $500,000 per site for assessment and cleanup
- Tribal lands are given priority consideration
- Cleanup funding reduces redevelopment cost and removes liability barriers

**SBA 8(a) Business Development Program:**
- The 8(a) program assists disadvantaged small businesses
- Tribal entities with SBA 8(a) certification can access federal contracting preferences
- Data center operations serving federal government agencies could qualify for 8(a) set-aside contracts

**HUD Community Development Block Grants (CDBG):**
- CDBG funds economic development in low- and moderate-income areas
- Grant County's distressed designation supports CDBG eligibility
- Funding applicable to workforce training, site infrastructure

**USDA Rural Development:**
- Business and Industry Loan Guarantees for rural businesses
- Rural Energy for America Program (REAP) for on-site renewable energy
- Electric City's rural designation makes these programs applicable

## 5. Geothermal-Specific Regulatory Landscape

**BLM Categorical Exclusion (January 2025):**
The Bureau of Land Management issued a categorical exclusion for small geothermal exploration projects (under 5 MW), allowing these projects to bypass the standard NEPA environmental review process. This potentially saves one year per early-stage geothermal project in permitting time -- significant for cash-intensive exploration programs.

**GEO Act (proposed):**
The Geothermal Energy Optimization Act (GEO Act) would place geothermal on equal footing with oil and gas on public lands -- the same competitive lease auctions, the same fiscal terms, the same regulatory pathway. Currently geothermal leasing is governed by the Geothermal Steam Act, which is less favorable than oil and gas lease terms.

**USFS Geothermal Leasing:**
The U.S. Forest Service manages geothermal leasing on National Forest lands. The Mount Baker-Snoqualmie designation of 80,000 acres for potential development established a framework, but USFS leasing requires:
- Compatibility with forest management plan
- Endangered species consultation (Section 7 ESA)
- Cultural resource consultation (Section 106 NHPA)
- NEPA environmental analysis

**California State Policy (October 2025):**
California Governor Newsom signed legislation in October 2025 to speed geothermal permitting. While California-specific, this legislation signals state-level willingness to accelerate geothermal development and creates precedent for Oregon and Washington similar legislation.

## 6. State-Level Policy: Washington and Oregon

**Washington CETA (Clean Energy Transformation Act):**
- Requires the state's electricity grid to be fossil-free by 2045
- Creates a carbon adder that increases the effective cost of fossil fuel generation
- Makes clean firm resources (hydro + geothermal) more economically competitive over time
- Utility integrated resource plans must prioritize clean resources

**Washington Clean Buildings Act:**
- Performance standards for commercial buildings' energy intensity
- Compliance pathway includes on-site clean generation
- Creates demand for clean firm power from building operators

**Oregon Renewable Portfolio Standard:**
- Requires 50% renewable electricity by 2040
- Geothermal qualifies as renewable
- Oregon utilities will need to demonstrate geothermal capability as RPS increases

**Pacific Northwest Economic Region (PNWER):**
A statutory interstate body covering WA, OR, ID, MT, AK, BC, AB, SK, and YT. PNWER provides a coordination framework for cross-border energy development that could facilitate Cascade Range EGS development spanning state and provincial boundaries.

## 7. BPA Contract Framework

**Provider of Choice Contracts (executed December 2025):**
BPA's long-term power purchase agreements with 130+ Northwest utilities lock in wholesale rates for two decades. For the Fox corridor:
- Electric City data center can execute a Provider of Choice contract for its power needs
- Locked-in $0.02/kWh rate through ~2045
- Rate certainty for the full Phase 1-2 development period

**Transmission access:**
BPA operates the Pacific Intertie -- the high-voltage DC transmission link connecting the Pacific Northwest to California. This provides the ability to sell geothermal generation to California utilities at peak pricing.

**BPA Tier 1 vs. Tier 2:**
- Tier 1: historic hydro baseload at very low cost ($0.02-0.04/kWh)
- Tier 2: power above historic hydro baseload at market rates
- The Fox corridor should optimize for maximum Tier 1 hydro consumption and minimum dependence on Tier 2 pricing

## 8. Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| EGS resource not confirmed in Cascade Range geology | Medium | High | Phased exploration; fallback to import geothermal from Nevada/Utah via Pacific Intertie |
| BPA rate structure changes unfavorably | Low | High | Lock in Provider of Choice contracts; diversify to include EGS generation |
| Colville tribal political change affects partnership | Low | High | TERA structure protects; negotiated governance with clear terms |
| IRA tax credits curtailed by future Congress | Low | Medium | Projects under construction before 2030 are protected; direct pay reduces tax liability dependence |
| FEMA BRIC-like geothermal funding disruption | Medium | Medium | Maintain multiple funding pathways; FORGE + DOE Indian Energy + ITC/PTC |
| EGS induced seismicity concern from communities | Medium | High | FORGE monitoring protocols; community engagement; transparent reporting |
| USFS National Forest leasing opposition | Medium | Medium | Focus initial development on BLM lands and private/tribal lands |
