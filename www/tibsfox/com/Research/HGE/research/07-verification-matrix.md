## 6. Execution Summary

**Project:** HGE -- Hydro-Geothermal Energy
**Mission source:** `mission-pack/hydro-geothermal-mission.tex`
**Modules created:** 7 (6 substantive + 1 verification)
**Verification result:** 15/15 criteria PASS

**Core thesis confirmed:** The Fox Infrastructure Group's Electric City thesis is coherent and well-supported. The complementarity of Columbia Basin hydroelectric (flexible dispatch, $0.02/kWh BPA rates) and Cascade Range EGS geothermal (always-on baseload, weather-independent) forms a clean firm power portfolio that no single resource can match. The Colville Confederated Tribes' transition from annual compensation recipient to majority equity holder in a data center is the structural shift that makes the economics work for the community, not just for the investor.

**Key data points preserved across modules:**
- Grand Coulee: 6,809 MW, built on tribal land for $63,000 (1940)
- BPA rate: $0.02/kWh (Provider of Choice, locked through ~2045)
- Fervo Cape Station: 107 kg/s flow rate, 49% cost reduction, 10+ MW per well
- IRA ITC for tribal projects: up to 60% with all bonus stacking
- Electric City Phase 1: 50 MW pilot, 2026-2028, tribal majority equity
- AI load growth: Altman 2025 Senate testimony — "AI abundance will be limited by energy abundance"
