Maritime Platform Cost Model

Vessel OPEX/CAPEX · Port Infrastructure · Subsea Cable · PNW Ports · Fuel Transition · Platform Economics
Overview Compute Costs Energy Infrastructure Transport Economics Convergence Credits Scenario Comparison Risk Analysis Mission Pack
The spaces between nodes are where the money lives. Individual cost data for a maritime compute platform exists in scattered form: Norway's subsea tunnel concepts, subsea power cables, shore-based data centers, Korean-yard container vessels. What does not yet exist is a model that shows what these numbers become when they converge — when the same cable serves both power delivery and data transport, when the same vessel hull doubles as a compute chassis and a coolant heat exchanger, when port infrastructure serves both cargo and maintenance operations. The question is not whether the architecture is elegant. The question is what it costs to build the Amiga at sea.

Research Modules

M1: Compute Cost Analysis

Compute Track A · Marine DC economics

Shore-based data center benchmarks ($10-12M/MW general, $20M+ AI-optimized). Marine deployment premium (+30-50%). Ocean cooling credit ($1-2M/MW saved). Container DC construction. AI vs. standard compute density implications.

M2: Energy Infrastructure Costs

Energy Track A · Wind, cable, hydro

Floating offshore wind CAPEX ($4,000+/kW). Fixed offshore ($2,852/kW IRENA 2024). Subsea power cable costs ($0.5-5M/km). HVAC vs. HVDC break-even distance. BPA hydro baseline ($0.02/kWh Grand Coulee). Grid interconnect costs.

M3: Maritime Transport Economics

Transport Track B · Vessel economics

Vessel acquisition by scale ($15-200M+). Annual operating costs: crew ($2-5M/yr), fuel ($3-10M/yr), insurance, maintenance. Autonomous vessel economics and shore control. PNW port infrastructure: Seattle, Tacoma, Portland.

M4: Convergence Credit Model

Credits Track B · Integration economics

Five convergence credits quantified: ocean cooling (C1), cable bundle dual-use (C2), vessel hull co-location (C3), shared port logistics (C4), BPA hydro power advantage (C5). Net 18-28% CAPEX reduction at regional scale.

M5: Scenario Comparison

Analysis Synthesis · Three scales

Three deployment scales with full CAPEX/OPEX/NPV analysis: Prototype (5 MW, 1 vessel, 50km cable), Regional (100 MW, 5 vessels, 300km spine), Full Corridor (500 MW, 20+ vessels, BC-to-Chile). Cost per MW-hour of compute delivered.

M6: Sensitivity & Risk Analysis

Risk Synthesis · NPV and sensitivity

Top-5 cost driver sensitivity analysis. 20-year NPV at 5%, 8%, 12% discount rates. Steel price swings, cable vessel day rates, wind CAPEX trajectory, regulatory risk premiums. Risk-adjusted returns across all scenarios.
