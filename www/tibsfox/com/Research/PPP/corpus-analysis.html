Knowledge Gaps Remaining

  • Nearshore-specific data — forage fish spawning surveys are still unique to PPP. No other corpus project documents intertidal habitat at this resolution.
  • Indigenous knowledge — IBC (Indigenous Broadcasting) is now connected, but no dedicated Tulalip Tribes research project exists. The gap is narrower but still present.
  • Shellfish closure science — water quality monitoring and DOH closure protocols have no corpus parallel. PPP is the only project documenting this regulatory mechanism.
  • Drift cell dynamics — USGS seismic connection exists but Puget Sound sediment transport remains unique to PPP. Opportunity for dedicated geomorphology study.

Pipeline Architecture

The analysis was performed by the Research Co-Processor, a three-layer system:

  1. Unison core — Typed pipeline with 4 IO handlers (handleKnowledgeIO, handleResearchIO, handleOrchestrateIO, handleContextIO), 33 pure tests, 6 IO smoke tests. Content-addressed code ensures pipeline reproducibility.
  2. Python bridge — research-coprocessor-bridge.py connects Unison types to filesystem IO. Scans the 239-project corpus, runs multi-pass keyword expansion with convergence detection, produces HTML output with complex plane metadata.
  3. Corpus scoring — 6-signal OOPS relevance algorithm (keyword overlap, type prior, recency, path proximity, continuity bonus, branch relevance). Each concept mapped to the complex plane where |z| = research depth and arg(z) = breadth/depth balance.

Convergence: the pipeline expands search terms from each pass's discoveries and stops when no new cross-references are found. v2 modules converged in 2–8 passes (v1: 2–5). The deeper expansion for Restoration (8 passes) and Geology (8 passes) reflects the richer keyword surface area of the engineering site review titles.

Key insight: Abstract titles (“Ecology & Nearshore Habitat”) produce shallow cross-referencing. Engineering titles with specific keywords (“Intertidal Beach, Forage Fish Spawning, Riparian Corridor, Marine Food Web”) produce 2–4x more connections. Future pipeline runs should always use the most specific available titles.
