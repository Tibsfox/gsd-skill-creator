The Park as a Restoration Transect

Picnic Point Park occupies one of the last accessible, ecologically intact shoreline windows along the densely railed corridor between Edmonds and Mukilteo on the eastern shore of Puget Sound. Separated from its parking lot by the BNSF mainline — the same rail barrier that long blocked salmon passage at nearby Meadowdale Beach — the park is reached by a pedestrian overpass that has become both a practical crossing and an emblem of the negotiated relationship between industry, recreation, and nature that defines this stretch of coast.

This research study examines what Picnic Point Park actually contains — ecologically, historically, geologically, and socially — and what its restoration trajectory reveals about the larger challenge of recovering Puget Sound's nearshore.

Research Modules

Module A: Ecology & Nearshore Habitat

Intertidal beach substrate, forage fish spawning (surf smelt, sand lance), Picnic Point Creek riparian corridor, marine food web from forage fish to Chinook to Southern Resident Orcas, bird community.

WDFW Snohomish MRC NOAA

Module B: Restoration & Conservation

Snohomish County Healthy Forest Project (2019–present), Olympic Fly Fishers of Edmonds partnership, nine community restoration events, shellfish closure analysis, Meadowdale Beach comparative model.

HFP OFF WDFW/DOH

Module C: Geology & Coastal Processes

Glacially derived bluff sediments, Puget Sound drift cell dynamics, BNSF railroad and seawall effects on sediment transport, USGS landslide hazard mapping for the Seattle–Everett corridor.

USGS WDFW USACE

Module D: History & Cultural Context

MV Pacific Queen chronology (WWII origins, Bristol Bay career, 1957 sinking, Franzen Beach beaching), BNSF railroad history, Tulalip Tribes and the pre-railroad Indigenous shoreline.

PSMHS Mukilteo Historical Society

Module E: Recreation, Access & Community Science

Park facilities and access constraints, tide-dependent beach hiking routes, City Nature Challenge bioblitz (April 25, 2026), iNaturalist integration, MRC forage fish survey protocol.

Snohomish County DCNR WTA iNaturalist
