Pacific Garbage Patch Cleanup

North Pacific Subtropical Gyre — Oceanography · Ecology · Cleanup Technology · Policy · Economics
Overview Mission Pack
Between California and Hawai'i, the North Pacific Subtropical Gyre has accumulated an estimated 100 million kilograms of plastic debris across 1.6 million km². The Ocean Cleanup's System 03 — a 2.2-kilometer floating barrier — has extracted nearly 500,000 kg. Ten such systems could clean the entire patch in a decade for $7.5 billion. The math is clear. The question is whether humanity chooses to do the math.

Research Modules

01. Oceanographic Context

Gyre Formation · Ekman Convergence · Vertical Distribution

Physical oceanography of the North Pacific Subtropical Gyre. Four-current system (California, North Equatorial, Kuroshio, North Pacific), Ekman convergence accumulation mechanism, Subtropical Convergence Zone transport highway, vertical microplastic distribution to 2,000m depth, UV fragmentation dynamics.

02. Ecological Impact

914 Species · Entanglement · Bioaccumulation

Species-level documentation of plastic harm across marine taxa. 914 affected species, 100,000 marine mammal deaths annually, ghost fishing gear as dominant entanglement vector, plasticosis pathology in seabirds, microplastic bioaccumulation through trophic levels, carbon cycle disruption.

03. Cleanup Technology

System 03 · Interceptors · Emerging Tech

Engineering analysis of The Ocean Cleanup's system evolution (001 through 03), System 03 specifications and AI-guided targeting, River Interceptor program (20 deployments, 9 countries), alternative technologies (Seabin, enzymatic, magnetic nanocoils, NASA CYGNSS), net environmental benefit assessment.

04. Source Reduction & Policy

UN Plastics Treaty · EPR · National Measures

UN Global Plastics Treaty timeline (INC-1 through INC-5.3), High-Ambition Coalition vs petrochemical bloc positions, EU single-use directive, China phase-out plan, US Save Our Seas 2.0, Extended Producer Responsibility, river-source targeting of 1,000 most-polluting waterways.

05. Environmental Economics

$7.5B Estimate · Carbon Trade-offs · Net Benefit

Cost-benefit framework for GPGP cleanup. The Ocean Cleanup's $7.5B estimate, comparative cost-per-kilogram analysis, recycled ocean plastic markets, net environmental benefit assessment (vulnerability scores 2.3/1.9/1.8), carbon accounting: cleanup GHG vs microplastic impact on ocean carbon export.
