## 1. 20th-Century Classical and Avant-Garde

Debussy's Impressionist music dissolved structural clarity into pure color. Schoenberg abandoned tonality with his twelve-tone system (serialism, c. 1921). Stravinsky's *Rite of Spring* (1913, Paris) caused a riot. Bartok synthesized Hungarian folk music with modernist technique. Mid-century: Cage (chance operations), Stockhausen (electronic), Xenakis (spatial music).

## 2. Jazz, Blues, Ragtime, Gospel

Born in African American communities of New Orleans. Ragtime (Scott Joplin's *Maple Leaf Rag*, 1899) and early jazz from the intersection of blues, gospel, and European harmony. Jazz is the most technically sophisticated improvised music in the world. Bebop (1940s -- Charlie Parker, Monk, Miles Davis) increased complexity to brilliant virtuosity.

## 3. Rock and Roll, Soul, Popular Music

Rock and roll (1950s -- Chuck Berry, Little Richard, Elvis) electrified blues. British Invasion (Beatles, Stones, 1964). **Soul** (Aretha Franklin, Otis Redding, Marvin Gaye) channeled gospel into secular emotion. **Funk** (James Brown, Parliament-Funkadelic) decomposed the beat into polyrhythmic groove. Progressive rock (Pink Floyd, King Crimson) extended the album as compositional statement.

## 4. Hip-Hop

Born 1973, Bronx, NY. DJ Kool Herc isolated the breakbeat. Four elements: MCing, b-boying, graffiti, DJing. Grandmaster Flash developed turntable technique. Run-DMC crossed into mainstream. Public Enemy and N.W.A. brought political fire. Jay-Z, Nas, Kendrick Lamar, and global expansion made hip-hop the most-streamed genre on Earth.

## 5. Electronic Music

Kraftwerk (1970s) predicted electronic dance music. Brian Eno invented ambient music (*Music for Airports*, 1978). Industrial (Throbbing Gristle, 1975). Techno (Detroit, 1980s -- Atkins, May), house (Chicago). A massive international electronic ecosystem.

## 6. The Digital Revolution

The DAW (ProTools, Logic, Ableton, FL Studio) democratized production. MIDI (1983) enabled digital communication. MP3 (1991), iTunes (2001), Spotify (2008) restructured economics. AI composition tools (MuseNet, Magenta, Suno AI) generate music indistinguishable from human composers.
