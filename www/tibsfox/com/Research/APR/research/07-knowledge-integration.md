## 1. Complex Plane Framework

The College of Knowledge positions all concepts from this mission on the GSD Complex Plane of Experience:

- **Real axis** (theta = 0 to pi): Formal/technical development -- from pure craft toward pure concept
- **Imaginary axis** (theta = pi/2): Spiritual/emotional orientation -- from secular craft toward transcendent expression
- **Magnitude r:** Influence -- how many subsequent movements cite this as root

Example positions: Renaissance perspective (high r, low theta -- highly influential, primarily technical). Romanticism (high r, high theta -- influential and emotionally oriented). Minimalism (moderate r, theta near 0 -- technical with deliberate emotional restraint).

## 3. L-System Growth Grammar

L-Systems (Lindenmayer Systems) model branching growth patterns. Applied to art history, they describe how movements branch and recurse:

- **Axiom:** Prehistoric mark-making (the seed)
- **Rule 1:** Technical innovation triggers branching (perspective -> Baroque + Mannerism)
- **Rule 2:** Rejection of prior movement creates opposing branch (Rococo -> Neoclassicism)
- **Rule 3:** Synthesis of opposing branches creates new trunk (Impressionism from Realism + Romanticism)

## 4. Cross-Domain Connections

The Creative Continuum is a single continuous signal, not a sequence of disconnected events. Every era inherits from all prior eras and transmits to all subsequent ones. The Rosetta Core translates across domains. The Complex Plane maps positions. The L-System grammar generates the branching tree.
