## 1. Legal Structure

Options for a home-based art business in the U.S.:

- **Sole Proprietorship:** Simplest. No separate legal entity. Income on Schedule C. Personal liability exposure.
- **LLC:** Recommended for most artists. Separates personal and business assets. Low cost ($50-$500 by state).
- **S-Corporation:** Useful when income is substantial; saves self-employment tax. Higher complexity.

Additional: business bank account, EIN from IRS (free, online), business license, sales tax permit if selling tangible goods.

## 2. Building an Artist Website

1. **Domain:** Register your artist name (.com or .art)
2. **Portfolio:** High-resolution images, organized by series. Clean, fast-loading.
3. **About Page:** Artist statement, bio, exhibition history, press
4. **Shop/E-Commerce:** Squarespace, Wix, Shopify, or Etsy integration
5. **Blog/Process:** Regular posts build audience and SEO
6. **Contact:** Simple form. Gallery/press inquiry protocols.
7. **Email List:** Most valuable marketing asset. Signup on every page.
8. **SSL Certificate:** HTTPS required for trust.

## 3. Brand Identity

- **Artist Voice:** How you see, not what you paint
- **Visual Identity:** Logo, typeface, color palette -- consistent across all touchpoints
- **Platform Consistency:** Same name, same profile image, same bio everywhere
- **Niche vs. Versatility:** A brand is a labeled box; the audience learns what to expect without knowing what's inside

## 4. Copyright vs. Trademark

| | Copyright | Trademark |
|---|----------|-----------|
| Protects | The creative work itself | Brand identifiers: name, logo, slogan |
| Exists | Automatically upon creation | Only when registered or through commerce use |
| Duration | Life + 70 years | Indefinitely, if renewed every 10 years |
| Registration | Optional but strengthens standing | Required for federal protection |
| Cost | $65-$150 (Copyright Office) | $250-$350 per class (USPTO) |

## 5. USPTO Trademark Registration

1. Conduct a TESS Search (Trademark Electronic Search System)
2. Determine Use Basis: "Use in commerce" or "Intent to Use"
3. Select International Class(es): 016 (artwork), 025 (apparel), 041 (entertainment), 009 (digital)
4. File via TEAS ($250-$350 per class)
5. USPTO examination (8-12 months)
6. Publication for opposition (30 days)
7. Registration certificate. Valid 10 years; renew years 5-6 and every 10 years.

**Personal name as trademark:** USPTO resists surnames. Demonstrate "secondary meaning" through sales records, media coverage, duration of use. Consult an attorney.
