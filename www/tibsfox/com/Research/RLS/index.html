The wind moves through twelve steel pipes on the shore of Lake Washington and produces a sound that is not quite music. A young man hears it and names his band. Decades later, the sculpture becomes his memorial. Ten thousand feet higher and a hundred miles east, the wind moves through Spider Meadow and produces a silence that is not quite silence. On the Olympic coast, the surf crashes through a hole in a rock wall and the sound carries the memory of Chilean sailors who never made it home. In the Himalayas, at Crystal Mountain, a man who went looking for a snow leopard discovered that the most profound encounter is with what is not there. The spaces between the sounds are where we live.

Urban & Sculptural

Sound and Sculpture

Sound Sculpture

Douglas Hollis’s aeolian sculpture “A Sound Garden” on the NOAA campus at Sand Point. Twelve steel towers, organ pipes, and the wind off Lake Washington. The band that took its name. Minoru Yamasaki’s Pacific Science Center — Gothic arches from the 1962 World’s Fair.

Wilderness & Coast

Glacier Peak Wilderness

Alpine

Spider Meadow through Spider Gap at 7,100 feet. Lyman Lakes reflecting retreating glaciers. Image Lake — the crown jewel — holding Glacier Peak’s reflection at sunset. The 40-mile loop through two national forests and 3.6 miles of the Pacific Crest Trail.

Olympic Peninsula Coastal Trails

Coastal

Rialto Beach to Ozette through the Graveyard of the Pacific. Hole in the Wall at low tide. Chilean and Norwegian memorials to lost sailors. Lake Ozette — largest unaltered natural lake in Washington. The Ozette archaeological site: 55,000 Makah artifacts sealed by a 1560 mudslide.

Maritime & Contemplative

Islands and the Salish Sea

Islands

Mount Constitution on Orcas Island — CCC-built stone tower modeled on Caucasian watchtowers. Moran State Park donated by a former Seattle mayor. The naming layers: Spanish, British, American. Coast Salish peoples — Lummi, Samish, Straits Salish — the original inhabitants.

Northern Journeys & Contemplation

Contemplative

Sitka: Tlingit homeland, Russian colony, Presbyterian mission. Sheldon Jackson’s 5,000 artifacts and the suppression of a language. Peter Matthiessen’s two-month walk to the Crystal Monastery. “Have you seen the snow leopard? No! Isn’t that wonderful!”
