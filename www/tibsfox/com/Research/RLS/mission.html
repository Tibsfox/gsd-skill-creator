Mission Pack

The original research brief and mission package that initiated the Resonant Landscapes project. A comprehensive TeX document covering the full vision-to-mission pipeline — from Douglas Hollis’s aeolian sculpture on the shore of Lake Washington through alpine wilderness, coastal archaeology, island maritime culture, and northern colonial-theological history to Peter Matthiessen’s Crystal Monastery and the teaching of sacred absence.

Source Documents

The mission pack comprises an 870-line TeX research companion covering six interconnected modules: Sound and Sculpture (Hollis, Soundgarden, Yamasaki’s Pacific Science Center), Glacier Peak Wilderness (Spider Meadow, Image Lake, Lyman Lakes), Olympic Peninsula Coastal Trails (Rialto Beach to Ozette, Makah archaeology), Islands and the Salish Sea (Mount Constitution, Moran State Park, Coast Salish history), and Northern Journeys and Contemplation (Sitka, Sheldon Jackson, Matthiessen’s Snow Leopard). Full chipset configuration, wave execution plan, and source bibliography.

Research Scope

  • Sound and Sculpture: Douglas Hollis’s “A Sound Garden” (1982–83) — twelve 21-foot steel towers with organ pipes on the NOAA campus. Soundgarden (formed 1984, Rock Hall 2025). Minoru Yamasaki’s Pacific Science Center from the 1962 World’s Fair. The NOAA Art Walk at Sand Point.
  • Parks Hierarchy: PNW park system across six jurisdictional levels — municipal (Seattle Parks), county, state (Moran, Deception Pass), national park (Olympic, Rainier, North Cascades), national forest/wilderness (Glacier Peak, Alpine Lakes, The Enchantments), and tribal lands.
  • Glacier Peak Wilderness: Spider Meadow via Phelps Creek Trail (3,500’ to 5,300’). Spider Gap glacier crossing at 7,100’. Lyman Lakes and retreating glaciers. Image Lake at 6,050’ — the crown jewel. Miners Ridge fire lookout. The 40–50 mile loop through two national forests.
  • Olympic Coast: Rialto Beach to Ozette — the Graveyard of the Pacific. Hole in the Wall tidal access. Chilean Memorial (W.J. Pirrie, 1920) and Norwegian Memorial (Prince Arthur, 1903). Lake Ozette (29.5 km², Makah name q’a’uk). Ozette archaeological site: 55,000+ Makah artifacts from longhouses buried circa 1560.
  • Islands and the Salish Sea: Mount Constitution (2,409’) on Orcas Island. CCC-built stone tower (1936, architect Ellsworth Storey). Moran State Park (5,252 acres). Spanish, British, and American naming layers. Coast Salish peoples — Lummi, Samish, Straits Salish.
  • Northern Journeys and Contemplation: Sitka — Kiks.ádi clan homeland, Russian colonial history, American purchase. Sheldon Jackson (1834–1909): Princeton Seminary, Sitka Mission, 5,000+ artifacts, language suppression. Peter Matthiessen’s The Snow Leopard (1978): 250-mile trek to Shey Gompa, Crystal Monastery, Lama Tupjuk, and the snow leopard never seen.

Key Connections

RLS connects to COL (Columbia Valley rainforest ecology), CAS (Cascade Range geology, Glacier Peak volcanic context), ECO (species taxonomy and classification), DAA (deep audio analysis, aeolian sound), FQC (frequency continuum, acoustic physics), ARC (sculptural geometry, Gothic arches), SPA (spatial awareness, trail navigation), AVI (avian species along coastal and alpine trails), CRV (corvid intelligence, ravens at tide pools), and SAL (salmon heritage, Coast Salish cultural ecology).
