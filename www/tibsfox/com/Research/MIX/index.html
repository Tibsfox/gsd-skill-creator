Sir Mix-A-Lot & the West Coast

The DIY King — Auburn · Central District · NastyMix · Rhyme Cartel · West Coast Hip-Hop
Overview Mission Pack
Anthony Ray built every layer of Seattle's hip-hop infrastructure from a home studio in Auburn, Washington — the production, the label, the distribution, the cross-genre experiments — and proved that hip-hop was not a regional genre but a national language spoken fluently from Seattle to Houston to New York.

Research Modules

01 — Origin & Identity

Auburn · Central District · Roosevelt HS · NastyMix

Anthony Ray's biography from Auburn birth through the Central District, Seattle's busing program, community center DJing, meeting Nasty Nes, founding NastyMix Records, and building a home studio that produced platinum records.

02 — Discography

Swass · Seminar · Mack Daddy · Rhyme Cartel

Complete album-by-album analysis from Swass (1988, platinum) through Daddy's Home (2003), tracing the NastyMix to Rhyme Cartel to Def American label trajectory as a story about creative control and independence.

03 — West Coast Context

KDAY · NWA · G-Funk · Third Position

The broader West Coast hip-hop timeline from Watts Prophets (1971) through the G-funk era, radio infrastructure mapping (KDAY, KPWR, KMEL, KUBE), and Mix-A-Lot's unique third position: not LA, not Bay Area, but PNW.

04 — Cross-Genre & Legacy

Metal Church · Mudhoney · POTUSA · Seattle Symphony

Genre-crossing collaborations unique to Seattle: rap-metal with Metal Church (1988), hip-hop-grunge with Mudhoney (1993), rock-rap fusion with POTUSA (1999), orchestral hip-hop with the Seattle Symphony (2014), and the cultural afterlife of "Baby Got Back."

05 — Community & Ecosystem

Dark Side · Kid Sensation · 206 Zulu · MOHAI

"My dream is to create 10 black millionaires right here in Seattle." The scene-building efforts, NastyMix roster, "Seattle... The Dark Side" compilation, and the legacy institutions carrying the story forward.
