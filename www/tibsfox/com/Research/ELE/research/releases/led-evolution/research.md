# LED Evolution: From Electroluminescence to Solid State Lighting

**Catalog:** ELE-LED | **Cluster:** Electronics
**Date:** 2026-04-04 | **Source:** castlepast (EPZbhZahY8A)
**Duration:** 2h 22m | **College:** Electronics, Physics, Chemistry, Science

## Abstract

A comprehensive history of light-emitting diodes from H.J. Round's 1907 silicon carbide observation through the 2014 Nobel Prize for the blue LED to 2026 MicroLED and LiFi. Covers semiconductor physics (band gap engineering, direct vs indirect gap), the 30-year quest for blue emission, phosphor conversion chemistry, and the ongoing green gap problem.

## Key Timeline

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1907 | H.J. Round observes yellow-green glow from SiC at 10V — first electroluminescence |
| 1927 | Oleg Losev publishes first comprehensive EL study, writes to Einstein |
| 1962 | Nick Holonyak Jr. (GE) creates first visible-spectrum red LED |
| 1993 | Shuji Nakamura demonstrates first high-brightness InGaN blue LED at Nichia |
| 1996 | First white LED: blue InGaN + YAG:Ce phosphor |
| 2014 | Nobel Prize: Akasaki, Amano, Nakamura for blue LED |
| 2023 | EU eliminates all fluorescent lighting; US bans lamps below 45 lm/W |
| 2026 | MicroLED, tandem OLED, QD-OLED, LiFi, UVC LEDs, vertical farming LEDs |

## Key Numbers

- LED efficiency: 150-220 lm/W (incandescent: 10-15 lm/W)
- Energy savings: 75-90% vs incandescent
- Lifespan: 25,000-50,000 hours (incandescent: ~1,000)
- GaN band gap: 3.44 eV
- GaN/sapphire lattice mismatch: 16%
- YAG:Ce quantum efficiency: >85% at 200C
- LA street retrofit: 140,000 fixtures, 60% energy reduction, $10M/yr saved

## Forest Sim Connection

The LED spectral emission research directly informed the enhanced firefly bioluminescence rendering in the PNW Forest Simulation. Fireflies now have individual spectral variation (amber to green shift) modeled on LED junction temperature effects on emission wavelength. The Stokes shift principle (blue excitation -> yellow-green emission) parallels bioluminescent luciferin chemistry.
