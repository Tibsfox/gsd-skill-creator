# Electric Battery Evolution: Lead Acid to Sodium-Ion

**Catalog:** ELE-BAT | **Cluster:** Electronics, Energy
**Date:** 2026-04-04 | **Source:** castlepast (44M7NtTKRzM)
**Duration:** 1h 14m | **College:** Electronics, Chemistry, Economics, Engineering

## Abstract

The complete history of electrochemical energy storage from Volta's 1800 pile through 2025 sodium-ion vehicles. Covers electrochemistry fundamentals, lead-acid dominance of automotive, the lithium revolution (Whittingham -> Goodenough -> Yoshino), Wright's Law cost reduction (93% in 15 years), and the emerging diversified chemistry era. Geopolitical analysis of critical mineral supply chains.

## Key Timeline

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1800 | Volta invents the Voltaic Pile — first sustained electric current |
| 1836 | Daniell Cell — stable voltage, backbone of telegraph networks |
| 1859 | Plante invents lead-acid — first rechargeable battery |
| 1976 | Whittingham demonstrates TiS2/Li cell at 2.5V |
| 1980 | Goodenough demonstrates LiCoO2 at 4V — Oxford refuses to patent |
| 1985 | Yoshino pairs LiCoO2 with carbon anode — safe Li-ion, no dendrites |
| 1991 | Sony commercializes first Li-ion (80 Wh/kg) |
| 2008 | Tesla Roadster: 6,831 x 18650 cells, 240+ mi range |
| 2020 | BYD Blade Battery: LFP cell-to-pack, structural battery |
| 2024 | First sodium-ion vehicles in China |
| 2025 | Li-ion pack cost: $118/kWh (93% reduction from 2010) |

## Key Numbers

- Li-ion cost: $1,474/kWh (2010) -> $118/kWh (2025) = 93% reduction
- Wright's Law: 18-20% cost reduction per doubling of cumulative production
- NMC 811: >270 Wh/kg
- LFP: 170-190 Wh/kg, thousands of cycles, intrinsically safe
- Sodium-ion: 140-160 Wh/kg, no lithium/cobalt/nickel needed
- Stationary storage: ~$70/kWh turnkey (2025)
- CATL global market share: >37%
- Recycling recovery: up to 95% of critical minerals
