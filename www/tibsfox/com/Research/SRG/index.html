The Susan Rogers Arc

Engineer · Producer · Scientist · Paisley Park to the Auditory Cortex
Overview Technician Prince Studio Production Arc Academic Pivot Berklee Lab Mission Pack
There is a specific shape to Susan Rogers' life that does not occur in nature very often: a self-taught audio technician from Orange County, a high-school dropout who repaired MCI consoles in Los Angeles, hired sight-unseen by the most demanding recording artist of the 1980s, who spent four years capturing what would become the most celebrated pop catalog of that era, then left to produce experimental records, then produced the record that would finance her own reinvention, then went back to school at age 44 with no diploma, emerged a decade later with a doctorate in cognitive psychology, and is now the director of a laboratory studying why human beings respond to music the way they do. Each phase is not a departure from the previous one but a deepening of it.

Research Modules

M1: The Technician Formation (1978–1983)

Biography Track A · Self-taught electronics

Self-directed education via Army manuals and the Opamp bookstore. Audio Industries Corporation: MCI console and tape machine repair. Rudy Records: Crosby & Nash. The professional grapevine that led to Prince. The technician advantage: mechanism-first understanding.

M2: The Prince Studio (1983–1988)

Engineering Track A · Paisley Park era

Five albums from Purple Rain through The Black Album. Engineering methodology under extreme conditions. Five specific technical decisions. Jesse Johnson mentorship. The Vault creation. The Paisley Park transition: the node becomes a network.

M3: The Production Arc (1988–1999)

Production Track B · Geggy Tah focus

Transition from engineering to production. Tony Berg and Michael Penn mentorships. Geggy Tah: both albums on Luaka Bop, “Whoever You Are” (#16 Modern Rock). Greg Kurstin’s trajectory. Stunt: the album that bought eight years. The deliberate exit.

M4: The Academic Pivot (2000–2010)

Science Track B · McGill doctorate

GED at 44. University of Minnesota: neuroscience and psychology. McGill: Levitin and McAdams. The dissertation: consonance/dissonance roughness perception, short-term memory for musical intervals. The technician’s question becomes the scientist’s hypothesis.

M5: The Berklee Laboratory (2010–Present)

Science Synthesis · Convergence

Music Perception and Cognition Laboratory. Auditory brainstem response research. Tinnitus in musician populations. The seven-dimension listener profile model. This Is What It Sounds Like (2022). The science-to-studio translation. Three cross-module through-lines.
