The Architecture Family Tree

Yann LeCun's position that autoregressive LLMs are insufficient for grounded, planning-capable intelligence has generated a specific architecture lineage over four years. Each step is a single design change whose practicality depends on the previous step succeeding.

V-JEPA2024Video extension. Learns motion + content from raw video.

LeWM2026First end-to-end pixel JEPA world model. Two loss terms, ~15M params, 192-dim latent, 48× CEM planning speedup. The payoff.

LeWorldModel Architecture

LeWM has two components and two loss terms. That's the entire architecture.

z_t     = Enc(o_t)              # single frame → 192-dim CLS-style latent
ẑ_{t+1} = Pred(z_t, a_t)        # next-frame latent given current latent + action
L_LeWM  = L_pred + λ · SIGReg(Z)

Roughly 15M parameters end-to-end from pixels. The 192-dim latent is roughly 200× fewer tokens per frame than DINO-WM's full patch grid. Six tunable loss-weighting hyperparameters collapse to one.

Planning via Cross-Entropy Method

At test time LeWM encodes start and goal images into latent space, then uses CEM to optimize an action sequence: sample candidate action sequences, roll out through the predictor, select those whose final latent lands closest to the goal latent.

LeWM CEM: ~1 s per episode. DINO-WM CEM: ~47 s per episode. 48× speedup — attributed to the compact latent, not to any single engineering optimization.

The attribution matters. CEM cost scales with the dimensionality of the state space being optimized. Shrinking that state space by ~200× in dimensionality is the dominant factor. It's not faster sampling, better initialization, or tighter tolerance; it's that there's less to optimize over.

This has a direct downstream implication for Phase 732 (Mission-state world-model). If we understand that the speedup derives from the compact latent, we keep the mission-state latent compact (target ≤192-dim) rather than, say, a sparse feature grid over all possible mission variables.

LeCun's Lecture — Broader Frame

Yann LeCun's Special Lecture on AI and World Models (Al-Khwarizmi Applied Mathematics Webinar, 14 April 2026, ~1h45m) situates LeJEPA and LeWM inside his longer argument:

  • Autoregressive generative models (LLMs, Sora, Veo) do not learn world models in the sense required for planning — they produce plausible continuations in pixel or token space.
  • The path forward is predicting future latent embeddings rather than raw observations (JEPA). The latent is where a world model lives.
  • Practical JEPA had been fragile. LeJEPA is the first instantiation where theory drives practice rather than the reverse.
  • LeWM shows that once the latent space is clean (via SIGReg), planning is orders of magnitude faster than methods that rely on large pretrained encoders as world models.

Note on lecture use (SC-ADV compliance): every lecture claim above is corroborated in the LeJEPA or LeWM papers. The lecture is framed as framing, not as an authority for standalone claims.

Practical Takeaway

Stop treating "anti-collapse" as a bag of tricks and start treating it as a distributional constraint to be measured.
