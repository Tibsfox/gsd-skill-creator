The Fifty Lines

LeJEPA's reference implementation is roughly fifty lines of PyTorch. It replaces a decade of accumulated anti-collapse heuristics — stop-gradient, teacher-student networks, EMA schedulers, negative samples — with a single regularizer parameterized by a single hyperparameter λ.

Fifty lines. That is not a shortcut. It is what a theoretically grounded objective lets you get away with. The heuristics that used to guard against collapse turn out to be symptoms of an unstated distributional target. Once the target is stated (isotropic Gaussian) and measured with a well-chosen univariate test (Epps-Pulley, 1983) over a well-chosen sketch (Cramér-Wold slicing, 1936), the symptoms vanish. The Whac-A-Mole ends.

What 1936 and 1983 Already Knew

SIGReg's two mathematical ingredients were sitting on a shelf. Harald Cramér and Herman Wold published their projection theorem in 1936: two multivariate distributions agree iff all their one-dimensional projections agree. T. W. Epps and L. B. Pulley published their empirical-characteristic-function normality test in 1983: a linear-complexity, differentiable, multi-GPU-friendly test for univariate normality.

LeJEPA's contribution is not to invent new mathematics. It is to see that these two classical results, combined, give exactly the tool that modern self-supervised learning had been approximating with a decade of heuristics. Stating the target distribution out loud is the move. Cramér-Wold and Epps-Pulley did the rest.

The Amiga Analogy

The Commodore Amiga did not beat its competitors by brute force. It beat them by giving each function a chipset of its own — Agnus for memory, Denise for graphics, Paula for sound, Gary for glue logic — and letting them cooperate on the cycle. The CPU was a 7.16 MHz Motorola 68000, not meaningfully faster than competitors. But the system pulled off animations, sampled sound, blitter- accelerated graphics, and multitasking in 1985 that other architectures struggled with for years because the load-bearing work happened in the spaces between the chips.

gsd-skill-creator's architecture is this same pattern. The chipset model (Agnus/Denise/ Paula/Gary → Opus/Sonnet/Haiku) specializes by inference cost. CAPCOM gates specialize by safety role. Bounded-learning caps (MB-1 Lyapunov, MB-5 dead-zone) specialize by stability property. The College of Knowledge specializes by domain coverage. DACP specializes by handoff discipline. Each is a specialized elegant piece, and the meaning — the work the system actually does — lives at the thresholds between them.

LeJEPA shows us a rigorous new threshold — the distribution of the representation itself — and gives us the tool to measure it.

The Transposition

This mission picks up that tool and teaches gsd-skill-creator to sharpen its own latent space the same way LeJEPA sharpens a neural network's. The skill library is a latent space. Every skill is a point, every task is a region, and the library's value depends on how well the points cover the regions the user actually encounters. No mechanism currently measures whether new skills fill gaps or duplicate coverage. Half B of this milestone adds that measurement as a default-off audit (src/skill-isotropy/), adds the SIGReg primitive itself as a first-class skill (src/sigreg/, ≤80 LOC), adds the mission-state world-model that makes wave rehearsal cheap (src/mission-world-model/, sits under CAPCOM), and adds the intrinsic telemetry that gives CAPCOM a Spearman-style label-free pre-verification signal (src/intrinsic-telemetry/).

The Through-Line

In both LeJEPA and the Amiga, the winning architecture is not the one with the most firepower. It is the one where specialized elegant pieces cooperate on the cycle and meaning lives at the thresholds between them. Load-bearing work happens in the spaces between.

This mission is a new threshold.

What This Means Practically

When the principles are right, the implementation becomes forgiving. SIGReg takes fifty lines of code because Cramér-Wold did the hard work in 1936 and Epps and Pulley did the rest in 1983. gsd-skill-creator's ecosystem already has its philosophical foundations in place — the Amiga Principle, the spaces between, bounded learning, the College of Knowledge. What this mission hands the ecosystem is a new rigorous instrument: a measurable, projection-based way to tell whether the skill library is filling its task space like an isotropic Gaussian or collapsing into a handful of shortcut corners.

The heuristics that have accumulated to prevent collapse are not wrong. They are symptoms of an implicit distributional target that, once stated, lets us stop playing Whac-A-Mole and start measuring.
