Why Parallels Matter

The architectural parallels between LeJEPA's design and gsd-skill-creator's existing structures are not coincidental. Both projects rest on the same philosophical claim — that the right architecture beats brute force — and both arrive at that claim from the same observational position: accumulated heuristics that work in practice but resist principled explanation. LeJEPA's contribution is to show that when a distributional target is finally stated explicitly, the heuristics dissolve into symptoms. This page asks what that same move would look like applied to gsd-skill-creator.

The Four Load-Bearing Parallels

Parallels (a), (b), (g), and (i) carry the main argument of this mission. Each deserves an explicit defense.

(a) Heuristics replaced by provable objective ↔ Amiga Principle

The Amiga Principle is gsd-skill-creator's foundational architectural claim: remarkable outcomes come from well-specialized pieces cooperating on the cycle, not from brute-force accumulation. LeJEPA makes the same bet in ML form. Both projects observe that the industry has been accumulating workarounds — teacher-student networks, EMA schedulers, stop-gradient, negative samples on the ML side; bounded-learning caps, correction minimums, cooldowns, confidence gates on the GSD side — and both projects ask whether a single principled objective could subsume those workarounds. LeJEPA proves yes for representation learning. Phases 730-731 pilot the same question for gsd-skill-creator.

(b) Isotropic Gaussian target ↔ skill library coverage

The skill library is a latent space. Every skill is a point, every task is a region, and the library's value depends on how well the points cover the regions the user actually encounters. No mechanism currently measures whether new skills fill coverage gaps or duplicate existing coverage. LeJEPA's distributional-target methodology — state a target, measure the deviation, let the measurement drive design — transposes directly. The target need not be isotropic Gaussian in the literal sense (appropriate target for a skill library is an empirical question, potentially task-weighted); what transposes is the discipline of stating a target explicitly so the measurement becomes principled.

(g) Spearman 0.99 training signal ↔ retrospective feed-forward

GSD's mission-quality feedback loop currently runs through retrospectives: humans review, extract lessons, feed a small number of items forward to the next subversion. That's the right loop for qualitative synthesis, but it's external and human-bound. LeJEPA's Spearman-0.99 result shows that a cheap, label-free, internal signal can almost perfectly rank outcomes. Phase 733 (Intrinsic Telemetry) reproduces the pattern with GSD-internal signals (test pass density, skill invocation entropy, DACP fidelity compliance). The internal signal never replaces the retrospective — it augments it with a pre-verification estimate.

(i) LeWM predictor ↔ mission-state world-model

The GSD orchestrator dispatches waves and observes their outcomes. It does not predict them. LeWM's payoff is to show that planning in a compact latent world model is orders of magnitude faster than planning through a heavy pretrained encoder. Phase 732 ports the idea: a small encoder maps mission state to a compact latent, a predictor forecasts next mission state under a candidate action, and CEM over that predictor rehearses waves in latent space before dispatching them.

Hard constraint: the predictor sits under CAPCOM. The orchestrator's human-gate authority is never bypassed; predictions are advisory.
