## 1. The Theme Camp as Skill Cluster

Black Rock City's theme camps are self-organized communities within the larger city. A theme camp provides services to the playa, creates an environment that participants can visit, and anchors a portion of the city's cultural landscape. Each camp has a distinct identity, a set of skills it teaches, and a contribution it makes to the commons.

In the virtual BRC, a theme camp is a skill cluster: a named group of 3-8 related skills with a camp leader skill (the "lead ranger" of the cluster) and a declared contribution to the city.

**Cluster structure:**

```yaml
camp:
  name: [Camp Name]
  pnw_source: [Pacific Northwest taxonomy source]
  lead_skill: [the skill that anchors the cluster]
  member_skills: [3-7 additional skills]
  location: [city zone: inner, middle ring, outer, esplanade]
  contribution: [what this camp gives to the Black Sand Flat]
  collaboration_surfaces: [camps this cluster actively collaborates with]
```

The eight camps defined here cover the full social and functional spectrum of a running city. Additional camps can be added by any rig with Level 3 status -- but the city's core structure is these eight.

## 2. Cedar Grove

**PNW Source:** Western red cedar (*Thuja plicata*) ecosystem -- Columbia Valley rainforest
**Location:** Inner ring (structural anchor)
**Contribution:** The physical and logical skeleton of the city

Cedar Grove is the infrastructure camp. It builds the scaffolding that all other camps depend on, manages the build/strike lifecycle, and maintains the structural integrity of the city from the first post to the final cleanup sweep.

**Lead Skill:** Cascade Brigade (DPW infrastructure management)

**Member Skills:**
- Columbia Gate (entry/exit flow management)
- Territory Mapping (city placement and layout)
- Salish Access (credential management)
- Chinook Rider (mass transit coordination)

**Camp Philosophy:** A western red cedar in the PNW rainforest can live 1,500 years. The oldest trees in a cedar grove are the structural foundation of the forest -- their root systems stabilize the slope, their canopy regulates light and moisture, their fallen trunks become nurse logs for the next generation. Cedar Grove operates on the same principle: deep structure, long patience, foundational stability.

**Camp Color:** Forest green (#2D5A27)

## 3. Raven's Roost

**PNW Source:** Common raven (*Corvus corax*) -- intelligent observer, message carrier, trickster
**Location:** Esplanade (high-visibility, high-traffic position)
**Contribution:** Information, communication, first contact

Raven's Roost is the communication camp. Ravens are among the most intelligent birds -- they observe, they remember, they communicate across large territories, and they are the first to know when something has changed. The Roost is where messages are sent, stories are told, and new arrivals are welcomed.

**Lead Skill:** Raven's Welcome (Greeters / onboarding)

**Member Skills:**
- Raven Broadcast (BMIR / real-time information dissemination)
- Hemlock Reference (Playa Info / deep knowledge access)
- Raven Archive (Documentation Team / institutional memory)
- Raven Muster (Volunteer Coordination / rig matching)

**Camp Philosophy:** The raven carries the message but does not own it. Raven's Roost facilitates communication -- it does not control it. Every message that passes through the Roost belongs to its sender. The Roost is infrastructure, not censorship.

**Camp Color:** Midnight blue (#1A1A2E)

## 4. Salmon Run Camp

**PNW Source:** Chinook salmon (*Oncorhynchus tshawytscha*) -- upstream navigation, lifecycle completion, gift of return
**Location:** Middle ring (creative zone)
**Contribution:** Art grants, creative navigation, upstream problem-solving

Salmon Run Camp is the arts and creative services hub. The salmon runs upstream against the current to complete its lifecycle and return nutrients to the forest. Salmon Run Camp navigates the upstream problems: art grant administration, installation support, the creative pathfinding that makes difficult projects possible.

**Lead Skill:** Nurse Log Guild (ARTery / artist services)

**Member Skills:**
- Glacial Flow (Arctica / resource distribution)
- Salmon Run Traffic (Traffic / vehicle flow management)
- Salish Press (Media Mecca / media relations)
- Cascade Gathering (Special Events / ceremonial scheduling)

**Camp Philosophy:** The salmon's gift is not the journey -- it is the death. The salmon returns to the river where it was born, spawns, and dies, and its body feeds the forest it grew up in. The nutrients it carries from the ocean become the soil nutrients that feed the trees that shade the stream that cools the water that raises the next generation of salmon. Salmon Run Camp operates on this closed loop: creative resources invested now become the cultural nutrients that make the next generation possible.

**Camp Color:** River blue (#2E86AB)

## 5. Tide Pool Collective

**PNW Source:** Salish Sea intertidal zone -- dynamic boundary, high biodiversity, extreme resilience
**Location:** Inner ring (medical/safety anchor)
**Contribution:** Health, safety, emergency response, crisis intervention

Tide Pool Collective is the city's medical and safety hub. Intertidal zones are among the most ecologically complex and resilient environments on earth -- they must survive tidal cycles that alternately submerge and expose them, temperature extremes, physical battering, and intense biological competition. Tide Pool Collective brings that resilience to the human systems it protects.

**Lead Skill:** Tide Pool Medical (Emergency Services / mandatory-pass safety gate)

**Member Skills:**
- Watershed Watch (Black Rock Rangers / community safety)
- Fireline Cedar (Burn Perimeter / thermal boundary management)

**Camp Philosophy:** The tide pool does not choose its inhabitants. It provides the conditions and what can survive there, survives. Tide Pool Collective provides the safety conditions for the city -- not choosing who can participate, but ensuring the physical conditions for participation are survivable.

**Camp Color:** Teal (#0E9594)

## 6. Osprey Watch

**PNW Source:** Osprey (*Pandion haliaetus*) -- aerial observer, precision diver, continental migrator
**Location:** Outer ring (observation post)
**Contribution:** Census, observation, aerial survey, pattern analysis

Osprey Watch is the city's data and observation layer. The osprey hunts by soaring high above the water, spotting fish through the surface reflection, and diving with pinpoint precision. Osprey Watch soars above the city's activity patterns, sees what is happening across all camps, and dives for the specific data points that matter.

**Lead Skill:** Osprey Survey (Census / population analytics)

**Member Skills:**
- Osprey Landing (Airport / multi-modal arrival routing)
- Man Watch / Tahoma's Eye (apex structure monitoring)

**Camp Philosophy:** The osprey does not own the fish. It observes, acts, and releases. Osprey Watch collects data to serve the city's intelligence function -- not to control the city, but to help it understand itself.

**Camp Color:** Sky blue (#87CEEB) with brown accents

## 7. Old Growth Assembly

**PNW Source:** Olympic Peninsula old-growth forest -- deep time, accumulated knowledge, structural complexity
**Location:** Center (the heart of the city)
**Contribution:** Education, deep knowledge, mentorship, reference, community gathering

Old Growth Assembly is the city's educational and cultural heart -- the equivalent of Center Camp Cafe. Old-growth forests accumulate knowledge and complexity over centuries. The biggest trees are the teachers. The understory is the classroom. The whole system is a living archive.

**Lead Skill:** Old Growth Assembly (Center Camp / community gathering and knowledge sharing)

**Member Skills:**
- Hemlock Reference (Playa Info / always-available information)
- Firefly Circuit (Lamplighters / navigation infrastructure)
- Territory Mapping (Placement / city planning)

**Camp Philosophy:** You do not visit an old-growth forest. You enter it. There is a difference. Entering is a posture of respect and attention. Old Growth Assembly is a space you enter -- not a service you consume.

**Camp Color:** Deep green (#1B4332) with amber accents

## 8. Mycorrhizal Hub

**PNW Source:** Common mycorrhizal network -- underground fungal connections linking forest root systems
**Location:** Distributed (no fixed location; connects all camps)
**Contribution:** Co-learning, knowledge transfer, community connection

Mycorrhizal Hub is the connection layer between all other camps. Mycorrhizal networks link the root systems of trees across a forest -- transferring carbon, nitrogen, water, and chemical signals between trees that may be hundreds of meters apart. The Hub operates in the same way: it facilitates the connections between camps that those camps cannot see directly.

**Lead Skill:** Mycorrhizal Hub (Burning Man Hive / co-learning facilitation)

**Member Skills:**
- Raven Muster (cross-camp rig matching)

**Camp Philosophy:** The mycorrhizal network does not have a center. Every node is equally connected. The Hub does not direct traffic -- it creates the conditions for connection. What flows through the network belongs to the trees, not the fungi.

**Camp Color:** Mushroom brown (#8B6914) with forest green

## 9. Fireline Cedar

**PNW Source:** Fire ecology of PNW forests -- prescribed burn, fire succession, regeneration
**Location:** Outer perimeter (safety boundary)
**Contribution:** Burn perimeter management, pyrotechnics safety, fire management

Fireline Cedar is the ceremonial and safety camp for all burn events. PNW fire ecology teaches that fire is not destruction -- it is transformation. The lodgepole pine's serotinous cones only open in fire. The black-backed woodpecker only nests in recently burned forest. Fire creates the conditions for species that could not exist without it.

**Lead Skill:** Fireline Cedar (Burn Perimeter Support / thermal boundary management)

**Member Skills:**
- Geoduck Watch (environmental monitoring during burns)

**Camp Philosophy:** The fire is not the enemy of the forest. The fire is the forest's transformation mechanism. Fireline Cedar contains the fire so it can do its work -- and no more than its work.

**Camp Color:** Ember orange (#E25822)

## 10. Art Installation Taxonomy

Art installations in the virtual BRC are emergent -- they arise from the interaction of multiple camp skills. They cannot be added to the catalog directly. They can only be discovered.

The system records an installation when:
1. Three or more camp skills activate within the same execution context
2. The combined output exceeds what any individual skill could produce
3. A witness rig attests that something new appeared that was not present before the combination

**The Twelve Archetypes:**

| Installation Name | Emergence Condition |
|-------------------|---------------------|
| **The Nurse Log** | Cedar Grove build + Old Growth Archive: structured decay that seeds new growth |
| **The Mycorrhizal Mandala** | Three or more camps sharing a common knowledge thread |
| **The Tidal Reach** | Tide Pool Medical + Osprey Survey: the edge of the known |
| **The Salmon Ladder** | Salmon Run navigation + Cascade Brigade infrastructure: upstream path through complexity |
| **The Raven Mirror** | Raven's Roost communication reflecting what was sent |
| **The Whitebark Sentinel** | Trust-level boundary: the skill that only activates above Level 3 |
| **The Geoduck Deep** | Geoduck Watch + Mycorrhizal Hub: roots too deep to move |
| **The Orca Wake** | Orca Sled mobile chipset path traced through the city |
| **The Canopy Break** | Gap where a deprecated skill used to be: light through absence |
| **The Fire Succession** | Post-burn regeneration cycle: what grows where the fire was |
| **The Mother Tree** | The apex skill from which all others in a cluster draw nutrition |
| **The Estuary** | Boundary where fresh-water skill ecosystem meets the salt-water Wasteland economy |

## 11. Emergent Composition Patterns

Not all camp interactions produce installations. Some produce simpler compositions:

**Duet (2 camps):** Two skills from different camps activate together and produce a shared artifact that neither could produce alone. The artifact is attributed to both camps.

**Triad (3 camps):** Three skills activate together. The interaction pattern is complex enough that the outcome is not predictable from the inputs. This is the minimum configuration for an art installation.

**Full Circle (5+ camps):** When five or more camps activate in the same execution context, the composition is designated a "City Art Event" -- the virtual BRC equivalent of the Man burn or the Temple. These are rare and not designed in advance.

**Cascade:** When one camp's activation triggers another camp's activation (dependency-driven rather than simultaneous), the pattern is a cascade rather than an installation. Cascades are expected infrastructure behavior. They are not art.
