There is a frequency no commercial broadcaster will ever hold. It is not licensed to be profitable; it is licensed to exist. In 1932, Graham Spry distilled the entire debate into seven words: “it’s the state, or the United States.” Ninety-four years later, the theorem still holds. The geography has not changed. The border still runs five thousand kilometres. The American content machine has only grown more powerful. What has changed is that the CBC now faces that external pressure and a new internal one — and the gap between those two forces is where the entire institution currently lives.

Research Modules

M1: History and Mandate

History Track A · 1929–2026

The Aird Commission (1929), founding of the CBC (1936), television expansion (1952), Massey and Fowler Commissions, Broadcasting Acts of 1958 and 1991, Canadian content regulation, and the cultural sovereignty doctrine that has animated every chapter of the institution's life.

M2: Funding and Governance

Governance Track A · Crown corporation

Crown corporation structure, board composition, parliamentary appropriation mechanics ($1.4B/yr), per-capita funding gap (Canada $32 vs G7 $62), the defunding debate, Liberal statutory-funding proposal, and the architectural impossibility of separating English from French services.

M3: Service Architecture

Services Track B · Full portfolio

Four radio networks, four television networks, CBC Gem streaming (6,500+ hours), Northern Service, Radio Canada International (11 languages), Canadian content quotas, major programming franchises, and the organizational distinction between CBC and Radio-Canada.

M4: Indigenous Languages and Reconciliation

Indigenous Track B · OCAP compliant

CBC Northern Service (1958), eight Indigenous-language broadcasts with nation-specific attribution, the 2024 National Indigenous Strategy, Indigenous Language Archives (64,000+ programs), APTN relationship, Beyond 94 TRC tracking, and OCAP principles throughout.

M5: Digital Transformation and Cultural Defence

Digital Synthesis · Future-facing

CBC Gem trajectory, the 2025–2030 strategic plan, YouTube pivot (The National viewership exceeding linear), trust data trends (Reuters Institute), the Trump-era sovereignty argument, statutory appropriation proposal, and the “state or the United States” theorem in 2026.
