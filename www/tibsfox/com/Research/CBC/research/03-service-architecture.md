# Service Architecture

> **Domain:** Canadian Public Broadcasting
> **Module:** 3 -- Radio Networks, Television Services, CBC Gem, Northern Service, and Radio Canada International
> **Through-line:** *The CBC is not one broadcaster. It is four radio networks, four television networks, a streaming platform, a Northern Service broadcasting in eight Indigenous languages, and an international service in eleven languages -- all operated by a single Crown corporation serving a country that stretches 5,515 kilometres across six time zones.* The service architecture is the mandate made physical: every frequency, every channel, every stream is an answer to the question of whether the country can hear itself.

## 1. Service Portfolio Overview

CBC/Radio-Canada operates six categories of service, each serving a distinct audience and mandate requirement:

```
CBC/RADIO-CANADA SERVICE PORTFOLIO
================================================================

  RADIO (4 networks)
  +-- CBC Radio One (English: news, current affairs, arts)
  +-- CBC Music (English: music, arts, culture)
  +-- Ici Radio-Canada Premiere (French: news, current affairs)
  +-- Ici Musique (French: music, arts, culture)

  TELEVISION (4+ networks)
  +-- CBC Television (English: general entertainment, news)
  +-- Ici Radio-Canada Tele (French: general entertainment, news)
  +-- CBC News Network (English: 24-hour news)
  +-- ICI RDI (French: 24-hour news)
  +-- ICI ARTV (French: arts and culture)
  +-- ICI EXPLORA (French: science and discovery)
  +-- Documentary (English: shared with Shaw)

  STREAMING
  +-- CBC Gem (English + French; ad-supported and premium)

  NORTHERN SERVICE
  +-- CBC North (8 Indigenous languages + English + French)

  INTERNATIONAL
  +-- Radio Canada International (11 languages; web + podcast)

  DIGITAL
  +-- cbc.ca (English)
  +-- radio-canada.ca (French)
  +-- CBC Indigenous platform
```

## 2. Radio Networks

### CBC Radio One

CBC Radio One is the English-language network carrying news, current affairs, arts, and documentary programming. It operates as a network of local and regional stations, each contributing programming to the national schedule while carrying local content during designated time slots [1].

Key characteristics:
- **Format:** Spoken word, news, current affairs, documentary, arts
- **Distribution:** FM and AM terrestrial; live streaming via cbc.ca and CBC Listen app
- **Flagship programs:** The Current, As It Happens, Metro Morning (Toronto), The Early Edition (Vancouver)
- **Reach:** Available in every Canadian province and territory

### CBC Music

CBC Music (formerly CBC Radio 2 until 2007) focuses on music, arts, and cultural programming. The rebrand from "Radio 2" to "CBC Music" reflected a shift toward cross-platform distribution including online streaming and curated playlists [2].

### Ici Radio-Canada Premiere

The French-language equivalent of CBC Radio One, carrying news, current affairs, and cultural programming for francophone audiences. Primary coverage area is Quebec, but available via cable, satellite, and streaming nationally and in francophone communities outside Quebec [1].

### Ici Musique

The French-language music and cultural network, equivalent to CBC Music. Serves Quebec and francophone communities nationally.

### The Microwave Network

CBC's terrestrial radio infrastructure includes a microwave relay network stretching approximately 6,400 kilometres from British Columbia to Nova Scotia -- one of the longest microwave relay chains in the world. This physical infrastructure predates satellite distribution and remains operational as a backbone for signal transmission [3].

## 3. Television Networks

### CBC Television (English)

CBC Television launched September 6, 1952, from Montreal (CBFT) and Toronto (CBLT) and operates as a must-carry service on cable and satellite systems nationally. It carries a general programming schedule including drama, comedy, documentary, news, sports, and children's programming [4].

### Ici Radio-Canada Tele (French)

The French-language general television service, carrying drama, entertainment, news, and cultural programming for francophone audiences. Major productions include Tout le monde en parle, District 31, and Les Enfants de la tele [4].

### CBC News Network

English-language 24-hour news channel, launched in 1989 as CBC Newsworld. Carries rolling news coverage, analysis programs, and breaking news. Distributed via cable and satellite as a specialty channel [5].

### ICI RDI (Reseau de l'information)

French-language 24-hour news channel, the francophone equivalent of CBC News Network. Launched in 1995 [5].

### Specialty Channels

- **ICI ARTV:** French-language arts and culture channel
- **ICI EXPLORA:** French-language science and discovery programming
- **Documentary Channel:** English-language documentary programming (shared ownership with Shaw/Corus)

### The National: Flagship Newscast

The National is CBC Television's flagship evening newscast, hosted by Adrienne Arsenault and Ian Hanomansing. It has served as the corporation's primary news program since its launch in 1952 and has undergone multiple format changes while maintaining its role as the CBC's journalistic centerpiece [4].

The program's significance extends beyond ratings. As the most-watched CBC news program, The National serves as the public face of CBC journalism. Its credibility and perceived impartiality directly affect public trust in the CBC as an institution. The Reuters Institute trust data (71% in 2020, declining to 63% in 2024) measures trust in "CBC News" as a brand -- and The National is the primary expression of that brand [5].

The milestone of The National's YouTube viewership exceeding its linear TV viewership represents a fundamental shift in how Canadians access their national newscast. It is documented in detail in [Module 5: Digital Transformation](05-digital-transformation.md).

### News Network Operations

CBC News Network (English) and ICI RDI (French) operate as 24-hour news channels distributed via cable and satellite. They carry:

- Rolling news coverage with regular anchor-led updates
- Analysis and opinion programs throughout the day
- Breaking news coverage with full-screen continuous format
- Specialty programs on politics, business, culture, and international affairs

Both channels serve as feeder services for CBC/Radio-Canada's news ecosystem: stories break on the 24-hour channels, are developed by newsroom teams, and are packaged for The National, radio programs, cbc.ca, and social platforms. The 24-hour channels are the newsroom's operational backbone [5].

## 4. CBC Gem Streaming Platform

CBC Gem launched in 2018, replacing the CBC TV app. It represents the corporation's primary digital distribution platform and the centerpiece of its 2025-2030 digital strategy [6].

### Content Library

- **6,500+ hours** of live and on-demand content
- **800+ documentaries** across all genres
- **500 hours** of ad-free children's programming
- **200+ Canadian feature films**
- Original series, acquired content, and international co-productions

### Distribution

CBC Gem is available on: iOS, Android, Roku, Samsung Smart TV, Apple TV, Amazon Fire TV, LG Smart TV, Google Chromecast, Android TV, and Xbox. It offers two tiers:

- **Free tier:** Ad-supported access to most content
- **Premium tier:** Ad-free viewing plus exclusive content

### Paris 2024 Olympic Metrics

The Paris 2024 Olympics demonstrated CBC Gem's growing significance as a distribution platform [7]:

| Metric | Value | Comparison |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| TV viewers (all platforms) | 27 million (7 in 10 Canadians) | -- |
| Hours streamed on digital platforms | 24.3 million hours | +170% vs Tokyo 2020 |
| Time on CBC Gem | (significant increase) | +307% vs Tokyo 2020 |
| Connected TVs share of Gem viewing | 65% | -- |
| The National: YouTube vs linear | YouTube viewership > linear | First time exceeded |

The 307% increase in Gem viewing time from Tokyo (2021) to Paris (2024) represents the fastest growth metric in CBC's digital history and validates the platform as a primary rather than supplementary distribution channel.

### Content Highlights

Original Canadian programming available on CBC Gem includes:

- **Schitt's Creek** -- international breakout hit; 9 Emmy Awards in 2020
- **Kim's Convenience** -- Toronto-set comedy; international distribution via Netflix
- **Heartland** -- longest-running Canadian scripted series (18+ seasons)
- **Murdoch Mysteries** -- period detective drama (18+ seasons)
- **SKYMED** -- Northern Canadian medical drama
- **Sort Of** -- critically acclaimed dramedy
- **North of North** -- co-production with APTN and Netflix

## 5. CBC Northern Service

CBC's Northern Service was established in 1958 to bring broadcasting to Canada's northern communities. It operates as a distinct service division with unique programming requirements driven by geography, language diversity, and the cultural needs of Indigenous communities [8].

The Northern Service broadcasts in eight Indigenous languages in addition to English and French. Coverage extends across the Northwest Territories, Nunavut, Yukon, northern Quebec (Nunavik), and northern regions of several provinces.

The Northern Service is documented in detail in [Module 4: Indigenous Languages and Reconciliation](04-indigenous-languages.md), which covers the language portfolio, National Indigenous Strategy, and OCAP compliance framework.

## 6. Radio Canada International

Radio Canada International (RCI) historically operated as a shortwave broadcasting service, transmitting Canadian content globally via high-frequency radio from the Sackville, New Brunswick transmitter site. In 2012, as part of budget reductions, the shortwave service was terminated and RCI transitioned to a web and podcast service [9].

### Current Operations

RCI now operates as a multilingual web and podcast service in 11 languages:

| Language | Target Audience |
|----------|----------------|
| English | Global anglophone |
| French | Francophone world |
| Spanish | Latin America, Spain |
| Arabic | Middle East, North Africa |
| Simplified Chinese | China, diaspora |
| Traditional Chinese | Taiwan, Hong Kong, diaspora |
| Punjabi | Punjab (India/Pakistan), diaspora |
| Tagalog | Philippines, diaspora |
| Russian | Russia, diaspora |
| Korean | Korea, diaspora |
| Portuguese | Brazil, Portugal, diaspora |

The language selection reflects Canada's immigration patterns and diaspora connections. Content focuses on Canadian news, immigration information, and cultural programming relevant to newcomers and internationally connected Canadians [9].

### Accessibility

CBC Gem provides:
- Closed captioning on all long-form content
- Described video (audio description) on select programming
- Interface accessibility features compliant with WCAG guidelines

Accessibility is both a mandate requirement (CRTC license conditions) and a service obligation consistent with the Broadcasting Act's requirement that CBC programming be "available throughout Canada" -- a phrase interpreted to include availability to Canadians with disabilities [6].

## 7. Canadian Content in Practice

The Canadian content (CanCon) regulatory framework, administered by the CRTC, shapes every aspect of CBC's programming schedule. The CBC, as the national public broadcaster, is held to higher CanCon standards than private broadcasters [10].

### Television CanCon Requirements

CBC's broadcasting license requires that:
- A specified percentage of daytime programming be certified Canadian content
- A specified percentage of prime-time programming be certified Canadian
- Expenditure thresholds on Canadian programming be met annually

Programs qualify as Canadian content through a points system based on the nationality of key creative positions: director, screenwriter, lead performers, director of photography, editor, and others. A program must accumulate a minimum number of points to qualify [10].

### The CanCon Tension

The CanCon system creates a structural tension: the CBC must program Canadian content that audiences choose to watch in competition with American programming available through cable, satellite, and streaming. If Canadian content is not competitive in quality and appeal, audiences migrate to alternatives, which undermines both ratings and the political case for public broadcasting.

The most successful CBC programs -- Schitt's Creek, Kim's Convenience, Heartland -- demonstrate that Canadian content can achieve international recognition, but these successes exist alongside programming that draws modest domestic audiences.

## 8. Major Programming Franchises

### News and Current Affairs

- **The National:** Flagship evening newscast; hosted by Adrienne Arsenault and Ian Hanomansing. YouTube viewership now exceeds linear TV viewership -- a milestone that defines the digital transition [7].
- **The Current:** Morning current affairs program on CBC Radio One
- **As It Happens:** Evening radio program; one of CBC's longest-running formats
- **Power & Politics:** Political affairs program on CBC News Network

### Drama and Comedy

- **Heartland:** Longest-running scripted Canadian television series (18+ seasons)
- **Murdoch Mysteries:** Period detective drama set in turn-of-century Toronto (18+ seasons)
- **SKYMED:** Contemporary drama set in northern Manitoba's medical air service
- **Sort Of:** Award-winning dramedy

### Sports

The CBC's sports broadcasting history includes decades as the home of Hockey Night in Canada, which moved to Rogers/Sportsnet in 2014. The CBC now focuses on Olympic Games broadcasting as its primary sports property, with the Paris 2024 Olympics drawing 27 million Canadian viewers [7].

### Documentary

- **The Passionate Eye:** Flagship documentary strand on CBC Television
- **The Fifth Estate:** Investigative journalism program (running since 1975)
- **CBC Docs:** Digital-first documentary content

### Children's

- **CBC Kids:** Dedicated programming block with 500+ hours of ad-free content on CBC Gem

### The Hockey Night in Canada Era

For decades, Hockey Night in Canada was the CBC's most-watched program and a cornerstone of Canadian cultural identity. The program aired Saturday evenings on CBC Television from 1952 until 2014, when Rogers Communications acquired the national NHL broadcasting rights in a 12-year, $5.232 billion deal [14].

The loss of Hockey Night in Canada was a defining moment for the CBC. It removed the corporation's single most-watched program and its most valuable advertising property. The decision forced the CBC to rebuild its entertainment and sports programming strategy, ultimately leading to greater investment in Olympic broadcasting as the primary sports property.

The Paris 2024 Olympics (27 million Canadian viewers; 307% increase in Gem streaming) demonstrated that the Olympic model could fill some of the audience gap left by hockey, but on a quadrennial rather than weekly basis [3].

### Dragons' Den and Format Licensing

Dragons' Den, the Canadian adaptation of the Japanese format Money no Tora, is one of the CBC's longest-running unscripted programs (since 2006). It demonstrates the CBC's participation in the global format licensing market -- acquiring proven entertainment formats and producing them with Canadian talent, Canadian entrepreneurs, and Canadian business stories [14].

The format licensing model illustrates the tension in CanCon regulation: a program produced in Canada with Canadian participants qualifies as Canadian content, even if the format concept originated elsewhere. This is by design -- the CanCon system measures Canadian creative employment, not concept originality.

## 9. The English-French Service Distinction

The CBC and Radio-Canada operate as separate editorial entities within a single Crown corporation. The distinction is more than linguistic:

- **Separate newsrooms:** English and French journalists operate independently with separate editorial management chains
- **Separate programming:** Drama, comedy, and documentary content is commissioned and produced separately for each language market
- **Separate audiences:** English services compete primarily with American content; French services compete primarily within the Quebec media market (TVA, Noovo, Club illico)
- **Shared infrastructure:** Buildings, transmission systems, IT, and corporate services are shared

The market dynamics differ fundamentally. English CBC competes against the full weight of American media -- Netflix, Disney+, Amazon, and all US broadcast and cable networks. Radio-Canada competes primarily within the Quebec francophone market, where it has a stronger competitive position partly because the French-language market has a natural linguistic barrier against anglophone content [8].

This asymmetry explains why the Conservative defunding proposal targets English services specifically: the competitive argument (that the CBC duplicates what the private market already provides) is strongest in English-language markets where American content is plentiful. In French-language markets, Radio-Canada fills a role with fewer substitutes.

## 10. Audience Reach and Measurement

### Linear Television

Winter/Spring 2024 data (Numeris measurement): 21.3 million Canadians aged 18+ reached by CBC/Radio-Canada's linear conventional and specialty television stations [7].

### Digital

- **CBC Gem:** 30+ million video content starts in Winter/Spring 2024 (Adobe Analytics) [7]
- **cbc.ca:** One of Canada's most-visited news websites
- **CBC Listen:** Audio streaming app for radio and podcasts
- **YouTube:** The National's YouTube audience now exceeds its linear TV audience [7]

### Demographics Challenge

The 2025-2030 strategic plan identifies three underserved audience segments:
1. **Youth and Gen Z:** An estimated 40% of Canadians aged 18-24 use social media and video networks as their primary news source [11]
2. **Newcomers to Canada:** Growing immigrant population with specific information needs
3. **"Non-users or dissatisfied users":** Canadians who have disengaged from the CBC

CEO Bouchard has stated that the corporation must move away from formats that "parents and grandparents used" to reach these segments [11].

### The Perception Problem: Toronto/Montreal Centrism

A persistent criticism of the CBC, particularly from Western Canadian audiences, is that the corporation's programming reflects the perspectives and priorities of Toronto and Montreal at the expense of other regions. This perception has contributed to lower CBC viewership and trust in Western provinces compared to Ontario and Atlantic Canada [11].

The 2025-2030 strategic plan explicitly addresses this by committing to a larger on-the-ground journalistic presence in rural areas and Western Canada. CEO Bouchard has acknowledged the perception as a real problem requiring structural change, not merely a messaging issue [11].

The regional dimension of CBC's audience challenge intersects with the political dimension: the Conservative Party's strongest support base is in Western Canada, where CBC viewership is lowest and criticism is highest. The correlation is not causal in a simple direction -- critics argue both that low viewership causes political vulnerability and that political criticism suppresses viewership.

## 11. Infrastructure and Distribution

### Terrestrial

- **Radio:** FM and AM transmitters across all provinces and territories
- **Television:** Over-the-air transmitters in major markets; must-carry status on cable and satellite
- **Microwave relay:** 6,400 km east-west backbone (one of the world's longest) [3]

### Satellite

- CBC services distributed via satellite to cable head-ends and direct-to-home subscribers nationally
- Northern Service relies heavily on satellite for reaching remote communities

### Digital

- CBC Gem: OTT streaming platform (geo-restricted to Canadian IP addresses)
- cbc.ca / radio-canada.ca: Web properties
- CBC Listen: Audio streaming
- Social platforms: YouTube, Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, X

### Cross-Border Signal

Vancouver-area CBC Radio One (CBU-FM, 690 AM / 88.1 FM) is receivable in the Puget Sound area of Washington State. CBC Television signals from Vancouver historically reached into northern Washington. Digital geo-fencing has replaced signal propagation as the primary distribution boundary [12].
