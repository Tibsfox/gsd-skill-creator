LIGO Gravitational Waves

Interferometry · Strain Measurement · Chirp Analysis · Multi-Messenger Astronomy · Sonification
Overview Interferometry GW150914 Chirp Analysis Multi-Messenger Visualization Mission Pack
On September 14, 2015, at 09:50:45 UTC, the twin LIGO detectors — one in Hanford, Washington, deep in the Pacific Northwest — recorded a transient gravitational-wave signal. The strain amplitude was 10−21: a displacement one-thousandth the diameter of a proton across a 4-kilometer arm. Two black holes, 1.3 billion light-years away, had spiraled together and merged in 0.2 seconds. Einstein predicted the wave in 1916. It took a century of engineering to hear it. The space between prediction and detection is where this story lives.

Research Modules

M1: LIGO Hanford & Interferometer Design

Detector Track A · PNW connection

LIGO Hanford Observatory on the DOE Hanford Site in Washington state. Michelson interferometer with 4-km Fabry-Pérot arms. Quantum squeezing, seismic isolation, mirror suspension. O1–O4 observing runs. KAGRA and Virgo network. The 10−21 strain sensitivity that required reinventing precision measurement.

M2: First Detection — GW150914

Discovery Track A · Nobel 2017

The first direct observation of gravitational waves. Binary black hole merger at z=0.09, 36+29 solar masses. Weiss, Thorne, and Barish Nobel Prize 2017. Matched-filter detection, parameter estimation, statistical significance. From Einstein’s 1916 prediction through Weber bars to the LIGO breakthrough.

M3: Chirp Signal Analysis & Processing

DSP Track A · Signal recovery

Matched filtering against compact binary coalescence templates. Whitening, bandpassing, Q-transform spectrograms. PyCBC and GWpy pipelines. Waveform approximants (IMRPhenom, EOB). Noise characterization: seismic, thermal, quantum shot noise. The signal buried 10,000× below the noise floor.

M4: Multi-Messenger Astronomy

Astrophysics Track B · GW170817

GW170817: the binary neutron star merger that launched multi-messenger astronomy. Electromagnetic counterpart GRB 170817A detected 1.7 seconds later. Kilonova AT 2017gfo. r-process nucleosynthesis confirmed. 70+ observatories across the spectrum. GraceDB alerts, sky localization, O4 detection rates.

M5: Data Visualization & Sonification

Rendering Track B · The cosmic oscilloscope

GWOSC open data pipelines. Q-transform spectrograms as 3D terrain. OpenGL screensaver architecture with GLSL shaders. Sonification: frequency-shifting chirps into audible range (+400 Hz). Minecraft data bridge via RCON. The entire path from 10−21 strain to photon and sound wave.
