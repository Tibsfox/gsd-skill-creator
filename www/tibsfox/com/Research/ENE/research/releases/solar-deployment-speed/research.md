## Abstract

Climate change is caused by cumulative emissions -- it matters how much CO2 we emit between now and midcentury, not just whether we eventually decarbonize. Speed of solar deployment is therefore the critical variable. Solar panel installation is fundamentally a labor-intensive manual process: modules weigh 70-80 lbs, must be lifted overhead hundreds of times per day, and panels are getting bigger and heavier. Labor shortages already constrain deployment. Maximo, a robotics solution from AES (Fortune 500), is designed specifically for solar installation to break through the labor bottleneck.

## Key Findings

### Cumulative Emissions Framing
- Climate change driven by total CO2 emitted between now and midcentury
- Speed of deployment matters more than eventual decarbonization
- Every year of delay adds permanently to the cumulative total

### The Labor Bottleneck
- Panel weight: 70-80 lbs per module (equivalent to 3 stacked 24-packs of water)
- Must be lifted overhead hundreds of times per day
- Panels getting bigger and heavier as efficiency improves
- Existing labor shortages intensifying with growing demand

### Multiple Interconnected Bottlenecks
- Labor availability
- Interconnection availability (grid connection queue)
- Land availability
- Raw materials
- Construction timelines
- "Everything is interconnected" -- solving one reveals the next

### Robotics Solution
- Maximo: robotics designed specifically for solar panel installation
- General-purpose automation insufficient -- must address unique physical demands
- Created within AES by Daisy Yumi Asami (EE background), founded 2023
- Benefits entire EPC ecosystem: faster builds, more schedule certainty

### Hyperscaler Demand
- Data center operators driving new urgency
- Dramatic energy demand projections
- Pressure for faster construction with delivery certainty
