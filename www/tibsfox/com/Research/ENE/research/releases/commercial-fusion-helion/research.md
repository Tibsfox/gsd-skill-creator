## Abstract

Helion Energy is building the world's first commercial fusion power plant, with a power purchase agreement to supply Microsoft by 2028. The core technology -- Field Reversed Configuration (FRC) with deuterium-helium-3 aneutronic fusion -- produces charged particles instead of neutrons, enabling direct energy recovery at 95% pulse power efficiency without steam turbines. 1 kg of fusion fuel provides as much energy as 10 million kg of coal. The NRC ruled fusion will be regulated by states (like particle accelerators), dramatically shortening deployment timelines.

## Key Findings

### Field Reversed Configuration (FRC)
- Two FRCs formed separately, accelerated together, merged in compression section
- Electromagnetic field coils squeeze merged FRC until fusion conditions reached (100M+ degrees)
- Charged fusion products push back against magnetic field -- direct energy to electricity
- "Diesel engine analogy": magnetic piston compresses fuel, nuclear energy pushes piston back

### Aneutronic Fuel Cycle
- Primary: Deuterium + Helium-3 --> Proton + Helium-4 (both charged, no neutrons)
- Side reaction: D + D --> Tritium + He-3 (generates future fuel)
- Closed fuel loop: only external input is deuterium distilled from seawater
- No ionizing radiation damage to materials, longer system lifetimes, simpler regulation

### 95% Pulse Power Efficiency
- Only ~5% of input energy must be recovered through fusion to achieve net gain
- Fundamentally low-Q system -- engineering advantage over tokamaks
- Direct energy conversion bypasses 30-40% steam turbine efficiency losses

### Deployment Architecture
- Standard shipping containers for pulse power and fusion cores
- Assembly-line production: "building aircraft, not airports"
- Standard tilt-up concrete warehouse (~100,000 sq ft)
- No cooling towers, no massive turbine infrastructure
- Geography-independent: no sun, wind, geothermal requirements

### Regulatory Breakthrough
- NRC ruled fusion regulated by states (like particle accelerators), not federal nuclear fission regulations
- Dramatically shortens deployment timelines
