Mission Package

The hardest single part of building a software system is deciding precisely what to build. — Fred Brooks, The Mythical Man-Month (1975). This complete 16-lecture course from Carnegie Mellon covers the entire engineering discipline between “we trained a model” and “it works in production.”

Mission Objectives

  1. Complete course coverage — all 16 lectures of CMU 17-214/17-614 (Spring 2025), from requirements through fairness and operations
  2. Extract the engineering discipline that distinguishes ML research from ML production: requirements, testing, deployment, monitoring, data quality
  3. Map pedagogical patterns — how Kastner and Le Goues teach complex engineering concepts through case studies, breakouts, and deliberate provocation
  4. Connect to our architecture — Gastown chipset, GSD workflow, and the Amiga Principle as production ML infrastructure

Key Case Studies

Target Canada ($5.4B Failure)

75,000 products entered by inexperienced staff. ~30% accuracy. Wrong dimensions meant items didn’t fit shipping containers. 133 stores closed. Stock price rose on announcement.

The Legal Chatbot

CTO explicitly wanted 80% accuracy. A perfect chatbot would cannibalize lead generation. Data scientists optimizing accuracy were working against the business objective.

Booking.com 150-Model Study

Zero correlation between offline accuracy and online conversion rates across 150 deployed models. Hypothesized uncanny valley: too-accurate recommendations felt creepy.
