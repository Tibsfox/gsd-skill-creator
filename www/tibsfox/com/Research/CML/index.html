Research Modules

M1: Requirements & Goals Decomposition

Requirements Lecture 3 · Claire Le Goues

Goals decompose into four conflicting layers: organizational, leading indicators, system goals, model properties. The “World vs. Machine” framework. Environmental assumptions as failure origin. The legal chatbot case: 80% accuracy was the feature, not the bug. Booking.com’s 150-model study: zero correlation between offline accuracy and online conversion.

M2: Model Quality & Measurement

Quality Lecture 5 · Le Goues / Kastner

“Performance” is dangerously overloaded. 99% accuracy means nothing without baseline, error distribution, and cost model. Measure-data-operationalize pipeline. Inter-annotator agreement as the true ceiling. Label leakage as epidemic. Temporal dependence: the single most common student mistake. The streetlight effect in metric selection.

M3: Teams, Conflict & Premortems

Teams Lecture 6 · Christian Kastner

Group think and social loafing are the two dominant failure modes. Conflict avoidance is more destructive than conflict itself. Premortems more valuable than postmortems. Difficult conversations have a learnable structure: problem not blame, I-statements, active listening. Autonomy, mastery, and purpose drive motivation.

M4: Behavioral Model Testing

Testing Lecture 7 · Claire Le Goues

Not all inputs are equal. Capabilities as partial specifications. Metamorphic testing solves the oracle problem without labels. Property-based testing for generative AI. LLM-as-judge requires calibration against human agreement. Simulation-based testing for autonomous systems. Software testing terminology does not transfer cleanly to ML.

M5: Pipeline Testing & CI

Pipelines Lecture 8 · Kastner

The model is a tiny fraction of a production ML system. Notebooks are anti-testable by design. Data wrangling code is where silent bugs live. CI’s primary benefit: tests actually get executed. TDD produces inherently more testable code. “If somebody asks you to achieve a specific amount of coverage, quit.”

M6: Deployment Architecture

Deployment Lecture 9 · Kastner

Deploying a model is trivially easy — until it isn’t. Five deployment patterns: static, client-side, server-centric, backend-cached, hybrid. Architectural thinking is making hard-to-change decisions early. Training-serving skew is a silent killer. Feature stores solve redundant reimplementation. Multi-model composition is the norm.

M7: Data Quality

Data Lecture 11 · Kastner

Garbage in, garbage out is not overcome by scale. Target Canada’s $5.4B failure from data entry errors. Schema enforcement is necessary but an incredibly low bar. Data drift vs. concept drift require different remediation. “Raw data” is an oxymoron. Four systemic failure modes compound over time.

M8: Synthesis — Engineering ML Systems

Synthesis Cross-lecture · Course Integration

The complete 16-lecture arc from requirements through operations. The model is not the system. The system is not the organization. Requirements engineering, quality measurement, team dynamics, testing methodology, deployment architecture, data quality, and monitoring as a unified engineering discipline.
