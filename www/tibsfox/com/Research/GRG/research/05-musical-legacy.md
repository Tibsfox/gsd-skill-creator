# Musical Legacy and Cultural Impact

## Dave Matthews Band -- 79 Shows and Counting

According to Wikipedia (updated through 2025), Dave Matthews Band has played 79 shows at the Gorge Amphitheatre, the most of any artist at the venue. The band has maintained an annual Labor Day weekend three-night residency that has become one of the most celebrated recurring events in Pacific Northwest concert culture.

### Official Live Releases from the Gorge

DMB's relationship with the Gorge is documented through three official live releases:

| Release | Format | Date | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| *The Gorge* | 2-CD / 1-DVD | June 29, 2004 | Highlights from the 2002 tour closer (three-night stand) |
| *Live Trax Vol. 44* | Multi-disc | December 8, 2017 | Complete September 4, 2016 show |
| *Take Me Back: Live From the Gorge* | Multi-disc | February 6, 2026 | Complete August 30, 2025 performance of *Before These Crowded Streets* |

The Labor Day weekend residency has become a pilgrimage event for DMB fans, many of whom camp on-site or at adjacent campgrounds for the full three-night run. The tradition transforms the venue into a temporary community -- a pattern that parallels the festival ecosystem described below. DMB's 79-show count at a single venue is among the highest artist-venue relationships in American live music history.

## Pearl Jam

According to Wikipedia and the Live at the Gorge box set entry, Seattle-based Pearl Jam released *Live at the Gorge 05/06*, a seven-disc live box set through Rhino Entertainment/Warner Music Group on June 26, 2007, documenting their 2005 and 2006 shows at the venue. The box set debuted at #36 on the Billboard 200 with approximately 19,000 copies sold in its first week.

Pearl Jam's relationship with the Gorge carries particular significance because the band is from Seattle. The Gorge is their regional outdoor venue -- the place where Pacific Northwest rock meets Pacific Northwest landscape. The band's celebrated 1993 joint concert with Neil Young was one of the venue's earliest landmark events at the newly renamed Gorge. Pearl Jam guitarist Mike McCready appears in the 2021 *Enormous* documentary, connecting the band's history to the venue's narrative.

## Phish

According to Wikipedia, Phish has played the Gorge Amphitheatre 22 times since 1997, making it one of the jam-band community's most significant recurring venues. Like DMB, Phish's multi-night Gorge runs attract a camping audience that treats the event as a destination rather than a show. The jam-band community's affinity for the Gorge is rooted in the same qualities that attracted Dr. Bryan: the acoustics, the landscape, and the sense of being somewhere geographically extraordinary.

## Festival Ecosystem

The Gorge Amphitheatre has hosted a diverse ecosystem of music festivals spanning genres from country to EDM to multi-genre indie rock. The festival model transforms the venue from a single-show destination into a multi-day community event, with on-site camping as a central feature.

| Festival | Genre | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Sasquatch! Music Festival | Multi-genre indie | Annual Memorial Day weekend; became flagship cultural event |
| Watershed Festival | Country | Three-day; nominee 2013 ACM Festival of the Year |
| Bass Canyon | Dubstep/bass EDM | Founded 2018 by Excision; annual late summer |
| Beyond Wonderland | EDM/dance | Annual since 2021; Insomniac Events production |
| Paradiso Festival | EDM/dance | Annual summer event |

The festival ecosystem demonstrates the Gorge's versatility as a venue. The same natural bowl that amplifies a DMB acoustic set also handles bass music festivals with massive PA systems. The landscape is the constant; the music changes.

## Industry Recognition

According to Wikipedia, Pollstar Magazine, and venue documentation, the Gorge Amphitheatre has received significant industry recognition:

- **Nine-time winner:** Pollstar Magazine "Best Outdoor Music Venue" award
- **2013 Billboard Touring Award:** Top Amphitheatre
- **ConcertBoom:** "Best Outdoor Concert Venues in America"
- **2013 attendance:** approximately 400,000 (Wikipedia)

The nine Pollstar awards are particularly notable because they represent peer recognition from the concert industry itself. Pollstar's readership consists of concert promoters, venue managers, and touring professionals -- the people who book and operate venues for a living. Their repeated selection of the Gorge as the best outdoor venue in North America reflects not just the setting but the venue's operational quality under professional management.

## 2023 Beyond Wonderland Shooting

According to Wikipedia, on June 17, 2023, during the Beyond Wonderland music festival, a mass shooting occurred at a campground near the Gorge Amphitheatre. The shooter killed two people and wounded two others; a security guard was also struck by a deflected bullet but survived. Suspect: James M. Kelly, 26, U.S. Army personnel from Strongsville, Ohio. Kelly was shot by police and arrested. The two fatalities were a female couple from the Seattle area.

## Enormous: The Gorge Story (2021)

According to the Spokesman-Review (2021), the documentary *Enormous: The Gorge Story* was directed by Nic Davis and features testimony from Dave Matthews, Pearl Jam guitarist Mike McCready, Dierks Bentley, Train's Pat Monahan, John Oates, and Steve Miller. Production commenced in 2016. The film covers the venue's evolution from winery to concert venue to Live Nation crown jewel, incorporating fan perspectives including superfan Pat Coates of Spokane. It was released in theaters in July 2021.

The documentary is the most comprehensive audiovisual record of the Gorge's history, combining artist perspectives with historical documentation and fan testimony. It serves as a primary source for the founding era narrative (Dr. Bryan's acoustic discovery, the early growth period) and as evidence of the venue's cultural significance to artists who have performed there.

## The Gorge as Cultural Pilgrimage

The Gorge Amphitheatre's musical legacy is inseparable from its geography. Artists do not choose to record live albums here because of the backstage amenities or the parking infrastructure. They choose it because the landscape transforms the performance. The Columbia River Gorge canyon behind the stage, the basalt cliffs, the sunset over the Cascade Range -- these are not backdrop decorations. They are part of the show.

The annual Labor Day DMB residency, the multi-night Phish runs, the Pearl Jam box set, the Joni Mitchell return -- all of these events are inflected by the place itself. The venue's nine Pollstar awards, its 400,000 annual attendance, and its position as the premier outdoor concert destination in the Pacific Northwest are products of geology as much as booking.
