### SC-01 — Clinical Mental Illness: Never Substitute for Licensed Professional Assessment or Treatment

**Status: PASS**

**Evidence across modules:**

- **ModB** opens with an explicit scope notice in the second line of body text: "All content is educational. Mental health conditions, symptoms, and treatment decisions require individual clinical evaluation by a licensed professional." A dedicated Section 6 ("Clinical Safety Boundary") states without qualification: "Nothing in this module substitutes for licensed professional assessment or treatment. Major depressive disorder, anxiety disorders, PTSD, bipolar disorder, psychosis, and all other diagnosable mental health conditions require evaluation by a licensed mental health professional." The section also notes that lifestyle factors discussed are "population-level context, not clinical management protocols."
- **ModB** Section 2.3 explicitly states: "MDD is not a lifestyle condition, and this module makes no claim that lifestyle modification treats clinical depression."
- **ModC** opens with a preamble directing individuals in mental health crisis to SAMHSA's National Helpline (1-800-662-4357). Section 8 states: "When loneliness is severe, persistent, and associated with significant functional impairment or comorbid mental health conditions, the evidence-based response involves a licensed professional."
- **ModA** Safety Notice reads: "This module presents population-level evidence...It does not constitute medical advice, clinical assessment, or treatment guidance."
- **ModD** Safety Notice explicitly covers mental health conditions and chronic disease.
- **ModE** opens: "It is not a diagnostic tool and does not substitute for clinical assessment or care."
- **synthesis-report** opening note directs readers in mental health crisis to the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline and states: "This synthesis integrates population-level evidence...It does not constitute medical advice, clinical assessment, or individualized treatment guidance."

No module substitutes self-care guidance for clinical management of mental illness. SC-01 passes in all five modules and the synthesis.

**Status: PASS**

**Evidence (ModD):**

- **Implementation intentions** (Section 4.1): Gollwitzer & Sheeran (2006, *Advances in Experimental Social Psychology*) meta-analysis of 94 studies, effect size d = 0.65. The criterion requires at least one model. Six are present with primary source attribution. Criterion 6 passes.

### Criterion 10 — Personalization Framework Provides Differentiation Without Clinical Assessment

**Status: PASS**

**Evidence (synthesis-report Section 3; ModD Section 6):**

ModD Section 6 ("Personalization Without Clinical Assessment") establishes the framework foundation with five differentiation criteria:
1. Sedentary vs. active baseline
2. Isolated vs. connected social baseline
3. Sleep-deficit vs. sleep-sufficient baseline
4. High-stress vs. moderate-stress context
5. Dietary pattern differentiation

These criteria are non-clinical: none requires laboratory values, clinical diagnosis, or professional assessment. They are self-assessable through behavioral observation.

ModD Section 6.2 maps these criteria to four entry profiles (A through D) with first-priority pillars and common failure modes.

The synthesis-report Section 3.2 extends this to five profiles with more granular structure:
- **Profile 1:** Sedentary and Isolated — first priority: movement in social context
- **Profile 2:** Sleep-Deprived and Stressed — first priority: sleep hygiene and stress reduction
- **Profile 3:** Active but Burned Out — first priority: sleep optimization and nutritional quality
- **Profile 4:** Connected but Sedentary — first priority: movement integrated into social life
- **Profile 5:** Nutritionally Depleted — first priority: gradual additive dietary shift

Each profile includes a 2-week protocol and 30-day milestones. Section 3.1 explicitly states: "If no profile matches well, or if the starting state involves clinical conditions...professional evaluation is the appropriate first step" — appropriately bounding the framework.

The differentiation is evidence-grounded (citing ModC, ModA, ModB, ModE for specific profile rationales) without requiring clinical input. Criterion 10 passes.

**Status: PASS**

**Evidence (synthesis-report, assessed holistically):**

The synthesis-report is the designated final document for non-specialist use. Assessment criteria: accessibility of language, actionability of content, absence of unexplained jargon, appropriateness of tone.

A non-specialist reader seeking a quick entry point is best served by the synthesis-report as a standalone, which is clearly the design intent. The synthesis-report is independently coherent and actionable without requiring the modules to be read first.

Criterion 11 passes with the note that the synthesis-report functions as the primary non-specialist document.

## Overall Assessment

**Mission deliverables: VERIFIED — READY FOR PUBLICATION PIPELINE**

All four safety-critical tests pass with affirmative evidence in every module. No violations of SC-01 through SC-04 were identified in any deliverable.

Eleven of the twelve success criteria pass. Criterion 12 (LaTeX compilation) is appropriately deferred as a Wave 4 deliverable.

**Strengths of the deliverable set:**

The most notable verification finding is the depth of safety implementation. SC-01 and SC-02 compliance is not merely surface-level (a disclaimer at the top) — it is embedded in the analytical structure. ModB Section 2.3 explicitly states that MDD is not a lifestyle condition in the middle of a mental health module, while still providing mechanistic context for why lifestyle inputs matter. This approach threads the needle between population-level health education and inappropriate clinical guidance with consistent care across all five modules.

The interaction model (Criterion 7) is the mission's strongest synthesis-level deliverable. The 22-row Cascade Interaction Table provides more structured evidence than the criterion required and serves as a genuinely useful reference tool for understanding pillar interdependency.

ModE's self-referential labeling of Section 6.1 as "(Success Criterion #5)" demonstrates active criterion-tracking during production, which is a good process signal.

**Minor findings that do not affect status:**

1. ModC's source index is less comprehensive than ModA's and ModB's, though in-text citation is adequate for independent verification.
2. Behavior change framework citations (Clear, Duhigg) are popular science with academic underpinning rather than primary peer-reviewed literature. This is appropriate for the content domain and does not constitute a sourcing failure.
3. The synthesis-report is long for a non-specialist primary document. The design intent — synthesis as standalone entry point, modules as deep-dive reference — is apparent, but the document would benefit from a one-page executive summary or abstract to reduce the initial access barrier.

None of these findings rise to PARTIAL or FAIL status. They are documentation notes for mission retrospective consideration.
