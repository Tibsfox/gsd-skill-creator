## Government & Agency Sources (GOV-XX)

### GOV-01 — Oregon Building Codes Division (BCD)

- **Organization:** Oregon Department of Consumer and Business Services
- **Function:** Administers state building codes per ORS 455.148; manages Oregon-specific amendments to ICC model codes
- **URL:** https://www.oregon.gov/bcd/codes-stand/
- **Authority Level:** Primary — regulatory authority for all Oregon building codes
- **Current Adoptions Managed:**
  - 2025 OSSC (IBC 2024 base)
  - 2023 ORSC (IRC 2021 base)
  - 2023 OESC (NEC 2023)
  - 2023 OPSC (UPC 2021 base)
  - 2022 OMSC (IMC base)
  - 2022 Oregon Fire Code
  - OEESC 2025 (ASHRAE 90.1-2022 base)
- **Module Coverage:** M1, M2, M3, M4, M5
- **Verification Date:** 2026-03-08

### GOV-02 — Washington State Building Code Council (SBCC)

- **Organization:** State of Washington, established by RCW 19.27
- **Function:** Adopts and maintains the State Building Code as minimum statewide construction requirement; manages WAC 51-xx code series
- **URL:** https://sbcc.wa.gov/
- **Authority Level:** Primary — regulatory authority for all Washington building codes
- **Current Code Cycle:** 2021 editions (effective March 15, 2024)
- **Next Code Cycle:** 2024 editions delayed to adoption August 21, 2026 / effective May 3, 2027
- **Code Precedence:** IBC > IRC > IMC > IFC > WUI > UPC (earlier-named codes prevail in conflicts)
- **Module Coverage:** M1, M2, M3, M4, M5
- **Verification Date:** 2026-03-08

### GOV-03 — Portland Bureau of Development Services (BDS)

- **Organization:** City of Portland
- **Function:** Local building permit authority; administers Portland-specific amendments including Title 24 Building Regulations, Ch. 24.85 Seismic Design Standards, and Ch. 32-33 provisions
- **URL:** https://www.portland.gov/bds
- **Authority Level:** Primary — local regulatory authority (Portland)
- **Key Provisions:**
  - Ch. 24.85 mandates ASCE 41 evaluation for change of use, occupancy, or significant alterations
  - Adopted IBC 2021 Chapters 32 and 33

- **Module Coverage:** M1, M5
- **Verification Date:** 2026-03-08

## Code Development Organizations (CODE-XX)

### CODE-01 — International Code Council (ICC)

- **Organization:** International Code Council
- **Function:** Develops and publishes the International Building Code (IBC), International Residential Code (IRC), International Existing Building Code (IEBC), International Fire Code (IFC), International Mechanical Code (IMC), International Fuel Gas Code (IFGC), and International Wildland-Urban Interface Code (IWUIC)
- **Authority Level:** Primary — model code development organization
- **Editions Referenced:**
  - IBC 2024 — base for Oregon OSSC 2025
  - IBC 2021 — base for Washington SBC (WAC 51-50)
  - IRC 2021 — base for Oregon ORSC 2023 and Washington (WAC 51-51)
  - IEBC 2024 — base for Oregon existing building provisions
  - IFC 2024 — base for Oregon Fire Code
  - IMC — base for Oregon OMSC and Washington (WAC 51-52)
  - IFGC — fuel gas provisions
  - IWUIC — base for Washington WUI (WAC 51-55)
- **Key Sections Referenced:**
  - IRC R703.7.3 — drainage requirements for stucco in certain climate zones
- **Module Coverage:** M1, M2, M3, M4, M5
- **Verification Date:** 2026-03-08

### CODE-02 — National Fire Protection Association (NFPA)

- **Organization:** NFPA
- **Function:** Develops and publishes the National Electrical Code (NEC/NFPA 70), the foundational electrical installation standard in the United States
- **Authority Level:** Primary — model code development organization
- **Current Edition:** NEC 2023 (NFPA 70)
- **Key 2023 Cycle Changes:**
  - Section 210.8(A)(6) — expanded GFCI: all kitchen receptacles in dwelling units
  - Section 210.8(B)(4) — expanded GFCI: commercial buffet/serving areas
  - Section 230.67 — surge protection device (SPD) requirements for dwelling units, minimum 10kA nominal discharge
  - Section 230.85 — emergency disconnect requirements for one- and two-family dwellings
  - Section 110.21(B) — enhanced labeling requirements for outdoor equipment
  - Article 220 — service entrance load calculations
  - Article 250 — grounding and bonding
  - Article 310.16 — conductor ampacity tables
  - Article 408 — panel layout requirements
  - Article 625 — EV charging infrastructure
  - Article 690 — solar PV systems
  - Article 706 — energy storage systems
- **Module Coverage:** M2, M5
- **Verification Date:** 2026-03-08

### CODE-03 — International Association of Plumbing and Mechanical Officials (IAPMO)

- **Organization:** IAPMO
- **Function:** Develops the Uniform Plumbing Code (UPC) under ANSI-accredited consensus procedures; covers residential, commercial, and industrial plumbing in a single document
- **Authority Level:** Primary — model code development organization
- **Current Edition:** UPC 2021
- **Key 2021 Innovations:**
  - Peak Water Demand Calculator — first significant update to plumbing system sizing methodology since the 1940s, using statistical data and probability models for modern low-flow fixtures
- **Key Chapters:**
  - Chapter 6 — water supply sizing
  - Chapter 7 — drainage fixture unit methodology
  - Chapter 9 — venting requirements
- **Washington Amendments:** Chapters 12 and 14 not adopted; Chapter 5 (appliance venting/combustion air) not adopted; building sewer provisions handled separately
- **Module Coverage:** M3, M5
- **Verification Date:** 2026-03-08

### CODE-04 — American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE)

- **Organization:** ASCE
- **Function:** Develops structural engineering standards adopted by reference in building codes
- **Authority Level:** Primary — engineering standards organization
- **Standards Referenced:**
  - **ASCE 7-22** — Minimum Design Loads and Associated Criteria for Buildings and Other Structures (seismic, wind, snow, rain, flood loads)
  - **ASCE 41-17** — Seismic Evaluation and Retrofit of Existing Buildings (Tier 1/Tier 2 deficiency-based evaluation; Basic Performance Objective for Existing Buildings — BPOE)
- **Key Applications:**
  - Portland Ch. 24.85 mandates ASCE 41 evaluation
  - Cascadia Subduction Zone design: 37% probability of M7.1+ within 50 years
  - Last major event: M9.0 in 1700
- **Module Coverage:** M1, M5
- **Verification Date:** 2026-03-08

### CODE-05 — American National Standards Institute (ANSI)

- **Organization:** ANSI
- **Function:** Accredits standards development organizations and consensus procedures; UPC (IAPMO) and NEC (NFPA) both developed under ANSI-accredited processes
- **Authority Level:** Reference — accreditation body (does not develop building codes directly)
- **Module Coverage:** M2, M3, M5
- **Verification Date:** 2026-03-08

- **Function:** Accredits engineering, computing, applied science, and engineering technology programs at colleges and universities

  2. Building mechanical systems
  3. Building electrical systems

## Washington 2024 Code Cycle Delay

The 2024 Washington code cycle has been significantly delayed:

- **Original Timeline:** Adoption expected 2025
- **Revised Timeline:** Final adoption August 21, 2026; effective date May 3, 2027
- **Reason:** SBCC review process per RCW 19.27.074
- **Impact:** Washington continues operating under 2021 IBC/IRC/IMC/IFC editions until May 2027
- **Local Amendment Rule:** Local amendments may not reduce minimum performance standards; residential amendments affecting single- or multi-family buildings must be reviewed and approved by SBCC

## Portland Prescriptive Seismic Retrofit Requirements

Portland's residential seismic strengthening program provides prescriptive retrofit methods:

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Foundation anchoring | Mudsill bolting to foundation |
| Cripple wall bracing | 1/2-inch CDX five-ply plywood |
| Plywood coverage — 1-story | 40% of cripple wall area |
| Plywood coverage — 2-story | 50% of cripple wall area |
| Plywood coverage — 3-story | 80% of cripple wall area |

Seattle SDCI prescriptive approach follows the ABC method:
- **A** — Anchor to foundation
- **B** — Brace cripple walls
- **C** — Connect to first floor framing
