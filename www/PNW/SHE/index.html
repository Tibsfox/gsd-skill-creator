The 500-in-1 electronics kit of the 1980s taught a generation to solder, to read schematics, to build radios and alarm circuits from discrete components. This is the spiritual successor to that kit — updated for an era of microcontrollers, mesh networks, and machine learning at the edge. The projects start with a blinking LED and end with a complete, self-hosted home automation system that the student built, understands, and controls.

The Six Safety Rules

#RuleStandard
1De-energize before wiring. Verify with multimeter. Lock out breaker.NEC/NFPA 70
2Use properly rated components. Wire gauge matches circuit breaker.NEC/NFPA 70
3Flyback diodes on every relay coil, solenoid, and motor.Circuit theory
4Lithium batteries require TP4056 + DW01 protection minimum.Battery safety
5RF transmitters comply with FCC Part 15. Use pre-certified modules.FCC Part 15
6When in doubt, hire a licensed electrician. NEC compliance is not optional.NEC/NFPA 70
