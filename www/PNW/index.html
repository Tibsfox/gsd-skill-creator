Threads Across the Bioregion

Seven ecological threads connect the four projects. Each thread traces a single relationship system across the entire research series, linking specific documents where that thread appears.

The Salmon Thread

Pacific salmon carry marine-derived nitrogen (N-15) from the ocean into riparian forests. Bears, eagles, and floods distribute carcass nutrients. Trees absorb them through mycorrhizal networks. The forest feeds the stream that feeds the salmon.

COL Aquatic — 51 fish, N-15 pathway
CAS Aquatic — salmonid profiles, nutrient cycle
ECO Marine Fauna — 39 species, orca-salmon
ECO Networks — salmon-nitrogen pathway

The Mycorrhizal Web

Underground fungal networks connect trees across entire forest stands. Mother trees provision seedlings through common mycorrhizal networks (CMN). Old-growth forests host the most complex networks. Even garden soil participates.

COL Fungi — EMF/AMF/CMN, mother trees
CAS Fungi — ectomycorrhizal, matsutake
ECO Fungi — 23 species, quiet light
GDN Soil — mycorrhizal management
GDN Synthesis — soil-mycorrhizal cascade

The Vertical Gradient

Every thousand feet of elevation rewrites the rules. Temperature, moisture, snow depth, growing season — all shift, and every shift opens a niche. The same gradient that stratifies the Cascades determines what grows in a garden.

CAS Zones — 5 elevation zones defined
ECO Attributes — 8 elevation bands
ECO Coordinates — elevation to Minecraft Y
GDN Climate — USDA zones, microclimates

Old-Growth Architecture

Ancient forests are not just old trees. They are multi-layered structures with nurse logs, snag habitat, canopy gaps, and fungal communities that take centuries to develop. Logging removes not just timber but irreplaceable biological architecture.

COL Flora — canopy structure, nurse logs
CAS Flora — montane old-growth, whitebark
ECO Flora — 41 species by elevation
CAS Threats — old-growth loss vector

Climate Futures

The PNW is warming. Snowpack is declining. Fire seasons are lengthening. Heat domes are emerging. Each project documents what this means for its domain — from alpine species loss to garden adaptation strategies.

CAS Threats — 5 vectors, glacial loss
ECO Networks — fire-succession pathway
GDN Pest/Disease — heat dome protocol
GDN Climate — 2023 zone shift, GDD
GDN Synthesis — climate resilience cascade
