## 2. Peer-Reviewed Research

Peer-reviewed publications provide the scientific foundation for all physics mechanisms, biological implementations, and quantitative claims in this atlas. Sources range from foundational studies (Walker 1977, Au & Simmons 2007) to cutting-edge discoveries (Knauer et al. 2025 bioRxiv).

---

### P1 — Putman, N.F. et al. (2020)

```
Citation:      Putman, N.F., Scanlan, M.M., Billman, E.J., et al. (2020).
               An inherited magnetic map guides ocean navigation in juvenile
               Pacific salmon. Current Biology, 24(4), 446-450.
               [Also published in: Journal of Comparative Physiology A]
DOI/URL:       https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8918461/

Topics:
  - Pacific pink salmon two-coordinate magnetic map (F + I)
  - Inherited (not learned) magnetic navigation in juvenile fish
  - Experimental evidence: magnetic displacement → appropriate orientation
  - Magnetic map resolution: ~10-100 km (sufficient for open-ocean navigation)
  - Field intensity gradient: ~2-5 nT/km; inclination gradient: ~0.01-0.05 deg/km
  - Elliptical migratory route: 3,000-5,000 km in North Pacific

Pages citing:  10, pnw-02, pnw-06
Reliability tier: 1 — Gold (peer-reviewed journal, experimental data)
```

### P4 — Cerveny, J. et al. (2011)

```
Citation:      Cerveny, J., Begall, S., Koubek, P., Novakova, P.,
               and Burda, H. (2011). Directional preference may enhance
               hunting accuracy in foraging foxes. Biology Letters,
               7(3), 355-357.

Topics:
  - Fox mousing pounce directional preference
  - 592 pounces from 84 individual foxes
  - NE direction: 74% success rate (vs 18% other directions)
  - Statistical significance: p < 0.001 (Rayleigh test)
  - Mean vector direction: ~20 deg magnetic
  - Rules out wind, sun, visual landmarks, prey direction
  - Only consistent reference frame: geomagnetic field

Pages citing:  10, 11, pnw-05
Reliability tier: 1 — Gold (peer-reviewed Biology Letters,
                  Royal Society; large sample size, robust statistics)

### P10 — Walker et al. (1977)

```
Citation:      Walker, M.M., et al. (1977). Magnetite in the snouts
               of rainbow trout. [Foundational study identifying
               biogenic magnetite in vertebrate fish]

Topics:

  - Crystal characteristics: ~50 nm, single-domain, cubo-octahedral
  - Organization: chains of 10-20 crystals

  - Neural response abolished by local anesthetic and by magnetic pulse
  - Foundational evidence for vertebrate magnetoreception
