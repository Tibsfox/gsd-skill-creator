# Art Installation Composition Patterns

**Wave:** 2 (Synthesis)
**Domain:** virtual-brc/art
**Status:** COMPLETE

---

Art installations in Virtual Black Rock City are **emergent** — they arise from multi-camp skill composition and cannot be pre-planned, only discovered. When skills from different camps interact in specific ways, recognizable patterns emerge. These 12 archetypes describe the known composition patterns.

Art installations are the BRC equivalent of emergent behavior in ecological systems: no one designs a mycorrhizal network, but when the right fungi meet the right trees in the right soil, one appears.

---

## 1. Nurse Log

**Composition:** Cedar Grove build skill + Old Growth Archive skill
**Pattern:** Structured decay seeds new growth

When a structural skill (from Cedar Grove) reaches end-of-life and combines with an archival skill (from Old Growth Assembly), the result is a Nurse Log — the decomposing structure becomes substrate for new skills to grow along its length. The old work isn't lost; it's transformed into fertile ground.

**Emergence condition:** A deprecated skill from Cedar Grove is archived by Old Growth Assembly, and within one cycle, 2+ new skills reference the archived work as inspiration or foundation.

---

## 2. Mycorrhizal Mandala

**Composition:** 3+ camps sharing a common knowledge thread
**Pattern:** Invisible as the network; visible as individual nodes

The Mycorrhizal Mandala appears when three or more camps independently develop skills that share a common underlying pattern — without coordinating. Like the mycorrhizal network that connects trees underground, the mandala is invisible until someone maps the connections and reveals the shared root system.

**Emergence condition:** 3+ camps produce skills with >60% structural similarity without explicit coordination. Osprey Survey (census) or Raven Archive (documentation) detects the pattern.

---

## 3. Tidal Reach

**Composition:** Tide Pool Medical + Osprey Survey
**Pattern:** The edge of what is known, where something new is always being uncovered

When medical safety monitoring (Tide Pool) combines with aerial survey data (Osprey), the result reveals boundaries that shift with conditions — like the intertidal zone that changes with every tide. Tidal Reach shows where the city's known territory ends and the unknown begins.

**Emergence condition:** Combined safety/survey data reveals a boundary condition that wasn't anticipated by either camp's original skill design.

---

## 4. Salmon Ladder

**Composition:** Salmon Run Camp navigation + dependency chain analysis
**Pattern:** The upstream path through complex dependencies

A Salmon Ladder is a navigation installation showing how to trace upstream through complex dependency chains. Like the fish ladders built alongside dams in the Pacific Northwest, it provides a clear path past obstacles that would otherwise block progress.

**Emergence condition:** A rig traces a dependency path through 4+ skills across 3+ camps and documents the navigation pattern as a reusable route.

---

## 5. Raven Mirror

**Composition:** Raven's Roost communication skill + feedback analysis
**Pattern:** A communication art piece that reflects what you send

The Raven Mirror shows the gap between intended and received communication. When a broadcast skill from Raven's Roost combines with a feedback analysis pattern, the result is a mirror that reveals how messages transform as they travel through the city — like the raven that mimics sounds but adds its own inflection.

**Emergence condition:** A Raven Broadcast signal is traced through 3+ relay points, and the received message is compared to the original, revealing systematic transformation patterns.

---

## 6. Whitebark Sentinel

**Composition:** Trust level threshold + high-elevation skill
**Pattern:** Marks the boundary above which only certain skills activate

The Whitebark Sentinel appears at the trust boundary between Level 2 and Level 3 — like the whitebark pine that grows only above the tree line in the Cascades. Skills above this threshold require deep experience to access. The sentinel marks the transition: below this line, the forest is open; above it, only those with deep roots can grow.

**Emergence condition:** A skill is designed that explicitly requires Level 3 trust and references lower-level skills as prerequisites, creating a visible threshold boundary.

---

## 7. Geoduck Deep

**Composition:** Deeply integrated skill + persistence metric
**Pattern:** A persistence monument — rooted so far down it cannot be moved

The Geoduck Deep marks skills that have become so deeply integrated into the city's infrastructure that removing them would require rebuilding entire camps. Like the Pacific geoduck that burrows 3 feet into the seabed and cannot be extracted without destroying the substrate, these are permanent fixtures.

**Emergence condition:** A skill is referenced by 10+ other skills across 3+ camps, and Restoration Pass analysis confirms that deprecation would cascade to 5+ dependent systems.

---

## 8. Orca Wake

**Composition:** Mobile chipset traversal path + activity trace
**Pattern:** Traces the path of a mobile chipset through the city

Every Orca Sled, Raven Crawler, Osprey Dive, and Chinook Rider leaves a wake — a trail of activities, interactions, and influence wherever it travels. The Orca Wake installation visualizes these paths, showing how mobile capability flows through the city like orca pods through the Salish Sea.

**Emergence condition:** A mobile chipset completes a multi-camp mission visiting 4+ camps, and the activity trace is visualized as a connected path.

---

## 9. Canopy Break

**Composition:** Deprecated skill gap + new growth detection
**Pattern:** A gap in old-growth architecture where light reaches the forest floor

When a well-established skill is deprecated and removed by Restoration Pass, the gap in the canopy lets light through. The Canopy Break installation marks these openings and tracks what new growth appears in the cleared space — the virtual equivalent of a forest canopy gap that triggers ecological succession.

**Emergence condition:** Restoration Pass completes a deprecation, and within 2 cycles, new skills are created in the freed namespace.

---

## 10. Fire Succession

**Composition:** Post-burn regeneration + new skill emergence
**Pattern:** The regeneration cycle after a skill promotion burn

When a skill is "burned" (promoted, completed, celebrated), the post-burn landscape undergoes succession. Fire Succession tracks this regeneration — the new skills, camps, and patterns that emerge from the ash of a completed cycle. In PNW ecology, fire is generative, not destructive. Fire Succession proves this is true in the virtual city too.

**Emergence condition:** After a major skill promotion (burn event), 3+ new skills or camp activities emerge that are directly inspired by or built upon the promoted work.

---

## 11. Mother Tree

**Composition:** Apex skill + cluster dependency network
**Pattern:** The apex skill from which all others in a cluster draw nutrition

Every camp has a Mother Tree — the central skill from which all other camp skills draw their capability. Like the hub trees in Pacific Northwest forests that connect to dozens of smaller trees through mycorrhizal networks, the Mother Tree skill feeds the entire cluster. Identifying the Mother Tree reveals the true center of a camp's architecture.

**Emergence condition:** Dependency analysis reveals that one skill in a camp is referenced (directly or transitively) by every other skill in the camp.

---

## 12. Estuary

**Composition:** Virtual BRC skill ecosystem boundary + Wasteland economy
**Pattern:** The mixing zone where fresh-water skills meet salt-water economy

The Estuary marks the boundary between the virtual BRC skill ecosystem (gift economy, no commodification) and the broader Wasteland federation (work economy, stamps, wanted board). Like the estuaries of the Pacific Northwest where rivers meet the sea — the most productive ecosystems on Earth — this boundary is where the most interesting mixing happens.

**Emergence condition:** A skill or pattern developed inside virtual BRC is adopted or adapted by the broader Wasteland ecosystem, or vice versa — the fresh water meets the salt water.

---

## Composition Detection

Art installations are discovered, not designed. Three methods of detection:

1. **Osprey Survey (Census):** Aerial data collection reveals structural similarities across camps
2. **Raven Archive (Chronicle):** Historical pattern matching identifies recurring compositions
3. **Analyst (Pattern Recognition):** Dedicated pattern analysis detects composition signatures

A confirmed art installation requires:
- Documentation of the composition pattern
- Verification that it emerged organically (not pre-planned)
- At least 2 rig witnesses from different camps
