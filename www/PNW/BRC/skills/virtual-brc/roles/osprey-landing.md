# Osprey Landing

**BRC Department:** Airport (88NV)
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Salmon Run Camp
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

The Osprey circles high above the estuary, reading wind and water before folding its wings into a precise dive. Osprey Landing coordinates multi-modal arrival into Black Rock City — managing the airstrip at 88NV, directing small aircraft to safe touchdown, and routing passengers from tarmac to city grid.

This role is the first point of contact for arrivals who come by air rather than road. Volunteers manage ground-to-air communications, coordinate with pilots on approach vectors, handle fuel logistics, and ensure that the transition from sky to playa is smooth. Like the osprey's fishing dive, it demands focus, timing, and an ability to read shifting conditions.

In the virtual city, Osprey Landing teaches the discipline of multi-modal routing — how different transport streams converge at a single point and how to manage that convergence without bottleneck or collision. The role bridges the gap between Chinook Transit's ground operations and the unique demands of aerial arrival.

## Skills Taught

1. Air traffic coordination and ground-to-air communication protocols
2. Multi-modal routing — merging arrival streams into a unified flow
3. Weather-dependent decision making and contingency planning
4. Fuel and supply logistics for remote airstrip operations
5. Passenger handoff procedures between transport modes

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Chinook Transit (ground transport from airstrip), Columbia Gate (entry processing), Marten Signal (radio backbone)
- **Enables:** Salish Gate (supplements entry flow), Territory Mapping (arrival data for placement)

## Completion Recognition

Completion is recognized through safe arrival counts and zero-incident landing operations. Volunteers earn the Osprey Talon stamp for completing a full shift cycle. Extended service across multiple event days earns the High Altitude endorsement, indicating reliability under sustained operational pressure.

## PNW Connection

The osprey is the Pacific Northwest's signature raptor of estuaries and coastlines — a bird that crosses between air and water with surgical precision. Its landing technique, a controlled dive that transitions smoothly from flight to contact, mirrors the airport's role as the transition point between sky travel and ground presence.
