# Raven's Welcome

**BRC Department:** Greeters
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Raven's Roost
**Trust Level Required:** 1
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

The common raven is the first bird you encounter in any Pacific Northwest ecosystem — coastal headlands, alpine ridges, urban parking lots, deep forest. Ravens are curious, social, and unafraid of strangers. Raven's Welcome is the Greeters department: the first human contact every participant has upon entering Black Rock City.

After passing through Gate, every vehicle stops at the Greeters station. Volunteers welcome first-timers and veterans alike, offer hugs (consensually), ring a bell for virgins, explain the basic principles, and mark the transition from the default world to the playa. For first-timers, this moment is transformative — the first tangible proof that radical inclusion is not just a slogan. For returning participants, it is a homecoming.

In the virtual city, Raven's Welcome teaches the practice of radical inclusion, first-contact communication, emotional attunement, and the art of making a stranger feel like they belong. The role is deceptively simple — stand at a gate and say hello — but doing it well requires genuine warmth sustained across 12-hour shifts in extreme heat, greeting thousands of people with the same authentic welcome.

## Skills Taught

1. First-contact communication and radical inclusion practice
2. Emotional attunement and consent-based physical greeting
3. Cultural onboarding and principle transmission
4. Sustained authentic engagement under physically demanding conditions
5. Transition facilitation — marking the boundary between worlds

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Columbia Gate (physical entry processing), Hemlock Reference (orientation follow-up)
- **Enables:** All participants (first experience of the culture), Hemlock Reference (warm handoff for questions), Watershed Watch (community tone-setting)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the First Call stamp after completing a full greeting shift. The Dawn Chorus endorsement recognizes service during the highest-volume arrival windows when the greeting line stretches for miles. Raven's Welcome is often the first volunteer role participants take — it serves as an entry point into the broader volunteer ecosystem.

## PNW Connection

Common ravens are the Pacific Northwest's universal greeter. They are the first wildlife most visitors encounter, perched on trailhead signs, calling from parking lot light poles, circling above ferry terminals. They approach without fear, vocalize constantly, and seem genuinely interested in every newcomer. Greeters channel the same energy: omnipresent, vocal, curious, and welcoming to all who arrive.
