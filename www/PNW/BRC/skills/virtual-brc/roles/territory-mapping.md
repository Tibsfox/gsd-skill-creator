# Territory Mapping

**BRC Department:** Surveying / Mapping
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Salmon Run Camp
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

Before any species can establish territory, the landscape must be surveyed — elevation measured, water sources mapped, boundaries identified. Territory Mapping is the Surveying and Mapping department: the team that lays out Black Rock City's radial grid, assigns camp placements, and ensures that the city's physical plan translates from paper to playa.

Black Rock City is one of the largest planned cities built each year — a radial grid of named streets and numbered avenues covering seven square miles. Territory Mapping volunteers survey the playa surface, drive stakes to mark street corners, measure and flag camp boundaries, and resolve the inevitable conflicts when physical reality does not match the city plan. The work happens in the weeks before the event, in brutal heat, with survey equipment that must maintain precision across miles of featureless desert.

In the virtual city, Territory Mapping teaches spatial planning, survey methodology, conflict resolution in resource allocation, and the discipline of translating abstract plans into physical reality. The role develops an understanding that city planning is not just geometry — it is politics, logistics, and community negotiation compressed into stake-and-string measurements.

## Skills Taught

1. Survey methodology and spatial measurement in field conditions
2. Radial grid layout and urban planning at temporary-city scale
3. Camp placement logistics and resource allocation negotiation
4. Conflict resolution when plan meets terrain
5. Precision measurement and quality assurance across large survey areas

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Cascade Brigade (survey infrastructure support), Chinook Transit (equipment transport)
- **Enables:** All theme camps (placement), Cedar Canopy (camp setup guidance), Columbia Gate (entry routing), Hemlock Reference (city layout data)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Survey Stake stamp after completing a full mapping rotation. The Meridian endorsement recognizes service across both planning and field phases — helping design the city layout and then physically surveying it onto the playa. Territory Mapping volunteers who demonstrate precision and diplomatic skill in camp placement earn trust advancement through their reliability as spatial planners.

## PNW Connection

The Pacific Northwest was one of the last regions of North America to be surveyed — the terrain was too steep, too forested, and too wet for conventional methods. When surveyors finally mapped it, they discovered that the landscape demanded adaptation: straight section lines bent around mountains, township grids warped along river valleys. Territory Mapping on the playa faces the same lesson — the plan must respect the ground it is drawn on.
