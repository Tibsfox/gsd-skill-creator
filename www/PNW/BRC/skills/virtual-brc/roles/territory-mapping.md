# Territory Mapping

**BRC Department:** Surveying / Mapping
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Salmon Run Camp
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

Territory Mapping handles the spatial layout of Virtual Black Rock City. Named for the bioregion cartography tradition of the Pacific Northwest — where watersheds, elevation zones, and ecological boundaries define the map rather than political borders — this role determines where camps are placed and how the city's geography flows.

In Black Rock City, surveyors lay out the city grid each year: the streets, the esplanade, the open playa. Every camp receives a placement based on size, theme, and infrastructure needs. Territory Mapping translates this into the virtual city — assigning camps to elevation zones, ensuring adequate spacing, and maintaining the city's spatial coherence.

Level 2 trust is required because placement decisions affect every camp in the city. A misplaced camp can block sightlines, create dependency conflicts, or strain infrastructure. The mapper must see the whole terrain.

## Skills Taught

1. Spatial planning — organizing skill clusters for coherent city layout
2. Dependency routing — ensuring camp placements respect infrastructure connections
3. Bioregion mapping — translating ecological elevation zones into virtual city geography

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Cascade Brigade (infrastructure grid must exist before placement), Osprey Survey (census data for sizing)
- **Enables:** All camps (receive their placement), Raven Broadcast (city map for navigation)

## Completion Recognition

Commonly receives **canopy-reach** stamps (creative spatial solutions) and **root-depth** stamps (reliable, well-tested city layouts).

## PNW Connection

Pacific Northwest bioregion mapping draws boundaries by watershed and elevation rather than political jurisdiction. The Salish Sea bioregion, the Columbia Basin, the Cascade Crest — these are ecological territories that reflect how the land actually works. Territory Mapping draws the virtual city the same way: by how things connect, not by arbitrary lines.
