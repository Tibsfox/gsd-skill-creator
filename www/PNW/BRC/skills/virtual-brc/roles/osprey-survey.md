# Osprey Survey

**BRC Department:** Census
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Osprey Watch
**Trust Level Required:** 1
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

The osprey hunts by hovering above the water, scanning the surface for movement patterns invisible from ground level. Osprey Survey conducts the Black Rock City Census — collecting demographic data, tracking population patterns, and building the statistical portrait of who the community is and how it changes over time.

Volunteers design survey instruments, collect data through interviews and observation, tabulate results, and produce reports that inform the organization's understanding of its own population. The census is one of the few data-driven operations at Burning Man, and it provides the empirical foundation for decisions about city size, infrastructure scaling, and community demographics.

In the virtual city, Osprey Survey teaches population tracking, survey methodology, pattern analysis, and the ethics of data collection in a community that values anonymity. The role sits at the intersection of social science and operational planning — counting heads not for control but for understanding.

## Skills Taught

1. Survey design and data collection methodology
2. Population tracking and demographic analysis
3. Statistical tabulation and pattern recognition
4. Ethical data handling in privacy-conscious communities
5. Report generation and data visualization

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Salish Gate (entry data), Hemlock Reference (distribution point for surveys)
- **Enables:** Territory Mapping (population density data), Cascade Brigade (infrastructure scaling decisions)

## Completion Recognition

Surveyors earn the Osprey Eye stamp after completing a full data collection cycle. The Thermal Column endorsement recognizes analysts who contribute to final census reports or develop new survey methodologies. Data quality metrics — completion rates, response validity — serve as the primary quality signal.

## PNW Connection

The osprey's hunting technique is aerial survey in its purest form — hovering at altitude, scanning for patterns, then diving with precision to capture a single data point. Pacific Northwest ospreys patrol thousands of acres of waterway this way, building a mental map of fish distribution that guides their movements. The census does the same for the human population of the city.
