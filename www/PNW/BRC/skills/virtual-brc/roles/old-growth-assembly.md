# Old Growth Assembly

**BRC Department:** Center Camp
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Old Growth Assembly
**Trust Level Required:** 1
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

An old-growth forest is not just large trees — it is the full vertical structure from canopy to understory to forest floor, each layer hosting different species, different light conditions, different conversations. Old Growth Assembly operates Center Camp — the community gathering space at the geographic and social heart of Black Rock City.

Center Camp provides shade, a stage for performances, a cafe, and most importantly, a place to simply be. Volunteers manage the space: coordinating performance schedules, maintaining the physical structure, running the cafe operations, and ensuring that the gathering place remains welcoming to all. The work ranges from barista shifts to stage management to sweeping the floor.

In the virtual city, Old Growth Assembly teaches community space stewardship — the art of maintaining a place where diverse activities coexist without crowding each other out. How do you keep a performance stage and a quiet conversation area in the same structure? How do you serve coffee to thousands while keeping the space clean? The answers require the same layered thinking that sustains an old-growth canopy.

## Skills Taught

1. Community space stewardship and public gathering management
2. Performance scheduling and stage coordination
3. Food service operations (cafe) under field conditions
4. Multi-use space management — balancing competing activities
5. Radical inclusion in practice — welcoming all into a shared space

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Cascade Brigade (structural build and maintenance), Salmon Run Pool (volunteer staffing)
- **Enables:** Mycorrhizal Hub (educational programming), Cascade Ceremonies (event staging), Raven Archive (performance documentation)

## Completion Recognition

Assembly volunteers earn the Canopy stamp after completing a full shift rotation in any Center Camp function. The Understory endorsement recognizes service across multiple functions (cafe, stage, maintenance) within a single event. Long-term service earns the Standing Dead recognition — honoring those who have held the space across many seasons.

## PNW Connection

Pacific Northwest old-growth forests are the most structurally complex ecosystems on Earth. A single stand contains trees over 500 years old, dozens of canopy layers, and more biomass per acre than tropical rainforest. Center Camp aspires to the same layered complexity — a single structure hosting a full ecosystem of community activity.
