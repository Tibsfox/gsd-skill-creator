# Chinook Rider

**BRC Department:** Burner Express Bus
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Salmon Run Camp
**Trust Level Required:** 1
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

The chinook salmon does not wander upstream alone — it moves in runs, hundreds of fish surging through the same corridor at the same time. Chinook Rider operates the Burner Express Bus system: mass transit that moves thousands of participants from cities like Reno and San Francisco directly to Black Rock City, bypassing the single-vehicle bottleneck of the gate road.

Volunteers coordinate bus loading, manage luggage logistics, handle passenger manifests, and ensure that the journey from pickup point to playa is smooth. The role spans multiple locations — urban departure points, highway rest stops, and the arrival zone on the playa — and requires the ability to manage large groups of excited people in confined spaces.

In the virtual city, Chinook Rider teaches high-volume movement orchestration. How do you move five hundred people and their gear from point A to point B on a schedule, with comfort, and without losing anyone? The answer involves manifest management, communication chains, and the kind of logistical thinking that scales from a single bus to a fleet.

## Skills Taught

1. Mass transit orchestration and fleet coordination
2. Passenger manifest management and headcount accountability
3. Multi-stop logistics with time-window constraints
4. Communication across distributed teams at multiple locations
5. Crowd management in confined spaces (buses, loading zones)

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Marten Signal (communications between buses and base), Columbia Gate (arrival coordination)
- **Enables:** Salish Gate (pre-processed passenger lists), Osprey Survey (arrival data)

## Completion Recognition

Riders earn the Chinook Run stamp after completing a full round-trip shift cycle (outbound and return). The Spawning Ground endorsement recognizes service across multiple departure cities or across the full event (arrival and exodus runs). On-time performance and zero-loss passenger counts are tracked as quality metrics.

## PNW Connection

The chinook salmon is the largest Pacific salmon species, and its spawning runs are the Pacific Northwest's most dramatic mass-movement events. Hundreds of thousands of fish navigate the same rivers on the same schedule, driven by instinct and timing. The Burner Express buses mirror this — a coordinated run of vehicles carrying the community upstream to the city.
