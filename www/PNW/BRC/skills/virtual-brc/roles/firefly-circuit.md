# Firefly Circuit

**BRC Department:** Lamplighters
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Fireline Cedar
**Trust Level Required:** 1
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

On summer nights in the Pacific Northwest, fireflies trace circuits through the understory — each flash a signal, each pattern a message, the collective display turning the forest into a living constellation. Firefly Circuit is the Lamplighters: the volunteer team that lights and maintains the lamp posts lining the streets of Black Rock City, and that leads the ceremonial lamp-lighting procession that marks each evening's transition from day to night.

Every evening at dusk, Lamplighters walk the city in a solemn procession, carrying flame from lamp to lamp, igniting each one by hand. The ritual is one of the oldest traditions at Burning Man — a deliberate slowing of pace, a communal acknowledgment that darkness is arriving and light must be tended. Beyond the ceremony, volunteers maintain the lamp infrastructure: filling fuel reservoirs, replacing wicks, repairing posts damaged by wind or vehicles, and ensuring the city's streets remain navigable after dark.

In the virtual city, Firefly Circuit teaches infrastructure maintenance through ritual, the power of ceremony to create community cohesion, and the practical skills of fire safety in a fuel-handling context. The role bridges the utilitarian (the city needs light) with the sacred (the lighting is a ceremony), teaching that maintenance can be meaningful.

## Skills Taught

1. Open-flame safety and fuel handling procedures
2. Infrastructure maintenance on a recurring schedule
3. Ceremonial coordination and processional logistics
4. Community building through shared ritual practice
5. Night operations and low-light navigation

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Cascade Brigade (lamp post installation), Chinook Transit (fuel supply)
- **Enables:** All participants (nighttime navigation), Cascade Ceremonies (ceremonial tradition), Watershed Watch (illuminated patrol routes)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Bioluminescence stamp after completing a full evening lamp-lighting procession and maintenance shift. The Constellation endorsement recognizes service across multiple nights within a single event. Lamplighters who have served across many years earn the Old Circuit recognition — honoring those whose steady flame has marked the transition from day to night season after season.

## PNW Connection

Bioluminescent organisms are woven throughout the Pacific Northwest — from fireflies in the eastern valleys to phosphorescent plankton in Puget Sound. Each produces light through chemistry and patience, not combustion. The Lamplighters carry the same ethos: light as a gift given through labor, tended nightly, a circuit of care that makes the dark navigable for everyone.
