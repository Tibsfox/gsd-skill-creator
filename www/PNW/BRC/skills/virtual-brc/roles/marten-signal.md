# Marten Signal

**BRC Department:** Telecommunications
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Osprey Watch
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

The Pacific marten moves through the forest canopy with silent speed, carrying information between territories that ground-dwelling species cannot reach. Marten Signal is the Telecommunications team: the crew that builds and maintains the radio network, data infrastructure, and communications backbone that allows every department in Black Rock City to coordinate in real time.

When a ranger calls dispatch, when a medical team requests transport, when Gate needs to halt inbound traffic during a whiteout — all of it flows through the communications infrastructure that Marten Signal builds and maintains. Volunteers install repeater towers, manage radio frequency allocation, troubleshoot interference, maintain backup systems, and operate the central dispatch relay that routes critical traffic. In a city with no cell service and no landlines, the radio network is the nervous system.

In the virtual city, Marten Signal teaches network infrastructure design, radio communications protocols, frequency management, and the discipline of maintaining a system where failure means someone cannot call for help. The role develops an understanding of communications as critical infrastructure — invisible when it works, catastrophic when it does not.

## Skills Taught

1. Radio network design, installation, and maintenance
2. Frequency allocation and interference management
3. Dispatch relay operations and priority traffic routing
4. Backup system design and failover procedures
5. Field troubleshooting of communications equipment under adverse conditions

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Cascade Brigade (tower installation and power infrastructure)
- **Enables:** All roles (communications), Watershed Command (dispatch backbone), Tide Pool Medical (emergency communications), Columbia Gate (gate-to-dispatch link)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Canopy Route stamp after completing a full shift rotation in any telecommunications function. The Silent Runner endorsement recognizes advanced skills in network design and crisis communications management. Multi-year service in Marten Signal contributes to trust level advancement, as communications reliability directly affects safety outcomes.

## PNW Connection

The Pacific marten is a mustelid that lives almost entirely in the forest canopy, traveling routes invisible from the ground. It connects territories across miles of old-growth forest, moving information (scent marks, territorial signals) through a network that other species cannot access. The telecommunications team operates the same hidden network — canopy-level infrastructure that connects every ground operation in the city.
