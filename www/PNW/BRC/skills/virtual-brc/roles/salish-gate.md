# Salish Gate

**BRC Department:** Box Office
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Tide Pool Collective
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

The Salish Sea is the inland waterway that connects Puget Sound, the Strait of Juan de Fuca, and the Strait of Georgia — a single entry system serving an entire bioregion. Salish Gate operates the Box Office: the point where tickets become wristbands, where identity is verified, and where a person's right to enter the city is confirmed.

Volunteers at Salish Gate process Will Call tickets, handle credential verification, manage special access passes, and resolve discrepancies — lost tickets, name mismatches, transfer issues. The work is high-volume during gate rush and demands accuracy under pressure. A mistake here means someone either enters without authorization or is wrongfully denied entry.

In the virtual city, this role teaches identity verification, access control, and the administrative precision required when a single error can cascade into a significant problem. It also teaches the human side of gatekeeping — how to deliver a "no" with compassion and a "yes" with efficiency.

## Skills Taught

1. Identity verification and credential processing
2. Access control systems and authorization workflows
3. High-volume transaction processing under time pressure
4. Error handling and discrepancy resolution
5. Customer-facing communication in high-stress environments

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Columbia Gate (physical entry coordination), Marten Signal (communications)
- **Enables:** All participants (entry authorization), Osprey Survey (population data)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Salish Tide stamp after completing a full shift cycle at Box Office. The Strait Passage endorsement recognizes service during peak gate-rush periods where transaction volume exceeds normal capacity. Error-free shift records contribute to trust level advancement.

## PNW Connection

The Salish Sea functions as the Pacific Northwest's natural gatehouse — all maritime traffic entering the inland waterways passes through its straits. The name honors both the geographic chokepoint and the Salish peoples who have navigated these waters for millennia, managing access to the region's richest marine resources.
