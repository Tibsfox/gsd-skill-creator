# Tide Pool Medical

**BRC Department:** Emergency Services
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Tide Pool Collective
**Trust Level Required:** 3
**Safety Classification:** MANDATORY-PASS

## What This Role Does

Tide Pool Medical is the emergency services skill of Virtual Black Rock City. Named for the intertidal tide pools of the Pacific Northwest — the resilient micro-ecosystems that survive the most extreme coastal conditions — this role provides the professional medical, fire, and crisis response capability that the city depends on for safety.

This is the single most safety-critical role in the entire city. SC-01 enforces it: attempting to skip Emergency Services gate returns BLOCK with mandatory escalation. There is no workaround, no override, no exception. When safety is at stake, Tide Pool Medical has absolute authority.

The role requires Level 3 (Old Growth) trust because emergency response demands the deepest understanding of city systems, the calm to function under pressure, and the authority to halt any operation that threatens safety. The tide pool does not yield to the wave — it holds.

## Skills Taught

1. Crisis response — rapid assessment and intervention under pressure
2. Safety gate enforcement — maintaining MANDATORY-PASS discipline without exception
3. Cross-civic coordination — working with all civic agents during emergencies

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Marten Signal (communications for dispatch), Watershed Watch (safety monitoring feeds)
- **Enables:** All burn events (medical clearance required), all camp activities with physical risk, Columbia Gate override (medical emergencies only)

## Completion Recognition

Commonly receives **root-depth** stamps (the most reliable role in the city) and **salmon-mark** stamps (persistent service against the hardest challenges). Tide Pool Medical volunteers rarely seek stamps — the work is its own recognition.

## PNW Connection

Pacific Northwest tide pools survive twice-daily exposure to air, sun, and predators, followed by the full force of ocean waves. The organisms that live there — sea anemones, purple sea urchins, ochre sea stars — are among the most resilient on the coast. They do not move. They do not yield. They hold against everything the ocean throws at them. Tide Pool Medical holds the same way.
