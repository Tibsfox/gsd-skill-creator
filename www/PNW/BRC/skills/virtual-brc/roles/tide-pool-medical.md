# Tide Pool Medical

**BRC Department:** Emergency Services
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Tide Pool Collective
**Trust Level Required:** 3
**Safety Classification:** MANDATORY-PASS

## What This Role Does

A tide pool is a closed system where every organism is visible, every injury is exposed, and the margin between life and death is measured in inches of water and degrees of temperature. Tide Pool Medical is Emergency Services: the professional medical, fire, and crisis response team that provides acute care to 80,000 people in one of the most hostile environments on the continent.

The Black Rock Desert delivers heat stroke, dehydration, dust pneumonia, burns, fractures, lacerations, substance-related emergencies, and psychological crises in volumes that would overwhelm many urban emergency departments. Tide Pool Medical operates field hospitals, dispatches ambulances, staffs first aid stations, and provides the fire suppression capability that supports every burn event. Volunteers include licensed physicians, nurses, paramedics, EMTs, firefighters, and crisis counselors — all working in conditions that would be considered unacceptable in any permanent medical facility.

In the virtual city, Tide Pool Medical teaches emergency medicine in austere environments, mass casualty triage, crisis intervention, and the discipline of maintaining clinical standards when infrastructure fails. This is a MANDATORY-PASS safety role — the most critical in the entire system. A failure in medical response does not result in inconvenience; it results in death.

## Skills Taught

1. Austere environment emergency medicine and field hospital operations
2. Mass casualty triage and resource allocation under constraint
3. Crisis intervention and psychological first aid
4. Fire suppression and burn injury management
5. Clinical standards maintenance in degraded infrastructure conditions

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Marten Signal (emergency communications), Chinook Transit (ambulance access and transport), Watershed Command (dispatch coordination)
- **Enables:** All roles (medical safety net), Fireline Cedar (burn event medical standby), Cascade Ceremonies (event medical coverage), Columbia Gate (gate medical screening)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Tide Pool stamp after completing a full medical shift rotation with verified credential documentation. The Intertidal endorsement recognizes service across multiple medical disciplines — emergency medicine, crisis counseling, and fire suppression — within a single event. Because this is a MANDATORY-PASS role, all clinical decisions are documented, reviewed, and logged in the safety registry. Trust level 3 reflects the life-or-death authority this role carries. Credential verification is mandatory and non-negotiable.

## PNW Connection

Pacific Northwest tide pools are the most intensively studied ecosystems on Earth because they make everything visible. Every organism, every interaction, every stress response plays out in a contained, observable space. Emergency medicine on the playa operates the same way: every patient, every intervention, every outcome is documented and reviewed. The tide pool hides nothing, and neither does the medical record.
