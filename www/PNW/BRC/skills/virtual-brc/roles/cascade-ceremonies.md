# Cascade Ceremonies

**BRC Department:** Special Events
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Old Growth Assembly
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

A cascade is water falling from height — not a single drop, but a sequence of pools and ledges, each one feeding the next. Cascade Ceremonies coordinates the permitted events that punctuate the week: the Man burn, the Temple burn, art burns, large-scale performances, and any gathering that requires formal permitting. Each event is a pool in the cascade, and each must be staged before the next can flow.

Special Events is the logistics backbone behind spectacle. Volunteers coordinate burn permits, manage pyrotechnician schedules, liaise with the fire safety perimeter teams, sequence event timing to avoid conflicts, and ensure that every large-scale happening has the infrastructure, safety coverage, and communications it needs. The work is invisible when done well — the audience sees fire and beauty, not the permit binder and radio checks that made it possible.

In the virtual city, Cascade Ceremonies teaches event production logistics, permitting workflows, multi-team coordination, and the art of making complex operations look effortless. The role bridges the creative vision of artists with the operational reality of safety, timing, and resource constraints.

## Skills Taught

1. Event production logistics and permitting workflows
2. Multi-team coordination across safety, infrastructure, and creative teams
3. Pyrotechnic event scheduling and sequencing
4. Contingency planning for weather, equipment failure, and crowd dynamics
5. Stakeholder communication — translating artist vision into operational plans

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Fireline Cedar (burn perimeter), Watershed Watch (ranger presence), Tide Pool Medical (medical standby), Cascade Brigade (event infrastructure)
- **Enables:** All art burns and large-scale events, Old Growth Assembly (Center Camp programming), Tahoma's Eye (Man burn coordination)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Falls stamp after coordinating logistics for at least one permitted event. The Plunge Pool endorsement recognizes service across multiple event types — burns, performances, and processions — within a single season. Long-term coordinators who have staged events across many years earn the Headwater recognition.

## PNW Connection

The Pacific Northwest's cascading waterfalls are not random — they follow geological structure, each ledge and pool shaped by the rock beneath. A waterfall looks spontaneous but is entirely determined by the terrain it crosses. Ceremony coordination works the same way: what the audience experiences as spontaneous spectacle is shaped entirely by the logistical bedrock underneath.
