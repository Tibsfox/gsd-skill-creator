# Geoduck Watch

**BRC Department:** Earth Guardians
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Cedar Grove
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

The geoduck clam burrows three feet into the substrate and stays there for over a century, filtering water and monitoring the health of its environment with quiet persistence. Geoduck Watch is the Earth Guardians team: the environmental monitoring force that tracks the city's impact on the playa surface, conducts MOOP (Matter Out Of Place) sweeps, and ensures that when the city vanishes, the desert shows no trace of its presence.

Leave No Trace is not a suggestion at Burning Man — it is a condition of the Bureau of Land Management permit that allows the event to exist. Earth Guardians walk the playa grid square by grid square after the event, picking up every sequin, every wood screw, every cigarette butt, every paint drip. During the event, they monitor high-traffic areas for MOOP accumulation, educate participants about environmental impact, and coordinate with theme camps on waste management practices.

In the virtual city, Geoduck Watch teaches environmental stewardship at scale, systematic survey methodology, waste stream management, and the discipline of caring for a landscape that is not yours. The role develops attention to detail at ground level — the ability to scan a square meter of desert and see the single zip tie that does not belong.

## Skills Taught

1. Systematic environmental survey and MOOP mapping methodology
2. Waste stream identification, sorting, and management
3. Environmental education and participant behavior change
4. Leave No Trace compliance verification at industrial scale
5. Grid-based search patterns and ground-level observation discipline

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Territory Mapping (grid reference data), Cascade Brigade (post-strike site access)
- **Enables:** Restoration Pass (final environmental clearance), All roles (LNT compliance awareness)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Substrate stamp after completing a full MOOP sweep rotation. The Deep Burrow endorsement recognizes multi-year Earth Guardian service and a demonstrated record of environmental advocacy within the community. Geoduck Watch volunteers frequently earn the Leave No Trace certification stamp, which is referenced in the city's BLM permit compliance documentation.

## PNW Connection

The Pacific geoduck is the world's longest-lived burrowing clam — specimens over 160 years old have been documented in Puget Sound. It survives by embedding itself deep in the substrate and filtering everything that passes through. Earth Guardians practice the same patient, substrate-level attention: embedded in the ground, filtering what does not belong, enduring long after the surface activity has passed.
