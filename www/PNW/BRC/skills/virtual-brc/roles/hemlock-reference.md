# Hemlock Reference

**BRC Department:** Playa Info
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Raven's Roost
**Trust Level Required:** 1
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

Western hemlock grows in deep shade, thriving where other trees cannot — a quiet presence that knows the forest floor better than any species in the canopy. Hemlock Reference operates Playa Info: the information booth at the heart of Black Rock City where anyone can walk up and ask anything. Where is the nearest medical tent? What time does the Temple burn? Is there a camp that teaches glassblowing? Has anyone seen my friend?

Playa Info volunteers are the city's collective memory. They maintain a comprehensive directory of theme camps, events, art installations, and services. They answer questions ranging from the practical (where to find ice) to the existential (what should I do with my life on the playa). The role requires encyclopedic knowledge of the city's layout and offerings, combined with the patience to answer the same question hundreds of times a day with genuine warmth.

In the virtual city, Hemlock Reference teaches information architecture, wayfinding, community knowledge management, and the skill of meeting people where they are. Not everyone arrives knowing what they need — a good reference volunteer helps people discover the question before offering the answer.

## Skills Taught

1. Information architecture and community knowledge curation
2. Wayfinding and spatial orientation in temporary environments
3. Active listening and needs assessment in casual encounters
4. Rapid information retrieval under high-volume conditions
5. Cross-cultural communication and radical inclusion through service

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Territory Mapping (city layout data), Raven Archive (event and camp directory), Marten Signal (communications for real-time updates)
- **Enables:** All participants (navigation and discovery), Ravens Welcome (post-greeting orientation), Old Growth Assembly (Center Camp integration)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Understory stamp after completing a full shift rotation at Playa Info. The Shade Tolerance endorsement recognizes multi-year service and the deep institutional knowledge that comes from seasons of answering every conceivable question. Hemlock Reference volunteers often earn the Communal Effort stamp for exemplifying gifting through knowledge.

## PNW Connection

Western hemlock is the Pacific Northwest's most shade-tolerant conifer — it germinates on nurse logs, grows beneath dense canopy, and accumulates knowledge of the forest floor that no sun-loving species possesses. Playa Info operates the same way: rooted in the understory, thriving in the shade of louder operations, knowing more about the ground-level reality of the city than anyone in the canopy.
