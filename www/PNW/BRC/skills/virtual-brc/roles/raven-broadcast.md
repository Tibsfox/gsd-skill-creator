# Raven Broadcast

**BRC Department:** Info Radio
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Raven's Roost
**Trust Level Required:** 1
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

Ravens are the loudest birds in the Pacific Northwest — their calls carry across valleys, cut through wind, and reach every corner of the forest. A raven does not whisper; it broadcasts. Raven Broadcast operates BMIR (Burning Man Information Radio): the low-power FM station that serves as the city's real-time public information channel, broadcasting weather updates, event schedules, safety announcements, and community programming.

BMIR is the only broadcast medium that reaches the entire city simultaneously. When a dust storm approaches, BMIR announces it. When an art car parade begins, BMIR describes the route. When someone is lost, BMIR puts out the call. Volunteers host shows, read announcements, manage the broadcast schedule, maintain transmitter equipment, and curate the mix of practical information and creative programming that makes the station both essential and enjoyable.

In the virtual city, Raven Broadcast teaches real-time information dissemination, broadcast production, editorial judgment under time pressure, and the discipline of serving as a trusted public information source. The role develops the ability to distill complex situations into clear, calm, actionable language — and to do it live, with no second take.

## Skills Taught

1. Live broadcast production and on-air communication
2. Real-time editorial judgment and information triage
3. Transmitter maintenance and signal management
4. Public information dissemination during routine and emergency conditions
5. Community programming curation and scheduling

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Marten Signal (transmitter infrastructure), Hemlock Reference (event schedule data)
- **Enables:** All participants (real-time information access), Watershed Watch (public safety announcements), Cascade Ceremonies (event promotion)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Broadcast stamp after completing a full on-air shift or production support rotation. The Valley Call endorsement recognizes sustained contribution across multiple shows or emergency broadcast events. Raven Broadcast volunteers who demonstrate consistent accuracy and calm under pressure earn trust advancement through their reliability as a public information source.

## PNW Connection

The common raven's vocalizations are the most complex of any North American bird — over 30 distinct call types, each carrying different information across different distances. Ravens broadcast territorial boundaries, food locations, predator warnings, and social status through sound alone. BMIR serves the same function: a single voice that reaches the entire territory, carrying the information the community needs to navigate its environment.
