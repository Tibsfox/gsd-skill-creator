# Cedar Canopy

**BRC Department:** Theme Camp Support
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Cedar Grove
**Trust Level Required:** 1
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

A cedar canopy does not just shade the tree beneath it — it shapes the microclimate for everything below. Temperature drops, moisture rises, wind calms, and a sheltered space emerges where seedlings can establish. Cedar Canopy provides theme camp support: helping new and returning camps get set up, offering structural guidance, and mentoring camp organizers through the logistical challenges of building a functional camp on the playa.

Theme camps are the social backbone of Black Rock City. Each one creates a unique offering — a bar, a workshop, a sound stage, a healing space — and each must be built from nothing in a hostile desert environment. Cedar Canopy volunteers visit camps during early arrival, help troubleshoot shade structure engineering, advise on power distribution, and connect camps with resources they did not know existed. The role is equal parts construction consultant and community counselor.

In the virtual city, Cedar Canopy teaches mentorship, structural problem-solving, and the art of empowering others rather than doing the work for them. A good canopy volunteer does not build the camp — they teach the camp to build itself, then check back to make sure the structure held.

## Skills Taught

1. Mentorship and knowledge transfer in field conditions
2. Shade structure engineering and improvised construction techniques
3. Power distribution planning for temporary installations
4. Community resource mapping and connection brokering
5. Empowerment-based support — teaching rather than doing

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Cascade Brigade (infrastructure context), Territory Mapping (camp placement data)
- **Enables:** All theme camps (setup success), Old Growth Assembly (Center Camp integration), Mycorrhizal Hub (educational programming at camp level)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Canopy stamp after providing documented support to at least three theme camps during a single event. The Nurse Stump endorsement recognizes ongoing mentorship relationships with camps across multiple years. Cedar Canopy volunteers frequently earn the Communal Effort stamp for embodying radical self-reliance through teaching.

## PNW Connection

Western red cedar canopies create the most productive understory habitat in the Pacific Northwest. The shade they cast is not a limitation but a gift — reducing evaporation, buffering temperature swings, and creating conditions where species that cannot survive in direct sun can flourish. Theme camp support works identically: the canopy does not compete with what grows beneath it, it creates the conditions for growth.
