# Fireline Cedar

**BRC Department:** Burn Perimeter Support
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Cedar Grove
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** MANDATORY-PASS

## What This Role Does

Western red cedar does not burn easily. Its thick, fibrous bark resists flame, and old cedars often survive fires that consume everything around them. Fireline Cedar holds the burn perimeter — the safety boundary that separates the crowd from the Man burn, the Temple burn, and other large-scale fire events.

Volunteers in this role form a human perimeter around the burn site, maintaining a safe distance between spectators and the fire. The work is physically demanding — standing for hours in heat radiated from massive structures burning at thousands of degrees — and requires absolute discipline. A gap in the perimeter could mean a participant walks into the fire zone.

In the virtual city, Fireline Cedar teaches thermal boundary management, crowd control at emotional events, and the discipline of holding a line when instinct says move. The burns are the emotional climax of the event, and the perimeter team ensures that climax does not become a crisis. This is a MANDATORY-PASS safety role.

## Skills Taught

1. Perimeter management and crowd containment at large-scale events
2. Thermal hazard awareness and radiant heat safety
3. Team coordination across extended physical lines
4. Discipline under emotional and environmental pressure
5. Emergency response protocols for perimeter breaches

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Watershed Watch (ranger backup), Tide Pool Medical (medical standby), Cascade Brigade (fire infrastructure)
- **Enables:** Cascade Ceremonies (safe execution of burns), Tahoma's Eye (pre-burn handoff)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Fireline stamp after completing perimeter duty for at least one major burn event. The Heartwood endorsement is awarded to those who serve the full burn season (Man burn, Temple burn, and art burns). Because this is a MANDATORY-PASS role, completion is verified by shift supervisors and logged in the safety registry.

## PNW Connection

Western red cedar is the Pacific Northwest's fire-resistant sentinel. Its thick bark and high moisture content allow it to survive low-intensity fires that clear the understory. The name honors the cedar's role as a standing boundary — the tree that holds the line between burned and unburned forest.
