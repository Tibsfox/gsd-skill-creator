# Salish Press

**BRC Department:** Media Mecca / Placement
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Raven's Roost
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

The Salish Sea has been a crossroads of trade and communication for millennia — a place where information, goods, and stories from distant origins converge and are redistributed. Salish Press manages Media Mecca: the department that coordinates journalist access, media credentials, photographer placement, and the relationship between Black Rock City and the outside world's press.

The city has a complicated relationship with media. Participants value immediacy, presence, and the freedom from surveillance that comes with being in a space where cameras are not pointed at you by default. Yet the event also has stories worth telling, and telling them well requires journalists who understand the culture. Salish Press volunteers vet media applicants, issue credentials, brief journalists on community norms (especially consent and photography ethics), manage press placement at major events, and serve as the interface between the city's internal culture and external reporting.

In the virtual city, Salish Press teaches media relations, credential management, editorial ethics, and the delicate art of controlling narrative without censorship. The role develops the skill of protecting a community's story while allowing outsiders to tell it — a balance that requires trust, clear boundaries, and genuine engagement.

## Skills Taught

1. Media credential vetting and journalist access management
2. Press briefing and cultural orientation for external reporters
3. Photography and filming ethics in consent-based communities
4. Narrative management without censorship
5. Stakeholder communication between internal culture and external media

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Raven Archive (media assets and historical context), Marten Signal (communications), Watershed Watch (on-site media escort coordination)
- **Enables:** Raven Broadcast (media content integration), Raven Archive (journalist contributions), All participants (cultural narrative protection)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Crossroads stamp after completing a full media placement cycle. The Trade Wind endorsement recognizes service across multiple media functions — vetting, briefing, placement, and post-event review — within a single season. Salish Press volunteers who demonstrate consistent judgment in balancing access and protection earn trust advancement through their reliability as cultural stewards.

## PNW Connection

The Salish Sea was the Pacific Northwest's original press room — the place where stories from the coast, the interior, and distant trading partners converged. Indigenous trade networks carried not just goods but narratives across thousands of miles, and the Salish waterways were the hub where these narratives were received, evaluated, and redistributed. Media Mecca serves the same function: a convergence point where external stories meet internal culture.
