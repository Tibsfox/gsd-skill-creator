# Glacial Flow

**BRC Department:** Arctica (Ice)
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Cedar Grove
**Trust Level Required:** 1
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

Glaciers move slowly but carry enormous weight. They carve valleys, feed rivers, and distribute water across entire watersheds. Glacial Flow operates Arctica — the ice distribution service that keeps Black Rock City hydrated and cool in the desert heat. This is cold-chain logistics at scale: receiving bulk ice deliveries, breaking them into manageable quantities, and distributing them to the population.

Volunteers manage the queue — which can stretch for blocks in peak heat — while maintaining stock levels, handling cash transactions, and keeping the workspace clean. The role demands physical endurance (moving hundreds of pounds of ice per shift) and emotional steadiness (managing a crowd of hot, thirsty people who have been waiting in the sun).

In the virtual city, Glacial Flow teaches resource distribution under constraint. Ice is finite, demand is unpredictable, and the window between delivery and melt is unforgiving. Every shift is a lesson in flow management, inventory tracking, and serving a community with patience when conditions are harsh.

## Skills Taught

1. Cold-chain logistics and perishable resource management
2. Queue management and crowd flow under high demand
3. Physical endurance and workplace safety in extreme conditions
4. Inventory tracking with real-time depletion rates
5. Community service under sustained environmental stress

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Cascade Brigade (infrastructure for ice storage), Chinook Transit (delivery logistics)
- **Enables:** All camps and participants (ice is a universal dependency in desert conditions)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Glacial Core stamp after completing a full shift rotation. The role recognizes sustained physical contribution — carrying, stacking, distributing — with the Meltwater endorsement for those who serve during peak-heat shifts. Volume served is tracked but recognition focuses on reliability and demeanor under pressure.

## PNW Connection

The glaciers of the Pacific Northwest — from Mount Rainier's Emmons to the Blue Glacier on Olympus — are the region's original cold-chain infrastructure. They store winter snowfall and release it slowly through summer, feeding rivers that sustain entire ecosystems. Arctica does the same for the city: storing cold and releasing it when the community needs it most.
