# Columbia Gate

**BRC Department:** Gate, Perimeter, Exodus
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Tide Pool Collective
**Trust Level Required:** 3
**Safety Classification:** MANDATORY-PASS

## What This Role Does

The Columbia River Gorge is the only sea-level passage through the Cascade Range — a single gateway carved by catastrophic floods, channeling everything that enters or exits the interior basin. Columbia Gate manages the physical entry and exit of Black Rock City: the gate where vehicles arrive, the perimeter fence that defines the city boundary, and the exodus traffic management that empties 80,000 people onto a two-lane highway.

Gate volunteers process every arriving vehicle — checking tickets, searching for stowaways, verifying early arrival passes, and welcoming participants to the playa. Perimeter teams patrol the trash fence that rings the city, monitoring for unauthorized entry and maintaining the physical boundary. Exodus teams manage the controlled release of traffic after the event, metering vehicles onto the highway to prevent gridlock that could strand people in the desert for 12 hours or more.

In the virtual city, Columbia Gate teaches access control at scale, perimeter security, traffic flow management, and the critical discipline of managing a chokepoint without creating a crisis. This is a MANDATORY-PASS safety role because a failure at the gate — whether a perimeter breach, a missed search, or an exodus gridlock — directly endangers lives.

## Skills Taught

1. Large-scale access control and vehicle processing
2. Perimeter integrity management and breach response
3. Traffic flow metering and exodus management
4. Search procedures and contraband interdiction
5. Chokepoint management under sustained high-volume pressure

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Salish Gate (Box Office credential verification), Marten Signal (communications), Watershed Watch (ranger support)
- **Enables:** All participants (physical entry/exit), Chinook Transit (vehicle flow coordination), Osprey Survey (population tracking)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Gorge stamp after completing a full shift rotation at Gate, Perimeter, or Exodus. The Floodwater endorsement is awarded for service during peak gate-rush or exodus — the highest-pressure operational windows. Because this is a MANDATORY-PASS role, all gate processing is logged and reviewed. Trust level 3 reflects the security sensitivity and life-safety implications of controlling the city's only entry and exit.

## PNW Connection

The Columbia River Gorge was carved by the Missoula Floods — the largest floods in geological history, channeling an inland sea through a single gap in the mountains at 80 miles per hour. The gorge is a chokepoint that handles enormous flow, and it works because the channel is deep, wide, and structurally sound. Gate operations must be the same: a controlled channel handling massive throughput without failure.
