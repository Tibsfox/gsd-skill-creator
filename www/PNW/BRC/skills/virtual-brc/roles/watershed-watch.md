# Watershed Watch

**BRC Department:** Black Rock Rangers
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Tide Pool Collective
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** MANDATORY-PASS

## What This Role Does

A watershed connects every ridge, stream, and valley into a single drainage system. When one tributary floods, the whole basin feels it. Watershed Watch is the Black Rock Rangers — the community's non-enforcement safety presence. Rangers walk the playa, monitor conditions, de-escalate conflicts, and serve as the connective tissue between participants and the operational infrastructure.

Rangers do not enforce rules. They mediate. They listen. They assess situations and connect people to the right resources — whether that is medical support, a quiet conversation, or simply a reminder that the desert demands hydration. The role requires emotional intelligence, physical stamina, and the ability to remain calm when everyone around you is not.

In the virtual city, Watershed Watch teaches conflict de-escalation, situational awareness, and the art of non-authoritarian safety work. Rangers operate at the boundary between participant autonomy and community well-being, and learning to hold that boundary with grace is the core skill this role develops.

## Skills Taught

1. Conflict de-escalation and active listening techniques
2. Situational awareness and threat assessment without enforcement authority
3. Community safety through presence rather than control
4. Resource routing — connecting people to appropriate services
5. Emotional resilience and boundary maintenance during sustained shifts

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Watershed Command (dispatch and coordination), Tide Pool Medical (medical escalation path), Marten Signal (radio communications)
- **Enables:** All participants (safety net), Columbia Gate (perimeter support), Cascade Ceremonies (event safety)

## Completion Recognition

Rangers earn the Watershed stamp after completing ranger training and a minimum shift rotation. The Headwaters endorsement is awarded for multi-year service and demonstrated de-escalation skill. Safety-critical incidents are reviewed by Watershed Command; successful resolution contributes to trust level advancement.

## PNW Connection

The Pacific Northwest is defined by its watersheds — the Columbia, the Fraser, the Skagit. These systems connect everything: snow on distant peaks feeds rivers that sustain salmon, forests, and cities hundreds of miles downstream. Rangers function the same way, connecting every part of the community into a single safety drainage basin where problems flow toward resolution.
