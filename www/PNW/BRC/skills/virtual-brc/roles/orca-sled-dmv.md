# Orca Sled DMV

**BRC Department:** Dept of Mutant Vehicles
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Cedar Grove
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## What This Role Does

Orcas are the ocean's apex mobile structures — multi-ton bodies moving through three-dimensional space with precision, communicating in pod-specific dialects, navigating by echolocation. The Orca Sled DMV licenses and registers mutant vehicles: the kinetic art platforms, fire-breathing cars, and multi-story rolling sculptures that define Black Rock City's mobile landscape.

Volunteers inspect vehicles for safety compliance, issue day and night permits (night permits require adequate lighting), manage the registration database, and patrol the playa to enforce licensing. The role requires mechanical knowledge, safety assessment skill, and the diplomatic ability to tell an artist that their creation needs modifications before it can roam the city.

In the virtual city, Orca Sled DMV teaches mobile structure governance — how to balance creative freedom with public safety when the objects in question are one-of-a-kind machines built in garages. Every vehicle is unique, so every inspection is a first-principles assessment. The role builds judgment that no checklist can fully capture.

## Skills Taught

1. Mobile structure safety inspection and first-principles assessment
2. Vehicle licensing and registration database management
3. Night-safety evaluation (lighting, visibility, sound)
4. Diplomatic enforcement — delivering safety requirements to artists
5. Mechanical systems knowledge and structural integrity assessment

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Watershed Watch (enforcement backup), Cascade Brigade (road infrastructure)
- **Enables:** All mutant vehicle operators, Cascade Ceremonies (vehicle participation in events)

## Completion Recognition

Inspectors earn the Pod Signal stamp after completing a full inspection rotation. The Echolocation endorsement recognizes night-patrol service, where vehicles are assessed in darkness for lighting and visibility compliance. Inspection accuracy — measured by incident-free operation of licensed vehicles — is the primary quality metric.

## PNW Connection

The orca pods of the Salish Sea are the Pacific Northwest's most recognizable mobile communities. Each pod has its own dialect, its own territory, and its own movement patterns — but all share the same waters and must navigate without collision. Mutant vehicles operate the same way: unique machines sharing common ground, requiring coordination to coexist safely.
