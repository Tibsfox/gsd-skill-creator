# Cascade Brigade

**BRC Department:** Dept of Public Works
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Cedar Grove
**Trust Level Required:** 3
**Safety Classification:** MANDATORY-PASS

## What This Role Does

The Cascade Range is built from volcanic basalt, glacial till, and centuries of tectonic force — raw geology shaped into mountains. Cascade Brigade is the Department of Public Works: the crew that builds and strikes Black Rock City. Every road, every structure, every piece of infrastructure that makes the city habitable is placed, maintained, and removed by this team.

DPW arrives weeks before anyone else and leaves weeks after. They grade roads in alkali dust, erect shade structures in 100-degree heat, run electrical lines, build the Man base, and construct everything the city needs to function. When the event ends, they reverse the process — striking every structure, pulling every stake, grading every road until the playa shows no trace of human presence. The work is physically brutal and operationally unforgiving.

In the virtual city, Cascade Brigade teaches large-scale construction logistics, material handling, infrastructure sequencing, and the discipline of Leave No Trace at industrial scale. This is a MANDATORY-PASS safety role because structural failures endanger lives. Every bolt torqued, every cable tensioned, every road graded affects the safety of 80,000 people.

## Skills Taught

1. Large-scale construction and deconstruction logistics
2. Infrastructure sequencing — build order and strike order planning
3. Heavy equipment operation and material handling safety
4. Leave No Trace compliance at industrial scale
5. Structural integrity verification under extreme environmental conditions

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Territory Mapping (site survey and placement), Chinook Transit (material transport), Marten Signal (communications)
- **Enables:** All roles (physical infrastructure), Fireline Cedar (burn infrastructure), Old Growth Assembly (Center Camp structure), Cascade Ceremonies (event structures)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Basalt stamp after completing a full build or strike cycle. The Columnar endorsement is awarded for service across both build and strike — seeing the city rise and fall in the same season. Because this is a MANDATORY-PASS role, all structural work is inspected and logged in the safety registry. Trust level 3 reflects the extreme physical risk and operational authority this role carries.

## PNW Connection

The Cascade Range was built by the most violent geological forces on the continent — subduction, eruption, glaciation. Yet the result is a mountain range of extraordinary beauty and structural integrity. DPW builds a city from nothing in the same way: raw force applied with precision, creating infrastructure that must withstand wind, heat, and the weight of a temporary civilization.
