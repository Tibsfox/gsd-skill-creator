# Restoration Pass

**BRC Department:** Cleanup / Restoration
**Domain:** virtual-brc/roles
**Camp Affiliation:** Cedar Grove
**Trust Level Required:** 2
**Safety Classification:** MANDATORY-PASS

## What This Role Does

After a wildfire moves through a Pacific Northwest forest, the first green shoots appear within weeks. Restoration is not an afterthought — it is the forest's primary response. Restoration Pass executes the Leave No Trace mandate: after the event ends, every trace of Black Rock City must be removed from the Black Rock Desert. Every scrap of MOOP (Matter Out Of Place), every tent stake hole, every paint splatter.

Volunteers walk grid patterns across the playa, picking up debris at the scale of individual staples and sequin fragments. They sweep camp areas, inspect infrastructure removal sites, and verify that the land has been returned to its pre-event condition. The work is physically exhausting, emotionally sobering, and absolutely non-negotiable — the Bureau of Land Management inspects the site and the event's future depends on passing.

In the virtual city, Restoration Pass teaches the discipline of leave-better-than-found. This is the civic role that closes the loop — ensuring that the community's existence does not degrade the commons. It is a MANDATORY-PASS safety role because failure here threatens the event's survival.

## Skills Taught

1. Leave No Trace execution at industrial scale
2. Grid-pattern survey and systematic area clearing
3. Environmental restoration and site remediation
4. Quality assurance and regulatory compliance
5. Post-event accountability and civic responsibility

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Geoduck Watch (environmental monitoring data), Cascade Brigade (infrastructure removal), Territory Mapping (grid assignments)
- **Enables:** Future events (BLM compliance), Geoduck Watch (final environmental verification)

## Completion Recognition

Volunteers earn the Restoration stamp after completing a full cleanup shift. The First Green endorsement recognizes those who serve the entire post-event restoration period. Because this is a MANDATORY-PASS role, completion is verified through grid inspection reports and supervisor sign-off. The BLM inspection result serves as the ultimate quality metric for the entire team.

## PNW Connection

Mountain passes in the Pacific Northwest are corridors of renewal — places where disturbance and recovery cycle continuously. Avalanches strip slopes bare; wildflowers colonize the debris within a season. Restoration Pass embodies this cycle: the city is the disturbance, and the cleanup is the ecosystem's recovery response.
