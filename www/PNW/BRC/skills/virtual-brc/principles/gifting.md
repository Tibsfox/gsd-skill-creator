# Gifting — Unconditional Skill Share

**Principle:** Burning Man is devoted to acts of gift giving. The value of a gift is unconditional. Gifting does not contemplate a return or an exchange for something of equal value.
**GSD Guideline:** Unconditional Skill Share
**Domain:** virtual-brc/principles
**Safety Classification:** RECOMMENDED

## Pattern

In the temperate rainforest, a western red cedar does not negotiate terms before its
fallen trunk becomes a nurse log. It gives its body to the forest floor
unconditionally — decades of accumulated structure offered without contract, without
expectation of reciprocity. Hemlock seedlings, sword ferns, and shelf fungi colonize
the gift freely. This is the model for skill sharing in Virtual Black Rock City.

Skills are published to the commons without license restrictions on skill content.
When a rig authors a skill and submits it to the shared registry, it relinquishes
any expectation of reciprocal use, attribution credit, or conditional access. The
skill becomes part of the commons — available to every rig on the playa. The system
does not track "who used whose skill" for the purpose of creating obligation graphs.
There are no dependency licenses that restrict downstream use, no "share-alike"
clauses that condition access on contribution.

The boundary is practical: safety constraints still apply (a skill that violates
warden policy is rejected regardless of its gift status), but no economic or social
constraint gates access. The gift is the gift. A rig that publishes one skill and
consumes a hundred is as welcome as one that publishes a hundred and consumes none.
The playa does not keep accounts.

## Testable Behaviors

1. Published skills in the shared registry contain no license field that restricts downstream use, modification, or redistribution.
2. The system does not maintain or expose an obligation graph linking skill consumers to skill authors.
3. A rig that has published zero skills can access and execute any skill in the shared registry without restriction.
4. Skill metadata does not include usage counters, download counts, or consumer lists visible to the authoring rig.
5. The submission pipeline accepts skills without requiring the submitting rig to have consumed or reviewed other skills first.

## Dependencies

- `radical-inclusion` — Open access ensures gifts reach everyone
- `decommodification` — Anti-gamification prevents gifts from becoming social currency
- `communal-effort` — Multi-agent topology means gifts naturally compose into collaborative structures

## PNW Metaphor

Like a nurse log on the Olympic Peninsula forest floor, a published skill offers its
structure to the commons without negotiation. The cedar does not ask the hemlock
seedling to justify its need — the gift simply persists until it is consumed by time
and succession.
