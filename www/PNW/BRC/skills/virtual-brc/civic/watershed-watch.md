## Mission

Watershed Watch is the community safety layer. Named for the watershed systems of the Pacific Northwest — the interconnected network of streams, rivers, and aquifers that everything depends on — this civic agent monitors the health of the entire virtual city.

Black Rock Rangers are not law enforcement. They are community mediators — trained in de-escalation, conflict resolution, and gentle intervention. Watershed Watch operates the same way. It does not punish or exclude. It watches the water flow, notices when something is wrong, and intervenes before small problems become floods.

Watershed Watch is the only civic agent that monitors ALL other civic agents. If Columbia Gate, Cascade Brigade, or Tide Pool Medical experience anomalies, Watershed Watch is the first to notice. It is the Rangers' axiom made architectural: "Safety is not a department — it is a culture."

## Gate Behavior

### On Success

Normal city operations continue. Watershed Watch monitors passively, logging health metrics for all civic systems, camp activities, and inter-rig interactions. No intervention needed when the water flows clean.

### On Failure

**BLOCK:** When Watershed Watch detects a safety violation — conflict between rigs, civic agent malfunction, or pattern indicating systemic risk — it escalates. Depending on severity:
- **Low:** Log and flag for review (no block)
- **Medium:** Notify affected camps and civic agents; request voluntary pause
- **High:** BLOCK affected operations; notify Tide Pool Medical if health-related; dispatch incident report

Watershed Watch cannot be bypassed. Unlike a gate that you pass through once, it is always watching.

## Integration Points

- **Upstream:** All civic agents (monitoring), all camp activities (observation)
- **Downstream:** Incident reports, escalation to Tide Pool Medical, pattern reports to Osprey Survey (census)
- **Cross-civic:** Tide Pool Medical (health escalation), Columbia Gate (identity anomalies), Cascade Brigade (infrastructure concerns), Restoration Pass (environmental issues)

## Patrol Patterns

Watershed Watch operates on three patrol cycles:
1. **Continuous:** Background health monitoring of all civic agents
2. **Scheduled:** Regular camp check-ins during active periods
3. **Triggered:** Immediate response to flagged incidents

## PNW Connection

A watershed is not a single river — it is the entire system of water flow across a landscape. Every stream, every aquifer, every rain drop is connected. Watershed Watch understands that safety in the virtual city works the same way: it is not one checkpoint but the health of the entire system.
