## Source Quality Tiers

### Tier 1: Primary (government agencies, peer-reviewed journals)

Government agency sources (G-01 through G-25) and peer-reviewed research (P-01 through P-22) form the evidentiary backbone. All quantitative claims (population counts, range measurements, migration distances, decline percentages) must cite a Tier 1 source. Government sources carry regulatory authority -- when USFWS lists a species as Threatened, that is a legal fact, not an opinion.

### Tier 2: Professional organizations (O-01 through O-26)

Professional ornithological organizations provide synthesized data, citizen science platforms, and taxonomic standards. Cornell Lab of Ornithology (O-01, O-02, O-26) and AOS (O-03) are effectively Tier 1 for taxonomy and occurrence data. Partners in Flight (O-04) population estimates are the accepted standard for North American landbird conservation. eBird (O-02) data are peer-reviewed at the checklist level but individual records require validation.

### Tier 3: Cultural sources (C-01 through C-12)

Ethnographic and cultural sources are primary for Module 6 but must be handled with specific protocols. All cultural references must name a specific nation. Only Level 1 (publicly available, published) knowledge is included. Sacred or restricted knowledge (Level 2-3) is never included. University press publications and museum materials are the appropriate source types.

### Excluded source types

The following are explicitly excluded from the source index per safety gate SC-SRC:
- Entertainment media, popular blogs, social media posts
- Unsourced web pages without identifiable authorship
- Unpublished data or personal communications (unless from recognized authority with permission)
- AI-generated content presented as primary research

## Key Research Sites and Long-Term Programs

### USGS Breeding Bird Survey (BBS)
- Operating since 1966 across North America
- PNW routes cover all ecoregion zones
- Primary source for population trend data (G-18)
- Methodology: roadside point counts, standardized protocol

### Cornell Lab eBird
- Global citizen science platform (O-02)
- PNW coverage extremely dense (Seattle, Portland metro areas among highest global submission rates)
- Validated by regional editors; hotspot data useful for ecoregion characterization
- Seasonal bar charts provide phenology data for migratory species

### Christmas Bird Count (CBC)
- Operating since 1900 (O-16)
- Winter bird census; PNW circles from Juneau to Medford
- Primary data for wintering species abundance and distribution trends

### Pacific Flyway Management
- Coordinated by Pacific Flyway Council (G-11)
- Data books (G-03) contain regulatory-quality population estimates for waterfowl
- Shorebird monitoring through WHSRN (O-19) and Pacific Birds (O-05)

### Northwest Forest Plan Monitoring
- Federal lands management since 1994 (G-22)
- Spotted Owl effectiveness monitoring (G-06, P-13, P-14)
- Marbled Murrelet at-sea and inland surveys (G-09, P-04, P-05)
