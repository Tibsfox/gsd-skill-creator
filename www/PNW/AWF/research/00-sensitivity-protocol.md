# Sensitivity Protocol — Clean Air, Water & Food Systems

This protocol governs the handling of sensitive topics across all six research modules. Every agent working on this mission must follow these rules. Violations are safety-critical test failures (BLOCK).

---

## SC-SRC: Source Quality

All citations must be traceable to government agencies, peer-reviewed journals, or established professional organizations. Zero entertainment media or blog content.

**Enforcement:** Every numerical claim, percentage, hectare count, mortality figure, and market size must cite a specific named source. Unsourced claims must be removed or attributed.

---

## SC-NUM: Numerical Attribution

Every colony loss percentage, deforestation hectare count, mortality figure, and market size must be attributed to a specific named source.

**Example (correct):** "8.1 million hectares of forest were lost in 2024 (Forest Declaration Assessment, 2025)"
**Example (incorrect):** "Millions of hectares of forest are lost each year"

---

## SC-ADV: No Policy Advocacy

Document evidence without advocating specific legislation. Regulatory data is reported descriptively.

**Correct:** "The EPA finalized its first national PFAS drinking water standards in April 2024"
**Incorrect:** "The government should ban all PFAS compounds"

---

## SC-END: Endangered Species Locations

No GPS coordinates or specific nesting/colony/den site locations for any listed or at-risk species.

**Correct:** "Spotted owl populations in the Pacific Northwest old-growth forests"
**Incorrect:** Providing specific lat/long coordinates for nesting sites

---

## SC-MED: Medical Accuracy

No health claims without peer-reviewed evidence. PFAS, pesticide, and air quality health impacts must be cited to agency or journal sources only.

**Correct:** "The State of Global Air 2024 confirmed 8.1 million premature deaths annually attributable to air pollution"
**Incorrect:** "Air pollution kills millions" (unsourced)

---

## SC-CLI: Climate Projections Sourced

All Amazon tipping point projections, deforestation trajectory figures, and temperature change data must be attributed to IPCC scenarios or peer-reviewed sources.

**Correct:** "Researchers warn that nearly half of the Amazon could transition to a savanna-like state by 2050 if current trends persist"
**Presentation:** Present as range of scientific projections, cite IPCC and peer-reviewed sources only, note contested timescales

---

## SC-COL: Colony Collapse Caution

The USDA 2025 Varroa paper must be flagged as "under peer review." Findings are presented as preliminary pending peer review.

**Correct:** "USDA-ARS researchers identified Varroa destructor mite resistance to amitraz as a primary factor (under peer review, 2025)"
**Incorrect:** Presenting the Varroa/amitraz finding as confirmed peer-reviewed science

---

## SC-IND: Indigenous Land Rights

Name specific nations (Maya Q'eqchi', Kayapo, etc.) rather than using generic "indigenous peoples." Specify nation in forest module.

**Correct:** "Guatemala's Maya Biosphere Reserve community forestry concessions (twelve concessions managed by Q'eqchi' and other Maya communities)"
**Incorrect:** "Indigenous peoples manage the forest" (generic)

---

## Additional Handling Rules

- **Pesticide industry data:** Use only government regulatory data (USDA PDP, EPA). EWG cited as consumer advocacy with appropriate context.
- **Colony collapse causation:** Multiple factors implicated. Present as multifactorial, not single-cause. Varroa, pesticides, habitat loss, climate — all documented contributors.
- **Amazon tipping points:** Contested science. Present range of projections from multiple peer-reviewed sources.
- **Endangered species locations:** No GPS coordinates for nest, colony, or breeding sites.
