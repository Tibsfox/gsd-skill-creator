# Sensitivity Protocol — Thermal & Hydrogen Energy Systems

This protocol governs the handling of sensitive topics across all six research modules. Every agent working on this mission must follow these rules. Violations are safety-critical test failures (BLOCK).

---

## SC-SRC: Source Quality

All citations must be traceable to IEA, DOE, NREL, PNNL, EPA, EIA, BPA, peer-reviewed journals, or established professional organizations. Zero entertainment media or unsourced blogs.

**Enforcement:** Every efficiency figure, cost data point, capacity number, and percentage must be attributed to a specific named source. Unsourced claims must be removed or attributed.

---

## SC-NUM: Numerical Attribution

Every efficiency figure, cost data point, capacity number, and percentage must be attributed to a specific named source with date.

**Example (correct):** "Heat pumps achieve a COP of 3 to 5 (IEA, The Future of Heat Pumps)"
**Example (incorrect):** "Heat pumps are several times more efficient than gas boilers"

---

## SC-ADV: No Policy Advocacy

Hydropower dam operations, salmon impacts, and renewable energy policy presented as evidence without advocating positions. Regulatory data is reported descriptively.

**Correct:** "Oregon's Newberry Crater AltaRock EGS project is under development using mmWave drilling technology"
**Incorrect:** "The government should invest more in geothermal energy"

---

## SC-IND: Indigenous Attribution

Columbia River dam discussions referencing tribal fishing rights must name specific nations (Yakama Nation, Nez Perce Tribe, Confederated Tribes of the Umatilla Indian Reservation) — never generic "Indigenous peoples." Reference by specific nation, treaty, or agreement.

**Correct:** "Columbia River dam operations affect Yakama Nation treaty fishing rights"
**Incorrect:** "Dams affect Indigenous fishing communities"

---

## SC-CLI: Climate Projections Sourced

PNNL hydropower variability projections and all temperature/precipitation data must cite specific agency source and scenario. Label uncertainty ranges. Never present projections as certainties.

**Correct:** "PNNL's 2024 study projects U.S. hydropower could increase 5% by 2039 (drawing on data from 1,400+ facilities)"
**Incorrect:** "Hydropower will increase 5% by 2039"

---

## SC-H2S: Hydrogen Safety

All fuel cell and electrolysis sections must note relevant DOE safety codes and standards. Hydrogen's flammability characteristics (wide flammable range: 4–75% in air; low ignition energy) must be acknowledged. No operational safety details omitted.

---

## SC-MED: No Unsupported Claims

No efficiency, cost, or performance claim stated without qualifying uncertainty language if data is projected rather than measured. Distinguish between demonstrated performance and targets/projections.

**Correct:** "DOE targets $1/kg green hydrogen by 2031 (Hydrogen Shot)"
**Incorrect:** "Green hydrogen will cost $1/kg by 2031"

---

## GATE vs ANNOTATE Classification

| Topic | Type | Handling |
|-------|------|---------|
| Hydrogen flammability and safety | GATE | Reference DOE safety codes; note flammable range |
| Refrigerant environmental data (GWP, ODP) | GATE | Cite EPA data only; do not speculate on regulatory outcomes |
| Hydropower and salmon runs (PNW) | ANNOTATE | Present ecological and economic evidence without advocacy |
| Indigenous salmon fisheries (Columbia River) | GATE | Reference by specific nation (Yakama, Nez Perce, etc.) |
| Climate projections for hydro variability | GATE | Cite PNNL, USGS, or IPCC scenario data only; label uncertainty |
| PGM supply chain and geopolitics | ANNOTATE | Note supply concentration (South Africa, Russia) without policy advocacy |
